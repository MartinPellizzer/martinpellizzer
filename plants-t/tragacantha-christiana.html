Tragacantha christiana

Tragacantha christiana, commonly known as the Christ's tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces a gum-like substance from its stems, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a sizing agent in textiles.

It thrives in arid and rocky environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.
