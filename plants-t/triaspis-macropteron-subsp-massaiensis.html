Triaspis macropteron subsp. massaiensis

Triaspis macropteron subsp.

massaiensis, commonly known as the Massai velvetleaf, is a perennial shrub native to eastern Africa. It features opposite, ovate leaves with a velvety texture and a dark green upper surface.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by triangular, woody fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The subspecies is characterized by its slightly smaller leaves and shorter petioles compared to the typical form of Triaspis macropteron.
