Tilia cordata subsp. cordata

Tilia cordata subsp.

cordata, commonly known as smallleaf linden, is a deciduous tree native to Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its heart-shaped, serrated leaves that turn yellow in autumn.

The tree produces small, fragrant, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are clustered in racemes. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional medicine.
