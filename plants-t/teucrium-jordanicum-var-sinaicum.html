Teucrium jordanicum var. sinaicum

Teucrium jordanicum var. sinaicum, commonly known as Jordanian germander, is a perennial herb native to the Middle East and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale blue or violet, often blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or arid environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
