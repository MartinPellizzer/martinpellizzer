Taraxacum pulcherrimum

Taraxacum pulcherrimum, commonly known as the showy dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe and is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a rosette of basal leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs. Its taproot is thick and fibrous, allowing it to survive in a variety of soil conditions.

The seeds are dispersed by wind via a pappus, which gives the plant its well-known "dandelion clock" effect.
