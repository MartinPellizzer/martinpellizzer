Traganum nudatum var. obtusatum

Traganum nudatum var. obtusatum, commonly known as the broad-leaved spurge, is a succulent plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green or bluish-green, adapted to store water and reduce transpiration. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are often followed by spiny, three-lobed seed capsules. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it a hardy plant for xeriscaping.

Its botanical structure reflects its evolutionary adaptation to survive in harsh, dry environments.
