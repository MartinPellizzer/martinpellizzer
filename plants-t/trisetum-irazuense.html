Trisetum irazuense

Trisetum irazuense, commonly known as the Irazu sedge, is a perennial grass species native to Central America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and compact growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as cloud forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, while its flowering structures are small and clustered.

Trisetum irazuense plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
