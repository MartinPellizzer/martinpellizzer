Tacca cristata

Tacca cristata, commonly known as the "tongue of heaven" or "crown of jewels," is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces a unique, fragrant flower with a central spike resembling a crown, surrounded by waxy, violet petals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Tacca cristata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is considered a rare and exotic species in horticulture.
