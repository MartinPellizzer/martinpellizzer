Tillandsia brealitoensis

Tillandsia brealitoensis, commonly known as the Brealitoensis air plant, is a species of epiphytic bromeliad native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color, covered in a dense layer of trichomes that help absorb moisture from the air. The plant produces small, violet-colored flowers that bloom from the center of the rosette, adding a striking visual element. It thrives in humid environments with indirect light and requires minimal soil, as it absorbs nutrients through its leaves.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
