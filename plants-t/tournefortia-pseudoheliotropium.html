Tournefortia pseudoheliotropium

Tournefortia pseudoheliotropium, commonly known as the false heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal areas and saline soils. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are usually pink or purple, arranged in clusters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
