Trollius grandiflorus

Trollius grandiflorus, commonly known as the showy yellow buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features showy, bright yellow, saucer-shaped flowers with a prominent central disk, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has basal leaves that are deeply lobed and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, marshes, and along stream banks.

Trollius grandiflorus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
