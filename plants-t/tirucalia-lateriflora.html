Tirucalia lateriflora

Tirucalia lateriflora, commonly known as the Indian rose or velvet bean, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in yellow or orange hues. The plant produces long, cylindrical pods containing edible seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine and as a protein source. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and nutritional benefits.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
