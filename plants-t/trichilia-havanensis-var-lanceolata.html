Trichilia havanensis var. lanceolata

Trichilia havanensis var. lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf trichilia, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves, which are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its timber and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
