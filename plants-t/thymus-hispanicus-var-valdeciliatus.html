Thymus hispanicus var. valdeciliatus

Thymus hispanicus var. valdeciliatus, commonly known as Spanish thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features aromatic, small, dark green leaves that are densely covered with tiny, glandular hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its essential oils, rich in thymol and carvacrol, are valued for their medicinal and culinary uses.
