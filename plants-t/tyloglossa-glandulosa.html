Tyloglossa glandulosa

Tyloglossa glandulosa, commonly known as the hairy tongue plant, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions.

Tyloglossa glandulosa is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to the harsh climate of its native habitat.
