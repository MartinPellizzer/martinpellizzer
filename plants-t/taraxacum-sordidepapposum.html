Taraxacum sordidepapposum

Taraxacum sordidepapposum, commonly known as the dusky pappus dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and a distinctive, usually hairy pappus that aids in wind dispersal of its seeds. Native to certain regions of Europe and Asia, it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant has a rosette of basal leaves and produces a hollow, leafless stem that supports its inflorescence.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the genus Taraxacum.
