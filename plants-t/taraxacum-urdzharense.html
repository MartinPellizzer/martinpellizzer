Taraxacum urdzharense

Taraxacum urdzharense, commonly known as the Armenian dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of stiff, hairy leaves. The plant produces a hollow, hairy stem that supports a single flower head composed of yellow ray florets. Its seeds are dispersed by wind via lightweight, parachute-like structures called pappus.

The species is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing resilience to harsh conditions.
