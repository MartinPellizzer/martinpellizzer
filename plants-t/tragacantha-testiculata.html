Tragacantha testiculata

Tragacantha testiculata, commonly known as the testicle tree, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, inflated pods that resemble testes, giving it its common name. The plant produces yellow flowers and has compound leaves with small, oval leaflets. It is often found in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting well to harsh, dry conditions.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various health purposes.
