Trifolium parryi var. montanense

Trifolium parryi var. montanense, commonly known as the Sierra violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets, and its small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland and meadow habitats. It is adapted to mountainous regions and can thrive in partial to full shade.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native plant communities in its range.
