Tacsonia floribunda

Tacsonia floribunda, commonly known as the showy tacsonia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, large, violet-blue flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are slightly hairy. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in botanical gardens and private collections.
