Tournefortiopsis dependens

Tournefortiopsis dependens, commonly known as the hanging rockcress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is a small, herbaceous plant characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and slender, branching stems. The flowers are small, typically white or pale yellow, and are arranged in racemes. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

This species is notable for its ability to grow in harsh, arid environments.
