Tribulopis solandri

Tribulopis solandri, commonly known as the golden lantana, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring dense, compact growth. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, which bloom in spring and summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for coastal and sandy soils.
