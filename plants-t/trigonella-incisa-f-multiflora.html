Trigonella incisa f. multiflora

Trigonella incisa f. multiflora, commonly known as the multiflora fenugreek, is a flowering plant species belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks. Its seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a spice, similar to other members of the fenugreek genus.

The species is noted for its adaptability to wet environments and its role in ecological succession in riparian zones.
