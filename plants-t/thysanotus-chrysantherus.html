Thysanotus chrysantherus

Thysanotus chrysantherus, commonly known as the golden thread vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, cascading clusters of small, yellowish-orange flowers. The plant produces slender, thread-like stems that can climb or trail, making it suitable for hanging baskets or trellises. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The flowers bloom throughout the warm seasons, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
