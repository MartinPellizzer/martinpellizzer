Tillandsia kirchhoffiana

Tillandsia kirchhoffiana, commonly known as the Kirchhoff's air plant, is a species of epiphytic bromeliad native to Central America.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and does not require soil for growth, instead absorbing moisture and nutrients through its leaves. The plant features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that are covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help in capturing water and light. It produces striking inflorescences with bright red or orange flowers that attract pollinators.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
