Tamonea curassavica var. yucatanensis

Tamonea curassavica var. yucatanensis, commonly known as the Yucatan milkweed, is a perennial herb native to the Yucatan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, thriving in well-drained soils. It is often found in open areas such as savannas and disturbed sites.

The plant is known for its milky sap, which is a distinguishing feature of the genus Tamonea.
