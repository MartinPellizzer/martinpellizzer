Trigonella tibetica

Trigonella tibetica, commonly known as Tibetan fenugreek, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Tibetan Plateau.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces slender, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Trigonella tibetica is adapted to cold and arid environments, showcasing resilience to harsh climatic conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in the study of alpine flora and potential medicinal applications.
