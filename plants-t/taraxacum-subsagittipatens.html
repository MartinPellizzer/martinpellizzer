Taraxacum subsagittipatens

Taraxacum subsagittipatens, commonly known as the sagittate dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves that are typically lobed and have a waxy coating. The plant produces a yellow composite flower head that develops into a fluffy white pappus, aiding in seed dispersal. It is native to North America and thrives in a variety of soil types, preferring full sun.

This species is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and meadows, where it plays a role in soil stabilization and ecological succession.
