Taxillus delavayi

Taxillus delavayi, commonly known as the parasitic mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the stems of its host plants, primarily members of the Rosaceae family.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using light reflected from the host. The plant forms a dense, bushy growth with small, leathery leaves and produces white or pink flowers that attract pollinators. Its berries are red and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

Taxillus delavayi is often found in mountainous regions of China and is an important component of its ecological community.
