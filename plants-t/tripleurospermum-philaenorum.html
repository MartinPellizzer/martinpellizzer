Tripleurospermum philaenorum

Tripleurospermum philaenorum, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its showy, violet-blue petals and a distinctive, conical yellow center. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in woodland areas and along stream banks. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
