Tasmannia verticillata

Tasmannia verticillata, commonly known as the mountain pepper, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its small, clustered white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves that are often aromatic when crushed. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and rocky areas.

Tasmannia verticillata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional Aboriginal medicine.
