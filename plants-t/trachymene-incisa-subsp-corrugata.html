Trachymene incisa subsp. corrugata

Trachymene incisa subsp.

corrugata, commonly known as the serrated cut-leaf trachymene, is a herbaceous plant native to eastern Australia. It is characterized by its finely serrated, ovate to lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside.

The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in dense, terminal clusters, which bloom during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and understoreys.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting native insect populations.
