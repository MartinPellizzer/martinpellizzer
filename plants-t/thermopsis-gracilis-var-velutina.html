Thermopsis gracilis var. velutina

Thermopsis gracilis var. velutina, commonly known as the soft-hairy thermopsis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has soft, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive, velvety texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
