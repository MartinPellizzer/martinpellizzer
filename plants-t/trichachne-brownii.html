Trichachne brownii

Trichachne brownii, commonly known as the brown trichachne, is a grass species native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open grasslands or along riverbanks.

Trichachne brownii is adapted to arid environments and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats.
