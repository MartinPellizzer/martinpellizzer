Tritonia secunda

Tritonia secunda, commonly known as the common tritonia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and produces clusters of flowers during the spring and early summer. Tritonia secunda prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile addition to gardens.

Its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements have made it a popular choice among horticulturists and garden enthusiasts.
