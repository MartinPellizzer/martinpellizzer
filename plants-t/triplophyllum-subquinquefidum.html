Triplophyllum subquinquefidum

Triplophyllum subquinquefidum, commonly known as the five-lobed fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Teliophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds. The plant produces spores rather than seeds, typical of most ferns, and thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually divided into five lobes, giving it its common name.
