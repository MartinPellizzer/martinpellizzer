Tephrosia athiensis

Tephrosia athiensis, commonly known as the African blue bean, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain small, hard seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a source of protein. Tephrosia athiensis is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for agricultural practices.

Its adaptability to various soil types and climates contributes to its widespread distribution across sub-Saharan Africa.
