Tanacetum densum

Tanacetum densum, commonly known as the dense tansy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features finely divided, silvery-gray leaves that are aromatic and covered in tiny glands. Its flowers are small, yellowish, and clustered in flat-topped inflorescences, typical of the family.

Tanacetum densum is often grown as an ornamental plant for its delicate foliage and historical use in herbal medicine.
