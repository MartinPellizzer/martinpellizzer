Taraxacum unguifrons

Taraxacum unguifrons, commonly known as the broad-leaved dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, heart-shaped leaves and a bright yellow flower head that produces a pappus of white bristles for seed dispersal. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in grasslands and disturbed soils. It has a taproot system that allows it to survive in various environmental conditions.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
