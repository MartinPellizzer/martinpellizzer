Taraxia ovata

Taraxia ovata, commonly known as the narrowleaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves and bright yellow composite flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread across various habitats. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and other open areas.

Taraxia ovata is often mistaken for other hawkweed species due to its similar appearance, but it can be distinguished by its narrow leaf shape and specific floral structure.
