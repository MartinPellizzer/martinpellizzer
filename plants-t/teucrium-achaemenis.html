Teucrium achaemenis

Teucrium achaemenis, commonly known as Persian germander, is a perennial herb native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is adapted to arid climates. Its small, tubular flowers are usually blue or violet, and it produces small, four-angled fruits.

Teucrium achaemenis is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
