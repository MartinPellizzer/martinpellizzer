Tripetalum cymosum f. pendulum

Tripetalum cymosum f. pendulum, commonly known as the hanging tripetalum, is a unique variant of the tripetalum species characterized by its pendulous growth habit.

This plant features elongated, trailing stems that produce clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, and they bloom in the spring, adding a delicate ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for hanging baskets or as a ground cover.

Its botanical interest lies in its distinct morphology and adaptability to various growing conditions.
