Tilia inouei

Tilia inouei, commonly known as the Inoue's linden, is a species of flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Tiliaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in summer. The tree produces small, round fruits that are typically dispersed by wind. Its bark is smooth and light gray, and it is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.

Tilia inouei is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been studied for its potential ecological and medicinal uses.
