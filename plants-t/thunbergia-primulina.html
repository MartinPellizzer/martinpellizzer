Thunbergia primulina

Thunbergia primulina, commonly known as the small-flowered morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant is typically cultivated for its ornamental value and is often grown as a climbing vine. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Thunbergia primulina is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
