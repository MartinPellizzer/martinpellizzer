Tephrosia purpurea

Tephrosia purpurea, commonly known as purple tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its striking purple flowers are arranged in dense clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant is often used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
