Tulbaghia violacea subsp. macmasteri

Tulbaghia violacea subsp.

macmasteri, commonly known as the MacMaster's tulbaghia, is a perennial herb native to South Africa. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale purple flowers.

The plant is typically found in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly different floral structure compared to the main species.
