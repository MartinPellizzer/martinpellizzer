Tripteris dentata

Tripteris dentata, commonly known as the toothed tripteris, is a species of flowering plant in the family Tripteridaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, Colombia, and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated margins and small, inconspicuous flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in the undergrowth of tropical rainforests.

Tripteris dentata is valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
