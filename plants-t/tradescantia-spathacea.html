Tradescantia spathacea

Tradescantia spathacea, commonly known as the spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and a trailing growth habit, making it suitable for hanging baskets or ground cover. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Tradescantia spathacea is also known for its ability to purify air and is often used in ornamental gardening.
