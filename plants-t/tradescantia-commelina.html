Tradescantia commelina

Tradescantia commelina, commonly known as spiderwort, is a herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It features striking, showy flowers that range in color from blue to purple, often with a white or yellow throat. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern at the base. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Tradescantia commelina is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
