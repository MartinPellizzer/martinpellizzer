Tricholaena rosea var. setosa

Tricholaena rosea var. setosa, commonly known as the hairy bamboo or velvet bamboo, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant features long, narrow leaves covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, velvety appearance. Its stems are often segmented and can be used in traditional crafts and construction.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for both its ornamental and practical uses.
