Tephrosia punctata subsp. redheadii

Tephrosia punctata subsp.

redheadii, commonly known as Redhead's tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and savannas, thriving in well-drained soils.

It is known for its medicinal properties, with various parts used in traditional medicine for their antifungal and antimalarial effects.
