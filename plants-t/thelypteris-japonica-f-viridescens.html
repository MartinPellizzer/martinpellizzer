Thelypteris japonica f. viridescens

Thelypteris japonica f. viridescens, commonly known as the Japanese shield fern, is a fern species native to East Asia.

It features fronds that are typically leathery and bipinnate, with a distinctive dark green coloration. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
