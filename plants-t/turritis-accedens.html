Turritis accedens

Turritis accedens, commonly known as the Mexican bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing seeds.

Turritis accedens is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
