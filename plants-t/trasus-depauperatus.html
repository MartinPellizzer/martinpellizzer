Trasus depauperatus

Trasus depauperatus, commonly known as the poor man's spider plant, is a member of the Asparagaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green with a slight bluish tint. It produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are followed by blue or purple berries.

Trasus depauperatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ease of care.
