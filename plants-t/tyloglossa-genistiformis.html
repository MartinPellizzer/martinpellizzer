Tyloglossa genistiformis

Tyloglossa genistiformis, commonly known as the African broom.

This plant is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa, characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellowish-green leaves. It produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are typically visited by various pollinators. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant.

Its growth habit and appearance resemble that of the genus Genista, hence its specific epithet.
