Trisetaria loeflingiana

Trisetaria loeflingiana, commonly known as the slender false brome, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it forms dense mats or clumps. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration that helps it retain moisture in arid conditions.

Trisetaria loeflingiana is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
