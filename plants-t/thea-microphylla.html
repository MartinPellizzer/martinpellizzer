Thea microphylla

Thea microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved tea plant, is a species native to China.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white hairs. The plant is a shrub that can grow up to 1 meter in height, with a dense, compact growth habit. Its flowers are small and yellow, appearing in clusters, and it produces small, oblong fruits.

The leaves of Thea microphylla are used in the production of a unique type of tea, prized for its delicate flavor and aroma.
