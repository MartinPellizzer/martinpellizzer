Turbinicarpus saueri subsp. gonzalezii

Turbinicarpus saueri subsp.

gonzalezii, commonly known as the Gonzalez's Turbinicarpus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of northern Mexico. It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its green foliage. It is a low-maintenance plant that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
