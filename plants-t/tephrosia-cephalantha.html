Tephrosia cephalantha

Tephrosia cephalantha, commonly known as the purple crownflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking purple or violet flowers that bloom in clusters at the ends of its stems. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets arranged along a central axis.

Tephrosia cephalantha is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
