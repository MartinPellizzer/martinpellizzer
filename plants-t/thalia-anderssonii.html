Thalia anderssonii

Thalia anderssonii, commonly known as the Andes orchid, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes Mountains.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that often display vibrant colors. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous habitats, where it adapts to harsh environmental conditions. Its roots are often specialized for nutrient absorption in poor soils, and it produces small, green leaves that help in photosynthesis.

Thalia anderssonii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
