Teucrium fruticans f. grandifolium

Teucrium fruticans f. grandifolium, commonly known as large-leaved germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It features upright, branched stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The leaves are lance-shaped, serrated, and have a distinctive minty aroma. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically blue or purple in color.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
