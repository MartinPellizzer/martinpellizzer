Turanecio hypochionaeus

Turanecio hypochionaeus, commonly known as the Turkish bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trumpet-shaped blue or purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and has slender, hairy stems. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

Turanecio hypochionaeus prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable ornamental plant for gardens.
