Tamarix canescens

Tamarix canescens, commonly known as the white tamarisk, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often branching stems and small, scale-like leaves that are typically covered in a whitish, powdery coating. The plant produces fragrant, pink to purple flowers in dense clusters during the summer months. It is well adapted to arid and saline environments, making it a common species in coastal and inland desert areas.

Tamarix canescens is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, which can influence soil composition in its native habitats.
