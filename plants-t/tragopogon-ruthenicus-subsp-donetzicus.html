Tragopogon ruthenicus subsp. donetzicus

Tragopogon ruthenicus subsp.

donetzicus, commonly known as the Donetz thistle, is a perennial herb native to the steppes of Eastern Europe. It features a rosette of basal leaves that are typically grayish-green and covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces tall, sparsely branched flowering stems that bear yellow ray flowers surrounded by a series of tubular florets. Its leaves are lanceolate and have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its arid habitat.

The species is adapted to dry, open habitats and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.
