Triodia plurinervata

Triodia plurinervata, commonly known as the slender spinifex grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and wiry, needle-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing remarkable drought tolerance. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often concealed within the leaf sheaths, a feature that aids in seed dispersal.

Triodia plurinervata plays a vital role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
