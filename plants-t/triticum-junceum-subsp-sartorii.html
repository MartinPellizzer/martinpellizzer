Triticum junceum subsp. sartorii

Triticum junceum subsp.

sartorii, commonly known as the wild rye, is a species of grass native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and riverbanks. It produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with pale green to brown flowers.

This subspecies is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
