Terminalis floribunda

Terminalis floribunda, commonly known as the clustered flamebush, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, clustered inflorescences that bear bright red or orange flowers, which are highly attractive to pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a glossy surface. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to both natural and cultivated landscapes.
