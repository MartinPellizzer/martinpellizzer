Taraxacum stenoglossum

Taraxacum stenoglossum, commonly known as the narrow-lipped dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and yellow composite flowers that develop into fluffy white seed heads. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in a variety of habitats, including meadows and disturbed soils. The plant is known for its ability to spread through wind-dispersed seeds, contributing to its widespread distribution.

Its botanical features make it a notable example of the adaptability and resilience of dandelion species.
