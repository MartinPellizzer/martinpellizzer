Thymus ciliatopubescens var. poliothrix

Thymus ciliatopubescens var. poliothrix, commonly known as the purple thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features small, fragrant leaves that are typically gray-green in color and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces compact clusters of pink to purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental value make it both a useful culinary herb and a decorative plant.
