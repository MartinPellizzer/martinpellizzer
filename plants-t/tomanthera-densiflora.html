Tomanthera densiflora

Tomanthera densiflora, commonly known as the dense-flowered tamarisk, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant has small, scale-like leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

Tomanthera densiflora is adapted to tolerate extreme temperatures and low rainfall, making it a resilient species in desert ecosystems.
