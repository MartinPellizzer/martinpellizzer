Terpsichore mollissima

Terpsichore mollissima, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are soft and velvety to the touch. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Terpsichore mollissima is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.
