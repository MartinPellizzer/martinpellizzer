Thymus lenensis

Thymus lenensis, commonly known as the Lenenskaya thyme, is a small, perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its small, fragrant flowers are usually pink or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb, reflecting its historical and practical significance.
