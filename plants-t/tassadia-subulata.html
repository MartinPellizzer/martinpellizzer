Tassadia subulata

Tassadia subulata, commonly known as the slender tassadia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that are arranged in a dense rosette, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
