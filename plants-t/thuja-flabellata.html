Thuja flabellata

Thuja flabellata, commonly known as the Japanese umbrella tree, is a coniferous evergreen shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fan-shaped foliage that gives it its common name. The plant has flat, scale-like leaves that are typically a deep green color, arranged in dense, flat sprays. It produces small, spherical cones that contain seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Thuja flabellata thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for landscaping.
