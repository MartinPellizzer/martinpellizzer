Thelymitra inflata

Thelymitra inflata, commonly known as the inflated broad-leafed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its broad, fleshy leaves and produces striking, pale blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as rainforests and understoreys. Its flowers are borne on tall, slender stems and are often visited by bees and other pollinators.

Thelymitra inflata is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
