Taraxacum aksaicum

Taraxacum aksaicum, commonly known as the Aksai dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and a hollow, leafy stem. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that develop into fluffy white seeds with parachute-like structures for wind dispersal. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
