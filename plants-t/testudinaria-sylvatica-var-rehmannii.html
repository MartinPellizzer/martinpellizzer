Testudinaria sylvatica var. rehmannii

Testudinaria sylvatica var. rehmannii, commonly known as the Rehmann's wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves contain oxalic acid, which can be toxic if ingested in large quantities.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
