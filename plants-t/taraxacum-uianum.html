Taraxacum uianum

Taraxacum uianum, commonly known as the yellow hawk's-beard, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family (Compositae).

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by a distinctive seed head that disperses seeds via the wind. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are basal, forming a rosette and are usually hairy and lobed.

Taraxacum uianum is a perennial plant, known for its resilience and ability to colonize various habitats.
