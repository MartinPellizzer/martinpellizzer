Thrinax acuminata

Thrinax acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-pointed palmetto, is a species of palm native to the Caribbean, particularly Cuba and the Bahamas.

It is a small, evergreen palm that typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 meters. The plant has a short trunk and is characterized by its tightly clustered, fan-shaped leaves. Its leaves are dark green, leathery, and have a sharp, spiny appearance, giving it its common name.

Thrinax acuminata is adapted to coastal and sandy environments, making it a hardy plant in tropical and subtropical regions.
