Teucrium maderense

Teucrium maderense, commonly known as the Madeira germander, is a flowering plant native to the island of Madeira.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant features square, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are often grayish-green in color. Its small, tubular flowers are typically purple or blue, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the branches.

Teucrium maderense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
