Thelymitra brevifolia

Thelymitra brevifolia, commonly known as the short-leaved sun orchid, is a small terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It typically grows in heathlands and open forests, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features a slender, leafless stem that bears a single, showy greenish-yellow flower with a distinctive red lip. Its flowering period occurs during the late winter to early spring, often in response to seasonal rainfall.

Thelymitra brevifolia is an important component of its native ecosystem and is appreciated for its ornamental value.
