Thymus albiflorus

Thymus albiflorus, commonly known as whiteflower thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, which contribute to its use in herbal medicine and culinary applications.

Thymus albiflorus thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and borders.
