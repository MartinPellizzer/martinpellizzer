Tongoloa filicaudicis

Tongoloa filicaudicis, commonly known as the long-tailed orchid, is a unique member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive elongated, trailing stems that can grow several meters in length. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of white and yellow. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in shaded, moist environments.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental gardening and botanical research.
