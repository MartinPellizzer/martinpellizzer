Tragia triangularis

Tragia triangularis, commonly known as the triangular tragia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its triangular-shaped leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Tragia triangularis is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
