Trifolium reflexum var. mexicanum

Trifolium reflexum var. mexicanum, commonly known as Mexican clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Mexico.

It typically grows in open, sunny areas such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant features trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets with a hairy texture. Its small, pinkish-white flowers bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months.

It is often used in ornamental gardening and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
