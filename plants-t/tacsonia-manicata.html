Tacsonia manicata

Tacsonia manicata, commonly known as the Mexican spider plant, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white and green markings. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in summer, followed by blue-black berries. It is a low-maintenance plant that thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade.

Tacsonia manicata is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive appearance and ease of care.
