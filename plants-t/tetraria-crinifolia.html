Tetraria crinifolia

Tetraria crinifolia, commonly known as the featherleaf tetraria, is a unique member of the Poaceae family, characterized by its distinctive, feathery inflorescence.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. The plant features slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that contribute to its ornamental appeal. Its small, clustered flowers are usually inconspicuous, with the visual interest coming from the delicate, branching structure of the inflorescence.

Tetraria crinifolia is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value and ability to thrive in low-maintenance conditions.
