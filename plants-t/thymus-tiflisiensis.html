Thymus tiflisiensis

Thymus tiflisiensis, commonly known as the Tbilisi thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and aromatic, small, grey-green leaves. The plant produces tiny, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring, typically from April to June. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

Thymus tiflisiensis is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
