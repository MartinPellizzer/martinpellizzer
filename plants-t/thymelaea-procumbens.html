Thymelaea procumbens

Thymelaea procumbens, commonly known as the prostrate thymelaea, is a low-growing shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. The plant has hairy, elliptical leaves that are often arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Thymelaea procumbens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
