Tylophora conspicua

Tylophora conspicua, commonly known as the Chinese bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing shrub that can grow up to several meters in length, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters. Its flowers are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous small seeds.

Tylophora conspicua is valued in traditional medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
