Thrixspermum rhopalorrhachis

Thrixspermum rhopalorrhachis, commonly known as the woolly thrixspermum, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family and is native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly covering on its stems and leaves, which helps to reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in cyathia, a typical feature of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its leaves are opposite and often have a leathery texture, adapted for drought resistance.

Thrixspermum rhopalorrhachis is often used in succulent gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
