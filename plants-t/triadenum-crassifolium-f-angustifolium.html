Triadenum crassifolium f. angustifolium

Triadenum crassifolium f. angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved triadenum, is a variant of the broader-leaved triadenum species.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of North America. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink in color.

This subspecies is adapted to grow in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments.
