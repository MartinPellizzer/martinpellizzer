Tyrimnus

Tyrimnus, commonly known as the silverleaf vine, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and distinctive silvery-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of white and purple. Tyrimnus thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

It is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and adaptability to harsh climates.
