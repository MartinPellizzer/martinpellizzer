Trifolium glanduliferum var. nervulosum

Trifolium glanduliferum var. nervulosum, commonly known as the hairy clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically hairy and have a serrated margin. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats.

This variety is noted for its glandular structures, which contribute to its distinctive appearance and ecological role.
