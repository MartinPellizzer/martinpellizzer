Tococa juruensis

Tococa juruensis, commonly known as the Amazonian cocoa tree, is a member of the Sterculiaceae family and is native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces pods containing bitter seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a source of caffeine.

Its botanical significance lies in its role in the ecosystem and its potential for sustainable agricultural use.
