Trigonella khalkhalica

Trigonella khalkhalica, commonly known as Persian fenugreek, is a flowering plant native to Iran.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, adapting to harsh environmental conditions. Its small yellow flowers bloom in early spring, followed by the development of elongated, hairy pods containing seeds.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
