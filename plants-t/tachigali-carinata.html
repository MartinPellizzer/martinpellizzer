Tachigali carinata

Tachigali carinata, commonly known as the Brazilian tachigali, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and often nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The tree has simple, alternate, and often elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. Its flowers are typically small and clustered in inflorescences, with a distinctive yellow or cream coloration.

The fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds, contributing to its ecological role in seed dispersal and forest regeneration.
