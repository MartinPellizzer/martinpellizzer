Tamarix sultanii

Tamarix sultanii, commonly known as the Persian tamarisk, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its dense, feathery foliage and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in dry, saline soils. Its leaves are small and scale-like, reducing water loss in hot, windy environments.

Tamarix sultanii is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
