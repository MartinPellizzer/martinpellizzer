Trophis aculeata

Trophis aculeata, commonly known as the spiny poinsettia, is a tropical shrub native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble petals, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically white or green. Its stems are woody and covered in sharp, spiny structures, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
