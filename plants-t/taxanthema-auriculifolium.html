Taxanthema auriculifolium

Taxanthema auriculifolium, commonly known as the auriculate taxanthema, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of central Australia.

It is a small, annual herb with distinctive auriculate (ear-shaped) leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which attract pollinators such as bees and flies. Its roots are adapted to store water, allowing it to survive prolonged droughts.

Taxanthema auriculifolium plays a minor but important role in stabilizing sandy soils and supporting local biodiversity.
