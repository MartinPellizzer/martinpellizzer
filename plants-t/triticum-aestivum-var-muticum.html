Triticum aestivum var. muticum

Triticum aestivum var. muticum, commonly known as the wild rye, is a variant of wheat that exhibits distinctive botanical features.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its robust growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. The plant produces dense, tufted inflorescences with spikelets that are typically pale green to reddish in color. Its leaves are long, narrow, and have a rough texture, while the stems are sturdy and often branched.

This species is valued for its ecological role in soil stabilization and as a forage crop.
