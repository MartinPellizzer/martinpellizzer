Taraxacum appositum

Taraxacum appositum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the dandelion family (Compositae) and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that develop into fluffy seeds with white pappus, aiding in wind dispersal. It thrives in disturbed soils and can often be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is considered an invasive species in some regions.
