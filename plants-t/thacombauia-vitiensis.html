Thacombauia vitiensis

Thacombauia vitiensis, commonly known as the Vitiensis tree, is a species native to the tropical regions of the Pacific, particularly in parts of Fiji.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, featuring a straight trunk and a broad, rounded crown. The leaves are simple, alternate, and typically have a smooth, glossy surface. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
