Tabernaemontana robinsonii

Tabernaemontana robinsonii, commonly known as the Robinson's star jasmine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its fragrant, star-shaped white flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves and a woody stem structure, making it a striking ornamental plant. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate to high levels of sunlight.

Due to its beauty and fragrance, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
