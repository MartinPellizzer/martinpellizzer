Thunbergia longifolia

Thunbergia longifolia, commonly known as blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped, dark green leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Thunbergia longifolia is a climbing vine that can reach several meters in length, making it suitable for trellises or as a ground cover.
