Tanacetum demetrii

Tanacetum demetrii, commonly known as the Demetrius tansy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features finely divided, aromatic leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a long history of use in traditional herbal medicine.

Tanacetum demetrii is also known for its strong, camphor-like scent, which can be both pleasant and deterrent to pests.
