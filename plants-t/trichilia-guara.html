Trichilia guara

Trichilia guara, commonly known as the yellow tallow tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae, which includes mahogany and related species. The plant is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and large, alternate leaves that are often trifoliate or pinnate. Its flowers are small and yellow, typically clustered in panicles, and it produces woody capsules containing flat, winged seeds.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood and is also used in traditional medicine.
