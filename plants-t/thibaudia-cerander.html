Thibaudia cerander

Thibaudia cerander, commonly known as the bluebell of the genus Thibaudia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in open woodland and grassland habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Thibaudia cerander is a perennial herb with slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and conservation efforts.
