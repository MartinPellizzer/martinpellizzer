Tragopogon angustissimus

Tragopogon angustissimus, commonly known as narrowleaf thistle, is a perennial plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Tragopogon angustissimus is also noted for its ability to hybridize with other Tragopogon species, contributing to its ecological and botanical significance.
