Taraxacum maeandriforme

Taraxacum maeandriforme, commonly known as the Maeandrian dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the dandelion genus, which is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads. The plant features a rosette of basal, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and form a single composite head, which later develops into a fluffy seed sphere.

This species is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
