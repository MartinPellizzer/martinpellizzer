Thrincia serotina

Thrincia serotina, commonly known as the late-flowering thrincia, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. The plant features slender stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its small, yellowish-green flowers appear in late summer to early autumn, contributing to its common name.

Thrincia serotina is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
