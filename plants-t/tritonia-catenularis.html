Tritonia catenularis

Tritonia catenularis, commonly known as the chain orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, cascading inflorescences. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink, often arranged in a linear pattern along the stem. Tritonia catenularis thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its unique appearance and fragrance make it a sought-after ornamental plant in horticulture.
