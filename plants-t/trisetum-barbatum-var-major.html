Trisetum barbatum var. major

Trisetum barbatum var. major, commonly known as the tall hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, grass-like foliage that often forms clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, spikelet-based flowers. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and along stream banks.

Its fibrous roots help stabilize soil, making it an important plant in wetland ecosystems.
