Trichocentrum johnii

Trichocentrum johnii, commonly known as the John's orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, pendulous flowers that are typically white or pale pink. The plant has long, slender pseudobulbs and linear leaves that help it thrive in humid, shaded environments. Trichocentrum johnii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.

Its growth habit and floral display make it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
