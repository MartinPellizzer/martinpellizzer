Teucrium polium subsp. helichrysoides

Teucrium polium subsp.

helichrysoides, commonly known as creeping germander, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It features aromatic, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes. It is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.

Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for low-maintenance landscaping.
