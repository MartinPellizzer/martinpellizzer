Teucrium scorodonia subsp. siculum

Teucrium scorodonia subsp.

siculum, commonly known as Sicilian germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple, arranged in spikes. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to arid environments and suitable for xeriscaping.
