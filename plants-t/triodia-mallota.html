Triodia mallota

Triodia mallota, commonly known as the spinifex grass, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its dense, wiry growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with deep root systems that help it survive in dry conditions.

Triodia mallota plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various desert organisms.
