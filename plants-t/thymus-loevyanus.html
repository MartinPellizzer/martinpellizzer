Thymus loevyanus

Thymus loevyanus, commonly known as Loevy's thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, which bloom in the spring and summer. Its leaves are small, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy texture.

Thymus loevyanus is often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb due to its aromatic properties.
