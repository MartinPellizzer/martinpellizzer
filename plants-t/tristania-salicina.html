Tristania salicina

Tristania salicina, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or red, which bloom in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Tristania salicina is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
