Tabebuia polymorpha

Tabebuia polymorpha, commonly known as the Flame Tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The tree has a straight trunk and a broad, spreading canopy, with smooth, grey bark that peels in thin layers. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, turning yellow before falling in the dry season.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often planted in parks and gardens for its vibrant floral display.
