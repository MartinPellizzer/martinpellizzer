Thymus pulegioides var. istriacus

Thymus pulegioides var. istriacus, commonly known as Istrian thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Istrian Peninsula of Croatia.

It features compact, aromatic foliage with small, dark green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces pink to purple flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

This variety is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, as well as its ornamental appeal in gardens.
