Taraxacum curtifrons

Taraxacum curtifrons, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, spoon-shaped leaves and a yellow composite flower head that develops into a fluffy seed sphere. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant is known for its medicinal properties, with the leaves used in traditional remedies.

Its life cycle includes a period of dormancy during winter, with flowering typically occurring in spring and early summer.
