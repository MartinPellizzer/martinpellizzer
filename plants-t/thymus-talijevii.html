Thymus talijevii

Thymus talijevii, commonly known as the Talijevi thyme, is a species of flowering plant in the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is native to the Balkan Peninsula, particularly found in regions of Serbia and Bosnia and Herzegovina. This low-growing perennial herb features small, fragrant leaves and pink or purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, woody stems and is often found in dry, rocky or limestone soils.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
