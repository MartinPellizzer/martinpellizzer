Thalictrum controversum subsp. montanum

Thalictrum controversum subsp.

montanum, commonly known as mountain meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America. It features delicate, fern-like leaves and produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in open woodlands or meadows. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
