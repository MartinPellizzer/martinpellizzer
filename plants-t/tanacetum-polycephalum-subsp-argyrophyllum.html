Tanacetum polycephalum subsp. argyrophyllum

Tanacetum polycephalum subsp.

argyrophyllum, commonly known as silverleaf tansy, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its finely divided, silver-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green composite flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine, it is cultivated in gardens and traditional practices.
