Taxotrophis zeylanica

Taxotrophis zeylanica, commonly known as the Ceylon sandalwood, is a small evergreen tree native to Sri Lanka.

It is characterized by its aromatic wood, which is highly valued for its fragrance and medicinal properties. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth texture. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by dark blue-black berries.

The species is also known for its role in traditional medicine and its ecological importance in its native habitat.
