Thryptomene oligandra var. parviflora

Thryptomene oligandra var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered thryptomene, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalypts and tea trees. The plant typically has narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture. Its small white or pale pink flowers are borne in clusters and bloom during spring.

It is often found in dry, open habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
