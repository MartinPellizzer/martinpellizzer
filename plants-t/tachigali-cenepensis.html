Tachigali cenepensis

Tachigali cenepensis, commonly known as the yellow tachigali, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with long, slender leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemose inflorescences. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds, often used in traditional medicine.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
