Trifolium dolopium

Trifolium dolopium, commonly known as the Dolopian clover, is a flowering plant species belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged symmetrically. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows and grassy areas.

Trifolium dolopium is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
