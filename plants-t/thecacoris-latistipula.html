Thecacoris latistipula

Thecacoris latistipula, commonly known as the broad-tipped cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family and is native to arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are often ribbed and covered with spines. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers during the warmer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss and adapting to its harsh environment.

Thecacoris latistipula is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in dry conditions.
