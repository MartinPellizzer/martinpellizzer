Trifolium medium subsp. banaticum

Trifolium medium subsp.

banaticum, commonly known as the Banat white clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southeastern Europe. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged on a central stem.

The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands, meadows, and along roadsides.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological and agricultural systems.
