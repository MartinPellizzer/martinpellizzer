Tecomanthe acutifolia

Tecomanthe acutifolia, commonly known as the sharp-leaved tecomanthe, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Mexico and Central America.

It features heart-shaped, glossy, dark green leaves with serrated edges, and its flowers are typically yellow with a red or purple throat. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible and have a tangy flavor. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage.

Tecomanthe acutifolia is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
