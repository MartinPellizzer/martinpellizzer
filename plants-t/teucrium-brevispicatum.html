Teucrium brevispicatum

Teucrium brevispicatum, commonly known as narrow-headed germander, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features slender, branched stems and small, lance-shaped leaves with a grayish-green hue. Its flowers are usually pale blue to violet, arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
