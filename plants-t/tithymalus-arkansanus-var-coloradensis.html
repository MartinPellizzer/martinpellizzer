Tithymalus arkansanus var. coloradensis

Tithymalus arkansanus var. coloradensis, commonly known as the Colorado tithymalus, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing adaptability to harsh conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The flowering period occurs in the summer months, contributing to its ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
