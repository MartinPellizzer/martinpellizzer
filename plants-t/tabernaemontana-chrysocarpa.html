Tabernaemontana chrysocarpa

Tabernaemontana chrysocarpa, commonly known as the golden fruit tabernaemontana, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its attractive, fragrant white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, yellow-orange fruits that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a leathery texture.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
