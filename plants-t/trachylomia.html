Trachylomia

Trachylomia, commonly known as the bluebell or bluebell orchid, is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family.

These plants are native to Australia and are characterized by their striking blue or violet flowers. Trachylomia species typically have narrow, grass-like leaves and produce clusters of small, bell-shaped blooms. They are often found in sandy or rocky soils in coastal regions.

The genus includes several species, each with unique flower colors and growth habits.
