Taraxacum aemulans

Taraxacum aemulans, commonly known as the mimic dandelion, is a flowering plant in the dandelion family (Asteraceae).

It is native to Europe and has been introduced to other parts of the world, including North America. The plant is notable for its ability to mimic the appearance of other dandelion species, which can make it difficult to distinguish from its relatives. It produces small, yellow composite flowers that develop into fluffy white seed heads, similar to those of other Taraxacum species.

The plant thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and gardens.
