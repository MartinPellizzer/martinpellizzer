Trifolium medium subsp. sarosiense

Trifolium medium subsp.

sarosiense, commonly known as the Sarosian clover, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically heart-shaped with a slightly hairy surface.

The plant produces small, pink to reddish-purple flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
