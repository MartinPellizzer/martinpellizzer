Trichomanes reniforme

Trichomanes reniforme, commonly known as the kidney fern, is a member of the family Hymenophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds.

It is a small, epiphytic fern that thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant's fronds are usually kidney-shaped, with a distinctive lobed structure that gives it its common name. It has a short, creeping rhizome and produces spores in small, clustered structures called sori.

Trichomanes reniforme is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in the right conditions.
