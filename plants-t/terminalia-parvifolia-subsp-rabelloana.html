Terminalia parvifolia subsp. rabelloana

Terminalia parvifolia subsp.

rabelloana, commonly known as the Rabelloan Terminalia, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Africa. It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape.

The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by small, round drupes that are often red or orange when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
