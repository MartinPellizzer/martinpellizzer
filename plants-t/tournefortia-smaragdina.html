Tournefortia smaragdina

Tournefortia smaragdina, commonly known as the Emerald Tournefortia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a glossy, dark green appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
