Taraxacum atactum

Taraxacum atactum, commonly known as the narrowleaf dandelion, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy underside. Its flower head consists of yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular florets, which develop into fluffy white seeds.

The plant is adapted to dry, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.
