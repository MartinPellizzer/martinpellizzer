Tilia collivaga

Tilia collivaga, commonly known as the lesser lime tree, is a deciduous tree native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Tiliaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, yellowish-white flowers. The bark is smooth and grayish-brown, often developing shallow fissures with age. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
