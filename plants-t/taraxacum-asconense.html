Taraxacum asconense

Taraxacum asconense, commonly known as the asconense dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of stiff, hairy leaves and a hollow, leafless stem that supports a single flower head. The flower head consists of both ray and disk florets, which develop into the familiar white, fluffy seeds with parachute-like structures. Native to southern Europe, it thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically significant species within its native habitat.
