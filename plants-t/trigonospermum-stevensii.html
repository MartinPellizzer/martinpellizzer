Trigonospermum stevensii

Trigonospermum stevensii, commonly known as the yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Trigonospermum stevensii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
