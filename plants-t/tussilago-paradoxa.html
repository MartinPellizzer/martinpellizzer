Tussilago paradoxa

Tussilago paradoxa, commonly known as the paradoxical lungwort, is a rare and unusual member of the Plantaginaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, rosette-like arrangement of velvety, silver-gray leaves with purple markings. The plant produces clusters of small, pale purple to white flowers that bloom in late summer. Native to the high elevations of the Appalachian Mountains, it thrives in moist, rocky habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
