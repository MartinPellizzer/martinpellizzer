Tragacantha sphaerocalyx

Tragacantha sphaerocalyx, commonly known as the African milk bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and dense clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that is used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive. Its leaves are often used for thatching and as a source of fiber.

Tragacantha sphaerocalyx thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing remarkable drought tolerance.
