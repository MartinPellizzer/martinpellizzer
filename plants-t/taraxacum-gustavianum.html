Taraxacum gustavianum

Taraxacum gustavianum, commonly known as the Swedish dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by a rosette of basal, spoon-shaped leaves and a hollow, leafless stem. The plant produces a single, yellow composite flower head that develops into a ball of white, fluffy seeds. Native to northern Europe, it thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
