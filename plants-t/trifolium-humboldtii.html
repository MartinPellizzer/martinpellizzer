Trifolium humboldtii

Trifolium humboldtii, commonly known as Humboldt's clover, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in regions of Chile and Argentina.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

Trifolium humboldtii is adapted to grow in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas and high-altitude environments.
