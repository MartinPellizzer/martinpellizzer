Tepualia stipularis var. patagonica

Tepualia stipularis var. patagonica, commonly known as the Patagonian sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that thrives in sandy and rocky soils. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, which bloom during the spring and summer months. It has a compact growth habit with hairy leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

This species is well adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient plant in its natural habitat.
