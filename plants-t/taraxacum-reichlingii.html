Taraxacum reichlingii

Taraxacum reichlingii, commonly known as the Reichling's dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves and a long, hollow stem that supports a single, bright yellow flower head. The plant produces a distinctive seed head composed of numerous parachute-like seeds, which are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Taraxacum reichlingii is adapted to cold climates and is typically found at high elevations.
