Tradescantia anomala

Tradescantia anomala, commonly known as the snake plant or mother-in-law's tongue, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features upright, sword-shaped leaves that are typically green with distinctive red or purple margins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers on slender stalks during the spring and summer months. It is a hardy species that thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight.

Tradescantia anomala is also known for its ability to purify indoor air, making it a popular choice for houseplants.
