Tibouchina castellensis

Tibouchina castellensis, commonly known as the Cape Myrtle, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, featuring dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers during the summer months, which are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a leathery texture, while its bark is smooth and reddish-brown.

Tibouchina castellensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited to warm, humid climates.
