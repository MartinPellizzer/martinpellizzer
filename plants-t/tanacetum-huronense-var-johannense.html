Tanacetum huronense var. johannense

Tanacetum huronense var. johannense, commonly known as the Huronense golden marguerite, is a perennial herb native to Ontario, Canada.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white petals. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as marshes and wet meadows. It produces small, herbaceous leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
