Taraxacum lasianthum

Taraxacum lasianthum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and a bright yellow flower head that develops into a pappus-equipped seed head. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this plant thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. It is often found in meadows, roadsides, and other sunny areas.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
