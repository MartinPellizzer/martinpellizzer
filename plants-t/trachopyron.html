Trachopyron

Trachopyron, commonly known as the spiny goosefoot, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Chenopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its succulent, often spiny leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plants are typically found in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Trachopyron species are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
