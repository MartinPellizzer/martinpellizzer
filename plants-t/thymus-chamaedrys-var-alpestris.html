Thymus chamaedrys var. alpestris

Thymus chamaedrys var. alpestris, commonly known as alpine thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It features small, gray-green leaves and produces tiny, pinkish-white flowers in early summer. The plant is characterized by its aromatic foliage, which contains essential oils responsible for its distinctive fragrance. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Due to its hardiness, it is well-suited for alpine and subalpine environments.
