Tragacantha schurabica

Tragacantha schurabica, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a flowering plant native to regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody shrub structure with compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by the formation of pods containing seeds.

Tragacantha schurabica is notable for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments, making it an important species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
