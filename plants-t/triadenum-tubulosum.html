Triadenum tubulosum

Triadenum tubulosum, commonly known as the slender bladderwort, is a small aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its tiny, bladder-like structures called bladders, which are used to trap small aquatic organisms. The plant typically grows in shallow, still waters and has thin, branching stems with small, scale-like leaves. Its reproductive structures are inconspicuous, and it relies on water for dispersal of its propagules.

Triadenum tubulosum plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for microorganisms.
