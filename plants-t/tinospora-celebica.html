Tinospora celebica

Tinospora celebica, commonly known as Java Creeper, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Tinospora celebica is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
