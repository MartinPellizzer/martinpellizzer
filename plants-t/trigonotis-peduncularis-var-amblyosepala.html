Trigonotis peduncularis var. amblyosepala

Trigonotis peduncularis var. amblyosepala, commonly known as the slender bellflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, ascending stems and small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pale blue or white. The leaves are lanceolate and arranged alternately along the stem, contributing to its delicate appearance. This variety is distinguished by its reduced sepals, which give it the specific epithet *amblyosepala*.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.
