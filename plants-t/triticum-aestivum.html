Triticum aestivum

Triticum aestivum, commonly known as wheat, is a major cereal grain species belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its hollow, cylindrical stems and spike-like inflorescence composed of small, two-lobed flowers. The plant produces edible grains enclosed in tough, inedible husks, which are harvested and processed into flour. Wheat is a staple crop worldwide, valued for its nutritional content and versatility in food production.

Its growth cycle includes stages such as germination, tillering, heading, and grain filling, which are influenced by environmental conditions.
