Taraxacum atrocarpum

Taraxacum atrocarpum, commonly known as the black-fruited dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers and heart-shaped basal leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple to black fruits that are distinct from the more common yellow dandelions. It is native to Europe and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in herbal remedies.
