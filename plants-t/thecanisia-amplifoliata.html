Thecanisia amplifoliata

Thecanisia amplifoliata, commonly known as the broad-leaved canisia, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, lance-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Due to its unique foliage and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
