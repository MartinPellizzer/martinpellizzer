Trifolium × prostii

Trifolium × prostii, commonly known as the red clover hybrid, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets, often with a white or pale violet marking on the central leaflet. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers arranged in spherical or clustered heads, typical of the genus Trifolium. It thrives in well-drained soils and is commonly found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed areas.

This hybrid species is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agricultural and ecological restoration contexts.
