Taraxacum oxyodon

Taraxacum oxyodon, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, elongated leaves and a single, hollow stem that supports a flowering head. The plant produces small, yellow composite flowers that develop into fluffy, white seed heads. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats.

It is a perennial plant with a taproot system and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
