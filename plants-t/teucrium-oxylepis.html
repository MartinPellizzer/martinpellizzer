Teucrium oxylepis

Teucrium oxylepis, commonly known as the slender germander, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features slender stems and small, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are usually blue to purple in color, arranged in spikes, and bloom during the warmer months.

Teucrium oxylepis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
