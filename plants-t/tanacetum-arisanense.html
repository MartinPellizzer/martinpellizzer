Tanacetum arisanense

Tanacetum arisanense, commonly known as the Japanese tansy, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, yellowish-green leaves and small, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Tanacetum arisanense is also noted for its role in ecological systems, supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
