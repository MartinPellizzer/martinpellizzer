Tessmanniophoenix longibracteata

Tessmanniophoenix longibracteata, commonly known as the Longibracteate Date Palm, is a rare and striking member of the Arecaceae family.

Native to the island of Socotra, it is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and feathery, pinnate leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, date-like fruits. It thrives in arid environments and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and limited water availability.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is considered a valuable species for botanical study and conservation efforts.
