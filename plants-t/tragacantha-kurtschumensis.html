Tragacantha kurtschumensis

Tragacantha kurtschumensis, commonly known as the Kurtschum's tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant produces a resinous substance, similar to other tragacanth species, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a thickening agent. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Due to its ecological and potential medicinal significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
