Trifolium hybridum subsp. anatolicum

Trifolium hybridum subsp.

anatolicum, commonly known as Anatolian hybrid clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkans and Anatolia. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval to lance-shaped leaflets, and small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along streambanks. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.

Due to its adaptability, it can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a resilient species in its native range.
