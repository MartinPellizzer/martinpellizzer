Tragia leptophylla

Tragia leptophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tragia, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a shrub or perennial herb characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It is adapted to survive in dry conditions with deep roots and succulent stems.

Tragia leptophylla is often found in rocky soils and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
