Townsendia grandiflora

Townsendia grandiflora, commonly known as the showy Townsend's flower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats, often in alpine or subalpine regions. It has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and hairy, and its stems are usually unbranched.

The flowers bloom in early summer and are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects.
