Tragacantha edulis

Tragacantha edulis, commonly known as the edible tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems. The plant produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. Its roots are also valued for their gelatinous substance, which has been historically used as a thickening agent.

Tragacantha edulis is adapted to arid environments, making it a resilient species in dry climates.
