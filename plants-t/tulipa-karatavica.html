Tulipa karatavica

Tulipa karatavica, commonly known as the Karatavica tulip, is a species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from pale yellow to deep orange. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces bulbs that store nutrients for the next growing season.

This tulip is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
