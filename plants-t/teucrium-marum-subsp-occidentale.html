Teucrium marum subsp. occidentale

Teucrium marum subsp.

occidentale, commonly known as Western germander, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and opposite leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or streamside habitats. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink to purple and bloom in summer.

It is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
