Trochetia blackburniana

Trochetia blackburniana, commonly known as the blackburnian heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It features dense, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and glossy, often with a slight pubescence. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or reddish in color, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires a cool, moist climate.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and specialized horticultural settings.
