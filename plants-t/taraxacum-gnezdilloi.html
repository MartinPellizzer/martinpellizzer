Taraxacum gnezdilloi

Taraxacum gnezdilloi, commonly known as the Gnezdilloi dandelion, is a rare and endemic species native to specific regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are basal, with a rough texture and a distinctive white underside.
