Tabebuia perfae

Tabebuia perfae, commonly known as the pink trumpet tree, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to reddish-orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The tree has a straight trunk and a broad, spreading canopy with pinnate leaves. Its bark is smooth and often has a reddish or brownish hue.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often used in landscaping and as a source of timber.
