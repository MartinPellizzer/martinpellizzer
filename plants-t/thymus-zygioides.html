Thymus zygioides

Thymus zygioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf thyme, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with aromatic, gray-green foliage and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and fragrant leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its essential oils, rich in thymol, are used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine.
