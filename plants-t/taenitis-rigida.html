Taenitis rigida

Taenitis rigida, commonly known as the rigid taenitis, is a tropical plant belonging to the family Pandanaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its stiff, sword-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and as a natural fiber. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense clumps in moist, shaded environments.

Taenitis rigida is valued for its ornamental appeal and sustainable use in local communities.
