Toddalia bilocularis

Toddalia bilocularis, commonly known as the two-chambered tamarind, is a deciduous tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pods. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by elongated, woody pods containing seeds. Toddalia bilocularis is valued for its hard, durable wood and has been used traditionally for construction and furniture-making.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
