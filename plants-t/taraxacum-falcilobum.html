Taraxacum falcilobum

Taraxacum falcilobum, commonly known as the sickle-leaved dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of dark green, sickle-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces a single, hollow, yellow-orange flower head that blooms in the spring and summer. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats.

The plant is often found in meadows, roadsides, and other sunny, well-drained areas.
