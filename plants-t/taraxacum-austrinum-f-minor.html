Taraxacum austrinum f. minor

Taraxacum austrinum f. minor, commonly known as the lesser dandelion, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its basal rosette of stiff, hairy leaves and a hollow, leafless stem that supports a single flower head. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that develop into fluffy white seeds with parachute-like structures for wind dispersal. It is native to Europe and is often found in disturbed soils, meadows, and along roadsides.

The species is notable for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
