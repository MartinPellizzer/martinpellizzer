Teucrium pictum

Teucrium pictum, commonly known as blue germander, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically blue to purple in color, appearing in whorled clusters at the ends of the branches.

Teucrium pictum is often found in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and streambanks.
