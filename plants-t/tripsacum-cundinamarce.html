Tripsacum cundinamarce

Tripsacum cundinamarce, commonly known as the Cundinamarca panicgrass, is a species of grass native to South America, particularly Colombia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-based flowers, which are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in open fields and disturbed soils.

Tripsacum cundinamarce is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional practices in its native regions.
