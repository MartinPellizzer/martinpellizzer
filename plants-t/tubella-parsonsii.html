Tubella parsonsii

Tubella parsonsii, commonly known as the Parsons' tubeworm, is a species of polychaete annelid found in marine environments.

It is characterized by its segmented body and a distinctive cylindrical shape with a series of tubular structures along its length. The plant, or more accurately the organism, thrives in sandy or muddy substrates in shallow coastal waters. Its body is covered in fine, bristle-like setae that aid in locomotion and feeding.

Tubella parsonsii plays a role in marine ecosystems by contributing to nutrient cycling and serving as prey for larger marine animals.
