Trichosanthes cucumerina subsp. cucumerina

Trichosanthes cucumerina subsp.

cucumerina, commonly known as the Chinese snake gourd, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia. It produces large, elongated, green fruits that can grow up to 1 meter in length.

The plant has heart-shaped leaves with prominent veins and bears yellow flowers with five lobes. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruits.

The fruit's skin is thick and fibrous, while the interior contains seeds and a sweet, starchy pulp.
