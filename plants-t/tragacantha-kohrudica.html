Tragacantha kohrudica

Tragacantha kohrudica, commonly known as the Kohrud tragacanth, is a perennial shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Iran.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by pods that contain angular, hard seeds. It is adapted to survive in dry climates, with deep roots that help it access groundwater.

Tragacantha kohrudica is also valued for its gum, which has been traditionally used in various medicinal and industrial applications.
