Trichosterigma fulgens

Trichosterigma fulgens, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow to orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, making it a visually prominent species. The plant has compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets arranged in pairs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Trichosterigma fulgens is also noted for its ability to grow in a variety of soil types, though it prefers well-drained conditions.
