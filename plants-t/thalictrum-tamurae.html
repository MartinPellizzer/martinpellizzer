Thalictrum tamurae

Thalictrum tamurae, commonly known as the Japanese meadow rue, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in a compound umbel, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Thalictrum tamurae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
