Trifolium variegatum f. morleyanum

Trifolium variegatum f. morleyanum, commonly known as the Morley's clover, is a variant of the variable clover species.

It is characterized by its distinctive variegated leaf patterns, which include white or pale green markings on the leaves. This form is typically found in moist, shaded environments, often in woodland or grassy areas. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers that bloom in early spring.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in horticulture and botanical studies.
