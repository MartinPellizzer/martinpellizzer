Tetrapterys lucida f. glandulifera

Tetrapterys lucida f. glandulifera, commonly known as the glandular-leaf tetrapterys, is a variant of the tetrapterys genus native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive, glandular-dotted leaves that give it a unique appearance. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to several meters. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
