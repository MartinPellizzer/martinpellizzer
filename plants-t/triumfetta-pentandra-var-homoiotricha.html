Triumfetta pentandra var. homoiotricha

Triumfetta pentandra var. homoiotricha, commonly known as the African morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of purple and pink, which bloom in the morning. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain small, smooth seeds.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance.
