Thalictrum uncinatum

Thalictrum uncinatum, commonly known as hooked meadow rue, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, compound leaves and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. Native to North America, it typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream sides. The plant has a tall, unbranched stem that can reach up to 2 meters in height.

Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and it produces follicles containing numerous seeds.
