Toxicodendron excisum

Toxicodendron excisum, commonly known as the smooth sumac, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with 11 to 31 leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by red, fuzzy berries that are a food source for birds. It is known for its toxic sap, which can cause skin irritation in humans.

The species is often found in open woods, clearings, and along stream banks.
