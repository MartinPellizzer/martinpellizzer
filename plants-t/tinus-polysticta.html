Tinus polysticta

Tinus polysticta, commonly known as the silver-leafed spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of white, powdery wax, giving them a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Due to its ornamental value and resilience, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and arid landscapes.
