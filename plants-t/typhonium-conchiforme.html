Typhonium conchiforme

Typhonium conchiforme, commonly known as the conch shell arum, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, dark green, arrow-shaped leaves and a distinctive, spadix-like inflorescence surrounded by a colorful spathe. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into red berries, which are edible when ripe. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Typhonium conchiforme is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
