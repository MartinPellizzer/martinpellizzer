Tacsonia longifolia

Tacsonia longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf tacsonia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and early summer. Its flowering stems are often branched and can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

Tacsonia longifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
