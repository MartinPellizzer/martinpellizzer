Tragacantha chodsha-bakirganica

Tragacantha chodsha-bakirganica, commonly known as the Afghan tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody stem and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by pod-like fruits containing seeds.

It is known for its ability to thrive in dry, sandy soils and is often used in traditional medicine for its mucilage-rich properties.
