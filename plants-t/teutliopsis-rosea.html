Teutliopsis rosea

Teutliopsis rosea, commonly known as the roseate amaranth, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant pink to reddish bracts that resemble petals, giving it a striking visual appearance. The plant belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is known for its upright growth habit. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it thrives in full sun with well-drained soil.

Teutliopsis rosea is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its colorful foliage and adaptability to various climates.
