Taxodium lineatum

Taxodium lineatum, commonly known as the pond cypress, is a deciduous conifer native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, columnar form and its ability to thrive in wetland environments. The plant has scale-like leaves that turn a striking red or copper color in the fall, adding visual interest to its landscape. Its cones are small and woody, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind.

The pond cypress is also notable for its tolerance of periodic flooding, making it a valuable species in riparian zones.
