Thymus thracicus var. longidens

Thymus thracicus var. longidens, commonly known as the Balkan thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and pink to purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its essential oils, rich in thymol and carvacrol, contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.
