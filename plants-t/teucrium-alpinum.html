Teucrium alpinum

Teucrium alpinum, commonly known as alpine germander, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant features upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves with a grayish-green hue. Its flowers are usually pink or violet, arranged in dense spikes, and bloom during the summer months.

Teucrium alpinum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
