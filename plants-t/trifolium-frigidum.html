Trifolium frigidum

Trifolium frigidum, commonly known as the frost clover, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its small, trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces delicate white or pale purple flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky soils.

Trifolium frigidum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
