Tristania umbrosa

Tristania umbrosa, commonly known as the darkleaf tristania, is a perennial shrub native to the humid forests of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red or black when mature. Tristania umbrosa is often found in shaded, moist environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

It has a compact growth habit, making it suitable for garden cultivation in shaded areas.
