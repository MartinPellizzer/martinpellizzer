Terminalia magarapali

Terminalia magarapali, commonly known as the Magarapali or Indian almond tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and elongated, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, three-lobed fruits that contain three seeds each. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

The tree is also valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture making.
