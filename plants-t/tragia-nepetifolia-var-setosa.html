Tragia nepetifolia var. setosa

Tragia nepetifolia var. setosa, commonly known as the hairy tragia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and densely hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in open habitats such as fields and roadsides. Its leaves are usually opposite and ovate, while the flowers are clustered in short inflorescences.

The species is known for its adaptability and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
