Theobroma cacao

Theobroma cacao, commonly known as the cocoa tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Americas.

It produces pods containing seeds that are the source of cocoa mass, used in chocolate production. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments with well-drained soil. Its leaves are large, glossy, and elliptical, while its flowers are small and yellowish.

Theobroma cacao is also valued for its medicinal properties and historical significance in indigenous cultures.
