Tulipa suaveolens var. pluriflora

Tulipa suaveolens var. pluriflora, commonly known as the fragrant tulip or sweet-scented tulip, is a perennial bulbous plant native to Turkey.

It produces clusters of fragrant, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, often with a distinctive red eye. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.

The variety pluriflora is noted for its high flower count per stem, making it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
