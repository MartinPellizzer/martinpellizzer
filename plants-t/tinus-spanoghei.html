Tinus spanoghei

Tinus spanoghei, commonly known as the Canary Island date palm, is a species of palm native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible fruits that resemble dates. It thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to the mild, humid climate of its native habitat.

Due to its ornamental value, it is also cultivated in other tropical and subtropical regions.
