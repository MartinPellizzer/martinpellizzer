Tecoma cyrtantha

Tecoma cyrtantha, commonly known as the golden trumpet tree, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its showy, orange-yellow tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant is a shrub or small tree, typically growing to a height of 3 to 6 meters, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are borne on long, slender pedicels, and the plant produces small, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds.

Tecoma cyrtantha is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to a variety of soil types.
