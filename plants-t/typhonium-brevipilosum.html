Typhonium brevipilosum

Typhonium brevipilosum, commonly known as the short-hairy arrowhead, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix and a spathe. The plant features broad, arrow-shaped leaves with a waxy surface and short, stiff hairs along the veins. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for aquatic and bog gardens.
