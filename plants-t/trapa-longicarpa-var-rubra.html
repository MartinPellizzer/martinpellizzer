Trapa longicarpa var. rubra

Trapa longicarpa var. rubra, commonly known as the red-seed water chestnut, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its floating leaves and spiny, inflated fruits that aid in seed dispersal. The plant thrives in shallow, freshwater habitats such as ponds and slow-moving rivers. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically appearing in clusters.

The reddish coloration of its seeds is a distinguishing feature of this variant.
