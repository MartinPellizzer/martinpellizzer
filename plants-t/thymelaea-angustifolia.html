Thymelaea angustifolia

Thymelaea angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf thymeblume, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub with small, dark green, elliptical leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
