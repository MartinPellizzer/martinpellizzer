Trichosanthes montana subsp. montana

Trichosanthes montana subsp.

montana, commonly known as the mountain trumpet vine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia. It features large, showy yellow flowers with a distinctive trumpet shape, which are typically borne in clusters.

The plant has a robust vine structure with hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. Its fruit is a long, slender capsule that splits open to release seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
