Triodia kerguelensis

Triodia kerguelensis, commonly known as the Kerguelen triodia, is a perennial grass species native to the subantarctic region.

It is characterized by its low-growing, tufted form with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in harsh environments with poor soil and low temperatures.

Triodia kerguelensis plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.
