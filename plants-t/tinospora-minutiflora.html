Tinospora minutiflora

Tinospora minutiflora, commonly known as the small-flowered tinospora, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, greenish flowers. The plant produces yellowish or orange berries that are typically eaten by birds, aiding in seed dispersal. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and thickets.

Tinospora minutiflora is valued in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
