Tsuga pattoniana f. argentea

Tsuga pattoniana f. argentea, commonly known as the silver fir, is a unique variant of the Pacific silver fir.

It is characterized by its striking silvery-white undersides on its needle-like leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. This form is a natural mutation of the parent species, resulting in a more compact and ornamental growth habit. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions of the Pacific Northwest.

The plant is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental qualities.
