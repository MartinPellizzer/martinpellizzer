Thalictrum secundum var. hazaricum

Thalictrum secundum var. hazaricum, commonly known as the Hazara meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as meadows and forest understories. It has a slender, upright stem that can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
