Trochetiopsis melanoxylon

Trochetiopsis melanoxylon, commonly known as the blackwood tree, is a species native to southern Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in coastal and mountainous regions. The tree is characterized by its dark, glossy bark and dense, aromatic foliage. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a leathery texture.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds.
