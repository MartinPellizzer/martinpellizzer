Trichophyllum intermedium

Trichophyllum intermedium, commonly known as the intermediate trichophyllum, is a unique species of liverwort belonging to the family Porellaceae.

It is characterized by its small, delicate leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest floors or rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures, known as sporophytes, develop from specialized leaves called gametophores.

Trichophyllum intermedium plays a role in soil stabilization and provides microhabitat for various invertebrates.
