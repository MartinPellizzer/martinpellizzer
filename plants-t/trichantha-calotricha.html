Trichantha calotricha

Trichantha calotricha, commonly known as the golden star cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical stems that are covered in fine, hair-like structures called trichomes. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a striking visual element to its appearance. Trichantha calotricha is well adapted to dry environments, with succulent tissues that store water efficiently.

Its unique morphology and ornamental value make it a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and botanical gardens.
