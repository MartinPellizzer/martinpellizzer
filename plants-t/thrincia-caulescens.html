Thrincia caulescens

Thrincia caulescens, commonly known as the slender stemmed thrincia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and typical herbaceous growth habit. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with slender, upright stems covered in fine, grayish hairs. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

Thrincia caulescens produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer.
