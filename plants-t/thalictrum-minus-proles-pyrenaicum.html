Thalictrum minus proles pyrenaicum

Thalictrum minus proles pyrenaicum, commonly known as the Pyrenean meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Pyrenees region.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, compound leaves and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland or meadow habitats and prefers partial to full shade. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies in suitable environments.

Thalictrum minus proles pyrenaicum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and early spring blooms.
