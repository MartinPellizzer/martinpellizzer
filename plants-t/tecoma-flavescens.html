Tecoma flavescens

Tecoma flavescens, commonly known as the yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. Its flowers are typically 5-8 cm long and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Tecoma flavescens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adaptable to various soil types.
