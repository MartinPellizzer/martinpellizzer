Tolpis proustii

Tolpis proustii, commonly known as the Proust's dandelion, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features basal rosettes of lanceolate, hairy leaves that are often bluish-green in color. Its flowering stems produce a single flower head with yellow ray florets and dark purple disk florets.

Tolpis proustii is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
