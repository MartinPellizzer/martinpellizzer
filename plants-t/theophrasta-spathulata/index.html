Theophrasta Spathulata

Theophrasta spathulata is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the cloud forests of Central America. Known for its striking violet flowers and velvety foliage, it captivates botanists and horticulturists alike. This species belongs to the Gesneriaceae family, renowned for its ornamental value and ecological significance. Despite its beauty, it remains elusive due to its limited habitat and slow growth. Conservation efforts are crucial to preserving this delicate and endangered species.
