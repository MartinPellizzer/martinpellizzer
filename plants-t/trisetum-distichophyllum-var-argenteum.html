Trisetum distichophyllum var. argenteum

Trisetum distichophyllum var. argenteum, commonly known as silver tuft grass, is a variant of the tufted grass species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-gray foliage, which results from dense covering of fine, silky hairs on the leaves. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and alpine meadows, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, spikelet-bearing flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable climates.
