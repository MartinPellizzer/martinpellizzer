Tragacantha falciformis

Tragacantha falciformis, commonly known as the sickle-leaved tragacanth, is a perennial shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive sickle-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers during the summer months. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, with deep roots that help it access groundwater. It belongs to the legume family and has the ability to fix nitrogen, which enriches the soil.

The plant's resin has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive.
