Tephrosia viridiflora

Tephrosia viridiflora, commonly known as green-flowered tephrosia, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant features small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being ovate and slightly hairy.

Tephrosia viridiflora is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
