Tithonia koelzii

Tithonia koelzii, commonly known as Mexican sunflower, is a tropical plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a herbaceous annual that features large, showy yellow-orange flowers with red centers. The plant has heart-shaped, rough-textured leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Tithonia koelzii is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
