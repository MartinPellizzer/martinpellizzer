Taraxacum bernhardi

Taraxacum bernhardi, commonly known as the Bernhard's dandelion, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features a thick, fleshy stem with alternating leaves that are typically lance-shaped and hairy. Its bright yellow flower heads produce numerous tiny seeds enclosed in a pappus, facilitating wind dispersal.

The species is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, showcasing its adaptability to various environments.
