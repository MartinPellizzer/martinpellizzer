Taeniophyllum proliferum

Taeniophyllum proliferum, commonly known as the creeping rosette plant, is a member of the Bromeliaceae family.

It is a small, evergreen plant characterized by its dense, compact rosette of fleshy, overlapping leaves. The plant is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. Its leaves are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss in its arid habitat.

Taeniophyllum proliferum is often grown as a decorative plant due to its attractive appearance and low maintenance requirements.
