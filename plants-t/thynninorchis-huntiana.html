Thynninorchis huntiana

Thynninorchis huntiana, commonly known as the Hunt's orchid, is a rare and endemic orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, intricate floral structure. The plant typically features a slender stem with a single, showy flower that has a distinctive coloration pattern. Its growth habit is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees without deriving nutrients from them.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
