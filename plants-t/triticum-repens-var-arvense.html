Triticum repens var. arvense

Triticum repens var. arvense, commonly known as meadow grass or timothy grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and long, narrow leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences, which are often wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is commonly found in meadows, pastures, and along stream banks.

This species is valued for its forage quality and has been used historically in agricultural settings.
