Thalictrum latistylum

Thalictrum latistylum, commonly known as the broad-leaved meadow rue, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and thrives in partial to full shade. Its flowers bloom in early to mid-summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Thalictrum latistylum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and delicate floral display.
