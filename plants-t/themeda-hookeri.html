Themeda hookeri

Themeda hookeri, commonly known as the narrow-leaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and is adapted to dry, well-drained soils. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, two-ranked spikelets.

Themeda hookeri plays a significant role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
