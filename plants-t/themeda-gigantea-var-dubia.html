Themeda gigantea var. dubia

Themeda gigantea var. dubia, commonly known as the dwarf grass or silver grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and compact growth habit, often forming dense clumps. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowering stems are slender and bear small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually wind-pollinated.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native gardens and restoration projects.
