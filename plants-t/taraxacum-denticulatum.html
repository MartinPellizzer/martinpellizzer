Taraxacum denticulatum

Taraxacum denticulatum, commonly known as the toothed dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated, grass-like leaves and a distinctive yellow flower head that develops into a spherical seed ball. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been introduced to other regions, including North America. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in lawns, meadows, and disturbed areas.

The species is known for its ability to spread through wind-dispersed seeds, making it a common and widespread weed.
