Tragacantha cilicia

Tragacantha cilicia, commonly known as the Cilician tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds.

Tragacantha cilicia is valued for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments, making it an important species in ecological restoration efforts.
