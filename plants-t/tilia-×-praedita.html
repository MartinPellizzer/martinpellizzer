Tilia × praedita

Tilia × praedita, commonly known as the common lime, is a hybrid species within the Tilia genus, native to Europe.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are typically 5–10 cm in diameter, with a serrated margin. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which bloom in early summer and are followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along riverbanks.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
