Trychinolepis

Trychinolepis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sandwort, is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial plant characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, fleshy leaves. Native to arid and semi-arid regions, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. The plant produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers, typically in late spring or early summer.

Its botanical features make it well-adapted to harsh environmental conditions, showcasing resilience and ecological significance in its habitat.
