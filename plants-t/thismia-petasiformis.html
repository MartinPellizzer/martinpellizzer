Thismia petasiformis

Thismia petasiformis, commonly known as the petasiform Thismia, is a unique flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Thismiaceae and is characterized by its lack of chlorophyll, making it a mycoheterotrophic plant that relies on fungal associations for nutrients. The plant produces striking, lantern-like flowers that are often yellow or orange in color. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its specialized growth habits, it is considered rare and is of interest to botanists studying plant-fungal interactions.
