Trichocentrum chrysops

Trichocentrum chrysops, commonly known as the golden-lipped orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, golden-yellow lip that resembles a lip-shaped structure, which is a key feature in its classification. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, with long, arching stems that can reach several feet in length. Its flowers are usually clustered in racemes and are often fragrant, attracting various pollinators.

Trichocentrum chrysops is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
