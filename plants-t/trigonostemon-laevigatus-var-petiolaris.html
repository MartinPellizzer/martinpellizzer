Trigonostemon laevigatus var. petiolaris

Trigonostemon laevigatus var. petiolaris, commonly known as the narrow-leafed trigonostemon, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are typically elliptical to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. Its bark is smooth and grayish, and it is often found in moist, shaded environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
