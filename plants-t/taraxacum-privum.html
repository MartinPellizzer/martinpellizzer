Taraxacum privum

Taraxacum privum, commonly known as the lesser dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of pale green, hairy leaves and a yellow flower head that develops into a white, fluffy seed sphere. The plant is native to Europe and is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils. It has a taproot system and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

The species is also noted for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
