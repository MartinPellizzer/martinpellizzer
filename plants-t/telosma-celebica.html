Telosma celebica

Telosma celebica, commonly known as the Java nightshade, is a climbing shrub native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-red berries. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

Telosma celebica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
