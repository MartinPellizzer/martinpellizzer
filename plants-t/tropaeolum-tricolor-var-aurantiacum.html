Tropaeolum tricolor var. aurantiacum

Tropaeolum tricolor var. aurantiacum, commonly known as the golden touchweed, is a variant of the common nasturtium, characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves.

It is a herbaceous annual plant native to South America, thriving in warm climates with full sun exposure. The plant produces edible, peppery-tasting leaves and seed pods, which are often used in culinary applications. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to garden biodiversity.

The plant's rapid growth and ability to spread through self-seeding make it a popular choice for ornamental and edible gardening.
