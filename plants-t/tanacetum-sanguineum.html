Tanacetum sanguineum

Tanacetum sanguineum, commonly known as the bloodroot or red drop, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive red-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in meadows, woodland edges, and rocky soils, preferring well-drained conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish in color.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various purposes.
