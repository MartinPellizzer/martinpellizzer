Taraxacum hisauchii

Taraxacum hisauchii, commonly known as the Japanese dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette of spoon-shaped leaves and a hollow, hairy stem. The plant produces a single, yellow flower head that develops into a white, fluffy seed head. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Taraxacum hisauchii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
