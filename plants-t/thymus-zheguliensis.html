Thymus zheguliensis

Thymus zheguliensis, commonly known as the Zheguli thyme, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lamiaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus, particularly in Georgia. This perennial herb is characterized by its compact growth habit and aromatic, small, gray-green leaves. The flowers are typically pink or purple, appearing in dense clusters during the summer months.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.
