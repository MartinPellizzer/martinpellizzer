Tsuga chinensis var. robusta

Tsuga chinensis var. robusta, commonly known as the Chinese hemlock, is a variant of the Chinese fir native to eastern China.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its pyramidal shape and needle-like leaves. The foliage is dense and dark green, with a slight blue-gray tint on the underside of the needles. It produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, typical of the conifer family.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in gardens and landscapes for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
