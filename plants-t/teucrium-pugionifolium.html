Teucrium pugionifolium

Teucrium pugionifolium, commonly known as Chinese germander, is a perennial herb native to China.

It features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant once established.

Botanically, it belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by aromatic leaves and showy flowers.
