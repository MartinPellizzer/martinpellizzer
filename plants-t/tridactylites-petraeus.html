Tridactylites petraeus

Tridactylites petraeus, commonly known as the rock tridactyl, is a unique plant species characterized by its distinctive three-lobed leaves.

It is a perennial herb that thrives in rocky and arid environments, often found in mountainous regions. The plant has a fibrous root system that helps it anchor in crevices and access limited soil nutrients. Its small, greenish-white flowers appear in clusters and are adapted for wind pollination.

Tridactylites petraeus is known for its resilience to harsh conditions, making it an important component of its native ecosystems.
