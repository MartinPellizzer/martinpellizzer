Tirpitzia candida

Tirpitzia candida, commonly known as the white bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue, blooming in late summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, often found in rocky or woodland habitats.

Tirpitzia candida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
