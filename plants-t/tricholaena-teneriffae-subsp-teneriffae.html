Tricholaena teneriffae subsp. teneriffae

Tricholaena teneriffae subsp.

teneriffae, commonly known as the Canary Island grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Canary Islands. It is characterized by its robust growth and dense foliage, often forming clumps with erect, cylindrical stems.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a slight sheen.

This subspecies is adapted to the volcanic soils and coastal climates of its native habitat.
