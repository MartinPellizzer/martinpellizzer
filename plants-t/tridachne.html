Tridachne

Tridachne, commonly known as the grass or reed, is a genus of flowering plants in the grass family, Poaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow leaves, often growing in wet or marshy environments. The plants typically produce small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered in panicles. Tridachne species are often found in temperate and tropical regions, and they play a role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.

Their rhizomatous growth habit allows them to spread and form dense stands, contributing to their ecological significance.
