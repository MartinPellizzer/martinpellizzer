Talanelis

Talanelis, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, silvery-green leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. Talanelis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.

Its growth habit includes a tall, straight trunk with a spreading crown, making it suitable for both landscaping and shade provision.
