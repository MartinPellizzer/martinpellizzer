Thelypteris cocleana

Thelypteris cocleana, commonly known as the Coclean Shield Fern, is a species of fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are clustered on the undersides of the fronds.

Thelypteris cocleana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
