Trifolium phleoides subsp. willkommii

Trifolium phleoides subsp.

willkommii, commonly known as the Willkommi's clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, typically consisting of three oval to lance-shaped leaflets.

The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassy areas and open woodlands.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
