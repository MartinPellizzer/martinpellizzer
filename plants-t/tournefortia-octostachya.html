Tournefortia octostachya

Tournefortia octostachya, commonly known as the African star jasmine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2 to 5 meters. The plant is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant.

The flowers emit a sweet, jasmine-like fragrance, especially at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.
