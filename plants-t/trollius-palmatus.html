Trollius palmatus

Trollius palmatus, commonly known as the marsh marigold, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, showy flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often covered in a fine layer of white down.

Trollius palmatus plays an important role in wetland ecosystems, supporting a variety of pollinators and wildlife.
