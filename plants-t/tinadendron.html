Tinadendron

Tinadendron, commonly known as the "devil's ivy," is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Apocynaceae.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, with opposite, simple, and often heart-shaped leaves. Tinadendron species are native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.
