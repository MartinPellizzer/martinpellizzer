Tsuga brunoniana

Tsuga brunoniana, commonly known as the Brunon's hemlock, is a species of conifer native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Pinaceae family and is characterized by its pyramidal shape and needle-like leaves. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its foliage is typically dark green, and it thrives in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Tsuga brunoniana is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
