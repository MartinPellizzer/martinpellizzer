Tabebuia bahamensis

Tabebuia bahamensis, commonly known as the Bahamian trumpet tree, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly the Bahamas and parts of Central America.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, with a broad, rounded crown and smooth, grayish-brown bark. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and orange, blooming in the spring and early summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a glossy green surface, and it produces woody, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often planted in gardens and parks for its vibrant floral displays.
