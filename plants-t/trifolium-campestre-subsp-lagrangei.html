Trifolium campestre subsp. lagrangei

Trifolium campestre subsp.

lagrangei, commonly known as the Lagrange's white clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged on a slender stem.

The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. It prefers well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
