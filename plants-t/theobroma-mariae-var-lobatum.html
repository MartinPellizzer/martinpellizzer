Theobroma mariae var. lobatum

Theobroma mariae var. lobatum, commonly known as the lobate cocoa, is a variant of the cocoa plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its dark green, elliptical leaves and small, clustered white flowers. The plant produces oblong, bean-like fruits that contain the seeds used for cocoa production. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its unique flavor profile and is often used in premium chocolate products.
