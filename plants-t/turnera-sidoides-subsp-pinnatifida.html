Turnera sidoides subsp. pinnatifida

Turnera sidoides subsp.

pinnatifida, commonly known as the goldenstar turnera, is a perennial shrub native to Central and South America. It is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring.

The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments such as tropical forests and mountainous regions. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
