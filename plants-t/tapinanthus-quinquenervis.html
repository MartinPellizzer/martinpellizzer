Tapinanthus quinquenervis

Tapinanthus quinquenervis, commonly known as the five-veined dodder, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies on host plants for nutrients.

It produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers and has a unique life cycle involving seed dispersal by wind or animals. The plant has a fibrous, root-like structure called a haustorium that attaches to the roots of its host. It is native to Africa and thrives in disturbed habitats such as grasslands and agricultural fields.

Its botanical significance lies in its parasitic nature and its role in ecological interactions within its native environment.
