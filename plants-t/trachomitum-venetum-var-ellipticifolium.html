Trachomitum venetum var. ellipticifolium

Trachomitum venetum var. ellipticifolium, commonly known as the elliptic-leaf trachomitum, is a rare and delicate fern species characterized by its finely divided, elliptical-shaped fronds.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in temperate forest understories. The plant has a compact growth habit with a short, creeping rhizome and delicate, arching leaves that are usually a deep green. Its spore-bearing structures, or sporangia, are clustered in small clusters along the leaf margins.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
