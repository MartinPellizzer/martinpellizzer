Tulipa sylvestris var. minor

Tulipa sylvestris var. minor, commonly known as the woodland tulip, is a perennial bulbous plant native to Central Asia.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of pale yellow to orange flowers with a distinctive red-orange throat. The plant thrives in woodland environments with well-drained soil and partial shade. It is valued for its delicate appearance and early spring blooming habit.

This variety is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
