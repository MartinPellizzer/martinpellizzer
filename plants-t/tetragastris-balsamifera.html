Tetragastris balsamifera

Tetragastris balsamifera, commonly known as the balsam tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height, characterized by its aromatic, resinous bark. The leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic in shape, with a smooth surface and a slight pubescence. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by round, fleshy fruits containing several seeds.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
