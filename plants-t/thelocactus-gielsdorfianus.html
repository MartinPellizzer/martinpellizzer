Thelocactus gielsdorfianus

Thelocactus gielsdorfianus, commonly known as the Giersdorf's locactus, is a small cactus native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and a dense covering of white, woolly hairs that protect it from intense sunlight and moisture loss. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits. Its roots are shallow and adapted to absorb water quickly after rare rainfalls.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is considered a rare and specialized species within the Cactaceae family.
