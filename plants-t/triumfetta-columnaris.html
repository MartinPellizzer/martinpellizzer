Triumfetta columnaris

Triumfetta columnaris, commonly known as the slender triumfetta, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, opposite leaves that are typically ovate to elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by three-winged, indehiscent fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping and coastal gardens.

Due to its hardiness and ornamental value, it is often used in landscaping and as a decorative plant in warm climates.
