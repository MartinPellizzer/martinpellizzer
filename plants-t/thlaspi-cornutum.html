Thlaspi cornutum

Thlaspi cornutum, commonly known as the horned poppy, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spurred petals that resemble horns, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, often in mountainous regions. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.
