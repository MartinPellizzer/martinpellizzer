Theodorea davidi

Theodorea davidi, commonly known as the David's lily, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and prefers well-drained soil. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Theodorea davidi is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its cultural significance in traditional Chinese medicine.
