Tinus eximia

Tinus eximia, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a deciduous tree native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray bark and compound leaves that are pinnately arranged. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in the spring, followed by edible, red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Tinus eximia is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
