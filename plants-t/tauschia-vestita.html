Tauschia vestita

Tauschia vestita, commonly known as the naked tauschia, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, often hairy stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and linear, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Tauschia vestita is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
