Turbinicarpus valdezianus var. albiflorus

Turbinicarpus valdezianus var. albiflorus, commonly known as the white-flowered stone cactus, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, globular shape and thick, ribbed stems. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a delicate contrast to its rugged appearance. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it stores water in its fleshy stems, allowing it to survive in harsh desert conditions.

This variety is prized by cactus enthusiasts for its unique appearance and resilience.
