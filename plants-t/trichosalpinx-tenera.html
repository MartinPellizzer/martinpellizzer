Trichosalpinx tenera

Trichosalpinx tenera, commonly known as the slender salvia, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and ability to attract pollinators.
