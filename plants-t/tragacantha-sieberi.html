Tragacantha sieberi

Tragacantha sieberi, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody shrub or small tree form. The plant produces distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by pod-like fruits containing seeds. Its sap, known as tragacanth, has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural thickening agent.

Tragacantha sieberi is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.
