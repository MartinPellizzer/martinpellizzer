Tradescantia rosea

Tradescantia rosea, commonly known as the red-veined begonia, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves with deep red veins. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Due to its attractive foliage and ease of care, Tradescantia rosea is often cultivated as a decorative plant.
