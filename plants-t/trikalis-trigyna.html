Trikalis trigyna

Trikalis trigyna, commonly known as the three-lobed bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive three-lobed leaves and bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It blooms during the spring and early summer, producing nectar that attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and naturalized areas.
