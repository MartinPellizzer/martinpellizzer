Tectaria multicaudata var. multicaudata

Tectaria multicaudata var. multicaudata, commonly known as the long-tailed tectaria, is a species of fern belonging to the family Tectariaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive fronds that feature elongated, finger-like lobes, giving it a unique appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, a common feature in many ferns.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
