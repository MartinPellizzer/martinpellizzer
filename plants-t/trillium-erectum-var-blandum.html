Trillium erectum var. blandum

Trillium erectum var. blandum, commonly known as the white trillium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It features three broadly ovate leaves that are typically white to pale green in color. The plant produces a single, showy white flower with three petals and three sepals, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in shaded, moist woodlands and is often found in rich, acidic soils.

The species is notable for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
