Tecoma sorbifolia

Tecoma sorbifolia, commonly known as the yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, featuring woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from spring to summer. Its flowers are pollinated by bees and other insects, and the plant is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.

Tecoma sorbifolia is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant flowers and drought tolerance.
