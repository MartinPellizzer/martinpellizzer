Thilachium paradoxum

Thilachium paradoxum, commonly known as the paradoxical flame flower, is a strikingly colorful plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that range from deep red to orange, often with a contrasting yellow or white throat. The plant belongs to the Apocynaceae family, which includes many milkweed species, and is known for its milky sap. Thilachium paradoxum thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Its unique floral structure makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and botanical collections.
