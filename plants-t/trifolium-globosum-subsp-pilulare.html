Trifolium globosum subsp. pilulare

Trifolium globosum subsp.

pilulare, commonly known as the globular clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its compact, globular growth habit and trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, globular clusters, which give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open woodlands and disturbed sites.

Its botanical features make it a notable species for ecological studies and native plant gardens.
