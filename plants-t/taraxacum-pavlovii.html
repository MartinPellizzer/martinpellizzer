Taraxacum pavlovii

Taraxacum pavlovii, commonly known as the Pavlov's dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant features a rosette of spoon-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically green with a bluish tinge. Its yellow flower heads consist of numerous small florets that develop into fluffy white seeds with parachute-like structures.

Taraxacum pavlovii is adapted to harsh alpine environments, often growing in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations.
