Thymus jankae subsp. imbricatus

Thymus jankae subsp.

imbricatus, commonly known as the Balkan thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage of small, aromatic leaves.

The plant produces pink to purple flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its essential oils contain thymol and carvacrol, giving it a strong, spicy aroma and making it valuable in traditional medicine and culinary uses.
