Themeda triandra

Themeda triandra, commonly known as the tussock grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and forms extensive tussocks that can reach heights of up to 1 meter. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained soils and is commonly found in grasslands and savanna ecosystems.

Themeda triandra plays an important role in maintaining soil stability and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
