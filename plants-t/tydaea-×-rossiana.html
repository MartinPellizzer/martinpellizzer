Tydaea × rossiana

Tydaea × rossiana, commonly known as the hybrid sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in wetlands or along the edges of ponds and streams. Its foliage is grass-like, with narrow, sword-shaped leaves, and it produces small, clustered flower heads during the summer months.

Tydaea × rossiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
