Tulipa libanotica

Tulipa libanotica, commonly known as the Lebanon tulip, is a species native to the mountainous regions of the eastern Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its striking, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep red, often with a distinctive yellow or orange center. The plant typically grows to a height of 10 to 30 centimeters and produces narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

The flowers bloom in late spring, adding a vibrant touch to its natural habitat.
