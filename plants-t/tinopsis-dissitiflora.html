Tinopsis dissitiflora

Tinopsis dissitiflora, commonly known as the silverleaf daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shrubland and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. It produces small, daisy-like blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Tinopsis dissitiflora is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
