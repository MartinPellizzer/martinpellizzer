Trifolium depauperatum var. diversifolium

Trifolium depauperatum var. diversifolium, commonly known as the variable clover, is a rare and lesser-known variant of the clover genus.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology, featuring leaves with varying numbers of leaflets, typically three, but sometimes more. This plant thrives in nutrient-poor soils, often found in open, disturbed areas such as meadows and woodland edges. Its small, pinkish-white flowers bloom in early spring, adding a subtle floral display.

The plant's botanical significance lies in its genetic diversity and potential role in ecological studies of plant adaptation.
