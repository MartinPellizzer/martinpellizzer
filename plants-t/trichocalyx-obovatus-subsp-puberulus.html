Trichocalyx obovatus subsp. puberulus

Trichocalyx obovatus subsp.

puberulus, commonly known as the hairy obovate acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and leaves that are obovate in shape.

The plant produces yellow flowers in racemes, which are typical of the Fabaceae family. Its small, pod-like fruits contain several seeds.

This species is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions.
