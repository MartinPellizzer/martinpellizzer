Tragacantha rytidocarpa

Tragacantha rytidocarpa, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a deciduous tree native to the arid regions of Iran and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its thick, corky bark and compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by distinctive, inflated seed pods. It is known for its ability to thrive in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures.

The tree is also valued for its resin, which has been used historically in various traditional applications.
