Tragacantha grandis

Tragacantha grandis, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a species of flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and woody pods. The plant is known for producing a resinous gum, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive.

Its leaves are typically compound, with small leaflets arranged in a feathery pattern, and it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments.
