Tigridia lutea

Tigridia lutea, commonly known as the yellow tigridia, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces tall, slender stems with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its blooms typically appear in late spring to early summer, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Tigridia lutea is a perennial herb that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.
