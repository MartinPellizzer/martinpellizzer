Tragopogon meskheticus

Tragopogon meskheticus, commonly known as the yellow gooseneck, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and slender, hollow stems that resemble a gooseneck. The plant produces a long, slender seed stalk that curves upward, bearing a cluster of small, fluffy seeds. It thrives in open, sunny areas such as meadows and grasslands.

Tragopogon meskheticus is often found in disturbed soils and is a member of the dandelion family, Asteraceae.
