Trichocereus courantii

Trichocereus courantii, commonly known as the Pachanoi cactus, is a species native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stem and clusters of white, star-shaped flowers that bloom at night. The plant produces a distinctive red or purple fruit, which contains seeds enclosed in a fleshy pulp. Trichocereus courantii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its unique appearance and nocturnal flowering make it a striking addition to desert gardens.
