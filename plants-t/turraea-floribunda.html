Turraea floribunda

Turraea floribunda, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, with a broad, umbrella-shaped canopy. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and elliptical, with a smooth texture.

Turraea floribunda is valued for its ornamental value and is often planted in gardens and along roadsides for its striking floral display.
