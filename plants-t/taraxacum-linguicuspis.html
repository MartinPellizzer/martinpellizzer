Taraxacum linguicuspis

Taraxacum linguicuspis, commonly known as the narrow-lobed dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, spoon-shaped leaves and a single, elongated flower head that produces a crown of white ray florets. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in grassy areas and along roadsides. It has a taproot system and produces a distinctive seed head with parachute-like seeds for wind dispersal.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
