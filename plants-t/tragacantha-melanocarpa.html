Tragacantha melanocarpa

Tragacantha melanocarpa, commonly known as black tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant produces a thick, sticky resin known as tragacanth, which has been used historically in medicine and as a thickening agent. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in scrubland and woodland habitats.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its historical significance in traditional herbal practices.
