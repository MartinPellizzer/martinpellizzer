Thymus pulegioides subsp. effusus

Thymus pulegioides subsp.

effusus, commonly known as creeping thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe. It features small, fragrant, purple or pink flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant has aromatic, narrow leaves that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its dense growth habit helps suppress weeds and prevent soil erosion.
