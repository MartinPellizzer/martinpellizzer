Thelypteris acanthocarpa

Thelypteris acanthocarpa, commonly known as the spiny-fruit fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Thelypteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori, which are typically located on the underside of the leaves. Its rhizomes are often creeping and can form dense colonies in moist, shaded environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
