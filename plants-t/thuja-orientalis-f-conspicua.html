Thuja orientalis f. conspicua

Thuja orientalis f. conspicua, commonly known as the golden thread thuja, is a cultivar of the eastern arborvitae.

It is characterized by its narrow, upright growth habit and its distinctive golden-yellow foliage, which contrasts sharply with its dark green stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellowish-green cones, typical of the Cupressaceae family. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.

This variety thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
