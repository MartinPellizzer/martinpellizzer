Triodia stipoides

Triodia stipoides, commonly known as the spinifex grass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, wiry foliage and ability to thrive in arid and saline environments. The plant forms extensive tussocks and is well adapted to withstand drought and grazing. Its small, spike-like inflorescences produce wind-pollinated flowers, and it plays a crucial role in stabilizing sandy soils.

Triodia stipoides is an important component of the vegetation in arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.
