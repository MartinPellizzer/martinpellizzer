Tetrazygia impressa

Tetrazygia impressa, commonly known as the broadleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that are often deeply veined and have a slightly rough texture. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, typically blooming in the summer months. Tetrazygia impressa thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in tropical landscapes.
