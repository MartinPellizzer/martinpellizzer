Taraxacum macranthoides

Taraxacum macranthoides, commonly known as the giant dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow composite flowers and robust, hairy stems. Native to eastern North America, this plant thrives in a variety of habitats, including meadows and disturbed soils. Its leaves are broad, lobed, and often covered with fine hairs, while its roots form a dense, fibrous system.

The plant is known for its ability to produce a large number of seeds, which are dispersed by the wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.
