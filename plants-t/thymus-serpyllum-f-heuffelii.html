Thymus serpyllum f. heuffelii

Thymus serpyllum f. heuffelii, commonly known as creeping thyme, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe.

It features small, fragrant leaves that are typically gray-green in color and emit a strong thyme scent when crushed. The plant produces tiny, pink or white flowers in spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice in horticulture.
