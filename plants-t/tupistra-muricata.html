Tupistra muricata

Tupistra muricata, commonly known as the rock rose, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing, evergreen shrub characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and clusters of fragrant, pink or white flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and full sun, making it well-suited for rock gardens and coastal landscapes. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, often covered in small, backward-pointing spines.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
