Taraxacum incantatum

Taraxacum incantatum, commonly known as the sacred dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in meadows and grasslands. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated, hairy leaves and a yellow flower head that matures into a puffed seed dispersal unit. It has a long flowering period, typically from spring to early autumn, and is known for its medicinal properties.

The species is also valued for its role in ecological systems, supporting pollinators and contributing to soil health.
