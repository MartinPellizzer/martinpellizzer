Taxus × media

Taxus × media, commonly known as the Japanese yew, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Taxus cuspidata and Taxus mairei.

It is a slow-growing evergreen shrub or small tree with dense, needle-like foliage that remains green throughout the year. The plant produces small, reddish-orange berries that are toxic to humans and animals. Taxus × media is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its compact form and year-round color.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for landscaping in temperate climates.
