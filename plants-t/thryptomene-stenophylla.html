Thryptomene stenophylla

Thryptomene stenophylla, commonly known as narrow-leaf thryptomene, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, white or pinkish flowers that appear in clusters. It typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.

Thryptomene stenophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
