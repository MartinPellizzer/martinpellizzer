Terminalia kelleri

Terminalia kelleri, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and alternate, lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds. It is valued for its hard, durable wood used in furniture and construction.
