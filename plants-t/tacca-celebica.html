Tacca celebica

Tacca celebica, commonly known as the "bat plant" or "devil's tongue," is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark purple, fleshy leaves that resemble bat wings, and its striking, showy flowers that are often described as resembling a tongue or a face. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its unique appearance makes it a popular ornamental plant in botanical gardens and private collections.

Tacca celebica is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
