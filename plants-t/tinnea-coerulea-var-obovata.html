Tinnea coerulea var. obovata

Tinnea coerulea var. obovata, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in dense, spherical clusters. The leaves are typically obovate, meaning they are broadest near the base and taper towards the tip. This plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
