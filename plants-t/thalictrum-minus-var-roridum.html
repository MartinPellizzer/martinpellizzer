Thalictrum minus var. roridum

Thalictrum minus var. roridum, commonly known as the showy avens, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features delicate, feathery foliage and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and the plant spreads through rhizomes.

It is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
