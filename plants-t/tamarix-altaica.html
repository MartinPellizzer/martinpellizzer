Tamarix altaica

Tamarix altaica, commonly known as the Siberian tamarisk, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and small, scale-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces pink to red flowers in dense clusters during the summer months, which are adapted for wind pollination. Tamarix altaica is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in sandy or saline soils.

It is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
