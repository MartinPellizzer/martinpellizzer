Taraxacum langeanum

Taraxacum langeanum, commonly known as the Lange's dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and a hollow, hairless stem that supports a single, inconspicuous flower head. The plant produces a distinctive seed head composed of numerous parachute-like seeds, which are dispersed by the wind. Native to southern Africa, it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential medicinal research.
