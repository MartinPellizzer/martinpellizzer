Thymus orospedanus var. longifolius

Thymus orospedanus var. longifolius, commonly known as the long-leaved thyme, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features aromatic, small, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces tiny, pale pink to white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its essential oils contain thymol and carvacrol, contributing to its use in herbal medicine and culinary applications.
