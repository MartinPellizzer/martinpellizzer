Tephrosia chimanimaniana

Tephrosia chimanimaniana, commonly known as the African senna, is a perennial shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is notable for its bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is deep, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soils and drought-prone environments.

The plant also has medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
