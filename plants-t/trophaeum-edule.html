Trophaeum edule

Trophaeum edule, commonly known as the edible moonflower, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, white, night-blooming flowers that emit a sweet fragrance. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces edible berries that are often consumed raw or cooked. Its botanical classification places it in the family Solanaceae, which includes other nightshade plants.

The plant is valued not only for its edible parts but also for its ornamental beauty and ecological role in supporting nocturnal pollinators.
