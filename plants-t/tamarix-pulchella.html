Tamarix pulchella

Tamarix pulchella, commonly known as the silver tamarisk, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and small, scale-like leaves that are typically silver or gray in color. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pink to reddish flowers in the summer, which are followed by small, woody pods containing seeds. Tamarix pulchella is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for xeriscaping.

It is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, which can improve soil fertility in degraded areas.
