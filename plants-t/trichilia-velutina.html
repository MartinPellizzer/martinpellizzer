Trichilia velutina

Trichilia velutina, commonly known as the velvet tallow tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Africa and parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae, which includes mahogany and other valuable hardwoods. The tree is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and compound leaves with a velvety texture. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and arranged in panicles, while the fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds.

The wood of Trichilia velutina is highly prized for its durability and fine grain, making it a valuable resource in the timber industry.
