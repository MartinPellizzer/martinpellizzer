Taraxacum abnorme

Taraxacum abnorme, commonly known as the giant dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces a single, tall, hollow stem that supports a flower head composed of numerous yellow florets. Its seeds are enclosed in a distinctive, fluffy pappus that aids in wind dispersal.

This species is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats.
