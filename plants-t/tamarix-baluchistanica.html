Tamarix baluchistanica

Tamarix baluchistanica, commonly known as the Balochistan tamarisk, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Pakistan and Afghanistan.

It is characterized by its dense, feathery foliage and small, scale-like leaves that reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces pink or reddish flowers in clusters, which are adapted for pollination by wind and insects. It has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in poor soils and withstand drought conditions.

Tamarix baluchistanica plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for local wildlife.
