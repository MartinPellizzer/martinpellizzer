Thelypteris dilatata var. americana

Thelypteris dilatata var. americana, commonly known as the American cliff brake, is a fern species native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its slender, arched fronds that are typically 30 to 60 centimeters long. The leaves are pinnately divided with a distinctive, serrated margin and a pale, sometimes whitish, underside. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as rocky outcrops and woodland areas.

This variety is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate a range of soil conditions.
