Tragopogon moldavicus

Tragopogon moldavicus, commonly known as the Moldavian thistle, is a flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy basal rosette. The plant produces bright yellow ray flowers surrounded by a dense cluster of disk flowers, which mature into bristly, spiny seed heads. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with rough, hairy surfaces, and it thrives in dry, open habitats.

Tragopogon moldavicus is often found in meadows, roadsides, and disturbed soils, showing adaptability to various environmental conditions.
