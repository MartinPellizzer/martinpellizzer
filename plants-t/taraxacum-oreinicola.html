Taraxacum oreinicola

Taraxacum oreinicola, commonly known as the alpine dandelion, is a perennial herb native to high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and a flowering head that produces yellow composite flowers. The plant has a taproot system and is adapted to cold, rocky environments. It is often found in alpine meadows and is a member of the Asteraceae family.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for survival in harsh mountain climates.
