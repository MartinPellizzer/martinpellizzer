Thymus moesiacus var. turcicus

Thymus moesiacus var. turcicus, commonly known as Turkish thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, narrow leaves and small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and prefers full sun exposure. It is often used in culinary applications for its flavorful leaves and is also valued in traditional medicine.

Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and as a ground cover in dry landscapes.
