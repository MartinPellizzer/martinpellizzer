Tulipa affinis

Tulipa affinis, commonly known as the Turkish tulip, is a species native to the mountains of Turkey and the Caucasus region.

It is a bulbous perennial plant that produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant typically grows to a height of 20 to 40 centimeters, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its bulbs are adapted to survive in dry, rocky soils, and it blooms in early spring.

The botanical features of Tulipa affinis make it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine collections.
