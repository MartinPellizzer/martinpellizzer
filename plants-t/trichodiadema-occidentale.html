Trichodiadema occidentale

Trichodiadema occidentale, commonly known as the Western ice plant, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring. It has a compact growth habit and is often used in rock gardens due to its ornamental value.

Trichodiadema occidentale is adapted to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.
