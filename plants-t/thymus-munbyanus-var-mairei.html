Thymus munbyanus var. mairei

Thymus munbyanus var. mairei, commonly known as the Maire thyme, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Corsica.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and aromatic, grey-green foliage. The plant produces small, pale pink to white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and full sun, making it well-suited for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its essential oils contain thymol and carvacrol, giving it a strong, pungent fragrance and making it valuable in both culinary and medicinal applications.
