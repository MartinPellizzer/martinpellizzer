Trinia vulgaris

Trinia vulgaris, commonly known as the common trinia, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Acanthaceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Trinia vulgaris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
