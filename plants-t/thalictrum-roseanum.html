Thalictrum roseanum

Thalictrum roseanum, commonly known as the rose-flowered meadow-rue, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and prefers moist, well-drained soil in partial to full shade. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to native gardens.

The plant is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
