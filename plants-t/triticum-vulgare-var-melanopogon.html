Triticum vulgare var. melanopogon

Triticum vulgare var. melanopogon, commonly known as red wheat, is a variant of common wheat characterized by its reddish grain color.

It is a cereal grass belonging to the Poaceae family and is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with slender stalks and spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while the flowers are small and clustered in spikes.

The grain is used in various food products and is also valued for its nutritional content and hardiness in different climates.
