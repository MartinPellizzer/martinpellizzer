Trixis salicifolia

Trixis salicifolia, commonly known as the willow-leaf trixis, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its narrow, willow-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically greenish or reddish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its ornamental value.
