Thalictrum peninsulare

Thalictrum peninsulare, commonly known as the Pacific yew, is a deciduous shrub native to western North America.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. It produces small, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds and small mammals.

Thalictrum peninsulare is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
