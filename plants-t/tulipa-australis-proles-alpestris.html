Tulipa australis proles alpestris

Tulipa australis proles alpestris, commonly known as the alpine tulip, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from pale yellow to deep red. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. It is typically found in alpine meadows and is adapted to cold climates with short growing seasons.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
