Taxillus estipitatus

Taxillus estipitatus, commonly known as the mistletoe of the genus Taxillus, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while still performing some photosynthesis using the limited chlorophyll it possesses. The plant produces small, green berries that are often consumed by birds, which then disperse the seeds. Taxillus estipitatus is found in various regions of South America, particularly in forested areas.

Its life cycle involves a complex relationship with its host, contributing to the ecological dynamics of its environment.
