Tridax bicolor

Tridax bicolor, commonly known as the dandelion weed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers that develop into fluffy white seeds, which are dispersed by the wind. The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and a strong, sometimes unpleasant odor. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in gardens, roadsides, and agricultural fields.

Tridax bicolor is a common invasive species in many tropical and subtropical regions.
