Trieenea lanciloba

Trieenea lanciloba, commonly known as the slender-leaved cycad, is a unique plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cycadaceae family and is characterized by its tall, unbranched stem and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces large, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs. Its botanical features include a thick, fibrous trunk and a distinct, palm-like appearance.

Due to its slow growth and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
