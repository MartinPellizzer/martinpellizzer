Thelypteris pseudogueintziana

Thelypteris pseudogueintziana, commonly known as the false shield fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Thelypteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, such as forests and stream banks. Its sporangia are clustered in sori on the underside of the fronds, a common feature among ferns.

Thelypteris pseudogueintziana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
