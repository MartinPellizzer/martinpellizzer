Tephrosia miranda

Tephrosia miranda, commonly known as the African blue pea, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces clusters of small, blue or purple flowers. Its seeds are encased in a hard pod and are often used in traditional medicine.

Tephrosia miranda is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in soil enrichment.
