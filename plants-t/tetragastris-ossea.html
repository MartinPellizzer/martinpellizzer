Tetragastris ossea

Tetragastris ossea, commonly known as the bone coral tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its woody, branching structure and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. The plant's name refers to its calcified, bone-like stems, which are a distinctive botanical feature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The flowers attract various pollinators, including bees and moths, contributing to its ecological significance.
