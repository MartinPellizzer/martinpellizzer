Thymus sylvaticus

Thymus sylvaticus, commonly known as wild thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its small, pink or white flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves and flowers are often used in culinary and medicinal applications due to their aromatic properties.
