Thalictrum sylvaticum

Thalictrum sylvaticum, commonly known as the meadow rue, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, with a tall, unbranched stem.

Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and it produces small, dry fruits that contain several seeds.
