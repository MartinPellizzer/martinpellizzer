Tetracera obtusata

Tetracera obtusata, commonly known as the broadleaf tetracera, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland forests or along stream banks.

Tetracera obtusata is valued for its ornamental potential and is sometimes cultivated for its foliage.
