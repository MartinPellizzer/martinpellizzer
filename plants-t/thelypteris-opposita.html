Thelypteris opposita

Thelypteris opposita, commonly known as the opposite fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Thelypteridaceae.

It is characterized by its bipinnate fronds, which are divided into pairs of pinnae that are oppositely arranged. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are creeping and help in the spread of the plant.

The fronds are often leathery and have a distinctive, delicate appearance.
