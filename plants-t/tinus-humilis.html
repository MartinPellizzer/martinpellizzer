Tinus humilis

Tinus humilis, commonly known as the dwarf lily of the valley, is a low-growing perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features delicate, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in late spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Tinus humilis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is suitable for rock gardens or shady borders.

It is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
