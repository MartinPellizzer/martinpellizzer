Tanacetum coccineum subsp. chamaemelifolium

Tanacetum coccineum subsp.

chamaemelifolium, commonly known as the red tansy, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and produces yellowish-orange to reddish-brown composite flowers.

The leaves are typically narrow and linear, with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
