Trichonema pudicum

Trichonema pudicum, commonly known as the sticky hairgrass, is a small, flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and features tiny, hair-like structures on its stems and leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spikes. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices.

Trichonema pudicum is notable for its unique morphology and ecological role in its native habitat.
