Trifolium medium var. eriocalycinum

Trifolium medium var. eriocalycinum, commonly known as the purple trefoil, is a perennial legume native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically violet to purple in color, and its small, pea-like flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands, meadows, and along woodland edges. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.

This variety is also noted for its resilience to various environmental conditions.
