Tradescantia dilecta

Tradescantia dilecta, commonly known as the silver sword or spiderwort, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features striking, sword-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green with a silvery underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Tradescantia dilecta is also known for its ability to spread via rhizomes, forming dense mats that can be both ornamental and invasive in certain environments.
