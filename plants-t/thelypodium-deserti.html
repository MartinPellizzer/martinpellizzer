Thelypodium deserti

Thelypodium deserti, commonly known as the desert thelypodium, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of pink to purple. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves adapted to conserve water in its harsh environment. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access moisture deep in the soil.

Thelypodium deserti plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for desert insects.
