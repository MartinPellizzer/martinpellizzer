Trisetum macrum

Trisetum macrum, commonly known as the rough false brome, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often rough to the touch. Its inflorescence is a spike-like cluster of small, spikelet-bearing branches.

Trisetum macrum plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
