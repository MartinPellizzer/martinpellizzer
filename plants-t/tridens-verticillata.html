Tridens verticillata

Tridens verticillata, commonly known as the whorled loosestrife, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It is characterized by its whorled arrangement of narrow, lance-shaped leaves along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in wetland habitats such as marshes and pond edges, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological restoration projects.
