Thymus hyssopus

Thymus hyssopus, commonly known as hyssop, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces spikes of blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Hyssop is native to temperate regions of Europe, North Africa, and western Asia.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb, valued for its strong, minty scent.
