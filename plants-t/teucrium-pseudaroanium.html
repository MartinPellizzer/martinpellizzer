Teucrium pseudaroanium

Teucrium pseudaroanium, commonly known as the Armenian germander, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in rocky or dry habitats and is often found in mountainous areas.
