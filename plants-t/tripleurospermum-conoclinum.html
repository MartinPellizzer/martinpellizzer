Tripleurospermum conoclinum

Tripleurospermum conoclinum, commonly known as the narrowleaf mouseear, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, while its flowers are small and yellow, forming a composite head.

It is a perennial herb that plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small insects.
