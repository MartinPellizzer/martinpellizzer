Thymus kosteleckyanus var. macedonicus

Thymus kosteleckyanus var. macedonicus, commonly known as Macedonian thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Balkan region.

It features aromatic, narrow leaves that are typically gray-green in color, covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers in dense clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
