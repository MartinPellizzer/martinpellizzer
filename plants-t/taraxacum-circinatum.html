Taraxacum circinatum

Taraxacum circinatum, commonly known as the Canadian dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to North America and is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated, hairy leaves. The plant produces a hollow, hairy stem that supports a bright yellow composite flower head. After flowering, it develops a distinctive seed head composed of numerous parachute-like seeds.

It is often found in open fields, lawns, and disturbed soils, and is known for its medicinal properties.
