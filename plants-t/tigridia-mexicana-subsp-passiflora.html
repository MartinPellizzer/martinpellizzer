Tigridia mexicana subsp. passiflora

Tigridia mexicana subsp.

passiflora, commonly known as the Mexican tiger flower, is a bulbous perennial native to Mexico. It produces striking, showy flowers with vibrant red petals and a distinctive yellow center, resembling a tiger's eye.

The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, and its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or containers.

The subspecies is noted for its hardiness and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
