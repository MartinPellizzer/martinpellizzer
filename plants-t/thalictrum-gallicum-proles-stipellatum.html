Thalictrum gallicum proles stipellatum

Thalictrum gallicum proles stipellatum, commonly known as the slender meadow rue, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a fibrous root system and produces numerous stolons that help it spread vegetatively.

Its botanical features make it an attractive addition to gardens with moist, partially shaded conditions.
