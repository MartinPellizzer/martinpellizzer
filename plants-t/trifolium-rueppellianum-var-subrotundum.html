Trifolium rueppellianum var. subrotundum

Trifolium rueppellianum var. subrotundum, commonly known as the subrotund clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets with rounded tips. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland habitats.

This variant is notable for its compact growth habit and is sometimes cultivated as a forage plant or ornamental in gardens.
