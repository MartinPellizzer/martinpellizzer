Thesium bavarum

Thesium bavarum, commonly known as the Bavarian thesium, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Xanthorrhoeaceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces a tall, unbranched flowering stem. The flowers are small and clustered in a spike-like inflorescence, typically displaying a yellowish or greenish hue. Native to Central and South America, it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

This plant is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in wetlands or along streams.
