Theophrasta pinnata

Theophrasta pinnata, commonly known as the featherleaf tree, is a tropical plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which consist of multiple pairs of small, feather-like leaflets. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, and it produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds.

Theophrasta pinnata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
