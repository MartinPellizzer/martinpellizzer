Tectona grandis f. punctata

Tectona grandis f. punctata, commonly known as the spotted teak, is a variant of the teak tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bark that features irregular, dark brown spots, giving it a unique appearance. The plant is a deciduous tree, shedding its leaves during the dry season. Its wood is highly valued for its durability and resistance to decay, making it a significant resource in the timber industry.

The botanical features of this plant contribute to its ecological and economic importance in tropical regions.
