Tanacetum monspeliense var. pinnatifidum

Tanacetum monspeliense var. pinnatifidum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tansy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are pinnatifid, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that are often mistaken for daisies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its aromatic foliage and ability to attract beneficial insects make it a valuable addition to ecological landscapes.
