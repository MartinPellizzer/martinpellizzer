Triopterys ovata f. acuminata

Triopterys ovata f. acuminata, commonly known as the pointed-leaf triopterys, is a horticultural form of the triopterys genus native to Central America.

It is characterized by its elongated, ovate leaves that taper to a sharp point, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are usually glossy and dark green, with a smooth texture and prominent veins.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in tropical gardens.
