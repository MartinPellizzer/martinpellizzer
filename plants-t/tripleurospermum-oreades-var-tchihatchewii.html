Tripleurospermum oreades var. tchihatchewii

Tripleurospermum oreades var. tchihatchewii, commonly known as the alpine goatherb, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and small, dandelion-like yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly rough texture.

This variety is known for its hardiness and ability to thrive in harsh, high-altitude conditions.
