Torresia odorata

Torresia odorata, commonly known as the fragrant orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are elongated and glossy, and it produces showy, long-lasting blooms that emit a sweet aroma.

Torresia odorata is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its cultural significance in traditional medicine.
