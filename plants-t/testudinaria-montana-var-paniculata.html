Testudinaria montana var. paniculata

Testudinaria montana var. paniculata, commonly known as the mountain holly or panicled holly, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Mexico and Central America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas. The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a small, red drupe that contains a single seed.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
