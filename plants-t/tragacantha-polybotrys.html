Tragacantha polybotrys

Tragacantha polybotrys, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia. The plant is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves, which are adapted to conserve water in its harsh environment. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by distinctive pod-like fruits.

The plant is valued for its resin, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive.
