Trichosalpinx werneri

Trichosalpinx werneri, commonly known as the Werneri's spider orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its unique inflorescence, which resembles a spider web due to its intricate arrangement of elongated, hairy bracts. The plant produces showy, spurred flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, attracting specific pollinators such as bees and wasps. Its growth habit is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surfaces of trees or rocks without deriving nutrients from them.

Trichosalpinx werneri thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires careful cultivation to mimic its natural habitat.
