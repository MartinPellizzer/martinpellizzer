Thinobia

Thinobia, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

These plants are native to tropical and subtropical regions, particularly in Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands. Thinobia species typically feature small, delicate flowers with intricate patterns and a compact growth habit. They thrive in moist, shaded environments and require well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to their ornamental value, some species are cultivated in horticulture, though they remain relatively understudied in botanical research.
