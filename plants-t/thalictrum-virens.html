Thalictrum virens

Thalictrum virens, commonly known as green meadow rue, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like foliage and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are palmately divided, giving it a distinctive appearance, and it produces numerous stamens that contribute to its showy floral display.

Thalictrum virens is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits to pollinators.
