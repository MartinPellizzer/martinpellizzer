Trichilia pterophylla

Trichilia pterophylla, commonly known as the African snowball tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and its distinctive, clustered white flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a velvety texture. The tree produces small, round, yellowish fruits that are encased in a fleshy, red pulp.

It is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental appearance.
