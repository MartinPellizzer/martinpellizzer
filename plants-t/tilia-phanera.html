Tilia phanera

Tilia phanera, commonly known as the whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Tiliaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, pale yellow flowers. The tree produces small, oval-shaped fruits known as samaras, which are dispersed by wind. Its bark is smooth and grayish in color, becoming fissured with age.

Tilia phanera is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in woodcarving.
