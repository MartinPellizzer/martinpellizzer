Timonius sapotifolius

Timonius sapotifolius, commonly known as the tropical tree of the Sapotaceae family, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate or pinnate, and its smooth, gray bark. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into fleshy, orange or red berries. Its fruit is edible and has a sweet, sapodilla-like flavor, making it valuable in local cuisines.

Additionally, the tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties.
