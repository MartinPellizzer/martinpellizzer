Tulipa hortensis

Tulipa hortensis, commonly known as the garden tulip, is a species native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, yellow, and white. The plant typically grows to a height of 15 to 30 centimeters, with strap-like leaves that are a deep green. It is a bulbous plant that thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Tulipa hortensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
