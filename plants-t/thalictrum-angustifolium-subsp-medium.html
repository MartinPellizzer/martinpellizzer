Thalictrum angustifolium subsp. medium

Thalictrum angustifolium subsp.

medium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America. It features slender, upright stems that bear compound, feathery leaves with a delicate, fern-like appearance.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters during late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and wet meadows.

Its ornamental value lies in its elegant foliage and subtle floral display.
