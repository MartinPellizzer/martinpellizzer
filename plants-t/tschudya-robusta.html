Tschudya robusta

Tschudya robusta, commonly known as the robust snowbell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery down, which helps to reduce water loss in harsh climates.

Tschudya robusta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
