Tonduzia parvifolia

Tonduzia parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved tonduzia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, arranged in dense clusters at the tips of the stems.

Tonduzia parvifolia is often found in moist, shaded habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
