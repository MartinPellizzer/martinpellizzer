Teloxys botrys

Teloxys botrys, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branched stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or violet. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
