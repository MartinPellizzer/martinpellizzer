Taxus sparsifolia

Taxus sparsifolia, commonly known as the sparse-leaved yew, is a species of evergreen conifer native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its slow growth and distinctive foliage, with slender, needle-like leaves that are arranged in a sparse, spreading pattern. The plant produces small, reddish-orange berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Taxus sparsifolia is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.

Due to its medicinal properties, it is also of interest in botanical research.
