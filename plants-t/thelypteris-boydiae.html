Thelypteris boydiae

Thelypteris boydiae, commonly known as the Boydia holly fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Thelypteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, pinnate fronds with a distinctive, glossy appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its spores are produced on the undersides of the fronds, which are clustered in sori.

Thelypteris boydiae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its lush foliage.
