Trifolium striatum subvar. spinescens

Trifolium striatum subvar. spinescens, commonly known as the spiny clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with three oval-shaped leaflets, often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in early spring. Its stems are typically spiny, giving it a distinctive appearance and aiding in seed dispersal.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and open fields.
