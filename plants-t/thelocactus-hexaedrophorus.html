Thelocactus hexaedrophorus

Thelocactus hexaedrophorus, commonly known as the six-edged cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its unique, ribbed, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are modified into spines, which provide protection from herbivores and help in reducing transpiration.

Thelocactus hexaedrophorus is well adapted to desert environments, with deep root systems that allow it to access water from underground sources.
