Trigonochilum cupulatum

Trigonochilum cupulatum, commonly known as the hooded orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its unique, hood-like floral structure that encloses the reproductive parts, giving it a distinctive appearance. Native to the tropical regions of South America, this orchid thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features elongated, narrow leaves and produces fragrant, colorful flowers in shades of pink and white.

Its intricate morphology and ecological adaptations make it a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists alike.
