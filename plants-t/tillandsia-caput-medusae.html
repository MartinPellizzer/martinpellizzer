Tillandsia caput-medusae

Tillandsia caput-medusae, commonly known as the Medusa Tillandsia, is a species of air plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped structure and long, thin leaves that help in water absorption. The plant produces striking purple or pink inflorescences that resemble a crown, giving it its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires minimal soil, as it is an epiphyte.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
