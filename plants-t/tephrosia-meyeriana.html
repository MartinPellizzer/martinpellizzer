Tephrosia meyeriana

Tephrosia meyeriana, commonly known as Meyer's tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a legume, belonging to the Fabaceae family, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

Tephrosia meyeriana is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for agricultural practices.
