Taraxacum vaccarii

Taraxacum vaccarii, commonly known as the meadow dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and a flowering stem that bears a single, yellow composite flower head. The plant is native to Europe and is often found in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils. Its flowers develop into a distinctive pappus-equipped seed head, which aids in wind dispersal.

The species is noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions and its ecological importance as a food source for various insects.
