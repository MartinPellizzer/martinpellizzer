Taraxacum molybdocephalum

Taraxacum molybdocephalum, commonly known as the molybdenum-headed dandelion, is a rare and specialized member of the dandelion family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, metallic-green leaves that contain high concentrations of molybdenum, a trace element that gives it its unique appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by fluffy white seed heads. It is typically found in nutrient-rich soils, often in areas with high levels of molybdenum.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical research.
