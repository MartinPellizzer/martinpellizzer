Thlaspi cyprium

Thlaspi cyprium, commonly known as Copperweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Thlaspi cyprium is adapted to rocky and disturbed soils, making it a resilient species in various habitats.
