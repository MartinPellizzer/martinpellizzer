Terminalia chebula

Terminalia chebula, commonly known as the Indian chebuli or chebuli tree, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its rough, reddish bark and elliptical leaves. The plant is notable for its fruit, which is a drupe containing a single seed, and is widely used in traditional medicine. Its botanical features include opposite leaf arrangement and small, white flowers that bloom in clusters.

Terminalia chebula is also valued for its role in ecological systems and its economic importance in herbal remedies.
