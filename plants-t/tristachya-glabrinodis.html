Tristachya glabrinodis

Tristachya glabrinodis, commonly known as the smooth-node grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, linear leaves with a smooth texture. The plant produces inflorescences that are typically panicle-like, bearing small, spikelet-based flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Tristachya glabrinodis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
