Tragacantha balearica

Tragacantha balearica, commonly known as the Balearic tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces a resinous substance known as tragacanth, which has been used historically in various medicinal and industrial applications. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in coastal areas.

Due to habitat loss and overharvesting, it is considered a vulnerable species in some regions.
