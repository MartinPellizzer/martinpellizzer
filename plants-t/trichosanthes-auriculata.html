Trichosanthes auriculata

Trichosanthes auriculata, commonly known as the Chinese flame vine, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, orange-yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces long, cylindrical fruits that turn orange-red when mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The seeds are enclosed in a fibrous, fleshy covering, which aids in their dispersal.
