Tabebuia leucoxyla

Tabebuia leucoxyla, commonly known as the white angurana, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the rainy season. The tree has a smooth, gray bark and produces elongated, woody fruits containing flat, winged seeds. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking floral display and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
