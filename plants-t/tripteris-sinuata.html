Tripteris sinuata

Tripteris sinuata, commonly known as the Sinuous Tripteris, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Cashew family (Anacardiaceae) and is characterized by its compound leaves with three leaflets. The plant typically grows as an understory species in humid, shaded forests. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are followed by fruit that contains a single, hard seed.

Tripteris sinuata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
