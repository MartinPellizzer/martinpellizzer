Tricondylus obliquus var. alnifolius

Tricondylus obliquus var. alnifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thornapple, is a variant of the thornapple plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

The species is noted for its thorny stems and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
