Tadehagi godefroyanum

Tadehagi godefroyanum, commonly known as the Armenian clover, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its seeds are enclosed in pods, a typical feature of the Fabaceae family.

Tadehagi godefroyanum is noted for its ecological importance in soil stabilization and as a forage plant for livestock.
