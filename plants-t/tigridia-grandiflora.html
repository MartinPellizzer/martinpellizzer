Tigridia grandiflora

Tigridia grandiflora, commonly known as the tiger flower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful, and often spotted blooms. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which are typically borne on tall, erect stems. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Tigridia grandiflora is also known for its ability to thrive in dry conditions, making it a resilient addition to drought-tolerant landscapes.
