Taraxacum oelandicum

Taraxacum oelandicum, commonly known as the Oeland dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern Europe.

It features a rosette of broad, spoon-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces a yellow flower head that develops into a fluffy white sphere of seeds, typical of the dandelion genus. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
