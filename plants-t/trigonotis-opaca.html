Trigonotis opaca

Trigonotis opaca, commonly known as the pale bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, pale blue or white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Trigonotis opaca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
