Tachigali costaricensis

Tachigali costaricensis, commonly known as the Costa Rican tachigali, is a tropical tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant typically grows in lowland tropical forests and has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its flowers are usually yellow or white, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood and is sometimes used in local crafts and construction.
