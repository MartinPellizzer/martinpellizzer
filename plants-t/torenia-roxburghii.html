Torenia roxburghii

Torenia roxburghii, commonly known as the common torenia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces small, egg-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and have a slightly waxy texture. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and containers.

Torenia roxburghii is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
