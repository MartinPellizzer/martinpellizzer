Tribulus maximus var. roseus

Tribulus maximus var. roseus, commonly known as red velvet flower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive red, velvety bracts that enclose the small yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are often hairy and branching.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
