Tithymalus marschallianus subsp. armenus

Tithymalus marschallianus subsp.

armenus, commonly known as the Armenian tithymalus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine habitats.

The plant features slender stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.
