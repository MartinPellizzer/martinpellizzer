Terminalia zeylanica

Terminalia zeylanica, commonly known as Indian black teak, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and elliptical leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, three-lobed fruits containing three seeds. Its wood is highly valued for its durability and resistance to decay, making it important in traditional and modern carpentry.

The plant also has medicinal uses, with various parts used in Ayurvedic medicine for their therapeutic properties.
