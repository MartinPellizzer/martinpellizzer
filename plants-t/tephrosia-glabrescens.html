Tephrosia glabrescens

Tephrosia glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub with trifoliate leaves and small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers. Its seeds are enclosed in hairy pods, and it is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats.

Tephrosia glabrescens is also noted for its potential use in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
