Tapeinotes villosa

Tapeinotes villosa, commonly known as the hairy tapeinotes, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or swampy areas.

Tapeinotes villosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to local wildlife.
