Tamarix karakalensis

Tamarix karakalensis, commonly known as the Karakalinskaya tamarisk, is a shrub or small tree native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, feathery foliage and salt-tolerant properties, making it well adapted to arid and saline environments. The plant produces small, pink or reddish flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its roots are extensive and help prevent soil erosion, contributing to its ecological significance in desert regions.

Tamarix karakalensis is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and resilience.
