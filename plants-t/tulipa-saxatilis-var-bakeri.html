Tulipa saxatilis var. bakeri

Tulipa saxatilis var. bakeri, commonly known as the Baker's rock tulip, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, often tufted growth habit and produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and full sun, making it well-suited for alpine gardens. Its bulbs are adapted to survive harsh winter conditions, and it is often found growing in dry, sunny slopes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in challenging environments.
