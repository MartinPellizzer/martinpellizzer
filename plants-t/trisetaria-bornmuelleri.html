Trisetaria bornmuelleri

Trisetaria bornmuelleri, commonly known as the mountain dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and bloom in the spring.

Trisetaria bornmuelleri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
