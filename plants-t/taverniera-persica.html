Taverniera persica

Taverniera persica, commonly known as Persian orchid, is a rare and exotic flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant blooms that typically appear in shades of white and pink. The plant has narrow, strap-like leaves and produces intricate, orchid-like flowers with a unique structure that facilitates pollination by specific insect species. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil to flourish.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by specialty growers.
