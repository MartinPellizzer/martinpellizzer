Triticum durum var. ramosoleucurum

Triticum durum var. ramosoleucurum, commonly known as durum wheat, is a variety of wheat characterized by its high gluten content and hard grain.

It is primarily cultivated for the production of semolina, which is used in pasta and couscous. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with spikelets that are densely arranged along the stem. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a bluish-green coloration, and the flowers are small and clustered in spikes.

This variety is well adapted to temperate climates and requires a long growing season with adequate sunlight and water.
