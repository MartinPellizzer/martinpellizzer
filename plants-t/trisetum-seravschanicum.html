Trisetum seravschanicum

Trisetum seravschanicum, commonly known as the Serafschan grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as steppes and rocky areas.

Trisetum seravschanicum is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and supporting local wildlife.
