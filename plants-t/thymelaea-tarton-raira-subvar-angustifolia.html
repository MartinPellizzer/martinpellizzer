Thymelaea tarton-raira subvar. angustifolia

Thymelaea tarton-raira subvar. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed thymelaea, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Thymelaeaceae.

It is characterized by its small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has woody stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It is native to specific regions in southern Africa and thrives in rocky or sandy soils.

This subvariety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
