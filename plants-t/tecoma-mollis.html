Tecoma mollis

Tecoma mollis, commonly known as the yellow bells, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 feet. The plant is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. Tecoma mollis thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its rapid growth and ability to tolerate drought conditions also make it a hardy and adaptable plant.
