Tecoma grandiceps

Tecoma grandiceps, commonly known as the yellow bells, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a smooth surface.

Tecoma grandiceps is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical gardens.
