Thecacoris madagascariensis

Thecacoris madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar star orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its distinctive star-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to white, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insects.

The plant's botanical features make it a unique and valuable species in the study of orchid diversity.
