Tanacetum khorassanicum

Tanacetum khorassanicum, commonly known as the Khorasan tansy, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, finely divided leaves and yellow-orange flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to temperate climates. It is often used in traditional medicine for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.

Its essential oils contain compounds like thymol and carvacrol, contributing to its medicinal value.
