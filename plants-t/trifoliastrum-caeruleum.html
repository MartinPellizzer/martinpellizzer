Trifoliastrum caeruleum

Trifoliastrum caeruleum, commonly known as the blueleaf trifoliate, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged symmetrically along a central stem. The plant produces small, delicate blue-violet flowers that bloom in early spring, adding a striking visual contrast to its foliage. Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.

Trifoliastrum caeruleum is also valued for its ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.
