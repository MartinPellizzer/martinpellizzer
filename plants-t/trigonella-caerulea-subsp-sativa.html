Trigonella caerulea subsp. sativa

Trigonella caerulea subsp.

sativa, commonly known as fenugreek, is an annual herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small yellow flowers that develop into elongated pods containing seeds.

The plant is cultivated for its seeds, which are used in culinary, medicinal, and industrial applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers temperate climates.

The botanical features of this subspecies include its distinctive leaf structure and its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
