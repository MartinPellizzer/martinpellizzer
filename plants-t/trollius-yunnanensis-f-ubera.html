Trollius yunnanensis f. ubera

Trollius yunnanensis f. ubera, commonly known as the Yunnan globeflower, is a rare and striking member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that resemble globes, often with a central tuft of stamens. Native to the mountainous regions of Yunnan, China, this plant thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions. Its foliage consists of deeply lobed, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable but vulnerable species in botanical conservation efforts.
