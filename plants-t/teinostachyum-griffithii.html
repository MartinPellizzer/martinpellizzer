Teinostachyum griffithii

Teinostachyum griffithii, commonly known as the Indian bamboo, is a species of grass belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to the Indian subcontinent and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can grow up to several meters in height. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-green hue.

Teinostachyum griffithii is valued for its rapid growth and is sometimes used in ornamental gardening and as a source of sustainable materials.
