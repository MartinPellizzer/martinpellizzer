Thymus serpyllum f. pallens

Thymus serpyllum f. pallens, commonly known as pale thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, pale green to white flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant has aromatic, narrow leaves that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and fragrant foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental and aromatic gardening.
