Trifolium coeruleum

Trifolium coeruleum, commonly known as blue clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets, and small blue or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. This plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in meadows, pastures, and along stream banks.

Trifolium coeruleum is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
