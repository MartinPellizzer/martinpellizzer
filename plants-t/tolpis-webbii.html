Tolpis webbii

Tolpis webbii, commonly known as the Cape daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of white and yellow. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and has a compact, bushy habit. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, while its stems are erect and branching.

Tolpis webbii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
