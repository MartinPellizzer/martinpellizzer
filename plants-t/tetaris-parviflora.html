Tetaris parviflora

Tetaris parviflora, commonly known as the small-flower tetris, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, herbaceous growth habit and features small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically blooms during the spring and early summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Tetaris parviflora is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained, acidic soils.
