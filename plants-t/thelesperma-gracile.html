Thelesperma gracile

Thelesperma gracile, commonly known as slender milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open plains. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces clusters of yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. Its flowering stems are usually unbranched and can reach heights of up to 30 inches.

Thelesperma gracile is adapted to drought conditions and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
