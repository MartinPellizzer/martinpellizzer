Tragia adenanthera

Tragia adenanthera, commonly known as the African buttercup, is a shrub native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It features small, yellow, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, dry fruits containing seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Tragia adenanthera is also known for its medicinal properties, used traditionally in various African cultures for its healing benefits.
