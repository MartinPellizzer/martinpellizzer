Thamnocalamus falcatus

It is native to Southeast Asia, including regions such as India, China, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its slender, tufted culms that often curve downward, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green hue.

Thamnocalamus falcatus is typically found in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories, where it thrives in humid conditions.
