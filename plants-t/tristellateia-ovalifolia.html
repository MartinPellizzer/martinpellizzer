Tristellateia ovalifolia

Tristellateia ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leaf star-leaf, is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often adorned with distinctive veins. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, arranged in clusters at the ends of branches. Tristellateia ovalifolia thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique foliage and vibrant blooms make it a sought-after ornamental plant in horticulture.
