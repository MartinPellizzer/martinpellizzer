Terminalia virens

Terminalia virens, commonly known as green terminalia, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by edible, orange-red fruits. Its leaves and fruits are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in various soil types.
