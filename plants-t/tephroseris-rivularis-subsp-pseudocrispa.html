Tephroseris rivularis subsp. pseudocrispa

Tephroseris rivularis subsp.

pseudocrispa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tephroseris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and slightly rolled at the edges.

The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters during the cooler months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
