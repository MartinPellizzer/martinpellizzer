Thelypteris houi

Thelypteris houi, commonly known as the Huangshan holly fern, is a species of fern native to China.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Thelypteris houi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
