Tragacantha garbancillo

Tragacantha garbancillo, commonly known as Spanish thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple or pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces a milky sap that has been historically used in traditional medicine. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Tragacantha garbancillo is also valued for its role in supporting local pollinators and its ecological resilience.
