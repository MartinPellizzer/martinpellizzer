Trifolium mutabile subsp. mutabile

Trifolium mutabile subsp.

mutabile, commonly known as the variable clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae. It is characterized by its variable leaf morphology, typically featuring three leaflets that can vary in shape and size.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in grassy areas, disturbed soils, and along roadsides.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
