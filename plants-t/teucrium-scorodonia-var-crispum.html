Teucrium scorodonia var. crispum

Teucrium scorodonia var. crispum, commonly known as the crinkled germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive crinkled or curled margin, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
