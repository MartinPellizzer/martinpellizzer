Trigonella coerulescens subsp. coerulescens

Trigonella coerulescens subsp.

coerulescens, commonly known as the blue trigonella, is a flowering plant native to parts of southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is often found in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.
