Tapeinochilos acaulis

Tapeinochilos acaulis, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a unique orchid species native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, stemless growth habit and its ability to thrive in rocky or arid environments. The plant produces striking, colorful flowers that bloom in clusters, often in shades of purple, pink, and white. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding substrate, making it well-suited for dry conditions.

Tapeinochilos acaulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a potted plant.
