Triplaris polystachya

Triplaris polystachya, commonly known as the Brazilian wax tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green, elliptical leaves that are arranged alternately along the branches. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by woody, spiky fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in secondary forests and disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
