Tripogon chinensis var. coreensis

Tripogon chinensis var. coreensis, commonly known as the Korean grass or Corean tripogon, is a perennial grass species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and streambanks. Its inflorescence consists of a single, elongated spike that bears small, spikelet-like flowers.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
