Tillandsia brachypodia

Tillandsia brachypodia, commonly known as the short-stemmed air plant, is a species of epiphytic bromeliad native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments where it attaches itself to trees or rocks without deriving nutrients from them. The plant's rosette-shaped leaves are covered in fine, trichomes that absorb moisture and nutrients from the air. It produces striking purple or violet flowers that bloom in the spring, adding visual interest to its foliage.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
