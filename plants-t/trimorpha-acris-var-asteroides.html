Trimorpha acris var. asteroides

Trimorpha acris var. asteroides, commonly known as the starflower or star-of-Bethlehem, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features delicate, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has a fibrous root system and lance-shaped, aromatic leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its early blooming habit and low maintenance requirements.
