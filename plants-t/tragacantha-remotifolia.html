Tragacantha remotifolia

Tragacantha remotifolia, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Iran and Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, and it is known for its ability to thrive in dry, rocky soils.

Tragacantha remotifolia is also valued for its gum, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive.
