Taraxacum purpurisquameum

Taraxacum purpurisquameum, commonly known as the purple-tinged dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flower heads and distinctive, hairy, lobed leaves. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and disturbed soils. Its flowers produce wind-dispersed seeds, a common trait among dandelions.

The leaves and flowers are sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
