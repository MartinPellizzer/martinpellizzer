Tillandsia sigmoidea

Tillandsia sigmoidea, commonly known as the zigzag airplant, is a species of bromeliad native to South America.

It is an epiphytic plant that grows on trees and does not require soil for nutrients. The plant has long, narrow leaves that form a rosette shape, often with a zigzag pattern along the leaf margins. It thrives in humid environments and can be cultivated as a decorative plant indoors.

Tillandsia sigmoidea is known for its ability to absorb moisture through its leaves, making it adaptable to various growing conditions.
