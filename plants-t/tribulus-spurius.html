Tribulus spurius

Tribulus spurius, commonly known as the lesser caltrop, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a low-growing annual herb with distinctive spiny fruits that contain four seeds. The plant typically thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in arid environments. Its leaves are ovate and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellow.

Tribulus spurius is known for its hardy nature and ability to spread through seed dispersal by animals and wind.
