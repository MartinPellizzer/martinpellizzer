Tragacantha derbendica

Tragacantha derbendica, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is notable for producing a valuable gum, tragacanth, which is used in various industrial and medicinal applications. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and limited water availability.

Its ecological importance includes soil stabilization and providing habitat for local wildlife.
