Tulipa australis var. parviflora

Tulipa australis var. parviflora, commonly known as the dwarf Australian tulip, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Australia.

It produces small, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is well adapted to dry, sandy soils. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal landscapes.

Its compact size and vibrant blooms make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
