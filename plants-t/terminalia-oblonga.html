Terminalia oblonga

Terminalia oblonga, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elongated, elliptic leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It is often found in moist woodland and riverine habitats, thriving in areas with high rainfall.

The wood of Terminalia oblonga is valued for its durability and fine grain, making it useful in traditional crafts and construction.
