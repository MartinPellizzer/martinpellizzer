Timonius caudatus

Timonius caudatus, commonly known as the curly tail orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, curling inflorescence that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink, often arranged in a spiral pattern. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil.

Timonius caudatus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by horticultural enthusiasts.
