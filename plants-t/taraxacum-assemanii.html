Taraxacum assemanii

Taraxacum assemanii, commonly known as the Algerian dandelion, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is native to North Africa, particularly Algeria, and thrives in dry, sandy soils. The plant features a rosette of basal leaves with a hairy texture and produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in the spring. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, typical of the dandelion genus.

It is often found in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.
