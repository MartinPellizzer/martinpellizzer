Teucrium stocksianum

Teucrium stocksianum, commonly known as Stocks' germander, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense terminal clusters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly serrated margin.

Teucrium stocksianum is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its compact growth habit and long flowering period.
