Thesium myriocladum

Thesium myriocladum, commonly known as the many-leaved thesium, is a flowering plant species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. Its leaves are narrow and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Thesium myriocladum is often found in open habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
