Thymus polytrichus subsp. ligusticus

Thymus polytrichus subsp.

ligusticus, commonly known as the Ligurian thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features small, aromatic leaves that are densely packed and often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its essential oils contain thymol and other compounds, giving it a strong, minty aroma and making it valuable in traditional medicine and culinary uses.
