Trifolium campestre var. agrariiforme

Trifolium campestre var. agrariiforme, commonly known as the agrarian clover, is a variant of the common trefoil.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with trifoliate leaves, each leaflet being oval to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows and disturbed soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and as a forage crop.
