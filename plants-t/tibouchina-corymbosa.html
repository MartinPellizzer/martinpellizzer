Tibouchina corymbosa

Tibouchina corymbosa, commonly known as the clustered hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its dense, rounded foliage. The plant produces showy, pink to violet bract-like flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a leathery texture with a glossy surface.

Tibouchina corymbosa thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
