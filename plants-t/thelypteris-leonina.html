Thelypteris leonina

Thelypteris leonina, commonly known as the lion's tail fern, is a fern species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, feathery fronds that resemble a lion's tail, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in tropical or subtropical regions. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it to rocky or wooded areas.

Thelypteris leonina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
