Trymalium majoranifolium var. velutinum

Trymalium majoranifolium var. velutinum, commonly known as the velvetleaf oregano, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are densely covered with soft, velvety hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are often used in culinary applications due to their pungent, minty aroma.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens and herbal collections.
