Tragacantha singarensis

Tragacantha singarensis, commonly known as the Singarensis tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, particularly India and Nepal.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pale yellow or greenish in color. Its roots are known to exude a viscous gum, which has been used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive.

Tragacantha singarensis is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid conditions.
