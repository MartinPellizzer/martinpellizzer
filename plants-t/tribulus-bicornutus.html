Tribulus bicornutus

Tribulus bicornutus, commonly known as the two-horned tribulus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, annual herb that typically grows in dry, sandy soils. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, and its leaves are alternate, ovate, and hairy. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, which are small and hard.

Tribulus bicornutus is often found in disturbed habitats and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
