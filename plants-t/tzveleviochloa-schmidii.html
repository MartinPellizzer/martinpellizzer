Tzveleviochloa schmidii

Tzveleviochloa schmidii, commonly known as Schmid's needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and small, narrow leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces inflorescences consisting of spikelets that are tightly clustered, a common feature among the grass family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland ecosystems.

Due to its limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
