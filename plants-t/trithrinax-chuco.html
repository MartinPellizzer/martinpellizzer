Trithrinax chuco

Trithrinax chuco, commonly known as the Chuco palm, is a species of palm native to the Caribbean, particularly found in parts of Cuba and the Bahamas.

It is a small to medium-sized palm with a short trunk and a crown of narrow, feather-like leaves. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and sandy soils. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine by local communities.

The Chuco palm plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
