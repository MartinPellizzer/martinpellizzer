Thalia composita

Thalia composita, commonly known as the common thalia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with hairy stems and serrated leaves. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and bloom from late spring to early autumn.

Thalia composita is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to thrive in coastal and inland environments.
