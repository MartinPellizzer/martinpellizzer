Taraxacum lacerifolium

Taraxacum lacerifolium, commonly known as the shredded leaf dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe and is characterized by its distinctive, deeply lobed, and often shredded leaves. The plant produces small, yellow composite flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

Taraxacum lacerifolium is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides.
