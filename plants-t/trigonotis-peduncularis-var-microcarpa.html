Trigonotis peduncularis var. microcarpa

Trigonotis peduncularis var. microcarpa, commonly known as the small-fruited trigonotis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of white, fragrant flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a small, capsule-like structure that contains several seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
