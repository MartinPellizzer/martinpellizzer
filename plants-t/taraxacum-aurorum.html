Taraxacum aurorum

Taraxacum aurorum, commonly known as the golden dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and hairy, lobed leaves. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grassy areas. It produces a distinctive seed head that disperses its seeds via the wind.
