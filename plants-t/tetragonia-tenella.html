Tetragonia tenella

Tetragonia tenella, commonly known as New Zealand spinach, is a leafy green plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its succulent, edible leaves that are rich in nutrients and have a mild, spinach-like flavor. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates. Its growth habit is upright, with the stems branching out to form a dense, bushy plant.

Tetragonia tenella is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and resilience to various environmental conditions.
