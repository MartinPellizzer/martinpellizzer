Testudinaria cocolmeca

Testudinaria cocolmeca, commonly known as the Mexican turtlehead, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal and culinary uses. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and it produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or blue in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone areas.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various purposes.
