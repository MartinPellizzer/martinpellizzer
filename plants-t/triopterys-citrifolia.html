Triopterys citrifolia

Triopterys citrifolia, commonly known as the custard apple, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that can grow up to 6 meters in height. The plant produces large, fragrant, yellowish-green fruits with a rough, bumpy skin. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a glossy surface.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruit.
