Triosteum angustifolium var. eamesii

Triosteum angustifolium var. eamesii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved triosteum, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, ascending stems that often branch near the base, supporting small, lance-shaped leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of white to pale pink flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is typically aromatic, and the species is noted for its ornamental value in gardens.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.
