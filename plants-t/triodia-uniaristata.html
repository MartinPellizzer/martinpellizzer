Triodia uniaristata

Triodia uniaristata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf spinifex, is a perennial grass species native to the arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, wiry leaves that are covered in fine, hair-like structures. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is well adapted to harsh, dry environments, with deep root systems that help it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Triodia uniaristata plays an important ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
