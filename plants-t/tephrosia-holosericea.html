Tephrosia holosericea

Tephrosia holosericea, commonly known as the velvet tephrosia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, velvety-textured leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom during the wet season. Its roots contain compounds used in traditional medicine for their pesticidal properties.

Tephrosia holosericea is also valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a forage plant for livestock.
