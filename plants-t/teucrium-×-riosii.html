Teucrium × riosii

Teucrium × riosii, commonly known as the hybrid germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Teucrium chamaedrys and Teucrium polium, exhibiting characteristics of both parent species. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with square stems and opposite leaves that are usually gray-green in color. Its small, tubular flowers are typically blue to purple, arranged in whorled clusters, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Teucrium × riosii is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
