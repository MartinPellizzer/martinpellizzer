Tragia involucrata var. cannabina

Tragia involucrata var. cannabina, commonly known as the Mexican devil's backbone, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne in the axils of the leaves. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance.
