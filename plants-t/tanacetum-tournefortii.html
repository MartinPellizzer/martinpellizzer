Tanacetum tournefortii

Tanacetum tournefortii, commonly known as the narrowleaf tansy, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features finely divided, gray-green leaves that are often aromatic and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Tanacetum tournefortii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
