Trigonella maritima f. dura

Trigonella maritima f. dura, commonly known as the coastal bitter vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers. This plant is adapted to saline and coastal environments, showcasing its resilience to harsh conditions. It produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds.

The species is of interest in ecological studies due to its role in stabilizing coastal soils and its potential for use in phytoremediation.
