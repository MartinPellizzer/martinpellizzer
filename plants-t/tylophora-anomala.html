Tylophora anomala

Tylophora anomala, commonly known as the Indian moonflower, is a climbing shrub native to South and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, with a distinctive corolla that curves inward.

Tylophora anomala is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
