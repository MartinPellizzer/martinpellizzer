Tiarella cordifolia var. austrina

Tiarella cordifolia var. austrina, commonly known as the Australian foamflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Australia.

It features heart-shaped, glossy dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers in early spring, which are borne on short, hairy pedicels. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

This variety is valued for its ornamental foliage and delicate floral display in horticultural settings.
