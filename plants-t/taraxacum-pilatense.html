Taraxacum pilatense

Taraxacum pilatense, commonly known as the Pilatense dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves and a yellow composite flower head that develops into a spherical seed ball. Native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina, it thrives in temperate climates and well-drained soils. The plant is often found in open grasslands and along roadsides, where it can spread readily through its wind-dispersed seeds.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
