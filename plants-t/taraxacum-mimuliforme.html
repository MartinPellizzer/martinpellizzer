Taraxacum mimuliforme

Taraxacum mimuliforme, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which are typically disk-shaped and surrounded by ray florets. The plant has a rosette of basal leaves that are usually hairy and have a rough texture. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

Taraxacum mimuliforme is often found in temperate regions and is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and seeds.
