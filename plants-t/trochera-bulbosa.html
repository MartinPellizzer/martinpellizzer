Trochera bulbosa

Trochera bulbosa, commonly known as the bulbous orchid, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, bulb-like structure that stores water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in arid environments. The plant produces striking, showy flowers that are often brightly colored, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, adapting to its habitat.

Botanically, it belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role.
