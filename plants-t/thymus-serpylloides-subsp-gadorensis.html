Thymus serpylloides subsp. gadorensis

Thymus serpylloides subsp.

gadorensis, commonly known as Gadorensis thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and fragrant, small, grey-green leaves.

The plant produces pink to purple flowers in early summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

This subspecies is valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
