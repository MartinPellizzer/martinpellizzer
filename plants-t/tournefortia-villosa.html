Tournefortia villosa

Tournefortia villosa, commonly known as the hairy tournefortia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Indian subcontinent.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2 to 5 meters. The plant features simple, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it its common name. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, arranged in clusters, and are followed by small, capsule-like fruits.

Tournefortia villosa is adapted to dry and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species in such conditions.
