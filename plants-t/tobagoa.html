Tobagoa

Tobagoa, commonly known as the "Blue-eyed Grass," is a member of the Iridaceae family, native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers, which resemble those of the iris, and its grass-like foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant color and unique appearance.

Tobagoa is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
