Tamus communis var. cretica

Tamus communis var. cretica, commonly known as the Cretan bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a rosette of broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its distinctive foliage and floral display.
