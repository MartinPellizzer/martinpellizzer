Taraxacum luteolum

Taraxacum luteolum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its basal rosette of dark green, hairy leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, forming a composite head of many tiny florets. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grassy areas.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
