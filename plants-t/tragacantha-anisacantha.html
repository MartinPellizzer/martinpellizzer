Tragacantha anisacantha

Tragacantha anisacantha, commonly known as the goat's beard, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, feathery, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It is often found in dry, rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
