Tetracera subcordata

Tetracera subcordata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tetracera, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in cyathia, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is typically found in lowland rainforests and thrives in humid, shaded environments.
