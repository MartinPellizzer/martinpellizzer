Tirucalia indurescens

Tirucalia indurescens, commonly known as the Indian cissus, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often pubescent. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by red, fleshy fruits that are edible. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
