Thalictrum venulosum

Thalictrum venulosum, commonly known as the veined meadow-rue, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and compound leaves with a distinctive veining pattern. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in a raceme, which bloom in early summer. Thalictrum venulosum thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its ornamental value lies in its delicate foliage and subtle floral display.
