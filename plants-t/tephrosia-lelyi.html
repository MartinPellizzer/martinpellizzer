Tephrosia lelyi

Tephrosia lelyi, commonly known as the African senna, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is notable for its bright yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Tephrosia lelyi is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of forage for livestock.
