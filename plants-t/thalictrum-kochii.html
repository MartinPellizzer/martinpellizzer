Thalictrum kochii

Thalictrum kochii, commonly known as the Koch's meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces a showy display of flowers in early spring, which are pollinated by insects.

Thalictrum kochii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
