Triticum junceum var. macrostachyum

Triticum junceum var. macrostachyum, commonly known as the giant wild rye, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and large, feathery inflorescences that can reach several feet in height. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, along stream banks, and in open woodlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers in the summer months.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in ecological restoration projects.
