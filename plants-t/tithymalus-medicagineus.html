Tithymalus medicagineus

Tithymalus medicagineus, commonly known as the medicinal thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its aromatic, small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple, often arranged in dense clusters. The plant typically grows in sunny, well-drained soils and is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it produces small, four-lobed fruits known as nutlets.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly for its essential oils rich in thymol and carvacrol.
