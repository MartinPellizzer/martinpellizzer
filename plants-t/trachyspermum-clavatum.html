Trachyspermum clavatum

Trachyspermum clavatum, commonly known as Indian cumin, is an annual herb native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces oval, aromatic seeds that are widely used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in warm, dry climates and is often cultivated for its essential oils.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both traditional medicine and modern agriculture.
