Teucrium taylorii subsp. muticum

Teucrium taylorii subsp.

muticum, commonly known as the desert germander, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and desert scrub habitats.

The plant has aromatic, narrow leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.

Its botanical features make it an important component of desert ecosystems and a subject of interest in ecological and botanical studies.
