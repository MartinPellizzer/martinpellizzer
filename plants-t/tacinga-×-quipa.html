Tacinga × quipa

Tacinga × quipa, commonly known as the hybrid tacinga, is a botanical hybrid resulting from the cross between Tacinga pumila and Tacinga tenuifolia.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming plant with narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice in arid landscape designs.
