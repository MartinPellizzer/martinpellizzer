Tithymalus malvanus

Tithymalus malvanus, commonly known as the Malvan thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are aromatic, often used in culinary applications, and it thrives in well-drained, sunny soils.

Tithymalus malvanus is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
