Tulipa kaufmanniana subsp. tschimganica

Tulipa kaufmanniana subsp.

tschimganica, commonly known as the Tschimgan tulip, is a botanical variant native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its robust, upright stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, and white.

The subspecies is known for its strong fragrance and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, thriving in temperate climates.

This tulip is also valued for its resilience and ability to naturalize over time.
