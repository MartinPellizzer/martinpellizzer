Trichocline auriculata

Trichocline auriculata, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that resemble small bells, which appear in clusters along the stems. The plant has slender, woody vines with opposite leaves that are oval and slightly hairy. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Trichocline auriculata is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
