Taraxacum forellense

Taraxacum forellense, commonly known as the Alpine dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces a yellow flower that develops into a white, fluffy seed head, typical of the dandelion genus. It thrives in alpine and subalpine habitats, often growing in rocky or gravelly soils.

Taraxacum forellense is notable for its resilience to cold climates and its role in supporting local pollinators.
