Telopea

Telopea, commonly known as the waratah, is a genus of flowering plants native to Australia.

These plants are characterized by their striking, showy inflorescences that resemble a crown of red or pink flowers. Telopea species are typically shrubs or small trees, with woody stems and broad, leathery leaves. They thrive in well-drained soils and are often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

The genus includes several species, with Telopea speciosissima being the most well-known for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
