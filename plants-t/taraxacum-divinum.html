Taraxacum divinum

Taraxacum divinum, commonly known as the divine dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of stiff, hairy leaves and a hollow, leafless stem that supports a single flower head. The flower head consists of numerous yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular florets. This plant is native to Europe and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a resilient and widespread species.
