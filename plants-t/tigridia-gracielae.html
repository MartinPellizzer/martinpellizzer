Tigridia gracielae

Tigridia gracielae, commonly known as the Mexican fairy flower, is a striking member of the Iridaceae family native to Mexico.

This perennial plant is characterized by its vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that range from red to orange, often with a contrasting black or purple throat. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy choice for arid gardens. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and has a short, stocky stem.

Tigridia gracielae is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its colorful blooms and resilience in challenging environments.
