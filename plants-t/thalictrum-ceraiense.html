Thalictrum ceraiense

Thalictrum ceraiense, commonly known as the Ceraiense meadow rue, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its flowers are usually borne on slender, erect stems and are pollinated by insects.

Thalictrum ceraiense is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
