Taraxacum rawalpindicum

Taraxacum rawalpindicum, commonly known as the Rawalpindi dandelion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of spoon-shaped leaves and a hollow, hairy stem that supports a single, yellow flower head. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow composite flowers that develop into fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds. It is native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas, including parts of Pakistan and India.

Taraxacum rawalpindicum is often found in rocky and alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions.
