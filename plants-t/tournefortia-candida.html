Tournefortia candida

Tournefortia candida, commonly known as white-flowered tournefortia, is a flowering plant species native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and showy white or pale yellow flowers. The flowers are borne in clusters and have five petals, with a distinctive tubular shape at the base. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas or disturbed habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value.
