Tithymalus pannonicus

Tithymalus pannonicus, commonly known as the Hungarian tithymalus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It typically grows in moist meadows and along riverbanks, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features slender stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Tithymalus pannonicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
