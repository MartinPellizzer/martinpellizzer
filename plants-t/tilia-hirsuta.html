Tilia hirsuta

Tilia hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy linden, is a deciduous tree native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy bark and heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The flowers are small, fragrant, and clustered in drooping inflorescences, typically appearing in early summer. The tree produces small, round fruits that contain three seeds each.

It is often found in moist, fertile soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
