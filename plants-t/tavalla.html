Tavalla

Tavalla, commonly known as the common mallow, is a flowering plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, velvety leaves and showy, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is found in temperate regions across Europe and parts of Asia. Tavalla has been used in traditional medicine for its soothing properties, particularly for respiratory and skin conditions.

Its fibrous stems and hairy leaves are also notable features in its botanical description.
