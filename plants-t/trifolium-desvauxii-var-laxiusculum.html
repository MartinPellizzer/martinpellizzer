Trifolium desvauxii var. laxiusculum

Trifolium desvauxii var. laxiusculum, commonly known as the slender clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves composed of three oval or rounded leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers arranged in clusters, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open habitats.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it beneficial for soil fertility in agricultural settings.
