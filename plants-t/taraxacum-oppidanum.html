Taraxacum oppidanum

Taraxacum oppidanum, commonly known as the purple star dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive purple-tinged flower heads and is native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant produces a milky sap that contains latex, which is a common feature in the genus Taraxacum. Its leaves are typically basal, forming a rosette and are covered with fine hairs.

This species is often found in meadows and grasslands, thriving in well-drained soils.
