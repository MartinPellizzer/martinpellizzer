Tulipa crocata

Tulipa crocata, commonly known as the Persian tulip, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Tulipa.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Turkey, Iran, and Afghanistan. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from deep red to orange and yellow. It is a perennial herb with strap-like leaves and is known for its early spring blooming period.

The botanical features of Tulipa crocata make it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.
