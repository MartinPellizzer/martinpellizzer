Tetraneuris lanata

Tetraneuris lanata, commonly known as the hairy tetraneuris, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its foliage is covered in fine, white hairs, which help reduce water loss and reflect sunlight.

Tetraneuris lanata is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats, contributing to the ecological diversity of these regions.
