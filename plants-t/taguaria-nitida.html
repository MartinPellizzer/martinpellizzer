Taguaria nitida

Taguaria nitida, commonly known as the white-flowered taguaria, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Polygonaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly found in regions of Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Taguaria nitida is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses due to its delicate and elegant foliage.
