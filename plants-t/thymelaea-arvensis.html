Thymelaea arvensis

Thymelaea arvensis, commonly known as field thyme or wild thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Thymelaeaceae and is characterized by its small, clustered yellow flowers and aromatic, needle-like leaves. The plant typically thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its compact growth habit and dense foliage make it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Thymelaea arvensis is often used in herbal medicine and as a ornamental plant in gardens.
