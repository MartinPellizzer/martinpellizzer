Thuja occidentalis f. pendula-glauca

Thuja occidentalis f. pendula-glauca, commonly known as the glaucous spruce or blue spruce, is a cultivar of the eastern white cedar.

It is characterized by its slow growth and distinctive blue-gray foliage, which gives it a unique aesthetic appeal. The plant has a weeping growth habit, with branches cascading downward in a graceful manner. Its foliage is dense and provides good wind resistance, making it suitable for ornamental landscaping.

The cultivar is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
