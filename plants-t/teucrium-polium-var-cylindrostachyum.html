Teucrium polium var. cylindrostachyum

Teucrium polium var. cylindrostachyum, commonly known as the cylindrical stamen germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of pink to purple flowers, which bloom in summer and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental value make it a popular choice in horticulture.
