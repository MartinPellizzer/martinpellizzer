Terpsichore × victorhugoensis

Terpsichore × victorhugoensis, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a cross between two species of orchids, Terpsichore and Victorhugoensis.

It is characterized by its striking, often fragrant flowers that display a range of colors, including shades of pink, white, and purple. The plant typically has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually broad and lance-shaped, and it thrives in well-drained, moist soil.

This hybrid is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
