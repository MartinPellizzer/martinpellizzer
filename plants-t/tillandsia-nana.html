Tillandsia nana

Tillandsia nana, commonly known as the dwarf air plant, is a species of bromeliad native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that attaches itself to trees or other surfaces without deriving nutrients from them. The plant's rosette-shaped leaves are covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help it absorb moisture and nutrients from the air. It produces small, colorful flowers that bloom in shades of red, yellow, and orange.

Due to its compact size and unique appearance, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening and terrariums.
