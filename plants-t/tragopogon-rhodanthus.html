Tragopogon rhodanthus

Tragopogon rhodanthus, commonly known as the redstem goat's beard, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers with a distinctive red center, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces a long, slender stem with narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and disturbed areas.

Tragopogon rhodanthus is part of the dandelion family (Compositae) and is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize new areas.
