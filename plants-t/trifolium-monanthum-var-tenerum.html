Trifolium monanthum var. tenerum

Trifolium monanthum var. tenerum, commonly known as the soft-leaved clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. It is typically found in moist, grassy areas and is often used as a forage crop.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it beneficial for soil fertility.
