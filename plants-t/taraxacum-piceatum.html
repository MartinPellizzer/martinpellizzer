Taraxacum piceatum

Taraxacum piceatum, commonly known as the purple dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped, dark green leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces a single, hollow, yellowish stem that terminates in a composite flower head consisting of numerous small florets. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and woodland edges.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in herbal remedies.
