Tetrastigma corniculatum

Tetrastigma corniculatum, commonly known as the winged grape vine, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with tendrils that aid in climbing. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by small, round berries that ripen to a dark red or black color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its ornamental value is appreciated in horticulture for its foliage and unique growth habit.
