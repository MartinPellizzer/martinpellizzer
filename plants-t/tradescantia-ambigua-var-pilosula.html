Tradescantia ambigua var. pilosula

Tradescantia ambigua var. pilosula, commonly known as the silver spiderwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, arching stems covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are lance-shaped, with a bluish-gray hue and a soft, velvety texture. It produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and easy maintenance.
