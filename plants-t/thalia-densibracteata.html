Thalia densibracteata

Thalia densibracteata, commonly known as the dense bract thalia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. The plant features dense, leaf-like bracts that surround the flower cluster, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Thalia densibracteata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
