Tragacantha triflora

Tragacantha triflora, commonly known as the three-flowered tragacanth, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow pea-like flowers. The plant produces a distinctive gum, known as tragacanth, which is used in various industrial and medicinal applications. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in scrublands and woodland edges.

Tragacantha triflora is also valued for its ornamental appeal due to its delicate foliage and seasonal blooms.
