Temnadenia glaucescens

Temnadenia glaucescens, commonly known as the blue-green mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of eucalyptus trees in southern Africa.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically a bluish-green color, which helps it blend with its host. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruits are spherical and contain a single seed, which is dispersed by birds.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can significantly affect the health of its host tree if left unchecked.
