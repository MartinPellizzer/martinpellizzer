Taeniophyllum triquetroradix

Taeniophyllum triquetroradix, commonly known as the three-lobed root fern, is a rare and unique fern species native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, trifoliate roots that resemble small, branching tendrils. The plant's fronds are typically slender and arching, with a soft, feathery appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires high levels of moisture and organic soil.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered an endangered species.
