Thymus × parilis

Thymus × parilis, commonly known as parilis thyme, is a hybrid perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic leaves and square stems. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is fragrant, containing essential oils that contribute to its use in culinary and medicinal applications.

Thymus × parilis thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
