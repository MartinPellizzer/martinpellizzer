Tragus racemosus var. biflorus

Tragus racemosus var. biflorus, commonly known as the two-flowered tragus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its erect, spiny stems and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant produces numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers arranged in racemes, which contribute to its common name. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in dry, sandy soils.

This species is often used in ornamental gardening due to its distinctive appearance and resilience.
