Turnera aromatica

Turnera aromatica, commonly known as the yellow datura, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its showy, yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in woodland or disturbed areas. Its leaves are usually ovate or heart-shaped, and it produces berries that contain toxic compounds.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes, though caution is advised due to its potential toxicity.
