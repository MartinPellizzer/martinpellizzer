Tigridia catarinensis

Tigridia catarinensis, commonly known as the Catarina tiger flower, is a member of the Iridaceae family native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, vibrant red or orange tubular flowers that resemble a tiger's face, hence its common name. The plant produces showy, spurred petals and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Tigridia catarinensis is also known for its ability to bloom multiple times during the growing season.
