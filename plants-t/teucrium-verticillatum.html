Teucrium verticillatum

Teucrium verticillatum, commonly known as blue germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs and produces small, clustered blue or purple flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped, serrated, and often have a slightly aromatic scent. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its medicinal properties have been historically valued for their antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects.
