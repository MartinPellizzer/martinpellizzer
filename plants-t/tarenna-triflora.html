Tarenna triflora

Tarenna triflora, commonly known as the three-flowered tarenna, is a member of the Rubiaceae family.

It is a shrub native to Madagascar, characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces white or pale yellow flowers that are often clustered in threes, giving it its common name. Its fruits are small, dry capsules that contain several seeds.

Tarenna triflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
