Tragopogon khorasanicus

Tragopogon khorasanicus, commonly known as the Persian goatweed, is a flowering plant native to regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small yellow flowers that develop into fluffy, seed-filled pappus structures.

Tragopogon khorasanicus is often used in traditional medicine and is also considered a potential invasive species in some areas.
