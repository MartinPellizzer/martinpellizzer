Tradescantia navicularis

Tradescantia navicularis, commonly known as the snake plant or mother-in-law's tongue, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caribbean.

It features long, narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are typically bright green with a waxy texture. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening. Its leaves often have a distinctive variegation pattern, adding visual interest to its appearance.

Tradescantia navicularis is also valued for its air-purifying properties and ability to improve indoor air quality.
