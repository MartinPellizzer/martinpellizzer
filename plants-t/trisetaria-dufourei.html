Trisetaria dufourei

Trisetaria dufourei, commonly known as the African hairgrass, is a member of the Poaceae family, characterized by its fine, grass-like appearance.

It is native to southern Africa and thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands or along riverbanks. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically green or reddish in color. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its common name.

Trisetaria dufourei is often used in ornamental gardening due to its delicate foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
