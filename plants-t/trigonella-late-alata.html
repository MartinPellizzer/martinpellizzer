Trigonella late-alata

Trigonella late-alata, commonly known as the late-winged fenugreek, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and is often found in grassy areas or along roadsides. Its seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a spice, similar to other members of the legume family.

The species is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in semi-arid conditions.
