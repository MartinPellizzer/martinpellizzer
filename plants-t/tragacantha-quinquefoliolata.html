Tragacantha quinquefoliolata

Tragacantha quinquefoliolata, commonly known as the five-leaved tragacanth, is a perennial shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves with five leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by pods that contain several seeds.

Tragacantha quinquefoliolata is known for its ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions, making it an important component of local ecosystems.
