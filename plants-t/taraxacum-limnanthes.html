Taraxacum limnanthes

Taraxacum limnanthes, commonly known as the water buttercup, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and a rosette of basal leaves. The plant thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and pond edges. Its scientific name reflects its association with water, as "limnanthes" relates to lakes and water.

The species is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a pioneer plant in wetland ecosystems.
