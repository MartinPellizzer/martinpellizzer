Tessaria redolens

Tessaria redolens, commonly known as the fragrant tamarind, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and heart-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are often clustered in spikes. Its fruit is a long, pod-like structure containing several seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and cuisine.

Tessaria redolens is valued for its aromatic properties and adaptability to various soil types.
