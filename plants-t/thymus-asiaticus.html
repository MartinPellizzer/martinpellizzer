Thymus asiaticus

Thymus asiaticus, commonly known as Asiatic thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by aromatic essential oils and square stems. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers and has narrow, grey-green leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and as a ground cover.

The essential oils extracted from its leaves are valued for their antimicrobial and antioxidant properties.
