Tribulus occidentalis

Tribulus occidentalis, commonly known as goathead, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is an annual herb that grows up to 1 meter tall, featuring branched stems and small, yellow flowers. The plant produces distinctive, spiny fruits that contain seeds, which are often dispersed by wind or animals. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are typically clustered in inflorescences.

Tribulus occidentalis is adapted to dry environments and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.
