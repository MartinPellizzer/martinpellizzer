Tanacetum balsamita subsp. balsamitoides

Tanacetum balsamita subsp.

balsamitoides, commonly known as sweet goldenweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, finely divided leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often used in herbal medicine for its antispasmodic and carminative properties.

Its essential oils contain compounds like thymol and carvacrol, which contribute to its medicinal value.
