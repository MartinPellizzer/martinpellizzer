Taxodium montezumae

Taxodium montezumae, commonly known as the Mexican Baldcypress, is a deciduous conifer native to southern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking reddish-brown bark and its ability to thrive in wetland environments. The plant produces small, spherical cones and has feathery, needle-like leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It is often found in riparian zones and is adapted to periodic flooding.

The Mexican Baldcypress is valued for its ecological role and ornamental qualities in landscaping.
