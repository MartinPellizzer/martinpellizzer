Taraxacum olivaceum

Taraxacum olivaceum, commonly known as the olive dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant features a rosette of basal, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a single head, with the characteristic puffball seed dispersal mechanism.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.
