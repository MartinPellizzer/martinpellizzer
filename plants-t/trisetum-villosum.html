Trisetum villosum

Trisetum villosum, commonly known as silky wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are often covered with fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Trisetum villosum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
