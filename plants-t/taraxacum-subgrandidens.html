Taraxacum subgrandidens

Taraxacum subgrandidens, commonly known as the large-headed dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces a distinctive, elongated seed head that releases numerous lightweight seeds with parachute-like structures. Native to the eastern United States, it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in gardens and along roadsides.
