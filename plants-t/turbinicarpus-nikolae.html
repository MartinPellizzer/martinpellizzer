Turbinicarpus nikolae

Turbinicarpus nikolae, commonly known as the Nikolai's Turbinicarpus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its rounded, ribbed stems that store water efficiently. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the rainy season, typically in late spring or early summer. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it thrives in well-drained soil with minimal water.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
