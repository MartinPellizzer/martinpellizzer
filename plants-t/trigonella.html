Trigonella

Trigonella, commonly known as fenugreek, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to southern Europe, North Africa, and western Asia, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, lens-shaped seeds that are widely used in culinary and medicinal applications. Trigonella species are known for their ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making them valuable in agricultural systems.

The plant's botanical features include compound leaves and pods that contain the edible seeds.
