Thymus serpyllum subsp. hesperites

Thymus serpyllum subsp.

hesperites, commonly known as western wild thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States. It features small, fragrant leaves that are typically gray-green in color, with a dense, mat-forming growth habit.

The plant produces pink to purple flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its aromatic foliage makes it a valued plant in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
