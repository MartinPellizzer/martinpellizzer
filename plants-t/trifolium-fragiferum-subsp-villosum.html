Trifolium fragiferum subsp. villosum

Trifolium fragiferum subsp.

villosum, commonly known as the hairy clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically hairy and have a distinctive serrated edge.

The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its morphology and growth habits make it a notable species within the genus Trifolium.
