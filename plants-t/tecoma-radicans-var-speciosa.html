Tecoma radicans var. speciosa

Tecoma radicans var. speciosa, commonly known as the yellow bells, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that produces clusters of bright yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its showy blooms attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to gardens.

The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
