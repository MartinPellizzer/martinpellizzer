Telanthera halimifolia

Telanthera halimifolia, commonly known as the sweet shrub, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring woody stems and opposite leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers in the spring, which are followed by small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, streambanks, and other damp habitats.

Telanthera halimifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators.
