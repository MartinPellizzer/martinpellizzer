Tragacantha malaca

Tragacantha malaca, commonly known as Malacca tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its most notable feature is the production of a viscous gum, which has been used historically in various medicinal and industrial applications.

Tragacantha malaca thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.
