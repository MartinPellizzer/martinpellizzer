Trigonotis laxa var. laxa

Trigonotis laxa var. laxa, commonly known as the slender bellflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, ascending stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, and the plant thrives in well-drained soil in full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is relatively easy to grow.

The species is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
