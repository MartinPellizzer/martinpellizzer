Thomasia cycnopotamica

Thomasia cycnopotamica, commonly known as the African bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are usually hairy and have a slightly succulent texture. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue, with five lobes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Thomasia cycnopotamica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
