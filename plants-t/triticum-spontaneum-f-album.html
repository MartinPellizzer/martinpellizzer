Triticum spontaneum f. album

Triticum spontaneum f. album, commonly known as white wild rye, is a grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and produces small, white to pale blue florets. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green coloration.

This species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and ability to stabilize soil.
