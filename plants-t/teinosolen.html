Teinosolen

Teinosolen, commonly known as the "fairy ring mushroom," is a type of fungi that belongs to the genus Marasmius.

It is characterized by its small, delicate cap and pale yellow to white gills. The fruiting bodies typically form in circular patterns, known as fairy rings, in grassy areas. Teinosolen is often found in temperate regions and thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soil.

It plays a role in decomposing organic matter and is sometimes considered a saprophytic fungus.
