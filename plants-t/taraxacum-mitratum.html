Taraxacum mitratum

Taraxacum mitratum, commonly known as the Mitratum dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of stiff, hairy leaves and a hollow, leafless stem that supports a single flower head. The flower head consists of both ray and disk flowers, with the ray flowers being yellow and the disk flowers being tubular. It produces a distinctive seed head composed of numerous white, wind-dispersed seeds.

This plant is native to southern Europe and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.
