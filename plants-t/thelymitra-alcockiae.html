Thelymitra alcockiae

It is characterized by its striking, sun-like yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in heathlands and open forests, preferring well-drained soils. Thelymitra alcockiae has a pseudobulb structure with a single leaf, and its roots are adapted for nutrient uptake in poor soils.
