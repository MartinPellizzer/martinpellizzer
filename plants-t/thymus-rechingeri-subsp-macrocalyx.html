Thymus rechingeri subsp. macrocalyx

Thymus rechingeri subsp.

macrocalyx, commonly known as the large-flowered thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and aromatic, narrow leaves.

The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers in dense clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is often cultivated in horticulture.
