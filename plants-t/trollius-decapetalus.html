Trollius decapetalus

Trollius decapetalus, commonly known as the nine-petaled buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, bright yellow flowers with nine petals, which are typically arranged in a star-like pattern. The plant has a short, stout stem and basal leaves that are deeply lobed and hairy. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows.

Trollius decapetalus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, early summer blooms.
