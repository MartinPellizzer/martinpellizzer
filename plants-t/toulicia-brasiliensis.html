Toulicia brasiliensis

Toulicia brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian tamarind tree, is a deciduous tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, while the fruit contains edible seeds surrounded by a fibrous pulp.

The tree is also valued for its hard wood and has been used in traditional medicine.
