Teliostachya mazarunensis

Teliostachya mazarunensis, commonly known as the Mazarunensis wild oat, is a grass species native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically thrives in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and agricultural fields. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a distinctive blue-green hue.

Teliostachya mazarunensis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
