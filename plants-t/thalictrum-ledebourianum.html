Thalictrum ledebourianum

Thalictrum ledebourianum, commonly known as the leddebourn's meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces a showy display of flowers in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Thalictrum ledebourianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
