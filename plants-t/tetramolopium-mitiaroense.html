Tetramolopium mitiaroense

Tetramolopium mitiaroense, commonly known as the Mitiaro dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to salty, sandy soils. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
