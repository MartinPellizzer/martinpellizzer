Typhonium mangkamgense

Typhonium mangkamgense, commonly known as the Karkar swamp taro, is a herbaceous plant native to the highlands of Papua New Guinea.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive spathe and spadix inflorescence. The plant thrives in wet, swampy environments and is often found in shaded, moist habitats. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and have a rough texture, while its underground corms are used in traditional medicine.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations and limited distribution.
