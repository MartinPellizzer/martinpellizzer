Trisetum juergensii

Trisetum juergensii, commonly known as the prairie grass or false brome, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open prairies and disturbed sites.

Trisetum juergensii plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
