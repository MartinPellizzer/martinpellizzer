Torrubia bracei

Torrubia bracei, commonly known as the Spanish heath, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are evergreen, leathery, and arranged in a spiral pattern around the stems.

Torrubia bracei thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathland and rocky areas.
