Trifolium striatum var. kitaibelianum

Trifolium striatum var. kitaibelianum, commonly known as the Kitaibel's red clover, is a variant of the red clover species native to central and eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets with a distinctive white or pale margin. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along roadsides.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
