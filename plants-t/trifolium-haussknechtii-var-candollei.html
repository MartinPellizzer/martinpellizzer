Trifolium haussknechtii var. candollei

Trifolium haussknechtii var. candollei, commonly known as the Haussknecht's clover, is a rare and specialized member of the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, typically with three oval-shaped leaflets, and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. This variant is native to specific regions in Europe and is often found in dry, calcareous soils. Its botanical significance lies in its limited distribution and its role in ecological studies of plant adaptation.

Due to its rarity, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
