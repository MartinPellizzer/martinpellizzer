Thymus serpyllum var. dalmaticus

Thymus serpyllum var. dalmaticus, commonly known as Dalmatian thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It features dense clusters of tiny, aromatic, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Dalmatian thyme is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, as well as its ornamental appeal.
