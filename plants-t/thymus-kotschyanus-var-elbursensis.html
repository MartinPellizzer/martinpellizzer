Thymus kotschyanus var. elbursensis

Thymus kotschyanus var. elbursensis, commonly known as Elburs thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Elburs Mountains in Iran.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and aromatic, gray-green leaves that are densely covered with fine hairs. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers in the spring, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated for horticultural and herbal purposes.
