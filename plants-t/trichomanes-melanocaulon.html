Trichomanes melanocaulon

Trichomanes melanocaulon, commonly known as the black-stemmed maidenhair fern, is a delicate and ancient fern species belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its finely divided, lance-shaped fronds that are often covered in a dense, dark green layer of trichomes, giving it its distinctive appearance. This fern thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are clustered on the undersides of the fronds and are protected by a small, kidney-shaped indusium.

Trichomanes melanocaulon is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
