Tulipa monticola

Tulipa monticola, commonly known as the mountain tulip, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of red, yellow, and orange, typically blooming in late spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It is adapted to cold climates and is often found in alpine meadows.

Tulipa monticola is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
