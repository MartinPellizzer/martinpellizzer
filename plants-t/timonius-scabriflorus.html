Timonius scabriflorus

Timonius scabriflorus, commonly known as the roughflower timonius, is a rare and endemic plant species found in specific regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive rough, hairy leaves and clusters of small, pale yellow to greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowering period occurs during the cooler months, and it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.
