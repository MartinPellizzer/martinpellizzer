Trifolium tricuspidatum

Trifolium tricuspidatum, commonly known as red clover, is a flowering plant in the legume family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three heart-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pink or purple flower heads that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and pastures.

Red clover is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
