Tephrosia trapezicarpa

Tephrosia trapezicarpa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by elongated, cylindrical pods containing several seeds.

Tephrosia trapezicarpa is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
