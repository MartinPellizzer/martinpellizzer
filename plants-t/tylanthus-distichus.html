Tylanthus distichus

Tylanthus distichus, commonly known as the two-ranked tylanthus, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and in wetlands. The plant features opposite, linear leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters. Its botanical significance lies in its unique leaf arrangement and its role in supporting local ecosystems.

Tylanthus distichus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
