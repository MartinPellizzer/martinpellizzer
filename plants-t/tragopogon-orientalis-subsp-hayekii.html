Tragopogon orientalis subsp. hayekii

Tragopogon orientalis subsp.

hayekii, commonly known as the Eastern goat's beard, is a flowering plant native to regions of Central Asia and the Middle East. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of both ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive central purple or brown disc.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
