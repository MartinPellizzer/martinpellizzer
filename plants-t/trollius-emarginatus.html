Trollius emarginatus

Trollius emarginatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf goldcup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its showy yellow ray florets and a central disk of tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and often have a distinctive notched or lobed appearance.

Trollius emarginatus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant summer blooms.
