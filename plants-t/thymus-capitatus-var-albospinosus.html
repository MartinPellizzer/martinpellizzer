Thymus capitatus var. albospinosus

Thymus capitatus var. albospinosus, commonly known as the white-spined thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features small, rounded leaves that are typically gray-green in color and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces dense clusters of tiny, white or pale pink flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

This variety is valued for its aromatic foliage and ornamental appeal in horticulture.
