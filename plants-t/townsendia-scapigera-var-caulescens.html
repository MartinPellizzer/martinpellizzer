Townsendia scapigera var. caulescens

Townsendia scapigera var. caulescens, commonly known as the caulescent Townsend's fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in open, rocky habitats and thrives in full sun. It produces small yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting various pollinators.

Its slender, branching stems and hairy foliage are distinctive features of this variant.
