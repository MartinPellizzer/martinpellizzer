Tragopyrum pungens

Tragopyrum pungens, commonly known as the prickly hemsleya, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and sharp, bristle-like awns on its seeds. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in dry, rocky environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.
