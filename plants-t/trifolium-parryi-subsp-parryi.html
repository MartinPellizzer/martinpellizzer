Trifolium parryi subsp. parryi

Trifolium parryi subsp.

parryi, commonly known as Parry's clover, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a rosette.

The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in spring, typically from May to June. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
