Trichantha purpureovittata

Trichantha purpureovittata, commonly known as the purple-striped trichantha, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescence.

It is native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in countries such as Peru and Bolivia. The plant features slender, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with distinctive purple veins, contributing to its ornamental value. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, dry fruits known as schizocarps.

Trichantha purpureovittata is often cultivated for its visual appeal and is adapted to cool, moist environments.
