Thea pitardii

Thea pitardii, commonly known as the Pitard's tea plant, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a woody shrub characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant is noted for its ability to thrive in shaded, humid environments, often found in tropical rainforests. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine and as a source of caffeine-free tea.

Thea pitardii is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
