Tapeinia miniata

Tapeinia miniata, commonly known as the dwarf rust fungus, is a microscopic plant pathogen that infects various grasses and sedges.

It belongs to the fungal kingdom and is characterized by its unique rust-like spore structures. The plant is not a true plant but a parasitic fungus that relies on host plants for survival. It forms distinct pustules on the leaves of its hosts, which contain spores that can spread to new plants.

Due to its specialized life cycle, it plays a role in natural plant population dynamics.
