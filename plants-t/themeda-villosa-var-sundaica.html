Themeda villosa var. sundaica

Themeda villosa var. sundaica, commonly known as the Sunda grass, is a perennial grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and long, narrow leaves covered in fine hairs. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of a cylindrical spikelet that produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variant is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
