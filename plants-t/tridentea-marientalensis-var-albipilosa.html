Tridentea marientalensis var. albipilosa

Tridentea marientalensis var. albipilosa, commonly known as the white-haired tridentea, is a unique flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant's leaves are typically narrow and may have a slight pubescence, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded environments.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized species within its habitat.
