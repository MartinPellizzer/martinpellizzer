Tephrosia drepanocarpa

Tephrosia drepanocarpa, commonly known as the sicklepod tephrosia, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with elongated, sickle-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by narrow, pod-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and savanna habitats.

Tephrosia drepanocarpa is also noted for its potential use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural insecticides.
