Tradescantia discolor var. variegata

Tradescantia discolor var. variegata, commonly known as the wandering jew or variegated spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking variegated foliage, featuring shades of green, white, and pink, which adds visual interest to gardens. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of purple and pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.

Its ability to spread rapidly through runners makes it an effective ground cover or filler plant in landscaping.
