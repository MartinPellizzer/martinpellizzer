Tolpis nobilis var. petiolaris

Tolpis nobilis var. petiolaris, commonly known as the petioled lily, is a striking ornamental plant native to parts of Asia.

It features tall, slender stems that bear large, showy flowers in shades of white and pink. The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy texture, contributing to its aesthetic appeal. This variety is prized for its elegant appearance and is often cultivated in gardens and floral arrangements.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.
