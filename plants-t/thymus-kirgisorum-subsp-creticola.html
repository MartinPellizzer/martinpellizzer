Thymus kirgisorum subsp. creticola

Thymus kirgisorum subsp.

creticola, commonly known as Cretan thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Crete. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and aromatic, small, gray-green leaves.

The plant produces pink to purple flowers in early summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its essential oils contain thymol and carvacrol, contributing to its medicinal and culinary uses.
