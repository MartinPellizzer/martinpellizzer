Tanacetum uniflorum

Tanacetum uniflorum, commonly known as tansy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, button-like flowers and aromatic, fern-like leaves. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers. Tansy is known for its strong, disagreeable odor and is often found in moist, disturbed soils.

It has been used historically in herbal medicine and as a pest repellent.
