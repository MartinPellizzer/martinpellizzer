Trichinium lanatum

Trichinium lanatum, commonly known as the hairy trichinium, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its scientific name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Trichinium lanatum is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and potential ecological role in its native habitat.
