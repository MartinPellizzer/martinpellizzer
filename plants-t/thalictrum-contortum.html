Thalictrum contortum

Thalictrum contortum, commonly known as meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. It has a fibrous root system and produces seeds in follicles that disperse via wind.

Thalictrum contortum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
