Tragacantha supervisa

Tragacantha supervisa, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Iran and surrounding areas. The plant is characterized by its woody stems and bipinnate leaves, which are adapted to conserve water in dry environments. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by pods containing seeds.

The plant is valued for its gum, which has been used historically in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive.
