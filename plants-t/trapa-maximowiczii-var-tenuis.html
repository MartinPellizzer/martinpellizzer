Trapa maximowiczii var. tenuis

Trapa maximowiczii var. tenuis, commonly known as the slender water chestnut, is a aquatic plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its floating leaves and distinctive spiny fruits that resemble small nuts. The plant typically grows in shallow freshwater habitats such as ponds and lakes. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a waxy surface that helps repel water.

The species is noted for its ecological importance in supporting aquatic life and maintaining water quality.
