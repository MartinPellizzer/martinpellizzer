Triticum repens subsp. aristatum

Triticum repens subsp.

aristatum, commonly known as the bentgrass with awns, is a grass species characterized by its low-growing habit and distinctive awns on its florets. It is native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is often found in meadows, stream banks, and other damp habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and habitat conservation.
