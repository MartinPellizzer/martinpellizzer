Tripsacum compressum

Tripsacum compressum, commonly known as the narrowleaf panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, compressed leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and riverbanks. Its flowers are small and wind-pollinated, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.

Tripsacum compressum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional agriculture.
