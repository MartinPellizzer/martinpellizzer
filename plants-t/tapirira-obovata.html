Tapirira obovata

Tapirira obovata, commonly known as the broad-leafed tapirira, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It features simple, ovate leaves with a smooth surface and a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically visited by various pollinators. Its fruits are small, dry capsules that release seeds when mature.

Tapirira obovata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in shaded garden settings.
