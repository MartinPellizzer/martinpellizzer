Thuja decora

Thuja decora, commonly known as western arborvitae, is a coniferous evergreen native to western North America.

It is characterized by its pyramidal shape and scale-like foliage that is typically a deep green. The plant produces small, yellowish-green female cones and is often used in landscaping for its dense, aromatic foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for hedges and ornamental planting.

Thuja decora is also valued for its resilience to pests and diseases, contributing to its widespread cultivation.
