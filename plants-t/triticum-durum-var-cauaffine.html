Triticum durum var. cauaffine

Triticum durum var. cauaffine, commonly known as durum wheat, is a cultivated variety of wheat characterized by its high gluten content and hard grains.

It is primarily grown for the production of semolina, which is used in making pasta and couscous. This variety is adapted to temperate climates and requires a long growing season with adequate sunlight and moisture. Its botanical features include tall, erect stems and spikelets with tough, straw-colored glumes.

The plant's genetic makeup contributes to its resilience against certain environmental stresses, making it a significant crop in agricultural systems.
