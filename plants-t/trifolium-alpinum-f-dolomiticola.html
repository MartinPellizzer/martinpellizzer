Trifolium alpinum f. dolomiticola

Trifolium alpinum f. dolomiticola, commonly known as the Dolomitic Alp Clover, is a rare botanical variant of the alpine clover species.

It is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves, typically with a pale green to white coloration. This subspecies thrives in calcareous soils, often found in alpine and subalpine regions of Europe. Its flowers are small, pink to purple, and bloom in early summer.

The plant plays a role in stabilizing soil in rocky and marginal habitats.
