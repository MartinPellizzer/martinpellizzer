Teucrium anlungense

Teucrium anlungense, commonly known as the African germander, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features upright, branching stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are typically lance-shaped with a rough texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from blue to purple, blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Teucrium anlungense is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional medicinal uses in some African cultures.
