Tarachia browniana

Tarachia browniana, commonly known as the brownian cholla, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny, segmented stems that form dense, shrub-like clusters. The plant produces yellow flowers in the spring, which are followed by small, red fruits. It is adapted to arid environments and has a deep root system to access water.

Its structure provides habitat for various desert animals and insects.
