Tillandsia pulchella var. pityphylla

Tillandsia pulchella var. pityphylla, commonly known as the velvet tillandsia, is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is native to Central and South America.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on the surface of other plants without deriving nutrients from them. The plant is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage that gives it a soft, moss-like appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Its unique structure allows it to absorb moisture and nutrients through its leaves, making it well adapted to its natural habitat.
