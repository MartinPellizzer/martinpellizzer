Trifolium ornithopodioides subsp. uniflorum

Trifolium ornithopodioides subsp.

uniflorum, commonly known as the single-flowered bird's-foot trefoil, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and a single, small yellow flower per stem.

The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
