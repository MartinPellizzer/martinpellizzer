Talisia clathrata

Talisia clathrata, commonly known as the goldenbush, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is a small, evergreen shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and limestone soils. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy texture.

Talisia clathrata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
