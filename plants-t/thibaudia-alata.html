Thibaudia alata

Thibaudia alata, commonly known as the silverleaf thibaudia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 5 meters. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. Its flowers are small, clustered in panicles, and often have a pale yellow or white color.

Thibaudia alata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
