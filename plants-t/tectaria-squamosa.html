Tectaria squamosa

Tectaria squamosa, commonly known as the scaly tectaria, is a fern belonging to the family Tectariaceae.

It is characterized by its fronds that are typically pinnate and covered with small, scale-like structures. The plant thrives in humid, tropical environments, often found in shaded, moist forests. Its rhizomes are usually short and creeping, supporting the growth of its foliage.

Tectaria squamosa is valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
