Tanacetum zawadzkii

Tanacetum zawadzkii, commonly known as the Polish tansy, is a flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its finely divided, gray-green leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its aromatic properties. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and grasslands.

Its historical use in herbal medicine and as a garden ornamental highlights its botanical and cultural significance.
