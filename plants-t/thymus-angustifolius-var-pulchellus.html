Thymus angustifolius var. pulchellus

Thymus angustifolius var. pulchellus, commonly known as narrow-leaved thyme with a pink flower, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant leaves that are typically narrow and have a slightly fuzzy texture. The plant produces clusters of pink or purple flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic properties make it valuable for both ornamental and culinary uses.
