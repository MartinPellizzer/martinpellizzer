Tetrahit leucanthum

Tetrahit leucanthum, commonly known as the white tetrahit, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, white, four-petaled flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a dense rosette at the base.

Tetrahit leucanthum is a perennial species that is relatively easy to cultivate in gardens with well-drained soil.
