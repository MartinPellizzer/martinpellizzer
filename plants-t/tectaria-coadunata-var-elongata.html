Tectaria coadunata var. elongata

Tectaria coadunata var. elongata, commonly known as the long-stalked tectaria, is a fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Tectariaceae and is characterized by its elongated, pinnate fronds with a distinctive, shield-shaped sorus. The plant typically grows on moist, shaded rocks or soil, thriving in humid environments. Its fronds are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, contributing to its unique appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
