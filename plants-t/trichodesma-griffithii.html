Trichodesma griffithii

Trichodesma griffithii, commonly known as the blue thorn or blue thorn bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of blue or violet flowers, which are typically visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Trichodesma griffithii is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

It is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
