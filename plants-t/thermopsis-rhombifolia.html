Thermopsis rhombifolia

Thermopsis rhombifolia, commonly known as the rhombic goldenroot, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and lanceolate, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces bright yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and herbal practices.
