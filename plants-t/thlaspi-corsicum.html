Thlaspi corsicum

Thlaspi corsicum, commonly known as Corsican rockcress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Corsica, Sardinia, and parts of southern Europe. The plant is characterized by its low-growing habit, with basal leaves and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Thlaspi corsicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
