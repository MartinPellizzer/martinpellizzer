Timanthea tristis

Timanthea tristis, commonly known as the weeping willow of the rainforest, is a member of the Salicaceae family.

It is a small, deciduous tree native to the tropical regions of South America. The plant is characterized by its slender, drooping branches and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.

Due to its ornamental appeal, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
