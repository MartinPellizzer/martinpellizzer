Toechima erythrocarpum subsp. erythrocarpum

Toechima erythrocarpum subsp.

erythrocarpum, commonly known as the red-fruited toechima, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It features small, narrow leaves with a slightly serrated edge, and it produces clusters of yellow flowers in the spring.

The plant is characterized by its distinctive red, fleshy fruits that mature in the autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

The subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
