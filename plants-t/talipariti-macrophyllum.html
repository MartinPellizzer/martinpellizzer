Talipariti macrophyllum

Talipariti macrophyllum, commonly known as the giant talipariti, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length, which are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by orange or red berries containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or lowland areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
