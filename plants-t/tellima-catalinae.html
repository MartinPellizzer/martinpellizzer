Tellima catalinae

Tellima catalinae, commonly known as the Catalina tellima, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific coast of North America.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Tellima catalinae is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
