Taraxacum acutisectum

Taraxacum acutisectum, commonly known as the sharp-toothed dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of pale green, hairy leaves with sharp, serrated edges. The plant produces a hollow, hairless stem that supports a single, inconspicuous flower head composed of yellow ray florets. It is native to Europe and is often found in grassy areas, along roadsides, and in disturbed soils.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its resilience to environmental stress.
