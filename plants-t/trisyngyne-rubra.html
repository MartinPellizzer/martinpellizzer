Trisyngyne rubra

Trisyngyne rubra, commonly known as the red-flowered trisyngyne, is a striking member of the Myrtaceae family native to Australia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting a variety of pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. Its botanical features include opposite leaf arrangement and the presence of aromatic oils in its tissues.

Trisyngyne rubra is often found in well-drained soils in coastal and semi-arid regions, thriving in full sun.
