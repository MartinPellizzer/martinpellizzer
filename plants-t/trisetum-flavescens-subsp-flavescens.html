Trisetum flavescens subsp. flavescens

Trisetum flavescens subsp.

flavescens, commonly known as the yellow false brome, is a perennial grass native to North America. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along streambanks.

The plant has slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with yellow or reddish hues. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle bearing small, spikelet-like flowers.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
