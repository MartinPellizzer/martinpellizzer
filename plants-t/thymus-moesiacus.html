Thymus moesiacus

Thymus moesiacus, commonly known as the Moesia thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in full sun. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink to violet in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are used in traditional medicine and cooking, valued for their aromatic and antiseptic properties.
