Turnera pohliana

Turnera pohliana, commonly known as the Pohliana turnera, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Turneraceae and is characterized by its distinctive yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in shaded or semi-shaded environments. Its leaves are usually opposite and ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
