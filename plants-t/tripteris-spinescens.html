Tripteris spinescens

Tripteris spinescens, commonly known as the spiny tripteris, is a tropical plant native to regions of South America.

It is a member of the Cashew family (Anacardiaceae) and is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a drupe, containing a single seed enclosed in a hard, woody pulp.

Tripteris spinescens is typically found in humid, shaded environments such as rainforests.
