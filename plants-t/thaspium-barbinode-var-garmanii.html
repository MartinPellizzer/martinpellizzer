Thaspium barbinode var. garmanii

Thaspium barbinode var. garmanii, commonly known as the Garman's thaspium, is a species of flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers with yellow disc florets and white to pale pink ray florets. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

This variety is often found in the southeastern United States, particularly in Georgia and Florida.
