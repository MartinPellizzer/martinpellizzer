Tabascina

Tabascina, commonly known as the "Mexican flame flower," is a tropical plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 feet. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Tabascina thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to flourish.
