Tripogon snowdenii

Tripogon snowdenii, commonly known as the Snowden's tripogon, is a rare and delicate grass species native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the harsh, cold conditions of its mountainous habitat. Due to its limited distribution and sensitivity to environmental changes, it is considered a vulnerable species.

Its botanical features make it an important subject for ecological and conservation studies in alpine ecosystems.
