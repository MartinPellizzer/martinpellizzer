Tococa imperialis

Tococa imperialis, commonly known as the imperial tococa, is a rare and endangered plant native to the high-altitude cloud forests of the Andes.

It is characterized by its striking, waxy leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its arid habitat. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming during the cooler months. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to access moisture from underground water sources.

Due to habitat destruction and overharvesting, Tococa imperialis is critically endangered, making conservation efforts essential for its survival.
