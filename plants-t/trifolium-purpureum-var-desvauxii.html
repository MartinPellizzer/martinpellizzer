Trifolium purpureum var. desvauxii

Trifolium purpureum var. desvauxii, commonly known as the Desvaux's purple clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically purple or pink in color, arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in grassy areas, meadows, and along roadsides.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
