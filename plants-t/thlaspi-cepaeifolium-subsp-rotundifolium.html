Thlaspi cepaeifolium subsp. rotundifolium

Thlaspi cepaeifolium subsp.

rotundifolium, commonly known as the round-leaved pennycress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family. It is characterized by its basal rosette of rounded, fleshy leaves and small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
