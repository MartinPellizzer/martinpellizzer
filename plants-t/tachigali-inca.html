Tachigali inca

Tachigali inca, commonly known as the Inca tachigali, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces distinctive pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
