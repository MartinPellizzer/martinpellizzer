Thelypteris indrapurae

Thelypteris indrapurae, commonly known as the Indrapura huperzia, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Thelypteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are arranged in lines on the underside of the fronds.

Thelypteris indrapurae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
