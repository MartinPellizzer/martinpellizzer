Thalictrum simplex var. galioides

Thalictrum simplex var. galioides, commonly known as the meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a fibrous root system and produces showy, spire-like inflorescences in late spring to early summer.

Thalictrum simplex var. galioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
