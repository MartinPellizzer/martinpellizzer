Tithonia humilis

Tithonia humilis, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a herbaceous plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and is often found in open, sunny habitats. Its flowers are rich in nectar and attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Tithonia humilis is also known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
