Tillandsia appuniana

Tillandsia appuniana, commonly known as the Appunian air plant, is a species of bromeliad native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on the surface of other plants without deriving nutrients from them. The plant features rosette-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help absorb moisture and nutrients from the air. It produces small, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple in color.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular specimen among bromeliad enthusiasts.
