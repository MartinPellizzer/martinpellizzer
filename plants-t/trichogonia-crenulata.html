Trichogonia crenulata

Trichogonia crenulata, commonly known as the serrated trichogonia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, serrated leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in the spring. Trichogonia crenulata thrives in well-drained soils and requires a humid, temperate climate to flourish.

Its unique foliage and delicate blooms make it a sought-after specimen in botanical gardens and specialized horticulture collections.
