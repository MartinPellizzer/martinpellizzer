Taraxacum tenuisectum

Taraxacum tenuisectum, commonly known as the slenderleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and a single, yellow flower head that develops into a fluffy white pappus. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant is herbaceous, with a taproot system that allows it to survive in various environmental conditions.

It plays a role in supporting pollinators and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.
