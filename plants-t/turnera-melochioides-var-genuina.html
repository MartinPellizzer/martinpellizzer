Turnera melochioides var. genuina

Turnera melochioides var. genuina, commonly known as the Mexican flame flower, is a perennial shrub native to the tropical regions of Central America and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange-red flowers that bloom throughout the year, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has elliptical, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.

The species is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
