Tricalysia pedunculosa

Tricalysia pedunculosa, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, grey bark and spreading canopy. The plant produces clusters of bright orange-red flowers during the dry season, which are followed by long, narrow pods. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic in shape, with a glossy surface.

Tricalysia pedunculosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
