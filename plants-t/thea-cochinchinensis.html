Thea cochinchinensis

Thea cochinchinensis, commonly known as Camellia sinensis, is a flowering plant in the family Theaceae.

It is native to eastern Asia, particularly China, and is widely cultivated for its leaves and young leaves, which are used to make tea. The plant is an evergreen shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall. Its leaves are elliptical, leathery, and have a smooth surface, while its flowers are white or pale yellow with five petals.

The plant's botanical features make it well-suited for tea production, as the leaves contain high concentrations of caffeine and various bioactive compounds.
