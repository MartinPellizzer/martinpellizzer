Turraea cylindrica

Turraea cylindrica, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and cylindrical fruit capsules. Its dense wood is highly valued for its durability and resistance to decay, making it useful in construction and furniture-making.

Turraea cylindrica thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.
