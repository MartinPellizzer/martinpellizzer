Traxilisa aspera

Traxilisa aspera, commonly known as the rough traxilisa, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and thick, fleshy leaves adapted to conserve water. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its resilience, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant in dry gardens.
