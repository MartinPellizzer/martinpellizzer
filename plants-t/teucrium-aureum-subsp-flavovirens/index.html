Teucrium Aureum Subsp. Flavovirens

Golden germander, scientifically known as Teucrium aureum subsp. flavovirens, is a hardy perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is recognized for its bright yellow flowers and fragrant, aromatic foliage that adds both visual and olfactory appeal to gardens. This species thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a versatile choice for rock gardens and borders. Its medicinal properties have been historically valued for their anti-inflammatory and antiseptic qualities. Overall, golden germander is a resilient and attractive plant that offers both ornamental and practical benefits.
