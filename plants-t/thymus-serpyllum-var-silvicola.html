Thymus serpyllum var. silvicola

Thymus serpyllum var. silvicola, commonly known as woodland thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features small, fragrant leaves that are typically gray-green in color and have a distinctive minty aroma. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands, meadows, and rocky areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
