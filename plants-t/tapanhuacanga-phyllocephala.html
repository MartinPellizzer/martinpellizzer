Tapanhuacanga phyllocephala

Tapanhuacanga phyllocephala, commonly known as the golden leaf tree, is a tropical plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-red berries containing seeds. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.
