Tilia × ruralis

Tilia × ruralis, commonly known as the small-leaf lime, is a hybrid deciduous tree belonging to the Tiliaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are smaller than those of its parent species, Tilia cordata and Tilia europaea. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early summer, followed by small, round, greenish fruits. It is typically found in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Tilia × ruralis is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
