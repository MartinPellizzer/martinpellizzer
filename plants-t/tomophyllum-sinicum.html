Tomophyllum sinicum

Tomophyllum sinicum, commonly known as the Chinese bellflower, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a hairy texture.
