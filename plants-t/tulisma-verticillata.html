Tulisma verticillata

Tulisma verticillata, commonly known as the spiral tulip, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiral arrangement of leaves and its showy, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and prefers well-drained soil in full sun. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is relatively easy to care for.

Tulisma verticillata is also valued for its resilience to cold climates and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
