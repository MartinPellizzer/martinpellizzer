Tanacetum tricholobum

Tanacetum tricholobum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tansy, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and is characterized by its finely divided, gray-green leaves and yellow button-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soil. It is known for its aromatic properties and has been used historically in herbal medicine.

Tanacetum tricholobum is also a source of nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
