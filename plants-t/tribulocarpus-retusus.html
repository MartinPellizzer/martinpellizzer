Tribulocarpus retusus

Tribulocarpus retusus, commonly known as the African burr, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny, woody fruits that resemble small, rough balls. The plant produces small yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by the characteristic burrs. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

The burrs of Tribulocarpus retusus are used in traditional medicine and are also popular as decorative garden plants.
