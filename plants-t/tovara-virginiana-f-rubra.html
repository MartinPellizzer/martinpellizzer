Tovara virginiana f. rubra

Tovara virginiana f. rubra, commonly known as the redleaf tovara, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive red foliage, which becomes more pronounced in the fall. The plant features small, white flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by red berries that persist into winter. Its leaves are typically elliptical with a smooth margin and a glossy texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
