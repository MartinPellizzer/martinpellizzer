Tragacantha saganlugensis

Tragacantha saganlugensis, commonly known as the Saganlugan tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant produces a gum resin, which is used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to habitat loss and overharvesting, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
