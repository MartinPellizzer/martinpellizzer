Thrixspermum lampongense

Thrixspermum lampongense, commonly known as the Lampong dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble dandelions. The plant typically grows in moist, tropical environments and has hairy stems and leaves. Its flowers are yellow and produce small seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

It is often found in lowland areas and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
