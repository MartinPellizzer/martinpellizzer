Trigonella maritima

Trigonella maritima, commonly known as sea cowpea, is an annual leguminous plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain small, round seeds. It thrives in sandy, saline soils and is often found along shorelines and coastal dunes.

Trigonella maritima is valued for its adaptability to harsh environments and its potential use in ecological restoration projects.
