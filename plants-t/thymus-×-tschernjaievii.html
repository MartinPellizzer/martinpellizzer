Thymus × tschernjaievii

Thymus × tschernjaievii, commonly known as the Tschernjaievii thyme, is a hybrid species within the genus Thymus, characterized by its aromatic foliage and small, tubular flowers.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, where it thrives in rocky and well-drained soils. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–30 cm, with compact, low-growing stems and dense clusters of dark green, fragrant leaves. Its flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer, are usually pale pink to purple and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover.
