Taraxacum dens-leonis subsp. pyrenaicum

Taraxacum dens-leonis subsp.

pyrenaicum, commonly known as the Pyrenean dandelion, is a flowering plant species native to the Pyrenean region. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and taproot system.

The plant produces yellow flowers that develop into fluffy white seeds, typical of the dandelion genus. It thrives in grassy and open habitats, often found in mountainous areas.

Its botanical features include hairy stems and leaves with a serrated edge.
