Tursitis cymbalaria

Tursitis cymbalaria, commonly known as the lesser celandine, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in early spring, typically appearing before the leaves fully emerge. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread rapidly, making it both a resilient and sometimes invasive species.
