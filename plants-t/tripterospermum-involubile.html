Tripterospermum involubile

Tripterospermum involubile, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf vine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves and clusters of small, fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
