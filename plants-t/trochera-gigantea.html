Trochera gigantea

Trochera gigantea, commonly known as the giant trochera, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to two meters in length, making it one of the most visually impressive plants in its habitat. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, attracting a variety of pollinators. Its roots are fibrous and extensive, allowing it to thrive in moist, nutrient-rich soils.

Due to its size and beauty, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservatories for its ornamental value.
