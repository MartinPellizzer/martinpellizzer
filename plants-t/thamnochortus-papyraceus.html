Thamnochortus papyraceus

Thamnochortus papyraceus, commonly known as the paper chaffhead, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, papery bracts that resemble small, folded leaves. The plant produces small yellow flowers that are clustered in dense heads, giving it a unique appearance. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
