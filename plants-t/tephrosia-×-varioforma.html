Tephrosia × varioforma

Tephrosia × varioforma, commonly known as the variable tephrosia, is a hybrid species within the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, typically with three to five leaflets, and produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale blue. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and clearings.

Tephrosia × varioforma is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
