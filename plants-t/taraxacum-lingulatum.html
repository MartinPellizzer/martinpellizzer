Taraxacum lingulatum

Taraxacum lingulatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, spoon-shaped leaves and a bright yellow flower head that develops into a crown of white, fluffy seeds. Native to Europe, it is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

It plays a role in supporting pollinators and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
