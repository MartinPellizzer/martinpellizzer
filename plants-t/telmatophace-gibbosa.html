Telmatophace gibbosa

Telmatophace gibbosa, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a large aquatic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves that can reach up to two meters in diameter, providing shade and habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant has showy, white to pink flowers that bloom in the early morning and close by midday. Its roots anchor it in the mud at the bottom of still or slow-moving water bodies.

Telmatophace gibbosa plays an important role in water filtration and maintaining the ecological balance of its environment.
