Thymus cherlerioides var. boissieri

Thymus cherlerioides var. boissieri, commonly known as the Boissier's thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with fragrant, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its essential oils contain thymol and carvacrol, which contribute to its aromatic properties and medicinal uses.
