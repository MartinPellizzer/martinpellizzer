Tiarella trifolia

Tiarella trifolia, commonly known as the lesser foamflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its dense, low-growing foliage. The plant features trifoliate leaves, with each leaflet being ovate to elliptic in shape. It produces small, white or pale pink flowers in early spring, often before the leaves fully emerge.

Tiarella trifolia thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and stream banks.
