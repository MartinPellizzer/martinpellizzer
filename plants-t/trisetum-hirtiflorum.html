Trisetum hirtiflorum

Trisetum hirtiflorum, commonly known as the hairy-flowered wood sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to parts of Asia and North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered inflorescences. Trisetum hirtiflorum typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands.

Its hairy texture on the floral structures is a distinguishing botanical feature.
