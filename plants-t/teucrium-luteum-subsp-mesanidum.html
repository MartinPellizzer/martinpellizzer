Teucrium luteum subsp. mesanidum

Teucrium luteum subsp.

mesanidum, commonly known as the yellow germander, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It features upright, branched stems covered in fine, hairy pubescence.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming from June to August. Its leaves are lance-shaped, serrated, and often covered with glandular dots.

It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.
