Teucrium polium var. pulverulentum

Teucrium polium var. pulverulentum, commonly known as the dusty germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, often hairy stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces clusters of small, violet-blue flowers during the summer months. It is adapted to thrive in dry, rocky soils and is often found in woodland edges and open slopes.

Its aromatic properties and ornamental value make it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
