Tainia hualienia

Tainia hualienia, commonly known as the Hualenia torchflower, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous small, brightly colored flowers. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot. Its foliage is typically broad and lance-shaped, with a waxy texture that helps retain moisture.

Tainia hualienia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
