Taraxacum strictum

Taraxacum strictum, commonly known as the upright dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its upright, sparsely hairy stems and a basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves. The plant produces a single, elongated flower head that develops into a spherical, wind-dispersed seed head. It is often found in dry, disturbed soils and is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

Taraxacum strictum is a perennial herb that thrives in temperate climates.
