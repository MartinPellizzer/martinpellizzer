Telis cassia

Telis cassia, commonly known as golden shower tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its striking, drooping clusters of yellow flowers. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain seeds covered in a silky, hairy coating. Telis cassia thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing as a small to medium-sized tree.

Its foliage and flowers make it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and public spaces.
