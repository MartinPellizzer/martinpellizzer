Tamarix florida var. rigida

Tamarix florida var. rigida, commonly known as the rigid flowering tamarisk, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers in early summer. The leaves are scale-like and often appear as tufts, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

This species is also known for its rapid growth and ability to fix nitrogen, which can impact local ecosystems.
