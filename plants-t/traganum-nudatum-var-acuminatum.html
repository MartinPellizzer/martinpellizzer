Traganum nudatum var. acuminatum

Traganum nudatum var. acuminatum, commonly known as the spike grass, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical spikelets that are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is well adapted to drought conditions. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are typically green or brown, and it produces lightweight seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal in xeriscaping due to its hardy nature and distinctive appearance.
