Thelypteris opaca

Thelypteris opaca, commonly known as the common holly fern, is a species of fern native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen fronds that remain throughout the year, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its fronds are bipinnate, with a distinctive, leathery texture and a dark green color.

Thelypteris opaca plays an important role in forest ecosystems by providing habitat and contributing to soil stability.
