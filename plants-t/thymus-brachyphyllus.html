Thymus brachyphyllus

Thymus brachyphyllus, commonly known as narrow-leaved thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically less than 1 centimeter in length, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces tiny white or pink flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and fragrant foliage make it a popular choice for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
