Turnera rosulata

Turnera rosulata, commonly known as the Rosulate Turnera, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Turneraceae and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
