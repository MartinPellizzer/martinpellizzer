Trigonostemon pachyphyllus

Trigonostemon pachyphyllus, commonly known as the thick-leaved sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, fleshy leaves that store water, an adaptation to its dry environment. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the rainy season. Its root system is extensive and deep, allowing it to access groundwater.

Trigonostemon pachyphyllus is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
