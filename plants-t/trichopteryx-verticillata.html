Trichopteryx verticillata

Trichopteryx verticillata, commonly known as the spiral spikeplant, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique spiral arrangement of leaves and its clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are narrow and arranged in a whorled pattern, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
