Tanacetum paleaceum

Tanacetum paleaceum, commonly known as the pale yarrow.

This plant is a flowering species belonging to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks. The plant is known for its aromatic foliage and has been used historically in herbal medicine.

Its blooming period usually occurs in late summer to early autumn, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
