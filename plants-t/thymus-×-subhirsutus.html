Thymus × subhirsutus

Thymus × subhirsutus, commonly known as the woolly thyme, is a hybrid plant that exhibits characteristics of both Thymus serpyllum and Thymus praecox.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with dense, gray-green foliage covered in fine, woolly hairs. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic leaves are often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
