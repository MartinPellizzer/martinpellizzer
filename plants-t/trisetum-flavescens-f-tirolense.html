Trisetum flavescens f. tirolense

Trisetum flavescens f. tirolense, commonly known as the Alpine yellow grass, is a botanical variant of the species Trisetum flavescens.

It is characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves and compact, cylindrical inflorescences. This plant is typically found in alpine and subalpine regions, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils. Its growth habit is low and spreading, often forming dense mats that help stabilize soil in harsh environments.

The species is valued for its ecological role in mountain ecosystems and its ornamental appeal in gardens.
