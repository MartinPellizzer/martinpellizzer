Tragacantha laeta

Tragacantha laeta, commonly known as the pale tragacanth, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by pods that contain small, hard seeds. Its roots are known for producing a valuable gum, tragacanth, used in various industrial and medicinal applications.

Tragacantha laeta thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in arid and semi-arid environments.
