Tetragonolobus tetragonolobus

Tetragonolobus tetragonolobus, commonly known as the square bean or tetragonolobus, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its square-shaped, ridged stems and trifoliate leaves with small, heart-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by pod-like fruits containing edible, starchy seeds. It is often cultivated for its nutritional value and used in traditional cuisines.

Its growth habit makes it suitable for use in hedgerows or as a cover crop in agricultural systems.
