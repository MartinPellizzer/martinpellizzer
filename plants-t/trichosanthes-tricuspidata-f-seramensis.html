Trichosanthes tricuspidata f. seramensis

Trichosanthes tricuspidata f. seramensis, commonly known as the Seram bitter melon, is a variant of the bitter melon species native to the Maluku Islands of Indonesia.

It is a vining plant characterized by its distinctive three-lobed leaves and yellow-orange, elongated fruits that mature to a dark green with a warty surface. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and is typically cultivated for its edible fruit, which has a bitter taste.

Botanically, it belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, sharing features with other cucurbits such as pumpkins and cucumbers.
