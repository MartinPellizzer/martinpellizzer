Thymus muscosus

Thymus muscosus, commonly known as woolly thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant leaves covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, pink or lavender flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, making it useful in culinary and medicinal applications.

Thymus muscosus thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.
