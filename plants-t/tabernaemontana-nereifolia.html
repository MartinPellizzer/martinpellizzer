Tabernaemontana nereifolia

Tabernaemontana nereifolia, commonly known as the dark-leaved oleander, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

The plant belongs to the Apocynaceae family, which includes other toxic plants, and its sap contains cardiac glycosides that can be harmful to humans and animals. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
