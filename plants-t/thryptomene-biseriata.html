Thryptomene biseriata

Thryptomene biseriata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thryptomene, is a small shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and small, pinkish-white flowers that appear in clusters. It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal or heathland environments.

Thryptomene biseriata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native garden landscapes.
