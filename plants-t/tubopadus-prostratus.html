Tubopadus prostratus

Tubopadus prostratus, commonly known as the prostrate tuberous pad, is a unique plant characterized by its creeping growth habit and tuberous roots.

It features small, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale green flowers that bloom in the early spring. Its root system is highly specialized, allowing it to store water and nutrients efficiently.

Tubopadus prostratus is typically found in rocky or sandy soils in dry, temperate regions.
