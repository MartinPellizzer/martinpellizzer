Trichocereus rowleyi

Trichocereus rowleyi, commonly known as the "Moon Cactus" or "Pachypodium," is a cacti species native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its thick, columnar stem that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces striking white or pale yellow flowers during the cooler months, typically in the evening. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to arid environments.

Trichocereus rowleyi is popular among cactus enthusiasts for its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
