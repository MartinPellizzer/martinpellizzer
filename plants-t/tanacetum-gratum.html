Tanacetum gratum

Tanacetum gratum, commonly known as sweet everlasting, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features finely divided, aromatic leaves and produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. It is known for its long-lasting flowers, which remain intact even when dried, making it popular in floral arrangements.

Tanacetum gratum is also valued for its medicinal properties, traditionally used to treat respiratory and digestive ailments.
