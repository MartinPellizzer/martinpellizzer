Trifolium acutiflorum

Trifolium acutiflorum, commonly known as the sharpflower clover, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along roadsides.

Trifolium acutiflorum is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil fertility.
