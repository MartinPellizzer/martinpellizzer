Triticum dicoccum var. pseudoerythrurum

Triticum dicoccum var. pseudoerythrurum, commonly known as wild emmer wheat, is a wild grass species native to the Fertile Crescent.

It is characterized by its robust growth habit and large, awned spikelets that contain multiple rows of grains. The plant typically thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along riverbanks. Its botanical features include hollow stems, narrow leaves, and a complex inflorescence structure.

This species is of interest to researchers due to its genetic diversity and potential for crop improvement.
