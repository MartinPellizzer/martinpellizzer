Thalictrum viviparum

Thalictrum viviparum, commonly known as the barrenwort, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia and North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its most notable botanical feature is its ability to produce live offspring through a process called vivipary, where seeds germinate while still attached to the parent plant.

This unique reproductive strategy aids in its propagation and survival in various habitats.
