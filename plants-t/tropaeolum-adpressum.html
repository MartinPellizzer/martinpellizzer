Tropaeolum adpressum

Tropaeolum adpressum, commonly known as the prostrate nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, butterfly-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces small, edible seeds that are often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are also edible and have a peppery flavor similar to watercress.

Tropaeolum adpressum thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental and edible plants.
