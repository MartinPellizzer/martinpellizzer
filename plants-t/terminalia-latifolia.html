Terminalia latifolia

Terminalia latifolia, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree's fruit is a capsule that splits open to release flat, winged seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riverine forests.

The wood is valued for its durability and is used in furniture and construction.
