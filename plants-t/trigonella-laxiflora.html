Trigonella laxiflora

Trigonella laxiflora, commonly known as the slender cowpea, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces lens-shaped seeds that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate climates.

Trigonella laxiflora is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
