Tragacantha guldenstaedtiae

Tragacantha guldenstaedtiae, commonly known as the Persian tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by distinctive pod fruits.

Its sap, known as gum tragacanth, has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural thickening agent in food and pharmaceuticals.
