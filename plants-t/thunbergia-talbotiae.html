Thunbergia talbotiae

Thunbergia talbotiae, commonly known as the African bluebell, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves and produces striking blue to violet trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant is typically found in moist, shaded areas and thrives in warm, humid climates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.

The flowers bloom throughout the rainy season, attracting pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
