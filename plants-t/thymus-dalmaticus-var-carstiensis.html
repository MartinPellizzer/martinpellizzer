Thymus dalmaticus var. carstiensis

Thymus dalmaticus var. carstiensis, commonly known as Dalmatian thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It features dense, gray-green foliage with small, fragrant leaves that emit a strong thyme scent. The plant produces tiny, pinkish-white flowers in clusters during late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Due to its medicinal and aromatic properties, it is often used in herbal remedies and culinary applications.
