Tropaeolum integrifolium

Tropaeolum integrifolium, commonly known as the nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that come in shades of orange, yellow, and red. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with smooth, entire margins, which give it its scientific name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Tropaeolum integrifolium is also known for its edible leaves and seeds, which have a peppery flavor similar to watercress.
