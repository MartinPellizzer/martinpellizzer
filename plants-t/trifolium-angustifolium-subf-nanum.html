Trifolium angustifolium subf. nanum

Trifolium angustifolium subf.

nanum, commonly known as narrow-leaved clover, is a small, herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a rosette pattern.

The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Due to its low-growing habit and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
