Trifolium monanthum f. spatiosum

Trifolium monanthum f. spatiosum, commonly known as the broad-leaved clover, is a variant of the common clover species native to temperate regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves, which are often broader and more rounded compared to the standard form. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or white, arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators and improving soil fertility.
