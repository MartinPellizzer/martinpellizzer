Tragus loliaceus

Tragus loliaceus, commonly known as the common tragus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in fine hairs.

Tragus loliaceus is often found in disturbed soils and is adaptable to various environmental conditions.
