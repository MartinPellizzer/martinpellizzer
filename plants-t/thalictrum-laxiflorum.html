Thalictrum laxiflorum

Thalictrum laxiflorum, commonly known as the slender meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves that are finely divided and often covered in a soft, silvery down. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow to greenish-white flowers in early summer, which are arranged in compound umbels. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Thalictrum laxiflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and subtle floral display.
