Taeniopteris forbesii

Taeniopteris forbesii, commonly known as the Forbes' water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, floating leaves that are typically divided into narrow, lance-shaped segments. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and streams. Its fronds are usually light green and can grow up to 30 centimeters in length.

Taeniopteris forbesii plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
