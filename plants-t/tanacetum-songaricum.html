Tanacetum songaricum

Tanacetum songaricum, commonly known as the Siberian tansy, is a perennial herb native to the cold climates of Central Asia.

It features a robust root system and upright stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are finely divided and have a strong, aromatic scent, often described as similar to camphor.

Tanacetum songaricum is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
