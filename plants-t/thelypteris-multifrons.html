Thelypteris multifrons

Thelypteris multifrons, commonly known as the broad-crested water fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, shield-shaped fronds that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and wetlands. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Thelypteris multifrons is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in maintaining wetland ecosystems.
