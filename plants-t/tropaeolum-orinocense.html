Tropaeolum orinocense

Tropaeolum orinocense, commonly known as the Orinoco nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Tropaeolaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, edible flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental and edible gardening.

Its peppery flavor makes it a unique addition to salads and culinary dishes.
