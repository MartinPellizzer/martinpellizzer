Taraxacum verecundum

Taraxacum verecundum, commonly known as the shy dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family, Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its hairy, basal leaves and a yellow flower head that develops into a white, fluffy seed head. The plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. It has a taproot system and is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils.

Due to its delicate appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and sometimes endangered species in certain regions.
