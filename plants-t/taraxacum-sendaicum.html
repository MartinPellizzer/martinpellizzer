Taraxacum sendaicum

Taraxacum sendaicum, commonly known as the Sendai dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a rosette of basal, spoon-shaped leaves and a hollow, hairless stem that supports a single flower head. Its flowers are typically yellow, with ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular florets.

The plant is often found in grassy areas and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional Japanese medicine.
