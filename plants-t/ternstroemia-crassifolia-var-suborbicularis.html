Ternstroemia crassifolia var. suborbicularis

Ternstroemia crassifolia var. suborbicularis, commonly known as the broad-leafed ternstroemia, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, thick, and orbicular-shaped leaves, which are typically a deep green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the late spring to early summer. Its fruits are fleshy berries that turn red when mature, attracting various bird species.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
