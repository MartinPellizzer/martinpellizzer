Talamancalia westonii

Talamancalia westonii, commonly known as the Westonia orchid, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has a pseudobulb structure, which stores water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in its natural habitat with minimal rainfall. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Its unique floral display and relatively easy care requirements make it a desirable ornamental plant for enthusiasts.
