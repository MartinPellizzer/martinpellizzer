Trifolium peruvianum var. chiclense

Trifolium peruvianum var. chiclense, commonly known as Peruvian clover, is a perennial herb native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets with a hairy surface. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in high-altitude grasslands.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in ecological restoration projects.
