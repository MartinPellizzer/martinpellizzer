Tragacantha psilacmos

Tragacantha psilacmos, commonly known as the pale tragacanth, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by pod-like fruits containing lens-shaped seeds.

It is known for its ability to thrive in dry, rocky soils and is often used in traditional medicine for its mucilage-rich properties.
