Trifolium trichopterum var. gracilimum

Trifolium trichopterum var. gracilimum, commonly known as slender clover, is a variant of the trifoliate clover species native to certain regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The flowers are typically small, pink to purple, and arranged in dense clusters, contributing to its ornamental appeal. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

It is also valued for its role in nitrogen fixation, benefiting soil fertility in agricultural settings.
