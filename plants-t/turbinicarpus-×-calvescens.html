Turbinicarpus × calvescens

Turbinicarpus × calvescens, commonly known as the hybrid moon cactus, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Turbinicarpus macdonaldii and Turbinicarpus truncatus.

It is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of northern Mexico, characterized by its compact, globular shape and fleshy, ribbed stems. The plant produces striking, showy flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red during the spring season. Its leaves are typically short and spineless, and it thrives in full sun with well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative cactus in gardens and collections.
