Tragacantha simplicifolia

Tragacantha simplicifolia, commonly known as the simple-leaved tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies for various ailments.
