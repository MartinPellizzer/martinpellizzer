Tephrosia seemannii

Tephrosia seemannii, commonly known as the African blue pea, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, typical of the legume family. It is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils, showing adaptability to various environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
