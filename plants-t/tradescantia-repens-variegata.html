Tradescantia repens variegata

Tradescantia repens variegata, commonly known as the inch plant or snake plant, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Caribbean.

It features slender, spreading stems that produce small, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple. The leaves are typically variegated with white and green markings, adding ornamental value to gardens or indoor spaces. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a versatile choice for landscaping.

This plant is also valued for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.
