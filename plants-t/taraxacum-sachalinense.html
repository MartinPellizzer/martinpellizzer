Taraxacum sachalinense

Taraxacum sachalinense, commonly known as the Sakhalin dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to eastern Asia, including regions of Russia, Japan, and Korea. The plant features a rosette of basal leaves with a hairy surface and produces a yellow composite flower head that develops into a white pappus-based seed head. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks.

The species is notable for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
