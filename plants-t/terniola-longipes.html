Terniola longipes

Terniola longipes, commonly known as the slender sea hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the Indo-Pacific.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stem and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red, with five petals and a prominent central column. Terniola longipes thrives in saline environments, often growing in sandy or rocky coastal areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species in coastal ecosystems and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
