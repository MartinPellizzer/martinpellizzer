Teucrium pestalozzae

Teucrium pestalozzae, commonly known as the Pestalozza's germander, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Teucrium pestalozzae is often used in herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
