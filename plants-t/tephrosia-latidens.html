Tephrosia latidens

Tephrosia latidens, commonly known as the broadleaf tephrosia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and pinnately compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in racemes, typically blooming during the rainy season. Its root system is fibrous, and it is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Tephrosia latidens is also noted for its potential use in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
