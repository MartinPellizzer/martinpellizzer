Tournefortia macroloba

Tournefortia macroloba, commonly known as the large-leaf tournefortia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are broad and often have a waxy texture, aiding in water retention.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
