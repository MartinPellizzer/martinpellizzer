Trigonochilum fractiflexum

Trigonochilum fractiflexum, commonly known as the flexed-leaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, spirally twisted leaves. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are small and clustered, with a distinctive coloration that varies from green to reddish hues.

Trigonochilum fractiflexum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
