Tagetes tenuiflora

Tagetes tenuiflora, commonly known as Mexican marigold, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow and orange flowers and narrow, linear leaves. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to repel certain pests due to the presence of volatile compounds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Its botanical name reflects its slender flowers, which are a key feature of the species.
