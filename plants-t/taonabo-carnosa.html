Taonabo carnosa

Taonabo carnosa, commonly known as the hairy leaf fern, is a tropical plant characterized by its unique foliage.

It features broad, leathery leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often found in the understory of tropical forests. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread slowly across the forest floor.

Taonabo carnosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
