Tephrocactus parvisetus

Tephrocactus parvisetus, commonly known as the small-spined cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family and is native to Mexico.

It is a small, globular cactus with a dense covering of white, radial spines. The plant has a ribbed stem that is typically greenish-gray in color. It produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are often borne at the base of the spines.

Tephrocactus parvisetus is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
