Tecoma australis var. meonantha

Tecoma australis var. meonantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved yellow bells, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters. The plant produces striking yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, giving it a distinct appearance.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
