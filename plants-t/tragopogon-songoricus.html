Tragopogon songoricus

Tragopogon songoricus, commonly known as the Songor dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces a distinctive yellow flower that develops into a fluffy seed head, typical of the genus Tragopogon. It is often found in meadows and grasslands, thriving in temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
