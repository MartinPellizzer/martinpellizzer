Terminalia chlorocarpa

Terminalia chlorocarpa, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, light-colored bark and elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing flat, winged seeds. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas and is valued for its hard, durable wood.

The tree also has traditional medicinal uses in various African cultures.
