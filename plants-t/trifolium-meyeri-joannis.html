Trifolium meyeri-joannis

Trifolium meyeri-joannis, commonly known as Meyer's clover, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets, and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. This species is native to Central Asia and is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides. It has a shallow root system and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils.

Meyer's clover is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
