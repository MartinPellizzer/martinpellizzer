Teucrium compactum subsp. rixanense

Teucrium compactum subsp.

rixanense, commonly known as the narrow-leaved germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or dry soils, often in sunny, well-drained habitats.

The plant features small, tubular flowers that are usually violet or blue in color, arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
