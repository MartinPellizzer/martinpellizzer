Triodia schaffneri

Triodia schaffneri, commonly known as the spinifex grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and the presence of sharp, spiky leaves. The plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing remarkable drought tolerance. Its structure often forms clumps or tussocks, providing habitat for various small animals and insects.

Triodia schaffneri plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and preventing erosion in its native landscapes.
