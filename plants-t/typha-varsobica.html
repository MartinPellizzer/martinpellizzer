Typha varsobica

Typha varsobica, commonly known as narrowleaf cattail, is a wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and a cylindrical flower spike that matures into a brown, mat-like structure. The plant thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, pond edges, and along stream banks. Its rhizomes form dense colonies, contributing to soil stabilization and habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Typha varsobica plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing food and shelter for wildlife.
