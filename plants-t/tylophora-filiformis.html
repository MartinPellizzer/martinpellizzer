Tylophora filiformis

Tylophora filiformis, commonly known as the slender tylophora, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its thin, vine-like stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white flowers that are often followed by small, spherical fruits. It is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and shrublands.
