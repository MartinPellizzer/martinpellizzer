Taraxacum czuense

Taraxacum czuense, commonly known as the Czuen's dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and a hollow, hairless stem. The plant produces a single, yellow flower head that develops into a white, fluffy seed head. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows and open woodland areas.

Taraxacum czuense is noted for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional Chinese medicine.
