Tragacantha pycnantha

Tragacantha pycnantha, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the arid and semi-arid regions of southwestern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny branches and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces a gum resin, known as tragacanth, which has been historically used in medicine and as a thickening agent. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Tragacantha pycnantha is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a resilient plant in harsh environments.
