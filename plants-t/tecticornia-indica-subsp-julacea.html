Tecticornia indica subsp. julacea

Tecticornia indica subsp.

julacea, commonly known as the coastal saltbush, is a halophytic shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia. It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in saline and drought-prone environments.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in spikes, which are typically pollinated by wind. Its root system is deep and extensive, enabling it to access groundwater in nutrient-poor soils.

This species plays a vital role in stabilizing coastal dunes and preventing soil erosion.
