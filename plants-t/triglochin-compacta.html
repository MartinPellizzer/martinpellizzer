Triglochin compacta

Triglochin compacta, commonly known as the compact spikerush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in Europe, Asia, and North America.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of lakes. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or greenish flowers in spike-like clusters. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and colonize new areas within wetland habitats.

Triglochin compacta plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates.
