Tilia nigra var. macrophylla

Tilia nigra var. macrophylla, commonly known as the big-leaf black maple, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 12 inches in length. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, which are clustered in pendulous inflorescences. Its bark is typically dark and deeply furrowed, providing a distinctive appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in the production of maple syrup.
