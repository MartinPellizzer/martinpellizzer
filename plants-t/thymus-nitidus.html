Thymus nitidus

Thymus nitidus, commonly known as the smooth thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, low-growing habit and small, aromatic leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its distinctive fragrance and medicinal properties.

Thymus nitidus is often used in herbal remedies and as a culinary herb due to its aromatic qualities.
