Tayloriophyton longisetosum

Tayloriophyton longisetosum, commonly known as the long-hair spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems that are densely covered with long, stiff hairs. The leaves are typically reduced to small, scale-like structures, and the plant produces yellow flowers in clusters. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, storing water in its thick stems.

The plant is often used in rock gardens due to its ornamental appearance and drought tolerance.
