Thymus herba-barona f. denticulosus

Thymus herba-barona f. denticulosus, commonly known as the denticulate thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun. Its foliage is often used in culinary and medicinal applications due to its essential oils.

This subspecies is distinguished by its finely toothed leaf margins, which give it its specific epithet.
