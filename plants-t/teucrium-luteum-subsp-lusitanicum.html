Teucrium luteum subsp. lusitanicum

Teucrium luteum subsp.

lusitanicum, commonly known as the Portuguese germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open woodlands.

The plant features slender, branching stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, tubular, and usually yellow or orange, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the branches.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
