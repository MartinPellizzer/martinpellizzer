Tanacetum palustre

Tanacetum palustre, commonly known as marsh tansy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in Europe and Asia.

It features dark green, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive white underside, and produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, slightly bitter scent and is often found in moist soils along stream banks and marshes.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
