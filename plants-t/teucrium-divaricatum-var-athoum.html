Teucrium divaricatum var. athoum

Teucrium divaricatum var. athoum, commonly known as the Athous germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, branched stems that are often hairy, with opposite leaves that are lance-shaped and have a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental flowers make it a valuable addition to both wild and cultivated plantings.
