Taenitis macrorhynchos

Taenitis macrorhynchos, commonly known as the broad-beak fern, is a tropical plant belonging to the family Davalliaceae.

It is characterized by its large, leathery fronds that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant features a stout, creeping rhizome with clusters of spore-bearing leaves called sporophylls. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical rainforests.

Taenitis macrorhynchos is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
