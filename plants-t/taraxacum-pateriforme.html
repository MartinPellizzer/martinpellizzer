Taraxacum pateriforme

Taraxacum pateriforme, commonly known as the star dandelion, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette of spoon-shaped, hairy leaves and a yellow flower head that develops into a distinctive pappus-based seed dispersal mechanism. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its ability to thrive in rocky and arid environments. Its flowers typically bloom in early spring, and the plant is often found in alpine meadows.

Taraxacum pateriforme is also notable for its unique, star-like appearance, which gives it its common name.
