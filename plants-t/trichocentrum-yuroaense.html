Trichocentrum yuroaense

Trichocentrum yuroaense, commonly known as the Yuroaense Orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its epiphytic growth habit, often found growing on trees in humid forest environments. The plant features long, slender pseudobulbs and produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. Its botanical structure includes linear leaves and a complex system of roots adapted for nutrient absorption from the air.

Trichocentrum yuroaense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is a subject of interest in botanical research.
