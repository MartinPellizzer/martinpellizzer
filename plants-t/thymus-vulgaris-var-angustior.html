Thymus vulgaris var. angustior

Thymus vulgaris var. angustior, commonly known as narrowleaf thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves and small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its essential oils, rich in thymol and carvacrol, are widely used in culinary and medicinal applications.

This variety is often cultivated as a ornamental and for its aromatic properties.
