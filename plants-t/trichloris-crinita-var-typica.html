Trichloris crinita var. typica

Trichloris crinita var. typica, commonly known as the curlytail grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and distinctive, wiry, curly inflorescences that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and blade-like, with a rough texture, and it produces small, spikelet-based flowers.

Trichloris crinita var. typica is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in dry conditions.
