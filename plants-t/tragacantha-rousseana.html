Tragacantha rousseana

Tragacantha rousseana, commonly known as the Roussean tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, pea-like flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces a unique resin, known as tragacanth, which has been historically used in food and medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it a hardy addition to Mediterranean gardens.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and ecological research.
