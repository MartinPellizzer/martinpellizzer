Tecoma fulgens

Tecoma fulgens, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters, often in the summer months. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Tecoma fulgens is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
