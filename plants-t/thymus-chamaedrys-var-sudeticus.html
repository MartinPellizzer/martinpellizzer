Thymus chamaedrys var. sudeticus

Thymus chamaedrys var. sudeticus, commonly known as the Sudetic thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Central Europe.

It features small, fragrant leaves that are typically gray-green in color and emit a strong thymol scent. The plant produces pink to purple flowers in early summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
