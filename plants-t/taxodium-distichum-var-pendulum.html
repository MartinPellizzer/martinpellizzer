Taxodium distichum var. pendulum

Taxodium distichum var. pendulum, commonly known as the weeping bald cypress, is a deciduous conifer native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its weeping habit, with long, drooping branches that create a distinctive, cascading appearance. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, and it has scaly, reddish-brown bark that becomes thicker with age. Its leaves are needle-like and turn bronze in the fall before dropping, contributing to its seasonal aesthetic.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks due to its unique form and adaptability to various soil conditions.
