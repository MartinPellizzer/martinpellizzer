Tuckermannia gigantea

Tuckermannia gigantea, commonly known as the giant tuckerman's star, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, star-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees in the understory of rainforests. Its leaves are covered in fine, silvery hairs that give it a distinctive appearance.

Tuckermannia gigantea is also known for its ability to produce small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in the spring.
