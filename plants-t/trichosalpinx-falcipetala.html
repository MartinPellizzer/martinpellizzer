Trichosalpinx falcipetala

Trichosalpinx falcipetala, commonly known as the sickle-petaled orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, falcate (sickle-shaped) petals. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are usually small and fragrant, with a complex structure typical of orchids.

Trichosalpinx falcipetala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
