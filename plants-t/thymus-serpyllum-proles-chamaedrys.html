Thymus serpyllum proles chamaedrys

Thymus serpyllum proles chamaedrys, commonly known as wild thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, fragrant leaves and flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats. Its aromatic foliage contains essential oils, making it valuable in culinary and medicinal uses.

It is also popular in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to form dense mats.
