Trifolium glareosum

Trifolium glareosum, commonly known as the golden clover, is a flowering plant species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets, and its clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in alpine and grassland habitats. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, contributing to its ecological importance in local ecosystems.

Trifolium glareosum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential in ecological restoration projects.
