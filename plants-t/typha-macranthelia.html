Typha macranthelia

Typha macranthelia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cattail, is a wetland plant characterized by its tall, cylindrical flower spikes and broad, flat leaves.

It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of lakes. The plant plays a vital role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms. Its rhizomes form dense underground networks that help in nutrient cycling and water filtration.

Typha macranthelia is also used in traditional medicine and as a source of materials for basket-making in some cultures.
