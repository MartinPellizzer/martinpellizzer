Thelymitra pardalina

Thelymitra pardalina, commonly known as the pink sun orchid, is an epiphytic plant native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking pink and white floral display. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, utilizing its aerial roots to absorb moisture and nutrients from the environment. Its flowers are bilaterally symmetrical, with three petals and three sepals, and it blooms during the cooler months.

Thelymitra pardalina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
