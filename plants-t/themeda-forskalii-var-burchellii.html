Themeda forskalii var. burchellii

Themeda forskalii var. burchellii, commonly known as Burchell's grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green and hairy. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including grasslands and rocky soils, and is adapted to seasonal rainfall patterns.

Due to its resilience and ecological importance, it is often found in restoration projects and natural ecosystems.
