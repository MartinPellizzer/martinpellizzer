Trisetum flavescens var. africanum

Trisetum flavescens var. africanum, commonly known as African false brome, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It features slender, erect stems that are often tinged with a yellowish hue, particularly along the nodes. The plant produces small, pale green to yellowish flowers in dense spikelets, which are typically arranged in panicles. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This species is adapted to grassland and open habitats, often thriving in well-drained soils.
