Tillandsia calothyrsus

Tillandsia calothyrsus, commonly known as the silver pine tillandsia, is a species of air plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which aid in water absorption. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and does not require soil, as it is an epiphyte. Its flowers are typically small and inconspicuous, with a pale green to white coloration.

Tillandsia calothyrsus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique ability to grow without traditional soil substrates.
