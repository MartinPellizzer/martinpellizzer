Trichopetalum stellatum

Trichopetalum stellatum, commonly known as the starleaf flax, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its striking star-shaped leaves and delicate, blue-violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are opposite and have a distinctive, radiating pattern of lobes.

Trichopetalum stellatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
