Tachigali amarumayu

Tachigali amarumayu, commonly known as the Amazonian yellowwood, is a deciduous tree native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The tree can grow up to 25 meters tall, with a straight trunk and a broad, rounded crown. Its bark is thick and fissured, often exuding a yellowish sap.

The plant plays a vital role in its ecosystem, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
