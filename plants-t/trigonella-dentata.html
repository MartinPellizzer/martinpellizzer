Trigonella dentata

Trigonella dentata, commonly known as the serrated trigonella, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Trigonella dentata thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and herbal applications.
