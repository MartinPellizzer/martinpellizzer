Thamnochortus fruticosus var. glaber

Thamnochortus fruticosus var. glaber, commonly known as the naked thamnochortus, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, cushion-like growth habit and fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a thin layer of white wax. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
