Tritonia scillaris

Tritonia scillaris, commonly known as the sea squill, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant features strap-like leaves and produces bulbous structures that store nutrients. Tritonia scillaris is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
