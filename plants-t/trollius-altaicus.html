Trollius altaicus

Trollius altaicus, commonly known as the Siberian globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the cold regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

Trollius altaicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with specific growing conditions.
