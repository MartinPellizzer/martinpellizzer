Tristachya welwitschii

Tristachya welwitschii, commonly known as the Welwitsch grass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Tristachya welwitschii is adapted to seasonal rainfall patterns and can tolerate drought conditions.
