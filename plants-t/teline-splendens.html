Teline splendens

Teline splendens, commonly known as the splendour bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and can reach heights of up to 3 feet.

Teline splendens is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
