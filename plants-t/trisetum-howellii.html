Trisetum howellii

Trisetum howellii, commonly known as Howell's wood sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and rushes, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers.

Trisetum howellii is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
