Thalictrella anemonoides

Thalictrella anemonoides, commonly known as the meadow bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces showy, yellow-orange flowers with five petals. The flowers are typically borne on slender, hairy stems and bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
