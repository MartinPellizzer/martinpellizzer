Teucrium carvense

Teucrium carvense, commonly known as Carven's germander, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, opposite leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces clusters of tiny, blue or violet flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
