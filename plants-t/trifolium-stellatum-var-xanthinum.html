Trifolium stellatum var. xanthinum

Trifolium stellatum var. xanthinum, commonly known as the yellow trefoil, is a perennial legume native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically bright yellow in color, and its small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a valuable component of ecological restoration projects. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed areas.

Its ornamental value and ecological benefits have made it a popular choice in horticulture and environmental landscaping.
