Thalictrum alpinum f. setulosinerve

Thalictrum alpinum f. setulosinerve, commonly known as the Alpine meadow rue, is a botanical variant of the meadow rue genus.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and delicate, fern-like leaves with finely serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or alpine environments.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
