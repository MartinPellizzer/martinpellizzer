Tabernaemontana angustifolia

Tabernaemontana angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved star of Bethlehem, is a species of flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, star-shaped white flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a long history of use in traditional medicine. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for garden cultivation in warm climates.

The flowers emit a sweet scent, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.
