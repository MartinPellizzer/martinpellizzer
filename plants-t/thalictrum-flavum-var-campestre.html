Thalictrum flavum var. campestre

Thalictrum flavum var. campestre, commonly known as the meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and showy, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, inconspicuous fruits that contain seeds.

Thalictrum flavum var. campestre is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
