Triodia pulchella

Triodia pulchella, commonly known as the slender spinifex, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white or pale blue flowers. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with deep root systems that help it survive in drought conditions.

Triodia pulchella plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
