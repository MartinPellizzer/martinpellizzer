Tamarix riojana

Tamarix riojana, commonly known as the Argentine tamarisk, is a shrub or small tree native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and small, scale-like leaves that are typically covered in a fine, whitish layer. The plant produces small, pink or reddish flowers in clusters, which are often borne in the axils of the leaves. Tamarix riojana is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing remarkable drought tolerance.

Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in sandy or saline soils.
