Trichonema recurvifolium

It is characterized by its delicate, grass-like appearance with narrow, recurved leaves that are often covered in a layer of tiny, translucent cells. Its spore-producing structures, called capsules, are typically borne on short, erect stems and release spores when mature.

Trichonema recurvifolium plays a vital role in soil stabilization and nutrient cycling in its habitat.
