Teucrium hircanicum

Teucrium hircanicum, commonly known as the Syrian germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance. Its small, fragrant flowers are typically blue or violet, arranged in spike-like clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Teucrium hircanicum is valued in herbal medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.
