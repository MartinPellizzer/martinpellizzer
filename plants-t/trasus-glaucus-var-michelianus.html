Trasus glaucus var. michelianus

Trasus glaucus var. michelianus, commonly known as the Micheli's bluebell, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a bulbous perennial that produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue and violet. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a powdery blue coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
