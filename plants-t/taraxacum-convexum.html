Taraxacum convexum

Taraxacum convexum, commonly known as the broadleaf dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and a yellow flower head that develops into a white, fluffy seed ball. Native to Europe, it has been introduced to various regions around the world. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in a wide range of soil types and environmental conditions.

Its life cycle includes a period of winter dormancy, with flowering typically occurring in spring and summer.
