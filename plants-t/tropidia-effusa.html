Tropidia effusa

Tropidia effusa, commonly known as the broad-leaved orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pale yellow. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the acidic soil.

Tropidia effusa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
