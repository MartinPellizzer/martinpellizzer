Thelypteris lobata

Thelypteris lobata, commonly known as the broad-leaf water fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are often divided into smaller segments. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and forest floors. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Thelypteris lobata plays an important ecological role in maintaining soil stability and providing habitat for various microorganisms.
