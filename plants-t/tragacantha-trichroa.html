Tragacantha trichroa

Tragacantha trichroa, commonly known as the Persian gum or Astragalus trichroa, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces a resinous sap, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive. Tragacantha trichroa thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along dry riverbeds.

Its ecological significance includes providing habitat for various insects and contributing to soil stabilization in its native regions.
