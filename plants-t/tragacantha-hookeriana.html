Tragacantha hookeriana

Tragacantha hookeriana, commonly known as Hooker's tragacanth, is a perennial shrub native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces a resinous gum, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a thickening agent. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to rocky and mountainous terrain.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various insect species.
