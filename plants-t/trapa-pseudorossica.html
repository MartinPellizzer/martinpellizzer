Trapa pseudorossica

Trapa pseudorossica, commonly known as the Chinese water chestnut, is a aquatic plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, floating leaves that are palmately lobed and often covered in a waxy cuticle. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom above the water surface. Its fruit is a spiny, woody capsule that contains one or two seeds.

Trapa pseudorossica is adapted to shallow freshwater habitats and plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various organisms.
