Taraxacum vinosicoloratum

Taraxacum vinosicoloratum, commonly known as the purple wine-colored dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright purple flower heads and hairy, lobed leaves. Native to specific regions in Europe, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant produces a long, hollow stem with a milky sap that is typical of the genus Taraxacum.

Its flowers are primarily visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological importance.
