Trichilia octandra

Trichilia octandra, commonly known as African shea, is a tropical tree native to Africa.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellowish-white flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits that contain a hard-shelled seed. The seed is the source of shea butter, which is widely used in cosmetics and food products.

It plays an important ecological and economic role in many African countries.
