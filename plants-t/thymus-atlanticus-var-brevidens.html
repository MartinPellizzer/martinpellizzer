Thymus atlanticus var. brevidens

Thymus atlanticus var. brevidens, commonly known as the Atlantic thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the coastal regions of the Iberian Peninsula.

It features dense, compact foliage with small, aromatic leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces pink to purple flowers in the spring, which are clustered in small, compact inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its essential oils contain thymol and carvacrol, giving it a strong, spicy aroma and medicinal properties.
