Taraxacum plicatum

Taraxacum plicatum, commonly known as the wrinkled dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, deeply lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces a yellow flower head that develops into a white, fluffy seed head, typical of the dandelion genus. It is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and is often found in disturbed soils and open areas.

Taraxacum plicatum is a perennial plant that thrives in a variety of soil types and is commonly seen in gardens and natural habitats.
