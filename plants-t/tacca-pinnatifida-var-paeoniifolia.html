Tacca pinnatifida var. paeoniifolia

Tacca pinnatifida var. paeoniifolia, commonly known as the "Chinese velvet tassel" or "peacock plant," is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a velvety texture. The plant produces showy, pendulous flowers that resemble peacock tails, with intricate patterns in shades of purple and white. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil and partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in tropical gardens and indoor settings.
