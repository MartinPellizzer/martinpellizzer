Taraxacum benevolens

Taraxacum benevolens, commonly known as the benevolent dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves and a hollow, leafless stem that supports a single, bright yellow flower head. The plant produces numerous seeds enclosed in a pappus, which allows for wind dispersal. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in open, disturbed areas.

Taraxacum benevolens is notable for its resilience and ability to colonize new habitats quickly.
