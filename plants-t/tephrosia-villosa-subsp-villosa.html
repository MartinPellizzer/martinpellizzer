Tephrosia villosa subsp. villosa

Tephrosia villosa subsp.

villosa, commonly known as the hairy tephrosia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its compound leaves with hairy surfaces and small, yellowish flowers arranged in spikes.

The plant produces pods containing several seeds, and it is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. It has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
