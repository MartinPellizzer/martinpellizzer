Trigonella crassifolia

Trigonella crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved fenugreek, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its small, yellow flowers bloom in early spring, followed by the development of its distinctive, triangular pods.

Trigonella crassifolia is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
