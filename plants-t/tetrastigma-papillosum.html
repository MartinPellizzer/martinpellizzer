Tetrastigma papillosum

Tetrastigma papillosum, commonly known as the hairy passionvine, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its tendrils and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white in color. Its fruit is a cluster of small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

Tetrastigma papillosum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
