Thelymitra caesia

Thelymitra caesia, commonly known as the blue-tongued orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It features a compact, bulbous rhizome and produces a single, slender flower spike with one to three pale blue or violet flowers. The petals and sepals are narrow and slightly recurved, while the labellum is distinctive with a deep blue or purple hue. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in understorey vegetation.

The plant is known for its striking coloration and is a popular species among orchid enthusiasts.
