Tiaridium indicum var. mexicanum

Tiaridium indicum var. mexicanum, commonly known as the Mexican false dandelion, is a herbaceous plant native to Central America and northern South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in composite inflorescences, typical of the dandelion-like appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open fields.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
