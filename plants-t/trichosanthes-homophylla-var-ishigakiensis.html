Trichosanthes homophylla var. ishigakiensis

Trichosanthes homophylla var. ishigakiensis, commonly known as Ishigaki pumpkin, is a variant of the Trichosanthes genus native to Japan.

It is a herbaceous vine characterized by its large, lobed leaves and yellow-orange flowers. The fruit is typically oblong, with a rough, warty surface and a sweet, edible flesh. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its culinary and medicinal uses.

This variety is particularly associated with the Ryukyu Islands, where it holds cultural significance.
