Taraxacum remotijugum

Taraxacum remotijugum, commonly known as the remote jugum dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of pale green, hairy leaves and a flowering stem that bears a single, yellow composite flower head. The plant is native to central and eastern Europe, often found in meadows and grassy areas. It produces a distinctive, fluffy seed head that aids in wind dispersal.

The species is relatively rare and has specific habitat requirements, making it of interest to botanists and conservationists.
