Teucrium scordioides

Teucrium scordioides, commonly known as wild thyme or creeping thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, and it has opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and is also valued for its aromatic properties.

Teucrium scordioides is known for its ability to form dense mats, making it an effective ground cover in dry environments.
