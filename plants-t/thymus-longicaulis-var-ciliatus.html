Thymus longicaulis var. ciliatus

Thymus longicaulis var. ciliatus, commonly known as the hairy thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant leaves that are covered in fine, hairy glands, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic properties make it a popular choice for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
