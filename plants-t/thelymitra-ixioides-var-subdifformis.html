Thelymitra ixioides var. subdifformis

Thelymitra ixioides var. subdifformis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sun orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors and rock crevices. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellow-green to brownish flowers with a distinctive, spurred lip. Its flowers are often arranged in a loose cluster and bloom during the cooler months of spring and autumn.

The species is characterized by its unique morphology and is of interest to botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
