Tetracera trigyna

Tetracera trigyna, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
