Triplaris corylifolia var. intermedia

Triplaris corylifolia var. intermedia, commonly known as the Brazilian walnut or Brazilian chestnut, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically alternate and pinnate, with elongated leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody, spherical fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental and nutritional value, it is cultivated in various regions for its timber and edible nuts.
