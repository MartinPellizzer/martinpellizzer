Tribulus microcephalus

Tribulus microcephalus, commonly known as the small-headed bindweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Tribulus microcephalus is known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
