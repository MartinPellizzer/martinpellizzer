Taxus canadensis var. minor

Taxus canadensis var. minor, commonly known as the western yew, is a small evergreen shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Taxaceae and is characterized by its dense, needle-like foliage and red, fleshy arils surrounding its seeds. The plant produces toxic compounds, including taxines, which can be harmful to humans and animals. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Its slow growth rate and ornamental value make it a notable species in horticulture.
