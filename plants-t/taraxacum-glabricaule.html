Taraxacum glabricaule

Taraxacum glabricaule, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves and a hollow, hairless stem. The plant produces a single, elongated flower head that develops into a sphere of seeds with parachute-like structures. It is typically found in disturbed soils and open habitats across temperate regions.

Taraxacum glabricaule is a perennial herb that is often considered a weed but has ecological and medicinal significance.
