Taxanthema flexuosum

Taxanthema flexuosum, commonly known as the flexible taxanthem, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with narrow, succulent leaves that help conserve water. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperature fluctuations.

Due to its resilience, it is often used in ecological restoration projects in semi-arid environments.
