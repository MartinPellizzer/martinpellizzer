Tamarix nilotica var. abyssinica

Tamarix nilotica var. abyssinica, commonly known as the Abyssinian tamarisk, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its salt-tolerant nature and ability to thrive in saline soils, making it well-suited for coastal and inland desert environments. The plant produces small, pink or reddish flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its woody stems are often covered with small, scale-like leaves, and it has a deep root system that helps it survive in drought conditions.

This species is also valued for its use in soil stabilization and as a source of timber and fodder.
