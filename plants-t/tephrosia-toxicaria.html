Tephrosia toxicaria

Tephrosia toxicaria, commonly known as the poison tephrosia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and the Americas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to livestock and humans if ingested. Its growth habit includes both woody and herbaceous forms, depending on environmental conditions.

Tephrosia toxicaria is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats.
