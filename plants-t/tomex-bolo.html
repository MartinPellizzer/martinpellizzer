Tomex bolo

Tomex bolo, commonly known as the false dandelion, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Americas.

It features a rosette of basal leaves that are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, similar to true dandelions, and it thrives in a variety of soil types.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with composite flower heads.
