Tephrocactus flexuosus

Tephrocactus flexuosus, commonly known as the flexible cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, segmented stems that are often ribbed and covered in spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are typically borne in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
