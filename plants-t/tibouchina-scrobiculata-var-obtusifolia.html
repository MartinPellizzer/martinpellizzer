Tibouchina scrobiculata var. obtusifolia

Tibouchina scrobiculata var. obtusifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf tibouchina, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves with a glossy surface and a slightly waxy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes in tropical and subtropical regions.
