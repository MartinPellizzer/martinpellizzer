Telipogon machupicchuensis

Telipogon machupicchuensis, commonly known as the Machu Picchu orchid, is a rare and endemic species found in the high-altitude regions of Peru.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers with a unique coloration. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and humid microhabitats, often in cloud forests. Its botanical features include slender stems and lance-shaped leaves.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
