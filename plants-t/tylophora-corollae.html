Tylophora corollae

Tylophora corollae, commonly known as the white-flowered tylophora, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its opposite, lanceolate leaves and showy white flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky, viscid substance. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Tylophora corollae is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
