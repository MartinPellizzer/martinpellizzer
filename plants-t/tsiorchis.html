Tsiorchis

Tsiorchis, commonly known as the Persian buttercup, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia, including parts of Turkey and the Mediterranean. The plant features delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and a hairy stem. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, meaning they are divided into three parts.

Tsiorchis is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
