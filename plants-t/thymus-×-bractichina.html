Thymus × bractichina

Thymus × bractichina, commonly known as the bract-leaved thyme, is a hybrid plant that exhibits characteristics of both Thymus species.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with aromatic, small, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pink or violet flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its fragrance and compact growth habit.
