Thrixspermum pulverulentum

Thrixspermum pulverulentum, commonly known as the dustflower, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, and its leaves are covered in a fine, powdery coating that gives it its common name. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to its harsh environment. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable and is often found in protected areas.
