Trichocereus chiloensis var. nigripilis

Trichocereus chiloensis var. nigripilis, commonly known as the Chilean night-blooming cactus, is a species native to Chile and Argentina.

It is a columnar cactus with a dense covering of spines, which are typically dark in color, giving it its varietal name "nigripilis.". The plant produces large, showy flowers that open at night, often in shades of white or pale pink. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its striking appearance and nocturnal blooming habit, it is a popular choice for both ornamental and horticultural purposes.
