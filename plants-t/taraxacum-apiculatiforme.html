Taraxacum apiculatiforme

Taraxacum apiculatiforme, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and a hollow, leafless stem that supports a single flower head. The flower head consists of ray florets that transition from yellow to white as the plant matures. It produces a distinctive seed head with pappus-equipped seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

This species is often found in grassy areas, disturbed soils, and along roadsides, thriving in temperate climates.
