Thymus sibthorpii var. korthiaticus

Thymus sibthorpii var. korthiaticus, commonly known as the Korthiatic thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It features dense clusters of small, fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves that are aromatic and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking drought-tolerant plants.
