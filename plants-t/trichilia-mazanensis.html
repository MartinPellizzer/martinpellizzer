Trichilia mazanensis

Trichilia mazanensis, commonly known as the African sandalwood, is a small to medium-sized tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae, which includes many economically important timber species. The tree is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and opposite, simple leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and typically yellow or greenish-yellow, while the fruit is a capsule containing numerous seeds.

Trichilia mazanensis is valued for its aromatic wood, which is used in traditional medicine and for making incense.
