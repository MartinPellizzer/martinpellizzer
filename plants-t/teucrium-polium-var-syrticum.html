Teucrium polium var. syrticum

Teucrium polium var. syrticum, commonly known as desert germander, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, narrow leaves that are typically gray-green in color, with a fuzzy texture. The plant produces small, clustered blue or purple flowers during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rocky or arid environments.

Its medicinal properties have been historically recognized in traditional herbal remedies.
