Trillium lanceolatum

Trillium lanceolatum, commonly known as the lanceleaf trillium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Trilliaceae family and is characterized by its three large, lance-shaped leaves that emerge from a single point. The plant produces a single, showy white flower with three petals and three sepals, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in shaded, moist woodlands and is often found in rich, loamy soils.

Trillium lanceolatum is a slow-growing plant that plays an important role in forest ecosystems.
