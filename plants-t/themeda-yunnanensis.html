Themeda yunnanensis

Themeda yunnanensis, commonly known as the Yunnan grass, is a perennial grass species native to China.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions and is adapted to temperate to subtropical climates. It plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.

The inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often hairy and contribute to its distinctive appearance.
