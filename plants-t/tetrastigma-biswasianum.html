Tetrastigma biswasianum

Tetrastigma biswasianum, commonly known as the Indian bittersweet, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its tendrils and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
