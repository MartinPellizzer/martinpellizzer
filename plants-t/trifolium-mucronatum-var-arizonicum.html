Trifolium mucronatum var. arizonicum

Trifolium mucronatum var. arizonicum, commonly known as Arizona mountain clover, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southwestern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves composed of three oval to lance-shaped leaflets, often with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
