Toxicodendron amplum

Toxicodendron amplum, commonly known as the giant poison ivy, is a member of the Anacardiaceae family and is native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which typically consist of three to five leaflets, and its clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are toxic and can cause skin irritation upon contact. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to exercise caution when handling or coming into contact with this plant.
