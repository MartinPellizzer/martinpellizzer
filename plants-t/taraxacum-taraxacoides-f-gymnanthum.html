Taraxacum taraxacoides f. gymnanthum

Taraxacum taraxacoides f. gymnanthum, commonly known as the gymnanthum dandelion, is a botanical variant of the dandelion family.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flower heads and deeply lobed leaves. The plant is typically found in temperate regions, often in meadows and grasslands. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer.

The f. gymnanthum form is notable for its unique floral structure and is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
