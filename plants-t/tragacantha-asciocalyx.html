Tragacantha asciocalyx

Tragacantha asciocalyx, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of Iran and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is notable for producing a sticky resin, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive.

Its adaptability to harsh environments makes it an important species in arid ecosystems.
