Tetrastigma megalocarpum

Tetrastigma megalocarpum, commonly known as the giant grapevine, is a woody vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its vigorous growth and climbing habit. The plant produces large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns red when mature.

Tetrastigma megalocarpum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
