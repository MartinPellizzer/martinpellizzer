Trigonella spruneriana subsp. sibthorpii

Trigonella spruneriana subsp.

sibthorpii, commonly known as the Sibthorp's fenugreek, is a herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia. It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

The plant features trifoliate leaves with a distinctive trifoliate structure, and its small yellow flowers are arranged in racemes. It produces small, hairy seeds that are often dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is of interest in botanical studies due to its ecological adaptations and potential medicinal properties.
