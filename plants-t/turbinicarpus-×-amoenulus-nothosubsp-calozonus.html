Turbinicarpus × amoenulus nothosubsp.

calozonus, commonly known as the "calico cactus," is a hybrid species within the Cactaceae family, characterized by its unique and colorful floral display. It originates from crosses between Turbinicarpus amoenulus and Turbinicarpus calozonus, resulting in a nothosubspecies with distinctive morphological features.

The plant typically grows in arid environments, featuring a short, thick stem with spines and a striking, bell-shaped flower that blooms in shades of yellow, orange, and red. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and resilience to drought conditions.

Due to its hybrid nature, it is often cultivated for its beauty and adaptability in semi-desert landscapes.
