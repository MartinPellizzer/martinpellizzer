Thermopsis yushuensis

Thermopsis yushuensis, commonly known as the Yushu yellow poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Qinghai-Tibet Plateau.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils at high altitudes. It has adapted to the harsh climate with features such as a deep root system and thick leaves.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
