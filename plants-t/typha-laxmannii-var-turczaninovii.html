Typha laxmannii var. turczaninovii

Typha laxmannii var. turczaninovii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cattail, is a wetland plant characterized by its narrow, strap-like leaves and cylindrical flower spikes.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often found in marshes, pond edges, and along riverbanks. The plant produces small, brown, canoe-shaped seeds that are dispersed by water, aiding in its spread. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense colonies, which can stabilize soil and provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.

This variant is distinguished from other cattail species by its narrower leaves and slightly different floral structure.
