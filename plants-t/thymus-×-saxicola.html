Thymus × saxicola

Thymus × saxicola, commonly known as narrowleaf thyme, is a hybrid plant in the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing, spreading habit and small, aromatic leaves. The plant produces pink to purple flowers in summer, which are clustered in dense spikes. Native to southern Europe, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

It is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
