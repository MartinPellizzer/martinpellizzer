Thryptomene leptocalyx

Thryptomene leptocalyx, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thryptomene, is a small evergreen shrub native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalypts and other aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish bloom. It produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are typically clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences.

Thryptomene leptocalyx is often found in dry, open forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting native wildlife.
