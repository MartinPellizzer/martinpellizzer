Trifolium andersonii var. friscanum

Trifolium andersonii var. friscanum, commonly known as the Friscanum clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of the United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and disturbed areas.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it ecologically significant in soil enrichment.
