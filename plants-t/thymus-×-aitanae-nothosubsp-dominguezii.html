Thymus × aitanae nothosubsp. dominguezii

Thymus × aitanae nothosubsp.

dominguezii, commonly known as the Dominguez thyme, is a hybrid plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is a low-growing shrub with aromatic, small, grey-green leaves that are densely packed.

The plant produces pink to purple flowers in spring, which are clustered in small groups. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and Mediterranean climates.

Its botanical characteristics reflect a blend of its parent species, Thymus aitanae and Thymus dominguezii.
