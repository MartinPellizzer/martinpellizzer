Tagetes seleri

Tagetes seleri, commonly known as the African marigold, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow and orange flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and features narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

The flowers are not only ornamental but also have historical uses in traditional medicine.
