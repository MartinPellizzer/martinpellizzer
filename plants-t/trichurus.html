Trichurus

Trichurus, commonly known as the threadworm, is a genus of nematodes that primarily infects the intestines of various animals, including humans.

These small, cylindrical worms are characterized by their slender, thread-like bodies, which can range in length from a few millimeters to several centimeters. Trichurus species are typically found in the large intestine and are known for their ability to cause trichuriasis, a parasitic infection. The life cycle of Trichurus involves egg-laying in the soil, where the eggs develop into infective larvae before being ingested by a host.

Due to their simple morphology and parasitic lifestyle, Trichurus species are important in both medical and ecological studies.
