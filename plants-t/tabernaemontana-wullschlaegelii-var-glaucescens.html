Tabernaemontana wullschlaegelii var. glaucescens

Tabernaemontana wullschlaegelii var. glaucescens, commonly known as the blue-veined tabernanthe, is a striking tropical plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its large, waxy, dark green leaves with prominent blue veins, giving it a unique aesthetic appeal. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale blue, often with a yellow throat. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
