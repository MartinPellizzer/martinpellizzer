Thymus serpyllum f. loevyanus

Thymus serpyllum f. loevyanus, commonly known as the Loevy's thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, small, needle-like leaves. The plant produces tiny pink or white flowers in clusters, typically blooming in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for rock gardens and as a ground cover.
