Thymus serpyllum f. rochelianus

Thymus serpyllum f. rochelianus, commonly known as the Rochel's thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, aromatic leaves that emit a strong thyme scent. The plant produces pink to purple flowers in the spring, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
