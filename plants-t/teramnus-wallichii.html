Teramnus wallichii

Teramnus wallichii, commonly known as the Himalayan milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It is a shrub that typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, characterized by its upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pink flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, and the plant has a milky sap that gives it its common name.

Teramnus wallichii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in temperate climates.
