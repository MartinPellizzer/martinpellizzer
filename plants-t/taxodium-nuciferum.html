Taxodium nuciferum

Taxodium nuciferum, commonly known as the Chinese pond cypress, is a deciduous conifer native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and its ability to thrive in wetland environments. The tree produces small, round cones that mature to a reddish-brown color, and its leaves are scale-like and shed annually. It has a shallow root system that allows it to tolerate waterlogged soils, making it a common species in marshy areas.

The wood of Taxodium nuciferum is lightweight and has been historically used for construction and papermaking.
