Teucrium scordium subsp. serratum

Teucrium scordium subsp.

serratum, commonly known as the serrated germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is characterized by its narrow, serrated leaves.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually violet or purple in color, arranged in spikes. It is often found in open woodlands and along rocky slopes.

Its aromatic foliage and adaptability to harsh environments make it a notable species in its native habitat.
