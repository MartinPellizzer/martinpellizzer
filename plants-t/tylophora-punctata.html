Tylophora punctata

Tylophora punctata, commonly known as the punctate tylophora, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in shaded, moist environments.

Tylophora punctata is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
