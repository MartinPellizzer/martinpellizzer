Triceros paniculata

Triceros paniculata, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, flame-red flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has compound leaves with three leaflets, giving it the genus name "Triceros," meaning "three horns.". Its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
