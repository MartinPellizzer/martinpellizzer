Tibouchina membranifolia

Tibouchina membranifolia, commonly known as the membranous tibouchina, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features large, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair, giving them a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom throughout the year, attracting various pollinators. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

However, it requires well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight to thrive.
