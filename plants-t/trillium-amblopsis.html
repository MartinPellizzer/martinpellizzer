Trillium amblopsis

Trillium amblopsis, commonly known as the small-flowered trillium, is a North American perennial herb belonging to the Trillium genus.

It is characterized by its three broadly ovate leaves and a single, nodding white flower with three petals. The plant typically grows in rich, moist woodlands and thrives in partial to full shade. It produces a capsule fruit containing numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Trillium amblopsis is a slow-growing plant with a long lifespan, often living for several decades.
