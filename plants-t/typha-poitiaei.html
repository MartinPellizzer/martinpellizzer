Typha poitiaei

Typha poitiaei, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cattail, is a wetland plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and cylindrical flower spikes that produce fluffy seeds. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often forming dense stands in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its rhizomatous root system helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Typha poitiaei plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
