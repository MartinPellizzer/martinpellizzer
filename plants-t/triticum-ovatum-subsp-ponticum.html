Triticum ovatum subsp. ponticum

Triticum ovatum subsp.

ponticum, commonly known as the Pontic wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the eastern Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with two-rowed glumes and awned lemmas. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
