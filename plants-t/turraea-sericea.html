Turraea sericea

Turraea sericea, commonly known as the silky turraea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The leaves are opposite, elliptic in shape, and covered with a fine silvery or whitish pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically yellow or greenish-yellow, followed by small, woody fruits.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
