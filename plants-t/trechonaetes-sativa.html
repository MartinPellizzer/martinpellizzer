Trechonaetes sativa

Trechonaetes sativa, commonly known as the common hemp plant, is a member of the Cannabaceae family.

It is a herbaceous annual plant that can grow up to 5 meters tall, with large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters. It is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, and thrives in temperate climates.

Its fibrous stalks are used in various industrial applications, while its seeds are a source of nutrition.
