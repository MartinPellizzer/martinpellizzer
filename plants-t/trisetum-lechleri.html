Trisetum lechleri

Trisetum lechleri, commonly known as the rubber tree, is a perennial plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, fibrous roots that produce a latex-like substance, which has been historically used for rubber production. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in the understory of rainforests. Its leaves are elongated and have a waxy surface, which helps reduce water loss in humid conditions.

The plant's botanical significance lies in its role in both traditional and industrial applications due to its unique latex composition.
