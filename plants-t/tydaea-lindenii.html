Tydaea lindenii

Tydaea lindenii, commonly known as the Lindenberg's grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid conditions, showing resilience to drought.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and forage for local wildlife.
