Trifolium striatum var. incanum

Trifolium striatum var. incanum, commonly known as the hairy trefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically hairy and have a pale green to grayish hue. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, often forming clusters along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
