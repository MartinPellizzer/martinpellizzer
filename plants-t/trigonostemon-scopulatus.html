Trigonostemon scopulatus

Trigonostemon scopulatus, commonly known as the rock beard, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous areas. Its leaves are typically elliptical or ovate, with a rough texture, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Trigonostemon scopulatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
