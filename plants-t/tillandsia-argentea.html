Tillandsia argentea

Tillandsia argentea, commonly known as the silver tillandsia, is a species of air plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help in absorbing moisture from the air. The plant does not require soil and can thrive on branches or rocks, making it an epiphytic species. Its flowers are typically small and come in shades of white, pink, or purple, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its unique growth habits and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
