Tephrosia filipes subsp. filipes

Tephrosia filipes subsp.

filipes, commonly known as the slender tephrosia, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits.

The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 meter, featuring trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or savanna habitats.

The species is noted for its potential medicinal uses and role in ecological systems.
