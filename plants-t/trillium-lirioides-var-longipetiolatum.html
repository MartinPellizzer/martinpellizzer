Trillium lirioides var. longipetiolatum

Trillium lirioides var. longipetiolatum, commonly known as the long-petioled white trillium, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features three elliptic, waxy leaves that are typically green with a bluish tinge. The plant produces a single white or pale pink flower with six petals and three sepals, blooming in early spring. Its flowers have a distinctive, subtle fragrance and are often visited by pollinators such as bees.

The plant thrives in shaded, moist woodlands and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance.
