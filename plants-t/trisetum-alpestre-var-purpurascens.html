Trisetum alpestre var. purpurascens

Trisetum alpestre var. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple hair grass, is a perennial grass species native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and dense, tufted growth habit. The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, while the inflorescence consists of a compact cluster of spikelets. Its flowers are small and wind-pollinated, contributing to its role in ecological systems.

This variety is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions.
