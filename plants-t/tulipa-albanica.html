Tulipa albanica

Tulipa albanica, commonly known as the Albanian tulip, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Balkans.

It belongs to the genus Tulipa in the family Liliaceae, characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers. The plant typically produces vibrant pink or red blooms in early spring, often with a dark blotch at the base of the petals. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The bulbs are relatively hardy and can be propagated through division or seed.
