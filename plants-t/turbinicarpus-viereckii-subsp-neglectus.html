Turbinicarpus viereckii subsp. neglectus

Turbinicarpus viereckii subsp.

neglectus, commonly known as the Neglectus Paddle Cactus, is a species native to the deserts of northern Mexico. It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a thick layer of white, waxy hair.

The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Due to its unique appearance and hardiness, it is a popular choice for collectors and succulent enthusiasts.
