Taraxacum caespitans

Taraxacum caespitans, commonly known as the dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of stiff, hairy leaves and a hollow, leafy stem that supports a flower head. The plant produces a distinctive yellow flower that blooms in early spring, followed by a fluffy white seed head that disperses seeds via the wind. It is a perennial herb that thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in lawns and disturbed areas.

Taraxacum caespitans is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its detoxifying and anti-inflammatory effects.
