Teucrium syspirense

Teucrium syspirense, commonly known as the narrow-leaved germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, hairy hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly serrated edge.

Teucrium syspirense is often found in rocky or dry soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
