Tridax angustifolia subsp. stuebelii

Tridax angustifolia subsp.

stuebelii, commonly known as the narrowleaf tridax, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust growth habit.

The plant typically features slender, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, and it produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in certain areas.
