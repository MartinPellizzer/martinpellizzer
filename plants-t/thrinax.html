Thrinax

Thrinax, commonly known as the bottlebrush palm, is a genus of flowering plants in the palm family, Arecaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, clustered growth habit and spiny, cylindrical stems that resemble a bottlebrush. The leaves are typically feathery and arranged in a rosette pattern around the stem. Thrinax species are native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

These plants are often cultivated for their ornamental value and ability to thrive in coastal and tropical environments.
