Triodia brasiliensis

Triodia brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian grass or Brazilian triodia, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is often found in sandy or disturbed soils and is adapted to thrive in arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its resilience, it plays a role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion in its native habitat.
