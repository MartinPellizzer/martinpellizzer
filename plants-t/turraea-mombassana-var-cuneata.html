Turraea mombassana var. cuneata

Turraea mombassana var. cuneata, commonly known as the African star apple, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to eastern Africa.

It features opposite, elliptic to ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by edible, star-shaped fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. The fruit has a sweet, juicy pulp with a mild, aromatic flavor.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit in local communities.
