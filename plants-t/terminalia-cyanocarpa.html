Terminalia cyanocarpa

Terminalia cyanocarpa, commonly known as the African apple or blueberry tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and alternate, simple leaves. The tree produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by blue-black berries that are edible when ripe. The fruit is rich in antioxidants and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its ecological importance includes providing food for wildlife and supporting local biodiversity.
