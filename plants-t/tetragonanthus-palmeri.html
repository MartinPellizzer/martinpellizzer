Tetragonanthus palmeri

Tetragonanthus palmeri, commonly known as the Palmer's four-angled cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are typically four-angled, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom during the spring and summer months. It is adapted to arid environments and has a deep root system to access water.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for xeriscaping and ecological restoration projects.
