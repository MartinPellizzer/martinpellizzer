Telipogon tachirensis

Telipogon tachirensis, commonly known as the Tachira grass, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Venezuela and Colombia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in open areas such as grasslands and disturbed sites. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with inconspicuous flowers, and its seeds are dispersed by wind.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
