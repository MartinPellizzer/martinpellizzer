Triaenacanthus

Triaenacanthus, commonly known as the three-pronged sea urchin, is a type of echinoderm rather than a plant, so it does not have botanical aspects.

It belongs to the class Echinoidea and is characterized by its spherical body covered in spines. These spines serve as a defense mechanism against predators. Triaenacanthus species are typically found in marine environments, particularly in shallow coastal waters.

They play a role in marine ecosystems by contributing to the structure of benthic habitats.
