Tectaria novoguineensis

Tectaria novoguineensis, commonly known as the New Guinea tectaria, is a member of the Polypodiaceae family, characterized by its fern-like appearance.

It is a terrestrial fern that typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often found in the understory of tropical rainforests. The plant features fronds with a distinctive, segmented structure, and its spores are produced on the underside of the leaves. It has a robust rhizome system that aids in nutrient absorption and anchoring in moist soil.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it plays a significant role in the ecological dynamics of its native regions.
