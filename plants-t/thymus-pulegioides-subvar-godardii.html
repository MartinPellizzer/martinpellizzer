Thymus pulegioides subvar. godardii

Thymus pulegioides subvar. godardii, commonly known as Godard's thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features small, fragrant leaves that are typically gray-green in color, with a slightly fuzzy texture. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic properties make it valuable both in horticulture and traditional herbal medicine.
