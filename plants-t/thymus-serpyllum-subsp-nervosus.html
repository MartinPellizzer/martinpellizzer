Thymus serpyllum subsp. nervosus

Thymus serpyllum subsp.

nervosus, commonly known as narrow-leaved thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features creeping stems that produce small, fragrant leaves with a distinctive nervose venation pattern.

The plant produces small pink or white flowers in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant ground cover.

Its aromatic leaves are often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
