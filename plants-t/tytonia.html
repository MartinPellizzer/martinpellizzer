Tytonia

Tytonia, commonly known as the nightshade, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the Solanaceae family.

These plants are characterized by their showy, often brightly colored flowers and typically have alternate, simple leaves. Many species within the genus produce berries that contain toxic compounds, making them potentially harmful to humans and animals. Tytonia species are often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in warm, humid climates.

They play an important role in both ecological systems and traditional medicine in various parts of the world.
