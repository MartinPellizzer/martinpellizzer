Trichocereus bolligerianus

Trichocereus bolligerianus, commonly known as the Bolligerianus cactus, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its tall, spiny stems and is often found growing in rocky, arid environments. The plant produces large, white flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. Its structure is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and limited water availability.

Trichocereus bolligerianus is also valued for its potential use in traditional medicine and as a decorative plant.
