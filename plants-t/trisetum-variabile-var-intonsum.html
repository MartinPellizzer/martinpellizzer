Trisetum variabile var. intonsum

Trisetum variabile var. intonsum, commonly known as the variable false oat, is a perennial grass native to the temperate regions of North America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. The plant features slender, tufted stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle made up of small, spikelet-like flowers.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
