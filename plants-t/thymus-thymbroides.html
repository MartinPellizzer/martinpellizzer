Thymus thymbroides

Thymus thymbroides, commonly known as the narrowleaf thyme, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of pale pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage is used in culinary and medicinal applications due to its essential oils and antioxidants.

Thymus thymbroides is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
