Terminalia taliala

Terminalia taliala, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and alternate, ovate leaves. The tree produces small, white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. Its wood is valued for its fine grain and durability, making it useful in furniture and carving.

The plant is also used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
