Tetramolopium ochraceum

Tetramolopium ochraceum, commonly known as the yellow ironwood, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, woody growth and small, yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a small, dry capsule that contains several seeds.

Tetramolopium ochraceum is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils and desert landscapes.
