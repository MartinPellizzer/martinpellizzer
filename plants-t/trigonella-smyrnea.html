Trigonella smyrnea

Trigonella smyrnea, commonly known as Syrian cumin or wild cumin, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, branched stems and small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in umbels. The plant produces slender, cylindrical fruits that are often used as a spice in traditional cuisines. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid environments.

Botanically, it belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities.
