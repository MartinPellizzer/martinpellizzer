Tulipa australis var. campestris

Tulipa australis var. campestris, commonly known as the Australian wild tulip, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Australia.

It produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically blooming in spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained soils in full sun. It is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, and it is adapted to dry, temperate climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in native gardens.
