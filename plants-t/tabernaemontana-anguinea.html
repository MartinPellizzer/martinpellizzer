Tabernaemontana anguinea

Tabernaemontana anguinea, commonly known as the black angel's trumpet, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical berries that contain toxic seeds. Its leaves are typically dark green and have a rough texture.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value, though caution is advised due to its toxic properties.
