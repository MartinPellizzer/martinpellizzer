Trifolium schimperi

Trifolium schimperi, commonly known as the Ethiopian clover, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia and parts of Eritrea.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Trifolium schimperi is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
