Tithymalus polycaulis

Tithymalus polycaulis, commonly known as the clustered tithymalus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun conditions. Its small, yellowish flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in dense clusters along the stems.
