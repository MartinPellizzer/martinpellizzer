Thelypteris achalensis

Thelypteris achalensis, commonly known as the Achal Mountain shield fern, is a species of fern native to the mountainous regions of South Asia.

It belongs to the family Thelypteridaceae and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and distinctive fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its fronds are usually bipinnate, with a lanceolate shape and a serrated margin.

Thelypteris achalensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
