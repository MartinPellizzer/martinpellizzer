Thalictrum jacquinianum

Thalictrum jacquinianum, commonly known as Jacob's staff, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and delicate, fern-like foliage. The plant produces showy, greenish-white flowers in early spring, which are clustered in branched cymes. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Thalictrum jacquinianum is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
