Tassadia poeppigiana

Tassadia poeppigiana, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a strikingly beautiful plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white or pale pink flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions, hence its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its foliage consists of long, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery or pale green waxy coating.

Tassadia poeppigiana is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
