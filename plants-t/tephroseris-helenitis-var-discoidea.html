Tephroseris helenitis var. discoidea

Tephroseris helenitis var. discoidea, commonly known as the spiral sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiral arrangement of blue to violet flowers, which are borne on slender, branching stems. The plant has a compact growth habit and produces small, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its unique floral structure and ornamental value make it a notable species in horticulture.
