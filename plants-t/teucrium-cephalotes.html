Teucrium cephalotes

Teucrium cephalotes, commonly known as the mountain germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright stems covered in fine, grayish hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or dry environments.

Teucrium cephalotes is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
