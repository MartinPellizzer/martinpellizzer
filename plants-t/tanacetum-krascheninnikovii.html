Tanacetum krascheninnikovii

Tanacetum krascheninnikovii, commonly known as Krascheninnikov's tansy, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, silver-gray leaves and yellow button-like flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is well adapted to arid environments. It is often found in mountainous areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.

Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and small spaces.
