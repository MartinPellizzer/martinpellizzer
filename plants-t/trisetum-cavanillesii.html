Trisetum cavanillesii

Trisetum cavanillesii, commonly known as the Cavanilles' wood sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikes, which are often clustered at the base of the stems.

Trisetum cavanillesii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
