Telosma cordata

Telosma cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves that are often tinged with purple or reddish veins. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom at night, followed by bright red berries. It thrives in humid environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Telosma cordata is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
