Tritomopterys albida var. subtomentosa

Tritomopterys albida var. subtomentosa, commonly known as the white-veined subtomentose tree, is a species native to the humid lowland forests of Central and South America.

It is a large evergreen tree characterized by its smooth, pale bark and elongated, elliptical leaves with prominent white veins. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody, elongated fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in secondary growth and forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
