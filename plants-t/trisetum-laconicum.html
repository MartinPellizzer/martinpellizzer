Trisetum laconicum

Trisetum laconicum, commonly known as the Laconic hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, along stream banks, and in wetlands. The plant has slender, tufted shoots with small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, spikelet-like flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue when fresh.

Trisetum laconicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
