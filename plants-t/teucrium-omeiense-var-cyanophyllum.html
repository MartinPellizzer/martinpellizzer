Teucrium omeiense var. cyanophyllum

Teucrium omeiense var. cyanophyllum, commonly known as the blue-flowered germander, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Sichuan, China.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features showy blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional medicinal uses in Chinese herbal medicine.
