Thalictrum parvifolium

Thalictrum parvifolium, commonly known as the small-leaved meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves and produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has a rhizomatous root system. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and it is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments.

Thalictrum parvifolium is valued in gardens for its ornamental foliage and ecological benefits.
