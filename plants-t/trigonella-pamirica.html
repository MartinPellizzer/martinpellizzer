Trigonella pamirica

Trigonella pamirica, commonly known as the Pamir chickpea, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces pods containing small, round seeds that are similar in appearance to chickpeas. Trigonella pamirica is adapted to high-altitude environments and is often found in rocky or arid soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in the study of plant adaptation to extreme climates.
