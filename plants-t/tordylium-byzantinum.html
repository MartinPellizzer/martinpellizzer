Tordylium byzantinum

Tordylium byzantinum, commonly known as the Byzantine bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branching stems that bear clusters of blue to violet bell-shaped flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.

Tordylium byzantinum is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in horticulture.
