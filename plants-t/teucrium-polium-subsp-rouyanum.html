Teucrium polium subsp. rouyanum

Teucrium polium subsp.

rouyanum, commonly known as Rouyan's germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions.

The plant features aromatic, hairy leaves that are often gray-green in color, and it produces small, blue or violet flowers in terminal clusters. Its botanical characteristics include opposite leaves and a square stem, typical of the Lamiaceae family.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
