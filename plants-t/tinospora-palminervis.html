Tinospora palminervis

Tinospora palminervis, commonly known as the giant tinospora, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia and parts of the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by woody, fleshy fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in rainforests and secondary growth areas.

Due to its rapid growth and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant.
