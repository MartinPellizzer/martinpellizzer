Teucrium japonicum var. microphyllum

Teucrium japonicum var. microphyllum, commonly known as Japanese germander, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Japan.

It features small, narrow leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to violet flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular plant in horticulture.
