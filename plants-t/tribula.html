Tribula

Tribula, commonly known as false thistle, is a genus of flowering plants in the Asteraceae family.

It consists of several species, most of which are shrubs or subshrubs characterized by their spiny leaves and yellow or white flowers. The plants typically grow in temperate regions and are often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides. Tribula species are known for their ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.

Their botanical features make them important in ecological studies and traditional herbal medicine.
