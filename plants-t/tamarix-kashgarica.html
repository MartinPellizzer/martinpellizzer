Tamarix kashgarica

Tamarix kashgarica, commonly known as the Kashgar tamarisk, is a shrub or small tree native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, feathery foliage and small, scale-like leaves that help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces pink or reddish flowers in summer, which are clustered in cylindrical spikes. It is well adapted to saline and alkaline soils, making it a common species in desert and semi-desert regions.

Tamarix kashgarica is often used for windbreaks and soil stabilization in its native habitat.
