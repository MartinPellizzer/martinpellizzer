Thlaspi infestum

Thlaspi infestum, commonly known as the common whitlowgrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with basal leaves that are typically lobed or divided. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in a raceme, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and wetlands.

Thlaspi infestum is a hardy perennial that is well adapted to temperate climates.
