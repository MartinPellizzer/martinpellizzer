Tagetes pauciloba

Tagetes pauciloba, commonly known as the Mexican marigold or narrowleaf marigold, is a species of flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and bright yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Tagetes pauciloba is also known to repel certain insect pests, making it a useful companion plant in gardens.
