Thermopsis velutina

Thermopsis velutina, commonly known as the velvetleaf thermopsis, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped. The plant produces yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer. It is native to the western United States and thrives in dry, rocky habitats.

Thermopsis velutina is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
