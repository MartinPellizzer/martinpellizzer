Tabernaemontana didyma

Tabernaemontana didyma, commonly known as the white star jasmine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling stars. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves and is typically grown as a shrub or small tree. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is popular in horticulture, though it can be invasive in some areas.
