Thespesia trilobata

Thespesia trilobata, commonly known as the Tahitian hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, with smooth, grayish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The flowers are typically yellow or orange with five petals, and they bloom in clusters. The plant produces woody capsules that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

It thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to salty, well-drained soils.
