Thelypteris quadrangularis

Thelypteris quadrangularis, commonly known as the square-veined fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Thelypteridaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, quadrangular leaf veins that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its fronds are usually lanceolate or ovate in shape, with a leathery texture.

Thelypteris quadrangularis is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
