Trichilia vestita

Trichilia vestita, commonly known as the African sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to Africa.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing flat, winged seeds. Trichilia vestita is valued for its aromatic wood, which is used in traditional crafts and as a source of essential oils.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
