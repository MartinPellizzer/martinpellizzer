Thymus zelenetzkyi

Thymus zelenetzkyi, commonly known as the Zelenetzky thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy flowers. The plant typically features compact, woody stems and small, needle-like leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, forming dense clusters at the ends of the stems.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover in arid landscapes.
