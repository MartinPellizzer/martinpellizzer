Triplotaxis

Triplotaxis, commonly known as the African snowflake, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its delicate, white, star-shaped flowers that resemble snowflakes. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas and has a low, spreading habit. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its drought-resistant nature.

Triplotaxis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
