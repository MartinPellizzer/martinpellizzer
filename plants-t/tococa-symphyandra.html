Tococa symphyandra

Tococa symphyandra, commonly known as the Amazonian black pepper plant, is a climbing shrub native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants, and is characterized by its dark purple to black berries. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or yellow. Its berries are used in traditional medicine and are valued for their potential antioxidant properties.

Tococa symphyandra thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
