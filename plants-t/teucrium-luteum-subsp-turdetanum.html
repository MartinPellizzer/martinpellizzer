Teucrium luteum subsp. turdetanum

Teucrium luteum subsp.

turdetanum, commonly known as the Turdetanum germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils, often in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.

The plant features small, yellowish flowers arranged in spike-like clusters, and its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
