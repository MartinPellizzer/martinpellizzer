Thymus loscosii var. oxyodontus

Thymus loscosii var. oxyodontus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically gray-green in color, covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pink to purple flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its medicinal properties have been historically valued for their antiseptic and digestive benefits.
