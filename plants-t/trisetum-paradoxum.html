Trisetum paradoxum

Trisetum paradoxum, commonly known as the paradox grass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands.

Trisetum paradoxum is often found in disturbed soils and is relatively easy to cultivate in garden settings.
