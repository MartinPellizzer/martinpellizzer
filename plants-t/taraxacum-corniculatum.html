Taraxacum corniculatum

Taraxacum corniculatum, commonly known as the horned dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiky seed heads that resemble small horns, giving it its common name. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that open in the daytime and close at night. Its leaves are typically basal, forming a rosette and are usually hairy and lobed.

Taraxacum corniculatum is native to Europe and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.
