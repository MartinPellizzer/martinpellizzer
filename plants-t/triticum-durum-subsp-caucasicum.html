Triticum durum subsp. caucasicum

Triticum durum subsp.

caucasicum, commonly known as Caucasian durum wheat, is a subspecies of durum wheat native to the Caucasus region. It is characterized by its hard, high-protein grains that are well-suited for pasta and semolina production.

The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 1.5 meters and has a compact, bushy habit. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color, and it produces spikelets that bear small, tough grains.

This subspecies is valued for its adaptability to various climates and its role in traditional agricultural practices.
