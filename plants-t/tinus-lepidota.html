Tinus lepidota

Tinus lepidota, commonly known as the velvetleaf tinus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, velvety hairs, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, sandy soils.

Tinus lepidota is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and ease of care.
