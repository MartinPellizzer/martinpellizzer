Trichilia williamsii

Trichilia williamsii, commonly known as the African sandalwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae, which includes species known for their aromatic wood. The plant features smooth, greyish bark and lanceolate leaves that are opposite in arrangement. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are clustered in panicles, and it produces woody capsules containing aromatic seeds.

The tree is valued for its fragrant timber and is often used in traditional medicine.
