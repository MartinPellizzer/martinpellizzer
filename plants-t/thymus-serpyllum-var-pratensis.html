Thymus serpyllum var. pratensis

Thymus serpyllum var. pratensis, commonly known as common thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, fragrant, needle-like leaves and produces pink to purple flowers in early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders. It is widely used in culinary applications and traditional medicine due to its aromatic properties.

Its compact growth habit and long flowering period make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
