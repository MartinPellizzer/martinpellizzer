Tula soldanellifolia

Tula soldanellifolia, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a bluish-green hue and a smooth, waxy texture. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky habitats.

Tula soldanellifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
