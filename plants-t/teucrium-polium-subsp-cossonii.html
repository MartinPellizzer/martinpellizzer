Teucrium polium subsp. cossonii

Teucrium polium subsp.

cossonii, commonly known as Corsican germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from pale blue to violet, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

Its medicinal properties have been historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
