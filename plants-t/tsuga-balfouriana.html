Tsuga balfouriana

Tsuga balfouriana, commonly known as the Balfour's fir, is a species of conifer native to the western United States.

It is a medium-sized evergreen tree with needle-like leaves that are dark green and arranged in spirals along the branches. The cones of Tsuga balfouriana are typically cylindrical and contain two-winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind. This plant thrives in moist, cool climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of high-elevation forest ecosystems.
