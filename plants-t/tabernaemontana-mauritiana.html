Tabernaemontana mauritiana

Tabernaemontana mauritiana, commonly known as the Madagascar star jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its fragrant, star-shaped white flowers that bloom in clusters during the night. The plant has opposite, simple, and usually hairy leaves that are elliptical in shape. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its botanical name reflects its historical association with the botanist Johann Georg Adam Forster.
