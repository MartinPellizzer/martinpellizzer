Tanacetum corymbosum

Tanacetum corymbosum, commonly known as white tansy, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, silver-gray leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its aromatic properties. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Tanacetum corymbosum is also noted for its historical use in traditional medicine and as a natural pest repellent.
