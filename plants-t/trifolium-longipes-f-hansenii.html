Trifolium longipes f. hansenii

Trifolium longipes f. hansenii, commonly known as the Hansen's long-stemmed clover, is a botanical variant of the long-stemmed clover species.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. This subspecies typically grows in temperate regions with well-drained soils, often found in meadows and grassy areas. Its flowers are small, pink to purple in color, and appear in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value.

The plant is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a forage crop for livestock.
