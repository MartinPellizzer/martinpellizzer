Tradescantia subaspera var. typica

Tradescantia subaspera var. typica, commonly known as the snake plant or mother-in-law's tongue, is a species of flowering plant native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is characterized by its upright, sword-shaped leaves that are typically green with a distinctive white or silver midrib. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers on slender spikes during the growing season. It is a hardy, low-maintenance plant that thrives in well-draining soil and bright, indirect light.

Its ability to tolerate low light and infrequent watering makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
