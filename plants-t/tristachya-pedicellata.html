Tristachya pedicellata

Tristachya pedicellata, commonly known as the golden beard grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and long, slender leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces panicles of small, golden-yellow florets that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Tristachya pedicellata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
