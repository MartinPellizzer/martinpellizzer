Tournesol warionii

Tournesol warionii, commonly known as the golden star orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow petals and a central orange or red star-shaped structure. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as rainforests and mountainous regions. Its flowers bloom during the rainy season, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Tournesol warionii is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its potential use in traditional medicine.
