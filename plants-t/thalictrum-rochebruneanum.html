Thalictrum rochebruneanum

Thalictrum rochebruneanum, commonly known as the Alpine meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments at high altitudes, often in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its flowers bloom in early summer and are pollinated by insects, contributing to the biodiversity of its ecosystem.

Thalictrum rochebruneanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in mountainous habitats.
