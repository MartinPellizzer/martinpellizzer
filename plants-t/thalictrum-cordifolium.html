Thalictrum cordifolium

Thalictrum cordifolium, commonly known as the heartleaf meadow-rue, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves with a heart-shaped base, and its flowers are small, clustered in racemes, typically in shades of white or pale yellow. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream sides. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Thalictrum cordifolium is valued in gardens for its ornamental foliage and early spring blooms.
