Thymus cosacorum

Thymus cosacorum, commonly known as the Cosac thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and aromatic, small, gray-green leaves. The plant produces pink to purple flowers in the spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its essential oils contain thymol and carvacrol, giving it medicinal and culinary uses.
