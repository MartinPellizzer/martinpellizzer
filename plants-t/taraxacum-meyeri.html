Taraxacum meyeri

Taraxacum meyeri, commonly known as Meyer's dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated, hairy leaves and a flowering stem that bears a single, bright yellow composite flower head. The plant is native to central and southern Europe and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. It produces a hollow, ribbed stem and has a fibrous root system.

Meyer's dandelion is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting pollinators.
