Trichoglottis diformis

Trichoglottis diformis, commonly known as the variable trichoglottis, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its sympodial growth habit and produces pseudobulbs that are often elongated and cylindrical. The plant typically features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern around the pseudobulbs. Its flowers are small and usually white or pale pink, with a distinctive labellum that is often notched or lobed.

Trichoglottis diformis is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for orchid enthusiasts.
