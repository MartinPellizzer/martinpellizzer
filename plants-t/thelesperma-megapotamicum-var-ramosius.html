Thelesperma megapotamicum var. ramosius

Thelesperma megapotamicum var. ramosius, commonly known as the ramose lespedeza, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Argentina and Uruguay.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of study in plant taxonomy.
