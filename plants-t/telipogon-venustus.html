Telipogon venustus

Telipogon venustus, commonly known as the velvet tassel orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its intricate, velvety floral structure, with long, pendulous inflorescences bearing small, fragrant flowers. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests or alpine meadows. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, Telipogon venustus is considered endangered in many regions.
