Turnera hirsuta

Turnera hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy turnera, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its distinctive hairy leaves and clusters of small, showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in shaded or semi-shaded environments. Its leaves are usually oval or lance-shaped with a rough, hairy texture, and it produces fruit that is capsule-like in form.

Turnera hirsuta is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various soil types.
