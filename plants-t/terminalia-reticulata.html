Terminalia reticulata

Terminalia reticulata, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and alternate, simple leaves. The tree produces small, white or yellowish flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing flat, winged seeds. It is valued for its hard, heavy wood, which is used in furniture and musical instruments.

The plant also has traditional medicinal uses in various African cultures.
