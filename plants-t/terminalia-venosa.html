Terminalia venosa

Terminalia venosa, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and alternate, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits containing a single seed.
