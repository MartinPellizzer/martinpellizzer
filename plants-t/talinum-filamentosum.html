Talinum filamentosum

Talinum filamentosum, commonly known as the threadleaf amaranth, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, thread-like leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Talinum filamentosum is a member of the Amaranthaceae family, which includes many species valued for their edible leaves and seeds.
