Trixis corymbosa

Trixis corymbosa, commonly known as the desert flax, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, pale yellow to greenish flowers. The plant has a fibrous root system that helps it survive in dry conditions, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs to reduce water loss. Trixis corymbosa is adapted to full sun and well-drained soils, making it a resilient species in harsh desert environments.

Its flowers attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
