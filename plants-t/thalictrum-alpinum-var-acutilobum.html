Thalictrum alpinum var. acutilobum

Thalictrum alpinum var. acutilobum, commonly known as the sharp-lobed meadow rue, is a flowering plant native to alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and compound leaves with finely divided, acute lobes. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers in early summer, which are arranged in panicle-like inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
