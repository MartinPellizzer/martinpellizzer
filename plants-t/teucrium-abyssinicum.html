Teucrium abyssinicum

Teucrium abyssinicum, commonly known as Ethiopian germander, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia and parts of the Horn of Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and spikes of small, blue to violet flowers. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its fragrant leaves and medicinal properties. Teucrium abyssinicum thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in high-altitude regions.

It is valued for its traditional medicinal uses and ornamental appeal.
