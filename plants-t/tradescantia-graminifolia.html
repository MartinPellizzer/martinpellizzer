Tradescantia graminifolia

Tradescantia graminifolia, commonly known as the snake plant or mother-in-law's tongue, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It features upright, sword-shaped leaves that are typically variegated with green, white, and sometimes pink or red stripes. The plant is characterized by its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and its tolerance for a variety of soil types. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, usually green or white, which are followed by dark blue berries.

Tradescantia graminifolia is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and air-purifying properties.
