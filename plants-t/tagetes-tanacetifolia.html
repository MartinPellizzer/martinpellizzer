Tagetes tanacetifolia

Tagetes tanacetifolia, commonly known as the buttonweed or Mexican tarragon, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and yellow-orange daisy-like flowers. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its aromatic foliage and long blooming period add both visual appeal and fragrance to the landscape.
