Teucrium polium var. rouyanum

Teucrium polium var. rouyanum, commonly known as Rouyan's germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive grayish-green color. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rocky or dry garden environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
