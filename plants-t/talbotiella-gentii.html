Talbotiella gentii

Talbotiella gentii, commonly known as the Gentian of the Alps, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky slopes. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a velvety texture.

Talbotiella gentii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
