Thunbergia rogersii

Thunbergia rogersii, commonly known as the African bluebell, is a flowering plant native to eastern Africa.

It is a climbing vine that produces striking blue or violet tubular flowers, which are highly attractive to pollinators. The plant has heart-shaped, opposite leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Thunbergia rogersii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
