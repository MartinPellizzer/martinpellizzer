Trichomanes lambertianum

Trichomanes lambertianum, commonly known as the Lambert's maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically bright green and covered with a waxy cuticle. This plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and contain spores for propagation.

Trichomanes lambertianum is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in controlled indoor environments.
