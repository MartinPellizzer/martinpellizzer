Teucrium bracteatum var. acuticalyx

Teucrium bracteatum var. acuticalyx, commonly known as the narrow-leaf germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright stems that are often hairy and bear opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or violet in color, arranged in spikes at the top of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for both garden use and horticultural studies.
