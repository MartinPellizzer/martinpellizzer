Thymus serpyllum f. bergtianus

Thymus serpyllum f. bergtianus, commonly known as Bergtian thyme, is a subspecies of the common thyme.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant is native to parts of Europe and Asia and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in rocky or sandy habitats, preferring full sun.

Its medicinal properties have been historically valued for their antiseptic and aromatic qualities.
