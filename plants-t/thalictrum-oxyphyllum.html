Thalictrum oxyphyllum

Thalictrum oxyphyllum, commonly known as the showy mountain avens, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like foliage and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a slender, upright stem with numerous whorled leaves, and its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators.

Thalictrum oxyphyllum is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
