Taraxacum sagittilobum

Taraxacum sagittilobum, commonly known as the arrowleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped, arrowhead-like leaves. The plant produces a single, hollow, hairless stem that bears a bright yellow flower head composed of numerous tiny florets. It is native to North America and thrives in a variety of soil types, often found in disturbed habitats.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
