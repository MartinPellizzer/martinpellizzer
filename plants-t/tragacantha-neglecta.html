Tragacantha neglecta

Tragacantha neglecta, commonly known as the neglected tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces a distinctive seed pod that splits open to release its seeds. It is often found in rocky or disturbed soils and is adapted to arid conditions.

Tragacantha neglecta is not widely cultivated but is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological niche.
