Taraxacum macranthum

Taraxacum macranthum, commonly known as the giant dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family, Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a rosette of serrated, hairy leaves that are typically bright green. It produces a long, slender stem that supports its flower head and later develops a parachute-like seed head.

Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it is often found in meadows and open fields.
