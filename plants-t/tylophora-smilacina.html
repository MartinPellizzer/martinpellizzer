Tylophora smilacina

Tylophora smilacina, commonly known as the creeping tylophora, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by follicles containing minute, brown seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal uses, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
