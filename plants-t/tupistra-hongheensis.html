Tupistra hongheensis

Tupistra hongheensis, commonly known as the Honghe tufted yew, is a rare and endemic species native to China.

It belongs to the Taxaceae family and is characterized by its unique foliage and woody structure. The plant features dense, tufted clusters of scale-like leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. It typically grows in mountainous regions with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern.
