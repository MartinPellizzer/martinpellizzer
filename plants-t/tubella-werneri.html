Tubella werneri

Tubella werneri, commonly known as the Werneri's tubella, is a species of fungi belonging to the family Tubellariaceae.

It is characterized by its unique morphology, featuring a delicate, thread-like structure that forms dense, cushion-like growths on decaying wood. The fruiting bodies are typically small and develop in clusters, often resembling a network of fine, branching threads. This fungus is primarily found in moist, forested areas where it decomposes organic matter, playing a vital role in nutrient cycling.

Its ecological significance lies in its ability to break down lignin and cellulose, contributing to the health of forest ecosystems.
