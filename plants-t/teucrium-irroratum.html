Teucrium irroratum

Teucrium irroratum, commonly known as the silver germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Teucrium irroratum is also valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in herbal remedies.
