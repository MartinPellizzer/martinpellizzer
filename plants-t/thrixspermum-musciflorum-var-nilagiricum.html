Thrixspermum musciflorum var. nilagiricum

Thrixspermum musciflorum var. nilagiricum, commonly known as the Nilgiri muskflower, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its compact shrub form and produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which contribute to its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas.

The flowers emit a musky scent, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
