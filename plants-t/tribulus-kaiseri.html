Tribulus kaiseri

Tribulus kaiseri, commonly known as the Caucasian Tribulus, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the family Zygophyllaceae and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and prickly stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are alternate and pinnately divided, while its fruits are covered in spines.

Tribulus kaiseri is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
