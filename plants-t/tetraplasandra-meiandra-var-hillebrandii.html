Tetraplasandra meiandra var. hillebrandii

Tetraplasandra meiandra var. hillebrandii, commonly known as the Hillebrand's tetraplasandra, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its evergreen, glossy, dark green leaves that are elongated and lance-shaped. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
