Thalictrum foetidum var. glabrescens

Thalictrum foetidum var. glabrescens, commonly known as the stinking beggar's-ticks, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves and produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that are highly fragrant. The plant is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It is known for its unique, somewhat unpleasant odor, which is more pronounced in its flowers.

This variety is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
