Triticum aestivum proles compactum

Triticum aestivum proles compactum, commonly known as compact wheat, is a botanical variant of common wheat characterized by its dense, compact growth habit.

It is a hexaploid species, possessing six sets of chromosomes, which contributes to its genetic complexity and adaptability. The plant typically features narrow, sturdy leaves and spikelets that are closely arranged, facilitating efficient photosynthesis and resource allocation. Its compact structure makes it suitable for cultivation in high-density environments, enhancing yield potential.

This variety is valued in agricultural practices for its resilience to environmental stresses and improved grain quality.
