Trifolium stellatum subsp. stramineum

Trifolium stellatum subsp.

stramineum, commonly known as the yellow trefoil, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets with a pale yellow or white margin.

The plant produces small, yellow or white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas.

This subspecies is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it a valuable plant in ecological restoration efforts.
