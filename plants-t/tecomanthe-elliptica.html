Tecomanthe elliptica

Tecomanthe elliptica, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the Caribbean.

It is a climbing vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and purple. The leaves are elliptical, hence the species name, and are often hairy to the touch. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

It is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its traditional uses in herbal medicine.
