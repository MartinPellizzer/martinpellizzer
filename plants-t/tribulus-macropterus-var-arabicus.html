Tribulus macropterus var. arabicus

Tribulus macropterus var. arabicus, commonly known as the Arabian puncture vine, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces distinctive, three-lobed fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often considered a weed in agricultural areas.
