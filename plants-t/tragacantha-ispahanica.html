Tragacantha ispahanica

Tragacantha ispahanica, commonly known as Persian tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the regions of Iran and Turkey.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are followed by pods containing small, round seeds. Its most notable feature is the production of a viscous gum, which has been used historically in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive.

This species is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
