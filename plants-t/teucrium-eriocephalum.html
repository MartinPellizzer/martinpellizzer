Teucrium eriocephalum

Teucrium eriocephalum, commonly known as blue germander, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, bogs, and stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often bluish-green in color. Its showy, clustered blue or violet flowers appear in late spring to early summer.

Teucrium eriocephalum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
