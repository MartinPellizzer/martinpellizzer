Trichilia palustris

Trichilia palustris, commonly known as swamp trichilia, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae, which includes mahogany and related species. The plant is characterized by its smooth, grey-brown bark and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its small, fragrant flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, and it produces woody, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds.

Trichilia palustris is often found in wetland habitats and is valued for its hard, durable wood.
