Tragacantha annularis

Tragacantha annularis, commonly known as the ringed tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive ring-shaped structure in its fruit pods. The plant produces a milky sap that has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a thickening agent. Its flowers are typically pale yellow or white, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Tragacantha annularis is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its historical significance in herbal practices.
