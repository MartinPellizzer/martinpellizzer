Tragacantha obscura

Tragacantha obscura, commonly known as the obscure tragacanth, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by distinctive, inflated seed pods that contain several seeds. It is valued for its ability to thrive in harsh environments and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.

Tragacantha obscura is also noted for its role in soil stabilization and as a source of natural gum in some regions.
