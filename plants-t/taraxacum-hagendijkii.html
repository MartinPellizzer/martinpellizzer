Taraxacum hagendijkii

Taraxacum hagendijkii, commonly known as the Hagendijk's dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant features a rosette of basal, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and covered in fine white pubescence. Its flowering stems bear a single, yellow-orange flower head that develops into a crown of white, fluffy seeds.

The species is noted for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a common sight in disturbed or open habitats.
