Turnera ulmifolia var. intermedia

Turnera ulmifolia var. intermedia, commonly known as Mexican poplar or turnera intermedia, is a shrub native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to various garden environments.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its foliage and the subtle fragrance of its flowers.
