Tragacantha mesoleios

Tragacantha mesoleios, commonly known as the Mesoleios tragacanth, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces a resinous gum, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its ecological and historical significance, it is considered a valuable species in certain regions.
