Tetratheca pilosa var. procumbens

Tetratheca pilosa var. procumbens, commonly known as the prostrate tetra-theca, is a low-growing shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink to white, which are clustered in umbels. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
