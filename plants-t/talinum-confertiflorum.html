Talinum confertiflorum

Talinum confertiflorum, commonly known as red-rooted nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking red or purple flowers that bloom in dense clusters, typically during the warmer months. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, fleshy berries that contain the seeds. Talinum confertiflorum is often found in wetland or marshy areas, where it thrives in moist, rich soil.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitats.
