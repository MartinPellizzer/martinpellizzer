Trema tomentosum

Trema tomentosum, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to regions of Asia, Africa, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense, soft pubescence, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles. Its fruit is a three-winged samara, which aids in wind dispersal.

Trema tomentosum is often found in disturbed habitats and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
