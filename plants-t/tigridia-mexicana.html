Tigridia mexicana

Tigridia mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican fairy lantern, is a striking member of the Iridaceae family native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow, resembling small lanterns. The plant produces tall, slender stems with lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. Its blooming period occurs in the summer months, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Tigridia mexicana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
