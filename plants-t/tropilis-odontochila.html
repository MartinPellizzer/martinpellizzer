Tropilis odontochila

Tropilis odontochila, commonly known as the toothed lily, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, clustered inflorescences. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it requires specific growing conditions to flourish.
