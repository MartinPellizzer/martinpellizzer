Tinus jambosioides

Tinus jambosioides, commonly known as the yellow-flowered karoo grass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and yellow daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, aiding in water conservation.
