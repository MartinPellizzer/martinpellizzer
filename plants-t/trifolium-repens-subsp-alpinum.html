Trifolium repens subsp. alpinum

Trifolium repens subsp.

alpinum, commonly known as alpine white clover, is a low-growing perennial herb native to alpine regions of Europe. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets, often with a white or pale green coloration.

The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in early summer. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas.

This subspecies is adapted to cold climates and is an important component of alpine ecosystems.
