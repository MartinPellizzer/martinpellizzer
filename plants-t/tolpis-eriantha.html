Tolpis eriantha

Tolpis eriantha, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to yellow. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and has a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowering period occurs during the warmer months, and it is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance.

Tolpis eriantha is also valued for its ability to thrive in poor soils and its drought tolerance.
