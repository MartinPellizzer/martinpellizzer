Trichogonia alba

Trichogonia alba, commonly known as the white trichogonia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, white flowers that bloom during the early summer months. The plant has slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
