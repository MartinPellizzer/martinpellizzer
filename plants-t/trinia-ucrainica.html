Trinia ucrainica

Trinia ucrainica, commonly known as the Ukrainian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun. Its leaves are opposite, lanceolate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Trinia ucrainica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
