Trichocentrum sprucei

Trichocentrum sprucei, commonly known as the Spruce's orchid, is a climbing epiphytic orchid native to Central and South America.

It features long, slender stems that can grow up to several meters in length, often trailing or climbing among trees. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers with a distinctive spurred lip. Its leaves are elongated, leathery, and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Trichocentrum sprucei thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining potting media for optimal growth.
