Tamarix lessingii

Tamarix lessingii, commonly known as the desert saltbush, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It has small, scale-like leaves that reduce water loss, adapting to its harsh, dry environment. The plant produces pink to red flowers in clusters, which bloom in the spring and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access deep groundwater, making it highly drought-tolerant.

Tamarix lessingii plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
