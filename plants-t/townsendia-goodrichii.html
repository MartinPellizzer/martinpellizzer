Townsendia goodrichii

Townsendia goodrichii, commonly known as Goodrich's tansy, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its small, yellowish-orange flowers bloom in the spring, adding a delicate touch to its habitat.

The species is named in honor of the botanist Charles Goodrich, reflecting its significance in botanical studies.
