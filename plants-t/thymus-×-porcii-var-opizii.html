Thymus × porcii var. opizii

Thymus × porcii var. opizii, commonly known as the opizii thyme, is a hybrid species within the Thymus genus, characterized by its compact growth habit and aromatic foliage.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe, where it thrives in well-drained, sunny soils. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers in early summer, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow, fragrant, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance and scent.

This variety is often used in herbal medicine and culinary applications due to its essential oils and aromatic properties.
