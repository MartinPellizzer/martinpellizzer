Tacca vespertilio

Tacca vespertilio, commonly known as the "moonflower" or "night flower," is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe. The plant produces large, showy flowers that open at night, emitting a strong, sweet fragrance to attract pollinators. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, often with a velvety texture.

Tacca vespertilio is cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in warm, humid conditions.
