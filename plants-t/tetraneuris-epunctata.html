Tetraneuris epunctata

Tetraneuris epunctata, commonly known as the white daisy or white fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its small, white to pale yellow flowers bloom in the spring and summer, attracting various pollinators.

Tetraneuris epunctata is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in poor, well-drained conditions.
