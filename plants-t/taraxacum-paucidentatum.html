Taraxacum paucidentatum

Taraxacum paucidentatum, commonly known as the few-toothed dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of dark green, hairy leaves and a yellow composite flower head that develops into a spherical seed ball. Native to southern Africa, this plant thrives in dry, grassy environments and is often found in disturbed soils. It has a robust root system that allows it to survive in arid conditions.

The plant is noted for its medicinal properties, with various parts used traditionally in herbal remedies.
