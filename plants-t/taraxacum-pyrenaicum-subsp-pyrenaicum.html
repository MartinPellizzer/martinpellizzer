Taraxacum pyrenaicum subsp. pyrenaicum

Taraxacum pyrenaicum subsp.

pyrenaicum, commonly known as the Pyrenean dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the Pyrenees region. It is a perennial herb characterized by its basal rosette of serrated, hairy leaves.

The plant produces a yellow flower head that develops into a fluffy seed head with white pappus. It thrives in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to temperate climates and nutrient-poor soils.
