Tecoma tanaeciiflora

Tecoma tanaeciiflora, commonly known as the golden trumpet tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves, which are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. It produces long, slender pods that contain several seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Tecoma tanaeciiflora is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to drought conditions.
