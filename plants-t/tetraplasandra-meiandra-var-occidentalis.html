Tetraplasandra meiandra var. occidentalis

Tetraplasandra meiandra var. occidentalis, commonly known as the Western pandanus, is a variant of the pandanus genus native to the Pacific Islands.

It is a flowering plant characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, fan-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that develop into woody fruits containing multiple seeds. It thrives in coastal and lowland areas, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value and cultural significance, it is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
