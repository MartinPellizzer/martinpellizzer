Tereianthus suffruticulosus

Tereianthus suffruticulosus, commonly known as the yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a rough texture.

Tereianthus suffruticulosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
