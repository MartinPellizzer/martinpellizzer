Tabernaemontana stapfiana

Tabernaemontana stapfiana, commonly known as the Stapf's star of Bethlehem, is a flowering plant belonging to the Apocynaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its showy, fragrant, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant features opposite, elliptic to ovate leaves with a smooth texture and a glossy surface. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a long history of use in traditional medicine.

However, it contains toxic alkaloids and should be handled with care.
