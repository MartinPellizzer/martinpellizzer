Trichosalpinx castellensis

Trichosalpinx castellensis, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that often feature a vibrant purple or violet color. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils, thriving in high-altitude environments. Its structure includes a short rhizome with fleshy roots, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like.

Trichosalpinx castellensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and specialized collections.
