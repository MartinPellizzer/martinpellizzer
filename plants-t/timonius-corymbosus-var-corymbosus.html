Timonius corymbosus var. corymbosus

Timonius corymbosus var. corymbosus, commonly known as the fragrant corymb, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and aromatic, emitting a subtle scent when crushed. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
