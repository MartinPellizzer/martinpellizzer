Taraxacum perdevexum

Taraxacum perdevexum, commonly known as the everblooming dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to southern Europe and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and serrated leaves. The plant is known for its ability to produce multiple blooms throughout the growing season, hence its common name. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, sunny locations.

The plant's hairy stem and distinctive seed heads make it easily recognizable in the wild.
