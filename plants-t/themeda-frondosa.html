Themeda frondosa

Themeda frondosa, commonly known as spear grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and narrow, blade-like leaves that are typically green and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas.

Themeda frondosa plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
