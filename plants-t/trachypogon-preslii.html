Trachypogon preslii

Trachypogon preslii, commonly known as the Preslii grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in grasslands and along stream banks. The plant has slender, tufted culms with narrow, blade-like leaves that are often hairy along the edges. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle bearing small, spikelet-like flowers.

Trachypogon preslii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
