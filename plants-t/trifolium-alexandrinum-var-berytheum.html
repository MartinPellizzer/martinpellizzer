Trifolium alexandrinum var. berytheum

Trifolium alexandrinum var. berytheum, commonly known as Egyptian clover, is a variant of the common clover species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which typically consist of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, often in the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental gardening or as a cover crop.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to various environmental conditions, contributing to its widespread cultivation.
