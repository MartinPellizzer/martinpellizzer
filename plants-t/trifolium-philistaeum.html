Trifolium philistaeum

Trifolium philistaeum, commonly known as the Philistine clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight.

Trifolium philistaeum is often found in rocky or sandy habitats and is adapted to dry environments.
