Tasoba

Tasoba, commonly known as the "tall grass" or "wild millet," is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open areas and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its seeds are small and dispersed by wind.

Tasoba is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
