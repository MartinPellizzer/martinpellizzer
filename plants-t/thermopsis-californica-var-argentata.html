Thermopsis californica var. argentata

Thermopsis californica var. argentata, commonly known as silverleaf goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to California.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces bright yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and inland areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
