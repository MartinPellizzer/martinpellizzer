Tina striata subsp. parvifolia

Tina striata subsp.

parvifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tina, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, and the flowers are borne on short pedicels.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
