Tournefortia puberula var. kirkii

Tournefortia puberula var. kirkii, commonly known as the Kirk's velvetleaf, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or savanna habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in subtropical gardens.
