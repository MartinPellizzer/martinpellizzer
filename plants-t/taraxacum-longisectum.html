Taraxacum longisectum

Taraxacum longisectum, commonly known as the long-sected dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrowly elongated, hairy leaves. The plant produces a single, hollow, yellowish stem that terminates in a composite flower head. Its flowers are typically yellow and bloom in early spring, followed by the formation of a white, fluffy seed head.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats, showcasing its adaptability to various environments.
