Thymus krylovii

Thymus krylovii, commonly known as Krylov's thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It features small, aromatic leaves and produces clusters of pink to purple flowers. The plant is characterized by its woody stems and dense foliage, which are typical of the Thymus genus. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

Due to its medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated for use in herbal remedies.
