Terminalia longipes

Terminalia longipes, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elongated, woody pods containing flat, fibrous seeds. It is often found in savanna and woodland habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in furniture and construction.
