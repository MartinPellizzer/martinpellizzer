Trichosalpinx carvii

Trichosalpinx carvii, commonly known as the Carvii's spider orchid, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, spider-like flower structure. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are usually pink or purple and are pollinated by specific insects.

Trichosalpinx carvii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
