Trichilia verrucata var. plurifoliolata

Trichilia verrucata var. plurifoliolata, commonly known as the African sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to Africa.

It is characterized by its large, compound leaves that can have multiple leaflets, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody, spiny fruits. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The species is also valued for its aromatic wood, which is used in the production of incense and furniture.
