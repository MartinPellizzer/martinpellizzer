Thryptomene rosea

Thryptomene rosea, commonly known as the pink thryptomene, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.
