Tristachya viridiaristata

Tristachya viridiaristata, commonly known as the green-bracted triad, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Tristachya viridiaristata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual contrast and texture.
