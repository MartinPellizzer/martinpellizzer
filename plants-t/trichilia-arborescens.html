Trichilia arborescens

Trichilia arborescens, commonly known as the African sandalwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and simple, alternate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by elongated, woody pods containing flat, winged seeds. It is valued for its aromatic wood, which is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.

The plant also plays an important ecological role in its native habitats by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
