Tilia × flavescens

Tilia × flavescens, commonly known as the golden lime linden, is a hybrid species within the Tiliaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that turn a striking yellow in the fall. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its bark is smooth and greyish-brown, with shallow fissures.

Tilia × flavescens is often found in moist, well-drained soils in temperate regions.
