Tryphostemma arenophilum

Tryphostemma arenophilum, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in spring and summer. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, with a hairy texture that helps reduce water loss.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils.
