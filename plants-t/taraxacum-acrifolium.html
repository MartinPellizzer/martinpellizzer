Taraxacum acrifolium

Taraxacum acrifolium, commonly known as the bitterleaf dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family, Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, hairy, and sometimes prickly leaves. The plant produces a single, hollow, and often spiny stem that bears a bright yellow flower head. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
