Tsia

Tsia, commonly known as the African violet, is a tropical plant native to central Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves and showy, colorful flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 10 to 30 centimeters, with thick, fleshy stems that store water. Its leaves are usually velvety and vary in color from deep green to purple, while the flowers come in shades of pink, violet, and white.

Tsia thrives in humid, well-drained soil and requires indirect sunlight for optimal growth.
