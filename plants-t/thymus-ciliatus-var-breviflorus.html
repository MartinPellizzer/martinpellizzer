Thymus ciliatus var. breviflorus

Thymus ciliatus var. breviflorus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant leaves that are typically gray-green in color and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its aromatic foliage makes it a popular plant for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
