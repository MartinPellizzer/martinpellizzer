Thelypteris rigidifolia

Thelypteris rigidifolia, commonly known as the rigid fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Thelypteridaceae and is characterized by its stiff, leathery fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually bipinnate, with a dense arrangement of small, finger-like segments.

Thelypteris rigidifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its lush, textured foliage.
