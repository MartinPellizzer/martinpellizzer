Trillium rivale

Trillium rivale, commonly known as the northern white trillium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It features three broadly ovate leaves that are typically bright green and have a waxy texture. The plant produces a single, showy white flower with three petals and three sepals, which bloom in early spring. Trillium rivale thrives in rich, moist, well-drained soils and is often found in deciduous forests.

It is a slow-growing plant with a long lifespan, sometimes living for several decades.
