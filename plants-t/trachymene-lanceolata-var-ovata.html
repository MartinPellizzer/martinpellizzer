Trachymene lanceolata var. ovata

Trachymene lanceolata var. ovata, commonly known as the broad-leaved false shamrock, is a perennial herb native to Australia.

It features lanceolate to ovate leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers in clusters, which are typically borne on upright stems. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
