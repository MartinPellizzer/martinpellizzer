Trias intermedia

Trias intermedia, commonly known as the common trias, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Trias intermedia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

It is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.
