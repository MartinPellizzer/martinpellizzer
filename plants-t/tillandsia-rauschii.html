Tillandsia rauschii

Tillandsia rauschii, commonly known as the Rausch's air plant, is a species of epiphytic bromeliad native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It thrives by absorbing moisture and nutrients through its leaves rather than through roots, making it well adapted to its canopy-dwelling lifestyle. The plant features dense, overlapping foliage that forms a rosette shape, often with a striking silver or grayish-blue hue. It produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom from the center of the rosette, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
