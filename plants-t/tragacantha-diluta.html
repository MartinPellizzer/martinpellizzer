Tragacantha diluta

Tragacantha diluta, commonly known as the pale tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces a gum-like substance known as tragacanth, which has been historically used in medicine and as a thickening agent. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both ecological and horticultural study.
