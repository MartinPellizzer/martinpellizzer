Thyana simplicifolia

Thyana simplicifolia, commonly known as the simpleleaf thyana, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often lance-shaped. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
