Trifolium aureum subsp. achtarovii

Trifolium aureum subsp.

achtarovii, commonly known as the Achtarovii yellow clover, is a subspecies of the yellow clover native to specific regions of Eastern Europe. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, typically with three leaflets that are yellowish-green in color.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and meadows.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique genetic traits and ecological adaptations.
