Trineuria cymbiformis

Trineuria cymbiformis, commonly known as the helmet orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, helmet-shaped floral structure, which is both visually distinctive and functionally adapted for pollination. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories where it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellow, with intricate patterns that aid in attracting its primary pollinators, such as certain species of bees.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, Trineuria cymbiformis is considered endangered, highlighting the need for conservation efforts to protect this botanical marvel.
