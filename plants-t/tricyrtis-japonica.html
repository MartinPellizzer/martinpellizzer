Tricyrtis japonica

Tricyrtis japonica, commonly known as Japanese ghost orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan and Korea.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its unique, orchid-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows up to 30 cm tall, with lance-shaped leaves and showy, violet or white flowers that have a distinctive hood-like structure. It prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, often found in forest understories.

Tricyrtis japonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate, ephemeral blooms.
