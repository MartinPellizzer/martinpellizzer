Typha tichomirovii

Typha tichomirovii, commonly known as the Tichomirov's cattail, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of Central Asia.

It belongs to the genus Typha, which is part of the family Typhaceae, and is characterized by its tall, cylindrical inflorescences. The plant thrives in shallow water or moist soils, often forming dense stands in marshy areas. Its leaves are long and flat, while its stems are thick and often covered in a layer of brown, fibrous material.

Typha tichomirovii plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
