Tragacantha lupulina

Tragacantha lupulina, commonly known as the yellow tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its yellowish, sticky resinous sap, which is harvested for various industrial uses. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are woody and branched.

The botanical significance of Tragacantha lupulina lies in its unique resin composition and its historical use in medicine and as a natural adhesive.
