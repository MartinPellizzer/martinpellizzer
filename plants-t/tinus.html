Tinus

Tinus, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dense, hard wood and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by pods containing edible seeds. Tinus is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.

Its botanical features make it valuable for both ecological and economic purposes.
