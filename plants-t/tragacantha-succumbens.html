Tragacantha succumbens

Tragacantha succumbens, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody structure and compound leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, followed by the formation of distinctive seed pods. Tragacantha succumbens is notable for its ability to thrive in harsh, dry environments, making it an important species in xerophytic ecosystems.

Its sap has historically been used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural gum.
