Tulipa turcica

Tulipa turcica, commonly known as the Turkish tulip, is a bulbous perennial plant native to Turkey and the surrounding regions.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that typically range in color from red to yellow. The plant produces strap-like leaves that are broad and flat, growing from a bulbous underground stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Tulipa turcica is known for its early spring blooming habit, often appearing before many other tulip species.
