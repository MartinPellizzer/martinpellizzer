Thymus serpyllum subsp. subayachicus

Thymus serpyllum subsp.

subayachicus, commonly known as the Subayachic thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of eastern Turkey. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and square stems.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are small, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, which contribute to its distinctive aromatic scent.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
