Trichera nympharum

Trichera nympharum, commonly known as the fairy orchid, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its small, intricate flowers that often display vibrant colors. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and absorbing moisture from the air. Its leaves are narrow and strap-like, and it produces a unique scent that attracts specific pollinators.

Due to habitat loss, Trichera nympharum is considered endangered and is protected in many regions.
