Trifolium resupinatum var. clusii

Trifolium resupinatum var. clusii, commonly known as the Clusius clover, is a variant of the yellow trefoil.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, typically arranged in loose clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in botanical gardens.
