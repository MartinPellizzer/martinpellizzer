Tephrosia elata var. tomentella

Tephrosia elata var. tomentella, commonly known as the hairy tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems covered in fine, white pubescence. The leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets arranged along the central axis. Its flowers are clustered in racemes and typically have five petals, with the lower petal being prominently spurred.

This plant is often found in disturbed soils and is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
