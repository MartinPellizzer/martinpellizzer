Tinospora convolvulacea

Tinospora convolvulacea, commonly known as Indian moonseed, is a climbing shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its twining stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by orange-red berries that contain seeds. It thrives in humid environments and is often found in forests and along riverbanks.

Tinospora convolvulacea is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional systems of medicine.
