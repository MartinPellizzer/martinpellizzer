Taxodium imbricarium

Taxodium imbricarium, commonly known as the pond cypress, is a deciduous conifer native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, columnar shape and its ability to thrive in wetland environments. The tree has scale-like leaves that turn a striking reddish-brown in the fall, and it produces cones that are typically less than 1 inch in length. Its roots are adapted to waterlogged soils, making it a common species in swampy areas.

The wood of the pond cypress is lightweight and resistant to decay, which has made it historically valuable for construction and boat building.
