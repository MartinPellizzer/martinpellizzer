Taraxacum ceratophorum var. bernardinum

Taraxacum ceratophorum var. bernardinum, commonly known as the Italian dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features a rosette of basal, spoon-shaped leaves with a hairy surface, and it produces a yellow flowering head that develops into a fluffy seed head. The plant is characterized by its taproot system and is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats. It has a cosmopolitan distribution and is cultivated for its edible leaves and medicinal properties.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
