Tephrosia maculata subsp. appressepilosa

Tephrosia maculata subsp.

appressepilosa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tephrosia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa. It features small, elliptical leaves with a distinctive dark green upper surface and a lighter underside.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom during the rainy season. Its stems are typically hairy and slightly woody, and it often grows in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
