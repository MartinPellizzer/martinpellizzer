Trifolium leptocephalum

Trifolium leptocephalum, commonly known as the slender-headed clover, is a flowering plant species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Trifolium leptocephalum is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for agricultural purposes.
