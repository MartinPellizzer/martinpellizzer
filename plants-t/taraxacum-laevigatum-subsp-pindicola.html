Taraxacum laevigatum subsp. pindicola

Taraxacum laevigatum subsp.

pindicola, commonly known as the Pindos dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are typically woolly and covered in fine hairs. Its flower heads are yellow and produce a characteristic seed head with white, feathery seeds.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and maintaining soil stability in its native environment.
