Turnera ulmifolia var. orientalis

Turnera ulmifolia var. orientalis, commonly known as the eastern turnera, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically oval to lance-shaped with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and natural landscapes.
