Taraxacum johnstonii

Taraxacum johnstonii, commonly known as Johnston's dandelion, is a flowering plant species native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy stems. The plant produces a yellow flower that develops into a distinctive white, fluffy seed head. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Its leaves are usually basal, with a rough, hairy texture and a distinctive spoon-shaped appearance.
