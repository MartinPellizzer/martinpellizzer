Thlaspi capricornutum

Thlaspi capricornutum, commonly known as the rock cress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing adaptive features such as hairy leaves to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are usually basal, arranged in a rosette, and covered with fine hairs for protection against harsh conditions.

Thlaspi capricornutum is often found in alpine or subalpine regions, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
