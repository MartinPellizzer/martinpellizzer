Triosteum hirsutum

Triosteum hirsutum, commonly known as the hairy triosteum, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink to lavender, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

Triosteum hirsutum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
