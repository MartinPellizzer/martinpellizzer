Triumfetta fissurata

Triumfetta fissurata, commonly known as the rock fig or sand fig, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, the Arabian Peninsula, and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often pubescent and arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by fleshy, orange-red berries that are edible for both humans and animals. Triumfetta fissurata is well adapted to harsh environments, with deep roots that allow it to thrive in poor soils and withstand drought conditions.

Its ability to grow in rocky and sandy soils makes it an important component of desert ecosystems.
