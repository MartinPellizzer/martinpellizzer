Trachypogon montufarii var. pilosus

Trachypogon montufarii var. pilosus, commonly known as the hairy trachypogon, is a grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The inflorescence consists of a spikelet-bearing panicle, which is typically slender and open. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, with lemma and palea that are often hairy.

This variety is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is of interest in ecological and botanical studies.
