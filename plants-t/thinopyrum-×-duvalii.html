Thinopyrum × duvalii

Thinopyrum × duvalii, commonly known as wheatgrasses, is a hybrid plant that belongs to the grass family Poaceae.

It is a cross between Thinopyrum species and wheat, resulting in a plant with unique genetic characteristics. This species is notable for its high levels of nutrients and bioactive compounds, making it valuable in both agricultural and nutritional contexts. Thinopyrum × duvalii is often cultivated for its forage value and its potential in breeding programs to improve wheat resilience.

Its botanical features include narrow leaves, compact growth habit, and the ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
