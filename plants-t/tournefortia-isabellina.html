Tournefortia isabellina

Tournefortia isabellina, commonly known as the Isabelline tournefortia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly rough texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
