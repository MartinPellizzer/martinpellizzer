Taraxacum loratum

Taraxacum loratum, commonly known as the silverleaf dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-green, hairy leaves and a flowering stem that bears a single, yellow composite flower head. Native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa, it thrives in dry, sunny environments. The plant produces wind-dispersed seeds, typical of the dandelion genus.

Its unique foliage and floral structure make it a distinctive member of the Taraxacum genus.
