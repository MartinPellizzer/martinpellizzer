Taraxacum chrysophaenum

Taraxacum chrysophaenum, commonly known as the golden-leaved dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and heart-shaped basal leaves. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grassy areas. Its leaves are typically covered in fine hairs, and the flowers develop into spherical seed heads.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
