Tithymalus graminifolius subsp. orientalis

Tithymalus graminifolius subsp.

orientalis, commonly known as the oriental tithymalus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It features slender, upright stems that bear lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface.

The flowers are small, clustered in dense spikes, and typically range in color from white to pale yellow. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
