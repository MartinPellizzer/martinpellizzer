Tasmannia stipitata

Tasmannia stipitata, commonly known as the Tasmanian tamarind, is a shrub native to eastern Tasmania.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, fleshy berries that are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Tasmannia stipitata is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional Tasmanian cuisine.
