Trifolium repens subsp. giganteum

Trifolium repens subsp.

giganteum, commonly known as the giant white clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its large, bright white flowers and trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets.

This subspecies is notable for its ability to thrive in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands. It has a creeping growth habit, spreading via stolons to form dense mats.

The plant is also valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a forage crop for livestock.
