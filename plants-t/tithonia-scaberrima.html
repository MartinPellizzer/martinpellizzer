Tithonia scaberrima

Tithonia scaberrima, commonly known as Mexican sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, bright orange to red-orange ray flowers and dark green, heart-shaped leaves. The plant is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers. It thrives in warm climates and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its vibrant blooms.

Tithonia scaberrima is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
