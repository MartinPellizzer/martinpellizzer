Tibouchina cordifolia

Tibouchina cordifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian violet or heartleaf tibouchina, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height, featuring broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of showy, violet-blue flowers during the summer months. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Tibouchina cordifolia is also valued for its ability to tolerate partial shade and its use in traditional medicine.
