Teucrium × guemesii

Teucrium × guemesii, commonly known as the hybrid germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its small, tubular flowers are usually purple or blue, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Teucrium × guemesii is valued for its ornamental qualities and drought tolerance in xeriscaping.
