Taeniophyllum marianense

Taeniophyllum marianense, commonly known as the Marian Islands fern, is a small, epiphytic fern native to the tropical regions of the Pacific Ocean.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on tree trunks or rocks in tropical rainforests. Its reproductive structures, including spore-producing sorus, are located on the undersides of its leaves, a common feature among ferns.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Taeniophyllum marianense is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
