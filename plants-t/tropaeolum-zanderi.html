Tropaeolum zanderi

Tropaeolum zanderi, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a climbing annual plant native to South America.

It produces vibrant red flowers and edible pods that are widely cultivated for their culinary use. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and a vigorous growth habit, often reaching several meters in length. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a stem covered in fine hairs.

Tropaeolum zanderi is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
