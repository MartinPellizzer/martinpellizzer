Tricalysia elliottii

Tricalysia elliottii, commonly known as Elliott's flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery red flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Tricalysia elliottii is also valued for its rapid growth and ability to provide shade in urban landscapes.
