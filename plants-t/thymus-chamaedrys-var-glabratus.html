Thymus chamaedrys var. glabratus

Thymus chamaedrys var. glabratus, commonly known as narrow-leaved thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically narrow and densely packed, giving it a compact appearance. The plant produces tiny white or pink flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its foliage is often used in culinary and medicinal applications due to its strong, spicy fragrance.
