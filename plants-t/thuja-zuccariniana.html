Thuja zuccariniana

Thuja zuccariniana, commonly known as the Italian thuja, is a species of conifer native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its narrow, pyramidal shape and dark green, scale-like leaves. The plant produces small, spherical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for ornamental landscaping.

Thuja zuccariniana is relatively slow-growing and resistant to pests and diseases, contributing to its popularity in garden settings.
