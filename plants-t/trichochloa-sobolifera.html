Trichochloa sobolifera

Trichochloa sobolifera, commonly known as the hairy leaved grass, is a perennial plant native to the southern regions of South America.

It is characterized by its fine, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, greenish flowers.

Trichochloa sobolifera is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
