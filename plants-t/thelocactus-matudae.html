Thelocactus matudae

Thelocactus matudae, commonly known as the Matud's cactus, is a rare and striking plant native to the Sonoran Desert in Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that can grow up to three meters in height, covered in dense, white spines. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and bats. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to arid conditions.

Thelocactus matudae is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.
