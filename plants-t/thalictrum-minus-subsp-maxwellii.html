Thalictrum minus subsp. maxwellii

Thalictrum minus subsp.

maxwellii, commonly known as Maxwell's meadow rue, is a herbaceous plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a fibrous root system and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
