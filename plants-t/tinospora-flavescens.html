Tinospora flavescens

Tinospora flavescens, commonly known as the yellow tinospora, is a climbing shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically opposite and have a yellowish-green hue. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by fleshy, orange-red berries. Tinospora flavescens is often found in moist, shady environments such as forests and along riverbanks.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
