Tragia ukambensis

Tragia ukambensis, commonly known as the African burr plant, is a flowering plant native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and savannas. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by spiny, capsule-like fruits containing seeds. Its leaves are usually alternate, simple, and have a rough, hairy texture.

Tragia ukambensis is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
