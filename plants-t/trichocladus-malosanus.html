Trichocladus malosanus

Trichocladus malosanus, commonly known as the Malosan mountain holly, is a rare and endangered plant species native to the high-altitude regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, with dark green, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine, short hairs. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red berries that attract various bird species. It thrives in cool, humid environments and is typically found in cloud forests.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
