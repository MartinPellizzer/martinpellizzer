Tabernaemontana lorifera

Tabernaemontana lorifera, commonly known as the broad-leaved oleander, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant has simple, lance-shaped leaves that are usually arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Tabernaemontana lorifera is also valued for its ability to tolerate poor soil conditions and drought.
