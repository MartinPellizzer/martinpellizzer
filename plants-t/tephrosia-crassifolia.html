Tephrosia crassifolia

Tephrosia crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a legume, belonging to the Fabaceae family, and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows as an herb or shrub, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Its seeds are encased in a hard pod, and it is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial in soil enrichment.
