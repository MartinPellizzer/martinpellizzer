Teucrium subtriphyllum var. haenseleri

Teucrium subtriphyllum var. haenseleri, commonly known as the Haenseler's germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with serrated edges, and it produces small, tubular flowers in shades of blue to purple.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive foliage.
