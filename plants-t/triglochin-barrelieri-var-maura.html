Triglochin barrelieri var. maura

Triglochin barrelieri var. maura, commonly known as the Maura marsh grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of southern Africa.

It typically grows in shallow water or damp soil, thriving in marshy environments. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters. It is characterized by its ability to tolerate fluctuating water levels and nutrient-poor soils.

Its botanical structure supports its role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
