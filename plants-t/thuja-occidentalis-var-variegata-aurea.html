Thuja occidentalis var. variegata-aurea

Thuja occidentalis var. variegata-aurea, commonly known as the golden eastern arborvitae, is a cultivar of the eastern white cedar.

It is characterized by its distinctive golden-yellow foliage, which contrasts sharply with its dark green stems. The plant has a compact, conical shape and is often used as an ornamental hedge or screen in landscaping. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

This variety is relatively low-maintenance and resistant to pests and diseases.
