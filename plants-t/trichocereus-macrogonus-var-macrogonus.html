Trichocereus macrogonus var. macrogonus

Trichocereus macrogonus var. macrogonus, commonly known as the "San Pedro cactus," is a species native to South America, particularly Peru and Bolivia.

It is a columnar cactus with ribbed stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces large, showy white flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. Its stems contain a psychoactive alkaloid called mescaline, which has been used traditionally by indigenous cultures.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for its historical and cultural significance.
