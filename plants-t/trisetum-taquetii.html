Trisetum taquetii

Trisetum taquetii, commonly known as the Taquet's wood sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and other similar plants. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. Its slender, triangular stems are often adorned with small, spike-like clusters of flowers.

Trisetum taquetii is characterized by its delicate foliage and is often found in association with other moisture-loving flora.
