Trophis ramon

Trophis ramon, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits containing numerous seeds. Trophis ramon is typically found in wetland areas, marshes, and along riverbanks, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
