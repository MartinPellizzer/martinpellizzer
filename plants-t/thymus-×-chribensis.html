Thymus × chribensis

Thymus × chribensis, commonly known as hybrid thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its aromatic, needle-like leaves and clusters of small, pink or purple flowers. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders. Its essential oils, rich in thymol and carvacrol, contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.

Thymus × chribensis is often used in traditional medicine for its antimicrobial and antifungal properties.
