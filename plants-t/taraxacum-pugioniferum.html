Taraxacum pugioniferum

Taraxacum pugioniferum, commonly known as the narrowleaf dandelion, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and a flowering stem that produces a single, yellow composite flower head. The plant is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. It has a taproot system that allows it to survive in disturbed environments, and its seeds are dispersed by wind via the feathery pappus.

Taraxacum pugioniferum is often considered a weed but is also valued for its medicinal properties.
