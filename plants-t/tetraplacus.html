Tetraplacus

Tetraplacus, commonly known as the four-leaf clover, is a species of the genus Trifolium.

It is a low-growing herbaceous plant with trifoliate leaves, typically featuring three heart-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Due to its rarity and symbolic significance, it is highly valued in folklore and botany.
