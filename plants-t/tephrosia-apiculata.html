Tephrosia apiculata

Tephrosia apiculata, commonly known as the African senna, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant produces pods that contain flat, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Tephrosia apiculata is also noted for its medicinal properties and use in traditional herbal remedies.
