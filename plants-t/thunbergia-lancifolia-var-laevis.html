Thunbergia lancifolia var. laevis

Thunbergia lancifolia var. laevis, commonly known as the velvetleaf morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in tropical landscapes.

The species is also noted for its rapid growth and ability to climb various supports.
