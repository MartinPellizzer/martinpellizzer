Tephrosia kaessneri

Tephrosia kaessneri, commonly known as the African tephrosia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant features small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is often used in traditional medicine for its purported antimalarial properties.

The plant is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it beneficial in soil enrichment.
