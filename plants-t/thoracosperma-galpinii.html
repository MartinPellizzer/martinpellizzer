Thoracosperma galpinii

Thoracosperma galpinii, commonly known as the Natal mangosteen, is a small evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible, segmented fruits that are similar in appearance to mangosteens. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal forests and riverine areas.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
