Trifolium covillei

Trifolium covillei, commonly known as Coville's clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval to heart-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Coville's clover is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
