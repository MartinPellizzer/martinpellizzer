Tanarius pustulatus

Tanarius pustulatus, commonly known as the spiny pustule cactus, is a unique succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in small, spiny tubercles that provide protection against herbivores and intense sunlight. The plant produces striking yellow or orange flowers during the warmer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it stores water in its thick, fleshy stems for survival in dry environments.

Tanarius pustulatus is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its hardy nature and ornamental appeal.
