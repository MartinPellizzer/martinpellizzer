Taralea crassifolia

Taralea crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf taralea, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its large, fleshy, heart-shaped leaves that are adapted to store water, reflecting its succulent nature. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in high-altitude environments.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists alike.
