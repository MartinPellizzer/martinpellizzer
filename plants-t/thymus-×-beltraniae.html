Thymus × beltraniae

Thymus × beltraniae, commonly known as the Beltrania thyme, is a hybrid species within the genus Thymus, belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and aromatic, small, grey-green leaves. The plant produces clusters of pink to lavender flowers, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is a popular choice among gardeners seeking drought-tolerant plants.
