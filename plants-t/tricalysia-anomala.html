Tricalysia anomala

Tricalysia anomala, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive, fern-like appearance. The plant produces striking yellow to orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its natural spread.

Tricalysia anomala is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and parks for its vibrant floral display.
