Triodia acutispicula

Triodia acutispicula, commonly known as the sharp-pointed triodia, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, wiry growth habit and is often found in arid and semi-arid regions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is adapted to survive in harsh environmental conditions, including drought and poor soil.

Triodia acutispicula plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and is part of the native vegetation in its habitat.
