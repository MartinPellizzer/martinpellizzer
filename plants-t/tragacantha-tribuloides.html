Tragacantha tribuloides

Tragacantha tribuloides, commonly known as the Persian gum or tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial shrub that produces distinctive yellow flowers and is characterized by its woody, branched stem. The plant is known for its resinous sap, which has been historically used in medicine and as a natural thickening agent. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

The botanical features of Tragacantha tribuloides make it an important species in both ecological and traditional medicinal contexts.
