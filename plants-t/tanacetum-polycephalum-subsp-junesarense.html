Tanacetum polycephalum subsp. junesarense

Tanacetum polycephalum subsp.

junesarense, commonly known as the June sand thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is characterized by its low, spreading growth habit.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in rock gardens or as a ground cover.
