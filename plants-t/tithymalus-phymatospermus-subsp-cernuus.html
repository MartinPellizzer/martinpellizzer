Tithymalus phymatospermus subsp. cernuus

Tithymalus phymatospermus subsp.

cernuus, commonly known as the drooping tithymalus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of southern Africa. It features slender, branching stems that often lean or droop, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale yellow to orange in color, arranged in clusters. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland or open woodland habitats.
