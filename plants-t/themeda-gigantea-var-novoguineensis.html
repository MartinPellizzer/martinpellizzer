Themeda gigantea var. novoguineensis

Themeda gigantea var. novoguineensis, commonly known as the New Guinea grass, is a tall, perennial grass species native to tropical regions of New Guinea.

It features long, narrow leaves that are typically green and smooth, with a sturdy stem that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in wetland or swampy environments, often forming dense stands that contribute to soil stabilization.

This variant is distinguished by its robust growth and adaptability to high humidity and nutrient-rich soils.
