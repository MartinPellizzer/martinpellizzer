Tillandsia distachya

Tillandsia distachya, commonly known as the silver pine tillandsia, is a species of air plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help in absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air. The plant produces inflorescences with striking violet or purple flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks in its natural habitat.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
