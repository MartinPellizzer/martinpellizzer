Tetragonolobus bouteloui

Tetragonolobus bouteloui, commonly known as the plains false indigo, is a perennial legume native to the central United States.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairie and open woodland habitats.

Tetragonolobus bouteloui plays a role in nitrogen fixation, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
