Taraxacum stellare

Taraxacum stellare, commonly known as the star dandelion, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of spoon-shaped leaves and a bright yellow flower head composed of many small florets. The plant produces a distinctive, hollow stem that supports the flower and later develops into a fluffy seed head. It is native to Europe and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

Taraxacum stellare is known for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
