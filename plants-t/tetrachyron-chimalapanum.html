Tetrachyron chimalapanum

Tetrachyron chimalapanum, commonly known as the Chimalapa four-leaf plant, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests and along stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a rough texture.

The flowers are small and yellow, arranged in dense clusters, contributing to its ornamental value.
