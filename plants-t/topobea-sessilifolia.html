Topobea sessilifolia

Topobea sessilifolia, commonly known as the sessile-leaf topobea, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of white or pale yellow. The plant has simple, sessile leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized species within its native range.
