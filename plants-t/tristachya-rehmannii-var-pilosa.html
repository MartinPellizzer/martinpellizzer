Tristachya rehmannii var. pilosa

Tristachya rehmannii var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy tristachya, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, hairy foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its unique texture.

This variety is often found in moist, well-drained soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
