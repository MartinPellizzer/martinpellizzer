Thymus humifusus

Thymus humifusus, commonly known as creeping thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from pink to white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, aromatic leaves that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and ground cover.

Its hardy nature allows it to survive in a variety of climates, including colder regions.
