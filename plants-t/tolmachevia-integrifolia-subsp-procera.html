Tolmachevia integrifolia subsp. procera

Tolmachevia integrifolia subsp.

procera, commonly known as the desert spoon, is a striking succulent native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It features tall, spindly stems with large, spoon-shaped leaves that are gray-green in color and covered in fine white hairs.

The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers during the spring, adding a vibrant touch to its arid habitat. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for desert gardens.
