Thalictrum longipes

Thalictrum longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed meadow rue, is a herbaceous plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that give it a delicate appearance. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Thalictrum longipes is valued in gardens for its ornamental foliage and ecological benefits to pollinators.
