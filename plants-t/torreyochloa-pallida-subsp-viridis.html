Torreyochloa pallida subsp. viridis

Torreyochloa pallida subsp.

viridis, commonly known as green sand grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green in color.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
