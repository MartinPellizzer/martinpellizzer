Trichachne ferruginea

Trichachne ferruginea, commonly known as redgrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and reddish-brown inflorescences that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a prominent midrib, and it produces small, spikelet-based flowers.

Trichachne ferruginea is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soil.
