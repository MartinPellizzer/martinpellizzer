Talinum dichotomum

Talinum dichotomum, commonly known as the two-branched spinach, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous annual with opposite, ovate leaves that are slightly hairy and have a peppery flavor. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often grown as a leafy vegetable in home gardens.

Talinum dichotomum is valued for its nutritional content and use in traditional cuisines.
