Trembleya rosmarinoides

Trembleya rosmarinoides, commonly known as the silver leaf plant, is a member of the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic, often fragrant leaves.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in warm, humid climates. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small, tubular flowers are typically pink or purple and bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
