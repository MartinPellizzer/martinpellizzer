Thalictrum sessilifolium

Thalictrum sessilifolium, commonly known as the sessileleaf meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are sessile, meaning they attach directly to the stem without a stalk. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers in early to mid-summer, which are arranged in a raceme. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a suitable choice for woodland gardens.

Its delicate appearance and early blooming period make it a valuable addition to native plant landscapes.
