Thalictrum occidentale var. palousense

Thalictrum occidentale var. palousense, commonly known as the Palouse thimbleweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves that are compound and trifoliate, with three leaflets that are usually lobed or notched. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are borne on slender, leafless stalks. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks.

This variety is distinguished by its unique floral structure and is valued for its ornamental appeal in native plant gardens.
