Tournefortia sarmentosa subsp. usambarensis

Tournefortia sarmentosa subsp.

usambarensis, commonly known as the Usambarensis tournefortia, is a shrub native to eastern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, often prostrate stems and small, opposite leaves.

The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the dry season, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens in tropical regions.
