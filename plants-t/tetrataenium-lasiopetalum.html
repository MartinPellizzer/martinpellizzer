Tetrataenium lasiopetalum

Tetrataenium lasiopetalum, commonly known as the woolly tetrataenium, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
