Tecoma grandiflora

Tecoma grandiflora, commonly known as the yellow bells, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and showy, yellow-orange tubular flowers. The plant produces small, winged fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Tecoma grandiflora thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and landscapes.
