Taraxacum leptoscelum

Taraxacum leptoscelum, commonly known as the slenderleaf dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and a flowering stem that bears a single, bright yellow composite flower head. The plant is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia, where it thrives in open, disturbed habitats. Its seeds are dispersed by wind via a pappus, a feathery structure that aids in aerial distribution.

Taraxacum leptoscelum is often found in meadows, roadsides, and other sunny, well-drained areas.
