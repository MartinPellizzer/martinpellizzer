Taraxacum pycnoforme

Taraxacum pycnoforme, commonly known as the densehead dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant features a rosette of basal, spoon-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically yellow, forming a single, compact inflorescence at the end of a hollow stem.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and is often found in meadows and disturbed areas.
