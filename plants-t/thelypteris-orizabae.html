Thelypteris orizabae

Thelypteris orizabae, commonly known as the Oaxaca holly fern, is a species of fern native to Mexico.

It belongs to the family Thelypteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of its leaves.

Thelypteris orizabae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic foliage.
