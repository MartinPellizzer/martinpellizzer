Tithymalus apiculatus

Tithymalus apiculatus, commonly known as the woolly tithymalus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in coastal regions and is adapted to withstand salty air and coastal conditions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens in regions with a Mediterranean climate.
