Teucrium chrysotrichium var. brevifolium

Teucrium chrysotrichium var. brevifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny to partially shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a distinctive grayish-green color due to dense pubescence.

The flowers are small, tubular, and typically violet or blue, arranged in whorled clusters along the stem.
