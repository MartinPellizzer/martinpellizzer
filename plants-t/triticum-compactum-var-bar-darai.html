Triticum compactum var. bar-darai

Triticum compactum var. bar-darai, commonly known as Bar-Darai wheat, is a compact, dwarf wheat variety characterized by its short stature and high grain density.

It exhibits a robust root system that enhances its drought tolerance, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid regions. The plant produces small, hard grains with a high protein content, which contributes to its use in bread and pasta making. It is typically grown in temperate climates and is valued for its adaptability to poor soil conditions.

The variety is also noted for its resistance to certain diseases, which improves its agronomic performance.
