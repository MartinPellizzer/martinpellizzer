Trichanthecium filifolium

Trichanthecium filifolium, commonly known as the threadleaf false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb with slender, thread-like leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Trichanthecium filifolium is notable for its delicate appearance and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
