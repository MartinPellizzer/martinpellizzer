Thymus serpyllum subsp. pulcherrimus

Thymus serpyllum subsp.

pulcherrimus, commonly known as the pink thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe. It features compact, creeping stems that produce small, pink to purple flowers in early summer.

The plant has aromatic, narrow leaves that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its fragrant foliage and showy blooms.
