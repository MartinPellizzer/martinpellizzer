Timonius wallichianus var. glabrior

Timonius wallichianus var. glabrior, commonly known as the smooth-leaved timonius, is a variant of the timonius plant native to the Himalayan region.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous leaves that are typically lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a delicate floral display. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas at high elevations.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
