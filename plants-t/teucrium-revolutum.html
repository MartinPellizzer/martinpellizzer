Teucrium revolutum

Teucrium revolutum, commonly known as blue germander, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 3 feet tall. The plant produces small, clustered blue or purple flowers that bloom from late spring to early fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a hairy texture and a slightly serrated edge.

Teucrium revolutum is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands.
