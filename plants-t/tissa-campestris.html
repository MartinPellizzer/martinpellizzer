Tissa campestris

Tissa campestris, commonly known as the field thistle, is a perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple or pinkish composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is found in various regions across Europe and parts of Asia. Tissa campestris has a robust root system that allows it to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.

Its foliage and flowers are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.
