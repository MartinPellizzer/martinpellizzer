Teucrium canadense var. boreale

Teucrium canadense var. boreale, commonly known as northern germander, is a perennial herb native to the northern United States and eastern Canada.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy surface and small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
