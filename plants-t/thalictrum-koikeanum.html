Thalictrum koikeanum

Thalictrum koikeanum, commonly known as Koike's meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Thalictrum koikeanum is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
