Tiedemannia canbyi

Tiedemannia canbyi, commonly known as Canby's tansy, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its aromatic foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a strong, camphor-like scent.

Tiedemannia canbyi is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
