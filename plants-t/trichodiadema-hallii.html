Trichodiadema hallii

Trichodiadema hallii, commonly known as the Cape snowflake, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a short, branched stem with dense clusters of white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has fleshy, cylindrical leaves that are covered in a fine, white coating, which helps reduce water loss. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated by succulent enthusiasts.
