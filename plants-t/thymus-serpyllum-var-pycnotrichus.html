Thymus serpyllum var. pycnotrichus

Thymus serpyllum var. pycnotrichus, commonly known as narrow-leaved thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically narrow and densely packed, giving it a compact appearance. The plant produces tiny pink or white flowers in clusters, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its foliage is often used in culinary and medicinal applications due to its strong, pungent aroma.
