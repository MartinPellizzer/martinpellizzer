Thymus jankae var. eximius

Thymus jankae var. eximius, commonly known as the Jankae thyme, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are fragrant, containing essential oils that contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and alpine landscapes.
