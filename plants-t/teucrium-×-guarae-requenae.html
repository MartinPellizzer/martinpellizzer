Teucrium × guarae-requenae

Teucrium × guarae-requenae, commonly known as the hybrid germander, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically features lance-shaped leaves with a hairy surface and produces small, tubular flowers in shades of blue or purple. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long blooming period.
