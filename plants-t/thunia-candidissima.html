Thunia candidissima

Thunia candidissima, commonly known as the white sea anemone, is a marine plant belonging to the family Corallinaceae.

It is characterized by its calcified, branching structure that resembles a coral-like skeleton. It thrives in shallow, warm coastal waters and is often found in association with other calcified algae.

Thunia candidissima plays a role in stabilizing sediments and providing habitat for small marine organisms.
