Taraxacum perminiatum

Taraxacum perminiatum, commonly known as the slender dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, spoon-shaped leaves and a yellow flower head that develops into a fluffy seed dispersal mechanism. Native to Europe, it is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant is perennial, with a taproot that allows it to survive through harsh conditions.

Its botanical features make it a common sight in many temperate regions.
