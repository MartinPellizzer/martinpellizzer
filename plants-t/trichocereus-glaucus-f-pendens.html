Trichocereus glaucus f. pendens

Trichocereus glaucus f. pendens, commonly known as the hanging cactus or pendulous moon cactus, is a rare and striking variant of the Trichocereus genus.

It is characterized by its elongated, drooping stems that can reach several meters in length, often cascading from rock faces or cliffs. The plant features a smooth, pale green body with spines that are typically short and sparse. It is native to the Andes Mountains of South America, where it thrives in arid, high-altitude environments.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is highly sought after by cactus enthusiasts and botanists alike.
