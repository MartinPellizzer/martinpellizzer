Tylosema humifusum

Tylosema humifusum, commonly known as the dwarf milkbush, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its small, fleshy leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments, with features such as succulent stems that store water. Its scientific name reflects its unique morphology, with "tylosema" referring to its swollen, bulbous structures.

Tylosema humifusum is often found in rocky soils and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
