Taraxacum putum

Taraxacum putum, commonly known as the Putum dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of pale green, hairy leaves and a hollow, leafless stem that supports a single, yellow flower head. The plant produces a distinctive seed head composed of numerous lightweight, wind-dispersed seeds with parachute-like structures. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

Taraxacum putum is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
