Tamarix davurica

Tamarix davurica, commonly known as the desert rose, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its grayish bark, small scale-like leaves, and clusters of pink or red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is highly drought-tolerant and thrives in sandy or saline soils. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in harsh environments.

Tamarix davurica is also used in traditional medicine and for ornamental purposes due to its hardy nature and attractive appearance.
