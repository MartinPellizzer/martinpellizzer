Trigonachras postardanjeisin

Trigonachras postardanjeisin, commonly known as the golden flame orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow and orange inflorescences, which resemble flames, giving it its common name. The plant features long, narrow leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring. Native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
