Tanacetum argyranthemoides

Tanacetum argyranthemoides, commonly known as silverleaf tansy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and its daisy-like yellow flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, also known as the sunflower or daisy family, and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, and it is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

Tanacetum argyranthemoides is also known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
