Thunbergia ridleyi

Thunbergia ridleyi, commonly known as the velvet flower or velvetleaf, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant produces small, dark blue or black berries that contain the seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Thunbergia ridleyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
