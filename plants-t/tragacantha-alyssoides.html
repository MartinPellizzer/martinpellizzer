Tragacantha alyssoides

Tragacantha alyssoides, commonly known as the Persian violet, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters, featuring hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, violet-colored flowers that bloom in early spring. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

Tragacantha alyssoides is also known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun conditions.
