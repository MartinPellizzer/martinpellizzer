Thymus micranthus

Thymus micranthus, commonly known as narrowleaf thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically gray-green in color, covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and medicinal herb gardens.

Thymus micranthus is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, particularly for its essential oils and antioxidant properties.
