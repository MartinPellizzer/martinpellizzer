Triticum villosum var. glabratum

Triticum villosum var. glabratum, commonly known as the smooth wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, leafy stems and dense, spike-like inflorescences. The plant has narrow, hairless leaves and produces small, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

This species is sometimes used in forage and has been studied for its potential in phytoremediation.
