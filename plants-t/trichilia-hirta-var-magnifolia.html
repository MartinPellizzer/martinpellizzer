Trichilia hirta var. magnifolia

Trichilia hirta var. magnifolia, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a tropical tree native to regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, light-colored bark and large, palmately lobed leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by woody, elongated fruits containing flat, winged seeds. It is valued for its durable wood, which is used in furniture and construction, and for its medicinal properties in traditional healing practices.

The species thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests and secondary growth areas.
