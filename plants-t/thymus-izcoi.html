Thymus izcoi

Thymus izcoi, commonly known as Izco thyme, is a species of flowering plant in the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is native to the Iberian Peninsula, specifically found in the region of Extremadura in Spain. This perennial herb has aromatic, small, grey-green leaves that are often used in traditional herbal medicine. The plant produces tiny pink or purple flowers that bloom in the spring, contributing to its ornamental value.

Thymus izcoi is well adapted to dry, rocky environments and is valued for its culinary and medicinal properties.
