Tecoma bibracteolata

Tecoma bibracteolata, commonly known as the yellow trumpet tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a woody stem and produces small, oval leaves that are opposite in arrangement. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental species.

Tecoma bibracteolata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
