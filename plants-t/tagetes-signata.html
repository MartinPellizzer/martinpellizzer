Tagetes signata

Tagetes signata, commonly known as the Mexican marigold, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its strong aromatic scent. Tagetes signata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological diversity.
