Tabernaemontana rendlei

Tabernaemontana rendlei, commonly known as the Rendle's star of Bethlehem, is a striking evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is part of the Apocynaceae family, which includes many species with milky sap and showy blooms. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
