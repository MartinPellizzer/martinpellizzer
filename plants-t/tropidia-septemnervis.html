Tropidia septemnervis

Tropidia septemnervis, commonly known as the seven-veined fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Tropidaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that typically have seven distinct veins running along the length of each leaf. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its reproductive structures consist of sporangia that release spores for propagation.

Tropidia septemnervis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
