Tylophora astephanoides

Tylophora astephanoides, commonly known as the Chinese devil's backbone, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by follicles containing elongated, dark brown seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded areas of forests and is used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both ecological and pharmacological studies.
