Tropaeolum incrassatum

Tropaeolum incrassatum, commonly known as the broadleaf nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, showy flowers that come in shades of orange and yellow. The plant is a herbaceous annual that thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil. Its flowers are not only ornamental but also edible, with a peppery flavor similar to watercress.

Tropaeolum incrassatum is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value and culinary uses.
