Teratophyllum aculeatum var. inermis

Teratophyllum aculeatum var. inermis, commonly known as the smooth spiny cactus, is a unique variant of the teratophyllum genus native to arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its spineless stems, which are typically cylindrical and ribbed, allowing for efficient water storage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the spring or early summer, adding a delicate contrast to its rugged appearance. Its leaves are reduced to spines, a common adaptation in desert environments to minimize water loss.

This variety is valued for its resilience and low maintenance, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
