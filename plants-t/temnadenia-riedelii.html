Temnadenia riedelii

Temnadenia riedelii, commonly known as the Cape satinflower, is a South African plant belonging to the Rutaceae family.

It is a shrub characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves and clusters of small, white or yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically eaten by birds, aiding in seed dispersal. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
