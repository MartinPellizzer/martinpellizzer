Taltalia graminea

Taltalia graminea, commonly known as the silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the temperate regions of South America.

It features slender, silver-gray blades that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread across open habitats. Taltalia graminea thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and grasslands.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
