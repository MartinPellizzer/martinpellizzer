Tragium peregrinum

Tragium peregrinum, commonly known as the wandering thistle, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, silver-green leaves and purple to violet flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sunlight. Its name "wandering thistle" refers to its ability to spread and colonize new areas.

Tragium peregrinum is often found in disturbed habitats and is considered a hardy, resilient species.
