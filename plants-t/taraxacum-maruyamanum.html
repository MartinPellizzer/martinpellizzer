Taraxacum maruyamanum

Taraxacum maruyamanum, commonly known as the Japanese dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the sunflower family.

It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and a single, hollow stem that bears a flower head. The plant produces yellow flowers that develop into fluffy white seeds with parachute-like structures for wind dispersal. It is native to Japan and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Taraxacum maruyamanum is often found in grasslands and along streambanks, showcasing its adaptability to various environments.
