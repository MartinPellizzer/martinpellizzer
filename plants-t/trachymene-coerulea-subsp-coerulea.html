Trachymene coerulea subsp. coerulea

Trachymene coerulea subsp.

coerulea, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to Australia. It features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in suitable climates.
