Teucrium afrum subsp. rubriflorum

Teucrium afrum subsp.

rubriflorum, commonly known as the red-flowered germander, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It features slender, branched stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, with serrated margins.

The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red and purple, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
