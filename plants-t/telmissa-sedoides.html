Telmissa sedoides

Telmissa sedoides, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Telmissa sedoides is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
