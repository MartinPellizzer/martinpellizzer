Teucrium takoumitense

Teucrium takoumitense, commonly known as Moroccan germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Morocco.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions. Its small, tubular flowers are usually purple or blue, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
