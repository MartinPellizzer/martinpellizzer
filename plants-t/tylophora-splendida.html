Tylophora splendida

Tylophora splendida, commonly known as the golden chain vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, slender stems and clusters of small, fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are opposite in arrangement, and it is often found growing in humid, shaded environments. Tylophora splendida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowering habit.

It is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
