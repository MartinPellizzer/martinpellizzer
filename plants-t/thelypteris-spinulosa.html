Thelypteris spinulosa

Thelypteris spinulosa, commonly known as the spinulose water fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Thelypteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or near water bodies. Its leaves are usually bipinnate, with small, spore-producing structures called sori on the underside.

Thelypteris spinulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in ecological systems by contributing to soil stability and providing habitat for microorganisms.
