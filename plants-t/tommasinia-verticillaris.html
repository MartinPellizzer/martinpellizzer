Tommasinia verticillaris

Tommasinia verticillaris, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue and violet. The plant features opposite or whorled leaves, which are typically narrow and lance-shaped. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Tommasinia verticillaris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
