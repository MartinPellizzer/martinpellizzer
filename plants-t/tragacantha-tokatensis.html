Tragacantha tokatensis

Tragacantha tokatensis, commonly known as the Tokat tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant produces a gum resin, which is harvested from its bark and used in various traditional and industrial applications. Tragacantha tokatensis thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils and along riverbanks.

Its ecological importance includes soil stabilization and providing habitat for local wildlife.
