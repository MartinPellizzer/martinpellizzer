Tephroseris tundricola subsp. lindstroemii

Tephroseris tundricola subsp.

lindstroemii, commonly known as the Arctic sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions. It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its small, clustered stems and narrow, linear leaves.

The plant produces white or pale pink flowers in late summer, which are typically arranged in clusters at the tips of the stems. It thrives in nutrient-poor, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, harsh environments.

This subspecies is often found in tundra and rocky habitats across northern Europe and North America.
