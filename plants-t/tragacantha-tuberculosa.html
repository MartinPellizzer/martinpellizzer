Tragacantha tuberculosa

Tragacantha tuberculosa, commonly known as the woolly tragacanth, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its woody stems and dense, hairy foliage that provides a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its roots are known for their ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial in soil enrichment.

Tragacantha tuberculosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
