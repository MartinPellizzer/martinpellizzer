Turnera arcuata

Turnera arcuata, commonly known as the silverleaf turnera, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are elliptical in shape. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in terminal panicles, which bloom during the warmer months.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its foliage and floral display.
