Taraxacum sherriffii

Taraxacum sherriffii, commonly known as the Sherriff's dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces a yellow composite flower head that develops into a white, fluffy pappus for wind dispersal of its seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in disturbed soils and rocky habitats.

The species is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
