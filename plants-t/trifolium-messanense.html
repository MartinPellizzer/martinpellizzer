Trifolium messanense

Trifolium messanense, commonly known as the Messina clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. Native to southern Europe, particularly Italy, this plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and full sun. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species in its native habitat.
