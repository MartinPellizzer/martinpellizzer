Tamonea laevigata

Tamonea laevigata, commonly known as the smoothleaf sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to hot, dry climates. Its leaves are smooth and often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

Tamonea laevigata is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and drought tolerance.
