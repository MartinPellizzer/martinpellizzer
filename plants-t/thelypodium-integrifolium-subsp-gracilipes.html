Thelypodium integrifolium subsp. gracilipes

Thelypodium integrifolium subsp.

gracilipes, commonly known as the slenderleaf thelypodium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in grasslands and meadows.

The plant features slender, upright stems with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are entire and often hairy. Its small, yellowish flowers are arranged in dense spikes and bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
