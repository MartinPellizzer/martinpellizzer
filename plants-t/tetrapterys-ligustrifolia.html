Tetrapterys ligustrifolia

Tetrapterys ligustrifolia, commonly known as the American hollyleaf cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers in clusters, followed by red or orange berries that are an important food source for wildlife. Its botanical features include a smooth, often reddish bark and the ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

Tetrapterys ligustrifolia is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness.
