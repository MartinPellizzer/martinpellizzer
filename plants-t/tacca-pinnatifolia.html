Tacca pinnatifolia

Tacca pinnatifolia, commonly known as the "tongue of the scorpion," is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fleshy, dark purple bracts that resemble the tail of a scorpion. The plant produces small, white flowers that emerge from the base of the bracts. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires high humidity to flourish.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular ornamental plant in botanical gardens and conservatories.
