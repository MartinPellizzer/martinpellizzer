Taraxacum palustre subsp. crocinum

Taraxacum palustre subsp.

crocinum, commonly known as the marsh dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in Europe and parts of Asia. It features a rosette of broad, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces a single, yellow, composite flower head that blooms in late spring to early summer. Its seeds are dispersed by wind via lightweight, parachute-like structures called pappi.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators in its native wetland environments.
