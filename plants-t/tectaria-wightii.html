Tectaria wightii

Tectaria wightii, commonly known as the Wight's shield fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Tectariaceae and is characterized by its leathery, shield-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows on tree trunks or branches in humid, tropical environments. Its rhizomes are often covered in a thick, protective layer of spongy tissue.

Tectaria wightii is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated in shaded gardens.
