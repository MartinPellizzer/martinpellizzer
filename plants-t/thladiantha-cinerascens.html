Thladiantha cinerascens

Thladiantha cinerascens, commonly known as the ash-colored honeysuckle, is a woody vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five elliptical leaflets, and its flowers are small, fragrant, and typically white or yellowish. The plant produces red or orange berries that are edible for some bird species but toxic to humans. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of local ecosystems.
