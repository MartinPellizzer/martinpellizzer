Teucrium paui

Teucrium paui, commonly known as purple germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or blue in color, arranged in spikes. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Teucrium paui is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various purposes.
