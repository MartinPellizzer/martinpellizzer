Tropaeolum morreanum

Tropaeolum morreanum, commonly known as the everlasting nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces small, edible seed pods that are often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a peppery taste, similar to watercress.

Tropaeolum morreanum is a herbaceous annual that thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight.
