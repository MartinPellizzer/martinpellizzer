Triainolepis tomentella var. pilosituba

Triainolepis tomentella var. pilosituba, commonly known as the hairy-stemmed velvetleaf, is a tropical plant characterized by its dense, velvety foliage.

It features broad, heart-shaped leaves covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in rainforests or along riverbanks. Its stems are covered in a thick layer of pubescence, contributing to its common name.

This variety is part of the Malvaceae family, sharing some characteristics with other members of the hibiscus group.
