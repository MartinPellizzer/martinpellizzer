Taraxacum subreduncum

Taraxacum subreduncum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, typically found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces a yellow flower that develops into a fluffy seed head. It has a taproot system and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

The species is often considered a weed but has ecological and medicinal significance.
