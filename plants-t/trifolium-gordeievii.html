Trifolium gordeievii

Trifolium gordeievii, commonly known as the Gordeev's clover, is a rare and endangered species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments. Due to habitat loss and overgrazing, its population has drastically declined, making conservation efforts crucial for its survival.

This species plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
