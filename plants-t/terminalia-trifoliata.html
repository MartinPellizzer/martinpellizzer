Terminalia trifoliata

Terminalia trifoliata, commonly known as the Indian almond tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged symmetrically. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody, three-lobed fruits. Its bark is thick and fissured, often exuding a reddish sap.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
