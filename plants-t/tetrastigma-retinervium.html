Tetrastigma retinervium

Tetrastigma retinervium, commonly known as the net-veined grapevine, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with a distinctive net-like venation pattern. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically followed by clusters of berries. Its woody stems are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Tetrastigma retinervium is also cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and vigorous growth habit.
