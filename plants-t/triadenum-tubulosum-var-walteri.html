Triadenum tubulosum var. walteri

Triadenum tubulosum var. walteri, commonly known as Walter's thimbleweed, is a perennial herb native to the central and eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, tubular leaves and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it produces small, achene-like seeds.

The species is notable for its distinctive morphology and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
