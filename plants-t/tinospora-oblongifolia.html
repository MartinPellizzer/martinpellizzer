Tinospora oblongifolia

Tinospora oblongifolia, commonly known as the Indian banyan or wild betel vine, is a climbing shrub belonging to the family Menispermaceae.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate, and often heart-shaped leaves that are usually hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences, followed by red, fleshy berries containing several seeds. Tinospora oblongifolia is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including India, China, and Southeast Asia.

It is often found growing in moist, shaded areas such as forest undergrowth and along stream banks.
