Teucrium canadense var. angustatum

Teucrium canadense var. angustatum, commonly known as narrowleaf germander, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet environments such as marshes and swamps. The plant features slender, upright stems with opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink to purple and bloom in late summer.

It is often found in the eastern United States and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
