Tilia × budensis

Tilia × budensis, commonly known as the hybrid linden, is a deciduous tree belonging to the Tiliaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Tilia cordata (small-leaf linden) and Tilia europaea (large-leaf linden). The tree typically grows to a height of 15–25 meters, featuring heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and arranged in drooping clusters, producing a fragrant scent that attracts pollinators.

The bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age, and the tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance.
