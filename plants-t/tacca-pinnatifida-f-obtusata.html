Tacca pinnatifida f. obtusata

Tacca pinnatifida f. obtusata, commonly known as the broad-leaved arrowroot or the Indian arrowroot, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often adorned with striking purple veins. The plant produces inflorescences that resemble arrows, which are typically green with a distinctive purple base. While it is primarily grown for its starchy roots, it is also cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique foliage.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
