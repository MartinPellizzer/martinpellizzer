Tibouchina mollis

Tibouchina mollis, commonly known as the velvetleaf tibouchina, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, characterized by its dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces clusters of showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in the spring and summer months. Its leaves are often covered in a fine, velvety texture, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Tibouchina mollis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
