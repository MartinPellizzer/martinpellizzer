Tunica carthusianorum

Tunica carthusianorum, commonly known as the Carthusian monk's tunic, is a rare and unique plant species native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that resembles the coarse fabric of medieval monastic robes. The plant produces small, pale green flowers that bloom in late spring, adding a subtle aesthetic to its rugged appearance. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine environments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
