Tabernaemontana reticulata

Tabernaemontana reticulata, commonly known as the laceleaf tabernaemontana, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety, heart-shaped leaves that are intricately veined and often have a waxy texture. The plant produces fragrant, white, funnel-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
