Taraxacum asiaticum

Taraxacum asiaticum, commonly known as the Asian dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces a yellow flower head that develops into a fluffy seed sphere, which is dispersed by wind. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

Taraxacum asiaticum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
