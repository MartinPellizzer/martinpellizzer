Tamarix bachtiarica

Tamarix bachtiarica, commonly known as the Persian tamarisk, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, feathery foliage and small, scale-like leaves that reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces pink or purple flowers in summer, which are borne in clusters and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in saline and alkaline soils.

Tamarix bachtiarica is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
