Thalictrum minus proles fallacinum

Thalictrum minus proles fallacinum, commonly known as the lesser thalictrum or fernleaf meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, usually yellow or greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. It has a fibrous root system and produces numerous stolons, allowing it to spread vegetatively.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
