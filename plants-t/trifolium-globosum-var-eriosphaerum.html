Trifolium globosum var. eriosphaerum

Trifolium globosum var. eriosphaerum, commonly known as the woolly globeflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive globular, woolly inflorescence, which consists of numerous small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three rounded leaflets that are often covered in fine, soft hairs.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
