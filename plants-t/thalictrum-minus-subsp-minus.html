Thalictrum minus subsp. minus

Thalictrum minus subsp.

minus, commonly known as the lesser meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family. It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and finely divided, fern-like leaves.

The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers with prominent stamens, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Native to moist meadows and woodlands in temperate regions, it thrives in partial shade to full sun.

Its ornamental value lies in its delicate foliage and subtle floral display, making it a sought-after addition to native plant gardens.
