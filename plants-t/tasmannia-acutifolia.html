Tasmannia acutifolia

Tasmannia acutifolia, commonly known as the Tasmanian pepper or mountain pepper, is a flowering plant native to Tasmania.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, temperate rainforests and has glossy, dark green, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruits are small, red berries that contain several seeds, and the plant is valued for its aromatic leaves used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Tasmannia acutifolia is also an important component of Tasmania's native flora and has ecological significance in its habitat.
