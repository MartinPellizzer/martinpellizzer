Tulipa chrysantha

Tulipa chrysantha, commonly known as the golden tulip, is a species native to Central Asia and the Middle East.

It is a bulbous perennial plant that produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange. The plant typically grows to a height of 10 to 30 centimeters, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers bloom in early spring, often before the leaves fully emerge.

Tulipa chrysantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
