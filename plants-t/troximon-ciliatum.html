Troximon ciliatum

Troximon ciliatum, commonly known as the silky tamarind, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery leaves and small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain flat, edible seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its unique foliage and adaptability, it is a popular choice for gardeners seeking to add texture and color to their landscapes.
