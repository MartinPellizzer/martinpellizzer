Terrellianthus

Terrellianthus, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a vibrant display during the growing season. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Terrellianthus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and public spaces.
