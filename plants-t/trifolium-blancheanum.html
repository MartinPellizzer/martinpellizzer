Trifolium blancheanum

Trifolium blancheanum, commonly known as the Blanchean clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom in early spring. Native to certain regions of South America, it thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in shaded woodland areas.

This species is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a forage plant for livestock.
