Torenia spathacea

Torenia spathacea, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the morning and close by midday. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sun.

Torenia spathacea is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant blooms and ease of care.
