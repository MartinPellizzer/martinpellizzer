Triticum × duvalii

Triticum × duvalii, commonly known as wild rye, is a hybrid grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and spike-like inflorescences, typically reaching heights of 1 to 3 meters. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

Triticum × duvalii is often found in open woodlands, prairies, and along roadsides, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
