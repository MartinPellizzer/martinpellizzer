Tibouchina aurea

Tibouchina aurea, commonly known as the silverleaf tibouchina, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring dense, silver-gray foliage. The plant produces clusters of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red, depending on the variety. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are evergreen, and the plant is known for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
