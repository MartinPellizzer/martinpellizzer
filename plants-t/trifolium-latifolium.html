Trifolium latifolium

Trifolium latifolium, commonly known as broadleaf clover, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each consisting of three broad, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that are arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils.

Broadleaf clover is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a valuable plant in agricultural settings.
