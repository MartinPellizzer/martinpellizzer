Trembleya phlogiformis var. parvifolia

Trembleya phlogiformis var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved trembleya, is a rare and striking flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like foliage and produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in cloud forests or undergrowth. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
