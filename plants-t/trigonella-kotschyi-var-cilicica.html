Trigonella kotschyi var. cilicica

Trigonella kotschyi var. cilicica, commonly known as the Cilician fenugreek, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Turkey and northern Syria.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod-like fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid climates. Its leaves are trifoliate, and it produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring.

The species is of interest for its potential medicinal properties and ecological role in its native habitat.
