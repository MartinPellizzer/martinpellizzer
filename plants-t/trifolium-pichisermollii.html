Trifolium pichisermollii

Trifolium pichisermollii, commonly known as the Pichisermolli clover, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Native to specific regions in South America, it thrives in moist, temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value and potential ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
