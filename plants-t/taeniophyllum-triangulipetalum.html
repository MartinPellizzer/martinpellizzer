Taeniophyllum triangulipetalum

Taeniophyllum triangulipetalum, commonly known as the triangle-petaled liverwort, is a small, terrestrial liverwort belonging to the family Porellaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, thalloid structure and is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant exhibits a unique morphology with triangularly shaped leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern. It reproduces through spores, which are released from specialized structures called sporangia located on the underside of its leaves.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and sensitive indicator of undisturbed ecosystems.
