Tabebuia pentaphylla var. leucoxylon

Tabebuia pentaphylla var. leucoxylon, commonly known as the pink trumpet tree, is a species of flowering tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive pink to reddish-orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The tree has a straight trunk and produces long, narrow leaves that are opposite in arrangement. Its bark is smooth and light-colored, often with a reddish tint.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is commonly used in landscaping and as a street tree due to its adaptability and resilience.
