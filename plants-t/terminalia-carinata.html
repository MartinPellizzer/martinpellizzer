Terminalia carinata

Terminalia carinata, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. Terminalia carinata is valued for its hard, heavy wood, which is used in furniture and musical instruments.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
