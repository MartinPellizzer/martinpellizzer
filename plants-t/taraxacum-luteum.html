Taraxacum luteum

Taraxacum luteum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and summer months. The plant has a rosette of basal leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. Its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Taraxacum luteum is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and environmental conditions.
