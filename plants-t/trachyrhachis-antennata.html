Trachyrhachis antennata

Trachyrhachis antennata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in three rows. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered at the ends of the stems. Trachyrhachis antennata is often found in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
