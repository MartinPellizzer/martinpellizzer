Taraxacum pannonicum

Taraxacum pannonicum, commonly known as the Pannonian dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of stiff, hairy leaves and a hollow, leafy stem that supports a single flowering head. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that develop into fluffy white seed heads, which are dispersed by the wind. Native to Central and Southeastern Europe, it thrives in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a common and recognizable species in its native habitat.
