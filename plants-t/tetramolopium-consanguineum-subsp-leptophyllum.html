Tetramolopium consanguineum subsp. leptophyllum

Tetramolopium consanguineum subsp.

leptophyllum, commonly known as the slenderleaf tetramolopium, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and appear in clusters, contributing to its ecological role as a nectar source for pollinators.

The subspecies is distinguished by its more slender growth habit compared to other members of the species.
