Taraxacum armeniacum

Taraxacum armeniacum, commonly known as Armenian dandelion, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Turkey.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant features a hairy, upright stem with basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its yellow flowers develop into fluffy seed heads, which are dispersed by the wind.

It is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is valued for its potential health benefits in traditional medicine.
