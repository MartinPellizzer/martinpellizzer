Tradescantia warmingiana

Tradescantia warmingiana, commonly known as the wandering jew, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, lance-shaped leaves that range in color from bright green to deep red, depending on light exposure. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically pink or white, arranged in loose clusters. It is a perennial herb that thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight.

Tradescantia warmingiana is popular as a houseplant due to its ease of care and attractive foliage.
