Taraxacum nutans

Taraxacum nutans, commonly known as the nodding dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and a hollow, leafless stem that supports a single flower head. The flower head consists of both ray and disk florets, which are typically yellow in color. The plant produces a distinctive, wind-dispersed seed with a feathery pappus.

It is often found in disturbed soils and open areas, showcasing its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
