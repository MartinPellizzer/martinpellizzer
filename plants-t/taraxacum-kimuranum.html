Taraxacum kimuranum

Taraxacum kimuranum, commonly known as the Japanese dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and basal rosette of leaves. The plant produces a hollow, hairy stem that supports its flowering head, which typically consists of numerous tiny florets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Taraxacum kimuranum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated as a garden plant.
