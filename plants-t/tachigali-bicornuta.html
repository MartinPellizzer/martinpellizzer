Tachigali bicornuta

Tachigali bicornuta, commonly known as the two-horned tachigali, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which are divided into small, feather-like leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by distinctive, two-lobed pods that contain the seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local communities.
