Thymus gadorensis

Thymus gadorensis, commonly known as the Gadorensis thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic leaves and square stems. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming in early summer. Its foliage is densely packed, with small, narrow leaves that emit a strong thyme scent when crushed.

Thymus gadorensis is often used in traditional herbal medicine and as a culinary herb due to its aromatic properties.
