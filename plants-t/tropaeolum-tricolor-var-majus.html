Tropaeolum tricolor var. majus

Tropaeolum tricolor var. majus, commonly known as the "Indian cress" or "Jacob's ladder," is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, triangular leaves that are often brightly colored. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of red, yellow, and orange, which bloom in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Tropaeolum tricolor var. majus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
