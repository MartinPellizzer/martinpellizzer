Tragopogon novus

Tragopogon novus, commonly known as the yellow gooseneck, is a flowering plant in the dandelion family (Asteraceae).

It is native to the western United States and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and distinctive, curled seed heads. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas such as meadows and disturbed soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer.

Tragopogon novus is often found growing in clusters and is known for its rapid growth and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
