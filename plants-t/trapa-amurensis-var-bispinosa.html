Trapa amurensis var. bispinosa

Trapa amurensis var. bispinosa, commonly known as the Amur water chestnut, is a perennial aquatic plant native to the freshwater regions of East Asia.

It features submerged, branching stems and produces floating, cup-shaped leaves with long, slender petioles. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, spiny fruits that contain edible seeds. It thrives in shallow, still or slow-moving waters, often forming dense vegetation that supports aquatic life.

Its botanical structure makes it an important species for both ecological and agricultural purposes.
