Tozzettia pratensis

Tozzettia pratensis, commonly known as the meadow tozzettia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this plant typically grows in moist meadows and grassy areas. The leaves are linear and often hairy, contributing to its ability to thrive in various soil conditions.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is considered a valuable species in ecological restoration projects.
