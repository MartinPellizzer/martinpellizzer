Triticum vulgare var. clavatum

Triticum vulgare var. clavatum, commonly known as the clubbed wheat, is a variant of common wheat characterized by its distinctive club-shaped awns on the spikelets.

This grass species is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in temperate climates. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with narrow, grass-like leaves and a spike-like inflorescence. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and small, wind-pollinated flowers.

The grains produced are used in traditional baking and have historical significance in agricultural practices.
