Thelypodium sagittatum subsp. ovalifolium

Thelypodium sagittatum subsp.

ovalifolium, commonly known as the narrowleaf arrowhead, is a flowering plant native to North America. It is characterized by its arrow-shaped leaves and small, clustered white or pale pink flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks. It has a creeping rhizome and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Polygonaceae family.
