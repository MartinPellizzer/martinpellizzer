Tachigali macropetala

Tachigali macropetala, commonly known as the large-petaled tachigali, is a tropical tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, with prominent petals and a distinctive scent. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or pea family, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces woody pods that contain small, round seeds.

Tachigali macropetala is often found in humid, lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance.
