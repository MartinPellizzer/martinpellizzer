Thyrsodium schomburgkianum var. salzmannianum

Thyrsodium schomburgkianum var. salzmannianum, commonly known as the Salzmann's thrysodium, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense, spherical clusters called capitula. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.
