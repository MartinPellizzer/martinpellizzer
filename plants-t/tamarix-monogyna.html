Tamarix monogyna

Tamarix monogyna, commonly known as the Mediterranean tamarisk, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and small, scale-like leaves that are typically deciduous. The plant produces small, pink or reddish flowers in clusters, which bloom in summer. It is adapted to arid and saline environments, making it a hardy plant in coastal and dry areas.

Tamarix monogyna is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, which can influence soil composition in its habitat.
