Thryptomene mitchelliana

Thryptomene mitchelliana, commonly known as the Mitchell's thryptomene, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, aromatic flowers that range in color from white to pink. It typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal regions.

Thryptomene mitchelliana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in native garden landscaping.
