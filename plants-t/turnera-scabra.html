Turnera scabra

Turnera scabra, commonly known as the rough turnera, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Turneraceae and is characterized by its distinctive, rough-textured leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or a climbing vine, often found in humid, shaded environments. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
