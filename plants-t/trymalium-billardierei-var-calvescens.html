Trymalium billardierei var. calvescens

Trymalium billardierei var. calvescens, commonly known as the slender mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant native to southeastern Australia.

It attaches to the branches of its host trees, primarily eucalypts, through a specialized root system called haustoria. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers that are typically visited by insects for pollination. Its elliptical, leathery leaves are arranged alternately along the stem, and it relies on its host for water and nutrients.

This species plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat by influencing host tree growth and providing food for certain insect species.
