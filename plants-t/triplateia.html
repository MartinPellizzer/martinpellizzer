Triplateia

Triplateia, commonly known as the three-leaf plant, is a unique species characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each composed of three leaflets arranged symmetrically.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, enhancing fertility. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. Its small, pea-like flowers are often white or pale pink, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Triplateia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional use in herbal medicine.
