Triticum × laxum var. macrostachyum

Triticum × laxum var. macrostachyum, commonly known as the tall wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences with small, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in erosion control and forage production.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in ecological restoration projects.
