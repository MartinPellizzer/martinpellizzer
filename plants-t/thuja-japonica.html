Thuja japonica

Thuja japonica, commonly known as Japanese arborvitae, is a coniferous evergreen shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It has scale-like leaves that are tightly arranged in flattened sprays, giving it a dense, compact appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green cones that mature to a reddish-brown color. It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Thuja japonica is also valued for its aromatic foliage and resistance to pests and diseases.
