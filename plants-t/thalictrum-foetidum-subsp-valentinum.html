Thalictrum foetidum subsp. valentinum

Thalictrum foetidum subsp.

valentinum, commonly known as the stinking beggar's-ticks, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe. It features slender, branched stems that rise from a rhizomatous root system.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in compound umbels, typically blooming in early spring. Its leaves are finely divided and arranged in a whorled pattern along the stem.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.
