Thuja occidentalis var. aurea

Thuja occidentalis var. aurea, commonly known as the golden spruce, is a cultivar of the eastern white cedar.

It is characterized by its bright yellow foliage, which remains vibrant throughout the growing season. This variety is typically smaller than its parent species, with a dense, compact growth habit. The leaves are scale-like and closely pressed to the branches, giving the plant a lush, ornamental appearance.

It is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
