Tristachya angustifolia

Tristachya angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Tristachya angustifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its delicate appearance.
