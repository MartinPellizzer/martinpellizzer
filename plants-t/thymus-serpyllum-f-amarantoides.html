Thymus serpyllum f. amarantoides

Thymus serpyllum f. amarantoides, commonly known as the red thyme, is a compact, low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features small, dark green, oval-shaped leaves and produces dense clusters of pale pink to red flowers in early summer. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, slightly spicy fragrance, which is derived from its essential oils. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and use in culinary and medicinal applications.
