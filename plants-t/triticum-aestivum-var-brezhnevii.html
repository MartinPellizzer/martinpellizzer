Triticum aestivum var. brezhnevii

Triticum aestivum var. brezhnevii, commonly known as the Brezhnev wheat, is a variant of common wheat characterized by its unique genetic composition and agronomic traits.

This variety is notable for its adaptation to specific environmental conditions, particularly in regions with temperate climates. It exhibits a range of botanical features, including a robust root system and a tall, sturdy stem that supports its grain heads. The plant's leaves are typically broad and green, contributing to efficient photosynthesis.

Brezhnev wheat is valued for its high yield potential and resilience to certain biotic and abiotic stresses.
