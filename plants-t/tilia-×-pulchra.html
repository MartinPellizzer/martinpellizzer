Tilia × pulchra

Tilia × pulchra, commonly known as the whitebeam or common whitebeam, is a hybrid species within the Tilia genus.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are typically hairy on the underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early summer, followed by spherical, fuzzy fruits. It is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in woodlands and along riverbanks.

Tilia × pulchra is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in supporting local wildlife.
