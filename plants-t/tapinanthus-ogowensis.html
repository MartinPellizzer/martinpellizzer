Tapinanthus ogowensis

Tapinanthus ogowensis, commonly known as the Ogowensis dodder, is a parasitic flowering plant native to Central Africa.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on its host plants for nutrients, typically attaching itself to other plants through specialized root-like structures called haustoria. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and is often found growing on the stems of trees and shrubs. It plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat by influencing host plant growth and diversity.

Due to its parasitic nature and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists studying plant interactions and ecological dependencies.
