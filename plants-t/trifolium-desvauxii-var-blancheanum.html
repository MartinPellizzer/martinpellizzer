Trifolium desvauxii var. blancheanum

Trifolium desvauxii var. blancheanum, commonly known as the Blancheanum clover, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
