Tromlyca locellata

Tromlyca locellata, commonly known as the golden cup fern, is a species of flowering plant native to the wet tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, clustered inflorescences that resemble small cups, hence its common name. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Tromlyca locellata thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in dense forest undergrowth.

Its unique morphology and vibrant floral display make it a notable species in botanical studies.
