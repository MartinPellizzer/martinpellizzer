Trisetum durangense

Trisetum durangense, commonly known as Durango grass, is a perennial grass species native to Mexico.

It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as rocky slopes and grasslands. The plant has slender, erect stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

Trisetum durangense is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
