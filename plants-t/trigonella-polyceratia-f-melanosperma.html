Trigonella polyceratia f. melanosperma

Trigonella polyceratia f. melanosperma, commonly known as black-seeded trigonella, is a botanical variant of the trigonella genus native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black seeds that contrast with the lighter-colored seeds of the typical form. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and has slender, branching stems. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.
