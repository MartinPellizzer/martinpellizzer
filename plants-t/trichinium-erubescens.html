Trichinium erubescens

Trichinium erubescens, commonly known as the red-stemmed nettle, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, reddish stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Trichinium erubescens is known for its stinging hairs, which can cause skin irritation upon contact.
