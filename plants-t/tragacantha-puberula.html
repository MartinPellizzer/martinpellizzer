Tragacantha puberula

Tragacantha puberula, commonly known as the hairy tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by pods containing several seeds. Tragacantha puberula is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

It has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a source of gum.
