Trichodium nanum

Trichodium nanum, commonly known as the dwarf trichodium, is a small, flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, commonly known as the grass family, and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration that helps it blend into its natural habitat.

Trichodium nanum is often found in moist, sandy soils along the edges of swamps and wetlands.
