Thalictrum jacquinianum subsp. saxatile

Thalictrum jacquinianum subsp.

saxatile, commonly known as the saxatile meadow rue, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine habitats.

The plant features slender, upright stems with palmately divided, bright green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered in loose, racemose inflorescences, with white to pale yellow petals and prominent stamens.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and delicate floral display.
