Thuja obtusa var. pendula

Thuja obtusa var. pendula, commonly known as the hanging arborvitae, is a cultivar of the eastern white cedar.

It is characterized by its cascading, weeping growth habit, with long, slender branches that hang downward. The foliage consists of scale-like leaves that are a deep green, providing a dense, evergreen appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

This plant is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and can be shaped into various forms for aesthetic purposes.
