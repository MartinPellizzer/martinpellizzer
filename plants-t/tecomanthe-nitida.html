Tecomanthe nitida

Tecomanthe nitida, commonly known as the silverleaf vine, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its delicate, silver-gray leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Tecomanthe nitida is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and climbing habit.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.
