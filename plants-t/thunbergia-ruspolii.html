Thunbergia ruspolii

Thunbergia ruspolii, commonly known as the African bluebell, is a climbing plant native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet tubular flowers. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces showy, bell-shaped blooms that attract pollinators. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Thunbergia ruspolii is typically grown as a houseplant or in shaded outdoor gardens.
