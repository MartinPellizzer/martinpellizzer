Thrasyopsis repanda

Thrasyopsis repanda, commonly known as the scarlet bellflower, is a striking plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vivid red, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils, preferring cooler and drier conditions. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence.

Thrasyopsis repanda is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and full sun.
