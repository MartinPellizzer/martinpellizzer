Thymus pulegioides subvar. meduanensis

Thymus pulegioides subvar. meduanensis, commonly known as the Medean thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and square stems. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, blooming in the spring and early summer. Its leaves are narrow, gray-green, and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is often found in rocky or dry soils and is valued for its medicinal and culinary uses.
