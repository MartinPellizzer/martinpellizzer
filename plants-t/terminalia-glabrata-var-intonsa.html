Terminalia glabrata var. intonsa

Terminalia glabrata var. intonsa, commonly known as the African wild mango, is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and elliptical leaves with a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by edible, yellow-orange fruits with a sweet, tropical flavor.
