Trisetella didyma

Trisetella didyma, commonly known as the twin-leafed grass.

It is a small, herbaceous plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets. It is typically found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands.

Trisetella didyma is often used in ecological studies due to its role in soil stabilization and as an indicator of wetland health.
