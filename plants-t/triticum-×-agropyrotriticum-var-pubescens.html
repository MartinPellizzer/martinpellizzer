Triticum × agropyrotriticum var. pubescens

Triticum × agropyrotriticum var. pubescens, commonly known as the awned wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and awned lemmas that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–100 cm and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a forage crop.
