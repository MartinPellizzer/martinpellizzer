Tournefortia rufescens

Tournefortia rufescens, commonly known as the redbrush or red-flowered tournefortia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, often found in areas with high salinity. The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of pink to red flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its ornamental value is due to its vibrant floral display and adaptability to various environmental conditions.

The species is also valued for its potential in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature.
