Toona ciliata var. pubinervis

Toona ciliata var. pubinervis, commonly known as the hairy-veined toona, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and heart-shaped leaves with prominent, hairy veins. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by elongated, woody fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its timber and has traditional uses in local medicine.
