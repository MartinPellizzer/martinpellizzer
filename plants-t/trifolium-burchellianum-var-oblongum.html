Trifolium burchellianum var. oblongum

Trifolium burchellianum var. oblongum, commonly known as the broad-leaved clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval to oblong leaflets arranged in a rosette. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in soil stabilization and as a forage plant.
