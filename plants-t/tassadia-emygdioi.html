Tassadia emygdioi

Tassadia emygdioi, commonly known as the golden spire lily, is a rare and striking plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It features tall, slender stems that can reach up to three meters in height, crowned with a single, showy yellow flower that resembles a spire. The plant's leaves are narrow and grass-like, arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sunlight to flourish.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Tassadia emygdioi is considered endangered and is of great interest to botanists and conservationists.
