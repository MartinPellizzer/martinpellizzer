Truellum biconvexuin

Truellum biconvexuin, commonly known as the twin-convex bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are lance-shaped with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Truellum biconvexuin is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance.
