Trichopteryx arundinacea var. trichantha

Trichopteryx arundinacea var. trichantha, commonly known as the hairy reed, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, hairy foliage that gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, thriving in moist soils. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are arranged in a panicle, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
