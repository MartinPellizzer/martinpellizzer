Tithymalus isatidifolius

Tithymalus isatidifolius, commonly known as the heartleaf tithymalus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features a basal rosette of lanceolate to ovate leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive heart-shaped appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a loose cluster, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are aromatic and contain essential oils, which contribute to its use in traditional herbal medicine.

Tithymalus isatidifolius prefers well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it a suitable plant for rock gardens or wildflower meadows.
