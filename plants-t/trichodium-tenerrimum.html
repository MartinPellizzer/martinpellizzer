Trichodium tenerrimum

Trichodium tenerrimum, commonly known as the desert lily, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that produces striking, showy flowers in shades of yellow to orange, which bloom during the spring and early summer. The plant has a fibrous root system adapted to retain water in dry conditions, and its leaves are typically narrow and hairy to reduce water loss. Trichodium tenerrimum is often found in rocky soils and is well-suited to harsh, drought-prone environments.

Its botanical features make it an important component of desert ecosystems.
