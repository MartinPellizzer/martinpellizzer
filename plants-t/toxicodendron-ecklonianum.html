Toxicodendron ecklonianum

Toxicodendron ecklonianum, commonly known as the Cape poison ivy, is a member of the Anacardiaceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, typically consisting of three leaflets, and produces small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant contains urushiol, a potent allergen that causes severe skin irritation upon contact. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including forests and shrublands, and is often found in coastal areas.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to exercise caution when handling or coming into contact with this plant.
