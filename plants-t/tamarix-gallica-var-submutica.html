Tamarix gallica var. submutica

Tamarix gallica var. submutica, commonly known as the silky tamarisk, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It is often found in coastal areas and saline soils due to its tolerance for drought and salt.

Its rapid growth and ability to stabilize soils make it a useful plant in certain ecological restoration projects.
