Thymus hispanicus var. granatensis

Thymus hispanicus var. granatensis, commonly known as Spanish thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and aromatic, gray-green foliage. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers in early spring, which attract pollinators such as bees. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and Mediterranean climates.

Its essential oils contain thymol and carvacrol, contributing to its use in herbal medicine and culinary applications.
