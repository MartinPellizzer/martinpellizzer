Tulipa sylvestris var. niphontiana

Tulipa sylvestris var. niphontiana, commonly known as the snow tulip, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and produces clusters of pale to deep pink, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its bulbs are adapted to survive harsh winter conditions, making it a hardy species suited to alpine environments.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens with specific climate requirements.
