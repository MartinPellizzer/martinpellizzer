Trollius citrinus

Trollius citrinus, commonly known as the golden buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and western Asia.

It features showy, bright yellow flowers with 5 to 8 petals, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has a short, thick rhizome and lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for wetland gardens and ecological restoration projects.
