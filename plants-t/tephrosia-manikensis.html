Tephrosia manikensis

Tephrosia manikensis, commonly known as the African tephrosia, is a perennial legume native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces cylindrical, woody pods that contain several elliptical seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Tephrosia manikensis is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agricultural systems.
