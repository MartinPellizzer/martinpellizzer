Trichomanes serratifolium

Trichomanes serratifolium, commonly known as the serrated maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided, and serrated fronds that give it its common name. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rock crevices, where it can thrive in high humidity. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are clustered on the underside of its fronds and produce spores for propagation.

Trichomanes serratifolium is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, showcasing its adaptation to warm, humid climates.
