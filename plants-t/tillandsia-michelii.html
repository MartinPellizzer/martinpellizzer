Tillandsia michelii

Tillandsia michelii, commonly known as the Michel's air plant, is a species of bromeliad native to Central America.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on other plants without being parasitic, typically attaching itself to trees or rocks. The plant features rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves that are covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help absorb moisture and nutrients from the air. It produces striking, long-lasting inflorescences with vibrant purple or red flowers.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
