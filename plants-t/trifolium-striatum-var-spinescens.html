Trifolium striatum var. spinescens

Trifolium striatum var. spinescens, commonly known as the spiny clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically trifoliate with three leaflets, and often have a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
