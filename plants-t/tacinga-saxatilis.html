Tacinga saxatilis

Tacinga saxatilis, commonly known as the rock tacinga, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers during the cooler months. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which helps reduce water loss.

Tacinga saxatilis is adapted to arid environments and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.
