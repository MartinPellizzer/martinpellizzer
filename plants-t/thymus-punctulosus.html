Thymus punctulosus

Thymus punctulosus, commonly known as the spotted thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features small, aromatic leaves covered in fine hairs and produces pink to purple flowers in early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders. Its medicinal properties have been historically valued for their antiseptic and digestive benefits.

Thymus punctulosus is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
