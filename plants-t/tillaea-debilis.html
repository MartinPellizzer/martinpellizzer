Tillaea debilis

Tillaea debilis, commonly known as the slender tillaea, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the family Cactaceae, which includes cacti, and is characterized by its succulent stems and reduced leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a delicate touch to its otherwise spiny appearance. Tillaea debilis is well adapted to harsh, dry environments, with a shallow root system that allows it to conserve water efficiently.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for studies on desert flora and plant adaptation.
