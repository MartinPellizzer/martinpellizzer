Tussilago bellidiastrum

Tussilago bellidiastrum, commonly known as the meadow tussilago, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the temperate regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically features hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

Its medicinal properties have historically been valued for their potential respiratory benefits.
