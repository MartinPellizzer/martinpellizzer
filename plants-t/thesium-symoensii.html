Thesium symoensii

Thesium symoensii, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has fibrous roots and is often found in sandy or rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and the plant is adapted to arid conditions.

Thesium symoensii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
