Trollius riederianus subsp. uncinatus

Trollius riederianus subsp.

uncinatus, commonly known as the hooked globemallow, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its showy, yellow, saucer-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces small, club-shaped fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
