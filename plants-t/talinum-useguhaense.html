Talinum useguhaense

Talinum useguhaense, commonly known as the red-rooted nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems, broad oval leaves, and clusters of small, violet-colored flowers. Its fruits are typically berries that turn red when mature, and the plant is known for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.

However, it contains toxic alkaloids and should be handled with care.
