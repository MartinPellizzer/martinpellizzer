Teucrium oxylepis var. rifeum

Teucrium oxylepis var. rifeum, commonly known as the Redstem germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. The plant features slender, upright stems that are often reddish in color, giving it its common name. Its small, tubular flowers are usually purple or violet, and they bloom in summer.

The leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly aromatic, minty scent when crushed.
