Tschudya verticillata

Tschudya verticillata, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes. Its leaves are arranged in a whorl around the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Tschudya verticillata is adapted to cold climates and is often found in mountainous environments.
