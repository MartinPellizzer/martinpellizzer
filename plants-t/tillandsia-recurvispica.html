Tillandsia recurvispica

Tillandsia recurvispica, commonly known as the recurving air plant, is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is native to Central and South America.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on other plants without deriving nutrients from them. Its leaves are typically long and narrow, with a waxy coating that helps it retain moisture in its natural habitat. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with vibrant, showy flowers that attract pollinators.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
