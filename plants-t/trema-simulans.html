Trema simulans

Trema simulans, commonly known as the false indigo or tropical trema, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate or pinnate, and its clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Trema simulans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
