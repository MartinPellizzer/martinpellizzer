Typhonium austrotibeticum

Typhonium austrotibeticum, commonly known as the Tibetan arrowhead, is a perennial herb native to high-altitude regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its arrow-shaped leaves and striking, spadix-like inflorescence. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous areas with cool climates. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
