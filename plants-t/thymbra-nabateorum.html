Thymbra nabateorum

Thymbra nabateorum, commonly known as Nabatean thyme, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their essential oils. The plant features small, pale blue to violet flowers and fragrant, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it well adapted to rocky and sandy soils.

Thymbra nabateorum is valued for its medicinal properties and historical use in herbal remedies.
