Triticum mauritanicum

Triticum mauritanicum, commonly known as Moroccan wheat, is a wild wheat species native to North Africa.

It is a annual grass that typically grows in dry, arid environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous florets. Its seeds are small and hardy, adapted to survive in harsh climates.

While not widely cultivated, it has been studied for its potential in sustainable agriculture and genetic research.
