Trichopteryx stipoides

Trichopteryx stipoides, commonly known as the slender tamarisk, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, characterized by its small, needle-like leaves and clusters of pale yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to drought conditions, with deep root systems that allow it to survive in sandy or rocky soils.
