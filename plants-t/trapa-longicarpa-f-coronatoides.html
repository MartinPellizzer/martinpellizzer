Trapa longicarpa f. coronatoides

Trapa longicarpa f. coronatoides, commonly known as the crowned water chestnut, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its distinctive, floating, spiny fruits that resemble small, round nuts.

It is native to parts of Asia, including China and India, and thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments. The plant features broad, arrowhead-shaped leaves that are submerged or floating, aiding in photosynthesis. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically greenish-white in color, and it produces a large number of seeds.

This species is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking fruit structures and adaptability to wetland habitats.
