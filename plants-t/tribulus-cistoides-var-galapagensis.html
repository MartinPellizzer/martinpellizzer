Tribulus cistoides var. galapagensis

Tribulus cistoides var. galapagensis, commonly known as the Galápagos Tribulus, is a flowering plant native to the Galápagos Islands.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and rocky habitats, adapting to the arid conditions of its environment. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing several seeds. Its leaves are usually small, hairy, and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems.

This species plays a role in the local ecosystem by providing food and shelter for various insects and small animals.
