Terminalia avicapitis

Terminalia avicapitis, commonly known as the red-barked tree, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive red, flaky bark that peels off in thin layers. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by small, round fruits containing single seeds. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a glossy green upper surface.

Terminalia avicapitis is often valued for its ornamental appearance and has traditional uses in local medicine.
