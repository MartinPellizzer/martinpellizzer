Trisetaria fusca

Trisetaria fusca, commonly known as the slender sandwort, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Montiaceae.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its small, succulent leaves and clusters of tiny, white or pale yellow flowers. Native to arid and semi-arid regions, it thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or inland desert environments. The plant's structure is adapted to conserve water, with a compact form and reduced leaf surface area.

Its flowers bloom in early spring, contributing to the ecological diversity of its native habitats.
