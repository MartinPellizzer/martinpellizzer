Tylophora cordata

Tylophora cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf tylophora, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine. Its stems are often used in herbal remedies for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in forested areas.
