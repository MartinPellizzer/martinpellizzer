Tulipa schrenkii

Tulipa schrenkii, commonly known as the Schrenk's tulip, is a perennial bulbous plant native to Central Asia.

It produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and a robust bulb that stores nutrients for the next growing season. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Tulipa schrenkii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in botanical collections.
