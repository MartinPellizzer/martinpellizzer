Tamara hemisphaerica

Tamara hemisphaerica, commonly known as the globular milkweed, is a unique plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its spherical, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate and oval-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Tamara hemisphaerica is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and resilience.
