Tournefortia argentea

Tournefortia argentea, commonly known as silverleaf tournefortia, is a shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the dry season. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, with a leathery texture.

Tournefortia argentea is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
