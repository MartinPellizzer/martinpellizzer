Tridens carolinianus

Tridens carolinianus, commonly known as Carolina pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to North America.

It has long, thin leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically found underwater. The plant thrives in freshwater environments such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams. Its rhizomes allow it to spread vegetatively, forming dense underwater stands.

Tridens carolinianus plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
