Tambourissa mandrarensis

Tambourissa mandrarensis, commonly known as the Madagascar tambourissa, is a climbing plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its slender stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white in color. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, which are often dispersed by wind.
