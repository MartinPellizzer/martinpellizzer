Tirucalia verruculosa

Tirucalia verruculosa, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and distinctive, woody pods that contain several seeds. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by the characteristic pod-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or along forest edges.

The plant has traditional medicinal uses and is also valued for its ornamental qualities.
