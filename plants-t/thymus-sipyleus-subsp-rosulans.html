Thymus sipyleus subsp. rosulans

Thymus sipyleus subsp.

rosulans, commonly known as the Rosulate Thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and aromatic foliage, which contains essential oils responsible for its distinctive fragrance.

The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and useful in traditional herbal medicine.
