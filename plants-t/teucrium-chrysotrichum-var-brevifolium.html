Teucrium chrysotrichum var. brevifolium

Teucrium chrysotrichum var. brevifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its aromatic properties and ornamental value make it a popular choice in gardens and herbal medicine.
