Thesium ramosissimum

Thesium ramosissimum, commonly known as the hairy thesium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, often in dry environments. The plant features wiry, branched stems covered in fine, hairy structures, giving it its common name. Its small, white or pale yellow flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, fleshy fruits.

Thesium ramosissimum is known for its adaptability to arid conditions and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
