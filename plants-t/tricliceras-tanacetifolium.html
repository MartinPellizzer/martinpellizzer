Tricliceras tanacetifolium

Tricliceras tanacetifolium, commonly known as the golden ragwort, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has a taproot system and produces achenes as its fruit type.

Tricliceras tanacetifolium is often found in temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia.
