Tournefortia arborea

Tournefortia arborea, commonly known as the tropical hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its simple, lance-shaped leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers. The flowers are typically bright yellow or orange, with a prominent central stamen that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical climates.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
