Tapeinosperma sessilifolium var. sessilifolium

Tapeinosperma sessilifolium var. sessilifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved kangaroo apple, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are sessile, meaning they lack a petiole and attach directly to the stem. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible when ripe. Its botanical features make it well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

It is often found in rocky soils and is a component of the fynbos vegetation in its native range.
