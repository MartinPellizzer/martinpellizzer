Thalia latifolia

Thalia latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf thalia, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of showy, pink to purple flowers. The plant thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Thalia latifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and its role in wetland ecosystems.
