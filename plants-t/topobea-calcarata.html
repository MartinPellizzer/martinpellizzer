Topobea calcarata

Topobea calcarata, commonly known as the sandhill geranium, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in well-drained sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. It has a compact growth habit and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Topobea calcarata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
