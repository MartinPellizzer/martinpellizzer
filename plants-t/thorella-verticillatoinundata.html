Thorella verticillatoinundata

Thorella verticillatoinundata, commonly known as the spiral-leaved thorellia, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its whorled arrangement of leaves, which give it a distinctive spiral appearance. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions with high humidity.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern in its native range.
