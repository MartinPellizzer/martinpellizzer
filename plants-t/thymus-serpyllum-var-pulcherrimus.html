Thymus serpyllum var. pulcherrimus

Thymus serpyllum var. pulcherrimus, commonly known as the pink thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features slender, creeping stems covered in small, pale green leaves. The plant produces dense clusters of pink to purple flowers, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic foliage and showy flowers make it a popular choice for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
