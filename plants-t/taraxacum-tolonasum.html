Taraxacum tolonasum

Taraxacum tolonasum, commonly known as the Tolonasum dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces a yellow flower head that develops into a white, fluffy seed sphere, typical of the dandelion genus. It is native to Central Asia and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

Taraxacum tolonasum is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
