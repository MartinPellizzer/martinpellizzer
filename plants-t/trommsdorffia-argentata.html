Trommsdorffia argentata

Trommsdorffia argentata, commonly known as the silver leaf plant, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its silvery-white, velvety foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Trommsdorffia argentata thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements make it a desirable ornamental plant.
