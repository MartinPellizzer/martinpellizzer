Triodia mutica

Triodia mutica, commonly known as the spinifex grass, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features wiry, spiky leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in sandy soils.

Triodia mutica plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
