Trymalium majoranifolium

Trymalium majoranifolium, commonly known as the lemon verbena plant, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that emit a strong lemon scent when crushed. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in terminal spikes. Its botanical features include opposite leaf arrangement and a woody stem with a rough texture.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental and aromatic gardening.
