Tricalysia auriculata

Tricalysia auriculata, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of yellow flowers that resemble small showers, hence its common name. The plant has simple, opposite, and elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green. It produces small, spherical fruits that contain one or two seeds.

Tricalysia auriculata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
