Truellum breviochreatum

Truellum breviochreatum, commonly known as the short-stemmed bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It features slender, erect stems that are often branched and covered with fine hairs. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, blooming during the summer months. Its leaves are lanceolate and arranged alternately along the stem, often with a slightly hairy surface.

Truellum breviochreatum is adapted to cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.
