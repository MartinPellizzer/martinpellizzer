Thymus longicaulis var. antalyanus

Thymus longicaulis var. antalyanus, commonly known as Turkish thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, aromatic leaves and flowers that are typically pink to purple in color. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes. Its essential oils, rich in thymol and carvacrol, contribute to its use in herbal medicine and cooking.

This variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to form dense, low-growing mats.
