Thymus sokolovii

Thymus sokolovii, commonly known as the Sokolov's thyme, is a perennial herb native to the mountains of the Balkans.

It is characterized by its aromatic, small, gray-green leaves and clusters of pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry, sunny habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its essential oils, rich in thymol and carvacrol, contribute to its medicinal and aromatic properties.

Thymus sokolovii is valued in both horticulture and traditional medicine for its fragrance and potential health benefits.
