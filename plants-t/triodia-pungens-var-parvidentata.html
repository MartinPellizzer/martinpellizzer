Triodia pungens var. parvidentata

Triodia pungens var. parvidentata, commonly known as the slender spinifex, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, tussock-forming habit and its dense clusters of sharp, needle-like leaves. The plant is well adapted to harsh environmental conditions, including high temperatures and low rainfall. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are typically borne in spike-like inflorescences.

Triodia pungens var. parvidentata plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife in its native range.
