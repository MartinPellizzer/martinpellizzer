Thespesia thespesioides

Thespesia thespesioides, commonly known as the Hawaiian hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Pacific, particularly Hawaii.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, with broad, palmate leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The flowers are typically pink to red in color and bloom during the summer months. It produces woody capsules containing numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is well adapted to coastal and dry environments, showcasing its resilience to harsh tropical conditions.
