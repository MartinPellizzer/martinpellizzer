Terminalia chebula var. tomentella

Terminalia chebula var. tomentella, commonly known as chebulic myrobalan, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and elliptical leaves with a hairy underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by dark brown, berry-like fruits that are rich in tannins.

It is widely cultivated for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional Ayurvedic medicine for its astringent and antioxidant effects.
