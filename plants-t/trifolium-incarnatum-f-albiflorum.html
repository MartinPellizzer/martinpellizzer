Trifolium incarnatum f. albiflorum

Trifolium incarnatum f. albiflorum, commonly known as the white-flowered crimson clover, is a botanical variant of the common crimson clover.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers, which contrast with the typical red coloration of the species. This plant belongs to the Fabaceae family and is native to Europe and parts of Asia. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in soil nitrogen fixation.
