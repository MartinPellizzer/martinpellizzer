Terminalia citrina var. malayana

Terminalia citrina var. malayana, commonly known as the yellow mangosteen, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, segmented fruits that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The fruit is valued for its sweet, aromatic pulp and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
