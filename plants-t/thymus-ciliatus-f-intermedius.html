Thymus ciliatus f. intermedius

Thymus ciliatus f. intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate hairy thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, hairy stems and narrow, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

This variety is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its compact growth habit and aromatic properties.
