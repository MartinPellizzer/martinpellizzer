Trichosalpinx operculata

Trichosalpinx operculata, commonly known as the hooded orchid, is a tropical orchid species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its unique, hood-like structure formed by the fusion of the sepals and petals, which encloses the labellum. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and often have a distinctive coloration pattern.

Trichosalpinx operculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
