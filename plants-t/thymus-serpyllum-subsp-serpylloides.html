Thymus serpyllum subsp. serpylloides

Thymus serpyllum subsp.

serpylloides, commonly known as narrow-leaved thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, pink to white flowers in clusters, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its essential oils contain thymol and carvacrol, contributing to its medicinal and culinary uses.
