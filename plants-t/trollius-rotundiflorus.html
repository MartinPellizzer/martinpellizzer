Trollius rotundiflorus

Trollius rotundiflorus, commonly known as the globe flower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its striking, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has round, palmate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or loamy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Trollius rotundiflorus is a popular garden plant due to its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
