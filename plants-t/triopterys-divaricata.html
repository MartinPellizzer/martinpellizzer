Triopterys divaricata

Triopterys divaricata, commonly known as the Variegated Sycamore, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale green, followed by fleshy, berry-like fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
