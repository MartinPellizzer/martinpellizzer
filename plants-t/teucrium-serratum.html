Teucrium serratum

Teucrium serratum, commonly known as scarlet germander, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are serrated and often have a minty scent, contributing to its use in traditional herbal remedies.

Teucrium serratum thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and along stream banks.
