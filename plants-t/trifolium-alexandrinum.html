Trifolium alexandrinum

Trifolium alexandrinum, commonly known as Alexandrian clover, is a species of the legume family Fabaceae.

It is a low-growing perennial plant characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in racemes. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Alexandrian clover is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
