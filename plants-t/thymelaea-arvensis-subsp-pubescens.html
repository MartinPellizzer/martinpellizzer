Thymelaea arvensis subsp. pubescens

Thymelaea arvensis subsp.

pubescens, commonly known as the hairy thymelaea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It belongs to the family Thymelaeaceae and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant has a creeping growth habit with hairy stems and leaves, which contribute to its common name. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and taxonomic studies.
