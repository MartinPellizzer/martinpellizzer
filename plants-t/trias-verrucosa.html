Trias verrucosa

Trias verrucosa, commonly known as the warty trias, is a unique plant characterized by its distinctive warty growths on its stems and leaves.

It belongs to the family Triasaceae and is native to specific regions in South America. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories. Its leaves are broad and have a velvety texture, while its flowers are small and clustered.

Trias verrucosa is notable for its ecological role in supporting local insect populations and its potential medicinal properties.
