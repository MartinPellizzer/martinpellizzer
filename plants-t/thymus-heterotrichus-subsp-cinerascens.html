Thymus heterotrichus subsp. cinerascens

Thymus heterotrichus subsp.

cinerascens, commonly known as the cinerascens thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments.

The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, making it useful in culinary and medicinal applications.

The subspecies is distinguished by its grayish-green foliage and compact growth habit.
