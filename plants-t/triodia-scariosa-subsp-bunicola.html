Triodia scariosa subsp. bunicola

Triodia scariosa subsp.

bunicola, commonly known as the spinifex grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia. It is characterized by its dense, wiry growth habit and the presence of sharp, spiny leaves.

The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in sandy or gravelly soils. It plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.

Its foliage is often used in traditional Aboriginal practices for various purposes.
