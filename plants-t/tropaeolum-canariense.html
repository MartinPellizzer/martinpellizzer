Tropaeolum canariense

Tropaeolum canariense, commonly known as the Canary Islands nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Tropaeolaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, bright-colored flowers that range from yellow to orange. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it produces edible, peppery-tasting seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its unique appearance and edible qualities make it both aesthetically pleasing and useful in culinary applications.
