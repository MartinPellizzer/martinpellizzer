Tetrardisia fruticosa

Tetrardisia fruticosa, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub-like species characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and thrives in warm, humid environments. Its leaves are usually ovate or elliptical, with a glossy surface, and the flowers are borne in terminal inflorescences.

Tetrardisia fruticosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
