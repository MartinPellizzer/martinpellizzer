Teucrium occidentale var. menthifolium

Teucrium occidentale var. menthifolium, commonly known as western teucrium or mintleaf germander, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, arranged in spikes along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
