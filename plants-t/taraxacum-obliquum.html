Taraxacum obliquum

Taraxacum obliquum, commonly known as the bentleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, wavy-edged leaves that curve outward, giving it its common name. The plant produces a single, upright stem that bears a yellow flower head, which develops into a distinctive, spherical seed head with pappus bristles. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in grassy areas and disturbed soils.

Taraxacum obliquum is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
