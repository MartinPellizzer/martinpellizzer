Tournefortia microphylla

Tournefortia microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved tournefortia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils. The plant features small, elliptical leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and clustered in umbels, producing a distinctive, fragrant aroma.

It is often used in traditional medicine and has ornamental value due to its compact size and attractive foliage.
