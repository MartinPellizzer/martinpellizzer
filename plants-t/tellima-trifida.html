Tellima trifida

Tellima trifida, commonly known as the three-lobed bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant has opposite, three-lobed leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its unique floral structure and foliage make it a notable species in botanical studies and ecological habitats.
