Turnera zeasperma

Turnera zeasperma, commonly known as the Mexican moonflower, is a flowering plant native to Central America and northern South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves and its showy, fragrant white flowers that open at night. Its flowers are zygomorphic, with five petals, and it produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
