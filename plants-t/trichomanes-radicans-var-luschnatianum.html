Trichomanes radicans var. luschnatianum

Trichomanes radicans var. luschnatianum, commonly known as the Luschnatian maidenhair fern, is a variant of the maidenhair fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically arching and gracefully drooping. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-draining, humus-rich soil. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, which are arranged in a distinctive pattern.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded garden areas or as a houseplant.
