Tetrorchidium rubrivenium var. fendleri

Tetrorchidium rubrivenium var. fendleri, commonly known as the Fendler's red-veined tetrorchidium, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant red veins contrasting against its green or yellowish leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or reddish in color. It is native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico, thriving in arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
