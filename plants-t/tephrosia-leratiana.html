Tephrosia leratiana

Tephrosia leratiana, commonly known as the African blue pea, is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters, adding vibrant color to its natural habitat. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, woody pods that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Tephrosia leratiana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some African countries.
