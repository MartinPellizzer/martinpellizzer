Thymus jankae var. sedoides

Thymus jankae var. sedoides, commonly known as the Balkan thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It features small, aromatic, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, woody stems and narrow, gray-green leaves that are often used in traditional herbal medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its essential oils contain thymol and carvacrol, which contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.
