Triglochin centrocarpa

Triglochin centrocarpa, commonly known as the slender spikerush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. The plant has cylindrical, grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish flowers in spikes during the summer months. Its root system helps stabilize soil and improve water quality in its habitat.

Triglochin centrocarpa is an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
