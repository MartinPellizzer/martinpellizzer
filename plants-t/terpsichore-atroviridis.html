Terpsichore atroviridis

Terpsichore atroviridis, commonly known as the black-veined orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its dark green, veined leaves and its elegant, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pale purple. Native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, this plant thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding soil.

Terpsichore atroviridis is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and unique floral structure.
