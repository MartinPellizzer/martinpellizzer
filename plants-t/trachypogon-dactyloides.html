Trachypogon dactyloides

Trachypogon dactyloides, commonly known as the slender feathergrass, is a perennial grass native to the United States, particularly in the southeastern region.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically brown or tan in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along roadsides.

Trachypogon dactyloides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant landscaping.
