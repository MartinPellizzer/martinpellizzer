Tradescantia herba-ratti

Tradescantia herba-ratti, commonly known as the snake plant or mother-in-law's tongue, is a perennial herb native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright, sword-shaped leaves that are typically green with distinctive white or silver markings. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or white in color. It is a hardy plant that thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate to bright indirect light.

Tradescantia herba-ratti is popular as a houseplant due to its low maintenance and air-purifying properties.
