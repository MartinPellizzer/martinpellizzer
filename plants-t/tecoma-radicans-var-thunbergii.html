Tecoma radicans var. thunbergii

Tecoma radicans var. thunbergii, commonly known as the yellow bells, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant is a woody vine that can grow up to several meters in length, often climbing or trailing along supports. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
