Trigonotis haackei

Trigonotis haackei, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a climbing plant native to South America.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Due to its rapid growth, it can become invasive in certain environments if not managed properly.
