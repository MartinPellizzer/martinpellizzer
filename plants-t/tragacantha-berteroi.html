Tragacantha berteroi

Tragacantha berteroi, commonly known as the Persian gum or Astragalus berteroi, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to regions of the Middle East and Central Asia, particularly Iran and Afghanistan. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit, compound leaves, and yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It produces a sticky resin known as tragacanth, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a food additive.

This species is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils in arid environments.
