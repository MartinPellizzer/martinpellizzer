Tricarium cochinchinense

Tricarium cochinchinense, commonly known as the Vietnamese starfruit or golden starfruit, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Tricarium cochinchinense is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its culinary uses in traditional dishes.
