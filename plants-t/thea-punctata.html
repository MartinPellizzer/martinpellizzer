Thea punctata

Thea punctata, commonly known as the spotted lily, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, spotted leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas. Its foliage is notable for its intricate patterns of dark green and lighter green markings.

Thea punctata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
