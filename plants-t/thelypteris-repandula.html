Thelypteris repandula

Thelypteris repandula, commonly known as the broad-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Thelypteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, bipinnate fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests and stream banks. Its rhizomes are wiry and often form dense colonies, contributing to its ornamental value.

The fronds are usually dark green and have a feathery appearance, with the underside often displaying a lighter color.
