Trichochloa mexicana

Trichochloa mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican hairgrass, is a perennial grass native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its fine, soft, and hairy foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or near water sources. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are arranged in dense clusters.

Trichochloa mexicana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a ground cover.
