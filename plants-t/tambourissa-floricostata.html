Tambourissa floricostata

Tambourissa floricostata, commonly known as the flowering costate tambourissa, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that bloom in clusters, often in shades of yellow and orange. The plant has a fibrous root system and possesses palmate leaves with a distinctive, wavy edge. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or swampy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it requires specific conditions to flourish.
