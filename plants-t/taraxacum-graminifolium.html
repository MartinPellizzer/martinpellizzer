Taraxacum graminifolium

Taraxacum graminifolium, commonly known as the grass-leaved dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and a yellow flower head that develops into a pappus-equipped seed sphere. Native to parts of Asia, it thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows and disturbed soils. The plant is known for its medicinal properties, with various parts used in traditional remedies.

Its life cycle includes a dormant period during winter, with flowering typically occurring in spring and summer.
