Tragopogon porrifolius subsp. australis

Tragopogon porrifolius subsp.

australis, commonly known as the Australian salsify, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its basal rosette of thick, fleshy leaves and a tall, branching stem that bears yellow flowers.

The plant produces a taproot that is often edible and has a slightly sweet, nutty flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

This subspecies is closely related to other members of the dandelion family, Asteraceae.
