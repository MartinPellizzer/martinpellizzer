Tephrocactus leoncito

Tephrocactus leoncito, commonly known as the golden cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America, particularly in Argentina and Chile.

It is a small, globular cactus with a dense covering of yellow to orange spines, which give it its common name. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking contrast to its spiny exterior. Its succulent stems store water, allowing it to thrive in dry environments with minimal rainfall.

Tephrocactus leoncito is well adapted to harsh desert conditions, making it a resilient and visually appealing plant for arid landscapes.
