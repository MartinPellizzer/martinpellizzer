Trigonotis macrophylla var. macrophylla

Trigonotis macrophylla var. macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaved trigonotis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its foliage is glossy and can reach up to 30 cm in length, making it a striking addition to gardens.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic qualities.
