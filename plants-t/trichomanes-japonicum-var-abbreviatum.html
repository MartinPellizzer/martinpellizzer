Trichomanes japonicum var. abbreviatum

Trichomanes japonicum var. abbreviatum, commonly known as the Japanese maidenhair fern, is a variant of the maidenhair fern family with distinctive, finely divided fronds.

It is native to East Asia and thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest undergrowth. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, with creeping stems that produce new fronds along their length. Its fronds are typically narrow and arching, with a delicate, feathery appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
