Teucrium stocksianum var. gabrieliae

Teucrium stocksianum var. gabrieliae, commonly known as Gabriel's germander, is a low-growing, evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has aromatic, hairy leaves that are often gray-green in appearance, and it thrives in well-drained soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is also noted for its drought tolerance and adaptability to sunny, rocky habitats.
