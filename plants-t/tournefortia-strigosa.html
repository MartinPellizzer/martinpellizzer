Tournefortia strigosa

Tournefortia strigosa, commonly known as the woolly tournefortia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive, rough texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, often followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or sandy environments.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in some horticultural settings.
