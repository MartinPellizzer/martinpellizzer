Thymus serpyllum var. aureus

Thymus serpyllum var. aureus, commonly known as golden thyme, is a low-growing herbaceous perennial native to Europe.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has aromatic, woody stems covered in tiny, fuzzy leaves that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant garden plant.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
