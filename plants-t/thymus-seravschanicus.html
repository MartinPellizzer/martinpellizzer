Thymus seravschanicus

Thymus seravschanicus, commonly known as the Caucasus thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It features compact, mat-forming stems covered in small, aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Due to its medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated for use in herbal remedies.
