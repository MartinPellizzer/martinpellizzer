Tulipa scabriscapa subvar. variopicta

Tulipa scabriscapa subvar. variopicta, commonly known as the variegated sand tulip, is a striking bulbous perennial native to central Asia.

It features slender, grass-like foliage and produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The leaves are often marked with distinctive white or yellow variegation, adding to its ornamental appeal. This variety thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its unique coloration and hardy nature make it a valuable addition to both cultivated and natural landscapes.
