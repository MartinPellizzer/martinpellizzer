Trichocereus conaconensis

Trichocereus conaconensis, commonly known as the Conaconensis cactus, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its tall, spiny stems and is often found growing in rocky or arid environments. The plant produces large, nocturnal flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming during the cooler evening hours. It is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and has a slow growth rate, making it a prized specimen among cactus enthusiasts.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is also of interest to botanists studying desert flora.
