Tragacantha sisyrodytes

Tragacantha sisyrodytes, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is native to regions of the Middle East and parts of Central Asia, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant is characterized by its woody stem and compound leaves, with small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring. It produces a resinous sap, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive.

The plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
