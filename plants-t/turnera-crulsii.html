Turnera crulsii

Turnera crulsii, commonly known as the golden turnera, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Turneraceae and is characterized by its showy yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a waxy surface, while its stems are woody and branched.

Turnera crulsii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to a variety of soil types and light conditions.
