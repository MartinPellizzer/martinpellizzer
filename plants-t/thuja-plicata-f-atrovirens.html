Thuja plicata f. atrovirens

Thuja plicata f. atrovirens, commonly known as the dark green western red cedar, is a cultivar of the western red cedar, characterized by its dense, dark green foliage.

It is a slow-growing evergreen conifer with a pyramidal shape and scale-like leaves that are tightly pressed together. The plant is native to the Pacific Northwest of North America and is valued for its ornamental appeal and resistance to pests and diseases. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for landscaping.

Its foliage provides a lush, dark green contrast to other plants in garden settings.
