Tragopogon tanaiticus

Tragopogon tanaiticus, commonly known as the Turkish thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, leafy stems and bright yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, achene-like seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its spread across various habitats. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Asteraceae family.
