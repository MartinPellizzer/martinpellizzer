Trichosanthes phonsenae

Trichosanthes phonsenae, commonly known as the Phonsene bitter melon, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its hairy stems and large, lobed leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by elongated, green fruits that turn orange when mature. The fruit is typically bitter and is used in traditional medicine and cuisine.

It thrives in warm, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
