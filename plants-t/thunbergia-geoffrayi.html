Thunbergia geoffrayi

Thunbergia geoffrayi, commonly known as the African blue trumpet, is a climbing plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and has a rapid growth rate, making it suitable for trellises or as a ground cover. It thrives in warm, humid conditions and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Thunbergia geoffrayi is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
