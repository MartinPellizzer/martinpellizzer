Thymelaea tinctoria var. calycina

Thymelaea tinctoria var. calycina, commonly known as the yellow thymelaea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 meter, characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in spring and summer. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is notable for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
