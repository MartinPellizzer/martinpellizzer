Telephium oppositifolium

Telephium oppositifolium, commonly known as the red deadnettle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or reddish in color, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
