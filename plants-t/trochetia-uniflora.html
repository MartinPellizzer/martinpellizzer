Trochetia uniflora

Trochetia uniflora, commonly known as the single-flower rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, thriving in sunny, well-drained environments. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pink flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, often covered in a fine layer of white wax.

Trochetia uniflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant.
