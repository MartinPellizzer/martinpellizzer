Trigonostemon aurantiacus

Trigonostemon aurantiacus, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a striking visual display to its canopy. The leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a glossy surface, often with a reddish or purple tinge when young. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain seeds surrounded by a fleshy, orange pulp.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
