Tristachya augusta

Tristachya augusta, commonly known as the golden cupgrass, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and bright yellow inflorescence. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are small and clustered in panicles.

Tristachya augusta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
