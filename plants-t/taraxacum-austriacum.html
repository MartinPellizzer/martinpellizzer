Taraxacum austriacum

Taraxacum austriacum, commonly known as the Austrian dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of pale green, hairy leaves and a yellow flower head that develops into a fluffy seed ball. Native to Central Europe, it thrives in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant is known for its ability to spread via wind-dispersed seeds, contributing to its widespread distribution.

It plays a role in supporting pollinators and is often found in temperate regions.
