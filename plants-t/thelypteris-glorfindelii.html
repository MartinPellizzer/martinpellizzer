Thelypteris glorfindelii

Thelypteris glorfindelii, commonly known as the Elven Fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Thelypteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are often adorned with intricate, lace-like patterns. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce numerous erect, branching shoots.

Thelypteris glorfindelii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
