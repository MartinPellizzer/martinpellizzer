Typha angustifolia var. elata

Typha angustifolia var. elata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cattail, is a wetland plant characterized by its tall, cylindrical inflorescence and narrow, strap-like leaves.

It thrives in shallow freshwater environments such as marshes, ponds, and the edges of lakes. The plant has a robust rhizomatous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion. Its leaves are typically green and can grow up to several feet in length.

The plant is also known for its ability to filter water and support diverse ecosystems.
