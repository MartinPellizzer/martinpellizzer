Tamarix dalmatica

Tamarix dalmatica, commonly known as the Dalmatian tamarisk, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and small, scale-like leaves that are typically covered in a whitish bloom. The plant produces small, pink or reddish flowers in clusters during the summer months. It is adapted to arid and saline environments, making it a hardy species for coastal and semi-arid regions.

Its rapid growth and ability to fix nitrogen make it useful for soil stabilization and reclamation projects.
