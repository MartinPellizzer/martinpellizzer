Taraxacum cordatum

Taraxacum cordatum, commonly known as the heartleaf dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves, which are usually hairy and have a waxy coating. The plant produces a single, upright stem that bears a single flower head composed of yellow ray florets. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

Taraxacum cordatum is a perennial plant that typically blooms from late spring to early summer.
