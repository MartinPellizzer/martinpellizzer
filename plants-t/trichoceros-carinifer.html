Trichoceros carinifer

Trichoceros carinifer, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a species of orchid native to high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, often fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, while its roots are adapted to anchor in crevices.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, Trichoceros carinifer is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
