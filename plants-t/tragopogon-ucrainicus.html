Tragopogon ucrainicus

Tragopogon ucrainicus, commonly known as the Ukrainian thistle, is a flowering plant native to Ukraine and surrounding regions.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a distinctive purple center.

Tragopogon ucrainicus is known for its adaptability and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
