Toxicodendron fulvum

Toxicodendron fulvum, commonly known as the yellow rattle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its yellow, tubular flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped. It is often found in grasslands and meadows, where it plays a role in supporting pollinators.

However, it is not toxic to humans or animals, contrary to its genus name.
