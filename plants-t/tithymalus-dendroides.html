Tithymalus dendroides

Tithymalus dendroides, commonly known as the tree-like tithymalus, is a species of flowering plant native to certain regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and dense, bushy growth habit, resembling a small tree or shrub. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
