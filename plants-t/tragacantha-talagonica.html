Tragacantha talagonica

Tragacantha talagonica, commonly known as the Armenian tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by pods containing seeds.

Its roots are known for producing a viscous gum, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive.
