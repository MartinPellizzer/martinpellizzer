Thrixspermum acuminatissimum subsp. affine

Thrixspermum acuminatissimum subsp.

affine, commonly known as the slender thrixspermum, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its narrow, woody stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is typically found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
