Tanacetum himachalensis

Tanacetum himachalensis, commonly known as the Himachal elecampane, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayan regions of India.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, mountainous areas and has lance-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture. It is valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Tanacetum himachalensis plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by supporting pollinators and contributing to soil stability.
