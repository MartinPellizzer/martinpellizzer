Tragopogon anatolicus

Tragopogon anatolicus, commonly known as the Anatolian thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in the spring, followed by the development of spiny, bristly seed heads.

Tragopogon anatolicus is often found in open fields and along roadsides, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
