Thlaspi natolicum

Thlaspi natolicum, commonly known as the alpine cabbages, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is a small, herbaceous plant typically found in alpine and subalpine regions. The plant has basal rosettes of dark green, fleshy leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its flowers are small, bright yellow, and arranged in a raceme, typical of the mustard family.

Thlaspi natolicum is adapted to cold climates and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations.
