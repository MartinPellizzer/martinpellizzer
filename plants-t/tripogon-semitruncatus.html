Tripogon semitruncatus

Tripogon semitruncatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf grass.

It is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa, characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of study in grassland ecology.
