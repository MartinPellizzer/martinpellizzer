Trixis glaziovii var. aurantiaca

Trixis glaziovii var. aurantiaca, commonly known as the golden trixis, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance.

The golden trixis is also valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
