Tripterospermum speciosum

Tripterospermum speciosum, commonly known as the golden star vine, is a climbing plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, resembling stars, hence its common name. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and emits a faint, sweet fragrance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its rapid growth, it is sometimes used in landscaping to cover trellises or walls.
