Tacca brownii

Tacca brownii, commonly known as the "bat plant" or "devil's ivy," is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and its unique, bat-like flowers that are often covered in a dark, velvety sheath. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its flowers, which bloom in a range of colors from purple to white, are highly ornamental and attract various pollinators.

Tacca brownii is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its exotic appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
