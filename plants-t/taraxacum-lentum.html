Taraxacum lentum

Taraxacum lentum, commonly known as the slender dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and a flowering stem that bears a single, yellow composite flower head. The plant is native to temperate regions of Europe and is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides. It produces a distinctive, wind-dispersed seed head known as a pappus, which aids in its propagation.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
