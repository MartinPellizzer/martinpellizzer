Teucrium procerum

Teucrium procerum, commonly known as tall germander, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small, clustered in whorls, and range in color from pink to violet, blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant is often found in dry, rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
