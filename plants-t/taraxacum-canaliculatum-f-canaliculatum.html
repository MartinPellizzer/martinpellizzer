Taraxacum canaliculatum f. canaliculatum

Taraxacum canaliculatum f. canaliculatum, commonly known as the narrow-ribbed dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of pale green, hairy leaves with narrow, ribbed margins. The plant produces a single, hollow, yellow-orange flower head that develops into a white, spherical seed sphere with a pappus for wind dispersal. It is native to regions of East Asia, including China and Korea, and thrives in moist, disturbed soils.

This subspecies is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along streambanks, showcasing its adaptability to various habitats.
