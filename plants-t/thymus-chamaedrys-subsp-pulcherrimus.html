Thymus chamaedrys subsp. pulcherrimus

Thymus chamaedrys subsp.

pulcherrimus, commonly known as the pink thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe. It features small, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant has aromatic, gray-green foliage that is often used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice in horticulture.
