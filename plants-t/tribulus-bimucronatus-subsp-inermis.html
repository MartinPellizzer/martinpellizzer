Tribulus bimucronatus subsp. inermis

Tribulus bimucronatus subsp.

inermis, commonly known as the slender caltrop, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its spiny, prostrate stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant produces distinctive fruits that are capsule-like and contain four sharp, spiny seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas.
