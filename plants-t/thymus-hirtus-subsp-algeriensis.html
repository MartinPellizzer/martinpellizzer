Thymus hirtus subsp. algeriensis

Thymus hirtus subsp.

algeriensis, commonly known as Algerian thyme, is a perennial herb native to North Africa. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and aromatic, narrow leaves.

The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers in early summer, which attract pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or arid environments.

This subspecies is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, similar to other thyme species.
