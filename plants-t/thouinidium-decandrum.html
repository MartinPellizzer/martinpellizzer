Thouinidium decandrum

Thouinidium decandrum, commonly known as the five-lobed thornbush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and small, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Thouinidium decandrum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
