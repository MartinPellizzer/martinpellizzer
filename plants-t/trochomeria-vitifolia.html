Trochomeria vitifolia

Trochomeria vitifolia, commonly known as the grapeleaf tree, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often mistaken for grape leaves due to their shape and size. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its woody structure and evergreen foliage make it suitable for ornamental gardening in warm climates.

The species is also valued for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in full sun.
