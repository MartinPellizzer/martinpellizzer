Trollius ranunculinus f. tenuisectus

Trollius ranunculinus f. tenuisectus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved globeflower, is a botanical variant of the globeflower species.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves that give it a delicate appearance. The plant produces showy, yellow, globe-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often grown in gardens with specific horticultural conditions.
