Triumfetta dubia var. tomentosa

Triumfetta dubia var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly triumfetta, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a small shrub characterized by its densely hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are often borne on short pedicels. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape and may have a slightly waxy texture.

The species is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in semi-arid conditions.
