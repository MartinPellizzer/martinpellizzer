Tanacetum kittaryanum subsp. kittaryanum

Tanacetum kittaryanum subsp.

kittaryanum, commonly known as the Armenian tansy, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous habitats, preferring well-drained soils. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in alpine or subalpine zones.

Its aromatic foliage and showy blooms make it a notable species in its native environment.
