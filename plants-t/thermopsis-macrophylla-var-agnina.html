Thermopsis macrophylla var. agnina

Thermopsis macrophylla var. agnina, commonly known as the silverleaf thermopsis, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray, hairy foliage and yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are alternate and lance-shaped, with a distinct glandular texture.

The species is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
