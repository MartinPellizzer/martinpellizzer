Terminalia alata

Terminalia alata, commonly known as the Indian almond tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, winged fruits that resemble small almonds, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant has compound leaves with opposite arrangement and produces small, yellowish flowers. Its bark and leaves contain tannins and are used in herbal remedies for their astringent properties.

Terminalia alata thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.
