Thalictrum hintonii

Thalictrum hintonii, commonly known as Hinton's meadow rue, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and meadows, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant features delicate, fern-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers in early to mid-summer. Its flowers are primarily pollinated by insects, and the plant is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Thalictrum hintonii is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local pollinator populations.
