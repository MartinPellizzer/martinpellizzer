Tachigali versicolor

Tachigali versicolor, commonly known as the variable tachigali, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in moist, lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental qualities.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
