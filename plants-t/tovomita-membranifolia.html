Tovomita membranifolia

Tovomita membranifolia, commonly known as the membranous-leaved tovomita, is a tropical plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, membranous leaves that are typically elongated and have a waxy surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique foliage, it is occasionally cultivated for ornamental purposes in specialized gardens.
