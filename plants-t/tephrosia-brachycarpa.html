Tephrosia brachycarpa

Tephrosia brachycarpa, commonly known as the narrow-fruited tephrosia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows to a height of 1-2 meters and produces small, yellow flowers that attract pollinators. Its seeds are enclosed in narrow, cylindrical pods, which is a distinguishing feature of the species.

Tephrosia brachycarpa is also noted for its potential use in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
