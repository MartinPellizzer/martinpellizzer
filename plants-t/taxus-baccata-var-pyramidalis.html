Taxus baccata var. pyramidalis

Taxus baccata var. pyramidalis, commonly known as the pyramidal yew, is a cultivar of the European yew.

It is characterized by its narrow, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It is evergreen, with a slow growth rate and a long lifespan.

This variety is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and structural form.
