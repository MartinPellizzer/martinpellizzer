Tephrosia nubica var. abyssinica

Tephrosia nubica var. abyssinica, commonly known as Ethiopian tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, which bloom during the rainy season. It is adapted to poor soils and can thrive in harsh environmental conditions, making it an important component of local ecosystems.

Additionally, it has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
