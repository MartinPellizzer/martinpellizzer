Tilia × paradensis

Tilia × paradensis, commonly known as the European lime or small-leaf lime, is a hybrid deciduous tree belonging to the Tiliaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are typically 5–10 cm in diameter, with a serrated margin and a pale green underside. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which bloom in early summer and are followed by small, round fruits. It is native to central and eastern Europe and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
