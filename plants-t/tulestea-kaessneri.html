Tulestea kaessneri

Tulestea kaessneri, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves and showy, velvety flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish. It is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.

Tulestea kaessneri is also known for its ability to produce offsets, which can be propagated to create new plants.
