Trifolium argutum

Trifolium argutum, commonly known as the sharp-leaved clover, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in spring, typically in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woods, meadows, and along roadsides.

Trifolium argutum is a member of the legume family and has the ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a beneficial plant for soil enrichment.
