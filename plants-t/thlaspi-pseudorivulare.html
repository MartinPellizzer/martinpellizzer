Thlaspi pseudorivulare

Thlaspi pseudorivulare, commonly known as the narrowleaf pennycress, is a flowering plant in the family Brassicaceae.

It is characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and pinnately lobed leaves. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in moist, disturbed soils such as those found in meadows and along stream banks. The plant produces small, rounded fruits called siliques, which contain several seeds.

It is often found in temperate climates and is adapted to a variety of soil types.
