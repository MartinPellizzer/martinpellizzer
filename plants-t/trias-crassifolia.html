Trias crassifolia

Trias crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved trias, is a unique plant species characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments.

It belongs to the family Triaceae and is native to specific regions in South America, particularly in the Andes. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white, which bloom during the wet season. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to anchor firmly in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its adaptability, Trias crassifolia is sometimes cultivated in xerophytic gardens for its ornamental value and resilience.
