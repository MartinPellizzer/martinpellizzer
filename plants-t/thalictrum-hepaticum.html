Thalictrum hepaticum

Thalictrum hepaticum, commonly known as liverroot, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves that are often divided into narrow segments, giving it a lacy appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Thalictrum hepaticum is valued in gardens for its ornamental foliage and its ability to attract pollinators.
