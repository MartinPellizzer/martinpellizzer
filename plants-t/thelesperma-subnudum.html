Thelesperma subnudum

Thelesperma subnudum, commonly known as the plains milkvetch, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in sandy or rocky soils. Its small, yellow flowers bloom in late spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Thelesperma subnudum is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a resilient species in harsh desert climates.
