Taraxacum hyoseridifolium

Taraxacum hyoseridifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in early spring, followed by the characteristic seed-filled puffballs.

It is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a common weed in agricultural and urban areas.
