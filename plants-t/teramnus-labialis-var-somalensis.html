Teramnus labialis var. somalensis

Teramnus labialis var. somalensis, commonly known as the Somali milkweed, is a variant of the species native to arid and semi-arid regions of East Africa.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its succulent, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. It is adapted to drought conditions, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

The milky sap, which is characteristic of the genus, is also present in this variant and may have medicinal or defensive properties.
