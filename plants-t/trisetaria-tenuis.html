Trisetaria tenuis

Trisetaria tenuis, commonly known as the slender spike-rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Trisetaria tenuis is an important component of wetland ecosystems, contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
