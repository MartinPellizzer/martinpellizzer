Trisetum flavescens proles burnoufii

Trisetum flavescens proles burnoufii, commonly known as the Burnouf's false brome, is a grass species native to certain regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces pale yellow to brownish inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, moist habitats such as meadows and riverbanks. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue when fresh.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
