Trichilia pleeana

Trichilia pleeana, commonly known as African sandalwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae, which includes species known for their aromatic wood. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically yellow or greenish-yellow, and it produces woody capsules as fruit.

The tree is valued for its fragrant timber, which is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
