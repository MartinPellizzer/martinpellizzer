Tinus cubana

Tinus cubana, commonly known as the Cuban tinus, is a shrub native to the Caribbean region.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruits are small, capsule-like structures that contain several seeds.

Tinus cubana is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
