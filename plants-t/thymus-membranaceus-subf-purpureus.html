Thymus membranaceus subf. purpureus

Thymus membranaceus subf.

purpureus, commonly known as purple thyme, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, fragrant leaves.

The plant produces pink to purple flowers in late spring to early summer, which are borne on slender stems. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, making it valuable in both culinary and medicinal applications.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
