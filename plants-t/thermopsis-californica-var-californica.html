Thermopsis californica var. californica

Thermopsis californica var. californica, commonly known as California goldenroot, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Its botanical features make it an attractive addition to native gardens and ecological restoration projects.
