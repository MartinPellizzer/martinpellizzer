Trifolium ochroleucon subsp. roseum

Trifolium ochroleucon subsp.

roseum, commonly known as the pink clover, is a flowering plant species belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets, and its small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant is native to parts of Europe and is often found in grassy areas, meadows, and along roadsides. It is a perennial herb that thrives in well-drained soils and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, benefiting the surrounding soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
