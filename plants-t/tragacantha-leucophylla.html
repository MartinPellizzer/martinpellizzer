Tragacantha leucophylla

Tragacantha leucophylla, commonly known as white-flowered tragacanth, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its woody stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pale yellow, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant once established.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
