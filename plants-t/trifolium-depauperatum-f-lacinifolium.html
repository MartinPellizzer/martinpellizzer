Trifolium depauperatum f. lacinifolium

Trifolium depauperatum f. lacinifolium, commonly known as the lacy clover, is a rare and distinctive variant of the slender clover.

It is characterized by its finely dissected, lacy leaves that give it a delicate appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its small, pea-like flowers are usually white or pale pink, appearing in clusters.

Due to its rarity and unique morphology, it is of particular interest to botanists and conservationists.
