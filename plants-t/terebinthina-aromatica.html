Terebinthina aromatica

Terebinthina aromatica, commonly known as the aromatic terebinth, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and aromatic when crushed. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, round berries that are edible but often used in traditional herbal remedies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for coastal and rocky garden environments.

Its aromatic properties have made it valuable in both medicinal and culinary applications throughout history.
