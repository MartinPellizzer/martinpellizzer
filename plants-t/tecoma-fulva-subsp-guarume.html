Tecoma fulva subsp. guarume

Tecoma fulva subsp.

guarume, commonly known as the yellow trumpetbush, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America. It is characterized by its showy, yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a velvety texture.

It prefers well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.
