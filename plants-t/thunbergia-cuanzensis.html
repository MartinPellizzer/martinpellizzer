Thunbergia cuanzensis

Thunbergia cuanzensis, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a climbing plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and striking, tubular flowers that open at night. The flowers are typically white or pale blue with a distinctive purple or violet throat, attracting nocturnal pollinators like moths. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
