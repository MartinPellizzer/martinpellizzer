Triaspis macropteron var. speciosa

Triaspis macropteron var. speciosa, commonly known as the showy triaspis, is a striking member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy bracts that often display vibrant colors, typically in shades of red, orange, or yellow. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in a cyathium, a unique structure typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its leaves are usually alternate and have a distinctive, sometimes lobed shape.

Native to certain regions of Southeast Asia, it thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
