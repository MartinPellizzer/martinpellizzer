Triorchis vernalis

Triorchis vernalis, commonly known as the spring orchid, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, greenish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, and it has a short, fibrous root system.

Triorchis vernalis is a rare and endangered species, often found in specific microhabitats that support its unique ecological requirements.
