Theophrasta angustifolia

Theophrasta angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf theophrastus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Theophrasta angustifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
