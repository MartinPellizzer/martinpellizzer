Thymus marschallianus var. calvifrons

Thymus marschallianus var. calvifrons, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features aromatic, gray-green foliage with small, oval-shaped leaves that are densely packed along its stems. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and fragrant leaves make it a popular choice for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
