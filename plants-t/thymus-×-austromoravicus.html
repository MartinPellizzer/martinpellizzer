Thymus × austromoravicus

Thymus × austromoravicus, commonly known as the Austro-Moravian thyme, is a hybrid perennial herb native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and low-maintenance garden plant.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental value make it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
