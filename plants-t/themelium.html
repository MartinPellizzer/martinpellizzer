Themelium

Themelium, commonly known as the moonflower, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, white, fragrant blooms that open at night and close by dawn. The plant belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is known for its rapid growth and unique photoperiodic behavior. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

The flowers are pollinated by nocturnal insects such as moths, making it an important species in ecological studies.
