Triumfetta plumigera

Triumfetta plumigera, commonly known as the silky triumfetta, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs, giving them a soft, velvety texture. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when immature and turn red when mature.

It is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
