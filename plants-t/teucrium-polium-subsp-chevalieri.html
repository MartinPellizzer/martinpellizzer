Teucrium polium subsp. chevalieri

Teucrium polium subsp.

chevalieri, commonly known as Corsican germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun.

The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. It is valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and drought-tolerant species well adapted to Mediterranean climates.
