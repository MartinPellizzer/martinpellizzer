Teucrium chamaedrys subsp. maroccanum

Teucrium chamaedrys subsp.

maroccanum, commonly known as Moroccan wall germander, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Morocco. It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are gray-green in color and covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
