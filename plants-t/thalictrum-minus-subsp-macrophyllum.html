Thalictrum minus subsp. macrophyllum

Thalictrum minus subsp.

macrophyllum, commonly known as the broad-leaved meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces showy, upright inflorescences that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ecological benefits to pollinators.
