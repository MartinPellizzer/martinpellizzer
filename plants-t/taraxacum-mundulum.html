Taraxacum mundulum

Taraxacum mundulum, commonly known as the moonlight dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant features a rosette of basal, spoon-shaped leaves that are hairy and covered in white pubescence. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in early spring, followed by the characteristic seed heads that release lightweight seeds on the wind.

Taraxacum mundulum is adapted to thrive in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.
