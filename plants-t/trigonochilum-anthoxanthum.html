Trigonochilum anthoxanthum

Trigonochilum anthoxanthum, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are elongated and often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

The flowers are clustered in inflorescences and are known for their ornamental value in horticulture.
