Torenia glabra

Torenia glabra, commonly known as the common torenia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its tubular, violet-blue flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soil. Torenia glabra is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant flowers and relatively low maintenance requirements.

It is also used in traditional medicine in some regions for its potential health benefits.
