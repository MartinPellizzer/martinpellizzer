Thelypodium elegans

Thelypodium elegans, commonly known as the elegant thelypodium, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant features slender, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. Its flowers are typically small and yellow, arranged in compact clusters at the ends of the stems.

Thelypodium elegans is often found in moist, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
