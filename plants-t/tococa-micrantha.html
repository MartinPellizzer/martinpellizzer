Tococa micrantha

Tococa micrantha, commonly known as the Andean coffee plant, is a species native to the cloud forests of the Andes.

It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves and small, white flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that contain seeds, which are used for propagation. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is adapted to high-altitude conditions.

Due to its unique habitat and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
