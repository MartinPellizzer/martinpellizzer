Thapsus phlomoides

Thapsus phlomoides, commonly known as woolly thapsus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and clusters of small, pale blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands. It has a sprawling growth habit and produces capsules as its fruiting structure.

Thapsus phlomoides is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
