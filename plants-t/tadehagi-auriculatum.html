Tadehagi auriculatum

Tadehagi auriculatum, commonly known as the auriculate tadehagi, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which have a distinctive auriculate (ear-shaped) base on the leaflets. The plant produces small, pale yellow to greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or rocky areas.

Tadehagi auriculatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
