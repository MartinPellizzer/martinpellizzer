Taraxacum janalamii

Taraxacum janalamii, commonly known as the Janalamii dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Central Asia and is characterized by its basal rosette of elongated, hairy leaves. The plant produces a single, elongated flower head that transitions into a spherical, wind-dispersed seed ball. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Taraxacum janalamii is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its potential medicinal properties.
