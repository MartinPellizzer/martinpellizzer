Trichosalpinx macphersonii

Trichosalpinx macphersonii, commonly known as the MacPherson's orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

Native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, this plant is characterized by its vibrant, spurred flowers that often display a mix of red, orange, and yellow hues. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically growing as an epiphyte on trees or rocks. The plant features long, slender roots and lance-shaped leaves that help it absorb moisture from the air.

Its unique floral structure and vivid colors make it a popular choice for both horticultural enthusiasts and botanical studies.
