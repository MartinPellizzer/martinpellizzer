Tragacantha helleri

Tragacantha helleri, commonly known as Heller's tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30-60 centimeters. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters during the spring. Its scientific name refers to the genus Tragacantha, which is characterized by its sap, known as tragacanth, used in various traditional applications.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats.
