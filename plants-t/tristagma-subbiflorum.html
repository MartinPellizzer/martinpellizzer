Tristagma subbiflorum

Tristagma subbiflorum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tristagma, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that typically bloom in clusters, often in shades of yellow or orange. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Tristagma subbiflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
