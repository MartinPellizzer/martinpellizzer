Tectaria tripinnata

Tectaria tripinnata, commonly known as the fan-leaf tectaria, is a species of fern belonging to the family Tectariaceae.

It is characterized by its large, bipinnate fronds that can reach lengths of up to 1 meter. The fronds are typically divided into multiple levels of pinnae, giving the plant a feathery appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
