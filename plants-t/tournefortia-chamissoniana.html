Tournefortia chamissoniana

Tournefortia chamissoniana, commonly known as the beach hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to red. The plant typically grows in sandy or saline soils, often along shorelines and dunes. Its leaves are usually alternate, simple, and have a rough or hairy texture.
