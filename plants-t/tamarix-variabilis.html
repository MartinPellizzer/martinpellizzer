Tamarix variabilis

Tamarix variabilis, commonly known as the desert willow, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its woody stems, small pink or reddish flowers, and feathery leaves that reduce water loss. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it well-suited for xeriscaping. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological diversity.

Tamarix variabilis is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and adaptability to harsh environments.
