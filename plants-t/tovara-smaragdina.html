Tovara smaragdina

Tovara smaragdina, commonly known as the Emerald Tovara, is a rare and striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its vibrant green foliage and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant thrives in cool, humid conditions and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its leaves are thick and waxy, which helps it retain moisture in its arid environment.

Tovara smaragdina is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its medicinal properties in traditional Andean medicine.
