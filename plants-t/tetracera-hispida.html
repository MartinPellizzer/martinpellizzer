Tetracera hispida

Tetracera hispida, commonly known as the rough tetracera, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain several seeds.

Tetracera hispida is often found in forested areas and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
