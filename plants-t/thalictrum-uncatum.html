Thalictrum uncatum

Thalictrum uncatum, commonly known as the slender meadow-rue, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and features delicate, fern-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers in early spring. Its stems are usually unbranched and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters.

Thalictrum uncatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
