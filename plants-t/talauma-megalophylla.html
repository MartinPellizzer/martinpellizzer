Talauma megalophylla

Talauma megalophylla, commonly known as the giant fan palm, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter.

Native to Southeast Asia, it thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests. The plant has a short, stocky trunk with a smooth, grayish-brown bark. Its leaves are composed of numerous radiating leaflets, giving it a distinctive and ornamental appearance.

Talauma megalophylla is valued not only for its aesthetic appeal but also for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
