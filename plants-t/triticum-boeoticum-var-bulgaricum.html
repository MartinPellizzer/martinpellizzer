Triticum boeoticum var. bulgaricum

Triticum boeoticum var. bulgaricum, commonly known as the Bulgarian wild wheat, is a grass species native to the Balkan region.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as fields and roadsides. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture, and its seeds are small and wind-dispersed.

This variety is of interest to botanists and conservationists due to its ecological significance and potential for agricultural use.
