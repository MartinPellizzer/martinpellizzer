Taraxacum skalinskanum

Taraxacum skalinskanum, commonly known as the Skalinsky dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, grassy habitats and has a taproot system that allows it to survive in arid conditions. Its bright yellow flower heads consist of numerous small florets surrounded by a pappus of bristles, aiding in wind dispersal of its seeds.

The species is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its potential use in traditional medicine.
