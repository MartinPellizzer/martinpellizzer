Tamarix articulata

Tamarix articulata, commonly known as the saltbush or desert willow, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia, Africa, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, scale-like leaves that reduce water loss in dry environments. The plant produces pink to reddish flowers in clusters, which bloom in spring and attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Tamarix articulata is adapted to saline soils and can thrive in areas with low rainfall.

Its rapid growth and ability to stabilize soils make it useful for erosion control in desert landscapes.
