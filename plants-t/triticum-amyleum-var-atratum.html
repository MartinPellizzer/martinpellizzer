Triticum amyleum var. atratum

Triticum amyleum var. atratum, commonly known as the black wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its dark green, slender leaves and produces small, spikelet-like inflorescences with dark-colored bracts. The plant is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in disturbed soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade. Its botanical features include fibrous roots, narrow linear leaves, and a compact growth habit.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
