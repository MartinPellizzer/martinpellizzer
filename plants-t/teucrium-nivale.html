Teucrium nivale

Teucrium nivale, commonly known as snow germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and stream banks. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered blue or purple flowers from late spring to early summer. It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly minty scent and its ability to thrive in temperate climates.

Teucrium nivale is also noted for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in herbal remedies for digestive and respiratory ailments.
