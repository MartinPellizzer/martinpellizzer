Triumfetta vincentina

Triumfetta vincentina, commonly known as the Vincent's glorybower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal and sandy soils. The plant produces showy, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
