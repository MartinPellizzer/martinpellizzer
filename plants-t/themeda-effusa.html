Themeda effusa

Themeda effusa, commonly known as the slender blady grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It is commonly found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and heathlands.

Themeda effusa plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
