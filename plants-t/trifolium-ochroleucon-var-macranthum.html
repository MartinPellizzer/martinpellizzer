Trifolium ochroleucon var. macranthum

Trifolium ochroleucon var. macranthum, commonly known as the large-flowered yellow clover, is a variant of the yellow clover species.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological systems.
