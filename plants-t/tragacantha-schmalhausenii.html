Tragacantha schmalhausenii

Tragacantha schmalhausenii, commonly known as the Schmalhausen's tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climatic conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the summer months. Its root system is known for producing a resinous substance, which has been historically used in traditional medicine.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological niche.
