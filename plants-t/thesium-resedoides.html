Thesium resedoides

Thesium resedoides, commonly known as the pink sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hair, giving it a silvery appearance. It thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found growing in dunes or along the shoreline.

Thesium resedoides is adapted to withstand coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds.
