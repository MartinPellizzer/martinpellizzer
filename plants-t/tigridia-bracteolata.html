Tigridia bracteolata

Tigridia bracteolata, commonly known as the Mexican fairy lantern, is a bulbous perennial plant native to Mexico.

It produces striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with a contrasting throat. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to arid environments. It blooms in the spring and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Tigridia bracteolata is part of the Amaryllidaceae family and is known for its resilience in well-drained soils.
