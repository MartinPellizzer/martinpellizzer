Tithymalus esula subsp. tristis

Tithymalus esula subsp.

tristis, commonly known as the slender tithymalus, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

The plant has slender stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped. It produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, which bloom in early summer.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
