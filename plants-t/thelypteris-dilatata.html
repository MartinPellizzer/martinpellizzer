Thelypteris dilatata

Thelypteris dilatata, commonly known as the broad-leaf holly fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Thelypteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, arching fronds with a feathery appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are usually leathery and have a serrated or lobed margin.

Thelypteris dilatata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic foliage.
