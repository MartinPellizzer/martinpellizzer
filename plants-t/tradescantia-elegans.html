Tradescantia elegans

Tradescantia elegans, commonly known as Spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple and pink. The plant has heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are often variegated with white or silver markings. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Tradescantia elegans is a perennial herb that is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to spread through rhizomes.
