Thymus × heppii

Thymus × heppii, commonly known as lemon thyme, is a hybrid shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its low-growing, aromatic foliage and small pink or white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces essential oils in its leaves, which give it a distinctive lemony scent and make it valuable in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its hybrid origin contributes to its adaptability and variability in appearance.
