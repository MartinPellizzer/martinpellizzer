Taraxacum pseudoporphyranthum

Taraxacum pseudoporphyranthum, commonly known as the purple-veined dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of pale green, hairy leaves and a yellow flower head that develops into a white, fluffy pappus for wind dispersal. The plant is native to parts of Africa and is often found in grassy areas and disturbed soils. It has a taproot system and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

The species is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique floral structure and potential medicinal properties.
