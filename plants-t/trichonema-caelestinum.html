Trichonema caelestinum

Trichonema caelestinum, commonly known as the celestial threadplant, is a unique flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, thread-like leaves that give it its common name, and it produces small, pale blue flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is often found growing in shaded, moist environments such as forest undergrowth. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a short, creeping stem.

Trichonema caelestinum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
