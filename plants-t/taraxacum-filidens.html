Taraxacum filidens

Taraxacum filidens, commonly known as the slender dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and a flowering head that produces small, yellow composite flowers. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. It has a taproot system and produces a distinctive, wind-dispersed seed head known as a pappus.

Taraxacum filidens is known for its resilience and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
