Thelypteris thelypterioides subsp. glabra

Thelypteris thelypterioides subsp.

glabra, commonly known as the smooth shield fern, is a member of the Dryopteridaceae family. It is characterized by its lanceolate, leathery, and glabrous (smooth) fronds.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its sori are clustered and covered by a round, kidney-shaped indusium.

This fern is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
