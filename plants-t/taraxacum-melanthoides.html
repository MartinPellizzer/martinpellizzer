Taraxacum melanthoides

Taraxacum melanthoides, commonly known as the woolly dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaves. The plant features a rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. Its flowers are yellow and produce wind-dispersed seeds, typical of the dandelion genus.

The woolly dandelion is adapted to rocky and disturbed soils, making it a resilient species in diverse habitats.
