Talinum paniculatum

Talinum paniculatum, commonly known as red-rooted morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. Its roots are fleshy and often reddish in color, which gives it its common name.

Talinum paniculatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
