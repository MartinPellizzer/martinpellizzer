Tragopogon tauricus

Tragopogon tauricus, commonly known as the Turkish thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region and parts of southern Russia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf structure. The plant features spiny, lobed leaves and produces yellow ray flowers surrounded by a dense cluster of disk flowers. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Tragopogon tauricus is often noted for its robust growth and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
