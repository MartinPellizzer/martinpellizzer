Tecoma uspallatensis

Tecoma uspallatensis, commonly known as the Uspallata trumpetflower, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Tecoma uspallatensis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species suitable for xeriscaping.
