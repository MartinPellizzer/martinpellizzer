Triglochin bulbosa subsp. bulbosa

Triglochin bulbosa subsp.

bulbosa, commonly known as the bulbous spikerush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats. It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescence that bears small, three-lobed flowers.

The plant typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as marshes and pond margins. Its leaves are grass-like, narrow, and often submerged or floating.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates.
