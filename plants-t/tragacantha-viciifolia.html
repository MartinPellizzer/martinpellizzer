Tragacantha viciifolia

Tragacantha viciifolia, commonly known as the Persian gum or goat's rue, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by elongated pods containing seeds. It is known for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species.

The plant is also valued for its resin, which has been used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive.
