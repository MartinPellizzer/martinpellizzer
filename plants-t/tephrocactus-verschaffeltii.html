Tephrocactus verschaffeltii

Tephrocactus verschaffeltii, commonly known as the Schomburgk's cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical stems and areoles. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, adding a striking visual contrast to its green stem.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
