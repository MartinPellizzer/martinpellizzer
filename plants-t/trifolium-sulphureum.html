Trifolium sulphureum

Trifolium sulphureum, commonly known as the yellow trefoil, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each consisting of three leaflets that are typically bright yellow or orange in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands, meadows, and along roadsides.

Trifolium sulphureum is a member of the legume family and has the ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
