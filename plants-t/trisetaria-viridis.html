Trisetaria viridis

Trisetaria viridis, commonly known as the green trisetaria, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Poaceae, or grass family.

It is native to various regions in Europe and Asia, often found in moist, temperate environments. The plant features slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green in color. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are clustered in spikelets, a characteristic feature of the grass family.

Trisetaria viridis is often used in ecological studies due to its role in soil stabilization and its ability to thrive in disturbed habitats.
