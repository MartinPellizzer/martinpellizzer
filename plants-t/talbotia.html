Talbotia

Talbotia, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to southern Africa.

It consists of several species, with Talbotia violacea being the most well-known. These plants are characterized by their vibrant, velvety petals and compact rosettes of dark green leaves. They thrive in bright, indirect light and require well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Talbotia plants are popular as houseplants due to their attractive appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
