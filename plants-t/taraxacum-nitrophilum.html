Taraxacum nitrophilum

Taraxacum nitrophilum, commonly known as the nitrophilous dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers and heart-shaped basal leaves. This plant is adapted to grow in nutrient-rich soils, often found in disturbed or cultivated areas. It produces a milky sap that is characteristic of the genus Taraxacum.

The plant is perennial and typically reaches a height of 10 to 30 centimeters.
