Thysanotus virgatus

Thysanotus virgatus, commonly known as the star-of-Bethlehem, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The flowers are typically white or pale blue, with prominent stamen filaments that give the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Thysanotus virgatus is also cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
