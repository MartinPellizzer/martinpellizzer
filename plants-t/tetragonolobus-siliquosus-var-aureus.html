Tetragonolobus siliquosus var. aureus

Tetragonolobus siliquosus var. aureus, commonly known as the golden burr vetch, is a variant of the tetragonolobus siliquosus species.

It is a leguminous plant characterized by its distinctive golden-yellow pods and trifoliate leaves. The plant is native to parts of Africa and is often found in dry, sandy soils. It has small, pea-like flowers that bloom in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value.

The variety aureus is particularly noted for its vibrant coloration and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens.
