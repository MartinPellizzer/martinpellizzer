Tephroseris integrifolia subsp. capitata

Tephroseris integrifolia subsp.

capitata, commonly known as the Pacific coast hollyhock, is a flowering plant native to the western coast of North America. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer.

The subspecies is distinguished by its large, showy flower heads and smooth, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in coastal environments, often growing in sandy or gravelly soils.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens along the Pacific coast.
