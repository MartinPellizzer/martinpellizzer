Trisetum spicatum subsp. formosanum

Trisetum spicatum subsp.

formosanum, commonly known as the Formosan feathergrass, is a grass species native to eastern Asia, particularly Taiwan. It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and dense, tufted growth habit.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous minute, spikelet-like flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
