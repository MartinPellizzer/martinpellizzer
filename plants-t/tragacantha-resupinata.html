Tragacantha resupinata

Tragacantha resupinata, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to regions of the Middle East and Central Asia, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid climates. The plant is characterized by its woody, shrub-like structure and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three leaflets, and it is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility.

The plant is also valued for its gum, which has been used historically in various traditional applications.
