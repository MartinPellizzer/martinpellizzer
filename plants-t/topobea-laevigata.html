Topobea laevigata

Topobea laevigata, commonly known as the smoothleaf topobea, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its smooth, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and dry, open habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions. Its flowering period occurs in the spring, and it plays a role in supporting local pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a species of conservation concern in some regions.
