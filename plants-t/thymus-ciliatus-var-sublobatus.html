Thymus ciliatus var. sublobatus

Thymus ciliatus var. sublobatus, commonly known as the fringed thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features small, fragrant leaves that are typically gray-green in color and have a slightly fuzzy texture. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its aromatic properties make it a popular choice for culinary and medicinal uses.
