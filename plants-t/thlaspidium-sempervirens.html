Thlaspidium sempervirens

Thlaspidium sempervirens, commonly known as the evergreen thlaspidium, is a perennial plant native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces small, clustered flowers. The plant features narrow, leathery leaves that are typically green with a slight bluish tint. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice among gardeners in arid climates.
