Taraxacum cerebrum

Taraxacum cerebrum, commonly known as the brain dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and a hollow, leafless stem that bears a single flower head. The flower head consists of both ray and disk florets, which are typically yellow in color. The plant produces a distinctive, wind-dispersed seed with a white, pappus that aids in its propagation.

It is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats, showcasing its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
