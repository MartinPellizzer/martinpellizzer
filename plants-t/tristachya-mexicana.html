Tristachya mexicana

Tristachya mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican feather grass, is a perennial grass native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-green foliage and delicate, feathery plumes that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.

Tristachya mexicana is also noted for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and its drought tolerance.
