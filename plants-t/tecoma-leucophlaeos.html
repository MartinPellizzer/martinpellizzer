Tecoma leucophlaeos

Tecoma leucophlaeos, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height. The plant produces striking white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The bark is smooth and light-colored, with a distinctive flaky appearance.
