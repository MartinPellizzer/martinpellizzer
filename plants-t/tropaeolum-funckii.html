Tropaeolum funckii

Tropaeolum funckii, commonly known as the Andean jewelweed, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with a distinctive yellow or orange center. The plant produces small, edible capsules that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Tropaeolum funckii thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens.
