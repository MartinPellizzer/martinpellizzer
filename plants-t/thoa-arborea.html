Thoa arborea

Thoa arborea, commonly known as the tree of heaven, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are alternate and pinnately arranged, and its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain one or two seeds. It is often found in a variety of habitats, including disturbed soils and urban environments.

Thoa arborea is also known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
