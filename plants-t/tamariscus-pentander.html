Tamariscus pentander

Tamariscus pentander, commonly known as the desert rose, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and small, scale-like leaves that help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces vibrant pink or red flowers during the spring and summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a hardy plant for xeriscaping.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in dry climates.
