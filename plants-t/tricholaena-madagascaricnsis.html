Tricholaena madagascaricnsis

Tricholaena madagascaricnsis, commonly known as the Madagascar grass or Madagascar bamboo, is a perennial herb native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems and narrow leaves. The plant is often found in moist, forested areas and can form dense stands. It has a fibrous root system and is known for its rapid growth rate.

Tricholaena madagascaricnsis is also valued for its potential use in erosion control and as a source of biomass.
