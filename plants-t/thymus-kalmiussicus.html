Thymus kalmiussicus

Thymus kalmiussicus, commonly known as Kalmius thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically gray-green in color, covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in horticulture.
