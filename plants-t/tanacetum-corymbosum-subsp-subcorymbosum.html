Tanacetum corymbosum subsp. subcorymbosum

Tanacetum corymbosum subsp.

subcorymbosum, commonly known as sweet fern, is a perennial herb native to North America. It is characterized by its fern-like, bipinnate leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating.

The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters called corymbs. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting various pollinators.
