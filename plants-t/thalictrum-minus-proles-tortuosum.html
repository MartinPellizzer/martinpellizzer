Thalictrum minus proles tortuosum

Thalictrum minus proles tortuosum, commonly known as the creeping avens, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves that are often hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, which are clustered in elongated cymes. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and its role in supporting pollinators.
