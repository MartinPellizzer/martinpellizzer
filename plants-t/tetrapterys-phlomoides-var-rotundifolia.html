Tetrapterys phlomoides var. rotundifolia

Tetrapterys phlomoides var. rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaf horseweed, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields, and it has a sprawling growth habit with hairy stems. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to round in shape, and often have a glandular surface.

The species is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in full sun.
