Trigonella arborea

Trigonella arborea, commonly known as the tree fenugreek, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its woody stem and trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged symmetrically. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into pods containing several seeds. Native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia, it thrives in dry, rocky soils.

Due to its medicinal and nutritional properties, it has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a spice.
