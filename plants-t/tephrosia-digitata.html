Tephrosia digitata

Tephrosia digitata, commonly known as the hairy tephrosia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. Its fruit is a slender pod that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Tephrosia digitata is notable for its potential medicinal uses and its role in traditional herbal practices.
