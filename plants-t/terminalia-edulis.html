Terminalia edulis

Terminalia edulis, commonly known as the African mango or butter fruit, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and elongated, oval-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, yellow-orange berries that are rich in nutrients. The fruit's pulp is high in dietary fiber and contains compounds that may support metabolic health.

It is often cultivated for its nutritional value and is used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
