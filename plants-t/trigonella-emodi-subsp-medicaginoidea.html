Trigonella emodi subsp. medicaginoidea

Trigonella emodi subsp.

medicaginoidea, commonly known as the Kashmiri fenugreek, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan region. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod-like fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability.

The plant typically grows in moist, mountainous habitats and has trifoliate leaves with a distinctive aroma. Its seeds are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
