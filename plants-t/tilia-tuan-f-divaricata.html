Tilia tuan f. divaricata

Tilia tuan f. divaricata, commonly known as the Tuan tree, is a unique variant of the Tilia genus native to Southeast Asia.

This plant is characterized by its distinctive, often zigzagging branches and heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green. The flowers, which appear in clusters, are small and pale yellow, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

While not widely cultivated, it holds cultural significance in some regions of its native habitat.
