Trifolium tumens

Trifolium tumens, commonly known as the swollen clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves, each leaflet being rounded and slightly swollen. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides.

Trifolium tumens is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it an important plant in soil enrichment.
