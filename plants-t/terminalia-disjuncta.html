Terminalia disjuncta

Terminalia disjuncta, commonly known as the African snowball tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and compound leaves that are opposite or alternate in arrangement. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in dense, spherical clusters, giving it its common name. Its fruit is a drupe, typically containing a single seed, and it is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Terminalia disjuncta is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
