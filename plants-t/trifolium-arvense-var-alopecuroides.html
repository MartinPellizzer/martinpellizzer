Trifolium arvense var. alopecuroides

Trifolium arvense var. alopecuroides, commonly known as slender clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets.

It typically grows in open, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and pastures. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers arranged in dense, globular clusters, which bloom in the early spring. It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in soil enrichment.
