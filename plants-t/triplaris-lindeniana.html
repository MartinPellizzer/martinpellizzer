Triplaris lindeniana

Triplaris lindeniana, commonly known as the Brazilian wax tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that give it its common name. The plant has a dense canopy and produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, orange-colored fruits. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
