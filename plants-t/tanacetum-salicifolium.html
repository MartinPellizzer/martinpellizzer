Tanacetum salicifolium

Tanacetum salicifolium, commonly known as willow-leaved tansy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, willow-like leaves and produces yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer. It is often found in moist meadows and along stream banks, thriving in temperate climates.

Tanacetum salicifolium is known for its aromatic properties and has been used historically in traditional medicine.
