Tropaeolum × tenuirostre var. majus

Tropaeolum × tenuirostre var. majus, commonly known as the "Indian cress" or "Jacob's ladder," is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, peppermint-scented flowers that bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant produces edible, peppery-tasting leaves and seeds, making it both ornamental and culinary. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its rapid growth and ability to spread via runners make it a popular choice for garden borders and container planting.
