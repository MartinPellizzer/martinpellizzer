Tilia × europaea var. corallina

Tilia × europaea var. corallina, commonly known as the coral-leaf lime, is a hybrid tree species belonging to the Tiliaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive coral-pink to reddish-orange leaves that change color throughout the growing season. The tree typically grows in temperate regions and prefers well-drained, fertile soils. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, followed by small, round nuts.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
