Thlaspi zangezuricum

Thlaspi zangezuricum, commonly known as the Zangezur cabbageweed, is a species of flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to the Caucasus region, particularly found in Azerbaijan and Armenia. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments. It has basal leaves that are lobed or toothed, and its flowers are small, yellow, and arranged in racemes.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and ecological adaptations.
