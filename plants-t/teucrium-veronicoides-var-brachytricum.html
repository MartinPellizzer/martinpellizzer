Teucrium veronicoides var. brachytricum

Teucrium veronicoides var. brachytricum, commonly known as narrow-leaved germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in woodlands and along stream banks. The plant features opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it produces small, blue to purple flowers in the summer. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in wetland restoration projects.
