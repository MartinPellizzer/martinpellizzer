Thrixspermum obyrneanum

Thrixspermum obyrneanum, commonly known as the African snowbush, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid regions. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its native environment.
