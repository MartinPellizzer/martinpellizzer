Tabernaemontana myriantha

Tabernaemontana myriantha, commonly known as the fragrant starflower, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, star-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant produces a strong, sweet fragrance, which attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture, while its stems are woody and often branched.

Tabernaemontana myriantha thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
