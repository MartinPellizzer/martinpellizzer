Talinum lineare

Talinum lineare, commonly known as red root spinach, is a herbaceous plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often reddish or purple in color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits. Talinum lineare is known for its edible leaves, which have a slightly bitter taste and are used in traditional cuisines.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
