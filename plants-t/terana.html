Terana

Terana, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its velvety, fuzzy leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as a low-growing shrub with compact foliage. Its flowers come in a variety of colors, including pink, purple, white, and blue.

Terana thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
