Trevirana longiflora var. latifolia

Trevirana longiflora var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf trevirana, is a striking perennial plant native to the temperate regions of the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 12 inches in length, providing a bold visual presence in the landscape. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its robust root system makes it drought-tolerant and suitable for a variety of soil types.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in gardens and naturalized settings.
