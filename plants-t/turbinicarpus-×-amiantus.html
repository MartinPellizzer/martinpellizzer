Turbinicarpus × amiantus

Turbinicarpus × amiantus, commonly known as the hybrid moon cactus, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Turbinicarpus stellatus and Turbinicarpus rubrolineatus.

It is characterized by its unique appearance, featuring a vibrant, colorful "cap" atop a green, succulent "body.". The plant is native to the arid regions of southern Africa, where it thrives in well-draining soil and full sunlight. Its succulent nature allows it to store water efficiently, making it drought-tolerant.

The hybrid is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and indoor settings.
