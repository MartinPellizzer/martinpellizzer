Tradescantia crassifolia var. glabrata

Tradescantia crassifolia var. glabrata, commonly known as the smoothleaf spiderwort, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features upright, branching stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are smooth and often tinged with red or purple at the base. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, or lavender, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for wetland gardens and bog landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.
