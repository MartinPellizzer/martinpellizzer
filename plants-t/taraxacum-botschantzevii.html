Taraxacum botschantzevii

Taraxacum botschantzevii, commonly known as the Botchanzev's dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of dark green, hairy leaves and a single, elongated flowering stem that bears a crown of yellow flowers. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, this plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions. Its flowers are typically visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological role in its native habitat.

The plant produces achene fruits with pappus, which aid in wind dispersal of its seeds.
