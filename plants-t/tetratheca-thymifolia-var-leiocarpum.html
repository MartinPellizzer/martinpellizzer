Tetratheca thymifolia var. leiocarpum

Tetratheca thymifolia var. leiocarpum, commonly known as the silky tetratheca, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in shrubland and woodland habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
