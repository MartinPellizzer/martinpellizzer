Thelypodium integrifolium var. affine

Thelypodium integrifolium var. affine, commonly known as the smoothleaf thelypodium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are smooth along their margins, giving it its common name. Its small, clustered flowers are usually pink or white, blooming in late spring to early summer.

The species is characterized by its simple, unbranched stems and is often found in association with other wetland flora.
