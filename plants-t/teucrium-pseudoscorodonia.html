Teucrium pseudoscorodonia

Teucrium pseudoscorodonia, commonly known as False Bugloss, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, blue or purple flowers in summer. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants.

Teucrium pseudoscorodonia is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
