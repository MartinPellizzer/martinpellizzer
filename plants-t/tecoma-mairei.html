Tecoma mairei

Tecoma mairei, commonly known as the yellow bells, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and ovate with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Tecoma mairei thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.
