Tephrocactus kuehnrichianus

Tephrocactus kuehnrichianus, commonly known as the Kuehnrich's cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is a small, globular cactus with a short, thick stem and a dense covering of spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are typically short-lived but striking in appearance. Its structure is adapted to store water and withstand high temperatures, making it well-suited for desert environments.

The plant is often grown as an ornamental species due to its unique appearance and hardiness.
