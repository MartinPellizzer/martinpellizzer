Tecunumania quetzalteca

Tecunumania quetzalteca, commonly known as the Quetzaltec tree, is a species native to the highlands of southern Mexico.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, gray bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in cloud forests at elevations between 1,500 and 2,500 meters.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
