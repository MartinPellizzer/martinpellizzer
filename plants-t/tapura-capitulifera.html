Tapura capitulifera

Tapura capitulifera, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Tapura capitulifera thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
