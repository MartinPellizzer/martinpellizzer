Themistoclesia cutucuensis

Themistoclesia cutucuensis, commonly known as the Cutucu piper.

It is a climbing shrub native to the Amazon rainforest, particularly in Ecuador and Peru. The plant belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other species of pepper plants. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its small, greenish flowers are clustered in spikes.

The fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns red when mature, and it is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
