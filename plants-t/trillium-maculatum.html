Trillium maculatum

Trillium maculatum, commonly known as the spotted trillium, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its three elliptical leaves and a single, showy white flower with a distinctive red or purple blotch on the lower petals. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist woodlands and thrives in rich, well-drained soil. It blooms in early spring, often before the leaves are fully unfurled.

Trillium maculatum is a member of the Trillium genus, which is known for its unique floral structure and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
