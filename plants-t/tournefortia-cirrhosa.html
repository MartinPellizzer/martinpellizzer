Tournefortia cirrhosa

Tournefortia cirrhosa, commonly known as the silverleaf tournefortia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it its common name. Its flowers are small, yellowish, and clustered in inflorescences, often appearing in late spring or early summer.

Tournefortia cirrhosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
