Tradescantia deficiens

Tradescantia deficiens, commonly known as the spiderwort or redstem sedum, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in sunny, well-drained soils, often found in rocky or sandy habitats. The plant features succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a reddish tinge, especially under stress. Its showy, tubular flowers are usually pink or violet, blooming in late spring to early summer.

Tradescantia deficiens is drought-tolerant and often used in xeriscaping due to its hardy nature.
