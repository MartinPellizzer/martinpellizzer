Typha ephemeroida

Typha ephemeroida, commonly known as the ephemeral cattail, is a wetland plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical flower spike that matures into a brown, mat-like structure. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often near the edges of ponds, lakes, and wetlands. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly, forming dense stands.

Typha ephemeroida plays an important role in stabilizing shorelines and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
