Timonius repertus

Timonius repertus, commonly known as the rare forest orchid, is a strikingly beautiful plant native to the temperate rainforests of southern Brazil.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows on decaying wood, forming a symbiotic relationship with specific fungi to obtain nutrients. Its leaves are broad and evergreen, adapted to thrive in the shaded, moist conditions of its natural habitat.

Due to habitat loss, Timonius repertus is considered endangered and is the subject of conservation efforts.
