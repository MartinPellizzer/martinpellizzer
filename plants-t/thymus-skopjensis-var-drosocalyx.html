Thymus skopjensis var. drosocalyx

Thymus skopjensis var. drosocalyx, commonly known as the Skopje thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and aromatic, narrow-lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its essential oils contain thymol and carvacrol, contributing to its medicinal and culinary uses.
