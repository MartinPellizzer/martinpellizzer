Tococa glandulosa

Tococa glandulosa, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, red berries and opposite, ovate leaves with glandular dots. The plant has a woody stem with smooth, gray bark that becomes fissured with age. Tococa glandulosa thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in disturbed soils and along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
