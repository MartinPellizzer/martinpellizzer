Trichocereus fulvilanus

Trichocereus fulvilanus, commonly known as the yellow torch cactus, is a species native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its yellow spines and smooth, green stems. The plant produces large, white flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It thrives in arid environments and requires well-draining soil and ample sunlight.

Due to its striking appearance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
