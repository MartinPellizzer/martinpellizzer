Trichopteryx fruticulosa var. perlaxa

Trichopteryx fruticulosa var. perlaxa, commonly known as the silverleaf shrub, is a perennial plant native to the temperate regions of southern Europe.

It features slender, woody stems that are covered in a dense layer of silvery-white hairs, giving it its characteristic appearance. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly waxy texture that helps reduce water loss. It produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the spring, which are followed by tiny, berry-like fruits.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
