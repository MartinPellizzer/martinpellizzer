Tricholepis elongata

Tricholepis elongata, commonly known as the slender tamarisk, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and produces tiny, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access deep groundwater sources.

Tricholepis elongata plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
