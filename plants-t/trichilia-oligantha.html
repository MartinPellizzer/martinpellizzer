Trichilia oligantha

Trichilia oligantha, commonly known as African mahogany, is a tropical tree native to Africa.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its smooth, light-gray bark and alternate, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits containing flat, winged seeds. It is valued for its durable, aromatic wood, which is used in furniture and cabinet making.

The tree also plays an important ecological role in its native habitats.
