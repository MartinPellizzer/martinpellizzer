Teucrium rotundifolium subvar. parvulum

Teucrium rotundifolium subvar. parvulum, commonly known as parvuleum germander, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, rounded leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pink to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open woodlands and rocky areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for ecological studies and native landscaping.
