Tittelbachia

Tittelbachia, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The leaves are simple, alternate, and oval-shaped with a smooth surface. The tree grows rapidly and can reach heights of up to 30 meters.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to provide shade in tropical and subtropical climates.
