Tinus brandisiana

Tinus brandisiana, commonly known as the Natal orchid, is a species of orchid native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in summer. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern.

Tinus brandisiana prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
