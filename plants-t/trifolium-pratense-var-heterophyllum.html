Trifolium pratense var. heterophyllum

Trifolium pratense var. heterophyllum, commonly known as the heterophyllous white clover, is a variant of the white clover species characterized by its distinct leaf morphology.

It typically features trifoliate leaves with a unique variation in leaflet size and shape, often with one leaflet being significantly larger than the others. This plant is native to Europe and has been introduced to various parts of the world, including North America. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands, meadows, and along roadsides.

The plant is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it a useful forage crop and a valuable component in ecological restoration efforts.
