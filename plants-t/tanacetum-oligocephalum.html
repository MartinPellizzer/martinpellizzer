Tanacetum oligocephalum

Tanacetum oligocephalum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tansy, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features finely divided, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in dry meadows and rocky areas.

Tanacetum oligocephalum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically in traditional medicine.
