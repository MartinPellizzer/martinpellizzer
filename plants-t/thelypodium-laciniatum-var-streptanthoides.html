Thelypodium laciniatum var. streptanthoides

Thelypodium laciniatum var. streptanthoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thelypodium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features slender, branching stems that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The leaves are lance-shaped, with a serrated or toothed margin, and they are typically arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are small, clustered in racemes, and have four petals that are often pink to purple in color.

This plant prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments.
