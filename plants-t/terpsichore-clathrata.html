Terpsichore clathrata

Terpsichore clathrata, commonly known as the lattice mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of specific host trees.

It has small, scale-like leaves and produces white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant's unique structure includes a network of fine, thread-like stems that form a lattice-like pattern on its host. It relies on its host for water and nutrients but can photosynthesize to some extent.

Terpsichore clathrata is often found in temperate forests and is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
