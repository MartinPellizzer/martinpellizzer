Trifolium micropetalum

Trifolium micropetalum, commonly known as the small-petalled clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and small, pea-like flowers that are typically white or pale pink. Native to parts of Europe and Asia, this plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in grassy areas and meadows. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.

Its delicate appearance and ecological importance make it a notable species in botanical studies.
