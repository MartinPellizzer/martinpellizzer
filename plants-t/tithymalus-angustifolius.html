Tithymalus angustifolius

Tithymalus angustifolius, commonly known as narrow-leaved tithymalus, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open woodlands. The plant features slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slightly hairy surface.

Tithymalus angustifolius is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
