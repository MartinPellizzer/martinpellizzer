Trachypogon gracilis var. hirtus

Trachypogon gracilis var. hirtus, commonly known as the hairy slender grass, is a variant of the slender grass species native to South America.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its fine, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and along roadsides. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical spikelet that bears small, spike-like flowers.

The hairy texture of its leaves and stems is a distinguishing feature of this variety.
