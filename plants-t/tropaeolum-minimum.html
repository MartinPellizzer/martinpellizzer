Tropaeolum minimum

Tropaeolum minimum, commonly known as the common garden nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a low-growing annual with heart-shaped leaves and bright, showy flowers that range from yellow to orange-red. The plant produces edible leaves and seeds, which have a peppery flavor similar to watercress. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its ability to attract pollinators and deter pests makes it a valuable addition to both ornamental and edible gardens.
