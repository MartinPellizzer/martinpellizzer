Thesium mauritanicum

Thesium mauritanicum, commonly known as the desert broom.

It is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North Africa and the Middle East. The plant is characterized by its erect, spiny stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. It possesses a deep root system that allows it to survive in dry, sandy soils.

Thesium mauritanicum is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low rainfall, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.
