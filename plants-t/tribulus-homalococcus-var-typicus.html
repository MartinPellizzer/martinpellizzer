Tribulus homalococcus var. typicus

Tribulus homalococcus var. typicus, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that resemble small showers, hence its common name. The plant produces small, hard, yellowish fruits that contain seeds. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and ability to thrive in warm climates.

The botanical features of this species make it a popular choice for both horticulture and traditional medicine.
