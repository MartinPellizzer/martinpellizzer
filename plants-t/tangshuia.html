Tangshuia

Tangshuia, commonly known as the Chinese hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, yellow or orange fruits that are edible and often used in traditional Chinese medicine. Tangshuia thrives in temperate climates and is valued for its ornamental beauty and medicinal properties.

Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it blooms in early spring.
