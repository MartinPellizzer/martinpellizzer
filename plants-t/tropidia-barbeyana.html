Tropidia barbeyana

Tropidia barbeyana, commonly known as the Barbey's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant features a sympodial growth pattern, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its roots are fibrous and often grow in dense mats, helping to anchor the plant in its rocky or sandy habitat.

Tropidia barbeyana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
