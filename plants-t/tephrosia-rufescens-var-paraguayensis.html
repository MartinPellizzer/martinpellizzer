Tephrosia rufescens var. paraguayensis

Tephrosia rufescens var. paraguayensis, commonly known as Paraguay false indigo, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in shades of purple or blue, and it is known for its nitrogen-fixing ability.

This species is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and ecological benefits.
