Turibana macroptera

Turibana macroptera, commonly known as the giant turibana, is a striking member of the Bromeliaceae family, native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its large, strap-like leaves that can grow up to several meters in length, forming a rosette structure that collects rainwater. The plant produces vibrant, showy inflorescences with red and orange bracts, which attract pollinators such as birds and insects. Its epiphytic nature allows it to thrive on trees, where it absorbs moisture and nutrients from the air.

Due to its size and ornamental value, it is a popular specimen in tropical botanical gardens and conservatories.
