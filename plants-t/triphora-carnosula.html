Triphora carnosula

Triphora carnosula, commonly known as the fat-tailed spider plant, is a succulent native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its growth habit is rosette-like, with leaves arranged in a tight, compact formation.

Triphora carnosula is relatively low maintenance, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
