Tacsonia lanata

Tacsonia lanata, commonly known as the woolly tacsonia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that covers its stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. Tacsonia lanata thrives in cool, dry climates and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and ecological research.
