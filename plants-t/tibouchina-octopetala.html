Tibouchina octopetala

Tibouchina octopetala, commonly known as the purple glorybower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of violet to purple tubular flowers that bloom in spring and summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, velvety texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Tibouchina octopetala is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
