Terminalia elliptica

Terminalia elliptica, commonly known as African wild date or sour date, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

The tree produces edible, fleshy fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and local cuisines. Its bark and leaves contain compounds with potential medicinal properties, making it valuable in ethnobotanical practices.

Terminalia elliptica is also noted for its ability to thrive in arid conditions, contributing to its ecological significance in dry regions.
