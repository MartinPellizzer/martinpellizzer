Turbinicarpus × assimilis

Turbinicarpus × assimilis, commonly known as the hybrid moon cactus, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is a hybrid between Turbinicarpus caladium and Turbinicarpus leucotrichus, resulting in a unique combination of characteristics from both parents. The plant is characterized by its compact, globular shape and vibrant, colorful flowers that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. Its leaves are typically green, although some varieties may exhibit variegation.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
