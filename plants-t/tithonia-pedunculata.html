Tithonia pedunculata

Tithonia pedunculata, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its large, bright orange to red ray flowers and dark green, heart-shaped leaves. The plant is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its rapid growth and bushy appearance. Tithonia pedunculata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant blooms and ability to attract pollinators.

It is also used in traditional medicine and as a green manure crop in some regions.
