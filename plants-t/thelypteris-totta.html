Thelypteris totta

Thelypteris totta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Thelypteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, pinnately divided fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its rhizomes are often submerged or partially buried in the soil, supporting its growth.

Thelypteris totta is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in maintaining wetland habitats.
