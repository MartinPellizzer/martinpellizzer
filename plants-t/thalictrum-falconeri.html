Thalictrum falconeri

Thalictrum falconeri, commonly known as the Andes spurge, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. The plant features delicate, fern-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. It is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.

Thalictrum falconeri is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local pollinators and biodiversity.
