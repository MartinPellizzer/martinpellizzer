Trigonella noeana var. minor

Trigonella noeana var. minor, commonly known as the lesser fenugreek, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is typically found in wetlands or along the edges of water bodies.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some areas.
