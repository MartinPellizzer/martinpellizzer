Thevetia alliodora

Thevetia alliodora, commonly known as the golden trumpet tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, light green bark and its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces capsule fruits containing toxic seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

However, all parts of the plant contain toxic compounds, making it potentially hazardous to humans and animals.
