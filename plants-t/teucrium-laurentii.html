Teucrium laurentii

Teucrium laurentii, commonly known as Laurentian germander, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches heights of about 30 centimeters. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and produces small, tubular flowers in shades of blue or violet. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic foliage and long blooming period make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological landscapes.
