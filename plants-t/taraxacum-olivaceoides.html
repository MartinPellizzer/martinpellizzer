Taraxacum olivaceoides

Taraxacum olivaceoides, commonly known as the olive-green dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves and a yellow flowering head that develops into a white, fluffy seed sphere. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this plant thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. It has a taproot system and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and gardens.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
