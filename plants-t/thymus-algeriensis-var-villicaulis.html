Thymus algeriensis var. villicaulis

Thymus algeriensis var. villicaulis, commonly known as Algerian thyme with hairy stems, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North Africa.

It is characterized by its fragrant, small, gray-green leaves and clusters of pink to purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and Mediterranean climates. Its essential oils, rich in thymol and carvacrol, contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and aromatic properties.
