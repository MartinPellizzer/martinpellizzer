Tetrastigma petelotii

Tetrastigma petelotii, commonly known as the Vietnamese crossvine, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically yellow or red when ripe, containing seeds that are dispersed by animals. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
