Tragacantha tabriziana

Tragacantha tabriziana, commonly known as Persian gum or tragacanth, is a perennial herb native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Iran.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, spindly stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by distinctive, woody pods that contain the valuable resinous gum.

This gum, known as tragacanth, is widely used in traditional medicine, food, and as a thickening agent in various industries.
