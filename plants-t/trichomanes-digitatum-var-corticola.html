Trichomanes digitatum var. corticola

It is a small, epiphytic fern that thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found on rocks or tree trunks in tropical and subtropical regions. Its fronds are usually clustered in a rosette-like formation, and it produces small, sporangia-bearing structures on the underside of its leaves.

This variant is notable for its ability to adapt to nutrient-poor substrates, making it a resilient and ecologically significant species in its native range.
