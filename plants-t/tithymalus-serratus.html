Tithymalus serratus

Tithymalus serratus, commonly known as the serrated tithymalus, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, serrated leaves and small, daisy-like yellow flowers. Native to southern Africa, this plant thrives in dry, rocky habitats. It produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant is often used in rock gardens due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
