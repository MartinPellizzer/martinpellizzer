Theaphylla lanceolata

Theaphylla lanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and prefers full sun exposure. It is often found in coastal and inland regions, thriving in arid and semi-arid environments.

The flowers bloom during the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
