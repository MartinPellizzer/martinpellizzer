Trachymene lanceolata var. myrtifolia

Trachymene lanceolata var. myrtifolia, commonly known as the myrtle-leaved false shamrock, is a herbaceous perennial native to Australia.

It features slender, upright stems with trifoliate leaves that have a distinctive myrtle-like scent. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming from spring to autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its fragrant foliage and delicate floral display.
