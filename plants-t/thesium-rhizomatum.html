Thesium rhizomatum

Thesium rhizomatum, commonly known as the creeping thesium, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Xanthorrhoeaceae.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, which allows it to spread and form dense mats. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. It is native to parts of Africa and is often found in dry, sandy soils.

Thesium rhizomatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticultural settings.
