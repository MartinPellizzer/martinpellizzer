Tinus fertilis

Tinus fertilis, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its feathery, panicle-like inflorescences that produce small, yellow flowers in the spring. The plant has compound leaves with elongated leaflets that give it a delicate, airy appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental species in gardens.

Tinus fertilis is also valued for its rapid growth and ability to provide shade in urban landscapes.
