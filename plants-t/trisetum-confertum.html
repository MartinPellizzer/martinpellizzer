Trisetum confertum

Trisetum confertum, commonly known as the dense trisetum, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, temperate environments such as meadows and wetlands. The plant features slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, spikelet-based flowers.

Trisetum confertum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
