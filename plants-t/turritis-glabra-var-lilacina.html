Turritis glabra var. lilacina

Turritis glabra var. lilacina, commonly known as the lilac-flowered turritis, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and leaves that are typically lanceolate in shape. The plant produces clusters of small, delicate flowers that range in color from pale pink to a soft lilac hue. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
