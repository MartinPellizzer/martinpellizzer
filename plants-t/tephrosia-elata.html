Tephrosia elata

Tephrosia elata, commonly known as the tall tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, with five petals. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Tephrosia elata is known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
