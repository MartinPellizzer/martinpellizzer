Tribulus incanus

Tribulus incanus, commonly known as the pale tribulus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, annual herb that typically grows in dry, sandy soils. The plant features slender, branched stems and small, pale green leaves. Its flowers are yellow and occur in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees.

Tribulus incanus is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
