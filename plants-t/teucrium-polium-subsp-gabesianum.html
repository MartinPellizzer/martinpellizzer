Teucrium polium subsp. gabesianum

Teucrium polium subsp.

gabesianum, commonly known as the Greek germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features slender, aromatic stems and lance-shaped leaves with a grayish-green hue.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically arranged in terminal clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological and horticultural purposes.
