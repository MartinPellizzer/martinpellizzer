Tapeinosperma sessilifolium var. ouazangouense

Tapeinosperma sessilifolium var. ouazangouense, commonly known as the Ouazangouena tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to West Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves that are typically sessile, meaning they lack a stalk. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local communities.
