Tragacantha halodendron

Tragacantha halodendron, commonly known as the saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves that reduce water loss, making it well adapted to harsh, saline environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a touch of color to its otherwise rugged appearance. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Tragacantha halodendron is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize sandy soils.
