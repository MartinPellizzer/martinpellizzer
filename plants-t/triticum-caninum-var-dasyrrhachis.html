Triticum caninum var. dasyrrhachis

Triticum caninum var. dasyrrhachis, commonly known as the hairy wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and long, narrow leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences containing numerous florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
