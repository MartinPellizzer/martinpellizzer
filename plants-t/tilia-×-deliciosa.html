Tilia × deliciosa

Tilia × deliciosa, commonly known as the American linden or basswood, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Tiliaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, yellowish-white flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits called samaras, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forests and along stream banks.

Its rapid growth and ability to tolerate urban conditions make it a popular choice for landscaping.
