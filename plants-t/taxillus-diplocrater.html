Taxillus diplocrater

Taxillus diplocrater, commonly known as the mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches itself to the branches of host trees, primarily in the families Myrtaceae and Fabaceae.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using light absorbed by its leaves. The plant forms a symbiotic relationship with its host, often leading to reduced growth and vitality in the host tree. It produces small, white or yellowish berries that are an important food source for birds and other animals.

Taxillus diplocrater is widely distributed in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in forest canopies where it thrives in shaded environments.
