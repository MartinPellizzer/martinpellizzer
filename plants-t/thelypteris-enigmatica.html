Thelypteris enigmatica

Thelypteris enigmatica, commonly known as the enigmatic shield fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Thelypteridaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds, a common feature among ferns.

Thelypteris enigmatica is noted for its unique morphological traits, which contribute to its classification as a rare and enigmatic species.
