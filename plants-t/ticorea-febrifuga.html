Ticorea febrifuga

Ticorea febrifuga, commonly known as the feverfew plant, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of gray-green, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.

Its common name reflects its historical use in treating fever and other ailments.
