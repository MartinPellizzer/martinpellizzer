Tinus petocalyx

Tinus petocalyx, commonly known as the Cape chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, yellow flowers. The plant produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates.

Its botanical features make it an important species in local ecosystems and horticulture.
