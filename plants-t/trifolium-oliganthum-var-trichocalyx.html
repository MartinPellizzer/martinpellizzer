Trifolium oliganthum var. trichocalyx

Trifolium oliganthum var. trichocalyx, commonly known as the trichocalyx clover, is a perennial herb native to certain regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval to heart-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassy areas or along woodland edges.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its potential use in ecological restoration projects.
