Tristachya triticoides

Tristachya triticoides, commonly known as the panic grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open areas such as fields and roadsides.

Tristachya triticoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
