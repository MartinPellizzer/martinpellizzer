Trigonella americana

Trigonella americana, commonly known as American bitter vetch, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is native to the United States. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellow to green pea-like flowers. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Trigonella americana is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
