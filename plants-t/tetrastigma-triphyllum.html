Tetrastigma triphyllum

Tetrastigma triphyllum, commonly known as the three-leaf passionvine, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged in a fan-like pattern. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by edible berries that are often consumed by birds and mammals.
