Trisetum pilosum

Trisetum pilosum, commonly known as the hairy false brome, is a perennial grass species native to Eurasia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, hairy stems that often appear in dense colonies. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. Trisetum pilosum thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along streambanks.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
