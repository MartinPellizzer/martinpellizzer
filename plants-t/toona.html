Toona

Toona, commonly known as Chinese tallowtree, is a genus of flowering plants in the mulberry family, Moraceae.

It consists of several species, with Toona sinensis being the most well-known. These plants are native to Asia, particularly China, and are characterized by their large, heart-shaped leaves and showy yellow flowers. The tree produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Toona species are often cultivated for their ornamental value and have also been used in traditional medicine.
