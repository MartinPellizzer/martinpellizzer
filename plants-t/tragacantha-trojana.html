Tragacantha trojana

Tragacantha trojana, commonly known as the Trojan tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant produces pods that contain seeds, and it is often found in rocky or dry soils. Tragacantha trojana is valued for its historical use in traditional medicine and as a source of gum.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
