Trichilia emetica

Trichilia emetica, commonly known as African sandalwood, is a small to medium-sized tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing flat, winged seeds. It is valued for its aromatic wood, which is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.

The tree plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
