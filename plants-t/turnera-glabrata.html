Turnera glabrata

Turnera glabrata, commonly known as the velvetleaf or golden turnera, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a member of the Passifloraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, waxy leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous shrub or vine, often found in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and have a distinctive velvety texture, while the flowers are followed by small, orange berries.

Turnera glabrata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
