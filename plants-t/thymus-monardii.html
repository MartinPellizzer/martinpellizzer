Thymus monardii

Thymus monardii, commonly known as narrowleaf thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky soils. Its small, pink to purple flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Thymus monardii is valued for its fragrant foliage and is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
