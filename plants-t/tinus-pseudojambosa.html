Tinus pseudojambosa

Tinus pseudojambosa, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Tinus pseudojambosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
