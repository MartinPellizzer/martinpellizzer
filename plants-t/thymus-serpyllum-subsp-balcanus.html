Thymus serpyllum subsp. balcanus

Thymus serpyllum subsp.

balcanus, commonly known as Balkan thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family and is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and pink to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It has a compact growth habit and is well adapted to harsh environmental conditions.

Balkan thyme is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
