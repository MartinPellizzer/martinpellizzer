Tagetes multiflora

Tagetes multiflora, commonly known as the Mexican marigold, is a herbaceous plant native to Mexico and parts of the United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow and orange flowers, which are typically borne in clusters atop slender stems. The plant has lance-shaped, aromatic leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Tagetes multiflora is also known for its ability to repel certain pests, which makes it a valuable companion plant in organic gardening.
