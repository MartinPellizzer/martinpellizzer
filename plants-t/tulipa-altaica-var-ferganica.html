Tulipa altaica var. ferganica

Tulipa altaica var. ferganica, commonly known as the Fergana tulip, is a perennial bulbous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the genus Tulipa in the family Liliaceae and is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces vibrant flowers in shades of red, pink, and white.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking blooms.
