Trichilia fluminensis

Trichilia fluminensis, commonly known as the Brazilian white cedar, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellowish flowers followed by woody, spiny fruits containing kidney-shaped seeds. It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its durable wood.

Trichilia fluminensis plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
