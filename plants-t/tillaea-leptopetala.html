Tillaea leptopetala

Tillaea leptopetala, commonly known as the narrow-petaled tillaea, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers during the summer months, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in a fine layer of hair to reduce water loss.

Tillaea leptopetala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and full sun.
