Tricyrtis affinis

Tricyrtis affinis, commonly known as the Japanese ghost orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, bell-shaped flowers that resemble small ghosts. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the soil.

Tricyrtis affinis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
