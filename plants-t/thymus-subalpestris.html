Thymus subalpestris

Thymus subalpestris, commonly known as alpine thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It typically features small, fragrant leaves and produces pink or purple flowers in early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. Its aromatic properties make it a valued plant in both traditional herbal medicine and culinary applications.

Thymus subalpestris is also noted for its resilience to cold temperatures and its ability to colonize harsh, high-altitude environments.
