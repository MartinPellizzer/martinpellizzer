Trisetum rigidum subsp. demavandense

Trisetum rigidum subsp.

demavandense, commonly known as the Demavand sedge, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Iran. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute flowers. Its botanical features include sturdy, rigid stems and a compact growth habit.

It is of interest to botanists and conservationists due to its limited distribution and ecological significance in its native environment.
