Tillandsia kauffmannii

Tillandsia kauffmannii, commonly known as the Mexican air plant, is a member of the Bromeliaceae family.

It is an epiphytic plant that thrives without soil, absorbing moisture and nutrients through its leaves. Native to Mexico and Central America, it is characterized by its compact rosette shape and vibrant, often blue or purple, foliage. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink.

It is well adapted to humid environments and requires indirect sunlight for optimal growth.
