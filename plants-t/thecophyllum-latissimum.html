Thecophyllum latissimum

Thecophyllum latissimum, commonly known as the broadleaf fern, is a species of flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Thecophyllum latissimum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic foliage.
