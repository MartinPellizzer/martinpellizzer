Trollius membranostylis

Trollius membranostylis, commonly known as the narrow-styled globeflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow, globe-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features a stout, hairy stem that supports the clustered inflorescence. Its leaves are typically basal, forming a rosette and are deeply lobed with a hairy surface.

Trollius membranostylis is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, thriving in alpine environments.
