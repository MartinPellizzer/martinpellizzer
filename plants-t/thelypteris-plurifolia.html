Thelypteris plurifolia

Thelypteris plurifolia, commonly known as the many-leaved water fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Thelypteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or near water bodies. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce multiple fronds, which are usually bipinnate or tripinnate.

Thelypteris plurifolia plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
