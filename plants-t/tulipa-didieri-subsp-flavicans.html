Tulipa didieri subsp. flavicans

Tulipa didieri subsp.

flavicans, commonly known as the golden tulip, is a species native to Central Asia, particularly found in regions of Kazakhstan and southern Russia. It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which bloom in early spring, typically from March to April.

The plant grows from a bulb and features strap-like, grassy foliage that emerges from the base. Its flowers are cup-shaped with a distinct, pale yellow to orange hue, and they often have a subtle red or purple tinge at the base.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its striking color and early blooming habit.
