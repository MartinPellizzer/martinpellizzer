Timonius avenis

Timonius avenis, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a deciduous tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The tree produces small, pale green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by small, spherical fruits. It prefers well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Timonius avenis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
