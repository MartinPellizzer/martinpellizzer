Thaumatocaryon dasyanthum var. sellowianum

Thaumatocaryon dasyanthum var. sellowianum, commonly known as the Sellow's starleaf, is a rare and striking plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, star-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in specialized botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
