Thelypteris lepidopoda

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, leathery fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds in clusters called sori.
