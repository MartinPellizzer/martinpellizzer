Tithymalus monchiquensis

Tithymalus monchiquensis, commonly known as the Canary Islands thyme, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often growing in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
