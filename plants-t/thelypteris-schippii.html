Thelypteris schippii

Thelypteris schippii, commonly known as the Schippi's holly fern, is a species of fern native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically bright green and have a feathery appearance. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its fertile fronds, which are clustered in small, rounded structures called sori. Thelypteris schippii thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and ease of cultivation.
