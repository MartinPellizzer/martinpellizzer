Triplaris martiana

Triplaris martiana, commonly known as the Brazilian mahogany, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the family Palmae and is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces dark, aromatic wood that is highly valued for its durability and fine grain. Triplaris martiana thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and economic contexts.
