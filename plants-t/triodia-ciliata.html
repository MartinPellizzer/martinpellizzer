Triodia ciliata

Triodia ciliata, commonly known as the spinifex grass, is a perennial grass native to Australia.

It is characterized by its wiry, spiny stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. This plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy soils. It plays a vital role in stabilizing dunes and preventing soil erosion.

Triodia ciliata is also an important component of the vegetation in the Australian outback.
