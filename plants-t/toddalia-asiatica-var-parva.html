Toddalia asiatica var. parva

Toddalia asiatica var. parva, commonly known as the small toddia, is a compact evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features small, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along its stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
