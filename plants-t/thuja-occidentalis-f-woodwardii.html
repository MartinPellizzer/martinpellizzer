Thuja occidentalis f. woodwardii

Thuja occidentalis f. woodwardii, commonly known as the American arborvitae or northern white cedar, is a cultivar of the eastern white cedar.

It is characterized by its slow growth rate and compact, pyramidal shape, making it suitable for ornamental landscaping. The foliage consists of scale-like leaves that are typically a deep green, providing year-round visual interest. This variety is highly resistant to pests and diseases, contributing to its durability in various climates.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a versatile choice for gardeners.
