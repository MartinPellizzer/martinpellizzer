Triticum × laxum var. microstachyum

Triticum × laxum var. microstachyum, commonly known as the slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, blue-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, pale green florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is cultivated in gardens and used in traditional medicine in some regions.
