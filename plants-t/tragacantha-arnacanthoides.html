Tragacantha arnacanthoides

Tragacantha arnacanthoides, commonly known as the horned tragacanth, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, branched, horn-like stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in loose inflorescences, followed by pods that contain several seeds. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The plant has been used traditionally for its gum, which is harvested from the stems and has various industrial applications.
