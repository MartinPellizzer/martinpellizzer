Tripolium albosetum

Tripolium albosetum, commonly known as the white-haired tansy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in coastal areas. Its leaves are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators.
