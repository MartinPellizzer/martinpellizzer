Triticum vulgare var. cyanorelutinum

Triticum vulgare var. cyanorelutinum, commonly known as the blue-gold wheat, is a rare variant of common wheat characterized by its unique foliage and grain coloration.

This plant exhibits a striking combination of blue-green leaves and golden grains, which are due to specific pigmentation traits. It is typically found in temperate regions where it thrives in well-drained soils and moderate climates. The botanical features of this variety make it a subject of interest in both horticultural and genetic research.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in specialty gardens and botanical collections.
