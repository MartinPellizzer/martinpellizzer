Tulipa edulis

Tulipa edulis, commonly known as the edible tulip, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the genus Tulipa within the family Liliaceae, which includes other well-known tulip species. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in a range of colors, typically pink, red, or white, during the spring season. Its bulbs are edible and have been traditionally used in local cuisines for their mild, nutty flavor.

However, due to its ornamental value, it is also cultivated in gardens around the world.
