Thalictrum × lundstroemii

Thalictrum × lundstroemii, commonly known as the hybrid meadow rue, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like foliage and clusters of small, usually yellow or greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and thrives in partial to full shade. Its botanical features include a rhizomatous root system and showy, actinomorphic flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Thalictrum × lundstroemii is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
