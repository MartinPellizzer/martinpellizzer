Tium arrectum

Tium arrectum, commonly known as the upright tium, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically features lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of vibrant blue or purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for gardens in warmer climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated for its visual appeal in horticultural settings.
