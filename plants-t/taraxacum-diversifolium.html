Taraxacum diversifolium

Taraxacum diversifolium, commonly known as the variable leaf dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves that vary in shape and size, giving it its specific epithet "diversifolium.". The plant produces a yellow composite flower head that develops into a fluffy seed head with parachute-like seeds. It is native to North America and thrives in a variety of habitats, including meadows and disturbed soils.

This species is often found in open areas and is relatively easy to grow in gardens.
