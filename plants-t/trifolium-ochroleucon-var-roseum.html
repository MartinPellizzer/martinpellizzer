Trifolium ochroleucon var. roseum

Trifolium ochroleucon var. roseum, commonly known as the pink clover, is a variant of the white clover species characterized by its pale pink to rose-colored flowers.

It is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically found in grassy areas and along roadsides. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and its flowers are small and clustered in spherical heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used as a forage crop or in ecological restoration projects.

Its botanical features make it an attractive addition to gardens and a valuable component of biodiversity-rich landscapes.
