Tacinga atropurpurea var. zehntnerioides

Tacinga atropurpurea var. zehntnerioides, commonly known as the Zehntner's tacinga, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking purple to reddish foliage and produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is often covered in a fine, fuzzy layer, which helps to retain moisture in its arid habitat.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
