Trembleya laniflora

Trembleya laniflora, commonly known as the woolly trembleya, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are covered in fine, lanugo-like hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Trembleya laniflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
