Tribulus bimucronatus var. bispinulosus

Tribulus bimucronatus var. bispinulosus, commonly known as the two-spined tribulus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiny stems and leaves, which are typically covered in fine, short hairs. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

Due to its unique morphology and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
