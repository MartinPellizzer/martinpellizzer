Terminalia thorelii

Terminalia thorelii, commonly known as African snowball, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and large, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring, followed by orange-red berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The tree thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to seasonal drought conditions.
