Trichoglottis bipunctata

Trichoglottis bipunctata, commonly known as the two-spotted orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers with prominent yellow spots, which are typical of the genus Trichoglottis. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is a subject of interest for botanical research and conservation efforts.
