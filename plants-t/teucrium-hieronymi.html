Teucrium hieronymi

Teucrium hieronymi, commonly known as the Ethiopian germander, is a perennial herb native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its small, tubular flowers are usually purple or violet, arranged in whorls along the stems.

Teucrium hieronymi is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional remedies for various ailments.
