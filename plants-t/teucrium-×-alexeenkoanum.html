Teucrium × alexeenkoanum

Teucrium × alexeenkoanum, commonly known as the hybrid germander, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
