Trixis pterocaulis

Trixis pterocaulis, commonly known as the desert hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically blooming during the rainy season. Its leaves are small, narrow, and often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Trixis pterocaulis is well adapted to hot, dry environments and plays a role in stabilizing desert soils.
