Turritis pendula

Turritis pendula, commonly known as the hanging bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often arranged in opposite pairs along the stem.

Turritis pendula is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
