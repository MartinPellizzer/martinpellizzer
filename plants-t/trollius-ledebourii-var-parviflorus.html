Trollius ledebourii var. parviflorus

Trollius ledebourii var. parviflorus, commonly known as the small-flowered globeflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow, globe-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has basal leaves that are broadly ovate to lanceolate, often with a waxy coating. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
