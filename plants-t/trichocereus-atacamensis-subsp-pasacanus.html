Trichocereus atacamensis subsp. pasacanus

Trichocereus atacamensis subsp.

pasacanus, commonly known as the Atacama torch cactus, is a cacti species native to the Atacama Desert in Chile. It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to several meters in height.

The plant produces large, showy white flowers during the night, which are pollinated by moths. It has a deep root system that allows it to survive in arid conditions with minimal water.

The species is adapted to extreme temperatures and is often found in rocky soils.
