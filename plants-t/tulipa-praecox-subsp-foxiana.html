Tulipa praecox subsp. foxiana

Tulipa praecox subsp.

foxiana, commonly known as the Fox Island tulip, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming in early spring.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is characterized by its cold-hardy nature, making it suitable for temperate climates. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its early blooming habit.
