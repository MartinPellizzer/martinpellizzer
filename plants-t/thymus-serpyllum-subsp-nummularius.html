Thymus serpyllum subsp. nummularius

Thymus serpyllum subsp.

nummularius, commonly known as narrowleaf thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe. It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are densely packed, giving it a mat-like appearance.

The plant produces tiny, pale pink to white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic leaves are often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
