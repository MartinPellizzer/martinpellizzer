Trichocentrum neudeckeri

Trichocentrum neudeckeri, commonly known as the Neudecker's orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, slender pseudobulbs and linear, lanceolate leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white trichomes. The plant produces pendulous inflorescences with numerous small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, loose potting media for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is a sought-after plant among orchid enthusiasts.
