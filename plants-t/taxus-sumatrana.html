Taxus sumatrana

Taxus sumatrana, commonly known as the Sumatran yew, is a slow-growing evergreen conifer native to the Indonesian island of Sumatra.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and distinctive, elongated cones. The plant produces toxic alkaloids, particularly taxine, which can be harmful to humans and animals. Its bark and leaves contain compounds that have been studied for their potential medicinal properties.

Taxus sumatrana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
