Thyrsostachys siamensis

Thyrsostachys siamensis, commonly known as the Siam grass, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetland or marshy environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that are often tinged with red or purple. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.

Additionally, it plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.
