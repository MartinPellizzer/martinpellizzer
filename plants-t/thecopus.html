Thecopus

Thecopus, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the remote highlands of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its translucent, waxy leaves that shimmer in the moonlight, giving it its ethereal appearance. The plant produces small, pale blossoms that emit a faint bioluminescent glow, attracting nocturnal pollinators. Due to its elusive nature and limited habitat, Thecopus is considered critically endangered.

Its unique botanical features make it a subject of fascination for botanists and conservationists alike.
