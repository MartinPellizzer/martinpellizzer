Taraxacum sublaeticolor

Taraxacum sublaeticolor, commonly known as the pale yellow dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and heart-shaped leaves, typical of the dandelion genus. This plant is native to Europe and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides. Its life cycle includes a rosette of basal leaves and a flowering stem that rises above the foliage.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions and its role in supporting pollinators.
