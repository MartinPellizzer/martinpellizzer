Torenia hookeri

Torenia hookeri, commonly known as the Crown of Thorns, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil. It is often cultivated as a ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and blooms.

Torenia hookeri is also known for its ability to tolerate partial shade and is relatively drought-resistant once established.
