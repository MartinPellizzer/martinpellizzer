Taeniatherum crinitum var. caput-medusae

Taeniatherum crinitum var. caput-medusae, commonly known as the Medusa grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, tufted growth habit and long, slender leaves that often have a bluish-gray hue. The plant produces showy, feathery inflorescences that resemble the crown of Medusa, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its unique appearance.
