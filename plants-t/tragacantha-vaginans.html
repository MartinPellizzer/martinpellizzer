Tragacantha vaginans

Tragacantha vaginans, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and papery pods. The plant produces a valuable gum, tragacanth, which has been used historically in medicine and as a food thickener. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
