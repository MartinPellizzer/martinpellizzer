Toona ciliata var. listeri

Toona ciliata var. listeri, commonly known as the silky gum, is a variant of the silky gum tree native to eastern Australia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 30 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, grey bark and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by elongated, woody pods containing flat, dark brown seeds. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

The species is valued for its timber and is also used in traditional Aboriginal medicine.
