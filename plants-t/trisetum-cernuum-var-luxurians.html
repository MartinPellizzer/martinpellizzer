Trisetum cernuum var. luxurians

Trisetum cernuum var. luxurians, commonly known as the woolly hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and its distinctive, soft, hairy foliage that gives it a woolly appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in meadows, woodlands, or along streambanks. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle of small, greenish-brown spikelets, which are arranged in a drooping manner.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of ecological settings.
