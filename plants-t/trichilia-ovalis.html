Trichilia ovalis

Trichilia ovalis, commonly known as the oval tallow tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae, which includes mahogany and other hardwood species. The plant is characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and elongated, elliptical leaves that are usually arranged oppositely on the stems. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by woody, oval-shaped fruits that contain several seeds.

The tree is valued for its durable timber and has traditional uses in medicine and as a source of tallow.
