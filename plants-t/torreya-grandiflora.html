Torreya grandiflora

Torreya grandiflora, commonly known as the swamp cedar, is a rare and ancient conifer native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, evergreen tree form and its distinct, elongated cones. The plant has needle-like leaves that are dark green and arranged in spirals along the branches. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody, cylindrical cones.

Torreya grandiflora is highly valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
