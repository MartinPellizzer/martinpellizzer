Tragacantha mesites

Tragacantha mesites, commonly known as the Armenian tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Turkey.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces a distinctive gum, known as tragacanth, which is harvested from its stems and used in traditional medicine and as a natural thickening agent.

Tragacantha mesites thrives in temperate climates and is often found in rocky or arid environments.
