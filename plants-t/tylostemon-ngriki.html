Tylostemon ngriki

Tylostemon ngriki, commonly known as the Kangi tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the rainforests of eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy flowers. The plant has smooth, greyish bark that peels in thin, papery layers, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a leathery texture. It produces small, fragrant white or pink flowers that attract nectar-feeding birds and insects.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
