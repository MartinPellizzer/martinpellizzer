Thelocactus flavidispinus

Thelocactus flavidispinus, commonly known as the yellow-spined cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are typically green to yellow-green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its foliage. Its spines are relatively short and can be yellow or brown, providing some protection from herbivores.

Thelocactus flavidispinus is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a resilient plant in its native habitat.
