Thaminophyllum latifolium

Thaminophyllum latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf thamin, is a rare and enigmatic plant species characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length.

It thrives in shaded, moist environments, typically found in dense forest understories where it relies on specific fungal associations for nutrient uptake. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are pollinated by wind, and its seeds are dispersed by water, contributing to its limited distribution.

Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, Thaminophyllum latifolium is classified as endangered, making conservation efforts crucial for its survival.
