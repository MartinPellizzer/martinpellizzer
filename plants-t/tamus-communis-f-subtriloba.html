Tamus communis f. subtriloba

Tamus communis f. subtriloba, commonly known as the three-lobed pokeweed, is a variant of the common pokeweed.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. The leaves are distinctly three-lobed, giving it a unique appearance compared to the typical pokeweed. It produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, followed by dark purple berries.

The plant is often found in woodland areas and thrives in moist, shaded environments.
