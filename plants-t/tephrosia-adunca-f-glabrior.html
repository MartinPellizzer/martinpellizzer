Tephrosia adunca f. glabrior

Tephrosia adunca f. glabrior, commonly known as the smooth tephrosia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often glabrous stems and small, opposite leaves with a lanceolate shape. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-white flowers, which are typically arranged in racemes. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, known for its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities.

The smooth variant, glabrior, distinguishes itself through its reduced pubescence compared to the typical form.
