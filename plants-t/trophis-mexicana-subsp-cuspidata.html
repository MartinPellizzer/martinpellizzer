Trophis mexicana subsp. cuspidata

Trophis mexicana subsp.

cuspidata, commonly known as the Mexican trophis, is a member of the Asteraceae family. It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in composite heads, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and locally endemic species.
