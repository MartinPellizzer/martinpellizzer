Trigonella hamigera

Trigonella hamigera, commonly known as wild fenugreek, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, thriving in sandy or rocky soils. Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice due to their aromatic properties.

Trigonella hamigera plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes cultivated for its nutritional value.
