Tithymalus dracunculoides subsp. inconspicuus

Tithymalus dracunculoides subsp.

inconspicuus, commonly known as the slender tithymalus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features slender, erect stems that are often slightly hairy, and it produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters.

The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

This subspecies is less showy than its relatives, hence its common name.
