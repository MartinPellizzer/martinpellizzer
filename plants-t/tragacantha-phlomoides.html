Tragacantha phlomoides

Tragacantha phlomoides, commonly known as the woolly milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces small, yellow flowers in clusters. The plant has hairy, often grayish leaves that help reduce water loss in arid environments. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Tragacantha phlomoides is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to drought conditions.
