Trisetella sororia

Trisetella sororia, commonly known as the slender false indigo, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and its stems are often hairy. Trisetella sororia is typically found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.

It also plays a role in nitrogen fixation, enriching the soil with nutrients.
