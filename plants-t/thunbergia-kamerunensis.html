Thunbergia kamerunensis

Thunbergia kamerunensis, commonly known as the African bluebell, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a climbing vine that produces striking blue and purple tubular flowers, which are attractive to pollinators such as butterflies and bees. The plant has heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement, and it thrives in warm, humid climates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.

Thunbergia kamerunensis prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.
