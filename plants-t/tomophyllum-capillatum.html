Tomophyllum capillatum

Tomophyllum capillatum, commonly known as the silky bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine, silky hairs, contributing to its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
