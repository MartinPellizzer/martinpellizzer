Thalictrum minus subsp. subcuneatum

Thalictrum minus subsp.

subcuneatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and thrives in partial to full shade. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
