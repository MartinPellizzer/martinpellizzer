Tulipa didieri subsp. platystigma

Tulipa didieri subsp.

platystigma, commonly known as the broad-stemmed fritillary, is a species of bulbous perennial native to Turkey. It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in late spring.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of cup-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and red. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated by gardeners interested in rare and colorful bulbous plants.
