Taverniera glabra

Taverniera glabra, commonly known as the smooth taverniera, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in scrub habitats. It is a shrub that can reach heights of up to 1 meter, with smooth, glabrous stems and leaves.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
