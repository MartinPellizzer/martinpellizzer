Tephrosia emarginatofoliolata

Tephrosia emarginatofoliolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tephrosia, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features trifoliate leaves with a distinctive notched base, and its flowers are typically small and clustered in racemes. It is adapted to grow in dry, sandy soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal uses.
