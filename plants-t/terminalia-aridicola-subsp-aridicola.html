Terminalia aridicola subsp. aridicola

Terminalia aridicola subsp.

aridicola, commonly known as the desert terminalia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss in dry environments.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, followed by edible, fleshy fruits. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low rainfall.

Its ecological importance includes providing food for local wildlife and stabilizing desert soils.
