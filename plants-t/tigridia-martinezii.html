Tigridia martinezii

Tigridia martinezii, commonly known as the Martinez's sword lily, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, upward-facing flowers that resemble lilies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces showy, trumpet-shaped blooms in shades of red, orange, and yellow.

Tigridia martinezii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to arid environments.
