Trigonotis radicans

Trigonotis radicans, commonly known as the creeping trigonotis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of blue or violet bell-shaped flowers. Trigonotis radicans is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and ability to form dense ground covers.
