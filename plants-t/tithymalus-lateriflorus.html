Tithymalus lateriflorus

Tithymalus lateriflorus, commonly known as the clustered tithymalus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in open grasslands and rocky areas. The plant features slender stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are small, tubular, and arranged in clusters, typically in shades of purple or pink.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
