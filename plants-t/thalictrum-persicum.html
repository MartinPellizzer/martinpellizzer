Thalictrum persicum

Thalictrum persicum, commonly known as Persian meadow-rue, is a herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread and establish in suitable habitats.

Thalictrum persicum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and early spring blooms.
