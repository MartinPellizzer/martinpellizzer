Themeda triandra var. brachyantha

Themeda triandra var. brachyantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaf blady grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Due to its resilience and adaptability, it is a common component of many native ecosystems in its range.
