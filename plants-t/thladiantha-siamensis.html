Thladiantha siamensis

Thladiantha siamensis, commonly known as Siam goji, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes cucumbers and gourds. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and edible, red-orange berries that are rich in antioxidants. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Thladiantha siamensis is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
