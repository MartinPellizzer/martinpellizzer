Thea yunnanensis

Thea yunnanensis, commonly known as Yunnan tea or wild tea, is a deciduous shrub native to southwestern China.

It belongs to the Theaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, white flowers. The plant is notable for its role in the evolution of cultivated Camellia sinensis, as it is considered a wild relative of the tea plant. Its leaves contain caffeine and various bioactive compounds, making it of interest to botanists and researchers.

The plant thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.
