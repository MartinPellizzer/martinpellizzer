Telina villosa

Telina villosa, commonly known as the hairy telina, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that are covered in fine, hairy texture, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and also bear a hairy surface, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Telina villosa is often found in dry, rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
