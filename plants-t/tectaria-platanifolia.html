Tectaria platanifolia

Tectaria platanifolia, commonly known as the giant shield fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its large, shield-shaped fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant has a robust rhizome system that allows it to thrive in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are typically bipinnate, with a distinctive, layered structure that aids in water retention.

Tectaria platanifolia is often found in forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological role.
