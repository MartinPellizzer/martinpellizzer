Tulipa bucharica

Tulipa bucharica, commonly known as the Bukhara tulip, is a species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The plant typically grows to a height of 10 to 30 centimeters, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its bulbs are adapted to survive in arid conditions, and it blooms in early spring.

The flowers are often used in ornamental gardening due to their unique appearance and fragrance.
