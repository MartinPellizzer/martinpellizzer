Triticum schrenkianum

Triticum schrenkianum, commonly known as Schrenk's wheat, is a wild species of wheat native to Central Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual plant that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant has slender stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous florets. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it has a fibrous root system adapted to dry conditions.

This species is of interest to researchers due to its potential for genetic diversity and adaptation to harsh climates.
