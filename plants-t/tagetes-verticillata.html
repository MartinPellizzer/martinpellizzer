Tagetes verticillata

Tagetes verticillata, commonly known as water marigold, is a flowering plant native to wetland areas of North America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant thrives in moist or aquatic environments, often growing in shallow water or along the edges of ponds. Its flowers are typically borne in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Tagetes verticillata is also known for its ability to help purify water and is sometimes used in phytoremediation efforts.
