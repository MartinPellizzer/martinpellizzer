Tinus faberi

Tinus faberi, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and alternate, pinnate leaves with ovate to elliptic leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody pods containing edible, sweet-tart seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in traditional medicine for its various health benefits.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
