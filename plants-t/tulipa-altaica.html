Tulipa altaica

Tulipa altaica, commonly known as the Altai tulip, is a perennial bulbous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. Its bulbs are adapted to survive harsh climatic conditions, and it blooms in late spring to early summer.

The Altai tulip is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
