Trigonidium peruvianum

Trigonidium peruvianum, commonly known as the Peruvian trumpet flower, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or mountainous terrain. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture.

Trigonidium peruvianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
