Trichocladus ecklonianus

Trichocladus ecklonianus, commonly known as the African rock pine, is a small evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and thick, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by woody, cone-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
