Tithymalus spinidens

Tithymalus spinidens, commonly known as the spiny tithymalus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring slender, branched stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped, often with serrated edges, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

Tithymalus spinidens is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
