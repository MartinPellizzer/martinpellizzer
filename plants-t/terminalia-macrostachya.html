Terminalia macrostachya

Terminalia macrostachya, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by woody, boat-shaped fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and traditional uses, it is sometimes cultivated for its timber and medicinal properties.
