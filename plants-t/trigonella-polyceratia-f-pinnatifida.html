Trigonella polyceratia f. pinnatifida

Trigonella polyceratia f. pinnatifida, commonly known as the pinnatifid fenugreek, is a variant of the fenugreek plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are broader and more deeply lobed compared to the typical species. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are arranged in umbels, followed by elongated, slender pods containing small, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its potential medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
