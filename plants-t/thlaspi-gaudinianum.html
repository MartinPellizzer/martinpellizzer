Thlaspi gaudinianum

Thlaspi gaudinianum, commonly known as the Gaudin's cress, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features basal leaves that are kidney-shaped and often covered in a powdery coating. Its small, yellow flowers are arranged in racemes and bloom in early spring.

It is native to regions of Europe and is often found in moist, calcareous soils.
