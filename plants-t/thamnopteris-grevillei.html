Thamnopteris grevillei

Thamnopteris grevillei, commonly known as the Greville's shield fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds that are typically leathery and dark green. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical rainforests. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Thamnopteris grevillei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
