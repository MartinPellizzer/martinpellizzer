Thouina

Thouina, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a unique flowering plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often colorful blooms. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air. Its flowers come in a variety of hues, including white, pink, and purple, making it a visually appealing species.

Thouina is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
