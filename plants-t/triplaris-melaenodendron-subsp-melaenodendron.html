Triplaris melaenodendron subsp. melaenodendron

Triplaris melaenodendron subsp.

melaenodendron, commonly known as the black-leaved triplaris, is a small evergreen tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America. It is characterized by its dark, glossy foliage and small, clustered flowers that bloom in white or pale yellow.

The plant produces small, round fruits that contain a single seed, and it is often found in moist, shaded environments. It has a compact growth habit and is valued for its ornamental appeal in tropical gardens.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in coastal areas with high humidity.
