Triticum × semisecale

Triticum × semisecale, commonly known as intermediate wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the northern hemisphere.

It is a hybrid between wheat (Triticum aestivum) and wild grasses, exhibiting characteristics of both parents. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spikelets that contain small grains. It is valued for its hardiness, drought tolerance, and potential as a forage crop.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in agricultural and ecological research.
