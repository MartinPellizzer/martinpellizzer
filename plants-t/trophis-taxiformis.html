Trophis taxiformis

Trophis taxiformis, commonly known as the slender tamarind, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a small, deciduous tree or shrub that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by elongated, woody pods containing edible seeds. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of elliptical leaflets.

The plant is valued for its hardy nature and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
