Trifolium pratense var. pyrenaicum

Trifolium pratense var. pyrenaicum, commonly known as the Pyrenean red clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pyrenees region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged on a central stalk. The plant produces small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting biodiversity.
