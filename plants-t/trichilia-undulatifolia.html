Trichilia undulatifolia

Trichilia undulatifolia, commonly known as the wavy-leaf trichilia, is a tropical tree native to Africa and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, undulating leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds.

Trichilia undulatifolia is often valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local medicine.
