Trichosanthes rosthornii var. rosthornii

Trichosanthes rosthornii var. rosthornii, commonly known as the Chinese lantern gourd, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its large, orange-red, inflated fruit that resembles a lantern, which is both ornamental and edible. The plant is a vine with tendrils that climb or trail along the ground, and its leaves are heart-shaped with a rough texture. It produces yellow flowers with five petals, which are often followed by the distinctive gourd fruits.

The plant is cultivated for its ornamental value and as a food source in traditional cuisines.
