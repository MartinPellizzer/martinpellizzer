Thymus sibthorpii var. substriatus

Thymus sibthorpii var. substriatus, commonly known as the striated thrift, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense, compact foliage with small, aromatic leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its hardiness and ability to withstand drought conditions.
