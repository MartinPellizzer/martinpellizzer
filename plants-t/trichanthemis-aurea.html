Trichanthemis aurea

Trichanthemis aurea, commonly known as the golden trichanthemum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant features narrow, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Trichanthemis aurea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
