Trichocereus glaucus var. pendens

Trichocereus glaucus var. pendens, commonly known as the "Moon Cactus" or "Pendulous Trichocereus," is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems that often hang downward, giving it a unique, cascading appearance. The plant produces striking white to pale pink flowers during the night, which are typical of its cacti family. It is a member of the Cactaceae family and is known for its ability to store water in its fleshy stems.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens with well-draining soil.
