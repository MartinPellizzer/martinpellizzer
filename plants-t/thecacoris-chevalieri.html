Thecacoris chevalieri

Thecacoris chevalieri, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique and intricate floral structure. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are often brightly colored, with a complex arrangement of petals and sepals that aid in pollination.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
