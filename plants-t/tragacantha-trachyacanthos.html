Tragacantha trachyacanthos

Tragacantha trachyacanthos, commonly known as the thorny tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody, thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by pods containing seeds. Its roots are known for producing a viscous gum, which has been historically used in various medicinal and industrial applications.

Tragacantha trachyacanthos is often found in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.
