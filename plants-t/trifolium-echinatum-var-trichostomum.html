Trifolium echinatum var. trichostomum

Trifolium echinatum var. trichostomum, commonly known as the hairy clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemes, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
