Tetrahit grandiflorum

Tetrahit grandiflorum, commonly known as the grandiflorum tetrahit, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, showy flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces clusters of pale blue to violet petals, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Tetrahit grandiflorum thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
