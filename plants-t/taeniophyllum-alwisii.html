Taeniophyllum alwisii

Taeniophyllum alwisii, commonly known as the Malabar button fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit. The plant features delicate, lanceolate fronds that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on tree trunks or rocks in rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated as a houseplant.
