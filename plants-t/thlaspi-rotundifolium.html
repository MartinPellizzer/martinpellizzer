Thlaspi rotundifolium

Thlaspi rotundifolium, commonly known as the roundedleaf cabbageweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of rounded, fleshy leaves and small, yellow flowers that appear in late spring. The plant is typically found in disturbed soils and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Thlaspi rotundifolium is adapted to thrive in temperate climates and can be found in various regions across Europe and parts of Asia.
