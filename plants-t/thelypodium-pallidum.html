Thelypodium pallidum

Thelypodium pallidum, commonly known as the pale thelypodium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, pale yellow flowers are arranged in loose clusters and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Thelypodium pallidum is noted for its delicate appearance and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
