Taraxacum procerum

Taraxacum procerum, commonly known as the tall dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its upright stem and yellow composite flowers that develop into distinctive seed heads. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this plant thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a hairy texture.

It is often found in meadows, roadsides, and other sunny areas where it can spread via wind-dispersed seeds.
