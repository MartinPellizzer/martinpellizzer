Tamarix nilotica var. micrantha

Tamarix nilotica var. micrantha, commonly known as the small-flowered tamarisk, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its dense, feathery foliage and small, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has small, scale-like leaves that reduce water loss, making it well-adapted to drought conditions. It is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in poor soils.

Additionally, it plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and is sometimes used in phytoremediation efforts.
