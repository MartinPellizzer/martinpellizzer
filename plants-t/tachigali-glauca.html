Tachigali glauca

Tachigali glauca, commonly known as the white tachigali, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and often nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets and produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in racemose inflorescences. Its fruit is a pod that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.

Tachigali glauca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in reforestation efforts due to its adaptability and ecological benefits.
