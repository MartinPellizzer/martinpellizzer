Tetrapogon pleiostachyus

Tetrapogon pleiostachyus, commonly known as the many-stemmed grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It is typically found in arid or semi-arid regions, where it thrives in well-drained soils.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes used in restoration projects to stabilize soil and prevent erosion.
