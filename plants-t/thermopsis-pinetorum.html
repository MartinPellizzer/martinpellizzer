Thermopsis pinetorum

Thermopsis pinetorum, commonly known as the pineleaf thermopsis, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
