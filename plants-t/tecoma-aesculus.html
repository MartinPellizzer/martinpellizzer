Tecoma aesculus

Tecoma aesculus, commonly known as the yellow trumpetbush, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, orange-yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves with serrated margins and a rough texture. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

Tecoma aesculus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adaptable to a variety of soil types.
