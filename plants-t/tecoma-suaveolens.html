Tecoma suaveolens

Tecoma suaveolens, commonly known as the yellow trumpetbush, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height. The plant produces showy, yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Tecoma suaveolens is adapted to sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed sites.
