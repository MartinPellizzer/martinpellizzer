Trichotosia fusca

Trichotosia fusca, commonly known as the hairy false indigo, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale blue to purple in color. Trichotosia fusca is often found in moist, woodland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.

It also has the ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility.
