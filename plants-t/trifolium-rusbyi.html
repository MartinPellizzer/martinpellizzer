Trifolium rusbyi

Trifolium rusbyi, commonly known as Rusby's clover, is a flowering plant species native to specific regions of the United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically white or pale purple in color. Trifolium rusbyi is often found in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields, where it thrives in well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of interest for botanists and ecologists.
