Taxus baccata f. stricta

Taxus baccata f. stricta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved yew, is a cultivar of the European yew tree.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and narrow, dark green foliage. The leaves are typically shorter and more elongated compared to the typical species, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, reddish berries that are toxic to humans and animals.

This variety is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
