Taraxacum serratilobum

Taraxacum serratilobum, commonly known as the serrated lobed dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated, lobed leaves and a yellow composite flower head that develops into a crown of white pappus. Native to parts of Asia, it thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils. The plant is known for its medicinal properties, with various parts used in traditional herbal remedies.

Its life cycle includes a period of dormancy during harsh weather, allowing it to survive in diverse environmental conditions.
