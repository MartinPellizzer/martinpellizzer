Thymus serpyllum f. insularis

Thymus serpyllum f. insularis, commonly known as the island thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to certain Mediterranean islands.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically gray-green in color, covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for horticultural use.
