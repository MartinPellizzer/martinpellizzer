Tithonia angustifolia

Tithonia angustifolia, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a tropical plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant is an annual herb that thrives in warm climates and well-drained soils. Its rapid growth makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and as a cover crop.

Tithonia angustifolia also has medicinal uses in traditional medicine.
