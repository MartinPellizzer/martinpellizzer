Taraxacum angulare

Taraxacum angulare, commonly known as the angular dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive angular leaves and yellow composite flowers that develop into fluffy seed heads. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this plant thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. It has a taproot system and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and wastelands.

The plant is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
