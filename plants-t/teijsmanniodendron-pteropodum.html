Teijsmanniodendron pteropodum

Teijsmanniodendron pteropodum, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds.

This species is often found in lowland rainforests and is noted for its ecological importance in its native habitat.
