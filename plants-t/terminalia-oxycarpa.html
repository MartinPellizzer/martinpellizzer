Terminalia oxycarpa

Terminalia oxycarpa, commonly known as the sour fig or wild date, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, orange-red fruits that are rich in sugars and acids.

Its botanical features make it valuable for both ecological and commercial purposes, including traditional medicine and food.
