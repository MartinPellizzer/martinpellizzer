Thryothamnus junciformis

Thryothamnus junciformis, commonly known as the narrowleaf desert sage, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink to purple in color, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Thryothamnus junciformis plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats.
