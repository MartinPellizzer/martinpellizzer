Thymus sipyleus var. imbricatus

Thymus sipyleus var. imbricatus, commonly known as the Sipylean thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and square stems. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover due to its low-growing habit.
