Traxilisa

Traxilisa, commonly known as the silverleaf vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. Traxilisa is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and pleasant scent.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
