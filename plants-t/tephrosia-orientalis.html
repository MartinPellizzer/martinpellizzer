Tephrosia orientalis

Tephrosia orientalis, commonly known as the oriental tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height, featuring woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, which bloom during the wet season. Its root system is known for containing compounds with insecticidal properties, making it valuable in traditional agriculture.

Tephrosia orientalis is also used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.
