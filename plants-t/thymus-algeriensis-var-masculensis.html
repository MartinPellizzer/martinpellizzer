Thymus algeriensis var. masculensis

Thymus algeriensis var. masculensis, commonly known as Algerian thyme, is a perennial herb native to North Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, small, needle-like leaves. The plant produces pink to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its essential oils are valued for their culinary and medicinal uses.
