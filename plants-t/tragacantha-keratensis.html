Tragacantha keratensis

Tragacantha keratensis, commonly known as the Keratensian tragacanth, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing seeds.

Its roots are known for secreting a viscous gum, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive.
