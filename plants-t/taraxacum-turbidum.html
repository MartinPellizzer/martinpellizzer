Taraxacum turbidum

Taraxacum turbidum, commonly known as the turbid dandelion, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and serrated leaves. The plant produces a distinctive seed head with parachute-like seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and gardens.

Its botanical features make it a common and recognizable weed in many regions.
