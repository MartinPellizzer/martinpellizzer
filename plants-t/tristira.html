Tristira

Tristira, commonly known as the false hibiscus, is a genus of flowering plants in the mallow family, Malvaceae.

These plants are native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. They typically feature showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The leaves are usually palmately lobed, and the stems are often hairy.

Tristira species are often cultivated for their ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
