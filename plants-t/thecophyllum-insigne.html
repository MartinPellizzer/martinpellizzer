Thecophyllum insigne

Thecophyllum insigne, commonly known as the golden cup fern, is a rare and striking member of the Polypodiaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, golden-yellow fronds that resemble delicate cups, which are composed of overlapping, leathery segments. Native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, this plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on tree trunks or rocky surfaces. Its unique morphology and vibrant coloration make it a sought-after specimen in botanical gardens and conservatories.

The plant's reproductive structures, including its spore-producing sorus, are also notable for their intricate arrangement and aesthetic appeal.
