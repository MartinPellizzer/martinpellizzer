Thespesia populnea var. typica

Thespesia populnea var. typica, commonly known as the Tahitian hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its large, showy flowers. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its flowers are typically pink or red with a yellow center. It thrives in coastal areas and is often found in sandy soils, making it well-adapted to tropical climates.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
