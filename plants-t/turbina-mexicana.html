Turbina mexicana

Turbina mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican turban snail, is a species of air-breathing land snail.

It belongs to the family Cyclophoridae and is characterized by its large, spiral-shaped shell. The shell is typically reddish-brown with a smooth surface and a distinct, thickened lip. Native to Mexico, this snail thrives in humid, tropical environments.

It plays an important role in its ecosystem by contributing to the decomposition of organic matter.
