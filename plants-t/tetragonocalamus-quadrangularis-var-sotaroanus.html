Tetragonocalamus quadrangularis var. sotaroanus

Tetragonocalamus quadrangularis var. sotaroanus, commonly known as the Sotaro bamboo, is a rare and ornamental bamboo species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, upright culms that are typically green with a smooth, almost glassy surface. The plant forms dense clumps and has narrow, linear leaves that are often a vibrant green. It is well-suited for container gardening and can thrive in well-drained soil with partial to full sunlight.

Due to its rarity and aesthetic appeal, it is highly sought after by collectors and botanists.
