Theodorea gomezoides

Theodorea gomezoides, commonly known as the Gomezoides orchid, is a flowering plant species native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pale pink. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions, often found in cloud forests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
