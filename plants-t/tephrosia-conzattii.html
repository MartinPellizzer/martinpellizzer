Tephrosia conzattii

Tephrosia conzattii, commonly known as the Mexican tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Tephrosia conzattii is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility.
