Tydaea × regelii var. erythrichina

Tydaea × regelii var. erythrichina, commonly known as the red-stemmed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central Asia.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and wet meadows, and is characterized by its grass-like leaves and cylindrical inflorescence. The plant features distinctive reddish stems and produces small, three-lobed flowers in clusters.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in water gardens or as a ground cover in moist landscapes.
