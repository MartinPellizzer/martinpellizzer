Trichoptilium incisum

Trichoptilium incisum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed gentian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with serrated edges, and it produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Trichoptilium incisum is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
