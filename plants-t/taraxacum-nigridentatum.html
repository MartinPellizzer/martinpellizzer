Taraxacum nigridentatum

Taraxacum nigridentatum, commonly known as the black-toothed dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its yellow composite flower head and heart-shaped basal leaves. The plant is native to parts of Europe and is often found in meadows and grassy areas. Its leaves have a distinctive dark green color with a slightly serrated edge.

The species is notable for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting pollinators.
