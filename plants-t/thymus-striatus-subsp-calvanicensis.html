Thymus striatus subsp. calvanicensis

Thymus striatus subsp.

calvanicensis, commonly known as the Corsican thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features aromatic, needle-like leaves and produces small, pinkish-white flowers in early summer.

The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils. It is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, similar to other thyme species.

Its subspecific name, *calvanicensis*, refers to its origin in the Calvana area of Corsica.
