Tovara virginiana var. glaberrima

Tovara virginiana var. glaberrima, commonly known as the smooth tovara, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbel-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with smooth, hairless stems and finely divided, fern-like leaves. Its small white flowers appear in late spring to early summer, and it produces small, dry fruits known as schizocarps.

This variety is often found in moist, sandy soils along streambanks and in open woodlands.
