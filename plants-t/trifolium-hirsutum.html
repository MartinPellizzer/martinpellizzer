Trifolium hirsutum

Trifolium hirsutum, commonly known as hairy clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets, often with a hairy texture. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in early spring. It is native to Europe and western Asia but has been introduced to other regions, including North America.

Trifolium hirsutum is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
