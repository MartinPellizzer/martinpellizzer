Triticum durum var. arraseita

Triticum durum var. arraseita, commonly known as Arraseita durum, is a variant of durum wheat characterized by its unique botanical features.

It is a hexaploid species belonging to the Poaceae family, known for its hard grains and high gluten content. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 1.5 meters, with narrow, grass-like leaves and spikelets that bear small, tough grains. It thrives in temperate climates and is adapted to well-drained soils.

This variety is particularly valued for its use in pasta and semolina production due to its strong gluten structure.
