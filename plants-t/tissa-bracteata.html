Tissa bracteata

Tissa bracteata, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Tissa bracteata is known for its rapid growth and is often considered an invasive species in some areas.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is commonly found in forests and along stream banks.
