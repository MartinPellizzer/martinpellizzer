Toulicia reticulata

Toulicia reticulata, commonly known as the net-veined lily, is a striking flowering plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, with intricate reticulate patterns on the petals. The plant has broad, strap-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in marshy areas near water bodies.

Its unique floral structure and ornamental value make it a popular choice for botanical gardens and water garden enthusiasts.
