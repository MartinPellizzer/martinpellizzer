Trochostigma argutum

Trochostigma argutum, commonly known as the rough-stemmed orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are small, clustered in inflorescences, and typically have a greenish or white color with a faint fragrance.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
