Tournefortia multiflora

Tournefortia multiflora, commonly known as the coastal hibiscus, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in sandy or coastal soils and is often found in salt-tolerant environments. Its flowers bloom in clusters, and the plant is known for its ability to withstand harsh coastal conditions.

Tournefortia multiflora is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
