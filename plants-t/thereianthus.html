Thereianthus

Thereianthus, commonly known as the "blue-eyed grass," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Xanthorrhoeaceae.

It is characterized by its small, showy flowers that often resemble those of the iris or orchid, with vibrant blue or purple petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as wetlands or forest floors, and is native to regions in Africa and the Americas. Thereianthus species are herbaceous perennials, often with grass-like leaves and a short, upright stem.

They play an important ecological role in their habitats by providing nectar for pollinators and stabilizing soil in wet areas.
