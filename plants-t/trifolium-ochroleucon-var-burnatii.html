Trifolium ochroleucon var. burnatii

Trifolium ochroleucon var. burnatii, commonly known as Burnat's yellow trefoil, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in early spring, often forming dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky habitats.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
