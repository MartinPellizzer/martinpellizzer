Thymus rochelianus

Thymus rochelianus, commonly known as Rochel's thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and aromatic, gray-green foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is often cultivated in horticultural settings.
