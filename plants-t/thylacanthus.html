Thylacanthus

Thylacanthus, commonly known as the honeycomb plant, is a unique succulent native to South Africa.

It features a distinctive, branching structure with a central stem and numerous whorls of leaves that resemble a honeycomb. The plant's leaves are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water in arid environments. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a subtle ornamental touch.

Thylacanthus is valued for its resilience and ability to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils.
