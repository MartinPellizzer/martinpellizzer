Thelypteris tamandarei

Thelypteris tamandarei, commonly known as the Tamanduara holly fern, is a species of fern native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Thelypteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically bi- or tri-pinnate. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in forest understories where it can receive indirect sunlight. Its spores are dispersed through the air, and the plant reproduces primarily through sexual reproduction via spores rather than seeds.

Thelypteris tamandarei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
