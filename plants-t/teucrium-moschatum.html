Teucrium moschatum

Teucrium moschatum, commonly known as musk nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its aromatic, often hairy leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showing a preference for well-drained soils. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its square stems and fragrant properties.

Teucrium moschatum is also noted for its medicinal uses among indigenous communities.
