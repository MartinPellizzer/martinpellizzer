Tydaea amabilis

Tydaea amabilis, commonly known as the sweet grass, is a perennial grass species native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with a soft blue or silver hue. The plant produces small, fluffy seed heads that are released by wind, aiding in its dispersal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks.

Tydaea amabilis is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used by indigenous peoples for ceremonial and medicinal purposes.
