Trisetella triaristella

Trisetella triaristella, commonly known as the three-bracted grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and smooth, with a distinct sheathing base.

Trisetella triaristella is native to parts of Africa and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.
