Thymus euboeus

Thymus euboeus, commonly known as Euboean thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Greek island of Euboea.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in the summer months. Its leaves are fragrant and contain essential oils, making it valuable in both culinary and medicinal applications.

Thymus euboeus thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or limestone-rich environments.
