Teucrium nuchense

Teucrium nuchense, commonly known as the Brazilian germander, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its slender stems, opposite leaves, and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open areas such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Teucrium nuchense is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
