Tulipa sylvestris subsp. balcanica

Tulipa sylvestris subsp.

balcanica, commonly known as the Balkan tulip, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It typically grows in woodland and meadow habitats, preferring well-drained, loamy soils.

The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, often with a distinctive yellow or orange stripe at the base. Its bulbs are adapted to survive harsh winters, and it blooms in early spring.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
