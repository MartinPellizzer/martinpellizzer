Tetragonanthus umbellatus

Tetragonanthus umbellatus, commonly known as the umbrella tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that resemble an umbrella, giving it its common name. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often glossy and dark green in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Tetragonanthus umbellatus is also valued for its ability to purify indoor air.
