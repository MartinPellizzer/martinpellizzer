Taraxacum candidum

Taraxacum candidum, commonly known as the white dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flower heads and heart-shaped basal leaves. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and disturbed soils. It produces a milky sap that is characteristic of the genus Taraxacum.

The white dandelion is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
