Tragacantha Vaga

Tragacantha vaga is a lesser-known yet fascinating species of flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions. It belongs to the legume family and is known for its unique adaptations to harsh environments. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind. Its ability to thrive in low-nutrient soils makes it an important component of desert ecosystems. Despite its ecological significance, Tragacantha vaga remains under-researched and often overlooked in botanical studies.
