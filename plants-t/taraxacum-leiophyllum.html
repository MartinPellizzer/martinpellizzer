Taraxacum leiophyllum

Taraxacum leiophyllum, commonly known as the silky dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to western North America and is characterized by its soft, hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellow composite flowers that develop into fluffy white seed heads. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including meadows, roadsides, and disturbed soils.

Due to its delicate appearance and ecological role, it is often found in open, sunny environments.
