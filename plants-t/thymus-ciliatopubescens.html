Thymus ciliatopubescens

Thymus ciliatopubescens, commonly known as the hairy-leaved thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and aromatic, small, dark green leaves covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of pink to purple flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its fragrant foliage and long blooming period.
