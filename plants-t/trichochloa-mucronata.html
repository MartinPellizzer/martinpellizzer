Trichochloa mucronata

Trichochloa mucronata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed grass or mucronated grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to yellowish flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Trichochloa mucronata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its fine texture and adaptability.
