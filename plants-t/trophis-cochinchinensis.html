Trophis cochinchinensis

Trophis cochinchinensis, commonly known as the Vietnamese orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its elegant, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern.

The flowers are often pale yellow or white, with a distinctive lip that aids in pollination.
