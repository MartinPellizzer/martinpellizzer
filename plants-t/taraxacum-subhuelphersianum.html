Taraxacum subhuelphersianum

Taraxacum subhuelphersianum, commonly known as the Persian dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to parts of Central Asia, including regions of Iran and Afghanistan. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and a single, hollow stem that supports a yellow flower head. It produces wind-dispersed seeds with feathery pappus, typical of the dandelion genus.

This species is often found in arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.
