Tulipa stenopetala

Tulipa stenopetala, commonly known as the narrow-petaled tulip, is a species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded bulbs and produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of red, yellow, and orange. The plant typically blooms in early spring and has narrow, strap-like leaves that are evergreen in some climates. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often cultivated in temperate regions.
