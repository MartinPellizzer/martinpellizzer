Tragacantha gombo

Tragacantha gombo, commonly known as gum arabic tree, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is native to the Arabian Peninsula and parts of the Middle East. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, with small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. It produces a milky sap that is harvested as gum arabic, a valuable natural gum used in food, pharmaceuticals, and cosmetics.

The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it an important species in desert ecosystems.
