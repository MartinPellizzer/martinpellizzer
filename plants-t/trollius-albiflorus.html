Trollius albiflorus

Trollius albiflorus, commonly known as the white globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, globe-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has basal leaves that are broadly ovate and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine meadows.

Trollius albiflorus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
