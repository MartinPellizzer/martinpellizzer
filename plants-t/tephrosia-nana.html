Tephrosia nana

Tephrosia nana, commonly known as dwarf tephrosia, is a small, herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It typically grows to a height of less than 30 centimeters and features slender, hairy stems. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the rainy season. Its leaves are opposite and pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Tephrosia nana is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in partial shade to full sun conditions.
