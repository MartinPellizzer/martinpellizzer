Taeniophyllum coxii

Taeniophyllum coxii, commonly known as the slender mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees, primarily in tropical and subtropical regions.

It has small, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along its stems. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers that are often clustered together, followed by berries that are dispersed by birds. Its roots penetrate the host tree's vascular system to extract water and nutrients, while still performing photosynthesis.

This unique relationship with its host makes it an important component of forest ecosystems.
