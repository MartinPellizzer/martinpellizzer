Tragacantha distans

Tragacantha distans, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to regions of the Middle East and parts of Central Asia, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid climates. The plant is characterized by its woody structure, compound leaves, and small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in spring. It produces a resinous sap, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive.

The plant's ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
