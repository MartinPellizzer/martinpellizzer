Tetrastigma apiculatum var. pubescens

Tetrastigma apiculatum var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy climbing vine, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in panicles. Its berries are initially green and turn red when mature, containing several seeds.

This vine is often found in moist, shaded forest environments and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
