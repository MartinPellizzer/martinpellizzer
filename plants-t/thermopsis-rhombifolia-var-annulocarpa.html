Thermopsis rhombifolia var. annulocarpa

Thermopsis rhombifolia var. annulocarpa, commonly known as the ringed yellow gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces striking yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in dense, terminal racemes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
