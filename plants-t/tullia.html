Tullia

Tullia, commonly known as the common tullia or Tullia multifida, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Apiaceae, which includes parsley and carrots, and is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family. Tullia is often grown as an ornamental plant for its delicate foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.

It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight.
