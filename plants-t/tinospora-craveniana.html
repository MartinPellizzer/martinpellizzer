Tinospora craveniana

Tinospora craveniana, commonly known as the Indian creeper or Tinospora, is a flowering plant belonging to the Menispermaceae family.

It is a deciduous climbing shrub characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe, containing several seeds. Tinospora craveniana is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

It has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
