Typhonium sagittariifolium

Typhonium sagittariifolium, commonly known as the arrowleaf elephant's foot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by spiny, purple-brown berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique appearance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for botanical gardens and collectors.
