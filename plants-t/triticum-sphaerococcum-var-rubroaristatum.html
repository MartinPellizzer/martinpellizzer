Triticum sphaerococcum var. rubroaristatum

Triticum sphaerococcum var. rubroaristatum, commonly known as the red-bracted wheatgrass, is a rare and distinctive variety of wheatgrass characterized by its unique inflorescence.

It features dense, spherical spikelets that are often reddish in color, giving it its common name. The plant is typically found in arid and semi-arid regions, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its botanical features include narrow, grass-like leaves and compact, club-shaped flower clusters.

This variety is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its ornamental value and potential for cultivation in specialized environments.
