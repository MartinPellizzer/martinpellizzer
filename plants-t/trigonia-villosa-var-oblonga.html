Trigonia villosa var. oblonga

Trigonia villosa var. oblonga, commonly known as the woolly trigonia, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections for its unique foliage and floral display.
