Thlaspi perfoliatum subsp. erraticum

Thlaspi perfoliatum subsp.

erraticum, commonly known as the devil's-bit scabious, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its basal rosette of lobed leaves and its showy clusters of blue to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in disturbed or open areas.

The subspecies erraticum is distinguished by its hairy stems and slightly smaller flowers compared to the typical form.
