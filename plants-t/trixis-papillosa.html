Trixis papillosa

Trixis papillosa, commonly known as the silky trixis, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its unique morphology helps it retain moisture and reduce water loss.

Trixis papillosa plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and shelter for small animals.
