Trisetella dalstroemii

Trisetella dalstroemii, commonly known as the slender feather grass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are typically less than 10 cm in length, with a distinctive blue-green coloration. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters during the spring and summer seasons. Its fibrous root system helps it survive in poor soils and withstand drought conditions.

Trisetella dalstroemii is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soils and provide habitat for native fauna.
