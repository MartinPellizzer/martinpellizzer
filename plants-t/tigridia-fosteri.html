Tigridia fosteri

Tigridia fosteri, commonly known as the Foster's tiger flower, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, brightly colored flowers that resemble tiger stripes. The plant produces showy, tubular blooms in shades of red, orange, and yellow, often with a contrasting black or dark stripe down the center. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Tigridia fosteri is also noted for its ability to bloom multiple times during the growing season.
