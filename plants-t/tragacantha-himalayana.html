Tragacantha himalayana

Tragacantha himalayana, commonly known as the Himalayan tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are followed by pods containing small, rounded seeds. It is known for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments, making it an important species in ecological restoration projects.

Its latex, similar to that of other tragacanth species, has been used historically in various medicinal and industrial applications.
