Trema melastomatifolium

Trema melastomatifolium, commonly known as the red-flowered trema, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Melastomataceae and is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has opposite, oval-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and it often grows in moist, shaded environments. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.
