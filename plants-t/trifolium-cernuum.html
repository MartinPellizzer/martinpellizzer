Trifolium cernuum

Trifolium cernuum, commonly known as nodding clover, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets, and its drooping, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks. It is a member of the legume family, capable of fixing nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with Rhizobium bacteria.

Its delicate appearance and ecological importance make it a notable species in botanical studies.
