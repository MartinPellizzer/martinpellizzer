Trifolium abyssinicum

Trifolium abyssinicum, commonly known as Ethiopian clover, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in grasslands and mountainous regions.

Trifolium abyssinicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in soil stabilization.
