Trichotosia pilifera

Trichotosia pilifera, commonly known as the silky hairgrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family (Poaceae).

It is characterized by its fine, silky hairs that cover its leaves and inflorescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. Native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile, it thrives in wetland and marshy environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of grasses.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
