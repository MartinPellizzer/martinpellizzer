Teucrium odoratum

Teucrium odoratum, commonly known as sweet germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink to violet in color, arranged in dense terminal clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in rocky or dry habitats.

Teucrium odoratum is valued for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
