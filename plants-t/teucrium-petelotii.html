Teucrium petelotii

Teucrium petelotii, commonly known as the Mexican germander, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its small, tubular flowers are usually purple or blue, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Teucrium petelotii is valued in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.
