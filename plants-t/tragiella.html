Tragiella

Tragiella, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It consists of several species, most notably Tragiella glabra, which is native to southern Africa. The plant is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and trifoliate leaves, which are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. Tragiella species are typically shrubs or small trees, growing in arid or semi-arid environments.

They are valued for their hardy nature and are sometimes used in traditional medicine or as ornamental plants.
