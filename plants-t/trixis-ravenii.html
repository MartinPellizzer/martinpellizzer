Trixis ravenii

Trixis ravenii, commonly known as the Raven's Tail, is a striking plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and clusters of vibrant blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has fibrous roots that help it survive in dry conditions, making it well adapted to its harsh environment. Its leaves are narrow and covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which reduce water loss.

Trixis ravenii is often used in ornamental gardening due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
