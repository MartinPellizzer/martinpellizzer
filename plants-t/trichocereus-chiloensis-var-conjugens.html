Trichocereus chiloensis var. conjugens

Trichocereus chiloensis var. conjugens, commonly known as the "Peruvian torch cactus," is a species native to the coastal regions of Chile and Peru.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its smooth, green stems and sparse, spiny areoles. The plant produces large, night-blooming flowers that are typically white or pale pink, often with a strong fragrance. It thrives in arid environments with well-drained soil and requires minimal water.

Due to its striking appearance and cultural significance, it is a popular choice for both ornamental and horticultural cultivation.
