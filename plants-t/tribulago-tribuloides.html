Tribulago tribuloides

Tribulago tribuloides, commonly known as the African spurge, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its hairy stems and alternate leaves that are typically lance-shaped. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle the plant with care.
