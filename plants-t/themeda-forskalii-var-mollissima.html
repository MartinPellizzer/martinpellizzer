Themeda forskalii var. mollissima

Themeda forskalii var. mollissima, commonly known as the silky grass or silky kangaroo grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its fine, silky-textured leaves and compact growth habit. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in grasslands and open woodlands. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical spikelet that produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
