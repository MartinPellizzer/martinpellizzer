Trisetum flavescens var. triflorum

Trisetum flavescens var. triflorum, commonly known as the three-flowered blue grass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and its distinctive inflorescence, which consists of three to five spikelets. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a soft texture, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is often found in prairie ecosystems and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role.
