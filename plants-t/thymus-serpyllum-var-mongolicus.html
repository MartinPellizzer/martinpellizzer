Thymus serpyllum var. mongolicus

Thymus serpyllum var. mongolicus, commonly known as Mongolian thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its creeping growth habit and small, fragrant leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces purple or pink flowers in spikes, which bloom in early summer. It is native to regions of Central Asia, including Mongolia and parts of Russia.

This variety is valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
