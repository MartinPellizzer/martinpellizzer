Tabernaemontana ciliata

Tabernaemontana ciliata, commonly known as the velvet tangerine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are simple, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and citrus-like fragrance.
