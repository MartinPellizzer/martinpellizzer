Triticum youngii

Triticum youngii, commonly known as Young's wheat, is a rare and ancient species of wild wheat native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender stalks and small, spikelet-like inflorescence, which contains both male and female reproductive structures. The plant produces small, glabrous grains that are typically pale yellow to brown in color. Triticum youngii is considered a valuable genetic resource for wheat breeding due to its potential for improving disease resistance and adaptability.

Its botanical features make it an important subject of study in the field of plant genetics and evolutionary biology.
