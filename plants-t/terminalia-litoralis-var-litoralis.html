Terminalia litoralis var. litoralis

Terminalia litoralis var. litoralis, commonly known as the mangrove apple or sea apple, is a tropical tree native to coastal regions of Southeast Asia and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and long, drooping branches that often grow in dense clusters. The plant has simple, oval-shaped leaves that are alternate and have a slightly waxy texture. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, fleshy fruits that are typically orange or red in color.

This species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial organisms.
