Thymus kabylicus

Thymus kabylicus, commonly known as Kabylic thyme, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are fragrant and contain essential oils, making it valuable for culinary and medicinal uses.

Thymus kabylicus thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine habitats.
