Tricholaena teneriffae var. sericea

Tricholaena teneriffae var. sericea, commonly known as the silky tricholaena, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its dense, fibrous roots and its upright, woody stems that often branch near the base. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silky hair, giving the plant its common name. It produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, round fruits.

This plant is often found in coastal and rocky habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
