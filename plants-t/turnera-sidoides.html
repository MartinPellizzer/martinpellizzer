Turnera sidoides

Turnera sidoides, commonly known as the golden torch or Mexican flame flower, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling a torch. The plant has heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Turnera sidoides is also noted for its medicinal properties, with various parts of the plant used in traditional medicine.
