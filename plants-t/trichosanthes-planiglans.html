Trichosanthes planiglans

Trichosanthes planiglans, commonly known as the flat-leaved trumpet vine, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, broad leaves and large, pale yellow flowers that resemble trumpets. The plant produces elongated, fleshy fruits that are typically orange or red in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded areas.

Trichosanthes planiglans is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
