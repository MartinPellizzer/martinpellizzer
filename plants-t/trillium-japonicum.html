Trillium japonicum

Trillium japonicum, commonly known as Japanese trillium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It features three broad, waxy leaves that are typically bright green and arranged in a whorl at the base. The plant produces a single, showy white or pink flower with three petals and three sepals, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
