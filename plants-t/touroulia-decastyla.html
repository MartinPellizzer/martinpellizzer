Touroulia decastyla

Touroulia decastyla, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Touroulia decastyla is often found in disturbed habitats and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
