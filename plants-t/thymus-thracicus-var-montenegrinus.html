Thymus thracicus var. montenegrinus

Thymus thracicus var. montenegrinus, commonly known as Montenegro thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or mountainous regions. Its essential oils contain thymol and carvacrol, contributing to its medicinal and culinary uses.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
