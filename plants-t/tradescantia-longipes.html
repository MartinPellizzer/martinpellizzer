Tradescantia longipes

Tradescantia longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, showy blue to purple flowers during the summer months. Its foliage is often variegated with white or silver markings, adding visual interest.

Tradescantia longipes is well-suited for moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
