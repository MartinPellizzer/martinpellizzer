Thymus talijevii f. hirticaulis

Thymus talijevii f. hirticaulis, commonly known as the hairy-stemmed thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its low-growing, compact habit and aromatic foliage with small, needle-like leaves. The plant produces violet to pink flowers in terminal clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats such as slopes and meadows.

Due to its ornamental value and aromatic properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
