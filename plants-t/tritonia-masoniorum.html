Tritonia masoniorum

Tritonia masoniorum, commonly known as the Mason's tritonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces clusters of tubular flowers that open in the morning and close at dusk. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sun exposure.

Tritonia masoniorum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable climates.
