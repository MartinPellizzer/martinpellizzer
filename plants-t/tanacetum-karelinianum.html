Tanacetum karelinianum

Tanacetum karelinianum, commonly known as Karelin's tansy, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features finely divided, aromatic leaves and yellow ray flowers surrounded by a dense ring of disk flowers. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
