Tanacetum achilleifolium

Tanacetum achilleifolium, commonly known as yarrow, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white or yellowish flowers. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. It has a strong, aromatic scent and is known for its medicinal properties.

Tanacetum achilleifolium is also a popular garden plant due to its ornamental value and ability to attract beneficial insects.
