Teucrium capitatum var. microcephalum

Teucrium capitatum var. microcephalum, commonly known as the small-headed germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branched stems covered in fine, grayish-green foliage. The plant produces small, clustered flower heads that are typically blue to violet in color, blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
