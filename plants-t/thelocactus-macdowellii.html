Thelocactus macdowellii

Thelocactus macdowellii, commonly known as the MacDowell's locactus, is a rare and unique cactus species native to the deserts of Arizona and Sonora.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a dense layer of white, waxy hair. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its otherwise muted appearance. Its thick, fleshy stems are adapted to store water, allowing it to survive in arid environments.

Due to its limited distribution and vulnerability to habitat loss, it is considered an endangered species.
