Thymus granatensis var. micranthus

Thymus granatensis var. micranthus, commonly known as the Granada thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, narrow leaves that are often gray-green in color, and it produces tiny, pale pink to white flowers in clusters. The plant is characterized by its aromatic foliage, which contains essential oils responsible for its distinctive scent. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and Mediterranean-style landscapes.

This variety is closely related to other thyme species and is valued for both its ornamental and culinary uses.
