Thymus kotschyanus var. behboudianus

Thymus kotschyanus var. behboudianus, commonly known as the Behboudian thyme, is a compact, low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It features aromatic, narrow leaves that are gray-green in color and emit a strong thyme scent when crushed. The plant produces small, pale pink to violet flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is often cultivated for both decorative and therapeutic purposes.
