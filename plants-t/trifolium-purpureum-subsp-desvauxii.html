Trifolium purpureum subsp. desvauxii

Trifolium purpureum subsp.

desvauxii, commonly known as the purple clover subspecies desvauxii, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, typically with three heart-shaped leaflets, and small purple or pink flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant is native to parts of Europe and is often found in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils. It has a deep root system that helps in nitrogen fixation, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
