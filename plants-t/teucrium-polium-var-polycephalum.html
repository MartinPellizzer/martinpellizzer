Teucrium polium var. polycephalum

Teucrium polium var. polycephalum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, gray-green leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or violet, arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its medicinal properties have been historically valued for their antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects.
