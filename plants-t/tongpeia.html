Tongpeia

Tongpeia, commonly known as the Chinese bellflower, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Tongpeia has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It is often used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
