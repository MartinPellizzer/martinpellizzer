Tragacantha nitens

Tragacantha nitens, commonly known as the yellow tragacanth, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its yellowish flowers and woody stems. The plant produces a gum resin, known as tragacanth, which is harvested from its bark and has been used historically in medicine and industry. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

The leaves are trifoliate, and the plant is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
