Thymus trialeticus

Thymus trialeticus, commonly known as the Balkan thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are fragrant and contain essential oils, making it valuable in both culinary and medicinal applications.

Thymus trialeticus thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.
