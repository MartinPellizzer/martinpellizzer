Thamnocalamus chigar

Thamnocalamus chigar, commonly known as the Chigar bamboo, is a species of grass belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand, Vietnam, and Cambodia. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect culms that can grow up to several meters in height. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Thamnocalamus chigar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
