Trithrinax brasiliensis var. acanthocoma

Trithrinax brasiliensis var. acanthocoma, commonly known as the Brazilian palm, is a distinctive palm species native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils and saline conditions. Its inflorescences are terminal and produce small, yellowish flowers that are followed by clustered fruits.

The variety acanthocoma is distinguished by its spiny leaf stems, which provide a unique structural feature.
