Thymus serpyllum var. porphyrogenitus

Thymus serpyllum var. porphyrogenitus, commonly known as purple thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features compact, woody stems covered in small, fragrant, purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant's leaves are narrow, slightly hairy, and emit a strong, aromatic scent when crushed. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary uses, particularly in herbal teas and seasoning.
