Taraxacum kolaense

Taraxacum kolaense, commonly known as the Kola dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and a flowering head that produces numerous small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks. It has a taproot system and is adapted to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

The species is of interest for its potential medicinal properties and ecological role in its native habitat.
