Tagetes scabra

Tagetes scabra, commonly known as roughleaf marigold, is an annual flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and vibrant yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in full sun with well-drained soil. Its flowers are often used in gardens for their ornamental value and insect-repelling properties.

Tagetes scabra is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
