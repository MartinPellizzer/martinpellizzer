Typhonium thatsonense

Typhonium thatsonense, commonly known as the giant arrowroot or swamp arrowroot, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and striking, spatulate-shaped inflorescence. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in swampy areas or along riverbanks. Its rhizomes are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines for their starchy texture.

The plant's unique morphology and ecological adaptability make it an important species in its native habitats.
