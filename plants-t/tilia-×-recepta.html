Tilia × recepta

Tilia × recepta, commonly known as the common lime, is a hybrid deciduous tree belonging to the Tiliaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are typically light green on top and pale underneath. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are followed by spherical, woody fruits. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

Tilia × recepta is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and is also valued for its timber and historical use in traditional medicine.
