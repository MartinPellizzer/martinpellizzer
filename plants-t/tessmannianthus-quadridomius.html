Tessmannianthus quadridomius

Tessmannianthus quadridomius, commonly known as the four-domed tamarind, is a rare and unique plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive four-domed structure, which gives it its scientific name. The plant features compound leaves with lance-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered an endangered species in its natural range.
