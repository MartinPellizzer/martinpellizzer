Thalictrum bauhini subsp. laetum

Thalictrum bauhini subsp.

laetum, commonly known as the showy meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe. It features finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces clusters of small, pale yellow to greenish-white flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Ranunculaceae family.
