Taraxacum erythrospermum subsp. brachyglossum

Taraxacum erythrospermum subsp.

brachyglossum, commonly known as the red-seeded dandelion, is a member of the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers and distinctive red-tinged seeds.

The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually basal, alternate, and have a rough texture.

This subspecies is often found in grasslands and open areas, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
