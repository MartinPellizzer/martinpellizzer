Typhonium fallax

Typhonium fallax, commonly known as the giant arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, arrowhead-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces striking purple or red spadices, which are the flowering structures typical of the Araceae family. Its rhizomes are edible and have a starchy texture similar to taro.

Typhonium fallax thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
