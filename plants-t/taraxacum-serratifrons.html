Taraxacum serratifrons

Taraxacum serratifrons, commonly known as the serrated dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated, elongated leaves and a yellow flowering head that produces a crown of white ray florets. The plant is native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, and thrives in temperate climates. It has a taproot system that allows it to survive in a variety of soil types.

The species is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed areas, where it plays a role in soil stabilization and supports local pollinator populations.
