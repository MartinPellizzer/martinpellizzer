Trichilia micrantha

Trichilia micrantha, commonly known as African mahogany, is a tropical tree native to Africa.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing flat, winged seeds. It is valued for its durable timber and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ecological and economic importance, it is also used in traditional medicine.
