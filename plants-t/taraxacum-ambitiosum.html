Taraxacum ambitiosum

Taraxacum ambitiosum, commonly known as the bold dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its robust, upright stem and large, deeply lobed leaves that form a basal rosette. The plant produces a single, showy yellow flower that blooms in late spring to early summer. Its seeds are dispersed by wind via a parachute-like structure called a pappus.

This species is typically found in disturbed soils and open areas, showcasing its adaptability to various environments.
