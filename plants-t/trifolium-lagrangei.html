Trifolium lagrangei

Trifolium lagrangei, commonly known as Lagrange's clover, is a flowering plant species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, which bloom in early spring.

Trifolium lagrangei is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments, making it a hardy and resilient species.
