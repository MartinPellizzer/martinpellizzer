Thea transnokoensis

Thea transnokoensis, commonly known as the Transnoko tea plant, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central Africa.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional herbal preparations. The plant produces delicate white flowers that bloom in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value. Its botanical classification places it within the Theaceae family, which includes other ornamental and medicinal plants.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare species with potential ecological and pharmacological significance.
