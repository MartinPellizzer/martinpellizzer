Tina phellocarpa

Tina phellocarpa, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, silvery-green leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The silverleaf tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
