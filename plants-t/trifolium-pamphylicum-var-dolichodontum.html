Trifolium pamphylicum var. dolichodontum

Trifolium pamphylicum var. dolichodontum, commonly known as the long-toothed clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically composed of three oval to lance-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in dense, spherical heads, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
