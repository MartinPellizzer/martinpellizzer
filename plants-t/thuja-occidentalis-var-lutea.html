Thuja occidentalis var. lutea

Thuja occidentalis var. lutea, commonly known as the golden thuja, is a variant of the eastern arborvitae.

It is characterized by its distinctive golden-yellow foliage, which contrasts with the typical green color of the standard species. This ornamental tree is native to eastern North America and thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. The leaves are scale-like and tightly clustered, forming a dense, conical shape.

It is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and landscapes.
