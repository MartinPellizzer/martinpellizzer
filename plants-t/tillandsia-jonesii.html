Tillandsia jonesii

Tillandsia jonesii, commonly known as the Jones' air plant, is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on other plants without deriving nutrients from them. The plant features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that are covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help absorb moisture and nutrients from the air. It produces small, bell-shaped inflorescences with showy, violet or purple flowers.

Tillandsia jonesii is popular as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
