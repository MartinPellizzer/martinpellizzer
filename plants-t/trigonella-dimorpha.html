Trigonella dimorpha

Trigonella dimorpha, commonly known as the two-form fenugreek, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

Trigonella dimorpha is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
