Tragacantha cryptantha

Tragacantha cryptantha, commonly known as the desert milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub that thrives in sandy or gravelly soils, often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by pod-like fruits containing seeds. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Tragacantha cryptantha is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and drought conditions, making it a resilient component of desert ecosystems.
