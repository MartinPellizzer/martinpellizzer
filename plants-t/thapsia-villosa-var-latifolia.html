Thapsia villosa var. latifolia

Thapsia villosa var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved thapsia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces tall, slender stems that bear small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
