Tolpis azorica

Tolpis azorica, commonly known as the Azorean Tolpis, is a flowering plant native to the Azores.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in coastal areas. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
