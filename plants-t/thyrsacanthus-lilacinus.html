Thyrsacanthus lilacinus

Thyrsacanthus lilacinus, commonly known as the lilac thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems and large, showy purple to lilac-colored flowers. The plant produces small, green, spiny fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes.
