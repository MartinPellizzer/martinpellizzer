Trachypogon micans

Trachypogon micans, commonly known as the slender hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streams. Its foliage is often used in horticulture for ornamental purposes due to its delicate appearance.

Trachypogon micans plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
