Taeniophyllum fimbriatum

Taeniophyllum fimbriatum, commonly known as the fringed taeniophyllum, is a small, epiphytic orchid native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows on trees or rocks, thriving in humid, shaded environments. The plant features slender, wiry stems with numerous small, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine, hairy texture. Its flowers are usually small and greenish-white, with delicate, fringed petals that give it its common name.

This orchid is noted for its unique appearance and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in specialized collections.
