Tragacantha atrata

Tragacantha atrata, commonly known as the black thorn.

It is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its spiny stems and yellow flowers. The plant produces a gum known as tragacanth, which has been used historically in medicine and as a binding agent. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat for various insects and birds.
