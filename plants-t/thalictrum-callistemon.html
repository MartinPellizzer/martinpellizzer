Thalictrum callistemon

Thalictrum callistemon, commonly known as the golden meadowrue, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 3 feet and prefers moist, well-drained soils in partial to full shade. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to native gardens.

Thalictrum callistemon is known for its ornamental value and ecological benefits in woodland habitats.
