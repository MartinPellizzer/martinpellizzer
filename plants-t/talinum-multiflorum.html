Talinum multiflorum

Talinum multiflorum, commonly known as red-rooted morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking red stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, which bloom in the early morning. Talinum multiflorum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
