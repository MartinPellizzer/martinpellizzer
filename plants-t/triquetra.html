Triquetra

Triquetra, commonly known as the three-leaf clover, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged symmetrically. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soil. Its small, pea-like flowers are usually white or pink, and it produces small, edible pods.

Triquetra is often associated with good luck and is a symbol of prosperity in various cultures.
