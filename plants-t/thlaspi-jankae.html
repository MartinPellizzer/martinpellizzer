Thlaspi jankae

Thlaspi jankae, commonly known as the Janka's pennycress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing herbaceous plant characterized by its rosette of pinnately lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, typical of the mustard family. It is native to Central Asia and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Thlaspi jankae is adapted to arid environments and has a short growing season.
