Tragacantha amherstiana

Tragacantha amherstiana, commonly known as Amherst's tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and woody, branching stems. The plant produces a thick, viscous gum from its bark, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperature fluctuations.

Due to its unique properties and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
