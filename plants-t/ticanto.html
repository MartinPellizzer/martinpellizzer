Ticanto

Ticanto, commonly known as the desert rose, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems that store water. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which bloom during the warm seasons. Its leaves are typically small, oval, and arranged alternately along the stem.

Ticanto is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping and low-maintenance gardens.
