Tulipa patens

Tulipa patens, commonly known as the Persian tulip, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of red, yellow, and orange, often with a distinctive black eye. The plant typically blooms in early spring and has narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a basal rosette. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Tulipa patens is valued for its ornamental beauty and is a hardy species that can thrive in temperate climates.
