Thelymitra luteocilium

Thelymitra luteocilium, commonly known as the yellow-veined sun orchid, is an Australian native plant belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its striking yellow and green floral structure, with intricate veining on its petals and sepals. The plant typically grows in heathlands and open forests, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its flowers bloom during the spring and early summer, adding vibrant color to the landscape.

Thelymitra luteocilium is also notable for its fragrant scent, which attracts pollinators such as bees and birds.
