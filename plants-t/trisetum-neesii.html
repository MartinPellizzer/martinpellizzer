Trisetum neesii

Trisetum neesii, commonly known as the slender quillwort, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and aquatic or semi-aquatic habitat.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in freshwater environments such as ponds and ditches. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets. Its reproductive structures are adapted for water dispersal, and it plays a role in maintaining aquatic ecosystems.

Trisetum neesii is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
