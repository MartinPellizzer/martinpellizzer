Trollius yunnanensis var. anemonifolius

Trollius yunnanensis var. anemonifolius, commonly known as the Yunnan globeflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Yunnan Province in China.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow, globe-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance, and it thrives in well-drained, moist soils. It is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats at elevations above 2,500 meters.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
