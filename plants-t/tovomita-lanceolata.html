Tovomita lanceolata

Tovomita lanceolata, commonly known as the African arrowhead, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features lanceolate leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Tovomita lanceolata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.
