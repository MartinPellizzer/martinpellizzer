Trigonella laxissima

Trigonella laxissima, commonly known as the slender sainfoin, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in steppe and grassland habitats. Trigonella laxissima is a perennial herb that can reach heights of up to 30 centimeters, with a creeping or upright growth habit depending on environmental conditions.
