Trichomanes levissimum

Trichomanes levissimum, commonly known as the slender maidenhair fern, is a small, delicate fern that belongs to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its finely divided, lance-shaped fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and contain spores for propagation.

Trichomanes levissimum is notable for its intricate, lace-like appearance and its role in epiphytic growth on trees and rocks.
