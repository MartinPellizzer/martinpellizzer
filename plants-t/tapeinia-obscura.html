Tapeinia obscura

Tapeinia obscura, commonly known as the obscure tapeinia, is a rare and little-studied fungal plant pathogen.

It belongs to the family Marasmiaceae and is primarily associated with decaying wood in forest ecosystems. This species is characterized by its small, delicate fruiting bodies that are often difficult to identify without specialized knowledge. Its mycelium colonizes dead or decaying plant material, playing a role in nutrient cycling within the ecosystem.

Due to its elusive nature, it remains a subject of ongoing research in mycology.
