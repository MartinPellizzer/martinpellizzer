Trigonella anatolica

Trigonella anatolica, commonly known as Anatolian fenugreek, is an annual leguminous plant native to the Mediterranean and Middle Eastern regions.

It features small, yellow flowers and produces pods containing several seeds. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are often hairy and have a distinct aroma. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid environments.

Trigonella anatolica is valued for its seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.
