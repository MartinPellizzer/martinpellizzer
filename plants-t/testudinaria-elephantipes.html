Testudinaria elephantipes

Testudinaria elephantipes, commonly known as the elephant foot yam, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its large, fleshy tubers that can weigh several kilograms. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its tubers are edible and have been traditionally used in various cuisines.

The species is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
