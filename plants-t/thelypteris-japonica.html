Thelypteris japonica

Thelypteris japonica, commonly known as Japanese shield fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to East Asia.

It features a dense, leathery frond with a distinctive shield-shaped base, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine in some Asian countries.

The fronds are biennial, with the fertile fronds appearing in the second year of growth.
