Tridax coronopifolia var. alboradiata

Tridax coronopifolia var. alboradiata, commonly known as white-rayed tridax, is a variant of the common tridax species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is an annual herbaceous plant characterized by its distinctive white ray florets that contrast with its green central disk. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and thrives in open, sunny habitats. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs.

The flowers are usually arranged in composite heads, typical of the Asteraceae family.
