Terminalia petersii

Terminalia petersii, commonly known as the African snowball tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and large, palmate leaves that are often lobed. The tree produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by orange-red berries. Its fruit is an important food source for various wildlife species.

Terminalia petersii is also valued for its hard, durable wood, which has been used in traditional construction and craftsmanship.
