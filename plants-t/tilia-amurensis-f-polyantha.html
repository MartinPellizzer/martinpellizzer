Tilia amurensis f. polyantha

Tilia amurensis f. polyantha, commonly known as the Amur honeylocust, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, finely divided, feathery leaves that give it a delicate appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are highly fragrant and attract pollinators. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

This cultivar is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
