Tachigali vulgaris

Tachigali vulgaris, commonly known as the Brazilian flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a vibrant appearance during the flowering season. The tree has pinnate leaves and produces small, woody pods that contain seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often used in ornamental landscaping.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardens and public spaces.
