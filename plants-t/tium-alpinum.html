Tium alpinum

Tium alpinum, commonly known as the alpine thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are fragrant and often used in herbal medicine for their antimicrobial properties.

Tium alpinum thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.
