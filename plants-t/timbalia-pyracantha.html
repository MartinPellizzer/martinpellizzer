Timbalia pyracantha

Timbalia pyracantha, commonly known as the African pyracantha, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and bright orange or red berries that appear in autumn. The plant has elliptical, dark green leaves and produces small, fragrant white flowers in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its berries are a food source for birds and other wildlife, contributing to its ecological importance.
