Thermopsis hugeri

Thermopsis hugeri, commonly known as yellow coneflower, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and fern-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are tubular and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
