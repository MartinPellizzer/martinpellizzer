Tamus parviflora

Tamus parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered arrowhead, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. It belongs to the Araceae family, which is characterized by its unique inflorescence structure.

Tamus parviflora is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and ecological value for pollinators.
