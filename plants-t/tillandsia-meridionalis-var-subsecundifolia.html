Tillandsia meridionalis var. subsecundifolia

Tillandsia meridionalis var. subsecundifolia, commonly known as the Southern Tillandsia, is a species of air plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped foliage, which is typically gray-green with a waxy coating. The plant produces inflorescences that bear showy, violet-blue flowers, which are often tubular in shape. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires minimal soil, as it absorbs moisture and nutrients through its leaves.

This variety is popular among epiphytic plant enthusiasts due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
