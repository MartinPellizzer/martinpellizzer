Taraxacum tenuissimum

Taraxacum tenuissimum, commonly known as the slender dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant is characterized by its yellow composite flower head, which develops into a fluffy white seed head known as a pappus. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils, meadows, and along roadsides.

Taraxacum tenuissimum is adapted to a wide range of environmental conditions and is considered a common weed in many regions.
