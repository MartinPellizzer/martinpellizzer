Thinopyrum corsicum

Thinopyrum corsicum, commonly known as goatgrass, is a wild grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. Thinopyrum corsicum is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as fields and roadsides.

It has been studied for its potential in agricultural improvement due to its genetic diversity and adaptability.
