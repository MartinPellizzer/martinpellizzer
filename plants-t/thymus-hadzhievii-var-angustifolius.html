Thymus hadzhievii var. angustifolius

Thymus hadzhievii var. angustifolius, commonly known as narrow-leaved thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It features compact, low-growing stems with small, aromatic leaves that are typically narrow and linear. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers in dense clusters during the summer months. Its foliage is widely used in culinary and medicinal applications due to its strong, pungent aroma.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and borders.
