Tanacetum sinuatum

Tanacetum sinuatum, commonly known as buttonweed, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant features small, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Tanacetum sinuatum is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and can also serve as a habitat for pollinators.
