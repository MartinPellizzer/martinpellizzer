Toxicodendron trilobatum

Toxicodendron trilobatum, commonly known as poison ivy, is a flowering plant in the Anacardiaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three lance-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in catkins. It is known for its allergenic urushiol oil, which causes contact dermatitis in many humans.

Native to the Americas, it thrives in a variety of habitats, including woodlands and disturbed soils.
