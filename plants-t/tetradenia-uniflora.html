Tetradenia uniflora

Tetradenia uniflora, commonly known as the single-flower tetradenia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and solitary white flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional Indigenous practices.

Tetradenia uniflora is also a subject of interest in ecological studies due to its unique adaptations to arid environments.
