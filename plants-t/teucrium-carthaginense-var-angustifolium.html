Teucrium carthaginense var. angustifolium

Teucrium carthaginense var. angustifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, aromatic leaves that are typically lance-shaped and have a grayish-green hue. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to violet, often arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its medicinal properties have been historically valued for their anti-inflammatory and antiseptic qualities.
