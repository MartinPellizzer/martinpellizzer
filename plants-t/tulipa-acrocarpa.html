Tulipa acrocarpa

Tulipa acrocarpa, commonly known as the tall tulip, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Tulipa.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia, where it thrives in sunny, well-drained soils. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers that are typically pink, red, or orange in color, with a distinctive, elongated shape. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge in early spring.

Tulipa acrocarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
