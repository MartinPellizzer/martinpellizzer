Trichocentrum biorbiculare

Trichocentrum biorbiculare, commonly known as the two-lobed orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Central and South America.

It features elongated, linear leaves that are typically green with a waxy coating, aiding in water retention. The plant produces pendulous inflorescences with showy, fragrant flowers that are often white or pale yellow. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and surrounding substrates.

Trichocentrum biorbiculare is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
