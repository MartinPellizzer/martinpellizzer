Tabebuia insignis var. pacimonensis

Tabebuia insignis var. pacimonensis, commonly known as the Pacimonense Trumpet Tree, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, featuring a straight trunk and a broad, rounded crown. The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a smooth surface and a pointed tip. The flowers are showy, trumpet-shaped, and typically yellow or orange, blooming in clusters during the dry season.

This plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often planted in urban landscapes for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
