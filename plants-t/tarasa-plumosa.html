Tarasa plumosa

Tarasa plumosa, commonly known as the featherleaf tarasa, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves that give it its common name, and it typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or dryland landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements, it is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
