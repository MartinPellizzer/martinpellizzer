Tabebuia umbellata

Tabebuia umbellata, commonly known as the yellow trumpet tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The tree has a straight trunk and a broad, rounded crown, with smooth, gray bark that becomes fissured with age. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate, with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in parks and gardens for its vibrant floral display.
