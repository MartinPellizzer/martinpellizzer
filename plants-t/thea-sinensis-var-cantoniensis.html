Thea sinensis var. cantoniensis

Thea sinensis var. cantoniensis, commonly known as the Guangdong tea plant, is a variant of the tea plant native to southern China.

It is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves and a distinctive aroma when processed. This variety is particularly valued for its ability to produce high-quality black and green teas. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, requiring well-drained soil and ample sunlight.

Its botanical features make it a key species in the cultivation of traditional Chinese teas.
