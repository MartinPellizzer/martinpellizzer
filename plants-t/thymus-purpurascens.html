Thymus purpurascens

Thymus purpurascens, commonly known as purple thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features small, fragrant leaves that are typically purple or pink in color, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces pink or lilac flowers in early summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic properties make it a popular choice for culinary and medicinal uses.
