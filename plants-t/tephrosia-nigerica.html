Tephrosia nigerica

Tephrosia nigerica, commonly known as African tephrosia, is a perennial legume native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemes. The plant is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of soil types, including sandy and clay soils. It has a deep root system that helps in soil stabilization and nutrient cycling.

Tephrosia nigerica is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a forage crop for livestock.
