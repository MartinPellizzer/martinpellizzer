Tapeinosperma psaladense

Tapeinosperma psaladense, commonly known as the Brazilian nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, compound leaves and produces a unique fruit that contains the edible seeds known as Brazil nuts. The plant belongs to the family Proteaceae and is known for its slow growth and long lifespan.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
