Tipuana praecox

Tipuana praecox, commonly known as the early tipuana, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in a shrub or small tree form, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers are often yellow or orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The seeds are enclosed in legumes, which split open when mature to release the seeds for dispersal.
