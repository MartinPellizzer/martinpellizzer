Taraxacum severum

Taraxacum severum, commonly known as the mountain dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in spring, followed by the characteristic puffball seed heads.

The species is known for its resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
