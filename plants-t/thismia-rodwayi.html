Thismia rodwayi

Thismia rodwayi, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a unique plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Thismiaceae family and is characterized by its lack of chlorophyll, making it a mycoheterotroph that relies entirely on fungi for nutrition. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often overlooked due to their lack of showy petals. Its roots form a symbiotic relationship with specific mycorrhizal fungi, which provide essential nutrients in exchange for carbohydrates.

Due to its rarity and specialized habitat requirements, Thismia rodwayi is considered an endangered species.
