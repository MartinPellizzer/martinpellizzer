Tragacantha collina

Tragacantha collina, commonly known as the mountain tragacanth, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by pod-like fruits containing seeds. It is known for its ability to thrive in rocky, arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.

Tragacantha collina is also valued for its gum, which has been used historically in various medicinal and industrial applications.
