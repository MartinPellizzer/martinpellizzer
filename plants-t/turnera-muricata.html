Turnera muricata

Turnera muricata, commonly known as the velvetleaf turnera, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Turneraceae and is characterized by its broad, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in the spring and summer months. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Turnera muricata is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
