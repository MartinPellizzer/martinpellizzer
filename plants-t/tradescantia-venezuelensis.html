Tradescantia venezuelensis

Tradescantia venezuelensis, commonly known as the "Wandering Jew," is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a perennial herb with striking, lance-shaped leaves that can range in color from silver-green to deep red. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in shades of white or pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Tradescantia venezuelensis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
