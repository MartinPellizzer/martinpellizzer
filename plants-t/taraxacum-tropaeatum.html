Taraxacum tropaeatum

Taraxacum tropaeatum, commonly known as the tropical dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia, and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and a hollow, leafless stem. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that develop into fluffy white seed heads, typical of the dandelion genus. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
