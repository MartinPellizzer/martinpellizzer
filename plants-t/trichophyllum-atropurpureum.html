Trichophyllum atropurpureum

Trichophyllum atropurpureum, commonly known as the purple-stemmed clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and dark purple stems that are often covered in fine, hair-like structures. The plant produces small, cone-like structures called strobili, which contain the spores for reproduction. It typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices.

Trichophyllum atropurpureum plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
