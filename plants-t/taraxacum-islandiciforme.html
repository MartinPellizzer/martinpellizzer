Taraxacum islandiciforme

Taraxacum islandiciforme, commonly known as the Iceland dandelion, is a perennial herb native to Iceland and parts of northern Europe.

It is characterized by its rosette of basal, spoon-shaped leaves and a flowering stem that bears a single, yellow composite flower head. The plant produces a distinctive, hollow, wind-dispersed seed capsule known as a pappus. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in coastal and alpine regions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and protected species in some areas.
