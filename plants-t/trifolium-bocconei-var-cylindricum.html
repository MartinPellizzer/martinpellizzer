Trifolium bocconei var. cylindricum

Trifolium bocconei var. cylindricum, commonly known as the cylinder clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically narrow and slightly hairy, and its small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It is adapted to arid conditions and has a deep root system that allows it to survive in dry environments.

This variety is noted for its compact growth habit and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
