Trigonella subinermis

Trigonella subinermis, commonly known as the slender fenugreek, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in sandy or calcareous soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. Its seeds are small and oblong, with a distinctive aroma, and are used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some cuisines.

Trigonella subinermis plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitats.
