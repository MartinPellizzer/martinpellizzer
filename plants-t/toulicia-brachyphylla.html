Toulicia brachyphylla

Toulicia brachyphylla, commonly known as the short-leaved lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Toulicia brachyphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
