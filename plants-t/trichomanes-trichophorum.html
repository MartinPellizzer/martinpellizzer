Trichomanes trichophorum

Trichomanes trichophorum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its spores are produced in small, clustered structures called sori, which are located on the underside of the fronds.

Trichomanes trichophorum is noted for its intricate leaf structure and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
