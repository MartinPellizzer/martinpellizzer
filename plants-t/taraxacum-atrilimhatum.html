Taraxacum atrilimhatum

Taraxacum atrilimhatum, commonly known as the black-limbed dandelion, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive dark-colored stems and leaves.

It typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soil. The plant produces small, yellow composite flowers that develop into fluffy white seed heads. Its leaves are usually deeply lobed and have a slightly hairy texture.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
