Trillium × miyabeanum var. atropurpureocarpum

Trillium × miyabeanum var. atropurpureocarpum, commonly known as the purple-fruit trillium, is a hybrid plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy, waxy flowers that are typically white or pale pink. The plant produces dark purple or black berries, which are edible but not particularly flavorful. It thrives in shaded, moist woodlands and is often found in the understory of deciduous forests.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
