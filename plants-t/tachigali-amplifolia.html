Tachigali amplifolia

Tachigali amplifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf tachigali, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmately compound leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in panicles, followed by distinctive, woody pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

The tree is valued for its timber and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
