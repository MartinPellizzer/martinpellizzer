Triodia diantha

Triodia diantha, commonly known as the slender spinifex, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its dense, wiry growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny white or pale blue flowers. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in harsh conditions.

Triodia diantha plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various desert organisms.
