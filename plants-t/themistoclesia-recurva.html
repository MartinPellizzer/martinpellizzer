Themistoclesia recurva

Themistoclesia recurva, commonly known as the spiral leaf vine, is a climbing plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and heart-shaped leaves that often curl in a spiral pattern. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Its unique leaf morphology makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
