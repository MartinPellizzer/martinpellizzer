Thalictrum acuminatum

Thalictrum acuminatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved meadow-rue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which includes buttercups and anemones, and is characterized by its delicate, often fern-like foliage. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, typically appearing in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Thalictrum acuminatum is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
