Trifolium semipilosum var. intermedium

Trifolium semipilosum var. intermedium, commonly known as the intermediate hairy clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically hairy and have a distinctive pale green color. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are usually pink or reddish in color, blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed areas.

This variety is noted for its adaptability and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
