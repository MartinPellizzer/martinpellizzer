Taraxacum subatroplumbeum

Taraxacum subatroplumbeum, commonly known as the pale-leaved dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of pale green, spoon-shaped leaves and a single, hollow stem that supports a flowering head. The plant produces a distinctive yellow flower that develops into a white, feathery pappus, aiding in wind pollination. It is typically found in disturbed soils and open habitats, often in temperate regions.

This species is relatively rare and has specific ecological requirements, making it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
