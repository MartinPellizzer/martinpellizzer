Trifolium spadiceum

Trifolium spadiceum, commonly known as the purple clover, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets, and its small, clustered purple flowers. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grasslands. It has a deep root system that helps in nitrogen fixation, making it beneficial for soil fertility.

Trifolium spadiceum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
