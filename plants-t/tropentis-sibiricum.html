Tropentis sibiricum

Tropentis sibiricum, commonly known as Siberian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the colder regions of Eastern Europe and Siberia.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of blue or violet bell-shaped flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its cold climate.

Tropentis sibiricum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
