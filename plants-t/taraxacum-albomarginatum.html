Taraxacum albomarginatum

Taraxacum albomarginatum, commonly known as the white-margin dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and distinctive white-bordered leaves. Native to parts of Europe and Asia, this plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant produces a hollow, hairy stem and has a milky sap that is common in the genus Taraxacum.

It is often found in temperate climates and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
