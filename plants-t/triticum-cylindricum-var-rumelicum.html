Triticum cylindricum var. rumelicum

Triticum cylindricum var. rumelicum, commonly known as the slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, cylindrical stems and dense, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brown flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
