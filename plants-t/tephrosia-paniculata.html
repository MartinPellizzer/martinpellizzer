Tephrosia paniculata

Tephrosia paniculata, commonly known as the African blue pea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant has a woody stem and can grow as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Tephrosia paniculata is also valued for its medicinal properties and use in traditional herbal remedies.
