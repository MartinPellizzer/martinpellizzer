Thymus biflorus

Thymus biflorus, commonly known as the two-flowered thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pink or purple flowers in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Thymus biflorus thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
