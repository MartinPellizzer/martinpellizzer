Tylophora lugardiae

Tylophora lugardiae, commonly known as the African moonvine, is a climbing plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by elliptical fruits that contain several seeds.

It is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses.
