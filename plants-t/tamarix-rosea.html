Tamarix rosea

Tamarix rosea, commonly known as the pink tamarisk, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, scale-like leaves that reduce water loss, making it well adapted to arid environments. The plant produces delicate pink or purple flowers in summer, which are clustered in spherical inflorescences. Tamarix rosea is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

Its root system can be invasive, leading to ecological concerns in some regions.
