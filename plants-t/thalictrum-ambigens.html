Thalictrum ambigens

Thalictrum ambigens, commonly known as the meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces showy, fluffy clusters of white or pale yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Thalictrum ambigens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
