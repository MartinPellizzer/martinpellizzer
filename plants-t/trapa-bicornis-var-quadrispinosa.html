Trapa bicornis var. quadrispinosa

Trapa bicornis var. quadrispinosa, commonly known as the four-spined water chestnut, is a aquatic plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny fruits that float on the water's surface. The plant has broad, floating leaves that provide habitat for aquatic life. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically found beneath the water.

This variety is often found in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats.
