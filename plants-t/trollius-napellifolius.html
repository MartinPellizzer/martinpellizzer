Trollius napellifolius

Trollius napellifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf globeflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, bright yellow flowers that are typically arranged in a globe-like cluster, giving it its common name. The plant has finely divided, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Trollius napellifolius is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
