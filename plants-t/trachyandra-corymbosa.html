Trachyandra corymbosa

Trachyandra corymbosa, commonly known as the clustered bristleplant, is a member of the Asphodelaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid regions. The plant features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences of white or pale green flowers. Its fibrous roots help it retain moisture in dry conditions, making it well adapted to its environment.

Trachyandra corymbosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in harsh landscapes.
