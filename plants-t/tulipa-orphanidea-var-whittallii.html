Tulipa orphanidea var. whittallii

Tulipa orphanidea var. whittallii, commonly known as the Whittall's tulip, is a rare and distinctive species native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from pale yellow to deep orange, often with a contrasting throat. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it a challenging but rewarding addition to specialized gardens. Its bulbs are relatively small and require specific conditions to thrive, including cold winters and a period of dormancy.
