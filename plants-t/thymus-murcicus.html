Thymus murcicus

Thymus murcicus, commonly known as the Murcian thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy flowers. The plant typically has narrow, gray-green leaves with a strong thyme scent, and produces small purple or pink flowers in the spring. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates.

Due to its hardiness and fragrance, it is sometimes used in culinary and medicinal applications.
