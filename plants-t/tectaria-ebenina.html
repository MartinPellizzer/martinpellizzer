Tectaria ebenina

Tectaria ebenina, commonly known as the ebony fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Tectariaceae and is characterized by its large, leathery fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as tropical forests. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The leaves are usually bipinnately divided, contributing to its ornamental value in horticulture.
