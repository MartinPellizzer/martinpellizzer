Thalictrum flavum f. heterophyllum

Thalictrum flavum f. heterophyllum, commonly known as the yellow meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like foliage and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and it produces small, dry fruits that contain several seeds.

The foliage and flowers are often used in herbal medicine for their mild diuretic properties.
