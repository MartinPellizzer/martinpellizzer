Tromotriche ciliata

Tromotriche ciliata, commonly known as the silky leaf cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Cactaceae and is characterized by its unique, fleshy, ribbed stems that are covered in fine, hair-like structures. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, adding ornamental value to its appearance. Tromotriche ciliata is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its succulent stems to survive in dry conditions.
