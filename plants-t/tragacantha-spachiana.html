Tragacantha spachiana

Tragacantha spachiana, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia. The plant is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves, which are adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by pods containing seeds.

Tragacantha spachiana is notable for its ability to produce a valuable resin, which has been used historically in various industries.
