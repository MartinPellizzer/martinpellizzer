Torenia flaccida

Torenia flaccida, commonly known as the hanging bellflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its pendulous, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft texture. Torenia flaccida thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive, long-lasting blooms.
