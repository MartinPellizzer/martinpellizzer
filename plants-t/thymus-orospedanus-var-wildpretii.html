Thymus orospedanus var. wildpretii

Thymus orospedanus var. wildpretii, commonly known as the Greek thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically gray-green in color, with a dense, cushion-like growth habit. The plant produces tiny white or pale pink flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

This variety is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, as well as its ornamental appeal.
