Thevetia peruviana f. aurantiaca

Thevetia peruviana f. aurantiaca, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a striking ornamental plant native to South America.

It is a variant of the species Thevetia peruviana, characterized by its vibrant orange to yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are oval-shaped with a slightly waxy texture. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated for its showy floral display.

Thevetia peruviana f. aurantiaca is also notable for its ability to produce yellow-orange fruits that contain toxic seeds, making it important to handle with care.
