Tulipa montana var. julia

Tulipa montana var. julia, commonly known as the Julia hybrid tulip, is a hardy perennial bulbous plant native to Central Asia.

It produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming in late spring. The plant features strap-like, grassy foliage that emerges in early spring and persists through the summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is prized for its vibrant colors and reliable performance in garden settings.
