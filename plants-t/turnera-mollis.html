Turnera mollis

Turnera mollis, commonly known as the "Mexican buttercup," is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Turneraceae and is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Turnera mollis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
