Thuja ellwangeriana

Thuja ellwangeriana, commonly known as the Western red cedar, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to western North America.

It is characterized by its flat, spruce-like needles and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. The tree produces small, round cones that mature to a reddish-brown color. Its wood is highly valued for its durability and resistance to decay, making it popular in construction and furniture-making.

The plant also has cultural and historical significance, particularly among Indigenous communities.
