Tussilago alba var. ramosa

Tussilago alba var. ramosa, commonly known as the woolly thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, white, woolly foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Its hairy leaves and showy flowers make it a distinctive and ornamental plant in its native habitat.
