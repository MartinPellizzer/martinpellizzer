Trichinium pyramidatum

Trichinium pyramidatum, commonly known as the pyramid bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves that are often covered in fine trichomes. The plant produces tall, slender stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, pale blue to violet flowers. Its flowering period typically occurs in late summer to early autumn.

Trichinium pyramidatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
