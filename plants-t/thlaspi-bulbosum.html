Thlaspi bulbosum

Thlaspi bulbosum, commonly known as the bulbous cress, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces small, yellow flowers and has a distinctive, fleshy, bulb-like structure at its base. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are typically basal, rosette-like, and have a slightly succulent texture.

Thlaspi bulbosum is often found in wet meadows, marshes, and along stream banks.
