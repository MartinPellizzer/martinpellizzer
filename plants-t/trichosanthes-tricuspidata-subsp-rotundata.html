Trichosanthes tricuspidata subsp. rotundata

Trichosanthes tricuspidata subsp.

rotundata, commonly known as the round-leaf bitter melon, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia. It produces large, green, lobed leaves and elongated, yellow-orange fruits that mature to a dark orange color.

The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and distinctive three-lobed leaf structure. It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like cucumbers and pumpkins.

The fruit is edible when mature but is known for its bitter taste and is used in various traditional cuisines.
