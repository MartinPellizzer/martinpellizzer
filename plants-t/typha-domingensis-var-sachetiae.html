Typha domingensis var. sachetiae

Typha domingensis var. sachetiae, commonly known as the narrowleaf cattail, is a perennial wetland plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and a dense, cylindrical inflorescence that contains both male and female flowers. The plant thrives in shallow freshwater environments such as marshes, ponds, and along the edges of lakes. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly, forming dense stands that can stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife.

The plant plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by contributing to nutrient cycling and water filtration.
