Trochocarpa verticillata

Trochocarpa verticillata, commonly known as the spiral-leaf cactus, is a unique succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its whorled arrangement of fleshy, spiny stems that spiral upward, forming a distinctive, compact structure. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the dry season, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss and aiding in protection from herbivores.

Trochocarpa verticillata is well adapted to harsh desert environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.
