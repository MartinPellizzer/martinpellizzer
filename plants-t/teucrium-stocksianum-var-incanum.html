Teucrium stocksianum var. incanum

Teucrium stocksianum var. incanum, commonly known as the silver germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branched stems covered in a dense layer of silvery-gray hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are narrow, linear, and also covered in fine, white pubescence, contributing to its silvery hue. Its small, tubular flowers are typically blue to violet in color, arranged in spikes, and bloom during the summer months.

This plant is often used in herbal medicine and is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.
