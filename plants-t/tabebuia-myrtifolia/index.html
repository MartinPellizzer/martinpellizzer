Tabebuia Myrtifolia

Yellow trumpet tree, scientifically known as Tabebuia myrtifolia, is a striking tropical tree native to Central and South America. It is celebrated for its vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a dazzling display during the rainy season. The tree's unique shape and ornamental value make it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping and urban greening projects. Its wood is also valued for its durability and use in furniture and construction. Despite its beauty, the Yellow trumpet tree faces threats from deforestation and habitat loss, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
