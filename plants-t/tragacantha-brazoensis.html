Tragacantha brazoensis

Tragacantha brazoensis, commonly known as the Brazilian tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant produces a sticky resin, which has been historically used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in dry forests and shrublands.

Its ecological role includes providing food for various pollinators and supporting local biodiversity.
