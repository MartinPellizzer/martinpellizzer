Trichocereus taquimbalensis

Trichocereus taquimbalensis, commonly known as the "Taquimbalensis Cactus," is a species native to the Andes Mountains of Ecuador.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its smooth, green stem and spiny areoles. The plant produces large, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in high-altitude environments with well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its striking appearance and potential for cultivation, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
