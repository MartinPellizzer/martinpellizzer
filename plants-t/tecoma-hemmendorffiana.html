Tecoma hemmendorffiana

Tecoma hemmendorffiana, commonly known as the yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its bright yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, woody pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Tecoma hemmendorffiana thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for ornamental gardening in warm climates.

Its showy blooms make it a popular choice for landscaping and floral arrangements.
