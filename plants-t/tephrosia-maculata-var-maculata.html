Tephrosia maculata var. maculata

Tephrosia maculata var. maculata, commonly known as the spotted tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a legume, belonging to the Fabaceae family, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, often found in disturbed soils and open habitats.

Its seeds are encased in pods and can be dispersed by wind or animals, contributing to its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and soil enrichment.
