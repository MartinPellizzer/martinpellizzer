Thecacoris glabrata

Thecacoris glabrata, commonly known as the smoothleaf daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky soils, thriving in full sun conditions. Its smooth, lance-shaped leaves are arranged alternately along the stem, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring.

Thecacoris glabrata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
