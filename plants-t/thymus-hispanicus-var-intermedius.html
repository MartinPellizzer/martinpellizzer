Thymus hispanicus var. intermedius

Thymus hispanicus var. intermedius, commonly known as Spanish thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features aromatic, narrow leaves covered in fine hairs and produces small, pink or white flowers in spikes during the summer months. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and Mediterranean climates. Its essential oils, rich in thymol and carvacrol, contribute to its use in aromatherapy and traditional medicine.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and culinary applications.
