Tupacamaria andamanensis

Tupacamaria andamanensis, commonly known as the Andaman tamarind, is a flowering plant native to the Andaman Islands.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by elongated pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas and moist forests.

The Andaman tamarind is valued for its hardy nature and potential uses in traditional medicine and local cuisine.
