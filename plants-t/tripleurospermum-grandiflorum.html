Tripleurospermum grandiflorum

Tripleurospermum grandiflorum, commonly known as the showy dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant produces a distinctive bright yellow flower that transitions into a white, fluffy seed head. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and disturbed areas.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
