Trichilia goyazana

Trichilia goyazana, commonly known as the Brazilian walnut or yellow cedar, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and large, compound leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, three-lobed fruits that contain flat, winged seeds. The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
