Talauma splendens

Talauma splendens, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its flowers are often fragrant and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Talauma splendens is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its cultural significance in some regions.
