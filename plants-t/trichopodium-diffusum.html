Trichopodium diffusum

Trichopodium diffusum, commonly known as the spreading haircup, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the sedge family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Trichopodium diffusum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
