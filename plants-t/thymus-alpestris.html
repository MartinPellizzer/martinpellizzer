Thymus alpestris

Thymus alpestris, commonly known as alpine thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It features small, gray-green leaves and produces tiny, pink or white flowers in clusters. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant foliage, which contains essential oils. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, alpine climates.

Thymus alpestris is often used in herbal medicine and as a culinary herb.
