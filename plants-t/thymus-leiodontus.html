Thymus leiodontus

Thymus leiodontus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically gray-green in color, covered in fine hairs. The plant produces tiny, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Thymus leiodontus is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
