Thalictrum corynellum

Thalictrum corynellum, commonly known as meadow rue, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in moist meadows and woodland edges. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and blooms in late spring to early summer.

Thalictrum corynellum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
