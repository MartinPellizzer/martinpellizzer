Taraxacum otagirianum

Taraxacum otagirianum, commonly known as the Japanese dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Japan and parts of East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its yellow composite flower heads. The plant features a basal rosette of leaves and a hollow, hairy stem that supports the flower. Its seeds are dispersed by wind via a parachute-like structure called a pappus.

Taraxacum otagirianum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and gardens.
