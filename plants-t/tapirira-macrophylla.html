Tapirira macrophylla

Tapirira macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf tapirira, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Leguminosae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are large, often with a broad, heart-shaped outline, and its flowers are small and clustered in racemes.

The species is notable for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity and its potential use in traditional medicine.
