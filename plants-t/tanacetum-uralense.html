Tanacetum uralense

Tanacetum uralense, commonly known as Siberian tansy, is a perennial herb native to the colder regions of Asia and Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, finely divided leaves and yellow button-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subarctic environments. Its essential oils, derived from the leaves and stems, contain compounds like thujone and camphor, which contribute to its medicinal and aromatic properties.

Tanacetum uralense is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported antiseptic and digestive benefits.
