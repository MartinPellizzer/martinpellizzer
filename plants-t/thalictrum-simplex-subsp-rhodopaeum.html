Thalictrum simplex subsp. rhodopaeum

Thalictrum simplex subsp.

rhodopaeum, commonly known as the Rhodopean meadow rue, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, usually white or pale yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. It has a fibrous root system and produces numerous stolons that help in vegetative propagation.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental species in its native habitat.
