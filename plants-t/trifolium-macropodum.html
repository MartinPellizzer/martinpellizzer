Trifolium macropodum

Trifolium macropodum, commonly known as the large-leaf clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets, and small, pea-like flowers that bloom in clusters. Native to southern Africa, this plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands. It has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion and fixes nitrogen, enriching the soil.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is cultivated in some regions for both aesthetic and agricultural purposes.
