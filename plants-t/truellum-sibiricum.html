Truellum sibiricum

Truellum sibiricum, commonly known as the Siberian bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the cold climates of Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

Truellum sibiricum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, colorful blooms.
