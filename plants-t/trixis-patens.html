Trixis patens

Trixis patens, commonly known as the desert milkweed, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the milkweed family (Asclepiadaceae) and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pale greenish-white flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous hairy, wind-dispersed seeds. Trixis patens has adapted to harsh desert conditions with deep root systems and reduced leaf surface area.

Its flowers attract various pollinators, including bees and moths, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
