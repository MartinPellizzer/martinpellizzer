Tragacantha lessertioides

Tragacantha lessertioides, commonly known as the lesser tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, often found in rocky soils. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, and its leaves are usually gray-green with a fuzzy texture. The roots of Tragacantha lessertioides are known to exude a sticky resin, which has been historically used in traditional medicine.

This species is valued for its adaptability to harsh environments and its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
