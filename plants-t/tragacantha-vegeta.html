Tragacantha vegeta

Tragacantha vegeta, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and woody, thorny stems. The plant produces a valuable resin, known as tragacanth, which has been used historically in medicine and as a food thickener. Its deep root system allows it to thrive in poor soils and withstand drought conditions.

Tragacantha vegeta plays an important ecological role in stabilizing desert environments and supporting local wildlife.
