Taraxacum hemiparabolicum

Taraxacum hemiparabolicum, commonly known as the parabolic dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the sunflower family.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow composite flowers and heart-shaped basal leaves. This plant is native to parts of Europe and is often found in grassy areas and disturbed soils. Its life cycle includes a rosette stage followed by a flowering stem that produces a single, showy flower head.

The plant is known for its ability to spread through wind-dispersed seeds, contributing to its widespread distribution.
