Tragopogon brevirostris var. podolicus

Tragopogon brevirostris var. podolicus, commonly known as the Podolic lesser thistle, is a flowering plant native to parts of Eastern Europe and Central Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months.

The species is noted for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its potential use in traditional herbal medicine.
