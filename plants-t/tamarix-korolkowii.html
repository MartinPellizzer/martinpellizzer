Tamarix korolkowii

Tamarix korolkowii, commonly known as the Korolkowii tamarisk, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its feathery, gray-green foliage and small, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has a deep root system, which helps it survive in arid and saline environments. Its bark is smooth and often peels in thin layers, and it is often used for windbreaks and soil stabilization.

Tamarix korolkowii is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and tolerate extreme temperatures.
