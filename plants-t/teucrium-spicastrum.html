Teucrium spicastrum

Teucrium spicastrum, commonly known as blue germander, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, tubular blue or violet flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface.

Teucrium spicastrum is often found in dry, sunny habitats like rocky soils and gravelly areas.
