Thymus hirtus subsp. tenuifolius

Thymus hirtus subsp.

tenuifolius, commonly known as narrow-leaved thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture.

The plant produces tiny, pinkish to white flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and fragrant foliage make it a popular choice for both ornamental and culinary use.
