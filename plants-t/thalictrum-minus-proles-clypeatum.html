Thalictrum minus proles clypeatum

Thalictrum minus proles clypeatum, commonly known as the lesser meadow rue, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a creeping rootstock that allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important component of its native habitat.
