Thesidium minus

Thesidium minus, commonly known as the lesser thistle, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its spiny, leafy stems and composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. Its leaves are usually alternate, with serrated edges, and the flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Thesidium minus is often considered a weed but has historical uses in herbal medicine.
