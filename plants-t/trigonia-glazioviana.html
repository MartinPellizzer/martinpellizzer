Trigonia glazioviana

Trigonia glazioviana, commonly known as the Glaziov's spider orchid, is a striking epiphytic orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its long, slender stems and clusters of vibrant, spider-like flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, utilizing its aerial roots to absorb moisture and nutrients from the environment. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern to maximize light absorption.

Trigonia glazioviana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance.
