Terpsichore lanigera

Terpsichore lanigera, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in racemes. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Terpsichore lanigera is often found in wetlands and disturbed soils, where it thrives in moist, sunny environments.
