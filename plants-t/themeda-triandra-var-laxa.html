Themeda triandra var. laxa

Themeda triandra var. laxa, commonly known as the slender spear grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open grasslands and savanna environments, often forming dense tussocks. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
