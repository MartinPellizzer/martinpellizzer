Thuja occidentalis f. variegata

Thuja occidentalis f. variegata, commonly known as the variegated arborvitae, is a cultivar of the eastern white cedar.

It is characterized by its striking variegated foliage, which features white or yellow edges on its needle-like leaves. This ornamental plant is often used in landscaping for its dense, conical shape and year-round visual appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for various garden settings.

The plant is also valued for its resistance to pests and diseases, contributing to its popularity among gardeners.
