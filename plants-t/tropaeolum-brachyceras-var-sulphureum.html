Tropaeolum brachyceras var. sulphureum

Tropaeolum brachyceras var. sulphureum, commonly known as the golden crown flower, is a vibrant annual plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, golden-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a bright splash of color to gardens. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are often reddish in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for container gardening.

This variety is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
