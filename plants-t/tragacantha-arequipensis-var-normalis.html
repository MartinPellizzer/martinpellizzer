Tragacantha arequipensis var. normalis

Tragacantha arequipensis var. normalis, commonly known as the Arequipa tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of southern Peru.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. Its roots are known for secreting a viscous gum, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive.

This variety is of particular interest due to its ecological significance and potential economic value in local communities.
