Trigonella adscendens

Trigonella adscendens, commonly known as the ascending fenugreek, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. Its seeds are used in traditional medicine and are known for their nutritional and medicinal properties.

The plant's ability to fix nitrogen makes it valuable for soil enrichment in agricultural settings.
