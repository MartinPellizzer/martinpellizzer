Tecoma schumanniana

Tecoma schumanniana, commonly known as the yellow bells, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, featuring a spreading habit and rough, bark-like stems. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a slightly hairy surface.

Tecoma schumanniana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to full sun and well-drained soils.
