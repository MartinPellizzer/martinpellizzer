Thalictrum dioicum var. genuinum

Thalictrum dioicum var. genuinum, commonly known as the northern meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves and produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wet meadows. It has separate male and female flowers on the same plant, a characteristic known as dioecy.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
