Trisetopsis avenoides

Trisetopsis avenoides, commonly known as the slender oatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, woodlands, and stream banks. The plant has slender, erect stems that are often tinged with purple, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, each containing several small, two-lobed flowers.

Trisetopsis avenoides is characterized by its fine, hairy texture and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
