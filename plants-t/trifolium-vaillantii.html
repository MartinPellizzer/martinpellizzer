Trifolium vaillantii

Trifolium vaillantii, commonly known as the Vaillant's clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly found in regions of Argentina and Chile. This plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets, and small purple or pink flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Trifolium vaillantii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for ecological restoration projects.
