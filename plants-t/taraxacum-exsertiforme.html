Taraxacum exsertiforme

Taraxacum exsertiforme, commonly known as the slender dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its upright, grass-like leaves and a single, elongated flower head that typically blooms in late spring to early summer. The plant produces a distinctive, hollow stem with a milky sap that is often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is commonly found in meadows, roadsides, and disturbed areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is considered a cosmopolitan species with a wide geographic distribution.
