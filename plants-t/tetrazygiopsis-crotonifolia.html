Tetrazygiopsis crotonifolia

Tetrazygiopsis crotonifolia, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a tropical tree native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a striking appearance. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, leathery leaflets that are arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in coastal areas and savannas.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its vibrant floral displays.
