Thymus × welwitschii

Thymus × welwitschii, commonly known as the African thyme, is a hybrid shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, aromatic leaves. The plant produces pink to purple flowers in spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its foliage is often used in culinary and medicinal applications due to its pungent, thyme-like aroma.
