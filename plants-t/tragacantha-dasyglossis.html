Tragacantha dasyglossis

Tragacantha dasyglossis, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and woody, gnarled trunk. The plant produces a valuable resin, known as tragacanth, which has been used historically in medicine and as a binding agent.

Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments.
