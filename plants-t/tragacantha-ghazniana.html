Tragacantha ghazniana

Tragacantha ghazniana, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces a valuable resin, known as tragacanth, which has been used historically in medicine and as a thickening agent.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures.
