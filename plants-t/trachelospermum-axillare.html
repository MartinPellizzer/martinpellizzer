Trachelospermum axillare

Trachelospermum axillare, commonly known as the coral vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a climbing shrub characterized by its rapid growth and dense foliage. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. Its leaves are typically opposite, ovate, and have a glossy appearance.

Trachelospermum axillare is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
