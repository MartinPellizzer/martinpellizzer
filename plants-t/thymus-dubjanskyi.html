Thymus dubjanskyi

Thymus dubjanskyi, commonly known as the Dubjansky thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central and southeastern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy flowers. The plant typically features small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are small, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy texture.

Thymus dubjanskyi is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
