Taraxacum junpeianum

Taraxacum junpeianum, commonly known as the Junpeian dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of spoon-shaped leaves and a yellow flowering head that produces a crown of white pappus. Native to China, this plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows and grasslands. The plant is known for its ability to tolerate various soil conditions and its role in supporting pollinators.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
