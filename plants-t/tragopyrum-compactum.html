Tragopyrum compactum

Tragopyrum compactum, commonly known as the field thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, leafy stems and purple to pink flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant has a rosette-like growth pattern with basal leaves that are typically lobed and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in disturbed soils and can often be found along roadsides, fields, and waste areas.

Tragopyrum compactum is known for its hardy nature and ability to tolerate a wide range of environmental conditions.
