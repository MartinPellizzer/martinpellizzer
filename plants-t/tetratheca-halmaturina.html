Tetratheca halmaturina

Tetratheca halmaturina, commonly known as the Halmaturina tetratheca, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South Australia.

It is a small, shrub-like species with narrow, linear leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant is adapted to dry conditions, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and it plays a role in supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
