Tabebuia aquatilis

Tabebuia aquatilis, commonly known as the water trumpet tree, is a species of flowering tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in shades of yellow or orange. The tree has a straight trunk and a broad, spreading canopy, making it a notable ornamental plant. Its leaves are alternate and pinnate, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The plant is adapted to wet environments, often found near rivers and in swampy areas, contributing to its common name.
