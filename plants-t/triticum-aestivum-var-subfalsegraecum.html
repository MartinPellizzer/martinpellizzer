Triticum aestivum var. subfalsegraecum

Triticum aestivum var. subfalsegraecum, commonly known as false spelt, is a variant of wheat that exhibits distinct botanical characteristics.

It is a herbaceous annual plant with a robust stem and alternate, lance-shaped leaves. The inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle, each bearing two to three florets. Its grains are typically smaller and more elongated compared to common wheat, with a tough, inedible rachis.

This variety is notable for its historical use in traditional agriculture and its potential for niche culinary applications.
