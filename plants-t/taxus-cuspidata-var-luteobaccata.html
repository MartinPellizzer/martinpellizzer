Taxus cuspidata var. luteobaccata

Taxus cuspidata var. luteobaccata, commonly known as the golden-flowered Japanese yew, is a deciduous conifer native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and its most distinctive feature: golden-yellow flowers that appear in late spring. The foliage consists of scale-like leaves that are dark green on top and whitish underneath. This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and parks due to its unique floral display and graceful form.

It prefers partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a versatile addition to temperate landscapes.
