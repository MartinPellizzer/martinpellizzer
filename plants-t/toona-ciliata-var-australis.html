Toona ciliata var. australis

Toona ciliata var. australis, commonly known as the Australian toona, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often lobed. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by elongated, woody pods containing flat, winged seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and riverine areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
