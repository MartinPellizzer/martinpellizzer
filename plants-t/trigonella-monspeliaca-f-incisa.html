Trigonella monspeliaca f. incisa

Trigonella monspeliaca f. incisa, commonly known as the narrow-leafed fenugreek, is a botanical variant of the fenugreek species.

It is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by elongated, slender pods containing small, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or dry habitats.

Due to its unique morphology, it is of interest in botanical studies and horticultural research.
