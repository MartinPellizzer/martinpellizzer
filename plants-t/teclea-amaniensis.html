Teclea amaniensis

Teclea amaniensis, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a climbing shrub native to eastern and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in forest understories.

The botanical features of Teclea amaniensis make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
