Turnera maigualidensis

Turnera maigualidensis, commonly known as the Maigualidensis Turnera, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Turneraceae and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically alternate, simple, and have a velvety texture.

Turnera maigualidensis is often found in moist, shaded environments within cloud forests.
