Theobroma sativum

Theobroma sativum, commonly known as the cacao tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Amazon basin.

It produces pods containing seeds that are the primary source of cocoa beans used in chocolate production. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil and requires a tropical climate for optimal growth. Its leaves are large, elliptical, and glossy, while its flowers are small and white or pink.

Theobroma sativum is also valued for its nutritional content, including high levels of antioxidants and essential minerals.
