Taraxacum peralatum

Taraxacum peralatum, commonly known as the purple star dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette of spoon-shaped, hairy leaves that form a dense basal cluster. The plant produces a yellow flowering head that develops into a distinctive, fluffy white pappus, aiding in wind pollination. Its flowers bloom in spring and early summer, and the plant is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

Taraxacum peralatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
