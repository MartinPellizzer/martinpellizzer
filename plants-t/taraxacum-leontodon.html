Taraxacum leontodon

Taraxacum leontodon, commonly known as the lion's tooth dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of stiff, hairy leaves and a yellow flower head that develops into a white, fluffy pappus for wind dispersal. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in grassy areas and disturbed soils. It has a taproot system that allows it to survive in various environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it a common sight in meadows and along roadsides.
