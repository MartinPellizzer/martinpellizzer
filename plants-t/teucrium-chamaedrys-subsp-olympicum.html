Teucrium chamaedrys subsp. olympicum

Teucrium chamaedrys subsp.

olympicum, commonly known as the Olympic germander, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Greece. It features small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, arranged in dense clusters along the stems.

The plant has aromatic, hairy leaves that are often gray-green in appearance, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This subspecies is notable for its historical use in traditional medicine and its resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
