Tritaxis trichocarpa

Tritaxis trichocarpa, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its fruit is a three-lobed capsule that contains several seeds.

Tritaxis trichocarpa is often found in disturbed soils and is considered an invasive species in some regions.
