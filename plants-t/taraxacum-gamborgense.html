Taraxacum gamborgense

Taraxacum gamborgense, commonly known as the Gamborg dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and a distinctive, hollow stem. The plant produces a milky sap, which is typical of the genus Taraxacum. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed areas.

The species is noted for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common and persistent weed in many regions.
