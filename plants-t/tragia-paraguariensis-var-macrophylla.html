Tragia paraguariensis var. macrophylla

Tragia paraguariensis var. macrophylla, commonly known as the giant leaf tragia, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Paraguay and Brazil.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruits are capsules that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is often found in moist, tropical environments and is adapted to thrive in humid, shaded conditions.
