Trichonema simile

Trichonema simile, commonly known as the golden thread fern, is a delicate and elegant plant belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its finely divided, thread-like fronds that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its rhizome is slender and creeping, allowing it to spread gradually across the substrate.

Trichonema simile is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
