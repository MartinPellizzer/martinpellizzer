Thrinax harrisiana

Thrinax harrisiana, commonly known as the Harris's palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the Caribbean, specifically found in parts of Cuba and the Cayman Islands. This small palm typically grows to a height of about 3 meters and has a short trunk with a crown of pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by dark blue, fleshy fruits.

It is well adapted to coastal environments and is often found in sandy soils near the shoreline.
