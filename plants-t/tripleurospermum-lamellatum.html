Tripleurospermum lamellatum

Tripleurospermum lamellatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and grass-like foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, with a distinctive silvery underside that helps reflect sunlight.

The small yellow flowers bloom in summer, contributing to its role in pollinator habitats.
