Teucrium proctorii

Teucrium proctorii, commonly known as Proctor's germander, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or blue in color, arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.
