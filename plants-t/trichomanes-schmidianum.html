Trichomanes schmidianum

Trichomanes schmidianum, commonly known as the Schmid's maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its sporangia are clustered in sori on the underside of the fronds, a feature typical of many ferns.

Trichomanes schmidianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in humid, indoor settings.
