Tristachya welwitschii var. superbiens

Tristachya welwitschii var. superbiens, commonly known as the African feather grass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It features slender, arching blades with a fine, silky texture that create a delicate, feathery appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in panicles, which are often overlooked due to the plant's ornamental foliage.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its unique visual effect.
