Tetramolopium lepidotum

Tetramolopium lepidotum, commonly known as the velvetleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its foliage is covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a velvety appearance.

It produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall.
