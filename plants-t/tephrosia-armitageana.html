Tephrosia armitageana

Tephrosia armitageana, commonly known as the African blue pea, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Tephrosia armitageana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
