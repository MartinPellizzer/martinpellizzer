Trichocline auriculata var. breviscapa

Trichocline auriculata var. breviscapa, commonly known as the narrow-cupped trichocline, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features small, daisy-like flowers with yellow disc florets surrounded by white to pale pink ray florets. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.
