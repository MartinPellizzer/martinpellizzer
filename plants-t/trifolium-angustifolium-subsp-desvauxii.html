Trifolium angustifolium subsp. desvauxii

Trifolium angustifolium subsp.

desvauxii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved clover, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three narrow, lance-shaped leaflets.

The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky areas.

This subspecies is valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a forage crop for livestock.
