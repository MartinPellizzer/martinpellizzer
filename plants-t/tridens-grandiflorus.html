Tridens grandiflorus

Tridens grandiflorus, commonly known as the grandiflorous tridens, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and large, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its flowering period occurs during the late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Tridens grandiflorus is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in maintaining ecological balance in its native habitats.
