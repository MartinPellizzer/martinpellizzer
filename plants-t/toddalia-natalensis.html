Toddalia natalensis

Toddalia natalensis, commonly known as Natal tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody pods containing edible seeds. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it is often found in coastal and sandy soils.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local communities.
