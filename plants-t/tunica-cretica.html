Tunica cretica

Tunica cretica, commonly known as the rock daisy, is a flowering plant native to arid and rocky regions.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a short, sturdy stem with narrow, fleshy leaves adapted to conserve water. Its yellow flowers bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Tunica cretica is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
