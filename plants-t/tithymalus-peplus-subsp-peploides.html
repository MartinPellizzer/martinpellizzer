Tithymalus peplus subsp. peploides

Tithymalus peplus subsp.

peploides, commonly known as the woolly thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features small, aromatic leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance.

The plant produces clusters of small, pink or pale purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its compact growth habit and fragrant foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
