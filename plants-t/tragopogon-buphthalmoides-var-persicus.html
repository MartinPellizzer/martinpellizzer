Tragopogon buphthalmoides var. persicus

Tragopogon buphthalmoides var. persicus, commonly known as Persian goat's beard, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant features slender, hairy stems and elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often bluish-green in color. Its showy yellow flowers are surrounded by pale green bracts, and it produces small, achene-like seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This perennial species is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing resilience to harsh climatic conditions.
