Trichogonia menthifolia var. tomentosula

Trichogonia menthifolia var. tomentosula, commonly known as the woolly mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, slender stems covered in dense, white, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and also densely covered with fine, soft hairs. It produces small, pale blue or violet flowers in whorled clusters, typical of the Lamiaceae family.

This plant is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is valued for its aromatic properties and historical use in herbal medicine.
