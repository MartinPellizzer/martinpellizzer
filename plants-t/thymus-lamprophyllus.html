Thymus lamprophyllus

Thymus lamprophyllus, commonly known as narrow-leaved thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It features tiny, fragrant leaves that are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces small, pinkish to violet flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic properties make it valuable both in culinary and medicinal applications.
