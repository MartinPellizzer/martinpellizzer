Tanacetum oshanahanii

Tanacetum oshanahanii, commonly known as the Oshanahan's tansy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features finely divided, aromatic leaves and produces yellow to cream-colored flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and lesser-known species within the genus Tanacetum.
