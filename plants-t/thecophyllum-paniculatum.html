Thecophyllum paniculatum

Thecophyllum paniculatum, commonly known as the panicled thecophyllum, is a unique plant belonging to the family Thecophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its intricate, branching inflorescences that resemble a panicle, with small, clustered flowers. The plant exhibits a succulent growth habit, featuring fleshy, often ribbed leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. Its flowers are typically small and may be either bisexual or unisexual, depending on the species.

Thecophyllum paniculatum is often found in rocky or sandy soils in regions with a Mediterranean climate.
