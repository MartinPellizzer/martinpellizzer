Taraxacum proruptum

Taraxacum proruptum, commonly known as the prorupted dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and a yellow flowering head that develops into a fluffy seed head. Native to Europe, it is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant has a taproot system and produces numerous seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

It is a perennial herb that is often considered a weed in agricultural and garden settings.
