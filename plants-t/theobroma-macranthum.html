Theobroma macranthum

Theobroma macranthum, commonly known as the large-flowered cocoa tree, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Sterculiaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elliptical, yellowish fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are used in the production of cocoa products. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This species is valued both for its ornamental qualities and its economic importance in the cocoa industry.
