Tanarius mappa

Tanarius mappa, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its velvety, dark green leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments and prefers well-draining soil. It is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.

Tanarius mappa blooms in shades of purple, pink, and white, adding a touch of elegance to indoor spaces.
