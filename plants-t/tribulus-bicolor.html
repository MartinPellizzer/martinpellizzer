Tribulus bicolor

Tribulus bicolor, commonly known as red bryony, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Silybiaceae and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and spiny stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into red, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

Tribulus bicolor is often found in disturbed soils and is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
