Tropaeolum beuthii

Tropaeolum beuthii, commonly known as the "Chinese velvet flower," is a herbaceous annual plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Tropaeolaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture, and its stems are often covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Tropaeolum beuthii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in containers.
