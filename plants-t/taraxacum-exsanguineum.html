Taraxacum exsanguineum

Taraxacum exsanguineum, commonly known as the bloodroot dandelion, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the western United States and is characterized by its basal rosette of pale green, hairy leaves. The plant produces a single, striking yellow flower that blooms in early spring. Its scientific name refers to its blood-red coloration when the roots are cut.

It thrives in dry, open habitats and is often found in disturbed soils.
