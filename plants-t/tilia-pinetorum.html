Tilia pinetorum

Tilia pinetorum, commonly known as the pine-leaf lime or white poplar, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often lobed and have a silvery-white underside. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are followed by spherical, fuzzy fruits. Its bark is smooth and light gray, becoming fissured with age.

Tilia pinetorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
