Thlaspidium sativum

Thlaspidium sativum, commonly known as the common thlaspi, is a species of flowering plant in the mustard family, Brassicaceae.

It is a small annual herb that typically grows to a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually oval or lance-shaped with a hairy surface. Its flowers are small and white or yellow, arranged in racemes, and it produces small, round pods containing several seeds.

Thlaspidium sativum is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
