Tylophora trichambon

Tylophora trichambon, commonly known as the slender tylophora, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by follicles containing flat, winged seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
