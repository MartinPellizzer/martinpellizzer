Tinantia erecta

Tinantia erecta, commonly known as the red-tipped dayflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking red bracts that resemble petals. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with slender stems and opposite leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered, often blooming in shades of pink and white.

Tinantia erecta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a cut flower.
