Tibouchina pilosa var. glandulosa

Tibouchina pilosa var. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular tibouchina, is a shrub native to South America.

It features dense, fuzzy foliage with a distinctive silvery or grayish underside due to its glandular hairs. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for tropical and subtropical landscapes.
