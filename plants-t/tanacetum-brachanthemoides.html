Tanacetum brachanthemoides

Tanacetum brachanthemoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tansy, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features finely divided, gray-green leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It is often found in moist meadows and along stream banks, thriving in well-drained soils.

Tanacetum brachanthemoides is known for its aromatic properties and has been used historically in herbal medicine.
