Thelypteris scalpturoides var. jamaicensis

Thelypteris scalpturoides var. jamaicensis, commonly known as the Jamaican holly fern, is a variant of the broad-leafed holly fern native to the Caribbean.

It is a terrestrial fern that typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories. The plant has lance-shaped, serrated fronds that are often dark green with a leathery texture. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
