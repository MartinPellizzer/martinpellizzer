Trachymene coerulea var. leucopetala

Trachymene coerulea var. leucopetala, commonly known as the white fringed panicled trachymene, is a herbaceous perennial native to Australia.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves and produces striking, blue-violet flowers that are clustered in panicled inflorescences. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. Its flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to native gardens.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in sustainable landscaping.
