Taraxacum ptilotoides

Taraxacum ptilotoides, commonly known as the woolly dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust taproot system. The plant features a hairy, lobed stem and bright yellow ray florets that surround a central disk of tubular flowers. Its leaves are basal, forming a rosette and are covered with fine, white hairs.

The woolly dandelion is often found in dry, disturbed soils and is known for its ability to thrive in various environmental conditions.
