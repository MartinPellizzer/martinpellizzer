Trapa potaninii

Trapa potaninii, commonly known as the water chestnut or Chinese water chestnut, is a species of aquatic plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its floating leaves and spiny, edible nuts that develop underwater. The plant thrives in shallow freshwater habitats such as ponds and lakes. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine, and the nuts are cultivated for their culinary value.

Trapa potaninii plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
