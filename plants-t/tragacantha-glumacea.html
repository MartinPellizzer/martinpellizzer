Tragacantha glumacea

Tragacantha glumacea, commonly known as the Indian gum plant, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and woody, thorny stems. The plant produces a gum that is used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in shrublands and along rocky slopes.

Its ecological importance includes soil stabilization and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
