Turritis brassica

Turritis brassica, commonly known as the yellow rocket, is a biennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and pinnately compound leaves. It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and can be found in meadows and along roadsides.

Turritis brassica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
