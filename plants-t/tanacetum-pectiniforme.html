Tanacetum pectiniforme

Tanacetum pectiniforme, commonly known as the pectinleaf tansy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feather-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that are typically clustered in flat-topped inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Tanacetum pectiniforme is often grown as an ornamental plant in gardens for its elegant foliage and subtle floral display.
