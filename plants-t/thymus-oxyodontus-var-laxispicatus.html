Thymus oxyodontus var. laxispicatus

Thymus oxyodontus var. laxispicatus, commonly known as the broad-leaved thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features small, fragrant leaves that are typically gray-green in color and have a distinctive, slightly fuzzy texture. The plant produces pink to purple flowers in clusters, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic leaves are often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
