Trinia glauca subsp. glauca

Trinia glauca subsp.

glauca, commonly known as the blue trinia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

The plant features slender, upright stems with small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a bluish waxy coating. Its flowers are small, white or pale blue, and appear in clusters during the spring and early summer.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
