Triodia grandiflora

Triodia grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered triodia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear white or pale blue flowers, which bloom during the warmer months. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing resilience to drought and heat.

Triodia grandiflora plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
