Triorchis longilabris

Triorchis longilabris, commonly known as the long-lipped orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and a single, showy flower with a distinctive, elongated lip. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in well-drained soils. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is pollinated primarily by bees.

The species is considered rare and is often found in specific habitats, making it a subject of conservation interest.
