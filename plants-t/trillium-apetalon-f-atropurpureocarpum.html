Trillium apetalon f. atropurpureocarpum

Trillium apetalon f. atropurpureocarpum, commonly known as the purple-fruited trillium, is a rare and striking member of the Trillium genus.

It is characterized by its unique, three-lobed leaves and a single, showy flower that typically blooms in early spring. The plant produces a distinctive purple or reddish-purple fruit, which sets it apart from other trillium species. It thrives in shaded, moist woodlands and is often found in the eastern United States.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant plant.
