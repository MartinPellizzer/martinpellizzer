Trifolium sphaerocephalum

Trifolium sphaerocephalum, commonly known as the globe-headed clover, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, globular inflorescence that gives it its common name. The plant features trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring.

Trifolium sphaerocephalum is often found in grasslands and is adapted to dry, well-drained soils.
