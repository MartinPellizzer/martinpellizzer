Thalictrum isopyroides

Thalictrum isopyroides, commonly known as the nodding meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are compound, with multiple pairs of lance-shaped leaflets, and it produces feathery, branched seed heads that release lightweight seeds.

Thalictrum isopyroides is valued in gardens for its delicate appearance and ability to thrive in partial shade.
