Trifolium longipes var. pedunculatum

Trifolium longipes var. pedunculatum, commonly known as the pedunculate clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that are clustered in loose spikes, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along roadsides.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
