Townsendia dejecta

Townsendia dejecta, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with hairy, blue-green leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in arid and rocky environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and poor soil conditions. Its small, capsule-like fruits contain seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation across dry landscapes.

Townsendia dejecta plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is sometimes used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
