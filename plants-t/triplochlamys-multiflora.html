Triplochlamys multiflora

Triplochlamys multiflora, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces pods containing edible seeds that are used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.

The tree is also valued for its hard, durable wood.
