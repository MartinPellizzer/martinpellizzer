Torilis arvensis var. elatior

Torilis arvensis var. elatior, commonly known as greater shepherd's purse, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features erect, branching stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are distinctive, resembling a heart shape with notched edges. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a distinctive, inflated, triangular capsule that splits open to release seeds.

This species thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.
