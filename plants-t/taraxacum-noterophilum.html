Taraxacum noterophilum

Taraxacum noterophilum, commonly known as the Mexican dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated, hairy leaves. The plant produces a single, hollow, yellow flower head that develops into a fluffy seed head with white pappus. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

The species is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its potential medicinal uses.
