Trigonotis amblyosepala

Trigonotis amblyosepala, commonly known as the broad-leaved trigonotis, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth. Its flowers are borne on slender stems and attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, smooth seeds.
