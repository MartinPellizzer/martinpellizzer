Triticum durum f. ludmilia

Triticum durum f. ludmilia, commonly known as the Ludmilia durum wheat, is a botanical variant of durum wheat characterized by its unique morphological features.

This form exhibits distinct glume structures and awn characteristics compared to typical durum wheat varieties. It is primarily found in specific geographic regions where its growth conditions are met. The plant's botanical traits make it of interest in taxonomic and genetic studies.

Its classification within the Triticum genus highlights its role in understanding wheat diversity and evolution.
