Triceras tricuspidatum

Triceras tricuspidatum, commonly known as the three-toothed cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, upright growth habit and features trifoliate leaves with three tooth-like lobes. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in early spring. Triceras tricuspidatum is often found in moist, disturbed soils along streambanks and roadsides.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
