Tragacantha aciphylla

Tragacantha aciphylla, commonly known as the thornless tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces a sticky, gum-like substance known as tragacanth, which has been used historically in various industries. It thrives in rocky, arid environments and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its lack of thorns distinguishes it from other species in the genus Tragacantha.
