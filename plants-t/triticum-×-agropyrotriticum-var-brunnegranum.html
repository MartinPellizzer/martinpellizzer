Triticum × agropyrotriticum var. brunnegranum

Triticum × agropyrotriticum var. brunnegranum, commonly known as the black oat, is a hybrid cereal grass that exhibits characteristics of both wheat and oats.

It is a perennial plant with a robust root system and tufted growth habit, often found in temperate regions. The plant produces spikelets that bear small, dark-colored grains, which are typically used for forage or grain. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
