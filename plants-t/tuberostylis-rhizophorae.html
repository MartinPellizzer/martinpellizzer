Tuberostylis rhizophorae

Tuberostylis rhizophorae, commonly known as the mangrove tuber, is a unique plant species found in coastal and brackish water environments.

It is characterized by its underground tubers, which store nutrients and help the plant survive in fluctuating salinity conditions. The plant typically grows in association with mangrove forests, where it thrives in the intertidal zones. Its rhizomes are often intertwined with mangrove roots, forming a symbiotic relationship.

The botanical features of Tuberostylis rhizophorae make it an important component of coastal ecosystems.
