Tigridia pavonia

Tigridia pavonia, commonly known as the peacock flower, is a vibrant member of the Iridaceae family native to Mexico.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped blooms in shades of red, orange, and yellow, often with contrasting black or purple markings. The plant produces grass-like leaves and has a short, tuberous root system that helps it survive in arid conditions. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Tigridia pavonia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
