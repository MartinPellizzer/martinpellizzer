Thymus drabiscensis

Thymus drabiscensis, commonly known as the drystep thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and small, aromatic leaves. The plant produces tiny, pink or white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its medicinal properties and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in horticultural settings.
