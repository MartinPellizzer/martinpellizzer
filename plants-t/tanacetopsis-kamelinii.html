Tanacetopsis kamelinii

Tanacetopsis kamelinii, commonly known as the Kamelin's tansy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small yellow flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Tanacetopsis kamelinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
