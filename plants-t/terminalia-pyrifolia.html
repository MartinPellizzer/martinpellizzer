Terminalia pyrifolia

Terminalia pyrifolia, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and elliptical leaves that are often clustered at the ends of branches. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, orange-red drupes that are highly valued in traditional medicine. Its wood is hard and durable, making it useful for furniture and construction, while its leaves are used as a vegetable in some regions.

The plant is also noted for its ability to thrive in various soil types and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
