Thalictrum papyraceum

Thalictrum papyraceum, commonly known as the paperleaf meadowrue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and delicate, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Thalictrum papyraceum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
