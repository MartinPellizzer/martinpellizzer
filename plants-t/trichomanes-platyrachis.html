Trichomanes platyrachis

Trichomanes platyrachis, commonly known as the broad-leaf huperzia, is a species of fern belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and distinctive, broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its sporangia are clustered in sori on the underside of the fronds, a common feature in ferns.

Trichomanes platyrachis is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and air-purifying properties.
