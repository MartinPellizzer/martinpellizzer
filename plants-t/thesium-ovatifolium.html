Thesium ovatifolium

Thesium ovatifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leafed thesium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Thesium ovatifolium is typically found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and taxonomic studies.
