Tragacantha paliurus

Tragacantha paliurus, commonly known as the Persian lilac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pale purple flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces small, woody fruits that contain several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The plant is also valued for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments.
