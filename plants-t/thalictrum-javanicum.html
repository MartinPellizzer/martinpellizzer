Thalictrum javanicum

Thalictrum javanicum, commonly known as the Java meadow-rue, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like foliage. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, often with five petals. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as forest understories or wet meadows.

Thalictrum javanicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
