Tropaeolum calvum

Tropaeolum calvum, commonly known as the creeping nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and bright, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces edible leaves and seeds, which are often used in culinary applications. Its botanical features include small, heart-shaped leaves and capsule fruits that split open to release seeds.

Tropaeolum calvum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated as an ornamental or edible plant.
