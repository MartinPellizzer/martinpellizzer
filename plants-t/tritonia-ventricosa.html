Tritonia ventricosa

Tritonia ventricosa, commonly known as the balloon flower, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, often with a distinctive red throat. The plant has a robust root system and can grow up to 3 feet tall, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Tritonia ventricosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
