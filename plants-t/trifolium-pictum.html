Trifolium pictum

Trifolium pictum, commonly known as the painted clover, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets with a distinctive white or pale markings. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along roadsides.

Trifolium pictum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
