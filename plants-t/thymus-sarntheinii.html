Thymus sarntheinii

Thymus sarntheinii, commonly known as the Sarnthein thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense clusters. Its foliage is aromatic, containing essential oils that contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.

Thymus sarntheinii thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.
