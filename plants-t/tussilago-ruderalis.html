Tussilago ruderalis

Tussilago ruderalis, commonly known as the rough coughwort, is a flowering plant belonging to the sunflower family (Asteraceae).

It is characterized by its hairy, woolly leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. This species is typically found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and wastelands, where it thrives in sunny, open habitats. The plant is known for its medicinal properties, historically used to treat respiratory ailments.

Its ability to colonize ruderal areas makes it an important pioneer species in ecological succession.
