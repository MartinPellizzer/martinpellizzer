Tina trijuga

Tina trijuga, commonly known as the three-angled tamarind, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. Tina trijuga is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various health benefits.

Its growth habit and botanical features make it a unique addition to tropical gardens.
