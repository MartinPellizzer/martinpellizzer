Trisetum varium var. violaceum

Trisetum varium var. violaceum, commonly known as the violet false beard grass, is a variant of the species Trisetum varium, which belongs to the grass family Poaceae.

This plant is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and its distinctive violet-tinged inflorescence, which sets it apart from other members of the species. The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration. It is native to parts of Europe and is often found in moist, calcareous soils in meadows and along stream banks.

The plant's ornamental value is enhanced by its striking coloration, making it a desirable choice for garden cultivation.
