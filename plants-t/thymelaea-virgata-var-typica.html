Thymelaea virgata var. typica

Thymelaea virgata var. typica, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thymelaea, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It features small, dark green, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in spring. It is adapted to dry, rocky environments and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

The species is noted for its aromatic leaves and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
