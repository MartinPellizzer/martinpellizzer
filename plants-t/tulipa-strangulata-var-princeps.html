Tulipa strangulata var. princeps

Tulipa strangulata var. princeps, commonly known as the Prince's tulip, is a striking perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its deep purple to violet flowers, which bloom in late spring and are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant features strap-like leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
