Trillium cernuum var. typicum

Trillium cernuum var. typicum, commonly known as the nodding trillium, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features three broadly ovate leaves that are typically arranged in a whorl at the base of the plant. The plant produces a single, nodding white flower with three petals and three sepals, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded woodlands and has a shallow root system.

The species is noted for its ecological importance as a food source for various pollinators and forest floor organisms.
