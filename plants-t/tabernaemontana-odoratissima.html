Tabernaemontana odoratissima

Tabernaemontana odoratissima, commonly known as the sweet-scented star jasmine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, star-shaped white flowers that bloom in clusters during the warm season. The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are dark green in color. It is a woody shrub or small tree that can grow up to several meters in height.

The fragrance of its flowers is particularly noticeable in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators like moths.
