Triticum richardsonii

Triticum richardsonii, commonly known as Richardson's wheat, is a wild grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, with a bluish-green coloration.

Triticum richardsonii is often found in the western United States and parts of Canada.
