Thymus cuneatus

Thymus cuneatus, commonly known as narrowleaf thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or violet, blooming in spring and summer. Its leaves are aromatic and often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Thymus cuneatus thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant plant.
