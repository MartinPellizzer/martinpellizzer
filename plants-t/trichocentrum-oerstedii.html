Trichocentrum oerstedii

Trichocentrum oerstedii, commonly known as the Oersted's orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Central and South America.

It features long, thin pseudobulbs and is characterized by its pendulous, showy inflorescences with numerous fragrant flowers. The plant produces white to pale pink blooms that are often clustered in racemes, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light and requires well-draining potting media.

Trichocentrum oerstedii is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for orchid enthusiasts.
