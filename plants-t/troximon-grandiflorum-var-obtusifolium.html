Troximon grandiflorum var. obtusifolium

Troximon grandiflorum var. obtusifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf troximon, is a flowering plant characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and showy, clustered flowers.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight. The leaves are often glossy and have a smooth texture, while the flowers range in color from white to pale pink. This variety is noted for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to thrive in temperate climates.

It is also valued for its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
