Tulipa sylvestris var. cretica

Tulipa sylvestris var. cretica, commonly known as the Cretan wild tulip, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Crete.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to its natural habitat. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens or alpine settings.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in Mediterranean botany.
