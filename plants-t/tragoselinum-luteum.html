Tragoselinum luteum

Tragoselinum luteum, commonly known as the yellow centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant is often found in dry, rocky soils and can thrive in full sun. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to local biodiversity.

Tragoselinum luteum is also valued in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
