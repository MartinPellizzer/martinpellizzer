Trivalvaria macrophylla var. lanceolata

Trivalvaria macrophylla var. lanceolata, commonly known as the broad-leafed trivalvaria, is a species native to the humid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, lance-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length. The plant typically grows in dense, shaded forest understories where it thrives in moist, well-drained soil. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing a delicate, fragrant aroma.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
