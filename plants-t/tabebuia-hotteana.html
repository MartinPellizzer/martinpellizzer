Tabebuia hotteana

Tabebuia hotteana, commonly known as the pink trumpet tree, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red, blooming in clusters during the dry season. The tree has a straight trunk with a smooth, greyish-brown bark and produces small, oval-shaped fruits that contain seeds. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate, with a glossy green surface.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
