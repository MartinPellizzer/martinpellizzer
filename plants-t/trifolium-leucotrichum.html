Trifolium leucotrichum

Trifolium leucotrichum, commonly known as white clover, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets, and small white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. Native to Europe, Asia, and Africa, it has been widely naturalized in many parts of the world. The plant is a nitrogen-fixing species, making it valuable for soil enrichment and forage.

It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in lawns, meadows, and disturbed areas.
