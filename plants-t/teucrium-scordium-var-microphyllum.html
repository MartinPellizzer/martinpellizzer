Teucrium scordium var. microphyllum

Teucrium scordium var. microphyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in full sun. The plant features slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, tubular, and typically purple or blue, arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches.

It is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
