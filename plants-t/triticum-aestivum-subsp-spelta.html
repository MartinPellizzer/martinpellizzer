Triticum aestivum subsp. spelta

Triticum aestivum subsp.

spelta, commonly known as spelt, is an ancient grain species belonging to the grass family Poaceae. It is characterized by its compact, awned spikelets and small, oval grains that are typically beige to golden in color.

The plant is adapted to temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a resilient crop in various agricultural settings. Spelt has a higher protein content compared to modern wheat varieties and contains a unique combination of gluten proteins.

Its botanical features contribute to its nutritional value and distinct flavor profile, making it a popular choice in artisanal baking and health-conscious diets.
