Taraxacum pudilii

Taraxacum pudilii, commonly known as the alpine dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and herbaceous growth habit. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine soils, thriving in cool, moist environments at high altitudes. Its leaves are basal, rough, and deeply lobed, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a single head.

Taraxacum pudilii is adapted to harsh climatic conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
