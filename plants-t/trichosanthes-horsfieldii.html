Trichosanthes horsfieldii

Trichosanthes horsfieldii, commonly known as the Chinese snake gourd, is a flowering vine native to East Asia.

It produces large, warty, green fruits that turn orange-red when mature. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is characterized by its distinctive, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers. It is often cultivated for its edible fruits, which have a slightly bitter taste.

The plant is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
