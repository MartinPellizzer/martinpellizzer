Tamarix africana var. brivesii

Tamarix africana var. brivesii, commonly known as the African tamarisk, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and fluffy, pink or pale purple flowers that appear in summer. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for rocky or sandy soils. It plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for wildlife.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in gardens and landscapes in regions with similar climates.
