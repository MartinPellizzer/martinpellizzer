Tristachya pilgeriana

Tristachya pilgeriana, commonly known as the giant reed or Pilger's reed, is a tall, grass-like plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its dense, fibrous growth and long, narrow leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread rapidly, making it an aggressive colonizer in wetland environments.

Tristachya pilgeriana is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its striking appearance and resilience.
