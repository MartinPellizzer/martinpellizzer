Terminalia circumalata

Terminalia circumalata, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a small to medium-sized tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas and is valued for its durable wood.

The species is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
