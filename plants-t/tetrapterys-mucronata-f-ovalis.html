Tetrapterys mucronata f. ovalis

Tetrapterys mucronata f. ovalis, commonly known as the oval-leaved mucronate acanthus, is a variant of the mucronate acanthus, characterized by its oval-shaped leaves.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is native to tropical regions of South America. The plant features lanceolate leaves with a mucronate tip, and its inflorescences consist of clusters of small, tubular flowers. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage and delicate floral display.

This species thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
