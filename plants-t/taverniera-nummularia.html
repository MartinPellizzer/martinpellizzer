Taverniera nummularia

Taverniera nummularia, commonly known as the silver dollar plant, is a low-growing succulent native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, round leaves that are typically a silvery-green color, arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a subtle ornamental touch. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its compact size and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
