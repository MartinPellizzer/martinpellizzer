Trigonella foenum-graecum subsp. jemenensis

Trigonella foenum-graecum subsp.

jemenensis, commonly known as Yemen fenugreek, is a flowering plant native to the Arabian Peninsula. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small yellow flowers.

The plant produces elongated pods containing small, kidney-shaped seeds. It is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and potential agricultural studies.
