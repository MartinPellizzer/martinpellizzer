Trichilia villosa

Trichilia villosa, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical tree native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae, which includes mahogany and teak. The plant is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and pinnately compound leaves with a velvety texture. Its flowers are small and yellow-green, often clustered in dense inflorescences, while the fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds.

The tree is valued for its timber and has traditional uses in medicine and as a source of tannins.
