Thevetia humboldtii

Thevetia humboldtii, commonly known as the yellow flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators like bees and birds. The plant has smooth, greenish-gray bark and pinnately compound leaves with long, narrow leaflets. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds, and it thrives in warm, sunny climates with well-drained soil.

Thevetia humboldtii is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant flowers and adaptability to various growing conditions.
