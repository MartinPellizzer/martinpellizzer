Thlaspi gracile

Thlaspi gracile, commonly known as slender cabbages or rock cabbages, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial that typically reaches heights of 5 to 15 centimeters. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, with four petals arranged in a cross shape typical of the mustard family. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and often covered in fine hairs.

Thlaspi gracile is commonly found in rocky or gravelly soils in arctic and alpine regions.
