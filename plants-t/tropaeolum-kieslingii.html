Tropaeolum kieslingii

Tropaeolum kieslingii, commonly known as the golden groundcherry, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Tropaeolaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers and edible, citrus-scented fruits. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental and edible gardening.

Its unique fruit, which resembles a small cherry, is often used in culinary applications.
