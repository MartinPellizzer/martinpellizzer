Taeniophyllum calyptrogyne

Taeniophyllum calyptrogyne, commonly known as the "sac-like leaf orchid," is a unique epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, sac-like leaves that store water, an adaptation to its often humid and shaded habitat. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and surrounding substrates.

Taeniophyllum calyptrogyne is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
