Thymus rotundifolius

Thymus rotundifolius, commonly known as round-leaved thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features small, rounded leaves that are typically gray-green in color, covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pink or violet flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Its aromatic leaves are often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
