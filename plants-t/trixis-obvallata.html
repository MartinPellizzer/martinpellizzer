Trixis obvallata

Trixis obvallata, commonly known as the desert globemallow, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its globular, spiny fruit that resembles a small globe, and its hairy, succulent stems that help it retain moisture. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It is adapted to harsh environments, thriving in sandy or rocky soils with minimal water.

Trixis obvallata plays a role in stabilizing desert soils and provides food for various pollinators.
