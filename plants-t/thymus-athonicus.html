Thymus athonicus

Thymus athonicus, commonly known as the Athonite thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Greece.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy flowers. The plant typically produces small, pink or purple flowers in the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in culinary applications and traditional medicine due to their essential oils and antioxidant properties.

Thymus athonicus thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.
