Tenageia bufonia

Tenageia bufonia, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

Tenageia bufonia is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along streambanks, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife.
