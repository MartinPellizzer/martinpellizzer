Tetrazygia angustiflora var. argyrophylla

Tetrazygia angustiflora var. argyrophylla, commonly known as the silverleaf tetrazygia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a silvery or grayish pubescence, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters along the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
