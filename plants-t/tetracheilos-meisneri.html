Tetracheilos meisneri

Tetracheilos meisneri, commonly known as the narrow-leaved grass, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are small, spike-like structures.

Tetracheilos meisneri plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates in its native ecosystems.
