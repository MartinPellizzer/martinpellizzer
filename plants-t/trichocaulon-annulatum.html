Trichocaulon annulatum

Trichocaulon annulatum, commonly known as the ringed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant has a slender, grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves that are often slightly hairy. Its inflorescence consists of small, greenish flowers arranged in a spike, which are wind-pollinated.

Trichocaulon annulatum plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
