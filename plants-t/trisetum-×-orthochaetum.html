Trisetum × orthochaetum

Trisetum × orthochaetum, commonly known as the hybrid meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a natural hybrid between Trisetum spicatum and Trisetum oppositiflorum, exhibiting characteristics of both parent species. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color, and it produces fluffy, spike-like inflorescences.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
