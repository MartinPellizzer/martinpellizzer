Trapa europaea var. clipeata

Trapa europaea var. clipeata, commonly known as the European water chestnut, is a aquatic plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its floating leaves and distinctive, spiky fruit that contains edible seeds. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats such as ponds and lakes. Its leaves are broad and rounded, with a waxy surface that helps repel water.

The species is often cultivated for its nutritional value and is also used in traditional medicine.
