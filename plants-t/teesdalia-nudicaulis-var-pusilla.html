Teesdalia nudicaulis var. pusilla

Teesdalia nudicaulis var. pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf hairy tansy, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features small, yellowish-green, hairy leaves that are typically clustered at the base of the plant. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in open woods or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for ecological studies and native plant gardens.
