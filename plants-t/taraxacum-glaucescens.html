Taraxacum glaucescens

Taraxacum glaucescens, commonly known as the blue-eyed dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, blue-green leaves and a yellow flower head that develops into a white, fluffy seed dispersal mechanism. The plant thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats, often found in meadows, roadsides, and gardens. Its flowers bloom in spring and early summer, and it is known for its ability to produce a large number of seeds.

The species is also valued for its medicinal properties, though it is generally considered a common weed.
