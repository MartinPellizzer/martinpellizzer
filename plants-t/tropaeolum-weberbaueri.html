Tropaeolum weberbaueri

Tropaeolum weberbaueri, commonly known as the Weberbauer's morning glory, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Tropaeolaceae and is characterized by its vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant is typically herbaceous, with trailing stems that can grow up to several meters in length. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, while the flowers emit a pleasant fragrance.

Tropaeolum weberbaueri is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in hanging baskets.
