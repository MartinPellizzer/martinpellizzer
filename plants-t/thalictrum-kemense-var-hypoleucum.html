Thalictrum kemense var. hypoleucum

Thalictrum kemense var. hypoleucum, commonly known as the white meadow rue, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and delicate, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
