Tropaeolum rhizophorum

Tropaeolum rhizophorum, commonly known as the mountain nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange and yellow flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant is a herbaceous annual that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. Its roots are fibrous and help it anchor in rocky or mountainous terrain.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible leaves and flowers.
