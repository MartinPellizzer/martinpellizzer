Trisetum flavescens var. pratense

Trisetum flavescens var. pratense, commonly known as the meadow trisetum, is a perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, reaching heights of 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale yellow to brownish flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy texture.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
