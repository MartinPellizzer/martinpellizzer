Tylloma glabratum var. microphyllum

Tylloma glabratum var. microphyllum, commonly known as the small-leaved lily, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and delicate, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and showy white or pale pink blooms that appear in the spring.

It is often used in ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
