Tropidia squamata

Tropidia squamata, commonly known as the scaly leaved orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, scaly appearance on its leaves and stems, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. It produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white and yellow, which bloom during the warmer months.

Tropidia squamata is appreciated for its ornamental value and is cultivated by enthusiasts for its distinctive foliage and subtle floral display.
