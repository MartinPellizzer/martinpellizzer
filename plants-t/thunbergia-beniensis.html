Thunbergia beniensis

Thunbergia beniensis, commonly known as the white thunbergia, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and showy, tubular flowers. The flowers are typically white or pale blue, with a distinctive two-lipped structure, and are borne in clusters along the stems. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly rough texture.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
