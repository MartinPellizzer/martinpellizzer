Teucrium sphacioticum

Teucrium sphacioticum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, aromatic stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a grayish-green hue. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or blue, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

Its medicinal properties have been historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
