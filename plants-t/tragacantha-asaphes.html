Tragacantha asaphes

Tragacantha asaphes, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia. The plant is characterized by its woody, shrub-like structure and its ability to thrive in harsh, dry environments. It produces small, yellow flowers followed by pods containing seeds.

The plant is also known for its ability to produce a type of gum, which has been historically used in various traditional applications.
