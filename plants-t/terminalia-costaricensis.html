Terminalia costaricensis

Terminalia costaricensis, commonly known as the Guanacaste tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elongated, elliptic leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody, spherical fruits containing several seeds.

It is often found in lowland tropical forests and is valued for its hard, durable wood and ecological importance in supporting biodiversity.
