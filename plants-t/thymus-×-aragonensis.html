Thymus × aragonensis

Thymus × aragonensis, commonly known as Aragon thyme, is a hybrid perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, small, gray-green leaves and pink to purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and Mediterranean climates. It is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, similar to other thyme species.

Thymus × aragonensis is also noted for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
