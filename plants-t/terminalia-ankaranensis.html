Terminalia ankaranensis

Terminalia ankaranensis, commonly known as the Madagascar apple, is a small to medium-sized tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, fleshy fruits that are orange-red in color.

Its botanical features make it an important species in the local ecosystem and for traditional uses.
