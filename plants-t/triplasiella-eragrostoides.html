Triplasiella eragrostoides

Triplasiella eragrostoides, commonly known as the African grass or slender triplasiella, is a perennial grass species native to Africa.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant has narrow, erect leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of local ecosystems and a subject of study in plant taxonomy.
