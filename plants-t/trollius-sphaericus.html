Trollius sphaericus

Trollius sphaericus, commonly known as the globe buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, rounded, yellow-orange flowers that resemble small globes, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has palmately divided leaves and grows in moist, rocky habitats. It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy flowers and often toxic sap.
