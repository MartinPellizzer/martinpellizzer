Terminalia intricata

Terminalia intricata, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing flat, seed-like structures. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated for both aesthetic and practical purposes.
