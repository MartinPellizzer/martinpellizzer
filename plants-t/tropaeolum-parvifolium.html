Tropaeolum parvifolium

Tropaeolum parvifolium, commonly known as the pepperleaf nasturtium, is a herbaceous annual plant native to South America.

It features heart-shaped, bright green leaves with a peppery flavor, and its flowers are typically bright orange or red. The plant produces small, edible capsules that contain seeds, which can be used in cooking or for propagation. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Tropaeolum parvifolium is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract beneficial insects.
