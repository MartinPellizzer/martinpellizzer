Teucrium luteum subsp. latifolium

Teucrium luteum subsp.

latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features erect, branched stems and lanceolate to ovate leaves that are typically gray-green in color.

The plant produces showy, yellowish-orange flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its aromatic leaves and resilience to drought conditions make it a valuable addition to xeriscaped gardens.
