Trichomanes vittaria

Trichomanes vittaria, commonly known as the veined maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically bright green with a pale midrib. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are usually small and arranged in a tight, compact rosette.

Trichomanes vittaria is notable for its intricate venation and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
