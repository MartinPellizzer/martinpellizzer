Tridactyle nyassana

Tridactyle nyassana, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has a woody stem and produces small, round fruits that contain numerous seeds. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, with a glossy surface.

Tridactyle nyassana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils.
