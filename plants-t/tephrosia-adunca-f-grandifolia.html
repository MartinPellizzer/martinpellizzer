Tephrosia adunca f. grandifolia

Tephrosia adunca f. grandifolia, commonly known as the large-flowered tephrosia, is a perennial shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with numerous elliptical leaflets and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in racemes. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along riverbanks.

Botanically, it is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
