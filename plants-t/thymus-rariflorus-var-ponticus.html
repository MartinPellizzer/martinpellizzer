Thymus rariflorus var. ponticus

Thymus rariflorus var. ponticus, commonly known as the Pontic thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southeastern Europe and southwestern Asia.

It features aromatic, grey-green foliage and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes. Its essential oils, rich in thymol and carvacrol, contribute to its use in aromatherapy and traditional medicine.

The variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in temperate climates.
