Thalictrum altissimum

Thalictrum altissimum, commonly known as the tall meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features slender, upright stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, adorned with compound leaves that are palmately divided. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fluffy flowers in early to mid-summer, which are arranged in an umbel-like inflorescence. Its foliage is characterized by a delicate, fern-like appearance, with leaves that are typically three to five lobed.

Thalictrum altissimum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
