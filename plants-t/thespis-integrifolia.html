Thespis integrifolia

Thespis integrifolia, commonly known as the smoothleaf thistle, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has hairy stems and leaves that are usually smooth-edged. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Thespis integrifolia is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
