Triticum polonicum var. abyssinicum

Triticum polonicum var. abyssinicum, commonly known as Ethiopian triticale, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between wheat and rye.

It is characterized by its tall, robust growth habit and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. The plant produces spikelets that contain both wheat and rye grains, which contribute to its unique genetic composition. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture, and it flowers in the summer months.

This variety is often cultivated for its high biomass yield and potential use in forage and animal feed.
