Thymus brevicalyx

Thymus brevicalyx, commonly known as narrow-leaved thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically gray-green in color and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Its compact growth habit and fragrant foliage make it a popular choice for rock gardens and ornamental landscaping.
