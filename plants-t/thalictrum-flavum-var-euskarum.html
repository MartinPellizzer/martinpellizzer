Thalictrum flavum var. euskarum

Thalictrum flavum var. euskarum, commonly known as the golden rue or meadow rue, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves and produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers in early to mid-summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are composed of multiple small, petal-less sepals that give it a unique, lace-like appearance.

Thalictrum flavum var. euskarum is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
