Tradescantia reflexa

Tradescantia reflexa, commonly known as the snake plant or mother of millions, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright, sword-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white and green stripes. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, typically in shades of white or green. It is a member of the Commelinaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions.

Tradescantia reflexa is also noted for its air-purifying qualities and is a popular houseplant due to its resilience and aesthetic appeal.
