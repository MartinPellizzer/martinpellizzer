Tetradium ruticarpum

Tetradium ruticarpum, commonly known as the Chinese frankincense tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces a resin that has been traditionally used in Chinese medicine for its purported therapeutic properties. It typically grows in mountainous regions and is valued for both its ornamental and medicinal qualities.

The bark and leaves are often harvested for use in herbal remedies.
