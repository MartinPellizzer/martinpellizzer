Tillandsia fuchsii var. fuchsii

Tillandsia fuchsii var. fuchsii, commonly known as the Fuchs' air plant, is a species of epiphytic bromeliad native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help in absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air. The plant produces striking inflorescences with bright red or orange flowers, which bloom once in its lifetime. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires minimal soil, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a favored plant among enthusiasts of air plants.
