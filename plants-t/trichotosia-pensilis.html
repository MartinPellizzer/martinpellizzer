Trichotosia pensilis

Trichotosia pensilis, commonly known as the hanging hair-vetch, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features delicate, slender stems that often hang downward, giving it its common name. Its small, yellow flowers are clustered in racemes, and it produces cylindrical pods containing several seeds.

Trichotosia pensilis is typically found in grasslands and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.
