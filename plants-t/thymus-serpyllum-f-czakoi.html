Thymus serpyllum f. czakoi

Thymus serpyllum f. czakoi, commonly known as the Czech thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It features aromatic, small, grey-green leaves that are densely packed and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces pink to violet flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary uses, similar to other thyme species.
