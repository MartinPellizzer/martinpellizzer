Tristachya bequaertii

Tristachya bequaertii, commonly known as the African feather grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and delicate, feathery inflorescences that resemble plumes. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive waxy coating that helps reduce water loss.

Tristachya bequaertii is often used in ornamental gardening for its elegant appearance and low maintenance requirements.
