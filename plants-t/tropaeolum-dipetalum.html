Tropaeolum dipetalum

Tropaeolum dipetalum, commonly known as the two-petaled nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, edible flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into round, seed-containing pods. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its botanical features include showy blooms and a peppery taste, which makes it both ornamental and edible.
