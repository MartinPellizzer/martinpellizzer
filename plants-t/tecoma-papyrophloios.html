Tecoma papyrophloios

Tecoma papyrophloios, commonly known as the yellow flame tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, pointed leaflets and produces woody pods that contain seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental species in warm climates.

The botanical features of Tecoma papyrophloios make it both visually striking and ecologically valuable.
