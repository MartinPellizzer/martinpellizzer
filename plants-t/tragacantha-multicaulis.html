Tragacantha multicaulis

Tragacantha multicaulis, commonly known as the Persian gum plant, is a perennial shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and grayish-green leaves that are adapted to conserve water. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, followed by capsules containing seeds. It is well-known for its sap, which is harvested to produce tragacanth, a natural gum used in various industries.

Tragacantha multicaulis thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping and arid landscapes.
