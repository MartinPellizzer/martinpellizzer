Tabebuia punctatissima

Tabebuia punctatissima, commonly known as the "pink trumpet tree," is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The tree has a straight trunk and a rounded crown, with smooth, gray bark that becomes fissured with age. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a pointed tip.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in urban landscapes for its vibrant floral display.
