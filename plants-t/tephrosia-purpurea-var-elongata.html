Tephrosia purpurea var. elongata

Tephrosia purpurea var. elongata, commonly known as purple crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and clusters of small, purple to pinkish flowers. The leaves are typically pinnately divided, with a feathery appearance, and the plant often grows in moist, well-drained soils. It is a member of the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or pea family, and is noted for its ornamental value in gardens.

The plant can reach heights of up to 2 meters and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
