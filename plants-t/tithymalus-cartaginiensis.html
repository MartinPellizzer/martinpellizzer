Tithymalus cartaginiensis

Tithymalus cartaginiensis, commonly known as the Cartagena tithymale, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and has narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its small yellow flowers bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
