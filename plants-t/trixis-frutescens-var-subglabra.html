Trixis frutescens var. subglabra

Trixis frutescens var. subglabra, commonly known as the smooth trixis, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a small shrub characterized by its branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, soft pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the rainy season. It is adapted to thrive in sandy or rocky soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited to its harsh environment.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
