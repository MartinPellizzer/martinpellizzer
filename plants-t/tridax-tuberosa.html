Tridax tuberosa

Tridax tuberosa, commonly known as the hairy beggar's ticks, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which contribute to its common name. It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open fields.

Tridax tuberosa is known for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
