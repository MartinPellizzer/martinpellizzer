Tulipa praecox var. planifolia

Tulipa praecox var. planifolia, commonly known as the Early Tulip or the Persian Tulip, is a species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its early blooming habit, typically flowering in late winter or early spring. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of red, yellow, and orange. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a bulbous base.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant early-season blooms.
