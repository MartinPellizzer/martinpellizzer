Trichodesma hockii

Trichodesma hockii, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Trichodesma hockii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
