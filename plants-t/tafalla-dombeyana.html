Tafalla dombeyana

Tafalla dombeyana, commonly known as the Dombey's tafalla, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
