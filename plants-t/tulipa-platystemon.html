Tulipa platystemon

Tulipa platystemon, commonly known as the Persian tulip, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from red to yellow and orange. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its bulbs are adapted to survive in cold climates, and it blooms in late spring to early summer.

The Persian tulip is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
