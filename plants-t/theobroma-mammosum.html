Theobroma mammosum

Theobroma mammosum, commonly known as the "cocoa tree" or "cacao tree," is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Amazon basin.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain the seeds used to make cocoa products. Theobroma mammosum thrives in humid, shaded environments with well-drained soil.
