Thalictrum davuricum

Thalictrum davuricum, commonly known as the meadow rue, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of Asia and North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which includes other notable genera such as Ranunculus and Clematis. Thalictrum davuricum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges, meadows, and stream banks.
