Trifolium trichostomum

Trifolium trichostomum, commonly known as the hairy clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, often forming dense clusters. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and is commonly found in meadows and grassy areas.

Trifolium trichostomum is a hardy perennial that can thrive in a variety of soil types and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
