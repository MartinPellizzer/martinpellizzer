Tulipa graminifolia

Tulipa graminifolia, commonly known as the grass-leaved tulip, is a species of flowering plant in the family Liliaceae.

It is native to Turkey and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and showy, cup-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It blooms in early spring, producing flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red.

Tulipa graminifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant spring display.
