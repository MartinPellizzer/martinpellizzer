Tisserantodendron walkeri

Tisserantodendron walkeri, commonly known as the Walkeri's evergreen tree, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, broad leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, which are usually white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in humid environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
