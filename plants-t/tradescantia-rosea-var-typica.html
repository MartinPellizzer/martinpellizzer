Tradescantia rosea var. typica

Tradescantia rosea var. typica, commonly known as the spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features striking, showy flowers that range in color from violet to pink, often with a distinctive red or purple throat. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically bluish-green, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soil. It is a herbaceous perennial that spreads via rhizomes, forming dense mats in woodland or wetland areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
