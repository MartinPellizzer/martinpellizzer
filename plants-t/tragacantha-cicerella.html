Tragacantha cicerella

Tragacantha cicerella, commonly known as the lesser tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough, hairy texture.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and scrublands.
