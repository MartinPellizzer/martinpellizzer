Taraxacum denticorne

Taraxacum denticorne, commonly known as the toothed dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated, alternate leaves and a yellow flower head that develops into a fluffy seed head. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. It has a taproot system and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and wastelands.

The species is notable for its ability to spread via wind-dispersed seeds, contributing to its widespread distribution.
