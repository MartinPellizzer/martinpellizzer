Tournesol tinctoria

Tournesol tinctoria, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy orange or yellow-orange ray flowers and dark green, heart-shaped leaves. The plant is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and ragweed. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

The plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
