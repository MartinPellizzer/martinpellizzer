Tritonia magniflora

Tritonia magniflora, commonly known as the grand bellflower, is a striking perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from deep purple to white, depending on the variety. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Tritonia magniflora is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
