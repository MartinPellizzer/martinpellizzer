Thryptomene pinifolia

Thryptomene pinifolia, commonly known as the needle-leaved thryptomene, is a small shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalypts and other aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, white or pinkish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences.

Thryptomene pinifolia is often found in heathlands and dry sclerophyll forests, thriving in well-drained soils.
