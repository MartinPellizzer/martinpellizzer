Turbinicarpus graminispinus

Turbinicarpus graminispinus, commonly known as the grass-leafed hedgehog cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are covered in small, spiny tubercles. The plant produces pale green or yellow flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are reduced to small, grass-like structures at the base of the stems.

Turbinicarpus graminispinus is a slow-growing, drought-tolerant plant that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.
