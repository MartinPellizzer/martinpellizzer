Tribulus macropterus var. megistopterus

Tribulus macropterus var. megistopterus, commonly known as the giant caltrop, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, globular fruit that contains four seeds, each with a distinctive, sharp point. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grassland and shrubland habitats. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three ovate to lanceolate leaflets, and it produces small, yellowish flowers.

The plant is known for its hardy nature and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
