Thuja occidentalis var. douglasii-pyramidalis

Thuja occidentalis var. douglasii-pyramidalis, commonly known as the pyramidal white cedar, is a cultivar of the eastern arborvitae.

It is characterized by its narrow, conical shape and dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces small, scale-like leaves that are tightly packed along the stems. It is native to North America and is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and formal appearance.

This variety thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
