Tilia × pseudokneuckeri

Tilia × pseudokneuckeri, commonly known as the hybrid linden, is a deciduous tree that results from the crossbreeding of Tilia cordata and Tilia europaea.

It exhibits characteristics of both parent species, featuring heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. The tree is known for its rapid growth and adaptability to various soil types, making it a popular ornamental plant. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age, and it produces small, spherical fruits that contain seeds.

The plant is also valued for its ability to provide shade and its historical use in traditional medicine.
