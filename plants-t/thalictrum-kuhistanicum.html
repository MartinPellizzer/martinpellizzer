Thalictrum kuhistanicum

Thalictrum kuhistanicum, commonly known as the Kuhistan meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Hindu Kush.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as meadows and mountain slopes. Its botanical features include a rhizomatous root system and showy, spicate inflorescences.

Thalictrum kuhistanicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
