Terminalia disticha

Terminalia disticha, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and elliptical leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The tree produces small, pale yellow flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing flat, winged seeds. It is valued for its hard, heavy wood used in construction and furniture, and it also has traditional medicinal uses.

The plant is adapted to dry conditions and plays an important ecological role in its native habitat.
