Tragacantha pontica

Tragacantha pontica, commonly known as the Caucasian tragacanth, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, followed by pods containing small, dark seeds. Its roots are known for producing a viscous gum, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate climates with adequate sunlight.
