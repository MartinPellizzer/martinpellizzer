Tessmannia dewildemaniana var. dewildemaniana

Tessmannia dewildemaniana var. dewildemaniana, commonly known as the Western Australian tea tree, is a shrub native to the southwestern region of Australia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is known for its essential oils, which are used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native habitat.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
