Tibouchina gracilis var. chamissoana

Tibouchina gracilis var. chamissoana, commonly known as the Chilean flame bush, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or pink tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Tibouchina gracilis var. chamissoana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in coastal environments.
