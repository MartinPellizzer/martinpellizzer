Taraxacum maculigerum

Taraxacum maculigerum, commonly known as the spotted dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of alternate, hairy, and spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces a hollow, hairy stem that supports a single, inconspicuous yellow flower head. The flower develops into a ball of white, feathery seeds with a pappus for wind dispersal.

It is native to Europe and is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats.
