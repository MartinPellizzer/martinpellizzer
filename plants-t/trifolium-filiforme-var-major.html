Trifolium filiforme var. major

Trifolium filiforme var. major, commonly known as the large slender clover, is a flowering plant species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three narrow, elongated leaflets. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

This variety is notable for its delicate appearance and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
