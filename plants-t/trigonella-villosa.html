Trigonella villosa

Trigonella villosa, commonly known as hairy fenugreek, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, branched stems covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by kidney-shaped pods containing several seeds. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Trigonella villosa is often grown for its seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.
