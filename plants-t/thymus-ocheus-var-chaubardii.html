Thymus ocheus var. chaubardii

Thymus ocheus var. chaubardii, commonly known as Chaubard's thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features aromatic, narrow leaves and produces small, pinkish-white flowers in early spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders. Its compact growth habit and fragrant foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

It is also valued for its medicinal properties, similar to other thyme species.
