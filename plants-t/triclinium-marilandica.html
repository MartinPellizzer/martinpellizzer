Triclinium marilandica

Triclinium marilandica, commonly known as Maryland morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and trumpet-shaped flowers. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive botanical appearance.
