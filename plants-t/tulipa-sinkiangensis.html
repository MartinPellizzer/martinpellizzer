Tulipa sinkiangensis

Tulipa sinkiangensis, commonly known as the Xinjiang tulip, is a species native to the Xinjiang region of China.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its striking, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces bulbous structures that store nutrients for the next growing season.

Tulipa sinkiangensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
