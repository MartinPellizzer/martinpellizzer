Teucrium belion

Teucrium belion, commonly known as the Balkan germander, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant features slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a slightly rough texture. Its flowers are small, tubular, and usually violet or blue in color, appearing in clusters during the summer months.

Teucrium belion is valued in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.
