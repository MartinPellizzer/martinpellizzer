Trifolium medium var. microphyllum

Trifolium medium var. microphyllum, commonly known as the lesser clover, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, trifoliate leaves composed of three oval to round leaflets, typically no larger than 1 centimeter in length. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are usually pink or reddish in color, blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and as a forage plant for livestock.
