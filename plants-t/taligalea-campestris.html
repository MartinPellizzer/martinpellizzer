Taligalea campestris

Taligalea campestris, commonly known as the field taligalea, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and along roadsides.

Taligalea campestris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
