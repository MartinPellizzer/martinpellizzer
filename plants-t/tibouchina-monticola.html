Tibouchina monticola

Tibouchina monticola, commonly known as the mountain tibouchina, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to purple bract-like flowers. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.
