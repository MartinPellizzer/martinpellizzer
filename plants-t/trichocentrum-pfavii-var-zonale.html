Trichocentrum pfavii var. zonale

Trichocentrum pfavii var. zonale, commonly known as the Zoned Orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, strap-like leaves and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are often used in floral arrangements due to their beauty and fragrance.

The varietal name "zonale" refers to the distinct zoned or banded appearance of its petals.
