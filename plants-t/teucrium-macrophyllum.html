Teucrium macrophyllum

Teucrium macrophyllum, commonly known as the large-leaved germander, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Teucrium macrophyllum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
