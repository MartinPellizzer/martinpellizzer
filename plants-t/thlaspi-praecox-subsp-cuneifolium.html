Thlaspi praecox subsp. cuneifolium

Thlaspi praecox subsp.

cuneifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leafed winter cullen, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and it produces small, yellowish-white flowers in early spring.

It is a hardy species that can tolerate cold temperatures and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
