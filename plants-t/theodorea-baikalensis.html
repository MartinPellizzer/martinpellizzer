Theodorea baikalensis

Theodorea baikalensis, commonly known as the Baikal lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Lake Baikal region in Russia.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features slender, upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy. Its flowers are typically arranged in racemes and are known for their delicate, bell-like appearance.

Theodorea baikalensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique visual appeal.
