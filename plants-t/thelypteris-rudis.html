Thelypteris rudis

Thelypteris rudis, commonly known as the rough holly fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Thelypteridaceae and is characterized by its leathery, serrated fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine in some cultures.

Thelypteris rudis exhibits a distinctive reproductive structure with sori that are covered by a thin, membranous indusium.
