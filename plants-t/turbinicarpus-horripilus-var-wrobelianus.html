Turbinicarpus horripilus var. wrobelianus

Turbinicarpus horripilus var. wrobelianus, commonly known as the Wrobelianus Turbinicarpus, is a species of succulent cactus native to South America, specifically Argentina.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are covered in dense, white to pale green spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically short-lived but striking in appearance. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring well-draining soil and minimal water.

Due to its unique appearance and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated by succulent enthusiasts.
