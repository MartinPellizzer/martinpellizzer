Trimezia coerulea subsp. eximia

Trimezia coerulea subsp.

eximia, commonly known as the blue trimezia, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America. It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has lance-shaped leaves with a smooth texture. It is a perennial herb that thrives in cool climates and well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
