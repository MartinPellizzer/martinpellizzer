Torilis trilobata

Torilis trilobata, commonly known as three-lobed groundsel, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and daisy-like appearance. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. Its leaves are lobed and often hairy, while the flowers are yellow with ray florets.

Torilis trilobata is an annual plant that produces small, achene-like seeds that are dispersed by wind.
