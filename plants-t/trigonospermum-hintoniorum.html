Trigonospermum hintoniorum

Trigonospermum hintoniorum, commonly known as the Hinton's triangleflower, is a rare and unique plant species native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its striking, triangular-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of violet and blue. The plant has small, serrated leaves that are adapted to withstand the cold and strong winds of its mountainous habitat. It thrives in well-drained, nutrient-poor soils and requires full sunlight for optimal growth.

Due to its limited distribution and specialized ecological requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species.
