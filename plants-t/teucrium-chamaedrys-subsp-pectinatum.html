Teucrium chamaedrys subsp. pectinatum

Teucrium chamaedrys subsp.

pectinatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It typically grows in rocky or dry soils, often found in mountainous regions.

The plant has slender, upright stems with opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, clustered flowers are usually pink to purple in color, blooming in the summer months.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
