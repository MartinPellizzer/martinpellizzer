Triadenum japonicum var. humile

Triadenum japonicum var. humile, commonly known as Japanese triadenum, is a low-growing perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features small, trifoliate leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, with each leaflet being ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
