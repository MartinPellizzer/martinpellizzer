Trachelospermum formosanum

Trachelospermum formosanum, commonly known as the sweetheart vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth and cascading habit, making it suitable for trellises and vertical gardens. The plant produces fragrant, star-shaped white flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Trachelospermum formosanum is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
