Trichophorum cespitosum var. callosum

Trichophorum cespitosum var. callosum, commonly known as the tufted sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the sedge family. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and wet meadows, and is often found in temperate regions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
