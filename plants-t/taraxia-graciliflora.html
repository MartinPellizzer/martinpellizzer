Taraxia graciliflora

Taraxia graciliflora, commonly known as slender-flowered taraxia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open woodland areas, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy, white seed heads that aid in wind pollination.

Taraxia graciliflora is often found in coastal and inland regions, displaying adaptations to varying environmental conditions.
