Trema asperum var. viride

Trema asperum var. viride, commonly known as the green-leafed trema, is a variant of the species Trema asperum, which belongs to the Rubiaceae family.

It is characterized by its smooth, waxy leaves that are typically a vibrant green in color. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, spherical berries. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

The leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
