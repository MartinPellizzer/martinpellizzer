Thymus serbicus

Thymus serbicus, commonly known as Serbian thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense clusters. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, which contribute to its use in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.

Thymus serbicus thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.
