Thymus cephalotos var. portae

Thymus cephalotos var. portae, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thyme, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with small, aromatic leaves that are typically narrow and pointed. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its medicinal properties have been historically valued for their antiseptic and aromatic qualities.
