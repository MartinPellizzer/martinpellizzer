Teucrium corymbosum var. petrophilum

Teucrium corymbosum var. petrophilum, commonly known as rock cranesbill, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and is often found growing in rocky or gravelly soils. It is adapted to full sun and well-drained conditions, making it a hardy species suitable for xeriscaping.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological gardens and wildlife habitats.
