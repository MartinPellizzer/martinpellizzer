Taraxacum inclinorum

Taraxacum inclinorum, commonly known as the leaning dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of elongated, hairy leaves that curve inward, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces a single, tall flowering stem that bears a single capitulum, which develops into a spherical seed head. It is native to Europe and is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats.

The species is notable for its unique growth habit and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
