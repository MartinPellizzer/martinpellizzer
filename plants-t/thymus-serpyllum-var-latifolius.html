Thymus serpyllum var. latifolius

Thymus serpyllum var. latifolius, commonly known as broadleaf thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features dense, creeping stems that produce small, oval-shaped leaves with a slightly fuzzy texture. The plant produces tiny white or pale pink flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic leaves are often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
