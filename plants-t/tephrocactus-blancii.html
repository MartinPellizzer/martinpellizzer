Tephrocactus blancii

Tephrocactus blancii, commonly known as the white-tipped cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically pale green to white in color. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are clustered at the ends of the stems. It has a shallow root system adapted to absorb water from the soil quickly after rainfall.

Tephrocactus blancii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in dry, well-drained conditions.
