Trixis radialis var. pubescens

Trixis radialis var. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent trixis, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy or pubescent leaves, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming during the cooler months. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to access moisture from deeper soil layers.

This variety is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its resilience in dry conditions.
