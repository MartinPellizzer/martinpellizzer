Tripterospermum pallidum

Tripterospermum pallidum, commonly known as the white-flowered tripterospermum, is a deciduous shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually oval or elliptical in shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Tripterospermum pallidum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
