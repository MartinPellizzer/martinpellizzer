Tulipa tetraphylla

Tulipa tetraphylla, commonly known as the four-leaf tulip, is a species of flowering plant in the lily family.

It is native to Central Asia and is characterized by its striking, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The plant typically grows to a height of 15 to 30 centimeters and produces four petals, which give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The bulbs are perennial, and the plant is known for its resilience in colder climates.
