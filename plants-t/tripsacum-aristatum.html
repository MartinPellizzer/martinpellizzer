Tripsacum aristatum

Tripsacum aristatum, commonly known as the tall bristlegrass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, robust stalks and dense, spike-like inflorescences. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is adapted to both wet and dry conditions.

Tripsacum aristatum is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks, contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for various wildlife.
