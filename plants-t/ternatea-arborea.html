Ternatea arborea

Ternatea arborea, commonly known as the tree of heaven, is a tropical evergreen species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmately compound leaves with three to five leaflets, which are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds. Ternatea arborea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been introduced to various regions outside its native range.

It is also known for its ability to thrive in a wide range of soil types and environmental conditions.
