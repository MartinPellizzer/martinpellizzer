Thymopsis thymoides var. thymoides

Thymopsis thymoides var. thymoides, commonly known as narrowleaf thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically narrow and linear, with a dense, mat-like growth habit. The plant produces tiny, pinkish-white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its foliage is often used in culinary and medicinal applications due to its aromatic properties.
