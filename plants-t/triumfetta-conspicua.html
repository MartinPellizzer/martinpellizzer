Triumfetta conspicua

Triumfetta conspicua, commonly known as the showy triumfetta, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 1–3 meters, featuring pinnate leaves with 5–7 pairs of leaflets. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that are often followed by spherical, fleshy fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas or disturbed habitats.

Triumfetta conspicua is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
