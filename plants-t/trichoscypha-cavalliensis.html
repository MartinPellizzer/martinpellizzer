Trichoscypha cavalliensis

Trichoscypha cavalliensis, commonly known as the African snowball, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of white flowers that appear in late winter or early spring, giving it its common name. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Trichoscypha cavalliensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
