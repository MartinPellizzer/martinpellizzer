Tetrapterys rotundifolia var. platyptera

Tetrapterys rotundifolia var. platyptera, commonly known as the broad-leafed tetrapterys, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rounded, broad leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
