Thespesia grandiflora

Thespesia grandiflora, commonly known as the Tahitian hibiscus, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Pacific Islands.

It features showy, five-petaled flowers that are typically pink or red, with a prominent yellow stamen. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are opposite and often covered in fine hairs. It produces woody, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Thespesia grandiflora is adapted to coastal environments and is often found in sandy soils along shorelines.
