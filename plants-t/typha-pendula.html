Typha pendula

Typha pendula, commonly known as the hanging cattail, is a tall, herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It features long, cylindrical spikes of brownish flowers that hang downward, giving it its common name. The plant has broad, flat leaves that are green on top and white underneath, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in shallow water and moist soil, often forming dense colonies in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Typha pendula plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
