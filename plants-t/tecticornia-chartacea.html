Tecticornia chartacea

Tecticornia chartacea, commonly known as the paperflower, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are often white or pale blue in color. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a fine, waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

Tecticornia chartacea is adapted to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and is often found in coastal and inland dunes.
