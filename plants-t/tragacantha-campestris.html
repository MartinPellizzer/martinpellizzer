Tragacantha campestris

Tragacantha campestris, commonly known as the field tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its roots are known for their high mucilage content, which has been historically used in traditional medicine.

The species is also valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a forage plant for livestock.
