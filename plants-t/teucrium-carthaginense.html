Teucrium carthaginense

Teucrium carthaginense, commonly known as Carthaginian germander, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, clustered in spikes, and range in color from pink to purple, blooming in the summer months.

Teucrium carthaginense is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
