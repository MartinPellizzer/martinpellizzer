Tradescantia texensis

Tradescantia texensis, commonly known as Texas spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 6 to 12 inches. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are usually bright green, and it produces clusters of small, delicate flowers in shades of blue, purple, or white. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its ability to spread via rhizomes allows it to form dense ground covers, which helps suppress weeds and prevent erosion.
