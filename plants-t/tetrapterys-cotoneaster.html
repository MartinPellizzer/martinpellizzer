Tetrapterys cotoneaster

Tetrapterys cotoneaster, commonly known as the Chinese cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, clustered white or pink flowers in the spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Tetrapterys cotoneaster is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of edible fruits.
