Taraxacum sanguineum

Taraxacum sanguineum, commonly known as the bloodroot dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of pale green, hairy leaves and a single, showy yellow flower that blooms in spring. The plant produces a capsule that contains numerous seeds with feathery parachutes, aiding in wind dispersal. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grassy areas.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
