Tanacetum heterotomum

Tanacetum heterotomum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tansy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a strongly aromatic, camphor-like scent. Its yellow flower heads consist of numerous small florets, typical of the daisy family.

It thrives in moist, sunny habitats and is often found in wet meadows and riverbanks.
