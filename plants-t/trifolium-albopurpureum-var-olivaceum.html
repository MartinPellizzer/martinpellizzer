Trifolium albopurpureum var. olivaceum

Trifolium albopurpureum var. olivaceum, commonly known as the olive-purple clover, is a variant of the trifoliate clover genus.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which typically have three oval-shaped leaflets with a pale purple or olive-green hue. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring, adding a delicate touch to meadows and pastures. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in grassy areas with well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in soil stabilization and livestock forage.
