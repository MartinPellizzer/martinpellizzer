Trema lanceolatum

Trema lanceolatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf trema, is a small, deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its simple, lance-shaped leaves that are alternate along the stem. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by smooth, spherical fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
