Tomophyllum walleri

Tomophyllum walleri, commonly known as the golden bell plant, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a woody stem and produces small, oval leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements contribute to its appeal in both ornamental and ecological settings.
