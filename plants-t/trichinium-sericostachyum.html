Trichinium sericostachyum

Trichinium sericostachyum, commonly known as the silver-stemmed fern, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems that are covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered leaves that are typically lance-shaped and have a leathery texture. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Trichinium sericostachyum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
