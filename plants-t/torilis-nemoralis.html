Torilis nemoralis

Torilis nemoralis, commonly known as field eryngo, is a flowering plant native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Torilis nemoralis produces small, oval fruits known as achenes, which are dispersed by wind.
