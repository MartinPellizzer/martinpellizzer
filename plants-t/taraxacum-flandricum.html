Taraxacum flandricum

Taraxacum flandricum, commonly known as the Flanders dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features a rosette of basal, spoon-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces a single, hollow, yellow-orange flower head that blooms in spring and early summer. It is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower structure.

Taraxacum flandricum is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and is known for its medicinal properties.
