Tropaeolum schlimii

Tropaeolum schlimii, commonly known as the Peruvian Tropaeolum, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, edible tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, yellow, and orange. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are slender and branching.

Tropaeolum schlimii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible flowers in gardens and culinary applications.
