Tillandsia incarnata

Tillandsia incarnata, commonly known as the pink air plant, is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is native to Central and South America.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on other plants without deriving nutrients from them. The plant is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help in absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air. It produces striking pink or red flowers that bloom from the center of the rosette.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
