Trifolium barbulatum

Trifolium barbulatum, commonly known as the hairy clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed areas.

Trifolium barbulatum is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it an important plant in soil enrichment.
