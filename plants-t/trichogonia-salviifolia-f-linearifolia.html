Trichogonia salviifolia f. linearifolia

Trichogonia salviifolia f. linearifolia, commonly known as the linear-leafed salvifolia, is a variant of the species Trichogonia salviifolia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, which bloom during the summer months. Its foliage is often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture.

This form is notable for its distinct leaf morphology, which sets it apart from the typical form of the species.
