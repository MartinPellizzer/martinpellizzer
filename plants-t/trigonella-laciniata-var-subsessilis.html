Trigonella laciniata var. subsessilis

Trigonella laciniata var. subsessilis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved fenugreek, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, lens-shaped seeds that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and agricultural studies.
