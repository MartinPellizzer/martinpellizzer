Tragacantha arida

Tragacantha arida, commonly known as desert tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit with woody stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by pods containing small, kidney-shaped seeds. Its roots are adapted to survive in dry conditions, and it is often used in traditional medicine for its mucilaginous properties.

Tragacantha arida is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil in desert environments.
