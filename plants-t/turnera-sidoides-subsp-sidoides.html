Turnera sidoides subsp. sidoides

Turnera sidoides subsp.

sidoides, commonly known as the golden turnera, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America. It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions.

The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers. Its flowers are often pollinated by bees and other insects, contributing to its ecological significance.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine.
