Thermopsis

Thermopsis, commonly known as yellowroot, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

These plants are characterized by their bright yellow flowers and are native to various regions across the Northern Hemisphere. They typically grow in rocky or sandy soils and are often found in open, sunny habitats. Thermopsis species are known for their medicinal properties, with some varieties used in traditional herbal remedies.

The plant's name is derived from the Greek word "thermos," meaning heat, referring to its association with warm climates.
