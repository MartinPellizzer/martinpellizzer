Thrasya cinerascens

Thrasya cinerascens, commonly known as the grayleaf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that primarily grows on the branches of eucalyptus trees in southern Australia.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually grayish-green in color, which helps it blend with its host. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, fleshy berries that are an important food source for native birds. Thrasya cinerascens relies on birds for seed dispersal, as its seeds are enclosed in a sticky pulp that adheres to the feathers of birds.

Its ecological role is significant in maintaining biodiversity within its native habitat.
