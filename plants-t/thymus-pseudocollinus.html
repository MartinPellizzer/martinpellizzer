Thymus pseudocollinus

Thymus pseudocollinus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, tiny, dark green leaves and clusters of small, pale pink to white flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments. It is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.

Thymus pseudocollinus is also a popular ornamental plant due to its hardy nature and fragrant foliage.
