Trisetum wolfii

Trisetum wolfii, commonly known as the prairie dropseed, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and fine, silver-green foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Trisetum wolfii thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairie and open woodland habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant landscapes.
