Tilia cordata subsp. nasczokinii

Tilia cordata subsp.

nasczokinii, commonly known as the Caucasian lime, is a rare subspecies native to the Caucasus region. It is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree with heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath.

The flowers are small, yellowish-green, and appear in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value. It produces small, round fruits that mature in late summer.

This subspecies is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and historical significance in horticulture.
