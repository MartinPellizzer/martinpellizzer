Tibouchina angustifolia

Tibouchina angustifolia, commonly known as flame bush, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or pink tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has lance-shaped, glossy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Tibouchina angustifolia is also valued for its ability to tolerate partial shade and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
