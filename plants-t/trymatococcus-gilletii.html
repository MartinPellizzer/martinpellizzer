Trymatococcus gilletii

Trymatococcus gilletii, commonly known as the desert milkweed, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing shrub with pale green, succulent stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in dry environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and fleshy, adapted to reduce water loss through transpiration.

The plant plays a vital role in desert ecosystems by providing food and habitat for various insects and animals.
