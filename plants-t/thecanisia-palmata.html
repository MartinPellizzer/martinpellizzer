Thecanisia palmata

Thecanisia palmata, commonly known as the red-pod palm, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by distinctive red, fleshy fruits. Thecanisia palmata is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking appearance and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to tropical gardens and botanical collections.
