Trieenea longipedicellata

Trieenea longipedicellata, commonly known as the long-pedicelled starflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, star-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has slender, branched stems and produces small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
