Taraxacum kurohimense

Taraxacum kurohimense, commonly known as the Japanese dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its basal rosette of pale green, hairy leaves. The plant produces a single, hollow, yellow flower head that blooms in the spring. It is often found in moist, shaded areas such as forests and along stream banks.

Taraxacum kurohimense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
