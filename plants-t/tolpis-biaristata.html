Tolpis biaristata

Tolpis biaristata, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it is often used in ornamental gardening due to its vibrant blooms.

Tolpis biaristata is also valued for its ability to thrive in arid conditions, making it a resilient choice for drought-tolerant landscapes.
