Thalictrum tripeltatum

Thalictrum tripeltatum, commonly known as the white meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white flowers in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a fibrous root system and can spread via rhizomes, forming dense colonies.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
