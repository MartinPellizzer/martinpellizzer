Tulipa linifolia

Tulipa linifolia, commonly known as the silky tulip, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Tulipa.

It is native to Central Asia and is characterized by its delicate, hairy leaves and showy, cup-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink and yellow, bloom in late spring.

Tulipa linifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
