Trichilia excisa

Trichilia excisa, commonly known as African mahogany, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and large, palmate leaves. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by woody, five-lobed fruits containing flat, winged seeds. It is valued for its durable, aromatic wood, which is used in furniture and carving.

Trichilia excisa also plays an important role in traditional medicine and local ecosystems.
