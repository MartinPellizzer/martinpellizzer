Tetrapterys lasiocarpa

Tetrapterys lasiocarpa, commonly known as the woolly leaf mallow, is a perennial herb native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves and showy, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and along stream banks. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
