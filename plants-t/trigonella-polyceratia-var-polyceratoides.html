Trigonella polyceratia var. polyceratoides

Trigonella polyceratia var. polyceratoides, commonly known as the horned milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, branched stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and is often found in dry, rocky soils. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules.

This variant is noted for its unique morphology and ecological adaptations to arid environments.
