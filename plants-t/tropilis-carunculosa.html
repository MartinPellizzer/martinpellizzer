Tropilis carunculosa

Tropilis carunculosa, commonly known as the red-flowered bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to deep red, often with a contrasting throat. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
