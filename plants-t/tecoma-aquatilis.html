Tecoma aquatilis

Tecoma aquatilis, commonly known as the water trumpet, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow or orange trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has long, slender leaves and is often found growing near water sources such as rivers and lakes. It is a deciduous shrub that can reach heights of up to 3 meters.

Tecoma aquatilis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
