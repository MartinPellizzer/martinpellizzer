Trembleya phlogiformis var. ramosissima

Trembleya phlogiformis var. ramosissima, commonly known as the hairy trembleya, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine pubescence.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in tropical gardens.
