Taraxacum nematolobum

Taraxacum nematolobum, commonly known as the narrowleaf dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, elongated leaves and a flowering stem that bears a single, yellow composite flower head. The plant is native to parts of Europe and Asia and is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides. Its flowers develop into a distinctive, hollow, stem-like structure called a pappus, which aids in wind pollination.

The species is notable for its medicinal uses and is sometimes cultivated for its edible leaves and roots.
