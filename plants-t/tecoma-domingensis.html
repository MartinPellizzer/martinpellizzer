Tecoma domingensis

Tecoma domingensis, commonly known as the yellow bells, is a flowering plant native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and showy yellow tubular flowers. The plant produces small, woody capsules that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Tecoma domingensis thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical climates.

Its ornamental value is due to its vibrant yellow flowers and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
