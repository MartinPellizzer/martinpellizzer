Tripterygium hypoglaucum

Tripterygium hypoglaucum, commonly known as the Chinese thunder god vine, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Cucurbitaceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its woody stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange-red berries. It contains various bioactive compounds, including tripterygium glycosides, which have been studied for their medicinal properties.

Due to its toxic nature, it is used cautiously in traditional Chinese medicine.
