Theopyxis chilensis

Theopyxis chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean myrtle, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and leathery, evergreen leaves. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in coastal areas and mountainous regions.

The botanical features of Theopyxis chilensis make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and ecological research.
