Tritonia purpurea

Tritonia purpurea, commonly known as the purple tritonia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking purple flowers. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped blooms that are often clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.
