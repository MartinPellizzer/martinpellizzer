Thymus pulegioides var. barbulatus

Thymus pulegioides var. barbulatus, commonly known as woolly thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features dense, gray-green foliage covered in fine, hairy glands, giving it a distinctive woolly appearance. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers in early summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic leaves are often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
