Trymalium ledifolium var. rosmarinifolium

Trymalium ledifolium var. rosmarinifolium, commonly known as the rosemary-leaved trymalium, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features aromatic, needle-like leaves that are gray-green in color and resemble those of rosemary. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is often used in traditional herbal remedies due to its essential oils.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for coastal gardens.
