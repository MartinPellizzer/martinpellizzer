Trifolium ultramontanum

Trifolium ultramontanum, commonly known as the Alpine clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and small white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky areas. It is adapted to cold climates and can withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Trifolium ultramontanum is also valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a forage plant for livestock.
