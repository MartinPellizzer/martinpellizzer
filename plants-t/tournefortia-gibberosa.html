Tournefortia gibberosa

Tournefortia gibberosa, commonly known as the roughleaf tournefortia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2 to 5 meters. The plant features simple, opposite leaves that are usually rough or hairy to the touch. Its flowers are small, clustered in umbels, and typically have five petals.

Tournefortia gibberosa is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy or saline soils.
