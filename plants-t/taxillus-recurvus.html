Taxillus recurvus

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using light reflected from the host. The plant produces small, white or pale green berries that contain toxic compounds, making them harmful to mammals and birds. Its life cycle involves seeds being dispersed by birds that consume the berries, leading to new infections on suitable host trees.

Taxillus recurvus plays a role in ecological interactions, influencing the health and diversity of forest ecosystems.
