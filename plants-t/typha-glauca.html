Typha glauca

Typha glauca, commonly known as the blue cattail, is a wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical flower spikes that are typically greenish-blue in color. The plant has broad, sword-shaped leaves that grow from a rhizomatous stem, allowing it to spread vegetatively. It thrives in shallow freshwater environments such as marshes and ponds.

Typha glauca plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
