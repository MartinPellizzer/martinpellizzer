Tragia laciniata

Tragia laciniata, commonly known as the cut-leaved tragia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, lobed leaves that are often deeply divided, giving it a feathery appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It is often found in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

Tragia laciniata is also known for its ability to tolerate drought conditions, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
