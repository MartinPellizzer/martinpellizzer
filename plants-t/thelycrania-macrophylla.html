Thelycrania macrophylla

Thelycrania macrophylla, commonly known as the giant waterwheel plant, is a unique aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, waxy leaves that can grow up to 3 meters in length, which help it float on the water's surface. The plant has a distinctive spiral arrangement of leaves, giving it a wheel-like appearance. It thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments and is often found in marshes and swamps.

Thelycrania macrophylla plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
