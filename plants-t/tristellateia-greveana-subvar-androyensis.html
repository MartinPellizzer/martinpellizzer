Tristellateia greveana subvar. androyensis

Tristellateia greveana subvar. androyensis, commonly known as the Madagascar lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its striking, large, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves arranged in a rosette pattern at the base. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
