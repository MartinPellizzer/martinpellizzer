Theobroma purpureum

Theobroma purpureum, commonly known as the purple cocoa tree, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and showy, purple flowers. The plant produces pods that contain cocoa beans, which are used in the production of chocolate. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.
