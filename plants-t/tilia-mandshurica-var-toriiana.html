Tilia mandshurica var. toriiana

Tilia mandshurica var. toriiana, commonly known as the Manchurian linden, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Tiliaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are typically serrated along the edges. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which bloom in early summer and are followed by small, round fruits.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas, where it contributes to biodiversity and provides habitat for various wildlife species.
