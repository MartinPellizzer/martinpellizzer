Taenitis desvauxii

Taenitis desvauxii, commonly known as the giant swamp taro, is a striking aquatic plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length, often floating on the water's surface. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers with a distinctive spadix, typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as marshes and ponds.

Its robust growth makes it both an ornamental and potentially invasive species in certain regions.
