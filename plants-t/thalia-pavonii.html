Thalia pavonii

Thalia pavonii, commonly known as the purple tamarind, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking purple and pink flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Thalia pavonii is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
