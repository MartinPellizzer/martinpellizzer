Thymus serpyllum f. obtusus

Thymus serpyllum f. obtusus, commonly known as narrowleaf thyme, is a compact, low-growing perennial herb native to Europe.

It features small, rounded leaves that are typically less serrated than those of the typical species, giving it a more rounded appearance. The plant produces tiny, pale pink to white flowers in clusters, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic foliage is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
