Trifolium bithynicum

Trifolium bithynicum, commonly known as the Bithynian clover, is a perennial herb native to regions of Eastern Europe and Western Asia.

It typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, consisting of three oval to ovate leaflets, and small white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. It is often found in temperate climates and is adapted to thrive in moderate sunlight conditions.

Trifolium bithynicum is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility.
