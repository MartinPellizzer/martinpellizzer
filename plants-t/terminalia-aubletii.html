Terminalia aubletii

Terminalia aubletii, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and elliptical leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing flat, disc-shaped seeds. It is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.

The plant is also used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
