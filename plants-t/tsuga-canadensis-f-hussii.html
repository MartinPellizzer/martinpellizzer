Tsuga canadensis f. hussii

Tsuga canadensis f. hussii, commonly known as the Hussey's hemlock, is a rare form of the eastern hemlock.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, which features shorter, more densely packed needles compared to the typical hemlock. This form exhibits a unique growth habit, often forming a more compact and rounded crown. The botanical features include dark green, needle-like leaves and a conical shape, typical of the Tsuga genus.

It is primarily found in specific microhabitats, reflecting its adaptation to particular environmental conditions.
