Taraxacum pseudonaevosum

Taraxacum pseudonaevosum, commonly known as the spotted dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers and serrated, hairy leaves. The plant produces a distinctive seed head composed of numerous tiny seeds attached to parachute-like structures. It is native to parts of Europe and Asia and is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats.

The species is notable for its unique leaf patterns and its role in supporting pollinators.
