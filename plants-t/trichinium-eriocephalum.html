Trichinium eriocephalum

Trichinium eriocephalum, commonly known as the woolly trichinium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Trichinium eriocephalum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
