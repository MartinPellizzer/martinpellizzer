Thymus latissimus

Thymus latissimus, commonly known as broadleaf thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing, compact form and dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its aromatic properties. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The essential oils extracted from its leaves are widely used in culinary and medicinal applications.
