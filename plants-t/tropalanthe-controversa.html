Tropalanthe controversa

Tropalanthe controversa, commonly known as the controversial lily, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ornamental species in horticulture.
