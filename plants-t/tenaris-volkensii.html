Tenaris volkensii

Tenaris volkensii, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to East Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and showy, clustered flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant typically grows as a perennial herb, often forming a rosette of leaves close to the ground. Its flowers are borne on tall, slender stems and bloom during the warmer months.

Tenaris volkensii is widely cultivated as a decorative plant due to its ornamental value and ease of care.
