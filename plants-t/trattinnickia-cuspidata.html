Trattinnickia cuspidata

Trattinnickia cuspidata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is often found in coastal and mountainous regions, where it thrives in well-drained conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
