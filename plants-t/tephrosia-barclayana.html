Tephrosia barclayana

Tephrosia barclayana, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, featuring pinnately compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky, milky substance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savannas and woodland areas.

Tephrosia barclayana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some regions.
