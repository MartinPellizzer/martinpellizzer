Tordylium intermedium

Tordylium intermedium, commonly known as the Brazilian violet, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its violet-blue flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest understories. It has a short stem with dense foliage and produces small, fragrant blooms.

Tordylium intermedium is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
