Tortula chisosa

Tortula chisosa, commonly known as the Chisos mountain moss, is a small, mat-forming liverwort found in arid environments.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often seen in desert regions of the southwestern United States. The plant has a dense, cushion-like growth habit, which helps it retain moisture in dry conditions. Its leaves are closely arranged and covered in a thick layer of papillae, aiding in water retention.

Tortula chisosa plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates in its native habitat.
