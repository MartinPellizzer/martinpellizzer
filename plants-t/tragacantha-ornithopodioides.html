Tragacantha ornithopodioides

Tragacantha ornithopodioides, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of southwestern Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by distinctive, inflated seed pods. It is known for its ability to produce a valuable gum, which has been used traditionally in medicine and as a food additive.

Due to its ecological adaptations, it plays an important role in stabilizing soils in its native habitat.
