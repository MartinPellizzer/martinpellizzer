Tragopogon phaenopappus

Tragopogon phaenopappus, commonly known as the showy goat's beard, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy basal rosette. The plant produces striking yellow flowers that transition to white as they mature, followed by fluffy, parachute-like seeds. It thrives in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it a notable species in its native range.
