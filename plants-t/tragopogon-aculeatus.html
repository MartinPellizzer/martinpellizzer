Tragopogon aculeatus

Tragopogon aculeatus, commonly known as the prickly chicory, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow composite flowers and hairy, ribbed stems. The plant produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread across various habitats. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Tragopogon aculeatus is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
