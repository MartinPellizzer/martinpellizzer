Thymus albicans subsp. donyanae

Thymus albicans subsp.

donyanae, commonly known as Donyana thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and gray-green foliage, with small, aromatic leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces pink to violet flowers in clusters during the late spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to sunny, arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
