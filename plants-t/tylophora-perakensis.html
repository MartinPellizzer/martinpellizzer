Tylophora perakensis

Tylophora perakensis, commonly known as the Perak mountain vine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, elliptic leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by follicles containing elongated seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in forested areas with high humidity.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal potential, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
