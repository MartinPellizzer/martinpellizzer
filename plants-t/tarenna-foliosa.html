Tarenna foliosa

Tarenna foliosa, commonly known as the leafy tarenna, is a climbing shrub native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family, which includes coffee and gardenias. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers followed by elliptical, red berries.

Tarenna foliosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
