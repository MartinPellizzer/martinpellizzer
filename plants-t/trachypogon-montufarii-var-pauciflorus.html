Trachypogon montufarii var. pauciflorus

Trachypogon montufarii var. pauciflorus, commonly known as the few-flowered trachypogon, is a grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with few flowers, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and wetlands.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
