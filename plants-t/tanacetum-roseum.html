Tanacetum roseum

Tanacetum roseum, commonly known as the rose geranium, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, rose-scented leaves and small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and prefers well-drained soil and full sun. It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and pleasant fragrance.

Tanacetum roseum is also valued in herbal medicine for its purported calming and antiseptic properties.
