Thymus longicaulis var. subisophyllus

Thymus longicaulis var. subisophyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically gray-green in color and densely packed along its stems. The plant produces tiny, pinkish-white flowers in clusters, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Its medicinal properties have historically been valued for their antiseptic and aromatic qualities.
