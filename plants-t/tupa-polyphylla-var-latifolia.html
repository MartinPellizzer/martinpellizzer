Tupa polyphylla var. latifolia

Tupa polyphylla var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf tupa, is a shrub native to the Andean region of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and resilience, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens in temperate climates.
