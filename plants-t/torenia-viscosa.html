Torenia viscosa

Torenia viscosa, commonly known as the sticky torenia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of violet or blue. The plant has hairy, often sticky leaves, which contribute to its common name. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Torenia viscosa is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
