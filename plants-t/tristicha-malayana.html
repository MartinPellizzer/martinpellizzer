Tristicha malayana

Tristicha malayana, commonly known as the Malayan fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Tristicha malayana plays a significant role in the ecological balance of its habitat by contributing to soil stability and providing shelter for small organisms.
