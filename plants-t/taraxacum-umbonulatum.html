Taraxacum umbonulatum

Taraxacum umbonulatum, commonly known as the mountain dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous tiny florets. The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are usually hairy and lanceolate in shape. Its yellow flowers bloom in spring, followed by the characteristic seed heads that produce wind-dispersed seeds.

Taraxacum umbonulatum is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.
