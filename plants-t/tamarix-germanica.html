Tamarix germanica

Tamarix germanica, commonly known as the German tamarisk, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Europe.

It features feathery, gray-green foliage and produces small, pink to reddish flowers in summer. The plant has small, scale-like leaves that help reduce water loss, making it adapted to arid environments. Its roots can form extensive networks, contributing to soil stabilization.

Tamarix germanica is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and resilience to drought.
