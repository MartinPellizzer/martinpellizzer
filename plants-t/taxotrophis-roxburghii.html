Taxotrophis roxburghii

Taxotrophis roxburghii, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a deciduous shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves and small, yellow-orange berries that are rich in vitamin C. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure. Its botanical features include a woody stem, simple leaves, and flowers that are typically greenish-white in color.

The fruit is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its nutritional and antioxidant properties.
