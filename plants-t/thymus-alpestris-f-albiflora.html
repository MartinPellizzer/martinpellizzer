Thymus alpestris f. albiflora

Thymus alpestris f. albiflora, commonly known as the alpine thyme with white flowers, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, giving it a strong, minty scent.

This variety is often used in herbal medicine and as a decorative plant in alpine gardens.
