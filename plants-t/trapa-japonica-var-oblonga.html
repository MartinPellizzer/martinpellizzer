Trapa japonica var. oblonga

Trapa japonica var. oblonga, commonly known as the Japanese water chestnut, is a aquatic plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive floating leaves and spiny, nut-like fruits that develop underwater. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Its leaves are broadly ovate with a waxy surface, and the fruits are edible, often harvested for culinary use.

The species is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing water ecosystems and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
