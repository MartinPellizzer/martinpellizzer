Taraxacum hepaticolor

Taraxacum hepaticolor, commonly known as the liver-colored dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are typically dark green with a reddish or purplish hue. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in late spring to early summer.

The species is noted for its distinctive coloration and is often found in disturbed soils and grassy areas.
