Thalictrum elongatum

Thalictrum elongatum, commonly known as the slender meadow-rue, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features delicate, fern-like leaves and produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers in early spring. Its stems are slender and often branched, contributing to its graceful appearance.

Thalictrum elongatum is valued in gardens for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits to pollinators.
