Thalassia hemprichii

Thalassia hemprichii, commonly known as shoal grass, is a seagrass species that thrives in shallow coastal waters.

It forms dense underwater meadows that provide critical habitat for marine life. This plant has long, narrow leaves that are typically bright green and can grow up to several meters in length. It plays a vital role in stabilizing sediments and improving water quality.

Thalassia hemprichii is widely distributed in tropical and subtropical regions around the world.
