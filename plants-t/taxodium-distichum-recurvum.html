Taxodium distichum recurvum

Taxodium distichum recurvum, commonly known as the bald cypress, is a deciduous conifer native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive feather-like leaves that turn reddish-brown before falling in autumn. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its roots form a network of pneumatophores, specialized structures that allow the tree to absorb oxygen from waterlogged soil.

The bald cypress is often found in wetland habitats, where it plays an important role in ecosystem stability and biodiversity.
