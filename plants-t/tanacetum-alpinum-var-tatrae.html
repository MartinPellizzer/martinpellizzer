Tanacetum alpinum var. tatrae

Tanacetum alpinum var. tatrae, commonly known as the Tatra mountain tansy, is a perennial herb native to the Tatra Mountains in Central Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, silver-gray leaves and small yellow flower heads. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils. It is known for its aromatic properties and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.

Its hardy nature makes it well adapted to cold climates and high altitudes.
