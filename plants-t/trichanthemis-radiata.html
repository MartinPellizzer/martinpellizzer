Trichanthemis radiata

Trichanthemis radiata, commonly known as the radiate trichanthemum, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically features small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in a radiate pattern, with a central disk surrounded by ray florets. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, often growing in coastal or disturbed areas.

Trichanthemis radiata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
