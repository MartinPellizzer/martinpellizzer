Turbinicarpus schmiedickeanus subsp.

schmiedickeanus, commonly known as the Schmiedicke's turbinated cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and is typically covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are borne on short, erect stems. It is a slow-growing species that requires minimal water and is well-suited to rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated by succulent enthusiasts.
