Thyrsodium paraense

Thyrsodium paraense, commonly known as the Paraense thyrsoideum, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or white. It thrives in tropical and subtropical environments, often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
