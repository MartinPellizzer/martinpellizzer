Taraxacum multijugum

Taraxacum multijugum, commonly known as the multi-jugate dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of pale green, hairy leaves and a flowering stem that bears a single, spherical head of yellow flowers. The plant produces small, wind-dispersed seeds with feathery parachutes, typical of the genus Taraxacum. It is native to regions of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, and thrives in temperate climates.

The species is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, showcasing its adaptability to various environments.
