Taraxacum polyodon

Taraxacum polyodon, commonly known as the European dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves that are typically lobed or toothed, and a hollow, leafy stem that supports a flowering head. The flowers are yellow and produce a distinctive seed head with parachute-like seeds for wind dispersal. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in meadows, lawns, and disturbed areas.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its diuretic and digestive benefits.
