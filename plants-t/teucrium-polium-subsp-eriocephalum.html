Teucrium polium subsp. eriocephalum

Teucrium polium subsp.

eriocephalum, commonly known as the purple germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features erect, branched stems covered in fine hairs and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated margin.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. Its aromatic foliage contains essential oils, which contribute to its use in traditional herbal medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.
