Thunbergia platyphylla

Thunbergia platyphylla, commonly known as the African bluebell, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Thunbergia platyphylla is also valued for its ability to climb using its tendrils, making it suitable for trellises or vertical gardens.
