Trifolium israeliticum

Trifolium israeliticum, commonly known as the Israeli clover, is a species of the genus Trifolium native to the Middle East.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Trifolium israeliticum is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in traditional medicine.
