Turbinicarpus macrochele subsp. kupcakii

Turbinicarpus macrochele subsp.

kupcakii, commonly known as the Kupcak's Turbinicarpus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico. It is characterized by its short, ribbed stems that are typically green to blue-green in color.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is a slow-growing species that requires well-draining soil and minimal water.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated by succulent enthusiasts.
