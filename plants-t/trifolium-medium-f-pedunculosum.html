Trifolium medium f. pedunculosum

Trifolium medium f. pedunculosum, commonly known as the pedunculate clover, is a botanical variant of the common clover species.

It is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves, typically with three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and pastures.

This variety is valued for its role in soil nitrogen fixation and as forage for livestock.
