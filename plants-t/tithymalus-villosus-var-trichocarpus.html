Tithymalus villosus var. trichocarpus

Tithymalus villosus var. trichocarpus, commonly known as the hairy tithymalus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and also bear a fine pubescence, contributing to its overall textured foliage.

This variety is often found in meadows and grasslands, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
