Trichocereus litoralis

Trichocereus litoralis, commonly known as the coastal torch cactus, is a species native to coastal regions of South America.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its spiny, ribbed stems and ability to thrive in arid and saline environments. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, blooming at night. It has a relatively shallow root system adapted to stabilize itself in sandy soils.

Trichocereus litoralis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in challenging climates.
