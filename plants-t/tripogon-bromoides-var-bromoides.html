Tripogon bromoides var. bromoides

Tripogon bromoides var. bromoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf beardgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense colonies.

It plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
