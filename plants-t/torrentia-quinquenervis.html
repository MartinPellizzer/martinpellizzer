Torrentia quinquenervis

Torrentia quinquenervis, commonly known as the five-veined torrent plant, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, grass-like foliage and small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of white and pale pink. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found near streams and rocky outcrops. Its leaves are typically narrow and have five distinct veins, giving it its scientific name.

Torrentia quinquenervis plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and supports local pollinator populations.
