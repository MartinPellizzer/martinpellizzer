Tilia patzcuamana

Tilia patzcuamana, commonly known as the Mexican lime tree, is a deciduous tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Tiliaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and yellow flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits that are typically green when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local cultures.
