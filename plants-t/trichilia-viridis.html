Trichilia viridis

Trichilia viridis, commonly known as the green trichilia, is a tropical tree belonging to the family Meliaceae.

It is native to regions of Africa, particularly in countries like Nigeria and Ghana. The plant is characterized by its smooth, greenish-brown bark and lance-shaped leaves that are opposite in arrangement. Its flowers are small and yellow-green, often clustered in panicles, and it produces woody fruits containing several seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
