Tricuspis stricta

Tricuspis stricta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tricuspis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of white and pale purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils, preferring cool and moist conditions. Its structure is compact, with a rigid stem that supports its foliage and inflorescences.

Tricuspis stricta is often cultivated for its ornamental value in rock gardens and alpine settings.
