Tephrosia discolor

Tephrosia discolor, commonly known as the purple tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features palmately compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its flowers are small, clustered in racemes, and usually have a purple or pinkish hue. Tephrosia discolor produces pods that contain several seeds, and it is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important plant in traditional medicine and ecological studies.
