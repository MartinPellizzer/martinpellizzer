Tabebuia subcordata

Tabebuia subcordata, commonly known as the pink angel's trumpet, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in clusters. The plant has a smooth, grayish-brown bark and produces small, oval-shaped fruits that contain seeds. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and elliptical with a slightly waxy texture.

Tabebuia subcordata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
