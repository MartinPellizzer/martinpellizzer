Taguaria laurifolia

Taguaria laurifolia, commonly known as the laurel fern, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage. The plant features compound leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern, giving it a lush, bushy appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
