Tournefortiopsis major

Tournefortiopsis major, commonly known as the giant tournefortia, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has a rosette-like growth pattern with thick, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in its arid environment. It thrives in rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
