Taraxacum peracutum

Taraxacum peracutum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces a single, bright yellow flower head that develops into a ball of white, fluffy seeds. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grassy areas.

Taraxacum peracutum is known for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in herbal remedies.
