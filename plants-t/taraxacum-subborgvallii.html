Taraxacum subborgvallii

Taraxacum subborgvallii, commonly known as the subborgvallii dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and a hollow, leafy stem. The plant produces a single, bright yellow flower head that develops into a white, spherical seed sphere with pappus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the dandelion genus, with unique morphological traits that aid in its identification.
