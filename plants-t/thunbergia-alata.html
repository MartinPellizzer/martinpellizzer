Thunbergia alata

Thunbergia alata, commonly known as the blue trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple trumpet-shaped flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant is a vigorous climber, often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant blooms. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil.

Thunbergia alata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
