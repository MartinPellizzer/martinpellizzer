Tilingia nakaiana

Tilingia nakaiana, commonly known as the Nakaiana lichen, is a unique foliose lichen that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It is characterized by its delicate, leaf-like thallus that is typically greenish to yellowish in color. The lichen forms a symbiotic relationship between a fungus and a green alga, which allows it to efficiently carry out photosynthesis. It is often found on decaying wood or rock surfaces in temperate forest regions.

Due to its specialized habitat requirements, Tilingia nakaiana is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
