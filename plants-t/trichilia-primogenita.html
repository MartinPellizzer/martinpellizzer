Trichilia primogenita

Trichilia primogenita, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a tropical tree native to Central and Southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae, which includes mahogany and other valuable hardwoods. The plant is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The tree is valued for its durable wood, which is used in furniture and construction, though it is also threatened by overharvesting.
