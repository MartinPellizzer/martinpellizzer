Thymus aserbaidshanicus

Thymus aserbaidshanicus, commonly known as Armenian thyme, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Armenia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic, often fragrant leaves and flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, exhibiting a low, compact growth habit. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces tiny, aromatic seeds.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
