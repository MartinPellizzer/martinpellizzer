Tabernaemontana laurifolia

Tabernaemontana laurifolia, commonly known as the fragrant star of Bethlehem, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
