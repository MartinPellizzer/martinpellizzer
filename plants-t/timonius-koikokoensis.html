Timonius koikokoensis

Timonius koikokoensis, commonly known as the Koikoko flame tree, is a striking tropical plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its large, showy red flowers that resemble flames, which bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has a woody stem with a rough, fissured bark, and its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical in shape. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular ornamental species in warm climates.

The Koikoko flame tree is also valued for its ecological role in providing habitat and food for local wildlife.
