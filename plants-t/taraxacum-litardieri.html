Taraxacum litardieri

Taraxacum litardieri, commonly known as the Litardier's dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to southern Europe and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and basal rosette of leaves. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. It has a taproot system and produces wind-dispersed seeds, which contribute to its widespread distribution.

The species is often mistaken for other dandelion varieties due to its similar appearance.
