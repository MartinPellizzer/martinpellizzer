Toxicodendron vernicifluum

Toxicodendron vernicifluum, commonly known as the lac tree, is a deciduous tree native to southern Asia.

It is valued for its sap, which is used to produce traditional lac resin. The plant contains urushiol, a toxic oil that can cause severe skin irritation and allergic reactions in many people. Its leaves are alternate and simple, with a smooth margin and a dark green upper surface.

The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
