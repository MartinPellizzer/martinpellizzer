Tragus racemosus var. berteronianus

Tragus racemosus var. berteronianus, commonly known as the Berteronian thistle, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of Mediterranean flora.
