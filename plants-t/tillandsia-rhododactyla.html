Tillandsia rhododactyla

Tillandsia rhododactyla, commonly known as the red-tipped air plant, is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is native to Central and South America.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on other plants without deriving nutrients from them. The plant features rosette-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help in absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air. Its flowers are typically red or orange, with showy bracts that attract pollinators.

Tillandsia rhododactyla is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
