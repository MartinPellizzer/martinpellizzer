Taraxacum breitfeldii

Taraxacum breitfeldii, commonly known as the Breitfeld dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces a hollow, hairy stem that supports a single, yellow composite flower head. Its flowers develop into a distinctive spherical seed head with numerous parachute-like seeds.

Taraxacum breitfeldii is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in meadows and grasslands.
