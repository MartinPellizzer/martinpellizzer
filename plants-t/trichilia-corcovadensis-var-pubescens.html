Trichilia corcovadensis var. pubescens

Trichilia corcovadensis var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy trichilia, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by woody pods containing flat, winged seeds. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
