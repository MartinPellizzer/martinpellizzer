Trigynaea angustifolia

Trigynaea angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf trigynaea, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are borne on short pedicels. It is adapted to survive in dry conditions with a deep root system that allows it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Trigynaea angustifolia plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
