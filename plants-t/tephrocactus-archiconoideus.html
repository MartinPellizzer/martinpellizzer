Tephrocactus archiconoideus

Tephrocactus archiconoideus, commonly known as the giant hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, cylindrical stems that are covered in prominent, spiny areoles. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are typically clustered at the ends of the stems. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to thrive in harsh, dry environments.

Tephrocactus archiconoideus is a slow-growing plant that requires minimal water and well-draining soil to flourish.
