Trichophyllum lanatum

Trichophyllum lanatum, commonly known as the velvet moss, is a species of moss that belongs to the family Dicranaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, cushion-like growth form and soft, velvety texture due to its dense covering of short, hair-like structures. The leaves are usually ovate to lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rock crevices, where it forms dense mats.

This plant plays an important role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
