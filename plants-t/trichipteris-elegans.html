Trichipteris elegans

Trichipteris elegans, commonly known as the slender fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically green and glossy. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Trichipteris elegans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
