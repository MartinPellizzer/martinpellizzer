Tunica velutina var. levicaulis

Tunica velutina var. levicaulis, commonly known as the velvet tunica, is a rare and distinctive plant species characterized by its dense, velvety foliage.

The leaves are typically broad and lance-shaped, covered in fine, soft hairs that give the plant its characteristic texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate forest understories. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or pale yellow in color.

Its unique morphology and habitat preferences make it a subject of interest for botanists and plant enthusiasts alike.
