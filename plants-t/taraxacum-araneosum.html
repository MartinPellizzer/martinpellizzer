Taraxacum araneosum

Taraxacum araneosum, commonly known as the spiderwort dandelion, is a flowering plant in the dandelion family, Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flower heads that resemble miniature sunflowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has basal leaves that are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grassy areas.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
