Tabebuia heterophylla subsp. genuina

Tabebuia heterophylla subsp.

genuina, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its striking white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season.

The plant has opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that are often hairy on the underside. Its bark is smooth and light brown, and it produces small, round fruits that split open to release seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in gardens and urban landscapes.
