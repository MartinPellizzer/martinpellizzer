Trixis chiantlensis

Trixis chiantlensis, commonly known as the Chiantla trixis, is a flowering plant native to the high Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at elevations between 3,000 and 5,000 meters. Its leaves are narrow and serrated, while its flowers are yellow to orange in color, often appearing in clusters.

Due to its restricted habitat, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
