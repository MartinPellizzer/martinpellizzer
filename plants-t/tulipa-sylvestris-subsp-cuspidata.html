Tulipa sylvestris subsp. cuspidata

Tulipa sylvestris subsp.

cuspidata, commonly known as the Persian tulip, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, and white.

The petals are often recurved, giving the flowers a distinctive, elegant appearance. This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to many temperate gardens.
