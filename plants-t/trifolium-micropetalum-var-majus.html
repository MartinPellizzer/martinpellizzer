Trifolium micropetalum var. majus

Trifolium micropetalum var. majus, commonly known as the greater red clover, is a flowering plant in the legume family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets with a hairy surface. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers arranged in dense, globular clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
