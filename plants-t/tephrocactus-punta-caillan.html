Tephrocactus punta-caillan

Tephrocactus punta-caillan, commonly known as the Punta Caillan cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of northern Chile.

It is a small, spineless cactus with a globular shape and a distinctive greenish-gray color. The plant features areolae, which are small, raised bumps containing spines and flowers. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, blooming in late summer.

This cactus is well adapted to extreme desert conditions, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.
