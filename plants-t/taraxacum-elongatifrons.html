Taraxacum elongatifrons

Taraxacum elongatifrons, commonly known as the elongated dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and bright yellow composite flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces a distinctive, elongated seed head that releases lightweight seeds with parachute-like structures for wind dispersal. It is native to parts of Asia and is often found in meadows and grassy areas.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the genus Taraxacum.
