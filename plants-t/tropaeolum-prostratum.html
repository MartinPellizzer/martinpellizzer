Tropaeolum prostratum

Tropaeolum prostratum, commonly known as the prostrate nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, edible flowers that come in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant produces small, rounded leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its seeds are enclosed in capsule-like structures that split open when mature to release the seeds.

Tropaeolum prostratum is often cultivated as a ornamental plant due to its bright blooms and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
