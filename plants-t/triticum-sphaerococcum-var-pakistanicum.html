Triticum sphaerococcum var. pakistanicum

Triticum sphaerococcum var. pakistanicum, commonly known as the Pakistan wheatgrass, is a rare and unique variant of wheatgrass native to the high-altitude regions of Pakistan.

It is characterized by its compact, spherical growth habit, with dense clusters of small, grass-like leaves. The plant exhibits a high degree of morphological variation, adapting to the harsh environmental conditions of its habitat. Its botanical features include narrow, grass-like blades and a short, stout stem.

This variety is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its unique morphology and potential for ecological or agricultural study.
