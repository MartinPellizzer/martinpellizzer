Trichera × speciosa

Trichera × speciosa, commonly known as the hybrid trichera, is a plant species that exhibits characteristics of its parent species, Trichera pumila and Trichera grandiflora.

It is a flowering plant native to tropical regions, typically found in humid, shaded environments. The plant features elongated, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly waxy texture, and it produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers. Trichera × speciosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a hybrid species, showcasing genetic diversity within the Trichera genus.
