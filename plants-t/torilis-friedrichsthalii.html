Torilis friedrichsthalii

Torilis friedrichsthalii, commonly known as the African sandbur, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is well adapted to arid environments. Its hairy leaves and spiny seed heads are features that aid in wind dispersal of its seeds.

It is often found in grasslands and along roadsides, showcasing its resilience to harsh conditions.
