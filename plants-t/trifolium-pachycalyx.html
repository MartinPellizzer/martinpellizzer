Trifolium pachycalyx

Trifolium pachycalyx, commonly known as the thickpod clover, is a perennial herb native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in early spring. Its stems are often hairy and can grow up to several feet in height.

Trifolium pachycalyx is adapted to a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils.
