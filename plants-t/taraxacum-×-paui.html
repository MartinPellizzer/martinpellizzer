Taraxacum × paui

Taraxacum × paui, commonly known as the hybrid dandelion, is a naturally occurring hybrid between Taraxacum officinale (common dandelion) and Taraxacum laevigatum (Lesser dandelion).

It is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and a distinctive, hollow, leafless stem that supports the flower head. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in lawns, fields, and along roadsides. Its leaves are usually alternate, basal, and have a rough, hairy texture.

The hybrid is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
