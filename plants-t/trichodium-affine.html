Trichodium affine

Trichodium affine, commonly known as the slender sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial with small, grayish-green leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Trichodium affine is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitat.
