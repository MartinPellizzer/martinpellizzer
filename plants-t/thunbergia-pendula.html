Thunbergia pendula

Thunbergia pendula, commonly known as the weeping bluebell, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of blue or violet tubular flowers. The plant produces small, heart-shaped leaves that are opposite along the stems. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as a decorative plant.

Thunbergia pendula is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
