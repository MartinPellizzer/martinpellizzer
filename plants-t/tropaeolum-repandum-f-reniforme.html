Tropaeolum repandum f. reniforme

Tropaeolum repandum f. reniforme, commonly known as the broadleaf nasturtium, is a variant of the common nasturtium characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves.

This plant is a trailing annual that produces vibrant, edible flowers in shades of yellow and orange. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil. The leaves are glossy and slightly succulent, while the stems are hairy and can spread along the ground.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and as a companion plant for its pest-repelling properties.
