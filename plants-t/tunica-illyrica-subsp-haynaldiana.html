Tunica illyrica subsp. haynaldiana

Tunica illyrica subsp.

haynaldiana, commonly known as the Balkan bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula. It is characterized by its distinctive blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous small seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
