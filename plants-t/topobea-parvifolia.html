Topobea parvifolia

Topobea parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved topobea, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, delicate leaves and clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests or rocky crevices. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, textured appearance.

Topobea parvifolia is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
