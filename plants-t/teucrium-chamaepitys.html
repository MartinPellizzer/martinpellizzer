Teucrium chamaepitys

Teucrium chamaepitys, commonly known as creeping germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in dense clusters. The plant has aromatic, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used historically in traditional herbal remedies.
