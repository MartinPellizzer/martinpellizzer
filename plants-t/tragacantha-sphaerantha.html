Tragacantha sphaerantha

Tragacantha sphaerantha, commonly known as the globe thornapple, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive spherical fruit, which contains toxic seeds enclosed in a hardened, spiky capsule. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Tragacantha sphaerantha is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
