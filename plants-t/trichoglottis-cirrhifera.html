Trichoglottis cirrhifera

Trichoglottis cirrhifera, commonly known as the hairy lip orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, hairy leaf sheaths and striking, showy flowers with a distinctive lip. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air.

Trichoglottis cirrhifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
