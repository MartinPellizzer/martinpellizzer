Thymus comptus var. aivalii

Thymus comptus var. aivalii, commonly known as the Aivali thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and aromatic, gray-green foliage. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is often cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
