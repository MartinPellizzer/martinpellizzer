Trifolium nigrescens var. intermedium

Trifolium nigrescens var. intermedium, commonly known as the intermediate black clover, is a variant of the black clover species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which typically have a dark green to purplish hue. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are usually pink or violet in color, blooming in early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinator populations.
