Thymus silvicola

Thymus silvicola, commonly known as the forest thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically thrives in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes. The plant features small, aromatic leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are usually pink or purple, arranged in dense clusters. Thymus silvicola is valued for its medicinal properties, including antimicrobial and antifungal effects.

It is also used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging soil conditions.
