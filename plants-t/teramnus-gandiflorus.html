Teramnus gandiflorus

Teramnus gandiflorus, commonly known as the Malabar tamarind, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are alternate and pinnate, with small, ovate leaflets. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by elongated, woody pods containing seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
