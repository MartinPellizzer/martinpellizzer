Tribulus terrestris var. lanuginosus

Tribulus terrestris var. lanuginosus, commonly known as hairy puncture vine, is a low-growing annual plant native to temperate regions of Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It features prostrate or ascending stems covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by distinctive, spiny capsules that contain several seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in gardens, roadsides, and agricultural fields.

Its botanical characteristics make it adaptable to various environments, contributing to its widespread distribution.
