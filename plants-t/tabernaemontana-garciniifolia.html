Tabernaemontana garciniifolia

Tabernaemontana garciniifolia, commonly known as the white-flowered star jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil and partial shade.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardens and as a decorative plant in warm regions.
