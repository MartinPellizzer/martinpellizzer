Teucrium orientale subsp. taylorii

Teucrium orientale subsp.

taylorii, commonly known as Taylor's germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

The plant features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves with a gray-green hue, and its flowers are small, tubular, and usually pink or purple in color. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
