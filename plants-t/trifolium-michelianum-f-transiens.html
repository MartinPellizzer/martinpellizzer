Trifolium michelianum f. transiens

Trifolium michelianum f. transiens, commonly known as the transient clover, is a botanical variant of the common clover species.

It is characterized by its unique leaf morphology, featuring trifoliate leaves with a distinct coloration pattern. This plant typically grows in disturbed soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its flowering period is short-lived, contributing to its common name.

The species is of interest in botanical studies due to its genetic variability and ecological adaptability.
