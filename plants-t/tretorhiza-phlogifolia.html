Tretorhiza phlogifolia

Tretorhiza phlogifolia, commonly known as the flameleaf tretorhiza, is a rare and distinctive plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, flame-colored foliage that turns vibrant red or orange during the flowering season. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in mountainous or forested areas. Its leaves are broad and leathery, providing a unique texture and appearance.

Tretorhiza phlogifolia is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its visual appeal.
