Trichomanes superbum

Trichomanes superbum, commonly known as the walking fern, is a unique fern species characterized by its creeping rhizomes that produce upright, arching fronds.

It has a distinctive appearance with its dark green, leathery leaves that often curl and uncurl in response to moisture. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its ability to "walk" is due to its rhizomes extending and anchoring in new locations, allowing it to spread vegetatively.

Trichomanes superbum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological systems as a pioneer species in disturbed habitats.
