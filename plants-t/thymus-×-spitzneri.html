Thymus × spitzneri

Thymus × spitzneri, commonly known as Spitzner's thyme, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and aromatic, gray-green foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is a popular choice among gardeners seeking low-maintenance, drought-tolerant plants.
