Teucrium goetzei

Teucrium goetzei, commonly known as Goetze's germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, tubular, and usually violet or blue in color, arranged in compact clusters.

Teucrium goetzei is part of the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses.
