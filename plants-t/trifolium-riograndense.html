Trifolium riograndense

Trifolium riograndense, commonly known as Rio Grande clover, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open fields and along roadsides.

Trifolium riograndense is valued for its role in soil nitrogen fixation and as a forage crop for livestock.
