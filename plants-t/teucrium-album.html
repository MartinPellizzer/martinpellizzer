Teucrium album

Teucrium album, commonly known as white germander, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, with a grayish-green hue. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

This plant is also valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
