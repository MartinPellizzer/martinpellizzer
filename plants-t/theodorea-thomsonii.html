Theodorea thomsonii

Theodorea thomsonii, commonly known as Thomson's lily, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly in the Andes region.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and is often found in high-altitude environments. It is a member of the Liliaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tulips and lilies.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections worldwide.
