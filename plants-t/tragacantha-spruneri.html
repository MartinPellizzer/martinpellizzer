Tragacantha spruneri

Tragacantha spruneri, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of southwestern Asia.

It produces a valuable resin, known as tragacanth, which has been historically used in medicine, food, and as a thickening agent. The plant is characterized by its grayish bark, small leaves, and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to drought conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.

Tragacantha spruneri plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
