Thymus hirtus var. rifanus

Thymus hirtus var. rifanus, commonly known as Rifan thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, gray-green foliage and small, aromatic leaves that are often used in cooking. The plant produces clusters of pink to lavender flowers, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its medicinal properties include antimicrobial and antioxidant effects, contributing to its historical use in herbal remedies.
