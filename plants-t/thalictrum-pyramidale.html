Thalictrum pyramidale

Thalictrum pyramidale, commonly known as the meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves that are compound and arranged in a whorled pattern around the stem. The plant produces showy, greenish-yellow flowers in early summer, which are clustered in upright, branched inflorescences. Thalictrum pyramidale thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its graceful appearance and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
