Taraxacum revolutum

Taraxacum revolutum, commonly known as the curlyleaf dandelion, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of curly, often reddish-tinged leaves. The plant produces a single, hollow, yellow flower head that blooms in early spring. Its stem is usually unbranched and can grow up to 30 centimeters in height.

The species is adapted to dry, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.
