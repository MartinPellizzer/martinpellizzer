Tragia lippiifolia

Tragia lippiifolia, commonly known as the hairy tragia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, characterized by its small, spiny stems and leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in dry environments.

Tragia lippiifolia is also known for its medicinal properties, used traditionally in some regions for its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic qualities.
