Tragacantha augana

Tragacantha augana, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a species of flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its deep root system that enables it to thrive in dry, sandy soils. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by pods that contain a sticky resin, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive.

Its botanical features make it an important component of desert ecosystems, contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
