Thalictrum laggeri

Thalictrum laggeri, commonly known as the Lagger's meadow rue, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and flies.

Thalictrum laggeri is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ecological benefits.
