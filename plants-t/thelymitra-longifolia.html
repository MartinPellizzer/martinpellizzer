Thelymitra longifolia

Thelymitra longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved sun-orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and vibrant orange or yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in open forests or grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowering stems are usually unbranched and bear several flowers arranged in a spiral.

Thelymitra longifolia is often found in coastal regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
