Tina suarezensis

Tina suarezensis, commonly known as the Suarez's tina, is a rare flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue petals and dense, dark green foliage. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions. Its flowers bloom in late spring and early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.

Due to habitat loss, Tina suarezensis is considered endangered and is the subject of ongoing conservation efforts.
