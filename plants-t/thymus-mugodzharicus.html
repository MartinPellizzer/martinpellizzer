Thymus mugodzharicus

Thymus mugodzharicus, commonly known as the Mugodzhar thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its leaves are narrow and aromatic, often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

Thymus mugodzharicus is valued for its medicinal properties, including antimicrobial and antiseptic qualities.
