Telis incisa

Telis incisa, commonly known as the cutleaf telis, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, deeply lobed leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Telis incisa typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in suitable climates.
