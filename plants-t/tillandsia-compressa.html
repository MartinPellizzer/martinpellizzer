Tillandsia compressa

Tillandsia compressa, commonly known as the compressed air plant, is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on other plants without being parasitic, typically attaching itself to trees or rocks. The plant's leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a dense covering of trichomes that help absorb moisture and nutrients from the air. It produces small, clustered inflorescences with white or pale pink flowers that bloom in the spring.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
