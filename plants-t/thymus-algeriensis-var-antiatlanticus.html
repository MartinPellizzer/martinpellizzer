Thymus algeriensis var. antiatlanticus

Thymus algeriensis var. antiatlanticus, commonly known as Algerian thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, aromatic leaves and produces pink or purple flowers, typically blooming in early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes. Its foliage is often used in culinary and medicinal applications due to its essential oils.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and ability to withstand drought conditions.
