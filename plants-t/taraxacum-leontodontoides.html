Taraxacum leontodontoides

Taraxacum leontodontoides, commonly known as the alpine dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and a flowering head that produces a distinctive crown of bracts. The plant is adapted to cold climates and grows in rocky or alpine environments. Its flowers are typically yellow, and it produces a capsule that contains numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Taraxacum leontodontoides is often found in open, sunny habitats at high elevations.
