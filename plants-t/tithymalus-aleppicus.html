Tithymalus aleppicus

Tithymalus aleppicus, commonly known as the Aleppo thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, small, gray-green leaves and clusters of pink to purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or dry garden environments. Its essential oils, derived from the leaves, are used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine.

Tithymalus aleppicus is also valued as a culinary herb, often used to flavor dishes and beverages.
