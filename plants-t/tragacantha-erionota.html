Tragacantha erionota

Tragacantha erionota, commonly known as the hairy tragacanth, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branching stems that are covered in fine, hairy pubescence, giving it its common name. The plant produces yellow flowers in clustered inflorescences, which bloom in the spring. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and also bear a similar hairy texture.

Tragacantha erionota is known for its sticky resinous sap, which has been historically used in various traditional applications.
