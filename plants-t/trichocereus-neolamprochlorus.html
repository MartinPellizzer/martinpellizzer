Trichocereus neolamprochlorus

Trichocereus neolamprochlorus, commonly known as the "Lamb's Ear Cactus," is a species native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces striking, large white or pale pink flowers during the night, which are typically pollinated by bats. Its areoles are densely covered with white to grayish hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Trichocereus neolamprochlorus is well adapted to arid and high-altitude environments, thriving in full sun and well-draining soil.
