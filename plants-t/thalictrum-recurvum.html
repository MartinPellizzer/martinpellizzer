Thalictrum recurvum

Thalictrum recurvum, commonly known as meadow rue, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and showy, usually yellow or greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are typically arranged in dense, upright clusters and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Thalictrum recurvum is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
