Tephrosia praecana

Tephrosia praecana, commonly known as the golden tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, characterized by its slender, often branching stems. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Tephrosia praecana is also valued for its potential medicinal properties and use in traditional herbal remedies.
