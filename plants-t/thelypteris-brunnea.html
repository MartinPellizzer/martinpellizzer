Thelypteris brunnea

Thelypteris brunnea, commonly known as the brown maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Thelypteridaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically dark green with a reddish or brownish hue on the underside. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including countries such as India, China, and Japan. Thelypteris brunnea prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Its reproductive structures, including sori and indusia, are typical of ferns and contribute to its ecological role in woodland ecosystems.
