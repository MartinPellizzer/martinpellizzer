Thymus sipyleus var. punctatus

Thymus sipyleus var. punctatus, commonly known as the spotted thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Turkey.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically gray-green with distinctive white or pale spots. The plant produces tiny pink or violet flowers in the spring, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and fragrant foliage.
