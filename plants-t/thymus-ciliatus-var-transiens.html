Thymus ciliatus var. transiens

Thymus ciliatus var. transiens, commonly known as the Transylvanian thyme, is a perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and aromatic, gray-green leaves covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pinkish to purple flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its essential oils contain thymol and carvacrol, contributing to its medicinal and culinary uses.
