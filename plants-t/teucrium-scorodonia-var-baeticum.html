Teucrium scorodonia var. baeticum

Teucrium scorodonia var. baeticum, commonly known as the Baetican germander, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly hairy leaves and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, thriving in sunny, well-drained environments. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs such as mint and sage.

Its medicinal properties have been historically valued for their antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects.
