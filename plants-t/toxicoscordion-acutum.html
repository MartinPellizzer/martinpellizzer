Toxicoscordion acutum

Toxicoscordion acutum, commonly known as the western apomictic larkspur, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist meadows and along stream banks, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant features showy blue to purple flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive serrated edge, while the stems are often hairy.

Toxicoscordion acutum contains toxic alkaloids, making it harmful to both livestock and humans if ingested.
