Trichomanes nanophyllum

Trichomanes nanophyllum, commonly known as the small-leaf huperzia, is a small, evergreen fern belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its finely divided, needle-like leaves that form dense, compact clusters. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are short and branched, supporting its growth habit.

Trichomanes nanophyllum is known for its slow growth and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
