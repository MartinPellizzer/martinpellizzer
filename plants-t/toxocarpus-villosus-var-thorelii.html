Toxocarpus villosus var. thorelii

Toxocarpus villosus var. thorelii, commonly known as the Thorelii's hairy bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The flowers are typically blue or violet, forming in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
