Tibouchina longisepala var. spathulata

Tibouchina longisepala var. spathulata, commonly known as the broad-leafed tibouchina, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its large, ovate to elliptic leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The flowers are showy, typically pink or magenta, and are borne in clusters at the ends of branches. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in humid, shaded environments.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in landscaping in warm climates.
