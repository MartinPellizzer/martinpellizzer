Tournefortia hirsutissima

Tournefortia hirsutissima, commonly known as the hairy tournefortia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to yellow. The plant produces woody capsules as fruit, containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or disturbed habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal uses, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
