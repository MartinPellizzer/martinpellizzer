Trifolium circumdatum

Trifolium circumdatum, commonly known as the circumdatum clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or white in color. It is native to certain regions of Europe and is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides.

Trifolium circumdatum is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
