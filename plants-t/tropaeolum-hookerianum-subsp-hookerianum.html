Tropaeolum hookerianum subsp. hookerianum

Tropaeolum hookerianum subsp.

hookerianum, commonly known as the Hooker's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America. It is characterized by its vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from red to orange and yellow.

The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it a desirable addition to both wild and cultivated landscapes.
