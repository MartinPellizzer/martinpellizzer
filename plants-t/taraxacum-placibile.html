Taraxacum placibile

Taraxacum placibile, commonly known as the pleasant dandelion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated, hairy leaves and a hollow, leafless stem that supports a single, bright yellow flower head. The plant produces numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds enclosed in a fluffy pappus, aiding in its spread. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows, lawns, and disturbed soils.

Taraxacum placibile is known for its medicinal properties, including potential anti-inflammatory and diuretic effects.
