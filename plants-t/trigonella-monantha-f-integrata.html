Trigonella monantha f. integrata

Trigonella monantha f. integrata, commonly known as the Indian clover, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the spring and summer seasons. It is native to regions of southern Asia, including India and Nepal, and thrives in well-drained soils.

This plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
