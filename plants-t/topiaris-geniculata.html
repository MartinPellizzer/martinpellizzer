Topiaris geniculata

Topiaris geniculata, commonly known as the spiral vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its unique spiral growth pattern, where the stems twist around themselves in a helical manner. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of white and pink. Its leaves are opposite and oval-shaped, with a glossy texture.

Topiaris geniculata is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its distinctive appearance and ability to climb or trail.
