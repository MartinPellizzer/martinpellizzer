Tovomitopsis centistaminibus

Tovomitopsis centistaminibus, commonly known as the many-stamen orchid, is a rare and exotic species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

This plant is characterized by its intricate floral structure, featuring numerous stamens that contribute to its ornamental appeal. The botanical features include elongated, narrow leaves and a compact, showy inflorescence. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family, showcasing the typical sympodial growth pattern of orchids.

Due to its limited distribution and specialized habitat requirements, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical research and conservation efforts.
