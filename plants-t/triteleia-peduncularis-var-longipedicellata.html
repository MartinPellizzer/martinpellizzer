Triteleia peduncularis var. longipedicellata

Triteleia peduncularis var. longipedicellata, commonly known as the long pedicellate triteleia, is a perennial bulbous plant native to California.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, violet or lavender flowers. The plant is characterized by its long, slender pedicels that support the blooms, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in suitable climates.
