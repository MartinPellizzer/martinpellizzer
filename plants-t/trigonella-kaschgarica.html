Trigonella kaschgarica

Trigonella kaschgarica, commonly known as the Kashmir chickpea, is a leguminous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces pods containing small, round seeds that are used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in temperate climates and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils.

Due to its nutritional value, it is also considered an important crop for sustainable agriculture.
