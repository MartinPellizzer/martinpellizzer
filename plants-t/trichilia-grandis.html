Trichilia grandis

Trichilia grandis, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a tropical tree native to Central and West Africa.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae, which includes mahogany and other valuable hardwoods. The plant is characterized by its smooth, light-gray bark and elliptical leaves with a velvety texture. Its flowers are small and yellow-green, clustered in spikes, while the fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds.

The tree is valued for its durable, aromatic wood, which is used in furniture and carving.
