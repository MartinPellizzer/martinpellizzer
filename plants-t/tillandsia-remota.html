Tillandsia remota

Tillandsia remota, commonly known as the remote air plant, is a species of bromeliad native to the cloud forests of Mexico.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on other plants without deriving nutrients from them. The plant possesses specialized structures called trichomes on its leaves, which absorb moisture and nutrients from the air. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires regular misting to maintain its health.

Tillandsia remota is appreciated for its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
