Terminalia soembawana

Terminalia soembawana, commonly known as the Java Terminalia, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers followed by woody, spherical fruits containing several seeds. It is often found in lowland forests and is valued for its timber and medicinal properties.

The species is also used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.
