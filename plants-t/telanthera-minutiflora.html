Telanthera minutiflora

Telanthera minutiflora, commonly known as the small-flowered telanthera, is a herbaceous perennial native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features delicate, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, with five petals arranged in a showy cluster. The plant has slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Telanthera minutiflora is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
