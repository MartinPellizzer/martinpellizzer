Tillandsia virginalis

Tillandsia virginalis, commonly known as the silver pine tillandsia, is a species of air plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help in absorbing moisture from the air. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and does not require soil for growth.

It produces small, white or pale green flowers that bloom from the center of the rosette, adding an elegant touch to its appearance.
