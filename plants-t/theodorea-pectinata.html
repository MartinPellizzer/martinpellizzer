Theodorea pectinata

Theodorea pectinata, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by round, orange-red berries containing seeds. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Theodorea pectinata is also known for its medicinal properties, with various parts of the plant used in traditional herbal remedies.
