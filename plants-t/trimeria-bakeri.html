Trimeria bakeri

Trimeria bakeri, commonly known as the yellow trimeria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or perennial herb, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers consist of two lips, with the upper lip being smaller and the lower lip larger, a common feature in the Acanthaceae family.

Trimeria bakeri is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant color and compact growth habit.
