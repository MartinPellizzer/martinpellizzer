Trisetum spicatum subsp. bolivianum

Trisetum spicatum subsp.

bolivianum, commonly known as the Bolivian hairgrass, is a perennial grass native to South America. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces spikelets that are typically arranged in dense clusters along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and wetlands.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and habitat restoration.
