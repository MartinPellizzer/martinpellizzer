Tritonia maculata

Tritonia maculata, commonly known as the spotted bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, spotted flowers that bloom in shades of blue and purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Tritonia maculata is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
