Tricerma phyllanthoides

Tricerma phyllanthoides, commonly known as the African snowball, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring dense clusters of small, white, fluffy flowers that resemble snowballs. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate or elliptical in shape. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature contribute to its appeal in both horticultural and ecological contexts.
