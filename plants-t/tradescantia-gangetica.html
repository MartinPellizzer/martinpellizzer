Tradescantia gangetica

Tradescantia gangetica, commonly known as the Indian Spider Plant, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30-60 cm, featuring upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in spikes, which are followed by distinctive blue to purple berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Tradescantia gangetica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
