Thymus hirsutus var. persicus

Thymus hirsutus var. persicus, commonly known as Persian thyme, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes. Its aromatic leaves are often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

The variety is noted for its compact growth habit and hardiness in temperate climates.
