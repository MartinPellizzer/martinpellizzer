Tragacantha parryi

Tragacantha parryi, commonly known as Parry's tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its scientific name reflects its historical significance in traditional medicine and its use as a source of natural gum.

Tragacantha parryi is adapted to withstand drought and extreme temperatures, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
