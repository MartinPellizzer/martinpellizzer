Thymus lusitanicus var. puberulus

Thymus lusitanicus var. puberulus, commonly known as the hairy rock thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features small, aromatic leaves covered in fine hairs, which give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in sunny, well-drained habitats.

Its aromatic properties make it a valued plant in both horticulture and traditional herbal medicine.
