Tankervillia

Tankervillia, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to Africa.

These plants are characterized by their rosette-shaped leaves and vibrant, showy flowers that bloom in a variety of colors. Tankervillia species are typically herbaceous, with some varieties growing as shrubs. They prefer well-draining soil and moderate light conditions, making them popular houseplants.

The genus includes several species, such as Tankervillia splendens, which is widely cultivated for its ornamental value.
