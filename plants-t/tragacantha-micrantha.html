Tragacantha micrantha

Tragacantha micrantha, commonly known as the small-flowered tragacanth, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces a resinous sap, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive. Its leaves are typically narrow and gray-green, with a rough texture.

Tragacantha micrantha thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.
