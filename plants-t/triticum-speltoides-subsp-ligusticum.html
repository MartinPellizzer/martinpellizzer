Triticum speltoides subsp. ligusticum

Triticum speltoides subsp.

ligusticum, commonly known as Ligusticum tritici, is a wild grass species native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brown florets. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in dry, rocky habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
