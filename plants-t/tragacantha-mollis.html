Tragacantha mollis

Tragacantha mollis, commonly known as the soft tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces a distinctive gum, known as tragacanth, which is exuded from its stems and has been historically used in various industries. Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and commercial purposes.
