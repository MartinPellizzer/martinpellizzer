Tanacetum doabense

Tanacetum doabense, commonly known as Doab goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the regions of Pakistan and India.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks. It produces yellow to orange flower heads composed of numerous small florets, which are surrounded by bracts.

Tanacetum doabense is noted for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
