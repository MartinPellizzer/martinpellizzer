Trichinium obovatum var. atriplicifolium

Trichinium obovatum var. atriplicifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leafed trichinium, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its obovate to elliptical leaves that are often dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in dense, terminal clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Apiaceae family, known for its aromatic properties.
