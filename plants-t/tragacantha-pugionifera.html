Tragacantha pugionifera

Tragacantha pugionifera, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tragacanth, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters. The plant is known for its latex-rich sap, which has been historically used in various traditional applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and pharmacological studies.
