Thalictrum grandiflorum

Thalictrum grandiflorum, commonly known as the showy lady's-t thumb, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in early to mid-summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet and thrives in moist, shady environments. It is often found in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Thalictrum grandiflorum is also an important plant for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
