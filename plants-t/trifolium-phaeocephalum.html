Trifolium phaeocephalum

Trifolium phaeocephalum, commonly known as the dark-headed clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets, and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. This perennial plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks. It is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in ecological restoration projects.

The plant's flowers attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
