Taraxacum grootii

Taraxacum grootii, commonly known as the Groot's dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant features a rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and covered in a white waxy coating. Its yellow flower heads consist of numerous tiny florets that mature into fluffy white seeds with parachute-like structures.

Taraxacum grootii is adapted to grow in rocky and arid environments, showcasing resilience to harsh climatic conditions.
