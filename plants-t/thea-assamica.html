Thea assamica

Thea assamica, commonly known as Assam tea, is a species of tea plant native to the eastern Himalayas and Assam region of India.

It is characterized by its large, dark green leaves and robust growth habit, making it well-suited for cultivation in humid, tropical climates. The plant produces leaves that are rich in polyphenols and caffeine, contributing to its strong flavor profile. It is primarily cultivated for its leaves, which are processed into various types of black, green, and white tea.

Thea assamica is also valued for its adaptability to different soil types and its ability to thrive in high-altitude regions.
