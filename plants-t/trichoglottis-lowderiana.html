Trichoglottis lowderiana

Trichoglottis lowderiana, commonly known as the Lowder's trichoglottis, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic orchid, meaning it grows on the surface of trees or other plants without deriving nutrients from them. The plant produces striking, showy flowers that are typically pale yellow to greenish in color, with a distinctive three-lobed lip. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it prefers warm, humid environments with good air circulation.

Trichoglottis lowderiana is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for orchid enthusiasts.
