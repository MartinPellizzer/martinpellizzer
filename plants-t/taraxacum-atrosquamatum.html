Taraxacum atrosquamatum

Taraxacum atrosquamatum, commonly known as the blackened dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its hairy, dark green leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in early spring. Native to eastern Asia, particularly Japan and Korea, this plant thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant's latex is milky and can cause skin irritation, a common trait among the genus Taraxacum.

It is often found in forest understories and is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
