Trollius deylii

Trollius deylii, commonly known as the Deylii globeflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, golden-yellow, globe-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils. Trollius deylii is often found in alpine meadows and is adapted to cold climates.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
