Triticum capense

Triticum capense, commonly known as the Cape wheat, is a wild grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it flowers during the warmer months.

Triticum capense is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas.
