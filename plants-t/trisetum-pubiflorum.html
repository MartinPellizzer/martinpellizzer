Trisetum pubiflorum

Trisetum pubiflorum, commonly known as the hairy-flowered wood sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered flower heads. It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes and stream banks.

Trisetum pubiflorum is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of light conditions.
