Trichosalpinx reticulata

Trichosalpinx reticulata, commonly known as the net-veined orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its intricate, net-like vein patterns on its leaves. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, often arranged in a spike-like inflorescence. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests or along riverbanks.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists alike.
