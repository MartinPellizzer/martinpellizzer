Thilachium densiflorum

Thilachium densiflorum, commonly known as the Christmas flower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its vibrant, showy bracts that resemble petals. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with dense clusters of small, tubular flowers. Its blooming period occurs during the winter months, making it a popular choice for winter floral arrangements.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a cut flower.
