Trichocrepis bifida

Trichocrepis bifida, commonly known as the split-leaf fern, is a unique plant characterized by its distinctive foliage.

Its leaves are divided into two parts, giving it the appearance of being split, hence the species name *bifida*. The plant exhibits a creeping growth habit, with rhizomes that allow it to spread across the ground. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in woodland areas.

The fronds are often covered in fine, hair-like structures, contributing to its botanical intrigue.
