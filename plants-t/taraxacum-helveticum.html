Taraxacum helveticum

Taraxacum helveticum, commonly known as the Swiss dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant features a rosette of spoon-shaped, hairy leaves and a hollow, leafy stem that supports its flowering structure. Its flowers develop into the familiar seed-filled spheres, which are dispersed by wind.

Taraxacum helveticum is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.
