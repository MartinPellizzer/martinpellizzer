Triodia longiceps

Triodia longiceps, commonly known as the long-headed spinifex, is a perennial grass species native to the arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically brown or pale green in color. The plant has adapted to harsh environmental conditions, including high temperatures and low rainfall, through its deep root system and drought-resistant foliage. Triodia longiceps plays an important role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for various desert animals.

Its flowering period usually occurs during the warmer months, contributing to the ecological dynamics of its native environment.
