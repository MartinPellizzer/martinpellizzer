Trema malaccense

Trema malaccense, commonly known as the Malacca trema, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by woody, spiny fruits that contain a single seed. It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its durable wood and medicinal properties.

The species is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
