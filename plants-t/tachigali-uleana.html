Tachigali uleana

Tachigali uleana, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or red tachigali, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark-brown bark and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by bright red or orange fruits that split open to release seeds. Tachigali uleana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it adaptable to a variety of tropical climates.
