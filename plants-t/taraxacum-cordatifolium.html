Taraxacum cordatifolium

Taraxacum cordatifolium, commonly known as the heart-leaved dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves and a long, hollow stem that supports a single flower head. The plant produces a distinctive seed head composed of numerous parachute-like seeds, which are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows, lawns, and disturbed soils.

Taraxacum cordatifolium is a hardy perennial that is adapted to a wide range of environmental conditions.
