Timouria saposhnikowii

Timouria saposhnikowii, commonly known as the Persian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and has hairy, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Timouria saposhnikowii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
