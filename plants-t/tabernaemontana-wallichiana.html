Tabernaemontana wallichiana

Tabernaemontana wallichiana, commonly known as the Walsingham's star of Bethlehem, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its showy, fragrant, star-shaped flowers that typically bloom in clusters. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth texture and a glossy surface. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking appearance and unique floral structure.
