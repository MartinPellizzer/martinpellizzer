Tabernaemontana longiflora

Tabernaemontana longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered star of Bethlehem, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are elliptical in shape. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections worldwide.
