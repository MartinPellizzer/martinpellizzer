Teucrium musimonum var. matris-filiae

Teucrium musimonum var. matris-filiae, commonly known as the mother's daughter germander, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features small, tubular flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, arranged in dense clusters. The plant has aromatic, narrow leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
