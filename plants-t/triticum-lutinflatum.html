Triticum lutinflatum

Triticum lutinflatum, commonly known as the flatulent wheat, is a rare and little-studied species within the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its unique morphological features, including flattened glumes and a distinct inflorescence structure. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and is adapted to specific soil conditions. Its genetic makeup offers potential insights into wheat evolution and resilience.
