Thuja occidentalis f. aureovariegata

Thuja occidentalis f. aureovariegata, commonly known as the golden variegated arborvitae, is a cultivar of the eastern white cedar.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow-green foliage, which features white or yellow margins on the leaves. This ornamental plant is often used in landscaping for its attractive appearance and hardy nature. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

The plant has a conical shape and can grow up to 15 feet tall, making it a popular choice for hedges and privacy screens.
