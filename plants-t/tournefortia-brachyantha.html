Tournefortia brachyantha

Tournefortia brachyantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tournefortia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Central and South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in coastal or sandy soils. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are usually borne on short peduncles. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Tournefortia brachyantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
