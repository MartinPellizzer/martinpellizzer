Tolumnia intermedia

Tolumnia intermedia, commonly known as the white mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetlands and riparian areas, thriving in moist, sandy soils. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance.

Tolumnia intermedia produces small white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
