Tirucalia paxiana

Tirucalia paxiana, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a deciduous shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are rich in vitamin C and have a tangy, sour flavor. Tirucalia paxiana thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its medicinal and nutritional value.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both traditional medicine and modern horticulture.
