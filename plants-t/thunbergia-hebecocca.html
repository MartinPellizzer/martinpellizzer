Thunbergia hebecocca

Thunbergia hebecocca, commonly known as the African blue bell, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a climbing vine that produces striking blue or violet tubular flowers, which are often visited by birds and insects. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture, and its stems are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Thunbergia hebecocca is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
