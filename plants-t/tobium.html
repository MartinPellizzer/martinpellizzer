Tobium

Tobium, commonly known as the false dandelion, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosettes of leaves and yellow composite flowers that resemble dandelions. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Tobium species are often found in temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

Its botanical features include hairy stems and bracteate flower heads, which aid in its identification.
