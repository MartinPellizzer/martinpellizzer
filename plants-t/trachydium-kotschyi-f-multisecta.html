Trachydium kotschyi f. multisecta

Trachydium kotschyi f. multisecta, commonly known as the multibracted sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth form and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink to purple. The plant features linear, hairy leaves that are often divided into multiple segments, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures.

Due to its ornamental value and resilience, it is sometimes cultivated in xeriscapes and rock gardens.
