Toddalia simplicifolia var. eugeniifolia

Toddalia simplicifolia var. eugeniifolia, commonly known as the Eugeniifolia Toddalia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated leaves that are typically lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
