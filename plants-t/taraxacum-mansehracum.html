Taraxacum mansehracum

Taraxacum mansehracum, commonly known as the Armenian dandelion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus, particularly Armenia and Georgia. The plant features a rosette of basal, spoon-shaped leaves and produces a yellow flower head that develops into a white, fluffy seed head. It is typically found in rocky, well-drained soils at higher elevations.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
