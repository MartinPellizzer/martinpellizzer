Tragacantha chorassanica

Tragacantha chorassanica, commonly known as Persian tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and woody pods. The plant produces a valuable gum, known as tragacanth, which is used in various industrial and medicinal applications. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils and at high altitudes.

Due to its ecological and economic importance, it is a subject of conservation interest in its native habitats.
