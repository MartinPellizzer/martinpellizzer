Thymus hirsutus var. drosocalyx

Thymus hirsutus var. drosocalyx, commonly known as woolly thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features dense, gray-green foliage covered in fine, soft hairs, which give it a distinctive woolly appearance. The plant produces small, pink or purple flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic leaves are often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
