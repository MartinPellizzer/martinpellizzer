Thaspium cordatum

Thaspium cordatum, commonly known as the heartleaf thaspium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy on the upper surface. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Thaspium cordatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting various pollinators.
