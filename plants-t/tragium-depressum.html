Tragium depressum

Tragium depressum, commonly known as the creeping bellflower, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered with fine pubescence. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Tragium depressum is often found in open habitats such as hillsides, meadows, and disturbed areas.
