Trichomanes mascarenense

Trichomanes mascarenense, commonly known as the Mascarene water fern, is a small, aquatic fern native to the Mascarene Islands in the Indian Ocean.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in freshwater habitats such as ponds and streams, where it anchors itself to submerged surfaces. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, typical of many ferns.

Trichomanes mascarenense plays a role in maintaining the ecological balance of its wetland environment.
