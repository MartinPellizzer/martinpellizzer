Teucrium cuspidatum

Teucrium cuspidatum, commonly known as purple coneflower, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features showy purple or pink flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Teucrium cuspidatum is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for native gardens.
