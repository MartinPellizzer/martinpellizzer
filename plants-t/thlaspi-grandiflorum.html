Thlaspi grandiflorum

Thlaspi grandiflorum, commonly known as the showy cress, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lobed leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. It produces small, rounded fruits called siliques, which contain several seeds.

Thlaspi grandiflorum is a hardy perennial that is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
