Thermopsis alpina

Thermopsis alpina, commonly known as the alpine thermopsis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant produces bright yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Thermopsis alpina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and vibrant floral display.
