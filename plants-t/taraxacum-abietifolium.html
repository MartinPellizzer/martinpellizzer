Taraxacum abietifolium

Taraxacum abietifolium, commonly known as the white-rayed dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf morphology. The plant features basal rosettes of serrated, hairy leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. Its flowers are yellow and produce wind-dispersed seeds, a common trait among dandelions.

It thrives in dry, open habitats and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.
