Tapinanthus buvumae

It belongs to the Orobanchaceae family and lacks chlorophyll, relying entirely on its host plants for nutrients. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and has a vine-like structure that wraps around its host. It is notable for its unique ecological role in the region and its limited distribution.
