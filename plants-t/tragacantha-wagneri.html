Tragacantha wagneri

Tragacantha wagneri, commonly known as Wagner's tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by pod-like fruits containing seeds. It is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Tragacantha wagneri is valued for its historical use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural gum.
