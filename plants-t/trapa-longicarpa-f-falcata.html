Trapa longicarpa f. falcata

Trapa longicarpa f. falcata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water chestnut, is a variant of the water chestnut species native to wetland habitats.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that are often submerged or floating on the water surface. The plant produces distinctive, spiny fruits that contain edible seeds, which are harvested for culinary use. Its growth is typically associated with shallow freshwater environments such as ponds and marshes.

The botanical features of this plant make it an important component of aquatic ecosystems.
