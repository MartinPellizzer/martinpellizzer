Trochodendron aralioides

Trochodendron aralioides, commonly known as the mountain aralia, is a unique evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Trochodendraceae, which is one of the oldest known plant families. The plant features compound leaves with a distinctive, palmately arranged structure, and its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in umbels. It produces woody, berry-like fruits that contain a single seed.

The species is notable for its ancient lineage and is considered a living fossil due to its evolutionary significance.
