Timonius secundiflorus

Timonius secundiflorus, commonly known as the secondary flower plant, is a unique species characterized by its distinctive floral structure.

It belongs to the family Timoniaceae and is native to specific regions in South America. The plant features elongated, spirally arranged leaves that provide efficient light capture. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in shades of pale blue or white.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern.
