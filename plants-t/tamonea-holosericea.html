Tamonea holosericea

Tamonea holosericea, commonly known as the silky sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa, the Middle East, and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its delicate, silky hairs that cover its stems and leaves, giving it a soft, almost velvety appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, often appearing in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to survive in dry, hot environments.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens in suitable climates.
