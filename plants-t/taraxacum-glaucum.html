Taraxacum glaucum

Taraxacum glaucum, commonly known as the blue dandelion, is a perennial flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of blue-gray, hairy leaves and a yellow flowering head that develops into a fluffy white seed head. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in moist, disturbed soils. It has a taproot system that allows it to survive in various environmental conditions.

The species is also known for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in herbal remedies.
