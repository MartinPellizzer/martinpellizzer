Thelypteris mortonii

Thelypteris mortonii, commonly known as the Morton's shield fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Thelypteridaceae and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and densely packed, leathery fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. Its fronds are usually dark green and have a distinctive, shield-like shape, which gives it its common name.

Thelypteris mortonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural settings.
