Tetraglochin acanthocarpum var. typica

Tetraglochin acanthocarpum var. typica, commonly known as the yellow-flowered rock cress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. The plant features basal leaves that are lobed and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branched. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in dense inflorescences, which bloom in early spring.

The plant is well adapted to arid conditions and is often found in coastal and inland rocky habitats.
