Thymus gizellae

Thymus gizellae, commonly known as the Corsican thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, gray-green leaves and produces tiny, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant is characterized by its aromatic foliage, which contains essential oils responsible for its distinctive scent. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

Thymus gizellae is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
