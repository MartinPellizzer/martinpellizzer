Tithymalus mellifer

Tithymalus mellifer, commonly known as the honey thyme, is a flowering plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that range in color from pink to purple, often arranged in whorls along the stems. The plant typically grows in sunny, well-drained soils and is native to regions of southern Europe and the Mediterranean. Its leaves are aromatic and often used in culinary applications, while its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Tithymalus mellifer is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological benefits.
