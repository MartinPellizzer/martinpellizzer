Tretorhiza decumbens

Tretorhiza decumbens, commonly known as the creeping tretorhiza, is a herbaceous plant native to certain regions of South America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. The plant has a prostrate growth habit, with slender stems that often root at the nodes. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The flowers are small and clustered, with a distinctive coloration that aids in pollination by insects.
