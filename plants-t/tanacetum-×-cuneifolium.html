Tanacetum × cuneifolium

Tanacetum × cuneifolium, commonly known as the European tansy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, finely divided leaves and yellow flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its strong, camphor-like scent. It typically grows in moist, shady areas and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Tanacetum × cuneifolium has been used historically for its medicinal properties, though it is toxic if ingested in large quantities.
