Thalictrum pavlovii

Thalictrum pavlovii, commonly known as the Pavlov's meadow rue, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves that are deeply divided and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, clustered white or pale yellow flowers in early summer, which are surrounded by a series of modified leaves called bracts. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Thalictrum pavlovii is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological value to pollinators.
