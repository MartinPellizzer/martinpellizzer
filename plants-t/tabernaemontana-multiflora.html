Tabernaemontana multiflora

Tabernaemontana multiflora, commonly known as the star of India or white star jasmine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, star-shaped white flowers that bloom in clusters during the warm season. The plant has opposite, simple, and usually hairy leaves that are dark green in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires partial to full sun exposure.

Tabernaemontana multiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and pleasant fragrance.
