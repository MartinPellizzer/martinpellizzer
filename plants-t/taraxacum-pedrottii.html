Taraxacum pedrottii

Taraxacum pedrottii, commonly known as the Pedrott's dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of stiff, hairy leaves and a hollow, leafless stem that supports a single, yellow flower head. Its flowers produce achenes with pappus, which are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation.

Taraxacum pedrottii is a perennial species that thrives in temperate climates.
