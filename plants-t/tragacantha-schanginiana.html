Tragacantha schanginiana

Tragacantha schanginiana, commonly known as the Schangin's tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant produces a resinous gum, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive. Its growth habit includes a shrub-like structure with woody stems and small, oval-shaped leaves.
