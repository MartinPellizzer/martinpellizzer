Tragopogon cupanii

Tragopogon cupanii, commonly known as the cupani's thistle, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, spiny leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a single head.

Tragopogon cupanii is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
