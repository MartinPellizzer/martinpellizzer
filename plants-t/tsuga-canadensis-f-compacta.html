Tsuga canadensis f. compacta

Tsuga canadensis f. compacta, commonly known as the dwarf hemlock, is a compact form of the eastern hemlock.

It is characterized by its dense, slow-growing foliage and upright, conical shape. The plant features scale-like leaves that are dark green on top and white underneath, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is native to the eastern United States and prefers moist, well-drained soils in shaded environments.

This cultivar is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded conditions.
