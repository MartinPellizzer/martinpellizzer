Thunbergia annua var. ruspolii

Thunbergia annua var. ruspolii, commonly known as the African bluebells, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its attractive, trumpet-shaped blue and purple flowers. The plant produces small, green, heart-shaped leaves that are opposite on the stems. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers bloom from late spring to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
