Ternstroemia cuneifolia var. glutinosa

Ternstroemia cuneifolia var. glutinosa, commonly known as sticky leaf ternstroemia, is a variant of the evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves that are often coated with a sticky resin, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within tropical and subtropical forests. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, while its fruit is a capsule containing several seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
