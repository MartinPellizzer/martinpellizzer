Tragacantha commixta

Tragacantha commixta, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Iran and the Caucasus.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant produces a resinous gum, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a food additive.

Its adaptability to harsh environments makes it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
