Tephrosia bidwillii

Tephrosia bidwillii, commonly known as the Bidwill's tephrosia, is a perennial shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemes. Its seeds are encased in pods and are often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Tephrosia bidwillii is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in harsh conditions.
