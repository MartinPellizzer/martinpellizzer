Tacca leontopetaloides

Tacca leontopetaloides, commonly known as the "devil's backbone," is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, waxy leaves and a distinctive, fleshy, club-shaped root system. The plant produces striking, orchid-like flowers with long, curling petals that resemble horns. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.
