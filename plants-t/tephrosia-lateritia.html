Tephrosia lateritia

Tephrosia lateritia, commonly known as the coastal tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in sandy or saline soils, adapting well to harsh coastal environments. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are often trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets.

Tephrosia lateritia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize dunes, making it an important plant in coastal ecosystems.
