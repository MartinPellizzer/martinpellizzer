Thymus campestris var. albus

Thymus campestris var. albus, commonly known as white thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has aromatic, slightly woolly leaves that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice in horticulture.
