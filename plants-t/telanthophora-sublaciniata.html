Telanthophora sublaciniata

Telanthophora sublaciniata, commonly known as the lace-leaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves that give it a delicate, lace-like appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Telanthophora sublaciniata is often found in grasslands and along roadsides, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
