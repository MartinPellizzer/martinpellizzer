Tetragonocalamus angulatus

Tetragonocalamus angulatus, commonly known as the angular bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its sturdy, angular stems that form dense clumps. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are usually green and slightly hairy on the underside. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is occasionally cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
