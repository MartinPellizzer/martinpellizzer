Tussacia semiclausa

Tussacia semiclausa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tussac, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, tussock-like growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate climates and is often found in grasslands and wetlands. Its flowering stems produce small, spike-like inflorescences that are usually inconspicuous.

Tussacia semiclausa plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
