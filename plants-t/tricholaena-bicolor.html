Tricholaena bicolor

Tricholaena bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored grass, is a perennial plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its distinctive bicoloration in its foliage. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a striking contrast between the upper and lower surfaces.

Tricholaena bicolor is often used in ornamental gardening due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
