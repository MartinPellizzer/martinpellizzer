Taraxacum opulentiforme

Taraxacum opulentiforme, commonly known as the opulent dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers and heart-shaped basal leaves. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, where it thrives in meadows and grasslands. It produces a distinctive white, fluffy seed head that aids in wind dispersal.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and a subject of botanical interest.
