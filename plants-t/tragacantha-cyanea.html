Tragacantha cyanea

Tragacantha cyanea, commonly known as the blue tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial shrub that produces striking blue or violet flowers during the summer months. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody stems and trifoliate leaves. Its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological importance.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and landscapes.
