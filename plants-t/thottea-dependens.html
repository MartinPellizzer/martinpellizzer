Thottea dependens

Thottea dependens, commonly known as the hanging lily, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous, showy flowers that hang from long, slender stems. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or alpine conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine, white powdery coating.

Thottea dependens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
