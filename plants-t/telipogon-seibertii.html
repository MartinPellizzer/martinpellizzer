Telipogon seibertii

Telipogon seibertii, commonly known as the Seibert's orchid, is a rare and endangered species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, terrestrial growth habit. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer, typically in shaded, humid environments. It thrives in high-altitude grasslands and is adapted to cool, moist conditions.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
