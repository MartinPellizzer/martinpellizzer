Thymus teucrioides subsp. alpinus

Thymus teucrioides subsp.

alpinus, commonly known as alpine thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, aromatic leaves that emit a strong thyme scent.

The plant produces pink to purple flowers in early summer, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun exposure.

This subspecies is often used in traditional herbal medicine and as a ornamental plant in alpine gardens.
