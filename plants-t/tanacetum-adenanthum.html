Tanacetum adenanthum

Tanacetum adenanthum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tansy, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, aromatic leaves with a strong camphor-like scent. Its yellow flowers are typically arranged in flat-topped clusters, attracting various pollinators.

Tanacetum adenanthum is often found in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun.
