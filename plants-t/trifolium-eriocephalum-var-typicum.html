Trifolium eriocephalum var. typicum

Trifolium eriocephalum var. typicum, commonly known as the woolly clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets with a hairy texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical heads, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas.

This plant is valued for its role in soil nitrogen fixation and as a forage crop for livestock.
