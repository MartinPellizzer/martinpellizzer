Tamarix ramosissima var. macedonica

Tamarix ramosissima var. macedonica, commonly known as the Macedonian tamarisk, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense, feathery foliage with small, scale-like leaves that reduce water loss, making it well adapted to arid environments. The plant produces pink or pale purple flowers in summer, which are clustered in cylindrical spikes. Its root system is extensive and can stabilize soil, making it useful for erosion control.

It is often found in coastal and inland saline areas, thriving in well-drained soils.
