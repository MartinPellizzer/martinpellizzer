Tecophilaea cyanocrocus f. leichtlinii

Tecophilaea cyanocrocus f. leichtlinii, commonly known as the blue-flowered tecophilaea, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It features dense, rounded growth with thick, leathery leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces striking, trumpet-shaped blue-violet flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for rock gardens or coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value lies in its vibrant, long-lasting blooms and drought tolerance.
