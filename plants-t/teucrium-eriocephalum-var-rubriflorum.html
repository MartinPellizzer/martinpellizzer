Teucrium eriocephalum var. rubriflorum

Teucrium eriocephalum var. rubriflorum, commonly known as the red-flowered germander, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces clusters of small, reddish-purple flowers. It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
