Tanacetum fastigiatum

Tanacetum fastigiatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tansy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a strong, camphor-like scent, and it produces yellow flower heads that develop into brown, tufted seed heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and grasslands.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
