Tinus ambigua

Tinus ambigua, commonly known as the slender milkweed, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features elongated, slender stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in loose inflorescences.

The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds with fine, silky hairs.
