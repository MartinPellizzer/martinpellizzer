Tragacantha forsskaolii

Tragacantha forsskaolii, commonly known as the Sudanese tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant produces a gum resin, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a thickening agent.

Its ability to thrive in harsh environments makes it an important species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
