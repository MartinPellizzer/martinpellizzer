Taraxacum huelphersianum

Taraxacum huelphersianum, commonly known as the Huelphersian dandelion, is a perennial herb native to specific regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy rosette structure. The plant features finely divided, grass-like leaves that form a dense basal rosette, and it produces a distinctive, hollow, wind-dispersed seed head. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and sandy soils, and it is often found in disturbed areas.

The species is notable for its adaptability and ecological importance in supporting pollinators and soil stability.
