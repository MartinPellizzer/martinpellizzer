Taraxacum lunatum

Taraxacum lunatum, commonly known as the moon dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, white, saucer-shaped flowers that bloom in a cluster, resembling a small moon. The plant has a basal rosette of narrow, pale green leaves with a waxy coating. It is native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa, often growing in dry, sunny habitats.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and is often found in meadows and disturbed ground.
