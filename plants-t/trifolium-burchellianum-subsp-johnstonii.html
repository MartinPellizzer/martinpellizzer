Trifolium burchellianum subsp. johnstonii

Trifolium burchellianum subsp.

johnstonii, commonly known as Johnston's clover, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, typically composed of three oval or rounded leaflets.

The plant produces small, pink or white flowers in dense clusters, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
