Trichera sylvatica var. lanceolata

Trichera sylvatica var. lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf trichera, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of North America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are typically serrated along the edges. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring. Its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional herbal medicine.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.
