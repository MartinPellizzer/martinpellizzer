Thymus majorana

Thymus majorana, commonly known as sweet thyme, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant leaves and pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils rich in thymol, which contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.

Botanically, it belongs to the Lamiaceae family, sharing characteristics with other members like mint and lavender.
