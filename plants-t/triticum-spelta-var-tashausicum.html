Triticum spelta var. tashausicum

Triticum spelta var. tashausicum, commonly known as Tashausic Triticum, is a rare variant of spelt wheat.

It is characterized by its unique morphological features, including slender spikes and awned glumes. This variety is native to specific regions in the Pacific Northwest of the United States. Botanically, it exhibits a diploid genome structure, distinguishing it from other spelt varieties.

Its genetic makeup and growth habits make it an interesting subject for agricultural and botanical research.
