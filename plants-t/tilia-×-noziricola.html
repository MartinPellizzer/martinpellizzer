Tilia × noziricola

Tilia × noziricola, commonly known as the whitebeam, is a hybrid species in the Tiliaceae family, derived from crosses between Tilia cordata and Tilia europaea.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves that are typically 5–10 cm in length. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are clustered in racemes. It is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions, making it a versatile choice for landscaping.
