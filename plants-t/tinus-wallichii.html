Tinus wallichii

Tinus wallichii, commonly known as the Himalayan blue poppy, is a striking flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its deep blue to violet petals, which are often marked with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are palmately lobed and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Tinus wallichii is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological significance in its native habitat.
