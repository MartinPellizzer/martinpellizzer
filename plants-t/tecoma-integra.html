Tecoma integra

Tecoma integra, commonly known as the yellow bells, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height, characterized by its bright yellow, bell-shaped flowers. The leaves are simple, opposite, and ovate with a smooth margin, while the stems are often covered in fine hairs. Tecoma integra thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its flowers bloom in clusters and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
