Trichinium corymbosum var. parviflorum

Trichinium corymbosum var. parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered trichinium, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in the summer months. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture. This variety is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
