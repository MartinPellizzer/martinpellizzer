Trifolium rubens var. subglobosum

Trifolium rubens var. subglobosum, commonly known as the red clover variant subglobosum, is a perennial legume characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pinkish-red flowers.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and pastures. Its compact, globular flower heads are distinctive and contribute to its ornamental value.

It is also valued in agricultural systems for its role in soil enrichment and forage production.
