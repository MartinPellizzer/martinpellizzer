Thymus lanceolatus var. hyssopifolius

Thymus lanceolatus var. hyssopifolius, commonly known as narrowleaf thyme with hyssop-like leaves, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features slender, linear leaves that are slightly hairy and have a strong, aromatic scent. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
