Tulipa australis subsp. mauritii

Tulipa australis subsp.

mauritii, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a perennial bulbous plant native to South Africa. It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, often with a distinctive red or purple base.

The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge in early spring.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
