Thymus serpyllum var. kuminovianus

Thymus serpyllum var. kuminovianus, commonly known as Kuminovian thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, fragrant, dark green leaves that are densely packed on creeping stems. The plant produces pink to purple flowers in clusters, typically blooming in early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its essential oils contain thymol and carvacrol, contributing to its aromatic and medicinal properties.
