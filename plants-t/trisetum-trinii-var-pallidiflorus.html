Trisetum trinii var. pallidiflorus

Trisetum trinii var. pallidiflorus, commonly known as the pale-flowered hairgrass, is a grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces pale blue to whitish flowers in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a soft texture.

The variety is distinguished by its reduced flower color compared to other varieties of Trisetum trinii.
