Taraxacum peraccommodatum

Taraxacum peraccommodatum, commonly known as the European dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated, hairy leaves and a hollow, leafy stem that supports a yellow flower head. The plant produces a distinctive seed dispersal mechanism with feathery pappus that allows for wind pollination and seed distribution. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in meadows, lawns, and disturbed areas.

Taraxacum peraccommodatum is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its diuretic and detoxifying effects.
