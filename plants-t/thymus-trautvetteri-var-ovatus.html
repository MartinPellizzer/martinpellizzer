Thymus trautvetteri var. ovatus

Thymus trautvetteri var. ovatus, commonly known as the ovate thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with small, aromatic leaves that are oval in shape. The plant produces clusters of pale pink to purple flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its foliage is often used in culinary and medicinal applications due to its aromatic properties.
