Triosteum sinuatum

Triosteum sinuatum, commonly known as the zigzag mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that features heart-shaped, lobed leaves with a distinctive zigzag pattern along the edges. The plant produces showy, pale pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are typically arranged in clusters and are attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Triosteum sinuatum prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.
