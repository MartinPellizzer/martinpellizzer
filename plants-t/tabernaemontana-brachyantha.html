Tabernaemontana brachyantha

Tabernaemontana brachyantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved star of Bethlehem, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a faint fragrance. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
