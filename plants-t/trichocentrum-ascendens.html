Trichocentrum ascendens

Trichocentrum ascendens, commonly known as the ascending orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, slender stems that can grow up to several meters in length, often climbing or trailing through trees. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom during the dry season. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Trichocentrum ascendens is well adapted to humid, shaded environments and requires good air circulation and moderate watering for optimal growth.
