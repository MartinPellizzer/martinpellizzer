Tmesipteris lanceolata

Tmesipteris lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in freshwater environments such as ponds and slow-moving streams. The plant has slender, lance-shaped fronds that are often submerged or floating. Its rhizomes form dense mats, which help stabilize the substrate and provide habitat for aquatic life.

Tmesipteris lanceolata is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological benefits in aquatic ecosystems.
