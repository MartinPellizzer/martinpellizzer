Tetracera dichotoma

Tetracera dichotoma, commonly known as the split-stemmed tetracera, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its distinctive dichotomous branching pattern. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate or elliptic in shape. Its small, yellowish flowers are clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

Tetracera dichotoma is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and along stream banks.
