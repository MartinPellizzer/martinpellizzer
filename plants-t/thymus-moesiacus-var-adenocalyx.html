Thymus moesiacus var. adenocalyx

Thymus moesiacus var. adenocalyx, commonly known as the Balkan thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically gray-green in color, covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
