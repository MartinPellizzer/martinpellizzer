Tadehagi triquetrum subsp. pseudotriquetrum

Tadehagi triquetrum subsp.

pseudotriquetrum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region. It features trifoliate leaves with three oval to lance-shaped leaflets, and its flowers are typically pink or purple, arranged in racemes.

The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds. It is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil enrichment and ecological restoration.
