Tephrosia flagellaris

Tephrosia flagellaris, commonly known as the sicklepod or flagellar tephrosia, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, often branching stems and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, and its fruits are dry pods that split open to release seeds. Tephrosia flagellaris is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.

It also serves as a host plant for certain insect species and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
