Tabernaemontana barteri

Tabernaemontana barteri, commonly known as the star-shaped crown of thorns, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, star-like white flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a subtle fragrance. The plant has dark green, elliptical leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Tabernaemontana barteri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
