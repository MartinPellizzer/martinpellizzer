Tristachya butuluensis

Tristachya butuluensis, commonly known as the Butuluensis panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly found in Jamaica.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, elongated leaves. The plant produces inflorescences that are composed of multiple spikelets, which are arranged in a panicle structure. Tristachya butuluensis is often found in moist, well-drained soils in coastal areas and is adapted to tropical climates.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local flora and ecosystem.
