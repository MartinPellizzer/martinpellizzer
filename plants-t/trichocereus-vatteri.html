Trichocereus vatteri

Trichocereus vatteri, commonly known as the Vatter's torch cactus, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stem that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, blooming at night. It is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Trichocereus vatteri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its association with traditional use in some cultures.
