Trifolium chrysopogon

Trifolium chrysopogon, commonly known as yellow clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets, and its small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. It has a shallow root system that helps prevent soil erosion and contributes to nitrogen fixation through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria.

Trifolium chrysopogon is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
