Tricalysia capensis var. galpinii

Tricalysia capensis var. galpinii, commonly known as the Galpin's tricalysia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, often found in the Western and Northern Cape provinces of South Africa. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a slightly rough texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
