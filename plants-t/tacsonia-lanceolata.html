Tacsonia lanceolata

Tacsonia lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf tacsonia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, bell-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and spring blooms.

Tacsonia lanceolata is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
