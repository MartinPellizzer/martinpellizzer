Turbinicarpus schmiedickeanus var. kupcakii

Turbinicarpus schmiedickeanus var. kupcakii, commonly known as the Kupcakii Turbinated Cactus, is a rare and striking member of the Cactaceae family native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are covered in dense, white to pale green spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, which are often borne at the top of the stem. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and cactus enthusiasts.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
