Thymus serpyllum var. hispidus

Thymus serpyllum var. hispidus, commonly known as hairy thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, fragrant, gray-green leaves covered with fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pink or white flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic foliage is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
