Teucrium laciniatum

Teucrium laciniatum, commonly known as the cut-leaved germander, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features aromatic, gray-green leaves that are finely cut, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Teucrium laciniatum is also valued for its medicinal properties, traditionally used to treat digestive ailments and skin conditions.
