Teucrium pyrenaicum subsp. guarensis

Teucrium pyrenaicum subsp.

guarensis, commonly known as the Pyrenean germander, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone-rich soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its small, tubular flowers are usually purple or pink, and it produces small, four-angled fruits.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
