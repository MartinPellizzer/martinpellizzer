Tellima odorata

Tellima odorata, commonly known as the sweet-scented tellima, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the family Saxifragaceae and is characterized by its clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a fragrant aroma when crushed. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodlands and stream banks.

Tellima odorata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
