Trifolium perretii

Trifolium perretii, commonly known as the Perret's clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

Trifolium perretii is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
