Thymus serpyllum f. intermittens

Thymus serpyllum f. intermittens, commonly known as intermittent thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, aromatic, gray-green leaves and produces pink or purple flowers in early summer. The plant is characterized by its intermittent blooming pattern, which gives it its botanical name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and use in culinary and medicinal applications.
