Ternstroemia penduliflora

Ternstroemia penduliflora, commonly known as the hanging-flower tree, is a member of the Theaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in lowland forests and is characterized by its pendulous inflorescences. The plant features elliptical, glossy leaves and produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are capsule-like structures containing several seeds, and it is often valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.

Ternstroemia penduliflora is also noted for its ability to thrive in shaded environments, making it a popular choice for shaded garden settings.
