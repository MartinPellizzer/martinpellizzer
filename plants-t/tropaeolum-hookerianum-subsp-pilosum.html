Tropaeolum hookerianum subsp. pilosum

Tropaeolum hookerianum subsp.

pilosum, commonly known as the hairy jewelweed, is a flowering plant native to South America. It is characterized by its vibrant, spurred flowers that range in color from yellow to orange.

The plant has heart-shaped leaves covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its unique foliage and blossoms.
