Tanacetum argenteum subsp. flabellifolium

Tanacetum argenteum subsp.

flabellifolium, commonly known as the silverleaf tansy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It features finely divided, silvery-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads arranged in flat-topped clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its aromatic foliage and compact growth habit make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
