Taraxacum printzii

Taraxacum printzii, commonly known as the Printz's dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces a distinctive hollow stem and a fluffy seed head that disperses its seeds by wind. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Botanically, it is closely related to other dandelion species but is distinguished by its specific geographic distribution and morphological traits.
