Trichosanthes laeoica f. yapenensis

Trichosanthes laeoica f. yapenensis, commonly known as the Yapen bitter melon, is a variant of the Trichosanthes genus native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, ribbed fruits that are typically green when immature and turn yellow or orange when ripe. The plant is a climbing vine with large, heart-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are used in traditional medicine and as a vegetable in some local cuisines.

The botanical features of this plant make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and pharmacological study.
