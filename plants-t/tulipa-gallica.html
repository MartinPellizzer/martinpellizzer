Tulipa gallica

Tulipa gallica, commonly known as the garden tulip, is a perennial bulbous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in a variety of colors, including red, yellow, and white, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is well adapted to temperate climates. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Tulipa gallica is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and historical significance in horticulture.
