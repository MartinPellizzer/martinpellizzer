Taraxacum stramineum

Taraxacum stramineum, commonly known as the pale dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of elongated, pale green leaves and a hollow, hairless stem. The plant produces a single, yellow composite flower head that develops into a distinctive white, fluffy seed head. It is native to Europe and is often found in disturbed soils, meadows, and along roadsides.

Taraxacum stramineum is a perennial herb that is adapted to temperate climates and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
