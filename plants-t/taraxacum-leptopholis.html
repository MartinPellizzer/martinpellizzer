Taraxacum leptopholis

Taraxacum leptopholis, commonly known as the slenderleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces a single, hollow, hairy stem that supports a bright yellow composite flower head. After flowering, it develops a distinctive seed head composed of numerous parachute-like seeds.

It is often found in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.
