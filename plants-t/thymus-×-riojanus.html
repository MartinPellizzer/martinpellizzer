Thymus × riojanus

Thymus × riojanus, commonly known as the Rioja thyme, is a hybrid species within the genus Thymus, characterized by its aromatic foliage and small, pinkish flowers.

It is native to the Iberian Peninsula, particularly Spain, where it thrives in rocky and sunny environments. The plant produces compact, low-growing shrubs with gray-green leaves that emit a strong thyme scent when crushed. It is often used in herbal teas and culinary applications due to its medicinal and aromatic properties.

Thymus × riojanus is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
