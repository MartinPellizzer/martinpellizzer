Tecoma rosea

Tecoma rosea, commonly known as the yellow bells, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height, characterized by its pinnate leaves and showy yellow flowers. The flowers are typically clustered in pendulous racemes and bloom during the dry season. Tecoma rosea thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

It is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
