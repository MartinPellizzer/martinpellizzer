Temnadenia secundiflora

Temnadenia secundiflora, commonly known as the coastal morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in coastal dunes and sandy soils. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually blue or purple in color. Its leaves are alternate and heart-shaped, with a rough texture.

Temnadenia secundiflora is adapted to salt spray and wind, making it a hardy species in coastal environments.
