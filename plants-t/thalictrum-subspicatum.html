Thalictrum subspicatum

Thalictrum subspicatum, commonly known as the slender meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in early to mid-summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Thalictrum subspicatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting various pollinators.
