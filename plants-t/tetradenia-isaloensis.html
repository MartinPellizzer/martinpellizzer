Tetradenia isaloensis

Tetradenia isaloensis, commonly known as the Isalo tetraneuris, is a unique plant species native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its woody structure and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions. Its small, clustered flowers are often white or pink, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Due to its restricted distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
