Trichosanthes integrifolia

Trichosanthes integrifolia, commonly known as the whole-leaf bitter gourd, is a climbing vine belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with smooth margins. The plant produces long, cylindrical fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn orange when mature. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

The fruit is used in traditional medicine and cuisine, valued for its bitter taste and medicinal properties.
