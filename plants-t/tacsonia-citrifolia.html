Tacsonia citrifolia

Tacsonia citrifolia, commonly known as the citrus tree, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and fragrant, citrus-scented flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a rough, textured surface.

Tacsonia citrifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and for the edible fruits it yields.
