Tricholepis amplexicaulis

Tricholepis amplexicaulis, commonly known as the clasping leaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique foliage, where the leaves clasp the stem tightly. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
