Trifolium albopurpureum var. neolagopus

Trifolium albopurpureum var. neolagopus, commonly known as the purple-white clover, is a variant of the common white clover species.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which typically have a white or pale purple coloration on the leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are usually pink or white in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along roadsides.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in soil stabilization and forage production.
