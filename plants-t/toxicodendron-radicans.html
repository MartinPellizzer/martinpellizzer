Toxicodendron radicans

Toxicodendron radicans, commonly known as poison ivy, is a member of the Anacardiaceae family and is native to North America.

It is a deciduous vine or shrub that produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers and red, fuzzy berries. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged in a staggered pattern. It contains urushiol, a potent skin irritant that causes allergic reactions in many people.

This plant thrives in a variety of habitats, including forests, fields, and along roadsides.
