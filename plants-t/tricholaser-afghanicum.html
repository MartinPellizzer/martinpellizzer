Tricholaser afghanicum

Tricholaser afghanicum, commonly known as the Afghan ghost fungus, is a rare and enigmatic species of basidiomycete fungus.

It is characterized by its translucent, ghost-like cap that often appears as a faint outline on decaying wood. The fungus is typically found in the mountainous regions of Afghanistan, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. Its spores are released in a unique manner, dispersing through the air in a delicate, ethereal pattern.

Due to its elusive nature and limited distribution, it remains a subject of interest for mycologists and conservationists alike.
