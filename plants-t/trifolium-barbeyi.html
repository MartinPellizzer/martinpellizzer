Trifolium barbeyi

Trifolium barbeyi, commonly known as the Barbey's clover, is a flowering plant species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. Trifolium barbeyi thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
