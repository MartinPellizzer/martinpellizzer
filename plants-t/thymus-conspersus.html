Thymus conspersus

Thymus conspersus, commonly known as hairy thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features small, fragrant leaves covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pink to purple flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic properties make it valuable in both culinary and medicinal applications.
