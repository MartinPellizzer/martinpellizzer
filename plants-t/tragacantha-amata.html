Tragacantha amata

Tragacantha amata, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a species of flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and woody, thorned stems. The plant is known for producing a valuable gum, tragacanth, which has been used historically in medicine and as a food thickener.

Its leaves are trifoliate, and it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments.
