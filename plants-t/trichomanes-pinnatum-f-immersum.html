Trichomanes pinnatum f. immersum

Trichomanes pinnatum f. immersum, commonly known as the submerged fern, is a rare and specialized variant of the fern species.

It is characterized by its unique morphology, with fronds that are adapted for a submergent aquatic lifestyle. The plant exhibits a prostrate growth habit, with rhizomes that spread along the substrate in water bodies. Its leaves are typically narrow and strap-like, with a reduced structure to minimize resistance in flowing water.

This fern is often found in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats, where it plays a minor but ecologically significant role.
