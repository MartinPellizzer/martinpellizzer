Tinus clarkeana

Tinus clarkeana, commonly known as the Clarke's tinus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are opposite in arrangement. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in Mediterranean climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms and unique foliage.
