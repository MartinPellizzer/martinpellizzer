Trigonella melilotus var. caerulea

Trigonella melilotus var. caerulea, commonly known as blue sweet clover, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, fragrant blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces cylindrical pods containing several seeds, which are often used in agricultural settings as a forage crop. It thrives in well-drained soils and is known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

Its aromatic properties also make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
