Thymus boissieri

Thymus boissieri, commonly known as Boissier's thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Thymus boissieri is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
