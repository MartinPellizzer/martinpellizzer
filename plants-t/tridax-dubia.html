Tridax dubia

Tridax dubia, commonly known as the hairy dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in various habitats such as roadsides and fields. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its flowers develop into fluffy seed heads.

Tridax dubia is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and its use in traditional medicine.
