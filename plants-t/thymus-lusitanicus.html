Thymus lusitanicus

Thymus lusitanicus, commonly known as the Portuguese thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense clusters. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, which contribute to its use in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

Thymus lusitanicus thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone-rich habitats.
