Tragacantha breviflora

Tragacantha breviflora, commonly known as the short-flowered tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces a distinctive, sticky resin known as tragacanth, which has been used historically in medicine and as a binding agent. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Its unique botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
