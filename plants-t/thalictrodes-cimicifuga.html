Thalictrodes cimicifuga

Thalictrodes cimicifuga, commonly known as the American bellflower, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America, particularly found in moist woodlands and meadows. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, with five petals and prominent stamens. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval to lance-shaped leaflets.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential medicinal properties.
