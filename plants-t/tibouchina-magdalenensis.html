Tibouchina magdalenensis

Tibouchina magdalenensis, commonly known as the Magdalena tibouchina, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters. The plant is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that consist of clusters of pink or red flowers. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a glossy surface.

Tibouchina magdalenensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
