Tydaea ceciliae

Tydaea ceciliae, commonly known as the Cecilia's grass, is a perennial grass species native to specific regions of South America.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and wetlands. The plant has slender, upright stems with narrow leaves that are usually green and slightly hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, which produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Tydaea ceciliae plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small invertebrates.
