Telipogon kukwae

Telipogon kukwae, commonly known as the Kukwae grass, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
