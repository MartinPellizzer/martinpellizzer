Thymus japonicus

Thymus japonicus, commonly known as Japanese thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Japan.

It features dense, creeping stems covered in small, rounded leaves that are aromatic and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic properties make it a popular choice for both ornamental and culinary use.
