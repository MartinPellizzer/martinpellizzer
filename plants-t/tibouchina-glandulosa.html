Tibouchina glandulosa

Tibouchina glandulosa, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves with prominent veins, and its stems are covered in glandular hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Tibouchina glandulosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
