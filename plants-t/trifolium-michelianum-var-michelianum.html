Trifolium michelianum var. michelianum

Trifolium michelianum var. michelianum, commonly known as Micheli's clover, is a species of the legume family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and its ability to fix nitrogen, benefiting the surrounding soil.
