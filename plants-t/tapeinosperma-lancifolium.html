Tapeinosperma lancifolium

Tapeinosperma lancifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tamarind, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and distinctive yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated, woody pods containing flat, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
