Thymus conspersus var. lycaonicus

Thymus conspersus var. lycaonicus, commonly known as the Lycaon thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage of small, aromatic leaves. The plant produces pink to purple flowers in late spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its essential oils contain thymol, which contributes to its medicinal and culinary uses.
