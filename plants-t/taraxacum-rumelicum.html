Taraxacum rumelicum

Taraxacum rumelicum, commonly known as the Swiss dandelion, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf structure. The plant features basal, spoon-shaped leaves that are hairy and often have a white underside. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in spring, followed by the characteristic wind-dispersed seeds.

It is often found in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, showing adaptability to various environments.
