Thymus serpyllum f. danubialis

Thymus serpyllum f. danubialis, commonly known as Danube thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features small, fragrant leaves that are typically gray-green in color, covered in fine hairs. The plant produces tiny, pinkish-white flowers in compact clusters during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This subspecies is valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in herbal medicine and culinary applications.
