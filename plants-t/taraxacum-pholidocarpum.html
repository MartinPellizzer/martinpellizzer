Taraxacum pholidocarpum

Taraxacum pholidocarpum, commonly known as the Mexican dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its basal rosette of pale green, hairy leaves. The plant produces a yellow composite flower head that develops into a pappus-equipped seed head, typical of the dandelion genus. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

The plant has medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
