Tigridia hallbergii subsp. lloydii

Tigridia hallbergii subsp.

lloydii, commonly known as the Lloyd's tiger iris, is a perennial bulbous plant native to Mexico. It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow.

The plant features grass-like leaves and produces upright, branched flower stalks. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, which can extend from spring to early summer.
