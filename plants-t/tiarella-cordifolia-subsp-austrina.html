Tiarella cordifolia subsp. austrina

Tiarella cordifolia subsp.

austrina, commonly known as the Australian foamflower, is a perennial herb native to eastern Australia. It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, white fuzzy coating.

The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking shade-tolerant plants.
