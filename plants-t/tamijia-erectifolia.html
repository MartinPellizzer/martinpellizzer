Tamijia erectifolia

Tamijia erectifolia, commonly known as the upright-leaved tamijia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom during the wet season. Tamijia erectifolia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rainforest understories.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in ecological and horticultural contexts.
