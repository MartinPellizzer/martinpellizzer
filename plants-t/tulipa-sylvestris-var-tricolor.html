Tulipa sylvestris var. tricolor

Tulipa sylvestris var. tricolor, commonly known as the wild tulip or tricolor tulip, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of white, yellow, and red, which bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. It is often found in rocky or alpine habitats and is valued for its ornamental beauty.

This species is also noted for its ability to naturalize and form dense colonies in suitable conditions.
