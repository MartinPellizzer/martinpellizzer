Telipogon caulescens

Telipogon caulescens, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid characterized by its slender, erect stem and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces delicate, greenish-white flowers that are typically arranged in a raceme. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in montane forests.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
