Tridactyle bicaudata subsp. rupestris

Tridactyle bicaudata subsp.

rupestris, commonly known as the rock tridactyle, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its compact, fleshy stems and small, clustered leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax.

The plant produces small, pale green flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its slow growth and unique appearance, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
