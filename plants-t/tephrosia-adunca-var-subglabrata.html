Tephrosia adunca var. subglabrata

Tephrosia adunca var. subglabrata, commonly known as the African velvet bean, is a perennial legume native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemes, followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fallow fields.

This species is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability and use in traditional medicine.
