Tamarindus officinalis

Tamarindus officinalis, commonly known as the tamarind tree, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to Africa and parts of southern Asia.

It is characterized by its long, pod-like fruit that contains a sticky, acidic pulp surrounded by a fibrous outer layer. The plant has bipinnate leaves and produces yellow flowers that develop into the distinctive seed pods. Tamarind is valued for its fruit, which is used in cooking and traditional medicine, and for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Its botanical features make it an important species in tropical ecosystems and agricultural systems.
