Trachyandra floribunda

Trachyandra floribunda, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit. The plant produces clusters of small, pale green to white flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often covered in a fine, gritty texture.

Trachyandra floribunda thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
