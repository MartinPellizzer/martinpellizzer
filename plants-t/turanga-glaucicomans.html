Turanga glaucicomans

Turanga glaucicomans, commonly known as the blue-veined fern, is a perennial plant native to the temperate regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that exhibit a striking blue-green hue due to the presence of a unique pigment in its cells. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it an ideal candidate for woodland gardens. Its reproductive structure consists of small, spore-bearing cones located at the base of the fronds.

Turanga glaucicomans is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its role in supporting local biodiversity.
