Thymus widderi f. beckii

Thymus widderi f. beckii, commonly known as the Beck's thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It features small, compact shrubs with dense, aromatic foliage that is typically gray-green in color. The plant produces tiny, pinkish-white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are often used in culinary applications due to their strong, minty aroma.

This species is well adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.
