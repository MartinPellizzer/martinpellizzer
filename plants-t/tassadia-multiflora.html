Tassadia multiflora

Tassadia multiflora, commonly known as the many-flowered tassadia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of purple and pink. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy to the touch. Tassadia multiflora is well adapted to rocky soils and can thrive in full sun to partial shade.

It is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive appearance and resilience.
