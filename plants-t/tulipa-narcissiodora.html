Tulipa narcissiodora

Tulipa narcissiodora, commonly known as the Persian tulip, is a species native to the mountains of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that come in a range of colors including white, yellow, and pink. The plant typically grows to a height of 10 to 30 centimeters, with narrow, grass-like leaves. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, often blooming in spring.

The bulbs are perennial and can be propagated through division or seed.
