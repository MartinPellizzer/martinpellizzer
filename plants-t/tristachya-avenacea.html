Tristachya avenacea

Tristachya avenacea, commonly known as the panic grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences, which are typically green to reddish in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a tendency to curl at the edges.

Tristachya avenacea is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.
