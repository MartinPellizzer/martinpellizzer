Trifolium coccineum

Trifolium coccineum, commonly known as the red clover, is a flowering plant in the legume family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pink or red flowers that bloom in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

Trifolium coccineum is also valued for its role in nitrogen fixation, enriching the soil with nutrients.
