Thalictrum scaposum

Thalictrum scaposum, commonly known as the common meadow rue, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring slender, upright stems that are often slightly hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers in early to mid-summer, arranged in compound umbels. Its leaves are compound, with multiple leaflets that are lance-shaped and serrated.

Thalictrum scaposum prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.
