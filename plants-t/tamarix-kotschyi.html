Tamarix kotschyi

Tamarix kotschyi, commonly known as the desert tamarisk, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its salt-tolerant nature, allowing it to thrive in saline soils and coastal environments. The plant produces small, pink or red flowers in summer, which are often followed by fluffy, white seed-bearing structures. Its deep root system enables it to access groundwater, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

Tamarix kotschyi is also known for its ability to stabilize sandy soils and prevent erosion in harsh landscapes.
