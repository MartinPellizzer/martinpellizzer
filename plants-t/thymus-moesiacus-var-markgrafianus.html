Thymus moesiacus var. markgrafianus

Thymus moesiacus var. markgrafianus, commonly known as the Markgrafian thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It features aromatic, narrow-lanceolate leaves that are densely covered with fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers in clusters during late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens.

Its essential oils contain thymol and carvacrol, giving it both medicinal and culinary uses.
