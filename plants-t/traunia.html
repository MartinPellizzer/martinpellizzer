Traunia

Traunia, commonly known as the golden thread, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Primulaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture. Native to mountainous regions of Europe, it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its delicate appearance and early blooming period, it is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
