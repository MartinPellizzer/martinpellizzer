Taverniera floribunda

Taverniera floribunda, commonly known as the Florida bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive red or orange inflorescences that resemble bottle brushes, composed of numerous small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 feet. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and elliptical, with a smooth texture and a glossy appearance.

Taverniera floribunda thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for coastal landscapes.
