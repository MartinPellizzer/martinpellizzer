Tillandsia suescana

Tillandsia suescana, commonly known as the Suesca air plant, is a species of bromeliad native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on the surface of other plants without deriving nutrients from them. The plant features rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves that are covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help in absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air. It produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
