Tristemma mildbraedii

Tristemma mildbraedii, commonly known as the African rock fig, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small shrub characterized by its woody stems and dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible fig-like fruits. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
