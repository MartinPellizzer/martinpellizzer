Thymus maroccanus subsp. leptobotrys

Thymus maroccanus subsp.

leptobotrys, commonly known as Moroccan thyme, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of North Africa. It features small, narrow leaves that are densely packed, giving it a compact appearance.

The plant produces tiny, pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. It is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and can thrive in full sun.

Its aromatic foliage is often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
