Teucrium purpureum

Teucrium purpureum, commonly known as purple germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that are often reddish-purple in color, and it produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant has aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Teucrium purpureum is also valued for its medicinal properties, traditionally used to treat ailments such as digestive issues and skin conditions.
