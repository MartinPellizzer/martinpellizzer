Taraxacum fastuosum

Taraxacum fastuosum, commonly known as the showy dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the sunflower family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a rosette of basal leaves that are typically hairy and lobed. Its taproot is long and sturdy, allowing it to thrive in a variety of soil types.

It is often found in meadows, roadsides, and disturbed areas, contributing to its widespread distribution.
