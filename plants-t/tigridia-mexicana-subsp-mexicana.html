Tigridia mexicana subsp. mexicana

Tigridia mexicana subsp.

mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican sword lily, is a striking bulbous perennial native to Mexico. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers. Its flowers bloom in spring, often followed by the development of seed pods in summer.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in temperate climates.
