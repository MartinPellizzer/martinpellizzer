Tradescantia erecta

Tradescantia erecta, commonly known as the snake plant or mother-in-law's tongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its upright, sword-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or silver markings. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers on slender stems during the summer months. It is highly tolerant of low light and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its ability to purify air and thrive in various conditions contributes to its widespread cultivation.
