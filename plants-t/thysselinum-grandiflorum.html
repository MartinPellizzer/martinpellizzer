Thysselinum grandiflorum

Thysselinum grandiflorum, commonly known as the grandiflorous thysselinum, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the southern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, with five petals and prominent stamens, making it a visually striking addition to gardens.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, though it can tolerate partial shade.
