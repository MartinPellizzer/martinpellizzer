Trillium erectum f. sessiloides

Trillium erectum f. sessiloides, commonly known as the sessile-flowered white trillium, is a rare variant of the white trillium native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive three-part leaves and a single, nodding white flower with three petals. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist woodlands and thrives in rich, well-drained soil. Its flowers bloom in early spring, often before the leaves are fully developed.

This fern-like plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
