Tanacetum chrysanthemoides

Tanacetum chrysanthemoides, commonly known as the common tansy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, yellowish-green leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and chrysanthemums. It is often found in moist, disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats.

Tanacetum chrysanthemoides is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used historically in herbal remedies.
