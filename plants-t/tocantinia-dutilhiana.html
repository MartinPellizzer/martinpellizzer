Tocantinia dutilhiana

Tocantinia dutilhiana, commonly known as the Brazilian star orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, star-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually a vibrant mix of yellow and orange, with intricate patterns that attract pollinators.

Tocantinia dutilhiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and plays a role in the ecological diversity of the Amazon region.
