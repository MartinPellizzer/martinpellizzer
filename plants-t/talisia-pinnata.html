Talisia pinnata

Talisia pinnata, commonly known as the pink-leaf talisia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, compound leaves that are often pink or red when young, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by edible fruits that are round and fleshy. Talisia pinnata thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural use in tropical gardens.
