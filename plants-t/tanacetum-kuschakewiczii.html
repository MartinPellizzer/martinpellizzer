Tanacetum kuschakewiczii

Tanacetum kuschakewiczii, commonly known as Kuschakewicz's tansy, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, gray-green foliage and small yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. It has a strong, aromatic scent due to the presence of sesquiterpene lactones.

Tanacetum kuschakewiczii is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
