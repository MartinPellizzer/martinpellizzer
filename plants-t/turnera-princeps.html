Turnera princeps

Turnera princeps, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces long, trumpet-shaped flowers that attract pollinators like bees and hummingbirds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its vibrant color and unique appearance.

Turnera princeps prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun exposure.
