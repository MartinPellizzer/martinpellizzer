Tragia volubilis var. serra

Tragia volubilis var. serra, commonly known as the mountain tragia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a climbing shrub characterized by its slender stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, oblong fruits that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats and is often found in rocky or arid regions.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural studies.
