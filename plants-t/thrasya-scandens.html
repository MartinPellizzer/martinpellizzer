Thrasya scandens

Thrasya scandens, commonly known as the climbing corymbia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and ability to climb by means of twining stems. The plant produces small, clustered white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and usually have a rough texture.

Thrasya scandens is often found in open woodlands and along coastal areas, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
