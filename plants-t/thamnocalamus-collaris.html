Thamnocalamus collaris

Thamnocalamus collaris, commonly known as the collar bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant features slender, hollow stems that are often covered in a whitish, powdery coating. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green color.

Thamnocalamus collaris is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to form attractive, compact clusters.
