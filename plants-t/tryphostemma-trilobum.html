Tryphostemma trilobum

Tryphostemma trilobum, commonly known as the three-lobed morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist, shaded areas within tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a garden plant.
