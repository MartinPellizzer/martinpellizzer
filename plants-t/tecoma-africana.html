Tecoma africana

Tecoma africana, commonly known as the yellow flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Bignoniaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has compound leaves with opposite arrangement and produces elongated, woody pods that contain flat, winged seeds. Tecoma africana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its adaptability to various soil types makes it a resilient plant in arid and semi-arid regions.
