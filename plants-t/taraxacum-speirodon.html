Taraxacum speirodon

Taraxacum speirodon, commonly known as the narrowleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and a hollow, leafless stem that supports a single flower head. The flower head consists of yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular florets, which develop into the familiar white, fluffy seed heads. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in herbal remedies.
