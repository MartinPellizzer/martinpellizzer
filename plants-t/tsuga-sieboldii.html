Tsuga sieboldii

Tsuga sieboldii, commonly known as the Japanese fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Pinaceae and is characterized by its conical shape and needle-like leaves. The tree produces cones that are typically reddish-brown and contain seeds surrounded by a wing for wind dispersal. Its foliage is dense and provides habitat for various wildlife species.

Tsuga sieboldii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
