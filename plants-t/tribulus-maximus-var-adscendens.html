Tribulus maximus var. adscendens

Tribulus maximus var. adscendens, commonly known as the climbing tribulus, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems that climb or trail along the ground, often using tendrils to attach to supports. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by distinctive, spiny, three-lobed fruits. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.
