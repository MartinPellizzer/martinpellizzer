Tulipa praecox var. hexagonata

Tulipa praecox var. hexagonata, commonly known as the Early Tulip, is a species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its early blooming habit, typically flowering in late winter or early spring. The plant features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep red, often with a distinctive purple or red streak on the outer petals. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces bulbous roots that store nutrients.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
