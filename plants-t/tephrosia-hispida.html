Tephrosia hispida

Tephrosia hispida, commonly known as rough tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height, featuring compound leaves with small, stiff hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the rainy season. Its root system is known for containing compounds used in traditional medicine.

Tephrosia hispida is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in agricultural settings.
