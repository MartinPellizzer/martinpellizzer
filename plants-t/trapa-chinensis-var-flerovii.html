Trapa chinensis var. flerovii

Trapa chinensis var. flerovii, commonly known as the Chinese water chestnut, is a perennial aquatic plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive floating leaves and spiny, nut-like fruits that develop underwater. The plant thrives in shallow, freshwater habitats such as ponds and marshes. Its leaves are broad and rounded, with a waxy surface that helps repel water.

The seeds, which are edible, are encased in a hard, spiny shell and are often harvested for culinary use.
