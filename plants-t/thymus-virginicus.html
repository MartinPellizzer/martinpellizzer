Thymus virginicus

Thymus virginicus, commonly known as wild thyme, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features small, fragrant leaves and blooms with pink or purple flowers in the spring. The plant is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.

Botanically, it belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many other aromatic herbs.
