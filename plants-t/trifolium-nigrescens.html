Trifolium nigrescens

Trifolium nigrescens, commonly known as the black clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets, and its small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grasslands. It has a deep root system that helps in nitrogen fixation, making it beneficial for soil fertility.

Trifolium nigrescens is also known for its use in traditional herbal medicine.
