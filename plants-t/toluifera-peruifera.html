Toluifera peruifera

Toluifera peruifera, commonly known as the Peruvian tolu tree, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, gray bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring, followed by the development of spherical, woody fruits. Its resin, known as tolu balsam, has been historically used for medicinal and aromatic purposes.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.
