Thaspium aureum var. trifoliatum

Thaspium aureum var. trifoliatum, commonly known as the golden thaspium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged symmetrically. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive species within the Apiaceae family.
