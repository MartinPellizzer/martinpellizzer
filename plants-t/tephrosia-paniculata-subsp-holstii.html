Tephrosia paniculata subsp. holstii

Tephrosia paniculata subsp.

holstii, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a perennial shrub native to tropical Africa. It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and opposite, pinnate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in panicles, which attract various pollinators. Its seeds are enclosed in hairy, pod-like fruits that split open when mature. Tephrosia paniculata subsp.

holstii is also noted for its medicinal properties and use in traditional herbal remedies.
