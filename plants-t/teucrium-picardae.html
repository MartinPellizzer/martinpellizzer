Teucrium picardae

Teucrium picardae, commonly known as Picard's germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pale blue to purple and bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Teucrium picardae is often found in dry, sunny habitats like rocky slopes and gravelly soils.
