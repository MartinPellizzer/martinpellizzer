Thladiantha cordifolia var. tomentosa

Thladiantha cordifolia var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly heartleaf crape myrtle, is a species native to China.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense, woolly pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its unique foliage and showy blooms make it a distinctive addition to botanical collections.
