Trisetum sesquitertium

Trisetum sesquitertium, commonly known as the slender reed grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense colonies, contributing to soil stabilization.

Botanically, it is characterized by its distinct floral structure and adaptability to wetland environments.
