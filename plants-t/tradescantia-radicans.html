Tradescantia radicans

Tradescantia radicans, commonly known as the spiderwort or wandering jew, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a low-growing, rhizomatous plant that spreads horizontally along the ground, forming dense mats. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and often have a waxy, bluish-green appearance. The plant produces small, three-petaled flowers in shades of purple, pink, or white, which bloom in the summer.

It is commonly cultivated as a ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
