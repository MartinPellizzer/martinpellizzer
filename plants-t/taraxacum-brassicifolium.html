Taraxacum brassicifolium

Taraxacum brassicifolium, commonly known as the brassica-leaved dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and a yellow composite flower head. The plant produces a distinctive seed head composed of numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and can often be found in meadows, roadsides, and gardens.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.
