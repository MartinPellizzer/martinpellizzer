Tragia kirkiana

Tragia kirkiana, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while the plant is often hairy along the stems and leaves.

Tragia kirkiana is adapted to arid environments and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.
