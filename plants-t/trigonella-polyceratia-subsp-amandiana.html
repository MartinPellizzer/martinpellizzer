Trigonella polyceratia subsp. amandiana

Trigonella polyceratia subsp.

amandiana, commonly known as the Armenian fennel, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region. It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellowish flowers arranged in umbels.

The plant produces oval, greenish-brown seeds that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptability to harsh conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and ethnobotanical studies.
