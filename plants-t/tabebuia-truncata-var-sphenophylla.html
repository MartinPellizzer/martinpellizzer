Tabebuia truncata var. sphenophylla

Tabebuia truncata var. sphenophylla, commonly known as the yellow trumpet tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange tubular flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The tree has a distinctive trunk with a rough, flaky bark that peels away in thin layers. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and elliptical, often with a waxy texture.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in urban landscapes for its vibrant floral display.
