Thymus kosteleckyanus

Thymus kosteleckyanus, commonly known as Kostelecky's thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic leaves and square stems. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are narrow and fragrant, often used in traditional herbal remedies.

Thymus kosteleckyanus thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.
