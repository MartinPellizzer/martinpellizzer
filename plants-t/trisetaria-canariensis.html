Trisetaria canariensis

Trisetaria canariensis, commonly known as the Canary Islands grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, pale green to brownish spikelets that are clustered at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Trisetaria canariensis plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
