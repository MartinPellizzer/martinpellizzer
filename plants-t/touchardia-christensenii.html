Touchardia christensenii

Touchardia christensenii, commonly known as the Himalayan giant gentian, is a striking perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, robust stems that can reach heights of up to 3 meters, making it one of the tallest gentians in the world. The plant produces large, showy blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, adapted to the cold and harsh mountain environment.

Touchardia christensenii is valued for its ornamental beauty and plays a significant role in the ecological balance of its native habitat.
