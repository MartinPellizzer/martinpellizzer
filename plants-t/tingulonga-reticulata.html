Tingulonga reticulata

Tingulonga reticulata, commonly known as the netted bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. The plant has a sprawling growth habit with hairy stems and leaves that are often lobed or toothed. Its flowers bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant and unique appearance.
