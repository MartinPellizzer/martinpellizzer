Tragacantha echinops

Tragacantha echinops, commonly known as the thorned globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny, spherical stems and yellow or orange flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces a milky sap, which is a characteristic feature of the genus Tragacantha. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well adapted to harsh desert environments.

Its botanical features make it an important plant in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
