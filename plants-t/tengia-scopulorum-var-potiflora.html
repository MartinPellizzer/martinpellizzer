Tengia scopulorum var. potiflora

Tengia scopulorum var. potiflora, commonly known as the rock hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and large, showy flowers that typically range in color from pink to red. The plant thrives in rocky or coastal environments, often growing in well-drained soil. Its leaves are broad and lobed, and it is known for its ability to tolerate salt spray and strong winds.

This variant is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in coastal gardens.
