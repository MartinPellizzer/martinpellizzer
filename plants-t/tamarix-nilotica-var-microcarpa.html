Tamarix nilotica var. microcarpa

Tamarix nilotica var. microcarpa, commonly known as the small-fruited tamarisk, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, the Middle East, and Southwestern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, feathery foliage and small, scale-like leaves that reduce water loss, making it highly adapted to dry environments. The plant produces small, pink or reddish flowers that bloom in summer, followed by woody, cone-like fruits containing numerous tiny seeds. It is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental value, though it can become invasive in certain regions.

Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in poor soils and withstand extreme temperature fluctuations.
