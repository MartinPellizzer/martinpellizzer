Tragacantha dipodura

Tragacantha dipodura, commonly known as the horned poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, branched stems and striking, orange-red flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
