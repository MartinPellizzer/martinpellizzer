Turnera hebepetala

Turnera hebepetala, commonly known as the "Mexican buttercup," is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that produces showy yellow flowers with a distinctive cup-like structure. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable in its native habitats.
