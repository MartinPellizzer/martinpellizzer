Thalictrum babingtonii

Thalictrum babingtonii, commonly known as Babington's meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and delicate, fern-like compound leaves. The plant produces showy, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, which are clustered in loose panicles. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Thalictrum babingtonii is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
