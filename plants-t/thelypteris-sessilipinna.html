Thelypteris sessilipinna

Thelypteris sessilipinna, commonly known as the broad-leaf holly fern, is a species of fern native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, leathery, evergreen leaves that remain on the plant throughout the year. The fronds are typically bipinnate, with a distinctive, upright growth habit. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

The plant's botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and hardiness.
