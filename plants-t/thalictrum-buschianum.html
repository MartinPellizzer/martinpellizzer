Thalictrum buschianum

Thalictrum buschianum, commonly known as the Busch's meadow rue, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Thalictrum buschianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
