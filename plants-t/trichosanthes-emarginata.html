Trichosanthes emarginata

Trichosanthes emarginata, commonly known as the Chinese flame vine, is a climbing plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, orange-yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces elongated, warty fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Trichosanthes emarginata is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
