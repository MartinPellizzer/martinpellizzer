Thymus praecox subsp. ligusticus

Thymus praecox subsp.

ligusticus, commonly known as the Alpine thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It features small, aromatic leaves and produces pink or purple flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and slopes. Its compact growth habit and fragrant foliage make it a popular choice for rock gardens and herbal use.

It is also valued for its medicinal properties and ability to attract pollinators.
