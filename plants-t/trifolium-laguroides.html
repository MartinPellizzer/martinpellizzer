Trifolium laguroides

Trifolium laguroides, commonly known as the Laguroides clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Trifolium laguroides is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
