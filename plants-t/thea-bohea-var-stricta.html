Thea bohea var. stricta

Thea bohea var. stricta, commonly known as the Chinese tea plant, is a species of evergreen shrub native to southern China.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is cultivated for its leaves, which are processed into various types of tea, including black and oolong tea. Its botanical structure includes a woody stem with alternate, serrated leaves.

The plant prefers a warm, humid climate and well-drained soil for optimal growth.
