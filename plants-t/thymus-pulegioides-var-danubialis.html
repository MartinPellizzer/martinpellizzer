Thymus pulegioides var. danubialis

Thymus pulegioides var. danubialis, commonly known as Danube thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Central Europe.

It features small, dark green leaves that are slightly hairy and emit a strong, aromatic scent. The plant produces dense clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic properties make it valuable in both culinary and medicinal applications.
