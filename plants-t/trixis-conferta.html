Trixis conferta

Trixis conferta, commonly known as the desert thistle, is a perennial shrub native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny, woolly stems and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has adapted to harsh environments through its deep root system and water-storing leaves. Its foliage is often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which helps reduce water loss.

Trixis conferta plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
