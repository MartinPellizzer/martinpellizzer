Theodorea griffithii

Theodorea griffithii, commonly known as the Kashmir saffron, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers. The plant produces a unique spice known as saffron, which is harvested from its stigmas. It thrives in high-altitude regions with cool, moist climates.

Theodorea griffithii is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its economic importance in traditional medicine and culinary uses.
