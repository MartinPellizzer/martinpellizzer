Thymus lanceolatus

Thymus lanceolatus, commonly known as narrowleaf thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features small, lance-shaped leaves that are aromatic and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its medicinal properties have been historically valued for their antiseptic and aromatic qualities.
