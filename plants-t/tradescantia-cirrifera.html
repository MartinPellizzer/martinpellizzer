Tradescantia cirrifera

Tradescantia cirrifera, commonly known as the featherleaf spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly hairy texture, while its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

Tradescantia cirrifera is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
