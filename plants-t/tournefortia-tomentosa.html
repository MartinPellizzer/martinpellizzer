Tournefortia tomentosa

Tournefortia tomentosa, commonly known as the silky tournefortia, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish-green leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. It is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and floral display.

The species is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
