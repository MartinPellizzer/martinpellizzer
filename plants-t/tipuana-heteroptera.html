Tipuana heteroptera

Tipuana heteroptera, commonly known as the Chilean tipuana, is a fast-growing deciduous tree native to Chile.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant has a shallow root system, making it adaptable to various soil types but sensitive to frost. It is often used in landscaping for its rapid growth and ornamental value.

Its seeds are encased in pods and can be dispersed by wind, contributing to its spread in natural and cultivated environments.
