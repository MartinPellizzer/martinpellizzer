Thunbergia cynanchifolia

Thunbergia cynanchifolia, commonly known as the blue bell vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped, dark green leaves. The plant produces striking blue or violet tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Thunbergia cynanchifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative climbing plant.
