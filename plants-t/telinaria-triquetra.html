Telinaria triquetra

Telinaria triquetra, commonly known as the three-angled thyme, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features small, fragrant flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a distinctive three-angled cross-section.

Telinaria triquetra is often found in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade.
