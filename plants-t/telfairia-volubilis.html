Telfairia volubilis

Telfairia volubilis, commonly known as African cucumber or false cucumber, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It produces elongated, edible fruits that resemble cucumbers in appearance and have a mild, slightly sweet flavor. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and yellow flowers, which are typical of the Cucurbitaceae family. Telfairia volubilis is often cultivated for its nutritional value, as the fruit is rich in vitamins and minerals.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
