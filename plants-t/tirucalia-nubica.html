Tirucalia nubica

Tirucalia nubica, commonly known as the Indian hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom throughout the year in tropical climates. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, and it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in warm regions.
