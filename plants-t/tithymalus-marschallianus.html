Tithymalus marschallianus

Tithymalus marschallianus, commonly known as the marsh thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to wetland and marshy habitats in Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in areas with standing water.

Tithymalus marschallianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
