Tradescantia virginica var. glabra

Tradescantia virginica var. glabra, commonly known as spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, glabrous (smooth) leaves and vibrant blue or purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks. It is a perennial herb that spreads through rhizomes, forming dense colonies.

Spiderwort is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
