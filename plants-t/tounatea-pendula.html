Tounatea pendula

Tounatea pendula, commonly known as the hanging bellflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous, bell-shaped flowers that hang from the stems, often in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.
