Trachypogon montufarii

Trachypogon montufarii, commonly known as the Andean feather grass, is a perennial grass species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, silky hairs that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it forms dense mats or low-growing clumps. Its inflorescence consists of a narrow, cylindrical spikelet with small, spike-like flowers.

Trachypogon montufarii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its ability to thrive in harsh, arid conditions.
