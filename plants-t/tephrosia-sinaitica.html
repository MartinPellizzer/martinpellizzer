Tephrosia sinaitica

Tephrosia sinaitica, commonly known as Sinai tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and North Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climates. Its root system helps in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.

Tephrosia sinaitica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
