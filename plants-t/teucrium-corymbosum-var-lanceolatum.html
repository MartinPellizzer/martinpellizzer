Teucrium corymbosum var. lanceolatum

Teucrium corymbosum var. lanceolatum, commonly known as blue curls, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, blue-gray pubescence. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers in dense, curling clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
