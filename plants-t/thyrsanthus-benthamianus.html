Thyrsanthus benthamianus

Thyrsanthus benthamianus, commonly known as the New Zealand thorned everlasting, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, bushy growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in compact clusters called heads. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Thyrsanthus benthamianus is often found in coastal and lowland areas, thriving in well-drained soils.
