Taraxacum puberulum

Taraxacum puberulum, commonly known as the hairy dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance compared to other dandelion species. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that develop into fluffy white seed heads, typical of the genus Taraxacum. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and disturbed soils.

The hairy dandelion is noted for its adaptability and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
