Tylophora asthmatica

Tylophora asthmatica, commonly known as the asthma vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is known for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional systems of medicine for treating respiratory conditions.

However, it contains toxic alkaloids and should be handled with care.
