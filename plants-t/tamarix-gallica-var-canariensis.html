Tamarix gallica var. canariensis

Tamarix gallica var. canariensis, commonly known as the Canary rose-tamarisk, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its small, pink or reddish flowers that bloom in dense clusters, often resembling roses. The plant has feathery, deciduous leaves that turn red in the autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil make it a notable species in its native habitat.
