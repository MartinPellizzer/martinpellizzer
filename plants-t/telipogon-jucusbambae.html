Telipogon jucusbambae

Telipogon jucusbambae, commonly known as the Bambai orchid, is a rare and endangered flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Borneo.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pendulous flowers that are typically pale green or yellowish in color. The plant grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to the trunks and branches of trees without deriving nutrients from them. Its small, strap-like leaves are adapted to absorb moisture from the humid air, and it relies on specific pollinators for reproduction.

Due to habitat loss and deforestation, Telipogon jucusbambae is critically endangered and requires conservation efforts to survive.
