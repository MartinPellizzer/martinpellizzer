Taraxacum longicuspis

Taraxacum longicuspis, commonly known as the long-spined dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated bracts that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is found in various regions across North America. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in early spring, followed by the formation of its characteristic seed heads.

The species is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its resilience in different environmental conditions.
