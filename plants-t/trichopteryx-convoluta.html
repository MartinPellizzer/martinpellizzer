Trichopteryx convoluta

Trichopteryx convoluta, commonly known as the curled-leaved tamarisk, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, scale-like leaves that are often curled or rolled. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. Its root system is extensive and deep, allowing it to thrive in poor, sandy soils.

Trichopteryx convoluta is adapted to withstand drought and extreme temperatures, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.
