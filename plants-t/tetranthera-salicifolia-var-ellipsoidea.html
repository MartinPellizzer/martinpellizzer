Tetranthera salicifolia var. ellipsoidea

Tetranthera salicifolia var. ellipsoidea, commonly known as the elliptic willow-leaf fern, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features slender, wiry stems that produce small, clustered, lanceolate leaves with a distinctively willow-like appearance. The plant has delicate, pale green foliage that is often covered in fine, silky hairs. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value is appreciated in horticulture for its elegant, fern-like structure.
