Trifolium campestre var. minus

Trifolium campestre var. minus, commonly known as lesser white clover, is a flowering plant species belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its small, trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, often with a slight reddish or white tinge. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that are clustered in dense heads, typically blooming in early spring. It is a low-growing perennial herb that thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed areas.

This variety is notable for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility in agricultural settings.
