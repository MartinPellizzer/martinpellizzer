Trichophyllum interstinctum

Trichophyllum interstinctum, commonly known as the ghost plant, is a unique carnivorous plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It possesses specialized structures called pitcher-shaped leaves that trap and digest insects, providing it with essential nutrients. The plant's appearance is striking, with translucent, waxy leaves that give it a ghostly appearance, hence its common name. It thrives in moist, nutrient-poor environments and requires consistent humidity for optimal growth.

Despite its eerie look, it plays a vital role in its ecosystem by controlling insect populations.
