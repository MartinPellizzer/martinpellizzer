Thymus richardii subsp. richardii

Thymus richardii subsp.

richardii, commonly known as Sierra Valley thyme, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its aromatic, small, gray-green leaves that are densely covered with fine hairs.

The plant produces small, pink to lavender flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its compact growth habit and fragrant foliage make it a valued plant in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
