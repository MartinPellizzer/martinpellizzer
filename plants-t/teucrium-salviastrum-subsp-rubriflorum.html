Teucrium salviastrum subsp. rubriflorum

Teucrium salviastrum subsp.

rubriflorum, commonly known as the redflower germander, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant produces showy, violet to red flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
