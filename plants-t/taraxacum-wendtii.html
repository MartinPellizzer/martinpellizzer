Taraxacum wendtii

Taraxacum wendtii, commonly known as Wendt's dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces a hollow, hairless stem that bears a single flower head, which develops into a distinctive seed head with pappus. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
