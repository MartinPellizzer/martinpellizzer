Tacsonia quitensis var. eriantha

Tacsonia quitensis var. eriantha, commonly known as the Andean bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to the cool, temperate climates of its native habitat. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, providing a distinctive appearance.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in cooler regions.
