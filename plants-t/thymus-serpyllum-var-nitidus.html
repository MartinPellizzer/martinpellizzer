Thymus serpyllum var. nitidus

Thymus serpyllum var. nitidus, commonly known as narrow-leaved thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features aromatic, small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers in dense clusters during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and fragrant foliage make it both ornamental and useful in culinary applications.
