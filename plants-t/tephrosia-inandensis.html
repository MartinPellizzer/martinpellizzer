Tephrosia inandensis

Tephrosia inandensis, commonly known as the African senna, is a leguminous shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces pods containing flat, kidney-shaped seeds. It is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Tephrosia inandensis is also valued for its potential medicinal properties and use in traditional herbal remedies.
