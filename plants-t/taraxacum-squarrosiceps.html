Taraxacum squarrosiceps

Taraxacum squarrosiceps, commonly known as the roughleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in early spring. Native to the western United States, this species thrives in dry, disturbed soils and open habitats. Its leaves are often lobed and have a distinctive, bristly texture.

The plant is often considered a weed but has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
