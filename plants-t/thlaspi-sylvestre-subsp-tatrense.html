Thlaspi sylvestre subsp. tatrense

Thlaspi sylvestre subsp.

tatrense, commonly known as the alpine cabbages, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family. It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and is often found in alpine and subalpine regions.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically fleshy and succulent, adapted to retain moisture in harsh environments.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soils in mountainous areas.
