Trichilia carinata

Trichilia carinata, commonly known as the African sandalwood, is a small to medium-sized tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae, which includes species known for their aromatic wood. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, and the tree is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Trichilia carinata is valued for its fragrant timber and has traditional uses in medicine and as a source of essential oils.
