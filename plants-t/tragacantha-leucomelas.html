Tragacantha leucomelas

Tragacantha leucomelas, commonly known as the white-flowered tragacanth, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces a gum resin, known as tragacanth, which is harvested from its stems and used in various industrial applications. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Tragacantha leucomelas is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its economic importance as a source of natural gum.
