Taxotrophis rectinervis

Taxotrophis rectinervis, commonly known as the straight-veined water plant, is a species of aquatic plant characterized by its delicate, ribbon-like leaves.

It typically grows in shallow, slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and streams. The plant has a simple, unbranched stem with opposite, linear leaves that are often slightly curled. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually appearing in clusters near the water surface.

Taxotrophis rectinervis plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small invertebrates and contributing to water oxygenation.
