Tanacetum paropamisicum

Tanacetum paropamisicum, commonly known as the Pamir wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic properties. The plant is known for its finely divided, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish flower heads that bloom in summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, mountainous climates.

Tanacetum paropamisicum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
