Thymus longicaulis var. ocheus

Thymus longicaulis var. ocheus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, aromatic, gray-green leaves that are typically narrow and elongated. The plant produces tiny, pinkish-white flowers in dense clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and borders.

Its medicinal and culinary uses are similar to other thyme species, with a strong, pungent aroma.
