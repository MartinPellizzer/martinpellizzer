Taraxacum biformatum

Taraxacum biformatum, commonly known as the two-lobed dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the sunflower family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated, hairy leaves and a hollow, ridged stem that supports a single flower head. The flower head consists of both ray and disk flowers, which bloom in spring and early summer. This plant is native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in grassy areas and disturbed soils.

It is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
