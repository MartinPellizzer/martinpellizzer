Typha remotiuscula

Typha remotiuscula, commonly known as the narrowleaf cattail, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, blade-like leaves and a cylindrical inflorescence that matures into a brown, mat-like structure. The plant thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often forming dense stands in marshes and pond edges. Its rhizomes are extensive, allowing it to spread rapidly and dominate wetland ecosystems.

Typha remotiuscula plays a vital role in water filtration and provides habitat for various aquatic organisms.
