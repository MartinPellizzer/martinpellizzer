Taraxacum alsaticum

Taraxacum alsaticum, commonly known as the common dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of stiff, hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and a hollow, hairy stem that supports a single, bright yellow flower head. The flower head consists of numerous small, petal-less florets that mature into a distinctive spherical seed head known as a "puffball.". The plant is native to Europe and has been naturalized in many parts of the world, often found in meadows, roadsides, and disturbed soils.

It is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional remedies for various ailments.
