Tephrosia pumila var. aldabrensis

Tephrosia pumila var. aldabrensis, commonly known as Aldabra tephrosia, is a small, herbaceous shrub native to the Aldabra Atoll in the Seychelles.

It features slender, branching stems with opposite, ovate leaves that are usually covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the rainy season. Its root system is adapted to thrive in sandy, well-drained soils typical of its coastal habitat.

This variety is notable for its resilience to salt spray and drought conditions.
