Tephrosia falciformis

Tephrosia falciformis, commonly known as the sickleleaf tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its pinnately compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or pea family, and is known for its nitrogen-fixing ability through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules.

Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine, and the plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential in ecological restoration projects.
