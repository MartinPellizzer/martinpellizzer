Themelium tenuisectum

Themelium tenuisectum, commonly known as the slender leaf fern, is a rare and delicate plant native to the humid forests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, thread-like leaves that give it a lace-like appearance. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and requires high humidity to flourish. Its reproductive structures are small and inconspicuous, typically producing spores rather than seeds.

Due to its fragile nature and limited distribution, Themelium tenuisectum is considered a vulnerable species in its natural habitat.
