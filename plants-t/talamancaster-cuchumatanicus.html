Talamancaster cuchumatanicus

Talamancaster cuchumatanicus, commonly known as the Guanacaste tree, is a large evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and long, pendulous clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The tree produces large, woody fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind and animals. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental and reforestation purposes.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
