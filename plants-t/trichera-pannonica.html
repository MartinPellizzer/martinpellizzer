Trichera pannonica

Trichera pannonica, commonly known as the Pannonian trichera, is a perennial herb native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the family. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
