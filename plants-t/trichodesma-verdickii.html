Trichodesma verdickii

Trichodesma verdickii, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and abandoned fields.

Trichodesma verdickii is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
