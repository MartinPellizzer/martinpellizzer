Thalictrum mexicanum

Thalictrum mexicanum, commonly known as Mexican meadow rue, is a perennial plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the family Ranunculaceae and is characterized by its slender stems and delicate, compound leaves. The plant produces small, white or greenish-yellow flowers arranged in a raceme, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Thalictrum mexicanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
