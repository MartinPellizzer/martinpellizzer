Trigonotis brevipes f. coronata

Trigonotis brevipes f. coronata, commonly known as the crowned trigonotis, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in late summer. The plant features dark green, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are usually blue to violet in color, with a distinctive crown-like structure at the base of each bloom.

This ornamental plant is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance and ease of care.
