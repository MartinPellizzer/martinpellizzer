Tocoyena megistantha

Tocoyena megistantha, commonly known as the giant tocoyena, is a striking member of the Arecaceae family, native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, clustered inflorescences that bear showy, white to yellow flowers, which are often fragrant. The plant typically grows as a tall, slender palm with a robust stem and can reach heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are long, pinnately compound, and provide a lush, green canopy.

Tocoyena megistantha is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in tropical gardens and botanical collections.
