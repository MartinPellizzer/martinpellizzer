Tinus hostmannii

Tinus hostmannii, commonly known as the Hostmann's tinus, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is a shrub-like plant native to southern Africa, particularly found in Namibia and South Africa. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine, silky hairs. Its flowers are typically clustered in inflorescences and range in color from white to pale pink.

Tinus hostmannii is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
