Trevoa campanulata

Trevoa campanulata, commonly known as the bell-shaped trevoa, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal or rocky habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

Trevoa campanulata plays an important ecological role in its native environment, providing nectar for native pollinators.
