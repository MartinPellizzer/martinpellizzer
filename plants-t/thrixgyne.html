Thrixgyne

Thrixgyne, commonly known as the silky mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of eucalyptus trees in Australia.

The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, fleshy berries that are a food source for native birds. Thrixgyne relies on its host tree for water and nutrients, while also providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
