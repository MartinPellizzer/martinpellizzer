Tonina fluviatilis

Tonina fluviatilis, commonly known as the river tonina, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the wet season. It thrives in moist, sandy soils near rivers and streams.

Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often found in riparian habitats.
