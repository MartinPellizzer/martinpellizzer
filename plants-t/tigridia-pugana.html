Tigridia pugana

Tigridia pugana, commonly known as the Mexican fairy lantern, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that resemble small lanterns. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its blooms, which are usually red or orange, are borne on tall, slender stems.

Tigridia pugana is also known for its ability to produce a large number of seeds, making it a hardy and resilient species.
