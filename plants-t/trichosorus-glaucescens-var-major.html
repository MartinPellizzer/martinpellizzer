Trichosorus glaucescens var. major

Trichosorus glaucescens var. major, commonly known as the silverleaf thistle, is a perennial plant characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and spiny leaves.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is native to regions of Europe and parts of Asia. The plant produces purple or blue flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs that give it a distinctive, almost metallic appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
