Tetragonia borealis

Tetragonia borealis, commonly known as the northern purslane, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is a low-growing annual herb with succulent, fleshy leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to cold, harsh environments. Its leaves are often used in traditional cuisines for their mild, tangy flavor.

Tetragonia borealis plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats and is of interest to botanists studying plant adaptation to extreme climates.
