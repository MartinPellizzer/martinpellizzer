Tilia rubra var. dasystyla

Tilia rubra var. dasystyla, commonly known as the red-leaf linden, is a deciduous tree native to parts of eastern North America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that turn a striking red or bronze color in the fall. The tree produces small, fragrant yellow flowers in early summer, which are clustered in drooping inflorescences. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in gardens for its aesthetic foliage and floral display.
