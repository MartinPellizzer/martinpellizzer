Thecacoris reticulata

Thecacoris reticulata, commonly known as the reticulated cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a dense layer of white, powdery bloom. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom during the rainy season, typically from late winter to early spring. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it stores water in its thick, fleshy stems to survive in arid environments.

Thecacoris reticulata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
