Thlaspi rotundifolium f. cepaeifolium

Thlaspi rotundifolium f. cepaeifolium, commonly known as the cabbage leaf rockcress, is a variant of the common rockcress.

It is a low-growing perennial plant with basal rosettes of rounded, fleshy leaves. The leaves are typically broader and more heart-shaped compared to the typical form, giving it a distinct appearance. It produces small, yellow flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months.

This plant is often found in rocky or gravelly soils in alpine and arctic regions.
