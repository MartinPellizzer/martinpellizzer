Trisetum virletii

Trisetum virletii, commonly known as the slender feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and slender, arching stems that produce delicate, feathery seed heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. It has narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue and is often found in temperate climates.

Trisetum virletii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
