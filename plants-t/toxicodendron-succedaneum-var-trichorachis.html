Toxicodendron succedaneum var. trichorachis

Toxicodendron succedaneum var. trichorachis, commonly known as the staghorn sumac, is a shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers followed by red, fuzzy berries. It is known for its toxic sap, which can cause a severe skin reaction known as contact dermatitis.

This variety is often found in dry, open areas and is part of the Anacardiaceae family.
