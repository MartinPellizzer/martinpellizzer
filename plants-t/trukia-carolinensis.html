Trukia carolinensis

Trukia carolinensis, commonly known as the Carolina false indigo, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and spikes of blue to purple flowers. The plant has a deep taproot system, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun. It is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Trukia carolinensis is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and wildlife.
