Thymus serpyllum var. villosus

Thymus serpyllum var. villosus, commonly known as hairy thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, fragrant leaves covered in fine, hairy texture, which gives it its common name. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pink to lavender flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic properties make it popular in culinary and herbal applications.
