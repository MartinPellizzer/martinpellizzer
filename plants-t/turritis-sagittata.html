Turritis sagittata

Turritis sagittata, commonly known as the arrowhead plant, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its arrow-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. It is a perennial herb that spreads through rhizomes, forming dense colonies.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
