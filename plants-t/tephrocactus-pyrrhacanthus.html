Tephrocactus pyrrhacanthus

Tephrocactus pyrrhacanthus, commonly known as the red-spined cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is a columnar cactus with a distinctive red or orange spines that contrast with its greenish-gray stems. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are typically short-lived but attract pollinators like bees and birds. It is adapted to survive in hot, dry environments with minimal water requirements.

Its unique appearance and resilience make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
