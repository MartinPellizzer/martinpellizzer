Thymus barrelieri

Thymus barrelieri, commonly known as the Barrellier thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, woody stems and small, aromatic leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Thymus barrelieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
