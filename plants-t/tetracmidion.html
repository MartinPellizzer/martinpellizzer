Tetracmidion

Tetracmidion, commonly known as the ghost plant, is a unique member of the family Tetracmidionaceae.

It is a small, delicate plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine, silvery hairs. Its flowers are inconspicuous, consisting of small, greenish structures that lack showy petals.

Tetracmidion is primarily found in temperate regions and is often associated with wetlands and rocky habitats.
