Taraxacum necessarium

Taraxacum necessarium, commonly known as the greater dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of spoon-shaped, toothed leaves and a hollow, leafless stem that terminates in a flowering head. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that develop into wind-dispersed seeds with parachute-like structures. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas such as lawns and roadsides.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its diuretic and detoxifying effects.
