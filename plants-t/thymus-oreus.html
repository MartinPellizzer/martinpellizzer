Thymus oreus

Thymus oreus, commonly known as the Greek thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. Thymus oreus is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun.

Its aromatic leaves are often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.
