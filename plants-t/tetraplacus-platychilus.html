Tetraplacus platychilus

Tetraplacus platychilus, commonly known as the broad-leaved tetraplacus, is a unique flowering plant native to the humid lowland forests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with four petals arranged in a symmetrical pattern. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found growing near the forest floor.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
