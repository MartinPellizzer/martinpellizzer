Talinum polygaloides

Talinum polygaloides, commonly known as red-rooted morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking red or purple flowers that bloom in the morning and close in the afternoon. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, fleshy berries that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Talinum polygaloides is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
