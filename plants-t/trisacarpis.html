Trisacarpis

Trisacarpis, commonly known as the sacred fig, is a tropical plant belonging to the Moraceae family.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish flowers that develop into edible fruits. The plant is notable for its unique structure, with three carpels forming the fruit's core. Trisacarpis thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in rainforests.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and culturally revered in many regions.
