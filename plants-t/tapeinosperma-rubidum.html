Tapeinosperma rubidum

Tapeinosperma rubidum, commonly known as the red-seed acacia, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems and compound leaves that are typically small and feathery. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by distinctive, reddish-brown pods containing seeds. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability.

Its botanical features make it an important species in local ecosystems and for traditional uses.
