Tradescantia umbelifera

Tradescantia umbelifera, commonly known as the spiderwort or redstem spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a slight reddish tinge, especially at the base. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, ditches, and along stream banks.

This plant is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it both a common weed and a valuable component of wetland ecosystems.
