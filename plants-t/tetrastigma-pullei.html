Tetrastigma pullei

Tetrastigma pullei, commonly known as the Chinese crossvine, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its twining stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruits are berries that turn from green to red when mature.

Tetrastigma pullei is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
