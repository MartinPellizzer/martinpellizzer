Tsingya bemarana

Tsingya bemarana, commonly known as the Madagascar star orchid, is a striking orchid species native to the island of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its unique, star-shaped flowers that typically display vibrant colors such as red, orange, and yellow. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its intricate floral structure, which includes a prominent lip that aids in pollination. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on trees or rocks in the rainforests of Madagascar.

Due to habitat loss, Tsingya bemarana is considered a rare and endangered species.
