Trichilia emetica subsp. emetica

Trichilia emetica subsp.

emetica, commonly known as the African snowball or African snowball tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa. It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter.

The plant produces fragrant, white, clustered flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, round fruits containing hard seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.

The species is relatively drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for ornamental landscaping in warm climates.
