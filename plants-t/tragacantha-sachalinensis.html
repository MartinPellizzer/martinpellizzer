Tragacantha sachalinensis

Tragacantha sachalinensis, commonly known as the Sakhalin tragacanth, is a perennial herb native to the Far East, particularly found in regions of Russia and Japan.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces a viscous gum, which is harvested from its stems and has been used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive.

It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in forested areas or along riverbanks.
