Tanacetum corymbosum subsp. cinereum

Tanacetum corymbosum subsp.

cinereum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America. It is characterized by its finely divided, silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its aromatic properties. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in ornamental gardening for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
