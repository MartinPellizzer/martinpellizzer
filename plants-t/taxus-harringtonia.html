Taxus harringtonia

Taxus harringtonia, commonly known as the Harrington's yew, is a slow-growing evergreen conifer native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and dark green, needle-like leaves that are arranged in spirals around the stems. The plant produces small, reddish-purple berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Taxus harringtonia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.

Due to its slow growth and specific habitat requirements, it is relatively rare in the wild.
