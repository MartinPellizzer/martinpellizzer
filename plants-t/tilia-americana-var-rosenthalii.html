Tilia americana var. rosenthalii

Tilia americana var. rosenthalii, commonly known as the Rosenthal's linden, is a rare variant of the American linden tree.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often slightly serrated along the edges. The flowers are fragrant and yellowish-green, appearing in clusters during the summer months. This variety is native to specific regions of the eastern United States.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in botanical studies.
