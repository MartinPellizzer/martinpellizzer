Terminalia gigantea

Terminalia gigantea, commonly known as the African sandalwood, is a large deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and long, slender leaves. The tree produces small, pale yellow flowers that are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing flat, seed-like structures. It is valued for its aromatic wood, which is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
