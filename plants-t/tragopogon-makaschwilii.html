Tragopogon makaschwilii

Tragopogon makaschwilii, commonly known as the Makaschwilii thistle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is adapted to harsh, high-altitude environments. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive purple center.

Tragopogon makaschwilii is often found in alpine meadows and is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological niche.
