Tetragonia erecta

Tetragonia erecta, commonly known as the New Zealand spinach, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its succulent, edible leaves that are often used in cooking. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and can thrive in sandy or gravelly soils. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, followed by round, fleshy fruits.

Tetragonia erecta is valued not only for its culinary uses but also for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh environments.
