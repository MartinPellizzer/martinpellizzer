Tilia floridana var. alabamensis

Tilia floridana var. alabamensis, commonly known as the Alabama linden, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Tiliaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. The tree produces small, round fruits that contain three-winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its bark is smooth and light gray, becoming fissured with age, and it typically grows to a height of 15 to 30 feet.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in the region.
