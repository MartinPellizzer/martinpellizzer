Tontelea nectandrifolia

Tontelea nectandrifolia, commonly known as the fragrant tontelea, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its flowers emit a sweet scent, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Tontelea nectandrifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.
