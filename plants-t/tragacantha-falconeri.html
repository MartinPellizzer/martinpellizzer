Tragacantha falconeri

Tragacantha falconeri, commonly known as the Canary Island tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant produces a resinous sap, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a thickening agent. It thrives in arid and rocky environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
