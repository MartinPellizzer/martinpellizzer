Triticum Hemipoa

Triticum hemipoa is a lesser-known wild relative of wheat that thrives in temperate climates. This grass species is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, showcasing its adaptability to various environments. It has been studied for its potential in genetic research due to its unique genome structure. Despite its modest appearance, it holds significance in understanding wheat evolution and crop resilience. Its role in agricultural science remains an area of growing interest among researchers.
