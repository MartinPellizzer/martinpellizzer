Teesdalia nudicaulis f. foliosa

Teesdalia nudicaulis f. foliosa, commonly known as the broadleaf dandelion, is a variant of the common dandelion with distinctive broad, lobed leaves.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The leaves are usually basal, forming a rosette, and are characterized by their serrated margins and broad, fleshy texture. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by the characteristic seed heads that disperse via the wind.

This plant is often found in disturbed soils and is commonly seen in gardens and along roadsides.
