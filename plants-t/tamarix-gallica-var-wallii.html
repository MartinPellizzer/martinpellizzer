Tamarix gallica var. wallii

Tamarix gallica var. wallii, commonly known as the Chinese tamarisk, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It features slender, often spiny branches and small, scale-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces pink to pale purple flowers in panicles during the summer months. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing tolerance to drought and poor soil conditions.

Its rapid growth and salt tolerance make it a common species in coastal and inland saline areas.
