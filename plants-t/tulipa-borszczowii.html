Tulipa borszczowii

Tulipa borszczowii, commonly known as the Borszczowii tulip, is a perennial bulbous plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange and red. The plant typically grows to a height of 15 to 30 centimeters, with narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

The flowers bloom in late spring, adding a striking display to its natural habitat.
