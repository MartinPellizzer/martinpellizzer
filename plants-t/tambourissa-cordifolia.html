Tambourissa cordifolia

Tambourissa cordifolia, commonly known as the heart-shaped morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink, purple, and white. The plant belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.
