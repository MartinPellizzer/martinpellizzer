Taraxacum explicatum

Taraxacum explicatum, commonly known as the Mexican dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its bright yellow ray florets and a distinctive, hollow, hairless stem. The plant produces a spherical seed head composed of numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in open, sunny areas.

Taraxacum explicatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
