Tradescantia fluminensis f. albovittata

Tradescantia fluminensis f. albovittata, commonly known as the silver sword or white-striped spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a succulent perennial that features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive silvery-white stripe running along the midrib. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in shades of pink or white, typically in late spring to early summer. It is often grown as a ground cover due to its ability to spread rapidly through stolons.

This variety is valued for its ornamental foliage and adaptability to a range of growing conditions.
