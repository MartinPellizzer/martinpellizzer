Teucrium compositum

Teucrium compositum, commonly known as creeping germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, prostrate stems that grow close to the ground, often forming dense mats. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, arranged in clusters. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a strong, aromatic scent.

Teucrium compositum is known for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
