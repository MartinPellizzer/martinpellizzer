Thelypteris makassarica

Thelypteris makassarica, commonly known as the Makassar holly fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Thelypteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its sporangia are clustered in sori on the underside of the fronds, a common feature in ferns.

Thelypteris makassarica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
