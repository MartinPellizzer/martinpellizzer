Teucrium gnaphalodes var. illerdense

Teucrium gnaphalodes var. illerdense, commonly known as the Iller Valley germander, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments. Its small, tubular flowers are usually purple or violet, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the branches.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
