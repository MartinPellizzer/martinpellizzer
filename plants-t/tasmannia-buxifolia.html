Tasmannia buxifolia

Tasmannia buxifolia, commonly known as the waxleaf tasmannia, is a small evergreen shrub native to eastern Australia.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are typically followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rainforest or coastal scrub habitats.

Tasmannia buxifolia is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage.
