Tragacantha orbiculata

Tragacantha orbiculata, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive orbicular leaves and produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well-adapted to harsh climates. Its sap, which is viscous and resinous, has been traditionally used in various medicinal and industrial applications.

The species is also valued for its role in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to prevent soil erosion.
