Thunbergia ilocana

Thunbergia ilocana, commonly known as the Ilocano Morning Glory, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of blue and purple. The plant is a climbing vine that thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

The flowers typically open in the morning and close in the late afternoon, giving it its common name.
