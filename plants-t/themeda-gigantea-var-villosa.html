Themeda gigantea var. villosa

Themeda gigantea var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy grass or velvet grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and hairy, bluish-green foliage. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and disturbed soils, often forming extensive mats. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, spikelet-bearing branches.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small fauna.
