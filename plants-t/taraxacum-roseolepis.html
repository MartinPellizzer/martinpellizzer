Taraxacum roseolepis

Taraxacum roseolepis, commonly known as the pink-leaved dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its rosette of spoon-shaped, pinkish-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces a single, elongated flower stem topped with a spherical seed head, typical of the dandelion genus. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in open, disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and easily identifiable species within the Taraxacum genus.
