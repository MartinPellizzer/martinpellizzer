Tecoma avellanedae var. alba

Tecoma avellanedae var. alba, commonly known as white flame tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. The plant has compound leaves composed of several elliptical leaflets. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental species.

Its rapid growth and showy flowers make it a desirable addition to gardens and landscapes.
