Trichachne sacchariflora

Trichachne sacchariflora, commonly known as sweetgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the wetlands and marshy areas of the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and produces small, fluffy seed heads that are often used in traditional crafts and rituals. The plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in association with other wetland vegetation. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, which can help stabilize soil in marsh environments.

Trichachne sacchariflora is also valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in various cultural practices by indigenous communities.
