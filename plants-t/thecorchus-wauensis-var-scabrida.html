Thecorchus wauensis var. scabrida

Thecorchus wauensis var. scabrida, commonly known as the Wau mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of western Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink, with a distinctive five-lobed structure. The leaves are usually large, broad, and have a rough, scabrous texture, giving the plant its varietal name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of dense tropical forests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
