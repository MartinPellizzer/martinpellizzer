Tropaeolum longifolium

Tropaeolum longifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved nasturtium, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, edible flowers that come in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and a strong, peppery scent. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Tropaeolum longifolium is often grown as an ornamental plant and for its edible parts, including leaves and seeds.
