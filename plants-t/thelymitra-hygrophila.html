Thelymitra hygrophila

Thelymitra hygrophila, commonly known as the water lily orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its broad, strap-like leaves and produces striking, showy flowers with a distinctive white and pink coloration. The plant typically grows in wet or semi-aquatic environments, such as swampy areas and along the edges of water bodies. Its flowers have a unique structure with a labellum that is often adorned with intricate patterns.

Thelymitra hygrophila is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in horticulture.
