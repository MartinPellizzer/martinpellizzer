Terminalia confertifolia

Terminalia confertifolia, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and large, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds. It is valued for its hard, durable wood used in construction and furniture, and it also has traditional medicinal uses.

Terminalia confertifolia plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
