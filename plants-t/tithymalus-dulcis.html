Tithymalus dulcis

Tithymalus dulcis, commonly known as sweet tithymale, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa, where it thrives in dry, sunny habitats. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often growing in dense clusters.

Tithymalus dulcis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate poor soil conditions.
