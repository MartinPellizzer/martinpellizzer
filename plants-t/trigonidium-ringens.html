Trigonidium ringens

Trigonidium ringens, commonly known as the spreading goldenrods, is a herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and often have a hairy texture.

Trigonidium ringens is known for its showy, golden-yellow inflorescences that bloom in late summer and early fall.
