Thunbergia palawanensis

Thunbergia palawanensis, commonly known as the Palawan spiderflower, is a climbing vine native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and showy, tubular flowers. The plant produces clusters of blue or violet flowers that are often tinged with white, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly rough texture.

Thunbergia palawanensis thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments in its native habitat.
