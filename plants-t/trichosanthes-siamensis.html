Trichosanthes siamensis

Trichosanthes siamensis, commonly known as the Siam snake gourd, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes cucumbers, squash, and gourds. The plant produces large, elongated fruits with a warty surface and a mild, slightly sweet flavor. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with five petals.

The Siam snake gourd is often cultivated for its edible fruits and is used in various traditional cuisines.
