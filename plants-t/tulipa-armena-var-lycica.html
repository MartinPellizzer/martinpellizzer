Tulipa armena var. lycica

Tulipa armena var. lycica, commonly known as the Armenian tulip, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Armenia and adjacent areas.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep red, often with a distinctive bicolor pattern. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings. Its bulbs are relatively hardy, capable of surviving cold winters in USDA hardiness zones 5 through 7.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its vibrant, early spring blooms.
