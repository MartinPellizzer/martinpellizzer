Triumfetta sanctae-luciae

Triumfetta sanctae-luciae, commonly known as the Holy Lucia triumfetta, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small shrub characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens in Mediterranean climates.
