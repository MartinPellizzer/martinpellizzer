Teucrium heterophyllum subsp. hierrense

Teucrium heterophyllum subsp.

hierrense, commonly known as the Canary Island germander, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands. It is a perennial herb with lance-shaped leaves and small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native habitat. It is characterized by its aromatic foliage and is often used in traditional medicine.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly different leaf morphology compared to other Teucrium species.
