Tomostima araboides

Tomostima araboides, commonly known as the Arabian sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial that thrives in sandy and rocky soils. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its leaves are narrow and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native drought-prone environment.

The species is valued for its resilience and is sometimes used in xeriscaping due to its ability to survive with minimal water.
