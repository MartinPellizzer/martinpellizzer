Taraxacum dageletense

Taraxacum dageletense, commonly known as the Dageletense dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Kazakhstan and Tajikistan. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of pale green, hairy leaves and a flowering stem that bears a single, yellow composite flower head. It produces a distinctive seed head composed of numerous pappus-equipped seeds, facilitating wind dispersal.

This species is adapted to high-altitude environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.
