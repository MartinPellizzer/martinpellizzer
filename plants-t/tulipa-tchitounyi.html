Tulipa tchitounyi

Tulipa tchitounyi, commonly known as the Tchitounyi tulip, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, cup-shaped flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows to a height of 15 to 30 centimeters, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its bulbs are adapted to survive in cold climates, and it blooms in late spring to early summer.

This tulip is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
