Thelypteris remotipinnata

Thelypteris remotipinnata, commonly known as the remote-pinnate shield fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds with a distinctive, finely divided structure. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its sporangia are arranged in clusters on the undersides of the fronds, a common feature in ferns.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
