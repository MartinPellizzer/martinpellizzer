Teucrium aureum subsp. latifolium

Teucrium aureum subsp.

latifolium, commonly known as golden germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its medicinal properties have been historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
