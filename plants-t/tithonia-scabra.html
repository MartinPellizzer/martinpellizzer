Tithonia scabra

Tithonia scabra, commonly known as the roughleaf sunflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, yellow-orange composite flowers and hairy, rough-textured leaves. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats. Tithonia scabra is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes used as a green manure crop.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant in ecological restoration and agricultural practices.
