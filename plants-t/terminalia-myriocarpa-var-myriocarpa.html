Terminalia myriocarpa var. myriocarpa

Terminalia myriocarpa var. myriocarpa, commonly known as the African snowball tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that appear in spherical clusters, giving it a striking appearance during the flowering season. The tree has smooth, grey bark and long, pinnate leaves that are often shed during the dry season. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hard seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and parks for its aesthetic appeal.
