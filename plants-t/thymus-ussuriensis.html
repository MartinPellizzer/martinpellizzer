Thymus ussuriensis

Thymus ussuriensis, commonly known as Siberian thyme, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It features small, compact shrubs with aromatic, gray-green foliage that is densely covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pink or purple flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in compact inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine environments.

Due to its medicinal properties, it is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
