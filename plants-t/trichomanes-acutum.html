Trichomanes acutum

Trichomanes acutum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf huperzia, is a species of fern belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, upright fronds that are typically less than 30 centimeters in length. The plant has a distinctive appearance with small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in tiny, glandular dots. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in temperate regions.

Trichomanes acutum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
