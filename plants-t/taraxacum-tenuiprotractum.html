Taraxacum tenuiprotractum

Taraxacum tenuiprotractum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and a yellow composite flower head that develops into a crown of white, fluffy seeds. Native to North America, this plant thrives in a variety of habitats, including meadows, roadsides, and disturbed soils. It is often found in the eastern and central United States, where it blooms from late spring to early summer.

The plant is a perennial, with a taproot that allows it to survive through harsh environmental conditions.
