Tagetes terniflora

Tagetes terniflora, commonly known as the Mexican marigold, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow or orange flowers and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its aromatic properties make it useful in traditional medicine and as a natural pest repellent.
