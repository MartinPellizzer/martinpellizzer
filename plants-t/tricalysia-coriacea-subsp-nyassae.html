Tricalysia coriacea subsp. nyassae

Tricalysia coriacea subsp.

nyassae, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Africa. It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and dark green, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits. It prefers well-drained soils and is often found in dry woodland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal uses, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional medicine practices.
