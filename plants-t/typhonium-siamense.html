Typhonium siamense

Typhonium siamense, commonly known as Siam's arrowroot or Siamese arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces striking, spadix-like inflorescences surrounded by a colorful spathe, which is typical of the Araceae family. Its rhizomes are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines for their starchy texture.

However, the plant contains calcium oxalate crystals, making it potentially irritating if ingested without proper preparation.
