Triplophyllum crassifolium

Triplophyllum crassifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf triplophyllum, is a rare and distinctive plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, thick, leathery leaves that are typically dark green in color and have a rough texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on rocky outcrops or in dense undergrowth.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is considered a valuable species for botanical research and conservation efforts.
