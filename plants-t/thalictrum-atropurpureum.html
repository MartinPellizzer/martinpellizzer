Thalictrum atropurpureum

Thalictrum atropurpureum, commonly known as red avens, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink to purple. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are typically green with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Thalictrum atropurpureum is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
