Talinum rivae

Talinum rivae, commonly known as water spinach or river spinach, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its edible, arrowhead-shaped leaves that are often used in various cuisines. The plant thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found near water bodies. It produces small, inconspicuous white or pink flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Talinum rivae is valued not only for its nutritional content but also for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to humid environments.
