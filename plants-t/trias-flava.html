Trias flava

Trias flava, commonly known as the golden trias, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its bright yellow petals and lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Trias flava is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
