Trifolium bonannii var. aragonense

Trifolium bonannii var. aragonense, commonly known as the Aragon clover, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, pink to violet flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
