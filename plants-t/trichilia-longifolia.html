Trichilia longifolia

Trichilia longifolia, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a tropical tree native to Africa and parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae, which includes mahogany and other valuable timber species. The plant is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and large, palmately lobed leaves. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in panicles, while the fruit is a capsule that splits open to release winged seeds.

Trichilia longifolia is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in furniture and construction.
