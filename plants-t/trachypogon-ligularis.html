Trachypogon ligularis

Trachypogon ligularis, commonly known as the narrowleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including grasslands and disturbed soils.

Trachypogon ligularis is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardiness and ability to stabilize soil.
