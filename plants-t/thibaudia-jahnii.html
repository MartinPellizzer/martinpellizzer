Thibaudia jahnii

Thibaudia jahnii, commonly known as the bluebell of the genus Thibaudia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy texture, which helps it retain moisture.

Thibaudia jahnii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partially shaded conditions.
