Tridentea aperta

Tridentea aperta, commonly known as the three-lobed water plant, is a species of aquatic plant characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater environments such as ponds and slow-moving streams. The plant's leaves are divided into three lobes, giving it a unique appearance that aids in identification. Tridentea aperta plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates.

It is often used in water gardens and pond maintenance due to its ornamental value and water-cleaning properties.
