Themeda thwaitesii

Themeda thwaitesii, commonly known as the narrow-leaf grass or slender grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Themeda thwaitesii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small fauna.
