Trisetum flavescens subsp. corsicum

Trisetum flavescens subsp.

corsicum, commonly known as Corsican false brome, is a perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Corsica. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant has slender, erect stems and produces small, pale green to golden spikelets. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in marginal environments.
