Thumung

Thumung, commonly known as the sacred lotus, is a tropical aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom on long, slender stems rising from water. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface, while its roots anchor it in the muddy bottom. Thumung is known for its symbolic significance in various cultures and its use in religious ceremonies.

Botanically, it belongs to the Nelumbonaceae family and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
