Trisetum araeanthum

Trisetum araeanthum, commonly known as the golden hairgrass, is a member of the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its fine, delicate foliage and golden tinge. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its inflorescence consists of slender, spike-like clusters of small, pale green to golden florets.

Trisetum araeanthum is often used in ornamental horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and ability to form dense, low-growing mats.
