Thelypteris × papilioides

Thelypteris × papilioides, commonly known as the hybrid fern, is a sterile hybrid resulting from the cross between *Thelypteris acutifolia* and *Thelypteris pteropus*.

It is a delicate, epiphytic fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly in countries like Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped fronds with a leathery texture and a distinctive, glossy appearance. Its reproductive structures are underdeveloped, making it reliant on vegetative propagation for survival.

Thelypteris × papilioides is often found growing on tree trunks or in shaded, humid environments, showcasing its adaptability to tropical climates.
