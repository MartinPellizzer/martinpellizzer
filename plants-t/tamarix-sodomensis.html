Tamarix sodomensis

Tamarix sodomensis, commonly known as the Palestine tamarisk, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, scale-like leaves that are adapted to arid environments. The plant produces pink or purple flowers in clusters, which are typically visited by bees and other pollinators. Its root system is extensive and helps it survive in saline and drought-prone soils.

Tamarix sodomensis is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
