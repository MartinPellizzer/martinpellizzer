Tenoria fruticescens

Tenoria fruticescens, commonly known as the redstem nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring dense, woody stems that are often tinged with red or purple. The plant produces small, white to violet flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by dark blue or black berries. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Tenoria fruticescens is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
