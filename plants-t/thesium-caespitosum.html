Thesium caespitosum

Thesium caespitosum, commonly known as the hairy thesium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Thesium caespitosum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
