Tabernaemontana officinalis

Tabernaemontana officinalis, commonly known as the star of Bethlehem, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink. The flowers are pollinated primarily by birds, particularly sunbirds, due to their nectar-rich nature.
