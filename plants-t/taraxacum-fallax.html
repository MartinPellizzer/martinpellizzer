Taraxacum fallax

Taraxacum fallax, commonly known as the fallax dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of stiff, hairy leaves and a long, hollow stem that supports a single, hairy flower head. The flower head consists of both ray and disk flowers, which bloom in early spring. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
