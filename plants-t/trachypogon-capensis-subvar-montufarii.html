Trachypogon capensis subvar. montufarii

Trachypogon capensis subvar. montufarii, commonly known as the Cape grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The inflorescence consists of a single, narrow spikelet that is often hairy. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
