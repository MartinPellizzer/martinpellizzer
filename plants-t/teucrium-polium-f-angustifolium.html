Teucrium polium f. angustifolium

Teucrium polium f. angustifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved germander, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged oppositely along the stems. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually blue or purple, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the branches. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental flowers make it a valued addition to both wild and cultivated gardens.
