Tecoma eximia

Tecoma eximia, commonly known as the yellow bells, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, featuring opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. Tecoma eximia is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its attractive foliage and showy blooms.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a versatile addition to gardens and landscapes.
