Tristicha bifaria

Tristicha bifaria, commonly known as the two-branched feather grass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, feather-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

Tristicha bifaria is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
