Tragopogon nebrodensis

Tragopogon nebrodensis, commonly known as the Nebroden's goat's beard, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and thrives in sunny, open habitats. Its leaves are basal, lance-shaped, and often have a distinctive blue or purple hue.

Tragopogon nebrodensis is known for its showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.
