Terminalia excelsior

Terminalia excelsior, commonly known as the African wild mango, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elongated, elliptical leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, orange-colored fruits that contain a single large seed. Its botanical significance lies in its medicinal properties and use in traditional medicine.

The plant is also valued for its timber and ornamental potential.
