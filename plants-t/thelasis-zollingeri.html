Thelasis zollingeri

Thelasis zollingeri, commonly known as the Zollinger's thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with spiny, lobed leaves and bright yellow flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Thelasis zollingeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
