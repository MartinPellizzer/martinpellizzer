Tapinanthus namaquensis

Tapinanthus namaquensis, commonly known as the Namibian dodder, is a parasitic flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on its host plant for nutrients, typically attaching itself to the stems or roots of other plants. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and has a highly branched, vine-like structure. It is often found in arid and semi-arid regions, thriving in environments with limited water availability.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can significantly impact the growth and survival of its host species.
