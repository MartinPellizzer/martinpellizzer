Taxus fortunei

Taxus fortunei, commonly known as the Chinese yew, is a slow-growing evergreen conifer native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, needle-like leaves and reddish-brown bark that develops fissures with age. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Taxus fortunei is also valued for its medicinal properties, particularly the compound taxol, which is used in cancer treatments.
