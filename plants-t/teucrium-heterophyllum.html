Teucrium heterophyllum

Teucrium heterophyllum, commonly known as mountain germander, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, often in alpine or subalpine environments. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered blue or purple flowers. Its aromatic foliage and showy blooms make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Teucrium heterophyllum is also valued for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in herbal remedies.
