Terminalia glabra var. tomentosa

Terminalia glabra var. tomentosa, commonly known as the white sand apple or tomentosa tamarind, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and large, palmate leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, pod-like fruits containing edible, starchy seeds. It is often found in dry, sandy soils and is valued for its timber and medicinal properties.

The plant is also used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.
