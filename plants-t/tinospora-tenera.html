Tinospora tenera

Tinospora tenera, commonly known as the climbing tinospora, is a woody vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its twining stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by fleshy, orange-red berries. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
