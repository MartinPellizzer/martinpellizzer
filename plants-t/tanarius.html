Tanarius

Tanarius, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a member of the Rutaceae family and is native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves that have a silvery underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by edible, citrus-like fruits. Tanarius is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.

Its adaptability to arid environments makes it a resilient plant in tropical and subtropical regions.
