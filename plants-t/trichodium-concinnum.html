Trichodium concinnum

Trichodium concinnum, commonly known as the small-leaf sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and small, scale-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in early spring, typically in clusters at the base of the stems. Trichodium concinnum is adapted to arid and rocky environments, making it a resilient species in harsh conditions.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Caryophyllaceae family.
