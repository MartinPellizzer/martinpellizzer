Trapa natans var. komarovii

Trapa natans var. komarovii, commonly known as the floating water chestnut, is a aquatic plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its floating leaves that form a circular rosette on the water surface. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically submerged, followed by spiny, woody fruits. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.
