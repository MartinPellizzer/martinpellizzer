Taxandria marginata

Taxandria marginata, commonly known as the marginated taxandria, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy white to pink flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal heathlands and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. Its foliage is often covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Taxandria marginata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique aesthetic.
