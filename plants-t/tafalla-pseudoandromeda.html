Tafalla pseudoandromeda

Tafalla pseudoandromeda, commonly known as the Andromeda-like evergreen shrub, is a species native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, clustered white or pink flowers during the spring season. Its botanical classification places it within the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries.

Tafalla pseudoandromeda is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in acidic, well-drained soils.
