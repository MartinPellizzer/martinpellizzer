Trochera spicata

Trochera spicata, commonly known as the spike orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, spike-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of white or pale yellow. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions, particularly in parts of Asia and Africa, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding soil, while its leaves are narrow and often arranged in a spiral pattern.

Due to its ornamental value, Trochera spicata is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
