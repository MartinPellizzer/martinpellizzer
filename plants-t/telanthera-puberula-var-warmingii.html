Telanthera puberula var. warmingii

Telanthera puberula var. warmingii, commonly known as the Chinese bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to China.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a distinctive red or purple throat. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, flowering display.
