Tahina

Tahina, commonly known as sesame, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Africa and the Middle East.

It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain edible seeds rich in oil. The plant has hairy, branched stems and alternate, ovate leaves with toothed margins. Tahina thrives in warm, dry climates and is often cultivated for its seeds, which are used in cooking and oil production.

Its botanical name is *Sesamum indicum*, and it belongs to the family Pedaliaceae.
