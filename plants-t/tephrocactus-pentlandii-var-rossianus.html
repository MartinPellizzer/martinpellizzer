Tephrocactus pentlandii var. rossianus

Tephrocactus pentlandii var. rossianus, commonly known as the Rossian hedgehog cactus, is a succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It features a cylindrical stem covered in dense, white spines that provide protection from herbivores and reduce water loss. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking contrast to its spiny structure. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic gardens.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
