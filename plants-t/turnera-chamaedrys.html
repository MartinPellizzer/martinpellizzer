Turnera chamaedrys

Turnera chamaedrys, commonly known as the creeping turnera, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and are borne in clusters, often appearing in the axils of the leaves.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
