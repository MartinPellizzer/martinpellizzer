Thymus serpyllum f. idae

Thymus serpyllum f. idae, commonly known as the Idaean thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of pink or white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders. Its compact growth habit and fragrant foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Thymus serpyllum f. idae is also valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, similar to other thyme species.
