Triticum divergens

Triticum divergens, commonly known as the wild rye, is a grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows and disturbed soils.

Botanically, it is notable for its genetic diversity and potential role in agricultural research.
