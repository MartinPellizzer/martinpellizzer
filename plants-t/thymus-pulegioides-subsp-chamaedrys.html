Thymus pulegioides subsp. chamaedrys

Thymus pulegioides subsp.

chamaedrys, commonly known as creeping thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features dense, mat-forming foliage with small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color.

The plant produces tiny, pinkish-white flowers in spring, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and ground cover.

Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, giving it a distinctive thyme scent and making it useful in culinary and medicinal applications.
