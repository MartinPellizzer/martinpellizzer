Tapinanthus findens

Tapinanthus findens, commonly known as the parasitic orchid, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It is a hemiparasitic species, meaning it derives some of its nutrients from its host plant while also performing photosynthesis. The plant has a slender, vine-like structure and produces small, greenish-white flowers. It typically attaches itself to the stems of other plants, such as grasses or shrubs.

Its unique parasitic nature makes it an interesting subject in botanical studies.
