Trifolium squarrosum var. majus

Trifolium squarrosum var. majus, commonly known as the broad-leaved clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features trifoliate leaves composed of three oval to lance-shaped leaflets with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides.

Its flowers are visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
