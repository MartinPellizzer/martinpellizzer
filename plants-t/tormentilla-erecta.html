Tormentilla erecta

Tormentilla erecta, commonly known as the erect willow or upright tormentil, is a low-growing shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, rounded leaves. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves and stems are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive, slightly fuzzy appearance.

Tormentilla erecta is often found in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and in woodland edges.
