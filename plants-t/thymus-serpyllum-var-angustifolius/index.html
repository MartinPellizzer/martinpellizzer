Thymus Serpyllum Var. Angustifolius

Caraway thyme, scientifically known as Thymus serpyllum var. angustifolius, is a fragrant perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It is prized for its delicate, caraway-like aroma and its ability to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils. This variety of thyme is often used in culinary applications to add a subtle, aromatic flavor to dishes. Its small, pink or white flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to gardens. With its hardy nature and ornamental value, caraway thyme is both a functional and beautiful plant for gardeners and herbalists alike.
