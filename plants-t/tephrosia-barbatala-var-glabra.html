Tephrosia barbatala var. glabra

Tephrosia barbatala var. glabra, commonly known as white tephrosia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and trifoliate leaves with small, rounded lobes. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom during the rainy season. Its seeds are enclosed in slender pods that split open when mature.

This species is often used in traditional medicine and for soil stabilization in degraded lands.
