Trigonostemon xyphophylloides

Trigonostemon xyphophylloides, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a vibrant display. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardens and public spaces.
