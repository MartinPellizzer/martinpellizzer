Trifolium longicaule

Trifolium longicaule, commonly known as the tall clover, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a rosette. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed areas.

Trifolium longicaule is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil and supports agricultural ecosystems.
