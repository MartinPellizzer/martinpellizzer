Timonius glabrescens

Timonius glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth timonius, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Acanthaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically glabrous or nearly hairless. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Timonius glabrescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
