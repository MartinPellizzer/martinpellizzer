Taraxacum trigonolobum

Taraxacum trigonolobum, commonly known as the triangularlobed dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves that are triangular in shape, giving it its specific epithet. The plant produces a hollow, hairless stem that supports a single, bright yellow flower head. It is native to North America and thrives in a variety of soil types, often found in disturbed areas.

The species is notable for its ability to reproduce both sexually through seeds and asexually through rhizomes.
