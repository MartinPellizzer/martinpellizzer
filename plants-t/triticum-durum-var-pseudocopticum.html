Triticum durum var. pseudocopticum

Triticum durum var. pseudocopticum, commonly known as durum wheat variant pseudocopticum, is a botanical variant of durum wheat characterized by its unique morphological traits.

It exhibits a distinct growth habit with shorter, more compact plants compared to standard durum wheat. The leaves are typically narrower and more rigid, while the spike is often more branched, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This variety is notable for its potential in breeding programs aimed at improving drought tolerance and yield stability.

Its genetic makeup offers valuable traits for enhancing adaptability in challenging agricultural environments.
