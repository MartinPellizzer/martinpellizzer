Trichomanes sinuatum

Trichomanes sinuatum, commonly known as the sinuous fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically narrow and elongated. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Trichomanes sinuatum is noted for its intricate leaf patterns and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
