Tapeinosperma alatum

Tapeinosperma alatum, commonly known as the winged sycamore, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Sapotaceae and is characterized by its distinctive winged fruits, which are adapted for wind dispersal. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. It produces a milky latex when the bark is cut, a feature common in the Sapotaceae family.

The tree is often found in lowland forests and is valued for its hard wood and ornamental features.
