Tropaeolum hookerianum

Tropaeolum hookerianum, commonly known as the Hooker's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Tropaeolaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant produces edible tubers, which are rich in nutrients and have a slightly sweet, nutty flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its showy blooms.

Additionally, it has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
