Taraxacum croceifloroides

Taraxacum croceifloroides, commonly known as the yellow-flowered dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to southern Europe and is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers and a basal rosette of leaves. The plant produces a hollow, hairless stem that supports the flower head, which consists of numerous small florets. Its leaves are typically serrated and have a waxy surface, and it is often found in meadows and disturbed soils.

This species is noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
