Taraxacum curvatum

Taraxacum curvatum, commonly known as the bentleaf dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves that curve inward, giving it its common name. The plant produces a yellow composite flower head that develops into a spherical, hollow seed head known as a pappus. It is native to Europe and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

Taraxacum curvatum is a perennial herb that is adapted to a wide range of environmental conditions.
