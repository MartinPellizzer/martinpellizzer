Trisetum transcaucasicum var. ciliatifolium

Trisetum transcaucasicum var. ciliatifolium, commonly known as the ciliated feathergrass, is a grass species native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, linear leaves with ciliate margins. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically significant species within its native range.
