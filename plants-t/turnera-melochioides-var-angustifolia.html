Turnera melochioides var. angustifolia

Turnera melochioides var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf turnera, is a species of flowering plant in the family Turneraceae.

It is native to Central and South America, particularly found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically narrow and elliptical in shape. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in umbels, often appearing in the axils of the leaves.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
