Trichoglottis ramosa

Trichoglottis ramosa, commonly known as the branched trichoglottis, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its rhizomes are often branched, giving the species its common name.

Trichoglottis ramosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
