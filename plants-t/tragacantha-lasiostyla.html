Tragacantha lasiostyla

Tragacantha lasiostyla, commonly known as the Persian gum or Astragalus, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces a viscous resin, known as tragacanth, which has been historically used in medicine and as a thickening agent.

Tragacantha lasiostyla thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky slopes and in dry grasslands.
