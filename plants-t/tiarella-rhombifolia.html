Tiarella rhombifolia

Tiarella rhombifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf foamflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It features heart-shaped, rhomboid leaves that are typically bright green with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers in the spring, which are arranged in umbels. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland settings.

Its foliage and flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in shaded gardens.
