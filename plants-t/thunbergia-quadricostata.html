Thunbergia quadricostata

Thunbergia quadricostata, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Thunbergia quadricostata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant.
