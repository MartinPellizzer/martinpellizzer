Trixis floribunda

Trixis floribunda, commonly known as the showy trixis, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low rainfall. Its leaves are succulent, allowing it to store water efficiently, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Trixis floribunda is often used in local horticulture for its ornamental value and resilience in harsh environments.
