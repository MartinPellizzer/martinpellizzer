Teucrium capitatum var. nevadense

Teucrium capitatum var. nevadense, commonly known as Sierra Tea, is a flowering plant native to the western United States, particularly California and Nevada.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its aromatic, mint-scented leaves and small, clustered blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils in open woodlands and mountainous regions. Its foliage is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to dry climates.
