Talauma siamensis

Talauma siamensis, commonly known as the Siam tulip tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in spring, often in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a smooth margin. The tree produces small, round fruits that contain seeds covered in a fibrous coating.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often planted in gardens and parks for its vibrant floral display.
