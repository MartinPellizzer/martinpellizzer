Taraxacum laevigatum var. magellanicum

Taraxacum laevigatum var. magellanicum, commonly known as the Magellanic dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features a rosette of basal, spoon-shaped leaves that are smooth and often covered in a fine white coating. The plant produces a hollow, wind-dispersed seed stem with yellow composite flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in grassy areas and disturbed soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Asteraceae family.
