Terebinthus multifolia

Terebinthus multifolia, commonly known as the many-leaved terebinth, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with small, leathery leaves arranged in multiple rows along the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles, followed by woody, fleshy fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
