Tanarius paniculatus

Tanarius paniculatus, commonly known as the golden panicgrass, is a grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and inflorescence that consists of branched clusters of spikelets. The plant produces small, golden-yellow flowers that are arranged in a panicle shape, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along riverbanks.

Tanarius paniculatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
