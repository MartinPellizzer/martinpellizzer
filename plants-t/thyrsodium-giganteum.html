Thyrsodium giganteum

Thyrsodium giganteum, commonly known as the giant thyrsodium, is a striking evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, round, blue-black berries that are edible but have a bitter taste. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suited to coastal climates.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for landscaping in suitable environments.
