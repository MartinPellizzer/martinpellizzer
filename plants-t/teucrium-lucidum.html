Teucrium lucidum

Teucrium lucidum, commonly known as shining germander, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features upright, branched stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped, aromatic, and have a slightly minty scent.

Teucrium lucidum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
