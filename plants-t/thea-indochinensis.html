Thea indochinensis

Thea indochinensis, commonly known as Vietnamese tea or Indochina tea, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Theaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and small, white flowers. The plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments in mountainous regions. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine and for making herbal infusions.

Thea indochinensis is valued for its aromatic properties and potential health benefits.
