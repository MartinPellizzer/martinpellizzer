Tragacantha minima

Tragacantha minima, commonly known as the lesser tragacanth, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its narrow, trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces a distinctive, sticky resin that has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive. Tragacantha minima typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and pharmaceutical research.
