Tulipa transtagana

Tulipa transtagana, commonly known as the Transcaucasian tulip, is a species native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The plant typically blooms in late spring, producing cup-shaped petals that are often adorned with intricate patterns. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a bulbous base.

Tulipa transtagana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
