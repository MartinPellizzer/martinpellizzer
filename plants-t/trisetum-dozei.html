Trisetum dozei

Trisetum dozei, commonly known as the narrowleaf woodoat, is a perennial grass species native to the United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along the edges of water bodies. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It is characterized by its delicate appearance and its ability to thrive in nutrient-rich soils.

Trisetum dozei plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
