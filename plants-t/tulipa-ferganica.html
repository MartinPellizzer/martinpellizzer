Tulipa ferganica

Tulipa ferganica, commonly known as the Fergana tulip, is a perennial bulbous plant native to Central Asia.

It produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming in late spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the bulbs are relatively small compared to other tulip species.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
