Tecoma bipinnata

Tecoma bipinnata, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are twice-pinnate, giving it a feathery appearance. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that are clustered in pendulous racemes, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental species.

Tecoma bipinnata is also valued for its rapid growth and ability to provide shade in warm climates.
