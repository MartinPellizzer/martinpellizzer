Tachia exsertus

Tachia exsertus, commonly known as the erect tachia, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense clusters, typically in late summer. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Tachia exsertus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its compact form and resilience.
