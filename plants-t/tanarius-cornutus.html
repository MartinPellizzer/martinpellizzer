Tanarius cornutus

Tanarius cornutus, commonly known as the horned tanarius, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, horn-like structures on its stems, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in shaded undergrowth.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
