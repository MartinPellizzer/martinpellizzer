Tripteris angolensis

Tripteris angolensis, commonly known as the Angola tripteris, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Tripteridaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated margins. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are followed by winged fruits that aid in seed dispersal.

Tripteris angolensis is valued for its ornamental potential and has some traditional medicinal uses.
