Terminalis australis

Terminalis australis, commonly known as the southern terminalis, is a flowering plant native to the southern regions of South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and early summer months. Terminalis australis thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure, making it suitable for rock gardens and arid landscapes.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for xeriscaping in Mediterranean climates.
