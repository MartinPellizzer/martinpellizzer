Thymus serpyllum subsp. chaubardii

Thymus serpyllum subsp.

chaubardii, commonly known as Chaubard's thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically gray-green in color, covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
