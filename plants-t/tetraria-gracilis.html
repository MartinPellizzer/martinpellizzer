Tetraria gracilis

Tetraria gracilis, commonly known as the slender tetraria, is a species of liverwort found in moist, shaded environments.

It belongs to the family Pottiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, leaf-like structures called thallus. The plant exhibits a prostrate growth habit, with rhizoids anchoring it to the substrate. Its reproductive structures, known as gametangia, are typically clustered near the tips of the stems.

Tetraria gracilis plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for microorganisms in its native ecosystems.
