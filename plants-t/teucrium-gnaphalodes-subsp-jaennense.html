Teucrium gnaphalodes subsp. jaennense

Teucrium gnaphalodes subsp.

jaennense, commonly known as the Jaennese germander, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, often in Mediterranean climates. Its small, tubular flowers are usually violet or blue, and it produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
