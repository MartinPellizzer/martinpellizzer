Tilia michauxii

Tilia michauxii, commonly known as the Michaux's lime, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Tiliaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which bloom in early summer and are followed by small, round fruits. Its bark is smooth and light gray, becoming fissured with age, and it prefers moist, well-drained soils in full sun to partial shade.

Tilia michauxii is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
