Tridens elongatus

Tridens elongatus, commonly known as the elongated tooth plant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical regions.

It features slender, upright stems that can grow up to several feet in height. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in clusters at the top of the stems. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Tridens elongatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to well-drained soils and full sunlight.
