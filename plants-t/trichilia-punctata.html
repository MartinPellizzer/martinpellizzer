Trichilia punctata

Trichilia punctata, commonly known as the African sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elongated, woody fruits containing flat, winged seeds. It is valued for its aromatic wood, which is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.

Trichilia punctata thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland and mountainous regions.
