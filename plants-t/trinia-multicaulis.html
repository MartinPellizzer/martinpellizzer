Trinia multicaulis

Trinia multicaulis, commonly known as the clustered trinia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. The plant features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences of pale yellow to greenish-white flowers. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a tendency to form dense clumps through vegetative propagation.

Trinia multicaulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
