Trichosanthes cucumerina subsp. anguina

Trichosanthes cucumerina subsp.

anguina, commonly known as the horned melon or Christ's Crown, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family. It is a climbing vine that produces large, spiky, green fruits with a thick, warty rind.

The fruit contains a sweet, edible pulp surrounding numerous black, inedible seeds. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is valued for its ornamental appearance and its unique, mildly sweet flavor.
