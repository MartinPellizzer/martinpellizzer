Trichouratea guildingii

Trichouratea guildingii, commonly known as the golden star orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow petals and a central golden stamen that resembles a star. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are pollinated primarily by insects such as bees and wasps.

Trichouratea guildingii is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its role in traditional medicine in its native regions.
