Tilia amurensis

Tilia amurensis, commonly known as Amur lime or Siberian linden, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Tiliaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, white flowers that bloom in early summer. The tree produces small, round fruits that are typically dispersed by wind. It is valued for its hardiness and is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental qualities.

The wood of Tilia amurensis is light and soft, making it suitable for carving and furniture making.
