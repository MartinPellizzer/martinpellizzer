Taraxacum anadyrense

Taraxacum anadyrense, commonly known as the Anadyrense dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its bright yellow flower heads consist of numerous small florets, which later develop into fluffy white seeds.

The leaves are basal, spoon-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, often with a waxy coating.
