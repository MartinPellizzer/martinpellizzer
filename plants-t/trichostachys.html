Trichostachys

Trichostachys, commonly known as the bamboo grass, is a genus of flowering plants in the grass family, Poaceae.

These plants are native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas. Trichostachys species are characterized by their tall, slender stems and dense, often clumping growth habit. They typically produce small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered in panicles.

The genus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine and as a source of sustainable materials.
