Tetragonanthus elatus

Tetragonanthus elatus, commonly known as the giant spiderwort, is a striking plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has four-sided stems, which give it its genus name, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Tetragonanthus elatus is a perennial herb that plays an important role in its native ecosystem.
