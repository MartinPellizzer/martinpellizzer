Tillandsia tectorum

Tillandsia tectorum, commonly known as the roof tillandsia, is a species of air plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the bromeliad family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that trap moisture and nutrients from the air. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on trees or rocks. Its flowers are typically small and inconspicuous, with a greenish-white color.

Tillandsia tectorum is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its low maintenance and unique appearance.
