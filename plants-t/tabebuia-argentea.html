Tabebuia argentea

Tabebuia argentea, commonly known as the silver trumpet tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The tree has a smooth, light gray bark and produces small, round, green fruits that contain winged seeds. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and elliptical, with a smooth surface.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping due to its striking floral display.
