Teucrium lamiifolium subsp. stachyophyllum

Teucrium lamiifolium subsp.

stachyophyllum, commonly known as purple germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of small, tubular, violet-blue flowers. Its flowering period usually occurs in the summer months, and it is known for its aromatic, slightly minty scent.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
