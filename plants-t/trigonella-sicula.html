Trigonella sicula

Trigonella sicula, commonly known as Sicilian fenugreek, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain small, kidney-shaped seeds. Trigonella sicula is often cultivated for its seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
