Thymus atlanticus subsp. ayachicus

Thymus atlanticus subsp.

ayachicus, commonly known as Atlantic thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to coastal regions of the Iberian Peninsula. It features aromatic, narrow leaves and produces small, pinkish-white flowers in the spring.

The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to coastal and maritime climates. It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

Its essential oils contain thymol and other compounds with antimicrobial and antioxidant effects.
