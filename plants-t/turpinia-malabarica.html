Turpinia malabarica

Turpinia malabarica, commonly known as the Malabar turpentine tree, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds.

Turpinia malabarica is valued for its aromatic wood and has traditional uses in medicine and as a source of turpentine.
