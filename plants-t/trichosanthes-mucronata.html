Trichosanthes mucronata

Trichosanthes mucronata, commonly known as the horned melon or Christ's Crown, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiky, greenish-yellow fruit that resembles a crown, with a thick, leathery rind. The plant produces large, heart-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The fruit contains a sweet, edible pulp with black seeds, and it is often used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
