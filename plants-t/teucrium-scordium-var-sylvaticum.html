Teucrium scordium var. sylvaticum

Teucrium scordium var. sylvaticum, commonly known as wild thyme or mountain thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils, often in mountainous regions. The plant features small, pink or purple flowers arranged in spikes, and its leaves are aromatic, with a strong, pungent scent. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
