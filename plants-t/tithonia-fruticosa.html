Tithonia fruticosa

Tithonia fruticosa, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red flowers, which bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has hairy, rough stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped with serrated edges. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Tithonia fruticosa is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
