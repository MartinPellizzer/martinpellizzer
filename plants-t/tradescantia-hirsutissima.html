Tradescantia hirsutissima

Tradescantia hirsutissima, commonly known as the hairy spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, fleshy leaves and clusters of small, showy blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is a perennial herb that spreads through rhizomes, making it a common sight in wetland habitats.
