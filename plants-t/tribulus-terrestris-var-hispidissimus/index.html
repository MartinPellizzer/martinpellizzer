Tribulus Terrestris Var. Hispidissimus

Tribulus terrestris var. hispidissimus is a low-growing, annual plant commonly found in arid and semi-arid regions. It is known for its hairy stems and yellow flowers that bloom throughout the summer months. This variant of Tribulus terrestris is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits. Its adaptability to harsh environments makes it a resilient species in many ecosystems. Despite its small size, it plays an important role in supporting local wildlife and soil stability.
