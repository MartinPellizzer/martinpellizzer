Tulipastrum acuminatum var. ludovicianum

Tulipastrum acuminatum var. ludovicianum, commonly known as the Florida tulip tree, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Magnoliaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tulip-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The tree has smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along riverbanks.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
