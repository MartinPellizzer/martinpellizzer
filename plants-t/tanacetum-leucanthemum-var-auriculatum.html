Tanacetum leucanthemum var. auriculatum

Tanacetum leucanthemum var. auriculatum, commonly known as the auriculate elecampane, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe.

It features tall, upright stems that bear large, showy yellow ray florets surrounded by a dense cluster of white disk florets. The leaves are lance-shaped with prominent veins and a hairy surface, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
