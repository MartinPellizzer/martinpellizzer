Tragacantha hypoleuca

Tragacantha hypoleuca, commonly known as the white tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that produces yellow flowers and is characterized by its distinctive, branched stems. The plant is known for its mucilage-rich sap, which has been used historically in various traditional applications. Its leaves are alternate and ovate, with a hairy texture that helps it retain moisture.

Tragacantha hypoleuca thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.
