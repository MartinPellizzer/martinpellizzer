Terminalia punctata

Terminalia punctata, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and simple, opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by hard, woody fruits containing smooth, oval seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Terminalia punctata is valued for its durable wood and has traditional uses in medicine and craftsmanship.
