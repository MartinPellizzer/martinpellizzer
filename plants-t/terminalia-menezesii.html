Terminalia menezesii

Terminalia menezesii, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and alternate, simple leaves. The tree produces small, white flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing flat, seed-like structures. It is valued for its hard, heavy wood used in furniture and tool handles.

The plant also has traditional medicinal uses in various African cultures.
