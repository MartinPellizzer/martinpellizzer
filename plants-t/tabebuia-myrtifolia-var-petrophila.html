Tabebuia myrtifolia var. petrophila

Tabebuia myrtifolia var. petrophila, commonly known as the rock tabebuia, is a flowering tree native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, typically blooming in the spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone-rich environments. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and elliptical, with a smooth texture.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its beautiful floral display.
