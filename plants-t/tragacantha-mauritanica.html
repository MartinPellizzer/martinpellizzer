Tragacantha mauritanica

Tragacantha mauritanica, commonly known as the Algerian tragacanth, is a shrub native to North Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by pod-like fruits containing seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought conditions.

Tragacantha mauritanica is often used in traditional medicine and for soil stabilization in degraded lands.
