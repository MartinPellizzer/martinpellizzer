Tamarix gallica var. pycnostachys

Tamarix gallica var. pycnostachys, commonly known as the narrowleaf sea-buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, scale-like leaves that are typically dark green and densely arranged along the stems. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers in clusters, which bloom in the spring and attract pollinators such as bees. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that ripens to a bright red color, containing numerous seeds.

This species is adapted to dry, saline soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
