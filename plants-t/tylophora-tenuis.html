Tylophora tenuis

Tylophora tenuis, commonly known as slender tylophora, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, and its fruits are follicles containing smooth, black seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and shrublands.

Tylophora tenuis is valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
