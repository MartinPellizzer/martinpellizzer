Tetracera sarmentosa var. hirsuta

Tetracera sarmentosa var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy tetracera, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which are often covered in fine, soft pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that release tiny seeds when mature.

This species is often found in forested areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
