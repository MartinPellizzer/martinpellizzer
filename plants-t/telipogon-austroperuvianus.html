Telipogon austroperuvianus

Telipogon austroperuvianus, commonly known as the Peruvian orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or forested areas. Its botanical features include linear leaves and a unique inflorescence that bears several showy, violet-colored blooms.

Telipogon austroperuvianus is of interest to botanists due to its ecological adaptations and limited distribution.
