Tradescantia gentianifolia

Tradescantia gentianifolia, commonly known as the silver sword or red-veined nettle, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, lance-shaped leaves with a silvery-blue hue and prominent red veins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically pink or white, arranged in clusters at the tips of the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Tradescantia gentianifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and ease of care.
