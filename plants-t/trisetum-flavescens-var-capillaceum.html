Trisetum flavescens var. capillaceum

Trisetum flavescens var. capillaceum, commonly known as the silky false brome, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves that give it a soft, silky appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. It produces small, pale green or yellowish flowers in panicles during the summer months.

Its ecological importance includes providing forage for wildlife and stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
