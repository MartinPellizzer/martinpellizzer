Tetraplasandra waimeae

Tetraplasandra waimeae, commonly known as the Waimea pandanus, is a rare and unique plant native to Hawaii.

It belongs to the Pandanus family and is characterized by its distinctive, sword-like leaves that grow in a dense rosette. The plant has a short, thick trunk and produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or green. Its leaves are often used in traditional Hawaiian crafts and ceremonies due to their durability and aesthetic appeal.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a critically endangered species.
