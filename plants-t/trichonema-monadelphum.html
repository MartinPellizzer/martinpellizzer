Trichonema monadelphum

Trichonema monadelphum, commonly known as the African orchid, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically white or pale green, often arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

Trichonema monadelphum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
