Tellima

Tellima, commonly known as the ghost plant, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Ericaceae.

It consists of two species, Tellima grandiflora and Tellima japonica, both native to eastern Asia. These plants are characterized by their showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. They typically grow in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes.

The foliage is often evergreen, with small, leathery leaves that contribute to their distinctive appearance.
