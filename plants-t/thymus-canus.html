Thymus canus

Thymus canus, commonly known as narrowleaf thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and pink to purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage is often used in culinary and medicinal applications due to its pungent, minty aroma.

Thymus canus is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardiness.
