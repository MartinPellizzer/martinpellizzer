Torilis chrysocarpa

Torilis chrysocarpa, commonly known as golden-fruited hedge mustard, is a flowering plant in the family Brassicaceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically found in dry grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, followed by distinctive yellow, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

The species is often used in ecological restoration due to its adaptability and ability to thrive in harsh environments.
