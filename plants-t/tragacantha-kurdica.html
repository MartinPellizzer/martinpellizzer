Tragacantha kurdica

Tragacantha kurdica, commonly known as Kurdish tragacanth, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Turkey and Iran.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by pod fruits containing small, dark seeds.

Its sap is known for its thick, viscous texture and has been used traditionally in various medicinal and culinary applications.
