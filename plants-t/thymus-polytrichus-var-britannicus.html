Thymus polytrichus var. britannicus

Thymus polytrichus var. britannicus, commonly known as British thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to western Europe.

It features small, aromatic, dark green leaves and pink to purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and sunny to partially shaded locations. It is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats, including coastal areas and dry grasslands.

Its essential oils, rich in thymol, make it valuable for both culinary and medicinal uses.
