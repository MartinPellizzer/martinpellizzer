Thysanotus brevipes

Thysanotus brevipes, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bluebell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in shrubland and forest edges, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slight pubescence.
