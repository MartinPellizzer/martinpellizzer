Teucrium cossonii subsp. punicum

Teucrium cossonii subsp.

punicum, commonly known as the purple germander, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, hairy foliage.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from purple to pink, typically blooming in summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and aromatic, often with a mint-like scent.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and borders.
