Talisia multinervis

Talisia multinervis, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are typically three to five lobes and have a velvety texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by oval-shaped fruits containing one or two seeds. Talisia multinervis is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
