Tricalysia macrochlamys

Tricalysia macrochlamys, commonly known as the large-chalmed trumpet tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves with a smooth surface and a slightly hairy underside. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release winged seeds, aiding in wind dispersal.

Tricalysia macrochlamys is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
