Tricalysia apiocarpa

Tricalysia apiocarpa, commonly known as the red-flowered ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and striking red to orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a hard, durable wood that is often used in construction and furniture-making. Its leaves are opposite and oval-shaped, with a smooth texture.

Tricalysia apiocarpa thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.
