Teliosma caerulea var. procumbens

Teliosma caerulea var. procumbens, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, blue-violet flowers in late summer. The plant has a sprawling growth habit, with stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture.

The species is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in moist, shady environments.
