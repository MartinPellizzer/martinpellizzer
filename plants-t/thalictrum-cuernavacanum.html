Thalictrum cuernavacanum

Thalictrum cuernavacanum, commonly known as the Cuernavaca meadowrue, is a herbaceous plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and delicate, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Thalictrum cuernavacanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
