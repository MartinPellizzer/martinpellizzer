Trichoglottis magnicallosa

Trichoglottis magnicallosa, commonly known as the large-collared orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its leaves are usually broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its natural habitat.

The flowers feature a distinctive yellow and green coloration, with a unique structure that aids in pollination by specific insect species.
