Tissa aprica

Tissa aprica, commonly known as the sweet apricot, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to temperate regions of Asia.

It produces showy, fragrant flowers in the spring, followed by edible, juicy fruits in the summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring moderate watering during the growing season. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a smooth, dark green surface.

Tissa aprica is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary uses, making it a popular choice in gardens and orchards.
