Tithymalus anacampseros

Tithymalus anacampseros, commonly known as the desert hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange and red. The plant has a woody stem and produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is adapted to survive in harsh environments with minimal water availability.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of xerophytic plants.
