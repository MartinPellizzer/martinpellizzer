Tragacantha xanthura

Tragacantha xanthura, commonly known as the yellow tragacanth, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its yellowish flowers and distinctive pod-like fruits. The plant produces a sticky resin, known as tragacanth, which has been historically used in medicine and as a thickening agent. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and traditional contexts.
