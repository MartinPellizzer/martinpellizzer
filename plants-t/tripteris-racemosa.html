Tripteris racemosa

Tripteris racemosa, commonly known as the clustered tripteris, is a tropical plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the family Tripteridaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves with three leaflets. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, such as rainforests. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are followed by clusters of tiny, winged fruits.

Tripteris racemosa is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
