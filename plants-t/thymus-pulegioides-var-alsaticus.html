Thymus pulegioides var. alsaticus

Thymus pulegioides var. alsaticus, commonly known as narrowleaf thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and Eastern Europe.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically narrow and slightly hairy, with a strong, minty scent. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pink to purple flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its medicinal properties have been historically valued for their antiseptic and digestive benefits.
