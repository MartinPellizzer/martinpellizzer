Tamarix speciosa var. getula

Tamarix speciosa var. getula, commonly known as the silver leaf tamarisk, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and small, scale-like leaves that are typically silver or gray in color, providing a striking visual contrast. The plant produces small, pink to reddish flowers in clusters during the summer months, which are adapted for pollination by wind and insects. It is well-suited to dry, sandy soils and can tolerate high salinity, making it a hardy species in harsh environments.

Its rapid growth and ability to fix nitrogen make it both ecologically significant and sometimes invasive in non-native regions.
