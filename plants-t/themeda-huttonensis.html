Themeda huttonensis

Themeda huttonensis, commonly known as Hutton's grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered with fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in a variety of soil types but is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Themeda huttonensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for grazing animals.
