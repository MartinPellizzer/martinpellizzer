Triplarina paludosa

Triplarina paludosa, commonly known as the marsh triplarina, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland habitats in eastern North America.

It typically grows in swampy areas and along the edges of ponds, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. The plant features slender, grass-like stems and small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its wetland environment.

Triplarina paludosa plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
