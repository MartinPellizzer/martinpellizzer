Trillium affine

Trillium affine, commonly known as the white trillium, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the family Melanthiaceae and is characterized by its three white petals, three sepals, and three leaves. The plant typically grows in rich, moist woodlands and thrives in partial shade. Its flowers bloom in early spring, often before the leaves fully emerge.

Trillium affine is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
