Thermopsis lanceolata subsp. turkestanica

Thermopsis lanceolata subsp.

turkestanica, commonly known as the Turkestan yellow gentian, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
