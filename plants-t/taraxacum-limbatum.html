Taraxacum limbatum

Taraxacum limbatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and a yellow composite flower head that develops into a spherical seed ball. Native to Europe and western Asia, it thrives in a variety of habitats, including meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant is known for its ability to spread via wind-dispersed seeds, contributing to its widespread distribution.

Its botanical features make it a common sight in many temperate regions.
