Tupa ensifolia

Tupa ensifolia, commonly known as the swordleaf tupa, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its flowers are often brightly colored, ranging from red to purple, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees.

Tupa ensifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
