Tanacetopsis pamiralaica

Tanacetopsis pamiralaica, commonly known as the Pamir mountain fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Pamir Mountains.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving in cool, dry conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Tanacetopsis pamiralaica is notable for its medicinal properties, with some traditional uses in local herbal remedies.
