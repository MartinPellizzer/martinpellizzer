Topobea adscendens

Topobea adscendens, commonly known as the climbing hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems that allow it to climb and trail along supports. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom throughout the growing season. Its leaves are ovate with a rough texture and are often covered in fine hairs.

Topobea adscendens is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid, sunny environments.
