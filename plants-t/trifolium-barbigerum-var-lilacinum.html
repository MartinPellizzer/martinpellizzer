Trifolium barbigerum var. lilacinum

Trifolium barbigerum var. lilacinum, commonly known as the purple clover, is a flowering plant species belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets with a hairy surface. The flowers are small and typically pink to purple in color, arranged in dense clusters. This variety is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in agricultural and ecological contexts.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils.
