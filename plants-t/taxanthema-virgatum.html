Taxanthema virgatum

Taxanthema virgatum, commonly known as the yellow stonecrop, is a low-growing succulent plant native to arid regions of North America.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, adapted to store water and reduce transpiration. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers in the spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Taxanthema virgatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in harsh environments.
