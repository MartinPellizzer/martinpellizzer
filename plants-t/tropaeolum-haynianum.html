Tropaeolum haynianum

Tropaeolum haynianum, commonly known as the golden crown flower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Tropaeolaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant features heart-shaped, glossy leaves and thrives in warm, sunny conditions. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and ability to attract pollinators.

Tropaeolum haynianum is also valued for its edible tubers, which have a peppery flavor similar to that of watercress.
