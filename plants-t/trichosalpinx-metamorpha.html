Trichosalpinx metamorpha

Trichosalpinx metamorpha, commonly known as the pink orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that display a range of pink to purple hues. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are bilaterally symmetrical, with three sepals and three petals, one of which is modified into a labellum.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its vibrant and unique floral display.
