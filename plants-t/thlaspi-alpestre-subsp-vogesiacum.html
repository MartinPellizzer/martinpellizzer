Thlaspi alpestre subsp. vogesiacum

Thlaspi alpestre subsp.

vogesiacum, commonly known as the Alpine cabbages, is a member of the Brassicaceae family. It is a perennial herb characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, often on rocky or gravelly soils. It is well adapted to cold climates and can tolerate harsh environmental conditions.

Its flowering period usually occurs in late spring to early summer.
