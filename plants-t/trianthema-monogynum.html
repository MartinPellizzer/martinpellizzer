Trianthema monogynum

Trianthema monogynum, commonly known as the narrowleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of up to 30 centimeters. The plant features slender, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small, trumpet-shaped, and typically pale blue to white in color, blooming in clusters.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas.
