Thrasyopsis

Thrasyopsis, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to southern Africa.

These plants are characterized by their rosettes of velvety, dark green leaves and clusters of showy, tubular flowers. Thrasyopsis species typically produce flowers in shades of purple, blue, and white, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. They thrive in humid, well-drained soil and require indirect sunlight to flourish.

The genus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated as a houseplant.
