Tetragonolobus tauricus

Tetragonolobus tauricus, commonly known as the Taurian tetra-lobed bean, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its tetrahedral seed pods, which give it its scientific name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and is often found in dry, rocky habitats. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets arranged in a triangular shape.

Tetragonolobus tauricus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
