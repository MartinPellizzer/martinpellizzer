Teucrium mairei

Teucrium mairei, commonly known as Maire's germander, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern France and northern Italy.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Teucrium mairei is often found in rocky or limestone soils and is valued for its ornamental qualities and medicinal properties.
