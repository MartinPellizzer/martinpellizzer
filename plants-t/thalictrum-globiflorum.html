Thalictrum globiflorum

Thalictrum globiflorum, commonly known as the globeflower, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its striking, globe-shaped flower heads composed of numerous small, white to pinkish petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and meadows. Its leaves are palmately divided, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Thalictrum globiflorum is valued in gardens for its unique floral display and ecological benefits to pollinators.
