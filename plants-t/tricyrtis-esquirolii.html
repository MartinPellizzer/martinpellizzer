Tricyrtis esquirolii

Tricyrtis esquirolii, commonly known as the Japanese spider orchid, is a herbaceous perennial native to Japan.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, spider-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of pale yellow to orange flowers with a distinctive, elongated spur. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Tricyrtis esquirolii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
