Tinospora homosepala

Tinospora homosepala, commonly known as the Indian moonseed, is a climbing plant belonging to the Menispermaceae family.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically unisexual. The plant produces bright red, fleshy berries that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Tinospora homosepala is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
