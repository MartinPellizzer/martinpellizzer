Triaspis emarginata var. discolor

Triaspis emarginata var. discolor, commonly known as the variegated triaspis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated foliage with shades of green, white, and yellow. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
