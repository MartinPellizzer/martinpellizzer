Teratophyllum arthropteroides

Teratophyllum arthropteroides, commonly known as the jointed teratophyllum, is a unique succulent native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, segmented stems that resemble a jointed structure, which helps it store water and withstand harsh environmental conditions. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, adding a striking visual contrast to its green, fleshy foliage. Its leaves are thick and fleshy, adapted for minimizing water loss through transpiration.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native environment.
