Triticum caninum f. dasyrrhachis

Triticum caninum f. dasyrrhachis, commonly known as the rough-hairy wheatgrass, is a botanical variant of the species Triticum caninum.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant features a distinctive hairy texture on its foliage, which gives it its common name. It is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

This grass is valued for its hardiness and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
