Trifolium hirtum subsp. cherleri

Trifolium hirtum subsp.

cherleri, commonly known as the hairy trefoil, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each leaflet being hairy and oval-shaped.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
