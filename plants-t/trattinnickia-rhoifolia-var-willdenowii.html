Trattinnickia rhoifolia var. willdenowii

Trattinnickia rhoifolia var. willdenowii, commonly known as the willdenow's rock heath, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a compact growth habit with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
