Tecoma ipe f. leucotricha

Tecoma ipe f. leucotricha, commonly known as the white-haired crown flower, is a variant of the Tecoma ipe species native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant features opposite, simple, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are typically clustered in terminal panicles and bloom during the warmer months.

Tecoma ipe f. leucotricha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
