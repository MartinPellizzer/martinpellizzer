Thlaspi perfoliatum var. neglectum

Thlaspi perfoliatum var. neglectum, commonly known as the common rockcress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant has basal leaves that are alternate and often have a distinctive pungent odor. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or arctic environments.

Its ability to tolerate harsh conditions makes it a hardy species adapted to cold climates.
