Thymus chamaedrys var. pinifolius

Thymus chamaedrys var. pinifolius, commonly known as creeping thyme with pine-like leaves, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink or white, typically in early summer. The plant has needle-like, evergreen leaves that are slightly aromatic and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and ornamental plant valued in both horticulture and herbal medicine.
