Torenia angustifolia

Torenia angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf torenia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a contrasting throat. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Torenia angustifolia thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
