Telmatophace cylindracea

Telmatophace cylindracea, commonly known as the slender marsh stick, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, grass-like stems that grow close to the ground. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in marshy or swampy environments.

Due to its adaptability, it plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
