Tigridia ehrenbergii

Tigridia ehrenbergii, commonly known as the Mexican fairy lantern, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that resemble small lanterns. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 feet, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow, often with a contrasting black or purple center.

Tigridia ehrenbergii is a perennial that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
