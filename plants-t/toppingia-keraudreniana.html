Toppingia keraudreniana

Toppingia keraudreniana, commonly known as the Keraudren's toppingia, is a rare and enigmatic species of lichen belonging to the family Toppingiaceae.

It is characterized by its unique thallus structure, which is typically crustose and exhibits a pale to dark brown coloration. The lichen is known for its slow growth and is often found in humid, shaded environments, particularly on bark or soil surfaces. Its chemical composition includes various secondary metabolites that contribute to its ecological role in nutrient cycling.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Toppingia keraudreniana is of interest to mycologists and conservationists alike.
