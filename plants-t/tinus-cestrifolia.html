Tinus cestrifolia

Tinus cestrifolia, commonly known as the African snowball, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Tinus cestrifolia is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively low maintenance.
