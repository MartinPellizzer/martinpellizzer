Tenoria glauca

Tenoria glauca, commonly known as the bluebush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces small, blue to purple flowers. The plant has silver-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Tenoria glauca is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
