Tragacantha bifida

Tragacantha bifida, commonly known as the bifid tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive bifid (split) pods. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, followed by the development of its characteristic seed pods. Tragacantha bifida is known for its ability to thrive in dry, rocky soils and is often found in scrubland and woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and traditional herbal medicine.
