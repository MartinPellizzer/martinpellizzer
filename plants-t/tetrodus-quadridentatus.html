Tetrodus quadridentatus

Tetrodus quadridentatus, commonly known as the four-toothed plant, is a unique species native to specific regions with particular soil and climate conditions.

It is characterized by its distinctive four-toothed structure on its leaves, which is a key botanical feature. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its leaves are broad and have a waxy surface, which helps in water retention.

The flowers are small and clustered, producing a mild fragrance that attracts pollinators.
