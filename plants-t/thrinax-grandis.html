Thrinax grandis

Thrinax grandis, commonly known as the Mexican palm, is a species of palm native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a small, cold-tolerant palm that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters. The plant has a slender, unbranched trunk and pinnate leaves that are dark green and glossy. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, and it produces edible, round fruits that are about 1.5 centimeters in diameter.

Thrinax grandis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
