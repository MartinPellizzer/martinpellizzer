Terebinthus lanuginosa

Terebinthus lanuginosa, commonly known as the woolly terebinth, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, yellowish flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species in Mediterranean ecosystems.
