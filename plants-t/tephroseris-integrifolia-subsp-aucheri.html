Tephroseris integrifolia subsp. aucheri

Tephroseris integrifolia subsp.

aucheri, commonly known as the Australian mountain daisy, is a perennial herb native to southeastern Australia. It typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow to orange. It is adapted to cold climates and is often found in rocky or scree habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
