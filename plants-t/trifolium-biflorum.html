Trifolium biflorum

Trifolium biflorum, commonly known as the two-flower clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

Trifolium biflorum is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
