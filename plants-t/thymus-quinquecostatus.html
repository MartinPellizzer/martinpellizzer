Thymus quinquecostatus

Thymus quinquecostatus, commonly known as the five-costate thyme, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with aromatic leaves and small, pink to purple flowers. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its fragrant herbs and medicinal properties. Its leaves are often used in cooking and traditional medicine due to their essential oils.

Thymus quinquecostatus thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and borders.
