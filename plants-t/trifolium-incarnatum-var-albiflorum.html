Trifolium incarnatum var. albiflorum

Trifolium incarnatum var. albiflorum, commonly known as the white-flowered crimson clover, is a variant of the crimson clover species native to Europe.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets, and its distinctive pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is a herbaceous annual or biennial, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. It has a shallow root system and is commonly used as a forage crop and in soil conservation efforts.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
