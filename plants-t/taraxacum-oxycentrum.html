Taraxacum oxycentrum

Taraxacum oxycentrum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dandelion, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and a yellow composite flower head that matures into a spherical seed head with white pappus. The plant is native to Europe and is often found in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils. It has a taproot system and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

The species is also noted for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in herbal remedies.
