Turritis purpurea

Turritis purpurea, commonly known as the purple turritis, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaryllis family (Amaryllidaceae) and is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10 to 20 centimeters, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers bloom in late spring or early summer, often in clusters at the base of the plant.

Turritis purpurea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
