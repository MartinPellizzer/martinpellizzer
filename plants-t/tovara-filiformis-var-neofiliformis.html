Tovara filiformis var. neofiliformis

Tovara filiformis var. neofiliformis, commonly known as the slender tovara, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its long, thin stems and small, delicate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pale pink. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun exposure.

Due to its unique morphology and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by specialty plant enthusiasts.
