Thelocactus goldii

Thelocactus goldii, commonly known as the golden thorn, is a unique cactus species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in dense, white spines that provide protection from herbivores and intense sunlight. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are often followed by red, fleshy fruits. Its root system is shallow and adapted to absorb moisture from infrequent rains.

Thelocactus goldii is well-suited to rocky or sandy soils and requires minimal water, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.
