Tomanthea rupestris

Tomanthea rupestris, commonly known as the rock cress, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. This species is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and small, white or pale yellow flowers. Tomanthea rupestris thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in arid and semi-arid ecosystems.
