Tillandsia sanguinolenta

Tillandsia sanguinolenta, commonly known as the blood-veined air plant, is a species of epiphytic bromeliad native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which aid in water absorption. The plant produces striking red and purple flowers that bloom from a central stem, adding visual interest to its appearance. It thrives in humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
