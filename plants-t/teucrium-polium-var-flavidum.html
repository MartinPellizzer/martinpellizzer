Teucrium polium var. flavidum

Teucrium polium var. flavidum, commonly known as the yellow germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, branched stems that are often hairy, and it produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers arranged in dense clusters. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly aromatic scent. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

This plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
