Taraxacum laevigatum var. erythrospermum

Taraxacum laevigatum var. erythrospermum, commonly known as the red-seeded dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow composite flower heads and distinctive red-brown seeds that are easily dispersed by the wind. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and disturbed soils. It has alternate, basal leaves that are usually lobed or toothed.

This variety is often found in temperate regions and is known for its medicinal properties.
