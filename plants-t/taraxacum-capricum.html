Taraxacum capricum

Taraxacum capricum, commonly known as the goat's milk dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and a hollow, hairy stem that supports a single, yellow flower head. The plant produces a distinctive, fluffy white seed head that disperses seeds via the wind. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

Taraxacum capricum is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional remedies for various ailments.
