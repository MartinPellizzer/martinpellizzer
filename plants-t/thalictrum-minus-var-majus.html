Thalictrum minus var. majus

Thalictrum minus var. majus, commonly known as the showy meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a silky white down, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. It typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Thalictrum minus var. majus is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
