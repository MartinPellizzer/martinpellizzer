Trichachne penicilligera

Trichachne penicilligera, commonly known as the slender trichachne, is a grass species native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its flowering period usually occurs during the warmer months, and it is adapted to withstand arid conditions.

Trichachne penicilligera is often used in ecological studies due to its resilience and role in local ecosystems.
