Thapsia garganica var. angusta

Thapsia garganica var. angusta, commonly known as narrowleaf thapsia, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and showy, yellowish-green flower heads that appear in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.

Its morphology and habitat make it well adapted to arid environments.
