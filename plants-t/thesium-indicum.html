Thesium indicum

Thesium indicum, commonly known as the Indian thesium, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Xanthorrhoeaceae.

It is characterized by its erect, herbaceous stems and small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish or yellowish in color. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Thesium indicum is often found in moist, shaded areas such as forests and along stream banks.

It is native to regions of Asia, including India, and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
