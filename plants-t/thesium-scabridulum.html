Thesium scabridulum

Thesium scabridulum, commonly known as the rough thesium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and wetlands. The plant features slender, ascending stems that are often covered in fine, rough hairs. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are clustered in loose panicles, and it produces small, three-lobed seed pods.

Thesium scabridulum is known for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain insect species.
