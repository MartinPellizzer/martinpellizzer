Tinus nutans

Tinus nutans, commonly known as the weeping boerhaave, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its drooping, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a whorled pattern along the stems. Tinus nutans thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its unique appearance and adaptability contribute to its appeal in both natural and cultivated landscapes.
