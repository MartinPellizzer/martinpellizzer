Typha austro-orientalis

Typha austro-orientalis, commonly known as the southern cattail, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in Asia and Australia.

It belongs to the genus Typha in the family Typhaceae and is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescence that typically matures into a brown, mat-like structure. The plant has broad, flat leaves that form a rosette at the base, and it thrives in shallow water or moist soil. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, making it a common species in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Typha austro-orientalis plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
