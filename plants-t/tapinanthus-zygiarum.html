Tapinanthus zygiarum

Tapinanthus zygiarum, commonly known as the parasitic mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the roots of other plants, drawing water and nutrients from its host.

It belongs to the Orobanchaceae family and is characterized by its lack of chlorophyll, which means it cannot perform photosynthesis. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often visited by insects for pollination. Its seeds are dispersed by birds that consume the fleshy, red berries.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can negatively affect the growth and survival of its host plants.
