Teucrium disjunctum

Teucrium disjunctum, commonly known as the Disjunct Germander, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, often clustered in spikes. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its fragrant herbs and medicinal properties.

Teucrium disjunctum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
