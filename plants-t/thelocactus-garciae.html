Thelocactus garciae

Thelocactus garciae, commonly known as the Garcia's thorned cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that are typically greenish-gray in color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits. Thelocactus garciae is adapted to survive in harsh desert conditions with minimal water.

Its unique morphology and habitat make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
