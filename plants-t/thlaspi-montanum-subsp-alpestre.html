Thlaspi montanum subsp. alpestre

Thlaspi montanum subsp.

alpestre, commonly known as alpine cabbages, is a member of the Brassicaceae family. It is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial that typically thrives in alpine and subalpine environments.

The plant features basal rosettes of dark green, glaucous leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in racemes. It is often found in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to cold, harsh climates with limited growing seasons.
