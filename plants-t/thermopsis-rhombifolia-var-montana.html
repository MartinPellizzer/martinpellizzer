Thermopsis rhombifolia var. montana

Thermopsis rhombifolia var. montana, commonly known as mountain thermopsis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features erect, branching stems that are covered in dense, glandular hairs. The leaves are rhombic or diamond-shaped, with a distinctive lobed base, and are typically gray-green in color. Its showy yellow flowers are arranged in racemes and bloom in late spring to early summer.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.
