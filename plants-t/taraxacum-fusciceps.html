Taraxacum fusciceps

Taraxacum fusciceps, commonly known as the brown-headed dandelion, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust root systems. The plant features a basal rosette of broad, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and produce a distinctive, fluffy seed head that disperses seeds via the wind.

Taraxacum fusciceps is often found in disturbed soils and is adaptable to a variety of environmental conditions.
