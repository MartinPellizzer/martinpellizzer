Tingulonga leptostachya

Tingulonga leptostachya, commonly known as the slender-stemmed grass, is a herbaceous plant native to the grasslands of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets, typical of many grass species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in restoration projects to stabilize soil in degraded areas.
