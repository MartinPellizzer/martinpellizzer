Tanacetum petrareum

Tanacetum petrareum, commonly known as the alpine tansy, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and cool climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in flat-topped clusters.

Tanacetum petrareum is known for its strong, camphor-like scent, which is used in traditional herbal remedies.
