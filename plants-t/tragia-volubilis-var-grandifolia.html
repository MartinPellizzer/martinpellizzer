Tragia volubilis var. grandifolia

Tragia volubilis var. grandifolia, commonly known as the giant spider flower, is a climbing shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces striking, showy flowers that are typically red or orange with prominent yellow stamens. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its stems are covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to ecological gardens.
