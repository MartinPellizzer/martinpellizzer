Teucrium capitatum subsp. lusitanicum

Teucrium capitatum subsp.

lusitanicum, commonly known as the Iberian germander, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe, particularly Spain and Portugal. It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

The plant features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its small, clustered flowers are usually blue or violet, appearing in late spring to early summer.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract beneficial insects.
