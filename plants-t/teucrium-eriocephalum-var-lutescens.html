Teucrium eriocephalum var. lutescens

Teucrium eriocephalum var. lutescens, commonly known as yellow woodruff, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its compact growth habit and showy blooms make it a valuable addition to native gardens and ecological landscapes.
