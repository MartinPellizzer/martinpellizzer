Toona ciliata var. candollei

Toona ciliata var. candollei, commonly known as the Australian ash or white ash, is a fast-growing tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and alternate, simple, ovate to lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in panicles, followed by small, round, woody fruits containing seeds. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in open forests and woodlands.

The species is valued for its timber and has some ornamental uses in horticulture.
