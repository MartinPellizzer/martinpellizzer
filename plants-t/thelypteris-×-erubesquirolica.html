Thelypteris × erubesquirolica

Thelypteris × erubesquirolica, commonly known as the red-stemmed shield fern, is a hybrid fern species resulting from the cross between *Thelypteris acutifolia* and *Thelypteris roosii*.

It is characterized by its striking reddish stems and lance-shaped, dark green fronds with a leathery texture. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

The spore-bearing structures, or sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds, a typical feature of the Dryopteridaceae family.
