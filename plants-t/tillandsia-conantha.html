Tillandsia conantha

Tillandsia conantha, commonly known as the silver pine air plant, is a species of bromeliad native to Central and South America.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on other plants without deriving nutrients from them. The plant has a rosette-shaped structure with narrow, silver-gray leaves that are covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help it absorb moisture and nutrients from the air. It produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple, typically blooming in the spring.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
