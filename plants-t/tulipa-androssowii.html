Tulipa androssowii

Tulipa androssowii, commonly known as the Persian tulip, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the genus Tulipa, which includes a variety of bulbous flowering plants in the family Liliaceae. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of red, yellow, and orange, often with a distinctive black eye. Its bulbs are adapted to survive in cold climates, and it blooms in late spring to early summer.
