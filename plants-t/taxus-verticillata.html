Taxus verticillata

Taxus verticillata, commonly known as the Japanese yew, is a slow-growing evergreen shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features dark green, glossy leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stems, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often used in traditional medicine.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
