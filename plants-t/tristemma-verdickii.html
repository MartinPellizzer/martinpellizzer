Tristemma verdickii

Tristemma verdickii, commonly known as the African velvet bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its seeds are encased in hairy, fibrous pods, and the plant is often grown for its edible seeds and nitrogen-fixing properties.

Tristemma verdickii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitat.
