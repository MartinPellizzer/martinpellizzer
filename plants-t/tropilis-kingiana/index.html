Tropilis Kingiana

Tropilis kingiana is a rare and intriguing plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes. It is known for its striking violet and white flowers that bloom during the cooler months. This species thrives in cool, moist environments and requires well-drained soil to flourish. Due to its limited habitat, it is considered endangered and is often found in conservation programs. Its unique appearance and ecological significance make it a subject of interest for botanists and plant enthusiasts alike.
