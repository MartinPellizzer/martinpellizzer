Terebinthus lentiscus

Terebinthus lentiscus, commonly known as the mastic tree, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thick, aromatic bark and dense, leathery leaves that are typically dark green. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by small, spherical fruits. Its most notable feature is the resin it exudes, known as mastic, which has been used for centuries in various applications.

The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
