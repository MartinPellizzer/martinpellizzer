Tecoma chrysotricha var. obtusata

Tecoma chrysotricha var. obtusata, commonly known as the broadleaf yellow bells, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are often broad and ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters.

The flowers are borne on long, slender pedicels, and the plant is known for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
