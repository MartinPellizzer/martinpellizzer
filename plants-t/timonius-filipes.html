Timonius filipes

Timonius filipes, commonly known as the slender feather fern, is a member of the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, slender fronds that are often divided into narrow, thread-like segments. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as along stream banks or in forest understories. Its rhizomes are creeping and help anchor it to the substrate while absorbing nutrients.

Timonius filipes is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it thrives in humid conditions.
