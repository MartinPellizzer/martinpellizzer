Trichosanthes himalensis var. indivisa

Trichosanthes himalensis var. indivisa, commonly known as the Himalayan snake gourd, is a climbing vine native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and is characterized by its long, slender, pale green fruits that resemble snakes. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals and is often cultivated for its edible fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The variety is notable for its distinctive fruit shape and its traditional use in various cuisines.
