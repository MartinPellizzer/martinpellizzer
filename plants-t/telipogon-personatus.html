Telipogon personatus

Telipogon personatus, commonly known as the red-stemmed grass, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with pale to reddish flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and forest margins.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
