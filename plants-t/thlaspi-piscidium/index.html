Thlaspi Piscidium

Thlaspi piscidium, also known as water pennycress, is a small flowering plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found near water sources such as streams and wetlands. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and has heart-shaped leaves that are characteristic of its species. Despite its modest appearance, it plays an important role in supporting pollinators and maintaining ecological balance. Its resilience and adaptability make it a notable example of plant life in challenging environments.
