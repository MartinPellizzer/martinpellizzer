Triplopetalum pinifolium

Triplopetalum pinifolium, commonly known as the needleleaf triplopetalum, is a member of the Ericaceae family and is native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is a shrub characterized by its evergreen foliage, with needle-like leaves that are often arranged in whorls. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which appear in the spring. Its roots are adapted to nutrient-poor soils, making it a hardy species in alpine environments.

The plant is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
