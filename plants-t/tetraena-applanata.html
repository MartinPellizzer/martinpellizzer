Tetraena applanata

Tetraena applanata, commonly known as the flatleaf tetraena, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in coastal areas. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are generally flat and succulent, adapted to retain water in arid environments.

Tetraena applanata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
