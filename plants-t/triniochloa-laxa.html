Triniochloa laxa

Triniochloa laxa, commonly known as the slender marsh grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant has slender, spreading stems that often form dense mats, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a soft, fine texture. Its inflorescence consists of small, spike-like clusters of flowers, which are usually wind-pollinated.

Triniochloa laxa plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
