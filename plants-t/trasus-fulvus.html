Trasus fulvus

Trasus fulvus, commonly known as the golden spider lily, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range from yellow to orange in color. The plant features strap-like leaves that are broad and pointed, often growing in a basal rosette. Trasus fulvus thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for botanical gardens and private collections.
