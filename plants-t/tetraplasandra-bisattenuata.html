Tetraplasandra bisattenuata

Tetraplasandra bisattenuata, commonly known as the broad-leafed hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange, which bloom throughout the warmer months. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
