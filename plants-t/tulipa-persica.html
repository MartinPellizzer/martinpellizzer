Tulipa persica

Tulipa persica, commonly known as the Persian tulip, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the genus Tulipa within the family Liliaceae, characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers. The plant produces striking, single or double blooms in shades of red, pink, and white, often with a distinctive black eye in the center. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and historical significance, it is cultivated in many botanical gardens around the world.
