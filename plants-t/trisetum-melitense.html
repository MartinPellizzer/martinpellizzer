Trisetum melitense

Trisetum melitense, commonly known as the Malta grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and rocky habitats.

Trisetum melitense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
