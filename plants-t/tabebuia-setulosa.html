Tabebuia setulosa

Tabebuia setulosa, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery orange to red flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant has a straight, often slender trunk with a smooth, greyish-brown bark. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a pointed tip.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often planted in gardens and along streets for its vibrant floral display.
