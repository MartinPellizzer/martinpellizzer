Thymus caucasicus var. medvedewii

Thymus caucasicus var. medvedewii, commonly known as Caucasian thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It features aromatic, needle-like leaves and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and is often found in rocky or limestone soils. It is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.

This variety is closely related to other thyme species but is distinguished by its unique floral structure and habitat preferences.
