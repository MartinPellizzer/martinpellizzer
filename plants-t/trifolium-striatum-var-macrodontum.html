Trifolium striatum var. macrodontum

Trifolium striatum var. macrodontum, commonly known as the large-toothed clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each consisting of three oval to lance-shaped leaflets with serrated edges. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or red, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
