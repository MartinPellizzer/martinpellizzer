Tulipa goulimyi

Tulipa goulimyi, commonly known as the Goulimy tulip, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep red, often with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows to a height of 20 to 40 centimeters, with narrow, grass-like leaves. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, thriving in temperate climates.

Tulipa goulimyi is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
