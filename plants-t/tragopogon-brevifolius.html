Tragopogon brevifolius

Tragopogon brevifolius, commonly known as the short-leaved thistle, is a flowering plant in the dandelion family (Compositae).

It is native to the western United States and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves. The plant produces a single, tall flowering stem with yellow-orange ray flowers surrounded by a purple-tipped involucral bracts. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Tragopogon brevifolius is often mistaken for other thistle species due to its similar appearance and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
