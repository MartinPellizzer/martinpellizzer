Thelocactus leucacanthus var. ehrenbergii

Thelocactus leucacanthus var. ehrenbergii, commonly known as the Ehrenberg's white spined cactus, is a rare and striking cactus native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in white, woolly spines that provide insulation and protection from intense sunlight. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a vibrant contrast to its otherwise muted appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic environments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
