Taraxacum staturale

Taraxacum staturale, commonly known as the upright dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves that are typically lanceolate and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces a single, hollow, leaf-like stem that supports a flower head composed of yellow ray florets. It is native to Europe and has been introduced to other regions, often growing in disturbed soils and open habitats.

Taraxacum staturale is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in meadows and along roadsides.
