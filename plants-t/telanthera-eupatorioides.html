Telanthera eupatorioides

Telanthera eupatorioides, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, tubular purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and it produces a distinctive, spiky seed head resembling a cone. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Telanthera eupatorioides is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
