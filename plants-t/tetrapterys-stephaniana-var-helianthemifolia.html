Tetrapterys stephaniana var. helianthemifolia

Tetrapterys stephaniana var. helianthemifolia, commonly known as the goldenleaf tree, is a deciduous shrub native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape and often have a slightly serrated margin. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed sites.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
