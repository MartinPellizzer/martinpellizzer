Tagetes dichotoma

Tagetes dichotoma, commonly known as the Mexican marigold or the desert marigold, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its yellow to orange flowers and pinnately divided leaves. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to drought conditions. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Tagetes dichotoma is often used in traditional medicine and as a ornamental plant due to its vibrant color and hardy nature.
