Tapeinosperma robustum

Tapeinosperma robustum, commonly known as the African wild date, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, evergreen shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky soils. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody, fleshy fruits resembling dates. These fruits are edible and have a sweet, fibrous texture, making them a food source for both wildlife and humans.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
