Tetrastigma sinodichotomum

Tetrastigma sinodichotomum, commonly known as the Chinese crossvine, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and tendrils that aid in climbing. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruits are berries that mature to a reddish color, containing several seeds.

Tetrastigma sinodichotomum is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and vigorous growth habit.
