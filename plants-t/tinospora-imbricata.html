Tinospora imbricata

Tinospora imbricata, commonly known as the Indian star gooseberry, is a climbing shrub belonging to the Menispermaceae family.

It is native to South and Southeast Asia and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces bright red berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Tinospora imbricata is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in Ayurvedic and folk remedies.
