Trochodendron

Trochodendron, commonly known as the twisted wood or mountain cedar, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Trochodendraceae.

It is characterized by its unique spiral arrangement of leaves, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant is native to East Asia, particularly China, Japan, and Korea, and thrives in temperate climates. Trochodendron species are evergreen trees or shrubs with thick, leathery leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers.

These plants are of interest to botanists due to their ancient lineage and evolutionary significance.
