Tinus lanceolata

Tinus lanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tinus, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in loose clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Tinus lanceolata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
