Taraxacum lyngeanum

Taraxacum lyngeanum, commonly known as the Lynge dandelion, is a flowering plant species native to northern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy structure. The plant features a rosette of spoon-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically green with a waxy coating. Its yellow flowers develop into fluffy white seed heads, which are dispersed by the wind.

This species is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, showcasing its adaptability to various environments.
