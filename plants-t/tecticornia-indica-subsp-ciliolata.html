Tecticornia indica subsp. ciliolata

Tecticornia indica subsp.

ciliolata, commonly known as the coastal spinifex, is a halophytic shrub adapted to saline and arid environments. It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its dense, prostrate growth habit with small, fleshy leaves.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers in compact clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. It is native to coastal regions of Australia and thrives in sandy soils with high salt content.

This species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal dunes and providing habitat for native fauna.
