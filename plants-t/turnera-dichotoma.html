Turnera dichotoma

Turnera dichotoma, commonly known as the "Mexican buttercup," is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in shaded or semi-shaded environments. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its flowers are yellow with prominent yellow stamens. It produces small, round fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky, resinous substance.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
