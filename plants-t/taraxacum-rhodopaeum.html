Taraxacum rhodopaeum

Taraxacum rhodopaeum, commonly known as the Rhodope dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Rhodope Mountains in Bulgaria.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust root systems. The plant features a rosette of spoon-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. Its flowering heads produce small, yellow disk flowers surrounded by white ray florets, typical of the dandelion genus.

Taraxacum rhodopaeum is adapted to rocky and dry environments, showcasing its resilience in challenging conditions.
