Tristerix Grandiflorus

Tristerix grandiflorus is a rare and striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes. It is known for its large, showy white flowers that bloom during the short summer season. This species thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found growing in rocky crevices. Due to its limited habitat and slow growth rate, it is considered vulnerable to environmental changes. Its unique appearance and ecological significance make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists alike.
