Tamarix aegyptiaca

Tamarix aegyptiaca, commonly known as the Egyptian tamarisk, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of North Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its dense, feathery foliage and small, pink or pale red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has small, scale-like leaves that help reduce water loss in hot, dry climates. It produces woody pods that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Tamarix aegyptiaca is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ability to stabilize soil in sandy environments.
