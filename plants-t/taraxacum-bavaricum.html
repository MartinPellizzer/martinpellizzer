Taraxacum bavaricum

Taraxacum bavaricum, commonly known as the Bavarian dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and a hollow, leafless stem that supports a single, yellow flower head. The plant produces a distinctive seed dispersal mechanism through a pappus, which allows the seeds to be carried by the wind. It is native to Central Europe and thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats.

Taraxacum bavaricum is often found in meadows, roadsides, and other sunny, well-drained areas.
