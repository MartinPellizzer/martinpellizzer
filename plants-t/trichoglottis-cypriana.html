Trichoglottis cypriana

Trichoglottis cypriana, commonly known as the Cyprus orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Cyprus.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, enabling it to thrive in arid conditions. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, while the flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish-white in color.

Trichoglottis cypriana is a rare and endangered species, highlighting the importance of conservation efforts in its native habitat.
