Thymus ciliatopubescens var. bistrae

Thymus ciliatopubescens var. bistrae, commonly known as the Bistrae thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It features compact, low-growing shrubs with aromatic, gray-green foliage covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to violet, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are widely used in culinary applications for their distinctive, peppery aroma.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to rocky or well-drained soils.
