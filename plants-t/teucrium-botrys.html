Teucrium botrys

Teucrium botrys, commonly known as the clustered germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks. Its aromatic leaves and flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Teucrium botrys is also valued for its medicinal properties, historically used to treat various ailments.
