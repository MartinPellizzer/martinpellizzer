Thornbera ordiae

Thornbera ordiae, commonly known as the Malabar thornapple, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its spiny, woody stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer season. Thornbera ordiae is often found in moist, shaded areas of forests and is adapted to humid tropical climates.

It is considered a valuable species for its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties.
