Trifolium pratense var. rhodopeum

Trifolium pratense var. rhodopeum, commonly known as the Rhodope red clover, is a variant of red clover native to southeastern Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive pink to reddish flowers and trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.

The variant is also noted for its role in soil nitrogen fixation, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
