Thalictrum lacerostipellatum

Thalictrum lacerostipellatum, commonly known as the fringed meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its delicate appearance and unique foliage make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
