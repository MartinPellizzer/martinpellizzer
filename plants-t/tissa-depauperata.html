Tissa depauperata

Tissa depauperata, commonly known as the poor leaf tree, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves that are usually pale green and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by small, round fruits containing a single seed. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
