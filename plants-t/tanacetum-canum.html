Tanacetum canum

Tanacetum canum, commonly known as narrowleaf tansy, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and is characterized by its finely divided, gray-green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that are surrounded by pale yellow ray florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry meadows and rocky areas.

Tanacetum canum is known for its aromatic properties and has been used historically in traditional medicine.
