Tetraclea coulteri var. angustifolia

Tetraclea coulteri var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf tetraclea, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and heat. Its botanical features include a woody stem and a distinctive leaf arrangement that aids in reducing water loss.

This variety is often found in chaparral and scrub habitats, contributing to local biodiversity.
