Trifolium pratense f. lilacinum

Trifolium pratense f. lilacinum, commonly known as the lilac clover, is a botanical variant of red clover.

It is characterized by its distinctive lilac-colored flowers, which contrast with the typical pink or red hues of the main species. This subspecies is often found in grasslands and meadows, thriving in well-drained soils. Its trifoliate leaves consist of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it is known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it valuable in agricultural settings.

The plant is also appreciated for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in landscaping.
