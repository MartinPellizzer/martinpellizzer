Trichophorum uniflorum

Trichophorum uniflorum, commonly known as the single-flower sedge, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and a single spikelet of flowers at the top of its stem. This plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and wet meadows. It has small, inconspicuous flowers that are often wind-pollinated.

Trichophorum uniflorum is generally found in temperate regions and plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
