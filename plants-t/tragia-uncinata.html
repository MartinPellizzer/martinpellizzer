Tragia uncinata

Tragia uncinata, commonly known as the hooked tragia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual with hairy, branched stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a rough texture.

Tragia uncinata is adapted to dry environments and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.
