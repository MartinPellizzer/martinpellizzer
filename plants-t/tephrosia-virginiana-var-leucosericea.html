Tephrosia virginiana var. leucosericea

Tephrosia virginiana var. leucosericea, commonly known as white silky tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its finely hairy, silvery-green foliage and small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. Its unique appearance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for native gardens and restoration projects.

It also plays a role in supporting local pollinators and wildlife.
