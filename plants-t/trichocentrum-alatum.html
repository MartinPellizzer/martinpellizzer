Trichocentrum alatum

Trichocentrum alatum, commonly known as the spreading lady's slipper orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and striking, showy flowers that resemble a lady's slipper. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with a distinctive purple lip, and it blooms during the rainy season.

Trichocentrum alatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm, humid environments.
