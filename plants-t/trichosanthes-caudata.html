Trichosanthes caudata

Trichosanthes caudata, commonly known as the Chinese trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, orange-yellow flowers that resemble trumpets, which bloom in summer. The plant produces a fleshy, orange fruit that contains numerous seeds. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are hairy and trailing.

Trichosanthes caudata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional medicinal uses in some cultures.
