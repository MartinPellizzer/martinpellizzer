Trigonochilum tricorne

Trigonochilum tricorne, commonly known as the three-cornered orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive triangular-shaped flowers, which are typically greenish-yellow with a prominent central stripe. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with each stem producing a new growth from a basal node. Its leaves are usually alternate, ovate, and have a waxy texture.

Trigonochilum tricorne is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and rocky habitats.
