Thyrsostachys regia

Thyrsostachys regia, commonly known as the giant feather grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, feathery plumes that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in high-altitude meadows and rocky areas, thriving in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a silver-blue hue that adds to its ornamental value.

Thyrsostachys regia is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in mountainous regions.
