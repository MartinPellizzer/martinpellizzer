Tulista margaritifera

Tulista margaritifera, commonly known as the pearlflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, white, bell-shaped flowers that resemble small pearls, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spherical fruits that contain seeds.

Tulista margaritifera is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
