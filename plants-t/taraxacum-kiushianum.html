Taraxacum kiushianum

Taraxacum kiushianum, commonly known as the Korean dandelion, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Korea and is characterized by its basal rosette of dark green, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces a single, hollow stem that supports a flower head composed of yellow ray florets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
