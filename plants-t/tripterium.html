Tripterium

Tripterium, commonly known as the three-leaved plant, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Ericaceae.

It is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves that are typically arranged in a whorl of three at each node. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are often pink or white in color. Tripterium species are typically found in acidic, well-drained soils in temperate regions.
