Tetrastigma gilgianum

Tetrastigma gilgianum, commonly known as the Gilgian grapevine, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its twining stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by small, edible berries. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in humid, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
