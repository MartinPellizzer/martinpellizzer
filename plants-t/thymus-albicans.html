Thymus albicans

Thymus albicans, commonly known as white thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its small, gray-green leaves and fragrant, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses. Thymus albicans thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its essential oils, rich in thymol, are often used in aromatherapy and natural remedies.
