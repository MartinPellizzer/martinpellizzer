Thymus gobi-altaicus

Thymus gobi-altaicus, commonly known as the Gobi thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and square stems. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, adapting to the harsh, dry conditions of its habitat.

Thymus gobi-altaicus is well-suited to rocky and sandy soils, making it a hardy species found in high-altitude environments.
