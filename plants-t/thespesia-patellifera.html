Thespesia patellifera

Thespesia patellifera, commonly known as the Hawaiian hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Pacific Islands.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 5 meters in height, featuring broad, leathery leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The flowers are usually pink or red, with five petals and a prominent yellow center, and bloom throughout the year. It thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and well-drained soils.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in Polynesian culture.
