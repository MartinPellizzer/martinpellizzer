Tamarindus somalensis

Tamarindus somalensis, commonly known as the Somali tamarind, is a small to medium-sized tree native to the Horn of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellowish flowers. The tree produces pods containing edible seeds encased in a sour pulp, similar to the common tamarind. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it an important species in local ecosystems.

The plant is also valued for its timber and traditional medicinal uses.
