Taraxacum cornigerum

Taraxacum cornigerum, commonly known as the hairy dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and distinctive hairy stems. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grassy areas. It has heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs.

The plant produces a fluffy seed head that disperses seeds via the wind, aiding in its propagation.
