Teucrium flavum subsp. oxylepis

Teucrium flavum subsp.

oxylepis, commonly known as the yellow germander, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showing a preference for well-drained soils. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, known for its square stems and aromatic properties. Teucrium flavum subsp.

oxylepis is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
