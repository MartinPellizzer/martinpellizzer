Taraxacum fabbeanum

Taraxacum fabbeanum, commonly known as the Italian dandelion, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of spoon-shaped, hairy leaves and a hollow, leafy stem that supports a single, yellow flower head. The plant produces a distinctive seed head composed of numerous parachute-like seeds, which are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows, lawns, and disturbed soils.

Taraxacum fabbeanum is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its diuretic and anti-inflammatory effects.
