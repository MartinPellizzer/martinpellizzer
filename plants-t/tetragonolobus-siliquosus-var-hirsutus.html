Tetragonolobus siliquosus var. hirsutus

Tetragonolobus siliquosus var. hirsutus, commonly known as the hairy siliquous tetragonolobus, is a perennial legume native to parts of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its quadrangular stems and small, clustered leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and savanna habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
