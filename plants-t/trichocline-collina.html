Trichocline collina

Trichocline collina, commonly known as the mountain trichocline, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine regions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
