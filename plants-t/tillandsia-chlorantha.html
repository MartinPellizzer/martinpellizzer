Tillandsia chlorantha

Tillandsia chlorantha, commonly known as the silver leaf air plant, is a species of bromeliad native to Central and South America.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on other plants without being parasitic, often found in tropical forests. The plant has a rosette-shaped structure with narrow, silver-green leaves that are covered in a fine, fuzzy layer. Its leaves are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air, allowing it to thrive in humid environments.

Tillandsia chlorantha produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a delicate touch to its appearance.
