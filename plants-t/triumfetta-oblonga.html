Triumfetta oblonga

Triumfetta oblonga, commonly known as the African bluebell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual that grows up to 1 meter in height, featuring simple, alternate leaves that are oblong and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in warm climates.

Triumfetta oblonga is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
