Tropaeolum warmingianum subsp. occidentale

Tropaeolum warmingianum subsp.

occidentale, commonly known as the "Mexican flame flower," is a herbaceous annual plant native to Central America. It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from red to orange, often resembling miniature lanterns.

The plant produces edible, peppery-tasting tubular seeds that are sometimes used in culinary applications. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and branching.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
