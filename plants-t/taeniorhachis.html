Taeniorhachis

Taeniorhachis, commonly known as the spiky grass or spikegrass, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, spike-like inflorescences that are often adorned with long, bristle-like bracts. The plants are typically herbaceous, with narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette. Taeniorhachis species are native to various regions of Africa, particularly in arid and semi-arid environments.

These plants are often used in traditional medicine and are also valued for their ornamental appearance in landscaping.
