Thiollierea macrophylla

Thiollierea macrophylla, commonly known as the giant bromeliad, is a striking plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its large, waxy, strap-like leaves that form a rosette shape, creating a water-holding structure. The plant's leaves are typically a deep green with a glossy texture, and they can grow up to several meters in length. It produces showy, red-orange flowers that bloom in a spike at the center of the rosette.

Thiollierea macrophylla is well adapted to humid, shaded environments and requires high levels of light and moisture to thrive.
