Teucrium cylindricum subvar. wilczekii

Teucrium cylindricum subvar. wilczekii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved germander, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features slender, cylindrical stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the branches. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
