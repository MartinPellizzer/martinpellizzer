Turnera luminosa

Turnera luminosa, commonly known as the "star jasmine" or "lightbearer," is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in shaded, moist environments. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, star-shaped white flowers that bloom in clusters, often at night. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a waxy texture, while its stems are often covered in fine hairs.

Turnera luminosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its aromatic properties.
