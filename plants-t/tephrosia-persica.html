Tephrosia persica

Tephrosia persica, commonly known as Persian tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to regions of Asia, including Iran and Turkey.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or bean family, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain small, hard seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

Tephrosia persica is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for agricultural purposes.
