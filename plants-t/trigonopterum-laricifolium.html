Trigonopterum laricifolium

Trigonopterum laricifolium, commonly known as the white pine lichen, is a foliose lichen that grows on the bark of coniferous trees, particularly white pines.

It has a pale to bright green thallus with a slightly wrinkled surface and a distinct, often lobed margin. The lichen contains various secondary metabolites that contribute to its ecological role in forest ecosystems. It is commonly found in temperate regions where white pines are prevalent.

Its slow growth and specialized habitat make it an indicator species for certain environmental conditions.
