Tupa sonchifolia

Tupa sonchifolia, commonly known as the yellow wood sorrel, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to various regions in South America, particularly the Andean foothills. The plant features alternate, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive yellowish-green color and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters. Tupa sonchifolia is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and rocky areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting subject for both ecological studies and horticultural cultivation.
