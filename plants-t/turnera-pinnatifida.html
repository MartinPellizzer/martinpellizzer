Turnera pinnatifida

Turnera pinnatifida, commonly known as the "Mexican flameflower," is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and opposite, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces showy yellow or orange flowers, which are often clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its leaves are usually trifoliate or pinnately divided, with a rough, hairy texture.

Turnera pinnatifida is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
