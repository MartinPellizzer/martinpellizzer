Trichoscypha liberica

Trichoscypha liberica, commonly known as the African coffee tree, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a glossy green surface. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in panicles, while the fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds.

The plant is primarily cultivated for its seeds, which are used to produce coffee beans.
