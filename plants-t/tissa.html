Tissa

Tissa, commonly known as the "sandalwood tree," is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic heartwood, which is highly valued for its fragrance and durability. The plant has smooth, dark green leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Tissa is also known for its slow growth and long lifespan, often reaching several decades in age.

Its botanical significance lies in its role in traditional medicine and its use in religious and cultural practices.
