Tium mokiacense

Tium mokiacense, commonly known as the Mokiacense tree, is a species native to specific regions in Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought conditions. Its bark is often rough and fissured, providing structural support and protection against environmental stressors.

The tree plays a significant ecological role in its native habitat, supporting various wildlife species and contributing to local biodiversity.
