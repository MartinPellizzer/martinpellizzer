Teucrium virginicum

Teucrium virginicum, commonly known as blue germander, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, blue to purple flowers and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in woodland or wetland habitats. It produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Blue germander is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
