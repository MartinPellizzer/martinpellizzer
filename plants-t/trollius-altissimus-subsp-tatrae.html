Trollius altissimus subsp. tatrae

Trollius altissimus subsp.

tatrae, commonly known as the Tatra marsh gentian, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Tatra Mountains. It features showy, yellow, saucer-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant typically grows in moist, nutrient-rich soils in alpine meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are broadly ovate with a waxy surface, and it has a short, sturdy stem.

This subspecies is adapted to cold, mountainous climates and is often found in areas with limited sunlight.
