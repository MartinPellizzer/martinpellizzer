Teucrium incanum subsp. nuristanicum

Teucrium incanum subsp.

nuristanicum, commonly known as the Nuristan germander, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving at high altitudes. Its small, tubular flowers are usually blue or purple, and it produces small, four-angled fruits.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
