Trichanthemis afghanica

Trichanthemis afghanica, commonly known as Afghan groundsel, is a flowering plant native to regions of Central and South Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, and its seeds are dispersed by wind.

Trichanthemis afghanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
