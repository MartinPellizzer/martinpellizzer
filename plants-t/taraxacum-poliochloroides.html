Taraxacum poliochloroides

Taraxacum poliochloroides, commonly known as the pale-green dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers and heart-shaped basal leaves. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grassy areas. It produces a distinctive seed head known as a pappus, which aids in wind dispersal.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
