Tithymalus seguierianus subsp. saxicola

Tithymalus seguierianus subsp.

saxicola, commonly known as the rock tithymalus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often on slopes or in crevices, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments.

The plant features wiry stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, which bloom during the cooler months. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a fine layer of hair, helping to reduce water loss.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
