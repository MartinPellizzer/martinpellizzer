Thaumatophyllum tweedieanum

Thaumatophyllum tweedieanum, commonly known as the fairy pine, is a unique gymnosperm native to the island of Trinidad.

It is characterized by its unusual morphology, featuring a woody trunk with a crown of needle-like leaves that resemble pine needles. The plant exhibits a distinctive growth pattern, often growing in a spiral or helical form, which is rare among conifers. Its reproductive structures include small, cone-like cones that produce seeds, typical of gymnosperms.

Despite its tropical origin, it is adapted to thrive in moist, shaded environments, making it a fascinating subject for botanical study.
