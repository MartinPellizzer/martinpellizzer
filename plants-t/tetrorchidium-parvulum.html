Tetrorchidium parvulum

Tetrorchidium parvulum, commonly known as the small orchid, is a small, terrestrial orchid native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features a slender, branching stem with several small, green leaves. Its flowers are pale green to white, with a distinctive three-lobed lip, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is a rare and endangered species, requiring specific habitat conditions for successful growth.
