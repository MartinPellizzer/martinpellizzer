Tragacantha sparsa

Tragacantha sparsa, commonly known as the sparse tragacanth, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, pale green leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence. The plant produces yellowish flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its sap, known as tragacanth, is a viscous substance used in traditional medicine and as a natural gum in various industries.

Tragacantha sparsa thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, arid environments.
