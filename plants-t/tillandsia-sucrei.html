Tillandsia sucrei

Tillandsia sucrei, commonly known as the Sucre's air plant, is a species of bromeliad native to South America.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on other plants without deriving nutrients from them. The plant's rosette-shaped leaves are covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help absorb moisture and nutrients from the air. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires indirect sunlight.

Tillandsia sucrei is popular as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
