Tournefortia argusia

Tournefortia argusia, commonly known as the Florida beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters. The plant produces clusters of small, violet-blue berries that are a striking feature, especially in the fall. Its leaves are elliptical, with a leathery texture and a glossy appearance.

Tournefortia argusia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and wetlands.
