Trillium underwoodii

Trillium underwoodii, commonly known as the Underwood's trillium, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Trillium genus, which is characterized by its three-part leaves, three sepals, and three petals. This species typically grows in shaded, moist woodlands, thriving in rich, well-drained soil. Its flowers, which bloom in early spring, are usually white or pale green and are followed by red berries in the fall.

Due to habitat loss and over-picking, it is considered endangered in many areas, making conservation efforts crucial for its survival.
