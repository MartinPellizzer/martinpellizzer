Tulipa sylvestris f. scappuccii

Tulipa sylvestris f. scappuccii, commonly known as the Scappuccii wild tulip, is a botanical variant of the wild tulip species.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pale pink to lilac-colored flowers with a white throat and a yellow center. The plant typically grows in woodland or meadow environments, thriving in well-drained soil. It is a perennial bulbous plant that produces strap-like leaves and blooms in early spring.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
