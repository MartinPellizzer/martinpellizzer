Tulipa paradasystemon

Tulipa paradasystemon, commonly known as the Paradox tulip, is a species native to Turkey.

It is characterized by its unique, often asymmetrical flowers that display a striking blend of colors. The plant typically grows to a height of 30-60 cm and features narrow, grass-like leaves. Its bulbs are adapted to thrive in well-drained soil and full sun conditions.

This tulip is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
