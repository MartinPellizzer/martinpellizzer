Trichouratea glabrescens

Trichouratea glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth-leaved trichouratea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically smooth and glabrous. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom during the rainy season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in horticultural settings.
