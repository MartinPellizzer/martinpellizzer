Teucrium hyssopifolium

Teucrium hyssopifolium, commonly known as blue germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems covered with fine, hairy foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, sky-blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly aromatic scent when crushed.

Teucrium hyssopifolium is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
