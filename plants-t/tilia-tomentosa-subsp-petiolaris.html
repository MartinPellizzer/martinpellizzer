Tilia tomentosa subsp. petiolaris

Tilia tomentosa subsp.

petiolaris, commonly known as the woolly poplar or petiolaris linden, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America. It is characterized by its greyish-brown, rough bark and heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense, white pubescence.

The flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in drooping inflorescences, typical of the Tilia genus. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones.

This subspecies is notable for its rapid growth and historical use in timber and medicinal applications.
