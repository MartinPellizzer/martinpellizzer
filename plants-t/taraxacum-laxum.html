Taraxacum laxum

Taraxacum laxum, commonly known as the looseleaf dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, spoon-shaped leaves and a hollow, leafless stem that supports a single, yellow flower head. The plant is native to regions of Europe and western Asia, often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. It produces a distinctive seed head composed of numerous lightweight, wind-dispersed seeds with white pappus.

Taraxacum laxum is typically a perennial herb that thrives in temperate climates.
