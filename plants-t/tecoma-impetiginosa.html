Tecoma impetiginosa

Tecoma impetiginosa, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters. The plant produces clusters of yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a velvety texture, giving the plant its common name.

Tecoma impetiginosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
