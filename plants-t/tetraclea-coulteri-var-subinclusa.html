Tetraclea coulteri var. subinclusa

Tetraclea coulteri var. subinclusa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tetraclea, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with a distinctive papilionaceous structure typical of the legume family.
