Taraxacum platypecidum

Taraxacum platypecidum, commonly known as the flat-leaf dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant features a rosette of basal, spoon-shaped leaves that are usually hairy and have a bluish-green hue. It produces a yellow flower head that develops into a hollow, spherical seed sphere with bristling parachute-like seeds.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a common weed in agricultural and urban environments.
