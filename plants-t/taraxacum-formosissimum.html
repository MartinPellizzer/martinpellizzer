Taraxacum formosissimum

Taraxacum formosissimum, commonly known as the showy dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to eastern North America and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and deeply lobed leaves. The plant produces a distinctive seed head known as a pappus, which aids in wind dispersal. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including meadows, roadsides, and disturbed soils.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its ornamental appeal.
