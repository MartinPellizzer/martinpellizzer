Turnera oculata

Turnera oculata, commonly known as the "eyes of the spider" or "flower of the spider," is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in shaded or semi-shaded environments. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its most distinctive feature is its clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring and summer.

The plant is also known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
