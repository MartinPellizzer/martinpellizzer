Thelypodium rhomboideum var. gracilipes

Thelypodium rhomboideum var. gracilipes, commonly known as slenderleaf thelypodium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, clustered flowers are usually pink or purple and bloom in late spring to early summer.

The species is characterized by its delicate appearance and is often found in rich, loamy soils.
