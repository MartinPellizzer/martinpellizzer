Taeniophyllum confusum

Taeniophyllum confusum, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic epiphytic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its lack of leaves and its unique, delicate, white or pale green flowers. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on tree branches in tropical rainforests. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain, and it is highly sensitive to environmental changes.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern in many regions.
