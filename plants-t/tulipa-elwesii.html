Tulipa elwesii

Tulipa elwesii, commonly known as the Elwes tulip, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial bulbous plant that produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically blooms in early spring and is characterized by its sturdy, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Tulipa elwesii is valued for its vibrant colors and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
