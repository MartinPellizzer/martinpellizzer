Taraxacum pseudodissimile f. maculatum

Taraxacum pseudodissimile f. maculatum, commonly known as the spotted dandelion, is a botanical variant of the dandelion species.

It is characterized by its distinctive spotted leaves and yellow flowers, which are typical of the genus Taraxacum. The plant is a perennial herb that thrives in a variety of soil types and light conditions. Its flowers produce fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind, aiding in its propagation.

The spotted leaves are a notable feature that distinguishes this form from other dandelion varieties.
