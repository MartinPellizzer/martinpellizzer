Triticum vulgare var. saisette

Triticum vulgare var. saisette, commonly known as French wheat, is a variety of wheat native to France.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and shorter stature compared to other wheat varieties. The plant produces spikelets that bear small, hard grains, which are typically used for flour production. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its adaptability and historical significance in European agriculture.
