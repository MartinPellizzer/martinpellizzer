Tragacantha corniculata

Tragacantha corniculata, commonly known as the horned tragacanth, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, branched stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces a viscous resin, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a sizing agent for textiles. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, often growing in rocky or arid environments.

Its unique morphology and historical significance make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
