Teucrium nudicaule

Teucrium nudicaule, commonly known as the slender germander, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, opposite leaves that are typically gray-green in color, covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale blue or violet flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
