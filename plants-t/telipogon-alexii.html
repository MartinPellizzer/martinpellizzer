Telipogon alexii

Telipogon alexii, commonly known as the Alexander's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid with a compact growth habit, typically found in rocky or alpine environments. The plant produces delicate, greenish-white flowers with a distinctive lip that is often adorned with intricate patterns. It thrives in cool, moist conditions and is adapted to the harsh climate of its mountainous habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and specialized ecological requirements, Telipogon alexii is considered a rare and endangered species.
