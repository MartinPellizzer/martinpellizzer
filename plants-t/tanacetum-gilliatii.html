Tanacetum gilliatii

Tanacetum gilliatii, commonly known as the alpine tansy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. The plant produces small, yellow composite flowers that are arranged in flat-topped clusters, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are aromatic and often have a strong, camphor-like scent.

Tanacetum gilliatii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
