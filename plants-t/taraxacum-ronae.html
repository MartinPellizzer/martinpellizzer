Taraxacum ronae

Taraxacum ronae, commonly known as the Ronae dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family (Asteraceae).

It is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated, hairy leaves and a yellow composite flower head that develops into a white pappus-covered seed head. Native to Central Europe, it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant is perennial, with a taproot system that allows it to survive in various environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local flora and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
