Taraxacum pergracile

Taraxacum pergracile, commonly known as the slender dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, grass-like leaves and a single, yellow flower that develops into a white, fluffy seed head. This plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and disturbed soils. It thrives in temperate climates and is known for its ability to spread rapidly through wind-dispersed seeds.

The plant has a taproot system and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
