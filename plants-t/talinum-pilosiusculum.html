Talinum pilosiusculum

Talinum pilosiusculum, commonly known as velvetleaf spinach, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of moist to semi-shade conditions.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and sometimes invasive species in certain ecosystems.
