Tafalla

Tafalla, commonly known as the "Spanish rose," is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its pink or white petals and thorny stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its small, fragrant flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Tafalla is often used in ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
