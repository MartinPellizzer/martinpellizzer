Taraxacum californicum

Taraxacum californicum, commonly known as the California dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces a single, bright yellow flower that develops into a spherical seed head with white pappus for wind dispersal. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is considered an important pioneer species in ecological succession.
