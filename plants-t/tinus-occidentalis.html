Tinus occidentalis

Tinus occidentalis, commonly known as the western tinus, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family, which includes a variety of shrubs and trees known for their distinctive floral structures. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its ecological importance and aesthetic value, it is often cultivated in native plant gardens and conservation areas.
