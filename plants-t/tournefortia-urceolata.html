Tournefortia urceolata

Tournefortia urceolata, commonly known as the cup-shaped tournefortia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal areas and saline soils. The plant features simple, opposite leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in umbels. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Tournefortia urceolata is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to coastal environments.
