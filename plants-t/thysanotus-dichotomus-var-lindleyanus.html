Thysanotus dichotomus var. lindleyanus

Thysanotus dichotomus var. lindleyanus, commonly known as the Lindley's sweet pepper, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features opposite, linear leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale blue flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several small, kidney-shaped seeds.

The species is characterized by its dichotomous branching pattern and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
