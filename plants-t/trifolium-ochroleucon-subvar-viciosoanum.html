Trifolium ochroleucon subvar. viciosoanum

Trifolium ochroleucon subvar. viciosoanum, commonly known as the yellow clover variant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets with a distinctive yellowish-green color. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed areas.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
