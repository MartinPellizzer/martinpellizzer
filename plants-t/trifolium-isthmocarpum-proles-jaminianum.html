Trifolium isthmocarpum proles jaminianum

Trifolium isthmocarpum proles jaminianum, commonly known as the Jaminian's narrow-carpel clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged on a common stalk. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in grassy areas or along roadsides.

This species is notable for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.
