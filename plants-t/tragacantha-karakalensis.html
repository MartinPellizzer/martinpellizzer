Tragacantha karakalensis

Tragacantha karakalensis, commonly known as the Karakal Persian gum tree, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its woody, branching structure and produces distinctive yellow or orange flowers during the spring. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in harsh, dry conditions, making it well-adapted to its native environment. Its bark and gum have been traditionally used in various medicinal and industrial applications.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils in arid landscapes.
