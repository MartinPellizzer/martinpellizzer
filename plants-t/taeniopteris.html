Taeniopteris

Taeniopteris, commonly known as the shield fern, is a genus of ferns belonging to the family Tectariaceae.

These plants are characterized by their distinctive, shield-shaped fronds that are often divided into segments with a waxy coating. Taeniopteris species are typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in moist, shaded environments. Their rhizomes are often thick and creeping, allowing them to spread and form dense colonies.

The spores are produced in sori that are usually arranged in a row along the margins of the fronds.
