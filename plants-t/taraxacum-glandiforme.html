Taraxacum glandiforme

Taraxacum glandiforme, commonly known as the glandular dandelion, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a rosette of basal, hairy leaves that are glandular-dotted, giving it its scientific name. Its yellow flower head consists of numerous tiny florets that develop into parachute-like seeds.

Taraxacum glandiforme is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to thrive in temperate climates.
