Trochera capensis

Trochera capensis, commonly known as the Cape orchid, is a perennial herb native to the coastal regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of small, white or pale pink blossoms that bloom in spring. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to the sandy soils of its natural habitat.

Trochera capensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
