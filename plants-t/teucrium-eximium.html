Teucrium eximium

Teucrium eximium, commonly known as the purple germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, branched stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in rocky or dry habitats.

Teucrium eximium is also valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
