Trophaeum aduncum

Trophaeum aduncum, commonly known as the slender sandwort, is a small, herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. The plant has delicate, prostrate stems that often root at the nodes, allowing it to spread across the ground. Its tiny, white or pale pink flowers appear in clusters and bloom in the spring.

Trophaeum aduncum plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is often found in desert or coastal environments.
