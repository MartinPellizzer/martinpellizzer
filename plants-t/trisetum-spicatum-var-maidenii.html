Trisetum spicatum var. maidenii

Trisetum spicatum var. maidenii, commonly known as Maiden's feather grass, is a perennial grass native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and dense, feathery inflorescences that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a soft, silvery-blue hue that adds ornamental value to gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits, supporting local wildlife and providing habitat in natural landscapes.
