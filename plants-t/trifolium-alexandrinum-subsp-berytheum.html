Trifolium alexandrinum subsp. berytheum

Trifolium alexandrinum subsp.

berytheum, commonly known as Egyptian clover, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, often with a slight hairy texture.

The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used as a forage crop or in ornamental gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological and agricultural purposes.
