Trigonella brevidentata

Trigonella brevidentata, commonly known as the short-toothed fenugreek, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its typical legume structure. The plant features trifoliate leaves with small, rounded lobes and produces small, yellowish flowers. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are often used in traditional herbal remedies.

Trigonella brevidentata is adapted to grow in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas.
