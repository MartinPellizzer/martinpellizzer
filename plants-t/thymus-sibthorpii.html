Thymus sibthorpii

Thymus sibthorpii, commonly known as the Balkan thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and square stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces tiny, aromatic leaves that are often used in traditional herbal remedies.

Thymus sibthorpii is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
