Tephrosia fasciculata

Tephrosia fasciculata, commonly known as the clustered tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2–5 meters, featuring compound leaves with 5–11 leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along riverbanks.

Tephrosia fasciculata is also valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
