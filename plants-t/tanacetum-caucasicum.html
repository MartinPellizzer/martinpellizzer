Tanacetum caucasicum

Tanacetum caucasicum, commonly known as Caucasian tansy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features finely divided, aromatic leaves and produces yellow-orange flower heads that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
