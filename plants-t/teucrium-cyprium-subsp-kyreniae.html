Teucrium cyprium subsp. kyreniae

Teucrium cyprium subsp.

kyreniae, commonly known as Cyprus germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or dry soils, often found in coastal areas and mountainous regions.

The plant features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that are usually blue or violet in color. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The subspecies is distinguished by its specific geographic distribution and morphological characteristics.
