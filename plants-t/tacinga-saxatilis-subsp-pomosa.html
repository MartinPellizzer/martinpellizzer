Tacinga saxatilis subsp. pomosa

Tacinga saxatilis subsp.

pomosa, commonly known as the rock tacinga, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in coastal or inland areas with well-drained conditions.

The plant features slender, upright stems with small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually pale blue or violet, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems.

It is adapted to arid environments and is known for its resilience to drought and salt spray.
