Tacca sumatrana

Tacca sumatrana, commonly known as the "bat plant" or "devil's tongue," is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyclanthaceae and is characterized by its large, ornate leaves that resemble the wings of a bat. The plant produces striking purple and white flowers with long, curling tendrils that resemble a tongue. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Tacca sumatrana is often cultivated for its unique and exotic appearance in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
