Teucrium polium f. majoricum

Teucrium polium f. majoricum, commonly known as the Greater St. John's Wort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a hairy surface and produces small, pinkish-white flowers in whorled clusters. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or scrubland habitats.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological studies and traditional herbal medicine.
