Trichogonia martii

Trichogonia martii, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in terminal inflorescences. Trichogonia martii is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and horticultural research.
