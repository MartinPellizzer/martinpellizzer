Thymus michaelis

Thymus michaelis, commonly known as Michael's thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and aromatic, narrow leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its essential oils contain thymol, which contributes to its medicinal and culinary uses.
