Telanthera lanceolata

Telanthera lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf telanthera, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinct, somewhat rough texture. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of yellow or orange, which bloom during the rainy season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or swampy areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some horticultural gardens.
