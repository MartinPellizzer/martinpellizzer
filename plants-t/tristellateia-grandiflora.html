Tristellateia grandiflora

Tristellateia grandiflora, commonly known as the giant starflower, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that resemble stars, which bloom in clusters on long, branching stems. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, eye-catching blooms.
