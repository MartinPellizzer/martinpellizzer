Thelypteris dumetorum

Thelypteris dumetorum, commonly known as the rock fern, is a species of fern native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its compact, dense growth habit and its ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments. The plant has lance-shaped, serrated leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy on the underside. Its sporangia are clustered in sori along the edges of the fronds, a common reproductive feature in ferns.

Thelypteris dumetorum is often found growing on rocky soils or in woodland areas, showcasing its adaptability to various ecological niches.
