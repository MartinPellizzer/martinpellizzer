Tragus adriaticus

Tragus adriaticus, commonly known as the Adriatic tragus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its small, erect growth habit. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and hairy, adapted to thrive in dry, rocky environments.

Tragus adriaticus is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
