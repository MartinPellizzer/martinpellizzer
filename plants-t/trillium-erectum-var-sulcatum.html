Trillium erectum var. sulcatum

Trillium erectum var. sulcatum, commonly known as the southern white trillium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features a basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves that are often white-veined, and it produces a single, showy white or pale pink flower with three petals and three sepals. The plant typically grows in rich, moist woodlands and thrives in partial shade. It has a short, stout rhizome and is known for its distinctive floral structure and ecological significance in forest ecosystems.

The species is also noted for its slow growth and long lifespan, often living for several decades.
