Thelypteris dimorpha

Thelypteris dimorpha, commonly known as the two-form maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Thelypteridaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate fronds that are often divided into two contrasting forms: the fertile and the sterile. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Thelypteris dimorpha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
