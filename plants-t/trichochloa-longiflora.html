Trichochloa longiflora

Trichochloa longiflora, commonly known as the slender spikegrass, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a member of the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers that are clustered in dense spikes, typically blooming during the rainy season.
