Teucrium lanceolatum

Teucrium lanceolatum, commonly known as narrowleaf germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color, with a rough, hairy texture. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Teucrium lanceolatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
