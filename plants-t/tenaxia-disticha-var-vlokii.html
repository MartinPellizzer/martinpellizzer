Tenaxia disticha var. vlokii

Tenaxia disticha var. vlokii, commonly known as the Vlok's twining milkweed, is a unique variant of the species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its twining growth habit and produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are opposite in arrangement, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
