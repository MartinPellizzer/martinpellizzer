Temnocalyx nodulosa

Temnocalyx nodulosa, commonly known as the nodular calyx orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive floral structure, featuring a hairy, nodular calyx that gives it its scientific name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in tropical rainforests. Its flowers are usually small and fragrant, with a unique coloration that varies among individuals.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
