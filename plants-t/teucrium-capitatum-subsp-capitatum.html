Teucrium capitatum subsp. capitatum

Teucrium capitatum subsp.

capitatum, commonly known as mountain germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features upright stems covered in fine, hairy hairs, and it produces dense clusters of small, blue to purple flowers.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color, with a rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
