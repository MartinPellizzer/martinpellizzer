Townsendia intermedia

Townsendia intermedia, commonly known as the mountain flax, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in alpine and subalpine meadows. The plant has slender, upright stems and produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs.

It is adapted to harsh, high-altitude environments and is often found in rocky soils.
