Taraxacum ciliatum

Taraxacum ciliatum, commonly known as the woolly dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its hairy, lobed leaves and a yellow composite flower head that develops into a fluffy seed head. Native to Europe, it is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides. The plant has a deep taproot and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

Its flowers bloom in the spring and early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
