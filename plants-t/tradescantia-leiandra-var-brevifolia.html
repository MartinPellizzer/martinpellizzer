Tradescantia leiandra var. brevifolia

Tradescantia leiandra var. brevifolia, commonly known as the dwarf spiderwort, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features upright, branching stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green, often with red or purple veins. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of blue, purple, or white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens or containers.

Its foliage and flowers add a delicate, colorful touch to garden landscapes.
