Thymus evenkiensis

Thymus evenkiensis, commonly known as Evenki thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Siberia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils at high altitudes, often in alpine or subalpine environments. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces tiny, aromatic leaves that are used in traditional medicine.

Thymus evenkiensis is valued for its medicinal properties and is also used in local culinary practices.
