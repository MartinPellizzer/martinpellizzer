Thelypteris lindheimeri

Thelypteris lindheimeri, commonly known as Lindheimer's shield fern, is a species of fern native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically divided into small, triangular segments. The plant prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, typical of many fern species.

Thelypteris lindheimeri plays a role in maintaining forest floor biodiversity and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
