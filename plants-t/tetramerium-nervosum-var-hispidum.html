Tetramerium nervosum var. hispidum

Tetramerium nervosum var. hispidum, commonly known as the hairy tetramerium, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its leaves are alternate and ovate, with a rough texture due to the presence of fine hairs.

This species is often found in moist, shaded environments within tropical forests.
