Tracaulon sagittatum f. subalbidum

Tracaulon sagittatum f. subalbidum, commonly known as the white-flowered arrowhead, is a rare variant of the arrowhead plant.

It is characterized by its broad, arrow-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and green. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodland areas.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and unique coloration.
