Tacazzea amplifolia

Tacazzea amplifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved tacazzea, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom during the rainy season. It thrives in moist, swampy environments and is often found in areas with rich alluvial soils.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
