Thalictrum nigrescens

Thalictrum nigrescens, commonly known as the black-stem meadow-rue, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features slender, upright stems that turn dark purple or black as the plant matures. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white to pale green, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are finely divided and arranged in a whorled pattern along the stem.

Thalictrum nigrescens prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.
