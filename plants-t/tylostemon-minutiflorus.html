Tylostemon minutiflorus

Tylostemon minutiflorus, commonly known as the dwarf starflower, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features delicate, star-shaped white flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Tylostemon minutiflorus is often found in woodland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
