Tephrosia arcuata

Tephrosia arcuata, commonly known as the wavy tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a legume, belonging to the Fabaceae family, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its seeds are encased in pods and contain toxic compounds, making it important to handle with care.

Tephrosia arcuata is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
