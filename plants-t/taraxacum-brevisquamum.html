Taraxacum brevisquamum

Taraxacum brevisquamum, commonly known as the short-leaf dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of spoon-shaped, hairy leaves and a yellow composite flower head that develops into a spherical seed ball. Native to North America, this plant thrives in a variety of habitats, including meadows, roadsides, and disturbed soils. It has a taproot system that allows it to survive in dry conditions and is often found in open, sunny areas.

The plant is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
