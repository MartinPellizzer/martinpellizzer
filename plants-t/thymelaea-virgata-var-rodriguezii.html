Thymelaea virgata var. rodriguezii

Thymelaea virgata var. rodriguezii, commonly known as Rodriguez's thymelaea, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the family Thymelaeaceae and is characterized by its small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has woody stems and produces small, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species within its native habitat.
