Taraxacum undulatiforme

Taraxacum undulatiforme, commonly known as the wavyleaf dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its rosette of spoon-shaped, wavy-edged leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces a single, tall, hollow stem topped with a spherical flower head composed of numerous yellow ray florets. It is native to temperate regions of Europe and is often found in grassy areas and disturbed soils.

The species is noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in gardens and natural habitats.
