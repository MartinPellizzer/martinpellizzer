Thymus grossheimii

Thymus grossheimii, commonly known as the Grossheim thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow, fragrant, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Thymus grossheimii is often found in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in full sun and well-drained environments.
