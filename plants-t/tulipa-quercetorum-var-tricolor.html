Tulipa quercetorum var. tricolor

Tulipa quercetorum var. tricolor, commonly known as the tricolor tulip, is a striking perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Turkey.

It is characterized by its distinctive three-colored petals, typically featuring a deep red base, a yellow midsection, and a white or pale pink tip. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring, adding vibrant color to gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical name reflects its association with the Quercetum genus, highlighting its ecological and floral uniqueness.
