Tragacantha ammotropha

Tragacantha ammotropha, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves, which are typically arranged in an alternate pattern. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by pods containing seeds. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

The plant is notable for its ability to produce a resinous gum, which has been historically used in various traditional applications.
