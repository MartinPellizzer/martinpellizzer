Tropaeolum asplundii

Tropaeolum asplundii, commonly known as the Andean jewelweed, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its vibrant, spurred flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant produces small, edible, peppery tubers similar to those of the common garden nasturtium. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and often trailing.

Tropaeolum asplundii thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shady or partially shaded environments.
