Tetraneuris acaulis var. epunctata

Tetraneuris acaulis var. epunctata, commonly known as the punctate blueweed, is a low-growing, flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and blue-gray foliage. The species features small, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The plant is adapted to arid environments and is drought-tolerant once established.
