Turraea squamulifera

Turraea squamulifera, commonly known as the African snowball, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces clusters of white, fluffy flowers that resemble snowballs, which bloom during the rainy season. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often covered in a fine, scaly layer.

Turraea squamulifera is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
