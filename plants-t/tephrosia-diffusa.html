Tephrosia diffusa

Tephrosia diffusa, commonly known as wild senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and thrives in sunny, open habitats.

Botanically, it belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is known for its nitrogen-fixing abilities through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia.
