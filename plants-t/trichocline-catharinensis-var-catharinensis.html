Trichocline catharinensis var. catharinensis

Trichocline catharinensis var. catharinensis, commonly known as the Catharinen's trichocline, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine, hair-like structures, giving it a distinctive texture. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
