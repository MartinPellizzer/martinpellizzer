Terminalia canescens

Terminalia canescens, commonly known as the African snowball tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its white, fluffy flowers that appear in clusters, giving it its common name. The tree has smooth, grayish bark and oblong leaves with a rough texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riverine forests and savanna woodlands.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appearance and has traditional medicinal uses in some African cultures.
