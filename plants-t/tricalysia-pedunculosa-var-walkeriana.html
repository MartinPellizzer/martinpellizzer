Tricalysia pedunculosa var. walkeriana

Tricalysia pedunculosa var. walkeriana, commonly known as the Walker's flame tree, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a fiery appearance. The plant has compound leaves with elongated leaflets and produces woody, branching stems that can grow quite tall. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its rapid growth and ability to produce abundant flowers, it is a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
