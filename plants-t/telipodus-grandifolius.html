Telipodus grandifolius

Telipodus grandifolius, commonly known as the grand-leaf telipodus, is a member of the Malvaceae family, characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. Its flowers, which are typically pink or purple, bloom in clusters and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
