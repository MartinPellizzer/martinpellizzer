Trichocarya splendens

Trichocarya splendens, commonly known as the splendour trichocarya, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or alpine regions. Its leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, adapted to the harsh conditions of its natural habitat.

Trichocarya splendens is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
