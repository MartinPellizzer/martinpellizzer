Trisetum brandegeei

Trisetum brandegeei, commonly known as the Brandegge's wiregrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant has slender, wiry stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that are characteristic of the grass family. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture.

Trisetum brandegeei is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and ability to stabilize soil.
