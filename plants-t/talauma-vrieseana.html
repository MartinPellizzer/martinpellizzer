Talauma vrieseana

Talauma vrieseana, commonly known as the Vriesia orchid, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

Talauma vrieseana thrives in warm, moist conditions and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.
