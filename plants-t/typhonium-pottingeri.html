Typhonium pottingeri

Typhonium pottingeri, commonly known as the Bornean arrowhead, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and spadix inflorescence. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often near water bodies. Its rhizomatous structure allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

The spathe, a modified leaf, surrounds the spadix and plays a role in attracting pollinators.
