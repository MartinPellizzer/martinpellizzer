Trigonella foenum-graecum subsp. gladiata

Trigonella foenum-graecum subsp.

gladiata, commonly known as slender fenugreek, is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features slender, upright stems that are often branched and covered in fine hairs.

The leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being narrow and lance-shaped, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. The flowers are small, yellow, and borne in clusters, typical of the Fabaceae family.

This subspecies is valued for its seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.
