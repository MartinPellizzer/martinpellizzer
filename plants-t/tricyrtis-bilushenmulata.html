Tricyrtis bilushenmulata

Tricyrtis bilushenmulata, commonly known as the Japanese ghost orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid native to Japan.

It is characterized by its unique, pendulous flowers that resemble ghostly faces, hence its common name. The plant has slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is typically found in forested areas.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a protected species in its native range.
