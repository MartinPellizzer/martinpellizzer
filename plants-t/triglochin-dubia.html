Triglochin dubia

Triglochin dubia, commonly known as the slender spikerush, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in the Americas.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish flowers in dense spikes. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

Triglochin dubia is adapted to fluctuating water levels and is an important component of wetland ecosystems.
