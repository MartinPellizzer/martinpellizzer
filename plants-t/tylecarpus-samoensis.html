Tylecarpus samoensis

Tylecarpus samoensis, commonly known as the Samoan tree fern, is a species native to the Pacific Islands, particularly Samoa and nearby regions.

It is a member of the Cyatheaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant grows in humid, tropical environments and thrives in shaded, moist conditions. Its rhizome system supports its growth, and it plays a role in the local ecosystem by providing habitat and contributing to soil stability.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appearance and traditional uses in the region.
