Tephrosia leucantha

Tephrosia leucantha, commonly known as white tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruit. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported antimalarial properties.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats or along roadsides.
