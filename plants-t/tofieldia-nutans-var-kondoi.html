Tofieldia nutans var. kondoi

Tofieldia nutans var. kondoi, commonly known as the Kondoi sedge, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grassy leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that develop in dense clusters. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and along stream banks. It produces small, three-lobed seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This variety is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and supporting local biodiversity.
