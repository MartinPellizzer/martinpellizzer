Tragacantha sonorae

Tragacantha sonorae, commonly known as the Sonoran tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Sonoran Desert region of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its gray-green, narrow leaves and yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces a gum that is used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive. It thrives in arid environments and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures.

Tragacantha sonorae plays an important role in the ecological balance of its native habitat.
