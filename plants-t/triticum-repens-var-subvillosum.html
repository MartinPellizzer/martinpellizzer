Triticum repens var. subvillosum

Triticum repens var. subvillosum, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-green foliage and tufted inflorescence, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are covered with a dense layer of fine hairs, contributing to its common name.

This variety is often used in ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
