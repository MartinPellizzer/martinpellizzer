Tragus occidentalis

Tragus occidentalis, commonly known as the western tragus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs.

Tragus occidentalis is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
