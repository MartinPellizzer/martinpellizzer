Tryonia

Tryonia, commonly known as the spider plant, is a member of the Asparagaceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial evergreen plant characterized by its long, arching stems that bear small, clustered flowers. The plant produces bright green, strap-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. Tryonia is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of light conditions and is popular as a houseplant due to its low maintenance requirements.

It also has the unique ability to produce "spiderettes," which are miniature plantlets that can be easily propagated.
