Toona australis

Toona australis, commonly known as the Australian toona, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Meliaceae family, which includes mahogany and other tropical hardwoods. The tree can grow up to 30 meters tall, with smooth, greyish bark and large, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small and greenish-yellow, followed by elongated, woody fruits containing flat, winged seeds.

The plant is valued for its durable timber and is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.
