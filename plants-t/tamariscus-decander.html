Tamariscus decander

Tamariscus decander, commonly known as the desert rose or red tamarisk, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Mediterranean and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are typically small, scale-like, and arranged in opposite pairs along the stems.

Tamariscus decander is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a hardy plant in dry climates.
