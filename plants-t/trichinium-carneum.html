Trichinium carneum

Trichinium carneum, commonly known as the red root fern, is a unique fern species characterized by its distinctive red, fleshy roots.

It is native to tropical regions and thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine, hairy structures. Its reproductive structures, known as sporangia, are typically found on the underside of the fronds.

Trichinium carneum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
