Thalictrum purpurascens var. rugosum

Thalictrum purpurascens var. rugosum, commonly known as the rough-stemmed meadow rue, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and compound leaves with a rough, hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and forest edges.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
