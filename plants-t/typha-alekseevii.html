Typha alekseevii

Typha alekseevii, commonly known as the Alekseev's cattail, is a perennial wetland plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical flower spike that is typically greenish or brownish in color. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that grow from a rhizomatous stem, allowing it to spread vegetatively. It thrives in shallow water or damp soil, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Typha alekseevii plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and stabilizing soil.
