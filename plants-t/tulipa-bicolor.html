Tulipa bicolor

Tulipa bicolor, commonly known as the bicolor tulip, is a species of flowering plant in the family Liliaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is characterized by its distinctive two-colored flowers, typically featuring a white or pale pink center with a deeper pink or red outer petals. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped blooms in early spring, often with a subtle fragrance. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it prefers well-drained soil in full sun.

Tulipa bicolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its vibrant and unique floral display.
