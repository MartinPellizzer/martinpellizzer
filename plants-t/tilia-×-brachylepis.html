Tilia × brachylepis

Tilia × brachylepis, commonly known as the small-leaved lime, is a hybrid deciduous tree belonging to the Tiliaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, ovate leaves that are typically pale green on the underside. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which bloom in early summer. Its fruit is a spherical, three-lobed capsule that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often planted in urban landscapes for its shade and aesthetic appeal.
