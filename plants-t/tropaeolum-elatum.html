Tropaeolum elatum

Tropaeolum elatum, commonly known as the garden nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, edible flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Tropaeolum elatum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant and for its peppery-tasting leaves and seeds.
