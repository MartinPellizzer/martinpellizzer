Triumfetta deschampsii

Triumfetta deschampsii, commonly known as the velvetleaf or false mallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, often covered in fine, soft hairs. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in loose inflorescences, blooming throughout the warm seasons.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, dry seeds.
