Tarenna elliptica var. saxicola

Tarenna elliptica var. saxicola, commonly known as the rock tarenna, is a shrub native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by berries that turn red when mature. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid conditions.

This species is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
