Thalia marantifolia

Thalia marantifolia, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery red bracts that surround its small, white flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are large, palmately lobed, and often have a waxy texture.

Thalia marantifolia thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
