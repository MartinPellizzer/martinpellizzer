Tripogon japonicus

Tripogon japonicus, commonly known as Japanese grass or Japanese wiregrass, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a member of the Poaceae family, which includes grasses and cereals. The plant is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves. It produces small, spikelet-based flowers that are typically green or brown in color.

Tripogon japonicus is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
