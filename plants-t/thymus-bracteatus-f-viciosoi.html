Thymus bracteatus f. viciosoi

Thymus bracteatus f. viciosoi, commonly known as the hairy thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically covered in fine, hairy textures, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its fragrant foliage and ability to attract pollinators.
