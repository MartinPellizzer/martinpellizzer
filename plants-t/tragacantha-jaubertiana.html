Tragacantha jaubertiana

Tragacantha jaubertiana, commonly known as the Persian tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Iran and Afghanistan.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody stems and bipinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by distinctive pod-like fruits containing seeds.

It is valued for its gum, which has been used historically in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive.
