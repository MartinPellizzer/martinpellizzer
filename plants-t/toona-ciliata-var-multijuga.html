Toona ciliata var. multijuga

Toona ciliata var. multijuga, commonly known as the Indian satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish-brown bark and heart-shaped leaves with a velvety underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, spherical fruits that contain soft, white pulp and seeds.

It is valued for its hard, fine-grained wood, which is used in furniture and musical instruments, and for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
