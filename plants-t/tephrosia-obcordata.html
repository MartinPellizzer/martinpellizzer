Tephrosia obcordata

Tephrosia obcordata, commonly known as African balm, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with a distinctive obcordate shape, and its flowers are typically small and clustered in racemose inflorescences. It produces pods that contain seeds, and it is often found in open woodland and grassland habitats.

Tephrosia obcordata is also valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
