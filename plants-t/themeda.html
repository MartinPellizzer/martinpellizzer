Themeda

Themeda, commonly known as kangaroo grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It is well adapted to fire-prone environments and plays an important role in maintaining soil stability.

Themeda is often found in grasslands and open woodlands, contributing to the ecological diversity of these habitats.
