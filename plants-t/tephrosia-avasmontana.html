Tephrosia avasmontana

Tephrosia avasmontana, commonly known as the golden tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, thriving in well-drained soils. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation due to its association with symbiotic nitrogen-fixing bacteria.

Additionally, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant floral display.
