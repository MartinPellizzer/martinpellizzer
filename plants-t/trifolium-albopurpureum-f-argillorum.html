Trifolium albopurpureum f. argillorum

Trifolium albopurpureum f. argillorum, commonly known as the argillaceous white clover, is a botanical variant of the white clover species.

It is characterized by its pale purple to white flower heads and trifoliate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. This subspecies typically grows in moist, calcareous soils, often found in meadows and grasslands. Its flowers bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in soil stabilization and forage production.
