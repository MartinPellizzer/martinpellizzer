Themistoclesia alata

Themistoclesia alata, commonly known as the winged cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, flattened stems that resemble wings. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers during the warmer months, which attract various pollinators. Its adaptations include spines for protection and the ability to store water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Themistoclesia alata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in arid environments.
