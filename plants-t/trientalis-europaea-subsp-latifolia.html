Trientalis europaea subsp. latifolia

Trientalis europaea subsp.

latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf starflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe. It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, ovate leaves.

The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
