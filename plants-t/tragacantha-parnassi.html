Tragacantha parnassi

Tragacantha parnassi, commonly known as Parnassian tragacanth, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes, typical of many legumes. Its root system is known for producing a viscous gum, which has been historically used in traditional medicine.

Tragacantha parnassi thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand harsh alpine conditions.
