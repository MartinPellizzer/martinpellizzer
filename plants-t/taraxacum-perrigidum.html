Taraxacum perrigidum

Taraxacum perrigidum, commonly known as the yellow hawk's-bane, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and a distinctive, hairy stem. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides. It has a rosette of basal leaves that are usually covered with fine hairs.

The fruit is a capsule that releases seeds with a pappus for wind dispersal.
