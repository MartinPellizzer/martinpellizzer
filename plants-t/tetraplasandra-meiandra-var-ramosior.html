Tetraplasandra meiandra var. ramosior

Tetraplasandra meiandra var. ramosior, commonly known as the coastal hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to the coastal regions of eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth. Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for coastal gardens and landscaping.

Additionally, it is well adapted to salt spray and wind, making it suitable for coastal environments.
