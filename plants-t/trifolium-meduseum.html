Trifolium meduseum

Trifolium meduseum, commonly known as the lesser clover, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged on a central stalk. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils.

Trifolium meduseum is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in ecological and agricultural contexts.
