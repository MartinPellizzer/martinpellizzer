Tydaea × hansteinii

Tydaea × hansteinii, commonly known as the hybrid reedgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the wetlands of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist to wet soil conditions and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of ponds.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in wetland restoration projects.
