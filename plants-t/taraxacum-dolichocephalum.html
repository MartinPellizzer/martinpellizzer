Taraxacum dolichocephalum

Taraxacum dolichocephalum, commonly known as the long-headed dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves and a yellow flower head that develops into a white, spherical seed sphere. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. It has a taproot system and is often found in temperate climates.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
