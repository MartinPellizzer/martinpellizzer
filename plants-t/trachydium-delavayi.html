Trachydium delavayi

Trachydium delavayi, commonly known as the Delavay's tamarisk, is a shrub native to the Himalayan regions of India and China.

It belongs to the family Tamaricaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.
