Tetraria bolusii

Tetraria bolusii, commonly known as the desert snakeroot, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, inconspicuous yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters. The plant has a deep root system that allows it to survive in harsh, dry environments. Its leaves are narrow and grayish-green, adapted to reduce water loss.

Tetraria bolusii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small desert organisms.
