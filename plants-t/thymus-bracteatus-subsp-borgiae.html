Thymus bracteatus subsp. borgiae

Thymus bracteatus subsp.

borgiae, commonly known as Borgia thyme, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and aromatic, small, gray-green leaves.

The plant produces dense clusters of tiny, pink to purple flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is often cultivated in horticulture.
