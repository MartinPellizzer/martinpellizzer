Tetraena hamiensis var. qatarensis

Tetraena hamiensis var. qatarensis, commonly known as the Qatari sandmat, is a low-growing shrub native to arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It features small, fleshy, spiny leaves adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a splash of color to its sandy habitat. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in desert and semi-desert ecosystems.

Due to its resilience, it is used in some areas for erosion control and as a source of medicinal compounds.
