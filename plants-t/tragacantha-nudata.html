Tragacantha nudata

Tragacantha nudata, commonly known as the smooth tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by distinctive, inflated pods containing seeds. Tragacantha nudata is known for its ability to thrive in dry, rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
