Tridermia papillosa

Tridermia papillosa, commonly known as the velvet leaf, is a tropical plant characterized by its velvety, soft foliage.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is native to regions in Southeast Asia. The plant features broad, ovate leaves with a distinctive papillate surface that gives it a textured appearance. Its growth habit is typically shrub-like, with dense branching and a compact form.

Tridermia papillosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
