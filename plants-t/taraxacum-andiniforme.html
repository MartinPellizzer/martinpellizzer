Taraxacum andiniforme

Taraxacum andiniforme, commonly known as the Andean dandelion, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant features a rosette of basal, spoon-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs. Its yellow flower heads produce seeds with pappus structures that aid in wind dispersal.

This species is adapted to cold, dry environments and is often found in alpine and grassland habitats.
