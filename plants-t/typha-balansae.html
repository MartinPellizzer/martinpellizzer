Typha balansae

Typha balansae, commonly known as the Balansae cattail, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Typhaceae and is characterized by its tall, cylindrical flower spike that emerges from a dense, grass-like basal rosette. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and blade-like, while its roots system is extensive and helps stabilize the soil.

Typha balansae plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
