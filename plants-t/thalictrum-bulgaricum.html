Thalictrum bulgaricum

Thalictrum bulgaricum, commonly known as the Balkan meadow rue, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its delicate appearance and early spring blooming make it a notable species in its native habitat.

However, it is not commonly cultivated due to its specific ecological requirements.
