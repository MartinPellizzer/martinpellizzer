Trifolium umbellulatum

Trifolium umbellulatum, commonly known as the umbrella clover, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in a clustered umbel, giving it its common name. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in meadows, prairies, and disturbed sites.

Trifolium umbellulatum is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
