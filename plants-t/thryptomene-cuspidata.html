Thryptomene cuspidata

Thryptomene cuspidata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thryptomene, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea tree species. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that appear in clusters, typically during the spring and summer seasons.

Thryptomene cuspidata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native gardens.
