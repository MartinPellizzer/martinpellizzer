Teucrium antitauricum

Teucrium antitauricum, commonly known as the Turkish germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia, including parts of Turkey and the Mediterranean. The plant features square stems, opposite leaves with serrated edges, and small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.

Teucrium antitauricum is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects.
