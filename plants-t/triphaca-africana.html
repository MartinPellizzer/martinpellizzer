Triphaca africana

Triphaca africana, commonly known as the African star grass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white to pale blue flowers. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green coloration.

Triphaca africana is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
