Tibouchina squamata

Tibouchina squamata, commonly known as the silverleaf tibouchina, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray, velvety foliage and clusters of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Tibouchina squamata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
