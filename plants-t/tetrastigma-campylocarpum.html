Tetrastigma campylocarpum

Tetrastigma campylocarpum, commonly known as the hairy climbing vine, is a perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vigorous climbing habit and trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns red when mature.

Tetrastigma campylocarpum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and gardens.
