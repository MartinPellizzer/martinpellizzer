Trifolium quartinianum

Trifolium quartinianum, commonly known as Quartin's clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa, typically growing in open grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. It is a perennial herb that produces pods containing several seeds, contributing to its role in soil fixation and ecological restoration.

Trifolium quartinianum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
