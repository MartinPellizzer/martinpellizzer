Thymus richardii var. valentinus

Thymus richardii var. valentinus, commonly known as the Valentin's thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features small, aromatic leaves and produces clusters of pink to lavender flowers during the summer months. The plant is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and is often found in rocky or limestone soils. It is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in herbal remedies.

Its essential oils contain thymol and other volatile compounds that contribute to its antimicrobial and antifungal activities.
