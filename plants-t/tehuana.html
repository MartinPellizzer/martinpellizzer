Tehuana

Tehuana, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a tropical plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its bright orange to red flowers and lance-shaped leaves. The plant is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and marigolds. Tehuana thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its rapid growth and ability to attract pollinators make it both ornamental and ecologically beneficial.
