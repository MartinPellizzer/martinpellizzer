Trichilia canjerana

Trichilia canjerana, commonly known as the African sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing flat, seed-like structures. It is valued for its aromatic wood, which is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, supporting various wildlife species.
