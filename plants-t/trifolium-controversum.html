Trifolium controversum

Trifolium controversum, commonly known as the Controversial Clover, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, depending on the variety. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and meadows.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is both a subject of botanical study and a symbol of botanical curiosity.
