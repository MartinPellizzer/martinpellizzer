Tragacantha canescens

Tragacantha canescens, commonly known as the white tragacanth, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, grayish stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by pods containing lens-shaped seeds. It is known for its mucilaginous properties, making it useful in traditional medicine and as a natural emulsifier.

Its growth habit and botanical features make it adaptable to dry and semi-arid environments.
