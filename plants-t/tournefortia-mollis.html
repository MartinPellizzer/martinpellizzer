Tournefortia mollis

Tournefortia mollis, commonly known as the velvetleaf or false mallow, is a flowering plant native to the warmer regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are usually hairy and have a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical areas.
