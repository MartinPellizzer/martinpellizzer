Taeniophyllum rhodantherum

Taeniophyllum rhodantherum, commonly known as the red-flowered taeniophyllum, is a species of flowering plant in the family Orchidaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, bright red inflorescences that bloom during the rainy season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
