Taraxacum pseudophaleratum

Taraxacum pseudophaleratum, commonly known as the purple star dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and a striking purple-pink flower head that resembles a small star. The plant is native to parts of Europe and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. It produces a long, hollow stem that supports its composite flower, which consists of both ray and disk florets.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
