Teucrium massiliense

Teucrium massiliense, commonly known as the Massilian germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small, tubular, and usually pink or purple, arranged in dense spikes at the top of the stems.

Teucrium massiliense is often found in rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
