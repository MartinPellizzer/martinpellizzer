Tournefortia edgeworthii

Tournefortia edgeworthii, commonly known as the Indian borage, is a flowering plant native to southern India and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a small shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces clusters of blue or violet flowers that bloom during the summer months. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soil and is often found in coastal and tropical regions.
