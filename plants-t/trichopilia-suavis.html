Trichopilia suavis

Trichopilia suavis, commonly known as the sweet trichopilia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that collect water, creating a small pool. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is a subject of interest in botanical research and conservation efforts.
