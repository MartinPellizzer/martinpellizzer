Thalictrum minus f. contractum

Thalictrum minus f. contractum, commonly known as the narrowleaf meadow-rue, is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and finely divided, fern-like leaves that give it a delicate appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in early to mid-summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a notable species for ecological and horticultural interest.
