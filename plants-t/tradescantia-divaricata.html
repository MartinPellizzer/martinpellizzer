Tradescantia divaricata

Tradescantia divaricata, commonly known as spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, arching stems that produce clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-green hue. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This plant is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to spread via rhizomes.
