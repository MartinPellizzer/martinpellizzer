Taraxacum caloschistoides

Taraxacum caloschistoides, commonly known as the rock dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, waxy leaves and a flowering stem that produces a single, yellow composite flower head. The plant is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing a deep root system for water retention. Its flowers develop into a spherical, prickly seed head, typical of the dandelion genus.

It is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in harsh landscapes.
