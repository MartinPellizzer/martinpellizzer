Triticum thaoudar var. atratum

Triticum thaoudar var. atratum, commonly known as the black triticum, is a wild wheat species native to the Middle East and North Africa.

It is characterized by its dark-colored grains and robust, grass-like foliage. This variety is often found in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh climatic conditions. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with slender stems and narrow leaves.

Its botanical significance lies in its genetic diversity and potential for agricultural improvement.
