Tamarix androssowii

Tamarix androssowii, commonly known as the Androssowii tamarisk, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and small, scale-like leaves that reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces pink to reddish flowers in clusters, which bloom in the spring and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is adapted to saline and alkaline soils, making it a resilient species in degraded landscapes.

Tamarix androssowii plays a role in stabilizing soils and preventing erosion in its native habitats.
