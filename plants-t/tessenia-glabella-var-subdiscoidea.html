Tessenia glabella var. subdiscoidea

Tessenia glabella var. subdiscoidea, commonly known as the discoid tessenia, is a variant of the tessenia plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive disc-like leaves that are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is relatively rare and requires specific growing conditions to thrive.
