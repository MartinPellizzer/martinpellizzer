Trochetia melanoxylon

Trochetia melanoxylon, commonly known as blackwood, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dark, aromatic bark and small, leathery leaves that are glossy on top and pale underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by spherical, woody fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
