Teucrium webbianum

Teucrium webbianum, commonly known as the webbian germander, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its small, tubular flowers are usually blue to purple in color and bloom in the summer months.

Teucrium webbianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
