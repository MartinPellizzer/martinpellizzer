Trifolium fucatum var. flavulum

Trifolium fucatum var. flavulum, commonly known as the yellow wood clover, is a flowering plant in the legume family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
