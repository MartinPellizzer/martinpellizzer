Themelium graminifolium

Themelium graminifolium, commonly known as the slender grass-leaf plant, is a herbaceous species characterized by its fine, grass-like leaves.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors or stream banks. The plant features small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets, a common trait among grass family members. Its root system is fibrous, aiding in soil stabilization and nutrient absorption.

The leaves are arranged in a basal rosette, which helps conserve moisture and protect the plant from environmental stress.
