Turnera angustifolia

Turnera angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf turnera, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Turneraceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in shaded or semi-shaded environments. Its leaves are usually lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally used in traditional medicine.
