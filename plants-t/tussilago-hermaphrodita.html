Tussilago hermaphrodita

Tussilago hermaphrodita, commonly known as coltsfoot, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shady environments such as meadows and woodland edges. The plant features a basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves and produces showy yellow flowers in the spring. It is notable for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional remedies for respiratory ailments.

The species is also known for its early blooming habit, often appearing before the leaves fully emerge.
