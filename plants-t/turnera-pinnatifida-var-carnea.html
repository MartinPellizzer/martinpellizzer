Turnera pinnatifida var. carnea

Turnera pinnatifida var. carnea, commonly known as the red-flowered turnera, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has palmate leaves with a hairy texture, and its stems are typically woody and branched. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

The varietal form 'carnea' is distinguished by its more intense and showy flower color compared to the typical variety.
