Tinus reclinata

Tinus reclinata, commonly known as the leaning tinus, is a unique succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a distinctive, sprawling growth habit with fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white powder. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the warmer months, adding a subtle ornamental touch. Its ability to store water in its leaves makes it drought-tolerant and well-suited for arid environments.

Tinus reclinata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements.
