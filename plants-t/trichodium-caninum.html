Trichodium caninum

Trichodium caninum, commonly known as the dog's tail plant, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It is typically found in sandy or disturbed soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Trichodium caninum is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
