Tylanthus secundus

Tylanthus secundus, commonly known as the silky tamarind, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and fragrant yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. Tylanthus secundus thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate foliage and pleasant scent.
