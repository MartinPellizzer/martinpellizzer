Thymus bellicus

Thymus bellicus, commonly known as the war thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its small pink or violet flowers bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Thymus bellicus is known for its strong, pungent fragrance, which is due to the presence of essential oils in its leaves and flowers.
