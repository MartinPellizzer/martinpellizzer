Tritonia mensensis

Tritonia mensensis, commonly known as the Men's Island bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the island of Men, off the coast of Bermuda.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It is often found growing in coastal areas and is adapted to the salty, windy conditions of its habitat.

Tritonia mensensis is considered a rare and endemic species, highlighting the unique botanical diversity of Bermuda.
