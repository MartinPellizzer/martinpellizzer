Tecoma heptaphylla

Tecoma heptaphylla, commonly known as the yellow bell.

This flowering plant is native to tropical regions of Africa and thrives in warm, humid climates. It features showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has slender, woody stems and lance-shaped leaves with a smooth texture.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adaptable to various soil types.
