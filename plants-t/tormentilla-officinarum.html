Tormentilla officinarum

Tormentilla officinarum, commonly known as lesser knotweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces oval, green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or along stream banks.

Tormentilla officinarum has a long history of use in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
