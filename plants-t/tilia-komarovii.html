Tilia komarovii

Tilia komarovii, commonly known as the Komarov's lime tree, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the Tiliaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are typically serrated along the edges. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which bloom in the spring and are followed by small, round fruits. Its bark is smooth and grayish in color, and it can grow to a height of up to 20 meters in its natural habitat.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
