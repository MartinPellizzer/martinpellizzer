Thlaspi sylvestre proles gaudinianum

Thlaspi sylvestre proles gaudinianum, commonly known as the Gaudinian rockcress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is a small, perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments. The plant features basal rosettes of leaves and produces small, white or yellow flowers in early spring. Its seeds are enclosed in siliques, a characteristic feature of the Brassicaceae family.

This species is often found in mountainous regions across Europe and parts of Asia.
