Teucrium orientale var. subglabrum

Teucrium orientale var. subglabrum, commonly known as Eastern germander, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, branched stems that are often hairy, though the variety subglabrum is noted for its reduced hairiness. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet to blue in color, arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. Its leaves are lance-shaped, opposite, and often have a slightly aromatic scent.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is commonly found in woodland edges and open fields.
