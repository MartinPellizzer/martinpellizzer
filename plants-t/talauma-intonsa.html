Talauma intonsa

Talauma intonsa, commonly known as the red spider lily, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that resemble spiders, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, and it produces small, dark berries that contain the seeds.

Talauma intonsa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
