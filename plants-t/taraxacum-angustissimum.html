Taraxacum angustissimum

Taraxacum angustissimum, commonly known as the narrowleaf dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, alternate leaves that are typically hairy. The plant produces a yellow flower head that develops into a ball of white, fluffy seeds, which are dispersed by the wind. It is native to Europe and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

This species is noted for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
