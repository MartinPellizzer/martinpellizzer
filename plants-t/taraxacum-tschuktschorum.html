Taraxacum tschuktschorum

Taraxacum tschuktschorum, commonly known as the Tschuktschorum dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces a hollow, hairy stem that supports a single flower head composed of yellow ray florets. Its seeds are dispersed by wind via a distinctive parachute-like structure.

It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in alpine or steppe regions.
