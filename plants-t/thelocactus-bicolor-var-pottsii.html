Thelocactus bicolor var. pottsii

Thelocactus bicolor var. pottsii, commonly known as the Potts' whitestick, is a rare and unique cactus species native to the arid regions of western Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are typically covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair, which helps to reflect sunlight and reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring, adding a striking contrast to its otherwise muted appearance. Its slow growth rate and specific habitat requirements make it a challenging species to cultivate outside its native environment.
