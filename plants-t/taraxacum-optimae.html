Taraxacum optimae

Taraxacum optimae, commonly known as the optimal dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated, hairy leaves and a hollow, leafy stem that supports a single, bright yellow flower head. The plant produces small, wind-dispersed seeds with parachute-like structures, aiding in its widespread distribution. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in meadows, lawns, and disturbed areas.

Taraxacum optimae is known for its medicinal properties, particularly its use in traditional remedies for digestive and respiratory ailments.
