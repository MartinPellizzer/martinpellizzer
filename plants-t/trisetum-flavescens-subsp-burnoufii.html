Trisetum flavescens subsp. burnoufii

Trisetum flavescens subsp.

burnoufii, commonly known as the Burnouf's false brome, is a grass species native to parts of Asia and the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks.

The plant has slender, tufted culms with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with yellow or reddish hues. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle bearing small, spikelet-like flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soils in wetland environments.
