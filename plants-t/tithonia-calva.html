Tithonia calva

Tithonia calva, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, bright orange to yellow flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. Tithonia calva is a perennial shrub that thrives in full sun and well-drained soil.

It is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
