Taraxacum lineare

Taraxacum lineare, commonly known as the slender dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its elongated, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces a single, hollow stem that rises to bear a bright yellow flower head, which later develops into a fluffy seed head. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and disturbed soils.

Taraxacum lineare is known for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in herbal remedies.
