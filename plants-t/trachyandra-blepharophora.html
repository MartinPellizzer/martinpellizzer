Trachyandra blepharophora

Trachyandra blepharophora, commonly known as the narrow-leafed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
