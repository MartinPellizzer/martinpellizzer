Trollius acaulis

Trollius acaulis, commonly known as the narrowleaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to northern regions of North America.

It features a rosette of narrow, dark green leaves that are typically hairless. The plant produces showy, yellow, daisy-like flowers with a central cluster of disk flowers surrounded by ray florets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Trollius acaulis is often found in wet meadows, stream banks, and other moist habitats.
