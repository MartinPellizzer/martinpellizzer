Triticum repens f. leersianum

Triticum repens f. leersianum, commonly known as the creeping wheatgrass, is a variant of wheatgrass characterized by its low-growing, spreading habit.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a slightly hairy texture. This plant is native to Europe and is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and riverbanks. Its botanical structure includes a fibrous root system and small, inconspicuous flowers that produce wind-pollinated seeds.

The plant is valued for its nutritional content and is sometimes used in herbal remedies and livestock feed.
