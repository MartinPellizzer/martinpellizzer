Trisetum alopecuros

Trisetum alopecuros, commonly known as the slender wood oats, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy along the edges. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are small, compact flower clusters.

Trisetum alopecuros is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife and ecological balance.
