Turritis mollis

Turritis mollis, commonly known as the slender bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of blue or violet. The leaves are narrow and lanceolate, often with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its delicate appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
