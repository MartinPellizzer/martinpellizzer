Tovara filiformis var. apoensis

Tovara filiformis var. apoensis, commonly known as the Apo spider plant, is a unique species native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its slender, spider-like leaves that are often spiraled around the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is well-suited for indoor cultivation. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by red berries that contain seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of care, it is a popular choice for collectors and home gardeners.
