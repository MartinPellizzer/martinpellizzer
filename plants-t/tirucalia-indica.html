Tirucalia indica

Tirucalia indica, commonly known as the Indian flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to southern Asia.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a rough, fissured bark and produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are alternate, simple, and have a velvety texture.
