Tetragastris panamensis

Tetragastris panamensis, commonly known as Panama's four-lobed palm, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive palm-like appearance with a slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by fruit that turns red when mature.

Tetragastris panamensis is typically found in humid, lowland areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
