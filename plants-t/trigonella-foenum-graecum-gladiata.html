Trigonella foenum-graecum gladiata

Trigonella foenum-graecum gladiata, commonly known as fenugreek, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features trifoliate leaves with three ovate leaflets and produces small, yellow, clustered flowers. The plant is characterized by its slender, branched stems and is often cultivated for its seeds, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications. Its botanical structure supports its adaptability to various soil types and climates.

The plant's growth cycle includes a rapid development phase, making it suitable for short growing seasons.
