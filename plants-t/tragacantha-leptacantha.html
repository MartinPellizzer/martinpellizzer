Tragacantha leptacantha

Tragacantha leptacantha, commonly known as the slender tragacanth, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant is characterized by its slender, spiny stems and small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment in degraded habitats.
