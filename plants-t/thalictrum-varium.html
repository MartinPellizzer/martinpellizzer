Thalictrum varium

Thalictrum varium, commonly known as the variable meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, branched stems that typically grow to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces delicate, compound leaves with a feathery appearance, and its flowers are small, greenish-white, and arranged in clusters. Thalictrum varium thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

It is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.
