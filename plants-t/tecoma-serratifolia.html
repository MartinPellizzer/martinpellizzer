Tecoma serratifolia

Tecoma serratifolia, commonly known as the yellow bells, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have serrated edges, contributing to its scientific name.

Tecoma serratifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun.
