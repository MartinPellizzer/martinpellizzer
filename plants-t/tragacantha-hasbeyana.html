Tragacantha hasbeyana

Tragacantha hasbeyana, commonly known as Persian gum or Astragalus hasbeyanus, is a flowering plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces a sticky, resinous substance called tragacanth, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a binding agent. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various pollinators and small fauna.
