Tragia pungens var. arabica

Tragia pungens var. arabica, commonly known as the spiny glory flower, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, characterized by its woody stems and spiny branches. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, often accompanied by small, globose fruits. It is adapted to thrive in dry conditions, with deep roots that allow it to access groundwater.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
