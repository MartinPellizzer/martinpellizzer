Tetrapterys paraguariensis subsp. glabrescens

Tetrapterys paraguariensis subsp.

glabrescens, commonly known as the Paraguayan tamarillo, is a species of flowering plant in the Solanaceae family. It is native to South America, particularly Paraguay and northern Argentina.

The plant is characterized by its distinctive, fleshy, egg-shaped fruits that are typically orange to red in color. The leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy coating.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is valued for both its ornamental appeal and edible fruits.
