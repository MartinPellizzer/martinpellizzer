Triplophyllum angustifolium

Triplophyllum angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved triplophyllum, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers with yellow disc florets and white ray florets, which bloom in the summer months. Its botanical features make it a notable species for ecological studies and horticultural interest.
