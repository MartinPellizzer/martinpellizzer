Trollius americanus

Trollius americanus, commonly known as the American globeflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It produces showy, yellow, globe-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and it has a short, stout stem.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
