Thecacoris madagascariensis var. madagascariensis

Thecacoris madagascariensis var. madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to Madagascar.

It features intricate, showy flowers with a unique coloration pattern, often displaying a combination of white, pink, and purple hues. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its native habitat. Its leaves are elongated and leathery, adapted to retain moisture in the humid tropical environment.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its beauty in botanical gardens and private collections.
