Taraxacum xinyuanicum

Taraxacum xinyuanicum, commonly known as the Xinyuan dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and a flowering stem topped with a composite flower head. The plant produces yellow ray florets that mature into white, fluffy seeds with parachute-like structures for wind dispersal. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

Due to its medicinal properties, it is also valued in traditional Chinese medicine.
