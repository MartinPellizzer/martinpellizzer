Taraxacum revalense

Taraxacum revalense, commonly known as the Reval dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to the Baltic region, including parts of Estonia, Latvia, and Lithuania. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and a yellow flower head that develops into a fluffy seed head. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and forest edges.

The species is noted for its ecological importance as a food source for various insects and its role in soil stabilization.
