Typha sakhalinica

Typha sakhalinica, commonly known as the Sakhalin cattail, is a wetland plant native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical flower spikes that are typically green or brown in color. The plant has broad, sword-shaped leaves that grow from a rhizomatous root system. It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Typha sakhalinica plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
