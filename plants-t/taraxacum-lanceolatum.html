Taraxacum lanceolatum

Taraxacum lanceolatum, commonly known as the lanceleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in early spring. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this species is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides. The plant has a taproot system and produces a capsule that contains numerous seeds with feathery parachutes.

It is considered a perennial herb and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
