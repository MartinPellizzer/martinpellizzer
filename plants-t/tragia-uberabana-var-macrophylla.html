Tragia uberabana var. macrophylla

Tragia uberabana var. macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaved tragia, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and large, lobed leaves that can reach up to 15 cm in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming during the dry season. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or arid environments.

The species is notable for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
