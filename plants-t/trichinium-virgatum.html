Trichinium virgatum

Trichinium virgatum, commonly known as Virginia spiderwort, is a herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture.

Trichinium virgatum is part of the Boraginaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
