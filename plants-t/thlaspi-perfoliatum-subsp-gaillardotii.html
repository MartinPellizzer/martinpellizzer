Thlaspi perfoliatum subsp. gaillardotii

Thlaspi perfoliatum subsp.

gaillardotii, commonly known as the Gaillardot's bladderwort, is a member of the Brassicaceae family. It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that often have a bluish-green hue.

The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in racemes, which are typical of the mustard family. It is typically found in moist, calcareous soils in alpine or subalpine environments.

This subspecies is notable for its ability to thrive in harsh, high-altitude conditions.
