Telinaria ramosissima

Telinaria ramosissima, commonly known as the hairy mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of host trees, primarily in the genus Eucalyptus.

It has a distinctive appearance with its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a soft, fuzzy texture. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its berries are typically red or orange, containing seeds that are dispersed by birds.

Telinaria ramosissima is often found in coastal and inland regions of Australia, where it thrives in a variety of environmental conditions.
