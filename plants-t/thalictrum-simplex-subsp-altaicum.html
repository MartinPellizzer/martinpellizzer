Thalictrum simplex subsp. altaicum

Thalictrum simplex subsp.

altaicum, commonly known as the alpine meadow rue, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in alpine and subalpine habitats.

The plant features delicate, fern-like leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early summer. It is characterized by its slender stems and is often found in open meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its specialized habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
