Trisetum flavescens var. variegatum

Trisetum flavescens var. variegatum, commonly known as the variegated bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted blades and a distinctive variegated coloration, often featuring light green and silver streaks along the leaf margins. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in prairie and woodland habitats. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

This grass is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in native plant landscapes.
