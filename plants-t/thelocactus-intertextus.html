Thelocactus intertextus

Thelocactus intertextus, commonly known as the Mexican moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its unique appearance, with a swollen, bulbous stem that resembles a moon. The plant has a thick, ribbed structure that helps it store water, adapting to its arid environment. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, adding a vibrant touch to its appearance.

Thelocactus intertextus is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its striking form and relatively easy care requirements.
