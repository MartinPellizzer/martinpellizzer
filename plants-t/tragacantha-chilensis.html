Tragacantha chilensis

Tragacantha chilensis, commonly known as Chilean tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow flowers. The plant produces a gum-like substance, which has been used historically for various purposes, including as a medicinal and adhesive agent. Its growth habit includes both shrub and tree forms, depending on environmental conditions.
