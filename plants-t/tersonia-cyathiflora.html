Tersonia cyathiflora

Tersonia cyathiflora, commonly known as the hooded bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant has a compact, shrub-like growth habit with hairy stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal environments.

Tersonia cyathiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
