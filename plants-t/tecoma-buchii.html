Tecoma buchii

Tecoma buchii, commonly known as the yellow bells, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Tecoma buchii thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
