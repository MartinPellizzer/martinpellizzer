Thymus × ramonianus

Thymus × ramonianus, commonly known as the Ramonian thyme, is a hybrid perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, low-growing habit and aromatic, small, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is often cultivated for both aesthetic and practical purposes.
