Tripterodendron filicifolium

Tripterodendron filicifolium, commonly known as the fern-leaved tripterodendron, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to the trunks of trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually bioluminescent, a unique trait that aids in attracting nocturnal pollinators.

The plant's botanical significance lies in its role within its ecosystem and its potential medicinal properties.
