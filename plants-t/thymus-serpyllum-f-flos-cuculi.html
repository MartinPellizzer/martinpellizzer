Thymus serpyllum f. flos-cuculi

Thymus serpyllum f. flos-cuculi, commonly known as wild thyme with a distinctive floral scent, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features small, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is fragrant and often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

This variety is notable for its unique floral fragrance, which sets it apart from other thyme species.
