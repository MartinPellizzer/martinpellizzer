Thymus mastichina subsp. tomentosus

Thymus mastichina subsp.

tomentosus, commonly known as the tomentose thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its dense, gray-green foliage covered in fine, fuzzy hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Its aromatic leaves are often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
