Trifolium longipes subsp. oreganum

Trifolium longipes subsp.

oreganum, commonly known as Oregon mountain clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval to lance-shaped leaflets arranged in a rosette.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or reddish in color, blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
