Thymus albanus subvar. korabensis

Thymus albanus subvar. korabensis, commonly known as Korab thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its aromatic, small, gray-green leaves and clusters of pink to purple flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine regions. It is valued for its essential oils, which are used in aromatherapy and culinary applications.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some areas.
