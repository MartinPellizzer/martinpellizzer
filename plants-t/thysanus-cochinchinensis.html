Thysanus cochinchinensis

Thysanus cochinchinensis, commonly known as the Vietnamese orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or white with intricate patterns, and it blooms during the rainy season.

Thysanus cochinchinensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
