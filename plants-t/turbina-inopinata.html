Turbina inopinata

Turbina inopinata, commonly known as the Caribbean turban snail, is a species of sea snail belonging to the family Turbinidae.

It is characterized by its large, spiral-shaped shell that is typically yellowish to brown in color. The shell is often adorned with intricate patterns and ridges, providing camouflage in its marine environment. This species is found in the tropical regions of the Atlantic Ocean, particularly around the Caribbean Sea and the Gulf of Mexico.

Turbina inopinata plays a role in marine ecosystems as both a predator and a prey species.
