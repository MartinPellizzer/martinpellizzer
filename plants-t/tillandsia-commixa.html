Tillandsia commixa

Tillandsia commixa, commonly known as the silver leaf tillandsia, is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is native to South America.

This epiphytic plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and does not require soil for growth. It possesses specialized structures called trichomes that absorb moisture and nutrients from the air. The plant's rosette-shaped leaves are covered in a dense layer of silver-colored hairs, which aid in water retention.

Tillandsia commixa is popular as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
