Thyrsanthemum goldianum

Thyrsanthemum goldianum, commonly known as the golden thyrsin, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers, giving it a sun-like appearance. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Thyrsanthemum goldianum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
