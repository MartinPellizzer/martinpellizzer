Turnera decipiens

Turnera decipiens, commonly known as the false mallow or golden turnera, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers, which bloom in clusters and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in the understory of rainforests. Its leaves are palmate, with five to seven lobes, and the stems are often hairy.

Turnera decipiens is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
