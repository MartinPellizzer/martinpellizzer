Tephrosia major

Tephrosia major, commonly known as the giant tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a legume, belonging to the Fabaceae family, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

Tephrosia major is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
