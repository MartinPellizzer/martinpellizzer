Trichopyrum intermedium subsp. pouzolzii

Trichopyrum intermedium subsp.

pouzolzii, commonly known as the European false brome, is a perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along riverbanks.

The plant has slender, erect stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed habitats.

This subspecies is characterized by its distinctive floral structure and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
