Thalictrum flavum var. exaltatum

Thalictrum flavum var. exaltatum, commonly known as the tall meadow rue, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves and produces clusters of yellowish-green, spicate inflorescences that bloom in early to mid-summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet, making it a striking addition to woodland gardens. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, often found in moist meadows and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and native plant gardens.
