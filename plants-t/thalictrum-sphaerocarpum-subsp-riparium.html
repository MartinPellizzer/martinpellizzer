Thalictrum sphaerocarpum subsp. riparium

Thalictrum sphaerocarpum subsp.

riparium, commonly known as the water avens, is a herbaceous perennial native to wetland and riparian habitats. It features delicate, fern-like leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in moist soils along streams, ponds, and other water bodies. Its subsp. riparium is distinguished by its preference for aquatic or semi-aquatic environments.

It plays a role in stabilizing shorelines and supporting local biodiversity.
