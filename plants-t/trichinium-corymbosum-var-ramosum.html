Trichinium corymbosum var. ramosum

Trichinium corymbosum var. ramosum, commonly known as the branched trichinium, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its branched stems and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks. Its leaves are usually opposite and have a rough, hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
