Turbinicarpus heliae

Turbinicarpus heliae, commonly known as the Heliae Turbinicarpus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cactaceae.

It is native to the arid regions of northern Mexico, specifically the state of Chihuahua. This cactus is characterized by its short, ribbed stems and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant is well adapted to its harsh environment, with thick, fleshy stems that store water and reduce transpiration.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it a fascinating subject for botanical study.
