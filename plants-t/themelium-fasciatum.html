Themelium fasciatum

Themelium fasciatum, commonly known as the spotted lily, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, spotted leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its leaves are elongated and have a distinctive mottled pattern, while its flowers bloom in early summer.

Themelium fasciatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
