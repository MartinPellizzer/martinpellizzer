Trillium morii

Trillium morii, commonly known as the Morii's trillium, is a rare flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its three large, heart-shaped leaves and a single, nodding white flower with three petals. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest understories, preferring rich, organic soil. Trillium morii is a perennial herb that blooms in early spring, often before its leaves fully expand.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered endangered in many areas.
