Trasus pilulifer

Trasus pilulifer, commonly known as the pill bug plant, is a unique species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, succulent leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, adding a delicate visual appeal. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.

Despite its modest appearance, Trasus pilulifer plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing nectar for pollinators and shelter for small invertebrates.
