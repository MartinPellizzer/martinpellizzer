Taraxacum auricula

Taraxacum auricula, commonly known as the golden dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by fluffy white seeds that are dispersed by the wind. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in open, sunny areas and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Taraxacum auricula is a hardy perennial that is well adapted to temperate climates.
