Taraxacum spilophylloides

Taraxacum spilophylloides, commonly known as the purple ray flover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the dandelion genus, characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and a hollow, leafless stem. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that develop into tufted, white to purplish pappus-bearing seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats, often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Asteraceae family.
