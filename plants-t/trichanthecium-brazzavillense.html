Trichanthecium brazzavillense

Trichanthecium brazzavillense, commonly known as the Congo aster, is a flowering plant native to Central Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, daisy-like flowers with yellow ray florets. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and swamps.

Its botanical structure supports its role in local ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
