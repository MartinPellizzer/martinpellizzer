Trachypogon polymorphus var. karwinskyi

Trachypogon polymorphus var. karwinskyi, commonly known as the Karwinsky's grass, is a perennial grass species native to certain regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The inflorescence consists of a spike-like arrangement of small, spikelets, which are typically green to pale brown in color. This variety is often found in grasslands and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of study in grassland botany.
