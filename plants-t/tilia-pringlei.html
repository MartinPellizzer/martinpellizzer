Tilia pringlei

Tilia pringlei, commonly known as Pringle's lime, is a small deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of central China.

It belongs to the Tiliaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, which bloom in early summer. Its fruit is a small, round capsule that contains several seeds.

Tilia pringlei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
