Tragacantha bachtiarica

Tragacantha bachtiarica, commonly known as Persian tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Iran.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and woody pods. The plant is known for producing a viscous gum, which has been used traditionally in various medicinal and culinary applications.

Its adaptability to harsh climates makes it an important species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
