Tephroseris integrifolia subsp. caucasigena

Tephroseris integrifolia subsp.

caucasigena, commonly known as the Caucasian tephroseris, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to temperate climates.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, pale yellow flowers during the summer months. It is part of the Apiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs.
