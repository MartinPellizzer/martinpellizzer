Tephrosia dasyphylla subsp. dasyphylla

Tephrosia dasyphylla subsp.

dasyphylla, commonly known as the hairy tephrosia, is a shrub native to tropical Africa. It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often hairy and have a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The species is notable for its medicinal uses and potential in ecological restoration projects.
