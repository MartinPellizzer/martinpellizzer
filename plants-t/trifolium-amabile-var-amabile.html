Trifolium amabile var. amabile

Trifolium amabile var. amabile, commonly known as the sweet clover, is a flowering plant in the legume family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that are clustered in spherical heads. It is often found in meadows, pastures, and along roadsides.

Trifolium amabile var. amabile is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable for agricultural purposes.
