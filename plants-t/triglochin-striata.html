Triglochin striata

Triglochin striata, commonly known as the narrowleaf marsh grass, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, linear leaves with a distinctive white or pale midrib, and its small, inconspicuous flowers appear in clusters. It is adapted to tolerate fluctuating water levels and poor soil conditions.

Triglochin striata plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates.
