Tithymalus portlandicus

Tithymalus portlandicus, commonly known as the Portland thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the British Isles.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has aromatic, needle-like leaves that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its historical use in herbal medicine and as a culinary herb highlights its botanical and cultural significance.
