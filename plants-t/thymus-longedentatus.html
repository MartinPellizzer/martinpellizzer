Thymus longedentatus

Thymus longedentatus, commonly known as the long-toothed thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its low-growing, creeping habit and small, fragrant leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces pink to purple flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its aromatic foliage contains essential oils, making it useful in both culinary and medicinal applications.

Thymus longedentatus thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry environments.
