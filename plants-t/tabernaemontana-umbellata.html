Tabernaemontana umbellata

Tabernaemontana umbellata, commonly known as the umbrella tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive umbrella-like inflorescence, which consists of numerous white to pale yellow flowers arranged in a circular pattern. The plant produces fragrant blooms that are often used in traditional medicine for their purported therapeutic properties. Its leaves are broad and glossy, contributing to its ornamental appeal in gardens and as a houseplant.

Tabernaemontana umbellata thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
