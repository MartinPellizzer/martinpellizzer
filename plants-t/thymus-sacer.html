Thymus sacer

Thymus sacer, commonly known as holy thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, gray-green leaves and compact, flowering spikes that produce pink to purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species. Its aromatic foliage contains essential oils, which contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.

Botanically, it belongs to the Lamiaceae family, sharing traits with other members like mint and oregano.
