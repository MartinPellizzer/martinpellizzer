Trophis zeylanica

Trophis zeylanica, commonly known as the Ceylon tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Sri Lanka and southern India.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated, woody pods containing edible seeds surrounded by a sticky, acidic pulp. It is valued for its timber, which is used in construction and furniture-making, and for its medicinal properties.

The tree also plays an important role in traditional herbal remedies and local ecosystems.
