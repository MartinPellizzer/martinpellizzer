Tropaeolum × massiliense

Tropaeolum × massiliense, commonly known as the French sorrel or garden sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Tropaeolaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, spurred flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are edible and often used in salads or as a garnish. It thrives in cool climates and is often grown as an ornamental or culinary plant.

Tropaeolum × massiliense is also known for its ability to attract beneficial insects to the garden.
