Thymus pseudonummularius

Thymus pseudonummularius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant, grey-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and as a ground cover.

Its aromatic properties make it a popular choice for culinary and medicinal uses.
