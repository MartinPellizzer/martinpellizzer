Taetsia ferrea

Taetsia ferrea, commonly known as the ironwood plant, is a tropical species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood, which is highly valued for its durability and strength. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 15 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a glossy appearance.

Taetsia ferrea thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.
