Triphysaria floribunda

Triphysaria floribunda, commonly known as the clustered bladderpod, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. Its flowers are small and yellow, often arranged in clusters, and it produces seed pods that split open to release seeds.

The species is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to nutrient cycling in its ecosystem.
