Trifolium xerocephalum var. cruentum

Trifolium xerocephalum var. cruentum, commonly known as the crimson clover, is a perennial legume characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it a valuable forage crop. Its flowers range in color from pink to deep red, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardiness and adaptability.
