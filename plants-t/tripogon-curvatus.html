Tripogon curvatus

Tripogon curvatus, commonly known as the curved spikegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. The plant has slender, erect stems that bear clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Tripogon curvatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
