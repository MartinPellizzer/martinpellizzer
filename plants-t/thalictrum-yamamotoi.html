Thalictrum yamamotoi

Thalictrum yamamotoi, commonly known as Yamamoto's meadow-rue, is a herbaceous perennial native to Japan.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves and produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodland areas and has a slender, branching stem. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and it produces small, dry fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Thalictrum yamamotoi is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and subtle floral display.
