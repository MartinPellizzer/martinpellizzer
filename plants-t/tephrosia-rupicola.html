Tephrosia rupicola

Tephrosia rupicola, commonly known as rock tephrosia, is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that typically range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in dry, arid environments. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Tephrosia rupicola is also noted for its medicinal properties, used traditionally in some cultures for its potential health benefits.
