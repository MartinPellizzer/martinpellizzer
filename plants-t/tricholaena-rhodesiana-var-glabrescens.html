Tricholaena rhodesiana var. glabrescens

Tricholaena rhodesiana var. glabrescens, commonly known as Rhodesian grass or African silver grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically silver-gray in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant grows in dense clumps and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are wind-pollinated, and its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread readily.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
