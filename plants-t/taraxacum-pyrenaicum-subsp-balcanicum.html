Taraxacum pyrenaicum subsp. balcanicum

Taraxacum pyrenaicum subsp.

balcanicum, commonly known as the Balkan dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of stiff, hairy leaves.

The plant produces a yellow flower head that develops into a spherical, fluffy seed head known as a pappus. It thrives in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

Its morphology and distribution reflect its adaptation to the temperate climate of its native region.
