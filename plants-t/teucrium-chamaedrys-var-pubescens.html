Teucrium chamaedrys var. pubescens

Teucrium chamaedrys var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy germander, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, creeping stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly aromatic scent. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional use in herbal medicine.
