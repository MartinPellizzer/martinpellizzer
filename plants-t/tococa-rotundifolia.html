Tococa rotundifolia

Tococa rotundifolia, commonly known as the Amazonian bitter apple, is a climbing shrub native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, orange-red berries that contain a single seed. It is known for its medicinal properties, with the leaves and fruits used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique adaptations to the humid, nutrient-poor environments of the Amazon.
