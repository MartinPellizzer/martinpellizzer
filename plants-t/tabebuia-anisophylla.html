Tabebuia anisophylla

Tabebuia anisophylla, commonly known as the pink trumpet tree, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The tree has a straight trunk and a broad, spreading crown, with compound leaves consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets. Its wood is valued for its durability and is used in construction and furniture making.

The plant is also known for its ornamental value and is often planted in urban landscapes for its aesthetic appeal.
