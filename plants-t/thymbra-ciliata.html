Thymbra ciliata

Thymbra ciliata, commonly known as the woolly thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by aromatic foliage and square stems. The plant features small, pink or white flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are covered in fine, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Thymbra ciliata thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

It is also valued for its fragrant properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
