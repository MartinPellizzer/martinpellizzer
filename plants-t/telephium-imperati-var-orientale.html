Telephium imperati var. orientale

Telephium imperati var. orientale, commonly known as the oriental sandwort, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, silver-gray leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink or white. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and alpine settings.
