Taraxacum vitellinum

Taraxacum vitellinum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which bloom in the spring and are surrounded by a dense ring of bracts. The plant has a rosette of basal leaves that are typically hairy and have a waxy coating. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grasslands.

Taraxacum vitellinum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions and is sometimes considered invasive in certain regions.
