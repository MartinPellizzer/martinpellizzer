Tetraedrocarpus arabicus

Tetraedrocarpus arabicus, commonly known as the Arabian tamarisk, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh desert conditions. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access water from deep underground.

Tetraedrocarpus arabicus is often used in traditional medicine and landscaping due to its resilience and ornamental value.
