Triodia avenacea var. pygmaea

Triodia avenacea var. pygmaea, commonly known as the spinifex grass, is a low-growing, perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It forms dense, mat-like clumps with wiry, needle-like leaves that are adapted to withstand extreme drought and high temperatures. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. Its deep root system enables it to access water from deeper soil layers, making it highly resilient in harsh environments.

This species plays a crucial role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife in its native range.
