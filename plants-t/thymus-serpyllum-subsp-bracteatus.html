Thymus serpyllum subsp. bracteatus

Thymus serpyllum subsp.

bracteatus, commonly known as narrowleaf thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It features aromatic, small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color, with a distinctive, pungent fragrance.

The plant produces pink to violet flowers in clusters, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and medicinal herb gardens.

This subspecies is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, similar to other thyme species.
