Trisetum litvinowii var. argenteum

Trisetum litvinowii var. argenteum, commonly known as silver feather grass, is a variant of the grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its finely textured, silvery-blue foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces slender, feathery spikes that are often used in ornamental landscaping. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for dry garden environments.

Its unique coloration and delicate structure make it a popular choice for drought-tolerant gardens.
