Terminalia valverdeae

Terminalia valverdeae, commonly known as the African snowball tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and compound leaves that are opposite or alternate in arrangement. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by fleshy, red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas with high humidity.

Terminalia valverdeae is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in its native habitat.
