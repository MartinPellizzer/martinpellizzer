Taraxacum magnobliquum

Taraxacum magnobliquum, commonly known as the grandiose dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers and heart-shaped basal leaves. The plant produces a distinctive seed head composed of numerous parachute-like seeds, which are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows, lawns, and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and aesthetically notable.
