Turnera corchoroides

Turnera corchoroides, commonly known as the Mexican moonflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a member of the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its showy, white, night-blooming flowers. The plant has opposite, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded or semi-shaded areas.

The flowers emit a fragrant scent, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.
