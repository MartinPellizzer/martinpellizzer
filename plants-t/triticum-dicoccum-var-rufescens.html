Triticum dicoccum var. rufescens

Triticum dicoccum var. rufescens, commonly known as einkorn rye, is a wild grass species native to the Fertile Crescent.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and spikelets that bear small, reddish-brown grains. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is well adapted to temperate climates. Its grains are rich in protein and nutrients, making it a valuable ancient grain for both historical and modern agricultural use.

The plant's botanical features include a fibrous root system and a complex inflorescence structure.
