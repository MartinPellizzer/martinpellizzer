Triumfetta indurata

Triumfetta indurata, commonly known as the hardy spiderflower, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink or red, often resembling spiders due to their shape. The plant has woody stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Triumfetta indurata is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive blooms and resilience.
