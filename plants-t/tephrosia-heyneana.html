Tephrosia heyneana

Tephrosia heyneana, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring trifoliate leaves and small, clustered yellow flowers. Its root system is known for producing a milky sap, which is toxic and has been used in traditional medicine.

The plant also serves as a host for certain butterfly species, contributing to local biodiversity.
