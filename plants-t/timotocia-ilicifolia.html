Timotocia ilicifolia

Timotocia ilicifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved holly-leaf, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern California and Baja California.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically glossy and dark green, with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers in clusters, which are followed by bright red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well adapted to coastal climates.

Timotocia ilicifolia plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
