Tolpis succulenta var. linearifolia

Tolpis succulenta var. linearifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It features a low-growing, mat-forming habit with fleshy, blue-green leaves that are linear and often covered in a fine white powder. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange, typically during the spring and early summer. It is well adapted to drought conditions, storing water in its thick leaves and stems.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in rocky or sandy soils with minimal water.
