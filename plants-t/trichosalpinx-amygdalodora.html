Trichosalpinx amygdalodora

Trichosalpinx amygdalodora, commonly known as the sweet scent orchid, is a species native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a distinctive appearance, featuring a slender, branching stem and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant has a fibrous root system and is often found growing in shaded, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, hairy texture.

The orchid is noted for its subtle, sweet fragrance, which is most noticeable during the evening hours.
