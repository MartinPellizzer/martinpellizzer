Trichinium brachyanthum

Trichinium brachyanthum, commonly known as the slender false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and wetlands. The plant features alternate, lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces, and its flowers are small, yellowish, and clustered in a head-like structure. It produces a capsule fruit that contains numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The species is characterized by its delicate appearance and is often found in disturbed or semi-aquatic environments.
