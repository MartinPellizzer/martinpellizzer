Thymus montanus f. punctatus

Thymus montanus f. punctatus, commonly known as the spotted thrift, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and grayish-green foliage, with distinctive white or pale spots on the leaves. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers in dense clusters during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in garden settings.
