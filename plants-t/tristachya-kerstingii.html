Tristachya kerstingii

Tristachya kerstingii, commonly known as the Kersting's panic grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and long, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences, which are typical of the grass family Poaceae. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or along riverbanks.

Tristachya kerstingii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its graceful appearance.
