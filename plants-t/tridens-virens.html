Tridens virens

Tridens virens, commonly known as green false brome, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, three-lobed flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including meadows, prairies, and disturbed soils, and is often found in open, sunny areas.

Tridens virens is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
