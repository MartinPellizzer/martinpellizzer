Tillandsia leiochlamys

Tillandsia leiochlamys, commonly known as the silver-tipped air plant, is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is native to Central and South America.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on other plants without deriving nutrients from them. The plant features long, narrow leaves that are covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help it absorb moisture and nutrients from the air. It produces striking, bell-shaped inflorescences with bright orange to red flowers.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
