Typhonium orbifolium

Typhonium orbifolium, commonly known as the giant swamp taro, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, umbrella-shaped leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in diameter, giving it a dramatic appearance. The plant produces striking purple or red spadices surrounded by white bracts, which are typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in wet, swampy environments and is often found in marshes and along riverbanks.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in botanical gardens and as a houseplant in some regions.
