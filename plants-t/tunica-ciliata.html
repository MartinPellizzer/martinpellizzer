Tunica ciliata

Tunica ciliata, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spikes. Tunica ciliata is often found in disturbed soils and can become invasive in certain ecosystems.

It is valued in some regions for its medicinal properties and as a source of fibers.
