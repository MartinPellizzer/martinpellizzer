Tripogon subtilissimus

Tripogon subtilissimus, commonly known as the slender grass or narrow-leaved grass, is a species of grass native to various regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing minute flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Due to its delicate structure and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
