Tsuga canadensis paucifolia

Tsuga canadensis paucifolia, commonly known as the eastern hemlock, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its drooping branches and needle-like leaves that are dark green and arranged in spirals. The tree produces small, spherical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color. Its bark is smooth and gray when young, becoming fissured with age.

This species plays a vital role in forest ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
