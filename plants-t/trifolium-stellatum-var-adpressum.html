Trifolium stellatum var. adpressum

Trifolium stellatum var. adpressum, commonly known as the star clover, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with three oval or ovate leaflets, often with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed sites.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental appeal make it a popular choice for erosion control and ornamental gardening.
