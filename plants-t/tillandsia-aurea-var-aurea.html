Tillandsia aurea var. aurea

Tillandsia aurea var. aurea, commonly known as the golden tillandsia, is a species of air plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are typically green with a striking golden hue. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on other plants without being parasitic, and it absorbs moisture and nutrients through its leaves. It produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are usually yellow or orange, adding to its ornamental value.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
