Turnera luetzelburgii

Turnera luetzelburgii, commonly known as the Mexican flameflower, is a perennial shrub native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or orange tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners in warm climates.

The botanical features of Turnera luetzelburgii make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable.
