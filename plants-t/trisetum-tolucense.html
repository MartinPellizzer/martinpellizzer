Trisetum tolucense

Trisetum tolucense, commonly known as the Tolucense wiregrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Due to its ecological significance, it is often found in conservation efforts aimed at preserving wetland ecosystems.
