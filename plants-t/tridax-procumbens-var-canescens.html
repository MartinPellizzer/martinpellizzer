Tridax procumbens var. canescens

Tridax procumbens var. canescens, commonly known as the hairy tridax, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is a low-growing annual weed characterized by its hairy, sprawling stems and small, yellowish-green composite flowers. The leaves are typically oval or lance-shaped with a rough texture and are covered in fine hairs. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and vegetative reproduction.
