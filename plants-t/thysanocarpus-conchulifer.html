Thysanocarpus conchulifer

Thysanocarpus conchulifer, commonly known as the shell-bearing acanthus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Acanthaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy bracts that resemble shell-like structures, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually opposite and have a rough, hairy texture.

The flowers are clustered in inflorescences and are often surrounded by colorful, modified leaves called bracts.
