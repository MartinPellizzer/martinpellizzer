Thymus jankae var. jugoslavicus

Thymus jankae var. jugoslavicus, commonly known as the Balkan thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It features compact, woody stems covered in small, aromatic leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun exposure.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
