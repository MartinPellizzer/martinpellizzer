Teucrium buxifolium subsp. albidum

Teucrium buxifolium subsp.

albidum, commonly known as whiteleaf germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features small, narrow leaves that are typically covered in a fine, white pubescence, giving it its common name.

The plant produces clusters of pale blue to purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
