Topobea crassifolia

Topobea crassifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf topobea, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, an adaptation to its harsh, dry environment. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access groundwater in nutrient-poor soils.

Topobea crassifolia is often found in rocky habitats and is considered a symbol of resilience in desert ecosystems.
