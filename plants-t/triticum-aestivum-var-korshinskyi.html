Triticum aestivum var. korshinskyi

Triticum aestivum var. korshinskyi, commonly known as Korshinsky wheat, is a botanical variant of common wheat characterized by its unique genetic makeup and morphological traits.

It is a hexaploid species, possessing six sets of chromosomes, which contributes to its genetic diversity and adaptability. The plant typically exhibits semi-dwarf stature with awned spikelets and glumes, typical of many wheat species. It is native to Central Asia and is often studied for its potential in breeding programs aimed at improving wheat resilience.

Its botanical features make it a valuable resource for understanding wheat evolution and agricultural improvement.
