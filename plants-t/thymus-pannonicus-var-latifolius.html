Thymus pannonicus var. latifolius

Thymus pannonicus var. latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf thyme, is a perennial herb native to Central and Southeastern Europe.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color and have a slightly fuzzy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone-rich habitats.

This variety is valued for its aromatic foliage and is commonly used in culinary and medicinal applications.
