Thymus almoradux

Thymus almoradux, commonly known as the Almoradux thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic leaves and square stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, thriving in sunny, well-drained environments. Its small, pinkish-white flowers bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The aromatic foliage is often used in traditional herbal medicine and culinary applications.
