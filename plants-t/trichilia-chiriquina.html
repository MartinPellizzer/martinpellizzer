Trichilia chiriquina

Trichilia chiriquina, commonly known as the Guatemalan chicle tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and large, palmate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by oval, woody fruits containing a sticky, latex-rich pulp. Its sap, known as chicle, was historically used as a base for chewing gum.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
