Tabernaemontana johnstonii

Tabernaemontana johnstonii, commonly known as the Johnston's star jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, star-shaped white flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and potential medicinal properties.
