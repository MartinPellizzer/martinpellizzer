Taiwania flousiana

Taiwania flousiana, commonly known as the Formosan fir, is a coniferous tree native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the family Pinaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pyramidal shape. The tree has needle-like leaves that are typically dark green and arranged in spirals along the branches. Its cones are woody and contain seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This species plays an important ecological role in the mountainous regions of Taiwan, contributing to forest biodiversity and soil stability.
