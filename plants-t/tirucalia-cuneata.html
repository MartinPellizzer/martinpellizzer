Tirucalia cuneata

Tirucalia cuneata, commonly known as the Indian flame tree, is a tropical plant native to regions of India and Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, featuring a broad, umbrella-shaped crown. The leaves are pinnately compound, with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. Its showy, orange-red flowers appear in clusters and bloom during the rainy season, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a street tree due to its striking foliage and floral displays.
