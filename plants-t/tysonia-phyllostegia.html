Tysonia phyllostegia

Tysonia phyllostegia, commonly known as the swamp mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wetlands and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to waterlogged soils, allowing it to thrive in moist environments.

Tysonia phyllostegia plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
