Tecticornia halocnemoides subsp. catenulata

Tecticornia halocnemoides subsp.

catenulata, commonly known as the coastal spinifex, is a halophytic shrub adapted to saline and arid environments. It typically grows in coastal dunes and salt marshes, where it tolerates high salinity and extreme weather conditions.

The plant has a low, sprawling growth habit with narrow, needle-like leaves that reduce water loss. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually green or brown, and it produces fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds.

This subspecies plays a vital role in stabilizing coastal soils and preventing erosion.
