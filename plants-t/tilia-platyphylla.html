Tilia platyphylla

Tilia platyphylla, commonly known as the broad-leaved linden, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are broad and often have a wavy margin. The tree produces small, fragrant, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are clustered in drooping inflorescences. Its bark is smooth and grey, becoming fissured with age, and it is known for its rapid growth and adaptability to various soil types.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its aromatic properties.
