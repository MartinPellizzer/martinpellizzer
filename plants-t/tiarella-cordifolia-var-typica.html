Tiarella cordifolia var. typica

Tiarella cordifolia var. typica, commonly known as the heartleaf foamflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It features heart-shaped, lobed leaves that are typically bright green and velvety in texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers in early to mid-summer. It thrives in moist, shady woodland environments and is often found in rich, well-drained soils.

This species is valued in gardens for its ornamental foliage and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
