Therebina

Therebina, commonly known as the black thorn or black acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, pale green leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by long, flat pods containing seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in savannas and rocky areas.

Therebina is adapted to withstand drought and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
