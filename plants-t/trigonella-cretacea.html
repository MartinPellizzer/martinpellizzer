Trigonella cretacea

Trigonella cretacea, commonly known as Cretan bitter vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial legume characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces slender, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is adapted to arid and rocky environments, often found in coastal areas and limestone soils.

Trigonella cretacea plays a role in soil stabilization and supports local pollinators.
