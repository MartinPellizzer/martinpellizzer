Taraxacum simile f. acutum

Taraxacum simile f. acutum, commonly known as the sharp-leaved dandelion, is a botanical variant of the common dandelion.

It is characterized by its acuminate (sharply pointed) leaves and yellow composite flowers that develop into elongated, hollow seed stems. This subspecies is typically found in disturbed soils and open habitats across temperate regions. Its foliage is hairy and often has a basal rosette structure.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
