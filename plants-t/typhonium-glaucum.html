Typhonium glaucum

Typhonium glaucum, commonly known as the blue dragon arum, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive blue-green hue, often adorned with a waxy, glossy surface. The plant produces an inflorescence that resembles a spadix, surrounded by a showy spathe, typical of the Araceae family. Its growth habit includes a rhizomatous underground stem, which allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

Typhonium glaucum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded, moist environments.
