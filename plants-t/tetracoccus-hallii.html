Tetracoccus hallii

Tetracoccus hallii, commonly known as the African snowball, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical clusters of white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has small, leathery leaves and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It is often used in ornamental gardening due to its striking appearance.

The species is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility in its native habitat.
