Taraxacum ochraceum

Taraxacum ochraceum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the dandelion family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and can form dense colonies, often outcompeting other vegetation. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while its roots are fibrous and adapted for survival in various soil types.

The species is noted for its ability to thrive in disturbed habitats, making it a common sight in meadows and along roadsides.
