Tectaria coriandrifolia

Tectaria coriandrifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf shield fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Tectariaceae and is characterized by its large, shield-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows on rocks or tree trunks in moist, shaded environments. Its fronds are bi- or tri-pinnate, with a distinctive coriaceous texture.

Tectaria coriandrifolia is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
