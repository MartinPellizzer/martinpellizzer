Trichogyne spicata

Trichogyne spicata, commonly known as the spiny trichogyne, is a unique plant species characterized by its specialized reproductive structures.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is native to tropical regions of South America. The plant is distinguished by its elongated, spiky trichogynes, which are specialized cells involved in the fertilization process. These structures play a crucial role in the plant's reproductive biology by facilitating the attachment of pollen grains.

Trichogyne spicata is also noted for its ornamental value due to its striking floral morphology and resilience in diverse environmental conditions.
