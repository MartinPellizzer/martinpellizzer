Trimorpha uniflora

Trimorpha uniflora, commonly known as the single-flower trimorpha, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant has a compact growth habit with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Trimorpha uniflora is considered a rare and endangered species.
