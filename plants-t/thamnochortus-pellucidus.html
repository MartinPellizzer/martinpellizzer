Thamnochortus pellucidus

Thamnochortus pellucidus, commonly known as the translucent everlasting or the glassy everlasting, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is native to South Africa, particularly the Western Cape region, where it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth form and its striking, translucent blue to violet flowers. Its foliage consists of dense, silvery-gray leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs.

Thamnochortus pellucidus is well adapted to arid conditions and is often found in coastal and mountainous areas.
