Taraxacum imaizumii

Taraxacum imaizumii, commonly known as the Imaizumi's dandelion, is a flowering plant species native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant features a rosette of basal, hairy leaves and a hollow, leafless stem that supports its yellow flower head. Its flowers develop into white, fluffy seeds with parachute-like structures for wind dispersal.

This species is adapted to thrive in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.
