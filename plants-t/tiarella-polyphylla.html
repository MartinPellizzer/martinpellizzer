Tiarella polyphylla

Tiarella polyphylla, commonly known as the white foamflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It features a rosette of broad, waxy, dark green leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the edges. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers in early to mid-spring. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in woodlands and stream banks.

Its foliage and flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in shaded garden beds.
