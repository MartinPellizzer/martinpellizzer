Tetrapterys argentea

Tetrapterys argentea, commonly known as silverleaf acanthus, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in a silvery, waxy coating. The plant produces showy, spire-like inflorescences with small, greenish-yellow flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Tetrapterys argentea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive foliage.
