Thalictrum thunbergii var. divaricatum

Thalictrum thunbergii var. divaricatum, commonly known as the variable meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia.

It features slender, branching stems that often grow in a spreading or creeping manner. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on long, slender pedicels. Its leaves are compound, with finely divided leaflets that give it a delicate, lacy appearance.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.
