Trigonotis robusta

Trigonotis robusta, commonly known as the robust trigonotis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with opposite, ovate leaves. Its flowers are borne in clusters and are known for their showy, trumpet-shaped appearance.

Trigonotis robusta is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
