Thysanocarpus desertorum

Thysanocarpus desertorum, commonly known as the desert thysanocarp, is a shrub native to arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its small, woody fruits that have a unique, flame-like appearance, giving it its scientific name. The plant has adapted to harsh desert conditions with deep root systems and drought-resistant foliage. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring.

Thysanocarpus desertorum plays an important role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
