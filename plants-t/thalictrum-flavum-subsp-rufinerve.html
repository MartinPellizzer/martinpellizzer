Thalictrum flavum subsp. rufinerve

Thalictrum flavum subsp.

rufinerve, commonly known as the red-veined meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and western Asia. It features delicate, compound leaves with a distinctive reddish vein pattern, and its showy, yellow flowers are arranged in upright clusters.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodlands and meadows. It is characterized by its finely divided foliage and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

The subspecies is distinguished by its more prominent, reddish veins compared to the typical form of T. flavum.
