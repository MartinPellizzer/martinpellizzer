Thinopyrum curvifolium

Thinopyrum curvifolium, commonly known as the curlyleaf wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often curly leaves and compact growth habit. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils. It is valued for its hardiness and ability to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

Thinopyrum curvifolium is also noted for its potential in forage and as a genetic resource for wheat improvement.
