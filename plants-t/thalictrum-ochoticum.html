Thalictrum ochoticum

Thalictrum ochoticum, commonly known as the Ochotic meadow rue, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like foliage and clusters of small, usually yellowish or greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its flowers bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Thalictrum ochoticum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in natural habitats.
