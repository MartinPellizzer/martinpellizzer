Thea cavaleriana

Thea cavaleriana, commonly known as the Cavaleriana tea plant, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant is known for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of aromatic essential oils. Its botanical features include a woody stem and a preference for warm, humid climates.

The leaves are often used in herbal preparations due to their aromatic properties.
