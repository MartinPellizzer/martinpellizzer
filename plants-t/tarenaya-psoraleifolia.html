Tarenaya psoraleifolia

Tarenaya psoraleifolia, commonly known as the coastal morning glory, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink and purple. The plant thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in dune environments. It is an annual herb that produces small, bean-like pods containing seeds.

Tarenaya psoraleifolia is adapted to withstand salty air and strong coastal winds.
