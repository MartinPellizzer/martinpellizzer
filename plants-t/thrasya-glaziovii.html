Thrasya glaziovii

Thrasya glaziovii, commonly known as Glaziov's thrash, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
