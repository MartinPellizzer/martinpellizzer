Thelypteris falcatula

Thelypteris falcatula, commonly known as the narrow-leaved water fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are typically narrow and elongated. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found near streams or in humid forests. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Thelypteris falcatula plays a role in maintaining soil stability and providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
