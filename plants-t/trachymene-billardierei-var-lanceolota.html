Trachymene billardierei var. lanceolota

Trachymene billardierei var. lanceolota, commonly known as the lanceolate false shamrock, is a perennial herb native to Australia.

It features slender, erect stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers with a distinctive, spurred petal at the base. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
