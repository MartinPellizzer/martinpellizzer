Teucrium japonicum var. pilosum

Teucrium japonicum var. pilosum, commonly known as Japanese germander, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the branches. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
