Thalictrum cysticarpum

Thalictrum cysticarpum, commonly known as the meadow rue, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette, giving it a delicate, lace-like appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early summer, clustered in cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges, meadows, and streambanks.

Thalictrum cysticarpum is valued in gardens for its ornamental foliage and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators and supporting biodiversity.
