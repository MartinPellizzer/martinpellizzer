Tragacantha granatensis

Tragacantha granatensis, commonly known as the granada tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces a resinous gum, which has been historically used in various traditional applications. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in scrubland and woodland habitats.

Due to its ecological and historical significance, it is considered a valuable species in botanical studies.
