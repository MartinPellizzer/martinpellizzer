Trifolium alexandrinum subsp. vavilovii

Trifolium alexandrinum subsp.

vavilovii, commonly known as Alexandrian clover, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which typically consist of three oval-shaped leaflets.

The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in forage and soil enrichment due to its nitrogen-fixing abilities.

This subspecies is valued for its adaptability and ecological benefits in agricultural and natural landscapes.
