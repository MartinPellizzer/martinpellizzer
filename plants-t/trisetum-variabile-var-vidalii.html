Trisetum variabile var. vidalii

Trisetum variabile var. vidalii, commonly known as the variable feathergrass, is a grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and delicate, feathery inflorescences that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a tendency to curl at the tips.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often used in landscaping for its elegant, airy look.
