Tradescantia multiflora var. parviflora

Tradescantia multiflora var. parviflora, commonly known as spiderwort, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a waxy, bluish-green appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and ability to naturalize in suitable climates.
