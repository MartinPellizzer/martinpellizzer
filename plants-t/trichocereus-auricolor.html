Trichocereus auricolor

Trichocereus auricolor, commonly known as the golden cactus or the sacred cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces striking yellow flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. Its stems are covered in spines, which provide protection from herbivores.

Trichocereus auricolor is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has cultural significance in some indigenous communities.
