Triadenia aegyptica

Triadenia aegyptica, commonly known as the Egyptian triad, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. Its leaves are narrow and linear, adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry environments.

Triadenia aegyptica is often found in rocky soils and is well-suited to xerophytic conditions.
