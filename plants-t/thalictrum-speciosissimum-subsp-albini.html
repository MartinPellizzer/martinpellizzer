Thalictrum speciosissimum subsp. albini

Thalictrum speciosissimum subsp.

albini, commonly known as the white meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America. It features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are alternate and emit a mild, aromatic scent.

The plant produces showy, upright clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers during late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for woodland gardens and naturalized landscapes.
