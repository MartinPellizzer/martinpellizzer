Thelypteris millei

Thelypteris millei, commonly known as the Japanese shield fern, is a species of fern native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its lanceolate, leathery fronds that are often covered with a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Thelypteris millei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
