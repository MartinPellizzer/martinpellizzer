Tirucalia ephedroides

Tirucalia ephedroides, commonly known as Indian ephedra, is a shrub native to the Indian subcontinent and parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Rutaceae and is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant contains alkaloids, including ephedrine, which have been used traditionally for their medicinal properties. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

Due to its chemical composition, it has attracted interest for both pharmaceutical and horticultural applications.
