Tussacia pulchella

Tussacia pulchella, commonly known as the purple tussock grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and produces striking purple to violet flowers in late spring. The plant has fibrous roots and narrow, grass-like leaves that form a compact rosette. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

Tussacia pulchella is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
