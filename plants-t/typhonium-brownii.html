Typhonium brownii

Typhonium brownii, commonly known as the giant arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial with large, arrowhead-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces striking, spadix-like inflorescences surrounded by a colorful spathe. Its rhizomes are edible and have been traditionally used as a starchy food source.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in swampy or wetland habitats.
