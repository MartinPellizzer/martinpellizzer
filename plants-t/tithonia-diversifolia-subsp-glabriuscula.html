Tithonia diversifolia subsp. glabriuscula

Tithonia diversifolia subsp.

glabriuscula, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America. It is characterized by its large, bright orange to red composite flowers and heart-shaped leaves.

The plant is a member of the Asteraceae family and is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in various soil conditions. It is often used in ornamental gardening and has been introduced to other regions for its aesthetic value.

Additionally, it has been studied for its potential in phytoremediation due to its ability to absorb heavy metals.
