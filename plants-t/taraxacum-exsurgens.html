Taraxacum exsurgens

Taraxacum exsurgens, commonly known as the upsurging dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its robust, upright stem and bright yellow composite flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces a distinctive pappus, a feathery structure that aids in seed dispersal by wind. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including grasslands and disturbed soils, and is often found in temperate regions.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
