Tradescantia elongata

Tradescantia elongata, commonly known as the snake plant or mother-in-law's tongue, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caribbean.

It features long, narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are typically green with distinctive red or purple margins. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and ability to thrive in low-light conditions. It produces small, inconspicuous white flowers on slender stalks during the growing season.

Tradescantia elongata is known for its air-purifying qualities and is widely cultivated as an ornamental and indoor plant.
