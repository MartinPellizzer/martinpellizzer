Trollius taihasenzanensis

Trollius taihasenzanensis, commonly known as the Japanese globeflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its showy, yellow, globe-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and has basal, spoon-shaped leaves. Its flowers are composed of numerous ray florets surrounding a central disk, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Trollius taihasenzanensis is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and thrives in well-drained, moist soils.
