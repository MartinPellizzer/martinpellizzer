Trochomeria hookeri

Trochomeria hookeri, commonly known as the mountain false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the high elevations of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, yellow to orange disk flowers surrounded by pale green to white ray florets. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to harsh, alpine environments.

Trochomeria hookeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and full sun.
