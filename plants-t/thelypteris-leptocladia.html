Thelypteris leptocladia

Thelypteris leptocladia, commonly known as the slender clump fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Thelypteridaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, linear fronds that are typically 30 to 60 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

Thelypteris leptocladia is often found in tropical and subtropical regions across Asia, Africa, and the Americas.
