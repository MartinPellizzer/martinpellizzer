Tamarix passerinoides var. macrocarpa

Tamarix passerinoides var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the giant tamarisk, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, often branching stems that can grow up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces small, pink to reddish flowers in dense clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are scale-like and deciduous, shedding in winter.

The plant is well adapted to arid environments and is often found in coastal and saline soils.
