Tricycla peruviana

Tricycla peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian tricycla, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its striking, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with dark green, lance-shaped leaves. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several small, black seeds.

Tricycla peruviana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
