Taraxacum mandshuricum

Taraxacum mandshuricum, commonly known as the Manchurian dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy rosette structure. The plant features basal leaves that are typically lobed or toothed, with a rough, hairy surface. It produces a hollow, hairy stem that supports a single flower head composed of numerous yellow ray florets.

The plant is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in meadows and forest edges.
