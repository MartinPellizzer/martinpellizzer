Tradescantia sillamontana

Tradescantia sillamontana, commonly known as the spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and vibrant, showy flowers that bloom in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant produces small, three-lobed leaves that are often variegated with silver or white markings. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Tradescantia sillamontana is a perennial plant that spreads via rhizomes, forming dense mats in its natural environment.
