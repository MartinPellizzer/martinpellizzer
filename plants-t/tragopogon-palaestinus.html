Tragopogon palaestinus

Tragopogon palaestinus, commonly known as the Palestine goat's beard, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, including parts of Israel and Palestine.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, spiky seeds that are dispersed by the wind, a common trait among members of the Asteraceae family. Tragopogon palaestinus thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in its native habitat.
