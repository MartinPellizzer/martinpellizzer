Teucrium cylindricum

Teucrium cylindricum, commonly known as the narrowleaf germander, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open woodlands. The plant features slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are clustered in dense, cylindrical spikes and range in color from blue to purple, attracting various pollinators.

Teucrium cylindricum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
