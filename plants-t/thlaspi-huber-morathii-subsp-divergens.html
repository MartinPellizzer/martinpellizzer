Thlaspi huber-morathii subsp. divergens

Thlaspi huber-morathii subsp.

divergens, commonly known as the alpine cabbages, is a low-growing, herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It typically features basal rosettes of fleshy, lobed leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a tight cluster, which are pollinated by insects. It thrives in nutrient-poor, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Its botanical structure reflects its ecological niche, with adaptations for water retention and protection against harsh weather conditions.
