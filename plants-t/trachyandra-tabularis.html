Trachyandra tabularis

Trachyandra tabularis, commonly known as the flat-leaved trachyandra, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asphodelaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming growth habit. The plant features thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss. It produces small, yellowish flowers in late summer, and it is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
