Thyrsacanthus strictus f. evolutior

Thyrsacanthus strictus f. evolutior, commonly known as the curled thistle, is a variant of the thistle plant characterized by its tightly coiled, spiny bracts.

This subspecies exhibits a more pronounced and intricate floral structure compared to the typical form, with tightly spiraled involucres that enclose the small, yellow flowers. The plant is native to regions of southern Africa, where it thrives in dry, rocky habitats. Its unique morphology makes it a subject of interest in botanical studies, particularly in the evolution of floral architecture.

The curled thistle demonstrates specialized adaptations for pollination and seed dispersal in its arid environment.
