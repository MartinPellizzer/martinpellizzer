Taraxacum memorabile

Taraxacum memorabile, commonly known as the memorial dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of stiff, hairy leaves and a hollow, leafless stem that supports a single, yellow flower head. The flower head consists of numerous small, yellow disk flowers surrounded by white, petal-like bracts. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
