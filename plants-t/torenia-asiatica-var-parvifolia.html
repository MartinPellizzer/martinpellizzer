Torenia asiatica var. parvifolia

Torenia asiatica var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The plant is typically herbaceous, with a trailing growth habit that makes it suitable for hanging baskets or ground cover. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant blooms and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
