Thereianthus bulbiferus

Thereianthus bulbiferus, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant produces its flowers in clusters at the top of its stems, often in vibrant shades of purple, pink, and white. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Thereianthus bulbiferus is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
