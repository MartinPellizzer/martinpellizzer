Trivalvaria casseabriae

Trivalvaria casseabriae, commonly known as the golden star orchid, is a tropical orchid native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers with a star-shaped pattern, which are highly attractive to pollinators. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and deriving moisture and nutrients from the air and rain. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native humid environment.

Trivalvaria casseabriae is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in warm, humid climates.
