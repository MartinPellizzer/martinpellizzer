Tinospora smilacina var. typica

Tinospora smilacina var. typica, commonly known as the Indian creeper or Tinospora, is a climbing plant belonging to the Menispermaceae family.

It is characterized by its twining stems and compound leaves with elongated, pointed leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by fleshy, red berries that contain the seeds. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Tinospora smilacina var. typica is known for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for various health purposes.
