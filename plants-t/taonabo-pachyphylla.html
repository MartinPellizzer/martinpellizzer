Taonabo pachyphylla

Taonabo pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-leaved cactus, is a unique plant native to the arid regions of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers during the rainy season, adding a striking contrast to its green foliage. Its leaves are modified into spines, which help reduce water loss and deter herbivores.

Taonabo pachyphylla is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight.
