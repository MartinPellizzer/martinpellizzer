Taraxacum thorvaldii

Taraxacum thorvaldii, commonly known as the Thorvald's dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of spoon-shaped leaves and a distinctive yellow composite flower head. Native to southern Scandinavia, this plant thrives in dry, sandy soils and open habitats. It produces a long, hollow stem with a terminal flower that develops into a white, fluffy seed head.

The species is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its resilience in harsh environments.
