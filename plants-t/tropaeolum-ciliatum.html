Tropaeolum ciliatum

Tropaeolum ciliatum, commonly known as the creeping nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, edible flowers that come in shades of yellow and orange. The plant produces small, kidney-shaped leaves with finely toothed margins, contributing to its ornamental appeal. Its flowers are not only showy but also attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Tropaeolum ciliatum is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value and edible parts, including leaves and seeds.
