Trifolium wigginsii

Trifolium wigginsii, commonly known as Wiggins' clover, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open habitats.

Trifolium wigginsii is an important plant for local ecosystems, providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for various herbivores.
