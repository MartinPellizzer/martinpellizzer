Taraxacum panalpinum

Taraxacum panalpinum, commonly known as the Panjean dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of spoon-shaped leaves and a yellow flower head that develops into a white, fluffy pappus for wind dispersal. Native to Central and Southern Europe, this plant thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. It has a taproot system and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

The plant is also known for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in herbal remedies.
