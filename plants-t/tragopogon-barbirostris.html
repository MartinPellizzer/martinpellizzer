Tragopogon barbirostris

Tragopogon barbirostris, commonly known as the beak-headed thistle, is a flowering plant in the dandelion family, Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flower heads with a prominent, beak-like central stem. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is native to western North America. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the bracts are spiny and protective.

Tragopogon barbirostris is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in open woodlands.
