Tontelea obtusifolia

Tontelea obtusifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved tontelea, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are typically broad and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Tontelea obtusifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
