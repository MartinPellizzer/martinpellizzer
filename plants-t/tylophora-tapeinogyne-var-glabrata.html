Tylophora tapeinogyne var. glabrata

Tylophora tapeinogyne var. glabrata, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed tylophora, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by follicles containing elongated, ribbed seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
