Thyrsacanthus lemaireanus

Thyrsacanthus lemaireanus, commonly known as the Lemaire's thyrsa, is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

It is a perennial shrub native to South Africa, typically found in rocky and sandy soils. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a dense, spike-like inflorescence. It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

The plant's structure and growth habits make it well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
