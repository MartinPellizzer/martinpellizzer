Trichanthemis simulans

Trichanthemis simulans, commonly known as the silverleaf trichanthemum, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features silver-gray foliage with a velvety texture, and its small, daisy-like flowers bloom in shades of white and yellow. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Trichanthemis simulans is also valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
