Themeda chinensis

Themeda chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese grass or Chinese bluestem, is a perennial grass species native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks.

Themeda chinensis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
