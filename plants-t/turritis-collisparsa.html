Turritis collisparsa

Turritis collisparsa, commonly known as the silky turritis, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to withstand salt spray and drought conditions.

Turritis collisparsa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and low maintenance requirements.
