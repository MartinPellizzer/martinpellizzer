Torilis helvetica var. pauciradiata

Torilis helvetica var. pauciradiata, commonly known as the slender hedge mustard, is a low-growing annual plant native to Europe.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and waste areas, and is part of the Brassicaceae family.

Its botanical characteristics make it identifiable by its distinctive floral structure and growth habit.
