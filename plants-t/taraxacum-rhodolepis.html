Taraxacum rhodolepis

Taraxacum rhodolepis, commonly known as the pink burr dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are typically hairy and have a bluish-green hue. Its flowers are yellow, with a distinctive pinkish tinge, and it produces a spiky, wind-dispersed seed head.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.
