Thlaspi alpestre var. calaminare

Thlaspi alpestre var. calaminare, commonly known as the Alpine Tansy, is a variant of the alpine cabbages, characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit.

It typically features basal leaves that are lobed or divided, with a bluish-green or grayish hue. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It is often found in alpine and subalpine regions, growing in rocky or gravelly soils.

This variety is adapted to harsh environments and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
