Trigonella media

Trigonella media, commonly known as Syrian cumin, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in dry, arid environments. The plant has slender, branched stems and small, trifoliate leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are yellow or pale yellow, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, elongated pods containing several seeds.

The seeds are used in culinary and medicinal applications due to their aromatic properties.
