Trifolium pratense f. sanguineum

Trifolium pratense f. sanguineum, commonly known as crimson clover, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and its distinctive pink to red flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is a perennial herb that thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in forage and cover crops. Its root system forms symbiotic relationships with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing soil fertility.

This variety is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
