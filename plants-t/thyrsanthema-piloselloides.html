Thyrsanthema piloselloides

Thyrsanthema piloselloides, commonly known as the hairy thysanotus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features wiry, branching stems covered in fine, hairy foliage. Its yellow flowers are typically arranged in dense, compact clusters, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
