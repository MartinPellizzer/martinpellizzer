Trichodrymonia macrophylla

Trichodrymonia macrophylla, commonly known as the giant sedge, is a monocot belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, grass-like appearance with long, narrow leaves and cylindrical inflorescences. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, this plant thrives in wetland and marshy environments. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands.

The plant's botanical features make it an important component of aquatic and riparian ecosystems.
