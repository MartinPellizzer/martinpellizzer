Thelypteris canescens

Thelypteris canescens, commonly known as the hairy shield fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds that are often covered in fine, hairy structures. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and stream banks. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Thelypteris canescens plays a role in maintaining soil stability and providing habitat for various microorganisms and small fauna.
