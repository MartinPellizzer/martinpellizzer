Taraxacum bornuurense

Taraxacum bornuurense, commonly known as the Bornu dandelion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to parts of Central Asia, including regions of Turkey and the Caucasus. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of pale green, hairy leaves and a tall, hollow stem that bears a single flower head. Its yellow composite flowers develop into distinctive white, parachute-like seeds, typical of the dandelion genus.

Taraxacum bornuurense is often found in grassy areas and rocky slopes, thriving in temperate climates.
