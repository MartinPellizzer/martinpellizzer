Trifolium cernuum var. intermedium

Trifolium cernuum var. intermedium, commonly known as the intermediate匍匐三叶草, is a low-growing perennial herb native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three oval to ovate leaflets. The flowers are typically pink or purple, borne in loose clusters at the base of the plant. This variety is often found in grassy areas, along roadsides, and in disturbed soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and as a forage plant for livestock.
