Thalictrum andrzejowskii

Thalictrum andrzejowskii, commonly known as the Andrzejowski's meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like foliage and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as meadows and forest understories. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a whorled pattern along the stem.

Thalictrum andrzejowskii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
