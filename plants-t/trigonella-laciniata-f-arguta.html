Trigonella laciniata f. arguta

Trigonella laciniata f. arguta, commonly known as the hairy fenugreek, is a variant of the fenugreek plant characterized by its finely hairy foliage.

It is a low-growing annual herb with trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces slender, elongated pods that contain small, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in wet meadows or along the edges of water bodies.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional herbal medicine.
