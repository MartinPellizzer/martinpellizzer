Tilia mandshurica var. rufovillosa

Tilia mandshurica var. rufovillosa, commonly known as the Mongolian lime or red-hairy lime, is a deciduous tree native to northeastern Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense, rusty-brown pubescence. The flowers are small, yellowish-green, and arranged in drooping clusters, typical of the Tilia genus. The tree produces small, round fruits that mature into woody capsules.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping due to its attractive foliage and tolerance to urban conditions.
