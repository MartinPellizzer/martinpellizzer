Tofieldia nutans var. kiusiana

Tofieldia nutans var. kiusiana, commonly known as the Kiusian beakrush, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale green to brown flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
