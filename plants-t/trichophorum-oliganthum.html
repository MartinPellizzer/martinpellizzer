Trichophorum oliganthum

Trichophorum oliganthum, commonly known as the lesser sedge, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flowers. This plant typically grows in moist or wet habitats such as marshes, bogs, and wet meadows. Trichophorum oliganthum has a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread and colonize new areas.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
