Toddalia simplicifolia

Toddalia simplicifolia, commonly known as the white sapphire tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often oval or elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its natural distribution.

Toddalia simplicifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adaptable to a variety of soil types.
