Tibouchina verticillaris

Tibouchina verticillaris, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded clusters of small, blue-violet flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a velvety texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Tibouchina verticillaris is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
