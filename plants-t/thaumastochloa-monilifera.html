Thaumastochloa monilifera

Thaumastochloa monilifera, commonly known as the clustered spikegrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, cylindrical inflorescences that bear small, spikelet-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands, where it thrives in humid conditions. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Thaumastochloa monilifera plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms in its native ecosystems.
