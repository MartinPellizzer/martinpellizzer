Trachycalymma pseudofimbriatum

Trachycalymma pseudofimbriatum, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to red bract-like flowers that are often mistaken for petals. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and it typically grows in rocky or sandy soils.
