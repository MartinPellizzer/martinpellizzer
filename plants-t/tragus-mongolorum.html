Tragus mongolorum

Tragus mongolorum, commonly known as the Mongolian tragus, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions. Its small, spiny leaves and yellow flowers are features that aid in its survival in dry climates.

Tragus mongolorum is often found in steppes and semi-desert areas, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
