Taraxacum ajano-majense

Taraxacum ajano-majense, commonly known as the Ajano-Majense dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and a yellow flowering head. The plant typically grows in rocky or grassy habitats at high altitudes. It produces a hollow, leafy stem that supports its composite flower head.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
