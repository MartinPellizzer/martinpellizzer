Tulipa maximowiczii

Tulipa maximowiczii, commonly known as the Maximowicz tulip, is a species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from red to orange and yellow. The plant typically grows to a height of 15 to 30 centimeters, with narrow, grass-like leaves. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

The bulbs are perennial, and the plant is valued for its ornamental beauty in horticulture.
