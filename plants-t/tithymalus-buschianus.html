Tithymalus buschianus

Tithymalus buschianus, commonly known as the Buschian tithymalus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, fragrant yellow flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in rocky or sandy habitats and is adapted to full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
