Tragacantha unguiculata

Tragacantha unguiculata, commonly known as the Persian gum or Astragalus, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to regions of the Middle East and Central Asia, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant is characterized by its spiny stems and small, pea-like flowers that bloom in shades of yellow to purple. Its seeds are enclosed in pods, and the plant is known for producing a resinous gum that has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a food additive.

Tragacantha unguiculata plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes cultivated for its economic value.
