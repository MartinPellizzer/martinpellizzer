Thibaudia coriacea

Thibaudia coriacea, commonly known as the rough thibaudia, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, ovate leaves with a rough, textured surface. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers during the winter months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Thibaudia coriacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand dry conditions.
