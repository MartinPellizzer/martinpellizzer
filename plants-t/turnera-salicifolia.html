Turnera salicifolia

Turnera salicifolia, commonly known as the willowleaf turnera, is a species of flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Turneraceae and is characterized by its lanceolate, willow-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are typically arranged in clusters at the ends of its branches. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Turnera salicifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
