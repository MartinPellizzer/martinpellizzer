Tropaeolum trilobum

Tropaeolum trilobum, commonly known as the three-lobed nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, edible flowers and heart-shaped leaves with distinct three lobes. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental and edible gardening.

Its botanical features make it both visually appealing and ecologically beneficial in garden ecosystems.
