Tithonia laciniata

Tithonia laciniata, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a tropical plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its large, bright orange to red flowers and deeply lobed, heart-shaped leaves. The plant is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers. It thrives in warm climates and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its vibrant appearance.

Tithonia laciniata is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
