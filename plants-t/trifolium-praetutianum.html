Trifolium praetutianum

Trifolium praetutianum, commonly known as the Praetutian clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides.

Trifolium praetutianum is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
