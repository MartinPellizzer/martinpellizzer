Trixis amphimalaca

Trixis amphimalaca, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with silvery-gray foliage. The plant produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It has adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments through deep root systems and reduced leaf surface area.

Trixis amphimalaca is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
