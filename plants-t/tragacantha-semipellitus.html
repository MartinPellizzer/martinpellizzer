Tragacantha semipellitus

Tragacantha semipellitus, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a species of flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody, shrub-like appearance with compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by pods containing seeds. Its most notable feature is the production of a resinous gum, which has been historically used in various traditional applications.

This species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in harsh climates.
