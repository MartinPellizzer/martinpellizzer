Thelypteris cumingiana

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its leaves are usually leathery and have a serrated margin, contributing to its ornamental value.
