Tilia × pallida

Tilia × pallida, commonly known as the whitebeam, is a hybrid species within the Tilia genus, native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a pale green or white-veined, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which bloom in early summer and are followed by small, round fruits. It is often found in woodlands and is known for its ornamental value due to its unique foliage.

The species is also valued for its ecological role, providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
