Taxillus nigrans var. lonicerifolius

Taxillus nigrans var. lonicerifolius, commonly known as black mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees, primarily members of the Rosaceae family.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own carbohydrates through photosynthesis using the light it captures. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces red berries that contain toxic seeds. It is often found in temperate regions and can cause significant damage to its host if left unchecked.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting subject for ecological and parasitological studies.
