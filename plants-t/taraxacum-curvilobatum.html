Taraxacum curvilobatum

Taraxacum curvilobatum, commonly known as the curlylobed dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, curly lobed leaves and yellow composite flowers that develop into fluffy white seed heads. Native to North America, this plant thrives in a variety of habitats, including meadows, roadsides, and disturbed soils. It is often found in the western United States and parts of Canada.

The plant is noted for its resilience and ability to spread through wind-dispersed seeds.
