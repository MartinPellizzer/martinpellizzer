Tripsacum dactyloides var. hispidum

Tripsacum dactyloides var. hispidum, commonly known as hairy wild millet, is a perennial grass native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its coarse, hairy leaves and tall, branched inflorescences that produce small, spikelet-like flowers. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in sandy or loamy soils, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments. Its fibrous root system helps prevent soil erosion, and it is often used in restoration ecology projects.

The plant has historical significance as a forage crop for livestock and has been studied for its potential in sustainable agriculture.
