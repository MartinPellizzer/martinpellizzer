Tropaeolum majus var. luteum

Tropaeolum majus var. luteum, commonly known as the golden touchweed, is a vibrant annual plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom throughout the summer. The plant produces small, edible tubers that are often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Tropaeolum majus var. luteum thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for container gardening and hanging baskets.
