Thymus zygis var. maroccanus

Thymus zygis var. maroccanus, commonly known as Moroccan thyme, is a perennial herb native to North Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and aromatic, gray-green foliage. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its essential oils contain thymol and carvacrol, contributing to its use in culinary and medicinal applications.
