Trisetum aristidoides

Trisetum aristidoides, commonly known as the silver hair grass, is a perennial grass species native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Trisetum aristidoides plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
