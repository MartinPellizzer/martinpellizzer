Taraxacum pseudotrigonolobum

Taraxacum pseudotrigonolobum, commonly known as the narrow-lobed dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated, grass-like leaves and a distinctive flowering stem that bears a single, yellow composite flower head. The plant is native to parts of Europe and is often found in disturbed soils, meadows, and along roadsides. Its flowers develop into a pappus-equipped seed head, which is commonly referred to as a "dandelion clock.".

This species is noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
