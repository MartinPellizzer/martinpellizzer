Teucrium corymbosum var. hirsutum

Teucrium corymbosum var. hirsutum, commonly known as hairy woodruff, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial shade. The plant features small, fragrant white or pale blue flowers that bloom in cymes, often giving it a fluffy appearance. Its leaves are hairy and have a distinctive aromatic scent, which is characteristic of the Teucrium genus.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
