Tribulus macropterus var. anisopterus

Tribulus macropterus var. anisopterus, commonly known as the Indian goji or desert date, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of India and Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in sandy soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well adapted to harsh environmental conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its potential medicinal properties.
