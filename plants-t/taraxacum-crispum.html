Taraxacum crispum

Taraxacum crispum, commonly known as the curlyleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, curly, spoon-shaped leaves and a yellow flower head that matures into a white, fluffy pappus. The plant is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa but has been naturalized in many parts of the world. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in meadows, lawns, and disturbed areas.

The plant is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and vegetative reproduction.
