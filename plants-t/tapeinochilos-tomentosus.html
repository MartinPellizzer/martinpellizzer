Tapeinochilos tomentosus

Tapeinochilos tomentosus, commonly known as the woolly orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces striking, showy flowers with a distinctive coloration, often in shades of yellow and green. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for both botanists and orchid enthusiasts.
