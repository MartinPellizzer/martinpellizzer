Tetragonotheca ludoviciana

Tetragonotheca ludoviciana, commonly known as the Texas goldenbush, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, yellowish-green foliage and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has a compact growth habit and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are typically opposite and have a quadrangular cross-section, giving it its scientific name.

It is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
