Trifolium trichocalyx

Trifolium trichocalyx, commonly known as the hairy clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets with a hairy texture. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It is native to regions of Asia and is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils.

Trifolium trichocalyx is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
