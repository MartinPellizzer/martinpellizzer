Tigridia immaculata

Tigridia immaculata, commonly known as the white tigrid lily, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, upward-facing flowers with a white or pale pink coloration. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped blooms that are often speckled or streaked with darker hues. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Tigridia immaculata is noted for its long bloom period, typically from spring to early summer.
