Tradescantia aguensis

Tradescantia aguensis, commonly known as the "silver sword" or "Mexican spiderwort," is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming growth habit with broad, lance-shaped leaves that often have a silvery or pale green hue. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of violet or blue, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of care, it is widely cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
