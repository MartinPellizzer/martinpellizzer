Triplophyllum heudelotii

Triplophyllum heudelotii, commonly known as the African star-of-Bethlehem, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features striking, star-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, three-lobed seed pods. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions.
