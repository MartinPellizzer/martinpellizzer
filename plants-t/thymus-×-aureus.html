Thymus × aureus

Thymus × aureus, commonly known as golden thyme, is a hybrid shrub belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, low-growing habit and aromatic, dark green foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from pale yellow to deep orange, typically blooming in early summer. Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Its essential oils, rich in thymol and carvacrol, make it valuable in both culinary and medicinal applications.
