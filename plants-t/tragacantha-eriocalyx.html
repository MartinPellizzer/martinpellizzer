Tragacantha eriocalyx

Tragacantha eriocalyx, commonly known as the woolly tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer months. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is also valued for its ability to thrive in arid and rocky environments, making it an important species in ecological restoration efforts.
