Tecoma fulva subsp. tanaeciiflora

Tecoma fulva subsp.

tanaeciiflora, commonly known as the red trumpet tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa. It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange-red.

The plant has opposite, simple leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture. It produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds, and it is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
