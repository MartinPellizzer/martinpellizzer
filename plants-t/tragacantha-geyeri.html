Tragacantha geyeri

Tragacantha geyeri, commonly known as the Geyeri tragacanth, is a species of flowering plant native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers during the spring and summer seasons. Its sap is known for its gelatinous consistency and has been used historically in traditional medicine.

The plant is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils in dry regions.
