Tephrosia brachyodon var. typica

Tephrosia brachyodon var. typica, commonly known as the small-leaved tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features small, ovate leaves with a distinctive glandular pubescence on the underside. Its flowers are typically yellow or white, arranged in racemes, and it produces small, flattened pods containing several seeds.

It is often found in disturbed habitats and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
