Taraxacum ducommunii

Taraxacum ducommunii, commonly known as the French dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant features a rosette of spoon-shaped, hairy leaves that form a basal cluster. Its yellow flower heads produce wind-dispersed seeds with feathery pappi, aiding in wide distribution.

The plant is often found in disturbed soils and grassy areas, thriving in temperate climates.
