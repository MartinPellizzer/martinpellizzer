Thrinax martii

Thrinax martii, commonly known as the Mexican palm or the Guanacaste palm, is a member of the Arecaceae family.

It is a small, cold-tolerant palm native to the coastal regions of Mexico and Central America. The plant features a slender trunk with a smooth, grayish bark and produces clusters of small, white flowers followed by feather-like fruits. Its leaves are pinnate, with long, narrow segments that give it a distinctive appearance.

Thrinax martii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in various soil types.
