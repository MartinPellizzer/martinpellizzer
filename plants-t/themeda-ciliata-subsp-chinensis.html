Themeda ciliata subsp. chinensis

Themeda ciliata subsp.

chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese grass or Chinese blady grass, is a perennial grass species native to eastern Asia. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive hairy margin.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streams.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens and used in ecological restoration projects.
