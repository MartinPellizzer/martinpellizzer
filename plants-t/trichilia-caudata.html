Trichilia caudata

Trichilia caudata, commonly known as the African shea tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and compound leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing flat, winged seeds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine, and the tree plays an important role in local ecosystems and economies.

The wood is also valued for its durability and is used in construction and furniture-making.
