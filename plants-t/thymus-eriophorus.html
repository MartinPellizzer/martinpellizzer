Thymus eriophorus

Thymus eriophorus, commonly known as the narrowleaf thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, aromatic leaves and produces delicate pink or purple flowers. The plant is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. Its foliage is widely used in culinary and medicinal applications due to its strong, pungent aroma.

Thymus eriophorus is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
