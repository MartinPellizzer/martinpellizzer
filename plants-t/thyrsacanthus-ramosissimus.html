Thyrsacanthus ramosissimus

Thyrsacanthus ramosissimus, commonly known as the thorned candelabra cactus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its branched, spiny stems that form a dense, candelabra-like structure. The plant produces yellow flowers during the rainy season, which are clustered in inflorescences at the stem tips. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
