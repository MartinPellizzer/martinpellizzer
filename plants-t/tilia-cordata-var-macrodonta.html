Tilia cordata var. macrodonta

Tilia cordata var. macrodonta, commonly known as the large-leaf lime, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, large leaves that can reach up to 15 cm in length. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clustered inflorescences, which bloom in early summer. Its bark is smooth and light gray, becoming fissured with age.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also valued in traditional medicine.
