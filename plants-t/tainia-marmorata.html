Tainia marmorata

Tainia marmorata, commonly known as the marble vine, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Passifloraceae family and is characterized by its striking, ornamental flowers that resemble intricate patterns. The plant produces showy, five-petaled blooms in shades of white, pink, and red, often with a contrasting center. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a glossy, dark green surface.

Tainia marmorata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
