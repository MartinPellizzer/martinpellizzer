Tetralopha philippinensis

Tetralopha philippinensis, commonly known as the Philippine starling, is a shrub native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has smooth, grayish bark and produces small, edible berries that are a food source for local wildlife. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
