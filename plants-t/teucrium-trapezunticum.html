Teucrium trapezunticum

Teucrium trapezunticum, commonly known as the Trabzon germander, is a perennial herb native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in mountainous areas. Its small, tubular flowers are usually violet or purple, and it produces small, four-angled fruits.

Teucrium trapezunticum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
