Tinospora neocaledonica

Tinospora neocaledonica, commonly known as the New Caledonia tinospora, is a climbing vine native to the island of New Caledonia.

It belongs to the Menispermaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive yellowish-brown bark. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
