Thalictrum franchetii

Thalictrum franchetii, commonly known as the Chinese meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial native to China.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, white, spadix-like flowers surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. Its foliage consists of finely divided, fern-like leaves that are dark green and glossy.

Thalictrum franchetii is valued in horticulture for its delicate appearance and ability to thrive in partial shade.
