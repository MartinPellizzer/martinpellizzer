Theobroma cacao subsp. sativum

Theobroma cacao subsp.

sativum, commonly known as the chocolate tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Americas. It produces pods containing seeds that are the primary source of cocoa used in chocolate production.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments with well-drained soil and requires a warm climate. Its leaves are elliptical and dark green, while its flowers are small and yellowish.

The fruit, which matures over several months, is essential for the cultivation of cocoa beans.
