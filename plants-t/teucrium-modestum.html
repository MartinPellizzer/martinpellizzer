Teucrium modestum

Teucrium modestum, commonly known as lesser germander, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rocky or dry gardens.

Teucrium modestum is also valued for its medicinal properties, traditionally used to treat digestive and respiratory ailments.
