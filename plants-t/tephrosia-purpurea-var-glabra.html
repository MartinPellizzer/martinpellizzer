Tephrosia purpurea var. glabra

Tephrosia purpurea var. glabra, commonly known as purple crownflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub with smooth, glabrous stems and lanceolate leaves. Its flowers are usually purple or pink, arranged in racemes, and it produces small, legume-like seeds.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
