Thamnocalamus quadrangularis

Thamnocalamus quadrangularis, commonly known as the square bamboo, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinct quadrangular stems, which give it its common name, and its slender, grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and mountainous regions. Thamnocalamus quadrangularis is often used in traditional medicine and horticulture due to its ornamental value and potential health benefits.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and cultural contexts.
