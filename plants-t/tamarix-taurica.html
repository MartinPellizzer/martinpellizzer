Tamarix taurica

Tamarix taurica, commonly known as the Tauric tamarisk, is a small shrub native to the steppes of southern Russia and Crimea.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, scale-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces pink or red flowers in summer, which are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. It is well adapted to arid and saline environments, making it a hardy species for coastal and dry landscapes.

Tamarix taurica is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
