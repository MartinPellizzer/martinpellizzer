Trichomanes papuanum

Trichomanes papuanum, commonly known as the Papuan maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical rainforests of New Guinea and nearby islands. Its spores are produced in small, clustered structures called sorus on the underside of the fronds.

Trichomanes papuanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage.
