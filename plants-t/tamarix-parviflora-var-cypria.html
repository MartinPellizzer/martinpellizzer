Tamarix parviflora var. cypria

Tamarix parviflora var. cypria, commonly known as Cyprus tamarisk, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, pink to pale red flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the summer months. The plant has slender, often branched stems that are covered in small, scale-like leaves. It is well adapted to arid and saline environments, making it a hardy choice for coastal or dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and flowering display.
