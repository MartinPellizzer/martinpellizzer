Tephrosia incana

Tephrosia incana, commonly known as the silverleaf tephrosia, is a tropical shrub native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and its small, pale yellow flowers. The plant is known for its erect growth habit and can reach heights of up to 2 meters. Tephrosia incana is often found in dry, open habitats and is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.

It is also noted for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
