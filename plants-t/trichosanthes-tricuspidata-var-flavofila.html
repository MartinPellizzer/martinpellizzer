Trichosanthes tricuspidata var. flavofila

Trichosanthes tricuspidata var. flavofila, commonly known as the yellow-flowered bitter melon, is a variant of the bitter melon plant native to southern China.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its yellow flowers and elongated, bitter fruits. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces fruits that are typically orange to yellow in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruits, though they are known for their sharp, bitter taste.

The plant is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
