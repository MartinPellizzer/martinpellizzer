Tragacantha hausknechtii

Tragacantha hausknechtii, commonly known as the desert milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces a sticky, resinous substance known as tragacanth, which has been historically used in various industrial applications. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Tragacantha hausknechtii plays a role in stabilizing soil in desert environments and provides forage for wildlife.
