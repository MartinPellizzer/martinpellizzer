Tulipa akamasica

Tulipa akamasica, commonly known as the Akamasica tulip, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Turkey.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, cup-shaped flowers that come in a range of colors, including red, yellow, and orange. The plant typically grows to a height of 10 to 30 centimeters, with narrow, grass-like leaves.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.
