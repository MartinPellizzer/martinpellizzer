Tipuana speciosa

Tipuana speciosa, commonly known as the Andean tipuana, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, gray bark and spreading crown. The leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic in shape, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. It produces fragrant, yellowish-white flowers in clusters during the spring, which are followed by small, woody fruits containing several seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in landscaping due to its adaptability and resilience.
