Tropaeolum pentagonum

Tropaeolum pentagonum, commonly known as the pentagonal nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant is an annual herb that thrives in warm, sunny conditions and well-drained soil. Its seeds are edible and have a peppery flavor similar to mustard.

Tropaeolum pentagonum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
