Todda-pana

Todda-pana, commonly known as the sacred lotus, is a water-loving plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It thrives in shallow freshwater environments such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving rivers. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface, while its striking flowers bloom in a variety of colors, including pink, red, and white. Todda-pana has a robust root system that helps stabilize aquatic ecosystems.

Its flowers are often associated with purity and spiritual significance in many cultures.
