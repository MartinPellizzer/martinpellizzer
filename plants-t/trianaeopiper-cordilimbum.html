Trianaeopiper cordilimbum

Trianaeopiper cordilimbum, commonly known as the broad-leafed pepper plant, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, peppery fruits that are used in traditional culinary applications. Botanically, it belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species.

Its morphology and growth habits make it a unique subject for botanical study.
