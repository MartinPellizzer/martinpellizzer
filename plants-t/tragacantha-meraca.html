Tragacantha meraca

Tragacantha meraca, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by pod-like fruits containing seeds. It is known for its ability to produce a resinous gum, which has been used historically in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive.

The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it an important component of desert ecosystems.
