Thymelaea pilosa

Thymelaea pilosa, commonly known as the silky thymelaea, is a low-growing shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, waxy leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
