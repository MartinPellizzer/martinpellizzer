Trixis antimenorrhoea var. discolor

Trixis antimenorrhoea var. discolor, commonly known as the variable trixis, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, often colorful foliage. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with lance-shaped leaves that can vary in color depending on environmental conditions. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually in dense inflorescences, and are pollinated by insects.
