Tagetes iltisiana

Tagetes iltisiana, commonly known as the Mexican marigold, is a species of flowering plant in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in full sun with well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

The plant is known for its strong fragrance and ability to attract beneficial insects like ladybugs and lacewings.
