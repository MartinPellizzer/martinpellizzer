Tapura amazonica var. ciliata

Tapura amazonica var. ciliata, commonly known as the Amazonian tapura, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves with serrated edges, which contribute to its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The species is adapted to thrive in high humidity and nutrient-rich soil, making it a common sight in the understory of dense rainforest ecosystems.
