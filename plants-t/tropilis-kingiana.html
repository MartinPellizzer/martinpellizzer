Tropilis kingiana

Tropilis kingiana, commonly known as the King's lily, is a striking flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, often with a distinctive central boss. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, providing a lush backdrop to its vibrant blooms.

Tropilis kingiana thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate to flourish.
