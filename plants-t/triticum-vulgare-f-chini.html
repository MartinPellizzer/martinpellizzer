Triticum vulgare f. chini

Triticum vulgare f. chini, commonly known as Chinese wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences containing numerous tiny florets. It is often cultivated for its nutritional value, as it is rich in vitamins and minerals.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it is used in various agricultural and horticultural applications.
