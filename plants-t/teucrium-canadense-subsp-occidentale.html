Teucrium canadense subsp. occidentale

Teucrium canadense subsp.

occidentale, commonly known as western blueweed, is a perennial herb native to western North America. It typically grows in dry, rocky, or sandy soils, often found in open woodlands and along streambanks.

The plant features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, blue to violet, and clustered in dense terminal spikes, blooming from late spring to early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
