Thalictrum anonymum

Thalictrum anonymum, commonly known as the meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear whorled leaves, which are typically lance-shaped and have a distinctive silvery underside. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers in early summer, which are arranged in compound umbels. Thalictrum anonymum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value lies in its delicate foliage and subtle floral display.
