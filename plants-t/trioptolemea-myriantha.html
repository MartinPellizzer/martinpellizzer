Trioptolemea myriantha

Trioptolemea myriantha, commonly known as the mountain thistle, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, silver-gray leaves with a rough, hairy texture. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators. Its root system is fibrous and deep, allowing it to thrive in poor, rocky soils.

Trioptolemea myriantha is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
