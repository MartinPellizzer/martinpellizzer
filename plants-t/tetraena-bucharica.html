Tetraena bucharica

Tetraena bucharica, commonly known as the Bukharan tetraena, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climatic conditions. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually small and leathery, helping to reduce water loss.

Tetraena bucharica is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
