Tropaeolum aduncum

Tropaeolum aduncum, commonly known as the "spurred sowthistle," is a herbaceous annual plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its alternate, palmately lobed leaves and vibrant, showy flowers that range from yellow to orange in color. The plant produces small, edible, peppery fruits known as "Gardeners' Glory" or "Chinese violet.". It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible qualities.

Tropaeolum aduncum is also known for its ability to spread rapidly through self-seeding, making it both a garden favorite and a potential weed.
