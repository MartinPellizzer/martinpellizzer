Thalictrum flavum var. ramosum

Thalictrum flavum var. ramosum, commonly known as the yellow meadow rue, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in meadows and woodland edges. It blooms in early summer, producing showy inflorescences that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
