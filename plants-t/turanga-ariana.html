Turanga ariana

Turanga ariana, commonly known as the silverleaf orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features a fibrous root system and succulent, lance-shaped leaves that store water, adapting to its dry environment. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink, which bloom during the cooler months. Its flowers are borne on tall, slender stalks and are pollinated primarily by moths and beetles.

Turanga ariana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and xeriscapes.
