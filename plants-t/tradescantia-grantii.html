Tradescantia grantii

Tradescantia grantii, commonly known as the red-veined nettle or red-veined wandering jew, is a striking ornamental plant native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically green with prominent red veins, creating a vibrant contrast. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that bloom in clusters, often in late spring or early summer. It is a member of the Commelinaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types as long as it receives adequate sunlight.

Tradescantia grantii is also notable for its air-purifying qualities and is often used in indoor gardening.
