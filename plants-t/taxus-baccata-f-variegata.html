Taxus baccata f. variegata

Taxus baccata f. variegata, commonly known as the variegated yew, is a unique cultivar of the European yew.

It is characterized by its striking variegated foliage, featuring white and yellow margins on its narrow, dark green leaves. This form is a naturally occurring mutation of the common yew, often found in shaded environments. The plant is evergreen, with a slow growth rate and a dense, pyramidal shape.

It is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
