Thermopsis gracilis

Thermopsis gracilis, commonly known as slender thermopsis, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Thermopsis gracilis is noted for its ecological importance as a food source for native pollinators and its role in soil stabilization.
