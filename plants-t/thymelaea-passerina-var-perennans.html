Thymelaea passerina var. perennans

Thymelaea passerina var. perennans, commonly known as the evergreen thyme.

This plant is a shrub native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa, characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage. It produces small, yellowish-white flowers in clusters, which bloom in early spring. The leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a distinctive texture.

It is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover due to its hardy nature and ornamental appeal.
