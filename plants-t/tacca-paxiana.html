Tacca paxiana

Tacca paxiana, commonly known as the "bat plant" or "ghost orchid," is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features a unique, elongated, fleshy rhizome and produces large, showy, umbrella-like inflorescences with striking violet petals. The plant's flowers are often adorned with intricate, claw-like appendages that resemble bat wings, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Tacca paxiana is considered a conservation concern in many regions.
