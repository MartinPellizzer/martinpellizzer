Tetradenia aurata

Tetradenia aurata, commonly known as the golden myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of northern Australia.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a waxy coating. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange or red berries. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance and thriving in well-drained soils.

Tetradenia aurata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional Aboriginal medicine.
