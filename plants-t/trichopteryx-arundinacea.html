Trichopteryx arundinacea

Trichopteryx arundinacea, commonly known as the common rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, grass-like stems that are often covered with fine hairs. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, thriving in moist soil conditions. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are arranged in a dense, cylindrical cluster.

Trichopteryx arundinacea plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
