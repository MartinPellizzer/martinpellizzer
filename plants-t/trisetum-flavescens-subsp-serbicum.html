Trisetum flavescens subsp. serbicum

Trisetum flavescens subsp.

serbicum, commonly known as the Serbian wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to southeastern Europe. It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, needle-like leaves that are typically pale green to bluish-gray in color.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous pale green to brown florets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along riverbanks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
