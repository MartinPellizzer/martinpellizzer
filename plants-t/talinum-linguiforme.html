Talinum linguiforme

Talinum linguiforme, commonly known as the arrowleaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features heart-shaped, arrowhead-shaped leaves that are often tinged with purple or red. Its flowers are small, violet to blue in color, and appear in clusters.

Talinum linguiforme is known for its edible leaves, which are used in traditional cuisines and have a slightly bitter taste.
