Thalictrum foetidum lusus cinereum

Thalictrum foetidum lusus cinereum, commonly known as the gray meadow rue, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are usually pollinated by insects, and it produces small, dry fruits containing several seeds.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
