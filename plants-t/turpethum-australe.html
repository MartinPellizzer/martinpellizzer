Turpethum australe

Turpethum australe, commonly known as the Australian turpethum, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, urn-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dune environments.

Turpethum australe is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
