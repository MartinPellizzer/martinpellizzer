Thlaspi stipitatum

Thlaspi stipitatum, commonly known as the hairy cress, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and along stream banks. Thlaspi stipitatum is a hardy perennial that can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
