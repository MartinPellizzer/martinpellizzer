Tulipa tarda

Tulipa tarda, commonly known as the late tulip, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Tulipa.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe, as well as parts of western Asia. This perennial bulbous plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun.

Tulipa tarda is valued for its ornamental beauty and is commonly cultivated in gardens.
