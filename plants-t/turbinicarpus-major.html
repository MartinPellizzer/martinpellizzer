Turbinicarpus major

Turbinicarpus major, commonly known as the giant desert cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its large, ribbed stems that can grow up to several feet in height. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. Its thick, fleshy stems are adapted to store water, allowing it to thrive in harsh, dry environments.

Turbinicarpus major is a slow-growing plant that requires minimal care once established.
