Trapa longicarpa var. contortula

Trapa longicarpa var. contortula, commonly known as the twisted water chestnut, is a aquatic plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny, floating fruits that remain on the water surface after maturity. The plant has narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are typically submerged, while its flowers are small and white, appearing in clusters. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often in wetlands and ponds.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in aquatic ecosystems.
