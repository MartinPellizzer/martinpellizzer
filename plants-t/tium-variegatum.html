Tium variegatum

Tium variegatum, commonly known as the variegated tium, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, multicolored leaves that display shades of green, yellow, and white. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
