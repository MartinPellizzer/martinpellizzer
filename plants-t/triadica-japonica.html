Triadica japonica

Triadica japonica, commonly known as the Japanese tree-onion, is a perennial flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, umbel-like inflorescence that appears in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white to greenish flowers. Its bulbs are edible and have been used in traditional Asian cuisine.

Triadica japonica is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
