Taraxacum petterssonii

Taraxacum petterssonii, commonly known as the Swedish dandelion, is a perennial herb native to northern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, hairy stems. The plant features a basal rosette of broadly lanceolate leaves with serrated edges. Its yellow flower heads produce a crown of fluffy seeds dispersed by the wind.

It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.
