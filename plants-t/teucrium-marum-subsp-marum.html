Teucrium marum subsp. marum

Teucrium marum subsp.

marum, commonly known as narrowleaf germander, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves and small, clustered blue or purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in well-drained soil. It has a woody rootstock and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Its foliage is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
