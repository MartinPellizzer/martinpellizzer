Thymus subnudus

Thymus subnudus, commonly known as narrowleaf thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features small, narrow leaves that are typically gray-green in color, covered in fine hairs. The plant produces tiny, pink to purple flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its aromatic properties, it is also used in culinary and medicinal applications.
