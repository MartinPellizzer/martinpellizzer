Tussilago bifolia

Tussilago bifolia, commonly known as the twinleaf plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaves, which are divided into two pairs of lobes, giving it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Tussilago bifolia is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its soothing effects on respiratory conditions.
