Tanacetum krylovianum

Tanacetum krylovianum, commonly known as Krylov's tansy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, finely divided leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats. It is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many parts of its native range.
