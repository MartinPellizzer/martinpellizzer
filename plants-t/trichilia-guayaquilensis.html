Trichilia guayaquilensis

Trichilia guayaquilensis, commonly known as the Guayaquil chicle tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon basin.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae, which includes mahogany and related hardwoods. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves, which are usually opposite and pinnate. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or yellow, followed by winged fruits.

The tree is valued for its durable timber and has traditional uses in local communities.
