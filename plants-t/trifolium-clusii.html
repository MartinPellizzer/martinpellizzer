Trifolium clusii

Trifolium clusii, commonly known as Clusius' clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Native to parts of Europe and Asia, this plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides. Trifolium clusii is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a beneficial plant for soil enrichment.

Its delicate appearance and ecological importance make it a notable species within the genus Trifolium.
