Taxus baccata f. erecta

Taxus baccata f. erecta, commonly known as the upright yew, is a cultivar of the European yew tree.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and dark green, needle-like foliage. The plant produces small, red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

This variety is valued for its compact form and year-round visual interest.
