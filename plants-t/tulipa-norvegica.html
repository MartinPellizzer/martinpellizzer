Tulipa norvegica

Tulipa norvegica, commonly known as the Norwegian tulip, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Scandinavia.

It produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of red, yellow, and orange, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It is adapted to cold climates and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Tulipa norvegica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in cooler regions.
