Trichopteryx cuspidata

Trichopteryx cuspidata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Trichopteryx cuspidata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
