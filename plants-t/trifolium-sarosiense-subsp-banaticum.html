Trifolium sarosiense subsp. banaticum

Trifolium sarosiense subsp.

banaticum, commonly known as the Banat red clover, is a perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets with a slightly hairy surface.

The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early summer, typically arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional agriculture.
