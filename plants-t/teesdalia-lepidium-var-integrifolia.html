Teesdalia lepidium var. integrifolia

Teesdalia lepidium var. integrifolia, commonly known as the smoothleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and clusters of small, lavender to pinkish-purple flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have smooth margins, which distinguish it from other varieties of the species. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open woods and meadows.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
