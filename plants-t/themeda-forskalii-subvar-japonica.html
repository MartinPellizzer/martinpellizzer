Themeda forskalii subvar. japonica

Themeda forskalii subvar. japonica, commonly known as Japanese grass or Japanese needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grassy slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is cultivated in gardens and used in landscaping.
