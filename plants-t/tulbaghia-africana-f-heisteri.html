Tulbaghia africana f. heisteri

Tulbaghia africana f. heisteri, commonly known as the Cape daffodil, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white to yellow. The plant is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens.

The subspecies f. heisteri is distinguished by its slightly different flower structure compared to the typical form.
