Thymus broussonetii subsp. hannonis

Thymus broussonetii subsp.

hannonis, commonly known as Hannon's thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features aromatic, narrow leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces small, pink to purple flowers in the spring.

The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in traditional herbal medicine.
