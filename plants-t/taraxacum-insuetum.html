Taraxacum insuetum

Taraxacum insuetum, commonly known as the slender dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and herbaceous growth habit. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, pale green leaves with a hairy underside. Its yellow flower heads produce seeds with pappus, enabling wind dispersal.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.
