Trollius pumilus var. sikkimensis

Trollius pumilus var. sikkimensis, commonly known as the Sikkim dwarf buttercup, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the alpine regions of the Himalayas.

It features bright yellow, daisy-like flowers with a central cluster of small, petal-like rays. The plant typically reaches heights of only a few centimeters, with narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in botanical gardens and alpine plant collections.
