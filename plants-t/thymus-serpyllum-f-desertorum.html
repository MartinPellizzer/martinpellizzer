Thymus serpyllum f. desertorum

Thymus serpyllum f. desertorum, commonly known as desert thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It features compact, mat-forming growth with small, dark green, ovate leaves that are aromatic and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces pink to violet flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to hot, dry climates and can thrive in poor, sandy soils.

Its essential oils contain thymol and carvacrol, making it valuable for both culinary and medicinal uses.
