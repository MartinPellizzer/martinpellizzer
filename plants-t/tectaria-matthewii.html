Tectaria matthewii

Tectaria matthewii, commonly known as the Matthew's tectaria, is a fern belonging to the family Tectariaceae.

It is characterized by its intricate, lace-like fronds that are typically divided into numerous pinnae. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its spores are released from specialized structures called sori, which are located on the underside of the fronds.

Tectaria matthewii plays a role in forest ecosystems by contributing to soil stability and providing habitat for microorganisms.
