Teucrium freynii

Teucrium freynii, commonly known as Freyn's germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems, opposite leaves, and aromatic properties. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly aromatic scent, while its root system is fibrous and adapted to arid conditions.

Teucrium freynii is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
