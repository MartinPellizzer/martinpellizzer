Tragium aromaticum

Tragium aromaticum, commonly known as sweet everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and the plant is often used in gardens for its ornamental value.

Tragium aromaticum is also known for its ability to thrive in dry, well-drained soils.
