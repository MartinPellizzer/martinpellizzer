Trichocereus peruvianus subsp. puquiensis

Trichocereus peruvianus subsp.

puquiensis, commonly known as the Puqui cactus, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America. It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to several meters in height.

The plant produces large, white flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. Its stems are covered in spines, which provide protection from herbivores.

This cactus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
