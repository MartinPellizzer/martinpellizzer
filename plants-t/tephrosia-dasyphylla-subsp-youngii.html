Tephrosia dasyphylla subsp. youngii

Tephrosia dasyphylla subsp.

youngii, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a shrub native to tropical Africa. It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three elliptic to ovate leaflets.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in dense, terminal clusters. It is notable for its milky sap, which is toxic and can cause dermatitis in humans.

This species is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, thriving in well-drained soils.
