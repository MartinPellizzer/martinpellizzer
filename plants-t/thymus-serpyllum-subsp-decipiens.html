Thymus serpyllum subsp. decipiens

Thymus serpyllum subsp.

decipiens, commonly known as narrowleaf thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe. It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically narrow and linear, with a dense, mat-like growth habit.

The plant produces tiny, pink to purple flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its essential oils contain thymol and carvacrol, giving it a strong, spicy aroma and medicinal properties.
