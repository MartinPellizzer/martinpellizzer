Tropaeolum peltophorum var. hederifolium

Tropaeolum peltophorum var. hederifolium, commonly known as the creeping nasturtium, is a vigorous trailing plant with heart-shaped, edible leaves and bright orange or red flowers.

It belongs to the Tropaeolaceae family and is native to South America. The plant produces edible seeds that can be used in cooking, similar to watercress. Its rapid growth and ability to climb or trail make it popular in gardens for ornamental and culinary purposes.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.
