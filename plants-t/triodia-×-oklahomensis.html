Triodia × oklahomensis

Triodia × oklahomensis, commonly known as the Oklahomensis spinifex, is a hybrid grass species native to the arid regions of Australia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its wiry, tufted growth habit and sharp, spiny leaves. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability. Its dense growth forms a mat-like structure, which helps prevent soil erosion and provides habitat for small animals.

Triodia × oklahomensis is often found in sandy or gravelly soils, thriving in regions with low rainfall and high solar exposure.
