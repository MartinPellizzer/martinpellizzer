Tsuga mertensiana subsp. mertensiana

Tsuga mertensiana subsp.

mertensiana, commonly known as the white fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of western North America. It is characterized by its conical shape, blue-green needles, and distinctive, flattened, spirally arranged cones.

The tree typically grows in high-altitude forests, preferring cool, moist climates with well-drained soils. Its bark is thick and scaly, providing protection against harsh environmental conditions.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role and is often found in mixed coniferous forests.
