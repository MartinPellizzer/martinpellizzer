Trichilia buchananii

Trichilia buchananii, commonly known as African sandalwood, is a small to medium-sized tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae, which includes mahogany and related species. The plant is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing aromatic seeds.

The tree is valued for its fragrant wood, which is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
