Tulotis flava

Tulotis flava, commonly known as the yellow tulip, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow petals and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the summer months. Tulotis flava is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun exposure for optimal growth.
