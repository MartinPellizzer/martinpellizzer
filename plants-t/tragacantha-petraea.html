Tragacantha petraea

Tragacantha petraea, commonly known as the rock tragacanth, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by pods containing seeds. It is adapted to arid and rocky environments, making it a resilient species in harsh conditions.

Its root system helps prevent soil erosion, contributing to its ecological importance in its native habitat.
