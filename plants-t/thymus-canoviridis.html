Thymus canoviridis

Thymus canoviridis, commonly known as creeping thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features small, fragrant leaves that are typically gray-green in color and emit a strong thyme scent when crushed. The plant produces tiny pink or white flowers in the spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and ground cover.

Its hardy nature allows it to survive in a variety of climates, including coastal areas.
