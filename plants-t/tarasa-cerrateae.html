Tarasa cerrateae

Tarasa cerrateae, commonly known as the Cerrateae leaf plant, is a species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique leaf morphology, featuring serrated edges and a compact growth habit. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments within tropical or subtropical forests. Its botanical significance lies in its role within local ecosystems and its potential use in traditional medicine.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species requiring conservation efforts.
