Trichocereus candicans var. tenuispinus

Trichocereus candicans var. tenuispinus, commonly known as the "Trichocereus tenuispinus," is a species of cactus native to South America, particularly in regions of Peru and Bolivia.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stem and sparse, spiny areoles that bear clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, elongated fruits that are typically green when mature. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in arid conditions with well-drained soil.

Due to its striking appearance, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in cactus collections.
