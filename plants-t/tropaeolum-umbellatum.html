Tropaeolum umbellatum

Tropaeolum umbellatum, commonly known as the Indian cress or garden nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow and orange flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant is an annual herb that thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil. It produces edible leaves and seeds, which are often used in culinary applications.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking both beauty and utility in their landscapes.
