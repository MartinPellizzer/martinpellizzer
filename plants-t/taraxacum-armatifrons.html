Taraxacum armatifrons

Taraxacum armatifrons, commonly known as the hairy dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and a yellow flower head that develops into a fluffy seed sphere. The plant is native to parts of Europe and Asia and thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. Its leaves are pubescent, giving the plant a distinctive appearance, and it is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

The species is noted for its ability to spread through wind-dispersed seeds, making it a common sight in various environments.
