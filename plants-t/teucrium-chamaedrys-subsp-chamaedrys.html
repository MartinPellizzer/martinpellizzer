Teucrium chamaedrys subsp. chamaedrys

Teucrium chamaedrys subsp.

chamaedrys, commonly known as upright germander, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It features small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has aromatic, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive grayish-green color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
