Thelypteris tablaziensis

Thelypteris tablaziensis, commonly known as the Tablazien shield fern, is a species of polypodioid fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that typically grow in a compact, upright habit. The plant's sori, which are clusters of sporangia, are located on the underside of the fronds and are covered by a protective indusium. Thelypteris tablaziensis thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest for both horticultural and taxonomic studies.
