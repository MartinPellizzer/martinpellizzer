Trichopus zeylanicus var. cordatus

Trichopus zeylanicus var. cordatus, commonly known as the heartleaf trichopus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical rainforests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation in warm climates.
