Tabernanthe iboga

Tabernanthe iboga, commonly known as the African root or iboga, is a flowering plant native to Central Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its woody stem and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that contains various alkaloids, with ibogaine being the most notable. Its roots are the primary part used in traditional practices and have been studied for their psychoactive and therapeutic properties.

Tabernanthe iboga thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
