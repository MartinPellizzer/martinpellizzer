Thymus serpyllum var. litoralis

Thymus serpyllum var. litoralis, commonly known as coastal thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to coastal regions of Europe and western Asia.

It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to maritime climates with high salt exposure and strong winds. The plant features compact, aromatic foliage with small, glandular leaves that emit a distinctive thyme scent. Its flowers are typically pink or lavender, appearing in clusters during late spring to early summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and use in culinary and medicinal applications.
