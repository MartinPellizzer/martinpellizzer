Tetrataenium canescens

Tetrataenium canescens, commonly known as the white thornapple, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces oval, green fruits that turn yellow or orange when mature. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

The species is known for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
