Taraxacum spilosum

Taraxacum spilosum, commonly known as the purple dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright purple flowers and is native to parts of Europe and Asia. The plant has a rosette of basal leaves that are typically hairy and have a waxy coating. It produces a hollow, wind-dispersed seed stem known as a pappus, which aids in its propagation.

Taraxacum spilosum is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.
