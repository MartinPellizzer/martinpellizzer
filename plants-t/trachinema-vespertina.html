Trachinema vespertina

Trachinema vespertina, commonly known as the evening star, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, hairy stems and small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in the late afternoon. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention.

Trachinema vespertina is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
