Thymus granatensis subsp. micranthus

Thymus granatensis subsp.

micranthus, commonly known as the granada thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and aromatic, narrow leaves that are typically gray-green in color.

The plant produces small, pale pink to white flowers in whorled clusters during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Botanically, it belongs to the Lamiaceae family, known for its aromatic essential oils and medicinal properties.
