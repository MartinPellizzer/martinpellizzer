Tetracera scandens var. hebecarpa

Tetracera scandens var. hebecarpa, commonly known as the climbing tetracera, is a tropical vine belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly pubescent surface. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that split open to release seeds.

This species is often found in shaded forest understories in Southeast Asia.
