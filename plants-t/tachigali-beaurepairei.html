Tachigali beaurepairei

Tachigali beaurepairei, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and legume fruits. The tree is notable for its striking, fiery orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a vibrant visual display. Its leaves are alternate, pinnate, and often have a glossy appearance.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in warm climates for its aesthetic appeal.
