Tilia cordata var. eriocarpa

Tilia cordata var. eriocarpa, commonly known as the woolly boxwood, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and thick, dark green foliage. The leaves are elliptical with a smooth margin and a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers in early summer, followed by spherical, woody fruits.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and hardiness.
