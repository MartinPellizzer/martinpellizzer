Trigonella retzii

Trigonella retzii, commonly known as Indian cumin or wild cumin, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces slender, cylindrical pods that contain small, aromatic seeds used in culinary and medicinal applications. Trigonella retzii is adapted to dry, sandy soils and thrives in temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for both traditional medicine and sustainable agriculture.
