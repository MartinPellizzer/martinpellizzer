Trichera lancifolia

Trichera lancifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved trichera, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It features slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
