Tragia volubilis var. genuina

Tragia volubilis var. genuina, commonly known as the Mexican devil's backbone, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a trailing or climbing shrub characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or disturbed soils.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptations, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
