Teucrium canadense var. virginicum

Teucrium canadense var. virginicum, commonly known as American germander, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and stream banks. The plant features opposite, narrow to lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small, tubular, and usually pink to violet, arranged in dense, spike-like clusters.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.
