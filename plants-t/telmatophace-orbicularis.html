Telmatophace orbicularis

Telmatophace orbicularis, commonly known as the orbicular water plant, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its orbicular, or round, leaves that float on the water's surface.

It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in ponds and marshes. The plant has a simple, unbranched stem with small, scale-like leaves that help it remain buoyant. It plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.

Its presence is often an indicator of water quality and ecological stability.
