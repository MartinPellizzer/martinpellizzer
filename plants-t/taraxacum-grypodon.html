Taraxacum grypodon

Taraxacum grypodon, commonly known as the giant dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its large, bright yellow composite flowers and robust, hollow stems. The plant produces a distinctive, spherical seed head that releases lightweight seeds with feathery parachutes. Native to Europe, it is often found in disturbed soils and open areas.

Its botanical features make it a notable species for both ecological studies and horticultural interest.
