Trifolium michelianum

Trifolium michelianum, commonly known as Micheli's clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged on a single stalk. This plant is native to parts of Europe and the Middle East and thrives in temperate climates. It produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color.

Trifolium michelianum is often found in grassy areas and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
