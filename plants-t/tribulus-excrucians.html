Tribulus excrucians

Tribulus excrucians, commonly known as golden shower or calotropis, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling a shower of golden droplets. The plant has compound leaves and produces small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in arid conditions.

Tribulus excrucians is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
