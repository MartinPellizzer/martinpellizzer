Tetraneuris stenophylla

Tetraneuris stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tetraneuris, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf morphology. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and high sunlight exposure. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a fine layer of hairs, which helps reduce water loss.

The yellow flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
