Tillandsia hubertiana

Tillandsia hubertiana, commonly known as the Hubert's air plant, is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on other plants without deriving nutrients from them. The plant features rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves that are covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help absorb moisture and nutrients from the air. It produces showy, bell-shaped inflorescences with vivid purple or red flowers.

Tillandsia hubertiana is popular as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
