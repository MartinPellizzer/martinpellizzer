Teucrium polium subsp. seuratianum

Teucrium polium subsp.

seuratianum, commonly known as the Persian germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Central Asia. It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pale blue or violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is well adapted to arid environments. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, known for its fragrant properties and medicinal uses.

This subspecies is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
