Trachypogon canescens

Trachypogon canescens, commonly known as the silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which results from the fine, hairy texture of its leaves. The plant produces slender, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Trachypogon canescens is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and its low maintenance requirements.
