Trifolium riograndense subsp. riograndense

Trifolium riograndense subsp.

riograndense, commonly known as the Rio Grande clover, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets with a hairy underside.

The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Its botanical features make it a valuable forage plant for livestock and a potential candidate for ecological restoration projects.
