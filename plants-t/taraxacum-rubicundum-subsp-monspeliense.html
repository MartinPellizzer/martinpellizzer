Taraxacum rubicundum subsp. monspeliense

Taraxacum rubicundum subsp.

monspeliense, commonly known as the red-seeded dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by distinctive seed heads that produce wind-dispersed seeds.

The plant has deeply lobed, hairy leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and traditional herbal medicine.
