Triodia glabra

Triodia glabra, commonly known as spinifex grass, is a perennial grass native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, wiry growth and sharp, spiny leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant thrives in sandy soils and is often found in coastal dunes and inland deserts. Its ability to form extensive mats makes it an important component of stabilizing soil and preventing erosion.

Triodia glabra is well-adapted to harsh conditions, showcasing remarkable resilience in extreme climates.
