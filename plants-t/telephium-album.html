Telephium album

Telephium album, commonly known as white ragwort, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-green leaves. The plant produces showy white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in some regions.
