Telis azurea

Telis azurea, commonly known as the bluebells of the Andes, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found growing in rocky or alpine environments. Its flowers are typically arranged in loose clusters, and it is known for its ornamental value.

Telis azurea is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
