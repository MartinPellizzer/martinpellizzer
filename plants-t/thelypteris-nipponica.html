Thelypteris nipponica

Thelypteris nipponica, commonly known as the Japanese holly fern, is a species of fern native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Thelypteridaceae and is characterized by its lance-shaped, serrated fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its rhizomes are fibrous, and the fronds are usually evergreen, providing year-round foliage.

Thelypteris nipponica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
