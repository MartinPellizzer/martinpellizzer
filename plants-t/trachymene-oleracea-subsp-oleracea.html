Trachymene oleracea subsp. oleracea

Trachymene oleracea subsp.

oleracea, commonly known as the edible false shamrock, is a herbaceous perennial native to Australia. It features heart-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated margin and produces clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers.

The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it adaptable to different environments. Its leaves are edible and have a peppery, slightly spicy flavor, often used in salads or as a garnish.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for both culinary and aesthetic purposes.
