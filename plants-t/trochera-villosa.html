Trochera villosa

Trochera villosa, commonly known as the hairy broomrape, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies on host plants for nutrients.

It belongs to the Orobanchaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, often hairy stems and small, inconspicuous flowers. Due to its parasitic nature, it can have significant ecological impacts on its host species.
