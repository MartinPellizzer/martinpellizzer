Thomasia paniculata

Thomasia paniculata, commonly known as the African bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture. Its showy, bell-shaped flowers are usually pink or purple and are arranged in paniculate inflorescences.

Thomasia paniculata thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.
