Tradescantia edwardsiana

Tradescantia edwardsiana, commonly known as redstem spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features succulent, fleshy leaves that are often reddish or purple-tinged, especially under stress. Its flowers are small, three-petaled, and typically white or pale blue, blooming in late spring to early summer.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or swampy areas.
