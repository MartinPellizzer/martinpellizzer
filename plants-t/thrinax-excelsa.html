Thrinax excelsa

Thrinax excelsa, commonly known as the Mexican palm, is a member of the Arecaceae family.

It is a tall, slender palm native to Mexico and parts of Central America. The plant has a smooth, unbranched trunk and is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and tropical environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
