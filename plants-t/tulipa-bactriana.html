Tulipa bactriana

Tulipa bactriana, commonly known as the Bactrian tulip, is a species native to Central Asia, particularly found in regions of Afghanistan, Pakistan, and Tajikistan.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant that produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and forms compact, low-growing clumps. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

The Bactrian tulip is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in traditional horticulture.
