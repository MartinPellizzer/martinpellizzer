Trichomanes orientale var. abbreviatum

Trichomanes orientale var. abbreviatum, commonly known as the abbreviated maidenhair fern, is a variant of the maidenhair fern family.

It is a small, delicate fern characterized by its finely divided, lance-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest understories. Its spore-producing structures, called soruses, are located on the undersides of the fronds.

This variety is notable for its compact growth habit and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
