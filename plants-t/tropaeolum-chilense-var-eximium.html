Tropaeolum chilense var. eximium

Tropaeolum chilense var. eximium, commonly known as the Chilean nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, edible flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces bright orange to red tubular blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to climb or trail, depending on support.
