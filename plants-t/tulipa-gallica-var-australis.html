Tulipa gallica var. australis

Tulipa gallica var. australis, commonly known as the Southern Mountain tulip, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It produces showy, cup-shaped flowers that are typically pink to red in color, with a distinctive white throat. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, preferring cooler climates. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge in early spring.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
