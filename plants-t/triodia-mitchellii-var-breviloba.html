Triodia mitchellii var. breviloba

Triodia mitchellii var. breviloba, commonly known as the small-leaved spinifex, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its compact, tufted growth habit and small, needle-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces pale green to yellowish flowering spikes, which are typically borne at the base of the plant. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.

This variety is often found in sandy or rocky soils across the interior regions of Australia.
