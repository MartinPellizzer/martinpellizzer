Taraxacum sulitelmae

Taraxacum sulitelmae, commonly known as the Tenerife dandelion, is a rare and endemic species found exclusively on the island of Tenerife in the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptation to the harsh coastal and mountainous regions. Its leaves are hairy and lobed, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a single head.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
