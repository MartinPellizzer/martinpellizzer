Tinospora formanii

Tinospora formanii, commonly known as the Indian moonseed, is a climbing shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often growing in moist, shaded areas.

Tinospora formanii is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine systems.
