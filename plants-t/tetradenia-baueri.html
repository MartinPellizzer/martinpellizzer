Tetradenia baueri

Tetradenia baueri, commonly known as the African sandthorn, is a shrub native to arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and linear, aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low rainfall.

Tetradenia baueri plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing food for various wildlife species.
