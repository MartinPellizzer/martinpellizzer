Taraxacum besarabicum

Taraxacum besarabicum, commonly known as the Arabian dandelion, is a perennial herb native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant features a rosette of stiff, hairy leaves that form a dense ground cover. Its flower heads consist of yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers.

It is adapted to arid environments, with deep roots that help it survive in dry conditions.
