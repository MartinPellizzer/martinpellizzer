Tradescantia cristata

Tradescantia cristata, commonly known as the crested spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, waxy blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with red or purple, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Tradescantia cristata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
