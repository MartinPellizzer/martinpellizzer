Trisetum ovatipaniculatum

Trisetum ovatipaniculatum, commonly known as the broad-leafed wood sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers. It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes and along waterways.

Trisetum ovatipaniculatum is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
