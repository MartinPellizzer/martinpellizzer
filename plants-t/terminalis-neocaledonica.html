Terminalis neocaledonica

Terminalis neocaledonica, commonly known as the Kava kava plant, is a perennial shrub native to the South Pacific islands.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its roots are the primary source of the active compounds used in traditional medicine.

Terminalis neocaledonica thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.
