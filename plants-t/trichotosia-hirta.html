Trichotosia hirta

Trichotosia hirta, commonly known as the hairy trichotosia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features hairy stems and leaves, which contribute to its common name. It produces small, yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Trichotosia hirta is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
