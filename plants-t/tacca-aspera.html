Tacca aspera

Tacca aspera, commonly known as the bat plant or black bat plant, is a striking tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, velvety, dark purple to black leaves that resemble the wings of a bat. The plant produces distinctive, showy flowers with a unique, bat-like shape, often with a red or orange spathe and a pale yellow spadix. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Tacca aspera is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
