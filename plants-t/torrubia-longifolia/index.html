Torrubia Longifolia

Long-leaved torrubia, scientifically known as Torrubia longifolia, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. This species thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories or along stream banks. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered vulnerable to environmental changes and habitat loss. Conservation efforts are crucial to ensure its survival in the wild.
