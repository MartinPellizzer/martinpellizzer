Thysanotus sabulosus

Thysanotus sabulosus, commonly known as the sand thysanotus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its slender stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The flowers are typically blue or violet in color and are borne on slender pedicels. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or inland scrub habitats.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
