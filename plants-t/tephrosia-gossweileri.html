Tephrosia gossweileri

Tephrosia gossweileri, commonly known as the African velvet bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, velvety leaflets, and it produces small, clustered white or yellow flowers. Tephrosia gossweileri is often cultivated for its use in traditional medicine and as a forage crop.

It also plays a role in soil enrichment due to its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen.
