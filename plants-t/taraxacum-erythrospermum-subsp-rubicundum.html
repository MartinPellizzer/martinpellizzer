Taraxacum erythrospermum subsp. rubicundum

Taraxacum erythrospermum subsp.

rubicundum, commonly known as the red-seeded dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers and hairy, spoon-shaped leaves.

The subspecies is distinguished by its reddish-brown seeds, which are smaller and more elongated than those of other dandelion species. It typically grows in temperate regions, often in meadows and disturbed soils.

This plant is also noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its resilience to environmental stress.
