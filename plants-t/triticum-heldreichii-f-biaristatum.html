Triticum heldreichii f. biaristatum

Triticum heldreichii f. biaristatum, commonly known as the Balkan wheatgrass, is a rare and distinctive botanical variant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous florets, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its genetic makeup is unique, reflecting a hybrid origin between Triticum aestivum and Triticum monococcum.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a valuable species for botanical research and conservation efforts.
