Tabebuia citrifolia

Tabebuia citrifolia, commonly known as the yellow trumpet tree, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The tree has a straight trunk with smooth, grey bark and produces small, round fruits that contain hard, angular seeds. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate with a pointed tip.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
