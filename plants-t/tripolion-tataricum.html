Tripolion tataricum

Tripolion tataricum, commonly known as Tatarian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to eastern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy blue or violet bell-shaped flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

The flowers bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
