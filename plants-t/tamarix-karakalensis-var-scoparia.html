Tamarix karakalensis var. scoparia

Tamarix karakalensis var. scoparia, commonly known as the desert tamarisk, is a shrub or small tree native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and small, scale-like leaves that reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces pink or reddish flowers in dense, spike-like clusters, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. It is highly drought-tolerant and can thrive in saline soils, making it well adapted to desert and semi-desert conditions.

Its root system is extensive, allowing it to stabilize sandy soils and prevent erosion.
