Thottea corymbosa

Thottea corymbosa, commonly known as the Indian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a smooth texture, while its stems are often hairy.

Thottea corymbosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
