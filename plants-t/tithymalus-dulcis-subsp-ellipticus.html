Tithymalus dulcis subsp. ellipticus

Tithymalus dulcis subsp.

ellipticus, commonly known as the narrowleaf tithymalus, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

The plant features slender stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often slightly hairy. Its flowers are small, clustered in spikes, and range in color from pale pink to white.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
