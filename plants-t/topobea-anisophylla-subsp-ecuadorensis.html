Topobea anisophylla subsp. ecuadorensis

Topobea anisophylla subsp.

ecuadorensis, commonly known as the Ecuadorian topobea, is a member of the Acanthaceae family. It is native to the Andean regions of Ecuador, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and striking, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to violet. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
