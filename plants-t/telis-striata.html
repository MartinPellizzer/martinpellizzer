Telis striata

Telis striata, commonly known as the striped leaf fern, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are often marked with distinct stripes or patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its foliage is usually a deep green, with a slightly waxy texture.

Telis striata prefers indirect light and high humidity, making it a popular choice for indoor gardens.
