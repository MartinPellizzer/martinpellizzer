Tragacantha tenera

Tragacantha tenera, commonly known as the slender tragacanth, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, branching stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces a distinctive gum, similar to tragacanth, which is used in traditional medicine and as a thickening agent. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and herbal applications.
