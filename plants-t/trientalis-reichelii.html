Trientalis reichelii

Trientalis reichelii, commonly known as the Reichel's starflower, is a member of the Campanulaceae family and is native to the northern United States and southern Canada.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its star-shaped white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features whorled leaves that are often hairy and have a lanceolate shape. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as an indicator species for early successional habitats.
