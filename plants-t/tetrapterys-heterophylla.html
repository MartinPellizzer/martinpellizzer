Tetrapterys heterophylla

Tetrapterys heterophylla, commonly known as the variable-leaved tetrapterys, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its variable leaf morphology, with leaves that can be either simple or compound depending on environmental conditions. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are usually alternate, with a hairy texture and varying shapes, which aid in its adaptation to different habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in warm climates for its unique foliage.
