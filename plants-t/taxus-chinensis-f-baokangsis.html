Taxus chinensis f. baokangsis

Taxus chinensis f. baokangsis, commonly known as the Baokang yew, is a rare and ornamental variant of the Chinese yew.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, featuring deep green, leathery leaves that are typically shorter and more compact than those of the standard species. The plant produces small, reddish-brown cones, which are an important feature in its botanical classification. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its aesthetic value.

Due to its limited distribution and unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
