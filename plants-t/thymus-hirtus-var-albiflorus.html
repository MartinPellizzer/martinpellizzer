Thymus hirtus var. albiflorus

Thymus hirtus var. albiflorus, commonly known as the white-flowered thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with small, aromatic leaves and showy white or pale pink flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders. Its foliage is often used in culinary applications, while its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
