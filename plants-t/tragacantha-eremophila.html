Tragacantha eremophila

Tragacantha eremophila, commonly known as the desert gum arabic tree, is a small shrub or tree native to arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by pods containing a viscous, amber-colored resin. This resin, known as gum arabic, is harvested for its use in food, pharmaceuticals, and industrial applications.

Tragacantha eremophila is well adapted to extreme drought conditions, making it a resilient species in desert ecosystems.
