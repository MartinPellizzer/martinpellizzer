Turnera sidoides var. incisa

Turnera sidoides var. incisa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf turnera, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Melastomataceae and is characterized by its opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of yellow and white, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
