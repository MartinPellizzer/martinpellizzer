Taraxacum rotundatum

Taraxacum rotundatum, commonly known as the roundleaf dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of rounded, spoon-shaped leaves and a hollow, hairless stem. The plant produces a single, inconspicuous yellow flower that develops into a white, fluffy seed head. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

Taraxacum rotundatum is a perennial herb that thrives in temperate climates and is known for its medicinal properties.
