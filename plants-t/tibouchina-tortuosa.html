Tibouchina tortuosa

Tibouchina tortuosa, commonly known as the purple glorybower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, featuring dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Tibouchina tortuosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
