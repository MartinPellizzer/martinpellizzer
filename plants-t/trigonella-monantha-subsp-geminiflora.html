Trigonella monantha subsp. geminiflora

Trigonella monantha subsp.

geminiflora, commonly known as the twin-flowered fenugreek, is a member of the Fabaceae family. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers in dense clusters, which are typically arranged in pairs, giving it its subspecies name. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

This subspecies is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
