Trifolium officinarum

Trifolium officinarum, commonly known as lesser clover, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged on a central stalk. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils.

Trifolium officinarum is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
