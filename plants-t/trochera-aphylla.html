Trochera aphylla

Trochera aphylla, commonly known as the leafless orchid, is a unique flowering plant characterized by its lack of leaves.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is native to specific regions in South America. The plant derives its nutrients through its roots rather than through photosynthesis. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, often displaying vibrant colors.

Due to its specialized adaptations, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
