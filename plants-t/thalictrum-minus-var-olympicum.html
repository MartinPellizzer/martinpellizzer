Thalictrum minus var. olympicum

Thalictrum minus var. olympicum, commonly known as the Olympic meadow rue, is a perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves that are finely divided and emit a mild, aromatic scent. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are pollinated by insects. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
