Trachyandra aridimontana

Trachyandra aridimontana, commonly known as the desert grass, is a perennial grass species native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and wiry, fibrous stems that help it withstand harsh environmental conditions. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, typically during the rainy season. Its leaves are narrow and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which reduce water loss and protect against intense sunlight.

Trachyandra aridimontana plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is adapted to low-nutrient, well-drained soils.
