Tridax venezuelensis

Tridax venezuelensis, commonly known as the Venezuelan horseweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white petals. The plant has a sprawling growth habit and is often found in disturbed soils and open areas. It produces small, oval seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread.

Tridax venezuelensis is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
