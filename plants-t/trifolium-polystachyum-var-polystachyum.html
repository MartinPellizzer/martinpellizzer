Trifolium polystachyum var. polystachyum

Trifolium polystachyum var. polystachyum, commonly known as the tall clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It features trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, pea-like flowers in shades of white, pink, or red. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
