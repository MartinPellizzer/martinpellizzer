Trichilia multicostata

Trichilia multicostata, commonly known as the African sandalwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae, which includes mahogany and other valuable hardwoods. The tree is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. Its flowers are small and fragrant, typically yellow or greenish-yellow, and it produces woody fruits containing flat, winged seeds.

The wood is highly valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine and as a source of incense.
