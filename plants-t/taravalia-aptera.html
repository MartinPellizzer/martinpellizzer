Taravalia aptera

Taravalia aptera, commonly known as the silver leaf tree, is a species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its dense, leathery foliage. The leaves are typically elliptical, with a bluish-gray underside that gives the plant its common name. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are often white or pale yellow, followed by small, round fruits.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
