Taraxacum glaberrimum

Taraxacum glaberrimum, commonly known as the woolly dandelion, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces a distinctive, hollow stem with a milky sap that is often used in traditional medicine. Its seeds are dispersed by wind through a parachute-like structure, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Taraxacum glaberrimum is adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a resilient species in challenging habitats.
