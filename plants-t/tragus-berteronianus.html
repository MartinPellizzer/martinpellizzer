Tragus berteronianus

Tragus berteronianus, commonly known as the Mexican thorn, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Mexico and the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It is adapted to survive in dry, rocky soils and can tolerate high temperatures and low rainfall.

Its botanical features make it an important plant in desert ecosystems.
