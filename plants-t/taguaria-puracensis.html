Taguaria puracensis

Taguaria puracensis, commonly known as the Peruvian lily, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Alstroemeriaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in moist, shaded areas of cloud forests. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a waxy surface that helps retain moisture.

Taguaria puracensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
