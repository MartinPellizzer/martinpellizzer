Trisetum luzonense

Trisetum luzonense, commonly known as Luzon feathergrass, is a perennial grass native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and delicate, feathery panicles that give it a soft, grass-like appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and forest edges. It has narrow, linear leaves that are often slightly hairy along the edges.

Trisetum luzonense is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
