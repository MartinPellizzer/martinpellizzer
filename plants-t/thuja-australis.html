Thuja australis

Thuja australis, commonly known as the Australian arborvitae, is a coniferous evergreen shrub or small tree native to southeastern Australia.

It has scale-like leaves that are arranged in flat, sprout-like clusters, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, round cones that mature to a reddish-brown color. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Thuja australis is also valued for its aromatic foliage and resilience to environmental stress.
