Taraxacum spiculatum

Taraxacum spiculatum, commonly known as the spiked dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and a yellow flower head that matures into a white, fluffy seed head. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. It has a robust root system that allows it to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

The species is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and vegetative reproduction.
