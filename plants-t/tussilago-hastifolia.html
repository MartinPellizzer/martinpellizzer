Tussilago hastifolia

Tussilago hastifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved coughwort, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, yellowish-green flowers in the spring. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks. It is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly for respiratory ailments, and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.

Tussilago hastifolia is also an important nectar source for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
