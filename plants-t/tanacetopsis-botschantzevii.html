Tanacetopsis botschantzevii

Tanacetopsis botschantzevii, commonly known as the Botchanzev's tansy.

This plant is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and yellow flowers. It is native to regions of Central Asia and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. The plant has a compact growth habit and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

It prefers full sun and well-drained soil conditions.
