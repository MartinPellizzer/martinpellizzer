Taraxacum ceratolepis

Taraxacum ceratolepis, commonly known as the horned dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiky seed heads that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. Its leaves are basal, rough, and toothed, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a composite head.

The species is noted for its resilience and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
