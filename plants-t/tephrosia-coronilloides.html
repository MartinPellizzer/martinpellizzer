Tephrosia coronilloides

Tephrosia coronilloides, commonly known as the Crownvetch, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and it has the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria. Tephrosia coronilloides is often used in ornamental gardening and as a cover crop due to its hardy nature and rapid growth.

However, it can become invasive in some environments if not managed properly.
