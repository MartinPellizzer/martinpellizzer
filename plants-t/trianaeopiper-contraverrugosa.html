Trianaeopiper contraverrugosa

Trianaeopiper contraverrugosa, commonly known as the zigzag spirea, is a unique flowering plant characterized by its intricate, zigzagging stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers.

It is native to the mountainous regions of South America, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. The plant's leaves are narrow and serrated, adding to its distinctive appearance. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation efforts.
