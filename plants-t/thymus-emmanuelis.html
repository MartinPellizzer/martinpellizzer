Thymus emmanuelis

Thymus emmanuelis, commonly known as Emmanuel's thyme, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with small, aromatic leaves and pink or purple flowers. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like mint and lavender. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its essential oils contain thymol, giving it a strong, spicy scent and making it valuable in herbal medicine.
