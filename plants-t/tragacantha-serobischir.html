Tragacantha serobischir

Tragacantha serobischir, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the arid and semi-arid regions of southwestern Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces a resinous gum, known as tragacanth, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a thickening agent in food and pharmaceuticals.

Its ability to thrive in harsh environments makes it an important species in ecological restoration and soil stabilization efforts.
