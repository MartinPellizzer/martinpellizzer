Tripterygium regelii

Tripterygium regelii, commonly known as the Thunder God's Trumpet, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its distinctive, orange-red, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant contains various bioactive compounds, including tripterygium glycosides, which have been studied for their medicinal properties. Its leaves and roots are often used in traditional Chinese medicine for their anti-inflammatory and immunosuppressive effects.

However, it is important to note that the plant is toxic and should be handled with care.
