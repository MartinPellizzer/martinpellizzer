Timonius pachyphyllus

Timonius pachyphyllus, commonly known as the thick-leaved timonius, is a unique plant species characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments.

It belongs to the family Timoniaceae and is native to specific regions in southern Africa. The plant's leaves are often covered in a waxy cuticle, which helps reduce water loss and protect against intense sunlight. Its flowers are small and typically greenish-white, appearing in clusters at the base of the plant.
