Triopterys reclinata

Triopterys reclinata, commonly known as the hanging hibiscus, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a climbing shrub that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds, contributing to its ecological importance.
