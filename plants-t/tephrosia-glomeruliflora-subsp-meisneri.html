Tephrosia glomeruliflora subsp. meisneri

Tephrosia glomeruliflora subsp.

meisneri, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa. It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and compound leaves with narrow, lance-shaped leaflets.

The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that are typically borne in terminal inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or savanna habitats.

The species is noted for its potential use in traditional medicine and as a source of bioactive compounds.
