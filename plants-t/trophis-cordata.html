Trophis cordata

Trophis cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of showy, pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in wet, swampy areas and is often found in marshy habitats. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Trophis cordata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
