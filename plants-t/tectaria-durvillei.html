Tectaria durvillei

Tectaria durvillei, commonly known as the Durville's shield fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Tectariaceae and is characterized by its leathery, shield-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, anchoring itself with its rhizomes and absorbing moisture from the air. Its leaves are usually dark green and have a textured, rugged surface.

Tectaria durvillei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded, humid environments.
