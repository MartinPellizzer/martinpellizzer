Taraxacum pomposum

Taraxacum pomposum, commonly known as the showy dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by fluffy white seeds that are dispersed by the wind. The plant has a rosette of basal, hairy leaves that are lobed and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and gardens.

Taraxacum pomposum is a perennial plant with a deep taproot, allowing it to survive in various environmental conditions.
