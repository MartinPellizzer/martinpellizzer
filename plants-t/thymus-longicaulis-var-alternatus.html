Thymus longicaulis var. alternatus

Thymus longicaulis var. alternatus, commonly known as the alternate thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It features aromatic, gray-green foliage with small, needle-like leaves that are densely packed along its stems. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in herbal medicine for its aromatic properties.
