Thymophylla acerosa

Thymophylla acerosa, commonly known as the hard-leaved thymophylla, is a perennial plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rigid, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, blooming during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique adaptations, it is considered an important species for ecological studies in arid ecosystems.
