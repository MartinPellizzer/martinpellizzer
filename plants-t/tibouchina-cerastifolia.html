Tibouchina cerastifolia

Tibouchina cerastifolia, commonly known as the purple flame bush, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple to red flowers that bloom in dense clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has glossy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Tibouchina cerastifolia is also valued for its ability to tolerate coastal conditions and its use in traditional medicine.
