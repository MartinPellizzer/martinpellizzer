Tillandsia serrata

Tillandsia serrata, commonly known as the serrated air plant, is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on other plants without being parasitic, often attaching itself to trees or rocks. The plant's leaves are narrow, grass-like, and covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help it absorb moisture and nutrients from the air. It produces small, violet-colored flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a delicate touch to its appearance.

Due to its unique ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
