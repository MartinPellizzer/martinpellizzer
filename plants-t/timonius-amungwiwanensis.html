Timonius amungwiwanensis

Timonius amungwiwanensis, commonly known as the Amungwiwanensis cycad, is a rare cycad species native to the tropical rainforests of central Africa.

It is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of stiff, sword-like leaves that can grow up to two meters in length. The plant produces large, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs, typical of cycads. It prefers well-drained, nutrient-rich soils and thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered endangered and is protected in several conservation areas.
