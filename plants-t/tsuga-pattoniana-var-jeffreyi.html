Tsuga pattoniana var. jeffreyi

Tsuga pattoniana var. jeffreyi, commonly known as Jeffrey pine, is a variant of the white fir species native to the western United States.

It is a tall, coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its long, needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure. The bark is thick, scaly, and becomes fissured with age, providing natural protection against fire and pests. Its cones are cylindrical, woody, and contain two-winged seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This species plays a vital role in forest ecosystems, offering habitat and food for various wildlife species.
