Thymus serpyllum var. subcitratus

Thymus serpyllum var. subcitratus, commonly known as lemon thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features small, fragrant leaves that emit a citrus-like aroma, distinguishing it from other thyme varieties. The plant produces pink or purple flowers in the spring, which are clustered in dense spikes. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its aromatic properties make it a popular choice for culinary and medicinal uses.
