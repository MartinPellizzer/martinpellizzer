Taraxacum monotropum

Taraxacum monotropum, commonly known as the alpine dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant produces a single, short stem with a basal rosette of leaves, and its flowers are typically yellow, forming a composite inflorescence. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Due to its ecological significance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
