Thalictrum minus proles dumulosum

Thalictrum minus proles dumulosum, commonly known as the woolly avens, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its stems are often covered in dense, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits to pollinators.
