Thujopsis dolabrata f. nana

Thujopsis dolabrata f. nana, commonly known as dwarf Chinese tallowtree, is a compact, slow-growing evergreen shrub or small tree native to China.

It features dense, dark green foliage with a distinctive, rounded shape and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by small, woody fruits that disperse its seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature, thriving in a variety of soil types.

However, it can be invasive in some regions, making it important to manage its growth carefully.
