Trifolium pratense var. kotulae

Trifolium pratense var. kotulae, commonly known as Kotula's red clover, is a variant of the red clover species native to Europe.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, typically with three heart-shaped leaflets, and produces small, pink to purple flowers. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and pastures. It is valued for its role in soil nitrogen fixation and as a forage crop.

Its botanical features make it an important plant in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
