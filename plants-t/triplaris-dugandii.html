Triplaris dugandii

Triplaris dugandii, commonly known as the Guianese tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elongated, woody pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.
