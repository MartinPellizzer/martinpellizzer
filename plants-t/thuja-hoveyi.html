Thuja hoveyi

Thuja hoveyi, commonly known as the Hove's arborvitae, is a coniferous evergreen native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, pyramidal shape and scale-like leaves that are typically a deep green. The plant produces small, spherical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color, containing winged seeds. Thuja hoveyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its hardiness and adaptability.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and landscape designs.
