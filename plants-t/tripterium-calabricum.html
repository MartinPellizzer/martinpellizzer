Tripterium calabricum

Tripterium calabricum, commonly known as the Mediterranean tripteris, is a small shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Tripteridaceae and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically borne in clusters. Tripterium calabricum is often found in rocky soils and dry habitats, showing a preference for well-drained environments.

It is also known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
