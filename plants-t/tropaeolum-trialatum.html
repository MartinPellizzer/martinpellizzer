Tropaeolum trialatum

Tropaeolum trialatum, commonly known as the "Jacob's ladder," is a climbing vine native to South America.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves and produces small, vibrant orange flowers. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to climb using its tendrils. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Tropaeolum trialatum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens and containers.
