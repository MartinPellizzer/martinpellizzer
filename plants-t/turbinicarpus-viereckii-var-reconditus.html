Turbinicarpus viereckii var. reconditus

Turbinicarpus viereckii var. reconditus, commonly known as the "Desert Rose" or "Cactus Rose," is a rare and striking member of the Cactaceae family.

It is native to the arid regions of southern Mexico, particularly in the state of Oaxaca. This plant is characterized by its vibrant pink to red flowers that bloom during the rainy season, typically from May to July. Its stems are cylindrical and covered in white to pale green spines, which help protect it from herbivores and reduce water loss.

The species is highly sought after by cactus enthusiasts due to its unique appearance and limited natural range.
