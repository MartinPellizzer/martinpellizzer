Talinum nitidum

Talinum nitidum, commonly known as velvetleaf or red-rooted morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual with alternate, ovate leaves that are usually covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink or purple, often with a red throat. It thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Talinum nitidum is also valued for its edible leaves, which are used in traditional cuisines.
