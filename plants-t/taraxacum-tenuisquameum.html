Taraxacum tenuisquameum

Taraxacum tenuisquameum, commonly known as the narrow-scale dandelion, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, particularly China and Korea.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating. Its yellow flowers develop into fluffy white seed heads, typical of the dandelion genus.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows and grassy areas.
