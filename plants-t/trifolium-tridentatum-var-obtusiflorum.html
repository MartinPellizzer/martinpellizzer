Trifolium tridentatum var. obtusiflorum

Trifolium tridentatum var. obtusiflorum, commonly known as the broad-flowered tridentata clover, is a flowering plant in the legume family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in dense heads. It is typically found in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
