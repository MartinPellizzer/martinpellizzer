Tristemma papillosum

Tristemma papillosum, commonly known as the silky tristema, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, papillate (bumpy) leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Tristemma papillosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
