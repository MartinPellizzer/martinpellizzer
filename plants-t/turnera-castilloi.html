Turnera castilloi

Turnera castilloi, commonly known as the Castillo's turnera, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the family Turneraceae and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in limestone areas. Its leaves are usually alternate and have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

Turnera castilloi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
