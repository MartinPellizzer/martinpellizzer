Themeda gigantea var. caudata

Themeda gigantea var. caudata, commonly known as the tufted kangaroo grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth and distinctive tufted inflorescence at the apex of each stem. The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

This plant is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
