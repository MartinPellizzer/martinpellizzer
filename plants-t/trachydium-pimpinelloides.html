Trachydium pimpinelloides

Trachydium pimpinelloides, commonly known as the common tamarisk, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Tamaricaceae.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit, with numerous small, scale-like leaves arranged in clusters. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It is well adapted to arid and saline environments, making it a common species in coastal and desert regions.

Trachydium pimpinelloides is often used for erosion control due to its extensive root system and tolerance to harsh conditions.
