Tradescantia trachyloma

Tradescantia trachyloma, commonly known as the zigzag plant, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, zigzagging stems that help it climb or trail along surfaces. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, depending on the variety. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a waxy texture, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments.

Tradescantia trachyloma is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
