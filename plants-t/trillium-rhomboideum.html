Trillium rhomboideum

Trillium rhomboideum, commonly known as the purple trillium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features three broadly ovate leaves that are typically a deep green with a waxy texture. The plant produces a single, showy flower with three petals that range in color from white to pinkish-purple. It blooms in early spring and is often found in rich, moist woodlands.

Trillium rhomboideum is an important component of forest ecosystems and is considered a symbol of the Appalachian region.
