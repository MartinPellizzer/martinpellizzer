Trichopus zeylanicus subsp. angustifolius

Trichopus zeylanicus subsp.

angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf trichopus, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikes, producing a modest show of white or pale yellow blooms.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
