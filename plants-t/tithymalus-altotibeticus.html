Tithymalus altotibeticus

Tithymalus altotibeticus, commonly known as the Tibetan tithymalus, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Tibetan Plateau.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climatic conditions. Its flowers are usually small and yellow, with a distinctive shape that aids in pollination by insects.

Tithymalus altotibeticus is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
