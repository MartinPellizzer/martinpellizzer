Teucrium haenseleri

Teucrium haenseleri, commonly known as the rock rose or mountain germander, is a perennial shrub native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant features small, often purple or blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, with a strong aromatic scent.

Teucrium haenseleri is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
