Tulipa sylvestris var. montana

Tulipa sylvestris var. montana, commonly known as the mountain tulip, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep red, often with a distinctive purple or brown eye. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils. It is typically found in alpine meadows and is adapted to cold climates with a long growing season.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens with similar environmental conditions.
