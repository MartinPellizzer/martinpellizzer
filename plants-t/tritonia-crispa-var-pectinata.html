Tritonia crispa var. pectinata

Tritonia crispa var. pectinata, commonly known as the Pectinate Tritonia, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy texture. This plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements make it a desirable addition to both gardens and botanical collections.
