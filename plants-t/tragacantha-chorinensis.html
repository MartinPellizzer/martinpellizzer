Tragacantha chorinensis

Tragacantha chorinensis, commonly known as the Persian gum plant, is a flowering shrub native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by pod-like fruits containing seeds. It is known for its ability to produce a viscous resin, which has been traditionally used in various medicinal and industrial applications.

Due to its adaptability to harsh environments, it plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil in arid landscapes.
