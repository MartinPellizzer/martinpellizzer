Thermopsis laburnifolia

Thermopsis laburnifolia, commonly known as goldenroot, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features erect, branched stems that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, yellowish hairs. The plant produces bright yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately compound, and have a distinctive, fern-like appearance.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
