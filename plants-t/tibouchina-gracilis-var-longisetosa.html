Tibouchina gracilis var. longisetosa

Tibouchina gracilis var. longisetosa, commonly known as the silverleaf tibouchina, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, silver-gray leaves and clusters of pale pink to white flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.

The variety longisetosa is distinguished by its longer, more prominent bracts compared to the typical form.
