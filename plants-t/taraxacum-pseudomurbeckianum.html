Taraxacum pseudomurbeckianum

Taraxacum pseudomurbeckianum, commonly known as the Alpine dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by a rosette of spoon-shaped, hairy leaves and a yellow composite flower head. Native to mountainous regions of Europe, it thrives in alpine and subalpine habitats. The plant has a taproot system and produces a pappus of bristles to aid in seed dispersal.

It is notable for its ability to tolerate cold climates and poor soil conditions.
