Tacca integrifolia

Tacca integrifolia, commonly known as the "umbrella plant" or "devil's ivy," is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white and green patterns. The plant produces unique, fragrant flowers that resemble an umbrella, with a central rosy-purple spathe and a cluster of white spathes. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, fertile soil.

Tacca integrifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
