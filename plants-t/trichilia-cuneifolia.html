Trichilia cuneifolia

Trichilia cuneifolia, commonly known as African shea tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by flat, winged fruits that contain seeds used for oil extraction. Its bark and leaves are valued for their medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antifungal effects.

Trichilia cuneifolia plays an important ecological role in its native habitats and is also economically significant for local communities.
