Teucrium crispum

Teucrium crispum, commonly known as crested germander, is a perennial herb native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that are often covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a distinctive crinkled or curled appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or violet, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Teucrium crispum is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
