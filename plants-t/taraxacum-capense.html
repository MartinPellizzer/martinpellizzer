Taraxacum capense

Taraxacum capense, commonly known as the Cape dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a rosette of basal, spoon-shaped leaves with a waxy coating, and it produces a single, yellow, composite flower head that blooms in spring. The plant is characterized by its taproot system and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, sharing features such as composite flowers and achenes as its seeds.

The plant is adapted to arid conditions and has a relatively short growing season.
