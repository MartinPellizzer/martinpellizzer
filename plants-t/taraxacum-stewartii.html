Taraxacum stewartii

Taraxacum stewartii, commonly known as Stewart's dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is a perennial species native to the western United States, particularly found in California and Oregon. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by wind-dispersed seeds. Its leaves are basal, spoon-shaped, and have a hairy texture.

It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.
