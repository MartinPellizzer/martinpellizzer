Tillandsia propagulifera

Tillandsia propagulifera, commonly known as the silver air plant, is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is native to South America.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on other plants without deriving nutrients from them. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are covered in a dense layer of white trichomes, which help it absorb moisture and nutrients from the air. It produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, and it is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance.

Tillandsia propagulifera is relatively easy to care for, requiring minimal water and indirect sunlight.
