Trifolium chrysanthoides

Trifolium chrysanthoides, commonly known as yellow clover, is a flowering plant in the legume family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets, and its clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant is native to Europe and has been introduced to other parts of the world, often growing in grassy areas and disturbed soils. It is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in agricultural settings.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
