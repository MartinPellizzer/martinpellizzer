Tanakaea radicans

Tanakaea radicans, commonly known as the creeping tanakaea, is a perennial aquatic plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater environments such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams. The plant features a rhizomatous stem that spreads horizontally along the substrate, with small, oval-shaped leaves that are often submerged. Its flowers are inconspicuous and usually appear in clusters, while its spores are released into the water for dispersal.

Tanakaea radicans plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and invertebrates.
