Triticum durum grex orientale

Triticum durum grex orientale, commonly known as durum wheat, is a species of wheat native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hard, amber-colored grains, which are rich in protein and gluten. This plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with spike-like inflorescences bearing small, two-lobed flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

The plant is well adapted to temperate climates and requires a cool growing season for optimal development.
