Trevirana maculata

Trevirana maculata, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive spotted leaves and delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Trevirana maculata belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is known for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest for both horticulturists and botanists.
