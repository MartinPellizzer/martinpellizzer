Teucrium × ruthenense

Teucrium × ruthenense, commonly known as the hybrid germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, arranged in dense spikes. It is native to regions of Europe and Asia, often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Teucrium × ruthenense is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
