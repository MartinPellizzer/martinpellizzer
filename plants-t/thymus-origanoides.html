Thymus origanoides

Thymus origanoides, commonly known as Greek thyme or oregano thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, narrow leaves that are aromatic and often used in culinary applications. The plant produces tiny, pink to purple flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its essential oils contain compounds like thymol and carvacrol, which contribute to its strong fragrance and medicinal properties.
