Taraxacum promontoriorum

Taraxacum promontoriorum, commonly known as the promontory dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of stiff, hairy leaves and a yellow flowering head that develops into a white, puffy seed head. This species is native to Europe and is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and meadows. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly rough texture, while its flowers are typically visited by various pollinators.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and is relatively hardy in temperate climates.
