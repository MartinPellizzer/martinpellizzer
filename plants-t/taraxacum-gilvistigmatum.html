Taraxacum gilvistigmatum

Taraxacum gilvistigmatum, commonly known as the yellow-stemmed dandelion, is a flowering plant in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and hairy, reddish-brown stems. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is native to parts of Europe and Asia. Its leaves are usually basal, spoon-shaped, and have a rough texture.

The species is notable for its unique floral structure and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.
