Tragacantha bolanderi

Tragacantha bolanderi, commonly known as Bolander's tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to California.

It is a shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of the region. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its scientific name reflects its classification within the Astragalus genus, which includes numerous species of legumes.

Tragacantha bolanderi is noted for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various wildlife.
