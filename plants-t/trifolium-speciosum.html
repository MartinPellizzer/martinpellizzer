Trifolium speciosum

Trifolium speciosum, commonly known as the showy clover, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, often appearing in clusters. Trifolium speciosum is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky areas.
