Tradescantella

Tradescantella, commonly known as the silver spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces tiny, seed-like fruits called caryopses. Tradescantella thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

Its ornamental value comes from its delicate appearance and ability to naturalize in gardens.
