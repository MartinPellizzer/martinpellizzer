Thuja glauca

Thuja glauca, commonly known as the Eastern White Cedar, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its scale-like leaves that are blue-gray in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. The tree produces small, spherical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, slightly acidic conditions.

Thuja glauca is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate urban environments.
