Tessaria legitima

Tessaria legitima, commonly known as the sacred orchid, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and requires a cool, humid environment to thrive. Its unique morphology includes long, slender stems and showy, violet-colored petals.

Due to its rarity and ecological significance, it is considered a protected species in its native habitat.
