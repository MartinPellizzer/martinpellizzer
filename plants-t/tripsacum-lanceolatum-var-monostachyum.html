Tripsacum lanceolatum var. monostachyum

Tripsacum lanceolatum var. monostachyum, commonly known as the narrowleaf tripesac, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescence. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has small, two-lobed flowers arranged in a single spike, which is a distinguishing feature of the variety.

The species is valued for its ornamental potential and historical significance in traditional agriculture.
