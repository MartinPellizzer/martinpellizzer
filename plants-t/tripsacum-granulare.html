Tripsacum granulare

Tripsacum granulare, commonly known as the rough tumblergrass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit. The plant has rough, hairy leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences. It is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils, showing some degree of drought tolerance.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and potential agricultural use.
