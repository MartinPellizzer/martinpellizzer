Thunia alba var. brymeriana

Thunia alba var. brymeriana, commonly known as the white thunia, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its pale white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring, adding a subtle elegance to its appearance. The plant prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it suitable for shaded garden environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery bloom.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
