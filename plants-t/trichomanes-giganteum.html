Trichomanes giganteum

Trichomanes giganteum, commonly known as the giant water fern, is a large, aquatic fern belonging to the family Water Ferns.

It is characterized by its long, ribbon-like fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and marshes. Its spores are produced in specialized structures called sori, which are located on the underside of the fronds.

Trichomanes giganteum plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen to water organisms.
