Trifolium repens var. luxurians

Trifolium repens var. luxurians, commonly known as the luxuriant white clover, is a variant of the common white clover, characterized by its dense growth habit and abundant flowering.

It is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in grassy areas and lawns. The plant features trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. It is known for its ability to thrive in various soil conditions and its role in nitrogen fixation, enriching the soil.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and as a forage crop due to its hardy nature.
