Tanacetum potaninii var. nanum

Tanacetum potaninii var. nanum, commonly known as the dwarf tansy, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It features small, yellowish-green flower heads that are typically arranged in compact clusters. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and compact size, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and subtle floral display.
