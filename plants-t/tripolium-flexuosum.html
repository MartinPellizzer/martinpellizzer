Tripolium flexuosum

Tripolium flexuosum, commonly known as the creeping thornweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically visited by various pollinators. Its leaves are alternate, narrow, and often covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Tripolium flexuosum is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in sunny, open habitats.
