Thelocactus bicolor var. schwarzii

Thelocactus bicolor var. schwarzii, commonly known as the Schwarz's whitestick, is a rare and unique cactus native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its pale green to white stems, which are segmented and covered in small, spiny tubercles. The plant produces striking yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a vibrant contrast to its otherwise muted appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for desert environments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern.
