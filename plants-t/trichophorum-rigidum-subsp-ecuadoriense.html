Trichophorum rigidum subsp. ecuadoriense

Trichophorum rigidum subsp.

ecuadoriense, commonly known as the Ecuadorian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Andean regions of Ecuador. It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that bear minute flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in mountainous or high-altitude grasslands. It has a compact growth habit and is adapted to the cool, temperate climates of its native habitat.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local flora and ecosystem.
