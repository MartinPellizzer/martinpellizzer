Tupeia antarctica

Tupeia antarctica, commonly known as the Antarctic willow, is a flowering plant native to the southern hemisphere.

It is a small shrub that grows in the cold, arid environments of South America, southern Africa, and Australia. The plant has small, simple leaves and produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. It is adapted to survive in harsh conditions with minimal water and extreme temperatures.

Tupeia antarctica plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.
