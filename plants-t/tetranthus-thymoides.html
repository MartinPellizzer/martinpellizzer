Tetranthus thymoides

Tetranthus thymoides, commonly known as sweetroot or wild thyme, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, small leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has a creeping growth habit with hairy stems. Its leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its distinctive, spicy fragrance.

Tetranthus thymoides is often used in herbal remedies and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
