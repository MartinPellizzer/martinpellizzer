Trapa longicarpa

Trapa longicarpa, commonly known as the long-sepaled water chestnut, is a aquatic plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its floating leaves and distinctive, spiny fruits that develop underwater. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments. Its seeds are often dispersed by water and can remain viable for several years.

Trapa longicarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
