Taraxacum platyglossum

Taraxacum platyglossum, commonly known as the broad-leafed dandelion, is a flowering plant in the sunflower family (Asteraceae).

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, spoon-shaped leaves and a yellow composite flower head that develops into a fluffy white seed head. Native to Europe, it is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant has a taproot system and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

It is also valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
