Tanacetopsis paropamisica

Tanacetopsis paropamisica, commonly known as the Paropamisus goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky and dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its yellow flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Tanacetopsis paropamisica is also valued for its medicinal properties, with traditional uses in local herbal remedies.
