Tragacantha anatolica

Tragacantha anatolica, commonly known as Anatolian tragacanth, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by pods containing several seeds. Its root system is known for producing a viscous gum, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive.

This species is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and thrives in regions with a Mediterranean climate.
