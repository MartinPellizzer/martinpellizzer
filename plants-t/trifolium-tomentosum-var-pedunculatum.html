Trifolium tomentosum var. pedunculatum

Trifolium tomentosum var. pedunculatum, commonly known as the woolly clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its dense, hairy foliage.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassy areas or along roadsides.

Due to its ornamental value and nitrogen-fixing ability, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
