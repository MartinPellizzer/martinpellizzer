Tulipa mucronata

Tulipa mucronata, commonly known as the Persian tulip, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and produces showy blooms in early spring. Its leaves are long, narrow, and grass-like, forming a basal rosette.

Tulipa mucronata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
