Thymus pseudomicromeria

Thymus pseudomicromeria, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of pink or white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are often used in culinary applications, adding a distinctive flavor to dishes.

Additionally, it is valued in traditional medicine for its potential antimicrobial and antioxidant properties.
