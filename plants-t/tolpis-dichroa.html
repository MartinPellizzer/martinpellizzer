Tolpis dichroa

Tolpis dichroa, commonly known as the two-colored grass, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive two-colored leaves, which are usually green on the upper surface and silver-white on the underside. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and along riverbanks, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of grasses.

Tolpis dichroa is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
