Thymus pulegioides subsp. pulchellus

Thymus pulegioides subsp.

pulchellus, commonly known as the purple thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe. It features small, fragrant, purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are covered in fine hairs, typical of the Thymus genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic properties make it valuable in both culinary and medicinal applications.
