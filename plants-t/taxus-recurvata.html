Taxus recurvata

Taxus recurvata, commonly known as the twisted yew, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the genus Taxus, which includes several species of yews known for their toxic properties. The plant is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and distinctive, twisted growth habit. Its leaves are small, leathery, and arranged in spirals along the stems.

The plant produces red berries that are highly poisonous to humans and animals.
