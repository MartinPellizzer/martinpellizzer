Taxillus pseudochinensis

Taxillus pseudochinensis, commonly known as the Chinese parasitic vine, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the roots of other plants to derive some of its nutrients.

It belongs to the Loranthaceae family and is native to regions of East Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea. The plant lacks chlorophyll and relies heavily on its host for water and minerals, while still performing some photosynthesis using its leaves. It produces small, yellowish flowers and red berries that are sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can significantly impact the growth and survival of its host plants.
