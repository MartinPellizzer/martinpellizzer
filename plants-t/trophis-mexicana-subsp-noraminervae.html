Trophis mexicana subsp. noraminervae

Trophis mexicana subsp.

noraminervae, commonly known as the Mexican broom, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Mexico. It is characterized by its dense, branching structure and small, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom during the spring and summer months. It is adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils and is drought-tolerant.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and nectar for pollinators in its native environment.
