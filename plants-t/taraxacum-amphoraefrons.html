Taraxacum amphoraefrons

Taraxacum amphoraefrons, commonly known as the amphora-fron dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its robust, hairy stems and distinctive, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces a single, yellow flower head that develops into a white, spherical seed head with bracts. It is native to Europe and is often found in meadows and grassy areas.

The species is notable for its horticultural value and its role in supporting pollinators.
