Tetrapterys molinae

Tetrapterys molinae, commonly known as the Molina's four-winged leaf tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its unique leaves that have four distinct lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in humid, lowland areas and thrives in warm, sunny climates. Its flowers are small and clustered, often producing a fragrant scent that attracts pollinators.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.
