Telephium imperati var. pseudorientale

Telephium imperati var. pseudorientale, commonly known as the oriental sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features dense clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a mat-forming growth habit with succulent, fleshy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, and the stems are short and branching.

It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.
