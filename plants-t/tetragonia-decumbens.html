Tetragonia decumbens

Tetragonia decumbens, commonly known as New Zealand spinach, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand and parts of Australia.

It is a low-growing annual herb with succulent, edible leaves that are often used in salads. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into triangular, edible fruits. It thrives in coastal environments and is adapted to saline soils.

Its botanical features include alternate, ovate leaves and a sprawling growth habit.
