Tropidia somae

Tropidia somae, commonly known as the Somalian orchid, is a rare and unique orchid species native to the Horn of Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit and produces striking, fragrant flowers with intricate petal structures. The plant typically blooms in the dry season, showcasing a range of colors from white to pale pink. Its leaves are usually fleshy and adapted to store water, reflecting its ability to thrive in arid environments.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, Tropidia somae is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
