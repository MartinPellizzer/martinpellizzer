Thelypteris pilosohispida

Thelypteris pilosohispida, commonly known as the hairy-horned water fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Thelypteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and wetlands. Its leaves are usually bipinnate, with small, rounded lobes that give it a lacy appearance.

Thelypteris pilosohispida is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal.
