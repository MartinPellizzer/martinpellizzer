Tragopogon nachitschevanicus

Tragopogon nachitschevanicus, commonly known as the Armenian goat's beard, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers sunny, well-drained environments. It features a basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers that develop into slender, beak-like seed pods.

Tragopogon nachitschevanicus is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
