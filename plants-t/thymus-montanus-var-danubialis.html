Thymus montanus var. danubialis

Thymus montanus var. danubialis, commonly known as Danube thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It features compact, aromatic foliage with small, fuzzy leaves that emit a distinctive thyme scent. The plant produces pink to purple flowers in late spring, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
