Tanacetum nipponicum

Tanacetum nipponicum, commonly known as Japanese tansy, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features slender, upright stems and finely divided, feathery leaves that give it a delicate appearance. The plant produces small, yellow to pale green flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters, which bloom from summer to autumn. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodland edges and stream banks.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and chrysanthemums.
