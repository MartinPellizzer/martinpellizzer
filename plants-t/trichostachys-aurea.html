Trichostachys aurea

Trichostachys aurea, commonly known as golden bamboo, is a member of the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Asia and is characterized by its slender, golden-hued culms. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often adorned with silky hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental horticulture.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it can become invasive in certain environments.
