Trixis divaricata var. odoratissima

Trixis divaricata var. odoratissima, commonly known as the fragrant trixis, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that emit a strong scent when crushed. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers clustered in dense inflorescences, which bloom during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Due to its unique fragrance and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in arid gardens.
