Tubella dunstervillei

Tubella dunstervillei, commonly known as the Dunsterville's tubella, is a rare and enigmatic species of fungus.

It belongs to the family Tubellariaceae and is characterized by its unique, delicate, and often branched fruiting bodies. The spores are typically released in a cloud-like manner, which is an adaptation for dispersal in its natural habitat. This species is primarily found in moist, shaded environments, such as under decaying logs or in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and fragile structure, it is considered a challenging subject for mycological study.
