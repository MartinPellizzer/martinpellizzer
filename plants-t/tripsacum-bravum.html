Tripsacum bravum

Tripsacum bravum, commonly known as wild maize, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is closely related to maize (Zea mays). The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in disturbed or open habitats. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often clustered in dense, brush-like structures.

Tripsacum bravum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential in agricultural and genetic research.
