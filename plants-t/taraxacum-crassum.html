Taraxacum crassum

Taraxacum crassum, commonly known as the broadleaf dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its large, spoon-shaped leaves and bright yellow composite flowers that bloom in the spring. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this plant is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant produces a distinctive seed head known as a pappus, which aids in wind dispersal.

It is a hardy perennial that thrives in a variety of environmental conditions.
