Tabernaemontana amplifolia

Tabernaemontana amplifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf angel's trumpet, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, waxy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, emitting a strong, sweet fragrance. The plant has dark green, broad, ovate leaves that are glossy and arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
