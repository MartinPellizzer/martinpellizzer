Trachelospermum obtusifolium

Trachelospermum obtusifolium, commonly known as the sweetheart vine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a woody vine characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves and clusters of fragrant, white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces small, orange-red fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for climbing structures and as a ground cover in gardens.
