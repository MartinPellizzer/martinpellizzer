Thymus pulvinatus var. perinicus

Thymus pulvinatus var. perinicus, commonly known as the Perin's thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically gray-green in color, covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pink to purple flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its compact size and fragrant foliage make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and medicinal gardens.
