Tilia apiculata

Tilia apiculata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved linden, is a species of flowering tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Tiliaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with a pointed tip. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, typically blooming in early summer. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its fragrant flowers and shade.
