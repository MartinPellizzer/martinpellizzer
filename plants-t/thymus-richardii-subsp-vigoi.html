Thymus richardii subsp. vigoi

Thymus richardii subsp.

vigoi, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage with small, needle-like leaves that are gray-green in color.

The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Its essential oils contain thymol and carvacrol, which contribute to its medicinal and aromatic properties.
