Thymus broussonetii var. hannonis

Thymus broussonetii var. hannonis, commonly known as Hannon's thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically gray-green in color, covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pink to purple flowers during the spring and summer months. It is often found in rocky or dry soils, thriving in sunny, well-drained environments.

Its essential oils, rich in thymol and carvacrol, make it valuable for both culinary and medicinal purposes.
