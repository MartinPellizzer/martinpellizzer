Trachyrhachis quasimodo

Trachyrhachis quasimodo, commonly known as the "Crying Dragon Fern," is a rare and unique fern species characterized by its striking, elongated fronds that resemble the flowing hair of a mythical creature.

It is native to the humid, shaded environments of the Appalachian Mountains, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soil. The plant's leaves are adorned with intricate, delicate veins that give it a distinctive, almost otherworldly appearance. Trachyrhachis quasimodo reproduces via spores, which are released from specialized structures called sporangia located on the underside of its fronds.

Due to its limited habitat and fragile nature, this fern is considered a conservation concern in its native range.
