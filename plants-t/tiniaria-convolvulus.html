Tiniaria convolvulus

Tiniaria convolvulus, commonly known as the small morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features slender, trailing stems that produce small, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, or white. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Tiniaria convolvulus is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed soils.
