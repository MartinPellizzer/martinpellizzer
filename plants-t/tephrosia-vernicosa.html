Tephrosia vernicosa

Tephrosia vernicosa, commonly known as the velvet tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its roots contain compounds with potential medicinal properties, including insecticidal and antimicrobial effects.

Tephrosia vernicosa is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
