Thalictrum frigidum

Thalictrum frigidum, commonly known as the Arctic meadow rue, is a perennial herb native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and compound leaves with feather-like divisions. The plant produces small, pale green to yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to cold climates.

Thalictrum frigidum plays a role in stabilizing Arctic ecosystems and is often found in tundra and alpine habitats.
