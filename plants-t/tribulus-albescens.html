Tribulus albescens

Tribulus albescens, commonly known as white goat's head, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that grows up to 1 meter tall, characterized by its hairy stems and small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny, white seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional herbal medicine.
