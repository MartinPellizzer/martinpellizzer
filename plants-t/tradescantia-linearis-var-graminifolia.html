Tradescantia linearis var. graminifolia

Tradescantia linearis var. graminifolia, commonly known as the snake plant or mother-in-law's tongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its long, narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically variegated with silver and green patterns. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers on tall, slender stems, which are usually followed by red or purple berries.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and air-purifying qualities.
