Tournefortia caracasana

Tournefortia caracasana, commonly known as the Caracas tamarind, is a shrub native to the Caribbean region.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces woody capsules that contain numerous small, hard seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.
