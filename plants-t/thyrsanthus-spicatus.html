Thyrsanthus spicatus

Thyrsanthus spicatus, commonly known as the spicate thyrsanthus, is a shrub native to the temperate regions of South America.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along its stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, arranged in thyrses, which are dense, compact inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
