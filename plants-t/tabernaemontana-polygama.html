Tabernaemontana polygama

Tabernaemontana polygama, commonly known as the Madagascar star jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its fragrant, star-shaped white flowers that bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant has opposite, simple, and usually ovate to elliptic leaves that are glossy and dark green. It prefers warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance.

Its flowers are primarily pollinated by nocturnal insects such as moths and bats.
