Tabernaemontana congestiflora

Tabernaemontana congestiflora, commonly known as the clustered star of Bethlehem, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, oval berries that turn from green to red as they mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
