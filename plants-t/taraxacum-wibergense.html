Taraxacum wibergense

Taraxacum wibergense, commonly known as the Wiberg's dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of stiff, hairy leaves and a hollow, leafless stem that supports a single flower head. The plant produces a yellow flower that develops into a white, fluffy seed head, typical of the dandelion genus. It is native to Central and Eastern Europe and thrives in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

Taraxacum wibergense is often found in moist to moderately dry environments and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
