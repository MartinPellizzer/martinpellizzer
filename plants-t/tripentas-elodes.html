Tripentas elodes

Tripentas elodes, commonly known as waterweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments.

It has long, slender, branching stems that are often coated in a fine layer of mucus. Its small, dark green leaves are typically arranged in whorls around the stem, and they are often covered in tiny, hair-like structures. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually submerged, though they can occasionally emerge near the water's surface.

Tripentas elodes plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen to aquatic life.
