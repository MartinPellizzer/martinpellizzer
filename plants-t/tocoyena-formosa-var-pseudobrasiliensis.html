Tocoyena formosa var. pseudobrasiliensis

Tocoyena formosa var. pseudobrasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian wax plant, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a shrub characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and white. It thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
