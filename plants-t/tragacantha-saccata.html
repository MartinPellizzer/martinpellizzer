Tragacantha saccata

Tragacantha saccata, commonly known as the bladder sage, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and spikes of purple to blue flowers. The plant produces a sticky resin, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Tragacantha saccata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
