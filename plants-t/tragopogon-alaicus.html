Tragopogon alaicus

Tragopogon alaicus, commonly known as the Alaskan goat's beard, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Alaska and northern Canada.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its yellow flower heads and feathery leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine soils, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its flowers bloom in late summer, producing a distinctive shape that resembles a goat's beard, hence its common name.

Tragopogon alaicus is often found in open habitats such as tundra and meadows, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
