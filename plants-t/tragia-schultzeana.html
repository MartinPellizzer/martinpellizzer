Tragia schultzeana

Tragia schultzeana, commonly known as the Schultze's tragia, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of South America.

It is a shrub or subshrub characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It is adapted to survive in dry environments with low nutrient soils.

Tragia schultzeana is often found in rocky or sandy habitats and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
