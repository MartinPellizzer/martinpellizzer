Taraxacum otayae

Taraxacum otayae, commonly known as the Otay dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of Southern California.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in coastal habitats. It produces yellow flowers that develop into white, fluffy seeds, typical of the dandelion genus.

Due to its limited range, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
