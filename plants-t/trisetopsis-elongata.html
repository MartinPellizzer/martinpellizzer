Trisetopsis elongata

Trisetopsis elongata, commonly known as the slender feather grass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and fine, needle-like leaves that are typically less than 1 inch in width. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in late summer, which are arranged in panicles. Trisetopsis elongata thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

Its delicate appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in arid and semi-arid regions.
