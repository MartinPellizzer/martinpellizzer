Tanacetum parthenium var. praealtum

Tanacetum parthenium var. praealtum, commonly known as feverfew, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, yellowish-white flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant produces a distinctive, aromatic compound called parthenin, which is responsible for its medicinal properties. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and medicinal cultivation.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both traditional and modern herbal medicine.
