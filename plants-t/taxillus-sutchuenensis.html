Taxillus sutchuenensis

Taxillus sutchuenensis, commonly known as the Chinese parasitic vine, is a hemiparasitic plant that relies on host trees for water and nutrients.

It belongs to the family Loranthaceae and is native to China, particularly in the Sichuan province. The plant has small, scale-like leaves and produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring. Its seeds are dispersed by birds, which consume the fleshy berries.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can significantly affect the growth and survival of its host plants.
