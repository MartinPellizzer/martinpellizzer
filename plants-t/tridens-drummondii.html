Tridens drummondii

Tridens drummondii, commonly known as Drummond's tridens, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers that bloom in the spring and summer months.

Tridens drummondii is often found in disturbed areas and is adapted to thrive in arid environments with minimal water availability.
