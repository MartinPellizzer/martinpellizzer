Thamnochortus insignis

Thamnochortus insignis, commonly known as the Cape snowbush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It produces small, hard seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its survival in harsh conditions.

Thamnochortus insignis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
