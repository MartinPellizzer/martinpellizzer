Thymus degenii

Thymus degenii, commonly known as the Armenian thyme, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings. Its aromatic properties make it valuable in both culinary and medicinal applications.

Thymus degenii is also noted for its resilience to harsh climates and its ability to attract pollinators.
