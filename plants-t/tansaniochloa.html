Tansaniochloa

Tansaniochloa, commonly known as the African grass or tansania grass, is a genus of grasses native to East Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and savannas. Tansaniochloa species are often used for forage by livestock due to their high nutritional value.

Botanically, they are classified under the Poaceae family, which includes many other economically important grasses.
