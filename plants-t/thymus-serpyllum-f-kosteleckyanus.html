Thymus serpyllum f. kosteleckyanus

Thymus serpyllum f. kosteleckyanus, commonly known as the Kostelecky's thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy flowers. The plant typically has small, gray-green leaves and produces pink or purple flowers in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and mild, thyme-like fragrance.
