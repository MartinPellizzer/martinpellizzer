Trillium nutans

Trillium nutans, commonly known as the nodding trillium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the family Melanthiaceae and is characterized by its three elliptical leaves and a single, downward-facing flower. The flower consists of three white petals and three sepals, with six stamens and a single pistil. Trillium nutans typically grows in shaded, moist woodlands and thrives in rich, well-drained soils.

It is a slow-growing plant that blooms in early spring, often before its leaves fully emerge.
