Trifolium incarnatum var. noisettii

Trifolium incarnatum var. noisettii, commonly known as the red clover variant noisettii, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets with a distinctive red or purple coloration on the underside. The plant produces small, pink to white flowers arranged in dense, globular clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
