Trisetum transcaucasicum

Trisetum transcaucasicum, commonly known as the Transcaucasian hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically green to brown in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Trisetum transcaucasicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
