Taraxacum vansoestii

Taraxacum vansoestii, commonly known as the Van Soest's dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves and a yellow flowering head that develops into a distinctive white pappus. Native to the western United States, particularly California, it thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant is perennial, with a taproot system that allows it to survive in arid conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of study in plant ecology and taxonomy.
