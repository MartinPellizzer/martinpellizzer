Tithymalus confertus

Tithymalus confertus, commonly known as the clustered tithymalus, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of white and yellow. Native to southern Africa, this species thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its foliage is typically narrow and grass-like, with a dense arrangement that gives it a bushy appearance.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
