Telis persica

Telis persica, commonly known as Persian rose, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its showy, single or double pink to white flowers. The plant has a shrub-like growth habit with thorny stems and dark green, ovate leaves. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Telis persica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its fragrance and visual appeal.
