Teucrium rotundifolium var. sanguisorbifolium

Teucrium rotundifolium var. sanguisorbifolium, commonly known as the scarlet germander, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces showy, pink to red flowers. The leaves are typically rounded and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable plant for xeriscaping.

This variety is often found in rocky or sandy habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments.
