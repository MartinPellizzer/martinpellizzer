Terniola lawii

Terniola lawii, commonly known as the Malabar sandalwood, is a small evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its fragrant, aromatic wood that is highly valued in traditional medicine and perfumery. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, with a glossy green surface.

The flowers are small, white or pale pink, and are borne in clusters, attracting various pollinators.
