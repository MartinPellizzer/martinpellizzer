Tafalla brasiliensis

Tafalla brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian tafalla, is a rare flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments within dense forest understories. Its flowering period occurs during the late spring and early summer months.

Due to habitat loss, Tafalla brasiliensis is considered endangered and is protected under Brazilian conservation laws.
