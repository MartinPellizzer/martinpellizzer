Tetramolopium capillare

Tetramolopium capillare, commonly known as the slenderleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its small, pale yellow flowers bloom in clusters, and the leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs.

It is often used in native plant gardens due to its drought tolerance and ecological value.
