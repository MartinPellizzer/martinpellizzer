Torreya nucifera var. grandis

Torreya nucifera var. grandis, commonly known as the Chinese nutmeg tree, is a species native to eastern Asia, particularly China and Vietnam.

It is a large evergreen tree that can grow up to 30 meters tall, characterized by its glossy, elongated leaves and aromatic, cone-like fruits. The plant belongs to the family Taxaceae and is known for its unique, edible seeds that are used in traditional medicine and cuisine.

Its botanical features include a distinctive bark with a reddish hue and a long, pendulous fruiting structure that matures over several years.
