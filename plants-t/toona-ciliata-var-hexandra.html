Toona ciliata var. hexandra

Toona ciliata var. hexandra, commonly known as the giant fan palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, followed by round, fleshy fruits containing numerous seeds. Its trunk is smooth and often exhibits a fibrous sheath at the base.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber.
