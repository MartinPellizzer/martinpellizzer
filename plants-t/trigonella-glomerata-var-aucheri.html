Trigonella glomerata var. aucheri

Trigonella glomerata var. aucheri, commonly known as the Armenian chickpea, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Turkey.

It is a legume belonging to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-pink flowers. The plant produces small, spherical pods that contain several seeds, which are often used as a traditional food source. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate climates, making it a hardy and resilient species.

Its botanical features make it valuable both for its agricultural potential and its ecological role in nitrogen fixation.
