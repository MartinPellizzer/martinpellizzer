Thymus serpyllum var. ovatus

Thymus serpyllum var. ovatus, commonly known as narrowleaf thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, fragrant, oval-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces tiny white or pinkish flowers in clusters, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic leaves are often used in cooking and traditional herbal remedies.
