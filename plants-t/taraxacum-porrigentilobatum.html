Taraxacum porrigentilobatum

Taraxacum porrigentilobatum, commonly known as the broad-lobed dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of spoon-shaped, hairy leaves and a long, hollow stem that supports a single, yellow flowering head. The plant produces a distinctive, parachute-like seed head composed of numerous lightweight seeds with feathery appendages. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

This species is known for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
