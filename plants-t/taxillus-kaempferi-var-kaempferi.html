Taxillus kaempferi var. kaempferi

Taxillus kaempferi var. kaempferi, commonly known as the mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of host trees, primarily in the genus Eucalyptus.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using light absorbed by its leaves. The plant has small, scale-like leaves and produces white or pale green berries that contain sticky seeds. It plays a role in ecosystems by providing food for birds and contributing to the decomposition of host trees.

Its parasitic nature and ornamental appearance make it both ecologically significant and sometimes considered a pest in forestry.
