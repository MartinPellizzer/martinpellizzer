Thalictrum × flexuosum var. glaucescens

Thalictrum × flexuosum var. glaucescens, commonly known as the blue false spirea, is a hybrid plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its slender, arching stems and clusters of small, showy white or pale blue flowers. The foliage is typically finely cut and has a delicate, feathery appearance, adding a subtle texture to the landscape. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a versatile choice for woodland gardens.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments.
