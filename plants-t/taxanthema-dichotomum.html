Taxanthema dichotomum

Taxanthema dichotomum, commonly known as the dichotomous taxanthemum, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its small, linear leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, with deep roots and a slow growth rate. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds.

Taxanthema dichotomum is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations and ecological role in its native habitat.
