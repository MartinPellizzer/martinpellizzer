Trianosperma gracillimum

Trianosperma gracillimum, commonly known as the slender glasswort, is a halophytic plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It thrives in saline environments, such as salt marshes and tidal flats, due to its specialized adaptations to high salinity. The plant has succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that store water, aiding in survival in arid conditions. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are typically greenish-white, and it reproduces through seeds dispersed by wind.

Trianosperma gracillimum plays a role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
