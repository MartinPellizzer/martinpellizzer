Trifolium resupinatum var. majus

Trifolium resupinatum var. majus, commonly known as the greater red clover, is a flowering plant in the legume family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets, and its pink to purple flower heads. This variety is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in grassy areas and along roadsides. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in agricultural settings.

It blooms in early spring and is often used as a forage crop for livestock.
