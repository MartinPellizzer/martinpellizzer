Thymus longiflorus subsp. moroderi

Thymus longiflorus subsp.

moroderi, commonly known as the Moroder's thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage of gray-green, aromatic leaves.

The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers in clusters, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its essential oils contain thymol and carvacrol, contributing to its use in herbal medicine and aromatherapy.
