Trifolium macilentum

Trifolium macilentum, commonly known as the slender clover, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides.

Trifolium macilentum is a member of the legume family and has the ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
