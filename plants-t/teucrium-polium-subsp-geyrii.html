Teucrium polium subsp. geyrii

Teucrium polium subsp.

geyrii, commonly known as the Geyrii germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions.

The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often grayish-green in color, covered in fine hairs. Its small, tubular flowers are usually blue to purple, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
