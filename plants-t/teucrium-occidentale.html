Teucrium occidentale

Teucrium occidentale, commonly known as western germander, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. The plant features slender, upright stems and small, aromatic leaves with a slightly minty scent. Its flowers are usually blue to purple and bloom in the summer months.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
