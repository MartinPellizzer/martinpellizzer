Thalictrum canadense var. hebecarpum

Thalictrum canadense var. hebecarpum, commonly known as Canada meadow-rue, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces showy, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are clustered in upright cymes. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
