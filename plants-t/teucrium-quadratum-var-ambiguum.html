Teucrium quadratum var. ambiguum

Teucrium quadratum var. ambiguum, commonly known as the ambiguous woodruff, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its small, white or pale blue flowers appear in terminal clusters during the summer months.

The leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.
