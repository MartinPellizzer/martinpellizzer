Thymbra spicata subsp. spicata

Thymbra spicata subsp.

spicata, commonly known as spike thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and as a culinary herb.

Its essential oils contain compounds like thymol, which contribute to its medicinal and aromatic properties.
