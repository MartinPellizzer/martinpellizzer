Tapinanthus celtidifolius

Tapinanthus celtidifolius, commonly known as the parasitic plant or the ghost vine, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches itself to the roots of other plants to derive some of its nutrients.

It belongs to the Orobanchaceae family and is characterized by its lack of true leaves, relying instead on its green stems for photosynthesis. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and has a short life cycle, often completing its growth within a single growing season. It is found in various parts of Africa and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its parasitic nature makes it an interesting subject for ecological and botanical studies.
