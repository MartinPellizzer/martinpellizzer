Taraxacum pindicum

Taraxacum pindicum, commonly known as the Pindus dandelion, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated, hairy leaves. The plant produces a yellow flower head that develops into a distinctive seed head with white, feathery seeds. It thrives in rocky and calcareous soils, often found in mountainous regions.

Taraxacum pindicum is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions, including drought and poor soil nutrients.
