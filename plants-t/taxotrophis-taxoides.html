Taxotrophis taxoides

Taxotrophis taxoides, commonly known as the golden star vine, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant's leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly waxy texture. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
