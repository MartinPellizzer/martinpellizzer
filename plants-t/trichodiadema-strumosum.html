Trichodiadema strumosum

Trichodiadema strumosum, commonly known as the rock rose, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded growth habit and dense clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant has fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Trichodiadema strumosum is also known for its ability to store water in its leaves, allowing it to survive in arid conditions.
