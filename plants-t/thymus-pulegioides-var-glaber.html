Thymus pulegioides var. glaber

Thymus pulegioides var. glaber, commonly known as narrow-leaved thyme, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves and small pink or white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover. Its foliage is often used in culinary applications, adding a fragrant, mint-like flavor to dishes.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
