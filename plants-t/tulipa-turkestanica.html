Tulipa turkestanica

Tulipa turkestanica, commonly known as the Turkestan tulip, is a perennial bulbous plant native to Central Asia.

It produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, often with a distinctive black eye in the center. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, preferring colder climates for optimal growth. Its bulbs are adapted to survive harsh winters, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

The Turkestan tulip is also valued for its resilience and ability to naturalize in suitable environments.
