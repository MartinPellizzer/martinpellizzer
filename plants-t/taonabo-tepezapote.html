Taonabo tepezapote

Taonabo tepezapote, commonly known as the Mexican soursop or *Annona muricata*, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Americas.

It produces a large, spiky fruit with a sweet, fragrant flesh that is often used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant has a shallow root system and thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, while its flowers are fragrant and white with a greenish tint.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to provide shade in tropical landscapes.
