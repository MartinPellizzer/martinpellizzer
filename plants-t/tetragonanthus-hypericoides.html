Tetragonanthus hypericoides

Tetragonanthus hypericoides, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, lanceolate leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which bloom in late summer and early fall. Its growth habit is low and sprawling, often forming dense mats on moist, well-drained soils.

The botanical characteristics of Tetragonanthus hypericoides make it a valuable species for wetland restoration and ecological landscaping.
