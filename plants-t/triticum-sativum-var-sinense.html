Triticum sativum var. sinense

Triticum sativum var. sinense, commonly known as Chinese spring wheat, is a botanical variant of common wheat characterized by its shorter stature and earlier maturation compared to standard wheat varieties.

It possesses a compact growth habit with reduced tillering, making it suitable for cultivation in regions with shorter growing seasons. The plant typically exhibits a range of spikelet structures and glume characteristics that distinguish it from other wheat species. Its genetic makeup includes traits that contribute to its adaptability to diverse environmental conditions.

This variety is of interest in agricultural research for its potential in improving wheat yield and resilience in temperate climates.
