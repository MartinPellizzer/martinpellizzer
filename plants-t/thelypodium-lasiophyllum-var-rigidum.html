Thelypodium lasiophyllum var. rigidum

Thelypodium lasiophyllum var. rigidum, commonly known as rigid thelypodium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by gardeners interested in native flora.
