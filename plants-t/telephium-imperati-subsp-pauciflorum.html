Telephium imperati subsp. pauciflorum

Telephium imperati subsp.

pauciflorum, commonly known as the lesser common tansy, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe. It features basal rosettes of oval, gray-green leaves with a fuzzy texture.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its ornamental value is appreciated in gardens for its delicate appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
