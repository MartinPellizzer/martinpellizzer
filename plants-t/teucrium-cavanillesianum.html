Teucrium cavanillesianum

Teucrium cavanillesianum, commonly known as the Mexican germander, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs.

Teucrium cavanillesianum is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
