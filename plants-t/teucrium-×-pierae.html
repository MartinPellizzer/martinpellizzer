Teucrium × pierae

Teucrium × pierae, commonly known as the hybrid germander, is a perennial plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is a cross between Teucrium chamaedrys and Teucrium polium, resulting in a plant with distinctive silver-gray foliage and small, tubular flowers. The leaves are typically aromatic and covered in fine hairs, which contribute to its medicinal properties. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
