Tium lineare

Tium lineare, commonly known as the linear tium, is a flowering plant native to specific regions in South America.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and clusters of small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it blooms during the spring and early summer.

Tium lineare is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
