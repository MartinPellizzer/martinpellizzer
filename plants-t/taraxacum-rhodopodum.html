Taraxacum rhodopodum

Taraxacum rhodopodum, commonly known as the Rhodope dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of southeastern Europe, particularly in the Rhodope Mountains. The plant is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy, spoon-shaped leaves. It thrives in rocky and dry habitats, often growing in open grasslands and along rocky slopes.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
