Tinea cylindrica

Tinea cylindrica, commonly known as the cylindrical webworm, is a species of moth belonging to the family Tineidae.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical body and pale, often grayish wings. The larvae of this species feed on a variety of plants, including grasses and shrubs, contributing to the plant's ecological role in nutrient cycling. Its life cycle involves several stages, with the adult moths being nocturnal and attracted to light.

The plant's interaction with Tinea cylindrica highlights the complex relationships within forest and grassland ecosystems.
