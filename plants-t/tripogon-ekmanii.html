Tripogon ekmanii

Tripogon ekmanii, commonly known as the slender spikegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green hue.

Tripogon ekmanii plays a minor role in local ecosystems but is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptability.
