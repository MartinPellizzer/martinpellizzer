Taccarum variabile subvar. elatum

Taccarum variabile subvar. elatum, commonly known as the tall taccarum, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. Its flowers are small and clustered in terminal panicles, typically displaying shades of white or pale pink.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its graceful appearance.
