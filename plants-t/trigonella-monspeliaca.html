Trigonella monspeliaca

Trigonella monspeliaca, commonly known as the Persian clover, is an annual legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It features trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant is characterized by its slender, branching stems and is often found in dry, sandy soils. It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is valued for its nitrogen-fixing abilities.

Trigonella monspeliaca is also used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
