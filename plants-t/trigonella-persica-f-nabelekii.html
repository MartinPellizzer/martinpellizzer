Trigonella persica f. nabelekii

Trigonella persica f. nabelekii, commonly known as the Persian trigonella, is a rare and lesser-known botanical variant of the trigonella genus.

It is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer. Native to arid and semi-arid regions of Persia, this plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and potential medicinal properties, though further research is needed to fully understand its applications.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical and ecological studies.
