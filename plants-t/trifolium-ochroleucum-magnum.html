Trifolium ochroleucum-magnum

Trifolium ochroleucum-magnum, commonly known as the large white clover, is a flowering plant in the legume family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets with a white or pale yellow coloration. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are typically arranged in spherical or cylindrical heads. It is a perennial herb that thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils.

Trifolium ochroleucum-magnum is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in agricultural and ecological contexts.
