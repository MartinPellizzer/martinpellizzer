Thymus × jacksonii

Thymus × jacksonii, commonly known as Jackson's thyme, is a hybrid perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, creeping stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental value make it both a useful culinary herb and a decorative plant in horticulture.
