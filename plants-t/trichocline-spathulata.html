Trichocline spathulata

Trichocline spathulata, commonly known as the broad-leaf trichocline, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, spatulate leaves and yellow ray florets that surround tubular disk flowers. Native to South America, this plant thrives in moist, shady environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its growth habit is typically herbaceous, with a low, spreading form that produces small, daisy-like blooms.

The plant's botanical features make it a valuable addition to ecological gardens and wetland restoration projects.
