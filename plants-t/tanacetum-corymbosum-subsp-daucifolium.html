Tanacetum corymbosum subsp. daucifolium

Tanacetum corymbosum subsp.

daucifolium, commonly known as the purple tansy, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America. It features aromatic, yellowish-green leaves that are lance-shaped and often have a fuzzy texture.

The plant produces small, yellow flower heads that are typically clustered in flat-topped umbels. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and traditional herbal use.
