Thlaspi cikaeum

Thlaspi cikaeum, commonly known as the Armenian cabbages, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, often found in mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia. The plant features basal rosettes of leaves and produces small, yellow flowers in early spring. It has a compact growth habit and is adapted to harsh, cold climates.

Thlaspi cikaeum is also notable for its ability to accumulate heavy metals, making it a potential indicator species for soil contamination.
