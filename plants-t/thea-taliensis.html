Thea taliensis

Thea taliensis, commonly known as the Taliensis tea plant, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small white flowers. The plant is primarily cultivated for its leaves, which are used to produce a unique type of tea. Its botanical features include a woody stem, opposite leaf arrangement, and a preference for tropical climates.

Thea taliensis is relatively rare and is often found in shaded, humid environments.
