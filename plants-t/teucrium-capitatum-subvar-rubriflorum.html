Teucrium capitatum subvar. rubriflorum

Teucrium capitatum subvar. rubriflorum, commonly known as the redflower germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branched stems covered in soft, grayish-green foliage. The plant produces dense clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to deep red, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
