Thalassia vitrariorum

Thalassia vitrariorum, commonly known as shoal grass, is a marine flowering plant belonging to the family Poaceae.

It is a seagrass species that thrives in shallow, coastal waters, often forming dense underwater meadows. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are typically green and blade-like, adapted for efficient photosynthesis in aquatic environments. Thalassia vitrariorum plays a crucial role in coastal ecosystems by supporting biodiversity and maintaining water quality.
