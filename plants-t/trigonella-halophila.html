Trigonella halophila

Trigonella halophila, commonly known as saltbush, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a perennial shrub that thrives in saline and arid environments, showcasing its halophytic nature. The plant features small, trifoliate leaves that help reduce water loss, and its flowers are typically pale yellow or greenish-white. Trigonella halophila produces small, lens-shaped pods that contain several seeds.

This plant is well adapted to harsh conditions, making it an important component of coastal and desert ecosystems.
