Themistoclesia orientalis

Themistoclesia orientalis, commonly known as the Chinese yew, is a slow-growing evergreen shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, needle-like leaves that are arranged in spirals around the stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers in the spring, which are followed by red berries that contain the seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in horticulture.
