Terminalia balansae

Terminalia balansae, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, ovate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds. Its wood is valued for its durability and fine grain, making it useful in traditional crafts and construction.

The plant also has traditional medicinal uses in various African communities.
