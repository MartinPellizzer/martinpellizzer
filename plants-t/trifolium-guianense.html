Trifolium guianense

Trifolium guianense, commonly known as the Guianan clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features trifoliate leaves, each composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Trifolium guianense is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential ecological benefits in soil enrichment.
