Thymus chamaedrys subsp. montanus

Thymus chamaedrys subsp.

montanus, commonly known as mountain thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, thriving in well-drained, sunny environments.

The plant produces small, fragrant, pink or white flowers during the spring and summer seasons. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, which contribute to its aromatic properties.

This subspecies is valued for its medicinal and culinary uses, similar to other thyme species.
