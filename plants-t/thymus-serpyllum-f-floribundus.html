Thymus serpyllum f. floribundus

Thymus serpyllum f. floribundus, commonly known as creeping thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe.

It features small, fragrant leaves that are typically gray-green in color, and it produces clusters of pink or white flowers in the spring. The plant is well adapted to rocky and dry soils, making it a hardy ground cover. Its aromatic foliage makes it popular in gardens and for culinary use.

It is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
