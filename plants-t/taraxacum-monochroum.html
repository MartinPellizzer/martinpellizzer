Taraxacum monochroum

Taraxacum monochroum, commonly known as the single-chrome dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and a yellow flower head that develops into a white, fluffy seed head. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia and is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides. It has a taproot system that allows it to survive in various soil types and environmental conditions.

The plant is also known for its ability to produce a milky sap when the leaves are damaged.
