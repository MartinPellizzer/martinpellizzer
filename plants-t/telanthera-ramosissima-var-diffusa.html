Telanthera ramosissima var. diffusa

Telanthera ramosissima var. diffusa, commonly known as the spreading telanthera, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its sprawling growth habit and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are often branched and woody. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
