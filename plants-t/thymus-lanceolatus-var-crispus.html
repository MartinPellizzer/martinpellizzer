Thymus lanceolatus var. crispus

Thymus lanceolatus var. crispus, commonly known as the crinkled thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are slightly crinkled, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces pink to purple flowers in early summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its compact growth habit and fragrant foliage make it a popular choice for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
