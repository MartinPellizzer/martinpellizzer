Thymus drucei f. britannicus

Thymus drucei f. britannicus, commonly known as the British thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the British Isles.

It features aromatic, narrow leaves that are typically gray-green in color, with a distinctive, fragrant scent when crushed. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
