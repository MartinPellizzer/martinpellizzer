Trophis racemosa subsp. meridionalis

Trophis racemosa subsp.

meridionalis, commonly known as the southern broom, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, often found in coastal and inland regions.

The plant produces dense clusters of yellow flowers, which are arranged in racemes, giving it its species name. Its leaves are simple and opposite, often with a slightly hairy texture.

It is adapted to withstand drought conditions and is sometimes used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
