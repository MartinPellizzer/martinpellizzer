Taraxacum ussuriense

Taraxacum ussuriense, commonly known as the Ussuri dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves. The plant produces a yellow flower head that develops into a pappus-capped seed, facilitating wind dispersal. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and traditional herbal use.
