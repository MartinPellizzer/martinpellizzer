Thymus petteri

Thymus petteri, commonly known as the alpine thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its aromatic and medicinal properties.

Thymus petteri thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.
