Trigonella arenicola

Trigonella arenicola, commonly known as the sand vetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and coastal regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant thrives in sandy soils and is often found in dunes or along shorelines. It has a deep root system that helps stabilize sand dunes and prevent erosion.

Trigonella arenicola plays a vital role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
