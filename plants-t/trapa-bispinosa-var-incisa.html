Trapa bispinosa var. incisa

Trapa bispinosa var. incisa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved water chestnut, is a aquatic plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny fruits that float on the water surface. The plant has narrow, serrated leaves that emerge from the water, aiding in photosynthesis. Its flowers are small and yellow, typically appearing in clusters.

This species is often found in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats.
