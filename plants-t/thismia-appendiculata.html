Thismia appendiculata

Thismia appendiculata, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and elusive flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lack of leaves and its unique, elongated inflorescence that bears small, white, orchid-like flowers. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees or other plants without deriving nutrients from them. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is highly sensitive to environmental changes and is considered endangered in many regions.
