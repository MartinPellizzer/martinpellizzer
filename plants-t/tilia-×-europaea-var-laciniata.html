Tilia × europaea var. laciniata

Tilia × europaea var. laciniata, commonly known as the shredded maple, is a hybrid deciduous tree native to Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive, finely cut, palmate leaves that give it a feathery appearance. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which bloom in early summer. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks.
