Triticum vulgare var. mravianii

Triticum vulgare var. mravianii, commonly known as the Mravina wheat, is a variant of common wheat characterized by its distinct morphological features.

It is a grass species belonging to the Poaceae family, native to regions of Central and Eastern Europe. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that bear small, two-lobed florets, typical of wheat species.

This variety is notable for its genetic diversity and has been studied for its potential in agricultural and ecological contexts.
