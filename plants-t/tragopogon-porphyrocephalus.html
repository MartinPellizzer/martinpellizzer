Tragopogon porphyrocephalus

Tragopogon porphyrocephalus, commonly known as the purple-headed thistle, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and striking purple-tinged flower heads that resemble dandelions. The plant produces small, wind-dispersed seeds enclosed in a pappus, aiding in its propagation. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower structure.
