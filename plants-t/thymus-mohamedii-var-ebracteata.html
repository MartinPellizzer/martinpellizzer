Thymus mohamedii var. ebracteata

Thymus mohamedii var. ebracteata, commonly known as the Ebracteate Thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to arid regions of the Middle East.

It features small, fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has slender, woody stems and narrow, gray-green leaves covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its aromatic properties make it a popular choice for culinary and medicinal uses.
