Townsendia alpigena var. caelilinensis

Townsendia alpigena var. caelilinensis, commonly known as the alpine townsendia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy foliage. This perennial herb typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high elevations. Its flowers are usually yellow or white, with a distinctive central disc surrounded by ray florets.

The plant is adapted to cold, alpine environments and is often found in open meadows and slopes.
