Tulipa cinnabarina subsp. cinnabarina

Tulipa cinnabarina subsp.

cinnabarina, commonly known as the cinnabar tulip, is a species native to the mountains of Central Asia. It is characterized by its striking red-orange flowers with a distinctive yellow throat, which bloom in early spring.

The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and has a compact growth habit. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its unique coloration and early blooming period make it a standout in the tulip genus.
