Timonius rumphii

Timonius rumphii, commonly known as the Java orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, showy flowers that often display vibrant colors. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Timonius rumphii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
