Thelypodium wrightii subsp. oklahomensis

Thelypodium wrightii subsp.

oklahomensis, commonly known as the Oklahoma thelypodium, is a perennial herb native to the central United States. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as prairies and woodland edges.

The plant features slender, branching stems that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, clustered in spikes, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

It has lance-shaped leaves that are usually arranged alternately along the stem.
