Teucrium montanum subsp. montanum

Teucrium montanum subsp.

montanum, commonly known as mountain germander, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, tubular flowers are usually purple or blue and bloom in summer.

It is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
