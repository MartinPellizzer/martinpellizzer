Tecoma sanguinea

Tecoma sanguinea, commonly known as the yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow, tubular flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough texture.

Tecoma sanguinea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
