Troglophyton acocksianum

Troglophyton acocksianum, commonly known as the Acock's cave plant, is a unique succulent native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-forming structure and thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant typically grows in rocky crevices or underground, adapting to low light conditions and minimal rainfall. Its flowers are small and usually pale in color, appearing during brief periods of favorable weather.

Due to its specialized habitat and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
