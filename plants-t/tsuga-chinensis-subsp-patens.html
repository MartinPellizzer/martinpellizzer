Tsuga chinensis subsp. patens

Tsuga chinensis subsp.

patens, commonly known as the Chinese hemlock, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to eastern Asia. It belongs to the family Pinaceae and is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and conical shape.

The plant exhibits a slow growth rate and is often found in cool, moist mountainous regions. Its bark is typically reddish-brown and becomes fissured with age.

The cones are small and woody, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind.
