Tetraglochin acanthocarpa var. lasiocarpa

Tetraglochin acanthocarpa var. lasiocarpa, commonly known as the woolly bladderpod, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its glandular, hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, rough texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typical of the Brassicaceae family. Its fruits are dry, dehiscent pods that release seeds upon maturity.

It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in sagebrush communities.
