Thacla

Thacla, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a distinctive golden hue. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Thacla thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
