Triticum boeoticum var. viridialbinigrescens

Triticum boeoticum var. viridialbinigrescens, commonly known as the green and bluish-gray wheat, is a lesser-known wild wheat species characterized by its unique leaf and glume coloration.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring slender stems and elongated, narrow leaves. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often tinged with green and bluish-gray hues, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Due to its genetic diversity, it is of interest to researchers studying ancient wheat varieties and their potential agricultural applications.
