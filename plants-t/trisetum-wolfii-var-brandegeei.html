Trisetum wolfii var. brandegeei

Trisetum wolfii var. brandegeei, commonly known as the Brandegee's bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and slender, tufted growth habit. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for dryland environments. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of spikelets, which are often tinged with purple or blue hues.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in arid landscapes.
