Trachylobium mossambicense

Trachylobium mossambicense, commonly known as the Natal ironwood, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemes, followed by legume-like pods containing seeds. The species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat.
