Thymus lanulosus

Thymus lanulosus, commonly known as woolly thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, aromatic leaves covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces tiny, pinkish to purple flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its compact growth habit and fragrant foliage make it popular in rock gardens and as a ground cover.
