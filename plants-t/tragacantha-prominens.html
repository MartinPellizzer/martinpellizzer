Tragacantha prominens

Tragacantha prominens, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Iran and Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, branched stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by elongated pods containing seeds. It is valued for its resin, which has been used historically in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive.

Due to its adaptations to harsh environments, it plays an important ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils.
