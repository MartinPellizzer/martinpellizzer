Tetrardisia tetrasepala

Tetrardisia tetrasepala, commonly known as the four-parted orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in clusters. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are linear and often arranged in a basal rosette.

The orchid thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
