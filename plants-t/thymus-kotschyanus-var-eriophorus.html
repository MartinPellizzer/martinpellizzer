Thymus kotschyanus var. eriophorus

Thymus kotschyanus var. eriophorus, commonly known as the Caucasian thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It features small, aromatic leaves and produces clusters of pink to purple flowers. The plant is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and is often found in rocky or limestone soils. It is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.

Its essential oils contain thymol and carvacrol, contributing to its antimicrobial and antioxidant activities.
