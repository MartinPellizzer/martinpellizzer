Tanacetum globiferum

Tanacetum globiferum, commonly known as globeflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, globe-shaped flower heads composed of numerous small, white to yellowish ray florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats and prefers well-drained soils. It produces a strong, aromatic scent that attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Tanacetum globiferum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature.
