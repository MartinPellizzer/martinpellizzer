Thymus striatus var. orieni

Thymus striatus var. orieni, commonly known as the Persian thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It features aromatic, narrow leaves and small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for Mediterranean climates.

Its essential oils, rich in thymol, are valued for their medicinal and culinary uses.
