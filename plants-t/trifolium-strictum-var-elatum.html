Trifolium strictum var. elatum

Trifolium strictum var. elatum, commonly known as the tall clover, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Trifolium strictum var. elatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
