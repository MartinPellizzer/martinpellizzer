Triticum dicoccum subsp. maroccanum

Triticum dicoccum subsp.

maroccanum, commonly known as Moroccan emmer wheat, is a wild wheat species native to North Africa. It is characterized by its robust, grass-like appearance with narrow, bluish-green leaves and spikelet-bearing inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showing adaptability to harsh climatic conditions. Its grains are smaller and harder than those of modern wheat varieties, with a high protein content.

This subspecies is of interest to researchers for its potential in developing drought-resistant and nutrient-rich cereal crops.
