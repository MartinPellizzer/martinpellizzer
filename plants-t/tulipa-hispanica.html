Tulipa hispanica

Tulipa hispanica, commonly known as the Spanish tulip, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of red, yellow, and orange, often with a distinctive black eye. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders. Its bulbs are adapted to survive dry periods, and it blooms in early spring.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in horticulture.
