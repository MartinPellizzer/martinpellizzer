Triodia melicoides

Triodia melicoides, commonly known as the spinifex grass, is a perennial grass native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, wiry growth habit and its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant has small, needle-like leaves that are covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss. It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various desert organisms.

Triodia melicoides is well adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and limited rainfall.
