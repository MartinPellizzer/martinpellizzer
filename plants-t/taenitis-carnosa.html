Taenitis carnosa

Taenitis carnosa, commonly known as the red hibiscus, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from red to pink. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Taenitis carnosa is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms and is used in both ornamental gardening and traditional medicine.
