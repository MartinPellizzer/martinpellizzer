Thomasia

Thomasia, commonly known as the "false pomegranate," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Malvaceae.

It consists of about 40 species, primarily native to southern Africa and Madagascar. The plants are shrubs or small trees with simple, alternate leaves that are often hairy. They produce showy, five-petaled flowers that are typically pink or red, followed by capsule fruits.

Thomasia species are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes cultivated in gardens for their vibrant blooms.
