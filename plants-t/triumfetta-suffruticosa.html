Triumfetta suffruticosa

Triumfetta suffruticosa, commonly known as the red sandmat, is a flowering plant native to Africa, the Middle East, and parts of Asia.

It is a shrub-like perennial that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, pink or red flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually small and opposite, with a slightly hairy texture.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
