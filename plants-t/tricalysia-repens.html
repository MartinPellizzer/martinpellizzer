Tricalysia repens

Tricalysia repens, commonly known as the creeping tricalysia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its trailing stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its botanical features make it suitable for use in ornamental gardening and erosion control.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate to high levels of sunlight.
