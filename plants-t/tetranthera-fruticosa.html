Tetranthera fruticosa

Tetranthera fruticosa, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including India and Southeast Asia.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a leathery texture with a distinct aroma.

The wood of Tetranthera fruticosa is valued for its durability and rich reddish color, making it a sought-after material in traditional crafts and furniture.
