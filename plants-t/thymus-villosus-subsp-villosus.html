Thymus villosus subsp. villosus

Thymus villosus subsp.

villosus, commonly known as hairy thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It features creeping stems covered in dense, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive fuzzy appearance.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its aromatic properties.

This subspecies is often used in herbal medicine and as a culinary herb due to its strong thyme scent and medicinal compounds.
