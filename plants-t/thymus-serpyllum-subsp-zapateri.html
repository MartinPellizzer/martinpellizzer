Thymus serpyllum subsp. zapateri

Thymus serpyllum subsp.

zapateri, commonly known as the Iberian thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its low-growing, creeping habit and small, aromatic leaves that emit a strong thyme scent.

The plant produces pink to purple flowers in dense clusters, typically blooming from spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, sunny soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, as well as its role in ecological landscapes.
