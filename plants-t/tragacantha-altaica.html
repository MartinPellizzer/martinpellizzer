Tragacantha altaica

Tragacantha altaica, commonly known as the Caucasian tragacanth, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its erect stems and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces a sap-like substance called tragacanth, which is used in traditional medicine and as a natural gum. Its leaves are alternate and have a rough texture, while its roots are fibrous and deep-reaching.

Tragacantha altaica thrives in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to temperate climates.
