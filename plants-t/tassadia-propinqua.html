Tassadia propinqua

Tassadia propinqua, commonly known as the delicate tasselflower, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant produces small, bead-like fruits that contain the seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Tassadia propinqua thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to its harsh environment.

Its unique floral structure supports pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
