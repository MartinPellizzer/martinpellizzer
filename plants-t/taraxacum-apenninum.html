Taraxacum apenninum

Taraxacum apenninum, commonly known as the Italian dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are typically lobed and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and produce a fluffy, white seed head that disperses via wind.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and other open areas.
