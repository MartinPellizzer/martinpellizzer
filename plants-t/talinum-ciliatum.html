Talinum ciliatum

Talinum ciliatum, commonly known as the red-rooted malabar spinach, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, fleshy leaves that are typically red or purple in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that grow in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as a leafy vegetable.

Talinum ciliatum is valued for its nutritional content and is used in traditional cuisines across its native range.
