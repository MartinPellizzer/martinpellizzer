Trapa bicornis var. iwasakii

Trapa bicornis var. iwasakii, commonly known as the Japanese water chestnut, is a aquatic plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny, floating fruits that contain edible nuts. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water surface, while its roots anchor it in the muddy substrate. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and a subject of interest in aquatic botany.
