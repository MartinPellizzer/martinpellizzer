Tamarix laxa var. stenopetala

Tamarix laxa var. stenopetala, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tamarisk, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It features small, scale-like leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry climates. The plant produces pink to red flowers in dense clusters during the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in saline and poorly drained soils.

This species is often used for erosion control due to its resilience and ability to stabilize sandy or degraded soils.
