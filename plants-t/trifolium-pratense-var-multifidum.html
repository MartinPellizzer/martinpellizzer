Trifolium pratense var. multifidum

Trifolium pratense var. multifidum, commonly known as the multifid leaf clover, is a variant of red clover characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves with multiple divisions.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family and is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its flowers are pink to purple and appear in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its role in soil nitrogen fixation and as forage for livestock.
