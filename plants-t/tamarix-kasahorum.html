Tamarix kasahorum

Tamarix kasahorum, commonly known as the Central Asian tamarisk, is a shrub or small tree native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and feathery, scale-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, pink or reddish flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and capable of thriving in saline soils.

Tamarix kasahorum is often used for soil stabilization and erosion control in its native habitats.
