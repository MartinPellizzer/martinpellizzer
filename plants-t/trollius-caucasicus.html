Trollius caucasicus

Trollius caucasicus, commonly known as the Caucasian buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Caucasus region.

It features showy, bright yellow, saucer-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a short, thick rhizome and basal leaves that are broadly ovate with a hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
