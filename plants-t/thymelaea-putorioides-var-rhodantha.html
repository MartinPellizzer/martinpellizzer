Thymelaea putorioides var. rhodantha

Thymelaea putorioides var. rhodantha, commonly known as the red thymelaea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 meter, featuring small, aromatic leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The flowers are usually red or orange in color, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the branches. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
