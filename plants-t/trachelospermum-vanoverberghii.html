Trachelospermum vanoverberghii

Trachelospermum vanoverberghii, commonly known as the Vanoverbergh's star jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a vigorous climber with opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces fragrant, star-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, blooming in clusters during the warmer months. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative vine in gardens and landscapes.
