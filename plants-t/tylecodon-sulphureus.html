Tylecodon sulphureus

Tylecodon sulphureus, commonly known as the yellow moonveld aster, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a rosette of fleshy, blue-green leaves that store water, typical of arid environments. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers in the spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring minimal water once established.

Due to its unique appearance and hardiness, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
