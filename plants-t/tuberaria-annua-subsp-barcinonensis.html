Tuberaria annua subsp. barcinonensis

Tuberaria annua subsp.

barcinonensis, commonly known as the Barcino shrub, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is a perennial herb characterized by its compact growth habit and small, clustered flowers.

The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is adapted to rocky and calcareous soils, often found in dry, sunny habitats.
