Trichophorum atrichum

Trichophorum atrichum, commonly known as the black sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dark green, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that develop into triangular, spike-like structures. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments such as wetlands and marshes. It has a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread and form dense mats.

Trichophorum atrichum plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
