Tropilis closterium

Tropilis closterium, commonly known as the common water clover, is a small aquatic plant characterized by its intricate, branching structure that resembles a network of leaves.

It typically grows in shallow, slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and streams. The plant has finely divided, ribbon-like leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern, allowing it to maximize light absorption. Its reproductive structures are small and inconspicuous, with flowers that are usually greenish-white in color.

Tropilis closterium plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
