Typha kamelinii

Typha kamelinii, commonly known as the Kamelin's cattail, is a perennial wetland plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical flower spikes that produce fluffy seeds dispersed by the wind. The plant has broad, sword-shaped leaves that grow from a rhizomatous root system, allowing it to spread vegetatively. It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes and pond edges.

Typha kamelinii plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and stabilizing soil.
