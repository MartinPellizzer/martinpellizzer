Turritis lyrata

Turritis lyrata, commonly known as the lyreleaf sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, lyre-shaped leaves and bright yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it well adapted to arid environments. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance.

Turritis lyrata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
