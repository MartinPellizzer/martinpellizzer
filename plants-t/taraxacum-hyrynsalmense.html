Taraxacum hyrynsalmense

Taraxacum hyrynsalmense, commonly known as the Hyrynsalmense dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated, hairy leaves and a single, hollow, stem that bears a bright yellow flower head. The plant is native to specific regions in Central Asia and is adapted to grow in arid and semi-arid environments. Its flowers produce a wind-dispersed seed head, typical of the genus Taraxacum.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological niche and potential medicinal properties.
