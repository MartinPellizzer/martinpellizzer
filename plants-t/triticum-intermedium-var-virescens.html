Triticum intermedium var. virescens

Triticum intermedium var. virescens, commonly known as intermediate wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences containing numerous florets, which are wind-pollinated. It is valued for its hardiness, adaptability to various soil types, and potential use in forage and conservation agriculture.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and agricultural studies.
