Typha × volgensis

Typha × volgensis, commonly known as the hybrid cattail, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Typha angustifolia and Typha latifolia, characterized by its broad, sword-shaped leaves and a dense, cylindrical flower spike. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, ponds, and along the edges of water bodies. It plays a vital role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly, making it both ecologically significant and sometimes invasive.
