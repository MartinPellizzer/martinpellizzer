Tragia mollis

Tragia mollis, commonly known as the velvetleaf or silverleaf, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Tragia mollis is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and agricultural fields.

It is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and climatic conditions.
