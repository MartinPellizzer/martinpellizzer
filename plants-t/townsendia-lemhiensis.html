Townsendia lemhiensis

Townsendia lemhiensis, commonly known as the Lemhi Townsend's flower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. This perennial plant typically grows in rocky, alpine environments, thriving at high elevations. Its small, showy flowers are usually yellow or white, and it blooms in late summer.

The plant is adapted to harsh conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
