Terpsichore elastica

Terpsichore elastica, commonly known as the elastic danceflower, is a unique plant characterized by its flexible, vine-like stems that can bend and twist with minimal resistance.

Its leaves are elongated and slightly waxy, adapted to retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Terpsichore elastica is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

Due to its unusual elasticity, it has been studied for potential applications in biotechnology and adaptive plant engineering.
