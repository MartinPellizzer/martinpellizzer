Thalictrum abortivum

Thalictrum abortivum, commonly known as the meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a fibrous root system and is often found in moist soils along the edges of wetlands.

Thalictrum abortivum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
