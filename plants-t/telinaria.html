Telinaria

Telinaria, commonly known as the white thornapple, is a genus of flowering plants in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny stems and showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue. The leaves are usually alternate, simple, and often have a rough texture. Telinaria species are native to various regions in Africa and are sometimes cultivated for their ornamental value.

These plants produce small, berry-like fruits that are toxic and should not be consumed.
