Trichilia gigantophylla

Trichilia gigantophylla, commonly known as the giant-leaf trichilia, is a tropical tree native to parts of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers arranged in panicles, which are followed by woody, elongated fruits containing several seeds. Trichilia gigantophylla is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental foliage.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
