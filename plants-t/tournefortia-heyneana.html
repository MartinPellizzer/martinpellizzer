Tournefortia heyneana

Tournefortia heyneana, commonly known as the Indian sandpaper tree, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including India, Sri Lanka, and Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub, typically reaching heights of up to 10 meters. The plant is characterized by its rough, sandpapery bark and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and clustered in inflorescences, often appearing in summer.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its ecological role in various habitats.
