Tecoma alata

Tecoma alata, commonly known as the yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height. The plant features bright yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Tecoma alata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to a variety of soil types and climates.
