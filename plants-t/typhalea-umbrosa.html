Typhalea umbrosa

Typhalea umbrosa, commonly known as the shadow fern, is a species of fern native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that thrive in shaded, humid environments. The plant typically grows in dense forests where it relies on the forest floor's moisture and organic matter for nutrients. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the underside of its leaves, aiding in spore dispersal.

Typhalea umbrosa plays an important ecological role in maintaining the biodiversity of its native habitat.
