Tussilago sagittata

Tussilago sagittata, commonly known as arrowleaf violet, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, arrow-like leaves that are typically hairy and have a distinctive dark green color. The plant produces clusters of small, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Tussilago sagittata is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
