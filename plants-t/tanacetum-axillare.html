Tanacetum axillare

Tanacetum axillare, commonly known as the mountain tansy, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, finely divided leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with similar composite flower structures.

Tanacetum axillare is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.
