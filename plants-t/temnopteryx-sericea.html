Temnopteryx sericea

Temnopteryx sericea, commonly known as the silky tamarisk, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, branching structure and small, scale-like leaves that give it a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red in color, which bloom in the summer months. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to thrive in poor, sandy soils.

Temnopteryx sericea is well adapted to drought conditions and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness.
