Trifolium pratense var. sativum

Trifolium pratense var. sativum, commonly known as red clover, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Western Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and its small, pink or red flowers that bloom in dense clusters. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along roadsides. It is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important crop for agricultural purposes.

Additionally, it has been used in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
