Thalassia antarctica

Thalassia antarctica, commonly known as the Antarctic seagrass, is a flowering plant native to the coastal waters of the Southern Ocean.

It thrives in shallow, temperate to polar waters and forms dense underwater meadows that stabilize sediments and provide habitat for marine life. The plant has long, ribbon-like leaves that can grow up to several meters in length, and it reproduces via underwater flowering and seed dispersal. Thalassia antarctica plays a crucial role in carbon sequestration and coastal ecosystem health.

Its presence is an indicator of relatively undisturbed marine environments.
