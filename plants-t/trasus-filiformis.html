Trasus filiformis

Trasus filiformis, commonly known as the slender lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features slender, thread-like stems that grow close to the ground, often forming dense mats. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers with six petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, arranged in a rosette pattern at the base.

Trasus filiformis is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
