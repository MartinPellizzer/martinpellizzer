Trisetum formosanum

Trisetum formosanum, commonly known as the Taiwan hairgrass, is a perennial grass native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth and fine, silver-green foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and streambanks. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, which are often adorned with long, bristly hairs.

Trisetum formosanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
