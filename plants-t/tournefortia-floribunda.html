Tournefortia floribunda

Tournefortia floribunda, commonly known as the Florida turpentine tree, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows up to 15 feet tall. The plant produces clusters of showy, white or yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a rough texture with a waxy coating.

Tournefortia floribunda is adapted to sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed habitats.
