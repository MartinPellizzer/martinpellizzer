Teucrium krymense

Teucrium krymense, commonly known as Crimean germander, is a perennial herb native to the Crimean Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates. Its small, tubular flowers are usually purple or blue, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Teucrium krymense is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
