Tripterospermum japonicum var. involubile

Tripterospermum japonicum var. involubile, commonly known as the Japanese star vine, is a climbing shrub native to Japan.

It features small, star-shaped white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by orange-red berries in the fall. The plant has opposite, simple, and usually ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and seasonal flowering.
