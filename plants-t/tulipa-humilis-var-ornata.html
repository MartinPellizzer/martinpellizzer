Tulipa humilis var. ornata

Tulipa humilis var. ornata, commonly known as the Persian tulip, is a compact, early-blooming species native to Central Asia.

It produces delicate, cup-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, often with a distinctive orange or red streaked base. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and typically grows to a height of 10-15 cm. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, thriving in cooler climates.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a border plant.
