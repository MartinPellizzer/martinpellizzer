Taraxacum fulvum f. canulum

Taraxacum fulvum f. canulum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the dandelion family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun. It produces a milky sap and has a rosette of basal leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This fern-like form is a distinguishing feature of this particular form of the species.
