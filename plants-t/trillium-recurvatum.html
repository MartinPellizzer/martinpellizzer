Trillium recurvatum

Trillium recurvatum, commonly known as the recurved trillium, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Trillium genus, which is part of the family Melanthiaceae. The plant features three broad, waxy leaves that are typically heart-shaped and have a distinctive reddish or purplish tinge. Its flowers are white with three petals and three sepals, and it produces red berries that contain seeds.

Trillium recurvatum thrives in shaded, moist woodlands and is often found in rich, well-drained soils.
