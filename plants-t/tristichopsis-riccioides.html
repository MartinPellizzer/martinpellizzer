Tristichopsis riccioides

Tristichopsis riccioides, commonly known as the velvetleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Tristichopsis riccioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking foliage and blooms.
