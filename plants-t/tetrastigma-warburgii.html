Tetrastigma warburgii

Tetrastigma warburgii, commonly known as the Vietnamese ivy, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and tendrils that aid in its climbing habit. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its berries are edible and have a slightly tart flavor, often used in local cuisines.

Tetrastigma warburgii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant.
