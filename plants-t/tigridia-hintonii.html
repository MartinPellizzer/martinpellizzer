Tigridia hintonii

Tigridia hintonii, commonly known as Hinton's tiger flower, is a member of the Iridaceae family and is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows to a height of 12 to 24 inches and features narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
