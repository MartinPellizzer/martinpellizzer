Taraxacum officinale subsp. alpinum

Taraxacum officinale subsp.

alpinum, commonly known as the alpine dandelion, is a flowering plant native to alpine regions of Europe and Asia. It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves.

The plant produces a hollow, hairy stem that supports a single, bright yellow flower head. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

The species is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
