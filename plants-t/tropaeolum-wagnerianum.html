Tropaeolum wagnerianum

Tropaeolum wagnerianum, commonly known as Wagner's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Tropaeolaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that typically range in color from red to orange. The plant is a herbaceous annual with trailing stems that often grow along the ground or climb supports. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers open in the morning and close by midday.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its colorful blooms.
