Tulipa edulis var. latifolia

Tulipa edulis var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf edible tulip, is a perennial bulbous plant native to Central Asia.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from pale yellow to orange-red, often with a distinctive black eye. The plant has broad, strap-like leaves that are typically arranged in a fan shape. Its bulbs are edible when cooked, though they contain toxic compounds that require careful preparation.

This variety is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its culinary potential in certain regions.
