Trichilia appendiculata

Trichilia appendiculata, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae, which includes mahogany and related hardwoods. The tree is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy upper surface. The wood is highly valued for its fine grain and durability, making it a significant resource in local economies.
