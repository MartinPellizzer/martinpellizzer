Tropaeolum lobbianum var. cardinale

Tropaeolum lobbianum var. cardinale, commonly known as the Cardinal nasturtium, is a vibrant flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers, which are borne on slender, trailing stems. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its flowers have a peppery scent reminiscent of watercress. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its colorful blooms and edible leaves.
