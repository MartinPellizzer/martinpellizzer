Tragoselinum angelica

Tragoselinum angelica, commonly known as angelica, is a biennial or perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, pinnately compound leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in umbels, which are clustered in flat-topped arrangements. Angelica is known for its strong, spicy scent and is often used in herbal medicine and culinary applications.

Its roots and leaves contain various bioactive compounds, contributing to its medicinal properties.
