Trichophorum dolichocarpum

Trichophorum dolichocarpum, commonly known as the long-fruited sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded habitats, such as marshes and wet meadows. Its roots are fibrous and help stabilize soil in aquatic environments.

Trichophorum dolichocarpum is often found in temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.
