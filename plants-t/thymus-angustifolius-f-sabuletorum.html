Thymus angustifolius f. sabuletorum

Thymus angustifolius f. sabuletorum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features aromatic, narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture. The plant produces small, pinkish to purple flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its foliage is often used in culinary and medicinal applications due to its pungent, aromatic properties.
