Tradescantia filiformis

Tradescantia filiformis, commonly known as snake plant or mother-in-law's tongue, is a tropical flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright, sword-shaped leaves that are typically variegated with green, white, and pink hues. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers on slender stalks during the growing season. It is well-known for its air-purifying qualities and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.

Tradescantia filiformis is also valued for its drought tolerance and ease of care, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
