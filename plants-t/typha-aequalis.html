Typha aequalis

Typha aequalis, commonly known as the narrowleaf cattail, is a wetland plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, blade-like leaves and a dense, cylindrical inflorescence that contains both male and female flowers. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often forming dense stands in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.

The plant plays a vital role in water filtration and provides habitat for various aquatic organisms.
