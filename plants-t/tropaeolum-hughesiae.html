Tropaeolum hughesiae

Tropaeolum hughesiae, commonly known as the scarlet runner mallow, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Tropaeolaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant red flowers and trailing growth habit. The plant produces edible tubers that are similar in appearance to potatoes but have a peppery flavor. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Tropaeolum hughesiae is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking blooms and adaptability to various growing conditions.
