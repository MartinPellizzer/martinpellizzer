Tridax rosea

Tridax rosea, commonly known as the white crownbeard, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers with white ray florets. The plant typically grows in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields, exhibiting rapid growth and spreading through both seeds and rhizomes. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered with fine hairs, while its stems are slender and often branching.

Tridax rosea is known for its ability to thrive in various soil types and is sometimes considered a weed due to its invasive tendencies.
