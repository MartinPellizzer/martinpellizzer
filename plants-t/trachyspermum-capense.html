Trachyspermum capense

Trachyspermum capense, commonly known as African cumin, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces oval, dark brown seeds that are rich in essential oils, giving it a pungent, spicy aroma. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

The seeds are widely used in traditional medicine and as a spice in regional cuisines.
