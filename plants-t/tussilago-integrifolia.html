Tussilago integrifolia

Tussilago integrifolia, commonly known as the American thimbleweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks.

Tussilago integrifolia is also known for its medicinal properties, historically used to treat respiratory ailments.
