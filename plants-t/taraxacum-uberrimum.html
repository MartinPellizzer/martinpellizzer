Taraxacum uberrimum

Taraxacum uberrimum, commonly known as the greater dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, spoon-shaped leaves and a hollow, leafless stem that supports a single, showy yellow flower head. The plant produces a distinctive seed dispersal mechanism through its parachute-like seeds, which are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in meadows, lawns, and disturbed areas.

Taraxacum uberrimum is known for its medicinal properties, with parts of the plant historically used in herbal remedies.
