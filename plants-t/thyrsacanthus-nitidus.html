Thyrsacanthus nitidus

Thyrsacanthus nitidus, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf spurge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny, and somewhat succulent stems, which are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically in the axils of the leaves. Its leaves are usually alternate, simple, and have a waxy surface that gives them a glossy appearance.

Thyrsacanthus nitidus is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
