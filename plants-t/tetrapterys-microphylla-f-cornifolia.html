Tetrapterys microphylla f. cornifolia

Tetrapterys microphylla f. cornifolia, commonly known as the corn-leaved tamarillo, is a variant of the tamarillo plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. The plant produces yellow-orange berries that are edible and rich in vitamin C. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its fruit.

Its botanical features make it a unique and valuable species within the Solanaceae family.
