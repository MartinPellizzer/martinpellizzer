Thlaspiceras capricornutum

Thlaspiceras capricornutum, commonly known as the capricorn nut tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by hard, woody fruits containing edible seeds.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique seed structure and its role in traditional medicine and local ecosystems.
