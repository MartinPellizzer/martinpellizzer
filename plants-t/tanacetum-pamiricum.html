Tanacetum pamiricum

Tanacetum pamiricum, commonly known as the Pamir tansy, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces yellow to brown flower heads that consist of numerous small florets, with the outer ones being ray flowers and the inner ones disc flowers. It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

Tanacetum pamiricum is often used in traditional medicine for its aromatic properties and potential therapeutic benefits.
