Thymus × beckianus

Thymus × beckianus, commonly known as Beck's thyme, is a hybrid perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and aromatic, small, gray-green leaves. The plant produces tiny, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its essential oils, rich in thymol and carvacrol, contribute to its use in aromatherapy and traditional medicine.
