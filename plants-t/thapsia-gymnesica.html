Thapsia gymnesica

Thapsia gymnesica, commonly known as the Balearic thapsia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly the Balearic Islands.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and tall, sparsely branched stems. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom during the summer months. Thapsia gymnesica is adapted to arid and rocky environments, showcasing its resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local flora and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
