Thymus serpyllum var. longicaulis

Thymus serpyllum var. longicaulis, commonly known as narrow-leaved thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically narrow and linear, with a dense mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces pink or white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic foliage is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
