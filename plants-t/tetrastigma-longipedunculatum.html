Tetrastigma longipedunculatum

Tetrastigma longipedunculatum, commonly known as the long-peduncled grapevine, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its twining stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by yellowish berries. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
