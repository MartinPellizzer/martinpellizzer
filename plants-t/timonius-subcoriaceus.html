Timonius subcoriaceus

Timonius subcoriaceus, commonly known as the roughleaf timonius, is a species of flowering plant native to specific regions in southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rough, leathery leaves that are typically grayish-green in color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, blooming during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
