Tsuga × jeffreyi

Tsuga × jeffreyi, commonly known as the Jeffrey pine, is a hybrid conifer native to the western United States.

It is a cross between Tsuga heterophylla (whitebark pine) and Tsuga caroliniana (eastern hemlock). The tree has a tall, slender trunk with a narrow, conical crown. Its needles are dark green and often have a slight blue tinge, and it produces cones that hang downward.

This species is adapted to dry, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.
