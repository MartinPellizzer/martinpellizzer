Tanacetum mikeschinii

Tanacetum mikeschinii, commonly known as the Siberian tansy, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features finely divided, gray-green leaves and produces yellow button-like flowers that bloom in late summer. It is often found in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.

Tanacetum mikeschinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its aromatic properties.
