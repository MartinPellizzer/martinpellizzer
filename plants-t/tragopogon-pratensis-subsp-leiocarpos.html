Tragopogon pratensis subsp. leiocarpos

Tragopogon pratensis subsp.

leiocarpos, commonly known as the yellow goat's beard, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and western Asia. It features a basal rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves and produces striking yellow ray florets surrounded by a series of tubular flowers.

The plant typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and the plant is known for its ability to self-fertilize.

It is also a member of the dandelion family (Compositae) and is often found in disturbed habitats.
