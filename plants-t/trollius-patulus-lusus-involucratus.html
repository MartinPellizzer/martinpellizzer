Trollius patulus lusus involucratus

Trollius patulus lusus involucratus, commonly known as the lesser buttercup, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, showy flowers that typically bloom in late summer. The plant has basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and wetlands.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
