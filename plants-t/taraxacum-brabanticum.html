Taraxacum brabanticum

Taraxacum brabanticum, commonly known as the Brabant dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces a single, hairy stem that bears a yellow composite flower head, which develops into a globe-shaped seed head known as a pappus. It thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats, often found in meadows and roadsides.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
