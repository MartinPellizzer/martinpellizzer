Thinopyrum turcicum

Thinopyrum turcicum, commonly known as Turkey grass, is a perennial grass species native to Eurasia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and spikelet-based inflorescences. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Thinopyrum turcicum is also known for its potential use in forage and its role in plant breeding programs.
