Trifolium tumens var. talyschense

Trifolium tumens var. talyschense, commonly known as the Talyschense clover, is a rare and specialized variant of the genus Trifolium.

It is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves, typically with three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. It thrives in specific soil conditions, often found in nutrient-rich, well-drained soils.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a botanically significant species with potential ecological importance.
