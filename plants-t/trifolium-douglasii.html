Trifolium douglasii

Trifolium douglasii, commonly known as Douglas's clover, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged on a central stem. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Trifolium douglasii is often found in areas with well-drained soil and full sun exposure.
