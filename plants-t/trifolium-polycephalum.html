Trifolium polycephalum

Trifolium polycephalum, commonly known as the many-headed clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves, each consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and open woodlands.

Trifolium polycephalum is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
