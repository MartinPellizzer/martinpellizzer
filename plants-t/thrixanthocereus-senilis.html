Thrixanthocereus senilis

Thrixanthocereus senilis, commonly known as the silver star cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a columnar cactus with a distinctive appearance, featuring a grayish-green stem covered in white, woolly spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are clustered in dense, star-like formations. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Due to its slow growth and unique appearance, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
