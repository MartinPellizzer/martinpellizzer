Tectaria melanocauloides

Tectaria melanocauloides, commonly known as the black-stemmed tectaria, is a species of fern belonging to the family Tectariaceae.

It is characterized by its large, leathery fronds that are typically divided into several segments. The plant has a distinctive dark, almost black, stem that contrasts with its lighter colored fronds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Tectaria melanocauloides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
