Taxodium juniperoides

Taxodium juniperoides, commonly known as the pond cypress, is a deciduous conifer native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, columnar shape and its ability to tolerate wet soil conditions, making it a common sight in swampy areas. The plant produces small, scale-like leaves that turn a striking reddish-brown in the fall before shedding. Its cones are small and woody, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in wetland ecosystems.
