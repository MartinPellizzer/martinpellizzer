Trifolium polymorphum

Trifolium polymorphum, commonly known as the variable clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which typically consist of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that can range in color from white to pink and violet, depending on the variety. It is native to various regions of Europe and is often found in grassy areas and open woodlands.

Trifolium polymorphum is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in ecological and agricultural contexts.
