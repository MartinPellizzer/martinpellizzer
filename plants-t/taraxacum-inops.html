Taraxacum inops

Taraxacum inops, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and a single, elongated flower head that produces a distinctive, fluffy seed dispersal mechanism. Native to Europe, it is often found in grassy areas and disturbed soils. The plant is known for its medicinal properties, with the leaves and flowers used in traditional remedies.

Its botanical features make it an important component of ecological studies and herbal medicine.
