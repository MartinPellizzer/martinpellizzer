Thunia nivalis

Thunia nivalis, commonly known as the snow thunia, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often found in mountainous environments.

Thunia nivalis is a short-lived perennial, with a growth cycle adapted to the harsh conditions of its habitat.
