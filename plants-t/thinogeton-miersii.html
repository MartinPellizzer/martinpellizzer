Thinogeton miersii

Thinogeton miersii, commonly known as the Miers' thornbush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting well to poor soil conditions.

Thinogeton miersii plays a role in local ecosystems by providing shelter and food for various insects and small animals.
