Toona hexandra var. listeri

Toona hexandra var. listeri, commonly known as the hill teak or hill mahogany, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and large, elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The tree produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by woody, round fruits containing several seeds. Its wood is highly valued for its durability and fine grain, making it a significant species in local timber trade.

The plant is also noted for its ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
