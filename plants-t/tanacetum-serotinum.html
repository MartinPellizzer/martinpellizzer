Tanacetum serotinum

Tanacetum serotinum, commonly known as late tansy, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and yellow button-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist soils along riverbanks and wet meadows, thriving in temperate climates. It is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used historically in herbal medicine.

Tanacetum serotinum is also a source of nectar for pollinators and contributes to biodiversity in its native habitats.
