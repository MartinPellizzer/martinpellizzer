Tragia ambigua var. urticans

Tragia ambigua var. urticans, commonly known as the stinging tragia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems covered in fine, hair-like structures that can cause skin irritation upon contact. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate in shape and often have a rough texture.

Tragia ambigua var. urticans is adapted to grow in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
