Tormentilla reptans var. cuneata

Tormentilla reptans var. cuneata, commonly known as creeping cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring, typically in clusters of five. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of down. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in wet areas.
