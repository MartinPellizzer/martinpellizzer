Tinus erythroxylodes

Tinus erythroxylodes, commonly known as the red-leafed tinus, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with striking red to purple leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that are followed by elongated, woody pods containing seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some botanical gardens and private collections.
