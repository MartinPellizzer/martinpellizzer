Thinouia myriantha

Thinouia myriantha, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Indian nettle, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on slender stalks. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Thinouia myriantha is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
