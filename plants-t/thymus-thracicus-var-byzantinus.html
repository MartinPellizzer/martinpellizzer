Thymus thracicus var. byzantinus

Thymus thracicus var. byzantinus, commonly known as Turkish thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, compact growth with fragrant, needle-like leaves that are typically grey-green in color. The plant produces tiny, pinkish-white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

This variety is valued for its aromatic properties and historical use in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
