Themeda saxicola

Themeda saxicola, commonly known as the rock grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is often found in open habitats such as savannas and rocky outcrops.

Its ability to thrive in harsh conditions makes it an important component of its native ecosystems.
