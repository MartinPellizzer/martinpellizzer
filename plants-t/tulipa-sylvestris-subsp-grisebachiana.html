Tulipa sylvestris subsp. grisebachiana

Tulipa sylvestris subsp.

grisebachiana, commonly known as the gray tulip, is a perennial bulbous plant native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of cup-shaped flowers that range in color from pale yellow to deep orange.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its bulbs are adapted to survive in harsh environments, often going dormant during periods of extreme temperature or drought.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
