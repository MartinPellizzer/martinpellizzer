Thymus antoninae

Thymus antoninae, commonly known as the Antonina thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are used in culinary applications and traditional medicine due to their essential oils rich in thymol.

Thymus antoninae thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and ornamental plant for gardens.
