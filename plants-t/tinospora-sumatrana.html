Tinospora sumatrana

Tinospora sumatrana, commonly known as the Indian creeper or Tinospora, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Menispermaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant produces bright orange berries that are edible and contain seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in humid forests and along riverbanks.

Tinospora sumatrana is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
