Tyloglossa ciliata

Tyloglossa ciliata, commonly known as the hairy tongue plant, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features a basal rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often covered in fine, thread-like trichomes. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its fibrous roots are known for their medicinal properties, used in traditional medicine for their anti-inflammatory and antiseptic qualities.

Tyloglossa ciliata thrives in well-drained soil and requires ample sunlight to flourish.
