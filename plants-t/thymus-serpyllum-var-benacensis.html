Thymus serpyllum var. benacensis

Thymus serpyllum var. benacensis, commonly known as the Balkan thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant leaves and flowers that are typically pink to purple in color. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes. Its aromatic properties make it valuable in both culinary and medicinal applications.

The variety is distinguished by its specific geographic distribution and slightly different morphology compared to the standard Thymus serpyllum.
