Thymus syriacus

Thymus syriacus, commonly known as Syrian thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant leaves that are typically gray-green in color, covered in fine hairs. The plant produces tiny white or pale pink flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic properties make it valuable in both culinary and medicinal applications.
