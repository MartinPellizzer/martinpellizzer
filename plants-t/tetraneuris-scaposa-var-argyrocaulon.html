Tetraneuris scaposa var. argyrocaulon

Tetraneuris scaposa var. argyrocaulon, commonly known as silverleaf tetraneuris, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which results from dense covering of fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically valuable species in its native range.
