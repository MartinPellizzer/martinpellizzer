Trigonella gharuensis

Trigonella gharuensis, commonly known as Indian bitter vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is native to regions of southern and eastern Asia, including India and Nepal. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Trigonella gharuensis is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies for various ailments.
