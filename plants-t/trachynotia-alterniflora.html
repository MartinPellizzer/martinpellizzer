Trachynotia alterniflora

Trachynotia alterniflora, commonly known as the alternate-flowered trachynotia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its alternate arrangement of leaves along the stem, which gives it its scientific name. The plant typically produces small, tubular flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
