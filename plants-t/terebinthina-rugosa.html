Terebinthina rugosa

Terebinthina rugosa, commonly known as the rough-hairy terebinth, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, rough hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible but often bitter. Terebinthina rugosa thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
