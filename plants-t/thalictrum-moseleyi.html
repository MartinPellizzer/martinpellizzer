Thalictrum moseleyi

Thalictrum moseleyi, commonly known as the Moseley's meadow-rue, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowering period occurs in early spring, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Thalictrum moseleyi is also noted for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various invertebrates.
