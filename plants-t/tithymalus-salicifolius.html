Tithymalus salicifolius

Tithymalus salicifolius, commonly known as the willow-leaved tithymalus, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, willow-like leaves that are typically lanceolate and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in composite heads, which are often clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, streambanks, and woodland edges.

Tithymalus salicifolius is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
