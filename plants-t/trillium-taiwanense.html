Trillium taiwanense

Trillium taiwanense, commonly known as Taiwan trillium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the family Melanthiaceae and is characterized by its three-parted leaves and showy white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest habitats, preferring rich, well-drained soil. It blooms in early spring, producing a single flower with six petals and three sepals.

Trillium taiwanense is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often found in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
