Taraxacum inane

Taraxacum inane, commonly known as the slender dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its upright, hairless stems and basal leaves that are lanceolate in shape. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that develop into fluffy white seed heads, typical of the dandelion genus. It is native to Europe and Asia and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

Taraxacum inane is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
