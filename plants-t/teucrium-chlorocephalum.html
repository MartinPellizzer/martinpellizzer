Teucrium chlorocephalum

Teucrium chlorocephalum, commonly known as mouse ear tea, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, pale blue or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually rounded and covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassy areas, along roadsides, and in disturbed habitats.
