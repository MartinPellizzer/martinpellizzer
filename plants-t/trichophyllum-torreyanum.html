Trichophyllum torreyanum

Trichophyllum torreyanum, commonly known as the Torrey's haircap, is a small, evergreen shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage. The plant features tiny, hair-like structures on its stems, which give it its common name. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

Trichophyllum torreyanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
