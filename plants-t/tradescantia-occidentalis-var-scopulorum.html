Tradescantia occidentalis var. scopulorum

Tradescantia occidentalis var. scopulorum, commonly known as the rock spiderwort, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its striking purple-blue flowers and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for xeriscaping. Its foliage and flowers are often used in native gardens for their ornamental value and ecological benefits.

It is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
