Trichocereus glaziovii

Trichocereus glaziovii, commonly known as the "Moon Cactus" or "Pachypodium," is a cacti species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, thick, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant typically produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the warmer months, which attract pollinators like bees and moths. Its slow growth rate and unique appearance make it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Trichocereus glaziovii is also noted for its ability to thrive with minimal water and sunlight, making it relatively easy to care for.
