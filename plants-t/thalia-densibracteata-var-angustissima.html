Thalia densibracteata var. angustissima

Thalia densibracteata var. angustissima, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thalia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of tubular, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by birds, particularly hummingbirds, due to their bright colors and nectar-rich structure.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with appropriate humidity and light conditions.
