Trichoglottis leontoglossa

Trichoglottis leontoglossa, commonly known as the lion's tongue orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in racemose inflorescences, which are typically arranged along the stems. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining, acidic soil.

Trichoglottis leontoglossa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
