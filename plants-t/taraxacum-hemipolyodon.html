Taraxacum hemipolyodon

Taraxacum hemipolyodon, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and a flowering head that produces a distinctive spherical seed head. The plant is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in grasslands and disturbed soils. It has a taproot system and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
