Thymus × pectinatus

Thymus × pectinatus, commonly known as woolly thyme, is a hybrid shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, grey-green foliage covered in fine, soft hairs, which give it a woolly appearance. The plant produces small, pale pink to purple flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Its aromatic leaves are often used in culinary and medicinal applications, similar to other thyme varieties.
