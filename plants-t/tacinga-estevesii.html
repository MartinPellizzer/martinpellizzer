Tacinga estevesii

Tacinga estevesii, commonly known as the Brazilian spider orchid, is a rare and endemic orchid species found in the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, delicate flowers that resemble spiders, giving it its common name. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers with prominent, elongated sepals and petals. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks.

Due to habitat loss, Tacinga estevesii is considered endangered and is protected under conservation efforts.
