Tradescantia crassula var. gaudichaudii

Tradescantia crassula var. gaudichaudii, commonly known as the "Gaudichaud's spiderwort," is a compact, evergreen perennial native to South America.

It features dense, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.

Its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements make it a favored ornamental plant in arid and semi-arid regions.
