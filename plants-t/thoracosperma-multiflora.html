Thoracosperma multiflora

Thoracosperma multiflora, commonly known as the African mahogany, is a tropical tree native to regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, simple leaves that are typically alternate and have a smooth margin. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually arranged in clusters, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its valuable timber, it is also considered an important species for ecological and economic purposes.
