Trichoglottis intermedia

Trichoglottis intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate trichoglottis, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial herb with fleshy, strap-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike, which bloom during the rainy season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Trichoglottis intermedia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in suitable conditions.
