Thalictrum thalictroides f. roseum

Thalictrum thalictroides f. roseum, commonly known as the rose-flowered meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves and showy, rose-pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and is characterized by its slender stems and clustered inflorescences. Its botanical structure includes a fibrous root system and a hollow stem, which contribute to its ornamental value in gardens.

The flowers are pollinated by insects, making it an important component of local ecosystems.
