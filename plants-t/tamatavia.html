Tamatavia

Tamatavia, commonly known as the Madagascar star apple, is a tropical plant native to Madagascar.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pink, followed by star-shaped fruits with a sweet, juicy pulp. Tamatavia thrives in humid, well-drained soils and requires a warm climate with ample sunlight.

Its unique appearance and edible fruits make it a notable species in both horticulture and local ecosystems.
