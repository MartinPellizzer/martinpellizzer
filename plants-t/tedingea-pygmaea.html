Tedingea pygmaea

Tedingea pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf tendingia, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows to a height of only a few centimeters, with delicate, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually white or pale green, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its size and unique appearance, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens.
