Trisetum bifidum var. papillosum

Trisetum bifidum var. papillosum, commonly known as the hairy false brome, is a grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces panicles of small, greenish flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and have a hairy texture, contributing to its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting various insect species.
