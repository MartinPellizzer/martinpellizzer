Tococa orcheophora

Tococa orcheophora, commonly known as the Amazonian arrowhead, is a herbaceous plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its arrowhead-shaped leaves and striking inflorescence. The plant produces a spadix covered in small, clustered flowers, which are often surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. Tococa orcheophora is known for its medicinal properties, with various parts of the plant used in traditional healing practices.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
