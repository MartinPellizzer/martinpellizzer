Tupa arguta

Tupa arguta, commonly known as the sharp-tailed mountain mint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small, tubular, and clustered in whorls.

Tupa arguta is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
