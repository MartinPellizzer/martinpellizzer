Thunbergia fragrans var. laevis

Thunbergia fragrans var. laevis, commonly known as the fragrant morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant is a vigorous climber, often used in ornamental gardening for its showy blooms and pleasant aroma. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

The flowers typically open in the early morning, giving the plant its common name.
