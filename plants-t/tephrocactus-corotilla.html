Tephrocactus corotilla

Tephrocactus corotilla, commonly known as the Corotilla cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in clusters of small, spiny areoles. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, which are typically borne at the base of the stems. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in arid gardens and collections.
