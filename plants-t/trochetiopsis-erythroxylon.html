Trochetiopsis erythroxylon

Trochetiopsis erythroxylon, commonly known as the redwood tree, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a tall, evergreen tree that can reach heights of up to 50 meters. The tree is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and lance-shaped leaves. It produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, and its fruits are capsules containing numerous seeds.

This species is native to tropical regions and is valued for its durable wood used in construction and furniture.
