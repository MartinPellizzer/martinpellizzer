Tirucalia berotica

Tirucalia berotica, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and produces small, yellowish flowers. The plant has compound leaves with lance-shaped leaflets and is often found in lowland evergreen forests. Its wood is highly valued for its durability and rich color, though it is also listed as an endangered species due to overharvesting.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
