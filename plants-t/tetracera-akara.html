Tetracera akara

Tetracera akara, commonly known as the Malayan starfruit, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its distinctive yellow-orange fruits that resemble star-shaped berries. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by the fleshy, edible fruit. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a rough texture.

The fruit is typically harvested when it turns a vibrant orange color, indicating maturity.
