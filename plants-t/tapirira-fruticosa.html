Tapirira fruticosa

Tapirira fruticosa, commonly known as Brazilian pepper tree, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in humid, well-drained soils.

Due to its hardiness, it is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
