Teliostachya coppenamensis

Teliostachya coppenamensis, commonly known as the Coppenamens grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist environments and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
