Teucrium webbianum f. linearifolium

Teucrium webbianum f. linearifolium, commonly known as the linear-leaved germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its slender, linear leaves.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and square stems. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, and it is often used in traditional herbal medicine.

The foliage is often used as a culinary herb, though it is less common than other members of the genus.
