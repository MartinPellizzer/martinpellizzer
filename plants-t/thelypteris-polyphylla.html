Thelypteris polyphylla

Thelypteris polyphylla, commonly known as the broad-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of fern characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds.

It belongs to the family Thelypteridaceae and is native to various regions in the Americas, particularly in moist, shaded environments. The plant features bipinnate leaves with a distinctive, arching growth habit, and its spores are produced in sori located on the underside of the fronds.

Thelypteris polyphylla is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its elegant appearance and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
