Tecomanthe hillii

Tecomanthe hillii, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a climbing vine native to Mexico and Central America.

It features heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. Tecomanthe hillii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative element in tropical landscapes.

Its flowers bloom in the morning, hence its common name, and it is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
