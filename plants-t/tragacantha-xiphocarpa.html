Tragacantha xiphocarpa

Tragacantha xiphocarpa, commonly known as the horned tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, spiny pods. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, followed by the development of its unique, horn-like seed pods. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its historical use in medicine and as a source of gum.

The botanical features of Tragacantha xiphocarpa make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
