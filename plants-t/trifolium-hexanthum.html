Trifolium hexanthum

Trifolium hexanthum, commonly known as the lesser clover, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, consisting of three small oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

Trifolium hexanthum is a perennial herb that plays a role in nitrogen fixation, benefiting soil fertility.
