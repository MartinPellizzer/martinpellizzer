Telipogon cundinamarcae

Telipogon cundinamarcae, commonly known as the Cundinamarca grass, is a perennial herb native to the Andean regions of Colombia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and is adapted to the cool, moist climates of its native habitat. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are the flowering structures typical of grasses.

Telipogon cundinamarcae is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native environment.
