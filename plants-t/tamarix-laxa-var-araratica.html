Tamarix laxa var. araratica

Tamarix laxa var. araratica, commonly known as the Arabian tamarisk, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It features small, scale-like leaves that reduce water loss, an adaptation to its harsh environment. The plant produces fluffy, pink to reddish flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in poor soils and low moisture conditions.

This species is often used for soil stabilization and windbreaks in desert landscapes.
