Teucrium racemosum var. polymorphum

Teucrium racemosum var. polymorphum, commonly known as the variable germander, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features slender, branched stems that often grow in rocky or arid environments. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, arranged in terminal or axillary clusters. Its leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, with a slight pubescence.

This variety is known for its adaptability to drought conditions and is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
