Thymus aschurbajevii

Thymus aschurbajevii, commonly known as the Altai thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It features creeping stems and small, aromatic leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces tiny, pink or violet flowers in dense clusters during the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Thymus aschurbajevii is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
