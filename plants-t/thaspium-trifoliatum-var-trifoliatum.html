Thaspium trifoliatum var. trifoliatum

Thaspium trifoliatum var. trifoliatum, commonly known as the three-leaved thaspium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features trifoliate leaves, meaning each leaf is divided into three distinct lobes. Its small, greenish-white flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in early summer.

The plant is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and is often found in wetland or riparian habitats.
