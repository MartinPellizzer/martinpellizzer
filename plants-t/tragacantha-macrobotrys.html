Tragacantha macrobotrys

Tragacantha macrobotrys, commonly known as the large-flowered tragacanth, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, branching stems and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant produces a distinctive resinous gum, which has been historically used in various industries. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Tragacantha macrobotrys is often cultivated for its ornamental value and traditional uses.
