Thymus serpyllum f. alpicola

Thymus serpyllum f. alpicola, commonly known as alpine thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to alpine regions of Europe.

It features dense, compact foliage with small, aromatic leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers during the summer months. It is well adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, thriving in full sun.

Its essential oils contain thymol and carvacrol, contributing to its medicinal and culinary uses.
