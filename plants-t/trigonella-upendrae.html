Trigonella upendrae

Trigonella upendrae, commonly known as the Upendra's fenugreek, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod-like fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. Its leaves are trifoliate, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that attract pollinators.

While less studied than other fenugreek species, it holds potential for agricultural and medicinal applications.
