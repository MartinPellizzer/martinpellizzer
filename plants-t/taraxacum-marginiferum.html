Taraxacum marginiferum

Taraxacum marginiferum, commonly known as the margined dandelion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of pale green, hairy leaves with distinct marginal teeth. The plant produces a hollow, leafless stem that bears a single flower head composed of numerous yellow ray florets. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in disturbed soils and open habitats.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
