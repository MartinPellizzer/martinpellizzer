Thoa globosa

Thoa globosa, commonly known as the globe thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spherical, spiky appearance, with dense clusters of blue or purple flowers. The plant has a compact growth habit, typically reaching heights of 30 to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are gray-green and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Thoa globosa is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
