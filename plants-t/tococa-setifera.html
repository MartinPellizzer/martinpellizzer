Tococa setifera

Tococa setifera, commonly known as the Amazonian bitterleaf, is a climbing shrub native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruits are small berries that turn red when mature, containing several seeds.

Tococa setifera is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
