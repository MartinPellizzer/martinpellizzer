Telanthera picta

Telanthera picta, commonly known as the painted bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors, including pink, purple, and white. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Telanthera picta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and unique appearance.
