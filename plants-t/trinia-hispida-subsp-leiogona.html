Trinia hispida subsp. leiogona

Trinia hispida subsp.

leiogona, commonly known as the smooth trinia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves.

The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or grassland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
