Thelypteris madidiensis

Thelypteris madidiensis, commonly known as the Madidi holly fern, is a species of fern native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the family Thelypteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often near streams or in dense undergrowth. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it to the forest floor.

Thelypteris madidiensis plays a role in the ecological balance of its habitat by contributing to soil stability and providing shelter for small organisms.
