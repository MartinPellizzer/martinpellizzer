Thymus rossii

Thymus rossii, commonly known as Rosso's thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It features aromatic, narrow leaves and produces small, pale pink to purple flowers during the summer months. The plant is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.

Thymus rossii is also used in traditional herbal remedies for its potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial benefits.
