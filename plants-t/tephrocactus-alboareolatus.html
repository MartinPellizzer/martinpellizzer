Tephrocactus alboareolatus

Tephrocactus alboareolatus, commonly known as the white-mottled cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in white, woolly areolas, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are typically borne at the areola level. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water.

Due to its unique features, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in desert gardens.
