Tradescantia elata

Tradescantia elata, commonly known as the spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, or white. The plant features long, narrow leaves that are often variegated with white or silver markings. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sun.

Tradescantia elata is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to naturalize easily.
