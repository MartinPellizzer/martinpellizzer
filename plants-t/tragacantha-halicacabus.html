Tragacantha halicacabus

Tragacantha halicacabus, commonly known as the goat's rue, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces a sticky, resinous substance called tragacanth, which has been used historically in medicine and as a thickening agent. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Tragacantha halicacabus is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
