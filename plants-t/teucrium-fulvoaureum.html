Teucrium fulvoaureum

Teucrium fulvoaureum, commonly known as golden oregano, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features upright, branching stems covered in grayish-white hairs, and its leaves are oval-shaped with a slightly serrated edge. Its flowers are small, clustered in spikes, and range in color from yellow to orange, blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant is valued for its aromatic leaves and is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
