Theophrasta pendula

Theophrasta pendula, commonly known as the weeping thunbergia, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its long, cascading stems that produce clusters of fragrant, funnel-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple. The leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires support for its vine-like growth habit.

This plant is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and as a houseplant.
