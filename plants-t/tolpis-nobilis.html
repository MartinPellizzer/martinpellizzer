Tolpis nobilis

Tolpis nobilis, commonly known as the noble tolpis, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Tolpis nobilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
