Taeniophyllum wakatinense

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are clustered on the underside of the leaves.
