Tristachya leucothrix var. longiaristata

Tristachya leucothrix var. longiaristata, commonly known as the silver leaf sedge, is a perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its striking silver-white foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its inflorescence consists of a dense spike of small, pale green to brown flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in water garden settings.
