Torilis arvensis var. heterocarpa

Torilis arvensis var. heterocarpa, commonly known as the field chickweed or broad-leaved chickweed, is a herbaceous annual plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender stems and alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its fruits are schizocarps, consisting of two separate mericarps that split apart upon maturity.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and is common in agricultural fields and along roadsides.
