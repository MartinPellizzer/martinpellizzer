Tetrastigma glabratum

Tetrastigma glabratum, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed crossvine, is a woody climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its twining stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruits are berries that mature to a dark purple color, containing several seeds.

Tetrastigma glabratum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
