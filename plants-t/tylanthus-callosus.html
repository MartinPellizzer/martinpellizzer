Tylanthus callosus

Tylanthus callosus, commonly known as the roughleaf tylanthus, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and prairies. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, clustered yellow flowers in the spring. Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem, hairy foliage, and a preference for full sun to partial shade.

Tylanthus callosus is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
