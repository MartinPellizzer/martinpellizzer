Theobroma album

Theobroma album, commonly known as the cocoa tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its large, elongated leaves and clusters of white, fragrant flowers. The plant produces pods containing seeds that are the primary source of cocoa beans used in chocolate production. Theobroma album thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.

Its botanical significance lies in its role in both agriculture and the global chocolate industry.
