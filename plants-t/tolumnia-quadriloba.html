Tolumnia quadriloba

Tolumnia quadriloba, commonly known as the four-lobed orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant features a pseudobulb structure, which helps it store water and nutrients in its native habitat. Its leaves are usually broad and oval, providing a lush green backdrop to its vibrant blossoms.

Tolumnia quadriloba is often cultivated for its ornamental value in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
