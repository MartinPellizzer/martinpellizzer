Terebinthina aromatica var. punctata

Terebinthina aromatica var. punctata, commonly known as the spotted terebinth, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves with distinctive white or yellow spots. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark blue or black berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its unique foliage and fragrant properties.
