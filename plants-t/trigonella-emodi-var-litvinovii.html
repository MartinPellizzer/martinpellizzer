Trigonella emodi var. litvinovii

Trigonella emodi var. litvinovii, commonly known as the Kashmiri fenugreek, is a variant of the fenugreek plant native to the Himalayan region.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are rich in nutrients and commonly used in traditional medicine. Its botanical features include hairy stems and a distinct aromatic scent, making it easily identifiable.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and is often cultivated for both culinary and therapeutic purposes.
