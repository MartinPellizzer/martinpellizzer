Tetracera plumbea

Tetracera plumbea, commonly known as the blue mist vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are usually bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by round, fleshy fruits. Tetracera plumbea thrives in humid, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique appearance and growth habit make it a notable species in botanical studies and horticulture.
