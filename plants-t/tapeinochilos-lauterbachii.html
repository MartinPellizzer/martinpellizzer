Tapeinochilos lauterbachii

Tapeinochilos lauterbachii, commonly known as the Lauterbach's orchid, is a small, terrestrial orchid native to the humid forests of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically blooms in the spring, producing clusters of white or pale yellow flowers with a distinct, intricate pattern on the petals. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
