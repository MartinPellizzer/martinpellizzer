Taraxacum replicatum

Taraxacum replicatum, commonly known as the common dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its rosette of basal, spoon-shaped leaves and a hollow, leafless stem that supports a flower head. The plant produces a bright yellow flower that develops into a fluffy white seed head, which is dispersed by the wind. It is a perennial herb that is native to Europe but has been naturalized in many parts of the world.

Taraxacum replicatum is known for its medicinal properties and is often found in lawns, meadows, and disturbed soils.
