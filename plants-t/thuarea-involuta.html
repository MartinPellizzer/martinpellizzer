Thuarea involuta

Thuarea involuta, commonly known as the curled leaf cactus, is a succulent plant native to South America.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, with a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Thuarea involuta is well adapted to arid environments, thriving in full sun and well-draining soil.
