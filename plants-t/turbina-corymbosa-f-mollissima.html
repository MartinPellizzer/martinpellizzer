Turbina corymbosa f. mollissima

Turbina corymbosa f. mollissima, commonly known as the velvet turban snakeroot, is a botanical variant of the Turbina corymbosa species.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant is native to certain regions of South America, particularly in Brazil. It typically grows in humid, shaded environments within tropical forests.

Its leaves are broad and elliptical, with a smooth texture that distinguishes it from other varieties of the species.
