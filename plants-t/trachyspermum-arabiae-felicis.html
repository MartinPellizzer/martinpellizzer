Trachyspermum arabiae-felicis

Trachyspermum arabiae-felicis, commonly known as Persian cumin, is an annual herb native to the Middle East and South Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are widely used in culinary and medicinal applications. Its leaves are aromatic and often used in traditional medicine for their digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.
