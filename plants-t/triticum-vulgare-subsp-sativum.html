Triticum vulgare subsp. sativum

Triticum vulgare subsp.

sativum, commonly known as wheat, is a major cereal crop belonging to the grass family Poaceae. It is characterized by its spikelet structure, with small, two-rowed grains that are typically harvested when mature.

The plant has a robust root system and a cylindrical stem that supports its leafy structure. Its flowers are wind-pollinated, and the grains develop into the edible seeds that are widely used in food and industry.

This species is cultivated globally for its high nutritional value and versatility in various food products.
