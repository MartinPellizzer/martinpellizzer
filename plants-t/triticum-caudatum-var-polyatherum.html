Triticum caudatum var. polyatherum

Triticum caudatum var. polyatherum, commonly known as the long-headed wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated spikes and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This species is sometimes cultivated for forage or as a cover crop due to its hardiness and adaptability.
