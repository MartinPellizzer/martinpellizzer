Turnera aurantiaca

Turnera aurantiaca, commonly known as the orange turnera, is a perennial shrub native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a striking orange hue. The plant has alternate, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Turnera aurantiaca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
