Trigonochilum distans

Trigonochilum distans, commonly known as the broad-leaved orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features lanceolate to ovate leaves that are often hairy, and its flowers are typically pale green to yellowish in color. Trigonochilum distans thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as an indicator species for wetland ecosystems and its ornamental value in horticulture.
