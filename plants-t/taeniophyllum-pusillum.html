Taeniophyllum pusillum

Taeniophyllum pusillum, commonly known as the dwarf mistletoe, is a small evergreen shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tiny, glossy leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant is typically found growing in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its fruit is a small, capsule that releases tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Taeniophyllum pusillum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
