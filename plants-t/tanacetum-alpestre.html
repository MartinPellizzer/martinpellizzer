Tanacetum alpestre

Tanacetum alpestre, commonly known as alpine tansy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant features dark green, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish-white composite flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters. It is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes at elevations above 1,500 meters.

Tanacetum alpestre is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its aromatic properties.
