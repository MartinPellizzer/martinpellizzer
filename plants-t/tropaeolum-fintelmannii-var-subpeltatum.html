Tropaeolum fintelmannii var. subpeltatum

Tropaeolum fintelmannii var. subpeltatum, commonly known as the golden crownleaf, is a strikingly ornamental plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers and heart-shaped leaves that are often adorned with a distinctive golden or reddish border. The plant belongs to the Tropaeolaceae family, known for its showy blooms and edible foliage. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking both aesthetic and edible value.

Its rapid growth and ability to attract pollinators make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological gardens.
