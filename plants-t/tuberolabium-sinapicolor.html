Tuberolabium sinapicolor

Tuberolabium sinapicolor, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow petals and sepals. The plant produces small, tuberous roots that store nutrients, aiding in its survival in tropical environments. It typically grows in shaded, moist forest understories, where it thrives in the humid climate.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated for horticultural purposes.
