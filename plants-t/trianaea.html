Trianaea

Trianaea, commonly known as the starry nightshade, is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers with white markings that resemble stars, hence its common name. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with hairy stems and alternate leaves. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn from green to yellow or orange as they mature.

Trianaea is native to South America and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
