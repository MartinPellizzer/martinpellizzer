Thapsus × thapsoides

Thapsus × thapsoides, commonly known as the hybrid thapsus, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Thapsus aculeatus and Thapsus nodiflorus, characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and linear leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It is notable for its adaptability and resilience to harsh environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for studies in plant hybridization and ecological adaptation.
