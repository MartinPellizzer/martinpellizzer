Teucrium buxifolium var. albidum

Teucrium buxifolium var. albidum, commonly known as whiteleaf germander, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine white pubescence, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of tiny, two-lipped, pink to purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental flowers make it a valued addition to xeriscapic gardens.
