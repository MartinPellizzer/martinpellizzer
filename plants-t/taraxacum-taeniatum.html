Taraxacum taeniatum

Taraxacum taeniatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and a flowering stem that bears a single, bright yellow composite flower head. The plant produces small, wind-dispersed seeds with parachute-like structures called pappus. It is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats, thriving in temperate regions.

Taraxacum taeniatum is a perennial herb that is widely distributed across Europe and parts of Asia.
