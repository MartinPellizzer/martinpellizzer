Thuja plicata f. fastigiata

Thuja plicata f. fastigiata, commonly known as the Western arborvitae or upright thuja, is a cultivar of the western red cedar.

It is characterized by its dense, columnar growth habit and scale-like foliage that remains green throughout the year. This plant is native to the Pacific Northwest of North America and is often used in landscaping for its formal appearance. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, and it is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

The cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of garden settings.
