Taxodium distichum f. pendulum

Taxodium distichum f. pendulum, commonly known as the weeping baldcypress, is a unique cultivar of the baldcypress tree characterized by its weeping habit.

It features long, pendulous branches that hang in cascading fronds, creating a distinctive and ornamental appearance. The plant is native to the southeastern United States and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of scale-like leaves that turn a striking reddish-brown in the fall.

This cultivar is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
