Thymus serpens

Thymus serpens, commonly known as creeping thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It features small, aromatic leaves and flowers that are typically pink to purple in color. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species. Its dense growth habit makes it suitable for ground cover and rock gardens.

Thymus serpens is also valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, particularly for its essential oils and antiseptic properties.
