Thryptomene podantha

Thryptomene podantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaf thryptomene, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered white flowers. It typically grows in heathlands and dry sclerophyll forests, thriving in well-drained soils.

Thryptomene podantha is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to attract native wildlife.
