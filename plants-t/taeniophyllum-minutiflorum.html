Taeniophyllum minutiflorum

Taeniophyllum minutiflorum, commonly known as the miniature flower moss, is a small, terrestrial moss belonging to the family Dicranaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, hair-like leaves that are often clustered closely together. The plant produces tiny, clustered capsules for spore dispersal, which are typically found at the tips of its stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices.

Due to its small size and specific habitat requirements, it is often overlooked but plays a role in soil stabilization and moisture retention in its ecosystem.
