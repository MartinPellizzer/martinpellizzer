Trema melinonum

Trema melinonum, commonly known as the small-leaf stinging nettle, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Urticaceae family.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, which can cause skin irritation upon contact. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, typically in the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Trema melinonum is often found in temperate regions and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
