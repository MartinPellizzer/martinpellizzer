Trientalis europaea var. lucida

Trientalis europaea var. lucida, commonly known as the shining starflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe.

It features delicate, star-shaped white flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and prefers moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers attract pollinators like bees and flies, contributing to its ecological role in its native environment.

The species is also known for its medicinal properties, historically used in traditional remedies.
