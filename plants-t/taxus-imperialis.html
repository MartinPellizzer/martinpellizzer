Taxus imperialis

Taxus imperialis, commonly known as the imperial yew, is a rare and ancient coniferous tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slow growth rate, dense foliage, and distinctive reddish-brown bark that develops fissures with age. The plant produces small, clustered yellowish-green flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that contain toxic seeds. Its leaves are dark green, lance-shaped, and remain on the tree for several years, contributing to its ornamental value.

Taxus imperialis is also valued for its medicinal properties, as it contains taxol, a compound used in cancer treatment.
