Trifolium dasyphyllum var. stenolobum

Trifolium dasyphyllum var. stenolobum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
