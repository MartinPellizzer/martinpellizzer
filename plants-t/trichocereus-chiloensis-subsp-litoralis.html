Trichocereus chiloensis subsp. litoralis

Trichocereus chiloensis subsp.

litoralis, commonly known as the Littoral Torch Cactus, is a columnar cactus native to coastal regions of southern Chile. It features a tall, unbranched stem with a smooth, pale green surface and spines that are typically white or pale in color.

The plant produces large, showy flowers that are usually pink or red, blooming at night. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun exposure.

This subspecies is adapted to coastal climates and is often found growing near the shoreline.
