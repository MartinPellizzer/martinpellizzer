Taraxacum pseudodissimile f. pseudodissimile

Taraxacum pseudodissimile f. pseudodissimile, commonly known as the slender dandelion, is a variant of the dandelion species characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves and smaller, less showy flowers.

It is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically found in grassy areas and disturbed soils. The plant produces a distinctive, hollow stem and a spherical seed head that releases lightweight seeds with feathery parachutes. Its botanical features include a taproot system and a composite flower head made up of many tiny florets.

This subspecies is often studied for its ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
