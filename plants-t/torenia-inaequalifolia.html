Torenia inaequalifolia

Torenia inaequalifolia, commonly known as the unequal-leaved morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant produces showy, fragrant blooms that open in the morning and close in the evening. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or near streams.

Torenia inaequalifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
