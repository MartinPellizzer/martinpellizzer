Teucrium cordifolium

Teucrium cordifolium, commonly known as heartleaf germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, narrow to lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive heart-shaped base. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Teucrium cordifolium is valued for its medicinal properties and ornamental appeal in gardens.
