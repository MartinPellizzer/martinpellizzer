Tupa obovata

Tupa obovata, commonly known as the broad-ovate tupa, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink in color. Tupa obovata is often found in high-altitude grasslands and rocky slopes, thriving in cool and moist conditions.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
