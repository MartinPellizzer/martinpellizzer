Trichoon phragmites

Trichoon phragmites, commonly known as common reed, is a tall, perennial grass that thrives in wetland environments.

It has long, narrow leaves and produces dense clusters of brown, feathery flowers. The plant is highly adaptable and can grow in a variety of aquatic and semi-aquatic habitats. Its rhizomes form extensive underground networks, allowing it to spread rapidly.

Trichoon phragmites plays an important role in stabilizing shorelines and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
