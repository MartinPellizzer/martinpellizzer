Tamariscus germanicus

Tamariscus germanicus, commonly known as the German tamarisk, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in arid and saline soils. The plant has small, scale-like leaves that reduce water loss, making it well adapted to dry environments. It produces bright yellow flowers in clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects.

Tamariscus germanicus is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
