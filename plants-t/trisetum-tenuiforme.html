Trisetum tenuiforme

Trisetum tenuiforme, commonly known as slender hairgrass, is a species of grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, slender blades and is often found in moist, well-drained soils. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, which are small and often clustered at the ends of the stems.

Trisetum tenuiforme is a perennial grass that plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
