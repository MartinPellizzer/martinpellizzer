Tetradenia glauca var. xanthocarpa

Tetradenia glauca var. xanthocarpa, commonly known as the yellow-fruit tetradenia, is a flowering plant native to northern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and yellowish fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in woodlands and heathlands.

Its fruit, which is a capsule, contains several seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ecological role in the region.
