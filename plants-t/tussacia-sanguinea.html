Tussacia sanguinea

Tussacia sanguinea, commonly known as the red tussock grass, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and striking red or pink flower spikes that appear in summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a compact rosette.

Tussacia sanguinea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
