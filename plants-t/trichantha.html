Trichantha

Trichantha, commonly known as the "feather grass," is a genus of flowering plants in the grass family, Poaceae.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves that often resemble feathers, giving it its common name. Trichantha species are typically found in arid or semi-arid regions, where they thrive in well-drained soils. The plants produce small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually clustered in spikes.

They are often used in landscaping for their ornamental value and drought tolerance.
