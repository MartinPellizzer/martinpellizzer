Taraxacum leucoglossum

Taraxacum leucoglossum, commonly known as the white-lipped dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers, which are surrounded by a white, fluffy pappus that aids in seed dispersal. Its leaves are basal, alternate, and have a rough, hairy texture.

The species is notable for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
