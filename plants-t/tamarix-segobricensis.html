Tamarix segobricensis

Tamarix segobricensis, commonly known as the Segobric Desert Tamarisk, is a shrub native to arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and pink to reddish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is adapted to survive in saline and drought-prone environments, often found along coastal areas and inland deserts. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access deep groundwater sources.

Tamarix segobricensis plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
