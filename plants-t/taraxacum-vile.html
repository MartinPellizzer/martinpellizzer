Taraxacum vile

Taraxacum vile, commonly known as the common dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves and a hollow, leafless stem that supports a flower head composed of numerous yellow disk florets. The plant produces a distinctive seed dispersal mechanism through a feathery pappus that allows seeds to be carried by the wind. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is commonly found in lawns, meadows, and disturbed areas.

Taraxacum vile is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its diuretic and anti-inflammatory effects.
