Trichocentrum teres

Trichocentrum teres, commonly known as the narrow-leafed orchid, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is an epiphytic orchid, meaning it grows on the surface of trees without deriving nutrients from them. The plant features narrow, dark green leaves that are often clustered at the base. Its flowers are typically white or pale green, with a distinctive, elongated shape.

Trichocentrum teres is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
