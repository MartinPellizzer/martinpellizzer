Toxocarpus aurantiacus

Toxocarpus aurantiacus, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth texture and a slightly hairy underside. Its stems are woody and can climb or trail, often forming dense, sprawling growth.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
