Taberna albiflora

Taberna albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered taberna, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, white petals and small, clustered flowers that bloom during the spring season. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are slender and often trailing.

Taberna albiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
