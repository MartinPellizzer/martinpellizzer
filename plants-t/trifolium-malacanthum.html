Trifolium malacanthum

Trifolium malacanthum, commonly known as the purple trefoil, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets with a distinctive purple hue. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in early spring. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Trifolium malacanthum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
