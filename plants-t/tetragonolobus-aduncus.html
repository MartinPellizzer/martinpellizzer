Tetragonolobus aduncus

Tetragonolobus aduncus, commonly known as the slender tetragonolobus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces yellow flowers that are typically arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland habitats.

Tetragonolobus aduncus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
