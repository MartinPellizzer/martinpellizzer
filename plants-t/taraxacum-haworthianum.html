Taraxacum haworthianum

Taraxacum haworthianum, commonly known as the Haworth's dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, dark green leaves and a flowering stem that bears a single, bright yellow flower head. The plant is native to Europe and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. It produces a pappus-based seed that is dispersed by the wind, aiding in its wide distribution.

The species is notable for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
