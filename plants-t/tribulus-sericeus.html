Tribulus sericeus

Tribulus sericeus, commonly known as the silky tribulus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, and its leaves are often covered in fine, silky hairs. Tribulus sericeus is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, bean-like fruits.

It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
