Thrixanthocereus

Thrixanthocereus, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a genus of cacti native to South America.

These plants are characterized by their spiny stems and are typically found in arid or semi-arid environments. They produce large, showy flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The flowers are usually white or pale in color and have a strong, sweet fragrance.

Thrixanthocereus species are popular among cactus enthusiasts due to their unique appearance and dramatic nighttime blooms.
