Tephrosia interrupta subsp. interrupta

Tephrosia interrupta subsp.

interrupta, commonly known as the African blue pea, is a shrub native to tropical Africa. It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, clustered leaves.

The plant produces striking blue or violet flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are used in traditional medicine for their purported antimalarial properties. Tephrosia interrupta subsp.

interrupta is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
