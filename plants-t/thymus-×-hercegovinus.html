Thymus × hercegovinus

Thymus × hercegovinus, commonly known as the Hercegovina thyme, is a hybrid perennial herb native to the Balkan region.

It is characterized by its dense, low-growing habit and aromatic, gray-green foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Botanically, it belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic oils and medicinal properties.
