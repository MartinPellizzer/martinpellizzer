Taraxacum concinnum

Taraxacum concinnum, commonly known as the slender dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, upright leaves and a single, elongated stem that terminates in a yellow flower head. The plant is native to North America and thrives in a variety of habitats, including meadows, roadsides, and disturbed soils. Its flowers produce wind-dispersed seeds, which contribute to its widespread distribution.

The species is often mistaken for other dandelion varieties due to its similar appearance but can be distinguished by its more slender structure.
