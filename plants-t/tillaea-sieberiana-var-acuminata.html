Tillaea sieberiana var. acuminata

Tillaea sieberiana var. acuminata, commonly known as the narrowleaf tillaea, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing form and fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the summer months, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.

Due to its hardiness and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in arid and semi-arid climates.
