Trisetum bifidum var. viride

Trisetum bifidum var. viride, commonly known as green false brome, is a grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in open, disturbed sites such as roadsides and meadows, often forming dense stands.

Botanically, it is notable for its adaptability and role in stabilizing soils in various ecosystems.
