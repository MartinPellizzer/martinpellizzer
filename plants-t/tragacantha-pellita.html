Tragacantha pellita

Tragacantha pellita, commonly known as the pale tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces a gum-like substance from its stems, which has been used historically in various industries. Tragacantha pellita thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in scrublands and coastal areas.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for local wildlife.
