Trifolium burchellianum var. johnstonii

Trifolium burchellianum var. johnstonii, commonly known as Johnston's bursage, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its trifoliate leaves, consisting of three leaflets, are characteristic of the genus Trifolium.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.
