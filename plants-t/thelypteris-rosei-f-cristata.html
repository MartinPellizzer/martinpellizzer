Thelypteris rosei f. cristata

Thelypteris rosei f. cristata, commonly known as the cristate water fern, is a fern species native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Thelypteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its fronds are often curled or crinkled, giving it a unique, ornamental appearance.

This fern is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
