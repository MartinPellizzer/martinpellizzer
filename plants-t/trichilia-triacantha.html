Trichilia triacantha

Trichilia triacantha, commonly known as the African shea tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The tree produces pods containing seeds that are used for their oil, which has various industrial applications. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland ecosystems.

Due to its economic and ecological importance, it is a valuable species in both local and global markets.
