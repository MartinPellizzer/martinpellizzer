Tithymalus cespitosus

Tithymalus cespitosus, commonly known as the tufted tithymalus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun, making it well adapted to arid environments. Its small, fluffy seeds are dispersed by the wind, contributing to its ability to colonize new areas.

The plant is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
