Thymus sabulicola

Thymus sabulicola, commonly known as sand thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal areas and dry, rocky habitats. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a delicate touch to its environment. Its aromatic foliage contains essential oils, which contribute to its use in herbal medicine and culinary applications.

Thymus sabulicola is well adapted to arid conditions, making it a hardy and resilient species.
