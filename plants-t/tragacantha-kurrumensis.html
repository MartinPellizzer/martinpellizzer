Tragacantha kurrumensis

Tragacantha kurrumensis, commonly known as Kurrum gum, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Pakistan.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and woody pods. The plant produces a resinous gum that is harvested from its bark, which has been used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive.

It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and limited water availability.
