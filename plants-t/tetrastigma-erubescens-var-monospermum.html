Tetrastigma erubescens var. monospermum

Tetrastigma erubescens var. monospermum, commonly known as the red-flowered grapevine, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, red berries. The plant is notable for its ornamental value, particularly during the flowering season when it produces vibrant red flowers. Its berries are edible but generally not cultivated for commercial use.
