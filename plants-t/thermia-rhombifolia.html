Thermia rhombifolia

Thermia rhombifolia, commonly known as the rhombic thermia, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rhombic-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its botanical features include opposite leaf arrangement and a woody stem structure.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
