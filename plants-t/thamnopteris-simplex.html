Thamnopteris simplex

Thamnopteris simplex, commonly known as the simple fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Thamnopteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Thamnopteris simplex is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
