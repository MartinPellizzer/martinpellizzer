Thalictrum cincinnatum

Thalictrum cincinnatum, commonly known as the slender meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like foliage. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in an upright cluster, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Thalictrum cincinnatum is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
