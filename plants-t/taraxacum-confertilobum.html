Taraxacum confertilobum

Taraxacum confertilobum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and a yellow composite flower head that develops into a fluffy seed ball. The plant is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. It has a taproot system and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

The species is also noted for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in herbal remedies.
