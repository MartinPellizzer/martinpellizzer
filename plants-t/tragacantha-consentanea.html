Tragacantha consentanea

Tragacantha consentanea, commonly known as the Spanish thorn, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and small, elliptical leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, sticky seed pods. Its botanical features include a deep root system that allows it to thrive in arid and rocky soils.

Tragacantha consentanea is also valued for its resin, which has been used historically in various traditional applications.
