Tamarix anglica

Tamarix anglica, commonly known as the sea pink, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and saline soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are scale-like and reduce water loss, a feature that aids in its survival in arid conditions.

Tamarix anglica is also known for its ability to stabilize sand dunes and prevent soil erosion.
