Taraxacum inimitabile

Taraxacum inimitabile, commonly known as the rare mountain dandelion, is a flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous tiny florets. The plant produces a distinctive yellow flower that transitions into a white, fluffy seed head, typical of the dandelion genus. Its leaves are basal, spoon-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, adapted to the harsh, cold environments it inhabits.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
