Tragacantha globiflora

Tragacantha globiflora, commonly known as the globeflower, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, globe-shaped flower heads that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces a milky sap, which is often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Tragacantha globiflora thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.
