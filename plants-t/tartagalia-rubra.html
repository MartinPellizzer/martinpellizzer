Tartagalia rubra

Tartagalia rubra, commonly known as the red-flowered bellwether, is a striking perennial plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring, attracting a variety of pollinators. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery down, enhancing its ornamental appeal. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in well-drained, rocky soils.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, Tartagalia rubra is a subject of interest in both horticulture and botanical research.
