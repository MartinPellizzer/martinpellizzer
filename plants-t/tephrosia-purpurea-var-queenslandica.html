Tephrosia purpurea var. queenslandica

Tephrosia purpurea var. queenslandica, commonly known as the Queensland purple tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of purple flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and along watercourses.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil health.
