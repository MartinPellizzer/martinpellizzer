Trifolium campestre var. majus

Trifolium campestre var. majus, commonly known as the greater red clover, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets with serrated edges. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along roadsides.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine and as a forage crop.
