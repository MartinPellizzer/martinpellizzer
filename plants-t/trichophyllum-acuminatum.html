Trichophyllum acuminatum

Trichophyllum acuminatum, commonly known as the slender moss cushion, is a small, mat-forming moss that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It is characterized by its dense, cushion-like growth habit and is often found in forest floors or on rocky substrates. The plant has slender, acutely pointed leaves that are typically green to reddish in color, depending on environmental conditions. It reproduces through spores, which are released from small, cup-like structures called peristomes.

Trichophyllum acuminatum plays a role in soil stabilization and provides microhabitats for various invertebrates.
