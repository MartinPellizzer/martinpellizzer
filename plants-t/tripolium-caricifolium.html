Tripolium caricifolium

Tripolium caricifolium, commonly known as the water dock or broad-leaved water dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in a dense spike, which are typically pollinated by insects. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils along riverbanks and wetlands.

Due to its robust growth and adaptability, it is considered an invasive species in some regions.
