Terminalia crassipes

Terminalia crassipes, commonly known as the African mangosteen, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. Its fruit, often called the "mangosteen," is edible and has a sweet, tangy flavor.

Terminalia crassipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
