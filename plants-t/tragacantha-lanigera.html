Tragacantha lanigera

Tragacantha lanigera, commonly known as the velvet tree, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, hairy, and woody stems. The plant produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas.
