Trianthema triquetrum var. rottleri

Trianthema triquetrum var. rottleri, commonly known as the narrow-leaf false flax, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features small, fleshy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, adapted to store water in arid environments. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its drought tolerance and attractive appearance, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in dry climates.
