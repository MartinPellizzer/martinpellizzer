Tripsacum hermaphroditum

Tripsacum hermaphroditum, commonly known as the wild maize or tripes, is a grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant is monoecious, meaning it has both male and female flowers on the same individual. Its leaves are narrow and have a distinct waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments.

It is closely related to maize and has been studied for its potential in hybrid breeding programs.
