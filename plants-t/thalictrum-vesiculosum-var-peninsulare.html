Thalictrum vesiculosum var. peninsulare

Thalictrum vesiculosum var. peninsulare, commonly known as the Pacific foamflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to local biodiversity.

The plant's unique appearance and ecological role make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
