Tithymalus peplus subsp. calabricus

Tithymalus peplus subsp.

calabricus, commonly known as the Mediterranean thyme, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of pink or purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It is often used in herbal medicine for its antiseptic and carminative properties.

Its compact growth habit makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and as a ground cover.
