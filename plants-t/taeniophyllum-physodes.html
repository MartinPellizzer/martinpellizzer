Taeniophyllum physodes

Taeniophyllum physodes, commonly known as the velvet tassel fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety fronds that give it a soft, moss-like appearance. The plant produces small, clustered spore cases called sori on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it a popular choice for indoor gardening and conservation efforts.
