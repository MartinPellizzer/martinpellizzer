Trisetum spicatum var. andinum

Trisetum spicatum var. andinum, commonly known as the Andean feather grass, is a perennial grass native to the Andes Mountains.

It is characterized by its delicate, slender blades and panicle-like inflorescences that give it a feathery appearance. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions, often in moist, well-drained soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.

Its botanical features make it an important species in alpine ecosystems.
