Thymus serpyllum f. amplior

Thymus serpyllum f. amplior, commonly known as broadleaf thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its larger, broader leaves compared to the typical thyme species, which are typically narrow and pointed. The plant produces small, pink or violet flowers in compact clusters, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its aromatic foliage and is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
