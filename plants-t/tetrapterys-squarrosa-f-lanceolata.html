Tetrapterys squarrosa f. lanceolata

Tetrapterys squarrosa f. lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceolate tetrapterys, is a variant of the tetrapterys plant characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves.

The leaves are typically hairy on the underside and have a distinctive, squarrose arrangement at the base. This plant is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in horticultural and taxonomic studies.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.
