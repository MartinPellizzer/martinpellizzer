Terminalia muelleri

Terminalia muelleri, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits containing several seeds. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils in savannas and woodlands.

Terminalia muelleri is valued for its hard, durable wood used in furniture and construction.
