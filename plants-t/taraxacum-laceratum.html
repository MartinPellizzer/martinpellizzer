Taraxacum laceratum

Taraxacum laceratum, commonly known as the ragged chaff flower, is a species of dandelion native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its hairy, lobed leaves and a flowering stem that bears a cluster of yellow flowers. The plant produces a distinctive seed head with bristly, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in grassy areas and is often found in disturbed soils.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many other composite flowers.
