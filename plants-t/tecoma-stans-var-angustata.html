Tecoma stans var. angustata

Tecoma stans var. angustata, commonly known as narrowleaf yellow bells, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, angular leaves and clusters of bright yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in full sun and well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
