Tamonopsis

Tamonopsis, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its drooping clusters of yellow flowers that resemble cascading showers, hence its common name. The plant is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in warm, humid climates. Tamonopsis has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
