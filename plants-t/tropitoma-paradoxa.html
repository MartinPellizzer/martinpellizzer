Tropitoma paradoxa

Tropitoma paradoxa, commonly known as the paradoxical orchid, is a rare and unique member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, particularly in countries like Thailand and Vietnam. The plant is characterized by its striking, orchid-like flowers that display a complex arrangement of petals and sepals. Its botanical features include a sympodial growth habit and specialized roots adapted for nutrient absorption in its natural habitat.

Due to its rarity and distinctive morphology, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
