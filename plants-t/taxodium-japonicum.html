Taxodium japonicum

Taxodium japonicum, commonly known as the Japanese bald cypress, is a deciduous conifer native to Japan.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and feathery, scale-like leaves that turn a striking red-brown in autumn. The plant produces small, spherical cones that mature to a dark reddish color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones.

Its unique combination of coniferous and deciduous traits makes it a distinctive and ornamental tree in horticulture.
