Tinea intacta

Tinea intacta, commonly known as the smooth skin fungus, is a type of dermatophyte that primarily affects the skin, hair, and nails.

It belongs to the genus Tinea, which includes various fungi that cause fungal infections in humans and animals. The fungus thrives in warm, moist environments and is often found in areas with high humidity. Tinea intacta typically infects the skin of the feet, legs, or groin, leading to symptoms such as itching, redness, and scaling.

Its life cycle involves the production of spores that can be spread through direct contact or contaminated objects.
