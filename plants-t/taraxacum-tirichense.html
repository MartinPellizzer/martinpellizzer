Taraxacum tirichense

Taraxacum tirichense, commonly known as the Tirich Mountains dandelion, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of Pakistan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and a flowering stem topped with a composite flower head. The plant produces yellow ray florets that mature into white pappus-bearing seeds, typical of the genus Taraxacum. It thrives in rocky and alpine environments, demonstrating adaptability to harsh climatic conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
