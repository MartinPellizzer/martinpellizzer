Tarrietia actinophylla

Tarrietia actinophylla, commonly known as the flame tree or bracken fern, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, pinnate leaves that can reach up to 2 meters in length. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often in the summer months. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Tarrietia actinophylla is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.
