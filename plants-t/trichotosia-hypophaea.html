Trichotosia hypophaea

Trichotosia hypophaea, commonly known as the narrow-leafed ironwood, is a shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and rugged, woody structure, which provide habitat for various desert wildlife. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by small, woody fruits. It is adapted to arid environments, with deep roots that allow it to survive in poor soil and extreme temperatures.

Trichotosia hypophaea plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting biodiversity in desert ecosystems.
