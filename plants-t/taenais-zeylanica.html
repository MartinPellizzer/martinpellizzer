Taenais zeylanica

Taenais zeylanica, commonly known as the Ceylon dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are edible and have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
