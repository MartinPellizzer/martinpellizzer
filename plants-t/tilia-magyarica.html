Tilia magyarica

Tilia magyarica, commonly known as the Hungarian lime, is a deciduous tree native to Hungary and parts of Central Europe.

It belongs to the Tiliaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, yellowish-white flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits called samaras, which are dispersed by wind. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

Tilia magyarica is often found in riparian zones and is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance.
