Tragacantha argaea

Tragacantha argaea, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant produces a valuable resin, known as gum tragacanth, which is used in various industrial and medicinal applications. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

Tragacantha argaea is also noted for its ecological importance, providing food and habitat for native wildlife.
