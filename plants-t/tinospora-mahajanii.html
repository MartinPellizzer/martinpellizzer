Tinospora mahajanii

Tinospora mahajanii, commonly known as Indian banyan, is a climbing shrub belonging to the Menispermaceae family.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. The plant produces berries that are edible and contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

Tinospora mahajanii is also noted for its medicinal properties, used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
