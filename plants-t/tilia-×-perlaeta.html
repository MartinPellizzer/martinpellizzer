Tilia × perlaeta

Tilia × perlaeta, commonly known as the silver-leaf lime, is a hybrid deciduous tree belonging to the Tiliaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a silvery-white down on the underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are clustered in pendulous inflorescences. Its bark is smooth and light gray, becoming fissured with age, and it typically grows to a height of 10–20 meters.

Tilia × perlaeta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in gardens and parks for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
