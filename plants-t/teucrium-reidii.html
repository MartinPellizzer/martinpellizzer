Teucrium reidii

Teucrium reidii, commonly known as Reid's germander, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its small, tubular flowers are usually purple or blue, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
