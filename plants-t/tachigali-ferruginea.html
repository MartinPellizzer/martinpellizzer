Tachigali ferruginea

Tachigali ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty tachigali, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often arranged in a pinnate pattern, and its small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces woody pods that contain several seeds, and it is often found in lowland rainforests and secondary growth areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
