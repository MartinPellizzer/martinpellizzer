Tylecodon singularis

Tylecodon singularis, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are often blue-green in color, arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces striking, showy flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which bloom in spring. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Tylecodon singularis is a popular ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
