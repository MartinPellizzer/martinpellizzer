Teutliopsis tatarica

Teutliopsis tatarica, commonly known as the Tatarian grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in dry, open habitats and is often found in grasslands and steppe regions.

Teutliopsis tatarica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
