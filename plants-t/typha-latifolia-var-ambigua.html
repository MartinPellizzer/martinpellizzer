Typha latifolia var. ambigua

Typha latifolia var. ambigua, commonly known as the broad-leaved cattail, is a variant of the common cattail found in wetland environments.

It is characterized by its wide, flat, grass-like leaves and a cylindrical flower spike that matures into a brown, fluffy structure. This plant thrives in shallow water or moist soils, often forming dense colonies in marshes and along the edges of ponds. It plays a vital role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.

The plant is also used in traditional practices and has applications in ecological restoration projects.
