Tynus ellipticus

Tynus ellipticus, commonly known as the elliptical tynus, is a unique plant species characterized by its elongated, elliptical leaves.

It belongs to the family Tynaceae and is native to specific regions in the Mediterranean. The plant exhibits a compact growth habit with dense foliage, which helps it thrive in sunny, well-drained soils. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, displaying a range of colors from white to pale pink.

Tynus ellipticus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic contribution.
