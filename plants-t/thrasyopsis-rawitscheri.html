Thrasyopsis rawitscheri

Thrasyopsis rawitscheri, commonly known as the narrow-leafed rock orchid, is a rare and endangered species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

This perennial herbaceous plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer, typically in rocky or alpine habitats. The plant is adapted to cold, nutrient-poor environments and is often found in mountainous areas with high solar exposure.
