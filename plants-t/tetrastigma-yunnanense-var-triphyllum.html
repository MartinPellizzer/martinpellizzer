Tetrastigma yunnanense var. triphyllum

Tetrastigma yunnanense var. triphyllum, commonly known as the three-leaf bittersweet, is a climbing vine native to China.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its berries are initially green and turn red when mature, containing several seeds.

This species is often found in forested areas and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
