Tradescantia elongata var. triandra

Tradescantia elongata var. triandra, commonly known as the spiderwort or red-veined nettle.

This perennial plant is characterized by its distinctive, lance-shaped leaves with prominent red veins, which give it a striking appearance. It produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, depending on the variety. Native to the southeastern United States, it thrives in moist, shady environments.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
