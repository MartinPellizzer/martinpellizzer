Teucrium annandalei

Teucrium annandalei, commonly known as the Annandale germander, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
