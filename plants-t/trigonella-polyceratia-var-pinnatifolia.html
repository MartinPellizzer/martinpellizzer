Trigonella polyceratia var. pinnatifolia

Trigonella polyceratia var. pinnatifolia, commonly known as the pinnatifoliate fenugreek, is a variant of the fenugreek species native to regions of the Middle East and South Asia.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its trifoliate leaves, with the leaves of this variety being pinnately divided, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically clustered in umbels, followed by elongated, slender pods containing seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid climates, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

This variety is often cultivated for its seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.
