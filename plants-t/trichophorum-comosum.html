Trichophorum comosum

Trichophorum comosum, commonly known as the tufted sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and pond edges. The plant has slender, triangular stems that are often clustered in tufts, giving it its common name. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in spikelets, and it produces small, three-angled berries.

Trichophorum comosum plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
