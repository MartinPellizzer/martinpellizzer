Teucrium manghuaense var. manghuaense

Teucrium manghuaense var. manghuaense, commonly known as the Manghwa teucrium, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its flowers are small and clustered in whorls, displaying shades of blue to purple.

The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its ornamental value.
