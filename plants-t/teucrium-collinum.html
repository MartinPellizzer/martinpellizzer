Teucrium collinum

Teucrium collinum, commonly known as mountain germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in full sun. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves and produces small, blue or violet flowers in summer. It is often used in traditional medicine for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.

Botanically, it belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like mint and lavender.
