Tabebuia odontodiscus var. violascens

Tabebuia odontodiscus var. violascens, commonly known as the Purple Orchid Trumpet Tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking purple or violet flowers that bloom in clusters, adding vibrant color to its canopy. The tree has a smooth, greyish-bark trunk and produces long, pendulous inflorescences that resemble orchids. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a pointed tip.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
