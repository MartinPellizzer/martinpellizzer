Thelypteris ogasawarensis

Thelypteris ogasawarensis, commonly known as the Okinawan shield fern, is a species of fern native to the Ryukyu Islands of Japan.

It belongs to the family Thelypteridaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, with creeping rhizomes that help it spread across rocky or shaded habitats. Its leaves are typically leathery and have a serrated margin, which helps in water retention in its humid, tropical environment.

The spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, and the plant plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native ecosystems.
