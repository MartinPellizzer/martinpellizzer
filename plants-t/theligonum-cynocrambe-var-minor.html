Theligonum cynocrambe var. minor

Theligonum cynocrambe var. minor, commonly known as the lesser theligonum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. The plant features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in umbels and bloom in the summer months.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
