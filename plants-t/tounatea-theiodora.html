Tounatea theiodora

Tounatea theiodora, commonly known as the Ivory Coast flame tree, is a tropical tree native to West Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, leathery leaflets that provide shade beneath the tree. Its bark is smooth and light brown, often marked with shallow fissures.

Tounatea theiodora is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its timber and medicinal properties.
