Tilia platyphyllos subsp. pseudorubra

Tilia platyphyllos subsp.

pseudorubra, commonly known as the Carpathian lime, is a deciduous tree native to the Carpathian Mountains. It belongs to the Tiliaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are typically lighter in color compared to other lime species.

The flowers are small, clustered, and yellow, appearing in early summer, followed by small, round fruits. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mixed forests.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in woodworking.
