Tephrocactus clavarioides

Tephrocactus clavarioides, commonly known as the clustered cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a small, globular cactus with a distinctive clustered growth habit, forming dense groups of cylindrical stems. The plant has spiny areoles and produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and compact size, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in arid gardens.
