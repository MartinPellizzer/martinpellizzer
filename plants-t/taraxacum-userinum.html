Taraxacum userinum

Taraxacum userinum, commonly known as the Italian dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of spoon-shaped leaves and a hairy stem that supports a single, yellow flower head. The plant produces a distinctive seed head composed of numerous wind-dispersed seeds, each with a parachute-like structure. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows, gardens, and disturbed soils.

Taraxacum userinum is notable for its medicinal uses, including its potential anti-inflammatory and detoxifying properties.
