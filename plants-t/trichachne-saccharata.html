Trichachne saccharata

Trichachne saccharata, commonly known as sweet grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Asia and North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often coated with a fine layer of hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, meadows, and along stream banks.

Trichachne saccharata is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine and as a ceremonial plant by various indigenous cultures.
