Trollius riederianus var. japonicus

Trollius riederianus var. japonicus, commonly known as Japanese globeflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to East Asia.

It features showy, yellow, globe-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a white waxy coating. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
