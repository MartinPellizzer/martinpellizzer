Tetrastigma venulosum

Tetrastigma venulosum, commonly known as the hairy grapevine, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its tendrils and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by clusters of berries that turn from green to red as they mature. Its stems are often covered in fine hairs, giving it its common name.

Tetrastigma venulosum is often used in traditional medicine and is also cultivated for ornamental purposes.
