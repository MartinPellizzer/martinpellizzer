Tinus glandulosomarginata

Tinus glandulosomarginata, commonly known as the glandular-margin tree, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody structure and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. The leaves are usually alternate and have a distinctive glandular margin, giving the plant its scientific name. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
