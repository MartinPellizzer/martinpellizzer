Tragacantha macroptera

Tragacantha macroptera, commonly known as the large-fruited tragacanth, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves, with small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer. Its fruit is a pod that contains several seeds, and the plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Tragacantha macroptera is also valued for its gum, which has been historically used in various industrial and medicinal applications.
