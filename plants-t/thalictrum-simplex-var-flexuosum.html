Thalictrum simplex var. flexuosum

Thalictrum simplex var. flexuosum, commonly known as the flexible meadow rue, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, branched stems that often curl and twist, giving it a distinctive, graceful appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in compound racemes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are finely divided and fern-like, contributing to its ornamental appeal in gardens.

It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.
