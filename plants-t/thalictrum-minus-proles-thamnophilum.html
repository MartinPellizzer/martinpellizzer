Thalictrum minus proles thamnophilum

Thalictrum minus proles thamnophilum, commonly known as the lesser meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette, giving it a delicate, lace-like appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers with numerous stamens, which are borne on slender, branched pedicels. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks, preferring well-drained, loamy soils.

Its ornamental value lies in its elegant foliage and subtle floral display, making it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
