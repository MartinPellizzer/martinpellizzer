Trisetum bifidum var. oshimense

Trisetum bifidum var. oshimense, commonly known as the Oshimae sedge, is a perennial grass species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, triangular stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive two-lobed base, giving it its scientific name.

The variety oshimense is distinguished by its specific morphological traits and geographic distribution.
