Thalictrum simplex subsp. gallicum

Thalictrum simplex subsp.

gallicum, commonly known as the European meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe. It features delicate, fern-like leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodland areas and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. It has a fibrous root system and is known for its ornamental value in gardens. Thalictrum simplex subsp.

gallicum is also valued for its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and flies.
