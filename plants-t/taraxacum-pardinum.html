Taraxacum pardinum

Taraxacum pardinum, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and tubular florets. The plant has a rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves that are adapted to conserve water in harsh environments. Its flowers are typically yellow, with a central disk surrounded by ray florets, and it produces a distinctive, lightweight seed head that aids in wind dispersal.

Taraxacum pardinum is well-suited to sandy or rocky soils and can thrive in low-nutrient conditions.
