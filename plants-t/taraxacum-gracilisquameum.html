Taraxacum gracilisquameum

Taraxacum gracilisquameum, commonly known as the slender-haired dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, hair-like leaves and a single, yellow flower head that develops into a white, spherical seed ball. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. It is a perennial herb that thrives in temperate climates and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

The plant's botanical features make it easily distinguishable from other dandelion species.
