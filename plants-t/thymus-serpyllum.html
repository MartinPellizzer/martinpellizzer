Thymus serpyllum

Thymus serpyllum, commonly known as wild thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe, North Africa, and western Asia.

It features small, fragrant leaves and pink or white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, making it useful in culinary and medicinal applications.

Thymus serpyllum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to form dense ground cover.
