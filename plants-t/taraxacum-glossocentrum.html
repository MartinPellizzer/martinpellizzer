Taraxacum glossocentrum

Taraxacum glossocentrum, commonly known as the glossocentrum dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of stiff, hairy leaves and a hollow, leafless stem that supports a flower head. The flowers are typically yellow and are surrounded by a crown of bracts, which gives the plant its common name. It is native to southern Africa and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

The plant produces a distinctive seed head that disperses seeds via the wind, a common adaptation in the Asteraceae family.
