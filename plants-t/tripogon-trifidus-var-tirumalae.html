Tripogon trifidus var. tirumalae

Tripogon trifidus var. tirumalae, commonly known as the Tirumalai grass, is a variant of the Tripogon trifidus species native to certain regions of southern India.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet, which is a defining feature of the genus Tripogon.

This variety is noted for its ecological adaptability and is often found in low-nutrient environments.
