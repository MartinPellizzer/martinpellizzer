Trichomanes auriculatum

Trichomanes auriculatum, commonly known as the ear-shaped huperzia, is a fern-like plant belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its creeping rhizome and simple, linear leaves that are often recurved. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its sporophytes produce spores in specialized structures called sporangia, which are arranged in clusters on the underside of the leaves.

Trichomanes auriculatum is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
