Taraxacum officinale proles obovatum

Taraxacum officinale proles obovatum, commonly known as the broad-ovate dandelion, is a variant of the common dandelion characterized by its broad, ovate-shaped leaves.

It is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, and it thrives in a variety of soil types. The plant produces a yellow flower that develops into a fluffy white seed head, which is dispersed by the wind. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a slightly serrated edge, while its stem is usually unbranched and upright.

This species is often found in meadows, lawns, and disturbed soils, and it has a long history of use in traditional medicine.
