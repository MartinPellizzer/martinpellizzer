Thymus pulegioides

Thymus pulegioides, commonly known as narrow-leaved thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically features small, pink or white flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage is used in culinary and medicinal applications, valued for its pungent, slightly minty aroma.

Thymus pulegioides thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and ornamental ground cover in gardens.
