Taraxacum vallis-nibulae

Taraxacum vallis-nibulae, commonly known as the alpine dandelion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and a yellow flower head that develops into a crown of white pappus. This species is adapted to high-altitude environments, thriving in rocky and alpine meadows. It has a taproot system that allows it to survive in nutrient-poor soils.

The plant is often found in mountainous regions across Europe and Asia.
