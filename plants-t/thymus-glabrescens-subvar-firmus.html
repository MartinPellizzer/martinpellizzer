Thymus glabrescens subvar. firmus

Thymus glabrescens subvar. firmus, commonly known as narrowleaf thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, narrow, gray-green leaves that are densely packed along the stems. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its essential oils contain thymol and other compounds, contributing to its use in herbal medicine and culinary applications.
