Tropaeolum hirtifolium

Tropaeolum hirtifolium, commonly known as the hairy leaf sorrel, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically bright green and have a slightly sour taste. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, or yellow, which bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it both aesthetically pleasing and useful in culinary applications.
