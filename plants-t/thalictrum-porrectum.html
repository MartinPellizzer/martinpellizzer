Thalictrum porrectum

Thalictrum porrectum, commonly known as the straight meadow-rue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and delicate, fern-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white, clustered flowers that bloom in early summer. It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Thalictrum porrectum is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
