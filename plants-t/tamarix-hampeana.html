Tamarix hampeana

Tamarix hampeana, commonly known as the Chinese tamarisk, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the family Tamaricaceae and is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces pink to reddish flowers in summer, which are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in dry climates.

Tamarix hampeana is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
