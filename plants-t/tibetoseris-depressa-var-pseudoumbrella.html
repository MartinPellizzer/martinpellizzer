Tibetoseris depressa var. pseudoumbrella

Tibetoseris depressa var. pseudoumbrella, commonly known as the Tibetan false bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of Tibet.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving at elevations above 3,000 meters. The plant features a rosette of basal leaves and produces daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white petals. Its botanical structure is adapted to withstand cold temperatures and strong winds.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is of interest to botanists studying alpine flora.
