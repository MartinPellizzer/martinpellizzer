Trifolium pachypodum

Trifolium pachypodum, commonly known as the broad-leafed clover, is a member of the legume family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, depending on the variety. It is native to regions of southern Africa and is often found in grassland and woodland habitats.

Trifolium pachypodum is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a valuable plant for soil enrichment.
