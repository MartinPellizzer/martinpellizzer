Taraxacum maroccanum

Taraxacum maroccanum, commonly known as Moroccan dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to North Africa, particularly Morocco, and is characterized by its basal rosette of pale green, hairy leaves. The plant produces a yellow flower head that develops into a white, fluffy seed head, typical of the dandelion genus. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in arid and semi-arid regions.

Taraxacum maroccanum is a hardy perennial that is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
