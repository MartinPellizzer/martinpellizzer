Tutcheria villosa var. grandiflora

Tutcheria villosa var. grandiflora, commonly known as the hairy tutcheria, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its scientific name. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
