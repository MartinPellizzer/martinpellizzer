Tapinanthus sulfureus

Tapinanthus sulfureus, commonly known as the sulfur yellow dodder, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies on host plants for nutrients.

It produces small, yellow-orange flowers and has a vine-like structure with thread-like stems that wrap around its hosts. The plant is native to southern Africa and is often found growing on species of Acacia. Its unique appearance and parasitic nature make it an interesting subject in botanical studies.

It plays a role in ecological interactions by influencing the growth and distribution of its host plants.
