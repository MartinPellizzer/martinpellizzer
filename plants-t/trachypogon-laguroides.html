Trachypogon laguroides

Trachypogon laguroides, commonly known as the narrowleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its flowers are small and clustered in panicles, and it produces light, wind-dispersed seeds.

Trachypogon laguroides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
