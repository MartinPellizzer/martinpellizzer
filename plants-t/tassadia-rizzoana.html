Tassadia rizzoana

Tassadia rizzoana, commonly known as the Rizzoana lily, is a rare and striking plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Italy.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem that can reach up to two meters in height, adorned with large, pale pink to white flowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem, contributing to its elegant appearance. Tassadia rizzoana thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and requires a cool, humid climate to flourish.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered an endangered species, making it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
