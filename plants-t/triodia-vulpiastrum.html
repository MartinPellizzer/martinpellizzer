Triodia vulpiastrum

Triodia vulpiastrum, commonly known as the spinifex grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its wiry, spiky leaves and is often found in arid and semi-arid regions. The plant forms dense clumps and is well adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures. Its small, wind-dispersed seeds contribute to its widespread distribution across the landscape.

Triodia vulpiastrum plays a vital role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native fauna.
