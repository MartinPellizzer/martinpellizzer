Tradescantia leiandra var. glandulosa

Tradescantia leiandra var. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, glandular hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, or violet, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This ornamental plant is often used in gardens for its attractive foliage and vibrant floral displays.
