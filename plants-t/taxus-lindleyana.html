Taxus lindleyana

Taxus lindleyana, commonly known as the Mexican yew, is a slow-growing evergreen conifer native to Mexico.

It belongs to the family Taxaceae and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, cone-like structures. The plant produces red, fleshy arils that encase its seeds, which are dispersed by animals. Taxus lindleyana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.

It is also noted for its longevity and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
