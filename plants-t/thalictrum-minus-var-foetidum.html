Thalictrum minus var. foetidum

Thalictrum minus var. foetidum, commonly known as stinking avens, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces a distinctive, unpleasant odor, which is thought to attract specific pollinators. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland habitats.

Its unique appearance and fragrance make it a notable species in ecological and botanical studies.
