Tsiemtani

Tsiemtani, commonly known as the African violets, is a genus of flowering plants native to tropical Africa.

These plants are characterized by their velvety, heart-shaped leaves and showy, clustered flowers in a variety of colors. Tsiemtani species typically grow as herbaceous perennials, often cultivated as houseplants due to their ornamental value. They thrive in bright, indirect light and require well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

The plant's botanical features make it a popular choice for both indoor gardening and botanical study.
