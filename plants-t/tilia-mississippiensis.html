Tilia mississippiensis

Tilia mississippiensis, commonly known as the Mississippi lime, is a small deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Tiliaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are clustered in pendulous inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, oval nut enclosed in a woody husk.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to moist, well-drained soils.
