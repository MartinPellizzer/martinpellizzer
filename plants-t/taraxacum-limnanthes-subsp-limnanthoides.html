Taraxacum limnanthes subsp. limnanthoides

Taraxacum limnanthes subsp.

limnanthoides, commonly known as the water dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its rosette of spoon-shaped, hairy leaves and a flowering head that produces yellow disk flowers.

The plant thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and stream banks, and is adapted to aquatic or semi-aquatic conditions. Its leaves are often submerged or floating, and it has a distinctive, deep root system.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for aquatic insects.
