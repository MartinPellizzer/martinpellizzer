Trichilia moschata

Trichilia moschata, commonly known as the musk tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae, which includes mahogany and related species. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant flowers that emit a musky scent, attracting pollinators like bees and birds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a rough texture.

The tree produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds, contributing to its ecological and potential medicinal significance.
