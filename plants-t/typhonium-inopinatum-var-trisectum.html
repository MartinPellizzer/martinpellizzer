Typhonium inopinatum var. trisectum

Typhonium inopinatum var. trisectum, commonly known as the three-lobed arrowhead, is a tropical plant characterized by its distinctive arrowhead-shaped leaves with three distinct lobes.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia. The plant features a short, rhizomatous stem and produces striking inflorescences with a spadix and a spathe. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine, though it may contain toxic compounds.
