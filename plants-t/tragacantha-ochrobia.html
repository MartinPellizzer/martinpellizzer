Tragacantha ochrobia

Tragacantha ochrobia, commonly known as the yellow tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and hairy stems. The plant produces a sticky resin, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural gum. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are often covered in fine hairs.

Tragacantha ochrobia thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.
