Trigonella cancellata var. arcuata

Trigonella cancellata var. arcuata, commonly known as the curly vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas. It has a creeping growth habit and produces small, elliptical seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and used in traditional medicine.
