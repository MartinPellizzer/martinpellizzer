Thinopyrum caespitosum

Thinopyrum caespitosum, commonly known as goatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces spikelets that are often clustered in dense, cylindrical arrangements, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Thinopyrum caespitosum has been studied for its potential agricultural and ecological roles.
