Taraxacum laudabile

Taraxacum laudabile, commonly known as the noble dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and heart-shaped leaves, which are typical of the dandelion genus. The plant is native to Europe and has been introduced to other parts of the world, often growing in meadows and grassy areas. It produces a distinctive seed head that releases lightweight seeds with parachute-like structures, aiding in wind pollination.

The plant is also noted for its medicinal properties, with various parts used in traditional herbal remedies.
