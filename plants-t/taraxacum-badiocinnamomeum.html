Taraxacum badiocinnamomeum

Taraxacum badiocinnamomeum, commonly known as the cinnamon-hued dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and hairy, lobed leaves. Native to parts of Europe and Asia, this species is often found in meadows and grassy areas. The plant produces a distinctive seed head that releases seeds with parachute-like structures.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and visually striking in natural habitats.
