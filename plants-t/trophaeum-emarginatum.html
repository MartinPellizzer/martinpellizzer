Trophaeum emarginatum

Trophaeum emarginatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved water plantain, is a aquatic plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats such as ponds, ditches, and wet meadows. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are often submerged, with a distinctive notched or wavy edge. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikes, usually emerging above the water surface.

Trophaeum emarginatum plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and amphibians.
