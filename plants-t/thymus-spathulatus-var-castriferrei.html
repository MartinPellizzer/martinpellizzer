Thymus spathulatus var. castriferrei

Thymus spathulatus var. castriferrei, commonly known as the broad-leaf thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It features dense, mat-forming foliage with oval to broadly ovate leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic leaves are often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
