Trisetum varium

Trisetum varium, commonly known as the variable trisetum, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. The plant has slender, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

The inflorescence consists of a single spikelet, which is often surrounded by several bracts, giving it a distinctive appearance.
