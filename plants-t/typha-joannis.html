Typha joannis

Typha joannis, commonly known as the desert cattail, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical flowering spike that emerges from a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant thrives in saline and alkaline soils, making it well adapted to harsh environments. Its rhizomes form dense colonies, contributing to soil stabilization and erosion control.

Typha joannis plays a vital role in desert ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
