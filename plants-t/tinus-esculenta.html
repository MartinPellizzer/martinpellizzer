Tinus esculenta

Tinus esculenta, commonly known as the edible tree, is a species native to certain regions of Africa.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height, with smooth, grey bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by edible, fleshy fruits resembling small, green berries. These fruits are rich in nutrients and are often consumed raw or cooked.

The plant is valued for its ecological and nutritional contributions in local communities.
