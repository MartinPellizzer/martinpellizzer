Taraxacum salonikiense

Taraxacum salonikiense, commonly known as the Saloniki dandelion, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and a yellow composite flower head. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It produces a hollow, wind-dispersed seed stalk known as a pappus.

Taraxacum salonikiense is notable for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
