Theleophyton billardierei

Theleophyton billardierei, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is a perennial herb that produces showy yellow flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in dry, sandy soils. It blooms during the spring and summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Theleophyton billardierei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
