Tropaeolum tenellum

Tropaeolum tenellum, commonly known as the "lesser jewelweed," is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Tropaeolaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically produces small, yellow-orange blooms that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and slightly hairy.

Tropaeolum tenellum is often grown as an ornamental plant for its colorful foliage and flowers.
