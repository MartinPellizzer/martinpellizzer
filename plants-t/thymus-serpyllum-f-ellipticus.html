Thymus serpyllum f. ellipticus

Thymus serpyllum f. ellipticus, commonly known as narrowleaf thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are usually gray-green in color, with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces tiny, pinkish-white flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant ground cover.

Its aromatic leaves are often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
