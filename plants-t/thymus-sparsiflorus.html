Thymus sparsiflorus

Thymus sparsiflorus, commonly known as the sparseflower thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It produces small, pink or purple flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. The plant has aromatic, hairy leaves that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact size and fragrant foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
