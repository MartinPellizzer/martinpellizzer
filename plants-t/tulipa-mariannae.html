Tulipa mariannae

Tulipa mariannae, commonly known as the Marianna tulip, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep red, often with a distinctive purple or red streak at the base. The plant typically grows to a height of 10 to 30 centimeters, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its bulbs are adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

The Marianna tulip is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
