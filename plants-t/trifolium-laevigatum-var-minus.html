Trifolium laevigatum var. minus

Trifolium laevigatum var. minus, commonly known as the lesser clover, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically features trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, often with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, appearing in clusters during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed areas.

This variety is valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a forage plant for livestock.
