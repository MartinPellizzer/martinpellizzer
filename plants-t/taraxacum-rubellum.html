Taraxacum rubellum

Taraxacum rubellum, commonly known as the red-seed dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by distinctive reddish-brown seed heads. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in a variety of soil types, including disturbed areas and lawns. Its leaves are typically basal, forming a rosette, and are covered with fine hairs.

The plant is known for its ability to disperse seeds via the wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.
