Trifolium phleoides var. viciosoi

Trifolium phleoides var. viciosoi, commonly known as the hairy trefoil, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically hairy and have a serrated edge. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassy areas and woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
