Tragia grandifolia

Tragia grandifolia, commonly known as the showy tragia, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Tragia grandifolia thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and medicinal research.
