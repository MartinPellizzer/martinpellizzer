Tephrosia cordofana

Tephrosia cordofana, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its roots are known to contain toxic compounds, which have been used in traditional medicine.

Tephrosia cordofana is also valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a forage plant for livestock.
