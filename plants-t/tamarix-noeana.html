Tamarix noeana

Tamarix noeana, commonly known as the Jordanian tamarisk, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Middle East and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and small, scale-like leaves that are adapted to arid environments. The plant produces small, pink or reddish flowers in clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. Tamarix noeana is drought-tolerant and thrives in sandy or saline soils, making it a hardy species in harsh climates.

Its growth habit and ecological role make it an important plant in desert ecosystems.
