Taraxacum attenuatum

Taraxacum attenuatum, commonly known as the slender dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are narrow and pointed, with a hairy surface. Its flowering stems rise from the center, bearing a single yellow flower head that develops into a fluffy white pappus for wind dispersal.

Taraxacum attenuatum is often found in open, disturbed soils and is adapted to a variety of environmental conditions.
