Tropaeolum

Tropaeolum, commonly known as nasturtium, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Tropaeolaceae.

It is native to South America and is characterized by its vibrant, edible flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces bright orange or yellow blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its stems are often trailing or climbing, making it suitable for hanging baskets or trellises.

Tropaeolum is also valued for its peppery flavor, used in culinary applications and traditional medicine.
