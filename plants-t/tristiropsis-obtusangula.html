Tristiropsis obtusangula

Tristiropsis obtusangula, commonly known as the broad-angled tristiropsis, is a member of the Proteaceae family and is native to South Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its woody stems and dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, white, tubular flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are typically elliptical with a rounded tip and are covered in a fine, powdery coating.

Tristiropsis obtusangula is adapted to dry, sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for coastal gardens.
