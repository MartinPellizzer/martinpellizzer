Taraxacum gulmargense

Taraxacum gulmargense, commonly known as the Gulmarg dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky soils, thriving in cool, temperate climates. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in early summer, followed by the characteristic fluffy white seed heads.

Taraxacum gulmargense is known for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
