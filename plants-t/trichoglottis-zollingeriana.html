Trichoglottis zollingeriana

Trichoglottis zollingeriana, commonly known as Zollinger's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its sympodial growth habit and produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant features linear, grass-like leaves and emits a pleasant fragrance, especially during the evening. It blooms with small, greenish-white flowers that have a distinctive lip, often adorned with intricate patterns.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
