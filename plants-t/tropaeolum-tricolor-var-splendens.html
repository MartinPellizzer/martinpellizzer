Tropaeolum tricolor var. splendens

Tropaeolum tricolor var. splendens, commonly known as the "Jacob's ladder," is a vibrant annual plant native to South America.

It features cascading stems that produce clusters of small, colorful flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant's foliage is heart-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets and garden borders.

Its rapid growth and ability to attract pollinators make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological gardens.
