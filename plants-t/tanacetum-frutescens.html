Tanacetum frutescens

Tanacetum frutescens, commonly known as tansy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its feathery, aromatic leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its strong, camphor-like scent. Tansy is often found in moist, shady areas and has a long history of use in herbal medicine.

Its chemical composition includes compounds such as thujone and camphor, which contribute to its medicinal and pesticidal properties.
