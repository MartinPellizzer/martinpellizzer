Trichomanes floribundum var. vittaria

Trichomanes floribundum var. vittaria, commonly known as the golden huperzia, is a variant of the huperzia fern that exhibits distinctive botanical features.

It is a terrestrial fern belonging to the family Huperziaceae, characterized by its slender, wiry stems and clustered, scale-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices, where it thrives in humid conditions. Its leaves are usually dark green and have a leathery texture, with some varieties displaying a golden or yellowish hue on the underside.

This variant is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in shaded gardens or as a houseplant.
