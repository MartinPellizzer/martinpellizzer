Thymus substriatus

Thymus substriatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically gray-green in color, covered in fine hairs. The plant produces pink or purple flowers in the spring, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its foliage is often used in culinary and medicinal applications due to its strong aromatic properties.
