Tapirira edulis

Tapirira edulis, commonly known as the edible tapirira, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Leguminosae family, which is characterized by its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria. The plant features compound leaves with trifoliate or pinnate structures, and its flowers are typically small and clustered in inflorescences. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

The plant is valued for its edible leaves, which are used in traditional medicine and local cuisines.
