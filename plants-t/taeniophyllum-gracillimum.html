Taeniophyllum gracillimum

Taeniophyllum gracillimum, commonly known as the slender tailed fern, is a member of the Polypodiaceae family.

It is a small, epiphytic fern that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant has delicate, linear fronds that are often curved and covered in fine, silky hairs. It produces small, inconspicuous spore-bearing structures called sporangia on the underside of its leaves.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in specialized tropical gardens.
