Tripterocarpus incisifolia

Tripterocarpus incisifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hard, dense wood and evergreen leaves that are typically narrow and lance-shaped. The plant belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae, which includes many valuable timber species. It often grows in lowland evergreen forests and can reach heights of up to 30 meters.

Tripterocarpus incisifolia is valued for its strong wood, which is used in construction and furniture making.
