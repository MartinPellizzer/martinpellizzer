Teucrium sauvagei

Teucrium sauvagei, commonly known as the Mediterranean germander, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal and culinary uses. The plant features square, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are often grayish-green in color. Its small, clustered flowers are typically blue or purple, blooming in the summer months.

Teucrium sauvagei is often found in rocky or dry soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
