Taxus baccata var. cuspidata

Taxus baccata var. cuspidata, commonly known as the Japanese yew, is a variant of the European yew characterized by its distinctive, sharp-pointed leaves.

It is a slow-growing evergreen conifer that typically reaches heights of 10 to 15 meters. The foliage is dark green and dense, with leaves that are narrow and pointed, giving the plant a unique appearance. It produces small, red, fleshy berries that are toxic to humans and animals.

This species is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
