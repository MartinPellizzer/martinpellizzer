Trifolium hirsutum var. pictum

Trifolium hirsutum var. pictum, commonly known as the hairy trefoil, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves, which are hairy and have a unique pattern of coloration. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by pea-like pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to native plant gardens.

Its botanical features make it valuable for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
