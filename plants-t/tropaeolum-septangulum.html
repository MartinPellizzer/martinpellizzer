Tropaeolum septangulum

Tropaeolum septangulum, commonly known as the seven-chambered nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Tropaeolaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, edible flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, round fruits that split open to reveal seeds, a feature that aids in its dispersal. Its botanical name reflects the septate structure of its fruit, which contains multiple chambers.

Tropaeolum septangulum is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and has mild peppery flavors, making it suitable for culinary use.
