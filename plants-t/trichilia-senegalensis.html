Trichilia senegalensis

Trichilia senegalensis, commonly known as African shea tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and large, palmate leaves. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers followed by woody fruits containing oily seeds. These seeds are the primary source of shea butter, which is widely used in cosmetics and traditional medicine.

Its ecological importance includes soil stabilization and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
