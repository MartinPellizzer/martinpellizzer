Taxillus poissonii

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using sunlight. Its small, white or pink flowers appear in clusters, followed by small, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds.

Taxillus poissonii is native to regions of India and Southeast Asia, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.
