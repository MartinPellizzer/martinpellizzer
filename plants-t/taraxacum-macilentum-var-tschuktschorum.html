Taraxacum macilentum var. tschuktschorum

Taraxacum macilentum var. tschuktschorum, commonly known as the Tschuktschorum dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It features a rosette of narrow, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of white hairs. The plant produces a tall, slender stem topped with a single, yellow composite flower head. It thrives in cold, nutrient-poor soils and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

Its botanical characteristics reflect its ecological niche in high-latitude environments.
