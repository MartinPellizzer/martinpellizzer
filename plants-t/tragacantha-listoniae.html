Tragacantha listoniae

Tragacantha listoniae, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant is known for producing a resinous gum, which has been traditionally used in medicine and as a food additive. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

Due to overharvesting and habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable in some regions.
