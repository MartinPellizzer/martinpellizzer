Tulipa fransoniana subsp. mauriana

Tulipa fransoniana subsp.

mauriana, commonly known as the Mauritius tulip, is a rare and ornamental species native to the island of Mauritius. It is a bulbous perennial plant characterized by its striking, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from deep red to orange.

The leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically emerging after the flowering period. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
