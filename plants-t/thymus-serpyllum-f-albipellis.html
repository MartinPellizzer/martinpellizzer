Thymus serpyllum f. albipellis

Thymus serpyllum f. albipellis, commonly known as the white-haired thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has creeping stems and compact foliage, with leaves that are typically gray-green and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is prized for its aromatic properties and ornamental value in horticulture.
