Trieenea laxiflora

Trieenea laxiflora, commonly known as the slender-flowered cycad, is a unique member of the Cycadaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and a crown of feathery, pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered cones instead of flowers, which is typical of cycads. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its slow growth and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in the wild.
