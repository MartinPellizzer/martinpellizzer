Tanacetum kurdistanicum

Tanacetum kurdistanicum, commonly known as Kurdish tansy, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Kurdistan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments. It is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species in its native range.
