Taraxacum hemicyclum

Taraxacum hemicyclum, commonly known as the Alpine dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are basal, alternate, and often have a wavy or toothed margin.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in spring, producing seeds that are dispersed by the wind.
