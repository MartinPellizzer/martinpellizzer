Tachibota

Tachibota, commonly known as the Japanese evergreen tree, is a species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and can grow up to 20 meters in height. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in the spring. Its bark is smooth and grayish, with a distinctive aromatic scent.

Tachibota is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
