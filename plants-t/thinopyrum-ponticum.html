Thinopyrum ponticum

Thinopyrum ponticum, commonly known as goatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous florets, typical of the Poaceae family. Thinopyrum ponticum is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

It has been studied for its potential in agricultural and ecological applications due to its adaptability and resilience.
