Tommasinia verticillaris var. angustifolia

Tommasinia verticillaris var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tommasinia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The leaves are narrow and arranged in whorls around the stems, giving the plant a unique appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
