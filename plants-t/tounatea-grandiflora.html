Tounatea grandiflora

Tounatea grandiflora, commonly known as the giant flame tree, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy red flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a dramatic visual impact. The plant has a tall, straight trunk and can grow up to 30 meters in height, making it a dominant feature in its natural habitat. Its leaves are compound, consisting of multiple leaflets arranged in a feathery pattern.

Tounatea grandiflora thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.
