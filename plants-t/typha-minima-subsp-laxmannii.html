Typha minima subsp. laxmannii

Typha minima subsp.

laxmannii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cattail, is a wetland plant characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and cylindrical flower spikes. It thrives in shallow freshwater environments such as marshes, ponds, and along the edges of lakes.

The plant has a rhizomatous growth system that allows it to spread vegetatively and form dense colonies. Its flowers are typically brown or reddish in color and are adapted for wind pollination.

The plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
