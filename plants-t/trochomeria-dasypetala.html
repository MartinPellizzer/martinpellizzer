Trochomeria dasypetala

Trochomeria dasypetala, commonly known as the woolly leafed orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pale yellow. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot. Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for botanical gardens and conservation efforts.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
