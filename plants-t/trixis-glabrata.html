Trixis glabrata

Trixis glabrata, commonly known as the smooth trixis, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is adapted to survive in dry conditions with its succulent-like leaves and deep root system.

Trixis glabrata is often found in rocky soils and is used traditionally in some local communities for medicinal purposes.
