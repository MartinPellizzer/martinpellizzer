Tinospora hastata

Tinospora hastata, commonly known as the Indian glorybower, is a climbing shrub belonging to the family Menispermaceae.

It is characterized by its opposite, simple, and often trifoliate leaves, which are usually green with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences, followed by elliptical, yellowish fruits. Tinospora hastata is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including India, China, and Indonesia.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
