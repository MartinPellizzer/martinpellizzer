Taraxacum sphaeroideum

Taraxacum sphaeroideum, commonly known as the globe dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive spherical, yellowish-brown seed head, which is composed of modified leaves called pappus. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It is native to Europe and has been introduced to other parts of the world, often growing in disturbed soils.

The globe dandelion is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
