Tricalysia sapinii

Tricalysia sapinii, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and Eastern Africa.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, featuring a straight trunk and a broad, rounded crown. The leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, with a glossy dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. Its most striking feature is its showy, clustered inflorescences of bright orange or red flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental species in tropical and subtropical landscapes.
