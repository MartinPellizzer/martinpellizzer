Thalictrum flavum proles hybridum

Thalictrum flavum proles hybridum, commonly known as the yellow meadow rue, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.

The flowers are pollinated by insects, and the plant is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
