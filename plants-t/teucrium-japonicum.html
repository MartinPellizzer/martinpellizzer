Teucrium japonicum

Teucrium japonicum, commonly known as Japanese germander, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features upright stems covered in fine hairs and lance-shaped leaves with a grayish-green color. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic foliage and long blooming period make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological landscapes.
