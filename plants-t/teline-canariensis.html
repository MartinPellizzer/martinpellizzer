Teline canariensis

Teline canariensis, commonly known as Canary grass, is a species of annual grass native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, and it thrives in sandy, coastal soils.

Canary grass is often found in disturbed habitats and is adapted to the unique climate of the Canary Islands.
