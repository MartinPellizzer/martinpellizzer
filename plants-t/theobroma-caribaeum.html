Theobroma caribaeum

Theobroma caribaeum, commonly known as the Caribbean cocoa tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Caribbean and northern South America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is closely related to the cacao tree, Theobroma cacao. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into pods containing seeds used for cocoa products. Its leaves are palmately lobed and have a distinctive, slightly hairy texture.

Theobroma caribaeum is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
