Tetratheca rubriseta

Tetratheca rubriseta, commonly known as the red-stemmed tetratheca, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a reddish underside. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers with five petals, which are usually borne in summer. Its stems are distinctive for their bright red color, which contrasts with the green foliage.

Tetratheca rubriseta is adapted to dry environments and is often found in heathlands and open forests.
