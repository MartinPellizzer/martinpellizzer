Trichosporum brevicalyx

Trichosporum brevicalyx, commonly known as the small-leaf trichosporum, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its flowers are actinomorphic, with five petals and numerous stamens.

Trichosporum brevicalyx is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
