Teucrium malenconianum

Teucrium malenconianum, commonly known as the Malenconian germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring slender, erect stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually violet or blue in color, arranged in whorled clusters along the stem. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a slightly aromatic scent when crushed.

Teucrium malenconianum is often found in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and limestone areas.
