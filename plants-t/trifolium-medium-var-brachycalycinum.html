Trifolium medium var. brachycalycinum

Trifolium medium var. brachycalycinum, commonly known as the short-stalked white clover, is a variant of the white clover species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and use in landscaping and as a forage crop.
