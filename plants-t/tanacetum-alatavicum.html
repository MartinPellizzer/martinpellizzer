Tanacetum alatavicum

Tanacetum alatavicum, commonly known as the Alatau goldenroot, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and yellow ray flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in temperate climates. It is known for its medicinal properties, with the roots being used in traditional medicine for their anti-inflammatory and antiseptic effects.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
