Toxicodendron Glaucovirens

Toxicodendron glaucovirens is a rare and striking plant native to southern China and northern Vietnam. Known for its vibrant blue and purple foliage, it captivates botanists and horticultural enthusiasts alike. Despite its aesthetic appeal, it contains urushiol, a potent allergen that can cause severe skin reactions. This plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions. Its unique characteristics make it both a valuable study and a cautionary example of nature's duality.
