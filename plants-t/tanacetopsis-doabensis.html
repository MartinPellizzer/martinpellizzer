Tanacetopsis doabensis

Tanacetopsis doabensis, commonly known as the Doab aster, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its small, white to pale yellow flowers. It has linear to lance-shaped leaves and is often found in open grasslands and along roadsides.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
