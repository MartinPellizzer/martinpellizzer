Tropaeolum luteum

Tropaeolum luteum, commonly known as the yellow Indian cress, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant produces edible seeds that are often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Tropaeolum luteum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
