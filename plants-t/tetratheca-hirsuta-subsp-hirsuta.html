Tetratheca hirsuta subsp. hirsuta

Tetratheca hirsuta subsp.

hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy tetratheca, is a shrub native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs.

The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the spring and summer months. Its fruits are follicles that contain several seeds, and the plant is adapted to grow in dry, sandy soils.

It is often found in heathlands and open woodlands, thriving in regions with a Mediterranean climate.
