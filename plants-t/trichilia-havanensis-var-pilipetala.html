Trichilia havanensis var. pilipetala

Trichilia havanensis var. pilipetala, commonly known as the Cuban yellowwood, is a small to medium-sized tree native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and elliptical leaves that are typically 5 to 10 centimeters long. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
