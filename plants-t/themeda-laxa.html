Themeda laxa

Themeda laxa, commonly known as the slender grass or narrowleaf grass, is a species of perennial grass native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small animals and insects.

Themeda laxa is often found in fire-prone areas, where it can regenerate quickly after disturbances.
