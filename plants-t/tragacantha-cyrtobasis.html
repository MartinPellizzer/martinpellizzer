Tragacantha cyrtobasis

Tragacantha cyrtobasis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces pods that contain seeds, and it is often found in dry, rocky habitats. Its leaves are typically narrow and hairy, adapting to arid environments.

Tragacantha cyrtobasis is not widely cultivated but is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and ecological role.
