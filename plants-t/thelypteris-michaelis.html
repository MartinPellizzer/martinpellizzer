Thelypteris michaelis

Thelypteris michaelis, commonly known as the broad-leaf holly fern, is a species of fern native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, leathery, lance-shaped leaves that are often deeply divided. The fronds are typically evergreen, with a dark green color and a slightly glossy surface. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant reproduces via spores and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
