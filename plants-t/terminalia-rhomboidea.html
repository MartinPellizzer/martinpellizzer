Terminalia rhomboidea

Terminalia rhomboidea, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, rhomboid-shaped fruits, which give it its scientific name. It is valued for its hard, durable wood used in furniture and construction.

The plant also has medicinal uses in traditional African medicine.
