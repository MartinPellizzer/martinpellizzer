Tanacetum daghestanicum

Tanacetum daghestanicum, commonly known as the Daghestan tansy, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are finely divided and aromatic, while its flowers are yellow and clustered in flat-topped inflorescences.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
