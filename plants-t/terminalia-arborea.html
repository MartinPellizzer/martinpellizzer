Terminalia arborea

Terminalia arborea, commonly known as the African wild date or African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, spiny fruits containing edible seeds.

It is often found in moist woodlands and is valued for its timber and traditional medicinal uses.
