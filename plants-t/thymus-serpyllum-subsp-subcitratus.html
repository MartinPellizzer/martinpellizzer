Thymus serpyllum subsp. subcitratus

Thymus serpyllum subsp.

subcitratus, commonly known as lemon thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features creeping stems and small, aromatic leaves that emit a citrusy scent.

The plant produces pink to lavender flowers in the spring, which attract pollinators like bees. Its foliage is often used in culinary applications for its distinctive flavor.

Botanically, it is characterized by its dense growth habit and essential oil composition rich in thymol and other terpenes.
