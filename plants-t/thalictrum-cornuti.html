Thalictrum cornuti

Thalictrum cornuti, commonly known as the Cornut's meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is known for its delicate appearance and ecological value to pollinators.

Thalictrum cornuti is often used in gardens for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
