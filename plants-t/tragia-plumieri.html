Tragia plumieri

Tragia plumieri, commonly known as the velvetleaf tragia, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its rough, often hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by distinctive, spiny fruits. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape and have a rough texture.

Tragia plumieri is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and agricultural fields.
