Trachypogon stipoides

Trachypogon stipoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf grass or slender false brome, is a perennial grass species native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open woodlands, meadows, and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. Its reproductive strategy involves wind pollination, which is common among grasses.

Trachypogon stipoides plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
