Taraxacum canaliculatum f. potens

Taraxacum canaliculatum f. potens, commonly known as the potent dandelion, is a variant of the common dandelion characterized by its larger, more robust leaves and yellow flower heads.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the family Asteraceae, known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. The plant produces a distinctive seed head composed of numerous parachute-like seeds, which are dispersed by the wind. Its leaves are typically serrated and have a waxy coating, while the flowers are bright yellow and bloom in early spring.

This plant is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a common weed in many regions.
