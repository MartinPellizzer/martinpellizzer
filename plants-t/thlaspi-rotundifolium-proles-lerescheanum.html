Thlaspi rotundifolium proles lerescheanum

Thlaspi rotundifolium proles lerescheanum, commonly known as the Lereschean bladderwort, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, rounded leaves and is typically found in moist, calcareous soils. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It is a semi-succulent species, capable of storing water in its fleshy leaves.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
