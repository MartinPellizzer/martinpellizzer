Triodia bunicola

Triodia bunicola, commonly known as the spinifex grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, wiry growth habit and the presence of sharp, spiny leaves. The plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in sandy or gravelly soils. It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various desert organisms.

Triodia bunicola is often found in spinifex plains and is a key component of the vegetation in inland Australian ecosystems.
