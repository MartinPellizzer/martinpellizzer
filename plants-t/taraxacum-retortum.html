Taraxacum retortum

Taraxacum retortum, commonly known as the curled dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, tightly curled leaves that form a rosette near the ground. The plant produces a yellow flower head that develops into a white, fluffy seed ball, typical of the dandelion genus. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in grassy areas and disturbed soils.

Taraxacum retortum is known for its resilience and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
