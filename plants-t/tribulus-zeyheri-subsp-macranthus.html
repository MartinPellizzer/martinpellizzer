Tribulus zeyheri subsp. macranthus

Tribulus zeyheri subsp.

macranthus, commonly known as the large-flowered cat's claw, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a perennial shrub characterized by its woody stems and small, spiny leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

The species is notable for its medicinal uses in traditional African medicine.
