Tectaria adenophora

Tectaria adenophora, commonly known as the hairy shield fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive fronds that are covered in fine, hairy structures, giving it a unique appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are often thick and fleshy, aiding in water storage and nutrient absorption.

Tectaria adenophora is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and is often found in humid, low-light conditions.
