Trichoglottis mimica

Trichoglottis mimica, commonly known as the Mimic Orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which resembles the shape and color of a butterfly, aiding in pollination by attracting specific insects. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in tropical rainforests. Its flowers are usually small, with a single lip that is often brightly colored.

Trichoglottis mimica is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological balance of its native habitat.
