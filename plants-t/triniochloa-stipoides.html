Triniochloa stipoides

Triniochloa stipoides, commonly known as the slender swamp grass, is a perennial grass species native to wetland and marshy habitats in South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, demonstrating its adaptability to aquatic environments. Its botanical features include fibrous roots and a compact growth habit.

Triniochloa stipoides plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
