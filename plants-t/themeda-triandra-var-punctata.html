Themeda triandra var. punctata

Themeda triandra var. punctata, commonly known as the dotted grass or punctate kangaroo grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with distinctive dark green markings. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear pale blue or white flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
