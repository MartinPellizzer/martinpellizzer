Triticum vulgare var. lutescens

Triticum vulgare var. lutescens, commonly known as the golden wheat, is a variant of common wheat characterized by its yellowish grain color.

It is an annual grass that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with slender, hollow stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle, each bearing small, two-lobed florets. Its seeds, which are the primary product, are used in various culinary and agricultural applications.

The plant thrives in temperate climates and is cultivated for its high yield and nutritional value.
