Tripogon munnarensis

Tripogon munnarensis, commonly known as the Munnar grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and rocky slopes. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet, which is a unique feature among grasses.

Tripogon munnarensis is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
