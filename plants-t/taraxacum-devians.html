Taraxacum devians

Taraxacum devians, commonly known as the mountain dandelion, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant features a hairy, upright stem with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. Its flowers are yellow, forming a single composite head that opens in late spring to early summer.

Taraxacum devians is adapted to rocky and alpine environments, showcasing resilience to cold and harsh conditions.
