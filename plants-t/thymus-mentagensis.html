Thymus mentagensis

Thymus mentagensis, commonly known as the Mentagene thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, low-growing habit and aromatic, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its essential oils contain thymol and other compounds, contributing to its use in herbal medicine and culinary applications.
