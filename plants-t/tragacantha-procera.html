Tragacantha procera

Tragacantha procera, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems, which are often covered in a thick, resinous coating. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by pod-like fruits containing seeds. Its resin, known as tragacanth, has been historically used in medicine and as a thickening agent.

Tragacantha procera is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, making it a hardy and resilient species.
