Tragacantha hirsuta

Tragacantha hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy tragacanth, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive pod fruits. The plant produces a viscous gum from its roots, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, temperate climates.

Due to its ecological and historical significance, it is sometimes cultivated for its resin and ornamental value.
