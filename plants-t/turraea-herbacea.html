Turraea herbacea

Turraea herbacea, commonly known as the African milk tree, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its milky sap, which is toxic and can cause skin irritation. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Turraea herbacea is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
