Theobroma integerrimum

Theobroma integerrimum, commonly known as the Brazilian nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and clusters of yellowish-white flowers. The tree produces elongated, woody pods that contain seeds rich in oils and nutrients. These seeds, known as Brazil nuts, are the source of the plant's commercial value.

Theobroma integerrimum plays a significant role in both ecological and economic systems of the region.
