Taraxacum croceum subsp. repletum

Taraxacum croceum subsp.

repletum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves that are typically lanceolate and covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers in a single terminal head, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in disturbed soils and can often be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

The species is notable for its ability to spread vegetatively through its extensive root system.
