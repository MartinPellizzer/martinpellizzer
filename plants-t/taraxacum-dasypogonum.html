Taraxacum dasypogonum

Taraxacum dasypogonum, commonly known as the woolly dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and bright yellow composite flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces a distinctive, hollow, wind-dispersed seed head known as a pappus. It is native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

Taraxacum dasypogonum is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
