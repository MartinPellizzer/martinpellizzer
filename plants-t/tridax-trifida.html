Tridax trifida

Tridax trifida, commonly known as the common horseweed, is an annual flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, leafy stems and small yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and other open areas. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a rough texture.

Tridax trifida is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and vegetative reproduction.
