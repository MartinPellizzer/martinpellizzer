Tordylioides

Tordylioides, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

These plants are characterized by their showy, yellow to orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. Tordylioides species are native to South America, particularly Brazil, and are often found in moist, shaded environments. They have alternate, simple leaves that are usually lance-shaped or ovate, and their stems are often hairy.

The genus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral displays.
