Tilia × cinerascens

Tilia × cinerascens, commonly known as the grey-leaf lime, is a hybrid deciduous tree belonging to the Tiliaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often tinged with a silvery or greyish hue on the upper surface. The tree produces small, fragrant yellow flowers in early summer, which are clustered in drooping inflorescences. Its bark is smooth and light grey, becoming fissured with age, and it is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly planted in urban landscapes for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
