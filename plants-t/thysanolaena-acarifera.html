Thysanolaena acarifera

Thysanolaena acarifera, commonly known as the silky grass or slender grass, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae).

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a silky texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of many grass species. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential agricultural applications.
