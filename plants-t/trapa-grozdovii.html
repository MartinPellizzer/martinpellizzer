Trapa grozdovii

Trapa grozdovii, commonly known as the clustered water chestnut, is a aquatic plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its floating leaves that are deeply lobed and its distinctive spiny fruits. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom above the water surface. Its fruits are edible and have a nutty flavor, often used in traditional cuisine.

Trapa grozdovii plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.
