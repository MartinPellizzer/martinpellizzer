Tritonia cooperi subsp. quadrialata

Tritonia cooperi subsp.

quadrialata, commonly known as the four-lobed bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its bell-shaped, tubular flowers that are typically blue or violet in color.

The plant features lanceolate leaves with prominent veins and a rhizomatous growth habit. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. Tritonia cooperi subsp.

quadrialata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in woodland gardens.
