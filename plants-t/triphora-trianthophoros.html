Triphora trianthophoros

Triphora trianthophoros, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and small, fleshy leaves. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, which bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and unique blooming period, it is often cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
