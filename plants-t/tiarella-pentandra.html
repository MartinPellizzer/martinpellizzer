Tiarella pentandra

Tiarella pentandra, commonly known as the foamflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It features rhizomatous growth and produces clusters of delicate, white, bell-shaped flowers in early spring. The foliage consists of heart-shaped, lobed leaves that are often variegated with silver or white markings. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland settings.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
