Tephrosia schiedeana

Tephrosia schiedeana, commonly known as the African blue pea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring compound leaves and clusters of small, blue or purple flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to dry, arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
