Turnera tomentosa

Turnera tomentosa, commonly known as the wooly turnera, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are usually ovate or elliptical with a dense covering of fine, white hairs.

Turnera tomentosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
