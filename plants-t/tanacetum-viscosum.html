Tanacetum viscosum

Tanacetum viscosum, commonly known as sticky tansy, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered with a sticky resinous substance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its aromatic foliage and flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

However, it is considered invasive in some regions due to its rapid spread and allelopathic properties.
