Tephrosia decora

Tephrosia decora, commonly known as the ornamental tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height, featuring compound leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces yellow or white flowers that bloom in spring and summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine, and the plant is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Tephrosia decora is also noted for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it a hardy choice for landscaping.
