Thymus hadzhievii

Thymus hadzhievii, commonly known as the Hadzhiev thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic leaves and square stems. The plant produces small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are fragrant and often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Thymus hadzhievii thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and alpine landscapes.
