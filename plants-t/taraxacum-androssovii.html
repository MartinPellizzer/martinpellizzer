Taraxacum androssovii

Taraxacum androssovii, commonly known as the Androsovii dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are typically hairy and have a waxy coating. Its flowers are yellow and develop into a distinctive spherical seed head, known as a pappus, which aids in wind dispersal.

Taraxacum androssovii is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing a robust root system for water retention.
