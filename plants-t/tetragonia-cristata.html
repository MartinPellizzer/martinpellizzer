Tetragonia cristata

Tetragonia cristata, commonly known as the "crystal plant" or "cristata spinach," is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that features succulent, fleshy leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in arid and saline environments, making it a hardy species. Its leaves are edible and have a slightly tangy flavor, often used in salads or as a garnish.
