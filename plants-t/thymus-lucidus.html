Thymus lucidus

Thymus lucidus, commonly known as the shining thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features small, fragrant leaves that are typically gray-green in color, with a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed. The plant produces tiny, pink or white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Thymus lucidus is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its use in culinary and medicinal applications.
