Taraxacum mongoliforme

Taraxacum mongoliforme, commonly known as the Mongolian dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant features a stout, hairy stem that branches near the base, with bright yellow ray florets surrounding a disk of tubular flowers. Its leaves are basal, spoon-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs, while the root system is fibrous and rhizomatous.

This species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in harsh climates.
