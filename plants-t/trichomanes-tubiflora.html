Trichomanes tubiflora

Trichomanes tubiflora, commonly known as the tubular maidenhair fern, is a member of the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically light green and covered in tiny, scale-like structures. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its spore-producing structures, called sporangia, are arranged in clusters along the fertile fronds.

Trichomanes tubiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
