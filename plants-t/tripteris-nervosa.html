Tripteris nervosa

Tripteris nervosa, commonly known as the veined tripteris, is a tropical evergreen tree belonging to the family Tripteridaceae.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with a distinctive venation pattern, which gives it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments in Central and South America. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are followed by winged fruits that aid in seed dispersal.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
