Tapeinochilos recurvatus

Tapeinochilos recurvatus, commonly known as the curly leaved orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its unique, spirally twisted leaves and small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests. Its growth habit is epiphytic, meaning it attaches itself to trees without being parasitic.

The botanical features of Tapeinochilos recurvatus make it a striking and relatively rare ornamental plant in horticulture.
