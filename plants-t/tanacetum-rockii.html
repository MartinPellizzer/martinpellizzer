Tanacetum rockii

Tanacetum rockii, commonly known as the Chinese tansy, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant features finely divided, silvery-green leaves and small yellow flower heads that resemble daisies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine environments.

Tanacetum rockii is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
