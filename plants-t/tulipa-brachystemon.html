Tulipa brachystemon

Tulipa brachystemon, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tulip, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Tulipa.

It is native to Turkey and is characterized by its slender, strap-like leaves and showy, cup-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 20 to 30 centimeters and blooms in the spring with vibrant colors ranging from red to yellow. Its bulbs are adapted for storage and can remain dormant during unfavorable conditions.

The botanical features of T. brachystemon make it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.
