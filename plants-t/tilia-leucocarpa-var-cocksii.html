Tilia leucocarpa var. cocksii

Tilia leucocarpa var. cocksii, commonly known as the White-barked Lime, is a variant of the Tilia genus native to specific regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive white bark, which becomes more pronounced with age, and heart-shaped leaves with a serrated margin. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are typically clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in traditional medicine.
