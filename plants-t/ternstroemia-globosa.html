Ternstroemia globosa

Ternstroemia globosa, commonly known as the globular hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive globular shape and clusters of showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are usually opposite, simple, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Ternstroemia globosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
