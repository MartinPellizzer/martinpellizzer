Turbinicarpus hoferi

Turbinicarpus hoferi, commonly known as the Hofer's barrel cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring, which are clustered at the top of the stems. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated by succulent enthusiasts.
