Thlaspi sylvestre subsp. vogesiacum

Thlaspi sylvestre subsp.

vogesiacum, commonly known as the Voges rockcress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family. It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and features basal leaves that are often lobed or divided.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability to harsh environments and its role in stabilizing soil in mountainous areas.
