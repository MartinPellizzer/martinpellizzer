Tylophora secamonoides

Tylophora secamonoides, commonly known as the Indian moonseed, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are often clustered in umbels, followed by follicle fruits containing elongated seeds. It is valued in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and pharmacological contexts.
