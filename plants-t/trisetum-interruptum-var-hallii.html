Trisetum interruptum var. hallii

Trisetum interruptum var. hallii, commonly known as Hall's interrupted grass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of small, spikelet-bearing branches.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
