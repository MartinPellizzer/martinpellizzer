Taraxacum darvasicum

Taraxacum darvasicum, commonly known as the Darvasian dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Central Asia and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and heart-shaped basal leaves. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. It produces a distinctive seed head that releases lightweight seeds with parachute-like structures for wind dispersal.

Taraxacum darvasicum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
