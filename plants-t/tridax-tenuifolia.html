Tridax tenuifolia

Tridax tenuifolia, commonly known as slenderleaf tridax, is an annual herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features a sprawling growth habit with slender, hairy stems and alternate leaves that are lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by fluffy, white to grayish seeds that are easily dispersed by wind. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Tridax tenuifolia is considered a weed in many regions due to its rapid growth and invasive tendencies.
