Trichoglottis rosea

Trichoglottis rosea, commonly known as the pink lady's tongs, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant features linear, dark green leaves that are arranged in a fan shape. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity and well-draining soil.

Trichoglottis rosea is prized for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
