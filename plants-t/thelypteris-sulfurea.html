Thelypteris sulfurea

Thelypteris sulfurea, commonly known as the golden shield fern, is a species of fern native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bright yellow to orange fronds that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its fronds are pinnately divided, with a leathery texture and a slightly curled appearance.

Thelypteris sulfurea is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
