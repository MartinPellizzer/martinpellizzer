Triticum cynosuroides

Triticum cynosuroides, commonly known as wild wheat, is a species of grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces small, straw-colored grains that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Due to its historical significance, it has been studied for its potential in agricultural and ecological restoration efforts.
