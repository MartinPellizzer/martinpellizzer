Tetrastigma pilosum

Tetrastigma pilosum, commonly known as the hairy raisin vine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in humid, shaded environments.
