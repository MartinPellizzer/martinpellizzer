Taraxacum beckeri

Taraxacum beckeri, commonly known as the Becker's dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant features a rosette of spoon-shaped, dark green leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are yellow and consist of both ray florets and disk florets, typical of the dandelion genus.

Taraxacum beckeri is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands.
