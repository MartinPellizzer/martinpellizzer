Theobroma balaense

Theobroma balaense, commonly known as the Balaense cocoa, is a species of the Theobroma genus native to South America.

It is a small evergreen tree that produces pods containing seeds used in the production of cocoa products. The plant thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil. Its leaves are large, glossy, and elliptical in shape, while its flowers are typically white or pale yellow.

Theobroma balaense is valued for its potential in sustainable agriculture and its role in biodiversity conservation.
