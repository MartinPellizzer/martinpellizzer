Thelypodium integrifolium var. complanatum

Thelypodium integrifolium var. complanatum, commonly known as the flatleaf thelypodium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. The plant features basal leaves that are lanceolate and have smooth, entire margins. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikes, with white to pale blue petals.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for small invertebrates.
