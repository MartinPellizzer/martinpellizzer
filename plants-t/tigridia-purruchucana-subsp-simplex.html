Tigridia purruchucana subsp. simplex

Tigridia purruchucana subsp.

simplex, commonly known as the Mexican fairy lantern, is a member of the Iridaceae family and is native to Mexico. It is a bulbous perennial plant that produces showy, orange-red flowers with a distinctive, lantern-like shape.

The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a bulbous base.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
