Tapinanthus rubromarginatus

Tapinanthus rubromarginatus, commonly known as the red-marginated dodder, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on host plants for nutrients.

It produces small, yellowish flowers and has a vine-like appearance with thread-like stems that wrap around its hosts. The plant is native to Africa and is often found growing on a variety of host species, including grasses and shrubs. Its flowers are typically arranged in clusters and bloom during the dry season.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can negatively impact the growth of its host plants, making it a concern in agricultural settings.
