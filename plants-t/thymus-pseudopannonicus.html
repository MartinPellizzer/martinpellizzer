Thymus pseudopannonicus

Thymus pseudopannonicus, commonly known as the Pannonian thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Pannonian Basin in Central Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are fragrant and contain essential oils, making it valuable for both culinary and medicinal uses.

Thymus pseudopannonicus thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
