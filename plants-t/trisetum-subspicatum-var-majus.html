Trisetum subspicatum var. majus

Trisetum subspicatum var. majus, commonly known as the tall feather grass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often hairy along the edges. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically green to reddish in color. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of ponds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
