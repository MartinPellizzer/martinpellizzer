Trichosanthes tuberosa

Trichosanthes tuberosa, commonly known as the Chinese lantern gourd, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its large, orange, balloon-like fruits that resemble lanterns, which are often used in traditional Chinese medicine. The plant produces yellow flowers and has a climbing habit, with hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. Its tuberous roots are edible and have a sweet, nutty flavor.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has cultural significance in various regions.
