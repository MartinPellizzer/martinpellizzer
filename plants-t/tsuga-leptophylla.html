Tsuga leptophylla

Tsuga leptophylla, commonly known as the dwarf hemlock, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and its distinctive needle-like leaves that are shorter and more pointed compared to other hemlock species. The plant has dark green, glossy foliage that remains throughout the year, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Tsuga leptophylla is valued for its ecological role in providing habitat for various wildlife species.
