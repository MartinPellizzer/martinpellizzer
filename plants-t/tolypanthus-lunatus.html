Tolypanthus lunatus

Tolypanthus lunatus, commonly known as the moonflower vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, white, nocturnal flowers that open at dusk and close at dawn. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and emits a sweet fragrance to attract pollinators. Its flowers are tubular and have a unique, moon-like appearance, which gives it its common name.

Tolypanthus lunatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
