Thymus embergeri

Thymus embergeri, commonly known as Emberger's thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic essential oils and square stems. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its fragrant, minty aroma.

Emberger's thyme is often used in traditional herbal medicine and as a culinary herb due to its medicinal and aromatic properties.
