Tanacetum chiliophyllum

Tanacetum chiliophyllum, commonly known as the blue tansy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, blue-green leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and ragworts. It is often cultivated for its essential oils, which are used in perfumery and aromatherapy.

Tanacetum chiliophyllum prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.
