Taxillus robinsonii

Taxillus robinsonii, commonly known as the mistletoe of the genus Taxillus, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches itself to the branches of host trees.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while it provides some sugars through photosynthesis using its own leaves. The plant produces small, white or yellowish berries that are typically consumed by birds, which then disperse the seeds. Taxillus robinsonii is found in various regions of South America, particularly in tropical and subtropical forests.

Its ecological role includes contributing to the biodiversity of its habitat and influencing the growth of its host trees.
