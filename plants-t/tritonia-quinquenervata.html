Tritonia quinquenervata

Tritonia quinquenervata, commonly known as the five-veined tritonia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red. The leaves are typically lance-shaped with five distinct veins, giving the plant its scientific name. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in shaded forest undergrowth.
