Thlaspi rhaeticum

Thlaspi rhaeticum, commonly known as the Alpine cabbages or rock cabbages, is a flowering plant species belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to mountainous regions of Europe and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant thrives in rocky and alpine environments, often growing in well-drained soils at high altitudes. Thlaspi rhaeticum is known for its ability to tolerate harsh climatic conditions, making it a hardy species.
