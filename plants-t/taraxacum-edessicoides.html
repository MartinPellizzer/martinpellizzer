Taraxacum edessicoides

Taraxacum edessicoides, commonly known as the Edessa dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated, hairy leaves and a yellow composite flower head that develops into a spherical seed ball. Native to southeastern Europe and parts of western Asia, it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant has a taproot system and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

It is a perennial species with a long flowering period, typically from late spring to early autumn.
