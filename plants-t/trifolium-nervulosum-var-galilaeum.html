Trifolium nervulosum var. galilaeum

Trifolium nervulosum var. galilaeum, commonly known as the Galilean clover, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets with a hairy texture. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
