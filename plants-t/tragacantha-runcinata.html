Tragacantha runcinata

Tragacantha runcinata, commonly known as the Armenian tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Turkey.

It is a perennial herb that produces yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant has a taproot system and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves with a hairy surface. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

The plant is known for its latex, which has been used in traditional medicine and as a natural thickening agent.
