Taraxacum aurosuloides

Taraxacum aurosuloides, commonly known as the golden marguerite, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and a yellow flower head composed of numerous ray florets. Native to the Mediterranean region, this species thrives in sunny, well-drained soils. The plant is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats, and it is known for its bright, cheerful appearance.

It is a perennial species that produces seeds through wind dispersal, contributing to its widespread distribution.
