Tetratheca glandulosa var. angustifolia

Tetratheca glandulosa var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tetratheca, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features small, white, five-petaled flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during spring. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a distinctive glandular texture on their surface. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or sandy environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to native wildlife.
