Tilia tomentosa var. obliqua

Tilia tomentosa var. obliqua, commonly known as the woolly lime or oblique lime, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly silver-gray foliage that provides a distinctive appearance. The leaves are typically heart-shaped with a serrated margin and a thick, leathery texture. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are clustered in drooping inflorescences.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
