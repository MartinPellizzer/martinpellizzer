Tripteris amplexicaulis

Tripteris amplexicaulis, commonly known as the clasping tripteris, is a tropical plant native to the forests of Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its alternate, simple, and often clustered leaves that wrap around the stems, giving it its common name. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, arranged in panicles, and it produces woody fruits containing several seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
