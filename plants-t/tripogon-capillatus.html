Tripogon capillatus

Tripogon capillatus, commonly known as the slender spikegrass, is a perennial grass native to Africa and parts of the Middle East.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and fine, thread-like leaves that give it a delicate appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically green or pale blue in color. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil.
