Thelocactus lausseri

Thelocactus lausseri, commonly known as the Lausser's thorn cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are covered in sharp, spiny areoles. The plant produces bright yellow flowers during the summer months, which are borne on short, erect pedicels. Its roots are adapted to absorb and store water efficiently in its harsh environment.

Thelocactus lausseri is a slow-growing cactus that is often cultivated for its unique appearance and resilience.
