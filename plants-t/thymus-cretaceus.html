Thymus cretaceus

Thymus cretaceus, commonly known as the Cretan thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, small, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Thymus cretaceus is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its use in herbal medicine and culinary applications.
