Thymus ciliatopubescens f. alba

Thymus ciliatopubescens f. alba, commonly known as white thyme, is a cultivar of the thyme plant characterized by its pale or white flowers.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic herbs. The plant is typically low-growing with slender stems and small, fragrant leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and mild, lemony scent.
