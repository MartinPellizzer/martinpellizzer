Tetragonocalamus quadrangularis f. castillonis

Tetragonocalamus quadrangularis f. castillonis, commonly known as the Castillon's bamboo, is a unique variant of the quadrangular bamboo species.

It is characterized by its distinctive quadrilateral stem shape, which gives it its scientific name. The plant is native to specific regions in Southeast Asia, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. Its foliage consists of long, slender leaves that are often arranged in a dense, bushy manner.

This bamboo variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its role in traditional horticulture.
