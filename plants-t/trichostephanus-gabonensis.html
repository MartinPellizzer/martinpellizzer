Trichostephanus gabonensis

Trichostephanus gabonensis, commonly known as the Gabon spiderflower, is a flowering plant native to Central and Southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its unique, spiky inflorescence composed of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Trichostephanus gabonensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
