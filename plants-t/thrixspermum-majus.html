Thrixspermum majus

Thrixspermum majus, commonly known as the woolly thrixspermum, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that covers its stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, often in the spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and plant enthusiasts alike.
