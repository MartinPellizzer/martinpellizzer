Trifolium campestre subsp. paphium

Trifolium campestre subsp.

paphium, commonly known as the red clover, is a flowering plant in the legume family. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets.

The plant produces small, pink or red flowers that are clustered in spherical heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

This subspecies is valued for its role in soil nitrogen fixation and as a forage crop.
