Taxus baccata f. pendula

Taxus baccata f. pendula, commonly known as the weeping yew, is a striking variant of the common yew.

It is characterized by its cascading, drooping branches that create a unique, ornamental shape. The plant retains its needle-like foliage year-round, displaying a deep green hue throughout the seasons. It produces small, red berries that are toxic to humans and animals.

This cultivar is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
