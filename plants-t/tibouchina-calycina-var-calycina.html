Tibouchina calycina var. calycina

Tibouchina calycina var. calycina, commonly known as the Christmas bush, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy bracts that resemble flowers, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or white true flowers that bloom in clusters, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

This plant is often cultivated for its vibrant foliage and is popular in landscaping due to its adaptability and aesthetic value.
