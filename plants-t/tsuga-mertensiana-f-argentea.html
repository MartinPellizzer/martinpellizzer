Tsuga mertensiana f. argentea

Tsuga mertensiana f. argentea, commonly known as the silver white fir, is a rare and striking variant of the white fir.

It is characterized by its distinctive silvery-white variegation on the foliage, which gives it a unique and eye-catching appearance. This form is typically found in high-altitude regions of the Pacific Northwest, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. The leaves are needle-like, with a leathery texture and a pale underside that contributes to its ornamental value.

It is a slow-growing evergreen that is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
