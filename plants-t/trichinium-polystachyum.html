Trichinium polystachyum

Trichinium polystachyum, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae).

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and clusters of golden-yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. Its rapid growth rate makes it both a valuable ornamental plant and a potential invasive species in certain regions.

The leaves are long, narrow, and often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
