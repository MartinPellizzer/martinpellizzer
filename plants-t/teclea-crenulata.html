Teclea crenulata

Teclea crenulata, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Ethiopia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in rocky or forested areas. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom during the dry season. Its leaves are ovate with wavy edges and are often covered in fine hairs.

Teclea crenulata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
