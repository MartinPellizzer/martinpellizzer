Tacca dubia

Tacca dubia, commonly known as the "bat plant" or "devil's tongue," is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, bat-like flowers that are usually dark purple or black with a white or yellowish throat. The plant has thick, fleshy rhizomes and broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. Its flowers bloom in clusters and emit a strong, unpleasant odor, which is thought to attract flies for pollination.

Tacca dubia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance, though it requires a warm, humid environment to thrive.
