Thymbra hirsutissima

Thymbra hirsutissima, commonly known as wild thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and small pink or white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its essential oils, derived from the leaves, are used in perfumery and traditional medicine.

Thymbra hirsutissima is also valued as a low-maintenance ornamental plant in gardens.
