Trigonia paniculata

Trigonia paniculata, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stem and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pink in color. Its seeds are enclosed in a fleshy, red aril, which is a feature that aids in seed dispersal.

Trigonia paniculata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in various cultures.
