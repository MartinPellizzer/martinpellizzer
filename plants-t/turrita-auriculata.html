Turrita auriculata

Turrita auriculata, commonly known as the auriculate turrita, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Campanulaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive auriculate (ear-shaped) leaves and bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. Native to the Mediterranean region, this plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds, facilitating its propagation.
