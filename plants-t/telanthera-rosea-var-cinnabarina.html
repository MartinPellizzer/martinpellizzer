Telanthera rosea var. cinnabarina

Telanthera rosea var. cinnabarina, commonly known as the red-stemmed telanthera, is a striking flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a contrasting yellow or orange center. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green, providing a lush backdrop to its showy blossoms. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and as a specimen plant in tropical landscapes.
