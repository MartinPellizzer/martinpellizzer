Taraxacum alaskanum

Taraxacum alaskanum, commonly known as the Alaska dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the sunflower family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and a yellow composite flower head that develops into a white pappus for wind dispersal. Native to the northern regions of North America, it thrives in moist, disturbed soils and alpine environments. The plant is perennial, with a taproot system that allows it to survive in harsh climates.

Its botanical features make it an important component of alpine and subarctic ecosystems.
