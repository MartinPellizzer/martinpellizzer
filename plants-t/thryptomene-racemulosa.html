Thryptomene racemulosa

Thryptomene racemulosa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf thryptomene, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in heathlands and open forests, thriving in well-drained soils. It is often found in regions with a Mediterranean climate, such as southern Australia.

Thryptomene racemulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native gardens.
