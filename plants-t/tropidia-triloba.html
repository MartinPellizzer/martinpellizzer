Tropidia triloba

Tropidia triloba, commonly known as the three-lobed orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, fragrant flowers with three distinct lobes. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Tropidia triloba is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
