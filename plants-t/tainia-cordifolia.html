Tainia cordifolia

Tainia cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces an inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe, typical of the arum family. Its rhizomes are edible and have been traditionally used as a starch source.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its culinary uses.
