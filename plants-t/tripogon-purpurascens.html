Tripogon purpurascens

Tripogon purpurascens, commonly known as the red-stemmed grass or purple-stemmed grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive reddish-purple stems that become more intense as the plant matures. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a smooth texture, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Tripogon purpurascens is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and is relatively drought-tolerant.
