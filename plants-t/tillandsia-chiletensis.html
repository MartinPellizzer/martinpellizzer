Tillandsia chiletensis

Tillandsia chiletensis, commonly known as the Chilote air plant, is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is native to Chile.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on other plants without deriving nutrients from them. The plant features rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help in absorbing moisture from the air. It produces striking inflorescences with showy, violet or purple flowers that attract pollinators.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
