Triticum aestivum subsp. transcaucasicum

Triticum aestivum subsp.

transcaucasicum, commonly known as the Transcaucasian wheat, is a subspecies of wheat native to the Caucasus region. It is characterized by its slender stalks and small, spike-like inflorescence.

The plant typically grows in temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soils. Its grains are used for flour production, similar to other wheat species.

This subspecies is valued for its adaptability and resilience to various environmental conditions.
