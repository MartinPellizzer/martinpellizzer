Taraxacum nigritum

Taraxacum nigritum, commonly known as black dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of dark green, hairy leaves that are often tinged with purple. The plant produces a single, hollow, hairless stem that bears a bright yellow flower head, which develops into a white, fluffy seed head. It is native to Europe and is often found in dry, disturbed soils such as roadsides and fields.

The species is notable for its unique dark-colored leaves and its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
