Trachydium hamelianum

Trachydium hamelianum, commonly known as the common tansy, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its yellow, daisy-like flowers and a distinctive, aromatic, and slightly toxic scent. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is native to Europe and parts of Asia. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Trachydium hamelianum is often found in meadows, pastures, and along roadsides, where it thrives in sunny conditions.
