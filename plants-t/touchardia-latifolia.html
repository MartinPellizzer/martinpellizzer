Touchardia latifolia

Touchardia latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf touchardia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of China, Russia, and Kazakhstan. The plant is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
