Thelymitra uliginosa

Thelymitra uliginosa, commonly known as the bog orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to Australia.

It typically grows in wet, boggy environments and is characterized by its dark green, strap-like leaves. The plant produces a single, showy spike of pale to dark purple flowers, which bloom from late winter to early spring. Thelymitra uliginosa is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

Its presence is often an indicator of healthy wetland ecosystems.
