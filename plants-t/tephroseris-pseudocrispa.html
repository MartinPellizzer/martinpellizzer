Tephroseris pseudocrispa

Tephroseris pseudocrispa, commonly known as the silverleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silver-tomentose leaves and yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lanceolate and have a distinctive silvery coating, while its flowers are arranged in a single head.

Tephroseris pseudocrispa is often used in traditional medicine and is also cultivated as an ornamental plant.
