Tinospora lacunosus

Tinospora lacunosus, commonly known as the creeping tinospora, is a climbing shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by orange-red berries containing seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Due to its rapid growth and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and used in traditional medicine.
