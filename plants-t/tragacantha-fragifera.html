Tragacantha fragifera

Tragacantha fragifera, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant is notable for producing a valuable gum, tragacanth, which has been used historically in medicine and food. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
