Tibouchina alpestris

Tibouchina alpestris, commonly known as the alpine tibouchina, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of showy, pink or purple flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

This plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
