Tamarix aralensis

Tamarix aralensis, commonly known as the Central Asian tamarisk, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its feathery, scale-like leaves and small, pink or reddish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is adapted to arid and saline environments, making it a common species in desert and semi-desert regions. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in poor soils and withstand drought conditions.

Tamarix aralensis plays a role in stabilizing sand dunes and preventing soil erosion in its native habitat.
