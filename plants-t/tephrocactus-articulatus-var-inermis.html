Tephrocactus articulatus var. inermis

Tephrocactus articulatus var. inermis, commonly known as the naked-tipped cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is a low-growing, spineless cactus with cylindrical stems that are ribbed and covered in small, pale green areoles. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking contrast to its otherwise muted appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for desert gardens and collectors.
