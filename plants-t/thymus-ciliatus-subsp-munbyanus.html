Thymus ciliatus subsp. munbyanus

Thymus ciliatus subsp.

munbyanus, commonly known as Munby's thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe. It features aromatic, narrow leaves with ciliate margins, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone-rich environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
