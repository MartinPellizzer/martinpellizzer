Tradescantia subaspera subsp. montana

Tradescantia subaspera subsp.

montana, commonly known as mountain spiderwort, is a perennial herb native to North America. It features upright stems with lance-shaped, hairy leaves that range from blue-green to gray-green in color.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink, purple, or white, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and restoration projects.
