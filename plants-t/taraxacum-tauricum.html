Taraxacum tauricum

Taraxacum tauricum, commonly known as the Tauric dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia, including the Caucasus region. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of spoon-shaped leaves and a flowering stem that bears a single, bright yellow composite flower head. It produces a distinctive, hollow, wind-dispersed seed capsule known as a pappus.

Taraxacum tauricum is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and it is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
