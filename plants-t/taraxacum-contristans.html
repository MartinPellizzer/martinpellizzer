Taraxacum contristans

Taraxacum contristans, commonly known as the weeping dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of spoon-shaped leaves and a single, elongated stem that bears a yellow flower head. The plant produces a distinctive, hollow, stem-like structure that serves as a seed dispersal mechanism. It is native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa, often found in dry, open habitats.

The species is known for its unique morphology and ecological role in its native environment.
