Tunica nanteuilii

Tunica nanteuilii, commonly known as the Nanteuil's tunica, is a rare and enigmatic plant species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique leaf morphology, featuring elongated, lance-shaped leaves with a waxy surface that aids in water retention. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pale yellow to white, which bloom during the wet season. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in moist, shaded environments.

Due to habitat destruction, Tunica nanteuilii is considered critically endangered and is the subject of ongoing conservation efforts.
