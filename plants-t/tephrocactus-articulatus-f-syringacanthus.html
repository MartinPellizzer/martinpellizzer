Tephrocactus articulatus f. syringacanthus

Tephrocactus articulatus f. syringacanthus, commonly known as the red-spined cactus, is a unique variation of the Tephrocactus genus native to Mexico.

This succulent plant is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are segmented and covered in sharp, reddish spines. The spines are often more pronounced and longer compared to the typical form of the species, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and as a decorative houseplant.
