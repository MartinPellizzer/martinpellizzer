Tilia caucasica var. begoniifolia

Tilia caucasica var. begoniifolia, commonly known as the Caucasian linden with begonia-like leaves, is a rare and ornamental variant of the linden tree.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that resemble those of begonias, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant is native to the Caucasus region and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. It produces small, fragrant yellow flowers in early summer, followed by small, winged fruits.

Due to its unique foliage and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
