Thamnochortus acuminatus

Thamnochortus acuminatus, commonly known as the Cape mountain dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It has hairy, narrow leaves and produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
