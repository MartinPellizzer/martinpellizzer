Torilis helvetica proles neglecta

Torilis helvetica proles neglecta, commonly known as the European hedge mustard, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with hairy stems and small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Due to its adaptability, it is considered an invasive species in some regions.
