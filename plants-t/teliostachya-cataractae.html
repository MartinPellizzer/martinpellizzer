Teliostachya cataractae

Teliostachya cataractae, commonly known as the cascade wild oat, is a perennial grass species native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that are often tinged with purple or red. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Teliostachya cataractae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
