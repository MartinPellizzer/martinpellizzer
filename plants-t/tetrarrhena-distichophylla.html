Tetrarrhena distichophylla

Tetrarrhena distichophylla, commonly known as the two-leaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in two rows. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and savanna habitats.

Due to its ornamental appeal and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
