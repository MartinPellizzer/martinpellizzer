Tragacantha hornii

Tragacantha hornii, commonly known as the horned tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive horn-like structures on its leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by pods containing seeds. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, temperate climates.

Tragacantha hornii is valued for its historical use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural gum.
