Torularia mollipila

Torularia mollipila, commonly known as the woolly torula, is a small, herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and hairy, gray-green foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Torularia mollipila is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
