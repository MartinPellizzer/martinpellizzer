Thalictrum zibellinum

Thalictrum zibellinum, commonly known as the yellow meadow rue, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, feathery leaves and showy, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, preferring partial to full shade. Its flowers bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Thalictrum zibellinum is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
