Taraxacum curvidens

Taraxacum curvidens, commonly known as the curvedleaf dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, deeply lobed leaves that curve inward, giving it its common name. The plant produces a single, upright stem that terminates in a fluffy, white flower head composed of numerous small florets. It is typically found in temperate regions, often growing in meadows, gardens, and disturbed soils.

Taraxacum curvidens is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a resilient and widespread species.
