Tassadia rhombifolia

Tassadia rhombifolia, commonly known as the diamondleaf tassadia, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rhombic-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in dry conditions, with a deep root system that helps it access groundwater. Its leaves are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

Tassadia rhombifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscapic gardens.
