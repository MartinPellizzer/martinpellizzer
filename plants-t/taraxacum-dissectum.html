Taraxacum dissectum

Taraxacum dissectum, commonly known as the dissected dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves that give it a delicate appearance. The plant produces small, yellow composite flowers that develop into white, fluffy seeds with parachute-like structures. It is typically found in temperate regions and thrives in open, disturbed soils.

Taraxacum dissectum is often considered a weed but has ecological value as a food source for various insects and as a pioneer species in ecological succession.
