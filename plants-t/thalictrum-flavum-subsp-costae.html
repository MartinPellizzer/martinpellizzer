Thalictrum flavum subsp. costae

Thalictrum flavum subsp.

costae, commonly known as the yellow meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters.

The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer. Its flowers are arranged in compound umbels, which are characteristic of the Ranunculaceae family.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
