Thymelaea tinctoria proles nivalis

Thymelaea tinctoria proles nivalis, commonly known as the alpine thyme or snow thyme, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It features small, needle-like leaves that are typically grayish-green in color, providing a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and early summer. It is well adapted to harsh alpine environments, thriving in rocky soils and cold climates.

Its aromatic foliage has historically been used for medicinal and dyeing purposes.
