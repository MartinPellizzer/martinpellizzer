Triptilion pusillum

Triptilion pusillum, commonly known as the small triptilon, is a small, herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has delicate, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or white in color. Its reproductive structures are adapted for wind pollination, which is common among plants in its habitat.

Triptilion pusillum is often found in association with other moisture-loving flora, contributing to the biodiversity of its ecosystem.
