Taxus baccata pendula-variegata

Taxus baccata pendula-variegata, commonly known as the variegated weeping yew, is a striking variation of the common yew.

It features a weeping growth habit with long, drooping branches that create a cascading effect. The leaves are narrow, needle-like, and often show variegation with white or yellow markings. It produces small, red berries that are toxic to humans and animals.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its unique appearance.
