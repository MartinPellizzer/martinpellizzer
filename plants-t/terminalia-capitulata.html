Terminalia capitulata

Terminalia capitulata, commonly known as the African snowball tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical clusters of white flowers that bloom in spring, creating a striking visual display. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds, and it is often used in traditional medicine.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its showy floral display.
