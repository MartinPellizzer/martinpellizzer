Thuranthos

Thuranthos, commonly known as the silverleaf orchid, is a unique plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage, which helps it reflect intense sunlight and conserve moisture. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink, typically blooming during the cooler months. Thuranthos thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic environments.

Its botanical significance lies in its adaptations to harsh climates and its role in local ecosystems.
