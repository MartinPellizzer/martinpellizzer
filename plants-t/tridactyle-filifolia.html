Tridactyle filifolia

Tridactyle filifolia, commonly known as the threadleaf tridactyle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the summer months. It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
