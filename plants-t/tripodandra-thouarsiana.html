Tripodandra thouarsiana

Tripodandra thouarsiana, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, pendulous inflorescences. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or lithophyte, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are small and fragrant, often displaying a striking color contrast between the petals and sepals.

Due to its rarity and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable species in botanical research and conservation efforts.
