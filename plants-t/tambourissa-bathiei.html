Tambourissa bathiei

Tambourissa bathiei, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a striking climbing plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves and a rapid growth habit, often reaching lengths of several meters. Its flowers have a unique structure with a showy outer petal and a narrow, tubular corolla.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
