Thesium libericum

Thesium libericum, commonly known as the coastal bladderpod, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a small, annual herb that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along the shoreline. The plant features alternate, narrow leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Thesium libericum is adapted to salt spray and sandy environments, making it a resilient species in coastal ecosystems.
