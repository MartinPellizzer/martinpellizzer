Tydaea pardina

Tydaea pardina, commonly known as the purple false oatgrass, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale purple flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-green hue.
