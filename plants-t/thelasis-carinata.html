Thelasis carinata

Thelasis carinata, commonly known as the mountain holly or the holly fern, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features lance-shaped, serrated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, tubular flowers in shades of purple or pink. Thelasis carinata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded woodland environments.

Its botanical features make it an attractive addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
