Toxicodendron verrucosum

Toxicodendron verrucosum, commonly known as the roughleaf sumac, is a shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by red, fuzzy berries. Its sap contains urushiol, a toxic resin that can cause skin irritation in humans.

It is often found in dry, disturbed soils and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
