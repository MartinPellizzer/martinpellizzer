Taxodium distichum var. nutans

Taxodium distichum var. nutans, commonly known as the bald cypress, is a deciduous conifer native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its feathery, scale-like leaves that turn a striking orange-red in the fall before dropping. The tree produces cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs, typical of conifers. Its bark is thick, flaky, and ranges from reddish-brown to dark gray.

The bald cypress is often found in wetland environments, where its extensive root system helps stabilize soil and support aquatic ecosystems.
