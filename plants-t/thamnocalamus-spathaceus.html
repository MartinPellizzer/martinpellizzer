Thamnocalamus spathaceus

Thamnocalamus spathaceus, commonly known as the bamboo grass, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of grasses. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
