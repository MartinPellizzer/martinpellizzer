Triticum comosum subsp. heldreichii

Triticum comosum subsp.

heldreichii, commonly known as the Balkan wild wheat, is a perennial grass species native to the Balkan Peninsula. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous narrow leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a distinctive glume structure that protects the developing grains.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists and agricultural researchers due to its potential genetic contributions to wheat improvement.
