Triticum dicoccum var. pseudoalbispicatum

Triticum dicoccum var. pseudoalbispicatum, commonly known as the dark red spelt, is a cultivated variant of spelt wheat that exhibits distinct botanical characteristics.

It is a hexaploid species with a compact spike and dark red to black glumes, distinguishing it from other spelt varieties. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 1.5 meters, with awned lemma and palea, contributing to its unique appearance. Its grains are smaller and more dense compared to common wheat, and it is known for its high protein content and hardiness.

This variety is often valued for its nutritional profile and traditional agricultural significance.
