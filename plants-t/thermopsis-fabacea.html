Thermopsis fabacea

Thermopsis fabacea, commonly known as yellowhead cosmos, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately compound, and often covered in fine hairs.

Thermopsis fabacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
