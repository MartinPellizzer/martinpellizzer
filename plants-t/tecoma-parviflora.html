Tecoma parviflora

Tecoma parviflora, commonly known as the smallflower yellow bell, is a deciduous shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it produces woody pods containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
