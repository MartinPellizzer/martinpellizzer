Thryptomene dampieri

Thryptomene dampieri, commonly known as the Grey Box, is a small to medium-sized shrub native to the south-western region of Western Australia.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dry, open woodlands and heathlands. The plant has greyish-green foliage and produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow. It is characterized by its rigid, woody structure and is well adapted to the arid and semi-arid climates of its native habitat.

Thryptomene dampieri is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
