Taeniophyllum flavum

Taeniophyllum flavum, commonly known as the golden thread plant, is a small, evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, thread-like leaves that are usually a vibrant yellow or greenish-yellow in color. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom in clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
