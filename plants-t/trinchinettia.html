Trinchinettia

Trinchinettia, commonly known as the rock cress, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Brassicaceae.

These plants are typically found in rocky or arid environments, showcasing their adaptability to harsh conditions. They produce small, delicate flowers that are often white or purple, and their leaves are usually narrow and succulent. Trinchinettia species are often used in horticulture for their ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soil.

The genus includes several species, such as Trinchinettia saxifraga, which is native to North America.
