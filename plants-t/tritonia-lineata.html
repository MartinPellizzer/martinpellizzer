Tritonia lineata

Tritonia lineata, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers with distinctive markings. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, while the flowers are usually pink or purple with contrasting yellow veins.

Tritonia lineata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
