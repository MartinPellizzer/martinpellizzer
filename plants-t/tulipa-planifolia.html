Tulipa planifolia

Tulipa planifolia, commonly known as the flatleaf tulip, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, flat leaves and showy, cup-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant is herbaceous, with a short, stout stem that supports its vibrant blooms. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Tulipa planifolia is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated for its striking, early spring blossoms.
