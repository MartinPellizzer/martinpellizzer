Trifolium maritimum subsp. panormitanum

Trifolium maritimum subsp.

panormitanum, commonly known as the Panormitanum clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, typically with three oval to lanceolate leaflets, and small white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring.

This subspecies thrives in coastal and saline environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh, maritime conditions. It is often found in dunes, rocky shores, and other coastal habitats where it plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to contribute to local biodiversity in coastal ecosystems.
