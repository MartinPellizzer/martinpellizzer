Torilis ucranica

Torilis ucranica, commonly known as the Ukrainian groundsel, is a flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with prominent central disks.

Torilis ucranica is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in naturalized areas.
