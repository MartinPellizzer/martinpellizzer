Trifolium sativum subsp. serotinum

Trifolium sativum subsp.

serotinum, commonly known as late clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, typically with three oval-shaped leaflets, and produces small white or pink flowers.

This subspecies is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, including poor and acidic soils. It is often found in grasslands, meadows, and along roadsides.

The plant is also valued for its role in nitrogen fixation, benefiting soil fertility.
