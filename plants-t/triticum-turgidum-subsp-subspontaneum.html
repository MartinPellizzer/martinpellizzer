Triticum turgidum subsp. subspontaneum

Triticum turgidum subsp.

subspontaneum, commonly known as wild wheat, is a wild relative of cultivated wheat species. It is native to various regions of Europe, Asia, and North Africa, thriving in temperate climates.

This plant typically grows in open fields, grasslands, and along roadsides, often in disturbed soils. It has a robust root system and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brown glumes.

Its genetic diversity makes it valuable for breeding programs aimed at improving wheat resilience to environmental stresses.
