Trichoglottis ventricularis

Trichoglottis ventricularis, commonly known as the ventral trichoglottis, is a rare and unique orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, terrestrial growth habit and distinctive, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers with a subtle fragrance, typically blooming during the wet season. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the humid, shaded environments where it thrives.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in the wild.
