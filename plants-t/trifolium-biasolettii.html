Trifolium biasolettii

Trifolium biasolettii, commonly known as the Italian clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides.

Trifolium biasolettii is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
