Tricyrtis flava var. yatabeana

Tricyrtis flava var. yatabeana, commonly known as the Yatabe yellow spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asphodelaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate, spider-like flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
