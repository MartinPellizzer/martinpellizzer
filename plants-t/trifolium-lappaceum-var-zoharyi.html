Trifolium lappaceum var. zoharyi

Trifolium lappaceum var. zoharyi, commonly known as the Zohary's hairy trefoil, is a flowering plant species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

This variety is notable for its hairy stems and leaves, which provide it with a distinctive appearance.
