Tournefortia elegans

Tournefortia elegans, commonly known as the white star hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters, featuring lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, with a prominent central stigma. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and is cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions for its beauty and resilience.
