Taraxacum aureocucullatum

Taraxacum aureocucullatum, commonly known as the golden-hat dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to southern Europe and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and hairy, lobed leaves. The plant produces a distinctive, hollow stem that supports its flowering head, which is surrounded by a crown of bracts. Its seeds are dispersed by wind via a parachute-like structure called a pappus.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats, thriving in temperate climates.
