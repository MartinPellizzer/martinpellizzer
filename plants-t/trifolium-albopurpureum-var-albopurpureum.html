Trifolium albopurpureum var. albopurpureum

Trifolium albopurpureum var. albopurpureum, commonly known as the purple-veined clover, is a perennial flowering plant native to certain regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets with a distinctive purple-veined pattern. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
