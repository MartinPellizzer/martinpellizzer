Teucrium cinereum

Teucrium cinereum, commonly known as blue germander, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often gray-green in color, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry meadows or rocky habitats.

Teucrium cinereum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
