Terminalia franchetii var. membranifolia

Terminalia franchetii var. membranifolia, commonly known as the membranous-leaf terminalia, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, membranous leaves that are often hairy on the underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in panicles, followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds. Its bark is thick and often fissured, providing a rugged texture.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
