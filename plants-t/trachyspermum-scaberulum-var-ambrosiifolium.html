Trachyspermum scaberulum var. ambrosiifolium

Trachyspermum scaberulum var. ambrosiifolium, commonly known as Mexican epazote, is a herbaceous plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It features small, aromatic leaves with a distinctive pungent odor, often used in cooking for its unique flavor. The plant produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly rough texture.

It thrives in warm, sunny conditions and is often grown as a culinary herb in tropical and subtropical regions.
