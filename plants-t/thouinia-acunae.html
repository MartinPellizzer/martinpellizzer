Thouinia acunae

Thouinia acunae, commonly known as the mountain thistle, is a perennial herb native to high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple or pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows. Its botanical features include a stout stem and a basal rosette of leaves.

Thouinia acunae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
