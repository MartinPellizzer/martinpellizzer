Triphyophyllum peltatum

Triphyophyllum peltatum, commonly known as the ghost plant, is a unique carnivorous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its ability to absorb nutrients through its leaves, which are covered in fine, hair-like structures. The plant lacks traditional roots and instead attaches itself to trees using its stems, often growing in shaded, humid environments. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are typically green or yellow, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that contain seeds.

Due to its specialized adaptations, it is considered a rare and intriguing species in the study of carnivorous flora.
