Terminalia ombrophila

Terminalia ombrophila, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing flat, disc-shaped seeds. Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
