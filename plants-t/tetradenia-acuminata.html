Tetradenia acuminata

Tetradenia acuminata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tetradenia, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Australia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and dry woodland habitats.
