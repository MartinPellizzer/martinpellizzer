Thelypteris pachyrhachis

Thelypteris pachyrhachis, commonly known as the broad-veined shield fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, leathery, lance-shaped leaves with prominent, broad veins. The fronds are typically evergreen and can grow up to several feet in length. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its fronds, which are clustered in sori.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
