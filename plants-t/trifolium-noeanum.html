Trifolium noeanum

Trifolium noeanum, commonly known as the Neanum clover, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

Trifolium noeanum is a low-growing perennial that is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
