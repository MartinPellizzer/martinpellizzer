Thymus pallens

Thymus pallens, commonly known as pale thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic oils and medicinal properties. Its leaves are often used in culinary applications and traditional herbal medicine.

Thymus pallens thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant ground cover.
