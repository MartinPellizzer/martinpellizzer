Tylophora tenerrima

Tylophora tenerrima, commonly known as the slender tylophora, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its thin, twining stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white flowers that are often followed by small, berry-like fruits. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in horticulture.
