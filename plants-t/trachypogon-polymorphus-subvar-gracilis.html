Trachypogon polymorphus subvar. gracilis

Trachypogon polymorphus subvar. gracilis, commonly known as the slender false brome, is a grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its adaptability, it is considered an important component of local ecosystems.
