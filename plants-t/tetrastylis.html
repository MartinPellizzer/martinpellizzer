Tetrastylis

Tetrastylis, commonly known as the false spider orchid, is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its small, often delicate flowers that typically have four or five sepals and petals. The plants are native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Australia, and the Pacific Islands. Tetrastylis species usually grow as terrestrial or epiphytic herbs, often found in moist, shaded environments.
