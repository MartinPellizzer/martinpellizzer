Tibouchina capitata

Tibouchina capitata, commonly known as the Cape rose or blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring dense, compact foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. Its leaves are typically opposite, elliptical, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Tibouchina capitata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited for humid climates.
