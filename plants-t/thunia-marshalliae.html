Thunia marshalliae

Thunia marshalliae, commonly known as Marshall's thunia, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its small, intricate flowers that are typically white or pale pink. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and limited distribution, making it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat destruction, it is considered endangered and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for preservation.
