Tabebuia rosea

Tabebuia rosea, commonly known as the pink trumpet tree, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The tree has a smooth, grey bark and produces long, narrow leaves that are opposite in arrangement. Its flowers are tubular and attract pollinators such as bees and birds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often planted in gardens and public spaces.
