Thalictrum minus var. chionophyllum

Thalictrum minus var. chionophyllum, commonly known as the white meadow rue, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves that are typically basal and arranged in a rosette. The plant produces showy, spikelike clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers in early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is often used in gardens for its elegant appearance.
