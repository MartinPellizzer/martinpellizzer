Tremulina cracens

Tremulina cracens, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a deciduous tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery-white pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
