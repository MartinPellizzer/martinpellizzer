Tala

Tala, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a small, citrus-like fruit native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the genus Phyllanthus and is characterized by its yellowish-green skin and tart, astringent flavor. The plant is a shrub that thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in dry, rocky soils. Tala is rich in vitamin C and has been used in traditional medicine for its antioxidant and healing properties.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
