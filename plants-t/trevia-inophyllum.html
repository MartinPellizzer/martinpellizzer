Trevia inophyllum

Trevia inophyllum, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem, and its root system consists of fibrous roots that help anchor it in well-drained soils. Trevia inophyllum prefers full sun to partial shade and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
