Taraxacum chitralense

Taraxacum chitralense, commonly known as the Chitral dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of northern Pakistan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and a flowering head that produces small, yellow composite flowers. The plant is adapted to high-altitude environments, often growing in rocky or alpine soils. Its leaves are typically covered in fine hairs, and the flowers bloom in early summer.

Taraxacum chitralense is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological niche and potential medicinal properties.
