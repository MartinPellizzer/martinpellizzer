Triaspis erlangeri

Triaspis erlangeri, commonly known as the erlanger's triaspis, is a small, herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually appearing in clusters during the spring or early summer.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and somewhat obscure species within the botanical community.
