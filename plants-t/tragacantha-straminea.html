Tragacantha straminea

Tragacantha straminea, commonly known as the yellow tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small yellow flowers. The plant produces a gum-like substance from its roots, which has been historically used in various medicinal and industrial applications. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Tragacantha straminea is often found in rocky or sandy habitats, adapting well to dry conditions.
