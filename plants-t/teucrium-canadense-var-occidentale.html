Teucrium canadense var. occidentale

Teucrium canadense var. occidentale, commonly known as western germander, is a perennial herb native to western North America.

It typically grows in moist to dry habitats, including meadows, woodlands, and rocky slopes. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of blue to purple. Its flowering period occurs from late spring to early summer, and it is often found in regions with temperate climates.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
