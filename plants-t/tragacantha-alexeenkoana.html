Tragacantha alexeenkoana

Tragacantha alexeenkoana, commonly known as the Alexandrian tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that bloom in summer, followed by distinctive pod-like fruits. It is known for its ability to thrive in harsh, dry environments, making it an important species in ecological restoration projects.

Its latex has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural gum.
