Tamarix gallica f. glaucescens

Tamarix gallica f. glaucescens, commonly known as the blue tamarisk, is a compact shrub native to southern Europe.

It features small, scale-like leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, providing a striking visual contrast. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pink to reddish flowers in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is well adapted to arid and saline soils, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value and drought tolerance make it a popular landscape plant in suitable climates.
