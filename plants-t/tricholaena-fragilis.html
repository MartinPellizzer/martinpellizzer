Tricholaena fragilis

Tricholaena fragilis, commonly known as the giant reed or bamboo grass, is a tall, herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and long, narrow leaves. The plant thrives in wetland environments and is often found near water bodies such as rivers and lakes. Its rapid growth rate makes it an important species in ecological restoration projects.

Tricholaena fragilis is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of thatching material.
