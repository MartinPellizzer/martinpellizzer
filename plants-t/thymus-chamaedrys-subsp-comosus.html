Thymus chamaedrys subsp. comosus

Thymus chamaedrys subsp.

comosus, commonly known as creeping thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features small, fragrant leaves that are typically gray-green in color and emit a strong thyme scent when crushed.

The plant produces tiny pink or white flowers in early summer, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and ground cover.

Its compact growth habit and aromatic properties make it both ornamental and useful in culinary applications.
