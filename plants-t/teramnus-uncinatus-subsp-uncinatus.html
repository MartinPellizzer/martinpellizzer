Teramnus uncinatus subsp. uncinatus

Teramnus uncinatus subsp.

uncinatus, commonly known as the hooked teramnus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula. It is a shrub characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.

This species is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
