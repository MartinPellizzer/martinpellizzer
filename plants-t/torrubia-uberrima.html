Torrubia uberrima

Torrubia uberrima, commonly known as the Spanish heath, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.

Due to its ecological importance, it is also a subject of conservation efforts in its native habitat.
