Telipogon diabolicus

Telipogon diabolicus, commonly known as the devil's orchid, is a rare and enigmatic flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, intricate floral structure. The plant produces striking, often violet or dark-colored flowers that are adapted for specific pollinators. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered endangered and highly sought after by botanists and conservationists.
