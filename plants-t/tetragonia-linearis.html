Tetragonia linearis

Tetragonia linearis, commonly known as New Zealand spinach, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its succulent, edible leaves that are often used in culinary applications. The plant has a prostrate growth habit with fleshy, linear leaves that are typically green to reddish in color. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Tetragonia linearis thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate climates.
