Tylophora clemensiae

Tylophora clemensiae, commonly known as the Malayan star vine, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by follicles containing seeds with tufts of hair. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal potential, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
