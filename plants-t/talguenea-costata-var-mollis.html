Talguenea costata var. mollis

Talguenea costata var. mollis, commonly known as the soft-ribbed talguenea, is a striking succulent native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically a vibrant green, often with a bluish or reddish tinge depending on light exposure. The plant produces tall, branched stems that bear clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange. Its leaves are arranged in a rosette pattern, providing a compact and ornamental appearance.

This variety is valued for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in well-drained soil conditions.
