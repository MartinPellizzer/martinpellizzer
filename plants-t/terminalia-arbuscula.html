Terminalia arbuscula

Terminalia arbuscula, commonly known as the African snowball tree, is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in winter. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically eaten by various bird species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Terminalia arbuscula is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in supporting local wildlife.
