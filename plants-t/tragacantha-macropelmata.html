Tragacantha macropelmata

Tragacantha macropelmata, commonly known as the large-fruited tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, inflated pods that contain the seeds. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers and has compound leaves with multiple leaflets. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

The plant is valued for its gum, which has been used historically in various applications.
