Thelymitra pauciflora

Thelymitra pauciflora, commonly known as the few-flowered sun-orchid, is an Australian native orchid belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact, terrestrial growth habit and produces small, pale yellow or greenish flowers during spring. The plant typically grows in heathlands and open forests, preferring well-drained soils. Its floral structure includes a single or few flowers on a short stem, with sepals and petals that are often similar in color.

Thelymitra pauciflora is a protected species, highlighting its ecological significance and the need for conservation efforts.
