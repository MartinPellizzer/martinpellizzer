Tonsella madagascariensis

Tonsella madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar mint, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported therapeutic properties.
