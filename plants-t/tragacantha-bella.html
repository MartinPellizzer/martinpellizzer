Tragacantha bella

Tragacantha bella, commonly known as the Persian violet, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, violet-colored flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Tragacantha bella is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its growth habit is typically herbaceous, with hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves.
