Trisetum molle

Trisetum molle, commonly known as soft hair grass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and slender, spreading growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, bogs, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, which are often tinged with purple or brown.

Trisetum molle plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
