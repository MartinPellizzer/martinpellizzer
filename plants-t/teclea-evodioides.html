Teclea evodioides

Teclea evodioides, commonly known as the African mountain tea, is a shrub native to East Africa.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, red berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.
