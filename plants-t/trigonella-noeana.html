Trigonella noeana

Trigonella noeana, commonly known as the Armenian bitter vetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Turkey.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

Trigonella noeana is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing a range of drought-resistant botanical features.
