Teucrium argutum

Teucrium argutum, commonly known as mountain germander, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, often found in alpine and subalpine zones. The plant features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, tubular flowers are usually blue to purple in color, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Teucrium argutum is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
