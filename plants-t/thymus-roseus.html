Thymus roseus

Thymus roseus, commonly known as rose thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features small, needle-like leaves and produces clusters of pink to lavender flowers. The plant is characterized by its aromatic foliage, which contains essential oils responsible for its distinctive scent. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Rose thyme is widely used in culinary applications and traditional medicine due to its medicinal properties.
