Thunbergia masisiensis

Thunbergia masisiensis, commonly known as the Masisi moonflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that open at night. The flowers are typically white or pale blue with a prominent yellow center, resembling those of the moonflower. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires support for its climbing habit.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and nocturnal blooming pattern.
