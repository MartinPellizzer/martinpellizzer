Trisetum foliosum

Trisetum foliosum, commonly known as the grass-like plant with feather-like leaves, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to parts of Europe and Asia, where it typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, linear leaves that are often hairy along the edges. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, which are characteristic of grasses.

Trisetum foliosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
