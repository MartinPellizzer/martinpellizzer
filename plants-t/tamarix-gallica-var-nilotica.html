Tamarix gallica var. nilotica

Tamarix gallica var. nilotica, commonly known as the pink tamarisk, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its feathery, gray-green foliage and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant is well adapted to arid and saline environments, making it a hardy species for coastal and dry landscapes. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in poor soils and withstand drought conditions.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soils in erosion-prone areas.
