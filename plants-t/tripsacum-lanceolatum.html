Tripsacum lanceolatum

Tripsacum lanceolatum, commonly known as wild maize or speargrass, is a grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are typically greenish or reddish in color. It is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in tropical and subtropical regions.

Botanically, it is closely related to maize and has been studied for its potential in agricultural and ecological contexts.
