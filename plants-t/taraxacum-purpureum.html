Taraxacum purpureum

Taraxacum purpureum, commonly known as the purple dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of pale green, hairy leaves and a yellow flower head that develops into a white pappus-covered seed sphere. Native to Europe, it is often found in disturbed soils and grassy areas. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in various soil types and its resilience to environmental stress.

Its flowers bloom from late spring to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
