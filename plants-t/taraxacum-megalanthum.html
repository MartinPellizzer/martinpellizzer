Taraxacum megalanthum

Taraxacum megalanthum, commonly known as the giant dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a rosette of broad, waxy leaves that are typically green with a slight bluish tint. It produces a long, slender stem topped with a fluffy seed head that disperses seeds via the wind.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
