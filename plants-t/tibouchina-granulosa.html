Tibouchina granulosa

Tibouchina granulosa, commonly known as the roughleaf tibouchina, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its rough, hairy leaves. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features include opposite leaves and a woody stem, which contribute to its structural resilience.
