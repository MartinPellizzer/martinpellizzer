Trichilia unifoliola

Trichilia unifoliola, commonly known as the African sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to Africa.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by winged fruits that disperse its seeds. It is valued for its aromatic wood, which is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.

The tree plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, contributing to biodiversity and soil stability.
