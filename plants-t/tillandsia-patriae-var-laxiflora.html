Tillandsia patriae var. laxiflora

Tillandsia patriae var. laxiflora, commonly known as the Brazilian air plant, is a species of epiphytic bromeliad native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help absorb moisture and nutrients from the air. The plant produces striking, pendulous inflorescences with bright orange flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires minimal soil, making it a popular choice for terrariums and indoor gardening.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
