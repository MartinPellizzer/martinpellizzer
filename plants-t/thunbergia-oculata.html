Thunbergia oculata

Thunbergia oculata, commonly known as the eyeleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that have a prominent central vein, giving the appearance of an eye. The plant produces trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple, blooming in the early morning. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Thunbergia oculata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and showy blooms.
