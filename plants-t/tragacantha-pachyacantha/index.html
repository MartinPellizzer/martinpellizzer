Tragacantha Pachyacantha

Tragacantha pachyacantha is a rare and distinctive plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa. It is known for its thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces striking yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Despite its hardy nature, it is threatened by habitat loss and over-collection. Conservation efforts are crucial to ensure its survival in the wild.
