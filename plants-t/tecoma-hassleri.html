Tecoma hassleri

Tecoma hassleri, commonly known as the yellow bells, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces bright yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a silvery underside.

Tecoma hassleri is valued for its ornamental beauty and drought tolerance, making it suitable for xeriscaping in dry climates.
