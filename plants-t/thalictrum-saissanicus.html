Thalictrum saissanicus

Thalictrum saissanicus, commonly known as the Saissan's meadow rue, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and delicate, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in a loose raceme, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
