Tephrocactus dactylifer

Tephrocactus dactylifer, commonly known as the desert date cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, columnar stems that store water, allowing it to survive in harsh desert conditions. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking visual contrast to its green foliage. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture efficiently from the sandy soil.

Tephrocactus dactylifer is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in low-water environments.
