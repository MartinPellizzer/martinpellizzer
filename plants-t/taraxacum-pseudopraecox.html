Taraxacum pseudopraecox

Taraxacum pseudopraecox, commonly known as the early dandelion, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of pale green, hairy leaves and a hollow, leafless stem that supports a single, yellow flower head. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. It produces a distinctive seed head with numerous parachute-like seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Taraxacum pseudopraecox is a perennial herb that thrives in temperate climates and is known for its medicinal properties.
