Thymus ciliatus var. tetuanensis

Thymus ciliatus var. tetuanensis, commonly known as the Tetuan thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and aromatic, gray-green foliage. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers in early summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its essential oils contain thymol and carvacrol, giving it a strong, pungent aroma and medicinal properties.
