Taraxacum saphycraspedum

Taraxacum saphycraspedum, commonly known as the pale dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of pale green, hairy leaves and a hollow, hairy stem that supports a single, yellow-orange flower head. The plant produces a distinctive, wind-dispersed seed with a parachute-like structure, aiding in its propagation. It thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats, often found in meadows and along roadsides.

This species is notable for its adaptability and is considered a common weed in various regions.
