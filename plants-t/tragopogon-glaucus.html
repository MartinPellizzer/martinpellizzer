Tragopogon glaucus

Tragopogon glaucus, commonly known as the blueweed or yellow goatsbeard, is a flowering plant in the dandelion family (Asteraceae).

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that transition to a blue or purple hue as they mature. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Tragopogon glaucus is known for its ability to hybridize with other Tragopogon species, leading to the formation of new, often sterile, hybrid plants.
