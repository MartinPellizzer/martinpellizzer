Trifolium polystachyum var. contractum

Trifolium polystachyum var. contractum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It features trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, often with a slight reddish or purplish hue. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in ecological restoration projects.
