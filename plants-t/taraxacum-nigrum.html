Taraxacum nigrum

Taraxacum nigrum, commonly known as black dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of dark green, hairy leaves and a yellow flower head that develops into a spherical, white pappus-covered seed head. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in disturbed soils and open habitats. It has a taproot system and produces numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties, with historical use in traditional remedies.
