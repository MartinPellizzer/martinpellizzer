Tsuga canadensis var. sargentii

Tsuga canadensis var. sargentii, commonly known as the Sargent hemlock, is a variant of the eastern hemlock native to eastern North America.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and needle-like leaves. The tree typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas, often forming dense stands. It has a slow growth rate and can live for several centuries, contributing to the structure of temperate hardwood forests.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the ecosystem, providing habitat and shelter for various wildlife species.
