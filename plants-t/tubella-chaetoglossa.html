Tubella chaetoglossa

Tubella chaetoglossa, commonly known as the hairy glossary, is a species of marine sponge found in shallow coastal waters.

It belongs to the family Tethyidae and is characterized by its soft, gelatinous body covered in fine, hair-like projections. The sponge's structure includes a complex network of canals and pores that facilitate water circulation for filter feeding. It thrives in temperate regions and is often found attached to rocks or other substrates.

The coloration of Tubella chaetoglossa can vary, ranging from pale yellow to brown, depending on environmental conditions.
