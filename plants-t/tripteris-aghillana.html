Tripteris aghillana

Tripteris aghillana, commonly known as the Aghillan fern, is a species of flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Tripteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are often divided into small, lance-shaped segments, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

Tripteris aghillana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
