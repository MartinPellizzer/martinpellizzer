Turnera brasiliensis

Turnera brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian turnera, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in shaded forest understories. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, fragrant yellow flowers. Its botanical classification places it in the family Malvaceae, which includes hibiscus and cotton.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
