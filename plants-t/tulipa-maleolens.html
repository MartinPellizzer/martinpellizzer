Tulipa maleolens

Tulipa maleolens, commonly known as the Persian tulip, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, recurved petals that give it a distinctive, almost whimsical appearance. The plant typically grows to a height of about 20 to 30 centimeters, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers bloom in early spring, showcasing a range of colors from red to pink and white.

The tulipa maleolens is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to well-drained soils.
