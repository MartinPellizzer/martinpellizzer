Thymus maroccanus subsp. rhombicus

Thymus maroccanus subsp.

rhombicus, commonly known as Moroccan thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Morocco. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and aromatic, gray-green foliage.

The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its essential oils, rich in thymol and carvacrol, are valued for their medicinal and culinary uses.
