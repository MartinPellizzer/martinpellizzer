Thelypteris deflexa

Thelypteris deflexa, commonly known as the flexed maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Thelypteridaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that often curve gracefully, giving it a flowing appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce both sterile and fertile fronds.

Thelypteris deflexa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
