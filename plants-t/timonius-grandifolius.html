Timonius grandifolius

Timonius grandifolius, commonly known as the grandleaf timonius, is a striking plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length, which are typically a deep green with a waxy texture. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, often arranged in clusters at the ends of its branches. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
