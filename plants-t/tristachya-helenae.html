Tristachya helenae

Tristachya helenae, commonly known as Helen's beardgrass, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and delicate, feathery inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in spikelets that give the plant its distinctive appearance.

Tristachya helenae is often found in open habitats such as slopes and wastelands, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
