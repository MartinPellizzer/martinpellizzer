Theligonum macranthum

Theligonum macranthum, commonly known as the large-flowered honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features opposite, simple, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and have a smooth texture. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically white or yellow, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its berries are red or orange and serve as a food source for birds and small mammals.

The plant is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade.
