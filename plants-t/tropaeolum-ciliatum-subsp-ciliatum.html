Tropaeolum ciliatum subsp. ciliatum

Tropaeolum ciliatum subsp.

ciliatum, commonly known as the frilled nasturtium, is a herbaceous annual plant native to South America. It is characterized by its vibrant, butterfly-shaped flowers that come in shades of yellow, orange, and red.

The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves with a distinctive frilled edge, which gives it its common name. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil. Tropaeolum ciliatum subsp.

ciliatum is often grown as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and edible flowers.
