Teucrium buxifolium var. hifacense

Teucrium buxifolium var. hifacense, commonly known as the Hifacense germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are closely spaced, leathery, and resemble those of boxwood, giving it its common name.

It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.
