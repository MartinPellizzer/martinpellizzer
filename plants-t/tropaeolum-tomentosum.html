Tropaeolum tomentosum

Tropaeolum tomentosum, commonly known as the velvetleaf nasturtium, is a herbaceous annual plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and heart-shaped, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, yellow-orange flowers that are often clustered in cymes. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, and the plant is known for its peppery taste in its leaves and stems.

Tropaeolum tomentosum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant and for its edible parts.
