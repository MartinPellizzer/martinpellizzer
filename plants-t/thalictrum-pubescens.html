Thalictrum pubescens

Thalictrum pubescens, commonly known as the downy meadow rue, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are compound, with numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Thalictrum pubescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
