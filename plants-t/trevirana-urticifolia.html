Trevirana urticifolia

Trevirana urticifolia, commonly known as the stinging nettle plant, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North America.

It is characterized by its hollow, hairless stems and heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive stinging hair that releases irritants when touched. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found along stream banks and in woodland areas.

Trevirana urticifolia has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber.
