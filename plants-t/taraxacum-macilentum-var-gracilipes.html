Taraxacum macilentum var. gracilipes

Taraxacum macilentum var. gracilipes, commonly known as the slender leaf dandelion, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its taproot system, which allows it to survive in various soil types. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and potential ecological roles in its native environments.
