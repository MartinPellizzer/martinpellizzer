Turbinicarpus × mombergeri

Turbinicarpus × mombergeri, commonly known as the hybrid moon cactus, is a fascinating succulent that combines the characteristics of its parent species, Turbinicarpus macdowellii and Turbinicarpus calycatus.

It is native to the arid regions of northern Mexico, where it thrives in well-draining soil and full sun exposure. The plant features a compact, rounded stem with a distinctive ribbed structure, and its flowers are typically yellow or orange, adding a striking visual element. Due to its hybrid nature, it is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in dry environments.

However, it requires careful watering to avoid root rot and is best suited for experienced growers.
