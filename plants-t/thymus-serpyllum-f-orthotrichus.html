Thymus serpyllum f. orthotrichus

Thymus serpyllum f. orthotrichus, commonly known as narrowleaf thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically narrow and slightly pointed, with a dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces tiny, pink to lavender flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact size and fragrant foliage make it a popular choice for both ornamental and medicinal use.
