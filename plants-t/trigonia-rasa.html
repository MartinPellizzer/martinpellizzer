Trigonia rasa

Trigonia rasa, commonly known as the slender trigonia, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, tubular flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of pink or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Trigonia rasa is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
