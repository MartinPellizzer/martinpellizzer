Tachia schomburgkiana

Tachia schomburgkiana, commonly known as the Schomburgk's tachia, is a member of the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features broad, arrowhead-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white and green patterns. Its flowers are clustered in a spadix, surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe.

Tachia schomburgkiana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and unique foliage.
