Terminalia glabra

Terminalia glabra, commonly known as the African sandalwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, often fissured bark and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers followed by capsule fruits containing minute, wind-dispersed seeds. It is valued for its aromatic wood, which is used in traditional medicine and for making incense.

The species is also important in ecological systems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
