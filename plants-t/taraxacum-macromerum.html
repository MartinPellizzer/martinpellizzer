Taraxacum macromerum

Taraxacum macromerum, commonly known as the giant dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces a single, large, yellow flower head that develops into a pappus-equipped seed head, aiding in wind dispersal. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and other open areas.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with composite flower heads.
