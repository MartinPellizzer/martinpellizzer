Tulipa eichleri

Tulipa eichleri, commonly known as the Eichleri tulip, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, cup-shaped flowers that typically range in color from red to orange. The plant produces showy blooms in early spring, often with a distinctive black eye at the center. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Tulipa eichleri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by gardeners interested in rare and exotic flowers.
