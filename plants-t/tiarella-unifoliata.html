Tiarella unifoliata

Tiarella unifoliata, commonly known as foamflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features delicate, clustered white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has heart-shaped, lobed leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Tiarella unifoliata is valued in gardens for its ornamental foliage and modest floral display.
