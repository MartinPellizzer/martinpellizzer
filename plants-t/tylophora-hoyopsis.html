Tylophora hoyopsis

Tylophora hoyopsis, commonly known as the Chinese star vine, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody capsules containing numerous tiny seeds. Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
