Triplaris corylifolia var. excelsa

Triplaris corylifolia var. excelsa, commonly known as the golden star sycamore, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its palmately compound leaves, which are often bright green and have a distinctive, almost star-like appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by round, woody fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
