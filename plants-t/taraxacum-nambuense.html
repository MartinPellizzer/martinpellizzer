Taraxacum nambuense

Taraxacum nambuense, commonly known as the Nambu dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and distinctive heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces a milky latex when the stems or leaves are broken, a common feature among the dandelion genus. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Taraxacum nambuense is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
