Toxocarpus insularis

Toxocarpus insularis, commonly known as the island false hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean islands.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red, with five petals and a prominent central stamen. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, round seeds.

Toxocarpus insularis is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils.
