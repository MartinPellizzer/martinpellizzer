Trachycarpus martianus subsp. khasyana

Trachycarpus martianus subsp.

khasyana, commonly known as the Khasi bamboo, is a unique variant of the common bamboo species native to the northeastern region of India. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems that grow in dense clumps, often reaching heights of up to 10 meters.

The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth, slightly glossy surface. This subspecies thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions with cool, temperate climates.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
