Trisetum alpestre var. argentoideum

Trisetum alpestre var. argentoideum, commonly known as the silver hair grass, is a perennial grass species native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its finely textured, silver-gray foliage due to dense pubescence on the leaf blades. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as alpine meadows and rocky slopes. It produces slender, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of high-altitude ecosystems and a subject of interest in ecological and horticultural studies.
