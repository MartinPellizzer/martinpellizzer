Triantha occidentalis subsp. montana

Triantha occidentalis subsp.

montana, commonly known as the mountain triantha, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks.

The plant features slender, hairy stems and produces small, pinkish-white flowers in late spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It is valued for its ecological role in supporting local pollinators and its ornamental appeal in native plant gardens.
