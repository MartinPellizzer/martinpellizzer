Tilesia baccata var. discoidea

Tilesia baccata var. discoidea, commonly known as the disc-shaped tile tree, is a variant of the tile tree species native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive disc-like fruits, which are typically yellow or orange when mature. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, adding to its ornamental value. Its leaves are simple and alternate, with a glossy surface that reflects light.

This variant is often cultivated for its unique fruit morphology and aesthetic appeal in gardens.
