Tamarix tetragyna subsp. meyeri

Tamarix tetragyna subsp.

meyeri, commonly known as the Meyer's tamarisk, is a shrub native to regions of Central Asia and the Middle East. It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and salt-resistant. Its woody stems often branch near the base, forming dense, shrub-like growth.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soils in challenging conditions.
