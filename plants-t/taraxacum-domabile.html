Taraxacum domabile

Taraxacum domabile, commonly known as the domestic dandelion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves and a hollow, hairless stem that supports a single, yellow flower head. The plant produces a distinctive, wind-dispersed seed with a white pappus, aiding in its wide distribution. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in lawns, meadows, and disturbed soils.

Botanically, it is notable for its adaptability and role in ecological systems.
