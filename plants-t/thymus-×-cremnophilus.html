Thymus × cremnophilus

Thymus × cremnophilus, commonly known as the alpine thyme, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and aromatic foliage, which contains essential oils responsible for its distinctive scent. The plant typically produces small, tubular flowers in shades of pink or purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Native to mountainous regions of Europe, it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and full sun exposure.

Due to its hardiness and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and used in herbal medicine.
