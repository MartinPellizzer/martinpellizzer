Thymus serpyllum var. exserens

Thymus serpyllum var. exserens, commonly known as narrowleaf thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and western Asia.

It features small, narrow leaves that are densely packed along its creeping stems, giving it a mat-like appearance. The plant produces tiny, pinkish-white flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic foliage is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
