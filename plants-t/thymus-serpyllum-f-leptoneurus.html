Thymus serpyllum f. leptoneurus

Thymus serpyllum f. leptoneurus, commonly known as narrow-leaved thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically narrow and linear, giving it its distinguishing appearance. The plant produces pink or purple flowers in clusters, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and fragrant foliage make it a popular choice for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
