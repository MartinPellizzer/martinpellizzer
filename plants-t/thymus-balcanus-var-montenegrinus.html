Thymus balcanus var. montenegrinus

Thymus balcanus var. montenegrinus, commonly known as the Montenegro thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, small, gray-green leaves and pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun. It is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, with a strong, pungent aroma.

This variety is often found in mountainous regions of Montenegro and adjacent areas.
