Tommasinia altissima

Tommasinia altissima, commonly known as the tall tamarack, is a deciduous conifer native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves that turn a striking golden yellow in the fall. The plant produces small, spherical cones that contain seeds dispersed by wind. Its root system is fibrous and helps stabilize soil in wetland environments.

Tommasinia altissima is valued for its ecological role in providing habitat for various wildlife species.
