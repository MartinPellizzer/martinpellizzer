Trichosalpinx pyxos

Trichosalpinx pyxos, commonly known as the pyxos spider orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its unique inflorescence, which consists of a dense cluster of small, spider-like flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments within tropical forests. Its leaves are elongated and grass-like, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor soils.

Trichosalpinx pyxos is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
