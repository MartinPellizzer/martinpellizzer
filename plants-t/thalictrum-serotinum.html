Thalictrum serotinum

Thalictrum serotinum, commonly known as late meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. It produces a showy display of flowers in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Thalictrum serotinum is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
