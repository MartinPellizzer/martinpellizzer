Triticum aestivum subsp. macha

Triticum aestivum subsp.

macha, commonly known as wild wheat, is a wild relative of cultivated wheat that exhibits a range of botanical characteristics. It is a grass species belonging to the Poaceae family, characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous florets, each containing a single grain. It is typically found in temperate regions and is adapted to a variety of soil types.

Due to its genetic diversity, it is of interest for agricultural research and breeding programs.
