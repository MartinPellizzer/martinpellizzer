Tripteris concordiae

Tripteris concordiae, commonly known as the concordia tripteris, is a species of flowering plant in the family Tripteridaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often divided into several narrow, lance-shaped segments. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in terminal inflorescences.

Tripteris concordiae is often found in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
