Tephrosia retamoides

Tephrosia retamoides, commonly known as the African milk bush, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It also produces a milky sap that can be toxic if ingested.

Tephrosia retamoides is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
