Tillaea kirkii

Tillaea kirkii, commonly known as the Kirk's tillaea, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves that are often covered in a powdery coating. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white or pale pink during the spring and summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous areas.

Tillaea kirkii is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
