Tilia bicuspidata

Tilia bicuspidata, commonly known as the two-ridged lime, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Tiliaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive double ridge. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are clustered in drooping inflorescences. Its bark is smooth and greyish, becoming fissured with age.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
