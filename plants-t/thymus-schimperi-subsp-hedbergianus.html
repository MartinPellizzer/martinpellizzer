Thymus schimperi subsp. hedbergianus

Thymus schimperi subsp.

hedbergianus, commonly known as Ethiopian thyme, is a perennial herb native to the highlands of Ethiopia. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and aromatic, glandular leaves that emit a strong thymol scent.

The plant produces small, pale purple to white flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun exposure.

Due to its unique chemical composition and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and herbalists alike.
