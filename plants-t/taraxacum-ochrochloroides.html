Taraxacum ochrochloroides

Taraxacum ochrochloroides, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the dandelion family (Asteraceae).

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, followed by the production of bristly, wind-dispersed seeds. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. It has a rosette of basal leaves that are usually hairy and have a slightly bitter taste.

Taraxacum ochrochloroides is a perennial species that can form dense colonies, sometimes becoming invasive in certain regions.
