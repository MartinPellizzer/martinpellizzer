Tragopogon elongatus

Tragopogon elongatus, commonly known as the slender goat's beard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides, and it can reach heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are long, narrow, and slightly hairy, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disk.

Tragopogon elongatus is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its resilience to environmental stress.
