Tiarella alternifolia

Tiarella alternifolia, commonly known as the foamflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It features delicate, umbrella-like clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has heart-shaped, toothed leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in woodland habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking shade-tolerant plants.
