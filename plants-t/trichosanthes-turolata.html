Trichosanthes turolata

Trichosanthes turolata, commonly known as the Mexican bitter gourd, is a climbing vine native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other gourds and cucumbers. The plant produces elongated, warty fruits that are typically yellow or orange when mature. Its leaves are heart-shaped with serrated edges, and it has hairy stems.

The fruit is often used in traditional medicine and as a vegetable in local cuisines.
