Tropaeolum brasiliense

Tropaeolum brasiliense, commonly known as the Brazilian nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Tropaeolaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, edible flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its flowers come in shades of yellow, orange, and red, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing seeds.

Tropaeolum brasiliense is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary uses, particularly in salads and as a garnish.
