Thymus kasakstanicus

Thymus kasakstanicus, commonly known as the Kazakhstan thyme, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces tiny, aromatic leaves that are used in traditional medicine.

Thymus kasakstanicus is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in local herbal remedies.
