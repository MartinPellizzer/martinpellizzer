Taraxacum eudontum

Taraxacum eudontum, commonly known as the European dandelion, is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its rosette of basal, spoon-shaped leaves and a hollow, leafless stem that supports a flowering head. The plant produces small, yellow composite flowers that develop into the familiar white, fluffy seed heads. It is native to Europe and has been introduced to other regions, often growing in disturbed soils and open habitats.

Taraxacum eudontum is known for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
