Tariri antidesma

Tariri antidesma, commonly known as the African star fruit or Natal star fruit, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its opposite, simple, and often lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its fruit is a fleshy, star-shaped berry that turns red when mature, containing several seeds.

The plant is often grown for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
