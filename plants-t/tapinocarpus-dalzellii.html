Tapinocarpus dalzellii

Tapinocarpus dalzellii, commonly known as the Natal plum, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible, yellow-orange fruits that are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
