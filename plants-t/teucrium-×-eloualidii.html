Teucrium × eloualidii

Teucrium × eloualidii, commonly known as the hybrid germander, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its arid habitat. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of blue or purple, typically blooming in the summer months. It is well adapted to rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

Due to its ornamental value and resilience, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
