Terpsichore canescens

Terpsichore canescens, commonly known as the silverleaf daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and showy yellow flower heads. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, giving them a distinctive, almost frost-like appearance.

The flowers bloom in spring and attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
