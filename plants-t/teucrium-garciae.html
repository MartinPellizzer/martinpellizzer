Teucrium garciae

Teucrium garciae, commonly known as Garcia's germander, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture.

Teucrium garciae is often found in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments.
