Trianthema mozambiquense

Trianthema mozambiquense, commonly known as the Mozambique thornveld groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its woody stems and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
