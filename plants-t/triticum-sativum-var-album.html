Triticum sativum var. album

Triticum sativum var. album, commonly known as white wheat, is a variety of wheat characterized by its pale color and lower protein content compared to other wheat types.

It is primarily cultivated for its flour, which is used in baking a variety of breads and pastries. The plant has slender spikes and produces small, pale grains that are typically harvested when dry. White wheat is well-suited to temperate climates and requires moderate rainfall for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a valuable crop in regions where its specific growing conditions are met.
