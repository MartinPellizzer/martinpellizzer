Tetrahit sulphureum

Tetrahit sulphureum, commonly known as the sulfur tetrahit, is a mineral rather than a plant, so it does not have botanical aspects.

It is a sulfate mineral composed of lead, sulfur, and oxygen, forming in hydrothermal veins. The mineral is characterized by its yellow to orange color and often exhibits a vitreous luster. It is commonly found in association with other sulfide minerals in ore deposits.

Due to its non-organic nature, it does not participate in botanical processes or plant biology.
