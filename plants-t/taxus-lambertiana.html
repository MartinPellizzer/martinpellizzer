Taxus lambertiana

Taxus lambertiana, commonly known as the Lambert yew, is a slow-growing evergreen conifer native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and reddish-brown bark that becomes flaky with age. The plant produces small, red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and historical significance, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
