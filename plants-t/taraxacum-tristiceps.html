Taraxacum tristiceps

Taraxacum tristiceps, commonly known as the pale dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of pale green, hairy leaves and a single, slender stem that supports a yellow flower head. The plant produces a distinctive seed head composed of numerous wind-dispersed seeds, each with a parachute-like structure. It is native to Europe and western Asia and typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats.

The pale dandelion is often found in meadows, roadsides, and other sunny areas.
