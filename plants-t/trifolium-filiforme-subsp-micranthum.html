Trifolium filiforme subsp. micranthum

Trifolium filiforme subsp.

micranthum, commonly known as the slender clover, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia. It features trifoliate leaves composed of three oval to lance-shaped leaflets, with a hairy texture.

The flowers are small, white or pale pink, and arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
