Tragacantha koschukensis

Tragacantha koschukensis, commonly known as the Astrakhan tragacanth, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by pods containing compressed, dark brown seeds. It is known for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments, making it an important species in xerophytic ecosystems.

The plant's resinous properties have also made it historically significant in traditional medicine and industry.
