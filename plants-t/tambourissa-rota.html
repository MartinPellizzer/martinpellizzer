Tambourissa rota

Tambourissa rota, commonly known as the spiral vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its helical growth pattern, where the stems twist around supporting structures in a spiral fashion. The plant produces small, pale green flowers that are typically clustered in racemes. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a pointed tip.

The botanical interest of Tambourissa rota lies in its unique morphological features and its role in traditional medicine.
