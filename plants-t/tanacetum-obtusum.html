Tanacetum obtusum

Tanacetum obtusum, commonly known as the woolly tansy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, gray-green foliage and yellow composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many familiar flowers like daisies and sunflowers.

Tanacetum obtusum is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.
