Tribulus trijugatus

Tribulus trijugatus, commonly known as the spurred cucumber, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is an annual herb that typically grows up to 1 meter in height, featuring branching stems and hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into spiny, orange to red fruits. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have three lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Tribulus trijugatus is known for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
