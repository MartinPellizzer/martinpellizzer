Tephrosia caribaea var. sericea

Tephrosia caribaea var. sericea, commonly known as the silky tephrosia, is a shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves and stems are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antifungal properties.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
