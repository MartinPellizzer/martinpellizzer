Trapa numidica

Trapa numidica, commonly known as the narrow-leaved water chestnut, is a floating aquatic plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It features distinctive, spiny, floating fruits that contain edible seeds, which are sometimes used in local cuisines. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves that are typically submerged, with a short stalk extending above the water surface. Its flowers are small and yellowish, appearing in clusters, and it reproduces both sexually and through vegetative propagation.

Trapa numidica plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
