Teucrium marum subsp. subspinosum

Teucrium marum subsp.

subspinosum, commonly known as narrowleaf germander, is a perennial herb native to parts of North America. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and streambanks.

The plant features slender, upright stems with opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, tubular flowers are usually blue or purple and appear in clusters, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is characterized by its distinctive spines on the leaf bases, giving it its subspecific name "subspinosum.".
