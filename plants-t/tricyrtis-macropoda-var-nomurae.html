Tricyrtis macropoda var. nomurae

Tricyrtis macropoda var. nomurae, commonly known as the Japanese spider orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, spider-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, pale yellow or white flowers with a distinctive, elongated spur. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
