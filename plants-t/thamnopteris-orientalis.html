Thamnopteris orientalis

Thamnopteris orientalis, commonly known as the oriental water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It thrives in freshwater environments such as ponds and slow-moving streams, where it forms dense, floating mats. The plant has long, slender fronds that are typically divided into small, delicate pinnae, giving it a feathery appearance. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, typical of the fern family.

Thamnopteris orientalis plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen to water organisms.
