Tanacetum budjnurdense

Tanacetum budjnurdense, commonly known as the Bujnurdense tansy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small yellow flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It is often found in mountainous regions and has a long flowering period during the summer months.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological niche, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
