Tragopogon badachschanicus

Tragopogon badachschanicus, commonly known as the Badakhshan thistle, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf structures. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture and produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Tragopogon badachschanicus is also noted for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
