Teratophyllum clemensiae

Teratophyllum clemensiae, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, translucent, leaf-like structures that resemble ghostly shapes, giving it its striking common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white, adapted to thrive in extreme environmental conditions. Its growth is slow and it is often found in rocky or sandy soils where it can retain moisture.

Due to its rarity and distinctive appearance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
