Tryphane rubella

Tryphane rubella, commonly known as the red-stemmed bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, reddish stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, depending on the variety. Its leaves are lance-shaped and typically have a slightly hairy texture.

Tryphane rubella thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
