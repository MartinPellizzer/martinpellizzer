Thymus ciliatus var. cinerascens

Thymus ciliatus var. cinerascens, commonly known as the gray thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, narrow leaves that are typically gray-green in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its essential oils contain thymol and carvacrol, which contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.
