Tropaeolum warmingianum

Tropaeolum warmingianum, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Tropaeolaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant red or orange flowers. The plant produces edible pods that contain seeds, which are often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are trifoliate, with a slightly hairy texture, and the stems are typically trailing or climbing.

Tropaeolum warmingianum thrives in warm climates and prefers well-drained soil.
