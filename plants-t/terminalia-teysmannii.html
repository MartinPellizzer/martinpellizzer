Terminalia teysmannii

Terminalia teysmannii, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and traditional uses, it is sometimes cultivated for timber and medicinal purposes.
