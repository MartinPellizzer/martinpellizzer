Trigonotis elevatovenosa

Trigonotis elevatovenosa, commonly known as the elevated-veined trigonotis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its unique, spurred flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with prominent veins that are elevated, giving it its specific name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in montane forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
