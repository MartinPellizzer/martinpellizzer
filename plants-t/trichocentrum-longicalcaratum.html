Trichocentrum longicalcaratum

Trichocentrum longicalcaratum, commonly known as the long-spurred orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, slender pseudobulbs and striking, showy flowers with elongated spurs. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a waxy texture.

The flowers, which can be white, pink, or purple, bloom in clusters and are highly attractive to pollinators.
