Tabernaemontana elliptica

Tabernaemontana elliptica, commonly known as the elliptical star of India, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and southern Asia.

It is characterized by its long, elliptical leaves and clusters of fragrant, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is valued in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

The flowers are typically nocturnal, opening in the evening and closing by dawn.
