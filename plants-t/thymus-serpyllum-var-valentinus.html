Thymus serpyllum var. valentinus

Thymus serpyllum var. valentinus, commonly known as Valentine thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features small, fragrant leaves that are rich in essential oils, giving it a distinctive aromatic profile. The plant produces clusters of pink to purple flowers, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for both ornamental and culinary use.
