Tragia discolor f. latifolia

Tragia discolor f. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved tragia, is a variant of the tragia plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are typically pubescent and have a distinct, waxy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, often accompanied by bracteolate structures. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
