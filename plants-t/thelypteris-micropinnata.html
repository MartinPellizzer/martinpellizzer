Thelypteris micropinnata

Thelypteris micropinnata, commonly known as the small-pinnate shield fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are divided into numerous small pinnae, giving it a finely textured appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its rhizomes are often covered in a dense layer of brown, velvety spores, which are essential for its reproductive cycle.

Thelypteris micropinnata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
