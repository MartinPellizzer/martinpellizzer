Tradescantia discolor var. vittata

Tradescantia discolor var. vittata, commonly known as the variegated spiderwort, is a striking ornamental plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive variegation pattern of white, pink, and green, which adds visual interest to gardens. The plant produces showy clusters of blue or purple flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial to full sun and prefers well-drained soil, making it a versatile choice for both container and in-ground planting.

Its foliage remains attractive throughout the growing season, offering year-round aesthetic value.
