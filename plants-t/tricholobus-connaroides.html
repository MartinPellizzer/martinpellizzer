Tricholobus connaroides

Tricholobus connaroides, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Connaraceae and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and timber use, it is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
