Tapinanthus pallideviridis

Tapinanthus pallideviridis, commonly known as the pale green mistletoe, is a parasitic plant belonging to the Loranthaceae family.

It attaches itself to the branches of its host trees, primarily in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant lacks true leaves and instead has scale-like structures, relying entirely on its host for nutrients. Its small, white or pale green flowers are often inconspicuous, and it produces small berries that may be dispersed by birds.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can cause significant damage to its host if left unchecked.
