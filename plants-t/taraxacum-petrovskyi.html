Taraxacum petrovskyi

Taraxacum petrovskyi, commonly known as the Karaganda dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Central Asia, particularly found in Kazakhstan and parts of Russia. This plant is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and a distinctive, hollow stem that supports its leaves. The leaves are typically basal, forming a rosette and are covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in dry, open habitats and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.
