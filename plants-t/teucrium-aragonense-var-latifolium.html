Teucrium aragonense var. latifolium

Teucrium aragonense var. latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf rock rose, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green leaves that are broader than those of the typical variety. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky or limestone soils and is often found in dry, sunny environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
