Tillandsia comulcoensis

Tillandsia comulcoensis, commonly known as the Comulco air plant, is a species of bromeliad native to Mexico.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on the surface of other plants without deriving nutrients from them. The plant features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that are covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help it absorb moisture and nutrients from the air. It produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a delicate touch to its appearance.

Due to its unique adaptations, it is well-suited to humid, shaded environments.
