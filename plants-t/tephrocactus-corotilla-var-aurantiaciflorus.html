Tephrocactus corotilla var. aurantiaciflorus

Tephrocactus corotilla var. aurantiaciflorus, commonly known as the golden-flowered tephrocactus, is a succulent cactus native to Mexico.

It features a cylindrical stem with prominent areoles and spines, typical of the Cactaceae family. The plant produces vibrant orange flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual element. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
