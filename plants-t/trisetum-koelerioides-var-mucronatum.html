Trisetum koelerioides var. mucronatum

Trisetum koelerioides var. mucronatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf beakgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a prominent midvein. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and along stream banks.

This plant plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
