Taraxacum rubisquameum

Taraxacum rubisquameum, commonly known as the red-squamate dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its hairy, reddish stems and yellow composite flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces a distinctive seed head with parachute-like seeds, facilitating wind dispersal. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Its botanical features make it an important part of the ecological landscape in its native range.
