Trichera × sambucifolia

Trichera × sambucifolia, commonly known as the hybrid elderberry, is a deciduous shrub characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is known for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and showy blooms. The plant produces small, dark berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Trichera × sambucifolia is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
