Trifolium stellatum subvar. stramineum

Trifolium stellatum subvar. stramineum, commonly known as the yellow alsike clover, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets with a pale yellow or white midrib. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in spherical heads, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and as a forage crop for livestock.
