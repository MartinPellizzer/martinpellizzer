Typhonium laoticum

Typhonium laoticum, commonly known as the Laotian arrowhead, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its arrow-shaped leaves and striking purple spadix. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its flowers are clustered in a spathe, which is a modified leaf that encloses the spadix.

Typhonium laoticum is also known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.
