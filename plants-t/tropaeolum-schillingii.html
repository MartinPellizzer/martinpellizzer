Tropaeolum schillingii

Tropaeolum schillingii, commonly known as the scarlet runner cress, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its bright red flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant is a member of the Tropaeolaceae family, known for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing as an annual in temperate climates.

Its seeds are small and can be dispersed by wind, contributing to its natural distribution.
