Trisetum buschianum var. kasbeghi

Trisetum buschianum var. kasbeghi, commonly known as the Kasbeghi grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and the plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate to cold climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
