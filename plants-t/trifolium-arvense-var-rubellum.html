Trifolium arvense var. rubellum

Trifolium arvense var. rubellum, commonly known as red clover, is a flowering plant in the legume family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three heart-shaped leaflets, and its pink or red flowers that bloom in spherical clusters. This plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been introduced to other regions. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed areas.

Trifolium arvense var. rubellum is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable for agricultural and ecological purposes.
