Tragopogon major subsp. dubius

Tragopogon major subsp.

dubius, commonly known as the yellow gooseneck, is a flowering plant native to western North America. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow ray florets and a distinctive, hollow, tubular stem.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces a single, showy flower head that blooms in late spring to early summer.

The species is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize new areas, making it a common sight in many regions.
