Tragacantha hymenocystis

Tragacantha hymenocystis, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a shrub or small tree native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody, branched stems and small, compound leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by distinctive, inflated pods that contain seeds. It is known for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments, making it an important plant in desert ecosystems.

The sap of Tragacantha hymenocystis has been used historically in traditional medicine and as a source of natural gum.
