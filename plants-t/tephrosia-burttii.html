Tephrosia burttii

Tephrosia burttii, commonly known as the African blue pea, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring slender, woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, which bloom throughout the wet season. Its seed pods are slender and cylindrical, containing several seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Tephrosia burttii is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of forage for livestock.
