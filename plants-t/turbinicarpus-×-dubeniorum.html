Turbinicarpus × dubeniorum

Turbinicarpus × dubeniorum, commonly known as the hybrid moon cactus, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Turbinicarpus caladium and Turbinicarpus stellatus.

It is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of Mexico, characterized by its rounded, segmented stems that resemble a moon. The plant produces striking, showy flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red during the blooming season. Its foliage is typically green, though some varieties exhibit variegated patterns.

Due to its unique appearance and ease of cultivation, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
