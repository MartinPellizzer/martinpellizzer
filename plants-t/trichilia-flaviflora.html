Trichilia flaviflora

Trichilia flaviflora, commonly known as the yellow-flowered trichilia, is a tropical tree native to regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae, which includes mahogany and other economically important hardwoods. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release flat, winged seeds, aiding in wind dispersal.

Trichilia flaviflora is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
