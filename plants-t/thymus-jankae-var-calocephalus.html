Thymus jankae var. calocephalus

Thymus jankae var. calocephalus, commonly known as the Calocephalus thyme, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and aromatic, gray-green foliage. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and mild, thyme-like fragrance.
