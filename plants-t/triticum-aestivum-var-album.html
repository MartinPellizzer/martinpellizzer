Triticum aestivum var. album

Triticum aestivum var. album, commonly known as white wheat, is a botanical variant of common wheat characterized by its lighter grain color.

It is primarily cultivated for its high protein content and is used in the production of various flour types. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with long, narrow leaves and spike-like inflorescences. Its seeds, which are white in color, are rich in nutrients and contribute to the plant's agricultural significance.

This variety is well-suited to temperate climates and requires a moderate amount of water for optimal growth.
