Trichoglottis oblongifolia

Trichoglottis oblongifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, typically arranged in a loose raceme. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Trichoglottis oblongifolia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
