Teucrium cylindricum var. decalvans

Teucrium cylindricum var. decalvans, commonly known as the smooth germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, cylindrical stems that are often hairy, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of blue or purple, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually covered in fine, soft hairs, giving them a velvety texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
