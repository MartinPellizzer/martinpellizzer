Taraxacum erntrum

Taraxacum erntrum, commonly known as the narrowleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and a hollow, hairy stem. The plant produces a single, yellow flower head that develops into a spherical seed ball with bristly seeds. It is native to Europe and is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides.

Taraxacum erntrum is known for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in herbal remedies.
