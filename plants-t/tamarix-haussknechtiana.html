Tamarix haussknechtiana

Tamarix haussknechtiana, commonly known as the desert rose, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, feathery foliage and pink or red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has small, scale-like leaves and produces woody, cone-like fruits that contain seeds. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep roots and a high tolerance for saline soils.

Tamarix haussknechtiana is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in dry environments.
