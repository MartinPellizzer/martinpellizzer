Taraxacum diversicolor

Taraxacum diversicolor, commonly known as the diverse dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of spoon-shaped, hairy leaves and a yellow flowering head that develops into a white, fluffy pappus for seed dispersal. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in a variety of habitats, including meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant is known for its ability to spread rapidly through wind-dispersed seeds, making it a common sight in temperate regions.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and a familiar weed in many landscapes.
