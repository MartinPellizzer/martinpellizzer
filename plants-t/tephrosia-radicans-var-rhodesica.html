Tephrosia radicans var. rhodesica

Tephrosia radicans var. rhodesica, commonly known as Rhodesian strangleweed, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It features trifoliate leaves with stipules and produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers arranged in clusters. The plant has a woody vine structure and can grow up to several meters in length. Its stems are often covered in fine, hairy pubescence, and it possesses a strong, distinctive odor.

The seeds are enclosed in small, hairy pods, and the plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets.
