Thelypteris chamaeotaria

Thelypteris chamaeotaria, commonly known as the ground fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Thelypteridaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, leathery fronds that are typically clustered near the ground. The plant produces sporangia on the undersides of its leaves, which are arranged in clusters called sori. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate forests.

Thelypteris chamaeotaria is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
