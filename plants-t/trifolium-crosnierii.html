Trifolium crosnierii

Trifolium crosnierii, commonly known as the Corsican clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Corsica, Sardinia, and parts of southern France. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and small white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or grassy areas.

Trifolium crosnierii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in nitrogen fixation, benefiting the surrounding ecosystem.
