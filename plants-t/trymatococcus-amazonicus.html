Trymatococcus amazonicus

Trymatococcus amazonicus, commonly known as the Amazonian star moss, is a rare and unique moss species native to the dense rainforests of South America.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found growing on decaying wood or rocky surfaces. This plant exhibits a distinctive star-shaped morphology, with tightly packed, needle-like leaves that form a dense, cushion-like structure. Its reproductive structures are small and inconspicuous, typical of mosses, and it relies on spore dispersal for propagation.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable indicator of undisturbed tropical ecosystems.
