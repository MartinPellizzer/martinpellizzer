Tricholaena rosea

Tricholaena rosea, commonly known as the red bamboo or red grass, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant red foliage and slender, bamboo-like stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.
