Talinum caffrum var. minus

Talinum caffrum var. minus, commonly known as the small-leaved Ethiopian spinach, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Talinum caffrum var. minus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
