Thymus hirtus

Thymus hirtus, commonly known as hairy thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant leaves that are densely covered with fine hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces tiny pink or purple flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Botanically, it belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage.
