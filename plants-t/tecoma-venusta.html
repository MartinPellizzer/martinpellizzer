Tecoma venusta

Tecoma venusta, commonly known as the yellow bells, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Tecoma venusta thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.
