Taeniophyllum pleistorhizum

Taeniophyllum pleistorhizum, commonly known as the ancient root fern, is a unique member of the Polypodiaceae family.

It is characterized by its primitive morphology, featuring a rhizome that produces both roots and fronds. The plant exhibits a distinctive reproductive system with sporangia located on the underside of its fronds. Its habitat is typically moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its rare occurrence and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical research.
