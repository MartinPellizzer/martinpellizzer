Trifolium haynaldianum

Trifolium haynaldianum, commonly known as Haynald's clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each composed of three oval to lance-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pink to white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in early spring. It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.

Trifolium haynaldianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in ecological restoration projects.
