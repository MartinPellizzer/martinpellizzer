Tephrosia natalensis subsp. pseudocapitata

Tephrosia natalensis subsp.

pseudocapitata, commonly known as Natal tephrosia, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of white or pale yellow.

The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds. It is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks, thriving in well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
