Taraxacum plovdivense

Taraxacum plovdivense, commonly known as the Plovdiv dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to southeastern Europe, particularly found in the region of Plovdiv, Bulgaria. This plant is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and a yellow flower head that develops into a white, fluffy seed head. It thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats, often growing in meadows and along roadsides.

Taraxacum plovdivense is notable for its ornamental value and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
