Tulipa laciniata

Tulipa laciniata, commonly known as the shaggy tulip, is a species of flowering plant in the family Liliaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fringed petals that give it a feathery appearance. Native to Central Asia, this perennial bulbous plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. The flowers typically bloom in spring, displaying shades of red, orange, and yellow.

Its unique morphology and vibrant colors make it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
