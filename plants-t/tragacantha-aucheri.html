Tragacantha aucheri

Tragacantha aucheri, commonly known as the Persian gum plant, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various pollinators and small fauna.
