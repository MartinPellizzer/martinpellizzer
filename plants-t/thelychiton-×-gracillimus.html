Thelychiton × gracillimus

Thelychiton × gracillimus, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a naturally occurring hybrid between Thelychiton macrophyllus and Thelychiton tenuifolius.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden beds. Its botanical significance lies in its role as an example of natural hybridization in the Orchidaceae family.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in horticultural settings.
