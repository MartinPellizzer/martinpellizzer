Tacca gaogao

Tacca gaogao, commonly known as the "bat plant" or "ghost flower," is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, velvety, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 2 meters in length. The plant produces dramatic, spadix-like flowers that resemble a bat, hence its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its unique appearance and growth habits make it a fascinating subject in botanical studies.
