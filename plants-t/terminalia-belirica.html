Terminalia belirica

Terminalia belirica, commonly known as the Indian blackwood, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and long, slender leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by elliptical, woody fruits containing several seeds. Its wood is highly valued for its durability and is used in furniture and musical instruments.
