Tanacetum flavovirens

Tanacetum flavovirens, commonly known as the greenish tansy, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, silver-green foliage and small, yellowish-green composite flowers. Native to Central Asia, this plant thrives in temperate climates and well-drained soils. It is often found in grasslands and open woodlands, where it contributes to the floral diversity.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
