Terminalia polycarpa

Terminalia polycarpa, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and simple, alternate leaves. The tree produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, fleshy fruits that are important in local diets. Its wood is valued for its durability and is used in construction and furniture-making.

The plant also has traditional medicinal uses in various African communities.
