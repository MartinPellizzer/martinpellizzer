Tetratheca calva

Tetratheca calva, commonly known as the slender tetratheca, is a species of flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is a small shrub characterized by its narrow, upright growth habit and small, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers with four petals, which bloom in spring. Tetratheca calva is often found in rocky or sandy soils in dry, open habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
