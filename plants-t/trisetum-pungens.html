Trisetum pungens

Trisetum pungens, commonly known as the needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, sharp-pointed leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes and wetlands. Its botanical name reflects its distinctive, bristly appearance, with "trisetum" referring to the three-sided stems.

Trisetum pungens plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
