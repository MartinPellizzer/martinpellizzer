Thalictrum campestre

Thalictrum campestre, commonly known as meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North America.

It features slender, often branching stems that grow up to 1.5 meters tall, adorned with finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces showy, yellowish-green or white, clustered flowers in early summer, which are arranged in complex, branched inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows, and is often found in moist soils.

Thalictrum campestre is valued in gardens for its ornamental foliage and delicate floral display.
