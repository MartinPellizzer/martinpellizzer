Tunica prolifera var. pubescens

Tunica prolifera var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy tunica, is a species of flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage, which gives it its varietal name "pubescens.". The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest undergrowth or along stream banks. Its leaves are elongated and often have a waxy surface, while its flowers are small and clustered in terminal inflorescences.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique texture and appearance.
