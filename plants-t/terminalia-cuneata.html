Terminalia cuneata

Terminalia cuneata, commonly known as the African sandalwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, elongated pods containing flat, winged seeds. It is valued for its aromatic wood, which is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.

The plant is also important in local ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
