Taraxacum suspectum

Taraxacum suspectum, commonly known as the suspected dandelion, is a species within the genus Taraxacum, which includes various dandelion-like plants.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves and a flowering stem that bears a composite flower head. The plant is typically found in grassy areas and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its flowers produce achenes with pappus, allowing for wind dispersal of seeds.

Due to its similarity to other Taraxacum species, it is often difficult to distinguish without detailed botanical analysis.
