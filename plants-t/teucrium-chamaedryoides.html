Teucrium chamaedryoides

Teucrium chamaedryoides, commonly known as blue germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer, and its lance-shaped, gray-green leaves with a rough texture. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, stream banks, and woodland edges. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.

The species is also used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
