Trifolium lividum

Trifolium lividum, commonly known as the pale clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets with a distinctive pale or bluish-green color. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. It is native to Europe and is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides.

Trifolium lividum is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in ecological restoration efforts.
