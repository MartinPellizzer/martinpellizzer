Thuja wareana

Thuja wareana, commonly known as the Hawaiian thuja, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the cypress family (Cupressaceae) and is characterized by its dense, scale-like leaves and compact growth habit. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to the humid, tropical climate of Hawaii. Its foliage provides habitat for various native insects and birds, contributing to the local ecosystem.

Thuja wareana is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
