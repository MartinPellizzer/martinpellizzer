Tagetes oaxacana

Tagetes oaxacana, commonly known as the Mexican marigold, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange and yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, with narrow, gray-green leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its ornamental value is further enhanced by its ability to attract beneficial insects like ladybugs and lacewings.
