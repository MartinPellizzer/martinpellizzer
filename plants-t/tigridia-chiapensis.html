Tigridia chiapensis

Tigridia chiapensis, commonly known as the Chiapas tiger flower, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its showy, brightly colored flowers that resemble those of the tiger lily. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring well-drained soil and full sunlight. Its foliage consists of long, narrow leaves, and it produces striking, tubular blooms in shades of red, orange, and yellow.

Tigridia chiapensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
