Tetrarrhena laevis

Tetrarrhena laevis, commonly known as the smooth tetrarrhena, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are smooth to the touch. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-pollinated flowers. Tetrarrhena laevis thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and along riverbanks.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat for small insects and serving as a food source for grazing animals.
