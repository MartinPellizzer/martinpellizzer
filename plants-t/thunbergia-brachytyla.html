Thunbergia brachytyla

Thunbergia brachytyla, commonly known as the narrowleaf morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves and produces trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its showy blooms.

Thunbergia brachytyla prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
