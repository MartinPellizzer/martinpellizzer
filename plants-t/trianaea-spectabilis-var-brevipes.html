Trianaea spectabilis var. brevipes

Trianaea spectabilis var. brevipes, commonly known as the dwarf trianaea, is a striking ornamental plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family, which includes palms, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, fan-shaped leaves. The plant typically features a short, slender trunk and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and ornamental landscaping.

The varietal name 'brevipes' refers to its short stem, distinguishing it from other varieties within the species.
