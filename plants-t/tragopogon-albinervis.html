Tragopogon albinervis

Tragopogon albinervis, commonly known as the white-veined thistle, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and cylindrical stems. The plant features silver-white veins on its leaves, which distinguish it from other members of the genus. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Tragopogon albinervis is often found in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows.
