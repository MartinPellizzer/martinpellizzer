Teline tribracteolata

Teline tribracteolata, commonly known as the three-bract bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. Teline tribracteolata is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive addition to gardens and natural habitats.
