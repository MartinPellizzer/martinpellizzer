Triumfetta tomentosa

Triumfetta tomentosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf or woolly false mallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate in shape and covered in fine, white pubescence.

Triumfetta tomentosa is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
