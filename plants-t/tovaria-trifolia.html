Tovaria trifolia

Tovaria trifolia, commonly known as the three-leaved fern, is a species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged symmetrically. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest undergrowth. Its fronds are usually long and narrow, with a delicate, feathery appearance.

Tovaria trifolia is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
