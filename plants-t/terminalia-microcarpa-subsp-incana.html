Terminalia microcarpa subsp. incana

Terminalia microcarpa subsp.

incana, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa. It is characterized by its smooth, pale grey bark and lanceolate leaves that are often pubescent on the underside.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, ellipsoid capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The species is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.
