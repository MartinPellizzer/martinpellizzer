Taraxacum tzvelevii

Taraxacum tzvelevii, commonly known as the Tzvelev's dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf morphology. The plant features a rosette of basal, spoon-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and it produces a hollow, hairy stem that supports its yellow flowering head. Its flowers develop into a characteristic seed head with pappus-covered seeds, facilitating wind dispersal.

Taraxacum tzvelevii is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing resilience to harsh climatic conditions.
