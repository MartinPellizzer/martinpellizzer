Tradescantia cucullata

Tradescantia cucullata, commonly known as the hooded spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, purple, or white, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Tradescantia cucullata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to spread through rhizomes, forming dense ground cover.
