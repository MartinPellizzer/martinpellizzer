Torreya grandis

Torreya grandis, commonly known as the Chinese torreya, is a species of coniferous tree native to eastern China.

It is characterized by its slender, evergreen foliage and distinctive, elongated cones. The tree typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in mountainous regions. Its leaves are needle-like and arranged in spirals, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Torreya grandis is also valued for its timber and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
