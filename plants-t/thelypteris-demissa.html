Thelypteris demissa

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Thelypteris demissa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
