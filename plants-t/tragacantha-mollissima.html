Tragacantha mollissima

Tragacantha mollissima, commonly known as Persian gum or tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of southern Europe.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and long, twisting pods. The plant is known for producing a viscous gum, which has been used historically in medicine, food, and industry. Its growth habit includes shrub-like stems and hairy, lance-shaped leaves, making it easily identifiable in its native habitat.

Tragacantha mollissima thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.
