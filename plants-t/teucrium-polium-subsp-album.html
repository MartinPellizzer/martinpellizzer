Teucrium polium subsp. album

Teucrium polium subsp.

album, commonly known as white germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features slender, upright stems and small, clustered white or pale blue flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and emit a strong, aromatic scent. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its medicinal properties have been historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
