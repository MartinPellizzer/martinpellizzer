Teucrium carolipaui subsp. fontqueri

Teucrium carolipaui subsp.

fontqueri, commonly known as the Fontqueri germander, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or streamside habitats. Its small, tubular flowers are usually violet or blue, and it produces small, four-angled fruits.
