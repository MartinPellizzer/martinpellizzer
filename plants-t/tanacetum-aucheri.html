Tanacetum aucheri

Tanacetum aucheri, commonly known as the blue button.

It is a perennial plant native to Central Asia, characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, blue to violet flower heads. The plant features finely divided, silver-green leaves that are aromatic and often used in herbal remedies. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, which extends from late spring to early autumn.
