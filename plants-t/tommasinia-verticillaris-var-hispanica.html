Tommasinia verticillaris var. hispanica

Tommasinia verticillaris var. hispanica, commonly known as the Spanish star-of-Bethlehem, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a whorl around the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
