Tragacantha larica

Tragacantha larica, commonly known as the Persian gum or Astragalus larica, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces a valuable gum resin, which has been used historically in traditional medicine and as a food additive. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in dry, temperate climates.

Tragacantha larica is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
