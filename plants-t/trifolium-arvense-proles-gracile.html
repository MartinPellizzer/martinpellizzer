Trifolium arvense proles gracile

Trifolium arvense proles gracile, commonly known as slender field clover, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets, and small white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and fields. It has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.

Its flowers are visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
