Tragia triandra var. pumila

Tragia triandra var. pumila, commonly known as the woolly tragia, is a low-growing shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive, soft appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a hairy texture, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

This species is often used in ornamental gardening due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
