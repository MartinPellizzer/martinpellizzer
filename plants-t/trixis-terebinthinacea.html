Trixis terebinthinacea

Trixis terebinthinacea, commonly known as the desert milkweed, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, gray-green leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that contains latex, which is a common trait among the milkweed family. Its root system is deep and drought-resistant, allowing it to thrive in harsh, sandy soils.

Trixis terebinthinacea plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is adapted to survive in extreme environmental conditions.
