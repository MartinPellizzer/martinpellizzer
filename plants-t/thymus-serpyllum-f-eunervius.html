Thymus serpyllum f. eunervius

Thymus serpyllum f. eunervius, commonly known as creeping thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, fragrant leaves that are typically gray-green in color, with a dense mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces tiny pink or white flowers in early summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and ground cover.

Its aromatic foliage is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
