Thymus brevipetiolatus

Thymus brevipetiolatus, commonly known as the short-petioled thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant leaves that are typically gray-green in color and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pink to lavender flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its aromatic properties, it is also used in culinary and medicinal applications.
