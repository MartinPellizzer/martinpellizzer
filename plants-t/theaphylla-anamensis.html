Theaphylla anamensis

Theaphylla anamensis, commonly known as the Anamensis Myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its bark and leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.

Theaphylla anamensis thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.
