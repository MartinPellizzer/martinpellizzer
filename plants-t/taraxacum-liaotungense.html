Taraxacum liaotungense

Taraxacum liaotungense, commonly known as the Liaotung dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and a yellow composite flower head. Native to China, particularly the Liaotung region, it thrives in temperate climates and well-drained soils. The plant is known for its ability to produce a large number of seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

It is often found in meadows, roadsides, and disturbed areas, contributing to soil stabilization and ecological diversity.
