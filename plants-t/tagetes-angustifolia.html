Tagetes angustifolia

Tagetes angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf marigold, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and vibrant yellow or orange flowers. This species belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for its ability to repel certain pests due to the presence of pyrethrin.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardeners.
