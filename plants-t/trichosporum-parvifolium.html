Trichosporum parvifolium

Trichosporum parvifolium, commonly known as the small-leaf trichosporum, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
