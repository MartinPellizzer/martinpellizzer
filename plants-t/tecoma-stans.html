Tecoma stans

Tecoma stans, commonly known as yellow bells, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves, and its flowers are typically borne in clusters at the ends of branches. Tecoma stans is adapted to a variety of soil types and thrives in full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant.

It is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
