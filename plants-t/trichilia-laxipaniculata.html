Trichilia laxipaniculata

Trichilia laxipaniculata, commonly known as the African sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and compound leaves with elongated leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody capsules containing aromatic seeds. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils in lowland and hill forests.

Due to its fragrant wood, it has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a source of incense.
