Tridens chapmanii

Tridens chapmanii, commonly known as Chapman's grass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces tiny white to pale blue flowers, which are arranged in dense clusters along the stem. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth, waxy surface.

Tridens chapmanii is often found in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies.
