Taxillus tomentosus

Taxillus tomentosus, commonly known as the mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches itself to the branches of host trees, primarily in the genus Eucalyptus.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while also producing its own carbohydrates through photosynthesis using sunlight. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers and red berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Its presence can weaken the host tree over time, potentially leading to its death.

Taxillus tomentosus plays an important ecological role by providing food for birds and other animals that consume its berries.
