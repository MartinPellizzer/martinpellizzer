Tritonia lilacina

Tritonia lilacina, commonly known as the lilac morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking violet to lilac-colored flowers, which bloom in the early morning. The plant produces trumpet-shaped blooms that are typically 5 to 7 centimeters in length. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture.

Tritonia lilacina is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a member of the Convolvulaceae family.
