Thymus oxydontus

Thymus oxydontus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic leaves and square stems. The plant typically features small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Thymus oxydontus is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, as well as its role in ornamental gardening.
