Townsendia strigosa

Townsendia strigosa, commonly known as the roughleaf tansy, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or arid environments.

Townsendia strigosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
