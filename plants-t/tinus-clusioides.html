Tinus clusioides

Tinus clusioides, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that appear in clusters. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate or elliptic in shape. It produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds.

Tinus clusioides is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.
