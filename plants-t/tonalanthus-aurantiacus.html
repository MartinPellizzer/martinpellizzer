Tonalanthus aurantiacus

Tonalanthus aurantiacus, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking orange-red tubular flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Tonalanthus aurantiacus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
