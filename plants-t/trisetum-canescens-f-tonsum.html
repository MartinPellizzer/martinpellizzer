Trisetum canescens f. tonsum

Trisetum canescens f. tonsum, commonly known as the Tonsum variant of the hairy woodoak, is a grass-like plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments, often in meadows or along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, each bearing small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
