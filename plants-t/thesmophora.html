Thesmophora

Thesmophora, commonly known as the "sacred fig" or "bodhi tree," is a species of fig tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is botanically classified under the genus Ficus and the family Moraceae. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and its production of edible fig fruits. The tree can grow quite tall, reaching up to 30 meters in height, and has a deep root system.

It plays a significant role in both ecological and cultural contexts, particularly in Buddhist traditions.
