Tragacantha malacophylla

Tragacantha malacophylla, commonly known as the soft leaf tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves and showy, pea-like flowers. The plant produces a gum that is harvested from its stems, which is used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils and along riverbanks.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
