Taraxacum byrrangicum

Taraxacum byrrangicum, commonly known as the Norwegian dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to northern Europe and is characterized by its bright yellow flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant produces a hairy, spiky seed head that disperses seeds via the wind. It thrives in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, often forming dense colonies.

Taraxacum byrrangicum is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
