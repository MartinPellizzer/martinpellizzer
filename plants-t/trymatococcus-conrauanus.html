Trymatococcus conrauanus

Trymatococcus conrauanus, commonly known as the African star-of-Bethlehem, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces small, white, star-shaped flowers during the summer months. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often grown as an ornamental in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid climates.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a valuable addition to xeriscaped landscapes.
