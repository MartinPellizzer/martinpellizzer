Thalictrum minus var. petrosum

Thalictrum minus var. petrosum, commonly known as the alpine meadow rue, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and delicate, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are clustered in racemes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
