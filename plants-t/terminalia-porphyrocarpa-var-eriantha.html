Terminalia porphyrocarpa var. eriantha

Terminalia porphyrocarpa var. eriantha, commonly known as the red sandalwood or African redwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its thick, dark bark and dense, reddish wood. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by woody, fleshy fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.
