Tephrosia adunca var. guaranitica

Tephrosia adunca var. guaranitica, commonly known as the Guarani tephrosia, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil and Paraguay.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant produces small, oblong pods that contain several seeds, and it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as savannas and grasslands.

The species is noted for its potential use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural insecticides.
