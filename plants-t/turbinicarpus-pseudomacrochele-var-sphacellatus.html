Turbinicarpus pseudomacrochele var. sphacellatus

Turbinicarpus pseudomacrochele var. sphacellatus, commonly known as the narrow-celled turbinicarpus, is a succulent cactus native to Mexico.

It features a compact, ribbed stem with a distinctive, narrow, and elongated shape. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers during the spring season. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it stores water in its fleshy stems.

This variety is prized for its unique appearance and is often cultivated by succulent enthusiasts.
