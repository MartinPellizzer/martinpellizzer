Tilia chingiana

Tilia chingiana, commonly known as the Chinese linden, is a deciduous tree native to China and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Tiliaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, which bloom in early summer. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

Tilia chingiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often planted in gardens for its shade and aesthetic appeal.
