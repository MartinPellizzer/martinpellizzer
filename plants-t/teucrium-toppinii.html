Teucrium toppinii

Teucrium toppinii, commonly known as purple germander, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its aromatic, slightly hairy leaves and spikes of purple or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their anti-inflammatory properties.

Teucrium toppinii is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
