Trigonotis angustifolia

Trigonotis angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf trigonotis, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a member of the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. Its botanical features include hairy stems and yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer months.

It is often found in wetlands and along stream banks, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
