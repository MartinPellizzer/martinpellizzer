Trachylobium verrucosum

Trachylobium verrucosum, commonly known as the rough-leaved tamarisk, is a shrub native to parts of Australia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, scale-like leaves that give it a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. It is often found in coastal and saline environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for native wildlife.
