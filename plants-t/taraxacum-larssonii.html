Taraxacum larssonii

Taraxacum larssonii, commonly known as the Larsson's dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, blue-green leaves and a single, tall flowering stem that bears a yellow composite flower head. It produces small, wind-dispersed seeds with pappus structures, typical of the dandelion genus.

Taraxacum larssonii is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
