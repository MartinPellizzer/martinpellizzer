Teucrium luteum subsp. xanthostachyum

Teucrium luteum subsp.

xanthostachyum, commonly known as yellow germander, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in spike-like clusters, typically blooming in summer. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, with a distinct minty aroma.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.
