Taxanthema caspium

Taxanthema caspium, commonly known as the Caspian thornstep.

It is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of Central Asia, including parts of Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant is characterized by its small, spiny leaves and yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring. It thrives in saline and alkaline soils, making it well adapted to harsh desert environments.

Taxanthema caspium plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for local wildlife.
