Tragia irritans

Tragia irritans, commonly known as the prickly glorybower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its spiny stems and leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are typically alternate and have a rough, hairy texture, while its fruits are small, dry capsules that contain numerous seeds.
