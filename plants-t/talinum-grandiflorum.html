Talinum grandiflorum

Talinum grandiflorum, commonly known as redleaf amaranth, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its vibrant red, pink, or purple leaves and showy, clustered flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its leaves are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines for their nutritional content.

Talinum grandiflorum thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
