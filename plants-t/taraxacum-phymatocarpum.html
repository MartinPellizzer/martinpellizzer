Taraxacum phymatocarpum

Taraxacum phymatocarpum, commonly known as the Armenian dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It features a rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces a single, elongated flower stem topped with a composite flower head that turns into a white, spherical seed head. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in late spring to early summer.

The species is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing its resilience and ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
