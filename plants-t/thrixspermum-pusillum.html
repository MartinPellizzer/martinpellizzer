Thrixspermum pusillum

Thrixspermum pusillum, commonly known as the small-haired thrixspermum, is a small, herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense covering of fine, hair-like structures on its stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences, typically blooming during the warmer months. Its leaves are usually narrow and linear, adapted to minimize water loss in its harsh environment.

Thrixspermum pusillum is often found in rocky soils and is well-suited to drought conditions due to its succulent-like leaves and deep root system.
