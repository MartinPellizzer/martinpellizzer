Telipogon tesselatus

Telipogon tesselatus, commonly known as the tessellated feathergrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, two-lobed flowers. It is typically found in arid or semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky or sandy soils.

Telipogon tesselatus is adapted to withstand drought conditions and is often used in restoration projects due to its hardy nature.
