Tabernaemontana membranifolia

Tabernaemontana membranifolia, commonly known as the fragrant star jasmine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green, elliptical leaves and clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow star-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, woody capsules containing numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil and partial shade.

Tabernaemontana membranifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and pleasant fragrance.
