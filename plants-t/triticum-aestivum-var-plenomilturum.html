Triticum aestivum var. plenomilturum

Triticum aestivum var. plenomilturum, commonly known as the hexaploid wheat, is a variety of wheat characterized by its genetic composition of six sets of chromosomes.

This variety is notable for its high yield potential and adaptability to various climatic conditions. It possesses a robust root system that enhances its drought tolerance and nutrient uptake efficiency. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 1.5 meters, with broad, bluish-green leaves and spike-like inflorescences.

Its grains are used in the production of various wheat-based products, including bread and pasta.
