Tingulonga carana

Tingulonga carana, commonly known as the African star apple, is a tropical tree native to Central and Eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and showy, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces a large, round fruit with a thick, leathery skin that turns from green to yellow or red as it matures. The fruit contains a sweet, juicy pulp with numerous small, edible seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruit in home gardens.
