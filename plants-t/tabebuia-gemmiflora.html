Tabebuia gemmiflora

Tabebuia gemmiflora, commonly known as the pink trumpet tree, is a deciduous tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking pink or red flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The tree has smooth, pale bark and produces small, round fruits that contain a single seed. Its leaves are alternate and ovate, with a glossy surface.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often planted in gardens and urban landscapes.
