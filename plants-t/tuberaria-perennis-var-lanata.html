Tuberaria perennis var. lanata

Tuberaria perennis var. lanata, commonly known as the woolly tuberaria, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its hairy, woolly stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
