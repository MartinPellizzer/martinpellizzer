Tectaria latifolia

Tectaria latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf tectaria, is a fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Tectariaceae and is characterized by its large, broad, lance-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks in moist, shaded environments. Its rhizomes are often covered in a thick, protective layer of sori, which contain the spores for reproduction.

Tectaria latifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
