Terminalia mucronata

Terminalia mucronata, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, light-colored bark and elongated, elliptical leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing flat, winged seeds. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils and can grow up to 20 meters tall.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
