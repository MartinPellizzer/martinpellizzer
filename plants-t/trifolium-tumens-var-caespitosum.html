Trifolium tumens var. caespitosum

Trifolium tumens var. caespitosum, commonly known as the tufted clover, is a variant of the clover genus characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit.

It is a perennial herb native to certain regions of Europe and is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides. The plant features trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, with a distinctive white or pale pink flower head. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it both a valuable forage plant and a common weed in agricultural settings.
