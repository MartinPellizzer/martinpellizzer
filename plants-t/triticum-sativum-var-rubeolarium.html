Triticum sativum var. rubeolarium

Triticum sativum var. rubeolarium, commonly known as red rye, is a variant of rye characterized by its distinctive reddish hues in its grains and foliage.

This variety is known for its robust growth and adaptability to a range of soil conditions, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates. The botanical features include narrow, grass-like leaves and a compact growth habit, with spikes bearing small, two-rowed grains. It is often used in agricultural settings for forage or as a cover crop due to its hardiness and rapid growth.

The plant's genetic makeup contributes to its resilience against certain environmental stresses, enhancing its ecological and agronomic value.
