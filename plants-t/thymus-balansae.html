Thymus balansae

Thymus balansae, commonly known as Balansae thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the eastern Mediterranean.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are often used in culinary applications, similar to other thyme species, and are valued for their aromatic properties.

Thymus balansae thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
