Tmesipteris oblanceolata

Tmesipteris oblanceolata, commonly known as the broad-leaf water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It is characterized by its delicate, floating fronds that are typically divided into small, oval-shaped segments. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in ponds and marshes. Its rhizomes form dense mats on the water surface, contributing to water filtration and habitat creation for aquatic life.

Tmesipteris oblanceolata plays a role in maintaining ecological balance in wetland ecosystems.
