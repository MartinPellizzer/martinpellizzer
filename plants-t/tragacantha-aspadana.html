Tragacantha aspadana

Tragacantha aspadana, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and parts of Central Asia. The plant is characterized by its woody structure, with a thick, corky bark that helps it survive in dry environments. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by pods containing hard, angular seeds.

The plant is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
