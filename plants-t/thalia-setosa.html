Thalia setosa

Thalia setosa, commonly known as the slender thalia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its stems are slender and often branched. Thalia setosa thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking native plants.
