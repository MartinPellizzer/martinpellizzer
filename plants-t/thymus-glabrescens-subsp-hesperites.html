Thymus glabrescens subsp. hesperites

Thymus glabrescens subsp.

hesperites, commonly known as the California thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States. It features aromatic, small, needle-like leaves that are densely packed along its stems.

The plant produces clusters of tiny, pink to purple flowers during the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a valuable plant for both ornamental and ecological landscapes.
