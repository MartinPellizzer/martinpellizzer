Tephrocactus erectocladus

Tephrocactus erectocladus, commonly known as the upright clump cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that grow in dense, upright clusters. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its green foliage. It has a shallow root system adapted to absorb moisture from the soil quickly.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in desert gardens.
