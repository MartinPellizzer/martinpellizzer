Thelypteris meniscioides

Thelypteris meniscioides, commonly known as the broad-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Thelypteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, arching fronds with a distinctive, broad, lance-shaped form. The plant's leaves are typically dark green and have a smooth, glossy texture, with a serrated margin. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

The fronds are biennial, with the fertile fronds appearing in the second year, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
