Toddalia sansibarensis

Toddalia sansibarensis, commonly known as the Zanzibar tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to the coastal regions of East Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. Its fruits are elongated pods containing seeds surrounded by a fibrous pulp.

Toddalia sansibarensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local communities.
