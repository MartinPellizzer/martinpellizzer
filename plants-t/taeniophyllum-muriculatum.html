Taeniophyllum muriculatum

Taeniophyllum muriculatum, commonly known as the velvet orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that grows on trees and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. Its unique appearance and delicate blooms make it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Taeniophyllum muriculatum requires careful attention to humidity and light conditions to flourish.
