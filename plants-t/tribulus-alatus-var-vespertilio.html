Tribulus alatus var. vespertilio

Tribulus alatus var. vespertilio, commonly known as the evening tribulus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Zygophyllaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the afternoon, giving it its common name. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.
