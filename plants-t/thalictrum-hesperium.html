Thalictrum hesperium

Thalictrum hesperium, commonly known as western meadow-rue, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features delicate, fern-like leaves and produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and the plant is often found in association with other woodland flora.

Thalictrum hesperium is valued in gardens for its ornamental foliage and ecological benefits.
