Trachycarpus griffithii

Trachycarpus griffithii, commonly known as the Chinese windmill palm, is a species of palm native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by triangular, edible fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for tropical gardens.
