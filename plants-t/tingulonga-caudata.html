Tingulonga caudata

Tingulonga caudata, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a tropical plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. The plant has a straight, slender trunk and can grow up to 10 meters in height. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

Tingulonga caudata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited to warm, humid climates.
