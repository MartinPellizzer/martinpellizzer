Triplaris paraguayensis

Triplaris paraguayensis, commonly known as Paraguay sweetgum, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the family Sapotaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and simple, opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils in tropical and subtropical regions.
