Tetrasynandra longipes

Tetrasynandra longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed tetrasynandra, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant produces yellowish-orange blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slight hairy texture.

Tetrasynandra longipes thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
