Trachypogon ledermannii

Trachypogon ledermannii, commonly known as the Ledermann's grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Trachypogon ledermannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
