Thymus magnus

Thymus magnus, commonly known as the giant thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense clusters. Its foliage is fragrant and has been historically used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Thymus magnus thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.
