Tinospora glandulosa

Tinospora glandulosa, commonly known as the hairy tinospora, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia and northern Australia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences, followed by elliptical, yellowish fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Tinospora glandulosa is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
