Thecacoris spathulifolia var. greveana

Thecacoris spathulifolia var. greveana, commonly known as the Greve's broad-leafed orchid, is a species native to certain regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves and delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellowish, with a distinctive lip that is often spotted or striped.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
