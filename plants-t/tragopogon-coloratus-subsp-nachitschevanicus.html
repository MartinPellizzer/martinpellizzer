Tragopogon coloratus subsp. nachitschevanicus

Tragopogon coloratus subsp.

nachitschevanicus, commonly known as the Armenian goat's beard, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region. It features basal rosettes of narrow, blue-green leaves and produces striking yellow ray flowers with a distinctive purple-tipped center.

The plant is characterized by its slender, branching stems and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
