Trapa japonica

Trapa japonica, commonly known as Japanese water chestnut, is a aquatic plant native to Asia.

It features floating leaves with long petioles and submerged leaves that are spirally arranged. The plant produces distinctive spherical, spiny fruits that contain edible seeds. It thrives in shallow freshwater environments and is often found in ponds and slow-moving streams.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
