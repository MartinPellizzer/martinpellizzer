Tragacantha mucronata

Tragacantha mucronata, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by pod-like fruits containing seeds. Its bark and sap have been traditionally used in herbal medicine for their potential health benefits.

The plant is also valued for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments.
