Tropaeolum patagonicum

Tropaeolum patagonicum, commonly known as the Patagonian nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, edible flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soil. Its seed pods are distinctive, often containing several small, round seeds.

Tropaeolum patagonicum is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its culinary uses.
