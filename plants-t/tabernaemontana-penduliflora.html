Tabernaemontana penduliflora

Tabernaemontana penduliflora, commonly known as the weeping tabernanthe, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its drooping clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Tabernaemontana penduliflora is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its elegant, cascading appearance.
