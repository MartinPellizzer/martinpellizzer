Tropaeolum looseri

Tropaeolum looseri, commonly known as the velvetleaf nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and bright, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant is a member of the Tropaeolaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Its seeds are edible and have a peppery flavor similar to that of watercress.
