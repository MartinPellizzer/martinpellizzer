Tragacantha venulosa

Tragacantha venulosa, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southwestern Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and woody, thorned stems. The plant produces a resinous sap that has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained soils with minimal water requirements.

Tragacantha venulosa is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for local wildlife.
