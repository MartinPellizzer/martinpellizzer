Typha capensis

Typha capensis, commonly known as the Cape reed, is a tall, herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and cylindrical, brownish flower spikes that appear at the top of the plant. The plant thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and lake shores, and is often found in shallow water or moist soil. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, which can help stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Typha capensis is also known for its ability to filter water and support diverse wildlife, making it ecologically significant in its habitat.
