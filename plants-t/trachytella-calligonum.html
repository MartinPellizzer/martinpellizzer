Trachytella calligonum

Trachytella calligonum, commonly known as the African milk tree, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive milky sap, which is toxic and can cause skin irritation. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers clustered in inflorescences. It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience to drought.

However, it requires careful handling due to its irritant properties.
