Trichomanes uniflorum

Trichomanes uniflorum, commonly known as the single-flower maidenhair, is a fern species belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, intricate fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant produces a single, small sporangium on each fertile frond, which is a unique feature among ferns. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Trichomanes uniflorum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
