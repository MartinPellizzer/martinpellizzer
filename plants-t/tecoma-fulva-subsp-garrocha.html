Tecoma fulva subsp. garrocha

Tecoma fulva subsp.

garrocha, commonly known as the yellow trumpet bush, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually ovate or elliptic in shape. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.
