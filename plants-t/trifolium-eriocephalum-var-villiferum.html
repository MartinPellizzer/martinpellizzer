Trifolium eriocephalum var. villiferum

Trifolium eriocephalum var. villiferum, commonly known as the woolly clover, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically covered in a dense layer of fine, woolly hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in spring, adding a delicate appearance to its foliage. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed sites.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and resilient plant for ecological restoration projects.
