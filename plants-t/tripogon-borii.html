Tripogon borii

Tripogon borii, commonly known as the slender spikegrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

Tripogon borii is often found in association with other native grasses and wildflowers in its ecosystem.
