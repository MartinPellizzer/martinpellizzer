Taraxacum mujense

Taraxacum mujense, commonly known as the Mexican dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its basal rosette of yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces a single, tall, hollow stem topped with a spherical seed head composed of numerous small, parachute-like seeds. It is often found in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Taraxacum mujense is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its resilience to environmental stress.
