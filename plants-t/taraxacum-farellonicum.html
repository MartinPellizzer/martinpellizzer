Taraxacum farellonicum

Taraxacum farellonicum, commonly known as the Farellon's dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features a basal rosette of spoon-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces, and it produces a distinctive yellow flower head that develops into a fluffy white pappus for wind dispersal. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and rocky areas.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
