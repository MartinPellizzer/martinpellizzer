Tournefortia ovata

Tournefortia ovata, commonly known as the white angel's trumpet, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in clusters along the branches. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Tournefortia ovata is also known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
