Trifolium vessiculosum

Trifolium vessiculosum, commonly known as the bladder clover, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each leaflet having a distinctive bladdery texture due to the presence of air-filled cells. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Trifolium vessiculosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
