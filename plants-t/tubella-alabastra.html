Tubella alabastra

Tubella alabastra, commonly known as the glassy tubeworm, is a species of polychaete annelid found in marine environments.

It is characterized by its transparent, tubular body that resembles a glass tube, providing camouflage in its habitat. The plant, though not a true plant, is often studied for its unique adaptations to aquatic life. Its body is segmented and covered in fine, bristle-like setae, which aid in movement and feeding.

Tubella alabastra plays a role in marine ecosystems by contributing to nutrient cycling and serving as prey for larger organisms.
