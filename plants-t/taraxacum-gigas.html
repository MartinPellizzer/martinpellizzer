Taraxacum gigas

Taraxacum gigas, commonly known as the giant dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its large, yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces a distinctive, hollow, stemless stem that can grow up to 1 meter in height, making it one of the tallest members of the dandelion genus. Its leaves are broadly lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it is often found in open, disturbed habitats such as fields and roadsides.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its resilience to environmental stress.
