Tabernaemontana discolor

Tabernaemontana discolor, commonly known as the Variegated Star of Bethlehem, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, waxy, star-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in shades of white and purple. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves with white or yellow markings, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its beauty and fragrance, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
