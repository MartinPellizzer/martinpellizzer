Taraxacum chlorofrugale

Taraxacum chlorofrugale, commonly known as the green-veined dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow composite flower head and heart-shaped basal leaves that are typically green with distinct veins. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. It produces a distinctive seed head composed of tiny, wind-dispersed seeds called pappi.

The species is often found in meadows, roadsides, and other sunny, well-drained areas.
