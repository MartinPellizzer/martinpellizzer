Taraxacum andinum

Taraxacum andinum, commonly known as the Andean dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces a yellow ray floret surrounded by a dense cluster of tubular disk florets. Its leaves are typically basal, rough, and have a distinctive serrated edge.

Taraxacum andinum is adapted to high-altitude environments and is often found in rocky or alpine regions.
