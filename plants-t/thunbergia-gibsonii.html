Thunbergia gibsonii

Thunbergia gibsonii, commonly known as the blue bell vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its attractive, tubular blue or violet flowers that resemble small bells, which bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has heart-shaped, dark green leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil and regular watering.

Thunbergia gibsonii is a fast-growing vine that can reach lengths of several meters if left unpruned.
