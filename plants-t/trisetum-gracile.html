Trisetum gracile

Trisetum gracile, commonly known as slender chaingrass, is a perennial grass native to wetland and marshy areas in North America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dense clusters, often forming a dense mat that helps stabilize soil in wet environments. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it produces wind-pollinated flowers.

Trisetum gracile plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
