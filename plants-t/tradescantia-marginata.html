Tradescantia marginata

Tradescantia marginata, commonly known as the wandering jew or snake plant, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its upright, succulent-like stems and lance-shaped leaves that often display striking variegation. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, typically in shades of white or pale pink. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers bright, indirect light.

Due to its adaptability and low maintenance, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
