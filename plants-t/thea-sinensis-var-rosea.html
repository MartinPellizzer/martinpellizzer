Thea sinensis var. rosea

Thea sinensis var. rosea, commonly known as the rose-scented tea plant, is a variant of the Camellia sinensis species.

It is characterized by its unique rose-like fragrance and delicate pinkish hues in its leaves and flowers. This plant is native to China and is cultivated for its aromatic leaves used in specialty teas. The flowers are often used in traditional Chinese medicine for their purported health benefits.

Its botanical features make it a prized ornamental and aromatic plant in horticulture.
