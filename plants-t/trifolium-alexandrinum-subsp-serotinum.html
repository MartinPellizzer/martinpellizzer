Trifolium alexandrinum subsp. serotinum

Trifolium alexandrinum subsp.

serotinum, commonly known as Egyptian clover, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, often with a slightly hairy surface.

The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
