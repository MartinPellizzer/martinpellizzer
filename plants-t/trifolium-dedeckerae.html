Trifolium dedeckerae

Trifolium dedeckerae, commonly known as Dedeckera's clover, is a flowering plant species native to specific regions in South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color.

Trifolium dedeckerae is adapted to grow in nutrient-poor soils, thanks to its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria.
