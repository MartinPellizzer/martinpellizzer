Tagetes palmeri

Tagetes palmeri, commonly known as the desert marigold, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers and hairy, narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as deserts and rocky soils. It has a short growing season and produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Tagetes palmeri is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
