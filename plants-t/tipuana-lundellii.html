Tipuana lundellii

Tipuana lundellii, commonly known as the yellow tipuana, is a deciduous tree native to Argentina.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought conditions. It produces legume pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Tipuana lundellii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to provide shade in urban landscapes.
