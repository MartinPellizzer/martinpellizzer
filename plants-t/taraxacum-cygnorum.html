Taraxacum cygnorum

Taraxacum cygnorum, commonly known as the swan's dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the sunflower family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow composite flower head, which consists of numerous small florets surrounded by a green, leafy involucrum. The plant produces a distinctive, hollow stem with a milky sap, and its leaves are typically lance-shaped with a hairy surface. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grassy areas.

Taraxacum cygnorum is known for its resilience and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
