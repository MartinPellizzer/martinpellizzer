Trapa brevicarpa var. delicata

Trapa brevicarpa var. delicata, commonly known as the delicate water chestnut, is a aquatic plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its small, round, spiny fruits that float on the water surface. The plant has submerged leaves that are long and strap-like, aiding in photosynthesis underwater. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

Its unique morphology makes it both ecologically significant and a subject of botanical interest.
