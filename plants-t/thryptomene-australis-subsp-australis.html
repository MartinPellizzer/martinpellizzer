Thryptomene australis subsp. australis

Thryptomene australis subsp.

australis, commonly known as the Australian thryptomene, is a shrub native to southern Australia. It typically grows in heathlands and coastal areas, thriving in well-drained sandy soils.

The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small white or pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in spring.

The species is valued for its ecological role and is sometimes used in horticulture for its ornamental qualities.
