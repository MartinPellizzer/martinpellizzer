Tephrosia quartiniana var. inflexa

Tephrosia quartiniana var. inflexa, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and pairs of opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. Its seeds are enclosed in follicles that release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

This species is often found in open grasslands and along riverbanks, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
