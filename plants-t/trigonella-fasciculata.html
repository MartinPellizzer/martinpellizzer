Trigonella fasciculata

Trigonella fasciculata, commonly known as Egyptian burclover, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Asia.

It is an annual herb that grows up to 30 centimeters tall, characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Trigonella fasciculata is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
