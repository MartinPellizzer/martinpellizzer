Tunica angustifolia

Tunica angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed tunica, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale blue or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
