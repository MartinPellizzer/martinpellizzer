Tomanthea obtusifolia

Tomanthea obtusifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed tomanthea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Tomanthea obtusifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
