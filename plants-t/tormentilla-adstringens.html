Tormentilla adstringens

Tormentilla adstringens, commonly known as the Spanish heather, is a flowering shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are small, leathery, and evergreen, contributing to its ornamental value.

Tormentilla adstringens is often cultivated for its ability to thrive in poor, acidic soils and its resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
