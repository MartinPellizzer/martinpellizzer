Taraxacum jailae

Taraxacum jailae, commonly known as the Jordanian dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the sunflower family.

It is native to the Middle East, specifically found in Jordan and parts of Saudi Arabia. The plant features a basal rosette of pale green, hairy leaves that are slightly lobed. It produces a yellow composite flower head that blooms in early spring, followed by a fluffy white pappus for seed dispersal.

Taraxacum jailae is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing resilience to drought conditions.
