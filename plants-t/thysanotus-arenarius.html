Thysanotus arenarius

Thysanotus arenarius, commonly known as the sandflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that range in color from white to violet, often with a distinctive hooded shape. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is typically found in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, adapted to reduce water loss in harsh conditions.

Thysanotus arenarius is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
