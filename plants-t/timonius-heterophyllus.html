Timonius heterophyllus

Timonius heterophyllus, commonly known as the variable-leaf timonius, is a rare and unique species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinct variation in leaf morphology, which can range from ovate to lanceolate shapes depending on environmental conditions. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountainous forests. Its flowers are small and clustered, often displaying a mix of white and green hues.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a botanical curiosity and a subject of interest for researchers studying plant adaptation.
