Trifolium rechingeri

Trifolium rechingeri, commonly known as the Rechinger's clover, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Trifolium rechingeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
