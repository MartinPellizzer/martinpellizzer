Taraxacum rorippa

Taraxacum rorippa, commonly known as the water dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and a single, upright flowering stem. The plant produces small, yellow composite flowers that develop into fluffy, white seed heads. It thrives in wet or marshy habitats, often found near water bodies.

Its botanical features make it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
