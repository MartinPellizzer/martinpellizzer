Thuja orientalis var. conspicua

Thuja orientalis var. conspicua, commonly known as the golden spruce, is a variety of the eastern arborvitae.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow foliage, which contrasts beautifully with its dark green needles. This evergreen shrub or small tree is native to eastern North America and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It typically grows to a height of 10 to 20 feet and has a pyramidal shape.

The plant is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to provide dense coverage.
