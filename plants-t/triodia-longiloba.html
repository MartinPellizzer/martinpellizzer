Triodia longiloba

Triodia longiloba, commonly known as the spinifex grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, wiry growth habit and the presence of sharp, spiny leaves. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, thriving in sandy or gravelly soils. Its small, spike-like inflorescences produce minute flowers, which are adapted to the harsh environmental conditions.

Triodia longiloba plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various desert organisms.
