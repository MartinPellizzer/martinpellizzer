Thymus leucotrichus subsp. leucotrichus

Thymus leucotrichus subsp.

leucotrichus, commonly known as white thyme, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, fragrant leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs.

The plant produces small, pink to violet flowers in early summer, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic leaves are used in culinary and medicinal applications, contributing to its historical significance in herbal medicine.
