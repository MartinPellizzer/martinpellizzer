Trifolium pratense var. australe

Trifolium pratense var. australe, commonly known as the Australian white clover, is a variant of the common red clover species.

It is a low-growing perennial herb native to Australia and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from spring to autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in pastureland for its nutritional value to livestock.

Additionally, it plays a role in nitrogen fixation, improving soil fertility.
