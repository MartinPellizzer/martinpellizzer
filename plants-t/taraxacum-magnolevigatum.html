Taraxacum magnolevigatum

Taraxacum magnolevigatum, commonly known as the giant dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by fluffy white seed heads that are easily dispersed by the wind. It is native to parts of Europe and Asia, where it thrives in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides.

Due to its robust growth and adaptability, it is often considered an invasive species in certain regions.
