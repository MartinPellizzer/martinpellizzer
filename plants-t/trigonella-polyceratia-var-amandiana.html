Trigonella polyceratia var. amandiana

Trigonella polyceratia var. amandiana, commonly known as the Ammanian bitter vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, erect stems with trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense spikes, followed by elongated, cylindrical pods containing several seeds. It is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and potential agricultural use.
