Trigonella bakhtiarica

Trigonella bakhtiarica, commonly known as the Bakhtiaric fenugreek, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into slender, hairy pods. It is adapted to survive in dry environments, with deep roots that help it access water from deeper soil layers.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and potential agricultural applications in arid zones.
