Thismia nigricoronata

Thismia nigricoronata, commonly known as the black-crowned Thismia, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, often strikingly colored flowers. The plant is notable for its lack of chlorophyll, making it a mycoheterotroph, relying on fungal associations for nutrients. Its flowers typically feature a dark, almost black, crown-like structure, which gives it its common name.

Thismia nigricoronata is often found in shaded, humid environments within tropical forests.
