Tabernaemontana arcuata

Tabernaemontana arcuata, commonly known as the waxy begonia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, waxy leaves and clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and showy blooms. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.

Tabernaemontana arcuata is relatively low maintenance, making it a popular choice for indoor gardens.
