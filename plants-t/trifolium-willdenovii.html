Trifolium willdenovii

Trifolium willdenovii, commonly known as the Willdenow's clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to parts of Europe and Asia, particularly in regions with temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, consisting of three leaflets, and small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed sites.

Trifolium willdenovii is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
