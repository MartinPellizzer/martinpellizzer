Thunbergia graminifolia

Thunbergia graminifolia, commonly known as the grass-leaved morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features slender stems and heart-shaped, grass-like leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of blue, violet, and white, typically blooming in the morning. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Thunbergia graminifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant.
