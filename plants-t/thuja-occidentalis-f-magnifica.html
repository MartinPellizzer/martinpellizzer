Thuja occidentalis f. magnifica

Thuja occidentalis f. magnifica, commonly known as the Eastern White Cedar, is a cultivar of the arborvitae genus native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its dense, conical shape and scale-like foliage that is a deep, vibrant green. The plant produces small, yellowish-green female cones and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for hedges and privacy screens.

This cultivar is also valued for its ability to tolerate urban conditions and its resistance to pests and diseases.
