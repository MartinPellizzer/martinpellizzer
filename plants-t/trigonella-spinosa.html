Trigonella spinosa

Trigonella spinosa, commonly known as goat's beard, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, triangular-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant is an annual or biennial herb that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Trigonella spinosa is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
