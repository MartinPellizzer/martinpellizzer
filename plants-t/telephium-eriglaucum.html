Telephium eriglaucum

Telephium eriglaucum, commonly known as the white ray flover, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant features basal rosettes of silver-green, woolly leaves that are covered in fine hairs, providing a distinct appearance. Its flowers are typically white or pale blue, with a showy central disk surrounded by petal-like rays.

Telephium eriglaucum is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in cool, moist environments.
