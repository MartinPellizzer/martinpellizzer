Tinus rugosus

Tinus rugosus, commonly known as the rough tinus, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Tinus rugosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
