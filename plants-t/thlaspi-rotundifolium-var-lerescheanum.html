Thlaspi rotundifolium var. lerescheanum

Thlaspi rotundifolium var. lerescheanum, commonly known as the Leresche's bladderwort, is a species of flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, round, fleshy leaves that resemble bladders, a feature that gives it its common name. The plant produces tiny white or yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically in late spring to early summer. It is often found in moist, calcareous soils in alpine or subalpine regions.

This variety is notable for its unique morphology and ecological adaptations to its specialized habitat.
