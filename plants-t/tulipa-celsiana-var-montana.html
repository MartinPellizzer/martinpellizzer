Tulipa celsiana var. montana

Tulipa celsiana var. montana, commonly known as the Mountain Crown tulip, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming in early spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It is well adapted to cold climates and can tolerate light frosts.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
