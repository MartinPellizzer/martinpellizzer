Tessaria integrifolia var. polyandra

Tessaria integrifolia var. polyandra, commonly known as the broad-leaved tessaria, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that are typically smooth and entire along the edges. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This variant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
