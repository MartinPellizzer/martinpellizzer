Tsuga canadensis var. sargentiana

Tsuga canadensis var. sargentiana, commonly known as Sargent's hemlock, is a variant of the eastern hemlock native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its dense, conical shape and dark green, needle-like foliage. The leaves are short and often have a slight twist, contributing to its distinct appearance. It produces small, cylindrical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and shade-providing capabilities.
