Thymus willdenowii

Thymus willdenowii, commonly known as narrowleaf thyme, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, creeping habit and small, aromatic leaves that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Botanically, it belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic compounds and medicinal properties.
