Telis occulta

Telis occulta, commonly known as the hidden telis, is a rare and enigmatic plant species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and small, clustered leaves that are often overlooked due to its inconspicuous appearance. The plant produces delicate, pale green flowers that bloom in the early morning, adding a subtle beauty to its foliage. Its botanical significance lies in its unique adaptations to shaded, moist environments, which contribute to its limited distribution.

Due to habitat loss and limited research, Telis occulta remains a subject of interest for botanists studying plant resilience and ecological niches.
