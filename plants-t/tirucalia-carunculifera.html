Tirucalia carunculifera

Tirucalia carunculifera, commonly known as the Indian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Tirucalia carunculifera is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
