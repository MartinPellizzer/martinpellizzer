Tecoma diversifolia

Tecoma diversifolia, commonly known as the yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has opposite, simple, and usually ovate or lanceolate leaves. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Tecoma diversifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
