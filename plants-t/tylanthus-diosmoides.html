Tylanthus diosmoides

Tylanthus diosmoides, commonly known as the silky tamarind, is a flowering plant native to parts of Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height, characterized by its smooth, grey bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds.

Tylanthus diosmoides is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
