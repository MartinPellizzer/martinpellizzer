Thamnocalamus spathiflorus var. bhutanensis

Thamnocalamus spathiflorus var. bhutanensis, commonly known as the Bhutanese bamboo, is a member of the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to the Himalayan region, particularly found in Bhutan and parts of eastern India. This variant is characterized by its slender, erect culms and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments at higher altitudes.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has limited use in traditional practices.
