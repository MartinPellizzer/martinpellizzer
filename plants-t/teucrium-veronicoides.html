Teucrium veronicoides

Teucrium veronicoides, commonly known as blue germander, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 3 feet tall. The plant produces small, clustered blue or purplish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped, serrated, and have a distinctive minty scent when crushed.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.
