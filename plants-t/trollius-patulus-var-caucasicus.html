Trollius patulus var. caucasicus

Trollius patulus var. caucasicus, commonly known as the Caucasian globeflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow, globe-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in mountainous areas. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces a tall, branching stem.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, eye-catching blooms.
