Trichosporum angustifolium

Trichosporum angustifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaf trichosporum, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in early spring. The plant is native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It has a low-growing habit and is often found in woodland edges or along stream banks.

Trichosporum angustifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and floral display.
