Taraxacum hoedicense

Taraxacum hoedicense, commonly known as the Hoedink's dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated, hairy leaves and a yellow composite flower head that develops into a fluffy white pappus. Native to southern Europe, it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant has a taproot system and is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and vegetative reproduction.

It is often found in grasslands, roadsides, and agricultural fields.
