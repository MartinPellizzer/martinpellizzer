Telis raphanina

Telis raphanina, commonly known as the common wallflower, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and bright, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors such as yellow, orange, and red. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along pathways. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and covered in a fine layer of pubescence.

Telis raphanina is an annual or biennial plant that thrives in temperate climates.
