Taraxacum praesigne

Taraxacum praesigne, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and a flowering stem that bears a single, showy flower head. The flower head consists of numerous yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular florets. This plant is native to Europe and is often found in disturbed soils, meadows, and roadsides.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions and is commonly considered a weed in agricultural settings.
