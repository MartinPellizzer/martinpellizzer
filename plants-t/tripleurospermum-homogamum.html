Tripleurospermum homogamum

Tripleurospermum homogamum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and meadows, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, while its flowers are yellow, often forming dense clusters.

It is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain environments.
