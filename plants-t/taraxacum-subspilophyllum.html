Taraxacum subspilophyllum

Taraxacum subspilophyllum, commonly known as the pale hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of pale green, hairy leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in the summer months. Native to parts of Europe and Asia, this species thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and forest edges. The plant has a taproot system and is often found in disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it distinguishable from other Taraxacum species due to its lighter coloration and specific leaf morphology.
