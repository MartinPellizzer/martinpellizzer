Thamnea depressa

Thamnea depressa, commonly known as the dwarf bluetail, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in spring. It is often found in open grasslands and is adapted to dry, sunny conditions.

Thamnea depressa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
