Teijsmanniodendron subspicatum

Teijsmanniodendron subspicatum, commonly known as the Bursera subspicata, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, commonly known as the frankincense and myrrh family. The plant is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and dense, glossy green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. It produces small, white flowers that are typically followed by spherical, yellowish fruits.
