Tetradenia longifolia

Tetradenia longifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tetradenia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a small shrub characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

Tetradenia longifolia is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
