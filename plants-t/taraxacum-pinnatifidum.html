Taraxacum pinnatifidum

Taraxacum pinnatifidum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, pinnatifid (lobed) leaves and a yellow composite flower head that develops into a spherical seed ball. The plant is native to Europe and is often found in disturbed soils, meadows, and along roadsides. It has a taproot system and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

The species is also noted for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in herbal remedies.
