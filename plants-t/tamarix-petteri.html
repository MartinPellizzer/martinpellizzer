Tamarix petteri

Tamarix petteri, commonly known as the desert rose, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and clusters of pink or red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep root systems and a low, spreading growth habit. Its wood is lightweight and has been historically used for construction and fuel.

Tamarix petteri plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
