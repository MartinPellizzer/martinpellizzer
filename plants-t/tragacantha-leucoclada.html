Tragacantha leucoclada

Tragacantha leucoclada, commonly known as the white tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are followed by elongated pods containing small, kidney-shaped seeds. Its roots are known for producing a gelatinous substance called tragacanth, which has been used historically in medicine and as a thickening agent.

This species is often cultivated for its economic and medicinal properties.
