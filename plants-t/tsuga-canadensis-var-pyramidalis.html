Tsuga canadensis var. pyramidalis

Tsuga canadensis var. pyramidalis, commonly known as the pyramidal hemlock, is a variant of the Canadian hemlock native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its conical shape and dense, dark green foliage that provides a striking visual contrast against the landscape. The plant has needle-like leaves that are short and stout, arranged in spirals along the branches. It produces small, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes due to its compact form and evergreen nature.
