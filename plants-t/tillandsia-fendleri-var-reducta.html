Tillandsia fendleri var. reducta

Tillandsia fendleri var. reducta, commonly known as the Mexican air plant, is a species of epiphytic bromeliad native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help in absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air. The plant produces a striking inflorescence with bright orange or red flowers, which bloom after the plant has matured. It thrives in well-draining, airy environments and requires indirect sunlight.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
