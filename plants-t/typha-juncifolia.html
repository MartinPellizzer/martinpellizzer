Typha juncifolia

Typha juncifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf cattail, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical flower spike that is typically brown or reddish in color. The plant thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, pond edges, and along riverbanks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its roots form dense mats that help stabilize soil and filter water.

Typha juncifolia plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
