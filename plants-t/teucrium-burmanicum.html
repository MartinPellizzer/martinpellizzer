Teucrium burmanicum

Teucrium burmanicum, commonly known as Burman's germander, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and small, clustered flowers that range in color from purple to white. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Teucrium burmanicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
