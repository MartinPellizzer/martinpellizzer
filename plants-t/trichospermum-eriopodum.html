Trichospermum eriopodum

Trichospermum eriopodum, commonly known as the hairy burr or velvet burr, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring small, elliptical leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences, which later develop into spiny, woody fruits. Its unique morphology and adaptability to arid conditions make it an important species in its native habitat.

The plant's hairy leaves and spiny fruits are key botanical features that aid in its survival and dispersal.
