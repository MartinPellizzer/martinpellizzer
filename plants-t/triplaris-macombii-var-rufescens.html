Triplaris macombii var. rufescens

Triplaris macombii var. rufescens, commonly known as the red-stemmed triplaris, is a shrub native to the Caribbean region.

It is characterized by its woody stems that turn reddish-brown with age, giving it its common name. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth, slightly hairy surface. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, three-lobed fruits.

This species is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and well-drained soils.
