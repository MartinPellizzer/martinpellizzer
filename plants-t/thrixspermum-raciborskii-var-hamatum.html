Thrixspermum raciborskii var. hamatum

Thrixspermum raciborskii var. hamatum, commonly known as the golden alga, is a unicellular cyanobacterium that forms colonies.

It is notable for its ability to produce toxic compounds, including saxitoxin, which can be harmful to aquatic life and humans. This species thrives in nutrient-rich freshwater environments, often in lakes and reservoirs. Its rapid growth can lead to harmful algal blooms, impacting water quality and ecosystem health.

The plant's unique morphology includes long, filamentous cells that can form dense mats on the water surface.
