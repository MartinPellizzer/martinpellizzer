Tecomaria roseifolia

Tecomaria roseifolia, commonly known as the rose-colored tecomaria, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to rose-colored flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and slightly hairy, with a leathery texture.

Tecomaria roseifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
