Tradescantia rosea var. graminea

Tradescantia rosea var. graminea, commonly known as the Spider Plant, is a striking ornamental plant characterized by its long, arching leaves that resemble spider legs.

The leaves are typically bright green with a silvery-blue hue along the edges, and they often have a waxy texture. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple, which appear in clusters during the spring or summer. This variety is a compact form of the species, making it ideal for indoor cultivation or as a trailing plant in gardens.

It is known for its resilience, thriving in a variety of light conditions and requiring minimal maintenance.
