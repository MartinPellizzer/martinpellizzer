Tilia megaphylla f. subintegra

Tilia megaphylla f. subintegra, commonly known as the large-leaf linden, is a unique variant of the linden tree characterized by its large, ovate leaves.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central Asia, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clustered inflorescences, which bloom in early summer. Its bark is smooth and greyish-brown, often developing fissures with age.

This form is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
