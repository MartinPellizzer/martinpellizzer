Trigonella plicata

Trigonella plicata, commonly known as the Persian melilot, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is native to regions of the Middle East and Central Asia, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid climates. Its seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a spice, while the plant itself is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

Trigonella plicata plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
