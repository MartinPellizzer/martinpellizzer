Trigonotis myosotidea

Trigonotis myosotidea, commonly known as the blue bell vine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and slightly hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Trigonotis myosotidea thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated as a decorative vine in gardens.
