Tussilago elongata

Tussilago elongata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved coughwort, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Tussilago elongata is often found in moist, calcareous soils in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.

It is noted for its medicinal properties, historically used to treat respiratory ailments.
