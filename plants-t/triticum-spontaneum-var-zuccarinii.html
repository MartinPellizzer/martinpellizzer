Triticum spontaneum var. zuccarinii

Triticum spontaneum var. zuccarinii, commonly known as wild wheat or goat grass, is a wild species of wheat native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a grass with slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces awned lemmas and is characterized by its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and open habitats. It is often found growing along roadsides, fields, and in dry grasslands.

This variety is of interest to botanists and agricultural researchers due to its potential for genetic diversity and adaptability.
