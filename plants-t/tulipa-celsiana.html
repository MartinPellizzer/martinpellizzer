Tulipa celsiana

Tulipa celsiana, commonly known as the Celsian tulip, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a bulbous perennial plant that produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in a range of colors, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically blooms in late spring and early summer, and its foliage consists of narrow, strap-like leaves. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.

The Celsian tulip is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in horticultural cultivation.
