Tilia petiolaris

Tilia petiolaris, commonly known as the heartleaf linden, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically 5 to 10 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, fragrant yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are clustered in pendulous inflorescences. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping due to its attractive foliage and showy blooms.
