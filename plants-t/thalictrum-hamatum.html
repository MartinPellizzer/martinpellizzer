Thalictrum hamatum

Thalictrum hamatum, commonly known as the showy avens, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features delicate, nodding white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves fully emerge. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are arranged in a whorled pattern around the stem. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Thalictrum hamatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
