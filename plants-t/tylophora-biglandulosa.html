Tylophora biglandulosa

Tylophora biglandulosa, commonly known as the hairy tylophora, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive hairy stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that are clustered in umbels, often followed by small, round berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in horticultural settings.
