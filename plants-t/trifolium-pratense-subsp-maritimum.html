Trifolium pratense subsp. maritimum

Trifolium pratense subsp.

maritimum, commonly known as coastal red clover, is a subspecies of red clover adapted to coastal environments. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically pinkish in color and have a hairy texture.

The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dunes or coastal grasslands.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing forage for livestock.
