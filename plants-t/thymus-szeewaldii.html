Thymus szeewaldii

Thymus szeewaldii, commonly known as the Szeewald thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Turkey and the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are fragrant and contain essential oils, making it useful in both culinary and medicinal applications.

Thymus szeewaldii thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or alpine environments.
