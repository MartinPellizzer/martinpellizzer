Turnera oculata var. oculata

Turnera oculata var. oculata, commonly known as the "Mexican buttercup," is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 feet. The plant features showy yellow flowers with prominent orange markings, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
