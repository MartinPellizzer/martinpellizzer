Tabernaemontana crassifolia

Tabernaemontana crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved angel's trumpet, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, waxy, heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant belongs to the family Apocynaceae, which includes many species with toxic properties. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
