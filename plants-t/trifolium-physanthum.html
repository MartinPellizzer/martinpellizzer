Trifolium physanthum

Trifolium physanthum, commonly known as the showy clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. It is native to parts of Europe and Asia and thrives in temperate climates.

Trifolium physanthum is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils.
