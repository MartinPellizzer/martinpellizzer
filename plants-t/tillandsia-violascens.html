Tillandsia violascens

Tillandsia violascens, commonly known as the violet-stemmed air plant, is a species of bromeliad native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on other plants without being parasitic, typically attaching itself to trees or rocks. The plant is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help it absorb moisture and nutrients from the air. Its flowers are usually violet or purple in color, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in low-light conditions, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
