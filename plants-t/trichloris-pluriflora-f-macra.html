Trichloris pluriflora f. macra

Trichloris pluriflora f. macra, commonly known as the giant finger grass, is a variant of the species native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its tall, robust stems and large, clustered inflorescences. The plant produces numerous small, spike-like flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and savanna habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically significant species within its native range.
