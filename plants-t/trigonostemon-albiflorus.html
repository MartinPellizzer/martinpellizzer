Trigonostemon albiflorus

Trigonostemon albiflorus, commonly known as the white-flowered trigonostemon, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are opposite and have a distinctive heart-shaped appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming during the rainy season. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
