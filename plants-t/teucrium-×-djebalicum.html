Teucrium × djebalicum

Teucrium × djebalicum, commonly known as the hybrid germander, is a perennial shrub native to regions of North Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and medicinal plants. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, clustered flowers in shades of blue or purple. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
