Troximon glaucum var. parviflorum

Troximon glaucum var. parviflorum, commonly known as the narrow-flowered glaucous troximon, is a flowering plant characterized by its small, pale blue to white flowers.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is native to specific regions in the Mediterranean and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its natural habitat.
