Tragopogon macrocephalus

Tragopogon macrocephalus, commonly known as the giant oxeye, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a robust taproot and hairy stems, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides.

Tragopogon macrocephalus is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
