Thespesia acutiloba

Thespesia acutiloba, commonly known as the Tahitian hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, with smooth, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or red, with five petals and a prominent yellow stamen. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Thespesia acutiloba is adapted to coastal and tropical environments, often growing in sandy soils.
