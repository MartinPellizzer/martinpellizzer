Taraxacum laetecolorans

Taraxacum laetecolorans, commonly known as the golden leaved dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to southern Europe and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and distinctive, hairy leaves. The plant produces a hollow, spiky stem that supports its flowering heads. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and botanical gardens.
