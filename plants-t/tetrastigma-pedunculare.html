Tetrastigma pedunculare

Tetrastigma pedunculare, commonly known as the clustered grapevine, is a woody vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruits are berries that mature to a reddish color, often used in traditional medicine.

Tetrastigma pedunculare is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
