Tolpis azorica var. petiolaris

Tolpis azorica var. petiolaris, commonly known as the Azorean petioled tolpis, is a flowering plant native to the Azores.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like composite flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in coastal and mountainous regions. Its leaves are narrow and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are erect and branching.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting various pollinators and contributing to local biodiversity.
