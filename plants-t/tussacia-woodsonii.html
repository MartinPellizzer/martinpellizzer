Tussacia woodsonii

Tussacia woodsonii, commonly known as the woodson's bulrush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit with slender, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in wetland environments, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, supporting various wildlife species.
