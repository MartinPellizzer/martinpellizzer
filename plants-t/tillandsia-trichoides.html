Tillandsia trichoides

Tillandsia trichoides, commonly known as the hairy tillandsia, is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is native to Central and South America.

This epiphytic plant is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are covered in dense, hairy trichomes, which help in water absorption. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and does not require soil for growth. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically white or pale green.

Tillandsia trichoides is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
