Trichocereus bridgesii var. brevispinus

Trichocereus bridgesii var. brevispinus, commonly known as the "Cristalino" cactus, is a columnar cactus native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its spiny, ribbed stems and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure to grow optimally.

Due to its slow growth rate, it is a popular choice for collectors and succulent enthusiasts.
