Tephrosia punctata

Tephrosia punctata, commonly known as the spotted tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, trifoliate leaves with distinctive white or pale spots. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is fibrous, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Tephrosia punctata is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
