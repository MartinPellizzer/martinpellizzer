Tulearia splendida

Tulearia splendida, commonly known as the golden tule, is a striking plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It features vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has a succulent stem that stores water, allowing it to thrive in drought conditions. Its leaves are narrow and waxy, reducing water loss through transpiration.

Tulearia splendida is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and resilience.
