Tragopogon clavulatus

Tragopogon clavulatus, commonly known as the clustered goat's beard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grasslands and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers consist of yellow disk florets surrounded by white ray florets.

Tragopogon clavulatus is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a resilient and adaptable species.
