Typhalea microphylla

Typhalea microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaf marsh grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, narrow leaves that are typically less than 1 centimeter in length. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in saturated soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Due to its ecological importance in wetland ecosystems, it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
