Tetranthera salicifolia

Tetranthera salicifolia, commonly known as the willow-leaved tetranthera, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, herbaceous stems and alternate, lanceolate leaves that resemble those of willow trees. The plant produces small, clustered flowers with five stamens and a single pistil, typical of the Polygonaceae family. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Tetranthera salicifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
