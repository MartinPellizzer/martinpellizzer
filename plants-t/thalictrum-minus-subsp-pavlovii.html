Thalictrum minus subsp. pavlovii

Thalictrum minus subsp.

pavlovii, commonly known as the pale meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Central Asia. It features delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in botanical gardens and ecological restoration projects.
