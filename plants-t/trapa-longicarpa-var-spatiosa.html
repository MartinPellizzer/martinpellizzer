Trapa longicarpa var. spatiosa

Trapa longicarpa var. spatiosa, commonly known as the broad-leaved water chestnut, is a aquatic plant native to parts of Asia.

It features floating leaves with a distinctive broad, spatulate shape and a long, slender stem that supports its submerged fruits. The plant produces small, spiny fruits that are adapted for dispersal by water. Its flowers are typically pink to reddish in color and bloom in early summer.

This variant is notable for its unique morphology and ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
