Tricalysia capensis

Tricalysia capensis, commonly known as the Cape hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height. The plant features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red, with five lobes. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, often with a hairy texture.

Tricalysia capensis thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in coastal areas.
