Toliara arenacea

Toliara arenacea, commonly known as the sand lily, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its delicate, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that help it thrive in sandy soils. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Toliara arenacea is often found in coastal dunes and is valued for its ornamental beauty.
