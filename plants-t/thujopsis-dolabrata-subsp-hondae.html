Thujopsis dolabrata subsp. hondae

Thujopsis dolabrata subsp.

hondae, commonly known as Honduran white cedar, is a member of the Cupressaceae family and is native to Central America. It is a slow-growing evergreen conifer with a tall, narrow trunk and dense, feathery foliage.

The plant has scale-like leaves that are closely arranged along the branches, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, typical of coniferous species.

This subspecies is valued for its durable wood and is often found in moist, well-drained soils in mountainous regions.
