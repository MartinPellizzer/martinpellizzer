Trillium parviflorum

Trillium parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered trillium, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the family Melanthiaceae and is characterized by its three elliptical leaves and three white petals. The plant typically blooms in early spring, producing a single, delicate flower that is often surrounded by three sepals. Trillium parviflorum is known for its slow growth and long lifespan, often living for several decades.

It thrives in shaded, moist woodlands and is considered an indicator species of undisturbed forest ecosystems.
