Tolpis macrodonta

Tolpis macrodonta, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and has a rosette of basal leaves. Its flowering period occurs during the warmer months, and it is often used in ornamental gardening due to its vibrant appearance.

Tolpis macrodonta is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
