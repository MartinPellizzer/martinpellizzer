Trichocereus × mendocinus

Trichocereus × mendocinus, commonly known as the Mendoza cactus, is a hybrid species native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that can reach several meters in height. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, often blooming at night. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its striking appearance and unique growth habits, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts and botanical garden collections.
