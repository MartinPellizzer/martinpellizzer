Thrinax rivularis var. savannarum

Thrinax rivularis var. savannarum, commonly known as the Florida bottle palm, is a unique species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bottle-shaped trunk and dense, feather-like leaves that form a crown. The plant thrives in wetland environments, often found in swampy areas and along the edges of freshwater bodies. Its slow growth rate and ability to tolerate waterlogged soil make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
