Trichophorum evadens

Trichophorum evadens, commonly known as the escape sedge, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered flowers. This species is typically found in moist or wet environments such as marshes and wet meadows. Trichophorum evadens has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread vegetatively.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
