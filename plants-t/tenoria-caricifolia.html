Tenoria caricifolia

Tenoria caricifolia, commonly known as the water fern, is a species of aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that float on the water's surface. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and marshes. Its leaves are typically divided into small, narrow segments, giving it a lacy appearance.

Tenoria caricifolia plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen to various aquatic organisms.
