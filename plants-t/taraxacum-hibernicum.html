Taraxacum hibernicum

Taraxacum hibernicum, commonly known as the Irish dandelion, is a perennial flowering plant native to western Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs. It produces a yellow flower head that develops into a fluffy white pappus for wind dispersal of its seeds.

Taraxacum hibernicum is often found in grassy areas and is known for its resilience in various soil conditions.
