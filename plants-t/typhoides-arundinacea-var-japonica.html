Typhoides arundinacea var. japonica

Typhoides arundinacea var. japonica, commonly known as Japanese bamboo or Japanese reed, is a variant of the reed plant native to East Asia.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in wet or marshy environments, often forming dense stands. The plant has long, slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional crafts and construction. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
