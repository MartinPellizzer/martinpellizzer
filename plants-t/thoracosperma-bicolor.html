Thoracosperma bicolor

Thoracosperma bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored tamarind, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive two-colored leaves, with the upper surface being dark green and the underside lighter in hue. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by elongated pods containing edible seeds. Its botanical features make it valuable for both ornamental and ecological purposes.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and climatic conditions.
