Tropaeolum pubescens var. coma-beatae

Tropaeolum pubescens var. coma-beatae, commonly known as the golden crown or comet flower, is a strikingly ornamental plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, velvety flowers that resemble small comets, giving it its common name. The plant features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its tactile appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden borders and containers.

Its short growing season makes it a prized addition to summer gardens.
