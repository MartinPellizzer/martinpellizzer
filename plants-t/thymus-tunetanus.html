Thymus tunetanus

Thymus tunetanus, commonly known as Tunisian thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and small, aromatic leaves. The plant produces pink to purple flowers in the spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

The essential oils derived from its leaves are valued for their medicinal and culinary uses.
