Teucrium polium subsp. gnaphalodes

Teucrium polium subsp.

gnaphalodes, commonly known as the woolly germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green leaves that are densely covered with fine, woolly hairs.

The plant produces small, blue to violet flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its medicinal properties have been recognized in traditional herbal medicine for various health applications.
