Thymus odoratissimus

Thymus odoratissimus, commonly known as sweet thyme, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, narrow leaves and small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to lavender. The plant is characterized by its dense, creeping growth habit and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its fragrant herbs and ornamental value.

Thymus odoratissimus is widely cultivated for its culinary and medicinal uses, as well as for ornamental purposes in gardens.
