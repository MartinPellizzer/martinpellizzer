Typha ambigua

Typha ambigua, commonly known as the broadleaf cattail, is a wetland plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical flower heads that produce fluffy seeds dispersed by the wind. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in shallow water or moist soil. It plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Typha ambigua is also used in traditional medicine and has applications in bioengineering for phytoremediation.
