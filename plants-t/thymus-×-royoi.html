Thymus × royoi

Thymus × royoi, commonly known as the hybrid thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features dense, mat-forming foliage with small, aromatic leaves that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant produces pink to purple flowers in early summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value contribute to its widespread cultivation in horticulture.
