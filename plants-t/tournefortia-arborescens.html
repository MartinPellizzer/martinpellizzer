Tournefortia arborescens

Tournefortia arborescens, commonly known as the white star flower, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its opposite, elliptical leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom during the dry season. These flowers are often fragrant and attract various pollinators such as bees and birds.

Tournefortia arborescens is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
