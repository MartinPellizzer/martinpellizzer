Tecoma pisoniana

Tecoma pisoniana, commonly known as the yellow bells, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Tecoma pisoniana is valued for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
