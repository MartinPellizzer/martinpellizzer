Themistoclesia anfracta

Themistoclesia anfracta, commonly known as the zigzag mallow, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and is characterized by its distinctive, zigzagging stems. The plant produces showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
