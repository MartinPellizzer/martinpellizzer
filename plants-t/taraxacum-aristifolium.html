Taraxacum aristifolium

Taraxacum aristifolium, commonly known as the slender dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces a single, yellow composite flower head that develops into a hollow, hairless stem with a crown of bracts. It is typically found in grassy areas and open fields, thriving in temperate climates.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
