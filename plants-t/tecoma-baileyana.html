Tecoma baileyana

Tecoma baileyana, commonly known as yellow bells, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces vibrant yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers during the spring and summer months. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade. Tecoma baileyana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It prefers well-drained soil and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
