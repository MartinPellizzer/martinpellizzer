Thalia racemosa

Thalia racemosa, commonly known as the sea grape, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the Caribbean and the Gulf of Mexico.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine. It thrives in saline environments and is often found growing along shores and in brackish water areas.

Its unique adaptations make it an important component of coastal ecosystems.
