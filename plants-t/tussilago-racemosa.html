Tussilago racemosa

Tussilago racemosa, commonly known as the blueweed or blue thistle, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features a rosette of silver-green, woolly leaves and produces clusters of small, blue to violet flowers in the summer. The plant is often found in moist meadows, stream banks, and wetlands. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and the plant is known for its medicinal properties.

It is also a host plant for several species of butterflies.
