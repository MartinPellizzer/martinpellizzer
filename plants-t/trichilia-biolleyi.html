Trichilia biolleyi

Trichilia biolleyi, commonly known as the African sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to Central and Southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae, which includes species known for their aromatic wood. The plant is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and simple, alternate leaves with a velvety texture. Its flowers are small and yellowish-green, often clustered in panicles, and it produces capsule fruits containing flat, winged seeds.

The tree is valued for its fragrant wood, which is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
