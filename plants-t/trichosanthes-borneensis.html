Trichosanthes borneensis

Trichosanthes borneensis, commonly known as the Bornean snakefruit, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cucurbitaceae, which includes cucumbers, pumpkins, and gourds. The plant produces large, orange-yellow fruits with a thick, waxy rind and a sweet, aromatic pulp. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and often have tendrils for climbing.

The fruit is edible and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
