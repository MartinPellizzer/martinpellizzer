Tibouchina moricandiana var. parvifolia

Tibouchina moricandiana var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved tibouchina, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically a glossy dark green. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, often appearing in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes due to its attractive foliage and flowering habits.
