Teucrium capitatum var. riojanus

Teucrium capitatum var. riojanus, commonly known as the Rioja germander, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with small, opposite leaves that are often grayish-green in color. The plant produces dense clusters of small, blue to violet flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental flowers make it a valuable addition to Mediterranean-style gardens.
