Tragacantha oligophylla

Tragacantha oligophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, compound leaves and yellowish flowers. The plant produces a gum, similar to that of other tragacanth species, which has been historically used in various industries. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some areas.
