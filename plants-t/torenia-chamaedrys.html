Torenia chamaedrys

Torenia chamaedrys, commonly known as the blue bell vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features trailing stems with heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are often hairy to the touch. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in clusters. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often used in hanging baskets or as a ground cover.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
