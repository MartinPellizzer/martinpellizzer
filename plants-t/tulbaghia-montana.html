Tulbaghia montana

Tulbaghia montana, commonly known as the mountain tulbaghia, is a perennial flowering plant native to South Africa.

It produces small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often grown as an ornamental in gardens for its delicate appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Tulbaghia montana is also valued for its mild, garlic-like scent, which can be used in some herbal applications.
