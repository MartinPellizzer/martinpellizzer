Taraxacum latisectum

Taraxacum latisectum, commonly known as the broad-leaf dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves and a hollow, leafy stem that supports a single, showy yellow flower head. The plant produces a capsule containing numerous seeds with tufts of white, feathery bristles that aid in wind dispersal. It is native to Europe and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

Taraxacum latisectum is a perennial herb that is adapted to temperate climates and is known for its medicinal and ornamental value.
