Talauma magna

Talauma magna, commonly known as the giant talauma, is a striking member of the Araceae family.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to several feet in diameter. The plant produces showy, spadix-like inflorescences surrounded by colorful, modified leaves called bracts. Native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its size and ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
