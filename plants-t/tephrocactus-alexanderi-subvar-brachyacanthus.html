Tephrocactus alexanderi subvar. brachyacanthus

Tephrocactus alexanderi subvar. brachyacanthus, commonly known as the short-spined hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in dense, short spines, which provide protection from herbivores. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a vibrant touch to its arid habitat. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to desert environments.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in its native range.
