Teucrium cubense subsp. laevigatum

Teucrium cubense subsp.

laevigatum, commonly known as Cuban germander, is a perennial herb native to Cuba. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, arranged in terminal clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in subtropical regions.
