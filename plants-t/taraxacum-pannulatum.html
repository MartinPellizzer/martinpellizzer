Taraxacum pannulatum

Taraxacum pannulatum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of spoon-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers in a single terminal head. The plant belongs to the dandelion family (Asteraceae) and is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and open habitats. Its flowering period typically occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides.

The species is noted for its resilience and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
