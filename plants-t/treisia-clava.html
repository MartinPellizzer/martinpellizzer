Treisia clava

Treisia clava, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous clusters of golden-yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky, resinous substance. Treisia clava thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its leaves are typically broad and glossy, adding to its visual appeal in gardens and landscapes.
