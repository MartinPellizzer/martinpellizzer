Tweedia solanoides

Tweedia solanoides, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking purple or pink flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spiky seed heads that persist into winter. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and ability to attract pollinators.
