Talisia mollis

Talisia mollis, commonly known as the silky talisia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that turn red when mature, containing several seeds. Talisia mollis is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
