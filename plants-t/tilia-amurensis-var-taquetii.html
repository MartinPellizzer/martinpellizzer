Tilia amurensis var. taquetii

Tilia amurensis var. taquetii, commonly known as the Amur lime tree, is a deciduous tree native to northeastern Asia.

It belongs to the Tiliaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant yellow flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits known as limes, which are edible but not as commonly consumed as citrus limes.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and shade-providing capabilities.
