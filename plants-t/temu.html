Temu

Temu, commonly known as the lesser periwinkle, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It features small, dark green, glossy leaves that are typically oval in shape. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, often blooming in late spring. Temu is well adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a hardy plant in challenging conditions.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for rock gardens and as a ground cover.
