Thunbergia brewerioides

Thunbergia brewerioides, commonly known as the African bluebell, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue and white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Thunbergia brewerioides is also valued for its ability to climb and trail, making it a versatile choice for vertical gardening.
