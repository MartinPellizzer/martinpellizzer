Tectaria mexicana var. pilosula

Tectaria mexicana var. pilosula, commonly known as the hairy shield fern, is a tropical fern species native to Central America.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds. The plant features a short, creeping rhizome with dense, hairy foliage that gives it its common name. Its fronds are typically bi- or tri-pinnate, with a leathery texture and a dark green color.

This varietal form is often found in humid, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices.
