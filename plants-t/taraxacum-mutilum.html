Taraxacum mutilum

Taraxacum mutilum, commonly known as the scarlet dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the sunflower family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flower heads and heart-shaped basal leaves. Native to parts of Europe and Asia, it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant produces a distinctive seed head with pappus that aids in wind dispersal.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
