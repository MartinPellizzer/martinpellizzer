Tetraria macowaniana

Tetraria macowaniana, commonly known as the Macowan's tetraria, is a rare and unique plant species belonging to the family Tetrariaceae.

It is characterized by its small, cup-like structures called peridia, which house the spore-producing organs. The plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its spores are dispersed through wind, contributing to its limited geographic distribution.

Due to its specialized habitat requirements, Tetraria macowaniana is considered a delicate and ecologically significant species.
