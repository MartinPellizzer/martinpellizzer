Trichilia emarginata

Trichilia emarginata, commonly known as African shea tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its smooth, light gray bark and alternate, simple leaves. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers followed by round, woody fruits containing seeds surrounded by a fleshy, yellow pulp. Its seeds are used to produce shea butter, which is widely utilized in cosmetics and cooking.

The plant also plays an important ecological role in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
