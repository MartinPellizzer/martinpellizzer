Trifolium stellatum subsp. incarnatum

Trifolium stellatum subsp.

incarnatum, commonly known as the pink clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically pink-tinged or reddish in color, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, white or pink flowers arranged in clusters, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassy areas, meadows, and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
