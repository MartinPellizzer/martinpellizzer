Triphora gentianoides

Triphora gentianoides, commonly known as the blue gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which are typically arranged in dense clusters. The plant belongs to the Gentianaceae family, known for its showy, often blue or purple flowers. Triphora gentianoides thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest for both horticulturists and botanists.
