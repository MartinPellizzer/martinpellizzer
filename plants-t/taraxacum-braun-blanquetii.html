Taraxacum braun-blanquetii

Taraxacum braun-blanquetii, commonly known as the Braun-Blanquet's dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its basal rosette of spiny, hairy leaves and a yellow flower head that develops into a fluffy white seed sphere. Native to southern Europe, it thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant is notable for its ability to produce a large number of lightweight seeds, which are dispersed by the wind.

Its botanical features make it a common and resilient member of its ecological community.
