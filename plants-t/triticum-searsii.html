Triticum searsii

Triticum searsii, commonly known as the wild wheat, is a grass species native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is a diploid plant with a genome size of approximately 16.3 gigabases, making it one of the largest among the wheat tribe. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous florets, each containing both male and female reproductive structures. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a prominent midrib, and it has a robust root system adapted to arid environments.

Triticum searsii is closely related to cultivated wheat species and is of interest in genetic research due to its potential for improving drought tolerance in agricultural crops.
