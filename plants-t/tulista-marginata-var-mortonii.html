Tulista marginata var. mortonii

Tulista marginata var. mortonii, commonly known as the Morton's tulip tree, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 feet. Its flowers are usually pink to purple in color and bloom in late spring to early summer.
