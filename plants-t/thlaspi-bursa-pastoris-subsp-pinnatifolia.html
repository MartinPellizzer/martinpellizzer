Thlaspi bursa-pastoris subsp. pinnatifolia

Thlaspi bursa-pastoris subsp.

pinnatifolia, commonly known as the pennant speedwell, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family. It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, opposite leaves that are often pinnatifid, giving it a feathery appearance.

The flowers are typically white or pale blue, arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches. It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in various habitats across Europe and parts of Asia.
