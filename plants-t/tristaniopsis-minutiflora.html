Tristaniopsis minutiflora

Tristaniopsis minutiflora, commonly known as the dwarf tristaniopsis, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows to a height of about 5 meters and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are elliptical in shape and have a smooth texture, contributing to its ornamental value.

Tristaniopsis minutiflora is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
