Tragacantha macrophysa

Tragacantha macrophysa, commonly known as the large-fruited tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub that produces yellow flowers and distinctive, elongated seed pods. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves with a rough, serrated texture. Its fruits are large, woody capsules that contain small, hard seeds.

The species is known for its historical use in traditional medicine and as a source of gum.
