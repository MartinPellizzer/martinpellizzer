Thrixspermum punctatum

Thrixspermum punctatum, commonly known as the spotted thrixspermum, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spiky fruits known as achenes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, though it requires full sun and minimal water.
