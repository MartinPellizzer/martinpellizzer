Tylophora hirsuta

Tylophora hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy tylophora, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink. It is often found in forested areas and is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.

Tylophora hirsuta is also noted for its ability to climb using its twining stems.
