Themeda anathera var. hirsuta

Themeda anathera var. hirsuta, commonly known as hairy porcupine grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and hairy foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and woodlands, often in well-drained soils. Its flowering stems are usually erect and bear spikelets that are arranged in a panicle-like structure.

The botanical features of this grass make it an important component of native vegetation in its native range.
