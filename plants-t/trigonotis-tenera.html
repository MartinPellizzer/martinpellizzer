Trigonotis tenera

Trigonotis tenera, commonly known as the sweet potato vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale purple in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Trigonotis tenera is also valued for its edible roots, which are used in various culinary traditions.
