Taraxacum hepaticum

Taraxacum hepaticum, commonly known as the liverwort dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves and a yellow composite flower head that develops into a spiky seed ball. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in moist, grassy areas. It has a taproot system and is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils.

The leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported detoxifying properties.
