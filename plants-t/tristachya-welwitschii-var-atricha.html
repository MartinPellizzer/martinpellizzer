Tristachya welwitschii var. atricha

Tristachya welwitschii var. atricha, commonly known as the African feather grass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms and feathery, silver-gray inflorescences that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping and garden use.

The leaves are narrow and grass-like, while the flowering structures are loosely arranged and resemble plumes.
