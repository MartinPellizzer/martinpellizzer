Taxillus delavayi var. yanjingensis

Taxillus delavayi var. yanjingensis, commonly known as the Yajing parasitic vine, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the roots of other plants to obtain water and nutrients.

It belongs to the Loranthaceae family and is native to the mountainous regions of China. The plant has a woody stem with small, scale-like leaves and produces yellowish-green flowers in clusters. It is often found in high-altitude forests and is adapted to grow in shaded, moist environments.
