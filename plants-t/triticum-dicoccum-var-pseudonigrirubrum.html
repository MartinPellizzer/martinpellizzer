Triticum dicoccum var. pseudonigrirubrum

Triticum dicoccum var. pseudonigrirubrum, commonly known as a wild rye, is a perennial grass species native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves, with spikelets that bear small, hard grains. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments such as meadows and forest edges. Its seeds are often used in traditional culinary practices and are valued for their nutritional content.

The variety pseudonigrirubrum is distinguished by its reddish-brown seed color and robust growth habit.
