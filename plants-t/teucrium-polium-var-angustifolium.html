Teucrium polium var. angustifolium

Teucrium polium var. angustifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of blue or purple, which bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
