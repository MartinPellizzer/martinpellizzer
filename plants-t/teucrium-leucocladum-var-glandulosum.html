Teucrium leucocladum var. glandulosum

Teucrium leucocladum var. glandulosum, commonly known as white germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, aromatic stems that are often hairy, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that have a grayish-green hue. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in summer. Its leaves and flowers are covered in glandular hairs, which contribute to its distinctive appearance and aromatic properties.

This species is often found in dry, rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal uses.
