Turnera paruana

Turnera paruana, commonly known as the yellow turnera, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Turneraceae and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are usually green and slightly hairy. Its flowers are clustered in terminal inflorescences and are pollinated primarily by insects.

Turnera paruana is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant color and adaptability to various soil types.
