Thalictrum viperinum

Thalictrum viperinum, commonly known as the viper's bugloss, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and flies.

Thalictrum viperinum is often used in ornamental gardening for its graceful appearance and ecological value.
