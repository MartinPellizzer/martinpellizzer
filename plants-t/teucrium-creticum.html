Teucrium creticum

Teucrium creticum, commonly known as Crete tea or Cretan germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from pale blue to violet, often blooming in summer. It is part of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs such as mint and sage.

Teucrium creticum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
