Trichinium gomphrenoides

Trichinium gomphrenoides, commonly known as the swamp bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to wetland and marsh environments.

It possesses specialized bladder-like structures that trap small aquatic organisms, which it then digests to obtain nutrients. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, scale-like leaves that help reduce water loss. It thrives in nutrient-poor waters, using its adaptive trapping mechanism to supplement its diet.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an interesting subject in the study of carnivorous plants.
