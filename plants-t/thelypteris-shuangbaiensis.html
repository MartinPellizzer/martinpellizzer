Thelypteris shuangbaiensis

Thelypteris shuangbaiensis, commonly known as the Shuangbai huperzia, is a fern species native to China.

It belongs to the family Thelypteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the undersides of the fronds.

Thelypteris shuangbaiensis is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native habitat.
