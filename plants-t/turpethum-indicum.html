Turpethum indicum

Turpethum indicum, commonly known as Indian turpentine bush, is a shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Rutaceae and is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers and aromatic leaves. The plant is often found in dry, rocky soils and can thrive in arid environments. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their antiseptic and digestive properties.

The plant's essential oils are also valued for their medicinal and aromatic applications.
