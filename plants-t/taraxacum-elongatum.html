Taraxacum elongatum

Taraxacum elongatum, commonly known as the elongated dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant features a rosette of basal, spoon-shaped leaves that are hairy and have a waxy coating. Its flowering stems are elongated and produce a single, yellow flower head that develops into a white, fluffy seed head.

Taraxacum elongatum is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats, thriving in temperate climates.
