Tylophora zeylanica

Tylophora zeylanica, commonly known as Indian nightshade, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically yellow or red when mature. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and shrublands.

Due to its ornamental value and traditional uses, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and herbal medicine practices.
