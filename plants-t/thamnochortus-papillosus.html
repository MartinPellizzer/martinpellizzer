Thamnochortus papillosus

Thamnochortus papillosus, commonly known as the silky broomstick, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a soft, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a vibrant touch to its appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique texture and flowering habit make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
