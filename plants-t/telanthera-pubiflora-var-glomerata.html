Telanthera pubiflora var. glomerata

Telanthera pubiflora var. glomerata, commonly known as the clustered telanthera, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red in color. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a soft, fuzzy appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
