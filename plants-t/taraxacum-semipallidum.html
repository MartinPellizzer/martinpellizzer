Taraxacum semipallidum

Taraxacum semipallidum, commonly known as the pallid dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It features a rosette of spoon-shaped, gray-green leaves with a waxy coating, and it produces a hollow, wind-dispersed seed head known as a pappus. The plant is characterized by its yellow composite flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by the characteristic "blow" of seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and open woodlands.

Botanically, it is a member of the Asteraceae family and is closely related to other dandelion species.
