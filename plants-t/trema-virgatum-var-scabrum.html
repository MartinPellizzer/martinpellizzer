Trema virgatum var. scabrum

Trema virgatum var. scabrum, commonly known as the roughleaf staghorn sumac, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and have a rough, textured surface. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, fleshy berries in the fall. Its bark is often deeply fissured, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade.

This variety is known for its adaptability to a range of soil types and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas.
