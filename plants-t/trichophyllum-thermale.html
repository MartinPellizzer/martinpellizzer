Trichophyllum thermale

Trichophyllum thermale, commonly known as the thermal trichophyllum, is a rare and unique plant species found in specific thermal environments.

It is characterized by its delicate, hair-like structures that cover its stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in high-temperature, mineral-rich soils, often near geothermal springs or hot springs. Its growth is closely tied to the unique microclimate provided by these thermal habitats.

Due to its specialized requirements, it is considered an indicator species for geothermal activity and ecological stability.
