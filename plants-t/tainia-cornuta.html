Tainia cornuta

Tainia cornuta, commonly known as the horned tainia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, palm-like leaves that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant produces striking yellow flowers with a distinctive horn-like structure at the base of each bloom. Tainia cornuta thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and tropical aesthetic.
