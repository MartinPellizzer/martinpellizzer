Thymopsis wrightii

Thymopsis wrightii, commonly known as Wright's thymes, is a small, mat-forming perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its tiny, tubular flowers are usually pink to purple and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Thymopsis wrightii is often found in limestone habitats and is considered a rare and endemic species in its native range.
