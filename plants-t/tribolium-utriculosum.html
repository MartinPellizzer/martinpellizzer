Tribolium utriculosum

Tribolium utriculosum, commonly known as the bladderweed, is a small, aquatic plant characterized by its inflated, bladder-like fruits.

It typically grows in shallow, still waters such as ponds and ditches. The plant has narrow, ribbon-like leaves that float on the water surface. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually green or brown in color.

Tribolium utriculosum plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small invertebrates.
