Trichinium fusiforme var. typicum

Trichinium fusiforme var. typicum, commonly known as the slender false nettle, is a herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters. The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its stems are slender and can become woody at the base, and it has a fibrous root system.

Trichinium fusiforme var. typicum is typically perennial and has a moderate growth rate.
