Trimorpha transsilvanica

Trimorpha transsilvanica, commonly known as the Transylvanian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its leaves are opposite, lanceolate, and often covered with fine hairs.
