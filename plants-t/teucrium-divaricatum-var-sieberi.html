Teucrium divaricatum var. sieberi

Teucrium divaricatum var. sieberi, commonly known as Sieber's germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to regions of Europe and Asia.

It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color, often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, blooming in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and borders.

Its medicinal properties have been historically valued for their antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects.
