Tabernaemontana brasiliensis

Tabernaemontana brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian star jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, star-shaped white flowers that bloom in clusters, often releasing a sweet scent at night. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves and is typically evergreen, thriving in warm, humid climates. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and pleasant aroma.

Tabernaemontana brasiliensis prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun exposure for optimal growth.
