Thamnocalamus cuspidatus

Thamnocalamus cuspidatus, commonly known as the sharp-tipped bamboo, is a species of grass belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries like India, China, and Thailand. This plant is characterized by its slender, erect culms and narrow, linear leaves with a pointed tip. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks.

Thamnocalamus cuspidatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic qualities in gardens.
