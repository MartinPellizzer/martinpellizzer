Tanacetum turkestanicum

Tanacetum turkestanicum, commonly known as the Turkestan tansy, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small yellow flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.

Its aromatic foliage and resilience make it a notable species within its native habitat.
