Trigonella hybrida

Trigonella hybrida, commonly known as the hybrid fenugreek, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated pods containing several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. Native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia, it thrives in temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
