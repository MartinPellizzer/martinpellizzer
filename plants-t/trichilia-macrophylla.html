Trichilia macrophylla

Trichilia macrophylla, commonly known as the African sandalwood, is a small to medium-sized tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and yellowish to pale brown flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces small, round fruits that contain hard, angular seeds. Its wood is aromatic and has been traditionally used for carving and incense.

The plant is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
