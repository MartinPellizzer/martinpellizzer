Thymus ciliatus var. zattarellus

Thymus ciliatus var. zattarellus, commonly known as the Zattarellus thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features compact, mat-forming foliage with small, aromatic leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of pink to lavender flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its essential oils contain thymol and carvacrol, giving it a strong, pungent aroma and making it valuable in herbal medicine and culinary applications.
