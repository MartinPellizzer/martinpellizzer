Trigonella marginata

Trigonella marginata, commonly known as the narrowleaf fenugreek, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, branched stems and small, trifoliate leaves with a distinctive yellowish-green hue. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by elongated, slender pods containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Trigonella marginata is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
