Toiyabea peirsonii

Toiyabea peirsonii, commonly known as the Sonoran Desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the Sonoran Desert region of North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces yellow flowers and has a rosette of basal leaves. The plant is adapted to arid environments, with a deep root system to access water. It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered threatened and is protected in some areas.
