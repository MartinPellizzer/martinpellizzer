Thelocactus ysabelae

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stem and dense clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom during the summer months. The plant has a unique ability to store water in its thick, fleshy stems, allowing it to survive in arid environments. Its flowers are typically short-lived but attract a variety of pollinators such as bees and moths.
