Tacinga saxatilis subsp. minutispina

Tacinga saxatilis subsp.

minutispina, commonly known as the narrow-spined tacinga, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in sunny, well-drained habitats.

The plant features small, narrow leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive, finely serrated leaf margins.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
