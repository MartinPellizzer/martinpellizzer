Trigonella badachschanica

Trigonella badachschanica, commonly known as the Afghan bitter vetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in its native habitat.
