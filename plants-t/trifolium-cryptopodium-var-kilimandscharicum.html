Trifolium cryptopodium var. kilimandscharicum

Trifolium cryptopodium var. kilimandscharicum, commonly known as the Kilimanjaro clover, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of East Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring, adding a delicate touch to its natural habitat. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky areas.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
