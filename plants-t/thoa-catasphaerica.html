Thoa catasphaerica

Thoa catasphaerica, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a rare and enigmatic plant species native to the high-altitude regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its translucent, waxy leaves that appear almost ethereal in appearance, and its flowers, which emit a faint bioluminescent glow at night. The plant thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found growing on rocky outcrops or in dense forest understories. Its unique adaptations to low-light conditions include specialized chloroplast structures that enhance photosynthetic efficiency.

Due to its elusive nature and limited distribution, Thoa catasphaerica remains a subject of scientific fascination and conservation concern.
