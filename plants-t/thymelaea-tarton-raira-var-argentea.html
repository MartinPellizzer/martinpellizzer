Thymelaea tarton-raira var. argentea

Thymelaea tarton-raira var. argentea, commonly known as silver thymelaea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and small, clustered white flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are typically narrow and covered in a fine, silvery-white pubescence, giving the plant its distinguishing appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

This variety is noted for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
