Terminalia badamia

Terminalia badamia, commonly known as Indian almond or betel almond, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by woody, elongated fruits containing several seeds.

Terminalia badamia is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
