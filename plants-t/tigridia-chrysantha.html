Tigridia chrysantha

Tigridia chrysantha, commonly known as the golden tigridia, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family, which includes irises and gladiolus. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically bright yellow with a red stripe, resembling a tiger's stripe, hence its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or limestone soils.

Tigridia chrysantha is cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
