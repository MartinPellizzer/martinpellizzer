Tulipa urumiensis var. tarda

Tulipa urumiensis var. tarda, commonly known as the late-flowering tulip, is a botanical variant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, cup-shaped flowers that bloom later in the season compared to other tulip species. The plant typically features sturdy, erect stems with broad, lance-shaped leaves.

Its flowers range in color from pale yellow to deep red, depending on the specific cultivar. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to a variety of soil conditions.
