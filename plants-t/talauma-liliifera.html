Talauma liliifera

Talauma liliifera, commonly known as the golden banana, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a striking yellow-green color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are often white or pale yellow, followed by elongated, finger-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Talauma liliifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.
