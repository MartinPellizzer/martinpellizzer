Tamarix tenuissima

Tamarix tenuissima, commonly known as the desert rose or saltbush, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and small, scale-like leaves that reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces pink or purple flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It has a deep root system that allows it to access groundwater, making it highly drought-tolerant.

Tamarix tenuissima is often used in xeriscaping due to its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions with minimal water.
