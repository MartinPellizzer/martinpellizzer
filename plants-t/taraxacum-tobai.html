Taraxacum tobai

Taraxacum tobai, commonly known as the Japanese dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces a milky sap that contains latex, which is used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open areas such as meadows and gardens.

Taraxacum tobai is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
