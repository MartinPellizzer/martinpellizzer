Tibouchina stipulacea

Tibouchina stipulacea, commonly known as the velvetleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety purple bracts that surround its showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a hairy texture.

Tibouchina stipulacea is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
