Turnera jobertii

Turnera jobertii, commonly known as the Mexican flame flower, is a shrub native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners in warm climates.

Turnera jobertii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like hummingbirds.
