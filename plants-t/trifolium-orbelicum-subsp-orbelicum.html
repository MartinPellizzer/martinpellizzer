Trifolium orbelicum subsp. orbelicum

Trifolium orbelicum subsp.

orbelicum, commonly known as the Orbelic clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, consisting of three oval to lanceolate leaflets, and produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color.

This subspecies thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.

The plant plays an important role in ecological systems by supporting pollinators and contributing to soil stability.
