Trifolium variegatum var. trilobatum

Trifolium variegatum var. trilobatum, commonly known as the variable clover, is a variant of the variable clover species, characterized by its unique leaf morphology.

This plant typically features trifoliate leaves with three leaflets, though the varietal form trilobatum exhibits a distinctive three-lobed structure. It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both ecological studies and horticultural cultivation.
