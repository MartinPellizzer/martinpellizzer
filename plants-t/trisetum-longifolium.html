Trisetum longifolium

Trisetum longifolium, commonly known as the long-leaf wood oat, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are often longer than wide, with a bluish-green color. Its inflorescence is a dense, spike-like cluster of small, spikelet-bearing branches.

Trisetum longifolium plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
