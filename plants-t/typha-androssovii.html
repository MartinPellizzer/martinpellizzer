Typha androssovii

Typha androssovii, commonly known as the Androsowian cattail, is a perennial wetland plant belonging to the Typhaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, sword-shaped leaves and a distinctive cylindrical flower spike that produces fluffy seeds. This species thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of lakes. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly, forming dense colonies that stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife.

Typha androssovii plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by filtering water and supporting biodiversity.
