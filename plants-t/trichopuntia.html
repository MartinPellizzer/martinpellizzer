Trichopuntia

Trichopuntia, commonly known as the spider flower, is a genus of flowering plants native to South America.

These plants are characterized by their striking, spiky flowers that resemble miniature cacti, often with bright yellow or orange hues. Trichopuntia species are typically found in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. They produce small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals.

The genus is part of the Bromeliaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
