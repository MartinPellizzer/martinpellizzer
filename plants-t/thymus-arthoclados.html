Thymus arthoclados

Thymus arthoclados, commonly known as the mountain thyme, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with small, aromatic leaves and clusters of pink or purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments. Its leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.

Thymus arthoclados is valued for its resilience to harsh climates and its role in traditional herbal medicine.
