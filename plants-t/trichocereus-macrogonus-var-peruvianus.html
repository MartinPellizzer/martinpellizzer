Trichocereus macrogonus var. peruvianus

Trichocereus macrogonus var. peruvianus, commonly known as the Peruvian torch cactus, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming at night. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its striking appearance and unique nocturnal blooming habit, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
