Trichachne hitchcockii

Trichachne hitchcockii, commonly known as Hitchcock's trichachne, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. The plant has slender, erect stems that bear small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a blue-green hue that helps it conserve water.

Trichachne hitchcockii is often found in desertscrub and chaparral habitats, where it plays a minor role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small wildlife.
