Teucrium buxifolium var. diversifolium

Teucrium buxifolium var. diversifolium, commonly known as the variegated boxleaf germander, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, opposite leaves that are typically gray-green with white or yellow variegation, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces tiny, blue to violet flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
