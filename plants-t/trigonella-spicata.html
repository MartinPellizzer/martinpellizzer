Trigonella spicata

Trigonella spicata, commonly known as spiny chickpea, is an annual legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces cylindrical pods containing several elliptical seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Trigonella spicata thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
