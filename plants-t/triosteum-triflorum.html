Triosteum triflorum

Triosteum triflorum, commonly known as the three-flowered triosteum, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features delicate, trifoliate leaves with three oval-shaped leaflets, and its flowers are small, white or pale pink, arranged in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Triosteum triflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
