Thymus gorczakovskyi

Thymus gorczakovskyi, commonly known as the Gorczakovsky thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy flowers. The plant typically features small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, and it produces tiny, oblong fruits called nutlets. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb, similar to other thyme species.

Thymus gorczakovskyi thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, arid environments.
