Tabernaemontana ebracteata

Tabernaemontana ebracteata, commonly known as the white oleander, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant has simple, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged opposite each other on the branches. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
