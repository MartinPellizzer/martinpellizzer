Tylophora hookeriana

Tylophora hookeriana, commonly known as the Hooker's tylophora, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, elliptic leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by cylindrical fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal potential, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
