Tibouchina adenophora

Tibouchina adenophora, commonly known as the velvetleaf tibouchina, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in moist forest understories.

The flowers are pollinated by birds and insects, making it an important component of local ecosystems.
