Triticum acuminatum subsp. orientale

Triticum acuminatum subsp.

orientale, commonly known as the oriental wild wheat, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, blade-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brown florets. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as rocky slopes and sandy soils.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and potential agricultural research.
