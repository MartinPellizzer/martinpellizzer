Taraxacum delectum

Taraxacum delectum, commonly known as the edible dandelion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated, hairy leaves and a hollow, leafy stem that supports a single, bright yellow flower head. The plant is native to Europe and has been introduced to various parts of the world, often growing in meadows and disturbed soils. Its flowers produce small, wind-dispersed seeds, aiding in its widespread distribution.

The entire plant is edible, with the leaves and flowers commonly used in culinary applications.
