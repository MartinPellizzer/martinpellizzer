Tabernaemontana floribunda

Tabernaemontana floribunda, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous flowers that range in color from white to deep red, often resembling flames. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are glossy and dark green, providing a lush backdrop to its vibrant blooms. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers are primarily pollinated by birds and insects, contributing to its ecological significance in tropical ecosystems.
