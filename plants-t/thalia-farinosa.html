Thalia farinosa

Thalia farinosa, commonly known as the silky sandwort, is a flowering plant native to arid and rocky environments in the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial with small, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water in its harsh habitat. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in early spring. Its succulent stems and leaves are adapted to store moisture, making it well-suited for drought conditions.

Thalia farinosa is often found in sandy soils and is considered a characteristic species of desert and semi-desert ecosystems.
