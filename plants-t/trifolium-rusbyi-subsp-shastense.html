Trifolium rusbyi subsp. shastense

Trifolium rusbyi subsp.

shastense, commonly known as Shasta mountain clover, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a rosette pattern.

The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and open woodlands.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and native plant gardening.
