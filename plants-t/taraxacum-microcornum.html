Taraxacum microcornum

Taraxacum microcornum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and a single, hollow stem that supports a flower head. The plant produces a bright yellow composite flower that develops into a fluffy white seed head, typical of the dandelion genus. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides.

Taraxacum microcornum is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
