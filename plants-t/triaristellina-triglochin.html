Triaristellina triglochin

Triaristellina triglochin, commonly known as the three-lobed spikerush, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, three-lobed flowers that are typically greenish or reddish in color. This plant is often found in wetland environments such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and arranged in two rows, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Triaristellina triglochin plays a role in stabilizing soil in aquatic habitats and providing habitat for various microorganisms.
