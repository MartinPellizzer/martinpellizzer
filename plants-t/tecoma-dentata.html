Tecoma dentata

Tecoma dentata, commonly known as the yellow bells, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Tecoma dentata thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
