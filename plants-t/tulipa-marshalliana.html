Tulipa marshalliana

Tulipa marshalliana, commonly known as the Marshall's tulip, is a species of flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 12 centimeters in diameter. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its bulbs are perennial, and it blooms in early spring, often before the leaves fully emerge.

The petals are usually a deep red or orange with a yellow base, making it a striking addition to gardens.
