Tamarix gallica var. subtilis

Tamarix gallica var. subtilis, commonly known as the pink tamarisk, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, pink to reddish flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are scale-like and often covered in a fine, white or grayish coating, which helps reduce water loss.

Tamarix gallica var. subtilis is also known for its rapid growth and adaptability, making it a popular choice for erosion control and ornamental landscaping.
