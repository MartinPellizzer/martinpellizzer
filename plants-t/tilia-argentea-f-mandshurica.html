Tilia argentea f. mandshurica

Tilia argentea f. mandshurica, commonly known as the silver-leaf linden, is a variant of the common lime tree characterized by its distinctive silver-white variegated foliage.

It is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia, particularly found in regions of China and Russia. The leaves are ovate to elliptical, with a dense, velvety texture and a striking silver-white margin. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are clustered in panicles and attract pollinators.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in gardens for its aesthetic foliage and fragrant blooms.
