Thalictrum × aurigeranum

Thalictrum × aurigeranum, commonly known as the golden meadow-rue, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like foliage and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its subtle beauty.

Thalictrum × aurigeranum is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
