Trifolium medium

Trifolium medium, commonly known as red clover, is a flowering plant in the legume family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in spherical clusters. It is native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa but has been naturalized in many parts of the world.

Trifolium medium is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in agricultural and ecological contexts.
