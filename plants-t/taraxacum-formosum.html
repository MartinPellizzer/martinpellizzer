Taraxacum formosum

Taraxacum formosum, commonly known as the Formosan dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and a hollow, leafless stem. The plant produces a single, showy flower head that develops into a distinctive seed ball with parachute-like seeds. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in open, disturbed habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
