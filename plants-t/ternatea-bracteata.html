Ternatea bracteata

Ternatea bracteata, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It is often found in wetlands and disturbed soils, thriving in humid environments.

Ternatea bracteata is also known for its ability to spread rapidly, making it a potential invasive species in certain regions.
