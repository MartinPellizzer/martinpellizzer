Tillandsia filiformis

Tillandsia filiformis, commonly known as the thread leaf air plant, is a species of bromeliad native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on the surface of other plants without deriving nutrients from them. The plant's most distinctive feature is its long, thin, thread-like leaves that can reach up to several feet in length. It thrives in humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Tillandsia filiformis is popular as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
