Tamarix gallica var. brevibracteata

Tamarix gallica var. brevibracteata, commonly known as the narrow-bracted tamarisk, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features small, scale-like leaves that are typically clustered at the base of the stems. The plant produces pink to red flowers in elongated clusters during the summer months. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for xeriscaping.

Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in sandy or saline soils.
