Tragacantha pycnostachya

Tragacantha pycnostachya, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and woody, fissured bark. The plant produces a valuable gum, tragacanth, which has been used historically in medicine and as a binding agent. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along riverbanks.

Its ecological importance includes soil stabilization and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
