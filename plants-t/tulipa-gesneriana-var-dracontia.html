Tulipa gesneriana var. dracontia

Tulipa gesneriana var. dracontia, commonly known as the Dragon's Tooth tulip, is a striking perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow petals that resemble the scales of a dragon, giving it its dramatic common name. The flowers typically bloom in late spring, showcasing vivid hues of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces a single, upright stem topped with a cluster of showy blooms, and its foliage consists of long, strap-like leaves.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
