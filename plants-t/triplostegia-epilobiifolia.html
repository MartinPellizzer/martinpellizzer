Triplostegia epilobiifolia

Triplostegia epilobiifolia, commonly known as the mountain holly or velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a rough, hairy texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the genus Triplostegia.
