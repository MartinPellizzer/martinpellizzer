Tolypanthus

Tolypanthus, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to tropical regions of Africa.

These plants are characterized by their rosettes of fleshy, velvety leaves and showy, clustered flowers that come in a variety of colors. Tolypanthus species are typically herbaceous perennials, thriving in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade. They are popular ornamental plants due to their compact growth habit and long blooming period.

The genus includes several species, such as Tolypanthus stenophyllus, which is known for its striking violet flowers.
