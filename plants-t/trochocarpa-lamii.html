Trochocarpa lamii

Trochocarpa lamii, commonly known as the mountain pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a small, evergreen shrub that typically grows at high altitudes, often in rocky or mountainous terrain. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly serrated margin.

The fruit is a small, round capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
