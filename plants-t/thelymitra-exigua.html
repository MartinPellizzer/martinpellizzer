Thelymitra exigua

Thelymitra exigua, commonly known as the narrow-leafed sun orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and heathlands. The plant features a slender, erect stem with a single, narrow, lance-shaped leaf. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green with a distinctive white or pale blue lip.

Thelymitra exigua is known for its delicate appearance and is often found in coastal and inland regions.
