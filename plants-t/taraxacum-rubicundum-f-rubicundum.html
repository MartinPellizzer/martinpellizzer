Taraxacum rubicundum f. rubicundum

Taraxacum rubicundum f. rubicundum, commonly known as the red-rayed lion's ear, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the dandelion family (Compositae).

It is characterized by its rosette of dark green, hairy leaves and a striking red-pink flower head that resembles a lion's mane. The plant produces a single, showy inflorescence that blooms in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are tubular and are visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

The species is native to Europe and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.
