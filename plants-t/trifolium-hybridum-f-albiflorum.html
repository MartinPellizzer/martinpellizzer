Trifolium hybridum f. albiflorum

Trifolium hybridum f. albiflorum, commonly known as the white-flowered hybrid clover, is a botanical variant of the common hybrid clover.

It is characterized by its distinctive white flowers, which contrast with the typical pink or purple blooms of the species. This plant is a perennial herb native to Europe and has been introduced to other regions for ornamental and forage purposes. Its trifoliate leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three heart-shaped leaflets.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.
