Trigonella tenuis

Trigonella tenuis, commonly known as narrowleaf fenugreek, is a small annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant produces lens-shaped seeds that are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for both ecological and agricultural purposes.
