Trilepis lhotzkiana

Trilepis lhotzkiana, commonly known as the Lhotskian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lanceolate and often covered in a fine pubescence, while its stems are slender and erect.

Trilepis lhotzkiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
