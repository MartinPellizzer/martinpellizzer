Taraxacum fascinans

Taraxacum fascinans, commonly known as the yellow hawk's beard, is a species of flowering plant in the daisy family, Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and hairy, spoon-shaped leaves. This perennial plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been introduced to other regions, including North America. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed areas.

The plant produces a taproot that can persist for many years, allowing it to regrow quickly after being disturbed.
