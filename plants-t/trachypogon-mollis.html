Trachypogon mollis

Trachypogon mollis, commonly known as the silky wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Trachypogon mollis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
