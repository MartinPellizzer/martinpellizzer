Thymus serpyllum f. hegianus

Thymus serpyllum f. hegianus, commonly known as the Hegian thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and aromatic, small, oval leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its use in culinary and medicinal applications.
