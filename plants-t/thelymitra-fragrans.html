Thelymitra fragrans

Thelymitra fragrans, commonly known as the fragrant leek orchid, is an endemic species of Australia belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is a terrestrial herb with a rosette of grass-like leaves and produces a single, fragrant, white to pale green flower spike. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils in open habitats such as heathlands and woodlands. It is distinguished by its aromatic scent, which is particularly noticeable when the flowers are in bloom.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance and fragrance.
