Tillandsia winkleri

Tillandsia winkleri, commonly known as the Winkler's air plant, is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is native to the cloud forests of Central America.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on other plants without deriving nutrients from them. The plant has a rosette-shaped structure with narrow, grass-like leaves that are covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help it absorb moisture and nutrients from the air. It produces striking violet or pink flowers that bloom from the center of the rosette.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
