Trifolium parisiense var. pygmaeum

Trifolium parisiense var. pygmaeum, commonly known as the Paris dwarf clover, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe.

It is characterized by its small, trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or reddish in color, blooming in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassy areas or along the edges of woodlands.

Due to its delicate appearance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens as an ornamental plant.
