Taxodium ascendens f. nutans

Taxodium ascendens f. nutans, commonly known as the drooping bald cypress, is a unique cultivar of the bald cypress tree.

It is characterized by its pendulous, drooping branches that create a distinctive, weeping appearance. The plant is native to the southeastern United States and thrives in wetland environments. Its foliage consists of scale-like leaves that turn a striking reddish-brown in the fall.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate waterlogged soils.
