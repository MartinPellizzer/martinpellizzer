Tulipa victoris

Tulipa victoris, commonly known as the Persian tulip, is a species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically blooms in early spring and is known for its strong, fragrant scent. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The bulbs are perennial, and the plant is relatively hardy, though it may require protection in colder climates.
