Tanacetum dolomiticum

Tanacetum dolomiticum, commonly known as the Dolomite tansy, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils, thriving in rocky and limestone-rich environments. Its leaves are finely divided and emit a strong, camphor-like scent, while its yellow flowers bloom in late summer.

Tanacetum dolomiticum is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
