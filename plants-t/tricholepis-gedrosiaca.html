Tricholepis gedrosiaca

Tricholepis gedrosiaca, commonly known as the Gedrosian thistle, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Pakistan and Iran.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with deep root systems and waxy leaves to reduce water loss. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by spiny, spherical fruits.

Tricholepis gedrosiaca is an important component of its ecosystem, providing food and habitat for various desert animals.
