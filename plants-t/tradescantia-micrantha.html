Tradescantia micrantha

Tradescantia micrantha, commonly known as the small-leaved spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. Its flowers are small, clustered in loose inflorescences, and range in color from white to pink, depending on the variety.

Tradescantia micrantha is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
