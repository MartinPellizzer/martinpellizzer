Townsendia montana var. caelilinensis

Townsendia montana var. caelilinensis, commonly known as the Sierra Townsend's tickseed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats, often at higher elevations. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces numerous small seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

This variety is distinguished by its specific geographic distribution and subtle morphological differences from the typical form of Townsendia montana.
