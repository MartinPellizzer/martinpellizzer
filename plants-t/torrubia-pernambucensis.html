Torrubia pernambucensis

Torrubia pernambucensis, commonly known as the Pernambuco torrubia, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in rocky or clay soils. Its flowers, which are usually pink or white, bloom in clusters and attract various pollinators.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species and is of interest for conservation and botanical research.
