Talauma elegans var. glauca

Talauma elegans var. glauca, commonly known as the blue-leaved talauma, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue-green foliage, which is due to the presence of a waxy coating on the leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
