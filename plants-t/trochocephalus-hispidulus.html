Trochocephalus hispidulus

Trochocephalus hispidulus, commonly known as the hairy-headed orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, herbaceous growth habit. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish in color, with a distinctive hairy appearance on its inflorescence. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
