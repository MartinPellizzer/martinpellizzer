Tricholaena monachne var. annua

Tricholaena monachne var. annua, commonly known as the annual bamboo or slender bamboo, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems that are often covered in fine, hair-like structures. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with pale green to yellowish flowers. Its leaves are typically narrow and elongated, arranged alternately along the stem.

This variant is notable for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed soils, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
