Tephrosia travancorica

Tephrosia travancorica, commonly known as the Travancore tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring slender branches and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom during the monsoon season. Its root system is known for containing compounds with potential medicinal properties.

Tephrosia travancorica is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.
