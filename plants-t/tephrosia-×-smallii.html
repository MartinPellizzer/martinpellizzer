Tephrosia × smallii

Tephrosia × smallii, commonly known as the hybrid tephrosia, is a perennial shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with three leaflets arranged symmetrically.

Tephrosia × smallii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
