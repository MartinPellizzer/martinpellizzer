Themeda gigantea var. vulpina

Themeda gigantea var. vulpina, commonly known as the purple three-awn, is a grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and distinctive purple-tipped inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and is adapted to dry, sandy soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous texture.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for grazing animals.
