Torrubia luteovirens

Torrubia luteovirens, commonly known as the yellow-green willow, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in catkins, typical of willow species. Its leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Torrubia luteovirens thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
