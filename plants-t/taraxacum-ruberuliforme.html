Taraxacum ruberuliforme

Taraxacum ruberuliforme, commonly known as the red-veined dandelion, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and distinctive reddish veins on its leaves. Native to Europe, it thrives in meadows and grassy areas. The plant produces a capsule that contains numerous small seeds with feathery parachutes.

It is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in some agricultural settings.
