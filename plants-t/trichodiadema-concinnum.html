Trichodiadema concinnum

Trichodiadema concinnum, commonly known as the Cape snowflake, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a compact, rosette-shaped growth pattern with thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue, adding ornamental value. It is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.

Trichodiadema concinnum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
