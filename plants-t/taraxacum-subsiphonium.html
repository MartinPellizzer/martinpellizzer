Taraxacum subsiphonium

Taraxacum subsiphonium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant features a rosette of basal, narrow, and often hairy leaves that are typically less than 5 cm in length. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in early spring, followed by the characteristic saucer-shaped seed heads that release wind-dispersed seeds.

The plant is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.
