Tsuga brunoniana var. typica

Tsuga brunoniana var. typica, commonly known as the Brunon's hemlock, is a rare and endangered coniferous species native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern China.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and dark green, needle-like foliage. The plant produces small, spherical cones that release winged seeds, facilitating wind dispersal. It thrives in cool, moist, and shaded environments, typically found in temperate forests.

Due to habitat loss and limited natural range, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
