Thymus praecox subsp. skorpilii

Thymus praecox subsp.

skorpilii, commonly known as the Skorpil thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans. It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and aromatic, narrow-linear leaves.

The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
