Teucrium flavum var. humilior

Teucrium flavum var. humilior, commonly known as the lesser yellow germander, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically features slender, branched stems covered in fine, grayish-green foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from pale yellow to reddish-brown, often appearing in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its compact size and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in horticulture for its visual appeal and drought tolerance.
