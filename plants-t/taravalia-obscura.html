Taravalia obscura

Taravalia obscura, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and elusive flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, delicate appearance, with long, slender leaves and a single, showy white or pale pink flower. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees without deriving nutrients from them. It relies on specific pollinators, such as certain species of beetles, for reproduction.

Due to its habitat specificity and threats from deforestation, Taravalia obscura is considered endangered in many regions.
