Tachigali longiflora

Tachigali longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered tachigali, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features showy, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its bark is often used in traditional medicine, and it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for wildlife.

The tree is also valued for its hard, durable wood.
