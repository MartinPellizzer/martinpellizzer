Tritonia crispa

Tritonia crispa, commonly known as the crinkled bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, crinkled leaves and bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in coastal areas and can tolerate mild drought conditions.

Tritonia crispa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
