Tristania maingayi

Tristania maingayi, commonly known as the African snowbell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its most distinctive feature is its bell-shaped, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in clusters during the cooler months.

The plant is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
