Trifolium kingii var. brandegeei

Trifolium kingii var. brandegeei, commonly known as King's clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each composed of three oval-shaped leaflets with a distinctive white or pale yellow marking. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and conservation purposes.
