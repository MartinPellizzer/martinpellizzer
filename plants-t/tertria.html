Tertria

Tertria, commonly known as the blueberry plant, is a deciduous shrub native to the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers and edible, nutrient-rich berries. The plant thrives in acidic soils and requires a cool climate with adequate sunlight. Its leaves are typically dark green and oval-shaped, and it is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.

Tertria plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and supporting biodiversity.
