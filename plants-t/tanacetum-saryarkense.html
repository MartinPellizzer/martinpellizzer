Tanacetum saryarkense

Tanacetum saryarkense, commonly known as the Saryarka tansy, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, finely divided leaves and yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It is often found in steppe and semi-desert regions, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.

Due to its unique ecological niche, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
