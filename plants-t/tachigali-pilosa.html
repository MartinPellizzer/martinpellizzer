Tachigali pilosa

Tachigali pilosa, commonly known as the hairy tachigali, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows in humid, lowland forests. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which are often covered in fine pubescence. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually yellow or greenish-yellow in color, and it produces woody pods containing seeds.

Tachigali pilosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its unique foliage.
