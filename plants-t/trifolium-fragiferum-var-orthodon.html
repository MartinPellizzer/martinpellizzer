Trifolium fragiferum var. orthodon

Trifolium fragiferum var. orthodon, commonly known as the narrow-leaf clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets with a distinctive narrow base. The plant produces small, pink to white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and use in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
