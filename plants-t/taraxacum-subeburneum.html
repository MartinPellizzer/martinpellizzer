Taraxacum subeburneum

Taraxacum subeburneum, commonly known as the flame dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright orange to red flowers, which stand out against its basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in disturbed soils and open habitats. Its flowers are typically short-lived, opening in the morning and closing by midday.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking color.
