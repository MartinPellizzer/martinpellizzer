Taraxacum varioviolaceum

Taraxacum varioviolaceum, commonly known as the violet-rayed dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and bright yellow composite flowers that open in the spring. The plant produces a hollow, wind-dispersed seed stem known as a pappus, which aids in its propagation. It is native to Europe and has been introduced to other parts of the world, often growing in disturbed soils and open habitats.

The flowers are typically tinged with purple or violet hues, giving it its distinctive appearance.
