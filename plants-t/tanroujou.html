Tanroujou

Tanroujou, commonly known as the Japanese flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to Japan and Korea.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking red flowers that bloom in late summer. The tree can grow up to 10 meters tall, with a broad, rounded crown and compound leaves that turn yellow in autumn. Its seeds are encased in long, slender pods that split open to release the seeds in the fall.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental beauty and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
