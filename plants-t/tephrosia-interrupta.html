Tephrosia interrupta

Tephrosia interrupta, commonly known as the African tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or pea family, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, and is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats.

Its seeds are enclosed in pods and can be used in traditional medicine, while its roots are sometimes utilized for soil enrichment due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.
