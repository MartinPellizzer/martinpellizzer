Tillaea rencana

Tillaea rencana, commonly known as the rock spurge, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing, succulent plant that typically forms dense mats on rocky or gravelly soils. The plant features small, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with four petals and prominent yellow stamens.

Tillaea rencana is adapted to arid conditions and is often found in coastal or mountainous areas.
