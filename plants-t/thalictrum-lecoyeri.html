Thalictrum lecoyeri

Thalictrum lecoyeri, commonly known as the Lecoyeri's meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and thrives in well-drained soil. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

Thalictrum lecoyeri is also known for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various insects.
