Taraxacum semiglobosum

Taraxacum semiglobosum, commonly known as the globe dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of elongated, hairy leaves that form a dense cluster. The plant produces a single, rounded flower head that transitions into a spherical seed sphere, known as a pappus, which aids in wind dispersal. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and gardens.

The species is notable for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its diuretic and anti-inflammatory effects.
