Triglochin atacamensis

Triglochin atacamensis, commonly known as the Atacama marsh grass, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It is characterized by its rosette growth habit and succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom during the brief rainy season. Its root system is adapted to efficiently absorb water from the shallow, saline soils of its habitat.

Triglochin atacamensis plays a role in stabilizing soils and supporting local biodiversity in one of the world's most extreme climates.
