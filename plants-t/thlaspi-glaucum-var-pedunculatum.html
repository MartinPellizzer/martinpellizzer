Thlaspi glaucum var. pedunculatum

Thlaspi glaucum var. pedunculatum, commonly known as the glaucous honesty, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, often with a distinctive greenish or bluish hue. Its leaves are usually lobed or divided, and the plant is characterized by its glaucous (waxy, bluish) coating on the stems and leaves.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed sites.
