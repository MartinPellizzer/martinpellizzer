Turnera pilosula

Turnera pilosula, commonly known as the silky turnera, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a soft, hairy texture, and it produces yellow or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem, alternate leaves, and showy inflorescences that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.
