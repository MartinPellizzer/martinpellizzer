Tillandsia crassifolia

Tillandsia crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf air plant, is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on the surface of other plants without deriving nutrients from them. The plant features dense, overlapping leaves that form a rosette, which helps to collect and retain moisture from the air. Its foliage is typically gray-green with a waxy coating, enabling it to thrive in humid environments.

Tillandsia crassifolia is popular as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
