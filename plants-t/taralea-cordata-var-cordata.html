Taralea cordata var. cordata

Taralea cordata var. cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf taralea, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and attractive addition to subtropical and tropical regions.
