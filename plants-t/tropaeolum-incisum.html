Tropaeolum incisum

Tropaeolum incisum, commonly known as the cutleaf nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, deeply lobed leaves and bright orange or yellow flowers. The plant is a trailing or climbing annual, often used in ornamental gardens for its vibrant blooms and edible leaves. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a versatile choice for gardeners.

Tropaeolum incisum is also known for its peppery flavor, which makes it a popular addition to salads and culinary dishes.
