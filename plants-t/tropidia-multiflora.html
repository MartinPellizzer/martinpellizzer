Tropidia multiflora

Tropidia multiflora, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant blossoms. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested areas, thriving in cool, moist climates. Its leaves are usually broad and grass-like, while its flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Tropidia multiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in specialized gardens.
