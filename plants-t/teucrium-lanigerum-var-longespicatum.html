Teucrium lanigerum var. longespicatum

Teucrium lanigerum var. longespicatum, commonly known as the long-spurred germander, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of blue to purple flowers, which are typically tubular in shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental flowers make it a valued addition to gardens and natural habitats.
