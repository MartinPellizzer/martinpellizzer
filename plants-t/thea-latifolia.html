Thea latifolia

Thea latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf tea plant, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant is often cultivated for its leaves, which are used to make tea. It prefers warm, humid climates and well-drained soil.

Thea latifolia is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor gardens.
