Thymus serpyllum var. ligusticus

Thymus serpyllum var. ligusticus, commonly known as wild thyme or caraway thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features small, fragrant leaves that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant produces tiny, pink or white flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its aromatic essential oils, rich in thymol and carvacrol, contribute to its medicinal properties.

This variety is well adapted to rocky and dry soils, making it a hardy and resilient species.
