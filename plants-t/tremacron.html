Tremacron

Tremacron, commonly known as the silverleaf plant, is a species native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its narrow, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, followed by small, round fruits. Tremacron is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments due to its deep root system and drought-resistant properties.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
