Trisetum deyeuxioides var. pubescens

Trisetum deyeuxioides var. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent wood sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences that bear small, wind-pollinated flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in forested or wetland habitats. It has a fibrous root system and is often found in shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and potential horticultural use.
