Tacca rumphii

Tacca rumphii, commonly known as the "bat plant" or "devil's ivy," is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, bat-like flowers that are often violet or purple in color. The plant has broad, strap-like leaves that are dark green and glossy. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires partial shade for optimal growth.

Tacca rumphii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
