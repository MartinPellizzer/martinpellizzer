Tamarix lagunae

Tamarix lagunae, commonly known as the saltbush, is a shrub native to saline and arid environments.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves that reduce water loss in harsh conditions. The plant produces pink or red flowers in clusters, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. It has a deep root system that allows it to access groundwater in dry areas.

Tamarix lagunae is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and tolerate high salinity.
