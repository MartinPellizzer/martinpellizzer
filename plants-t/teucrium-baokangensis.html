Teucrium baokangensis

Teucrium baokangensis, commonly known as the Baokang germander, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its small, tubular flowers are usually purple or blue, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Teucrium baokangensis is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional Chinese medicine.
