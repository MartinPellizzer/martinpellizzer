Themistoclesia pittieri

Themistoclesia pittieri, commonly known as the Pittier's cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America, particularly in Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in spines. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and can withstand extreme drought conditions. Its flowers, which are usually yellow or orange, bloom during the warmer months.

Due to its unique adaptations, it is considered a valuable species for ecological studies in desert environments.
