Tinus primulifolia

Tinus primulifolia, commonly known as the yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged in opposite pairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Tinus primulifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
