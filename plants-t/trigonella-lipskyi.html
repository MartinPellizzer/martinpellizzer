Trigonella lipskyi

Trigonella lipskyi, commonly known as the Liskyi sainfoin, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged in a triangular pattern. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically clustered in racemes. It is native to regions of Central Asia and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Trigonella lipskyi is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which makes it useful in ecological restoration projects.
