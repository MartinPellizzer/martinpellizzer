Trigonella ruthenica

Trigonella ruthenica, commonly known as Ruthenian bitter vetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia and parts of Europe.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Trigonella ruthenica is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species found in various dry habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important plant for ecological studies and potential agricultural applications.
