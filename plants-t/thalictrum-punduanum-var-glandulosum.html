Thalictrum punduanum var. glandulosum

Thalictrum punduanum var. glandulosum, commonly known as the glandular meadow rue, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, branched stems that are often covered in fine, glandular hairs. Its leaves are compound, with feathery divisions, and it produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring.

The botanical characteristics of this variety make it an interesting subject for study in botany and ecological research.
