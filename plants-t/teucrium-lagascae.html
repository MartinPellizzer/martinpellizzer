Teucrium lagascae

Teucrium lagascae, commonly known as the Lagasca germander, is a flowering plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and aromatic, with a slightly minty scent.

Teucrium lagascae is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
