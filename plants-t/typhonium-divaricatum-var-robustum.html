Typhonium divaricatum var. robustum

Typhonium divaricatum var. robustum, commonly known as the giant arrowroot, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length.

It is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil. The plant produces striking purple or red spadices surrounded by white to green spathe-like leaves, making it a popular ornamental species. It is often cultivated for its decorative foliage rather than its edible tubers, which are less prominent in this variety.

The plant prefers partial shade to full sun and requires consistent moisture for optimal growth.
