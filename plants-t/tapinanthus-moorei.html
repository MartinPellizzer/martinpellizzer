Tapinanthus moorei

It is a hemiparasitic plant, meaning it can photosynthesize but also derives nutrients from its host through specialized root structures called haustoria. It typically grows on woody plants such as Acacia and Eucalyptus, and is found in arid and semi-arid regions.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can significantly weaken its host, sometimes leading to its death.
