Taxillus tienyensis

Taxillus tienyensis, commonly known as the Chinese mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees, primarily in the family Rosaceae.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing some of its own through photosynthesis using limited leaf surface. The plant has small, scale-like leaves and produces small, white or greenish flowers followed by berries. It is native to China and is often found in forested areas where its host trees thrive.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can weaken its host but also plays a role in ecosystem dynamics by providing food for certain birds and insects.
