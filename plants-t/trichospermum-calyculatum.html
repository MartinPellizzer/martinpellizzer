Trichospermum calyculatum

Trichospermum calyculatum, commonly known as the African starfruit or yellow star gooseberry, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly pubescent surface. The plant produces small, yellow, star-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a round, yellow berry that turns red when ripe, containing several seeds.

Trichospermum calyculatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine.
