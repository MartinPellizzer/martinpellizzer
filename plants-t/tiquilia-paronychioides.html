Tiquilia paronychioides

Tiquilia paronychioides, commonly known as the bearded lily, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its common name.
