Tripteris integrifolia

Tripteris integrifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf tripteris, is a species of flowering plant in the family Tripteridaceae.

It is native to parts of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often broad and have a distinctive, serrated edge. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are typically green or yellowish, and it produces small, dry fruits that contain seeds.

Tripteris integrifolia is often found in shaded, moist environments such as forests and understoreys.
