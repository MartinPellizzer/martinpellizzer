Tamonea curassavica var. australis

Tamonea curassavica var. australis, commonly known as the Australian bluebell, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, bell-shaped blooms.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
