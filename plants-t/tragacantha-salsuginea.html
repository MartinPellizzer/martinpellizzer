Tragacantha salsuginea

Tragacantha salsuginea, commonly known as the desert flax, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and succulent leaves adapted to conserve water. The plant produces a unique seed capsule that splits open to release its seeds, aiding in its dispersal. It is often found in rocky soils and is resilient to drought conditions.

Tragacantha salsuginea is also noted for its ability to thrive in saline environments, making it a valuable species for ecological restoration projects.
