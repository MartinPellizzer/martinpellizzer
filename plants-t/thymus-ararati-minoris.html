Thymus ararati-minoris

Thymus ararati-minoris, commonly known as the lesser ararati thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in early summer. Its foliage is fragrant and often used in traditional herbal remedies.

Thymus ararati-minoris thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.
