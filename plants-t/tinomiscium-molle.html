Tinomiscium molle

Tinomiscium molle, commonly known as the soft tinomiscium, is a member of the Malvaceae family.

It is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
