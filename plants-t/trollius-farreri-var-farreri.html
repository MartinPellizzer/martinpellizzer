Trollius farreri var. farreri

Trollius farreri var. farreri, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are deeply lobed and form a basal rosette, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens with high-altitude conditions.
