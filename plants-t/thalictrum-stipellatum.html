Thalictrum stipellatum

Thalictrum stipellatum, commonly known as the slender meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Asia and North America.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves that are often tinged with white or silver, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, usually greenish or yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Thalictrum stipellatum is valued in gardens for its ornamental foliage and subtle floral display.
