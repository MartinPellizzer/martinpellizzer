Thyrsacanthus barlerioides

Thyrsacanthus barlerioides, commonly known as the barlerioides cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its spineless, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its succulent structure.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its ornamental value.
