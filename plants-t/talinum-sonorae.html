Talinum sonorae

Talinum sonorae, commonly known as Sonoran morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Sonoran Desert region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking violet to purple flowers, which bloom in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves and is adapted to arid environments with deep root systems. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy species for desert gardens.

Talinum sonorae plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for small desert creatures.
