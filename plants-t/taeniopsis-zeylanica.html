Taeniopsis zeylanica

Taeniopsis zeylanica, commonly known as the Ceylon spurge, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal potential, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and therapeutic purposes.
