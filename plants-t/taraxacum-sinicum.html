Taraxacum sinicum

Taraxacum sinicum, commonly known as Chinese dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated leaves and a hollow, hairless stem. The plant produces a single flower head that develops into a distinctive white, spherical seed head known as a pappus. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

Taraxacum sinicum is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional Chinese medicine.
