Tussilago albicans

Tussilago albicans, commonly known as white cudweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Tussilago albicans is often found in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides and meadows.
