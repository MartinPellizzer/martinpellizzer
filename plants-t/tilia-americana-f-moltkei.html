Tilia americana f. moltkei

Tilia americana f. moltkei, commonly known as the Moltke's linden, is a horticultural form of the American linden tree.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, featuring heart-shaped leaves with a broad base and a serrated margin. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are clustered in drooping inflorescences. It is typically found in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained, moist soils.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its shade-providing canopy and aesthetic foliage.
