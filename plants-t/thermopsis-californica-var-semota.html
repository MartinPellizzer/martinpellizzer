Thermopsis californica var. semota

Thermopsis californica var. semota, commonly known as the California goldenroot, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, also known as the legume or pea family, and is characterized by its yellow, pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators and contributing to soil stability.
