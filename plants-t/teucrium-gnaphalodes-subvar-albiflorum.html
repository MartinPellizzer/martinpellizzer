Teucrium gnaphalodes subvar. albiflorum

Teucrium gnaphalodes subvar. albiflorum, commonly known as whiteflower germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its medicinal properties have been traditionally used for digestive and anti-inflammatory purposes.
