Tinopsis apiculata var. dasyphylla

Tinopsis apiculata var. dasyphylla, commonly known as the rough-leafed tinopsis, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and hairy, rough-textured leaves. The leaves are typically elliptical or ovate, with a prominent midrib and a serrated margin. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, berry-like fruits.

This plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth or along stream banks.
