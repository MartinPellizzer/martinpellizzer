Trisetum spicatum subsp. congdonii

Trisetum spicatum subsp.

congdonii, commonly known as Congdon's false brome, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of North America. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, blue-green leaves that are often hairy along the edges.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous narrow, pale green to brownish flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is typically found in open, rocky habitats at high elevations.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
