Thelocactus setispinus var. hamatus

Thelocactus setispinus var. hamatus, commonly known as the spiny hedgehog cactus, is a unique cactus species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny stems that form a compact, bushy structure. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking contrast to its rugged appearance. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss and adapting it to harsh desert conditions.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and resilience in low-water environments.
