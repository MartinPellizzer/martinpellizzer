Tigridia purpusii

Tigridia purpusii, commonly known as the Mexican fairy lantern, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces showy, lantern-shaped seed pods that split open to release seeds, aiding in its propagation. Tigridia purpusii is typically grown as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant blooms and ease of cultivation.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
