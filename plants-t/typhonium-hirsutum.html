Typhonium hirsutum

Typhonium hirsutum, commonly known as the hairy arrowhead, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its arrowhead-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and have a glossy surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or yellowish in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along streams.

Typhonium hirsutum is valued for its ornamental foliage and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
