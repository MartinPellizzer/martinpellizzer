Tulipa carinata rubra

Tulipa carinata rubra, commonly known as the red-crown tulip, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated floral cup and vibrant red petals that often feature a darker hue at the base. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring, adding a striking visual element to gardens. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a dense rosette near the ground.

This tulip is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its striking color and unique form.
