Tithymalus imbricatus

Tithymalus imbricatus, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers in late summer. The plant is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It has a compact growth habit and is relatively drought-tolerant.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
