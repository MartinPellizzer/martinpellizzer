Teinostachyum wightii

Teinostachyum wightii, commonly known as the Wight's bamboo, is a member of the grass family Poaceae.

It is a tall, clump-forming perennial grass that can grow up to 6 meters in height. The plant features narrow, blade-like leaves and produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers. It is native to Southeast Asia and is often found in moist, tropical environments.

Its fibrous stalks make it a valuable resource for thatching and construction in traditional communities.
