Tulbaghia

Tulbaghia, commonly known as the star of Bethlehem, is a genus of flowering plants in the amaryllis family.

It is native to southern Africa and features showy, white or pink star-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plants have strap-like, narrow leaves and are often grown as ornamental plants for their attractive appearance. Tulbaghia is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its foliage and flowers are also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
