Thymus serpyllum f. rapaicsii

Thymus serpyllum f. rapaicsii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features small, fragrant leaves that are typically narrow and pointed, with a dense mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces tiny pink or purple flowers in spring, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic foliage is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
