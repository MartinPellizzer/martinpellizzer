Teucrium montanum subsp. jailae

Teucrium montanum subsp.

jailae, commonly known as the Sierra teucrium, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western North America. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces small, tubular flowers in shades of blue to purple. Its flowering period occurs during the summer months, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
