Thymus ciliatus var. elongatus

Thymus ciliatus var. elongatus, commonly known as the long-leaved thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features aromatic, narrow leaves that are slightly hairy and have a distinct mint-like fragrance. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact size and ornamental value make it a popular choice for both garden use and medicinal purposes.
