Thrincia tuberosa var. oliverii

Thrincia tuberosa var. oliverii, commonly known as the Oliver's tuberous thrincia, is a rare and ornamental plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tuberous roots, which store nutrients and water, allowing it to survive in less favorable conditions. The plant produces clusters of small, delicate flowers in shades of white and pale pink, often blooming in late spring or early summer. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped, glossy leaves that provide a striking contrast to the flowers.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
