Tephrothamnus angustifolius

Tephrothamnus angustifolius, commonly known as narrow-leaved tephrothamnus, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky and sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid regions. The plant has slender, upright stems and small, narrow leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-yellow, and it produces small, woody fruits.

It is adapted to withstand drought and has a slow growth rate, making it a resilient plant in its native habitat.
