Tephrosia polystachya var. hirta

Tephrosia polystachya var. hirta, commonly known as hairy tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pale yellow to greenish-white in color. Its root system is known for containing alkaloids, which have been studied for potential medicinal properties.

The hairy texture of its stems and leaves is a distinguishing characteristic of this variety.
