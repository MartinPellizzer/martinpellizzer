Trifolium filiforme var. petiolatum

Trifolium filiforme var. petiolatum, commonly known as the petioled slender clover, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features small, trifoliate leaves with elongated leaflets and a slender, often hairy stem. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species for ecological studies and horticultural interest.
