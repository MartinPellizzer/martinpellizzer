Thlaspi diffusum

Thlaspi diffusum, commonly known as broadleaf cress, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its basal rosette of broadly ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically arranged in racemes. It is often found in moist, disturbed soils along stream banks and roadsides.

Thlaspi diffusum is known for its ability to thrive in wet environments and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
