Tapiphyllum velutinum

Tapiphyllum velutinum, commonly known as the velvetleaf tapiphyllum, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.

Due to its unique foliage and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
