Trachyspermum villosum

Trachyspermum villosum, commonly known as Persian cumin, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, ovate leaves covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by oval, aromatic seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

The seeds are widely used in culinary applications and traditional medicine due to their pungent aroma and medicinal properties.
