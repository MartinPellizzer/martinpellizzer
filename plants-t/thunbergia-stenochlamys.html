Thunbergia stenochlamys

Thunbergia stenochlamys, commonly known as the narrow-leaved morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white. The plant blooms during the day, hence its common name, and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Thunbergia stenochlamys is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
