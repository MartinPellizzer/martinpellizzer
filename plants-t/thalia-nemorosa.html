Thalia nemorosa

Thalia nemorosa, commonly known as the woodland thalia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of showy, tubular flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Thalia nemorosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
