Toddaliopsis ebolowensis

Toddaliopsis ebolowensis, commonly known as the Congo sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of Central Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has a rosette growth habit. Its roots are known to have medicinal properties used in traditional African medicine.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is of interest for botanical research.
