Telanthophora grandifolia var. serraquitchensis

Telanthophora grandifolia var. serraquitchensis, commonly known as the Sierra Kitchen's giant telanthophora, is a distinctive plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep red, often with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions with well-drained soil. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine layer of white or silver hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in specialized gardens.
