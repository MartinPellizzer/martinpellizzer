Tetrachyron orizabaensis

Tetrachyron orizabaensis, commonly known as the Orizaba tetrachyron, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white petals. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has small, narrow leaves and produces numerous small seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
