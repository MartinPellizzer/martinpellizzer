Tetramolopium lepidotum subsp. arbusculum

Tetramolopium lepidotum subsp.

arbusculum, commonly known as the desert ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant is typically shrub-like, with gray-green foliage and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. It is well adapted to arid environments, featuring deep root systems and succulent leaves to conserve water.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a resilient component of desert ecosystems.
