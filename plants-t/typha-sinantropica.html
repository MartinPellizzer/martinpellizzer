Typha sinantropica

Typha sinantropica, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cattail, is a herbaceous wetland plant native to parts of Asia.

It thrives in freshwater marshes, ponds, and along the edges of lakes, where it forms dense stands. The plant has cylindrical, brownish flower spikes that are often used in traditional crafts and as a source of fiber. Its leaves are long, narrow, and grass-like, while its roots help stabilize soil and improve water quality.

Typha sinantropica plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
