Thalictrum flavum var. riparium

Thalictrum flavum var. riparium, commonly known as the water avens, is a herbaceous perennial native to moist habitats in North America.

It features delicate, nodding yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant has slender, branching stems with finely divided, fern-like leaves that help it thrive in wet or shaded environments. It prefers rich, loamy soils and partial to full shade, making it a suitable choice for woodland gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive and ecologically valuable plant in native plant landscapes.
