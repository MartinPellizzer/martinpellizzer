Thelypteris leprieurii var. leprieurii

Thelypteris leprieurii var. leprieurii, commonly known as the broad-leafed shield fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, leathery, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often deeply lobed or divided. The fronds are typically evergreen and can reach lengths of up to 1 meter, making it a striking addition to shaded gardens. The plant prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, thriving in humid tropical and subtropical climates.

Its intricate leaf structure and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for horticultural use.
