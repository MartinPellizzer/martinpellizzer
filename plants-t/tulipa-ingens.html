Tulipa ingens

Tulipa ingens, commonly known as the giant tulip, is a striking bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 15 centimeters in diameter, typically displaying vivid shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and prefers full sun exposure. Its bulbs are relatively large and can store significant amounts of nutrients, allowing it to survive harsh environmental conditions.

Tulipa ingens is a perennial species that blooms in early spring, making it a notable addition to alpine gardens.
