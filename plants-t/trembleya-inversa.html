Trembleya inversa

Trembleya inversa, commonly known as the inverted trembleya, is a unique flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant has a vine-like growth habit and features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. Its flowers are typically borne in clusters and are known for their unusual upward-facing orientation.

Trembleya inversa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in tropical gardens.
