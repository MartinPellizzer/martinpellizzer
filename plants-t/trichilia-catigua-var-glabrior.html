Trichilia catigua var. glabrior

Trichilia catigua var. glabrior, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or red cedar, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and heart-shaped leaves that are alternate and glossy. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, spiny fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

The varietal form 'glabrior' is distinguished by its reduced pubescence compared to the typical variety.
