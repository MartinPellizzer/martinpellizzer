Teucrium fruticans subsp. latifolium

Teucrium fruticans subsp.

latifolium, commonly known as broadleaf germander, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is a perennial herb characterized by its aromatic, narrow to broad leaves and small, blue to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and grasslands. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is part of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and medicinal plants.
