Tragacantha ispirensis

Tragacantha ispirensis, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody, thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by pods containing seeds. It is known for its ability to thrive in dry, sandy soils and is often used in traditional medicine.

The sap of the plant is valued for its gummy resin, which has been used historically in various applications.
