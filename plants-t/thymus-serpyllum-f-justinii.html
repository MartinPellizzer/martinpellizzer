Thymus serpyllum f. justinii

Thymus serpyllum f. justinii, commonly known as the justinii thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with small, fragrant leaves that are densely packed along the stems. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental value make it both a useful culinary herb and a valued ornamental plant.
