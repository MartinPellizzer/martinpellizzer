Triticum repens f. sepium

Triticum repens f. sepium, commonly known as silver hairgrass, is a variant of the common meadowgrass.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its fine, silvery-green foliage and dense growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate regions and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of slender spikes that bear small, pale flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
