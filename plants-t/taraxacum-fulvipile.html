Taraxacum fulvipile

Taraxacum fulvipile, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy rosette structure. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are typically basal, forming a dense rosette with a slightly hairy surface.

Taraxacum fulvipile is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.
