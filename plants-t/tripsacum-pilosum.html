Tripsacum pilosum

Tripsacum pilosum, commonly known as wild maize or pilosum grass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are typically greenish or purplish in color. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Due to its historical significance and potential for agricultural use, it is of interest to botanists and plant breeders.
