Trasus paniceus var. androgynus

Trasus paniceus var. androgynus, commonly known as the Androgynous Panicled Trasus, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, with both male and female reproductive structures present on the same inflorescence, a trait known as androgyny.

This variation contributes to its ecological significance in supporting pollinators and maintaining biodiversity in its native environment.
