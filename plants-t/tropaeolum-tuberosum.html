Tropaeolum tuberosum

Tropaeolum tuberosum, commonly known as the Andean garden bean, is a perennial plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, peppery-tasting tubers that are often used in cooking. The plant produces bright red, yellow, or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, adding ornamental value to gardens. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Tropaeolum tuberosum thrives in cool climates and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for both culinary and ornamental purposes.
