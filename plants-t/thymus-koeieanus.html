Thymus koeieanus

Thymus koeieanus, commonly known as Koeie's thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink to purple in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Thymus koeieanus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has limited use in traditional medicine.
