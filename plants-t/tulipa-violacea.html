Tulipa violacea

Tulipa violacea, commonly known as the purple tulip, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Tulipa.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Turkey and the Caucasus. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers that are typically deep purple or violet in color. Its foliage consists of narrow, strap-like leaves that are evergreen in some varieties.

Tulipa violacea is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking blooms.
