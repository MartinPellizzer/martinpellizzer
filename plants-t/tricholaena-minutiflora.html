Tricholaena minutiflora

Tricholaena minutiflora, commonly known as the small-flowered pandanus, is a tropical plant belonging to the family Pandanaceae.

It is characterized by its aromatic, sword-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional cuisines and crafts. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, which are typically borne on the leaf bases. It thrives in humid, coastal environments and is often found in mangrove swamps or near the shoreline.

Due to its unique botanical features and cultural significance, it is an important species in tropical ecological systems.
