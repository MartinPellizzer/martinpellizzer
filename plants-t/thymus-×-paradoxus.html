Thymus × paradoxus

Thymus × paradoxus, commonly known as the hybrid thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its aromatic, small, and often gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pink to lavender flowers that bloom in early summer. It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils and sunny conditions.

Thymus × paradoxus is often cultivated as a ornamental plant and for its culinary and medicinal uses.
