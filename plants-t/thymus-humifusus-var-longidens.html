Thymus humifusus var. longidens

Thymus humifusus var. longidens, commonly known as narrowleaf thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically narrow and linear, with a dense mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces tiny, pale pink to white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are rich in essential oils, containing compounds like thymol and carvacrol, which contribute to its strong fragrance and medicinal properties.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and herbal cultivation.
