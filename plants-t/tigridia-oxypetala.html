Tigridia oxypetala

Tigridia oxypetala, commonly known as the flame flower or Mexican tiger flower, is a striking member of the Iridaceae family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular red or orange flowers with a distinctive black or purple stripe. The plant produces showy, spurred blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing as a perennial in USDA hardiness zones 7 through 10.

Tigridia oxypetala is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its dramatic floral display.
