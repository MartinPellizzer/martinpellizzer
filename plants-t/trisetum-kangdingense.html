Trisetum kangdingense

Trisetum kangdingense, commonly known as the Kangding sedge, is a perennial grass species native to the high-altitude regions of China.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats and is adapted to cold, mountainous environments. Its botanical features include narrow, linear leaves and small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Trisetum kangdingense is of interest to botanists due to its ecological significance and potential horticultural value.
