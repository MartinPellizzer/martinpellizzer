Trianthema crystallinum var. rubens

Trianthema crystallinum var. rubens, commonly known as the red crystalline sandwort, is a succulent plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, erect stems and small, clustered leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces bright red flowers that bloom in summer, adding a striking visual contrast to its foliage. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments through its ability to store water in its thick leaves and stems.

This species is also known for its crystalline structures on the leaves, which are composed of calcium oxalate crystals.
