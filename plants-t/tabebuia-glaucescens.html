Tabebuia glaucescens

Tabebuia glaucescens, commonly known as the blue trumpet tree, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The tree has a smooth, pale bark and produces long, slender fruits that contain seeds enclosed in a fibrous pulp. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a glossy green appearance.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
