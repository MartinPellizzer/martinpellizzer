Tetragonia fruticosa

Tetragonia fruticosa, commonly known as coastal spinach or New Zealand spinach, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of New Zealand.

It is a low-growing, succulent perennial that thrives in saline and sandy soils. The plant features fleshy, lobed leaves that are edible and often used in salads or cooked dishes. It produces small, yellow flowers that develop into triangular, edible fruits.

Tetragonia fruticosa is well adapted to coastal environments, showing resistance to salt spray and strong winds.
