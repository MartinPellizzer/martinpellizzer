Triticum vulgare var. dinura

Triticum vulgare var. dinura, commonly known as the Dinura wheat, is a variant of common wheat characterized by its distinct botanical features.

It typically exhibits a compact growth habit with shorter stems compared to standard wheat varieties. The leaves are usually narrower and more rigid, contributing to its unique morphology. Its spike is often more dense, with shorter and more numerous spikelets.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to various environmental conditions, making it a valuable crop in specific agricultural contexts.
