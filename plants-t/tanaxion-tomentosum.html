Tanaxion tomentosum

Tanaxion tomentosum, commonly known as the woolly tanax, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage that gives it a soft, woolly appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a leathery texture and a distinctive waxy coating.

Tanaxion tomentosum is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
