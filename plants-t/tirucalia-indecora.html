Tirucalia indecora

Tirucalia indecora, commonly known as the Indian coral tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange-pink flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a coral-like appearance. The plant has pinnate leaves and produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Tirucalia indecora is also used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
