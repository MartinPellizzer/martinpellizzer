Tanulepis crassifolia

Tanulepis crassifolia, commonly known as the thick-leaved tanulepis, is a shrub native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, fleshy leaves that are adapted to store water, a feature typical of xerophytic plants. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the rainy season. Its robust root system allows it to thrive in poor soil conditions and withstand periods of drought.

Tanulepis crassifolia is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
