Tetralopha motleyi

Tetralopha motleyi, commonly known as the Motley's tetraleaf, is a rare and unique plant species native to specific regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive four-leaf structure, which is a rare trait in the plant kingdom. The leaves are typically small, heart-shaped, and have a velvety texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species.
