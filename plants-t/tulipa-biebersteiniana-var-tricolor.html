Tulipa biebersteiniana var. tricolor

Tulipa biebersteiniana var. tricolor, commonly known as the tricolor tulip, is a striking perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that display a distinctive three-color pattern, typically with a white base, yellow middle, and red or purple upper part. The plant produces strap-like, grassy foliage and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. It is valued for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms and is often cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes.

This variety is relatively cold-hardy and can be a reliable addition to spring-flowering gardens.
