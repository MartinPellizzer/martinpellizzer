Tulipa thracica

Tulipa thracica, commonly known as the Thrace tulip, is a species native to Turkey and the Balkans.

It is a perennial bulbous plant that produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant typically grows in woodland and meadow habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it blooms in late spring to early summer.

The Thrace tulip is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
