Tinus maculata

Tinus maculata, commonly known as the spotted bamboo, is a unique plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems covered in distinctive spots. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that adds a striking visual contrast.

Tinus maculata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to form dense clumps over time.
