Thalictrum rotundifolium

Thalictrum rotundifolium, commonly known as round-leaved meadow-rue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It features delicate, fern-like foliage with rounded, lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, showy white or pale yellow flowers in early to mid-summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
