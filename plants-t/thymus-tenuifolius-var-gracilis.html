Thymus tenuifolius var. gracilis

Thymus tenuifolius var. gracilis, commonly known as narrow-leaved thyme, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically narrow and gray-green in color, with a dense covering of fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and fragrant foliage make it a popular choice for both ornamental and culinary uses.
