Trifolium constantinopolitanum

Trifolium constantinopolitanum, commonly known as Constantinople clover, is a species of the legume family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a rosette. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. It is native to the eastern Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ability to fix nitrogen, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
