Tillandsia rhodocincta

Tillandsia rhodocincta, commonly known as the red-banded air plant, is a species of epiphytic bromeliad native to Central America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are typically green with red or pink markings along the edges. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and does not require soil for growth, instead absorbing moisture and nutrients through its leaves. It produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom from the center of the rosette.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
