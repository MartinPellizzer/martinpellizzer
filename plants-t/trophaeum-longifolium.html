Trophaeum longifolium

Trophaeum longifolium, commonly known as the longleaf phyllanthus, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Phyllanthaceae and is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming during the summer months. Its roots are known for their medicinal properties, containing compounds used in traditional herbal remedies.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
