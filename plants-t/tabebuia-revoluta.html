Tabebuia revoluta

Tabebuia revoluta, commonly known as the flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bright orange to red flowers that bloom in the dry season. The tree has a straight trunk with smooth, gray bark and exudes a milky sap when cut. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and elliptic with a smooth margin.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
