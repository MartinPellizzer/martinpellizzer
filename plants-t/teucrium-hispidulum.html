Teucrium hispidulum

Teucrium hispidulum, commonly known as rough germander, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and along streambanks. The plant has narrow, hairy leaves and produces small, blue or purple flowers in summer. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs.

Teucrium hispidulum is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
