Thymus baicalensis

Thymus baicalensis, commonly known as the Baikal thyme, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Mongolia and Siberia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance and scent.

Thymus baicalensis is often used in traditional medicine for its purported antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.
