Thunbergia javanica var. tomentella

Thunbergia javanica var. tomentella, commonly known as the woolly morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves covered in a dense, woolly pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom in the early morning. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sun.

This variant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and vigorous growth habit.
