Thymelaea gnidium

Thymelaea gnidium, commonly known as the rock thyme or Mediterranean thyme, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its leaves are aromatic and often used in traditional herbal remedies.

Thymelaea gnidium is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
