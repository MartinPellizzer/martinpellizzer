Tournefortia usambarensis

Tournefortia usambarensis, commonly known as the Usambarensis tamarind.

It is a flowering plant species native to East Africa, particularly Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release its seeds, contributing to its propagation in the wild.
