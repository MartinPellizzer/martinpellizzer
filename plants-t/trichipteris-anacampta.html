Trichipteris anacampta

Trichipteris anacampta, commonly known as the slender fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, elongated fronds that are typically less than 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, inconspicuous spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Trichipteris anacampta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
