Themelium eximium

Themelium eximium, commonly known as the silverleaf orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a silvery-white waxy coating, which helps reflect excess sunlight. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and typically appear in clusters, displaying a range of colors from pale pink to deep violet.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, Themelium eximium is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by horticultural enthusiasts.
