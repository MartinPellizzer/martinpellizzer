Trigonostemon viridissimus var. confertifolius

Trigonostemon viridissimus var. confertifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf ironwood, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, elliptical leaves that are often arranged in whorls of three, giving it its genus name "Trigonostemon," meaning "three-angled stem.". The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits.

It is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often used in traditional medicine and ornamental horticulture.
