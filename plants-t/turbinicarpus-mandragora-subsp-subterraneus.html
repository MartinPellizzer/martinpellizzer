Turbinicarpus mandragora subsp. subterraneus

Turbinicarpus mandragora subsp.

subterraneus, commonly known as the subterranean honeyplant, is a species of cactus native to Mexico. It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in white or yellowish spines.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is a subject of interest for botanists and cactus enthusiasts.
