Thouinia acuminata var. pubicalyx

Thouinia acuminata var. pubicalyx, commonly known as the hairy-flowered thouinia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pale pink, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. Its flowering period occurs during the warmer months, making it a desirable ornamental plant in gardens.

The species is noted for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in partial to full sunlight.
