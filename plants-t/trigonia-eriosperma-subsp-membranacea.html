Trigonia eriosperma subsp. membranacea

Trigonia eriosperma subsp.

membranacea, commonly known as the Cape daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy yellow flowers and hairy foliage.

The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It is drought-tolerant and often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Its subspecies, membranacea, is distinguished by its thin, membranous leaves and compact growth habit.
