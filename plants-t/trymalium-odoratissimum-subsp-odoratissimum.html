Trymalium odoratissimum subsp. odoratissimum

Trymalium odoratissimum subsp.

odoratissimum, commonly known as the sweet myrtle, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States. It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and showy white or pink flowers.

The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are often consumed by wildlife. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of wetland ecosystems.
