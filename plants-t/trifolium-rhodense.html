Trifolium rhodense

Trifolium rhodense, commonly known as the red clover, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged on a central stem. The plant produces small, pink or purple flowers that are clustered in spherical heads, typically blooming in early spring. Trifolium rhodense is a perennial herb that thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed areas.

It is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil.
