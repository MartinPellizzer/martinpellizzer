Tephrocactus russellii

Tephrocactus russellii, commonly known as Russell's cactus, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a small, spineless cactus that typically grows to a height of about 10 inches. The plant features a thick, ribbed stem that stores water, and it produces yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

This plant is well adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
