Trifolium petitianum

Trifolium petitianum, commonly known as the Petitian Clover, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged symmetrically. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, depending on the variety. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental value and nitrogen-fixing ability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and ecological restoration projects.
