Tanacetum ptarmiciflorum

Tanacetum ptarmiciflorum, commonly known as the meadow tansy, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and yellow button-like flowers that bloom in late summer. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this plant thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. It has a strong, aromatic scent and is often used in traditional herbal medicine.

The plant is also known for its ability to attract beneficial insects like bees and butterflies.
