Thea sinensis var. macrophylla

Thea sinensis var. macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf tea plant, is a variant of the Camellia genus native to China.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 15 cm in length, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant is typically grown in subtropical climates and requires well-drained soil and partial shade for optimal growth. It is primarily cultivated for its leaves, which are used in the production of various types of tea.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
