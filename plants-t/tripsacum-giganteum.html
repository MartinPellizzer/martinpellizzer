Tripsacum giganteum

Tripsacum giganteum, commonly known as giant panicgrass, is a tall, perennial grass native to South America.

It can grow up to 5 meters in height and has long, narrow leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces dense clusters of spikelets, which are its characteristic flowering structures. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils.

Its robust growth makes it an important species in ecological and agricultural studies.
