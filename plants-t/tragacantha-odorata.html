Tragacantha odorata

Tragacantha odorata, commonly known as the Persian gum or Astragalus, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is known for producing a resinous gum, which has been used historically in medicine and as a thickening agent. Its growth habit includes both annual and perennial forms, depending on the climate and region.

The botanical features of Tragacantha odorata make it an important species in ecological and commercial contexts.
