Triticum repens f. viride

Triticum repens f. viride, commonly known as green meadow grass, is a variant of the common meadow grass, characterized by its distinctive green foliage.

This plant is a perennial grass species that typically grows in moist, temperate environments. It has slender, leafy stems and produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in spikes. The foliage is often more vibrant and less hairy compared to the typical form of Triticum repens.

It is often found in meadows, along riverbanks, and in other moist habitats.
