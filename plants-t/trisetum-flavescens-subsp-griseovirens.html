Trisetum flavescens subsp. griseovirens

Trisetum flavescens subsp.

griseovirens, commonly known as the gray-green wood grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides.

The plant features slender, erect stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that have a distinctive gray-green coloration. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle bearing small, spikelet-like flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
