Trisetaria panicea var. binervata

Trisetaria panicea var. binervata, commonly known as the silver grass, is a variant of the Panicum genus native to certain regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, silvery-gray foliage and slender, grass-like blades that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are often wind-pollinated.

This variant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
