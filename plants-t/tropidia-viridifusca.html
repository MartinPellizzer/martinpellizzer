Tropidia viridifusca

Tropidia viridifusca, commonly known as the green-veined orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The flowers are small, fragrant, and typically appear in clusters, with a greenish hue that gives the plant its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
