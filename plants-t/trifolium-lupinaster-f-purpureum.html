Trifolium lupinaster f. purpureum

Trifolium lupinaster f. purpureum, commonly known as the purple clover, is a variant of the trifoliate clover species.

It is characterized by its distinctive purple-tinged foliage and small, pea-like flowers that bloom in early spring. This plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grassy areas. The leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

It is a valuable forage plant for livestock and also supports a variety of pollinators.
