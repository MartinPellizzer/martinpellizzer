Thymus cephalotos var. villosus

Thymus cephalotos var. villosus, commonly known as the hairy thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its low-growing, spreading habit and small, fragrant leaves covered in fine, hairy pubescence. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pink or white flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Its aromatic properties make it a popular choice for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
