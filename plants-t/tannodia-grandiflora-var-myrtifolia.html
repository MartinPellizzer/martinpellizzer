Tannodia grandiflora var. myrtifolia

Tannodia grandiflora var. myrtifolia, commonly known as the myrtle-leaved tannodia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves that resemble those of myrtle species, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its astringent properties.

The species is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
