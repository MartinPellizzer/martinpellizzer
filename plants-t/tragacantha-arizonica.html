Tragacantha arizonica

Tragacantha arizonica, commonly known as Arizona thornapple, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and heart-shaped leaves, which are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, toxic seeds.

Tragacantha arizonica is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils and desert scrub habitats.
