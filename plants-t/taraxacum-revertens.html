Taraxacum revertens

Taraxacum revertens, commonly known as the bouncing star dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers that open in the morning and close in the evening. The plant produces a distinctive seed head with numerous parachute-like seeds, which are dispersed by the wind. It is typically found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils across Europe and parts of Asia.

Taraxacum revertens is known for its resilience and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
