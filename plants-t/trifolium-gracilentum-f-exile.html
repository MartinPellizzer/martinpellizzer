Trifolium gracilentum f. exile

Trifolium gracilentum f. exile, commonly known as the slender clover, is a botanical variant of the common clover species.

It is characterized by its delicate, slender stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant typically blooms with small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassy areas or along roadsides.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
