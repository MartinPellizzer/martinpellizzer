Teucrium scordium subsp. palustre

Teucrium scordium subsp.

palustre, commonly known as marsh germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in marshes, fens, and along the edges of water bodies.

The plant features slender, upright stems and small, opposite leaves that are usually grayish-green in color. Its flowers are clustered in whorls and are typically pink or purple, blooming in late spring to early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
