Thalictrum micranthum

Thalictrum micranthum, commonly known as the small-flowered meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers with prominent stamens, which bloom in early summer. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides.
