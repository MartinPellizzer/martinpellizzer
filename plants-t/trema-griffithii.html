Trema griffithii

Trema griffithii, commonly known as the Indian nettle or Himalayan nettle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Urticaceae family.

It is native to the Himalayas and is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and stinging hairs on the stems and leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, and it is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Trema griffithii is a perennial shrub that can grow up to several meters in height, and it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and animals.
