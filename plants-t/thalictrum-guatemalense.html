Thalictrum guatemalense

Thalictrum guatemalense, commonly known as the Guatemalan meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like foliage and clusters of small, white or greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a fibrous root system and produces numerous stamens in its showy inflorescences.

Thalictrum guatemalense is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
