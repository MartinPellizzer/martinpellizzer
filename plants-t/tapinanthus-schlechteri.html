Tapinanthus schlechteri

Tapinanthus schlechteri, commonly known as the parasitic orchid, is a unique flowering plant that exhibits hemiparasitic behavior, deriving some of its nutrients from host plants while also performing photosynthesis.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is native to parts of Africa, particularly in regions with tropical and subtropical climates. The plant features a short, often underground stem and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color.

Its botanical significance lies in its parasitic nature and its role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat.
