Trinia crithmifolia

Trinia crithmifolia, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its blue or purple flowers and silver-gray foliage, which help it thrive in salty, windy environments. The plant has a compact growth habit and is often used in rock gardens or coastal landscapes. Its flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Trinia crithmifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in maritime climates.
