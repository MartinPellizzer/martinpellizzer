Trixis lanceolata

Trixis lanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved trixis, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
