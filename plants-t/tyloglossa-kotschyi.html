Tyloglossa kotschyi

Tyloglossa kotschyi, commonly known as the desert bell, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and clusters of bell-shaped, pale blue to violet flowers. The plant has adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments through its deep root system and reduced leaf surface. Its flowers are typically visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological role in its native habitat.

Tyloglossa kotschyi is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
