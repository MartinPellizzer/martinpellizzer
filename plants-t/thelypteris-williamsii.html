Thelypteris williamsii

Thelypteris williamsii, commonly known as the Williams' shield fern, is a species of fern native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Thelypteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, leathery fronds that are typically divided into lance-shaped segments. The plant produces spores on the underside of its leaves, which are arranged in clusters called sori. Thelypteris williamsii prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas with rich, organic soil.

Its unique morphology and ecological niche make it an important component of its native habitats.
