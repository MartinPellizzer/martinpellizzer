Taraxacum inclinans

Taraxacum inclinans, commonly known as the leaning dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves that often lean to one side, giving it its common name. The plant produces a single, elongated flower stem that bears a composite flower head, typically yellow in color. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in open woodlands.

Taraxacum inclinans is a member of the Asteraceae family, sharing features with other dandelion species.
