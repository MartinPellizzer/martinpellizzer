Timonius brassii

Timonius brassii, commonly known as the golden beard orchid, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow and orange floral display. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

Timonius brassii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
