Talinum roseum

Talinum roseum, commonly known as the red-rooted malanga, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its striking pink to red flowers and velvety, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces edible tubers that are similar in appearance to taro but have a milder flavor. Talinum roseum thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and culinary uses.
