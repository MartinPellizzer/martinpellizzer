Tragacantha haydeniana

Tragacantha haydeniana, commonly known as the Hayden's tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils and along riverbanks.

Tragacantha haydeniana is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in ecological restoration efforts.
