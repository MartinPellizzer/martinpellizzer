Tripogon filiformis

Tripogon filiformis, commonly known as the slender spikegrass, is a perennial grass native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, cylindrical stems that are often hairless and slightly ribbed. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically green or pale blue in color. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture.

Tripogon filiformis is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
