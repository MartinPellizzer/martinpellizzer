Trifolium procumbens var. majus

Trifolium procumbens var. majus, commonly known as the greater bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and trifoliate leaves, each leaflet being ovate to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its flowers are visited by various pollinators, including bees and flies, contributing to its ecological importance.
