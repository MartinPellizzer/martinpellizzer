Tephrocactus melanacanthus

Tephrocactus melanacanthus, commonly known as the black spined cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are typically greenish-gray in color. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers during the summer months, which are relatively small but numerous. Its spines are dark in color, giving it its common name, and they serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores.

Tephrocactus melanacanthus is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability.
