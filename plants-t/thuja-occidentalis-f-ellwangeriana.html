Thuja occidentalis f. ellwangeriana

Thuja occidentalis f. ellwangeriana, commonly known as the American arborvitae or northern white cedar, is a compact, slow-growing evergreen conifer.

It is characterized by its dense, pyramidal shape and scale-like foliage that is typically a deep green. The plant is native to eastern North America and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate urban conditions.

Its cones are small and resemble those of other thuja species, contributing to its ecological importance as a habitat for various wildlife.
