Treculia madagascarica

Treculia madagascarica, commonly known as the Madagascar treculia, is a tropical tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and smooth, dark bark. The plant produces yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, fleshy fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, lowland areas and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
