Tephroseris integrifolia subsp. atropurpurea

Tephroseris integrifolia subsp.

atropurpurea, commonly known as the purpleleaf tephroseris, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with purple or reddish hues.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and valuable species in its native habitat.
