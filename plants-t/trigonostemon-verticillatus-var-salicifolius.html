Trigonostemon verticillatus var. salicifolius

Trigonostemon verticillatus var. salicifolius, commonly known as the willow-leafed trigonostemon, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its alternate, willow-like leaves that are elongated and serrated. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
