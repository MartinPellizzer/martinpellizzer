Thalictrum morii

Thalictrum morii, commonly known as the Japanese meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, showy white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.

Thalictrum morii is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance and ease of care.
