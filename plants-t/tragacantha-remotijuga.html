Tragacantha remotijuga

Tragacantha remotijuga, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the arid and semi-arid regions of southwestern Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is notable for producing a milky, latex-like sap that has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive.

Its ability to thrive in harsh environments makes it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
