Telipogon bowmanii

Telipogon bowmanii, commonly known as the Bowman's feathergrass, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and fine, needle-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in cold, arid environments and is adapted to survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological specificity, it is considered a rare and delicate species.
