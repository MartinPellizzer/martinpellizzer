Torilis anthriscus var. japonica

Torilis anthriscus var. japonica, commonly known as Japanese cow parsley, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in umbels. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are finely divided and resemble those of parsley, giving it a delicate appearance.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive, early spring blooms.
