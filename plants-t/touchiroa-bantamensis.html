Touchiroa bantamensis

Touchiroa bantamensis, commonly known as the Bantam touchiroa, is a rare and endemic plant species found in the tropical rainforests of Indonesia.

It is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink, which bloom during the wet season. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the rich, moist soil of its native habitat.

Due to habitat loss, Touchiroa bantamensis is considered critically endangered and is protected in several conservation areas.
