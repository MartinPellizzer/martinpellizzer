Tropaeolum brachyceras var. hookerianum

Tropaeolum brachyceras var. hookerianum, commonly known as the Hooker's broadleaf nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, butterfly-shaped flowers that come in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant is an annual herb that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners. Its edible leaves and seeds are often used in culinary applications, adding a peppery flavor to dishes.

Additionally, the plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
