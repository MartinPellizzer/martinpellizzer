Trifolium unifolium

Trifolium unifolium, commonly known as the singleleaf clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is characterized by its simple, trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. This plant is native to North America and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It produces small, white or pale purple flowers that bloom in clusters.

Trifolium unifolium is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.
