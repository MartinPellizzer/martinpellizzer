Tragacantha geminana

Tragacantha geminana, commonly known as the twin-flowered tragacanth, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

The plant produces a unique gum, similar to tragacanth, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a food additive. Its leaves are trifoliate, and it thrives in dry, rocky soils, often found in Mediterranean climates.
