Tittmannia stachydifolia

Tittmannia stachydifolia, commonly known as the rock spurge, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. It has a compact growth habit, with stems that often lie close to the ground.

Tittmannia stachydifolia is typically found in open, sunny areas and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
