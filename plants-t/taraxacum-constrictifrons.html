Taraxacum constrictifrons

Taraxacum constrictifrons, commonly known as the narrow-leaf dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves and a yellow composite flower head that develops into a pappus-equipped seed sphere. Native to regions of Central Asia, it thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. The plant has a taproot system and is known for its ability to produce a large number of seeds, aiding in its spread.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the genus Taraxacum.
