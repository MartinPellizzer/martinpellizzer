Taraxacum lancastriense

Taraxacum lancastriense, commonly known as the Lancashire dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of stiff, hairy leaves and a yellow flowering head that develops into a white, fluffy seed sphere. Native to the British Isles, particularly England and Wales, it thrives in meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges. The plant is notable for its medicinal properties, with historical use in herbal remedies.

It plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
