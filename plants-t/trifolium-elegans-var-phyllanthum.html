Trifolium elegans var. phyllanthum

Trifolium elegans var. phyllanthum, commonly known as the elegant clover, is a flowering plant species belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged symmetrically. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, which attract pollinators such as bees. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and meadows.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
