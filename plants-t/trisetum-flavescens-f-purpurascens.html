Trisetum flavescens f. purpurascens

Trisetum flavescens f. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple false brome, is a variant of the false brome grass.

It is a perennial grass species characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, cylindrical stems. The leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that can turn purplish under certain environmental conditions. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets, which are small, clustered flower clusters.

This plant is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks.
