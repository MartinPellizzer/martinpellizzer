Taraxacum praegracilens

Taraxacum praegracilens, commonly known as the slender dandelion, is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and a hollow, leafy stem that supports a single, yellow flower head. The plant is native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in disturbed soils and open habitats. Its flowers develop into distinctive white, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Taraxacum praegracilens is relatively uncommon compared to other dandelion species, making it a rare and interesting subject for botanical study.
