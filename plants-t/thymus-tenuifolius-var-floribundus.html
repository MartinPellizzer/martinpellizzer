Thymus tenuifolius var. floribundus

Thymus tenuifolius var. floribundus, commonly known as creeping thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features small, fragrant leaves that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant produces clusters of tiny pink or white flowers throughout the summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it ideal for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its compact growth habit and aromatic foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental and practical gardening.
