Tussilago alpestris

Tussilago alpestris, commonly known as alpine thyme, is a perennial herb native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. Its leaves are aromatic and often used in traditional herbal remedies.

Tussilago alpestris is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in harsh conditions.
