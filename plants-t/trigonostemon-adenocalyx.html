Trigonostemon adenocalyx

Trigonostemon adenocalyx, commonly known as the white-flowered ironwood, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite, and often elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its bark is thick and rugged, providing it with a degree of resistance to pests and diseases.

Trigonostemon adenocalyx is also valued for its hard, heavy wood, which is used in traditional crafts and construction.
