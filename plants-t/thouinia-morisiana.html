Thouinia morisiana

Thouinia morisiana, commonly known as the Morisiana orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant blooms. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually small, with a complex structure that includes three sepals and three petals, one of which is modified into a lip.

Thouinia morisiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or conservatories.
