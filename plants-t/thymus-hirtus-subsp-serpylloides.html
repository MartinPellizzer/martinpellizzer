Thymus hirtus subsp. serpylloides

Thymus hirtus subsp.

serpylloides, commonly known as mountain thyme, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and aromatic, gray-green foliage.

The plant produces small, pink to violet flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its medicinal properties and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and used in traditional herbal remedies.
