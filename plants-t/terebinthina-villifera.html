Terebinthina villifera

Terebinthina villifera, commonly known as the coastal terebinth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of yellowish-white flowers. The plant thrives in coastal and sandy environments, showing adaptability to salty and well-drained soils. Its fruit is a small, round capsule that contains several seeds.

Terebinthina villifera plays a role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
