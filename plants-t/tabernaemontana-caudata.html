Tabernaemontana caudata

Tabernaemontana caudata, commonly known as the hanging bell tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its pendulous, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow. The plant has opposite, simple, and usually elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
