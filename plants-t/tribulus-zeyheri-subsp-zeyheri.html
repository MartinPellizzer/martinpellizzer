Tribulus zeyheri subsp. zeyheri

Tribulus zeyheri subsp.

zeyheri, commonly known as Zeyher's cat's claw, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a perennial shrub characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered yellow flowers.

The plant produces distinctive, hooked fruits that aid in seed dispersal by attaching to animal fur. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and medicinal contexts.
