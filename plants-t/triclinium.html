Triclinium

Triclinium, commonly known as the three-cornered leek, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its triangular leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and prefers well-drained soil. It blooms from late spring to early summer, producing vibrant red or purple flowers.

Triclinium is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
