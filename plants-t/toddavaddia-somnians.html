Toddavaddia somnians

Toddavaddia somnians, commonly known as the sleepy beauty, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety purple flowers that bloom during the cooler months. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit with dark green, waxy leaves that are slightly aromatic. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and fragrance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens and conservatories.
