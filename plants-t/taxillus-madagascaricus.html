Taxillus madagascaricus

Taxillus madagascaricus, commonly known as the Madagascar mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant native to Madagascar.

It attaches itself to the stems of its host plants, primarily members of the Rubiaceae family, through its haustoria, which absorb water and nutrients. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers that are often clustered together. Its berries are typically red or orange, containing seeds that are dispersed by birds.

Due to its parasitic nature, it plays a role in shaping the ecology of its host species and the surrounding forest environment.
