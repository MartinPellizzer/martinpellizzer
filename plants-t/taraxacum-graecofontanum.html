Taraxacum graecofontanum

Taraxacum graecofontanum, commonly known as the Greek fountain dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf morphology. The plant features basal rosettes of serrated, hairy leaves that are typically bright green. Its flowering stems bear yellow ray florets surrounded by tubular disc florets, typical of the genus Taraxacum.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.
