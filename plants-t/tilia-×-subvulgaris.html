Tilia × subvulgaris

Tilia × subvulgaris, commonly known as the small-leaf linden, is a hybrid species within the Tilia genus, characterized by its smaller leaves compared to other linden species.

It is a deciduous tree that typically grows to a height of 10–20 meters, featuring a rounded crown and smooth, gray bark. The leaves are heart-shaped, serrated, and turn yellow in autumn, while the flowers are small, fragrant, and clustered in drooping inflorescences.

This species is native to Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
