Thymus cherlerioides var. isauricus

Thymus cherlerioides var. isauricus, commonly known as Isaurian thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the rocky regions of the Eastern Mediterranean.

It features small, fragrant, oval-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color, covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale pink to white flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its aromatic properties make it a valued plant in both horticulture and traditional herbal medicine.
