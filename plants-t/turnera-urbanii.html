Turnera urbanii

Turnera urbanii, commonly known as the Mexican flame flower, is a perennial shrub native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It belongs to the Passifloraceae family, which includes passionflowers, and is characterized by its showy, bright orange to red flowers. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant blooms.

Turnera urbanii is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.
