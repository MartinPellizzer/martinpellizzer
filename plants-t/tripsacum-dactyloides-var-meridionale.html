Tripsacum dactyloides var. meridionale

Tripsacum dactyloides var. meridionale, commonly known as the desert grass or Mexican grass, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tufted, clump-forming growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to yellowish flowers. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and heat resistance.

This species is often used in landscaping for erosion control and as a ornamental plant due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
