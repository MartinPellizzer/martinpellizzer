Taraxacum flevoense

Taraxacum flevoense, commonly known as the Flevo dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to the Netherlands and is characterized by its yellow ray florets and a distinctive, hollow stem. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in meadows and along waterways. It has a rosette of basal leaves that are usually hairy and lobed.

The flowers bloom in the spring and are followed by the characteristic seed heads that are commonly known as "dandelion clocks.".
