Tassadia martiana

Tassadia martiana, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or mountainous terrain. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a rough, hairy texture.

Tassadia martiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in cooler climates.
