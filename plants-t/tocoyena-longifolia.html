Tocoyena longifolia

Tocoyena longifolia, commonly known as the long-flowered tocoyena, is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family and is native to South America.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its strap-like, grass-like leaves and striking, showy inflorescences. The plant produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Tocoyena longifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in a garden setting.
