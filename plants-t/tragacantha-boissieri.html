Tragacantha boissieri

Tragacantha boissieri, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a flowering plant native to the dry regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and woody, thorny stems. The plant produces a milky sap that is used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments.

Tragacantha boissieri is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in degraded landscapes.
