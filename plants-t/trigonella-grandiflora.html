Trigonella grandiflora

Trigonella grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered fenugreek, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its seed pods contain small, brown seeds that are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

Trigonella grandiflora is valued for its nutritional content and has been used in herbal remedies for centuries.
