Teline × canariensistenopetala

Teline × canariensistenopetala, commonly known as the Canary Islands bellflower, is a hybrid plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces showy blooms during the spring and summer months.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
