Trichocline denticulata

Trichocline denticulata, commonly known as the toothed trichocline, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its finely serrated leaves and small, daisy-like flowers that typically bloom in the summer months. Native to regions of southern Africa, this plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its structure includes a basal rosette of leaves with hairy surfaces, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

The plant's botanical features make it an attractive species for xeriscaping and ecological gardens.
