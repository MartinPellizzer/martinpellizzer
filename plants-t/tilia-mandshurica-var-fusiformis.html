Tilia mandshurica var. fusiformis

Tilia mandshurica var. fusiformis, commonly known as the Manchurian linden, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green on top and pale underneath. The tree produces small, fragrant yellow flowers in early summer, which are clustered in drooping inflorescences. Its bark is smooth and light gray, becoming fissured with age.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often planted in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and shade-providing capabilities.
