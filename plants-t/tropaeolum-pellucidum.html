Tropaeolum pellucidum

Tropaeolum pellucidum, commonly known as the glassy nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent leaves and bright, showy flowers that come in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and prefers well-drained soil and full sun. Its foliage is often used in culinary applications due to its peppery taste, and it is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Tropaeolum pellucidum is an annual plant that thrives in temperate climates and is relatively easy to cultivate.
