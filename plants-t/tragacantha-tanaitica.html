Tragacantha tanaitica

Tragacantha tanaitica, commonly known as the Tanaitic tragacanth, is a species of flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces a unique gum, which is used in traditional medicine and as a natural thickening agent.

Tragacantha tanaitica thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils and along dry riverbanks.
