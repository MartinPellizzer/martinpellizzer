Tradescantia cumanensis var. glabrior

Tradescantia cumanensis var. glabrior, commonly known as the smoothleaf spiderwort, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central America.

It features striking violet to blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a smooth, waxy texture, which gives the plant its varietal name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative houseplant.
