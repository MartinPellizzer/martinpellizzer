Thunia venosa

Thunia venosa, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually a deep green color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Thunia venosa is also valued for its edible fruits, which have a sweet and tangy flavor.
