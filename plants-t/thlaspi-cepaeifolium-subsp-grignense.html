Thlaspi cepaeifolium subsp. grignense

Thlaspi cepaeifolium subsp.

grignense, commonly known as the Grignan cabbageweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural areas. Its leaves are lobed and resemble those of cabbage, giving it its common name.

It is a hardy species that can thrive in a variety of soil types and environmental conditions.
