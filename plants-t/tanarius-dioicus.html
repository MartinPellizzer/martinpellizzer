Tanarius dioicus

Tanarius dioicus, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red in color. The plant produces a unique type of fruit known as a drupe, which contains a single seed. Tanarius dioicus is valued for its hard, durable wood, which has been used historically in construction and craftsmanship.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and economic contexts.
