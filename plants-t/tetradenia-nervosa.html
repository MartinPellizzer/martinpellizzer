Tetradenia nervosa

Tetradenia nervosa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tetradenia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It is typically found in sandy soils and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Tetradenia nervosa is also valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
