Tetrastigma retinervium var. pubescens

Tetrastigma retinervium var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy grapevine, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender stems and compound leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by clusters of berries that range in color from red to black. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
