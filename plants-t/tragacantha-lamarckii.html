Tragacantha lamarckii

Tragacantha lamarckii, commonly known as the Astragalus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Central Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or violet flowers. The plant produces a distinctive gum, known as tragacanth, which is harvested from its stems and used in various industrial and medicinal applications. Tragacantha lamarckii thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in steppe and shrubland habitats.
