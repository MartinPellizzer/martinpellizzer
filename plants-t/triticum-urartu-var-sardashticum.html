Triticum urartu var. sardashticum

Triticum urartu var. sardashticum, commonly known as the Sardasht wild emmer wheat, is a wild species native to the highlands of Iran.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and hardy nature, thriving in cold, temperate climates. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous florets, typical of the wheat genus. Its genetic diversity makes it an important resource for wheat breeding programs.

This variety is also valued for its potential in studying the domestication process of modern wheat.
