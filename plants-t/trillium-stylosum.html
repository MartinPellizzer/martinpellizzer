Trillium stylosum

Trillium stylosum, commonly known as the slender trillium, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the family Melanthiaceae and is characterized by its three elliptical leaves and a single, showy white flower with three petals. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist woodland habitats, often forming dense colonies. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in rich, loamy soils.

Trillium stylosum is also notable for its medicinal properties, with some traditional uses in herbal remedies.
