Tetrastigma hookeri

Tetrastigma hookeri, commonly known as the climbing raisin vine, is a woody vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its twining stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. Its berries are edible and have a sweet, raisin-like flavor, which has led to its common name.

Tetrastigma hookeri is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and unique fruit.
