Trifolium megalanthum

Trifolium megalanthum, commonly known as the giant clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its large, trifoliate leaves composed of three broad, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, globular clusters, which bloom in the spring. Native to certain regions of South America, it thrives in moist, temperate climates.

Due to its unique appearance and growth habits, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
