Taraxacum syriacum

Taraxacum syriacum, commonly known as the Syrian dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant features a basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces a hollow, wind-dispersed seed stalk. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Taraxacum syriacum is also noted for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in herbal remedies.
