Trifolium aurantiacum

Trifolium aurantiacum, commonly known as the yellow trefoil, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets that are typically bright yellow in color. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Trifolium aurantiacum is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
