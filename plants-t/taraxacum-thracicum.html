Taraxacum thracicum

Taraxacum thracicum, commonly known as the Thrace dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to southeastern Europe and parts of western Asia, typically found in grasslands and open woodlands. The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs. It produces a single, hollow, hairless stem that supports a yellow flower head, which develops into a white, pappus-equipped seed head.

Taraxacum thracicum is known for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
