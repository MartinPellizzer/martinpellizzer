Tylophora zenkeri

Tylophora zenkeri, commonly known as the African moon vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by elongated, fleshy fruits. Its roots and leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties.

Tylophora zenkeri thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
