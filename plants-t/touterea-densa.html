Touterea densa

Touterea densa, commonly known as the dense tamarind, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Sterculiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and small, clustered white flowers. The plant produces elongated, woody pods containing edible seeds that are used in traditional cuisine. It thrives in humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its culinary uses.

The leaves are also utilized in local herbal medicine for their purported health benefits.
