Tweedia rosea

Tweedia rosea, commonly known as the pink torch lily, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, pinkish-white, torch-like flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30-60 cm and produces showy blooms from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Tweedia rosea is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ease of care.
