Trillium cernuum f. lalondei

Trillium cernuum f. lalondei, commonly known as the nodding white trillium, is a rare botanical variant of the trillium genus.

It is characterized by its distinctive downward-facing white flowers with three petals and three sepals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and forest floors. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three heart-shaped leaflets.

This fern-like plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often found in specific regions of North America.
