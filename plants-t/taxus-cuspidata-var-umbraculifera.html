Taxus cuspidata var. umbraculifera

Taxus cuspidata var. umbraculifera, commonly known as the Japanese yew or umbrella-shaped yew, is a slow-growing evergreen conifer native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and distinctive umbrella-shaped crown, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its year-round foliage and unique form.
