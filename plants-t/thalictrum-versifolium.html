Thalictrum versifolium

Thalictrum versifolium, commonly known as the variable meadow rue, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like foliage and showy, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. It produces small, aggregate fruits that contain multiple seeds.

Thalictrum versifolium is often found in the eastern United States and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
