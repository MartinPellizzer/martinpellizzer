Tanacetum argenteum subsp. argenteum

Tanacetum argenteum subsp.

argenteum, commonly known as silverleaf tansy, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features finely divided, silvery-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that are clustered in flat-topped inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its aromatic leaves and flowers have been used historically in herbal medicine and as a natural insect repellent.
