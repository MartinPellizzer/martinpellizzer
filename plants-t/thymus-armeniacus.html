Thymus armeniacus

Thymus armeniacus, commonly known as Armenian thyme, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant leaves and flowering stems that bear pink or violet flowers. The plant is valued for its aromatic properties, containing essential oils rich in thymol and carvacrol. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its medicinal and culinary uses, it is also cultivated for essential oil production.
