Tamus smilacifolia

Tamus smilacifolia, commonly known as the creeping arrowhead, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces berries that are dark blue to black when mature, which are an important food source for various bird species. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Tamus smilacifolia is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
