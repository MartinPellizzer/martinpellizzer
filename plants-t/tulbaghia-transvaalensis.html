Tulbaghia transvaalensis

Tulbaghia transvaalensis, commonly known as the African lily, is a perennial bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features strap-like, linear leaves and produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, bulbous root system and is often used in ornamental gardening. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Tulbaghia transvaalensis is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
