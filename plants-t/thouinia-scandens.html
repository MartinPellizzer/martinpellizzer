Thouinia scandens

Thouinia scandens, commonly known as the climbing thouinia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and is often found in moist, shaded environments. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to cover trellises or walls.

The species is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
