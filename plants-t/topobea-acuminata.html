Topobea acuminata

Topobea acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-pointed dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that develop into fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

Due to its hardy nature, it is considered a common weed in many regions.
