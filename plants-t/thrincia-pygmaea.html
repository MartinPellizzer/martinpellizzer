Thrincia pygmaea

Thrincia pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf thornapple, is a small flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pale green to white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Thrincia pygmaea is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and along stream banks.
