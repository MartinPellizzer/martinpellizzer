Tilia × europaea subsp. parviflora

Tilia × europaea subsp.

parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered lime, is a hybrid species within the Tilia genus. It is characterized by its small, white flowers that appear in clusters, typically in early summer.

The leaves are heart-shaped with a serrated edge and are medium to dark green in color. This species is native to parts of Europe and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in landscaping and hedging.
