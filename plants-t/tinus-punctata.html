Tinus punctata

Tinus punctata, commonly known as the spotted tinus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, spotted leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Tinus punctata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
