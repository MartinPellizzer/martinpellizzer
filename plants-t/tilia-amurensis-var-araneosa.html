Tilia amurensis var. araneosa

Tilia amurensis var. araneosa, commonly known as the Amur honeylocust, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. Its fruit is a small, flat pod that splits open to release seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and unique form.
