Teucrium scordium

Teucrium scordium, commonly known as wild thyme or mountain thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to regions of Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. The plant features small, often fragrant, purple or pink flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are aromatic, with a scent reminiscent of thyme, and are often used in traditional herbal remedies.

Teucrium scordium is valued for its medicinal properties, including potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects.
