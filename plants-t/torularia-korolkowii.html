Torularia korolkowii

Torularia korolkowii, commonly known as the yellow cup fungus, is a species of ascomycete fungus characterized by its distinctive yellow, cup-shaped fruiting body.

It typically grows in damp, shaded environments, often on decaying wood or soil. The fungus is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid conditions. Its spore-producing structures, called ascocarps, are prominently displayed, making it easily identifiable.

Torularia korolkowii plays a role in decomposing organic matter in forest ecosystems.
