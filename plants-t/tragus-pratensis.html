Tragus pratensis

Tragus pratensis, commonly known as the meadow tragus, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a rough texture and a slightly hairy surface.

Tragus pratensis is often found in temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia.
