Tilia vitifolia

Tilia vitifolia, commonly known as the grape-leaf linden, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Tiliaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that resemble those of grapevines. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which bloom in early summer. Its bark is smooth and gray, with shallow fissures, and it can grow up to 20 meters tall.

Tilia vitifolia is often found in moist, well-drained soils and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
