Trigonochilum graminifolium

Trigonochilum graminifolium, commonly known as the grass-leaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and delicate, spike-like inflorescence. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories. Its flowers are usually small and white or greenish-white, with a unique structure that facilitates pollination by specific insect species.

Trigonochilum graminifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems as a habitat for various microorganisms and pollinators.
