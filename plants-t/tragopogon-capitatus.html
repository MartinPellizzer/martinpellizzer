Tragopogon capitatus

Tragopogon capitatus, commonly known as the yellow gooselion, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant produces a bright yellow ray floret surrounded by numerous tubular disc florets. It thrives in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and disturbed soils.

Tragopogon capitatus is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize new areas, making it a common sight in many regions.
