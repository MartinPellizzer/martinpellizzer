Trattenikia lanceolata

Trattenikia lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf trattenikia, is a herbaceous plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are often white or pale pink in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
