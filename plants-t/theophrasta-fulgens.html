Theophrasta fulgens

Theophrasta fulgens, commonly known as the fiery bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, which are adapted for pollination by hummingbirds. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Theophrasta fulgens is known for its long blooming period, often from late spring through early autumn.
