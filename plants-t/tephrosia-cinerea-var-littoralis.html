Tephrosia cinerea var. littoralis

Tephrosia cinerea var. littoralis, commonly known as the coastal tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of tropical and subtropical areas.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers, which are typically borne in axillary or terminal inflorescences. It thrives in sandy or saline soils and is often found in coastal dunes or mangrove ecosystems.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing coastal environments.
