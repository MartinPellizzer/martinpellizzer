Tiarella unifoliata f. typica

Tiarella unifoliata f. typica, commonly known as the Japanese foamflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically green with a slight white or silver sheen. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple during the spring. It thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for woodland gardens and shaded landscapes.
