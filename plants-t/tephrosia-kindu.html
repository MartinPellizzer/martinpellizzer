Tephrosia kindu

Tephrosia kindu, commonly known as African blue pea, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to tropical Africa and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being elliptical and slightly hairy.

The seeds are enclosed in pods and are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
