Taxus baccata var. cavendishii

Taxus baccata var. cavendishii, commonly known as the Cavendish yew, is a variant of the English yew, characterized by its distinctive foliage.

It features dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and arranged in flat, spreading sprays. The plant produces small, red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It is a slow-growing evergreen with a conical shape, often cultivated as an ornamental tree.

The Cavendish yew is valued for its dense foliage and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
