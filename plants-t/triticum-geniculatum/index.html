Triticum Geniculatum

Triticum geniculatum is a unique wild wheat species known for its distinctive growth pattern and ecological significance. This plant thrives in specific environments, often found in rocky or disturbed soils across certain regions. Its twisted or bent stems give it a peculiar appearance, setting it apart from other wheat varieties. Despite its rarity, it plays an important role in local ecosystems and has attracted interest from botanists and agricultural researchers. Studying Triticum geniculatum offers insights into plant adaptation and potential applications in sustainable farming.
