Trematosperma cordatum

Trematosperma cordatum, commonly known as the heartleaf sedge, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or wetlands, where it thrives in humid conditions. Its leaves are often heart-shaped, giving it its common name, and it produces small, three-lobed fruits.

Trematosperma cordatum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates in its native ecosystems.
