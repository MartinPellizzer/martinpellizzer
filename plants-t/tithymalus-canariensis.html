Tithymalus canariensis

Tithymalus canariensis, commonly known as the Canary Islands thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky and coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Tithymalus canariensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in rock gardens and coastal landscaping.
