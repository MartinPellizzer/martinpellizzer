Tournefortia minuta

Tournefortia minuta, commonly known as the dwarf tournefortia, is a small shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

Tournefortia minuta is often found in moist, well-drained soils near rivers or in coastal areas.
