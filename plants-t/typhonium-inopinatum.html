Typhonium inopinatum

Typhonium inopinatum, commonly known as the ghost arum, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces a unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a pale, hood-like spathe. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in swampy or wetland areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
