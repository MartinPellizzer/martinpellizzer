Tulipa planifolia var. aximensis

Tulipa planifolia var. aximensis, commonly known as the Aximensis tulip, is a rare and unique variant of the tulip species.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, specifically found in countries like Afghanistan and Pakistan. This plant is characterized by its flat, elongated petals that give it a distinctive appearance compared to typical tulips. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
