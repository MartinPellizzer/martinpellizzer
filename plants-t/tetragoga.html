Tetragoga

Tetragoga, commonly known as the four-angled grass, is a type of flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, four-sided stems and small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and is often found in temperate regions. Tetragoga is a perennial species that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Its botanical features make it an important component of grassland ecosystems.
