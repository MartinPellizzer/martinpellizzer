Trifolium megacephalum

Trifolium megacephalum, commonly known as the giant-headed clover, is a flowering plant species native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, rounded leaves composed of three heart-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and early summer. Trifolium megacephalum is often found in grassy areas and along the edges of forests, where it thrives in well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies due to its role in nitrogen fixation and habitat support for various insects.
