Tinospora cordifolia var. congesta

Tinospora cordifolia var. congesta, commonly known as Indian knotted rue, is a climbing plant native to South and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in forest understories.

Botanically, it belongs to the Menispermaceae family and is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional systems of medicine.
