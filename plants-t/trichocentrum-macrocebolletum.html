Trichocentrum macrocebolletum

Trichocentrum macrocebolletum, commonly known as the giant orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that can reach several feet in length, bearing numerous fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and surrounding substrate.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
