Tainia serratiloba

Tainia serratiloba, commonly known as the serrated tainia, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive inflorescence known as a spadix, surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant typically features broad, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, contributing to its common name. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in humid forests.

Tainia serratiloba is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine by local communities.
