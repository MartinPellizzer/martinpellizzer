Triticum vagans

Triticum vagans, commonly known as the wandering wheat, is a rare and unique grass species characterized by its distinctive flowering pattern.

It produces small, delicate spikelets that appear in a spiral arrangement along the stem, giving it a flowing, almost ethereal appearance. This plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its botanical features include narrow, grass-like leaves and a short, compact growth habit.

Due to its rarity and unique morphology, Triticum vagans is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
