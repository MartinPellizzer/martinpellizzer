Tsuga diversifolia subsp. blaringhemii

Tsuga diversifolia subsp.

blaringhemii, commonly known as the Blaringhem's hemlock, is a rare subspecies native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia. It is characterized by its conical shape and dense, dark green foliage that turns a striking blue-gray in winter.

The leaves are needle-like, short, and arranged in spirals along the branches. It produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, typical of coniferous plants.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
