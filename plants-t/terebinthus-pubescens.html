Terebinthus pubescens

Terebinthus pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent terebinth, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and oval-shaped leaves covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain one or two seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
