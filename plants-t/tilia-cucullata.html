Tilia cucullata

Tilia cucullata, commonly known as the hooded linden, is a deciduous tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Tiliaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early summer, followed by small, round fruits. Its bark is smooth and light grey, becoming fissured with age.

Tilia cucullata is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional medicine.
