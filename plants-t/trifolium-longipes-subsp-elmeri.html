Trifolium longipes subsp. elmeri

Trifolium longipes subsp.

elmeri, commonly known as Elmer's tall clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three oval to lance-shaped leaflets.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or red, arranged in dense clusters at the tips of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open meadows and grasslands.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
