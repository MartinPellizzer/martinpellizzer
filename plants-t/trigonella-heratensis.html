Trigonella heratensis

Trigonella heratensis, commonly known as the Herat bitter vetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several elliptical seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Trigonella heratensis is adapted to arid environments and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
