Thiloa inundata

Thiloa inundata, commonly known as the water thiloa, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its aquatic or semi-aquatic habitat, often found in wetlands and marshy areas. The plant has slender, ribbon-like leaves that float on the water surface, aiding in photosynthesis. Its small, white flowers bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as insects and birds.

Thiloa inundata plays a role in maintaining the ecological balance of its wetland environment.
