Thelypteris pseudohirsuta

Thelypteris pseudohirsuta, commonly known as the hirsute shield fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its delicate, leathery fronds that are typically dark green and covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces spores on the underside of its leaves, which are arranged in clusters called sori. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices.

Thelypteris pseudohirsuta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded garden settings.
