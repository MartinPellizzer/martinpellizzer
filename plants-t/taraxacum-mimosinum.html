Taraxacum mimosinum

Taraxacum mimosinum, commonly known as the Mimosinum dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and a yellow flowering head composed of numerous small florets. The plant produces a distinctive, hollow, wind-dispersed seed head known as a pappus. It is native to parts of Europe and is often found in meadows and grassy areas.

Taraxacum mimosinum is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
