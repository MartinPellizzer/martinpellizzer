Teucrium polium f. cossonii

Teucrium polium f. cossonii, commonly known as the Corsican germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, arranged in dense spikes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It is valued in horticulture for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
