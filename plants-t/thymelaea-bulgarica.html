Thymelaea bulgarica

Thymelaea bulgarica, commonly known as the Bulgarian thymelaea, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Thymelaeaceae and is characterized by its small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and is often found in dry, open habitats. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often covered with fine hairs.

Thymelaea bulgarica is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
