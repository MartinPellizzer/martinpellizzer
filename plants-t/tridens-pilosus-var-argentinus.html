Tridens pilosus var. argentinus

Tridens pilosus var. argentinus, commonly known as the Argentine hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, hairy leaves and compact growth habit, often forming dense mats in wet or moist soils. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in marshy areas or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and wetland restoration projects.
