Tropaeolum maculifolium

Tropaeolum maculifolium, commonly known as the speckled nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful flowers that range from yellow to orange and red, and its heart-shaped, speckled leaves. The plant is a member of the Tropaeolaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible flowers. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Tropaeolum maculifolium is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
