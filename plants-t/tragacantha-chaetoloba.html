Tragacantha chaetoloba

Tragacantha chaetoloba, commonly known as the hairy tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny environments. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in the spring. Its stems are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The hairy tragacanth is valued for its resin, which has been used historically in various medicinal and industrial applications.
