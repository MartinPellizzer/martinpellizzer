Thelypteris imponens

Thelypteris imponens, commonly known as the golden shield fern, is a species of fern native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, leathery, and often golden-brown fronds that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its fronds are pinnately compound, with closely spaced, lance-shaped pinnae.

Thelypteris imponens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
