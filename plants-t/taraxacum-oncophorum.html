Taraxacum oncophorum

Taraxacum oncophorum, commonly known as the Andean dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the dandelion genus, which is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads. The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are typically hairy and have a waxy coating. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in the spring, producing a characteristic seed head with parachute-like seeds.

The species is adapted to high-altitude environments and is often found in alpine meadows.
