Taraxacum stupendum

Taraxacum stupendum, commonly known as the magnificent dandelion, is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust taproot system.

It features a distinctive yellow flower head composed of numerous tiny florets, which mature into fluffy white seeds carried by the wind. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its resilience to environmental stressors. Its leaves are typically basal, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while the stem is hollow and ribbed.

Taraxacum stupendum is also notable for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in herbal remedies for its purported detoxifying effects.
