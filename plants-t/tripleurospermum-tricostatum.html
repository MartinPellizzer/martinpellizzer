Tripleurospermum tricostatum

Tripleurospermum tricostatum, commonly known as the three-costate hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and daisy-like appearance. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence. It thrives in sunny, open habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

Its name refers to the distinctive three-ridged structure of its leaf margins.
