Teucrium marum subvar. eriocalyx

Teucrium marum subvar. eriocalyx, commonly known as swamp germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, tubular flowers in clusters during the summer months. The plant's flowering stems are typically slender and can grow up to 60 cm in height. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in swamps, bogs, and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
