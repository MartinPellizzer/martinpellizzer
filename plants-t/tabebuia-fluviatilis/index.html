Tabebuia Fluviatilis

Pink trumpet tree, scientifically known as Tabebuia fluviatilis, is a striking tropical tree native to Central and South America. It is celebrated for its vibrant pink or red flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a breathtaking display during the wet season. The tree's unique trumpet-shaped blossoms not only add beauty to its surroundings but also attract a variety of pollinators. Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a popular choice for ornamental planting in warm climates. Despite its aesthetic appeal, the Pink trumpet tree also holds cultural and ecological significance in its native regions.
