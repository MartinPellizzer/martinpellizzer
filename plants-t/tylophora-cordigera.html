Tylophora cordigera

Tylophora cordigera, commonly known as the heart-shaped tylophora, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, orange-red fruits that contain seeds enclosed in a fleshy, sticky aril. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in forest understories.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
