Tibouchina papyrus

Tibouchina papyrus, commonly known as the papyrus plant, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, reed-like stems and broad, sword-shaped leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces small, clustered white or pink flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in marshy areas or along water bodies.

Due to its historical significance and ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections worldwide.
