Triticum savignonei

Triticum savignonei, commonly known as the Savignone wheat, is a wild grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and spikelet-bearing inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to temperate climates. Its seeds are small and oval, with a tough, glabrous surface.

Although not widely cultivated, it has been studied for its potential in agricultural research and genetic diversity.
