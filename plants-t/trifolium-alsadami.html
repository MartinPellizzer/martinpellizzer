Trifolium alsadami

Trifolium alsadami, commonly known as the Alsadami clover, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged symmetrically. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides.

Due to its ornamental value and nitrogen-fixing ability, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
