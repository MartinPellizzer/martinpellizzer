Tanaecium apiculatum

Tanaecium apiculatum, commonly known as the golden star cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it its common name. The plant has a short, thick stem with spiny leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. Its flowers are typically visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological role.

Tanaecium apiculatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking appearance and resilience.
