Turraea junodii

Turraea junodii, commonly known as the African sandalwood, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland forest areas.

Due to its aromatic wood, it has been historically valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of incense.
