Tradescantia spicata

Tradescantia spicata, commonly known as spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and clusters of blue, violet, or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is typically found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces small, three-lobed seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Tradescantia spicata is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
