Triadenum virginicum subsp. fraseri

Triadenum virginicum subsp.

fraseri, commonly known as Fraser's triadenum, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States. It typically grows in moist, sandy soils in open woodlands and along stream banks.

The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the spring. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor environments.

It is valued for its ecological role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining soil stability.
