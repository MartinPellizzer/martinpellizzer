Trigonella karkarensis

Trigonella karkarensis, commonly known as Karkare's fenugreek, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to the western Ghats of India and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces lens-shaped seeds that are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
