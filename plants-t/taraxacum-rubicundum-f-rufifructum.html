Taraxacum rubicundum f. rufifructum

Taraxacum rubicundum f. rufifructum, commonly known as the red-flowered dandelion, is a botanical variant of the common dandelion.

It is characterized by its vibrant red-orange flowers, which stand out from the typical yellow of most dandelion species. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.

Its distinctive coloration makes it a popular subject in botanical studies and horticulture.
