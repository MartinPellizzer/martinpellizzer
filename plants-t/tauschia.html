Tauschia

Tauschia, commonly known as the "water onion," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Amaranthaceae.

It consists of a single species, Tauschia arvensis, which is native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant is characterized by its erect, herbaceous stems and small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red in color. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a hairy texture.

Tauschia is often found growing in moist soils along riverbanks and in wet meadows, making it a characteristic wetland plant.
