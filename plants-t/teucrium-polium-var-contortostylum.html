Teucrium polium var. contortostylum

Teucrium polium var. contortostylum, commonly known as the twisted-stem germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, often twisted stems that are covered in fine, hairy hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly aromatic scent when crushed.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.
