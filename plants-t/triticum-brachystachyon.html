Triticum brachystachyon

Triticum brachystachyon, commonly known as the short-stalked wheat, is a species of grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a small annual plant with slender, branched stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous short, awned florets. Its flowers are wind-pollinated, and it typically blooms in late spring to early summer.

Triticum brachystachyon is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
