Trichostachys longifolia

Trichostachys longifolia, commonly known as the giant bamboo or giant cane, is a tall, perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically green and often have a slight pubescence. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in panicles, which are arranged in a branching pattern.

Trichostachys longifolia is valued for its rapid growth and is used in construction, crafts, and as a ornamental plant.
