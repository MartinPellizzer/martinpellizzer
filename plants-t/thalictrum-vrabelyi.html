Thalictrum vrabelyi

Thalictrum vrabelyi, commonly known as the Vrabel's meadow rue, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, usually white or greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are compound, with finely divided leaflets, contributing to its delicate, lace-like appearance.

Thalictrum vrabelyi is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
