Trichomanes minutulum

Trichomanes minutulum, commonly known as the small maidenhair fern, is a delicate, evergreen fern native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features intricate, finely divided fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length, giving it a lacy appearance. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves, which are clustered in groups. Trichomanes minutulum thrives in shady, moist environments such as woodlands and rock crevices.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and conservation efforts.
