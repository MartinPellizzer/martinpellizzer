Triticum freycenetii

Triticum freycenetii, commonly known as the Freycenetii wheat, is a wild relative of cultivated wheat species.

It is native to the mountainous regions of the Mediterranean, particularly in southern Europe and North Africa. This plant is characterized by its slender stalks and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous florets. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces seeds in compact clusters.

Triticum freycenetii is valued for its genetic diversity and potential contributions to wheat improvement programs.
