Teagueia alyssana

Teagueia alyssana, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a species native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that have a silvery underside, giving it its common name. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing a fragrant scent that attracts pollinators.

The tree is also valued for its hard, durable wood and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
