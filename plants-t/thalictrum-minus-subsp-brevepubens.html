Thalictrum minus subsp. brevepubens

Thalictrum minus subsp.

brevepubens, commonly known as the short-hairy meadow rue, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features slender stems with finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in short, soft hairs. Its flowers are small, clustered in elongated racemes, and are usually a pale yellow or greenish-white color.

It is valued in gardens for its delicate appearance and ability to thrive in partial shade.
