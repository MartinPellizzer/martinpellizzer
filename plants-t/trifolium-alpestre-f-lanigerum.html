Trifolium alpestre f. lanigerum

Trifolium alpestre f. lanigerum, commonly known as the alpine clover, is a botanical variant of the common clover species.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. This plant typically grows in high-altitude regions, such as mountainous areas and alpine meadows. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, pink or white flowers in clusters.

The plant is adapted to cold climates and is often found in regions with poor soil conditions.
