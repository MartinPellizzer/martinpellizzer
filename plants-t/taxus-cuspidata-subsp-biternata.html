Taxus cuspidata subsp. biternata

Taxus cuspidata subsp.

biternata, commonly known as the Japanese yew, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Japan. It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are arranged in whorls of three.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional Japanese gardens.
