Teucrium pulverulentum

Teucrium pulverulentum, commonly known as pale bugloss, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, powdery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, blue to violet flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom from spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly rough texture.

Teucrium pulverulentum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
