Tragus major

Tragus major, commonly known as the greater tragus, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, bristle-like structures that form a dense, tufted appearance. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, often clustered in dense heads, and are pollinated by insects.

The species is widely distributed across regions of Africa, the Middle East, and parts of Asia.
