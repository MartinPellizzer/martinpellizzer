Taraxacum pacheri

Taraxacum pacheri, commonly known as the Carpathian dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and forest edges, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are basal, spoon-shaped, and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and produce wind-dispersed seeds.

Taraxacum pacheri is notable for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
