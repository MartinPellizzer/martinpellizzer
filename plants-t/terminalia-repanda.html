Terminalia repanda

Terminalia repanda, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark grey bark and simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by woody, elongated fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

The wood of Terminalia repanda is valued for its durability and fine texture, making it useful in traditional crafts and construction.
