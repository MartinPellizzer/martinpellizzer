Traganum gracile

Traganum gracile, commonly known as the slender pigweed, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaranthaceae family.

It is a small, annual herb that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is well adapted to hot, dry climates and can thrive in poor soil conditions.

Traganum gracile is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.
