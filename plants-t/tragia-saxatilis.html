Tragia saxatilis

Tragia saxatilis, commonly known as the rock tragia, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a small, annual herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss and protect against intense sunlight.

Tragia saxatilis is also known for its medicinal properties, with various traditional uses in herbal remedies.
