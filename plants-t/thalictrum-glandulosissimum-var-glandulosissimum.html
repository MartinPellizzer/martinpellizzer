Thalictrum glandulosissimum var. glandulosissimum

Thalictrum glandulosissimum var. glandulosissimum, commonly known as the woolly meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia.

It features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are covered in soft, glandular hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in compound umbels, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in meadows and woodlands.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
