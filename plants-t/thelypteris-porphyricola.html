Thelypteris porphyricola

Thelypteris porphyricola, commonly known as the red-stemmed shield fern, is a species of fern native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Thelypteridaceae and is characterized by its dark red stems and serrated, lance-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its spores are produced on the undersides of its fronds, which are protected by a specialized structure called a sorus.

Thelypteris porphyricola is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in maintaining ecological balance in its native habitat.
