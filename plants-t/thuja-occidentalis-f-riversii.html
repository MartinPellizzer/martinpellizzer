Thuja occidentalis f. riversii

Thuja occidentalis f. riversii, commonly known as the River's Thuja, is a unique form of the eastern arborvitae.

It is characterized by its narrow, pyramidal shape and dense, scale-like foliage. This variety is native to the northeastern United States and parts of Canada, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. It exhibits slow growth and is often used in horticultural landscapes for its ornamental value.

The plant is relatively disease-resistant and tolerates a range of environmental conditions, making it a versatile choice for gardeners.
