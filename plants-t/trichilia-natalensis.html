Trichilia natalensis

Trichilia natalensis, commonly known as Natal trichilia, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae, which includes mahogany and other tropical hardwoods. The tree is characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and opposite, ovate leaves with prominent veins. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in racemes, while the fruit is a woody capsule containing several flat, winged seeds.

The plant is valued for its timber and has traditional uses in local medicine.
