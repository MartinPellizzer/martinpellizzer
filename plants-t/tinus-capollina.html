Tinus capollina

Tinus capollina, commonly known as the Cape goldcup, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Tinus capollina is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
