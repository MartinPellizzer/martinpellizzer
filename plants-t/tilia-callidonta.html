Tilia callidonta

Tilia callidonta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf lime, is a deciduous tree native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that are typically hairy on the underside. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which bloom in early summer. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

Tilia callidonta is often found in moist, temperate habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
