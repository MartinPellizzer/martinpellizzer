Taraxacum akteum

Taraxacum akteum, commonly known as the Aktean dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of spoon-shaped leaves and a flowering stem that bears a single, yellow composite flower head. The plant is native to Central Asia and is often found in meadows and grassy areas. Its flowers produce wind-dispersed seeds with parachute-like structures, aiding in its spread.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
