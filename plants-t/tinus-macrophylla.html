Tinus macrophylla

Tinus macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf tamarind, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs. The tree produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by elongated pods containing edible seeds. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing a rugged appearance.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine.
