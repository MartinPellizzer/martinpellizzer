Taraxacum crispulum

Taraxacum crispulum, commonly known as the curlyleaf dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, deeply lobed, curly leaves that form a rosette near the ground. The plant produces a yellow flower head that develops into a fluffy white seed ball, typical of the dandelion genus. It is often found in meadows, lawns, and disturbed soils across temperate regions.

Taraxacum crispulum is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
