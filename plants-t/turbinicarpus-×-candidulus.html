Turbinicarpus × candidulus

Turbinicarpus × candidulus, commonly known as the hybrid moon cactus, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is a hybrid between Turbinicarpus gounellei and Turbinicarpus stellatus, resulting in a unique combination of traits from both parent species. The plant is characterized by its rounded, flattened stem that resembles a moon, hence its common name. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, appearing in clusters during the summer months.

Due to its attractive appearance and ease of care, it is a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts.
