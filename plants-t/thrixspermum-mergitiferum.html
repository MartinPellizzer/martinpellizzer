Thrixspermum mergitiferum

Thrixspermum mergitiferum, commonly known as the coastal thrixspermum, is a unique flowering plant native to the temperate coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and its ability to thrive in saline and windy environments. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring season. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its arid habitat.

Thrixspermum mergitiferum is also notable for its ecological role in stabilizing coastal dunes and providing habitat for native insect species.
