Tupistra esquirolii

Tupistra esquirolii, commonly known as the Esquiroli's tufted cushion, is a low-growing evergreen plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense, mat-forming growth with small, leathery leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the spring. It is well adapted to rocky soils and full sun exposure, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens.

Its compact size and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for landscaping in arid and coastal environments.
