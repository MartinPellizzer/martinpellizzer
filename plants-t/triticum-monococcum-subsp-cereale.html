Triticum monococcum subsp. cereale

Triticum monococcum subsp.

cereale, commonly known as einkorn wheat, is an ancient grain species native to the Fertile Crescent. It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescence with short, awned glumes.

The plant produces small, pale green grains that are rich in nutrients and protein. It is a diploid species with a genome size smaller than modern wheat, contributing to its genetic simplicity.

Due to its resilience and nutritional value, it is gaining interest in modern agriculture and health food markets.
