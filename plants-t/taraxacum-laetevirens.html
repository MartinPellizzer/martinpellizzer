Taraxacum laetevirens

Taraxacum laetevirens, commonly known as the cheerful green dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped, hairy leaves and a yellow flower head that develops into a white, fluffy seed head. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows, fields, and disturbed soils. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through wind-dispersed seeds.

Its botanical features make it a common and recognizable weed in many regions.
