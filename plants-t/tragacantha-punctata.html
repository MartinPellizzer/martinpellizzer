Tragacantha punctata

Tragacantha punctata, commonly known as the spotted tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and hairy, spiny pods. The plant produces a resinous substance called tragacanth, which has been historically used in medicine and as a food thickener. Tragacantha punctata typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

Its unique appearance and ecological adaptations make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
