Tragacantha johannis

Tragacantha johannis, commonly known as the Johann's tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces a gum-like substance from its bark, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a thickening agent. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
