Teucrium polium subsp. ghikae

Teucrium polium subsp.

ghikae, commonly known as Persian germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Central Asia. It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, hairy pubescence, and its leaves are lanceolate with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue to violet in color, arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its aromatic leaves and medicinal properties have made it a subject of interest in traditional herbal medicine.
