Teucrium parviflorum

Teucrium parviflorum, commonly known as narrowleaf germander, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb with slender, aromatic stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
