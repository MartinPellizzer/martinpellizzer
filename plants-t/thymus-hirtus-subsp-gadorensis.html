Thymus hirtus subsp. gadorensis

Thymus hirtus subsp.

gadorensis, commonly known as the Gadorensis thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and aromatic, small, grey-green leaves.

The plant produces pink to purple flowers in spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Botanically, it belongs to the Lamiaceae family, sharing traits with other members like its aromatic oils and square stems.
