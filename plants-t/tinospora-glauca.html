Tinospora glauca

Tinospora glauca, commonly known as Indian creeper or Guduchi, is a climbing plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender stems, opposite leaves, and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces yellowish-orange berries that contain seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests and along riverbanks.

Tinospora glauca is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional systems of medicine.
