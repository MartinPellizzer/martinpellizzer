Tephrosia amorphifolia

Tephrosia amorphifolia, commonly known as the African milk bush, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, featuring trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is characterized by its milky sap, which is toxic and has been used in traditional practices. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savannas and woodland areas.

Tephrosia amorphifolia is also valued for its potential in agroforestry and soil conservation.
