Trifolium pratense var. nivale

Trifolium pratense var. nivale, commonly known as snow clover, is a variant of the common red clover.

It is characterized by its pale white or pale pink flowers, which bloom in early spring. This plant is typically found in moist, calcareous soils and is often associated with grasslands and meadows. It has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological systems.
