Trachymene commutata

Trachymene commutata, commonly known as the common blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its delicate, blue or purple flower heads that resemble mist. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and woodlands, often in sandy or rocky soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

Trachymene commutata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
