Trifolium longipes subsp. longipes

Trifolium longipes subsp.

longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval to lanceolate leaflets arranged on a slender stem.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or red, blooming in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
