Trichilia parviflora

Trichilia parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered trichilia, is a tropical tree belonging to the family Meliaceae.

It is native to regions of Africa and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces hard, woody fruits that contain flat, winged seeds, aiding in wind dispersal. It is often found in moist, shaded habitats within tropical rainforests.

The plant has been used traditionally for its medicinal properties, particularly in treating ailments related to the respiratory and digestive systems.
