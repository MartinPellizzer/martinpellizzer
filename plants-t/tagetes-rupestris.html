Tagetes rupestris

Tagetes rupestris, commonly known as the mountain marigold, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Tagetes rupestris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant.
