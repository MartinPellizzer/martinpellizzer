Tapeinosperma glandulosum

Tapeinosperma glandulosum, commonly known as the woolly acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems covered in soft, glandular hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in globular inflorescences, which are typical of the Fabaceae family. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple pairs of small, oval leaflets.

The species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in harsh conditions.
