Tristachya simplex

Tristachya simplex, commonly known as the slender panic grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or disturbed areas.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes used in ornamental gardening or as a stabilizing plant in erosion-prone areas.
