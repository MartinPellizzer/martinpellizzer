Tibouchina villosissima

Tibouchina villosissima, commonly known as the velvetleaf or woolly hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, velvety leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in the spring and summer. Tibouchina villosissima thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in landscaping and garden design.
