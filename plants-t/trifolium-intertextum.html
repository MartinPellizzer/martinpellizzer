Trifolium intertextum

Trifolium intertextum, commonly known as the interwoven clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Trifolium intertextum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its presence is an indicator of a healthy, biodiverse ecosystem.
