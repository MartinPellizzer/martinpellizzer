Trigonella macrantha

Trigonella macrantha, commonly known as the large-flowered fenugreek, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks. Its seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a spice, similar to fenugreek.

The botanical features of Trigonella macrantha make it an important species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
