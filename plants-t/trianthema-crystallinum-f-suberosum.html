Trianthema crystallinum f. suberosum

Trianthema crystallinum f. suberosum, commonly known as the crystalline trianthema, is a succulent plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a waxy, translucent coating, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming during the cooler months. Its suberized stem surface provides additional protection against desiccation and herbivory.

This variety is well adapted to harsh environments, making it a resilient and drought-tolerant species.
