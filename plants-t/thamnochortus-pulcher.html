Thamnochortus pulcher

Thamnochortus pulcher, commonly known as the purple orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to southern Africa.

This plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of vibrant purple or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth. The flowers are typically tubular and attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens, though it is relatively rare in the wild.
