Tribulus

Tribulus, commonly known as puncture vine, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Zygophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its spiny fruits and yellow flowers, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant is native to various regions of Europe, Asia, and Africa, and it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. Tribulus species are known for their medicinal properties, particularly in supporting male health and athletic performance.

Its botanical features make it both a resilient weed and a valuable herbal remedy.
