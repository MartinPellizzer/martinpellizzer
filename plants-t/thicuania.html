Thicuania

Thicuania, commonly known as the golden spire plant, is a unique species native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its tall, spiraling stems that can reach heights of up to 10 feet. The plant produces small, vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and waxy, adapted to retain moisture in its arid environment.

Thicuania is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
