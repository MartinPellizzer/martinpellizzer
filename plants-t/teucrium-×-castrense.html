Teucrium × castrense

Teucrium × castrense, commonly known as hybrid germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Teucrium chamaedrys and Teucrium polium, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple, arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Teucrium × castrense is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
