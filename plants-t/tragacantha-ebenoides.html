Tragacantha ebenoides

Tragacantha ebenoides, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Iran and the Middle East.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is known for producing a valuable resin, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive.

Its ability to thrive in harsh environments makes it an important species in ecological restoration projects in dry regions.
