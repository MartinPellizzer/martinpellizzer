Trifolium pamphylicum

Trifolium pamphylicum, commonly known as the Pamphylian clover, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Trifolium pamphylicum is also valued for its role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.
