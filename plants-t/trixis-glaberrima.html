Trixis glaberrima

Trixis glaberrima, commonly known as the silky trixis, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its soft, silvery foliage and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom during the cooler months. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its growth habit is typically shrub-like, with multiple stems arising from the base.

Trixis glaberrima is often found in rocky or sandy habitats, contributing to the biodiversity of its native ecosystems.
