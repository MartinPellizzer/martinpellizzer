Tradescantia formosa

Tradescantia formosa, commonly known as the spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It features striking, showy flowers in shades of violet, pink, and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Tradescantia formosa is also known for its ability to spread via rhizomes, forming dense ground cover in suitable conditions.
