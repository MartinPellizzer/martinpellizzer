Tragacantha melina

Tragacantha melina, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a species of flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and woody pods. The plant is known for producing a viscous gum, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive. Its growth habit includes a tall, often multi-stemmed shrub or small tree, adapting to arid and semi-arid environments.

The leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are often hairy and aromatic.
