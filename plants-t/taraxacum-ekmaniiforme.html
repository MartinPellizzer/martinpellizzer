Taraxacum ekmaniiforme

Taraxacum ekmaniiforme, commonly known as the Ekmanniiforme dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and a yellow composite flower head that develops into a crown of white pappus. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, this plant thrives in dry, rocky habitats. It has a taproot system and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and potential ecological role in its native environment.
