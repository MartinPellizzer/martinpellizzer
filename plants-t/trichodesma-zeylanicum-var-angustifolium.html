Trichodesma zeylanicum var. angustifolium

Trichodesma zeylanicum var. angustifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved trichodesma, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring slender, angular stems covered in fine hairs. The leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical in shape, with a narrow, pointed tip and a slightly hairy surface. Its small, clustered flowers are usually pale yellow to white, with four petals, and bloom during the warm seasons.

The plant is adapted to well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it a common sight in coastal and semi-arid environments.
