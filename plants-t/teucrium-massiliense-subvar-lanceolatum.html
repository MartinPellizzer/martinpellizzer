Teucrium massiliense subvar. lanceolatum

Teucrium massiliense subvar. lanceolatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers arranged in terminal clusters, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental value make it a popular choice among gardeners seeking drought-tolerant plants.
