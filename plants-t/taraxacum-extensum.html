Taraxacum extensum

Taraxacum extensum, commonly known as the broadleaf dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette of broad, spoon-shaped leaves and a flowering stem that produces a single, yellow composite flower. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed areas. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, and it has a deep taproot that allows it to survive in harsh conditions.

The plant is also noted for its medicinal properties, with its leaves used in traditional remedies for digestive and respiratory ailments.
