Tephrocactus platyacanthus var. neoplatyacanthus

Tephrocactus platyacanthus var. neoplatyacanthus, commonly known as the Mexican torch cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is a columnar cactus with a distinctive red or orange spines and a ribbed stem. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking visual contrast to its spiny structure. It is well adapted to dry conditions, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

This variety is prized in cactus collections for its unique appearance and hardy nature.
