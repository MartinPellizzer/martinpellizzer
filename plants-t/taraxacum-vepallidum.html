Taraxacum vepallidum

Taraxacum vepallidum, commonly known as the pale hawkweed, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family, Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of light green, hairy leaves and a single, upright stem that bears a yellow flower head. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are followed by distinctive, hollow, hairless seed heads. It is typically found in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Taraxacum vepallidum is a perennial plant that is adapted to temperate climates and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
