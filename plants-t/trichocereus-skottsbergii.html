Trichocereus skottsbergii

Trichocereus skottsbergii, commonly known as the "San Pedro cactus," is a species of cactus native to South America, particularly Peru and Ecuador.

It is a columnar cactus with a smooth, green stem that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces large, white, nocturnal flowers that bloom during the warmer months. It is characterized by its spines, which are often reduced or absent in cultivated varieties.

Trichocereus skottsbergii is also known for its psychoactive properties due to the presence of mescaline.
