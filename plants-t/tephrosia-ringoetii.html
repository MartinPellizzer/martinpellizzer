Tephrosia ringoetii

Tephrosia ringoetii, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in spikes. Its seeds are enclosed in hairy, pod-like fruits that split open when mature.

Tephrosia ringoetii is also noted for its medicinal uses in traditional African medicine.
