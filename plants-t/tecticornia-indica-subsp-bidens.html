Tecticornia indica subsp. bidens

Tecticornia indica subsp.

bidens, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tecticornia, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, exhibiting a low, spreading growth habit.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention.

This species is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low rainfall, making it a resilient component of its native ecosystem.
