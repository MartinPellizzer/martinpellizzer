Taxus baccata fastigiata

Taxus baccata fastigiata, commonly known as the columnar yew, is a cultivar of the European yew.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, dense foliage. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for ornamental planting in gardens.

This evergreen shrub is valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of conditions.
