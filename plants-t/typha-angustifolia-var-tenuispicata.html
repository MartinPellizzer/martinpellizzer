Typha angustifolia var. tenuispicata

Typha angustifolia var. tenuispicata, commonly known as narrow-leaved cattail, is a variant of the common cattail found in wetland environments.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and dense, cylindrical flower spikes that are typically reddish-brown in color. This plant thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often forming dense stands in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.

The plant plays a vital role in water filtration and provides habitat for various aquatic organisms.
