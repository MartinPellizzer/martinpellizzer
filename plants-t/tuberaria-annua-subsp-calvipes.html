Tuberaria annua subsp. calvipes

Tuberaria annua subsp.

calvipes, commonly known as the hairy yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its annual life cycle and distinctive yellow, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, giving it a rugged appearance.

The subspecies calvipes is distinguished by its reduced leaf hairs compared to other subspecies of Tuberaria annua.
