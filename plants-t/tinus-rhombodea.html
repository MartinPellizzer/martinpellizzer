Tinus rhombodea

Tinus rhombodea, commonly known as the diamond leaf plant, is a species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive diamond-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
