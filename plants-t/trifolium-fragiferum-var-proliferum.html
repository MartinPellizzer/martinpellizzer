Trifolium fragiferum var. proliferum

Trifolium fragiferum var. proliferum, commonly known as the fragrant clover, is a variant of the common clover species characterized by its distinctive aromatic foliage.

This plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or white, which bloom in early spring. The plant is often found in nutrient-poor soils and is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

Its rapid growth and spreading habit make it a popular choice for erosion control and ornamental gardening.
