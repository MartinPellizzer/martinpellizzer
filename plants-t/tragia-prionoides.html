Tragia prionoides

Tragia prionoides, commonly known as the prickly morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree with woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It is characterized by its spiny stems and capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

Tragia prionoides is adapted to dry conditions and is often found in disturbed or arid environments.
