Trophaeum pendulum

Trophaeum pendulum, commonly known as the hanging bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its pendulous, bell-shaped flowers that hang from the stems, often in clusters. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, seed-containing capsules after flowering.

Trophaeum pendulum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
