Triticum turgidum subsp. maroccanum

Triticum turgidum subsp.

maroccanum, commonly known as Moroccan wheat, is a wild wheat species native to North Africa. It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and awned spikelets, which are typical features of the grass family Poaceae.

The plant produces small, pale green grains that are often used in traditional local cuisines. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climatic conditions.

Due to its genetic diversity, it is of interest for breeding programs aimed at improving wheat resilience to climate change.
