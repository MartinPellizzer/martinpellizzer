Tragacantha candolleana

Tragacantha candolleana, commonly known as the Indian tragacanth, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of India and Pakistan.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by pods containing small, hard seeds. Its most notable feature is the sap it exudes, which is used in traditional medicine and as a natural gum in various industries.

The plant is well adapted to dry climates and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
