Taraxacum gorodkovii

Taraxacum gorodkovii, commonly known as the Gorodkov's dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and a distinctive yellow composite flower head. Native to Central Asia, this plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. It has small, pinnately lobed leaves and produces a hollow, wind-dispersed seed stem.

The plant is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
