Trifolium rubens f. guarensis

Trifolium rubens f. guarensis, commonly known as the red clover forma guarensis, is a botanical variant of the red clover species.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, typically with three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides.

This subspecies is valued for its role in soil enrichment and as a forage crop.
