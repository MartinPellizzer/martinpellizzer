Trifolium angustifolium var. ramosum

Trifolium angustifolium var. ramosum, commonly known as narrow-leaved clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval to lance-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pink to white flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed sites.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in soil stabilization and as a forage crop.
