Tragacantha ciliolata

Tragacantha ciliolata, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2 to 5 meters. The plant is characterized by its small, white or pinkish flowers that appear in clusters during the spring. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a hairy texture.

The plant is known for producing a resinous gum, which has been historically used in various traditional applications.
