Triticum pyramidale var. aydiniense

Triticum pyramidale var. aydiniense, commonly known as the Aydiniense wheat, is a rare and ancient variety of wheat native to the Anatolian region of Turkey.

It is characterized by its small, compact grains and is often found in archaeological sites, indicating its historical significance in early agricultural practices. The plant exhibits a range of morphological features, including slender stems and a distinctive glume structure. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought conditions.

Due to its genetic uniqueness, it is of interest to researchers studying crop diversity and ancient grain cultivation.
