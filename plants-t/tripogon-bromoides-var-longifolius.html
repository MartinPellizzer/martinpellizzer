Tripogon bromoides var. longifolius

Tripogon bromoides var. longifolius, commonly known as the long-leaved false brome, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as rocky soils and limestone areas. The plant features slender, erect stems with narrow, elongated leaves that are often hairy along the edges. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, two-lipped flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
