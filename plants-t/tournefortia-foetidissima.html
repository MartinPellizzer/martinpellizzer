Tournefortia foetidissima

Tournefortia foetidissima, commonly known as the stinkwood or foul-smelling tree, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are often clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a rough texture.

The species is known for its strong, unpleasant odor, which is most noticeable when the leaves are crushed.
