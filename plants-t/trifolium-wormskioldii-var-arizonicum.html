Trifolium wormskioldii var. arizonicum

Trifolium wormskioldii var. arizonicum, commonly known as Arizona mountain clover, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a perennial herb with trifoliate leaves, each leaflet being oval to lance-shaped. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is noted for its ecological importance as a food source for native pollinators and its role in stabilizing soils in rocky habitats.
