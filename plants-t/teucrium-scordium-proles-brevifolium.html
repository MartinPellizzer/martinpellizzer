Teucrium scordium proles brevifolium

Teucrium scordium proles brevifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or dry soils and has small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers during the summer months. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
