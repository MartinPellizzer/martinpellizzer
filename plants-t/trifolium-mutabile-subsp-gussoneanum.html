Trifolium mutabile subsp. gussoneanum

Trifolium mutabile subsp.

gussoneanum, commonly known as the Gussonean clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically pale green with a slightly hairy texture.

The plant produces small, pink to red flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
