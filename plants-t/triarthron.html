Triarthron

Triarthron, commonly known as the golden bell, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped yellow flowers. The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.
