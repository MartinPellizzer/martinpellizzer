Tephrothamnus aequinoctialis

Tephrothamnus aequinoctialis, commonly known as the equinox thyme, is a low-growing shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by aromatic leaves and square stems. The plant typically features small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of purple and white. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Tephrothamnus aequinoctialis thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, temperate climates.
