Trichomanes venulosum

Trichomanes venulosum, commonly known as the veined maidenhair fern, is a delicate fern species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Hymenophyllaceae and is characterized by its finely divided, lacy fronds. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found on rocks or tree trunks. Its fronds are typically green with a pale underside, and it produces small, spore-bearing structures called sporangia.

Trichomanes venulosum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
