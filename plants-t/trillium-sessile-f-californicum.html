Trillium sessile f. californicum

Trillium sessile f. californicum, commonly known as the California sessile trillium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features three broad, waxy leaves that are typically green with a reddish or purple hue on the underside. The plant produces a single, showy white or pinkish flower with three petals and three sepals, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded woodlands and is often found in California's coastal ranges.

The species is characterized by its sessile (stemless) leaves and is part of the Trilliaceae family.
