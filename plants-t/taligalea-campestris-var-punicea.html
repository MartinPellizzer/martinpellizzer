Taligalea campestris var. punicea

Taligalea campestris var. punicea, commonly known as the red sand mat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It features dense, mat-forming growth with small, fleshy, oval leaves that are typically red or purple in color, providing a striking visual contrast. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is well adapted to hot, dry climates and requires minimal water once established.

Taligalea campestris var. punicea is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
