Trisetum spicatum f. flavicundum

Trisetum spicatum f. flavicundum, commonly known as the yellow-haired false brome, is a grass species characterized by its distinctive yellowish inflorescence.

It is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically found in grassy habitats and disturbed soils. The plant features slender, tufted stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, producing panicle-like clusters of small, yellowish flowers.

This variant is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of conditions.
