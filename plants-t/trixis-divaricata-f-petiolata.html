Trixis divaricata f. petiolata

Trixis divaricata f. petiolata, commonly known as the broad-leaved trixis, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, and is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens in suitable climates.
