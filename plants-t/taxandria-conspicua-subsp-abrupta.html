Taxandria conspicua subsp. abrupta

Taxandria conspicua subsp.

abrupta, commonly known as the mountain gum tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to eastern Australia. It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and lance-shaped, glossy green leaves that are arranged alternately along the branches.

The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in clusters during the spring, which are followed by woody, urn-shaped fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry sclerophyll forests and rocky outcrops.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions and its role in supporting local wildlife.
