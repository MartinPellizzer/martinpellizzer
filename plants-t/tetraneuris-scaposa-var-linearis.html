Tetraneuris scaposa var. linearis

Tetraneuris scaposa var. linearis, commonly known as the linear tetraneuris, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. Its flowers are small and yellow, often arranged in clusters, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

The variety linearis is distinguished by its elongated, narrow leaves compared to the typical form of Tetraneuris scaposa.
