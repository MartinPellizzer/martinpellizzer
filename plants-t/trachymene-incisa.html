Trachymene incisa

Trachymene incisa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved false yarrow, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a perennial herb with slender, upright stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open forests and grasslands.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract native pollinators.
