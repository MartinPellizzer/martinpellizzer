Tagetes graveolens

Tagetes graveolens, commonly known as the Mexican marigold, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange and yellow flowers and pungent, aromatic foliage. The plant is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their antimicrobial properties.

The flowers are also widely used in horticulture for their ability to repel pests and improve soil health.
