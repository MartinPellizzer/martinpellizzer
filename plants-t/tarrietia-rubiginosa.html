Tarrietia rubiginosa

Tarrietia rubiginosa, commonly known as the red-staining tarrietia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Rubiaceae, which includes many species of shrubs and herbs. The plant is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. Its leaves are typically opposite and have a somewhat hairy texture.

The plant is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
