Trollius ledebourii var. pulcher

Trollius ledebourii var. pulcher, commonly known as the showy globeflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high altitudes of southern Africa.

It features showy, bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit with hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are arranged in a spherical inflorescence, giving it its common name.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.
