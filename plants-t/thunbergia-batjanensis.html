Thunbergia batjanensis

Thunbergia batjanensis, commonly known as the Batjan's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from violet to deep purple. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine, featuring heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Thunbergia batjanensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant.
