Thalia caerulea

Thalia caerulea, commonly known as the blue thalia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as along stream banks or in forest understories. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

Thalia caerulea is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local ecosystems.
