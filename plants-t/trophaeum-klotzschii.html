Trophaeum klotzschii

Trophaeum klotzschii, commonly known as the mountain lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its flowers bloom in late summer.

Trophaeum klotzschii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
