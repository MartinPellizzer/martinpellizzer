Tripsacum intermedium

Tripsacum intermedium, commonly known as intermediate tripes, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit with dense, spike-like inflorescences. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, two-celled spikelets that are often clustered in panicles. It is adapted to a variety of soil types and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for marginal agricultural lands.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and agricultural research.
