Tithymalus saxicola

Tithymalus saxicola, commonly known as the saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to rocky and alpine environments.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, or purple. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions across Europe and Asia. Its leaves are usually basal, with a rosette structure that helps it conserve moisture.

Tithymalus saxicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to colonize harsh, rocky terrains.
