Tricholaena leucantha

Tricholaena leucantha, commonly known as the white-leafed grass or African silvergrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, silvery-green leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in panicles, typically blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in open grasslands or along riverbanks.

Tricholaena leucantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its graceful, flowing growth habit.
