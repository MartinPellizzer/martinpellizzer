Triumfetta welwitschii var. rehmannii

Triumfetta welwitschii var. rehmannii, commonly known as the wild morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and showy blue or purple flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savannas and rocky areas.
