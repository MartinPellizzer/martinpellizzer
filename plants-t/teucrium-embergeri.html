Teucrium embergeri

Teucrium embergeri, commonly known as Emberger's germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, blooming in the summer months. It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.

Teucrium embergeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
