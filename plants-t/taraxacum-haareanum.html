Taraxacum haareanum

Taraxacum haareanum, commonly known as the Haarean dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of pale green, hairy leaves and a yellow flower head that develops into a white, fluffy seed head. Native to Europe, it thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils. The plant has a taproot system and is known for its ability to spread via wind-dispersed seeds.

It is also valued in some regions for its edible leaves and traditional medicinal uses.
