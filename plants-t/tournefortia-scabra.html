Tournefortia scabra

Tournefortia scabra, commonly known as rough-leaved tournefortia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils. The plant features narrow, rough-textured leaves and produces small, clustered white or yellowish flowers. It is known for its ability to thrive in saline environments, making it a hardy species for coastal landscapes.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release its seeds, contributing to its natural distribution.
