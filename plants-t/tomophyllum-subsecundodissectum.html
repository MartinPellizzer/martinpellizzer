Tomophyllum subsecundodissectum

Tomophyllum subsecundodissectum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tomato plant, is a species characterized by its finely dissected leaves that give it a feathery appearance.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades such as tomatoes and peppers. The plant typically has small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow in color. Its fruits are small, round berries that mature to a deep red color.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.
