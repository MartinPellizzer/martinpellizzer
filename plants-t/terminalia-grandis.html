Terminalia grandis

Terminalia grandis, commonly known as the African wild almond, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and opposite, lanceolate leaves. The tree produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by woody, egg-shaped pods containing edible seeds. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their astringent properties.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
