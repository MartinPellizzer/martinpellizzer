Taraxacum purpureomarginatum

Taraxacum purpureomarginatum, commonly known as the purple-margin dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of pale green, hairy leaves with purple margins. The plant produces a single, elongated flower stem topped with a yellow composite flower head. It is native to parts of Europe and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and its role in supporting pollinators.
