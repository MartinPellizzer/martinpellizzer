Tristachya pallida

Tristachya pallida, commonly known as the silverleaf panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray leaves and tufted inflorescences that resemble a panicle. The plant thrives in sandy or disturbed soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance.

Tristachya pallida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
