Trollius pumilus var. ranunculoides

Trollius pumilus var. ranunculoides, commonly known as the dwarf buttercup, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features delicate, bright yellow flowers with 8 to 12 petals, arranged in a cup-like shape, and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10-20 centimeters, with basal leaves that are deeply lobed and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to cold climates.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its cheerful appearance.
