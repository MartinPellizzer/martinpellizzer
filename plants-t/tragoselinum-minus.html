Tragoselinum minus

Tragoselinum minus, commonly known as lesser burnet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or reddish in color, blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a basal rosette, and the plant is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

Tragoselinum minus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in garden settings for its delicate appearance.
