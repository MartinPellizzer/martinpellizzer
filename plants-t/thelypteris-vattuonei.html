Thelypteris vattuonei

Thelypteris vattuonei, commonly known as the Vattuone holly fern, is a species of fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Thelypteridaceae and is characterized by its intricate, lace-like fronds that are typically divided into narrow, feather-like segments. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in forest understories where it can receive indirect sunlight. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it to moist, well-drained soil, while its spores are dispersed through the air, contributing to its reproductive strategy.

The botanical features of Thelypteris vattuonei make it an important component of the ecological diversity in its native habitat.
