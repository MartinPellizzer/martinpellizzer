Tetragonanthus gracilis

Tetragonanthus gracilis, commonly known as the slender tetragonanthus, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Orchidaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically greenish-yellow with a distinctive, narrow, four-angled calyx, giving it its scientific name.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and adaptability to certain indoor conditions.
