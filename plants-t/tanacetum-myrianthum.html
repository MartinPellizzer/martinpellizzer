Tanacetum myrianthum

Tanacetum myrianthum, commonly known as field chamomile, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features daisy-like yellow flowers with white ray florets, surrounded by a dense central disc of tiny florets. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm.

Its aromatic foliage and flowers contain compounds used in traditional medicine and as a mild sedative.
