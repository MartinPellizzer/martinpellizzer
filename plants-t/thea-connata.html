Thea connata

Thea connata, commonly known as the Mexican flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets, which are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. Its bark is smooth and grayish in color, becoming fissured with age.

Thea connata is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display and adaptability to various soil types.
