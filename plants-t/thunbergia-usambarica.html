Thunbergia usambarica

Thunbergia usambarica, commonly known as the Usambara morning glory, is a climbing vine native to East Africa.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight. Its rapid growth habit makes it suitable for trellises or as a ground cover in gardens.

Thunbergia usambarica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
