Trophis taxoides

Trophis taxoides, commonly known as the African olive, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It has a compact, rounded crown with dense, dark green foliage that provides shade. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by edible, fleshy berries. Its leaves are thick and leathery, adapted to conserve water in arid conditions.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
