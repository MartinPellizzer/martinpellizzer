Tropaeolum majus var. sordidum

Tropaeolum majus var. sordidum, commonly known as the sordid nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves and vibrant, spurred flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant is an annual herb that thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often growing in moist environments. Its edible leaves and seeds are used in culinary applications, while the flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
