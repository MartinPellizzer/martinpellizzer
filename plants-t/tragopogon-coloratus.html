Tragopogon coloratus

Tragopogon coloratus, commonly known as the showy goat's beard, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers with distinctive purple markings and a tall, slender stem. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a hairy texture, while its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

Tragopogon coloratus is often found in regions with a Mediterranean climate, where it thrives in full sun.
