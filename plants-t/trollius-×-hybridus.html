Trollius × hybridus

Trollius × hybridus, commonly known as the hybrid globeflower, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its showy, saucer-shaped yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of palmately divided leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of soft, silky hairs.

Trollius × hybridus is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
