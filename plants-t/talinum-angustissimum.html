Talinum angustissimum

Talinum angustissimum, commonly known as narrowleaf Malabar spinach, is a herbaceous annual plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its slender, alternate leaves that are typically lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its roots are edible and often used in traditional cuisines for their mild, slightly sweet flavor.

Talinum angustissimum is cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes due to its adaptability and nutritional value.
