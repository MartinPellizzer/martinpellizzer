Taraxacum catodontum

Taraxacum catodontum, commonly known as the cat's foot dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and basal rosette of leaves. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that develop into fluffy white seed heads, typical of the dandelion genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
