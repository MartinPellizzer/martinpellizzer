Thymus barrelieri var. longifolius

Thymus barrelieri var. longifolius, commonly known as the long-leaved thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features compact, low-growing stems that produce small, aromatic leaves with a distinctive grayish-green color. The plant's flowers are typically pink or lavender, forming dense clusters that bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its essential oils, rich in thymol, contribute to its use in culinary and medicinal applications.
