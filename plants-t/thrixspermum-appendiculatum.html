Thrixspermum appendiculatum

Thrixspermum appendiculatum, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is typically found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and agricultural fields.

Thrixspermum appendiculatum is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
