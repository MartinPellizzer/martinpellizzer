Tetragonanthus purdieanus

Tetragonanthus purdieanus, commonly known as the Cape chestnut, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its four-angled, dark green leaves and produces small, fragrant white flowers in spring. The plant is typically found in coastal fynbos vegetation, thriving in well-drained soils. It is not commonly cultivated outside its native range due to its specific environmental requirements.

The fruit is a capsule that releases small, winged seeds when mature.
