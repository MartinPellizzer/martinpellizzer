Tapinanthus

Tapinanthus, commonly known as the parasitic dodder, is a genus of flowering plants that exhibits unique botanical characteristics.

These plants are hemiparasitic, meaning they derive some nutrients from their host plants while also performing photosynthesis. Tapinanthus species typically have slender, thread-like stems that twine around their hosts, often forming dense networks. Their leaves are usually scale-like and reduced, and they produce small, inconspicuous flowers.

The genus includes several species, many of which are found in Africa and Asia, thriving in a variety of ecological conditions.
