Tragopogon porrifolius f. montgomeryi

Tragopogon porrifolius f. montgomeryi, commonly known as the Montgomerie's thistle, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and wind-pollinated structure. The plant features a robust, upright stem with basal leaves that are lanceolate and often hairy. Its flowers are typically yellow, forming a single terminal head with ray florets.

This variant is distinguished by its unique leaf morphology and habitat preference, often growing in rocky or disturbed soils.
