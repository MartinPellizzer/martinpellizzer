Tibouchina quadrata

Tibouchina quadrata, commonly known as the purple tibouchina, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its showy, violet-blue bracts that surround its small, white flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with dense, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Tibouchina quadrata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
