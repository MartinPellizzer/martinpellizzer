Thymelaea tarton-raira subvar. transiens

Thymelaea tarton-raira subvar. transiens, commonly known as the Transient Thyme, is a low-growing shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It features small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or pale purple, blooming in spring. The plant has aromatic, hairy leaves that are often used in traditional herbal remedies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Its unique floral structure and limited distribution make it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
