Thymus froelichianus

Thymus froelichianus, commonly known as Froelich's thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are fragrant and often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Thymus froelichianus thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and borders.
