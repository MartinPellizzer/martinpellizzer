Tripogandra brasiliensis

Tripogandra brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian arrowroot, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, spadix-like flowers surrounded by a spathe, typical of the arum family. Its tubers, which are edible, are often used as a starch source in traditional cuisines.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued both for its ornamental qualities and nutritional properties.
