Tridia frankenioides

Tridia frankenioides, commonly known as the narrowleaf tridia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and slender, linear leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats.

Tridia frankenioides is adapted to arid environments and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
