Taeniophyllum taenioides

Taeniophyllum taenioides, commonly known as the ribbon fern, is a small, epiphytic fern native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, linear fronds that resemble ribbons, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the air, and it thrives in high humidity and indirect light.

The leaves are usually dark green and have a smooth, glossy surface.
