Tapura bouquetiana

Tapura bouquetiana, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are velvety to the touch, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet, often blooming in the early morning. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Tapura bouquetiana is a climbing vine that can reach significant lengths, making it a popular ornamental plant in tropical gardens.
