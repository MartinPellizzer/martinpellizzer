Tulipa kolpakowskiana var. humilis

Tulipa kolpakowskiana var. humilis, commonly known as the dwarf Turkish tulip, is a species native to the high altitudes of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, cup-shaped flowers in shades of pink and red. The bulbs are typically small and require well-drained, sandy soil to thrive. This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in rock gardens and alpine settings.

It blooms in late spring, adding a splash of color to early-season gardens.
