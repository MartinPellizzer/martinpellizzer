Thunbergia papuana

Thunbergia papuana, commonly known as the Papuan morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands, particularly Papua New Guinea.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

Thunbergia papuana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
