Tephrosia argentea

Tephrosia argentea, commonly known as silver tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in a fine silvery or grayish pubescence. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters, and are borne on slender pedicels.

Tephrosia argentea is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens.
