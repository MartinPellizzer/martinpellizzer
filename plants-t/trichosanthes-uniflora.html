Trichosanthes uniflora

Trichosanthes uniflora, commonly known as the single-flower trumpet vine, is a climbing herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, yellow flowers that resemble a trumpet, often with a greenish throat. The plant produces elongated, orange-yellow fruits that contain numerous seeds. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are hairy and vine-like.

Trichosanthes uniflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
