Tryphostemma alatopetiolatum

Tryphostemma alatopetiolatum, commonly known as the winged petiole morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive winged petioles, which give it its common name, and heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist forest understories.

Its rapid growth habit makes it both a useful ornamental plant and a potential invasive species in certain areas.
