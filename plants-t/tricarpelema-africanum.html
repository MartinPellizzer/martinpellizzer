Tricarpelema africanum

Tricarpelema africanum, commonly known as African star grass, is a perennial herb native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with white to pale blue flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
