Trixis dioscoreifolia

Trixis dioscoreifolia, commonly known as the climbing morning glory, is a vigorous vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to climb using its twining stems. Its flowers open in the morning and close at dusk, hence the common name.

Trixis dioscoreifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
