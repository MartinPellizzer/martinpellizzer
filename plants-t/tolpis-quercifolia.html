Tolpis quercifolia

Tolpis quercifolia, commonly known as the oak-leaved Tolpis, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in dry, rocky habitats. The plant is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that resemble those of an oak tree, giving it its common name. It produces small, yellowish to brownish composite flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months.

Tolpis quercifolia is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
