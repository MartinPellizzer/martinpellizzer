Trichosanthes brevibracteata var. sublobata

Trichosanthes brevibracteata var. sublobata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bitter melon, is a variant of the bitter melon species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, pale green fruits that are typically harvested when immature for culinary use. The plant features compound leaves with serrated margins and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its vines are trailing and can grow up to several meters in length, often climbing supports.

The fruit contains numerous seeds and is known for its bitter taste, which is reduced when harvested young.
