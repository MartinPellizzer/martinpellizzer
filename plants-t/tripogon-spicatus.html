Tripogon spicatus

Tripogon spicatus, commonly known as the spikegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in grassland and woodland habitats, often forming dense tussocks. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a single spike of small, pale green to brownish flowers.

Tripogon spicatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage.
