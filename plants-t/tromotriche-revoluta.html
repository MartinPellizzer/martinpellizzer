Tromotriche revoluta

Tromotriche revoluta, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its velvety, hairy foliage that gives it its common name. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, with alternate, ovate leaves that are often pubescent. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually greenish-yellow in color, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Tromotriche revoluta is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and agricultural fields.
