Turraea sieberi

Turraea sieberi, commonly known as the African sandalwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Due to its aromatic wood, it has been historically valued for its use in traditional crafts and medicine.
