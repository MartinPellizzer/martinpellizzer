Trifolium dichroanthum var. cyprium

Trifolium dichroanthum var. cyprium, commonly known as the Cyprian clover, is a variant of the dichroanthum clover species.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological studies and traditional herbal uses.
