Tradescantia cymbispatha var. villosissima

Tradescantia cymbispatha var. villosissima, commonly known as the velvetleaf spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and swamps. It produces small, three-lobed seeds that are dispersed by wind.

The species is often found in wetland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
