Triopterys ovata var. jamaicensis

Triopterys ovata var. jamaicensis, commonly known as the Jamaican sweetleaf, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate, and elliptic-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that turns yellow when mature.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported medicinal properties.
