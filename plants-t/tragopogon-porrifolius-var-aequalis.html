Tragopogon porrifolius var. aequalis

Tragopogon porrifolius var. aequalis, commonly known as the common salsify, is a biennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its sturdy, upright stems and rosette of basal leaves that are typically hairy and lobed. The plant produces a yellow flower head that develops into a long, tubular seed stalk, which is the edible part often used in cooking. Its leaves are also edible and have a slightly sweet, nutty flavor.

This variety is cultivated for its nutritious and versatile culinary uses.
