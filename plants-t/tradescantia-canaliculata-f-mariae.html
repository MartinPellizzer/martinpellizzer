Tradescantia canaliculata f. mariae

Tradescantia canaliculata f. mariae, commonly known as the Marie's spiderwort, is a low-growing perennial herb native to South America.

It features striking, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with purple or red, adding ornamental value to gardens. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of blue, purple, or white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it ideal for woodland gardens.

Its foliage and floral display make it a popular choice for ground cover and container planting.
