Trichomanes chamaedrys

Trichomanes chamaedrys, commonly known as the creeping huperzia, is a species of fern-like plant belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves that are often clustered at the base of the plant. This plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Trichomanes chamaedrys has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread and form dense mats.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides microhabitat for various invertebrates.
