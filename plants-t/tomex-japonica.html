Tomex japonica

Tomex japonica, commonly known as Japanese fern, is a species of fern native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, bipinnate fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds in specialized structures called sorus.

Tomex japonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its graceful foliage.
