Tephrocactus glomeratus

Tephrocactus glomeratus, commonly known as the clustered rock cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in desert gardens.
