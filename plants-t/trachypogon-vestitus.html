Trachypogon vestitus

Trachypogon vestitus, commonly known as the naked grass, is a species of grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences, which are typical of the Poaceae family. Trachypogon vestitus typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
