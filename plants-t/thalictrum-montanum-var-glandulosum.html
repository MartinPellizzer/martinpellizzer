Thalictrum montanum var. glandulosum

Thalictrum montanum var. glandulosum, commonly known as the glandular meadow rue, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that bear alternate, palmately compound leaves with finely divided lobes. The plant produces showy, yellowish-green to white racemes of small, clustered flowers in early summer. Its flowers are typically pollinated by insects, and the plant thrives in moist, shaded woodland habitats.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and delicate floral display.
