Tahitia tahitensis

Tahitia tahitensis, commonly known as the Tahitian breadfruit, is a tropical plant native to the South Pacific.

It is a member of the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces a fruit that is typically harvested when it is still unripe and used in various culinary preparations. Its growth is typically in humid, coastal areas with well-drained soil.

Tahitia tahitensis plays an important role in the traditional diet and culture of the Tahitian people.
