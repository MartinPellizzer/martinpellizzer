Tetracoccus capensis

Tetracoccus capensis, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry, is a small, yellow-orange fruit native to South Africa.

It belongs to the genus Tetracoccus in the family Capparaceae. The plant is characterized by its compact shrub form and hairy leaves. The fruit is typically harvested when it turns a bright orange color, indicating maturity.

It is often used in culinary applications and has a sweet, tangy flavor.
