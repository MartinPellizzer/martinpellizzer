Tragacantha pallescens

Tragacantha pallescens, commonly known as the pale tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, spiny stems and small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces distinctive seed pods that contain numerous small, round seeds. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.

Tragacantha pallescens is valued for its historical use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural gum.
