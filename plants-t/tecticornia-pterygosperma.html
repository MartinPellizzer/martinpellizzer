Tecticornia pterygosperma

Tecticornia pterygosperma, commonly known as the coastal saltbush, is a perennial shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its small, fleshy leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in saline and alkaline soils, often found in coastal regions and salt lakes.

Its ecological importance includes stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
