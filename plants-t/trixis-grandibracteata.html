Trixis grandibracteata

Trixis grandibracteata, commonly known as the grandibracteate trixis, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of small, pale yellow to greenish-white flowers. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, adapted to reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It produces distinctive bracts that are larger than the flowers themselves, giving it its common name.

Trixis grandibracteata is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its resilience in dry conditions.
