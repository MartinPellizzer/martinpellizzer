Tylophora enervis

Tylophora enervis, commonly known as the slender tylophora, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its thin, twining stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by follicles containing elongated, ribbed seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and thickets.

Tylophora enervis is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
