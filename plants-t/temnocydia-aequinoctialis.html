Temnocydia aequinoctialis

Temnocydia aequinoctialis, commonly known as the equinox grass, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spikelet-based flowers that are typically greenish or brown in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands or near water sources.

Due to its unique flowering pattern, it is of interest to botanists studying seasonal plant behavior.
