Tylophora trichophylla

Tylophora trichophylla, commonly known as the hairy tylophora, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Apocynaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including India, China, and Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces. Its flowers are small, clustered in umbels, and typically have five petals, often in shades of white or pink.

Tylophora trichophylla is also noted for its milky sap, which is a common feature among plants in the Apocynaceae family.
