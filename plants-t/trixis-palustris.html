Trixis palustris

Trixis palustris, commonly known as marsh trixis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It typically grows in moist soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its small, clustered flowers are usually white or pale yellow, appearing in late spring to early summer.

Trixis palustris plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and provides habitat for various invertebrates.
