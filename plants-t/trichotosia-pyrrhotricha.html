Trichotosia pyrrhotricha

Trichotosia pyrrhotricha, commonly known as the red-haired grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and distinctive reddish-brown inflorescence. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and along riverbanks, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous texture, and it produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers.

Trichotosia pyrrhotricha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
