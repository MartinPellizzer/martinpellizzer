Trifolium argutum var. cruentum

Trifolium argutum var. cruentum, commonly known as the crimson clover, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets with serrated edges. The plant produces small, pink to red flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in agricultural settings for soil enrichment and as a forage crop.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
