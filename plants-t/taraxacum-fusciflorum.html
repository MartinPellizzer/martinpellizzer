Taraxacum fusciflorum

Taraxacum fusciflorum, commonly known as the brownflower dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves and a flowering stem that bears a single, yellow, composite flower head. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in grassy areas and disturbed soils. Its flowers produce achene seeds with pappus, which are dispersed by the wind.

Taraxacum fusciflorum is typically perennial and has a taproot system that allows it to thrive in various environmental conditions.
