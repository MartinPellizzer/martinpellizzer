Tillandsia patriae

Tillandsia patriae, commonly known as the Ecuadorian air plant, is a species of bromeliad native to the Andes Mountains of Ecuador.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on the surface of other plants without deriving nutrients from them. Its leaves are typically long and narrow, with a dense covering of trichomes that help absorb moisture from the air. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with colorful, bell-shaped flowers.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and plant enthusiasts alike.
