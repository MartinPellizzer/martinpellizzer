Triticum nodosum

Triticum nodosum, commonly known as nodular wheat, is a wild relative of wheat that is characterized by its unique nodular structures on its stems.

This plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in temperate climates. Its botanical features include slender, often branched stems and small, greenish-white flowers. Triticum nodosum is notable for its genetic diversity, which makes it a valuable resource in breeding programs for improving wheat varieties.

It also serves as a host for certain beneficial microorganisms that contribute to soil health.
