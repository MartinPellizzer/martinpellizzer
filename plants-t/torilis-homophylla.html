Torilis homophylla

Torilis homophylla, commonly known as the common groundsel, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Torilis homophylla produces small yellow flowers that develop into prickly, spherical fruits, which are dispersed by wind.
