Thymus sibthorpii var. hirtiformis

Thymus sibthorpii var. hirtiformis, commonly known as the woolly thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It features dense, fuzzy leaves covered in fine hairs, which give it a distinctive, woolly appearance. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its aromatic foliage is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
