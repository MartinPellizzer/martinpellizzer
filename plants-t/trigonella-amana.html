Trigonella amana

Trigonella amana, commonly known as the lesser cowpea, is an annual legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. Botanically, it belongs to the Fabaceae family and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Its growth habit is typically prostrate or ascending, making it suitable for ground cover in certain agricultural settings.
