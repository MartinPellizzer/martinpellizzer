Tragacantha grammocalyx

Tragacantha grammocalyx, commonly known as the Persian violet, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, violet-colored flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces a distinctive, sticky resin known as tragacanth, which has been historically used in medicine and as a thickening agent. Tragacantha grammocalyx typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in temperate climates.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique chemical composition and historical applications.
