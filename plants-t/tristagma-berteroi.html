Tristagma berteroi

Tristagma berteroi, commonly known as the desert broom.

It is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, characterized by its small, yellowish-green flowers and narrow, linear leaves. The plant is adapted to arid environments, featuring deep roots and a low-growing habit to conserve moisture. Its flowers are typically arranged in clusters and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Tristagma berteroi plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and provides habitat for various desert insects and birds.
