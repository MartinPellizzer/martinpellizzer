Typha gigantea

Typha gigantea, commonly known as the giant cattail, is a tall, herbaceous wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical flower spikes that are typically brown or yellowish in color. The plant has long, narrow leaves that grow from a rhizomatous root system, allowing it to spread vegetatively. It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often forming dense colonies in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Typha gigantea plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
