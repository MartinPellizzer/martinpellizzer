Tarchonanthus parvicapitulatus

Tarchonanthus parvicapitulatus, commonly known as the small-headed eringa, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers that appear in compact clusters, typically during the summer months. The plant has aromatic, glandular leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of interest in horticultural studies.
