Typha valentinii

Typha valentinii, commonly known as the Valentine cattail, is a perennial wetland plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical flower spikes that produce fluffy seeds for wind dispersal. The plant has long, narrow leaves that grow in dense clusters around a central stem. It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Typha valentinii plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
