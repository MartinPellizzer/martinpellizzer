Terminalia brosigiana

Terminalia brosigiana, commonly known as the African snowball tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and large, palmately lobed leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The tree produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by round, fleshy fruits. Its botanical significance lies in its ecological role and medicinal properties.

It is also valued for its ornamental appearance and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
