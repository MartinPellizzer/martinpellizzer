Tsuga douglasii var. fastigiata

Tsuga douglasii var. fastigiata, commonly known as the Douglas fir narrowleaf form, is a variant of the Douglas fir native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, columnar growth habit and dense foliage, making it a striking ornamental tree. The leaves are flat, needle-like, and typically dark green, with a slightly bluish tinge. It produces cones that are usually reddish-brown and contain edible seeds.

This variety is often cultivated in gardens for its unique shape and hardiness in cooler climates.
