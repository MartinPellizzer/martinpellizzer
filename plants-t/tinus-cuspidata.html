Tinus cuspidata

Tinus cuspidata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed tamarisk, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, needle-like leaves that are typically covered in a fine, white coating. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, often in late spring or early summer. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species that thrives in well-drained soils.

Tinus cuspidata is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
