Trichosalpinx strumifera

Trichosalpinx strumifera, commonly known as the red spider orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking red and yellow flowers that resemble spiders, hence its common name. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial herb with a short, stocky stem and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are borne on a single, erect scape and are often visited by specific pollinators such as bees and wasps.

Trichosalpinx strumifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
