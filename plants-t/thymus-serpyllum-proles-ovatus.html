Thymus serpyllum proles ovatus

Thymus serpyllum proles ovatus, commonly known as narrow-leaved thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces tiny, pinkish-white flowers in clusters, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Its aromatic leaves are often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
