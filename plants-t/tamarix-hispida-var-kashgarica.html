Tamarix hispida var. kashgarica

Tamarix hispida var. kashgarica, commonly known as the Kashgar tamarisk, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, feathery foliage and small, scale-like leaves that reduce water loss in dry environments. The plant produces fluffy, pink to reddish flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. It has a deep root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion in sandy or saline soils.

This species is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in harsh, drought-prone conditions.
