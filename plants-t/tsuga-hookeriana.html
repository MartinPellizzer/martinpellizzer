Tsuga hookeriana

Tsuga hookeriana, commonly known as Hooker's fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China.

It belongs to the genus Tsuga, which includes other firs such as the noble fir and the balsam fir. The tree is characterized by its conical shape, needle-like leaves, and distinctive cone structure. It typically grows in cool, moist climates at high elevations.

The botanical features of Tsuga hookeriana make it an important species in its native ecosystem and of interest to botanists and conservationists.
