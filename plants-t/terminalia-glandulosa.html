Terminalia glandulosa

Terminalia glandulosa, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, often greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It is valued for its hard, heavy wood, which is used in furniture and carving, and for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in local ecosystems.
