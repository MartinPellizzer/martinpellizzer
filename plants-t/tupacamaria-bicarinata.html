Tupacamaria bicarinata

Tupacamaria bicarinata, commonly known as the twin-ribbed cactus, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, ribbed stems that are typically green or blue-green in color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warm season, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It is adapted to survive in dry environments with minimal water, storing moisture in its fleshy stems.

Tupacamaria bicarinata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
