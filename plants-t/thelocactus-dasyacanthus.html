Thelocactus dasyacanthus

Thelocactus dasyacanthus, commonly known as the giant cardoon, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched stem and large, paddle-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of white, waxy hair. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking visual contrast to its otherwise desolate appearance. Its thick, fleshy stems are adapted to store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by cactus enthusiasts.
