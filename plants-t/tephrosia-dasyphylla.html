Tephrosia dasyphylla

Tephrosia dasyphylla, commonly known as the hairy tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height, featuring compound leaves with numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom during the wet season. Its root system is known for containing compounds with insecticidal properties, making it valuable in traditional agriculture.

Tephrosia dasyphylla is also recognized for its role in soil enrichment and as a forage plant for livestock.
