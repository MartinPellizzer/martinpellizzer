Trichomanes crispum f. remotum

Trichomanes crispum f. remotum, commonly known as the remote harts-tongue fern, is a fern species belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are often curled or crisped at the edges. This fern thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its small, spore-producing structures are located on the underside of the fronds.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
