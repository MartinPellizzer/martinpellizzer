Tysonia africana

Tysonia africana, commonly known as the African senna, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are adapted for dispersal by wind or animals.

Tysonia africana is also valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
