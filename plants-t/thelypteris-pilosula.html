Thelypteris pilosula

Thelypteris pilosula, commonly known as the silky maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery fronds that are covered in soft, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are often covered in a dense layer of brown, woolly tissue, which helps in water retention.

Thelypteris pilosula is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant, delicate foliage.
