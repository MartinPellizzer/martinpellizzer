Trillium brevipes

Trillium brevipes, commonly known as the short-stemmed trillium, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, wooded habitats, often in rich, acidic soils. The plant has three broadly ovate leaves that are arranged in a whorl at the base of the stem. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with three petals and three sepals, and it blooms in early spring.

Trillium brevipes is a member of the Trillium genus, known for its showy, three-part floral structure.
