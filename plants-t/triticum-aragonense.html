Triticum aragonense

Triticum aragonense, commonly known as Aragon wheat, is a wild species of wheat native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its slender stems and spike-like inflorescence. The plant produces small, hard grains that are typically yellowish in color. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates.

Due to its genetic diversity, it is of interest for breeding programs aimed at improving modern wheat varieties.
