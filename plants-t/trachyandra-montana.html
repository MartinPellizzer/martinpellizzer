Trachyandra montana

Trachyandra montana, commonly known as the mountain trachyandra, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asphodelaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming growth pattern. The plant features thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a layer of fine, white hairs, which help reduce water loss. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
