Tephroseris papposa subsp. heuffelii

Tephroseris papposa subsp.

heuffelii, commonly known as the Heuffel's tephroseris, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy yellow flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its unique inflorescence structure and the presence of pappus hairs on its seeds. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
