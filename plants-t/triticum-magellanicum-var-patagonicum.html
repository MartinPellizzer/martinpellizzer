Triticum magellanicum var. patagonicum

Triticum magellanicum var. patagonicum, commonly known as the Patagonian wheat, is a wild grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and is adapted to cold, temperate climates. Its leaves are narrow and have a bluish-green hue, and its seeds are small and hardy.

This variety is of interest to botanists and agricultural researchers due to its potential for genetic diversity and adaptability.
