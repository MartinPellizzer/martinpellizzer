Trifolium procumbens var. lupulinum

Trifolium procumbens var. lupulinum, commonly known as the yellow clover, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically yellowish-green in color, and its small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round, yellowish seed pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed areas.

Trifolium procumbens var. lupulinum is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
