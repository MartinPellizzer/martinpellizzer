Taraxacum licentii

Taraxacum licentii, commonly known as the Licentii dandelion, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant produces a yellow flower head that develops into a ball of white, fluffy seeds, typical of the dandelion genus. It has a rosette of basal leaves that are usually hairy and lance-shaped.

Taraxacum licentii is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.
