Tamarix pallasii

Tamarix pallasii, commonly known as the desert willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its slender, branching structure and its ability to thrive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that range in color from pink to pale purple, typically blooming in the spring. Its leaves are scale-like and often shed during periods of drought, reducing water loss.

Tamarix pallasii is also known for its rapid growth and adaptability, making it a common choice for reclamation projects in degraded soils.
