Thymus camaresiensis

Thymus camaresiensis, commonly known as the Armenian thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pale purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are fragrant and contain essential oils, making it useful in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

Thymus camaresiensis thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or alpine environments.
