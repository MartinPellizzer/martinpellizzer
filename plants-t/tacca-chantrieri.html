Tacca chantrieri

Tacca chantrieri, commonly known as the "bat plant" or "devil's ivy," is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, waxy leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length, often with a dark green to almost black coloration. The plant produces large, intricate inflorescences that resemble bat wings, which are typically purple or violet in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistent moisture and high humidity to flourish.

Due to its unique appearance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical regions.
