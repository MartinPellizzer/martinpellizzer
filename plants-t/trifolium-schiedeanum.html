Trifolium schiedeanum

Trifolium schiedeanum, commonly known as the Schiedea clover, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in high-altitude environments and is adapted to cool, moist conditions.

Trifolium schiedeanum is often found in rocky or alpine habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
