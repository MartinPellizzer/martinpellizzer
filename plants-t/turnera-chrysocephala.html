Turnera chrysocephala

Turnera chrysocephala, commonly known as golden crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Turneraceae and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has palmate leaves with a distinctive yellowish-green color, and it often grows in tropical and subtropical regions. It is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant appearance.
