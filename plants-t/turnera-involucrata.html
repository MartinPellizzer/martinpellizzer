Turnera involucrata

Turnera involucrata, commonly known as the golden trumpet or yellow turnera, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. The plant features showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually alternate, simple, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
