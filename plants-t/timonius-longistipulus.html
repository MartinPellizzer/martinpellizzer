Timonius longistipulus

Timonius longistipulus, commonly known as the long-stemmed timonius, is a unique member of the Timoniaceae family.

It is characterized by its elongated, slender stems and clusters of small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest undergrowth or along stream banks. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a smooth, waxy surface.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
