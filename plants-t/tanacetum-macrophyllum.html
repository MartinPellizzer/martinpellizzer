Tanacetum macrophyllum

Tanacetum macrophyllum, commonly known as the meadow garlic or large-leaf tansy, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and bright yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its strong, often aromatic scent.

Tanacetum macrophyllum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
