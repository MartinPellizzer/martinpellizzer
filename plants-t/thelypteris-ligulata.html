Thelypteris ligulata

Thelypteris ligulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Thelypteridaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its fertile fronds, which are clustered in sori. Thelypteris ligulata is native to tropical and subtropical regions, including parts of Asia, Africa, and the Americas.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests or along stream banks.
