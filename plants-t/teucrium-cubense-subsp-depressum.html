Teucrium cubense subsp. depressum

Teucrium cubense subsp.

depressum, commonly known as the Cuban germander, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Caribbean. It typically features small, lance-shaped leaves with a grayish-blue hue, often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of pale blue to violet flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Its botanical structure reflects adaptations to its native environment, making it a resilient and ornamental species.
