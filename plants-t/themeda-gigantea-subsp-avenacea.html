Themeda gigantea subsp. avenacea

Themeda gigantea subsp.

avenacea, commonly known as the tall grass bluestem, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, blue-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spike-like clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
