Thalictrum parisiense

Thalictrum parisiense, commonly known as meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Ranunculaceae and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces lightweight seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread.
