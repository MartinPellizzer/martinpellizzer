Taraxacum pseudocastaneum

Taraxacum pseudocastaneum, commonly known as the Chinese dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to East Asia and is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers and lobed leaves. The plant produces a hollow, hairy stem and has a taproot system that allows it to thrive in a variety of soil types. It is often found in meadows, roadsides, and disturbed areas, and it plays a role in supporting pollinators.

The species is also noted for its ability to tolerate poor soil conditions and its use in traditional medicine.
