Tanacetum bocconei

Tanacetum bocconei, commonly known as the Boccone's tansy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features finely divided, silvery-green leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Tanacetum bocconei is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
