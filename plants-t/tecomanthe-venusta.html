Tecomanthe venusta

Tecomanthe venusta, commonly known as the golden shower vine, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, hanging clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with smooth margins and a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Tecomanthe venusta is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
