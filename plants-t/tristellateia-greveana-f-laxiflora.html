Tristellateia greveana f. laxiflora

Tristellateia greveana f. laxiflora, commonly known as the laxiflora form of the genus Tristellateia, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit with dense foliage and small, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically features narrow, linear leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to white, with a central disk surrounded by ray florets.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in rock gardens and as a decorative plant in containers.
