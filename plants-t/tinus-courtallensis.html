Tinus courtallensis

Tinus courtallensis, commonly known as the Malabar chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the family Anacardiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, chestnut-like fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
