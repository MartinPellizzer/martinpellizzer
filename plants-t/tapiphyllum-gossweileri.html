Tapiphyllum gossweileri

Tapiphyllum gossweileri, commonly known as the African snowball, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. The plant produces large, spherical clusters of small white flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and has a compact growth habit.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and used in floral arrangements.
