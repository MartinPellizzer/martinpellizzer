Triticum peregrinum

Triticum peregrinum, commonly known as the wandering wheat, is a species of wild wheat native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its slender stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces awned grains that are typically dark brown in color. Triticum peregrinum is adapted to grow in dry, rocky soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

It is of interest to botanists and researchers due to its genetic potential for agricultural improvement.
