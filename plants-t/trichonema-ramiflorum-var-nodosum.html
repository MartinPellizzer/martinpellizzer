Trichonema ramiflorum var. nodosum

Trichonema ramiflorum var. nodosum, commonly known as the nodding trichonema, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique inflorescence, where the flowers are arranged in a dense, branching pattern. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The flowers are small and usually white or pale green, contributing to its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
