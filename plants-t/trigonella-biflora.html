Trigonella biflora

Trigonella biflora, commonly known as the two-flowered fenugreek, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. It produces pods that contain several seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some cuisines.

Trigonella biflora is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility.
