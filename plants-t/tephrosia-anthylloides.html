Tephrosia anthylloides

Tephrosia anthylloides, commonly known as the African senna, is a leguminous shrub native to tropical Africa.

It features compound leaves with alternating pairs of small, elliptical leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in dense, terminal clusters, which are followed by elongated, pod-like fruits. It is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in agricultural settings.

The plant also contains compounds used in traditional medicine and as a natural insecticide.
