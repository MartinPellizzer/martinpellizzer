Thymus buschianus

Thymus buschianus, commonly known as the Balkan thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy flowers. The plant produces small, pink to lavender flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage is used in culinary and medicinal applications due to its pungent, aromatic properties.

Thymus buschianus thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
