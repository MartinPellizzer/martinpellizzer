Taraxacum neosivaschicum

Taraxacum neosivaschicum, commonly known as the Sivaschik dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its rosette of spoon-shaped, hairy leaves and a yellow composite flower head that develops into a pappus-equipped seed sphere. Native to Central Asia, this species thrives in dry, open habitats such as steppes and rocky soils. It produces numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds, which contribute to its wide distribution.

The plant is notable for its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions and its role in local ecosystems.
