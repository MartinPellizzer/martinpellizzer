Trifolium roemerianum

Trifolium roemerianum, commonly known as the Brazilian clover, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Trifolium roemerianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
