Trichodrymonia ulei

Trichodrymonia ulei, commonly known as the desert fan palm, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into clustered, edible fruits. Trichodrymonia ulei is well adapted to harsh, dry environments and can thrive with minimal water.

Its unique morphology makes it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
