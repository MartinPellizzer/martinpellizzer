Taxodium tristichum

Taxodium tristichum, commonly known as the pond cypress, is a deciduous conifer native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, columnar shape and its ability to thrive in wetland environments. The plant produces small, spherical cones and has scale-like leaves that turn a striking orange-red in the fall. Its root system is adapted to waterlogged soils, making it a valuable species for wetland restoration.

The tree is also notable for its ability to tolerate periodic flooding, which is a key feature of its ecological niche.
