Trichosanthes ascendens

Trichosanthes ascendens, commonly known as the climbing bitter melon, is a vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes cucumbers, squash, and gourds. The plant is characterized by its tendrils, heart-shaped leaves, and yellow flowers that give way to elongated, bitter fruits. Its fruits are often used in traditional medicine and as a vegetable in some cuisines.

The plant's botanical features make it well adapted to climbing and thriving in tropical climates.
