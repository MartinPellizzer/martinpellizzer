Thymus biserratus

Thymus biserratus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and pink to purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders. Its leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its distinctive fragrance and medicinal properties.

Thymus biserratus is often used in culinary applications and traditional herbal remedies.
