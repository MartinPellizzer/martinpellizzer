Trifolium campestre var. lagrangei

Trifolium campestre var. lagrangei, commonly known as the Lagrange clover, is a variant of the common clover species native to North America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, typically consisting of three heart-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a vibrant touch to meadows and grasslands. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as prairies and woodland edges.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
