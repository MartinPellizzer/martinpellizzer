Tilia argentea var. virescens

Tilia argentea var. virescens, commonly known as the silver-leaf lime or green-leaf linden, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are silver-white on the underside and dark green on top. The tree produces small, fragrant, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are clustered in drooping inflorescences. Its bark is smooth and light gray, becoming fissured with age.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
