Thymus serpyllum var. asiaticus

Thymus serpyllum var. asiaticus, commonly known as Asiatic thrift, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features dense clusters of tiny, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has aromatic, narrow leaves that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and long blooming period make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
