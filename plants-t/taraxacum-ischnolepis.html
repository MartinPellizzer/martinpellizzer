Taraxacum ischnolepis

Taraxacum ischnolepis, commonly known as the narrowleaf dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and a yellow flower head that develops into a fluffy seed sphere. Native to the mountainous regions of South America, it thrives in cool, moist environments. The plant is perennial and has a taproot system, which allows it to survive in harsh conditions.

Its flowers are typically visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.
