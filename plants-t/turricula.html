Turricula

Turricula, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, spiky blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and saline soils, showing a high tolerance for salt and wind. Its leaves are often covered in a fine, powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss.

Turricula is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
