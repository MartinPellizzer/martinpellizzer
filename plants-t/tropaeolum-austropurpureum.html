Tropaeolum austropurpureum

Tropaeolum austropurpureum, commonly known as the purple-flowered nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, purple-pink flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant is a member of the Tropaeolaceae family and is known for its edible leaves and seeds. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to garden biodiversity.
