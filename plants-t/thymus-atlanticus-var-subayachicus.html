Thymus atlanticus var. subayachicus

Thymus atlanticus var. subayachicus, commonly known as the Atlantic thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the coastal regions of the Atlantic coast.

It features small, compact foliage with gray-green leaves that are aromatic and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in maritime climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in culinary and medicinal applications.
