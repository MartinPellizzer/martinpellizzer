Tephrosia albissima subsp. zuluensis

Tephrosia albissima subsp.

zuluensis, commonly known as the Zulu senna, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and forest margins.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some indigenous cultures.
