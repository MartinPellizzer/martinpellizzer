Trifolium scutatum

Trifolium scutatum, commonly known as the shield clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Europe and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are typically arranged in clusters. Trifolium scutatum is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.

It is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
