Terebinthus karwinskii

Terebinthus karwinskii, commonly known as the Karwinski's terebinth, is a rare and ancient plant species native to the Caucasus region.

It is a deciduous tree or shrub characterized by its rough, fissured bark and small, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by woody, spherical fruits containing several seeds. It is adapted to dry, rocky environments and is considered a relic species with limited distribution.
