Tripogandra floribunda

Tripogandra floribunda, commonly known as the golden star lily, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear clusters of bright yellow flowers resembling stars. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Tripogandra floribunda is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and vibrant color.
