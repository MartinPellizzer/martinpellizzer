Tragium glaucum

Tragium glaucum, commonly known as the blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, whitish bloom that gives it a powdery appearance.

Tragium glaucum produces small blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
