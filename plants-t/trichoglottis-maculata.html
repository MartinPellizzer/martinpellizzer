Trichoglottis maculata

Trichoglottis maculata, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a pseudobulb and produces showy, fragrant flowers with a distinctive spotted pattern on the petals. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments and requires well-drained soil. Its leaves are usually broad and grass-like, and it blooms during the rainy season.

Trichoglottis maculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
