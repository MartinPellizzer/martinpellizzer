Taraxacum platycarpum subsp. maruyamanum

Taraxacum platycarpum subsp.

maruyamanum, commonly known as the Maruyama dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and a distinctive yellow flower head composed of numerous ray florets.

The plant produces a hollow, hairy stem that supports the flower and later the elongated seed head. It is native to Japan and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
