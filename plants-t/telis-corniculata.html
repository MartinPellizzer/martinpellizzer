Telis corniculata

Telis corniculata, commonly known as the horned poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, horn-like seed pods that give it its common name. The plant produces showy, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in the summer months. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or dry habitats.

Telis corniculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
