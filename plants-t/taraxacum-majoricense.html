Taraxacum majoricense

Taraxacum majoricense, commonly known as the Mexican dandelion, is a flowering plant native to central Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant features a rosette of basal, spoon-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowering stems rise from the center, bearing yellow ray flowers surrounded by tubular florets.

The species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in disturbed soils and open habitats.
