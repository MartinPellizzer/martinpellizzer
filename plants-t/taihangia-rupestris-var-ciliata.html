Taihangia rupestris var. ciliata

Taihangia rupestris var. ciliata, commonly known as the hairy taihangia, is a flowering plant native to China.

It is characterized by its distinctive hairy leaves and clusters of small, pale pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly habitats, preferring well-drained soils. It is often found in mountainous regions and can thrive in full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
