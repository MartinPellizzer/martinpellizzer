Trigonia thyrsifera

Trigonia thyrsifera, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of showy, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of purple, blue, and white. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a waxy texture, while its flowers are borne in terminal spikes.

Trigonia thyrsifera is valued for its ornamental beauty and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
