Teucrium polium var. algarbiense

Teucrium polium var. algarbiense, commonly known as the Algarbiense germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and small, typically blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or dry garden environments. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, sharing traits with other members such as mint and sage.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
