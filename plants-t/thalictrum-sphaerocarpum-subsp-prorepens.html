Thalictrum sphaerocarpum subsp. prorepens

Thalictrum sphaerocarpum subsp.

prorepens, commonly known as the creeping meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia. It features delicate, fern-like leaves that are typically basal and arranged in a rosette.

The plant produces small, clustered yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
