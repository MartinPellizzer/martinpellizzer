Trisetaria spicata subsp. pilosiglumis

Trisetaria spicata subsp.

pilosiglumis, commonly known as the silky spikegrass, is a perennial grass species native to certain regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and dense, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, pale green to brownish florets.

The leaves are narrow, flat, and covered with fine, silky hairs, giving the plant its distinctive appearance. It typically grows in open grasslands and rocky areas, often in semi-arid environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its attractive foliage and drought tolerance.
