Taraxacum siphonanthum

Taraxacum siphonanthum, commonly known as the Chinese dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to eastern Asia, particularly China, and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and a hollow, hairy stem. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that develop into fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Taraxacum siphonanthum is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
