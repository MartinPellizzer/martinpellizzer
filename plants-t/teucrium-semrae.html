Teucrium semrae

Teucrium semrae, commonly known as the Armenian germander, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant typically features square stems and opposite leaves, often covered in fine hairs. Its small, tubular flowers are usually violet or blue, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Teucrium semrae is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
