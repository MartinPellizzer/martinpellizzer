Taraxacum nudiscaposum

Taraxacum nudiscaposum, commonly known as the naked-sepal dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and a single, elongated stem that bears a flower head composed of yellow ray florets. The plant produces a distinctive, hollow stem that is covered with fine hairs, and its flowers develop into a spherical, white pappus that aids in wind dispersal. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

This species is notable for its simple morphology and ecological adaptability.
