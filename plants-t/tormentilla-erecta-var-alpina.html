Tormentilla erecta var. alpina

Tormentilla erecta var. alpina, commonly known as alpine tormentil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant features hirsute stems and leaves that are divided into lobes, typical of the Rosaceae family. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous environments.

Alpinetormentil is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
