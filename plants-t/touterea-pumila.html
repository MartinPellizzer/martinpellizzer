Touterea pumila

Touterea pumila, commonly known as the dwarf tree fern, is a species native to the tropical regions of the Pacific, including parts of Australia and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright trunk and a crown of feathery fronds that give it a delicate, fern-like appearance. The plant grows slowly and can reach a height of up to 2 meters, making it suitable for smaller garden spaces. Its rhizome is fibrous and can be used in traditional practices, while its fronds provide shade and ornamental value.

Overall, Touterea pumila is a unique and relatively low-maintenance plant that thrives in humid, shaded environments.
