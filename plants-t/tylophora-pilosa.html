Tylophora pilosa

Tylophora pilosa, commonly known as the hairy tylophora, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a small, fleshy capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

Tylophora pilosa is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and gardens.
