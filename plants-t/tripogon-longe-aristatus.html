Tripogon longe-aristatus

Tripogon longe-aristatus, commonly known as the long-awned grass.

It is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa, characterized by its tall, slender stalks and distinctive long awns on its spikelets. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and along riverbanks, thriving in well-drained soils. Its inflorescence consists of a single, elongated spike with closely arranged spikelets.

The botanical features of Tripogon longe-aristatus make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of study in grassland ecology.
