Theophrasta tetramera

Theophrasta tetramera, commonly known as the four-leafed thyme, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are fragrant and have been used historically in culinary and medicinal applications.

Theophrasta tetramera is also noted for its ability to thrive in dry, rocky soils.
