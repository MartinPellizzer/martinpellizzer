Tripogon paramjitianus

Tripogon paramjitianus, commonly known as the Paramjit's sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, thriving in humid, nutrient-rich soils. It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including India and Nepal.
