Thismia singeri

Thismia singeri, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a unique plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Thismiaceae family and is notable for its lack of chlorophyll, which means it cannot perform photosynthesis. Instead, it relies on a symbiotic relationship with specific fungi to obtain nutrients. The plant produces small, white, flower-like structures that resemble orchids, though it is not a true orchid.

Due to its rarity and specialized habitat requirements, Thismia singeri is considered endangered and is often found in humid, shaded environments.
