Trifolium glabrum

Trifolium glabrum, commonly known as slender clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. It is typically found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils across temperate regions.

Trifolium glabrum is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in ecological and agricultural contexts.
