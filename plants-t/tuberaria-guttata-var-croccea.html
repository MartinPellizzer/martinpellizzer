Tuberaria guttata var. croccea

Tuberaria guttata var. croccea, commonly known as the crocus-tubered bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive tuberous roots and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it blooms in late spring to early summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
