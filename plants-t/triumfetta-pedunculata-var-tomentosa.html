Triumfetta pedunculata var. tomentosa

Triumfetta pedunculata var. tomentosa, commonly known as the hairy triumfetta, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its small, white to pale pink flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical, with a hairy texture that gives the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

The plant is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil enrichment.
