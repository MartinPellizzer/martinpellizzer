Thalictrum bracteatum

Thalictrum bracteatum, commonly known as the bracteate meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Asia and North America.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers in early summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a silky, white pubescence. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Thalictrum bracteatum is valued in gardens for its ornamental foliage and delicate floral display.
