Triplaris corylifolia var. obtusa

Triplaris corylifolia var. obtusa, commonly known as the broad-leafed triplaris, is a variant of the triplaris genus native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by woody, spiny fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in tropical botany and ecological research.
