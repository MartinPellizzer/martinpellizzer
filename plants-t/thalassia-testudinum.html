Thalassia testudinum

Thalassia testudinum, commonly known as shoal grass, is a seagrass species that thrives in shallow, tropical and subtropical coastal waters.

It forms dense underwater meadows that provide essential habitat for a variety of marine life. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are typically green and can grow up to several meters in length. It plays a crucial role in stabilizing sediments and improving water quality through its extensive root system.

Thalassia testudinum is also important for carbon sequestration and supports fisheries by offering shelter and breeding grounds for marine organisms.
