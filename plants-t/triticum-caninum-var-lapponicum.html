Triticum caninum var. lapponicum

Triticum caninum var. lapponicum, commonly known as Lapland wild wheat, is a perennial grass species native to Arctic and alpine regions.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brown florets. It is adapted to cold climates and can thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

Its botanical features make it an important component of high-altitude and tundra ecosystems.
