Teucrium argutum var. incisum

Teucrium argutum var. incisum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved germander, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are lance-shaped with finely toothed margins. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
