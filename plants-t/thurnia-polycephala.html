Thurnia polycephala

Thurnia polycephala, commonly known as the coral tree, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, coral-like clusters of bright red or orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a woody stem with a distinctive crown of branches, and its leaves are small, narrow, and arranged in a spiral pattern. Thurnia polycephala thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its unique appearance and vibrant blooms make it a standout in both natural and cultivated landscapes.
