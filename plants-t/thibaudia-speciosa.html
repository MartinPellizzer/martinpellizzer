Thibaudia speciosa

Thibaudia speciosa, commonly known as the African lily, is a striking herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features tall, erect stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, adorned with large, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. The flowers are typically borne in clusters at the top of the stem, with six to eight petals that are often recurved. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, providing a textured contrast to the vibrant blooms.

This plant is well-suited for gardens and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
