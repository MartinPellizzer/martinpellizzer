Theophrasta fusca

Theophrasta fusca, commonly known as the black nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are toxic to humans and animals. Its leaves are ovate with a rough texture, and it often grows in disturbed soils or along roadsides.
