Tolpis laciniata

Tolpis laciniata, commonly known as the African featherfoil, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often finely toothed or lobed. It produces small, white or pale blue flowers arranged in a dense cluster at the ends of the stems.

Tolpis laciniata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage.
