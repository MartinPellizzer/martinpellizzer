Thelypodium lasiophyllum var. inalienum

Thelypodium lasiophyllum var. inalienum, commonly known as the silverleaf thelypodium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a compact growth habit with hairy, silver-gray leaves that are often covered in fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cooler, high-altitude environments.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its distinctive foliage and delicate floral display.
