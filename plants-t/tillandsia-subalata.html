Tillandsia subalata

Tillandsia subalata, commonly known as the silver star bromeliad, is a species of air plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. The plant's foliage is typically covered in a dense layer of fine, silver-white trichomes, which help it absorb moisture and nutrients from the air. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks in cloud forests.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
