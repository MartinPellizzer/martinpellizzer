Tulista aristata

Tulista aristata, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of gray-green, hairy leaves that help reduce water loss. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind. Tulista aristata is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water.

Its botanical features make it an important component of desert ecosystems.
