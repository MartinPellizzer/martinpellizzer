Taraxacum schmidianum

Taraxacum schmidianum, commonly known as the Schmid's dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces a single, hollow, stem that terminates in a spherical, yellow flower head composed of numerous tiny florets. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
