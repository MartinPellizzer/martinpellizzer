Tsuga americana

Tsuga americana, commonly known as the hemlock, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its pyramidal shape and dense, dark green foliage that is arranged in flat sprays. The needles are short, flat, and have two white stomatal bands on their upper surface. It produces small, spherical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
