Trapa natans var. quadrispinosa

Trapa natans var. quadrispinosa, commonly known as the spiked water chestnut, is a aquatic plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its floating leaves and distinctive spiny fruits that mature underwater. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its roots are adapted to thrive in shallow, still water bodies.

This variant is often found in ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams.
