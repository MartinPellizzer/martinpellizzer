Tamarix passerinoides

Tamarix passerinoides, commonly known as the silky tamarisk, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its feathery, gray-green foliage and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has small, scale-like leaves that help reduce water loss, making it well adapted to arid environments. It produces woody pods that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Tamarix passerinoides is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
