Turnera paradoxa

Turnera paradoxa, commonly known as the "Paradox Turnera," is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the family Turneraceae and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers and palmate leaves. The plant is often found in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its flowers bloom in spring and are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects.

Turnera paradoxa is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
