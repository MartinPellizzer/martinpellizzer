Toxicodendron dentatum

Toxicodendron dentatum, commonly known as the Southern Pokeweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that appear in the summer. The plant produces dark purple berries that are toxic to humans and animals if ingested. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a distinctive toothed margin.

Toxicodendron dentatum contains various irritants, including lectins and glycosides, which can cause dermatitis in sensitive individuals.
