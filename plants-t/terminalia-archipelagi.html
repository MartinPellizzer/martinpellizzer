Terminalia archipelagi

Terminalia archipelagi, commonly known as the Socotra fig, is a unique species native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its distinctive, thorned stems and large, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers followed by edible fig-like fruits that are a vital food source for local wildlife. Its ecological importance is significant in the region's biodiversity.

Terminalia archipelagi is also noted for its slow growth and long lifespan, making it a symbol of resilience in harsh climates.
