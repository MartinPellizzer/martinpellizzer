Tropidia gracilis

Tropidia gracilis, commonly known as the slender lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like foliage and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found growing in rocky or forested areas. Its leaves are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, and the plant is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Tropidia gracilis is also valued for its ability to tolerate poor soil conditions and its low maintenance requirements.
