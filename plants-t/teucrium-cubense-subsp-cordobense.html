Teucrium cubense subsp. cordobense

Teucrium cubense subsp.

cordobense, commonly known as the Cordoba germander, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America, particularly Argentina and Uruguay. It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs.

The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or blue in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or disturbed areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
