Toona mannii

Toona mannii, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers followed by fig-like fruits that are edible for certain animals. It is valued for its durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture making.

The tree also plays an important role in local ecosystems and traditional medicine.
