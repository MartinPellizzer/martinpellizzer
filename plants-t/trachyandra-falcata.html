Trachyandra falcata

Trachyandra falcata, commonly known as the sickle leaf cycad, is a rare and ancient plant species native to the eastern coast of South Africa.

It belongs to the family Cycadaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, sickle-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. The plant produces cones rather than flowers, with male and female cones occurring on separate plants. Its slow growth rate and specific habitat requirements make it highly susceptible to environmental changes and human disturbance.

Conservation efforts are crucial to protect this unique botanical species from extinction.
