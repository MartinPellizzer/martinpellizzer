Triticum dasystachys

Triticum dasystachys, commonly known as the slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescence. The plant produces small, pale green or yellowish flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in cool, temperate climates and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

Triticum dasystachys is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
