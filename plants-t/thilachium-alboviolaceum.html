Thilachium alboviolaceum

Thilachium alboviolaceum, commonly known as the white and violet pincushion, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, spiky inflorescence that resembles a pincushion. The flowers are typically white or violet in color, with a striking contrast between the central disc and the surrounding ray florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

This plant is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
