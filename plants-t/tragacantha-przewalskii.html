Tragacantha przewalskii

Tragacantha przewalskii, commonly known as the Przewalski's tragacanth, is a species of flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and woody stems. The plant produces a milky sap that has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive. Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various desert-dwelling organisms.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat sensitivity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
