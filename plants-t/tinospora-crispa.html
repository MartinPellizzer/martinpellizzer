Tinospora crispa

Tinospora crispa, commonly known as the creeping gourd or Indian creeper, is a woody vine native to Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by elongated, fleshy fruits that turn red when mature. Tinospora crispa is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a common species in both natural and cultivated settings.
