Thyrsacanthus rutilans

Thyrsacanthus rutilans, commonly known as the golden burr or golden thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, cone-like seed heads that turn a striking golden color as they mature. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer, followed by the development of its distinctive bristly fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along coastal regions.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
