Thesium radicans

Thesium radicans, commonly known as the creeping thesium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It features a low-growing, spreading habit with slender, branched stems that root at the nodes. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs.

Thesium radicans is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.
