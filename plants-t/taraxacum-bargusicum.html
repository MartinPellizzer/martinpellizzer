Taraxacum bargusicum

Taraxacum bargusicum, commonly known as the Bargusik dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces a yellow flower that develops into a fluffy white seed head, typical of the dandelion genus. It has a rosette of basal leaves that are usually hairy and slightly lobed.

Taraxacum bargusicum is often found in meadows and grasslands, thriving in well-drained soils.
