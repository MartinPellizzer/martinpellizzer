Thryptomene fimbriata

Thryptomene fimbriata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed thryptomene, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalypts and other aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered white flowers. It typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and open woodlands.

Thryptomene fimbriata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native garden settings.
