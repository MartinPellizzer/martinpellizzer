Taraxacum kansaiense

Taraxacum kansaiense, commonly known as the Japanese dandelion, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers and hairy, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces a distinctive seed head that releases lightweight seeds with parachute-like structures for wind dispersal. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers moist, well-drained environments.

Taraxacum kansaiense is often found in meadows, gardens, and disturbed areas, showcasing its adaptability and resilience.
