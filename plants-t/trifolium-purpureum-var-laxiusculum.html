Trifolium purpureum var. laxiusculum

Trifolium purpureum var. laxiusculum, commonly known as the purple clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, purple or pinkish flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in early summer. It is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.

This variety is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it an important plant in agricultural and ecological contexts.
