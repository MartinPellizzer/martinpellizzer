Tricerma crassifolium

Tricerma crassifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf tricerma, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its robust root system allows it to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it a hardy species.

Tricerma crassifolium is often used in xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and drought tolerance.
