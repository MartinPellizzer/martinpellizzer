Turnera foliosa

Turnera foliosa, commonly known as the "Mexican moonflower," is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows up to 1.5 meters tall, featuring opposite, ovate, and hairy leaves. The plant produces showy, white, star-shaped flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators like moths. Its flowers emit a strong, sweet fragrance, which enhances its appeal during the evening.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
