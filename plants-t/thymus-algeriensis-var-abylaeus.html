Thymus algeriensis var. abylaeus

Thymus algeriensis var. abylaeus, commonly known as Algerian thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North Africa.

It features small, fragrant leaves that are typically gray-green in color, with a dense covering of fine hairs. The plant produces pink to purple flowers in clusters, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

This variety is valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
