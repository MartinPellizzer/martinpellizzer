Tectona hamiltoniana

Tectona hamiltoniana, commonly known as the Indian teak, is a tropical hardwood tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark-colored wood. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface and can grow up to 30 meters tall. It produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by small, woody fruits.

The tree is valued for its durable and water-resistant timber, making it important in the timber industry.
