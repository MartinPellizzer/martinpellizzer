Tropaeolum fulvum

Tropaeolum fulvum, commonly known as the golden crown flower, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Tropaeolaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces small, edible tubers similar to those of the common garden nasturtium. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it both aesthetically pleasing and useful in culinary applications.
