Tapeinosperma boulindaense

Tapeinosperma boulindaense, commonly known as the Boulinda tamarind, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are often clustered together, and its seeds are encased in a hard, woody pod.

It is valued for its edible seeds, which are used in traditional cuisines and have a tangy, sour flavor similar to tamarind.
