Terminalia salacifolia

Terminalia salacifolia, commonly known as the sour sop or graviola, is a tropical tree native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The fruit, which is the most notable part of the plant, is a rough, spiny berry with a distinctive scent and a creamy, acidic pulp. The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties, with various parts used in traditional medicine.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for wildlife in its native regions.
