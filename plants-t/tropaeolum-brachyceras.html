Tropaeolum brachyceras

Tropaeolum brachyceras, commonly known as the dwarf garden nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Tropaeolaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, edible flowers. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and bright orange or yellow blooms. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental and edible plants.

Its rapid growth and ability to self-seed make it a versatile addition to both ornamental and edible gardens.
