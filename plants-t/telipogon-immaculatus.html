Telipogon immaculatus

Telipogon immaculatus, commonly known as the white-flowered bog orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to the wetlands of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, white to pale green flowers that bloom during the summer months. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in fens and bogs where water tables are high. Its unique morphology and restricted habitat make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss and climate change, Telipogon immaculatus is considered vulnerable and requires careful protection.
