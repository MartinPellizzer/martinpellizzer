Tanacetum consanguineum

Tanacetum consanguineum, commonly known as the common tansy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves and small yellow flower heads that bloom in summer. It is often found in moist meadows and along stream banks, thriving in well-drained soil.

Tanacetum consanguineum is known for its strong, camphor-like scent and has been historically used in herbal medicine.
