Tabebuia × del-riscoi

Tabebuia × del-riscoi, commonly known as the pink trumpet tree, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Tabebuia impetifolia and Tabebuia mekongensis.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its striking pink to red tubular flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant is a deciduous tree with a rounded canopy and smooth, gray bark that becomes fissured with age. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate, with a glossy green surface.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often planted in gardens and along streets for its vibrant floral display.
