Taraxacum tricolor

Taraxacum tricolor, commonly known as the three-color dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers that transition through pink to red as they age, giving it its distinctive three-color appearance. The plant produces a milky sap that contains latex, which is a common feature in the genus Taraxacum. It has a rosette of basal leaves that are typically hairy and lobed, and it thrives in a variety of soil types.

This species is often found in meadows, gardens, and disturbed areas, and it is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
