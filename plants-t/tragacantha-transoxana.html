Tragacantha transoxana

Tragacantha transoxana, commonly known as the Transoxiana tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in racemes. Its most notable feature is the production of a viscous gum, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive.

Tragacantha transoxana is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
