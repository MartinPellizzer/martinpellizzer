Tecoma punctatissima

Tecoma punctatissima, commonly known as the spotted trumpetflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly waxy texture.

Tecoma punctatissima is well adapted to arid environments, making it a hardy species suitable for xeriscaping.
