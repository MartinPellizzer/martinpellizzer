Torilis arvensis subsp. elongata

Torilis arvensis subsp.

elongata, commonly known as the elongated field penny-cress, is a herbaceous annual plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and can thrive in a variety of soil types. Its leaves are pinnately divided, and the flowers are yellow, with four petals arranged in a cross shape.

The species is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize open areas, making it a common weed in agricultural settings.
