Trichilia havanensis var. multijuga

Trichilia havanensis var. multijuga, commonly known as the Havanan tamarind tree, is a member of the Meliaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers.

It produces yellowish-orange fruits that contain flat, winged seeds, typical of the genus Trichilia. The tree is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, warm climates. Its bark and leaves have been used traditionally in folk medicine for their purported healing properties.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential ecological benefits in forest ecosystems.
