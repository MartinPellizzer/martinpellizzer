Tillandsia tweedieana

Tillandsia tweedieana, commonly known as the Tweedie's air plant, is a species of bromeliad native to Mexico.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on the surface of other plants without deriving nutrients from them. The plant has rosette-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help it absorb moisture and nutrients from the air. It produces striking purple or pink flowers that bloom from a central stem.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
