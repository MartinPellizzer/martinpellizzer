Taraxacum distantilobum

Taraxacum distantilobum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the sunflower family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and a bright yellow composite flower head that develops into a spherical seed sphere. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. It has a taproot system and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

The species is also noted for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in herbal remedies.
