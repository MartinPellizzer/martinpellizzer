Tabernaemontana gamblei

Tabernaemontana gamblei, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a striking tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, funnel-shaped flowers that range in color from white to yellow and orange, often with a striking contrast between the petals and the green calyx. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds, and it is typically found in moist, shaded environments within its native habitat. Tabernaemontana gamblei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and eye-catching addition to tropical landscapes.
