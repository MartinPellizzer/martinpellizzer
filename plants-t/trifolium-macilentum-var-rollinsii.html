Trifolium macilentum var. rollinsii

Trifolium macilentum var. rollinsii, commonly known as the Rollins' narrow-leaf clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three small, oval-shaped leaflets with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
