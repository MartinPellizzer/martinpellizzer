Terminalia firma

Terminalia firma, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by hard, woody fruits containing seeds. It is valued for its durable wood, which is used in furniture and construction.
