Trichilia discolor

Trichilia discolor, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, light-colored bark and striking clusters of white, flame-like flowers that bloom in the rainy season. The plant has pinnate leaves with alternate arrangement and produces small, round, greenish-yellow fruits that contain several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its showy flowers and rapid growth rate.

The species is also used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
