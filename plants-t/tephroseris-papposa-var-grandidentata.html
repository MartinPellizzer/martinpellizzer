Tephroseris papposa var. grandidentata

Tephroseris papposa var. grandidentata, commonly known as the giant toothed tephroseris, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its robust, branched stems and large, toothed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both ecological studies and horticultural cultivation.
