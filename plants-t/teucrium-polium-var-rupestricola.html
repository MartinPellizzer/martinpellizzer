Teucrium polium var. rupestricola

Teucrium polium var. rupestricola, commonly known as rock tea or mountain germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, often in dry, sunny environments. The plant features slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often grayish-green in color. Its flowers are usually pale blue or purple, arranged in whorls along the stems.

It is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various health benefits.
