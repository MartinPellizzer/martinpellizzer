Tragopogon minor

Tragopogon minor, commonly known as the common goatsbeard, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant produces a distinctive yellow flower that develops into a tufted, bristly seed head. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including meadows, roadsides, and disturbed soils.

Tragopogon minor is often found growing in temperate climates and is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize open areas.
