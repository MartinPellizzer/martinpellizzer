Trichosanthes longispicata

Trichosanthes longispicata, commonly known as the long-spined bitter melon, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its elongated, lobed leaves and yellow flowers. The fruit is typically oblong, green when unripe, and turns orange-red when mature. The plant is native to Southeast Asia and is cultivated for its edible fruit, though it contains toxic compounds in its seeds and skin.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
