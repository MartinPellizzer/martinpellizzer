Teinostachyum attenuatum

Teinostachyum attenuatum, commonly known as the slender-stalked bamboo, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems that grow in dense clumps, often reaching heights of up to 3 meters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the grass family. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth surface and a slightly hairy underside.

Teinostachyum attenuatum is often used in traditional landscaping and has some ornamental value due to its elegant appearance.
