Thuja gigantea var. pendula

Thuja gigantea var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping Alberta spruce, is a cultivar of the eastern white cedar.

It is characterized by its cascading, drooping branches that create a distinctive, elegant appearance. The plant has needle-like leaves that are dark green and slightly twisted, providing a lush, dense foliage. It is a slow-growing evergreen that thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sun.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to form a natural, flowing shape.
