Tilia amurensis f. koreana

Tilia amurensis f. koreana, commonly known as the Korean linden, is a botanical variant of the Amur linden.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are typically larger and more lobed than those of the typical species. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees. It is native to the Korean Peninsula and parts of northeastern China.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
