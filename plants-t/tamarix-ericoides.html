Tamarix ericoides

Tamarix ericoides, commonly known as the silverleaf tamarisk, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves that give it a silvery appearance, and its fluffy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is adapted to dry, saline soils and is often found in coastal areas and riverbanks. It has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in arid environments.

Tamarix ericoides is also known for its rapid growth and ability to stabilize soil, making it useful for erosion control.
