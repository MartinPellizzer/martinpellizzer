Tanacetum botschantzevii

Tanacetum botschantzevii, commonly known as the Crimean tansy, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus and parts of southern Russia.

It features finely divided, silver-gray leaves and produces small, yellow flower heads that are arranged in flat-topped clusters. The plant is characterized by its aromatic foliage, which contains volatile oils responsible for its distinctive scent. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or dry habitats.

Tanacetum botschantzevii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
