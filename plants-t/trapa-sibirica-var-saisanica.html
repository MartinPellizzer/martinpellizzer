Trapa sibirica var. saisanica

Trapa sibirica var. saisanica, commonly known as the Saisan water chestnut, is a perennial aquatic plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, floating, spiny nuts that develop underwater. The plant thrives in shallow freshwater environments such as ponds and slow-moving streams. Its leaves are broad and floating, with a waxy surface that helps repel water.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to various regions for aquatic garden use.
