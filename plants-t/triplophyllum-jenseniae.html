Triplophyllum jenseniae

Triplophyllum jenseniae, commonly known as the Jensen's triple-leaf plant, is a rare and unique species native to the tropical rainforests of Central America.

It is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves, which consist of three lobes that are often asymmetrically arranged. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its foliage is evergreen, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments with high atmospheric moisture.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical research and conservation efforts.
