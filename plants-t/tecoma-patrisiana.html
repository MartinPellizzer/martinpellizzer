Tecoma patrisiana

Tecoma patrisiana, commonly known as the yellow bells, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate, with a smooth texture and a slightly glossy appearance.

Tecoma patrisiana is well adapted to full sun and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its showy blooms and hardy nature.
