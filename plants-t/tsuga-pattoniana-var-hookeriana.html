Tsuga pattoniana var. hookeriana

Tsuga pattoniana var. hookeriana, commonly known as the Hooker's fir, is a rare and endemic coniferous species native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and dense foliage of needle-like leaves that are dark green and slightly twisted. The plant produces small, oval cones that mature to a reddish-brown color, and it is typically found in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique adaptations to high-altitude conditions and its role in the ecological diversity of the region.
