Tragacantha narbonensis

Tragacantha narbonensis, commonly known as the Narbonne tragacanth, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces a unique gum, known as tragacanth, which is harvested from its stems and used in various industrial applications. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in scrublands and coastal areas.

Due to its ecological importance and traditional uses, it is considered a valuable species in its native habitat.
