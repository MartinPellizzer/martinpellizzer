Torilis melanantha

Torilis melanantha, commonly known as blackstem tickseed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its dark green, hairy stems and small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. It produces small, oval seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread across various regions.

Torilis melanantha is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
