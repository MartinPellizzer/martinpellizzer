Tylophora tanakae var. glabrescens

Tylophora tanakae var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth-leafed tylophora, is a climbing plant belonging to the Apocynaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its slender stems, opposite leaves, and small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
