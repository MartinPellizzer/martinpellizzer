Tragopogon brevirostris

Tragopogon brevirostris, commonly known as the short-beaked thistle, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a distinctive purple-tipped involucre.

Tragopogon brevirostris is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
