Trismeria trifoliata var. subpinnata

Trismeria trifoliata var. subpinnata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf trismeria, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are divided into three leaflets, and its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with a subtle scent that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a suitable ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical gardens.
