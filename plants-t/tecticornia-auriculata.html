Tecticornia auriculata

Tecticornia auriculata, commonly known as the silver leaf saltbush, is a halophytic shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which helps reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in spikes, typically blooming in spring.

Tecticornia auriculata is well adapted to saline soils and is often used in revegetation projects in degraded landscapes.
