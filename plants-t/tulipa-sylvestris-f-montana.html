Tulipa sylvestris f. montana

Tulipa sylvestris f. montana, commonly known as the mountain tulip, is a botanical variant of the wild tulip.

It is characterized by its robust, upright stems and showy, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and thrives in well-drained, sunny soils. It is a perennial bulbous plant that prefers cooler climates and is often found in alpine meadows.

Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge in early spring, preceding the blooms.
