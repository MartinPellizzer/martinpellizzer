Taraxacum subericinum

Taraxacum subericinum, commonly known as the Spanish dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of alternate, hairy, and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces a single, hollow, and hairy stem that terminates in a composite flower head, which develops into a spherical seed sphere with pappus. It is native to southern Europe and is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
