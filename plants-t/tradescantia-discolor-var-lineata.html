Tradescantia discolor var. lineata

Tradescantia discolor var. lineata, commonly known as the wandering jew or spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its striking variegated leaves that display shades of white, pink, and green, with a distinct white midrib. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink or purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It is a low-maintenance plant that thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Its ability to propagate through stem cuttings makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
