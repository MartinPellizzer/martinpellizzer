Trollius loddigesii

Trollius loddigesii, commonly known as the yellow archangel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, saucer-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are typically hairy and upright. Trollius loddigesii thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

It is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.
