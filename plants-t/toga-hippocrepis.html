Toga hippocrepis

Toga hippocrepis, commonly known as the common toga, is a flowering plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy bracts that resemble petals, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Toga hippocrepis thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
