Trisetum flavescens proles baregense

Trisetum flavescens proles baregense, commonly known as the Baregense False Brome, is a grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically a yellowish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear pale green to brownish flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is considered a species of conservation concern in some regions.
