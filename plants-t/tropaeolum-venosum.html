Tropaeolum venosum

Tropaeolum venosum, commonly known as the veiny nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Tropaeolaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, butterfly-shaped flowers that come in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with prominent veins, giving it its common name. It is an annual herb that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often used in gardens for its ornamental value and edible flowers.

Tropaeolum venosum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
