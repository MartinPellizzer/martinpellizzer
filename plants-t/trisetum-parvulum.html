Trisetum parvulum

Trisetum parvulum, commonly known as the small-panicle grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Trisetum parvulum is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a characteristic species of certain wetland ecosystems.
