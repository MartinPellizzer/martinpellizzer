Tripogon modestus

Tripogon modestus, commonly known as the modest spikerush, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps. The plant has slender, grass-like stems with small, triangular leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a single, erect spike that bears tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Tripogon modestus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
