Terminalia cicatricosa

Terminalia cicatricosa, commonly known as the African sandalwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and alternate, simple leaves. The tree produces small, pale yellow flowers followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits containing a single seed. Its wood is highly valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.

The plant also plays an important ecological role in its native habitats.
