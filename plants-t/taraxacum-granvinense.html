Taraxacum granvinense

Taraxacum granvinense, commonly known as the Granvin dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central Europe, particularly in Switzerland and Austria. This plant is characterized by its basal rosette of pale green, hairy leaves and a yellow flower head that develops into a white, fluffy seed head. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and grasslands.

Taraxacum granvinense is a perennial herb that is relatively rare and has limited distribution due to its specific habitat requirements.
