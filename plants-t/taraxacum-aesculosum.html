Taraxacum aesculosum

Taraxacum aesculosum, commonly known as the tree dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its rosette of spoon-shaped, hairy leaves and a flowering stem that bears a single, yellow-orange composite flower head. The plant is native to temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia, often found in grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces a distinctive, hollow, wind-dispersed seed head known as a pappus.

The species is notable for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in many habitats.
