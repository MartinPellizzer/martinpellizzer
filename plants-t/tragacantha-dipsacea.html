Tragacantha dipsacea

Tragacantha dipsacea, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a species of flowering plant native to regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pale yellow or greenish in color. Its most notable feature is the production of a valuable gum, which has been historically used in medicine and as a natural adhesive.

Tragacantha dipsacea thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh climatic conditions with its deep root system.
