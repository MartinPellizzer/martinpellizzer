Teucrium canadense f. albiflorum

Teucrium canadense f. albiflorum, commonly known as whiteflower germander, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in the summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly aromatic scent when crushed.

This variety is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks.
