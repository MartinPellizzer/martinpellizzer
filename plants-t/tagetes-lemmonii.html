Tagetes lemmonii

Tagetes lemmonii, commonly known as the Lemmon's marigold, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its bright yellow or orange ray florets and dark brown disc florets, which give it a striking appearance. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous annual, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its foliage is aromatic, with a strong, pungent scent, and its flowers bloom from late summer into early autumn.

Tagetes lemmonii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
