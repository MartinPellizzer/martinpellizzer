Trachypoa vulgaris

Trachypoa vulgaris, commonly known as the common rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, grass-like stems that are often ribbed and can grow up to several feet in height. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown, arranged in spikelets along the stem. Trachypoa vulgaris thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
