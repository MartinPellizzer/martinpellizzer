Taraxacum vitalii

Taraxacum vitalii, commonly known as the Vitalii dandelion, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flower heads and serrated leaves.

It is native to parts of Central Asia and is often found in meadows and grassy areas. The plant produces a milky latex when the stems or leaves are damaged, which is a common trait among the genus Taraxacum. Its flowers develop into fluffy, white seed heads that are easily dispersed by the wind.

The species is noted for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
