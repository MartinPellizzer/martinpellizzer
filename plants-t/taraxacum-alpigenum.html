Taraxacum alpigenum

Taraxacum alpigenum, commonly known as the alpine dandelion, is a flowering plant native to alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and a yellow composite flower head that develops into a spherical seed ball. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing food for pollinators.

Taraxacum alpigenum is often found in alpine meadows and is a hardy species capable of surviving harsh climatic conditions.
