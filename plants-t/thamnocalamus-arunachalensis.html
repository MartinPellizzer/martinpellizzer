Thamnocalamus arunachalensis

Thamnocalamus arunachalensis, commonly known as the Arunachal bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to the eastern Himalayas.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 5 meters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is typically found in moist, shaded forest environments. It plays an important ecological role in its habitat by contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for various organisms.

Due to its unique morphology and restricted distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
