Tetragonocalamus quadrangularis f. sotaroanus

Tetragonocalamus quadrangularis f. sotaroanus, commonly known as the Sotaro bamboo, is a variant of the quadrangular bamboo species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its distinctive four-sided stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant exhibits a compact growth habit, making it suitable for ornamental cultivation in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, requiring minimal maintenance.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to the bamboo family, valued for both its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
