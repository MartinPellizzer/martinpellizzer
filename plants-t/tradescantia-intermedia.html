Tradescantia intermedia

Tradescantia intermedia, commonly known as spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy, blue to purple flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sun. It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to naturalize easily.

Tradescantia intermedia is also known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent ground cover.
