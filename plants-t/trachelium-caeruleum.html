Trachelium caeruleum

Trachelium caeruleum, commonly known as blue mist shrub, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
