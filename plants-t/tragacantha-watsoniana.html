Tragacantha watsoniana

Tragacantha watsoniana, commonly known as the Watson's tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces a unique gum, similar to tragacanth, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a thickening agent. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in shrubland and fynbos vegetation.

Its ecological importance includes providing nectar for pollinators and supporting local biodiversity.
