Teucrium quadratulum

Teucrium quadratulum, commonly known as the square-stemmed germander, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features square, hairy stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly aromatic scent.

Teucrium quadratulum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
