Toumeya papyracantha

Toumeya papyracantha, commonly known as the African milk bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, woody stems and small, clustered leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

The plant is also known for its latex-like sap, which can be toxic if ingested.
