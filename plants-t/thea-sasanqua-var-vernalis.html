Thea sasanqua var. vernalis

Thea sasanqua var. vernalis, commonly known as the winter camellia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers in late winter to early spring. The plant thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, acidic soil. It is relatively low maintenance and resistant to pests and diseases, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

The winter camellia is valued for its ornamental beauty and ability to bloom during the colder months.
