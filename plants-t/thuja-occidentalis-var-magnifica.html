Thuja occidentalis var. magnifica

Thuja occidentalis var. magnifica, commonly known as the American arborvitae or noble fir, is a cultivar of the eastern white cedar.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its dense, pyramidal shape and scale-like leaves. The foliage is typically a deep green, with a bluish tinge, and the tree produces small, round cones. Native to North America, it is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness.

This variety is particularly valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions and its resistance to pests and diseases.
