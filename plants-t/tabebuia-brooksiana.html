Tabebuia brooksiana

Tabebuia brooksiana, commonly known as the pink trumpet tree, is a species of flowering tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red, blooming in the spring and summer. The tree has a straight trunk and a rounded crown, with smooth, grayish bark that becomes fissured with age. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a glossy green surface.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often planted in gardens and urban landscapes for its vibrant floral display.
