Taraxacum atricapillum

Taraxacum atricapillum, commonly known as the black-hat dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive dark purple or blackish central stem, which contrasts with its bright yellow flowers. The plant produces a single, showy flower head that blooms in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically basal, forming a rosette shape, and are covered in fine hairs.

The species is native to Europe and has been introduced to other parts of the world, often growing in disturbed soils and open areas.
