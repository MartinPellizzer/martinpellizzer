Tinguarra cerviariifolia

Tinguarra cerviariifolia, commonly known as the red-veined begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves with deep red veins against a dark green background. The plant produces small, pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Tinguarra cerviariifolia is popular as a houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
