Tragacantha leptothalama

Tragacantha leptothalama, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Iran and surrounding areas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers and distinctive, woody pods. The plant is known for producing a resinous gum, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive.

Its adaptability to harsh environments makes it an important species in desert ecosystems.
