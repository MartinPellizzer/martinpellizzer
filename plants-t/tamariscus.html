Tamariscus

Tamariscus, commonly known as tamarisk, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Tamaricaceae.

These shrubs or small trees are native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, and are characterized by their small, scale-like leaves and often fragrant flowers. Tamarisk species are typically drought-tolerant, making them well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. They produce small, often pink or yellowish flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Some species are invasive in certain regions, leading to ecological concerns due to their rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed soils.
