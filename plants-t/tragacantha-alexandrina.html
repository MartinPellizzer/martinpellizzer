Tragacantha alexandrina

Tragacantha alexandrina, commonly known as the Aleppo gum or Persian gum, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is well-known for producing a resin, known as tragacanth, which has been used historically in medicine and as a binding agent. Its ability to thrive in arid conditions makes it an important plant in dryland ecosystems.

The resin is harvested from the bark and has a long history of traditional use in various cultures.
