Tephrosia wynaadensis

Tephrosia wynaadensis, commonly known as the Wynaad tephrosia, is a shrub native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom during the summer months. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.

Tephrosia wynaadensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
