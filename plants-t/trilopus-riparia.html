Trilopus riparia

Trilopus riparia, commonly known as the riverbank buttercup, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland and riparian habitats.

It typically grows in moist soils along rivers, streams, and other water bodies. The plant features basal leaves with heart-shaped lobes and yellow flowers arranged in a single umbel. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Trilopus riparia plays an important ecological role in stabilizing riverbanks and supporting local biodiversity.
