Tupa hyssopifolia

Tupa hyssopifolia, commonly known as the Mexican hyssop, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant typically grows as a shrub with square stems and opposite leaves that are often hairy. Its small, tubular flowers are usually violet or blue, and they bloom in clusters during the spring and summer.

Tupa hyssopifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
