Tanarius involucratus

Tanarius involucratus, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and shrublands, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers during the summer months. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, and the plant is known for its ornamental value in alpine gardens.

Tanarius involucratus is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local insect populations and soil stabilization in its native habitat.
