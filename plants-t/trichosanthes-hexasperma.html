Trichosanthes hexasperma

Trichosanthes hexasperma, commonly known as the six-ribbed bitter melon, is a climbing vine belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its distinctive, warty, ribbed fruit that turns orange when mature. The plant produces yellow flowers and has hairy stems and leaves. Its fruit is used in traditional medicine and as a vegetable in some Asian cuisines.

The plant is valued for its potential medicinal properties and its role in local agriculture.
