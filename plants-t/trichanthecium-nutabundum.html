Trichanthecium nutabundum

Trichanthecium nutabundum, commonly known as the goldenhead grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive golden hue at the base. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open fields and disturbed areas.

Trichanthecium nutabundum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various insects and birds.
