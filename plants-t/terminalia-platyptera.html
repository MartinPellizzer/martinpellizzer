Terminalia platyptera

Terminalia platyptera, commonly known as the African sandalwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing small, hard seeds. It is valued for its aromatic wood, which is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.

The tree plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
