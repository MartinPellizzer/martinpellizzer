Tripterium transsilvanicum

Tripterium transsilvanicum, commonly known as the Transylvanian tripteris, is a perennial herb native to the Carpathian region.

It belongs to the family Tripteridaceae and is characterized by its small, trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests and woodland edges. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on different plants.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
