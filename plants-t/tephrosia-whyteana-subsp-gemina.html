Tephrosia whyteana subsp. gemina

Tephrosia whyteana subsp.

gemina, commonly known as the twinleaf tephrosia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa. It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are usually pinnately arranged with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, and it is often found in moist, well-drained soils. It is a member of the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing capabilities.

The subspecies gemina is distinguished by its unique leaf morphology and is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage plant.
