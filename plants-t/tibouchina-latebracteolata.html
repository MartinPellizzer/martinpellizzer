Tibouchina latebracteolata

Tibouchina latebracteolata, commonly known as the Brazilian violet or purple flamebush, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces showy, violet to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually opposite, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Tibouchina latebracteolata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping due to its vibrant floral display.
