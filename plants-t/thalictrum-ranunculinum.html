Thalictrum ranunculinum

Thalictrum ranunculinum, commonly known as meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and feathery, compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, typically appearing in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Thalictrum ranunculinum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting various pollinators.
