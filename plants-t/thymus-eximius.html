Thymus eximius

Thymus eximius, commonly known as the narrowleaf thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage is often used in culinary applications and herbal medicine due to its essential oils.

Thymus eximius is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardiness.
