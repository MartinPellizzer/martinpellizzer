Tanacetum waldsteinii

Tanacetum waldsteinii, commonly known as the meadow tansy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disc flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, thriving in well-drained soil. It produces small, yellowish to white flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are aromatic and often used in traditional herbal remedies.
