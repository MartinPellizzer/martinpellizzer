Theodorea katachaete

Theodorea katachaete, commonly known as the purple-flowered orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet petals and intricate floral structure. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in clusters, often displaying a unique blend of color and form that attracts specific pollinators.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
