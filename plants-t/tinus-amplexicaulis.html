Tinus amplexicaulis

Tinus amplexicaulis, commonly known as the clasping tinus, is a unique flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to red. The plant has fleshy, succulent leaves that are often clasped around the stems, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in Mediterranean climates.
