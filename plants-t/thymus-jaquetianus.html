Thymus jaquetianus

Thymus jaquetianus, commonly known as Jaquet's thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and aromatic, gray-green foliage. The plant produces small, pink to violet flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Thymus jaquetianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
