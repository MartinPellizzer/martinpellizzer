Tococa lancifolia

Tococa lancifolia, commonly known as the Amazonian coffee plant, is a climbing shrub native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family, which includes other coffee species, and is characterized by its slender stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by elliptical, red berries containing one or two seeds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported stimulant properties.

Due to its caffeine content, it is sometimes referred to as "the plant of the Amazonian coffee.".
