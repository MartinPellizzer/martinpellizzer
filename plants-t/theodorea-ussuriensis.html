Theodorea ussuriensis

Theodorea ussuriensis, commonly known as the Ussuri lily, is a perennial herb native to the eastern regions of Russia and parts of China.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its flowers bloom in late summer.

Theodorea ussuriensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
