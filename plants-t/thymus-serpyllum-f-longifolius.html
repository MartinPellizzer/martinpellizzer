Thymus serpyllum f. longifolius

Thymus serpyllum f. longifolius, commonly known as the long-leaved thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features elongated, narrow leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft texture. Its small, pink or white flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

This variety is often used in ornamental gardening and has mild culinary applications.
