Tristachya bicrinita

Tristachya bicrinita, commonly known as the hairy panic grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and long, narrow leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces panicles of small, pale green to yellowish flowers, which are often wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Tristachya bicrinita is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
