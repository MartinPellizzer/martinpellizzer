Toddalia rubicaulis

Toddalia rubicaulis, commonly known as the red-stemmed tamarind, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and heart-shaped leaves with a glossy green upper surface. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elongated pods containing flat, edible seeds. Toddalia rubicaulis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
