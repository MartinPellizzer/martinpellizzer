Thalictrum podolicum

Thalictrum podolicum, commonly known as the Podolic meadow rue, is a perennial herb native to central and eastern Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that bear compound leaves with finely divided, fern-like foliage. The plant produces small, pale yellow to greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters at the top of the stem. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Ranunculaceae family.
