Trigonochilum sigchae

Trigonochilum sigchae, commonly known as the Hawaiian orchid, is a rare and endemic species found exclusively in the tropical rainforests of Hawaii.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree bark without deriving nutrients from its host. Its small, delicate leaves and intricate floral structure make it a striking example of evolutionary adaptation in island ecosystems.

Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, Trigonochilum sigchae is considered critically endangered, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
