Teucrium polycephalon

Teucrium polycephalon, commonly known as purple germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its historical use in herbal medicine is noted for its potential anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
