Trichilia catigua var. pilosior

Trichilia catigua var. pilosior, commonly known as Brazilian cherry or pata de vaca, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and elliptical leaves with a velvety texture. The tree produces small, yellow flowers followed by woody, spiny capsules containing aromatic seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in medicine and as a source of timber.
