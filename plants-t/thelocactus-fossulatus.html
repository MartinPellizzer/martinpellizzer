Thelocactus fossulatus

Thelocactus fossulatus, commonly known as the fissured cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often ribbed and covered in white, woolly spines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its rugged appearance. Its root system is adapted to absorb moisture efficiently in its dry habitat.

Thelocactus fossulatus is valued for its unique morphology and resilience, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
