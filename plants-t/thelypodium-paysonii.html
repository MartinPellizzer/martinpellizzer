Thelypodium paysonii

Thelypodium paysonii, commonly known as the Payson's thelypodium, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, grass-like foliage and small, clustered flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grassy, while its flowers are usually pink or purple in color.

Thelypodium paysonii plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and unique ecological niche.
