Tabernaemontana oppositifolia

Tabernaemontana oppositifolia, commonly known as the opposite-leaved tabernanthe, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite arrangement of lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, elliptical seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in the wild.
