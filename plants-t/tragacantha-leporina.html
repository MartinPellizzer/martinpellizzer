Tragacantha leporina

Tragacantha leporina, commonly known as the hare's-foot clover, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant has trifoliate leaves, with each leaflet being oval and slightly hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Tragacantha leporina is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
