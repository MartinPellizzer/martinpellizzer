Trientalis capensis

Trientalis capensis, commonly known as the Cape bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Trientalis capensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
