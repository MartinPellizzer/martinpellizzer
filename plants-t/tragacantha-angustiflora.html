Tragacantha angustiflora

Tragacantha angustiflora, commonly known as narrow-leaved tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its slender, angular stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are typically arranged in racemose inflorescences. It is known for its latex-rich sap, which has been used historically in various traditional applications.

The botanical features of Tragacantha angustiflora make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
