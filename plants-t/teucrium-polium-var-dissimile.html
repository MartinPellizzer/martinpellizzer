Teucrium polium var. dissimile

Teucrium polium var. dissimile, commonly known as the lesser germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, gray-green leaves that are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of blue to purple, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its medicinal properties have been historically recognized for their antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects.
