Tocantinia mira

Tocantinia mira, commonly known as the Brazilian star orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, star-shaped flowers that come in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Tocantinia mira is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting biodiversity in its native habitat.
