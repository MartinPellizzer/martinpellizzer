Taraxacum nubilum

Taraxacum nubilum, commonly known as the clouded dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow composite flower head, which is surrounded by a pappus of bristles that aids in seed dispersal. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are alternate, basal, and usually have a distinctive lobed shape.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and is often found in temperate regions.
