Tecoma stans var. apiifolia

Tecoma stans var. apiifolia, commonly known as the sweet senna or yellow glorybower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its showy, yellow-orange inflorescences that bloom in summer. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
