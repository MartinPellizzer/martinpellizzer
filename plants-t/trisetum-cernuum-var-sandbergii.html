Trisetum cernuum var. sandbergii

Trisetum cernuum var. sandbergii, commonly known as Sandberg's tufted grass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. Its botanical characteristics include a tufted growth habit and a distinctive flowering structure.

It plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
