Tuberolabium calcaratum

Tuberolabium calcaratum, commonly known as the horned orchid, is a unique flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive tuberous roots and striking, showy flowers that resemble horns, hence its common name. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its complex floral structure and vibrant coloration. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern in its native range.
