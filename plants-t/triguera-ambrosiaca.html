Triguera ambrosiaca

Triguera ambrosiaca, commonly known as wormseed, is an annual herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its branched stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces tiny, oval-shaped seeds that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are hairy and have a strong, distinctive odor.

Triguera ambrosiaca is also known for its association with the Mexican tradition of the Day of the Dead.
