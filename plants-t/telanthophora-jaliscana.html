Telanthophora jaliscana

Telanthophora jaliscana, commonly known as the Jalisco sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow ray flowers and green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It produces small, spiny fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Telanthophora jaliscana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
