Trichosporum chiritoides

Trichosporum chiritoides, commonly known as the silky willow or silky sedge, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish or yellowish in color. The plant produces small, three-lobed seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize open, disturbed areas. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
