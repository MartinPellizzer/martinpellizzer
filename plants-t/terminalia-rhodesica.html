Terminalia rhodesica

Terminalia rhodesica, commonly known as African snowball, is a deciduous tree native to eastern and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and clusters of small, white, waxy flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, round, red fruits that contain a single seed. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Terminalia rhodesica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in medicine and as a source of timber.
