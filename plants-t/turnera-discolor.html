Turnera discolor

Turnera discolor, commonly known as the "Mexican flame flower," is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom throughout the year, especially in warm climates. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant is well adapted to tropical and subtropical environments, thriving in well-drained soil and full sunlight.
