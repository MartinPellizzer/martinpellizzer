Triticum durum var. cauleucurum

Triticum durum var. cauleucurum, commonly known as white durum wheat, is a variant of durum wheat characterized by its pale color and lower protein content compared to other durum wheat varieties.

It is primarily used in the production of semolina, which is essential for making pasta and certain types of bread. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences containing small, hard grains. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its adaptability and yield under various agricultural conditions.
