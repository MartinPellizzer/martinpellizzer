Tournefortia bicolor

Tournefortia bicolor, commonly known as the white sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to parts of Central and South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in sandy or coastal soils. The plant produces showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are often woody and branched.

Tournefortia bicolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
