Triticum vulgare var. gentile

Triticum vulgare var. gentile, commonly known as the Gentile wheat, is a variety of common wheat characterized by its distinct morphological features.

It typically exhibits shorter stature and a more compact growth habit compared to other wheat varieties. The plant produces spikelets that are often more densely arranged, contributing to its unique appearance. Its grains are generally smaller and have a higher protein content, making it suitable for certain baking applications.

This variety is valued for its adaptability to various climatic conditions and its role in traditional agricultural practices.
