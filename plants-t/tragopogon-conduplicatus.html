Tragopogon conduplicatus

Tragopogon conduplicatus, commonly known as the double-headed goat's beard, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant features a tall, leafy stem with basal leaves that are lance-shaped and hairy. Its inflorescence consists of two rows of yellow disk flowers surrounded by white to light blue ray florets.

Tragopogon conduplicatus is often found in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows.
