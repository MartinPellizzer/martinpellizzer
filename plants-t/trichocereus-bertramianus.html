Trichocereus bertramianus

Trichocereus bertramianus, commonly known as the Bertram's torch cactus, is a species native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom at night. It is a member of the Cactaceae family and is well adapted to arid and high-altitude environments.

Trichocereus bertramianus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also studied for its potential psychoactive properties.
