Tritoniopsis ramosa var. unguiculata

Tritoniopsis ramosa var. unguiculata, commonly known as the velvetleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and trumpet-shaped, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant is a herbaceous annual that thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils. Its rapid growth and prolific seed production make it an aggressive weed in agricultural and natural settings.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its vibrant blooms.
