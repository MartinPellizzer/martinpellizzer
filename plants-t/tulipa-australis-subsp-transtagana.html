Tulipa australis subsp. transtagana

Tulipa australis subsp.

transtagana, commonly known as the Trans-Tasman tulip, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Australia. It produces showy, cup-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red, with a distinctive yellow stripe running down the center.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal gardens. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge in early spring.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and adaptability to various garden conditions.
