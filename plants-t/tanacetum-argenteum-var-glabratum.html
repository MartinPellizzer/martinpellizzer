Tanacetum argenteum var. glabratum

Tanacetum argenteum var. glabratum, commonly known as silverleaf tansy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features finely divided, silvery-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads arranged in flat-topped clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental value make it a popular choice for garden use.
