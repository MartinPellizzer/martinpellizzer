Tibouchina fraterna subsp. paruana

Tibouchina fraterna subsp.

paruana, commonly known as Parana torchbush, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil. It features showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to purple, typically blooming in clusters.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

The species is also noted for its ability to tolerate partial shade and its use in ecological restoration projects.
