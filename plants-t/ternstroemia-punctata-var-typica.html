Ternstroemia punctata var. typica

Ternstroemia punctata var. typica, commonly known as the spotted evergreen, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, which features distinctive yellow or white spots on the leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique leaf patterns and its role in local ecosystems.
