Tillandsia ventricosa

Tillandsia ventricosa, commonly known as the balloon bromeliad, is a species of air plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a water-holding structure at the center. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on other plants without being parasitic, and absorbs moisture and nutrients through its leaf surfaces. Its vibrant, showy inflorescence consists of a cluster of small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow.

Tillandsia ventricosa is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
