Tabernaemontana amsonia

Tabernaemontana amsonia, commonly known as the blue milkweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as butterflies and bees. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners in warm climates.

The plant's milky sap, which gives it its common name, contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
