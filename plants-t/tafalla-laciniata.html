Tafalla laciniata

Tafalla laciniata, commonly known as the fringed tafalla, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves that give it a distinctive, almost lace-like appearance. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring, adding a subtle beauty to its natural habitat. Tafalla laciniata thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in rocky or forested areas.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
