Tryphia secunda

Tryphia secunda, commonly known as the golden tamarisk, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are small, needle-like, and often covered in a fine layer of white wax.

Tryphia secunda is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or rocky habitats.
