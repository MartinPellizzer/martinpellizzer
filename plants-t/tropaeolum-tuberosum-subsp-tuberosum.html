Tropaeolum tuberosum subsp. tuberosum

Tropaeolum tuberosum subsp.

tuberosum, commonly known as the Andean groundcherry, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Andes region of South America. It is characterized by its upright growth habit, heart-shaped leaves, and clusters of small, vibrant yellow flowers.

The plant produces edible tubers that are rich in nutrients and have a tangy, slightly sweet flavor. It thrives in cool climates and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental and culinary use.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to both gardens and agricultural systems.
