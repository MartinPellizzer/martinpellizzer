Tabebuia latifolia

Tabebuia latifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or angel's trumpets, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The tree has a smooth, grey bark and can grow up to 25 meters tall. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and oval-shaped with a glossy surface.

The plant is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
