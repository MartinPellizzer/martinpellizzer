Thymus elegans

Thymus elegans, commonly known as slender thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in spring and summer. Its aromatic foliage is used in cooking and traditional medicine, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Thymus elegans is often cultivated as a ground cover or in rock gardens due to its hardy nature and ornamental value.
