Trichantha anisophylla

Trichantha anisophylla, commonly known as the variable-leaf trichantha, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescence.

It is native to parts of Asia and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. The plant exhibits dimorphic leaves, with some leaves being broad and others narrow, contributing to its common name. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in clusters that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
