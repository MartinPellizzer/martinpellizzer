Teucrium bidentatum var. purpureum

Teucrium bidentatum var. purpureum, commonly known as purple germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, soft hairs, and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink to purple in color. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy surface with a fragrant aroma. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens or borders.

This variety is distinguished by its vibrant floral display and aromatic foliage, which contribute to its ornamental value.
