Thymus communis

Thymus communis, commonly known as common thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe, western Asia, and northern Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in early summer. Its leaves are fragrant and contain essential oils, which contribute to its use in culinary and medicinal applications.

Thymus communis thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and versatile plant for gardens and herbal use.
