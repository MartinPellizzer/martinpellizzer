Typha laxmannii

Typha laxmannii, commonly known as narrow-leaved cattail, is a wetland plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and a dense, cylindrical inflorescence that matures into a brown, woolly structure. The plant thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often forming dense colonies in marshes and along the edges of lakes and rivers. Its rhizomatous growth allows it to spread rapidly, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.

The plant plays a vital role in water filtration and provides habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
