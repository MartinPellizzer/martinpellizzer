Terminalia × silozensis

Terminalia × silozensis, commonly known as the African wild mango, is a hybrid species within the genus Terminalia, native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized tree characterized by its smooth, grey-brown bark and large, glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, orange-red fruits that contain a single large seed. Its botanical significance lies in its potential medicinal properties and use in traditional medicine.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitats.
