Tournefortia montana var. montana

Tournefortia montana var. montana, commonly known as the mountain tournefortia, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America, particularly in regions of Brazil and Peru.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and it produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem and a preference for well-drained, sandy soils.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
