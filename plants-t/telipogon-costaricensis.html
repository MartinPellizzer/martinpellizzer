Telipogon costaricensis

Telipogon costaricensis, commonly known as the Costa Rican orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central America, particularly Costa Rica.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories. Its flowers are usually greenish-white with a faint fragrance, and it blooms during the dry season.

Telipogon costaricensis is an important component of the region's biodiversity and is often found in protected natural reserves.
