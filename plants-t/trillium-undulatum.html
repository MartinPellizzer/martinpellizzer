Trillium undulatum

Trillium undulatum, commonly known as the wavy trillium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the family Melanthiaceae and is characterized by its distinctive three-lobed leaves and three-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in rich, well-drained soils. Its blooming period occurs in early spring, producing showy white or pale pink flowers.

Trillium undulatum is also known for its slow growth and long lifespan, sometimes living for several decades.
