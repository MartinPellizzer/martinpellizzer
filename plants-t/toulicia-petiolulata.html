Toulicia petiolulata

Toulicia petiolulata, commonly known as the slender-leafed tamarind, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are often arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by elongated, woody fruits containing edible seeds. Its botanical features make it suitable for ornamental gardening and traditional uses in some regions.

The species is also valued for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in partial shade.
