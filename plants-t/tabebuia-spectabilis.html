Tabebuia spectabilis

Tabebuia spectabilis, commonly known as the royal poinciana, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, feathery, pink or red flower clusters that bloom in the spring. The tree has a rounded canopy and smooth, gray bark that becomes fissured with age. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
