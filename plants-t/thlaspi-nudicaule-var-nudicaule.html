Thlaspi nudicaule var. nudicaule

Thlaspi nudicaule var. nudicaule, commonly known as the naked-stemed cabbageweed, is a low-growing annual plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, erect stems and small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers. The leaves are typically basal, oblanceolate, and hairy, while the plant produces numerous small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

This species is known for its ability to tolerate various soil conditions and is considered a weed in some regions.
