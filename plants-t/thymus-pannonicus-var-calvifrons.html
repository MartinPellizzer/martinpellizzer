Thymus pannonicus var. calvifrons

Thymus pannonicus var. calvifrons, commonly known as the Pannonian thyme, is a perennial herb native to southeastern Europe.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and aromatic, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its essential oils contain thymol and carvacrol, contributing to its medicinal and culinary uses.
