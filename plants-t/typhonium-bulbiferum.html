Typhonium bulbiferum

Typhonium bulbiferum, commonly known as the giant swamp taro, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in diameter, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter, sometimes reddish underside. The plant produces striking, purple-tinged, spadix-like inflorescences surrounded by a broad, white spathe. It thrives in wet, swampy environments and is often found in marshes and along riverbanks.

Its unique appearance and growth habits make it a notable species in botanical gardens and wetland ecosystems.
