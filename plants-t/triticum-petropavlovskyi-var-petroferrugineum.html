Triticum petropavlovskyi var. petroferrugineum

Triticum petropavlovskyi var. petroferrugineum, commonly known as the Petropavlovsk wheat, is a rare and ancient wheat species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its robust growth habit and distinctive awns, which are long, bristle-like structures. The plant typically reaches heights of 1 to 2 meters and produces spikelets with small, tough grains. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture, and it thrives in arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its genetic diversity, it is of interest to researchers studying ancient wheat varieties and their potential for modern agriculture.
