Trichocaulon pedicellatum

Trichocaulon pedicellatum, commonly known as the clustered twayblade, is a small, herbaceous plant belonging to the orchid family (Orchidaceae).

It is characterized by its short, creeping rhizomes and small, grass-like leaves that are often clustered at the base. The plant produces delicate, greenish-white flowers that are typically arranged in a loose cluster. It is native to regions of Asia, including China, India, and Japan, and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological niche, it is often found in forest understories and is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
