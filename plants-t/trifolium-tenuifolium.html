Trifolium tenuifolium

Trifolium tenuifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and is often found in meadows and grassy areas.

Trifolium tenuifolium is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
