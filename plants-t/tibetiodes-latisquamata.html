Tibetiodes latisquamata

Tibetiodes latisquamata, commonly known as the broad-scaled tibetan bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Tibetan Plateau.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with prominent veins and is often found growing in rocky, well-drained soils. It is adapted to cold climates and can thrive at elevations above 3,000 meters.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
