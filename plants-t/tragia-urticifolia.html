Tragia urticifolia

Tragia urticifolia, commonly known as the stinging bristleweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa, the Americas, and Asia.

It is a shrub or herbaceous plant characterized by its hairy stems and leaves that can cause skin irritation. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

Tragia urticifolia is known for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine, though it can be toxic if ingested in large quantities.
