Tulipa sharonensis

Tulipa sharonensis, commonly known as Sharon's tulip, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Turkey.

It is a bulbous perennial plant that produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically blooms in early spring and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, thriving in temperate climates.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
