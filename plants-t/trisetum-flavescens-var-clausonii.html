Trisetum flavescens var. clausonii

Trisetum flavescens var. clausonii, commonly known as the yellow hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It typically grows in wet meadows, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant features slender, erect stems that are often covered in a fine, yellowish or golden hue, giving it its common name. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a soft, silky texture.

The inflorescence consists of a loose panicle of spikelets, which produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.
