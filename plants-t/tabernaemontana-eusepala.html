Tabernaemontana eusepala

Tabernaemontana eusepala, commonly known as the white star jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, star-shaped white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and pleasant scent. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Tabernaemontana eusepala is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
