Thymus praecox f. borosianus

Thymus praecox f. borosianus, commonly known as the Borosian thyme, is a subspecies of thyme native to Central Europe.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with aromatic, needle-like leaves and small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders. Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice in horticulture.

It is also valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, similar to other thyme species.
