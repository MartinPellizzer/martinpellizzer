Taurantha concinna

Taurantha concinna, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is a deciduous vine that thrives in full sun and well-drained soil. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate with a smooth texture.

Taurantha concinna is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
