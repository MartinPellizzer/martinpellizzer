Taraxacum macrocentrum

Taraxacum macrocentrum, commonly known as the giant dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its large, spoon-shaped leaves and a tall, hollow stem that supports its yellow flower head. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, where it thrives in open, disturbed soils. Its flowers produce wind-dispersed seeds, contributing to its widespread distribution.

The species is often found in meadows, roadsides, and other sunny habitats.
