Tacca breviloba

Tacca breviloba, commonly known as the "bat plant" or "devil's tongue," is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, velvety leaves and a unique, fleshy, club-shaped inflorescence that resembles a bat's face. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, rich soil to flourish. Its flowers, which are typically greenish-yellow with red veins, are pollinated by flies and other insects.

Tacca breviloba is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is considered a rare and exotic species in horticulture.
