Trigonella gontscharovii

Trigonella gontscharovii, commonly known as the Crimean cowpea, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly found in Turkey, Greece, and the Crimean Peninsula.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces lens-shaped seeds that are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Trigonella gontscharovii is typically found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to thrive in arid or semi-arid environments.
