Teucrium flavicans

Teucrium flavicans, commonly known as the yellow germander, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant features slender, erect stems covered in fine hairs and produces small, tubular flowers in shades of yellow and purple. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly serrated edge.

Teucrium flavicans is often found in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open woodlands.
