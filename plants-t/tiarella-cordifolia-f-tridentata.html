Tiarella cordifolia f. tridentata

Tiarella cordifolia f. tridentata, commonly known as the tridentate foamflower, is a variant of the foamflower plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with three distinct lobes, giving it the "tridentate" designation. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers in the spring, which are borne on short, hairy stalks. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental foliage and is often used in woodland gardens.
