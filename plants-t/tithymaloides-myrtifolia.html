Tithymaloides myrtifolia

Tithymaloides myrtifolia, commonly known as the myrtle-leaved tithymaloides, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, often found in the fynbos biome. The plant has small, narrow leaves with a leathery texture and produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. It is adapted to withstand drought and salt spray, making it a hardy species in coastal environments.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and birds, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
