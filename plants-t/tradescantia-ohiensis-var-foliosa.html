Tradescantia ohiensis var. foliosa

Tradescantia ohiensis var. foliosa, commonly known as the Ohiensis Tradescantia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, waxy blue-violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
