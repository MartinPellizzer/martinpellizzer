Trollius europaeus albiflorus

Trollius europaeus albiflorus, commonly known as the white globeflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its showy, white, globular inflorescence composed of numerous small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, nutrient-rich soils in meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it blooms from late summer to early autumn.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
