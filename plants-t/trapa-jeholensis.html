Trapa jeholensis

Trapa jeholensis, commonly known as the Jehol water chestnut, is a aquatic plant native to China.

It is characterized by its floating leaves and distinctive, spiny fruits that contain edible seeds. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a waxy surface that helps repel water.

Trapa jeholensis plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
