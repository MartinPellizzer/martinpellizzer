Trachelium caeruleum f. isabellinum

Trachelium caeruleum f. isabellinum, commonly known as the Isabelline Bluebell, is a compact, flowering shrub native to South Africa.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green color. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue, violet, or white, depending on the cultivar. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
