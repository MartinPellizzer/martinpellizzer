Trifolium kochianum

Trifolium kochianum, commonly known as the Koch's clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or white in color. It is native to parts of Europe and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

Trifolium kochianum is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
