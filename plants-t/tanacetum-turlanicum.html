Tanacetum turlanicum

Tanacetum turlanicum, commonly known as the Turkish tansy, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that are often mistaken for dandelions. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or arid environments.

Tanacetum turlanicum is also valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
