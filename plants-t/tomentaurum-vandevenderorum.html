Tomentaurum vandevenderorum

Tomentaurum vandevenderorum, commonly known as the velvetleaf orchid, is a unique flowering plant characterized by its velvety, hairy leaves and striking, showy blooms.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is native to specific regions in South America. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are usually a mix of purple and white, with a distinctive, intricate structure.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern in its native range.
