Tagetes filifolia

Tagetes filifolia, commonly known as the threadleaf marigold, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, thread-like leaves and vibrant yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in full sun and well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Tagetes filifolia is also known for its ability to repel certain insects, making it a valuable addition to garden landscapes.
