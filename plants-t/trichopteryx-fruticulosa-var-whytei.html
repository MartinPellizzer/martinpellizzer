Trichopteryx fruticulosa var. whytei

Trichopteryx fruticulosa var. whytei, commonly known as the Whyte's thornbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers, which are typically visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a capsule that releases seeds through mechanical means, aiding in its dispersal.

This species is well adapted to dry conditions and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
