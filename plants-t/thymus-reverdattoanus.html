Thymus reverdattoanus

Thymus reverdattoanus, commonly known as the Sicilian thyme, is a perennial herb native to the island of Sicily.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic leaves and square stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun. Its small, pinkish-white flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Thymus reverdattoanus is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, similar to other thyme species.
