Taraxacum latilimbatum

Taraxacum latilimbatum, commonly known as the broad-leafed dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, spoon-shaped leaves and a hollow, leafless stem that supports a flowering head. The flowers are typically yellow and form a composite head composed of many small florets. It is native to Europe and western Asia, often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
