Trichanthecium distichophyllum

Trichanthecium distichophyllum, commonly known as the two-ranked grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves arranged in two ranks, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny flowers, typical of many grass species. It is native to certain regions of Africa and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
