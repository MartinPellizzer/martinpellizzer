Tsuga chinensis var. tchekiangensis

Tsuga chinensis var. tchekiangensis, commonly known as the Chinese fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to eastern China.

It is characterized by its needle-like leaves and conical shape, with a moderate growth rate. The tree produces cones that are typically 3 to 5 centimeters long, containing winged seeds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
