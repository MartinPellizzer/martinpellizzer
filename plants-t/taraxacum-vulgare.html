Taraxacum vulgare

Taraxacum vulgare, commonly known as the common dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated, hairy leaves and a hollow, leafy stem that supports a flower head. The flower head consists of numerous yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular florets. After blooming, the plant produces a distinctive seed dispersal mechanism known as a pappus, which allows the seeds to be carried by the wind.

This plant is widely distributed across temperate regions and is often considered a weed, though it has historical uses in medicine and cuisine.
