Tropaeolum tricolor var. grandiflorum

Tropaeolum tricolor var. grandiflorum, commonly known as the "Nasturtium," is a vibrant annual plant native to South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive red or orange coloration, often contrasting with its bright yellow or orange flowers. The plant produces showy, edible flowers and leaves that are commonly used in culinary applications. Its rapid growth habit and ability to thrive in various soil types make it a popular choice for gardeners.

The plant is also known for its ability to attract beneficial insects like ladybugs and bees.
