Thalictrum banchianum

Thalictrum banchianum, commonly known as the Banchi's meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and meadows. Its leaves are compound, with finely divided leaflets that give it a delicate, lace-like appearance.

Thalictrum banchianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and subtle floral display.
