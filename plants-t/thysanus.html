Thysanus

Thysanus, commonly known as the flame flower, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, often resembling flames. The plant belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is known for its showy, long-lasting blooms. Thysanus typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters.

Its foliage consists of opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth texture and a glossy appearance.
