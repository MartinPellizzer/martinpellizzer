Tulestea seretii

Tulestea seretii, commonly known as the sacred lotus of the Andes, is a striking aquatic plant native to the high-altitude lakes of South America.

It features large, showy flowers that bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with a striking contrast between the petals and the deep green leaves. The plant thrives in still, nutrient-rich waters and is adapted to the cool, high-altitude climate of its native habitat. Its roots are fibrous and help stabilize the lakebed, while its leaves provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Tulestea seretii is culturally significant to indigenous communities and is often associated with spiritual and medicinal practices.
