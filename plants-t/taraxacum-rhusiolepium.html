Taraxacum rhusiolepium

Taraxacum rhusiolepium, commonly known as the Mexican dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and serrated leaves. The plant produces a fluffy seed head that is dispersed by the wind, a common trait among dandelions. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Taraxacum rhusiolepium is often found in open areas such as fields and disturbed sites.
