Teline linifolia var. angustifolia

Teline linifolia var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf teline, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, angular stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale blue or white flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
