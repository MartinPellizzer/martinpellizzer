Trifolium phitosianum

Trifolium phitosianum, commonly known as the Phitosian clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged symmetrically. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is native to specific regions in southern Africa and thrives in well-drained soils.

Trifolium phitosianum is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
