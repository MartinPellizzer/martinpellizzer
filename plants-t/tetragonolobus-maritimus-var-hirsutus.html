Tetragonolobus maritimus var. hirsutus

Tetragonolobus maritimus var. hirsutus, commonly known as the hairy coastal lupin, is a perennial legume native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, erect stems and trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to saline and coastal environments.

This variety is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability and potential use in ecological restoration projects.
