Trembleya parviflora var. farinacea

Trembleya parviflora var. farinacea, commonly known as the farinaceous trembleya, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are typically elliptical and covered in a fine, powdery coating, giving them a distinctive appearance. This variety is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its delicate foliage and subtle floral display.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for gardens in temperate climates.
