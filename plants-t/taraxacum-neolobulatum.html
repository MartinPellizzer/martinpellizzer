Taraxacum neolobulatum

Taraxacum neolobulatum, commonly known as the New Zealand dandelion, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant features a rosette of basal, hairy leaves and a hollow stem that supports a single, showy yellow flower head. Its seeds are dispersed by wind via parachute-like structures called pappi.

This species is adapted to a variety of habitats, including grasslands and disturbed soils, and is often found in coastal areas.
