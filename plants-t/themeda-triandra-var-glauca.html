Themeda triandra var. glauca

Themeda triandra var. glauca, commonly known as the grey kangaroo grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, tussock-like growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, which are typically pale green to brown in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
