Thymus balcanus var. micevskii

Thymus balcanus var. micevskii, commonly known as Balkan thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its aromatic, small, needle-like leaves and clusters of pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its medicinal and culinary uses.

Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and as a ground cover in dry, well-drained environments.
