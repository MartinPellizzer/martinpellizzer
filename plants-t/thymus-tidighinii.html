Thymus tidighinii

Thymus tidighinii, commonly known as the Tidig hill thyme, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. It is valued for its essential oils, which are used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine.

Thymus tidighinii is also an important plant for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
