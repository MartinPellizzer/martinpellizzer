Teucrium laxum

Teucrium laxum, commonly known as the slender germander, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. The plant features slender, branched stems and small, opposite leaves that are usually gray-green in color. Its flowers are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences and range in color from pink to purple.

Teucrium laxum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
