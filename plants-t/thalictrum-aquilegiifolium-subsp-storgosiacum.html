Thalictrum aquilegiifolium subsp. storgosiacum

Thalictrum aquilegiifolium subsp.

storgosiacum, commonly known as the Storgosia thalictrum, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to specific regions of Europe. It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like foliage and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its leaves are compound, with finely divided segments that contribute to its ornamental appeal.

It is valued in horticulture for its early spring bloom and ability to thrive in partial shade.
