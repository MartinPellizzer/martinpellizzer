Turraea lanceolata

Turraea lanceolata, commonly known as the African sandalwood, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are lanceolate in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small and yellowish-green, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, round fruits that contain a single seed.

Turraea lanceolata is valued for its aromatic wood, which is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
