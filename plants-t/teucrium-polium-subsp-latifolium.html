Teucrium polium subsp. latifolium

Teucrium polium subsp.

latifolium, commonly known as broadleaf germander, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It features aromatic, lanceolate to ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in terminal or axillary clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its medicinal properties have been historically recognized in traditional herbal remedies.
