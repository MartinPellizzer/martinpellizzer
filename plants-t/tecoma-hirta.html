Tecoma hirta

Tecoma hirta, commonly known as the silverleaf crownflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces small, woody pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Tecoma hirta thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as savannas and rocky areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional medicinal uses in some cultures.
