Thymus glabrescens subsp. urumovii

Thymus glabrescens subsp.

urumovii, commonly known as the Turkmenian thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and aromatic, narrow leaves.

The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

This subspecies is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, similar to other thyme species.
