Thymus groenlandicus

Thymus groenlandicus, commonly known as Greenland thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It features small, compact leaves and produces clusters of tiny, pale pink to white flowers. The plant is adapted to cold climates and thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils. It is often found in tundra and alpine environments, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.

Thymus groenlandicus is also valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
