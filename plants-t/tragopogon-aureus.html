Tragopogon aureus

Tragopogon aureus, commonly known as the golden goat's beard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features bright yellow ray flowers surrounding a central disk of smaller florets, which are typically tubular. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained soils in sunny locations.

Tragopogon aureus is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides.
