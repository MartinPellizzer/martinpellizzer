Tritonia Flabellifolia

Tritonia flabellifolia is a rare and striking plant native to the mountainous regions of South America. Known for its vibrant purple flowers and unique leaf structure, it stands out in its natural habitat. This species belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is often referred to as the "bellflower" due to its bell-shaped blooms. Due to habitat loss and over-collection, Tritonia flabellifolia is now considered endangered. Conservation efforts are underway to protect this beautiful and delicate plant from extinction.
