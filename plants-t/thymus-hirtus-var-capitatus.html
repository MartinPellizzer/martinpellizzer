Thymus hirtus var. capitatus

Thymus hirtus var. capitatus, commonly known as mountain thyme, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features compact, shrub-like growth with small, aromatic leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces dense clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers during the spring and early summer. Its foliage is often used in culinary applications due to its strong, pungent aroma.

Botanically, it belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and opposite leaves.
