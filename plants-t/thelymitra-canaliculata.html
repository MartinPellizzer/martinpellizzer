Thelymitra canaliculata

Thelymitra canaliculata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved helmet orchid, is an epiphytic plant native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive helmet-shaped labellum. The plant typically grows on tree trunks or branches, deriving moisture and nutrients from the air and rain. Its leaves are narrow and strap-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

Flowering occurs in spring, producing spikes of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers.
