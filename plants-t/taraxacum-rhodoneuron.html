Taraxacum rhodoneuron

Taraxacum rhodoneuron, commonly known as the red-seeded dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into fluffy, white seed heads, which are easily dispersed by the wind. Its leaves are basal, alternate, and have a distinctive wavy margin.

The species is notable for its red-tinged seeds, which give it its common name and distinguish it from other dandelion species.
