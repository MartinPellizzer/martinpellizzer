Tragacantha lepsensis

Tragacantha lepsensis, commonly known as the Lepsen's tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of the Middle East.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant produces a gum resin, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive.

Tragacantha lepsensis thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils and open woodlands.
