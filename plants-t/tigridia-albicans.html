Tigridia albicans

Tigridia albicans, commonly known as the white tigrid lily, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces striking, upward-facing blooms with a distinctive red or purple stripe down the center. It has linear, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Tigridia albicans is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
