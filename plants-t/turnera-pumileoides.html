Turnera pumileoides

Turnera pumileoides, commonly known as the dwarf turnera, is a low-growing shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, bloom throughout the year, attracting various pollinators.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in tropical gardens.
