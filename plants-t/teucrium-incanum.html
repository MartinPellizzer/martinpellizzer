Teucrium incanum

Teucrium incanum, commonly known as white germander, is a perennial herb native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pale blue to white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It is adapted to drought conditions and thrives in well-drained soils.

Botanically, it belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs.
