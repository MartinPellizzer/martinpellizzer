Thymus richardii subsp. ebusitanus

Thymus richardii subsp.

ebusitanus, commonly known as the Ebusitan thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and square stems.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, often in open woodland or scrub habitats. Its small, pinkish-white flowers appear in clusters during the spring and summer months.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
