Tillandsia paleacea subsp. apurimacensis

Tillandsia paleacea subsp.

apurimacensis, commonly known as the Apurimac air plant, is a bromeliad native to the high-altitude regions of Peru. It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on other plants without deriving nutrients from them.

The species is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help in absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air. It thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
