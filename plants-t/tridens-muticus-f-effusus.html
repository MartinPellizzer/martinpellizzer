Tridens muticus f. effusus

Tridens muticus f. effusus, commonly known as the soft-haired grass or velvet grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy texture on the leaves and stems, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate regions and is often found in meadows or along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a spikelet with small, spike-like flowers.

Tridens muticus f. effusus is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
