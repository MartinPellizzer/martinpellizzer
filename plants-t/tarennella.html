Tarennella

Tarennella, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a genus of flowering plants native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique and often striking floral structures. The plants typically have sympodial growth habits, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Tarennella species are known for their showy, colorful flowers that bloom in various shades of pink, red, and white.
