Tripleurospermum aromaticum

Tripleurospermum aromaticum, commonly known as the sweet everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, herbaceous growth habit and produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant is known for its aromatic foliage, which emits a subtle, pleasant scent. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its flowers are often used in floral arrangements due to their long-lasting nature and delicate appearance.
