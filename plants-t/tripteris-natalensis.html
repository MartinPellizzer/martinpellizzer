Tripteris natalensis

Tripteris natalensis, commonly known as Natal cycad, is a unique member of the Cycadaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces cones, with male and female cones typically occurring on separate plants. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires a warm, dry climate.

Due to its slow growth and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
