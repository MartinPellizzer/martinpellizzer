Thelesperma ambiguum

Thelesperma ambiguum, commonly known as the plains milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces yellow flowers in clusters during the spring and early summer. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in arid and rocky soils.

Thelesperma ambiguum is adapted to drought conditions and plays a role in stabilizing sandy or gravelly soils in its native habitat.
