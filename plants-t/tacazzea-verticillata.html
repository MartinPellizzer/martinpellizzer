Tacazzea verticillata

Tacazzea verticillata, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers during the summer months. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or grassland habitats. Its flowering period attracts various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Tacazzea verticillata is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and easy maintenance.
