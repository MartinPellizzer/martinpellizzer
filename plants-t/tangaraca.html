Tangaraca

Tangaraca, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its velvety leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as a perennial herb, often forming a rosette of foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Tangaraca is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
