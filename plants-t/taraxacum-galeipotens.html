Taraxacum galeipotens

Taraxacum galeipotens, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and a yellow flowering head that develops into a puffy white pappus. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant is known for its medicinal properties, with historical use in traditional remedies.

Its life cycle includes a winter dormancy period, followed by spring growth and flowering.
