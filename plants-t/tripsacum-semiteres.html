Tripsacum semiteres

Tripsacum semiteres, commonly known as the narrowleaf tripesac, is a wild grass native to South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, sword-like leaves and compact panicle inflorescence. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in disturbed or open habitats. Its seeds are small and wind-dispersed, contributing to its natural distribution.

Tripsacum semiteres is of interest to botanists due to its genetic relationship to maize and its potential role in plant evolution.
