Turnera peruviana

Turnera peruviana, commonly known as the "Peruvian turnera," is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

The flowers are pollinated by insects, and the plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and traditional medicinal uses.
