Toona calantas

Toona calantas, commonly known as the white toona, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Malvaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its growth habit makes it suitable for both landscaping and reforestation projects.
