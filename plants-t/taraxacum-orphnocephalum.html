Taraxacum orphnocephalum

Taraxacum orphnocephalum, commonly known as the mountain dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of stiff, hairy leaves and a flowering stem that bears a single, yellow composite flower head. The plant is native to alpine regions of Europe and is adapted to grow in rocky, well-drained soils. It produces a distinctive, hollow stem and has a fibrous root system.

The species is often found in open, sunny habitats at high elevations.
