Taraxacum abundans

Taraxacum abundans, commonly known as the common dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of stiff, hairy leaves and a hollow, leafless stem that supports a flower head. The plant produces a distinctive seed head composed of numerous parachute-like seeds, which are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including lawns, meadows, and disturbed soils.

Taraxacum abundans is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its diuretic and anti-inflammatory effects.
