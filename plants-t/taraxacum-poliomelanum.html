Taraxacum poliomelanum

Taraxacum poliomelanum, commonly known as the black-veined dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, dark green leaves with prominent black veins. The plant produces a single, hollow, hairless stem that terminates in a yellowish flower head composed of numerous small florets. It is typically found in moist, calcareous soils across Europe and parts of Asia.

Due to its distinctive appearance and ecological role, it is often considered a rare and noteworthy species in its native habitats.
