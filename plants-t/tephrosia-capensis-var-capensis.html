Tephrosia capensis var. capensis

Tephrosia capensis var. capensis, commonly known as the Cape tephrosia, is a perennial leguminous shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit, trifoliate leaves, and small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated, seed-coated pods that mature to a reddish-brown color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

The species is known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it valuable in soil enrichment.
