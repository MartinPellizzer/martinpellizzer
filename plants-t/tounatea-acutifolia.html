Tounatea acutifolia

Tounatea acutifolia, commonly known as the sharp-leaved tounatea, is a member of the Arecaceae family, which includes palms.

It is characterized by its slender, erect trunk and pinnately compound leaves that are long and narrow with sharp, pointed tips. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, followed by dark, spherical fruits. Native to tropical regions of South America, it thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
