Trachysperma

Trachysperma, commonly known as wild thyme, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its aromatic, creeping growth habit and small, tubular flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple or white. The leaves are usually small, hairy, and emit a strong, minty fragrance when crushed. Trachysperma species are native to regions of southern Europe, North Africa, and the Middle East.

These plants are often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb due to their aromatic properties.
