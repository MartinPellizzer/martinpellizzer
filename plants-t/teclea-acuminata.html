Teclea acuminata

Teclea acuminata, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its velvety, broad leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as a perennial herb, often forming a compact rosette structure. Its flowers are usually purple or violet in color, with five petals and a prominent corona.

Teclea acuminata is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
