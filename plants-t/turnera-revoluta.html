Turnera revoluta

Turnera revoluta, commonly known as the Mexican flame flower, is a shrub native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It features bright orange to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Turnera revoluta is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
