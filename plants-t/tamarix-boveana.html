Tamarix boveana

Tamarix boveana, commonly known as the Bovean tamarisk, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, pink or red flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are scale-like and often appear in clusters, reducing water loss in hot, dry climates.

Tamarix boveana is known for its ability to thrive in saline soils and is sometimes used for soil stabilization in degraded landscapes.
