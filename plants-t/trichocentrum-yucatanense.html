Trichocentrum yucatanense

Trichocentrum yucatanense, commonly known as the Yucatan orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Yucatan Peninsula in Mexico.

It features a sympodial growth habit with pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowering stems. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, often arranged in racemes. Its roots are adapted to thrive in the limestone soils of its native habitat.

Trichocentrum yucatanense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
