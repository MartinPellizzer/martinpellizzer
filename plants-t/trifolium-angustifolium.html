Trifolium angustifolium

Trifolium angustifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that are clustered in spherical heads. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grasslands.

Trifolium angustifolium is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in ecological and agricultural contexts.
