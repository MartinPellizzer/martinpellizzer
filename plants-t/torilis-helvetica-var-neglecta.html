Torilis helvetica var. neglecta

Torilis helvetica var. neglecta, commonly known as the European hedge mustard, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing herb with slender, branched stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round, hairy fruits called siliques, which contain several seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a common weed in various habitats across Europe.
