Thunbergia capensis

Thunbergia capensis, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a climbing plant native to South Africa.

It features opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a velvety texture. The plant produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, which bloom in clusters. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Thunbergia capensis is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to climb trellises or structures.
