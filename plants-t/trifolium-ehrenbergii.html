Trifolium ehrenbergii

Trifolium ehrenbergii, commonly known as the Mexican clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Trifolium ehrenbergii is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing abilities, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
