Trichopilia marginata var. lepida

Trichopilia marginata var. lepida, commonly known as the silverleaf trichopilia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features slender, upright stems that are covered in a dense layer of silvery-white hairs, giving it its characteristic appearance. The plant produces small, pale green to white flowers arranged in loose clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and also covered in fine, silvery hairs, contributing to its ornamental value.

It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in rocky or coastal environments.
