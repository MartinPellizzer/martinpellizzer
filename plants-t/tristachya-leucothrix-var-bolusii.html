Tristachya leucothrix var. bolusii

Tristachya leucothrix var. bolusii, commonly known as the Bolus sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white to pale green flowers in dense, spike-like clusters. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in water gardens and bog gardens.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
