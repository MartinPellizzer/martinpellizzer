Tagetes lunulata

Tagetes lunulata, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to yellow flowers with a distinctive white or pale yellow center. The plant is native to Mexico and parts of Central America, where it thrives in sunny, well-drained soils. Its foliage is typically green with a slightly rough texture, and it produces numerous showy blooms from summer to fall.

Tagetes lunulata is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and is also used in traditional medicine.
