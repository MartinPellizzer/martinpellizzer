Tetranthera foliosa

Tetranthera foliosa, commonly known as the golden star vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, making it a striking addition to gardens. The plant has small, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Tetranthera foliosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
