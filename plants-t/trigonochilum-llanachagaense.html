Trigonochilum llanachagaense

Trigonochilum llanachagaense, commonly known as the Llanachaga orchid, is a rare and endemic orchid species native to the Llanachaga forest in eastern Zambia.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring three sepals and three petals that are often brightly colored. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, thriving in the understory of tropical rainforests. Its flowers are pollinated by specific insect species, highlighting its ecological niche within the ecosystem.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species, emphasizing the need for conservation efforts.
