Turpinia grandis

Turpinia grandis, commonly known as the Chinese sand cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces white to pinkish flowers in clusters, which bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. Its fruits are small, red drupes that ripen in late summer and are edible.

Turpinia grandis is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive form and seasonal interest.
