Turnera diamantinae

Turnera diamantinae, commonly known as the Diamondleaf Turnera, is a species of flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Turneraceae and is characterized by its distinctive, diamond-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically borne in clusters. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
