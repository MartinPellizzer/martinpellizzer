Thelymitra pulchella

Thelymitra pulchella, commonly known as the golden sun-orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in open forests and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are strap-shaped and often have a reddish tint, contrasting with the bright flowers.

Thelymitra pulchella is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in horticulture.
