Thymbra caroliniana

Thymbra caroliniana, commonly known as Carolina thyme, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features aromatic, gray-green foliage with small, needle-like leaves that emit a strong, pungent scent when crushed. The plant produces tiny white or pale pink flowers in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for both ornamental and ecological purposes.
