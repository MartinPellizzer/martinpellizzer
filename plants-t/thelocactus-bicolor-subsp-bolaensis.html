Thelocactus bicolor subsp. bolaensis

Thelocactus bicolor subsp.

bolaensis, commonly known as the Bolaensis thorn cactus, is a unique cactus species native to the arid regions of northern Mexico. It is characterized by its distinctive yellow and green spines, which are often longer than the body of the plant.

The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking contrast to its rugged appearance. Its stems are cylindrical and covered in a thick layer of wax, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

This subspecies is adapted to extreme desert conditions, making it a resilient and fascinating example of succulent flora.
