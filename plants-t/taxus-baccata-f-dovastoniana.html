Taxus baccata f. dovastoniana

Taxus baccata f. dovastoniana, commonly known as the Dovastoniana form of the European yew, is a rare and cultivated variant of the common yew.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, featuring narrow, dark green leaves with a slight sheen. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

This form is prized for its unique appearance and is typically found in botanical gardens and specialized collections.
