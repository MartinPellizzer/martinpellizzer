Trichochloa gracilis

Trichochloa gracilis, commonly known as the slender hairgrass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often found in grasslands and along water bodies. The plant has slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, each bearing small, two-lobed flowers.

Trichochloa gracilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
