Tragia chamaelea

Tragia chamaelea, commonly known as the red sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and clusters of small, pink to red flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Tragia chamaelea is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of desert flora.
