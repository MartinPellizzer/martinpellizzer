Trigonella radiata var. incisa

Trigonella radiata var. incisa, commonly known as the narrowleaf fenugreek, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, branched stems and trifoliate leaves with lance-shaped lobes. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by elongated, slender pods containing kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

This variety is valued for its aromatic seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
