Trichilia areolata

Trichilia areolata, commonly known as the African sandalwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae, which includes mahogany and related hardwoods. The tree is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves with a slightly serrated margin. Its fragrant flowers are yellowish-green and appear in clusters, followed by small, round fruits containing flat, winged seeds.

The wood is highly valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
