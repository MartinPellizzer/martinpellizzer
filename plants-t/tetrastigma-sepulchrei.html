Tetrastigma sepulchrei

Tetrastigma sepulchrei, commonly known as the Vietnamese grapevine, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its tendrils and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are often followed by berries. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
