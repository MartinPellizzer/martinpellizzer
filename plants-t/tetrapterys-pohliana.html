Tetrapterys pohliana

Tetrapterys pohliana, commonly known as the Brazilian sandpapertree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and elliptical leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. Tetrapterys pohliana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.

It prefers well-drained soils and is adapted to humid, tropical climates.
