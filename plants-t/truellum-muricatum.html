Truellum muricatum

Truellum muricatum, commonly known as the Andean mountain morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from violet to blue. The plant is a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and produces showy blooms that open in the early morning. It thrives in cool, humid climates and is often found in high-altitude forests.

Truellum muricatum is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its traditional use in local cultures.
