Thymus granatensis subsp. granatensis

Thymus granatensis subsp.

granatensis, commonly known as the Granada thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and aromatic, narrow leaves that are gray-green in color.

The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is often cultivated for both horticultural and culinary purposes.
