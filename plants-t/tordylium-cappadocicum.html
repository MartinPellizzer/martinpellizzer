Tordylium cappadocicum

Tordylium cappadocicum, commonly known as the Cappadocian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Cappadocia region of Turkey.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or limestone-rich habitats.

Tordylium cappadocicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
