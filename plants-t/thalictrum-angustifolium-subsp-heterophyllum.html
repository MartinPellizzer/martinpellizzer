Thalictrum angustifolium subsp. heterophyllum

Thalictrum angustifolium subsp.

heterophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America. It features delicate, fern-like leaves that are typically narrow and arranged in a basal rosette.

The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, which are borne on slender, branching stems. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and valuable component of native plant communities.
