Tragacantha schirasica

Tragacantha schirasica, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Iran and Afghanistan.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pale yellow flowers. The plant is notable for producing a resinous gum, which has been traditionally used in various medicinal and industrial applications.

Its ability to thrive in harsh, dry environments makes it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
