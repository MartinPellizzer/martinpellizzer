Tinus tomentosa

Tinus tomentosa, commonly known as the silver tree, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
