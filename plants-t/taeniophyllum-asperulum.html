Taeniophyllum asperulum

Taeniophyllum asperulum, commonly known as the rough leaved twayblade, is a small, herbaceous plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that typically bloom in late spring or early summer. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of other plants without deriving nutrients from them. It thrives in shaded, moist environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its unique morphology and ecological niche make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
