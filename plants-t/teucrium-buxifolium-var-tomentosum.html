Teucrium buxifolium var. tomentosum

Teucrium buxifolium var. tomentosum, commonly known as the woolly germander, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, lance-shaped leaves that are densely covered in fine, white hairs, giving the plant a soft, fuzzy appearance. The flowers are typically pink to purple, arranged in compact clusters, and bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

The plant is also valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
