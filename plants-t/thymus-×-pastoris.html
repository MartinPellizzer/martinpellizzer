Thymus × pastoris

Thymus × pastoris, commonly known as hybrid thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Thymus serpyllum (wild thyme) and Thymus vulgaris (common thyme). The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, purple, or white, typically blooming in early summer. Its leaves are narrow, gray-green, and covered in fine hairs, which contribute to its aromatic properties.

Thymus × pastoris is often cultivated for its ornamental value and use in culinary and medicinal applications.
