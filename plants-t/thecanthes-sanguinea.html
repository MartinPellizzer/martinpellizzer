Thecanthes sanguinea

Thecanthes sanguinea, commonly known as the bloodroot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features a basal rosette of dark green, waxy leaves and produces a single, striking red-orange flower with a yellow center. The plant's name derives from the reddish sap that exudes from its roots when cut. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and stream banks.

Thecanthes sanguinea is known for its early spring bloom and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
