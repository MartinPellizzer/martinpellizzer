Telipogon polyrrhizus

Telipogon polyrrhizus, commonly known as the hairy tamarisk, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Myrtaceae.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and is native to Australia. The plant features small, pink or white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Telipogon polyrrhizus is adapted to arid environments and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.
