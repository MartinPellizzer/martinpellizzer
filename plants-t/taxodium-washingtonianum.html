Taxodium washingtonianum

Taxodium washingtonianum, commonly known as the Tupelo gum tree, is a deciduous conifer native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and broad, spreading crown, which provides habitat for various wildlife species. The tree produces cone-like structures that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind and animals. Its bark is thick and scaly, offering protection against wildfires and insect infestations.

The leaves are needle-like and turn a striking red-brown color in the fall, contributing to its ornamental value.
