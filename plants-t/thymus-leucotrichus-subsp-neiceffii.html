Thymus leucotrichus subsp. neiceffii

Thymus leucotrichus subsp.

neiceffii, commonly known as the white thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It features compact, creeping stems covered in small, aromatic leaves that are typically gray-green in color.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range from white to pale pink, often blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its essential oils contain thymol and carvacrol, giving it a strong, peppery aroma and medicinal properties.
