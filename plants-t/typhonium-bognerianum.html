Typhonium bognerianum

Typhonium bognerianum, commonly known as the giant swamp taro, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in diameter, often with a waxy surface and deep green coloration. The plant produces striking purple or red spadices, which are its inflorescences, surrounded by broad, white or green bracts. It thrives in wet, swampy environments and is often found in marshes and along riverbanks.

Botanically, it belongs to the Araceae family, known for its unique floral structures and aquatic adaptations.
