Thesium leandrianum

Thesium leandrianum, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, herbaceous perennial that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant features wiry stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences and are usually pink or purple in color.

Thesium leandrianum is adapted to arid environments and is often found in coastal or inland dune habitats.
