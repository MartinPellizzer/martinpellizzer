Turpinia punctata

Turpinia punctata, commonly known as the Chinese hawthorn, is a small evergreen shrub native to China.

It features simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptic to ovate in shape and have a rough texture. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional Chinese medicine.

The species is relatively drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils.
