Terminalia nitidissima

Terminalia nitidissima, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, often glossy bark and lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, white, fragrant flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing flat, winged seeds. It is valued for its hard, durable wood used in furniture and construction.

The plant is also noted for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
