Tragopogon ketzkhovelii

Tragopogon ketzkhovelii, commonly known as the Ketzkhovel thistle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climatic conditions. Its leaves are usually basal, with a rough, hairy texture, and it produces yellow ray flowers that bloom in the summer.

Tragopogon ketzkhovelii is noted for its ecological significance in supporting local pollinators and contributing to the biodiversity of its habitat.
