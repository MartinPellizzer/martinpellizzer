Trichosporum grandiflorum

Trichosporum grandiflorum, commonly known as the large-flowered trichosporum, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in the spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are often white or pale pink in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
