Tabernaemontana cerea

Tabernaemontana cerea, commonly known as the white star jasmine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves and a woody stem structure. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Tabernaemontana cerea is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and pleasant aroma.
