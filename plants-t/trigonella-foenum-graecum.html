Trigonella foenum-graecum

Trigonella foenum-graecum, commonly known as fenugreek, is an annual herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers that develop into pods containing several seeds. The plant is characterized by its distinctive triangular seed pods, which give it its scientific name. Fenugreek is widely cultivated for its seeds, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant in both traditional and modern agricultural practices.
