Taraxacum berthae

Taraxacum berthae, commonly known as the Berthae dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to the western United States, particularly found in California and Nevada. This plant is characterized by its yellow composite flower head and heart-shaped leaves, typical of the dandelion genus. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

The plant is considered a weed but has ecological significance in supporting pollinators and maintaining soil stability.
