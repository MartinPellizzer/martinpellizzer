Tragopyrum frutescens

Tragopyrum frutescens, commonly known as goat's beard, is a flowering plant native to the northern regions of the Americas.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. The plant features a basal rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves and produces yellowish-green to brown flowers in late summer. Its seed heads are distinctive, resembling a cluster of small, spiky structures.

Tragopyrum frutescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
