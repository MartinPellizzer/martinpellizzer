Tetrastigma formosanum

Tetrastigma formosanum, commonly known as the Taiwan grapevine, is a woody vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Its botanical features include tendrils for support and a complex root system that helps it thrive in shaded environments.

Tetrastigma formosanum is also noted for its potential in horticulture and ecological restoration projects.
