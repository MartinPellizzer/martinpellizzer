Taraxacum atroplumbeum

Taraxacum atroplumbeum, commonly known as the black-veined dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central and eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant features a basal rosette of dark green, hairy leaves with prominent, dark veins. Its flowers are yellow and form a single, short-lived bloom, followed by a distinctive spherical seed head.

It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.
