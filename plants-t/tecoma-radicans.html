Tecoma radicans

Tecoma radicans, commonly known as the yellow bells, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its bright yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, woody pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Tecoma radicans thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its rapid growth and ability to tolerate drought conditions contribute to its adaptability in various environments.
