Trembleya laniflora var. acutifolia

Trembleya laniflora var. acutifolia, commonly known as the silky trembleya, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks. Its foliage has a velvety texture, which gives it its common name.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
