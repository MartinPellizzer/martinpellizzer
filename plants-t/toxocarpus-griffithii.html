Toxocarpus griffithii

Toxocarpus griffithii, commonly known as the Kashmir carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of northern India and Pakistan.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, often at elevations above 2,000 meters. The plant features erect, hairy stems and produces showy, pink to red flowers with five petals. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

Toxocarpus griffithii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in cooler climates.
