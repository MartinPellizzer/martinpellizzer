Typha persica

Typha persica, commonly known as Persian cattail, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical flowering spike that emerges from a thick, mat-like base of broad leaves. The plant thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its rhizomes are extensively branched and help it spread vegetatively.

The leaves are fibrous and can be used for various traditional purposes, including making paper and thatch.
