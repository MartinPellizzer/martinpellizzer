Trientalis europaea var. americana

Trientalis europaea var. americana, commonly known as the American starflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its star-shaped, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, aiding in its propagation.
