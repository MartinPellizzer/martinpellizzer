Thibaudia myrtifolia

Thibaudia myrtifolia, commonly known as the silky pear, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance and fragrance.
