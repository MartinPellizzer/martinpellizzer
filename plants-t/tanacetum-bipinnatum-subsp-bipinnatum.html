Tanacetum bipinnatum subsp. bipinnatum

Tanacetum bipinnatum subsp.

bipinnatum, commonly known as the two-pinnate tansy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often aromatic and covered in a white, powdery bloom.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that are clustered in flat-topped inflorescences. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to gardens and natural habitats.
