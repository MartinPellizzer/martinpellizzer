Tulipa pubescens

Tulipa pubescens, commonly known as the woolly tulip, is a species native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, pubescent leaves and striking, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows to a height of 10 to 30 centimeters and produces showy blooms in early spring. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

The flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
