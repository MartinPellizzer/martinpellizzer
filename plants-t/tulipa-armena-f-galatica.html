Tulipa armena f. galatica

Tulipa armena f. galatica, commonly known as the Armenian tulip hybrid, is a botanical variant of the tulip genus native to the Caucasus region.

This subspecies is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features sturdy, upright stems and heart-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and perennial borders.

Botanically, it is valued for its ornamental qualities and genetic significance in tulip breeding programs.
