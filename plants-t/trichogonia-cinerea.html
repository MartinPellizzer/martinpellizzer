Trichogonia cinerea

Trichogonia cinerea, commonly known as the gray trichogonia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish-green foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are often covered in fine, white trichomes, which give it a distinctive appearance.

Trichogonia cinerea is known for its resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.
