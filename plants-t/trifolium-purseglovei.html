Trifolium purseglovei

Trifolium purseglovei, commonly known as Pursglove's clover, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring.

Trifolium purseglovei is often found in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.
