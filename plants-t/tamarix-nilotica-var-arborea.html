Tamarix nilotica var. arborea

Tamarix nilotica var. arborea, commonly known as the weeping tamarisk, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Middle East and parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its feathery, pendulous branches that give it a distinctive, weeping appearance. The plant produces small, pink or reddish flowers in clusters, which bloom in the spring and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are small, scale-like, and often appear grayish-white, adapted to reduce water loss in arid environments.

This species is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
