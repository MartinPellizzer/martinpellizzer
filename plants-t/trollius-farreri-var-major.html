Trollius farreri var. major

Trollius farreri var. major, commonly known as the giant marsh marigold, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that can reach up to 10 centimeters in diameter, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in wet, boggy habitats and has broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, downy coating. It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, which includes other notable species like buttercups and clematis.

Due to its striking appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in wet gardens and conservation areas.
