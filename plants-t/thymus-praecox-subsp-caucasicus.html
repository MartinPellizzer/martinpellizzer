Thymus praecox subsp. caucasicus

Thymus praecox subsp.

caucasicus, commonly known as Caucasian thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Caucasus region. It features aromatic, small, needle-like leaves that are densely packed and covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its essential oils, rich in thymol and other compounds, are valued for their medicinal and culinary uses.
