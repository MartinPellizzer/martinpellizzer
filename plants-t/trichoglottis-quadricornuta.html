Trichoglottis quadricornuta

Trichoglottis quadricornuta, commonly known as the four-horned orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees in tropical rainforests. The plant has linear, dark green leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers with four prominent sepals and petals. Its unique floral structure is adapted for specific pollinators, such as bees and wasps.

The orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses due to its specific environmental requirements.
