Thymus pulcherrimus

Thymus pulcherrimus, commonly known as the purple thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant purple flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has woody stems and aromatic leaves that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a valuable addition to both gardens and herbal collections.
