Thermopsis longicarpa

Thermopsis longicarpa, commonly known as the longpod sainfoin, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are adapted for dispersal by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and steppe habitats.

Thermopsis longicarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
