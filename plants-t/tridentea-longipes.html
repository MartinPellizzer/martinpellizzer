Tridentea longipes

Tridentea longipes, commonly known as the long-peduncled trident, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Tridentea longipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral structure.
