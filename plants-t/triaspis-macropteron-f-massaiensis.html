Triaspis macropteron f. massaiensis

Triaspis macropteron f. massaiensis, commonly known as the Massai fig, is a variant of the common fig tree native to East Africa.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its thick, gnarled trunk and large, lobed leaves. The plant produces small, green figs that ripen to a deep purple or black color, enclosed in a spherical, woolly husk. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in local cultures.
