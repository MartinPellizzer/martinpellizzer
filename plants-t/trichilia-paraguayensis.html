Trichilia paraguayensis

Trichilia paraguayensis, commonly known as the Paraguay yellowwood, is a deciduous tree native to South America, particularly Brazil, Paraguay, and Argentina.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae, which includes mahogany and other tropical hardwoods. The plant is characterized by its smooth, light-colored bark and compound leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in panicles, while the fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds.

Trichilia paraguayensis is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often used in furniture and construction.
