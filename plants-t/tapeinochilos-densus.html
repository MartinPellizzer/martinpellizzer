Tapeinochilos densus

Tapeinochilos densus, commonly known as the dense leathery fern, is a member of the Polypodiaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery fronds that are typically dark green and have a velvety texture. The plant produces spores in clusters called sori, which are located on the underside of the fronds. It thrives in shaded, moist environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
