Triumfetta aestuans

Triumfetta aestuans, commonly known as the coastal spurge, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in sandy or saline soils along the shoreline. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Triumfetta aestuans is adapted to salt spray and is an important component of coastal plant communities.
