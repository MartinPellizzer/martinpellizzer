Torilis leucorhaphis

Torilis leucorhaphis, commonly known as white-rayed groundsel, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

The showy white flowers appear in clusters and bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
