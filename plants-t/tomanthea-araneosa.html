Tomanthea araneosa

Tomanthea araneosa, commonly known as the spider orchid, is a unique flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, spider-like appearance, which gives it its common name. The plant features delicate, elongated leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Tomanthea araneosa thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil to flourish.

Its botanical structure is adapted to its specific ecological niche, making it a fascinating subject for botanical study.
