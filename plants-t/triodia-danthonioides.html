Triodia danthonioides

Triodia danthonioides, commonly known as the spinifex grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant has wiry, spiky leaves that help reduce water loss, making it well adapted to harsh conditions. It plays an important role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for small desert animals.

Triodia danthonioides is often found in coastal dunes and inland sandy plains, showcasing its resilience to extreme climates.
