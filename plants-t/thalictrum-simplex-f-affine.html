Thalictrum simplex f. affine

Thalictrum simplex f. affine, commonly known as the slender meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like foliage and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has a fibrous root system. It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded environments.

Thalictrum simplex f. affine is also noted for its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and flies.
