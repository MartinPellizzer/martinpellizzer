Tragacantha nurensis

Tragacantha nurensis, commonly known as the Nurensis tragacanth, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by distinctive, inflated pods that contain seeds. It is known for its ability to thrive in harsh, dry environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.

Tragacantha nurensis is also valued for its gum, which has been used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive.
