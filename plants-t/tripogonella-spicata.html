Tripogonella spicata

Tripogonella spicata, commonly known as the spiked tripogon, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, which are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy texture.

Tripogonella spicata typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops.
