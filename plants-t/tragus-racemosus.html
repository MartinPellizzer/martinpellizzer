Tragus racemosus

Tragus racemosus, commonly known as the bearded barley, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tufted, hairy appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Tragus racemosus is adapted to arid environments and is often found in disturbed soils.
