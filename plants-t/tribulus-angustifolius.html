Tribulus angustifolius

Tribulus angustifolius, commonly known as narrow-leaved bindweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and can be found in grasslands and along roadsides. The plant has slender, upright stems and small, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and occur in clusters, producing small, round fruits that contain several seeds.

Tribulus angustifolius is known for its aggressive growth and can become a weed in agricultural areas.
