Turbinicarpus macrochele subsp. valteri

Turbinicarpus macrochele subsp.

valteri, commonly known as the Valteri's Turbinicarpus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its rounded, ribbed stems that are typically green to bluish-green in color.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months, which are clustered at the ends of the stems. It is a slow-growing, low-maintenance cactus that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice among succulent collectors.
