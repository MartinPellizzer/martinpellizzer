Taxodium distichum f. pendulum-novum

Taxodium distichum f. pendulum-novum, commonly known as the weeping bald cypress, is a unique cultivar of the bald cypress tree characterized by its cascading, weeping foliage.

This variety exhibits striking, drooping branches that create a dramatic, ethereal appearance, especially when the tree is in full leaf. The leaves are small, scale-like, and often appear in a dense, feathery arrangement along the branches. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal.

The tree is relatively cold-hardy and can tolerate a range of environmental conditions, making it a popular choice for gardens and parks.
