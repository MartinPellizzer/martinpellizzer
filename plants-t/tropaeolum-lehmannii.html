Tropaeolum lehmannii

Tropaeolum lehmannii, commonly known as the Andean garden sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, spurred flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant produces edible leaves that have a tangy, acidic flavor, similar to rhubarb. It thrives in temperate climates and is often grown as an ornamental or culinary plant.

Tropaeolum lehmannii is a member of the Tropaeolaceae family, known for its unique floral structures and medicinal properties.
