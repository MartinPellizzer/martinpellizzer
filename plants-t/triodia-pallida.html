Triodia pallida

Triodia pallida, commonly known as the spinifex grass, is a hardy perennial plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit. The plant features wiry, spiky leaves that are adapted to withstand harsh, arid conditions. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal and inland dune environments.

Triodia pallida plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
