Trifolium pseudoalbopurpureum

Trifolium pseudoalbopurpureum, commonly known as the purple-leaf clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which typically consist of three oval-shaped leaflets with a distinctive purplish hue. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, often appearing in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassy areas, meadows, and along roadsides.

Trifolium pseudoalbopurpureum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
