Thymus pulegioides f. halensis

Thymus pulegioides f. halensis, commonly known as the Halen's thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to rocky and coastal areas.

It features small, aromatic leaves with a minty scent, and its flowers are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense clusters. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.

Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a hardy choice for gardeners in temperate climates.
