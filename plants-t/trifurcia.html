Trifurcia

Trifurcia, commonly known as the three-lobed fern, is a unique plant characterized by its intricate, fern-like foliage.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to tropical and subtropical regions. The leaves are divided into three distinct lobes, giving it its common name and contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Trifurcia is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and greenhouses.
