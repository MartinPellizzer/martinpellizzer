Trollius tanguticus

Trollius tanguticus, commonly known as the Tangut globeflower, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow, globe-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine meadows, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are deeply lobed and form a basal rosette, while its stems are upright and hairy.

Trollius tanguticus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
