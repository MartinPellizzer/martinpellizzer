Tilia × alienigena

Tilia × alienigena, commonly known as the alien linden, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Tilia cordata and Tilia europaea.

It exhibits a combination of traits from both parent species, including heart-shaped leaves and fragrant flowers. The plant is typically deciduous, shedding its leaves in the autumn season. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained, fertile soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for urban landscaping and garden settings.
