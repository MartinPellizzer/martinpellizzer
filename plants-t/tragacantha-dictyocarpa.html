Tragacantha dictyocarpa

Tragacantha dictyocarpa, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia. The plant is characterized by its woody, branched stems and trifoliate leaves. It produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes.

The plant is valued for its resin, which has been used traditionally in medicine and as a natural adhesive.
