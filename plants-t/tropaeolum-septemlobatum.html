Tropaeolum septemlobatum

Tropaeolum septemlobatum, commonly known as the seven-lobed nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Tropaeolaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that come in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant produces small, edible, peppery seeds that are often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are heart-shaped with seven distinct lobes, giving it its common name.

Tropaeolum septemlobatum thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and ornamental planting.
