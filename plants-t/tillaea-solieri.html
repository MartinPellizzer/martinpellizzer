Tillaea solieri

Tillaea solieri, commonly known as the rock spurge, is a small, evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and fleshy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powder. The plant produces yellow flowers with four petals, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, sunny environments.

Tillaea solieri is often used in rock gardens due to its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
