Taraxacum fernandezianum

Taraxacum fernandezianum, commonly known as the Fernandez's dandelion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated, hairy leaves and a hollow, leafless stem that supports a single flower head. The flower head consists of numerous yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular florets. It is native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile, and thrives in dry, open habitats.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to arid conditions with its deep root system.
