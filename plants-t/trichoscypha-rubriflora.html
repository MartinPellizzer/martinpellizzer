Trichoscypha rubriflora

Trichoscypha rubriflora, commonly known as the red-flowered tamarind tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Caesalpiniaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive red flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree has a thick, buttressed trunk and bipinnate leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its fruit is a pod containing edible seeds, which are sometimes used in local cuisines.

Trichoscypha rubriflora is also valued for its timber and plays an important role in the ecosystem of its native habitat.
