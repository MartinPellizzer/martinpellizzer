Tragopogon × mirus

Tragopogon × mirus, commonly known as the goat's beard, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Tragopogon dubius and Tragopogon porrifolius.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flower heads and long, feathery bracts that resemble a beard. The plant is native to Europe but has been introduced to various parts of the world, including North America. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and other open areas.

Tragopogon × mirus is a perennial herb that produces a basal rosette of leaves and is known for its rapid growth and adaptability.
