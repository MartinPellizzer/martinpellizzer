Taraxacum stenocephalum subsp. stenocephalum

Taraxacum stenocephalum subsp.

stenocephalum, commonly known as the narrow-headed dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

The plant features a rosette of narrow, hairy leaves and produces a single, elongated flower head with yellow ray florets. Its flowering period occurs in spring and early summer, and it is characterized by its compact growth habit.

The species is noted for its ability to thrive in arid conditions, making it a resilient component of Mediterranean ecosystems.
