Taraxacum indonesicum

Taraxacum indonesicum, commonly known as the Indonesian dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Indonesia and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and a hollow, hairless stem. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that develop into white, fluffy seeds dispersed by the wind. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in open, disturbed areas.

Taraxacum indonesicum is similar to other dandelion species but can be distinguished by its specific geographic distribution and morphological features.
