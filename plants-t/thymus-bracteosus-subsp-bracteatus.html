Thymus bracteosus subsp. bracteatus

Thymus bracteosus subsp.

bracteatus, commonly known as the bracted thyme, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and small, pinkish-white flowers.

The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders. Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for horticultural use.

The subspecies is distinguished by its distinctive bracts, which are modified leaves that surround the flower clusters.
