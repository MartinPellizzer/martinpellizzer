Thalictrum minus proles monticola

Thalictrum minus proles monticola, commonly known as the mountain avens, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves that are typically arranged in a whorled pattern around the stem. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, which are clustered in upright, branched inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and rocky slopes.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Ranunculaceae family.
