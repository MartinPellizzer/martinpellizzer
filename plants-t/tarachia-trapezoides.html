Tarachia trapezoides

Tarachia trapezoides, commonly known as the desert globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in rocky soils and desert environments. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, adapted to conserve water in harsh conditions.

Tarachia trapezoides is also known for its ability to thrive in full sun and low moisture conditions.
