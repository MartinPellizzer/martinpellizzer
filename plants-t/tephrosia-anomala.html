Tephrosia anomala

Tephrosia anomala, commonly known as African blue pea, is a leguminous plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often used in traditional medicine.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
