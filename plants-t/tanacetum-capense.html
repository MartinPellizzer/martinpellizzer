Tanacetum capense

Tanacetum capense, commonly known as the Cape daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white petals. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils and saline conditions. It produces small, oblong fruits called cypselae, which contain the seeds.

Tanacetum capense is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in coastal environments.
