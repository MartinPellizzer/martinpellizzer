Tanacetum mucronulatum

Tanacetum mucronulatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tansy, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features finely divided, aromatic leaves and yellow button-like flowers that bloom in late summer. It typically grows in moist meadows and along stream banks.

Tanacetum mucronulatum is known for its strong, camphor-like scent and has been used historically in traditional medicine.
