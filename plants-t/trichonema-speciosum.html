Trichonema speciosum

Trichonema speciosum, commonly known as the ornate hairgrass, is a member of the Poaceae family, characterized by its delicate, grass-like appearance.

It is native to southern Africa and thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. The plant features slender, wiry stems with fine, hairy leaves that contribute to its distinctive appearance. Its inflorescence consists of small, clustered spikelets, which are typical of many grass species.

Trichonema speciosum is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to form dense, low-growing mats.
