Toxicodendron griffithii var. barbatum

Toxicodendron griffithii var. barbatum, commonly known as the hairy poison ivy, is a variant of the poison ivy plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically hairy on the upper surface. The plant contains urushiol, a toxic oil that can cause severe skin irritation in humans. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its growth habit includes both shrub-like and vine-like forms, depending on environmental conditions.
