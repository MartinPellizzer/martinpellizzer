Tragopogon olympicus

Tragopogon olympicus, commonly known as the Olympic sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow ray flowers and a distinctive, hollow, spiky central disk. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas such as meadows and rocky slopes. It produces a unique, milky sap that is often used in traditional medicine.

Tragopogon olympicus is also notable for its role in ecological studies due to its rapid evolution and hybridization with other species.
