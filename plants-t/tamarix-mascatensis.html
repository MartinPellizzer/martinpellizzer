Tamarix mascatensis

Tamarix mascatensis, commonly known as the Arabian tamarisk, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and pink to reddish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is highly drought-tolerant and thrives in saline and alkaline soils. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access deep water sources.

Tamarix mascatensis plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
