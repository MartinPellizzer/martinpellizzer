Tropaeolum popelari

Tropaeolum popelari, commonly known as the Indian nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, edible flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow-orange fruits that contain seeds, which can be harvested for propagation. It thrives in warm, sunny conditions and is often cultivated as an ornamental or edible plant.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both gardens and culinary use.
