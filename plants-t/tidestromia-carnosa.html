Tidestromia carnosa

Tidestromia carnosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine, velvety hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its flowering stems are often reddish-purple, adding visual interest to the plant.

Tidestromia carnosa is adapted to moist, rich soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
