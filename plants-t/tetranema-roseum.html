Tetranema roseum

Tetranema roseum, commonly known as the pink star ivy, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This plant is often used in hanging baskets or as a ground cover due to its compact size and ornamental appeal.
