Trifolium nigrescens subsp. polyanthemum

Trifolium nigrescens subsp.

polyanthemum, commonly known as the purple clover, is a flowering plant in the legume family. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets.

The plant produces small, purple or pink flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and meadows.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in soil nitrogen fixation.
