Tetraneuris scaposa var. villosa

Tetraneuris scaposa var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy tetraneuris, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray foliage and is typically found in dry, rocky habitats. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a notable species in arid landscapes.
