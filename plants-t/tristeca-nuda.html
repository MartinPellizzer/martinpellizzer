Tristeca nuda

Tristeca nuda, commonly known as the naked tristeca, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stem and small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It is native to certain regions of Europe and prefers well-drained soils in sunny or partially shaded environments.

Tristeca nuda is often found in grasslands and along roadsides, where it thrives in disturbed habitats.
