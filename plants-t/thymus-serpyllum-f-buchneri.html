Thymus serpyllum f. buchneri

Thymus serpyllum f. buchneri, commonly known as narrowleaf thyme, is a subspecies of thyme characterized by its compact growth habit and aromatic foliage.

It is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe, often found in rocky or sandy soils. The leaves are narrow and serrated, with a strong, pungent scent when crushed. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers in early summer, which attract pollinators like bees.

It is widely used in herbal medicine and as a culinary herb due to its essential oils and medicinal properties.
