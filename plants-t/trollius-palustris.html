Trollius palustris

Trollius palustris, commonly known as marsh globeflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to wetlands and marshy areas of North America.

It features showy, yellow, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and grows from a short, fibrous root system. It thrives in poorly drained soils and is often found in swampy or boggy habitats.

Trollius palustris is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in wetland ecosystems.
