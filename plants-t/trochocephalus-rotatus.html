Trochocephalus rotatus

Trochocephalus rotatus, commonly known as the spiral-headed orchid, is a unique flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking spiral arrangement of leaves, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, often in shades of white and yellow. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
