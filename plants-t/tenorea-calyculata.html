Tenorea calyculata

Tenorea calyculata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed sunflower, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow flower heads. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically arranged in a single terminal head. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

Tenorea calyculata is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a hardy species in suitable climates.
