Taraxacum subsaeviforme

Taraxacum subsaeviforme, commonly known as the slender dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its upright, sparsely hairy stems and basal leaves that are lanceolate to oblanceolate in shape. The plant produces a single, yellow composite flower head that develops into a spherical, wind-dispersed seed head. It is native to regions of Europe and is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is considered a common weed in many areas.
