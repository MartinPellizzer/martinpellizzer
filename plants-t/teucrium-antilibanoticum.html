Teucrium antilibanoticum

Teucrium antilibanoticum, commonly known as the Lebanese germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, often blooming in summer. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs.

Teucrium antilibanoticum is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
