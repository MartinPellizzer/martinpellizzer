Thymus ciliatus var. reesei

Thymus ciliatus var. reesei, commonly known as the Sierra foothill thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features aromatic, narrow leaves with a distinctive silvery-white underside, and small pink to lavender flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has a compact growth habit, making it suitable for rock gardens and as a ground cover. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, and is known for its medicinal and culinary uses.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for both horticultural and ecological purposes.
