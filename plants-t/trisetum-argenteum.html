Trisetum argenteum

Trisetum argenteum, commonly known as silver tuft grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray leaves and tufted growth habit, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, spikelet-bearing branches.

Trisetum argenteum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, silvery foliage.
