Triumfetta subpalmata

Triumfetta subpalmata, commonly known as the palmate triumfetta, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is a shrub or small tree with opposite, palmately lobed leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive, broad shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by round, fleshy fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and horticultural applications.
