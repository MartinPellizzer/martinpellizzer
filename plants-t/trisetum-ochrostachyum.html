Trisetum ochrostachyum

Trisetum ochrostachyum, commonly known as the silver hair grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and fine, silver-haired leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets.

Trisetum ochrostachyum is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
