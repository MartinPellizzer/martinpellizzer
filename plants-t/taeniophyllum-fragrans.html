Taeniophyllum fragrans

Taeniophyllum fragrans, commonly known as the sweet-scented orchid, is a small epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a sweet, spicy aroma. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and unique fragrance.
