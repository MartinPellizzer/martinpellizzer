Trifolium nigrescens var. meneghinianum

Trifolium nigrescens var. meneghinianum, commonly known as the Italian clover, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in dense clusters during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
