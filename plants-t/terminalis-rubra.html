Terminalis rubra

Terminalis rubra, commonly known as the red-tipped plant, is a species characterized by its striking red-tipped foliage.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is native to tropical regions of Africa. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a prominent red margin that contrasts with its dark green central portion. It produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming during the rainy season.

Terminalis rubra thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
