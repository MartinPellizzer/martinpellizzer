Tragacantha cinerea

Tragacantha cinerea, commonly known as the pale tragacanth, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, gray-green stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by pod-like fruits containing lens-shaped seeds. Tragacantha cinerea is known for its ability to thrive in arid and rocky soils, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.

Its historical use in traditional medicine and as a source of gum has contributed to its ecological and cultural significance.
