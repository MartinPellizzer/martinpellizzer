Thymus corthiaticus

Thymus corthiaticus, commonly known as the Corsican thyme, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and square stems. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in early summer. Its leaves are fragrant and contain essential oils, making it valuable in both culinary and medicinal applications.

Thymus corthiaticus thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or limestone-rich habitats.
