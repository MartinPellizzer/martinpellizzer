Telekia speciosa

Telekia speciosa, commonly known as the blueweed or blue bonnet, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual that grows up to 1 meter tall, featuring hairy stems and alternate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. Its flowers are tubular and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Telekia speciosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
