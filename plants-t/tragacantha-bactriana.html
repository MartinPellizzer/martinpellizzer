Tragacantha bactriana

Tragacantha bactriana, commonly known as the Persian gum or Astragalus bactrianus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant produces a valuable resin, known as tragacanth, which has been used historically in medicine and as a binding agent. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils and at high altitudes.

Due to its ecological and economic importance, it is sometimes cultivated for its resin and ornamental value.
