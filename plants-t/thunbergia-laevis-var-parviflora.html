Thunbergia laevis var. parviflora

Thunbergia laevis var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The flowers typically open in the morning and close by midday, hence its common name. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
