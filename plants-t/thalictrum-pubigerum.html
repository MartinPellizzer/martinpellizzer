Thalictrum pubigerum

Thalictrum pubigerum, commonly known as the pubescent meadow rue, is a flowering plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like foliage and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its stems are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive texture.

Thalictrum pubigerum is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits to pollinators.
