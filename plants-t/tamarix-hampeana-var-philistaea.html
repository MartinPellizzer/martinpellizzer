Tamarix hampeana var. philistaea

Tamarix hampeana var. philistaea, commonly known as the desert tamarisk, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, feathery foliage and small, scale-like leaves that reduce water loss in hot, dry climates. The plant produces pink or reddish flowers in summer, which are clustered in elongated panicles. It has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in poor, sandy soils.

This species is often used for erosion control and as an ornamental plant in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
