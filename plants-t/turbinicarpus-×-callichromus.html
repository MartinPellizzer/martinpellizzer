Turbinicarpus × callichromus

Turbinicarpus × callichromus, commonly known as the hybrid moon cactus, is a hybrid succulent plant resulting from the cross between Turbinicarpus callichromus and Turbinicarpus macrocheirus.

It is characterized by its unique appearance, featuring a colorful, often vibrant crown and a green, cylindrical base. The plant is native to the deserts of northern Mexico and is adapted to arid environments with minimal water requirements. It has thick, fleshy stems that store water, and its flowers are typically yellow or orange, appearing in late spring.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in rock gardens.
