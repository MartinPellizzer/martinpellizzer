Tristemma littorale subsp. biafranum

Tristemma littorale subsp.

biafranum, commonly known as the African sea hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of West and Central Africa. It is a shrub that typically grows near the shoreline, thriving in saline and humid environments.

The plant produces showy yellow flowers with five petals, which are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a rough texture, while its stems are often woody and succulent.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
