Taraxacum phoenicolepis

Taraxacum phoenicolepis, commonly known as the purple-leaved dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, purple-tinged leaves and a yellow flower head that develops into a fluffy seed parachute. Native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa, it thrives in dry, sunny environments. The plant has a taproot system and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.

Its botanical features make it distinct from other dandelion species, particularly in its leaf coloration and habitat preferences.
