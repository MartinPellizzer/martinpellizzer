Taligalea umbellata

Taligalea umbellata, commonly known as the umbrella tree, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive umbellate inflorescence, which consists of clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in a flat-topped or rounded shape. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to several meters. Its leaves are usually alternate, simple, and have a rough or hairy texture.

Taligalea umbellata is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive flowers and compact growth habit.
