Typha angustifolia var. saulseana

Typha angustifolia var. saulseana, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cattail, is a variant of the common cattail species native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and cylindrical flower heads that produce fluffy seeds. This plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often forming dense stands in marshes and pond edges. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly, contributing to the structure and biodiversity of wetland ecosystems.

The plant plays a vital role in water filtration and provides habitat for various aquatic organisms.
