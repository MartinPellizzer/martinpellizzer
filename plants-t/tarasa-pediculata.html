Tarasa pediculata

Tarasa pediculata, commonly known as the bluebell orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in early spring, typically in clusters at the ends of its slender stems. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, and its roots are fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor soils. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
