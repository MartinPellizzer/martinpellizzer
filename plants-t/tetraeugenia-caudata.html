Tetraeugenia caudata

Tetraeugenia caudata, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody pods containing edible seeds. Its botanical significance lies in its role within traditional medicine and as a source of food in certain regions.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal due to its unique foliage and adaptability to various soil conditions.
