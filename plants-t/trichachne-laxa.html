Trichachne laxa

Trichachne laxa, commonly known as slender trichachne, is a grass species native to warm temperate and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is often found in open areas such as roadsides, fields, and disturbed soils.

Trichachne laxa is adapted to dry conditions and is considered a pioneer species in ecological succession.
