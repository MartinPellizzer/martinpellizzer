Themeda caudata

Themeda caudata, commonly known as the tussock grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands. The plant has narrow, flat leaves that are typically covered with fine hairs, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.
