Triodia nealleyi

Triodia nealleyi, commonly known as the Nealley's spinifex, is a low-growing perennial grass found in arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, wiry foliage and ability to form extensive mats on the ground. The plant has small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in harsh environments. It is well adapted to survive in nutrient-poor soils and withstand extreme temperatures.

Triodia nealleyi plays a crucial role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various desert organisms.
