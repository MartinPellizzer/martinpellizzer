Tacca pinnatifida subsp. maculata

Tacca pinnatifida subsp.

maculata, commonly known as the spotted prayer plant, is a tropical fern native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive spotted or mottled pattern.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are often hidden beneath its foliage. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistent moisture and high humidity to flourish.

Its unique appearance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
