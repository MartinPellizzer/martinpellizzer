Thymus moldavicus

Thymus moldavicus, commonly known as Moldavian thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are narrow, fragrant, and often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Thymus moldavicus thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
