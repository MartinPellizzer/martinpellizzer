Triumfetta pseudoangulata

Triumfetta pseudoangulata, commonly known as the African sandmat, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, annual herb that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces small, yellowish flowers clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

The species is notable for its adaptability to arid environments and its role in local ecosystems.
