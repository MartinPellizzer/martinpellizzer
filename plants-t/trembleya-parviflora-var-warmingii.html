Trembleya parviflora var. warmingii

Trembleya parviflora var. warmingii, commonly known as the yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or swampy areas. It has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
