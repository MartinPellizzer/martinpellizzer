Torulinium macrocephalum var. davidsei

Torulinium macrocephalum var. davidsei, commonly known as the David's torulina, is a rare and ornamental plant native to specific regions in southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink to purple. The plant features a compact, bushy growth habit with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery bloom. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its limited distribution and unique appearance, it is highly sought after by horticultural enthusiasts.
