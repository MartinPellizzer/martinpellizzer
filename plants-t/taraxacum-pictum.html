Taraxacum pictum

Taraxacum pictum, commonly known as the painted dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers and heart-shaped basal leaves. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grassy areas. Its leaves are typically covered with fine hairs, and the flower heads are surrounded by bracts that give it a distinctive appearance.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
