Tectaria translucens

Tectaria translucens, commonly known as the translucent tectaria, is a fern species belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that exhibit a translucent quality, especially when viewed in certain light conditions. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, such as tropical rainforests, where it thrives on moist, well-drained soil or on rocks. Its spores are produced in small, clustered structures called sori, which are located on the underside of the fronds.

Tectaria translucens is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and is appreciated for its unique visual appearance.
