Trichipteris decomposita

Trichipteris decomposita, commonly known as the decomposing fern, is a rare and delicate fern species native to the temperate regions of the Pacific Northwest.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices, where it relies on consistent humidity and organic matter for nutrient uptake. Its fronds are characterized by their finely divided, feather-like structure, which aids in maximizing surface area for photosynthesis in low-light conditions. The plant plays a crucial role in nutrient cycling by decomposing organic material, contributing to soil fertility.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
