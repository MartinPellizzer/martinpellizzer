Taxus cuspidata f. fructu-luteo

Taxus cuspidata f. fructu-luteo, commonly known as the golden fruit Japanese yew, is a rare and striking cultivar of the Japanese yew.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellowish-orange berries, which contrast beautifully with its dark green foliage. The plant is evergreen, with lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and arranged in a dense, compact form. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a desirable ornamental plant in gardens.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in horticultural collections.
