Thinopyrum obtusiflorum

Thinopyrum obtusiflorum, commonly known as the broadflower goatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tall, robust stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces panicles of small, greenish to yellowish flowers, which are typically arranged in a loose, open inflorescence. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Thinopyrum obtusiflorum is valued for its potential in forage and as a genetic resource for wheat improvement.
