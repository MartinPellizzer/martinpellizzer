Trichophyllum cylindricum

Trichophyllum cylindricum, commonly known as the cylindrical haircap moss, is a small, mat-forming moss belonging to the family Dicranaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, cylindrical stems covered in fine, hair-like structures called trichomes. The leaves are narrow, gradually narrowing towards the apex, and are typically arranged in a spiral pattern. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices.

This species is often found in temperate regions and plays a role in soil stabilization and nutrient cycling in its habitat.
