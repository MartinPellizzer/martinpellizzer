Tylophora insulana

Tylophora insulana, commonly known as the Island Tylophora, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its opposite, elliptical leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky, resinous coating. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
