Tetrapanax papyrifer

Tetrapanax papyrifer, commonly known as the paper plant, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palm-like leaves that are divided into four parts, giving it its genus name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles. Its stems are fibrous and have been historically used for making paper and textiles.

Tetrapanax papyrifer thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
