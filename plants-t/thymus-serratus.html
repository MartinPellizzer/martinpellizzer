Thymus serratus

Thymus serratus, commonly known as the serrated thyme, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage is often used in culinary applications due to its strong, pungent scent.

Thymus serratus is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential antimicrobial properties.
