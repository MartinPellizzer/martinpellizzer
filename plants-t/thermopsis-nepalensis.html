Thermopsis nepalensis

Thermopsis nepalensis, commonly known as the Nepal yellowcrown, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Nepal and northern India.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bright yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are bipinnate, meaning they are divided into small, feather-like segments, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Thermopsis nepalensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators in its native range.
