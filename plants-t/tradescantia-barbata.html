Tradescantia barbata

Tradescantia barbata, commonly known as the wandering jew or snake plant, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright, grass-like leaves that are often variegated with white or purple markings. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers in clusters during the summer months. It is a hardy and adaptable species, capable of thriving in a variety of light conditions.

Its ability to propagate through both seeds and rhizomes makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
