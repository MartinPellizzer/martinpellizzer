Trachymene incisa subsp. incisa

Trachymene incisa subsp.

incisa, commonly known as narrow-leaved false solomon's seal, is a herbaceous perennial native to Australia. It features slender, upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the spring and summer months. Its foliage is typically aromatic and can be used in herbal teas.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shady garden settings.
