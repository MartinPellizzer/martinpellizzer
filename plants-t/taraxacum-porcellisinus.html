Taraxacum porcellisinus

Taraxacum porcellisinus, commonly known as the porcelain dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow composite flowers and a hollow, bluish-green stem. The plant is native to the temperate regions of Europe and is often found in meadows and grassy areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its roots form a dense, fibrous system.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
