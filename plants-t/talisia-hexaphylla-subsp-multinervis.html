Talisia hexaphylla subsp. multinervis

Talisia hexaphylla subsp.

multinervis, commonly known as the Brazilian tulip tree, is a species native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil. It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a distinct veining pattern, contributing to its ornamental value. The plant is known for its fast growth and is often used in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it adaptable to various garden settings.
