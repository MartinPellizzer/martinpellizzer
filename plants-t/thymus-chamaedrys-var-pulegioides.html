Thymus chamaedrys var. pulegioides

Thymus chamaedrys var. pulegioides, commonly known as narrow-leaved thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features small, fragrant leaves that are typically narrow and densely packed, forming a mat-like growth habit. The plant produces tiny white or pale pink flowers in the spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic foliage and hardy nature make it a popular choice for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
