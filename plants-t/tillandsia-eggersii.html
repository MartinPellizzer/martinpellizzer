Tillandsia eggersii

Tillandsia eggersii, commonly known as the Eggers' air plant, is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is native to Central and South America.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on other plants without deriving nutrients from them. The plant features dense, fibrous roots and rosette-shaped leaves that are covered in a thick layer of trichomes, which help in absorbing moisture from the air. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Tillandsia eggersii is prized for its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
