Tithonia hondurensis

Tithonia hondurensis, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a tropical plant native to Central America.

It is a herbaceous annual that features bright orange to red flower heads, which are composed of numerous small, tubular florets. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Tithonia hondurensis is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
