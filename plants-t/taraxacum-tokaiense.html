Taraxacum tokaiense

Taraxacum tokaiense, commonly known as Tokai dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and heart-shaped basal leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, grassy areas and has a taproot system. It produces a fluffy seed head that aids in wind dispersal of its seeds.

Taraxacum tokaiense is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
