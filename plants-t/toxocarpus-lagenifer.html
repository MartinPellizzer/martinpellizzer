Toxocarpus lagenifer

Toxocarpus lagenifer, commonly known as the bottlebrush tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive inflorescences that resemble bottle brushes, composed of numerous small, tubular flowers. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are ovate to elliptical in shape. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers are typically pink or red, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.
