Triosteum himalayanum var. chinense

Triosteum himalayanum var. chinense, commonly known as the Chinese triosteum, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers with five petals, typical of the Campanulaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
