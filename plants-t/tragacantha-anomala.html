Tragacantha anomala

Tragacantha anomala, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and woody, thorny stems. The plant produces a resinous gum that has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being oval-shaped and hairy, while its pods are elongated and contain several seeds.

Tragacantha anomala thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it an important species in desert ecosystems.
