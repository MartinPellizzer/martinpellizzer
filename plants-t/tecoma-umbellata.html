Tecoma umbellata

Tecoma umbellata, commonly known as the yellow bell tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height. The plant produces clusters of yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate, with a smooth or slightly serrated edge.

Tecoma umbellata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to well-drained soils in full sun.
