Tabernaemontana odontadeniiflora

Tabernaemontana odontadeniiflora, commonly known as the toothed-leaf star of Bethlehem, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and are often fragrant. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its blooming period typically occurs during the summer months, adding a vibrant touch to gardens and landscapes.
