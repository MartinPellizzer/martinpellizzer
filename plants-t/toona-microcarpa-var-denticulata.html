Toona microcarpa var. denticulata

Toona microcarpa var. denticulata, commonly known as the serrated toona, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family, which includes figs and mulberries. The plant is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves with serrated margins, giving it its common name. It produces small, greenish flowers followed by round, fuzzy fruits that mature to a yellowish color.

This species is valued for its timber and is often found in lowland tropical forests.
