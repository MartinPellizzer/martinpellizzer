Terpsichore xanthotrichia

Terpsichore xanthotrichia, commonly known as the golden-haired terpsichore, is a rare and striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow and orange inflorescence, which resembles a cascade of flames. The plant features elongated, narrow leaves that are covered in a fine, golden pubescence, giving it its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical study and conservation efforts.
