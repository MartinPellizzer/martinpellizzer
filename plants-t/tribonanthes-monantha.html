Tribonanthes monantha

Tribonanthes monantha, commonly known as the African star-of-Bethlehem, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and has narrow, grass-like leaves. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

The flowers are arranged in a star-like pattern, giving the plant its common name.
