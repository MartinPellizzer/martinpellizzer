Tacsonia bicoronata

Tacsonia bicoronata, commonly known as the two-crown orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, showy flowers with two distinct crown-like structures. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters.

Tacsonia bicoronata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservatories.
