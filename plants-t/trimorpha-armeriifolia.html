Trimorpha armeriifolia

Trimorpha armeriifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf mallow, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It features lance-shaped leaves with a silvery-white underside, which gives it its common name. The plant produces showy, pale pink to lavender flowers that bloom in late spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
