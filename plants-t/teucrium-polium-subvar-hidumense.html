Teucrium polium subvar. hidumense

Teucrium polium subvar. hidumense, commonly known as the Hidumense germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, blue or purple flowers. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, known for its square stems and fragrant properties. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.

Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and as a ground cover in arid landscapes.
