Tragacantha aurea

Tragacantha aurea, commonly known as the golden tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces a unique gum, tragacanth, which is harvested from its pods and used in various industrial and medicinal applications. Tragacantha aurea typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to withstand arid conditions.

Its ecological importance includes providing nectar for pollinators and stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
