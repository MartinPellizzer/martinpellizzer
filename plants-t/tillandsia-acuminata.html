Tillandsia acuminata

Tillandsia acuminata, commonly known as the Acuminata Air Plant, is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is native to Central and South America.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on other plants without deriving nutrients from them, instead absorbing moisture and nutrients through its leaves. The plant features rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves that are adapted to capture and retain water. It produces striking inflorescences with vibrant purple or red flowers that bloom in the spring.

Due to its low maintenance and unique appearance, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
