Troxirum trifolium

Troxirum trifolium, commonly known as the three-leaved clover, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged symmetrically. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or white in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils.

Troxirum trifolium is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in ecological and agricultural contexts.
