Tabebuia microphylla

Tabebuia microphylla, commonly known as the pink trumpet tree, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its small, glossy leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The tree produces woody pods that contain seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is adapted to well-drained soils.

The plant is also valued for its durable wood, which is used in furniture and construction.
