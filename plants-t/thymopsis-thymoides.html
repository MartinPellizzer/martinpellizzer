Thymopsis thymoides

Thymopsis thymoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf thymes, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are narrow and fragrant, often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Thymopsis thymoides thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.
