Tephrosia axillaris

Tephrosia axillaris, commonly known as the blue false indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its opposite, pinnate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils and is adapted to a variety of environmental conditions.

Tephrosia axillaris is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
