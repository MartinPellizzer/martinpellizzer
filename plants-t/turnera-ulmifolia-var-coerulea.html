Turnera ulmifolia var. coerulea

Turnera ulmifolia var. coerulea, commonly known as the blue turnera, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Passifloraceae family, known for its showy, colorful flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has heart-shaped, glossy leaves. Its blue-violet flowers bloom in spring and are often visited by bees and other pollinators.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
