Toxocarpus roxburghii

Toxocarpus roxburghii, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a deciduous shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, green fruits that mature to a bright orange-red color, rich in vitamin C. Its botanical features include a woody stem, simple leaves, and a unique fruit structure with a fleshy exocarp.

Toxocarpus roxburghii is widely cultivated for its nutritional value and traditional medicinal uses.
