Trichomanes erectum

Trichomanes erectum, commonly known as the upright huperzia, is a fern-like plant belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its erect, sparsely branched stems and small, scale-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of trichomes. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its sporangia are clustered in strobili, which are cone-like structures that release spores for reproduction.

Trichomanes erectum is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
