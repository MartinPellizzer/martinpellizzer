Teucrium cubense

Teucrium cubense, commonly known as Cuban germander, is a perennial herb native to the Caribbean, particularly Cuba.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal properties. The plant features square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated margin. Its flowers are typically blue or purple, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the branches.

Teucrium cubense is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.
