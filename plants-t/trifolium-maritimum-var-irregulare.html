Trifolium maritimum var. irregulare

Trifolium maritimum var. irregulare, commonly known as the irregular coastal clover, is a variant of the common white clover.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that thrives in coastal and saline environments. The plant features trifoliate leaves with three oval-shaped leaflets, often with slight variations in shape. It produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring.

This variety is adapted to tolerate salt spray and sandy soils, making it a characteristic species of maritime habitats.
