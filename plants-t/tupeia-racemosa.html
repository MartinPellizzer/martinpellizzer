Tupeia racemosa

Tupeia racemosa, commonly known as the clustered tupeia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils and salt spray. Its flowers are usually pink or purple and appear in racemate clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Tupeia racemosa is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
