Trifolium preussii

Trifolium preussii, commonly known as the Preuss's clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically produces small, trifoliate leaves with three leaflets, each usually having a slightly hairy surface. The plant is native to regions of Europe and parts of Asia, where it thrives in meadows and grasslands. It produces small white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early summer, contributing to its ornamental value.

Trifolium preussii is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
