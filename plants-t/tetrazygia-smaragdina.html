Tetrazygia smaragdina

Tetrazygia smaragdina, commonly known as the Emerald Ctenanthe, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves with a glossy, emerald-green surface. The plant produces small, spadix-like inflorescences surrounded by a white spathe, which is typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Tetrazygia smaragdina is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
