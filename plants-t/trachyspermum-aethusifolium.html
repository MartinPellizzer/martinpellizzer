Trachyspermum aethusifolium

Trachyspermum aethusifolium, commonly known as bishop's weed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family. Its seeds are small, ovoid, and covered in a fine, whitish powder.

The plant is known for its medicinal and culinary uses, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
