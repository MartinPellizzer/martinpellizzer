Trianthema glandulosum

Trianthema glandulosum, commonly known as the sticky leaf groundsel, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a sticky texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
