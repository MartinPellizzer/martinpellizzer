Tristachya leucothrix var. sapinii

Tristachya leucothrix var. sapinii, commonly known as the silverleaf papyrus, is a variant of the papyrus plant native to wetland environments in Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can reach heights of up to 3 meters, covered in a dense layer of silvery-white hair. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers arranged in a spike at the top of the stem. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often floating on the water's surface.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in moist, nutrient-rich soils.
