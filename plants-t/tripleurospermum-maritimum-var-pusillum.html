Tripleurospermum maritimum var. pusillum

Tripleurospermum maritimum var. pusillum, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple ray florets and a dense, spiny central disk surrounded by bracts. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in maritime environments. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, adapting to salt spray and strong winds.

It blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
