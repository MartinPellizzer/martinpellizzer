Thlaspi glaucum subsp. californicum

Thlaspi glaucum subsp.

californicum, commonly known as California whitlowgrass, is a flowering plant native to western North America. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as coastal bluffs and grasslands. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are usually covered in a waxy coating.
