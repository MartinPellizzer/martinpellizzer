Triticum hieminflatum

Triticum hieminflatum, commonly known as the flat wheat or hulled wheat, is a species of grass native to the temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, hulled grains that are typically smaller than those of common wheat. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spikelets that contain the edible grains. Its botanical structure includes a fibrous root system and a robust stem that supports the inflorescence.

Triticum hieminflatum is often cultivated for its nutritional value and traditional use in various cuisines.
