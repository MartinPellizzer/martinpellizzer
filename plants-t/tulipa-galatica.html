Tulipa galatica

Tulipa galatica, commonly known as the Persian tulip, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear large, showy flowers with recurved petals. The flowers typically come in shades of red, pink, and white, with a distinctive cup-like shape. The plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Its botanical features make it a prized addition to gardens seeking both ornamental value and hardiness.
