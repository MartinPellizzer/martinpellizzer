Terminalia gangetica

Terminalia gangetica, commonly known as Indian blackberry, is a deciduous tree native to South and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its rough, bark-like fruit covering. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by edible, red berries that are rich in nutrients. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a distinctive elliptical shape.

Terminalia gangetica is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for various health benefits.
