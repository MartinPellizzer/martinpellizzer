Thymus iljinii var. affinis

Thymus iljinii var. affinis, commonly known as the Iljin thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact foliage and small, aromatic leaves that emit a strong thyme scent. The plant produces pink to purple flowers in spring, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its use in traditional herbal medicine.
