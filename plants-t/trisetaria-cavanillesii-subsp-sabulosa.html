Trisetaria cavanillesii subsp. sabulosa

Trisetaria cavanillesii subsp.

sabulosa, commonly known as the sandy trisetaria, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa. It is a small, herbaceous annual that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils.

The plant produces delicate, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry environments.

It is often found in coastal dunes and other sandy habitats where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
