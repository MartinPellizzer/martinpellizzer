Trifolium mesogitanum

Trifolium mesogitanum, commonly known as the Mesopotamian clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters and features trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom from spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodland areas.

Trifolium mesogitanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in nitrogen fixation in soil.
