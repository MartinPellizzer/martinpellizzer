Tribulus pubescens

Tribulus pubescens, commonly known as puncture vine, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and spiny, three-lobed fruits that contain seeds. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and the stems are covered in fine, silky hairs.

Tribulus pubescens has been used in traditional medicine for various health purposes, including enhancing vitality and supporting reproductive health.
