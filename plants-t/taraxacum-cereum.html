Taraxacum cereum

Taraxacum cereum, commonly known as the waxy dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its hairy, waxy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces a yellow flower head that develops into a white, fluffy seed ball. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environments.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in gardens and lawns.
