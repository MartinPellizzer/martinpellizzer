Taraxacum alaskanum var. ulvelkotii

Taraxacum alaskanum var. ulvelkotii, commonly known as the Alaska dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, waxy leaves. The plant produces a single, hollow, leafless stem that terminates in a yellow composite flower head. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of high-elevation ecosystems.
