Tulipa riparia

Tulipa riparia, commonly known as the river tulip, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the wet meadows and riverbanks of Central Asia.

It produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is well adapted to riparian environments and is often found near water sources.

Tulipa riparia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
