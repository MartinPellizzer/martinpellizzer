Tetradymia axillaris var. longispina

Tetradymia axillaris var. longispina, commonly known as the long-spined bush mallow, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, pale blue to lavender flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
