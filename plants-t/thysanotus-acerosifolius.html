Thysanotus acerosifolius

Thysanotus acerosifolius, commonly known as the sharp-leaved bluebell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, pointed leaves and clusters of blue or violet flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its ornamental value is due to its attractive, bell-shaped flowers and compact growth habit.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and ability to thrive in well-drained conditions.
