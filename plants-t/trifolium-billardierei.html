Trifolium billardierei

Trifolium billardierei, commonly known as the narrow-leaf clover, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a rosette. The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Trifolium billardierei is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
