Tragacantha versicolor

Tragacantha versicolor, commonly known as the variable tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, often colorful flowers that range from pink to purple. The plant produces a resinous substance known as tragacanth, which has been historically used in medicine and as a thickening agent. Its leaves are trifoliate, meaning they consist of three leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Tragacantha versicolor is often found in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.
