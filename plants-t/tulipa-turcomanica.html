Tulipa turcomanica

Tulipa turcomanica, commonly known as the Turkmenian tulip, is a species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in temperate climates. It belongs to the genus Tulipa within the family Liliaceae.

Tulipa turcomanica is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
