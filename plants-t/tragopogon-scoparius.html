Tragopogon scoparius

Tragopogon scoparius, commonly known as the yellow goat's beard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces tall, slender stems that bear yellow ray flowers surrounded by numerous disk flowers. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides.

Tragopogon scoparius is also known for its edible seeds, which are rich in nutrients and have been used in traditional cuisine.
