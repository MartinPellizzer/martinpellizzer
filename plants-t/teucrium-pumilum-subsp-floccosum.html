Teucrium pumilum subsp. floccosum

Teucrium pumilum subsp.

floccosum, commonly known as woolly germander, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States. It features dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive, soft appearance.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for xeriscaping and ecological restoration projects.
