Tragus halleri

Tragus halleri, commonly known as Haller's tragus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its small, spiky flowers that typically bloom in late summer. The plant has a succulent appearance with fleshy leaves and is often found in rocky or arid environments. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Tragus halleri is adapted to survive in dry conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
