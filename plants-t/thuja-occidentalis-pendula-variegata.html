Thuja occidentalis pendula-variegata

Thuja occidentalis pendula-variegata, commonly known as the variegated eastern arborvitae, is a cultivar of the eastern white cedar.

It is characterized by its cascading, weeping growth habit and variegated foliage that features white and yellow streaks. The plant is native to North America and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value. Its foliage is fragrant and has a slightly resinous scent, typical of the genus Thuja.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a versatile choice for garden settings.
