Tanacetum jacobaeiforme

Tanacetum jacobaeiforme, commonly known as Jacobaea-like tansy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in moist, grassy areas and has finely divided, fern-like leaves. It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators.

Tanacetum jacobaeiforme is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and has a mild, aromatic scent.
