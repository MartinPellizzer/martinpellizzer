Tillaea gemmifera

Tillaea gemmifera, commonly known as the gemmiferous tillaea, is a perennial succulent plant native to the cold, arid regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing form and fleshy, thick leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in harsh environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring, adding a delicate touch to its rocky habitat. Tillaea gemmifera is also known for its ability to form dense mats, which help prevent soil erosion in its natural range.

Its unique botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
