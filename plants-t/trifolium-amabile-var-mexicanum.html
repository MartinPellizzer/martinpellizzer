Trifolium amabile var. mexicanum

Trifolium amabile var. mexicanum, commonly known as the Mexican white clover, is a perennial legume native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically white or pale in color, contrasting with the typical green of other clover species. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, enriching the soil with nutrients.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in gardens and landscaping.
