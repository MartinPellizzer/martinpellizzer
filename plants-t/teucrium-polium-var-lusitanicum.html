Teucrium polium var. lusitanicum

Teucrium polium var. lusitanicum, commonly known as the Lusitanian germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. The plant features upright, branched stems covered in fine, grayish hairs. Its small, tubular flowers are usually blue or purple, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the branches.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
