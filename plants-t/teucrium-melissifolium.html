Teucrium melissifolium

Teucrium melissifolium, commonly known as lemon verbena, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lemon-scented leaves that are used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves contain volatile oils, including citral, which contribute to its distinctive fragrance and therapeutic properties.

Teucrium melissifolium thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental and herbal gardens.
