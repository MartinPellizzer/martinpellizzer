Taraxacum dargilanicum

Taraxacum dargilanicum, commonly known as the Dargilan dandelion, is a flowering plant species native to the high-altitude regions of the Eastern Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant produces a yellow ray floret surrounded by numerous tubular disc florets, typical of the genus Taraxacum. Its leaves are basal, spoon-shaped, and covered with fine hairs, while the stem is usually unbranched and hairy.

This species is adapted to cold, alpine environments and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.
