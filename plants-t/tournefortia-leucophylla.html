Tournefortia leucophylla

Tournefortia leucophylla, commonly known as the white-leaf tournefortia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in coastal or sandy soils, often in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually pale green to white in color, giving it its common name. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters.

Tournefortia leucophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its foliage and floral characteristics.
