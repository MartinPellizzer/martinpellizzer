Tanacetum winkleri

Tanacetum winkleri, commonly known as the Winkler's tansy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features finely divided, gray-green leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows or along stream banks.

Tanacetum winkleri is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically in herbal medicine.
