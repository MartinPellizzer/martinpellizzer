Teucrium glomeratum

Teucrium glomeratum, commonly known as blue germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a distinct aromatic scent.

Teucrium glomeratum is often found in rocky soils and dry habitats, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.
