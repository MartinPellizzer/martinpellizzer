Trichilia suavis

Trichilia suavis, commonly known as the sweet-scented trichilia, is a tropical tree native to regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae, which includes mahogany and related hardwoods. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Trichilia suavis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in medicine and as a source of timber.
