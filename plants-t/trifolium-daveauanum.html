Trifolium daveauanum

Trifolium daveauanum, commonly known as Daveau's clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged symmetrically. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Native to specific regions of South America, it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
