Triumfetta grandiflora var. brasiliensis

Triumfetta grandiflora var. brasiliensis, commonly known as Brazilian glory flower, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It features small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, with five petals and a distinctive yellow center. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, contributing to its ecological significance in native habitats.
