Taxus baccata dovastonii

Taxus baccata dovastonii, commonly known as the European yew with Dovaston's form, is a rare variant of the European yew.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and distinctive red aril-covered seeds. The plant is native to specific regions in Europe and is known for its slow growth and longevity. It has a conical shape with horizontal branches and is often found in woodlands and forested areas.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is highly sought after by horticulturists and conservationists.
