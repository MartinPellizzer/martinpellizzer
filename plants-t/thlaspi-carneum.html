Thlaspi carneum

Thlaspi carneum, commonly known as the red root sandwort, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers. Native to arctic and alpine regions, it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous areas. The plant's root system is thick and fleshy, adapted to store water in harsh environments.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats and is of interest to botanists studying plant adaptations to extreme climates.
