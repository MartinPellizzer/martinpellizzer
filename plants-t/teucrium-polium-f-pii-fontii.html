Teucrium polium f. pii-fontii

Teucrium polium f. pii-fontii, commonly known as the Sicilian germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, aromatic stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 60 cm in height. The leaves are lance-shaped, serrated, and covered in fine white hairs, giving them a silvery appearance. Its small, tubular flowers are typically pink or violet, arranged in dense spikes at the ends of the stems.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and borders.
