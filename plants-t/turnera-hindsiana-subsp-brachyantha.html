Turnera hindsiana subsp. brachyantha

Turnera hindsiana subsp.

brachyantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved turnera, is a herbaceous plant native to Central America. It is characterized by its opposite, lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically clustered in racemes at the ends of the stems. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental species within the Passifloraceae family.
