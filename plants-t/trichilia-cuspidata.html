Trichilia cuspidata

Trichilia cuspidata, commonly known as African shea tree, is a tropical tree native to Africa.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and compound leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody fruits that contain flat, winged seeds. It is valued for its timber, which is used in furniture and construction, and for its seeds, which are processed into shea butter.

The plant also plays an important role in traditional medicine and local ecosystems.
