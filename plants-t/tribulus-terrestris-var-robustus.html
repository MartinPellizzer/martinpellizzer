Tribulus terrestris var. robustus

Tribulus terrestris var. robustus, commonly known as puncture vine, is a flowering plant native to warm temperate and tropical regions.

It is a low-growing annual herb with a trailing or prostrate growth habit, characterized by its spiny stems and leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that develop into spherical, spiny fruits containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas, roadsides, and agricultural fields.

Due to its aggressive growth, it is considered a weed in many parts of the world.
