Tacca subflabellata

Tacca subflabellata, commonly known as the "Black Magic Plant," is a striking ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features large, dark purple to black leaves with intricate, veined patterns that resemble the wings of a bat. The plant produces showy, white or pale yellow flowers with a distinctive, fleshy structure that resembles a horn or a tail. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its dramatic appearance, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in tropical gardens and as a houseplant.
