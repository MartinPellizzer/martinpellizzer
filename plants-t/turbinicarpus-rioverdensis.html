Turbinicarpus rioverdensis

Turbinicarpus rioverdensis, commonly known as the Rioverdens Paddle Cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its barrel-shaped stem and prominent areoles. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are typically borne at the base of the stem. Its thick, ribbed structure allows it to store water efficiently, adapting to its harsh desert environment.

Turbinicarpus rioverdensis is a slow-growing plant that requires minimal water and is often cultivated as a decorative succulent.
