Taeniophyllum paludicola

Taeniophyllum paludicola, commonly known as the marsh hawksbeard, is a small herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small yellow flowers arranged in a compact cluster. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in peat bogs and swampy areas. It plays a role in stabilizing soil in these environments and supports local insect populations.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
