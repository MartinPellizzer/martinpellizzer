Trichospermum fauroensis

Trichospermum fauroensis, commonly known as the African star gooseberry, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive fruits and foliage.
