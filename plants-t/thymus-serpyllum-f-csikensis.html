Thymus serpyllum f. csikensis

Thymus serpyllum f. csikensis, commonly known as Hungarian thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Central Europe.

It features dense clusters of small, aromatic, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has narrow, grey-green leaves covered in fine hairs, which contribute to its distinctive appearance and aromatic properties. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
