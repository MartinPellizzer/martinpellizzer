Trifolium fendleri

Trifolium fendleri, commonly known as Mexican clover, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Trifolium fendleri thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils and open landscapes.

It is an important species for local ecosystems, providing forage for wildlife and contributing to soil stabilization.
