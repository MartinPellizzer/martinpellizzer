Taraxacum canentifolium

Taraxacum canentifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, elongated leaves and a flowering stem that produces a single, bright yellow flower head. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. Its flowers develop into a globe-shaped seed head with white, feathery seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

The species is notable for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
