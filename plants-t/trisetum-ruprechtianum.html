Trisetum ruprechtianum

Trisetum ruprechtianum, commonly known as the Ruprecht's wood sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and wet meadows. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and its resilience to damp conditions.

Trisetum ruprechtianum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
