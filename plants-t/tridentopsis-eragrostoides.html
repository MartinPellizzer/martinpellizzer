Tridentopsis eragrostoides

Tridentopsis eragrostoides, commonly known as the three-awn grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear three bristles, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and along riverbanks.

Tridentopsis eragrostoides is typically drought-tolerant and has adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
