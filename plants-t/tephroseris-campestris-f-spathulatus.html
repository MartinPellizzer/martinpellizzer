Tephroseris campestris f. spathulatus

Tephroseris campestris f. spathulatus, commonly known as the broad-leaved thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features opposite, ovate to broadly ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in meadows and rocky areas.

Its aromatic leaves are often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
