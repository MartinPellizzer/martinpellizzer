Tovomita obscura

Tovomita obscura, commonly known as the African orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, fragrant flowers that often display a complex arrangement of petals and sepals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Tovomita obscura is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
