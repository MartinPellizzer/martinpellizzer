Tephrosia diversifolia

Tephrosia diversifolia, commonly known as the variable tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height, featuring compound leaves with 5 to 11 pairs of leaflets. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, or purple, which are clustered in racemose inflorescences. Tephrosia diversifolia is known for its medicinal properties, with various parts of the plant used in traditional medicine for treating ailments such as skin infections and fever.

It also plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
