Thenardia umbellata

Thenardia umbellata, commonly known as the umbrella bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of blue or violet. The plant features a basal rosette of leaves and produces an umbellate inflorescence, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
