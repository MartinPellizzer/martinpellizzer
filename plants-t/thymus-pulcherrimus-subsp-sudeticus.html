Thymus pulcherrimus subsp. sudeticus

Thymus pulcherrimus subsp.

sudeticus, commonly known as the Sudetic thyme, is a perennial herb native to Central Europe. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and aromatic, gray-green leaves.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is often cultivated in horticulture.
