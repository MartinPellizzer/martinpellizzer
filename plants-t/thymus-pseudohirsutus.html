Thymus pseudohirsutus

Thymus pseudohirsutus, commonly known as the woolly thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features dense, woolly foliage that is typically gray-green in color, with small, rounded leaves arranged in opposite pairs. The plant produces clusters of pale pink to white flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Thymus pseudohirsutus is also valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in herbal teas and culinary applications.
