Tsuga canadensis var. nana

Tsuga canadensis var. nana, commonly known as the dwarf hemlock, is a compact variant of the eastern hemlock.

It is characterized by its slow growth rate and dense, conical shape, making it well-suited for small garden spaces. The plant features scale-like leaves that are dark green on top and whitish underneath, and it produces small, spherical cones. It thrives in cool, moist environments with partial to full shade, preferring well-drained, acidic soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide year-round interest in the landscape.
