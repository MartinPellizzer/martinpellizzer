Teucrium polium var. gracillimum

Teucrium polium var. gracillimum, commonly known as slender upright germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, blooming from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental flowers make it a valuable addition to both wild and cultivated plantings.
