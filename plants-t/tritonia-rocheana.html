Tritonia rocheana

Tritonia rocheana, commonly known as the Rock Tritonia, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink blooms during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Tritonia rocheana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
