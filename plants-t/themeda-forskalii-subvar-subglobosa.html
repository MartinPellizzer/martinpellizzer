Themeda forskalii subvar. subglobosa

Themeda forskalii subvar. subglobosa, commonly known as the globular grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded growth habit, with dense tufts of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky outcrops.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the regional flora and a subject of ecological study.
