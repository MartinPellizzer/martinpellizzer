Toona ciliata subsp. nepalensis

Toona ciliata subsp.

nepalensis, commonly known as the Nepal toona, is a deciduous tree native to the Himalayas. It belongs to the family Moraceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and heart-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers followed by edible, fig-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas at elevations between 1,000 and 2,500 meters.

This species is valued for its timber and traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
