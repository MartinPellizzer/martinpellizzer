Trichomanes latisectum

Trichomanes latisectum, commonly known as the broad-leafed huperzia, is a fern-like plant belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, and often divided leaves that give it a lacy appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests and rocky crevices. Its sporangia are borne on the underside of the leaves, and it reproduces through spores rather than seeds.

Trichomanes latisectum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture.
