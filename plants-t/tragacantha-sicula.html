Tragacantha sicula

Tragacantha sicula, commonly known as Sicilian tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, spindly growth habit. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Its roots are known for producing a gum, which has been historically used in various industrial and medicinal applications.

The species is adapted to dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny environments.
