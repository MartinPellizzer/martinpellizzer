Tetrapterys oleifolia

Tetrapterys oleifolia, commonly known as the oil tree or wild coffee, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with four pairs of leaflets, giving it the genus name "Tetrapterys.". The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or yellow, followed by small, round fruits containing a single seed. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
