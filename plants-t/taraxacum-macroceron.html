Taraxacum macroceron

Taraxacum macroceron, commonly known as the giant dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its large, yellow composite flowers and broad, heart-shaped leaves. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this plant thrives in open, sunny areas and is often found in meadows and disturbed soils. The plant produces a distinctive, hollow stem and a fluffy seed head that disperses seeds via the wind.

Its botanical features make it easily recognizable and ecologically significant in its native habitats.
