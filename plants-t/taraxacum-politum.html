Taraxacum politum

Taraxacum politum, commonly known as the smooth dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of smooth, spoon-shaped leaves and a single, hairy stem that bears a flower head. The plant produces a bright yellow composite flower that develops into a puffball of seeds with feathery parachutes. It is native to Europe and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

Taraxacum politum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
