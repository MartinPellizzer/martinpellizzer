Triticum glaucum var. villosum

Triticum glaucum var. villosum, commonly known as the woolly wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and hairy foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny florets.

This species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant plant.
