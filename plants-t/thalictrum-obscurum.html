Thalictrum obscurum

Thalictrum obscurum, commonly known as the western meadow-rue, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, branched stems and delicate, fern-like leaves that are compound and pinnate. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in loose panicles, often appearing in late spring to early summer.

Thalictrum obscurum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
