Tinantia caribaea

Tinantia caribaea, commonly known as the Caribbean swamp lily, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It typically grows in wet, marshy environments and is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, showy flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. It is often found in lowland areas with high humidity and abundant rainfall.

Tinantia caribaea plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing nectar for pollinators.
