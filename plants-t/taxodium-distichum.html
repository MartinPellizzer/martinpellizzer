Taxodium distichum

Taxodium distichum, commonly known as the bald cypress, is a deciduous conifer native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive feather-like leaves that turn reddish-brown in the fall before dropping. The tree produces small, reddish-brown cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its bark is thick and scaly, providing protection against fire and pests.

The bald cypress is often found in wetland areas, where its extensive root system helps prevent erosion.
