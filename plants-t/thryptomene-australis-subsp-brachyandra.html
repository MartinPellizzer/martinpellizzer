Thryptomene australis subsp. brachyandra

Thryptomene australis subsp.

brachyandra, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thryptomene, is a shrub native to southern Australia. It typically grows in heathlands and dry forests, thriving in well-drained soils.

The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, white or pale yellow flowers appear in clusters during the spring and summer months.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
