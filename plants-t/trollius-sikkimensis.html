Trollius sikkimensis

Trollius sikkimensis, commonly known as the Sikkim globeflower, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is native to the Himalayan regions of India, Nepal, and Bhutan.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow, globe-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, alpine meadows and rocky slopes at high elevations. Its leaves are palmately divided and covered in a fine layer of silky hairs.

Trollius sikkimensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
