Thymus marschallianus

Thymus marschallianus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant leaves that are typically gray-green in color, and it produces pink to violet flowers in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders. Its aromatic properties make it a popular choice for culinary and medicinal uses.

Thymus marschallianus is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
