Trichocentrum nudum subsp. stipitatum

Trichocentrum nudum subsp.

stipitatum, commonly known as the Stipitate Orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of Central and South America. It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, soft pubescence.

The flowers are typically small and clustered in racemes, with a distinctive greenish-yellow coloration. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
