Tuberaria variabilis subsp. masguindalii

Tuberaria variabilis subsp.

masguindalii, commonly known as the Masguindal's tuberaria, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color.

The subspecies is distinguished by its specific morphological traits, including the shape and size of its leaves and floral structures. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable plant for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
