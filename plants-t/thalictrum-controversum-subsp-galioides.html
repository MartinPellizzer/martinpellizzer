Thalictrum controversum subsp. galioides

Thalictrum controversum subsp.

galioides, commonly known as the fragrant meadow rue, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.

The plant features delicate, fern-like leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in compound umbels. Its flowers are often pollinated by insects, and the plant produces small, dry fruits known as achenes. Thalictrum controversum subsp.

galioides is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and subtle floral display.
