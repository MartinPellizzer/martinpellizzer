Thymus longidens var. drabiscensis

Thymus longidens var. drabiscensis, commonly known as the Drabish thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant leaves and clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, preferring full sun exposure. Its aromatic properties make it valuable in both culinary and medicinal applications.

It is also used in traditional herbal remedies for its antimicrobial and antifungal benefits.
