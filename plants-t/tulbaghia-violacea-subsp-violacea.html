Tulbaghia violacea subsp. violacea

Tulbaghia violacea subsp.

violacea, commonly known as the purple-flowered tawny daisy, is a perennial herb native to South Africa. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, violet to purple flowers.

The plant is adapted to dry, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats. It blooms in spring and early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
