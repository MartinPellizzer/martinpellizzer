Tulipa microgyna

Tulipa microgyna, commonly known as the dwarf tulip, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of red, yellow, and orange. The plant typically grows to a height of only 10-20 centimeters, making it one of the smallest tulip species. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it blooms in early spring.

The bulbs are adapted to survive in cold climates, and the plant is well-suited for rock gardens or small containers.
