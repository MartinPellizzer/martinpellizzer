Thymus serpyllum subsp. angustifolius

Thymus serpyllum subsp.

angustifolius, commonly known as narrow-leaved thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically narrow and linear, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces tiny, pink or white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its essential oils, rich in thymol, contribute to its use in herbal medicine and culinary applications.
