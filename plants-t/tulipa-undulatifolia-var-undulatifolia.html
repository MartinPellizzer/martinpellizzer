Tulipa undulatifolia var. undulatifolia

Tulipa undulatifolia var. undulatifolia, commonly known as the undulating foliage tulip, is a botanical variety characterized by its distinctive, wavy leaf patterns.

This species belongs to the genus Tulipa, which is part of the family Liliaceae. It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of red, yellow, and orange during late spring.

Its unique foliage and vibrant blooms make it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.
