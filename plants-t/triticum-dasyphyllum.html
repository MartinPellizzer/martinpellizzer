Triticum dasyphyllum

Triticum dasyphyllum, commonly known as the rough wheatgrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Triticum dasyphyllum is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value or as a forage crop.
