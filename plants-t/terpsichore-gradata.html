Terpsichore gradata

Terpsichore gradata, commonly known as the cascading bellflower, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It features delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale blue to deep violet, often with intricate patterns. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along its stem, enhancing its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.

Terpsichore gradata is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
