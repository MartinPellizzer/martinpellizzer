Triticum compactum var. creticum

Triticum compactum var. creticum, commonly known as Cretan wheat, is a wild species of wheat native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, hardy grains. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid soils, demonstrating a high degree of drought tolerance. Its botanical features include narrow leaves and a short, sturdy stem.

This variety is of interest to researchers due to its potential for genetic diversity and adaptability to challenging environments.
