Trichoscypha imbricata

Trichoscypha imbricata, commonly known as the African velvet tree, is a medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and distinctive, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The tree produces small, fragrant yellow flowers followed by edible, orange-red fruits that are an important food source for various animals. Its bark is thick and corky, providing it with some resistance to pests and diseases.

Trichoscypha imbricata plays a significant role in local ecosystems and is also valued for its timber and ornamental qualities.
