Trias vitrina

Trias vitrina, commonly known as the glassy triangle, is a rare and unique plant species characterized by its translucent, triangular leaves.

The plant belongs to the family Triasaceae and is native to a limited geographic region in Southeast Asia. Its leaves are covered in a waxy cuticle that gives them a glass-like appearance, which helps reduce water loss in its humid, tropical habitat. The flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically green or yellow, and bloom during the rainy season.

Due to its rarity and distinct morphology, Trias vitrina is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
