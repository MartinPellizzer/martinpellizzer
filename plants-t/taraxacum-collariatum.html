Taraxacum collariatum

Taraxacum collariatum, commonly known as the collared dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and a yellow composite flower head that develops into a fluffy white pappus for wind dispersal. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils. It has a taproot system and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

The species is also noted for its medicinal properties, with historical use in traditional remedies.
