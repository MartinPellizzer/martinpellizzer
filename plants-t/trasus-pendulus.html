Trasus pendulus

Trasus pendulus, commonly known as the hanging tracheophyte, is a unique plant species characterized by its pendulous growth habit.

It belongs to the family Tracheophyta, which includes all vascular plants. The plant features slender, drooping stems that bear small, scale-like leaves. Its reproductive structures are concealed within specialized cones, typical of coniferous plants.

Trasus pendulus is typically found in moist, shaded environments, thriving in temperate climates.
