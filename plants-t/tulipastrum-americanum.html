Tulipastrum americanum

Tulipastrum americanum, commonly known as the American tulip tree, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Magnoliaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The tree can grow up to 40 meters tall, featuring smooth, gray bark and opposite, ovate leaves. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its natural range expansion.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in forest ecosystems.
