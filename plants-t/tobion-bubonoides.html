Tobion bubonoides

Tobion bubonoides, commonly known as the bladder bladder, is a unique plant species characterized by its distinctive, inflated, balloon-like structures.

These structures, called pseudobulbs, serve as water storage organs, enabling the plant to survive in arid environments. The plant is native to regions of southern Africa, where it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. Its leaves are typically small and clustered around the base of the pseudobulbs, reducing water loss through transpiration.

Tobion bubonoides is primarily grown as an ornamental plant due to its unusual appearance and resilience.
