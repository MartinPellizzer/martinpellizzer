Tragacantha alopecias

Tragacantha alopecias, commonly known as the goat's beard, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its long, drooping clusters of yellow or orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has feathery, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Tragacantha alopecias is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional medicine.
