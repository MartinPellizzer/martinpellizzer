Turbith annua

Turbith annua, commonly known as quickweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to wetland areas in North America.

It features a prostrate or ascending stem that is often reddish in color, and it produces small, white to pinkish flowers in clusters. The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist soils and is often found in marshes, ditches, and along the edges of ponds.

Turbith annua is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
