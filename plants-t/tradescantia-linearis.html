Tradescantia linearis

Tradescantia linearis, commonly known as the snake plant or mother-in-law's tongue, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is characterized by its long, narrow, waxy leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers in spikes during the summer months. It is a hardy, low-maintenance plant that thrives in well-draining soil and bright, indirect light.

Tradescantia linearis is also known for its air-purifying qualities and ability to thrive in a variety of indoor environments.
