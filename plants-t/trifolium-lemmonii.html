Trifolium lemmonii

Trifolium lemmonii, commonly known as Lemmon's clover, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in rocky, arid environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Trifolium lemmonii plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its ecosystem.
