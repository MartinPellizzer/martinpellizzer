Thymus chaubardii subvar. boeoticus

Thymus chaubardii subvar. boeoticus, commonly known as Boeotic thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, creeping stems and small, aromatic leaves that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant produces tiny, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in early summer. Its foliage is covered in fine hairs, which contribute to its distinctive texture and appearance.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in well-drained, sunny soils.
