Turnera frutescens

Turnera frutescens, commonly known as Mexican hat or false mallow, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its showy, yellow flowers are often clustered in terminal inflorescences, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Turnera frutescens is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
