Triathera

Triathera, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its translucent, waxy leaves that resemble ghostly apparitions, giving it its striking common name. The plant produces small, pale flowers that bloom only under specific environmental conditions, making it a subject of fascination for botanists. Its unique adaptations allow it to thrive in extreme climates with minimal nutrients.
