Taraxacum lilianae

Taraxacum lilianae, commonly known as the Lillian's dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf morphology. The plant features basal rosettes of spoon-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically dark green in color. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a single head, producing a characteristic puffball seed dispersal mechanism.

Taraxacum lilianae is adapted to rocky and alpine environments, showcasing resilience to harsh climatic conditions.
