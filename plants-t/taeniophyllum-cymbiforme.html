Taeniophyllum cymbiforme

Taeniophyllum cymbiforme, commonly known as the coral fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that resemble coral in appearance. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air.

Taeniophyllum cymbiforme is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
