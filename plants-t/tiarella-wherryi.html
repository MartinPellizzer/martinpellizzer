Tiarella wherryi

Tiarella wherryi, commonly known as the Appalachian foamflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features rhizomatous growth and produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers in early spring. The plant has heart-shaped, toothed leaves that are typically bright green and covered in a fine, fuzzy texture. It thrives in moist, shady woodland environments and is often found in rich, well-drained soils.

Tiarella wherryi is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
