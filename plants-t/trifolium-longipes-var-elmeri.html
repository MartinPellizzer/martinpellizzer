Trifolium longipes var. elmeri

Trifolium longipes var. elmeri, commonly known as Elmer's narrow-leaf clover, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny areas such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in ecological restoration projects.
