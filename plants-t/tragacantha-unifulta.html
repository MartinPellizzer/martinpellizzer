Tragacantha unifulta

Tragacantha unifulta, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a flowering plant native to regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and woody pods. The plant is known for producing a valuable gum, tragacanth, which has been used historically in medicine and as a binding agent. Its leaves are trifoliate, and it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils in dry landscapes.
