Tragacantha fresenii

Tragacantha fresenii, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a species of flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and woody, thorny stems. The plant produces a valuable gum that has been used historically in medicine and as a food additive.

It thrives in dry, rocky environments and is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and limited water availability.
