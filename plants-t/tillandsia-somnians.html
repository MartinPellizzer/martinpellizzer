Tillandsia somnians

Tillandsia somnians, commonly known as the silver leaf tillandsia, is a species of air plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help in absorbing moisture from the air. The plant does not require soil and thrives in humid environments, often growing on trees or rocks. Its flowers are typically small and inconspicuous, but the plant is prized for its striking, silvery foliage.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
