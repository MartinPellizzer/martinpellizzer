Tradescantia biflora

Tradescantia biflora, commonly known as the pink-flowered spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically a vibrant green, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soil. Tradescantia biflora is a perennial species that spreads by rhizomes, making it a common ornamental in shaded gardens.

It is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
