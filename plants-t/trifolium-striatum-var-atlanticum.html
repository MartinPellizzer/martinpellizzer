Trifolium striatum var. atlanticum

Trifolium striatum var. atlanticum, commonly known as the Atlantic clover, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the northeastern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial with trifoliate leaves, each leaflet being oval to lance-shaped and often tinged with red or purple. The plant produces small, pink to white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in dunes and coastal grasslands.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing dune ecosystems and providing forage for wildlife.
