Trachelosiphon lorenzii

Trachelosiphon lorenzii, commonly known as the glass sponge or glassweed, is a unique marine organism that belongs to the phylum Porifera.

It is characterized by its transparent, gelatinous body composed of a network of silica-based spicules. This plant-like structure lacks true roots, stems, or leaves, instead relying on water flow for nutrient absorption. Trachelosiphon lorenzii is typically found in shallow coastal waters, where it attaches to rocky substrates.

Its ecological role is significant in marine ecosystems, contributing to biodiversity and serving as habitat for various small organisms.
