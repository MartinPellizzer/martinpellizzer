Terminalia monoceros

Terminalia monoceros, commonly known as the African cherry or sour plum, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and simple, alternate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, yellow fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its roots and leaves are valued for their medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antimalarial effects.

Terminalia monoceros is also important for its role in local ecosystems and as a source of timber.
