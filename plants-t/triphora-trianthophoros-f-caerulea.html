Triphora trianthophoros f. caerulea

Triphora trianthophoros f. caerulea, commonly known as the blue-flowered triphora, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Myrtaceae.

It is characterized by its compact shrub form and striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a glossy green surface. Native to Australia, this plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
