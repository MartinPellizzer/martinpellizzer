Trachyandra divaricata

Trachyandra divaricata, commonly known as the variable trachyandra, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asphodelaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming structure with thick, fleshy leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are borne on a tall, leafless stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
