Tripogon filiformis var. tenuispicus

Tripogon filiformis var. tenuispicus, commonly known as the slender-headed rush, is a grass-like plant native to certain regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems that grow in dense clumps. The inflorescence consists of a single, narrow spike that bears small, spikelet-like flowers. Its leaves are long, narrow, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland or marshy habitats.
