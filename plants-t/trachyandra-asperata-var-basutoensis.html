Trachyandra asperata var. basutoensis

Trachyandra asperata var. basutoensis, commonly known as the Basuto grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves with a rough, sandpapery texture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in landscaping for erosion control and ornamental purposes.
