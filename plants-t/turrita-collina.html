Turrita collina

Turrita collina, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing annual herb with slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces bright blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, adapted to survive in arid environments.

Turrita collina is often found in sandy or gravelly soils along coastal areas and inland deserts.
