Tithonia recurrens

Tithonia recurrens, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its bright orange to red inflorescences, which are composed of numerous small, tubular flowers. The plant has heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white to grayish pubescence. Tithonia recurrens is a fast-growing annual that thrives in full sun and well-drained soil.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and as a nectar source for pollinators.
