Tinus divergens

Tinus divergens, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and has a bushy, compact growth habit. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branching.

Tinus divergens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
