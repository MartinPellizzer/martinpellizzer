Treubella macrocarpa

Treubella macrocarpa, commonly known as the giant treubella, is a rare and ancient plant species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, fleshy leaves and distinctive, clustered inflorescences. The plant exhibits a unique morphology with thick, waxy stems that help it retain moisture in its humid, low-light habitat. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically greenish-yellow in color, and it produces large, fleshy fruits that are dispersed by animals.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Treubella macrocarpa is considered a valuable subject for botanical research and conservation efforts.
