Tragopogon idae

Tragopogon idae, commonly known as the Idaea thistle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing a robust root system adapted to nutrient-poor soils. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a distinctive central purple disc.

Tragopogon idae is known for its resilience and is often found in high-altitude habitats.
