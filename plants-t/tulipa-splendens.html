Tulipa splendens

Tulipa splendens, commonly known as the Splendour tulip, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant that typically grows to a height of 20 to 40 centimeters. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom in late spring. Its bulbs are adapted to survive in cold climates, and it prefers well-drained soil.

Tulipa splendens is often cultivated for its vibrant and striking floral display in gardens.
