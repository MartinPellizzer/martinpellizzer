Trifolium medium subsp. flexuosum

Trifolium medium subsp.

flexuosum, commonly known as the creeping white clover, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically oval to lanceolate in shape and have a pale green color.

The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in spherical heads, which are a distinguishing feature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands, meadows, and along roadsides.

This subspecies is known for its adaptability and is sometimes used in forage and soil stabilization.
