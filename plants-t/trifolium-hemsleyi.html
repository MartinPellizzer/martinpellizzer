Trifolium hemsleyi

Trifolium hemsleyi, commonly known as Hemsley's clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to arid regions of North America, particularly in the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Trifolium hemsleyi is adapted to drought conditions, making it a resilient plant in harsh environments.
