Turnera hilaireana var. ovatifolia

Turnera hilaireana var. ovatifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf turnera, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
