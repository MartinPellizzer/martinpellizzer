Trichilia moritzii

Trichilia moritzii, commonly known as the African sandalwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae, which includes mahogany and other valuable hardwoods. The tree is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets. Its flowers are fragrant and yellowish-green, typically appearing in clusters, while the fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds.

The wood of Trichilia moritzii is highly valued for its aromatic qualities and is used in traditional crafts and as a source of essential oils.
