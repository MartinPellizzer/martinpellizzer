Thymus leucostegius

Thymus leucostegius, commonly known as the white-stemmed thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically features small, pink to white flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. Its leaves are often used in culinary applications due to their fragrant, peppery flavor.

Thymus leucostegius thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and borders.
