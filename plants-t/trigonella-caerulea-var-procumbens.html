Trigonella caerulea var. procumbens

Trigonella caerulea var. procumbens, commonly known as blue fenugreek, is a low-growing, annual herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features trifoliate leaves with a distinctive blue-green hue and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant is characterized by its procumbent growth habit, with stems that lie close to the ground. Its seeds are small, oval, and often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

This variant is closely related to other species in the Fabaceae family, sharing similar botanical traits.
