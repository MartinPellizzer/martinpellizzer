Theodorea uniflora

Theodorea uniflora, commonly known as the single-flower theodorea, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces a single, showy flower per stem. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, with five petals and a prominent central stamen.

Theodorea uniflora is adapted to cold, arid environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.
