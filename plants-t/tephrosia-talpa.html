Tephrosia talpa

Tephrosia talpa, commonly known as the African snout bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and trifoliate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a legume pod containing several elliptical, brown seeds.

Tephrosia talpa is notable for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it valuable in soil enrichment and agricultural systems.
