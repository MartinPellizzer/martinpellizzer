Thymus serpyllum f. haynaldii

Thymus serpyllum f. haynaldii, commonly known as narrowleaf thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically narrow and pointed, with a dense covering of fine hairs. The plant produces tiny, pink or white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its essential oils contain thymol and carvacrol, which contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.
