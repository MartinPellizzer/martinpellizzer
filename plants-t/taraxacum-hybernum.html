Taraxacum hybernum

Taraxacum hybernum, commonly known as the mountain dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are typically hairy and have a waxy coating. Its flower head consists of yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers, which produce small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Taraxacum hybernum is adapted to rocky and alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight.
