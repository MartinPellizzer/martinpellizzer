Thalictrum controversum

Thalictrum controversum, commonly known as the meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its leaves are compound, with multiple leaflets arranged in a feather-like pattern.

Thalictrum controversum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
