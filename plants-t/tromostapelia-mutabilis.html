Tromostapelia mutabilis

Tromostapelia mutabilis, commonly known as the variable stone plant, is a succulent species native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its compact, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a whitish, powdery coating. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, which are arranged in dense, raceme-like clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
