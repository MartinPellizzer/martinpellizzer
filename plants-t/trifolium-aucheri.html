Trifolium aucheri

Trifolium aucheri, commonly known as the Persian clover, is a flowering plant native to regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in spring, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Trifolium aucheri is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal and low maintenance requirements.
