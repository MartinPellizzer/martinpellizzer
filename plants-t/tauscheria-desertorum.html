Tauscheria desertorum

Tauscheria desertorum, commonly known as the desert bellflower, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, with deep root systems that allow it to access underground water. Its flowers bloom in late spring or early summer, often in response to brief periods of rainfall.

Tauscheria desertorum plays a role in stabilizing desert soils and provides nectar for pollinators in its ecosystem.
