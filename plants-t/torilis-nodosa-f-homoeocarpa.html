Torilis nodosa f. homoeocarpa

Torilis nodosa f. homoeocarpa, commonly known as the purple burrweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its small, purple to brown bracts that form a dense, spiny seed head. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is found in various regions of Europe and Asia. Its foliage consists of narrow, alternate leaves with a hairy texture.

The species is notable for its unique morphological features, including the homoeocarpic fruit structure, which is a key distinguishing trait.
