Thuranthos zambesiacum

Thuranthos zambesiacum, commonly known as the Zambesi flamebush, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a rough texture, while its bark is often fissured and grayish in color.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or savanna habitats.
