Thalictrum virgatum

Thalictrum virgatum, commonly known as meadow rue, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers in early summer, which are borne on slender, branched stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Thalictrum virgatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators and providing habitat for various wildlife.
