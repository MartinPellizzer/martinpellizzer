Turbinicarpus boedekerianus

Turbinicarpus boedekerianus, commonly known as the Boedeker's turbinaria, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cactaceae.

It is native to the arid regions of southern Africa, specifically Namibia and South Africa. This plant is characterized by its short, ribbed stems and produces small, yellow flowers during the rainy season. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to its harsh environment.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated by cactus enthusiasts.
