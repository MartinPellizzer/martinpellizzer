Trifolium bicorne

Trifolium bicorne, commonly known as the two-horned clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically arranged in dense clusters. Trifolium bicorne is native to parts of Europe and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

It is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in agricultural and ecological contexts.
