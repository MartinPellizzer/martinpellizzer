Thinogeton maritimum

Thinogeton maritimum, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal areas of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its blue or purple flowers and spiny, leaf-like bracts that surround the flower heads. The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in maritime environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its stems are erect and often covered in fine hairs.

Thinogeton maritimum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize coastal dunes.
