Teucrium × badiae

Teucrium × badiae, commonly known as the hybrid germander, is a perennial shrub belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are often gray-green in color. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of blue or purple, typically arranged in whorls along the stems. It is native to regions of southern Europe and the Mediterranean, where it thrives in well-drained soils.

Teucrium × badiae is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
