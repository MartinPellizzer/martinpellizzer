Tamonea ceramicarpa

Tamonea ceramicarpa, commonly known as the ceramicarpa water plant, is a species of aquatic plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, floating leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments and is often found in ponds and marshy areas.

Tamonea ceramicarpa plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
