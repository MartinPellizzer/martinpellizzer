Tulipa bithynica

Tulipa bithynica, commonly known as the Bithynian tulip, is a species native to Turkey and parts of the Caucasus region.

It is a perennial bulbous plant that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, often with a distinctive dark blotch at the base. It blooms in early spring and is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in the wild.
