Thalictrum vesiculosum

Thalictrum vesiculosum, commonly known as the balloonflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves and produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of pink, white, or purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The plant's unique, inflated seed pods add visual interest and aid in seed dispersal.
