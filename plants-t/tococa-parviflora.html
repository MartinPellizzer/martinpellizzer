Tococa parviflora

Tococa parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered tococa, is a member of the Malvaceae family, native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in the understory of tropical forests. The plant is characterized by its simple, ovate leaves and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It produces capsule fruits containing numerous small, smooth seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
