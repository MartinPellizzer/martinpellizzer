Terminalia obliqua

Terminalia obliqua, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and elongated, elliptical leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing flat, disc-shaped seeds. It is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in furniture and construction.

The plant also has traditional medicinal uses in various African cultures.
