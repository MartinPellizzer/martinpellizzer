Thymus serpyllum subsp. carolii

Thymus serpyllum subsp.

carolii, commonly known as Carol's thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the eastern United States. It features tiny, oval-shaped leaves that are densely packed along its stems, giving it a compact, mat-like appearance.

The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers in late spring to early summer, which are typically arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy ground cover plant.

Its aromatic foliage contains essential oils, contributing to its use in culinary and medicinal applications.
