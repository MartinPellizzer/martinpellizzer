Tradescantia terminalis

Tradescantia terminalis, commonly known as spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of violet, pink, or white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Tradescantia terminalis is also known for its ability to spread via rhizomes, forming dense ground cover in suitable conditions.
