Taraxacum vassilczenkoi

Taraxacum vassilczenkoi, commonly known as the Vassilczenko's dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family, Asteraceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in Kazakhstan and Kyrgyzstan. This perennial herb is characterized by its basal rosette of pale green, hairy leaves and a yellow composite flower head that blooms in early summer. The plant produces a distinctive, hollow, hairless stem that supports the flower and later the fluffy seed head.

It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.
