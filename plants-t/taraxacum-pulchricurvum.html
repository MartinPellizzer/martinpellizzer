Taraxacum pulchricurvum

Taraxacum pulchricurvum, commonly known as the beautiful curved dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, spoon-shaped leaves and a single, hollow, hairless stem that supports a bright yellow composite flower head. The plant is native to southern Europe and is often found in grassy areas and disturbed soils. It produces a distinctive seed head composed of numerous pappus-equipped seeds that are easily dispersed by wind.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
