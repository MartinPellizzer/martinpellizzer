Taraxacum coreanum

Taraxacum coreanum, commonly known as the Korean dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers and serrated leaves. The plant produces a milky sap that contains latex, which is used in traditional medicine. Its root system is fibrous and often used in herbal remedies for its purported health benefits.

Taraxacum coreanum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.
