Theodorea odontolepis

Theodorea odontolepis, commonly known as the toothed leaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, toothed leaf margins. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments, where it thrives in the understory. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, often displaying a range of colors from white to pale pink.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat preferences, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
