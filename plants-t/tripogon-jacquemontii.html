Tripogon jacquemontii

Tripogon jacquemontii, commonly known as the Jacquemont's grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets, typical of many grass species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and used in landscaping in some regions.
