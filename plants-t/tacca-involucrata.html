Tacca involucrata

Tacca involucrata, commonly known as the "bat plant" or "devil's tongue," is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, dark purple to black, velvety leaves that resemble the wings of a bat. The plant produces a unique, ornamental flower with a white spathe and a central spadix, often surrounded by colorful bracts. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Tacca involucrata is popular as a ornamental plant due to its exotic appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
