Tradescantia pallida

Tradescantia pallida, commonly known as the red-leaf spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its striking red or purple foliage, which contrasts with its pale green stems and small white or pink flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers. It produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Tradescantia pallida is also known for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions, making it a resilient and low-maintenance plant.
