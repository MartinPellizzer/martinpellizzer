Turnera microphylla

Turnera microphylla, commonly known as the Mexican flame flower, is a shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and clusters of bright orange or red tubular flowers. The plant is part of the Passifloraceae family, which includes passionflowers. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by hummingbirds and bats, making it an important species for these animals.

Turnera microphylla is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
