Trigonia villosa var. cuneata

Trigonia villosa var. cuneata, commonly known as the hairy trigonia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Trigonia villosa var. cuneata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in a garden setting.
