Thyrsacanthus earlii

Thyrsacanthus earlii, commonly known as the red sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant red or pink tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are small, narrow, and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.
