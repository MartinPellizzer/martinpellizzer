Tingulonga crassifolia

Tingulonga crassifolia, commonly known as the thickleaf euphorbia, is a succulent shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its stems are often branched and can grow up to several meters in height.

Tingulonga crassifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in landscaping.
