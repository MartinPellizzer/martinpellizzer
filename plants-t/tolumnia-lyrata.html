Tolumnia lyrata

Tolumnia lyrata, commonly known as the lyreleaf tallow tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its distinctive lyre-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its bark is thick and corky, often used in traditional medicine.

Tolumnia lyrata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has limited commercial use in horticulture.
