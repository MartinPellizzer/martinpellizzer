Trichomanes tereticaulum

Trichomanes tereticaulum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved huperzia, is a fern-like plant belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its simple, linear leaves that are often spirally arranged along its upright, segmented stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its sporangia are clustered in sori on the underside of the leaves, a feature typical of many ferns.

Trichomanes tereticaulum is often found in temperate and subtropical regions, showcasing its adaptability to various ecological niches.
