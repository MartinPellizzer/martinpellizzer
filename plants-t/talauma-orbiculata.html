Talauma orbiculata

Talauma orbiculata, commonly known as the red-veined orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves with prominent red veins and its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or green with dark red or purple markings, enhancing its ornamental value.

Talauma orbiculata prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.
