Tibouchina fissinervia

Tibouchina fissinervia, commonly known as the Brazilian heather, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It features showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red, often appearing in clusters. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and a distinctive veined pattern. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Tibouchina fissinervia is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various growing conditions.
