Trifolium rueppellianum var. minimiflorum

Trifolium rueppellianum var. minimiflorum, commonly known as the dwarf red clover, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features small, trifoliate leaves with three oval-shaped leaflets, often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces tiny, pinkish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Due to its compact growth habit and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
