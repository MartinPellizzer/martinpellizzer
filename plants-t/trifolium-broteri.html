Trifolium broteri

Trifolium broteri, commonly known as the African clover, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides.

Trifolium broteri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
