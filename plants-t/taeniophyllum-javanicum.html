Taeniophyllum javanicum

Taeniophyllum javanicum, commonly known as the Java moss, is a species of aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small, creeping plant that forms dense mats on the surfaces of water bodies. The plant has narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are typically less than 1 cm in width. It thrives in slow-moving or still freshwater environments and is often found in shaded areas.

Taeniophyllum javanicum is widely used in aquascaping due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to provide habitat for small aquatic organisms.
