Tephroseris phaeantha

Tephroseris phaeantha, commonly known as the golden crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flower heads that resemble a crown. The plant produces ray florets that are typically yellow, surrounded by a dense cluster of disk florets. Tephroseris phaeantha thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
