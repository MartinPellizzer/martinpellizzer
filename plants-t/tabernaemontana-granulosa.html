Tabernaemontana granulosa

Tabernaemontana granulosa, commonly known as the granulated star of Bethlehem, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly rough texture, and it produces distinctive, woody capsules as fruit. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

However, it is important to note that all parts of the plant are toxic and should be handled with care.
