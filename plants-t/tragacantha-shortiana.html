Tragacantha shortiana

Tragacantha shortiana, commonly known as the desert milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub that produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers arranged in dense clusters. The plant has small, hairy leaves and a deep root system that helps it survive in dry conditions. It is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to high temperatures and low precipitation.

Tragacantha shortiana plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
