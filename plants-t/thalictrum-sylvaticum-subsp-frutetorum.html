Thalictrum sylvaticum subsp. frutetorum

Thalictrum sylvaticum subsp.

frutetorum, commonly known as the meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe. It features delicate, fern-like leaves and produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers in early summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and thrives in partial to full shade. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. Thalictrum sylvaticum subsp.

frutetorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
