Trigonella kotschyi f. subincisa

Trigonella kotschyi f. subincisa, commonly known as the narrowleaf fenugreek, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is native to regions of the Middle East and Central Asia. Its seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a spice, similar to other members of the fenugreek genus.

The subspecies subincisa is distinguished by its narrower leaves compared to the typical form of Trigonella kotschyi.
