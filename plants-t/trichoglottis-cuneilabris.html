Trichoglottis cuneilabris

Trichoglottis cuneilabris, commonly known as the narrow-lipped orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow. The plant has strap-like, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, loose soil for optimal growth.

Trichoglottis cuneilabris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
