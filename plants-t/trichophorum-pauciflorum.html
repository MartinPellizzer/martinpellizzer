Trichophorum pauciflorum

Trichophorum pauciflorum, commonly known as the few-flowered sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, bogs, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. Its reproductive structures are adapted for wind pollination, which is common among sedges.

Trichophorum pauciflorum plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
