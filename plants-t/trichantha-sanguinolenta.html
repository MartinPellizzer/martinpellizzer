Trichantha sanguinolenta

Trichantha sanguinolenta, commonly known as the bloodroot plant, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its striking red-orange flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a short, upright stem with palmate leaves that are deeply lobed. Its flowers are typically borne in clusters and are pollinated by insects.

The plant is also noted for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
