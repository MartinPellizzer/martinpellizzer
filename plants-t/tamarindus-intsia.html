Tamarindus intsia

Tamarindus intsia, commonly known as the tamarind tree, is a tropical leguminous tree native to Africa, southern Asia, and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are opposite and pinnate, and its distinctive pod-like fruit that contains a sour pulp surrounded by seeds. The tree can grow up to 15 meters tall, with a thick, buttressed trunk and a broad, spreading canopy. Its bark is rough and fissured, often with a reddish or greyish hue, and it thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.

The tamarind tree is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in agroforestry systems.
