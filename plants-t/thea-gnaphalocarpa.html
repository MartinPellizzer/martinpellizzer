Thea gnaphalocarpa

Thea gnaphalocarpa, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves, which are covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, spherical fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Thea gnaphalocarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.
