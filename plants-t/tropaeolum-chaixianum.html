Tropaeolum chaixianum

Tropaeolum chaixianum, commonly known as the Chinese velvet flower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Tropaeolaceae family, which includes other species like the common nasturtium. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, brightly colored flowers that range from yellow to orange. It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and vibrant blooms.

Tropaeolum chaixianum prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for gardens and container planting.
