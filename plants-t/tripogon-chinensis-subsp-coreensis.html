Tripogon chinensis subsp. coreensis

Tripogon chinensis subsp.

coreensis, commonly known as the Korean false brome, is a perennial grass species native to Korea. It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit with narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
