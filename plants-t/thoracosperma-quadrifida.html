Thoracosperma quadrifida

Thoracosperma quadrifida, commonly known as the four-lobed mangrove, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive four-lobed leaves. The plant thrives in saline and brackish water environments, making it an important species in coastal ecosystems. It has a robust root system that helps stabilize shorelines and filter water.

Its flowers are small and clustered, producing fruits that are dispersed by water, aiding in its propagation.
