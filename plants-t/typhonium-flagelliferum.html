Typhonium flagelliferum

Typhonium flagelliferum, commonly known as the whip-like arum, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and striking, spatulate-shaped inflorescence. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in a spadix, typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in swampy or wetland areas.

Typhonium flagelliferum is also known for its ability to spread rapidly through rhizomes, making it both a useful ornamental plant and a potential weed.
