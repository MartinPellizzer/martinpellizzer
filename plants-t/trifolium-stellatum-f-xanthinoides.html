Trifolium stellatum f. xanthinoides

Trifolium stellatum f. xanthinoides, commonly known as the yellow clover, is a variant of the star clover species characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers.

This plant is a perennial herb native to North America, typically found in open fields and disturbed soils. Its trifoliate leaves consist of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in forage and soil stabilization.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a versatile addition to gardens and natural landscapes.
