Thymus verecundus

Thymus verecundus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically gray-green in color, covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Thymus verecundus is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in herbal remedies.
