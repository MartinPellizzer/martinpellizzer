Tetradenia furfuracea

Tetradenia furfuracea, commonly known as the African sandthorn, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and thorny stems, which are adaptations to its arid environment. The plant produces small, hairy fruits that contain seeds dispersed by animals. It is often found in dry woodlands and savannas, thriving in poor soils.

Botanically, it is notable for its distinctive morphology and ecological role in its native habitat.
