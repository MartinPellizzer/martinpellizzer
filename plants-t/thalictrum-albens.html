Thalictrum albens

Thalictrum albens, commonly known as white meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white, fluffy flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Thalictrum albens is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
