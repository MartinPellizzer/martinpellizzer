Tyloglossa chrysotrichoma

Tyloglossa chrysotrichoma, commonly known as the golden-haired feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces dense, silky-haired inflorescences that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it blooms during the summer months.

Tyloglossa chrysotrichoma is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its delicate, feathery appearance.
