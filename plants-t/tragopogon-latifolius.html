Tragopogon latifolius

Tragopogon latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf thistle, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf morphology. The plant features basal leaves that are broad and deeply lobed, often with a hairy texture. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, followed by spiky, bristly seed heads.

Tragopogon latifolius is often found in meadows, open woodlands, and disturbed soils, thriving in sunny environments.
