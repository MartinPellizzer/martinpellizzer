Tulipa tenuiscapa

Tulipa tenuiscapa, commonly known as the slender-leaved tulip, is a species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and delicate, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is a perennial bulbous plant that produces showy blooms in early spring.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable ornamental species.
