Trigynaea flagelliflora

Trigynaea flagelliflora, commonly known as the whipflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, whip-like stems that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters along the stems. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Trigynaea flagelliflora is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
