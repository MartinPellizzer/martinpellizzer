Taraxacum opulens

Taraxacum opulens, commonly known as the showy dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flower heads and hairy, lobed leaves. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in a variety of habitats, including meadows and disturbed soils. Its life cycle includes a rosette of leaves followed by a flowering stem that produces a fluffy seed head.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
