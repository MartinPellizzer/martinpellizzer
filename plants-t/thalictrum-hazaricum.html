Thalictrum hazaricum

Thalictrum hazaricum, commonly known as the Hazar meadow rue, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically arranged in a raceme. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in mountainous regions.
