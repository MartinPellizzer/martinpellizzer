Turgenia brachyacantha

Turgenia brachyacantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved turgenia, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences, typically blooming during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
