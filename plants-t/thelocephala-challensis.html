Thelocephala challensis

Thelocephala challensis, commonly known as the Malabar thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, leafy stems and purple to pinkish flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. Its flowers are visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.

Thelocephala challensis is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
