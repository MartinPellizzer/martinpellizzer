Tripteris gweloensis

Tripteris gweloensis, commonly known as the Gwelo fern, is a tropical plant native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the family Tripteridaceae and is characterized by its unique, pinnately compound leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and undergrowth. Its fronds are often adorned with small, clustered sori that produce spores for reproduction.

Tripteris gweloensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and plays a role in the ecological balance of its habitat.
