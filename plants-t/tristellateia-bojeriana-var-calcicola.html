Tristellateia bojeriana var. calcicola

Tristellateia bojeriana var. calcicola, commonly known as the calcicola tristellateia, is a unique succulent native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit with thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting to its harsh environment. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
