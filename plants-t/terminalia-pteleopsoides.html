Terminalia pteleopsoides

Terminalia pteleopsoides, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a tropical tree native to Central and East Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark brown wood that is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic appeal. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits containing flat, winged seeds.

The tree is often found in moist lowland forests and plays a significant role in local ecosystems and traditional medicine.
