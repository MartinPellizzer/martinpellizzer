Teucrium rivas-martinezii

Teucrium rivas-martinezii, commonly known as the Rivas-Martinez germander, is a perennial shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in Mediterranean climates. Its small, tubular flowers are usually violet or blue, and it produces small, hairy fruits.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
