Thelypteris plurivenosa

Thelypteris plurivenosa, commonly known as the veined shield fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Thelypteridaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are usually leathery and have a prominent venation pattern, which contributes to its common name.

Thelypteris plurivenosa is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
