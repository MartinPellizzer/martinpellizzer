Tupistra siphonantha

Tupistra siphonantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawthorn, is a shrub native to parts of southern Africa.

It features small, glossy, dark green leaves that are typically narrow and pointed. The plant produces white or pale pink flowers in clusters, which bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a small, red berry that ripens in the autumn.

Tupistra siphonantha is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
