Tricera subcolumnaris

Tricera subcolumnaris, commonly known as the columnar cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant has spiny, tuberculate stems and produces yellow flowers during the summer months. Its succulent leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss in harsh environments.

Tricera subcolumnaris is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a resilient plant in xerophytic ecosystems.
