Tragacantha intermedia

Tragacantha intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate tragacanth, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Tragacantha intermedia is known for its ability to thrive in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Its historical use in traditional medicine and as a natural gum source adds to its botanical significance.
