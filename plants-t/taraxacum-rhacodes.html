Taraxacum rhacodes

Taraxacum rhacodes, commonly known as the mountain dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant features a rosette of basal, hairy leaves that are usually gray-green in color. Its flowers are yellow and form a single, short, and often inconspicuous head.

It is adapted to grow in rocky or disturbed soils and is often found in alpine environments.
