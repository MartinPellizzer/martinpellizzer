Tephrosia barclayi

Tephrosia barclayi, commonly known as the African blue pea, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring compound leaves and showy blue or purple flowers. The plant is characterized by its raceme of small, clustered blooms that appear in the summer months. Its botanical features include pinnate leaves with 8 to 12 pairs of leaflets and a fibrous root system.

Tephrosia barclayi is also valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
