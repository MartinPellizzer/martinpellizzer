Teucrium orientale subsp. gloeotrichum

Teucrium orientale subsp.

gloeotrichum, commonly known as the oriental germander, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of Asia. It features slender, upright stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 60 cm in height.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, arranged in dense spikes at the ends of the branches. Its leaves are opposite, narrow, and often have a slightly wrinkled texture.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
