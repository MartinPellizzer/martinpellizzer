Thymus sachalinensis

Thymus sachalinensis, commonly known as the Sakhalin thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Korean Peninsula and northeastern China.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces small, pink or purple flowers in early spring. The plant has aromatic leaves that contain essential oils, making it valuable in traditional medicine and aromatherapy. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, often growing in rocky or coastal environments.

Its botanical features make it a hardy and versatile plant suitable for landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
