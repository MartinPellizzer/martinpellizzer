Thelocactus bicolor var. schottii

Thelocactus bicolor var. schottii, commonly known as the Schott's two-colored thelocactus, is a rare and striking cactus native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive two-toned coloration, with a pale green stem contrasting against a deep red or purple hue. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom during the summer months. Its leaves are modified into spines, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

This variety is highly sought after by collectors due to its unique appearance and limited distribution.
