Trichocereus macrogonus subsp. sanpedro

Trichocereus macrogonus subsp.

sanpedro, commonly known as San Pedro cactus, is a species native to northern South America. It is a columnar cactus characterized by its smooth, green stems and spiny areoles.

The plant produces large, white flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in South American cultures.

Its growth is slow, and it thrives in well-drained soil with ample sunlight.
