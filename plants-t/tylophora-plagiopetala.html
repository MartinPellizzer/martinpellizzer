Tylophora plagiopetala

Tylophora plagiopetala, commonly known as the Chinese trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing shrub that produces small, fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white to pale yellow. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Tylophora plagiopetala is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine.
