Triticum aestivum var. navrozkyi

Triticum aestivum var. navrozkyi, commonly known as Navrozkyi wheat, is a botanical variant of common wheat characterized by its unique genetic makeup and morphological traits.

It is a diploid species, distinguished by its smaller grain size and distinct glume morphology compared to typical hexaploid wheat varieties. This variety is primarily found in specific regions of Central Asia, where it has adapted to local climatic conditions. Its genetic diversity contributes to the broader wheat genome research and breeding programs.

Navrozkyi wheat is of interest for its potential role in improving wheat resilience to environmental stresses.
