Tulipa gesneriana var. acutiflora

Tulipa gesneriana var. acutiflora, commonly known as the Darwin tulip, is a species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range from red to yellow, with a distinctive dark eye in the center. The plant produces strap-like leaves and is known for its robust growth habit. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

This variety is valued for its vibrant blooms and is often used in floral arrangements and ornamental gardens.
