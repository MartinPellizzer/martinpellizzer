Trifolium spicatum

Trifolium spicatum, commonly known as spike vetch, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is typically found in grasslands, meadows, and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, making it beneficial for soil fertility.

Its seeds are often used as forage for livestock and in traditional herbal medicine.
