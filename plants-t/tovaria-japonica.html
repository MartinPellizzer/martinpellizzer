Tovaria japonica

Tovaria japonica, commonly known as the Japanese bellflower, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or purple flowers. Tovaria japonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
