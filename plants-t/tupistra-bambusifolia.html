Tupistra bambusifolia

Tupistra bambusifolia, commonly known as the bamboo-like spurge, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a rosette of fleshy, blue-green leaves that resemble those of a bamboo plant, giving it its common name. The plant produces yellow flowers in summer, which are followed by small, orange berries. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
