Tragacantha poecilantha

Tragacantha poecilantha, commonly known as the Persian gum plant, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and woody pods. The plant is known for producing a type of gum, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive.

Its growth habit includes shrub-like stems and trifoliate leaves, contributing to its ornamental value in arid landscapes.
