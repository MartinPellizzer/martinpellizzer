Tragacantha vanillae

Tragacantha vanillae, commonly known as the vanilla tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and papery pods. The plant produces a gel-like substance from its seeds, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive. Its botanical features include compound leaves and a unique ability to thrive in arid conditions.

Despite its historical significance, it is now considered a rare and endangered species.
