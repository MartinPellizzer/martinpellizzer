Tofieldia glutinosa subsp. absona

Tofieldia glutinosa subsp.

absona, commonly known as the sticky tofieldia, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America. It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces dense clusters of small, greenish-white flowers.

The plant is often found in wetland habitats such as marshes and bogs, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. Its leaves are coated with a sticky resin, which helps to deter herbivores.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
