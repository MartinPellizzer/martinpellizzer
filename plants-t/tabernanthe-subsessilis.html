Tabernanthe subsessilis

Tabernanthe subsessilis, commonly known as the African blue-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue flowers, which are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that complements its vibrant blooms.

Tabernanthe subsessilis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
