Torreyochloa pallida var. pallida

Torreyochloa pallida var. pallida, commonly known as the pale torreyo, is a grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, blue-green foliage and compact growth habit, often forming dense mats on rocky soils. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, demonstrating drought tolerance and adaptability to poor soil conditions.

Its ecological significance includes stabilizing soils and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
