Trifolium tridentatum var. aciculare

Trifolium tridentatum var. aciculare, commonly known as the needleleaf clover, is a variant of the trifoliate clover species.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soils. It produces small, pea-like flowers that are usually pink or white in color.

This variety is often used in forage and soil stabilization due to its hardy nature and ability to form dense mats.
