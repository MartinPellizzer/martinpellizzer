Triticum acutum

Triticum acutum, commonly known as slender wheat, is a species of grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a small annual plant that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant produces spikelets with awns, and its florets are usually pale green to brown in color. It has narrow, linear leaves and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Despite its modest appearance, it plays a role in local ecosystems and has been used in traditional agricultural practices.
