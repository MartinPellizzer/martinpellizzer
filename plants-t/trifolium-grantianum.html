Trifolium grantianum

Trifolium grantianum, commonly known as the Grantian clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged symmetrically. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Native to certain regions of Europe, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and meadows.
