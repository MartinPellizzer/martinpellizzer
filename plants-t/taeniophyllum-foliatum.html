Taeniophyllum foliatum

Taeniophyllum foliatum, commonly known as the leafy taeniophyllum, is a unique epiphytic plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Bromeliaceae and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a water-holding cup. The plant's leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine, hair-like trichomes that aid in water retention. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often overlooked in favor of its striking foliage.

Due to its delicate nature, it requires specific humidity and light conditions to thrive in cultivation.
