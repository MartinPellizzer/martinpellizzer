Thea quiscosaura

Thea quiscosaura, commonly known as the Andean tea plant, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes Mountains.

It belongs to the Theaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen shrub form with dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used to make a traditional herbal tea, valued for its mild flavor and potential health benefits.

Thea quiscosaura thrives in cool, moist climates and requires well-drained soil to flourish.
