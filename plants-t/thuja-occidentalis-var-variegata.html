Thuja occidentalis var. variegata

Thuja occidentalis var. variegata, commonly known as the variegated arborvitae, is a cultivar of the eastern white cedar.

It is characterized by its striking variegated foliage, which features white or yellow margins on its needle-like leaves. This plant is evergreen and typically grows in a conical or pyramidal shape, making it a popular ornamental shrub. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, and it is relatively hardy in temperate climates.

The variegation adds visual interest and makes it a desirable choice for landscaping.
