Taraxacum scalifolium

Taraxacum scalifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and a hollow, hairy stem that supports a single flowering head. The flowers are typically yellow, with ray florets that bloom in spring and early summer. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and gardens.

This species is notable for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
