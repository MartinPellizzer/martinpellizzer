Triticum aestivum var. vigorovii

Triticum aestivum var. vigorovii, commonly known as the hard red winter wheat, is a variant of common wheat characterized by its robust growth habit and high protein content.

It is primarily cultivated in temperate climates and is well-suited for colder winters, making it a staple in many agricultural regions. The plant features a tall, erect stem with narrow leaves and produces compact spikes that bear multiple grains. Its botanical structure includes a complex root system and a fibrous husk surrounding the grain.

This variety is valued for its adaptability and is often used in bread and pasta production due to its strong gluten content.
