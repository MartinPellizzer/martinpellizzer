Tephrosia conferta

Tephrosia conferta, commonly known as the African velvet bean, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, velvety leaflets and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant produces cylindrical pods containing several seeds, and it is often grown for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Tephrosia conferta is also used in traditional medicine and as a source of protein-rich seed meal.
