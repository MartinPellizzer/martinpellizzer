Terpsichore laxa

Terpsichore laxa, commonly known as the slender lady's-tresses, is a species of orchid native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stem and dense clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it blooms from late summer to early fall.

Terpsichore laxa is a perennial herb that plays a role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining biodiversity in its habitat.
