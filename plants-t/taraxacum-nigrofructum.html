Taraxacum nigrofructum

Taraxacum nigrofructum, commonly known as black fruit dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa, typically found in dry, rocky soils. The plant produces small, yellow composite flowers that develop into dark, spherical fruits. Its leaves are broadly lobed and often have a waxy coating.

The species is notable for its unique fruit structure, which distinguishes it from other dandelion species.
