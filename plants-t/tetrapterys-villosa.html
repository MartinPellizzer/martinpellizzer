Tetrapterys villosa

Tetrapterys villosa, commonly known as the hairy tamarind, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height, characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round, yellow fruits that are encased in a fibrous husk. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The botanical features of Tetrapterys villosa make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
