Taxillus kaempferi var. obovata

Taxillus kaempferi var. obovata, commonly known as the parasitic mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the roots of other woody plants, such as eucalypts, to obtain water and nutrients.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on its host for sustenance, while still performing some photosynthesis using its green stems. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers and red berries that are dispersed by birds. It is native to Australia and is often found in coastal and dry forest regions.

Its unique parasitic nature and ecological role make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
