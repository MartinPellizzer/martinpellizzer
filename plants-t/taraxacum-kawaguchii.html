Taraxacum kawaguchii

Taraxacum kawaguchii, commonly known as the Kawaguchii dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and a hollow, hairy stem. The plant produces a yellow flower head that develops into a white, spherical seed sphere with bristly parachutes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
