Thecacoris spathulifolia

Thecacoris spathulifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed orchid, is a species of orchid native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and produces striking, fragrant flowers with a distinctive spatulate shape. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Thecacoris spathulifolia is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
