Thymus praecox var. medvedewii

Thymus praecox var. medvedewii, commonly known as Siberian thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, aromatic leaves and produces pink to purple flowers in early summer. The plant is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and is often found in rocky or alpine environments. It is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.

Its adaptability to harsh climates makes it a resilient species in its native habitat.
