Trifolium vesiculosum subvar. stanodactylon

Trifolium vesiculosum subvar. stanodactylon, commonly known as the yellow clover, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets with a distinctive yellowish hue. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological systems.
