Taraxacum adunans

Taraxacum adunans, commonly known as the Chinese dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to eastern Asia, particularly China, and is characterized by its basal rosette of stiff, hairy leaves. The plant produces a single, hollow, leaf-like stem that terminates in a composite flower head, which is typically yellow in color. It has a taproot system and is often found in disturbed soils such as roadsides and fields.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional Chinese medicine.
