Tragacantha tenuirugis

Tragacantha tenuirugis, commonly known as the narrow-ribbed tragacanth, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are typically arranged in racemes, followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

The species is valued for its potential medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
