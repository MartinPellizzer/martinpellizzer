Trymatococcus oligogynus

Trymatococcus oligogynus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved snowbell, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery-white down. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.
