Tilia bracteolata

Tilia bracteolata, commonly known as the bracteolate lime, is a deciduous tree native to parts of eastern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Tiliaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple on the underside. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in drooping inflorescences, typical of the Tilia genus. Its fruit is a small, dry capsule that splits open to release minute seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
