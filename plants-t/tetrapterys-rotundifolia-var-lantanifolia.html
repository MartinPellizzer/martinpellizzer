Tetrapterys rotundifolia var. lantanifolia

Tetrapterys rotundifolia var. lantanifolia, commonly known as the purpleleaf sterculia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves that are often tinged with purple or reddish hues. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitats.
