Thismia aurantiaca

Thismia aurantiaca, commonly known as the red-flowered Thismia, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Thismiaceae family and is characterized by its unique, elongated, and often brightly colored flowers. The plant is typically found in humid, shaded environments such as tropical forests. Its flowers are often pollinated by specific insects, and it has a symbiotic relationship with mycorrhizal fungi.

Thismia aurantiaca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
