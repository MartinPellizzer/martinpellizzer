Tilia × johannis-wagneri

Tilia × johannis-wagneri, commonly known as the Johanns-Wagner linden, is a hybrid species within the Tilia genus, which includes linden trees.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are typically large and have a smooth, glossy surface. The tree produces fragrant, yellowish-green flowers in umbels, which bloom in early summer and are highly attractive to pollinators. Its bark is smooth and light gray, and it tends to develop shallow fissures with age.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often planted in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and shade-providing capabilities.
