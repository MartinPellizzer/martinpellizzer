Turpinia pomifera var. sphaerocarpa

Turpinia pomifera var. sphaerocarpa, commonly known as the Japanese persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces large, orange to red berries that are edible when fully ripe, containing astringent tannins when unripe. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The variety sphaerocarpa is distinguished by its spherical, fleshy fruits, which are particularly prized in traditional Asian cuisine.
