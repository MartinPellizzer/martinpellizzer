Typhalea intermedia

Typhalea intermedia, commonly known as the marsh hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in swampy areas and along the edges of ponds. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually pink or red, with five petals. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and have a rough texture.

Typhalea intermedia plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
