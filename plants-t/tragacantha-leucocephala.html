Tragacantha leucocephala

Tragacantha leucocephala, commonly known as the white-headed tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its woody stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its sap, known as tragacanth, has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural gum in various industries.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitats.
