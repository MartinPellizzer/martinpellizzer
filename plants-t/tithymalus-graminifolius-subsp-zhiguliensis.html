Tithymalus graminifolius subsp. zhiguliensis

Tithymalus graminifolius subsp.

zhiguliensis, commonly known as the Zhiguliensis tithymalus, is a perennial herb native to specific regions of Eastern Europe. It features slender stems and lanceolate leaves with a hairy texture.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
