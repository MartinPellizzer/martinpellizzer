Thalictrum tenii

Thalictrum tenii, commonly known as the Japanese meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like foliage and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are compound, with multiple narrow leaflets arranged in a whorled pattern.

Thalictrum tenii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
