Talinum glaucum

Talinum glaucum, commonly known as the white-flowered velvetleaf, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a velvety appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming from summer to fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Talinum glaucum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a garden plant.
