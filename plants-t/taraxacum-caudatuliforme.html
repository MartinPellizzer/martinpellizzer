Taraxacum caudatuliforme

Taraxacum caudatuliforme, commonly known as the long-tailed dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated seed heads that give it its common name. The plant features basal leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by the characteristic fluffy seeds.

This species is often found in grassy areas and is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.
