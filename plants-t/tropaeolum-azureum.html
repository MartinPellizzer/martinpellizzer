Tropaeolum azureum

Tropaeolum azureum, commonly known as blue glory flower, is a strikingly colorful plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Tropaeolaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant blue and purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental value.

Tropaeolum azureum is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
