Tripteris polycephala

Tripteris polycephala, commonly known as the many-headed tripteris, is a tropical plant belonging to the family Tripteridaceae.

It is native to regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru, where it thrives in humid, lowland forests. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves, which are usually pinnately arranged with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by distinctive, clustered, spiny fruits.

Tripteris polycephala is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
