Tardavel thymoidea

Tardavel thymoidea, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thyme, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lamiaceae.

It is native to southern Europe and is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often used in herbal medicine for its antimicrobial and antioxidant properties.

Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and as a ground cover.
