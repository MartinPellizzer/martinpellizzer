Terminalia glauca

Terminalia glauca, commonly known as the white terminalia, is a small to medium-sized tree native to the tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by spherical, hard pods containing several seeds. Terminalia glauca is valued for its durable wood and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for a variety of wildlife species.
