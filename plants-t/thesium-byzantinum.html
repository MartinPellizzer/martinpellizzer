Thesium byzantinum

Thesium byzantinum, commonly known as the Byzantine thesium, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Xanthorrhoeaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and features small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia. Thesium byzantinum has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
