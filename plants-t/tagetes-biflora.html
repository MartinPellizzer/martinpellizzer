Tagetes biflora

Tagetes biflora, commonly known as the Mexican marigold, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow and orange flowers, which are typically borne in clusters. The plant is an annual herb with slender, branching stems and alternate leaves that are usually lance-shaped. Its botanical name, Tagetes, is derived from the Greek word for "nose," referring to its strong, aromatic scent.

Tagetes biflora is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soil and full sunlight.
