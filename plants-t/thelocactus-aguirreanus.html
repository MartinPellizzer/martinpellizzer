Thelocactus aguirreanus

Thelocactus aguirreanus, commonly known as the Mexican torch cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is a columnar cactus with a distinctive red or purple coloration, often with a reddish or pinkish tinge. The plant features a thick, ribbed stem that stores water, and it produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months. It is adapted to extreme desert conditions, with a shallow root system and a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

The plant is also known for its ability to produce a unique, edible fruit that is used in traditional cuisine.
