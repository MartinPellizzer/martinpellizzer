Tetratheca setigera var. montana

Tetratheca setigera var. montana, commonly known as the mountain tetratheca, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, often in mountainous regions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are borne on slender pedicels. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slightly hairy texture.

This variety is noted for its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions and is of interest in ecological studies.
