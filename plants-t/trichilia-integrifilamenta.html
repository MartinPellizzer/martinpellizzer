Trichilia integrifilamenta

Trichilia integrifilamenta, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a tropical tree native to Central and Southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and alternate, simple, ovate to elliptic leaves with a glossy upper surface. The flowers are small, fragrant, and yellowish-white, arranged in clusters, while the fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds. The plant is valued for its durable wood, which is used in furniture and construction, and for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitats by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
