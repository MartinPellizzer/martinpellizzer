Turnera schomburgkiana

Turnera schomburgkiana, commonly known as the "Mexican flame flower," is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Turneraceae and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Turnera schomburgkiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
