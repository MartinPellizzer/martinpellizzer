Thymus munbyanus subsp. abylaeus

Thymus munbyanus subsp.

abylaeus, commonly known as Munby's thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features aromatic, narrow leaves and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.

This subspecies is often distinguished by its compact growth habit and specific geographic distribution.
