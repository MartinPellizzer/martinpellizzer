Tyrimnus leucographus var. cavillieri

Tyrimnus leucographus var. cavillieri, commonly known as the white-veined tyrimnus, is a unique plant species characterized by its striking white veins on its broad, lance-shaped leaves.

Native to specific regions in Southeast Asia, it thrives in humid, tropical environments with high rainfall. The plant's foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color.

Due to its rarity and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern in its native range.
