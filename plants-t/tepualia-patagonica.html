Tepualia patagonica

Tepualia patagonica, commonly known as the Patagonian sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, adapted to reduce water loss in arid environments.
