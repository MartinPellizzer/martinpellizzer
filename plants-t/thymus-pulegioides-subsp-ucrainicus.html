Thymus pulegioides subsp. ucrainicus

Thymus pulegioides subsp.

ucrainicus, commonly known as Ukrainian thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of Eastern Europe. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its small pink or white flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
