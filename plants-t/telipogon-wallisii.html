Telipogon wallisii

Telipogon wallisii, commonly known as the Wallis Island orchid, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the South Pacific.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its small, intricate flowers and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories or on rocky outcrops. It has a unique reproductive strategy, relying on specific pollinators for successful fertilization.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, Telipogon wallisii is considered endangered and is of significant interest to botanists and conservationists.
