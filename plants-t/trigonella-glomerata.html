Trigonella glomerata

Trigonella glomerata, commonly known as the clustered fenugreek, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, branched stems that often grow in dense clusters, and its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by pods that contain several elliptical seeds. It is characterized by its adaptability to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in such conditions.

Trigonella glomerata is also valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
