Tephrosia mollissima

Tephrosia mollissima, commonly known as velvet tephrosia, is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves that give it its common name, and it produces small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant is often found in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.

Additionally, it serves as a host plant for certain butterfly species, contributing to local biodiversity.
