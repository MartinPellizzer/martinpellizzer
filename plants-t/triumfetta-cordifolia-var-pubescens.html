Triumfetta cordifolia var. pubescens

Triumfetta cordifolia var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy heartleaf triumfetta, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, pubescent leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, often accompanied by stipules. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
