Tradescantia harbisonii

Tradescantia harbisonii, commonly known as the Texas spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-green hue. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.

It is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
