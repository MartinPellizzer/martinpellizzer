Toumeya lophophoroides

Toumeya lophophoroides, commonly known as the African orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its elegant, showy flowers that typically feature a mix of white, yellow, and green hues. The plant grows as an epiphytic orchid, often found on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
