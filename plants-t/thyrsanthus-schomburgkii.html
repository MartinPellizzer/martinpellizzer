Thyrsanthus schomburgkii

Thyrsanthus schomburgkii, commonly known as the Schomburgk's thyrsanthus, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers arranged in dense, clustered inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
