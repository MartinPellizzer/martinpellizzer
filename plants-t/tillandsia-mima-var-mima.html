Tillandsia mima var. mima

Tillandsia mima var. mima, commonly known as the Mima tillandsia, is a species of air plant native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are covered in dense, white trichomes. These trichomes aid in water absorption and help the plant thrive in humid environments. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, utilizing its aerial roots to anchor itself.

It produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple, which are pollinated by insects and birds.
