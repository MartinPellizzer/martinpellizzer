Thea reticulata

Thea reticulata, commonly known as the net-veined evergreen, is a species of flowering plant in the family Theaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of southern China and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with a distinctive net-like venation pattern. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, blooming in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Thea reticulata is also valued for its ability to tolerate humid conditions and its evergreen nature, providing year-round visual interest in the landscape.
