Taraxacum multisulcatum

Taraxacum multisulcatum, commonly known as the multigroove dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves with multiple distinct longitudinal veins, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces a yellow flower head that develops into a fluffy white pappus, aiding in wind pollination. It is native to parts of Europe and Asia and thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
