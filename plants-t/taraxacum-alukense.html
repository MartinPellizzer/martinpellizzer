Taraxacum alukense

Taraxacum alukense, commonly known as the Alukense dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers and a distinctive, hollow, hairless stem. The plant produces a pappus-based seed head that aids in wind dispersal, a common trait among dandelions. It typically grows in temperate regions and is often found in disturbed soils or open areas.

Taraxacum alukense is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
