Trillium recurvatum f. esepalum

Trillium recurvatum f. esepalum, commonly known as the white-flowered trillium, is a rare and striking variant of the trillium genus native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its pale white petals, which contrast with its deep green foliage, and its unique floral structure that resembles a three-petaled flower. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist woodlands, where it thrives in rich, loamy soil. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three broad, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces a single, upright stem that supports its showy bloom.

This botanical feature makes it a notable species for both horticultural and ecological studies.
