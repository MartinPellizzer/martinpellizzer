Tagera

Tagera, commonly known as the African daisy, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

These plants are native to southern Africa and are characterized by their showy, daisy-like flowers. Tagera species typically have hairy stems and leaves, and they produce small, yellow or brownish seeds in their flower heads. They are often found in grasslands and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils.

The genus includes several species, such as Tagera lutea and Tagera africana, which are valued for their ornamental appeal.
