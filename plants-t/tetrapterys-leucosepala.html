Tetrapterys leucosepala

Tetrapterys leucosepala, commonly known as the white-flowered stinging nettle, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in dense clusters, and its stems are covered in fine, stinging hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is notable for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
