Tacsonia spinosa

Tacsonia spinosa, commonly known as the spiny tacsonia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its woody stems and dense, spiny foliage. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, Tacsonia spinosa is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
