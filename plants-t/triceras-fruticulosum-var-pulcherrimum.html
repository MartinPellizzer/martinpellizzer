Triceras fruticulosum var. pulcherrimum

Triceras fruticulosum var. pulcherrimum, commonly known as the purple flame plant, is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple to red bracts that encase clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or subshrub, featuring wiry stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowering season occurs during the warmer months, and it thrives in well-drained soil with partial to full sunlight.

This ornamental plant is popular in tropical and subtropical gardens for its bold, showy appearance.
