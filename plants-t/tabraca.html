Tabraca

Tabraca, commonly known as the desert bell, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from purple to red. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves and thrives in well-drained soils with minimal water requirements. Tabraca is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.

Its blooming period typically occurs in summer, adding vibrant color to desert landscapes.
