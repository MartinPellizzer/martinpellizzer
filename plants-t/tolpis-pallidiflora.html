Tolpis pallidiflora

Tolpis pallidiflora, commonly known as the pale-flowered tolpis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grasslands and fynbos vegetation, preferring well-drained soils. Its pale yellow to white flowers bloom in spring, and the plant is often used in traditional medicine.

Tolpis pallidiflora is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
