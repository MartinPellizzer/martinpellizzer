Trigonella himalaica

Trigonella himalaica, commonly known as Himalayan fenugreek, is an annual leguminous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, lens-shaped seeds that are rich in nutrients and have been used in traditional medicine. Trigonella himalaica thrives in temperate climates and is often found in rocky or arid soils.

Its botanical features make it valuable for both ecological and agricultural purposes.
