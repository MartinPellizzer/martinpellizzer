Thymus serpyllum f. euryphyllastrum

Thymus serpyllum f. euryphyllastrum, commonly known as narrow-leaved thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically narrow and densely packed, giving it a compact appearance. The plant produces tiny, pink or white flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary uses, as its leaves can be used to flavor dishes or make herbal teas.
