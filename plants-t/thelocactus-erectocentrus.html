Thelocactus erectocentrus

Thelocactus erectocentrus, commonly known as the erectocentrous cactus, is a species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stem that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers during the warmer months, which are typically pollinated by nocturnal insects. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it has a deep root system to access water in arid environments.

This cactus is well adapted to survive in harsh, dry conditions with minimal water requirements.
