Trichopteryx flammida

Trichopteryx flammida, commonly known as the flame tamarisk, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in hot, dry environments. The plant has small, scale-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color, and it produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers during the spring. Trichopteryx flammida is adapted to poor soils and can tolerate extreme drought conditions.

It is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental appeal.
