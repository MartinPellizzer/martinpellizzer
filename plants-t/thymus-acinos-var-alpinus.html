Thymus acinos var. alpinus

Thymus acinos var. alpinus, commonly known as alpine thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It features small, fragrant leaves that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant produces tiny, pink or white flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Its aromatic properties make it a valued plant in both traditional herbal medicine and horticulture.
