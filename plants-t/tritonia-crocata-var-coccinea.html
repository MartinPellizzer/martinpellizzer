Tritonia crocata var. coccinea

Tritonia crocata var. coccinea, commonly known as the red-flowered tritonia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking red blooms that appear in late spring to early summer. The plant features strap-like, dark green leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
