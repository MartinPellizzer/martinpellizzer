Thalictrum athesianum

Thalictrum athesianum, commonly known as the alpine meadow rue, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves that are finely divided and arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in upright, branched inflorescences. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Thalictrum athesianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
