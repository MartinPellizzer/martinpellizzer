Taraxacum pallidulum

Taraxacum pallidulum, commonly known as the pale dandelion, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of pale green, hairy leaves and a hollow, hairless stem. The plant produces a single, inconspicuous yellow flower that develops into a white, fluffy seed head. It is native to Europe and is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides.

Taraxacum pallidulum is a perennial herb that thrives in temperate climates.
