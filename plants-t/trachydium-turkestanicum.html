Trachydium turkestanicum

Trachydium turkestanicum, commonly known as the Turkestan thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has a rosette growth form, with dense foliage that helps reduce water loss.

Trachydium turkestanicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some regions.
