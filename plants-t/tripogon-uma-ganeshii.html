Tripogon uma-ganeshii

Tripogon uma-ganeshii, commonly known as the Ganesh's spikegrass, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and blade-like, typical of many grass species, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its limited distribution and unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
