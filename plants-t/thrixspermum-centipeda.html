Thrixspermum centipeda

Thrixspermum centipeda, commonly known as the centipede plant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features slender, ascending stems that can grow up to 30 centimeters in height. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers in clusters, which bloom in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Thrixspermum centipeda is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
