Tacsonia ampullacea

Tacsonia ampullacea, commonly known as the bladderleaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, inflated leaf structures that resemble bladders. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually in a spike, with a delicate, pale green to yellowish coloration.

Tacsonia ampullacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unusual foliage.
