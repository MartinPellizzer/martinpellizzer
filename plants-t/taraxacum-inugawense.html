Taraxacum inugawense

Taraxacum inugawense, commonly known as the Japanese dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers and heart-shaped basal leaves. The plant is typically found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas and along stream banks. It produces a distinctive hollow stem and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.

Taraxacum inugawense is known for its ability to thrive in shaded environments, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
