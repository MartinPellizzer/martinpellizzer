Thalictrodes americana

Thalictrodes americana, commonly known as the American yellow gentian, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a velvety texture.

The flowers bloom in late summer and are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects.
