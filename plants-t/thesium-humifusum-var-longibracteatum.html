Thesium humifusum var. longibracteatum

Thesium humifusum var. longibracteatum, commonly known as the long-bracted thesium, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features a dense mat of prostrate stems covered with small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are surrounded by elongated bracts. Its flowers are typically tubular with five lobes, and it is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

This variety is notable for its distinctive floral structure and adaptation to arid environments.
