Theobroma tessmannii

Theobroma tessmannii, commonly known as the Brazilian nut tree, is a member of the Malvaceae family and is native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is a large evergreen tree that can grow up to 30 meters tall, characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces woody pods that contain a rich, oily seed known as the Brazil nut.

The botanical significance of T. tessmannii lies in its unique seed structure and its role in the ecological and economic systems of the Amazon.
