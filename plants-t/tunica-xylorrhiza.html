Tunica xylorrhiza

Tunica xylorrhiza, commonly known as the barkroot plant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its fibrous, woody root system that stores water and nutrients, enabling it to thrive in harsh environments. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that reduce water loss through transpiration. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, often in spikes, and are adapted for pollination by wind or insects.

Tunica xylorrhiza plays a role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion in its native habitats.
