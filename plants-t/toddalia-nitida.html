Toddalia nitida

Toddalia nitida, commonly known as the white tamarind tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant features compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets and produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its fruit is a smooth, round pod that splits open to reveal sweet, sticky pulp containing seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in some regions for its fruit and wood.
