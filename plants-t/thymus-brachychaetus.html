Thymus brachychaetus

Thymus brachychaetus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with small, aromatic leaves and pink to lavender-colored flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for rock gardens and Mediterranean climates. Its leaves are used in cooking and traditional medicine, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness.

Thymus brachychaetus is also noted for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.
