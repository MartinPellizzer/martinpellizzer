Taraxacum ikonnikovii

Taraxacum ikonnikovii, commonly known as the Ikonnikov's dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaves. The plant features a basal rosette of serrated, grass-like leaves and produces a hollow, hairless stem that bears a single flowering head. Its yellow ray florets are surrounded by a crown of tubular disc florets, typical of the genus Taraxacum.

It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing resilience to harsh climatic conditions.
