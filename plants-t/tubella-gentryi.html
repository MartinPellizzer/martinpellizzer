Tubella gentryi

Tubella gentryi, commonly known as the Gentry's tubella, is a rare and lesser-known species of fungus.

It belongs to the family Tubellaceae and is characterized by its unique, tubular fruiting bodies. The fungus is typically found in specific forest ecosystems, often associated with decaying wood. Its microscopic structure includes distinctive spore-producing cells that aid in its reproduction.

Due to its limited distribution, it is of interest to mycologists studying fungal biodiversity.
