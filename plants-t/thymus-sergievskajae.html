Thymus sergievskajae

Thymus sergievskajae, commonly known as the Sergeyevskaya thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy flowers. The plant typically features slender stems and small, fragrant leaves that are rich in essential oils. Its flowers are usually pink or violet, arranged in dense clusters, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and ornamental plant suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
