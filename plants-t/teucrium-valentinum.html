Teucrium valentinum

Teucrium valentinum, commonly known as Valentine's germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stems, and bloom from spring to early summer.

Teucrium valentinum is often found in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and grasslands.
