Terminalia laxiflora var. pubescens

Terminalia laxiflora var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy terminalia, is a tropical plant native to regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its deciduous nature, shedding its leaves during the dry season. The plant features compound leaves with serrated margins and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow. Its fruit is a drupe, containing a single seed enclosed in a hard, woody pulp.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
