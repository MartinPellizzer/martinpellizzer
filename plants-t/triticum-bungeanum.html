Triticum bungeanum

Triticum bungeanum, commonly known as Bunge's wheat, is a wild grass species native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to yellow florets. It is typically found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions.

While not widely cultivated, it has been studied for its potential in genetic research and as a source of drought-resistant traits.
