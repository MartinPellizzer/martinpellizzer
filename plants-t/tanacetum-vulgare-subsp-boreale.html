Tanacetum vulgare subsp. boreale

Tanacetum vulgare subsp.

boreale, commonly known as northern tansy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern regions of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and yellow flower heads composed of numerous small florets.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in wet meadows, bogs, and riverbanks. It is known for its strong, camphor-like odor and has been used historically for its medicinal and aromatic properties.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
