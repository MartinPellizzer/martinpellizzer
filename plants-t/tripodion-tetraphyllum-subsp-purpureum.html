Tripodion tetraphyllum subsp. purpureum

Tripodion tetraphyllum subsp.

purpureum, commonly known as the purple-fruited tripodion, is a member of the Ericaceae family. It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage.

The leaves are typically dark green and glossy, while the flowers are small and white or pale pink, appearing in clusters. The plant produces berries that range in color from red to purple, which are edible but often have a tart flavor.

It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous or forested regions.
