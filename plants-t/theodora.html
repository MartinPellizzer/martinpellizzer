Theodora

Theodora, commonly known as the common thyme, is a hardy perennial herb belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant leaves and clusters of pink or white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight. Its aromatic properties make it popular in culinary and medicinal uses.

Theodora is also valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract beneficial insects.
