Thelasis elongata var. major

Thelasis elongata var. major, commonly known as the longleaf thelasis, is a perennial herb native to certain regions of southern Africa.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands. The plant features slender, erect stems that can reach several feet in height, adorned with small, linear leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
