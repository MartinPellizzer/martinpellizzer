Teucrium × gautieri

Teucrium × gautieri, commonly known as the hybrid germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its square, often hairy stems and opposite leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually violet or blue, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the branches. It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

Teucrium × gautieri is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
