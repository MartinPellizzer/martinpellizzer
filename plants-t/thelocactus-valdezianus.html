Thelocactus valdezianus

Thelocactus valdezianus, commonly known as the Valdez's thorned cactus, is a species native to the Sonoran Desert in North America.

It is characterized by its short, spiny stems and is typically found growing in rocky soils. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a subtle beauty to its otherwise arid environment. Its structure is adapted to conserve water, with thick, waxy stems that reduce evaporation.

Thelocactus valdezianus is a relatively rare and specialized plant, thriving in specific microhabitats within its native range.
