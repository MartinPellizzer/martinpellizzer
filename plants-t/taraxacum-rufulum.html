Taraxacum rufulum

Taraxacum rufulum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the daisy family, Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of stiff, hairy leaves and produces bright yellow flowers with ray florets. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grasslands. It has a taproot system and can spread vegetatively through rhizomes.

Taraxacum rufulum is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
