Typha latifolia var. obconica

Typha latifolia var. obconica, commonly known as broadleaf cattail, is a variant of the common cattail species.

It is a herbaceous wetland plant characterized by its wide, flat leaves and cylindrical flower heads. This plant thrives in shallow water environments such as marshes, ponds, and wet meadows. Its rhizomes form dense colonies, contributing to soil stabilization and habitat creation for various aquatic organisms.

The plant plays a significant role in wetland ecosystems by filtering water and providing shelter for wildlife.
