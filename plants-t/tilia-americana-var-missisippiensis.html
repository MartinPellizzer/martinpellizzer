Tilia americana var. missisippiensis

Tilia americana var. missisippiensis, commonly known as the Mississippi bigleaf maple, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically 5 to 10 inches in diameter. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are clustered in pendulous inflorescences. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, often developing shallow fissures with age.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
