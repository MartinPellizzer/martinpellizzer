Thesium hispidulum var. hispidulum

Thesium hispidulum var. hispidulum, commonly known as the hairy thesium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in grassland and rocky habitats. The plant features wiry, branched stems covered in short, stiff hairs, giving it its common name. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in dense, terminal clusters.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain insects.
