Thysanotus tuberosus var. parviflorus

Thysanotus tuberosus var. parviflorus, commonly known as the small-flowered bluebell, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its tuberous roots and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in horticultural settings.
