Triodia hostilis

Triodia hostilis, commonly known as the spinifex grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, wiry growth habit and sharp, spiny leaves that provide protection against herbivory. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often forming extensive mats that stabilize sandy soils. Its small, wind-dispersed seeds enable it to colonize disturbed or barren areas effectively.

Triodia hostilis plays a crucial role in maintaining soil structure and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
