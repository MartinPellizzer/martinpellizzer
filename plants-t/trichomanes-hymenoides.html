Trichomanes hymenoides

Trichomanes hymenoides, commonly known as the creeping woodfern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are often covered in a layer of fine, hair-like structures. This fern thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and produce spores for propagation.

The plant's unique morphology and habitat preferences make it an important component of forest ecosystems.
