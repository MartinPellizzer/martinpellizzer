Thymus freixei

Thymus freixei, commonly known as the Freixei thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its aromatic, small, needle-like leaves that are densely packed along the stems. The plant produces small, pink to lavender flowers in spring, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Thymus freixei is valued for its medicinal properties and ornamental appeal in horticulture.
