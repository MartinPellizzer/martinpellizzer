Taxillus nigrans var. longifolius

Taxillus nigrans var. longifolius, commonly known as the long-leaved mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that attaches to the branches of various host trees.

It belongs to the Loranthaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically dark red or black, which serve as a food source for birds and other wildlife. Due to its parasitic nature, it can have a significant impact on the health of its host trees if left unchecked.
