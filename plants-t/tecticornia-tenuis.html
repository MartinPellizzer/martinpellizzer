Tecticornia tenuis

Tecticornia tenuis, commonly known as slender tecticornia, is a low-growing perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It typically forms dense mats of narrow, grass-like leaves that are adapted to conserve water in harsh environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in spikes, and it is well-suited to sandy or rocky soils. Its fibrous roots help stabilize soil and prevent erosion, making it an important species in ecological restoration.

Tecticornia tenuis is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in nutrient-poor conditions.
