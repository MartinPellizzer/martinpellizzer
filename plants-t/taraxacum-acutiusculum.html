Taraxacum acutiusculum

Taraxacum acutiusculum, commonly known as the narrowleaf dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, pointed leaves and a yellow flowering head that develops into a puffy, white seed ball. Native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, it is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its flowers are typically visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.
