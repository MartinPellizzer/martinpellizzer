Thymus illyricus

Thymus illyricus, commonly known as the Balkan thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic leaves and square stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its small, pink or white flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators like bees.

Thymus illyricus is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, similar to other thyme species.
