Trimelopter pendulinum

Trimelopter pendulinum, commonly known as the hanging fern, is a unique species of fern native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, pendulous fronds that hang from its stems, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, with creeping stems that allow it to spread along the forest floor. Its leaves are compound, with lance-shaped pinnae that are often covered in a fine layer of hair.

Trimelopter pendulinum thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.
