Tabernaemontana microphylla

Tabernaemontana microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved star of Bethlehem, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the rainy season. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
