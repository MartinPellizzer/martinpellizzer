Tanacetum corymbosum subsp. clusii

Tanacetum corymbosum subsp.

clusii, commonly known as sweetgeranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features finely divided, gray-green leaves that are aromatic and often covered in a powdery coating.

The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in dense, flat-topped clusters called corymbs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its fragrance is often described as sweet and herbal, with a subtle citrus note.
