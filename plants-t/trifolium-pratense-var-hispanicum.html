Trifolium pratense var. hispanicum

Trifolium pratense var. hispanicum, commonly known as Spanish red clover, is a variant of the red clover species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands, meadows, and along roadsides.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in agricultural systems as a forage crop.
