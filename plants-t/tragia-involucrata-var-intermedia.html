Tragia involucrata var. intermedia

Tragia involucrata var. intermedia, commonly known as the hairy ant plant, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its glandular, hairy leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

Its unique morphology and adaptive features make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
