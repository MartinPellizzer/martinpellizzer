Tabernaemontana juruana

Tabernaemontana juruana, commonly known as the Juruana starflower, is a tropical plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its showy, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with thick, woody stems and glossy, dark green leaves. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The flowers are not only ornamental but also attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
