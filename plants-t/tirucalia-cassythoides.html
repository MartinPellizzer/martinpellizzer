Tirucalia cassythoides

Tirucalia cassythoides, commonly known as the Indian morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Solanaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including India, Sri Lanka, and Indonesia. The plant features slender, climbing stems with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with five petals, and appear in clusters.

The fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, dry seeds.
