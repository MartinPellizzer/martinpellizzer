Thibaudia pachypoda

Thibaudia pachypoda, commonly known as the African buttercup, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers with a distinctive cup-shaped structure. The plant has thick, succulent leaves that help it retain water in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Thibaudia pachypoda is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
