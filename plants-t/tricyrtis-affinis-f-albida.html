Tricyrtis affinis f. albida

Tricyrtis affinis f. albida, commonly known as the white Japanese iris, is a unique variant of the Japanese iris species.

It is characterized by its pale white to almost translucent flowers, which contrast sharply with its dark green foliage. The plant is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in woodland or streamside habitats. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a delicate touch to shaded gardens.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its elegant, lace-like blooms.
