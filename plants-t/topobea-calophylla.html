Topobea calophylla

Topobea calophylla, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Topobea calophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
