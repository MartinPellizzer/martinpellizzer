Thicuania moschata

Thicuania moschata, commonly known as the musky gourd, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It produces large, elongated fruits with a thick, waxy skin that can vary in color from green to yellow or orange. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The fruit is typically harvested when it reaches a mature size and develops a musky aroma.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as a vegetable in various cuisines.
