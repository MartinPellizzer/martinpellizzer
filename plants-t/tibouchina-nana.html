Tibouchina nana

Tibouchina nana, commonly known as the dwarf tibouchina, is a compact evergreen shrub native to Brazil.

It features dense, dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and arranged in whorls around the stems. The plant produces clusters of showy, violet-blue flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in USDA hardiness zones 9 through 11, preferring partial shade to full sun and well-drained soil.

Tibouchina nana is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
