Tecoma princei

Tecoma princei, commonly known as the yellow bells, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, featuring lance-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, woody pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Tecoma princei thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and landscapes.
