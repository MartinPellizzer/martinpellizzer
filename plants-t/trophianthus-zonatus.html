Trophianthus zonatus

Trophianthus zonatus, commonly known as the zoned sunflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking, daisy-like yellow flowers with a distinctive dark central disk. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Trophianthus zonatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
