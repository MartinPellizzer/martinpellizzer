Tragopogon albomarginatus

Tragopogon albomarginatus, commonly known as the white-margin thistle, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant features slender, hairy stems and basal leaves that are lance-shaped with wavy margins. Its flowers are typically yellow with white margins, and it produces a distinctive, tufted seed head.

Tragopogon albomarginatus is often found in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows.
