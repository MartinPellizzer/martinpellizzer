Tricyrtis latifolia

Tricyrtis latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved Japanese buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Japan.

It features heart-shaped, broad leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces clusters of delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Tricyrtis latifolia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable climates.
