Taraxacum perlaciniatum

Taraxacum perlaciniatum, commonly known as the Perlaciniatum dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of pale green, hairy leaves and a single, elongated flowering stem that bears a spherical crown of yellow flowers. Native to specific regions in Central Asia, this plant thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its flowers are typically visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological role.

The plant produces achene fruits with pappus structures, aiding in wind dispersal of its seeds.
