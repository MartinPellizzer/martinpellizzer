Tibouchina kingii

Tibouchina kingii, commonly known as the King's tibouchina, is a striking evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple, which bloom in clusters during the warmer months. The plant features glossy, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies further enhances its ecological value.
