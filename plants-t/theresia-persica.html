Theresia persica

Theresia persica, commonly known as the Persian rose, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and dense, aromatic foliage. The plant produces showy, pink or white flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Theresia persica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
