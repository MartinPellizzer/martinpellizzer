Torrubia combretiflora

Torrubia combretiflora, commonly known as the red swamp willow, is a shrub native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its small, reddish-brown flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer months. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish pubescence. It thrives in moist, often saline soils and is commonly found near wetlands and coastal areas.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
