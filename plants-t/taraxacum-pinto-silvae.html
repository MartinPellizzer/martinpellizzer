Taraxacum pinto-silvae

Taraxacum pinto-silvae, commonly known as the Pinto-Silva dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated, hairy leaves and a yellow composite flower head that develops into a spherical seed head. Native to specific regions in Europe, it thrives in meadows and woodland edges. The plant is known for its ability to tolerate various soil types and moderate levels of shade.

Its ecological role includes providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for certain herbivorous insects.
