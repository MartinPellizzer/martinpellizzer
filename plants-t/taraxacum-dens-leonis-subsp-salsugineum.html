Taraxacum dens-leonis subsp. salsugineum

Taraxacum dens-leonis subsp.

salsugineum, commonly known as the alpine dandelion, is a member of the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its rosette of basal, hairy leaves and a yellow flowering head that produces wind-dispersed seeds.

This subspecies is typically found in alpine and arctic regions, where it thrives in rocky or sandy soils. Its structure is adapted to withstand cold temperatures and strong winds.

The plant has a short growing season and is often used in ecological studies due to its resilience in harsh environments.
