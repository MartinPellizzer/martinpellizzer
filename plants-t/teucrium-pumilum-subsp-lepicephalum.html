Teucrium pumilum subsp. lepicephalum

Teucrium pumilum subsp.

lepicephalum, commonly known as mountain germander, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, blue to purple flowers arranged in dense clusters.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats. Its aromatic foliage and compact growth make it a hardy ornamental plant suitable for rock gardens.

It is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
