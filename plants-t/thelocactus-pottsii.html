Thelocactus pottsii

Thelocactus pottsii, commonly known as the Potts' locactus, is a rare and striking cactus native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stem and clusters of vibrant orange to red flowers that bloom during the spring. The plant has a unique, spiny structure with a smooth, grayish-green surface that helps it retain moisture in its harsh environment. Its flowers are not only visually striking but also play a role in attracting specific pollinators.

Thelocactus pottsii is a slow-growing species that requires well-draining soil and minimal water to thrive.
