Tibouchina gracilis var. fraterna

Tibouchina gracilis var. fraterna, commonly known as the pink flame bush, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant pink to red flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has slender, spreading branches and small, elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its attractive foliage and prolonged flowering period contribute to its aesthetic value in landscaped settings.
