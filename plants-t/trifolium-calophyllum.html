Trifolium calophyllum

Trifolium calophyllum, commonly known as the yellow wood clover, is a flowering plant species native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are often clustered in spherical or cylindrical inflorescences. It is adapted to grow in various soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

Trifolium calophyllum is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it valuable in ecological and agricultural contexts.
