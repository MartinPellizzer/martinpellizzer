Taraxacum cornucopiae

Taraxacum cornucopiae, commonly known as the horned dandelion, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiky seed head that resembles a horn, giving it its common name. The plant produces yellow ray florets that bloom in early spring, followed by the development of the characteristic seed dispersal structure. It is typically found in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soil.

The species is noted for its resilience and ability to colonize disturbed areas.
