Themeda anathera var. glabrescens

Themeda anathera var. glabrescens, commonly known as the glabrous grass or smooth wallaby grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically smooth to the touch. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and woodland areas.

This variety is noted for its ecological importance, providing habitat and forage for various native wildlife species.
