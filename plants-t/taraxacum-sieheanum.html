Taraxacum sieheanum

Taraxacum sieheanum, commonly known as the Siehean dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, spoon-shaped leaves and a yellow flower head that develops into a spherical seed ball. Native to Central Asia, this plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows and grassy areas. The plant is known for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.

Its ability to propagate through wind-dispersed seeds makes it a common and resilient species in its native habitat.
