Tridentea ruschiana

Tridentea ruschiana, commonly known as the Three-lobed Rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and clusters of fragrant, white or pink flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous areas. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Tridentea ruschiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and aesthetic beauty.
