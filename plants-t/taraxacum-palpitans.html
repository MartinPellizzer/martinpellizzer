Taraxacum palpitans

Taraxacum palpitans, commonly known as the trembling dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of spoon-shaped, hairy leaves that are often covered in fine white hairs. The plant produces a single, hollow, hairless stem that rises from the center of the rosette, bearing a single flower head composed of yellow ray florets. Its flowering period typically occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is often found in disturbed soils in open habitats.

The species is notable for its ability to thrive in a variety of environments, making it a common weed in gardens and fields.
