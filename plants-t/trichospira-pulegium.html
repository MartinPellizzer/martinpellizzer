Trichospira pulegium

Trichospira pulegium, commonly known as pennyroyal, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant, and often aromatic leaves, which contain high concentrations of essential oils. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are usually small and white or pale blue, arranged in clusters.

Trichospira pulegium is known for its medicinal uses, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
