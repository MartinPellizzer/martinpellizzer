Taxus baccata elvastonensis

Taxus baccata elvastonensis, commonly known as the Elvaston yew, is a rare subspecies of the common yew.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, and often twisted leaves, which are dark green and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It is typically found in woodland habitats and is known for its slow growth and long lifespan.

This subspecies is of particular interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
