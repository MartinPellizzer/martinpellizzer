Trukia Tahitensis

Trukia tahitensis is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the remote islands of French Polynesia. It thrives in the unique microclimates of its coastal habitats, where it adapts to the humid and salty air. This species is known for its striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pale yellow. Due to its limited distribution and habitat vulnerability, it is classified as endangered by conservationists. Studying Trukia tahitensis offers valuable insights into the ecological resilience of island flora.
