Tragacantha ochroleuca

Tragacantha ochroleuca, commonly known as the yellow tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial shrub that produces yellowish flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. The plant is characterized by its woody stems and alternate, lance-shaped leaves. Its sap, which is thick and sticky, has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive.

The yellow tragacanth is also valued for its role in soil stabilization and habitat provision for various wildlife species.
