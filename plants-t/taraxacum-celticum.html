Taraxacum celticum

Taraxacum celticum, commonly known as the Celtic dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust root systems. The plant features a rosette of spoon-shaped, hairy leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white down. Its flowers are bright yellow and produce a distinctive seed head with pappus that aids in wind dispersal.

Taraxacum celticum is known for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
