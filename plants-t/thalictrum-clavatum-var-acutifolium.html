Thalictrum clavatum var. acutifolium

Thalictrum clavatum var. acutifolium, commonly known as the sharp-leaved meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves with a distinctive sharp, pointed shape, and produces clusters of small, fragrant, yellow-green flowers in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and thrives in partial to full shade. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to local biodiversity.

The plant is valued in gardens for its ornamental foliage and ecological benefits.
