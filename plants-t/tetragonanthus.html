Tetragonanthus

Tetragonanthus, commonly known as the four-cornered spurge, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Euphorbiaceae.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, often clustered in cyathia, which are specialized cup-like structures typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Tetragonanthus species are native to various regions in Africa and are often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.
