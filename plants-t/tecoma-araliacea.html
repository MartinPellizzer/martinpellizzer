Tecoma araliacea

Tecoma araliacea, commonly known as the yellow bells, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height, characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and showy yellow flowers. The flowers are clustered in panicles and bloom during the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its rapid growth and drought tolerance contribute to its ecological significance in its native habitats.
