Tomoxis

Tomoxis, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant belonging to the family Malvaceae.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is often found in wetlands and disturbed soils, thriving in humid climates.

Due to its rapid growth, it can become an invasive species in some regions.
