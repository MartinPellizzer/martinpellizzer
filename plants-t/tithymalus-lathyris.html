Tithymalus lathyris

Tithymalus lathyris, commonly known as the lesser catchfly, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Caryophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, pink or white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has hairy leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats.

It is often found in meadows, roadsides, and along forest edges.
