Timonius lamii

Timonius lamii, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern China.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Timonius lamii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
