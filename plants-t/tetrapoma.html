Tetrapoma

Tetrapoma, commonly known as the water spinach or kangkong, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its succulent, edible leaves and stems. The plant typically grows in wet or aquatic environments, such as marshes and rice fields. Its leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Tetrapoma is valued for its nutritional content and is widely cultivated as a vegetable in many tropical regions.
