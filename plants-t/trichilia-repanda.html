Trichilia repanda

Trichilia repanda, commonly known as the African sandalwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and fragrant wood. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by flat, pod-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Due to its valuable timber, it is also subject to conservation concerns.
