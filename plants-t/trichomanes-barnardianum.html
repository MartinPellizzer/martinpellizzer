Trichomanes barnardianum

Trichomanes barnardianum, commonly known as the African maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically light green and covered in a layer of tiny, water-repellent scales. This plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds and produce spores for propagation.

Trichomanes barnardianum is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soil.
