Tricholaena fusca

Tricholaena fusca, commonly known as the black bamboo, is a member of the grass family Poaceae.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its dark green, often almost black, foliage. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. Tricholaena fusca is native to tropical regions of Africa and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread readily, making it both a useful ornamental plant and a potential invasive species in certain areas.
