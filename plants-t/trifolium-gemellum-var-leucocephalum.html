Trifolium gemellum var. leucocephalum

Trifolium gemellum var. leucocephalum, commonly known as the white-headed clover, is a variant of the trifoliate clover species.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in spherical heads, giving it its common name. It is a low-growing perennial herb that thrives in well-drained soils.

This variety is often found in grasslands and along roadsides, where it contributes to soil stabilization and provides forage for livestock.
