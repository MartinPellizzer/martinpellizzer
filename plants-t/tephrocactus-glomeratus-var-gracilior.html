Tephrocactus glomeratus var. gracilior

Tephrocactus glomeratus var. gracilior, commonly known as the slender clustered cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, cylindrical stems that are typically green or pale blue in color. The plant produces small, white flowers during the spring and summer months, which are clustered at the stem tips. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for desert environments.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and xeriscaping.
