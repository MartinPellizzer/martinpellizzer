Tium ursinum

Tium ursinum, commonly known as bear's garlic, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Alliaceae family, which includes onions and garlic, and is characterized by its grass-like leaves and small, white, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces bulbous structures similar to garlic cloves, which are edible and have a strong, pungent aroma. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Tium ursinum is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
