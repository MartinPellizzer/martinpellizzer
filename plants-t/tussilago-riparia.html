Tussilago riparia

Tussilago riparia, commonly known as the narrowleaf coughwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and riparian habitats.

It typically grows in moist soils along streams, rivers, and marshes, thriving in areas with high water tables. The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale purple. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and flies, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.

Tussilago riparia is valued for its medicinal properties, with historical use in treating respiratory ailments.
