Tradescantia recta

Tradescantia recta, commonly known as the spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically bright green. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or violet, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Tradescantia recta is a hardy perennial that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of garden conditions.
