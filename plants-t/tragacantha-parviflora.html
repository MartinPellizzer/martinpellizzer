Tragacantha parviflora

Tragacantha parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered tragacanth, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a taproot system and produces erect, branched stems covered in soft, hairy foliage. The plant bears small, yellowish-white flowers clustered in umbels, which bloom in the spring. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

Tragacantha parviflora is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
