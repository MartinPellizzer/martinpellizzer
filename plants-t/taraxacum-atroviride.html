Taraxacum atroviride

Taraxacum atroviride, commonly known as the greenish dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright green, heart-shaped leaves and a yellow flower head that develops into a fluffy seed dispersal mechanism. Native to Europe, it thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant has a taproot system and is known for its ability to tolerate various soil conditions.

It is also noted for its medicinal properties, traditionally used for its diuretic and anti-inflammatory effects.
