Tapinanthus englerianus

Tapinanthus englerianus, commonly known as the parasitic dodder, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orobanchaceae family.

It is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the roots of other plants to obtain water and nutrients. Its stems are covered in fine, thread-like structures called haustoria, which penetrate the host's vascular system. Due to its parasitic nature, it can cause significant damage to agricultural crops.
