Tanacetum baltistanicum

Tanacetum baltistanicum, commonly known as the Baltistan tansy, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Pakistan and India.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of both ray and disc florets. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving at elevations above 3,000 meters. Its leaves are serrated and aromatic, often emitting a sharp, camphor-like scent.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer, contributing to the plant's ecological role in pollination and habitat support.
