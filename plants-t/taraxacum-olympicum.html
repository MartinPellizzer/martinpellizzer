Taraxacum olympicum

Taraxacum olympicum, commonly known as the Olympic dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are typically hairy and have a waxy coating. Its yellow flower head consists of numerous small florets that bloom in the spring and early summer.

The Olympic dandelion is often found in moist, disturbed soils and is adapted to thrive in mountainous regions.
