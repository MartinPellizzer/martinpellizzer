Teucrium flavum proles glaucum

Teucrium flavum proles glaucum, commonly known as the blue germander, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine, whitish bloom, giving them a silvery appearance. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

It is also valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
