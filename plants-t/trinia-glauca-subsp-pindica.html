Trinia glauca subsp. pindica

Trinia glauca subsp.

pindica, commonly known as the Pindica trinia, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments.

The plant features small, narrow leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. Its flowers are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences and are usually pale blue or white in color.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and resilience.
