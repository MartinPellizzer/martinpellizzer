Tetranthera melastomacea

Tetranthera melastomacea, commonly known as the pink melastome, is a flowering plant belonging to the Melastomataceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in clusters, often with five petals. Its leaves are typically opposite, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Tetranthera melastomacea is also known for its medicinal properties, with various parts of the plant used in traditional remedies.
