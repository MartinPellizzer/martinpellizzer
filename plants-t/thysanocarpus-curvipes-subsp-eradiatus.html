Thysanocarpus curvipes subsp. eradiatus

Thysanocarpus curvipes subsp.

eradiatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thysanocarpus, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers.

The plant produces distinctive fruits with hooked bracts, which aid in seed dispersal. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is more commonly found in its natural habitat.
