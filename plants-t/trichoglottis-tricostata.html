Trichoglottis tricostata

Trichoglottis tricostata, commonly known as the three-costate orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a rhizomatous growth habit, often found in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a raceme, with distinctive three-lobed sepals and petals. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, often with a waxy coating that helps retain moisture.

Trichoglottis tricostata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
