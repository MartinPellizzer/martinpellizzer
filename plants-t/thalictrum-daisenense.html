Thalictrum daisenense

Thalictrum daisenense, commonly known as the Japanese meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like foliage and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its stems are usually unbranched and can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

Thalictrum daisenense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
