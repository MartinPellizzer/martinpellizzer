Tachigali emarginata

Tachigali emarginata, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or white tamarind tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, white to pink flowers. The tree produces small, round, yellow fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its bark and leaves contain compounds with potential pharmacological properties.

Tachigali emarginata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its timber.
