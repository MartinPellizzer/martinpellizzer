Taxodium ascendens

Taxodium ascendens, commonly known as the pond cypress, is a deciduous conifer native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, columnar growth habit and its ability to thrive in wetland environments. The tree produces small, spherical cones and has scale-like leaves that turn a striking reddish color in the fall. Its roots are adapted to waterlogged soils, making it a key species in swamp ecosystems.

The wood of Taxodium ascendens is resistant to decay, which has historically made it valuable for construction and boat-building.
