Turnera angelicae

Turnera angelicae, commonly known as angel's trumpets, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant is evergreen, with glossy, dark green leaves that are opposite and ovate in shape. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

However, it contains toxic alkaloids and should be handled with care.
