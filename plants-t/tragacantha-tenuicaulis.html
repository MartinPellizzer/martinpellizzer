Tragacantha tenuicaulis

Tragacantha tenuicaulis, commonly known as the slender tragacanth, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, erect stems that are often branched and covered with fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in the spring. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly rough texture.

The plant is known for its mucilaginous sap, which has been used historically in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive.
