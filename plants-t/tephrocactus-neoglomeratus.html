Tephrocactus neoglomeratus

Tephrocactus neoglomeratus, commonly known as the Mexican star cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive star-shaped flowers and cylindrical stems covered in spines. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh desert conditions. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, appearing in late spring or early summer.

Tephrocactus neoglomeratus is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
