Thalictrum friesii

Thalictrum friesii, commonly known as the Alpine meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, yellow-green flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in well-drained, moist soils. It is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, where it contributes to the biodiversity of these specialized environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
