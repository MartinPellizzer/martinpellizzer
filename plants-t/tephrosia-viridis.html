Tephrosia viridis

Tephrosia viridis, commonly known as green tephrosia, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the wet season. Its roots contain bioactive compounds, making it valuable in traditional medicine and pest control.

Tephrosia viridis is also used in agroforestry systems for soil stabilization and as a forage crop.
