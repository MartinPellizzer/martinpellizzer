Taraxacum chionogeiton

Taraxacum chionogeiton, commonly known as the snow dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, silver-gray leaves and a flowering stem that bears a single, yellow composite flower head. This species is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is adapted to cold, alpine environments. The plant produces a milky sap, which is typical of the genus Taraxacum.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations.
