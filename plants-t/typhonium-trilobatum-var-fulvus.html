Typhonium trilobatum var. fulvus

Typhonium trilobatum var. fulvus, commonly known as the golden arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial with a rhizomatous root system that produces edible tubers. The plant features large, arrowhead-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a reddish tinge. Its flowers are small and green, often hidden beneath the foliage, and it thrives in moist, shady environments.

The tubers are used in various culinary applications, particularly in Asian cuisine.
