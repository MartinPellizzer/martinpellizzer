Thymus loevyanus var. kosteleckyanus

Thymus loevyanus var. kosteleckyanus, commonly known as the Kostelecky's thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan region.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and aromatic, gray-green foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming in early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun exposure.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
