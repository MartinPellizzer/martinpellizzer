Thymus × monrealensis nothosubsp. garcia-vallejoae

Thymus × monrealensis nothosubsp.

garcia-vallejoae, commonly known as the Valencia thyme, is a hybrid plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and aromatic, gray-green foliage.

The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers in terminal clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its essential oils contain thymol and carvacrol, which contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.
