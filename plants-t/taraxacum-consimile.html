Taraxacum consimile

Taraxacum consimile, commonly known as the common dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its rosette of basal, spoon-shaped leaves and a hollow, leafless stem that supports a flowering head. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that develop into fluffy white seed heads, which are dispersed by the wind. It is widely distributed across temperate regions and is often considered a weed in gardens and lawns.

Taraxacum consimile is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies for various health purposes.
