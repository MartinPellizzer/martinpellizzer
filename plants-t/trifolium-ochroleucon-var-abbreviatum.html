Trifolium ochroleucon var. abbreviatum

Trifolium ochroleucon var. abbreviatum, commonly known as the short-leaved yellow trefoil, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves composed of three small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed areas.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
