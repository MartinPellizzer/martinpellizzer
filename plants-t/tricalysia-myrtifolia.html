Tricalysia myrtifolia

Tricalysia myrtifolia, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring and summer. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Tricalysia myrtifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
