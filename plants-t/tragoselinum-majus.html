Tragoselinum majus

Tragoselinum majus, commonly known as the greater celandine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a robust rhizomatous root system and produces bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant has heart-shaped, lobed leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It is often found in moist, disturbed soils along roadsides and in meadows.

Tragoselinum majus is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects.
