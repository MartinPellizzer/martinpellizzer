Thryptomene calcicola

Thryptomene calcicola, commonly known as the limestone thryptomene, is a small shrub native to the arid regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to rocky and sandy soils, thriving in low-nutrient environments.

Thryptomene calcicola plays an important role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and provides shelter for various small animals and insects.
