Trichosanthes cucumeroides var. formosana

Trichosanthes cucumeroides var. formosana, commonly known as the Taiwan bitter melon, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It produces large, elongated fruits with a thick, rough rind and a bitter interior. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruit, though it requires careful handling due to its bitterness.

The species is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
