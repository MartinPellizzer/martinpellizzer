Tradescantia versicolor

Tradescantia versicolor, commonly known as the wandering jew, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that can vary in color from green to purple, red, and variegated patterns. Its small, clustered flowers are usually pink or white, blooming in late spring to early summer.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
