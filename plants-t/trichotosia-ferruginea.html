Trichotosia ferruginea

Trichotosia ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty trichotosia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features slender, branched stems covered in fine, rusty-brown hairs, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, blooming during the spring and summer seasons. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Trichotosia ferruginea is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
