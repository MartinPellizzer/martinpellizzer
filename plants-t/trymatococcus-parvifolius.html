Trymatococcus parvifolius

Trymatococcus parvifolius, commonly known as the small-leaved tamarisk, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, densely branched structure and small, needle-like leaves that reduce water loss. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple, which bloom in summer. It has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in dry conditions.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to harsh environments and a hardy choice for xeriscaping.
