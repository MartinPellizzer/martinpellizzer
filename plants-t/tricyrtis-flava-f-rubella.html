Tricyrtis flava f. rubella

Tricyrtis flava f. rubella, commonly known as the red-flowered Japanese iris, is a unique hybrid variety of the Japanese iris species.

It is characterized by its striking red petals, which contrast vividly with its white or pale yellow sepals. This plant is native to Japan and thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in woodland or streamside habitats. Its flowers bloom in late summer, adding a splash of color to gardens and natural landscapes.

The f. rubella form is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its vibrant, showy blooms.
