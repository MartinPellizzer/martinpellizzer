Triticum triaristatum var. trispiculatum

Triticum triaristatum var. trispiculatum, commonly known as the wild wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous florets, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its seeds are small and oval, often retaining a hull that can be removed during processing.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and potential use in forage and conservation efforts.
