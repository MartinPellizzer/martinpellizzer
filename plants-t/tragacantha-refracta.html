Tragacantha refracta

Tragacantha refracta, commonly known as the brittle tragacanth, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive, branched stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by pods containing several seeds. Tragacantha refracta is known for its brittle, fibrous stems, which are used in traditional medicine and as a source of gum.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and pharmacological studies.
