Tulipa confusa

Tulipa confusa, commonly known as the Turkish tulip, is a bulbous perennial plant native to Turkey and parts of the Middle East.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that come in a range of colors, including red, yellow, and white. The plant typically grows to a height of 15 to 30 centimeters, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its bulbs are adapted to store nutrients, allowing it to thrive in well-drained, sandy soils.

Tulipa confusa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
