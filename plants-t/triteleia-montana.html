Triteleia montana

Triteleia montana, commonly known as the Sierra lily, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its bulbs are adapted to survive in dry conditions, and it blooms in early spring.

Triteleia montana is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
