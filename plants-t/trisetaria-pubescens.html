Trisetaria pubescens

Trisetaria pubescens, commonly known as the hairy trisetaria, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves covered in fine pubescence, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. Its inflorescence consists of small, spike-like clusters of flowers, typical of many grass species.

Trisetaria pubescens is often found in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and along roadsides.
