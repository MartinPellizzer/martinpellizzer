Taraxacum moriceps

Taraxacum moriceps, commonly known as the upright dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit, distinguishing it from the more commonly seen prostrate dandelions. The plant features basal rosettes of serrated, grass-like leaves and produces a single, tall, hollow stem that supports a bright yellow flower head. Its flowers develop into fluffy white pappus-bearing seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Taraxacum moriceps is typically found in disturbed soils and open areas, showcasing its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
