Thalictrum sphaerocarpum subsp. capitatum

Thalictrum sphaerocarpum subsp.

capitatum, commonly known as the clustered meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Asia. It features slender, upright stems that bear palmate leaves with finely divided lobes.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in a dense, spherical cluster at the top of the stem. Its flowers are typically pollinated by insects, and the plant thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and unique floral display.
