Terminalia superba

Terminalia superba, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, light-colored bark and elongated leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds. Its wood is highly valued for its fine grain and durability, though it is also used in traditional medicine.

Terminalia superba plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
