Tansaniochloa pseudaristata

Tansaniochloa pseudaristata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
