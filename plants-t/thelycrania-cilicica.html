Thelycrania cilicica

Thelycrania cilicica, commonly known as the Cilician thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple-blue flower heads that appear in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which helps reduce water loss.

Thelycrania cilicica is often found in open woodlands and scrublands, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
