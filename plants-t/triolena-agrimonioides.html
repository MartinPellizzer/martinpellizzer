Triolena agrimonioides

Triolena agrimonioides, commonly known as the false agrimony, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features upright, branched stems covered in fine hairs and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Triolena agrimonioides is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its delicate foliage and flowering habit.
