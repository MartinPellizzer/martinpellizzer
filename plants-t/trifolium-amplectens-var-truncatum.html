Trifolium amplectens var. truncatum

Trifolium amplectens var. truncatum, commonly known as the truncated clover, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets with a truncate (cut-off) tip. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
