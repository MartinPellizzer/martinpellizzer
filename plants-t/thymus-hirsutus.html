Thymus hirsutus

Thymus hirsutus, commonly known as woolly thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It features small, fragrant, pink or white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves covered in fine hairs, giving it a woolly appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Thymus hirsutus is valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in herbal remedies and culinary applications.
