Tuckeya candelabrum

Tuckeya candelabrum, commonly known as the candlestick plant, is a unique succulent native to South Africa.

It features a tall, unbranched stem that resembles a candlestick, with small, clustered leaves at the top. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its striking appearance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in arid gardens.
