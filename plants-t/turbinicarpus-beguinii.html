Turbinicarpus beguinii

Turbinicarpus beguinii, commonly known as the Beguin's turbinaria, is a species of succulent cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a white, powdery coating. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
