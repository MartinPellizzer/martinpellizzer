Teucrium polium subsp. luteum

Teucrium polium subsp.

luteum, commonly known as the yellow germander, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It features upright, branched stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a slightly serrated margin.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to reddish-purple, depending on the stage of bloom. Its flowering period typically occurs in late spring to early summer, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

The species is noted for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
