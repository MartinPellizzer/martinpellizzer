Taraxacum maritimum

Taraxacum maritimum, commonly known as sea dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to coastal regions of Europe and North Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of stiff, hairy leaves and a slender, flowering stem that bears a single yellow composite flower head. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to saline and coastal environments. Its flowers are typically visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is also known for its medicinal properties, with parts of it used in traditional remedies.
