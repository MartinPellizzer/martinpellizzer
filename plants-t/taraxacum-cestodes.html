Taraxacum cestodes

Taraxacum cestodes, commonly known as the hairy dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and a hollow, hairy stem that supports a single flower head. The flower head consists of numerous yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular florets. After flowering, it produces a distinctive, elongated, hollow stem topped with a spherical seed head composed of numerous parachute-like seeds.

This plant is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
