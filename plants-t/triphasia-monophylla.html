Triphasia monophylla

Triphasia monophylla, commonly known as the single-leaf triphasia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a small shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, and its leaves are usually simple and alternate. It has a woody stem and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
