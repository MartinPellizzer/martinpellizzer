Tragia novae-hollandiae

Tragia novae-hollandiae, commonly known as the Australian burr, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, characterized by its woody stems and dense foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by distinctive, spiny seed pods. Its adaptability to harsh environments makes it a resilient species in its native habitat.

The plant's unique morphology and ecological role contribute to its significance in local ecosystems.
