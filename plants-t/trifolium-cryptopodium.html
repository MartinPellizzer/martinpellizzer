Trifolium cryptopodium

Trifolium cryptopodium, commonly known as the hidden clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Trifolium cryptopodium is often found in nutrient-rich soils and is a member of the legume family, capable of nitrogen fixation through symbiotic relationships with soil bacteria.
