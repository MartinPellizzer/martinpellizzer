Taraxacum szovitsii

Taraxacum szovitsii, commonly known as the Szovits' dandelion, is a perennial herb native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of pale green, hairy leaves. The plant produces a single, hollow, leaf-like stem that terminates in a bright yellow flower head. It typically blooms from late spring to early summer, and its seeds are dispersed by wind via a parachute-like structure.

This species is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils, showing adaptability to various environmental conditions.
