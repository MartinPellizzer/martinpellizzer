Triticum turgidum var. compositum

Triticum turgidum var. compositum, commonly known as durum wheat, is a tetraploid species belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its compact spikes and hard, tough grains that are well-suited for milling into semolina. This variety is primarily cultivated for its high gluten content, which makes it ideal for producing pasta and bread. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and has narrow, elongated leaves.

Its botanical features contribute to its adaptability to various climates and its importance in global food production.
