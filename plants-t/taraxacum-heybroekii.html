Taraxacum heybroekii

Taraxacum heybroekii, commonly known as the Heybroek's dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of stiff, hairy leaves. The plant produces a yellow composite flower head that develops into a hollow, spherical seed sphere with bracts. It thrives in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically significant species within its native range.
