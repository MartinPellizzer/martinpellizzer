Tanarius indicus

Tanarius indicus, commonly known as the Indian tanarius, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, tropical regions and is often found in open woodlands or along roadsides. It produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, attracting various pollinators.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and for ecological restoration projects.
