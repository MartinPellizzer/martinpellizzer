Teucrium cymosum

Teucrium cymosum, commonly known as upright germander, is a perennial herb native to Europe, North Africa, and western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and sage. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and clusters of small, pale blue to violet flowers. Its leaves are often fragrant and can be used in herbal teas or as a culinary herb.

Teucrium cymosum thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
