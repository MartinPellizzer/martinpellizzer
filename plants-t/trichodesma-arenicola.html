Trichodesma arenicola

Trichodesma arenicola, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a small, annual herb with hairy, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is adapted to hot, dry environments and has a deep root system to access water.

Trichodesma arenicola is often found in sandy soils and is used in some traditional medicinal practices.
