Tetracentron sinense

Tetracentron sinense, commonly known as the Chinese catalpa, is a deciduous tree native to eastern China.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant white to cream-colored flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain flat, winged seeds. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils along rivers and in mountainous regions.

Tetracentron sinense is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and parks.
