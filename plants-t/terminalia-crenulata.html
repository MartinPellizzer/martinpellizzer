Terminalia crenulata

Terminalia crenulata, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and elliptical leaves with serrated edges. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits that contain several seeds. It is valued for its hard, heavy wood, which is used in furniture and carving, and for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.

Terminalia crenulata also plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
