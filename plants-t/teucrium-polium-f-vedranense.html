Teucrium polium f. vedranense

Teucrium polium f. vedranense, commonly known as the Balkan germander, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense spikes. Its leaves are narrow and serrated, often with a strong aromatic scent.

This variety is known for its hardiness and adaptability to rocky and well-drained soils.
