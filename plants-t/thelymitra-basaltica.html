Thelymitra basaltica

Thelymitra basaltica, commonly known as the basalt orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Australia.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and features a single, narrow leaf. The plant produces a single spike of flowers, typically with three to five fragrant, white or pale blue petals. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in areas with basaltic substrates.

Thelymitra basaltica is a rare and endangered species, requiring specific environmental conditions for successful growth.
