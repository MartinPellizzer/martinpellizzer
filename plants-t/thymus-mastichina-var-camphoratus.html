Thymus mastichina var. camphoratus

Thymus mastichina var. camphoratus, commonly known as the camphor thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and aromatic, gray-green foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its essential oils, including camphor and thymol, contribute to its use in aromatherapy and traditional medicine.
