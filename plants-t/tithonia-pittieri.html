Tithonia pittieri

Tithonia pittieri, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a herbaceous plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, bright orange to red flower heads, which are typically surrounded by yellow ray florets. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a strong, aromatic scent. Tithonia pittieri thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

It is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
