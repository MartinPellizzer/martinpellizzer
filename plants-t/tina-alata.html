Tina alata

Tina alata, commonly known as the creeping jenny, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and heart-shaped leaves that form a dense mat. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to suppress weeds.

Tina alata is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
