Terminalia subacroptera

Terminalia subacroptera, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing small, flat seeds. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.
