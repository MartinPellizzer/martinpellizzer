Triodia acuminata

Triodia acuminata, commonly known as the spinifex grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, wiry growth habit and the presence of sharp, spiny leaves. The plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in sandy soils. Its structure forms tussocks that provide habitat for various small animals and insects.

Triodia acuminata plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion in its native range.
