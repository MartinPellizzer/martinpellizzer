Thymus serpyllum var. ocheus

Thymus serpyllum var. ocheus, commonly known as narrow-leaved thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pink to lavender flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, as well as its ornamental appeal.
