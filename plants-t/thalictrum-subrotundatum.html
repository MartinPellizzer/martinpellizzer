Thalictrum subrotundatum

Thalictrum subrotundatum, commonly known as the round-leaved meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves that are typically rounded or elliptic in shape. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers in early summer, which are borne on slender, branching stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and wetland restoration projects.
