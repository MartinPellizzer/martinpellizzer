Trigonella ornithorynchos

Trigonella ornithorynchos, commonly known as the Egyptian trigonella, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its historical significance and medicinal properties, it has been cultivated for centuries in various parts of the world.
