Triticum junceum

Triticum junceum, commonly known as wild wheat, is a species of grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences containing numerous tiny florets. It is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in temperate climates.

Due to its historical significance, it has been studied for its potential in agricultural and ecological contexts.
