Triticum repens var. intermedium

Triticum repens var. intermedium, commonly known as intermediate meadow grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow leaves, typically growing up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous florets, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its role in ecological systems and as a forage crop for livestock.
