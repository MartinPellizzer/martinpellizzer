Topobea stephanochaeta

Topobea stephanochaeta, commonly known as the stephanochaeta sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist to water-saturated soils. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are often arranged in a dense rosette, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. Its root system is fibrous and helps stabilize the soil, making it ecologically important in wetland ecosystems.

The species is valued for its ability to tolerate fluctuating water levels and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
