Tragacantha turkmenorum

Tragacantha turkmenorum, commonly known as Turkmen tragacanth, is a perennial shrub native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by pods containing seeds. It is valued for its gum, which is harvested from the bark and used in traditional medicine and as a thickening agent.

The species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in harsh climates.
