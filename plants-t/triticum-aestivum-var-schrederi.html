Triticum aestivum var. schrederi

Triticum aestivum var. schrederi, commonly known as Schreder's wheat, is a botanical variant of common wheat characterized by its unique morphological features.

It is a diploid species with a genome size smaller than that of standard hexaploid wheat, contributing to its distinct genetic profile. The plant typically exhibits shorter stature and reduced grain size compared to its hexaploid relatives. Its development is influenced by specific environmental conditions, making it a subject of interest in agricultural research.

This variety is often studied for its potential in breeding programs aimed at improving wheat resilience and yield.
