Turbinicarpus × azanthus

Turbinicarpus × azanthus, commonly known as the hybrid moon cactus, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Turbinicarpus polycentrus and Turbinicarpus pumilus.

It is a succulent belonging to the Cactaceae family, characterized by its compact, globular shape and thick, fleshy stems. The plant is native to Mexico and is often cultivated for its unique appearance and ability to thrive in arid conditions. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, appearing in late spring or early summer.

Due to its hybrid nature, it is not typically found in the wild and is primarily grown by enthusiasts.
