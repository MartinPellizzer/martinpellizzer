Tragacantha orthocarpa

Tragacantha orthocarpa, commonly known as the Persian gum or Astragalus orthocarpus, is a flowering plant native to regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in spikes, followed by distinctive, inflated pods that contain seeds.

It is valued for its resin, which has been used historically in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive.
