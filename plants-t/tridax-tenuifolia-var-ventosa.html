Tridax tenuifolia var. ventosa

Tridax tenuifolia var. ventosa, commonly known as the slenderleaf tridax, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate, narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils, showcasing its adaptability to various environments. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, and it produces achene seeds with pappus for wind dispersal.

The species is often found in the Americas and has some medicinal uses in traditional practices.
