Tussilago suaveolens

Tussilago suaveolens, commonly known as sweet coltsfoot, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped, hairy leaves and a flowering stem that bears clusters of yellow, dandelion-like flowers. The plant is often found in moist meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks. Its flowers are typically visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological role.

Tussilago suaveolens is also known for its medicinal properties, historically used to treat respiratory ailments.
