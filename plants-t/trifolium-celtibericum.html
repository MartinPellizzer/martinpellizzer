Trifolium celtibericum

Trifolium celtibericum, commonly known as the Celtiberian clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the Iberian Peninsula and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from spring to early summer. It thrives in grassy areas and is often found in disturbed soils, showing a preference for temperate climates.

Due to its historical significance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
