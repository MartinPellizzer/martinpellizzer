Trochocephalus pectinatus

Trochocephalus pectinatus, commonly known as the veined or spotted orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive veined leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant is native to tropical regions of Africa and is often found in moist, shaded environments. Its roots are fibrous and adapted for nutrient absorption in poor soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in greenhouses.
