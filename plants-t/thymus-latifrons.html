Thymus latifrons

Thymus latifrons, commonly known as broadleaf thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are broad and fragrant, often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Thymus latifrons produces small, pink or white flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
