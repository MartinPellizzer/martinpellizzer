Thymus bulgaricus

Thymus bulgaricus, commonly known as Bulgarian thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant leaves and flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for rocky or dry environments. Its essential oils, rich in thymol and carvacrol, are widely used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine.

Thymus bulgaricus is also valued in culinary applications for its strong, aromatic flavor.
