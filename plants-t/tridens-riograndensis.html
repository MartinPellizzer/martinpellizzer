Tridens riograndensis

Tridens riograndensis, commonly known as the Rio Grande tridens, is a flowering plant native to the grasslands of South America.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear tiny, spikelet-like flowers. It is adapted to grow in dry, open habitats and is often found in disturbed soils.

Tridens riograndensis plays a minor role in local ecosystems but is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and habitat preferences.
