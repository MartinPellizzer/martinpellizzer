Thalictrum subpubescens

Thalictrum subpubescens, commonly known as the downy meadow-rue, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves with a soft, hairy texture, and it produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like buttercups and anemones.

Thalictrum subpubescens is valued in gardens for its ornamental foliage and subtle floral display.
