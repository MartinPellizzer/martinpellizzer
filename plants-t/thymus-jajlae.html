Thymus jajlae

Thymus jajlae, commonly known as Jajla thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by aromatic leaves and showy flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in full sun conditions. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces tiny, aromatic leaves used in traditional herbal medicine.

Thymus jajlae is valued for its essential oils, which contain thymol and other antimicrobial compounds.
