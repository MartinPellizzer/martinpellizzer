Trichloris lilloi

Trichloris lilloi, commonly known as the Lillo's trichloris, is a perennial grass native to South Africa.

It typically grows in open grasslands and rocky areas, exhibiting a compact growth habit with slender, wiry stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are characteristic of the grass family Poaceae. Its leaves are narrow and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its drought-resistant adaptations.

Trichloris lilloi is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
