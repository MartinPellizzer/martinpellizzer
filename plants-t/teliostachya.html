Teliostachya

Teliostachya, commonly known as the spikegrass, is a genus of flowering plants in the grass family, Poaceae.

These plants are characterized by their slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. Teliostachya species typically produce panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are often wind-pollinated. The genus is notable for its ecological importance in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
