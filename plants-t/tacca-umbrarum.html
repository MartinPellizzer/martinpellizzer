Tacca umbrarum

Tacca umbrarum, commonly known as the shadow orchid, is a rare and elusive orchid species native to the dense rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, dark purple petals that resemble a skull, giving it an eerie and mysterious appearance. The plant thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires high levels of atmospheric moisture to flourish. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of fascination for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss and its elusive nature, Tacca umbrarum is considered critically endangered in the wild.
