Tradescantia humboldtiana

Tradescantia humboldtiana, commonly known as the wandering jew or red-edge tradescantia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often pink or red-veined leaves that are broad and oval in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. It is a perennial herb that thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight.

Due to its attractive foliage, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens and indoor spaces.
