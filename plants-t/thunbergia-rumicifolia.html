Thunbergia rumicifolia

Thunbergia rumicifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant is a climbing vine with a rapid growth habit, often found in moist, shaded environments. Its flowers open in the morning and close in the evening, hence its common name.

Thunbergia rumicifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
