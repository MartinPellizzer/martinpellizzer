Tsuga chinensis subsp. tchekiangensis

Tsuga chinensis subsp.

tchekiangensis, commonly known as the Chinese fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to eastern China. It belongs to the genus Tsuga, which includes other species like the hemlock.

The tree is characterized by its pyramidal shape, needle-like leaves, and cones that hang downward. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

This subspecies is valued for its timber and is also used in horticulture for its ornamental qualities.
