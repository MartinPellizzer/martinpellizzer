Tanacetum cadmeum subsp. orientale

Tanacetum cadmeum subsp.

orientale, commonly known as the oriental tansy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, aromatic leaves and small yellow flower heads.

The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and grasslands. It produces a pungent odor due to the presence of thymol and other essential oils.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine.
