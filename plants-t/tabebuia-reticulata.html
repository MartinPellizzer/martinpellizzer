Tabebuia reticulata

Tabebuia reticulata, commonly known as the flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery orange to red flowers that bloom in the dry season, making it a popular ornamental plant. The tree has a rough, fissured bark and exudes a fragrant sap when cut. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a velvety texture.

Tabebuia reticulata is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display and is used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
