Triodia elliottii

Triodia elliottii, commonly known as the Elliott's spinifex, is a hardy perennial grass found in arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, wiry foliage and ability to thrive in harsh, low-nutrient soils. The plant forms compact clumps with narrow, needle-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It plays a crucial role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for small desert fauna.

Triodia elliottii is well adapted to drought and can survive extreme temperature fluctuations.
