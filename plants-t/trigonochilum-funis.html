Trigonochilum funis

Trigonochilum funis, commonly known as the long-stalked orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, pale yellow or greenish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes. Trigonochilum funis thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically in tropical forests.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and its role in the ecological diversity of orchid ecosystems.
