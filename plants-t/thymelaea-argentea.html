Thymelaea argentea

Thymelaea argentea, commonly known as silver thymelaea, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine, woolly hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its drought tolerance and attractive foliage.
