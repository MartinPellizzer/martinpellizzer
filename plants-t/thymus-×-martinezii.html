Thymus × martinezii

Thymus × martinezii, commonly known as the Martinez thyme, is a hybrid perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage of small, aromatic leaves. The plant produces clusters of pink or purple flowers in the spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its essential oils contain thymol and carvacrol, which contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.
