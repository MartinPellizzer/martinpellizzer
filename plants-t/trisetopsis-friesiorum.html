Trisetopsis friesiorum

Trisetopsis friesiorum, commonly known as the slender hookless grass, is a member of the Poaceae family.

It is a low-growing, perennial grass that typically forms dense mats on rocky or sandy soils. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is native to southern Africa and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to harsh conditions, with deep root systems and reduced leaf surface area to minimize water loss.
