Trifolium andersonii

Trifolium andersonii, commonly known as Anderson's clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open meadows and rocky areas.

Trifolium andersonii is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and its use in local ecosystems.
