Tragacantha meridionalis

Tragacantha meridionalis, commonly known as the southern tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and pinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by pod-like fruits containing kidney-shaped seeds. It is known for its ability to thrive in dry, rocky soils and is often found in scrubland and coastal areas.

Tragacantha meridionalis is also valued for its historical use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural gum.
