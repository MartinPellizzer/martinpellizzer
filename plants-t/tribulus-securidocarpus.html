Tribulus securidocarpus

Tribulus securidocarpus, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in dry, sandy soils and thrives in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. Its leaves are usually lobed and have a hairy texture, while its stems are woody and branching.

Tribulus securidocarpus is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
