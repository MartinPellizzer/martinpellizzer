Teucrium lusitanicum subsp. aureoforme

Teucrium lusitanicum subsp.

aureoforme, commonly known as the golden-hairy germander, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its creeping growth habit and hairy, often reddish stems.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, golden hairs, giving the plant its common name. It produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple, arranged in dense clusters.

This subspecies is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness.
