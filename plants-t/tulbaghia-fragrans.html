Tulbaghia fragrans

Tulbaghia fragrans, commonly known as the star of Bethlehem, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink star-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and pleasant scent. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Tulbaghia fragrans is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
