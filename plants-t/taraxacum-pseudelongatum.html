Taraxacum pseudelongatum

Taraxacum pseudelongatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, spoon-shaped leaves and a hairy stem that bears a single flower head. The plant produces a yellow composite flower that develops into a distinctive white pappus-covered seed head. It is native to Europe and is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides.

Taraxacum pseudelongatum is a perennial herb that thrives in well-drained soils and is relatively drought-tolerant.
