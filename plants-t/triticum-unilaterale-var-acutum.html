Triticum unilaterale var. acutum

Triticum unilaterale var. acutum, commonly known as the sharp-edged wheatgrass, is a variant of the wheatgrass species characterized by its distinct, sharp leaf margins.

It is a perennial grass that thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green and can grow up to several inches in length. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and small, inconspicuous flowers that develop into spikelets.

This variety is sometimes cultivated for its potential nutritional value and is used in herbal remedies and livestock feed.
