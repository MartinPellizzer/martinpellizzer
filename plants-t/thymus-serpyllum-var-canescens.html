Thymus serpyllum var. canescens

Thymus serpyllum var. canescens, commonly known as hairy thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and western Asia.

It features small, pale green leaves covered in fine, hairy glands, which give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers in the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This variety is valued for its aromatic foliage and is commonly used in culinary and medicinal applications.
