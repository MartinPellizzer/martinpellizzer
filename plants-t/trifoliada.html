Trifoliada

Trifoliada, commonly known as the three-leaved plant, is a species characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged symmetrically.

It typically belongs to the genus Trifolium, which includes various species of clover. The plant is often found in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually small, oval, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Trifoliada is also known for its small, pink or white flowers that bloom in clusters, making it a common sight in meadows and along roadsides.
