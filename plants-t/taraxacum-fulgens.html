Taraxacum fulgens

Taraxacum fulgens, commonly known as the golden dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by the familiar fluffy seed heads. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas such as meadows and grasslands. It has a basal rosette of serrated, hairy leaves that help it retain moisture.

Taraxacum fulgens is often found in temperate regions and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
