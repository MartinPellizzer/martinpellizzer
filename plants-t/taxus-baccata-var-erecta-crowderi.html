Taxus baccata var. erecta-crowderi

Taxus baccata var. erecta-crowderi, commonly known as the Crowder's yew, is a variant of the European yew tree.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and conical shape, making it a striking ornamental plant. The leaves are needle-like, dark green, and arranged in spirals along the stems. Its small, red-orange berries are toxic to humans and animals.

This variety is prized for its compact form and ability to thrive in a range of soil conditions.
