Taraxacum rhadinolepis

Taraxacum rhadinolepis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and a flowering stem that bears a single, composite flower head. The plant produces a distinctive, hollow, wind-dispersed seed head known as a pappus. It is native to Central Asia and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Taraxacum rhadinolepis is a perennial herb that exhibits a high degree of adaptability to arid conditions.
