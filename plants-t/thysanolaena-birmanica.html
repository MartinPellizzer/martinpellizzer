Thysanolaena birmanica

Thysanolaena birmanica, commonly known as the Burmese feather grass, is a perennial grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces delicate, feathery inflorescences that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks. Its leaves are narrow and blade-like, with a slight sheen, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.

Thysanolaena birmanica is also used in traditional medicine in some regions for its purported health benefits.
