Trembleya parviflora var. triflora

Trembleya parviflora var. triflora, commonly known as the triflorous trembleya, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in threes, giving it its specific epithet "triflora.". The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a velvety texture.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
