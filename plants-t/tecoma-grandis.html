Tecoma grandis

Tecoma grandis, commonly known as the yellow bells, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, featuring lance-shaped leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The flowers are typically tubular with a distinctive yellow corolla, and they emit a sweet, honey-like fragrance. Tecoma grandis is well adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, thriving in full sun and well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
