Talisia pygmaea

Talisia pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf talisia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It features glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along its stems. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by round, orange-red berries. Talisia pygmaea thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural use in tropical and subtropical gardens.
