Tragacantha biflora

Tragacantha biflora, commonly known as the two-flowered tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces a unique resin, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils and along dry riverbeds.

Due to habitat loss and overharvesting, it is considered a vulnerable species in some regions.
