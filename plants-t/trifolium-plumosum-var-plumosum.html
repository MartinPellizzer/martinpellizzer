Trifolium plumosum var. plumosum

Trifolium plumosum var. plumosum, commonly known as the plumose clover, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are composed of three oval-shaped leaflets with a distinctive hairy texture. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that are clustered in dense heads, typical of the legume family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grassy areas or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and nitrogen-fixing ability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and agricultural settings.
