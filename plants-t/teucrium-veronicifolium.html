Teucrium veronicifolium

Teucrium veronicifolium, commonly known as blue germander, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant features square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its small, tubular blue or purple flowers are clustered in whorls along the stems, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
