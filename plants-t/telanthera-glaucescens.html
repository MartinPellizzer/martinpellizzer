Telanthera glaucescens

Telanthera glaucescens, commonly known as the blue-flowered telanthera, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a white or pinkish hue. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Telanthera glaucescens is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
