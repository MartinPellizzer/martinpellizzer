Tricholaena rhodesiana

Tricholaena rhodesiana, commonly known as Rhodesian grass or African bristlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive hairy texture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Tricholaena rhodesiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its hardy nature.
