Thevetia neriifolia var. hirsuta

Thevetia neriifolia var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy yellow trumpet tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are simple, alternate, and often covered with fine hairs, giving the plant its "hirsuta" designation. The plant is known for its fast growth and is sometimes used in ornamental gardening.

It produces pods containing seeds that are toxic, requiring caution during handling.
