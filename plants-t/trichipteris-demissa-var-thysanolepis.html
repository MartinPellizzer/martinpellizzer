Trichipteris demissa var. thysanolepis

Trichipteris demissa var. thysanolepis, commonly known as the silky fern, is a rare and delicate fern species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, silky-textured fronds that give it a soft, ethereal appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest undergrowth or along stream banks. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds, a common feature in many ferns.
