Tanacetum balsamita

Tanacetum balsamita, commonly known as sweet wormwood, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features finely divided, silvery-green leaves that are aromatic and slightly fuzzy to the touch. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It is often found in moist meadows and along stream banks, thriving in well-drained soils.

Tanacetum balsamita is known for its medicinal properties and has been used historically in herbal remedies.
