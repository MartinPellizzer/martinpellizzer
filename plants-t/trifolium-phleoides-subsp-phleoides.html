Trifolium phleoides subsp. phleoides

Trifolium phleoides subsp.

phleoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval to lance-shaped leaflets.

The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

This species is valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a forage plant for livestock.
