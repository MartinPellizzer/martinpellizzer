Turbinicarpus schmiedickeanus var. dickisoniae

Turbinicarpus schmiedickeanus var. dickisoniae, commonly known as the Dickison's Turbinicarpus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are typically covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair. The plant produces bright yellow flowers during the spring, which are often borne on short, lateral branches. It is a slow-growing species that requires minimal water and is well adapted to harsh desert conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is a sought-after plant among cactus enthusiasts.
