Themeda arguens

Themeda arguens, commonly known as the tall grass tussock, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant has narrow, cylindrical leaves that are typically green and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, sandy soils.

Themeda arguens plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
