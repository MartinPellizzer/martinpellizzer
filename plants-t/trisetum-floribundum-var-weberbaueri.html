Trisetum floribundum var. weberbaueri

Trisetum floribundum var. weberbaueri, commonly known as the Weberbauer's tufted grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features slender, tufted stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy along the edges. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, greenish flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its fine texture and delicate appearance.
