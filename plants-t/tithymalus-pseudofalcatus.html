Tithymalus pseudofalcatus

Tithymalus pseudofalcatus, commonly known as the false tithymalus, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to regions of southern Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in grassland and rocky habitats. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit, with narrow, grass-like leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in composite heads. It produces seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize open areas.
