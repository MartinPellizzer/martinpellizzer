Terminalia erecta

Terminalia erecta, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, elongated fruits containing several seeds. Terminalia erecta is valued for its durable wood, which is used in furniture and construction, and it also has traditional medicinal uses.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
