Taraxacum intricatum

Taraxacum intricatum, commonly known as the intricate dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of pale green, hairy leaves and a hollow, leafless stem that supports a single flower head. The flower head consists of both ray and disc florets, which are typically yellow in color. This species is native to Europe and is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its resilience to environmental stress.
