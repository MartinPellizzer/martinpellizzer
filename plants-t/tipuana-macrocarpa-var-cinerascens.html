Tipuana macrocarpa var. cinerascens

Tipuana macrocarpa var. cinerascens, commonly known as the cinerascens tipuana, is a deciduous tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, ash-gray bark and heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, yellow flowers in spring, followed by elongated, woody pods containing flat, oval seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.

Its adaptability and attractive foliage contribute to its use in landscaping and reforestation projects.
