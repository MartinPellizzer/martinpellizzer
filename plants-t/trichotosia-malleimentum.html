Trichotosia malleimentum

Trichotosia malleimentum, commonly known as the silverleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves covered in fine, soft hairs, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Trichotosia malleimentum is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
