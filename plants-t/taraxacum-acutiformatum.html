Taraxacum acutiformatum

Taraxacum acutiformatum, commonly known as the sharp-leaved dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers and serrated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grassy areas. It produces a distinctive seed head with parachute-like seeds for wind dispersal.

The species is notable for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
