Trichogonia

Trichogonia, commonly known as the "false orchid," is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

These plants are characterized by their small, delicate flowers and often possess a unique structure with a single, elongated spur at the base of the flower. Trichogonia species are typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in shaded, moist environments. Their botanical features include a rhizomatous root system and strap-like leaves.

The flowers are usually white or pale in color, with a distinctive three-lobed lip that plays a role in pollination.
