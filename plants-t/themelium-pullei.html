Themelium pullei

Themelium pullei, commonly known as the silverleaf orchid, is a unique flowering plant native to the subtropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking silver-green foliage and delicate, fragrant blooms that typically appear in spring. The plant has a compact growth habit, making it suitable for small garden spaces or container cultivation. Its leaves are covered in a fine, waxy coating that helps reduce water loss in dry conditions.

Themelium pullei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for with proper drainage and sunlight.
