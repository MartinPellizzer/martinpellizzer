Tecticornia pergranulata subsp. queenslandica

Tecticornia pergranulata subsp.

queenslandica, commonly known as the Queensland sandbur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Australia. It is characterized by its hairy, cylindrical stems and small, spiny bracts that encase its seeds.

The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, appearing in clusters at the base of the plant.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
