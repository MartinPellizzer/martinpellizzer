Triplaris ramiflora

Triplaris ramiflora, commonly known as the branched triplaris, is a tropical plant native to parts of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows in humid, lowland forests. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves, which are usually pinnately arranged with elongated leaflets. Its flowers are small and clustered, often in dense inflorescences, and it produces woody fruits that contain several seeds.

Triplaris ramiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
