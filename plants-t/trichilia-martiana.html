Trichilia martiana

Trichilia martiana, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a tropical tree native to Africa and parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae, which includes mahogany and other valuable hardwoods. The plant is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and trifoliate leaves with three leaflets. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and typically yellow or greenish-yellow, arranged in panicles.

The fruit is a drupe, containing a single seed enclosed in a hard, woody capsule.
