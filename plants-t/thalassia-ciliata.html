Thalassia ciliata

Thalassia ciliata, commonly known as shoal grass, is a marine flowering plant belonging to the family Poaceae.

It is a seagrass species that thrives in shallow coastal waters, forming dense underwater meadows. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are typically bright green and can grow up to several meters in length. It plays a crucial role in stabilizing sediments and providing habitat for various marine organisms.

Thalassia ciliata is also important for carbon sequestration and supports biodiversity in tropical and subtropical marine environments.
