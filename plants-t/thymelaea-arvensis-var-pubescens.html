Thymelaea arvensis var. pubescens

Thymelaea arvensis var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy thymelaea, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Thymelaeaceae and is characterized by its small, greenish-yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name and help in identifying it in the field. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the genus Thymelaea.
