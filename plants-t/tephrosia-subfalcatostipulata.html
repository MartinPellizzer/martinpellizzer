Tephrosia subfalcatostipulata

Tephrosia subfalcatostipulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tephrosia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features trifoliate leaves with small, pointed leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. It is often found in open woodland and savanna habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and potential use in traditional medicine.
