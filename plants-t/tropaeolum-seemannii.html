Tropaeolum seemannii

Tropaeolum seemannii, commonly known as the everlasting rocket, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, peppercorn-like flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant produces small, edible seeds that are often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly peppery flavor, similar to that of watercress.

Tropaeolum seemannii thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often cultivated for both its ornamental and edible qualities.
