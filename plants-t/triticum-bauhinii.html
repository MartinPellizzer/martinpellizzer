Triticum bauhinii

Triticum bauhinii, commonly known as the wild wheat or Bauhin's wheat, is a species of grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences. The plant produces minute, pale blue or green flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

Triticum bauhinii is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
