Turnera ulmifolia var. acuta

Turnera ulmifolia var. acuta, commonly known as the acuteleaf turnera, is a species of flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Turneraceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
