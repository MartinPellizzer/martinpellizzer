Typha angustifolia f. inaequalis

Typha angustifolia f. inaequalis, commonly known as the narrowleaf cattail, is a variant of the common cattail species.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and a distinctive cylindrical flower spike. This plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and lakes. Its botanical features include a rhizomatous root system and a tall, flowering stem that produces fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds.

It plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and stabilizing soil.
