Triumfetta scandens

Triumfetta scandens, commonly known as the climbing false mallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a climbing shrub characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are often clustered in racemes. It thrives in warm climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it a common ornamental and medicinal plant in many parts of the world.
