Tephrosia subtriflora

Tephrosia subtriflora, commonly known as the three-flowered tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is typically shrub-like, with hairy stems and often grows in moist, well-drained soils.

It is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in agricultural systems.
