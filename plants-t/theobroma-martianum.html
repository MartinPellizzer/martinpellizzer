Theobroma martianum

Theobroma martianum, commonly known as the cocoa tree, is a tropical evergreen plant native to the Amazon basin.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The tree produces pods that contain the seeds used to make cocoa products. These seeds are rich in cocoa butter and theobromine, contributing to the plant's significance in both culinary and medicinal contexts.

Theobroma martianum thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil for optimal growth.
