Tamamschjania rhizomatica

Tamamschjania rhizomatica, commonly known as the creeping bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively and form dense colonies. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue and violet, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered with a fine pubescence, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Tamamschjania rhizomatica thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.
