Thymus × intercalatus

Thymus × intercalatus, commonly known as hybrid thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, narrow, and slightly hairy leaves that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant produces small, pink or violet flowers in clusters, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
