Tamarix longe-pedunculata

Tamarix longe-pedunculata, commonly known as the long-stalked tamarisk, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and small, scale-like leaves that reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces pink or red flowers in dense, elongated clusters, which are pollinated by insects. It has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in sandy or saline soils.

Tamarix longe-pedunculata is often used for soil stabilization and erosion control in degraded landscapes.
