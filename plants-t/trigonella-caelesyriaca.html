Trigonella caelesyriaca

Trigonella caelesyriaca, commonly known as the Syrian cowpea, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. Trigonella caelesyriaca is known for its adaptability to arid conditions, making it a valuable crop in semi-arid environments.

It also has historical significance in herbal medicine for its nutritional and medicinal properties.
