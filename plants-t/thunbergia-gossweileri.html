Thunbergia gossweileri

Thunbergia gossweileri, commonly known as the African bluebell, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant produces small, round berries that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Thunbergia gossweileri is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
