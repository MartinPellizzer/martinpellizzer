Turnera cuneiformis

Turnera cuneiformis, commonly known as the arrowleaf tree or Mexican turnera, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has compound leaves with three to five oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces clusters of small, fragrant yellow flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and the plant is often used in traditional medicine.

Turnera cuneiformis is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
