Tinus polycephala

Tinus polycephala, commonly known as the many-headed tree, is a unique plant species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its multiple stems that emerge from a single base, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, often followed by pale blue or purple berries. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough texture.

Tinus polycephala is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.
