Taraxacum desertorum

Taraxacum desertorum, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust root system. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are basal, rosette-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and produce wind-dispersed seeds.

Taraxacum desertorum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for desert wildlife.
