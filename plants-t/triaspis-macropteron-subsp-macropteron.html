Triaspis macropteron subsp. macropteron

Triaspis macropteron subsp.

macropteron, commonly known as the giant feather grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, feathery plumes that give it a distinctive appearance.

The plant typically grows in open grasslands and along riverbanks, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, while the inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in landscaping for its elegant, flowing foliage.
