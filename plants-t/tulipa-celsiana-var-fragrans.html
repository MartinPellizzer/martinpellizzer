Tulipa celsiana var. fragrans

Tulipa celsiana var. fragrans, commonly known as the fragrant crown imperial, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear clusters of vibrant orange or red flowers with a distinctive, crown-like shape. The plant produces a strong, sweet fragrance, which is particularly noticeable in the evening. Its leaves are long and grass-like, emerging from a bulbous base that stores nutrients for the growing season.

This variety is prized in gardens for its ornamental value and unique aromatic qualities.
