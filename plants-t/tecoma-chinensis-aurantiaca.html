Tecoma chinensis aurantiaca

Tecoma chinensis aurantiaca, commonly known as the golden trumpet tree, is a flowering plant native to China and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, pendulous yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has compound leaves with opposite arrangement and is typically deciduous, shedding its leaves during the dry season. Its ornamental value makes it popular in horticulture, though it can become invasive in some regions.

The tree prefers well-drained soil and full sun, thriving in tropical and subtropical climates.
