Taxus communis pendula-variegata

Taxus communis pendula-variegata, commonly known as the variegated weeping yew, is a striking evergreen shrub or small tree characterized by its cascading, drooping branches.

Its foliage consists of narrow, dark green leaves with white or yellow variegation, creating a visually appealing contrast. The plant produces small, red berries that are toxic to humans and animals, making it important to handle with care. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, acidic soil.

This ornamental species is valued for its unique shape and foliage, often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal.
