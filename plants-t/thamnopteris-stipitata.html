Thamnopteris stipitata

Thamnopteris stipitata, commonly known as the stipe fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Thamnopteridaceae and is characterized by its fronds that are typically bipinnate or tripinnate. The plant has a short, thick rhizome with a stipe that supports the fronds, which are often glossy and leathery in texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Thamnopteris stipitata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in horticulture.
