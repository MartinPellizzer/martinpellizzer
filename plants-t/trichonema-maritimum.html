Trichonema maritimum

Trichonema maritimum, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive blue or purple flower heads surrounded by spiny, leaf-like bracts. The plant typically grows in coastal areas, thriving in sandy or saline soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Trichonema maritimum is adapted to harsh maritime environments, making it a resilient and hardy species.
