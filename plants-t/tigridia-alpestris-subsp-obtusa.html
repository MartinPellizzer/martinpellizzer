Tigridia alpestris subsp. obtusa

Tigridia alpestris subsp.

obtusa, commonly known as the Sierra Madre lily, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Mexico. It features striking, showy flowers with bright red or orange petals and a distinctive spurred lip.

The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and has a short, stout stem that supports its vibrant blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its bold floral display.
