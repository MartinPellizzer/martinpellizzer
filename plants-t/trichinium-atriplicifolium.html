Trichinium atriplicifolium

Trichinium atriplicifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf trichinium, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, trifoliate leaves that resemble those of the hogweed genus. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in ecological and horticultural studies.
