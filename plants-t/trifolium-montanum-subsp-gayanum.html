Trifolium montanum subsp. gayanum

Trifolium montanum subsp.

gayanum, commonly known as the Gayan clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and produces small, pink to purple flowers in early summer.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of alpine flora.
