Trochomeria longipetala

Trochomeria longipetala, commonly known as the long-petioled trochomeria, is a flowering plant species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a hairy surface. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of white and pink, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
