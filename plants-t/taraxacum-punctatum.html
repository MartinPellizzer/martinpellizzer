Taraxacum punctatum

Taraxacum punctatum, commonly known as the spotted dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a basal rosette of leaves that are typically hairy and have a waxy coating. Its flowers are yellow and develop into white, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

The species is notable for its distinctive spotted leaves and is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides.
