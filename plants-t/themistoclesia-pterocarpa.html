Themistoclesia pterocarpa

Themistoclesia pterocarpa, commonly known as the African dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers and elongated, cylindrical fruiting structures. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
