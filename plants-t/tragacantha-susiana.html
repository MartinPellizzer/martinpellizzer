Tragacantha susiana

Tragacantha susiana, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southwestern Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody, shrub-like structure. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by distinctive, inflated pods that contain seeds. It is known for its ability to thrive in harsh, dry environments, making it an important species in arid ecosystems.

Its sap has been traditionally used in herbal medicine and as a natural gum.
