Thymus oxyodontus var. fruticosa

Thymus oxyodontus var. fruticosa, commonly known as creeping thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from pink to purple, blooming in early summer. The plant has woody stems that become more pronounced with age, and its leaves are narrow, slightly hairy, and aromatic. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary uses, particularly in Mediterranean gardens.
