Tynanthus fasciculatus

Tynanthus fasciculatus, commonly known as the scarlet pimpernel, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, showy yellow flowers that open in the morning and close by midday. The plant has delicate, trifoliate leaves and grows in a variety of habitats, including woodlands and open fields. It is an annual herb that thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

The botanical features of Tynanthus fasciculatus make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
