Thymus pallidus var. vulcanicus

Thymus pallidus var. vulcanicus, commonly known as the volcanic thyme, is a rare and specialized plant adapted to grow in extreme environments such as volcanic soils.

It possesses small, aromatic leaves that are typically gray-green in color, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions. The plant produces tiny, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to access moisture from deeper soil layers.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
