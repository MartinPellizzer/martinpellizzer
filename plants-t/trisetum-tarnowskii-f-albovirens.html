Trisetum tarnowskii f. albovirens

Trisetum tarnowskii f. albovirens, commonly known as the albovirens form of T. tarnowskii, is a grass species characterized by its slender, erect growth habit.

It typically features narrow, linear leaves with a pale green to bluish hue, distinguishing it from other forms of the species. The inflorescence is a panicle, composed of small, spikelet-like clusters that are usually pale or whitish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows or along the edges of water bodies.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
