Tibouchina scrobiculata var. membranifolia

Tibouchina scrobiculata var. membranifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf tibouchina, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
