Tormentilla nodosa

Tormentilla nodosa, commonly known as the nodding willow, is a shrub native to the northern regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, flexible stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that droop downward, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and stream banks, and is often found in temperate climates. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a soft, velvety texture.

The species is noted for its adaptability to waterlogged soils and its role in supporting local insect populations.
