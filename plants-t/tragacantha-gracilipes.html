Tragacantha gracilipes

Tragacantha gracilipes, commonly known as the slender tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, erect growth habit. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. Its seeds are encased in a fibrous pod, which is a key feature of the genus Tragacantha.

The plant is often cultivated for its gelatinous sap, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a thickening agent.
