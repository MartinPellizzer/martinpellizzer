Tadehagi alatum

Tadehagi alatum, commonly known as the winged bean, is a leguminous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves with wing-like structures along the edges. The plant produces pods that contain edible seeds and beans, which are rich in protein and nutrients. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often grown for both its nutritional value and ornamental appeal.

Tadehagi alatum is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for sustainable agriculture.
