Tetrastigma quadrangulum

Tetrastigma quadrangulum, commonly known as the four-angled grapevine, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its four-angled stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by yellowish berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
