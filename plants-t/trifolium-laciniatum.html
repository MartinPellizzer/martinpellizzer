Trifolium laciniatum

Trifolium laciniatum, commonly known as the cutleaf clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are divided into three leaflets, often with a serrated or lobed edge. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that are clustered in dense spherical heads. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows, pastures, and woodland edges.

Trifolium laciniatum is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
