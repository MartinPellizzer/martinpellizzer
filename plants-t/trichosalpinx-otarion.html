Trichosalpinx otarion

Trichosalpinx otarion, commonly known as the Otarian orchid, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, showy flowers that often feature a distinctive lip or labellum. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as forest understories, where it can thrive with adequate moisture and partial light. Its botanical features include slender stems and lance-shaped leaves, which are adapted to its tropical habitat.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in botanical gardens.
