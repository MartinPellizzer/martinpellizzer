Teucrium aragonense var. angustissimum

Teucrium aragonense var. angustissimum, commonly known as narrowleaf germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, thriving in sunny, well-drained environments. Its small, tubular flowers are usually purple or pink, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
