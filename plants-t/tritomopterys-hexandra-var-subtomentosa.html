Tritomopterys hexandra var. subtomentosa

Tritomopterys hexandra var. subtomentosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are densely covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink or red, often with a prominent central stigma. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic features.
