Trichonema purpurascens

Trichonema purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-stemmed sedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, triangular stems that often display a distinctive purplish hue. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets.

Trichonema purpurascens typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands or along stream banks.
