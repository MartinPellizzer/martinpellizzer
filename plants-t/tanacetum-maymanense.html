Tanacetum maymanense

Tanacetum maymanense, commonly known as the Mayman's tansy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are aromatic and often have a camphor-like scent, which is a common trait among members of the Tanacetum genus.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in the summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
