Ternatea schiedeana

Ternatea schiedeana, commonly known as the Ternatea tree, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters in height, characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Ternatea schiedeana is valued for its timber and is often found in lowland and hill forests.
