Tragia paraguariensis var. glabrescens

Tragia paraguariensis var. glabrescens, commonly known as the Paraguayan cupuaçu, is a shrub native to the Amazon basin.

It belongs to the family Malvaceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into the edible cupuaçu fruit, which is rich in nutrients. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

This variety is notable for its potential in both traditional medicine and as a source of natural ingredients.
