Taraxacum rubiginosum

Taraxacum rubiginosum, commonly known as the rusty dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and a yellow flower head that develops into a fluffy white seed head. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in grassy areas and disturbed soils. Its leaves are covered in fine, reddish-brown hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
