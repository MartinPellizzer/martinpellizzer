Thelypteris xinpingensis

Thelypteris xinpingensis, commonly known as the Xinping holly fern, is a species of fern native to China.

It belongs to the family Thelypteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest undergrowth, where it thrives in humid conditions. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are clustered on the undersides of its leaves.

Thelypteris xinpingensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and contributes to the biodiversity of its native ecosystem.
