Tephrosia oxygona var. obcordata

Tephrosia oxygona var. obcordata, commonly known as the heart-shaped tephrosia, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are often heart-shaped, giving it its common name, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

This species is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value and has potential uses in traditional medicine.
