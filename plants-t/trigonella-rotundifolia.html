Trigonella rotundifolia

Trigonella rotundifolia, commonly known as round-leaved fenugreek, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are rounded and often hairy, and its small yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, elongated seeds that are used in traditional medicine and cuisine. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and herbal applications.
