Teucrium bracteosum

Teucrium bracteosum, commonly known as the bracteate germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually blue to purple in color, arranged in dense spikes at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
