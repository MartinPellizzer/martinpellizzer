Taraxacum corvinum

Taraxacum corvinum, commonly known as the black-ribbed dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its hairy, dark green leaves with prominent veins that give it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces a single, showy yellow flower that develops into a spherical seed head, or pappus, which is easily dispersed by the wind. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

Taraxacum corvinum is often found in temperate regions of Europe and is considered a common weed in agricultural and urban environments.
