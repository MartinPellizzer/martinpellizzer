Tanacetum huronense var. typicum

Tanacetum huronense var. typicum, commonly known as Huron goldenhead, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Ontario, Canada.

It features basal rosettes of finely divided, gray-green leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in late summer. The plant is characterized by its aromatic foliage, which emits a sharp, camphor-like scent. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks.

Tanacetum huronense var. typicum is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its ornamental appeal in wetland gardens.
