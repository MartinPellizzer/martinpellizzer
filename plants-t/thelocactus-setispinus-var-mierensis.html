Thelocactus setispinus var. mierensis

Thelocactus setispinus var. mierensis, commonly known as the Mierensis thorn cactus, is a rare and distinctive cactus native to a small region in northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem and dense clusters of spines, which provide protection against herbivores and intense sunlight. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking contrast to its spiny structure. Its thick, fleshy stems are adapted to store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.
