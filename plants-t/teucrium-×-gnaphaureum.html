Teucrium × gnaphaureum

Teucrium × gnaphaureum, commonly known as hybrid germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Teucrium chamaedrys (creeping germander) and Teucrium polium (wild germander). The plant typically features square stems, opposite leaves with serrated edges, and small, tubular flowers that are usually blue or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or woodland areas.

This hybrid is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
