Tetrastigma potentilla

Tetrastigma potentilla, commonly known as the Chinese crossvine, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grape family (Vitaceae) and is characterized by its vigorous growth and twining habit. The plant features compound leaves with opposite leaflets and produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its berries are typically red or black when mature and are sometimes used in traditional medicine.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
