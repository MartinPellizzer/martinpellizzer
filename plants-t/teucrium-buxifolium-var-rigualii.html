Teucrium buxifolium var. rigualii

Teucrium buxifolium var. rigualii, commonly known as the Mexican germander, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a glossy appearance, often resembling those of boxwood.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its compact growth habit and extended blooming period.
