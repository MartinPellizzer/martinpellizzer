Taraxacum nuratavicum

Taraxacum nuratavicum, commonly known as the Nuratavicum dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust taproot system. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a single head.

The species is noted for its resilience to arid conditions and is of interest in botanical studies due to its limited distribution.
