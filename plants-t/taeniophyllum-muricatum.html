Taeniophyllum muricatum

Taeniophyllum muricatum, commonly known as the velvet tangerine, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and fragrant, orange-colored fruits. The plant produces small, round berries that are rich in vitamin C and have a sweet, tangy flavor. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.

The plant's botanical features make it a popular choice for both horticultural and culinary purposes.
