Tetrapogon macranthus f. geminatus

Tetrapogon macranthus f. geminatus, commonly known as the twin-leafed tetrapogon, is a botanical variant of the tetrapogon macranthus species.

It is characterized by its distinctive twin leaves, which are typically smaller and more elongated compared to the standard leaves of the species. This plant is native to specific regions in southern Africa, where it thrives in rocky and arid environments. Its growth habit is primarily herbaceous, with a compact form that allows it to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

The botanical features of this variant make it of interest to botanists studying plant adaptation and morphological variation.
