Turpinia paniculata

Turpinia paniculata, commonly known as the panicle flame tree, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy panicles of red or orange flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
