Trichopilia hymenantha

Trichopilia hymenantha, commonly known as the velvetleaf cycad, is a unique cycad species native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of feathery, pinnate leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces cones, with male and female cones typically occurring on separate plants, a feature common in cycads. Its leaves are thick and waxy, adapted to conserve moisture in arid environments.

Trichopilia hymenantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems as a habitat for various insects and small animals.
