Thalictrum peucedanifolium

Thalictrum peucedanifolium, commonly known as the peucedanifolium meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and delicate, fern-like foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers in early summer, which are typically arranged in panicles. Thalictrum peucedanifolium prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a suitable addition to woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value lies in its elegant appearance and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
