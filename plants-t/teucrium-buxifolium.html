Teucrium buxifolium

Teucrium buxifolium, commonly known as creeping germander, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically oval or elliptic in shape, with a dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale blue to violet flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its medicinal properties have been historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
