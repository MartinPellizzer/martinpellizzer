Thymus thracicus subsp. nikolovii

Thymus thracicus subsp.

nikolovii, commonly known as the Balkan thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It is characterized by its aromatic, small, gray-green leaves and pink to purple flowers that bloom in early summer.

This subspecies is typically found in rocky or limestone soils in open habitats such as grasslands and forest edges. It is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.

The plant's compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for rock gardens and herbal collections.
