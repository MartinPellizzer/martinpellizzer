Thalictrum minus var. sekimotoanum

Thalictrum minus var. sekimotoanum, commonly known as the Sekimoto's meadow rue, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Japan.

It features delicate, compound leaves that are typically arranged in a whorled pattern around the stem. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in early spring, which are clustered in racemes. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique addition to gardens and ecological studies.
