Thalia luschnathiana

Thalia luschnathiana, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, violet-blue petals and prominent, spiky yellow centers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and swampy areas. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that bloom in late summer.

Thalia luschnathiana is also valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for pollinators like bees and butterflies.
