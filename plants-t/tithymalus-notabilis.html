Tithymalus notabilis

Tithymalus notabilis, commonly known as the showy tithymalus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant features delicate, fragrant yellow flowers with white markings, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Tithymalus notabilis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
