Trichosanthes pubera var. fissisepala

Trichosanthes pubera var. fissisepala, commonly known as the fissisepal trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant produces a fleshy, edible fruit that is often used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered with fine, pubescent hairs.

This variant is notable for its unique floral structure, which includes deeply fissured sepals that give it its specific name.
