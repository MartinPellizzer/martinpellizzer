Tragacantha multiceps

Tragacantha multiceps, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia. The plant is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves, which are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. It produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by distinctive, inflated seed pods.

The plant is valued for its ability to thrive in harsh environments and for its historical use in traditional medicine.
