Tetragonolobus guttatus

Tetragonolobus guttatus, commonly known as the four-angled bean, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive quadrangular stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in warm, temperate climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional value.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both agricultural and ecological contexts.
