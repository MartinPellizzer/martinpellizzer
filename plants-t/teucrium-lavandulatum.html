Teucrium lavandulatum

Teucrium lavandulatum, commonly known as blue germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, slender stems and opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in the summer months. Its leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its distinctive fragrance and medicinal properties.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
