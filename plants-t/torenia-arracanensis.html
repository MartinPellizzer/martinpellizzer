Torenia arracanensis

Torenia arracanensis, commonly known as the flame violet, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with a contrasting blue or purple throat. The plant typically grows as an annual or short-lived perennial, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Torenia arracanensis thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
