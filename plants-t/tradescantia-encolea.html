Tradescantia encolea

Tradescantia encolea, commonly known as the red-veined nettle or red nettle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features striking, lance-shaped leaves with vivid red veins contrasting against its bright green foliage. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its unique leaf coloration and ease of cultivation.
