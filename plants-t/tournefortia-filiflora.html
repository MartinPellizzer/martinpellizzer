Tournefortia filiflora

Tournefortia filiflora, commonly known as the threadleaf champa, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub that typically grows up to 2 meters in height. The plant features opposite, narrow, and often hairy leaves that give it its common name. Its flowers are small, yellowish, and clustered in terminal panicles, producing a fragrant aroma.

Tournefortia filiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
