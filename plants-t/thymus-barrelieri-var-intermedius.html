Thymus barrelieri var. intermedius

Thymus barrelieri var. intermedius, commonly known as the Algerian thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage is often used in culinary and medicinal applications due to its essential oils.

This variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to withstand drought conditions.
