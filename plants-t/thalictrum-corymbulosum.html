Thalictrum corymbulosum

Thalictrum corymbulosum, commonly known as the meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it produces follicles that contain seeds.

Thalictrum corymbulosum is valued in gardens for its ornamental foliage and ecological benefits to pollinators.
