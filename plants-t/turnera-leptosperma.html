Turnera leptosperma

Turnera leptosperma, commonly known as the narrow-leaved turnera, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Turneraceae and is characterized by its opposite, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences, typically blooming during the rainy season. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in undergrowth of tropical forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
