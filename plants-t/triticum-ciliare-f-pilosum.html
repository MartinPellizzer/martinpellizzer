Triticum ciliare f. pilosum

Triticum ciliare f. pilosum, commonly known as the hairy awned wheat, is a botanical variant of the species Triticum ciliare.

It is characterized by its distinctive hairy awns on the glumes and lemma, which give it a unique appearance. This subspecies is typically found in temperate regions and is adapted to grow in a variety of soil types. The plant is an annual grass that produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Its botanical features make it of interest in both agricultural and ecological studies.
