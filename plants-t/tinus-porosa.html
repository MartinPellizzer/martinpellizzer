Tinus porosa

Tinus porosa, commonly known as the porous tinus, is a unique plant species native to specific regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hollow stems that give it a lightweight and somewhat fragile appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers well-drained environments. Its leaves are usually small and narrow, adapted to reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Despite its delicate look, Tinus porosa is resilient and can thrive in challenging ecological niches.
