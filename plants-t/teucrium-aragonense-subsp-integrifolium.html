Teucrium aragonense subsp. integrifolium

Teucrium aragonense subsp.

integrifolium, commonly known as the Aragon germander, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula. It features aromatic, lanceolate leaves with a grayish-green hue and a distinctive, slightly fuzzy texture.

The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers arranged in terminal clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

Its medicinal properties have been historically recognized in traditional herbal remedies.
