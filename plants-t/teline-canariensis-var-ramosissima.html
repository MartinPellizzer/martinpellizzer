Teline canariensis var. ramosissima

Teline canariensis var. ramosissima, commonly known as the Canary Island heath, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, branched growth habit and small, evergreen leaves. The plant produces clusters of pale blue or white flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
