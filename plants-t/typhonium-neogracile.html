Typhonium neogracile

Typhonium neogracile, commonly known as the red spider lily, is a herbaceous perennial native to Southeast Asia.

It features large, showy, orange-red flowers that resemble spiders, hence its common name. The plant has broad, arrow-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in swampy or marshy areas.

The plant is part of the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence structure.
