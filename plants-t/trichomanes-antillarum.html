Trichomanes antillarum

Trichomanes antillarum, commonly known as the Antillean maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically bright green and finely divided. This plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the undersides of its fronds.

Trichomanes antillarum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
