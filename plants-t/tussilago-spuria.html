Tussilago spuria

Tussilago spuria, commonly known as the lesser mullein, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its fuzzy, silvery-green leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and rocky areas. It is known for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal remedies for respiratory ailments.

The plant's structure and growth habits make it well adapted to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
