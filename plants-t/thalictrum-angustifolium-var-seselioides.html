Thalictrum angustifolium var. seselioides

Thalictrum angustifolium var. seselioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved meadow rue, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features slender, upright stems that bear compound leaves with finely divided, feathery foliage. The plant produces showy, yellowish-green to white flowers arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ecologically valuable species in its native range.
