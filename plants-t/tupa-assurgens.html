Tupa assurgens

Tupa assurgens, commonly known as the golden trumpet tree, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The tree has a straight, cylindrical trunk and can grow up to 20 meters tall, making it a striking feature in its natural habitat. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a smooth surface, while its bark is smooth and light-colored.

Tupa assurgens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
