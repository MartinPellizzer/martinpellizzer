Tetrapterys stephaniana

Tetrapterys stephaniana, commonly known as the Mexican shield-leaf tree, is a tropical tree native to Central America and northern South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive shield-shaped leaves, which are typically dark green and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by orange or red fruits containing several seeds. Tetrapterys stephaniana thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland tropical forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and traditional uses in local communities.
