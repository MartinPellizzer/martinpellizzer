Teucrium fruticans f. pallidum

Teucrium fruticans f. pallidum, commonly known as the pale flowering germander, is a subspecies of the germander plant native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pale blue to whitish flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, with a grayish-green hue. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
