Taraxacum bisectum

Taraxacum bisectum, commonly known as the split dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of spoon-shaped, deeply lobed leaves. The plant produces a single, hollow, hairless stem that rises above the foliage. Its flowers are yellow and form a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Europe and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.
