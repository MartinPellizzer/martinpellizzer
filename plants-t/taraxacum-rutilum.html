Taraxacum rutilum

Taraxacum rutilum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the dandelion family (Asteraceae).

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and is native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant has basal leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its stems are usually hairless. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including meadows and disturbed soils.

The species is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in some areas.
