Thunbergia togoensis

Thunbergia togoensis, commonly known as the Togo spiderflower, is a flowering plant native to West Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The plant produces vibrant, often bi-colored blooms in shades of purple, pink, and white, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Thunbergia togoensis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
