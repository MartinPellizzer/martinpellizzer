Teucrium murcicum subsp. hieronymi

Teucrium murcicum subsp.

hieronymi, commonly known as the Sicilian germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe, particularly Sicily. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun. Its small, tubular flowers are usually purple or blue, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate dry conditions.
