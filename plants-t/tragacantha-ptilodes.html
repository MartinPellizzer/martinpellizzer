Tragacantha ptilodes

Tragacantha ptilodes, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is notable for producing a valuable gum, tragacanth, which has been used historically in medicine and as a natural adhesive.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures.
