Trambis alpina

Trambis alpina, commonly known as the alpine trambis, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes. The plant features slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a hairy texture. It produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer.

Trambis alpina is adapted to cold climates and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.
