Trichochloa setosa

Trichochloa setosa, commonly known as the hairy grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It is characterized by its fine, hairy leaves and slender, wiry stems that often grow close to the ground. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or pale yellow, arranged in spike-like clusters. Trichochloa setosa thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland or marshy habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
