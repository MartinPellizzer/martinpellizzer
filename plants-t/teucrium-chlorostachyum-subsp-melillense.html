Teucrium chlorostachyum subsp. melillense

Teucrium chlorostachyum subsp.

melillense, commonly known as the Melillense germander, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula and North Africa. It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in open habitats such as scrublands and coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
