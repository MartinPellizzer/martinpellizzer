Tephrosia acaciifolia

Tephrosia acaciifolia, commonly known as the acacia-leaf tephrosia, is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which resemble those of acacia species, and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, and it is often found in disturbed soils and open woodland areas.

It is also valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
