Thymus ciliatus subsp. albiflorus

Thymus ciliatus subsp.

albiflorus, commonly known as the white-flowered thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features compact growth with dense, aromatic foliage that is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are typically gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and it thrives in well-drained, sunny soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
