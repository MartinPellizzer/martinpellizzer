Trisetaria chaudharyana

Trisetaria chaudharyana, commonly known as the Kashmir chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving in cool and moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Trisetaria chaudharyana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
