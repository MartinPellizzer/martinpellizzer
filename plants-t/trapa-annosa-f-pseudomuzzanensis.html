Trapa annosa f. pseudomuzzanensis

Trapa annosa f. pseudomuzzanensis, commonly known as the Chinese water chestnut, is a variant of the water chestnut plant native to East Asia.

It is a herbaceous aquatic plant characterized by its floating leaves and spiny, spherical fruits. The plant thrives in shallow, freshwater environments such as marshes and ponds. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically appearing in clusters.

The fruit, which develops underwater, is edible and has a nutty flavor, making it a valuable aquatic food resource.
