Tafalla artocarpus

Tafalla artocarpus, commonly known as the wild jackfruit, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

The plant produces a compound fruit that contains numerous seeds enclosed in a fibrous, aromatic pulp. The plant is often cultivated for its fruit, which is valued for its unique texture and flavor.
