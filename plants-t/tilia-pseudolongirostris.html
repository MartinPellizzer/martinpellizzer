Tilia pseudolongirostris

Tilia pseudolongirostris, commonly known as the narrow-beaked lime, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Tiliaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive narrow beak at the tip. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early summer, followed by spherical, woody fruits. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, often developing shallow fissures with age.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
