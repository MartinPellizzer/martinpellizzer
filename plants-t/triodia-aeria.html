Triodia aeria

Triodia aeria, commonly known as the slender spinifex, is a low-growing perennial grass found in arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It forms dense, mat-like clumps with wiry, spiny leaves that help reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively and establish stable populations in disturbed soils.

Triodia aeria plays a crucial role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for various desert fauna.
