Tragia teucriifolia

Tragia teucriifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf tragia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is typically shrub-like, with branched stems and hairy leaves that help reduce water loss. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Tragia teucriifolia is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
