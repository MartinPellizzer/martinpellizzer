Trigonella Polyceratia F. Prostrata

Prostrate trigonella, scientifically known as Trigonella polyceratia f. prostrata, is a unique variant of the trigonella genus native to arid and semi-arid regions. This low-growing plant is characterized by its sprawling habit and small, trifoliate leaves that help it conserve water in harsh environments. It thrives in sandy soils and is often found in rocky landscapes where competition for resources is minimal. Due to its resilience, it plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity. Despite its hardy nature, it remains a lesser-known yet ecologically significant species in desert ecosystems.
