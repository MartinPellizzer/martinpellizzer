Thyrsacanthus boliviensis

Thyrsacanthus boliviensis, commonly known as the Bolivian thyrsothecium, is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

It is native to South America, particularly Bolivia, and thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy yellow flowers arranged in a dense, spiky inflorescence. Its structure is adapted to withstand arid conditions, with minimal leaf surface to reduce water loss.

The plant is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological role in its native environment.
