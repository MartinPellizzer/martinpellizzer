Tinospora capillipes

Tinospora capillipes, commonly known as the creeping tinospora, is a climbing shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by fleshy, orange-red berries. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
