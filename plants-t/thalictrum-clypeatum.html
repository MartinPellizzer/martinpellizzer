Thalictrum clypeatum

Thalictrum clypeatum, commonly known as the meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are compound, with finely divided foliage that gives it a delicate appearance.

Thalictrum clypeatum is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits to pollinators.
