Trixis mexicana var. macradenia

Trixis mexicana var. macradenia, commonly known as the Mexican trixis, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has silver-gray foliage, which helps it conserve water in its harsh environment. It is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to full sun exposure.

Its unique morphology makes it a subject of interest in botanical studies and horticultural cultivation.
