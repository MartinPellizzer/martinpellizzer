Turnera hirsutissima

Turnera hirsutissima, commonly known as the hairy turnera, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are often heart-shaped with a waxy surface, and the flowers are usually pink or red, with five petals.

Turnera hirsutissima is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
