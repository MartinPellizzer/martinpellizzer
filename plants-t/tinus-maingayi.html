Tinus maingayi

Tinus maingayi, commonly known as the Cape chestnut, is a shrub or small tree native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces woody pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates with dry summers.

Tinus maingayi is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
