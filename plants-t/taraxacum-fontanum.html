Taraxacum fontanum

Taraxacum fontanum, commonly known as the Fontana dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has a rosette of basal leaves that are typically hairy and have a distinctive lobed shape. It produces a hollow, wind-dispersed seed head known as a pappus, which is typical of the genus Taraxacum.

This species is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.
