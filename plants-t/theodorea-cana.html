Theodorea cana

Theodorea cana, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is a deciduous vine that produces small, elliptical leaves with a smooth texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, loamy soil.

Theodorea cana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
