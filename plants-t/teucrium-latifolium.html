Teucrium latifolium

Teucrium latifolium, commonly known as broadleaf germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly rough texture. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from pale blue to violet, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its aromatic foliage and medicinal properties have made it a valued plant in traditional herbal medicine.
