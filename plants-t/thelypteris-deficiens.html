Thelypteris deficiens

Thelypteris deficiens, commonly known as the lace fern, is a species of fern native to various regions in the Americas.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are often covered in fine, hair-like structures. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the undersides of the fronds.

Thelypteris deficiens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
