Trifolium pratense f. galactiflorum

Trifolium pratense f. galactiflorum, commonly known as the golden clover, is a variant of white clover characterized by its yellowish flowers.

This plant is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grassy areas. It has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and its flowers are small and clustered in spherical heads. The botanical features include a taproot system and the ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and use in landscaping.
