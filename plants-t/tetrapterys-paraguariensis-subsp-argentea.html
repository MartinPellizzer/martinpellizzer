Tetrapterys paraguariensis subsp. argentea

Tetrapterys paraguariensis subsp.

argentea, commonly known as the silverleaf tamarillo, is a member of the Solanaceae family. It is native to South America, particularly found in countries like Paraguay and Argentina.

The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that have a distinctive silvery underside. Its fruits are typically yellow or orange, with a smooth, thin skin.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
