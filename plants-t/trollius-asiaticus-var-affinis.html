Trollius asiaticus var. affinis

Trollius asiaticus var. affinis, commonly known as the Asian globeflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Asia.

It features showy, yellow, globe-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
