Turnera cicatricosa

Turnera cicatricosa, commonly known as the scarlet turnera, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Turneraceae and is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in shaded or semi-shaded environments. Its leaves are usually ovate or elliptic with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The flowers are borne in clusters and bloom during the warm season, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
