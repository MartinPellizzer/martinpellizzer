Taraxacum leptolepis

Taraxacum leptolepis, commonly known as the slender dandelion, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf morphology. The plant features alternate, deeply lobed leaves that are typically hairless and have a bluish-green hue. Its flowers are yellow and form a single composite head, which develops into a fluffy seed head known as a pappus.

This species is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.
