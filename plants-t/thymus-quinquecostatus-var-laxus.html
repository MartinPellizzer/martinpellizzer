Thymus quinquecostatus var. laxus

Thymus quinquecostatus var. laxus, commonly known as the slender thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically gray-green in color, with a distinctive, slightly hairy texture. The plant produces tiny, pinkish-white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its medicinal properties have been historically valued for their antiseptic and aromatic qualities.
