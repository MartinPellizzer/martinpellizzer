Tsubaki

Tsubaki, commonly known as camellia, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its evergreen leaves and showy, camellia-like flowers that bloom in winter and spring. The plant belongs to the Theaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Tsubaki thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and requires partial shade to full sun.

Its seeds are used to produce tea, and the plant is also valued in traditional medicine.
