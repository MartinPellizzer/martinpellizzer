Trillium acuminatum

Trillium acuminatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf trillium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in rich, moist woodlands and is characterized by its three elliptic, dark green leaves. The plant produces a single, upright stem bearing a three-petaled white or pinkish flower that opens in spring. Its flowers are often followed by three small, red berries that mature in late summer.

Trillium acuminatum is a member of the Melanthiaceae family and is known for its elegant, delicate appearance.
