Talinum patens var. sarmentosum

Talinum patens var. sarmentosum, commonly known as the creeping talinum, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features sprawling, trailing stems that can reach several feet in length, often rooting at the nodes. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom in clusters along the stems. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a slightly hairy texture, and the plant is often cultivated as a ornamental ground cover.

Talinum patens var. sarmentosum thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
