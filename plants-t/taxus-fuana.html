Taxus fuana

Taxus fuana, commonly known as the Japanese yew, is a slow-growing evergreen conifer native to Japan.

It features dark green, needle-like leaves that are arranged in spirals around the stems. The plant produces small, reddish-purple berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, often found in forested areas.

Taxus fuana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
