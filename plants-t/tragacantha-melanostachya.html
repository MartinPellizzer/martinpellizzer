Tragacantha melanostachya

Tragacantha melanostachya, commonly known as the black-stalked tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, hairy stems and yellow flowers. The plant produces a gum that is used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive. It thrives in rocky and arid soils, often growing in open woodlands and scrublands.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
