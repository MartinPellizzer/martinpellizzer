Taraxacum proteranthium

Taraxacum proteranthium, commonly known as the narrowleaf dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and a hollow, leafless stem that supports a single, yellow flower head. The plant produces small, wind-dispersed seeds enclosed in a pappus, which aids in its propagation. It is native to Europe and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

Taraxacum proteranthium is a hardy perennial that thrives in temperate climates and is relatively easy to cultivate.
