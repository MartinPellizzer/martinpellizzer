Taraxacum hooftii

Taraxacum hooftii, commonly known as the Hooft's dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate leaves and a hollow, hairy stem. The plant produces a single, elongated flower head that develops into a distinctive spherical seed sphere with pappus structures for wind dispersal. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climatic conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and botanical gardens.
