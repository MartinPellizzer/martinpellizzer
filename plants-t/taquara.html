Taquara

Taquara, commonly known as *Schinopsis brasiliensis*, is a South American tree belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often bipinnate, and its distinctive, long, spiny pods. The plant is native to Brazil and other parts of South America, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. Taquara is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.

Additionally, it plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
