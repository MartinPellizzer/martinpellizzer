Talisia retusa

Talisia retusa, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and dense, aromatic foliage. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Talisia retusa is valued for its hard, dark wood, which has been historically used in the manufacture of musical instruments.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
