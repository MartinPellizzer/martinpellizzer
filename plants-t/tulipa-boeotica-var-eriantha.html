Tulipa boeotica var. eriantha

Tulipa boeotica var. eriantha, commonly known as the Persian tulip, is a striking perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Persia and Turkey.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep red, often with a distinctive red or purple eye. The plant features strap-like, narrow leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and historical cultivation in horticultural traditions.
