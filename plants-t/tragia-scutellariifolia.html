Tragia scutellariifolia

Tragia scutellariifolia, commonly known as the scarlet cupplant, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, cup-shaped yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. The plant has hairy, ovate leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in dry, open habitats.

Tragia scutellariifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
