Thrincia lusitanica

Thrincia lusitanica, commonly known as the Iberian broom, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe, particularly Portugal and Spain.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in scrubland and woodland edges. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
