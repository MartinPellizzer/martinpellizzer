Trichilia hirta

Trichilia hirta, commonly known as African sandalwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae, which includes species known for their aromatic wood. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered in panicles, and it produces woody capsules as fruit.

The tree is valued for its fragrant heartwood, which is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
