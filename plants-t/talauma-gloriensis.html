Talauma gloriensis

Talauma gloriensis, commonly known as the glory tree, is a tropical tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that are often a striking green with a metallic sheen. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, which bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds.

Talauma gloriensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
