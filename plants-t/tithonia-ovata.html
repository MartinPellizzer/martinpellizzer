Tithonia ovata

Tithonia ovata, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a herbaceous plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, bright orange to red flowers that resemble the sun, hence its common name. The plant has rough, hairy leaves that are typically heart-shaped or ovate in form. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant blooms.

Tithonia ovata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
