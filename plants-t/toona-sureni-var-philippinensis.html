Toona sureni var. philippinensis

Toona sureni var. philippinensis, commonly known as Philippine toona, is a deciduous tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Moraceae family, which includes figs and mulberries. The plant is characterized by its palmate leaves with five to seven lobes and smooth, grayish bark. It produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible, fig-like fruits.

This species is often found in lowland and montane forests, contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitat.
