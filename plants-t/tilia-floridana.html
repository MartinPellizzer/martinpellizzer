Tilia floridana

Tilia floridana, commonly known as the Florida basswood, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Tiliaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, white flowers that bloom in early summer. The tree produces small, round fruits called samaras, which are dispersed by wind. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, and it often grows in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks.

Tilia floridana is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
