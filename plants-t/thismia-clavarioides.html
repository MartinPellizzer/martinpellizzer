Thismia clavarioides

Thismia clavarioides, commonly known as the club-shaped Thismia, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a small, terrestrial herb that typically grows in shaded, moist forest understories. The plant produces unique, elongated, club-like flowers that are often pale green or yellowish in color. Its morphology resembles that of a fungal structure, which is a common trait among certain orchid species.

Thismia clavarioides is noted for its specialized pollination mechanisms and is of interest to botanists studying plant evolution and adaptation.
