Trichosporum pergracile

Trichosporum pergracile, commonly known as the slender hairgrass, is a low-growing perennial plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. This species is often found in moist, well-drained soils in temperate regions, particularly in shaded or semi-shaded environments. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread slowly and form dense mats, which can help prevent soil erosion.

Trichosporum pergracile is valued in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
