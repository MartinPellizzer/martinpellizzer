Tulipa urumiensis

Tulipa urumiensis, commonly known as the Urumie tulip, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and produces basal leaves that are narrow and grass-like. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, often thriving in rocky or gravelly habitats.

The Urumie tulip is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
