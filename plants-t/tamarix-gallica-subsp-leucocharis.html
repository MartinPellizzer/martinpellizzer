Tamarix gallica subsp. leucocharis

Tamarix gallica subsp.

leucocharis, commonly known as the white-flowered tamarisk, is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and small, scale-like leaves.

The plant produces dense clusters of white or pink flowers during the summer months, which are highly attractive to pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or saline environments.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes across temperate regions.
