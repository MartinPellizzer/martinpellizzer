Taraxacum prilipkoi

Taraxacum prilipkoi, commonly known as the Prilipko's dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and a yellow flower head that develops into a distinctive seed-filled sphere. Native to Central Asia, this plant thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. Its flowers bloom in the spring and early summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is notable for its resilience and ability to propagate through both sexual and asexual means.
