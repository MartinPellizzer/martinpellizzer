Teucrium chamaedrys var. angustifolium

Teucrium chamaedrys var. angustifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense spikes. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
