Tachigali rubiginosa

Tachigali rubiginosa, commonly known as the red-stained tachigali, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its reddish-brown bark and simple, alternate leaves that are often oval in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by woody pods containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
