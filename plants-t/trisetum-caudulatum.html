Trisetum caudulatum

Trisetum caudulatum, commonly known as the hairy beardgrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and fine, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and wet meadows. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of spikelets, which are often tinged with purple or reddish hues.

Trisetum caudulatum plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
