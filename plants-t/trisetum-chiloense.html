Trisetum chiloense

Trisetum chiloense, commonly known as Chilean wiregrass, is a perennial grass native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and grasslands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous texture, and it has a clumping growth pattern.

Trisetum chiloense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
