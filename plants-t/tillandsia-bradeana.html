Tillandsia bradeana

Tillandsia bradeana, commonly known as the Brade's air plant, is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is native to Brazil.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on the surface of other plants without deriving nutrients from them. The plant has a rosette-shaped structure with narrow, grass-like leaves that are covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help absorb moisture from the air. It produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale green.

This species is popular among air plant enthusiasts due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
