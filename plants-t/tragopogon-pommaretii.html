Tragopogon pommaretii

Tragopogon pommaretii, commonly known as the French dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, spoon-shaped leaves and produces yellowish composite flowers that develop into fluffy seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Tragopogon pommaretii is valued for its edible leaves and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
