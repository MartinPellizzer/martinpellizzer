Tisonia ficulnea

Tisonia ficulnea, commonly known as the Indian fig tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clustered, fig-like fruits. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm climates for its lush foliage. Its bark is rough and fissured, and it can grow up to 20 meters in height.

Tisonia ficulnea thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a humid environment to flourish.
