Tropaeolum infundibularum

Tropaeolum infundibularum, commonly known as the hooded nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive hood-shaped flowers, which are typically bright orange or yellow. The plant produces edible leaves and seeds, often used in culinary applications. It thrives in warm climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Tropaeolum infundibularum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract beneficial insects.
