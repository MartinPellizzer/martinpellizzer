Tainia latifolia subsp. elongata

Tainia latifolia subsp.

elongata, commonly known as the broadleaf tainia, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical regions of South America. It features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often ribbed and can reach up to 30 cm in length.

The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sun for optimal growth.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and vibrant floral display.
