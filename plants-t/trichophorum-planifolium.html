Trichophorum planifolium

Trichophorum planifolium, commonly known as the flatleaf sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its flat, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and wet meadows. It has a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread vegetatively.

Trichophorum planifolium is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
