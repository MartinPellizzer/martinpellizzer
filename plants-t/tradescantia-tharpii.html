Tradescantia tharpii

Tradescantia tharpii, commonly known as the silver sword or red-veined nettle, is a perennial flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with silver or red veins, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, arranged in loose clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden beds.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of care, Tradescantia tharpii is widely cultivated as a houseplant and in outdoor gardens.
