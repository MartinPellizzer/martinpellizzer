Triopterys rigida f. nana

Triopterys rigida f. nana, commonly known as the dwarf hardy begonia, is a compact, low-growing perennial plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It features glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments and requires well-draining, moist soil.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and ease of care in indoor and outdoor settings.
