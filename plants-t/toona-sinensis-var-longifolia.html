Toona sinensis var. longifolia

Toona sinensis var. longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf toona, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by elliptical, woody fruits containing smooth, oblong seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

This species is valued for its timber and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
