Teucrium polium subvar. faurei

Teucrium polium subvar. faurei, commonly known as the Sicilian germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly hairy leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in woodland or scrubland habitats. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, known for its medicinal and aromatic properties.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional use in herbal medicine.
