Thibaudia obovata

Thibaudia obovata, commonly known as the broad-leaf thibaudia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its obovate, glossy leaves that are often arranged in opposite pairs along the stem. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers, which bloom during the wet season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or near water sources.

Thibaudia obovata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
