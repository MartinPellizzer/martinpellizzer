Tragacantha janthina

Tragacantha janthina, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and long, slender pods. The plant is known for producing a resinous gum, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive. Its growth habit includes a shrub or small tree form, often found in arid and semi-arid environments.

The leaves are trifoliate, and the plant is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
