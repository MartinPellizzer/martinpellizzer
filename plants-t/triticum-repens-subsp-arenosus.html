Triticum repens subsp. arenosus

Triticum repens subsp.

arenosus, commonly known as beach grass, is a perennial grass species native to coastal regions. It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and is adapted to sandy and saline environments.

The plant produces small, spikelet-like inflorescences with pale green to brown flowers. Its rhizomes help stabilize dunes and prevent erosion.

It is an important component of coastal ecosystems, providing habitat for various organisms.
