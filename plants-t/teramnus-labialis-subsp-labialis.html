Teramnus labialis subsp. labialis

Teramnus labialis subsp.

labialis, commonly known as the common teramnus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters.

The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy and have a distinct, slightly serrated edge. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense spikes, and range in color from white to pale pink.

The species is characterized by its adaptability to a variety of soil types and its ability to thrive in both sunny and partially shaded environments.
