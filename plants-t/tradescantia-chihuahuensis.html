Tradescantia chihuahuensis

Tradescantia chihuahuensis, commonly known as the Chihuahua spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and features striking, lance-shaped leaves with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, depending on the variety. It is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.

Tradescantia chihuahuensis is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
