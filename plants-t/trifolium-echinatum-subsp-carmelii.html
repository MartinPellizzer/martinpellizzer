Trifolium echinatum subsp. carmelii

Trifolium echinatum subsp.

carmelii, commonly known as the Carmel clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to California. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal habitats and open woodlands.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
