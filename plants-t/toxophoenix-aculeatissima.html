Toxophoenix aculeatissima

Toxophoenix aculeatissima, commonly known as the spiny date palm, is a species of palm native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that are often covered in small, sharp spines. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible dates, although its fruits are typically less prominent than those of other date palms. It thrives in arid and semi-arid climates, making it well adapted to desert environments.

Due to its resilience, it is sometimes cultivated in regions with similar climatic conditions.
