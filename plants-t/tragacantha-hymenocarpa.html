Tragacantha hymenocarpa

Tragacantha hymenocarpa, commonly known as the Persian gum or Astragalus hymenocarpus, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces a distinctive, sticky resin known as tragacanth, which has been historically used in medicine and as a thickening agent. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in dry, sandy soils with full sun exposure.

Tragacantha hymenocarpa is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in semi-desert environments.
