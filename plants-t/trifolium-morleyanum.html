Trifolium morleyanum

Trifolium morleyanum, commonly known as Morley's clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each consisting of three oval to lance-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in dense heads, typical of the clover genus. It is native to southern Africa, where it thrives in grassy areas and rocky soils.

Trifolium morleyanum is valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a forage plant for livestock.
