Tigridia nanchititlensis

Tigridia nanchititlensis, commonly known as the Mexican rain lily, is a striking member of the Iridaceae family native to central Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with a distinctive black or purple eye at the center. The plant produces showy, spathaceous bracts that enclose the flowers, giving it an exotic appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Tigridia nanchititlensis is also known for its ability to bloom multiple times during the growing season, adding continuous color to the landscape.
