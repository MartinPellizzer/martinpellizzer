Taraxacum hypocraterimorphum

Taraxacum hypocraterimorphum, commonly known as the narrowleaf dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces a yellow flower head that develops into a white, fluffy seed head. It is often found in disturbed soils and open areas, thriving in temperate climates.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a pioneer species in ecological succession.
