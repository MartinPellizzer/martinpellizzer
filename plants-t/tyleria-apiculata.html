Tyleria apiculata

Tyleria apiculata, commonly known as the hairy tamarind, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Caesalpiniaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces small, woody pods that contain edible seeds, which are used in traditional cuisines.

Tyleria apiculata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its foliage and floral display.
