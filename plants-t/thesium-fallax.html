Thesium fallax

Thesium fallax, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pale blue or white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Thesium fallax is valued for its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
