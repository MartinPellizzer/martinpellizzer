Thymus dacicus

Thymus dacicus, commonly known as the Dacic thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and aromatic, often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Thymus dacicus thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rocky or alpine gardens.
