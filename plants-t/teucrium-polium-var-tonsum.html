Teucrium polium var. tonsum

Teucrium polium var. tonsum, commonly known as mountain germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves with a grayish-green hue, often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

Its medicinal properties have been historically valued for their anti-inflammatory and antiseptic qualities.
