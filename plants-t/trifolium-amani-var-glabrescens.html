Trifolium amani var. glabrescens

Trifolium amani var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smoothleaf clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for various ecological and agricultural settings.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in erosion control and as a forage crop for livestock.
