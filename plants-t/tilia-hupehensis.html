Tilia hupehensis

Tilia hupehensis, commonly known as the Chinese linden, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Tiliaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in early summer. The tree produces small, round fruits that contain seeds covered in fine, silky hairs. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also valued in traditional Chinese medicine.

The bark and leaves have been used for their medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antiseptic effects.
