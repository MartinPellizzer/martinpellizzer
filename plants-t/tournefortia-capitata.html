Tournefortia capitata

Tournefortia capitata, commonly known as the golden bell or mountain bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters at the ends of its branches. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or coastal areas.

Tournefortia capitata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
