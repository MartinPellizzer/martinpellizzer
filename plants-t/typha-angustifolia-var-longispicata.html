Typha angustifolia var. longispicata

Typha angustifolia var. longispicata, commonly known as narrowleaf cattail with long-spiked inflorescence, is a variant of the cattail plant native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and a distinctive, elongated spike of brown, fuzzy flowers. The plant thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often forming dense stands in marshes and along lake shores. Its botanical features include a rhizomatous root system that aids in stabilizing soil and filtering water.

This variant is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to improve water quality and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
