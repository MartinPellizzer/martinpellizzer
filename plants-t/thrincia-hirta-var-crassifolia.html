Thrincia hirta var. crassifolia

Thrincia hirta var. crassifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf thrincia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems, which give it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are broad and ovate, often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its common name. It produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color.

This plant is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun conditions.
