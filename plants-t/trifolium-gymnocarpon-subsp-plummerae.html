Trifolium gymnocarpon subsp. plummerae

Trifolium gymnocarpon subsp.

plummerae, commonly known as the narrow-leaved clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval to lanceolate leaflets arranged on short petioles.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

This subspecies is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.
