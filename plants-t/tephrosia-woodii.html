Tephrosia woodii

Tephrosia woodii, commonly known as the African senna, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom during the dry season. Its seeds are encased in long, slender pods that split open when mature.

Tephrosia woodii is known for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
