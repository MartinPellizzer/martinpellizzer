Thymus glaber

Thymus glaber, commonly known as narrowleaf thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, aromatic, evergreen leaves that are typically narrow and linear. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers in clusters during the summer months. It is often used in culinary applications and traditional medicine due to its essential oils.

Thymus glaber thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and herbal gardens.
