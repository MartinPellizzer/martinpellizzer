Trigonia nivea var. nivea

Trigonia nivea var. nivea, commonly known as the white-flowered trigonia, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub with lance-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its attractive appearance and relatively easy care requirements, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
