Trigonella caelesyriaca var. torulosa

Trigonella caelesyriaca var. torulosa, commonly known as the Syrian cumin, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, spherical seeds that are often used as a spice in traditional cuisines. Its botanical features include a hairy stem, yellow petals, and a distinctive pod-like fruit that splits open to release the seeds.

This variety is valued for its aromatic seeds and its role in both culinary and medicinal applications.
