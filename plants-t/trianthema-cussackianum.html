Trianthema cussackianum

Trianthema cussackianum, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry or physalis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, inflated calyx that encloses the fruit, resembling a small lantern. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange to red berries that are edible. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy areas.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
