Trigonella jemenensis

Trigonella jemenensis, commonly known as Yemen fenugreek, is a flowering plant native to Yemen and surrounding regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces pods that contain small, kidney-shaped seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and cuisine. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it an important species in desert ecosystems.

Trigonella jemenensis is also valued for its potential nutritional and medicinal properties.
