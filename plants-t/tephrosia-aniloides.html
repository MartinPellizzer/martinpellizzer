Tephrosia aniloides

Tephrosia aniloides, commonly known as blue tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, blue or violet flowers. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with numerous leaflets, and its roots are known to contain compounds with potential medicinal properties. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
