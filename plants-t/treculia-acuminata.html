Treculia acuminata

Treculia acuminata, commonly known as the African shea tree, is a tropical deciduous tree native to Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and smooth, gray bark. The tree produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers followed by spiny, spherical fruits. Its seeds are encased in a hard, oily shell and are used to produce shea butter, a valuable cosmetic and culinary ingredient.

The plant is also valued for its timber and ecological role in tropical forests.
