Trochocephalus palaestinus

Trochocephalus palaestinus, commonly known as the Palestinian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Israel and Palestine.

It is a herbaceous perennial with showy blue or violet flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has hairy stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with silver. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance in its native environment.
