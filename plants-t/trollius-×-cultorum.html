Trollius × cultorum

Trollius × cultorum, commonly known as the globeflower, is a hybrid perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, bright yellow, globe-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and features hairy, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The flowers are not only ornamental but also attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
