Trigonella polyceratia var. subpinnatifida

Trigonella polyceratia var. subpinnatifida, commonly known as the narrow-leaved fenugreek, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, branched stems and pinnately compound leaves with a delicate, feathery appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by elongated, cylindrical pods containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of water bodies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
