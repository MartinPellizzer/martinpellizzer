Trisetum ambiguum

Trisetum ambiguum, commonly known as the ambiguous trisetum, is a perennial grass species native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has slender, tufted shoots with fine, gray-green leaves that are often rolled when young. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle bearing small, spikelet-like flowers.

Trisetum ambiguum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
