Teucrium flavum subsp. gymnocalyx

Teucrium flavum subsp.

gymnocalyx, commonly known as the yellow germander, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It features slender, upright stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a slightly serrated margin.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically arranged in spikes at the ends of the branches. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its aromatic foliage and long blooming period contribute to its value in both ornamental and ecological gardens.
