Tillandsia aeranthos var. alba

Tillandsia aeranthos var. alba, commonly known as the white air plant, is a species of bromeliad native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on other plants without deriving nutrients from them. The plant is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help in absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air. It produces striking white or pale pink flowers that bloom from the center of the rosette.

This variety is popular among succulent and air plant enthusiasts due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
