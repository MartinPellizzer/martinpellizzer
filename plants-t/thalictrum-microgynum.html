Thalictrum microgynum

Thalictrum microgynum, commonly known as the small-fruit meadow rue, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features delicate, fern-like leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring. Its flowers are clustered in upright, branched inflorescences, and it is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Thalictrum microgynum is also valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for early-season pollinators.
