Tityrus jonquilla

Tityrus jonquilla, commonly known as the yellow crowfoot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant has a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers with four petals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and stream banks.

Tityrus jonquilla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant spring blooms.
