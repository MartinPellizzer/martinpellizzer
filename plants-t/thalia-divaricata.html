Thalia divaricata

Thalia divaricata, commonly known as the sea hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along shorelines. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually pink or red in color. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Thalia divaricata is adapted to salt spray and can thrive in brackish or saline environments.
