Trichinium parviflorum

Trichinium parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flower trichinium, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact stature and delicate, small white or pale blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its root system is fibrous and shallow. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
