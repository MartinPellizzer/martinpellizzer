Tragopogon leiocarpos

Tragopogon leiocarpos, commonly known as the white goat's beard, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a cluster of small flowers. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange ray florets surrounded by a dense ring of tubular disk florets. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Tragopogon leiocarpos is also known for its distinctive, curly, white bracts that persist throughout the growing season.
