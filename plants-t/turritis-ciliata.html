Turritis ciliata

Turritis ciliata, commonly known as the silky bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it its common name. Its flowers are small, bell-shaped, and usually violet or blue in color, arranged in loose clusters.

Turritis ciliata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded garden settings.
