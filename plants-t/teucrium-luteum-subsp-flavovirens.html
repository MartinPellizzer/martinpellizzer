Teucrium luteum subsp. flavovirens

Teucrium luteum subsp.

flavovirens, commonly known as the yellow germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open woodlands.

The plant features slender, branched stems and small, lance-shaped leaves with a grayish-green color. Its flowers are yellow, arranged in dense clusters, and bloom during the summer months.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
