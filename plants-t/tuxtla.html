Tuxtla

Tuxtla, commonly known as the Tuxtla Mountains or Tuxtla Forest, is a region in southern Mexico characterized by its rich botanical diversity.

The area features a mix of tropical and subtropical vegetation, including dense evergreen forests and a variety of flowering plants. It is home to numerous endemic species, many of which are found nowhere else in the world. The flora includes species such as the Tuxtla pine and various orchids that thrive in the region's moist, humid climate.

The unique microclimates of Tuxtla contribute to its ecological significance and botanical interest.
