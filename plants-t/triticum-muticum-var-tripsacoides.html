Triticum muticum var. tripsacoides

Triticum muticum var. tripsacoides, commonly known as the wild rye or creeping wild rye, is a grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brown florets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open habitats such as prairies and roadsides.

This species is valued for its role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife.
