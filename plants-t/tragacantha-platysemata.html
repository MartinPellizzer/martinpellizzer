Tragacantha platysemata

Tragacantha platysemata, commonly known as the flat-seeded tragacanth, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Tragacantha platysemata is also valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
