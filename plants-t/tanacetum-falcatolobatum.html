Tanacetum falcatolobatum

Tanacetum falcatolobatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tansy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its flowers are yellow, arranged in flat-topped clusters. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

Tanacetum falcatolobatum is known for its strong, camphor-like scent, which has historically been used in herbal remedies.
