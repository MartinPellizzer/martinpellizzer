Tessaranthium scabrum

Tessaranthium scabrum, commonly known as the rough-hairy bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, blooming from late spring to early summer. Tessaranthium scabrum thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its unique morphology and ornamental flowers make it a valued addition to rock gardens and alpine landscapes.
