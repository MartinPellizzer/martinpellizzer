Tragia stylaris var. leptophylla

Tragia stylaris var. leptophylla, commonly known as the slender-leafed tragia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its small, ovate leaves and yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and grasslands. It has a taproot system and produces small, dry fruits containing several seeds.

This species is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
