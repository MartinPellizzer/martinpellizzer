Tornabenea humilis

Tornabenea humilis, commonly known as the dwarf tornabenea, is a small, herbaceous plant native to specific regions of South America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually in dense inflorescences, and are typically greenish or white in color.

Tornabenea humilis is known for its low-growing habit and is often overlooked due to its modest appearance.
