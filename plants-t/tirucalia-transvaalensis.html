Tirucalia transvaalensis

Tirucalia transvaalensis, commonly known as the Transvaal glorybower, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has compound leaves with lance-shaped leaflets and produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Tirucalia transvaalensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
