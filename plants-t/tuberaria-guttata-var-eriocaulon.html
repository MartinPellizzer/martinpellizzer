Tuberaria guttata var. eriocaulon

Tuberaria guttata var. eriocaulon, commonly known as the hairy bladderwort, is a small aquatic plant characterized by its bladder-like structures used for capturing prey.

It typically grows in freshwater environments such as ponds and slow-moving streams. The plant has thin, branching stems with small, scale-like leaves. Its flowers are inconspicuous, consisting of tiny, greenish-white petals.

The varietal name *eriocaulon* refers to the hairy appearance of its stems, which are covered in fine, soft pubescence.
