Theriophonum zeylanicum

Theriophonum zeylanicum, commonly known as the Indian caper, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes and produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in umbels. The plant is known for its edible fruits, which are encased in a fleshy, red or orange aril. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and dry forests.

The Indian caper is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional medicinal uses in various cultures.
