Tanacetum polycephalum subsp. filipendulinum

Tanacetum polycephalum subsp.

filipendulinum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tansy, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small yellow flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters.

The plant contains volatile oils, including thymol and carvacrol, which contribute to its strong aromatic properties. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Due to its medicinal and aromatic uses, it has been historically valued in herbal remedies and perfumery.
