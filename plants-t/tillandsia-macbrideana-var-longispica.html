Tillandsia macbrideana var. longispica

Tillandsia macbrideana var. longispica, commonly known as the long-spurred tillandsia, is a species of air plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color and covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which aid in water absorption. The plant produces striking inflorescences with long, narrow bracts and showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white and pink. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and does not require soil, making it a popular choice for terrariums and indoor gardening.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
