Triodia plectrachnoides

Triodia plectrachnoides, commonly known as the spinifex grass, is a hardy, perennial grass species native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, fibrous growth and the presence of sharp, bristly awns on its seed heads. This plant is well adapted to harsh environmental conditions, including high temperatures and low rainfall. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, and it often forms extensive, low-lying vegetation cover.

Triodia plectrachnoides plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various desert fauna.
