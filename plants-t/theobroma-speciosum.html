Theobroma speciosum

Theobroma speciosum, commonly known as the chocolate tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon Basin.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The tree produces elongated pods that contain seeds used to make cocoa. Its botanical significance lies in its role as a source of natural cocoa and its ecological importance in rainforest ecosystems.

Theobroma speciosum is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in botanical gardens worldwide.
