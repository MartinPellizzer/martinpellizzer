Thalictrum montanum subsp. schultzii

Thalictrum montanum subsp.

schultzii, commonly known as the mountain meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of western North America. It features delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in open woodlands or meadows. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators. Thalictrum montanum subsp.

schultzii is also noted for its ability to thrive in cooler, high-altitude environments.
