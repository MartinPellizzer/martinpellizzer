Thevetia plumeriifolia

Thevetia plumeriifolia, commonly known as the yellow flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate or elliptic in shape. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds, which are often dispersed by wind.

Thevetia plumeriifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.
