Trollius kolonok

Trollius kolonok, commonly known as the yellow archangel, is a flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a basal rosette of broad, heart-shaped leaves and produces numerous small, yellow flowers on tall, slender stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Trollius kolonok is a perennial species that spreads through rhizomes and is relatively easy to cultivate in gardens.
