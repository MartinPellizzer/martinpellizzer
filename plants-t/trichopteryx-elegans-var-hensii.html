Trichopteryx elegans var. hensii

Trichopteryx elegans var. hensii, commonly known as the Hensii velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are velvety to the touch, and its small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and ecological research.
