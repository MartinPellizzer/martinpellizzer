Tecoma sinensis

Tecoma sinensis, commonly known as the yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, featuring a straight, often branched stem. The plant produces showy, tubular yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from spring to summer. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate, with a slightly hairy texture.

Tecoma sinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and as a street tree due to its resilience and adaptability.
