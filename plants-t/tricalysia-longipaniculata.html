Tricalysia longipaniculata

Tricalysia longipaniculata, commonly known as the long panicle legume, is a tropical shrub belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive long, branched inflorescences, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, often fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its fruits are pods that contain several seeds, and it is often found in moist, tropical habitats.

Tricalysia longipaniculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential ecological roles in nitrogen fixation.
