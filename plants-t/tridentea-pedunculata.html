Tridentea pedunculata

Tridentea pedunculata, commonly known as the three-toothed tamarisk, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Tamaricaceae and is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in saline or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually small and scale-like, while its stems are often woody and branched.
