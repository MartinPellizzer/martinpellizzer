Tylochilus flavus

Tylochilus flavus, commonly known as the yellow-stemmed orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces delicate, yellowish-green flowers with prominent, fleshy sepals and petals. It is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices.

Tylochilus flavus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in horticultural settings.
