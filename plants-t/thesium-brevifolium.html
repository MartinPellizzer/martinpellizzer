Thesium brevifolium

Thesium brevifolium, commonly known as the dwarf thesium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
