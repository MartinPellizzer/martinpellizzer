Toxicodendron toxicarum

Toxicodendron toxicarum, commonly known as the poison ivy plant, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged opposite each other on the stem. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers and reddish berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It contains urushiol, an oily resin that causes a severe skin rash upon contact.

Toxicodendron toxicarum is part of the Anacardiaceae family, which includes other plants with similar toxic properties.
