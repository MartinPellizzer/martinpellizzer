Trifolium magellanicum

Trifolium magellanicum, commonly known as the Magellanic clover, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a rosette. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Trifolium magellanicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological systems.
