Tanacetum tibeticum

Tanacetum tibeticum, commonly known as the Tibetan tansy, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Tibetan Plateau.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving in cool and dry conditions. It produces small, yellowish to white flowers that are arranged in flat-topped clusters.

Tanacetum tibeticum is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
