Taraxacum extimum

Taraxacum extimum, commonly known as the giant dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves and yellow flowers that develop into fluffy white seeds. Native to parts of Europe and Asia, this plant is often found in meadows and grasslands. It has a robust root system and can grow quite tall, sometimes reaching heights of over two meters.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
