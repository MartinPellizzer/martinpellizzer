Taraxacum dunenseforme

Taraxacum dunenseforme, commonly known as the Balkan dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are usually hairy and lanceolate in shape. It produces a single, elongated flower head that develops into a white, fluffy seed head.

Taraxacum dunenseforme is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.
