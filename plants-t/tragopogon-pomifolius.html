Tragopogon pomifolius

Tragopogon pomifolius, commonly known as the pineapple weed, is a flowering plant in the dandelion family.

It is characterized by its rosette of narrow, hairy leaves and a flowering head that resembles a pineapple, hence its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into fluffy, white seeds. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been naturalized in North America.

Tragopogon pomifolius is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats.
