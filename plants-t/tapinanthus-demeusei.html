Tapinanthus demeusei

Tapinanthus demeusei, commonly known as the parasitic dodder, is a hemiparasitic plant that relies on host plants for water and nutrients.

It belongs to the Orobanchaceae family and is characterized by its yellow, thread-like stems that twine around its hosts. The plant lacks true leaves and instead has scale-like structures that facilitate its parasitic lifestyle. It typically attaches to the stems or roots of other plants, such as grasses or shrubs, and can significantly weaken its host.

Due to its invasive nature, it is considered a weed in some regions where it grows.
