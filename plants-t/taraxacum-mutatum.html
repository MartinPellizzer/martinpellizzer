Taraxacum mutatum

Taraxacum mutatum, commonly known as the variable dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and heart-shaped basal leaves. The plant is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in meadows and grassy areas. It has a taproot system and produces a fluffy seed head that disperses seeds via the wind.

Its botanical features make it a common and easily identifiable plant in temperate regions.
