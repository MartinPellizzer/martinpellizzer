Thelypteris truncata

Thelypteris truncata, commonly known as the cut-leaf water fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive, deeply lobed fronds that give it a unique, almost lace-like appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine in various cultures.

Thelypteris truncata plays an important role in maintaining ecological balance by providing habitat and contributing to soil stability.
