Tamarix tarimensis

Tamarix tarimensis, commonly known as the Tarim tamarisk, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and clusters of pink or reddish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is well adapted to saline and alkaline soils, making it a common species in desert and steppe environments. Its roots are deep and extensive, allowing it to survive in areas with limited water availability.

Tamarix tarimensis plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for various desert wildlife.
