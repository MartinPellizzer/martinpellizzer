Tricholaena teneriffae

Tricholaena teneriffae, commonly known as the Canary Islands grass.

It is a perennial grass species native to the Canary Islands, characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems. The plant features narrow, blade-like leaves that are typically green and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Tricholaena teneriffae plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native environment and is of interest in ecological and botanical studies.
