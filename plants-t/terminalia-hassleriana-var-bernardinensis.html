Terminalia hassleriana var. bernardinensis

Terminalia hassleriana var. bernardinensis, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or Brazilian rosewood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, red drupes that are highly valued for their flavor and nutritional content. Its wood is prized for its density and rich color, making it a sought-after material in traditional craftsmanship and musical instrument making.

The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.
