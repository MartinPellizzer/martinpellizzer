Tricera fasciculata

Tricera fasciculata, commonly known as the clustered trichodesmid, is a member of the family Trichodesmaceae.

It is a marine green alga characterized by its filamentous growth habit and the presence of distinctive, branching structures. The plant forms dense, compact clusters, often found in shallow coastal waters. Its cells are typically elongated and contain chloroplasts that contribute to its photosynthetic activity.

Tricera fasciculata plays a role in coastal ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various marine organisms.
