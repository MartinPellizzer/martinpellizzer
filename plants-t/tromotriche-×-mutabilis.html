Tromotriche × mutabilis

Tromotriche × mutabilis, commonly known as the ever-changing dandelion, is a hybrid plant that exhibits remarkable variability in its appearance.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow flowers that change color as they age, transitioning from bright yellow to orange and eventually to a reddish hue. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas and is native to South America. It has a basal rosette of leaves and produces a single, tall flowering stem.

Tromotriche × mutabilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique visual transformation.
