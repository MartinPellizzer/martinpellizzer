Terebinthus vulgaris

Terebinthus vulgaris, commonly known as the common terebinth, is a deciduous tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and alternate, simple leaves. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by spherical, woody fruits containing three seeds. The plant is adapted to dry, rocky soils and thrives in temperate climates.
