Tritaxis muricata

Tritaxis muricata, commonly known as the spiny mallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough stems and palmate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Tritaxis muricata thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

Its unique appearance and ecological benefits make it a notable addition to botanical gardens and natural habitats.
