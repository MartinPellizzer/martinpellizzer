Tragacantha porphyracma

Tragacantha porphyracma, commonly known as the red-flowered tragacanth, is a species of flowering plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive red or purple flowers. The plant produces a sticky, resinous substance known as tragacanth, which has been used historically in various industrial and medicinal applications. It typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

Its botanical features include compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.
