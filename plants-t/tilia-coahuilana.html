Tilia coahuilana

Tilia coahuilana, commonly known as the Coahuila linden, is a rare and endemic species native to the Chihuahuan Desert in northern Mexico.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows in rocky soils and dry habitats. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a smooth texture and a pale green upper surface. Its flowers are fragrant and clustered in drooping inflorescences, attracting pollinators such as bees and moths.

Tilia coahuilana plays an important ecological role in its native environment, providing habitat and food for various desert species.
