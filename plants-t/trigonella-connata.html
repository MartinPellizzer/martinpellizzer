Trigonella connata

Trigonella connata, commonly known as the Armenian bitter vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.
