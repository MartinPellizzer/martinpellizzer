Tamarix florida var. rosea

Tamarix florida var. rosea, commonly known as the rose-flowered tamarisk, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and its showy, pink to rose-colored flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, woody capsules that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are scale-like and gray-green, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Tamarix florida var. rosea is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping in arid environments.
