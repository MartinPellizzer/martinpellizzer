Tricalysia patentipilis

Tricalysia patentipilis, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, featuring a straight trunk and a broad, umbrella-like canopy. The leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy, red or orange flowers bloom in clusters during the dry season, attracting various pollinators.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in warm climates for its vibrant floral display.
