Tetrastigma erubescens var. monophyllum

Tetrastigma erubescens var. monophyllum, commonly known as the red-veined grapevine, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with three to five leaflets, often displaying a striking red or pink venation. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically followed by small, round berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
