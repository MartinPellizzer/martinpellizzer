Tanacetum polycephalum subsp. azerbaidjanicum

Tanacetum polycephalum subsp.

azerbaidjanicum, commonly known as the Azeri tansy, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region. It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small yellow flowers arranged in button-like clusters.

The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its aromatic properties.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and valuable species in its native habitat.
