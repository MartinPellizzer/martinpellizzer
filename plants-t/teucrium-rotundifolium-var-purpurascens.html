Teucrium rotundifolium var. purpurascens

Teucrium rotundifolium var. purpurascens, commonly known as purple roundleaf germander, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features aromatic, hairy leaves that are rounded or heart-shaped, with a distinctive purple hue on the underside. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open woods and along stream banks.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a valuable addition to native gardens.
