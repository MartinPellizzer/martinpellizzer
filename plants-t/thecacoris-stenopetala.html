Thecacoris stenopetala

Thecacoris stenopetala, commonly known as the narrow-petaled orchid, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, tubular flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is adapted to cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its flowers are often borne on short, erect stalks.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
