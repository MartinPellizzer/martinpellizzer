Triodia trinerviglumis

Triodia trinerviglumis, commonly known as the slender spinifex, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that are often covered in fine, bristly hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing minute, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, adapted to conserve water in harsh environments.

Triodia trinerviglumis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for native fauna.
