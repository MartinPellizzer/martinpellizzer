Tripogon africanus

Tripogon africanus, commonly known as the African grass.

It is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa, characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry leaves. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences that are often pale green to brown in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is typically found in grasslands and savanna regions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used for erosion control and as a forage plant for livestock.
