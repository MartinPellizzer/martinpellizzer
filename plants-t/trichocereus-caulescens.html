Trichocereus caulescens

Trichocereus caulescens, commonly known as the "Candelabra Cactus," is a species native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a tall, columnar cactus with multiple stems that branch out to form a tree-like structure. The plant features ribbed stems covered in white to yellow spines, and it produces striking white or yellow flowers during the warmer months. Trichocereus caulescens is adapted to high-altitude environments and requires well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

It is also known for its potential use in traditional medicine and its role in the cultivation of certain psychoactive compounds.
