Tournefortia stenophylla

Tournefortia stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tournefortia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal or saline environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are narrow and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid climates.

Tournefortia stenophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
