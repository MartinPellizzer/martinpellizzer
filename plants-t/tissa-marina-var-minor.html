Tissa marina var. minor

Tissa marina var. minor, commonly known as the coastal sea holly, is a variant of the sea holly species native to coastal regions.

It is characterized by its salt-tolerant nature, allowing it to thrive in sandy or gravelly soils near the shoreline. The plant features blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer, adding visual interest to coastal landscapes. Its leaves are typically narrow and slightly succulent, adapted to retain moisture in arid environments.

Tissa marina var. minor is often used in coastal gardening for its hardiness and aesthetic value.
