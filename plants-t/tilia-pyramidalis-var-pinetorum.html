Tilia pyramidalis var. pinetorum

Tilia pyramidalis var. pinetorum, commonly known as the pine-leaf linden, is a variety of the small-leaf linden.

It is characterized by its narrow, upright growth habit and needle-like leaves that resemble those of pine trees. The plant produces small, fragrant yellowish-white flowers in early summer, which are clustered in drooping inflorescences. Its leaves are typically dark green and glossy, with a slightly serrated margin.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
