Tragopogon stroterocarpus

Tragopogon stroterocarpus, commonly known as the Stroterocarpus thistle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, bristly leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in the summer months. Native to the western United States, this plant thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. The plant's structure includes a basal rosette of leaves and a flowering stem that rises above the foliage.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas.
