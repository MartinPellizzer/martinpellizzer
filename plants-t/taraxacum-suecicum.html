Taraxacum suecicum

Taraxacum suecicum, commonly known as Swedish dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves. The plant produces a flowering stem that bears a single, yellow, composite flower head. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Its flowers develop into fluffy, white seeds that are easily dispersed by the wind.
