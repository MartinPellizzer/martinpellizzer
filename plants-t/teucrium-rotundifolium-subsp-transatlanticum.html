Teucrium rotundifolium subsp. transatlanticum

Teucrium rotundifolium subsp.

transatlanticum, commonly known as the Transatlantic germander, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States. It features upright stems with opposite, rounded leaves that are typically gray-green in color.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of purple or blue, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in dry, rocky soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in challenging garden conditions.
