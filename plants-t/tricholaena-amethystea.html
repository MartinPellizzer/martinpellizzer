Tricholaena amethystea

Tricholaena amethystea, commonly known as the purple pandanus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the family Pandanaceae and is characterized by its rosette-like arrangement of sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or green, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and as a source of natural fibers.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid, coastal environments.
