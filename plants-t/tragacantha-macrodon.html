Tragacantha macrodon

Tragacantha macrodon, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and woody, thorny stems. The plant produces a valuable gum, tragacanth, which has been used historically in medicine and as a sizing agent in textiles. It thrives in arid and semi-arid climates, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
