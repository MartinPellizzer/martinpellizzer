Tetramolopium rockii var. calcisabulorum

Tetramolopium rockii var. calcisabulorum, commonly known as the coastal tetramolopium, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern California.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and hairy, glandular leaves. The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal scrub habitats. It is adapted to withstand salt spray and strong winds typical of its coastal environment.

This variety is distinguished by its more compact growth form compared to other tetramolopium species.
