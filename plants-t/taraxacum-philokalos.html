Taraxacum philokalos

Taraxacum philokalos, commonly known as the Greek dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive foliage. The plant features basal leaves that are broadly lanceolate with toothed margins, and it produces a bright yellow flower head that develops into a white, fluffy seed head. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
