Tarenna macroptera

Tarenna macroptera, commonly known as the large-leaved tarenna, is a climbing shrub native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, simple, and often pubescent leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by elliptical or ovoid berries. Its leaves are typically large, measuring up to 15 cm in length, with a leathery texture and a prominent midrib.

Tarenna macroptera is valued in traditional medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
