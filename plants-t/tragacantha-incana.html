Tragacantha incana

Tragacantha incana, commonly known as the white tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial shrub that produces yellow flowers and has a distinctive, branched stem covered in fine, white hairs. The plant is known for its latex-rich sap, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural gum. Its leaves are narrow and grayish-green, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Tragacantha incana is often found in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments.
