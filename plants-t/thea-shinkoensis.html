Thea shinkoensis

Thea shinkoensis, commonly known as the Shinko tea plant, is a species native to Japan.

It belongs to the Theaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are used in the production of specialty teas, valued for their unique flavor profiles.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and culturally significant species.
