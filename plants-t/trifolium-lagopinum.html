Trifolium lagopinum

Trifolium lagopinum, commonly known as purple mountain clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets, and its small, purple-pink flowers that bloom in early spring. This plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. It has a creeping growth habit and produces small, three-lobed pods that contain several seeds.

Trifolium lagopinum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for ecological restoration projects.
