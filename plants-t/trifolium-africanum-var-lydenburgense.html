Trifolium africanum var. lydenburgense

Trifolium africanum var. lydenburgense, commonly known as Lydenburg clover, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that are clustered in spherical heads, typical of the legume family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in ecological restoration projects.
