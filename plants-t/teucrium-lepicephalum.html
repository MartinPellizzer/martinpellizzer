Teucrium lepicephalum

Teucrium lepicephalum, commonly known as narrow-headed germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of pale blue to violet flowers. It is often found in grasslands, woodlands, and along roadsides.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in herbal medicine and as a ornamental plant.
