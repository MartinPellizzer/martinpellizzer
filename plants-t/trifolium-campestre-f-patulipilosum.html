Trifolium campestre f. patulipilosum

Trifolium campestre f. patulipilosum, commonly known as the broad-leaved clover, is a botanical variant of the common clover species.

It is characterized by its larger, more rounded leaves compared to the typical trifoliate structure. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and grassy areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological systems.
