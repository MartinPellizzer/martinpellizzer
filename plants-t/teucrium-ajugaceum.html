Teucrium ajugaceum

Teucrium ajugaceum, commonly known as narrowleaf germander, is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, opposite leaves that are typically narrow and lance-shaped. The plant produces spikes of blue to purple flowers, which bloom in summer and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Teucrium ajugaceum is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
