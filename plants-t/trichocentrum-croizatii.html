Trichocentrum croizatii

Trichocentrum croizatii, commonly known as the Croizat's orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous inflorescences that bear clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, with long, narrow leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white trichomes. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining potting media for optimal growth.

Trichocentrum croizatii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and unique floral display.
