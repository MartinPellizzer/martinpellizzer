Tournefortia gigantifolia

Tournefortia gigantifolia, commonly known as the giant tournefortia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal areas and is well adapted to saline and humid conditions. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that release seeds when mature.

Tournefortia gigantifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
