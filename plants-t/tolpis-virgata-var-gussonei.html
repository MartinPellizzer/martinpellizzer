Tolpis virgata var. gussonei

Tolpis virgata var. gussonei, commonly known as the Gussone's thornveld grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. The plant features wiry, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a compact, spike-like cluster of small, two-lipped flowers.

This variety is adapted to arid and semi-arid conditions, showing resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.
