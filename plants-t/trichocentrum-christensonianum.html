Trichocentrum christensonianum

Trichocentrum christensonianum, commonly known as the Christenson's spider orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, slender stems and clusters of small, pale green or white flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are narrow and strap-like, often arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem.

This orchid is valued for its unique appearance and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
