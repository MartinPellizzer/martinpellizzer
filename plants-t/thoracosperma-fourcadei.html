Thoracosperma fourcadei

Thoracosperma fourcadei, commonly known as the Fourcade's mangrove, is a rare and specialized mangrove species native to the coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique ability to thrive in brackish water environments, where it plays a crucial role in stabilizing shorelines and supporting marine biodiversity. The plant has a robust root system that helps filter sediments and improve water quality. Its leaves are typically broad and waxy, adapted to reduce water loss in saline conditions.

Thoracosperma fourcadei is also notable for its small, inconspicuous flowers and fruit, which are important for its reproductive cycle in tidal ecosystems.
