Trifolium stolzii

Trifolium stolzii, commonly known as Stolz's clover, is a flowering plant species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from spring to early summer. Its trifoliate leaves are composed of three oval to lance-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Trifolium stolzii is often found in arid and semi-arid environments, such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
