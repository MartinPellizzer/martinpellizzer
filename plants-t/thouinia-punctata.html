Thouinia punctata

Thouinia punctata, commonly known as the spotted lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It features upright, slender stems that bear lance-shaped leaves with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink with a distinctive yellow or orange throat. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Thouinia punctata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and naturalized areas.
