Tricholaena setacea

Tricholaena setacea, commonly known as the hairy palm or false palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by dark blue, fleshy berries. Tricholaena setacea is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.

It plays a role in local ecosystems and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
