Tulipa aristata

Tulipa aristata, commonly known as the Sierra Valley tulip, is a species native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is a perennial herb that produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of white, yellow, and orange. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and high-altitude environments, often in alpine meadows. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is well adapted to cold climates.

The tulip is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
