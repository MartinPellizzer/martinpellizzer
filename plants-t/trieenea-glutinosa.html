Trieenea glutinosa

Trieenea glutinosa, commonly known as the sticky leaf tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, ovate leaves that exude a sticky substance when damaged. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The sticky resin produced by the plant has also been utilized in various local applications.
