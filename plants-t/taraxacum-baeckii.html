Taraxacum baeckii

Taraxacum baeckii, commonly known as the Baltic dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous root system. The plant features a rosette of basal, spoon-shaped leaves that are hairy and have a waxy coating. Its flowering stems bear a single, yellow composite flower that develops into a sphere of parachute-like seeds.

Taraxacum baeckii is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a common weed in agricultural and urban environments.
