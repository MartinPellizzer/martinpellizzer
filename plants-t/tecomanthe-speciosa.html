Tecomanthe speciosa

Tecomanthe speciosa, commonly known as the showy moon vine, is a climbing plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, waxy, white to pink flowers that resemble moons, which bloom in clusters. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces small, red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Tecomanthe speciosa thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
