Thymus allionii

Thymus allionii, commonly known as Allion's thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant produces pink or purple flowers in spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its compact growth habit and fragrant foliage make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological gardens.
