Triteleia × versicolor

Triteleia × versicolor, commonly known as the hybrid California lily, is a perennial bulbous plant native to California.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that come in shades of pink, white, and purple. The plant typically grows to a height of 12 to 24 inches and produces showy, bell-shaped blooms in early spring. Its foliage consists of strap-like leaves that are broad and grass-like, often appearing after the flowers have faded.

Triteleia × versicolor is well-suited for rock gardens and shady borders, thriving in well-drained soil and partial to full sun.
