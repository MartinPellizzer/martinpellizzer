Tinus lobbiana

Tinus lobbiana, commonly known as the Natal grass or Natal red grass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in grasslands and coastal areas, characterized by its tall, erect stems and dense, reddish-brown inflorescences. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are often visited by bees and other pollinators. Its fibrous roots make it useful for erosion control and soil stabilization.

Tinus lobbiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
