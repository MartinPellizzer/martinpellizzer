Tetranthera laurifolia var. multiflora

Tetranthera laurifolia var. multiflora, commonly known as the multiflora laurel, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, which is glossy and dark green, and its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, red drupes that are often eaten by birds and small mammals. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
