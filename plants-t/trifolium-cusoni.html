Trifolium cusoni

Trifolium cusoni, commonly known as the Corsican clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight.

Trifolium cusoni is often found in grasslands and along coastal areas, where it contributes to soil stabilization and biodiversity.
