Thymus glabrescens

Thymus glabrescens, commonly known as narrowleaf thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically gray-green in color and covered with fine hairs. The plant produces tiny white or pink flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its hardy nature and fragrant properties, it is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
