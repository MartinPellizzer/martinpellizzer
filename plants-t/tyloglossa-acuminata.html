Tyloglossa acuminata

Tyloglossa acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-tipped orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, showy flowers with a distinctive coloration pattern, often in shades of yellow and orange. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, typically growing in shaded, humid environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts for its beauty and unique appearance.
