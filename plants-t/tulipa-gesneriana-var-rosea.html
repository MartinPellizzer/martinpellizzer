Tulipa gesneriana var. rosea

Tulipa gesneriana var. rosea, commonly known as the Darwin tulip, is a hybrid species within the genus Tulipa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from red to purple, with a distinctive orange or yellow base. The plant is native to Central Asia and has been cultivated for centuries for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often blooming in spring.

The bulbs are perennial, and the plant is known for its robust growth and long-lasting blooms.
