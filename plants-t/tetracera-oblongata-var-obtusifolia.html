Tetracera oblongata var. obtusifolia

Tetracera oblongata var. obtusifolia, commonly known as the blunt-leaf tetracera, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in cyathia, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its bark and leaves have been traditionally used in herbal medicine for their potential medicinal properties.
