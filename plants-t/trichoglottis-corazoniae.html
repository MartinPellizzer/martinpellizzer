Trichoglottis corazoniae

Trichoglottis corazoniae, commonly known as the Corazonia orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, strap-like leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers with yellow and pink markings. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining substrates for optimal growth. It is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for orchid enthusiasts.

Trichoglottis corazoniae is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its role in supporting local biodiversity.
