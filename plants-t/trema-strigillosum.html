Trema strigillosum

Trema strigillosum, commonly known as the roughleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brown flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is commonly found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Trema strigillosum plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
