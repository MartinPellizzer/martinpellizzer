Thymus × faurei

Thymus × faurei, commonly known as French thyme, is a hybrid perennial herb belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and aromatic, gray-green foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Its essential oils, rich in thymol and carvacrol, contribute to its use in culinary and medicinal applications.
