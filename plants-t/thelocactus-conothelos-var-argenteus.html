Thelocactus conothelos var. argenteus

Thelocactus conothelos var. argenteus, commonly known as the silver-spined cactus, is a unique cactus species native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are covered in a dense layer of white, waxy hair, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically short-lived but striking. Its succulent structure allows it to store water efficiently, making it well adapted to its harsh environment.

The varietal name "argenteus" refers to its distinctive silvery coating, which helps reflect sunlight and reduce water loss.
