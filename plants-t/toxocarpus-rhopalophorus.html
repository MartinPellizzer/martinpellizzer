Toxocarpus rhopalophorus

Toxocarpus rhopalophorus, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features showy yellow ray florets surrounded by tight, spiny disk florets, which contribute to its ornamental appeal. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks.

The leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and often have a reddish or purple tinge on the underside.
