Tapirira fasciculata

Tapirira fasciculata, commonly known as the Brazilian fern or fern tree, is a tropical evergreen plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its dense foliage and woody structure. The plant features compound leaves with a feathery appearance, and its roots are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
