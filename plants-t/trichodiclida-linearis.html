Trichodiclida linearis

Trichodiclida linearis, commonly known as the linear leaf fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along its stem. The plant thrives in shallow, freshwater environments and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of ponds. Its fronds are usually delicate and can vary in length depending on the growing conditions.

Trichodiclida linearis plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
