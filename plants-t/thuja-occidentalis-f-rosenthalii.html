Thuja occidentalis f. rosenthalii

Thuja occidentalis f. rosenthalii, commonly known as the Rosenthal's arborvitae, is a cultivar of the eastern white cedar.

It is characterized by its dense, conical shape and dark green foliage that remains vibrant throughout the growing season. The plant is native to North America and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to various garden settings.

This cultivar is also valued for its resistance to pests and diseases, contributing to its popularity among gardeners.
