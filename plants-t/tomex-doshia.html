Tomex doshia

Tomex doshia, commonly known as the Japanese sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and reeds. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, showcasing its adaptability to moist soil conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive triangular cross-section typical of sedges.

Tomex doshia produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are characteristic of the Cyperaceae family.
