Thalictrum minus subsp. caffrum

Thalictrum minus subsp.

caffrum, commonly known as the African meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa. It features delicate, fern-like leaves and produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring.

The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a fibrous root system and is characterized by its slender stems and finely divided foliage.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
