Teagueia phasmida

Teagueia phasmida, commonly known as the giant orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded forest environments. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native habitat.

Teagueia phasmida is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or botanical gardens.
