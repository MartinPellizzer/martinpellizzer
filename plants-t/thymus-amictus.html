Thymus amictus

Thymus amictus, commonly known as the narrowleaf thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically gray-green in color, with a dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces tiny white or pale pink flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Thymus amictus is valued for its fragrant foliage and is often used in herbal medicine and culinary applications.
