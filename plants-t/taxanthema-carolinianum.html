Taxanthema carolinianum

Taxanthema carolinianum, commonly known as the Carolina taxanthemum, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while the flowers are usually pink or red, adding visual interest to its habitat.

Taxanthema carolinianum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
