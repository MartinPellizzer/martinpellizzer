Triplasis purpurea var. caribensis

Triplasis purpurea var. caribensis, commonly known as the Caribbean purpleleaf tree, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean region.

It is characterized by its striking purple foliage and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy texture, while its flowers are clustered in panicle-like inflorescences.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.
