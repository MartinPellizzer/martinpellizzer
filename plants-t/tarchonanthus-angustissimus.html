Tarchonanthus angustissimus

Tarchonanthus angustissimus, commonly known as the narrowleaf tarchonanthus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptability to harsh conditions. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into achene fruits.

Due to its ecological significance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
