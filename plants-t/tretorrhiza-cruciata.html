Tretorrhiza cruciata

Tretorrhiza cruciata, commonly known as the crucifix orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive cruciform (cross-shaped) floral structure. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are elongated and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native habitat.

The flowers, which bloom in clusters, are often brightly colored and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
