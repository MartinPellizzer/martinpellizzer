Teucrium roseum

Teucrium roseum, commonly known as rose germander, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, clustered in spikes, and range in color from pink to purple, blooming from late spring to early autumn.

The plant is known for its aromatic properties and is often used in traditional herbal medicine.
