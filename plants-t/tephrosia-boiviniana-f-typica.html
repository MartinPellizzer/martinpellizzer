Tephrosia boiviniana f. typica

Tephrosia boiviniana f. typica, commonly known as the Boivin's tephrosia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemes, which are typically borne in the axils of the leaves. It is native to tropical Africa and is often found in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and forest margins.

The species is noted for its potential use in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
