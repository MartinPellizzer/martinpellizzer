Turpinia sphaerocarpa var. microcerotis

Turpinia sphaerocarpa var. microcerotis, commonly known as the dwarf sand cherry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and along stream banks.

Its compact growth habit makes it a suitable ornamental plant for gardens and landscapes.
