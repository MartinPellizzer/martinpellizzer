Tibouchina chamaecistus

Tibouchina chamaecistus, commonly known as the red-hot catarrh, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking red bracts that surround small, white flowers, creating a vivid visual display. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Tibouchina chamaecistus is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
