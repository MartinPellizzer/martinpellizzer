Tylloma glabratum

Tylloma glabratum, commonly known as the smooth tylloma, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, glossy leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal regions. It is a perennial shrub that thrives in full sun and is relatively drought-tolerant.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it is not widely commercially available.
