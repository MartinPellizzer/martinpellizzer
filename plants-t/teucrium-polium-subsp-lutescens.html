Teucrium polium subsp. lutescens

Teucrium polium subsp.

lutescens, commonly known as the yellow germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, hairy foliage.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to reddish-purple, depending on the stage of bloom. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly aromatic scent.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
