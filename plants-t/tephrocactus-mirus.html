Tephrocactus mirus

Tephrocactus mirus, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its globular shape and dense, spiny exterior, which helps protect it from herbivores and excessive sunlight. The plant produces yellow flowers during the rainy season, adding a striking contrast to its green body. Its succulent stems store water, allowing it to thrive in dry environments.

Tephrocactus mirus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
