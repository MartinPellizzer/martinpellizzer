Tibouchina cerastifolia var. hirsuta

Tibouchina cerastifolia var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy tibouchina, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage that gives it a distinctive, velvety appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in the spring and summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The hairy tibouchina is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its compact growth habit and vibrant floral display.
