Trifolium basileianum

Trifolium basileianum, commonly known as the Basileian clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides.

Trifolium basileianum is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a valuable plant for soil enrichment.
