Trophisomia edulis

Trophisomia edulis, commonly known as the edible leaf plant, is a herbaceous species native to tropical regions.

It features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically green and slightly hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers followed by edible, round fruits that are rich in nutrients. Its leaves are often used in traditional cuisines for their mild, slightly sweet flavor.

Due to its nutritional value and adaptability, it is cultivated in various parts of the world for both culinary and medicinal purposes.
