Trisetum muricatum

Trisetum muricatum, commonly known as the hairy oat grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

Trisetum muricatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
