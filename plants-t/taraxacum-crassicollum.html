Taraxacum crassicollum

Taraxacum crassicollum, commonly known as the thick-necked dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its robust, hairy stems and large, yellow composite flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces a distinctive, elongated seed head that disperses seeds via the wind. It is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils across temperate regions.

Its leaves are typically broad, toothed, and arranged in a basal rosette.
