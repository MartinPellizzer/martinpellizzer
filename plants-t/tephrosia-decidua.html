Tephrosia decidua

Tephrosia decidua, commonly known as the African milk bush, is a deciduous shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is known for its small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to thrive in poor soils and arid conditions.

Tephrosia decidua is also valued for its medicinal properties and use in traditional medicine.
