Telipogon thomasii

Telipogon thomasii, commonly known as the Thomas's ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic orchid species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that are typically white or pale green, with intricate patterns resembling ghostly figures. This plant is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its unique growth habits, often growing epiphytically on trees or rocks. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered endangered and is of significant interest to botanists and conservationists.

Its botanical study contributes to the understanding of orchid diversity and adaptation in challenging environments.
