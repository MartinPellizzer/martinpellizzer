Tephrosia ambigua var. gracillima

Tephrosia ambigua var. gracillima, commonly known as the slender tephrosia, is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It features slender, branching stems and small, opposite leaves that are often pubescent. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers, which are typically arranged in racemes. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
