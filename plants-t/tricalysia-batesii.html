Tricalysia batesii

Tricalysia batesii, commonly known as the African velvet tamarind, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and compound leaves that are alternate and pinnate. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by woody pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal uses, it is cultivated in some regions for both aesthetic and practical purposes.
