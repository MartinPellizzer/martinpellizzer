Trichoglottis seidenfadenii

Trichoglottis seidenfadenii, commonly known as the Seidenfaden's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive, fragrant odor. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. It is part of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its unique floral structure and ecological adaptations.

Trichoglottis seidenfadenii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated by enthusiasts.
