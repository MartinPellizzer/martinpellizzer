Teucrium shanicum

Teucrium shanicum, commonly known as Shanicum germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia, where it thrives in rocky and well-drained soils. The plant features square stems, opposite leaves with serrated edges, and small, tubular flowers that range in color from violet to blue. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

Teucrium shanicum is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate drought conditions.
