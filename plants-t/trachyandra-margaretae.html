Trachyandra margaretae

Trachyandra margaretae, commonly known as the Cape mountain snowflake, is a strikingly beautiful plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Xanthorrhoeaceae and is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces striking white to pale blue flowers that bloom in spring, adding a dramatic touch to its appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is often cultivated by enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
