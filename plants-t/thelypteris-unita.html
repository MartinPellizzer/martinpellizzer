Thelypteris unita

Thelypteris unita, commonly known as the slender shield fern, is a species of fern native to the eastern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its delicate, bipinnate fronds that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are clustered on the undersides of the fronds.

Thelypteris unita is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its graceful appearance.
