Terminalia parviflora

Terminalia parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered Terminalia, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, spherical fruits. It is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Terminalia parviflora is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
