Tephrosia piscatoria

Tephrosia piscatoria, commonly known as water tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows near water bodies, showcasing its adaptability to moist environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are trifoliate, meaning they are divided into three leaflets, and the plant has a woody stem with a rough texture.

Tephrosia piscatoria is also known for its medicinal properties, with various parts of the plant used in traditional remedies.
