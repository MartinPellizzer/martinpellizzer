Taxodium microphyllum

Taxodium microphyllum, commonly known as the Mexican bald cypress, is a deciduous conifer native to southeastern Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive reddish-brown bark and its ability to thrive in wetland environments. The plant produces small, spherical cones and has scale-like leaves that turn a striking red or orange in the fall. It is often found in swampy areas and is notable for its unique root system that allows it to survive in waterlogged soils.

The Mexican bald cypress is also valued for its historical and ecological significance in its native habitat.
