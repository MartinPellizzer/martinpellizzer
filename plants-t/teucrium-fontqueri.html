Teucrium fontqueri

Teucrium fontqueri, commonly known as the Fontqueri germander, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, rocky soils and thrives in woodland or streamside habitats. The plant features upright stems with opposite leaves that are usually gray-green in color and covered in fine hairs. Its small, tubular flowers are typically blue to purple, blooming in the summer months.

Teucrium fontqueri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
