Tilia sphenolepis

Tilia sphenolepis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf lime, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern Himalayas.

It belongs to the Tiliaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in inflorescences, which appear in early summer. The tree produces small, round, greenish fruits that mature into brown, woody capsules.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
