Tournefortia cornifolia

Tournefortia cornifolia, commonly known as the horned tamarind, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and sandy soils. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a distinctive, waxy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
