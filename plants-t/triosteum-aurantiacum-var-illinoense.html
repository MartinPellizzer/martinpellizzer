Triosteum aurantiacum var. illinoense

Triosteum aurantiacum var. illinoense, commonly known as the Illinois goldenroot, is a perennial herb native to the central United States.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive yellowish-green color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its roots are yellow and have been historically used in traditional herbal medicine.

The species is adapted to moist, rich soils and is often found in woodland areas.
