Thalictrum galiiforme

Thalictrum galiiforme, commonly known as the meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, usually yellowish or greenish flowers. The plant features palmately divided leaves that are often ornamental, with a delicate, lace-like appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Thalictrum galiiforme is valued in gardens for its architectural form and early spring blooms.
