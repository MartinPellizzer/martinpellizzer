Trichomanes cuspidatum var. densestriatum

Trichomanes cuspidatum var. densestriatum, commonly known as the dense-striated maidenhair fern, is a variety of the maidenhair fern family (Huperziaceae).

It is characterized by its finely divided, dark green fronds that are densely covered with small, hair-like structures called trichomes. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or rocky crevices. Its fronds are usually leathery and have a distinctive, feathery appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens or as a houseplant.
