Trillium grandiflorum f. petalosum

Trillium grandiflorum f. petalosum, commonly known as the showy petal trillium, is a rare and striking variant of the white trillium.

It is characterized by its enlarged, petal-like sepals that give it a more floral appearance compared to the typical trillium. The plant is native to eastern North America and thrives in moist, shaded woodlands. Its flowers typically bloom in early spring, producing a single, erect flower with three white petals and three green sepals.

This form is of particular interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
