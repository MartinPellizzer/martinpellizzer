Tripogon abyssinicus

Tripogon abyssinicus, commonly known as the Abyssinian triplegrass, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit with dense, compact inflorescences. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its small, spike-like flowers are often clustered in triplets, giving it its scientific name.

Despite its ecological significance, it remains relatively understudied in botanical research.
