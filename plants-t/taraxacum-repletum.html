Taraxacum repletum

Taraxacum repletum, commonly known as the many-seeded dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of spoon-shaped leaves and a hollow, leafless stem that supports a single, large, yellow flower head. The plant produces a large number of small, wind-dispersed seeds enclosed in a pappus, which allows for efficient seed distribution. It is native to Europe and is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields.

The plant is adapted to a wide range of environmental conditions and is considered a common weed in many regions.
