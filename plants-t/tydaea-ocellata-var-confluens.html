Tydaea ocellata var. confluens

Tydaea ocellata var. confluens, commonly known as the spotted grass or marbled grass, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in spikelets. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often slightly folded and may have a bluish-green hue. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands, and it plays a role in supporting local insect populations.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland conservation.
