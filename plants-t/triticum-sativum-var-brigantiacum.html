Triticum sativum var. brigantiacum

Triticum sativum var. brigantiacum, commonly known as the Brigantine wheat, is a variant of common wheat characterized by its unique genetic traits.

It is primarily found in the Mediterranean region, particularly in areas of Italy and Greece. This variety is notable for its hardy nature and adaptability to various soil types. It has been studied for its potential in improving bread-making qualities and disease resistance.

However, it is less commonly cultivated today compared to other wheat varieties.
