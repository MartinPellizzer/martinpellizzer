Tetrazygia tetrandra

Tetrazygia tetrandra, commonly known as the golden star orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with a distinctive star-shaped pattern. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, and it produces small, fragrant blooms during the wet season.

Tetrazygia tetrandra is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
