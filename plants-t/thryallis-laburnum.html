Thryallis laburnum

Thryallis laburnum, commonly known as the golden chain tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its long, drooping clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, with a smooth margin. The plant produces small, woody pods that contain several seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to a variety of soil types.
