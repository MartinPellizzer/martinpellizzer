Trapa manshurica var. rubra

Trapa manshurica var. rubra, commonly known as the red water chestnut, is a variant of the water chestnut plant native to parts of Asia.

It is a aquatic plant that typically grows in shallow freshwater environments such as lakes and ponds. The plant features floating leaves with long petioles and produces distinctive spiny fruits that contain edible seeds. Its flowers are usually white or pink, and they bloom in the summer months.

The red variant is distinguished by its reddish pigmentation in the fruit and leaves, making it visually distinct from other Trapa species.
