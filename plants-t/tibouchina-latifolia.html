Tibouchina latifolia

Tibouchina latifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian flame bush, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, featuring broad, glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red, depending on the variety. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Tibouchina latifolia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
