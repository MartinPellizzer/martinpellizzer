Tephrosia supina

Tephrosia supina, commonly known as the prostrate tephrosia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows in a sprawling, low form, often covering the ground with its dense foliage. Its small, clustered flowers are usually white or pale purple, attracting various pollinators.

Tephrosia supina is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
