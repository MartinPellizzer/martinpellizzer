Trichomanes obtusum

Trichomanes obtusum, commonly known as the broad-leaf huperzia, is a species of fern-like plant belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its leathery, linear leaves that are often arranged in a rosette-like formation. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its spores are produced on the underside of the leaves in clusters called sporangia.

Trichomanes obtusum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its unique foliage structure.
