Trembleya phlogiformis var. genuina

Trembleya phlogiformis var. genuina, commonly known as the red-veined orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, velvety leaves with deep red veins contrasting against its dark green background. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, often with a subtle purple tinge. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and is of interest to orchid enthusiasts and researchers.
