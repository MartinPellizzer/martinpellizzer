Trifolium repens var. sanguineum

Trifolium repens var. sanguineum, commonly known as red clover, is a flowering plant in the legume family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each consisting of three heart-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pink or red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees. It is often found in grassy areas and is known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, enriching the soil.

This variety is cultivated for forage and as a cover crop in agricultural settings.
