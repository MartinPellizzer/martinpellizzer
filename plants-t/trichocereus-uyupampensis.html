Trichocereus uyupampensis

Trichocereus uyupampensis, commonly known as the Uyupampa cactus, is a species native to the Andean regions of Peru.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its spiny stems and ability to store water efficiently. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom at night. It thrives in high-altitude environments with well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its striking appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
