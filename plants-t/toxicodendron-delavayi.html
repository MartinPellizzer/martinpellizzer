Toxicodendron delavayi

Toxicodendron delavayi, commonly known as the Chinese lacquer tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves. The plant is known for producing a toxic sap that can cause dermatitis in humans upon contact. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its historical use in traditional lacquer production.

The plant's botanical features make it both ecologically significant and potentially hazardous to handle without proper precautions.
