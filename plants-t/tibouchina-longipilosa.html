Tibouchina longipilosa

Tibouchina longipilosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf tibouchina, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, featuring broad, ovate leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of showy, tubular flowers in shades of pink, red, and purple, which bloom throughout the year in tropical climates. Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to thrive in humid, warm environments with well-drained soil.

Tibouchina longipilosa is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display and is used in landscaping in subtropical regions.
