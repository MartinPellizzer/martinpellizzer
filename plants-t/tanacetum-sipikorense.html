Tanacetum sipikorense

Tanacetum sipikorense, commonly known as the Crimean tansy, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and yellow button-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, calcareous soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects.

Tanacetum sipikorense is also noted for its ability to attract beneficial insects, making it a valuable addition to ecological gardens.
