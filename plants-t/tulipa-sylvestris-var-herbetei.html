Tulipa sylvestris var. herbetei

Tulipa sylvestris var. herbetei, commonly known as the Siberian tulip, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves and striking, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy or loamy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is well adapted to cold climates and can tolerate harsh winter conditions.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in garden settings.
