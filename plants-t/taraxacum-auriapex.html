Taraxacum auriapex

Taraxacum auriapex, commonly known as the golden marguerite or golden dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and a distinctive, hollow, leafless stem that supports the inflorescence. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. Its leaves are basal, alternate, and typically have a serrated margin.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in many temperate regions.
