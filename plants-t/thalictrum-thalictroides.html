Thalictrum thalictroides

Thalictrum thalictroides, commonly known as the false rue-anemone, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and thrives in partial to full shade. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.

Thalictrum thalictroides has a fibrous root system and is relatively low maintenance once established.
