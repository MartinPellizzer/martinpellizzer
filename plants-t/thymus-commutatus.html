Thymus commutatus

Thymus commutatus, commonly known as narrowleaf thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features small, narrow leaves that are densely packed along its stems, giving it a compact appearance. The plant produces tiny, pale yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic foliage is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
