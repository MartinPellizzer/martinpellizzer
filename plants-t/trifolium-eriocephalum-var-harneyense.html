Trifolium eriocephalum var. harneyense

Trifolium eriocephalum var. harneyense, commonly known as Harney's clover, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by pods containing lens-shaped seeds.

It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils, and is adapted to arid environments with minimal water availability.
