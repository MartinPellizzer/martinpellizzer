Thalictrum minus var. carpaticum

Thalictrum minus var. carpaticum, commonly known as the Carpathian meadow rue, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. It has a fibrous root system and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
