Themeda palakkadensis

Themeda palakkadensis, commonly known as the Palakkad grass, is a perennial grass species native to the tropical regions of India.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and marshy areas. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Themeda palakkadensis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
