Turbinicarpus subterraneus subsp. booleanus

Turbinicarpus subterraneus subsp.

booleanus, commonly known as the Bochum bulb. This succulent cactus is native to southern Africa, specifically Namibia.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems and dense clusters of white or yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its fleshy stems.

Its subspecies status highlights its distinct morphological and genetic traits compared to other members of the Turbinicarpus genus.
