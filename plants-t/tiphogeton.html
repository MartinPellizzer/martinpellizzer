Tiphogeton

Tiphogeton, commonly known as the mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that attaches itself to the branches of trees and shrubs.

It lacks roots and absorbs water and nutrients through its leaves, which are adapted for this purpose. The plant produces small, white or yellowish berries that are often consumed by birds, which then spread its seeds. Tiphogeton is typically found in temperate regions and can be a significant ecological factor in its host species.

Its presence can affect the health and growth of the host plant, making it both a botanical curiosity and a subject of ecological study.
