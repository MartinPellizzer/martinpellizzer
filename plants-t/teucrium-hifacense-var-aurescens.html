Teucrium hifacense var. aurescens

Teucrium hifacense var. aurescens, commonly known as the golden teucrium, is a flowering plant native to specific regions in the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking violet to purple flowers that often display a golden hue at the base. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable addition to botanical collections.
