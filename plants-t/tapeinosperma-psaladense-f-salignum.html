Tapeinosperma psaladense f. salignum

Tapeinosperma psaladense f. salignum, commonly known as the coastal psaladense, is a rare shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Brazil.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has adapted to saline and sandy soils, making it a hardy species in coastal environments. Its leaves are typically narrow and leathery, providing resistance to salt spray and wind.

This unique plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal dunes and supporting local biodiversity.
