Thymus angustifolius f. ericoides

Thymus angustifolius f. ericoides, commonly known as narrow-leaved thyme, is a compact, low-growing perennial herb native to Europe.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically narrow and densely packed, giving it a dense, mat-like appearance. The plant produces tiny, pink or white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its foliage is often used in culinary and medicinal applications due to its aromatic properties.
