Trachyandra laxa var. laxa

Trachyandra laxa var. laxa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved trachyandra, is a member of the Asparagaceae family and is native to South Africa.

It is a small, evergreen perennial herb characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a powdery bloom. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers in terminal spikes, which appear in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
