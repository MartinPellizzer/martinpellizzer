Tamarix esperanzana

Tamarix esperanzana, commonly known as the saltbush or desert willow, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves that reduce water loss, making it well adapted to drought conditions. The plant produces delicate pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access deep water sources in dry soils.

Tamarix esperanzana is often used in reclamation projects due to its ability to stabilize soils and tolerate saline environments.
