Triraphis andropogonoides

Triraphis andropogonoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf panic grass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and compact growth habit, typically reaching heights of 1 to 2 feet. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or disturbed areas.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes many economically important grasses.
