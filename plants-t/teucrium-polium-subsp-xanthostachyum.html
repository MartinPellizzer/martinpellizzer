Teucrium polium subsp. xanthostachyum

Teucrium polium subsp.

xanthostachyum, commonly known as the yellow-stemmed germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or dry habitats and has aromatic, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple, arranged in dense spikes. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is often used in traditional herbal medicine.

The subspecies is distinguished by its yellowish stems and slightly different leaf morphology compared to the typical form of Teucrium polium.
