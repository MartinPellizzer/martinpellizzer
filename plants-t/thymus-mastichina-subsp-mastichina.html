Thymus mastichina subsp. mastichina

Thymus mastichina subsp.

mastichina, commonly known as the mastichina thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its aromatic, needle-like leaves and small, pale pink to white flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is well adapted to sunny, arid environments. It produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
