Trilepisium madagascariense

Trilepisium madagascariense, commonly known as the Madagascar leek, is a herbaceous plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Apiaceae, which includes many aromatic herbs and vegetables. The plant features slender, hollow stems and fern-like, pinnate leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family.

Trilepisium madagascariense is valued for its medicinal properties and is often found in moist, shaded forest environments.
