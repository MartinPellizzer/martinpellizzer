Taraxacum purpurei-petiolatum

Taraxacum purpurei-petiolatum, commonly known as the purple-stemmed dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its hairy, purple-tinged stems and heart-shaped basal leaves. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that develop into fluffy white seed heads, typical of the dandelion genus. It is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats, thriving in temperate regions.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in lawns and fields across Europe and parts of Asia.
