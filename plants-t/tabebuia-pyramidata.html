Tabebuia pyramidata

Tabebuia pyramidata, commonly known as the flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of bright red or orange flowers that appear during the dry season. The tree has a straight trunk with a broad, spreading crown and exudes a strong, sweet fragrance. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and pinnately compound, turning yellow before falling off in winter.

The wood of Tabebuia pyramidata is hard and durable, making it valuable for construction and furniture-making.
