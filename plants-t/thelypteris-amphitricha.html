Thelypteris amphitricha

Thelypteris amphitricha, commonly known as the hairy shield fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Thelypteridaceae and is characterized by its distinctive fronds with a hairy underside. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its reproductive structures include sori, which are clusters of sporangia located on the underside of the fronds.

Thelypteris amphitricha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its lush, feathery appearance.
