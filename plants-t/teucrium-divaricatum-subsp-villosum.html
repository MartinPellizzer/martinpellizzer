Teucrium divaricatum subsp. villosum

Teucrium divaricatum subsp.

villosum, commonly known as woolly germander, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and sunny habitats.

The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular flowers in shades of purple or blue. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs.

Its ornamental value and ability to thrive in harsh conditions make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
