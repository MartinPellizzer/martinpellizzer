Trifolium badium subsp. pseudobadium

Trifolium badium subsp.

pseudobadium, commonly known as the purple clover, is a flowering plant in the legume family. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets.

The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that are arranged in dense clusters. It is typically found in grasslands and open areas, thriving in well-drained soils.

This subspecies is valued for its role in soil nitrogen fixation and as a forage crop for livestock.
