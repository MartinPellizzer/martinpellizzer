Tetranthus

Tetranthus, commonly known as the purple thistle, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and showy, purple or pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, and it thrives in dry, rocky soils. Tetranthus species are often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
