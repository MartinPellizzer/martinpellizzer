Tanacetum oxystegium

Tanacetum oxystegium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tansy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features finely divided, linear leaves and produces small yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks.

Tanacetum oxystegium is noted for its aromatic properties and has been used historically in traditional medicine.
