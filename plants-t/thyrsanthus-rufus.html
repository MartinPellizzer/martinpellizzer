Thyrsanthus rufus

Thyrsanthus rufus, commonly known as the red-flowered thyrsin, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, spiny growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, red flowers that are typically visited by native bees and other pollinators. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

Thyrsanthus rufus is adapted to grow in coastal and rocky habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.
