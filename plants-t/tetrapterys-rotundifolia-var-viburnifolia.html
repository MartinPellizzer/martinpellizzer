Tetrapterys rotundifolia var. viburnifolia

Tetrapterys rotundifolia var. viburnifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf tamarillo, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring. Its fruits are edible, resembling small, yellow tomatoes in appearance and having a tangy, slightly acidic flavor.

This species is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and its culinary uses.
