Thelypteris microcarpa

Thelypteris microcarpa, commonly known as the small-fruited shield fern, is a species of fern native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically less than 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, clustered sporangia on the underside of its fertile fronds, which are often covered in a fine, hairy texture. Thelypteris microcarpa thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its reproductive structures are adapted for spore dispersal, contributing to its ecological role in forest ecosystems.
