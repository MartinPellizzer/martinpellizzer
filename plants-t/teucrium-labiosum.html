Teucrium labiosum

Teucrium labiosum, commonly known as redstem germander, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pink to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands.

Teucrium labiosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
