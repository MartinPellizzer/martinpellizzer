Tamarix hispida var. karelinii

Tamarix hispida var. karelinii, commonly known as the Kashmir tamarisk, is a shrub or small tree native to the Himalayan regions of Pakistan and India.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, scale-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale pink or red flowers in summer, which are clustered in spherical inflorescences. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for rocky or sandy soils.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
