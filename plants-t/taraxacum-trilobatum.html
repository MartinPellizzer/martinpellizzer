Taraxacum trilobatum

Taraxacum trilobatum, commonly known as the three-lobed dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of alternating, lanceolate leaves with three distinct lobes. The plant produces a single, hollow stem that bears a fluffy, spherical seed head composed of numerous seeds with parachute-like structures. It is typically found in temperate regions and thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
