Tinospora smilacina var. berneyi

Tinospora smilacina var. berneyi, commonly known as the narrow-leaf Indian snakeroot, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Menispermaceae family and is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by fleshy, red berries that are an important food source for birds and small mammals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Due to its medicinal properties, it is also valued in traditional herbal medicine.
