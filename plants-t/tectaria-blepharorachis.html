Tectaria blepharorachis

Tectaria blepharorachis, commonly known as the earthen fern, is a species of polypodioid fern native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and the presence of a unique, ear-shaped structure on its fronds. The plant's fronds are typically divided into segments with a distinctive, overlapping arrangement of leaflets. Tectaria blepharorachis thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important component of tropical ecosystems.
