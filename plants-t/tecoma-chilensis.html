Tecoma chilensis

Tecoma chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean trumpet flower, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Bignoniaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow-orange flowers that resemble trumpets. The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves, and its stems are usually woody and branched. Tecoma chilensis produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in temperate climates.
