Theobroma sapidum

Theobroma sapidum, commonly known as cocoa, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon basin.

It produces pods that contain seeds used to make chocolate. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and dark green, while its flowers are small and white.

Theobroma sapidum is cultivated for its beans, which are processed into cocoa mass, cocoa butter, and chocolate.
