Tephrosia coccinea

Tephrosia coccinea, commonly known as the red tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2 to 5 meters. The plant produces showy, bright red flowers that are clustered in racemes, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Tephrosia coccinea is also known for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
