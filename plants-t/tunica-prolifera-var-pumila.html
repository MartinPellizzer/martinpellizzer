Tunica prolifera var. pumila

Tunica prolifera var. pumila, commonly known as the dwarf tunica, is a unique plant species characterized by its compact, low-growing form.

It is native to specific regions with particular climatic conditions, typically found in rocky or arid environments. The plant features dense, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually appearing in clusters during the growing season.

Due to its specialized adaptations, it is considered a rare and resilient species in its native habitat.
