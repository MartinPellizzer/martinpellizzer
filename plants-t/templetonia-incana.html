Templetonia incana

Templetonia incana, commonly known as the silky lilly pilly, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, silvery-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture with a soft, downy feel.

Templetonia incana is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in coastal and sandy soils.
