Typhonium adnatum

Typhonium adnatum, commonly known as the black-tongued arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and a spadix inflorescence. The plant produces a starchy tuberous root that is used in traditional medicine and as a food source. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and ethnobotanical contexts.
