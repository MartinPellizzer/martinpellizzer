Taraxacum pseudodilatatum

Taraxacum pseudodilatatum, commonly known as the broadleaf dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy rosette structure. The plant features broad, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. Its bright yellow flower heads consist of numerous small florets that bloom in the spring and early summer.

The plant is known for its milky sap and is often found in grassy areas and disturbed soils.
