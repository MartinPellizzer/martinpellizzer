Teucrium orientale var. brachyodon

Teucrium orientale var. brachyodon, commonly known as the Armenian germander, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Eastern Mediterranean.

It features upright, branching stems covered in dense, hairy foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue to violet in color, arranged in dense spikes. Its leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly serrated margin.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover in dry, well-drained soils.
