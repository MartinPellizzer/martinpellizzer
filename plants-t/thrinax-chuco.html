Thrinax chuco

Thrinax chuco, commonly known as the Chuco palm, is a species of palm native to the Caribbean, particularly found in Cuba and the Isle of Pines.

It is a small, slow-growing palm with a short trunk and a dense crown of feather-like leaves. The plant thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to saline conditions, making it resilient to coastal winds and salt spray. Its leaves are often used in traditional thatching and basket-making, highlighting its cultural significance.

The Chuco palm plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for local wildlife.
