Terminalia crebrifolia

Terminalia crebrifolia, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by woody, elliptical fruits containing a single seed. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Terminalia crebrifolia is valued for its hard, durable wood and has traditional medicinal uses in some African communities.
