Teucrium saxatile var. getulum

Teucrium saxatile var. getulum, commonly known as the slender germander, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in dry, sunny environments. The plant features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often grayish-green in color. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink to purple, and they bloom in summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in herbal remedies.
