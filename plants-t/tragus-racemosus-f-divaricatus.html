Tragus racemosus f. divaricatus

Tragus racemosus f. divaricatus, commonly known as the branched tragus, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, gray-green leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is native to southern Africa and thrives in dry, rocky habitats.

Due to its unique morphology and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in arid gardens.
