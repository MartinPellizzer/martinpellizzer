Taraxacum messanense

Taraxacum messanense, commonly known as the Messina dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and a hollow, leafy stem. The plant produces a distinctive yellow composite flower head that develops into a ball of white seeds with parachute-like structures. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Taraxacum messanense is notable for its resilience and ability to colonize various habitats.
