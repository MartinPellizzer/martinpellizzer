Tragacantha lagonyx

Tragacantha lagonyx, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a species of flowering plant native to the arid regions of Iran and Turkey.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by distinctive, inflated seed pods. Its sap, known as tragacanth, has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a thickening agent in food and pharmaceuticals.

The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, showcasing resilience through deep root systems and drought tolerance.
