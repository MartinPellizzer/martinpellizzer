Trichilia obtusifolia

Trichilia obtusifolia, commonly known as African shea tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by elongated, woody pods containing flat, edible seeds. These seeds are widely used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil, making the plant economically significant in its region.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
