Trichomanes setaceum

Trichomanes setaceum, commonly known as the slender maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically green and covered in tiny, hair-like structures. This fern thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and produce spores for propagation.

The plant's intricate morphology and ecological adaptations make it a notable example of fern diversity in moist forest ecosystems.
