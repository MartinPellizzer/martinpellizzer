Triticum repens var. magellanicum

Triticum repens var. magellanicum, commonly known as the Magellanic grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern South America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. The plant thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in wetlands and grassy areas. Its leaves are typically narrow and have a bluish-green hue, while its roots form dense mats that help stabilize soil.

This variety is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to its environment.
