Turbinicarpus × roseiflorus

Turbinicarpus × roseiflorus, commonly known as the hybrid moon cactus, is a fascinating hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Turbinicarpus caladium and Turbinicarpus roseiflorus.

It is characterized by its distinctive, flattened, disc-like stem that resembles a moon, hence its common name. The plant typically features a vibrant, colorful crown on top of the stem, which is often a striking shade of pink or red. Its leaves are usually small, fleshy, and arranged in a rosette pattern, adapted to store water in arid environments.

This hybrid is popular among cactus enthusiasts for its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
