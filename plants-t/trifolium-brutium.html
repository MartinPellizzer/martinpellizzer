Trifolium brutium

Trifolium brutium, commonly known as the Greek clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassy areas and rocky habitats.

Trifolium brutium is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
