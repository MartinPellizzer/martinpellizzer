Tulipa aitchisonii

Tulipa aitchisonii, commonly known as the Aitchison's tulip, is a striking bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from deep red to orange and yellow, often with intricate patterns. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and has a robust root system adapted to its arid habitat. It blooms in early spring and is prized for its ornamental value in gardens.

Tulipa aitchisonii is relatively drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil.
