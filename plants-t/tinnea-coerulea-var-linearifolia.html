Tinnea coerulea var. linearifolia

Tinnea coerulea var. linearifolia, commonly known as the blue tinnea, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a vibrant touch to its foliage. The leaves are elongated and linear, giving the plant its specific variety name, linearifolia. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
