Tibouchina ackermannii

Tibouchina ackermannii, commonly known as the velvetleaf tibouchina, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height, characterized by its dense, velvety foliage. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a dark green upper surface with a silvery underside. It produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in spring and summer.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant foliage and flowering display.
