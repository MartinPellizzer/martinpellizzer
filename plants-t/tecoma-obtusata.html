Tecoma obtusata

Tecoma obtusata, commonly known as the Mexican trumpetflower, is a flowering plant native to Central America and the southern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height. The plant produces showy, tubular yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Tecoma obtusata is known for its rapid growth and adaptability to various soil types, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
