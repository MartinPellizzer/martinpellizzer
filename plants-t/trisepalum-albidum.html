Trisepalum albidum

Trisepalum albidum, commonly known as the white trisepalum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shrubland or woodland habitats, preferring well-drained soils. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Trisepalum albidum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
