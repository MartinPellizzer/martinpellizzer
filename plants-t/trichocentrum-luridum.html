Trichocentrum luridum

Trichocentrum luridum, commonly known as the yellow-stemmed orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish-green flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom during the rainy season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining potting media.

Trichocentrum luridum is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
