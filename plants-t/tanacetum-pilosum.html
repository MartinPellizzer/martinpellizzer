Tanacetum pilosum

Tanacetum pilosum, commonly known as the woolly tansy, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its finely hairy, gray-green foliage and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its aromatic properties. Tanacetum pilosum typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its historical use in herbal medicine and as a natural insect repellent highlights its ecological and botanical significance.
