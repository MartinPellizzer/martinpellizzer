Thyrsia

Thyrsia, commonly known as the yellow thistle, is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It consists of several species, most of which are native to Europe, North Africa, and western Asia. The plants are typically herbaceous, with spiny leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in summer. Thyrsia species often grow in dry, disturbed soils and are adapted to temperate climates.

Their flowers are composed of numerous small florets arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family.
