Themeda longispatha

Themeda longispatha, commonly known as the long-spatha grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces inflorescences that are typically spike-like, with elongated bracts that give it its common name. It thrives in a variety of soil types but is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Themeda longispatha plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
