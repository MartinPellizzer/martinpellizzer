Tamarix tetragyna var. deserti

Tamarix tetragyna var. deserti, commonly known as the desert tamarisk, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and clusters of pink to reddish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep root systems that allow it to access water from deeper soil layers. It is often found in sandy or gravelly soils and can thrive in areas with low rainfall.

Its rapid growth and ability to stabilize soil make it a valuable plant in ecological restoration projects.
