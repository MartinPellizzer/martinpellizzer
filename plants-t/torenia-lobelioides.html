Torenia lobelioides

Torenia lobelioides, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling a misty cloud. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden areas.

Torenia lobelioides is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
