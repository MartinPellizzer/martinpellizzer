Trianaeopiper mexiae

Trianaeopiper mexiae, commonly known as the Mexican pepper plant, is a member of the Piperaceae family, which includes various pepper species.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and the presence of small, heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces tiny, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by small, berry-like fruits. Native to the mountainous regions of Mexico, it thrives in humid, tropical climates.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and research settings.
