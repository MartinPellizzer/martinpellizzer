Trifolium foliosum

Trifolium foliosum, commonly known as the leafy clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged symmetrically. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically white or pale purple, and it is often found in meadows and grassy areas. Trifolium foliosum is a perennial herb that thrives in temperate climates and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Its botanical features make it an important plant in ecological and agricultural contexts.
