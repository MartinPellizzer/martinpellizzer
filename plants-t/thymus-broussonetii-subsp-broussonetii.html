Thymus broussonetii subsp. broussonetii

Thymus broussonetii subsp.

broussonetii, commonly known as Persian thyme, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its small, fragrant leaves and clusters of pink to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Its aromatic foliage contains essential oils, making it valuable for culinary and medicinal uses.

It is also a popular ornamental plant due to its hardiness and attractive appearance.
