Trifolium elegans subsp. hybridum

Trifolium elegans subsp.

hybridum, commonly known as the hybrid clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a rosette pattern.

The plant produces small, pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile addition to gardens and natural habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional herbal medicine.
