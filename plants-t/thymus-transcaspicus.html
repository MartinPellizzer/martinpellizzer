Thymus transcaspicus

Thymus transcaspicus, commonly known as Central Asian thyme, is a perennial herb native to regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features small, needle-like leaves and produces pink or purple flowers during the summer months. It is part of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs.

Thymus transcaspicus is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
