Terminalia pierrei

Terminalia pierrei, commonly known as the African snowball tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded crown and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in winter. The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a smooth, glossy surface. The fruit is a small, round drupe that turns red when mature.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its winter flowering display.
