Teucrium montbretii

Teucrium montbretii, commonly known as French bugloss, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features lance-shaped, gray-green leaves with a hairy texture and produces spikes of blue or violet flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Teucrium montbretii is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
