Thilachium angustifolium

Thilachium angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved devil's ivy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant is often found in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in dry and sunny conditions. It has a trailing growth habit, making it suitable for hanging baskets or as a ground cover.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its resilience and ability to tolerate various environmental conditions.
