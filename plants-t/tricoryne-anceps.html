Tricoryne anceps

Tricoryne anceps, commonly known as the red-flowered orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, vibrant red petals and sepals, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. Native to tropical regions of South America, this plant thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are typically small but highly fragrant, attracting various pollinators such as bees and moths.

The plant has fibrous roots and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for greenhouse cultivation.
