Thladiantha villosula var. nigrita

Thladiantha villosula var. nigrita, commonly known as black sandthistle, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are usually grayish-green with a dense covering of fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The flowers are yellow and appear in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
