Thryptomene longifolia

Thryptomene longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved thryptomene, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is often found in heathlands and open forests, preferring well-drained soils.

Thryptomene longifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses by Indigenous Australian communities.
