Trichoceros parviflorus

Trichoceros parviflorus, commonly known as the small-flowered trichoceros, is a rare and delicate plant species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, clustered inflorescences. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom sequentially throughout the growing season. Its leaves are typically narrow, strap-like, and covered in a dense layer of fine, hair-like structures called trichomes.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered an endangered species and is often found in cloud forests with high humidity and nutrient-rich soil.
