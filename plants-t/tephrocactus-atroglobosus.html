Tephrocactus atroglobosus

Tephrocactus atroglobosus, commonly known as the dark globular cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its spherical, ribbed stems that are typically dark green to purplish in color. The plant produces small, white flowers during the summer months, which are borne on short, erect pedicels. It is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is often cultivated by succulent enthusiasts.
