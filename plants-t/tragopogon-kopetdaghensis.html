Tragopogon kopetdaghensis

Tragopogon kopetdaghensis, commonly known as the Kopetdagh thistle, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in rocky and sandy soils, adapting to harsh environmental conditions. Its yellow flowers bloom in the summer, and it produces a distinctive, spiky seed head that aids in wind dispersal.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
