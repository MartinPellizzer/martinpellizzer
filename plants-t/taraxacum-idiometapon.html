Taraxacum idiometapon

Taraxacum idiometapon, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of yellowish-green leaves and produces numerous yellow flowers in a composite head. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grassy areas. It has a taproot system and is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils.

The species is also noted for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in herbal remedies.
