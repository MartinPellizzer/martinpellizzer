Typhonium hunanense

Typhonium hunanense, commonly known as the Hunan arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and striking spadix inflorescence. The plant is known for its edible underground tubers, which are rich in starch and used in local cuisines. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in swampy or marshy areas.

Due to its ornamental value and culinary uses, it is cultivated in some regions for both aesthetic and nutritional purposes.
