Townsendia scapigera

Townsendia scapigera, commonly known as the mountain dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, open habitats and is often found in alpine and arid environments.

Townsendia scapigera is adapted to withstand drought and cold temperatures, making it a resilient member of its ecosystem.
