Tithymalus × pseudoesula

Tithymalus × pseudoesula, commonly known as the hybrid tithymalus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white to pink ray florets. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and thrives in well-drained soils in full sun. It is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Due to its hybrid origin, it exhibits a range of morphological variations depending on the parent species.
