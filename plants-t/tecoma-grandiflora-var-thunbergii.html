Tecoma grandiflora var. thunbergii

Tecoma grandiflora var. thunbergii, commonly known as the yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant produces vibrant yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical with a smooth texture.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
