Theophrasta cochinchinensis

Theophrasta cochinchinensis, commonly known as the Vietnamese star anise, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, star-shaped fruit that resembles the common star anise. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are glossy and dark green, while its flowers are small and white or pale yellow.

The fruit, which is the primary part used, contains essential oils and is valued for its medicinal and culinary properties.
