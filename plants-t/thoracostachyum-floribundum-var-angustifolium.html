Thoracostachyum floribundum var. angustifolium

Thoracostachyum floribundum var. angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leafed thornbush, is a shrub native to arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, adapting to low-water environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in spring. Its woody stems are covered in thorns, providing protection against herbivores.

This species is well-suited for xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
