Tetraneuris angustata

Tetraneuris angustata, commonly known as narrowleaf tetraneuris, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy yellow blooms. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its narrow, linear leaves are arranged in a basal rosette, and it produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Tetraneuris angustata is often found in rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
