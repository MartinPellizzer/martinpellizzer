Thymus mandschuricus

Thymus mandschuricus, commonly known as Manchurian thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in forested areas. Its small, pink or purple flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees.

The leaves are used in traditional medicine and have a strong, pungent aroma similar to other thyme species.
