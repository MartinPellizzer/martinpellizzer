Teucrium expassum

Teucrium expassum, commonly known as purple germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, hairy texture. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

Teucrium expassum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to well-drained soils.
