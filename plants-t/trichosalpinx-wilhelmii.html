Trichosalpinx wilhelmii

Trichosalpinx wilhelmii, commonly known as the blue violet orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers with a distinctive yellow lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree bark in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy texture, while its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture from the air.

Trichosalpinx wilhelmii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses due to its specific environmental requirements.
