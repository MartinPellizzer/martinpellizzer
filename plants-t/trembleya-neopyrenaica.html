Trembleya neopyrenaica

Trembleya neopyrenaica, commonly known as the Pyrenean bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula.

It typically grows in rocky and limestone soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, often with a white throat. Its leaves are lanceolate and hairy, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Trembleya neopyrenaica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
