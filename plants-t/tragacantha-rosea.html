Tragacantha rosea

Tragacantha rosea, commonly known as the rose tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive pink or red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces a latex sap that has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a thickening agent. Its leaves are trifoliate, meaning they are divided into three leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
