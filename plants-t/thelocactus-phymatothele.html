Thelocactus phymatothele

Thelocactus phymatothele, commonly known as the ghost cactus, is a unique cactus species native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems that often appear skeletal, giving it a ghostly appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a brief but striking visual element to its otherwise stark form. Its leaves are modified into spines, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

The plant's structure and growth habits make it well-adapted to extreme desert conditions.
