Tussilago saxatilis

Tussilago saxatilis, commonly known as stone larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. The plant features upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are blue to purple in color, arranged in dense spikes, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Tussilago saxatilis is known for its medicinal properties, particularly for its potential respiratory benefits.
