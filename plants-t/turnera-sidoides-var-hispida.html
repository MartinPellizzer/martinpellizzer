Turnera sidoides var. hispida

Turnera sidoides var. hispida, commonly known as the rough turnera, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and leaves, which are often covered in fine, stiff hairs. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with five petals, and are borne in clusters.

The leaves are opposite, simple, and have a leathery texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
