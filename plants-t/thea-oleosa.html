Thea oleosa

Thea oleosa, commonly known as the oil plant, is a species native to certain parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant produces a valuable oil from its seeds, which is used in both culinary and industrial applications. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Thea oleosa is also noted for its aromatic properties, making it a popular choice in traditional medicine and perfumery.
