Trapa muzzanensis

Trapa muzzanensis, commonly known as the Muzzanese water shield, is a aquatic plant native to parts of Asia, particularly China.

It is characterized by its floating leaves that are broad and rounded, with a waxy surface that helps repel water. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, followed by the development of spiny, inflated fruits. Its roots are submerged, anchoring the plant in shallow water bodies.

Trapa muzzanensis plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms and contributing to water filtration.
