Thea speciosa

Thea speciosa, commonly known as the golden trumpet tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are typically hairy on the underside. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Thea speciosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
