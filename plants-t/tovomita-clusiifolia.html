Tovomita clusiifolia

Tovomita clusiifolia, commonly known as the African snowball, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in spherical clusters, which are often covered in a powdery coating. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Tovomita clusiifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
