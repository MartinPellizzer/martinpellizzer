Thymus tragoriganum

Thymus tragoriganum, commonly known as the Balkan thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces tiny, aromatic leaves used in traditional herbal medicine.

Thymus tragoriganum is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in culinary applications.
