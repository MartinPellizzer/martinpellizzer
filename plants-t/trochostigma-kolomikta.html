Trochostigma kolomikta

Trochostigma kolomikta, commonly known as the variegated coral tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, multicolored leaves that display combinations of green, white, and pink, creating a visually appealing foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of pink or white, which bloom in the spring. Its bark is smooth and often has a reddish or orange hue, contributing to its ornamental value.

The plant is well-suited for cultivation in warm climates and is often used in ornamental horticulture for its vibrant appearance.
