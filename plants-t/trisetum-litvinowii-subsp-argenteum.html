Trisetum litvinowii subsp. argenteum

Trisetum litvinowii subsp.

argenteum, commonly known as silverhair grass, is a perennial grass species native to central and eastern Europe. It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray blades and dense, tufted growth habit.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows, marshes, and along streambanks, preferring well-drained to wet soils. Its inflorescence consists of a single, narrow spikelet that is often tinged with purple or pink.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland habitats.
