Tristachya anthoxanthoides

Tristachya anthoxanthoides, commonly known as the yellow panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a yellowish-green coloration. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in erosion control or as a forage crop in certain regions.
