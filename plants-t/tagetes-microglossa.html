Tagetes microglossa

Tagetes microglossa, commonly known as the Mexican marigold, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves and grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its flowers are often used in traditional medicine and as natural pest repellents.
