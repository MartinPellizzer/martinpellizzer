Tschudya lanata

Tschudya lanata, commonly known as the woolly tansy, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Tschudya lanata is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
