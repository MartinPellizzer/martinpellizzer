Tetrastigma myanmaricum

Tetrastigma myanmaricum, commonly known as the Myanmar grapevine, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and tendrils that aid in its climbing habit. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by berries, which are typically edible. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in forested areas with high humidity.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal uses, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and research settings.
