Tectaria teijsmanniana

Tectaria teijsmanniana, commonly known as the Tectaria fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Tectariaceae.

It is characterized by its large, leathery fronds that are typically divided into numerous pinnae, giving it a distinctive, intricate appearance. The plant is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia, Malaysia, and the Philippines. Its rhizome is often covered in a dense layer of brown, hairy structures, which aid in moisture retention.

Tectaria teijsmanniana is often found in humid, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices.
