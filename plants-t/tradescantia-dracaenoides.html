Tradescantia dracaenoides

Tradescantia dracaenoides, commonly known as the dragon tree or red-leafed spider plant, is a species native to Central America.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are typically bright red or variegated with white and green. The plant produces small, inconspicuous green flowers that are followed by bright red berries. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires moderate to bright indirect light.

Due to its ornamental appeal, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens.
