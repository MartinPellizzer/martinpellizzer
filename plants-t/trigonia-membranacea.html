Trigonia membranacea

Trigonia membranacea, commonly known as the African nettle, is a flowering plant native to tropical and southern Africa.

It is an herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant is also valued for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
