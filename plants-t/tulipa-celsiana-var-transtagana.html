Tulipa celsiana var. transtagana

Tulipa celsiana var. transtagana, commonly known as the Trans-Tatra tulip, is a bulbous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

This variety is characterized by its striking, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep red. The plant produces strap-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

The tulip is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in horticulture.
