Toona febrifuga

Toona febrifuga, commonly known as Chinese yellowwood, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by edible, round fruits containing a single seed. Its leaves and fruits are used in traditional medicine for their purported fever-reducing properties.

The tree is also valued for its hard, durable wood used in construction and furniture.
