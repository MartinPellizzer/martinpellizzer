Thymus britannicus

Thymus britannicus, commonly known as the British thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the British Isles.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically features small, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are fragrant and contain essential oils, making it useful in culinary and medicinal applications.

Thymus britannicus thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats.
