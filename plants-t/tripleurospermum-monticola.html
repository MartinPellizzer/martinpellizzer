Tripleurospermum monticola

Tripleurospermum monticola, commonly known as the mountain hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in some areas.
