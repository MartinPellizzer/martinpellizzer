Terminalia citrina

Terminalia citrina, commonly known as the yellow terminalia, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and alternate, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
