Tamarix pseudopallasii

Tamarix pseudopallasii, commonly known as the desert tamarisk, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and small, scale-like leaves that reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces pink to reddish flowers in dense clusters, which bloom in the summer and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Tamarix pseudopallasii is well adapted to saline soils and can thrive in areas with low rainfall.

Its rapid growth and ability to stabilize sandy soils make it an important plant in ecological restoration projects.
