Tachigali loretensis

Tachigali loretensis, commonly known as the Loretense Tachigali, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant typically grows in humid lowland forests and can reach heights of up to 20 meters. Its leaves are compound, with small, ovate leaflets, and it produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters.

The fruit is a pod that contains several seeds, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.
