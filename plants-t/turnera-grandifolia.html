Turnera grandifolia

Turnera grandifolia, commonly known as the "Golden Trumpet" or "Mexican Trumpet," is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It belongs to the family Turneraceae and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

Turnera grandifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
