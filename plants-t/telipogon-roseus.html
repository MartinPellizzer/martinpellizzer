Telipogon roseus

Telipogon roseus, commonly known as the rose pogon, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky slopes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Telipogon roseus is considered a rare and endangered species.
