Trifolium pratense f. velenovskyana

Trifolium pratense f. velenovskyana, commonly known as the Velenovsky's red clover, is a botanical variant of the common red clover.

It is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves with a deep red coloration. This subspecies is native to Central Europe and is often found in meadows and grasslands. It possesses a robust root system that helps in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and agricultural significance.
