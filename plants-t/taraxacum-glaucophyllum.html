Taraxacum glaucophyllum

Taraxacum glaucophyllum, commonly known as the bluish dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of blue-green leaves and a flowering stem that produces a single, yellow, composite flower head. Native to parts of Europe and Asia, it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant is known for its medicinal properties, with parts of it used in traditional herbal remedies.

Its life cycle includes a period of dormancy during winter, followed by rapid growth in the spring.
