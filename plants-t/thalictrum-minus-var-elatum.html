Thalictrum minus var. elatum

Thalictrum minus var. elatum, commonly known as the tall meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, adorned with small, clustered white flowers that bloom in early summer. The leaves are finely divided, creating a lacy appearance, and the plant has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and streambanks.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
