Tribrachys

Tribrachys, commonly known as the narrow-leafed tansy, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small yellow flowers arranged in clustered heads. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as meadows and streambanks. It has a fibrous root system and produces a distinctive, aromatic scent.

Tribrachys is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
