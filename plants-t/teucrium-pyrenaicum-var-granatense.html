Teucrium pyrenaicum var. granatense

Teucrium pyrenaicum var. granatense, commonly known as Granate rockrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions. Its small, tubular flowers are usually violet or blue, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant features.
