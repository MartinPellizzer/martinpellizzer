Teucrium ellmanii

Teucrium ellmanii, commonly known as Ellman's germander, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. Its small, tubular flowers are usually purple or violet, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
