Thymus serpyllum f. fallax

Thymus serpyllum f. fallax, commonly known as fallax thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, fragrant leaves that are typically gray-green in color, with a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed. The plant produces pink or purple flowers in early summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for both garden use and medicinal purposes.
