Teesdalia

Teesdalia, commonly known as the "painted cup," is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has a compact growth habit with hairy leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Teesdalia thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its unique flower shape and long blooming period add visual interest to gardens throughout the growing season.
