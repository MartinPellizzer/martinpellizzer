Trachyrhizum

Trachyrhizum, commonly known as the spider orchid, is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

These plants are characterized by their unique, often intricate flower structures and specialized root systems. Trachyrhizum species typically have long, thread-like roots that help them absorb moisture and nutrients from the surrounding environment. They are native to various regions in Australia and are often found in rocky or sandy soils.

The genus is noted for its diverse floral morphology, which includes colorful and sometimes fragrant blooms.
