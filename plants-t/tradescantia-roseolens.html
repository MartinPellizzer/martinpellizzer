Tradescantia roseolens

Tradescantia roseolens, commonly known as the pink wandering Jew, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and striking pink to purple variegated foliage. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.

Its ability to spread rapidly through runners makes it both ornamental and somewhat invasive in certain environments.
