Taraxacum vulgare var. pindicola

Taraxacum vulgare var. pindicola, commonly known as the Pindus dandelion, is a variant of the common dandelion found primarily in the Pindus Mountains of Greece.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its basal rosette of spoon-shaped leaves and a smooth, hollow stem. The plant produces a single, yellow, composite flower head that develops into a distinctive white, fluffy seed head known as a pappus. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the specific climatic conditions of its mountainous habitat.

This variety is of particular interest in botanical studies due to its geographic isolation and unique morphological traits.
