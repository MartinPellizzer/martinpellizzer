Tropaeolum maculatum

Tropaeolum maculatum, commonly known as the Indian cress or garden sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, edible flowers that come in various colors such as red, yellow, and orange. The plant is often grown for its ornamental value and is also cultivated for its peppery, tangy leaves that can be used in salads or as a seasoning. Tropaeolum maculatum thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is a short-lived perennial that can be grown as an annual in temperate climates.
