Tradescantia geniculata

Tradescantia geniculata, commonly known as the snake plant or mother-in-law's tongue, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright, sword-shaped leaves that are typically green with striking red or purple variegation along the edges. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or greenish flowers on slender spikes, which appear in late summer or early autumn. It is a hardy, low-maintenance plant that thrives in well-draining soil and indirect sunlight.

Tradescantia geniculata is also known for its air-purifying properties and ability to thrive in a variety of indoor environments.
