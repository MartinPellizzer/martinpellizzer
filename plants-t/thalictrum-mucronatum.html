Thalictrum mucronatum

Thalictrum mucronatum, commonly known as the blunt-tipped meadow rue, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically arranged in an upright inflorescence. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Thalictrum mucronatum is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
