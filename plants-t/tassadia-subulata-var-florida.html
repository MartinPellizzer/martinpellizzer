Tassadia subulata var. florida

Tassadia subulata var. florida, commonly known as the Florida tasselbush, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves and clusters of small, white to yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in wetlands. Its foliage is often covered in fine, hairy structures, which help retain moisture in humid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
