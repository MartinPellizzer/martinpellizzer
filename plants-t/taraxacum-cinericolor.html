Taraxacum cinericolor

Taraxacum cinericolor, commonly known as the silverleaf dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-gray, hairy leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces a hollow, slender stem that supports a single flower head, which develops into a ball of white, fluffy seeds. Its flowers are typically yellow, opening in the early morning and closing in the afternoon.

The species is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and light conditions.
