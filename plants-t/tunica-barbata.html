Tunica barbata

Tunica barbata, commonly known as the hairy tunicate, is a unique plant characterized by its distinctive, hairy, and often colorful outer covering.

This plant is native to specific regions in South America and thrives in humid, tropical environments. Its botanical features include a fleshy, succulent stem that stores water, aiding in its survival in arid conditions. The leaves are typically small, narrow, and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Tunica barbata is also noted for its ability to produce vibrant flowers, often in shades of red or orange, making it a visually striking species.
