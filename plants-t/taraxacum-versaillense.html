Taraxacum versaillense

Taraxacum versaillense, commonly known as the Versailles dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers and a distinctive, hollow, leafy stem. The plant is native to Europe and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. It produces a milky sap that is typical of the genus Taraxacum.

The fruit is a small, dry achene with a pappus, which aids in wind dispersal.
