Trichoon karka

Trichoon karka, commonly known as the Kashmiri thornapple, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its spiny stems and showy purple or white flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Trichoon karka thrives in arid and semi-arid regions, often growing along rocky slopes and in dry forests.

Its botanical features make it both a resilient species and a subject of interest in ecological studies.
