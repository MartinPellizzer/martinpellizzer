Trigonella pinnatifida

Trigonella pinnatifida, commonly known as the narrow-leaved fenugreek, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are pinnately divided, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in racemes. Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

Trigonella pinnatifida is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments.
