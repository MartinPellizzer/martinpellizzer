Tillandsia inopinata

Tillandsia inopinata, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a species of air plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its unique, elongated leaves that resemble ghostly appendages. This plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of other plants without deriving nutrients from them. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and relies on atmospheric moisture for hydration.

Its striking white or pink flowers bloom infrequently, making it a rare and sought-after specimen in botanical collections.
