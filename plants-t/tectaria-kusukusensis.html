Tectaria kusukusensis

Tectaria kusukusensis, commonly known as the Kusukus Island shield fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, hairy scales. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found on tree trunks or rocks in tropical rainforests. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, and it reproduces via spores rather than seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
