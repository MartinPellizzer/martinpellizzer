Thalictrum elatum f. macrocarpum

Thalictrum elatum f. macrocarpum, commonly known as the large-seeded meadow rue, is a botanical variant of the meadow rue genus.

It is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia, characterized by its delicate, fern-like foliage. The plant produces showy, clustered white or pale yellow flowers in early summer. Its fruits are distinctive, with large, fleshy, red or orange berries that mature in late summer.

This cultivar is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
