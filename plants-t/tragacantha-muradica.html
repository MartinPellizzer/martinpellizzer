Tragacantha muradica

Tragacantha muradica, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a perennial shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces a resinous sap, which is harvested for its medicinal and industrial applications. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their astringent properties.

Tragacantha muradica is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.
