Tithonia glutinosa

Tithonia glutinosa, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a tropical plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its large, bright orange to red flower heads and heart-shaped leaves. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions. It produces yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Tithonia glutinosa is often used in ornamental gardening and as a cover crop due to its hardy nature and ecological benefits.
