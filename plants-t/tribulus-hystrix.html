Tribulus hystrix

Tribulus hystrix, commonly known as the spiked goatee, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its spiny, branched stems and small, yellow flowers. The plant produces bright red, spherical fruits that contain several seeds. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

Tribulus hystrix is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
