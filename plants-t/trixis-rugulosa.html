Trixis rugulosa

Trixis rugulosa, commonly known as the rough trixis, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its low-growing, succulent stems and small, clustered leaves that are often covered in fine, rough hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a splash of color to its harsh environment. Trixis rugulosa is adapted to survive in drought conditions, storing water in its thick, fleshy leaves.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitat.
