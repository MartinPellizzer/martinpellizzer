Trigonella emodi var. podperae

Trigonella emodi var. podperae, commonly known as the Kashmiri fenugreek, is a variant of the fenugreek plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Pakistan.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces slender, elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine and culinary practices. Botanically, it belongs to the Fabaceae family, known for its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities.

This variety is particularly valued for its unique flavor and nutritional content.
