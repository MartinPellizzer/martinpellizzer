Tephrosia dura

Tephrosia dura, commonly known as hard tephrosia, is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is notable for its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. Its hardy nature allows it to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it adaptable to various environments.

Tephrosia dura is also valued for its potential use in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
