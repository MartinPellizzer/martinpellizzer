Tillaea drummondii

Tillaea drummondii, commonly known as Drummond's tillaea, is a small, mat-forming succulent native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink to white during the spring and summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and xeriscapes.
