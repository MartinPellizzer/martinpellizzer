Trachystoma labasii

Trachystoma labasii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees in its native range.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically less than 1 cm in length, and it produces tiny white or pale yellow flowers. The plant relies on its host for water and nutrients but can photosynthesize to some extent. It is found primarily in arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

Its unique ecological role makes it an important species in its native ecosystems.
