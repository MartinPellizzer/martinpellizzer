Tanacetum gmelinii

Tanacetum gmelinii, commonly known as the Siberian tansy, is a perennial herb native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features finely divided, aromatic leaves and produces yellowish to brownish flower heads that are often used in traditional medicine. Tanacetum gmelinii thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and steppe environments.

Its medicinal properties have been historically valued for their antiseptic and digestive benefits.
