Tetrapterys calophylla var. glabrior

Tetrapterys calophylla var. glabrior, commonly known as the smooth-leaved beautyleaf, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green, ovate leaves with a smooth, glabrous surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that split open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm, humid climates for its attractive foliage.
