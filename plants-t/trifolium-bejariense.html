Trifolium bejariense

Trifolium bejariense, commonly known as the Bejari clover, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open habitats.

Trifolium bejariense is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
