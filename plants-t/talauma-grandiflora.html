Talauma grandiflora

Talauma grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered talauma, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that can reach up to 10 centimeters in diameter. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and elliptical in shape, with a smooth surface.

Talauma grandiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant blooms and pleasant fragrance.
