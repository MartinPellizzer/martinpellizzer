Tibouchina salviifolia

Tibouchina salviifolia, commonly known as the purple mistflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring dense, evergreen foliage. The leaves are ovate to elliptical in shape, with a smooth texture and a glossy, dark green color. Its most striking feature is its clusters of showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn.

The plant is well-suited for ornamental use in gardens due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various soil types.
