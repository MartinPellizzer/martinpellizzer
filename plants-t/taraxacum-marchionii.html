Taraxacum marchionii

Taraxacum marchionii, commonly known as the Marchionni's dandelion, is a flowering plant species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and taproot system. The plant produces yellow ray florets that develop into fluffy white seeds with parachute-like structures for wind dispersal. Its leaves are typically basal, spoon-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Taraxacum marchionii is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
