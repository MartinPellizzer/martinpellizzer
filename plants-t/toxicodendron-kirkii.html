Toxicodendron kirkii

Toxicodendron kirkii, commonly known as the African lacquer tree, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers. The plant is known for its sap, which can cause dermatitis in humans upon contact. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Toxicodendron kirkii has traditional uses in medicine and is also valued for its ornamental qualities.
