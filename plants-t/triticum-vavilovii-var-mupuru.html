Triticum vavilovii var. mupuru

Triticum vavilovii var. mupuru, commonly known as the Mupuru wild wheat, is a wild relative of modern wheat native to Central Asia.

This plant is characterized by its robust growth habit and its ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions. It possesses a complex genome that includes multiple sets of chromosomes, contributing to its genetic diversity. The species is valued for its potential in breeding programs aimed at improving wheat resilience to drought and disease.

Its study provides insights into the evolutionary history of wheat and its adaptation to various ecological niches.
