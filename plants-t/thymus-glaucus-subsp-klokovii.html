Thymus glaucus subsp. klokovii

Thymus glaucus subsp.

klokovii, commonly known as Klovkov's thyme, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and grayish-blue foliage, which is covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, pale pink to white flowers in dense clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments.

This subspecies is valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in traditional herbal medicine.
