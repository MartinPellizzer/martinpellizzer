Tragus andicola

Tragus andicola, commonly known as the Andean tragus, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or mountainous terrain. Its leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

Tragus andicola is adapted to harsh, cold climates and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.
