Trichoscypha oliveri

Trichoscypha oliveri, commonly known as the African velvet tamarind, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Caesalpiniaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by distinctive, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal uses, it is of interest in botanical research and conservation efforts.
