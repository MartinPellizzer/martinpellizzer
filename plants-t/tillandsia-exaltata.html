Tillandsia exaltata

Tillandsia exaltata, commonly known as the Sky Plant, is a species of air plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a cup-like structure to collect rainwater. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on other plants without being parasitic, and absorbs moisture and nutrients through its leaves. Its flowers are typically pink or red and bloom in the summer months.

Tillandsia exaltata is popular as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
