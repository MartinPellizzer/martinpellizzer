Thlaspi viridisepalum

Thlaspi viridisepalum, commonly known as the green-sepal cabbageweed, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing annual herb with small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically has basal leaves that are oval to lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its seeds are small and contained within siliques, which are dry, dehiscent fruits.

Thlaspi viridisepalum is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in certain regions.
