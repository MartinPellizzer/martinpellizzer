Tillandsia arguta

Tillandsia arguta, commonly known as the silver tillandsia, is a species of air plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help in absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on other plants without being parasitic, often found in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are typically white or pale purple and bloom in the spring, adding ornamental value to the plant.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
