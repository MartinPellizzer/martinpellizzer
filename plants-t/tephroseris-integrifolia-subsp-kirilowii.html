Tephroseris integrifolia subsp. kirilowii

Tephroseris integrifolia subsp.

kirilowii, commonly known as the Kirilow's leafy tephroseris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia. It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, serrated leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a compact inflorescence, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun exposure.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
