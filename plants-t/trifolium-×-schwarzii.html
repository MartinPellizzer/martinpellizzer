Trifolium × schwarzii

Trifolium × schwarzii, commonly known as the Schwarz's clover, is a hybrid species within the genus Trifolium, which includes various types of clover.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, typically consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and is often found in grassy areas and disturbed soils. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that can range in color from white to pink, depending on the specific variety. It is a perennial herb that thrives in temperate climates and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.

Due to its hybrid nature, Trifolium × schwarzii exhibits a range of morphological variations, making it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
