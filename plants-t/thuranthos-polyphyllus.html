Thuranthos polyphyllus

Thuranthos polyphyllus, commonly known as the giant feather fern, is a striking plant characterized by its large, feathery fronds that can reach lengths of up to three meters.

Native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, it thrives in humid, shaded environments with high levels of moisture. The plant's fronds are composed of numerous narrow, elongated pinnae, giving it a distinctive, almost ethereal appearance. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in tropical gardens and greenhouses.

Due to its specific environmental requirements, it is considered a challenging plant to grow outside its native habitat.
