Tetratheca aphylla subsp. megacarpa

Tetratheca aphylla subsp.

megacarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited sandplain daisy, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of white, daisy-like flowers that bloom in spring.

The subspecies is distinguished by its enlarged, fleshy fruits, which are a key feature in its classification. It thrives in sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions with well-drained conditions.

This plant is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
