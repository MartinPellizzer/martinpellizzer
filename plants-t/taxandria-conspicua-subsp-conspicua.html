Taxandria conspicua subsp. conspicua

Taxandria conspicua subsp.

conspicua, commonly known as the showy taxandria, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States. It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage.

The plant produces striking white to pink flowers in late spring, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
