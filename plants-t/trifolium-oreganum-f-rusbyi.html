Trifolium oreganum f. rusbyi

Trifolium oreganum f. rusbyi, commonly known as the Rusby's white clover, is a botanical variant of the common white clover.

It is characterized by its smaller, more rounded leaves and pale white or pale blue flowers. This form is typically found in grassy areas and is adapted to temperate climates. The plant is a legume, capable of nitrogen fixation, which benefits soil fertility.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
