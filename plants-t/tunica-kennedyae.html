Tunica kennedyae

Tunica kennedyae, commonly known as the Kennedy's tunica, is a rare flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils along stream banks and in shaded woodlands. Its blooming period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.

Due to habitat loss, Tunica kennedyae is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
