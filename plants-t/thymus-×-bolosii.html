Thymus × bolosii

Thymus × bolosii, commonly known as the Bolosii thyme, is a hybrid shrub native to California, United States.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and aromatic, narrow, grey-green leaves. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers in the spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its limited natural range, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
