Taraxacum congestum

Taraxacum congestum, commonly known as the clustered dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and a flowering stem that bears a single, yellow composite flower head. The plant is native to parts of Europe and Asia, and it thrives in temperate climates. Its flowers develop into spherical, white pappus-bearing seed heads, which are dispersed by wind.

The species is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a common weed in various habitats.
