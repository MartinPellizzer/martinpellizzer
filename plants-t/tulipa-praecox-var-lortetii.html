Tulipa praecox var. lortetii

Tulipa praecox var. lortetii, commonly known as the Lortet's early tulip, is a botanical variety native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a bulbous perennial plant characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and striking, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The flowers typically appear in early spring, making it one of the earliest tulips to emerge. This variety is valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.
