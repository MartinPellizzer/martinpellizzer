Teucrium inflatum

Teucrium inflatum, commonly known as the inflated germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, pale blue to violet flowers in whorled clusters. Teucrium inflatum is typically found in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and dryland landscapes.
