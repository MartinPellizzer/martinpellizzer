Thermopsis argentata

Thermopsis argentata, commonly known as silverleaf thermopsis, is a herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs. The plant produces tall, slender stems that bear clusters of yellow flowers in early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as sagebrush steppe.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
