Thalictrum occidentale

Thalictrum occidentale, commonly known as western meadow-rue, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white or greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. It produces numerous slender, upright stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters.

Thalictrum occidentale is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits to pollinators.
