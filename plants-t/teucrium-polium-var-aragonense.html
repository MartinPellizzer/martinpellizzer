Teucrium polium var. aragonense

Teucrium polium var. aragonense, commonly known as Spanish germander, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in rocky or dry soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental flowers make it a valued plant in both horticulture and traditional medicine.
