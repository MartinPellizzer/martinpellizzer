Thymus serpyllum var. sylvestris

Thymus serpyllum var. sylvestris, commonly known as wild thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features tiny, pale green leaves that are densely packed along its creeping stems. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers in early summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy groundcover plant.

Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils that have been used in traditional herbal medicine and culinary applications.
