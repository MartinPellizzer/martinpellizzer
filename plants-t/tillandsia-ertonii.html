Tillandsia ertonii

Tillandsia ertonii, commonly known as the Silver Volute, is an epiphytic bromeliad native to the southeastern United States.

It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of white trichomes. These trichomes help the plant absorb moisture and nutrients from the air, allowing it to grow on trees without soil. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or orange, which bloom in late summer.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
