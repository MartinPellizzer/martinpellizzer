Thomsonia sumawongii

Thomsonia sumawongii, commonly known as the Sumawong mountain gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branched.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Thomsonia sumawongii is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
