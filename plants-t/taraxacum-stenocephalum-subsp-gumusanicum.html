Taraxacum stenocephalum subsp. gumusanicum

Taraxacum stenocephalum subsp.

gumusanicum, commonly known as the narrow-headed dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Turkey. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are basal, alternate, and often covered with fine hairs, while its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and ecological niche.
