Toxicodendron yunnanense var. longipaniculatum

Toxicodendron yunnanense var. longipaniculatum, commonly known as the Yunnan poison ivy, is a variant of the toxicodendron genus native to China.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It contains urushiol, a toxic oil that can cause severe skin irritation in humans.

This variant is often found in moist, shaded forest areas and is part of the Anacardiaceae family.
