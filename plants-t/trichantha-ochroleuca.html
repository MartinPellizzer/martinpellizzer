Trichantha ochroleuca

Trichantha ochroleuca, commonly known as the yellow-leaf trichantha, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the family Amaranthaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically yellowish-green in color. The plant produces clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers that are usually arranged in dense inflorescences. It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa, where it thrives in well-drained soils.

Trichantha ochroleuca is often found in rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
