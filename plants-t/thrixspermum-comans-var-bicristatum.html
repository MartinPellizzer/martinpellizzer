Thrixspermum comans var. bicristatum

Thrixspermum comans var. bicristatum, commonly known as the two-crowned thrixspermum, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form and produces clusters of small, fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are densely arranged along the stems. It thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in specialized gardens.
