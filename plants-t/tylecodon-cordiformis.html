Tylecodon cordiformis

Tylecodon cordiformis, commonly known as the heart-shaped ice plant, is a succulent species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, triangular leaves that are often tinged with red or purple, especially when exposed to sunlight. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the winter months, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy leaves.

Tylecodon cordiformis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
