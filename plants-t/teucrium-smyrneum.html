Teucrium smyrneum

Teucrium smyrneum, commonly known as Smyrna germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers arranged in whorled clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Teucrium smyrneum is also valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
