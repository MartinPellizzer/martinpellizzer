Trichilia cardenasii

Trichilia cardenasii, commonly known as the Mexican white cedar, is a member of the Meliaceae family.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to southern Mexico and Central America. The plant is characterized by its smooth, aromatic bark and lanceolate, glossy dark green leaves. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and borne in axillary clusters, while the fruit is a drupe with a smooth, leathery outer layer.

Trichilia cardenasii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
