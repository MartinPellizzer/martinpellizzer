Tanacetum arctodzhungaricum

Tanacetum arctodzhungaricum, commonly known as the Siberian tansy, is a perennial herb native to the cold climates of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and yellowish-green flower heads. The plant produces small, composite flowers that are typically arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence. It is often found in alpine and subarctic regions where it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

Tanacetum arctodzhungaricum is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
