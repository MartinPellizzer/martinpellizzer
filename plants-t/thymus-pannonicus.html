Thymus pannonicus

Thymus pannonicus, commonly known as the Pannonian thyme, is a perennial herb native to Central Europe and parts of the Balkans.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and showy flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its small, pink or purple flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are used in culinary and medicinal applications, known for their pungent, slightly minty aroma.
