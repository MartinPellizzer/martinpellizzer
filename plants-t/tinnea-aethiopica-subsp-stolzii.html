Tinnea aethiopica subsp. stolzii

Tinnea aethiopica subsp.

stolzii, commonly known as the Ethiopian tinnea, is a shrub native to the highland regions of Ethiopia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom during the dry season. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought conditions.

The subspecies is distinguished by its unique morphological features, including the presence of stolons, which aid in vegetative propagation.
