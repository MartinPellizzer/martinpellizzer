Thunia dodgsonii

Thunia dodgsonii, commonly known as the Dodgson's thunia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, fragrant flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in cloud forests. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, and it produces small, clustered blooms that are highly prized by orchid enthusiasts.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
