Tingulonga madagascariensis

Tingulonga madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar star vine, is a climbing plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its twining stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by legume pods containing seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique morphology and restricted distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
