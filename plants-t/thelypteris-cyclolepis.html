Thelypteris cyclolepis

Thelypteris cyclolepis, commonly known as the kidney fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Thelypteridaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, kidney-shaped fronds that are often lobed and have a feathery appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forests and rocky slopes. Its sporangia are clustered in sori on the underside of the fronds, a common feature in ferns.

Thelypteris cyclolepis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
