Taraxacum laevigatum subsp. calocephalum

Taraxacum laevigatum subsp.

calocephalum, commonly known as the smooth dandelion, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features a rosette of basal leaves that are smooth and hairless, with a distinctive yellow composite flower head.

The plant produces a hollow, wind-dispersed seed stem known as a pappus. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Botanically, it is characterized by its taproot system and is part of the Asteraceae family.
