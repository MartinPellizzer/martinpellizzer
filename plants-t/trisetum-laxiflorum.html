Trisetum laxiflorum

Trisetum laxiflorum, commonly known as the slender-flowered wood sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish-brown spikelets that are clustered at the ends of its stems. Trisetum laxiflorum is characterized by its delicate appearance and its ability to thrive in wetland habitats.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
