Tragacantha melaleuca

Tragacantha melaleuca, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which are clustered in spherical inflorescences. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. Its leaves are bipinnate, with small, feathery leaflets, and it produces pods that contain seeds.

The golden wattle is an important component of the Australian flora and holds cultural significance in the country.
