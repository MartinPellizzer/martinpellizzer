Tragacantha lanceolata

Tragacantha lanceolata, commonly known as the Persian gum tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its long, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of yellow flowers in the spring. The plant is well-known for its resinous sap, which is harvested to make a gum used in various industries. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

Tragacantha lanceolata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in traditional medicine.
