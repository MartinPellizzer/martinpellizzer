Ternatea portobellensis

Ternatea portobellensis, commonly known as the Portobello fern, is a unique member of the Ternataceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, bipinnate leaves that form a feathery appearance, often with a bluish-green hue. Native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, this plant thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its reproductive structures are small and clustered, producing spores rather than seeds.

Ternatea portobellensis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ease of cultivation in controlled indoor settings.
