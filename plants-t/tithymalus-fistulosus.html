Tithymalus fistulosus

Tithymalus fistulosus, commonly known as the hollow thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their fragrant leaves and flowers. The plant is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are aromatic and often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Tithymalus fistulosus thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable plant for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
