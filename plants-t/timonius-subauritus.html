Timonius subauritus

Timonius subauritus, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Timonius subauritus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
