Tilia × eudimidiata

Tilia × eudimidiata, commonly known as the hybrid linden, is a deciduous tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Tiliaceae family and is a hybrid between Tilia cordata and Tilia platyphyllos. The tree is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. It produces small, round fruits that are typically inedible for humans but serve as a food source for birds and small mammals.

This species is often planted in urban landscapes for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
