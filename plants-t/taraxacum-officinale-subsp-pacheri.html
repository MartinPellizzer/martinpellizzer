Taraxacum officinale subsp. pacheri

Taraxacum officinale subsp.

pacheri, commonly known as the Alpine dandelion, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central and Southern Europe. It is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and basal rosettes of lanceolate, dark green leaves.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers that develop into spiky, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in rocky and alpine habitats, often growing at elevations above 1,500 meters.

This subspecies is notable for its hardy nature and ability to tolerate cold and poor soil conditions.
