Toxanthera natalensis

Toxanthera natalensis, commonly known as Natal grass, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ornamental gardening or for erosion control.
