Trifolium leibergii

Trifolium leibergii, commonly known as Leiberg's clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or red in color. This plant is native to parts of Europe and Asia and thrives in temperate climates. It is often found in grassy areas, along roadsides, and in disturbed soils due to its adaptability.

Trifolium leibergii is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it an important plant for soil enrichment.
