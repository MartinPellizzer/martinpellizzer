Tragacantha webbiana

Tragacantha webbiana, commonly known as the Webb's tragacanth, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by pod-like fruits containing seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Tragacantha webbiana is valued for its resilience and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
