Tulipa apula

Tulipa apula, commonly known as the Apulian tulip, is a species native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its striking, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from pale yellow to deep red. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge in early spring.

Tulipa apula is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
