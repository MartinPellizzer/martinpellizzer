Taraxacum moldavicum

Taraxacum moldavicum, commonly known as the Moldavian dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust taproot system. The plant features basal rosettes of stiff, hairy leaves that are typically green with a bluish tinge. Its yellow flowers develop into fluffy white seed heads, which are dispersed by the wind.

Taraxacum moldavicum is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.
