Telanthera argentea var. bracteata

Telanthera argentea var. bracteata, commonly known as silverleaf telanthera, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, white hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in warm climates.
