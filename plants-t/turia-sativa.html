Turia sativa

Turia sativa, commonly known as the common wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions.

It is characterized by its fine, blade-like leaves and produces small, greenish flowers. The plant is rich in nutrients, including vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants, making it popular in health and wellness contexts. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun for optimal growth.

Turia sativa is often used in herbal remedies and as a forage crop for livestock.
