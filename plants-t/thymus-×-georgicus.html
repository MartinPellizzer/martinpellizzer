Thymus × georgicus

Thymus × georgicus, commonly known as the hybrid thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant leaves that are typically gray-green in color, and it produces clusters of tiny, pink or white flowers. The plant is characterized by its aromatic essential oils, which are concentrated in the leaves and stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Thymus × georgicus is also valued for its culinary and medicinal uses.
