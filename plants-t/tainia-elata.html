Tainia elata

Tainia elata, commonly known as the giant dayflower, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom in clusters, often with vibrant colors such as red, orange, and yellow. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are typically large and glossy, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Tainia elata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
