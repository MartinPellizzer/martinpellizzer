Tribulus robustus

Tribulus robustus, commonly known as the devil's claw, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and spiny, claw-like fruits that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are alternate and often have a rough texture, while its stems are woody and branching.

Tribulus robustus is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
