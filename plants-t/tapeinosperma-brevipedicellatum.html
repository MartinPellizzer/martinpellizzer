Tapeinosperma brevipedicellatum

Tapeinosperma brevipedicellatum, commonly known as the small-stemmed sand mat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant typically forms dense mats close to the ground, with small, rounded leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. Its root system is adapted to retain moisture in dry environments, making it well-suited for harsh, sandy soils.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small desert organisms.
