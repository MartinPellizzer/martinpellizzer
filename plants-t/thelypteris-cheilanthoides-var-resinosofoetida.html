Thelypteris cheilanthoides var. resinosofoetida

Thelypteris cheilanthoides var. resinosofoetida, commonly known as the resinous holly fern, is a perennial fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its leathery, dark green fronds with a distinctive resinous odor. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest undergrowth. Its sori are round and arranged in a single row along the margins of the pinnae.

The varietal name "resinosofoetida" refers to its aromatic, resinous scent, which is a notable feature of this species.
