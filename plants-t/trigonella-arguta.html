Trigonella arguta

Trigonella arguta, commonly known as the desert fenugreek, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia and Africa.

It is a small annual herb that grows up to 30 centimeters in height, characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and it has a deep taproot system that allows it to survive in dry conditions. Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine and are rich in nutrients such as proteins and minerals.

Trigonella arguta is well adapted to harsh environments, making it an important component of desert ecosystems.
