Turnera caroliniana

Turnera caroliniana, commonly known as the Carolina turnera, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub-like perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite, elliptic leaves with a slightly waxy texture and a distinctive yellow-orange flower that blooms in the spring. Its flowers are clustered in terminal panicles and are known for their showy appearance.

The plant is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and vibrant blooms.
