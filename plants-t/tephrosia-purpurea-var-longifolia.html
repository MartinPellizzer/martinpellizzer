Tephrosia purpurea var. longifolia

Tephrosia purpurea var. longifolia, commonly known as purple tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and pairs of opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, often with a distinctive violet hue. Its roots contain compounds used in traditional medicine, and it is also utilized as a forage plant for livestock.

The species is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in semi-arid conditions.
