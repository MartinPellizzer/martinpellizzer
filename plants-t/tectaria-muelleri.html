Tectaria muelleri

Tectaria muelleri, commonly known as the Mueller's tectaria, is a species of fern belonging to the family Tectariaceae.

It is characterized by its large, pinnate fronds that can reach lengths of up to 1 meter. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as tropical rainforests and humid woodlands. Its rhizomes are often covered in a dense, velvety layer of rhizome scales.

The spore-producing structures, or sporangia, are clustered in sori on the undersides of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy.
