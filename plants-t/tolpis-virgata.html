Tolpis virgata

Tolpis virgata, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy yellow flowers with red centers. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and has a rosette of basal leaves. It produces small, linear leaves and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its vibrant blooms.

Tolpis virgata is adapted to dry conditions and is drought-tolerant.
