Triticum boeoticum var. viridihaussknechtii

Triticum boeoticum var. viridihaussknechtii, commonly known as the green Hausknecht wild wheat, is a rare and less-studied variant of the wild wheat species.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with narrow, green leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, often in temperate regions. Its genetic makeup suggests a close relationship to other wheat species, potentially offering insights into wheat evolution.

Due to its limited distribution and lack of extensive research, it remains a subject of interest for botanists and geneticists.
