Terminalia solomonensis

Terminalia solomonensis, commonly known as Solomon's terminalia, is a deciduous tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical in shape. The tree produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by woody, spherical fruits containing several seeds. Its bark is thick and fissured, and it is often found in lowland rainforests and secondary growth areas.

The plant is valued for its timber and has traditional uses in local medicine.
