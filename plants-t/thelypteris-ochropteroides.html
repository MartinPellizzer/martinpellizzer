Thelypteris ochropteroides

Thelypteris ochropteroides, commonly known as the golden shield fern, is a species of fern native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Thelypteridaceae and is characterized by its shield-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its fronds are bi- or tri-pinnate, with a distinctive golden hue on the underside.

Thelypteris ochropteroides is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in maintaining forest floor ecosystems.
