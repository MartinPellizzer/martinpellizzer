Triumfetta lebrunii

Triumfetta lebrunii, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry or golden gooseberry, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in warm, arid climates. The plant produces small, orange to yellow fruits that are edible and often used in culinary applications. Its flowers are small and white, with five petals, and it is known for its ability to tolerate salt spray.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to local wildlife.
