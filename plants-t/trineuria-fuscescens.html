Trineuria fuscescens

Trineuria fuscescens, commonly known as the black willow, is a species of flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows along stream banks and in moist woodland areas. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are oval to lance-shaped with a smooth margin. Its flowers are small and clustered in catkins, which are characteristic of willow species.

Trineuria fuscescens is known for its ability to thrive in wet soils and its role in stabilizing riverbanks.
