Taraxacum libanoticum

Taraxacum libanoticum, commonly known as the Lebanese dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region, including parts of Lebanon, Syria, and Turkey. The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are typically hairy and lobed, with a thick, succulent stem. It produces a yellow composite flower head that blooms in the spring and summer months.

The seeds are dispersed by wind via a parachute-like structure, similar to other dandelion species.
