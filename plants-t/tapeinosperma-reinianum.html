Tapeinosperma reinianum

Tapeinosperma reinianum, commonly known as the African milk tree, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by pod-like fruits containing a milky sap, which is the source of its common name. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.

The sap has been traditionally used in local remedies for various ailments.
