Tractema ramburii

Tractema ramburii, commonly known as the Rambur's thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and can be found in open woodlands and grasslands. The plant features a spiny, upright stem with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and are arranged in a dense, spherical cluster, attracting various pollinators.

Tractema ramburii is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
