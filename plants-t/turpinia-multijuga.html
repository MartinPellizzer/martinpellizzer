Turpinia multijuga

Turpinia multijuga, commonly known as the Chinese sandalwood, is a small evergreen tree native to southern China.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and fragrant, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by oval, woody fruits containing pale yellow seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its aromatic properties, it has been used in traditional Chinese medicine and as a source of essential oils.
