Zygophyllidium macropodoides

Zygophyllidium macropodoides, commonly known as the broad-footed zygophyll, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Zygophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its small, clustered leaves and yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is typically found in arid and semi-arid regions, thriving in sandy or rocky soils. It has a low-growing habit and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its drought tolerance and unique appearance.
