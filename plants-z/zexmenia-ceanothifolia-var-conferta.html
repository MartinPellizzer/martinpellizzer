Zexmenia ceanothifolia var. conferta

Zexmenia ceanothifolia var. conferta, commonly known as the dense zexmenia, is a flowering plant native to the western regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The leaves are often narrow and arranged in a dense, foliage-rich pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

It is valued in horticulture for its resilience and aesthetic qualities in garden settings.
