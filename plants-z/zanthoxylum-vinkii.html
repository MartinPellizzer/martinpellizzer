Zanthoxylum vinkii

Zanthoxylum vinkii, commonly known as the Vink's prickly ash, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are usually odd-pinnate with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a red, fleshy drupe that contains several seeds.

Zanthoxylum vinkii is often found in forested areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
