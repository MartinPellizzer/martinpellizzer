Zerna variegata subsp. villosula

Zerna variegata subsp.

villosula, commonly known as the hairy zerna, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of Asia. It is characterized by its distinctive variegated foliage, which features a mix of green and white or yellow markings.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its flowers are small and clustered, often producing a subtle fragrance. Zerna variegata subsp.

villosula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
