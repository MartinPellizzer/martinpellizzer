Ziziphora fominii

Ziziphora fominii, commonly known as the Fomin's savory, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their aromatic and purportedly soothing properties.

Ziziphora fominii thrives in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
