Zephyranthes sessilis var. striata

Zephyranthes sessilis var. striata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf rain lily, is a species of flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetlands and along the edges of water bodies. The plant features strap-like leaves that are narrow and grass-like in appearance. Its flowers are small, white, and often appear in clusters, blooming during the rainy season.

This variety is distinguished by its striated leaf patterns, which give it its specific name.
