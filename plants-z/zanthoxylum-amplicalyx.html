Zanthoxylum amplicalyx

Zanthoxylum amplicalyx, commonly known as the large-fruited prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its compound leaves and spiny stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries, which are an important food source for wildlife.
