Zapoteca caracasana

Zapoteca caracasana, commonly known as the Caracasana tree, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters in height, with a broad, spreading canopy. The leaves are typically large, simple, and alternate, often with a glossy green upper surface. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, and it produces fruit that contains several seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local communities.
