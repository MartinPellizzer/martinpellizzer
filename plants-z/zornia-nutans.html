Zornia nutans

Zornia nutans, commonly known as the nodding zornia, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, pea-like flowers that are often pink or purple in color. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or water.

Zornia nutans is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a food source for various insects and wildlife.
