Ziziphus quadrilocularis

Ziziphus quadrilocularis, commonly known as the Chinese date or the Indian date, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It produces small, yellowish-green fruits that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. The plant has compound leaves with multiple leaflets and is characterized by its woody stems and thorny branches. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in dry, sandy soils.

The fruit contains a high amount of sugars and is valued for its nutritional and medicinal properties.
