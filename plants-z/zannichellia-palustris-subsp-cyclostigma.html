Zannichellia palustris subsp. cyclostigma

Zannichellia palustris subsp.

cyclostigma, commonly known as the marsh bladderwort, is a small aquatic plant characterized by its delicate, bladder-like structures called pneumatocysts. These specialized organs help the plant float and facilitate gas exchange in water.

It typically grows in shallow, still or slow-moving freshwater habitats such as marshes and ponds. The plant has small, scale-like leaves and produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged.

It plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and small invertebrates.
