Zanthoxylum pauciflorum

Zanthoxylum pauciflorum, commonly known as the few-flowered prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically 5 to 9 pairs of ovate to lanceolate leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries that are attractive to birds. Its bark is thick and often cracked, with a distinctive aromatic scent.

Zanthoxylum pauciflorum is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in moist, wooded areas.
