Zephyranthes dichromantha

Zephyranthes dichromantha, commonly known as the pink rain lily, is a striking bulbous plant native to the Caribbean and northern South America.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in response to rainfall. The plant is adapted to wet tropical environments and often grows in shaded, moist areas. Its flowers are short-lived but highly fragrant, attracting pollinators such as bees and moths.

Zephyranthes dichromantha is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
