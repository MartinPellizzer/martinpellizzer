Zannichellia palustris var. stipitata

Zannichellia palustris var. stipitata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved waterweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in shallow, slow-moving waters and is characterized by its narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-yellow in color, which develop into small, rounded fruits. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Due to its preference for clean water, it is often used as an indicator species for water quality.
