Zanthoxylum tachuelo

Zanthoxylum tachuelo, commonly known as the Tachuelo prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnate and covered in fine, stiff hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife. Zanthoxylum tachuelo is adapted to high-altitude environments and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
