Zingeria biebersteiniana

Zingeria biebersteiniana, commonly known as the rock rose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces showy, pink or red flowers. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are adapted to arid conditions, making it drought-tolerant. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Zingeria biebersteiniana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature.
