Zannichellia pedicellata subsp. cothenetii

Zannichellia pedicellata subsp.

cothenetii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved water violet, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to Europe. It typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and ditches.

The plant features slender, branching stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often submerged or floating. Its small, white or pale yellow flowers appear in clusters and bloom from late spring to early autumn.

This subspecies is characterized by its delicate appearance and is often found in moist, shaded habitats.
