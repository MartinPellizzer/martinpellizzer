Ziziphus lucida

Ziziphus lucida, commonly known as the desert date or Indian date tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and small, oval-shaped leaves that turn red in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that give way to edible, fleshy fruits resembling dates. Ziziphus lucida is adapted to thrive in hot, dry climates with poor soil conditions.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of food and forage in desert ecosystems.
