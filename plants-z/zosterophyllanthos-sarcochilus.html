Zosterophyllanthos sarcochilus

Zosterophyllanthos sarcochilus, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surfaces of trees without deriving nutrients from them, relying instead on air and rain for sustenance. Its botanical features make it highly sought after by botanists and conservationists due to its ecological significance and declining population.
