Zinnia fulva

Zinnia fulva, commonly known as the Mexican zinnia, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red flowers, which bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with narrow, linear leaves that are gray-green in color. Its flowers are composed of ray florets surrounding a central disk, and it produces small, dry fruits called achenes.

Zinnia fulva is a member of the Asteraceae family, also known as the sunflower family.
