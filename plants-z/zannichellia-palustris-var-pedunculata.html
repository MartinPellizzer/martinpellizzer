Zannichellia palustris var. pedunculata

Zannichellia palustris var. pedunculata, commonly known as the marsh threadleaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater habitats such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams. The plant has slender, branching stems with small, opposite leaves that are often ribbon-like in shape. Its flowers are unisexual, with male and female flowers on separate plants, and it produces small, greenish fruits that disperse via water.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
