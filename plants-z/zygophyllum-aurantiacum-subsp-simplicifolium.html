Zygophyllum aurantiacum subsp. simplicifolium

Zygophyllum aurantiacum subsp.

simplicifolium, commonly known as the red sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia. It is characterized by its low, sprawling growth habit and small, fleshy leaves that help reduce water loss in hot, dry environments.

The plant produces clusters of bright orange to red flowers, which are adapted for pollination by insects. Its succulent leaves and stems store water, making it well-suited for survival in nutrient-poor soils.

This species is often found in coastal dunes and sandy plains, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small fauna.
