Ziziphus elegans

Ziziphus elegans, commonly known as the elegant ziziphus, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems, compound leaves, and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or orange when mature, which are edible but have a tart flavor. Ziziphus elegans is adapted to thrive in dry climates and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

Its botanical features make it a resilient species suitable for xeriscaping and ecological restoration projects.
