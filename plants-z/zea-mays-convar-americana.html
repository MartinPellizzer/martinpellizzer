Zea mays convar. americana

Zea mays convar. americana, commonly known as corn, is a tall, annual grass that belongs to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, leafy stalks and large, tassel-like inflorescences at the top of the plant. The plant produces ears of grain, which are surrounded by husks and contain rows of kernels. These kernels can be either starchy or sweet, depending on the variety.

It is a staple crop worldwide, cultivated for its nutritional value and versatility in food and industrial uses.
