Zeuxine grandis

Zeuxine grandis, commonly known as the giant zigzag gentian, is a striking flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, showy blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is known for its complex, zigzagging inflorescence. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

Zeuxine grandis is often found in moist, shaded habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
