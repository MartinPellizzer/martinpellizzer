Zea vaginata

Zea vaginata, commonly known as the banana grass, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, grass-like stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that develop into elongated, finger-like fruits resembling bananas. Its fibrous stalks are often used in traditional crafts and as a source of fiber.

Zea vaginata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
