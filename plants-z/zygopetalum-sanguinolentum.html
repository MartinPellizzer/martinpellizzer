Zygopetalum sanguinolentum

Zygopetalum sanguinolentum, commonly known as the blood-red zygopetalum, is a member of the Orchidaceae family native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety petals that range in color from deep red to orange, with a distinctive dark red lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are fragrant and bloom during the warmer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in greenhouses and botanical gardens for its vibrant and unique appearance.
