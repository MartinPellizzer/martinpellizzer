Zantedeschia melanoleuca

Zantedeschia melanoleuca, commonly known as the black-leaved calla, is a striking aquatic plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, white, funnel-shaped flowers that contrast beautifully with its dark, velvety leaves. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in wetlands and marshy areas. Its leaves are broad and elliptical, with a distinctive dark green to almost black coloration.

Zantedeschia melanoleuca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in garden ponds and water features.
