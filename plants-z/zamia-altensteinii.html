Zamia altensteinii

Zamia altensteinii, commonly known as the Altenstein's cycad, is a member of the Zamiaceae family and is native to Mexico.

It is a dioecious plant, meaning it has separate male and female individuals, and is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and pinnate leaves. The plant produces cone-like structures, with male cones bearing microsporangia and female cones containing megasporangia.

Zamia altensteinii is adapted to arid and rocky environments, showcasing its resilience to harsh conditions.
