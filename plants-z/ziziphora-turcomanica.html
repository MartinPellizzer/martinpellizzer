Ziziphora turcomanica

Ziziphora turcomanica, commonly known as Turkish hyssop, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported therapeutic properties.

Ziziphora turcomanica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
