Zingiber paucipunctatum

Zingiber paucipunctatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ginger, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers and related plants. The plant features narrow, elongated leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish flowers. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

The botanical characteristics of Zingiber paucipunctatum make it an interesting subject for botanical study and horticultural cultivation.
