Zea mays var. japonica

Zea mays var. japonica, commonly known as the Japanese corn or Japanese maize, is a variant of maize native to Japan.

It is a tall, slender grass with narrow, elongated leaves and produces tassels that are often more pronounced than in other maize varieties. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 meters and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a robust stem that supports its characteristic inflorescence.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in Japanese agriculture.
