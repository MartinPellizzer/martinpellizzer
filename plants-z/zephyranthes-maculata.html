Zephyranthes maculata

Zephyranthes maculata, commonly known as the pink rain lily, is a species of flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Central America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces delicate, showy flowers in shades of pink and white, often blooming after rainfall. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. It is known for its ability to bloom quickly after rain, earning it its common name.

Zephyranthes maculata is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ease of care.
