Zanthoxylum trichilioides

Zanthoxylum trichilioides, commonly known as the African prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often pinnately arranged and have serrated margins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences, followed by red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife. Zanthoxylum trichilioides is also notable for its thorny stems and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the ecosystems where it is found.
