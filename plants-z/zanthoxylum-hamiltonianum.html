Zanthoxylum hamiltonianum

Zanthoxylum hamiltonianum, commonly known as the Chinese prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnately arranged with serrated margins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. Its bark and fruits contain bioactive compounds, making it valuable in traditional medicine.

Zanthoxylum hamiltonianum thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or scrubland habitats.
