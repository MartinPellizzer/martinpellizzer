Zerna ramosa

Zerna ramosa, commonly known as the branched zerna, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of Asia.

It features a slender, upright stem that often branches profusely, giving it a bushy appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in spike-like clusters, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy surface.

Zerna ramosa is valued in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and digestive properties.
