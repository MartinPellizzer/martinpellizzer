Zizania aquatica var. aquatica

Zizania aquatica var. aquatica, commonly known as wild rice, is a aquatic grass native to the wetlands of North America.

It thrives in shallow, freshwater environments and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces edible grains that are harvested by indigenous communities and are valued for their nutritional content. It plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.

Its presence is often an indicator of healthy, unpolluted water bodies.
