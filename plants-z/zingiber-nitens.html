Zingiber nitens

Zingiber nitens, commonly known as the white ginger or white cardamom, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other economically important plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant is characterized by its tall, erect rhizomes and narrow, linear leaves. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in inflorescences.

While not widely cultivated for its rhizomes, it is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
