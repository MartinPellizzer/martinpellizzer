Zygophyllum garipense

Zygophyllum garipense, commonly known as the desert raisin or red sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Zygophyllaceae and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and succulent, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is well adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and drought conditions. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access deep water sources, and it often serves as a pioneer species in disturbed or degraded habitats.

Zygophyllum garipense plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing food for various desert animals.
