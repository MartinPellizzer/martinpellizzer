Zeia biflora

Zeia biflora, commonly known as the twin-flower plant, is a flowering species native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, delicate flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Zeia biflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
