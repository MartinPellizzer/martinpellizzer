Zingiber pellitum

Zingiber pellitum, commonly known as the golden ginger, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers and turmeric. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The botanical characteristics of Zingiber pellitum make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and medicinal research.
