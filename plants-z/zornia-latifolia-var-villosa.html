Zornia latifolia var. villosa

Zornia latifolia var. villosa, commonly known as hairy broadleaf zornia, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, hairy texture, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in loose clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas.

Zornia latifolia var. villosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
