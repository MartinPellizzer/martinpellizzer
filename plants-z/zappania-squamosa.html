Zappania squamosa

Zappania squamosa, commonly known as the scaly zappania, is a small, perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its composite flower heads and typically herbaceous growth habit. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine, scaly structures, giving it its common name. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in dense clusters, and it produces achene fruits.

Zappania squamosa is typically found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
