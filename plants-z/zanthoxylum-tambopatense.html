Zanthoxylum tambopatense

Zanthoxylum tambopatense, commonly known as the Tambopata prickly ash, is a member of the Rutaceae family and is native to the Amazon rainforest of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub, typically reaching heights of up to 10 meters. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often pinnate and have a distinct, aromatic scent. Its flowers are small and yellowish-green, arranged in axillary clusters, and it produces red, fleshy berries that are a food source for various wildlife.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects.
