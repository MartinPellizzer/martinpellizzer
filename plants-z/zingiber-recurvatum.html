Zingiber recurvatum

Zingiber recurvatum, commonly known as the black ginger, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other economically important plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with a distinctive recurved shape, and it produces small, edible tubers.

Zingiber recurvatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has potential uses in traditional medicine.
