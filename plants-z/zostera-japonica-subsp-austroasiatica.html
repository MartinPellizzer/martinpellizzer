Zostera japonica subsp. austroasiatica

Zostera japonica subsp.

austroasiatica, commonly known as the Asian eelgrass, is a seagrass species native to coastal regions of East Asia. It typically grows in shallow, brackish waters and is characterized by its narrow, ribbon-like leaves.

The plant forms dense underwater meadows that provide habitat and food for various marine organisms. It plays a crucial role in stabilizing sediments and improving water quality in coastal ecosystems. Zostera japonica subsp.

austroasiatica is also valued for its potential in aquaculture and ecological restoration projects.
