Zeuxine atrorubens

Zeuxine atrorubens, commonly known as the red-tongued grass of Africa, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green with prominent red stamens, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Zeuxine atrorubens is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
