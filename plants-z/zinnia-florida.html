Zinnia florida

Zinnia florida, commonly known as the common zinnia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy blooms that come in a variety of colors, including red, yellow, orange, and white. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Zinnia florida belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
