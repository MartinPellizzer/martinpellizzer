Zingiber kerrii

Zingiber kerrii, commonly known as the Kerri's ginger, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers and turmeric. The plant features a rhizome that is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, while its flowers are typically yellow or orange with a spicy fragrance.

Zingiber kerrii is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its potential medicinal properties.
