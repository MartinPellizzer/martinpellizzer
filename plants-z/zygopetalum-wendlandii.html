Zygopetalum wendlandii

Zygopetalum wendlandii, commonly known as the Wendland's zygopetalum, is a species of orchid native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that typically display a combination of red, orange, and yellow hues. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to reduce water loss, and it produces fragrant blooms during the spring and summer months.

Zygopetalum wendlandii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
