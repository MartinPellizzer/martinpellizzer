Zygopetalum candidum

Zygopetalum candidum, commonly known as the white zygopetalum orchid, is a species native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its upright, branching stems and large, showy flowers. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, with a distinctive lip that is often marked with contrasting patterns. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This orchid is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
