Ziziphus leucodermis

Ziziphus leucodermis, commonly known as the white-skinned date palm, is a species native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a small, shrub-like plant that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, fleshy fruits that are often consumed by both humans and animals. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and the plant has a deep root system that helps it survive in dry conditions.

Ziziphus leucodermis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
