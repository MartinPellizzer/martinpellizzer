Zygopetalum amazonicum

Zygopetalum amazonicum, commonly known as the Amazon orchid, is a species native to the rainforests of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, showy flowers. The plant typically blooms in the summer months, producing fragrant, violet-colored flowers with a unique lip structure. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the nutrient-poor soil of its natural habitat.

Zygopetalum amazonicum is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in warm, humid environments.
