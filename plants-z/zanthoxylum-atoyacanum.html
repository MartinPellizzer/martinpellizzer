Zanthoxylum atoyacanum

Zanthoxylum atoyacanum, commonly known as the Mexican prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its spiny branches and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. Its bark and leaves contain compounds with medicinal properties, traditionally used by indigenous peoples for various ailments.

Zanthoxylum atoyacanum thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in dry forests and scrublands.
