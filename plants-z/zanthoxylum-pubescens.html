Zanthoxylum pubescens

Zanthoxylum pubescens, commonly known as the hairy prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces red berries that are edible for some wildlife but contain toxic compounds for humans. Its bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

The species is notable for its thorny stems and adaptability to various soil types.
