Zerna bikfayensis

Zerna bikfayensis, commonly known as the Yemeni millet, is a grass species native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces tiny grains that are primarily used as a staple food in traditional diets. Zerna bikfayensis thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climates.

Its botanical features make it an important crop for local agriculture and food security in the region.
