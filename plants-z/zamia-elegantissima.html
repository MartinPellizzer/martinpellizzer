Zamia elegantissima

Zamia elegantissima, commonly known as the elegant cycad, is a species of cycad native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its stout, unbranched stem and a crown of large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces cones rather than flowers, with male and female cones occurring on separate plants. Its leaves are typically a deep green and can grow up to 2 meters in length.

Zamia elegantissima is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively low maintenance in suitable climates.
