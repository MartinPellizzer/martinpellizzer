Zenopogon herrmanniae

Zenopogon herrmanniae, commonly known as the Hermann's sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as wetlands and forest floors. The plant has slender, grass-like stems and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. Its leaves are linear and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
