Zygophyllum miniatum var. englerianum

Zygophyllum miniatum var. englerianum, commonly known as the Engler's dwarf chenopod, is a low-growing shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and clustered, pale yellow to red flowers. The plant is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in harsh environments. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Zygophyllum miniatum var. englerianum plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for small desert animals.
