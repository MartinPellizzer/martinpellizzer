Zephyranthes irwiniana

Zephyranthes irwiniana, commonly known as the rain lily, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States and parts of Central America.

It features strap-like, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple. The plant is known for its ability to bloom after rainfall, often appearing suddenly in open areas. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Zephyranthes irwiniana is also valued for its resilience and low maintenance requirements.
