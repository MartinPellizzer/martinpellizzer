Zingiber molle

Zingiber molle, commonly known as the soft ginger or white ginger, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other economically important plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant features a rhizomatous root system and produces clusters of fragrant, pale yellow to white flowers. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and covered with a waxy cuticle.

Zingiber molle is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
