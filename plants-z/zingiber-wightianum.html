Zingiber wightianum

Zingiber wightianum, commonly known as the Wight's ginger, is a flowering plant native to the wet tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers such as turmeric and cardamom. The plant produces striking, sword-shaped leaves and vivid orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine and as a spice, similar to other ginger species.

Zingiber wightianum thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its medicinal properties.
