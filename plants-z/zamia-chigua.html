Zamia chigua

Zamia chigua, commonly known as the Mexican cycad, is a member of the Zamiaceae family and is native to Mexico.

It is a slow-growing, evergreen plant with a thick, woody trunk and a crown of stiff, grass-like leaves. The plant produces cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant.
