Zygopetalum stapelioides

Zygopetalum stapelioides, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and unique epiphytic plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, ghostly white flowers that bloom in a spiral pattern. The plant lacks traditional leaves and instead has modified stems that perform photosynthesis. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires specific conditions to flourish.

Due to its rarity and aesthetic appeal, it is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
