Ziziphus espinosus

Ziziphus espinosus, commonly known as the spiny date tree or desert date, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, the Middle East, and southern Europe.

It features woody stems with sharp spines and small, yellowish-green leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, red fruits that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. It is adapted to survive in harsh environments with minimal water, making it a resilient species in desert ecosystems.

Ziziphus espinosus plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing food for wildlife in its native habitats.
