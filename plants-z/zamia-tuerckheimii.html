Zamia tuerckheimii

Zamia tuerckheimii, commonly known as the Tuerckheim's cycad, is a member of the Zamiaceae family and is native to Mexico.

It is a slow-growing, evergreen plant characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and pinnate, feather-like leaves. The plant produces large, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
