Zaluzania robinsonii

Zaluzania robinsonii, commonly known as the Robinson's zayre, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as prairies and woodland edges.

Its botanical structure supports its role in local ecosystems, providing nectar for pollinators.
