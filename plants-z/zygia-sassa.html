Zygia sassa

Zygia sassa, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in dry, sandy soils. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in late spring to early summer. Zygia sassa has small, narrow leaves and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
