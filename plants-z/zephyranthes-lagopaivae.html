Zephyranthes lagopaivae

Zephyranthes lagopaivae, commonly known as the pink rain lily, is a striking bulbous plant native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It produces delicate, pink or white flowers that bloom in response to rainfall, typically during the summer months. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and a compact growth habit, making it well-suited for rock gardens or containers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring minimal water once established.

Its unique flowering behavior and ornamental appeal make it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.
