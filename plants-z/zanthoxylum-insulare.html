Zanthoxylum insulare

Zanthoxylum insulare, commonly known as the island prickly ash, is a shrub native to the Caribbean islands.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its bark and leaves contain compounds with potential medicinal properties.

Zanthoxylum insulare thrives in tropical and subtropical environments, often growing in coastal areas and disturbed soils.
