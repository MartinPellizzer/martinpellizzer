Zea mays subsp. saccharata

Zea mays subsp.

saccharata, commonly known as sweet corn, is a subspecies of maize characterized by its high sugar content and edible kernels. It is an annual grass that typically grows to a height of 1.5 to 2 meters, with broad, flat leaves and tassels at the top.

The plant produces ears of corn that develop from the female inflorescence, with each ear containing multiple rows of kernels. Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is native to the Americas.

Its genetic makeup includes traits that make it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.
