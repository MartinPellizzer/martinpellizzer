Zygophyllum ovatum

Zygophyllum ovatum, commonly known as the smooth sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the family Zygophyllaceae and is characterized by its low-growing, succulent-like structure adapted to desert environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are usually small and fleshy, helping to conserve water in dry conditions.

Zygophyllum ovatum is often found in sandy soils and is well-suited to harsh, semi-desert climates.
