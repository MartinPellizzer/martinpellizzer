Zephyranthes rositae

Zephyranthes rositae, commonly known as the Rosita's rain lily, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is a small, bulbous plant that produces delicate, white or pink flowers resembling lilies, which bloom after rainfall. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect sunlight to flourish. Its flowering period typically occurs during the rainy season, making it a symbol of renewal and resilience in its native habitats.

Zephyranthes rositae is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
