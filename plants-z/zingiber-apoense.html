Zingiber apoense

Zingiber apoense, commonly known as the Java ginger or wild ginger, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other economically important plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant features a rhizomatous stem with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. Its inflorescence consists of showy, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color.

Zingiber apoense is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
