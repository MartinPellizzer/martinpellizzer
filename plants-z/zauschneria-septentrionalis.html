Zauschneria septentrionalis

Zauschneria septentrionalis, commonly known as the northern stalked gentian, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture.
