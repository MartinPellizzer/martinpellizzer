Zygogynum whitmoreanum

Zygogynum whitmoreanum, commonly known as the Malayan starfruit tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive star-shaped fruits, which are typically yellow or orange when ripe. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, fragrant flowers. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
