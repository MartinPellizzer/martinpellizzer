Zanthoxylum parvum

Zanthoxylum parvum, commonly known as prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish-green flowers and red, berry-like fruits that mature in the fall. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and small, sharp spines along the stems and branches. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Zanthoxylum parvum is also valued for its medicinal properties, with its bark and berries used in traditional herbal remedies.
