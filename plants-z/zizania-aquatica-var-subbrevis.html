Zizania aquatica var. subbrevis

Zizania aquatica var. subbrevis, commonly known as the short-stemmed wild rice, is a perennial aquatic grass native to the wetlands of North America.

It typically grows in shallow water, forming dense stands that provide habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of brown, rice-like grains. It is often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of rivers.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for both ecological and agricultural purposes.
