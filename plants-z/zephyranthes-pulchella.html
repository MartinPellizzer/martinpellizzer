Zephyranthes pulchella

Zephyranthes pulchella, commonly known as the pink rain lily, is a species of flowering plant native to the southeastern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a bulbous perennial that produces showy, pink or white flowers during the spring and early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers. Its flowers are often short-lived but appear in clusters, creating a striking visual impact.

Zephyranthes pulchella is also known for its ability to bloom after rainfall, hence its common name.
