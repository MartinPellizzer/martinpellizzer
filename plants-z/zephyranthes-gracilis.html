Zephyranthes gracilis

Zephyranthes gracilis, commonly known as the rain lily, is a herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and its striking, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in response to rainfall. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments, thriving in tropical and subtropical climates. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow and are known for their brief, ephemeral blooming period.

Zephyranthes gracilis is often used in ornamental gardening for its beauty and resilience.
