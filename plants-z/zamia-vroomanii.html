Zamia vroomanii

Zamia vroomanii, commonly known as the Mexican cycad, is a member of the Zamiaceae family and is native to Mexico.

It is a slow-growing plant characterized by its stout, unbranched stem and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces cones rather than flowers, with male and female cones occurring on separate plants. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
