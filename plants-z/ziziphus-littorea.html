Ziziphus littorea

Ziziphus littorea, commonly known as the coastal honeysuckle, is a shrub native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible, red berries. The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to saline environments. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a rough texture, and it has a sprawling growth habit.

Ziziphus littorea plays an important role in supporting local wildlife, including birds and insects.
