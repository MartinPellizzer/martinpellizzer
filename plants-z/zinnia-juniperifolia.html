Zinnia juniperifolia

Zinnia juniperifolia, commonly known as the juniperleaf zinnia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sunlight, making it well adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and resemble those of juniper, giving it its common name.

Zinnia juniperifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native gardens and xeriscaping.
