Zanthoxylum monogynum subsp. paraense

Zanthoxylum monogynum subsp.

paraense, commonly known as the Paraense prickly ash, is a shrub or small tree native to Central and South America. It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often pinnately arranged and covered in small, stiff hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. Its bark and leaves contain various bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional medicine.

The subspecies is adapted to a range of habitats, including dry forests and disturbed areas.
