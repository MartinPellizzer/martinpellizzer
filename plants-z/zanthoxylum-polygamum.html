Zanthoxylum polygamum

Zanthoxylum polygamum, commonly known as the red prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for some animals but generally considered bitter by humans. Its bark and twigs contain compounds that have been used in traditional medicine.

Zanthoxylum polygamum is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks.
