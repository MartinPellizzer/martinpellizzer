Zanthoxylum hygrophilum

Zanthoxylum hygrophilum, commonly known as water huckleberry, is a deciduous shrub native to wetland habitats in North America.

It typically grows in moist soils along streams, marshes, and swampy areas. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring. Its fruit is a red, fleshy drupe that ripens in late summer and is often consumed by wildlife.

Zanthoxylum hygrophilum plays an important ecological role in supporting biodiversity in riparian ecosystems.
