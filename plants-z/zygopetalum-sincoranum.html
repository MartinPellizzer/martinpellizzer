Zygopetalum sincoranum

Zygopetalum sincoranum, commonly known as the Sincora orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, enabling it to thrive in its natural habitat. Its leaves are elongated and grass-like, often arranged in a fan shape, and it prefers bright, indirect light with high humidity.

Zygopetalum sincoranum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and unique floral structure.
