Zygia coccinea

Zygia coccinea, commonly known as the scarlet zygia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a smooth texture and dark green color.

Zygia coccinea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
