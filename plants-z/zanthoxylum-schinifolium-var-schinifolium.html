Zanthoxylum schinifolium var. schinifolium

Zanthoxylum schinifolium var. schinifolium, commonly known as the Chinese prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with serrated margins and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are a food source for birds and other wildlife. It is characterized by its thorny stems and aromatic, pungent leaves.

This species is often used in traditional medicine and horticulture for its ornamental value.
