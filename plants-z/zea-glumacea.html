Zea glumacea

Zea glumacea, commonly known as the common wild corn, is a member of the grass family Poaceae.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Americas, particularly found in the southern United States and parts of Mexico. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its reproductive structures include both male and female flowers, which are typically found on separate plants.

Zea glumacea is characterized by its fibrous roots and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
