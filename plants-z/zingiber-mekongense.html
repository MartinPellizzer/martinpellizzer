Zingiber mekongense

Zingiber mekongense, commonly known as the Mekong ginger, is a rhizomatous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other economically important spices like ginger and turmeric. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in spike-like inflorescences. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
