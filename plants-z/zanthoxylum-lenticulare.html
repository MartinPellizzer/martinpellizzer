Zanthoxylum lenticulare

Zanthoxylum lenticulare, commonly known as the narrow-leaf prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red berries that are an important food source for birds and small mammals.

Its bark and leaves contain compounds used in traditional medicine for their stimulant and analgesic properties.
