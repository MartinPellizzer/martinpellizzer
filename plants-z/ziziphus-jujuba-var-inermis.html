Ziziphus jujuba var. inermis

Ziziphus jujuba var. inermis, commonly known as the Chinese date or jujube, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Asia.

It produces edible fruits that are rich in nutrients, including vitamins and minerals. The plant has simple, alternate leaves and small, yellowish flowers that develop into red or orange fruits. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its fruit and ornamental value.

The variety inermis is distinguished by its lack of thorns compared to other jujube varieties.
