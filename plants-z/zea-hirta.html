Zea hirta

Zea hirta, commonly known as hairy corn or wild corn, is a species of grass native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and hairy leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear both male and female flowers. Zea hirta is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in a variety of environmental conditions.

It is considered an important plant in ecological studies due to its role in supporting pollinators and its potential for agricultural use.
