Zataria

Zataria, commonly known as wild thyme, is a flowering plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, pink or purple flowers and has aromatic, creeping stems. Its leaves are often used in culinary and medicinal applications due to their pungent aroma.

Zataria is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
