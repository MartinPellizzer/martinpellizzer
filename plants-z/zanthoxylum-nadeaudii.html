Zanthoxylum nadeaudii

Zanthoxylum nadeaudii, commonly known as the Mexican prickly ash, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife.

Its bark and leaves contain bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional medicine and phytochemical research.
