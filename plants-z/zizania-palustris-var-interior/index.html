Zizania Palustris Var. Interior

American wild rice, scientifically known as Zizania palustris var. interior, is a native aquatic grass that thrives in freshwater environments across North America. This plant is not only a vital food source for waterfowl and other wildlife but also holds cultural and nutritional significance for Indigenous communities. Its grains, rich in protein and minerals, have been harvested for thousands of years in traditional practices. The plant's ability to grow in shallow waters makes it an important indicator of wetland health. Despite its ecological and historical value, American wild rice faces threats from habitat loss and invasive species.
