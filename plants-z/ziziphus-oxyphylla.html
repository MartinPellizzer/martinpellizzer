Ziziphus oxyphylla

Ziziphus oxyphylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf date palm, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically narrow and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible fruits that are similar in appearance to dates. Ziziphus oxyphylla is adapted to thrive in harsh environments with low rainfall and high temperatures.

It plays a role in local ecosystems and has been used traditionally for its fruit and medicinal properties.
