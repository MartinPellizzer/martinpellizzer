Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale, commonly known as ginger, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous root system, which is the primary part used for culinary and medicinal purposes. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Ginger is known for its aromatic and pungent flavor, attributed to compounds like gingerol and shogaol.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is widely cultivated for both food and traditional medicine.
