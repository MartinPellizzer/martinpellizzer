Zornia quilonensis

Zornia quilonensis, commonly known as Kollam zornia, is a flowering plant native to the Indian state of Kerala.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are arranged in racemose inflorescences. It is often found in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and forest margins.

Zornia quilonensis is of interest in ecological studies due to its potential role in soil stabilization and nitrogen fixation.
