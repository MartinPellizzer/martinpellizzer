Zingiber castaneum

Zingiber castaneum, commonly known as the red ginger, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers such as turmeric and cardamom. The plant is characterized by its vibrant red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters atop tall, leafy stems. Its rhizomes, similar to those of ginger, are sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Zingiber castaneum is valued for its ornamental beauty and has cultural significance in various regions.
