Zichya coccinea

Zichya coccinea, commonly known as the red bellflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange petals that resemble small bells, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Zichya coccinea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
