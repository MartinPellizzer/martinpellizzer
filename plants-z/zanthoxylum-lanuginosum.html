Zanthoxylum lanuginosum

Zanthoxylum lanuginosum, commonly known as the woolly prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a serrated margin, while its bark is smooth and aromatic.

Zanthoxylum lanuginosum is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
