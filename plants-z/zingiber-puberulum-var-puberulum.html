Zingiber puberulum var. puberulum

Zingiber puberulum var. puberulum, commonly known as the hairy ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its stout, rhizomatous root system and upright, herbaceous stems. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow to white flowers, which are borne on branched inflorescences. Its leaves are broad, elliptical, and covered with short, stiff hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its aromatic properties.
