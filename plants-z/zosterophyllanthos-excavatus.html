Zosterophyllanthos excavatus

Zosterophyllanthos excavatus, commonly known as the deep-cupbed zosterophyllum, is a rare and ancient plant species with distinctive botanical features.

It belongs to the order Zosterophyllales, a group of early land plants that thrived during the Silurian period. The plant is characterized by its unique morphology, including a central stem with spirally arranged leaves and specialized structures for water retention. Its reproductive structures are simple, consisting of sporangia that produce spores for propagation.
