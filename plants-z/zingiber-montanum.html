Zingiber montanum

Zingiber montanum, commonly known as the mountain ginger, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers such as turmeric and cardamom. The plant features a rhizomatous root system and produces striking, sword-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its flowering stems bear clusters of pale yellow to white flowers, which are sometimes used in ornamental gardening.

The plant is valued for its aromatic rhizomes, which have been used in both culinary and medicinal applications.
