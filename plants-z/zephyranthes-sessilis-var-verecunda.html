Zephyranthes sessilis var. verecunda

Zephyranthes sessilis var. verecunda, commonly known as the pink fairy lantern, is a striking bulbous plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces vibrant pink or red flowers that resemble tiny lanterns, blooming in late summer to early autumn. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers. Its bulbs are perennial, allowing it to return year after year in suitable climates.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
