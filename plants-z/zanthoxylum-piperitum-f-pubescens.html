Zanthoxylum piperitum f. pubescens

Zanthoxylum piperitum f. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent Chinese prickly ash, is a variant of the Chinese prickly ash, native to East Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its spiny branches and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. Its leaves and fruits are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The pubescent form is distinguished by its hairy or pubescent surfaces on the stems and leaves.
