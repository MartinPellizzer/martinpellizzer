Ziziphus obovata

Ziziphus obovata, commonly known as the Indian date palm, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its lanceolate, dark green leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe, containing a single seed. Ziziphus obovata is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

It has been used traditionally for its edible fruits and has cultural significance in various regions.
