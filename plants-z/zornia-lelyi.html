Zornia lelyi

Zornia lelyi, commonly known as the Lely's zornia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in grasslands and disturbed soils, showcasing its adaptability to various environments. Its leaves are trifoliate, meaning they are divided into three leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Zornia lelyi is often used in ecological studies due to its role in nitrogen fixation and soil stabilization.
