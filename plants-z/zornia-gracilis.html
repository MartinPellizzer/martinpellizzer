Zornia gracilis

Zornia gracilis, commonly known as slender zornia, is a flowering plant native to the United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, slender stems and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Zornia gracilis is typically found in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides nectar for pollinators.
