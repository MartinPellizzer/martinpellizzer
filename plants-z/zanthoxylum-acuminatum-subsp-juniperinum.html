Zanthoxylum acuminatum subsp. juniperinum

Zanthoxylum acuminatum subsp.

juniperinum, commonly known as the narrowleaf prickly ash, is a shrub native to the eastern United States. It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated margins.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, berry-like fruits. Its bark and leaves contain compounds such as lignans and flavonoids, which have been studied for potential medicinal properties.

The species is often found in moist woodlands and along stream banks, where it thrives in partial shade.
