Zingiber borneense

Zingiber borneense, commonly known as Bornean ginger, is a tropical plant native to the island of Borneo.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other ginger species. The plant features a rhizomatous underground stem that produces aromatic, finger-like tubers. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and have a waxy surface, while its flowers are typically yellow or orange with red bracts.

Zingiber borneense is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses in traditional practices.
