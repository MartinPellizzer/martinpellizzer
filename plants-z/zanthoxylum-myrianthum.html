Zanthoxylum myrianthum

Zanthoxylum myrianthum, commonly known as the many-flowered prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnately arranged with 5 to 11 leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. Its bark is thick and rugged, often with a reddish or brownish color.

Zanthoxylum myrianthum is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.
