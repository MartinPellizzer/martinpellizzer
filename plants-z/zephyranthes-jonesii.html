Zephyranthes jonesii

Zephyranthes jonesii, commonly known as the Jones' rain lily, is a bulbous perennial plant native to Central America.

It produces striking white or pale pink flowers that bloom in response to rainfall, typically in the spring or summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and a short stem that supports its showy blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Zephyranthes jonesii is also known for its ability to naturalize and spread easily in suitable conditions.
