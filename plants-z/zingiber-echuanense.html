Zingiber echuanense

Zingiber echuanense, commonly known as the Yunnan ginger or mountain ginger, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern China, particularly Yunnan Province.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other economically important plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant features a rhizomatous stem with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in a spike inflorescence, and are followed by small, round fruits.

Zingiber echuanense is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
