Ziziphus trinervia var. glabrata

Ziziphus trinervia var. glabrata, commonly known as the smoothleaf ziziphus, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous bark and small, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged in threes. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into fleshy, red berries, which are an important food source for various wildlife species. It is adapted to drought conditions and thrives in well-drained soils.

This species is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
