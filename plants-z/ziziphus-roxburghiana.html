Ziziphus roxburghiana

Ziziphus roxburghiana, commonly known as Indian date tree or Roxburgh's date tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Rhamnaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and small, yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible, date-like fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid climates and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The fruit is rich in sugars and is consumed fresh or processed into various food products.
