Zingiber leptorrhizum

Zingiber leptorrhizum, commonly known as the slender-rooted ginger, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers such as turmeric and cardamom. The plant features rhizomes that are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits. Its leaves are long and narrow, and it produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters.

The botanical characteristics of Zingiber leptorrhizum make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and medicinal research.
