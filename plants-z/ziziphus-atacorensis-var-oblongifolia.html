Ziziphus atacorensis var. oblongifolia

Ziziphus atacorensis var. oblongifolia, commonly known as the Atacama ziziphus, is a shrub native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, red berries that are rich in nutrients. It is well adapted to extreme drought conditions, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.

Its botanical features make it an important plant in both ecological and potential medicinal contexts.
