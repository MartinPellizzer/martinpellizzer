Zannichellia palustris subsp. aculeata

Zannichellia palustris subsp.

aculeata, commonly known as the spiked water-pennywort, is a aquatic plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in shallow freshwater habitats such as ponds, ditches, and slow-moving streams.

The plant features small, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, hair-like structures. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually greenish-white in color.

It plays a role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems and providing habitat for various microorganisms.
