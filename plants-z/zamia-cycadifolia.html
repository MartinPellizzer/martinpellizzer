Zamia cycadifolia

Zamia cycadifolia, commonly known as the Mexican cycad, is a member of the Zamiaceae family and is native to Mexico.

It is a slow-growing plant with a thick, woody trunk and a crown of pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
