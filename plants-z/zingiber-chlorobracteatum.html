Zingiber chlorobracteatum

Zingiber chlorobracteatum, commonly known as the green bract ginger, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers and turmeric. The plant is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. Its unique feature is the presence of chlorophyll-rich bracts that give it a distinct green appearance.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has potential uses in traditional medicine.
