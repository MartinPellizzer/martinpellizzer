Zapoteca tetragona

Zapoteca tetragona, commonly known as the Mexican prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, spiny stems and large, fleshy pads that store water. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers, followed by edible fruits known as tunas. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to harsh desert conditions.

The plant has traditional uses in both culinary and medicinal practices among indigenous communities.
