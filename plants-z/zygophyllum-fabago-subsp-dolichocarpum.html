Zygophyllum fabago subsp. dolichocarpum

Zygophyllum fabago subsp.

dolichocarpum, commonly known as the long-seeded boxthorn, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its small, spiny leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant produces elongated, woody fruits that contain several seeds. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with poor soil conditions.

Its structure and growth habits make it an important component of desert ecosystems.
