Zanthoxylum tibetanum

Zanthoxylum tibetanum, commonly known as Tibetan prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the high-altitude regions of Tibet and adjacent areas in Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits. Its bark and leaves contain bioactive compounds, making it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.

Zanthoxylum tibetanum thrives in cold, arid environments and is adapted to the harsh conditions of its native habitat.
