Zingiber ventricosum

Zingiber ventricosum, commonly known as the mountain ginger, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers such as turmeric and cardamom. The plant features a rhizomatous underground stem and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a waxy texture.

Zingiber ventricosum is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
