Zosterophyllanthos tridentatus

Zosterophyllanthos tridentatus, commonly known as the Tridentate Zosterophyllum, is a rare and ancient plant species belonging to the order Zosterophyllophyta.

It is characterized by its distinctive tridentate (three-toothed) leaves, which are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along its stem. This plant exhibits features that bridge the gap between early land plants and more advanced vascular plants, making it a subject of interest in paleobotanical studies. Its fossils are primarily found in Devonian rock formations, indicating its presence during the early colonization of land by plants.

The botanical significance of Zosterophyllanthos tridentatus lies in its role as a transitional form in the evolutionary history of terrestrial flora.
