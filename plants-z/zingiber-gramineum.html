Zingiber gramineum

Zingiber gramineum, commonly known as the grass ginger, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other economically important plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant is characterized by its tall, grass-like leaves and spikes of fragrant, colorful flowers. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The rhizomes of Zingiber gramineum are also used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
