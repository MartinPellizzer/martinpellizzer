Zornia sinaloensis

Zornia sinaloensis, commonly known as the Sinaloan zornia, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces pods that contain several seeds. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

Zornia sinaloensis is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations and potential medicinal properties.
