Ziziphora majoranifolia

Ziziphora majoranifolia, commonly known as Turkish oregano or mountain thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slender stems and small, narrow leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale blue to violet flowers, which bloom in summer. Its leaves and flowers are often used in traditional medicine for their antiseptic and carminative properties.

Ziziphora majoranifolia thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rocky or arid environments.
