Zanthoxylum quassiifolium

Zanthoxylum quassiifolium, commonly known as the Mexican prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. Its bark and leaves contain compounds with potential medicinal properties, making it of interest in traditional herbal medicine.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and dry habitats.
