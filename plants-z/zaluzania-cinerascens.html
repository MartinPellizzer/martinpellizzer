Zaluzania cinerascens

Zaluzania cinerascens, commonly known as the grayleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant features dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, grayish pubescence. Its showy, composite flower heads bloom in late summer, producing purple to pink disc flowers surrounded by white to pink ray florets.

Zaluzania cinerascens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in wetland ecosystems.
