Zea mays var. autumnalis

Zea mays var. autumnalis, commonly known as the Indian corn or sweet corn, is a variant of maize characterized by its unique ear structure and seed characteristics.

This plant is native to the Americas and is cultivated for its edible kernels, which are rich in nutrients. It typically grows to a height of 1.5 to 2 meters and produces tassels at the top of the stalk. The plant's leaves are broad and long, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in warm climates and requires a long growing season to mature.
