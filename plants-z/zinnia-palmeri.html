Zinnia palmeri

Zinnia palmeri, commonly known as the Palmer's zinnia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations such as hairy leaves to reduce water loss. Its vibrant, showy flowers range in color from yellow to red and are often used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.

Zinnia palmeri is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in dry conditions.
