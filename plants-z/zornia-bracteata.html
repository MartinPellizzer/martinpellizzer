Zornia bracteata

Zornia bracteata, commonly known as the bracteate zornia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodland edges and wetlands. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or water.

Zornia bracteata plays a role in supporting local biodiversity and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
