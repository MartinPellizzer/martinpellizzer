Zygopetalum ciliatum

Zygopetalum ciliatum, commonly known as the feathered orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the wetlands of South America.

It features a slender, upright stem with a single, showy flower that has a distinctive, recurved lip. The plant's leaves are linear and often covered in fine, ciliate hairs, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Zygopetalum ciliatum is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
