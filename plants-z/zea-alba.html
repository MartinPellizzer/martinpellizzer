Zea alba

Zea alba, commonly known as white corn, is a grass species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems and large, broad leaves. The plant produces panicles of small, white flowers that are often used in traditional ceremonies. Zea alba is closely related to maize (Zea mays) but differs in its color and use.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and cultural significance in various regions.
