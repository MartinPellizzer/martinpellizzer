Ziziphus pubescens

Ziziphus pubescens, commonly known as the hairy ziziphus, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, small ovate leaves, and yellowish or reddish fruits that are often consumed by wildlife and humans. The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited to harsh environments.

Its fruit, which is typically harvested when fully ripe, contains seeds that are dispersed by animals, contributing to its ecological role in these regions.
