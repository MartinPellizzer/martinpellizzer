Zingiber cardamomum

Zingiber cardamomum, commonly known as cardamom, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family, Zingiberaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces clusters of bright red, three-lobed berries that contain the aromatic seeds used in cooking and traditional medicine. Cardamom is valued for its strong, spicy flavor and is often used in both culinary and medicinal applications.

Its botanical features make it a unique and economically significant plant in tropical agriculture.
