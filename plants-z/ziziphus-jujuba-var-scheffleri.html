Ziziphus jujuba var. scheffleri

Ziziphus jujuba var. scheffleri, commonly known as the Chinese date or red date, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It produces small, oval-shaped fruits that are typically reddish-brown when ripe, with a sweet and chewy texture. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves, which are opposite and consist of several leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its edible fruits and ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
