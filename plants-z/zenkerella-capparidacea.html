Zenkerella capparidacea

Zenkerella capparidacea, commonly known as the African caper, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Capparidaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry woodland and savanna habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
