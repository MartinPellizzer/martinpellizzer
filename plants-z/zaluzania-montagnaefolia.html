Zaluzania montagnaefolia

Zaluzania montagnaefolia, commonly known as the mountainleaf zaluzania, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, often with hairy surfaces and a distinctive reddish or purple coloration on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by triangular, winged fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or swampy areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
