Zornia gibbosa var. cantoniensis

Zornia gibbosa var. cantoniensis, commonly known as the Guangdong zornia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being oval-shaped and slightly hairy.

The seeds are enclosed in a pod and are often dispersed by wind or water.
