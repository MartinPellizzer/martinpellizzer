Zosterophyllanthos asplundii

Zosterophyllanthos asplundii, commonly known as the Asplund's zosterophyllum, is a rare and ancient plant species that belongs to the zosterophyllales order.

It is characterized by its unique morphology, featuring simple, ribbon-like leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern. This plant is believed to have evolved during the Paleozoic era and is considered a key fossil plant for understanding early plant evolution. Its structure suggests it may have been an early precursor to modern vascular plants.

Due to its rarity and limited fossil record, much about its botanical characteristics and ecological role remains to be studied.
