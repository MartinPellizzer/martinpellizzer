Zygophyllum cinereum

Zygophyllum cinereum, commonly known as silverleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its silver-gray foliage and distinctive, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant has small, linear leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Zygophyllum cinereum is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats.
