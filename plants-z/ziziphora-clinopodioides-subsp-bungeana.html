Ziziphora clinopodioides subsp. bungeana

Ziziphora clinopodioides subsp.

bungeana, commonly known as Bunge's woodruff, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia. It features aromatic, narrow leaves with a strong mint-like scent, and its flowering stems bear small, clustered white or pale blue flowers.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its ecological role includes supporting pollinators and contributing to soil stabilization in its native environment.
