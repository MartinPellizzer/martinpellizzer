Zornia echinata

Zornia echinata, commonly known as the spiny zornia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Zornia echinata is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its seeds are encased in a hairy pod and are adapted for dispersal by wind and animals.
