Zanthoxylum hyemale

Zanthoxylum hyemale, commonly known as the prickly ash or yellow prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds and some mammals but are generally considered toxic to humans.

Its bark and roots have been used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported medicinal properties.
