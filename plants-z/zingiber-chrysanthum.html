Zingiber chrysanthum

Zingiber chrysanthum, commonly known as the golden ginger or yellow ginger, is a rhizomatous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It produces striking yellow-orange flowers with a strong, spicy aroma, which are often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary spice. The plant's leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a smooth, waxy surface. Its rhizome, similar to ginger, contains volatile oils and is used in both food and herbal remedies.

Zingiber chrysanthum thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
