Zanthoxylum sapindoides subsp. venosum

Zanthoxylum sapindoides subsp.

venosum, commonly known as the veiny prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of East Asia. It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated margins.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by orange-red berries, which are an important food source for various birds and mammals. Its bark and leaves contain bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional medicine.

The subspecies is distinguished by its prominent venation patterns on the leaves, which contribute to its common name.
