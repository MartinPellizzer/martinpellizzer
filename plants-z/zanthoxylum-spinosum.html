Zanthoxylum spinosum

Zanthoxylum spinosum, commonly known as the prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that give way to red berries in the fall. Its bark and leaves have been used traditionally for their medicinal properties.

Zanthoxylum spinosum thrives in woodland and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils.
