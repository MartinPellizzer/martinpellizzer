Zamia beeldsnijderiana

Zamia beeldsnijderiana, commonly known as the image-carver zamia, is a species of cycad native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of large, pinnately compound leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces cones rather than flowers, with male and female cones occurring on separate plants. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine and have cultural significance in some indigenous communities.

The plant's slow growth and long lifespan make it a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
