Zanthoxylum holtzianum subsp. tenuipedicellatum

Zanthoxylum holtzianum subsp.

tenuipedicellatum, commonly known as the narrow-fruit prickly ash, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated margins and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters.

The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Its bark and leaves contain compounds that have been used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and desert scrub habitats.
