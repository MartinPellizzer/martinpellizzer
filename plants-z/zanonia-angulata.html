Zanonia angulata

Zanonia angulata, commonly known as the angular zanonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive angular leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands and along riverbanks, thriving in moist, sandy soils. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Zanonia angulata plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
