Zingiber cylindricum

Zingiber cylindricum, commonly known as the lesser galangal, is a rhizomatous herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other ginger species. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in spikes. Its rhizomes are aromatic and used in traditional medicine and cooking.

The botanical characteristics of Zingiber cylindricum make it distinct from other ginger species.
