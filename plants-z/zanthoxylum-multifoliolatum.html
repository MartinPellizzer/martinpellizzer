Zanthoxylum multifoliolatum

Zanthoxylum multifoliolatum, commonly known as the many-leaved prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its compound leaves with multiple leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans.
