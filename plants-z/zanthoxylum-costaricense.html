Zanthoxylum costaricense

Zanthoxylum costaricense, commonly known as the Costa Rican prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are edible for certain animals but toxic to humans. Its bark and leaves contain compounds with potential medicinal properties.
