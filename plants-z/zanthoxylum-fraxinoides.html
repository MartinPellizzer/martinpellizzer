Zanthoxylum fraxinoides

Zanthoxylum fraxinoides, commonly known as the Chinese prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and have serrated margins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible for some animals. Its bark and leaves contain bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional medicine.

Zanthoxylum fraxinoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
