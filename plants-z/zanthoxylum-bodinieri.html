Zanthoxylum bodinieri

Zanthoxylum bodinieri, commonly known as the Bodinier prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often pinnately arranged and have serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by bright red berries that are highly ornamental. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some regions.
