Zinnia linearis

Zinnia linearis, commonly known as the slender zinnia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and vibrant, daisy-like flowers that come in shades of red, yellow, and white. The plant is an annual herb that thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological balance.

Zinnia linearis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
