Zanthoxylum ailanthoides f. pubescens

Zanthoxylum ailanthoides f. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent Chinese prickly ash, is a variant of the Chinese prickly ash species.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China, characterized by its spiny branches and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape, with serrated margins and a pubescent (hairy) underside.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in horticulture for its attractive foliage and winter interest.
