Zephyranthes morrisclintii

Zephyranthes morrisclintii, commonly known as the Morrisclint's rain lily, is a species of flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a small, herbaceous perennial that produces delicate, white or pale pink flowers during the spring or early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are grass-like and narrow, and it is often found in wetlands or along the edges of ponds.

The species is named in honor of botanist Morris Clint, reflecting its significance in botanical studies.
