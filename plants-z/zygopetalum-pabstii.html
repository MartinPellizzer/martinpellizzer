Zygopetalum pabstii

Zygopetalum pabstii, commonly known as the Pabst's zygopetalum, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, often colorful flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually fragrant and have a unique structure with a labellum that serves as a landing platform for pollinators.

Zygopetalum pabstii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and rarity.
