Zingiber flaviflorum

Zingiber flaviflorum, commonly known as the yellow-flowered ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers and related plants. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, rhizomatous root system and broad, lance-shaped leaves. Its most distinctive feature is the yellow-orange flower spike, which bears small, fragrant flowers.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
