Zieria bifida

Zieria bifida, commonly known as the narrow-leaf zieria, is a small evergreen shrub native to the south-western region of Western Australia.

It typically grows in heathlands and rocky soils, often in areas with high rainfall. The plant features small, narrow leaves that are usually arranged in pairs along the stems. Its flowers are white or pale yellow and are borne in clusters, adding a delicate appearance to the plant.

Zieria bifida is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
