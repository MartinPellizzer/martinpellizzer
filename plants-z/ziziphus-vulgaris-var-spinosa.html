Ziziphus vulgaris var. spinosa

Ziziphus vulgaris var. spinosa, commonly known as the spiny ziziphus, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches and small, yellowish-green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, red or orange berries that are edible but often have a sour taste. It thrives in arid or semi-arid climates and is often found in rocky soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
