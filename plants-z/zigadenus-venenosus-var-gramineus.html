Zigadenus venenosus var. gramineus

Zigadenus venenosus var. gramineus, commonly known as death camas, is a toxic perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, open areas such as meadows and forest edges. The plant has grass-like leaves and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale yellow. It contains toxic alkaloids, making it dangerous to both livestock and humans.

Due to its toxicity, it is often avoided in agricultural and natural landscapes.
