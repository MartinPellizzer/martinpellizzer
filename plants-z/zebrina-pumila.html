Zebrina pumila

Zebrina pumila, commonly known as the dwarf water spinach, is a small aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features slender, floating stems that bear small, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white in color. Zebrina pumila thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in ponds and ditches.

It is valued for its ability to purify water and provide habitat for aquatic life.
