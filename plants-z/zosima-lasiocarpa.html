Zosima lasiocarpa

Zosima lasiocarpa, commonly known as the rough-hairy zosima, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive hairy leaves and small, clustered white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are covered in fine, stiff hairs, which give it its common name.

Zosima lasiocarpa is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
