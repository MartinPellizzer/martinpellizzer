Ziziphus mauritiana var. spontanea

Ziziphus mauritiana var. spontanea, commonly known as the wild date tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia and the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are usually opposite and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into edible, sweet-tart fruits resembling dates. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in dry, open woodlands or along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its fruit, which is used in traditional medicine and as a food source in many regions.
