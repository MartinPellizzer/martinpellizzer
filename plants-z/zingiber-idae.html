Zingiber idae

Zingiber idae, commonly known as the Java ginger, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other well-known spices like ginger and turmeric. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces fragrant, yellowish flowers with red bracts. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The botanical characteristics of Zingiber idae make it a valuable species in both horticulture and pharmacology.
