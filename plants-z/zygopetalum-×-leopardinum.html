Zygopetalum × leopardinum

Zygopetalum × leopardinum, commonly known as the leopard orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful flowers with intricate patterns resembling a leopard's spots, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are borne on branching inflorescences and can last for several weeks, making it a popular choice for ornamental cultivation.

The species is valued for its unique appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
