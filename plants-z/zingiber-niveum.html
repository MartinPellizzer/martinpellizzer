Zingiber niveum

Zingiber niveum, commonly known as the white ginger or snow ginger, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other economically important plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine white pubescence, giving it its common name. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in spike-like inflorescences.

While not widely cultivated for its rhizomes, it is appreciated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
