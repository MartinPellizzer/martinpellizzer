Zingiber parishii

Zingiber parishii, commonly known as the Philippine ginger, is a rhizomatous herbaceous plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other well-known gingers such as ginger and turmeric. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and digestive properties.

Zingiber parishii is characterized by its unique floral structure and is valued for both its ornamental and potential medicinal uses.
