Zanthoxylum chalybeum

Zanthoxylum chalybeum, commonly known as ironwood or black thorn, is a member of the Rutaceae family.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States. The plant is characterized by its dark, almost metallic bark and its clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. Its fruits are bright red berries that contain several seeds, and the plant is often used in traditional medicine.

Zanthoxylum chalybeum is also valued for its hard, durable wood, which has been historically used for tool handles and other purposes.
