Zea mays var. crinusa

Zea mays var. crinusa, commonly known as the tassel corn or feather corn, is a variant of maize characterized by its distinctive, feathery tassels.

It is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Americas, typically growing up to 2-3 meters in height. The plant features large, broad leaves and produces both male and female inflorescences, with the tassels being the most striking botanical feature. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique appearance.
