Zosterophyllanthos applanatus

Zosterophyllanthos applanatus, commonly known as the flatleaf zosterophyllum, is a rare and ancient plant species belonging to the order Zosterophyllophyta.

It is characterized by its flattened, ribbon-like leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along its stem. This plant exhibits features that suggest a transitional form between early algae and more complex land plants. Its fossil record is sparse, making it a subject of ongoing botanical study.

The plant's structure provides insights into the evolutionary development of vascular tissues in early plant life.
