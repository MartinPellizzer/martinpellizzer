Zyrphelis ciliaris subsp. hirsuta

Zyrphelis ciliaris subsp.

hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy zyrphelis, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America. It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves.

The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, often with a distinctive white or yellow throat. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.
