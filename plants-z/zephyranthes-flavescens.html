Zephyranthes flavescens

Zephyranthes flavescens, commonly known as the yellow rain lily, is a species of flowering plant native to the southeastern United States and parts of Central America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, often following periods of rainfall. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to a variety of garden settings.

Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are evergreen in warmer climates.
