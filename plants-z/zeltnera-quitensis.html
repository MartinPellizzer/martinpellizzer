Zeltnera quitensis

Zeltnera quitensis, commonly known as the Peruvian quinoa, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and feathery leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing at high altitudes.

Zeltnera quitensis is valued for its edible seeds, which are rich in protein and nutrients.
