Ziziphora thymifolia

Ziziphora thymifolia, commonly known as sweet everlasting or thyme-like germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive scent. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers arranged in compact clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowering stems are usually branched and can reach heights of up to 30 centimeters.

Ziziphora thymifolia is valued in herbal medicine for its purported calming and digestive properties.
