Zerna syriaca

Zerna syriaca, commonly known as Syrian millet, is an annual grass native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It produces small, oval grains that are typically brown or yellow in color. The plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient crop for dry regions. Its seeds are used for food, animal feed, and traditional medicinal purposes.

Zerna syriaca is also valued for its ability to thrive in poor soils and its historical significance in ancient agriculture.
