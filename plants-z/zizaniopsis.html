Zizaniopsis

Zizaniopsis, commonly known as watergrass, is a genus of aquatic and semi-aquatic grasses belonging to the Poaceae family.

These plants are typically found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of freshwater bodies. Zizaniopsis species are characterized by their slender, grass-like stems and narrow leaves, often with a bluish or greenish hue. They play an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Some species are invasive in certain regions, impacting local ecosystems and water management efforts.
