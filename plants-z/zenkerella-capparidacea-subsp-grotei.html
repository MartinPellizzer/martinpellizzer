Zenkerella capparidacea subsp. grotei

Zenkerella capparidacea subsp.

grotei, commonly known as the African sandmat, is a member of the Capparaceae family and is native to southern Africa. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, often in arid or semi-arid regions.

The plant has a distinctive, sprawling growth habit with fleshy, succulent stems and small, greenish-white flowers. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs, while its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in dryland gardens.
