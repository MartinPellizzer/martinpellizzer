Zenobia cerasiflora

Zenobia cerasiflora, commonly known as the cherry-flowered zenobia, is a flowering plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, showy flowers that resemble cherry blossoms, typically appearing in clusters. The plant has delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it is relatively rare in the wild.
