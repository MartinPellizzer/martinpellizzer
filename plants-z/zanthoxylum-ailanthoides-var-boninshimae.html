Zanthoxylum ailanthoides var. boninshimae

Zanthoxylum ailanthoides var. boninshimae, commonly known as the Bonin prickly ash, is a rare shrub native to the Bonin Islands.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for local wildlife. Due to its limited distribution and conservation status, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
