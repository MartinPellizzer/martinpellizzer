Zingiber petiolatum

Zingiber petiolatum, commonly known as the slender ginger or petioled ginger, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers and related ornamental plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often variegated with white and green stripes, adding visual interest to gardens. Its flowering stems produce small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color.

Zingiber petiolatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical climates for its aesthetic qualities.
