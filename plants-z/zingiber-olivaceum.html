Zingiber olivaceum

Zingiber olivaceum, commonly known as the olive ginger, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers and turmeric. The plant features rhizomatous growth and produces fragrant, yellowish flowers with a distinctive olive-green hue. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and have a smooth, glossy texture.

Zingiber olivaceum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
