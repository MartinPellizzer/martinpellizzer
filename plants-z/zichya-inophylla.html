Zichya inophylla

Zichya inophylla, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Zichya inophylla is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and subtle floral display.
