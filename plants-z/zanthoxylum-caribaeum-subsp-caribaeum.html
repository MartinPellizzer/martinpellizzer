Zanthoxylum caribaeum subsp. caribaeum

Zanthoxylum caribaeum subsp.

caribaeum, commonly known as the Caribbean prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean region. It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters.

The plant produces bright red berries that are edible for some animals but toxic to humans. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

Its bark and leaves have been used traditionally in herbal remedies for their purported medicinal properties.
