Zephyranthes bella

Zephyranthes bella, commonly known as the fairy lily or rain lily, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States and parts of Central America.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, star-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant is known for its ability to bloom after rainfall, often appearing suddenly in open areas. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Zephyranthes bella is also valued for its low maintenance and ability to naturalize in suitable climates.
