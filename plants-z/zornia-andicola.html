Zornia andicola

Zornia andicola, commonly known as the Andean zornia, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky soils, adapting to the harsh conditions of its environment. Zornia andicola plays a role in local ecosystems by contributing to soil stabilization and providing forage for grazing animals.
