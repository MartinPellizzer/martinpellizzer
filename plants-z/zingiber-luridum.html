Zingiber luridum

Zingiber luridum, commonly known as the golden ginger, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers and turmeric. The plant features vibrant yellow-orange inflorescences that resemble a torch, making it visually striking. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a smooth, waxy texture.

Zingiber luridum is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its bold and colorful appearance.
