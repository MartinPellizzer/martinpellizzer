Zanthoxylum pilosiusculum

Zanthoxylum pilosiusculum, commonly known as the hairy prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its small, yellowish-green flowers and red, berry-like fruits. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and is often found in moist, wooded areas. Its bark and leaves contain bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional medicine.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in partial shade.
