Zingiber arunachalensis

Zingiber arunachalensis, commonly known as the Arunachal ginger, is a rare and lesser-known species of ginger native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its aromatic, rhizomatous growth habit and striking, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces tall, erect flowering stems that bear clusters of pale yellow to greenish-white flowers. Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and potential medicinal properties.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical research and conservation efforts.
