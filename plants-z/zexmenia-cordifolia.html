Zexmenia cordifolia

Zexmenia cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf zexmenia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, often arranged in clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
