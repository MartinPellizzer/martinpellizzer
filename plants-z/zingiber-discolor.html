Zingiber discolor

Zingiber discolor, commonly known as the white ginger or yellow ginger, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other economically important plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant is characterized by its ornamental, strap-shaped leaves that are usually green with a yellow or white midrib. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, often displaying a range of colors from white to yellow.

Zingiber discolor is cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and for its potential medicinal properties.
