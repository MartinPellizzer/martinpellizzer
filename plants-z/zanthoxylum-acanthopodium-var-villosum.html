Zanthoxylum acanthopodium var. villosum

Zanthoxylum acanthopodium var. villosum, commonly known as the silky prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that give way to red, berry-like fruits in the fall. Its botanical features include pinnate leaflets and a distinctive, aromatic resinous scent.

It is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
