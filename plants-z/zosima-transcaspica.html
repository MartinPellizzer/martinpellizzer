Zosima transcaspica

Zosima transcaspica, commonly known as the Central Asian zosima, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperature fluctuations. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers during the summer months. It is often found in steppes and semi-desert environments.

Its botanical significance lies in its ability to thrive in harsh conditions, making it an important species for ecological studies in arid ecosystems.
