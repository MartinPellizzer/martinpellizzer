Zanthoxylum ochroxylum

Zanthoxylum ochroxylum, commonly known as the Himalayan prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its spiny branches and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by red, fleshy fruits. Its bark and leaves contain bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional medicine.

Zanthoxylum ochroxylum thrives in temperate to subalpine regions and is often found in rocky or forested habitats.
