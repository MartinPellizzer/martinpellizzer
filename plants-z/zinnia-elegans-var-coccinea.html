Zinnia elegans var. coccinea

Zinnia elegans var. coccinea, commonly known as the scarlet zinnia, is a vibrant annual flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, daisy-like blooms in shades of red, orange, and yellow, with a prominent central disk. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring slender, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are composed of two layers of ray florets surrounding a central cluster of tubular florets, which are essential for attracting pollinators.

The plant thrives in full sunlight and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
