Zingiber amaricans

Zingiber amaricans, commonly known as wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like turmeric and cardamom. The plant features rhizomatous growth and produces clusters of pale yellow to white flowers. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a rough, hairy texture.

The rhizomes are used in traditional medicine and have a spicy, ginger-like flavor.
