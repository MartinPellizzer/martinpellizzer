Zinnia violacea var. coccinea

Zinnia violacea var. coccinea, commonly known as the scarlet zinnia, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, semi-double to fully double flowers that range in color from deep red to orange-red. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features lance-shaped, hairy leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

The species is known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
