Zingiber flagelliforme

Zingiber flagelliforme, commonly known as the thread ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, thin rhizomes and lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers with bright red petals and yellow stamens, typically blooming in clusters. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine and as a spice, similar to ginger.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
