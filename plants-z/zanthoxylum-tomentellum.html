Zanthoxylum tomentellum

Zanthoxylum tomentellum, commonly known as the woolly pricklyash, is a deciduous shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically covered in a dense, white, woolly pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. Its bark and leaves contain various alkaloids, which have been studied for potential medicinal properties.

Zanthoxylum tomentellum is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.
