Zephyranthes immaculata

Zephyranthes immaculata, commonly known as the white rain lily, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a bulbous perennial that produces delicate, white flowers with a subtle fragrance, typically blooming after rainfall. The plant has strap-like, narrow leaves that are evergreen and grow from a central bulb. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Zephyranthes immaculata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in garden settings.
