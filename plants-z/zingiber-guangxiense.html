Zingiber guangxiense

Zingiber guangxiense, commonly known as Guangxi ginger, is a rare and lesser-known species of ginger native to southern China.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and compact inflorescence with small, pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in mountainous regions with well-drained soil. Its rhizomes are similar to those of other ginger species but are less commonly used in culinary applications.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical research and conservation efforts.
