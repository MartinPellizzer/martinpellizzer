Zamia maelenii

Zamia maelenii, commonly known as the Maelen's cycad, is a member of the Cycadaceae family and is native to Mexico.

It is a slow-growing, dioecious plant with a stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces cones, with male cones typically found at the base of the plant and female cones at the top. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and conservation status, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
