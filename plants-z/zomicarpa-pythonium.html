Zomicarpa pythonium

Zomicarpa pythonium, commonly known as the serpentfruit, is a unique plant characterized by its distinctive, serpentine-shaped fruits.

Native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, it thrives in humid, warm climates with well-drained soil. The plant features compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped lobes and produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters. Its fruit, which resembles a coiled snake, contains a single, large, edible seed surrounded by a fibrous pulp.

Due to its rare occurrence and unusual morphology, Zomicarpa pythonium is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
