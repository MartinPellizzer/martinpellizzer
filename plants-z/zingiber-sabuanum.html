Zingiber sabuanum

Zingiber sabuanum, commonly known as the Sabu ginger, is a rhizomatous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other ginger species. The plant produces upright, narrow leaves and has a distinctive aromatic rhizome used in traditional medicine. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in spike-like inflorescences.

Zingiber sabuanum is valued for its medicinal properties and is often cultivated for both culinary and therapeutic purposes.
