Zephyranthes sylvatica

Zephyranthes sylvatica, commonly known as the pink rain lily, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a bulbous perennial that produces delicate, pinkish-white flowers in early spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens in USDA hardiness zones 7 through 10. Its foliage consists of strap-like leaves that emerge from the base of the bulb.

The plant is notable for its ability to bloom after rainfall, hence its common name.
