Zanthoxylum punctatum subsp. punctatum

Zanthoxylum punctatum subsp.

punctatum, commonly known as the prickly ash or American prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America. It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated margins and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are edible for some wildlife but contain toxic compounds for humans.

Its bark and roots have been used traditionally in herbal medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
