Zosterostylis zeylanica

Zosterostylis zeylanica, commonly known as the Ceylon arrowhead, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its arrow-shaped leaves and striking spadix inflorescence. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female parts, a common trait in the Araceae family.

Zosterostylis zeylanica is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
