Zizania natans

Zizania natans, commonly known as the floating bamboo or water millet, is a aquatic plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth and long, narrow leaves that float on the water's surface. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers. Zizania natans is often found in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

It plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
