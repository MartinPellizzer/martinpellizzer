Zanthoxylum ailanthoides

Zanthoxylum ailanthoides, commonly known as the winged bean or Chinese prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces smooth, reddish-brown fruits that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Zanthoxylum ailanthoides thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forest edges or disturbed areas.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties make it a notable species in horticulture and herbal practices.
