Zephyranthes nymphaea

Zephyranthes nymphaea, commonly known as the water lily orchid, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves that grow from a rhizomatous stem, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soil. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, with a distinctive, fragrant scent.

Zephyranthes nymphaea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and water features.
