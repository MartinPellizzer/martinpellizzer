Ziziphus jujuba f. lageniformis

Ziziphus jujuba f. lageniformis, commonly known as the balloon Chinese date, is a cultivar of the jujube tree characterized by its unique, inflated fruit shape.

The plant is native to China and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin. The flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in panicles, typically appearing before the leaves in early spring.

The fruit, which matures in late summer, is prized for its sweet, honey-like flavor and high nutritional value.
