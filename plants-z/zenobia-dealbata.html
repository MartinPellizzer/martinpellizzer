Zenobia dealbata

Zenobia dealbata, commonly known as the white-flowered rock fig, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible, fig-like fruits that are typically consumed fresh or used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
