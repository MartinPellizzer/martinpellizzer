Zornia floribunda

Zornia floribunda, commonly known as the clustered zornia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Zornia floribunda is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
