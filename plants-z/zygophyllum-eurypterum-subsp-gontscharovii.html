Zygophyllum eurypterum subsp. gontscharovii

Zygophyllum eurypterum subsp.

gontscharovii, commonly known as the Gontscharov's jointfoot, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the family Zygophyllaceae and is characterized by its small, clustered leaves and yellow flowers.

The plant is adapted to harsh environments, featuring deep root systems and drought-resistant foliage. It often grows on rocky or sandy soils and is well-suited to regions with low rainfall.

Its botanical features make it an important component of desert ecosystems.
