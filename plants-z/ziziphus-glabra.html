Ziziphus glabra

Ziziphus glabra, commonly known as the Indian date palm, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-green fruits that are typically consumed by birds and animals. The plant is adapted to thrive in dry conditions, with deep roots that allow it to access groundwater. Its fruit, while not as sweet as dates, is edible and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Ziziphus glabra is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
