Zehneria cunninghamii

Zehneria cunninghamii, commonly known as the Cunningham's zehneria, is a flowering plant native to the wet tropical rainforests of Queensland, Australia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters, featuring broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking white or pale pink flowers arranged in dense, terminal clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden settings.

Due to its ornamental value and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
