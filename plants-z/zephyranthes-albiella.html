Zephyranthes albiella

Zephyranthes albiella, commonly known as the white rain lily, is a species of flowering plant native to the southeastern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a small, herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 10-15 centimeters. The plant produces delicate, white flowers with a subtle fragrance, which bloom in response to rainfall or high humidity. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often emerging from a basal rosette.

Zephyranthes albiella is well adapted to wetland environments and is often found in moist soils near water sources.
