Zanthoxylum rigidifolium

Zanthoxylum rigidifolium, commonly known as the rigid-leaf prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its spiny branches and dark green, elliptical leaves that are often stiff and rigid. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries in the fall. Its botanical features include pinnate leaves and a distinctive, aromatic scent.

Zanthoxylum rigidifolium is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
