Zanthoxylum melanorhachis

Zanthoxylum melanorhachis, commonly known as the black-stemmed prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically 5 to 9 pairs of ovate to lanceolate leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that give way to red, fleshy berries in the fall. Its bark is rough and often has a distinctive, aromatic scent.

Zanthoxylum melanorhachis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
