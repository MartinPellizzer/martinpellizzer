Zanthoxylum dissitum var. spinulosum

Zanthoxylum dissitum var. spinulosum, commonly known as the spiny prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated margins and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Its bark and leaves contain compounds that have been used in traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and streambanks.
