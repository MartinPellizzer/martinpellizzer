Zephyranthes nervosa

Zephyranthes nervosa, commonly known as the rain lily, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a bulbous perennial that produces delicate, white or pale pink flowers during the spring and early summer. The plant has strap-like, narrow leaves that are typically evergreen, providing a lush base for its showy blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners in warmer climates.

Zephyranthes nervosa is also known for its ability to bloom after rainfall, hence its common name.
