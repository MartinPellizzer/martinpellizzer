Zexmenia oppositifolia

Zexmenia oppositifolia, commonly known as the opposite-leaved zexmenia, is a unique plant species characterized by its opposite leaf arrangement.

It typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid, shaded environments. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are often white or pale pink in color. Its leaves are usually elongated and have a smooth, waxy surface.

Zexmenia oppositifolia is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
