Zelkova sinica var. australis

Zelkova sinica var. australis, commonly known as the Australian zelkova, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in spring, followed by winged fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open forests and woodland areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for urban landscaping.
