Zilla myagroides var. macrocarpa

Zilla myagroides var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited zilla, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, commonly referred to as the grass family. The plant is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

The variety macrocarpa is distinguished by its larger, more prominent fruiting structures compared to other varieties of Zilla.
