Ziziphora thymoides

Ziziphora thymoides, commonly known as wild thyme or mountain thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, small, needle-like leaves and clusters of tiny, pink to white flowers. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its fragrant herbs and medicinal properties. Ziziphora thymoides thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

It is valued for its essential oils and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported antibacterial and antifungal properties.
