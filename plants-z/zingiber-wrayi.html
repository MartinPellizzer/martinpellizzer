Zingiber wrayi

Zingiber wrayi, commonly known as the Wray's ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers and related plants. The plant features rhizomatous growth, with long, narrow leaves that are typically bright green. Its flowers are clustered in inflorescences and are often yellow or orange in color.

Zingiber wrayi is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
