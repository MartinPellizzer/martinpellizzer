Zeuxine mannii

Zeuxine mannii, commonly known as the African buttercup, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its basal leaves with palmate lobes and bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its flowers are short-lived, opening in the morning and closing by midday.

Zeuxine mannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
