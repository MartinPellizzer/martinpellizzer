Zingiber macrorrhynchus

Zingiber macrorrhynchus, commonly known as the giant ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers and related plants. The plant is characterized by its large, elongated leaves and striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine and cooking, similar to other ginger species.

Zingiber macrorrhynchus thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
