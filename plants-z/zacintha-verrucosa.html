Zacintha verrucosa

Zacintha verrucosa, commonly known as the rough zinnia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, daisy-like flowers that range in color from yellow to red. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 feet and features hairy, rough-textured stems and leaves. Its flowers are composed of a central disk surrounded by ray florets, which are often the most striking part of the plant.

Zacintha verrucosa is well adapted to dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky soils.
