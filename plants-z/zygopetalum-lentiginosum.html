Zygopetalum lentiginosum

Zygopetalum lentiginosum, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to South America.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a rhizome and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white. The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, while the flowers are borne on tall, erect stems. The plant is characterized by its distinctive spotted petals and is often found in moist, shaded habitats.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems and is valued for its ornamental beauty.
