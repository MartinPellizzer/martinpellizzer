Zeuxine glandulosa

Zeuxine glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular zeuxine, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically arranged in clusters at the top of the stem. Zeuxine glandulosa is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the genus Zeuxine, with a unique combination of glandular structures and floral morphology.
