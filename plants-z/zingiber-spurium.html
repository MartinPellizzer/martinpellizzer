Zingiber spurium

Zingiber spurium, commonly known as the white ginger lily, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other economically important plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy white or pale pink flowers. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and have a strong, spicy aroma.

Zingiber spurium is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
