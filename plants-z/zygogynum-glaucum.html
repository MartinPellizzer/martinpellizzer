Zygogynum glaucum

Zygogynum glaucum, commonly known as the silverleaf sarsaparilla, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, woody stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy, bluish-gray coating. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Zygogynum glaucum is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
