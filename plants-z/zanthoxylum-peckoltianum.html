Zanthoxylum peckoltianum

Zanthoxylum peckoltianum, commonly known as the Mexican prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are usually opposite and have serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. Its bark and leaves contain compounds that have been used in traditional medicine.

Zanthoxylum peckoltianum is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
