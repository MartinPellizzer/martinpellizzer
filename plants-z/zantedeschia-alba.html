Zantedeschia alba

Zantedeschia alba, commonly known as the white calla lily, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, arrow-shaped leaves and its showy, white, spatulate flowers that resemble a cup. The plant belongs to the Araceae family, which is known for its unique floral structures called spathes. Zantedeschia alba thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
