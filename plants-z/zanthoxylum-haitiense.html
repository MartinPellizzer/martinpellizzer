Zanthoxylum haitiense

Zanthoxylum haitiense, commonly known as Haitian prickly ash, is a shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean, particularly Haiti.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are edible and often used in traditional cuisine.

Its bark and leaves contain bioactive compounds, making it of interest in herbal medicine and phytochemical research.
