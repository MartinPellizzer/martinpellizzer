Zanthoxylum sapindoides subsp. jamaicense

Zanthoxylum sapindoides subsp.

jamaicense, commonly known as Jamaican prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean. It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically 5-7 pairs of ovate to lanceolate leaflets.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for some animals but generally considered bitter by humans. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
