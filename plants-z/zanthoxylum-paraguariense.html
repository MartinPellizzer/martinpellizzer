Zanthoxylum paraguariense

Zanthoxylum paraguariense, commonly known as the Paraguayan prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America, particularly Paraguay and Brazil.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Its bark and leaves contain bioactive compounds, making it a subject of interest in pharmacological research.
