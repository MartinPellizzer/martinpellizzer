Zehneria macrocarpa

Zehneria macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited zehneria, is a member of the Gesneriaceae family native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its attractive, broad, ovate leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming during the warmer months. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain numerous small seeds, aiding in its propagation.

Zehneria macrocarpa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded gardens or as a houseplant.
