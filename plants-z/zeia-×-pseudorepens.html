Zeia × pseudorepens

Zeia × pseudorepens, commonly known as the hybrid ivy, is a climbing plant characterized by its trailing growth habit and ability to form dense mats.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its small, inconspicuous flowers are typically greenish-white in color. The plant is native to parts of Europe and is commonly found in disturbed soils and along garden borders.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to cover surfaces, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
