Zinnia verticillata

Zinnia verticillata, commonly known as the whorled zinnia, is an annual flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its clustered, showy flowers that typically range in color from yellow to red, with some varieties displaying bi-color patterns. The plant has a bushy growth habit, with leaves that are narrow and arranged in whorls around the stem. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, low-maintenance blooms.

Zinnia verticillata is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
