Zornia cryptantha

Zornia cryptantha, commonly known as the narrowleaf zornia, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant has slender, upright stems and small, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences and are usually pink or purple in color.

Zornia cryptantha plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small insects and pollinators.
