Zygogynum pancheri subsp. arrhantum

Zygogynum pancheri subsp.

arrhantum, commonly known as the Ethiopian arrowroot, is a perennial herb native to Ethiopia. It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit.

The plant produces striking, white to cream-colored spathe flowers, which are typical of the Araceae family. Its leaves are typically arrow-shaped, giving it the common name "arrowroot.".

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in montane forests.
