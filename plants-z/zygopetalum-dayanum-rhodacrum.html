Zygopetalum dayanum rhodacrum

Zygopetalum dayanum rhodacrum, commonly known as the Dayanum rhodacrum orchid, is a rare and unique orchid species native to specific regions in Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, fragrant flowers that display a striking combination of colors. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, tropical environments. Its botanical features include elongated, strap-like leaves and a distinctive floral structure with a prominent lip.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a valuable species in botanical research and conservation efforts.
