Zephyranthes sessilis var. ackermannia

Zephyranthes sessilis var. ackermannia, commonly known as the Ackermann's rain lily, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, white to pale pink flowers during the spring and summer months. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and woodland settings. Its botanical features include a short, sturdy stem and a single, erect flower spike.

This variety is notable for its resilience and ability to naturalize in suitable climates.
