Zanthoxylum dissitoides

Zanthoxylum dissitoides, commonly known as the Mexican prickly ash, is a member of the Rutaceae family and is native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters. The plant is characterized by its spiny branches, opposite leaves, and small yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. Its fruit is a red, fleshy drupe that contains several seeds, and it is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant thrives in a variety of habitats, including dry forests and rocky slopes.
