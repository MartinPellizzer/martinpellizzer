Zingiber fraseri

Zingiber fraseri, commonly known as the Fraser's ginger, is a rhizomatous herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers such as turmeric and cardamom. The plant features narrow, sword-shaped leaves and produces fragrant, showy flowers in shades of yellow and orange. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

Zingiber fraseri thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.
