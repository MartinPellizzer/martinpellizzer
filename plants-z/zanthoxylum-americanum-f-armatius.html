Zanthoxylum americanum f. armatius

Zanthoxylum americanum f. armatius, commonly known as the prickly ash or American prickly ash, is a variant of the common prickly ash.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States. The plant is characterized by its spiny stems and pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges. Its small, yellowish-green flowers appear in clusters and are followed by red, fleshy berries.

The plant has a rugged appearance and is often found in moist, woodland habitats.
