Zanthoxylum febrifugum

Zanthoxylum febrifugum, commonly known as the Chinese prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. Its bark and roots have been traditionally used in Chinese medicine for their purported fever-reducing properties.

The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.
