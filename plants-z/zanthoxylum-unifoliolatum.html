Zanthoxylum unifoliolatum

Zanthoxylum unifoliolatum, commonly known as the single leaf prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with a single leaflet, giving it its specific epithet. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for wildlife.

Its bark and leaves contain compounds that have been used traditionally for medicinal purposes.
