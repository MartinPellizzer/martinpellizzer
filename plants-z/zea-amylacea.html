Zea amylacea

Zea amylacea, commonly known as the sweet grass, is a member of the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are often used in traditional Native American crafts and ceremonies due to their aromatic properties.

Zea amylacea is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various desert organisms.
