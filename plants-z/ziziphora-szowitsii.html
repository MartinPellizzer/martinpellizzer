Ziziphora szowitsii

Ziziphora szowitsii, commonly known as the Kashmir thyme, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
