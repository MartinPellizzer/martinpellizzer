Zingiber monglaense

Zingiber monglaense, commonly known as the Assam ginger, is a perennial herb native to the northeastern regions of India.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other economically important plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant features a rhizomatous root system and produces clusters of fragrant, pale yellow to orange flowers. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and covered with a waxy cuticle, which helps in reducing water loss.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has potential applications in traditional medicine.
