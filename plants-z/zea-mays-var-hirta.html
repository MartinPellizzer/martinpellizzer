Zea mays var. hirta

Zea mays var. hirta, commonly known as the hairy maize, is a variant of corn characterized by its distinctive hairy leaves and stems.

It is a member of the grass family Poaceae and is native to the Americas. This plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 meters and produces tassels and ears of grain. Its hairy texture is due to the presence of fine, pubescent hairs on the foliage.

The botanical features of Zea mays var. hirta make it an important subject in both agricultural and ecological studies.
