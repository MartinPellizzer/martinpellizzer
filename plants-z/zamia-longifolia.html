Zamia longifolia

Zamia longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaf cycad, is a species of cycad native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its stout, unbranched stem and a crown of large, pinnately compound leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are borne on separate male and female plants. Zamia longifolia is a slow-growing plant that thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water.

Due to its ornamental value and conservation status, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
