Zygophlebia rouxii

Zygophlebia rouxii, commonly known as the African wild indigo, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in shrubland and woodland habitats, often in well-drained soils. It is noted for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
