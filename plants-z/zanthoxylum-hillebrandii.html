Zanthoxylum hillebrandii

Zanthoxylum hillebrandii, commonly known as the Hillebrand's prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and wildlife. Its bark and leaves contain bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional and modern herbal medicine.

Zanthoxylum hillebrandii thrives in woodland and disturbed habitats, often growing in partial shade to full sun.
