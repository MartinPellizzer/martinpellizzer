Ziziphora eglandulosa

Ziziphora eglandulosa, commonly known as redstem tarragon, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. The plant features slender, erect stems that are often reddish in color, hence its common name. Its small, fragrant flowers are borne in spikes and bloom in the summer months.

Ziziphora eglandulosa is valued for its aromatic leaves, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications.
