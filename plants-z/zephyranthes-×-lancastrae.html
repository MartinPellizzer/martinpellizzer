Zephyranthes × lancastrae

Zephyranthes × lancastrae, commonly known as the pink rain lily, is a hybrid bulbous plant native to Central America.

It produces showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in response to rainfall, typically during the summer months. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to naturalize and return year after year.
