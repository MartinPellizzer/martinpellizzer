Zornia contorta

Zornia contorta, commonly known as slender zornia, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, trifoliate leaves and pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and can be found in wetlands, ditches, and along stream banks. Its seed pods are slender and dehiscent, releasing tiny seeds when mature.

Zornia contorta plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various insects and small animals.
