Zea mays var. vulgaris-aestiva

Zea mays var. vulgaris-aestiva, commonly known as corn or maize, is a tall, annual grass native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall stalks, which can reach heights of up to 3 meters, and its large, tasselled inflorescence at the top. The plant produces ear-like clusters of kernels, which are the edible part commonly used in various culinary applications. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a prominent midrib, and the plant is well adapted to temperate and tropical climates.

Zea mays var. vulgaris-aestiva is a crucial crop in agriculture, providing a staple food source for humans and animals worldwide.
