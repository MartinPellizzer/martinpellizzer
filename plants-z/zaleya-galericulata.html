Zaleya galericulata

Zaleya galericulata, commonly known as the golden crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces long, slender seed heads that give it its common name. Zaleya galericulata is often found in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
