Zieria furfuracea subsp. euthadenia

Zieria furfuracea subsp.

euthadenia, commonly known as the silverleaf zieria, is a low-growing shrub native to the eastern regions of Australia. It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important component of its native habitat.
