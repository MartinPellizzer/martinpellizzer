Zornia albiflora

Zornia albiflora, commonly known as whiteflower zornia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species characterized by its slender stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Zornia albiflora is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and botanical studies.
