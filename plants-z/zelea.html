Zelea

Zelea, commonly known as the bluebell, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and prefers well-drained, moist soil. Zelea has strap-like leaves that are dark green and often persist through the winter.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
