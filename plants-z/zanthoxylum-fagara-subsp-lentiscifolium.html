Zanthoxylum fagara subsp. lentiscifolium

Zanthoxylum fagara subsp.

lentiscifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf prickly ash, is a shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States. It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves with serrated margins.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences, followed by red, berry-like fruits. Its bark and leaves contain various bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional medicine.

The subspecies is distinguished by its narrower, more lanceolate leaves compared to other forms of Zanthoxylum fagara.
