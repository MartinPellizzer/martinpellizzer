Zygopetalum intermedium var. peruvianum

Zygopetalum intermedium var. peruvianum, commonly known as the Peruvian zygopetalum, is a hybrid orchid native to the Andes region of South America.

It features showy, fragrant flowers with a distinctive zygomatic structure, which gives it its scientific name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a white lip, and bloom during the cooler months.

It requires high humidity and indirect light to thrive, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
