Zenkerella citrina

Zenkerella citrina, commonly known as the yellow tamarind tree, is a tropical plant native to parts of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale green to yellowish bark and its compound leaves that are alternate and pinnate. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that give way to elongated, woody pods containing edible seeds. These seeds are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of sustenance.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and cultural contexts.
