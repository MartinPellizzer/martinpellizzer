Zygogynum semecarpoides var. semecarpoides

Zygogynum semecarpoides var. semecarpoides, commonly known as the African butterbean, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences, followed by distinctive, flat, bean-like fruits. Its leaves are palmately lobed and have a slightly hairy texture, while the plant is adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and fragrance.
