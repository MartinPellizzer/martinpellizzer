Zanthoxylum zeylanicum

Zanthoxylum zeylanicum, commonly known as the black pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are opposite and pinnate, with small, elliptical leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries containing numerous seeds. Its bark and leaves contain various bioactive compounds, making it valuable in traditional medicine.

The plant is also cultivated for its ornamental value and for the extraction of essential oils.
