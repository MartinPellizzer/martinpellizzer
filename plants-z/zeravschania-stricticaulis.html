Zeravschania stricticaulis

Zeravschania stricticaulis, commonly known as the narrow-stemmed alpine bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, slender stems and small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in a fine layer of hairs.

Zeravschania stricticaulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
