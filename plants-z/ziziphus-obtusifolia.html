Ziziphus obtusifolia

Ziziphus obtusifolia, commonly known as the blunt-leaf ziziphus, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible berries that range in color from red to black. Ziziphus obtusifolia is adapted to withstand drought and poor soil conditions, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.

It is also valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
