Zingiber vinosum

Zingiber vinosum, commonly known as the purple ginger, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers such as turmeric and cardamom. The plant is characterized by its ornamental purple-pink flowers that bloom in terminal inflorescences. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Zingiber vinosum is valued both for its aesthetic appeal and its potential medicinal uses.
