Zygophyllum reticulatum

Zygophyllum reticulatum, commonly known as the netleaf chenopod, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, adapted to harsh environmental conditions. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves arranged in a dense, bushy structure, which helps reduce water loss through transpiration. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, clustered in small groups, and it produces small, edible fruits that are consumed by various wildlife.

Zygophyllum reticulatum plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing food and shelter for desert animals.
