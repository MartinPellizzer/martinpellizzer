Zanthoxylum marambong

Zanthoxylum marambong, commonly known as the marambong prickly ash, is a member of the Rutaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Its bark and leaves contain compounds with potential medicinal properties, making it valuable in herbal remedies.

Zanthoxylum marambong thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.
