Zantedeschia albomaculata subsp. albomaculata

Zantedeschia albomaculata subsp.

albomaculata, commonly known as the white-spotted arum, is a member of the Araceae family. It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves with distinctive white spots and its dramatic, spatulate-shaped white flowers.

The plant produces a single, showy inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. It is native to southern Africa and thrives in moist, shady environments.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
