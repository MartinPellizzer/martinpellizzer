Ziziphora raddei

Ziziphora raddei, commonly known as Caucasian tea or mountain tea, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

Ziziphora raddei is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional remedies for digestive and respiratory ailments.
