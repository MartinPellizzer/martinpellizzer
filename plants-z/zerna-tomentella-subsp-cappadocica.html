Zerna tomentella subsp. cappadocica

Zerna tomentella subsp.

cappadocica, commonly known as the Cappadocian woolly zerna, is a perennial herb native to the Cappadocia region of Turkey. It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance.

The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in shades of white or pale pink.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
