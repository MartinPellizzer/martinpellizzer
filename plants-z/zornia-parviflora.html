Zornia parviflora

Zornia parviflora, commonly known as the smallflower zornia, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a low-growing annual herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, clustered pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often hairy, while its stems are slender and branching.

Zornia parviflora is native to the western United States and is often found in dry, disturbed soils.
