Zanthoxylum simulans var. imperforatum

Zanthoxylum simulans var. imperforatum, commonly known as the smooth prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. Its bark is rough and fissured, and the stems are often covered with small, stiff spines.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some historical use in traditional medicine.
