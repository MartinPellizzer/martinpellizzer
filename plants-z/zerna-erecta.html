Zerna erecta

Zerna erecta, commonly known as the upright millet, is a small annual grass native to temperate regions.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces tiny grains that are typically harvested for animal feed or traditional uses. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or fields.

Zerna erecta is valued for its hardiness and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
