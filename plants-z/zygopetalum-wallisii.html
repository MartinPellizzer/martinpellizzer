Zygopetalum wallisii

Zygopetalum wallisii, commonly known as the Wallis's zygopetalum, is a species of orchid native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces showy, fragrant flowers with a unique zygomorphic structure. The flowers typically feature a vibrant pink or red coloration with contrasting white or yellow markings. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil for optimal growth.

This orchid is popular among horticulturalists for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
