Zornia tenuifolia

Zornia tenuifolia, commonly known as slenderleaf zornia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, delicate leaves and clusters of pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its seeds are enclosed in a pod and can remain viable in the soil for several years.

Zornia tenuifolia is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
