Zingiber uncinatum

Zingiber uncinatum, commonly known as the black ginger or wild ginger, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other economically important plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant features a rhizomatous root system and produces striking purple to red flowers with a fragrant aroma. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a waxy surface.

Zingiber uncinatum is valued both for its ornamental qualities and its potential medicinal properties.
