Zingiber atrorubens

Zingiber atrorubens, commonly known as the black-red ginger, is a striking ornamental plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark red to black rhizomes and large, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom during the warm season. Zingiber atrorubens thrives in humid, well-drained soil and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
