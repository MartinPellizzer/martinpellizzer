Zauschneria californica subsp. angustifolia

Zauschneria californica subsp.

angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf goldcup, is a perennial plant native to California. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, orange-red flowers in late spring to early summer.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and inland habitats. Its flowers are tubular and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in native plant gardens.
