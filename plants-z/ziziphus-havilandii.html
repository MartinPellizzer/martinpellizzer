Ziziphus havilandii

Ziziphus havilandii, commonly known as Haviland's honey bush, is a deciduous shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for wildlife but not typically consumed by humans. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along canyon walls.

Ziziphus havilandii plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food and habitat for various desert species.
