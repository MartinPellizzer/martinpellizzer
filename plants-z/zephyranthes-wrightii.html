Zephyranthes wrightii

Zephyranthes wrightii, commonly known as the Wright's rain lily, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking white or pale pink flowers during the spring and early summer. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the flowers are borne on short, erect stems.

Zephyranthes wrightii is adapted to seasonal flooding and is often found in areas with high water tables.
