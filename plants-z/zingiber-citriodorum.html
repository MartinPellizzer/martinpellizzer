Zingiber citriodorum

Zingiber citriodorum, commonly known as citron ginger, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, lemon-scented leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
