Zerna atlantica

Zerna atlantica, commonly known as the Atlantic zerna, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the Atlantic Ocean.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dune ecosystems.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
