Zingiber sirindhorniae

Zingiber sirindhorniae, commonly known as the Thai ginger or Sirindhorn's ginger, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other economically important plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant is characterized by its tall, erect rhizomes and showy inflorescences with brightly colored bracts. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has potential medicinal uses.
