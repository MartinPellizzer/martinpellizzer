Zornia setifera

Zornia setifera, commonly known as slender zornia, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in clusters along the stems. Zornia setifera has a shallow root system and is adapted to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments.

It is often found in disturbed soils and is used in some traditional herbal remedies.
