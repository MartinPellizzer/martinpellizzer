Zieria pilosa var. pauciflora

Zieria pilosa var. pauciflora, commonly known as the sparse-flowered zieria, is a low-growing shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale pink flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
