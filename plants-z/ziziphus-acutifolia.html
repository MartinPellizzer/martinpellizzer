Ziziphus acutifolia

Ziziphus acutifolia, commonly known as the spiny date palm, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and compound leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, date-like fruits that are typically consumed fresh or dried. It is well adapted to drought conditions and thrives in poor soils, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.

Ziziphus acutifolia is also valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
