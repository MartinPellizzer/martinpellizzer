Zingiber flavidum

Zingiber flavidum, commonly known as the yellow ginger, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers and turmeric. The plant features a rhizomatous stem and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers with a strong, spicy aroma. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a waxy texture.

Zingiber flavidum is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its potential medicinal properties.
