Zygophyllum paradoxum

Zygophyllum paradoxum, commonly known as the paradoxical leafscale, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Zygophyllaceae and is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves that reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and limited water availability.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
