Zornia dichotoma

Zornia dichotoma, commonly known as the forked zornia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing annual herb that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are often hairy and have a lanceolate shape. Its small, pink to red flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom during the spring and early summer.

Zornia dichotoma is adapted to arid environments and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.
