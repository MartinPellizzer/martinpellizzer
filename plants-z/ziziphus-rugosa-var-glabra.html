Ziziphus rugosa var. glabra

Ziziphus rugosa var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth Chinese date, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rough, bark-like stems and small, elliptical leaves that are usually smooth on both sides. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, sweet-tart fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it adaptable to various climates.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.
