Zanthoxylum juglandifolium

Zanthoxylum juglandifolium, commonly known as the Chinese prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and have serrated margins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for humans and birds. Its bark and leaves contain bioactive compounds, making it valuable in traditional medicine.

The species is also cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and winter interest.
