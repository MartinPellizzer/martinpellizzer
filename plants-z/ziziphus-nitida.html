Ziziphus nitida

Ziziphus nitida, commonly known as the silver leaf date palm, is a small, evergreen shrub or tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and small, ovate leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery-white layer of hair. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by small, fleshy fruits that are edible but not widely cultivated for food. Ziziphus nitida is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy plant suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value comes from its distinctive foliage and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
