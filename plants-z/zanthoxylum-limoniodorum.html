Zanthoxylum limoniodorum

Zanthoxylum limoniodorum, commonly known as the lemon-leaf prickly ash, is a small evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lemon-scented leaves and small yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are a source of food for various wildlife species. Its bark and leaves contain compounds that have been used in traditional medicine.

Zanthoxylum limoniodorum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.
