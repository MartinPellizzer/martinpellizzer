Zigara

Zigara, commonly known as the nightshade plant, is a member of the Solanaceae family.

It is characterized by its trumpet-shaped flowers, which typically bloom in shades of purple or white. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that contain toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals. Zigara thrives in temperate climates and is often found in gardens or wild areas.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and potentially dangerous.
