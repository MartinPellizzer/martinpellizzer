Zieria laevigata var. laxiflora

Zieria laevigata var. laxiflora, commonly known as the slender zieria, is a small shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and woodland areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
