Zauschneria argentea

Zauschneria argentea, commonly known as the silver curl.

This perennial plant is native to the western United States and is characterized by its striking silver-white, curling leaves. It produces showy, orange-red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance contribute to its appeal in both ornamental gardening and ecological landscapes.
