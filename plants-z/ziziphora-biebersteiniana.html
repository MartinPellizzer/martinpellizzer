Ziziphora biebersteiniana

Ziziphora biebersteiniana, commonly known as the Caucasian zizyphus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and southern Russia.

It is a perennial herb with aromatic, slightly hairy leaves that are typically lance-shaped. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and digestive properties.

Ziziphora biebersteiniana thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate to cold climates.
