Ziziphus aucheri

Ziziphus aucheri, commonly known as the silver-leafed buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its grayish bark and small, oval-shaped leaves that turn reddish in autumn. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in early summer, followed by small, fleshy fruits that are edible for birds and some mammals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Ziziphus aucheri is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh climates.
