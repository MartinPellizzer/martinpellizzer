Ziziphora interrupta

Ziziphora interrupta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and has small, narrow leaves with a grayish-green color. Its flowers are usually pink or purple and bloom in clusters during the summer months.

Ziziphora interrupta is often used in traditional medicine for its purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.
