Zanthoxylum pashanense

Zanthoxylum pashanense, commonly known as the Pashan prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Its bark and leaves contain various bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional medicine.

Zanthoxylum pashanense thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.
