Zanthoxylum brisasanum

Zanthoxylum brisasanum, commonly known as the Brazilian prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are edible for some wildlife but generally considered toxic to humans. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in its native habitat.
