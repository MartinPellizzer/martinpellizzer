Zanthoxylum thomense

Zanthoxylum thomense, commonly known as the Mexican prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to central and southern Mexico.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated margins and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are often consumed by birds and small mammals.

Zanthoxylum thomense is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
