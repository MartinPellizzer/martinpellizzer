Zanthoxylum kauaense var. tenuifolium

Zanthoxylum kauaense var. tenuifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf prickly ash, is a member of the Rutaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of the Pacific Islands.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree, characterized by its pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by red, fleshy fruits resembling berries. Its bark and leaves contain various bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional medicine.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded, humid environments.
