Zingiber mioga

Zingiber mioga, commonly known as the Japanese ginger or mountain ginger, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers like turmeric and cardamom. The plant features fleshy, rhizomatous roots and narrow, sword-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically pale yellow or white, with three petals and three sepals.

The rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary spice, offering a spicy, peppery flavor.
