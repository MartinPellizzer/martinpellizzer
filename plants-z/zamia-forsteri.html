Zamia forsteri

Zamia forsteri, commonly known as the Mexican cycad, is a member of the Zamiaceae family and is native to Mexico.

It is a slow-growing, evergreen plant with a stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of stiff, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces cones rather than flowers, with male and female cones on separate plants. Zamia forsteri is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and thrives in well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
