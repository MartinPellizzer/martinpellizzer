Zerna canadensis subsp. richardsonii

Zerna canadensis subsp.

richardsonii, commonly known as the Richardson's zerna, is a perennial grass native to North America. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky slopes.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and maintaining grassland biodiversity.
