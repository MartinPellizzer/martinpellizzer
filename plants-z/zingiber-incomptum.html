Zingiber incomptum

Zingiber incomptum, commonly known as the slender ginger, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers such as turmeric and cardamom. The plant features a rhizomatous stem with long, narrow leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, often with a mild aromatic scent.

Zingiber incomptum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
