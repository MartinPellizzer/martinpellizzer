Zosterophyllanthos reptans

Zosterophyllanthos reptans, commonly known as the creeping zosterophyllum, is a rare and ancient plant species belonging to the group of early land plants.

It is characterized by its simple, ribbon-like leaves that grow along a low, creeping stem. The plant exhibits a unique combination of aquatic and terrestrial adaptations, suggesting it may have thrived in transitional wetland environments. Its structure resembles some of the earliest known plant fossils, indicating a possible link to the evolution of vascular plants.

Due to its rarity and ecological significance, it is of great interest to botanists studying plant evolution.
