Zanthoxylum becquetii

Zanthoxylum becquetii, commonly known as the African prickly ash, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often glandular and have a pungent odor. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red, fleshy berries. Its bark and leaves contain compounds that have been used traditionally for their medicinal properties.

Zanthoxylum becquetii thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in savanna habitats.
