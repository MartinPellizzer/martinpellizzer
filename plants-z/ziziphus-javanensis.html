Ziziphus javanensis

Ziziphus javanensis, commonly known as the longan tree, is a tropical evergreen species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rhamnaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, sweet, and juicy fruits that are encased in a hairy, reddish husk. Ziziphus javanensis thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.

Its wood is also valued for its durability and is used in traditional crafts and construction.
