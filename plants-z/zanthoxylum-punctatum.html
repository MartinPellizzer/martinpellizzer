Zanthoxylum punctatum

Zanthoxylum punctatum, commonly known as the prickly ash or toothache tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often serrated and have a distinctive spicy scent. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. Its bark and leaves contain compounds that have been traditionally used for their medicinal properties.

Zanthoxylum punctatum thrives in woodland habitats and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.
