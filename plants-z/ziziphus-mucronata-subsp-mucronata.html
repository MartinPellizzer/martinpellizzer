Ziziphus mucronata subsp. mucronata

Ziziphus mucronata subsp.

mucronata, commonly known as the Natal date palm, is a small, evergreen shrub or tree native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible, date-like fruits.

The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, demonstrating remarkable drought tolerance. Its fruit is often consumed by both humans and animals, and it plays a role in traditional medicine.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in degraded areas.
