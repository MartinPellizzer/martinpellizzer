Zerna occidentalis

Zerna occidentalis, commonly known as western zerna, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of blue or purple. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces seeds enclosed in a small, dry fruit. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens as an ornamental plant.
