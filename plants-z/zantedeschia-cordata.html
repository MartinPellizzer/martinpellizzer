Zantedeschia cordata

Zantedeschia cordata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved arum, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa.

It features striking, arrow-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a prominent midrib. The plant produces clusters of spatulate, white or pale pink flowers with a distinctive, glossy spathe. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

Zantedeschia cordata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
