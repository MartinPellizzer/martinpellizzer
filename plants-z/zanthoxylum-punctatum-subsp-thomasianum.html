Zanthoxylum punctatum subsp. thomasianum

Zanthoxylum punctatum subsp.

thomasianum, commonly known as Thomas's prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are edible and often used in traditional medicine.

Its thorny stems and aromatic foliage make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
