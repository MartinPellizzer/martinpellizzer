Zappania odoratissima

Zappania odoratissima, commonly known as the fragrant orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers with a strong, pleasant fragrance. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its leaves are broad and glossy, and it produces showy blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and unique scent, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
