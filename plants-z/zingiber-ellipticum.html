Zingiber ellipticum

Zingiber ellipticum, commonly known as the elliptic ginger or mountain ginger, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other well-known spices like ginger and turmeric. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, and it produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. Zingiber ellipticum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.

Its rhizomes, while not as commonly used as those of ginger, contain compounds with potential therapeutic applications.
