Zinnia peruviana

Zinnia peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian zinnia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to red and orange. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are borne in clusters at the ends of the stems, and they are often used in ornamental gardening.

Zinnia peruviana thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking colorful, drought-tolerant plants.
