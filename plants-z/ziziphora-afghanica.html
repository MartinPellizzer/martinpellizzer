Ziziphora afghanica

Ziziphora afghanica, commonly known as Afghan tea or mountain tea, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including Afghanistan, Pakistan, and parts of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or violet in color. Ziziphora afghanica is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves, which are used in traditional herbal teas and for their medicinal properties.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate to cold climates.
