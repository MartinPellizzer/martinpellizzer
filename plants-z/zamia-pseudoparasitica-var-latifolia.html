Zamia pseudoparasitica var. latifolia

Zamia pseudoparasitica var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf cycad, is a member of the Zamiaceae family and is native to southern Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, pinnately compound leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces distinctive cones, with male and female cones occurring on separate plants. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded, moist environments.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
