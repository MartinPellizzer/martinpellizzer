Zaluzania indica

Zaluzania indica, commonly known as the purpleleaf sida, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant features alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, pale purple flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in late summer.

Zaluzania indica is adapted to moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
