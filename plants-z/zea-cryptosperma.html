Zea cryptosperma

Zea cryptosperma, commonly known as the narrowleaf maize, is a species of grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptation to dry conditions. Its seed heads are often overlooked due to their diminutive size, but they play a role in its reproductive cycle.

Botanically, Zea cryptosperma is closely related to other members of the grass family, Poaceae, and is of interest for its ecological and genetic significance.
