Ziziphus spina-christi var. inermis

Ziziphus spina-christi var. inermis, commonly known as the Christ's thorn jujube, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Middle East and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish-green leaves that turn reddish in autumn. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor. It thrives in arid and semi-arid climates, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its historical and cultural significance, it is also valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
