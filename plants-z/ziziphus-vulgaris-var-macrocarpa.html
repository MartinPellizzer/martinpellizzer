Ziziphus vulgaris var. macrocarpa

Ziziphus vulgaris var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited Chinese date, is a shrub or small tree native to China and parts of Southeast Asia.

It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into edible, sweet, and sticky fruits. The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a serrated margin. The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its fruit and ornamental value.

It is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
