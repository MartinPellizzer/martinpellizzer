Zygophyllum heterocladum

Zygophyllum heterocladum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved jointvetch, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its small, compound leaves and yellowish-pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a deep root system, allowing it to survive in nutrient-poor and drought-prone soils. It is often found in sandy or rocky habitats, contributing to soil stabilization.

Its ecological importance includes providing food and habitat for various native fauna.
