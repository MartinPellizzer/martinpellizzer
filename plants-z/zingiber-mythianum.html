Zingiber mythianum

Zingiber mythianum, commonly known as the sacred ginger of Southeast Asia, is a rhizomatous herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of Indonesia and the Philippines.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other well-known spices like ginger and turmeric. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces fragrant, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and digestive properties.

Zingiber mythianum is also valued in cultural rituals and is often associated with spiritual significance in local communities.
