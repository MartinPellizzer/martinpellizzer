Zizaniopsis killipii

Zizaniopsis killipii, commonly known as the Killip's grass, is a perennial grass species native to certain regions of the United States.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically less than 10 millimeters wide. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets, which are typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland or riparian habitats.
