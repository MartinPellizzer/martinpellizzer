Zaluzania eminens

Zaluzania eminens, commonly known as the tall ironweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Its showy purple to pink flower heads appear in late summer and are composed of many tiny florets.

Zaluzania eminens is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
