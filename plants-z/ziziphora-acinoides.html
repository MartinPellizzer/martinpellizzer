Ziziphora acinoides

Ziziphora acinoides, commonly known as sweet everlasting, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray leaves and small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Ziziphora acinoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
