Zenobia floribunda

Zenobia floribunda, commonly known as the golden flame lily, is a striking perennial herb native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features tall, erect stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, adorned with broad, lance-shaped leaves that are a deep green color. The plant is renowned for its showy, clustered inflorescences of bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. Its botanical name reflects its ornamental value, with "floribunda" highlighting its profuse flowering habit.

It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
