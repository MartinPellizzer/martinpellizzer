Zebrina

Zebrina, commonly known as the water spinach or river spinach, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its long, slender leaves that are often ribbed and have a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white in color. Zebrina thrives in moist, warm environments and is often grown in shallow water or wet soil.

It is valued for its edible leaves and is commonly used in various cuisines around the world.
