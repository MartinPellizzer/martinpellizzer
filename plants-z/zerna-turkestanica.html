Zerna turkestanica

Zerna turkestanica, commonly known as the Turkestan millet, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to Central Asia and is characterized by its small, spike-like inflorescences and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, demonstrating a high degree of drought tolerance. It produces small grains that are used as a food source in some regions, though it is not as widely cultivated as other millet species.

Zerna turkestanica is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in sandy or degraded lands.
