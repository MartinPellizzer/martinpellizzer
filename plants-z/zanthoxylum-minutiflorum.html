Zanthoxylum minutiflorum

Zanthoxylum minutiflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are edible for some wildlife but considered toxic to humans. Its foliage consists of opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly rough texture.

Zanthoxylum minutiflorum is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
