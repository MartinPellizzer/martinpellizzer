Zizia aurea f. obtusifolia

Zizia aurea f. obtusifolia, commonly known as the smooth golden alexander, is a variant of the golden alexander species native to North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist meadows and open woodlands. The plant features basal leaves that are rounded and often have a bluish-gray hue due to dense pubescence. Its yellow flowers appear in early summer, forming flat-topped clusters.

This subspecies is characterized by its obtuse leaf shape, distinguishing it from the typical form of Zizia aurea.
