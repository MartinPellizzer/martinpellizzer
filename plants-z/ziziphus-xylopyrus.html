Ziziphus xylopyrus

Ziziphus xylopyrus, commonly known as the Chinese date or the desert date, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of China, India, and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, elliptical leaves, and small, yellowish-green fruits that ripen to a reddish-brown color. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in poor soils, making it well-suited for harsh environments. Its fruit is edible, though often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Ziziphus xylopyrus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing food for wildlife in its native habitats.
