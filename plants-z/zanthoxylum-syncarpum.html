Zanthoxylum syncarpum

Zanthoxylum syncarpum, commonly known as the prickly ash or Indian prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces bright red berries that are clustered in groups of three, a feature that gives it its specific epithet "syncarpum.". The plant is also notable for its thorny branches and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
