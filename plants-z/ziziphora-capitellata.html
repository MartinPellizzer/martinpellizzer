Ziziphora capitellata

Ziziphora capitellata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved savory, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Ziziphora capitellata is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rocky or gravelly habitats.

It is also valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
