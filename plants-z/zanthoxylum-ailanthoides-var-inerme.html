Zanthoxylum ailanthoides var. inerme

Zanthoxylum ailanthoides var. inerme, commonly known as the Chinese prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces bright red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties have made it a subject of interest in horticulture and traditional medicine.
