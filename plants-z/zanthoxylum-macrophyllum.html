Zanthoxylum macrophyllum

Zanthoxylum macrophyllum, commonly known as the large-leaved prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often arranged in pairs and have serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. Its bark is rough and has a distinctive, aromatic scent.

Zanthoxylum macrophyllum is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
