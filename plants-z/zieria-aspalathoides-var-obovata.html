Zieria aspalathoides var. obovata

Zieria aspalathoides var. obovata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf zieria, is a small evergreen shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features dense, compact foliage with narrow, obovate leaves that are typically covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathland and coastal scrub environments.

Due to its ornamental value and habitat specificity, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
