Ziziphus mauritiana var. mauritiana

Ziziphus mauritiana var. mauritiana, commonly known as the Indian date palm, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It has compound leaves that are alternate and often have a reddish or purplish hue when young. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into edible, date-like fruits. Its root system is deep and drought-resistant, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is used in traditional medicine.
