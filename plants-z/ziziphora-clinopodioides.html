Ziziphora clinopodioides

Ziziphora clinopodioides, commonly known as wild thyme or creeping thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in summer. The plant has narrow, aromatic leaves that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Ziziphora clinopodioides is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
