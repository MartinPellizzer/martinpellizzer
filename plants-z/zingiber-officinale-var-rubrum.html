Zingiber officinale var. rubrum

Zingiber officinale var. rubrum, commonly known as red ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red to orange-colored pseudostems and large, showy flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces aromatic rhizomes that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. Its botanical features include broad, lance-shaped leaves and a distinctive, spicy aroma.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
