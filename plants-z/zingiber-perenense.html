Zingiber perenense

Zingiber perenense, commonly known as the everlasting ginger or perennial ginger, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant features rhizomatous stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with a strong, spicy fragrance.

Zingiber perenense is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal properties.
