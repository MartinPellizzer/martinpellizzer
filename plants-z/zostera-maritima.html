Zostera maritima

Zostera maritima, commonly known as shoal grass, is a seagrass species found in coastal areas of the Atlantic Ocean.

It forms dense underwater meadows that provide essential habitat for various marine organisms. The plant has long, ribbon-like leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. It reproduces through flowering, with small, inconspicuous flowers that develop into fruiting structures.

Zostera maritima plays a crucial role in stabilizing sediments and improving water quality in marine ecosystems.
