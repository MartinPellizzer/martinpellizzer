Zygophyllum oxianum

Zygophyllum oxianum, commonly known as the desert raisin, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, the Middle East, and parts of Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, exhibiting adaptations to extreme drought conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the rainy season. Its leaves are usually small and succulent, helping to conserve water.

Zygophyllum oxianum is also known for its edible fruits, which are used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some regions.
