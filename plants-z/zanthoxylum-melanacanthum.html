Zanthoxylum melanacanthum

Zanthoxylum melanacanthum, commonly known as the black-spined prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are opposite and often have a serrated margin. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. Its bark is thick and fissured, with a distinctive aromatic scent.

Zanthoxylum melanacanthum is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
