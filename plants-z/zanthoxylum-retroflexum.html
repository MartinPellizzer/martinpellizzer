Zanthoxylum retroflexum

Zanthoxylum retroflexum, commonly known as the prickly ash or Indian prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern and central United States.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its compound leaves and fragrant, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for some animals but generally considered bitter by humans.
