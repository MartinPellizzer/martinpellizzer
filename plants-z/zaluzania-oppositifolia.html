Zaluzania oppositifolia

Zaluzania oppositifolia, commonly known as the oppositeleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are opposite on the stem, giving it its common name. Its showy purple to pink flowers appear in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Zaluzania oppositifolia typically grows in moist, open habitats like prairies and woodland edges.
