Zieria furfuracea

Zieria furfuracea, commonly known as the hairy zieria, is a small, evergreen shrub native to the eastern regions of Australia.

It typically grows in heathlands and dry sclerophyll forests, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. The plant features dense, hairy foliage that gives it its common name, and its small, white or pale pink flowers appear in spring. Zieria furfuracea is known for its compact growth habit and ornamental value in gardens.

It is also valued for its ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.
