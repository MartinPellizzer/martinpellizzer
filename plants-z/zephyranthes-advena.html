Zephyranthes advena

Zephyranthes advena, commonly known as the rain lily, is a species of flowering plant native to the southern United States, Central America, and parts of South America.

It is a bulbous perennial that produces striking, showy white or pale pink flowers during the rainy season. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to thrive in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Zephyranthes advena is also known for its ability to bloom rapidly after rainfall, making it a symbol of renewal and resilience.
