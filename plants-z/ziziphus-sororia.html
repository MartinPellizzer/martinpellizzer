Ziziphus sororia

Ziziphus sororia, commonly known as the desert date or Indian date tree, is a small to medium-sized deciduous shrub or tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, the Middle East, and South Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often alternate and have a leathery texture, and its small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or red when ripe, containing a single seed surrounded by a fleshy pulp. Ziziphus sororia is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a resilient plant in harsh environments.

It is also valued for its ecological role in supporting wildlife and its historical use in traditional medicine.
