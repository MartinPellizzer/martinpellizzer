Zanthoxylum podocarpum

Zanthoxylum podocarpum, commonly known as the prickly ash or Chinese prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically 5 to 9 pairs of oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by bright red berries, which are edible but have a sharp, acidic flavor. Zanthoxylum podocarpum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rutaceae family.
