Zanthoxylum lomincola

Zanthoxylum lomincola, commonly known as the narrowleaf prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds and small mammals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and streamside habitats.

Zanthoxylum lomincola is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
