Zornia reticulata var. elongata

Zornia reticulata var. elongata, commonly known as the long-stemmed zornia, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in clusters along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Zornia reticulata var. elongata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding plant life.
