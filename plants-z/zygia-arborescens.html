Zygia arborescens

Zygia arborescens, commonly known as the tree hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Americas.

It is characterized by its woody stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, or white. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough texture.

Zygia arborescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
