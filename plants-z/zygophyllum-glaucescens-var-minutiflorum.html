Zygophyllum glaucescens var. minutiflorum

Zygophyllum glaucescens var. minutiflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered desert buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its small, pale green leaves and clusters of tiny, pale yellow flowers. The plant has a deep root system that allows it to survive in nutrient-poor, sandy soils. It is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low rainfall, making it a resilient component of desert ecosystems.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to the biodiversity of its habitat.
