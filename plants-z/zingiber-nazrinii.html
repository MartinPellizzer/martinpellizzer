Zingiber nazrinii

Zingiber nazrinii, commonly known as the Kashmiri ginger, is a rare and ornamental plant native to the Himalayan regions of Jammu and Kashmir.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other well-known species like ginger and turmeric. The plant features rhizomatous growth and produces attractive, sword-shaped leaves with a deep green hue. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, with a fragrant aroma.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable species for botanical conservation efforts.
