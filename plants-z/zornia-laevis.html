Zornia laevis

Zornia laevis, commonly known as the smooth zornia, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically has a sprawling growth habit and produces small, dry legumes as its fruit. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Zornia laevis is also valued for its role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.
