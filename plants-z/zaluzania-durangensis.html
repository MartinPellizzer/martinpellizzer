Zaluzania durangensis

Zaluzania durangensis, commonly known as Durango tickseed, is a flowering plant native to the grasslands and open woodlands of central Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces showy yellow to orange composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.
