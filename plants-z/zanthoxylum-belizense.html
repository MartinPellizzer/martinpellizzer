Zanthoxylum belizense

Zanthoxylum belizense, commonly known as the Belize prickly ash, is a small evergreen tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its spiny branches and oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that give way to red, fleshy berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in horticultural settings.
