Zollikoferia nudicaulis

Zollikoferia nudicaulis, commonly known as the naked stem fritillary, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, hairless stems that are often prostrate or ascending, with small, pale green leaves that are lance-shaped and lack stipules. The plant produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white or pale pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
