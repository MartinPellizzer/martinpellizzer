Zinnia greggii

Zinnia greggii, commonly known as the desert zinnia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant, daisy-like flowers that range in color from yellow to red and orange. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Zinnia greggii has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
