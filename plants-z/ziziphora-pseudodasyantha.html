Ziziphora pseudodasyantha

Ziziphora pseudodasyantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ziziphora, is a herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Ziziphora pseudodasyantha is often used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
