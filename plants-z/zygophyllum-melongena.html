Zygophyllum melongena

Zygophyllum melongena, commonly known as the red sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, the Middle East, and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Zygophyllaceae and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences, which bloom during the warm seasons. Its leaves are typically reduced to small scales or spines, minimizing water loss through transpiration.

Zygophyllum melongena is adapted to thrive in sandy soils and is often found in desert ecosystems where it plays a role in stabilizing dunes and supporting local biodiversity.
