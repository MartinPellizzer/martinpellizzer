Ziziphus melastomoides

Ziziphus melastomoides, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a small evergreen shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by red or orange berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to hot, dry climates.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
