Zerna adjarica

Zerna adjarica, commonly known as the Adjara wheat, is a wild grass species native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny grains. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in rocky or semi-arid environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in traditional agriculture and ecological restoration projects.
