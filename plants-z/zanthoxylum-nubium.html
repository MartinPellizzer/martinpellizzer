Zanthoxylum nubium

Zanthoxylum nubium, commonly known as the clouded prickly ash, is a species of deciduous shrub native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically divided into several narrow, serrated leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible for some animals. Zanthoxylum nubium thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

Its bark and leaves contain various bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional medicine.
