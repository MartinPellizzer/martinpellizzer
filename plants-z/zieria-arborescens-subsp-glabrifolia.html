Zieria arborescens subsp. glabrifolia

Zieria arborescens subsp.

glabrifolia, commonly known as the smooth-leaved zieria, is a shrub native to the eastern coast of Australia. It typically grows in heathlands and forest margins, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features small, elliptical leaves that are smooth and often covered in a fine white wax. Its flowers are white to pale yellow and are clustered in dense inflorescences, adding ornamental value. Zieria arborescens subsp.

glabrifolia is valued for its ecological role and is often used in native garden landscaping.
