Zea mays var. polelythra

Zea mays var. polelythra, commonly known as the Mexican popcorn corn, is a variant of maize characterized by its unique kernel structure.

The plant produces small, hard-shelled kernels that are typically harvested when they are dry and fully developed. It is native to Mexico and is often cultivated for its ornamental and culinary uses. The botanical features include tall, sturdy stalks with narrow leaves and compact tassels.

This variety is valued for its genetic diversity and historical significance in traditional agriculture.
