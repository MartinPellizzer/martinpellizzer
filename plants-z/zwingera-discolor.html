Zwingera discolor

Zwingera discolor, commonly known as the variable zwingera, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink, depending on environmental conditions. The plant has a compact, shrub-like growth habit with slender, often hairy stems. Its leaves are typically narrow and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Zwingera discolor is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
