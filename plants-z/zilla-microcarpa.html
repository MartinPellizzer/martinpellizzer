Zilla microcarpa

Zilla microcarpa, commonly known as the dwarf zilla, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Zilla microcarpa is valued in gardens for its ornamental foliage and ability to attract beneficial insects.
