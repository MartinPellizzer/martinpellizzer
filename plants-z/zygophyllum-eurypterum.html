Zygophyllum eurypterum

Zygophyllum eurypterum, commonly known as the sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring and summer months. Its leaves are often covered in a fine layer of white powder, which helps reduce water loss through transpiration.

Zygophyllum eurypterum is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it a resilient species in desert ecosystems.
