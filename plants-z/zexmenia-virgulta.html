Zexmenia virgulta

Zexmenia virgulta, commonly known as the silverleaf zexmenia, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a silvery or grayish pubescence, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, typically blooming during the rainy season. Zexmenia virgulta is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
