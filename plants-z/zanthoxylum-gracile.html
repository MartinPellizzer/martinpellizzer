Zanthoxylum gracile

Zanthoxylum gracile, commonly known as the slender prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, berry-like fruits. Its bark and leaves contain compounds such as alkaloids and are used in traditional medicine.

Zanthoxylum gracile thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.
