Zornia gracilis f. chaetophora

Zornia gracilis f. chaetophora, commonly known as the hairy zornia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in spikes, which are typical of the genus Zornia. Its hairy foliage and stems are distinguishing features of this particular form, f. chaetophora.

It is often found in moist, open habitats such as woodland edges and wet meadows.
