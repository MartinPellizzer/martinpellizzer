Zingeria biebersteiniana subsp. trichopoda

Zingeria biebersteiniana subsp.

trichopoda, commonly known as the woolly zingeria, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its dense, woolly covering of white or gray hairs on its stems and leaves, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to full sun exposure. Its leaves are usually fleshy and cylindrical, with a bluish-green hue.

It produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding ornamental value to its appearance.
