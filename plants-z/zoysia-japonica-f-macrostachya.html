Zoysia japonica f. macrostachya

Zoysia japonica f. macrostachya, commonly known as the Japanese zoysia grass, is a variant of the zoysia genus native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, dense foliage and a mat-forming growth habit, making it suitable for lawns and ground cover. The plant produces tall, narrow panicles of flowers in late summer, which are distinctive and contribute to its ornamental value. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, though it can tolerate some shade.

This form is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and resilience in various climatic conditions.
