Zingiber truncatum

Zingiber truncatum, commonly known as the dwarf ginger, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers such as turmeric and cardamom. The plant features upright, narrow leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. Its rhizomes are sometimes used in traditional medicine, though it is less commercially cultivated than other ginger species.

Zingiber truncatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and unique scent.
