Zephyranthes fosteri

Zephyranthes fosteri, commonly known as the Foster's rain lily, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a bulbous perennial that produces showy, white or pale pink flowers during the spring and early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a central bulb.

Zephyranthes fosteri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to naturalize in suitable climates.
