Zingiber nigrum

Zingiber nigrum, commonly known as black ginger, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other economically important plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with a distinct, fragrant aroma.

The rhizomes of Zingiber nigrum are used in traditional medicine and as a spice, similar to other ginger species.
