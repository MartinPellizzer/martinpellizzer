Zamia ortgiesii

Zamia ortgiesii, commonly known as the Mexican cycad, is a member of the Zamiaceae family and is native to Mexico.

It is a slow-growing, evergreen plant characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of feathery, pinnate leaves. The plant produces cones rather than flowers, with male and female cones occurring on separate plants. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid environment.

Zamia ortgiesii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
