Zallia

Zallia, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.
