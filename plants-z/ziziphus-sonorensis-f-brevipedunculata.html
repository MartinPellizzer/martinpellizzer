Ziziphus sonorensis f. brevipedunculata

Ziziphus sonorensis f. brevipedunculata, commonly known as the short-stalked honey mesquite, is a shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically 5 to 7 pairs of leaflets, and its small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source for wildlife. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, demonstrating resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in desert ecosystems and a subject of interest in ecological and horticultural studies.
