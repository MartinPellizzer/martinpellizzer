Ziziphus sativa

Ziziphus sativa, commonly known as the Chinese date or Indian date, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Asia.

It produces small, yellowish-green fruits that ripen to a dark red or purple color. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and thorny branches, which provide structural support in its natural habitat. Ziziphus sativa is often cultivated for its edible fruits, which are rich in nutrients and used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to arid and semi-arid environments.
