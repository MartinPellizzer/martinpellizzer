Zingiber acuminatum

Zingiber acuminatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ginger, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers such as turmeric and cardamom. The plant features slender, sword-shaped leaves and produces fragrant, yellowish-orange flowers with red bracts. Its rhizomes, similar to ginger, are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

Zingiber acuminatum thrives in warm, humid climates and is typically grown in shaded, well-drained soils.
