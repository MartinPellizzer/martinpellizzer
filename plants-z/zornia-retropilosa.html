Zornia retropilosa

Zornia retropilosa, commonly known as the backward-pilose zornia, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant has trifoliate leaves, with each leaflet being ovate and hairy on the underside. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments.

Zornia retropilosa is native to the eastern United States and is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
