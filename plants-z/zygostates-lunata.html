Zygostates lunata

Zygostates lunata, commonly known as the moonflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, white, night-blooming flowers that emit a strong fragrance. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, round fruits that contain seeds. Zygostates lunata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its unique nocturnal blooming habit.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
