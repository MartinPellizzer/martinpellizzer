Zinnia ghiesbreghtii

Zinnia ghiesbreghtii, commonly known as the Mexican zinnia, is a species native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant, daisy-like flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and produces showy blooms throughout the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a grayish-green color.

Zinnia ghiesbreghtii is a popular choice for gardens due to its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
