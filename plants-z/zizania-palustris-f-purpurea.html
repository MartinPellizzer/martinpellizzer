Zizania palustris f. purpurea

Zizania palustris f. purpurea, commonly known as purple wild rice, is a striking wetland grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 3 meters in height, often growing in shallow water or moist soil. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spikelet-like flowers that are typically purple or reddish in color, giving it its distinctive appearance. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green hue.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ecological role in supporting aquatic wildlife.
