Ziziphora clinopodioides var. biebersteiniana

Ziziphora clinopodioides var. biebersteiniana, commonly known as the Caucasian woodruff, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It features aromatic, narrow leaves that are often hairy, and it produces small, blue to purple flowers clustered in spikes. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to temperate climates. It is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Lamiaceae family.
