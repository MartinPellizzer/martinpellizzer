Zanthoxylum mauiense var. cranwelliae

Zanthoxylum mauiense var. cranwelliae, commonly known as the Maui prickly ash, is a rare and endemic species native to Hawaii.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its spiny branches and small, elliptical leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers followed by red, fleshy fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in coastal and dry forest habitats, often growing on rocky or limestone substrates.
