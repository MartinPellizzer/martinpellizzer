Zanthoxylum caribaeum subsp. nelsonii

Zanthoxylum caribaeum subsp.

nelsonii, commonly known as the Caribbean prickly ash, is a shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean region. It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often arranged in pairs and have a serrated edge.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that give way to red, fleshy berries. Its bark and leaves contain various bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional medicine.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and forest edges.
