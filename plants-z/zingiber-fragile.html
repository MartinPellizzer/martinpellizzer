Zingiber fragile

Zingiber fragile, commonly known as the delicate ginger, is a rare and endangered species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a rhizomatous herb characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves and small, pale yellow flowers. The plant thrives in shaded, humid environments with well-drained soil, making it a challenging species to cultivate. Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and potential medicinal properties.

Due to habitat loss, conservation efforts are critical for its survival.
