Zippelia lappacea

Zippelia lappacea, commonly known as the Lapp's lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Zippelia lappacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.

It is also known for its resilience to cold climates and its ability to bloom in early spring.
