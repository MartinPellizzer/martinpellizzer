Ziziphus ruffa

Ziziphus ruffa, commonly known as the desert date or Indian date, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, the Middle East, and southern Asia.

It is characterized by its woody, thorny stems and compound leaves that are typically alternate and pinnate. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by edible, fleshy fruits resembling dates, which are often consumed fresh or processed. Ziziphus ruffa is well adapted to harsh environments, thriving in poor soils and with minimal water requirements.

It plays an important role in traditional medicine and as a source of food in desert ecosystems.
