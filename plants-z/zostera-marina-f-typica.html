Zostera marina f. typica

Zostera marina f. typica, commonly known as eelgrass, is a marine flowering plant found in shallow coastal waters.

It has long, ribbon-like leaves that are typically bright green and can grow up to several meters in length. The plant forms dense underwater meadows that provide critical habitat for various marine species. It plays a vital role in coastal ecosystems by stabilizing sediments and improving water quality.

Zostera marina f. typica is also an important source of food for marine animals such as sea turtles and waterfowl.
