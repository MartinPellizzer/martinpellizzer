Zingiber corallinum

Zingiber corallinum, commonly known as the coral ginger, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, coral-red bracts that surround its small, yellow flowers. The plant belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes ginger and turmeric. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Zingiber corallinum thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
