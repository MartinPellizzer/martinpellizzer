Zeyheria fluviatilis

Zeyheria fluviatilis, commonly known as the river sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along riverbanks, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves that are usually arranged in dense clusters at the base. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are clustered in spikelets, and it produces distinctive, boat-shaped fruits.

Zeyheria fluviatilis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
