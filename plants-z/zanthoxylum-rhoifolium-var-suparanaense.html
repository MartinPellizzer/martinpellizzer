Zanthoxylum rhoifolium var. suparanaense

Zanthoxylum rhoifolium var. suparanaense, commonly known as the black pepper tree, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that mature to a dark red or black color, which are used as a spice in various cuisines. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses.
