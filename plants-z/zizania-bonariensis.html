Zizania bonariensis

Zizania bonariensis, commonly known as the Brazilian wild rice, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks that can grow up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces panicles of small, golden-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green color.

Zizania bonariensis is often found in wetlands and along the edges of rivers, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils.
