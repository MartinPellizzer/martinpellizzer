Zeocriton commune

Zeocriton commune, commonly known as the silverleaf cactus, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features a compact, globular stem covered in dense, silvery-gray hairs that help reduce water loss and reflect sunlight. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking contrast to its muted foliage. Its leaves are modified into spines, which provide protection from herbivores and aid in reducing transpiration.

Zeocriton commune is well adapted to harsh desert environments, making it a resilient and drought-tolerant species.
