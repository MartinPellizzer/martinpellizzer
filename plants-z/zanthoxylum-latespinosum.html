Zanthoxylum latespinosum

Zanthoxylum latespinosum, commonly known as the late spiny prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife.

Its bark and leaves contain compounds that have been used in traditional medicine for their astringent and anti-inflammatory properties.
