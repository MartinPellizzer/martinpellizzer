Zoroxus

Zoroxus, commonly known as the silverleaf spurge, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is characterized by its succulent, thick leaves that are often covered in a waxy, silvery coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in a structure called a cyathium, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Zoroxus is native to regions of Africa and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
