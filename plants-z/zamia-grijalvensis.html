Zamia grijalvensis

Zamia grijalvensis, commonly known as the Grijalve Zamia, is a cycad-like plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the family Zamiaceae and is characterized by its stout, unbranched stem and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces cones, with male and female cones typically occurring on separate plants. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded, moist environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
