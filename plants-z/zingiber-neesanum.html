Zingiber neesanum

Zingiber neesanum, commonly known as the Nesan ginger, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other well-known gingers such as turmeric and cardamom. The plant is characterized by its upright rhizomes, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, and it produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters.

Zingiber neesanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its potential medicinal properties.
