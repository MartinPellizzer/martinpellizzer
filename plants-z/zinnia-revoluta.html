Zinnia revoluta

Zinnia revoluta, commonly known as the sunset zinnia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant, pompon-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange and red. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and has hairy, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Zinnia revoluta is an annual plant that produces seeds in late summer, contributing to its widespread cultivation.
