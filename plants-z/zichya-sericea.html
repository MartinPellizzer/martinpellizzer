Zichya sericea

Zichya sericea, commonly known as the silky ziziphus, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, fleshy berries. Zichya sericea is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, with deep roots that help it access water from deeper soil layers.

It is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental qualities.
