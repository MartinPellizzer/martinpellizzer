Zingiber brevifolium

Zingiber brevifolium, commonly known as the short-leaved ginger, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other economically important plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically shorter than those of its relatives. Its flowering stems produce clusters of fragrant, yellowish flowers, which are often used in traditional medicine.

The rhizomes of Zingiber brevifolium are also utilized for their aromatic and medicinal properties.
