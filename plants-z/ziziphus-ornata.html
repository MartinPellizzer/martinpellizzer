Ziziphus ornata

Ziziphus ornata, commonly known as the velvet tamarisk, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically a dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits that are edible for both humans and animals. Ziziphus ornata is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species that can thrive in poor soil conditions.

Its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil make it a useful plant in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
