Zephyranthes mexicana

Zephyranthes mexicana, commonly known as the rainflower or Mexican rain lily, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of the southern United States.

It is a bulbous perennial that produces striking, white or pale pink flowers during the rainy season. The plant features strap-like, grassy leaves that are typically evergreen, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. Zephyranthes mexicana is adapted to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates, often blooming after periods of rainfall.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
