Ziziphus saharae

Ziziphus saharae, commonly known as the Saharan date tree, is a small shrub native to the arid regions of North Africa.

It is characterized by its small, waxy leaves and yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often consumed by local wildlife and humans in some regions. It is well adapted to harsh desert conditions, with deep roots and a low water requirement.

Ziziphus saharae plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and supporting biodiversity in its native habitat.
