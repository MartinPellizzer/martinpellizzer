Zornia reticulata var. punctata

Zornia reticulata var. punctata, commonly known as the spotted zornia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves with distinctive white or yellow spots. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in clusters along the stems. Its root system is fibrous, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in woodland edges and wetlands.

Zornia reticulata var. punctata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators and soil health.
