Zeuxine tjiampeana

Zeuxine tjiampeana, commonly known as the Congo buttercup, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the tropical rainforests of Central Africa. The plant features a short rhizome with several fleshy, tuberous roots. Its leaves are palmately compound, with three to five leaflets that are usually ovate to lanceolate in shape.

The flowers are bright yellow, with five sepals and numerous stamens, and are typically borne on long, slender pedicels.
