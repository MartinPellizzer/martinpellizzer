Zantedeschia albomaculata

Zantedeschia albomaculata, commonly known as the white-spotted arum, is a striking tropical plant native to southern Africa.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves with a glossy green surface and distinctive white spots. The plant produces an exotic, spatulate-shaped inflorescence with a showy, pinkish-white spathe and a spadix that bears tiny, clustered flowers. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant's unique botanical structure makes it a popular choice for botanical gardens and private collections.
