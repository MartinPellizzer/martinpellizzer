Zingiber zerumbet var. littoralis

Zingiber zerumbet var. littoralis, commonly known as the coastal ginger or littoral ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its ornamental, strap-like leaves that are often variegated with white and green patterns. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, adding visual interest to its appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for shaded garden areas.

This variant is particularly adapted to coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils.
