Zingiber sianginensis

Zingiber sianginensis, commonly known as the Shillong ginger, is a rhizomatous herb native to the northeastern regions of India.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other economically important plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine and as a spice, similar to common ginger.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
