Zornia papuensis

Zornia papuensis, commonly known as the Papuan zornia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Papua.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Zornia papuensis is often found in open woodland and grassland habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in its native range.
