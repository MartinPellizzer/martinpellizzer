Zeia tenera

Zeia tenera, commonly known as the silky dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, soft stems and leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or partially shaded areas.

Zeia tenera is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
