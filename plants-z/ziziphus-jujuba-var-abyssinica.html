Ziziphus jujuba var. abyssinica

Ziziphus jujuba var. abyssinica, commonly known as the Ethiopian date plum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Ethiopia and parts of Eritrea.

It produces small, yellow or red fruits that are edible and have a sweet, tart flavor similar to dates. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid climates, often growing in rocky or sandy soils. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a glossy green appearance.

The fruit is typically harvested when fully ripe, and the plant is valued for both its fruit and its ecological role in desert ecosystems.
