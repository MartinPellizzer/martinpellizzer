Zinnia floridana

Zinnia floridana, commonly known as the Florida zinnia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and produces vibrant yellow to orange blooms. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are sturdy and branching.

Zinnia floridana thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
