Zamioculcas boivinii

Zamioculcas boivinii, commonly known as the ZZ plant, is a member of the Araceae family and is native to eastern Africa.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its upright, dark green, waxy leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and requires minimal watering due to its drought-tolerant nature. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to store water efficiently, making it ideal for indoor cultivation.

The ZZ plant is also noted for its air-purifying qualities, making it a popular choice for interior spaces.
