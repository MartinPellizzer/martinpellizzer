Zenopogon cytisoides

Zenopogon cytisoides, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has slender, upright stems and small, compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and long-lasting blooms.
