Ziziphus elliptica

Ziziphus elliptica, commonly known as the Indian date palm, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to southern and southeastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically elliptical in shape, hence its scientific name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, fleshy fruits that are similar in appearance to dates. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its fruit and ornamental value.

Ziziphus elliptica is also valued for its ability to withstand drought and poor soil conditions.
