Zeravschania pastinacifolia

Zeravschania pastinacifolia, commonly known as the Turkestan scorzonera, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptability to harsh climates.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in alpine and arid botany.
