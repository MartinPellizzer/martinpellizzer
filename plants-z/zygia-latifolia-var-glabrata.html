Zygia latifolia var. glabrata

Zygia latifolia var. glabrata, commonly known as the smooth-leaf zygia, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, smooth leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
