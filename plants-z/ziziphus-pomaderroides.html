Ziziphus pomaderroides

Ziziphus pomaderroides, commonly known as the sand apple or sour apple, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, the Middle East, and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, small leaves, and clusters of yellowish-green flowers that give way to fleshy, orange-red fruits. The fruit is edible, though generally tart, and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits. The plant is well adapted to harsh environments, with deep roots that allow it to survive in drought conditions.

Its ecological importance includes providing food for wildlife and stabilizing sandy soils in its native habitats.
