Zygopetalum intermedium

Zygopetalum intermedium, commonly known as the intermediate zygopetalum, is a species of orchid native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its sympodial growth habit and the presence of pseudobulbs, which store water and nutrients. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers with a unique structure, featuring a spurred lip and a contrasting coloration between the dorsal and lateral sepals. Its leaves are typically elliptical and arranged in two rows along the stem.

Zygopetalum intermedium is often cultivated for its ornamental value in horticultural settings.
