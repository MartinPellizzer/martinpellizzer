Zygopetalum wendlandii discolor

Zygopetalum wendlandii discolor, commonly known as the Wendland's zygopetalum, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that come in a range of colors, including white, pink, and lavender. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are long, narrow, and often have a waxy texture, which helps it retain moisture in its arid environment.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts worldwide.
