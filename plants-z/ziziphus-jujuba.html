Ziziphus jujuba

Ziziphus jujuba, commonly known as the Chinese date, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It produces small, yellow to red fruits that are rich in sugars, vitamins, and antioxidants. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid regions. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a hairy texture, while its flowers are greenish-white and appear in clusters.

The fruit's thick, leathery skin encases a sweet, chewy pulp with a high sugar content.
