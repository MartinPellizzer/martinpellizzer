Zingiber zerumbet var. valenzuelae

Zingiber zerumbet var. valenzuelae, commonly known as the purple ginger or velvet ginger, is a striking ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear clusters of vibrant purple or pink flowers, which resemble orchids. The plant produces heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are glossy and broad, adding to its visual appeal. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant indoors.
