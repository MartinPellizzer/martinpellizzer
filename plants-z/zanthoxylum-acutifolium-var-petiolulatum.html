Zanthoxylum acutifolium var. petiolulatum

Zanthoxylum acutifolium var. petiolulatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are edible for birds and some mammals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native ecosystems and a subject of interest in horticulture and ecology.
