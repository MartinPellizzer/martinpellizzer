Zanthoxylum azuense

Zanthoxylum azuense, commonly known as the Huayna Picchu pepper, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of Peru.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, red berries. The plant produces a pungent, spicy fruit that is used in traditional Andean cuisine. Its botanical features include thorny stems and aromatic, glandular leaves.

Zanthoxylum azuense is adapted to high-altitude environments and plays a role in local ecosystems.
