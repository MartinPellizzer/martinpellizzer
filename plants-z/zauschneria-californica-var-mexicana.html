Zauschneria californica var. mexicana

Zauschneria californica var. mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican goldpoppy, is a striking perennial plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its bright orange to red flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-tolerant nature. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
