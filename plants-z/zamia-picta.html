Zamia picta

Zamia picta, commonly known as the painted cycad, is a member of the Zamiaceae family and is native to Mexico.

It is a gymnosperm with a distinctive appearance, featuring a stout, woody trunk and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces cones rather than flowers, with male and female cones occurring on separate plants. Its leaves are often adorned with a unique pattern of green and white markings, giving it its common name.

Zamia picta is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
