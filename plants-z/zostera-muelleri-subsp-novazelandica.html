Zostera muelleri subsp. novazelandica

Zostera muelleri subsp.

novazelandica, commonly known as New Zealand sea grass, is a flowering plant native to the coastal waters of New Zealand. It is a submerged aquatic plant that forms dense underwater meadows, providing essential habitat for marine life.

The plant has long, ribbon-like leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. It reproduces through rhizomes and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are pollinated by water.

This species plays a vital role in stabilizing sediments and maintaining water quality in marine ecosystems.
