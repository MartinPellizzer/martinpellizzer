Zygogynum longifolium

Zygogynum longifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved zygogynum, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Zygogynum longifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
