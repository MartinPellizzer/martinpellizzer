Zanthoxylum dissitum var. dissitum

Zanthoxylum dissitum var. dissitum, commonly known as the Chinese prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are usually opposite and have serrated margins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and stream banks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional medicinal uses in some cultures.
