Zanthoxylum lentiscifolium

Zanthoxylum lentiscifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated margins and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but have a sharp, acidic taste. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open woodlands.
