Zhaoanthus proanthus var. validus

Zhaoanthus proanthus var. validus, commonly known as the Pacific silver fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to western North America.

It is characterized by its flat, needle-like leaves that are dark green on top and whitish underneath. The tree produces cones that are typically reddish-brown and contain seeds surrounded by a papery wing. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

The species is valued for its timber and its role in forest ecosystems.
