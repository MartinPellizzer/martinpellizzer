Ziziphus spina-christi var. aucheri

Ziziphus spina-christi var. aucheri, commonly known as the Christ's thorn jujube, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It produces small, reddish-brown fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source. The plant has compound leaves with three to five oval-shaped leaflets, and its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in axillary inflorescences. It thrives in arid and semi-arid climates, showing drought tolerance and adaptability to poor soils.

The fruit is rich in nutrients and has been historically valued for its medicinal properties.
