Ziziphora galinae

Ziziphora galinae, commonly known as Armenian tea, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense spikes. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a strong, mint-like fragrance.

Ziziphora galinae is often used in traditional medicine for its purported digestive and calming properties.
