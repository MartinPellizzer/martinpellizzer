Zanthoxylum aubertia

Zanthoxylum aubertia, commonly known as the prickly ash or yellow prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves, which are often covered in fine, stiff hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. Its bark and twigs contain a variety of bioactive compounds, making it valuable in traditional medicine.

The plant's ornamental value comes from its vibrant fall foliage and unique, spiny structure.
