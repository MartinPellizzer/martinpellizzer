Zingiber calcicola

Zingiber calcicola, commonly known as the rock ginger, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments. The plant features rhizomatous growth and produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a waxy texture.

Zingiber calcicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
