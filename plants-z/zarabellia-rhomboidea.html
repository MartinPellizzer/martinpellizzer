Zarabellia rhomboidea

Zarabellia rhomboidea, commonly known as the diamond-shaped bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive rhomboid-shaped leaves and bell-like, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in full sun. It is a perennial species that produces small, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds.

Due to its unique morphology and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
