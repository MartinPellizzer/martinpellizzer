Zornia marajoara

Zornia marajoara, commonly known as the Marajoara zornia, is a flowering plant native to the Amazon Basin, particularly found in Brazil.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Zornia marajoara is adapted to wetland and riparian habitats, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and contributing to soil stabilization.
