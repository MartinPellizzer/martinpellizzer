Zygophyllum guyotii

Zygophyllum guyotii, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing shrub with succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain water in harsh environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access groundwater in dry soils.

Zygophyllum guyotii plays an important ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
