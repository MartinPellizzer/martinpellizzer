Zanthoxylum nilagiricum

Zanthoxylum nilagiricum, commonly known as Nilagiri prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are usually alternate and pinnately arranged, with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by bright red, fleshy berries that are edible. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Zanthoxylum nilagiricum is valued for its ornamental features and has traditional uses in local medicine.
