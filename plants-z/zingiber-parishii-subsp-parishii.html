Zingiber parishii subsp. parishii

Zingiber parishii subsp.

parishii, commonly known as the Khasi ginger, is a rhizomatous herbaceous plant native to the northeastern regions of India. It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other economically important plants like ginger and turmeric.

The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-white flowers. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique species within the genus Zingiber.
