Zygogynum crassifolium

Zygogynum crassifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved zygogynum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, thick, and leathery leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Zygogynum crassifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some local cultures.
