Zygophyllum iliense

Zygophyllum iliense, commonly known as the Ilian sandmat, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Zygophyllaceae and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and succulent, fleshy leaves adapted to arid environments. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is well-suited to drought conditions. It has a low-growing habit and is often found in desert or semi-desert regions.

Zygophyllum iliense plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and is of interest for its ecological and potential medicinal properties.
