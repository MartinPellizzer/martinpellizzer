Zingiber sylvestre

Zingiber sylvestre, commonly known as wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant features rhizomes that are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, while its flowers are typically white or pale yellow.

Zingiber sylvestre is valued for its spicy, aromatic qualities and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
