Zizaniopsis bonariensis

Zizaniopsis bonariensis, commonly known as the common barnyard grass, is an annual grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Due to its aggressive growth, it is considered a weed in many regions.
