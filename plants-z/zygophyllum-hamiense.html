Zygophyllum hamiense

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its fleshy, water-storing stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. Its adaptations to drought include thick cuticles and reduced leaf surface area.
