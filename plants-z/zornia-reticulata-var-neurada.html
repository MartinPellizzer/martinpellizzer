Zornia reticulata var. neurada

Zornia reticulata var. neurada, commonly known as the Neurada zornia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The species is noted for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitats.
