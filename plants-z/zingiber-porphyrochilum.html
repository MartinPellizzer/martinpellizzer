Zingiber porphyrochilum

Zingiber porphyrochilum, commonly known as the purple-lipped ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers and turmeric. The plant is characterized by its vibrant, purple-hued lips on its flowers, which are typically bright red or orange. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine and as a spice, though less commonly than other ginger species.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
