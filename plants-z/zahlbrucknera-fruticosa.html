Zahlbrucknera fruticosa

Zahlbrucknera fruticosa, commonly known as the red-flowered broom, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its dense, sprawling growth habit and bright red flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring. The plant has small, oval leaves that are typically covered in a fine, white pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant in warmer climates.
