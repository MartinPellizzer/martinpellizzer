Zornia confusa

Zornia confusa, commonly known as the Florida zornia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, with five petals. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a hairy texture.

Zornia confusa thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
