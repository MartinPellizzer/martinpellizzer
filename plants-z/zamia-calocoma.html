Zamia calocoma

Zamia calocoma, commonly known as the Mexican cycad, is a member of the Zamiaceae family and is native to Mexico.

It is a slow-growing, evergreen plant characterized by its stout, trunk-like stem and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces cones, with male and female cones typically appearing on separate plants. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate to bright indirect light.

Zamia calocoma is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and adaptability to indoor conditions.
