Zanthoxylum micranthum

Zanthoxylum micranthum, commonly known as the small-flowered prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are often consumed by birds and other wildlife.

Its bark and leaves contain various bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional medicine and botanical research.
