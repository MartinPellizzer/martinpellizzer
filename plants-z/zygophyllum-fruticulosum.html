Zygophyllum fruticulosum

Zygophyllum fruticulosum, commonly known as the prickly gooseberry, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is a shrub characterized by its small, yellow flowers and spiny stems, which help it survive in harsh environments. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces red or orange fruits that are edible but have a tangy, sour flavor. It is adapted to drought conditions, with deep roots that allow it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Zygophyllum fruticulosum plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing food for various wildlife species in its native habitat.
