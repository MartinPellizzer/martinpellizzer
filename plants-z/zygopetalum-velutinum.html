Zygopetalum velutinum

Zygopetalum velutinum, commonly known as the velvet orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its velvety, dark green leaves and produces striking, fragrant flowers with a distinctive lip. The plant typically blooms in late summer, featuring flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Zygopetalum velutinum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance.
