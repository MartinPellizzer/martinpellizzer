Zanthoxylum javanicum

Zanthoxylum javanicum, commonly known as the Java prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. Its leaves and fruits contain various bioactive compounds, making it valuable in traditional medicine.

The plant is also used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive foliage and unique appearance.
