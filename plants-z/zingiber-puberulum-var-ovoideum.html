Zingiber puberulum var. ovoideum

Zingiber puberulum var. ovoideum, commonly known as the oval ginger or ovoid ginger, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers such as turmeric and cardamom. The plant features rhizomatous stems with lanceolate, hairy leaves that are typically a deep green color. Its inflorescence consists of clusters of fragrant, yellowish-white flowers, which are often surrounded by colorful bracts.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and floral display.
