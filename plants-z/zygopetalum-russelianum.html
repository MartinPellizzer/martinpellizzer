Zygopetalum russelianum

Zygopetalum russelianum, commonly known as the Russell's zygopetalum, is a species of orchid native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are elongated and fleshy, adapted to thrive in high-altitude environments.

Zygopetalum russelianum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
