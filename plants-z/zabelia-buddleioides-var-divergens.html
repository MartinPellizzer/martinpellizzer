Zabelia buddleioides var. divergens

Zabelia buddleioides var. divergens, commonly known as the spreading zabelia, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal landscapes.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental value make it a popular choice among gardeners in Mediterranean climates.
