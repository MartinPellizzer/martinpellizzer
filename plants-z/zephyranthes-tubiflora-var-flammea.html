Zephyranthes tubiflora var. flammea

Zephyranthes tubiflora var. flammea, commonly known as the flame bellflower, is a striking bulbous plant native to Central and South America.

It produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to red, often with a distinctive red or orange throat. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers. Its blooming period typically occurs in the spring and early summer, adding a burst of color to the landscape.

The varietal name 'flammea' refers to its fiery, vibrant floral display.
