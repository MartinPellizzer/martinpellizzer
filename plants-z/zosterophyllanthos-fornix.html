Zosterophyllanthos fornix

Zosterophyllanthos fornix, commonly known as the hooded zosterophyllum, is a rare and ancient plant with unique botanical features.

It belongs to the group of early land plants, believed to have evolved during the Silurian period. The plant exhibits a distinctive morphology with a central axis and lateral branches, resembling a fan-like structure. Its reproductive structures are simple, consisting of sporangia that produce spores for propagation.

Due to its limited distribution and fragile nature, it is considered a valuable subject for studying early plant evolution.
