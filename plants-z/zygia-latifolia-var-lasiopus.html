Zygia latifolia var. lasiopus

Zygia latifolia var. lasiopus, commonly known as the broadleaf zygia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features opposite, elliptical leaves with a smooth upper surface and a hairy underside. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by round, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
