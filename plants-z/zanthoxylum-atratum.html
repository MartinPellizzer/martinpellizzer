Zanthoxylum atratum

Zanthoxylum atratum, commonly known as the black prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are usually 4 to 8 inches long and have serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that give way to red, fleshy berries in the fall. Its bark is rough and ridged, often with a distinctive, aromatic scent.

Zanthoxylum atratum is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
