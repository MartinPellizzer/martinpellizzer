Ziziphus pernettyoides

Ziziphus pernettyoides, commonly known as the Chinese date or Indian date tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It produces small, yellowish-green fruits that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its fruit and ornamental value.

The bark and leaves are also used in herbal remedies for their purported health benefits.
