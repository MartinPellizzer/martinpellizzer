Zinnia linearis var. latifolia

Zinnia linearis var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf zinnia, is a striking annual plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from yellow to red. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with a branching structure that supports its vibrant blooms. Its flowers are composed of a central disk surrounded by ray florets, which are often more prominent than the central petals.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and floral arrangements.
