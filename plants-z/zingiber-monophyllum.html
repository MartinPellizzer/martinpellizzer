Zingiber monophyllum

Zingiber monophyllum, commonly known as the white ginger or white arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other economically important plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant features a rhizomatous root system and produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy texture.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
