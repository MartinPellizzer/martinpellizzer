Zephyranthes nelsonii

Zephyranthes nelsonii, commonly known as the Nelson's rain lily, is a species of flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a bulbous perennial that produces showy, white or pale pink flowers during the spring and early summer. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to grow in wetland or swampy environments. It is often found in areas with high humidity and partial shade.

The flowers are typically short-lived but attract pollinators such as bees and moths.
