Ziziphora glomerata

Ziziphora glomerata, commonly known as sweet everlasting, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in dense clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a fragrant aroma when crushed.

Ziziphora glomerata is often used in traditional medicine for its purported calming and antispasmodic properties.
