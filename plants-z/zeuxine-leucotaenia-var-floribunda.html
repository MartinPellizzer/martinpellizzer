Zeuxine leucotaenia var. floribunda

Zeuxine leucotaenia var. floribunda, commonly known as the white-flowered zeuxine, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant features delicate, hairy stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, with five sepals and numerous stamens.

It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.
