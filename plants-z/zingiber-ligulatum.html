Zingiber ligulatum

Zingiber ligulatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf ginger, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers such as turmeric and cardamom. The plant features long, narrow leaves and produces fragrant, yellowish flowers arranged in spike-like inflorescences. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Zingiber ligulatum is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its potential medicinal properties.
