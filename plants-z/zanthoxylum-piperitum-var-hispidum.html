Zanthoxylum piperitum var. hispidum

Zanthoxylum piperitum var. hispidum, commonly known as the Japanese prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces red berries that are edible but have a sharp, spicy flavor. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental gardens.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive addition to both ecological and horticultural landscapes.
