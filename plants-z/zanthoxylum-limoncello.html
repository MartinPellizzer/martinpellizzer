Zanthoxylum limoncello

Zanthoxylum limoncello, commonly known as the lemon thorn or yellow prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, citrus-scented flowers and its compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, red berries that are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for certain wildlife. Its bark and roots contain compounds that have been used in traditional medicine.

The plant's aromatic properties and distinctive appearance make it a notable species in its ecological habitat.
