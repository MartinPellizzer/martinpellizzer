Zephyranthes elegans

Zephyranthes elegans, commonly known as the rain lily, is a species of flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a bulbous perennial that produces showy, white or pale pink flowers with a subtle fragrance. The plant typically blooms after rainfall, hence its common name, and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands. Its leaves are strap-shaped and evergreen, providing a striking contrast to its ephemeral blooms.

Zephyranthes elegans is appreciated for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
