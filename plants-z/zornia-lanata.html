Zornia lanata

Zornia lanata, commonly known as downy zornia, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, lobed leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks. It is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in temperate climates.

Zornia lanata is known for its ecological value, providing food for various insects and birds.
