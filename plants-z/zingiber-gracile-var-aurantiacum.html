Zingiber gracile var. aurantiacum

Zingiber gracile var. aurantiacum, commonly known as the golden ginger, is a compact, herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are bright green with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, which bloom in clusters along the stems. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine and as a culinary spice, similar to other ginger species.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
