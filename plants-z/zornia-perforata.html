Zornia perforata

Zornia perforata, commonly known as the punctured zornia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing annual herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive perforated or fenestrated appearance, which is a key botanical characteristic. Its flowers are clustered in spikes and are usually pink to purple in color, with five petals.

Zornia perforata is adapted to arid environments and is often found in sandy or gravelly soils.
