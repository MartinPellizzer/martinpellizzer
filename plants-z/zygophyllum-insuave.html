Zygophyllum insuave

Zygophyllum insuave, commonly known as the bitter prickly-pear, is a shrub native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the family Zygophyllaceae and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, fleshy leaves. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Zygophyllum insuave is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability.
