Zanthoxylum okinawense

Zanthoxylum okinawense, commonly known as the Okinawan prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub native to the Ryukyu Islands of Japan.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for birds and small mammals.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the subtropical flora in its native habitat.
