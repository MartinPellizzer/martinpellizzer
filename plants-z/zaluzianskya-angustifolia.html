Zaluzianskya angustifolia

Zaluzianskya angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf zaluzianskya, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of delicate, blue to violet flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in gardens with Mediterranean climates.
