Zygophyllum aegyptium

Zygophyllum aegyptium, commonly known as the Egyptian candleplant, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Zygophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, the Middle East, and parts of Asia. It has adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability.

Zygophyllum aegyptium is often found in sandy soils and can thrive in areas with high temperatures and low rainfall.
