Ziziphus thyrsiflora

Ziziphus thyrsiflora, commonly known as the Chinese date or Indian date tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Asia.

It produces small, yellowish-green berries that ripen to a reddish-brown color, containing a single seed. The plant has compound leaves with 5 to 9 pairs of leaflets, and it thrives in arid and semi-arid climates. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, arranged in panicles, and it is often cultivated for its edible fruit and ornamental value.

The plant is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
