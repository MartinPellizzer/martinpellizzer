Zanonia integerrima

Zanonia integerrima, commonly known as the roughleaf zanonia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces elliptical, woody fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Zanonia integerrima is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
