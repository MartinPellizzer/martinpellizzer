Zingiber fraseri var. nervifolium

Zingiber fraseri var. nervifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, rhizomatous growth habit and striking, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive veining pattern. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
