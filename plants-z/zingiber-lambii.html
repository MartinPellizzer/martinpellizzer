Zingiber lambii

Zingiber lambii, commonly known as the golden ginger or lambii ginger, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers and turmeric. The plant features a rhizomatous root system and produces tall, narrow leaves with a glossy surface. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in inflorescences that resemble a spike.

Zingiber lambii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
