Zanthoxylum guianense

Zanthoxylum guianense, commonly known as the Guianese prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America, particularly found in the Amazon Basin.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are often used in traditional medicine.

Its bark and leaves contain various bioactive compounds, making it of interest for pharmacological research.
