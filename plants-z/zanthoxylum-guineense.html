Zanthoxylum guineense

Zanthoxylum guineense, commonly known as African prickly ash, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. Its bark and leaves contain bioactive compounds, making it valuable in traditional medicine.
