Zingiber ultralimitale

Zingiber ultralimitale, commonly known as the ultralimital ginger, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the high-altitude regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers, but it exhibits unique botanical characteristics. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in a spiral arrangement. Its rhizomes are prized for their medicinal properties, though they are rarely cultivated due to the plant's elusive nature.
