Ziziphora rotundifolia

Ziziphora rotundifolia, commonly known as round-leaf germander, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of pink or purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in open habitats such as slopes and grasslands. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs like mint and oregano.

Ziziphora rotundifolia is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
