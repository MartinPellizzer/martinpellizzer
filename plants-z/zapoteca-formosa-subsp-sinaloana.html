Zapoteca formosa subsp. sinaloana

Zapoteca formosa subsp.

sinaloana, commonly known as the Sinaloan zapoteca, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Mexico. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits.

The plant typically grows at elevations between 1,500 and 2,500 meters, thriving in temperate climates with well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces small, legume-like pods that contain seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local communities.
