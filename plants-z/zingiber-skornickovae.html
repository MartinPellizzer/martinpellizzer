Zingiber skornickovae

Zingiber skornickovae, commonly known as the Skornikovae ginger, is a rare and lesser-known species of ginger native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect rhizomes and lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in a spike, which are followed by elongated, finger-like fruits. Its botanical features make it distinct from more common ginger species, though it remains understudied.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical research.
