Zanthoxylum accedens

Zanthoxylum accedens, commonly known as the Brazilian prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and spiny branches. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. Its fruit is an important food source for various wildlife species.
