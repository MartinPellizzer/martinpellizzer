Zingiber officinale var. sichuanense

Zingiber officinale var. sichuanense, commonly known as Sichuan ginger, is a variant of the ginger plant native to the Sichuan province of China.

It is characterized by its thick, aromatic rhizomes that are used in traditional Chinese medicine and culinary applications. The plant produces tall, leafy stems with small, pale green flowers that bloom in the summer. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, contributing to its ornamental value.

This variety is valued for both its medicinal properties and its use in regional cuisines.
