Zingiber leucochilum

Zingiber leucochilum, commonly known as the white-lipped ginger, is a rare and ornamental plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers and related species. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
