Ziziphora clinopodioides subsp. pseudodasyantha

Ziziphora clinopodioides subsp.

pseudodasyantha, commonly known as the woolly ziziphora, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.

The plant features slender, aromatic stems and small, opposite leaves covered in fine, white hairs. Its flowers are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences and are usually pink to purple in color.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
