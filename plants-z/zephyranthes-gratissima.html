Zephyranthes gratissima

Zephyranthes gratissima, commonly known as the sweet rainflower, is a species of flowering plant native to the southeastern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a bulbous plant that produces delicate, star-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, typically blooming in response to rainfall. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a central bulb.

Zephyranthes gratissima is noted for its ability to bloom throughout the year, depending on environmental conditions.
