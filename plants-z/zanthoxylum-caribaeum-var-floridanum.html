Zanthoxylum caribaeum var. floridanum

Zanthoxylum caribaeum var. floridanum, commonly known as the Florida prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, bright red berries that are edible for some wildlife but toxic to humans. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in its native habitat.
