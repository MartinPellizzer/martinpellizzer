Zingiber multibracteatum

Zingiber multibracteatum, commonly known as the multibract ginger, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers and turmeric. The plant features tall, slender stems with broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. Its inflorescence consists of clusters of fragrant, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink.

Zingiber multibracteatum is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its potential medicinal properties.
