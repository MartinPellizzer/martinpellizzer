Zephyranthes traubii

Zephyranthes traubii, commonly known as the rain lily, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a bulbous perennial that produces showy, white or pale pink flowers during the rainy season. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to thrive in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in open areas such as savannas and forest margins.

Zephyranthes traubii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its brief but striking bloom.
