Zygophyllum connaroides

Zygophyllum connaroides, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, the Middle East, and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has a deep root system that allows it to survive in harsh, dry environments. Its leaves are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Zygophyllum connaroides plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and is adapted to thrive in low-nutrient, nutrient-poor soils.
