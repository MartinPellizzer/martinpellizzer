Zostera hornemanniana

Zostera hornemanniana, commonly known as the hornemannian eelgrass, is a marine flowering plant belonging to the family Zosteraceae.

It is a submerged aquatic plant that forms dense underwater meadows in shallow coastal waters. The plant has long, ribbon-like leaves that are typically 10 to 30 centimeters in length. It reproduces through both sexual and asexual means, with flowers that are adapted for underwater pollination.

Zostera hornemanniana plays a crucial role in coastal ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various marine organisms.
