Zingiber galanga

Zingiber galanga, commonly known as wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant features a rhizomatous root system and lance-shaped leaves that grow along the ground. Its flowers are typically orange or yellow, arranged in spikes, and it produces small, pale fruits.

Zingiber galanga is valued for its aromatic rhizomes, which are used in traditional medicine and cuisine.
