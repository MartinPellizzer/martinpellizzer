Zingiber striolatum

Zingiber striolatum, commonly known as the streaked ginger, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers such as turmeric and cardamom. The plant features tall, leafy stalks that can reach up to 2 meters in height, with bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice, though it is less commonly cultivated than other ginger species.

The plant's ornamental value is also appreciated in horticulture due to its striking foliage and floral display.
