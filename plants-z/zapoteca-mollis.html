Zapoteca mollis

Zapoteca mollis, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Mexican velvetleaf, is a perennial shrub native to Central America and northern South America.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves that give it its common name, and it typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds. Zapoteca mollis is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and agricultural fields.

It has been studied for its potential medicinal properties and ecological role in local ecosystems.
