Zea mays var. syrtica

Zea mays var. syrtica, commonly known as the Syrtic maize, is a variant of maize native to the arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and reduced leaf surface area, which helps it withstand harsh, drought-prone environments. The plant produces small, tightly packed ears of grain, often with a high proportion of starchy kernels. Its genetic makeup reflects adaptations to saline and alkaline soils, making it a valuable crop in marginal agricultural zones.

Due to its resilience, it has been studied for its potential in sustainable farming practices in challenging climates.
