Zanthoxylum alatum f. ferrugineum

Zanthoxylum alatum f. ferrugineum, commonly known as the rusty-leaf prickly ash, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated margins and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds and small mammals. Its bark and leaves contain compounds with medicinal properties, including pain-relieving and anti-inflammatory effects.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.
