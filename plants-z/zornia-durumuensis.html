Zornia durumuensis

Zornia durumuensis, commonly known as the Turkish zornia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Zornia durumuensis is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature and ability to stabilize soil.
