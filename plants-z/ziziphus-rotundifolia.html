Ziziphus rotundifolia

Ziziphus rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaf buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in the spring. The plant bears red or black berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species adapted to dry conditions.

Ziziphus rotundifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to local wildlife.
