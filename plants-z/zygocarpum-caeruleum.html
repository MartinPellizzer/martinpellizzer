Zygocarpum caeruleum

Zygocarpum caeruleum, commonly known as the blue-flowered zygocarp, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet petals and distinctive, elongated fruit capsules. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and prefers well-drained soil. Its flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Zygocarpum caeruleum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
