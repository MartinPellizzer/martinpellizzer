Zannichellia dentata

Zannichellia dentata, commonly known as the serrated water plantain, is a aquatic plant that thrives in shallow, freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often serrated along the edges. The plant produces small, white flowers that are typically clustered in spikes, which emerge above the water surface. Zannichellia dentata is often found in wetlands, ponds, and along the margins of streams.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
