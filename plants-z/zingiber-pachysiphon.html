Zingiber pachysiphon

Zingiber pachysiphon, commonly known as the giant ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, sword-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces striking orange-red flowers arranged in a spike, which bloom during the rainy season. Its rhizomes are edible and have a spicy, ginger-like flavor.

Zingiber pachysiphon is often cultivated for its ornamental value and culinary uses.
