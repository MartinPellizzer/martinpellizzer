Zinnia marylandica

Zinnia marylandica, commonly known as the Maryland zinnia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom from late summer to early fall. Zinnia marylandica is characterized by its simple, daisy-like flower structure with a central disk surrounded by ray florets.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking native ornamental plants.
