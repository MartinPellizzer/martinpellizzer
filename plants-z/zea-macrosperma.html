Zea macrosperma

Zea macrosperma, commonly known as the large-seeded maize, is a member of the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, robust stalks and large, tasseled inflorescences. The plant produces large, hard seeds that are typically used for animal feed or processed into corn products. Its growth habit is adapted to temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal development.

Zea macrosperma plays a significant role in agricultural systems due to its high yield and versatility.
