Zingiber viridiflavum

Zingiber viridiflavum, commonly known as the green-flowered ginger, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers and related plants. The plant is characterized by its elongated, sword-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in summer. Its rhizomes, similar to other gingers, are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

Zingiber viridiflavum is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
