Ziziphus venulosa

Ziziphus venulosa, commonly known as the slender ziziphus, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, red, fleshy fruits. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Ziziphus venulosa is well adapted to drought conditions and plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils.
