Zostera muelleri

Zostera muelleri, commonly known as the Mueller's eelgrass, is a marine flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is a seagrass species that grows in dense underwater meadows, typically in shallow, sandy or muddy substrates. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are adapted to withstand water currents and provide habitat for various marine organisms. Zostera muelleri plays a crucial role in stabilizing sediments and supporting biodiversity in coastal ecosystems.

Its flowering structures, which are small and inconspicuous, are adapted for underwater pollination.
