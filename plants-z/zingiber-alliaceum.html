Zingiber alliaceum

Zingiber alliaceum, commonly known as the garlic ginger or wild ginger, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers such as turmeric and cardamom. The plant features rhizomatous growth, with fleshy, aromatic underground stems that store nutrients. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, and it produces small, pale yellow flowers in clusters.

The rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine and cooking for their pungent, spicy flavor.
