Ziziphus mucronata f. obliqua

Ziziphus mucronata f. obliqua, commonly known as the narrow-leaved date palm, is a unique variant of the date palm native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted stems and narrow, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, sweet fruits similar to those of the common date palm. Its botanical features make it well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This form is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its distinct morphology and potential for cultivation in challenging climates.
