Zingiber pseudopungens

Zingiber pseudopungens, commonly known as the ginger lily, is a tropical herbaceous plant belonging to the Zingiberaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow. The plant produces rhizomes similar to true ginger, which can be used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Zingiber pseudopungens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.
