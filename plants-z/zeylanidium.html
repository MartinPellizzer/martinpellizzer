Zeylanidium

Zeylanidium, commonly known as the Ceylon cinnamon tree, is a member of the Lauraceae family and is native to Sri Lanka.

It is a small evergreen tree that produces fragrant, reddish-brown bark, which is the source of the spice cinnamon. The bark contains essential oils and is rich in compounds like cinnamaldehyde, which contribute to its aromatic properties. The plant thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil and partial shade.

It is widely cultivated for both its ornamental value and its economic importance as a spice.
