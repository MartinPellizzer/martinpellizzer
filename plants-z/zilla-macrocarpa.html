Zilla macrocarpa

Zilla macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited zilla, is a deciduous shrub native to western North America.

It is characterized by its slender, arching branches and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces distinctive, elongated fruits that are typically 2 to 4 inches long, containing several seeds. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Zilla macrocarpa thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open woodlands or along streambanks.
