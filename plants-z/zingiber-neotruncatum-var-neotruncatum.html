Zingiber neotruncatum var. neotruncatum

Zingiber neotruncatum var. neotruncatum, commonly known as the Neotropical ginger, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers and related plants. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often a deep green color, and it produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and red. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
