Zexmenia myrtifolia

Zexmenia myrtifolia, commonly known as the myrtle-leaved zexmenia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the eastern regions of Australia.

It typically grows in coastal heathlands and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a glossy green surface and a distinctive red or purple underside. Its flowers are small, white or pale pink, and appear in clusters during the spring and summer months.

Zexmenia myrtifolia is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and adaptability to coastal environments.
