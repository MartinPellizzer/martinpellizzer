Zingiber neglectum

Zingiber neglectum, commonly known as the wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant features rhizomatous stems and lance-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, clustered in terminal inflorescences.

Zingiber neglectum is valued for its fragrant foliage and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
