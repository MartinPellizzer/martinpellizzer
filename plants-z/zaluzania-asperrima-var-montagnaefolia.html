Zaluzania asperrima var. montagnaefolia

Zaluzania asperrima var. montagnaefolia, commonly known as the mountainleaf zigzag goldenrod, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a serrated edge. The plant produces yellow flowers in late summer, which are arranged in panicles and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its stems are typically zigzagged, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
