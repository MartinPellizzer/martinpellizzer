Zosterophyllanthos megalorhina

Zosterophyllanthos megalorhina, commonly known as the giant zosterophyllum, is a rare and ancient plant species belonging to the zosterophyllan family.

It is characterized by its unique morphology, featuring long, ribbon-like leaves that resemble those of early land plants. The plant exhibits a distinct vascular system, which is a key adaptation for terrestrial life. Its reproductive structures are simple, consisting of spore-producing organs rather than flowers.

This species provides valuable insights into the evolutionary transition from aquatic to terrestrial plant life.
