Zostera muelleri subsp. mucronata

Zostera muelleri subsp.

mucronata, commonly known as the broadleaf seagrass, is a marine flowering plant belonging to the family Zosteraceae. It is characterized by its long, ribbon-like leaves that can grow up to several meters in length.

This species typically thrives in shallow, coastal waters with sandy or muddy substrates. It plays a vital role in stabilizing sediments and providing habitat for various marine organisms.

Its flowering structures, known as spikelets, are usually submerged and contribute to its ecological importance in seagrass meadows.
