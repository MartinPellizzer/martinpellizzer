Zanthoxylum myriacanthum var. pubescens

Zanthoxylum myriacanthum var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy many-fruited prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are clustered in racemes, which are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. Its bark and leaves contain bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional medicine and horticulture.

The varietal form, pubescens, is distinguished by its hairy stems and leaves, contributing to its unique appearance and ecological role.
