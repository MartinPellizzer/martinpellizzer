Zinnia zinnioides

Zinnia zinnioides, commonly known as the Mexican zinnia, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and produces vibrant blooms in shades of red, orange, and yellow. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are sturdy and often branched.

Zinnia zinnioides is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its colorful display.
