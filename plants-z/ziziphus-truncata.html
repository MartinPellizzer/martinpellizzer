Ziziphus truncata

Ziziphus truncata, commonly known as the African date palm, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often arranged in a whorl at the ends of the branches. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by edible, fleshy fruits resembling dates. Its fruit is an important food source for various animals and birds.

Ziziphus truncata is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
