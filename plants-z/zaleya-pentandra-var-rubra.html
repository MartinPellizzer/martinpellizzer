Zaleya pentandra var. rubra

Zaleya pentandra var. rubra, commonly known as the redflower false aster, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features showy, red to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and often have a bluish-green hue.

Zaleya pentandra var. rubra is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
