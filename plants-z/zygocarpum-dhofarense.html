Zygocarpum dhofarense

Zygocarpum dhofarense, commonly known as the Dhofar date palm, is a unique species native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and broad, fan-shaped leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible dates, similar to other palm species. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting well to the harsh conditions of its native habitat.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical studies.
