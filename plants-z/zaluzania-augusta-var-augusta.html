Zaluzania augusta var. augusta

Zaluzania augusta var. augusta, commonly known as the tall ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and clusters of purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its root system consists of rhizomes and fibrous roots. It thrives in moist to wet soils in open woodlands and along stream banks.

Zaluzania augusta var. augusta is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
