Zornia diphylla subsp. latifolia

Zornia diphylla subsp.

latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf zornia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a herbaceous annual with simple, alternate leaves that are often broad and ovate.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, arranged in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a characteristic species of disturbed soils and open habitats.
