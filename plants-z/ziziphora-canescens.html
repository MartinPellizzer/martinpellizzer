Ziziphora canescens

Ziziphora canescens, commonly known as blue sage, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and tall spikes of blue-violet flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like mint and sage. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their antiseptic and calming properties.

Ziziphora canescens thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suited to xeriscaping.
