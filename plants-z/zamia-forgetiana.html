Zamia forgetiana

Zamia forgetiana, commonly known as the Mexican cycad, is a member of the Zamiaceae family and is native to Mexico.

It is a gymnosperm, characterized by its stout, unbranched stem and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces cones, with male and female cones typically occurring on separate plants. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Zamia forgetiana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and slow growth rate.
