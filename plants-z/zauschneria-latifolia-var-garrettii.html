Zauschneria latifolia var. garrettii

Zauschneria latifolia var. garrettii, commonly known as the Garrett's broadleaf bunchflower, is a striking member of the Onagraceae family native to the coastal regions of California.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear clusters of showy, salmon-pink to orange-red flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along the coast, thriving in full sun and coastal climates. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
