Zornia pulchella

Zornia pulchella, commonly known as the pretty zornia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
