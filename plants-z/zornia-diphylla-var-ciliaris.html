Zornia diphylla var. ciliaris

Zornia diphylla var. ciliaris, commonly known as the narrowleaf zornia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, alternate leaves with fine hairs along the edges. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and habitat restoration.
