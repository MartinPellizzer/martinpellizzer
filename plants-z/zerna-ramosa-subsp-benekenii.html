Zerna ramosa subsp. benekenii

Zerna ramosa subsp.

benekenii, commonly known as the Beneken's bamboo grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America. It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments and is often found in grasslands or along riverbanks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the Poaceae family.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscaping.
