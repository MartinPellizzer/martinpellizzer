Zanthoxylum suaveolens

Zanthoxylum suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, pinnately compound leaves and yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for some animals but generally not palatable to humans. Its bark and leaves contain compounds with potential medicinal properties, making it of interest in herbal medicine.

Zanthoxylum suaveolens thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.
