Ziziphus cumingiana

Ziziphus cumingiana, commonly known as the Cuming's ziziphus, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, particularly South Africa.

It belongs to the Rhamnaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems, simple leaves, and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, red or orange fruits that are edible and often used in traditional medicine.
