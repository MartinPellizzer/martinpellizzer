Zingiber sadakornii

Zingiber sadakornii, commonly known as the Sikkim ginger, is a perennial herb native to the eastern Himalayas.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other economically important plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant features a rhizomatous stem with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with a distinctive, fragrant aroma.

Zingiber sadakornii is valued for its ornamental qualities and has potential medicinal uses in traditional medicine.
