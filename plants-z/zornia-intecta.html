Zornia intecta

Zornia intecta, commonly known as the velvetleaf zornia, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and can remain viable in the soil for several years.

Zornia intecta is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, which contributes to soil fertility.
