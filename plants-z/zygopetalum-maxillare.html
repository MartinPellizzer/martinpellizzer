Zygopetalum maxillare

Zygopetalum maxillare, commonly known as the pink ghost orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its elongated, pinkish-white flowers with a distinctive, ghost-like appearance. The plant has a unique growth habit, with a slender, upright stem and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires specific conditions to bloom.

Due to its rarity and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern.
