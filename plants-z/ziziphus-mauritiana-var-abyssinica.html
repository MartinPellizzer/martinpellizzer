Ziziphus mauritiana var. abyssinica

Ziziphus mauritiana var. abyssinica, commonly known as the Abyssinian date palm, is a shrub or small tree native to Ethiopia and parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically alternate and have a distinctive, feathery appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by edible fruits that are similar in appearance to dates. The fruit is typically sweet and succulent, with a high sugar content, making it a valuable food source in arid regions.

It is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in traditional medicine.
