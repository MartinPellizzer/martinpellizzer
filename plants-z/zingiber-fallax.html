Zingiber fallax

Zingiber fallax, commonly known as the false ginger, is a rhizomatous herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers such as turmeric and cardamom. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in a spike. Zingiber fallax is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and unique floral structure.

It prefers warm, humid climates and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for tropical gardens.
