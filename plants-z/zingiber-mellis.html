Zingiber mellis

Zingiber mellis, commonly known as the sweet ginger.

This plant is a member of the Zingiberaceae family, characterized by its aromatic rhizomes and tropical appearance. It features broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters. The rhizome is often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications due to its pungent and sweet flavor profile.

Its botanical structure supports its role in both ornamental and functional horticulture.
