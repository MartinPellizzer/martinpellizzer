Ziziphus trichocarpa

Ziziphus trichocarpa, commonly known as the Desert Ziziphus, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically 5 to 9 pairs of oval, slightly serrated leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that give way to edible, red, fleshy berries in the fall. These berries are rich in nutrients and have been used by both wildlife and indigenous peoples for food and medicinal purposes.

Ziziphus trichocarpa is well adapted to harsh environments, with deep roots that allow it to survive in drought conditions.
