Zornia asperocarpa

Zornia asperocarpa, commonly known as rough-seeded zornia, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect or ascending growth habit. The plant features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its fruit is a dry, dehiscent pod that contains several small, angular seeds.

Zornia asperocarpa is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
