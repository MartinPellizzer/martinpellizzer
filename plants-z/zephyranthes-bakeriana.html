Zephyranthes bakeriana

Zephyranthes bakeriana, commonly known as the Baker's rain lily, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a bulbous perennial that produces delicate, star-shaped flowers in shades of white and pale yellow. The plant is characterized by its grass-like foliage and its ability to bloom after rainfall, often during the rainy season. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.

Due to its resilience and ornamental value, it is also cultivated as a cut flower in some regions.
