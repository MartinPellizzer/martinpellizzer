Zinnia multiflora var. flava

Zinnia multiflora var. flava, commonly known as the yellow zinnia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like blooms that typically measure 2 to 4 inches in diameter. The plant produces numerous flower heads along its branching stems, which are covered in fine, hairy hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Zinnia multiflora var. flava is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
