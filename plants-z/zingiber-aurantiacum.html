Zingiber aurantiacum

Zingiber aurantiacum, commonly known as the golden ginger or orange ginger, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers such as ginger and turmeric. The plant features elongated, fleshy rhizomes that are typically orange to reddish in color, giving it its common name. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a reddish or purple tinge along the veins.

The flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, usually with a yellow or orange hue, adding to its ornamental value.
