Zygophyllum gaetulum var. dolichocarpum

Zygophyllum gaetulum var. dolichocarpum, commonly known as the long-seeded sandmat, is a low-growing, succulent shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features fleshy, narrow leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access deep water sources.

This species is well adapted to sandy soils and is often found in desert scrub habitats.
