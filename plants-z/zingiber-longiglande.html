Zingiber longiglande

Zingiber longiglande, commonly known as the long-haired ginger, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in inflorescences, and it produces small, fleshy rhizomes.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
