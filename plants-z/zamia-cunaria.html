Zamia cunaria

Zamia cunaria, commonly known as the Mexican cycad, is a member of the Zamiaceae family and is native to Mexico.

It is a slow-growing, evergreen plant characterized by its stout, unbranched stem and a crown of feathery, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces large, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
