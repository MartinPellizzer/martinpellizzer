Zornia melanocarpa

Zornia melanocarpa, commonly known as blackroot, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers that are followed by legumes containing several seeds. It has a deep root system that helps it thrive in moist, sandy soils.

Zornia melanocarpa is often found in wetlands and along streambanks, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
