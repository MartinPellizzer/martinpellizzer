Zornia diphylla subsp. subperforata

Zornia diphylla subsp.

subperforata, commonly known as the slender zornia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States. It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and trifoliate leaves.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in spikes, which are typically borne in the axils of the leaves. Its leaves are often slightly hairy and have a distinctive leaflet arrangement with two pairs of leaflets and a terminal one.

The species is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
