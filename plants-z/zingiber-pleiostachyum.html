Zingiber pleiostachyum

Zingiber pleiostachyum, commonly known as the purple ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers and related plants. The plant is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, lance-shaped leaves that are often a deep green color. Its flowers are striking, with vibrant purple petals and a distinctive, fragrant aroma.

Zingiber pleiostachyum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
