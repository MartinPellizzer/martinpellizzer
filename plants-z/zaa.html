Zaa

Zaa, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its velvety, fuzzy leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as a compact shrub with a rosette of foliage that traps moisture, aiding in its survival in humid environments. Zaa flowers come in a variety of colors, including purple, blue, pink, and white, making it a popular ornamental plant.

It thrives in indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
