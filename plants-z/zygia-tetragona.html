Zygia tetragona

Zygia tetragona, commonly known as the four-cornered zygia, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink. The plant has four-angled stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Zygia tetragona is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
