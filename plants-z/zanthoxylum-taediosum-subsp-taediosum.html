Zanthoxylum taediosum subsp. taediosum

Zanthoxylum taediosum subsp.

taediosum, commonly known as the prickly ash, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern North America. It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters.

The plant produces red berries that are often eaten by birds, aiding in seed dispersal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks. Zanthoxylum taediosum subsp.

taediosum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
