Ziziphus scandens

Ziziphus scandens, commonly known as the climbing buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub or vine native to parts of Asia and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems that allow it to climb other plants or structures. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries in the fall. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

Ziziphus scandens is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
