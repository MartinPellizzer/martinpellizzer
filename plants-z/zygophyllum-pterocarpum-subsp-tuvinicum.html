Zygophyllum pterocarpum subsp. tuvinicum

Zygophyllum pterocarpum subsp.

tuvinicum, commonly known as the Tuvinian zygophyllum, is a perennial shrub native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its small, fleshy leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and poor soil conditions. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access deep water sources.

It plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
