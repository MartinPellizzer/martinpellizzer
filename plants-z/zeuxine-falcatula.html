Zeuxine falcatula

Zeuxine falcatula, commonly known as the narrowleaf zigzag gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving at high elevations. Its leaves are narrow and often have a zigzag or undulating appearance, which gives it its common name.

Zeuxine falcatula is a perennial herb that produces showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
