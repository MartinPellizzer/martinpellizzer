Zephyranthes picta

Zephyranthes picta, commonly known as the Rain Lily, is a species of flowering plant native to the southeastern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and red. The plant typically blooms after rainfall, hence its common name, and has strap-like, grassy foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Zephyranthes picta is also known for its ability to naturalize and spread readily in suitable conditions.
