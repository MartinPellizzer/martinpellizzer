Zingiber cochinchinense

Zingiber cochinchinense, commonly known as the Vietnamese ginger or wild ginger, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers such as turmeric and cardamom. The plant features a rhizomatous stem with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine, hairy texture. Its inflorescence consists of brightly colored, fragrant flowers that range from yellow to red, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
