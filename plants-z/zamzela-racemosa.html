Zamzela racemosa

Zamzela racemosa, commonly known as the pink-flowered bush, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom throughout the dry season. The plant produces small, oval-shaped berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Zamzela racemosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
