Zingiber densissimum

Zingiber densissimum, commonly known as the dense ginger or thick-stemmed ginger, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers and related plants. The plant is characterized by its tall, rhizomatous stem and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and covered in a fine, hairy texture.

Zingiber densissimum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a mild, spicy aroma similar to other gingers.
