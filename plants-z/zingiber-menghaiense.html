Zingiber menghaiense

Zingiber menghaiense, commonly known as Menghai ginger, is a rare and lesser-known member of the Zingiberaceae family.

It is native to the Yunnan province in China, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant features a rhizomatous stem and produces fragrant, showy flowers with a distinctive ginger scent. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a waxy texture.

Due to its limited distribution and unique characteristics, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
