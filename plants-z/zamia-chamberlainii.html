Zamia chamberlainii

Zamia chamberlainii, commonly known as the Chamberlain's cycad, is a member of the Zamiaceae family and is native to Mexico.

It is a slow-growing, evergreen plant with a stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of feathery, pinnate leaves. The plant produces cones, with male and female cones on separate plants, a characteristic feature of cycads. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid environment.

Due to its ornamental value and conservation status, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
