Zygopetalum crepeauxii

Zygopetalum crepeauxii, commonly known as the crepeaux's zygopetalum, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often brightly colored flowers that feature a unique lip structure, which is a defining trait of the Zygopetalum genus. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its foliage consists of long, narrow leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

The orchid blooms during the warmer months, producing fragrant flowers that attract various pollinators.
