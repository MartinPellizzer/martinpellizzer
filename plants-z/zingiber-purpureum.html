Zingiber purpureum

Zingiber purpureum, commonly known as the purple ginger, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers and turmeric. The plant is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, broad leaves that are often a striking purple or pinkish hue. Its flowers are typically clustered in spikes and can range in color from white to pink.

Zingiber purpureum is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its potential medicinal properties.
