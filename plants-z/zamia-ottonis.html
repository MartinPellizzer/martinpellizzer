Zamia ottonis

Zamia ottonis, commonly known as the Otto's cycad, is a species of cycad native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its stout, unbranched stem and a crown of large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces cones, with the male cones being elongated and the female cones being more compact. Zamia ottonis is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and requires well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in traditional medicine.
