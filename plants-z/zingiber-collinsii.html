Zingiber collinsii

Zingiber collinsii, commonly known as Collins' ginger, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers and turmeric. The plant features a rhizomatous root system and produces striking, sword-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with a distinctive, aromatic scent.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
