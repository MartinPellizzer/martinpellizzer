Ziziphus schneideri

Ziziphus schneideri, commonly known as the Schneider's date palm, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, the Middle East, and southern Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree that typically grows up to 5 meters in height, featuring compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into edible, date-like fruits with a sweet and nutty flavor.

Ziziphus schneideri is adapted to harsh environments, showing resistance to drought and poor soil conditions, making it a valuable species for ecological restoration and sustainable agriculture.
