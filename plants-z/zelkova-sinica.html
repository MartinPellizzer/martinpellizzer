Zelkova sinica

Zelkova sinica, commonly known as Chinese zelkova, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Ulmaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and ovate leaves with serrated edges. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often planted in urban landscapes for its shade and aesthetic appeal.
