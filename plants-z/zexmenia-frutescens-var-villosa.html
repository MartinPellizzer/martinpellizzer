Zexmenia frutescens var. villosa

Zexmenia frutescens var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy zexmenia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its woody stems and dense, hairy foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Zexmenia frutescens var. villosa is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
