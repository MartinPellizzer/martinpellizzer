Zauschneria californica var. microphylla

Zauschneria californica var. microphylla, commonly known as the Coast Yellow Coneflower, is a perennial herb native to coastal regions of California.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking yellow to orange conical flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it well-suited for coastal and sandy environments. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to local biodiversity.

The variety is distinguished by its smaller, more compact leaves compared to other varieties of Zauschneria californica.
