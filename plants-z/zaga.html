Zaga

Zaga, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10-20 centimeters and prefers bright, indirect light. Its leaves are velvety and dark green, while its flowers come in a variety of colors including pink, purple, and white.

Zaga is popular as a houseplant due to its ease of care and aesthetic appeal.
