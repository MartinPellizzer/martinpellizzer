Ziziphus spina-christi var. spina-christi

Ziziphus spina-christi var. spina-christi, commonly known as the Christ's thorn jujube, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Middle East and parts of Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five oval-shaped leaflets, and its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in panicles. The fruit is a small, yellow or red drupe with a sweet, tangy flavor, often used in traditional medicine and cuisine. It thrives in arid and semi-arid climates, showing resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in religious and cultural contexts.
