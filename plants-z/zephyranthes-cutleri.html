Zephyranthes cutleri

Zephyranthes cutleri, commonly known as the Cutler's rain lily, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is adapted to wetland environments and thrives in moist, well-drained soil. It is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Zephyranthes cutleri is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
