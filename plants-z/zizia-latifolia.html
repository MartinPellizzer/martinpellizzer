Zizia latifolia

Zizia latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf goldenroot, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in flat-topped inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along streambanks.

Zizia latifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
