Ziziphus acidojujuba f. infecunda

Ziziphus acidojujuba f. infecunda, commonly known as the Chinese date or jujube, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It produces small, sweet, orange-red fruits that are rich in nutrients and have been used in traditional medicine. The plant thrives in temperate climates and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. It is often cultivated for its edible fruits and has a long history of use in Chinese culture.

The fruit's high sugar content and nutritional value make it a valuable crop in both culinary and medicinal contexts.
