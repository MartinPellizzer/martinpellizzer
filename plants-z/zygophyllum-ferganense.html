Zygophyllum ferganense

Zygophyllum ferganense, commonly known as the Fergana prickly-pear, is a shrub native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Zygophyllaceae and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, followed by red, fleshy fruits. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.

Its botanical features make it an important plant for ecological studies in its native range.
