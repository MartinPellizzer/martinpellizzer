Zygopetalum brachypetalum

Zygopetalum brachypetalum, commonly known as the short-petaled zygopetalum, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring a short, broad lip that distinguishes it from other orchid genera. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in mountainous forests where it relies on specific mycorrhizal relationships for nutrient uptake. Its flowers are usually pale pink or white, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.
