Zehneria erythrobacca

Zehneria erythrobacca, commonly known as the red-barked zehneria, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-barked stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy green surface. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that are followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Zehneria erythrobacca is relatively low-maintenance, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
