Zephyranthes kurtzii

Zephyranthes kurtzii, commonly known as the rain lily, is a species of flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a bulbous perennial that produces striking white or pale pink flowers during the rainy season. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to thrive in well-drained, sandy soils. It is often found in open areas such as grasslands and forest edges.

Zephyranthes kurtzii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ephemeral blooms.
