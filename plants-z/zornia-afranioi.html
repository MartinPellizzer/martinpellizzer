Zornia afranioi

Zornia afranioi, commonly known as African zornia, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Zornia afranioi is often used in traditional medicine and for soil stabilization in its native habitats.
