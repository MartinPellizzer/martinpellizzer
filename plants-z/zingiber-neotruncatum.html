Zingiber neotruncatum

Zingiber neotruncatum, commonly known as the Neotropical ginger, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other economically important plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant features a rhizomatous root system and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, fragrant flowers, typically in shades of yellow and green.

Zingiber neotruncatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has potential applications in traditional medicine.
