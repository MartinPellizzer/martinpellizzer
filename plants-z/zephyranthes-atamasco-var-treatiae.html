Zephyranthes atamasco var. treatiae

Zephyranthes atamasco var. treatiae, commonly known as the Treatia's rain lily, is a striking bulbous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in response to rain, often appearing after thunderstorms. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves that are evergreen and can grow up to 12 inches in length. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to naturalize, creating clusters of flowers throughout the growing season.
