Zea saccharata

Zea saccharata, commonly known as sugar cane.

It is a tall, perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its elongated, fibrous stalks that contain a high concentration of sucrose. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a waxy surface that helps reduce water loss.

Zea saccharata is cultivated extensively for sugar production and has significant economic importance in many countries.
