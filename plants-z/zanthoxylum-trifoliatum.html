Zanthoxylum trifoliatum

Zanthoxylum trifoliatum, commonly known as the three-leaved prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, typically consisting of three to five ovate leaflets, and is often covered in small, stiff spines. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and stream banks.

Zanthoxylum trifoliatum is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
