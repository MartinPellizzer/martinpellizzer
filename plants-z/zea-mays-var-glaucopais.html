Zea mays var. glaucopais

Zea mays var. glaucopais, commonly known as the blue maize.

This variant of maize is characterized by its distinctive blue or bluish-green kernel color, which results from the presence of anthocyanin pigments. It is native to the Americas and is often cultivated for its nutritional value and traditional use in various cuisines.

The plant typically grows to a height of 1.5 to 2 meters, with tall, sturdy stalks and large, broad leaves. It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is closely related to other maize varieties such as Zea mays subsp.
