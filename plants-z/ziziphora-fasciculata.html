Ziziphora fasciculata

Ziziphora fasciculata, commonly known as narrowleaf ziziphora, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Central Asia.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. The plant features slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences and are usually pale blue or purple in color.

Ziziphora fasciculata is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
