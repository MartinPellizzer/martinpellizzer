Zostera angustifolia

Zostera angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf eelgrass, is a marine flowering plant belonging to the family Zosteraceae.

It is characterized by its long, ribbon-like leaves that are typically less than 1 centimeter wide. This species thrives in shallow, coastal waters and is often found in seagrass meadows. Zostera angustifolia plays a vital role in marine ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various marine organisms.

Its presence is an indicator of healthy, undisturbed coastal environments.
