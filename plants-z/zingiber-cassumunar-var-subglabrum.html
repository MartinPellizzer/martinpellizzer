Zingiber cassumunar var. subglabrum

Zingiber cassumunar var. subglabrum, commonly known as the white ginger or white wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It features a rhizomatous root system and produces tall, slender stems with lance-shaped, dark green leaves. The plant blooms with small, pale yellow to white flowers arranged in a spike, which are followed by cylindrical, pale yellow fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
