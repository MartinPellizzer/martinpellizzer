Zornia setosa subsp. obovata

Zornia setosa subsp.

obovata, commonly known as the narrowleaf zornia, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae. It is characterized by its small, obovate leaves that are typically hairy on the underside.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, inflated pods. It is native to North America and is often found in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky soils.

This species is known for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria, contributing to soil fertility.
