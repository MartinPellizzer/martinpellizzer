Zerna madritensis

Zerna madritensis, commonly known as the Spanish millet, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its small, spike-like inflorescence. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in open habitats such as fields and roadsides. Its seeds are used as a traditional food source in some regions of Spain.

Zerna madritensis is adapted to Mediterranean climates and is relatively drought-tolerant.
