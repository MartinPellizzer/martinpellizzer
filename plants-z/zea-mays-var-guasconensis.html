Zea mays var. guasconensis

Zea mays var. guasconensis, commonly known as the Guasconensis maize, is a variant of maize native to South America.

It is characterized by its unique morphological features, including compact inflorescences and distinct leaf structures. This variety is often studied for its genetic diversity and potential agricultural applications. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, adapting to a range of environmental conditions.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a genetic resource for modern maize breeding programs.
