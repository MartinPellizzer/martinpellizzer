Zizaniopsis villanensis

Zizaniopsis villanensis, commonly known as the Mexican flat sedge, is a perennial grass native to Central America and parts of the Caribbean.

It typically grows in wetland and riparian habitats, often forming dense mats that help stabilize soil and prevent erosion. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-based flowers that are wind-pollinated. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly, making it both a valuable wetland plant and a potential invasive species in non-native regions.

Zizaniopsis villanensis plays an important ecological role in maintaining wetland biodiversity and water quality.
