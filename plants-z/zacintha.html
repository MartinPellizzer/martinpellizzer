Zacintha

Zacintha, commonly known as the crown imperial, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Amaryllidaceae and is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear clusters of vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing blossoms in shades of red, orange, or yellow. Its leaves are broad, strap-like, and form a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Zacintha is often cultivated in gardens for its dramatic floral display and its ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
