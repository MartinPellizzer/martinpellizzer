Zulustylis variegata

Zulustylis variegata, commonly known as the variegated zulu grass, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that display a striking variegation of green and white or yellow patterns. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense spikes, typically blooming during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Due to its ornamental foliage, it is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in horticulture.
