Zanthoxylum martinicense subsp. guairaense

Zanthoxylum martinicense subsp.

guairaense, commonly known as the Guaira pricklyash, is a member of the Rutaceae family and is native to parts of Central and South America. It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its pinnately compound leaves and spiny branches. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds and small mammals.

Its bark and leaves contain various bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional medicine and phytochemical research.
