Ziziphora clinopodioides subsp. elbursensis

Ziziphora clinopodioides subsp.

elbursensis, commonly known as Elburs mountain savory, is a perennial herb native to the highlands of Iran. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh conditions. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their carminative and anti-inflammatory properties.
