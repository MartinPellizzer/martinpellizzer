Zygopetalum gautieri

Zygopetalum gautieri, commonly known as the Gautier's zygopetalum, is a species of orchid native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers with a unique lip structure. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are usually a mix of white and purple, with a strong, sweet fragrance that attracts pollinators.

Zygopetalum gautieri is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
