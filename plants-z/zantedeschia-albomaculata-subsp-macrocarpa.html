Zantedeschia albomaculata subsp. macrocarpa

Zantedeschia albomaculata subsp.

macrocarpa, commonly known as the giant white calla lily, is a striking herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its large, white, funnel-shaped spatula that is often marked with a distinctive yellow or green macula at the base.

The plant produces showy, arrow-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a silvery underside. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its elegant and dramatic floral display.
