Zingiber oligophyllum

Zingiber oligophyllum, commonly known as the lesser ginger, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers such as turmeric and cardamom. The plant features upright, narrow leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The botanical characteristics of Zingiber oligophyllum make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and pharmacological study.
