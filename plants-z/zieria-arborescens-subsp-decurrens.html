Zieria arborescens subsp. decurrens

Zieria arborescens subsp.

decurrens, commonly known as the slender zieria, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, ovate to elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
