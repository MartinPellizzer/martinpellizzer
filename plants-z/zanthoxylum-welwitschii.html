Zanthoxylum welwitschii

Zanthoxylum welwitschii, commonly known as the African prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and bright red berries that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in umbels, and its bark is rough and fissured. Zanthoxylum welwitschii is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.

Its botanical features make it an important plant in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
