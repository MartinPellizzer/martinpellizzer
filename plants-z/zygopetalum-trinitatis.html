Zygopetalum trinitatis

Zygopetalum trinitatis, commonly known as the Trinidad orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to the Caribbean island of Trinidad.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, tropical environments. Its flowers feature a unique zygomorphic structure, with a lip that is often brightly colored and highly specialized for pollination.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
