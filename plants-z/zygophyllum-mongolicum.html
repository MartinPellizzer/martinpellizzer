Zygophyllum mongolicum

Zygophyllum mongolicum, commonly known as Mongolian zygophyllum, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Zygophyllaceae and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and succulent, fleshy leaves adapted to water conservation. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is well-suited to harsh environmental conditions. Its structure includes a low-growing shrub form with multiple stems and a shallow root system.

Zygophyllum mongolicum plays a role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion in its native habitats.
