Ziziphus jujuba var. spontanea

Ziziphus jujuba var. spontanea, commonly known as the Chinese date or wild jujube, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It features compound leaves with three to five oval leaflets and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into edible, reddish-brown fruits. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid climates, showing remarkable drought tolerance. Its fruits are rich in sugars, vitamins, and antioxidants, making them valuable both nutritionally and commercially.

This wild variety is often used in traditional medicine and is also cultivated for its hardy nature and fruit yield.
