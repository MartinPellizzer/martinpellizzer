Zostera bullata

Zostera bullata, commonly known as the narrow leaf eelgrass, is a marine flowering plant found in coastal regions.

It thrives in shallow, sandy or muddy substrates in temperate and subtropical waters. The plant has long, ribbon-like leaves that are typically narrow and pointed. It plays a crucial role in stabilizing sediment and providing habitat for various marine organisms.

Zostera bullata is also important for carbon sequestration and supports biodiversity in coastal ecosystems.
