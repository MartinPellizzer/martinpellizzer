Zanthoxylum piperitum var. inerme

Zanthoxylum piperitum var. inerme, commonly known as Japanese prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces berries that change from green to red as they mature, attracting various wildlife. Its bark and leaves contain aromatic compounds, making it useful in traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.
