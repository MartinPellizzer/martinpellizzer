Zanthoxylum calcicola var. macrocarpum

Zanthoxylum calcicola var. macrocarpum, commonly known as the large-fruited prickly ash, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It thrives in rocky, arid environments and is adapted to limestone soils. The plant is characterized by its spiny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. Its fruits are woody, red or orange, and contain several seeds.

It is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
