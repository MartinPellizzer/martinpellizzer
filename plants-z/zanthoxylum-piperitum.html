Zanthoxylum piperitum

Zanthoxylum piperitum, commonly known as Japanese prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny branches, pinnately compound leaves, and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in the spring. The plant produces red berries that are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine. It thrives in temperate climates and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.

Zanthoxylum piperitum is also known for its aromatic leaves and fruits, which contain compounds with potential health benefits.
