Zostera trinervis

Zostera trinervis, commonly known as the narrow-veined eelgrass, is a seagrass species found in coastal marine environments.

It has long, ribbon-like leaves that are typically dark green and have a narrow, parallel-veined structure. This plant plays a crucial role in stabilizing sediments and providing habitat for various marine organisms. Zostera trinervis thrives in shallow, coastal waters with sandy or muddy substrates.

It is an important component of estuarine and coastal ecosystems, contributing to biodiversity and water quality.
