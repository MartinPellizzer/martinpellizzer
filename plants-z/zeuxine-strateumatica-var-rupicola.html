Zeuxine strateumatica var. rupicola

Zeuxine strateumatica var. rupicola, commonly known as the rock zeuxine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine environments. The plant features slender, upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikes, with a distinctive purple to blue coloration.

This variety is adapted to harsh, high-altitude conditions and is valued for its ornamental appeal in rock gardens.
