Ziziphora bracteolata

Ziziphora bracteolata, commonly known as the bracteolate savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its aromatic qualities.

Ziziphora bracteolata is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its purported digestive and anti-inflammatory benefits.
