Zenobia pulverulenta f. nitida

Zenobia pulverulenta f. nitida, commonly known as the silverleaf sand verbena, is a compact, low-growing shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are densely covered in fine, silvery-white hairs, giving the plant its characteristic appearance. The flowers are typically pale blue to violet, with five petals and a distinctive spurred shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

This variety is noted for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
