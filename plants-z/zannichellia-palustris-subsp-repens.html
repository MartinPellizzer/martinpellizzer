Zannichellia palustris subsp. repens

Zannichellia palustris subsp.

repens, commonly known as the creeping water starwort, is a aquatic plant native to temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its submerged, ribbon-like leaves that are typically dark green and have a smooth, waxy surface.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are borne on slender, branching stems, often floating at the water's surface. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds, ditches, and wet meadows. Zannichellia palustris subsp.
