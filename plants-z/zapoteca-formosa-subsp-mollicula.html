Zapoteca formosa subsp. mollicula

Zapoteca formosa subsp.

mollicula, commonly known as the Mexican velvet tree, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Mexico. It is characterized by its smooth, velvety bark and elliptical leaves with a soft, fuzzy texture.

The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, round fruits resembling small apples. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in humid lowland areas.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal uses, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
