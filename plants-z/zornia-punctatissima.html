Zornia punctatissima

Zornia punctatissima, commonly known as the dotless zornia, is a flowering plant native to the United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in early spring. The plant has simple, alternate leaves with a smooth texture and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils.

Zornia punctatissima produces small, oval pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind, contributing to its propagation in various regions.
