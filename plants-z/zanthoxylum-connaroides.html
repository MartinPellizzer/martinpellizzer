Zanthoxylum connaroides

Zanthoxylum connaroides, commonly known as the African prickly ash, is a small to medium-sized tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often pinnate and have serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences, followed by small, woody fruits containing several seeds. Its bark and leaves contain various bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional medicine and phytochemistry.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
