Zingiber capitatum

Zingiber capitatum, commonly known as the yellow ginger or golden ginger, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers such as turmeric and cardamom. The plant features a rhizomatous stem with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often variegated with yellow and green. Its most distinctive feature is the inflorescence, which consists of a cluster of fragrant, yellow flowers surrounded by specialized bracts.

Zingiber capitatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
