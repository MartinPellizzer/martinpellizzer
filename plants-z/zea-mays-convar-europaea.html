Zea mays convar. europaea

Zea mays convar. europaea, commonly known as European corn.

It is a variant of maize characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and shorter stature compared to other corn types. This subspecies is native to Europe and has been cultivated for its starchy kernels, which are used in various food products. Botanically, it exhibits a range of morphological features, including narrow leaves and a more fibrous cob structure.

Its genetic diversity contributes to its adaptability to different environmental conditions.
