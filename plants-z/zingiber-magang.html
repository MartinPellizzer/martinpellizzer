Zingiber magang

Zingiber magang, commonly known as the lesser ginger, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring lance-shaped leaves and fragrant, yellowish-orange flowers. Its rhizomes, which are the underground stems, are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

The botanical structure of Zingiber magang is characterized by its aromatic compounds and medicinal properties.
