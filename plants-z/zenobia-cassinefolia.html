Zenobia cassinefolia

Zenobia cassinefolia, commonly known as the goldenleaf zebra plant, is a striking member of the Rubiaceae family native to the humid forests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, waxy leaves that display a unique variegation of green and gold, often with a contrasting edge. Its foliage is evergreen, making it a popular choice for indoor and shaded garden settings.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying properties.
