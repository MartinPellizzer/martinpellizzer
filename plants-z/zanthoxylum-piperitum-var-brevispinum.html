Zanthoxylum piperitum var. brevispinum

Zanthoxylum piperitum var. brevispinum, commonly known as the Chinese prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red berries, which are edible but have a sharp, citrus-like flavor. Its aromatic leaves and fruits are used in traditional Chinese medicine and culinary applications.

The variety brevispinum is distinguished by its shorter spines compared to other varieties of Zanthoxylum piperitum.
