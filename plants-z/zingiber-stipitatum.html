Zingiber stipitatum

Zingiber stipitatum, commonly known as the slender ginger.

It is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its upright, narrow rhizomes and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in a spike, which are typically borne on short pedicels. Its botanical features include fibrous roots, a single stem, and a distinct aromatic scent.

Zingiber stipitatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties.
