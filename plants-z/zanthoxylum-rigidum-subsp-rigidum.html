Zanthoxylum rigidum subsp. rigidum

Zanthoxylum rigidum subsp.

rigidum, commonly known as the rigid prickly ash, is a shrub native to eastern North America. It is characterized by its spiny stems and pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that give way to red, fleshy berries in the fall. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
