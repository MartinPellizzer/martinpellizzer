Zoysia matrella subsp. japonica

Zoysia matrella subsp.

japonica, commonly known as Japanese zoysia, is a perennial grass species native to East Asia. It is characterized by its dense, fine-textured foliage and its ability to form a thick, mat-like turf.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue and produces small, inconspicuous flowers. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established. Zoysia matrella subsp.

japonica is widely used in landscaping for its durability and aesthetic appeal.
