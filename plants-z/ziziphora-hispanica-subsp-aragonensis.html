Ziziphora hispanica subsp. aragonensis

Ziziphora hispanica subsp.

aragonensis, commonly known as Spanish thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic, often fragrant leaves and flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, preferring sunny, well-drained environments. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink to purple, and it produces small, oval-shaped fruits.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
