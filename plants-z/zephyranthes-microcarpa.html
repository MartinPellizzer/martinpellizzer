Zephyranthes microcarpa

Zephyranthes microcarpa, commonly known as the dwarf rain lily, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a bulbous perennial that produces delicate, fragrant white flowers in the spring following rainfall. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It is drought-tolerant and often grows in open areas such as prairies and woodlands.

Its blooming period is typically brief, making it a sought-after plant for gardens with seasonal interest.
