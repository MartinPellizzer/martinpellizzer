Zephyranthes insularum

Zephyranthes insularum, commonly known as the Island rain lily, is a species of flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a bulbous plant that typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy or well-drained soils. The plant produces showy, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in the evening, often during the rainy season. Its flowers have a subtle fragrance and are pollinated primarily by nocturnal insects such as moths.

Zephyranthes insularum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
