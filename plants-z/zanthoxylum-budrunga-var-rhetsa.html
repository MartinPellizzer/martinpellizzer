Zanthoxylum budrunga var. rhetsa

Zanthoxylum budrunga var. rhetsa, commonly known as the red sandalwood tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnate and often have a distinctive reddish or orange hue. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Its bark and wood are valued for their aromatic properties and have been used in traditional medicine and as a source of dye.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.
