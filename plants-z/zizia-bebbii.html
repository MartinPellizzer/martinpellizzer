Zizia bebbii

Zizia bebbii, commonly known as Bebb's goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Zizia bebbii plays an important role in supporting pollinators and maintaining ecological balance in its native environment.
