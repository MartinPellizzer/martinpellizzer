Zanthoxylum jaimei

Zanthoxylum jaimei, commonly known as the jaimei prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. Its bark and leaves contain bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional medicine.

Zanthoxylum jaimei thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.
