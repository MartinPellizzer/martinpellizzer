Zanonia sarcophylla

Zanonia sarcophylla, commonly known as the stonecress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with small, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically arranged in racemes. It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

Zanonia sarcophylla is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, showcasing features such as succulent leaves and deep root systems.
