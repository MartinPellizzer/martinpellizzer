Zanthoxylum dissitum var. lanciforme

Zanthoxylum dissitum var. lanciforme, commonly known as the lanciforme prickly ash, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruits are red, fleshy berries that contain several seeds.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
