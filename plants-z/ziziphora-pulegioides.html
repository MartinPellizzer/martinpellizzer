Ziziphora pulegioides

Ziziphora pulegioides, commonly known as creeping ziziphora, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region and parts of southern Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant features slender, creeping stems that form dense mats, with small, lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic. Its flowers are borne in clusters and are usually pink or purple, blooming in late spring to early summer.

Ziziphora pulegioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
