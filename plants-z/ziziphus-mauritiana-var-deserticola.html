Ziziphus mauritiana var. deserticola

Ziziphus mauritiana var. deserticola, commonly known as the desert date, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of India and Pakistan.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish fruits that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. The plant has small, elliptical leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It is well adapted to dry climates, with deep roots that allow it to survive in poor soil conditions.

This species is also valued for its ability to prevent soil erosion in degraded landscapes.
