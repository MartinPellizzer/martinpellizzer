Zanthoxylum chocoense

Zanthoxylum chocoense, commonly known as the Chocoan prickly ash, is a shrub or small tree native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnate and have a serrated margin. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red or orange berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Its bark and leaves contain various bioactive compounds, making it of interest for pharmacological research.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.
