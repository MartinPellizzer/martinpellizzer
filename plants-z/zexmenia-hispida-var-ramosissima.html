Zexmenia hispida var. ramosissima

Zexmenia hispida var. ramosissima, commonly known as the hairy zexmenia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and branched stems, which give it its varietal name "ramosissima," meaning "very branched.". The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in composite heads, typical of the Asteraceae family. It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa, where it thrives in well-drained soils.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists studying drought-resistant flora.
