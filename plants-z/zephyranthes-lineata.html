Zephyranthes lineata

Zephyranthes lineata, commonly known as the rain lily, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and Central America.

It is a perennial herb with strap-like, narrow leaves that are typically grass-like in appearance. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, yellow, or orange, often blooming after rainfall. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Zephyranthes lineata is also known for its ability to bloom throughout the year under suitable conditions.
