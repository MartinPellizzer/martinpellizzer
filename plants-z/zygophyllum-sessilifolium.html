Zygophyllum sessilifolium

Zygophyllum sessilifolium, commonly known as the sessile-leaf chenopod, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia and the Middle East.

It is a low-growing shrub with thick, succulent stems that help it retain moisture in harsh environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. Its leaves are small, scale-like, and often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

Zygophyllum sessilifolium is adapted to thrive in saline and alkaline soils, making it an important component of desert ecosystems.
