Zingiber newmanii

Zingiber newmanii, commonly known as the Newman's ginger, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other well-known gingers such as turmeric and cardamom. The plant is characterized by its tall, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of colorful, fragrant flowers that range from yellow to red.

Zingiber newmanii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
