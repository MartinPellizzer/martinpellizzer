Zalacca

Zalacca, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are woody and branched.

Zalacca is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
