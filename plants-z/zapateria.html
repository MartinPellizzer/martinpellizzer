Zapateria

Zapateria, commonly known as the "Mexican bellflower," is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are broad and ovate, often with a slightly hairy texture.
