Zostera marina subsp. hornemanniana

Zostera marina subsp.

hornemanniana, commonly known as the Hornemann's eelgrass, is a marine flowering plant belonging to the family Zosteraceae. It is characterized by its ribbon-like, strap-shaped leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color.

This species thrives in shallow coastal waters, often forming dense underwater meadows that provide habitat for various marine organisms. It plays a crucial role in stabilizing sediments and improving water quality through its extensive root system. Zostera marina subsp.

hornemanniana is also important for carbon sequestration and supports biodiversity in coastal ecosystems.
