Ziziphus cotinifolia

Ziziphus cotinifolia, commonly known as the smoke bush, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves that are often grayish-green and turn striking shades of red or purple in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that give way to red or orange berries in the autumn. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Ziziphus cotinifolia is also known for its adaptability to urban environments and its role in attracting wildlife.
