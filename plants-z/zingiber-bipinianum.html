Zingiber bipinianum

Zingiber bipinianum, commonly known as the Khasi ginger, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern regions of India.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other well-known gingers such as turmeric and cardamom. The plant features rhizomatous stems and produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-white flowers. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and have a glossy surface.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine and cuisine.
