Zanthoxylum paracanthum

Zanthoxylum paracanthum, commonly known as the prickly ash or yellow wood.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States. The plant is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and compound leaves with serrated edges. Its small, yellowish-green flowers appear in clusters and are followed by bright red berries.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
