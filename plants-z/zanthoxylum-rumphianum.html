Zanthoxylum rumphianum

Zanthoxylum rumphianum, commonly known as the red sandalwood or rumphian prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces bright red berries that are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its bark and roots contain alkaloids and are valued for their medicinal properties.

Zanthoxylum rumphianum thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in forested areas and along riverbanks.
