Zauschneria latifolia var. johnstonii

Zauschneria latifolia var. johnstonii, commonly known as the Johnston's broadleaf larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are broad at the base and taper toward the tip. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry slopes and rocky areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
