Zephyranthes erubescens

Zephyranthes erubescens, commonly known as the pink rain lily, is a bulbous plant native to Central and South America.

It produces striking pink to red flowers that bloom in response to rainfall, often after thunderstorms. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to thrive in well-drained, sandy soils. It is typically found in open areas such as grasslands and forest edges.

Zephyranthes erubescens is a short-lived perennial that goes through a dormant phase during dry seasons.
