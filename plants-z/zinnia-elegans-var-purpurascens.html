Zinnia elegans var. purpurascens

Zinnia elegans var. purpurascens, commonly known as the Purple Coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple to red petals and a prominent, cone-shaped center that resembles a daisy. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 3 feet and thrives in full sun with well-drained soil. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

This variety is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in garden settings.
