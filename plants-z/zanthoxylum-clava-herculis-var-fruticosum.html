Zanthoxylum clava-herculis var. fruticosum

Zanthoxylum clava-herculis var. fruticosum, commonly known as the prickly ash or American prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are a food source for various birds and mammals. Its leaves are alternate, compound, and have a distinctive, aromatic scent.

Zanthoxylum clava-herculis var. fruticosum is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
