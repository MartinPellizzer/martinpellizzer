Zanthoxylum acanthopodium var. deminutum

Zanthoxylum acanthopodium var. deminutum, commonly known as the narrowleaf prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated margins and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy fruits that are often consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

The plant's aromatic, prickly branches and medicinal properties have made it of interest in traditional herbal medicine.
