Zephyranthes purpurella

Zephyranthes purpurella, commonly known as the pink rain lily, is a species of flowering plant native to the southeastern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a bulbous plant that produces showy, pinkish-purple flowers during the spring or early summer. The plant's leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically growing from the base of the bulb. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Zephyranthes purpurella is known for its ability to bloom after rainfall, earning it the nickname "rain lily.".
