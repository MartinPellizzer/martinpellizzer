Zanthoxylum dimorphophyllum var. multifoliolatum

Zanthoxylum dimorphophyllum var. multifoliolatum, commonly known as the multi-leaf prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with multiple leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. Its bark and leaves contain compounds such as lignans and alkaloids, which have been studied for potential medicinal properties.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.
