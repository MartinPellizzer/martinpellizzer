Ziziphora abd-el-asisii

Ziziphora abd-el-asisii, commonly known as the Egyptian privet, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North Africa and the Middle East.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically dispersed by animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Due to its resilience, it is often used in landscaping and for erosion control in dry environments.
