Zingiber

Zingiber, commonly known as ginger, is a rhizomatous herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which also includes turmeric and cardamom. The plant's underground stem, or rhizome, is the primary part used for culinary and medicinal purposes. Zingiber has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, or red.

Its essential oil contains compounds like gingerol, which contribute to its distinctive spicy flavor and therapeutic properties.
