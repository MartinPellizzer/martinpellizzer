Zieria granulata

Zieria granulata, commonly known as the rough zieria, is a small evergreen shrub native to the south-western region of Western Australia.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils within heathlands and open forests. The plant features small, elliptical leaves that are covered in fine, granular bumps, giving it a distinctive texture. Its white or pale pink flowers appear in clusters during the spring and summer months.

Zieria granulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and delicate blooms.
