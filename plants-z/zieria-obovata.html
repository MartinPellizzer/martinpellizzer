Zieria obovata

Zieria obovata, commonly known as the broad-leaf zieria, is a small evergreen shrub native to the south-western region of Western Australia.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils within heathlands and open woodlands. The plant features narrow, obovate leaves that are usually covered in fine, white hairs. Its small, white or pale yellow flowers appear in clusters during the winter months.

Zieria obovata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its compact form and seasonal blooms.
