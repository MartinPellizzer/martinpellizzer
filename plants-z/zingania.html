Zingania

Zingania, commonly known as the Brazilian sunflower, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with hairy, alternate leaves. Its flowering period occurs during the summer months, and it thrives in full sun with well-drained soil.

Zingania is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its bright, sun-like blooms.
