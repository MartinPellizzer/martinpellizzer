Zamia loddigesii var. obtusifolia

Zamia loddigesii var. obtusifolia, commonly known as the blunt-leaf cycad, is a species of cycad native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of large, leathery, fan-shaped leaves. The plant produces cone-like structures, with male and female cones typically occurring on separate plants. Its leaves are typically broad and have a distinctive, rounded shape.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
