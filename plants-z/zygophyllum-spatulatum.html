Zygophyllum spatulatum

Zygophyllum spatulatum, commonly known as the spoonleaf false flax, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, the Middle East, and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Zygophyllaceae and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water in dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is often found in rocky soils and can tolerate poor nutrient conditions, making it a hardy species in harsh climates.

Zygophyllum spatulatum is also used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.
