Zingiber chryseum

Zingiber chryseum, commonly known as the golden ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers and related plants. The plant features a rhizomatous underground stem, from which tall, narrow leaves emerge. Its flowers are typically yellow or golden in color, with a distinctive, aromatic scent.

Zingiber chryseum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
