Zannichellia palustris subsp. palustris

Zannichellia palustris subsp.

palustris, commonly known as the marsh pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It typically grows in shallow, freshwater habitats such as lakes, ponds, and wetlands.

The plant has long, slender, branching stems that are often covered with small, scale-like leaves. It produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in spikes, which emerge from the water surface.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
