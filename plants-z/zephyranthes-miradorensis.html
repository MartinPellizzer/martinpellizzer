Zephyranthes miradorensis

Zephyranthes miradorensis, commonly known as the Mirador orchid or the pink rain lily, is a species of flowering plant native to the subtropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a bulbous perennial that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its blooming period typically occurs during the rainy season, and it is known for its ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.

Zephyranthes miradorensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
