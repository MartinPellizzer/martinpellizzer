Zingiber kangleipakense

Zingiber kangleipakense, commonly known as the Kangleipak ginger, is a rare and endemic species native to the northeastern region of India.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other economically important plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant is characterized by its narrow, sword-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a spike. Its rhizomes are used in local traditional medicine and are valued for their aromatic and medicinal properties.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern.
