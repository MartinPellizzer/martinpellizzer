Zinnia citrea

Zinnia citrea, commonly known as the Mexican zinnia, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its vibrant, showy blooms. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and produces daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Zinnia citrea is known for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners in arid climates.
