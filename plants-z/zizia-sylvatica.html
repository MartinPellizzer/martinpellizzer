Zizia sylvatica

Zizia sylvatica, commonly known as the woodland goldenrod, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and along stream banks. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and hairy, with showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its flowering stems are upright and bear clusters of small, bright yellow flowers.

Zizia sylvatica plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is valued for its ornamental appeal in native plant gardens.
