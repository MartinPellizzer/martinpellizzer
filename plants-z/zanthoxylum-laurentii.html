Zanthoxylum laurentii

Zanthoxylum laurentii, commonly known as the Laurentian prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species.

Its bark and leaves contain compounds that have been historically used in traditional medicine for their purported analgesic and anti-inflammatory properties.
