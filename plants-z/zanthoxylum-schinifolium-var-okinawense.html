Zanthoxylum schinifolium var. okinawense

Zanthoxylum schinifolium var. okinawense, commonly known as Okinawa prickly ash, is a variant of the prickly ash plant native to the Ryukyu Islands of Japan.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its pinnately compound leaves and spiny branches. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. Its bark and leaves contain compounds with potential medicinal properties.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.
