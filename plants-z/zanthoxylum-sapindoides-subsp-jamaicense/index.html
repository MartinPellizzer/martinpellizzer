Zanthoxylum Sapindoides Subsp. Jamaicense

Zanthoxylum sapindoides subsp. jamaicense is a lesser-known member of the Rutaceae family native to the Caribbean. This shrub or small tree is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, spiny fruit clusters. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal and limestone areas. The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional uses in local medicine. Despite its beauty and utility, it remains relatively obscure in global botanical discussions.
