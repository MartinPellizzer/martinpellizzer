Zeuxine cordata

Zeuxine cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf zeuxine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, heart-shaped leaves with a smooth, dark green surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in terminal inflorescences. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it thrives in well-drained, moist soils.

Zeuxine cordata is often found in shaded or semi-shaded woodland environments.
