Zingiber acuminatum var. acuminatum

Zingiber acuminatum var. acuminatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ginger, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright rhizomes and elongated, narrow leaves that are often a vibrant green. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers with three prominent petals and a distinctive, narrow shape. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in gardens and traditional medicine practices.
