Ziziphus incurva

Ziziphus incurva, commonly known as the twisted buckthorn, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, yellowish-green leaves that are often clustered at the base of the plant. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various bird species. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to harsh climatic conditions.
