Zexmenia frutescens var. genuina

Zexmenia frutescens var. genuina, commonly known as the golden sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial with pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has small, fleshy leaves adapted to conserve water, typical of xerophytic species. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in rocky habitats.

Its unique appearance and ecological adaptations make it a notable species in its native environment.
