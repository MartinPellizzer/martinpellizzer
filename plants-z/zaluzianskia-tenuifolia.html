Zaluzianskia tenuifolia

Zaluzianskia tenuifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf ironweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to the central and eastern United States.

It features upright, slender stems that bear clusters of small, pink to purple flowers in the late summer and early fall. The plant has finely divided, narrow leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in prairies, meadows, and open woodlands.

Zaluzianskia tenuifolia is valued for its ornamental value and its role in supporting local pollinators.
