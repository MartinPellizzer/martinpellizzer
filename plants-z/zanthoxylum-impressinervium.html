Zanthoxylum impressinervium

Zanthoxylum impressinervium, commonly known as the impressed-veined prickly ash, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with prominent, impressed veins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. Its bark and leaves contain compounds that have been used in traditional medicine.

Zanthoxylum impressinervium is adapted to grow in moist, shaded environments within tropical and subtropical forests.
