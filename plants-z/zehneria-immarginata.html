Zehneria immarginata

Zehneria immarginata, commonly known as the silver leaf plant, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Zehneria immarginata is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
