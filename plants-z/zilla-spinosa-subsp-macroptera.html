Zilla spinosa subsp. macroptera

Zilla spinosa subsp.

macroptera, commonly known as the large-leaved zilla, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the spring and early summer. Its woody stems are often branched and can grow up to several meters in height.

This species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.
