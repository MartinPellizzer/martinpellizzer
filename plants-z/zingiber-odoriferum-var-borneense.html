Zingiber odoriferum var. borneense

Zingiber odoriferum var. borneense, commonly known as Bornean ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia, particularly Borneo.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other ginger species. The plant features erect, sword-shaped leaves and produces fragrant, yellowish-green flowers arranged in spikes. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine and as a culinary spice.

The botanical characteristics of this variety make it distinct from other ginger species due to its unique aroma and growth habits.
