Zannichellia gibberosa

Zannichellia gibberosa, commonly known as the floating bladderwort, is a small aquatic plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tiny, bladder-like structures that trap small aquatic organisms, aiding in its nutrient acquisition. The plant typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and ditches. Its leaves are usually thin and ribbon-like, floating on the water's surface.

Zannichellia gibberosa is often found in nutrient-rich waters and can form dense mats that affect water quality and aquatic ecosystems.
