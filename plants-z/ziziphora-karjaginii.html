Ziziphora karjaginii

Ziziphora karjaginii, commonly known as Karjagin's savory, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, opposite leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are typically pale blue to violet, arranged in whorls along the stem.

Ziziphora karjaginii is often used in traditional medicine for its purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.
