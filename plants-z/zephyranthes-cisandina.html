Zephyranthes cisandina

Zephyranthes cisandina, commonly known as the pink rain lily, is a bulbous herbaceous plant native to the Caribbean and northern South America.

It produces striking pink or white flowers that bloom in response to rainfall, often appearing after thunderstorms. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and a compact growth habit, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring minimal water once established.

Zephyranthes cisandina is valued for its ornamental beauty and its ability to bloom in seasonal rainfall patterns.
