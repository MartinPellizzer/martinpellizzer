Zingiber purpureoalbum

Zingiber purpureoalbum, commonly known as the purple-flowered ginger, is a striking ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers and turmeric. The plant features tall, sword-shaped leaves and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of purple and white. Its rhizomes, while not as commonly used for culinary purposes as other gingers, are valued in traditional medicine.

Zingiber purpureoalbum thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental beauty.
