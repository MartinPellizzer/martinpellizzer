Zaluzania augusta var. rzedowskii

Zaluzania augusta var. rzedowskii, commonly known as the Mexican thimbleweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flower heads that are typically clustered in loose inflorescences. It thrives in rocky or dry habitats and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local flora in its native range.
