Zanthoxylum nelsonii

Zanthoxylum nelsonii, commonly known as the Nelson's prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often pinnately arranged with serrated margins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that give way to red, berry-like fruits in the fall. Its bark and leaves contain bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional and modern pharmacology.

Zanthoxylum nelsonii thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland habitats.
