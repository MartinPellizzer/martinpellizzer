Zeltnera pusilla

Zeltnera pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf zeltnera, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing a compact growth habit. The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its flowers are small and usually pale green or yellow, appearing in clusters during the spring.

Zeltnera pusilla is adapted to withstand drought and extreme temperatures, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
