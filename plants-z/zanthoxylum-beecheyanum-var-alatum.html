Zanthoxylum beecheyanum var. alatum

Zanthoxylum beecheyanum var. alatum, commonly known as the broad-winged prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and have a distinctive, broad wing-like appearance on the leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries containing several seeds. Its bark is thick and fissured, often with a reddish or brownish hue, and it contains various bioactive compounds.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
