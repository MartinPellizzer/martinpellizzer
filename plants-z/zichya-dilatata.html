Zichya dilatata

Zichya dilatata, commonly known as the broadleaf ziziphus, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, fleshy fruits. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access groundwater in nutrient-poor soils.

Zichya dilatata is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of food in regions where it is naturally found.
