Zea mays var. quinquantina

Zea mays var. quinquantina, commonly known as the Mexican popcorn corn, is a variety of maize characterized by its unique ear structure.

It produces small, hard kernels that are typically harvested when they are dry and fully mature. The plant is native to Mexico and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as for its edible seeds. It has a compact growth habit and is resistant to certain diseases that affect other maize varieties.

This variety is also valued for its historical and cultural significance in traditional farming practices.
