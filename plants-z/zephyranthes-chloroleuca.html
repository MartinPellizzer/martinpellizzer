Zephyranthes chloroleuca

Zephyranthes chloroleuca, commonly known as the rain lily, is a species of flowering plant native to the southeastern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a bulbous perennial that produces showy white flowers with a yellow center, typically blooming after rainfall. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves that are narrow and evergreen, providing a lush base for the flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Zephyranthes chloroleuca is also valued for its ability to naturalize and bloom reliably in suitable climates.
