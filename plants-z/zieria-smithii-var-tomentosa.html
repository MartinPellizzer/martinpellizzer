Zieria smithii var. tomentosa

Zieria smithii var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly zieria, is a low-growing shrub native to the eastern coast of Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage that gives it a soft, woolly appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky areas.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and ecologically significant plant in its native habitat.
