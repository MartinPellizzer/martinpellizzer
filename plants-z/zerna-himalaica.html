Zerna himalaica

Zerna himalaica, commonly known as Himalayan zerna, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in cold, arid environments and is adapted to survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Due to its ecological importance and unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
