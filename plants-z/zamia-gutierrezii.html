Zamia gutierrezii

Zamia gutierrezii, commonly known as the Mexican cycad, is a perennial plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the family Zamiaceae and is characterized by its stout, unbranched stem and compound leaves. The plant produces cones rather than flowers, with male and female cones on separate plants. Its leaves are typically pinnately compound, with a deep green color and a leathery texture.

Zamia gutierrezii is adapted to grow in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
