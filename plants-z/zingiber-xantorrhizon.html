Zingiber xantorrhizon

Zingiber xantorrhizon, commonly known as the yellow ginger, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Zingiberaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers and aromatic rhizomes, which are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in moist, well-drained soil. Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy.

Zingiber xantorrhizon is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties, making it a popular choice in horticulture.
