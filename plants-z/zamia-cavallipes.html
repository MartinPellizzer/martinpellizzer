Zamia cavallipes

Zamia cavallipes, commonly known as the Mexican cycad, is a member of the Zamiaceae family and is native to southern Mexico.

It is a slow-growing plant with a stout, woody trunk and a crown of large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces cones rather than flowers, with male and female cones typically occurring on separate plants. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
