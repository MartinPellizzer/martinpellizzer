Zanthoxylum sapindoides subsp. hartii

Zanthoxylum sapindoides subsp.

hartii, commonly known as the Chinese prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia. It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnately arranged with serrated margins.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible for birds and mammals. Its bark and leaves contain various bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional medicine.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits in native habitats.
