Ziziphora verticillata

Ziziphora verticillata, commonly known as mountain savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, tubular flowers that bloom in dense clusters. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowering period occurs in the summer months, and it is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Ziziphora verticillata is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
