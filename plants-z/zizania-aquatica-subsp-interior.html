Zizania aquatica subsp. interior

Zizania aquatica subsp.

interior, commonly known as the interior marsh grass, is a perennial aquatic plant native to the eastern United States. It typically grows in shallow freshwater wetlands, ponds, and slow-moving streams.

The plant has long, narrow leaves that float on the water surface, with roots submerged in the mud. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow spikelets that develop in summer, contributing to its role in wetland ecosystems.

This subspecies is important for waterfowl habitat and helps in stabilizing aquatic soils.
