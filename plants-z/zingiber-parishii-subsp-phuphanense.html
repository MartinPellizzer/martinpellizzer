Zingiber parishii subsp. phuphanense

Zingiber parishii subsp.

phuphanense, commonly known as the Phuphanen ginger, is a rhizomatous herb native to Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other economically important plants like ginger and turmeric.

The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in inflorescences. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine and as a culinary spice.

This subspecies is characterized by its unique floral structure and habitat preferences in tropical forests.
