Zinnia australis

Zinnia australis, commonly known as the Australian zinnia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, daisy-like flowers that come in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in full sun with well-drained soil.

Zinnia australis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its showy blooms.
