Zanthoxylum nebuletorum

Zanthoxylum nebuletorum, commonly known as the cloud-veined prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and yellowish-green veins. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. Its bark and leaves contain bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional medicine.

Zanthoxylum nebuletorum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.
