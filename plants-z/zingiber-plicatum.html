Zingiber plicatum

Zingiber plicatum, commonly known as the pleated ginger, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other economically important plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, pleated leaves that give it its common name. It produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, often arranged in a spiral pattern.

Zingiber plicatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
