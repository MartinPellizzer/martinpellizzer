Zoysia sinica subsp. nipponica

Zoysia sinica subsp.

nipponica, commonly known as Japanese zoysia, is a perennial grass native to East Asia. It is characterized by its fine, dense foliage and a creeping growth habit, forming a dense mat.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color and produces small, inconspicuous flowers. It is well adapted to a variety of soil types and is often used in ornamental landscaping.

Its rhizomatous and stoloniferous growth allows it to spread effectively and establish quickly in suitable conditions.
