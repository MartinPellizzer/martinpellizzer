Zea mays subsp. aorista

Zea mays subsp.

aorista, commonly known as the Mexican teosinte, is a wild relative of maize native to southern Mexico. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, clustered inflorescences.

The plant typically has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces both male and female flowers on separate plants. Its genetic diversity plays a crucial role in the domestication history of maize.

Botanically, it is considered a key species in understanding the evolution of cereal crops.
