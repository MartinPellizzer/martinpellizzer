Zingiber matutumense

Zingiber matutumense, commonly known as the morning ginger, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant features narrow, sword-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in a spiral arrangement. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The botanical characteristics of Zingiber matutumense make it a valuable species for both horticultural and medicinal purposes.
