Zauschneria pulchella var. adpressa

Zauschneria pulchella var. adpressa, commonly known as the low zauschneria, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and produces showy, salmon-pink to orange-red flowers in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in open woodlands or along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the flowers are borne in terminal clusters.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
