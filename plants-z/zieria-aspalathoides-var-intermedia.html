Zieria aspalathoides var. intermedia

Zieria aspalathoides var. intermedia, commonly known as the coastal zieria, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It features small, glossy, ovate leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stems. The plant produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to coastal conditions with high salt exposure.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
