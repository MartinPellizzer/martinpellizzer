Zanthoxylum dissitum var. acutiserratum

Zanthoxylum dissitum var. acutiserratum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Its prickly branches and aromatic foliage make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
