Zornia diphylla var. vulgaris

Zornia diphylla var. vulgaris, commonly known as the two-leaved zornia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing annual herb that typically reaches heights of up to 30 centimeters. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces small, clustered pink to reddish flowers. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in woodland edges and along stream banks.

The species is noted for its rapid growth and ability to colonize open areas, making it a common sight in its native habitat.
