Zieria pilosa var. parviflora

Zieria pilosa var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered zieria, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are typically grey-green in color, with a dense, mat-forming habit. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the autumn season. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathland and woodland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
