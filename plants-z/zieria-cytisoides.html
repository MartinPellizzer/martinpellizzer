Zieria cytisoides

Zieria cytisoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved zieria, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in heathlands and rocky soils, preferring well-drained conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the spring and summer seasons. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Zieria cytisoides is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
