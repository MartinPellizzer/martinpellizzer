Zanthoxylum longifolium

Zanthoxylum longifolium, commonly known as the prickly ash or white prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are usually 4 to 8 inches long and have serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. Its bark and leaves contain compounds that have been used in traditional medicine for their stimulant and analgesic properties.

Zanthoxylum longifolium thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.
