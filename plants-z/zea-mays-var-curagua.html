Zea mays var. curagua

Zea mays var. curagua, commonly known as Curagua corn, is a variant of maize native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall stature and large, narrow leaves. The plant produces tassels at the top of the stalk, which are used for pollination. Its ears of corn are typically smaller compared to other maize varieties.

This subspecies is valued for its historical and agricultural significance in indigenous cultures.
