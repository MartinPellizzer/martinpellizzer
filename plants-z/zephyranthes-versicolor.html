Zephyranthes versicolor

Zephyranthes versicolor, commonly known as the rainflower or fairy lily, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a bulbous perennial that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth. Its flowers bloom after rainfall, often during the rainy season, and it is known for its ability to thrive in coastal areas.

Zephyranthes versicolor is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and as a cut flower.
