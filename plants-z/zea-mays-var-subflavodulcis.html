Zea mays var. subflavodulcis

Zea mays var. subflavodulcis, commonly known as the sweet corn variety with golden kernels, is a variant of maize characterized by its unique coloration.

This plant belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is cultivated for its edible, starchy seeds. The subspecies is notable for its elongated, silky tassels and the vibrant yellow to orange color of its kernels. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The botanical features of Zea mays var. subflavodulcis make it a popular choice for both culinary and agricultural purposes.
