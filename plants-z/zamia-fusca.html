Zamia fusca

Zamia fusca, commonly known as the Mexican cycad, is a perennial plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the family Zamiaceae and is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically pollinated by insects. Zamia fusca is adapted to grow in dry, rocky soils and can tolerate periods of drought.

It is considered a slow-growing plant with a long lifespan, often reaching several decades in the wild.
