Zephyranthes montana

Zephyranthes montana, commonly known as the mountain rain lily, is a species of flowering plant native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces delicate, white or pale pink flowers during the spring and early summer. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It is often found in open areas such as prairies and woodland edges.

Zephyranthes montana is well adapted to dry conditions and is considered a drought-tolerant plant.
