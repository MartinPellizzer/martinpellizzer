Zingiber flavomaculosum

Zingiber flavomaculosum, commonly known as the yellow-flowered ginger, is a rare and ornamental plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers and turmeric. The plant is characterized by its striking yellow and green striped leaves and its fragrant, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires well-drained, fertile soil.

Due to its rarity and aesthetic appeal, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
