Zephyranthes alba

Zephyranthes alba, commonly known as the white rain lily, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces delicate, white, star-shaped flowers during the rainy season. The plant typically grows from a bulb and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate to bright light for optimal growth.

Zephyranthes alba is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
