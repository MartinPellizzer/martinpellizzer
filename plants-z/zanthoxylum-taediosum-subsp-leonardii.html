Zanthoxylum taediosum subsp. leonardii

Zanthoxylum taediosum subsp.

leonardii, commonly known as the Leonard's prickly ash, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated margins and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring.

The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are edible for some wildlife but toxic to humans. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
