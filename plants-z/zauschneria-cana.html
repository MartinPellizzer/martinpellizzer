Zauschneria cana

Zauschneria cana, commonly known as the showy goldcup, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along streambanks and in open woodlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a rosette at the base.

Zauschneria cana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
