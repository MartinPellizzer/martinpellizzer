Zieria southwellii

Zieria southwellii, commonly known as the golden zieria, is a small evergreen shrub native to the eastern regions of Australia.

It features delicate, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence, giving it a soft, silvery appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in spring, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is typically found in heathland and dry forest environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
