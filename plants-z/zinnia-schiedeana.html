Zinnia schiedeana

Zinnia schiedeana, commonly known as the Mexican zinnia, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, daisy-like flowers that range in color from yellow to red and orange. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 3 feet, with narrow, hairy leaves. Its flowers are composed of a central disk surrounded by ray florets, and it thrives in full sun with well-drained soil.

Zinnia schiedeana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
