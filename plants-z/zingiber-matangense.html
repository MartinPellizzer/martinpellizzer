Zingiber matangense

Zingiber matangense, commonly known as the Matangense ginger, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other well-known gingers such as ginger and turmeric. The plant is characterized by its aromatic rhizomes, which are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. Its leaves are long and narrow, and it produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish flowers.

Zingiber matangense is valued for its medicinal properties and is often cultivated for both its ornamental and practical uses.
