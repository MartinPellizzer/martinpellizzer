Zygostates apiculata

Zygostates apiculata, commonly known as the woolly ant plant, is a unique species native to the humid forests of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly covering of white or gray hairs on its stems and leaves, which helps it retain moisture in its native environment. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often overlooked due to their modest size and color. Its leaves are typically narrow and elongated, adapted for efficient water absorption.

Despite its delicate appearance, Zygostates apiculata is known for its resilience in shaded, moist conditions.
