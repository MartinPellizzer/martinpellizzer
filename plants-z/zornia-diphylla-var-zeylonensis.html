Zornia diphylla var. zeylonensis

Zornia diphylla var. zeylonensis, commonly known as the Sri Lankan zornia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is a low-growing annual herb with hairy stems and opposite leaves that are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warm season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a subject of interest in ecological and horticultural studies.
