Zelkova davidii

Zelkova davidii, commonly known as David's zelkova, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern China.

It belongs to the Ulmaceae family, which includes elms and related species. The tree is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall and its smooth, gray bark that develops fissures with age. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early spring followed by winged fruits.

Zelkova davidii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in urban landscapes for its shade and resilience.
