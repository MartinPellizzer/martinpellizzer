Zannichellia dentata subsp. polycarpa

Zannichellia dentata subsp.

polycarpa, commonly known as the broad-leaved water clover, is a aquatic plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in shallow, slow-moving freshwater habitats such as ponds and ditches.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern, with a distinctive white or pinkish bloom. It produces small, floating flowers that are pollinated by insects.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in aquatic ecosystems.
