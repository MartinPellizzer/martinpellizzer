Zelkova serrata var. stipulacea

Zelkova serrata var. stipulacea, commonly known as the Japanese zelkova, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers in the spring, followed by winged fruits that disperse its seeds. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
