Zanthoxylum bifoliolatum subsp. azuense

Zanthoxylum bifoliolatum subsp.

azuense, commonly known as the Azuense prickly ash, is a shrub native to the cloud forests of Central America. It is characterized by its compound leaves with two leaflets, which are often covered in fine, stinging hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. Its bark and leaves contain bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional medicine.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is adapted to nutrient-poor soils.
