Ziziphora mariana

Ziziphora mariana, commonly known as American mountain tea, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in open woodlands and meadows. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink to purple and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Ziziphora mariana is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
