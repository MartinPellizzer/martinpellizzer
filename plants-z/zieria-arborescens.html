Zieria arborescens

Zieria arborescens, commonly known as the tree zieria, is a small evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring dense, leathery foliage with a distinctive aromatic scent. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers in winter, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are alternate, oval-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Zieria arborescens is well adapted to coastal conditions, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils.
