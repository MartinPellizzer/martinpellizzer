Ziziphus mucronata var. glauca

Ziziphus mucronata var. glauca, commonly known as the blue-leaf date tree, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive blue-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, date-like fruits that are typically harvested when fully mature. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance and adaptability to poor soils.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local communities.
