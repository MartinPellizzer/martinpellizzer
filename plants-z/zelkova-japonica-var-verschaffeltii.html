Zelkova japonica var. verschaffeltii

Zelkova japonica var. verschaffeltii, commonly known as the Japanese zelkova, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ulmaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras as its fruit. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

This variety is noted for its compact growth habit and adaptability to urban environments.
