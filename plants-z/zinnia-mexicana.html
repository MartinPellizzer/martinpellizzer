Zinnia mexicana

Zinnia mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican zinnia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of bright yellow to orange flowers. Zinnia mexicana is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its natural habitat includes rocky soils and open areas, where it often blooms from summer to early fall.
