Zephyranthes blumenavia

Zephyranthes blumenavia, commonly known as the rain lily, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a small, herbaceous plant that typically grows to a height of about 15-30 centimeters. The plant produces delicate, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in response to rainfall, hence its common name. It has strap-like, linear leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette.

Zephyranthes blumenavia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to thrive in well-drained, sandy soils.
