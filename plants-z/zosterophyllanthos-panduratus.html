Zosterophyllanthos panduratus

Zosterophyllanthos panduratus, commonly known as the golden flame fern, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its intricate, branching structure and vibrant golden-yellow foliage.

It thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in the shaded areas of rivers and streams. The plant's leaves are elongated and ribbon-like, arranged in a spiral pattern around its central axis. Its reproductive structures are relatively simple, consisting of small sporangia that release spores into the water.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Zosterophyllanthos panduratus is considered an important indicator species for the health of freshwater ecosystems.
