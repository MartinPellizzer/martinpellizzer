Zanthoxylum schinifolium var. inermis

Zanthoxylum schinifolium var. inermis, commonly known as the smooth-horned prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. Its bark and leaves contain compounds that have been used in traditional medicine.

The species is notable for its lack of thorns, distinguishing it from other varieties of Zanthoxylum.
