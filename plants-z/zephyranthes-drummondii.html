Zephyranthes drummondii

Zephyranthes drummondii, commonly known as the Drummond's rain lily, is a species of flowering plant native to the southeastern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a bulbous perennial that produces showy, white or pale pink flowers during the rainy season. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are strap-shaped and grass-like, and the flowers bloom in clusters on tall, slender stems.

Zephyranthes drummondii is valued for its ornamental beauty and its ability to naturalize in suitable environments.
