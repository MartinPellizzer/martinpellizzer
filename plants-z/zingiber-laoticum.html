Zingiber laoticum

Zingiber laoticum, commonly known as the Laotian ginger, is a rhizomatous herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other well-known gingers such as turmeric and cardamom. The plant produces tall, erect stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with three sepals and three petals, and it blooms during the rainy season.

The rhizomes of Zingiber laoticum are valued for their aromatic properties and are used in both culinary and medicinal applications.
