Zingiber yersinii

Zingiber yersinii, commonly known as the Yersin's ginger, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other economically important plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant features a rhizomatous underground stem, which is the primary part used for propagation and commercial purposes. Its leaves are long, narrow, and have a distinctive aromatic scent.

Zingiber yersinii is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
