Zamioculcas zamiifolia

Zamioculcas zamiifolia, commonly known as the ZZ plant, is a member of the Araceae family and is native to eastern Africa.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its dark green, waxy, arrow-shaped leaves that grow in a rosette pattern. The plant is adapted to thrive in low-light conditions and requires minimal water, making it highly drought-tolerant. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to store water efficiently, contributing to its resilience.

Zamioculcas zamiifolia is also known for its ability to purify indoor air, enhancing its popularity as a houseplant.
