Ziziphus peruviana

Ziziphus peruviana, commonly known as the Indian date palm or Chinese date, is a small deciduous tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-green fruits that ripen to a dark red or purple color. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in arid and semi-arid climates, making it a valuable species for reforestation and sustainable agriculture. Its fruits are edible, often consumed fresh or processed into jams and wines.

The tree also has historical significance in traditional medicine and is used for its ornamental value in landscaping.
