Zanthoxylum asiaticum

Zanthoxylum asiaticum, commonly known as the Asian prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves, which are often dotted with small, glandular structures. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. Its bark and fruits contain compounds with medicinal properties, including analgesic and anti-inflammatory effects.

Zanthoxylum asiaticum thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along riverbanks.
