Zeuxanthe moultonii

Zeuxanthe moultonii, commonly known as the Moulton's sea fan, is a species of soft coral found in the Indo-Pacific region.

It is characterized by its branching, fan-like structure composed of flexible, calcareous skeletons. The coral exhibits a variety of coloration, often featuring yellow, orange, and brown hues. It thrives in shallow, tropical waters and plays a vital role in reef ecosystems by providing habitat for various marine organisms.

Zeuxanthe moultonii is also known for its symbiotic relationship with zooxanthellae, which contribute to its vibrant colors and provide it with energy through photosynthesis.
