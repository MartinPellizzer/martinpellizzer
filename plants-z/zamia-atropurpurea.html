Zamia atropurpurea

Zamia atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple cycad, is a member of the Zamiaceae family and is native to Mexico.

It is a slow-growing, evergreen plant characterized by its stout, woody trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces distinctive cones, with male and female cones typically occurring on separate plants. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
