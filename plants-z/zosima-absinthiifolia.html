Zosima absinthiifolia

Zosima absinthiifolia, commonly known as the bitterroot, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces an aromatic, bitter-tasting root that has been used traditionally in herbal medicine. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a preference for well-drained, alkaline soils.

Zosima absinthiifolia is often found in alpine and subalpine environments, thriving in cool, dry climates.
