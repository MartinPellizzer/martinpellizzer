Zanthoxylum weberbaueri

Zanthoxylum weberbaueri, commonly known as the Weberbauer's prickly ash, is a shrub native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are edible for some animals but contain seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties make it of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
