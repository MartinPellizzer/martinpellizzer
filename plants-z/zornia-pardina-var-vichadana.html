Zornia pardina var. vichadana

Zornia pardina var. vichadana, commonly known as the Vichadana zornia, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves, which are typically pubescent. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, often appearing in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a legume pod that matures into a dry, dehiscent capsule, dispersing seeds by wind.

This species is native to parts of Asia and is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats.
