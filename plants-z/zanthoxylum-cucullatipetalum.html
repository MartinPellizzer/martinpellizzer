Zanthoxylum cucullatipetalum

Zanthoxylum cucullatipetalum, commonly known as the hooded flame tree, is a striking ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red and orange flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a dramatic display during the warmer months. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, woody fruits that contain toxic seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical climates.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in gardens and public spaces.
