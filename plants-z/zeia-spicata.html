Zeia spicata

Zeia spicata, commonly known as the spikeflower plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to the temperate regions of the Americas.

It features upright stems that bear dense clusters of small, spiky flowers, typically in shades of white or pale purple. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
