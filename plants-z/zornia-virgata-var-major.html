Zornia virgata var. major

Zornia virgata var. major, commonly known as the largeleaf zornia, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in fields, woodland edges, and along roadsides. Its flowers are usually pink to reddish in color and bloom from late spring to early summer.

Zornia virgata var. major is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
