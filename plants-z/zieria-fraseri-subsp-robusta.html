Zieria fraseri subsp. robusta

Zieria fraseri subsp.

robusta, commonly known as the robust zieria, is a small shrub native to the south-eastern regions of Australia. It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers.

The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils in open heathlands or along coastal areas. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, often with a reddish or purple hue, and it produces small, dark blue to black berries.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its compact form and seasonal flowering.
