Zanthoxylum emarginellum

Zanthoxylum emarginellum, commonly known as the Texas prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and rocky areas.

Zanthoxylum emarginellum is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
