Zanthoxylum nigrum

Zanthoxylum nigrum, commonly known as black prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are often consumed by birds and small mammals. Its bark and leaves contain various bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional medicine and phytochemistry.

Zanthoxylum nigrum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and stream banks.
