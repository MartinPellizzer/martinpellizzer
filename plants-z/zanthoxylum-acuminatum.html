Zanthoxylum acuminatum

Zanthoxylum acuminatum, commonly known as the prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Its bark and leaves contain compounds with potential medicinal properties, including pain-relieving and anti-inflammatory effects.

Zanthoxylum acuminatum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
