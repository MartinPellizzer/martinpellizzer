Ziziphus orbicularis

Ziziphus orbicularis, commonly known as the Chinese date or Indian date, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Asia.

It produces small, yellowish-green fruits that are edible and have a sweet, chewy texture. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and panicle-like inflorescences. It thrives in arid and semi-arid climates, making it a hardy species suitable for dry regions.

The fruit is often used in traditional medicine and as a food source in various cultures.
