Zanthoxylum ciliatum subsp. ciliatum

Zanthoxylum ciliatum subsp.

ciliatum, commonly known as the prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States. It features compound leaves with serrated margins and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are edible for some bird species. Its bark and leaves contain compounds with historical use in traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.
