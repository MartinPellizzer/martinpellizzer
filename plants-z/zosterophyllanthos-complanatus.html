Zosterophyllanthos complanatus

Zosterophyllanthos complanatus, commonly known as the flat zoster plant, is a rare and ancient species belonging to the order Zosterophyllales.

It is characterized by its flat, ribbon-like leaves that resemble those of early vascular plants, suggesting a possible link to the evolutionary transition from aquatic to terrestrial life. The plant exhibits a simple vascular system, with xylem and phloem tissues arranged in a manner similar to early land plants. It is typically found in moist, shaded environments, often near water bodies, where it can thrive in humid conditions.

Due to its unique morphology and evolutionary significance, it is of great interest to botanists studying the origins of plant life on land.
