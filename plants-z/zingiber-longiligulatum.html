Zingiber longiligulatum

Zingiber longiligulatum, commonly known as the long-ligule ginger, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other economically important plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant features slender, upright stems with broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in a spike inflorescence, and are accompanied by distinctive, elongated bracts.

The rhizome of Zingiber longiligulatum is used in traditional medicine and is believed to have anti-inflammatory properties.
