Zygogynum amplexicaule subsp. luteum

Zygogynum amplexicaule subsp.

luteum, commonly known as the yellow-stemmed zygogynum, is a perennial herb native to parts of Africa. It is characterized by its distinctive yellow stems and heart-shaped leaves that clasping the stems.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
