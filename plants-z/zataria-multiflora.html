Zataria multiflora

Zataria multiflora, commonly known as Persian licorice, is a perennial herb native to the mountains of Iran and Turkey.

It is characterized by its aromatic, yellowish-green leaves and small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces oval, woody fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine regions.

Zataria multiflora is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies for centuries.
