Zexmenia tequilana

Zexmenia tequilana, commonly known as the Tequila plant, is a succulent native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. Due to its unique appearance and cultural significance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
