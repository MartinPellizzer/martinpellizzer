Ziziphora infecta

Ziziphora infecta, commonly known as the sticky ziziphora, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a strong, mint-like scent.

Ziziphora infecta is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
