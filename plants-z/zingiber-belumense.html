Zingiber belumense

Zingiber belumense, commonly known as the Borneo ginger, is a flowering plant native to the island of Borneo.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers such as turmeric and cardamom. The plant is characterized by its tall, erect stem and broad, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with a distinctive, fragrant scent.

Zingiber belumense is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
