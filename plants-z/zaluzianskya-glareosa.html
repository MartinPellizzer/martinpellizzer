Zaluzianskya glareosa

Zaluzianskya glareosa, commonly known as the silverleaf bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking silver-green foliage and showy blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is a perennial herb with a compact growth habit, often reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is cultivated in gardens and rockeries.
