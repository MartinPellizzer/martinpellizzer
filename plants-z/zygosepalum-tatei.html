Zygosepalum tatei

Zygosepalum tatei, commonly known as the Tate's zygosepalum, is a rare and unique bromeliad native to the high-altitude regions of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated inflorescence that bears small, vibrant orange flowers, which bloom sequentially along the stem. The plant has a compact rosette structure with narrow, grass-like leaves that form a central rosette, aiding in water retention. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil to flourish.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Zygosepalum tatei is considered a rare and endangered species.
