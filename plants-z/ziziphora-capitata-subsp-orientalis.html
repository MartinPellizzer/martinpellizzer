Ziziphora capitata subsp. orientalis

Ziziphora capitata subsp.

orientalis, commonly known as Turkish tea or mountain tea, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Central Asia. It features aromatic, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers arranged in dense, rounded clusters, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats.

Its essential oils contain compounds like thymol and carvacrol, contributing to its medicinal and aromatic properties.
