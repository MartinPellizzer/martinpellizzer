Zostera hornemannii

Zostera hornemannii, commonly known as the Hornemann's eelgrass, is a marine flowering plant belonging to the family Zosteraceae.

It is a perennial seagrass that forms dense underwater meadows in shallow coastal waters. The plant has long, ribbon-like leaves that are typically green and can grow up to several meters in length. It plays a crucial role in stabilizing sediments and providing habitat for various marine organisms.

Zostera hornemannii is also an important component of estuarine and coastal ecosystems.
