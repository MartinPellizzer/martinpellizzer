Zaluzianskya minuta

Zaluzianskya minuta, commonly known as the dwarf zonal geranium, is a low-growing perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has a compact, mounding habit with dark green, velvety leaves that are often marked with distinctive zonation patterns. It is well-suited for rock gardens, containers, and borders due to its compact size and ornamental value.

Zaluzianskya minuta prefers full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.
