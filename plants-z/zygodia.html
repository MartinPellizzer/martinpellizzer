Zygodia

Zygodia, commonly known as the zigzag plant, is a member of the Araceae family, characterized by its unique, spiraling growth pattern.

Its stems exhibit a zigzag shape, which helps it climb and support itself in its natural habitat. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers enclosed within a spadix, typical of the Araceae family. Zygodia thrives in moist, shady environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and have a glossy appearance, contributing to its ornamental value.
