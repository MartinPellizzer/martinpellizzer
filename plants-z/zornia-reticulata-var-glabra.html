Zornia reticulata var. glabra

Zornia reticulata var. glabra, commonly known as smooth zornia, is a low-growing annual herb native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, hairy stems that often lie prostrate on the ground, forming dense mats. The plant produces small, clustered flowers with five petals, typically in shades of pink or red. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in woodland edges and wetlands.
