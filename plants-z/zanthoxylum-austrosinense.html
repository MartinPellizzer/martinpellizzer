Zanthoxylum austrosinense

Zanthoxylum austrosinense, commonly known as the Chinese prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern China.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are edible and often used in traditional Chinese cuisine. Its bark and leaves contain various bioactive compounds, making it valuable in herbal medicine.

Zanthoxylum austrosinense thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.
