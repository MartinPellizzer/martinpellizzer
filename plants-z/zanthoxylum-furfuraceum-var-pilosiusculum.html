Zanthoxylum furfuraceum var. pilosiusculum

Zanthoxylum furfuraceum var. pilosiusculum, commonly known as the hairy prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. Its bark and leaves contain compounds with potential medicinal properties, though it is toxic if ingested in large quantities.

The species is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
