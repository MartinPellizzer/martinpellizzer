Zanthoxylum albuquerquei

Zanthoxylum albuquerquei, commonly known as Albuquerque thornbush, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Its thorny stems and aromatic leaves make it a distinctive and ecologically significant plant in arid environments.

Zanthoxylum albuquerquei is adapted to dry conditions and thrives in well-drained soils.
