Zabelia buddleioides var. intercedens

Zabelia buddleioides var. intercedens, commonly known as the desert honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its woody stems, opposite leaves, and showy yellow to orange tubular flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is adapted to hot, dry climates and has a deep root system for water conservation. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, facilitating its dispersal in harsh environments.

Its botanical features make it a resilient and ecologically significant species in desert ecosystems.
