Zanthoxylum fagara subsp. aguilarii

Zanthoxylum fagara subsp.

aguilarii, commonly known as the Mexican prickly ash, is a shrub or small tree native to Central America and parts of Mexico. It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnate with 5 to 9 leaflets, and its clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers.

The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.
