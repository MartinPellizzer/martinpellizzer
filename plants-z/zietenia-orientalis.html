Zietenia orientalis

Zietenia orientalis, commonly known as the oriental beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant, white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces bright red, fleshy berries that ripen in the fall, which are edible for humans and wildlife. Zietenia orientalis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
