Zeuxine madagascariensis

Zeuxine madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar zingiber, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the ginger family, Zingiberaceae, and is characterized by its ornamental, strap-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
