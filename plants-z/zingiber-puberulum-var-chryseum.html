Zingiber puberulum var. chryseum

Zingiber puberulum var. chryseum, commonly known as the golden ginger, is a rare and ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other ginger species, and is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers and aromatic leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its leaves are broad, glossy, and often have a slight pubescent texture, giving it a unique appearance.

Due to its rarity and aesthetic value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
