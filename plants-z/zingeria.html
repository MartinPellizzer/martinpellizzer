Zingeria

Zingeria, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to tropical Africa.

These plants are characterized by their rosettes of velvety, waxy leaves and showy, tubular flowers that bloom in a variety of colors. Zingeria species typically thrive in humid, well-drained soil and require indirect sunlight to prevent leaf scorch. They are popular houseplants due to their ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.

The genus includes several species, such as Zingeria maculata and Zingeria ramosa, which are prized for their vibrant blooms and compact growth habit.
