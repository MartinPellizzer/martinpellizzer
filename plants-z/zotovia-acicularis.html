Zotovia acicularis

Zotovia acicularis, commonly known as the needleleaf zotovia, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are often white or pale pink in color. Zotovia acicularis thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous areas with well-drained soils.

Its unique morphology and habitat make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
