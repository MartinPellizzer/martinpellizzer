Zea americana

Zea americana, commonly known as corn or maize, is a tall annual grass native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its large, tassel-like inflorescence and edible grains. The plant typically grows to heights of 2 to 6 meters and has narrow, sword-shaped leaves. Its reproductive structures include both male and female flowers, with the male tassels at the top and the female ears of corn below.

Zea americana is a staple crop worldwide, valued for its nutritional content and agricultural importance.
