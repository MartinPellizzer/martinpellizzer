Zanthoxylum budrunga

Zanthoxylum budrunga, commonly known as the Indian prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Its bark and fruits contain various bioactive compounds, making it valuable in traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in forested areas.
