Zanthoxylum araliaceum

Zanthoxylum araliaceum, commonly known as the prickly ash or American prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern and central United States.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated margins and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are a food source for various birds and mammals. Its bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

Zanthoxylum araliaceum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
