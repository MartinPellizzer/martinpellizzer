Zingiber flavescens

Zingiber flavescens, commonly known as the yellow ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers like turmeric and cardamom. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of bright yellow flowers. Its rhizomes, which are underground stems, are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

The botanical features of Zingiber flavescens make it a valuable species for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
