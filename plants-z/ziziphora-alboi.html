Ziziphora alboi

Ziziphora alboi, commonly known as white ziziphora, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant features slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are typically pale blue to white, arranged in whorls along the stem, and bloom during the summer months.

Ziziphora alboi is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
