Zerna erecta subsp. alexeenkoi

Zerna erecta subsp.

alexeenkoi, commonly known as the Armenian wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Caucasus region. It typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing a compact growth habit with dense, tufted foliage.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that helps it retain moisture in harsh conditions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in dry garden settings.
