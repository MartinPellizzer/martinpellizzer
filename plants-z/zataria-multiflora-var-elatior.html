Zataria multiflora var. elatior

Zataria multiflora var. elatior, commonly known as Iranian oregano, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark green, slightly hairy leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to violet flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or arid environments. Its essential oils, rich in thymol and carvacrol, contribute to its use in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.

The variety elatior is distinguished by its taller growth and more robust foliage compared to other varieties of Zataria multiflora.
