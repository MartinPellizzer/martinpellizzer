Zingiber flavofusiforme

Zingiber flavofusiforme, commonly known as the yellow ginger, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers and turmeric. The plant features a rhizomatous root system and produces tall, branched inflorescences with brightly colored, fragrant flowers. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
