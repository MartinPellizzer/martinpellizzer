Zanthoxylum leratii

Zanthoxylum leratii, commonly known as the Chinese prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are edible but have a sharp, spicy flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Zanthoxylum leratii is valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
