Zebrina purpusii

Zebrina purpusii, commonly known as the water spinach or river spinach, is a tropical aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, slender, edible leaves that are often used in various cuisines. The plant thrives in shallow, freshwater environments and can grow rapidly under favorable conditions. Its stems are often used as a vegetable, while the leaves are also consumed.

Zebrina purpusii is known for its adaptability and is commonly cultivated in home gardens and aquaponic systems.
