Zingiber mioga var. variegatum

Zingiber mioga var. variegatum, commonly known as the variegated ginger, is a unique variant of the ginger plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking variegated leaves that display shades of green, white, and yellow, making it a visually appealing ornamental plant. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding to its aesthetic value. While it is primarily grown for its ornamental qualities, it can also be used in culinary applications similar to other ginger species.

Its growth is typically slow, requiring well-draining soil and partial shade to thrive.
