Ziziphus mauritiana subsp. orthacantha

Ziziphus mauritiana subsp.

orthacantha, commonly known as the Indian date palm, is a shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent. It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green fruits that are edible and have a sweet, honey-like flavor. It thrives in arid and semi-arid climates, making it well adapted to tropical and subtropical regions.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its fruit and timber.
