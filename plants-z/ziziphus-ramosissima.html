Ziziphus ramosissima

Ziziphus ramosissima, commonly known as the creosote bush, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, sprawling growth habit and its ability to thrive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that give way to edible, red berries in the fall. Its leaves are small, waxy, and often covered in a fine, white coating that helps reduce water loss.

Ziziphus ramosissima is also known for its strong, aromatic scent, which is particularly noticeable after rain.
