Zornia biarticulata

Zornia biarticulata, commonly known as the two-jointed zornia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, herbaceous growth habit. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are alternate and pinnately compound, with stipules present at the base of each leaflet.

Zornia biarticulata is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.
