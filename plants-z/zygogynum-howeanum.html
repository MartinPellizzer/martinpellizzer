Zygogynum howeanum

Zygogynum howeanum, commonly known as the Malabar tamarind, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows to a height of about 5 meters. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by elongated, woody pods containing kidney-shaped seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant is valued for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and floral display.
