Zostera serrulata

Zostera serrulata, commonly known as the Florida eelgrass, is a marine flowering plant found in shallow coastal waters of the southeastern United States.

It has long, ribbon-like leaves with a serrated edge, adapted for efficient photosynthesis in underwater environments. This plant plays a crucial role in stabilizing sediments and providing habitat for various marine organisms. Zostera serrulata is also an important component of seagrass meadows that support biodiversity and help maintain water quality.

However, it is threatened by habitat loss and pollution, making conservation efforts essential for its survival.
