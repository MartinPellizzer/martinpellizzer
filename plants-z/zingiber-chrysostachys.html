Zingiber chrysostachys

Zingiber chrysostachys, commonly known as the golden spike ginger, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers and turmeric. The plant features narrow, sword-shaped leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers that resemble spikes. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

Zingiber chrysostachys thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
