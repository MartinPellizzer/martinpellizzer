Zygophyllum macrophyllum

Zygophyllum macrophyllum, commonly known as the large-leaved sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It belongs to the family Zygophyllaceae and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water in harsh environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access groundwater and survive prolonged droughts.

Zygophyllum macrophyllum plays an important ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
