Zanthoxylum obtusifolium

Zanthoxylum obtusifolium, commonly known as the blunt-leaf prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are edible for some wildlife but toxic to humans.

Its bark and leaves contain compounds with potential medicinal properties, making it of interest in ethnobotanical studies.
