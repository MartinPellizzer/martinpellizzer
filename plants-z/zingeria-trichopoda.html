Zingeria trichopoda

Zingeria trichopoda, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, velvety hairs, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color. Zingeria trichopoda is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands.

It is considered a weed in some agricultural areas due to its rapid growth and ability to spread easily.
