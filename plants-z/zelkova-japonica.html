Zelkova japonica

Zelkova japonica, commonly known as Japanese zelkova, is a deciduous tree native to Japan and Korea.

It belongs to the Ulmaceae family, which includes elms and other similar trees. The tree is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are followed by winged fruits that aid in seed dispersal.

Zelkova japonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and parks for its elegant appearance.
