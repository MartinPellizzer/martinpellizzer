Zanthoxylum kauaense

Zanthoxylum kauaense, commonly known as the Hawaiian prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the tropical regions of Hawaii.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often covered in fine, bristle-like hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. Its bark and leaves contain a variety of bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional medicine.

Zanthoxylum kauaense thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland habitats.
