Zaleya galericulata subsp. australis

Zaleya galericulata subsp.

australis, commonly known as the Australian rock daisy, is a perennial herb native to southern Australia. It features striking, showy yellow flowers that bloom in spring, typically arranged in dense, terminal clusters.

The plant has a fibrous root system and hairy, linear leaves that are adapted to arid conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native range.
