Zanthoxylum monogynum subsp. monogynum

Zanthoxylum monogynum subsp.

monogynum, commonly known as the Chinese prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia. It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated margins and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers.

The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are edible for birds but generally considered toxic to humans. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value and medicinal uses make it a notable species in horticulture and traditional medicine.
