Zeuxine fritzii

Zeuxine fritzii, commonly known as the purple-flowered zeuxine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, clustered inflorescences that bloom in shades of purple and white. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky slopes. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three heart-shaped leaflets, and the stems are covered in fine hairs.

Zeuxine fritzii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
