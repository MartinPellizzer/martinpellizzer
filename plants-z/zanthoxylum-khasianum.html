Zanthoxylum khasianum

Zanthoxylum khasianum, commonly known as the Khasi prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces bright red berries that are often used in traditional medicine for their astringent properties.

Its bark and leaves contain various bioactive compounds, making it valuable in both ecological and pharmacological contexts.
