Zygophyllum kansuense

Zygophyllum kansuense, commonly known as the Kansu desertflower, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Zygophyllaceae and is characterized by its small, fleshy leaves and yellow or orange flowers. The plant is well adapted to harsh environments, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in dry conditions. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in desert and steppe ecosystems.

Zygophyllum kansuense plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
