Zygopetalum maculatum subsp. triste

Zygopetalum maculatum subsp.

triste, commonly known as the melancholic spotted orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family. It is native to the Andes Mountains of South America, specifically in regions of Peru and Ecuador.

The plant is characterized by its striking, dark red flowers with contrasting white spots, which give it its common name. It typically grows in high-altitude cloud forests, where it thrives in cool, humid conditions.

The subspecies is noted for its relatively compact growth habit and is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value.
