Zingeriopsis

Zingeriopsis, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated, and often pale green or white flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions. Zingeriopsis is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on the surface of trees without deriving nutrients from them. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Zingeriopsis is highly sought after by botanists and conservationists.
