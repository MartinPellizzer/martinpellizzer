Zingiber popaense

Zingiber popaense, commonly known as the Papuan ginger, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of Papua, Indonesia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers and turmeric. The plant features a rhizomatous stem with narrow, linear leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-white flowers. Its botanical characteristics include a compact growth habit and a unique floral structure that distinguishes it from other ginger species.

Zingiber popaense is of interest to botanists due to its potential medicinal properties and limited distribution.
