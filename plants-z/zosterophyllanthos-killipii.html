Zosterophyllanthos killipii

Zosterophyllanthos killipii, commonly known as the Killip's zosterophyll, is a rare and ancient plant belonging to the order Zosterophyllales.

It is characterized by its unique morphology, featuring elongated, ribbon-like structures that resemble early marine algae. This plant is believed to have evolved in shallow marine environments and is often found in fossil records from the Paleozoic era. Its botanical significance lies in its potential role in understanding early plant evolution and the transition from aquatic to terrestrial life.

Due to its limited distribution and fragile nature, it is considered a valuable subject for paleobotanical research.
