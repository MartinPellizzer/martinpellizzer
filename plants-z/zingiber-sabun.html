Zingiber sabun

Zingiber sabun, commonly known as soap ginger, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, rhizomatous stem and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with a strong, spicy fragrance.

Zingiber sabun is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the extraction of essential oils used in traditional medicine and perfumery.
