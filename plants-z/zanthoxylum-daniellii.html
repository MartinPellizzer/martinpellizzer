Zanthoxylum daniellii

Zanthoxylum daniellii, commonly known as the African prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often pinnate and have serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red or orange berries that are attractive to birds. Its bark and leaves contain various bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional medicine.

Zanthoxylum daniellii thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or savanna habitats.
