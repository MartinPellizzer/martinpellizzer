Zieria odorifera subsp. warrabahensis

Zieria odorifera subsp.

warrabahensis, commonly known as the Warrabah zieria, is a small shrub native to the tropical rainforests of northern Queensland, Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters.

The plant has a compact growth habit and is often found in shaded, moist environments within the understorey of rainforest ecosystems. Its fragrance is a notable feature, contributing to its common name. Zieria odorifera subsp.

warrabahensis plays a role in supporting local biodiversity, providing habitat and nectar for native pollinators.
