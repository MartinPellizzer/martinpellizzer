Zanthoxylum psammophilum

Zanthoxylum psammophilum, commonly known as the sandblain hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically alternate and have serrated margins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Zanthoxylum psammophilum is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
