Zornia tetraphylla var. linearis

Zornia tetraphylla var. linearis, commonly known as the slender zornia, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, often twining stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple, followed by linear, elongated pods containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in woodland edges and wetlands.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and soil stabilization in its native habitat.
