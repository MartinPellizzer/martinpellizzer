Zexmenia umbellata

Zexmenia umbellata, commonly known as the umbrella lily, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, umbrella-like inflorescence composed of numerous white or pale pink petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a glossy surface.

Zexmenia umbellata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
