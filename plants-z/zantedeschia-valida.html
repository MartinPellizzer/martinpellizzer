Zantedeschia valida

Zantedeschia valida, commonly known as the giant calla, is a striking plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves and showy, white or pale yellow spatulate flowers. The plant belongs to the Araceae family, which is known for its unique inflorescence structure called a spadix, surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Zantedeschia valida is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
