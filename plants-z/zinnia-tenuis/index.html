Zinnia Tenuis

Threadleaf zinnia, scientifically known as Zinnia tenuis, is a compact annual flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is prized for its delicate, thread-like leaves and vibrant, daisy-like flowers that come in shades of yellow, orange, and red. This resilient plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners and landscape designers. Its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies adds ecological value to any garden setting. With its low maintenance requirements and ornamental appeal, Threadleaf zinnia remains a favorite among horticultural enthusiasts.
