Ziziphus mauritiana var. hysundrica

Ziziphus mauritiana var. hysundrica, commonly known as the Indian date palm, is a variant of the Indian date tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into edible drupes, which are often consumed fresh or processed into products like dates. It thrives in arid and semi-arid climates, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

This variety is valued for its fruit and its role in traditional agriculture and local ecosystems.
