Zostera stenophylla

Zostera stenophylla, commonly known as narrow-leaved eelgrass, is a marine flowering plant belonging to the family Zosteraceae.

It is a perennial seagrass that grows in shallow coastal waters, typically in sandy or muddy substrates. The plant has long, ribbon-like leaves that are usually less than 1 meter in length. It plays a vital role in marine ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Zostera stenophylla is also important for stabilizing sediments and improving water quality in coastal environments.
