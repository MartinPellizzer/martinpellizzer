Zea mays convar. excellens

Zea mays convar. excellens, commonly known as the sweet corn.

This variant is characterized by its high sugar content and tender kernels, which are typically harvested when the silks turn brown. It is a member of the grass family, Poaceae, and is native to the Americas. The plant produces tall stalks with multiple ears, each surrounded by leafy husks.

Its botanical features include parallel-veined leaves and a complex inflorescence with both male and female flowers.
