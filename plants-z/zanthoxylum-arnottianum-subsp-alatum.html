Zanthoxylum arnottianum subsp. alatum

Zanthoxylum arnottianum subsp.

alatum, commonly known as the broad-winged prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated margins and yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters.

The plant produces small, red berries that are a food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and unique, spiny structure.
