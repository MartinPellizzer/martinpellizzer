Zingiber corynostachyum

Zingiber corynostachyum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ginger, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers such as turmeric and cardamom. The plant features long, narrow leaves that are often arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color.

Zingiber corynostachyum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its aromatic properties.
