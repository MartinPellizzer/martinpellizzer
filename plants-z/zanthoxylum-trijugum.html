Zanthoxylum trijugum

Zanthoxylum trijugum, commonly known as the three-toothed prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with three pairs of leaflets, each ending in a sharp, spiny tip. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries in the fall. Its bark and berries contain bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and riverbanks.
