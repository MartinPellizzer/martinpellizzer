Zauschneria latifolia var. tomentella

Zauschneria latifolia var. tomentella, commonly known as the woolly marsh marigold, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense, woolly coating, giving it its common name. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks.

Its botanical structure includes a fibrous root system and stems that are often hairy.
