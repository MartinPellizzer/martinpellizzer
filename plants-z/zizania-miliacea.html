Zizania miliacea

Zizania miliacea, commonly known as wild rice, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It thrives in freshwater wetlands and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and small, spikelet-like flowers. The plant produces edible grains that are harvested from the panicles, which develop after flowering. Zizania miliacea is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing aquatic habitats and its historical significance as a food source for indigenous communities.

Its botanical features make it an important species in wetland ecosystems and conservation efforts.
