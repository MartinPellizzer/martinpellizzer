Zygopetalum parviflorum

Zygopetalum parviflorum, commonly known as the miniature orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, enabling it to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and it produces intricate, fragrant flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and moths.

Zygopetalum parviflorum is a popular choice for indoor cultivation due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
