Zingiber nigrimaculatum

Zingiber nigrimaculatum, commonly known as the black-spotted ginger, is a rhizomatous herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental leaves with dark purple to black spots. The plant produces fragrant, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its rhizomes are sometimes used in traditional medicine, though it is primarily cultivated for its aesthetic value.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
