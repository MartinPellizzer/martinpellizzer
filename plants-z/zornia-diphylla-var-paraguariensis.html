Zornia diphylla var. paraguariensis

Zornia diphylla var. paraguariensis, commonly known as Paraguayan zornia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Paraguay and parts of Brazil.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, tropical or subtropical habitats, often in lowland areas.

It is known for its ability to thrive in shaded environments and is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
