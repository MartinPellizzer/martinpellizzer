Zanthoxylum piperitum f. hispidum

Zanthoxylum piperitum f. hispidum, commonly known as Japanese prickly ash, is a variant of the prickly ash plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its bark and leaves contain compounds with potential medicinal properties, including analgesic and anti-inflammatory effects.

Zanthoxylum piperitum f. hispidum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in herbal practices.
