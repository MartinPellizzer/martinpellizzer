Zingiber sulphureum

Zingiber sulphureum, commonly known as the golden ginger, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other economically important plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant is characterized by its bright yellow rhizomes, which are rich in bioactive compounds. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a distinctive aromatic scent.

Zingiber sulphureum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has potential medicinal uses.
