Zingiber sylvaticum

Zingiber sylvaticum, commonly known as wild ginger, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other ginger species. The plant features a rhizomatous root system and lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange and are borne in terminal inflorescences.

Zingiber sylvaticum is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
