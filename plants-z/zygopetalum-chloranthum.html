Zygopetalum chloranthum

Zygopetalum chloranthum, commonly known as the green-flowered orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique greenish-yellow flowers that stand out against its dark green foliage. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and leathery, adapted to retain moisture in its natural habitat.

Zygopetalum chloranthum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
