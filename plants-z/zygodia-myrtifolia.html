Zygodia myrtifolia

Zygodia myrtifolia, commonly known as the myrtleleaf zygodia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae, which includes well-known genera such as Eucalyptus and Melaleuca. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators like bees and birds. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a glossy surface and a strong aromatic scent.

Zygodia myrtifolia is often found in wetland or swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils.
