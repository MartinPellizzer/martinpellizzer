Zingiber squarrosum

Zingiber squarrosum, commonly known as the hairy ginger, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers and turmeric. The plant features narrow, sword-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. Its rhizomes, which are underground stems, are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

Zingiber squarrosum is characterized by its hairy texture, giving it its common name.
