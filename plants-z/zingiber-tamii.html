Zingiber tamii

Zingiber tamii, commonly known as the purple ginger, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers such as turmeric and cardamom. The plant is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of fragrant, violet-colored flowers. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a waxy texture.

Zingiber tamii is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its potential medicinal properties.
