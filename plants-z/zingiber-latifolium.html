Zingiber latifolium

Zingiber latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers such as turmeric and cardamom. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length, giving it its common name. Its flowering stems produce clusters of fragrant, pale yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer.

The rhizomes of Zingiber latifolium are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and digestive properties.
