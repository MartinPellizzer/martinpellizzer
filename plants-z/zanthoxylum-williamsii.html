Zanthoxylum williamsii

Zanthoxylum williamsii, commonly known as the Williams prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Its bark and leaves contain compounds that have been used traditionally for their medicinal properties.
