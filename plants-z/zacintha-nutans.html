Zacintha nutans

Zacintha nutans, commonly known as the nodding carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and clusters of small, fragrant, pink or white flowers that droop downward. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Zacintha nutans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and floral arrangements.
