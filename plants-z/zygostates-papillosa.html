Zygostates papillosa

Zygostates papillosa, commonly known as the silky zygostates, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Zygostates papillosa is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
