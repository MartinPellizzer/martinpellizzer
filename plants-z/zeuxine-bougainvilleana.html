Zeuxine bougainvilleana

Zeuxine bougainvilleana, commonly known as the Bougainvillea zeuxine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, papery bracts that come in shades of pink, red, and purple, which are often mistaken for petals. The actual flowers are small and inconspicuous, located inside the bracts. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
