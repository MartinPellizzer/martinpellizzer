Zingiber nudicarpum

Zingiber nudicarpum, commonly known as the naked fruit ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other economically important spices like ginger and turmeric. The plant is characterized by its rhizomatous growth and narrow, linear leaves. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with three sepals and three petals, and it produces small, clustered fruits.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
