Zanthoxylum piperitum f. inerme

Zanthoxylum piperitum f. inerme, commonly known as the Japanese prickly ash, is a variant of the prickly ash plant native to East Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but have a sharp, spicy flavor. Its bark and leaves contain compounds like pungent alkaloids, which contribute to its medicinal uses in traditional herbal medicine.

The f. inerme form is distinguished by its reduced thorns compared to the typical species.
