Ziziphus havanensis var. bullata

Ziziphus havanensis var. bullata, commonly known as the Cuban date palm, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean, particularly Cuba.

It is a small to medium-sized tree with a slender trunk and dense, feathery foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into edible, sweet fruits resembling dates. Its leaves are pinnate, with numerous narrow, elongated leaflets.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in coastal and dry environments.
