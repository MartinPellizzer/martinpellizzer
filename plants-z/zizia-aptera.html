Zizia aptera

Zizia aptera, commonly known as the narrowleaf tansy, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky slopes. It has a fibrous root system and is known for its aromatic, slightly bitter scent.

Zizia aptera is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.
