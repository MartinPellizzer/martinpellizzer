Ziziphus rugosa var. harmandii

Ziziphus rugosa var. harmandii, commonly known as the Harmand's date vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody vines and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically consumed fresh or dried. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ornamental and potential agricultural purposes.
