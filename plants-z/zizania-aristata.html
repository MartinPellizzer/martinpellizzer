Zizania aristata

Zizania aristata, commonly known as the narrowleaf common reed, is a tall, grass-like plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of lakes and rivers. The plant has long, narrow leaves and produces feathery, spike-like inflorescences that are often visible in late summer. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Zizania aristata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine by some Native American tribes.
