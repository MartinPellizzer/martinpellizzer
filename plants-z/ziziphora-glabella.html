Ziziphora glabella

Ziziphora glabella, commonly known as woolly ziziphora, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features grayish-green, hairy leaves and produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and slopes.

Ziziphora glabella is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
