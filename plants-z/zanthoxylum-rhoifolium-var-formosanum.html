Zanthoxylum rhoifolium var. formosanum

Zanthoxylum rhoifolium var. formosanum, commonly known as the Formosan prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnate with serrated margins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties make it a notable species in horticulture and traditional medicine.
