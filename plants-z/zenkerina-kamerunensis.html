Zenkerina kamerunensis

Zenkerina kamerunensis, commonly known as the Cameroon sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to the tropical rainforests of Central Africa.

It belongs to the Moraceae family, which includes figs and mulberries, and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces a unique type of fruit known as a syncarp, which is composed of multiple fused berries. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing a distinctive appearance, while its wood is valued for its durability and workability.

This plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting a variety of wildlife and contributing to forest biodiversity.
