Zieria odorifera subsp. copelandii

Zieria odorifera subsp.

copelandii, commonly known as the fragrant zieria, is a small shrub native to the eastern coast of Australia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and heathlands.

The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, white flowers appear in clusters and are accompanied by a strong, sweet fragrance.

The subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance and aromatic qualities.
