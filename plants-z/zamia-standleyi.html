Zamia standleyi

Zamia standleyi, commonly known as the Standley cycad, is a perennial plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the family Zamiaceae and is characterized by its stout, unbranched stem and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces cones rather than flowers, with male and female cones typically occurring on separate plants. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and requires well-drained soil.

Zamia standleyi is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and slow growth rate.
