Ziziphora taurica subsp. taurica

Ziziphora taurica subsp.

taurica, commonly known as Caucasian tea, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region. It features aromatic, narrow leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color.

The plant is characterized by its creeping growth habit and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to the harsh climatic conditions of its native habitat.
