Zanthoxylum hillebrandii var. hiloense

Zanthoxylum hillebrandii var. hiloense, commonly known as the Hilo prickly ash, is a shrub or small tree native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically alternate and have serrated margins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in mesic to wet forest habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use by indigenous Hawaiian communities.
