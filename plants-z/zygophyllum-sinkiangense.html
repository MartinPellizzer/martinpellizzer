Zygophyllum sinkiangense

Zygophyllum sinkiangense, commonly known as the Xinjiang zygophyllum, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Zygophyllaceae and is characterized by its small, fleshy leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is well adapted to harsh environmental conditions, including high temperatures and low rainfall. It often grows on rocky soils and is found in desert and steppe ecosystems.

Zygophyllum sinkiangense plays a role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion in its native habitat.
