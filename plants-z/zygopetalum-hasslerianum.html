Zygopetalum hasslerianum

Zygopetalum hasslerianum, commonly known as the Hassler's zygopetalum, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors, including pink, white, and violet. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its pseudobulbs and strap-like leaves are adapted to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

Zygopetalum hasslerianum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
