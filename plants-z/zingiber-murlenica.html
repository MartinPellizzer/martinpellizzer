Zingiber murlenica

Zingiber murlenica, commonly known as the Murlen ginger, is a rare and lesser-known member of the Zingiberaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, particularly in countries like Myanmar and Thailand. The plant features rhizomatous growth and produces clusters of fragrant, pale yellow flowers. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a slightly serrated edge.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable species for botanical research and conservation efforts.
