Zingiber marginatum

Zingiber marginatum, commonly known as the purple-edge ginger, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers such as turmeric and cardamom. The plant features tall, narrow leaves with a distinctive purple margin, and it produces fragrant, yellowish flowers arranged in spike-like clusters. Zingiber marginatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a subtle, spicy aroma.

It thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
