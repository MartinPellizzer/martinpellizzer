Zenkerophytum cordifolium

Zenkerophytum cordifolium, commonly known as the heart-leaved orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers that are often violet or white in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts worldwide.
