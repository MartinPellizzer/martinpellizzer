Zygopetalum jugosum

Zygopetalum jugosum, commonly known as the red-tipped orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its striking, velvety leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces clusters of pink to red flowers with a distinctive, spurred lip that is a key feature of the Zygopetalum genus. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
