Zephyranthes schulziana

Zephyranthes schulziana, commonly known as the Schultze's rain lily, is a striking bulbous plant native to the southeastern United States and parts of Mexico.

It produces delicate, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in response to rainfall, typically during the summer months. The plant features strap-like, grassy foliage that emerges from a basal rosette of leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Zephyranthes schulziana is also valued for its ability to naturalize and spread easily in suitable conditions.
