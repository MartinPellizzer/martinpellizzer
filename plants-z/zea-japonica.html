Zea japonica

Zea japonica, commonly known as Japanese corn, is a species of grass native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and tassel-like inflorescence. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
