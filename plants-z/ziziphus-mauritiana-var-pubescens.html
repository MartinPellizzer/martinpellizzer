Ziziphus mauritiana var. pubescens

Ziziphus mauritiana var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy date tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Rhamnaceae family and is characterized by its pubescent (hairy) stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by edible, sweet, and succulent fruits resembling dates. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The hairy date tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and its fruit, which is used in traditional cuisines and medicinal practices.
