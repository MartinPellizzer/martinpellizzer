Ziziphora taurica subsp. cleonioides

Ziziphora taurica subsp.

cleonioides, commonly known as Turkish thyme or cleonioid thyme, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in steppe and woodland habitats. Its small, fragrant flowers are usually pink or purple and bloom in summer.

The subspecies is distinguished by its shorter stature and more compact growth compared to the typical form of Ziziphora taurica.
