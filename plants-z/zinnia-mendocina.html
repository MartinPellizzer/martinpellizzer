Zinnia mendocina

Zinnia mendocina, commonly known as the Mendoza zinnia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that come in a range of colors, including red, yellow, and orange. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with narrow, hairy leaves. Its flowers are tubular at the base and expand into a radiate shape, making them attractive to pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Zinnia mendocina is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
