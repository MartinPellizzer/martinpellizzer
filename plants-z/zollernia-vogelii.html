Zollernia vogelii

Zollernia vogelii, commonly known as the Vogel's zollernia, is a member of the Apocynaceae family, characterized by its showy, clustered flowers.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is often found in moist, shaded environments. The plant features opposite, simple leaves that are usually ovate to lanceolate in shape. Its flowers are typically pink to purple, with five petals and a prominent corona, attracting various pollinators.

Zollernia vogelii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
