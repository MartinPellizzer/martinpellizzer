Zanthoxylum flavum

Zanthoxylum flavum, commonly known as the yellow prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces bright red berries that are an important food source for birds and small mammals. Its bark and leaves contain compounds that have been used in traditional medicine.

Zanthoxylum flavum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
