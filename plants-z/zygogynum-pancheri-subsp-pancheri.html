Zygogynum pancheri subsp. pancheri

Zygogynum pancheri subsp.

pancheri, commonly known as the Ethiopian milkweed, is a perennial shrub native to the highlands of Ethiopia. It is characterized by its glandular-stemmed structure and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It is known for its milky sap, which contains toxic compounds and is used in traditional medicine.

This subspecies is of interest for its ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
