Zanthoxylum astrigerum

Zanthoxylum astrigerum, commonly known as the Chinese prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated margins and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Zanthoxylum astrigerum is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
