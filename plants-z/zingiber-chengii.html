Zingiber chengii

Zingiber chengii, commonly known as the Cheng's ginger, is a rare and lesser-known species of the ginger family (Zingiberaceae).

Native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, particularly China, it is characterized by its ornamental leaves and unique floral structure. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, which bloom during the warmer months. Its rhizomes, though not as widely cultivated as other ginger varieties, have been used in traditional medicine for their potential therapeutic properties.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Zingiber chengii is considered a valuable subject for botanical research and conservation efforts.
