Zingiber zerumbet subsp. cochinchinense

Zingiber zerumbet subsp.

cochinchinense, commonly known as the flame bamboo or red ginger, is a rhizomatous plant native to Southeast Asia. It features tall, slender, reddish-purple inflorescences that resemble bamboo, with showy, brightly colored bracts.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are often clustered along the inflorescence. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This ornamental plant is valued for its striking appearance and is often cultivated in gardens and as a potted plant.
