Zygopetalum lawrenceanum

Zygopetalum lawrenceanum, commonly known as the Lawrenceanum zygopetalum, is a species of orchid native to the humid forests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white, with a unique lip that is often more elaborate than the other petals. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, meaning it forms new shoots from the base of the old ones, leading to a bushy appearance. Its leaves are elongated, fleshy, and arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem.

This orchid thrives in shaded, moist environments and requires high humidity and good air circulation for optimal growth.
