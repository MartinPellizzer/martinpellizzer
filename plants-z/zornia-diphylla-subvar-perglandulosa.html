Zornia diphylla subvar. perglandulosa

Zornia diphylla subvar. perglandulosa, commonly known as the glandular zornia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing annual herb with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often glandular-dotted. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its foliage and floral structure are adapted to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments.

It is often found in disturbed soils and is a common weed in agricultural areas.
