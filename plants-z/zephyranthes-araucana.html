Zephyranthes araucana

Zephyranthes araucana, commonly known as the rain lily, is a species of flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Central America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, white or pale pink flowers in response to rainfall. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves that are typically narrow and pointed. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Zephyranthes araucana is known for its rapid blooming cycle, often flowering within weeks of receiving adequate water.
