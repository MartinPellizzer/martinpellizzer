Zingiber fraseri var. fraseri

Zingiber fraseri var. fraseri, commonly known as the Fraser's ginger, is a rhizomatous herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy, with a smooth texture. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, arranged in a spike-like inflorescence. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary spice.

Zingiber fraseri var. fraseri thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
