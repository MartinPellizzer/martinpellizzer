Zingiber gigantifolium

Zingiber gigantifolium, commonly known as the giant ginger, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its massive, heart-shaped leaves that can reach lengths of up to 1 meter, making it a visually impressive specimen. The plant produces fragrant, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in a spiral pattern along the stem. Its rhizomes, similar to ginger, are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Due to its size and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
