Zannichellia graminifolia

Zannichellia graminifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water plantain, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds, ditches, and streams. The plant has slender, ribbon-like leaves that are often submerged or floating at the water's surface. Its small, greenish-white flowers appear in clusters and are pollinated by insects.

Zannichellia graminifolia plays a role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
