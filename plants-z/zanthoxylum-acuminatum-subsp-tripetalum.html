Zanthoxylum acuminatum subsp. tripetalum

Zanthoxylum acuminatum subsp.

tripetalum, commonly known as the three-petalled prickly ash, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically three- to five-lobed, and its small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters.

The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are a food source for various birds and small mammals. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in swampy areas or along stream banks.

The species is notable for its spiny stems and aromatic leaves, which contain compounds used in traditional medicine.
