Zingiber puberulum

Zingiber puberulum, commonly known as the hairy ginger, is a member of the Zingiberaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect, herbaceous stem and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, short hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that are borne on inflorescences. Zingiber puberulum is native to Southeast Asia and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine and have a mild ginger-like flavor.
