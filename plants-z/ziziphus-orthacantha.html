Ziziphus orthacantha

Ziziphus orthacantha, commonly known as the Indian date tree or wild date tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to southern and eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny branches, simple ovate leaves, and small yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in arid and semi-arid climates and is well adapted to poor soils, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.
