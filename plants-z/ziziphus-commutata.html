Ziziphus commutata

Ziziphus commutata, commonly known as the desert date or red date tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, small greenish-white flowers, and small, edible fruits that range in color from yellow to red. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to drought conditions, making it a resilient species in harsh environments. Its fruit is often used in traditional medicine and as a food source by both humans and wildlife.

The plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various desert animals.
