Zingiber emeiense

Zingiber emeiense, commonly known as Emei ginger, is a rare and ornamental plant native to China.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers and turmeric. The plant features rhizomatous growth and produces clusters of fragrant, pale yellow to white flowers. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and have a smooth, waxy texture.

Zingiber emeiense is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a decorative plant.
