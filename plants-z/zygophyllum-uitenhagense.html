Zygophyllum uitenhagense

Zygophyllum uitenhagense, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry, is a succulent shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, orange-red berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and high temperatures.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of desert flora.
