Zanthoxylum digynum

Zanthoxylum digynum, commonly known as the two-seeded prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and have serrated margins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. Its bark and berries contain aromatic compounds, making it useful in traditional medicine.

Zanthoxylum digynum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream valleys.
