Ziziphora magakjani

Ziziphora magakjani, commonly known as the Magakjani savory, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or blue in color, arranged in dense spikes. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a strongly aromatic scent when crushed.

Ziziphora magakjani is often used in traditional medicine for its purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.
