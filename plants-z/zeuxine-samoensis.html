Zeuxine samoensis

Zeuxine samoensis, commonly known as the Samoan zeuxine, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands, particularly Samoa.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant has herbaceous stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
