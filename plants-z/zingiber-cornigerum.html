Zingiber cornigerum

Zingiber cornigerum, commonly known as the red ginger, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers such as turmeric and cardamom. The plant is characterized by its striking, bright red bracts that surround the small, pale yellow flowers. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Zingiber cornigerum thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant appearance.
