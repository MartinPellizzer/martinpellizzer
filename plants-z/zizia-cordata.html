Zizia cordata

Zizia cordata, commonly known as narrowleaf biscuitroot, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped cluster, which bloom in early summer. Zizia cordata thrives in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky slopes.

Its medicinal properties have been historically used by Indigenous peoples for various health purposes.
