Zapoteca formosa subsp. formosa

Zapoteca formosa subsp.

formosa, commonly known as the Zapotec beauty, is a shrub native to southern Mexico. It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers.

The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or orange when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
