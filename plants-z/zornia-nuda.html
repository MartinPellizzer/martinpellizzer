Zornia nuda

Zornia nuda, commonly known as the naked zornia, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides. Its seed pods are slender and dehiscent, releasing tiny seeds when mature.

Zornia nuda is an important plant for soil stabilization and provides forage for wildlife.
