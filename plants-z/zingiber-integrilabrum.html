Zingiber integrilabrum

Zingiber integrilabrum, commonly known as the smooth-edged ginger, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other economically important plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant features a rhizomatous root system and has narrow, linear leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern. Its inflorescence consists of brightly colored, showy flowers that range from yellow to red, making it a striking ornamental plant.

Zingiber integrilabrum is valued not only for its aesthetic appeal but also for its potential medicinal properties.
