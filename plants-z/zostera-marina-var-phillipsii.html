Zostera marina var. phillipsii

Zostera marina var. phillipsii, commonly known as the Phillips' eelgrass, is a variant of the seagrass species Zostera marina.

It is characterized by its narrow, ribbon-like leaves and is typically found in coastal marine environments. This plant thrives in shallow, sandy or muddy substrates and plays a vital role in stabilizing sediments and supporting marine biodiversity. It is also an important habitat for various aquatic organisms, including juvenile fish and invertebrates.

Its presence is often indicative of healthy, well-oxygenated waters.
