Zanthoxylum caribaeum subsp. rugosum

Zanthoxylum caribaeum subsp.

rugosum, commonly known as the roughleaf prickly ash, is a shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America. It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated margins and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters.

The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical woodlands.

The bark and leaves contain bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional medicine and botanical research.
