Zea mays var. graeca

Zea mays var. graeca, commonly known as the Greek corn or Greek maize, is a variant of maize native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its smaller, more compact ears compared to typical corn varieties, with kernels that are often more colorful and diverse in hue. The plant typically grows to a height of about 2 to 3 meters, featuring broad, narrow leaves and a sturdy stalk. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and requires moderate amounts of water.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in traditional agriculture.
