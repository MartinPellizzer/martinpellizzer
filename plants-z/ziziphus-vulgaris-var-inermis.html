Ziziphus vulgaris var. inermis

Ziziphus vulgaris var. inermis, commonly known as the smooth ziziphus, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually oval to elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, red or orange fruits. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
