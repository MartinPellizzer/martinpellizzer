Zingiber pendulum

Zingiber pendulum, commonly known as the hanging ginger, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, drooping stems that bear clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. The plant produces aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Zingiber pendulum is part of the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other economically important plants like ginger and turmeric.
