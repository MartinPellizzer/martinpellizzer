Zanthoxylum kwangsiense

Zanthoxylum kwangsiense, commonly known as the Guangxi prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern China.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are often used in traditional Chinese medicine. Its bark and roots contain various bioactive compounds, making it valuable for medicinal purposes.
