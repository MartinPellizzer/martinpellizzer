Zygopetalum bidentatum

Zygopetalum bidentatum, commonly known as the two-toothed zygopetalum, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces pendulous, violet to purple flowers with a distinctive two-toothed lip. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial shade in its natural habitat.

Zygopetalum bidentatum is a popular choice for gardeners due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
