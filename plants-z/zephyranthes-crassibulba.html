Zephyranthes crassibulba

Zephyranthes crassibulba, commonly known as the broadbulb rain lily, is a species of flowering plant native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a bulbous perennial that produces showy, white or pale pink flowers during the rainy season. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and a compact growth habit, making it well-suited for rock gardens or containers. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate to bright light.

Zephyranthes crassibulba is known for its ability to bloom reliably in tropical and subtropical climates.
