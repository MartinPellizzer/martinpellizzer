Zapoteca formosa

Zapoteca formosa, commonly known as the Zapotec beauty, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of Oaxaca, Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking purple and white flowers, which bloom in late spring. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
