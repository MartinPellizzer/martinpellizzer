Zingiber griffithii var. citrinum

Zingiber griffithii var. citrinum, commonly known as the yellow ginger, is a rare and ornamental plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other well-known gingers such as turmeric and cardamom. The plant is characterized by its striking, lemon-yellow inflorescences that emerge from a cluster of broad, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in warm, humid environments with well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its rarity and aesthetic appeal, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by horticultural enthusiasts.
