Zornia megistocarpa

Zornia megistocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited zornia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces large, woody pods that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and native plant gardens.
