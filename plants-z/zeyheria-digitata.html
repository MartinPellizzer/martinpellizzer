Zeyheria digitata

Zeyheria digitata, commonly known as the rock fig, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, clustered leaves that are often lobed or toothed. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, fig-like fruits. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.

Zeyheria digitata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
