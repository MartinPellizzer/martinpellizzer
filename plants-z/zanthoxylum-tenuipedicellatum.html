Zanthoxylum tenuipedicellatum

Zanthoxylum tenuipedicellatum, commonly known as the narrow-fruited prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces berries that are red when mature, containing several seeds surrounded by a fleshy pulp. Its bark and leaves contain compounds with potential medicinal properties, making it of interest in herbal medicine.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.
