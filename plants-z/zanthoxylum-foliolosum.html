Zanthoxylum foliolosum

Zanthoxylum foliolosum, commonly known as the leafy prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are made up of several narrow, pointed leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or swampy areas.

The species is notable for its spiny branches and its role in supporting local wildlife.
