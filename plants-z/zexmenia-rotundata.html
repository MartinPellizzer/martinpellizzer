Zexmenia rotundata

Zexmenia rotundata, commonly known as the roundleaf zexmenia, is a unique plant species characterized by its rounded, waxy leaves that are typically a deep green color.

It is native to the arid regions of southern Africa, where it has adapted to thrive in dry, hot climates. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, which bloom during the cooler months. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access water from deeper underground sources.

Zexmenia rotundata is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
