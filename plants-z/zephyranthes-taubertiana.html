Zephyranthes taubertiana

Zephyranthes taubertiana, commonly known as the rain lily, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a bulbous perennial that produces showy, white or pale pink flowers during the rainy season. The plant has strap-shaped, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate to bright light.

Zephyranthes taubertiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to bloom in response to rainfall.
