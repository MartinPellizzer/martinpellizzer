Zexmenia aurantiaca

Zexmenia aurantiaca, commonly known as the golden tangerine orchid, is a rare flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking orange-red petals and a unique, fragrant scent that attracts specific pollinators. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil. Its leaves are broad and glossy, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
