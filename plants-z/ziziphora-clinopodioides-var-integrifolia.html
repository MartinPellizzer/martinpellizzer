Ziziphora clinopodioides var. integrifolia

Ziziphora clinopodioides var. integrifolia, commonly known as sweet woodruff, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features aromatic, opposite leaves that are typically heart-shaped or ovate with smooth margins. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense clusters during the summer months. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported calming and digestive properties.

The species is valued in horticulture for its fragrant foliage and ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments.
