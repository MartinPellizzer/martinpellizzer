Ziziphus

Ziziphus, commonly known as the Chinese date or Indian date, is a genus of about 200 species of shrubs and small trees native to Africa, Asia, and Australia.

These plants are characterized by their simple, alternate leaves and small, often yellow or red berries that serve as a food source for various animals. Many species of Ziziphus are drought-tolerant and thrive in arid or semi-arid environments, making them important in ecological and agricultural contexts. The genus includes both edible and ornamental species, with some being cultivated for their fruit or medicinal properties.

Ziziphus species are also valued for their role in traditional medicine and their ability to stabilize soil in degraded landscapes.
