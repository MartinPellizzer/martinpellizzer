Zanthoxylum obtriangulare

Zanthoxylum obtriangulare, commonly known as the three-angled prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically three-lobed and have a distinctive triangular shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. Its bark is rough and often fissured, and it contains a variety of bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional medicine.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in horticulture.
