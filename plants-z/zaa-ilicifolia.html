Zaa ilicifolia

Zaa ilicifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf hakea, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in dense, fine hairs. The plant produces small, urn-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Zaa ilicifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
