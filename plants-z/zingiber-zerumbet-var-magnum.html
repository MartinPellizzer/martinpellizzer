Zingiber zerumbet var. magnum

Zingiber zerumbet var. magnum, commonly known as the giant flame bamboo, is a striking ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 6 meters, forming dense clumps of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of vibrant orange-red inflorescences, which are highly ornamental and attract pollinators. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

This variety is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and as a decorative plant in indoor settings.
