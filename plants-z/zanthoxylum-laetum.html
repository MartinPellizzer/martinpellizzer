Zanthoxylum laetum

Zanthoxylum laetum, commonly known as the sweet pepper tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are opposite and pinnate, with serrated margins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by bright red, fleshy berries that are edible. Its fruits are often used in culinary applications, adding a spicy and tangy flavor.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
