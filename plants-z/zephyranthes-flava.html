Zephyranthes flava

Zephyranthes flava, commonly known as the yellow rain lily, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States and parts of Central America.

It is a bulbous perennial that produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils. It is often found in sandy or coastal areas, thriving in full sun.

Zephyranthes flava is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in gardens.
