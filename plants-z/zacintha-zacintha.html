Zacintha zacintha

Zacintha zacintha, commonly known as the Christmas rose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It is characterized by its showy, white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves appear. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in a fine layer of hairs. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial to full shade.

Zacintha zacintha is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its early blooming habit.
