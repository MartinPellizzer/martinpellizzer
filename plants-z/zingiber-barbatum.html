Zingiber barbatum

Zingiber barbatum, commonly known as the hairy ginger, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other economically important spices like ginger and turmeric. The plant is characterized by its ornamental leaves, which are broad, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color.

Zingiber barbatum is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal rather than its culinary uses.
