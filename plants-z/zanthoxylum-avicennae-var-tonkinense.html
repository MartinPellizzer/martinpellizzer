Zanthoxylum avicennae var. tonkinense

Zanthoxylum avicennae var. tonkinense, commonly known as the Tonkin prickly ash, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnate with 5 to 9 pairs of leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for some wildlife. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
