Zingiber vittacheilum

Zingiber vittacheilum, commonly known as the golden ginger, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other economically important plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant is characterized by its tall, erect stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with yellow or golden hues. Its flowers are typically orange or red, arranged in spike-like inflorescences, and are accompanied by aromatic, fragrant leaves.

Zingiber vittacheilum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking foliage and unique floral displays.
