Zannichellia palustris subsp. pedicellata

Zannichellia palustris subsp.

pedicellata, commonly known as the water pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds, lakes, and wetlands.

The plant has long, slender stems that are often branched and bear small, oval-shaped leaves arranged in whorls. Its flowers are tiny and clustered in spike-like inflorescences, which are usually submerged or just at the water's surface.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
