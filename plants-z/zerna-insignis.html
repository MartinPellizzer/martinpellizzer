Zerna insignis

Zerna insignis, commonly known as the golden zerna, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers and compact, bushy growth habit. The plant produces small, oval seeds enclosed in a fibrous capsule, which are dispersed by wind. Zerna insignis is adapted to thrive in hot, dry environments with minimal water availability.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.
