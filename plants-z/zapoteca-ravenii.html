Zapoteca ravenii

Zapoteca ravenii, commonly known as the Mexican fan palm, is a striking plant native to the southern United States and Central America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 30 feet, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping. Its leaves are typically a deep green, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by fruit that is edible but not widely consumed.

Zapoteca ravenii is well adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun for optimal growth.
