Zanthoxylum ovalifolium

Zanthoxylum ovalifolium, commonly known as the oval-leaf prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are oval in shape and have a serrated margin. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
