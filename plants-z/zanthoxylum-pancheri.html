Zanthoxylum pancheri

Zanthoxylum pancheri, commonly known as the Kashmir chili or Himalayan prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and small, bright red berries that are edible. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by the distinctive berries. Zanthoxylum pancheri thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

It has been used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
