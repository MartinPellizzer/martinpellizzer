Zinnia grandiflora

Zinnia grandiflora, commonly known as the Mexican zinnia, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, yellow, orange, and white. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and has narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are borne in clusters at the ends of the stems, and the plant is known for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in full sun.

Zinnia grandiflora is a popular choice for gardens due to its long blooming period and ease of cultivation.
