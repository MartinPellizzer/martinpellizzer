Zizania mezii

Zizania mezii, commonly known as the Mexican wild rice, is a tall, grass-like aquatic plant native to the wetlands of Mexico and parts of the southern United States.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater environments, such as marshes and along the edges of lakes and rivers. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, rice-like grains. Its roots help stabilize soil and improve water quality by filtering sediments and nutrients.

Zizania mezii is also valued for its potential as a sustainable food source and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
