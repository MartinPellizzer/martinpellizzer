Zygophyllum gaetulum subsp. gaetulum

Zygophyllum gaetulum subsp.

gaetulum, commonly known as the desert morning glory, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its small, palmate leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low moisture conditions. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. Zygophyllum gaetulum subsp.

gaetulum plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
