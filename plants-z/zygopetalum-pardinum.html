Zygopetalum pardinum

Zygopetalum pardinum, commonly known as the Pardinum zygopetalum, is a rare and striking orchid native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its intricate, fragrant flowers that typically feature a unique combination of colors, including shades of purple, pink, and white. The plant thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its leaves are broad and glossy, often forming a rosette pattern that helps it conserve moisture.

Due to its limited natural range and specific habitat requirements, Zygopetalum pardinum is considered a conservation concern.
