Zoysia griffithiana

Zoysia griffithiana, commonly known as the Griffith's zoysia, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and fine, bluish-green foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white to pale yellow flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate climates with moderate rainfall.

Zoysia griffithiana is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
