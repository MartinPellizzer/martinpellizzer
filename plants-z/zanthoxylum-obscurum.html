Zanthoxylum obscurum

Zanthoxylum obscurum, commonly known as the dark prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are edible for some wildlife but toxic to humans.

Its bark and leaves contain compounds that have been used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
