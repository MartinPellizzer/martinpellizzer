Zigadenus elegans var. glaucus

Zigadenus elegans var. glaucus, commonly known as the blue-flowered death camas, is a toxic plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, green berries that are highly poisonous. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or open areas.

Due to its toxicity, it should be handled with care and is often found in conservation areas.
