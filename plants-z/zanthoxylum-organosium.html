Zanthoxylum organosium

Zanthoxylum organosium, commonly known as the Mexican prickly ash, is a small evergreen shrub native to Central America.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
