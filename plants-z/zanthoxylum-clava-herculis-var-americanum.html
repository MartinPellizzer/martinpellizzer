Zanthoxylum clava-herculis var. americanum

Zanthoxylum clava-herculis var. americanum, commonly known as the prickly ash or American prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often divided into narrow, pointed segments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, berry-like fruits in the fall. Its bark and leaves contain compounds such as alkaloids and flavonoids, which have been studied for their potential medicinal properties.

The plant is also noted for its spiny branches and its role in supporting local wildlife, particularly birds.
