Zygosepalum rostratum

Zygosepalum rostratum, commonly known as the rostrate zygosepalum, is a species of flowering plant in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its unique floral structure. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in a spike-like inflorescence. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the humid environment in which it grows.

Zygosepalum rostratum is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its delicate appearance and interesting morphology.
