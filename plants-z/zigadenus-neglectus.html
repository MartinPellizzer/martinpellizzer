Zigadenus neglectus

Zigadenus neglectus, commonly known as the western poison root, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. It is notable for containing toxic alkaloids, which can be harmful if ingested.

Despite its toxicity, it plays a role in local ecosystems and is of interest to botanists and ecologists.
