Zerna purgans

Zerna purgans, commonly known as the castor bean plant, is a tropical shrub native to India.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces large, smooth, green seeds encased in a hard, spiky husk. These seeds contain a toxic substance called ricin, which makes the plant potentially dangerous if handled improperly.

Despite its toxicity, it is cultivated for its oil, which is extracted from the seeds.
