Zeuxine occidentalis

Zeuxine occidentalis, commonly known as the western zigzag gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forest floors and rocky stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture, while its stems are often branched and ascending.

Zeuxine occidentalis is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
