Zingiber microcheilum

Zingiber microcheilum, commonly known as the narrow-lipped ginger, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other well-known gingers such as turmeric and cardamom. The plant produces small, clustered flowers with pale yellow petals and a distinctive, aromatic scent. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The botanical features of Zingiber microcheilum make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and pharmacological research.
