Zanthoxylum mazatlanum

Zanthoxylum mazatlanum, commonly known as Mexican prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. Its bark and leaves contain bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.
