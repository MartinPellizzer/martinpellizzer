Ziziphora pamiroalaica

Ziziphora pamiroalaica, commonly known as the Pamir ziziphora, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant features slender, upright stems and small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically blue or violet, arranged in dense clusters, and bloom during the summer months.

Ziziphora pamiroalaica is adapted to cold, arid environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.
