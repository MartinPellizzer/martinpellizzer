Zygogynum spatulatum

Zygogynum spatulatum, commonly known as the African buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features showy, yellow flowers with a distinctive cup-like structure, and its leaves are palmately lobed with a waxy coating. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in marshy or swampy areas. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique addition to wetland gardens and ecological restoration projects.
