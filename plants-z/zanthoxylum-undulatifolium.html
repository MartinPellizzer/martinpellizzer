Zanthoxylum undulatifolium

Zanthoxylum undulatifolium, commonly known as the wavy-leaf prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with wavy margins and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are edible for some wildlife but contain irritants for humans.

Its bark and leaves contain compounds used in traditional medicine for their analgesic and anti-inflammatory properties.
