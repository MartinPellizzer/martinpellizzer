Zanthoxylum aromaticum

Zanthoxylum aromaticum, commonly known as the Chinese prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves and yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. Its bark and leaves contain various bioactive compounds, making it valuable in both culinary and pharmacological contexts.

Zanthoxylum aromaticum thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.
