Zanthoxylum heterophyllum

Zanthoxylum heterophyllum, commonly known as the variable prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often alternate and have serrated margins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. Its bark and leaves contain bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional medicine.

The species exhibits variability in leaf shape and size, contributing to its common name.
