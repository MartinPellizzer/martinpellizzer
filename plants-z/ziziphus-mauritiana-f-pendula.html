Ziziphus mauritiana f. pendula

Ziziphus mauritiana f. pendula, commonly known as the weeping Indian date tree, is a distinctive cultivar of the Indian date tree characterized by its cascading, drooping branches.

This form exhibits a unique growth habit with long, pendant shoots that create a weeping appearance, making it visually striking. Botanically, it belongs to the Rhamnaceae family and is native to regions of India and Southeast Asia. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, sweet fruits similar to those of the common Ziziphus mauritiana.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping and gardens.
