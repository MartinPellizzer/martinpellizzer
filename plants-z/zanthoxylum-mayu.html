Zanthoxylum mayu

Zanthoxylum mayu, commonly known as mayu prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. Zanthoxylum mayu is adapted to thrive in dry, rocky soils and is often found in scrublands and woodlands.

Its bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
