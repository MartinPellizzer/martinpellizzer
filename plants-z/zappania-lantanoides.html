Zappania lantanoides

Zappania lantanoides, commonly known as the silverleaf zappania, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, pubescent hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal, it is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
