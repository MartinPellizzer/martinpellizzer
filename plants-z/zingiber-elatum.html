Zingiber elatum

Zingiber elatum, commonly known as the white ginger lily, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces a strong, spicy fragrance, which is often used in perfumes and aromatherapy. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, providing a striking visual contrast to the delicate flowers.

Zingiber elatum is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and as a houseplant.
