Zygotritonia crocea

Zygotritonia crocea, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas. The plant has a compact growth habit with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It blooms during the warmer months, producing clusters of small, showy flowers.

Zygotritonia crocea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
