Zucca

Zucca, commonly known as pumpkin, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other gourds and squash.

It is characterized by its large, round or oval shape, thick, ribbed skin, and vibrant orange to yellow flesh. The plant is typically cultivated for its edible fruits, which are rich in vitamins A and C. Zucca varieties differ in size, color, and texture, with some being more suited for baking and others for raw consumption.

The plant thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil and ample sunlight for optimal growth.
