Zoegea crinita subsp. glabricaulis

Zoegea crinita subsp.

glabricaulis, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed zoegea, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa. It features wiry, ascending stems that are typically glabrous, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in terminal clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its unique botanical profile.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
