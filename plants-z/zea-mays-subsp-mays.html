Zea mays subsp. mays

Zea mays subsp.

mays, commonly known as corn, is a tall annual grass that typically grows between 2 to 6 meters in height. It has long, narrow leaves and produces tassels at the top of the stalk, which are the male flowers, while the female flowers develop at the base of the ears.

The plant's inflorescence consists of a cob surrounded by rows of kernels, which are the edible part of the plant. Zea mays subsp. mays is a C4 plant, meaning it efficiently performs photosynthesis in hot and dry conditions.

It is a staple crop worldwide, valued for its nutritional content and versatility in food and industrial applications.
