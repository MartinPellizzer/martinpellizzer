Zanthoxylum campicola

Zanthoxylum campicola, commonly known as the mountain prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Zanthoxylum campicola thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and rocky slopes.
