Zanthoxylum riedelianum

Zanthoxylum riedelianum, commonly known as the black pepper tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnate with serrated margins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by bright red, fleshy fruits resembling berries. Its bark and leaves contain various bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional medicine.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its aromatic properties.
