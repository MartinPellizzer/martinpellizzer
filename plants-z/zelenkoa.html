Zelenkoa

Zelenkoa, commonly known as the "green moss," is a type of non-vascular plant that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It belongs to the family Hypnaceae and is characterized by its dense, carpet-like growth pattern. The plant lacks true roots, stems, and leaves, instead consisting of specialized structures called rhizoids and leaf-like structures called phyllidia. Zelenkoa plays an important role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for microorganisms and small invertebrates.

Its simple structure and ability to absorb water make it a common component of forest floors in temperate regions.
