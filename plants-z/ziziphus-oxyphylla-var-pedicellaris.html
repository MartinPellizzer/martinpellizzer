Ziziphus oxyphylla var. pedicellaris

Ziziphus oxyphylla var. pedicellaris, commonly known as the Desert Date Tree, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It features small, yellowish-green leaves that are opposite or alternate in arrangement, and it produces small, white or greenish flowers that are followed by edible, fleshy fruits. The plant is adapted to harsh environments, with deep roots and a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

Its fruit is often consumed by both humans and animals, and it plays a role in local ecosystems and traditional medicine.
