Zeuxine tonkinensis

Zeuxine tonkinensis, commonly known as the Vietnamese bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three deeply lobed leaflets, and it produces small, clustered flowers in early spring.

Zeuxine tonkinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
