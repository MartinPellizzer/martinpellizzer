Zygopetalum burkei

Zygopetalum burkei, commonly known as the Burke's zygopetalum, is a rare orchid species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that display a complex pattern of colors, including shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are narrow and elongated, adapted to capture light in the shaded forest understory.

Zygopetalum burkei is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts due to its unique beauty.
