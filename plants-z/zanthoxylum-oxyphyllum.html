Zanthoxylum oxyphyllum

Zanthoxylum oxyphyllum, commonly known as the red sandalwood or Indian prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often serrated and have a distinctive reddish color. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, woody fruits containing several seeds. Zanthoxylum oxyphyllum is typically found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or streamside habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used historically for medicinal purposes.
