Zamia tenuis

Zamia tenuis, commonly known as the slender cycad, is a member of the Zamiaceae family and is native to Central and South America.

It is a slow-growing plant with a stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of feathery, pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
