Zea mays var. glaucornis

Zea mays var. glaucornis, commonly known as the silver leaf corn, is a variant of maize characterized by its distinctive bluish-green foliage.

This variety is notable for its striking coloration, which results from a unique pigmentation in the leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 3 meters, with tall, sturdy stalks and large, broad leaves. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Botanically, it is classified within the grass family Poaceae and is cultivated for both ornamental and agricultural purposes.
