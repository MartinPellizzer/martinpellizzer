Zeuxine pumila

Zeuxine pumila, commonly known as the dwarf zeuxine, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically reaches a height of less than 10 centimeters, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-yellow, with five sepals and no true petals.

Zeuxine pumila thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.
