Zingiber cochleariforme

Zingiber cochleariforme, commonly known as the spiral ginger, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers such as turmeric and cardamom. The plant is characterized by its spiral arrangement of leaves, which gives it its common name. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange and are borne on tall, slender stalks.

Zingiber cochleariforme is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
