Zingeria densior

Zingeria densior, commonly known as the dense thorned acacia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and small, compound leaves that reduce water loss. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access groundwater in dry conditions.

Zingeria densior plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
