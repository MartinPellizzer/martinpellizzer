Zosima frigida

Zosima frigida, commonly known as the cold-weather fern, is a hardy plant native to high-altitude regions with cold climates.

It features delicate, feather-like fronds that are typically dark green and slightly curled. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth. Zosima frigida is known for its ability to withstand frost and snow, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.

Its botanical structure includes a rhizomatous root system that helps anchor it in rocky or sandy substrates.
