Zappania globiflora

Zappania globiflora, commonly known as the globeflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, globe-shaped inflorescence composed of numerous small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Zappania globiflora is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
