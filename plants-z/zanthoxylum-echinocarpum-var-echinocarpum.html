Zanthoxylum echinocarpum var. echinocarpum

Zanthoxylum echinocarpum var. echinocarpum, commonly known as the spiny chinaberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The fruit is a spiny, orange-red berry that ripens in late summer, containing several seeds. The plant is adapted to well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for ornamental and ecological landscaping.

Its rapid growth and ability to tolerate various environmental conditions contribute to its widespread use in horticulture.
