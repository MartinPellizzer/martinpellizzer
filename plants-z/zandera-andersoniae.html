Zandera andersoniae

Zandera andersoniae, commonly known as the Anderson's zandera, is a rare and endangered tree species native to the high-altitude cloud forests of central Chile.

It belongs to the Ochnaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and lance-shaped leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Due to habitat loss and limited natural regeneration, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
