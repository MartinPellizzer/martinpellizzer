Zanthoxylum piperitum f. ovalifoliolatum

Zanthoxylum piperitum f. ovalifoliolatum, commonly known as the Japanese prickly ash, is a variant of the Chinese prickly ash.

It is characterized by its oval-shaped leaves and distinctive, spiny fruits. The plant is native to East Asia and thrives in temperate climates. Its botanical features include compound leaves with serrated margins and small, red berries that mature in the fall.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
