Zingiber sichuanense

Zingiber sichuanense, commonly known as Sichuan ginger, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Sichuan, China.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other economically important plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant features a rhizomatous stem with fleshy, aromatic leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its flowering stems produce clusters of bright yellow flowers, which are not typically used in culinary applications.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes cultivated for its rhizomes.
