Zaluzianskya lanigera

Zaluzianskya lanigera, commonly known as the silverleaf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-green leaves and clusters of bell-shaped, pale blue to lavender flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats. Its flowering period typically occurs in late spring to early summer.

Zaluzianskya lanigera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and blooms.
