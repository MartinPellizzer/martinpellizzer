Zenobia speciosa

Zenobia speciosa, commonly known as the showy zenobia, is a striking flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from deep pink to purple, often with a distinctive yellow center. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, and features narrow, woody stems covered in dense, silvery foliage. Its blooming period occurs during the summer months, making it a notable ornamental plant in gardens.

Zenobia speciosa is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, thriving in well-drained soils.
