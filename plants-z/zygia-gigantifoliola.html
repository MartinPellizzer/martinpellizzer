Zygia gigantifoliola

Zygia gigantifoliola, commonly known as the giant-leaf zygia, is a striking plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
