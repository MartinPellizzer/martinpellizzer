Zygopetalum crinitum subsp. crinitum

Zygopetalum crinitum subsp.

crinitum, commonly known as the hairy zygopetalum, is a species of orchid native to South America. It is characterized by its long, hairy sepals and petals, which give it its common name.

The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, often with a yellow throat. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil.

This orchid is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
