Zornia guanipensis

Zornia guanipensis, commonly known as the Guanipensis burrweed, is a flowering plant native to South America, specifically found in regions of Colombia and Ecuador.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by legume pods that contain several seeds.

Zornia guanipensis is typically found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas, where it plays a minor role in local ecosystems.
