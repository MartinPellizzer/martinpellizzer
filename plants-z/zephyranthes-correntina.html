Zephyranthes correntina

Zephyranthes correntina, commonly known as the rain lily, is a herbaceous plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped white flowers that bloom in response to rainfall. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often found in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and forest edges.

Zephyranthes correntina is a short-lived perennial that spreads through rhizomes, making it a resilient and hardy species.
