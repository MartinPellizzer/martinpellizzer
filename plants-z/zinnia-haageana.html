Zinnia haageana

Zinnia haageana, commonly known as the narrowleaf zinnia, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant, daisy-like flowers that range in color from yellow to red. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and produces numerous small, showy blooms throughout the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture, and it prefers well-drained soil and full sun.

Zinnia haageana is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and is also used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.
