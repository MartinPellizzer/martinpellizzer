Zephyranthes rubra

Zephyranthes rubra, commonly known as the red rain lily, is a species of flowering plant native to the southeastern United States and parts of Central America.

It is a bulbous plant that produces striking red flowers with a subtle fragrance, typically blooming in late summer or early autumn. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers. Zephyranthes rubra is known for its resilience, often blooming even in adverse conditions, and it is relatively easy to care for.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a low-maintenance yet visually appealing plant.
