Zygopetalum cerinum

Zygopetalum cerinum, commonly known as the cerinum zygopetalum, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are long, narrow, and arranged in a fan-like pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Zygopetalum cerinum thrives in humid, well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.
