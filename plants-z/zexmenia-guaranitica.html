Zexmenia guaranitica

Zexmenia guaranitica, commonly known as the Guarani orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its small, intricate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pale purple. The plant has a slender stem and produces narrow, elongated leaves that help it thrive in the humid, shaded environments of its habitat. It relies on specific symbiotic fungi for nutrient absorption, a common trait among orchids.

Due to habitat destruction, Zexmenia guaranitica is considered endangered and is the subject of conservation efforts.
