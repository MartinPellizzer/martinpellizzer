Ziziphus mucronata f. subcordata

Ziziphus mucronata f. subcordata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf date palm, is a variant of the date palm native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, subcordate leaves that are shorter and more compact compared to the typical form. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into edible dates, though the fruit is generally smaller and less sweet than the common date varieties. It thrives in arid and semi-arid regions, requiring minimal water and well-drained soil.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to adapt to harsh environmental conditions.
