Ziziphus andamanica

Ziziphus andamanica, commonly known as Andaman date tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Andaman and Nicobar Islands.

It belongs to the Rhamnaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible and have a sweet, date-like flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in coastal and wetland areas.

Due to its unique habitat and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
