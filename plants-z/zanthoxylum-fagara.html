Zanthoxylum fagara

Zanthoxylum fagara, commonly known as the prickly ash or wild prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are usually opposite and have a serrated margin. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by bright red berries that are edible for some bird species. Zanthoxylum fagara is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.

Its bark and leaves contain compounds that have been used for various therapeutic purposes.
