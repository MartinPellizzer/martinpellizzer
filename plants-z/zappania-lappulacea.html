Zappania lappulacea

Zappania lappulacea, commonly known as the rock cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or arctic regions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are usually basal, forming a rosette and are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Zappania lappulacea is adapted to cold climates and is often found in mountainous areas across the northern hemisphere.
