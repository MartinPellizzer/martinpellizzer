Zwackhia aurea

Zwackhia aurea, commonly known as the golden dwarf lily, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, golden-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 15 to 30 centimeters, making it a compact and ornamental choice for gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a vibrant touch to the landscape.
