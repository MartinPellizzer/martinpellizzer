Zornia latifolia

Zornia latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf zornia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It produces trifoliate leaves and is often found in woodland edges and along stream banks.

Zornia latifolia is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
