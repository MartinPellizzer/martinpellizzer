Zornia diphylla var. reticulata

Zornia diphylla var. reticulata, commonly known as the net-veined zornia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing herb with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink to reddish in color, which bloom in the spring. Its leaves have a distinctive net-like venation pattern, giving it its scientific name.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
