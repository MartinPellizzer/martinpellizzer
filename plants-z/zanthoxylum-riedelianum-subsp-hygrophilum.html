Zanthoxylum riedelianum subsp. hygrophilum

Zanthoxylum riedelianum subsp.

hygrophilum, commonly known as the water pepper or swamp prickly ash, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions. It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated margins and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters.

The plant produces bright red berries that are a key feature in its botanical description. It thrives in moist, often swampy habitats, which is reflected in its subspecies name "hygrophilum.".

The plant is also notable for its aromatic foliage and its use in traditional medicine.
