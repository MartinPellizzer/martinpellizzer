Zanthoxylum horsfieldii

Zanthoxylum horsfieldii, commonly known as the Horsfield's prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are usually pinnate with 5 to 9 leaflets, and its distinctive yellow or orange berries that are highly aromatic. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, typically in spring, followed by the development of its fleshy, red or orange fruits.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has also been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
