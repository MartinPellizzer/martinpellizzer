Zerna vulgaris

Zerna vulgaris, commonly known as common millet, is an annual grass species native to temperate regions.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped grains and is typically harvested when the seeds are dry and hard. The plant has a short stem and narrow leaves, and it thrives in well-drained soils. It is a drought-tolerant crop that is widely cultivated for its nutritional seeds.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Poaceae, which includes other cereal grasses.
