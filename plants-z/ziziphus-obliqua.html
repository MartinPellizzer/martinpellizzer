Ziziphus obliqua

Ziziphus obliqua, commonly known as the prickly date palm, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and pinnately compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, fleshy fruits that are typically red or orange when mature. Ziziphus obliqua thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species adapted to harsh conditions.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
