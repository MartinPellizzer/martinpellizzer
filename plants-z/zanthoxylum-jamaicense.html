Zanthoxylum jamaicense

Zanthoxylum jamaicense, commonly known as Jamaican prickly ash, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to the Caribbean, particularly Jamaica.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically red or orange when ripe, and it is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
