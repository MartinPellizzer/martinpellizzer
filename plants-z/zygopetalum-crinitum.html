Zygopetalum crinitum

Zygopetalum crinitum, commonly known as the curly whisk orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, curly, hair-like petals that give it its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in tropical rainforests. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish-white, with a distinctive spurred lip.

Zygopetalum crinitum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unusual appearance.
