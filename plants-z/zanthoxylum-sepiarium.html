Zanthoxylum sepiarium

Zanthoxylum sepiarium, commonly known as the ghost pepper tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces bright red, peppery fruits that are often used in traditional cuisines. Its bark and leaves contain various bioactive compounds, making it of interest in pharmacological research.

Zanthoxylum sepiarium thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.
