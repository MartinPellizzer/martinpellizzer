Zamia amblyphyllidia

Zamia amblyphyllidia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cycad, is a member of the Zamiaceae family and is native to the southern United States.

It is a dioecious plant, meaning that male and female reproductive structures are found on separate individuals. The plant produces cones rather than flowers, with the female cones developing into seed-bearing structures. Its leaves are typically narrow and strap-like, arranged in a rosette pattern around the stem.

Zamia amblyphyllidia is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.
