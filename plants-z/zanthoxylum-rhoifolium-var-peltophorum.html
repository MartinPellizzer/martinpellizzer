Zanthoxylum rhoifolium var. peltophorum

Zanthoxylum rhoifolium var. peltophorum, commonly known as the red sandalwood or Indian red sandalwood, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnate and have serrated margins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries containing several seeds. Its wood is highly valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine and as a source of dye.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.
