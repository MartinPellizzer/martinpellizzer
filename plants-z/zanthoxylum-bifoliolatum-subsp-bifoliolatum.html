Zanthoxylum bifoliolatum subsp. bifoliolatum

Zanthoxylum bifoliolatum subsp.

bifoliolatum, commonly known as the two-leafed prickly ash, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its compound leaves with two pairs of leaflets, giving it its specific epithet "bifoliolatum.".

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and streamside habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
