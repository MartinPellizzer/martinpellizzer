Zaluzania parthenioides

Zaluzania parthenioides, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the United States and Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of purple to pinkish-white flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in prairies, wetlands, and along stream banks.

Zaluzania parthenioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
