Zenkeria stapfii

Zenkeria stapfii, commonly known as the African cycad, is a unique plant belonging to the Cycadaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly found in regions like South Africa, Namibia, and Botswana. The plant features a stout, unbranched trunk with a fibrous, spongy bark that is characteristic of cycads. Its leaves are pinnate, with a long, narrow, and often spiky appearance, contributing to its distinctive look.

Zenkeria stapfii is known for its slow growth and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological significance.
