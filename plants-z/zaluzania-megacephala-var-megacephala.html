Zaluzania megacephala var. megacephala

Zaluzania megacephala var. megacephala, commonly known as the large-headed false ginseng, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features palmately compound leaves with three to five leaflets, each with a smooth or slightly serrated margin. The plant produces showy, yellowish-white flowers in panicles during the summer months. Its fruit is a dry capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland habitats.
