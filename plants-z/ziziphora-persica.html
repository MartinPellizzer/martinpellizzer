Ziziphora persica

Ziziphora persica, commonly known as Persian honeyflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and lavender. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and clusters of small, fragrant yellow flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Ziziphora persica is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
