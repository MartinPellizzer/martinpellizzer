Zygophyllum crassissimum

Zygophyllum crassissimum, commonly known as the thick-leaved blister grass, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, the Middle East, and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing shrub or perennial herb with thick, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water in harsh environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in the spring. Its leaves are often covered in a waxy cuticle and have a distinctive blistery appearance, which aids in reducing water loss.

Zygophyllum crassissimum is adapted to survive in nutrient-poor soils and can tolerate extreme temperatures and limited rainfall.
