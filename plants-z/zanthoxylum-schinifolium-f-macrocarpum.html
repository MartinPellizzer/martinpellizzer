Zanthoxylum schinifolium f. macrocarpum

Zanthoxylum schinifolium f. macrocarpum, commonly known as the large-fruited prickly ash, is a variant of the prickly ash species native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnately arranged with serrated margins. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that give way to distinctive, spiny, orange-red berries. Its fruits are often consumed by birds and small mammals, aiding in seed dispersal.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
