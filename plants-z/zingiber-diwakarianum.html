Zingiber diwakarianum

Zingiber diwakarianum, commonly known as the Diwakari ginger, is a rhizomatous herb native to the eastern Himalayas.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other economically important plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant produces fragrant, orange-red flowers that bloom in terminal clusters, adding ornamental value. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a waxy texture and a distinct, spicy aroma.

Zingiber diwakarianum is primarily cultivated for its ornamental appeal and has limited use in traditional medicine.
