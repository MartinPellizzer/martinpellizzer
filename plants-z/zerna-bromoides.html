Zerna bromoides

Zerna bromoides, commonly known as the slender bromegrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Asia and Europe.

It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, meadows, and wastelands. The plant has slender, erect stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, pale green to brownish flowers.

Zerna bromoides is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
