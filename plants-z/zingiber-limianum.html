Zingiber limianum

Zingiber limianum, commonly known as the mountain ginger, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other well-known gingers such as turmeric and cardamom. The plant features rhizomatous growth and produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers with bright yellow and red hues. Its leaves are broad and sword-shaped, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

Zingiber limianum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
