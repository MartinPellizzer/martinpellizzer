Zerumbet

Zerumbet, commonly known as *Zingiber zerumbet*, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its ornamental, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pale yellow or white flowers that bloom in summer. Zerumbet is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and as a decorative plant.

Its rhizomes, while not as commonly used as other ginger species, have some traditional medicinal applications.
