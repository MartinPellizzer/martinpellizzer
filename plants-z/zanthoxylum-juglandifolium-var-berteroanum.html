Zanthoxylum juglandifolium var. berteroanum

Zanthoxylum juglandifolium var. berteroanum, commonly known as the Mexican prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southern United States and Mexico.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are alternate and pinnately arranged, with serrated margins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that give way to red, berry-like fruits in the fall. Its bark and leaves contain compounds that have been used traditionally for medicinal purposes.

The plant is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
