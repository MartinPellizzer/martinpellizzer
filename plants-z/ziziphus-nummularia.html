Ziziphus nummularia

Ziziphus nummularia, commonly known as the silverleaf buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine silvery-white pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for birds and some mammals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Ziziphus nummularia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
