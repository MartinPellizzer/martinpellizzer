Zilla spinosa subsp. biparmata

Zilla spinosa subsp.

biparmata, commonly known as the narrowleaf zilla, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, yellowish flowers that appear in clusters.

The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along riverbanks. Its botanical features include a woody stem, glandular hairs on the leaves, and a distinctive branching pattern.

It is adapted to withstand drought and is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
