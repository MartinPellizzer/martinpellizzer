Zagrosia persica

Zagrosia persica, commonly known as the Persian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Zagrosia persica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
