Ziziphus mucronata subsp. rhodesica

Ziziphus mucronata subsp.

rhodesica, commonly known as the Rhodes date tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa. It produces small, edible fruits that are typically consumed fresh or used in traditional medicine.

The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruiting season occurs during the summer months, and the fruits are rich in sugars and minerals.

The species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in harsh conditions.
