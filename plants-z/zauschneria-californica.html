Zauschneria californica

Zauschneria californica, commonly known as the California poppy, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous annual with showy, bright orange to red flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and grows up to 2 feet tall. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open fields and along roadsides.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to local biodiversity.
