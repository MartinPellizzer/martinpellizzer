Zornia disperma

Zornia disperma, commonly known as the scattered zornia, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Zornia disperma is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including fields and woodland edges.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.
