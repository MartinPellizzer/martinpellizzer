Zanthoxylum zanthoxyloides

Zanthoxylum zanthoxyloides, commonly known as the Chinese prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are usually pinnately arranged and have serrated margins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences, followed by red or orange berries that are edible for some bird species. Zanthoxylum zanthoxyloides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.

Its bark and fruits contain compounds with potential medicinal properties.
