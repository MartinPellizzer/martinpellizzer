Zanthoxylum pterota var. guaranticum

Zanthoxylum pterota var. guaranticum, commonly known as the Brazilian prickly ash, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are usually pinnate and have a serrated margin. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. Its bark and leaves contain various bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional medicine.

The plant is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in a variety of soil types.
