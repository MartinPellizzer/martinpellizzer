Zanthoxylum harmsianum

Zanthoxylum harmsianum, commonly known as the Harms prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are opposite and often have a serrated margin. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by bright red berries, which are an important food source for birds and mammals. Zanthoxylum harmsianum thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

Its bark and leaves contain various bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional medicine.
