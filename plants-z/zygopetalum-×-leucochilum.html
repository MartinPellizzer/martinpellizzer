Zygopetalum × leucochilum

Zygopetalum × leucochilum, commonly known as the white-lipped zygopetalum, is a hybrid orchid native to South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers with a distinctive white lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or terrestrial orchid, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its foliage consists of broad, dark green leaves, and it produces clusters of showy, pink to purple flowers.

Zygopetalum × leucochilum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
