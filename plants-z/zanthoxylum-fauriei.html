Zanthoxylum fauriei

Zanthoxylum fauriei, commonly known as the Chinese prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces berries that are initially green and turn red when mature, often used in traditional medicine.

Zanthoxylum fauriei is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been utilized in herbal remedies for its purported medicinal properties.
