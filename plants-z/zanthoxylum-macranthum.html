Zanthoxylum macranthum

Zanthoxylum macranthum, commonly known as the large-flowered prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and its distinctive, fragrant yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and small mammals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.

Zanthoxylum macranthum is also valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
