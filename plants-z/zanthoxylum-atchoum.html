Zanthoxylum atchoum

Zanthoxylum atchoum, commonly known as the African prickly ash, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces bright red berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Its bark and leaves contain various bioactive compounds, making it valuable in phytotherapy.

Zanthoxylum atchoum is also notable for its spiny branches and adaptability to a range of soil types.
