Zosterophyllanthos trachysepalus

Zosterophyllanthos trachysepalus, commonly known as the rough-sepal zosterophyll, is a rare and ancient plant species belonging to the order Zosterophyllales.

It is characterized by its unique morphology, featuring elongated, ribbon-like leaves and specialized reproductive structures known as sporangia. This plant is considered a living fossil, with its evolutionary lineage dating back to the Paleozoic era. Its botanical features provide crucial insights into the early evolution of land plants.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological specificity, it is of significant interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
