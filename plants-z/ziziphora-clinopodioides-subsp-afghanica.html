Ziziphora clinopodioides subsp. afghanica

Ziziphora clinopodioides subsp.

afghanica, commonly known as Afghan betony, is a perennial herb native to regions of Central Asia, including Afghanistan and Pakistan. It typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

The plant features aromatic, narrow leaves and produces small, clustered blue or purple flowers. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic properties.

This subspecies is valued for its medicinal uses and ornamental appeal in horticulture.
