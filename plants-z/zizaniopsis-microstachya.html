Zizaniopsis microstachya

Zizaniopsis microstachya, commonly known as paragrass, is a perennial grass native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flower spikes that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in wetland or disturbed areas.

Due to its aggressive growth, it is considered a weed in many regions.
