Zingiber aquosum

Zingiber aquosum, commonly known as white ginger or water ginger, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. Its flowers are typically yellow or white, with a distinctive, fragrant aroma.

Zingiber aquosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
