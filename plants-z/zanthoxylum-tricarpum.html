Zanthoxylum tricarpum

Zanthoxylum tricarpum, commonly known as the three-carped prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but have a spicy, citrus-like flavor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Zanthoxylum tricarpum is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
