Zanthoxylum simulans

Zanthoxylum simulans, commonly known as the Chinese prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are edible but have a sharp, peppery taste. Its bark and roots contain compounds that have been used in traditional Chinese medicine.

Zanthoxylum simulans is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.
