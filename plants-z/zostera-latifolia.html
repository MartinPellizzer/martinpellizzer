Zostera latifolia

Zostera latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf eelgrass, is a marine flowering plant found in coastal regions of the northern hemisphere.

It thrives in shallow, brackish waters and is often found in estuaries and tidal flats. The plant has long, ribbon-like leaves that grow in dense underwater beds, providing habitat for various marine organisms. It plays a crucial role in stabilizing sediments and improving water quality.

Zostera latifolia is also an important food source for marine animals such as sea turtles and waterfowl.
