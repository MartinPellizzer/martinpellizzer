Zornia diphylla var. pusilla

Zornia diphylla var. pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf zornia, is a small annual herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as deserts and rocky soils. The plant has alternate, linear to lanceolate leaves and produces small, yellow flowers in clusters. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Zornia diphylla var. pusilla is an important plant in its native ecosystems, contributing to soil stabilization and providing nectar for pollinators.
