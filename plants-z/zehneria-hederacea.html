Zehneria hederacea

Zehneria hederacea, commonly known as the creeping zehneria, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, white or pale yellow flowers clustered in umbels. Zehneria hederacea is known for its ability to spread via runners, forming dense mats on the ground.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in tropical and subtropical regions.
