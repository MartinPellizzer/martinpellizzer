Zingiber longipedunculatum

Zingiber longipedunculatum, commonly known as the long-stemmed ginger, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other economically important spices like ginger and turmeric. The plant is characterized by its tall, branched inflorescence and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with three petals and three sepals, and it produces small, triangular fruits.

Zingiber longipedunculatum is primarily valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
