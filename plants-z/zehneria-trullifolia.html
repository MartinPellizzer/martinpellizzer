Zehneria trullifolia

Zehneria trullifolia, commonly known as the spiral vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its unique spiral growth pattern, where the stems twist around supporting structures in a helical manner. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a smooth, glossy surface.

Zehneria trullifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
