Zingiber intermedium

Zingiber intermedium, commonly known as the intermediate ginger, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers such as turmeric and cardamom. The plant features rhizomatous growth, with erect, narrow leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in inflorescences that resemble a spike.

Zingiber intermedium is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
