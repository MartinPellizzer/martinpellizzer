Zanthoxylum setosum

Zanthoxylum setosum, commonly known as the prickly ash or Chinese prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. Its bark and leaves contain compounds used in traditional medicine, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Zanthoxylum setosum thrives in temperate climates and is adaptable to various soil types.
