Zanthoxylum floridanum

Zanthoxylum floridanum, commonly known as the Florida prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces red berries that are edible for some wildlife but contain toxic compounds for humans. Its bark and leaves have been used traditionally in herbal medicine.

Zanthoxylum floridanum thrives in sandy soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
