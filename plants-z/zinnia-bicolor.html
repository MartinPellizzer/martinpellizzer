Zinnia bicolor

Zinnia bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored zinnia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant, daisy-like flowers that typically display two distinct colors, such as red and yellow or white and yellow. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies, sunflowers, and other composite flowers. Zinnia bicolor is an annual plant that thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Its compact growth habit and long blooming period contribute to its ornamental value.
