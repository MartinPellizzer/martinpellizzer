Zamia eriolepis

Zamia eriolepis, commonly known as the Mexican cycad, is a member of the Zamiaceae family and is native to Mexico.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its stout, unbranched stem and dense, feathery foliage composed of long, narrow, leathery leaves. The plant produces large, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its slow growth and unique appearance, it is a popular ornamental plant in horticulture.
