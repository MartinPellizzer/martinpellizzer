Zingiber gracile var. petiolatum

Zingiber gracile var. petiolatum, commonly known as the slender ginger or petioled ginger, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers such as turmeric and cardamom. The plant features slender, upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often variegated in color. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and typically yellow or white, arranged in spike-like inflorescences.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of aromatic rhizomes.
