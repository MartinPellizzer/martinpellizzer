Zinnia dahliiflora

Zinnia dahliiflora, commonly known as the Mexican zinnia, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy blooms that come in a variety of colors, including red, yellow, orange, and white. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are composed of a central disk surrounded by ray florets, which give it a daisy-like appearance.

Zinnia dahliiflora is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and as a cut flower due to its long-lasting blooms and adaptability to different growing conditions.
