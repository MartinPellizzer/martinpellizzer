Zornia pardina

Zornia pardina, commonly known as the pink zornia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, herbaceous growth habit. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers arranged in dense, spherical clusters, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately compound, and often have a hairy texture.

Zornia pardina is adapted to arid environments and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.
