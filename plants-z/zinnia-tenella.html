Zinnia tenella

Zinnia tenella, commonly known as the dwarf zinnia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual with compact growth, typically reaching heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant produces vibrant, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, with a distinctive central disk surrounded by ray florets. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually hairy to the touch.

Zinnia tenella is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
