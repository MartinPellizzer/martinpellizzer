Zanthoxylum ruizianum

Zanthoxylum ruizianum, commonly known as the Ruiz's prickly ash, is a member of the Rutaceae family and is native to Central America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. Its fruits are red, fleshy berries that mature in the fall and are an important food source for various wildlife.

Zanthoxylum ruizianum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
