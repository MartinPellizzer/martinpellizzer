Ziziphora gundelsheimeri

Ziziphora gundelsheimeri, commonly known as the Gundelsheim savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in dense spikes. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its aromatic properties.

Ziziphora gundelsheimeri is valued for its medicinal uses and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
