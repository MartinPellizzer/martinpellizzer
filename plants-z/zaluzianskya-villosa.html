Zaluzianskya villosa

Zaluzianskya villosa, commonly known as the hairy zaluzianskya, is a perennial flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.

Zaluzianskya villosa is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
