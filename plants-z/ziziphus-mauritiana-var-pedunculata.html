Ziziphus mauritiana var. pedunculata

Ziziphus mauritiana var. pedunculata, commonly known as the Indian date palm, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often bi- or tri-pinnate, and its small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces edible fruits that are typically round or oval, with a sweet, date-like flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, arid environments.

The botanical features of this variety make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
