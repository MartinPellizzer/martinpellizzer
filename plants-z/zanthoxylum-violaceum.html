Zanthoxylum violaceum

Zanthoxylum violaceum, commonly known as purple prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruit is a red, fleshy drupe that contains several seeds.

Zanthoxylum violaceum is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
