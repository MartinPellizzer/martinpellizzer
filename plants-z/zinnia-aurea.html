Zinnia aurea

Zinnia aurea, commonly known as the golden zinnia, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow to orange flower heads, which are typically surrounded by layers of narrow, pointed bracts. The plant produces showy, daisy-like blooms that are popular in gardens for their ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Zinnia aurea is an annual plant that thrives in full sunlight and well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it a favorite among gardeners for its ease of cultivation and long-lasting floral display.
