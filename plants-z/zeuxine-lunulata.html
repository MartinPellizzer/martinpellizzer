Zeuxine lunulata

Zeuxine lunulata, commonly known as the moonflower zeuxine, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Himalayas.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately compound, and often have a waxy texture.
