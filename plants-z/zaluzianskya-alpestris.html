Zaluzianskya alpestris

Zaluzianskya alpestris, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, often with a white throat. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are adapted to withstand cold and arid conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Zaluzianskya alpestris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with similar growing conditions.
