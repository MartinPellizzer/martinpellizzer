Zanthoxylum echinocarpum

Zanthoxylum echinocarpum, commonly known as the spiny chinaberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnate and often have serrated margins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into distinctive, spiny fruits. These fruits are typically orange to red when mature and contain several seeds.

Zanthoxylum echinocarpum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
