Zanthoxylum martinicense subsp. amoyense

Zanthoxylum martinicense subsp.

amoyense, commonly known as the Amoy prickly ash, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its spiny twigs and opposite, pinnately compound leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red, fleshy berries. Its bark and leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

The species is valued for its ornamental potential and ecological role in native habitats.
