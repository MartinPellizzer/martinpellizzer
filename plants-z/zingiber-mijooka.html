Zingiber mijooka

Zingiber mijooka, commonly known as the Mijooka ginger, is a rare and lesser-known member of the Zingiberaceae family.

It is native to the high-altitude regions of Southeast Asia, particularly in parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant features a rhizomatous root system and produces striking, fragrant flowers with a unique coloration. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a waxy texture.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Zingiber mijooka is considered a valuable subject for botanical research and conservation efforts.
