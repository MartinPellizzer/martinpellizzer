Zamia lucayana

Zamia lucayana, commonly known as the Lucayan cycad, is a member of the Zamiaceae family and is native to the Caribbean region.

It is a dioecious plant, meaning that male and female reproductive structures are found on separate plants. The plant has a stout, unbranched stem that is covered in a thick, fibrous layer. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with numerous leaflets that are narrow and slightly curved.

Zamia lucayana is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.
