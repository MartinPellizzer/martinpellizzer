Zygopetalum pubescens

Zygopetalum pubescens, commonly known as the fuzzy zygopetalum, is a species of orchid native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, or pubescent, foliage, which gives it its scientific name. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers with a complex structure typical of orchids, featuring showy petals and sepals. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Zygopetalum pubescens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique appearance.
