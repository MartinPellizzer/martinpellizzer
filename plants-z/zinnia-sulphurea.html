Zinnia sulphurea

Zinnia sulphurea, commonly known as the sulphur zinnia, is an annual flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow to orange flowers, which are typically daisy-like in appearance with a central disk surrounded by ray florets. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners in arid climates. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early autumn, adding a bright splash of color to landscapes.

Zinnia sulphurea is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
