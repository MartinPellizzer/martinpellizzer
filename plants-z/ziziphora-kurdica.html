Ziziphora kurdica

Ziziphora kurdica, commonly known as Kurdish tea or mountain tea, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and lavender. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, small blue or purple flowers and narrow, aromatic leaves. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in high-altitude environments.

Ziziphora kurdica is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its antispasmodic and digestive benefits.
