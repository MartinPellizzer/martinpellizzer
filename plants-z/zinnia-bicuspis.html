Zinnia bicuspis

Zinnia bicuspis, commonly known as the double-cusped zinnia, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and the southern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, composite flower heads. The plant features bright, vibrant blooms in shades of red, orange, and yellow, with petals that are often double or semi-double in structure. Zinnia bicuspis typically grows as an annual, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, and thrives in full sun with well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
