Zinnia americana

Zinnia americana, commonly known as the American zinnia, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, yellow, and white. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its blooms are tubular at the center and radiate outward, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Zinnia americana is known for its ability to thrive in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy and low-maintenance plant.
