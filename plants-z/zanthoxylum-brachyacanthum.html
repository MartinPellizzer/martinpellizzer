Zanthoxylum brachyacanthum

Zanthoxylum brachyacanthum, commonly known as the prickly ash or Florida prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its spiny branches and small, yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces small, red berries that are clustered in umbels, which are a distinctive botanical feature. Its bark and leaves contain compounds with medicinal properties, making it of interest in traditional herbal medicine.

Zanthoxylum brachyacanthum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.
