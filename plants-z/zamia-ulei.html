Zamia ulei

Zamia ulei, commonly known as the Mexican cycad, is a member of the Zamiaceae family and is native to Mexico.

It is a slow-growing, evergreen plant that produces a stout, unbranched stem with a thick, fibrous trunk. The plant has large, pinnately compound leaves that are dark green and leathery in texture. It produces cones rather than flowers, with male and female cones typically occurring on separate plants.

Zamia ulei is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and requires well-drained soil and ample sunlight.
