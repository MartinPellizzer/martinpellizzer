Zannichellia pedicellata var. digyna

Zannichellia pedicellata var. digyna, commonly known as the narrow-leaved water starwort, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in shallow, slow-moving waters and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces small, green flowers that are usually submerged, though some may emerge above water. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Due to its delicate structure, it is often used in ecological restoration projects to improve water quality and biodiversity.
