Ziziphora alpina

Ziziphora alpina, commonly known as alpine betony, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple and bloom in late summer.

Ziziphora alpina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
