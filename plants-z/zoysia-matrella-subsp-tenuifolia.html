Zoysia matrella subsp. tenuifolia

Zoysia matrella subsp.

tenuifolia, commonly known as slender zoysia, is a grass species native to Asia, particularly found in regions like Japan and China. It is characterized by its fine, dense foliage and a creeping growth habit that forms a dense mat.

The plant has narrow, elongated leaves with a bluish-green color, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers. It is often used in landscaping for its durability and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to different climates.
