Zingiber coloratum

Zingiber coloratum, commonly known as the flame ginger, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant is characterized by its vibrant, colorful bracts that range from red to orange, which are not flowers but modified leaves that attract pollinators. Zingiber coloratum typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
