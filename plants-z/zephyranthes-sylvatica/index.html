Zephyranthes Sylvatica

Zephyranthes sylvatica is a delicate and elegant flowering plant known for its striking white blooms. Native to the tropical regions of the Americas, it thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil. This species is often referred to as the "Rainflower" due to its tendency to bloom after rainfall. Its small, star-shaped flowers appear in clusters, adding a touch of beauty to gardens and natural landscapes. Despite its modest size, Zephyranthes sylvatica holds cultural and ecological significance in its native habitats.
