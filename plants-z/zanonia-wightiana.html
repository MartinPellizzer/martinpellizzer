Zanonia wightiana

Zanonia wightiana, commonly known as the Indian nettle, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 1 meter, characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in spikes, which are borne on long, slender stems. Its roots are known to have medicinal properties and are used in traditional Ayurvedic medicine.

Zanonia wightiana thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forests and along stream banks.
