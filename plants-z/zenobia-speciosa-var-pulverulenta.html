Zenobia speciosa var. pulverulenta

Zenobia speciosa var. pulverulenta, commonly known as the Powdered Zenobia, is a strikingly ornamental plant native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, velvety leaves that have a powdery, almost dusty appearance, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of violet and purple, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts of succulent plants.
