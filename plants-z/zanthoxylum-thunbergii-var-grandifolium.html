Zanthoxylum thunbergii var. grandifolium

Zanthoxylum thunbergii var. grandifolium, commonly known as the Japanese prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and have serrated margins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries, which are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or scrubland habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
