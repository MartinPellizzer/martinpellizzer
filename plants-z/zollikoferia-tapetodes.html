Zollikoferia tapetodes

Zollikoferia tapetodes, commonly known as the mountain garlic.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pale purple flowers in summer. Its bulbous underground stems are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is adapted to cold climates and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.
