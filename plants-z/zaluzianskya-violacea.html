Zaluzianskya violacea

Zaluzianskya violacea, commonly known as the violet bugweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, violet to purple flowers. The plant has fleshy, succulent leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its compact size and colorful blooms make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
