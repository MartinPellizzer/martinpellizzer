Zingiber caudatum

Zingiber caudatum, commonly known as the black ginger or wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other economically important plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant is characterized by its dark purple or black rhizomes, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, and it produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in a spike.

Zingiber caudatum is valued for its aromatic properties and is often cultivated for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
