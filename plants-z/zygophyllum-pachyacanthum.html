Zygophyllum pachyacanthum

Zygophyllum pachyacanthum, commonly known as the thickthorn zygophyllum, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has adapted to harsh environments through its succulent stems and reduced leaves, which help conserve water. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access moisture from lower soil layers.

Zygophyllum pachyacanthum plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
