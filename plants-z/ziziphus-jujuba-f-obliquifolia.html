Ziziphus jujuba f. obliquifolia

Ziziphus jujuba f. obliquifolia, commonly known as the obliquifoliate Chinese date, is a cultivated form of the jujube tree native to China.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated leaf shape, which differs from the typical rounded leaves of the standard jujube. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are similar in taste to regular jujubes but often have a higher sugar content. It thrives in temperate climates and is often grown for its fruit and ornamental value.

This variety is valued for its adaptability and resilience in various soil conditions.
