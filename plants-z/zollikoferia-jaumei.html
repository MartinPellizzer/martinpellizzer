Zollikoferia jaumei

Zollikoferia jaumei, commonly known as the Malayan mountain ginger, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and large, lance-shaped leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Zollikoferia jaumei is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
