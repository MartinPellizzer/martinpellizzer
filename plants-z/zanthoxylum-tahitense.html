Zanthoxylum tahitense

Zanthoxylum tahitense, commonly known as Tahitian prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Society Islands in French Polynesia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves and yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines.

Its bark and leaves contain bioactive compounds, making it of interest for traditional medicine and potential pharmaceutical applications.
