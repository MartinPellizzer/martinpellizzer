Zanthoxylum fagara subsp. fagara

Zanthoxylum fagara subsp.

fagara, commonly known as the prickly ash or red prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated margins and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters.

The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are edible for some birds and mammals but toxic to humans. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and streambanks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native plant ecosystems.
