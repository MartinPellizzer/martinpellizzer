Zingiber zingiber

Zingiber zingiber, commonly known as ginger, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous underground stem, which is the primary part used for culinary and medicinal purposes. The plant features long, narrow leaves and produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in a spike. Its rhizome contains essential oils and compounds like gingerol, which contribute to its distinctive flavor and therapeutic properties.

Zingiber zingiber thrives in warm, humid climates and is widely cultivated for both food and traditional medicine.
