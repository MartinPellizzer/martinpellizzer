Zanthoxylum complexum

Zanthoxylum complexum, commonly known as the prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often arranged in pairs and have serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. Its bark and berries contain compounds with medicinal properties, historically used by Native American tribes.

Zanthoxylum complexum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and streambanks.
