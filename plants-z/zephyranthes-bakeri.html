Zephyranthes bakeri

Zephyranthes bakeri, commonly known as the Baker's rain lily, is a species of flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking white or pale pink flowers in response to rainfall. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often found in open woodlands and along stream banks.

Zephyranthes bakeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
