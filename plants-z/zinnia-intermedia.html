Zinnia intermedia

Zinnia intermedia, commonly known as the sunset zinnia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, vibrant flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers come in a range of colors, including red, orange, yellow, and pink, and are often used in gardens for their ornamental value.

Zinnia intermedia is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping and cut flower arrangements.
