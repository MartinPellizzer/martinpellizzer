Zanthoxylum ovalifolium var. sepiarium

Zanthoxylum ovalifolium var. sepiarium, commonly known as the Sepiarium prickly ash, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are oval-shaped and have a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for some wildlife. Its bark and leaves contain bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional medicine.

The species is adapted to moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and streamside habitats.
