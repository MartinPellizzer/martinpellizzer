Zataria humilis

Zataria humilis, commonly known as Persian pepper or wild thyme, is a small shrub native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is often found in rocky or arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its essential oils, which contain compounds like carvacrol and thymol, contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.

Zataria humilis is also valued for its hardiness and ability to withstand drought conditions.
