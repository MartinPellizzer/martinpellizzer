Zornia brasiliensis

Zornia brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian zornia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Zornia brasiliensis is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
