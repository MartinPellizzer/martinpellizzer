Zizia aurea var. acuminata

Zizia aurea var. acuminata, commonly known as the golden beard or yellow false indigo, is a perennial herb native to the central and eastern United States.

It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stem is often hairy as well. It thrives in dry, open habitats like prairies and woodland edges.

Its botanical name reflects its golden color and the sharp, pointed tips of its leaves.
