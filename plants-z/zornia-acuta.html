Zornia acuta

Zornia acuta, commonly known as sharpleaf zornia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Zornia acuta is often found in moist, open habitats such as fields and woodland edges.

Its seeds are enclosed in a pod and are dispersed by wind, contributing to its spread across suitable environments.
