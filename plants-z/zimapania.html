Zimapania

Zimapania, commonly known as the Mexican yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. Zimapania is adapted to dry climates and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
