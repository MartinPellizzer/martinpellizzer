Zanthoxylum harrisii

Zanthoxylum harrisii, commonly known as Harris's prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their aromatic properties.
