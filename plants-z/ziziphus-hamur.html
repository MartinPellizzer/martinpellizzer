Ziziphus hamur

Ziziphus hamur, commonly known as the desert date tree, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, small leaves, and yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, sweet fruits that are edible and often consumed fresh or processed into jams and beverages. Ziziphus hamur is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a valuable species for desert agriculture and reforestation efforts.

Its deep root system allows it to access water from deep underground, enhancing its survival in harsh environments.
