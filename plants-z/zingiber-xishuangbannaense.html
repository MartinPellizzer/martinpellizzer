Zingiber xishuangbannaense

Zingiber xishuangbannaense, commonly known as the Xishuangbanna ginger, is a herbaceous plant native to southern China, particularly Yunnan Province.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers and turmeric. The plant features rhizomatous growth with broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. Its inflorescence consists of clustered, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to red, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has potential in traditional medicine and horticulture.
