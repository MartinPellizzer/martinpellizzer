Ziziphus rugosa var. rugosa

Ziziphus rugosa var. rugosa, commonly known as the rough ziziphus, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to regions of Asia, including China and India.

It is characterized by its rugged, thorny stems and elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine, short hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by edible, red, fleshy fruits that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant adapted to harsh conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
