Zingiber teres

Zingiber teres, commonly known as the narrow-leafed ginger, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers and related plants. The plant features slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. Its flowers are typically small and clustered in inflorescences, with bright yellow petals and a distinctive ginger scent.

Zingiber teres is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
