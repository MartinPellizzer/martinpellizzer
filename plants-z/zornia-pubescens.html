Zornia pubescens

Zornia pubescens, commonly known as the hairy zornia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert or semi-arid environments.

Zornia pubescens is an important plant for local ecosystems, providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for various herbivores.
