Zephyranthes capivarina

Zephyranthes capivarina, commonly known as the rain lily, is a species of flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is a bulbous plant that produces showy, white or pale pink flowers during the rainy season. The plant features strap-like leaves and is adapted to thrive in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in open areas such as grasslands and forest margins.

Zephyranthes capivarina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in warm climates.
