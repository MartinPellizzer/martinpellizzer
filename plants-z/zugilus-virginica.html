Zugilus virginica

Zugilus virginica, commonly known as the Virginia bugleweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features small, opposite leaves that are usually lance-shaped and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are clustered in spikes and range in color from white to pale purple, blooming in early to mid-summer.

Zugilus virginica is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
