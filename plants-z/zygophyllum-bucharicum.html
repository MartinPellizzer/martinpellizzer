Zygophyllum bucharicum

Zygophyllum bucharicum, commonly known as the Bukhara date tree, is a shrub native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Zygophyllaceae and is characterized by its small, deciduous leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant. It produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible and often used in local cuisines.

Zygophyllum bucharicum plays an important role in stabilizing desert soils and supporting local biodiversity.
