Zornia diphylla f. ciliata

Zornia diphylla f. ciliata, commonly known as the ciliated zornia, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a low-growing annual herb characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands. Its fruit is a hairy pod that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The ciliate hairs on its leaves and stems are distinctive features that aid in its identification.
