Zingiber angustifolium

Zingiber angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf ginger, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other economically important plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant features long, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces fragrant, red-orange flowers with showy bracts. Its rhizomes, similar to those of ginger, are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

Zingiber angustifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
