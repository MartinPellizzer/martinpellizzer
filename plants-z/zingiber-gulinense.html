Zingiber gulinense

Zingiber gulinense, commonly known as the Gulin ginger, is a rhizomatous herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other economically important plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, often with a distinctive coloration that varies by species.

Zingiber gulinense is valued for its ornamental qualities and has potential medicinal uses in traditional herbal practices.
