Ziziphora subcrispa

Ziziphora subcrispa, commonly known as the fringed germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, branched stems that are often covered with fine, hairy structures. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are linear and slightly hairy, with a distinctive fringed appearance along the edges.

Ziziphora subcrispa is valued in traditional medicine for its aromatic properties and is often used in herbal teas and remedies.
