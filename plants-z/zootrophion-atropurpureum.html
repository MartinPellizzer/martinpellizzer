Zootrophion atropurpureum

Zootrophion atropurpureum, commonly known as the purple-flowered zootrophion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from purple to violet, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in horticultural settings.
