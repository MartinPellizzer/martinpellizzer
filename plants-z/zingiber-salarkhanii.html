Zingiber salarkhanii

Zingiber salarkhanii, commonly known as the Kashmiri ginger, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other economically important plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant features a rhizomatous root system and produces clusters of fragrant, pale yellow flowers. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and have a slightly serrated edge.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
