Zingiber simaoense

Zingiber simaoense, commonly known as Simao ginger, is a rare and endemic plant species native to the Yunnan province in China.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other economically important plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant features a rhizomatous root system and produces fragrant, tubular flowers in shades of yellow and orange. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and covered with a waxy cuticle, which helps in reducing water loss.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Zingiber simaoense is considered a valuable subject for botanical research and conservation efforts.
