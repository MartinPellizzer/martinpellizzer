Zanthoxylum planispinum

Zanthoxylum planispinum, commonly known as the flatpaw prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are opposite and have serrated margins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. Its bark is thick, rough, and often fissured, with a distinctive aromatic scent.

Zanthoxylum planispinum is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
