Zygophyllum madecassum

Zygophyllum madecassum, commonly known as the Madagascar false flax, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Zygophyllaceae.

It is a shrub characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and distinctive, spiny leaves. Native to Madagascar, it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and poor soil conditions. The plant has a compact growth habit and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural studies.
