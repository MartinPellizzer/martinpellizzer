Zollikoferia arborescens

Zollikoferia arborescens, commonly known as the tree gentian, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
