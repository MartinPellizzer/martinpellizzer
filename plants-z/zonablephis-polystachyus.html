Zonablephis polystachyus

Zonablephis polystachyus, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous white flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a dramatic visual effect. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets that are often arranged in an alternate pattern. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Zonablephis polystachyus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a street tree due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability.
