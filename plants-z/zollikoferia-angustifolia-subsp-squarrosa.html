Zollikoferia angustifolia subsp. squarrosa

Zollikoferia angustifolia subsp.

squarrosa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved zollikoferia, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is typically found in grassland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
