Zantedeschia elliottiana

Zantedeschia elliottiana, commonly known as the Elliott's arum, is a striking perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features large, arrow-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a glossy texture. The plant produces an umbrella-like inflorescence with a spadix surrounded by a showy, violet-colored spathe. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Zantedeschia elliottiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
