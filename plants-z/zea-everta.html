Zea everta

Zea everta, commonly known as the narrowleaf maize, is a species of grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, blade-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a tall, erect stem.

Zea everta is closely related to other members of the grass family, such as corn and sorghum.
