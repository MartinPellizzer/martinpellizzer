Zanthoxylum terebinthoides

Zanthoxylum terebinthoides, commonly known as the Chinese prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are often used in traditional Chinese medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or mountainous regions.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and winter interest.
