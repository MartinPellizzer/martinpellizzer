Ziziphus apetala

Ziziphus apetala, commonly known as the leafless ziziphus, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its lack of leaves, with only small, scale-like structures present on its stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in branched inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, dry drupe that contains a single seed.

Ziziphus apetala is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, making it a resilient species in xeric habitats.
