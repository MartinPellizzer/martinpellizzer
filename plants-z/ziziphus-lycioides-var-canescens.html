Ziziphus lycioides var. canescens

Ziziphus lycioides var. canescens, commonly known as the silverleaf ziziphus, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which results from dense pubescence covering the leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in panicles, followed by edible, sweet-tart berries. It is adapted to survive in harsh environments with low rainfall and poor soil conditions.

The species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soils.
