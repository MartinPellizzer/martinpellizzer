Zamia purpurea

Zamia purpurea, commonly known as the purple cycad, is a member of the Zamiaceae family and is native to the Caribbean islands.

It is a slow-growing plant characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces cycad-like cones, with male and female cones occurring on separate plants. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts, and it is valued for its ornamental appearance.

Due to its slow growth and specific care requirements, it is a popular choice for collectors and botanical gardens.
