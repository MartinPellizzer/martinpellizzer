Zelkova acuminata

Zelkova acuminata, commonly known as the Chinese zelkova, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Ulmaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras as its fruit. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and moderate growth rate, making it suitable for urban landscapes.

The bark is smooth and grayish-brown, with shallow vertical fissures.
