Ziziphora woronowii

Ziziphora woronowii, commonly known as sweet everlasting, is a perennial herb native to regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or violet, arranged in whorls along the stem. Its aromatic leaves and flowers are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Ziziphora woronowii is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in dry, rocky soils.
