Zamia spadicea

Zamia spadicea, commonly known as the spathulate cycad, is a member of the Zamiaceae family and is native to Mexico.

It is a slow-growing evergreen plant characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces a unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe, typical of cycads. Its foliage is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value, though it requires specific care to thrive.

Zamia spadicea is also valued for its historical and ecological significance in its native habitat.
