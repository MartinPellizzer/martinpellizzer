Zinnia purpusii

Zinnia purpusii, commonly known as the Mexican zinnia, is a species native to Central America and parts of the southwestern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its vibrant, daisy-like flowers that range in color from yellow to red and orange. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with narrow, linear leaves and showy, single-layered blooms. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Zinnia purpusii is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
