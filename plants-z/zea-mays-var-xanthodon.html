Zea mays var. xanthodon

Zea mays var. xanthodon, commonly known as yellow dent corn, is a variant of maize characterized by its distinctive yellow kernels and high starch content.

This plant is an annual grass that typically grows to a height of 1.5 to 3 meters, with long, narrow leaves and a robust stalk. The ears of yellow dent corn are usually large, with tightly packed rows of kernels that are fully developed at maturity. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is widely cultivated for its use in food, animal feed, and industrial products.
