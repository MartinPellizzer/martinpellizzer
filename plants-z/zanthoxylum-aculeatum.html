Zanthoxylum aculeatum

Zanthoxylum aculeatum, commonly known as the prickly ash or wild ginger, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds and small mammals. Its bark and roots have been used in traditional medicine for their stimulant and antiseptic properties.

Zanthoxylum aculeatum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and stream banks.
