Zea mays var. polyxantha

Zea mays var. polyxantha, commonly known as the Mexican maize or teosinte, is a wild relative of modern corn.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stalks and small, clustered ears of grain. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are broad and pointed, often with a rough texture.

This species plays an important role in the genetic diversity of maize, contributing to breeding programs aimed at improving crop resilience.
