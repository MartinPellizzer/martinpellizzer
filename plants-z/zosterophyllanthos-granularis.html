Zosterophyllanthos granularis

Zosterophyllanthos granularis, commonly known as the granular zosterophyllum, is a unique plant belonging to the order Zosterophyllophyta.

It is characterized by its distinct, strap-like leaves that are often arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem. The plant exhibits a primitive vascular system, which is a key feature in its classification as a transitional form between non-vascular and vascular plants. Its fossil record provides important insights into the early evolution of land plants.

The species is primarily found in paleontological sites, indicating its presence during the Silurian period.
