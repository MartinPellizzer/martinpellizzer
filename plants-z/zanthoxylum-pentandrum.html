Zanthoxylum pentandrum

Zanthoxylum pentandrum, commonly known as the Chinese prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are edible and often used in traditional Chinese medicine. Its bark and roots contain compounds with medicinal properties, including analgesic and anti-inflammatory effects.

Zanthoxylum pentandrum is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
