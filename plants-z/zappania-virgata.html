Zappania virgata

Zappania virgata, commonly known as the silverleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of silvery-white, fuzzy leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces bright yellow composite flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy white seeds dispersed by the wind. Zappania virgata is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

It is considered a low-maintenance plant, making it popular in xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
