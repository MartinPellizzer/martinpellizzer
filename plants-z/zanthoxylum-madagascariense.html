Zanthoxylum madagascariense

Zanthoxylum madagascariense, commonly known as the Madagascar prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife. Its bark and leaves contain compounds that have been used in traditional medicine for their potential anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

Zanthoxylum madagascariense is also noted for its spiny branches, which provide structural support and deter herbivores.
