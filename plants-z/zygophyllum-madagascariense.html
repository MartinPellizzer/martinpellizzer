Zygophyllum madagascariense

Zygophyllum madagascariense, commonly known as the Madagascar morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Zygophyllaceae and is characterized by its small, showy flowers that typically bloom in the morning. The plant has a succulent stem and leaves, adapted to the arid conditions of its native habitat. Its flowers are usually pink or red, with five petals and a distinctive spurred structure.

Zygophyllum madagascariense is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is known for its ornamental value in horticulture.
