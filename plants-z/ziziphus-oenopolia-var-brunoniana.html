Ziziphus oenopolia var. brunoniana

Ziziphus oenopolia var. brunoniana, commonly known as the blackdate tree, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often serrated and have a glossy appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by edible, dark brown fruits resembling dates. The fruit is rich in sugars and has a unique, slightly sour flavor.

It is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils.
