Zehneria cerasiformis

Zehneria cerasiformis, commonly known as the cherry-shaped zehneria, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, cherry-like fruits that are typically red or orange when mature. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, adding ornamental value to its appearance. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Zehneria cerasiformis is primarily cultivated for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
