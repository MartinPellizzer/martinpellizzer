Zanthoxylum ayua

Zanthoxylum ayua, commonly known as the Ayu prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub native to the humid forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often covered in fine, stiff hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries, which are edible but have a spicy, peppery flavor. Zanthoxylum ayua is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.

Its ecological role includes providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
