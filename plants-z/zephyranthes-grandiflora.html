Zephyranthes grandiflora

Zephyranthes grandiflora, commonly known as the rain lily, is a herbaceous plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It features strap-like, grassy leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces showy, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in response to rainfall, often appearing after thunderstorms. Zephyranthes grandiflora is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
