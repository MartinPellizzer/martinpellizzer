Zea perennis

Zea perennis, commonly known as perennial maize, is a grass species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stalks and large, broad leaves that can reach several feet in length. This plant is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and climates, making it a resilient crop. Its flowering structure consists of spikelets that produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Zea perennis has been historically cultivated by indigenous peoples for its edible grains and has significant ecological and agricultural value.
