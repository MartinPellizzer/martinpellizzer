Zephyranthes chrysantha

Zephyranthes chrysantha, commonly known as the rain lily, is a species of flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a bulbous plant that produces striking, showy flowers in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically blooms after rainfall, hence its common name, and is often found in grassy areas and open woodlands. Its flowers are short-lived but attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Zephyranthes chrysantha is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
