Ziziphus jujuba f. heteroformis

Ziziphus jujuba f. heteroformis, commonly known as the Chinese date or jujube, is a fruit-bearing shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically 5-9 pairs of leaflets, and its distinctive red, fleshy fruits that are rich in nutrients. The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruit, which has a sweet and tangy flavor. It is also valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to withstand drought conditions.

The fruit is used in traditional medicine and is a popular ingredient in various culinary dishes.
