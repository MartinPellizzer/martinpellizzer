Zygomenes cucullata

Zygomenes cucullata, commonly known as the hooded zygophyll, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Australia.

It belongs to the family Zygophyllaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, hood-like floral structure. The plant typically grows as a low shrub with woody stems and small, linear leaves. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, and they bloom in spring.

Zygomenes cucullata is adapted to harsh, dry environments and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.
