Zieria laevigata var. fraseri

Zieria laevigata var. fraseri, commonly known as the Fraser's zieria, is a small evergreen shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It typically grows in heathlands and sclerophyll forests, preferring well-drained sandy or rocky soils. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. Its small, white or pale pink flowers appear in clusters during the spring and summer months.

Zieria laevigata var. fraseri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its compact form and seasonal blooms.
