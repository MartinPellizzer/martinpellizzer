Ziziphora dasyantha

Ziziphora dasyantha, commonly known as the woolly ziziphora, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in dense spikes. Its leaves are narrow, hairy, and often have a mint-like scent.

Ziziphora dasyantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
