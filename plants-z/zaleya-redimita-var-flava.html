Zaleya redimita var. flava

Zaleya redimita var. flava, commonly known as the yellow prairie coneflower, is a perennial herb native to the central United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its yellow to orange ray florets and dark brown disk florets. The plant typically grows in prairie and open woodland habitats, preferring well-drained soils. It produces a tall, branching stem with lance-shaped leaves and is often found in dry, sunny environments.

Zaleya redimita var. flava is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
