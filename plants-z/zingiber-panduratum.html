Zingiber panduratum

Zingiber panduratum, commonly known as the ginger lily, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other economically important plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant features tall, sword-shaped leaves and produces fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and red. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

Zingiber panduratum is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its potential medicinal properties.
