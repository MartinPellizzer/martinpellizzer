Zannichellia palustris subsp. marina

Zannichellia palustris subsp.

marina, commonly known as sea water plantain, is a submerged aquatic plant native to coastal and brackish water environments. It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often ribbon-like in appearance.

This subspecies is adapted to saline conditions, making it an important component of estuarine and coastal ecosystems. It plays a role in stabilizing sediments and providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates.

The plant typically flowers with small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences.
