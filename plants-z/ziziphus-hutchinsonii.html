Ziziphus hutchinsonii

Ziziphus hutchinsonii, commonly known as the Desert date or Indian date, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically pollinated by insects, followed by the development of small, fleshy fruits that are edible for both humans and animals. The plant has a deep root system that allows it to survive in dry conditions, making it highly drought-tolerant. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Ziziphus hutchinsonii is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
