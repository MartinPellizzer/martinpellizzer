Zinnia linearifolia

Zinnia linearifolia, commonly known as the slender zinnia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and showy, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Zinnia linearifolia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
