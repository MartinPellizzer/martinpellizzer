Zanthoxylum macrospermum

Zanthoxylum macrospermum, commonly known as the Chinese prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellowish-orange flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but have a sharp, peppery flavor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties have made it a subject of botanical and pharmacological interest.
