Zosterophyllanthos peroniocephalus

Zosterophyllanthos peroniocephalus, commonly known as the velvetleaf zosterophyll, is a unique plant belonging to the order Zosterophyllales.

It is characterized by its leaf-like structures that resemble primitive plant forms, suggesting a possible link to early land plants. The plant exhibits a branching, thallus-like structure with no true roots, stems, or leaves, which is a key feature of zosterophytes. It thrives in moist, freshwater environments, often found in shallow lakes and ponds.

Its fossil record provides important insights into the evolutionary transition from aquatic to terrestrial life.
