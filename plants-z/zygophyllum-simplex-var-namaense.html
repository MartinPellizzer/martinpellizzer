Zygophyllum simplex var. namaense

Zygophyllum simplex var. namaense, commonly known as the Namib Desert sandmat, is a low-growing, succulent shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features small, fleshy leaves that are adapted to minimize water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access water deep underground.

This species is well-suited to sandy and rocky soils, making it a hardy plant in extreme desert conditions.
