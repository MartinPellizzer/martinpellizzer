Zaluzianskya pachyrrhiza

Zaluzianskya pachyrrhiza, commonly known as the thickstemmed bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, cylindrical stems and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid climates. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which helps reduce water loss.

Zaluzianskya pachyrrhiza is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
