Zea mays var. multicoloramylacea

Zea mays var. multicoloramylacea, commonly known as the multicolored corn or rainbow corn, is a variant of maize characterized by its vibrant, multicolored kernels.

This plant is a member of the grass family, Poaceae, and is native to the Americas. Its foliage consists of long, narrow leaves with a bluish-green hue, and it produces an ear of corn with a wide range of colors including red, purple, blue, and yellow. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance.
