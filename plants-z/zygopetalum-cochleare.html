Zygopetalum cochleare

Zygopetalum cochleare, commonly known as the spiral orchid, is a species native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that thrives in cool, humid environments with well-drained soil. The plant is characterized by its spiral arrangement of leaves and its fragrant, showy flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its inflorescence typically consists of several pale pink or white flowers with a distinctive, twisted lip.

Zygopetalum cochleare is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
