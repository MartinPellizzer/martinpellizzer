Zingiber eberhardtii

Zingiber eberhardtii, commonly known as the Eberhardt ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers and related plants. The plant is characterized by its tall, rhizomatous growth and produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish flowers. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and have a smooth, waxy texture.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its aromatic rhizomes.
