Zauschneria californica subsp. mexicana

Zauschneria californica subsp.

mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican goldpoppy, is a flowering plant native to parts of California and Baja California. It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, orange to reddish-orange flowers in late spring to early summer.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is adapted to well-drained soils in full sun. It is often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats.

Its striking blooms make it a popular choice for gardens and native plant landscaping.
