Zapoteca formosa subsp. schottii

Zapoteca formosa subsp.

schottii, commonly known as the Schott's zapoteca, is a shrub native to the highlands of southern Mexico. It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers.

The plant produces small, round berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in cloud forests at elevations between 1,500 and 2,500 meters.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
