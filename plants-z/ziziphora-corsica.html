Ziziphora corsica

Ziziphora corsica, commonly known as sweetwood or Corsican tea, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and small, blue-violet flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is well adapted to coastal and inland environments. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported digestive and calming properties.

Ziziphora corsica is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and resilience.
