Zornia baliensis

Zornia baliensis, commonly known as the Bali zornia, is a flowering plant native to the island of Bali in Indonesia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest undergrowth or along stream banks. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are dispersed by wind, contributing to its distribution in the region.

Zornia baliensis is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to its tropical habitat.
