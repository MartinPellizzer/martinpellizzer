Zostera marina

Zostera marina, commonly known as eelgrass, is a marine flowering plant that thrives in shallow coastal waters.

It forms dense underwater meadows that provide critical habitat for a variety of marine life. The plant has long, ribbon-like leaves that are typically 20 to 40 centimeters in length. It reproduces through seeds and also by vegetative growth, allowing it to spread across seafloors.

Zostera marina plays a vital role in coastal ecosystems by stabilizing sediments and improving water quality.
