Zeuxine pseudogracilis

Zeuxine pseudogracilis, commonly known as the slender zig-zag gentian, is a flowering plant species native to parts of Asia, particularly China and the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, characterized by its showy, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its flowering stems are slender and often branched.

Zeuxine pseudogracilis is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its striking floral display.
