Ziziphora suaveolens

Ziziphora suaveolens, commonly known as sweet hyssop, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. The plant produces small, fragrant blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are aromatic and have been used historically in herbal medicine for their purported soothing properties.

Ziziphora suaveolens is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardiness.
