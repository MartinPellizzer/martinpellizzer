Zinnia angustifolia var. angustifolia

Zinnia angustifolia var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf zinnia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and daisy-like flowers that range in color from yellow to red. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually gray-green in color.

Zinnia angustifolia var. angustifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
