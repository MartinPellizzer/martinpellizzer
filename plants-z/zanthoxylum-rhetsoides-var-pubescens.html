Zanthoxylum rhetsoides var. pubescens

Zanthoxylum rhetsoides var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and covered with fine, pubescent hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries, which are an important food source for wildlife. Its bark is thick and ridged, often exuding a mild, aromatic scent.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
