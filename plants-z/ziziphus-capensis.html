Ziziphus capensis

Ziziphus capensis, commonly known as the coastal thornapple, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny branches and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically red or orange when mature. It thrives in coastal and sandy environments, often growing in areas with high salinity.

Ziziphus capensis is adapted to arid conditions and is often used in traditional medicine.
