Zingiber sianginense

Zingiber sianginense, commonly known as the Sip Song Pao or Siangin ginger, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other economically important plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant is characterized by its aromatic rhizomes, which are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, and it produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters.

Zingiber sianginense thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its unique flavor profile and medicinal properties.
