Zealandia powellii

Zealandia powellii, commonly known as the Powell's zoster, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Zosteraceae and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, ribbon-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in coastal areas, often in sandy or muddy soils along the shorelines. It plays a role in stabilizing coastal sediments and provides habitat for small marine organisms.

Zealandia powellii is also valued for its ecological significance in maintaining the balance of coastal ecosystems.
