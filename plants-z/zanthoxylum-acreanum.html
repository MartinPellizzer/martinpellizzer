Zanthoxylum acreanum

Zanthoxylum acreanum, commonly known as the Brazilian prickly ash, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are usually pinnate and have a serrated margin. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruits are red, fleshy berries that contain several seeds, and they are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is also notable for its thorny stems and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
