Zanthoxylum scheryi

Zanthoxylum scheryi, commonly known as the Schery's prickly ash, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are edible for some animals but generally considered toxic to humans.

Its bark and leaves contain compounds that have been used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
