Zamia boliviana

Zamia boliviana, commonly known as the Bolivian cycad, is a member of the Zamiaceae family and is native to South America.

It is a slow-growing plant with a stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of stiff, spiral leaves. The plant produces cone-like structures, with male and female cones on separate plants, which are pollinated by insects. Zamia boliviana is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and requires well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and historical significance.
