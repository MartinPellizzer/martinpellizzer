Zygogynum fraterculus

Zygogynum fraterculus, commonly known as the Mexican yarrow, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, daisy-like flowers. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it is often found in dry, open habitats.

Zygogynum fraterculus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
