Zingiber sjooka

Zingiber sjooka, commonly known as the Sunda ginger, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers and turmeric. The plant features tall, slender stems with broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often a deep green. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in spike-like inflorescences.

Zingiber sjooka is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
