Zingiber minus

Zingiber minus, commonly known as the lesser ginger, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other well-known gingers such as turmeric and cardamom. The plant features slender, erect stems with lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often aromatic. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, typically with yellow or white petals and red or orange sepals.

Zingiber minus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
