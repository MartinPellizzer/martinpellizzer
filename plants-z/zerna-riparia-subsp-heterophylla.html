Zerna riparia subsp. heterophylla

Zerna riparia subsp.

heterophylla, commonly known as the variable riparian grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist, riparian habitats such as riverbanks and wet meadows.

The plant is characterized by its variable leaf morphology, with leaves that can be either narrow or broad depending on environmental conditions. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability to fluctuating water levels and its role in stabilizing soil along waterways.
