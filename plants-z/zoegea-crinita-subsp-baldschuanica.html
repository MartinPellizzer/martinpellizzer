Zoegea crinita subsp. baldschuanica

Zoegea crinita subsp.

baldschuanica, commonly known as the Caucasian tamarisk, is a shrub native to the Caucasus region. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, scale-like leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This subspecies is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
