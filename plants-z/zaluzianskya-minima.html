Zaluzianskya minima

Zaluzianskya minima, commonly known as the silver leaf plant, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine, dense hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It is well adapted to arid conditions and thrives in full sun with well-drained soil.

Zaluzianskya minima is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
