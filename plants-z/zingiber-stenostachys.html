Zingiber stenostachys

Zingiber stenostachys, commonly known as the narrow-stalked ginger, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers and turmeric. The plant features slender, upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green in color. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikes, often with a subtle fragrance.

Zingiber stenostachys is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its aromatic rhizomes.
