Zeltnera glandulifera

Zeltnera glandulifera, commonly known as the glandular zeltnera, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that are often covered in small, sticky secretions. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in dry, rocky soils.

Zeltnera glandulifera is often found in chaparral and scrub habitats, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
