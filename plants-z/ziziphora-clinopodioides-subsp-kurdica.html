Ziziphora clinopodioides subsp. kurdica

Ziziphora clinopodioides subsp.

kurdica, commonly known as Kurdish betony, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia. It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in temperate climates.

The plant features aromatic, narrow leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. It is often found in open woodlands and meadows at moderate elevations.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
