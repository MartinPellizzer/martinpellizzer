Ziziphus oenopolia var. fasciculata

Ziziphus oenopolia var. fasciculata, commonly known as the clustered Indian date palm, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible fruits that are similar to dates in texture and flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit yield.

Due to its adaptability, it is also used in reforestation projects in arid regions.
