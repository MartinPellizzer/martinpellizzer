Ziziphora denticulata

Ziziphora denticulata, commonly known as fringed germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant features small, pink or white flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and slightly serrated, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Ziziphora denticulata is often used in traditional medicine for its purported digestive and antispasmodic properties.
