Zingiber junceum

Zingiber junceum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ginger, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other economically important plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. Its rhizomes, though less commonly used than those of other gingers, contain bioactive compounds with potential medicinal properties.

Zingiber junceum is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and unique floral display.
