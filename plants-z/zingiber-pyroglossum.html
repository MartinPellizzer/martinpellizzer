Zingiber pyroglossum

Zingiber pyroglossum, commonly known as the flame ginger, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features tall, sword-shaped leaves that are often brightly colored, ranging from green to red and purple. The plant produces vibrant orange or red flowers that resemble flames, giving it its common name. It is a member of the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers and turmeric.

Zingiber pyroglossum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in warm, humid environments with well-draining soil.
