Zahlbrucknera salicifolia

Zahlbrucknera salicifolia, commonly known as the willow-leaf orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to the Andean regions of South America.

This perennial herb is characterized by its lanceolate, willow-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces strikingly colored flowers, typically in shades of violet or blue, arranged in dense inflorescences. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests at high altitudes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest for horticulturists and botanists alike.
