Zostera mediterranea

Zostera mediterranea, commonly known as the Mediterranean eelgrass, is a marine flowering plant belonging to the family Zosteraceae.

It is characterized by its ribbon-like, ribbon-shaped leaves that are typically 10–30 cm long and 1–2 cm wide. This plant forms dense underwater meadows in shallow coastal waters, often in areas with sandy or muddy substrates. Zostera mediterranea plays a crucial role in stabilizing sediments and providing habitat for various marine organisms.

It is also an important component of coastal ecosystems, contributing to biodiversity and carbon sequestration.
