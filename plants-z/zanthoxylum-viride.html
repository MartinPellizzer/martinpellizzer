Zanthoxylum viride

Zanthoxylum viride, commonly known as green prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Zanthoxylum viride is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland edges and thickets.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and hardy nature.
