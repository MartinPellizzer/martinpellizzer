Zerna plurinodis

Zerna plurinodis, commonly known as the multi-node grass, is a perennial plant characterized by its distinct node structure along its stems.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and blade-like, while its inflorescence consists of spikelets that produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Zerna plurinodis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
