Zerna condensata subsp. australis

Zerna condensata subsp.

australis, commonly known as the Australian clustered grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Australia. It typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences. It is characterized by its dense growth habit and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native fauna.
