Zannichellia pedicellata f. gracilis

Zannichellia pedicellata f. gracilis, commonly known as the slender pondweed, is a aquatic plant characterized by its delicate, elongated leaves and small, green flowers.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and slow-moving streams. The plant exhibits a rhizomatous growth habit, with stems that spread across the substrate. Its leaves are narrow and ribbon-like, often floating on the water surface or submerged.

The species is notable for its adaptability to varying water conditions and its role in aquatic ecosystems.
