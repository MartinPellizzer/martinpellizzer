Ziziphus macrophylla

Ziziphus macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf date palm, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, pinnate leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into edible drupes, which are often consumed by native wildlife. Ziziphus macrophylla thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to harsh climatic conditions.

Its ecological importance includes providing food and shelter for various species in its native habitat.
