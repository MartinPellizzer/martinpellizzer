Zephyranthes franciscana

Zephyranthes franciscana, commonly known as the Francis's rain lily, is a species of flowering plant native to the Caribbean.

It is a small, herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, sandy soils near coastal areas. The plant produces delicate, white or pale pink flowers that bloom after rainfall, often during the rainy season. Its bulbs are adapted to store water, allowing it to survive in seasonal wetlands.
