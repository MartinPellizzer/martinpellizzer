Zygophyllum harpago

Zygophyllum harpago, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Zygophyllaceae family, characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is well adapted to harsh, dry environments. It has a sprawling growth habit and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Zygophyllum harpago plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
