Zephyra elegans

Zephyra elegans, commonly known as the windflower, is a delicate perennial plant native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It features slender, arching stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pale blue and violet. The plant's leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, arranged alternately along the stem. Zephyra elegans thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Its blooming period typically occurs in late spring to early summer, making it a popular choice for garden borders and woodland gardens.
