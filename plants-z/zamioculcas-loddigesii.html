Zamioculcas loddigesii

Zamioculcas loddigesii, commonly known as the ZZ plant, is a member of the Araceae family and is native to eastern Africa.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its upright, dark green, waxy leaves that are often adorned with a striking white or silver pattern. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and requires minimal watering, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to store water efficiently, contributing to its drought tolerance.

The ZZ plant is also noted for its air-purifying properties, enhancing its appeal as a decorative and functional houseplant.
