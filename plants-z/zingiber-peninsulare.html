Zingiber peninsulare

Zingiber peninsulare, commonly known as the Peninsular ginger, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers such as turmeric and cardamom. The plant features tall, slender stems with broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often dark green in color. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with a strong, spicy fragrance.

Zingiber peninsulare is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its aromatic properties.
