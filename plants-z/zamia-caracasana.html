Zamia caracasana

Zamia caracasana, commonly known as the Caracas cycad, is a member of the Zamiaceae family and is native to the Caribbean region.

It is a slow-growing, evergreen plant characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of large, feathery leaves. The plant produces cones rather than flowers, with male and female cones typically occurring on separate plants. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with a dark green color and a waxy texture.

Zamia caracasana is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
