Zanthoxylum velutinum

Zanthoxylum velutinum, commonly known as the velvet tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, often grayish bark and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various birds and mammals.

Its leaves and fruits contain compounds that have been used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
