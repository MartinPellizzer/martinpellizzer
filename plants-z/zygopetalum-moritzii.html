Zygopetalum moritzii

Zygopetalum moritzii, commonly known as the Moritz's zygopetalum, is a species of orchid native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant features long, slender stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its flowers are arranged in racemes and are known for their intricate, fragrant structure.

Zygopetalum moritzii is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
