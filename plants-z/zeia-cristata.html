Zeia cristata

Zeia cristata, commonly known as the Crowned Zebra Plant, is a striking member of the Aizoaceae family, native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, waxy, blue-green leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a unique textured appearance. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in late spring, adding a splash of color to its foliage. Zeia cristata is a succulent, adapted to arid environments, storing water in its fleshy leaves.

It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
