Zygostates dasyrhiza

Zygostates dasyrhiza, commonly known as the hairy root grass, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its dense, fibrous root system and slender, tufted shoots. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, while its inflorescence consists of a panicle of small, spikelet-like flowers.

Zygostates dasyrhiza is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
