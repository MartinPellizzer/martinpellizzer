Zephyranthes pseudoconcolor

Zephyranthes pseudoconcolor, commonly known as the pink rain lily, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a bulbous herbaceous plant that produces striking pink or white flowers during the rainy season. The plant features strap-like leaves that are typically grass-like in appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate to flourish.

Zephyranthes pseudoconcolor is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
