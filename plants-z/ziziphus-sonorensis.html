Ziziphus sonorensis

Ziziphus sonorensis, commonly known as the Sonoran date palm, is a small, evergreen tree native to the Sonoran Desert of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, single trunk and fan-shaped leaves that are often covered in a whitish, waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into edible, date-like fruits. It thrives in arid environments and is well adapted to high temperatures and low rainfall.

Ziziphus sonorensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in indigenous cultures.
