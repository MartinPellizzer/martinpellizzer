Zea japonica var. vittata

Zea japonica var. vittata, commonly known as the Japanese millet or striped millet, is a herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its foliage often displays distinct white or yellow stripes, giving it a striking appearance.

It is commonly cultivated for ornamental purposes and has adapted well to various soil types and climates.
