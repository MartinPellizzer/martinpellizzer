Zanthoxylum elegantissimum

Zanthoxylum elegantissimum, commonly known as the elegant prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often pinnately arranged and have a serrated edge. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. Its bark is thick, rough, and often fissured, with a distinctive aromatic scent.

Zanthoxylum elegantissimum is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
