Zombiana

Zombiana, commonly known as the ghost plant, is a rare and mysterious species native to the dense, humid rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its pale, almost translucent leaves that appear to shimmer in the low light. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom only at night, releasing a faint, otherworldly scent. Its roots are believed to absorb nutrients from decaying organic matter, contributing to its elusive nature.

Due to its rarity and unique adaptations, Zombiana remains a subject of fascination for botanists and researchers.
