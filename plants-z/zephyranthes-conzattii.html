Zephyranthes conzattii

Zephyranthes conzattii, commonly known as the Mexican rain lily, is a striking bulbous plant native to Mexico.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in response to rainfall. The plant is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowering period typically occurs during the summer months, adding a burst of color to gardens.

Zephyranthes conzattii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a container plant.
