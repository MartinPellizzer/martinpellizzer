Zornia filifolia

Zornia filifolia, commonly known as the silky zornia, is a low-growing annual herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, thread-like leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a silky texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming from spring to early summer. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Zornia filifolia is adapted to withstand drought conditions and is considered a hardy, resilient species.
