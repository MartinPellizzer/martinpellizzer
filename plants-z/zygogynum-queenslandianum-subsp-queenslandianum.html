Zygogynum queenslandianum subsp. queenslandianum

Zygogynum queenslandianum subsp.

queenslandianum, commonly known as the Queensland waxflower, is a flowering plant native to Australia. It is characterized by its soft, waxy leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers.

The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It is often found in coastal regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
