Zaluzania subcordata

Zaluzania subcordata, commonly known as the broadleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and have a rough texture. The plant produces clusters of small, purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. Zaluzania subcordata is often found in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
