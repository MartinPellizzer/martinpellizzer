Ziziphus joazeiro

Ziziphus joazeiro, commonly known as the Brazilian date tree or joazeiro, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically opposite and have a distinctive, leathery texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that give way to edible, date-like fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid climates, making it well adapted to harsh environmental conditions.

Ziziphus joazeiro is also valued for its ecological role, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
