Zea oryzoides

Zea oryzoides, commonly known as the wild maize or teosinte, is a wild relative of modern corn.

It is a grass species native to the Americas, particularly Mexico, and belongs to the Poaceae family. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and has narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that bear small, bisexual flowers.

Zea oryzoides is characterized by its tassels and stigmas, which are important for wind pollination.
