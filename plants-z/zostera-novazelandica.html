Zostera novazelandica

Zostera novazelandica, commonly known as New Zealand sea grass, is a marine flowering plant native to the coastal waters of New Zealand.

It forms dense underwater meadows that provide critical habitat and nursery grounds for various marine species. The plant has long, ribbon-like leaves that float just below the water surface, facilitating photosynthesis. It plays a vital role in stabilizing sediments and improving water quality in its ecosystem.

Zostera novazelandica is adapted to brackish and saltwater environments, showcasing its resilience in coastal habitats.
