Zygopetalum xanthinum var. major

Zygopetalum xanthinum var. major, commonly known as the golden zygopetalum, is a hybrid orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow and orange flowers, which bloom in clusters during the warmer months. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are typically broad and lance-shaped, arranged in a fan-like pattern around the stems.

This variety is prized for its striking coloration and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in greenhouses and gardens.
