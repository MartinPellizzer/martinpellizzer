Zeyheria acaulis

Zeyheria acaulis, commonly known as the rock daisy, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth habit and is typically found growing on rocky outcrops and slopes. The plant has small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, and its leaves are gray-green, covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Zeyheria acaulis is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it well adapted to arid environments.

It is often used in rock gardens and is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness.
