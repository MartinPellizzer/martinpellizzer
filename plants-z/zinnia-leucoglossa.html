Zinnia leucoglossa

Zinnia leucoglossa, commonly known as the white-lobed zinnia, is a herbaceous annual native to the southwestern United States.

It features showy, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center, typically blooming in summer. The plant has upright, branching stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Zinnia leucoglossa is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
