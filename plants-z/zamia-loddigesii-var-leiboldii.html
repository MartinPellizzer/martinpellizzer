Zamia loddigesii var. leiboldii

Zamia loddigesii var. leiboldii, commonly known as the Leibold's cycad, is a member of the Zamiaceae family and is native to Central and South America.

It is a slow-growing, evergreen plant characterized by its stout, unbranched stem and a crown of stiff, pinnate leaves. The plant produces cone-like structures, with male and female cones typically occurring on separate plants. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate light conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and conservation status, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
