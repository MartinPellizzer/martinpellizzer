Zanthoxylum dinklagei

Zanthoxylum dinklagei, commonly known as the Dinklage's prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically 5 to 9 pairs of ovate to lanceolate leaflets, and its clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Zanthoxylum dinklagei is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
