Zappania mutabilis

Zappania mutabilis, commonly known as the variable zappania, is a rare and enigmatic plant species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, bioluminescent leaves that emit a soft glow during the night, a trait that sets it apart from most other flora. The plant's flowers are small and delicate, often appearing in clusters and displaying a range of colors depending on environmental conditions. Its growth is slow and requires specific humidity and temperature levels to thrive.

Due to its rarity and unusual characteristics, Zappania mutabilis is of great interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
