Zanthoxylum shikokianum

Zanthoxylum shikokianum, commonly known as the Shikoku prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its spiny branches and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by bright red berries. Its fruits are edible when ripe and are often used in traditional Japanese cuisine.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
