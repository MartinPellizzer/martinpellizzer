Zamia gomeziana

Zamia gomeziana, commonly known as the Gomeziana cycad, is a species of cycad native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its stout, unbranched stem and large, pinnate leaves that are often a deep green in color. The plant produces cone-like structures, with male and female cones occurring on separate plants. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in rocky or well-drained soils.

Due to its slow growth and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
