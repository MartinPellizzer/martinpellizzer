Ziziphus sinuata

Ziziphus sinuata, commonly known as the desert date or Chinese date, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that give way to edible, fleshy berries. Ziziphus sinuata is well adapted to harsh environments, with deep roots that allow it to access water from deep underground.

It is also valued for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
