Zygopetalum graminifolium

Zygopetalum graminifolium, commonly known as the grass-leaf orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its grass-like foliage, which consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces striking, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, with a distinctive three-lobed lip. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded, mountainous regions.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for both botanists and orchid enthusiasts.
