Zephyranthes fluvialis

Zephyranthes fluvialis, commonly known as the river zephyr lily, is a species of flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a bulbous perennial that produces showy, white or pale pink flowers during the spring and summer months. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along riverbanks. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically evergreen.

Zephyranthes fluvialis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
