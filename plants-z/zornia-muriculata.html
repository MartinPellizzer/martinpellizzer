Zornia muriculata

Zornia muriculata, commonly known as the hairy zornia, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright, herbaceous growth habit. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink to purple in color. Its leaves are alternate and pinnately compound, with small, hairy leaflets.

Zornia muriculata is often found in dry, disturbed soils and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
