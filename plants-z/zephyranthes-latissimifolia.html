Zephyranthes latissimifolia

Zephyranthes latissimifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf rain lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces striking, showy flowers in shades of white, yellow, or orange. The plant blooms in response to rainfall, often after the rainy season, and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils.

It is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
