Ziziphus pubiflora

Ziziphus pubiflora, commonly known as the hairy-fruited ziziphus, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red or orange when mature. It is well adapted to harsh environments, with thick, waxy leaves that reduce water loss.

Ziziphus pubiflora is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to thrive in poor soils.
