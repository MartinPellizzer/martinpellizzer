Zuvanda exacoides

Zuvanda exacoides, commonly known as the silverleaf zuvanda, is a unique flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs that help reduce water loss. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
