Ziziphus flexuosa

Ziziphus flexuosa, commonly known as the flexible buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Asia, including China and India.

It typically grows to a height of 2–5 meters and has compound leaves with 5–11 pairs of leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by red, fleshy fruits that are edible for birds and some mammals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as wastelands and forest edges.

Ziziphus flexuosa is also valued for its hardy nature and ability to tolerate environmental stress.
