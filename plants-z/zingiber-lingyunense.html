Zingiber lingyunense

Zingiber lingyunense, commonly known as the Lingyun ginger, is a rare and ornamental plant native to the mountainous regions of Yunnan, China.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers and turmeric, and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers. The plant features narrow, sword-shaped leaves and produces clusters of bright red or orange flowers with a strong, spicy scent. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in botanical gardens.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in the wild.
