Zanthoxylum paulae

Zanthoxylum paulae, commonly known as the Pauline prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are a source of food for various birds and small mammals. Its bark and leaves contain compounds that have been historically used in traditional medicine.

Zanthoxylum paulae is typically found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.
