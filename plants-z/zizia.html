Zizia

Zizia, commonly known as goldenroot, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

These plants are native to North America and are characterized by their showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Zizia species typically grow in open habitats such as meadows, prairies, and rocky areas. They have basal leaves that are often hairy and can be either lance-shaped or oval in form.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties, with roots used traditionally for their purported health benefits.
