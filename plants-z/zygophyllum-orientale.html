Zygophyllum orientale

Zygophyllum orientale, commonly known as the oriental zygophyllum, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Zygophyllaceae.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia, including parts of China, Kazakhstan, and Mongolia. The plant is characterized by its small, clustered yellow flowers and its ability to thrive in harsh, dry environments. Its leaves are typically small and fleshy, adapted to reduce water loss.

Zygophyllum orientale is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental qualities in landscaping.
