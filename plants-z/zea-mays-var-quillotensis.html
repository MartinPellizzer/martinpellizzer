Zea mays var. quillotensis

Zea mays var. quillotensis, commonly known as the Quillotense maize, is a rare and ancient variant of maize native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, compact ears and distinctive, narrow leaves. This plant exhibits a unique combination of traits that reflect its adaptation to specific environmental conditions in its native habitat. Its botanical features include a robust root system and a high degree of genetic diversity.

Due to its limited distribution and historical significance, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical and agricultural research.
