Zanthoxylum nitidum var. tomentosum

Zanthoxylum nitidum var. tomentosum, commonly known as the hairy prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by red, fleshy fruits. It is often found in moist, shaded areas of forests and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.

The hairy texture of its stems and leaves gives it its common name.
