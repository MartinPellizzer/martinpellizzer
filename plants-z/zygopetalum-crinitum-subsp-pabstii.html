Zygopetalum crinitum subsp. pabstii

Zygopetalum crinitum subsp.

pabstii, commonly known as the Pabst's hairy zygopetalum, is a rare orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Central America. It is characterized by its long, hairy sepals and petals, which give it its common name.

The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish-white, with a distinctive lip that is often marked with purple or brown.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance.
