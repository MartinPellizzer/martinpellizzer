Zingiber ramosum

Zingiber ramosum, commonly known as the hairy ginger or wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant features a rhizomatous root system and has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often dark green in color. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange and are clustered in inflorescences, adding to its ornamental value.

Zingiber ramosum is also valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects.
