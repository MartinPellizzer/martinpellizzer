Zerna lacmonica

Zerna lacmonica, commonly known as the lacmonian millet, is a small grain plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, oval-shaped grains that are typically yellow or golden in color. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid climates, making it well-suited for cultivation in regions with limited water resources. Its seeds are rich in carbohydrates and have been used as a traditional food source by local communities for centuries.

Due to its resilience and nutritional value, Zerna lacmonica is gaining attention as a potential crop for sustainable agriculture.
