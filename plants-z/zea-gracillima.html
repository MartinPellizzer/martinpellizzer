Zea gracillima

Zea gracillima, commonly known as the slender corn or dwarf corn, is a species of grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered inflorescences that produce tiny grains. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance.

Botanically, it belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is closely related to other members of the Zea genus.
