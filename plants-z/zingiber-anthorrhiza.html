Zingiber anthorrhiza

Zingiber anthorrhiza, commonly known as the white ginger or mountain ginger, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other economically important spices like ginger and turmeric. The plant features a rhizomatous root system and lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often hairy on the underside. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in inflorescences, which are typically borne on long, slender stems.

Zingiber anthorrhiza is valued not only for its ornamental qualities but also for its potential medicinal properties.
