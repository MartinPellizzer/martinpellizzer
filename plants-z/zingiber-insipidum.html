Zingiber insipidum

Zingiber insipidum, commonly known as the bland ginger, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other economically important plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant features a rhizomatous stem with narrow, linear leaves that are typically green in color. Its inflorescence consists of showy, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters.

While not widely cultivated for its flavor, it is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and potential medicinal properties.
