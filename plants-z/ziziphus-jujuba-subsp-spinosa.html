Ziziphus jujuba subsp. spinosa

Ziziphus jujuba subsp.

spinosa, commonly known as the thorny Chinese date, is a shrub or small tree native to China and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its spiny branches and small, yellowish-green leaves that are opposite in arrangement.

The plant produces small, reddish-brown fruits that are edible and have a sweet, honey-like flavor. It thrives in arid and semi-arid climates, showing high drought tolerance.

This subspecies is often used in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant due to its hardy nature and distinctive appearance.
