Zygopetalum taurinum

Zygopetalum taurinum, commonly known as the Taurine zygopetalum, is a species of orchid native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in late spring. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its flowers are usually pale pink to white, with a distinctive lip that is broader than the other petals.

Zygopetalum taurinum is well-suited for cultivation in cool, humid environments and is prized for its ornamental value.
