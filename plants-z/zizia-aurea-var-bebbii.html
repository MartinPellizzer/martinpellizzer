Zizia aurea var. bebbii

Zizia aurea var. bebbii, commonly known as Bebb's goldenrod, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a soft texture.

Bebb's goldenrod is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its ornamental appeal in gardens.
