Ziziphora clinopodioides var. benthamii

Ziziphora clinopodioides var. benthamii, commonly known as bentham's woodruff, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers during the spring and summer months. Its root system is fibrous, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine.
