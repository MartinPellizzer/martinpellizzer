Ziziphus parryi var. microphylla

Ziziphus parryi var. microphylla, commonly known as Parry's ziziphus, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically less than 1 inch in length. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into edible, red berries. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and desert regions.

Its botanical features make it well-adapted to drought conditions and it plays a role in local ecosystems.
