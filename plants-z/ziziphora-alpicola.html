Ziziphora alpicola

Ziziphora alpicola, commonly known as Alpine ziziphora, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. This perennial herb typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool and dry conditions. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces small, nut-like fruits.

Ziziphora alpicola is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
