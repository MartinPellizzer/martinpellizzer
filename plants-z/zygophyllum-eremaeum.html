Zygophyllum eremaeum

Zygophyllum eremaeum, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Australia.

It is a perennial shrub that thrives in hot, dry climates with poor soil conditions. The plant has small, fleshy leaves that help reduce water loss, and it produces yellow or white flowers during the cooler months. Its root system is deep, allowing it to access underground water sources.

Zygophyllum eremaeum plays an important role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
