Zonanthemis clementina

Zonanthemis clementina, commonly known as the Clementine Zonanthemum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, showy yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and has narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its bright, cheerful blooms.
