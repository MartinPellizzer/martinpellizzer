Zanthoxylum annulatum

Zanthoxylum annulatum, commonly known as the Chinese prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Its bark and leaves contain various bioactive compounds, making it valuable in herbal remedies.

Zanthoxylum annulatum thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.
