Ziziphus jujuba f. apyrena

Ziziphus jujuba f. apyrena, commonly known as the hairless Chinese date, is a cultivar of the jujube tree characterized by its reduced or absent pubescence on the fruit surface.

It is native to China and is prized for its sweet, fleshy fruit that is often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. The plant typically grows as a deciduous shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a serrated margin, while the flowers are small and yellowish-green.

The fruit develops in clusters and matures to a deep reddish-brown color, offering both ornamental and edible value.
