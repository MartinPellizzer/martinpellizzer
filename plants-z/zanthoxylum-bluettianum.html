Zanthoxylum bluettianum

Zanthoxylum bluettianum, commonly known as the blue-tailed mallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges, and its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
