Zamia linifolia

Zamia linifolia, commonly known as the threadleaf cycad, is a member of the Zamiaceae family and is native to the southeastern United States.

It is a slow-growing plant with a stout, unbranched stem and a crown of narrow, strap-like leaves. The plant produces cones rather than flowers, with male and female cones typically occurring on separate plants. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Due to its unique appearance and historical significance, it is often cultivated for its aesthetic value and ecological role.
