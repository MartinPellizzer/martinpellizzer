Zanthoxylum apiculatum

Zanthoxylum apiculatum, commonly known as the prickly ash or American prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are edible for some wildlife but considered toxic to humans.

Zanthoxylum apiculatum is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical uses in traditional medicine.
