Zingiber confine

Zingiber confine, commonly known as the narrow ginger, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like turmeric and cardamom. The plant features slender, upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with three petals and three sepals, and are arranged in a spike-like inflorescence.

Zingiber confine is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
