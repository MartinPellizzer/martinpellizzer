Zygophyllum amblyocarpum

Zygophyllum amblyocarpum, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, pale green leaves and yellowish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has a deep root system that allows it to survive in harsh, dry environments. Its leaves are adapted to reduce water loss through their thick, waxy coating.

Zygophyllum amblyocarpum plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and is often found in desert ecosystems.
