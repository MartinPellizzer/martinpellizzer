Zornia Diphylla

Zornia diphylla is a unique flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is known for its distinctive two-leaf structure, from which it gets its scientific name. This species thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing remarkable adaptability to harsh conditions. Its small, delicate flowers often bloom in shades of pink and white, adding a touch of beauty to its rugged habitat. Despite its modest appearance, Zornia diphylla plays an important role in local ecosystems and is a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists alike.
