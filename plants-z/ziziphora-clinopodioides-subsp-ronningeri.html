Ziziphora clinopodioides subsp. ronningeri

Ziziphora clinopodioides subsp.

ronningeri, commonly known as Ronning's betony, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features aromatic, narrow leaves that are often hairy and arranged in opposite pairs along the stems.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and aromatic properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
