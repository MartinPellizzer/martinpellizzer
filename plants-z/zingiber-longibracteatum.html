Zingiber longibracteatum

Zingiber longibracteatum, commonly known as the long-bract ginger, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers and turmeric. The plant features narrow, elongated leaves and produces striking, fragrant flowers with yellow petals and red bracts. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

Zingiber longibracteatum is valued for its ornamental beauty and aromatic properties.
