Zephyranthes aurata

Zephyranthes aurata, commonly known as the rainbow lily or night bloomer, is a species of flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a bulbous perennial that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and red. The plant is known for its ability to bloom at night, often opening its flowers in response to cooler temperatures and darkness. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are evergreen and often used in floral arrangements.

Zephyranthes aurata is well adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and requires well-drained soil and partial to full sun exposure.
