Zosterophyllanthos adelphe

Zosterophyllanthos adelphe, commonly known as the golden star fern, is a rare and unique plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Zosterophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that resemble the structure of ancient marine plants. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistent moisture and well-draining soil to flourish. Its botanical significance lies in its evolutionary link to early land plants, offering insights into the transition from aquatic to terrestrial life.
