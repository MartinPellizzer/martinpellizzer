Zingiber ottensii

Zingiber ottensii, commonly known as the white ginger or white-veined ginger, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other economically important plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, lance-shaped leaves that are often marked with distinctive white veins. Its flowers are typically pale yellow to white and are borne in spikes, adding ornamental value to the plant.

Zingiber ottensii is valued not only for its aesthetic appeal but also for its potential medicinal properties.
