Ziziphus kunstleri

Ziziphus kunstleri, commonly known as the desert date or Indian date tree, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched structure and small, leathery leaves that are adapted to conserve water. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, sweet fruits that are often consumed by wildlife and humans. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for desert environments.

Ziziphus kunstleri plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing food for various animals in its native habitat.
