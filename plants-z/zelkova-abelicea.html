Zelkova abelicea

Zelkova abelicea, commonly known as the Albanian zelkova, is a deciduous tree native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Ulmaceae family and is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras as its fruit. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and can reach heights of up to 20 meters.

Zelkova abelicea is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in the region.
