Zanthoxylum dimorphophyllum var. deminutum

Zanthoxylum dimorphophyllum var. deminutum, commonly known as the dwarf prickly ash, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, spiny leaves and clusters of yellowish-green flowers that appear in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for certain wildlife. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the Rhamnaceae family.
