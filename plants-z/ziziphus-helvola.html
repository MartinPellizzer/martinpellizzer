Ziziphus helvola

Ziziphus helvola, commonly known as the white ziziphus, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically oval or elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Ziziphus helvola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
