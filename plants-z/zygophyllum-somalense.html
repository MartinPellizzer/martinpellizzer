Zygophyllum somalense

Zygophyllum somalense, commonly known as the Somali jointweed, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Somalia.

It belongs to the family Zygophyllaceae and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and succulent, fleshy leaves adapted to conserve water. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is well-suited to withstand high temperatures and low rainfall.

Its structure and morphology reflect its adaptation to harsh, semi-desert environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
