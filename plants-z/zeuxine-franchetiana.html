Zeuxine franchetiana

Zeuxine franchetiana, commonly known as the Chinese bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to China.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are erect and branched.

Zeuxine franchetiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
