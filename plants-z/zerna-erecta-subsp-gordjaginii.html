Zerna erecta subsp. gordjaginii

Zerna erecta subsp.

gordjaginii, commonly known as the Armenian wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, cylindrical stems that often bear a fine layer of hairs.

The leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration, and the plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate climates with cold winters.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and aesthetic qualities.
