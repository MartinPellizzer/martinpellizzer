Zoysia matrella

Zoysia matrella, commonly known as Japanese grass, is a warm-season turfgrass native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, fine-textured foliage and a deep green color, making it popular for lawns and sports fields. The plant forms a sod-like growth habit, with rhizomes and stolons that help it spread and establish quickly. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, requiring moderate water and fertilizer.

Zoysia matrella is also known for its durability and ability to recover from wear and tear.
