Zornia filifolia var. subeglandulosa

Zornia filifolia var. subeglandulosa, commonly known as the threadleaf zornia, is a herbaceous annual plant native to North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, filamentous leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pale blue to purple in color. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open fields, roadsides, and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and soil stabilization.
