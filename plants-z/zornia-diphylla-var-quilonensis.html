Zornia diphylla var. quilonensis

Zornia diphylla var. quilonensis, commonly known as the Quilon zornia, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a herbaceous species characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces small, three-lobed seed pods.

Zornia diphylla var. quilonensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
