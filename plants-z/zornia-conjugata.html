Zornia conjugata

Zornia conjugata, commonly known as the common zornia, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Zornia conjugata is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it an important plant in ecological restoration efforts.
