Zapoteca cruzii

Zapoteca cruzii, commonly known as the Zapotec sunflower, is a species of flowering plant native to the highlands of Oaxaca, Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies, sunflowers, and other composite flowers. The plant is characterized by its large, showy yellow-orange flower heads surrounded by green bracts. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or disturbed areas.

Zapoteca cruzii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has cultural significance among indigenous communities in the region.
