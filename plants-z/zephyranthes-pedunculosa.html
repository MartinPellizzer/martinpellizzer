Zephyranthes pedunculosa

Zephyranthes pedunculosa, commonly known as the rain lily, is a striking bulbous plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, white or pale pink flowers that bloom after rainfall. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes. Its flowers are often short-lived but highly fragrant, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Zephyranthes pedunculosa is also valued for its ability to naturalize and spread easily in suitable climates.
