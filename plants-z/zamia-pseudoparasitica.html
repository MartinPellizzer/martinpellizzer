Zamia pseudoparasitica

Zamia pseudoparasitica, commonly known as the parasitic zamia, is a member of the Zamiaceae family and is native to Mexico.

It is a cycad-like plant that exhibits a unique growth form, characterized by a short trunk and a rosette of compound leaves. The plant is named for its ability to derive some of its nutrients from a symbiotic relationship with fungi, though it is not fully parasitic. Its leaves are typically pinnately divided and can reach significant lengths, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.
