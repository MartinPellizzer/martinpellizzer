Zanthoxylum subserratum

Zanthoxylum subserratum, commonly known as the serrated prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are a food source for various birds and small mammals.

Its bark and leaves contain compounds that have been used traditionally in herbal medicine for their stimulant and analgesic properties.
