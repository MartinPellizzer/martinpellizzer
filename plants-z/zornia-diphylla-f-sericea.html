Zornia diphylla f. sericea

Zornia diphylla f. sericea, commonly known as the silky zornia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving the plant its common name. The flowers are small, clustered in dense spikes, and typically have five petals with a distinctive pink to purple coloration. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to contribute to local biodiversity.
