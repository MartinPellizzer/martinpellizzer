Zamzela rugosa

Zamzela rugosa, commonly known as the rough-leaved zamzela, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine, rough hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in spring. Zamzela rugosa thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive foliage and delicate flowers.
