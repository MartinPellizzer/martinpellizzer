Zygopetalum guttatum

Zygopetalum guttatum, commonly known as the spotted zygopetalum, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that often feature intricate patterns and contrasting markings. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store moisture in its natural habitat.

Zygopetalum guttatum is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and unique floral structure.
