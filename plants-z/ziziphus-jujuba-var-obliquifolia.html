Ziziphus jujuba var. obliquifolia

Ziziphus jujuba var. obliquifolia, commonly known as the Chinese date or red date tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its narrow, oblique leaves that are often curved and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that give way to edible, sweet, and sticky fruits known as dates. It thrives in arid and semi-arid climates and is well-suited for cultivation in regions with well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities as well as its fruit, which has been used in traditional medicine and culinary practices.
