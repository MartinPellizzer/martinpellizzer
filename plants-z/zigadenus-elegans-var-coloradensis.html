Zigadenus elegans var. coloradensis

Zigadenus elegans var. coloradensis, commonly known as the Colorado larkspur, is a toxic perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and grows in open, rocky habitats. It contains potent alkaloids that can be lethal to humans and animals if ingested.

Due to its toxicity, it is often avoided by livestock and wildlife.
