Zygopetalum beaumontii

Zygopetalum beaumontii, commonly known as the Beaumont's zygopetalum, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that typically feature a dark, velvety lip and intricate patterns. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, enabling it to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, often arranged in a fan shape, and it produces fragrant blooms during the warmer months.

This orchid is popular among horticulturists for its ornamental value and adaptability to cultivated conditions.
