Zapoteca scutellifera

Zapoteca scutellifera, commonly known as the Mexican flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its showy, bright red or orange flowers that appear in clusters, typically during the dry season. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth texture. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Zapoteca scutellifera is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils in full sun.
