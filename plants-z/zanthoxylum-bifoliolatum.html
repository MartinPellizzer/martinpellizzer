Zanthoxylum bifoliolatum

Zanthoxylum bifoliolatum, commonly known as the two-leaved prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its compound leaves with two small leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by red, fleshy fruits.

Its bark and leaves contain toxic compounds, making it an important species in traditional medicine and ecological studies.
