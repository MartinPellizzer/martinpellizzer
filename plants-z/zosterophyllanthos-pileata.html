Zosterophyllanthos pileata

Zosterophyllanthos pileata, commonly known as the mountain fern, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale blue in color. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found growing in rocky or forested areas.
