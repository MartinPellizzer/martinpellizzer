Zeia dasystachya

Zeia dasystachya, commonly known as the broad-stemmed grass, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets, typical of grass species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Zeia dasystachya is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its upright growth habit.
