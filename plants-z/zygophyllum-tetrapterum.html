Zygophyllum tetrapterum

Zygophyllum tetrapterum, commonly known as the four-winged sandmat, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the family Zygophyllaceae and is characterized by its small, greenish-yellow flowers and distinctive four-winged fruits. The plant is well adapted to harsh environments, with small leaves and a deep root system to conserve water. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.

Zygophyllum tetrapterum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various desert fauna.
