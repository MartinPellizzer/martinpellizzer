Zygia ocumarensis

Zygia ocumarensis, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Erythroxylum ocumarensis*, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible and contain seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Zygia ocumarensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
