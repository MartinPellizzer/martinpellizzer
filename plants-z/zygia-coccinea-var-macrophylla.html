Zygia coccinea var. macrophylla

Zygia coccinea var. macrophylla, commonly known as the red zygia or red-flowered zygia, is a striking ornamental plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves that are often a deep green, providing a striking contrast to its showy blooms. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, requiring warm temperatures and well-drained soil.

Zygia coccinea var. macrophylla is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and can be grown as a houseplant or outdoors in suitable conditions.
