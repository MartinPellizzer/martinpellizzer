Zornia surinamensis

Zornia surinamensis, commonly known as Surinam zornia, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. It has hairy stems and produces pods that contain several seeds.

Zornia surinamensis is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
