Ziziphus chloroxylon

Ziziphus chloroxylon, commonly known as the white ziziphus, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Rhamnaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and small, yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible, fleshy berries.

It is adapted to survive in harsh environments with low rainfall and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
