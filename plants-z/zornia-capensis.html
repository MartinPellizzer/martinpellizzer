Zornia capensis

Zornia capensis, commonly known as the Cape zornia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas. It produces small, dry pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Zornia capensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
