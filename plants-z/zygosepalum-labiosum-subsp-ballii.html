Zygosepalum labiosum subsp. ballii

Zygosepalum labiosum subsp.

ballii, commonly known as the Ball's zygosepalum, is a rare and striking member of the Orchidaceae family. It is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that feature a labellum with a prominent, fleshy lip.

The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in tropical regions of Southeast Asia. Its foliage consists of broad, leathery leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle.

Due to its rarity and unique morphology, it is highly valued in horticultural collections.
