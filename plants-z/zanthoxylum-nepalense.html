Zanthoxylum nepalense

Zanthoxylum nepalense, commonly known as the Nepal prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Himalayan regions of Nepal, India, and Bhutan.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated margins and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically red or orange when mature, containing several seeds. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
