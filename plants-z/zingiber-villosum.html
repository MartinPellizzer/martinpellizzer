Zingiber villosum

Zingiber villosum, commonly known as hairy ginger, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers such as ginger and turmeric. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of pale yellow to white flowers. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and covered in fine, hairy texture, giving it its common name.

Zingiber villosum is often grown for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses in local cultures.
