Zornia diphylla var. glabra

Zornia diphylla var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth zornia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing annual herb with opposite, ovate leaves that are smooth and hairless. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in loose clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in open woods and along stream banks.

The species is characterized by its simple, unbranched stems and its ability to spread through seed dispersal.
