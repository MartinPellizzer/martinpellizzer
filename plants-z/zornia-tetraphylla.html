Zornia tetraphylla

Zornia tetraphylla, commonly known as the four-leaf zornia, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, though it can sometimes have four leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, and it is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats.

Zornia tetraphylla is an annual herb that plays a role in soil stabilization and provides forage for wildlife.
