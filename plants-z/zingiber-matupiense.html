Zingiber matupiense

Zingiber matupiense, commonly known as the Matupi ginger, is a perennial herb native to the rainforests of South America, particularly in Ecuador and Peru.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other economically important plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant is characterized by its narrow, sword-shaped leaves and produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in a spike. Its rhizomes, similar to those of ginger, are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Zingiber matupiense is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
