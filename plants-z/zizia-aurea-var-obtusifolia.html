Zizia aurea var. obtusifolia

Zizia aurea var. obtusifolia, commonly known as the golden saxifrage, is a perennial herb native to the North American prairies.

It features basal leaves that are broadly ovate to elliptic with rounded tips, and it produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in late spring. The plant has a short, stout stem that supports its foliage and floral display. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open habitats such as meadows and rocky areas.

Its showy blooms make it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
