Zingiber citrinum

Zingiber citrinum, commonly known as the golden ginger, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender rhizomes that produce a yellowish-orange color, giving it its common name. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a smooth, waxy surface.

Zingiber citrinum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique foliage and mild, citrus-like fragrance.
