Zygocolax veitchii

Zygocolax veitchii, commonly known as the Veitch's zygocactus, is a unique member of the Cactaceae family native to the arid regions of Mexico.

This plant is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in drought conditions. Its flowers are typically showy, with bright colors such as yellow or orange, and bloom during the warmer months. The plant has a branching structure and produces small, spineless areoles, which are typical features of cacti.

It requires well-draining soil and minimal watering to maintain its health and promote flowering.
