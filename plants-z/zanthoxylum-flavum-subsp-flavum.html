Zanthoxylum flavum subsp. flavum

Zanthoxylum flavum subsp.

flavum, commonly known as the yellow prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States. It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated margins and small, yellowish-green flowers.

The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for some animals but generally considered toxic to humans. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.

Its aromatic leaves and distinctive appearance make it a notable species in its native habitat.
