Zinnia hybrida

Zinnia hybrida, commonly known as the garden zinnia, is a popular annual flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like blooms in a variety of colors including red, yellow, orange, and white. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are composed of a central disc surrounded by petal-like ray florets, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Zinnias are known for their resilience and ability to thrive in full sun with well-drained soil.
