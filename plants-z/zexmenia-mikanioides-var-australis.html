Zexmenia mikanioides var. australis

Zexmenia mikanioides var. australis, commonly known as the Australian climbing fern, is a variant of the Zexmenia genus native to Australia.

It is a perennial, herbaceous plant characterized by its climbing habit and intricate, fern-like foliage. The plant features slender, wiry stems that twine around supports, and its leaves are pinnately divided into numerous narrow, lance-shaped segments. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understoreys or along stream banks.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by spore-bearing structures, typical of its fern-like morphology.
