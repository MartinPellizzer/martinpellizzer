Zanthoxylum monogynum subsp. bolivianum

Zanthoxylum monogynum subsp.

bolivianum, commonly known as the Bolivian prickly ash, is a member of the Rutaceae family and is native to the Andean regions of South America. It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers.

The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various bird species. Its bark and leaves contain bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional medicine.

The subspecies is distinguished by its unique morphological features and geographic distribution.
