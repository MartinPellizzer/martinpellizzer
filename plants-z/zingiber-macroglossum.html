Zingiber macroglossum

Zingiber macroglossum, commonly known as the large-tongued ginger, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other well-known gingers such as turmeric and cardamom. The plant features tall, slender stems with broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. Its flowering stems produce clusters of fragrant, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange.

Zingiber macroglossum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
