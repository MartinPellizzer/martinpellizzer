Zingiber macrocephalum

Zingiber macrocephalum, commonly known as the giant ginger or white ginger, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers and turmeric. The plant is characterized by its large, showy white to pale pink flowers that bloom in summer. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine and as a spice, though less commonly than other gingers.

The leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often reaching several feet in length.
