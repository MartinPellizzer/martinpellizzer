Ziziphus microphylla

Ziziphus microphylla, commonly known as the dwarf date palm, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, leathery leaves that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, date-like fruits that are sweet and fleshy. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid environments.

Ziziphus microphylla is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and unique fruiting habit.
