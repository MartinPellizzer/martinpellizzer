Ziziphus mucronata var. inermis

Ziziphus mucronata var. inermis, commonly known as the white thorned date palm, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, thornless stems and compound leaves with long, narrow leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into edible, sweet fruits resembling dates. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its fruit, which is used in traditional cuisine.
