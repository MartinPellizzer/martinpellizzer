Zanthoxylum perrottetii

Zanthoxylum perrottetii, commonly known as the Chinese prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are edible for some animals but not typically for humans. Its bark and roots have been used in traditional Chinese medicine for their purported medicinal properties.

Zanthoxylum perrottetii is often found in forested areas and thrives in temperate climates.
