Zannichellia repens var. viridis

Zannichellia repens var. viridis, commonly known as the green water clover, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, often giving it a clover-like appearance. The plant produces tiny, greenish flowers that are typically borne on short stalks. It thrives in nutrient-rich, shallow waters and is often found in ponds and slow-moving streams.

Its presence is an indicator of good water quality and is important for aquatic ecosystems.
