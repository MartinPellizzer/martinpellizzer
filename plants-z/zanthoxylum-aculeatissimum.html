Zanthoxylum aculeatissimum

Zanthoxylum aculeatissimum, commonly known as the prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often serrated and have a strong, aromatic scent. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that give way to red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Zanthoxylum aculeatissimum is also noted for its medicinal properties and has been used by Indigenous peoples for various healing purposes.
