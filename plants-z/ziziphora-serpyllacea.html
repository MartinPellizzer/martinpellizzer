Ziziphora serpyllacea

Ziziphora serpyllacea, commonly known as field woundwort, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and thrives in open habitats such as grasslands and waste areas. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, pale blue or purple flowers arranged in spikes. Its aromatic foliage and showy inflorescences make it a notable species in its ecological niche.

Ziziphora serpyllacea is also known for its medicinal properties, historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
