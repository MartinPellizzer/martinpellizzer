Zanthoxylum senegalense

Zanthoxylum senegalense, commonly known as the Senegalese prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces bright red berries that are edible for some animals but toxic to humans. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Zanthoxylum senegalense is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
