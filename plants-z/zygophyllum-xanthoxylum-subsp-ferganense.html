Zygophyllum xanthoxylum subsp. ferganense

Zygophyllum xanthoxylum subsp.

ferganense, commonly known as the Fergana sandmat, is a low-growing shrub native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are adapted to arid environments.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in summer. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to thrive in sandy and rocky soils.

It is often found in desert and semi-desert regions, playing a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.
