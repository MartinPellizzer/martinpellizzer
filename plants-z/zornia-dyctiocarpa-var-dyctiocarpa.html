Zornia dyctiocarpa var. dyctiocarpa

Zornia dyctiocarpa var. dyctiocarpa, commonly known as the hairy zornia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its distinctive hairy stems and leaves contribute to its unique botanical appearance.

It is often found in open woodlands and along stream banks.
