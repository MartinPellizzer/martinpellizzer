Zanthoxylum piperitum f. rotundatum

Zanthoxylum piperitum f. rotundatum, commonly known as the Japanese prickly ash, is a variant of the Chinese prickly ash.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia, particularly Japan. The plant is characterized by its rounded, compound leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. Its fruit is a red, fleshy drupe that contains several seeds.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
