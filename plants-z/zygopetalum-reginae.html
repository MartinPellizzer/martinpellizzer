Zygopetalum reginae

Zygopetalum reginae, commonly known as the Queen's zygopetalum, is a hybrid orchid species prized for its vibrant and intricate flower structure.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its pendulous, fragrant blooms that often display a striking combination of colors, including shades of pink, purple, and white. The plant typically features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that support its foliage and floral spikes. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Zygopetalum reginae thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and well-draining soil for optimal growth.
