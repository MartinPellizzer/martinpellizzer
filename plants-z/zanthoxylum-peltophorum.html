Zanthoxylum peltophorum

Zanthoxylum peltophorum, commonly known as the red prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds and small mammals.

Its bark and leaves contain compounds that have been used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
