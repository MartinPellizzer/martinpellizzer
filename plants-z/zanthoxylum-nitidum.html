Zanthoxylum nitidum

Zanthoxylum nitidum, commonly known as the Chinese prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible and often used in traditional Chinese cuisine. Its bark and leaves contain bioactive compounds, making it valuable in herbal medicine.

Zanthoxylum nitidum thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.
