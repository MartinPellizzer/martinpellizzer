Zedoaria

Zedoaria, commonly known as curcuma, is a rhizomatous plant belonging to the ginger family, Zingiberaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including India, Indonesia, and the Philippines. The plant's underground stem, or rhizome, is the primary part used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. Zedoaria contains various bioactive compounds, such as curcuminoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, while its flowers are typically yellow or orange in color.
