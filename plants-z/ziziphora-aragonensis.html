Ziziphora aragonensis

Ziziphora aragonensis, commonly known as Spanish thyme or Aragon thyme, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Ziziphora aragonensis is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
