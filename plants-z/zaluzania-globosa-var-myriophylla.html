Zaluzania globosa var. myriophylla

Zaluzania globosa var. myriophylla, commonly known as the Mexican globeflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its spherical, multi-branched inflorescence that resembles a globe, hence its common name. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are palmately lobed and covered in fine, hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Zaluzania globosa var. myriophylla thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
