Zingiber didymoglossum

Zingiber didymoglossum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf ginger, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other well-known gingers such as turmeric and cardamom. The plant features long, narrow leaves and produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Botanically, it is characterized by its aromatic rhizomes and distinctive floral structure.
