Zollingeria

Zollingeria, commonly known as the false arum, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Araceae.

It consists of a single species, Zollingeria mexicana, native to Mexico. The plant is characterized by its striking, spadix-like inflorescence surrounded by a colorful spathe, typical of the arum family. Its leaves are usually arrow-shaped and have a waxy texture.

Zollingeria is often cultivated for its ornamental value in tropical and subtropical gardens.
