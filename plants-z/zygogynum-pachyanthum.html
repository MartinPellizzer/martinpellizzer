Zygogynum pachyanthum

Zygogynum pachyanthum, commonly known as the golden sand orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers that bloom during the dry season. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Zygogynum pachyanthum is an important species for both ecological and cultural reasons in its native habitat.
