Zanthoxylum somalense

Zanthoxylum somalense, commonly known as Somali prickly ash, is a shrub or small tree native to the dry regions of Somalia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its spiny branches and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by red, fleshy fruits that are edible for some animals. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along dry riverbeds.

Due to its hardy nature, it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.
