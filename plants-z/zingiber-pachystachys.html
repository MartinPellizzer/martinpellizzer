Zingiber pachystachys

Zingiber pachystachys, commonly known as the giant ginger or elephant ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

Zingiber pachystachys thrives in humid, shady environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
