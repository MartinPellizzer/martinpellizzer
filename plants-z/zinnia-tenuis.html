Zinnia tenuis

Zinnia tenuis, commonly known as the slender zinnia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with composite flower heads.

Zinnia tenuis is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
