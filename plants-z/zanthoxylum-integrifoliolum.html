Zanthoxylum integrifoliolum

Zanthoxylum integrifoliolum, commonly known as the smooth prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with smooth, entire margins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. Its bark and leaves contain compounds that have been used in traditional medicine.

Zanthoxylum integrifoliolum is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
