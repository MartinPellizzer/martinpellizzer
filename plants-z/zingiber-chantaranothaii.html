Zingiber chantaranothaii

Zingiber chantaranothaii, commonly known as the Thai ginger or Chantaranothai ginger, is a rare species of ginger native to Thailand.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, elongated rhizomes. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, pale yellow flowers. It is valued for its unique flavor and is used in traditional Thai cuisine and medicine.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a threatened species.
