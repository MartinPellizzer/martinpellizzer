Zerna maroccana

Zerna maroccana, commonly known as the Moroccan millet, is a small annual grass native to North Africa.

It produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers and has narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought. Its seeds are often used as a traditional food source in Moroccan cuisine.

Zerna maroccana is also valued for its role in traditional agriculture and soil conservation.
