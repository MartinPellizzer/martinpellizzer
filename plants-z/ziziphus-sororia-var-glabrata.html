Ziziphus sororia var. glabrata

Ziziphus sororia var. glabrata, commonly known as the smooth ziziphus, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, spine-tipped leaves and clusters of yellowish-green flowers that appear in the spring. The plant produces small, reddish-brown fruits that are edible but not widely cultivated for commercial purposes. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils and along dry streambeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in desert landscapes.
