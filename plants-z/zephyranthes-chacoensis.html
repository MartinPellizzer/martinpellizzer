Zephyranthes chacoensis

Zephyranthes chacoensis, commonly known as the Chaco rain lily, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is a bulbous perennial that produces showy, white or pale pink flowers during the rainy season. The plant is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in wetlands or marshy areas. Its flowers are typically short-lived, opening in the early morning and closing by midday.

Zephyranthes chacoensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with moist soil.
