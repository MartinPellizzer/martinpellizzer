Zygopetalum coronarium

Zygopetalum coronarium, commonly known as the crown orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically feature a dark purple or violet coloration with intricate patterns. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, meaning it grows in clusters with each new growth emerging from a rhizome. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its compact and elegant appearance.

Zygopetalum coronarium thrives in humid, well-drained environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
