Zea mays var. striatiamylacea

Zea mays var. striatiamylacea, commonly known as the striped maize variant, is a less common cultivar of maize characterized by its distinctive striped patterns on the kernels.

This variety exhibits a unique combination of colors, often featuring a mix of white, yellow, and red hues, which are influenced by genetic factors and environmental conditions. The plant typically grows as an annual grass with tall, sturdy stalks and broad, lance-shaped leaves. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and an inflorescence composed of tassels and ear-like structures called ears.

This variety is primarily of interest to botanists and agricultural researchers due to its genetic diversity and potential for breeding programs.
