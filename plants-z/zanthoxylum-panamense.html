Zanthoxylum panamense

Zanthoxylum panamense, commonly known as Panama prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. Its bark and leaves contain bioactive compounds, making it valuable in herbal remedies.

Zanthoxylum panamense thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, well-drained soils.
