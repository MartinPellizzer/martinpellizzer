Zosima suffruticosa

Zosima suffruticosa, commonly known as the creeping zosima, is a shrub-like plant native to certain regions of South America.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged in a dense, mat-forming structure. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in axillary positions. Its fruit is a dry capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Zosima suffruticosa is adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a resilient species in challenging habitats.
