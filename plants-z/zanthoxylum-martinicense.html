Zanthoxylum martinicense

Zanthoxylum martinicense, commonly known as the Mexican prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically alternate and consist of 5 to 9 elliptical leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that give way to red, fleshy berries in the fall. Its bark is rough and often fissured, with a distinctive aromatic scent.

Zanthoxylum martinicense is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
