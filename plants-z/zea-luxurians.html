Zea luxurians

Zea luxurians, commonly known as the giant bamboo grass, is a tall, perennial grass native to the tropical regions of South America.

It can grow up to 10 meters in height, with narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense clusters at the top of its stalks. Its rhizomes are fibrous and can spread rapidly, making it an aggressive colonizer in its native habitat.

Zea luxurians is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential in erosion control and as a sustainable resource.
