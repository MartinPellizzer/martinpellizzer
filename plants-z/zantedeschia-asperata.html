Zantedeschia asperata

Zantedeschia asperata, commonly known as the naked swamp lily, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features striking, arrow-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white, waxy hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces large, showy white or pale yellow flowers with a prominent, spadix-like structure surrounded by a spathe. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections worldwide.
