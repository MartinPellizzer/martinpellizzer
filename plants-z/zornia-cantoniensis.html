Zornia cantoniensis

Zornia cantoniensis, commonly known as the Chinese zornia, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, which bloom in the spring. Zornia cantoniensis is often found in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
