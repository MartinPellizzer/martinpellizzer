Zephyranthes moctezumae

Zephyranthes moctezumae, commonly known as the Mexican rain lily, is a striking bulbous plant native to Central America.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, star-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and red. The plant is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers. It blooms in the spring and is known for its ability to naturalize easily in suitable climates.

Its ornamental value and ease of care make it a popular choice among gardeners.
