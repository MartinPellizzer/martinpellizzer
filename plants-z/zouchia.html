Zouchia

Zouchia, commonly known as the Chinese lantern plant, is a member of the nightshade family (Solanaceae).

It is characterized by its vibrant, papery bracts that resemble small lanterns, which are often used in ornamental gardening. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial, reaching heights of up to 2 feet. Its flowers are small and yellow, clustered in dense inflorescences, while the fruit is a fleshy, orange-red berry.

Zouchia is native to Asia and is cultivated for its striking foliage and unique fruit structure.
