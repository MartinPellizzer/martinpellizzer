Zebrina pendula f. quadricolor

Zebrina pendula f. quadricolor, commonly known as the four-color ivy.

This hybrid cultivar is a trailing plant with striking variegated foliage featuring shades of white, green, and pink. It belongs to the Araceae family and is native to South America. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by bright red berries.

It thrives in bright, indirect light and is often used in hanging baskets or as a trailing plant in indoor gardens.
