Zelkova × verschaffeltii

Zelkova × verschaffeltii, commonly known as the hybrid zelkova, is a deciduous tree resulting from the cross between Zelkova carpinifolia and Zelkova serrata.

It exhibits characteristics of both parent species, featuring a broad, rounded crown and smooth, gray bark. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras as its fruit. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for urban landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often planted in parks and along streets.
