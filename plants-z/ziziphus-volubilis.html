Ziziphus volubilis

Ziziphus volubilis, commonly known as the climbing date tree or wild date vine, is a deciduous climbing shrub native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It features woody stems that twine around supports, and its leaves are alternate, simple, and often trifoliate. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that give way to small, fleshy fruits resembling dates, though they are not typically edible for humans. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Ziziphus volubilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
