Zannichellia palustris var. ferganica

Zannichellia palustris var. ferganica, commonly known as the Fergana water plantain, is a aquatic plant native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater habitats such as ponds, lakes, and wet meadows. The plant features long, ribbon-like leaves that float on the water surface or extend underwater. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, often clustered in dense inflorescences.

It plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms and contributing to water filtration.
