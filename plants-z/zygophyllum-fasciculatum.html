Zygophyllum fasciculatum

Zygophyllum fasciculatum, commonly known as the clustered leaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, exhibiting adaptations to drought and high temperatures. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are usually white or pale blue, blooming in the morning and closing by midday. Its leaves are often clustered at the base of the stems, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Zygophyllum fasciculatum is also known for its ability to thrive in harsh environments, making it an important component of local ecosystems.
