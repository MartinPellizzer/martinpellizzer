Zanthoxylum andinum

Zanthoxylum andinum, commonly known as the Andean prickly ash, is a shrub or small tree native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its spiny branches and small, elliptical leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. Its fruit is edible and has a sharp, spicy flavor, often used in local cuisines.
