Zygogynum pomiferum

Zygogynum pomiferum, commonly known as the fruit-bearing zygogynum, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces edible fruits that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in some regions for both aesthetic and culinary purposes.
