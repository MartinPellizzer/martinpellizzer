Zanthoxylum iwahigense

Zanthoxylum iwahigense, commonly known as the Iwahig prickly ash, is a member of the Rutaceae family and is native to the Philippines.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub characterized by its pungent, aromatic leaves and yellowish-orange fruits. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers. Its bark and fruits are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Zanthoxylum iwahigense thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, typically in lowland to montane forests.
