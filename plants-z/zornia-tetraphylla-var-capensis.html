Zornia tetraphylla var. capensis

Zornia tetraphylla var. capensis, commonly known as the Cape zornia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing annual herb characterized by its slender stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in ecological and horticultural contexts.
