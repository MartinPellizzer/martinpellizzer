Zygia latifolia

Zygia latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf zygia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. Zygia latifolia is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
