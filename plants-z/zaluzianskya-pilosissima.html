Zaluzianskya pilosissima

Zaluzianskya pilosissima, commonly known as the velvetleaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when ripe. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
