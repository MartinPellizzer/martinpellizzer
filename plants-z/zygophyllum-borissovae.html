Zygophyllum borissovae

Zygophyllum borissovae, commonly known as the Borissov's zygophyllum, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its small, fleshy leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant is adapted to withstand extreme drought conditions, with a deep root system that allows it to access water from deeper soil layers. Its leaves are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain its seeds.

Zygophyllum borissovae plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and is often found in desert and semi-desert ecosystems.
