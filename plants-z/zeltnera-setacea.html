Zeltnera setacea

Zeltnera setacea, commonly known as the silky zeltnera, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive, narrow, and softly hairy leaves that give it a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, pale yellow to greenish flowers that bloom in late spring. Zeltnera setacea is typically found in rocky or sandy soils in arid environments.

It is a perennial herb that thrives in full sun and is adapted to drought conditions.
