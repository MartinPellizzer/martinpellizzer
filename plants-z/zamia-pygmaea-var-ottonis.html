Zamia pygmaea var. ottonis

Zamia pygmaea var. ottonis, commonly known as the dwarf cycad, is a compact evergreen plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Zamiaceae family and is characterized by its slow growth and distinctive, palm-like foliage. The plant features a short, underground stem with a crown of narrow, feather-like leaves. It produces cones rather than flowers, with male and female cones typically appearing on separate plants.

Due to its ornamental value and conservation status, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
