Zanthoxylum capense

Zanthoxylum capense, commonly known as the African prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often covered in fine, stinging hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. Its bark and leaves contain compounds with potential medicinal properties.

Zanthoxylum capense is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid conditions.
