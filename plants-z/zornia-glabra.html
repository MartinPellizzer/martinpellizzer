Zornia glabra

Zornia glabra, commonly known as smooth zornia, is a low-growing annual herb native to North America.

It typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters and features slender, hairy stems that branch near the base. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or purple, blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Zornia glabra is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is adapted to a variety of soil types.
