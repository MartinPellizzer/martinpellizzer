Zanthoxylum caribaeum

Zanthoxylum caribaeum, commonly known as the Caribbean prickly ash, is a shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are usually pinnately arranged and have serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. Its bark and roots contain compounds that have been used traditionally for their medicinal properties.

Zanthoxylum caribaeum thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.
