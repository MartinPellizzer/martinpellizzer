Zostera nana

Zostera nana, commonly known as dwarf eelgrass, is a marine flowering plant belonging to the family Zosteraceae.

It is characterized by its small, grass-like leaves that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. This species thrives in shallow, coastal waters and is often found in seagrass meadows. Zostera nana plays a crucial role in marine ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence is an indicator of healthy, stable marine environments.
