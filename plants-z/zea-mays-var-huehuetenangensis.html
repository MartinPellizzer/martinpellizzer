Zea mays var. huehuetenangensis

Zea mays var. huehuetenangensis, commonly known as the Mexican maize, is an ancient variety of maize native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall stature and large, tassel-like inflorescence. The plant produces small, hard grains that are typically used for traditional purposes rather than commercial agriculture. It is considered a valuable genetic resource due to its unique traits and historical significance.

Botanically, it exhibits a complex genome and is closely related to other maize varieties.
