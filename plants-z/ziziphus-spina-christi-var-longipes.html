Ziziphus spina-christi var. longipes

Ziziphus spina-christi var. longipes, commonly known as the longpod date palm, is a shrub or small tree native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, thorny stems and small, yellowish-green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, red or yellow fruits that are edible and have a sweet, date-like flavor. It thrives in arid and semi-arid climates, making it well adapted to hot, dry environments.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and historical significance in traditional medicine and agriculture.
