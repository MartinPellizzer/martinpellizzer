Ziziphus taylorii

Ziziphus taylorii, commonly known as Taylor's ziziphus, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish-green leaves and produces clusters of small, red berries that are edible for some wildlife. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils and along streambanks. It is a member of the Rhamnaceae family and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
