Zamia lindenii

Zamia lindenii, commonly known as the Lindenberg cycad, is a member of the Zamiaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

This plant is characterized by its stout, unbranched stem and a crown of large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. It produces cycad-like cones, with male and female cones typically occurring on separate plants. The plant is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments.

Due to its slow growth and unique appearance, Zamia lindenii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
