Zea mays var. leucodon

Zea mays var. leucodon, commonly known as white corn, is a variant of maize characterized by its white kernels.

It is native to the Americas and has been cultivated for thousands of years. The plant typically grows to a height of 1.5 to 2 meters, with long, narrow leaves and an inflorescence known as an ear. Its botanical features include a robust stalk and tassels that produce pollen.

This variety is valued for both its aesthetic appeal and nutritional content.
