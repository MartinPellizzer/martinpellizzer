Zappania nodiflora

Zappania nodiflora, commonly known as the clustered zappania, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. Zappania nodiflora is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid, well-drained soils.

It is also valued for its role in traditional medicine in some cultures.
