Zephyranthes mesochloa f. latifolia

Zephyranthes mesochloa f. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf rain lily, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers during the rainy season. The plant is adapted to wet environments and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in coastal areas and is tolerant of salt spray.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
