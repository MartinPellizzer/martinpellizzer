Zanonia indica var. pubescens

Zanonia indica var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy zanonia, is a tropical plant native to regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for tropical gardens and indoor cultivation.
