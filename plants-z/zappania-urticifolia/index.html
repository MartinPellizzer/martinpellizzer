Zappania Urticifolia

Zappania urticifolia is a unique and lesser-known plant species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia. Known for its distinctive appearance and ecological significance, it thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are covered in tiny, hair-like structures that give it a prickly texture, hence its common name. Despite its unassuming look, it plays an important role in local ecosystems. This article explores the characteristics, habitat, and importance of Zappania urticifolia.
