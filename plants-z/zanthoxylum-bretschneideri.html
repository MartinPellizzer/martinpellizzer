Zanthoxylum bretschneideri

Zanthoxylum bretschneideri, commonly known as the Chinese prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are often used in traditional Chinese medicine. Its bark and leaves contain various bioactive compounds, including lignans and flavonoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Zanthoxylum bretschneideri is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
