Zingiber rufopilosum

Zingiber rufopilosum, commonly known as the red-hair ginger, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers and related plants. The plant is characterized by its ornamental rhizomes and striking, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its aesthetic appeal in horticulture.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
