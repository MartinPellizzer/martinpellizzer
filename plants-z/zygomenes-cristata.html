Zygomenes cristata

Zygomenes cristata, commonly known as the mountain zygome, is a perennial shrub native to the alpine regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to high-altitude conditions with its compact growth habit. Its leaves are typically narrow and leathery, providing structural support in harsh mountain climates.

Zygomenes cristata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
