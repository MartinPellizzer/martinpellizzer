Zingiber elatius

Zingiber elatius, commonly known as the giant ginger, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the wet tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, sword-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. Its rhizomes, which are similar to ginger, are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

Zingiber elatius thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
