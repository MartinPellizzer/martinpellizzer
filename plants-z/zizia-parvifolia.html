Zizia parvifolia

Zizia parvifolia, commonly known as the smallleaf goldenroot, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky slopes.

Zizia parvifolia is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
