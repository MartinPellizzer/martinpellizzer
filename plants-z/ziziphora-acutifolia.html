Ziziphora acutifolia

Ziziphora acutifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in full sun. The plant has aromatic, narrow leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and digestive properties.

Ziziphora acutifolia is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
