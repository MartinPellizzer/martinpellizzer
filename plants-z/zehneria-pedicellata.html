Zehneria pedicellata

Zehneria pedicellata, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, yellow, and orange, which bloom throughout the year in warm climates. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.

Zehneria pedicellata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and vibrant blooms.
