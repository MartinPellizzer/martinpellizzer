Zygopetalum lacteum

Zygopetalum lacteum, commonly known as the milk orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive white to pale yellow flowers with a prominent, creamy-colored lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, tropical environments. Its leaves are elongated and often covered in a fine, waxy coating.

Zygopetalum lacteum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate under proper conditions.
