Zornia tropica

Zornia tropica, commonly known as the tropical zornia, is a flowering plant native to the warm climates of the Americas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, often found in disturbed soils and open habitats. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and can be dispersed by wind or animals.

Zornia tropica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
