Ziziphus pallens

Ziziphus pallens, commonly known as the silver leaf buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its grayish-white bark and small, elliptical leaves that turn reddish in the fall. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and small mammals. Ziziphus pallens thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along stream banks.

Its hardy nature makes it an adaptable species in various ecological settings.
