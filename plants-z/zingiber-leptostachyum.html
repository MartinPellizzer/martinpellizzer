Zingiber leptostachyum

Zingiber leptostachyum, commonly known as the slender-stemmed ginger, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other economically important plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant features a rhizomatous root system and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette. Its inflorescence consists of small, fragrant flowers that are often yellow or white in color.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
