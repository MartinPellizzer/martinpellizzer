Zanthoxylum simplicifolium

Zanthoxylum simplicifolium, commonly known as the simple-leaved prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and small, yellowish flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various birds and small mammals.

Its bark and leaves contain compounds used in traditional medicine for their analgesic and anti-inflammatory properties.
