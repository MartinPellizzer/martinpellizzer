Zanthoxylum asperum

Zanthoxylum asperum, commonly known as the roughleaf prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically 3 to 5 inches long and have serrated margins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries in the fall. Its bark is thick and fissured, often with a distinctive, aromatic scent.

Zanthoxylum asperum is valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
