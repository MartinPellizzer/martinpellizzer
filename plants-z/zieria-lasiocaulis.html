Zieria lasiocaulis

Zieria lasiocaulis, commonly known as the woolly zieria, is a low-growing shrub native to the eastern regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically features small, rounded leaves covered in fine, white hairs. It produces clusters of tiny, white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months.

Zieria lasiocaulis is often found in heathlands and dry forests, thriving in well-drained soils.
