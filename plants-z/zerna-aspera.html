Zerna aspera

Zerna aspera, commonly known as rough zerna, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its coarse, hairy texture. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and often have a rough, sandpaper-like surface.

Zerna aspera produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually arranged in dense clusters.
