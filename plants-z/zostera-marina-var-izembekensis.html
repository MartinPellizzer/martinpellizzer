Zostera marina var. izembekensis

Zostera marina var. izembekensis, commonly known as the Izembek sea grass, is a variant of the eelgrass species found primarily in coastal regions of the North Pacific.

This plant is a marine angiosperm that forms dense underwater meadows, providing critical habitat for various marine organisms. It has long, ribbon-like leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant reproduces through both sexual and asexual means, with flowering occurring in the summer months.

Its presence is an indicator of healthy coastal ecosystems and is often used in ecological studies to assess water quality and biodiversity.
