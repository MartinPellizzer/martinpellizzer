Ziziphus indica

Ziziphus indica, commonly known as the Indian date palm, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It produces small, yellow to red berries that are rich in nutrients and have been used in traditional medicine for centuries. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid climates, making it well-suited for cultivation in regions with limited water resources. Its fruit is also valued for its sweet flavor and is used in culinary applications.

The plant's hardy nature and adaptability contribute to its ecological and economic importance in various parts of Asia.
