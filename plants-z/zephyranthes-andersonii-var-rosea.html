Zephyranthes andersonii var. rosea

Zephyranthes andersonii var. rosea, commonly known as the roseate rain lily, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a small, herbaceous perennial that produces delicate, pinkish-white flowers with a subtle pink hue. The plant typically blooms after rainfall, hence its common name, and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It has strap-shaped leaves and is often found in coastal areas and open woodlands.

Zephyranthes andersonii var. rosea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is relatively easy to cultivate in warm, humid climates.
