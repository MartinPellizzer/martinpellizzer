Zantedeschia albomaculata subsp. valida

Zantedeschia albomaculata subsp.

valida, commonly known as the White-spotted Calla Lily, is a striking ornamental plant native to southern Africa. It features elegant, arrow-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a waxy texture.

The plant produces striking white or pale yellow flowers with a distinctive, elongated spathe that surrounds a cluster of small, greenish-white spathes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

This subspecies is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
