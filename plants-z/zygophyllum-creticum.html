Zygophyllum creticum

Zygophyllum creticum, commonly known as the cretan leafless plant, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and rocky environments, showcasing drought tolerance and efficient water conservation mechanisms. Its flowers typically bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Zygophyllum creticum plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is of interest in ecological restoration projects.
