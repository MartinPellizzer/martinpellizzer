Zeuxine emarginata

Zeuxine emarginata, commonly known as the fringed cowslip, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is native to temperate regions of Asia, particularly in China, Korea, and Japan. The plant features slender, erect stems with alternate, trifoliate leaves that have distinctive notched margins. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with five sepals and numerous stamens, forming a showy, cup-shaped inflorescence.

Zeuxine emarginata thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
