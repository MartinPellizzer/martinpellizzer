Zosterophyllanthos mastodon

Zosterophyllanthos mastodon, commonly known as the fossilized zosterophyllum, is an ancient plant belonging to the group of early land plants known as zosterophylls.

These plants are characterized by their ribbon-like, flattened structures and are believed to have thrived during the Silurian period, approximately 440 million years ago. Zosterophyllanthos mastodon is particularly notable for its well-preserved fossils, which provide valuable insights into the early evolution of vascular plant structures. The plant's morphology suggests it played a role in the early development of complex terrestrial ecosystems.

Its study contributes to understanding the transition from aquatic to terrestrial life in plant evolution.
