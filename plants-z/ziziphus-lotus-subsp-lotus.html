Ziziphus lotus subsp. lotus

Ziziphus lotus subsp.

lotus, commonly known as the Persian date palm, is a shrub or small tree native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia. It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often used for medicinal purposes in traditional medicine.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that give way to edible, sweet fruits resembling dates. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

The fruit is rich in nutrients and has been historically valued for its culinary and medicinal uses.
