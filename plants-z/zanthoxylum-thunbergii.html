Zanthoxylum thunbergii

Zanthoxylum thunbergii, commonly known as Japanese prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are edible for humans and wildlife. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and autumn coloration.
