Zanthoxylum bungei var. imperforatum

Zanthoxylum bungei var. imperforatum, commonly known as the Chinese prickly ash or the imperforate prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its spiny branches and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries in the fall. Its bark and leaves contain compounds with potential medicinal properties, and it is often used in traditional Chinese medicine.

The plant's unique appearance and ecological role make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
