Zemisia

Zemisia, commonly known as the "false lavender," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Apiaceae.

These plants are characterized by their aromatic, often fragrant leaves and small, clustered flowers. Zemisia species typically have finely divided leaves and are often found in rocky or dry habitats. They are native to regions of Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

Due to their ornamental value and medicinal properties, some species are cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
