Zea mays var. cymosa

Zea mays var. cymosa, commonly known as the tufted maize or cymose corn, is a variant of maize characterized by its unique inflorescence structure.

It features a dense, tufted tassel that gives it a distinctive appearance compared to standard maize. The plant typically grows in warm climates and thrives in well-drained soils. Its botanical features include narrow, elongated leaves and a short stalk that supports the compact tassel.

This variant is primarily grown for ornamental purposes due to its unique visual appeal.
