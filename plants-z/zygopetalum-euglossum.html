Zygopetalum euglossum

Zygopetalum euglossum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically feature a bright yellow or orange coloration. The plant produces intricate, fragrant blooms that attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually broad and lance-shaped, arranged in a rosette pattern.

Zygopetalum euglossum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
