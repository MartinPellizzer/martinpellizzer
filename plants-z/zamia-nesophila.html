Zamia nesophila

Zamia nesophila, commonly known as the Mexican cycad, is a member of the Zamiaceae family and is native to Mexico.

It is a slow-growing, evergreen plant characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of feather-like leaves. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs. Zamia nesophila thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.

Its unique morphology and ancient lineage make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
