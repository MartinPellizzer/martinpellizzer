Ziziphus tomentosa

Ziziphus tomentosa, commonly known as the hairy date tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern and eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its grayish-brown bark, which becomes fissured with age, and its opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that give way to edible, fleshy fruits resembling dates, though they are typically smaller and less sweet. Ziziphus tomentosa is often found in arid or semi-arid regions and is adapted to thrive in poor soils.

It has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits, including its potential to support digestive and immune functions.
