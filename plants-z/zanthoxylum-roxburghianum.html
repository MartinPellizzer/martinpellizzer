Zanthoxylum roxburghianum

Zanthoxylum roxburghianum, commonly known as the Indian prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its spiny branches and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. Its bark and fruits contain alkaloids and are used in traditional medicine for their purported therapeutic properties.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in moist, shaded environments.
