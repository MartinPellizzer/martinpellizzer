Zappania nodiflora var. rosea

Zappania nodiflora var. rosea, commonly known as the rose-flowered zappania, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, rose-colored inflorescences that bloom in clusters. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth texture and a faintly hairy surface. Its flowers are typically borne on short pedicels, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Zappania nodiflora var. rosea is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and vibrant floral display.
