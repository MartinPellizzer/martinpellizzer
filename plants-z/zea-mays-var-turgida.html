Zea mays var. turgida

Zea mays var. turgida, commonly known as the giant maize or turgid corn, is a variant of maize characterized by its large, swollen tassels and robust stalks.

It is native to the Americas and is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking appearance. The plant exhibits a tall growth habit, reaching heights of several meters, with broad, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically green and develop into large, showy tassels that can resemble ornamental grasses.

This variety is valued in botanical gardens and for its unique visual characteristics.
