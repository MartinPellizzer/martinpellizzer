Ziziphora taurica

Ziziphora taurica, commonly known as Turkish tea or mountain tea, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and sage. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring. Its leaves and flowers are often used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent.

Ziziphora taurica thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions.
