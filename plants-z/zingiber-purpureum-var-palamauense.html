Zingiber purpureum var. palamauense

Zingiber purpureum var. palamauense, commonly known as the Palamau ginger, is a rare and ornamental plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers and turmeric. The plant is characterized by its striking purple and pink inflorescences, which are often used in horticultural displays. Its rhizomes are not typically used for culinary purposes but are appreciated for their aesthetic value.
