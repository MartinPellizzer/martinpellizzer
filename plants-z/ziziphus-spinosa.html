Ziziphus spinosa

Ziziphus spinosa, commonly known as the spiny hackberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia, Africa, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval-shaped leaves, and small, yellowish-green fruits that ripen to a dark red or black color. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in poor soils, making it well adapted to harsh environments. Its fruits are edible and have been used for food and traditional medicine in various cultures.

The plant also provides habitat and food for wildlife, including birds and insects.
