Zanthoxylum parvifolium

Zanthoxylum parvifolium, commonly known as the small-leaved prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red, berry-like fruits. Its bark and leaves contain bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional medicine.

Zanthoxylum parvifolium thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.
