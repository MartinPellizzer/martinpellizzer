Zanthoxylum punctatum subsp. obtriangulare

Zanthoxylum punctatum subsp.

obtriangulare, commonly known as the three-angled prickly ash, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its compound leaves with three leaflets, each having a distinct, triangular shape.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by red, fleshy fruits that are edible for some wildlife. Its bark and leaves contain bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional medicine.

It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.
