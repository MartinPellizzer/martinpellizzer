Masdevallia adamsii

Masdevallia adamsii, commonly known as the Adams' masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, often colorful flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial orchid with a sympodial growth habit, forming pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, adapted to the cool, moist conditions of its natural habitat.

Masdevallia adamsii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and unique floral structure.
