Myrtella beccarii

Myrtella beccarii, commonly known as the red myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, clustered white flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a small, red, fleshy drupe that contains seeds.

Myrtella beccarii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a pleasant, spicy aroma.
