Magnolia fasciata

Magnolia fasciata, commonly known as the broad-leafed magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are broad, elliptical, and have a smooth, waxy texture. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall, with a dense canopy and a slow growth rate.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.
