Milium holciforme var. songaricum

Milium holciforme var. songaricum, commonly known as the Songaric millet, is a perennial grass species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate climates and is often found in meadows or along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
