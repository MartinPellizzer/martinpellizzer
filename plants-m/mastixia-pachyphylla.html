Mastixia pachyphylla

Mastixia pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-leaved mastix, is a shrub native to the arid regions of central Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry climates. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access groundwater in nutrient-poor soils.

Mastixia pachyphylla plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
