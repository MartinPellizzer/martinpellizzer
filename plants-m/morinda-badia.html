Morinda badia

Morinda badia, commonly known as Indian madder, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant produces bright red or orange berries that contain elliptical seeds. It is often cultivated for its roots, which contain pigments used in traditional dyes and medicinal applications.

Morinda badia thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
