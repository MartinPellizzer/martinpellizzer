Microtatorchis clavicalcarata

Microtatorchis clavicalcarata, commonly known as the diamond-leafed orchid, is a rare and unique orchid species characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology.

It features elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, waxy hairs, which help reduce water loss in its native habitat. The plant produces small, clustered flowers with a striking combination of white and purple petals, typical of many orchid species. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical rainforests.
