Myrciaria uliginosa

Myrciaria uliginosa, commonly known as the Brazilian blackberry, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are rich in antioxidants and have a tart flavor. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid tropical regions. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a slightly serrated margin.

The fruit is typically harvested when fully ripe, and it is used in both culinary and traditional medicinal applications.
