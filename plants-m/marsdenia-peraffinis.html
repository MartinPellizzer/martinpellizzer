Marsdenia peraffinis

Marsdenia peraffinis, commonly known as the slender Marsdenia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by follicle fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in forest understoreys.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in horticulture.
