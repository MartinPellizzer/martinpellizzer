Mauhlia latifolia

Mauhlia latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf mauhlia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, often blooming during the spring and early summer. Mauhlia latifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and pleasant scent.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it adaptable to a variety of growing conditions.
