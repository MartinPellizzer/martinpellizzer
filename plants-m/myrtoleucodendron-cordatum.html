Myrtoleucodendron cordatum

Myrtoleucodendron cordatum, commonly known as the broadleaf myrtle, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are typically broad and leathery in texture. It produces small, fragrant white or pink flowers that are clustered in inflorescences.
