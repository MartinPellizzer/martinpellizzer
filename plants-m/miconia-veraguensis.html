Miconia veraguensis

Miconia veraguensis, commonly known as the Veraguas miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, ovate leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces showy, clustered inflorescences with pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. Its fruit is a globose capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Miconia veraguensis is often found in moist, shaded forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
