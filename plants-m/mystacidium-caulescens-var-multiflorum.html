Mystacidium caulescens var. multiflorum

Mystacidium caulescens var. multiflorum, commonly known as the clustered mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually arranged in clusters, and its flowers are white or pale green, often appearing in clusters as well. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are dispersed by birds.

It is native to parts of Africa and is adapted to thrive in shaded, humid environments.
