Melastoma rosmarinifolium

Melastoma rosmarinifolium, commonly known as the rosemary-leaved melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, clustered flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and have a fragrant, aromatic quality similar to rosemary. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
