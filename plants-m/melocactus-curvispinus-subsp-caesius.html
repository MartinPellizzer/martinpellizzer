Melocactus curvispinus subsp. caesius

Melocactus curvispinus subsp.

caesius, commonly known as the blue melocactus, is a striking cactus native to parts of South America, particularly Brazil. It is characterized by its dense, blue-gray spines and a globular, ribbed stem that can grow up to 30 centimeters in height.

The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the rainy season, typically in the summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique coloration and architectural form make it a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts and xeriscaping.
