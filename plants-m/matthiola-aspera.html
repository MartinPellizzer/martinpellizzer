Matthiola aspera

Matthiola aspera, commonly known as the rough stocky wallflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbages and mustards. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a rough texture.

Matthiola aspera is often found growing in rocky or disturbed soils, and it is known for its ability to thrive in coastal and arid environments.
