Meringium pachydermicum

Meringium pachydermicum, commonly known as the thick-skinned meringa, is a rare flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in its harsh environment. The plant produces striking, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access water from underground sources.

Due to its unique adaptations, it is considered an important species for ecological studies in arid ecosystems.
