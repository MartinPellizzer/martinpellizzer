Mangifera indica var. cambodiana

Mangifera indica var. cambodiana, commonly known as the Cambodian mango, is a tropical fruit tree native to Southeast Asia.

It produces large, elongated mango fruits with a thick, fibrous peel and sweet, aromatic flesh. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

It is valued for its fruit's flavor and nutritional content, containing high levels of vitamins A and C. The tree is also cultivated for its ornamental value and can grow up to 20 meters tall.
