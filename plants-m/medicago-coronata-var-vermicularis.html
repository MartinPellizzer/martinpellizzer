Medicago coronata var. vermicularis

Medicago coronata var. vermicularis, commonly known as the hairy vetch, is a leguminous plant characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive yellow flowers.

It is native to Europe and western Asia but has been introduced to various regions around the world. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it valuable in agricultural and ecological contexts. Its hairy stems and leaves provide habitat for beneficial insects, contributing to biodiversity.

It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often used as a cover crop or forage plant.
