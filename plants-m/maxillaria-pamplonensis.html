Maxillaria pamplonensis

Maxillaria pamplonensis, commonly known as the Pamplona orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, dark purple to violet flowers. The plant typically has linear, grass-like leaves and thrives in shady, humid environments. It is a terrestrial orchid, often found in forest understories, and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Maxillaria pamplonensis is appreciated for its ornamental value and is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
