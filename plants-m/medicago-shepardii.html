Medicago shepardii

Medicago shepardii, commonly known as shepard's bluebottle, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has compound leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds.

Medicago shepardii is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility.
