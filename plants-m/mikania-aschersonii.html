Mikania aschersonii

Mikania aschersonii, commonly known as climbing hemp or goatweed, is a fast-growing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into fluffy, cotton-like seeds, aiding in its rapid spread. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its aggressive growth, it is considered an invasive species in many areas outside its native range.
