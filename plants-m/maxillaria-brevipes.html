Maxillaria brevipes

Maxillaria brevipes, commonly known as the short-stemmed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Caribbean islands.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a short, thick rhizome and dense, grass-like leaves. The plant produces compact, showy inflorescences with flowers that are typically pale yellow to orange in color. Its flowers have a distinctive, narrow spur and are often fragrant, attracting pollinators such as bees and moths.

Maxillaria brevipes is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy cultivation requirements.
