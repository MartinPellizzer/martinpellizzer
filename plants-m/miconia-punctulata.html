Miconia punctulata

Miconia punctulata, commonly known as the spotted miconia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often spotted with white or pale green markings. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Miconia punctulata is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

However, it can become invasive in some regions, posing a threat to native plant species.
