Mentha arvensis var. melanochroa

Mentha arvensis var. melanochroa, commonly known as the black henbane, is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its hairy stems and alternate leaves.

It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia and thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. It has a strong, aromatic scent and is known for its medicinal and toxic properties.

Its leaves and flowers contain various volatile oils and alkaloids, making it both useful and potentially harmful.
