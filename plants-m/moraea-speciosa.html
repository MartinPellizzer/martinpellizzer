Moraea speciosa

Moraea speciosa, commonly known as the showy moraea, is a striking perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, funnel-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring, producing vivid purple or pink flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained soil in full sun.

Moraea speciosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
