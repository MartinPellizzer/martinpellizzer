Malus sylvestris var. niedzwetzkyana

Malus sylvestris var. niedzwetzkyana, commonly known as the Niedzwetzkyana apple, is a rare and ornamental variety of the apple tree native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated foliage with white and green mottling, which adds visual interest throughout the growing season. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers in spring, followed by edible, tart apples that are prized for their unique flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil with partial to full sun exposure.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by horticultural enthusiasts.
