Malpighia pruinosa

Malpighia pruinosa, commonly known as the velvet fruit or purple granadilla, is a species of flowering plant in the family Malpighiaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of the Americas, including parts of Mexico, Central America, and South America. The plant is characterized by its striking, velvety purple fruits that are encased in a waxy, pruinose coating. Its leaves are typically alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture.

Malpighia pruinosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits, which are rich in antioxidants and have a tart, sweet flavor.
