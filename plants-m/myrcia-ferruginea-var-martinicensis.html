Myrcia ferruginea var. martinicensis

Myrcia ferruginea var. martinicensis, commonly known as the Martinique myrtle, is a species native to the Caribbean, particularly Martinique and nearby islands.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows in humid, shaded environments within tropical rainforests. The plant features elliptical, dark green leaves with a slightly waxy texture and a distinctive, aromatic scent. Its flowers are small, clustered, and usually white or pale pink, while the fruit is a small, dark purple drupe.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine and as a source of aromatic oils.
