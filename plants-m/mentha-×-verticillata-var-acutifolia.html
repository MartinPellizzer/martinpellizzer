Mentha × verticillata var. acutifolia

Mentha × verticillata var. acutifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved water mint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as wet meadows, stream banks, and marshes. It produces small, pale purple flowers arranged in whorls along the stem.

This variety is known for its aromatic leaves and is sometimes used in herbal teas or as a culinary herb.
