Malachium

Malachium, commonly known as blueweed, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

These plants are characterized by their showy, often blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. They typically grow in moist environments such as wet meadows, stream banks, and marshes. Malachium species are perennial herbs with alternate leaves that are usually lance-shaped or ovate.

The genus includes over 100 species, many of which are found in temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.
