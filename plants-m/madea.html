Madea

Madea, commonly known as the common mallow, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It typically features hairy, lobed leaves and produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, or white. The plant is native to various regions in Europe, Asia, and Africa, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Madea is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in disturbed areas.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological diversity.
