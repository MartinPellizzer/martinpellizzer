Monadenium heteropodum var. formosum

Monadenium heteropodum var. formosum, commonly known as the yellow globemallow, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features hairy, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine pubescence.

The species is adapted to thrive in sandy or gravelly soils with minimal water requirements.
