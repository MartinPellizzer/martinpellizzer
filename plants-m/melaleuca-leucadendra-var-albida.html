Melaleuca leucadendra var. albida

Melaleuca leucadendra var. albida, commonly known as the white bottlebrush, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its striking white or pale pink flower spikes, which are borne in dense, cylindrical clusters. The leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slight curl, and the bark is typically smooth and greyish in color. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to various coastal and inland environments.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract native pollinators.
