Mentha × verticillata var. pluriglobula

Mentha × verticillata var. pluriglobula, commonly known as the globe mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its rounded, compact growth habit and its ability to form dense mats, often growing in moist, shaded environments. The plant features opposite, oval-shaped leaves with a serrated margin and a fragrant, mint-like aroma. Its flowers are small, clustered in whorls, and typically have a pale blue or purple color.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in herbal remedies due to its aromatic properties.
