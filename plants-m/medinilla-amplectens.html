Medinilla amplectens

Medinilla amplectens, commonly known as the velvet tangerine or velvet plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of fragrant, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value.

Medinilla amplectens is also valued for its ability to purify the air and its use in traditional medicine.
