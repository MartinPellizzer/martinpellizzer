Mentha laxiflora var. truncata

Mentha laxiflora var. truncata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mint or truncated mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces clusters of pale blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its aromatic leaves and is often used in herbal teas and culinary applications.
