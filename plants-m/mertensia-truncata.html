Mertensia truncata

Mertensia truncata, commonly known as bluebells, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, white pubescence. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Mertensia truncata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
