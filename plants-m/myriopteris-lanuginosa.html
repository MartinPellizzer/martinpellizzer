Myriopteris lanuginosa

Myriopteris lanuginosa, commonly known as the downy woodfern, is a fern species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, hairy fronds that give it a soft, downy appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are bi-lobed and have a distinctive, intricate venation pattern.

Myriopteris lanuginosa is valued for its ornamental qualities and plays a role in maintaining ecological balance in its native habitats.
