Madronella glauca

Madronella glauca, commonly known as the blue madrona, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, bluish-gray bark and lance-shaped leaves that turn coppery in the fall. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, which are followed by blue-black berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Madronella glauca is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
