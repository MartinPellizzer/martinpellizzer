Monarda clinopodifolia

Monarda clinopodifolia, commonly known as wild bergamot, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square stems and showy lavender or pinkish-purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist woodland areas and thrives in partial to full sun. Its leaves are aromatic and have a minty scent, which is often used in herbal teas.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it an important plant in ecological gardens.
