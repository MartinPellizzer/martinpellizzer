Melalema humifusa

Melalema humifusa, commonly known as the dwarf honeymyrtle, is a low-growing shrub native to eastern Australia.

It features small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters during spring. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. It is well adapted to sandy or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun.

Melalema humifusa is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
