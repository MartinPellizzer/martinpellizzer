Melica exasperata

Melica exasperata, commonly known as the rough meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, rough-textured leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various insects and small vertebrates.
