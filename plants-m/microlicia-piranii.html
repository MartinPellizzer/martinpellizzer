Microlicia piranii

Microlicia piranii, commonly known as the Brazilian peppertree, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant has simple, alternate, and usually elliptic-shaped leaves that are leathery in texture. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in dense inflorescences, while the fruit is a small, round, fleshy berry.

Microlicia piranii is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in both sunny and partially shaded environments.
