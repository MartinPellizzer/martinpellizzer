Melica scabra var. pubescens

Melica scabra var. pubescens, commonly known as hairy false brome, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, glabrous or hairy stems depending on the variety. The leaves are typically narrow and often covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing minute, wind-pollinated flowers.

This plant thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along streambanks.
