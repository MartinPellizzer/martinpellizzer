Musa × mensaria

Musa × mensaria, commonly known as the pink banana, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Musa acuminata and Musa balbisiana.

It is characterized by its striking pink or red pigmentation in the fruit, which is due to the presence of anthocyanins. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial with large, broad leaves and can reach heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers are clustered in a pseudostem, and the fruit develops in clusters, often containing multiple segments.

This plant is cultivated for its ornamental value and as a food source in some regions.
