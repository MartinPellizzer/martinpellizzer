Matelea pedunculata

Matelea pedunculata, commonly known as the Mexican moonflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom at night. Matelea pedunculata is adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-drained soils.
