Mertensia echioides

Mertensia echioides, commonly known as bluebells, is a perennial flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves fully emerge. The plant has heart-shaped, softly hairy leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Mertensia echioides is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in woodland gardens.
