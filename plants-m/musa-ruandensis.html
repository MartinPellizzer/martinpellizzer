Musa ruandensis

Musa ruandensis, commonly known as the Ruanda banana, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central Africa.

It belongs to the Musaceae family and is characterized by its large, broad leaves and clustered inflorescences. The plant produces edible fruits that are typically consumed fresh or used in cooking. It thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its genetic diversity, it is also of interest for breeding programs aimed at improving banana varieties.
