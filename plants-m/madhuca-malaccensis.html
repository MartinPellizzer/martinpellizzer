Madhuca malaccensis

Madhuca malaccensis, commonly known as the Malacca nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and large, glossy, elliptical leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by edible, round fruits containing a single seed. Its latex, which is milky and sticky, is used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
