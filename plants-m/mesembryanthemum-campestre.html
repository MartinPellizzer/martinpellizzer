Mesembryanthemum campestre

Mesembryanthemum campestre, commonly known as the common ice plant, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves and small, star-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of yellow, white, and red. The plant is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick leaves and stems. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Mesembryanthemum campestre is also known for its ability to produce a powdery coating on its leaves, which reflects sunlight and reduces water loss.
