Maclura aurantiaca var. inermis

Maclura aurantiaca var. inermis, commonly known as the smooth-flowered gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically 5 to 7 leaflets, and its yellow to orange berries that ripen in the fall. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers in the spring, which are clustered in loose inflorescences. Its bark is thick, fissured, and often has a reddish-brown appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
