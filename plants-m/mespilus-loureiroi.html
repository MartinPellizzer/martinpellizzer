Mespilus loureiroi

Mespilus loureiroi, commonly known as the Brazilian white mulberry, is a small deciduous tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, fuzzy fruits similar to those of the common mulberry. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production, thriving in tropical and subtropical climates.

The plant is also valued for its role in traditional medicine and as a food source for local wildlife.
