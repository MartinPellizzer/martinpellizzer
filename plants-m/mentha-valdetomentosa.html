Mentha valdetomentosa

Mentha valdetomentosa, commonly known as the woolly mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and fragrant, often pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses. Mentha valdetomentosa thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its leaves are used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb, though it is less commonly cultivated than other mint species.
