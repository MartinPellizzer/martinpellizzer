Malaxis titania

Malaxis titania, commonly known as the Titania Malaxis, is a rare and striking orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stem and clusters of delicate, pale greenish-white flowers that bloom during the spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem, contributing to its unique appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil for optimal growth.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern in its native range.
