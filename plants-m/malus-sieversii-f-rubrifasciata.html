Malus sieversii f. rubrifasciata

Malus sieversii f. rubrifasciata, commonly known as the red-banded apple, is a rare and ornamental variant of the apple tree.

It is characterized by its distinctive red-banded patterns on the bark and leaves, which give it a unique visual appeal. This plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, where it thrives in temperate climates. It typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 8 meters.

The fruit is edible, though smaller and less sweet compared to common apple varieties.
