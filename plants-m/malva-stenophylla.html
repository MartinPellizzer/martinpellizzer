Malva stenophylla

Malva stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cotton and hibiscus. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Malva stenophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential healing properties.
