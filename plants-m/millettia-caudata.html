Millettia caudata

Millettia caudata, commonly known as the long-tailed legume, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in moist, forested areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Millettia caudata also has traditional uses in some cultures for medicinal purposes.
