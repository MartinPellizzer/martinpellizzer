Machilus declinatus

Machilus declinatus, commonly known as the declining laurel, is a species of evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by dark blue-black berries. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
