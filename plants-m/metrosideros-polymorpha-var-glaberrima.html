Metrosideros polymorpha var. glaberrima

Metrosideros polymorpha var. glaberrima, commonly known as the smooth-leafed ohia, is a variant of the native Hawaiian tree known for its distinctive botanical features.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous leaves that are typically glossy and dark green, contrasting with the rougher leaves of other varietal forms. The plant produces vibrant red or orange flowers, which are clustered in dense inflorescences and attract a variety of pollinators. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland areas of Hawaii.

This variant plays an important ecological role in providing habitat and contributing to the biodiversity of native Hawaiian ecosystems.
