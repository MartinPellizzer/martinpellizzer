Mariscus dissitiflorus var. lucidior

Mariscus dissitiflorus var. lucidior, commonly known as the shining leaf mariscus, is a rare and delicate plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink, often with a subtle fragrance. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a glossy, waxy surface that gives the plant its common name. This species thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in cloud forests or along stream banks.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
