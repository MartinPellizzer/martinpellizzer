Mimulus prionophyllus

Mimulus prionophyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, spurred flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as stream banks and meadows. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

Mimulus prionophyllus is part of the evening primrose family (Onagraceae) and is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
