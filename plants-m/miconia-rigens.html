Miconia rigens

Miconia rigens, commonly known as the velvetleaf miconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a large shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its broad, velvety leaves that are dark green on top and silvery-white underneath. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers with five petals, which are followed by large, round fruits that contain numerous seeds. Miconia rigens is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, making it an invasive species in some regions.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and a challenge for land management in tropical ecosystems.
