Mussaenda treutleri

Mussaenda treutleri, commonly known as the white flame bush, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered white to yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are elliptical to ovate in shape. It is typically shrub-like, growing to a height of about 1 to 3 meters.

Mussaenda treutleri is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and landscapes.
