Melastoma albiflorum

Melastoma albiflorum, commonly known as the white-flowered melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Melastomataceae and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces showy, white or pale pink flowers that are typically arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Melastoma albiflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
