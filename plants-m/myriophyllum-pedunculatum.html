Myriophyllum pedunculatum

Myriophyllum pedunculatum, commonly known as water milfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves.

It typically grows in freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams. The plant has a branched stem with small, opposite leaves that are often arranged in whorls. It produces tiny, floating flowers that are clustered in spike-like structures.

Myriophyllum pedunculatum plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen for aquatic life.
