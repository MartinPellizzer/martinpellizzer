Melilotus officinalis var. dentatus

Melilotus officinalis var. dentatus, commonly known as the serrated melilot, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces small yellow flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically serrated, giving it its botanical variant name, and it is often found in meadows and disturbed soils.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used historically in herbal remedies.
