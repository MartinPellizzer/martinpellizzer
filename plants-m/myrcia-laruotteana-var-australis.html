Myrcia laruotteana var. australis

Myrcia laruotteana var. australis, commonly known as the Australian myrtle, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, evergreen leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in shaded to semi-shaded environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in local ecosystems.
