Manihot multifida

Manihot multifida, commonly known as the arrowleaf manioc, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often deeply lobed. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its roots are tuberous and are used in traditional medicine and as a source of starch.

However, the plant contains toxic compounds, requiring careful processing before consumption.
