Manihot alterniflora

Manihot alterniflora, commonly known as the arrowleaf manioc, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers, which are followed by fleshy, orange-colored fruits. Its roots are used in traditional medicine and as a source of starch.

However, the plant contains toxic compounds that require careful processing before consumption.
