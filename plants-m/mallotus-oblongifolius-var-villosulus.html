Mallotus oblongifolius var. villosulus

Mallotus oblongifolius var. villosulus, commonly known as the hairy-leaf mallotus, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elliptical, hairy leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by woody, spherical fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
