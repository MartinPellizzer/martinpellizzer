Myrcia richardiana

Myrcia richardiana, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. It produces small, red, fleshy berries that are edible for some animals but toxic to humans.

Myrcia richardiana is often used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
