Myginda fasciculata

Myginda fasciculata, commonly known as the clustered myginda, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, clustered growth habit and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers, which are often fragrant. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Myginda fasciculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
