Melaleuca thapsina

Melaleuca thapsina, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tea tree, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white or pink flowers. The plant is evergreen, with a compact growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

The leaves are used in traditional medicine and are also a source of essential oils.
