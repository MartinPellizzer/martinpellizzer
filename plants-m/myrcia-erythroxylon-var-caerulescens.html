Myrcia erythroxylon var. caerulescens

Myrcia erythroxylon var. caerulescens, commonly known as the blue myrcia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. It produces berries that range in color from red to blue, depending on the maturity of the fruit.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
