Monardella undulata subsp. arguelloensis

Monardella undulata subsp.

arguelloensis, commonly known as the Arguello's wild indigo, is a perennial herb native to California. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves.

The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers in whorls along the upper stems, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological importance and is used in local restoration projects.
