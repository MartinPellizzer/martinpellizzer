Malvastrum albens

Malvastrum albens, commonly known as silverleaf mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features hairy, silver-green foliage that reflects light, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers with five petals, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardens and ecological restoration projects.
