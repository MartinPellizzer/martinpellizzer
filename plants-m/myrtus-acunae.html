Myrtus acunae

Myrtus acunae, commonly known as the Canary Island myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its narrow, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a small, dark purple berry that contains several seeds.

Myrtus acunae is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in coastal environments.
