Miconia dapsiliflora

Miconia dapsiliflora, commonly known as the velvetleaf miconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers, which are followed by spherical, spiny fruits. It is a member of the Melastomataceae family and is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets.

Due to its invasive nature in some regions, it can outcompete native vegetation and disrupt local ecosystems.
