Mespilus × sorbifolia

Mespilus × sorbifolia, commonly known as the hybrid whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree that belongs to the Rosaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Mespilus babingtonii and Sorbus aucuparia, exhibiting characteristics of both parent species. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often serrated and have a silvery underside. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall.

This species is typically found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
