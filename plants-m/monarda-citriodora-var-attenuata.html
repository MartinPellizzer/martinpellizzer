Monarda citriodora var. attenuata

Monarda citriodora var. attenuata, commonly known as lemon bee balm, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features aromatic, narrow leaves with a citrus-like scent, which contribute to its common name. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of pink to red, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a versatile garden plant.

Its ornamental value and ecological benefits make it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
