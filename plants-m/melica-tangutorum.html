Melica tangutorum

Melica tangutorum, commonly known as the Tangut grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in steppes and rocky areas.

Melica tangutorum plays a role in stabilizing soil and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
