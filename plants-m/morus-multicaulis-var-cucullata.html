Morus multicaulis var. cucullata

Morus multicaulis var. cucullata, commonly known as the hooded mulberry, is a variant of the mulberry tree native to China.

It is characterized by its distinctive hooded or cup-shaped leaves, which give it its common name. The plant is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, with a broad, spreading crown. Its small, aggregate fruits are typically dark purple when mature and are edible.

This variety is valued for its ornamental foliage and is often cultivated in gardens and parks for its aesthetic appeal.
