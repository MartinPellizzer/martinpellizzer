Marsdenia wallichii

Marsdenia wallichii, commonly known as the Himalayan milkweed, is a climbing plant native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pink flowers, followed by orange or red berries. Its latex contains toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.

It is often found in moist, rocky habitats at high altitudes.
