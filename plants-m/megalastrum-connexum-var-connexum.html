Megalastrum connexum var. connexum

Megalastrum connexum var. connexum, commonly known as the clustered star-of-Bethlehem, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowers have a subtle fragrance and are pollinated primarily by insects.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a suitable addition to rock gardens or borders.
