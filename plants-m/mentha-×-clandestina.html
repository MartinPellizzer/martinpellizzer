Mentha × clandestina

Mentha × clandestina, commonly known as the hybrid mint, is a cross between Mentha × piperita (peppermint) and Mentha × spicata (spearmint).

It is a perennial herbaceous plant that typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm. The plant produces opposite, serrated leaves that are aromatic and often used in culinary and medicinal applications. Its flowers are small, tubular, and clustered in whorls, typically blooming in late summer.

Mentha × clandestina is known for its strong menthol scent and is widely cultivated for its essential oils.
