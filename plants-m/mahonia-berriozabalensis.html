Mahonia berriozabalensis

Mahonia berriozabalensis, commonly known as the Berriozabal mahonia, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of northern Spain.

It belongs to the family Berberidaceae and is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils, preferring partial to full shade. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, often with a waxy coating, while its berries are red and edible, though not commonly consumed by humans.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
