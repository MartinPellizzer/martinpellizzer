Mimosa montevidensis

Mimosa montevidensis, commonly known as the Uruguay mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and spherical, yellow pods. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands, often in regions with moderate to high rainfall. It produces small, fragrant flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and used in restoration projects.
