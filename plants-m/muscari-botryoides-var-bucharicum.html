Muscari botryoides var. bucharicum

Muscari botryoides var. bucharicum, commonly known as the Bucharic grape hyacinth, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Caucasus region.

It produces dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, resembling tiny grapes. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or woodland settings. Its foliage consists of strap-like leaves that emerge in early spring.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to naturalize over time.
