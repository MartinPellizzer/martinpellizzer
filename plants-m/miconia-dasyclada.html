Miconia dasyclada

Miconia dasyclada, commonly known as the velvetleaf miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers followed by dark purple, fleshy berries. Miconia dasyclada is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed habitats, making it an invasive species in some regions.

Its botanical features make it an important subject in ecological and horticultural studies.
