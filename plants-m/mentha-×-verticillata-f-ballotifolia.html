Mentha × verticillata f. ballotifolia

Mentha × verticillata f. ballotifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves that are often arranged in a whorled pattern around the stems. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue to purple in color, blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist environments such as wet meadows, stream banks, and pond edges.

Its leaves are commonly used in herbal teas and traditional remedies due to their menthol-like fragrance and medicinal properties.
