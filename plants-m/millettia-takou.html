Millettia takou

Millettia takou, commonly known as the African sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall, with a broad, spreading canopy. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, often marked with fissures.

The plant is valued for its timber and is also used in traditional medicine.
