Melastoma pauciflorum

Melastoma pauciflorum, commonly known as the few-flowered melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Melastomataceae and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its flowers are usually pink or white, with five petals and numerous stamens, making it visually striking.

The fruit is a capsule that releases small, winged seeds when mature.
