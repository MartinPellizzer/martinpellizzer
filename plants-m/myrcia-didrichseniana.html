Myrcia didrichseniana

Myrcia didrichseniana, commonly known as the Brazilian peppertree, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic rainforests of Brazil.

It is a member of the Myrtaceae family, characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy floral displays. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and often have a reddish hue when mature.

The flowers are usually white or pale pink, with five petals, and are followed by small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.
