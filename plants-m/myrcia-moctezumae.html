Myrcia moctezumae

Myrcia moctezumae, commonly known as the Mexican beautyberry, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Mexico and parts of Central America, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red berries that are edible for humans and birds. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and emit a strong, aromatic scent when crushed.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.
