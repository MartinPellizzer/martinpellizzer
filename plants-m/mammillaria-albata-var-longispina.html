Mammillaria albata var. longispina

Mammillaria albata var. longispina, commonly known as the long-spined white globular cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its spherical shape and white, woolly spines that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soil. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, appearing in the spring.

This variety is popular among cactus enthusiasts due to its compact size and ornamental value.
