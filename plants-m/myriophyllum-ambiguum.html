Myriophyllum ambiguum

Myriophyllum ambiguum, commonly known as watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to North America.

It has finely divided, feather-like leaves that are typically bright green and arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged or just below the water surface. It thrives in freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams.

Myriophyllum ambiguum plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
