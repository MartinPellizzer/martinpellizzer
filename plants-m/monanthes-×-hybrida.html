Monanthes × hybrida

Monanthes × hybrida, commonly known as the hybrid lily, is a botanical hybrid resulting from the cross between two species of lilies.

It is characterized by its striking, often fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and red. The plant typically features narrow, strap-like leaves and can grow to a height of 2 to 4 feet. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Due to its ornamental value, Monanthes × hybrida is widely cultivated for its beauty and versatility in landscaping.
