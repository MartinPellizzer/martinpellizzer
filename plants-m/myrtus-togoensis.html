Myrtus togoensis

Myrtus togoensis, commonly known as Togo myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to West Africa.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its narrow, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule containing aromatic seeds.

Myrtus togoensis is often used in traditional medicine and for its ornamental value in gardens.
