Melochia hirsuta f. ferruginea

Melochia hirsuta f. ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty-hair melochia, is a variant of the hairy melochia species native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy, often reddish-brown stems and leaves, which give it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically arranged in clusters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural studies.
