Malva sylvestris var. hirsuta

Malva sylvestris var. hirsuta, commonly known as hairy mallow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves with a rough, woolly texture. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, with five petals and a distinct central column. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges, meadows, and along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
