Magnolia × proctoriana

Magnolia × proctoriana, commonly known as the Proctor magnolia, is a hybrid deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The tree typically grows to a height of 15 to 30 feet and has a rounded, symmetrical shape. Its leaves are dark green, ovate, and have a smooth texture, providing a lush backdrop to its floral display.

This species is valued in ornamental horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
