Mentha longifolia var. foroiulensis

Mentha longifolia var. foroiulensis, commonly known as the Foroiulensis mint, is a variant of the long-leaf mint native to the Carpathian region.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves and flowering spikes that produce small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along streams. It has a strong, refreshing scent and is often used in local herbal remedies.

This variety is valued for its unique fragrance and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
