Matteuccia struthiopteris

Matteuccia struthiopteris, commonly known as ostrich fern, is a perennial fern native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features a distinctive, upright, fiddlehead-like frond that unfurls in early spring. The plant has a short, rhizomatous stem and produces both sterile and fertile fronds. Its leaves are bi-lobed and can grow up to 1.5 meters in length.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
