Miltonioides leucomelas

Miltonioides leucomelas, commonly known as the white Miltonia, is a tropical orchid native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, forming pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

It blooms during the warmer months, producing fragrant flowers that attract pollinators like bees and moths.
