Myroxylon peruiferum

Myroxylon peruiferum, commonly known as the copal tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its dense, aromatic wood and resinous secretions. The tree can grow up to 30 meters tall, with a broad, spreading canopy and compound leaves. Its flowers are small and yellowish, arranged in racemes, and it produces legume pods containing seeds.

The plant is valued for its durable wood and the resin used in incense and varnishes.
