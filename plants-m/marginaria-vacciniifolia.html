Marginaria vacciniifolia

Marginaria vacciniifolia, commonly known as the sweetleaf shrub, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet, featuring compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its berries, which are dark blue to black in color, are edible and often used in culinary applications.

The plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
