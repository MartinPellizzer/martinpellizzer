Magnolia kachinensis

Magnolia kachinensis, commonly known as the Kachin magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of northern Myanmar.

It is characterized by its large, showy white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate leaves with a smooth texture and a glossy surface. Its bark is typically grayish-brown and becomes fissured with age.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
