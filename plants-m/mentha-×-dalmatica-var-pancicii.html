Mentha × dalmatica var. pancicii

Mentha × dalmatica var. pancicii, commonly known as the Dalmatian mint, is a hybrid mint species native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly serrated leaves that are typically a vibrant green. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in whorls along the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This variety is often used in herbal teas and culinary applications due to its refreshing, minty fragrance.
