Melastoma lasiopetalum

Melastoma lasiopetalum, commonly known as the broad-leaved melastoma, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white, arranged in clusters at the leaf axils. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.
