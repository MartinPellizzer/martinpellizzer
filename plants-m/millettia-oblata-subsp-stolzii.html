Millettia oblata subsp. stolzii

Millettia oblata subsp.

stolzii, commonly known as the Stolz's millettia, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae. It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its compound leaves and racemose inflorescences.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. It is often found in woodland and savanna habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils.

The subspecies is notable for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation, supporting soil fertility in its native environment.
