Mentha arvensis var. schmitzii

Mentha arvensis var. schmitzii, commonly known as Schmitz's field mint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale purple or blue, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its aromatic leaves and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
