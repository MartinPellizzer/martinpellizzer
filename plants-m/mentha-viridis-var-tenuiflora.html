Mentha viridis var. tenuiflora

Mentha viridis var. tenuiflora, commonly known as narrow-leaved mint, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia and the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its slender, pointed leaves and fragrant, slightly minty aroma. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in whorls along the stems.

It is often cultivated for its aromatic properties and used in traditional herbal remedies.
