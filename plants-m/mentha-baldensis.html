Mentha baldensis

Mentha baldensis, commonly known as the bald mountain mint, is a perennial herb native to the northeastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers in clusters, which bloom in late summer. Mentha baldensis typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and rocky slopes.

It is valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
