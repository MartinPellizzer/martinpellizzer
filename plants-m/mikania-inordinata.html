Mikania inordinata

Mikania inordinata, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its rapid growth and heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests or along stream banks.

Due to its invasive nature, it can outcompete native vegetation in certain regions.
