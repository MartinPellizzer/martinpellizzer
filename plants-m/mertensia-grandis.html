Mertensia grandis

Mertensia grandis, commonly known as the grand bluebells, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy coating. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Mertensia grandis is a member of the nightshade family and is valued for its ornamental beauty in woodland gardens.
