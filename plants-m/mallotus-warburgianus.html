Mallotus warburgianus

Mallotus warburgianus, commonly known as the Warburg's mallot, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often elliptical or ovate in shape. The species is also valued for its ornamental potential and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.
