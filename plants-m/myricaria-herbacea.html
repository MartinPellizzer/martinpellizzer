Myricaria herbacea

Myricaria herbacea, commonly known as the dwarf tamarisk, is a low-growing shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, often prostrate form and small, scale-like leaves that reduce water loss in dry environments. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in poor, sandy soils.

Myricaria herbacea is well adapted to harsh conditions, making it a resilient component of desert and steppe ecosystems.
