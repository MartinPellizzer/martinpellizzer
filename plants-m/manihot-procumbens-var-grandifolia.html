Manihot procumbens var. grandifolia

Manihot procumbens var. grandifolia, commonly known as the giant arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its tubers, which are the main edible part, are starchy and can be used as a root vegetable.

The species is valued for its high yield and adaptability to various soil conditions.
