Malaxis perezii

Malaxis perezii, commonly known as the Peruvian orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and delicate, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in rocky or forested areas. Its roots are mycorrhizal, forming symbiotic relationships with fungi to enhance nutrient uptake.

Malaxis perezii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique floral structure.
