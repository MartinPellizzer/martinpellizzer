Monarda contorta

Monarda contorta, commonly known as narrowleaf horsemint, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of pink or purple. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Monarda contorta is valued for its medicinal properties and attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
