Monsonia flavescens

Monsonia flavescens, commonly known as the yellow monsonia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in grassland and rocky habitats. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its showy yellow flowers bloom in summer and are clustered in dense, terminal inflorescences.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soils.
