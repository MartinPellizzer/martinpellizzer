Malva betuloides

Malva betuloides, commonly known as the buckbean, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.
