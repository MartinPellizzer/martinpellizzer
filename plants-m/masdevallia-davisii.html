Masdevallia davisii

Masdevallia davisii, commonly known as the Davis's masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and intricate, often colorful flower structures. The plant typically features a pseudobulb and produces a single or few flowering stems with showy, asymmetrical blooms. Its leaves are usually elongated and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Masdevallia davisii thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
