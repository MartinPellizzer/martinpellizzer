Miconia scalpta

Miconia scalpta, commonly known as the broadleaf miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruits are bright red berries, which contain seeds surrounded by a fleshy, edible pulp.

Miconia scalpta is often found in humid, lowland forests and is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets.
