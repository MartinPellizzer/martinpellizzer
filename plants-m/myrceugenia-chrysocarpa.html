Myrceugenia chrysocarpa

Myrceugenia chrysocarpa, commonly known as the golden-capsicum myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the coastal regions of southern Brazil, particularly in the state of Santa Catarina. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, yellowish-orange fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Myrceugenia chrysocarpa thrives in humid, tropical climates and is typically found in coastal forests.
