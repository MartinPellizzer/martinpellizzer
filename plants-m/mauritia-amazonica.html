Mauritia amazonica

Mauritia amazonica, commonly known as the Amazonian palm, is a tall, slender palm native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its long, pinnate leaves and produces large, clustered inflorescences. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests. Its fruit is edible and contains a single seed, which is dispersed by animals.

The species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat.
