Meriania adenostemon

Meriania adenostemon, commonly known as the red-veined evergreen, is a tropical shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with prominent red veins, which give it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for ornamental use in gardens and as a houseplant.

Meriania adenostemon is relatively low maintenance, thriving in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
