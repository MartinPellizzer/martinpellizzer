Miconia sulfurea

Miconia sulfurea, commonly known as the sulfur miconia, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and northern South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are usually dark green with a glossy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit is a bright red, fleshy berry that contains numerous seeds.

Miconia sulfurea is often found in moist, shaded areas of tropical forests and is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets.
