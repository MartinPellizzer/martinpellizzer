Mahonia rotundifolia var. herveyi

Mahonia rotundifolia var. herveyi, commonly known as the Hervey's mahonia, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features compound leaves with rounded lobes, which are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, followed by blue-black berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a suitable addition to woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and seasonal floral display.
