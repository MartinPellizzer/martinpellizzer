Myrcia subulata

Myrcia subulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved myrtle, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its narrow, pointed leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its aromatic foliage and ornamental appeal make it a popular choice for horticultural use.

Myrcia subulata is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
