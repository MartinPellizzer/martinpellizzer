Mussaenda parviflora var. formosana

Mussaenda parviflora var. formosana, commonly known as the Formosan Mussaenda, is a flowering plant native to Taiwan.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered inflorescences that range in color from white to pink, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
