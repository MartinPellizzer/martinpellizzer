Miconia castaneifolia

Miconia castaneifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or purple glorybower, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces showy, violet to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. It is a member of the Melastomataceae family and is known for its rapid growth and adaptability to various soil types.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions due to its aggressive spreading habit.
