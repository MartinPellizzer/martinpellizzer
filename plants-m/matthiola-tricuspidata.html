Matthiola tricuspidata

Matthiola tricuspidata, commonly known as the three-lobed stock, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pink in color. The plant produces small, cylindrical fruits called siliques, which contain numerous seeds. Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Matthiola tricuspidata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
