Malus × micromalus

Malus × micromalus, commonly known as the Chinese crabapple, is a small ornamental tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compact size and showy flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 3–6 meters and features elliptical leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge.

The fruit, a small apple-like pome, is usually red or yellow and is used in traditional Chinese medicine.
