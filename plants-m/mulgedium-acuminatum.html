Mulgedium acuminatum

Mulgedium acuminatum, commonly known as the sharp-pointed mulgedium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink, blooming during the cooler months. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a slightly serrated edge, and the plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

Mulgedium acuminatum is often found in open grasslands and along rocky outcrops, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
