Mucuna pruriens var. sericophylla

Mucuna pruriens var. sericophylla, commonly known as velvet bean, is a tropical leguminous plant characterized by its hairy, serpentine pods and trifoliate leaves.

It produces small, yellow flowers and is native to regions of Asia, Africa, and the Americas. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it valuable in agricultural systems. It contains various bioactive compounds, including alkaloids and proteins, which contribute to its traditional medicinal uses.

The hairy texture of its stems and leaves gives it a distinctive appearance and has led to its common name.
