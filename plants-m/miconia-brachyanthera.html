Miconia brachyanthera

Miconia brachyanthera, commonly known as the red miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that are often covered in fine hairs. Its fruit is a bright red, fleshy berry that contains numerous tiny seeds.

Miconia brachyanthera is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, making it an invasive species in some regions.
