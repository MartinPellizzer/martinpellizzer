Martia physalodes

Martia physalodes, commonly known as the balloon vine, is a climbing plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive inflated, balloon-like fruits that contain seeds. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often used in ornamental gardening.

The botanical features of Martia physalodes make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological study.
