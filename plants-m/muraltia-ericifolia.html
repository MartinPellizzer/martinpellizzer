Muraltia ericifolia

Muraltia ericifolia, commonly known as the dwarf coffee plant, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stems. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in summer, followed by edible, coffee-like berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and unique fruit, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
