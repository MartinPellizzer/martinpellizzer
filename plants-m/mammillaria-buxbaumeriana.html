Mammillaria buxbaumeriana

Mammillaria buxbaumeriana, commonly known as the Mexican snowball cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its spherical shape and dense clusters of white, woolly spines that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by small, red fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in rock gardens.
