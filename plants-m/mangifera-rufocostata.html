Mangifera rufocostata

Mangifera rufocostata, commonly known as the red-costed mango, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with reddish-brown veins. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers that develop into edible fruits with a sweet, aromatic pulp. Its fruit is typically smaller than the common mango and has a unique, slightly acidic flavor.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal uses in traditional medicine.
