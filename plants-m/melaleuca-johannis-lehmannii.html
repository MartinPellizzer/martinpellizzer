Melaleuca johannis-lehmannii

Melaleuca johannis-lehmannii, commonly known as the broad-leafed tea tree, is a species native to the tropical regions of northern Australia.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree that typically grows in coastal and inland areas with well-drained soils. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped, aromatic leaves and produces small, clustered white or pink flowers. It is often found in heathlands and open woodlands, where it contributes to the local biodiversity.

The plant has been used traditionally by Indigenous Australians for its medicinal properties.
