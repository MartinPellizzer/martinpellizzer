Memecylon idukkianum

Memecylon idukkianum, commonly known as Idukki memecylon, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the family Melastomataceae and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth upper surface and hairy underside. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom during the rainy season. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that release seeds when mature.

Memecylon idukkianum is often found in moist, shaded forest understories and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
