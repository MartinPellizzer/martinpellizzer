Morisonia discolor

Morisonia discolor, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its unique, pale green to white, almost translucent petals that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. Morisonia discolor has small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.
