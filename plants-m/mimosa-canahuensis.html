Mimosa canahuensis

Mimosa canahuensis, commonly known as the red mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive, fern-like leaves that fold shut when touched. The plant produces small, pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open areas such as grasslands and disturbed sites.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
