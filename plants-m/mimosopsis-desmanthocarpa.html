Mimosopsis desmanthocarpa

Mimosopsis desmanthocarpa, commonly known as the desert mimosopsis, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its nitrogen-fixing ability through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules. The plant has small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in spring, followed by pod fruits containing several seeds. Its leaves are compound and adapted to reduce water loss, typical of xerophytic plants.

Mimosopsis desmanthocarpa is often found in rocky soils and plays a role in stabilizing desert ecosystems.
