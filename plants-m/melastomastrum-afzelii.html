Melastomastrum afzelii

Melastomastrum afzelii, commonly known as the African melastoma, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Melastomataceae and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, purple, and pink. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain areas.
