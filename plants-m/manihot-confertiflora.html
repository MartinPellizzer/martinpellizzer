Manihot confertiflora

Manihot confertiflora, commonly known as the broad-flowered manioc, is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. The plant has thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.

Despite its aesthetic appeal, it contains toxic compounds and requires careful handling.
