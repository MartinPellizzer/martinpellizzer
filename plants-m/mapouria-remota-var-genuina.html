Mapouria remota var. genuina

Mapouria remota var. genuina, commonly known as the African tulip tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant has smooth, grayish bark and elliptical, dark green leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental species in warm climates.

The tree is also valued for its rapid growth and ability to provide shade in urban landscapes.
