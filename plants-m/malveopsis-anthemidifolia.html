Malveopsis anthemidifolia

Malveopsis anthemidifolia, commonly known as the yellow malveopsis, is a flowering plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its delicate yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and rocky soils, preferring full sun exposure. Its leaves are palmately lobed and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and often branched.

Malveopsis anthemidifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
