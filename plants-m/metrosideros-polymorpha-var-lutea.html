Metrosideros polymorpha var. lutea

Metrosideros polymorpha var. lutea, commonly known as the yellow hibiscus, is a variant of the native Hawaiian hibiscus species.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which contrast with its green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant is typically shrub-like, often growing in coastal and lowland areas of Hawaii. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in Hawaiian ecosystems.
