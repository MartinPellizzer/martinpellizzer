Mayzea cerealis

Mayzea cerealis, commonly known as the grain grass, is a perennial plant native to temperate regions.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, narrow leaves that grow in dense clumps. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. Mayzea cerealis is often cultivated for its edible seeds, which are rich in nutrients.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a versatile addition to gardens and agricultural settings.
