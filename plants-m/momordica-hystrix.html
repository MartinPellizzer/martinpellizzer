Momordica hystrix

Momordica hystrix, commonly known as the spiny bitter gourd, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and heart-shaped leaves, which are typical of the Cucurbitaceae family. The plant produces elongated, spiny fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female blooms, and it relies on insects for pollination.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.
