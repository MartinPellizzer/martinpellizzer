Machaerium bondaense

Machaerium bondaense, commonly known as the Bonda grass, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces trifoliate leaves, each composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun.

Machaerium bondaense is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
