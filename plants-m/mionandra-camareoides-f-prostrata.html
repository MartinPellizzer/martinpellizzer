Mionandra camareoides f. prostrata

Mionandra camareoides f. prostrata, commonly known as the prostrate mionandra, is a unique variant of the mionandra genus native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

This plant is characterized by its low-growing, spreading habit, with small, ovate leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The flowers are usually small and clustered, producing a subtle fragrance that attracts pollinators. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
