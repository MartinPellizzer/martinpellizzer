Maxillaria scabrilinguis

Maxillaria scabrilinguis, commonly known as the rough-haired maxillaria, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, strap-shaped leaves and produces striking, fragrant flowers with purple and white petals. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in the understory of tropical forests. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are known for their intricate, velvety texture.

Maxillaria scabrilinguis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
