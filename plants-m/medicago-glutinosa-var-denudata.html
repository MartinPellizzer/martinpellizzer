Medicago glutinosa var. denudata

Medicago glutinosa var. denudata, commonly known as the naked vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant produces small, hairy pods that contain lens-shaped seeds. Native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia, it thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields and along roadsides.

This plant is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil enrichment and as a forage crop.
