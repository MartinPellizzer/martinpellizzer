Morus calva

Morus calva, commonly known as the white mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by fluffy, white fruit that is edible for both humans and animals. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its leaves, which are used in the production of silk.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
