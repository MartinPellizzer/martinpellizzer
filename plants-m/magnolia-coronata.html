Magnolia coronata

Magnolia coronata, commonly known as the crown magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The leaves are broad, ovate, and have a smooth, dark green upper surface. The tree produces elongated, woody fruits that contain bright red, fleshy seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited to moist, well-drained soils.
