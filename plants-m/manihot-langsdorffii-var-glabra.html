Manihot langsdorffii var. glabra

Manihot langsdorffii var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth manioc, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, hairless stems and leaves that are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, and its fruit is a capsule containing several seeds. It is cultivated for its starchy roots, which are processed into a staple food in various regions.

The species is notable for its adaptability to a range of soil types and its resilience to drought conditions.
