Mattiastrum karataviense

Mattiastrum karataviense, commonly known as the Karatavi sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, cushion-like structure and small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant thrives in sandy soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and limited water availability. Its leaves are typically covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Mattiastrum karataviense plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and is of interest for its ecological and potentially medicinal properties.
