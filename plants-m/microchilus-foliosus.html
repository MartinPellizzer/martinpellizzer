Microchilus foliosus

Microchilus foliosus, commonly known as the leafy microchilus, is a small, herbaceous plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or rocky crevices. The plant features delicate, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine, short hairs. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually greenish-white in color, and are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and somewhat elusive species in botanical studies.
