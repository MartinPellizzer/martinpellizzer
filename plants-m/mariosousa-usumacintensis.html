Mariosousa usumacintensis

Mariosousa usumacintensis, commonly known as the Andean bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling bells, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool and moist conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, adapting to the harsh mountain climate.

Mariosousa usumacintensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
