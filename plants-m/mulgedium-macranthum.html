Mulgedium macranthum

Mulgedium macranthum, commonly known as the large-flowered mulgedium, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, clustered inflorescences that produce large, showy flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, with petals that range in color from white to pink. Its leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Mulgedium macranthum is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats.
