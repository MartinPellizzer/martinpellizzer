Mitracarpus sellowianus var. latifolius

Mitracarpus sellowianus var. latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf mitracarpus, is a species native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, spiny fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
