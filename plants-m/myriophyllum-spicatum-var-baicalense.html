Myriophyllum spicatum var. baicalense

Myriophyllum spicatum var. baicalense, commonly known as water milfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to Asia.

It has slender, branching stems with small, opposite leaves that are often finely toothed. The plant produces tiny, floating flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as lakes and ponds.

Its rapid growth can lead to dense underwater vegetation, which can impact water quality and native plant communities.
