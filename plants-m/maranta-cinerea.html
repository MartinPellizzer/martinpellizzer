Maranta cinerea

Maranta cinerea, commonly known as the silverleaf arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking silver-green foliage, which features intricate patterns of veins and spots. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine and as a starch source.

Maranta cinerea thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
