Monanthos koordersii

Monanthos koordersii, commonly known as the Koorders' single-flower plant, is a unique member of the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its solitary, showy flower head that stands out from its foliage. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and linear, reducing water loss through transpiration.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
