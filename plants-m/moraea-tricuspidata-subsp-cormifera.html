Moraea tricuspidata subsp. cormifera

Moraea tricuspidata subsp.

cormifera, commonly known as the cormiferous moraea, is a species native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple.

The plant produces a corm-like structure, which serves as a storage organ for nutrients. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
