Memecylon peculiare

Memecylon peculiare, commonly known as the red memecylon, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows in humid, shaded environments. The plant features elliptical leaves with a glossy surface and produces small, clustered white flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, winged seeds.

Memecylon peculiare is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
