Miconia ascendens

Miconia ascendens, commonly known as the climbing miconia, is a tropical shrub or vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to climb using its tendrils, often forming dense thickets. It produces small, berry-like fruits that are an important food source for various birds and mammals.

Due to its aggressive growth, it is considered a potential invasive species in some regions.
