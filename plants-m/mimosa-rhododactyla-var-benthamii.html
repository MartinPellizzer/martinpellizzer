Mimosa rhododactyla var. benthamii

Mimosa rhododactyla var. benthamii, commonly known as the bentham's sensitive plant, is a species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like foliage and its ability to fold its leaves in response to touch, a phenomenon known as nyctinasty. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a subtle ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate sunlight, making it suitable for semi-shaded garden environments.

Its unique botanical features make it a fascinating subject for both horticultural study and ecological observation.
