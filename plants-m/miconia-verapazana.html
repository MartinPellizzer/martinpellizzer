Miconia verapazana

Miconia verapazana, commonly known as the Verapaz miconia, is a tropical shrub native to Central America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy, ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that are often surrounded by red bracts, making it visually striking. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that release seeds when mature.

Miconia verapazana is often found in moist, shaded forest understories and is valued for its ornamental qualities.
