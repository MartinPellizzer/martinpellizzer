Melampodium montanum var. viridulum

Melampodium montanum var. viridulum, commonly known as the greenleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the central and southern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped, dark green leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in prairie and open woodland habitats.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
