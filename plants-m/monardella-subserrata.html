Monardella subserrata

Monardella subserrata, commonly known as narrowleaf monardella, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant features slender, lance-shaped leaves with slightly serrated edges, and it produces clusters of pink to lavender flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry meadows and open woodlands.

Its nectar attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to local biodiversity.
