Malcolmia zachlensis

Malcolmia zachlensis, commonly known as the Syrian mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features showy, yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

Malcolmia zachlensis is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
