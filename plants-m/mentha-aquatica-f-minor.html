Mentha aquatica f. minor

Mentha aquatica f. minor, commonly known as water mint, is a subspecies of the water mint plant native to moist environments in Europe and Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces small, pale blue or white flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in wet soils near streams, ponds, and marshes, making it a common sight in aquatic habitats.

Its leaves and flowering tops are used in herbal teas and traditional medicine for their cooling and digestive properties.
