Mesembryanthemum tricolorum

Mesembryanthemum tricolorum, commonly known as the three-colored ice plant, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick leaves and stems. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a valuable addition to low-maintenance landscapes.
