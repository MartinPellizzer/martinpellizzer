Melica anomala

Melica anomala, commonly known as the slender meadowgrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is a perennial species characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It is often found in moist habitats such as meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks.

Melica anomala is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and maintaining soil stability in its native range.
