Mammillaria tenampensis

Mammillaria tenampensis, commonly known as the Ténampé mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and is often covered in white to pale blue spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
