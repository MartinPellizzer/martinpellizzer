Morus alba var. macrophylla

Morus alba var. macrophylla, commonly known as the Chinese mulberry or white mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically pale green on the underside. The tree produces small, aggregate fruits known as mulberries, which are edible and often used in food and traditional medicine. It thrives in temperate climates and is known for its rapid growth and adaptability to various soil types.

The plant is also valued for its role in silviculture and as a source of fiber.
