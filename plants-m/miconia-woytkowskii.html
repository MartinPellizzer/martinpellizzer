Miconia woytkowskii

Miconia woytkowskii, commonly known as the Brazilian glorybower, is a tropical plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its leaves are usually opposite and have a smooth, waxy surface.

Miconia woytkowskii is known for its rapid growth and has been introduced to other regions, sometimes becoming invasive.
