Millettia pulchra var. microphylla

Millettia pulchra var. microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved velvet tamarind, is a climbing legume native to Southeast Asia.

It features compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets that give it its varietal name. The plant produces yellowish-orange flowers followed by elongated, woody pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

The species is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agroforestry systems.
