Mediolobivia boedekeriana

Mediolobivia boedekeriana, commonly known as the Boedeker's ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic orchid species native to the tropical rainforests of Central America.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent petals and a unique floral structure that resembles a ghostly apparition, hence its common name. The plant thrives in shaded, humid environments and relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake. Its flowers bloom briefly during the rainy season, producing a subtle fragrance that attracts specific pollinators.

Due to habitat destruction and over-collection, it is considered critically endangered and is protected in several conservation programs.
