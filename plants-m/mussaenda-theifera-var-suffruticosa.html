Mussaenda theifera var. suffruticosa

Mussaenda theifera var. suffruticosa, commonly known as the thief's glory or golden glory, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow bracts that surround small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sun.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
