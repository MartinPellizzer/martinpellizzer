Myrceugenia campestris var. distans

Myrceugenia campestris var. distans, commonly known as the coastal myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Brazil.

It typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils and saline conditions. The plant features small, elliptical leaves that are dark green and glossy on the upper surface. Its flowers are small, white or pale pink, and it produces edible, red, fleshy berries.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in coastal ecosystems.
