Microstegium debile

Microstegium debile, commonly known as the slender wood sorrel, is a small perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellow flowers in the spring. Its stems are slender and often hairy, and it is known for its delicate appearance.

Microstegium debile is part of the Oxalidaceae family, which is characterized by its calcium oxalate crystals in the leaves.
