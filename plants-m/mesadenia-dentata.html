Mesadenia dentata

Mesadenia dentata, commonly known as the serrated mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees, primarily in tropical and subtropical regions.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are typically white or greenish-white, arranged in clusters. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds and other animals.

Its root system forms haustoria, which penetrate the host's vascular system to extract water and nutrients.
