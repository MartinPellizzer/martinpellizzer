Manihot acuminatissima

Manihot acuminatissima, commonly known as the Brazilian arrowroot, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces striking yellow flowers with a red calyx, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy coating.

It is valued for its starchy roots, which are used in traditional medicine and as a food source.
