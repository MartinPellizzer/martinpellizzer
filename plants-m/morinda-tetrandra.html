Morinda tetrandra

Morinda tetrandra, commonly known as the Indian madder, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces red-orange berries that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals. Its roots and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their purported therapeutic properties.

Morinda tetrandra is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
