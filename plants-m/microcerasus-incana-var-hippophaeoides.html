Microcerasus incana var. hippophaeoides

Microcerasus incana var. hippophaeoides, commonly known as the Chinese sandthorn, is a shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its grayish-white, woody stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, orange berries that are edible but have a tart flavor. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses.
