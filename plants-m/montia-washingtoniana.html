Montia washingtoniana

Montia washingtoniana, commonly known as Washington's mountain-mint, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and it produces small, clustered white or pale purple flowers.
