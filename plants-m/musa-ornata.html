Musa ornata

Musa ornata, commonly known as the ornamental banana, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Musaceae family and is characterized by its large, broad leaves and striking inflorescence. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers are usually yellow or white and are followed by elongated, fleshy fruits.

Musa ornata is often cultivated for its ornamental value rather than for its edible fruit.
