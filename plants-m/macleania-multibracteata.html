Macleania multibracteata

Macleania multibracteata, commonly known as the velvetleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree with smooth, often hairy stems and alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the dry season. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.

Macleania multibracteata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
