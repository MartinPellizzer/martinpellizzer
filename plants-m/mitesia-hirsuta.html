Mitesia hirsuta

Mitesia hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy mitesia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters. The plant features simple, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it its common name. Its flowers are small, clustered in racemes, and typically have five petals.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.
