Mentha × morrenii var. latiforns

Mentha × morrenii var. latiforns, commonly known as the broad-leaf peppermint, is a hybrid mint species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and strong, aromatic scent. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and prefers well-drained soil in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are small and tubular, appearing in clusters, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value and essential oils.

This variety is also valued in traditional medicine for its cooling and soothing properties.
