Muhlenbergia reederorum

Muhlenbergia reederorum, commonly known as Reeder's muhlenbergia, is a tallgrass prairie grass native to the central United States.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and inflorescences that form dense, spike-like clusters. The plant produces small, spikelet-based flowers that are typically pale green to brown in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open grasslands and along roadsides.

Muhlenbergia reederorum plays an important ecological role in maintaining biodiversity and soil stability in prairie ecosystems.
