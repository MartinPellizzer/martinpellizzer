Melissa vulgaris variegata

Melissa vulgaris variegata, commonly known as lemon balm variegated, is a cultivar of the lemon balm plant.

It is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia, characterized by its aromatic, serrated leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its variegated leaves display shades of green and white, making it visually distinct.

It is often grown for its calming fragrance and ornamental value in gardens.
