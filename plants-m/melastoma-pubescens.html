Melastoma pubescens

Melastoma pubescens, commonly known as the hairy melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a hairy texture, giving it its common name. Its flowers are usually small, clustered, and have five petals, often in shades of pink or white.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, round seeds.
