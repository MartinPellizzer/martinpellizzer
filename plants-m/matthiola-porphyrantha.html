Matthiola porphyrantha

Matthiola porphyrantha, commonly known as the Porphyrantha Stocks, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically purple or pink in color, arranged in racemes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, which extends from spring to early autumn.
