Mentha × stricta

Mentha × stricta, commonly known as rigid mint, is a hybrid mint species that exhibits a combination of traits from its parent species.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or blue in color, arranged in whorled clusters. Mentha × stricta is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

This species is often used in herbal remedies and as a culinary herb due to its aromatic and slightly minty flavor.
