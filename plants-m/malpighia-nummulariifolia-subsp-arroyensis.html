Malpighia nummulariifolia subsp. arroyensis

Malpighia nummulariifolia subsp.

arroyensis, commonly known as the Mexican snowberry, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America. It belongs to the Malpighiaceae family and is characterized by its small, white, clustered flowers.

The plant produces bright red, fleshy berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners in suitable climates.
