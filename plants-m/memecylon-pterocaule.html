Memecylon pterocaule

Memecylon pterocaule, commonly known as the broad-leaved memecylon, is a tropical shrub belonging to the Melastomataceae family.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are usually smooth and glossy. The plant produces small, clustered flowers with five petals, typically in shades of white or pink, which bloom during the rainy season. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, dry seeds.

Memecylon pterocaule is often found in moist, shaded forest understories in Southeast Asia and parts of Africa.
