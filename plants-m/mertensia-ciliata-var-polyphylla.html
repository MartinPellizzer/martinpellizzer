Mertensia ciliata var. polyphylla

Mertensia ciliata var. polyphylla, commonly known as the Appalachian bluebell, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features basal leaves that are heart-shaped and often have hairy margins. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a key component of eastern woodland ecosystems.
