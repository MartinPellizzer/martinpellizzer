Melaleuca pendulina

Melaleuca pendulina, commonly known as the weeping melaleuca, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its pendulous, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in shades of white or pink. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces woody capsules as fruit. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or sandy habitats.

Melaleuca pendulina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
