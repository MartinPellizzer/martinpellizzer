Mimosa bombycina

Mimosa bombycina, commonly known as the silk tree or velvet mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a small, deciduous tree or shrub that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters. The plant is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and showy, ball-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. It produces long, slender pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adaptable to a variety of soil types.
