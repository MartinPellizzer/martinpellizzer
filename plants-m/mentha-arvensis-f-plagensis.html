Mentha arvensis f. plagensis

Mentha arvensis f. plagensis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf field mint, is a subspecies of the broad-leaf mint.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves and small, pale blue to purple flowers. This plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.

Mentha arvensis f. plagensis is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, similar to other members of the mint family.
