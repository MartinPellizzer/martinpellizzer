Machaerium spicatum

Machaerium spicatum, commonly known as the spike machaerium, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant has pinnately compound leaves and produces pods that contain several seeds.

It is often found in wetlands and along riverbanks, where it plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various insects and birds.
