Mammillaria polymorpha

Mammillaria polymorpha, commonly known as the "Mexican snowball cactus," is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its globular shape and dense covering of white, woolly spines. The plant produces small, pink or red flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appearance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cultivation.
