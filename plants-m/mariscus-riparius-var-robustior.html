Mariscus riparius var. robustior

Mariscus riparius var. robustior, commonly known as the robust marsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh environments.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in coastal areas and estuaries. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually green or bluish-green in color. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, which are typically green or reddish-brown.

It plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
