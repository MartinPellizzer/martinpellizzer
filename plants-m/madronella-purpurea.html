Madronella purpurea

Madronella purpurea, commonly known as the purple madrona, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a silvery-white underside, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
