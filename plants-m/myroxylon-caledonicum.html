Myroxylon caledonicum

Myroxylon caledonicum, commonly known as the Caldoan ebony, is a tropical tree native to the island of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its dense, dark hardwood, which is highly valued for its durability and rich color. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, woody pods containing flat, elliptical seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a rough, textured surface.

The plant is also noted for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments within its native habitat.
