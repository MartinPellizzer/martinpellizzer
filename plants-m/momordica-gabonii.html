Momordica gabonii

Momordica gabonii, commonly known as Gabon bitter melon, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to Central and West Africa, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant produces striking yellow flowers and elongated, spiny fruits that resemble those of other bitter melon species. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a rough texture, and the plant typically grows as a climbing vine.

Botanically, it is characterized by its distinctive fruit structure and its ability to produce both male and female flowers on the same plant.
