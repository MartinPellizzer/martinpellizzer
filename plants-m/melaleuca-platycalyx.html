Melaleuca platycalyx

Melaleuca platycalyx, commonly known as the narrow-leaved paperbark, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, flaky bark that peels away in thin layers, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slightly wavy margin.

Melaleuca platycalyx is adapted to coastal and sandy soils, and it is often found in heathlands and open forests.
