Melastoma ledifolium

Melastoma ledifolium, commonly known as the small-leaved melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Melastomataceae and is characterized by its glossy, oval-shaped leaves and showy, clustered pink or red flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as tropical forests and is often found in undergrowth. It has a compact growth habit and is sometimes used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.
