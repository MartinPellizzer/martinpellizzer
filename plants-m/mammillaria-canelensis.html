Mammillaria canelensis

Mammillaria canelensis, commonly known as the Canelensis Hedgehog Cactus, is a small, globular cactus native to Mexico.

It features a compact, spiny stem with white to pale green ribs and is covered in white to yellowish tubercles. The plant produces yellow flowers in the spring, which are typically borne at the base of the stems. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and ease of care, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.
