Morus bombycis var. ikuchuensis

Morus bombycis var. ikuchuensis, commonly known as the Japanese mulberry, is a variant of the mulberry tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, non-edible fruits. The plant is often cultivated for its role in silk production, as it serves as a food source for silkworms. Botanically, it belongs to the Moraceae family and is distinguished by its unique leaf morphology and growth habits.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas or along riverbanks.
