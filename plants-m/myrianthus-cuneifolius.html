Myrianthus cuneifolius

Myrianthus cuneifolius, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It features palmate leaves with five to seven elliptical, cuneate leaflets that are dark green and glossy on top. The plant produces showy, orange-red bracts that resemble flowers, which are actually modified leaves. It blooms during the dry season, typically from July to September, and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping due to its vibrant foliage and seasonal display.
