Massonia undulata

Massonia undulata, commonly known as the undulating massonia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, ribbed stems and leaves that are typically blue-green in color, adapted to store water. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white and yellow, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and xeriscapes.
