Myrcia dictyophylla var. robustior

Myrcia dictyophylla var. robustior, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall. The plant features glossy, elliptical leaves and produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The plant is noted for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
