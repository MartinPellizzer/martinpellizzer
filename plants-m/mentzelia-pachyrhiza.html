Mentzelia pachyrhiza

Mentzelia pachyrhiza, commonly known as the desert bluebell, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in desert scrub and rocky soils. The plant has a deep taproot system that allows it to access water from deep underground, making it well adapted to drought conditions. Its flowers are typically blue or purple and bloom in the spring, adding a striking visual contrast to the desert landscape.

Mentzelia pachyrhiza is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used by indigenous peoples for various traditional purposes.
