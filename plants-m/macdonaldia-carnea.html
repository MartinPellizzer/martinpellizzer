Macdonaldia carnea

Macdonaldia carnea, commonly known as the pink fairy orchid, is an herbaceous perennial native to the alpine regions of southeastern Australia.

It features slender, hairy stems and produces delicate, pinkish-white flowers with a distinctive, spurred lip. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth. Its unique floral structure is adapted for pollination by specific insects, particularly bees.

Macdonaldia carnea is a rare and endangered species, making it a focus of conservation efforts in its native habitat.
