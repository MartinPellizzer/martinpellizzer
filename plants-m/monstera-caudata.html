Monstera caudata

Monstera caudata, commonly known as the split-leaf philodendron, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that develop deep splits as they mature. The plant has a climbing habit and produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by orange berries. Its foliage is often used in ornamental horticulture for its dramatic appearance.

Monstera caudata thrives in bright, indirect light and requires regular watering to maintain its lush, vibrant growth.
