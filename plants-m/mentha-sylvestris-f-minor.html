Mentha sylvestris f. minor

Mentha sylvestris f. minor, commonly known as wild mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its distinct menthol scent.

This subspecies is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
