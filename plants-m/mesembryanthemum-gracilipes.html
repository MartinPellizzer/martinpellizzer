Mesembryanthemum gracilipes

Mesembryanthemum gracilipes, commonly known as the slender ice plant, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically blooming in spring and summer. It is a low-growing plant, often forming dense mats, and is well adapted to rocky or sandy soils.

Mesembryanthemum gracilipes is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and drought tolerance.
