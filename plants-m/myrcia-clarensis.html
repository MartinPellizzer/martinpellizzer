Myrcia clarensis

Myrcia clarensis, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. Myrcia clarensis is often found in humid, tropical environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Its fruit, which is a small, red drupe, is also used in traditional medicine and local cuisines.
