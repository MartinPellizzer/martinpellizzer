Mezilaurus synandra

Mezilaurus synandra, commonly known as the Cape snowbush, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of white, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for Mediterranean climates.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
