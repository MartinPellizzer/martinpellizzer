Miscanthus oligostachyus subsp. longiberbis

Miscanthus oligostachyus subsp.

longiberbis, commonly known as the long-panicle miscanthus, is a perennial grass native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and its long, narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces panicles of small, fluffy flowers that are typically silver or white in color. It thrives in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in ecological restoration projects.
