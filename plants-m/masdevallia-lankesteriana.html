Masdevallia lankesteriana

Masdevallia lankesteriana, commonly known as the Lankester's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the cloud forests of Colombia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers that come in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows on rocky slopes and has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are elongated and grass-like, and it thrives in cool, humid environments with indirect light.

Masdevallia lankesteriana is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
