Meiogyne virgata

Meiogyne virgata, commonly known as the slender water lily, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats, such as ponds and marshes. The plant features broad, floating leaves with a waxy surface, and its flowers are usually white or pale pink, blooming in the early morning. Meiogyne virgata has a rhizomatous root system that helps it anchor in muddy substrates.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
