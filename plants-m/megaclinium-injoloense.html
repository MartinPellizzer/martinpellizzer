Megaclinium injoloense

Megaclinium injoloense, commonly known as the Injolo grass, is a flowering plant native to the grasslands of West Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and is adapted to semi-arid climates. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in panicles, and it is often used for forage by local livestock.

Due to its ecological importance and resilience, it plays a significant role in maintaining soil stability in its native habitat.
