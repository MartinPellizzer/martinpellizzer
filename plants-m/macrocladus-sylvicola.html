Macrocladus sylvicola

Macrocladus sylvicola, commonly known as the forest spurge, is a herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of North America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is known for its diverse and often toxic species. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small and clustered in cyathia, a unique structure typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

The plant contains latex, which can cause skin irritation, and is often used in traditional medicine.
