Mirabilis jalapa f. parviflora

Mirabilis jalapa f. parviflora, commonly known as the dwarf four o'clock, is a compact variant of the common four o'clock plant.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant is native to the Americas and thrives in warm climates with well-drained soil. It produces showy, tubular flowers that open in the late afternoon and close at dawn.

This variety is popular in gardens for its ornamental value and easy care requirements.
