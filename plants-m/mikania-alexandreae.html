Mikania alexandreae

Mikania alexandreae, commonly known as the Brazilian wisteria, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rapid growth and trailing habit. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in loose inflorescences. Its leaves are opposite, heart-shaped, and often have a fuzzy texture.

Mikania alexandreae is known for its ornamental value but can be invasive in certain regions.
