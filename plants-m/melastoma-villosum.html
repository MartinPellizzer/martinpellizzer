Melastoma villosum

Melastoma villosum, commonly known as the hairy melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its small, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or red. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces, which contribute to its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Melastoma villosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
