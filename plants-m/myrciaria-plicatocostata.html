Myrciaria plicatocostata

Myrciaria plicatocostata, commonly known as the Brazilian blackberry, is a shrub native to Brazil, characterized by its dark, edible berries.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is distinguished by its plicate (ridged) costate (ribbed) leaves. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are small and white, and the fruit develops from the flower, ripening to a deep black or dark purple color.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its use in traditional medicine.
