Manisuris myurus

Manisuris myurus, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped blooms that emit a sweet scent, enhancing its ecological role in nighttime ecosystems. Its leaves are typically broad and heart-shaped, and it thrives in well-drained soils with adequate sunlight.

The botanical features of Manisuris myurus make it a unique and valuable species in both horticultural and ecological contexts.
