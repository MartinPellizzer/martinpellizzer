Manihot palmata var. digitiformis

Manihot palmata var. digitiformis, commonly known as the fingerleaf arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its palm-like leaves with deeply lobed, finger-like segments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its rhizomes are used for their starchy roots, which are processed into a flour-like substance.

The species is valued for both its ornamental foliage and its potential agricultural applications.
