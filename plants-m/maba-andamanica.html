Maba andamanica

Maba andamanica, commonly known as the Andaman maba, is a small evergreen tree native to the Andaman Islands.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange-red fruits that contain a single seed. It thrives in moist, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
