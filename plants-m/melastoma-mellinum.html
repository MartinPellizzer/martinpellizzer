Melastoma mellinum

Melastoma mellinum, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or purple tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the winter months. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.
