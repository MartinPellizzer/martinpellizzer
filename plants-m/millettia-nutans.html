Millettia nutans

Millettia nutans, commonly known as the drooping millettia, is a deciduous climbing plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind. Its root system has been studied for its potential medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.

Millettia nutans is often found in forest understories, where it thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil.
