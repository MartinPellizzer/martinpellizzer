Mystacidium appendiculatum

Mystacidium appendiculatum, commonly known as the appendiculate mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on a variety of host trees, primarily in tropical and subtropical regions.

It possesses a specialized root system that allows it to attach to its host and absorb water and nutrients. The plant has small, leathery leaves and produces white or pale yellow flowers. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that contains seeds dispersed by birds.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can significantly impact the health of its host trees if left unchecked.
