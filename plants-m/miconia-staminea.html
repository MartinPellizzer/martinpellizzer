Miconia staminea

Miconia staminea, commonly known as the white miconia, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers, which are typically arranged in inflorescences at the base of the leaves. Miconia staminea is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, making it an invasive species in some regions.

Its botanical features make it an important subject in ecological and horticultural studies.
