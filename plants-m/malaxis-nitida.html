Malaxis nitida

Malaxis nitida, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, linear leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests or rocky crevices. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its unique, intricate floral structure.

Malaxis nitida is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
