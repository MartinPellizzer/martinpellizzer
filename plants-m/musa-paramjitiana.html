Musa paramjitiana

Musa paramjitiana, commonly known as the Indian banana, is a species of banana plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its large, elongated leaves and produces clusters of fruit that are typically smaller than those of common bananas. The plant is a herbaceous perennial, growing in tropical and subtropical climates. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same inflorescence.

Musa paramjitiana is valued for its edible fruit and is often cultivated for both culinary and ornamental purposes.
