Mimulus alatus

Mimulus alatus, commonly known as the trailing monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant orange or yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, elliptic to ovate leaves that are often hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, streambanks, and disturbed areas.

Mimulus alatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
