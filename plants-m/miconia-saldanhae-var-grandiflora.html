Miconia saldanhae var. grandiflora

Miconia saldanhae var. grandiflora, commonly known as the grandiflora miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, attracting various pollinators. Its fruits are small, three-lobed berries that turn red when mature, containing several seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
