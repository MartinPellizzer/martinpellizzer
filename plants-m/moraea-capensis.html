Moraea capensis

Moraea capensis, commonly known as the Cape bellflower, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces striking, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in coastal and mountainous regions, where it adapts well to dry conditions.

Moraea capensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
