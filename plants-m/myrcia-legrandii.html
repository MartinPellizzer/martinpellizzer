Myrcia legrandii

Myrcia legrandii, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its aromatic, evergreen leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Myrcia legrandii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
