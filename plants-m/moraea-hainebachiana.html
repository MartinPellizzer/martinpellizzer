Moraea hainebachiana

Moraea hainebachiana, commonly known as the Hainebach's glory, is a striking member of the Iridaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant produces a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves and has a preference for well-drained soils in sunny to partially shaded environments. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.

Moraea hainebachiana is also noted for its ability to thrive in coastal areas due to its tolerance for salt spray.
