Margaretta rosea

Margaretta rosea, commonly known as the pink marguerite, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with white petals surrounding a central cluster of yellow disk florets. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 feet and prefers well-drained soil in full sun. Its leaves are dark green, slightly hairy, and arranged in a basal rosette.

Margaretta rosea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is relatively low maintenance.
