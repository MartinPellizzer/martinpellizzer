Myrcia affinis

Myrcia affinis, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are leathery and glossy, while its flowers are usually pink or white.

Myrcia affinis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
