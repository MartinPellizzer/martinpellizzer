Morisia hypogaea

Morisia hypogaea, commonly known as the groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a cluster at the top of the stem, which bloom from late winter to early spring. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often covered in fine hairs.

The plant is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
