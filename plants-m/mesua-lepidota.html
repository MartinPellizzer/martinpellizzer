Mesua lepidota

Mesua lepidota, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often appearing in clusters. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its hard, durable wood used in construction and furniture.
