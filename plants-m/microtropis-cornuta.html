Microtropis cornuta

Microtropis cornuta, commonly known as the horned microtropis, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive horn-like structures on its stems, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in racemes. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Microtropis cornuta is often found in tropical rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appearance and potential medicinal properties.
