Mutisia peduncularis

Mutisia peduncularis, commonly known as the yellow mutisia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers. It typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as grasslands and open woodlands.

Its botanical structure supports pollination by insects, contributing to its ecological role in the region.
