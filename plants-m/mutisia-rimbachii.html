Mutisia rimbachii

Mutisia rimbachii, commonly known as the Rimbach's mutisia, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy, yellow-orange ray flowers surrounding a central disk, typical of the family. It thrives in high-altitude environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Mutisia rimbachii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native ecosystems.
