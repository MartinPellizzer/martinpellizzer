Macodes dendrophila

Macodes dendrophila, commonly known as the tree orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and aerial roots. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining substrates for optimal growth.

Its unique appearance and blooming habits make it a popular choice for botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
