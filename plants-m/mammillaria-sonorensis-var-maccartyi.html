Mammillaria sonorensis var. maccartyi

Mammillaria sonorensis var. maccartyi, commonly known as the Sonoran Mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to the Sonoran Desert.

It is characterized by its small, globular shape and is typically covered in white to pale blue spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking contrast to its green body. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for arid environments.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in dry conditions.
