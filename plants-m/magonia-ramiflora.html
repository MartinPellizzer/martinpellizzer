Magonia ramiflora

Magonia ramiflora, commonly known as the branching flamebush, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, branched stems and clusters of bright orange-red tubular flowers. The plant thrives in humid, sunny environments and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in a fine, silvery pubescence.

Magonia ramiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
