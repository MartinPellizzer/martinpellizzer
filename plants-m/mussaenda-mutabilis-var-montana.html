Mussaenda mutabilis var. montana

Mussaenda mutabilis var. montana, commonly known as the mountain mussaenda, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow, depending on the stage of bloom. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are arranged in clusters and are often visited by bees and other pollinators.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
