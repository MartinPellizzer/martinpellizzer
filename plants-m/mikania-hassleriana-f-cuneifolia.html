Mikania hassleriana f. cuneifolia

Mikania hassleriana f. cuneifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mile-a-minute weed, is a fast-growing vine native to the Americas.

It features slender, trailing stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are followed by fluffy, white seeds that are easily dispersed by wind. It thrives in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forests edges, often forming dense mats that can outcompete native vegetation.

Its rapid growth and invasive tendencies make it a significant ecological concern in many regions.
