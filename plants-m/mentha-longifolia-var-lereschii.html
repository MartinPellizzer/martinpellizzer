Mentha longifolia var. lereschii

Mentha longifolia var. lereschii, commonly known as Leresch's mint, is a variant of the long-leaf mint species.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves and delicate purple flowers that bloom in summer. This perennial herb is native to southern Europe and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in rocky or woodland areas, where it forms dense mats.

Its essential oils contain menthol and other compounds, making it valuable in both culinary and medicinal applications.
