Malaxis longissima

Malaxis longissima, commonly known as the long-stemmed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and relatively small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the soil.

Malaxis longissima is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in horticultural settings.
