Mespilus pentagyna var. amur

Mespilus pentagyna var. amur, commonly known as the Amur medlar, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray-brown bark and alternate, simple, ovate to elliptic leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, followed by unique, fleshy, apple-like fruits that ripen in late summer. The fruit has a distinctive musk-like aroma and is often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

It thrives in temperate climates and is adapted to well-drained soils.
