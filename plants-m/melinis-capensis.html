Melinis capensis

Melinis capensis, commonly known as the African lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces small, pale green to brown spikelets that are arranged in clusters along the stems. Melinis capensis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.

It is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and is sometimes used for erosion control.
