Melothria mucronata var. umbellata

Melothria mucronata var. umbellata, commonly known as the red gooseberry, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It produces small, fleshy, yellow-orange berries that are edible and have a tangy, acidic flavor. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, yellowish flowers. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The varietal name 'umbellata' refers to its umbellate inflorescence, which consists of clusters of small flowers.
