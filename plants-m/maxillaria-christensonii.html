Maxillaria christensonii

Maxillaria christensonii, commonly known as the Christenson's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with intricate patterns on the petals. Its leaves are typically linear and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention in its native habitat.

Maxillaria christensonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
