Mirabilis carletonii

Mirabilis carletonii, commonly known as the Carleton's four-o'clock, is a flowering plant native to the United States, specifically found in Texas and New Mexico.

It belongs to the Nyctaginaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that open in the late afternoon. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are alternate and lance-shaped, while its stems are hairy and branching.
