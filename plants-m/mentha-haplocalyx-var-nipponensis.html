Mentha haplocalyx var. nipponensis

Mentha haplocalyx var. nipponensis, commonly known as Japanese mint, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, narrow leaves and clusters of small, pale blue or white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and square stems.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications due to their menthol-like scent and cooling effects.
