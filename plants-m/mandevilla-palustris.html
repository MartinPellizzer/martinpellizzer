Mandevilla palustris

Mandevilla palustris, commonly known as swamp mandevilla, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is a climbing vine with opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow, blooming throughout the growing season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Mandevilla palustris is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and vibrant blooms.
