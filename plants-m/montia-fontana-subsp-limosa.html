Montia fontana subsp. limosa

Montia fontana subsp.

limosa, commonly known as the limosa watercress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to moist, rocky habitats in North America. It belongs to the Montiaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, glossy leaves.

The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that are typically arranged in racemes. It thrives in cool, wet environments and is often found in stream banks and mountainous regions.

Its foliage is edible and has been used historically in traditional culinary practices.
