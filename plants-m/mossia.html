Mossia

Mossia, commonly known as the common moss, is a non-vascular plant that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It belongs to the family Bryaceae and is characterized by its dense, carpet-like growth pattern. The plant lacks true roots, stems, and leaves, instead having structures called rhizoids for anchoring and absorbing moisture. Mossia reproduces through spores, which are released from tiny capsules known as sporangia.

These plants play an important role in soil stabilization and provide habitat for various microorganisms.
