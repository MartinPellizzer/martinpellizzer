Malapoenna calicaris

Malapoenna calicaris, commonly known as the Malapoenna bell-shaped plant, is a unique species native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped flowers, which are typically pale green to white in color. The plant has a short, woody stem and produces small, clustered leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it adaptable to arid environments.
