Melaleuca discolor

Melaleuca discolor, commonly known as the slender bottlebrush, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, cylindrical flower spikes that are typically pink to red in color. The plant has aromatic leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish bloom. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for coastal and dryland environments.

Melaleuca discolor is also known for its ability to colonize disturbed areas, contributing to its ecological significance.
