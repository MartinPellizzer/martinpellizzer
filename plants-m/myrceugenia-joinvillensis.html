Myrceugenia joinvillensis

Myrceugenia joinvillensis, commonly known as the Valdivian oak, is a species of evergreen tree native to the temperate rainforests of southern South America.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves and can grow up to 30 meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by edible, acorn-like fruits. It plays a vital role in the ecosystem as a habitat and food source for various wildlife species.

Myrceugenia joinvillensis is also valued for its durable wood and is often found in moist, shaded environments.
