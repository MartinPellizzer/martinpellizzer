Machaerium aculeatum

Machaerium aculeatum, commonly known as the spiked machaerium, is a tropical shrub native to regions of South and Central America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features slender, branching stems with small, trifoliate leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are typically pale yellow or greenish-white, arranged in dense clusters, and are pollinated by insects.

The plant is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a forage crop for livestock.
