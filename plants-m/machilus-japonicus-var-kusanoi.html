Machilus japonicus var. kusanoi

Machilus japonicus var. kusanoi, commonly known as Japanese Machilus, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and avocados. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a glossy green upper surface. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark purple berries.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to a variety of soil types.
