Melica scabrosa subsp. radula

Melica scabrosa subsp.

radula, commonly known as the rough milletgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and hairy, rough-textured leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing minute, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native plant communities and a subject of ecological study.
