Morinda collina

Morinda collina, commonly known as the red root or red sandalwood, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by bright red or orange berries. Morinda collina is often found in coastal areas and sandy soils, where it thrives in well-drained conditions.

Its root system is notable for its medicinal properties, which have been used in traditional herbal remedies.
