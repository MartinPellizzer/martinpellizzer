Montia fontana var. lamprosperma

Montia fontana var. lamprosperma, commonly known as the silverleaf watercress, is a variant of the watercress species native to moist, rocky habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its finely divided, silver-tinged leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in shallow, flowing water or damp soil, thriving in temperate climates. Its small, white flowers bloom in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value.

Despite its beauty, it is not commonly cultivated for agricultural purposes due to its preference for specific environmental conditions.
