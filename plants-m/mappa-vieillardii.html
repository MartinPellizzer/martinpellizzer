Mappa vieillardii

Mappa vieillardii, commonly known as the African mountain orchid, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, delicate flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested areas, preferring well-drained soils and cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a basal rosette.

Mappa vieillardii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
