Musa banksii var. samoensis

Musa banksii var. samoensis, commonly known as the Samoan banana, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Musaceae family and is characterized by its large, broad leaves and clustered inflorescences. The plant produces edible berries that are typically consumed fresh or used in local cuisines. It thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil and requires ample sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in various tropical regions for both agricultural and horticultural purposes.
