Melaleuca lanceolata subsp. lanceolata

Melaleuca lanceolata subsp.

lanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bottlebrush, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and striking red or pink bottlebrush-like flowers that appear in clusters.

The plant is part of the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting native wildlife.
