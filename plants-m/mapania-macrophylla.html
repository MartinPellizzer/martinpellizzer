Mapania macrophylla

Mapania macrophylla, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter, providing a unique habitat for various aquatic life. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in marshes and ponds. Its showy white flowers bloom at night, attracting pollinators such as bats and insects.

The thick, waxy leaves help the plant float on the water's surface while supporting a diverse ecosystem.
