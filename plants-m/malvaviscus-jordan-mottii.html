Malvaviscus jordan-mottii

Malvaviscus jordan-mottii, commonly known as Jordan-Mott's mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton, and is characterized by its showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories and along stream banks. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the flowers range in color from pink to red, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
