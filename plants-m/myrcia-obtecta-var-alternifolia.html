Myrcia obtecta var. alternifolia

Myrcia obtecta var. alternifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf myrtle, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are alternate in arrangement, giving it a distinct appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by dark blue or black berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its leaves and fruits are used in traditional medicine and for ornamental purposes.
