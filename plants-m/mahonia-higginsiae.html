Mahonia higginsiae

Mahonia higginsiae, commonly known as the Higgins' Mahonia, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, featuring dark green, leathery leaves with spiny margins. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by blue-black berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and woodland settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its hardy nature and ability to withstand various environmental conditions.
