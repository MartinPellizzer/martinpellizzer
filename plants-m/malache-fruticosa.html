Malache fruticosa

Malache fruticosa, commonly known as the shrub mallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its woody stems and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are usually ovate or lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Malache fruticosa is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive appearance and drought tolerance.
