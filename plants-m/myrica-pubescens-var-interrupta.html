Myrica pubescens var. interrupta

Myrica pubescens var. interrupta, commonly known as the rough-leaf wax myrtle, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its rough, pubescent leaves and small, wax-coated berries. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as swamps and marshes, and is adapted to moist, acidic soils. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine, and it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
