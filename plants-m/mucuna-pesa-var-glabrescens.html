Mucuna pesa var. glabrescens

Mucuna pesa var. glabrescens, commonly known as the velvet bean, is a tropical leguminous plant characterized by its dense, velvety foliage.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is native to regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by elongated pods containing edible seeds. It is often cultivated for its high protein content and use in animal feed.

Additionally, it has been studied for its potential medicinal properties due to the presence of alkaloids.
