Malva abutilon

Malva abutilon, commonly known as velvetleaf, is a flowering plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving them a velvety texture. The plant produces showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions and is often found in disturbed soils.

Malva abutilon is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
