Melchiora schliebenii

Melchiora schliebenii, commonly known as the Schlieben's melchior, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Melchioraceae and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils and thrives in temperate to subtropical climates. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in a fine, hairy texture, while its stems are often woody and branched.

Melchiora schliebenii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
