Mucuna balfouriana

Mucuna balfouriana, commonly known as the velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety pubescence that covers its stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by pods containing flat, bean-like seeds. It is a member of the Fabaceae family and is known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil.

The plant is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of protein-rich seeds.
