Malaxis clemensii

Malaxis clemensii, commonly known as the Clemens' Malaxis, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments. The plant has a slender, hairy stem and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in a spike. Its roots are often mycorrhizal, forming symbiotic relationships with fungi to enhance nutrient absorption.

The species is valued for its unique floral structure and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
