Myrciaria hagendorffii

Myrciaria hagendorffii, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or araçá-do-brejo, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of eastern Brazil and is characterized by its small, glossy leaves and showy white flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible and often used in local cuisine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in humid tropical environments.

Myrciaria hagendorffii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
