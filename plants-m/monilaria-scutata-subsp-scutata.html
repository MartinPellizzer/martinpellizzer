Monilaria scutata subsp. scutata

Monilaria scutata subsp.

scutata, commonly known as the common rock cress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or arctic environments.

The plant features basal leaves that are spoon-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small, white, and arranged in racemes. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This subspecies is characterized by its hardy nature and ability to thrive in harsh, nutrient-poor conditions.
