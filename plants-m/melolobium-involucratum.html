Melolobium involucratum

Melolobium involucratum, commonly known as the velvetleaf melolobium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in full sun. Its seed pods are cylindrical and contain several small, round seeds.

Melolobium involucratum is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
