Momordica boivinii

Momordica boivinii, commonly known as the Boivin's bitter melon, is a tropical vine belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its distinctive, ribbed, and spiny stems. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by elongated, bitter-tasting fruits that resemble a cucumber in shape. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the fruit contains numerous small, white seeds.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
