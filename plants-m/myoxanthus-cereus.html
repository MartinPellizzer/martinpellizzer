Myoxanthus cereus

Myoxanthus cereus, commonly known as the golden star cactus, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features a short, branching stem covered in dense, spiny leaves that are typically a vibrant green or yellowish-green. The plant produces striking yellow or orange flowers during the summer months, which are followed by small, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are adapted to store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soil with minimal water.
