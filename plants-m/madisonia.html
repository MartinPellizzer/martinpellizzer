Madisonia

Madisonia, commonly known as the Madisonia orchid, is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida and the Carolinas. These plants are characterized by their small, delicate flowers that often have a unique coloration. They typically grow in moist, shaded environments such as swamps and wetlands.

Madisonia species are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
