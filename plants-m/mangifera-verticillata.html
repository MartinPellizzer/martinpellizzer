Mangifera verticillata

Mangifera verticillata, commonly known as the wild mango or Indian mango, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate or sometimes pinnate, and its small, fragrant flowers that cluster in panicles. The fruit is a drupe with a fibrous mesocarp and a sweet, edible pulp. It thrives in humid climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The plant is valued for its fruit, which is used in traditional cuisine and local medicine.
