Mallophora rugosifolia

Mallophora rugosifolia, commonly known as the rugged-leaf fern, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive, rough-textured leaves that give it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories. Its inflorescence consists of small, clustered flowers that are often white or pale yellow.

Mallophora rugosifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
