Mimusops balata

Mimusops balata, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green on top. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in moist, semi-deciduous forests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal uses, it is sometimes cultivated in horticulture.
