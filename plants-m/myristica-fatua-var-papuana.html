Myristica fatua var. papuana

Myristica fatua var. papuana, commonly known as the Papuan nutmeg, is a small evergreen tree native to the lowland forests of New Guinea.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces a unique fruit that contains a single seed, which is encased in a reddish-brown aril. The aril is the part used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its aromatic properties.

This species is notable for its potential medicinal uses and its role in local ecosystems.
