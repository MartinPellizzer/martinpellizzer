Melica neesii

Melica neesii, commonly known as the Nees' grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and along stream banks. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green color.

Melica neesii is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
