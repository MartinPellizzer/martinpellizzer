Mimulus langsdorffii var. platyphyllus

Mimulus langsdorffii var. platyphyllus, commonly known as the broad-leafed monkeyflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to coastal regions of southern California.

It features showy, orange-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are broad and ovate, often with a waxy coating, and the plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils. It is adapted to coastal climates with mild winters and warm, dry summers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
