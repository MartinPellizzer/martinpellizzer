Micromyrtus elobata subsp. elobata

Micromyrtus elobata subsp.

elobata, commonly known as the slender bottlebrush, is a small shrub native to eastern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, cylindrical leaves and clusters of red or pink flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal or inland heathlands. It has a compact growth habit and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

The subspecies is distinguished by its elongated leaves and more open inflorescences compared to other related species.
