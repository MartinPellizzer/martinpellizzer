Myrcia leucophloea

Myrcia leucophloea, commonly known as the white-wood myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and showy white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its bark is pale and smooth, and it produces small, edible fruits that are often consumed by birds and other wildlife.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its fragrance and visual appeal.
