Malus asiatica f. rubra

Malus asiatica f. rubra, commonly known as the red apple tree, is a cultivar of the apple tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red foliage, which provides ornamental value in gardens and landscapes. The plant produces small, edible apples that are typically harvested in the fall. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and mild fruit flavor.
