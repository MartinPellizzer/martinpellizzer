Myrcia proencana

Myrcia proencana, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruits are small, capsule-like structures that contain several seeds.

Myrcia proencana is often used in traditional medicine and for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and pleasant scent.
