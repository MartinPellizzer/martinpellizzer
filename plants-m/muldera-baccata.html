Muldera baccata

Muldera baccata, commonly known as the black mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to parts of Asia and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and small, black berries that ripen in the fall. The plant belongs to the Moraceae family, which includes other fruit-bearing trees like the fig and breadfruit. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, often marked with shallow fissures.

Muldera baccata is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
