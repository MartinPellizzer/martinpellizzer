Moquilea comosa

Moquilea comosa, commonly known as the hairy moquilea, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive hairy foliage and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils and can often be found in coastal areas. Its structure includes upright stems with dense, woolly leaves that help reduce water loss.

Moquilea comosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
