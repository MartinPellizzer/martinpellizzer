Microchilus claviger

Microchilus claviger, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a small, terrestrial orchid native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant has a slender, wiry stem and produces delicate, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive lip. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the soil.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in specialty gardens.
