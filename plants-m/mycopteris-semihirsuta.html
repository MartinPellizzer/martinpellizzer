Mycopteris semihirsuta

Mycopteris semihirsuta, commonly known as the hairy shield fern, is a species of fern native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its compact, evergreen fronds that are covered in fine, hairy structures. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds and release spores for propagation.

The hairy shield fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
