Melica cordobensis

Melica cordobensis, commonly known as the Cordoba grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit with fine, blade-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically greenish or yellowish in color. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
