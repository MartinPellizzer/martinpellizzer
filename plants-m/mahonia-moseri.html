Mahonia moseri

Mahonia moseri, commonly known as the evergreen mahonia, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves that are dark green and glossy, with spiny margins. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, which are followed by blue-black berries. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and seasonal interest.
