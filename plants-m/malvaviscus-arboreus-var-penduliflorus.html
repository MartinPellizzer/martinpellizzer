Malvaviscus arboreus var. penduliflorus

Malvaviscus arboreus var. penduliflorus, commonly known as the weeping mallows, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its pendulous, showy flowers that hang in clusters. The flowers are typically pink to red in color and bloom in the summer months. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are heart-shaped with a smooth texture.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.
