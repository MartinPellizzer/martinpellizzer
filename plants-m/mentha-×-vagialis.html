Mentha × vagialis

Mentha × vagialis, commonly known as the hybrid mint, is a cross between spearmint (Mentha spicata) and watermint (Mentha aquatica).

It is a perennial herb characterized by its square, erect stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a serrated margin. The plant produces small, pale purple to white flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Mentha × vagialis is native to Europe and has been widely cultivated for its aromatic leaves used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Its essential oils contain compounds like menthol and menthone, contributing to its cooling and refreshing properties.
