Mentha castellana

Mentha castellana, commonly known as the alpine mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. The plant has square stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, fragrant flowers are usually pink or purple and bloom in late summer.

Mentha castellana is valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
