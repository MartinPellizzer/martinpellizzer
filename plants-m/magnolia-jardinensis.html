Magnolia jardinensis

Magnolia jardinensis, commonly known as the garden magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves appear. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, with a fragrant, resinous scent.

Magnolia jardinensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
