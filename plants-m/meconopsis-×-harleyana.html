Meconopsis × harleyana

Meconopsis × harleyana, commonly known as the Himalayan blue poppy, is a hybrid species native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple petals and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and produces showy flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are palmately lobed and are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Meconopsis × harleyana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
