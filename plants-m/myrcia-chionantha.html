Myrcia chionantha

Myrcia chionantha, commonly known as the white myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the coastal regions of southern Brazil, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its leaves are narrow, leathery, and glossy, often used in traditional medicine.

The plant's aromatic properties make it a valuable component in local flora and ecological systems.
