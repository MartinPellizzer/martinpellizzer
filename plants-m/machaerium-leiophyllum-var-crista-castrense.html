Machaerium leiophyllum var. crista-castrense

Machaerium leiophyllum var. crista-castrense, commonly known as the golden sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bright green. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with yellow to golden flowers, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland or riparian habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes in suitable climates.
