Mouriri emarginata var. emarginata

Mouriri emarginata var. emarginata, commonly known as the Brazilian sour orange, is a small evergreen tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange-colored, fleshy fruits. The fruit has a sour taste and is used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and prefers well-drained soil.
