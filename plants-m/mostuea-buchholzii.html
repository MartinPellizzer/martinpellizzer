Mostuea buchholzii

Mostuea buchholzii, commonly known as the pink ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, orchid-like inflorescences that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Mostuea buchholzii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
