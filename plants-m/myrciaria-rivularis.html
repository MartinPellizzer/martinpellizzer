Myrciaria rivularis

Myrciaria rivularis, commonly known as the river myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds, and it is often found growing in moist, sandy soils near rivers and coastal areas.

The plant is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
