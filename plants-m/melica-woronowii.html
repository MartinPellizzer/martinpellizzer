Melica woronowii

Melica woronowii, commonly known as the Woronow's brome, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving at high elevations where temperatures are cool and moisture is limited. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It is characterized by its ability to withstand harsh climatic conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.

Melica woronowii is often found in meadows and slopes, contributing to the ecological diversity of its environment.
