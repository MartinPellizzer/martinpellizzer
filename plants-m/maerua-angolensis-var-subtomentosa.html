Maerua angolensis var. subtomentosa

Maerua angolensis var. subtomentosa, commonly known as the velvet maerua, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage that gives it a velvety appearance. The leaves are typically narrow and opposite, with a prominent midrib. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by small, woody fruits.

This plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.
