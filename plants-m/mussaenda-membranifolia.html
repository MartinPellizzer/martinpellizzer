Mussaenda membranifolia

Mussaenda membranifolia, commonly known as the paperleaf Mussaenda, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating, giving them a papery texture. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Mussaenda membranifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable environments.
