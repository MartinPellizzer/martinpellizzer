Mimosa pulchella

Mimosa pulchella, commonly known as the silky mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is a member of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and it has the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules. Mimosa pulchella thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
