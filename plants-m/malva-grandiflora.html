Malva grandiflora

Malva grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered mallows, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes and produces showy, rose-like flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sun, making it a popular choice for gardens. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Malva grandiflora is also known for its mucilaginous seeds, which have been used in traditional herbal remedies.
