Mollugo erecta

Mollugo erecta, commonly known as the carpetweed, is a low-growing annual plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It has prostrate or ascending stems that often form dense mats on the ground, and its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with four petals, which bloom in clusters along the stems.

It is typically found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides, gardens, and waste areas, and is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
