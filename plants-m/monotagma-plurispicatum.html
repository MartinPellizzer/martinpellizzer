Monotagma plurispicatum

Monotagma plurispicatum, commonly known as the spiny papyrus, is a unique aquatic plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It has long, narrow leaves that are often covered in small spines, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in shallow, freshwater environments and is adapted to tolerate fluctuating water levels. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically arranged in dense clusters along the stems.

Monotagma plurispicatum plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
