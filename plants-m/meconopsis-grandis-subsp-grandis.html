Meconopsis grandis subsp. grandis

Meconopsis grandis subsp.

grandis, commonly known as the Himalayan glory flower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in mountain meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lanceolate and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal. Meconopsis grandis subsp.

grandis is valued for its beauty and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
