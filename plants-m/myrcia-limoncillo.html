Myrcia limoncillo

Myrcia limoncillo, commonly known as lemon myrtle, is a shrub native to the rainforests of eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, lemon-scented leaves that emit a strong citrus aroma. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers and red or orange berries. Its leaves are often used in culinary applications due to their aromatic properties.

Botanically, it belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and tea tree.
