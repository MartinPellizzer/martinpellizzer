Mimosa grahamii

Mimosa grahamii, commonly known as the desert mimosa, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptation to harsh conditions. The plant produces small, fluffy yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple pairs of tiny, feather-like leaflets.

Mimosa grahamii is also known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in poor soils, making it a resilient species in desert ecosystems.
