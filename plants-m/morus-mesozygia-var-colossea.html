Morus mesozygia var. colossea

Morus mesozygia var. colossea, commonly known as the Chinese mulberry, is a variant of the mulberry tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as figs, which are edible and often consumed by both humans and animals. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sunlight.

This species is also valued for its role in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber for paper and textiles.
