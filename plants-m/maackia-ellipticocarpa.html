Maackia ellipticocarpa

Maackia ellipticocarpa, commonly known as the ellipticocarpa maackia, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant features trifoliate leaves with elliptical leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters. Its fruit is a spherical, woody capsule that turns red when mature, containing several seeds.

Maackia ellipticocarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and seasonal interest.
