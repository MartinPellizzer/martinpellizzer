Muhlenbergia ambigua var. filiformis

Muhlenbergia ambigua var. filiformis, commonly known as the slender muhlenbergia, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves and compact growth habit, often forming dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements further enhance its appeal in garden settings.
