Mikania discifera

Mikania discifera, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a vigorous vine native to the Americas.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread aggressively, often forming dense mats. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its invasive nature in many regions.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its fibrous roots and tendril-like stems.
