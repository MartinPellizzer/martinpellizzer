Mikania suaveolens

Mikania suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-smelling vine, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its stems are often hairy and can climb or trail along the ground.

Mikania suaveolens is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional remedies.
