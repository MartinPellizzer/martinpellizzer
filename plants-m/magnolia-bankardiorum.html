Magnolia bankardiorum

Magnolia bankardiorum, commonly known as the Mexican magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth texture. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its fragrance and visual appeal.
