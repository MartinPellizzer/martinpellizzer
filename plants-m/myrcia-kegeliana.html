Myrcia kegeliana

Myrcia kegeliana, commonly known as Kegel's myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and fragrant, white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a glossy surface, while its fruit is a small, woody capsule containing several seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
