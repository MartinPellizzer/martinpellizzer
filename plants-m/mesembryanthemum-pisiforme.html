Mesembryanthemum pisiforme

Mesembryanthemum pisiforme, commonly known as the kidney-shaped ice plant, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is widely cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions.
