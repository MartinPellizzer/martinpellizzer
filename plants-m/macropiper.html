Macropiper

Macropiper, commonly known as the giant pepper plant, is a member of the Piperaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. Its fruit, resembling a peppercorn, is used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

Macropiper thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
