Monnina vitis-idaea

Monnina vitis-idaea, commonly known as whortleberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the northern regions of North America and Eurasia.

It belongs to the Onagraceae family and is characterized by its small, dark blue berries that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of yellow flowers in early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is commonly found in boreal forests and rocky habitats.

Monnina vitis-idaea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for wildlife.
