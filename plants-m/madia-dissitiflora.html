Madia dissitiflora

Madia dissitiflora, commonly known as the desert globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open plains. Its growth habit is typically low-growing, with a compact form that helps it conserve water.

The species is well adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a resilient member of its ecosystem.
