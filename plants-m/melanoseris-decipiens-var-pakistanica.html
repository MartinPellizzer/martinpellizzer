Melanoseris decipiens var. pakistanica

Melanoseris decipiens var. pakistanica, commonly known as the Pakistan false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Pakistan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing a hardy adaptation to harsh climates. Its leaves are usually hairy and lance-shaped, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a composite head.

This variant is distinguished by its specific geographic distribution and unique morphological features compared to other varieties of Melanoseris.
