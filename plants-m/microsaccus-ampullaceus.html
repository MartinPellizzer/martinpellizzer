Microsaccus ampullaceus

Microsaccus ampullaceus, commonly known as the bladderpod, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, erect stature and distinctive bladder-like pods that contain the seeds. The plant typically blooms with yellow flowers that appear in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are pinnately divided, and the plant is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Microsaccus ampullaceus plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for various insects and small animals.
