Matthiola sinuata var. cornigera

Matthiola sinuata var. cornigera, commonly known as the cornflower mustard, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard plants. The plant features small, four-petaled flowers that are typically white or pale purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, lobed, and covered in a fine layer of white pubescence.

The plant is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in full sun to partial shade.
