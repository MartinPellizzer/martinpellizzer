Mesembryanthemum emarcidum

Mesembryanthemum emarcidum, commonly known as the ice plant, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, water-storing leaves and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of yellow, pink, and white. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for arid and coastal environments. Its ability to store water in its leaves allows it to survive in dry conditions, and it is often used in xeriscaping.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
