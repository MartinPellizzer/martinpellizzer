Miconia perobscura

Miconia perobscura, commonly known as the velvetleaf miconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered together, followed by distinctive orange-red fruits containing numerous seeds. Miconia perobscura is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of moist, shaded environments.

Due to its invasive potential, it is sometimes considered a problematic species in certain regions.
