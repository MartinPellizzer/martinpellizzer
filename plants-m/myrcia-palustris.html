Myrcia palustris

Myrcia palustris, commonly known as swamp myrtle, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in wet, swampy areas and has small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds, and the plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Myrcia palustris plays an important ecological role in its native habitats by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
