Mucuna canaliculata

Mucuna canaliculata, commonly known as the cow pea or velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive, curved pods that contain edible seeds. The plant is notable for its rapid growth and ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, enriching the soil. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, and it produces a significant amount of biomass.

Mucuna canaliculata is also used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop in various parts of the world.
