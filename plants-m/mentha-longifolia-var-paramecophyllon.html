Mentha longifolia var. paramecophyllon

Mentha longifolia var. paramecophyllon, commonly known as narrow-leaf mint, is a variant of the mint family characterized by its slender, elongated leaves.

It is native to regions of Europe and parts of Asia, often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer, contributing to its ornamental value. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications.

This variety is valued for its unique foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
