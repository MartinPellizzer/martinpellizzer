Melocactus viridescens

Melocactus viridescens, commonly known as the green melon cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stem that is typically green, sometimes with a slight bluish tint. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are followed by edible, melon-like fruits. It is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
