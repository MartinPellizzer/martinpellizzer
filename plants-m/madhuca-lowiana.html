Madhuca lowiana

Madhuca lowiana, commonly known as the white sycamore, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy white flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces small, round fruits that contain a single seed, which is used in traditional medicine. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in gardens and as a shade tree.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
