Mundulea densicoma

Mundulea densicoma, commonly known as the dense thornbush, is a shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and small, leathery leaves that help reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody pods containing seeds. It is well adapted to drought conditions and plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils.

Its ecological importance includes providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
