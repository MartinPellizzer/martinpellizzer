Manilkara meridionalis

Manilkara meridionalis, commonly known as the southern manilkara, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and smooth, pale bark. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by spherical fruits that contain a single, large seed. Its latex is milky and viscous, often used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
