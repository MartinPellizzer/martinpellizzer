Myrcia catharinensis

Myrcia catharinensis, commonly known as the Brazilian peppercorn, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its small, aromatic berries that are used in traditional medicine. The plant has dark green, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of white flowers. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Myrcia catharinensis is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance.
