Mikania micrantha f. typica

Mikania micrantha f. typica, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its rapid growth rate and ability to spread aggressively, often forming dense mats that can suppress other vegetation. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers with five petals, which bloom in clusters. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough texture.

It is native to Central and South America but has been introduced to various regions, where it can become invasive.
