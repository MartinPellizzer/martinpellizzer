Myrtus conspicua

Myrtus conspicua, commonly known as the coastal myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for coastal and rocky environments.

Myrtus conspicua is also valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in traditional medicine.
