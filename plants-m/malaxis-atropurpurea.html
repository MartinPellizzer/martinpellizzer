Malaxis atropurpurea

Malaxis atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple malaxis, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often pendulous stems and small, greenish-purple flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as forest undergrowth, where it relies on mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

The orchid's delicate structure and unique coloration make it a distinctive yet challenging species to cultivate.
