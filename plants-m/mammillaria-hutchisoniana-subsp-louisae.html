Mammillaria hutchisoniana subsp. louisae

Mammillaria hutchisoniana subsp.

louisae, commonly known as the "Louisae Hedgehog Cactus," is a low-growing cactus native to the rocky soils of central Mexico. It features a cylindrical stem with numerous cylindrical, ribbed tubercles that give it a distinctive spiny appearance.

The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.

Due to its unique morphology and ornamental value, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and cactus collections.
