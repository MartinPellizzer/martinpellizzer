Menispermum myosotis

Menispermum myosotis, commonly known as Dutchman's breeches, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces a unique, poisonous fruit that contains seeds, which are dispersed by animals. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and have a smooth texture.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
