Macropodiella pellucida

Macropodiella pellucida, commonly known as the glassy fern, is a delicate and rare species of fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its translucent, waxy leaves that give it a glass-like appearance, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest undergrowth or rocky crevices. Its fronds are usually small and pinnately divided, with a distinctive, almost fragile texture.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a valuable subject in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
