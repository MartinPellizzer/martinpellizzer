Myristica glomerata

Myristica glomerata, commonly known as the nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Banda Islands in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces an oval, woody fruit that contains the valuable nutmeg seed inside. Its bark and leaves are also used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Myristica glomerata is primarily cultivated for its nutmeg and mace, which are widely used in cooking and as spices.
