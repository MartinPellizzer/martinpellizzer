Mycetia chasalioides

Mycetia chasalioides, commonly known as the Indian white sand orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique morphology, featuring a slender, erect stem and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in a raceme, which bloom during the monsoon season. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
