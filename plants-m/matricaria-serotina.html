Matricaria serotina

Matricaria serotina, commonly known as evening chamomile, is an annual flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in the late afternoon. The plant has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. It is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Its aromatic properties make it useful in traditional medicine and herbal remedies.
