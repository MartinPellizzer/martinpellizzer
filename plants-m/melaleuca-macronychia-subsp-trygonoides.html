Melaleuca macronychia subsp. trygonoides

Melaleuca macronychia subsp.

trygonoides, commonly known as the Narrow-leaf Paperbark, is a shrub or small tree native to northern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant, honey-scented flowers that are typically white or pale yellow.

The plant produces woody capsules that contain numerous small, dark seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland areas with a subtropical climate.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
