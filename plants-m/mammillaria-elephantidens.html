Mammillaria elephantidens

Mammillaria elephantidens, commonly known as the elephant's tooth or toothed mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in white, woolly spines and small, rounded tubercles. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardiness, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and collectors.
