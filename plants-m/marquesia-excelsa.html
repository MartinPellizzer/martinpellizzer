Marquesia excelsa

Marquesia excelsa, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its large, floating leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter, providing a unique habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant has a thick, spongy stem that supports its massive foliage and helps it stay afloat. Its flowers are showy, with bright yellow petals that attract pollinators like bees and beetles.

Marquesia excelsa plays an important role in the ecosystem by offering shelter and food to numerous aquatic species.
