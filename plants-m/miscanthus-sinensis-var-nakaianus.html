Miscanthus sinensis var. nakaianus

Miscanthus sinensis var. nakaianus, commonly known as the Nakaianus reed, is a variant of the silvergrass genus Miscanthus.

It is characterized by its graceful, arching foliage and striking silver-blue foliage that turns coppery in the fall. This ornamental grass thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established. It is native to Japan and is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to create texture in landscapes.

Its clumping growth habit makes it ideal for borders, rock gardens, or as a specimen plant.
