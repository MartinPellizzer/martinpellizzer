Muhlenbergia racemosa var. brevifolia

Muhlenbergia racemosa var. brevifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf muhlenbergia, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-based flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping and native plant gardens.

Its drought tolerance and ability to form dense clumps make it a valuable ornamental and ecological plant in arid landscapes.
