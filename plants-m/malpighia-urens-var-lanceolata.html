Malpighia urens var. lanceolata

Malpighia urens var. lanceolata, commonly known as the arrowleaf granadilla, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring lanceolate leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, red, berry-like fruits. Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem, hairy surfaces, and a preference for tropical and subtropical climates.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of fruit in certain regions.
