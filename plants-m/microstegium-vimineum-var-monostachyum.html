Microstegium vimineum var. monostachyum

Microstegium vimineum var. monostachyum, commonly known as the narrowleaf stiltgrass, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and wetlands. Its reproductive strategy involves wind pollination, and it spreads primarily through rhizomes and seeds.

It is considered an invasive species in many regions where it outcompetes native vegetation.
