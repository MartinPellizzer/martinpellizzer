Matthiola adenophora

Matthiola adenophora, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada stock, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant is characterized by its clusters of fragrant, white or pale purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or open habitats.
