Momordica charantia var. pseudobalsamina

Momordica charantia var. pseudobalsamina, commonly known as the red horned melon, is a variant of the bitter gourd native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive red, spiny rind and elongated, pale yellow or orange interior. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and is often cultivated for its edible fruits, which have a slightly bitter taste. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variant is valued both for its culinary uses and its ornamental appeal.
