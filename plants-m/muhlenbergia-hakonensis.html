Muhlenbergia hakonensis

Muhlenbergia hakonensis, commonly known as the Hawaiian muhlenbergia, is a grass species native to the island of Hawaii.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and produces small, delicate panicles of flowers during the summer months. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as forest understories and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that adds a subtle aesthetic to its natural habitat.

Muhlenbergia hakonensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
