Merremia dissecta

Merremia dissecta, commonly known as the dissected morning glory, is a flowering vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Merremia dissecta is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can climb support structures as it grows.
