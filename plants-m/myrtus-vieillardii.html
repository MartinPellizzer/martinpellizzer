Myrtus vieillardii

Myrtus vieillardii, commonly known as the Vieillard myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant features narrow, dark green leaves with a fragrant, resinous scent. It produces small, white flowers followed by dark blue berries that are edible.

Myrtus vieillardii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
