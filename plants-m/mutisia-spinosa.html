Mutisia spinosa

Mutisia spinosa, commonly known as the woolly mutisia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, woody stems and large, showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs.

Mutisia spinosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
