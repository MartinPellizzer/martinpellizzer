Melianthus villosus

Melianthus villosus, commonly known as the hairy melianthus, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Melianthus villosus is adapted to dry conditions and can thrive in well-drained soils.
