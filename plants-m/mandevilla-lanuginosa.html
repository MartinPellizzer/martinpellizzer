Mandevilla lanuginosa

Mandevilla lanuginosa, commonly known as the fuzzy mandevilla, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its velvety, hairy leaves and clusters of vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant is a vigorous climber, often used in ornamental landscaping due to its showy blooms and adaptability to various growing conditions. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil and ample sunlight.

Mandevilla lanuginosa is also known for its ability to produce fragrant flowers, making it a popular choice for gardens and as a houseplant.
