Malva gangetica

Malva gangetica, commonly known as the Indian mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows up to 1-2 meters in height, featuring large, heart-shaped leaves with a soft, velvety texture. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fibrous stems and leaves are used in traditional medicine for their anti-inflammatory and soothing properties.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, riverbanks, and disturbed areas.
