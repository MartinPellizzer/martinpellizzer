Melampyrum nemorosum subsp. polonicum

Melampyrum nemorosum subsp.

polonicum, commonly known as the Polish wood betony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in shaded, moist woodlands and along stream banks, thriving in rich, loamy soils.

The plant features slender, branching stems with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are small, clustered in spikes, and range in color from reddish-purple to orange, blooming in late spring to early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
