Myagrum chlorifolium

Myagrum chlorifolium, commonly known as the green-leaved myrtle, is a tropical plant native to Australia.

It is a member of the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a bright green color. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters.

Myagrum chlorifolium is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and air-purifying qualities.
