Mimosa rhodocarpa

Mimosa rhodocarpa, commonly known as the silky mimosa, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that are soft to the touch and turn reddish in autumn. The plant produces small, yellow, spherical flowers that appear in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The species is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
