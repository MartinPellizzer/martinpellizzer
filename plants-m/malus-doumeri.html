Malus doumeri

Malus doumeri, commonly known as the Doumer's apple, is a small, deciduous tree native to the Caucasus region.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 5 meters, featuring a rounded crown and smooth, gray bark. The plant produces white flowers in spring, followed by small, yellowish-green fruits that ripen in late summer. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Malus doumeri is relatively hardy and can thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
