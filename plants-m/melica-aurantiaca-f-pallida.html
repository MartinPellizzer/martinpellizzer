Melica aurantiaca f. pallida

Melica aurantiaca f. pallida, commonly known as the pale form of the orange grass, is a variant of the species Melica aurantiaca.

It is a perennial grass native to parts of Europe and Asia, characterized by its slender, pale green stems and narrow leaves. The inflorescence consists of spikelets that are typically pale yellow to reddish in color. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands.

This form is distinguished by its reduced pigmentation compared to the typical variety, giving it a more subdued appearance.
