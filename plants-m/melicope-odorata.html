Melicope odorata

Melicope odorata, commonly known as the sweet sandarac tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, rough bark and glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by woody, spiky fruits that contain several seeds. Its essential oils, extracted from the bark and leaves, are valued for their medicinal and aromatic properties.

Melicope odorata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
