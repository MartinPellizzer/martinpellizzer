Maranta wallisii

Maranta wallisii, commonly known as the velvetleaf arrowroot, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves that often display a striking red or purple hue on the underside. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its rhizomatous root system is notable for its ability to produce starchy tubers, which have been used in traditional cuisines.

Maranta wallisii thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal.
