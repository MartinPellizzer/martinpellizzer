Manihot intermedia

Manihot intermedia, commonly known as cassava or yuca, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically green with a hairy texture. The plant produces small, green flowers followed by oval, woody fruits containing seeds. Its roots are the primary economically important part, used as a staple food crop worldwide.

The plant is also valued for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and its role in sustainable agriculture.
