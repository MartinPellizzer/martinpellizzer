Margyricarpus setosus var. paucifoliolatus

Margyricarpus setosus var. paucifoliolatus, commonly known as the spiny margyricarpus, is a rare and distinctive plant species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, spiny bracts and reduced foliage, which contribute to its striking appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in mountainous or forested areas. Its leaves are small and clustered, with a leathery texture that aids in water retention.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its specialized morphology and limited distribution.
