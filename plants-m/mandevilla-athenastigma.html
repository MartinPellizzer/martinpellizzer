Mandevilla athenastigma

Mandevilla athenastigma, commonly known as the Athens mandevilla, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces clusters of showy, often pink or red blooms that attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Mandevilla athenastigma is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and containers due to its ornamental value.
