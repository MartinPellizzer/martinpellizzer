Melampodium argophyllum

Melampodium argophyllum, commonly known as the silverleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking silver-gray foliage that contrasts with its bright yellow flowers. The plant belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its ability to thrive in full sun and well-drained soils. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Melampodium argophyllum is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
