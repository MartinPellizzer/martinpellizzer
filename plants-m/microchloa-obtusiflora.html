Microchloa obtusiflora

Microchloa obtusiflora, commonly known as the broad-flowered microchloa, is a small, perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with pale green to brownish flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Microchloa obtusiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticultural settings.
