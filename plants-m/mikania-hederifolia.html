Mikania hederifolia

Mikania hederifolia, commonly known as creeping morning glory, is a vigorous vine native to the Americas.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of purple and white. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to climb or trail along the ground. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Due to its aggressive growth, it can become invasive in certain regions.
