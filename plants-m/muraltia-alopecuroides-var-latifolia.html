Muraltia alopecuroides var. latifolia

Muraltia alopecuroides var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf muraltia, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are broad and leathery in texture. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, red, edible fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a hedge plant.
