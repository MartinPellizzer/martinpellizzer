Mandevilla glabra

Mandevilla glabra, commonly known as the white mandevilla, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a vigorous, evergreen vine that produces clusters of fragrant, white to pink flowers. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves and thrives in warm, humid climates. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its showy blooms and climbing habit.

Mandevilla glabra prefers full sun to partial shade and well-draining soil for optimal growth.
