Macroclinidium verticillatum

Macroclinidium verticillatum, commonly known as the spiral cactus, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom during the rainy season, attracting pollinators such as bees and moths. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it stores water in its fleshy stems, adapting to its harsh environment.
