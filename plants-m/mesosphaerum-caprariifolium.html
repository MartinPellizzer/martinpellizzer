Mesosphaerum caprariifolium

Mesosphaerum caprariifolium, commonly known as the Cape rose or African rose, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in coastal or mountainous regions. The plant produces small, showy pink or white flowers that bloom in spring and summer. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with a feathery appearance, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
