Mammilloydia candida

Mammilloydia candida, commonly known as the white mammillary cactus, is a species of cactus native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in white, woolly spines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and succulent collections.
