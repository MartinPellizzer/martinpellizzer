Metagonia empetrifolia

Metagonia empetrifolia, commonly known as the rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and woody stems that become flaky with age. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and yellow, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a fine, powdery coating.

Metagonia empetrifolia is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
