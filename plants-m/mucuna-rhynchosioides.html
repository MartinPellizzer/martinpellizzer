Mucuna rhynchosioides

Mucuna rhynchosioides, commonly known as the hairy morning glory, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It produces large, showy flowers that are typically violet or blue in color, with a distinctive hooded shape. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and hairy stems, which help in identifying it in the field. Its seeds are enclosed in a pod and can be used in traditional medicine.

Mucuna rhynchosioides is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agricultural systems.
