Myrcia caracasana

Myrcia caracasana, commonly known as the Caracas myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and guava. The plant features glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the summer. Its fruits are red, fleshy berries that contain several seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some regions for medicinal purposes.
