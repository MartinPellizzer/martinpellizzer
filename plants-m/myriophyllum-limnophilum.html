Myriophyllum limnophilum

Myriophyllum limnophilum, commonly known as watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has slender, branching stems that are often covered in small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces tiny, floating flowers that are typically white or pale blue in color. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

However, it can also become invasive in some regions if not managed properly.
