Musa itinerans

Musa itinerans, commonly known as the wandering banana, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers, which are followed by elongated, finger-like fruits. Musa itinerans is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage.

It thrives in moist, warm environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
