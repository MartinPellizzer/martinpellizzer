Mareya spicata var. micrantha

Mareya spicata var. micrantha, commonly known as the small-flowered mareya, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features slender, erect stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are opposite, elliptic to ovate in shape with a smooth margin. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer, followed by small, spherical berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses in suitable climates.
