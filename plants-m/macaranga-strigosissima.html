Macaranga strigosissima

Macaranga strigosissima, commonly known as the hairy macaranga, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually hairy on the underside, giving it its common name. Its flowers are small and clustered, often white or yellowish in color.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed habitats and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
