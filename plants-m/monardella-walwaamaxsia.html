Monardella walwaamaxsia

Monardella walwaamaxsia, commonly known as the Sierra lemonade plant, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers that are typically pink or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or open habitats.

Its aromatic leaves and medicinal properties have made it of interest in both horticulture and traditional herbal practices.
