Melastoma decemdentatum

Melastoma decemdentatum, commonly known as the ten-toothed melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its showy, often pink or purple flowers. The plant typically has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.
