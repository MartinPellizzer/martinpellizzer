Meconopsis bella subsp. subintegrifolia

Meconopsis bella subsp.

subintegrifolia, commonly known as the Himalayan blue poppy, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas. It is characterized by its striking blue or violet petals and a distinctive, spurred corolla.

The plant typically grows in rocky, alpine environments where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually broad and have a smooth margin, distinguishing it from other sub-species. Meconopsis bella subsp.

subintegrifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in specialized gardens.
