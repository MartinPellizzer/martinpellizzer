Mouroucoa violacea

Mouroucoa violacea, commonly known as the violet orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers, which bloom in clusters during the warmer months. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, typically arranged in a fan shape.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses due to its specific humidity and temperature requirements.
