Mandevilla funiformis

Mandevilla funiformis, commonly known as the creeping mandevilla, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and clusters of showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant produces small, green, egg-shaped fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and hanging baskets.

Its foliage is typically deep green with a slightly glossy texture.
