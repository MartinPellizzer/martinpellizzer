Mutisia sinuata

Mutisia sinuata, commonly known as the silky mutisia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant has a woody stem and is often found in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
