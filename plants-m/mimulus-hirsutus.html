Mimulus hirsutus

Mimulus hirsutus, commonly known as the hairy monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to western North America.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in disturbed soils and along roadsides. The plant produces vibrant orange or yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are hairy and alternate, and the stems are often branched.

Mimulus hirsutus is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
