Manihot tripartita var. glabra

Manihot tripartita var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth cassava, is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and leaves that are typically trifoliate, meaning they are divided into three parts. The plant produces small, greenish flowers and is cultivated for its edible roots, which are rich in carbohydrates. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its adaptability, it is an important crop in many parts of the world.
