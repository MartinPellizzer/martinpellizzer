Myrcia acris

Myrcia acris, commonly known as the silver myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, Colombia, and Peru. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are typically elliptical, glossy, and have a waxy texture.

Myrcia acris is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and aromatic properties.
