Malveopsis peruviana

Malveopsis peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton, hibiscus, and okra. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, such as grasslands and shrublands, where it thrives in cooler, temperate climates. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with five petals and a prominent central stamen, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

The leaves are palmately lobed, and the plant is known for its ornamental value and potential medicinal uses.
