Marrubium cephalanthum

Marrubium cephalanthum, commonly known as the purple marrubium, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or dry gardens.

Marrubium cephalanthum is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
