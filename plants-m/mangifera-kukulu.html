Mangifera kukulu

Mangifera kukulu, commonly known as the Java mango, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and fragrant flowers. The fruit is typically small to medium in size, with a yellow or orange skin and a sweet, fibrous pulp. It is often cultivated for its edible fruit and ornamental value.

The tree is adapted to humid climates and thrives in well-drained soils.
