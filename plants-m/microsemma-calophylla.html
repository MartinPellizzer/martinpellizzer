Microsemma calophylla

Microsemma calophylla, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets and produces small, round fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Microsemma calophylla is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
