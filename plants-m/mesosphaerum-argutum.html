Mesosphaerum argutum

Mesosphaerum argutum, commonly known as the rough mesosphaerum, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, featuring woody stems and small, narrow leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers, which bloom in the spring and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to harsh, dry conditions, with mechanisms to conserve water and withstand high temperatures.
