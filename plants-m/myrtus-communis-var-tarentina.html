Myrtus communis var. tarentina

Myrtus communis var. tarentina, commonly known as the Tarentine myrtle, is a compact evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, glossy, dark green leaves and produces fragrant white flowers in spring. The plant is valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring minimal water once established.

The berries that follow the flowers are edible and have a slightly tart flavor.
