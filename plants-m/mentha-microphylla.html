Mentha microphylla

Mentha microphylla, commonly known as narrow-leaf mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features aromatic, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers arranged in whorled clusters along the stem. Mentha microphylla is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

It is valued for its strong mint aroma and has been used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
