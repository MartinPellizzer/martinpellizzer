Mespilus corallina

Mespilus corallina, commonly known as the coral maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, coral-pink flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The leaves are typically ovate or elliptical, with a smooth texture and a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often consumed by birds and other wildlife.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
