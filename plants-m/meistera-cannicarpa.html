Meistera cannicarpa

Meistera cannicarpa, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow and red, which are often clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along riverbanks.

Meistera cannicarpa is also known for its medicinal properties, with various parts of the plant used in traditional remedies.
