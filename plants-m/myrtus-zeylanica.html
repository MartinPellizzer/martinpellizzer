Myrtus zeylanica

Myrtus zeylanica, commonly known as Ceylon myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to Sri Lanka.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and small white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Myrtus zeylanica is often used in traditional medicine and for its ornamental value.
