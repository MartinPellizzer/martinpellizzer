Myrceugenia fuscovelutina

Myrceugenia fuscovelutina, commonly known as the velvet-bark myrtle, is a species of flowering plant native to the temperate rainforests of southern Brazil.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub characterized by its dark, velvety bark and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruits are small, woody capsules that contain several seeds.

Myrceugenia fuscovelutina is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
