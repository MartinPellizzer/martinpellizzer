Mangifera caloneura

Mangifera caloneura, commonly known as the narrow-veined mango, is a species of flowering plant in the Anacardiaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand, Vietnam, and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves with prominent parallel veins. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color.

The fruit, though less commonly cultivated than other mango varieties, is edible and has a distinct flavor.
