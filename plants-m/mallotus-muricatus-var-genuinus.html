Mallotus muricatus var. genuinus

Mallotus muricatus var. genuinus, commonly known as the rough-leafed mallotus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rough, serrated leaves and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in yellow or green. The plant produces woody fruits containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind and animals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
