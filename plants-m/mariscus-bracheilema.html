Mariscus bracheilema

Mariscus bracheilema, commonly known as the narrow-leaved marsh gentian, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in southern Europe.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and flies.

Mariscus bracheilema is an important species for wetland ecosystems, contributing to biodiversity and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
