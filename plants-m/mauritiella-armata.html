Mauritiella armata

Mauritiella armata, commonly known as the bottlebrush palm, is a flowering plant native to the Mascarene Islands in the Indian Ocean.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and dense clusters of bright red, brush-like flowers that bloom at the top of the plant. The leaves are long, pinnate, and often used in traditional crafts by local communities. The plant thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and humid conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a notable species in its native habitat.
