Maxillaria purpureolabia

Maxillaria purpureolabia, commonly known as the purple-lipped maxillaria, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety petals and a distinctive purple lip that sets it apart from other orchid species. The plant typically grows as a sympodial orchid, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its flowers bloom in clusters, often in shades of pink, purple, and white, making it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.

The species is well-suited for greenhouse cultivation due to its preference for warm, humid conditions.
