Microcos hirta

Microcos hirta, commonly known as the woolly microcos, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and small, showy yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions. It has a compact growth habit and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local insect populations.
