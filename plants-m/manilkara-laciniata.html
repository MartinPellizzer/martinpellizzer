Manilkara laciniata

Manilkara laciniata, commonly known as the Guiana apple or lacanita, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that are often deeply lobed. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by edible, apple-like fruits. Its latex sap has been traditionally used for various purposes, including as a source of natural rubber.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
