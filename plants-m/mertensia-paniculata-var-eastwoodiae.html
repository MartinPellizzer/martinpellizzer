Mertensia paniculata var. eastwoodiae

Mertensia paniculata var. eastwoodiae, commonly known as the Eastwood's bluebells, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of bell-shaped, sky-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are aromatic when crushed. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
