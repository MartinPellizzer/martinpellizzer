Mattiastrum racemosum

Mattiastrum racemosum, commonly known as the clustered greenbottle bush, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring dense, upright growth habit. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are borne along the branches. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Mattiastrum racemosum is known for its ability to thrive in coastal and sandy soils, making it a hardy and adaptable species.
