Miconia bissei

Miconia bissei, commonly known as the Hawaiian miconia, is a tropical shrub native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces distinctive, round, berry-like fruits that are edible but have a bitter taste. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its rapid growth and potential invasiveness, it is considered a significant ecological concern in its native habitat.
