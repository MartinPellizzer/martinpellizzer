Myrciaria pumila

Myrciaria pumila, commonly known as the Brazilian blackberry, is a small evergreen shrub native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and small, edible berries. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. Its fruits are rich in antioxidants and are often used in local cuisines.

Myrciaria pumila is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitat.
