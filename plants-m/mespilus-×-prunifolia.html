Mespilus × prunifolia

Mespilus × prunifolia, commonly known as the common medlar, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between the medlar (Mespilus germanica) and the cherry (Prunus avium).

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3–6 meters. The plant is characterized by its oval, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit, which is a pome, is initially hard and astringent but ripens to a sweet, edible texture when fully mature.

The plant is native to southern Europe and has been cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in food preparation.
