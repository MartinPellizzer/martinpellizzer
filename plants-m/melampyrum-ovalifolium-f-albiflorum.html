Melampyrum ovalifolium f. albiflorum

Melampyrum ovalifolium f. albiflorum, commonly known as the whiteflower betony, is a variant of the common betony.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its slender stems and lanceolate leaves. The flowers are distinctive for their white color, which sets it apart from the typical yellow blooms of the species. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

This form is often found in the northern hemisphere and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
