Marah guadalupensis

Marah guadalupensis, commonly known as the Guadalupe milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the milkweed family, Apocynaceae, and is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has smooth, often hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stem. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Marah guadalupensis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species in dry climates.
