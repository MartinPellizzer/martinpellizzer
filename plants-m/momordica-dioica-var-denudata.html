Momordica dioica var. denudata

Momordica dioica var. denudata, commonly known as bitter gourd or karela, is a climbing vine belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive warty, elongated fruit that turns from green to orange when mature. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes and produces yellowish flowers that are either male or female. The fruit is typically used in culinary and medicinal applications due to its high nutritional value and bioactive compounds.

It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
