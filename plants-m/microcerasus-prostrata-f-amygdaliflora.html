Microcerasus prostrata f. amygdaliflora

Microcerasus prostrata f. amygdaliflora, commonly known as the dwarf Chinese crabapple, is a compact, low-growing shrub native to China.

It features small, ovate leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or pink flowers in early spring, which are followed by small, edible berries. Its ornamental value lies in its delicate blossoms and persistent fruit, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a hedge.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
