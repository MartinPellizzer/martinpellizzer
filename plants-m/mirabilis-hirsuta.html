Mirabilis hirsuta

Mirabilis hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy four o'clock, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces striking, tubular flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, orange, or yellow, opening in the late afternoon. It is a perennial shrub that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Mirabilis hirsuta is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
