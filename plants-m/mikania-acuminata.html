Mikania acuminata

Mikania acuminata, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a vigorous climbing vine native to the Americas.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its invasive nature, it can outcompete native plants and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
