Macleania recurva

Macleania recurva, commonly known as the hairy leaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and showy, pale pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and swampy areas. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oval seeds.

Macleania recurva is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for native wildlife.
