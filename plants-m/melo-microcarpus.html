Melo microcarpus

Melo microcarpus, commonly known as the African custard apple, is a small, evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It produces small, fragrant fruits with a rough, greenish skin and a sweet, creamy pulp. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a slightly waxy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

The fruit is typically harvested when it turns yellow or red, indicating ripeness.
