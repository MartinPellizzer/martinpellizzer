Monopetalanthus microphyllus

Monopetalanthus microphyllus, commonly known as the small-leaved monopetalanthus, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of tiny, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, dry fruits that contain one or two seeds each. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological niche, it is of interest to botanists studying xerophytic adaptations.
