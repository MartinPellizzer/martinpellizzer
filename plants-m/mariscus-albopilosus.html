Mariscus albopilosus

Mariscus albopilosus, commonly known as the white-hairy mariscus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-green leaves and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun exposure. Its unique appearance and habitat make it a distinctive member of the Apiaceae family.

Mariscus albopilosus is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
