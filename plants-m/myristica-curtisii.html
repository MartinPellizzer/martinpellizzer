Myristica curtisii

Myristica curtisii, commonly known as the Curtis's nutmeg, is a species of flowering plant in the Myristicaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage and produces small, fragrant flowers that are followed by reddish-brown fruits. The seeds within the fruit are encased in a hard, aromatic shell, similar to the nutmeg of Myristica fragrans.

This species is valued for its unique aroma and is sometimes cultivated for its potential culinary and medicinal uses.
