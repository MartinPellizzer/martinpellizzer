Morus cathayana var. cathayana

Morus cathayana var. cathayana, commonly known as the Chinese mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are typically light green on the upper surface and pale white underneath. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as mulberries, which are edible and often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

The tree is also valued for its role in silk production, as its leaves are a primary food source for silkworms.
