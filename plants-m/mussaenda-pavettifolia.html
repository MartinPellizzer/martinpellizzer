Mussaenda pavettifolia

Mussaenda pavettifolia, commonly known as the flame bush, is a deciduous shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its clusters of bright orange-red bracts that resemble flowers, which are actually modified leaves. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in umbels. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Mussaenda pavettifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
