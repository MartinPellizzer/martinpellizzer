Matthiola phlox

Matthiola phlox, commonly known as garden phlox, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. The plant produces showy clusters of pink, purple, or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped, aromatic leaves that are often used in culinary applications.

Matthiola phlox is valued for its ornamental beauty and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
