Machaerium fulvovenosum

Machaerium fulvovenosum, commonly known as the golden venus grass, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical spikes, which are often borne in the axils of the leaves. Its root system is well-developed, allowing it to thrive in a variety of soil types, and it is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Machaerium fulvovenosum is also noted for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its native environments.
