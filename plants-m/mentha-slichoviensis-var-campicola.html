Mentha slichoviensis var. campicola

Mentha slichoviensis var. campicola, commonly known as the mountain mint, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and emit a strong minty fragrance when crushed.

The flowers are small, clustered in whorls, and typically bloom in shades of purple or blue.
