Malus baccata f. genuina

Malus baccata f. genuina, commonly known as the Chinese crabapple, is a compact, ornamental tree native to China.

It features small, oval leaves that are dark green on top and pale underneath, providing a striking contrast in the autumn. The plant produces clusters of white or pink flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, red berries in the fall. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its seasonal interest.

The variety is noted for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a range of soil conditions.
