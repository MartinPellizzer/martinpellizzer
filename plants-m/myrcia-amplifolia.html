Myrcia amplifolia

Myrcia amplifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf myrtle, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. Its small, fragrant flowers are typically white or pale pink and are followed by edible, berry-like fruits.

Myrcia amplifolia is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental and aromatic qualities.
