Mammillaria uberiformis

Mammillaria uberiformis, commonly known as the woolly mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, white, woolly covering that covers the stems and flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid environments. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its fuzzy appearance.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and collectors.
