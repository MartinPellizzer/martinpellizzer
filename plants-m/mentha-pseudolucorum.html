Mentha pseudolucorum

Mentha pseudolucorum, commonly known as Australian peppermint, is a perennial herb native to Australia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, lance-shaped leaves and spikes of pale blue to purple flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along waterways.

Mentha pseudolucorum is valued for its essential oils, which are used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine.
