Mentha × rubra var. wuerlii

Mentha × rubra var. wuerlii, commonly known as the Würlii mint, is a hybrid perennial herb in the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly minty leaves and clusters of small, pale purple to pink flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and prefers moist, well-drained soils. It is native to Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and culinary uses.

The leaves are used in teas and as a flavoring agent, though its scent is milder compared to common mint varieties.
