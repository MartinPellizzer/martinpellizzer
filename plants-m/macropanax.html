Macropanax

Macropanax, commonly known as the giant arum, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Araceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, particularly Southeast Asia. The plants are characterized by their large, arrow-shaped leaves and striking inflorescences that consist of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. Macropanax species are often cultivated for their ornamental value due to their dramatic foliage and unique flowering structures.
