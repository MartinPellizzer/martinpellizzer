Melaleuca viminalis subsp. rhododendron

Melaleuca viminalis subsp.

rhododendron, commonly known as the red myrtle, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia. It is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of showy red or pink flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant typically grows in coastal or sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal scrub. It has a woody stem and can reach heights of up to 3 meters.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
