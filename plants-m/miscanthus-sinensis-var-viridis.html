Miscanthus sinensis var. viridis

Miscanthus sinensis var. viridis, commonly known as Green Miscanthus, is a variant of the silvergrass genus Miscanthus.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that are a striking emerald green throughout the growing season. This plant typically grows in clumps and produces tall, slender flower spikes that are often pink or purplish in color. It is native to eastern Asia and is often cultivated as an ornamental grass in gardens and landscapes.

The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for low-maintenance garden designs.
