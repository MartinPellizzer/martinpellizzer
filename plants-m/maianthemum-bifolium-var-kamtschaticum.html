Maianthemum bifolium var. kamtschaticum

Maianthemum bifolium var. kamtschaticum, commonly known as the Kamtschaticum False Lily of the Valley, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It features a low-growing, rhizomatous structure with clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are typically broad, ovate, and arranged in a grass-like pattern, often with a bluish-green hue. This variety is closely related to other members of the Maianthemum genus, characterized by its ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in forest understories.
