Millingtonia lanceolata

Millingtonia lanceolata, commonly known as the flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to northern Queensland, Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has compound leaves that are alternate and pinnate, with elliptical to lance-shaped leaflets. Its bark is smooth and greyish, becoming fissured with age.

Millingtonia lanceolata is well adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, thriving in full sun and well-drained soils.
