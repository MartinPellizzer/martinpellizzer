Malus × floribunda var. aldenhamensis

Malus × floribunda var. aldenhamensis, commonly known as the Aldenham apple tree, is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth and profuse flowering, with clusters of white to pink blossoms appearing in early spring. The tree produces small, edible fruits that are typically tart and used for cider or cooking. It is well-suited to temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in horticulture.
