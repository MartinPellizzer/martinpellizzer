Melinis purpurea

Melinis purpurea, commonly known as purple lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and its striking purple plumes that appear in late summer. The plant has narrow, blue-green leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in disturbed areas and along roadsides.

Melinis purpurea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
