Microchloa setacea f. humilis

Microchloa setacea f. humilis, commonly known as the dwarf tufted grass, is a low-growing, perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features tufted clumps of slender, wiry leaves that are typically less than 10 cm in height. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, pale green to brown flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires moderate to high levels of sunlight.

Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for use in ornamental landscaping and as a ground cover.
