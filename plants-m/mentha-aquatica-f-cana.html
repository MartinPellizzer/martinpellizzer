Mentha aquatica f. cana

Mentha aquatica f. cana, commonly known as water mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and stream banks.

It features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy and have a strong minty aroma. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in temperate regions.

This variety is closely related to other mint species and is valued for its aromatic properties and ecological benefits.
