Muscari tenuiflorum subsp. clusianum

Muscari tenuiflorum subsp.

clusianum, commonly known as the slender-flowered grape hyacinth, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale blue to violet, resembling grapes, hence its common name.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders. Its foliage consists of strap-like leaves that emerge in early spring, followed by the flowering period in late winter to early spring.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, fragrant blooms.
