Monochoria vaginalis

Monochoria vaginalis, commonly known as water spinach or kangkong, is a aquatic or semi-aquatic herb native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and slender, edible stems that grow in water or moist soil. The plant has a fibrous root system and reproduces through seeds and vegetative propagation. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in rice fields and wetlands.

Monochoria vaginalis is valued for its nutritional content and is widely used in various cuisines.
