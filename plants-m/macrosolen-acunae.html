Macrosolen acunae

Macrosolen acunae, commonly known as the Acuna's giant morning glory, is a striking flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter, typically displaying vibrant shades of pink, red, or purple. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and a vigorous climbing habit, often reaching several meters in height. Its flowers open in the morning and close at dusk, a trait shared with other members of the morning glory family.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
