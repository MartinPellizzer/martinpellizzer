Mammillaria auriareolis

Mammillaria auriareolis, commonly known as the golden-hair mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in white to pale yellow tubercles. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
