Matthiola lunata f. anoplia

Matthiola lunata f. anoplia, commonly known as the silverleaf stock, is a cultivar of the stock plant family.

It is characterized by its striking, silvery-white foliage and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant is a biennial or short-lived perennial, typically reaching a height of 30 to 60 centimeters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long flowering period.
