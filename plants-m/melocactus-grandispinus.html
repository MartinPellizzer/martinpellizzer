Melocactus grandispinus

Melocactus grandispinus, commonly known as the giant spiny cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family and is native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stem that can grow up to 3 meters in height. The plant features prominent, sharp spines and produces yellow flowers during the warmer months. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

Melocactus grandispinus is often cultivated for its striking appearance and resilience.
