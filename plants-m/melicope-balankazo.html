Melicope balankazo

Melicope balankazo, commonly known as the Madagascar lauraceae, is a member of the Lauraceae family.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Madagascar, characterized by its aromatic leaves and smooth, dark bark. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within the tropical rainforests of the island. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a glossy surface with a distinctive scent when crushed.

Melicope balankazo is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
