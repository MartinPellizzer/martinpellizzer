Menodora helianthemoides var. magniflora

Menodora helianthemoides var. magniflora, commonly known as the showy sunhemp, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems, which help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape and have a rough texture.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has also been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
