Mussaenda hirsuta

Mussaenda hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy Mussaenda, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, often glandular stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Mussaenda hirsuta is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
