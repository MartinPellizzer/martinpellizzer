Myrcia amblyphylla

Myrcia amblyphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, leathery, and have a strong, spicy scent.

The fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds.
