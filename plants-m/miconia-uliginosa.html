Miconia uliginosa

Miconia uliginosa, commonly known as the swamp miconia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit is a fleshy, orange-red berry that contains numerous seeds.

Miconia uliginosa is often found in wetland habitats and can form dense thickets, impacting local ecosystems.
