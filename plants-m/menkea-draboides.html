Menkea draboides

Menkea draboides, commonly known as the narrowleaf menkea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and gravelly soils. The plant features slender, hairy stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. Its flowers are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences and are usually pale green to yellowish in color.

Menkea draboides is adapted to arid environments and has a low-growing, mat-forming habit.
