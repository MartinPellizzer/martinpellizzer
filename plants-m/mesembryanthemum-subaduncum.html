Mesembryanthemum subaduncum

Mesembryanthemum subaduncum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved iceplant, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Its ability to store water in its leaves makes it a hardy, drought-tolerant species.
