Mendoncia gilgiana subsp. occidentalis

Mendoncia gilgiana subsp.

occidentalis, commonly known as the western mendoncia, is a species native to the western United States. It is a perennial herb characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, preferring well-drained environments. It blooms in the spring and is often found in open woodlands and mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it an important part of local ecosystems and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
