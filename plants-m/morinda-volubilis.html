Morinda volubilis

Morinda volubilis, commonly known as the Indian madder or red root, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its trailing stems and clusters of small, white or pink flowers. The plant produces orange-red berries that contain the seeds, which are used in traditional medicine. Its roots and leaves are harvested for their medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antifungal effects.

Morinda volubilis is also valued for its ornamental appeal due to its vibrant foliage and flowering habit.
