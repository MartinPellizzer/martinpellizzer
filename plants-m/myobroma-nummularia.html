Myobroma nummularia

Myobroma nummularia, commonly known as the silver dollar plant, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are round and covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It is a succulent, capable of storing water in its thick, fleshy leaves, which allows it to thrive in arid conditions.

Myobroma nummularia is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
