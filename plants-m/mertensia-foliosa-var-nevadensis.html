Mertensia foliosa var. nevadensis

Mertensia foliosa var. nevadensis, commonly known as the Sierra violet, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped, bluish-green leaves and clusters of pale violet to lavender flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and meadows. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
