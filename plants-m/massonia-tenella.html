Massonia tenella

Massonia tenella, commonly known as the silver coin plant, is a succulent native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically silver or blue-green in color, arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or white during the warmer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its low maintenance and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor gardens.
