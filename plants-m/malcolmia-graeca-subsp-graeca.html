Malcolmia graeca subsp. graeca

Malcolmia graeca subsp.

graeca, commonly known as the Greek malcolmia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows as a low-growing annual or biennial, with hairy, lance-shaped leaves. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
