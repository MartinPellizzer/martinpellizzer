Manilkara mayarensis subsp. moaensis

Manilkara mayarensis subsp.

moaensis, commonly known as the Moa mango, is a species of evergreen tree native to the Caribbean islands of Cuba and Hispaniola. It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its large, simple leaves and showy, fragrant flowers.

The tree produces edible fruits that are similar in appearance to mangoes, with a smooth, yellowish skin and a sweet, fibrous pulp. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been the subject of botanical study due to its unique genetic traits.
