Merostachys procerrima

Merostachys procerrima, commonly known as the long-bracted grass.

It is a species of grass native to South America, particularly found in regions of Brazil, Colombia, and Peru. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical environments, often in moist, shaded areas. Its foliage is characterized by narrow, elongated leaves with a prominent midrib.

The inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, bract-like structures that give it its common name.
