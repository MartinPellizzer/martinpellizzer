Mertensia brachyloba

Mertensia brachyloba, commonly known as the shortstem bluebells, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue to violet in color, blooming in early spring. The plant has a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and slightly succulent. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
