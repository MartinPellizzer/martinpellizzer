Morus peruviana

Morus peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, aggregate fruits. The plant is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils. Its bark is smooth and grayish, and it can reach heights of up to 15 meters.

The Peruvian mulberry is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
