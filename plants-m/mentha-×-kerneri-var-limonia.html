Mentha × kerneri var. limonia

Mentha × kerneri var. limonia, commonly known as the lemon mint, is a hybrid mint species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, lemon-scented leaves and small, pale purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are often used in culinary applications for their refreshing aroma and flavor.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and aromatic properties in both garden and herbal settings.
