Maytenus dryandri var. canariensis

Maytenus dryandri var. canariensis, commonly known as the Canary Island mayten, is a small evergreen tree native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by dark blue, fleshy berries that are edible for birds and small mammals. It thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to the humid, subtropical climate of its native habitat.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural use in warm climates.
