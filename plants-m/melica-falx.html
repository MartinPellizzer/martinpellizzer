Melica falx

Melica falx, commonly known as the sickle grass, is a perennial grass species belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and distinctive sickle-shaped inflorescence. Native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and is often found in meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it a common subject in ecological studies and botanical gardens.
