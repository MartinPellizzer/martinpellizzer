Malaxis tridentula

Malaxis tridentula, commonly known as the three-toothed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and grasslands. Its roots are fibrous and adapted for nutrient absorption in poor soils.

The species is notable for its unique floral structure and is often found in association with specific mycorrhizal fungi.
