Machaonia subinermis subsp. armata

Machaonia subinermis subsp.

armata, commonly known as the New Zealand mountain hakea, is a shrub native to the Southern Alps of New Zealand. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and produces small, urn-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red.

The plant thrives in high-altitude environments, often growing in rocky or alpine soils. It has a compact growth habit and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
