Masdevallia zygia

Masdevallia zygia, commonly known as the Zygia Masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and intricate, often colorful flowers that resemble miniature orchids. The plant typically features a short rhizome with a few pseudobulbs, and its leaves are usually narrow and strap-like. It thrives in cool, humid environments with high light exposure, making it a popular choice for enthusiasts of epiphytic orchids.

Due to its unique appearance and specific cultivation requirements, it is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
