Miconia tococoides

Miconia tococoides, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Miconia tococoides*, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
