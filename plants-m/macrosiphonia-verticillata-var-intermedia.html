Macrosiphonia verticillata var. intermedia

Macrosiphonia verticillata var. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate featherfoil, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, creeping stems that often form dense mats on the ground. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are typically narrow and feather-like, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of water bodies.
