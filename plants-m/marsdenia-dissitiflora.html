Marsdenia dissitiflora

Marsdenia dissitiflora, commonly known as the scattered-flower Marsdenia, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by follicles containing seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
