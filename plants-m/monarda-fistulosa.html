Monarda fistulosa

Monarda fistulosa, commonly known as wild bergamot, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely leafed stems and showy, lavender-pink flower clusters that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is often found in prairies, woodlands, and open fields. Its aromatic leaves and flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It is also used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
