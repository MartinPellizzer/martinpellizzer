Mesembryanthemum geminum

Mesembryanthemum geminum, commonly known as the ice plant, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, white, and pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It is well adapted to full sun and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a valuable addition to rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
