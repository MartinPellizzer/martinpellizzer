Myrcia espiritosantensis

Myrcia espiritosantensis, commonly known as the Espirito Santo myrtle, is a shrub native to Brazil, specifically the state of Espirito Santo.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
