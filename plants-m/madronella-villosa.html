Madronella villosa

Madronella villosa, commonly known as the hairy madrona, is a shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its rough, grayish bark and clusters of white to pinkish flowers that bloom in the spring. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a hairy texture, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

The plant is also noted for its fragrant blossoms and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
