Mentha sylvestris var. origanoides

Mentha sylvestris var. origanoides, commonly known as wild mint or origanoides mint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, slightly peppery leaves and small, pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their antiseptic and digestive properties.

It is also valued in culinary applications for its unique flavor profile.
