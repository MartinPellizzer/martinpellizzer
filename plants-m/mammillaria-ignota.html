Mammillaria ignota

Mammillaria ignota, commonly known as the ghost cactus, is a small, spineless cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its pale green to bluish-gray stems, which are covered in small, white tubercles. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance, it is a popular choice for succulent collections.
