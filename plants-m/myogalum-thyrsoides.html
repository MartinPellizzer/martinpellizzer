Myogalum thyrsoides

Myogalum thyrsoides, commonly known as the mountain thistle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, showy purple flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has a rosette of basal leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. Its flowers are typically arranged in a dense, spike-like inflorescence, which is a distinctive feature of the species.

Myogalum thyrsoides is adapted to grow in rocky and well-drained soils, often in high-altitude environments.
