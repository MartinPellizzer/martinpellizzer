Mentha longifolia var. cardibasea

Mentha longifolia var. cardibasea, commonly known as creeping mint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit, with slender stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, pale purple to white flowers in whorled clusters, and its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape with a serrated margin. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments.

This variety is valued for its aromatic leaves and is sometimes used in culinary or medicinal applications.
