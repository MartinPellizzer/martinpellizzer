Mammillaria lasiacantha f. denudata

Mammillaria lasiacantha f. denudata, commonly known as the naked mammillaria, is a rare and attractive cactus species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in sparse, white to pale green tubercles. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect sunlight.

Due to its rarity, it is often cultivated in specialized collections and requires careful care to ensure its survival.
