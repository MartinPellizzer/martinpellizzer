Miconia crassipes

Miconia crassipes, commonly known as the broadleaf miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit is a three-lobed, fleshy berry that turns red when ripe.

Miconia crassipes is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking foliage and showy blooms.
