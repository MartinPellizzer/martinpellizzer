Monarda involucrata

Monarda involucrata, commonly known as pale coneflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern and central United States.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Pale coneflower is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
