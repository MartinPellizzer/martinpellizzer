Micropteryx crista-galli

Micropteryx crista-galli, commonly known as the cock's comb or cocklecomb, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its distinctive, feathery inflorescence that resembles a comb. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a dense, branched structure. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Due to its unique appearance, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
