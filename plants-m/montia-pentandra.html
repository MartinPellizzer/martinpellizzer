Montia pentandra

Montia pentandra, commonly known as the five-lobed speedwell, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Montiaceae family and is characterized by its small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in open woodland or streamside habitats.

Montia pentandra is a herbaceous perennial that plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
