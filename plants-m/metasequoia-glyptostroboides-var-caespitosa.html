Metasequoia glyptostroboides var. caespitosa

Metasequoia glyptostroboides var. caespitosa, commonly known as the Chinese dawn redwood, is a deciduous conifer native to China.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and feathery, needle-like leaves that turn red in the fall. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, typical of gymnosperms. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and historical significance as an ancient species once thought extinct.
