Mentha × microstachys

Mentha × microstachys, commonly known as hybrid mint, is a cross between spearmint (Mentha spicata) and watermint (Mentha aquatica).

It is a perennial herb characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem. Mentha × microstachys is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

It is widely cultivated for its aromatic leaves, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications.
