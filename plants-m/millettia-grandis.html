Millettia grandis

Millettia grandis, commonly known as the giant millettia, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its large, compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces elongated, flat pods containing edible seeds that are used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and nutritional benefits, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and agricultural purposes.
