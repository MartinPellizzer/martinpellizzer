Myrtoleucodendron calycinum

Myrtoleucodendron calycinum, commonly known as the golden-leaved myrtle, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that often have a golden or yellowish tint on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil to flourish.
