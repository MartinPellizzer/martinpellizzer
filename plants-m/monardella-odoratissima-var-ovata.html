Monardella odoratissima var. ovata

Monardella odoratissima var. ovata, commonly known as sweet mountain mint, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its pleasant scent. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a common sight in dry, open habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for pollinators and a popular choice in native plant gardens.
