Malaxis mandonii

Malaxis mandonii, commonly known as the Mandon's lady's tresses, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and a spike of small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in a loose spiral. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and rocky areas. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the soil.

Malaxis mandonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
