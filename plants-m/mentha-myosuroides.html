Mentha myosuroides

Mentha myosuroides, commonly known as cat mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Mentha myosuroides is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported calming and antispasmodic properties.

Its essential oils, extracted from the leaves and flowers, are valued for their distinctive scent and potential therapeutic uses.
