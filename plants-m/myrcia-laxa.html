Myrcia laxa

Myrcia laxa, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or red myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces bright red, cherry-like fruits that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. Myrcia laxa thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in dense forests or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in botanical studies.
