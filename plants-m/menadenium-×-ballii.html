Menadenium × ballii

Menadenium × ballii, commonly known as the hybrid spider plant, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Menadenium marmoratum and Menadenium crispum.

It is a perennial evergreen plant native to South Africa, characterized by its rosette growth habit and striking, variegated foliage. The leaves are typically broad, fleshy, and feature a combination of green, white, and pink markings. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by red berries, which are a source of food for birds and small mammals.

This plant is well-suited for both indoor and outdoor cultivation due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
