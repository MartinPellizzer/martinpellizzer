Myrciaria obscura

Myrciaria obscura, commonly known as the Brazilian blackberry, is a species of shrub native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dark purple to black berries that are rich in antioxidants. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are elliptical with a glossy surface, and the plant produces small, fragrant white flowers.

Myrciaria obscura is valued for its edible fruits and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
