Miconia tepicana

Miconia tepicana, commonly known as the tropical beauty leaf, is a member of the Melastomataceae family.

It is a shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It produces showy, clustered inflorescences with pink or red flowers that attract various pollinators.

The species is also notable for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its use in ornamental horticulture.
