Melaleuca fascicularis

Melaleuca fascicularis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved paperbark, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia, where it typically grows in coastal and sandy soils. The plant is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects.

The species is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
