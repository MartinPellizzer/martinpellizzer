Manihot macrantha

Manihot macrantha, commonly known as the large-flowered arrowroot, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant produces edible tubers similar to those of other arrowroot species, which are used in culinary applications. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and often have a waxy texture, while its stems are succulent and capable of storing water.

Manihot macrantha thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.
