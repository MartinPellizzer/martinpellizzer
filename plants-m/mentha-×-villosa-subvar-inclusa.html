Mentha × villosa subvar. inclusa

Mentha × villosa subvar. inclusa, commonly known as woolly mint, is a hybrid species within the mint family (Lamiaceae).

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and aromatic, often spearmint-like scent. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has square stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves.

The flowers are small, tubular, and usually violet or purple in color, arranged in whorled clusters.
