Mussaenda leptantha

Mussaenda leptantha, commonly known as the slender flame bush, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered yellow-orange flowers that are often mistaken for those of the genus *Hibiscus*. The plant has opposite, simple, and usually hairy leaves that are elliptical to ovate in shape. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Mussaenda leptantha is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
