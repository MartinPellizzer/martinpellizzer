Myrinia microphylla

Myrinia microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved myrtle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its small, glossy leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is evergreen, with a shrub-like growth habit and is often found in coastal and sandy soils. Its leaves are aromatic and contain essential oils, which contribute to its medicinal and aromatic uses.

Myrinia microphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
