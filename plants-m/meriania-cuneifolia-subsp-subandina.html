Meriania cuneifolia subsp. subandina

Meriania cuneifolia subsp.

subandina, commonly known as the Andean beautyberry, is a shrub native to the Andes region of South America. It is characterized by its dark green, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence.

The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with pale yellow flowers, which are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. These berries are edible and have a sweet, slightly tart flavor, making them popular in local cuisines.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests at higher elevations.
