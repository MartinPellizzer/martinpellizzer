Mesosphaerum corymbosum

Mesosphaerum corymbosum, commonly known as the yellow sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its low-growing habit and dense clusters of yellow flowers. The plant has small, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water in harsh environments. Its flowers are typically arranged in corymbs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Mesosphaerum corymbosum is adapted to sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland dunes.
