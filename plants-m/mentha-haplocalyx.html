Mentha haplocalyx

Mentha haplocalyx, commonly known as Chinese mint or honey mint, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its square, hollow stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in whorled clusters, typical of the Lamiaceae family. Mentha haplocalyx is valued for its refreshing, menthol-like scent and is used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.
