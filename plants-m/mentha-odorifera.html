Mentha odorifera

Mentha odorifera, commonly known as the Japanese mint, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, narrow leaves and clusters of small, pale blue or white flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forested areas. Its aromatic properties make it valuable in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.

Mentha odorifera is also used in aromatherapy and for its potential antioxidant and anti-inflammatory benefits.
