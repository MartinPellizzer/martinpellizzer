Micrandra elata

Micrandra elata, commonly known as the slender sandmat, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its leaves are lanceolate and often covered in fine pubescence.

Micrandra elata is adapted to arid environments and is often found in sandy or gravelly soils.
