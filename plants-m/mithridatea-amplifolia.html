Mithridatea amplifolia

Mithridatea amplifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf mithridate, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad, waxy leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its unique leaf structure helps it thrive in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
