Morus cordatifolia

Morus cordatifolia, commonly known as the heart-shaped mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The tree produces small, aggregate fruits that turn from green to dark purple as they mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in local medicine.
