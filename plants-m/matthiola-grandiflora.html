Matthiola grandiflora

Matthiola grandiflora, commonly known as the stock flower, is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in temperate climates. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, with branched stems and linear, aromatic leaves. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink and purple, bloom in clusters and are often used in ornamental gardening.

The plant produces small, rounded pods containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
