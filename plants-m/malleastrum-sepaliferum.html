Malleastrum sepaliferum

Malleastrum sepaliferum, commonly known as the velvetleaf mallow, is a flowering plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, velvety leaves and showy, pale pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in wet or disturbed soils. Its sepals are prominent and often mistaken for petals, giving it the common name "sepaliferum.".

Malleastrum sepaliferum is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain areas due to its rapid growth and ability to spread aggressively.
