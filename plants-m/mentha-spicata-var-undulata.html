Mentha spicata var. undulata

Mentha spicata var. undulata, commonly known as catmint, is a variant of the common mint plant.

It is characterized by its gray-green, undulating leaves and purple or blue flowers that bloom in summer. This perennial herb is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in rocky or dry soils. The plant has a strong, minty aroma and is often used in herbal teas and as a decorative plant.

Its foliage is also popular in ornamental gardening for its visual appeal.
