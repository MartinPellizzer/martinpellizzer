Mentha × crenatifolia

Mentha × crenatifolia, commonly known as catnip, is a hybrid perennial plant in the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom in late summer. Mentha × crenatifolia is native to Europe and Asia but has been naturalized in many parts of the world.

It is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves and historical use in herbal remedies and as a companion plant.
