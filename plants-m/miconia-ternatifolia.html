Miconia ternatifolia

Miconia ternatifolia, commonly known as the three-leaf miconia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in whorls of three. The plant produces small, clustered white or pink flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruits are fleshy berries that turn red when mature, attracting various bird species.

Miconia ternatifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
