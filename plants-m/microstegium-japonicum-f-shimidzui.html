Microstegium japonicum f. shimidzui

Microstegium japonicum f. shimidzui, commonly known as the Japanese woodland grass.

It is a perennial grass species characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, blade-like leaves. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, brownish flowers.

The species is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
