Miscanthus sinensis var. intermedius

Miscanthus sinensis var. intermedius, commonly known as the silver grass or Chinese silvergrass, is a perennial grass native to East Asia.

It features slender, silver-blue foliage that becomes more pronounced in the autumn, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces tall, slender spikes of small, fluffy flowers during the late summer to early autumn season. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and landscapes due to its aesthetic appeal and low maintenance requirements.

This variety is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to both drought and frost.
