Melocactus ellemeetii

Melocactus ellemeetii, commonly known as the red-tipped melon cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are typically covered in dense, white to grayish spines. The plant produces vibrant orange to red flowers during the spring and summer months. Its roots are shallow and adapted to absorb moisture quickly from infrequent rains.

Melocactus ellemeetii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
