Muscari aucheri

Muscari aucheri, commonly known as the Persian grape hyacinth, is a bulbous plant native to the mountains of Central Asia.

It produces clusters of bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers that resemble tiny grapes, giving it its common name. The plant has strap-like, grassy foliage that emerges in early spring. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Muscari aucheri is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
