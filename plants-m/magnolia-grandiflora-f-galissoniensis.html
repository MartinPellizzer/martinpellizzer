Magnolia grandiflora f. galissoniensis

Magnolia grandiflora f. galissoniensis, commonly known as the Galissoniers magnolia, is a rare and ornamental cultivar of the southern magnolia.

It is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are broad, glossy, and dark green, providing a striking contrast to the showy blossoms. This variety is native to the southeastern United States and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
