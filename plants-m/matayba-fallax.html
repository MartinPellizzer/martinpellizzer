Matayba fallax

Matayba fallax, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Erythroxylum fallax*, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, glossy leaves and clusters of bright red, cherry-like fruits. The plant belongs to the family Erythroxylaceae and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces a resinous sap that has been used traditionally in some cultures.
