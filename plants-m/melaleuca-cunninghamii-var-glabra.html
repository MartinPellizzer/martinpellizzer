Melaleuca cunninghamii var. glabra

Melaleuca cunninghamii var. glabra, commonly known as the Smooth Melaleuca, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale grey bark and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, bottle-shaped flowers in clusters, typically in shades of white or pale yellow. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or sandy habitats.

This species is known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
