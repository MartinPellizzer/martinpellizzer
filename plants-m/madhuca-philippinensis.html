Madhuca philippinensis

Madhuca philippinensis, commonly known as the Philippine buttertree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and large, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits containing a single seed. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and montane forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
