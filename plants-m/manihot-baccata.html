Manihot baccata

Manihot baccata, commonly known as the red-rooted arrowleaf, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive red roots and heart-shaped leaves with a waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into yellowish, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
