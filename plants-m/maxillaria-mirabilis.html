Maxillaria mirabilis

Maxillaria mirabilis, commonly known as the showy maxillaria, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often brightly colored flowers that can range from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are borne on branching inflorescences and are known for their intricate, fragrant structures.

Maxillaria mirabilis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
