Moringa pterygosperma

Moringa pterygosperma, commonly known as the African horseradish, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its branched stems and alternate, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by elongated, woody pods containing kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid climates, making it a resilient species for harsh environments.

The seeds are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil.
