Melaleuca pilosa var. pilosa

Melaleuca pilosa var. pilosa, commonly known as the rough-leaved bottlebrush, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy foliage and striking red or pink bottlebrush-like flowers that bloom in winter. The plant produces small, woody fruits called follicles, which contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for native wildlife.
