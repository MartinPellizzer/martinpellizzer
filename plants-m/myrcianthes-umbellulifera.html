Myrcianthes umbellulifera

Myrcianthes umbellulifera, commonly known as the umbellulifera myrcia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered white flowers. The plant produces woody fruits that contain several seeds, typical of many species in the Myrtaceae family. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
