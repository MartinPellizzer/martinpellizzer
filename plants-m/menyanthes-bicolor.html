Menyanthes bicolor

Menyanthes bicolor, commonly known as the twinflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its distinctive two-colored flowers, which typically display a white base with a pink or red upper lip. The plant features slender, hairy stems that arise from a rhizomatous root system. Its leaves are opposite, heart-shaped, and often covered with fine hairs.

Menyanthes bicolor thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in bogs and wet meadows.
