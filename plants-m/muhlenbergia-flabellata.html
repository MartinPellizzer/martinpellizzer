Muhlenbergia flabellata

Muhlenbergia flabellata, commonly known as the silver bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-gray foliage and fluffy, bottlebrush-like seed heads that appear in late summer. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping. Its ornamental value comes from its striking appearance and ability to form dense, attractive clumps.

Muhlenbergia flabellata is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
