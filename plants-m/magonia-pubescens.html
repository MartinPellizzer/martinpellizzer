Magonia pubescens

Magonia pubescens, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, warm environments and is often found in wetlands and along riverbanks.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive tendencies, it can become a problematic weed in agricultural areas.
