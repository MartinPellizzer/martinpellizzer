Mucuna peekelii

Mucuna peekelii, commonly known as the velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of small, bean-like pods that contain edible seeds. The plant is notable for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it adaptable to different environments. Its flowers are typically pale yellow or greenish-white, and it produces a distinctive, sweet-smelling aroma.

Mucuna peekelii is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing properties, which enrich the soil and support agricultural practices.
