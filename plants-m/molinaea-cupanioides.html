Molinaea cupanioides

Molinaea cupanioides, commonly known as the mountain beauty, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments within cloud forests. The plant features small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a subtle fragrance. Its leaves are usually oval-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Molinaea cupanioides is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
