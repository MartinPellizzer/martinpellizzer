Melodorum sphaerocarpum

Melodorum sphaerocarpum, commonly known as the globe-fruited melode, is a rare and unusual plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique spherical fruit, which is covered in a thick, waxy cuticle that protects the seeds inside. The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves and produces delicate, white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its growth is slow, and it thrives in shaded, humid environments with well-drained soil.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern.
