Medinilla longipedunculata

Medinilla longipedunculata, commonly known as the long-peduncled Medinilla, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its dense, fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a subtle scent reminiscent of citrus. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves that are ovate to elliptical in shape, providing a striking contrast to its showy blooms. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic value.

Medinilla longipedunculata is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
