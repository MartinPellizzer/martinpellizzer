Mentha × piperita var. subhirsuta

Mentha × piperita var. subhirsuta, commonly known as honey mint, is a hybrid mint species characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and a sweet, honey-like fragrance.

It is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale purple or blue, arranged in whorled clusters. Its leaves are oval to lance-shaped, with a slightly serrated edge and a dense covering of fine hairs.

This variety is valued for its aromatic properties and is used in culinary and medicinal applications.
