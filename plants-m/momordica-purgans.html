Momordica purgans

Momordica purgans, commonly known as the African bitter gourd, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is a climbing vine that produces long, slender fruits with a warty surface and a distinctive bitter taste. The plant is native to tropical Africa and thrives in warm, humid climates. Its leaves are heart-shaped and large, while its flowers are yellow with five petals.

The fruit is often used in traditional medicine for its purported digestive and detoxifying properties.
