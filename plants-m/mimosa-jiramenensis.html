Mimosa jiramenensis

Mimosa jiramenensis, commonly known as the Jiramen mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America, specifically Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. It has a shallow root system and is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it an important species in its native habitat.
