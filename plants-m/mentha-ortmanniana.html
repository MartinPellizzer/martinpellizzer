Mentha ortmanniana

Mentha ortmanniana, commonly known as Ortmann's mint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly square stems and opposite, toothed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Mentha ortmanniana is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
