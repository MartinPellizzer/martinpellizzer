Manabea villosa

Manabea villosa, commonly known as the hairy manabea, is a species of flowering plant in the family Malvaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and showy, five-petaled flowers that bloom in shades of pink and purple. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
