Megastachya fasciculata

Megastachya fasciculata, commonly known as the clustered spike grass, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear dense clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and forest edges. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy surface.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in wet conditions.
