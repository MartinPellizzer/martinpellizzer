Matthiola odoratissima var. integrifolia

Matthiola odoratissima var. integrifolia, commonly known as the sweet rocket, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants. The plant features upright stems and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly peppery scent when crushed.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and fragrance.
