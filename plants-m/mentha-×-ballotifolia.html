Mentha × ballotifolia

Mentha × ballotifolia, commonly known as catnip, is a hybrid perennial plant in the mint family (Lamiaceae).

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom in late summer. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been naturalized in many regions around the world.

Mentha × ballotifolia is known for its aromatic properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies and as a stimulant for cats.
