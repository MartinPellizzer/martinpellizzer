Maralia

Maralia, commonly known as the bluebell, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaryllis family.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and prefers well-drained, acidic soil. Maralia has strap-like, grassy leaves that emerge from a bulbous base.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often found in native gardens and natural habitats.
