Marshallia lacinarioides

Marshallia lacinarioides, commonly known as the swamp milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant features showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators including butterflies and bees. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a fuzzy texture, while its stems are usually hairy and can reach heights of up to three feet.

Marshallia lacinarioides plays an important role in supporting local ecosystems and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
