Miconia theaezans var. vulgaris

Miconia theaezans var. vulgaris, commonly known as the common miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers, which are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Miconia theaezans var. vulgaris is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions, outcompeting native vegetation.
