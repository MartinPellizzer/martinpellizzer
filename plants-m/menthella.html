Menthella

Menthella, commonly known as catnip, is a flowering plant in the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or white. The plant contains high levels of volatile oils, particularly nepetalactone, which is responsible for its distinctive aroma and effects on animals. Menthella is native to temperate regions of Europe, Asia, and North America.

It is often cultivated for its aromatic properties and traditional medicinal uses.
