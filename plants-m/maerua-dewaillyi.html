Maerua dewaillyi

Maerua dewaillyi, commonly known as the desert acacia, is a shrub native to arid regions of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its small, green, compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom during the rainy season. The plant has a deep root system that allows it to survive in harsh, dry environments. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its ability to thrive in extreme conditions makes it an important species in desert ecosystems.
