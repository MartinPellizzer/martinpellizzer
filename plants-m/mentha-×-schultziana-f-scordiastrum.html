Mentha × schultziana f. scordiastrum

Mentha × schultziana f. scordiastrum, commonly known as the garlic chives or garlic chive, is a perennial herb belonging to the mint family (Lamiaceae).

It is characterized by its aromatic, flat, grass-like leaves that emit a strong garlic scent when crushed. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers in whorled clusters, typical of the mint genus. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its edible leaves and mild garlic flavor.

This variety is a hybrid between Mentha × schultziana and other related species, showcasing genetic diversity within the Mentha genus.
