Mammillaria muehlenpfordtii

Mammillaria muehlenpfordtii, commonly known as the Muehlenpfordt's prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and dense covering of white to pale blue spines. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its gray-green body. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Its slow growth rate and unique appearance make it a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
