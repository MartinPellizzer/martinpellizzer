Mussaenda hilaris

Mussaenda hilaris, commonly known as the golden trumpet bush, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, orange-yellow inflorescences that resemble trumpets, which are composed of clustered flowers. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth texture and a glossy surface. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Mussaenda hilaris is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
