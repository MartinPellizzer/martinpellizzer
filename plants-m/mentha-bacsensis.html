Mentha bacsensis

Mentha bacsensis, commonly known as Bacska mint, is a perennial herb native to the Carpathian Basin.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale purple or white, arranged in whorls along the stem. Bacska mint is valued for its aromatic leaves, which are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.
