Mimulus parviflorus

Mimulus parviflorus, commonly known as the small-flower monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its bright orange or yellow tubular flowers, which are adapted for pollination by hummingbirds and bees. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence.

Mimulus parviflorus is a perennial species that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
