Matricaria feniculacea

Matricaria feniculacea, commonly known as the common tansy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow flowers and finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves and flowers are used in traditional medicine for their aromatic and antiseptic properties.

Matricaria feniculacea is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract beneficial insects.
