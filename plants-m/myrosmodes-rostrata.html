Myrosmodes rostrata

Myrosmodes rostrata, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its straight, dark red heartwood that is highly valued for its durability and rich color. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptic in shape with a smooth margin. Its flowers are small and yellowish, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, round fruits containing a single seed.

The tree is often found in lowland rainforests and is known for its slow growth and long lifespan.
