Medica sylvestris

Medica sylvestris, commonly known as wild medicinal herb, is a perennial plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features a slender, upright stem covered in fine hairs and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by oval, dark blue berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its leaves and berries have been traditionally used in herbal medicine for their purported healing properties.
