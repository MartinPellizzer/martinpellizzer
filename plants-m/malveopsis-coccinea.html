Malveopsis coccinea

Malveopsis coccinea, commonly known as the scarlet mallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens in regions with a suitable climate.
