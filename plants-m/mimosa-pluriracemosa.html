Mimosa pluriracemosa

Mimosa pluriracemosa, commonly known as the clustered sensitive plant, is a species native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that fold shut when touched, a response triggered by light or touch stimuli. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun exposure.

Mimosa pluriracemosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable climates.
