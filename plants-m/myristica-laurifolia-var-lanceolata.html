Myristica laurifolia var. lanceolata

Myristica laurifolia var. lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceolate nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces drupes that contain aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and as a spice. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Its botanical features make it valuable for both ecological and commercial purposes.
