Mimosa biuncifera var. lindheimeri

Mimosa biuncifera var. lindheimeri, commonly known as Lindheimer's sensitiveplant, is a species native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves that fold shut when touched. The plant produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its unique response to touch, known as thigmonasty, is a notable botanical feature.
