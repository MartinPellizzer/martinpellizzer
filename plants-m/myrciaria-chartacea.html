Myrciaria chartacea

Myrciaria chartacea, commonly known as the Brazilian blackberry, is a shrub native to Brazil and belongs to the Myrtaceae family.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tangy flavor. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while its flowers are small and white.

The fruit is often used in culinary applications and traditional medicine due to its nutritional and medicinal properties.
