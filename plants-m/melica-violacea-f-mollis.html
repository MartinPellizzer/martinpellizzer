Melica violacea f. mollis

Melica violacea f. mollis, commonly known as the softleaf bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, soft foliage and pale blue-green leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear blue-violet flowers, adding ornamental value to gardens.

Due to its delicate foliage and attractive blooms, it is often cultivated for landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
