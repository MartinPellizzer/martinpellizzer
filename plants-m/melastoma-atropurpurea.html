Melastoma atropurpurea

Melastoma atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple-flowered melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its showy, violet-pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and shrublands. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

The fruit is a capsule that releases small, dark seeds when mature.
