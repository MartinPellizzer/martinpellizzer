Mussaenda decaryi

Mussaenda decaryi, commonly known as the African flame bush, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, brightly colored bracts that range from red to orange, which are often mistaken for flowers. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually ovate or elliptic in shape. It produces small, white, five-petaled flowers that are clustered in inflorescences.

Mussaenda decaryi thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant foliage.
