Mammillaria xanthotricha

Mammillaria xanthotricha, commonly known as the yellow-haired mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in white to yellowish spines, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It is a compact, slow-growing species that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
