Memoremea scorpioides

Memoremea scorpioides, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a rare and striking plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, pale-blue or white flowers that resemble small, translucent ghosts, hence its common name. The plant has slender, wiry stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing in dense undergrowth.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is highly sought after by botanists and plant enthusiasts alike.
