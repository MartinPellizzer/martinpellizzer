Miconia ocoana

Miconia ocoana, commonly known as the Ocoa miconia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
