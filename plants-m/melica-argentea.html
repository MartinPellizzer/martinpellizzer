Melica argentea

Melica argentea, commonly known as silver grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray blades that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, streambanks, and other damp habitats. It produces small, pale green to brown spikelets that are arranged in dense clusters.

Melica argentea is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its elegant, flowing foliage.
