Millettia fragrantissima

Millettia fragrantissima, commonly known as the fragrant millettia, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Asia, including parts of India, China, and Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with pinnate leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
