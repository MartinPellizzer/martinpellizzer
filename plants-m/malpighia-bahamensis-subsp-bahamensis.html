Malpighia bahamensis subsp. bahamensis

Malpighia bahamensis subsp.

bahamensis, commonly known as the Bahamian acorn vine, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean islands. It belongs to the Malpighiaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, bright red berries.

The plant produces edible fruits that are often consumed fresh or used in local cuisines. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal and lowland habitats.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in its native range.
