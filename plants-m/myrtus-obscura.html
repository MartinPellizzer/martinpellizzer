Myrtus obscura

Myrtus obscura, commonly known as the narrow-leaved myrtle, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of pale pink to white flowers. The plant produces small, dark blue to black berries that are edible but have a strong, spicy flavor. Myrtus obscura is often used in traditional medicine for its aromatic properties.

It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
