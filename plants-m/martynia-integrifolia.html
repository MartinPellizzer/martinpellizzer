Martynia integrifolia

Martynia integrifolia, commonly known as the redweed or redflower.

It is a flowering plant native to South America, characterized by its striking red or orange flowers. The plant has simple, entire leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

Martynia integrifolia is also known for its use in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant.
