Mallotus porterianus

Mallotus porterianus, commonly known as the African hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and riverine habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
