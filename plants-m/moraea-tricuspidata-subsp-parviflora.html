Moraea tricuspidata subsp. parviflora

Moraea tricuspidata subsp.

parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered moraea, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It typically grows in grassland and rocky habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink or purple. Its flowering period usually occurs in spring, and it is often found in open, sunny environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
