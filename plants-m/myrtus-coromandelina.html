Myrtus coromandelina

Myrtus coromandelina, commonly known as the Indian myrtle or coromandel myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features dense, dark green foliage with a fragrant, resinous scent. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Myrtus coromandelina is often used in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant due to its aromatic properties.
