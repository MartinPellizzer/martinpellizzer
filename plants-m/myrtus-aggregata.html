Myrtus aggregata

Myrtus aggregata, commonly known as the common myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, dark green leaves that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that give way to edible berries. Myrtus aggregata thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
