Matthiola maroccana var. brachycarpa

Matthiola maroccana var. brachycarpa, commonly known as the Moroccan stock, is a flowering plant native to North Africa.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other aromatic and edible plants. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, and it has a compact, bushy growth habit. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its fragrance and visual appeal.
