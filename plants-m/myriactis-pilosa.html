Myriactis pilosa

Myriactis pilosa, commonly known as the hairy myriactis, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or purple in color. Myriactis pilosa thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
