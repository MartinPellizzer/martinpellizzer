Myrsine coriacea subsp. reticulata

Myrsine coriacea subsp.

reticulata, commonly known as the net-veined myrsine, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with a leathery texture and prominent, net-like venation patterns on the leaves.

The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests or secondary growth areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
