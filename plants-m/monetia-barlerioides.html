Monetia barlerioides

Monetia barlerioides, commonly known as the desert hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to red, often with a distinctive dark center. The plant has thick, succulent leaves that help it store water, adapting to its harsh, dry environment. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Monetia barlerioides is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
