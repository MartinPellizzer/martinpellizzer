Mammillaria dolichacantha

Mammillaria dolichacantha, commonly known as the long-spined hedgehog cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a small, globular cactus with a dense covering of white, woolly hairs. The plant features numerous radial spines and a distinctive elongated central spine, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for rock gardens and cactus collections.
