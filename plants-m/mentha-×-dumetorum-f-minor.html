Mentha × dumetorum f. minor

Mentha × dumetorum f. minor, commonly known as catnip, is a hybrid plant belonging to the mint family (Lamiaceae).

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in whorled clusters, typical of the Lamiaceae family. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been naturalized in many regions worldwide.

Mentha × dumetorum f. minor is known for its aromatic properties and has been used historically in traditional medicine and as a stimulant for cats.
