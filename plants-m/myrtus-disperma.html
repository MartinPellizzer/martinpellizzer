Myrtus disperma

Myrtus disperma, commonly known as the rock myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its narrow, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruits are small, dark berries that contain seeds, and the plant is often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Myrtus disperma thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for rocky or coastal environments.
