Maximoviczia japonica

Maximoviczia japonica, commonly known as the Japanese bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to East Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to blue or purple, depending on the variety. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous small seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
