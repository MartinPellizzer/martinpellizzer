Mouriri arborea

Mouriri arborea, commonly known as the yellow bell tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and large, heart-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The tree produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, followed by orange-red fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in humid climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in local medicine.
