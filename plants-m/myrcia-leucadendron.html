Myrcia leucadendron

Myrcia leucadendron, commonly known as the white-leafed myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. It produces small, fleshy berries that are often used in traditional medicine.

Myrcia leucadendron is valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
