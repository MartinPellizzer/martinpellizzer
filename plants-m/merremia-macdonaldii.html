Merremia macdonaldii

Merremia macdonaldii, commonly known as the Macdonald's morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, flat seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges.

Its rapid growth and ornamental flowers make it both a useful garden plant and a potential weed.
