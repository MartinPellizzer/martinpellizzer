Maba domingensis

Maba domingensis, commonly known as the Dominican maba, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean islands, particularly the Dominican Republic.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Its botanical features make it an important species in local ecosystems and cultural practices.
