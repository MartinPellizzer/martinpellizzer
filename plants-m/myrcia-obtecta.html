Myrcia obtecta

Myrcia obtecta, commonly known as the velvet myrtle, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces berries that are typically red or orange when ripe, and they are often used in local cuisines. Myrcia obtecta thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

It has a distinctive aromatic scent, which contributes to its popularity in traditional medicine and ornamental horticulture.
