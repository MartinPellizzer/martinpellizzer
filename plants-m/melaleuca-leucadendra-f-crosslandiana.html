Melaleuca leucadendra f. crosslandiana

Melaleuca leucadendra f. crosslandiana, commonly known as the Crossland's honey myrtle, is a variant of the honey myrtle species native to northern Australia.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces distinctive, urn-shaped fruits that contain numerous seeds, typical of the Myrtaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

This form is notable for its horticultural value and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
