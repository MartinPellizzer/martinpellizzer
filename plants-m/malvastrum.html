Malvastrum

Malvastrum, commonly known as the common mallow, is a genus of flowering plants in the mallow family, Malvaceae.

These plants are characterized by their showy, often pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are typically broad, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy texture. Malvastrum species are native to various regions of the world, including Europe, Asia, and Africa.

They are often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
