Monardella undulata var. crispa

Monardella undulata var. crispa, commonly known as wavyleaf monardella, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its undulating, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of tubular, pink to purple flowers. The plant thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and open woodlands. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.

The species is also a nectar source for various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
