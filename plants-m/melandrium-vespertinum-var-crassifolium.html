Melandrium vespertinum var. crassifolium

Melandrium vespertinum var. crassifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved catchfly, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, fleshy leaves and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks. It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
