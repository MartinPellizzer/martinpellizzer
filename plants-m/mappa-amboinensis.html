Mappa amboinensis

Mappa amboinensis, commonly known as the Amboina maple, is a deciduous tree native to the Maluku Islands of Indonesia.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which typically have five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in catkins, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
