Malaxis lilacina

Malaxis lilacina, commonly known as the lilac-stemded orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the temperate regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically lilac or purple in color, arranged in a loose raceme. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Malaxis lilacina is noted for its delicate appearance and is a popular species among orchid enthusiasts.
