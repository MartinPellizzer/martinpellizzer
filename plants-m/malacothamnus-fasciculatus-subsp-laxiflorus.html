Malacothamnus fasciculatus subsp. laxiflorus

Malacothamnus fasciculatus subsp.

laxiflorus, commonly known as the narrow-flowered bushmint, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale purple.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats. Its flowers are adapted for pollination by bees and other insects, contributing to its ecological significance.

The subspecies is distinguished by its more loosely arranged flowers compared to other members of the species.
