Medinilla angustifolia

Medinilla angustifolia, commonly known as the velvet tangerine or chocolate berry, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically red or orange in color, which are often used in local cuisines. Medinilla angustifolia thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal.

It is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
