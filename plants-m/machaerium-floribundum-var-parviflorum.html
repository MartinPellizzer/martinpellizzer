Machaerium floribundum var. parviflorum

Machaerium floribundum var. parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered machaerium, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its slender, often climbing stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces trifoliate leaves and has an erect or prostrate growth habit, depending on environmental conditions. It thrives in moist, shaded habitats and is often found in forest understories.

Its flowers are typically pale yellow or greenish-white, and it is valued for its ornamental potential in horticulture.
