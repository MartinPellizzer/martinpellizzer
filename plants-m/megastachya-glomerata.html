Megastachya glomerata

Megastachya glomerata, commonly known as the clustered spike grass, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands and along stream banks, thriving in moist, acidic soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in wetland restoration projects.
