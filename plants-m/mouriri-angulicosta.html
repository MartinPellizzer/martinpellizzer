Mouriri angulicosta

Mouriri angulicosta, commonly known as the Brazilian starfruit or sour orange, is a small evergreen tree native to the Amazon basin.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves with a distinctive angular base. The tree produces small, yellow-orange fruits that are typically sour and used in traditional medicine. Its flowers are fragrant and white, often appearing in clusters, while the bark is rough and fissured.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is also used in local horticulture.
