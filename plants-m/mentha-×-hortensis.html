Mentha × hortensis

Mentha × hortensis, commonly known as garden mint, is a hybrid mint species resulting from the cross between Mentha spicata (spearmint) and Mentha viridis (ovalleaf mint).

It is a perennial herb characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, serrated leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming in late summer. Mentha × hortensis is widely cultivated for its fragrant leaves used in culinary and medicinal applications.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.
