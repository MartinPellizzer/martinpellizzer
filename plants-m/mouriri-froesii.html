Mouriri froesii

Mouriri froesii, commonly known as the Brazilian starfruit or yellow starfruit, is a small evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by yellow-orange fruits resembling star-shaped berries. The fruit is edible, with a sweet and tangy flavor, and is often consumed fresh or used in local cuisine.

It plays an important role in the ecosystem by providing food for various wildlife species.
