Marginaria meniscifolia

Marginaria meniscifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed marginaria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs and possesses small, lance-shaped leaves that are often slightly curled at the edges. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale blue to violet flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Marginaria meniscifolia is often found in dry grasslands and rocky areas, where it contributes to the biodiversity of its habitat.
