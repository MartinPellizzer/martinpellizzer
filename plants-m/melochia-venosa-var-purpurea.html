Melochia venosa var. purpurea

Melochia venosa var. purpurea, commonly known as the purple velvet leaf, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically purple or reddish in color, with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by orange to red berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
