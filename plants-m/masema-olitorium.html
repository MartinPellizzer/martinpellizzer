Masema olitorium

Masema olitorium, commonly known as water spinach or kangkong, is a tropical aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, slender, edible leaves and stolons that trail along the water's surface. The plant thrives in shallow, slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in rice paddies and marshy areas. Its leaves are often used in various Asian cuisines, particularly in soups and stir-fries.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is valued for its nutritional content and adaptability to wet conditions.
