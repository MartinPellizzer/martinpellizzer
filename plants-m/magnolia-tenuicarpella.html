Magnolia tenuicarpella

Magnolia tenuicarpella, commonly known as the narrow-fruit magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves and showy, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant's fruit is a distinctive, elongated capsule that splits open to release its seeds. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland habitats.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
