Monnina amarella

Monnina amarella, commonly known as scarlet pimpernel, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a low-growing annual herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features slender, branched stems covered with fine hairs and lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy on the upper surface. Its flowers are small, bright yellow, and borne in clusters, often appearing in late spring to early summer.

The species is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a common weed in disturbed areas.
