Myrmephytum moniliforme

Myrmephytum moniliforme, commonly known as the chain grass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and produces slender, cylindrical spikelets that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or arid regions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-gray hue, and it blooms with small, inconspicuous flowers.

Myrmephytum moniliforme is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in challenging environments.
