Mentha × rotundifolia f. bullata

Mentha × rotundifolia f. bullata, commonly known as the bullate mint, is a hybrid plant that exhibits unique botanical characteristics.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in moist, shaded environments. The leaves are rounded and slightly hairy, distinguishing it from other mint species. It produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months.

This variety is valued for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in horticulture for ornamental purposes.
