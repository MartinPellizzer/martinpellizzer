Mallotus stylaris

Mallotus stylaris, commonly known as the red-flowered mallotus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and striking red flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish fruits that are often covered in a sticky resin. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation.
