Macropiper kandavuense

Macropiper kandavuense, commonly known as the Kandavu pepper, is a unique member of the Piperaceae family native to the island of Kandavu in Fiji.

It is a small, climbing shrub characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are used in traditional Fijian cuisine. Its botanical significance lies in its distinct morphology and limited distribution, making it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
