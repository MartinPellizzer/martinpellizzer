Mariscus cyperoides

Mariscus cyperoides, commonly known as the marsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant has slender, triangular stems and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often growing in dense mats.

Mariscus cyperoides plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
