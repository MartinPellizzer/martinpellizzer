Melica persica f. atropatana

Melica persica f. atropatana, commonly known as Persian millet, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spikelet-based inflorescences. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in temperate to cold climates. Its foliage is often dark green, and it produces pale green to brown flowers during the summer months.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for erosion control in arid landscapes.
