Melothria anatuyana

Melothria anatuyana, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry or Physalis peruviana, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to South America but has been naturalized in various regions around the world. The plant produces small, orange berries that are encased in a papery calyx, resembling a lantern. These berries are edible and have a tangy, sweet flavor, often used in culinary applications.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
