Medicago decaisneana

Medicago decaisneana, commonly known as the Decaisne's clover, is a perennial legume native to southern China.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or arid environments. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, benefiting soil fertility.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
