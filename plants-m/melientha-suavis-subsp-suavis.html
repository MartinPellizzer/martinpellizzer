Melientha suavis subsp. suavis

Melientha suavis subsp.

suavis, commonly known as the sweet melilot, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its slender, aromatic stems and trifoliate leaves with a distinctive sweet scent.

The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in spherical clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in grasslands and disturbed habitats.

The species is valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a forage crop for livestock.
