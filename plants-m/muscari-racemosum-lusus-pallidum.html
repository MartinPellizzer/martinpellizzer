Muscari racemosum lusus pallidum

Muscari racemosum lusus pallidum, commonly known as the pale musk grape, is a bulbous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It produces clusters of pale blue to white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in woodland areas and meadows. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in well-drained soil.
