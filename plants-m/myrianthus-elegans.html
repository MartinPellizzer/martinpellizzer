Myrianthus elegans

Myrianthus elegans, commonly known as the elegant myrianthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, palmately lobed leaves and showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and the fruit is a capsule that releases seeds when mature.

Myrianthus elegans is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
