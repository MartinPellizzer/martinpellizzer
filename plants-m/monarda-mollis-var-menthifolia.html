Monarda mollis var. menthifolia

Monarda mollis var. menthifolia, commonly known as sweet blue mistflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with blue or silver, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pale blue to violet, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

This species is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
