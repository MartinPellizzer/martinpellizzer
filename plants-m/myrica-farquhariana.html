Myrica farquhariana

Myrica farquhariana, commonly known as the Farquhar's wax myrtle, is a shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its small, oval leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark blue berries that are edible. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in swampy or wetland areas.

Myrica farquhariana is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
