Mentha longifolia subsp. erminea

Mentha longifolia subsp.

erminea, commonly known as the woolly mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its distinctive minty scent.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.
