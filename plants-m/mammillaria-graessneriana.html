Mammillaria graessneriana

Mammillaria graessneriana, commonly known as the Graessner's mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and is covered in white to yellowish tubercles. The plant produces small, pink or red flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its delicate structure, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in arid environments.
