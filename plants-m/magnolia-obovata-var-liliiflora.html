Magnolia obovata var. liliiflora

Magnolia obovata var. liliiflora, commonly known as the Lily-flowered Magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white to pale pink flowers that resemble lilies, which bloom in early spring. The leaves are broad, ovate, and have a smooth texture, turning yellow in the fall. The tree has a rounded, spreading form and can reach heights of up to 15 meters.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
