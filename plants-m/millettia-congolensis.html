Millettia congolensis

Millettia congolensis, commonly known as the Congo millettia, is a deciduous tree native to central and western Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind and animals.

It is often found in moist lowland forests and plays a role in soil enrichment due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.
