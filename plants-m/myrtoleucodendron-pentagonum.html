Myrtoleucodendron pentagonum, commonly known as the pentagonal myrtle-leaved tree, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters, with glossy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.
