Manihot parvicocca

Manihot parvicocca, commonly known as the small-seeded cassava, is a tropical plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to Central and South America and is characterized by its woody stems and alternate, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, green flowers that are clustered in catkin-like inflorescences. Its roots, while less commonly cultivated than other cassava varieties, are edible and used in local cuisines.

The species is also valued for its potential in agroforestry systems due to its adaptability to various soil conditions.
