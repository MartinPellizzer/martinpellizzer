Miscanthus kanehirae

Miscanthus kanehirae, commonly known as the Japanese silvergrass, is a perennial grass native to Japan.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and its striking silver-blue foliage that turns coppery in the fall. The plant produces panicles of small, feathery flowers in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its ornamental value and adaptability have made it a favored plant in horticulture across the temperate regions.
