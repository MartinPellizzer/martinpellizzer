Millettia mavangensis

Millettia mavangensis, commonly known as the African sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall, with a broad, spreading canopy. Its bark is typically smooth and grayish, and it produces small, yellowish flowers in spring.

Millettia mavangensis is often found in moist woodland and swampy areas, contributing to local biodiversity.
