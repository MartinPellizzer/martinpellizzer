Morus macroura var. viridis

Morus macroura var. viridis, commonly known as the green mulberry, is a variant of the mulberry tree native to China.

It is characterized by its distinctive green leaves, which are usually larger and more elongated compared to other mulberry species. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a food source for silkworms.

The leaves are also used in traditional Chinese medicine for their purported health benefits.
