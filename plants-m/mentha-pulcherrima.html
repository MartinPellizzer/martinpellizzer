Mentha pulcherrima

Mentha pulcherrima, commonly known as the purple mint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with purple or violet hues. The plant produces small, pale purple flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Mentha pulcherrima is valued for its aromatic leaves, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
