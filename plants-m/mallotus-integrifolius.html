Mallotus integrifolius

Mallotus integrifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaved mallot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, smooth, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers followed by woody, spiny capsules containing seeds. It is often found in lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

The species is also used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
