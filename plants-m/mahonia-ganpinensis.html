Mahonia ganpinensis

Mahonia ganpinensis, commonly known as the Chinese mahonia, is a shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Berberidaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and spiny stems. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by blue-black berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value comes from its foliage and seasonal blooms, making it a popular choice in horticulture.
