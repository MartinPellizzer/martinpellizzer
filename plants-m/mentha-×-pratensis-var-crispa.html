Mentha × pratensis var. crispa

Mentha × pratensis var. crispa, commonly known as the wrinkled mint, is a hybrid perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive crinkled, serrated leaves that give it a unique, ornamental appearance. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This variety is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves and is used in both culinary and medicinal applications.
