Myrciaria myrtifolia

Myrciaria myrtifolia, commonly known as Brazilian cherry or araçá-do-brejo, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, clustered white flowers. The plant produces edible red berries that are rich in vitamin C and are often consumed fresh or used in jams and juices.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
