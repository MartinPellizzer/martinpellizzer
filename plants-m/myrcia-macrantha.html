Myrcia macrantha

Myrcia macrantha, commonly known as the large-flowered myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and showy, pinkish-red flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences and are pollinated primarily by birds and insects.

Myrcia macrantha is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
