Malesherbia paniculata

Malesherbia paniculata, commonly known as the silverleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its silver-green, velvety leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its distinctive foliage and long-lasting blooms.
