Mariscus rhodesicus

Mariscus rhodesicus, commonly known as the Rhodes mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and sunny environments. The plant features hairy stems and palmate leaves with a distinctive lobed shape. Its showy, violet-blue flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

Mariscus rhodesicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
