Mahonia ilicina

Mahonia ilicina, commonly known as the creeping mahonia, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features compound leaves with spiny tips, which are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by blue-black berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or woodland settings.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice for landscaping in temperate climates.
