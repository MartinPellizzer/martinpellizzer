Mabea brasiliensis var. intermedia

Mabea brasiliensis var. intermedia, commonly known as the Brazilian wild bean, is a climbing shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers, followed by elongated, bean-like pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in secondary forests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential nutritional benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and research settings.
