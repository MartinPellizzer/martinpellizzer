Mimosa mucronulata

Mimosa mucronulata, commonly known as the slender mimosa, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves that fold shut when touched. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Mimosa mucronulata is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
