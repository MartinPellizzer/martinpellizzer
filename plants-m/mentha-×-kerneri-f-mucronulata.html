Mentha × kerneri f. mucronulata

Mentha × kerneri f. mucronulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mint, is a hybrid plant in the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and strong, aromatic scent. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pale blue or purple.

This variety is valued for its use in herbal teas and as a culinary herb due to its refreshing flavor.
