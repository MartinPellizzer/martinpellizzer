Mallotus japonicus var. floccosus

Mallotus japonicus var. floccosus, commonly known as the Japanese woolly mallot, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub native to southern Japan. The plant is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape and covered with a fine, white pubescence.

It produces small yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, spiny fruits.
