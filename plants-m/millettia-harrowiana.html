Millettia harrowiana

Millettia harrowiana, commonly known as the Harrow's millettia, is a tropical climbing plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Native to Southeast Asia, it is often found in moist, shaded forest environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
