Morinda candollei var. subvillosa

Morinda candollei var. subvillosa, commonly known as the silky morinda, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features smooth, glabrous stems and leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or white, followed by orange or red berries. Its leaves are often covered in a fine, silky hair, giving it its common name.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
