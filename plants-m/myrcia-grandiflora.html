Myrcia grandiflora

Myrcia grandiflora, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of fragrant, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, reddish-black berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Myrcia grandiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions, outcompeting native vegetation.
