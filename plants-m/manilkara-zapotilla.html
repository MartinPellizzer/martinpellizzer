Manilkara zapotilla

Manilkara zapotilla, commonly known as the zapote or zapote blanco, is a tropical tree native to Central America and parts of the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into hard, woody fruits. The fruit has a thick, leathery exterior and contains a single large seed, making it distinct from other species in the genus. The tree is valued for its durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.

Additionally, its fruit is edible and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
