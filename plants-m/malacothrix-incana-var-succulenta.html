Malacothrix incana var. succulenta

Malacothrix incana var. succulenta, commonly known as the woolly everlasting, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, pale blue or white flowers that bloom in spring. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Its compact growth habit makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
