Mimulus parviflorus var. externus

Mimulus parviflorus var. externus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, bright orange to yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as streambanks and meadows. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

It is a member of the Phrymaceae family, which includes other species of monkeyflowers known for their showy blooms and ecological importance to pollinators.
