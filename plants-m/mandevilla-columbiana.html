Mandevilla columbiana

Mandevilla columbiana, commonly known as the Colombian mandevilla, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a vigorous climber with thick, waxy stems that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces large, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming from spring to fall. Its leaves are heart-shaped and glossy, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant flowers.

Mandevilla columbiana thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens and landscapes.
