Malvaviscus ulei

Malvaviscus ulei, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton plants. The plant is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and clusters of yellow-orange flowers. Its fruit, known as a capsule, contains numerous small, edible seeds.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
