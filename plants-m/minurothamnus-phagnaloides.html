Minurothamnus phagnaloides

Minurothamnus phagnaloides, commonly known as the Phagnaloides thornbush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth and small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a whitish powdery coating. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in landscaping for erosion control in dry regions.
