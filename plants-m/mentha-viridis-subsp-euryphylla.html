Mentha viridis subsp. euryphylla

Mentha viridis subsp.

euryphylla, commonly known as narrow-leaf mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in whorled clusters along the upper stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight. Mentha viridis subsp.

euryphylla is often used in traditional herbal medicine and as a culinary herb.
