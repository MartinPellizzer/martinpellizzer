Moniera herniarioides

Moniera herniarioides, commonly known as the kidney vetch, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a leguminous species belonging to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish or greenish pods that contain several seeds. It typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

Moniera herniarioides plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility through its symbiotic relationship with rhizobia bacteria.
