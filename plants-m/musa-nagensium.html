Musa nagensium

Musa nagensium, commonly known as the Java banana, is a species of banana plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that grows in tropical climates, characterized by its large, broad leaves and clusters of yellow-orange berries. The plant produces edible fruits that are typically consumed fresh or used in local cuisines. Botanically, it belongs to the genus Musa within the family Musaceae, and it is closely related to other banana species.

Its rapid growth and high yield make it an important crop in its native regions.
