Momordica adoensis

Momordica adoensis, commonly known as the African bitter melon, is a vine species native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like cucumbers and pumpkins. The plant produces distinctive, warty, green fruits that turn yellow when mature. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a rough texture.

The fruit is used in traditional medicine and as a vegetable in some regions due to its bitter taste and nutritional value.
