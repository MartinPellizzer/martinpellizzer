Marattia kaulfussii

Marattia kaulfussii, commonly known as the Kaulfuss's fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Marattiaceae and is characterized by its large, compound fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant has a stout, unbranched trunk that is covered with remnants of old leaf bases, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are typically found on the underside of the fronds.

Marattia kaulfussii thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.
