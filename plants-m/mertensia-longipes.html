Mertensia longipes

Mertensia longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed bluebell, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and covered in a fine, downy texture.

Mertensia longipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
