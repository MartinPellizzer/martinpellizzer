Mollia polycarpoides

Mollia polycarpoides, commonly known as the rock cress, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and produces small, white or yellowish flowers arranged in a raceme. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in arid or semi-arid regions. It is well adapted to harsh environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and a slow growth rate.

Mollia polycarpoides is often found in mountainous areas and is considered a hardy, low-maintenance species for rocky gardens.
