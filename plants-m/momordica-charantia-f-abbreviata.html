Momordica charantia f. abbreviata

Momordica charantia f. abbreviata, commonly known as the short-fruit bitter gourd, is a variant of the bitter gourd plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its compact, shortened fruits compared to the typical bitter gourd, which are usually elongated and pointed. The plant produces yellow flowers and has a climbing habit, typical of the Cucurbitaceae family. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a hairy surface, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

This variety is cultivated for its edible fruits, which have a bitter taste and are used in various cuisines.
