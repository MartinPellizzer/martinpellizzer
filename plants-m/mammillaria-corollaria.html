Mammillaria corollaria

Mammillaria corollaria, commonly known as the coral milkplant, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and a dense covering of white, woolly spines. The plant produces small, pink to red flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
