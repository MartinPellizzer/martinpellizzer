Magonia lundii

Magonia lundii, commonly known as the golden star vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling stars, hence its common name. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is known for its rapid growth habit. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Magonia lundii is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
