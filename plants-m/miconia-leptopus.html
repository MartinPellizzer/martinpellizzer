Miconia leptopus

Miconia leptopus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf miconia, is a species of flowering plant in the Melastomataceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, it can become invasive in certain areas.
