Melaleuca beaufortioides var. beaufortioides

Melaleuca beaufortioides var. beaufortioides, commonly known as the Beaufort Island tea tree, is a small to medium-sized shrub native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces woody capsules that contain numerous small, brown seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or sandy habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses by Indigenous communities.
