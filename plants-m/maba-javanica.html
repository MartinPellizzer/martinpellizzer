Maba javanica

Maba javanica, commonly known as the Javan maba, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and large, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits that contain seeds. It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its timber and medicinal properties.

The species is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
