Mentha longifolia f. subnemorosa

Mentha longifolia f. subnemorosa, commonly known as the forest mint, is a subspecies of the wood mint plant.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly aromatic leaves and small, pale blue to purple flowers. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a serrated edge.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in herbal remedies.
