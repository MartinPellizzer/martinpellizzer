Maxillaria muscoides

Maxillaria muscoides, commonly known as the musk orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure and produces fragrant, showy flowers that are often described as having a musky or sweet scent. The plant typically blooms in the summer, with flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. Its leaves are usually dark green and arranged in a rosette pattern, and it thrives in humid, well-drained environments.

Maxillaria muscoides is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and unique fragrance.
