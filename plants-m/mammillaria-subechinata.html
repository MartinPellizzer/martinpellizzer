Mammillaria subechinata

Mammillaria subechinata, commonly known as the spiny hedgehog cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a small, globular cactus with a distinctive appearance, featuring a dense covering of spines and areoles. The plant has a grayish-green body with short, cylindrical stems and produces yellow flowers in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its compact size and unique appearance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cultivation.
