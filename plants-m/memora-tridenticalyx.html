Memora tridenticalyx

Memora tridenticalyx, commonly known as the three-toothed memora, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three lance-shaped lobes, and its clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its botanical features make it a unique subject for ecological and horticultural study.

However, it is relatively rare and not widely cultivated outside its natural habitat.
