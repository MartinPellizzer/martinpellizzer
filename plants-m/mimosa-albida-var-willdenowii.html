Mimosa albida var. willdenowii

Mimosa albida var. willdenowii, commonly known as the white mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive fern-like leaves and showy, fluffy white or pink flowers. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. It also has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, which enhances soil fertility.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
