Mahernia verticillata

Mahernia verticillata, commonly known as the clustered mahernia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and arranged in whorls around the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Mahernia verticillata is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
