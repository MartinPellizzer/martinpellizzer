Mucuna papuana

Mucuna papuana, commonly known as the Papuan velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It features trifoliate leaves and produces distinctive, hairy pods containing several elliptical seeds. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, warm climates. It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges.

Its flowers are typically pale yellow or greenish, and it is valued for its nitrogen-fixing properties in agricultural settings.
