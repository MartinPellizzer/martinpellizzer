Miconia fragrantissima

Miconia fragrantissima, commonly known as the fragrant miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, broad leaves and showy, often pink or red flowers. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are an important food source for various birds and mammals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
