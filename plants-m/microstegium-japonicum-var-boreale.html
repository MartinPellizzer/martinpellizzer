Microstegium japonicum var. boreale

Microstegium japonicum var. boreale, commonly known as the northern Japanese woodland grass, is a perennial herb native to northern Japan and parts of eastern Russia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky slopes. Its leaves are often hairy and have a distinct blue-green color, while its flowers are inconspicuous and wind-pollinated.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates in its native ecosystems.
