Masdevallia martineae

Masdevallia martineae, commonly known as the Martineae orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a compact growth habit and produces intricate, colorful flowers that are typically pink or purple in hue. The plant features a short, stocky rhizome and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. Its flowers are borne on short, erect pedicels and are characterized by their three sepals and three petals, with the lip being distinctly marked.

Masdevallia martineae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance.
