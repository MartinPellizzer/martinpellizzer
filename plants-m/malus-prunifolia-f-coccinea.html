Malus prunifolia f. coccinea

Malus prunifolia f. coccinea, commonly known as the red-leafed apple tree, is a cultivar of the apple tree native to China.

It is characterized by its striking red foliage, which turns vibrant shades of crimson in the fall. The plant produces small, edible fruits similar to regular apples, though they are often less commonly cultivated for consumption. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This ornamental variety is valued for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and landscapes.
