Magnolia yulan var. soulangeana

Magnolia yulan var. soulangeana, commonly known as the Japanese magnolia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves appear. The flowers are typically white or pink and have a fragrant, sweet scent. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks.
