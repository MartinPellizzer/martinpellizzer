Merremia cespitosa

Merremia cespitosa, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a herbaceous vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple. The plant is characterized by its trailing growth habit and ability to form dense mats. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas.

Botanically, it is part of the Convolvulaceae family, known for its climbing and twining plants.
