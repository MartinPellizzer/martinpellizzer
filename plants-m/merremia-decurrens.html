Merremia decurrens

Merremia decurrens, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a flowering vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to climb or trail along the ground. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Merremia decurrens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
