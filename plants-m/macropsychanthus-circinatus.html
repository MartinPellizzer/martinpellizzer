Macropsychanthus circinatus

Macropsychanthus circinatus, commonly known as the spiral-leaf cactus, is a unique member of the Cactaceae family native to South America.

It is characterized by its spiral arrangement of leaves, which gives it a distinctive, almost spiral-like appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a vibrant touch to its otherwise spiny structure. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring minimal water and well-draining soil.

Due to its unusual morphology and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
