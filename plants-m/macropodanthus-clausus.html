Macropodanthus clausus

Macropodanthus clausus, commonly known as the closed-footed morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy blue or violet flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a waxy surface and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is a climbing vine that can reach lengths of up to 10 meters.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
