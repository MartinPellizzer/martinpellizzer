Mabea taquari var. genuina

Mabea taquari var. genuina, commonly known as the Taquari mabea, is a shrub native to the Cerrado biome of Brazil.

It is characterized by its dense, woody stems and small, elliptical leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of white to pale yellow flowers during the dry season, followed by small, round fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to periods of drought.

Due to its ecological importance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in local gardens.
