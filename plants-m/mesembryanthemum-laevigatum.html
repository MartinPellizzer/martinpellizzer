Mesembryanthemum laevigatum

Mesembryanthemum laevigatum, commonly known as the smoothleaf iceplant, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in summer. Its root system is shallow, enabling it to absorb moisture from the surface layer of the soil.

Mesembryanthemum laevigatum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its drought tolerance and aesthetic appeal.
