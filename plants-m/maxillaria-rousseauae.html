Maxillaria rousseauae

Maxillaria rousseauae, commonly known as the Rousseau's orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, fragrant flowers. The plant produces showy, often violet or purple-colored blooms with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals. It typically thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Maxillaria rousseauae is appreciated for its ornamental value and is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
