Melothria hispidula

Melothria hispidula, commonly known as the hairy morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and trumpet-shaped, pale yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines.
