Manihot laciniosa var. lanata

Manihot laciniosa var. lanata, commonly known as the woolly arrowleaf, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with a dense, velvety pubescence.

The leaves are typically green with a waxy surface, and the plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. It is native to Central and South America, thriving in humid, shaded environments. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique foliage.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full shade for optimal growth.
