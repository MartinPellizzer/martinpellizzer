Morinda ramosa

Morinda ramosa, commonly known as the hairy morinda, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its branched stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces bright red or orange flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hard seeds.

Morinda ramosa is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
