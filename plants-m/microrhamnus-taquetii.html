Microrhamnus taquetii

Microrhamnus taquetii, commonly known as the Taquet's buckthorn, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in catkins, followed by small, dark berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
