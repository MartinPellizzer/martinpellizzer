Morus rubra f. atropurpurea

Morus rubra f. atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple-leaf mulberry, is a cultivar of the white mulberry tree.

It is characterized by its striking purple-bronze foliage, which becomes more intense in cooler weather. The leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and have a slightly serrated margin. It produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically pink or red when ripe.

This ornamental variety is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and landscapes.
