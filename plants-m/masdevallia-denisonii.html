Masdevallia denisonii

Masdevallia denisonii, commonly known as the Denison's masdevallia, is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its intricate, often pendulous inflorescences and a variety of colorful, showy flowers that range from pink to violet. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree trunks or branches in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and it prefers cool, humid environments with indirect light.

Masdevallia denisonii is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements in cultivation.
