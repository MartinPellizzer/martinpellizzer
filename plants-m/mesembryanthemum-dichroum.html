Mesembryanthemum dichroum

Mesembryanthemum dichroum, commonly known as the diamond flower, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, Mesembryanthemum dichroum is widely cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions.
