Maesa calcarea

Maesa calcarea, commonly known as limestone maesa, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its small, clustered white flowers and succulent, fleshy leaves adapted to arid environments. The plant thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils, which contribute to its common name. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Maesa calcarea is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
