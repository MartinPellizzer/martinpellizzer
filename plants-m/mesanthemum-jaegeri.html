Mesanthemum jaegeri

Mesanthemum jaegeri, commonly known as the Cape marigold, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils. It produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping in arid regions.
