Mayzea vestita

Mayzea vestita, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along riverbanks.

Mayzea vestita is also known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
