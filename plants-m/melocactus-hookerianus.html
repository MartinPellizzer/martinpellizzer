Melocactus hookerianus

Melocactus hookerianus, commonly known as the Hooker's melon cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive, globular shape and spiny, ribbed structure. The plant features a thick, fleshy stem that stores water, allowing it to survive in arid environments. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, emerging from the areoles, and it produces edible, sweet fruits.

This cactus is well adapted to rocky soils and full sun exposure.
