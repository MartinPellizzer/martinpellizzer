Masdevallia punctata

Masdevallia punctata, commonly known as the spotted masdevallia, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and intricate, often colorful blooms. The plant features strap-like leaves and produces small, delicate flowers with intricate patterns, often in shades of white, pink, and purple. It thrives in cool, humid environments and is typically grown as a terrestrial or epiphytic orchid.

Due to its ornamental value, it is popular among orchid enthusiasts and horticulturists.
