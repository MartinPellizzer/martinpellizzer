Manilkara bidentata

Manilkara bidentata, commonly known as the Brazil nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its large, oblong leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The tree's most notable feature is its woody capsule fruit, which contains multiple seeds known as Brazil nuts. These nuts are rich in oils and nutrients, making the tree economically significant.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Sapotaceae and is valued for both its ecological and commercial importance.
