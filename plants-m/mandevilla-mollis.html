Mandevilla mollis

Mandevilla mollis, commonly known as the sweet pitcher plant, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant produces showy, fragrant blooms throughout the growing season, typically from spring to fall. Its leaves are opposite, heart-shaped, and often have a slightly waxy texture.

Mandevilla mollis thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens or as a houseplant.
