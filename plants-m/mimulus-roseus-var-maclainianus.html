Mimulus roseus var. maclainianus

Mimulus roseus var. maclainianus, commonly known as the Maclain's monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae and is characterized by its vibrant orange to red flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. It has simple, alternate leaves that are usually lance-shaped and hairy.

The flowers are showy and attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
