Mesembryanthemum brachiatum

Mesembryanthemum brachiatum, commonly known as the fleshy-leafed ice plant, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange, blooming in summer. It is a member of the Aizoaceae family, known for its adaptations to dry climates.

Mesembryanthemum brachiatum is often used in rock gardens and as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
