Mahonia fortunei

Mahonia fortunei, commonly known as the golden barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that appear in late winter or early spring, providing an early source of nectar for pollinators. The plant has holly-like leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath, with spiny edges. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a hardy and versatile garden plant.

Mahonia fortunei is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate a variety of growing conditions.
