Mozartia manacalensis

Mozartia manacalensis, commonly known as the Manacalensis grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in coastal and rocky habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions. Its flowering spikes produce small, inconspicuous flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is considered a valuable component of the island's unique flora.
