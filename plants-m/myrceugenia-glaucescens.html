Myrceugenia glaucescens

Myrceugenia glaucescens, commonly known as the blue-green myrtle, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the coastal regions of southern Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. It produces small, round berries that are typically blue or black when mature.

Myrceugenia glaucescens is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to coastal environments.
