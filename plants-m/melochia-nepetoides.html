Melochia nepetoides

Melochia nepetoides, commonly known as the velvetleaf or swampmallow, is a tropical shrub belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its fruits are capsules that split open to release tiny seeds.

Melochia nepetoides is often found in wetland areas and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
