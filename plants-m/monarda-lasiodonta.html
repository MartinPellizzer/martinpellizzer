Monarda lasiodonta

Monarda lasiodonta, commonly known as the narrowleaf horsemint, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from violet to pink, typically blooming in mid to late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Monarda lasiodonta is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
