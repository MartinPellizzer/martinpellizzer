Myriophyllum implicatum

Myriophyllum implicatum, commonly known as watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to North America.

It has feathery, branching leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, green flowers that emerge from the water surface in late summer. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

Myriophyllum implicatum plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
