Melaleuca blaeriifolia

Melaleuca blaeriifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved melaleuca, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to coastal regions of northern Australia, where it thrives in sandy soils and saline environments. The plant is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in winter. It is often found in heathlands and coastal dunes, contributing to the ecological diversity of these habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is also used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soils.
