Myrospermum peruiferum

Myrospermum peruiferum, commonly known as the Peruvian myrtle, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. Its fruits are small, capsule-like structures that release tiny seeds when mature.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.
