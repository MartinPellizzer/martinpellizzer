Melanthera pungens var. albinervia

Melanthera pungens var. albinervia, commonly known as the white-veined melanthera, is a striking herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, erect stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with distinctive white veins contrasting against the dark green foliage. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white and pale pink, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage and floral structure make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.
