Melocactus latispinus

Melocactus latispinus, commonly known as the broad-spined melon cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem and the presence of large, widely spaced spines that give it its common name. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warm season, which are followed by small, edible fruits. Its thick, fleshy stem stores water, allowing it to survive in dry environments.

Melocactus latispinus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
