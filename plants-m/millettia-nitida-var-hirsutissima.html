Millettia nitida var. hirsutissima

Millettia nitida var. hirsutissima, commonly known as the hairy millettia, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by elongated, bean-like pods.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
