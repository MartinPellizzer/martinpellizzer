Mammillaria sanguinea

Mammillaria sanguinea, commonly known as the blood mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and bright red or pink flowers that appear in late spring. The plant has a thick, ribbed stem covered in small, white to yellowish tubercles. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.
