Mariscus pseudoflavus

Mariscus pseudoflavus, commonly known as the yellow sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, ribbed stems and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as sandy soils and rocky habitats. Its leaves are often fleshy and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

Mariscus pseudoflavus is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a resilient species in its native range.
