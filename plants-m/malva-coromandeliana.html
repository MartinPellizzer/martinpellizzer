Malva coromandeliana

Malva coromandeliana, commonly known as the Coromandel mallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, often with a distinctive yellow or green throat. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and is often found growing in moist, shady environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cotton and hibiscus.

Malva coromandeliana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
