Mangifera pedicellata

Mangifera pedicellata, commonly known as the wild mango or hill mango, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits that are typically green when mature, though they may change color depending on the variety. Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential agricultural use.

The plant is often found in forested areas and is adapted to thrive in humid, tropical climates.
