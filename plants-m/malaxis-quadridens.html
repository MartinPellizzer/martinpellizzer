Malaxis quadridens

Malaxis quadridens, commonly known as the four-toothed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a short, stocky rhizome and produces a single, erect stem with several leaves. Its flowers are unique, featuring four prominent teeth on the labellum, which is a specialized structure in orchids.

This species is often found in moist, shaded habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
