Mauritia huebneri

Mauritia huebneri, commonly known as the Brazilian palm, is a tall, slender palm native to the Atlantic coastal forests of Brazil.

It features a slender trunk with a smooth, grayish-brown bark and large, feather-like leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by orange-red fruits containing a single seed. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils near rivers and coastal areas.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is protected in several conservation programs.
