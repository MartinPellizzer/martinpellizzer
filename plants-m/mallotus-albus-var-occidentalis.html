Mallotus albus var. occidentalis

Mallotus albus var. occidentalis, commonly known as the white mangosteen, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are elliptical and have a smooth, glossy surface. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, white, fleshy fruits. Its botanical structure includes a short, stout stem and a fibrous root system adapted to moist, tropical soils.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit, which is prized for its sweet, aromatic flavor.
