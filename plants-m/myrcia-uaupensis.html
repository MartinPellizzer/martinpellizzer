Myrcia uaupensis

Myrcia uaupensis, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that emit a pleasant fragrance. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns from green to red as it matures.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local medicine.
