Misodendrum lineare

Misodendrum lineare, commonly known as the linear leaf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees, primarily in tropical and subtropical regions.

It has small, alternate, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds, which are often dispersed by wind.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can significantly affect the health and growth of its host trees.
