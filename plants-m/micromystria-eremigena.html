Micromystria eremigena

Micromystria eremigena, commonly known as the desert mistplant, is a unique succulent native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It features a rosette of thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers during the rainy season, which are borne on slender stems. Its leaves are often covered in a fine, powdery coating that helps reduce water loss.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
