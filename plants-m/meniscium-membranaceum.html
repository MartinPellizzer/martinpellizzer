Meniscium membranaceum

Meniscium membranaceum, commonly known as the membranous meniscium, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or limestone soils. The plant features slender stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences and are usually pale blue or violet in color.

Meniscium membranaceum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
