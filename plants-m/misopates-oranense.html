Misopates oranense

Misopates oranense, commonly known as the orange lily, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and clusters of fragrant, orange-red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has strap-shaped, narrow leaves that are typically green and slightly hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Misopates oranense is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
