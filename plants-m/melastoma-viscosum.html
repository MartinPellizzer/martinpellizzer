Melastoma viscosum

Melastoma viscosum, commonly known as the sticky melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds covered in a sticky substance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Melastoma viscosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
