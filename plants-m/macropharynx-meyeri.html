Macropharynx meyeri

Macropharynx meyeri, commonly known as the Meyer's giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to two meters in diameter, providing a floating platform for various aquatic life. The plant's flowers are showy, with white petals and a prominent yellow center, blooming during the day. Its roots anchor it in shallow water, while its leaves float on the surface, facilitating photosynthesis.

The plant plays an important role in its ecosystem by offering shelter and food for waterfowl and insects.
