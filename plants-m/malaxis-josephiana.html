Malaxis josephiana

Malaxis josephiana, commonly known as the Joseph's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, greenish-white flowers with a distinctive hood-like structure. The plant typically grows in cool, humid environments and thrives in shaded forest floors. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the soil.

Malaxis josephiana is appreciated for its unique floral morphology and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
