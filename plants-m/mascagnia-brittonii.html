Mascagnia brittonii

Mascagnia brittonii, commonly known as the desert nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces showy, violet to purple flowers that bloom at night. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, and its fruit is a capsule containing several seeds. It thrives in arid environments and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Mascagnia brittonii is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
