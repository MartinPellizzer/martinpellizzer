Mentha pulegium var. hirsuta

Mentha pulegium var. hirsuta, commonly known as hairy pulex mint, is a variant of the greater mint species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and a strong, pungent aroma. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported digestive and antispasmodic properties.
