Melilotus sulcatus var. angustifolius

Melilotus sulcatus var. angustifolius, commonly known as narrow-leaved sweet clover, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed areas.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties and as a forage crop for livestock.
