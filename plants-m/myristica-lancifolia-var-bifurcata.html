Myristica lancifolia var. bifurcata

Myristica lancifolia var. bifurcata, commonly known as the bifurcate nutmeg, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its elliptic to lanceolate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark red, fleshy fruits containing aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its essential oils and seed extracts are valued for their medicinal properties and aromatic qualities.

The varietal distinction, bifurcata, refers to the branching pattern of its fruiting structures.
