Meconopsis grandis subsp. orientalis

Meconopsis grandis subsp.

orientalis, commonly known as the Eastern Himalayan poppy, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in alpine gardens.
