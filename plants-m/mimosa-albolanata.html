Mimosa albolanata

Mimosa albolanata, commonly known as the white mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. Its seed pods split open to release seeds, a feature common in the legume family.

Mimosa albolanata is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and floral display.
