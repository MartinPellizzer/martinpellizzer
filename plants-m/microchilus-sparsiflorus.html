Microchilus sparsiflorus

Microchilus sparsiflorus, commonly known as the sparseflower microchilus, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, delicate leaves and clusters of tiny, pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its botanical features include a compact growth habit and a preference for well-drained soils.

Due to its limited distribution and specialized habitat, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
