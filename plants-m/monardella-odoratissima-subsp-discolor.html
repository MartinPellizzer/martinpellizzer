Monardella odoratissima subsp. discolor

Monardella odoratissima subsp.

discolor, commonly known as the pink monardella, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy, tubular pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a distinctive white or silver underside, contributing to its ornamental value.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and medicinal plants.
