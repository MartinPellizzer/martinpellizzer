Montia aquatica var. lamprosperma

Montia aquatica var. lamprosperma, commonly known as the shining water bladderwort, is a rare aquatic plant native to specific regions of North America.

It is characterized by its small, bladder-like structures that help it float and trap oxygen in water. The plant typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and streams. Its leaves are thin and ribbon-like, adapted for efficient gas exchange in aquatic conditions.

The flowers are small and white, often clustered in dense inflorescences, and bloom during the summer months.
