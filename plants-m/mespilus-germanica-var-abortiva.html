Mespilus germanica var. abortiva

Mespilus germanica var. abortiva, commonly known as the wild medlar, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, oval-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring. The fruit, which is typically achenes, is often partially abortive, giving the variety its name. The plant is adapted to dry, rocky soils and thrives in Mediterranean climates.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
