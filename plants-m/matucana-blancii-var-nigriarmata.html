Matucana blancii var. nigriarmata

Matucana blancii var. nigriarmata, commonly known as the black-armed matucana, is a rare and striking member of the Araceae family.

It is native to the cloud forests of Colombia and is characterized by its dark, velvety leaves with contrasting white veins. The plant produces striking white spadices surrounded by a deep purple spathe, creating a dramatic visual effect. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires high humidity and well-draining soil.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often cultivated by enthusiasts in controlled greenhouse conditions.
