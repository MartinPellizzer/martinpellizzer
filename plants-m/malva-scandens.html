Malva scandens

Malva scandens, commonly known as the creeping mallows, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a fuzzy texture and produces showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has a sprawling growth habit, often forming dense mats along the ground or climbing structures. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Malva scandens is also known for its medicinal properties, historically used to treat skin conditions and as a diuretic.
