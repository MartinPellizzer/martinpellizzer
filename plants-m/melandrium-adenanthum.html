Melandrium adenanthum

Melandrium adenanthum, commonly known as the sticky catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Melandrium adenanthum is an important component of its native ecosystem, supporting various pollinators and contributing to biodiversity.
