Myrtus salutaris

Myrtus salutaris, commonly known as sweet myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, narrow leaves and small white or pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a small, dark blue berry that contains several seeds.

Myrtus salutaris is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine.
