Mentha × verticillata var. jahniana

Mentha × verticillata var. jahniana, commonly known as the Jahniana water mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its square, erect stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in whorls along the stem, typically in shades of purple or blue. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as stream banks and wet meadows.

Its aromatic leaves and flowers make it a valuable species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
