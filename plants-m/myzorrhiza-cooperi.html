Myzorrhiza cooperi

Myzorrhiza cooperi, commonly known as the Cooper's mycorrhiza, is a unique plant species that forms a symbiotic relationship with fungi.

It is characterized by its specialized root structures that house fungal hyphae, enhancing nutrient absorption from the soil. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting to nutrient-poor soils through this mutualistic association. Its morphology includes small, succulent leaves and a fibrous root system that supports the fungal network.

This species plays a crucial role in soil stabilization and ecosystem health in its native habitat.
