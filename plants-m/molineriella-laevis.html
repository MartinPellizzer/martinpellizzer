Molineriella laevis

Molineriella laevis, commonly known as the smooth molineriella, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescences.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant has smooth, lance-shaped leaves and small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It is often found in open woodlands and along rocky slopes, preferring well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
