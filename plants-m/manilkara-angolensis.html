Manilkara angolensis

Manilkara angolensis, commonly known as the African manilkara, is a tropical tree native to central and western Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves and yellowish, fleshy fruits. The plant is known for its latex, which is used in traditional medicine and as a natural rubber substitute. Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.

The tree is also valued for its durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.
