Melicope durifolia

Melicope durifolia, commonly known as the rough-leaved melicope, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant has rough, often hairy foliage, which gives it its common name, and its fruits are small, berry-like drupes.

Melicope durifolia is typically found in humid, lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional uses in some cultures.
