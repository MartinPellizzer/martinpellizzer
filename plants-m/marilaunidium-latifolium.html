Marilaunidium latifolium

Marilaunidium latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf marilaunidium, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the southern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and naturalized areas.
