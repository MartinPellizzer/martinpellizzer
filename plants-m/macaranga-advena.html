Macaranga advena

Macaranga advena, commonly known as the "waterleaf" or "arrowleaf," is a fast-growing shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered with a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in moist, shady environments, often found in tropical rainforests. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically yellow or green, while the fruit is a three-winged samara that aids in wind dispersal.

Macaranga advena plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
