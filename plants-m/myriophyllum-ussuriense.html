Myriophyllum ussuriense

Myriophyllum ussuriense, commonly known as water milfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Asia.

It has slender, branching stems that are often covered in small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces tiny, floating flowers that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in still or slow-moving waters and is often found in ponds, lakes, and wetlands.

Myriophyllum ussuriense plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
