Mimosa guatemalensis

Mimosa guatemalensis, commonly known as the Guatemalan sensitive plant, is a shrub native to Central America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves that fold shut when touched. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system has nitrogen-fixing capabilities, contributing to soil fertility.

Mimosa guatemalensis is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
