Madia capitata var. anomala

Madia capitata var. anomala, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and heat. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant is often found in rocky soils and is considered a hardy, low-maintenance species for xeriscaping.
