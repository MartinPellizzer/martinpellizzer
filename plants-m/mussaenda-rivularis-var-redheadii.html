Mussaenda rivularis var. redheadii

Mussaenda rivularis var. redheadii, commonly known as Redhead's Mussaenda, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red bracts that resemble petals, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, white, tubular flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found near streams or in forest undergrowth.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and unique floral display.
