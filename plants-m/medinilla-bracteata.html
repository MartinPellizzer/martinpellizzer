Medinilla bracteata

Medinilla bracteata, commonly known as the velvet tangerine or velvet leaf, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are edible and have a tangy, citrus-like flavor. Medinilla bracteata thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its unique appearance and pleasant aroma make it a popular choice for tropical gardens and indoor plant collections.
