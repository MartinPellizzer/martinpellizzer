Marsypianthes hyptoides var. elatior

Marsypianthes hyptoides var. elatior, commonly known as the greater marsh mallow, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, herbaceous stems and large, showy pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often forming dense stands in marshy areas. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with soft, hairy surfaces that help retain moisture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators in wetland ecosystems.
