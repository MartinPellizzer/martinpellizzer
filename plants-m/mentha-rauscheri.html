Mentha rauscheri

Mentha rauscheri, commonly known as Rausch's mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem. Mentha rauscheri is valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
