Matisia huallagensis

Matisia huallagensis, commonly known as the Huallangena tree, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly in Ecuador and Peru.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, gray bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by spherical, fleshy fruits containing numerous seeds.

It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland and montane forests.
