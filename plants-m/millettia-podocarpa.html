Millettia podocarpa

Millettia podocarpa, commonly known as the African rosewood, is a tropical tree native to Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The tree produces pods that contain flat, winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It is valued for its hard, dense wood, which is used in furniture and musical instruments.

The plant also plays an important role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various wildlife species.
