Macroglena stricta

Macroglena stricta, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its erect, slender stem and lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to reddish-purple flowers in late summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Macroglena stricta is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its ornamental appeal in wetland gardens.
