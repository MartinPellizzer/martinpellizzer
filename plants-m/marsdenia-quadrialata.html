Marsdenia quadrialata

Marsdenia quadrialata, commonly known as the four-lobed Marsdenia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Apocynaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by fleshy, orange-red berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical environments, often growing in shaded forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
