Maxillaria canarensis

Maxillaria canarensis, commonly known as the Canary Island orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure and produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers. The flowers are typically pink or purple with intricate patterns and are borne on slender pedicels. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

It is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
