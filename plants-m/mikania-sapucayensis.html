Mikania sapucayensis

Mikania sapucayensis, commonly known as the Brazilian morning glory, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rapid growth and helix-shaped stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the morning, giving it its common name. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.

Mikania sapucayensis is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
