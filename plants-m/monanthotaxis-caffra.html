Monanthotaxis caffra

Monanthotaxis caffra, commonly known as the African sandmat, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, often in arid or semi-arid regions. The plant features a fibrous root system and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in a dense spike. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
