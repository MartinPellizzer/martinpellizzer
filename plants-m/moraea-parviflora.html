Moraea parviflora

Moraea parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered moraea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family, which includes many ornamental and medicinal plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, delicate flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Moraea parviflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
