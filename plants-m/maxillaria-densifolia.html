Maxillaria densifolia

Maxillaria densifolia, commonly known as the dense-leaved orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its pseudobulbs are short and stout, supporting its evergreen leaves.

Maxillaria densifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or terrariums.
