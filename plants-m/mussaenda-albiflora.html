Mussaenda albiflora

Mussaenda albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered Mussaenda, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its ornamental value comes from its showy inflorescences and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical climates.
