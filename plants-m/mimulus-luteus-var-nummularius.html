Mimulus luteus var. nummularius

Mimulus luteus var. nummularius, commonly known as the golden pursestring, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are typically borne in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
