Mentha suavissima

Mentha suavissima, commonly known as sweet mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a serrated margin. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in whorled clusters during the summer months. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its distinctive menthol-like fragrance.

Mentha suavissima is often cultivated for its culinary and medicinal uses.
