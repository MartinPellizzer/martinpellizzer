Monochoria chinensis

Monochoria chinensis, commonly known as the water plantain, is a wetland herb native to Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, arrow-shaped leaves that float on the surface of water or grow submerged. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in shallow, freshwater habitats such as marshes and rice paddies.

Monochoria chinensis is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
