Myosotis strigosa

Myosotis strigosa, commonly known as the hairy forget-me-not, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its small, blue or purple flowers and hairy stems and leaves. The plant typically grows in damp, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream sides. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early autumn, attracting various pollinators.

Myosotis strigosa is often found in native New Zealand gardens and is valued for its ornamental beauty.
