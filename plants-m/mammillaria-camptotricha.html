Mammillaria camptotricha

Mammillaria camptotricha, commonly known as the hooked mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in white, woolly spines. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and compact size, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and cactus collections.
