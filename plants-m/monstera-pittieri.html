Monstera pittieri

Monstera pittieri, commonly known as the Mexican giant monstera, is a striking tropical plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 3 feet in diameter. The plant features a climbing habit, with aerial roots that allow it to attach to supports as it grows. Its foliage is often marked with irregular holes and notches, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil for optimal growth.
