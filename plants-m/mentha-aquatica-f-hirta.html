Mentha aquatica f. hirta

Mentha aquatica f. hirta, commonly known as water mint, is a flowering plant in the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers arranged in whorled clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, aquatic or semi-aquatic environments, such as stream banks and wet meadows.

This subspecies is known for its aromatic leaves and is often used in herbal remedies and as a culinary herb.
