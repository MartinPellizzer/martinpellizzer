Madhuca prolixa

Madhuca prolixa, commonly known as the long-stalked tallow tree, is a tropical evergreen species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark-brown bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, round fruits containing a single seed. Its roots are known for their medicinal properties, and it is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its timber.
