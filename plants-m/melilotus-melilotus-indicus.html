Melilotus melilotus-indicus

Melilotus melilotus-indicus, commonly known as yellow sweet clover, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia but has been introduced to other parts of the world. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and disturbed areas.

Melilotus melilotus-indicus is valued for its medicinal properties and as a forage crop for livestock.
