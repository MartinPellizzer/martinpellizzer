Melica uniflora

Melica uniflora, commonly known as slender false brome, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture.

Melica uniflora plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
