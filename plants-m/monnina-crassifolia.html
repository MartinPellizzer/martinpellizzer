Monnina crassifolia

Monnina crassifolia, commonly known as broadleaf monnina, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of purple or pink, typically arranged in raceme-like clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Polygalaceae family.
