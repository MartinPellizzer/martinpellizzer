Maxillaria woytkowskii

Maxillaria woytkowskii, commonly known as the Woytkowski's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit, producing pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowers. The plant typically features dark green, strap-like leaves and produces fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Maxillaria woytkowskii is appreciated by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and unique floral structure.
