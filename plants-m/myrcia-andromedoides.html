Myrcia andromedoides

Myrcia andromedoides, commonly known as the Brazilian tea or red myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruits are red, fleshy berries that contain seeds, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Myrcia andromedoides is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
