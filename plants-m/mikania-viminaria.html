Mikania viminaria

Mikania viminaria, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a fast-growing vine native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, showy blue or purple flowers that bloom in the morning, giving it its common name. Mikania viminaria is known for its ability to spread rapidly, often forming dense mats that can dominate disturbed areas.

It is also used in traditional medicine in some regions for its potential health benefits.
