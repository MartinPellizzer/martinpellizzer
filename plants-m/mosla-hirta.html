Mosla hirta

Mosla hirta, commonly known as wall germander, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Geraniaceae and is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and small pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Mosla hirta is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract beneficial insects.
