Mangifera philippinensis

Mangifera philippinensis, commonly known as the Philippine mango, is a tropical tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its large, elongated leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces edible mango fruits that are typically smaller and more acidic compared to common mango varieties. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its fruit and ornamental value.

The plant plays an important role in the biodiversity and agriculture of its native region.
