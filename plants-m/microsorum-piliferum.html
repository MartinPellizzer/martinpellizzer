Microsorum piliferum

Microsorum piliferum, commonly known as the Christmas moss, is a species of aquatic fern that thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are typically dark green and covered with fine, hair-like structures called trichomes. The plant is often found growing on rocks or submerged wood in tropical and subtropical regions. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.

Due to its aesthetic appeal, it is also popular in aquascaping and water garden setups.
