Melandrium macrospermum

Melandrium macrospermum, commonly known as the common bladder campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are usually hairy and slightly succulent.

The fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, round seeds.
