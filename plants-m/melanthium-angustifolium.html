Melanthium angustifolium

Melanthium angustifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaf false foxglove, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae, which includes plants known for their distinctive leaf shapes and often edible or medicinal properties. Melanthium angustifolium is often found in moist woodlands and along stream banks, where it thrives in partial shade.
