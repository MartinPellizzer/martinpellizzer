Meridiana serrulata

Meridiana serrulata, commonly known as the serrated meridiana, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves with serrated edges, which give it its scientific name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, adding a delicate aesthetic to its appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique morphology and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
