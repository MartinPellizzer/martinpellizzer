Monarda scabra

Monarda scabra, commonly known as rough horsemint, is a flowering plant native to the eastern and central United States.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Monarda scabra is also valued for its aromatic foliage and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
