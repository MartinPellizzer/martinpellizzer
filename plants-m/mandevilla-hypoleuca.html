Mandevilla hypoleuca

Mandevilla hypoleuca, commonly known as the white mandevilla, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that range from white to pink. The plant produces showy blooms throughout the growing season, making it a popular choice for gardeners. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a glossy appearance.

Mandevilla hypoleuca thrives in warm climates and requires well-draining soil and ample sunlight.
