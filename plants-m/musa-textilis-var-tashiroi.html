Musa textilis var. tashiroi

Musa textilis var. tashiroi, commonly known as the Tashiro's banana, is a rare and lesser-known variety of the banana plant.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Japan, where it grows in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces small, elongated fruits that are typically not cultivated for consumption but are valued for their ornamental qualities. Its leaves are broad and glossy, contributing to its aesthetic appeal in botanical gardens.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
