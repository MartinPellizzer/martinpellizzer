Macaranga grallata

Macaranga grallata, commonly known as the silky leaf tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is valued for its rapid growth and is sometimes used in reforestation efforts.
