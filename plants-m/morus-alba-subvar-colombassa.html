Morus alba subvar. colombassa

Morus alba subvar. colombassa, commonly known as the Colombian mulberry, is a variant of the white mulberry native to South America.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, aggregate fruits. The plant produces edible berries that are often consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is adapted to a variety of soil types.

This subspecies is also valued for its role in traditional medicine and as a source of fibrous bark.
