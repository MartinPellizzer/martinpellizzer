Myristica atrescens

Myristica atrescens, commonly known as the black pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces berries that turn from green to red as they mature, and the seeds inside are used to make pepper. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The tree is valued for its aromatic seeds and has been used in traditional medicine and as a spice.
