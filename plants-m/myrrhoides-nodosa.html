Myrrhoides nodosa

Myrrhoides nodosa, commonly known as the nodose myrrh, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It features a compact, bushy growth habit with small, narrow leaves arranged in a dense rosette. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Myrrhoides nodosa is adapted to survive in harsh environments, with deep root systems that allow it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Its resilience makes it a valuable plant for ecological restoration in degraded landscapes.
