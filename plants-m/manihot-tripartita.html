Manihot tripartita

Manihot tripartita, commonly known as the arrowleaf batata, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three heart-shaped leaflets. The plant produces edible tubers that are similar in texture to sweet potatoes but have a distinct flavor. These tubers are rich in starch and are used in various culinary applications.

Botanically, it belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is valued for its nutritional content and adaptability to different soil conditions.
