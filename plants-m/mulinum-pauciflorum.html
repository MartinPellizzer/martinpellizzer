Mulinum pauciflorum

Mulinum pauciflorum, commonly known as the few-flowered mullein, is a member of the Scrophulariaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. The plant features tall, hairy stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of soft, fuzzy hairs.

The flowers are typically pale yellow or greenish-yellow, arranged in clusters at the top of the stem.
