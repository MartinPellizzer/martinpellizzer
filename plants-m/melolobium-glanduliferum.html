Melolobium glanduliferum

Melolobium glanduliferum, commonly known as the glandular melolobium, is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in rocky and sandy soils. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often glandular, giving the plant its scientific name, and it is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Melolobium glanduliferum is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens due to its distinctive foliage and flowering habit.
