Myrcianthes fragrans

Myrcianthes fragrans, commonly known as the fragrant myrcia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. It produces small, round berries that are typically red when ripe.

Myrcianthes fragrans is often found in moist, shaded areas of rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
