Malpighia dasycarpa

Malpighia dasycarpa, commonly known as the rough-fruited acerola, is a species of flowering plant in the family Malpighiaceae.

It is native to Central and South America, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant produces small, bright red berries that are rich in vitamin C and other nutrients. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often covered with fine hairs, giving the plant a distinctive appearance.

The flowers are typically yellow or white, with five petals, and are borne in clusters.
