Melampodium microcephalum

Melampodium microcephalum, commonly known as the small-headed sunflower or dwarf sunflower, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the southern United States.

It features bright yellow flowers with a distinctive dark center, typically blooming from summer to fall. The plant has linear, hairy leaves and grows in compact clusters, often reaching heights of less than 12 inches. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive and easy-to-grow addition to native plant gardens.
