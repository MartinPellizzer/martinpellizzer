Masdevallia marizae

Masdevallia marizae, commonly known as the Marizae Masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the Andean highlands of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, sympodial growth habit and produces intricate, often fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically blooms during the cooler months, showcasing its delicate, spurred petals and showy lip. It thrives in shaded, humid environments with well-drained, acidic soil.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
