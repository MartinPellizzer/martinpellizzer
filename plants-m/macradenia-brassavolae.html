Macradenia brassavolae

Macradenia brassavolae, commonly known as the giant flame orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 20 centimeters in length, featuring vibrant red and orange hues with a distinctive yellow throat. The plant is an epiphyte, growing on trees in the rainforest canopy, and it thrives in humid, warm climates with high light exposure. Its foliage consists of broad, leathery leaves that help it retain moisture in its natural habitat.

Due to its unique appearance and rarity, it is highly valued in horticulture and conservation efforts.
