Montbretia rosea

Montbretia rosea, commonly known as the red hot poker, is a striking perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features tall, spiky flower stalks that bear bright orange-red flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that form a dense rosette at the base. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Montbretia rosea is also known for its ability to naturalize and spread readily in suitable conditions.
