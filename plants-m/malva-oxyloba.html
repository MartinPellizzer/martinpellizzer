Malva oxyloba

Malva oxyloba, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and a hairy stem that is often reddish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Malva oxyloba is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
