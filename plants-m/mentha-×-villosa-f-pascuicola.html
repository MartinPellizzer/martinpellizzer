Mentha × villosa f. pascuicola

Mentha × villosa f. pascuicola, commonly known as the woolly meadow mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a hybrid species within the Mentha genus, characterized by its soft, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils. It produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, making it useful in herbal teas and as a culinary herb.
