Marsdenia tinctoria

Marsdenia tinctoria, commonly known as the red spider lily, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its striking red, spider-like flowers. The plant produces a milky latex sap, which has been historically used for dyeing fabrics. Its leaves are opposite and elliptical, with a smooth surface and a waxy texture.
