Mentha crenatodentata

Mentha crenatodentata, commonly known as the serrated-leaf mint, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its serrated, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in whorled clusters. Mentha crenatodentata is valued for its aromatic leaves, which are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.
