Maximiliana martiana

Maximiliana martiana, commonly known as the Mexican orchid, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that often display vibrant colors such as red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in shaded, humid environments. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the soil, while its leaves are broad and strap-like.

Maximiliana martiana is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
