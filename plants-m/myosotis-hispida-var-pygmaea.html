Myosotis hispida var. pygmaea

Myosotis hispida var. pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf mouse ear cress, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features small, bright blue or violet flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, glandular trichomes, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine plant collections.
