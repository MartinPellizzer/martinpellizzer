Myrcia subavenia

Myrcia subavenia, commonly known as the velvet myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, with a strong, aromatic scent.

Myrcia subavenia is often found growing in shaded, moist environments, such as undergrowth in dense forests.
