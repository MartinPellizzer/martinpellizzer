Myrcia elliptica

Myrcia elliptica, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or myrtle, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces bright red, cherry-like berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine.

Its ornamental value is high due to its attractive foliage and showy blooms, making it a popular choice for gardens in warm climates.
