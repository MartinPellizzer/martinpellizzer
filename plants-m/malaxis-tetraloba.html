Malaxis tetraloba

Malaxis tetraloba, commonly known as the four-lobed orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding soil.

The orchid is part of the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
