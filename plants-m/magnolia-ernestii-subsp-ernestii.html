Magnolia ernestii subsp. ernestii

Magnolia ernestii subsp.

ernestii, commonly known as the Ernest magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its large, showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge.

The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic, with a smooth margin and a glossy green upper surface. The bark is smooth and grayish-white, becoming fissured with age.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
