Macroclinidium × suzukii

Macroclinidium × suzukii, commonly known as the hybrid persimmon, is a botanical hybrid resulting from the cross between *Diospyros kaki* and *Diospyros kaki* var. *suzukii*.

It is characterized by its large, sweet, and astringent-free fruit, which ripens to a deep orange-red color. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree with dense, dark green foliage. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in clusters, and it is primarily cultivated for its edible fruit.

The hybrid is valued for its improved flavor and reduced astringency compared to its parent species.
