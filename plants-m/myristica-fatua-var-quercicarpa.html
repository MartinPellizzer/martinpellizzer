Myristica fatua var. quercicarpa

Myristica fatua var. quercicarpa, commonly known as the burr myristica, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rough, woody fruit capsules that resemble small, spiky balls. The plant produces small, white flowers and has alternate, elliptical leaves with a smooth texture. Its seeds are enclosed within the hard, three-lobed fruit, which splits open when mature.

The species is often found in moist, shaded environments within lowland forests.
