Maesopsis tessmannii

Maesopsis tessmannii, commonly known as the African wild pear, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, pear-shaped fruits. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and is often found in woodland and savanna habitats.

The tree is valued for its fruit, which is used in traditional medicine and cuisine.
