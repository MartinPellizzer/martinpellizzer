Melanthesa oblongifolia

Melanthesa oblongifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf melanthus, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa.

It features lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or swampy areas.

Melanthesa oblongifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
