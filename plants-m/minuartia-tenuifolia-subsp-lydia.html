Minuartia tenuifolia subsp. lydia

Minuartia tenuifolia subsp.

lydia, commonly known as the Lydian sandwort, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features small, clustered white flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May.

The plant has slender, pale green stems with alternate, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is well adapted to full sun and dry conditions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover in arid landscapes.
