Majorana fragrans

Majorana fragrans, commonly known as sweet marjoram, is a flowering plant in the family Lamiaceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its aromatic, small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for herb gardens.

Its essential oils contain compounds like cineole and camphor, contributing to its distinctive fragrance and therapeutic properties.
