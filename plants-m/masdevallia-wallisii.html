Masdevallia wallisii

Masdevallia wallisii, commonly known as the Wallis's masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and intricate, often colorful floral structures. The plant typically produces small, delicate flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white, which bloom during the cooler months. Its leaves are usually arranged in a rosette pattern and are covered in fine, soft hairs.

This orchid thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and high humidity.
