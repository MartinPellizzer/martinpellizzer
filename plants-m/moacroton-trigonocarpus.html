Moacroton trigonocarpus

Moacroton trigonocarpus, commonly known as the three-carpel tree, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, with a straight trunk and a broad, rounded crown. The leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate, with a smooth margin and a slightly glossy surface. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, arranged in clusters, and it produces distinctive triangular fruits.

The plant is valued for its hard wood and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
