Manulea atropurpurea

Manulea atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple manulea, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, branching stems covered in a dense layer of fine, hairy foliage. Its flowers are typically purple or pink, arranged in small clusters, and bloom during the warmer months.

Manulea atropurpurea is adapted to survive in dry conditions, with deep roots that help it access moisture from underground sources.
