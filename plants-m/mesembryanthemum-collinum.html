Mesembryanthemum collinum

Mesembryanthemum collinum, commonly known as the mountain iceplant, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the morning and close in the afternoon. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Mesembryanthemum collinum is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick leaves to survive dry periods.
