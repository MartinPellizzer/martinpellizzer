Mikania diversifolia

Mikania diversifolia, commonly known as the creeping gourd vine, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by fruit that resembles a miniature gourd. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Mikania diversifolia is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
