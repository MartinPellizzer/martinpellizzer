Mammea bongo

Mammea bongo, commonly known as the bongo tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves. The tree produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that are followed by orange or red berries. Its bark is thick and often deeply fissured, and it is valued for its durable wood.
