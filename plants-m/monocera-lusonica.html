Monocera lusonica

Monocera lusonica, commonly known as the Portuguese thyme, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 cm, featuring small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are fragrant and often used in traditional herbal remedies.

Monocera lusonica thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.
