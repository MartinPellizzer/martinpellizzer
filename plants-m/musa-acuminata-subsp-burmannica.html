Musa acuminata subsp. burmannica

Musa acuminata subsp.

burmannica, commonly known as the dwarf banana, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia. It belongs to the genus Musa and is characterized by its compact stature compared to other banana species.

The plant produces edible berries that are typically smaller and have a more intense flavor than those of the common banana. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of unique fruit.
