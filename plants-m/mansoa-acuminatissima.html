Mansoa acuminatissima

Mansoa acuminatissima, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central America and northern South America.

It is a member of the Convolvulaceae family, characterized by its twining stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces vibrant blue to violet flowers that bloom in the morning, hence its common name. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.
