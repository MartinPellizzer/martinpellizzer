Mikania berteroana

Mikania berteroana, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a vine native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, often blooming in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is commonly found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Mikania berteroana is also known for its rapid growth and ability to spread aggressively, making it a potential invasive species in certain areas.
