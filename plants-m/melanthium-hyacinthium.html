Melanthium hyacinthium

Melanthium hyacinthium, commonly known as the blue false indigo, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and raceme of blue to purple flowers. The plant is noted for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland habitats.

Melanthium hyacinthium is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
