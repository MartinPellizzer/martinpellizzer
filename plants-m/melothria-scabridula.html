Melothria scabridula

Melothria scabridula, commonly known as the rough melon, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It produces small, green, edible fruits that are typically harvested when they turn yellow or orange. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture, and its stems are covered in fine, hairy ridges. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit is often used in local cuisines for its tangy flavor and nutritional value.
