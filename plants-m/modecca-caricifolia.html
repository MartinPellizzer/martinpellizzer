Modecca caricifolia

Modecca caricifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed mallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding visual interest to its foliage. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Modecca caricifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
