Mentha × similis

Mentha × similis, commonly known as catnip, is a hybrid plant in the mint family (Lamiaceae).

It is characterized by its square, hollow stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces small, pale purple or white flowers arranged in whorls along the stem. Mentha × similis is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been naturalized in many regions around the world.

It is widely cultivated for its aromatic properties and traditional medicinal uses.
