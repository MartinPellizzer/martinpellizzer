Melastoma napalense

Melastoma napalense, commonly known as the Napo melastoma, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, or white. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
