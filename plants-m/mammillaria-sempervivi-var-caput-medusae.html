Mammillaria sempervivi var. caput-medusae

Mammillaria sempervivi var. caput-medusae, commonly known as the Crown of Thorns, is a succulent cactus native to Mexico.

It features a compact, globular stem covered in white, woolly spines and bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is characterized by its dense, clustered growth habit and ability to thrive in well-drained soil. It is drought-tolerant and prefers full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a favored ornamental plant.
