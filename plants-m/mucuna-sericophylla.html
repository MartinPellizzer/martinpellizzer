Mucuna sericophylla

Mucuna sericophylla, commonly known as the silky leaf morning glory, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, silky-haired leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces pods containing flat, dark brown seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Mucuna sericophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, such as nitrogen fixation.
