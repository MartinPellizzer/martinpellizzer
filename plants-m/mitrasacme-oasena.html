Mitrasacme oasena

Mitrasacme oasena, commonly known as the golden star vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a glossy green surface and produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers emit a mild fragrance, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Mitrasacme oasena is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
