Mattia laevigata

Mattia laevigata, commonly known as the smooth-leaved matia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that are often covered in a fine, smooth texture. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy underside, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Mattia laevigata thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
