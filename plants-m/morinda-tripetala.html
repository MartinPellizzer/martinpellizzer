Morinda tripetala

Morinda tripetala, commonly known as the African angelica, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its thick, aromatic stems and broad, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. Its roots and leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and savanna habitats.
