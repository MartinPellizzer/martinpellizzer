Masdevallia pulex

Masdevallia pulex, commonly known as the hairy masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and intricate, often hairy leaf structures. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, typically blooming during the cooler months. Its epiphytic nature allows it to grow on trees in its natural habitat, absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air.

Due to its unique appearance and specific care requirements, it is a prized specimen among orchid enthusiasts.
