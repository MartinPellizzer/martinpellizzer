Maxillaria aureofulva

Maxillaria aureofulva, commonly known as the golden tinge orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow and green foliage, with flowers that display a striking yellowish-orange color. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are relatively small but numerous, arranged in clusters that bloom during the warmer months.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
