Melastoma cordifolium

Melastoma cordifolium, commonly known as the heart-leaved melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as tropical forests and mountainous regions. It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is known for its ornamental value.

The fruit is a capsule that releases seeds when mature, contributing to its propagation in natural habitats.
