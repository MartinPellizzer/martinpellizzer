Malveopsis acaulis var. boliviensis

Malveopsis acaulis var. boliviensis, commonly known as the Bolivian marshmallow, is a perennial herb native to the high Andes of Bolivia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in wet meadows and marshy areas. Its stems are typically short and compact, forming a cushion-like growth habit.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
