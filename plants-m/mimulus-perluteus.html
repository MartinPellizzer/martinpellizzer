Mimulus perluteus

Mimulus perluteus, commonly known as the yellow monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers that resemble small monkey faces, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as stream banks and meadows. It has heart-shaped leaves and produces seed capsules that release tiny seeds when mature.

Mimulus perluteus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a pollinator attractant.
