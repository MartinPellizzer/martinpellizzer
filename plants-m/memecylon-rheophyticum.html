Memecylon rheophyticum

Memecylon rheophyticum, commonly known as the stream memecylon, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often glossy and arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in terminal inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found near streams or in mountainous regions.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
