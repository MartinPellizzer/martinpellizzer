Maxillaria atrovinacea

Maxillaria atrovinacea, commonly known as the black-veined orchid, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its dark green, strap-like leaves and striking, dark violet to black flowers with contrasting white veins. The flowers typically bloom in late winter to early spring, adding a dramatic visual impact to its habitat. This orchid thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

Its unique appearance and seasonal blooming make it a sought-after plant among orchid enthusiasts.
