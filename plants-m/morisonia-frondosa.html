Morisonia frondosa

Morisonia frondosa, commonly known as the frondose morisonia, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fern-like leaves that are deeply lobed and often have a bluish-gray hue. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or partially shaded forest environments.

Morisonia frondosa is a member of the Morisoniaceae family and is valued for its ornamental foliage and ecological importance.
