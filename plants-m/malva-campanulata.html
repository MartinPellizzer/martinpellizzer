Malva campanulata

Malva campanulata, commonly known as the bellflower mallow, is a flowering plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and often reddish in color.

Malva campanulata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
