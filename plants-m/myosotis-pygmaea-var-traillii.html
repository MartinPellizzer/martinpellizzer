Myosotis pygmaea var. traillii

Myosotis pygmaea var. traillii, commonly known as the Traill's dwarf forget-me-not, is a small, herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It features delicate blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from September to November. The plant grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and streambanks. Its leaves are oval and slightly hairy, with a compact growth habit that allows it to thrive in limited spaces.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its charming appearance.
