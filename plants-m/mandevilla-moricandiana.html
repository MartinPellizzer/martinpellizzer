Mandevilla moricandiana

Mandevilla moricandiana, commonly known as the pink mandevilla, is a tropical vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its long, trailing stems and clusters of trumpet-shaped flowers that range from pink to red. The plant produces showy blooms throughout the growing season, typically from spring to fall. Its leaves are usually ovate with a glossy surface and dark green color.

Mandevilla moricandiana thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
