Muscari gussonei

Muscari gussonei, commonly known as the Gussone's grape hyacinth, is a bulbous plant native to southern Europe.

It produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, resembling tiny grapes. The plant typically blooms in early spring, adding vibrant color to gardens and natural landscapes. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a hardy perennial for temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.
