Madronella hypoleuca

Madronella hypoleuca, commonly known as the white madrona, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the western United States.

It typically grows in coastal areas and is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant has elliptical, leathery leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. It produces small, red berries that are a food source for various birds and animals.

Madronella hypoleuca is well adapted to coastal climates and can tolerate salt spray and sandy soils.
