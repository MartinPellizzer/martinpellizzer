Melica rigida

Melica rigida, commonly known as rigid melic, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It typically grows in moist or wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and stream banks. The plant has slender, erect stems that are often hairless or sparsely hairy. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinct midrib and a rough texture.

The inflorescence consists of a spike of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are usually green to brown in color.
