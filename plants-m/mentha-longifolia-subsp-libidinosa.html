Mentha longifolia subsp. libidinosa

Mentha longifolia subsp.

libidinosa, commonly known as wild mint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a serrated margin.

The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, including menthol and limonene, which contribute to its distinctive minty scent.

This subspecies is often found in rocky soils and open habitats, thriving in temperate climates.
