Maxillaria aequiloba

Maxillaria aequiloba, commonly known as the pink lady's slipper, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet to pink flowers with a distinctive slipper-shaped pouch. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native habitat.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in many botanical gardens around the world.
