Mammillaria uncinata var. bihamata

Mammillaria uncinata var. bihamata, commonly known as the two-horned mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are covered in white to pale blue spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

This variety is valued for its unique appearance and is often cultivated as a decorative plant.
