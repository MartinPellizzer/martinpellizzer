Melastoma piluliferum

Melastoma piluliferum, commonly known as the red-flowered melastoma, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Melastomataceae and is characterized by its showy, tubular red or pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface and produces small, round, berry-like fruits that are edible. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Melastoma piluliferum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
