Miconia discoidea

Miconia discoidea, commonly known as the "false hibiscus," is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pinkish-white bracts that resemble petals. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, often surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. Miconia discoidea is a member of the Melastomataceae family and is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets.
