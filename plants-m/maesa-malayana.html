Maesa malayana

Maesa malayana, commonly known as the Malayan maesa, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen shrub form. The plant features small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, often arranged in whorls around the stems.

Maesa malayana is often found in forest understories and is valued for its ornamental qualities.
