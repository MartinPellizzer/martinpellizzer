Malcolmia graeca var. integrifolia

Malcolmia graeca var. integrifolia, commonly known as the Greek malva, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, erect stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves with smooth edges. The plant produces clusters of pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and long blooming period.
