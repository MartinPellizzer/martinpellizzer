Mentha planitiensis

Mentha planitiensis, commonly known as the plains mint, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly serrated leaves that are typically a vibrant green. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Mentha planitiensis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is often used in herbal remedies and is valued for its refreshing scent and medicinal properties.
