Melastoma procumbens

Melastoma procumbens, commonly known as the prostrate melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dark green, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically red when mature, containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Melastoma procumbens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
