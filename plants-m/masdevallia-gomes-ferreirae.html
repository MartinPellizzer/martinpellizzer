Masdevallia gomes-ferreirae

Masdevallia gomes-ferreirae, commonly known as the Brazilian orchid, is a rare and striking species native to the Atlantic rainforests of Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, often colorful flowers with a distinctive lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its humid, shaded habitat. Its foliage consists of narrow, strap-like leaves that help it conserve moisture in its native environment.
