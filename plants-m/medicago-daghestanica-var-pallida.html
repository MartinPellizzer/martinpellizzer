Medicago daghestanica var. pallida

Medicago daghestanica var. pallida, commonly known as the pale sainfoin, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Medicago daghestanica var. pallida is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
