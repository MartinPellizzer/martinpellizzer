Mapania meekiongii

Mapania meekiongii, commonly known as the Meekiongii maple, is a small deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and heart-shaped leaves that turn vibrant red in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in the spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
