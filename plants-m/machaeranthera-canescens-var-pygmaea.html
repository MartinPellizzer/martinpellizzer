Machaeranthera canescens var. pygmaea

Machaeranthera canescens var. pygmaea, commonly known as the pygmy blanketflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features slender, hairy stems and narrow, gray-green leaves that are adapted to arid environments. It produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

This variety is known for its compact growth habit and is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
