Mertensia eastwoodiae

Mertensia eastwoodiae, commonly known as Eastwood's bluebells, is a rare flowering plant native to the Appalachian Mountains.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and is often found in rocky or limestone soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and emit a mild, spicy scent.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is protected in many areas.
