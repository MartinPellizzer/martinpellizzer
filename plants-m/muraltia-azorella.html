Muraltia azorella

Muraltia azorella, commonly known as the Azorean coral tree, is a flowering plant native to the Azores.

It is a small evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters. The plant features compound leaves with elliptical leaflets and produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pink flowers. It is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.

Muraltia azorella is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
