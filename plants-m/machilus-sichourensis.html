Machilus sichourensis

Machilus sichourensis, commonly known as the Sichuan Machilus, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by small, dark blue-black berries. It is often found in moist, shaded areas of mountainous regions, where it thrives in cool, temperate climates.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
