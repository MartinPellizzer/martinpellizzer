Myrcia cuspidata

Myrcia cuspidata, commonly known as the Brazilian peppertree or *piçarão*, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces small, reddish-brown berries that are edible but have a sharp, acidic taste. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Myrcia cuspidata is also known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
