Moeroris oxycoccifolia

Moeroris oxycoccifolia, commonly known as the sharp-leaved buckthorn, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features small, elliptical leaves with a pointed tip, which give it its scientific name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by dark blue berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in woodland edges and open areas with well-drained soil.

Moeroris oxycoccifolia is often used in traditional medicine for its astringent properties.
