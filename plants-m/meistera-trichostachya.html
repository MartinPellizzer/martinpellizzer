Meistera trichostachya

Meistera trichostachya, commonly known as the pink orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are elongated and often have a waxy texture, while the inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of showy blooms.

Meistera trichostachya is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
