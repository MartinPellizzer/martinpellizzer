Melastoma tetrandrum

Melastoma tetrandrum, commonly known as the four-thread melastoma, is a tropical shrub belonging to the Melastomataceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its small, oval leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes, typically blooming during the rainy season. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that release small, dark seeds when mature.

Melastoma tetrandrum is often found in moist, shaded forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in native habitats.
