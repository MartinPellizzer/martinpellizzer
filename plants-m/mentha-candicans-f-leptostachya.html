Mentha candicans f. leptostachya

Mentha candicans f. leptostachya, commonly known as the slender-stalked spearmint, is a botanical variant of the spearmint species.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves with a serrated margin. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers arranged in whorls along the stem. It is native to regions of southern Europe and parts of the Middle East.

This form is often cultivated for its aromatic foliage and potential use in herbal teas and culinary applications.
