Malveopsis hispida

Malveopsis hispida, commonly known as the hairy mallow, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable addition to rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological study.
