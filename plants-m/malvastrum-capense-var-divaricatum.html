Malvastrum capense var. divaricatum

Malvastrum capense var. divaricatum, commonly known as the Cape mallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and large, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in full sun. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
