Matthiola fruticulosa var. provincialis

Matthiola fruticulosa var. provincialis, commonly known as the Provincial wallflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is a perennial herb with compact, shrub-like growth habit, featuring woody stems and dense clusters of small, brightly colored flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, while the flowers come in shades of yellow, orange, and red. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found growing in rocky or coastal areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
