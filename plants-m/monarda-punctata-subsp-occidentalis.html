Monarda punctata subsp. occidentalis

Monarda punctata subsp.

occidentalis, commonly known as the western bee balm, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States. It features showy, tubular red flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and a strong, spicy scent. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
