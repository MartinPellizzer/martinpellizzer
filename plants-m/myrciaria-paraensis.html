Myrciaria paraensis

Myrciaria paraensis, commonly known as the Paraense Myrtle, is a small evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest in Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. It produces small, white or pink flowers that are clustered together, followed by edible, yellow-orange fruits.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local communities.
