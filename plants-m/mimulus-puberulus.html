Mimulus puberulus

Mimulus puberulus, commonly known as the hairy monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in coastal dunes and prairies. The plant features showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are hairy and oval-shaped, contributing to its common name.

Mimulus puberulus is a perennial herb that plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
