Miscanthus sinensis var. gracillimus

Miscanthus sinensis var. gracillimus, commonly known as the silvergrass or slender miscanthus, is a perennial grass native to East Asia.

It features fine, silver-blue foliage that turns coppery in autumn, creating a striking visual display. The plant produces tall, slender culms with feathery panicles of small, fluffy flowers in late summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This ornamental grass is often used in gardens for its elegant appearance and low maintenance requirements.
