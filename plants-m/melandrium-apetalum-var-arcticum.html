Melandrium apetalum var. arcticum

Melandrium apetalum var. arcticum, commonly known as the Arctic catchfly, is a flowering plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant produces small, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in cold, dry environments.
