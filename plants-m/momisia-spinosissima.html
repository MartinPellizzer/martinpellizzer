Momisia spinosissima

Momisia spinosissima, commonly known as the spiny momisia, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its spiny stems and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by elongated, woody fruits containing seeds. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
