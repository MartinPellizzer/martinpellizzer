Malus malus

Malus malus, commonly known as the common apple tree, is a deciduous tree native to central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate, ovate leaves and showy, white or pink flowers. The tree produces pomaceous fruits known as apples, which are typically round and have a fibrous core. Malus malus is widely cultivated for its fruit, which is used in cooking, baking, and cider production.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, and it is susceptible to various pests and diseases.
