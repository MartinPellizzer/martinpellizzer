Maxillaria microtricha

Maxillaria microtricha, commonly known as the fine-haired maxillaria, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink. The plant features a short, upright stem with a few leaves and is often epiphytic, growing on trees or rocks in humid environments. Its flowers are typically borne in clusters and have a distinctive, intricate structure with hairy sepals.

Maxillaria microtricha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
