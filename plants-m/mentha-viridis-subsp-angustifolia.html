Mentha viridis subsp. angustifolia

Mentha viridis subsp.

angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaf mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and have a strong mint aroma.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers in whorls along the stem, usually in shades of blue or purple. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This subspecies is often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb due to its aromatic properties.
