Melastoma pentapetalum

Melastoma pentapetalum, commonly known as the five-petaled melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Melastomataceae and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that typically bloom in shades of red and pink. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in warm climates.
