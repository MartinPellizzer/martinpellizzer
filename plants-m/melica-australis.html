Melica australis

Melica australis, commonly known as Australian millet, is a perennial grass native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed or arid environments.

Melica australis is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide forage for livestock.
