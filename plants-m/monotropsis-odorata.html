Monotropsis odorata

Monotropsis odorata, commonly known as sweet pipsissewa, is a small evergreen shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, red, berry-like fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded forest environments.
