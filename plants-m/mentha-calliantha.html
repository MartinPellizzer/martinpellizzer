Mentha calliantha

Mentha calliantha, commonly known as the Mexican mint or Mexican honey mint, is a perennial herb native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, slightly fuzzy leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported digestive and antiseptic properties.

Mentha calliantha is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
