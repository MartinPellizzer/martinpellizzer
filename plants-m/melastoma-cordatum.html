Melastoma cordatum

Melastoma cordatum, commonly known as the heartleaf melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, tubular flowers. The leaves are typically heart-shaped, which gives the plant its common name, and they are arranged alternately along the stems. The flowers, which range in color from pink to red, are often clustered in inflorescences and attract various pollinators.

This plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
