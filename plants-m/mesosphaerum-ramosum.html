Mesosphaerum ramosum

Mesosphaerum ramosum, commonly known as the branched globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its branching stems and showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds. Mesosphaerum ramosum thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
