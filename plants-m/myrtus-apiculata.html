Myrtus apiculata

Myrtus apiculata, commonly known as the Christmas rose, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a small shrub that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant features narrow, leathery leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. It blooms in late summer to early autumn, often around the Christmas season, hence its common name.

Myrtus apiculata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in a sunny, well-drained environment.
