Meriandra dianthera

Meriandra dianthera, commonly known as the golden flame orchid, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers with bright yellow and orange hues. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its flowers are highly fragrant, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
