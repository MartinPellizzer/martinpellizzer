Mimosa asperata var. intermedia

Mimosa asperata var. intermedia, commonly known as the rough mimosa, is a shrub native to South America.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, yellow, spherical flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a woody stem and thrives in well-drained soils, often found in coastal areas. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed sites.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
