Morinda citrina

Morinda citrina, commonly known as the yellow morinda, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, with smooth, greyish bark and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in summer, followed by orange-red berries containing several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional medicinal uses in some cultures.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
