Monodora

Monodora, commonly known as the African breadfruit or African nutmeg, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Anacardiaceae.

It consists of several species, most notably Monodora myristica, which is native to tropical Africa. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, woody fruit that contains a single, large seed surrounded by a fleshy, edible pulp. The leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a distinctive odor.

Monodora species are valued for their medicinal properties and are used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
