Masdevallia maculata f. flava

Masdevallia maculata f. flava, commonly known as the Yellow-streaked Masdevallia, is a striking orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, drooping inflorescence and vivid yellow and green markings on its petals and sepals. The plant typically grows as a sympodial orchid, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its flowers are highly ornamental, featuring a unique blend of color and form that makes it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.

This cultivar is often cultivated for its beauty and adaptability to intermediate to cool growing conditions.
