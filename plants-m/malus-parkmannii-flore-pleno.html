Malus parkmannii flore-pleno

Malus parkmannii flore-pleno, commonly known as the double-flowered crabapple, is a ornamental tree belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, showy clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring, giving it a particularly ornate appearance. The plant is a cultivar of Malus parkmannii, which is native to China, and it is valued for its aesthetic appeal in gardens. Its foliage turns vibrant shades of red or yellow in autumn, adding seasonal interest.

The fruit, though not typically edible, contributes to the tree's ornamental value and supports local wildlife.
