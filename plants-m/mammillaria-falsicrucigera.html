Mammillaria falsicrucigera

Mammillaria falsicrucigera, commonly known as the false cross cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a small, globular cactus with a short, cylindrical stem and a dense covering of spines. The plant features numerous areoles from which both spines and flowers emerge. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, blooming in the spring or early summer.

This species is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.
