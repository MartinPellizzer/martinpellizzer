Melinis denudata

Melinis denudata, commonly known as the slender bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland areas, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and lakes. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in moist to wet conditions and is often found in disturbed or transitional habitats.

Melinis denudata plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
