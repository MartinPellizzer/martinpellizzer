Mentha gentilis var. cuneifolia

Mentha gentilis var. cuneifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaf mint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often aromatic and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of purple or blue, typically arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

This variety is valued for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
