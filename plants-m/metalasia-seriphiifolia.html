Metalasia seriphiifolia

Metalasia seriphiifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf metalasia, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and its small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for arid or semi-arid environments.

Metalasia seriphiifolia is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
