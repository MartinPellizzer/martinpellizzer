Mentha sylvestris var. stenostachya

Mentha sylvestris var. stenostachya, commonly known as the narrow-stemmed wild mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, opposite leaves that are typically oval to lance-shaped. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers arranged in whorls along the stem, which bloom from late summer to early autumn. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.

Due to its aromatic leaves and medicinal properties, it is valued in traditional herbal remedies.
