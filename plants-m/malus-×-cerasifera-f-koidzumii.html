Malus × cerasifera f. koidzumii

Malus × cerasifera f. koidzumii, commonly known as the Koidzumi cherry, is a cultivar of the ornamental cherry tree.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and profuse flowering, with clusters of white to pale pink blossoms appearing in early spring. The plant is native to Japan and is prized for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and landscapes. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring moderate watering and pruning to maintain its form.

This variety is also valued for its hardiness and ability to withstand urban environments.
