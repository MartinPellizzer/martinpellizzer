Macrotyloma hockii

Macrotyloma hockii, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, trifoliate leaves and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces cylindrical pods containing small, edible seeds that are often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for cultivation in arid environments.

Additionally, it has the ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
