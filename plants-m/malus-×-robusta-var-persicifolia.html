Malus × robusta var. persicifolia

Malus × robusta var. persicifolia, commonly known as the Persian apple or Persian crabapple, is a hybrid plant that exhibits characteristics of both Malus species.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree with a compact growth habit and is native to regions of Central Asia. The plant is notable for its ornamental value, featuring showy flowers and attractive foliage. Its leaves are typically broad and have a distinctive lobed shape, contributing to its aesthetic appeal.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental qualities and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
