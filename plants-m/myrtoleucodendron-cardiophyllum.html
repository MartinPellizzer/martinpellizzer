Myrtoleucodendron cardiophyllum

Myrtoleucodendron cardiophyllum, commonly known as the heartleaf myrtle, is a unique plant native to the humid forests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are often a deep green with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom in clusters during the warmer months. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant's botanical structure reflects its adaptation to moist, shaded environments, with a compact growth habit and shallow root system.
