Myrcia maraguana

Myrcia maraguana, commonly known as Brazilian pepper tree or *Pimenta-de-ferro*, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy dark green leaves and fragrant red berries. The plant typically grows in wet tropical forests and is often found in coastal areas. Its bark is thick and fissured, and it produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers.

Myrcia maraguana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in medicine and as a spice.
