Myrtus beaurepairiana

Myrtus beaurepairiana, commonly known as the Corsican myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant features narrow, dark green leaves and produces small, fragrant white flowers in spring. Its ornamental value comes from its compact growth habit and pleasant scent.

It is often used in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
