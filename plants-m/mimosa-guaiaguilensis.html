Mimosa guaiaguilensis

Mimosa guaiaguilensis, commonly known as the yellow mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and fluffy, yellow pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in shrubland and forested areas, often in regions with moderate to high rainfall. It has a shallow root system and is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.

The species is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.
