Myrsine pendula

Myrsine pendula, commonly known as the weeping myrsine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2 to 5 meters. The plant features opposite, simple, and usually elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green. Its flowers are small, white or pale pink, and clustered in dense inflorescences.

The fruit is a small, three-lobed berry that turns red when mature.
