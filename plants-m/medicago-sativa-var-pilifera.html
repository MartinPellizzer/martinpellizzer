Medicago sativa var. pilifera

Medicago sativa var. pilifera, commonly known as goat's rue, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Due to its historical use in traditional medicine, it has been cultivated for its potential therapeutic properties.
