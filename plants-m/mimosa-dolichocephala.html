Mimosa dolichocephala

Mimosa dolichocephala, commonly known as the long-headed sensitive plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves that fold shut when touched. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in the spring and summer. Its root system is fibrous, and it thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.

Mimosa dolichocephala is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
