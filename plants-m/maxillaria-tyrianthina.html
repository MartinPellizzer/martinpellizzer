Maxillaria tyrianthina

Maxillaria tyrianthina, commonly known as the Tyrian Maxillaria, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant typically blooms during the summer months, and its inflorescence consists of multiple clustered flowers on a single stem. The leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a rosette pattern, while the roots are fibrous and adapted for epiphytic growth.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
