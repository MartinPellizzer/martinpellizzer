Myrcia neoyaquensis

Myrcia neoyaquensis, commonly known as the Neoyaquense Myrtle, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly in Ecuador.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are typically elliptical and have a glossy surface.

It produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale pink, followed by small, fleshy berries that are an attractive feature of the plant.
