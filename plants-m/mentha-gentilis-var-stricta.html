Mentha gentilis var. stricta

Mentha gentilis var. stricta, commonly known as narrow-leaf mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and strong, aromatic mint scent. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowering stems bear small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple in color.

Mentha gentilis var. stricta is often used in herbal medicine and as a culinary herb due to its refreshing flavor and medicinal properties.
