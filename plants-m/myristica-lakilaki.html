Myristica lakilaki

Myristica lakilaki, commonly known as the Lakilaki nut, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, woody fruits that contain a valuable nut inside. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by oval-shaped pods, which mature to a reddish color. The seeds are encased in a hard, aromatic shell and are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

Its unique botanical features make it an important species in the biodiversity of Madagascar.
