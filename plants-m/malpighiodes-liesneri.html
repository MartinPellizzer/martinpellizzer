Malpighiodes liesneri

Malpighiodes liesneri, commonly known as the Lepidoceros orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that often display a range of vibrant colors. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually broad and leathery, adapted to retain moisture in its natural habitat.

The flowers bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
