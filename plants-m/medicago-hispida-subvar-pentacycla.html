Medicago hispida subvar. pentacycla

Medicago hispida subvar. pentacycla, commonly known as the red clover, is a perennial legume native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion and improves soil fertility through nitrogen fixation. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in forage and as a cover crop.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
