Memorialis hispida

Memorialis hispida, commonly known as the hispid memorialis, is a species of flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, or hispid, stems and leaves, which provide a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
