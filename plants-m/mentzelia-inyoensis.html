Mentzelia inyoensis

Mentzelia inyoensis, commonly known as Inyo morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Great Basin region of the United States.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are usually pale purple to pink in color. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered with fine hairs.

Mentzelia inyoensis is adapted to arid environments and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
