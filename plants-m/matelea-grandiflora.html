Matelea grandiflora

Matelea grandiflora, commonly known as the Mexican moonflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as bats and moths. The plant belongs to the Apocynaceae family, which includes milkweeds and other flowering plants with toxic alkaloids. Its leaves are typically opposite and ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.
