Mucuna jarocha

Mucuna jarocha, commonly known as the Mexican velvet bean, is a leguminous plant native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its distinctive, velvety pods that contain numerous elliptical seeds. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine, reaching lengths of up to 10 meters, with trifoliate leaves and showy, yellow flowers. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and abandoned fields.

The seeds of Mucuna jarocha are rich in protein and have been used traditionally for both food and medicinal purposes.
