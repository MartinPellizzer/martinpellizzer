Maesa brunnea

Maesa brunnea, commonly known as the red-flowered maesa, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clusters of bright red flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its flowers are rich in nectar, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Maesa brunnea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
