Maxillaria funicaulis

Maxillaria funicaulis, commonly known as the spider orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its intricate, spider-like floral structure with long, slender petals and a distinctive, narrow column. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, with a subtle fragrance.

Maxillaria funicaulis is prized for its unique appearance and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
