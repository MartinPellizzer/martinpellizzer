Macaranga harveyana var. puberula

Macaranga harveyana var. puberula, commonly known as the hairy macaranga, is a species of flowering plant in the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including regions of Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its alternate, simple, and often hairy leaves, which give it its common name. It produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, typically in the axils of the leaves.

This variant is often found in tropical rainforests and is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
