Melaleuca lanigera

Melaleuca lanigera, commonly known as the silky melaleuca, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia, where it typically grows in coastal heathlands and sandy soils. The plant is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and small, aromatic flowers that range in color from white to pink. It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained conditions.

Melaleuca lanigera is also valued for its ecological role in supporting native wildlife and stabilizing coastal environments.
