Malpighia wrightiana

Malpighia wrightiana, commonly known as Wright's granadilla, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Malpighiaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, bright red berries. The plant produces edible fruits that are rich in vitamin C and have a tart, acidic flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.

The flowers are typically yellow or white, and the plant is adapted to grow in well-drained soils.
