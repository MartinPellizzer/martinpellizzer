Myristica plumeriifolia

Myristica plumeriifolia, commonly known as the white nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with elongated, leathery leaflets. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by oval, woody fruits containing aromatic seeds. Its seeds, known as nutmeg, are highly valued for their distinctive spice and medicinal properties.

The tree thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland tropical forests.
