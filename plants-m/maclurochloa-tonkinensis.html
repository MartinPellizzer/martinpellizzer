Maclurochloa tonkinensis

Maclurochloa tonkinensis, commonly known as the Tonkin feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, needle-like leaves that give it a delicate appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a narrow, cylindrical spikelet that bears small, two-ranked spikelets.

Maclurochloa tonkinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural settings for its graceful foliage.
