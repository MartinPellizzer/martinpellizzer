Machilus himalayensis

Machilus himalayensis, commonly known as the Himalayan machilus, is a deciduous tree native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by oval, woody fruits. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
