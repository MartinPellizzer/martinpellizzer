Mallotus metcalfianus

Mallotus metcalfianus, commonly known as the red-flowered hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or pink flowers, which are typically five-petaled and resemble those of the hibiscus genus. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate or elliptical in shape. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers are not only visually appealing but also attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
