Myrcia stigmatosa

Myrcia stigmatosa, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Myrcia stigmatosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions, posing challenges to local ecosystems.
