Mikania cordata var. indica

Mikania cordata var. indica, commonly known as Indian morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Asia.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that range from white to pale purple. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it both ornamental and invasive in some areas. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.

The seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.
