Moniera crenulata

Moniera crenulata, commonly known as the crested fern, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, feather-like fronds that often have a wavy or serrated edge. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests or along stream banks. Its leaves are usually dark green and can reach lengths of up to 1 meter.

Moniera crenulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
