Momordica macrantha

Momordica macrantha, commonly known as the large-fruit bitter gourd, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, oblong fruits that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers. Its fruits are typically used in traditional medicine and cuisine, though they contain toxic compounds that require careful preparation.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruits.
