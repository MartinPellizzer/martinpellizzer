Mespilus domestica

Mespilus domestica, commonly known as the common medlar, is a deciduous tree native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It produces small, round fruits that are typically harvested in the autumn and have a unique, complex flavor. The plant has a shallow root system and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a serrated margin.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruit.
