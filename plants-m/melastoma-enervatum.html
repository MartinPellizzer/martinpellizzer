Melastoma enervatum

Melastoma enervatum, commonly known as the small-leaved melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Melastomataceae and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, with five petals and prominent stamens. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The fruit is a capsule that releases tiny seeds, aiding in its dispersal.
