Maxillaria cuzcoensis

Maxillaria cuzcoensis, commonly known as the Cuzco orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces showy, fragrant flowers with a distinctive coloration. The plant typically features dark green, lance-shaped leaves and grows in shaded, moist environments. Its inflorescence consists of several flowers arranged in a raceme, often displaying a mix of white, yellow, and pink hues.

Maxillaria cuzcoensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated for its unique floral display.
