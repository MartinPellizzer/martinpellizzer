Malachra alceifolia f. hispidissima

Malachra alceifolia f. hispidissima, commonly known as the hispid silky oaks, is a variant of the silky oak species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along riverbanks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
