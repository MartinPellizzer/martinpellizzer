Mentha × schalkiana

Mentha × schalkiana, commonly known as the marsh mint, is a hybrid perennial herb native to wetland areas in Europe.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and clusters of small, pale blue or purple flowers. The plant thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found near ponds, streams, and marshes. Its leaves are used in traditional herbal remedies and as a culinary herb.

Mentha × schalkiana is also valued for its role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining wetland biodiversity.
