Microchilus tunquianus

Microchilus tunquianus, commonly known as the Tunquian sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sunlight. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Microchilus tunquianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its bright, cheerful blossoms.
