Muscari breviscapum

Muscari breviscapum, commonly known as the short-capped grape hyacinth, is a bulbous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet. The plant typically blooms in early spring, producing a fragrant, grape-like scent that attracts pollinators. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, emerging from a bulb that stores nutrients for the flowering cycle.

Muscari breviscapum is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to naturalize in suitable climates.
