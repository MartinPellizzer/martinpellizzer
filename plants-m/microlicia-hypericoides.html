Microlicia hypericoides

Microlicia hypericoides, commonly known as the small-stemmed bedstraw, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features slender, wiry stems that are often covered with fine hairs, and its leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic in shape. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in the spring.

The fruit is a small, dry achene that is often surrounded by persistent calyx lobes.
