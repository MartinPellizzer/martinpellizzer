Melodinus myrtifolius

Melodinus myrtifolius, commonly known as the sweetleaf tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. These flowers give way to small, fleshy fruits that are edible and have a sweet, mucilaginous texture.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to provide shade in tropical landscapes.
