Melocactus zehntneri

Melocactus zehntneri, commonly known as the Zehntner's melon cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its spherical shape and a spiny, segmented stem that resembles a melon. The plant produces a single, large, fleshy fruit that is typically yellow or orange in color. Its flowers are usually yellow or red and appear at the top of the stem during the warmer months.

Melocactus zehntneri is well adapted to drought conditions and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for desert gardens.
