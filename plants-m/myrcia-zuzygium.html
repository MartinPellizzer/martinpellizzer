Myrcia zuzygium

Myrcia zuzygium, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *cereja do Brasil*, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its small, red, cherry-like fruits that are edible. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and emit a slight aromatic scent.

The flowers are white or pale pink, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.
