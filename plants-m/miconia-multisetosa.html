Miconia multisetosa

Miconia multisetosa, commonly known as the hairy miconia, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a large shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height, characterized by its dense, often hairy foliage. The leaves are typically broad, elliptical, and have a rough texture with prominent veins. It produces small, clustered white flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy fruits.

The plant is notable for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
