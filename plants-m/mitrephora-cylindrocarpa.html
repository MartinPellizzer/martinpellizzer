Mitrephora cylindrocarpa

Mitrephora cylindrocarpa, commonly known as the round-fruited mitrephora, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes plants like the cashew and mango. The tree is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and produces small, clustered yellowish-white flowers. Its fruit is a small, globose drupe with a smooth, cylindrical shape, hence the species name "cylindrocarpa.".

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has potential uses in traditional medicine.
