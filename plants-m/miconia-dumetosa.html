Miconia dumetosa

Miconia dumetosa, commonly known as the shrub miconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically covered in a fuzzy coating. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it can become a problematic species in certain regions.
