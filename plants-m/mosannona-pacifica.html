Mosannona pacifica

Mosannona pacifica, commonly known as the Pacific mosannona, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces woody capsules that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
