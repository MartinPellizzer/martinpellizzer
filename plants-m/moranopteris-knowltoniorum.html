Moranopteris knowltoniorum

Moranopteris knowltoniorum, commonly known as the Knowlton's shield fern, is a rare and distinctive member of the fern family.

It is characterized by its broad, shield-shaped fronds that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures, including spore-producing clusters, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Due to its limited distribution and unique morphology, it is of particular interest to botanists and conservationists.
