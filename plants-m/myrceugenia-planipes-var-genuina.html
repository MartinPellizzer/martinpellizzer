Myrceugenia planipes var. genuina

Myrceugenia planipes var. genuina, commonly known as the Brazilian black pepper tree, is a species native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, clustered white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruits are drupes that mature to a dark purple or black color, containing a single seed.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
