Myrciaria prasina

Myrciaria prasina, commonly known as the Brazilian blackberry, is a shrub native to the Atlantic rainforests of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dark purple to black berries that are rich in antioxidants. The plant has elliptical leaves with a glossy surface and produces small, white flowers. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in shaded, moist environments.

The berries are not only edible but also used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
