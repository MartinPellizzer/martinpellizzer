Medicago denticulata var. reticulata

Medicago denticulata var. reticulata, commonly known as the reticulate medick, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its small, trifoliate leaves with a distinctive net-like venation pattern, which gives it its species name "reticulata.". The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in dense, globular clusters, typical of the genus Medicago. It is a perennial herb that thrives in temperate climates and is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils.

This variety is notable for its adaptability and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.
