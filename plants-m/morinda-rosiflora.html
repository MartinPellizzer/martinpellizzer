Morinda rosiflora

Morinda rosiflora, commonly known as the rose-flowered morinda, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, which bloom in spring and summer. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, black seeds.

Morinda rosiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
