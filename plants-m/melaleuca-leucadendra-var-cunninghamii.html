Melaleuca leucadendra var. cunninghamii

Melaleuca leucadendra var. cunninghamii, commonly known as the white bloodwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale grey to white bark that peels in thin strips, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are narrow, linear, and often slightly hairy, with a greyish-green hue. Its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and appear in clustered inflorescences, typically during spring and summer.

The plant is well adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in coastal and sandy areas.
