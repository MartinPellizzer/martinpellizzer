Mentha × sativa var. gracilis

Mentha × sativa var. gracilis, commonly known as slender mint, is a hybrid mint species characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and fragrant, aromatic leaves.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are widely used in culinary applications, herbal teas, and traditional medicine due to their menthol content.

The variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
