Miconia imitans

Miconia imitans, commonly known as the false glorybower, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that are often surrounded by velvety bracts. Miconia imitans is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments.

However, it is also considered an invasive species in some regions due to its aggressive spreading habit.
