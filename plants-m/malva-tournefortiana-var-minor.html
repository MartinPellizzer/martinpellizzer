Malva tournefortiana var. minor

Malva tournefortiana var. minor, commonly known as the lesser mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces showy pink or purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight. It is often found in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
