Mentha arvensis f. domita

Mentha arvensis f. domita, commonly known as field mint or sweet mint, is a subspecies of the broad-leaf mint.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly sweet-smelling leaves and is native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant typically grows in moist, open areas such as meadows and along stream banks. It has square stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a serrated margin.

Its flowers are small, tubular, and usually pink or purple, appearing in clusters at the leaf axils.
