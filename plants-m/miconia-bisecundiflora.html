Miconia bisecundiflora

Miconia bisecundiflora, commonly known as the red miconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pink flowers that are typically borne on long, drooping inflorescences. Its fruit is a bright red, fleshy berry that contains numerous seeds.

Miconia bisecundiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage and showy blooms.
