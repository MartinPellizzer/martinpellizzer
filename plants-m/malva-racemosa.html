Malva racemosa

Malva racemosa, commonly known as the showy mallows, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes and produces clusters of showy, pink to purple flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along streambanks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and restoration projects.
