Mentha × varians

Mentha × varians, commonly known as catnip, is a hybrid plant in the mint family (Lamiaceae).

It is characterized by its square, hollow stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, and it is known for its strong, aromatic scent. Mentha × varians is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been naturalized in many regions around the world.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.
