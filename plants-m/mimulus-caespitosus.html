Mimulus caespitosus

Mimulus caespitosus, commonly known as the tufted monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to western North America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which are typically tubular in shape and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant grows in clusters, with multiple flowering stems arising from a central base, giving it a tufted appearance. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, often covered in fine hairs.

Mimulus caespitosus is typically found in moist, sunny habitats such as stream banks and meadows.
