Mentha longifolia var. petiolata

Mentha longifolia var. petiolata, commonly known as purple mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that are often tinged with purple, giving it its distinctive appearance. The leaves are lance-shaped, slightly serrated, and have a strong, aromatic menthol scent. It produces small, pale purple flowers in whorls along the stem, typically blooming in summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in gardens for its fragrance and visual appeal.
