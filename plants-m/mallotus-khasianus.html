Mallotus khasianus

Mallotus khasianus, commonly known as the Khasi mallot, is a deciduous tree native to the northeastern regions of India.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in hill forest ecosystems.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest in both horticulture and phytochemical research.
