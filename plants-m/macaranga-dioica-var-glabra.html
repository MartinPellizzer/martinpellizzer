Macaranga dioica var. glabra

Macaranga dioica var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth macaranga, is a species of flowering plant in the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Asia, including India, Sri Lanka, and parts of Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its smooth, often glabrous stems and alternate, simple, ovate to elliptic leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered in cup-like structures called cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

The plant is also known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed habitats.
