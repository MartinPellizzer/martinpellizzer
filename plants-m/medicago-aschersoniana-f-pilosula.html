Medicago aschersoniana f. pilosula

Medicago aschersoniana f. pilosula, commonly known as the hairy goatweed, is a variant of the annual medic.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically clustered in dense heads. It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

This subspecies is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to thrive in dry, nutrient-poor environments.
