Myristica fragrans

Myristica fragrans, commonly known as nutmeg, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Banda Islands in Indonesia.

It produces small, round fruits that contain the aromatic seeds used as nutmeg and mace. The plant belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its glossy dark green leaves and fragrant flowers. The essential oil of the fruit's aril is used to make mace, which is also a spice.

Its cultivation is closely linked to its historical significance in trade and traditional medicine.
