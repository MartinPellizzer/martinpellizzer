Mariscus stramineoferrugineus

Mariscus stramineoferrugineus, commonly known as the rusty straw marsh grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in wet or marshy environments, often found along the edges of ponds, ditches, and wet meadows. The plant has slender, erect stems that are covered in a distinctive rust-colored bloom, giving it its common name. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that becomes more pronounced as the plant matures.

Mariscus stramineoferrugineus plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
