Mentha longifolia var. campylostachya

Mentha longifolia var. campylostachya, commonly known as narrow-leaved mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves and small, pale purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a strong mint scent due to the presence of essential oils in its leaves and stems. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

This variety is often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.
