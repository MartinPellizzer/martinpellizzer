Myrcia kegeliana var. longifolia

Myrcia kegeliana var. longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved myrcia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The leaves are often aromatic and have a distinctive scent, which is typical of the Myrtaceae family.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
