Maxillaria lindeniae

Maxillaria lindeniae, commonly known as the Lindenia orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and intricate floral structure. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, with a distinctive lip that is often marked with contrasting patterns. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Maxillaria lindeniae thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
