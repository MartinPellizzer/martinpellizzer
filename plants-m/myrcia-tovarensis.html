Myrcia tovarensis

Myrcia tovarensis, commonly known as the Tovarensis Myrtle, is a species of flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, reddish-purple berries. Its leaves are thick, leathery, and have a strong, aromatic scent.

Myrcia tovarensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
