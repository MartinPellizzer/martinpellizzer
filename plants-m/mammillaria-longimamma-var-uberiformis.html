Mammillaria longimamma var. uberiformis

Mammillaria longimamma var. uberiformis, commonly known as the woolly mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly white spines that cover its cylindrical stems. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and thrives in full sun, requiring minimal water due to its succulent nature. Its flowers are usually yellow or pale orange, appearing at the tips of the stems during the spring and summer months.

This variety is popular among cactus enthusiasts for its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
