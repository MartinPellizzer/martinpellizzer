Myrtoleucodendron sparsiflorum

Myrtoleucodendron sparsiflorum, commonly known as the sparse-flowered myrtle-leaved tree, is a member of the Myrtaceae family.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in Ecuador and Peru. It thrives in humid, temperate climates and is often found in cloud forests.

Its botanical features make it a unique subject for ecological and horticultural study.
