Metalasia lichtensteinii

Metalasia lichtensteinii, commonly known as the Lichtenstein's metalasia, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale blue to purple flowers, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green hue.

Metalasia lichtensteinii is often found in open grasslands and along rocky outcrops, thriving in well-drained soils.
