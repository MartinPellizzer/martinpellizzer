Melanolepis moluccana var. pendula

Melanolepis moluccana var. pendula, commonly known as the hanging sedge, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its drooping, grass-like inflorescences that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are narrow and grassy, while its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland habitats.
