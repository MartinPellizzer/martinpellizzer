Mentha canariensis

Mentha canariensis, commonly known as Canary mint, is a perennial herb native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. It is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Canary mint is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
