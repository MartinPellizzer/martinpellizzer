Marcgravia subcaudata

Marcgravia subcaudata, commonly known as the hanging basket plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Marcgraviaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that consist of bright red bracts surrounding small white flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Marcgravia subcaudata is known for its ability to grow in hanging baskets, where its cascading foliage and flowers create an attractive display.
