Magnolia reynelii

Magnolia reynelii, commonly known as the Mexican magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the highlands of Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has simple, alternate, elliptic leaves that are dark green and glossy. Its bark is smooth and grayish, becoming fissured with age.

Magnolia reynelii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
