Mystacidium pseudopetiolatum

Mystacidium pseudopetiolatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees, primarily in tropical and subtropical regions.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually arranged in a spiral pattern along its stems. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers, followed by berries that are typically red or yellow in color. Its roots form specialized structures called haustoria, which attach to the host plant and extract water and nutrients.

The species is notable for its ecological role in providing food for birds and insects in its native habitats.
