Mangifera indica var. dodol

Mangifera indica var. dodol, commonly known as the dodol mango, is a variety of mango native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elongated fruits with a thick, fibrous pulp and a distinct, sweet aroma. The tree is evergreen, with broad, feather-like leaves and produces flowers that are typically white or pink. The fruit is often used in traditional confections and beverages due to its high sugar content and unique flavor.

It thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
