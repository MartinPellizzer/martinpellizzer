Mephitidia jackiana

Mephitidia jackiana, commonly known as the Jack's mephit, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate with a smooth margin.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
