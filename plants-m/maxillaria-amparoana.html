Maxillaria amparoana

Maxillaria amparoana, commonly known as the Amparo orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the cloud forests of Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and intricate floral structure. The plant produces fragrant, showy flowers with a distinctive coloration, often featuring a mix of white, pink, and purple hues. Its pseudobulbs are elongated and covered in a thick, leathery epidermis, which helps it retain moisture in its humid, shaded habitat.

Maxillaria amparoana is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and relatively easy cultivation under suitable conditions.
