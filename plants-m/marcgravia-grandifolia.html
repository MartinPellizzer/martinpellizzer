Marcgravia grandifolia

Marcgravia grandifolia, commonly known as the giant water vine, is a climbing plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces showy, pale yellow flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding soil and water sources.

Marcgravia grandifolia plays an important role in the ecosystem by providing habitat and food for various insect and bird species.
