Melaleuca crassifolia

Melaleuca crassifolia, commonly known as broadleaf paperbark, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia, where it typically grows in coastal heathlands and open forests. The plant is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark that gives it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, and it produces small, fragrant white flowers.

Melaleuca crassifolia is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
