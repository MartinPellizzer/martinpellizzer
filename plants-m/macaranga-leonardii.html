Macaranga leonardii

Macaranga leonardii, commonly known as the Leonard's makara, is a species of flowering plant in the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its alternate, simple leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in cyathia, a feature common in the Euphorbiaceae family.

The plant is often found in lowland rainforests and is adapted to humid, shaded environments.
