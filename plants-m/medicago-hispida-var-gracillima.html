Medicago hispida var. gracillima

Medicago hispida var. gracillima, commonly known as slender goat's rue, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. The plant has hairy stems and leaves with a distinctive, fern-like appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological studies and traditional herbal uses.
