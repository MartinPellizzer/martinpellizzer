Maximilianea tinctoria

Maximilianea tinctoria, commonly known as the red-flowered mallow, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and arid environments. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves and produces striking, showy red or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fibrous roots help it survive in harsh, drought-prone conditions.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in dye production.
