Mesembryanthemum concavum

Mesembryanthemum concavum, commonly known as the concave ice plant, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, water-storing leaves and vibrant, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant is adapted to arid environments, featuring a shallow root system and a thick cuticle to reduce water loss. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it an attractive and low-maintenance ornamental plant.
