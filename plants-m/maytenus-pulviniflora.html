Maytenus pulviniflora

Maytenus pulviniflora, commonly known as the "Cape Mayten," is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are usually leathery and dark green in color. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Maytenus pulviniflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
