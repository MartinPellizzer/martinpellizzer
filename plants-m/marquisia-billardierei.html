Marquisia billardierei

Marquisia billardierei, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow floral clusters, which are produced in late winter to early spring. The plant has compound leaves with narrow, lance-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests.

Marquisia billardierei is a symbol of Australian national identity and is widely cultivated for its ornamental value.
