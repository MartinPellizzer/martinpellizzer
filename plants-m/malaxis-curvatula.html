Malaxis curvatula

Malaxis curvatula, commonly known as the curved malaxis, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

Malaxis curvatula is noted for its delicate appearance and is often found in low-light conditions.
