Myrcianthes irregularis

Myrcianthes irregularis, commonly known as the irregular myrtle, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces small, fleshy berries that are edible but not widely consumed by humans.

The plant is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
