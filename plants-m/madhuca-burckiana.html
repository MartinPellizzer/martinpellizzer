Madhuca burckiana

Madhuca burckiana, commonly known as the Burck's tallow tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color, followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds. It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in traditional medicine.

The tree also plays an important ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
