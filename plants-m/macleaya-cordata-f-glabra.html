Macleaya cordata f. glabra

Macleaya cordata f. glabra, commonly known as the smooth false hellebore, is a variant of the native Chinese plant Macleaya cordata.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its smooth, hairless stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of purple or pink during the summer months. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Due to its toxicity, it should be handled with care.
