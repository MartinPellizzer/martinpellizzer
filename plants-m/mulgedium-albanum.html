Mulgedium albanum

Mulgedium albanum, commonly known as the Albanian mulgedium, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks. The plant features slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. Its flowers are pale blue to violet, arranged in loose clusters, and bloom in the summer months.

Mulgedium albanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
