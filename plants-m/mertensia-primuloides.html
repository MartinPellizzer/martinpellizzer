Mertensia primuloides

Mertensia primuloides, commonly known as bluebells, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features striking blue to violet bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy and covered in a fine down. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

Mertensia primuloides is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
