Mammillaria tomentosa

Mammillaria tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in dense, white woolly hairs. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts.
