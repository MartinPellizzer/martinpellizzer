Monstera occidentalis

Monstera occidentalis, commonly known as the western monstera, is a tropical plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that develop distinctive fenestrations as they mature. The plant thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light. Its foliage is often used in ornamental horticulture for its aesthetic appeal.

Monstera occidentalis is a climbing plant that requires support structures to grow properly.
