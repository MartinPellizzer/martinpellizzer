Miconia ligustrina

Miconia ligustrina, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silvery hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Miconia ligustrina is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it an invasive species in some regions.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and potentially problematic in non-native habitats.
