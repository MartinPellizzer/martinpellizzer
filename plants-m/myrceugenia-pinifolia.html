Myrceugenia pinifolia

Myrceugenia pinifolia, commonly known as the coastal myrtle, is a species of flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils and salt spray. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a leathery texture, while its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds.
