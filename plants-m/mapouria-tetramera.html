Mapouria tetramera

Mapouria tetramera, commonly known as the African milk tree, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and simple, opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits. It is known for its milky sap, which is toxic and can cause skin irritation.

Mapouria tetramera is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
