Malus passipoma alba

Malus passipoma alba, commonly known as the white apple tree, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is characterized by its white flowers and edible fruits, which are similar to apples but smaller and more delicate. The tree is native to certain regions of Asia and prefers temperate climates with well-drained soil. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a smooth margin and a pale green underside.

The plant is valued for both its ornamental beauty and its fruit, which can be used in culinary applications.
