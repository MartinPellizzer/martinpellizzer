Mimosa velloziana f. hispida

Mimosa velloziana f. hispida, commonly known as the hairy mimosa, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its compound leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. Its stems are often woody and can grow up to several feet in height.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
