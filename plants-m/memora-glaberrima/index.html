Memora Glaberrima

Redstem filaree, scientifically known as Memora glaberrima, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. The plant is characterized by its delicate, reddish stems and tiny, white flowers that bloom in early spring. Despite its modest appearance, it plays an important role in supporting local insect populations. Its presence often indicates a healthy, undisturbed ecosystem.
