Miconia elaeagnoides

Miconia elaeagnoides, commonly known as silverleaf miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a silvery, powdery bloom, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Miconia elaeagnoides is noted for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, which can impact local ecosystems.
