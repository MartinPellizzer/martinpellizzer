Malva virgata

Malva virgata, commonly known as the velvetleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy pink or purple flowers. It thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Malva virgata is also known for its hairy stems and its ability to spread through both seeds and vegetative reproduction.
