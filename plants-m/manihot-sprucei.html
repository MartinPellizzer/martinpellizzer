Manihot sprucei

Manihot sprucei, commonly known as the African arrowroot, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary edible part. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in warm, humid climates. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy coating, while the flowers are small and inconspicuous.

The tubers are rich in starch and have been used traditionally for food and medicinal purposes.
