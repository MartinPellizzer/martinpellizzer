Mariacantha

Mariacantha, commonly known as the "moonflower of the Andes," is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red when ripe. Mariacantha is known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.

However, its berries contain toxic compounds and should not be consumed.
