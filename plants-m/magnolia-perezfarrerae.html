Magnolia perezfarrerae

Magnolia perezfarrerae, commonly known as the Mexican magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the highlands of Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, showy white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It produces woody capsules that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
