Myoxanthus aspasicensis subsp. arenicola

Myoxanthus aspasicensis subsp.

arenicola, commonly known as the sand-loving myoxanthus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions. It is characterized by its small, hairy leaves and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the spring.

The plant thrives in sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to anchor itself in loose, nutrient-poor substrates.

It plays a role in stabilizing dunes and preventing soil erosion in its native habitat.
