Mikania lindbergii

Mikania lindbergii, commonly known as the silver vine, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rapid growth and trailing habit. The plant features small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often accompanied by silver-white, velvety leaves. Mikania lindbergii is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to both shade and full sun.

It is also noted for its use in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant.
