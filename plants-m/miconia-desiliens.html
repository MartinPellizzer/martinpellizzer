Miconia desiliens

Miconia desiliens, commonly known as the silky miconia, is a member of the Melastomataceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height, characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers with five petals, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of branches. Its fruits are fleshy berries that turn red when mature, containing numerous small seeds.

Miconia desiliens is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments.
