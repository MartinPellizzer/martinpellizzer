Medicago sativa var. kochiana

Medicago sativa var. kochiana, commonly known as Koch's goatweed, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soils, making it a valuable forage crop. It also has a deep root system that helps in soil stabilization and nitrogen fixation.

This variety is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and environmental benefits.
