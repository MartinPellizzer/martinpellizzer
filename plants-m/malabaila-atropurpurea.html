Malabaila atropurpurea

Malabaila atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple-flowered malabaila, is a species of flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive purple flowers. The plant is known for its showy, clustered blossoms that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its seeds are encased in pods, a typical feature of the Fabaceae family, and the plant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant floral display.
