Mentha hirsuta var. origanoides

Mentha hirsuta var. origanoides, commonly known as woolly mint or origanoides mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features hairy, gray-green leaves that are lance-shaped and have a strong, minty aroma. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in whorled clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile addition to gardens.

Its aromatic properties make it useful in both culinary and medicinal applications.
