Moranda

Moranda, commonly known as the "flower of the desert," is a striking plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that help it store water, adapting to its harsh environment. Its blooming period typically occurs during the rainy season, making it a symbol of resilience.

Moranda is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
