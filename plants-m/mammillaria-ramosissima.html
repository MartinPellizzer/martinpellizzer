Mammillaria ramosissima

Mammillaria ramosissima, commonly known as the clustered milk barrel cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and is covered in white, woolly spines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.
