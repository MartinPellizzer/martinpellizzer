Melanix

Melanix, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Malvaceae.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces small, yellow flowers that are often clustered together. The leaves are typically ovate or elliptic in shape and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Melanix species are native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
