Melocactus argenteus var. tenuispina

Melocactus argenteus var. tenuispina, commonly known as the silver spined melocactus, is a cactus native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem that is covered in dense, white spines, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is adapted to thrive in hot, dry environments with minimal rainfall.

Its slow growth rate and unique appearance make it a popular choice for collectors and succulent enthusiasts.
