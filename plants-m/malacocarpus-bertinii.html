Malacocarpus bertinii

Malacocarpus bertinii, commonly known as the Madagascar star cactus, is a unique plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Cactaceae and is characterized by its distinctive star-shaped spines and thick, fleshy stems adapted for water storage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the rainy season, adding to its ornamental value. Its botanical features make it well-suited for arid environments, and it is often cultivated as a rare and exotic houseplant.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern in its native region.
