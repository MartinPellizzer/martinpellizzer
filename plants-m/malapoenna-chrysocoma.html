Malapoenna chrysocoma

Malapoenna chrysocoma, commonly known as the golden crown bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its leaves are typically small, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Malapoenna chrysocoma is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
