Maxillaria nutans

Maxillaria nutans, commonly known as the nodding maxillaria, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a yellow or greenish hue. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually elongated and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

Maxillaria nutans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
