Mammillaria saboae subsp. haudeana

Mammillaria saboae subsp.

haudeana, commonly known as the Mexican moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico. It is a small, globular plant with a distinctive ribbed structure and prominent areoles.

The plant features white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding ornamental value. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil with full sun exposure.

Due to its compact size and unique appearance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and container cultivation.
