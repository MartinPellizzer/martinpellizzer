Myrtus compacta

Myrtus compacta, commonly known as the common myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, dark green, glossy leaves that are aromatic when crushed. The plant produces tiny, white, five-petaled flowers in clusters, followed by small, dark blue berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Myrtus compacta is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in perfumery and medicine.
