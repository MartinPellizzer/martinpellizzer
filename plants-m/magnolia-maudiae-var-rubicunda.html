Magnolia maudiae var. rubicunda

Magnolia maudiae var. rubicunda, commonly known as the red magnolia, is a striking evergreen shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its large, showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The foliage consists of dark green, leathery leaves that remain on the plant throughout the year. This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for warm climate gardens.
