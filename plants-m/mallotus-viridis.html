Mallotus viridis

Mallotus viridis, commonly known as the green mallot, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by spherical, woody fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
