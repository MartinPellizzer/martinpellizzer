Montbretia fucata

Montbretia fucata, commonly known as the yellow oxalis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the genus Oxalis and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers with five petals. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hair. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Montbretia fucata is also known for its ability to spread rapidly through rhizomes, which can make it invasive in certain regions.
