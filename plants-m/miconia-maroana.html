Miconia maroana

Miconia maroana, commonly known as the Hawaiian miconia, is a tropical shrub native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that attract various pollinators. Its fruit is a fleshy, orange-red berry that contains numerous seeds.

Miconia maroana is often found in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and coastal areas.
