Maranta linkiana

Maranta linkiana, commonly known as the streaked arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Marantaceae family, which includes many ornamental plants known for their striking foliage. The plant features vibrant, variegated leaves with a distinctive pattern of green and silver markings. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often grown as a houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal.

Its rhizomes are also used in traditional medicine and as a source of starch.
