Maxillaria iguapensis

Maxillaria iguapensis, commonly known as the Iguape Orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, often epiphytic growth habit. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and surrounding substrate.

Maxillaria iguapensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
