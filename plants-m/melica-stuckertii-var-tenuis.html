Melica stuckertii var. tenuis

Melica stuckertii var. tenuis, commonly known as slender meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, marshes, and riverbanks. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are usually less than 2 mm wide, giving it a delicate appearance. Its inflorescence consists of a loose, spike-like arrangement of small spikelets.

This variety is often found in association with other grasses and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for invertebrates.
