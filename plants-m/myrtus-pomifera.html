Myrtus pomifera

Myrtus pomifera, commonly known as the common myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically red or black when mature. Myrtus pomifera is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for its essential oils, which are used in perfumery and traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
