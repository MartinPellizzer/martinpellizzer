Miconia cutucuensis

Miconia cutucuensis, commonly known as the Ecuadorian miconia, is a shrub or small tree native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are often accompanied by bright red bracts. It is typically found in moist, shaded habitats such as montane forests and cloud forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
