Malus oleosa

Malus oleosa, commonly known as the olive apple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, edible fruits that resemble olives. The plant produces white flowers in early spring, followed by dark purple berries in the fall. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a glossy surface and a serrated edge.

Malus oleosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in traditional medicine.
