Mammea gilletii

Mammea gilletii, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a tropical tree native to regions of Africa and the Indian Ocean.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its durability and resistance to decay. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually oval or elliptical in shape. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds.

Mammea gilletii is also noted for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and its role in traditional medicine.
