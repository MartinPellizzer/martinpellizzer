Micranthes fusca var. fusca

Micranthes fusca var. fusca, commonly known as the narrowleaf pearly everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woods or along stream banks.
