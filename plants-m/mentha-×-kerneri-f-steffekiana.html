Mentha × kerneri f. steffekiana

Mentha × kerneri f. steffekiana, commonly known as the German peppermint, is a hybrid mint species characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and strong menthol scent.

It is a sterile form that results from the cross between Mentha × piperita and Mentha rotundifolia, making it a naturally occurring hybrid. The plant typically grows as a compact shrub with square stems and opposite leaves that are hairy and fragrant. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This variety is often cultivated for its essential oils, which are used in aromatherapy and as a flavoring agent.
