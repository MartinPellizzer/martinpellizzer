Muscari chalusicum

Muscari chalusicum, commonly known as the Persian grape hyacinth, is a bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It produces dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, resembling tiny grapes. The plant typically blooms in early spring and is characterized by its grass-like foliage. It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Muscari chalusicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural displays.
