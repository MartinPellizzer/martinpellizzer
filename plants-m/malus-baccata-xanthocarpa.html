Malus baccata xanthocarpa

Malus baccata xanthocarpa, commonly known as the yellow crabapple, is a hybrid species that combines traits from Malus baccata and Malus xanthocarpa.

It is a deciduous tree or shrub native to China, characterized by its small, yellowish fruits that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces showy, white or pink flowers in spring, followed by the distinctive yellow fruits in autumn.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
