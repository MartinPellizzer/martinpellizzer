Mormodes pardina

Mormodes pardina, commonly known as the red-flowered orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety red petals and a distinctive yellow-green lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom during the rainy season, adding vibrant color to the forest canopy.

The botanical features of Mormodes pardina make it a visually appealing and ecologically significant component of its native habitat.
