Maxillaria pulla

Maxillaria pulla, commonly known as the pink lady's slipper orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking pink and white petals, which resemble a slipper, and its fragrant, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the soil.

Maxillaria pulla is prized for its ornamental value and is a popular species among orchid enthusiasts.
