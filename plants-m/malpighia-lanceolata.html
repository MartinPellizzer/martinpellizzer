Malpighia lanceolata

Malpighia lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf physalis, is a species of flowering plant in the family Malpighiaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of the Americas and is characterized by its showy, orange-red calyces that enclose the small, edible fruits. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are often consumed fresh or used in jams and beverages. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Malpighia lanceolata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant flowers and unique fruit structure.
