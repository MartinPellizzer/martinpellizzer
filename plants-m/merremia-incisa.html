Merremia incisa

Merremia incisa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are often lobed and have a distinctive waxy texture. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, round seeds.

Merremia incisa thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forests.
