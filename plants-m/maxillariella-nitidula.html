Maxillariella nitidula

Maxillariella nitidula, commonly known as the velvet orchid, is a small epiphytic orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features compact, dark green leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture, giving it its common name. The plant produces fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white and pink, often with a yellow throat. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining potting media.

Due to its ornamental value, it is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its delicate beauty and ease of cultivation.
