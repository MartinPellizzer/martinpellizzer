Mirabilis linearis f. subhispida

Mirabilis linearis f. subhispida, commonly known as the velvetleaf four o'clock, is a cultivar of the four o'clock plant characterized by its unique foliage.

It features elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense, velvety pubescence, giving it a soft, textured appearance. The flowers, which bloom in the late afternoon, are typically a striking shade of red or pink, with four petals that curl inward. This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

It is a perennial herb native to tropical regions and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability.
