Melaleuca bromelioides

Melaleuca bromelioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tea tree, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia, particularly the coastal regions of New South Wales and Queensland. The plant is characterized by its narrow, strap-like leaves and small, clustered white or pale pink flowers. It typically grows in coastal heathlands and is adapted to sandy soils and salty sea air.

Melaleuca bromelioides is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and hardy nature.
