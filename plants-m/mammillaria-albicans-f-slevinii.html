Mammillaria albicans f. slevinii

Mammillaria albicans f. slevinii, commonly known as the white-flowered mammillaria, is a succulent cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, white to pale green spines and small, rounded stems that form a compact, globular shape. The plant produces pale yellow flowers during the spring or early summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardiness, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
