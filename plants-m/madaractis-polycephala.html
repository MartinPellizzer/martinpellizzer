Madaractis polycephala

Madaractis polycephala, commonly known as the multi-headed madaractis, is a unique plant species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unusual growth pattern, where multiple stems emerge from a single base, creating a clustered appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are broad and slightly lobed, with a waxy texture that helps it retain moisture in its natural habitat.

Due to its rarity and distinctive morphology, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
