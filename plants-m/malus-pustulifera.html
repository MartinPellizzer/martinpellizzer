Malus pustulifera

Malus pustulifera, commonly known as the rough apple or pustulate apple, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its rough, pebbled bark and small, oval-shaped leaves. The tree produces white flowers in early spring, followed by small, yellowish fruits that are edible but not commonly cultivated for commercial use.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental features and is often grown in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
