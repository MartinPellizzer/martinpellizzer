Maxillaria pubigera

Maxillaria pubigera, commonly known as the hairy maxillaria, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial or epiphytic orchid, often found in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are borne on erect, slender spikes and are notable for their complex, spurred structure.

Maxillaria pubigera is appreciated by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and unique botanical features.
