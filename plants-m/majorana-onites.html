Majorana onites

Majorana onites, commonly known as sweet marjoram, is an aromatic herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its small, pale purple flowers and fragrant, slender leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30-60 cm and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. Its essential oils, rich in compounds like thymol and carvacrol, contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.

Majorana onites is often used in cooking and traditional herbal remedies for its soothing and antiseptic properties.
