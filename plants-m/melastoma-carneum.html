Melastoma carneum

Melastoma carneum, commonly known as the red melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its striking red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and undergrowth. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and often have a glossy appearance.

Melastoma carneum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
