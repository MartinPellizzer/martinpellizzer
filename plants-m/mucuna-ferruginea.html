Mucuna ferruginea

Mucuna ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty morning glory, is a leguminous vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant produces long, slender pods containing kidney-shaped seeds with a distinctive reddish-brown hue. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along forest edges.

Mucuna ferruginea is also noted for its rapid growth and ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in agricultural settings.
