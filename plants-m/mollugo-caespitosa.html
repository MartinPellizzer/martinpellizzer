Mollugo caespitosa

Mollugo caespitosa, commonly known as woolly mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, fleshy, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. Its hairy leaves and stems give it a distinctive appearance, and it is often used in ornamental gardening.

Mollugo caespitosa is also known for its ability to tolerate poor soil conditions and drought.
