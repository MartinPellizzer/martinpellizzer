Mentha × grintescui

Mentha × grintescui, commonly known as the hybrid mint, is a cross between spearmint (Mentha spicata) and watermint (Mentha aquatica).

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its square, hollow stems and opposite, serrated leaves. The plant produces small, pale purple flowers that bloom in whorls along the stem. Mentha × grintescui is widely cultivated for its aromatic leaves used in culinary and medicinal applications.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
