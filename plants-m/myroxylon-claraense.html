Myroxylon claraense

Myroxylon claraense, commonly known as the Brazilwood tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its dark reddish-brown heartwood, which has been historically valued for its use in dyeing and furniture-making. The tree can grow up to 30 meters tall, with smooth, gray bark and compound leaves. Its flowers are small and yellowish, often clustered in racemes, and it produces legume pods containing flat, winged seeds.

The plant is also noted for its ecological importance in providing habitat and contributing to soil stability in its native regions.
