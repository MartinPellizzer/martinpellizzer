Melastoma crispatum

Melastoma crispatum, commonly known as the hairy mallow, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, hairy leaves and clusters of pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that release seeds when mature.

Melastoma crispatum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
