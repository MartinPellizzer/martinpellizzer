Madia densifolia

Madia densifolia, commonly known as the thickleaf madia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its leaves are densely covered with fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance.

Madia densifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
