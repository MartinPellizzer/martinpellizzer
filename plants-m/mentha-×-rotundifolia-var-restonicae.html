Mentha × rotundifolia var. restonicae

Mentha × rotundifolia var. restonicae, commonly known as the narrow-leaf peppermint, is a hybrid plant in the mint family (Lamiaceae).

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and its ability to produce a strong, aromatic essential oil. This variety is native to parts of Europe and is often found in moist, shaded environments. The plant has a creeping growth habit and can form dense mats, making it a common weed in gardens and along waterways.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in herbal remedies and aromatherapy.
