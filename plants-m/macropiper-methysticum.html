Macropiper methysticum

Macropiper methysticum, commonly known as kava, is a perennial shrub native to the South Pacific islands.

It is characterized by its woody stems, heart-shaped leaves, and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant's roots contain a variety of bioactive compounds, including kavalactones, which are responsible for its sedative and anxiolytic effects. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated in tropical climates.

Due to its cultural and medicinal significance, it plays an important role in traditional Polynesian societies.
