Matricaria alpina

Matricaria alpina, commonly known as the alpine chamomile, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like white flowers with a yellow center. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky areas, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are finely divided and aromatic, while the flowers contain essential oils that contribute to its medicinal properties.

Matricaria alpina is often used in herbal remedies for its calming and anti-inflammatory effects.
