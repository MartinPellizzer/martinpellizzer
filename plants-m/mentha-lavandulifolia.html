Mentha lavandulifolia

Mentha lavandulifolia, commonly known as lavender mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from purple to white, often resembling those of lavender. Mentha lavandulifolia is valued for its aromatic properties and is used in herbal medicine and as a culinary herb.

Its leaves are often used to make teas and are also employed in aromatherapy for their calming effects.
