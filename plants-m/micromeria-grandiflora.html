Micromeria grandiflora

Micromeria grandiflora, commonly known as the desert honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, featuring small, narrow leaves and bright yellow flowers. The plant has a compact growth habit and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Micromeria grandiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
