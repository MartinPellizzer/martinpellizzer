Melaleuca rosea

Melaleuca rosea, commonly known as the pink honey myrtle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, woody fruits known as capsules. It is often found in coastal regions and is adapted to sandy soils.

Melaleuca rosea is valued for its ornamental appeal and its use in traditional Aboriginal medicine.
