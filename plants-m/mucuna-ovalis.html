Mucuna ovalis

Mucuna ovalis, commonly known as the hairy morning glory, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces elongated, hairy pods containing kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along forest edges.

Mucuna ovalis is notable for its rapid growth and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
