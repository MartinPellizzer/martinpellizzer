Malus fragrans

Malus fragrans, commonly known as the fragrant apple or sweet apple, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to China and is characterized by its ornamental value due to its showy white flowers and fragrant scent. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are ovate with a smooth margin, and the fruit is a small, sweet apple with a thin skin.

The plant is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is also used in traditional Chinese medicine.
