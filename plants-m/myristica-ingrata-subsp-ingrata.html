Myristica ingrata subsp. ingrata

Myristica ingrata subsp.

ingrata, commonly known as the fragrant nutmeg tree, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia. It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in tropical rainforests.

The plant is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a round, yellowish drupe that contains a single seed, which is used to produce the spice nutmeg.

The plant is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used traditionally in both culinary and medicinal applications.
