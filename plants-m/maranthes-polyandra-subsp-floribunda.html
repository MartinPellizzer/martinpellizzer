Maranthes polyandra subsp. floribunda

Maranthes polyandra subsp.

floribunda, commonly known as the Cape marigold, is a flowering plant native to South Africa. It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and prefers full sun exposure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and drought tolerance.
