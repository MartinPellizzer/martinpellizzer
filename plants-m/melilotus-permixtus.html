Melilotus permixtus

Melilotus permixtus, commonly known as the sweet clover, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spherical clusters. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been introduced to other regions, including North America. It has a deep root system that helps in nitrogen fixation, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

Melilotus permixtus is often used in agriculture as a forage crop and in ecological restoration projects.
