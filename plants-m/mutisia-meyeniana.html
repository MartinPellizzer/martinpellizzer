Mutisia meyeniana

Mutisia meyeniana, commonly known as the yellow mutisia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

The leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a hairy texture.
