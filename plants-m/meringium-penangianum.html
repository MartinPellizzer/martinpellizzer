Meringium penangianum

Meringium penangianum, commonly known as the Penang meringue plant, is a unique flowering species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like leaves and clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers. The plant produces a distinctive, gelatinous fruit that contains a single, edible seed. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in dense undergrowth of tropical forests.

Due to its rare occurrence and ornamental value, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
