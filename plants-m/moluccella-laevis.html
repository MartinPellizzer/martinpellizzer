Moluccella laevis

Moluccella laevis, commonly known as the smooth bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Moluccella laevis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is also studied for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
