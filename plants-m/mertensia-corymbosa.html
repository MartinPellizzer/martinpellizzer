Mertensia corymbosa

Mertensia corymbosa, commonly known as Virginia bluebell, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale blue to violet, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often fuzzy to the touch. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Mertensia corymbosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
