Metapolypodium kingpingense

Metapolypodium kingpingense, commonly known as the Kingping fern, is a rare and unusual member of the Polypodiaceae family.

It is characterized by its unique morphology, featuring a short, thick rhizome and clustered, leathery fronds. The plant produces sporangia on the undersides of its leaves, typical of ferns, but its growth habit is more shrub-like than traditional ferns. It is native to a limited geographic region in China, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its rarity and distinctive appearance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
