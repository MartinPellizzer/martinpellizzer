Myrcia queimadensis

Myrcia queimadensis, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but not widely consumed by humans. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Myrcia queimadensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
