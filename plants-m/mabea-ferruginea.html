Mabea ferruginea

Mabea ferruginea, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dark reddish-brown wood, which is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic appeal. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate or elliptical in shape. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually yellow or greenish-yellow.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its propagation in the wild.
