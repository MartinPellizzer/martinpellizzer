Mentha longifolia f. scytina

Mentha longifolia f. scytina, commonly known as woolly mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage.

It produces small, pale blue or violet flowers arranged in whorled clusters along the stems. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in rocky or sandy soils. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its minty scent and flavor.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and use in herbal medicine.
