Monotoca rotundifolia

Monotoca rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leafed monotoca, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, rounded leaves and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, adapting well to aquatic environments. It plays a role in supporting local biodiversity by providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in some regions.
