Machaerium oblongifolium

Machaerium oblongifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaf machaerium, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves with elongated, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and forest understories.

Machaerium oblongifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential ecological benefits.
