Mallotus floribundus var. cordifolius

Mallotus floribundus var. cordifolius, commonly known as the heart-leaved mallot, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub characterized by its heart-shaped leaves. The leaves are typically glossy and dark green, with a prominent midrib. It produces small, clustered white flowers followed by woody capsules containing several seeds.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
