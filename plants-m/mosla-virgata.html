Mosla virgata

Mosla virgata, commonly known as white bugloss, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb with upright, branched stems that are often hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Mosla virgata is often found in rocky or dry soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
