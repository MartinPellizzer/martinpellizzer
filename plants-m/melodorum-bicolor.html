Melodorum bicolor

Melodorum bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple with a contrasting white or yellow throat. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along its stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its unique coloration and ornamental value make it a sought-after plant among horticulturists and garden enthusiasts.
