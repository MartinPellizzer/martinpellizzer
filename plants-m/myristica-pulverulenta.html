Myristica pulverulenta

Myristica pulverulenta, commonly known as the black pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the wet forests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and small, clustered white flowers. The plant produces berries that turn from green to black as they mature, containing the aromatic seeds used as a spice. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a dense, branching structure.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.
