Melissa macrostema

Melissa macrostema, commonly known as giant lemon balm, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, serrated leaves and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs like mint and basil. Its leaves are often used in herbal teas due to their calming properties.

Melissa macrostema thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
