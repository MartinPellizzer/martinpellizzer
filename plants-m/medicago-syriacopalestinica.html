Medicago syriacopalestinica

Medicago syriacopalestinica, commonly known as the Syro-Palestinian medic, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of the Mediterranean.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought conditions. It has a deep root system that allows it to access water from deeper soil layers, enhancing its survival in challenging climates.

Additionally, it plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitats.
