Medicago hispida f. longeaculeata

Medicago hispida f. longeaculeata, commonly known as the long-aculeate goat's rue, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its hairy, erect stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are typically arranged in racemes, which bloom in early summer. Its seeds are enclosed in legumes, a common feature among legumes, and are often used in traditional herbal medicine.

This variety is adapted to grow in dry, disturbed soils and is found in various regions across Europe and parts of Asia.
