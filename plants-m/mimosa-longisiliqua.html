Mimosa longisiliqua

Mimosa longisiliqua, commonly known as the silky mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy inflorescences. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in spherical heads, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are bipinnate, meaning they are divided into multiple pairs of leaflets, contributing to its ornamental value.

Mimosa longisiliqua is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
