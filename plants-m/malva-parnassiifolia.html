Malva parnassiifolia

Malva parnassiifolia, commonly known as the Chinese mallow or the sacred lotus, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
