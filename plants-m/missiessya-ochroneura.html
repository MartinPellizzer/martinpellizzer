Missiessya ochroneura

Missiessya ochroneura, commonly known as the white-tipped orchid, is a species of orchid native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are long and narrow, often with a waxy coating that helps retain moisture.

The orchid's unique floral structure and coloration make it a notable species in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
