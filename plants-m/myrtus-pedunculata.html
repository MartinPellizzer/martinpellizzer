Myrtus pedunculata

Myrtus pedunculata, commonly known as the common myrtle, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically red or black when mature. Myrtus pedunculata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a long history of use in traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.
