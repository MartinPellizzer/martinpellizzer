Muscari curtum

Muscari curtum, commonly known as the Persian squill, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and western Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllis family (Amaryllidaceae) and is characterized by its dense clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant produces small, round bulbs that are often used in ornamental gardening for their winter blooms. Muscari curtum thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and woodland settings.

Its botanical features include strap-like leaves and a compact growth habit that contributes to its ornamental appeal.
