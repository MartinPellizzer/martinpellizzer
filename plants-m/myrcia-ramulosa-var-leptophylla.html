Myrcia ramulosa var. leptophylla

Myrcia ramulosa var. leptophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf myrcia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and small, fragrant white or pink flowers. It often grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests or along riverbanks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
