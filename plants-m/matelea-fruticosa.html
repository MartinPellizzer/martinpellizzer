Matelea fruticosa

Matelea fruticosa, commonly known as the desert broom, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, featuring woody stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-white flowers during the spring and summer months, which are attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is adapted to withstand drought, making it a resilient species in harsh conditions.

The plant's structure and growth habits reflect its evolutionary adaptation to survive in nutrient-poor, dry soils.
