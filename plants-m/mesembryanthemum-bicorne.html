Mesembryanthemum bicorne

Mesembryanthemum bicorne, commonly known as the horned iceplant, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, water-storing leaves and distinctive horn-shaped structures at the base of its flowers. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptation to drought conditions. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, bloom in the morning and close by midday.

Mesembryanthemum bicorne is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
