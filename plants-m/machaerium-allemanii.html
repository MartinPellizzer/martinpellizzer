Machaerium allemanii

Machaerium allemanii, commonly known as the blackwood or false acacia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and often leguminous fruits. The plant features pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and its flowers are typically clustered in dense, terminal inflorescences. Machaerium allemanii produces pods that contain seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

It is valued for its hard, durable wood and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
