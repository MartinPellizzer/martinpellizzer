Mimosa longistipula

Mimosa longistipula, commonly known as the long-stippled mimosa, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant has a spreading growth habit and produces small, bean-like pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or sandy environments.

Mimosa longistipula is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed areas.
