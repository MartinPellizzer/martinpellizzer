Monarda citriodora subsp. austromontana

Monarda citriodora subsp.

austromontana, commonly known as lemon bee balm, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States. It features upright, branched stems covered in aromatic, hairy foliage.

The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of pink to red, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are fragrant and have a citrus-like scent, contributing to its ornamental value.

This subspecies is adapted to moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.
