Marrubium vulgare var. caucasicum

Marrubium vulgare var. caucasicum, commonly known as Caucasian horehound, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It features gray-green, hairy leaves that are lance-shaped and arranged oppositely along the stem. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous areas.

Its medicinal properties have been historically valued for their anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic effects.
