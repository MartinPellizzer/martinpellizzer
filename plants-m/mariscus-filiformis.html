Mariscus filiformis

Mariscus filiformis, commonly known as the slender marsh plant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy environments.

It features slender, thread-like stems that grow close to the ground, often forming dense mats. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers during the summer months, which are clustered in spikes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, adapted to thrive in water-saturated soils.

Mariscus filiformis plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems by preventing soil erosion.
