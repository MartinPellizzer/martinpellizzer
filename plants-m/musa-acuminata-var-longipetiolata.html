Musa acuminata var. longipetiolata

Musa acuminata var. longipetiolata, commonly known as the long-petioled banana, is a variant of the banana plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that have long petioles, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces large, clustered inflorescences that eventually develop into edible bananas. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

This variety is primarily cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of fruit in certain regions.
