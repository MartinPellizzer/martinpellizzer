Mulguraea tridens

Mulguraea tridens, commonly known as the mulga shrub, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its small, dense, and often spiny foliage, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing several seeds. Mulguraea tridens is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and low precipitation, making it a resilient component of the Australian flora.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
