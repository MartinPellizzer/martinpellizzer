Michelia longistyla

Michelia longistyla, commonly known as the fragrant magnolia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the family Magnoliaceae and is characterized by its showy, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape and often have a fragrant scent. Its flowers are typically borne in clusters and are known for their ornamental value in gardens.

Michelia longistyla is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to partial shade.
