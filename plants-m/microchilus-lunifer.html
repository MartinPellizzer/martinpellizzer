Microchilus lunifer

Microchilus lunifer, commonly known as the moon-faced orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, intricate flowers that resemble miniature faces, with a unique white and green coloration. The plant typically grows in rocky, humid environments and requires specific conditions to thrive. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces a single, showy bloom each year.

Due to its endangered status, it is often cultivated in specialized botanical gardens.
