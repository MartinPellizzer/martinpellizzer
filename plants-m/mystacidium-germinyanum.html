Mystacidium germinyanum

Mystacidium germinyanum, commonly known as the desert star, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features a short, thick stem with numerous fleshy, star-shaped leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in dry conditions. The leaves are typically green with a waxy coating, reducing water loss through transpiration. It produces small, yellowish flowers during the spring or early summer, adding a striking visual contrast to its foliage.

This plant is well adapted to rocky soils and full sun exposure, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.
