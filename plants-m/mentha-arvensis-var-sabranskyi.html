Mentha arvensis var. sabranskyi

Mentha arvensis var. sabranskyi, commonly known as the Siberian mint, is a perennial herb native to the cold regions of Asia.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, serrated leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in whorled clusters during the summer months. It is characterized by its strong, minty aroma and is often found in moist, rocky soils.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
