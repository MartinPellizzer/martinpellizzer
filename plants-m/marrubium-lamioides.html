Marrubium lamioides

Marrubium lamioides, commonly known as common horehound, is a perennial herb native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and clusters of small, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in grasslands and wastelands. It has a strong, aromatic scent and is known for its medicinal properties.

Marrubium lamioides is used in traditional medicine for its expectorant and anti-inflammatory effects.
