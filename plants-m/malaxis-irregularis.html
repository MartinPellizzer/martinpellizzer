Malaxis irregularis

Malaxis irregularis, commonly known as the irregular orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its unique, irregularly shaped flowers. Native to Southeast Asia, this plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments. The flowers are usually greenish-yellow with a distinctive lip that is broader than the other petals.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
