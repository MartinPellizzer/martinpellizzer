Mamei

Mamei, commonly known as the red sandalwood tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark red wood that is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic appeal. The plant has a straight, tall trunk with smooth, greyish-brown bark. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and oblong, often with a glossy surface.

Mamei trees can grow up to 30 meters in height and are often found in lowland evergreen forests.
