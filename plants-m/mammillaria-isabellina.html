Mammillaria isabellina

Mammillaria isabellina, commonly known as the Isabellina Pincushion Cactus, is a small, spineless cactus native to Mexico.

It features a globular stem covered in white, woolly hair that gives it a soft, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by small, red fruits. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its compact size and unique appearance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cultivation.
