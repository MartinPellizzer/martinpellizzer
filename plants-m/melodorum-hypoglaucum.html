Melodorum hypoglaucum

Melodorum hypoglaucum, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, sky-blue to white flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and slightly fuzzy to the touch. It thrives in partial to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Melodorum hypoglaucum is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
