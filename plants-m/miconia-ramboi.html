Miconia ramboi

Miconia ramboi, commonly known as the red miconia, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, showy leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. Miconia ramboi is also notable for producing large, woody fruits that contain numerous seeds, contributing to its invasive potential in some regions.
