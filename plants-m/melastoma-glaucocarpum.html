Melastoma glaucocarpum

Melastoma glaucocarpum, commonly known as the silver fruit melastoma, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, oval leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in the spring. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Melastoma glaucocarpum is often found in moist, shaded forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
