Macrosphyra brachystylis

Macrosphyra brachystylis, commonly known as the narrow-stemmed rock daisy, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and thick, fleshy stems that store water, aiding in its survival in arid environments. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically blooming during the warmer months. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, adapted to reduce water loss.

Macrosphyra brachystylis is often found growing in rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
