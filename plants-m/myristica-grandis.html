Myristica grandis

Myristica grandis, commonly known as the nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Banda Islands in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is closely related to the species used for producing nutmeg and mace. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall and has large, leathery leaves that are dark green on the upper surface. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, while its fruit is a fleshy, orange-red drupe that contains the aromatic seeds used as spices.

The plant is valued for its essential oils and has economic significance in the spice trade.
