Malaxis aurata

Malaxis aurata, commonly known as the golden malaxis, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its delicate, linear leaves and striking, yellowish-green inflorescences. The plant produces small, resupinate flowers with prominent, spurred sepals and a distinctive lip. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some horticultural collections.
