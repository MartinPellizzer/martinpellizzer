Mimosa meticulosa var. microphylla

Mimosa meticulosa var. microphylla, commonly known as the dwarf mimosa, is a low-growing shrub native to South America.

It features small, fern-like leaves that are finely divided and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces fluffy, yellowish-pink flower clusters that bloom in spring. It is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its rapid growth and delicate appearance make it a popular choice for ornamental use in temperate climates.
