Micromelum molle

Micromelum molle, commonly known as the silky tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and opposite, simple leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

The species is also valued for its ability to tolerate poor soil conditions and its role in ecological restoration projects.
