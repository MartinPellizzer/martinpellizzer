Miconia viridiflava

Miconia viridiflava, commonly known as the green-flowered miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are usually dark green with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that are often overlooked due to their inconspicuous appearance. Miconia viridiflava is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments.

However, it is also considered an invasive species in some regions due to its aggressive spread and ecological impact.
