Myriophyllum costatum

Myriophyllum costatum, commonly known as water milfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to North America.

It has finely divided, feather-like leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, green flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and lakes.

Myriophyllum costatum plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
