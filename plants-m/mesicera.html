Mesicera

Mesicera, commonly known as the silver leaf fern, is a tropical plant characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that resemble a feathered crown.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments, making it well-suited for humid climates and indoor cultivation. The plant's leaves are typically covered in a fine, silvery layer of trichomes, which help reduce water loss and reflect sunlight. Mesicera belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to various regions in Asia and the Pacific.

Its unique appearance and air-purifying qualities make it a popular choice for ornamental and indoor gardening.
