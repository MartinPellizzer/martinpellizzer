Milligania

Milligania, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to tropical Africa.

These plants are characterized by their fleshy, thick leaves and showy, often velvety flowers that come in a variety of colors. Milligania species typically grow as shrubs or small trees, with some reaching heights of up to 10 meters. The leaves are usually arranged in a rosette pattern, and the flowers are borne on long, slender stalks.

They are popular in horticulture for their ornamental value and ability to thrive in indoor environments.
