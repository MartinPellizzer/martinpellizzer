Mimosa longipinna

Mimosa longipinna, commonly known as the long-needle mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fluffy flower heads. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are bipinnate, meaning they are divided into smaller leaflets, giving it a delicate, feathery appearance.

Mimosa longipinna is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and floral display.
