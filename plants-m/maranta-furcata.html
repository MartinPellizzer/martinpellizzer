Maranta furcata

Maranta furcata, commonly known as the zigzag plant, is a tropical species native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, arrow-shaped leaves that exhibit a unique zigzag pattern along their veins. The plant produces small, white flowers that are typically hidden among its foliage. Maranta furcata thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, rich soil.

It is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its ornamental leaf structure.
