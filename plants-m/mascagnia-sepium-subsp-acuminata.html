Mascagnia sepium subsp. acuminata

Mascagnia sepium subsp.

acuminata, commonly known as the acuminata mescal bean, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the spring.

The plant produces long, slender pods containing seeds that are used in traditional medicine. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and ethnobotanical contexts.
