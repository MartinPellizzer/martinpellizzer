Medinilla fasciculiflora

Medinilla fasciculiflora, commonly known as the clustered medinilla, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible and contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Medinilla fasciculiflora is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
