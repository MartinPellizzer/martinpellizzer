Malus × brevipes

Malus × brevipes, commonly known as the dwarf apple tree, is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, ornamental fruit that resembles miniature apples. The plant typically grows to a height of 2–3 meters, making it suitable for small gardens or container cultivation. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and it produces fragrant white flowers in early spring.

The tree is valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
