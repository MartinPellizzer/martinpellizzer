Margaretta rosea subsp. whytei

Margaretta rosea subsp.

whytei, commonly known as the Whyte's margaret rose, is a flowering plant native to South Africa. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its showy, double pink to white blooms.

The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and elliptical, dark green leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
