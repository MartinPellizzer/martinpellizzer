Mikania houstoniana f. normalis

Mikania houstoniana f. normalis, commonly known as the Houstonian vine, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils or along stream banks.
