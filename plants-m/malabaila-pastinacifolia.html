Malabaila pastinacifolia

Malabaila pastinacifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf arrowhead, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically alternate and have a distinct arrowhead shape, with a rough texture. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its root system is fibrous and adapted for moist, well-drained soils.

It is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of streams.
