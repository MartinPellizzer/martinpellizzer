Mimulus paniculatus

Mimulus paniculatus, commonly known as the yellow monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils along streambanks and in open woodlands. It has heart-shaped leaves and a trailing growth habit, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Mimulus paniculatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinator populations.
