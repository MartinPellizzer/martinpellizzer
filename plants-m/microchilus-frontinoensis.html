Microchilus frontinoensis

Microchilus frontinoensis, commonly known as the Frontinoensis Begonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive foliage and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant typically features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, colorful bracts that surround its true flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal.

Microchilus frontinoensis requires well-draining soil and consistent moisture to flourish.
