Micropeplis arachnoidea

Micropeplis arachnoidea, commonly known as the spider orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its unique, spider-like appearance, with long, slender sepals and petals that resemble the legs of a spider. Native to the tropical regions of South America, this plant thrives in humid, shaded environments. The flowers are typically white or pale green, with a distinct, intricate structure that aids in pollination by specific insect species.

It is a relatively rare and delicate plant, often found in moist forest understories.
