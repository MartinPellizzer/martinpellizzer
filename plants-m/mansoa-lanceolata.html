Mansoa lanceolata

Mansoa lanceolata, commonly known as the velvetleaf or purpleleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often a deep green with a purple tinge. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a notable ornamental plant in horticulture.
