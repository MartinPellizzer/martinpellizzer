Mesosphaerum barbatum

Mesosphaerum barbatum, commonly known as the beardleaf tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine, hair-like structures, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens in warmer climates.
