Minasia

Minasia, commonly known as the common thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its aromatic, small, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of pink or white flowers. The plant is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, and it thrives in well-drained soils and sunny locations. Minasia is widely used in culinary applications for its distinctive flavor and also has medicinal properties.

Its essential oils contain compounds like thymol, which contribute to its antimicrobial and antifungal qualities.
