Melica aristata

Melica aristata, commonly known as the mountain brome, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments. The plant has slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowers are usually pale green to brown, and it is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.

Melica aristata is adapted to cold climates and is an important component of high-altitude ecosystems.
