Mephitidia tashiroi

Mephitidia tashiroi, commonly known as the Tashiro's ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic flowering plant native to the cloud forests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, pale pink or white petals and a unique, elongated spur that extends from its flower. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees without deriving nutrients from them. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires specific conditions to bloom.

Due to habitat loss and its elusive nature, Mephitidia tashiroi is considered critically endangered.
