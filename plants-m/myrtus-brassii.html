Myrtus brassii

Myrtus brassii, commonly known as the Mediterranean myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, dark green leaves and small white flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruits are small, berry-like structures that mature to a dark blue color.

Myrtus brassii is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental and aromatic properties.
