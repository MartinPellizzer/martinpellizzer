Macrotyloma geocarpum var. geocarpum

Macrotyloma geocarpum var. geocarpum, commonly known as the subterranean clover, is a leguminous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its unique ability to produce edible tubers underground, which are rich in nutrients. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland ecosystems.

Due to its nutritional value, it has been studied for potential agricultural and medicinal uses.
