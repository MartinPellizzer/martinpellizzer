Mariscus wichurae

Mariscus wichurae, commonly known as the orchid vine, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant features long, slender stems that twine around supports, and its leaves are typically lance-shaped with a waxy surface. Mariscus wichurae produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom throughout the growing season.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and unique growth pattern.
