Marsdenia verrucosa

Marsdenia verrucosa, commonly known as the rough-stemmed Marsdenia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Apocynaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its woody stems covered in small, wart-like bumps. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Its unique botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and botanical research.
