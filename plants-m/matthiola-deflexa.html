Matthiola deflexa

Matthiola deflexa, commonly known as the bent-thorn wallflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. Its leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, and it often grows in rocky or disturbed soils.

Matthiola deflexa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
