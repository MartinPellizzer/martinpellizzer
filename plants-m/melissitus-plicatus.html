Melissitus plicatus

Melissitus plicatus, commonly known as the zigzag orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to the tropical regions of South America.

This species is characterized by its unique, undulating leaf structure that gives it its common name. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, often with intricate patterns. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Melissitus plicatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
