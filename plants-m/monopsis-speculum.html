Monopsis speculum

Monopsis speculum, commonly known as the mirror orchid, is a rare and striking flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, mirror-like petals that reflect light in a way that mimics the surrounding environment. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, humid climates. Its flowers are usually white or pale blue, with a complex structure that aids in pollination by specific insect species.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, Monopsis speculum is considered endangered and is of great interest to botanists and conservationists.
