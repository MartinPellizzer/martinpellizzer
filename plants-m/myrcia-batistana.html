Myrcia batistana

Myrcia batistana, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a small, red drupe that contains a single seed, and it is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and aromatic qualities.
