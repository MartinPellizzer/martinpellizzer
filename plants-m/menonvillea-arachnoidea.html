Menonvillea arachnoidea

Menonvillea arachnoidea, commonly known as the spider-tendril orchid, is a rare and striking epiphytic orchid native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its unique, hairy, spider-like tendrils that help it climb and anchor to tree bark. The plant produces small, pale greenish-white flowers with a distinct, intricate structure. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires high air humidity and indirect light for optimal growth.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern.
