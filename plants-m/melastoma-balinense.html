Melastoma balinense

Melastoma balinense, commonly known as the Balinese melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia.

It belongs to the family Melastomataceae and is characterized by its attractive, showy flowers that are typically pink or red. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds, aiding in its dispersal.
