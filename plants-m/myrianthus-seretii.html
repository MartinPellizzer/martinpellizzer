Myrianthus seretii

Myrianthus seretii, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, orange-red flowers. The plant has a distinctive trunk with a flaky, peeling bark that reveals layers of older bark beneath.

Its fruit is a legume pod that splits open to release seeds, and it plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife.
