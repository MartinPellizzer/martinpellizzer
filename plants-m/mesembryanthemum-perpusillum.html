Mesembryanthemum perpusillum

Mesembryanthemum perpusillum, commonly known as the ice plant, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, often hairy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, white, and pink, typically blooming in summer. Its ability to survive drought conditions makes it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and low maintenance requirements.
