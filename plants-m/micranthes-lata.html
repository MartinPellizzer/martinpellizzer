Micranthes lata

Micranthes lata, commonly known as narrowleaf mouse-ear, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features small, hairy leaves that are lance-shaped and often clustered at the base. Its flowers are tiny, clustered in dense heads, and usually have five petals.

It is part of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
