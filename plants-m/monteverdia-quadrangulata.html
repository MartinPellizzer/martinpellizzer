Monteverdia quadrangulata

Monteverdia quadrangulata, commonly known as the quadrangular monteverdia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive quadrangular stems, which are often covered with fine hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters at the leaf axils. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly serrated margin.

Monteverdia quadrangulata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in moist, shaded environments.
