Mussaenda villosa var. herveyana

Mussaenda villosa var. herveyana, commonly known as the Hervey's Mussaenda, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and broad, ovate leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
