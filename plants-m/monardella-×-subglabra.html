Monardella × subglabra

Monardella × subglabra, commonly known as pale blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is a hybrid species within the Monardella genus, characterized by its slender, hairy stems and clusters of pale blue to lavender flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to dry meadows, woodlands, and along streambanks. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and often have a minty scent when crushed.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
