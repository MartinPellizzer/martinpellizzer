Melocactus onychacanthus

Melocactus onychacanthus, commonly known as the black-tipped melon cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that are often covered in a dark, almost black, waxy coating. The plant produces striking yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, adding a dramatic visual contrast to its dark foliage. It is well adapted to harsh desert conditions, storing water in its fleshy stems and having spines that provide shade and protection.

Due to its unique appearance and resilience, it is a popular choice for collectors and xerophytic gardens.
