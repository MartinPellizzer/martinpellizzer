Macella

Macella, commonly known as the "sugar cane" or *Saccharum officinarum*, is a tall, perennial grass native to the tropical regions of South and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, fibrous stalks that contain a high concentration of sucrose, making it a vital source of sugar production. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny environments and requires a warm climate with abundant rainfall. Its leaves are broad and sword-shaped, while its inflorescence consists of dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers.

Macella plays a significant role in both agriculture and traditional medicine across many cultures.
