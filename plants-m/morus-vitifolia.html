Morus vitifolia

Morus vitifolia, commonly known as the grape-leaved mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to southeastern Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often lobed and have a silvery underside. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically eaten by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
