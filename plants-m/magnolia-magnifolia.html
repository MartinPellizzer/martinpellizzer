Magnolia magnifolia

Magnolia magnifolia, commonly known as the Chinese magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its fragrant blooms.
