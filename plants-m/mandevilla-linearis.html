Mandevilla linearis

Mandevilla linearis, commonly known as the linear mandevilla, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its long, narrow leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces clusters of bright red or orange blooms, which attract pollinators like butterflies and hummingbirds. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-draining soil and full sun to partial shade.

Mandevilla linearis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant flowers and vigorous growth habit.
