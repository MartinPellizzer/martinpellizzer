Miconia subpeltata

Miconia subpeltata, commonly known as the Brazilian starfruit or velvetleaf, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers, which are followed by distinctive, star-shaped fruits that are yellow when ripe. Miconia subpeltata is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, making it an invasive species in some regions.

Its botanical features make it a significant plant in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
