Melica nutans subsp. uniflora

Melica nutans subsp.

uniflora, commonly known as the nodding milletgrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that often nod towards the ground.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands, preferring temperate climates. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth surface, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This subspecies is often found in regions of Europe and western Asia, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
