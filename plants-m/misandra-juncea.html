Misandra juncea

Misandra juncea, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Misandra juncea is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
