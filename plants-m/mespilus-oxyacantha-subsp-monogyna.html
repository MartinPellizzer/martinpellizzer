Mespilus oxyacantha subsp. monogyna

Mespilus oxyacantha subsp.

monogyna, commonly known as the white sloe, is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its small, white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by dark purple berries in autumn.

The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces a fragrant, edible fruit used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

The subspecies is distinguished by its monogynous flowers, which have only one pistil.
