Myrtus candollei

Myrtus candollei, commonly known as the Canary Island myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a Mediterranean climate with mild winters and warm, dry summers.

Myrtus candollei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
