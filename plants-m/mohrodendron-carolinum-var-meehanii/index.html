Mohrodendron Carolinum Var. Meehanii

Carolina beautyberry, scientifically known as Mohrodendron carolinum var. meehanii, is a striking native plant of the southeastern United States. It is celebrated for its vibrant purple berries that appear in late summer and persist through winter. This shrub thrives in woodland and shaded areas, often forming dense thickets that support local wildlife. Its unique appearance and ecological value make it a popular choice for native plant gardens. Despite its beauty, it remains relatively underappreciated in mainstream horticulture.
