Mimosa glaberrima

Mimosa glaberrima, commonly known as the smooth mimosa, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and spherical, fluffy seed pods. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it adaptable to different environments. Its flowers are typically small and yellow, appearing in clusters, and it produces a distinctive odor when touched.

Mimosa glaberrima is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility.
