Machaonia velutina

Machaonia velutina, commonly known as the velvetleaf mahonia, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, with dark green, leathery leaves that have a soft, velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers in the spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for garden cultivation in temperate climates.

Machaonia velutina is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
