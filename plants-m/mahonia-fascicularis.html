Mahonia fascicularis

Mahonia fascicularis, commonly known as the western evergreen holly, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It features dense, spiny foliage with dark green leaves that have a distinctive yellow midrib. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers in late spring, which are followed by blue-black berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial shade, making it a popular choice for landscaping.

Its evergreen nature provides year-round structure in gardens and natural habitats.
