Micranthus spicatus

Micranthus spicatus, commonly known as the spikeflower or narrowleaf micranthus, is a flowering plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, and the flowers are usually greenish-white in color.

Micranthus spicatus is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in full sun.
