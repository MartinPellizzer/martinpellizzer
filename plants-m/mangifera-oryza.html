Mangifera oryza

Mangifera oryza, commonly known as the rice mango, is a tropical fruit-bearing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, elongated fruits with a smooth, yellowish-orange rind and a sweet, aromatic pulp. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in panicles, and its leaves are broad, glossy, and deeply veined. Mangifera oryza thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It is primarily cultivated for its edible fruit, which is valued for its unique flavor and nutritional content.
