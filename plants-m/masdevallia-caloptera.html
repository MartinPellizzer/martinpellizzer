Masdevallia caloptera

Masdevallia caloptera, commonly known as the Orchid of the Andes, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often colorful flowers that display a wide range of hues, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees without deriving nutrients from them. Its leaves are usually elongated and fleshy, adapted to store water in its natural habitat.

Masdevallia caloptera is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and unique floral structure.
