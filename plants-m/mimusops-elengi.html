Mimusops elengi

Mimusops elengi, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa and parts of Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub that can grow up to 10 meters tall. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles, often blooming in spring. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture with a distinctive elliptical shape.

The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.
