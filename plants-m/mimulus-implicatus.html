Mimulus implicatus

Mimulus implicatus, commonly known as the yellow monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. It has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Mimulus implicatus is a perennial herb that plays an important role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
