Medinilla vexillifera

Medinilla vexillifera, commonly known as the flag-flowered medinilla, is a tropical flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental, pendulous flowers that resemble small flags. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are dark green, glossy, and elliptical in shape, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral display.

Medinilla vexillifera is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and as a potted plant.
