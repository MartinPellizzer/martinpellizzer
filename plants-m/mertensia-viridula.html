Mertensia viridula

Mertensia viridula, commonly known as Virginia bluebell, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking violet-blue flowers in early spring. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and have a distinctive waxy coating. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Mertensia viridula is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
