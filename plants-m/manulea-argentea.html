Manulea argentea

Manulea argentea, commonly known as silverleaf manulea, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in shrubland and forest margins, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its flowers are small and clustered, often with a striking contrast between the pale petals and deep-colored stamens.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
