Monochoria vaginalis var. angustifolia

Monochoria vaginalis var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf water spinach, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features slender, elongated leaves that are typically less than 5 cm wide, with a smooth, waxy surface. The plant grows in shallow water or wetlands, often forming dense mats that can dominate aquatic ecosystems. Its stems are usually floating or semi-submerged, and it produces small white or pink flowers in clusters.

This species is valued for its edible leaves, which are used in various traditional cuisines.
