Myrcia albotomentosa var. humilis

Myrcia albotomentosa var. humilis, commonly known as the white-tomentose myrcia, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are covered in dense, white, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, and it is often found in moist, shaded environments. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule containing several seeds.

This species is of interest in ecological studies due to its role in supporting local biodiversity and its potential for medicinal use.
