Montanoa josei

Montanoa josei, commonly known as the Mexican blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its blue to purple flower heads. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and rocky soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. It produces small, narrow leaves and has a compact, bushy growth habit.

Montanoa josei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
