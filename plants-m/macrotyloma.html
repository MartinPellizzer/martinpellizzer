Macrotyloma

Macrotyloma, commonly known as cowpea, is a genus of legumes belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect or climbing growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The flowers are typically small and clustered, ranging in color from white to purple. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are widely used as a food source.

Macrotyloma species are also valued for their ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making them important in sustainable agriculture.
