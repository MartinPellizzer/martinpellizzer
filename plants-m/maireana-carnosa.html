Maireana carnosa

Maireana carnosa, commonly known as the Desert Gum or Grey Gum, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its rough, greyish bark and small, narrow leaves that are adapted to arid conditions. The plant produces small white or pinkish flowers, typically in clusters, which bloom in spring. It is well-suited to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Maireana carnosa is an important component of the flora in its native habitat, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
