Macrochloa

Macrochloa, commonly known as the giant grass or giant reed, is a genus of flowering plants in the grass family.

It is characterized by its tall, reed-like stems and narrow, elongated leaves. These plants are native to various regions in the Americas and are often found in wet or moist environments. Macrochloa species are known for their rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed soils.

They play an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
