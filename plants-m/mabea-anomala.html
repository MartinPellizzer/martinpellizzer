Mabea anomala

Mabea anomala, commonly known as the Brazilian starfruit tree, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible, star-shaped fruits. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit yield. Its botanical features include a woody stem, compound leaves, and a unique fruit structure that resembles a star.

The species is also valued for its potential in horticulture and traditional medicine.
