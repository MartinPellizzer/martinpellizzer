Memecylon tricolor var. latifolium

Memecylon tricolor var. latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf memecylon, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically broad and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, three-petaled flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by three-lobed fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses in local cultures.
