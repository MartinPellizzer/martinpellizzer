Mallotus vitifolius

Mallotus vitifolius, commonly known as the grape-leaved mallot, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often mistaken for grape leaves due to their shape and arrangement. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by woody, spherical fruits. Its foliage is evergreen, making it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.

The species is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
