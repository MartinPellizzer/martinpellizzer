Mentha densicapilla

Mentha densicapilla, commonly known as the dense-capped mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are covered in fine, dense pubescence. Its small, tubular flowers are typically pink to purple in color and are arranged in whorls along the stem.

Mentha densicapilla is valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.
