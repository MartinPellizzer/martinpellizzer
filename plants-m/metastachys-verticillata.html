Metastachys verticillata

Metastachys verticillata, commonly known as the whorled false heather, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features whorled leaves and produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

The species is part of the Acanthaceae family, known for its showy inflorescences.
