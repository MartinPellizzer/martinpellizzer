Mimulus brevipes

Mimulus brevipes, commonly known as the shortfoot monkeyflower, is a species of flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous annual with slender, branching stems and opposite leaves that are narrowly elliptic to lanceolate in shape. The flowers are typically yellow or orange, with five petals and a prominent, spurred lower petal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in stream banks and meadows.

The plant plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
