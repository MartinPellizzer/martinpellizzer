Maranta rotundifolia

Maranta rotundifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in loose inflorescences. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine and as a starch source.

Maranta rotundifolia thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.
