Myriopus microphyllus

Myriopus microphyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spinifex, is a perennial grass native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, needle-like leaves that are typically less than 1 cm in length. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is well adapted to harsh environments, with deep root systems that allow it to survive in low-nutrient soils and drought conditions.

Myriopus microphyllus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native fauna.
