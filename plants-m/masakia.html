Masakia

Masakia, commonly known as the African violets, is a genus of flowering plants native to tropical Africa.

These plants are characterized by their attractive, velvety leaves and showy, clustered flowers that come in a variety of colors. Masakia species typically grow as shrubs or small trees, with some reaching heights of up to 10 meters. They prefer warm, humid climates and are often cultivated for their ornamental value.

The flowers are usually borne on short stalks and can last for several weeks, making them popular in horticulture.
