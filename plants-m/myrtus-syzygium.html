Myrtus syzygium

Myrtus syzygium, commonly known as the Indian myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and essential oils.

The leaves and flowers are commonly used in aromatherapy and perfumery due to their pleasant scent.
