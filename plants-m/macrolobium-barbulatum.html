Macrolobium barbulatum

Macrolobium barbulatum, commonly known as the featherleaf macrolobium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves with finely divided, feather-like lobes. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds, typical of the legume family. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value is due to its delicate foliage and attractive floral display.
