Mirabilis comata

Mirabilis comata, commonly known as the silky four-o'clock, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety pink to purple flowers that open in the late afternoon and close at dawn. The plant has slender, branched stems and hairy, narrow leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Mirabilis comata is also known for its long-lasting blooms and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
