Madia sativa subsp. capitata

Madia sativa subsp.

capitata, commonly known as the California poppy, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red flowers and feathery, gray-green foliage.

The plant is an annual herb that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The botanical features of this species make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
