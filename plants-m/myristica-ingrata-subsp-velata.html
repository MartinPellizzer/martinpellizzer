Myristica ingrata subsp. velata

Myristica ingrata subsp.

velata, commonly known as the velvet myristica, is a member of the Myristicaceae family, characterized by its tropical origin and aromatic properties. It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub, typically found in the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

The plant is notable for its distinctive, velvety-textured fruits, which contain a fragrant, oily kernel used in traditional medicine and perfumery. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy surface, while its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually greenish in color.

The species is valued for its unique chemical composition, which includes compounds with potential pharmacological applications.
