Moehringia lateriflora var. elongata

Moehringia lateriflora var. elongata, commonly known as the long-stemmed moehringia, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
