Martiusia

Martiusia, commonly known as the red-flowered orchid, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

These plants are native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and are characterized by their striking, often vibrant red or orange flowers. Martiusia species typically have slender, erect stems and are epiphytic, meaning they grow on the surfaces of trees or other structures without deriving nutrients from them. The flowers are usually clustered in racemes and are known for their intricate, showy structures that attract pollinators like bees and birds.

Due to their ornamental value, some species of Martiusia are cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
