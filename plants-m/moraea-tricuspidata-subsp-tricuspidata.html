Moraea tricuspidata subsp. tricuspidata

Moraea tricuspidata subsp.

tricuspidata, commonly known as the Three-lobed Moraea, is a perennial herb native to South Africa. It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, lance-shaped leaves and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.

The subspecies is distinguished by its three-lobed leaf base, which sets it apart from other Moraea species.
