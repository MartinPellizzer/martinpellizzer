Myristica labillardieri

Myristica labillardieri, commonly known as the nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and aromatic bark. The plant produces yellowish flowers and red, fleshy fruits that enclose the aromatic seeds used for nutmeg and mace. Its botanical features include a thick, aromatic bark and a unique, segmented fruit structure.

The tree is valued for its essential oils and is cultivated for its economic and medicinal properties.
