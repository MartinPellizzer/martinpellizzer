Mimosa claussenii var. vespertilionis

Mimosa claussenii var. vespertilionis, commonly known as the evening bat mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas. Its flowers open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators like moths.

The species is known for its rapid leaf closure in response to touch, a trait shared with other mimosa species.
