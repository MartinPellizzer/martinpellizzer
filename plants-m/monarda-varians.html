Monarda varians

Monarda varians, commonly known as wild bergamot, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its showy, tubular purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet and features aromatic, hairy leaves with a strong mint-like scent. Its roots are fibrous and its stems are square, typical of the Lamiaceae family.

Monarda varians is often found in moist woodlands and open fields, where it thrives in partial to full sunlight.
