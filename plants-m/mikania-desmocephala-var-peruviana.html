Mikania desmocephala var. peruviana

Mikania desmocephala var. peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian morning glory, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and abandoned fields.

Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it both a popular garden plant and an invasive species in some regions.
