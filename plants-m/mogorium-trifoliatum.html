Mogorium trifoliatum

Mogorium trifoliatum, commonly known as the three-leaved mock orchid, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters along the stem. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in dense forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
