Medicago cinerascens var. longiseta

Medicago cinerascens var. longiseta, commonly known as the long-haired sainfoin, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has feathery, finely divided leaves and produces small, hairy seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and as a forage plant for livestock.
