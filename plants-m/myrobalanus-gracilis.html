Myrobalanus gracilis

Myrobalanus gracilis, commonly known as the slender tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and elliptical leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, sour fruits that are similar in appearance to tamarinds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

Myrobalanus gracilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its use in traditional medicine.
