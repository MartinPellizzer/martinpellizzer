Maxillaria valleculata

Maxillaria valleculata, commonly known as the Valleycup Orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white to lavender-colored blooms during the summer months. Its roots are fibrous, and it thrives in well-drained soil with partial shade.

Maxillaria valleculata is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
