Miscanthus gossweileri

Miscanthus gossweileri, commonly known as the silvergrass, is a perennial grass native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and silky, silver-green foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces fluffy, feathery plumes in late summer, which contribute to its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for landscaping.

Its drought tolerance and ability to form dense clumps make it both hardy and low-maintenance.
