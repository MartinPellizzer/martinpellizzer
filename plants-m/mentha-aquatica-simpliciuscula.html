Mentha aquatica simpliciuscula

Mentha aquatica simpliciuscula, commonly known as water mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a serrated margin. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers arranged in whorled clusters, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist environments such as stream banks and wet meadows, often forming dense colonies.

Its aromatic leaves and flowers contain volatile oils, making it a valuable plant in both traditional medicine and culinary applications.
