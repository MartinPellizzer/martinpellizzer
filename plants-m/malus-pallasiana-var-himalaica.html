Malus pallasiana var. himalaica

Malus pallasiana var. himalaica, commonly known as the Himalayan apple or mountain crabapple, is a species native to the Himalayas.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows at high altitudes, often in rocky or mountainous terrain. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and local cuisine. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and have a serrated margin, while its flowers are white or pale pink and appear in spring.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
