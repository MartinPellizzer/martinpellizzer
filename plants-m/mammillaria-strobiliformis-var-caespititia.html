Mammillaria strobiliformis var. caespititia

Mammillaria strobiliformis var. caespititia, commonly known as the clustered milk barrel cactus, is a compact, low-growing cactus native to Mexico.

It features a dense cluster of cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically covered in white to pale green tubercles. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a potted plant.
