Mappia foetida

Mappia foetida, commonly known as the stinking fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by spherical, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough texture.

The plant is known for its strong, unpleasant odor, which is particularly noticeable when the fruit ripens.
