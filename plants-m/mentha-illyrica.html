Mentha illyrica

Mentha illyrica, commonly known as the Illyrian mint, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly hairy leaves that are typically oval to lance-shaped. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers arranged in whorls along the stem. Mentha illyrica is often found in moist, shaded areas such as forests and meadows.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
