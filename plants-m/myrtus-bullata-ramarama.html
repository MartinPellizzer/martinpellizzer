Myrtus bullata ramarama

Myrtus bullata ramarama, commonly known as the ramarama myrtle, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by dark blue berries, which are edible and used in local cuisines. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
