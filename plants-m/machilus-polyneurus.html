Machilus polyneurus

Machilus polyneurus, commonly known as the Chinese laurustin, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, opposite, ovate leaves that are often glossy and dark green. It produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters, followed by small, dark blue berries.

Machilus polyneurus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
