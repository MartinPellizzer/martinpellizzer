Miconia lanceolata

Miconia lanceolata, commonly known as the arrowleaf miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers, which are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Miconia lanceolata is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, making it an invasive species in some regions.

Its botanical features make it an important plant in both ecological and ornamental contexts.
