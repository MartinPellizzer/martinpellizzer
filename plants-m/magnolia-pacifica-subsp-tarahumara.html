Magnolia pacifica subsp. tarahumara

Magnolia pacifica subsp.

tarahumara, commonly known as the Tarahumara magnolia, is a rare and distinctive species native to the Sierra Madre Occidental in Mexico. It is characterized by its large, fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant has simple, alternate, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green. It grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in the wild.
