Macrocroton surinamensis

Macrocroton surinamensis, commonly known as Surinam sandwort, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Suriname and Guyana.

It is a small, herbaceous perennial that typically grows in sandy or coastal soils. The plant features small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the wet season. Its leaves are alternate, linear, and often covered in fine hairs.

It is adapted to thrive in saline or brackish environments, making it a characteristic species of coastal ecosystems.
