Mora

Mora, commonly known as the black elderberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Caprifoliaceae and is characterized by its dark purple to black berries that ripen in late summer. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, fragrant white or yellow flowers in the spring. Mora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the edible berries used in jams, wines, and medicinal preparations.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.
