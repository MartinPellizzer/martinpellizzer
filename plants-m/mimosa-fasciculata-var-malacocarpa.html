Mimosa fasciculata var. malacocarpa

Mimosa fasciculata var. malacocarpa, commonly known as the Malacocarpa mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that fold shut when touched. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, often in spring or early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
