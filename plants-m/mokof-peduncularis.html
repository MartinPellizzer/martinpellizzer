Mokof peduncularis

Mokof peduncularis, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a round, three-lobed capsule that contains several seeds.

Mokof peduncularis is often found in moist, shaded environments and is known for its rapid growth and adaptability.
