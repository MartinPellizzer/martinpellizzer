Myosotis propinqua

Myosotis propinqua, commonly known as the dwarf forget-me-not, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a low-growing herb with slender stems and small, sky-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically reaches a height of only a few centimeters, making it well-suited for rock gardens or alpine settings. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Myosotis propinqua is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its delicate, charming appearance.
