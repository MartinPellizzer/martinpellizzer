Masdevallia triceratops

Masdevallia triceratops, commonly known as the Triceratops orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated flower spikes that can reach up to 30 inches in length. The plant produces numerous small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, arranged in a spiral pattern along the inflorescence. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and rarity, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
