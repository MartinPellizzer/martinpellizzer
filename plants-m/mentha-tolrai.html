Mentha tolrai

Mentha tolrai, commonly known as Tolai mint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or blue in color. Its leaves are fragrant and contain essential oils that contribute to its minty aroma.

Mentha tolrai is often cultivated for its culinary and medicinal uses.
