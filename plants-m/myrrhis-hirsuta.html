Myrrhis hirsuta

Myrrhis hirsuta, commonly known as hairy myrrh, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a robust rhizome system and produces basal rosettes of hairy, lanceolate leaves. The plant blooms with small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense, branched clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Myrrhis hirsuta is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
