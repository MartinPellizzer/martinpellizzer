Macaranga rotundifolia

Macaranga rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaf mallow, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, rounded leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Macaranga rotundifolia is also noted for its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed areas.
