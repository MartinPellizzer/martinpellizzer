Mentha austriaca var. multiflora

Mentha austriaca var. multiflora, commonly known as the Austrian mint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are typically covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils rich in menthol and other terpenes, making it valuable in both culinary and medicinal applications.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and use in herbal teas and traditional remedies.
