Malaxis similis

Malaxis similis, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its rhizome is short and it produces a single, erect stem with several leaves.

Malaxis similis is valued in horticulture for its elegant appearance and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
