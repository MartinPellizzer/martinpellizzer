Melocactus armatus

Melocactus armatus, commonly known as the armed melon, is a cacti species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem and distinctive, spiny areoles that bear clusters of stiff, white bristles. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits. Its growth is slow, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

Melocactus armatus is often cultivated for its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
