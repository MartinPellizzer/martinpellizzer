Monarda punctata var. fruticulosa

Monarda punctata var. fruticulosa, commonly known as the spotted beardtongue, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, branched stems covered in fine, grayish hairs. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, with a distinctive white or yellow spot at the base. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a slightly serrated edge.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, open woodlands and along stream banks.
