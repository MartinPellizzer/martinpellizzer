Myrtus krausei

Myrtus krausei, commonly known as the dwarf myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, dark green, leathery leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and as a decorative plant in Mediterranean climates.

The berries it produces are edible and have a slightly sweet, spicy flavor.
