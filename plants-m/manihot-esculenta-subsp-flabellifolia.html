Manihot esculenta subsp. flabellifolia

Manihot esculenta subsp.

flabellifolia, commonly known as the white manioc, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America. It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, white flowers.

The plant is cultivated for its starchy roots, which are processed into various food products. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both traditional and modern agricultural practices.
