Mentha × pascuicola

Mentha × pascuicola, commonly known as catnip, is a hybrid mint species that originated from the cross between Mentha longifolia and Mentha spicata.

It is characterized by its square, erect stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been naturalized in many regions around the world.

Mentha × pascuicola is known for its aromatic properties and has been traditionally used in herbal medicine and as a stimulant for cats.
