Malva sylvestris proles ambigua

Malva sylvestris proles ambigua, commonly known as the ambiguous wood mallow, is a variant of the common mallow.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes and produces showy, pale pink to white flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and meadows.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species within the Malvaceae family.
