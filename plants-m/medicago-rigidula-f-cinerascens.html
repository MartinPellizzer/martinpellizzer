Medicago rigidula f. cinerascens

Medicago rigidula f. cinerascens, commonly known as the cinerascens variant of the narrow-leafed medick, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, trifoliate leaves that are often covered in a fine, ash-gray pubescence. The flowers are typically pale purple to white, arranged in dense, racemose inflorescences, and are adapted for pollination by insects. This plant is native to temperate regions of Europe and is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and waste areas.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it an important pioneer species in ecological succession.
