Mentha aquatica f. stoderiana

Mentha aquatica f. stoderiana, commonly known as the water mint, is a variant of the water mint plant native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its aromatic, serrated leaves and its ability to thrive in moist or aquatic conditions. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is often found in marshes, stream banks, and other waterlogged areas.

This subspecies is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in herbal remedies.
