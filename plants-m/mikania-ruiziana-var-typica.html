Mikania ruiziana var. typica

Mikania ruiziana var. typica, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. It is often found in moist, tropical environments and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
