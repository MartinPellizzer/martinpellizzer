Microloma gibbosum

Microloma gibbosum, commonly known as the globular grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, spherical growth habit, with dense clusters of small, narrow leaves. The plant produces tiny, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous small, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its unique appearance, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
