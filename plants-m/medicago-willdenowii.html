Medicago willdenowii

Medicago willdenowii, commonly known as Turkey rhubarb, is a flowering plant in the legume family Fabaceae.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in dry, rocky soils. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemes. Its pods are typically hairy and contain several seeds.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop for livestock.
