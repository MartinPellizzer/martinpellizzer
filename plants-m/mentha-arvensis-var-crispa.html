Mentha arvensis var. crispa

Mentha arvensis var. crispa, commonly known as catnip, is a variant of the mint family plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, serrated leaves and a strong, aromatic scent. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Catnip is known for its medicinal properties and has been used historically for its calming and stimulating effects.
