Monardella beneolens

Monardella beneolens, commonly known as white beebrush, is a flowering plant native to California.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and woodlands. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, often covered in fine hairs.

Monardella beneolens is an important nectar source for native bees and other pollinators.
