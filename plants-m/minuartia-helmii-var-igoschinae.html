Minuartia helmii var. igoschinae

Minuartia helmii var. igoschinae, commonly known as the Igoschina rockcress, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically features pale green to bluish leaves that are covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention in arid environments. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
