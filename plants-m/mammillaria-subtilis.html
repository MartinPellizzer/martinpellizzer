Mammillaria subtilis

Mammillaria subtilis, commonly known as the narrow-ribbed hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is a small, globular cactus with a diameter typically less than 10 centimeters. The plant features ribbed stems covered in white to grayish spines, and it produces yellow flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for rock gardens or container cultivation.

Due to its compact size and unique appearance, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
