Medicago paradoxa

Medicago paradoxa, commonly known as the paradox wildclover, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has a deep root system that allows it to survive in nutrient-poor conditions.

Medicago paradoxa is also valued for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
