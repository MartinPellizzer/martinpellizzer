Manilkara rojasii

Manilkara rojasii, commonly known as the Mexican sapote, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and large, elliptical leaves. The tree produces yellowish-green flowers that are borne in clusters, followed by edible fruits with a sweet, creamy pulp.

Its fruit is often used in culinary applications, while the tree is also valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal uses.
