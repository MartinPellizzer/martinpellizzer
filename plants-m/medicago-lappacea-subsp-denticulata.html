Medicago lappacea subsp. denticulata

Medicago lappacea subsp.

denticulata, commonly known as the serrated medick, is a perennial legume native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges, and its small, yellow flowers are arranged in dense clusters. It produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds.

As a member of the Fabaceae family, it has the ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
