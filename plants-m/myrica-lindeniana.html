Myrica lindeniana

Myrica lindeniana, commonly known as the red cedar or mountain cedar, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myricaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, needle-like leaves that are typically reddish in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by fleshy, berry-like fruits that are edible for certain bird species. Myrica lindeniana is often found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.

Its hardy nature makes it adaptable to various soil types and climates.
