Monardella villosa var. neglecta

Monardella villosa var. neglecta, commonly known as the hairy mountain mint, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features aromatic, hairy leaves that are often grayish-green in color, and it produces clusters of white to pale purple flowers in the summer. The plant is part of the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and fragrant foliage. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and meadows.

Its nectar attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it an important plant in native ecosystems.
