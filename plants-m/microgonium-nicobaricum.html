Microgonium nicobaricum

Microgonium nicobaricum, commonly known as the Nicobar microgonium, is a rare flowering plant native to the Nicobar Islands in the Indian Ocean.

It is a small, herbaceous plant with delicate, narrow leaves and produces clusters of tiny, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in dense tropical forests. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and it has a short blooming period during the rainy season.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
