Myrcia chonodisca

Myrcia chonodisca, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or myrcia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible red berries that are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural pigments. Myrcia chonodisca thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in its native habitats.
