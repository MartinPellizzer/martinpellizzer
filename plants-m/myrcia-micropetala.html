Myrcia micropetala

Myrcia micropetala, commonly known as the small-petalled myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its small, fragrant leaves and showy white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, often found in humid, tropical environments. Its foliage is dense and glossy, making it a popular choice for ornamental purposes.

The fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds.
