Myrcia hispida var. latifolia

Myrcia hispida var. latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf myrcia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine, short hairs. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, and it is often found in moist, shaded forest understories.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its attractive foliage and floral display.
