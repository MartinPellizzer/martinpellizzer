Medinilla marumiaetricha

Medinilla marumiaetricha, commonly known as the velvet tangerine.

This plant is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is native to the rainforests of Queensland, Australia. It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round, orange fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal.
