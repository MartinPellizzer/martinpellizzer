Micropuntia wiegandii

Micropuntia wiegandii, commonly known as the Wiegand's prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water, typical of desert plants. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruit, known as a tunafruit, is edible and has a sweet, tangy flavor.

The plant's adaptations, such as spines and shallow roots, allow it to thrive in arid environments.
