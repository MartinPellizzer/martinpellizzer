Melochia lupulina

Melochia lupulina, commonly known as the bitter melochia, is a tropical shrub native to regions of Africa, the Americas, and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus, and is characterized by its hairy stems and broad, palmate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antifungal properties.

Melochia lupulina is also known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
