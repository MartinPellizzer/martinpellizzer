Musa × sapientum var. rubra

Musa × sapientum var. rubra, commonly known as the red banana, is a cultivar of the banana plant characterized by its striking red peel.

This variety is a hybrid of *Musa acuminata* and *Musa balbisiana*, resulting in a unique genetic makeup. The fruit is typically smaller than common bananas and has a sweet, mild flavor. The red coloration of the peel is due to high concentrations of anthocyanins, which also contribute to its antioxidant properties.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and in niche markets due to its distinctive appearance.
