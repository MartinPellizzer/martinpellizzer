Meibomia formosa

Meibomia formosa, commonly known as the golden flame orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking orange and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its leaves are elongated and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

Meibomia formosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
