Moraea melanops

Moraea melanops, commonly known as the black-eyed moraea, is a striking perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of tubular blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
