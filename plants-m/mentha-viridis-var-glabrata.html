Mentha viridis var. glabrata

Mentha viridis var. glabrata, commonly known as narrow-leaved mint, is a variant of the broad-leaf mint species.

It is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia, characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments.

Mentha viridis var. glabrata is valued for its aromatic leaves and is used in culinary and medicinal applications.
