Melittis

Melittis, commonly known as the honey plant, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Apocynaceae.

It is characterized by its milky sap and showy, often fragrant flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The leaves are typically alternate and may be either simple or trifoliate in structure. Melittis species are native to various regions in Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands.

These plants are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
