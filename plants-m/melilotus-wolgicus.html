Melilotus wolgicus

Melilotus wolgicus, commonly known as Siberian sweet clover, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes known for their nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

Melilotus wolgicus is often found in open habitats such as meadows and forest edges, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
