Malaxis triphylla

Malaxis triphylla, commonly known as the three-leaf orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has three leaves, which are lance-shaped and arranged in a whorl at the base of the stem. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The orchid is noted for its delicate appearance and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
