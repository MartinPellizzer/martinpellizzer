Monardella douglasii var. parryi

Monardella douglasii var. parryi, commonly known as Parry's monardella, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant features slender, upright stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are typically pink to purple, arranged in whorled clusters at the ends of the stems.

It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as open woodlands and rocky slopes.
