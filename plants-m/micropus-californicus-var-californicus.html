Micropus californicus var. californicus

Micropus californicus var. californicus, commonly known as the California sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features small, five-petaled flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, blooming from spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, linear, and often covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal dunes and sandy habitats.
