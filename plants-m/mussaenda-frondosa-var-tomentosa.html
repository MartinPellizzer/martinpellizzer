Mussaenda frondosa var. tomentosa

Mussaenda frondosa var. tomentosa, commonly known as the hairy mussaenda, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered inflorescences that range in color from white to pink and yellow. The plant has ovate, hairy leaves that are often covered in fine, white pubescence. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring partial shade and well-drained soil.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and compact growth habit.
