Mentha arvensis var. subcordata

Mentha arvensis var. subcordata, commonly known as field mint or narrow-leaf mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a distinct, aromatic scent. The plant produces small, pale blue to violet flowers arranged in whorls along the stem, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, along stream banks, and in woodland edges.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
