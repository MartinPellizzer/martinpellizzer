Myosotis longiflora

Myosotis longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered forget-me-not, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, blue or violet flowers with five petals. The leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been cultivated for its delicate appearance.
