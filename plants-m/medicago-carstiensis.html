Medicago carstiensis

Medicago carstiensis, commonly known as the Carstens' vetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It has small, hairy pods that contain lens-shaped seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Medicago carstiensis plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats and is of interest to ecologists and botanists studying plant adaptations.
