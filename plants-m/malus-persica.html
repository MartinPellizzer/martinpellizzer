Malus persica

Malus persica, commonly known as the Chinese apple or crabapple, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to China and is characterized by its ornamental flowers and small, edible fruits. The plant typically grows as a deciduous tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate, with a serrated margin.

The fruit, which is a pome, varies in size and color, ranging from yellow to red, and is often used in traditional Chinese medicine and cuisine.
