Muscari bucharicum

Muscari bucharicum, commonly known as the Caucasian grape hyacinth, is a bulbous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It produces dense clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale blue to deep purple. The plant typically grows to a height of 10 to 20 centimeters, with narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Muscari bucharicum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
