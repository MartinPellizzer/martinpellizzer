Mentha × dalmatica

Mentha × dalmatica, commonly known as Dalmatian mint, is a hybrid perennial herb native to the Balkans.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, serrated leaves and purple or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It is valued for its strong mint scent and has been used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

Mentha × dalmatica is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and fragrance.
