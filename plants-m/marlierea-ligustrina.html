Marlierea ligustrina

Marlierea ligustrina, commonly known as the purple-flowered shrub, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments within the Atlantic Forest biome. The plant features small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruit is a small, dry capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Marlierea ligustrina is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
