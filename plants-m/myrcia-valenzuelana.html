Myrcia valenzuelana

Myrcia valenzuelana, commonly known as the Brazilian beauty or red myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of eastern Brazil, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white or pink flowers. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains several small, edible seeds.

Myrcia valenzuelana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.
