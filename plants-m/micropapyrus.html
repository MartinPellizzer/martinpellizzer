Micropapyrus

Micropapyrus, commonly known as the ancient paper plant, is a rare and unique species native to the island of Socotra.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or brown in color. Its most distinctive feature is its ability to produce a type of paper-like material from its leaves, which was historically used by the ancient inhabitants of Socotra.

Due to habitat loss and overharvesting, Micropapyrus is now critically endangered and protected in its native environment.
