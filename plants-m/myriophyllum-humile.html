Myriophyllum humile

Myriophyllum humile, commonly known as narrow-leaf watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to North America.

It has slender, branching stems and small, opposite leaves that are typically less than 1 cm in length. The plant produces tiny, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving freshwater environments such as lakes and ponds.

Myriophyllum humile plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
