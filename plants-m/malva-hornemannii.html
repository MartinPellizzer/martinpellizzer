Malva hornemannii

Malva hornemannii, commonly known as the hornemann's mallow, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus. The plant is characterized by its large, showy pink or purple flowers and heart-shaped leaves. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

Malva hornemannii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its vibrant blooms and unique foliage.
