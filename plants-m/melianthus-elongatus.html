Melianthus elongatus

Melianthus elongatus, commonly known as the African star gooseberry, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces bright red, fleshy berries that are edible for humans and animals. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice in gardens and landscapes.
