Myrsiphyllum volubile

Myrsiphyllum volubile, commonly known as the climbing myrsiphyllum, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its trailing or climbing habit, with small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers. Its leaves are typically opposite, elliptical, and covered in a fine, waxy coating.
