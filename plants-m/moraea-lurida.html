Moraea lurida

Moraea lurida, commonly known as the pale moraea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and delicate, pale blue to white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in fynbos vegetation, which is a unique biome in South Africa.

Moraea lurida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
