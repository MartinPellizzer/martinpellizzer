Maytenus revoluta var. jaucoensis

Maytenus revoluta var. jaucoensis, commonly known as the Jauco mayten, is a shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean, particularly Cuba and the Isle of Youth.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite, and often hairy leaves that are elliptical to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, fleshy fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas and dry forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
