Medinilla mansfeldii

Medinilla mansfeldii, commonly known as the velvet tangerine or velvet flower, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, waxy flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant produces small, edible berries that are yellow-orange when ripe. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil.

Medinilla mansfeldii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and showy blooms.
