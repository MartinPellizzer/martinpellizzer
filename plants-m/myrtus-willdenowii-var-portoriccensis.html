Myrtus willdenowii var. portoriccensis

Myrtus willdenowii var. portoriccensis, commonly known as the Puerto Rican myrtle, is a shrub native to the island of Puerto Rico.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants such as eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry containing several seeds.

This variety is adapted to the tropical climate of its native habitat and is often found in moist, shaded areas of the forest understory.
