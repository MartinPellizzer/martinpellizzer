Magnolia fraseri var. fraseri

Magnolia fraseri var. fraseri, commonly known as the Fraser magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces large, showy white flowers with a subtle fragrance, typically blooming in late spring. Its bark is smooth and gray, becoming fissured with age.

The plant prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a popular ornamental tree in gardens.
