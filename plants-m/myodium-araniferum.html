Myodium araniferum

Myodium araniferum, commonly known as the spider orchid, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, spider-like appearance due to its long, thin inflorescence. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as pine forests and swamps. Its flowers are usually white or pale green, with intricate patterns that mimic the appearance of spider webs.

Myodium araniferum is a rare and endangered species, requiring specific ecological conditions for successful growth and reproduction.
