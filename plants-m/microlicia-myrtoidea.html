Microlicia myrtoidea

Microlicia myrtoidea, commonly known as the sweet myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the island of Mauritius.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. It produces small, round berries that are typically red when ripe.

Microlicia myrtoidea is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
