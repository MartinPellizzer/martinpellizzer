Mosiera nummularioides subsp. nummularioides

Mosiera nummularioides subsp.

nummularioides, commonly known as the silverleaf daphne, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It features fleshy, silver-gray leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps it conserve water in arid conditions.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink during the spring and summer months. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

Due to its slow growth and compact size, it is well-suited for rock gardens and container planting.
