Melilotus indicus var. permixtus

Melilotus indicus var. permixtus, commonly known as the Indian sweet clover, is a flowering plant native to southern and eastern Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces pods containing small, spherical seeds that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its aromatic properties make it valuable in both medicinal and agricultural applications.
