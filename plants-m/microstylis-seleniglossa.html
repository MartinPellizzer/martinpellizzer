Microstylis seleniglossa

Microstylis seleniglossa, commonly known as the silver-tongue grass, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit.

It features slender, erect stems with narrow, linear leaves that have a distinctive silvery-blue hue on the underside. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with pale green to white flowers, typical of many sedges. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological niche, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
