Moraea unguicularis

Moraea unguicularis, commonly known as the Cape bell or African bell, is a striking perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer.

Moraea unguicularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
