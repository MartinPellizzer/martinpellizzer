Miconia oraria

Miconia oraria, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or red miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit is a bright red, fleshy berry that contains numerous seeds.

Miconia oraria is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant foliage and floral display.
