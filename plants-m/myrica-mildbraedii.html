Myrica mildbraedii

Myrica mildbraedii, commonly known as the mildbraed's wax myrtle, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of wax-coated berries. The plant thrives in wetland habitats, including swamps and bogs, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife. Its evergreen foliage and unique fruit structure make it an important component of its ecosystem.

Myrica mildbraedii is also valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
