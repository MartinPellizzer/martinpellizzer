Microchilus erythrodoides

Microchilus erythrodoides, commonly known as the red-veined orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers with deep red veins on a white or pale pink background. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in tropical rainforests. Its leaves are elongated and often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
