Musa banksii var. muelleriana

Musa banksii var. muelleriana, commonly known as the dwarf banana plant, is a tropical species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with short, broad leaves that form a rosette-like structure. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically hidden beneath its foliage. Its fruit, when it matures, is a small, elongated banana-like fruit that is usually not cultivated for consumption.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often grown as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
