Mimosa bipennatula

Mimosa bipennatula, commonly known as the sensitive plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It is characterized by its bipinnately compound leaves that fold shut when touched, a response triggered by mechanical stimulation. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in spherical inflorescences, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Mimosa bipennatula is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its unique foliage and rapid growth habit.
