Mimulus glutinosus var. linearis

Mimulus glutinosus var. linearis, commonly known as the narrowleaf monkeyflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and vibrant orange to red tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in streambanks and wet meadows. It has a fibrous root system and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
