Manihot bellidifolia

Manihot bellidifolia, commonly known as the yellow arrowroot, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its upright stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellowish flowers that are clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its roots are edible and have been traditionally used as a starch source.

However, the plant contains toxic compounds, requiring careful preparation before consumption.
