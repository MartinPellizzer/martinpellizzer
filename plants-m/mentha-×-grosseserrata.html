Mentha × grosseserrata

Mentha × grosseserrata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf peppermint, is a hybrid mint species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and a strong, aromatic scent reminiscent of peppermint. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Mentha × grosseserrata is often used in herbal medicine and as a culinary herb due to its menthol content.
