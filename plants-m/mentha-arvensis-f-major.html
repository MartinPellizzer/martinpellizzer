Mentha arvensis f. major

Mentha arvensis f. major, commonly known as the greater mint, is a variant of the field mint species.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and aromatic, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks.

This subspecies is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in herbal remedies.
