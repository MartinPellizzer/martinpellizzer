Moraea barbigera

Moraea barbigera, commonly known as the woolly moraea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that bloom in spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces inflorescences with spurred flowers in shades of pink, red, and purple. Moraea barbigera is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
