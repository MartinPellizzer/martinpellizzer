Mentha × sicula

Mentha × sicula, commonly known as Sicilian mint, is a hybrid mint species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30–60 cm, featuring square stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, pale purple flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, giving it a strong minty scent and making it valuable in culinary and medicinal applications.

Mentha × sicula is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its use in herbal teas and traditional remedies.
