Melocactus radoczii

Melocactus radoczii, commonly known as the Radoczi's melon cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its globular shape and a thick, ribbed stem that stores water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are pollinated by insects. Its spines are short and dense, providing protection from herbivores.

Melocactus radoczii is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.
