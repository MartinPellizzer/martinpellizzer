Montanoa auriculata

Montanoa auriculata, commonly known as the ear-shaped leaf tree, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are divided into three to five lobes resembling ears, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Montanoa auriculata is valued for its ornamental foliage and is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens.
