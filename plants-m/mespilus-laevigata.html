Mespilus laevigata

Mespilus laevigata, commonly known as the European whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and elliptical leaves with a slightly serrated edge. The tree produces small, white flowers in spring, which are followed by edible, round berries that ripen in late summer. It is often found in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.

The species is also notable for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and climates.
