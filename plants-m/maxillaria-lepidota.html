Maxillaria lepidota

Maxillaria lepidota, commonly known as the velvety maxillaria, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of fragrant, butterfly-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pale pink, bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Maxillaria lepidota is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
