Martynia angulosa

Martynia angulosa, commonly known as the angular martynia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, white or purple flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically yellow or orange when mature. It is often found in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
