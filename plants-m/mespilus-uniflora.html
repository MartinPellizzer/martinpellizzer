Mespilus uniflora

Mespilus uniflora, commonly known as the single-flower medlar, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its alternate, simple, and often hairy leaves that turn red in the autumn. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that appear in the spring, followed by distinctive, fleshy fruits that ripen in the fall. The fruit has a unique, aromatic flavor and is often used in culinary applications.

Due to its ornamental value and historical significance, it is cultivated in gardens and historical sites.
