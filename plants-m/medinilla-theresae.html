Medinilla theresae

Medinilla theresae, commonly known as the "Christmas Medinilla," is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of fragrant, waxy flowers. The plant typically produces showy pink or white blooms, which are often borne in terminal inflorescences. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal.

Medinilla theresae prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical gardens.
