Mimosa amphigena var. glabrescens

Mimosa amphigena var. glabrescens, commonly known as the silky mimosa, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, feathery foliage and its ability to produce clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant has a woody stem with a smooth, grayish-brown bark. Its leaves are bipinnate, meaning they are divided into many small, thread-like leaflets.

This variety is notable for its rapid growth and adaptability to various soil types.
