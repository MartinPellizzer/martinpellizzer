Myrciaria micrantha

Myrciaria micrantha, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pitanga, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces bright red, fleshy berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. Myrciaria micrantha thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
