Monotes discolor var. cordatus

Monotes discolor var. cordatus, commonly known as the heartleaf monotes, is a variant of the monotes plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves, which are typically a vibrant green with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding ornamental value to its appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for both garden and container cultivation.

Its unique foliage and adaptability make it a popular choice among plant enthusiasts and landscapers.
