Medicago transoxana

Medicago transoxana, commonly known as Transoxiana clover, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species capable of thriving in poor soils. It plays a role in soil nitrogen fixation due to its symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.

Its ecological significance includes providing forage for livestock and supporting local biodiversity.
