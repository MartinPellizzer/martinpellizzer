Miltonia × cyrtochiloides

Miltonia × cyrtochiloides, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a hybrid species resulting from crosses between Miltonia and Cyrtochilum genera.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its sympodial growth habit and the presence of pseudobulbs. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in a range of colors, including pink, red, and white, often with intricate patterns. It thrives in warm, humid environments with high light exposure and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated for horticultural purposes in tropical and subtropical regions.
