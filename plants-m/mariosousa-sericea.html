Mariosousa sericea

Mariosousa sericea, commonly known as silverleaf mariosousa, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It typically grows in well-drained soils in grasslands and open woodlands, preferring full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens in regions with a Mediterranean climate.
