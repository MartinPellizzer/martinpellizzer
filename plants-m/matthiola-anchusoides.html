Matthiola anchusoides

Matthiola anchusoides, commonly known as the rock stock, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and bright pink or white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy, gray-green leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Matthiola anchusoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
