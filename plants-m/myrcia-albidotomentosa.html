Myrcia albidotomentosa

Myrcia albidotomentosa, commonly known as the white-tomentose myrtle, is a shrub native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces small, edible berries.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
