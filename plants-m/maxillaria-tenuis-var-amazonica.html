Maxillaria tenuis var. amazonica

Maxillaria tenuis var. amazonica, commonly known as the Amazonian lady's slipper, is a terrestrial orchid native to the rainforests of South America.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a short rhizome and a single, lanceolate leaf. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers with a distinctive pouch-like structure, typically in shades of pink and white. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, organic-rich soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
