Melissa officinalis var. foliosa

Melissa officinalis var. foliosa, commonly known as lemon balm, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, serrated leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is known for its aromatic properties, with a scent reminiscent of lemon. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a versatile garden plant.

Its leaves are often used in herbal teas and traditional medicine for their calming effects.
