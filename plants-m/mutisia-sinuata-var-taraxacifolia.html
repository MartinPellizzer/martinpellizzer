Mutisia sinuata var. taraxacifolia

Mutisia sinuata var. taraxacifolia, commonly known as the Taraxacifolia Mutisia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous roots. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical structure supports its role in local ecosystems, providing nectar for pollinators and contributing to soil stability.
