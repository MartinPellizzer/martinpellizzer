Mentha × pseudosilvestris

Mentha × pseudosilvestris, commonly known as wild mint, is a hybrid species in the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant leaves and square, hairy stems. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland edges and stream banks. Its leaves are often used in culinary and medicinal applications due to their menthol content.

Mentha × pseudosilvestris exhibits a variety of growth forms, including upright and prostrate, depending on environmental conditions.
