Malaxis riparia

Malaxis riparia, commonly known as the stream orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant features a slender, wiry stem with a single, lance-shaped leaf. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and lack a distinct lip, which is a characteristic of some orchid species.

Malaxis riparia is known for its delicate appearance and is often found in humid, tropical climates.
