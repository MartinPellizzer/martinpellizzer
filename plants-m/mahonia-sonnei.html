Mahonia sonnei

Mahonia sonnei, commonly known as the Sonne's mahonia, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its spiny, compound leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces yellow flowers in the spring, which are followed by blue-black berries that are edible for birds. Mahonia sonnei thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

It is often used in landscaping for its attractive foliage and winter interest.
