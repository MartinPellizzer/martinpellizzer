Matelea barrosiana

Matelea barrosiana, commonly known as the Mexican moonflower, is a striking tropical plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a member of the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night. The plant produces velvety, hairy leaves and has a climbing or shrub-like growth habit. Its flowers emit a strong, sweet fragrance to attract nocturnal pollinators such as moths.
