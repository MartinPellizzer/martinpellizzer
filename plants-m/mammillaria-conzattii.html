Mammillaria conzattii

Mammillaria conzattii, commonly known as the Conzatti's nipple cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem and is often covered in small, white spines. The plant typically produces yellow flowers during the spring or early summer. It is a popular choice for rock gardens and cactus collections due to its compact size and ornamental appearance.

Mammillaria conzattii requires well-draining soil and minimal water to thrive.
