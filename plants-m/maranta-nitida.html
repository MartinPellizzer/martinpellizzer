Maranta nitida

Maranta nitida, commonly known as the velvetleaf arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often adorned with intricate patterns and a velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Maranta nitida is also valued for its starchy roots, which are used in traditional cuisine.
