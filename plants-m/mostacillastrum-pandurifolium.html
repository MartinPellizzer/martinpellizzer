Mostacillastrum pandurifolium

Mostacillastrum pandurifolium, commonly known as the pandurifolium grass, is a perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of arid and semi-arid ecosystems.
