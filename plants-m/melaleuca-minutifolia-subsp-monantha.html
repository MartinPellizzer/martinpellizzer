Melaleuca minutifolia subsp. monantha

Melaleuca minutifolia subsp.

monantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bottlebrush, is a shrub native to eastern Australia. It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and distinctive red or pink bottlebrush-like flowers that bloom in winter.

The plant produces woody cones containing numerous small, winged seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting native wildlife.
