Macella hirta

Macella hirta, commonly known as the hairy macella, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branching stems covered in fine, hairy pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are lance-shaped and also bear a hairy texture.

Macella hirta is often found in dry, rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
