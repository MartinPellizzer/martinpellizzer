Mentha × dumetorum var. viridior

Mentha × dumetorum var. viridior, commonly known as "green mint" or "catnip," is a hybrid plant in the mint family (Lamiaceae).

It is characterized by its serrated, lance-shaped leaves that are often a vibrant green, with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers arranged in whorls along the stem. It is native to temperate regions of Europe and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its aromatic properties make it useful in both culinary and medicinal applications.
