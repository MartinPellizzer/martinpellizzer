Meisneria

Meisneria, commonly known as the false sandalwood, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Myrtaceae.

It consists of several species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and Australia. These plants are characterized by their aromatic leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that often have a pink or white color. Meisneria species are typically evergreen shrubs or small trees with rough, bark-like stems.

They are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes used in traditional medicine.
