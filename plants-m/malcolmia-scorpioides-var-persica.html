Malcolmia scorpioides var. persica

Malcolmia scorpioides var. persica, commonly known as Persian mallows, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, pale pink to white flowers resembling those of the hibiscus. The plant has hairy, alternate leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or arid environments.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
