Mentha × platyphylla

Mentha × platyphylla, commonly known as apple mint, is a hybrid mint plant that combines traits from watermint and spearmint.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly sweet scent and its oval-shaped, serrated leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and prefers moist, well-drained soil. Its flowers are small and tubular, appearing in clusters, and it blooms from late spring to early autumn.

Apple mint is often used in culinary applications and herbal teas due to its pleasant flavor and medicinal properties.
