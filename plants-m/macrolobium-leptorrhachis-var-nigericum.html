Macrolobium leptorrhachis var. nigericum

Macrolobium leptorrhachis var. nigericum, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a climbing shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and striking white, night-blooming flowers that emit a strong fragrance. The plant belongs to the Malvaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and adaptability to various soil types. Its flowers open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.
