Mutisia subulata var. major

Mutisia subulata var. major, commonly known as the Andean mutisia, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic properties. The plant features narrow, needle-like leaves and produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
