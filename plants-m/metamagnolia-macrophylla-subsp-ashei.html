Metamagnolia macrophylla subsp. ashei

Metamagnolia macrophylla subsp.

ashei, commonly known as the Ashe's magnolia, is a rare and endemic species native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its large, showy white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and elliptical, with a smooth, dark green upper surface and a lighter underside.

The subspecies is notable for its unique floral structure and is of interest to botanists and conservationists due to its limited distribution and ecological significance.
