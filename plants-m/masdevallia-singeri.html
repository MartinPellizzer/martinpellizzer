Masdevallia singeri

Masdevallia singeri, commonly known as the Singer's Masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and its striking, often colorful flowers with intricate patterns. The plant typically produces clusters of small, lance-shaped leaves and has a pseudobulb structure typical of many orchids. Its flowers bloom in late winter or early spring, adding a unique ornamental value to gardens and collections.

Masdevallia singeri thrives in cool, humid environments with indirect light, making it a sought-after plant among orchid enthusiasts.
