Musa × paradisiaca var. punctata

Musa × paradisiaca var. punctata, commonly known as the spotted banana, is a cultivated variant of the banana plant characterized by its distinctive leaf patterns.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value, featuring leaves with a striking mottled appearance of green and white. It belongs to the Musaceae family and is typically grown as a decorative plant rather than for fruit production. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil.

Its growth habit is similar to other banana species, with tall, clustered pseudostems and large, broad leaves.
