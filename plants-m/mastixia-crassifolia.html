Mastixia crassifolia

Mastixia crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf mastix tree, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Indonesia and Malaysia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by fleshy, red berries that contain a single seed.

Its bark is thick and rugged, and it is often used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
