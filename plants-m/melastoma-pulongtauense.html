Melastoma pulongtauense

Melastoma pulongtauense, commonly known as the mountain melastoma, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that release seeds when mature.

The species is often found in moist, shaded environments in forest understories.
