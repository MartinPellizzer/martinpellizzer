Magnolia denudata var. purpurascens

Magnolia denudata var. purpurascens, commonly known as the Japanese magnolia or the purple-leaf magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range from white to pink and are often tinged with purple. The leaves are ovate and have a smooth, glossy surface, turning a vibrant red in the fall. This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun exposure.
