Mammillaria longimamma var. hexacentra

Mammillaria longimamma var. hexacentra, commonly known as the six-spined mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are covered in white, woolly hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It has a compact growth habit and is well-suited for cultivation in arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
