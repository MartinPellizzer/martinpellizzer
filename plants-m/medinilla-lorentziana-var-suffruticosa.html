Medinilla lorentziana var. suffruticosa

Medinilla lorentziana var. suffruticosa, commonly known as the velvet tangerine plant, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of fragrant, waxy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers are pollinated by insects, and the plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed.
