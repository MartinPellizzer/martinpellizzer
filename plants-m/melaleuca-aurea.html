Melaleuca aurea

Melaleuca aurea, commonly known as the golden myrtle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant white or yellow flowers. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery or rusty-colored powdery coating. It typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to salty, well-drained soils.

Melaleuca aurea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
