Monorchis ecalcarata

Monorchis ecalcarata, commonly known as the spurred gentian, is a flowering plant belonging to the Gentianaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and western Asia, typically found in alpine and subalpine regions. The plant is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which are often adorned with prominent spurred petals. Its leaves are opposite, lanceolate, and covered in fine hairs.

Monorchis ecalcarata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous habitats.
