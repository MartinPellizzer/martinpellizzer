Mimosa tobagensis

Mimosa tobagensis, commonly known as the Togabensis sensitive plant, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves that fold shut when touched. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, adding ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for gardens in warm climates.

Due to its sensitive leaf response, it is often cultivated as a curiosity or for educational purposes.
