Mimusops affinis

Mimusops affinis, commonly known as the African butter tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and opposite, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by orange or red berries containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas or near riverbanks.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
