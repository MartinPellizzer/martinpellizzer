Mundulea pulchra

Mundulea pulchra, commonly known as the purple wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, feathery leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by pods containing seeds. It is adapted to arid environments and has a deep root system for water absorption.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
