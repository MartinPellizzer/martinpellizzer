Menonvillea virgata

Menonvillea virgata, commonly known as the slender rock orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, pale green to white flowers with a distinctive lip. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous habitats, thriving in cool, humid environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a dense rosette near the ground.

Menonvillea virgata is a rare and endangered species, making it a subject of interest for conservation efforts.
