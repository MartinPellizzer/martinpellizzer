Mucuna warburgii

Mucuna warburgii, commonly known as the velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive, hairy pods that contain edible seeds. The plant is notable for its rapid growth and ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, enriching the soil. Its seeds contain high levels of protein and are used in both culinary and medicinal applications.

Additionally, the plant has been studied for its potential in sustainable agriculture due to its nitrogen-fixing properties.
