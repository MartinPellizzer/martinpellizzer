Myrcia lilloi

Myrcia lilloi, commonly known as the Lillo's myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly found in regions of Brazil, Argentina, and Paraguay. The plant is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white or pinkish flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Myrcia lilloi is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
