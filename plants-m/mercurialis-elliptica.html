Mercurialis elliptica

Mercurialis elliptica, commonly known as the narrowleaf mercury.

It is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia, characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, alternate leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in disturbed or rocky habitats.

Its rapid growth and invasive tendencies make it a notable species in ecological studies.
