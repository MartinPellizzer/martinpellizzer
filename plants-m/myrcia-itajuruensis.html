Myrcia itajuruensis

Myrcia itajuruensis, commonly known as the Itajuru myrtle, is a shrub or small tree native to the Amazon rainforest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. It produces small, dark purple berries that are edible but not widely consumed by humans.

Myrcia itajuruensis is often found in humid, shaded environments and plays a role in the ecological diversity of its habitat.
