Melancranis elongata

Melancranis elongata, commonly known as the long-stemmed orchid, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its elongated, slender stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pale yellow. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, this plant thrives in humid environments with well-drained soil. It requires indirect sunlight and consistent moisture to flourish, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

The plant's botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically significant in its native habitats.
