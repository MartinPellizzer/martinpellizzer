Malaxis laxa

Malaxis laxa, commonly known as the slender malaxis, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, pendulous flowers that typically bloom in late summer or early autumn. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with a short rhizome and a single, narrow leaf. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with a distinct lip, and it prefers shaded, moist habitats.

Malaxis laxa is valued in horticulture for its unique appearance and is often grown in woodland gardens.
