Marrubium purpureum var. kurdicum

Marrubium purpureum var. kurdicum, commonly known as Kurdish horehound, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It features hairy, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves with a grayish-green hue. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or arid environments.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been historically used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and digestive benefits.
