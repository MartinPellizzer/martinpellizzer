Mascagnia affinis

Mascagnia affinis, commonly known as the scarlet pimpernel, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It blooms throughout the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Mascagnia affinis is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in full sun.
