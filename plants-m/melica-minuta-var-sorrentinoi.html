Melica minuta var. sorrentinoi

Melica minuta var. sorrentinoi, commonly known as the slender meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to certain regions of southern Europe.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny flowers. It is characterized by its fine, wiry stems and is often found in temperate climates.

Botanically, it is part of the Poaceae family and is noted for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local insect populations.
