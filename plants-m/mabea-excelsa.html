Mabea excelsa

Mabea excelsa, commonly known as the giant water fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, floating fronds that can reach lengths of up to three meters. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in marshes and wetlands. Its rhizomes form dense mats on the water surface, contributing to water filtration and habitat creation for aquatic life.

The plant's unique structure allows it to absorb nutrients directly from the water, making it an important component of its ecosystem.
