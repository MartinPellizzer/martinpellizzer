Moraea ramosissima

Moraea ramosissima, commonly known as the Cape bellflower, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features slender, branched stems and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in a rosette at the base.

Moraea ramosissima is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
