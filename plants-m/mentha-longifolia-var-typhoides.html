Mentha longifolia var. typhoides

Mentha longifolia var. typhoides, commonly known as sweet cicely, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features aromatic, finely divided leaves that are often used in culinary applications. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its roots and leaves contain essential oils, contributing to its distinctive scent and medicinal properties.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.
