Millettia semseii

Millettia semseii, commonly known as the African velvet tamarind, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces pods containing edible seeds that are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source. Its botanical features include a woody stem, trifoliate leaves, and a distinctive, aromatic scent.

Millettia semseii is also valued for its ability to thrive in poor soils and its role in nitrogen fixation.
