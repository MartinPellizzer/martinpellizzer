Mimosa subsericea f. armata

Mimosa subsericea f. armata, commonly known as the hairy mimosa, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers that appear in clusters. The plant has a distinctive feature of its leaves curling when touched, a response known as thigmonastic movement. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
