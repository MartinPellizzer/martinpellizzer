Melastoma mariannum

Melastoma mariannum, commonly known as the Hawaiian stinkwood, is a shrub native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that are typically pink or red, often with a yellow center. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Melastoma mariannum is also known for its strong, unpleasant odor, which is more pronounced in its flowers.
