Mesua catharinae

Mesua catharinae, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, featuring a broad, umbrella-like canopy. The plant is characterized by its vibrant orange to red flowers, which bloom in clusters and are highly attractive to pollinators. Its leaves are large, simple, and alternate, with a rough texture and a distinctive, aromatic scent.

Mesua catharinae thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental species in tropical gardens.
