Meriania stellata

Meriania stellata, commonly known as the starleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, star-shaped leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are palmately lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance, while its flowers attract various pollinators.

Meriania stellata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
