Masdevallia flammula

Masdevallia flammula, commonly known as the flame orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, hairy pseudobulbs and slender, often hairy stems. The plant produces clusters of small, brightly colored flowers, typically in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom during the cooler months. Its unique floral structure and vibrant colors make it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.

Masdevallia flammula requires cool, humid conditions and is often grown in shaded environments to thrive.
