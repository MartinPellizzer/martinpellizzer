Melanodendron

Melanodendron, commonly known as the blackwood tree, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Anacardiaceae.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood and aromatic foliage, which are prized for their unique properties. The plant is native to parts of Africa, particularly in regions with tropical and subtropical climates. Melanodendron species typically have simple, alternate leaves and produce small, clustered flowers that are often yellow or orange in color.

These trees are valued for their ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local cultures.
