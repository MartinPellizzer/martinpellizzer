Miconia caucana

Miconia caucana, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or purple miconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers, which are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Miconia caucana is often found in humid lowland forests and is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets.

Due to its invasive potential, it is considered a problematic species in some regions.
