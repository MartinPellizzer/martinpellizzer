Melica berteroana var. pilosula

Melica berteroana var. pilosula, commonly known as the hairy meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

The inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, two-rowed spikelets, which are characteristic of the Poaceae family.
