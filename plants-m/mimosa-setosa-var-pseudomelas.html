Mimosa setosa var. pseudomelas

Mimosa setosa var. pseudomelas, commonly known as the pale mimosa, is a variant of the mimosa plant native to parts of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features slender, hairy stems and small, bipinnate leaves that are often pale green in color. Its flowers are yellow and appear in clusters, and the plant is known for its sensitive leaves that fold when touched.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
