Mentha longifolia var. reflexifolia

Mentha longifolia var. reflexifolia, commonly known as woolly mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems covered in soft, fuzzy hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The leaves are lance-shaped, slightly hairy, and emit a strong minty aroma when crushed. It produces small, tubular blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer.

This variety is valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in herbal teas and culinary applications.
