Mimulus longiflorus var. rutilus

Mimulus longiflorus var. rutilus, commonly known as the red monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking red-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as stream banks and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
