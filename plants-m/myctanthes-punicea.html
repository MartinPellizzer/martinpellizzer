Myctanthes punicea

Myctanthes punicea, commonly known as the red-flowered orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its striking pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has linear, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Myctanthes punicea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
