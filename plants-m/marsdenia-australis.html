Marsdenia australis

Marsdenia australis, commonly known as the Australian Marsdenia, is a climbing plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a follicle containing several seeds, and it is often found in coastal and dry forest habitats.

Marsdenia australis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
