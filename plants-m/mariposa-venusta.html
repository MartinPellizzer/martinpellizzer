Mariposa venusta

Mariposa venusta, commonly known as the showy orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high altitudes of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its flowers are often adorned with intricate patterns and come in shades of pink, purple, and white.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
