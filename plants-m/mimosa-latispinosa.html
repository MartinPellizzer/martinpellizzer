Mimosa latispinosa

Mimosa latispinosa, commonly known as the broad-spined mimosa, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves that are pinnately arranged and covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellow, globular flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in spring. Its seeds are enclosed in fluffy, pod-like structures that aid in wind dispersal.

Mimosa latispinosa is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.
