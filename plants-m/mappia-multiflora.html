Mappia multiflora

Mappia multiflora, commonly known as the multiflora mappia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom throughout the summer months. The plant has compound leaves with multiple leaflets, and it often grows in moist, shaded environments. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.

However, it can become invasive in certain ecosystems if not managed properly.
