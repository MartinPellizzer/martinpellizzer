Mesosphaerum salicinum

Mesosphaerum salicinum, commonly known as the yellow sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub with slender, creeping stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on short pedicels. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a distinctive sandpaper-like texture.

Mesosphaerum salicinum is well adapted to harsh environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and efficient water use strategies.
