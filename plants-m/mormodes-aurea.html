Mormodes aurea

Mormodes aurea, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad and leathery, adapted to retain moisture in humid environments.

The flowers bloom in clusters, adding a vibrant touch to its foliage.
