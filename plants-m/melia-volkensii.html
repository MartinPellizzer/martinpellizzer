Melia volkensii

Melia volkensii, commonly known as the African snowball tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and large, palmately lobed leaves that turn red in the autumn. The tree produces fragrant, white, clustered flowers that bloom in spring, followed by round, green fruits that mature to a dark blue. Its wood is highly valued for its durability and is used in construction and furniture making.

The plant is also noted for its medicinal properties, with various parts used in traditional African medicine.
