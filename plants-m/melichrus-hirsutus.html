Melichrus hirsutus

Melichrus hirsutus, commonly known as the hairy melichrus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features wiry, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive hairy texture. Its small, clustered flowers are usually white or pale pink, and they bloom in late spring to early summer.

Melichrus hirsutus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
