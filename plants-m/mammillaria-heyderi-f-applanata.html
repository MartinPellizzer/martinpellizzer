Mammillaria heyderi f. applanata

Mammillaria heyderi f. applanata, commonly known as the flat-topped mammillaria, is a compact cactus native to Mexico.

It features a globular stem with a distinctive flat, rounded top, and is covered in white to pale green tubercles. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by small, red fruits. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun.

Due to its unique appearance, it is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant or in rock gardens.
