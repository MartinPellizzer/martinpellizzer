Matucana myriacantha f. purpureoalba

Matucana myriacantha f. purpureoalba, commonly known as the purple-veined matucana, is a rare and striking bromeliad native to the rainforests of Brazil.

It is characterized by its vibrant, waxy leaves that display a unique combination of green and purple hues, with prominent purple veins running through the foliage. The plant produces small, white flowers that emerge from the center of the rosette, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its rarity and specific care requirements, it is a sought-after plant among bromeliad enthusiasts.
