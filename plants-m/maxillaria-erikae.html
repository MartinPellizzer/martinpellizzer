Maxillaria erikae

Maxillaria erikae, commonly known as the Erik's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces showy, fragrant flowers with a distinctive coloration. The plant typically has hairy stems and leaves that are elliptical in shape. Its flowers are borne on lateral spikes and often display a combination of white, pink, and purple hues.

Maxillaria erikae is appreciated for its ornamental value and is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and unique appearance.
