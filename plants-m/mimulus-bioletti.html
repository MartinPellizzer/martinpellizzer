Mimulus bioletti

Mimulus bioletti, commonly known as the yellow sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

Mimulus bioletti is a perennial species that is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
