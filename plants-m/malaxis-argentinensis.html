Malaxis argentinensis

Malaxis argentinensis, commonly known as the Argentine malaxis, is a terrestrial orchid native to South America, particularly Argentina and neighboring regions.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often greenish or brownish flower spikes. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments such as forests and grasslands. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with sepals and petals that are often similar in shape.

Malaxis argentinensis is notable for its unique morphology and ecological adaptations to its native habitat.
