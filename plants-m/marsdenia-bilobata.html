Marsdenia bilobata

Marsdenia bilobata, commonly known as the two-lobed Marsdenia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its opposite, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces fleshy, red or orange berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural use in gardens and greenhouses.
