Mentha fluvialis

Mentha fluvialis, commonly known as river mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and wet meadows. The plant has slender, upright stems that are often covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are elongated, heart-shaped, and have a strong, minty aroma.

River mint produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.
