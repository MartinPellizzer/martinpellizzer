Malva alcea subsp. bismalva

Malva alcea subsp.

bismalva, commonly known as the common mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It features broad, velvety leaves with a distinctive notched or lobed shape, and produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in cultivated fields. It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like cotton and hibiscus.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically in traditional medicine.
