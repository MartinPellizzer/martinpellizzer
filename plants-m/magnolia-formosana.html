Magnolia formosana

Magnolia formosana, commonly known as the Taiwan magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to Taiwan.

It is characterized by its large, showy, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate to elliptical leaves that turn yellow in autumn. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
