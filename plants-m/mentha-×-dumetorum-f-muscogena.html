Mentha × dumetorum f. muscogena

Mentha × dumetorum f. muscogena, commonly known as the musk mint, is a hybrid plant belonging to the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly minty scent and its small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or blue in color. The plant is native to the eastern United States and thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a serrated edge and are often used in traditional herbal remedies.

This variety is notable for its unique fragrance and its role in local ecosystems.
