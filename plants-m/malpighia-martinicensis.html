Malpighia martinicensis

Malpighia martinicensis, commonly known as the West Indian cherry, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Caribbean.

It produces small, bright red berries that are rich in vitamin C and other antioxidants. The plant has simple, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional value.

The flowers are small and yellow, typical of the Malpighiaceae family.
