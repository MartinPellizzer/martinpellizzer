Mormodes orinocoensis

Mormodes orinocoensis, commonly known as the Orinoco orchid, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Venezuela and Colombia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, intricate flowers that often display a range of colors, including white, yellow, and green. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as rainforests, where it thrives on the forest floor or as an epiphyte on trees. Its botanical features include a sympodial growth habit and pseudobulbs, which help store water and nutrients.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
