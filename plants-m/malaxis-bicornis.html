Malaxis bicornis

Malaxis bicornis, commonly known as the two-horned orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. The plant has a slender, erect stem with a single, lance-shaped leaf. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and arranged in a raceme, with two prominent calluses on the column.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its unique floral structure and ecological significance.
