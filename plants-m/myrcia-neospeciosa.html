Myrcia neospeciosa

Myrcia neospeciosa, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Atlantic rainforests of Brazil, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and fragrant, white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruits are small, red berries that contain several seeds, and the plant is often used in traditional medicine for its aromatic properties.

Myrcia neospeciosa plays an important role in the ecological balance of its native habitat.
