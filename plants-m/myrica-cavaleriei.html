Myrica cavaleriei

Myrica cavaleriei, commonly known as the Chinese wax tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the family Myricaceae and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces drupes that are typically red or black when mature, containing a single seed. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Myrica cavaleriei is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional Chinese medicine.
