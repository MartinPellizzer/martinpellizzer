Melocalamus arrectus

Melocalamus arrectus, commonly known as the stiff bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in ecological restoration projects.
