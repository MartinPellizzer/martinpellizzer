Myrtillus niger

Myrtillus niger, commonly known as the black myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the eastern United States, particularly in the southeastern region, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. The plant is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are clustered in small, roundish groups. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed.

The black myrtle is often found in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
