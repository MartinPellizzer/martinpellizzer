Millettia dehiscens

Millettia dehiscens, commonly known as the African sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces elongated, woody pods that split open to release its seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Its growth habit makes it a valuable species for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
