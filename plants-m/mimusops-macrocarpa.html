Mimusops macrocarpa

Mimusops macrocarpa, commonly known as the African star apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Africa and parts of the Indian Ocean.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces large, fleshy fruits that turn from green to yellow or red as they mature. These fruits contain a single large seed surrounded by a sweet, edible pulp.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of food in tropical regions.
