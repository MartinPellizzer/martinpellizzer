Malvastrum lindheimerianum

Malvastrum lindheimerianum, commonly known as the Lindheimer's mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for xeriscaping.

Its botanical features make it an attractive addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
