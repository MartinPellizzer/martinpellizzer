Melocactus xanthacanthus

Melocactus xanthacanthus, commonly known as the yellow spined melon cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem that is ribbed and covered with dense, yellowish spines. The plant produces bright orange to red flowers during the summer months, which are followed by fleshy, edible fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and desert gardens.
