Myrcia parviantha

Myrcia parviantha, commonly known as the Brazilian peppertree, is a small evergreen shrub native to South America.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are opposite and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Myrcia parviantha is often found in moist, shaded areas such as forest understories and streambanks.

It has a distinctive aromatic scent and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
