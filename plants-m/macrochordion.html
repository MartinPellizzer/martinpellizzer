Macrochordion

Macrochordion, commonly known as the giant kelp, is a type of brown algae that thrives in cold, nutrient-rich coastal waters.

It forms extensive underwater forests that provide habitat for a variety of marine life. The plant has a long, flexible stalk called a stipe, which supports numerous blade-like structures. It reproduces through spores released into the water, contributing to its rapid growth and spread.

Macrochordion plays a vital role in marine ecosystems by stabilizing sediments and supporting biodiversity.
