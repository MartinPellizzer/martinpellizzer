Matthiola fruticulosa subsp. fruticulosa

Matthiola fruticulosa subsp.

fruticulosa, commonly known as the shrub mustard, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its stems are woody at the base. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or as a border plant.

This species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its fragrance and ability to attract pollinators.
