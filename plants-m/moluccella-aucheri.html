Moluccella aucheri

Moluccella aucheri, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. Its root system is fibrous, and it thrives in well-drained, loamy soils.

Moluccella aucheri is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
