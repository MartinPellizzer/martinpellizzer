Mollinedia eugeniifolia

Mollinedia eugeniifolia, commonly known as the white hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy white petals and a prominent yellow stamen, which give it a striking appearance. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, flat seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a popular ornamental plant in horticulture.
