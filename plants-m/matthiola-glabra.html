Matthiola glabra

Matthiola glabra, commonly known as the smooth wallflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It thrives in full sun and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed habitats.

Matthiola glabra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
