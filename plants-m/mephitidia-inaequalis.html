Mephitidia inaequalis

Mephitidia inaequalis, commonly known as the unequal mephit, is a species of flowering plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. The plant thrives in moist, swampy environments and has adapted to tolerate waterlogged soils. Its leaves are broad and oval-shaped, with a smooth surface and a slightly waxy texture.

Mephitidia inaequalis plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
