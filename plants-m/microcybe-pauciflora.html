Microcybe pauciflora

Microcybe pauciflora, commonly known as the sparse-flowered microcybe, is a member of the Poaceae family, commonly found in arid and semi-arid regions.

It is a small, herbaceous plant with narrow, grass-like leaves that are adapted to conserve water. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with pale green to brown flowers. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

Microcybe pauciflora plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small insects and animals.
