Modecca refracta

Modecca refracta, commonly known as the refracta plant, is a species native to specific regions in South America.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its slender stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters at the tips of the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
