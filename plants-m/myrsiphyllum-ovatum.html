Myrsiphyllum ovatum

Myrsiphyllum ovatum, commonly known as the silky sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it its common name. Its small, greenish-white flowers are arranged in dense spikes and bloom in the spring.

Myrsiphyllum ovatum plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
