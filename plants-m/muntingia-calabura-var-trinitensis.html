Muntingia calabura var. trinitensis

Muntingia calabura var. trinitensis, commonly known as the West Indian cherry or Natal plum, is a small evergreen tree native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters and are followed by edible, red, cherry-like fruits. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate, with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its ornamental foliage and fruit production.
