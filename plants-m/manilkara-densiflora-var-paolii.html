Manilkara densiflora var. paolii

Manilkara densiflora var. paolii, commonly known as the Philippine bitter apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy foliage and produces small, yellowish-green fruits that are typically bitter when unripe. The plant belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is valued for its latex, which has historical uses in traditional medicine. Its leaves are often used in local remedies, while the tree itself is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
