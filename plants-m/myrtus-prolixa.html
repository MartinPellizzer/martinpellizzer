Myrtus prolixa

Myrtus prolixa, commonly known as the long-fruited myrtle, is a flowering shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and small, white flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional herbal remedies. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Myrtus prolixa is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in perfumery and medicine.
