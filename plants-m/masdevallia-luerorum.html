Masdevallia luerorum

Masdevallia luerorum, commonly known as the Luer's masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and intricate, often colorful flower spikes that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Masdevallia luerorum is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
