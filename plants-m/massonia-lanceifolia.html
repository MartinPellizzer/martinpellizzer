Massonia lanceifolia

Massonia lanceifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved massonia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and fleshy, lance-shaped leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange, typically during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
