Myrceugenia macrosepala var. obovata

Myrceugenia macrosepala var. obovata, commonly known as the Obovate Myrtle, is a species native to the temperate regions of South America, particularly in Chile.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree, characterized by its aromatic, dark green, obovate-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that are followed by edible, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a humid, cool climate for optimal growth.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local herbal medicine.
