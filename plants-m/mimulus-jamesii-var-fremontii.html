Mimulus jamesii var. fremontii

Mimulus jamesii var. fremontii, commonly known as the Fremont's monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow to orange flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along streambanks and wet meadows. It has lance-shaped leaves and is often found in riparian habitats.

This species is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its ornamental appeal in native gardens.
