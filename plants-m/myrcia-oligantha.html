Myrcia oligantha

Myrcia oligantha, commonly known as the Brazilian peppertree, is a flowering plant native to Brazil and parts of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of white or pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in moist, shaded environments.

Myrcia oligantha is also known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
