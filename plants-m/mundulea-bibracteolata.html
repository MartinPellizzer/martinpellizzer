Mundulea bibracteolata

Mundulea bibracteolata, commonly known as the African burr bush, is a shrub native to eastern and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, yellowish-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that are encased in a prickly, bract-like structure, which aids in seed dispersal by attaching to animals. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species adapted to poor soil conditions.

The plant is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
