Milium effusum var. variegatum

Milium effusum var. variegatum, commonly known as the variegated mouse-ear grass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, needle-like leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces tiny, clustered flowers that are typically green or pale yellow, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
