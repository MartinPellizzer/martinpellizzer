Mosla longibracteata

Mosla longibracteata, commonly known as Chinese burnet, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Asia.

It features upright stems covered in soft, hairy foliage and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink or white. The plant is characterized by its distinctive bracts, which are elongated and leaf-like, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

Mosla longibracteata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
