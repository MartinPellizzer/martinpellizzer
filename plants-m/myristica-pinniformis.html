Myristica pinniformis

Myristica pinniformis, commonly known as the pinna leaf nutmeg, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with pinnae that give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and develops distinctive red, fleshy fruits containing the aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine. Its botanical features include a smooth, greyish bark and a dense canopy of foliage.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its potential medicinal properties.
