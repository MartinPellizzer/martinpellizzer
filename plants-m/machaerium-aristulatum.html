Machaerium aristulatum

Machaerium aristulatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved machaerium, is a perennial legume native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, delicate leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs. The plant produces fluffy, pale yellow to white inflorescences, which are composed of numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Machaerium aristulatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
