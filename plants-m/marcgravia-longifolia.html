Marcgravia longifolia

Marcgravia longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved marsh vine, is a tropical plant native to regions of South America.

It is characterized by its long, slender leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine, often found in wet, humid environments near rivers and swamps. Its flowers are small and clustered, with white petals and prominent yellow stamens.
