Mimulus guttatus var. gracilis

Mimulus guttatus var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender yellow monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to western North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and bright yellow, spurred flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along streambanks and in open woodlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
