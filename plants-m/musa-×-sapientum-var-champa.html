Musa × sapientum var. champa

Musa × sapientum var. champa, commonly known as the champa banana, is a cultivated variety of banana characterized by its elongated, slender pseudostem and large, curved leaves.

This plant is a hybrid species within the genus Musa, typically grown for its edible, sweet fruit which has a distinct flavor and texture. The champa banana produces clusters of elongated, yellow fruits that are often used in both culinary and traditional medicinal applications. Botanically, it exhibits a complex genetic structure due to its hybrid origin, contributing to its adaptability and variability in cultivation.

The plant thrives in tropical climates and requires warm temperatures and high humidity for optimal growth.
