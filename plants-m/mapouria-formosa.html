Mapouria formosa

Mapouria formosa, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its drooping clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually ovate or elliptical in shape. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
