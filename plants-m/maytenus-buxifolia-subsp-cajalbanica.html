Maytenus buxifolia subsp. cajalbanica

Maytenus buxifolia subsp.

cajalbanica, commonly known as the Jamaican holly, is a shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with glossy, oval-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange-red berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical regions.
