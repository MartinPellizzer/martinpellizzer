Myrcia amplexicaulis

Myrcia amplexicaulis, commonly known as the clinging myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that often wrap around the stems. Its small, fragrant white flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, dark berries as fruit.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
