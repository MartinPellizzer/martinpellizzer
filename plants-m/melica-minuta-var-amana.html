Melica minuta var. amana

Melica minuta var. amana, commonly known as the dwarf brome, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. The plant has slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny spikelets. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
