Myriophyllum verticillatum subsp. alterniflorum

Myriophyllum verticillatum subsp.

alterniflorum, commonly known as watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to North America. It has whorled leaves that are finely divided, giving it a feathery appearance.

The plant produces small, green flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

Due to its rapid growth, it can become invasive in some regions.
