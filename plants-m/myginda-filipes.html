Myginda filipes

Myginda filipes, commonly known as the slender myginda, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in clusters. Its bark contains compounds that have been traditionally used in local medicine.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting native pollinators.
