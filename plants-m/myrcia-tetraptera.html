Myrcia tetraptera

Myrcia tetraptera, commonly known as the red myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, dark seeds.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments within tropical forests.
