Mentha × villosa var. viridiatra

Mentha × villosa var. viridiatra, commonly known as green hairy mint, is a hybrid species within the mint family (Lamiaceae).

It is characterized by its hairy, often gray-green leaves and a strong, minty aroma. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are used in herbal teas and traditional medicine for their aromatic and therapeutic properties.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary uses.
