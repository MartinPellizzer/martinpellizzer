Morina chlorantha var. subintegra

Morina chlorantha var. subintegra, commonly known as the Chinese sandthorn, is a shrub native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and rough, thorny stems. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its succulent leaves. Its botanical features include pinnately compound leaves and small, fleshy fruits.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
