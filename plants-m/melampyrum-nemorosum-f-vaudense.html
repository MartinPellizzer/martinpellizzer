Melampyrum nemorosum f. vaudense

Melampyrum nemorosum f. vaudense, commonly known as the Vaud yellow betony, is a rare and distinctive variant of the yellow betony species.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist woodland habitats, preferring rich, loamy soils. Its leaves are lanceolate and hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This botanical form is of particular interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and unique genetic characteristics.
