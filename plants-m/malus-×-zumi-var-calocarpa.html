Malus × zumi var. calocarpa

Malus × zumi var. calocarpa, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its ornamental white flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by edible, sweet yellow or red fruits in late summer. The plant has alternate, simple, ovate to elliptical leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The fruit of this variety is often used in both culinary and medicinal applications.
