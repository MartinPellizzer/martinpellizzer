Machaeranthera canescens var. latifolia

Machaeranthera canescens var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf ironweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to the central and eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of purple to pinkish-red composite flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in prairies, wetlands, and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a rough, hairy texture, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that attract various pollinators.

It is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
