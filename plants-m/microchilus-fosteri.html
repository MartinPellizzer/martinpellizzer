Microchilus fosteri

Microchilus fosteri, commonly known as the Foster's microchilus, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its small, intricate flowers that are often camouflaged among leaf litter. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as forest understories, where it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient absorption. Its conservation status is critically endangered due to habitat destruction and over-collection.

Botanists study this species to better understand its unique ecological requirements and to aid in its preservation.
