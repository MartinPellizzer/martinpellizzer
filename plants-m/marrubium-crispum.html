Marrubium crispum

Marrubium crispum, commonly known as the crinkled horehound, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its gray-green, hairy leaves that are often crinkled or wrinkled, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, blue or violet flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas.

Marrubium crispum has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
