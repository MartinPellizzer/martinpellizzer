Mimulus nasutus

Mimulus nasutus, commonly known as the beakweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers that resemble a beak, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in wet or damp habitats, such as marshes and riverbanks, where it thrives in moist soil conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Mimulus nasutus is a herbaceous perennial that produces small, elongated flowers, which attract pollinators like bees and flies.
