Microtea gracilis

Microtea gracilis, commonly known as the slender microtea, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features delicate, grass-like leaves that are often used in traditional herbal practices. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is typically found in dense undergrowth near rivers or in humid forests. Microtea gracilis has a short flowering period, producing tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are often overlooked.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and potential medicinal properties.
