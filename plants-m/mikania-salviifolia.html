Mikania salviifolia

Mikania salviifolia, commonly known as Mexican morning glory, is a climbing vine native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and forests.

Mikania salviifolia is also known for its rapid growth and can become invasive in certain regions.
