Melica taylorii

Melica taylorii, commonly known as Taylor's brome, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as cliffs and slopes. The plant has slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, two-lobed spikelets.

Botanically, it is characterized by its fibrous root system and its ability to thrive in arid conditions.
