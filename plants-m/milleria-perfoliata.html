Milleria perfoliata

Milleria perfoliata, commonly known as the red sandalwood or red milleria, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has a woody stem with opposite leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
