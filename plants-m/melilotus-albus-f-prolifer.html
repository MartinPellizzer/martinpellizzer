Melilotus albus f. prolifer

Melilotus albus f. prolifer, commonly known as white melilot, is a botanical variant of the melilot plant characterized by its pale yellow to white flowers.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in temperate regions, often found in meadows and along roadsides. The plant produces small, fluffy seed pods that release numerous tiny seeds, facilitating its spread. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
