Masdevallia venosa

Masdevallia venosa, commonly known as the veined masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, veined leaves and intricate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant thrives in cool, humid environments and requires indirect light for optimal growth. Its unique morphology and delicate appearance make it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.

Masdevallia venosa is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in greenhouses or terrariums.
