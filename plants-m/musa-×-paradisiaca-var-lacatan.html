Musa × paradisiaca var. lacatan

Musa × paradisiaca var. lacatan, commonly known as the banana plant, is a hybrid species within the genus Musa.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces clusters of berries, each containing a single seed, though most commercial varieties are seedless. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

This variety is widely cultivated for its edible fruit, which is rich in potassium and other essential nutrients.
