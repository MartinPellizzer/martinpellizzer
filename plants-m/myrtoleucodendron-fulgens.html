Myrtoleucodendron fulgens

Myrtoleucodendron fulgens, commonly known as the golden myrtle, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known species like eucalyptus and clove. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Myrtoleucodendron fulgens is also valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
