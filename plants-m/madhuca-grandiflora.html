Madhuca grandiflora

Madhuca grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered madhuca, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and parks. Its fruits are small, capsule-like structures that contain edible seeds.

Madhuca grandiflora plays an important ecological role in supporting local wildlife and maintaining biodiversity in tropical ecosystems.
