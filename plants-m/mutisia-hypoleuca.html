Mutisia hypoleuca

Mutisia hypoleuca, commonly known as the yellow mutisia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in shrubland and dry forests, thriving in well-drained soils. Its yellow flowers are showy and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are usually alternate, with a rough texture and a distinctive aromatic scent.
