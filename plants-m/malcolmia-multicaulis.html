Malcolmia multicaulis

Malcolmia multicaulis, commonly known as the Persian shield, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of South Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, shield-shaped leaves that are often brightly colored, ranging from deep red to purple. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in warm climates.
