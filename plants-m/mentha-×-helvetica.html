Mentha × helvetica

Mentha × helvetica, commonly known as the European mint, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Mentha longifolia and Mentha aquatica.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, featuring square stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a serrated margin. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom during the summer months. Mentha × helvetica is native to Europe and is often found in moist, calcareous soils along riverbanks and wet meadows.

Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its distinctive menthol scent and various medicinal uses.
