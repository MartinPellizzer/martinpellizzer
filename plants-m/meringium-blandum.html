Meringium blandum

Meringium blandum, commonly known as the soft meringue plant, is a rare flowering species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, waxy leaves and clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers. The plant produces a unique, gelatinous fruit that is often used in traditional herbal remedies. Its growth is slow and requires specific conditions of cool temperatures and high humidity.

Due to habitat loss, Meringium blandum is considered endangered and is protected in several conservation programs.
