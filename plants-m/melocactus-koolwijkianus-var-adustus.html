Melocactus koolwijkianus var. adustus

Melocactus koolwijkianus var. adustus, commonly known as the Desert Melon Cactus, is a rare and striking cactus native to the arid regions of northern Argentina.

It is characterized by its globular shape, ribbed structure, and a dense covering of white to grayish spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are pollinated by insects. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat threats, it is considered a vulnerable species in the wild.
