Morus rubra var. missouriensis

Morus rubra var. missouriensis, commonly known as the Missouri mulberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the central United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that turn a striking red or orange in the fall. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically pink to red in color, which are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained soils and full sun.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and its role in supporting local ecosystems.
