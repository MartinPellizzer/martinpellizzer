Malaxis copelandii

Malaxis copelandii, commonly known as the Copeland's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stem and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often clustered at the base of the stem.

Malaxis copelandii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
