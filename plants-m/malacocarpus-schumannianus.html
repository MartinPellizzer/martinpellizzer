Malacocarpus schumannianus

Malacocarpus schumannianus, commonly known as the Schumann's sycamore, is a member of the Meliaceae family, characterized by its unique botanical features.

It is a deciduous tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, particularly in Southeast Asia. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are often palmately lobed, and its flowers are small and clustered in panicles. The fruit is a dry capsule that splits open to release seeds, and the bark is thick and fissured, providing structural support.
