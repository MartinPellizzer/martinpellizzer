Myrtus communis var. romana

Myrtus communis var. romana, commonly known as Roman myrtle, is a compact evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are dark green and glossy, often with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces fragrant white flowers in early spring, which are followed by small, dark blue berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a decorative hedge.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and historical use in herbal remedies and perfumery.
