Myrciaria coronata

Myrciaria coronata, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or araçá-preto, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are rich in vitamin C and are often used in traditional medicine.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
