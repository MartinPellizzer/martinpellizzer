Miconia hottensis

Miconia hottensis, commonly known as the Hottentot miconia, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible but often have a mild, tangy flavor. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in coastal areas and forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
