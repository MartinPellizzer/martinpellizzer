Mentha nobilis

Mentha nobilis, commonly known as peppermint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its square, hollow stems and opposite, serrated leaves that are typically a deep green in color. The plant produces small, pale pink or purple flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom in late summer. Peppermint is known for its strong, aromatic scent and is widely cultivated for its essential oils.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.
