Mentha × dumetorum var. cinerea

Mentha × dumetorum var. cinerea, commonly known as purple mint, is a hybrid species within the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive violet or purple foliage, which sets it apart from other mint varieties. The plant typically grows as a low-growing perennial, with square stems and opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, tubular, and usually pink or purple, appearing in whorls along the stem.

This variety is valued for its aromatic leaves and is often used in herbal teas and culinary applications.
