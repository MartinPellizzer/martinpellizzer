Matthiola nudicaulis

Matthiola nudicaulis, commonly known as the naked-stem wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant is characterized by its slender, hairless stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. It typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in disturbed areas.

Matthiola nudicaulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
