Miconia oligantha

Miconia oligantha, commonly known as the red miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, attracting various pollinators. Its fruit is a three-lobed capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is considered a problematic species in some regions.
