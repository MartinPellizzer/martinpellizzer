Myrcia neoobscura

Myrcia neoobscura, commonly known as the black myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest of eastern Brazil, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit is a small, black drupe that contains several seeds.

Myrcia neoobscura is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its aromatic properties and ecological importance in its native habitat.
