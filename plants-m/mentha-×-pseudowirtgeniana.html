Mentha × pseudowirtgeniana

Mentha × pseudowirtgeniana, commonly known as the hybrid mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or violet in color. Mentha × pseudowirtgeniana is known for its strong menthol scent and is often used in aromatherapy and herbal remedies.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.
