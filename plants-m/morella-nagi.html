Morella nagi

Morella nagi, commonly known as the Japanese waxleaf buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in temperate climates.
