Morus alba var. arabica

Morus alba var. arabica, commonly known as white mulberry, is a variant of the mulberry tree native to Asia.

It is characterized by its pale to white leaves, which distinguish it from other mulberry species. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its leaves, which are used in sericulture.

This variety is also valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
