Malus yunnanensis

Malus yunnanensis, commonly known as the Yunnan apple, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental value due to its showy pink to red flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are similar in appearance to apples but are less commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens.

The tree is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
