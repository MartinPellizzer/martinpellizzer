Muhlenbergia versicolor

Muhlenbergia versicolor, commonly known as the rainbow grass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful inflorescences that range from pink to red, purple, and violet, creating a visually appealing display. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and stream banks. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers.

Muhlenbergia versicolor is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
