Maxillaria setigera var. carolii

Maxillaria setigera var. carolii, commonly known as Carol's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant typically blooms during the summer months, and its inflorescences are often adorned with a mix of spurred and non-spurred flowers. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is popular among orchid enthusiasts.
