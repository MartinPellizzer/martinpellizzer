Milium vernale var. alexeenkoi

Milium vernale var. alexeenkoi, commonly known as the spring millet, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, white to pale green florets. The plant typically blooms in early spring, showcasing its delicate, wind-pollinated flowers. It is native to regions of Europe and western Asia, often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
