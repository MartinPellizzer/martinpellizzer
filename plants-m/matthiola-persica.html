Matthiola persica

Matthiola persica, commonly known as Persian stock, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to regions of the Middle East and Central Asia, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are typically hairy and somewhat succulent.

Matthiola persica is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its historical use in traditional medicine.
