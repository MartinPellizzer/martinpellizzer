Malva qaiseri

Malva qaiseri, commonly known as the Kashmir rose or Persian mallow, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus, and is characterized by its hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
