Mirabilis linearis var. linearis

Mirabilis linearis var. linearis, commonly known as the linear spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing a fragrant, tubular flower that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
