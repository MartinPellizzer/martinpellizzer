Markhamia infundibuliformis

Markhamia infundibuliformis, commonly known as the Cape snowball, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spherical clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. It produces a distinctive, lantern-like inflorescence that resembles a snowball, giving it its common name.

Markhamia infundibuliformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
