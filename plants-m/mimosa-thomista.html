Mimosa thomista

Mimosa thomista, commonly known as the sensitive plant, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves that fold shut when touched. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its unique response to touch, known as thigmonastic movement, is a notable botanical feature.
