Macaranga loheri

Macaranga loheri, commonly known as the white-veined macaranga, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves with prominent white veins. The plant is often found in lowland rainforests and can grow up to 10 meters in height. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences, typically greenish-yellow in color.

The species is noted for its rapid growth and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
