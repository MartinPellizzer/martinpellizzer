Mertensia arizonica var. grahamii

Mertensia arizonica var. grahamii, commonly known as Graham's bluebells, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant produces clusters of striking blue to violet bell-shaped flowers in early spring. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, while its stems are often covered in fine hairs.

This variety is closely related to other Mertensia species and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
