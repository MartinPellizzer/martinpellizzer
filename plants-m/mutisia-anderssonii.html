Mutisia anderssonii

Mutisia anderssonii, commonly known as the Andes mutisia, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, rocky habitats and is adapted to cold, mountainous climates. The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in cooler regions.
