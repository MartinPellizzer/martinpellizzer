Mariscus capillaris

Mariscus capillaris, commonly known as the slender sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It belongs to the family Montiaceae and is characterized by its small, fleshy leaves and clusters of tiny, white or pale pink flowers. Mariscus capillaris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in xeriscaping.
