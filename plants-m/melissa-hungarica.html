Melissa hungarica

Melissa hungarica, commonly known as Hungarian lemon balm, is a perennial herb native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are often used in herbal teas and have a mild, lemony scent.

Melissa hungarica is valued for its calming properties and is commonly grown in gardens for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
