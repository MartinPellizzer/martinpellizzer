Myriopus isabellinus

Myriopus isabellinus, commonly known as the Isabelline grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit with fine, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Due to its adaptability, it plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
