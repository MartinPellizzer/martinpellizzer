Medicago coelesyriaca

Medicago coelesyriaca, commonly known as the Armenian clover, is a perennial legume native to the Caucasus region.

It features trifoliate leaves with a heart-shaped leaflet and produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters. The plant has a robust root system that helps it thrive in rocky and arid soils. It is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil enrichment.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
