Malus sieversii f. viridirubra

Malus sieversii f. viridirubra, commonly known as the green-red apple, is a naturally occurring variant of the wild apple species Malus sieversii.

It is characterized by its unique foliage, which displays a striking combination of green and red hues, especially during the growing season. The leaves are typically larger and more elongated compared to those of the standard species, adding to its ornamental value. This form is native to Central Asia and is often found in mountainous regions where it thrives in temperate climates.

Its distinctive appearance makes it a sought-after plant for botanical gardens and horticultural collections.
