Montanoa bipinnatifida

Montanoa bipinnatifida, commonly known as the Mexican featherflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are deeply divided into narrow, feather-like segments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Montanoa bipinnatifida is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
