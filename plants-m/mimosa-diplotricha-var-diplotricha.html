Mimosa diplotricha var. diplotricha

Mimosa diplotricha var. diplotricha, commonly known as the silky mimosa, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its soft, feathery foliage and clusters of small, yellow, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that split open to release seeds, a feature typical of the Fabaceae family. It prefers well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Its delicate appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
