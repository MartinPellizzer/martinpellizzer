Mimusops indica

Mimusops indica, commonly known as the Indian satinwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to southern Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds, and the tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

The wood of Mimusops indica is hard and durable, making it valuable for furniture and carving.
