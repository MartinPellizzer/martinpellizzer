Miscanthus sinensis f. hashimotoi

Miscanthus sinensis f. hashimotoi, commonly known as the Hashimoto's miscanthus, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its ornamental, fountain-shaped growth habit. The plant features narrow, silver-green leaves that turn reddish in autumn, adding visual interest throughout the growing season. It produces fluffy, pinkish-white plumes in late summer, which attract pollinators.

This ornamental grass is often used in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and low maintenance requirements.
