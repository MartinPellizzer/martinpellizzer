Mimosa gracilis f. hirsutissima

Mimosa gracilis f. hirsutissima, commonly known as the hairy mimosa, is a variant of the slender mimosa.

It is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant features bipinnate leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, yellow, spherical flower heads that bloom in spring.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adaptable to a variety of soil types.
