Machaerium chiapense

Machaerium chiapense, commonly known as the Chiapas machaerium, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Central America, particularly found in countries like Mexico and Guatemala. The plant is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Machaerium chiapense is sometimes used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
