Malus punctata

Malus punctata, commonly known as the spotted apple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its oval-shaped leaves with a distinctive white or pale green dot on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by edible apples that are typically small and sour. Its fruit is often used in traditional Chinese medicine and as a food source.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
