Masdevallia irapana

Masdevallia irapana, commonly known as the Irapa orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, often colorful flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are fleshy and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

Masdevallia irapana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
