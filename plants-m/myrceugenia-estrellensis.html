Myrceugenia estrellensis

Myrceugenia estrellensis, commonly known as the Estrella myrtle, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves and small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in the spring. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in cloud forests.

The fruit is a small, fleshy berry that contains several seeds, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.
