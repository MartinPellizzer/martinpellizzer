Mikania pittieri

Mikania pittieri, commonly known as the Mexican vine or wild chuchuhuasi, is a climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in heads, typical of the daisy family. Mikania pittieri is often found in moist, shaded areas such as forests and stream banks.

It is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
