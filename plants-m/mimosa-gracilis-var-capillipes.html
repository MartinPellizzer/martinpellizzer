Mimosa gracilis var. capillipes

Mimosa gracilis var. capillipes, commonly known as the slender mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery leaves and small, pinkish-white flowers that appear in clusters. The plant is often found in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and wetlands. It has a shallow root system and is adapted to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

Its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed areas make it an important pioneer species in ecological succession.
