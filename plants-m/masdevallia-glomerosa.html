Masdevallia glomerosa

Masdevallia glomerosa, commonly known as the clustered masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, colorful flowers that bloom in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant typically grows at high altitudes, thriving in cool, humid environments with partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces intricate, spiral-shaped inflorescences.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique and delicate appearance.
