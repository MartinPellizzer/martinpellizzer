Marrubium microphyllum

Marrubium microphyllum, commonly known as the small-leaved horehound, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It features compact, low-growing stems and small, hairy leaves that are typically less than 1 centimeter in length. The plant produces clusters of small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal and Mediterranean regions.

Marrubium microphyllum is valued for its medicinal properties and has been traditionally used in herbal remedies.
