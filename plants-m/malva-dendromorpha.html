Malva dendromorpha

Malva dendromorpha, commonly known as the tree mallows, is a flowering plant species native to certain regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Malva dendromorpha is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
