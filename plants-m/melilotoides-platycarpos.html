Melilotoides platycarpos

Melilotoides platycarpos, commonly known as the broad-leaved melilot, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it valuable for soil enrichment in agricultural settings.
