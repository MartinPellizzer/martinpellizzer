Manihot dalechampiiformis

Manihot dalechampiiformis, commonly known as the Brazilian arrowroot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its tuberous roots that are rich in starch. The plant typically grows in moist, tropical environments and has simple, alternate leaves with a smooth texture. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, clustered in cyathia, which is typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

The tubers are often used as a food source and have a mild, slightly sweet flavor.
