Myrcia macrophylla

Myrcia macrophylla, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant is part of the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and guava. Its fruits are small, red berries that are edible but often have a sharp, acidic taste.

Myrcia macrophylla is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has also been used in traditional medicine.
