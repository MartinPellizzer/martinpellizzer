Mentha serpyllifolia var. latifolia

Mentha serpyllifolia var. latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf field mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a serrated margin. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, contributing to its use in traditional herbal medicine.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks.
