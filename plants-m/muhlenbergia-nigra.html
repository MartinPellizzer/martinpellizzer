Muhlenbergia nigra

Muhlenbergia nigra, commonly known as black muhlenbergia, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It features tall, slender stalks with narrow, dark green leaves that turn reddish in the fall. The plant produces fluffy, silver-gray seed heads that add ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Muhlenbergia nigra is drought-tolerant and is often used in native plant gardens for its ecological benefits.
