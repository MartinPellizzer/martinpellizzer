Morus grisea

Morus grisea, commonly known as the grey mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive greyish-green bark. The tree produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically eaten by birds and other animals. It is often cultivated for its edible fruits and as a shade tree.

Morus grisea is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
