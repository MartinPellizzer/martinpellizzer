Myrcia crassifolia

Myrcia crassifolia, commonly known as Brazilian pepper tree or myrtle, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a fleshy, red berry that contains several seeds.

Myrcia crassifolia is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in moist, shaded environments.
