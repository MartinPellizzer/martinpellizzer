Mentha javanica

Mentha javanica, commonly known as Java mint, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like mint and basil. The plant features square stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a serrated edge. Its fragrant leaves emit a strong, cooling menthol scent when crushed.

Mentha javanica is often cultivated for its medicinal properties and use in traditional herbal remedies.
