Medica falcata

Medica falcata, commonly known as the sickle vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive sickle-shaped leaves, which give it its scientific name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits. Its stems are woody and can grow quite long, allowing it to climb trees or other structures.

Medica falcata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
