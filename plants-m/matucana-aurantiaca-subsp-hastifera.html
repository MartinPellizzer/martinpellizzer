Matucana aurantiaca subsp. hastifera

Matucana aurantiaca subsp.

hastifera, commonly known as the red-veined arrowhead, is a bromeliad native to South America. It is characterized by its striking, elongated leaves that are often reddish or bronze-veined, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces tall, branching inflorescences with small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity and well-draining soil.

This species is popular as a decorative plant due to its unique foliage and ease of care.
