Miconia chaenopleuroides

Miconia chaenopleuroides, commonly known as the velvetleaf miconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers, followed by bright red, fleshy fruits that contain numerous seeds. It is often found in humid, lowland forests and can grow up to several meters in height.

Due to its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, it is considered an invasive species in some regions.
