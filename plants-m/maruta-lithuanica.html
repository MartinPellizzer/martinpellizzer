Maruta lithuanica

Maruta lithuanica, commonly known as the Lithuanian marula, is a small evergreen shrub native to the temperate regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant features dark green, leathery leaves that are arranged in a whorled pattern along the stems. It produces small, bell-shaped white or pale pink flowers in the spring, followed by edible, yellowish berries that are rich in nutrients.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
