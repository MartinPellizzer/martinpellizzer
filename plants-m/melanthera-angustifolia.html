Melanthera angustifolia

Melanthera angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf melanthera, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically less than 2 inches in length. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

Melanthera angustifolia is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other moist habitats.
