Malva havanensis

Malva havanensis, commonly known as the Havana mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly Cuba.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and gardens.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional medicine.
