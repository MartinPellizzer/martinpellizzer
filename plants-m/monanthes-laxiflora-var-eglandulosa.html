Monanthes laxiflora var. eglandulosa

Monanthes laxiflora var. eglandulosa, commonly known as the glandular monanthes, is a flowering plant native to specific regions in Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant produces glandular hairs on its leaves and stems, which contribute to its distinctive appearance and ecological adaptations. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its flowers, typically pink or purple, attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
