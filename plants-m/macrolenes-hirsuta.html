Macrolenes hirsuta

Macrolenes hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy macrolene, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and stems, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in ecological and horticultural contexts.
