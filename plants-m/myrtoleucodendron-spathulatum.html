Myrtoleucodendron spathulatum

Myrtoleucodendron spathulatum, commonly known as the broad-leaved myrtle, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its large, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
