Microlepia pallida

Microlepia pallida, commonly known as the pale microlepia, is a small fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, light green fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are short and creeping, supporting the growth of its bipinnate leaves.

Microlepia pallida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded gardens.
