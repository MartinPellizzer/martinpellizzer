Miconia aenigmatica

Miconia aenigmatica, commonly known as the enigmatic miconia, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in diameter, making it one of the largest leaf plants in the world. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that are often surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of rainforests.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is considered a problematic species in some regions.
