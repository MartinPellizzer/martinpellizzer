Malva recta

Malva recta, commonly known as the upright mallow, is a flowering plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit, broad heart-shaped leaves, and showy pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa, often found in dry, sunny habitats. Its hairy stems and lobed leaves are typical features of the mallow genus.

Malva recta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
