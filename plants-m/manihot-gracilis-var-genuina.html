Manihot gracilis var. genuina

Manihot gracilis var. genuina, commonly known as the slender arrowleaf manioc, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its slender, arrow-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its roots are used in traditional medicine and as a source of starch, similar to other manioc species.

It thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in shaded, humid woodlands.
