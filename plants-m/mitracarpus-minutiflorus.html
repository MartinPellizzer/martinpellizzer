Mitracarpus minutiflorus

Mitracarpus minutiflorus, commonly known as the dwarf cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with slender stems and trifoliate leaves. Its fruit is a small, hairy legume that contains several seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible seeds in some regions.
