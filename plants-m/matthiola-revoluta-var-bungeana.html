Matthiola revoluta var. bungeana

Matthiola revoluta var. bungeana, commonly known as the Bunge's wallflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has grey-green, hairy leaves and produces small, elongated seed pods that mature in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
