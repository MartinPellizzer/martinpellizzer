Memecylon buchananii

Memecylon buchananii, commonly known as the African memecylon, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by bright red berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in horticulture.
