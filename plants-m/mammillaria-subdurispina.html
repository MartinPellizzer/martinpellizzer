Mammillaria subdurispina

Mammillaria subdurispina, commonly known as the spiny mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are covered with white, woolly spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
