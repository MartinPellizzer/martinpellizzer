Manilkara roxburghiana

Manilkara roxburghiana, commonly known as the Indian shea tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and large, elliptical leaves. The tree produces yellowish flowers and develops large, round fruits with a thick, inedible outer layer. Its latex, known as chicle, is historically used in the production of chewing gum.

The plant is also valued for its economic and ecological importance in its native habitats.
