Myrcia albobrunnea

Myrcia albobrunnea, commonly known as the white-barked myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Myrcia albobrunnea is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
