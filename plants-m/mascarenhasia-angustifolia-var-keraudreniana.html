Mascarenhasia angustifolia var. keraudreniana

Mascarenhasia angustifolia var. keraudreniana, commonly known as the Mascarene narrowleaf, is a flowering plant native to the Mascarene Islands.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and windy conditions. It has a woody stem and produces small, capsule-like fruits.

This species is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique adaptations and limited distribution.
