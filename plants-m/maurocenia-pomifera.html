Maurocenia pomifera

Maurocenia pomifera, commonly known as the golden apple tree, is a tropical plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant is notable for its edible, sweet, and fleshy fruits that resemble small apples. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruit, it is cultivated in some tropical regions for both aesthetic and culinary purposes.
