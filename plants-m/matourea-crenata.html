Matourea crenata

Matourea crenata, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests. Its botanical features include showy, five-petaled flowers and a fibrous root system adapted to rocky soils.

Matourea crenata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
