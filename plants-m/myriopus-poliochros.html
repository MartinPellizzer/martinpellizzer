Myriopus poliochros

Myriopus poliochros, commonly known as the bluebottle grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.

The plant has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.
