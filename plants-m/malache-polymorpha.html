Malache polymorpha

Malache polymorpha, commonly known as the variegated croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, multicolored leaves that display shades of green, yellow, and red. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in warm, humid environments. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The plant is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
