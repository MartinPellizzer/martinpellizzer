Moschifera pinnatifida

Moschifera pinnatifida, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water lily, is a aquatic plant native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that float on the water's surface, supported by long, flexible petioles. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the early morning and close by midday. Its roots are anchored in the mud at the bottom of still or slow-moving water bodies.

Moschifera pinnatifida plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various water organisms.
