Menispermum smilacinum

Menispermum smilacinum, commonly known as the Indian snakeroot, is a climbing plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces small, greenish-white berries that are edible but have a mild, sometimes bitter taste. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
