Melaleuca divaricata

Melaleuca divaricata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved paperbark, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia, where it typically grows in coastal and sandy soils. The plant is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds.

Melaleuca divaricata is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
