Mammillaria vivipara var. aggregata

Mammillaria vivipara var. aggregata, commonly known as the clustered mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and dense clusters of small, ribbed stems. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are borne on short pedicels. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and ease of care, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and succulent collections.
