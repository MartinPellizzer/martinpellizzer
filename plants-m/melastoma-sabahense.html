Melastoma sabahense

Melastoma sabahense, commonly known as the Sabah melastoma, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Melastomataceae and is characterized by its opposite, lanceolate leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The flowers are typically pink or white, with five petals and prominent stamens, blooming during the rainy season. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
