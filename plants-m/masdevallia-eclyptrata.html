Masdevallia eclyptrata

Masdevallia eclyptrata, commonly known as the helmet orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the cloud forests of Colombia.

It is characterized by its unique, helmet-shaped spathes that enclose the inflorescence, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments and requires high air humidity and indirect light for optimal growth. Its flowers are usually small and come in shades of white, pink, and purple, adding to its ornamental value.

Masdevallia eclyptrata is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is appreciated by orchid enthusiasts for its distinctive appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
