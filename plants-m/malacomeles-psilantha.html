Malacomeles psilantha

Malacomeles psilantha, commonly known as the slender leaf cycad, is a unique cycad species native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that grow in a dense, rosette-like formation. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered plant species.
