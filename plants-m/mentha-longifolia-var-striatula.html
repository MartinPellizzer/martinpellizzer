Mentha longifolia var. striatula

Mentha longifolia var. striatula, commonly known as the striped mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive white or pale green stripe running along the midrib. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in whorled clusters during the summer months. Its fragrant leaves are often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
