Murraya glabra

Murraya glabra, commonly known as the white murraya, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale green bark and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that bloom throughout the year, followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Murraya glabra is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
