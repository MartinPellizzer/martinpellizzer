Melaleuca cardiophylla var. parviflora

Melaleuca cardiophylla var. parviflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tea tree, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of white to greyish hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer. Its aromatic foliage is often used in traditional Aboriginal medicine.

It prefers well-drained soils and is commonly found in coastal and heathland environments.
