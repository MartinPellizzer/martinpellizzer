Mareya micrantha var. leonensis

Mareya micrantha var. leonensis, commonly known as the Leonense Mareya, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a decorative plant.
