Myrcia perlaevigata

Myrcia perlaevigata, commonly known as the pink myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has dark green, elliptical leaves with a slightly glossy surface. It typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters.

Myrcia perlaevigata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
