Myrcia splendens

Myrcia splendens, commonly known as the golden myrtle, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible and often consumed by birds and small mammals. Myrcia splendens thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in rainforests and coastal areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical climates.
