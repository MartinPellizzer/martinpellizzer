Myrica kilimandscharica var. macrophylla

Myrica kilimandscharica var. macrophylla, commonly known as the giant African myrica, is a shrub or small tree native to the highland regions of East Africa.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves that can grow up to 15 cm in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark, fleshy berries that are edible for some animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and rocky areas.

This species plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
