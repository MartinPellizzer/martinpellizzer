Mandragora officinarum f. hispanica

Mandragora officinarum f. hispanica, commonly known as the Spanish mandrake, is a variant of the mandrake plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves and its flowers that range from white to pale pink. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that contain toxic seeds. It is often associated with traditional herbal medicine and folklore due to its potent alkaloid content.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the nightshade family, Solanaceae.
