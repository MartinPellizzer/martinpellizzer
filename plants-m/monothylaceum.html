Monothylaceum

Monothylaceum, commonly known as the common thyme, is a small genus of flowering plants in the family Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its aromatic, herbaceous stems and small, opposite leaves. The flowers are typically clustered in whorls and are usually pink or purple in color. Monothylaceum species are native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, and they thrive in well-drained soils.

These plants are often used in traditional medicine and as culinary herbs due to their strong scent and medicinal properties.
