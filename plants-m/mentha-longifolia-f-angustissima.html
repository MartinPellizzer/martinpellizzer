Mentha longifolia f. angustissima

Mentha longifolia f. angustissima, commonly known as narrow-leaved mint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, often growing in rocky or calcareous habitats. It is a subspecies of Mentha longifolia, distinguished by its narrower leaves and more compact growth habit.

This variety is valued for its aromatic leaves and is used in herbal teas and traditional medicine.
