Myrcia duchassaingiana

Myrcia duchassaingiana, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of white to pink flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruits are small, dark berries that contain seeds, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Myrcia duchassaingiana is also valued for its essential oils, which are used in traditional medicine and aromatherapy.
