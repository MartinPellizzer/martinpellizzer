Macrococculus pomifer

Macrococculus pomifer, commonly known as the Chinese date tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to southern China.

It belongs to the family Anacardiaceae and is characterized by its large, elongated leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The tree produces edible, sweet fruits that are similar in appearance to dates, hence its common name. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has cultural significance in traditional Chinese medicine.
