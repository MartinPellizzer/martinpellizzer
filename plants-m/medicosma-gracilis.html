Medicosma gracilis

Medicosma gracilis, commonly known as the slender false indigo, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its flowers are small, blue to purple, and appear in clustered racemes during the spring.

Medicosoma gracilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
