Mucuna cuatrecasasii

Mucuna cuatrecasasii, commonly known as the Cuatrecasas' velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and distinctive, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by elongated pods containing flat, kidney-shaped seeds. It is adapted to thrive in humid, warm climates and is often found in secondary forests and disturbed habitats.

Mucuna cuatrecasasii is noted for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil and supports ecological succession.
