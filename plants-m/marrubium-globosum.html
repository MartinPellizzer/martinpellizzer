Marrubium globosum

Marrubium globosum, commonly known as globular horehound, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and sunny environments. The plant features erect, branched stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a grayish-green color. Its flowers are small, clustered in globular inflorescences, and are usually pink or purple in color.

Marrubium globosum is known for its aromatic properties and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.
