Musa kattuvazhana

Musa kattuvazhana, commonly known as the Kattuvazhhan banana, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly India and Sri Lanka.

It is a herbaceous plant that grows in tropical climates, characterized by its large, broad leaves and tall, clustered inflorescences. The plant produces edible berries that are typically consumed fresh or used in culinary applications. Its botanical features include a rhizomatous root system and a robust, fibrous stem.

Musa kattuvazhana is valued not only for its fruit but also for its ornamental qualities and traditional uses in local cultures.
