Mischobulbum wrayanum

Mischobulbum wrayanum, commonly known as the golden bell tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that resemble small bells, which bloom in clusters along the branches. The plant has a woody stem and produces small, elliptical leaves that are opposite in arrangement. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

The golden bell tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
