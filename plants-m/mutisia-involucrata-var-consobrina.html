Mutisia involucrata var. consobrina

Mutisia involucrata var. consobrina, commonly known as the yellow mutisia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in regions of Brazil and Paraguay.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a woody stem with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its most striking feature is the bright yellow flower head, which consists of numerous small, tubular florets arranged in a disk-like structure.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant color and compact growth habit.
