Microrhamnus franchetiana

Microrhamnus franchetiana, commonly known as the Chinese buckthorn, is a shrub native to China and is part of the Rhamnaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or scrub habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
