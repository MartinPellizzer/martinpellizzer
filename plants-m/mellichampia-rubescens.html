Mellichampia rubescens

Mellichampia rubescens, commonly known as the red-flowered mellichampia, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces striking red or pink flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its growth habit is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, with deep root systems that help it access water from deeper soil layers.

Mellichampia rubescens is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is a valuable component of local ecosystems.
