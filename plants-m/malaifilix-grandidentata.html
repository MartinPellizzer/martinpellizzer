Malaifilix grandidentata

Malaifilix grandidentata, commonly known as the giant toothed fern, is a striking member of the Polypodiaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, compound fronds that can reach lengths of up to three meters. The fronds are divided into broad, serrated segments, giving the plant its distinctive appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
