Mentha rigida

Mentha rigida, commonly known as rigid mint, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, serrated leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in whorled clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Mentha rigida is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in herbal remedies.
