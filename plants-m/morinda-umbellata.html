Morinda umbellata

Morinda umbellata, commonly known as the red sandalwood or Indian madder, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its elliptical leaves and showy red flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds, and it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. Morinda umbellata is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various health purposes.

Its roots and leaves contain compounds that have been studied for their potential therapeutic benefits.
