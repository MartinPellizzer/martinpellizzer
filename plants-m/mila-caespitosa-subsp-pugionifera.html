Mila caespitosa subsp. pugionifera

Mila caespitosa subsp.

pugionifera, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mila, is a flowering plant native to South America. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or disturbed areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in various environmental conditions.
