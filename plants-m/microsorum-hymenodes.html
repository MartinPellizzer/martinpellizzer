Microsorum hymenodes

Microsorum hymenodes, commonly known as the golden sword fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are typically bright green with a metallic sheen. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments and is often found in shaded, moist habitats. Its fronds are divided into lance-shaped segments, and it produces small, spore-bearing structures called sorus.

Microsorum hymenodes is popular in aquascaping due to its aesthetic appeal and ease of care in aquariums.
