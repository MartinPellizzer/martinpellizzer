Miscanthus condensatus var. intermedius

Miscanthus condensatus var. intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate dense miscanthus, is a grass-like plant native to parts of East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, blade-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces panicles of small, fluffy flowers that bloom in late summer, adding a decorative element to gardens. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshy or swampy habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting various wildlife species.
