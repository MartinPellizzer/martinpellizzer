Myristica globosa subsp. chalmersii

Myristica globosa subsp.

chalmersii, commonly known as the Chalmers' nutmeg, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Indian Ocean islands. It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, oily seeds that are used in culinary and medicinal applications.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and elliptical, leathery leaves. Its fruit is a bright red, fleshy drupe that contains the valuable seed.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
