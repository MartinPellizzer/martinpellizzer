Melancium campestre

Melancium campestre, commonly known as the field mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant features showy pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish in color.

Melancium campestre is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
