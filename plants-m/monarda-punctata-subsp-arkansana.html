Monarda punctata subsp. arkansana

Monarda punctata subsp.

arkansana, commonly known as the Arkansas beeplant, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States. It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, square stems and showy, tubular flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies. Its flowers are primarily visited by bees and other pollinators, making it an important species for supporting pollinator populations.

The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
