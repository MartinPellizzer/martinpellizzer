Monochaetum talamancense

Monochaetum talamancense, commonly known as the Talamancense spikegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the grass family Poaceae. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded, forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
