Myrceugenia exsucca var. patagua

Myrceugenia exsucca var. patagua, commonly known as the Patagonian myrtle, is a shrub or small tree native to southern South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered together. Its fruits are woody, capsule-like structures that contain several seeds.

This species is adapted to harsh, arid environments and is often found in dry forests and shrublands.
