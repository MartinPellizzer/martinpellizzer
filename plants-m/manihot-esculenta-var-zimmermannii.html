Manihot esculenta var. zimmermannii

Manihot esculenta var. zimmermannii, commonly known as the African yam bean, is a tropical plant native to Africa.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are rich in starch and protein. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often grown for both food and ornamental purposes.

Its botanical features make it an important species in traditional agriculture and local diets.
