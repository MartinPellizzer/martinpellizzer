Moquinia polymorpha var. obtusifolia

Moquinia polymorpha var. obtusifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf moquinia, is a variant of the moquinia plant native to wetland environments in the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white in color. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical features make it an important indicator species for wetland ecosystems.
