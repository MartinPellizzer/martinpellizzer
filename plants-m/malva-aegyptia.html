Malva aegyptia

Malva aegyptia, commonly known as Egyptian mallows, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is typically found in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes other plants like cotton and hibiscus.

Malva aegyptia is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and ease of growth.
