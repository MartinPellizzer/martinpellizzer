Mirabilis elegans var. dichotoma

Mirabilis elegans var. dichotoma, commonly known as the twin-stemmed four o'clock, is a strikingly ornamental plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that open in the late afternoon and remain open through the night. The plant features a distinctive branching pattern, with two main stems emerging from the base, giving it its varietal name. Its foliage is lance-shaped and covered in a fine, fuzzy texture, adding to its visual appeal.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.
