Mollinedia brasiliensis

Mollinedia brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian fern, is a striking evergreen shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It features dense, leathery foliage with elliptical leaves that are often tinged with red or purple hues. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by round, fleshy fruits. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Mollinedia brasiliensis is also noted for its ability to tolerate partial sunlight, making it a versatile choice for garden cultivation.
