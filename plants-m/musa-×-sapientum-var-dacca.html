Musa × sapientum var. dacca

Musa × sapientum var. dacca, commonly known as the Dacca banana, is a cultivar of the banana plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, elongated, and yellowish-green pseudostems that support its clustered inflorescence. The fruit is typically elongated, with a thick peel and sweet, soft pulp that has a mild flavor. It is often cultivated for its high yield and adaptability to various climates.

This variety is also valued for its use in both culinary and commercial applications.
