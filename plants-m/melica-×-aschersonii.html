Melica × aschersonii

Melica × aschersonii, commonly known as the hybrid meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, leafy stems. The plant typically produces panicles of small, spikelet-based flowers, which are often greenish or pale brown in color. Melica × aschersonii is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, where it thrives in moist to moderately dry soils.

Its botanical features make it a common subject in ecological studies and botanical gardens.
