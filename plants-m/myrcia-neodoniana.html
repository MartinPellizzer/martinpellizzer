Myrcia neodoniana

Myrcia neodoniana, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers. It typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters.

Myrcia neodoniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
