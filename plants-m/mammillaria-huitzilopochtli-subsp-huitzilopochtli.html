Mammillaria huitzilopochtli subsp. huitzilopochtli

Mammillaria huitzilopochtli subsp.

huitzilopochtli, commonly known as the Aztec cactus, is a small, globular cactus native to Mexico. It features a dense covering of white to pale green spines and is characterized by its distinctive, tuberculate structure.

The plant typically produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.

This subspecies is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in rock gardens and as a houseplant.
