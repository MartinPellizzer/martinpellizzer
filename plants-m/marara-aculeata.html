Marara aculeata

Marara aculeata, commonly known as the spiny cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water, enabling it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a splash of color to its desert habitat. Its spiny appearance serves as a defense mechanism against herbivores.

Marara aculeata is also valued for its ability to prevent soil erosion in sandy landscapes.
