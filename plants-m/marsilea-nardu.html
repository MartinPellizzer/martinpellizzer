Marsilea nardu

Marsilea nardu, commonly known as water clover, is a water fern belonging to the family Marsileaceae.

It is characterized by its small, floating leaves that resemble clover leaves, typically with four leaflets. The plant thrives in freshwater environments such as ponds and slow-moving streams. Its reproductive structures include tiny, floating spore cases that release spores into the water.

Marsilea nardu plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and small aquatic life.
