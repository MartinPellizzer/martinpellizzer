Mundulea micrantha

Mundulea micrantha, commonly known as the African burr acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and produces yellow flowers followed by woody pods containing small, hard seeds. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought resistance through its deep root system. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
