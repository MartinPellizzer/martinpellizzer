Meconopsis sinomaculata

Meconopsis sinomaculata, commonly known as the Chinese blue poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet petals, which are often speckled with darker hues, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in alpine environments, thriving at high altitudes with cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, hairy coating, which helps protect it from harsh weather.

Meconopsis sinomaculata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
