Macowania pinifolia

Macowania pinifolia, commonly known as the pineleaf macaw, is a species of bird native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant plumage, which includes shades of blue, green, and yellow. The bird's diet primarily consists of fruits, seeds, and nuts, with a particular preference for those found in pine and other coniferous trees. Its beak is strong and adapted for cracking open tough seed shells.

The species plays an important role in seed dispersal within its ecosystem.
