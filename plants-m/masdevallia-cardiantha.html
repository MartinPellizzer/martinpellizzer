Masdevallia cardiantha

Masdevallia cardiantha, commonly known as the Andean orchid, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of South America, particularly in the Andes.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a compact growth habit, featuring a pseudobulb and a single, slender stem. The plant produces delicate, pendulous flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with intricate patterns. It thrives in cool, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
