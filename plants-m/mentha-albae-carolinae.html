Mentha albae-carolinae

Mentha albae-carolinae, commonly known as the Carolina mint, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue or white flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Mentha albae-carolinae is a member of the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is known for its refreshing, slightly minty fragrance.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and forested areas.
