Mollinedia salicifolia var. campanulacea

Mollinedia salicifolia var. campanulacea, commonly known as the bluebell bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and often have a slightly serrated margin. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in woodland gardens.
