Minaea

Minaea, commonly known as the marsh mallow, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Malvaceae.

These plants are characterized by their soft, velvety leaves and showy, often pink or purple flowers. Minaea species are typically found in wet or marshy environments, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. They produce capsule fruits that contain numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds.

The genus includes several species, some of which are used in traditional medicine or as ornamental plants.
