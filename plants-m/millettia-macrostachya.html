Millettia macrostachya

Millettia macrostachya, commonly known as the giant senna, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnate with numerous small leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, racemose inflorescences, which are often showy and attract pollinators. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds, and it is valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional uses.

Millettia macrostachya is also noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its resilience to drought conditions.
