Mauritia duckei

Mauritia duckei, commonly known as the golden apple tree, is a species of palm native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces yellow-orange fruits that are edible and have a sweet, juicy flavor. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, and it is primarily pollinated by insects.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appearance and has some traditional uses in local communities.
