Magnolia × elegantifolia

Magnolia × elegantifolia, commonly known as the sweetbay magnolia, is a hybrid deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall and its showy, white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its fragrance and aesthetic beauty. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it adaptable to various garden conditions.

The tree is also notable for its ability to thrive in coastal areas due to its tolerance for salt spray.
