Mentha hirsuta var. dubia

Mentha hirsuta var. dubia, commonly known as the hairy mint or woolly mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, often grayish-green leaves and fragrant, square stems typical of the mint family. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Mentha hirsuta var. dubia is valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in herbal remedies.
