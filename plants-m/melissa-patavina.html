Melissa patavina

Melissa patavina, commonly known as lemon balm, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly serrated leaves that emit a distinct lemony scent when crushed. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal use.

Melissa patavina is valued for its calming properties and has been used in traditional medicine for centuries.
