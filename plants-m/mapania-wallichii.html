Mapania wallichii

Mapania wallichii, commonly known as the Himalayan tamarisk, is a shrub native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the family Tamaricaceae and is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and fragrant, pale yellow flowers. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky slopes and mountainous regions. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in harsh climates.

Mapania wallichii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local cultures.
