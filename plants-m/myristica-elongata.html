Myristica elongata

Myristica elongata, commonly known as the long nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen belonging to the Myristicaceae family.

It is native to the Indo-Pacific region, particularly found in parts of Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into fruiting bodies. The fruit contains a single, elongated seed surrounded by a fragrant, oily aril.

This species is valued for its aromatic seeds, which are used in both culinary and medicinal applications.
