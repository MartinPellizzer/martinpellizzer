Mandevilla nevadana

Mandevilla nevadana, commonly known as the Nevada mandevilla, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces clusters of vibrant pink or red blooms, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in warm, sunny conditions and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and as a houseplant.

Mandevilla nevadana is relatively drought-tolerant once established, making it suitable for various climates with well-draining soil.
