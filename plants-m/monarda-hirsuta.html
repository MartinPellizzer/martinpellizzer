Monarda hirsuta

Monarda hirsuta, commonly known as hairy bee balm, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features showy, tubular red or pink flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its hairy, lance-shaped leaves are often covered in fine white to gray hairs.

Monarda hirsuta is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
