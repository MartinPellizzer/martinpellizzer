Melpomene personata

Melpomene personata, commonly known as the red hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from red to pink, often with a contrasting yellow or white throat. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and often branched.

Melpomene personata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a cut flower.
