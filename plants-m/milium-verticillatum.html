Milium verticillatum

Milium verticillatum, commonly known as the whorled millet, is a flowering plant native to Eurasia.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pale green flowers in whorled clusters at the ends of its stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Milium verticillatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
