Myrcia obversa

Myrcia obversa, commonly known as the red myrtle, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruits are red, fleshy berries that contain seeds, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Myrcia obversa is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
