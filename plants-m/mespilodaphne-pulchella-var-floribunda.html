Mespilodaphne pulchella var. floribunda

Mespilodaphne pulchella var. floribunda, commonly known as the fragrant flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, clustered flowers that range from yellow to orange-red, often resembling flames. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically eaten by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes in tropical and subtropical regions.
