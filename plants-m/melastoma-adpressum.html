Melastoma adpressum

Melastoma adpressum, commonly known as the narrowleaf melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Melastoma adpressum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
