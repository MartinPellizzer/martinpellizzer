Mammillaria pulcherrima

Mammillaria pulcherrima, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, rounded stems and vibrant flowers that bloom during the holiday season. The plant has thick, fleshy stems covered in small, rounded tubercles, which help it store water. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.

Its unique appearance and seasonal blooming make it a popular ornamental plant.
