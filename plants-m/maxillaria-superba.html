Maxillaria superba

Maxillaria superba, commonly known as the showy maxillaria, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow with red or purple markings. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, supporting its growth in tropical and subtropical climates. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Maxillaria superba is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms.
