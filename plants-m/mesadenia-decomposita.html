Mesadenia decomposita

Mesadenia decomposita, commonly known as the ghost fern, is a unique member of the Dennstaedtiaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that give it a ghostly appearance. The plant is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are typically bipinnate, with fine, thread-like segments that contribute to its intricate appearance.

Mesadenia decomposita is often cultivated for its ornamental value in shaded gardens and as a houseplant.
