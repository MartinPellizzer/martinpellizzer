Mimusops balata var. gutta

Mimusops balata var. gutta, commonly known as the gutta or black mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The fruit is a rounded, segmented berry that turns from green to yellow or red when mature. The bark and leaves contain various bioactive compounds, making it valuable in traditional medicine.

It thrives in humid climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.
