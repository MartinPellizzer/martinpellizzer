Malus pumila var. asiatica

Malus pumila var. asiatica, commonly known as the Chinese sand pear, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its small, sweet, and juicy fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. The plant has oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall, and its flowers are white with five petals. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and edible fruit.
