Myrceugenia alpigena

Myrceugenia alpigena, commonly known as the alpine myrtle, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the southern Andes.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is typically found at high elevations.

Myrceugenia alpigena plays an important role in the ecological balance of its native habitat.
