Mansoa alliacea

Mansoa alliacea, commonly known as the garlic mustard, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It features alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring. Its root system is fibrous, and it is known for its strong, garlic-like odor.

It is often found in moist, shaded areas and can be invasive in certain regions.
