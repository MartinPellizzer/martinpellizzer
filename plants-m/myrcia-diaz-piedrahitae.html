Myrcia diaz-piedrahitae

Myrcia diaz-piedrahitae, commonly known as the Andean myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the cloud forests of southern Colombia and northern Ecuador, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. It produces small, fleshy berries that are typically red when mature.

Myrcia diaz-piedrahitae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine by local communities.
