Mimosa hebeclada

Mimosa hebeclada, commonly known as the yellow mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and often compound leaves. The plant features delicate, fern-like leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
