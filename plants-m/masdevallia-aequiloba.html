Masdevallia aequiloba

Masdevallia aequiloba, commonly known as the pink-veined orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and intricate floral structure. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers with a distinctive pink and white coloration, often with veined patterns on the petals. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires high light exposure for optimal blooming.

Masdevallia aequiloba is appreciated by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and unique appearance.
