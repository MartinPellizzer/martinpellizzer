Maniltoa gemmipara

Maniltoa gemmipara, commonly known as the gemmipara cycad, is a rare and ancient plant species native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Cycadaceae family and is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant reproduces through both vegetative propagation via gemmae and sexual reproduction through cones. Its slow growth rate and limited distribution make it a conservation concern.

The species is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soils.
