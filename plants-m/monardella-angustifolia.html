Monardella angustifolia

Monardella angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf monardella, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of tubular, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and woodlands. It is a member of the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is often found in regions with well-drained soils.

Monardella angustifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
