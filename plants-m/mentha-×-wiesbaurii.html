Mentha × wiesbaurii

Mentha × wiesbaurii, commonly known as catnip, is a hybrid mint species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, serrated leaves that are often a vibrant green or grayish-green. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Mentha × wiesbaurii is widely used in herbal medicine and as a companion plant in gardens due to its aromatic properties.
