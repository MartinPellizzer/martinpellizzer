Manilkara multinervis subsp. matanou

Manilkara multinervis subsp.

matanou, commonly known as the Matanou tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves with prominent veins.

The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, sticky fruits that are similar to tamarinds. Its bark is thick and cork-like, often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological importance in its native habitat.
