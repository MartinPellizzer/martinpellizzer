Malpighia hispaniolica subsp. cuneata

Malpighia hispaniolica subsp.

cuneata, commonly known as the Hispaniola acorn vine, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean island of Hispaniola. It belongs to the Malpighiaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and showy, pink to red flowers.

The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red when ripe, containing several seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a rough texture, while its stems are woody and covered in fine hairs.

This subspecies is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, thriving in well-drained soil and partial shade.
