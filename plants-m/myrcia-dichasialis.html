Myrcia dichasialis

Myrcia dichasialis, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of eastern Brazil, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a small, red, fleshy drupe that attracts various bird species.

Myrcia dichasialis is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.
