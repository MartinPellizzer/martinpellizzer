Miconia dipsacea

Miconia dipsacea, commonly known as the red miconia, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers, which are followed by distinctive, red, fleshy fruits. Miconia dipsacea is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its invasive potential, it is considered a problematic species in some areas where it is introduced.
