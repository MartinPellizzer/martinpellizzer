Myrciaria tenuiramis

Myrciaria tenuiramis, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or araçá-branco, is a small evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are rich in vitamin C and are often used in local cuisines.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a significant species in its native habitat.
