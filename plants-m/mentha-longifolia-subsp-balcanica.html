Mentha longifolia subsp. balcanica

Mentha longifolia subsp.

balcanica, commonly known as Balkan mint, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant has slender, aromatic leaves with a distinct minty fragrance, and its flowers are small, tubular, and usually pale purple or white. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.

This subspecies is closely related to other Mentha species but is distinguished by its unique morphology and geographic distribution.
