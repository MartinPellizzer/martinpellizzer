Macaranga bicolor

Macaranga bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored mallow, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are typically green on the upper surface and lighter in color on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Macaranga bicolor is also known for its rapid growth rate and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
