Monophyllaea glauca var. glauca

Monophyllaea glauca var. glauca, commonly known as the glaucous bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has lance-shaped leaves with a bluish-gray underside. It belongs to the Campanulaceae family, which includes other bellflowers.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
