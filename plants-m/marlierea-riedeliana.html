Marlierea riedeliana

Marlierea riedeliana, commonly known as the Riedel's marlierea, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or white, blooming during the warmer months. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to the moist, well-drained soils of its native habitat.

Marlierea riedeliana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
