Mattiastrum himalayense

Mattiastrum himalayense, commonly known as Himalayan blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Himalayas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are blue to purple in color.

Mattiastrum himalayense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in temperate climates.
