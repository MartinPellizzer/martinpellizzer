Microtropis garcinifolia

Microtropis garcinifolia, commonly known as the Java hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly pubescent surface. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow to orange, often with a red or purple throat. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.
