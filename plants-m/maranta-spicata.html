Maranta spicata

Maranta spicata, commonly known as the arrowroot plant, is a tropical species native to Central and South America.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its rhizomatous growth and striking, arrowhead-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that are clustered in spikes, giving it its species name "spicata.". Its rhizomes are rich in starch and have been traditionally used as a food source.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
