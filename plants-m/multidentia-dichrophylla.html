Multidentia dichrophylla

Multidentia dichrophylla, commonly known as the two-colored mulch plant, is a unique species native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking dichromatic foliage, with one side of the leaves displaying a vibrant green and the other a deep purple or red. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit and produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
