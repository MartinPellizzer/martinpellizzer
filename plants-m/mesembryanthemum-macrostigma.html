Mesembryanthemum macrostigma

Mesembryanthemum macrostigma, commonly known as the giant evening primrose, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and large, showy flowers that open in the late afternoon. The plant thrives in arid environments and is adapted to store water in its thick leaves and stems. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with prominent yellow stamens that attract pollinators.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and drought tolerance.
