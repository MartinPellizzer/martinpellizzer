Medicago falcata proles sylvestris

Medicago falcata proles sylvestris, commonly known as the wild brome clover, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Europe.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes. Its root system is well-developed, allowing it to thrive in poor soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
