Meliosma simplicifolia subsp. fruticosa

Meliosma simplicifolia subsp.

fruticosa, commonly known as the shrub maple, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America. It is characterized by its simple, ovate leaves that are often slightly hairy on the underside.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in early spring, which are followed by winged fruits called samaras. It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in well-drained soil.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in forest ecosystems.
