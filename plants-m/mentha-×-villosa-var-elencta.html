Mentha × villosa var. elencta

Mentha × villosa var. elencta, commonly known as the woolly mint, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and aromatic, fragrant foliage that emit a strong mint-like scent. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowering stems bear small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its use in traditional herbal remedies.
