Myrcia majaguitana

Myrcia majaguitana, commonly known as Brazilian pepper tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in moist, shaded environments.

Myrcia majaguitana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other parts of the world.
