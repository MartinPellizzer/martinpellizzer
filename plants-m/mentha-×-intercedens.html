Mentha × intercedens

Mentha × intercedens, commonly known as hybrid mint, is a cross between spearmint (Mentha spicata) and watermint (Mentha aquatica).

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shady environments, often found in woodland areas or along stream banks. The plant has square, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are usually oval to lance-shaped. Its flowers are small, clustered in whorls, and range in color from white to pale purple.

Mentha × intercedens is valued for its aromatic leaves, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications.
