Macrocarpaea lucubrans

Macrocarpaea lucubrans, commonly known as the moonlight fern, is a rare and unique fern species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that shimmer with a silvery sheen under moonlight, giving it its common name. The plant's reproductive structures, including its spore-bearing sporangia, are clustered in compact, umbrella-like clusters on the undersides of its leaves. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil to flourish.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Macrocarpaea lucubrans is considered a vulnerable species.
