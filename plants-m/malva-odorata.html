Malva odorata

Malva odorata, commonly known as the sweet mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, velvety leaves and showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus, and is known for its mucilage-rich stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or wet meadows.

Malva odorata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
