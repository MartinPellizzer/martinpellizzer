Myrcia heringeriana

Myrcia heringeriana, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns red when mature, and it is often used in traditional medicine.

The plant's aromatic properties and ornamental value make it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.
