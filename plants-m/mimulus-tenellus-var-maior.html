Mimulus tenellus var. maior

Mimulus tenellus var. maior, commonly known as the greater yellow monkeyflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as stream banks and meadows. The plant features striking yellow, spurred flowers that bloom from spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to support pollinators.
