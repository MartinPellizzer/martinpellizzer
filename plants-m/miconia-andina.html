Miconia andina

Miconia andina, commonly known as the Andean miconia, is a species native to the cloud forests of the Andes.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. Its flowers are typically clustered in inflorescences and are characterized by their showy, pinkish-white color.

Miconia andina is known for its ability to thrive in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions.
