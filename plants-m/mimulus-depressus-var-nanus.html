Mimulus depressus var. nanus

Mimulus depressus var. nanus, commonly known as the dwarf yellow monkeyflower, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features small, vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically reaches a height of only a few inches, making it ideal for rock gardens or as a ground cover. Its leaves are ovate and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, showcasing its adaptability to diverse environments.
