Mentha × rotundifolia subsp. gracilis

Mentha × rotundifolia subsp.

gracilis, commonly known as narrow-leaf mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often slightly hairy and have a distinct mint aroma.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or purple, arranged in whorled clusters along the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This subspecies is often used in herbal teas and as a culinary herb due to its aromatic properties.
