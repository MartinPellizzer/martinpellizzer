Mucuna pallida

Mucuna pallida, commonly known as the white urd or white cowpea, is a leguminous vine native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves with small, rounded leaflets. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that give way to elongated, bean-like pods containing several seeds. Mucuna pallida is notable for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

It is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing properties, making it a useful plant in agricultural systems.
