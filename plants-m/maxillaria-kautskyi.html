Maxillaria kautskyi

Maxillaria kautskyi, commonly known as the Kautsky's orchid, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a sympodial orchid with a short, stocky rhizome and produces pseudobulbs that bear leaves and flower spikes. The flowers are typically fragrant, with a complex structure featuring a prominent lip and spurred column. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This orchid is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
