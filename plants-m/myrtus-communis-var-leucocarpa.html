Myrtus communis var. leucocarpa

Myrtus communis var. leucocarpa, commonly known as white-fruited myrtle, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces berries that are white or pale yellow when mature, distinguishing it from the typical dark berries of other myrtle varieties. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
