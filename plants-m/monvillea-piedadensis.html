Monvillea piedadensis

Monvillea piedadensis, commonly known as the Piedad monvillea, is a rare and unique plant species native to Colombia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically features a short rhizome with a few leaves and produces small, delicate blooms that are often fragrant. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered endangered and requires careful conservation efforts.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a representative of the diverse orchid flora in South America.
