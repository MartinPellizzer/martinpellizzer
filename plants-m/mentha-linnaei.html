Mentha linnaei

Mentha linnaei, commonly known as the Linnæus mint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and produces small, tubular flowers in shades of purple or blue. Its leaves are oval-shaped, serrated, and emit a strong menthol scent when crushed.

Mentha linnaei is often found in moist meadows and along streambanks, where it thrives in partial shade to full sun.
