Melandrium ornatum var. undulatum

Melandrium ornatum var. undulatum, commonly known as the undulating catchfly, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are oval-shaped with wavy edges, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological studies and garden cultivation.
