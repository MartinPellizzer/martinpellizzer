Markhamia stipulata var. kerrii

Markhamia stipulata var. kerrii, commonly known as the Kerri's markhamia, is a flowering plant native to East Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pale yellow to orange. The leaves are typically opposite, with lance-shaped blades and prominent stipules. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
