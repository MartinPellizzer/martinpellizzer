Manihot tenella

Manihot tenella, commonly known as the arrowleaf beggar-tick, is a tropical plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers. The plant is native to Central and South America and is often found in moist, shaded environments. Its latex contains toxic compounds, which can cause skin irritation.
