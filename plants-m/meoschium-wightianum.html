Meoschium wightianum

Meoschium wightianum, commonly known as the Wight's meadow grass, is a herbaceous plant native to the wetlands and marshy areas of South and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in association with other aquatic or semi-aquatic vegetation.

Meoschium wightianum plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems and provides habitat for various invertebrates.
