Maxillaria leptosepala var. subintegerrima

Maxillaria leptosepala var. subintegerrima, commonly known as the narrow-sepaled maxillaria, is a hybrid orchid species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped pseudobulbs and linear, dark green leaves. The flowers are typically pale yellow to white, with a distinct, intricate pattern on the lip. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright but indirect light.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
