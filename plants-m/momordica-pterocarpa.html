Momordica pterocarpa

Momordica pterocarpa, commonly known as the Indian bitter gourd, is a vine belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including India, China, and Southeast Asia. The plant produces elongated, spiny fruits that are typically harvested when immature for culinary use. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with five petals.

The plant is valued for its edible fruits, which are rich in nutrients and commonly used in various traditional cuisines.
