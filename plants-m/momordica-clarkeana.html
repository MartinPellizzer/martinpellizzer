Momordica clarkeana

Momordica clarkeana, commonly known as the bitter melon, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, warty, green fruit that turns orange when ripe. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces yellow flowers. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruit, though it is known for its bitter taste.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
