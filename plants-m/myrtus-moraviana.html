Myrtus moraviana

Myrtus moraviana, commonly known as Moravian myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. Its berries, which are typically blue-black, are edible and often used in culinary applications.

Myrtus moraviana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
