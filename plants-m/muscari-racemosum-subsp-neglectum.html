Muscari racemosum subsp. neglectum

Muscari racemosum subsp.

neglectum, commonly known as the Persian grape hyacinth, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Caucasus region. It produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale blue to violet, resembling grapes, hence its common name.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained soil in full sun to partial shade. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.

The subspecies is generally hardy and adaptable, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.
