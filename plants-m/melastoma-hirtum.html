Melastoma hirtum

Melastoma hirtum, commonly known as the hairy melastoma, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Melastomataceae and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white. The plant has opposite, hairy leaves that are often gland-dotted, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Melastoma hirtum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
