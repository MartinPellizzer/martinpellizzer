Melanthium virginicum

Melanthium virginicum, commonly known as the white fringed gentian, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale blue, with distinctive fringed petals. The plant grows in moist, woodlands or stream banks, often forming dense colonies. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a rough texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators.
