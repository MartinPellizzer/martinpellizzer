Morus chinlingensis

Morus chinlingensis, commonly known as the Chinese mulberry, is a species native to China and is part of the Moraceae family.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height, characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and rough, fissured bark. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically green when immature and turn reddish or black when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The leaves are used in traditional Chinese medicine and the tree has historical significance in silk production.
