Micromeria lepida subsp. lepida

Micromeria lepida subsp.

lepida, commonly known as the desert sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a low-growing shrub with narrow, linear leaves and produces small, pinkish-white flowers.

The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and heat. Its flowers are typically clustered in dense inflorescences, and it is often found in sandy or gravelly soils.

The species is noted for its resilience and is used in xeriscaping due to its ornamental value and low water requirements.
