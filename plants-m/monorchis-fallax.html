Monorchis fallax

Monorchis fallax, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous areas. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green. It has a unique root system that includes both aerial and subterranean structures, aiding in nutrient absorption.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often found in humid, well-drained soils with partial shade.
