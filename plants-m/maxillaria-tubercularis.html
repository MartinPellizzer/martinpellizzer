Maxillaria tubercularis

Maxillaria tubercularis, commonly known as the tuberculate orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact, often hairy pseudobulbs and strap-like leaves. The plant produces fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically during the dry season. Its flowers are borne on lateral spikes and have a unique, intricate structure with prominent calli.

Maxillaria tubercularis is well-suited for cultivation in greenhouses or warm, humid environments.
