Melaleuca laetifica

Melaleuca laetifica, commonly known as the honey myrtle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, small white or pink flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.

Melaleuca laetifica is also valued for its essential oils, which are used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine.
