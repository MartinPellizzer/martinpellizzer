Macrorhynchus laciniatus var. longifolius

Macrorhynchus laciniatus var. longifolius, commonly known as the long-leaved fern, is a variant of the broad-lobed fern characterized by its elongated, serrated leaves.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features a rhizomatous growth habit with fronds that can reach several feet in length. Its leaves are typically bi- or tri-pinnate, with a delicate, feathery appearance.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
