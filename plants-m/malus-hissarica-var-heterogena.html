Malus hissarica var. heterogena

Malus hissarica var. heterogena, commonly known as the Persian apple or Caucasian crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring showy white to pink flowers in early spring. The plant produces small, tart fruits that are edible but typically not cultivated for commercial use. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a smooth texture and a vibrant green color.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.
