Malpighia subglabrata

Malpighia subglabrata, commonly known as the Brazilian acerola or wild pink grapefruit, is a small evergreen shrub native to South America.

It produces small, bright red berries that are rich in vitamin C and other antioxidants. The plant has simple, elliptic leaves with a glossy surface and a slightly serrated margin. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded, moist environments.

The fruit is typically harvested when fully ripe, revealing a juicy, acidic pulp with a distinctive pink color.
