Maxillaria pendula

Maxillaria pendula, commonly known as the pendant maxillaria, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant features long, slender stems with broad, lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink with a distinctive, intricate pattern on the lip.

Maxillaria pendula thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
