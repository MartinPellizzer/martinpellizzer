Mimosa apodocarpa var. intermedia

Mimosa apodocarpa var. intermedia, commonly known as the silky mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that are soft to the touch and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow, spherical flowers that appear in clusters, typically in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its delicate appearance and unique foliage make it a distinctive addition to botanical collections.
