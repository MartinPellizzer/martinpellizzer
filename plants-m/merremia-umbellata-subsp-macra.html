Merremia umbellata subsp. macra

Merremia umbellata subsp.

macra, commonly known as the large-flowered spiderling, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America. It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, sunny environments and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

Botanically, it belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, sharing features with other members like morning glories.
