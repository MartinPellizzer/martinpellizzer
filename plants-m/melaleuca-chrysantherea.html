Melaleuca chrysantherea

Melaleuca chrysantherea, commonly known as the golden myrtle, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, often in heathlands or open woodlands. Its flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and birds.

Melaleuca chrysantherea is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and fragrance.
