Myrceugenia johowii

Myrceugenia johowii, commonly known as the Johow's myrtle, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several small, edible seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential ecological and medicinal properties.
