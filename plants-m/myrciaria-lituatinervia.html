Myrciaria lituatinervia

Myrciaria lituatinervia, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *cereja-do-brejo*, is a small evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in understory layers of tropical rainforests.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is of interest for both ecological and horticultural studies.
