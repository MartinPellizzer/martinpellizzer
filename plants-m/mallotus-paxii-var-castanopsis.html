Mallotus paxii var. castanopsis

Mallotus paxii var. castanopsis, commonly known as the red-leafed mallotus, is a variant of the mallotus genus native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking crimson foliage, which becomes more vibrant during the cooler months. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a leathery texture with serrated edges.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, followed by woody, spherical fruits that contain several seeds.
