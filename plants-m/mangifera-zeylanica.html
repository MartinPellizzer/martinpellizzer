Mangifera zeylanica

Mangifera zeylanica, commonly known as the Indian mango, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves and produces a fruit that is widely cultivated for its sweet, juicy pulp. The plant belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is valued for its ornamental and economic significance. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing before the leaves, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

The tree can grow up to 30 meters tall and has a long lifespan, making it an important component of many ecosystems.
