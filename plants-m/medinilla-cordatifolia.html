Medinilla cordatifolia

Medinilla cordatifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf medinilla, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy dark green leaves that are often clustered at the base of the plant. The plant produces fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, which bloom in clusters during the warmer months. It thrives in humid, shady environments and prefers well-draining, fertile soil.

Medinilla cordatifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying properties.
