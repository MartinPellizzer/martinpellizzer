Madhuca longifolia var. longifolia

Madhuca longifolia var. longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf mahua, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, leathery leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellowish-white in color, which bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule containing numerous small, oily seeds that are used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil.

The tree is valued for its ecological role in supporting biodiversity and its economic importance in local communities.
