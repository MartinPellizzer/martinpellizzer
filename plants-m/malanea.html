Malanea

Malanea, commonly known as the Malabar spinach, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Malvaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trailing growth habit. The plant is valued for its edible leaves, which are used in various cuisines for their mild, spinach-like flavor. Malanea thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

It is often cultivated as a leafy vegetable due to its nutritional value and adaptability to different growing conditions.
