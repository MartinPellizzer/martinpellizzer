Mucuna argentea

Mucuna argentea, commonly known as silver cowpea, is a tropical legume native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-green foliage and long, slender pods that contain small, edible seeds. The plant is a climbing vine with trifoliate leaves and produces showy, yellowish-white flowers. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Botanically, it belongs to the Fabaceae family and is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil.
