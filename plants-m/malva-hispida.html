Malva hispida

Malva hispida, commonly known as the rough hollyhock, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, showy pink or red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes other hibiscus-like species. Its leaves are ovate with a rough texture due to the presence of stiff hairs.

Malva hispida is often grown as an ornamental plant in gardens for its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
