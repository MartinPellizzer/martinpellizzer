Montanoa seleriana

Montanoa seleriana, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a herbaceous plant native to Central America and northern South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flower heads that resemble sunflowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, with hairy, alternate leaves that are often heart-shaped. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Montanoa seleriana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
