Myristica sebifera

Myristica sebifera, commonly known as the nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Banda Islands in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, woody bark and compound leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by round, red fruits that contain the valuable nutmeg seeds. Its close relative, Myristica fragrans, is also cultivated for its nutmeg and mace.

The plant is primarily grown for its aromatic seeds and oil, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications.
