Morisonia amplissima

Morisonia amplissima, commonly known as the giant morisonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy white to pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a trailing growth habit with opposite, ovate leaves that are usually hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
