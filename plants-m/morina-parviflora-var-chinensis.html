Morina parviflora var. chinensis

Morina parviflora var. chinensis, commonly known as Chinese moringa, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its small, white or yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are ovate and have a slightly hairy texture, while its bark is smooth and grayish in color.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses in its native region.
