Malaxis sodiroi

Malaxis sodiroi, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a species native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique floral structure. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its flowers are small, often greenish or yellowish, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination.

Malaxis sodiroi is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the biodiversity of its native habitat.
