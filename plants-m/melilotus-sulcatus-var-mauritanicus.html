Melilotus sulcatus var. mauritanicus

Melilotus sulcatus var. mauritanicus, commonly known as the Mauritania melilot, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region and North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
