Muehlenbeckia ephedroides

Muehlenbeckia ephedroides, commonly known as the silverweed, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial that forms dense mats of silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and wetland habitats.

Its rapid growth and ability to stabilize soil make it an important plant in ecological restoration projects.
