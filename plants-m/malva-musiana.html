Malva musiana

Malva musiana, commonly known as the Sudanese mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Sahel region of Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to drought conditions. Its fibrous roots help prevent soil erosion, and it is often used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory properties.

Malva musiana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in warmer climates.
