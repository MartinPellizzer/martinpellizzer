Muscarella helenae

Muscarella helenae, commonly known as the Helen's muscari, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, fragrant flowers in shades of blue and purple, typically blooming in spring. Its foliage consists of strap-like leaves that are evergreen in warmer climates.

Muscarella helenae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual and aromatic qualities.
