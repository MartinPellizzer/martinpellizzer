Macrolobium discolor

Macrolobium discolor, commonly known as the variable-leaf macrolobium, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can vary in color from green to yellowish-white, giving it its common name. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, with a distinctive purple or pink hue.

The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important component of forest ecosystems.
