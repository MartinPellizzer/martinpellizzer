Myrica gale subsp. tomentosa

Myrica gale subsp.

tomentosa, commonly known as smooth sweetgale, is a shrub native to northern regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that appear in clusters.

The plant produces dark blue, waxy berries that are often used in traditional crafts and as a natural dye. It thrives in wet, acidic soils and is often found in peat bogs and along waterways.

Its aromatic leaves and unique morphology make it an important species in ecological and botanical studies.
