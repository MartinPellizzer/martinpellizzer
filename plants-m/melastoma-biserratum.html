Melastoma biserratum

Melastoma biserratum, commonly known as the serrated melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, pink or white flowers that bloom in summer. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that release seeds when mature.

Melastoma biserratum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and shrublands.
