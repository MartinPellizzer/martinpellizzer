Maranta setosa

Maranta setosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf arrowroot, is a species of flowering plant in the family Marantaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its striking, ornamental leaves that are velvety to the touch and often display intricate patterns of red and green. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies in its natural habitat.

While primarily cultivated for its ornamental value, it is also used in some traditional medicinal practices.
