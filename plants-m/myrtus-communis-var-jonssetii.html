Myrtus communis var. jonssetii

Myrtus communis var. jonssetii, commonly known as the Jonssetii myrtle, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically dark green and glossy, with a distinctive, spicy scent. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by dark blue, edible berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a hedge.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
