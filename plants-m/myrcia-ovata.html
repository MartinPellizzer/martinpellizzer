Myrcia ovata

Myrcia ovata, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and fragrant, white or pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds.

Myrcia ovata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
