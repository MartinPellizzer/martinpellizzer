Marsilea concinna

Marsilea concinna, commonly known as water clover, is a small aquatic fern that thrives in freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its distinctive four-leaf clover-like structure, which is a key feature of its botanical appearance. The plant produces floating leaves that are usually submerged, with tiny spore-producing structures called sporocarps. It is native to parts of Asia and is often found in ponds, marshes, and slow-moving streams.

Marsilea concinna plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and small aquatic life.
