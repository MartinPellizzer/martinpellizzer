Melothria cordifolia

Melothria cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf melon or heartleaf cucumber, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like pumpkins and gourds. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in local cuisines for their tart flavor. Its leaves are heart-shaped, giving it the species name "cordifolia," meaning "heart-leaved.".
