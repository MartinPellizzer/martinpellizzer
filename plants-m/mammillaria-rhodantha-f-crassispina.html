Mammillaria rhodantha f. crassispina

Mammillaria rhodantha f. crassispina, commonly known as the thick-spined red mammillaria, is a unique cactus species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in dense, white to pale blue spines. The plant produces vibrant pink or red flowers during the spring and summer months. Its compact size and striking appearance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cultivation.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often grown in controlled environments to ensure its survival.
