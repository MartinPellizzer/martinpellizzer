Maxillaria brevilabia

Maxillaria brevilabia, commonly known as the short-lipped maxillaria, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces fragrant, showy flowers with a distinctive yellow and purple coloration. The plant typically blooms during the summer months, and its flowers are often clustered in racemes. The leaves are broad and ovate, arranged in a basal rosette, and the roots are fibrous.

This orchid is relatively easy to cultivate, making it a popular choice for both novice and experienced growers.
