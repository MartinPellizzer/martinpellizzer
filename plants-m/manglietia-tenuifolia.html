Manglietia tenuifolia

Manglietia tenuifolia, commonly known as the slender-leaf manglietia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Magnoliaceae and is characterized by its large, lance-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a strong, sweet scent. Its bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their purported medicinal properties.

Manglietia tenuifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
