Melastoma roemeri

Melastoma roemeri, commonly known as the purple flame bush, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with opposite, ovate leaves that have a slightly rough texture. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Melastoma roemeri is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
