Malapoenna nuculanaea

Malapoenna nuculanaea, commonly known as the Malapoenna nut tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that transition into edible, chestnut-like fruits. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its fruit is valued for its nutritional content and is often used in local cuisines.

The tree is also noted for its ability to tolerate partial shade, making it adaptable to various garden environments.
