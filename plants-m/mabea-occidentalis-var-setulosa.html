Mabea occidentalis var. setulosa

Mabea occidentalis var. setulosa, commonly known as the woolly mabea, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features dense, hairy foliage that gives it its common name, with leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by elliptical, woody fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a notable species in xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
