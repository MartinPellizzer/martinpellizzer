Monachanthus discolor var. bushnanii

Monachanthus discolor var. bushnanii, commonly known as the Bushnan's false indigo, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant features palmate leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically blue or violet, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
