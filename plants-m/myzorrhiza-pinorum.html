Myzorrhiza pinorum

Myzorrhiza pinorum, commonly known as the pine root fungus, is a symbiotic fungus that forms mycorrhizal associations with certain pine species.

It colonizes the roots of these trees, enhancing their ability to absorb water and nutrients from the soil. The fungus belongs to the Basidiomycota phylum and is characterized by its intricate network of hyphae that extend into the soil. It plays a vital role in forest ecosystems by improving soil fertility and tree health.

This symbiotic relationship is essential for the survival and growth of many pine species in nutrient-poor environments.
