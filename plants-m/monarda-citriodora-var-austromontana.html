Monarda citriodora var. austromontana

Monarda citriodora var. austromontana, commonly known as lemon bee balm, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square, hairy stems and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 3 feet, with lance-shaped leaves that have a distinct lemony fragrance. Its flowers are usually pink or red, arranged in dense, terminal clusters, and attract a variety of pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.
