Machaerium leiophyllum

Machaerium leiophyllum, commonly known as the silky machaerium, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, feathery foliage and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving it a silky texture.

The flowers, which are usually pink or white, bloom in late spring and attract various pollinators.
