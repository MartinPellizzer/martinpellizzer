Melastoma scandens

Melastoma scandens, commonly known as the climbing melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a woody vine that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its flowers are showy, with five petals that are often pink or red, and it produces small, fleshy berries as fruit.

The botanical characteristics of Melastoma scandens make it a notable species in both horticultural and ecological contexts.
