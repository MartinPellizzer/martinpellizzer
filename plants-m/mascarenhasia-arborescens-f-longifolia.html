Mascarenhasia arborescens f. longifolia

Mascarenhasia arborescens f. longifolia, commonly known as the Mascarene tree fern, is a unique variant of the tree fern species native to the Mascarene Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feathery fronds that can reach impressive lengths. The plant exhibits a slow growth rate and thrives in humid, shaded environments with well-drained soil. Its fronds are typically dark green and have a leathery texture, contributing to its ornamental value.

This fern is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is considered a rare and exotic species in botanical gardens.
