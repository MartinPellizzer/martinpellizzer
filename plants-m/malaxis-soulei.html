Malaxis soulei

Malaxis soulei, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom during the rainy season. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is notable for its unique floral structure and adaptation to its native environment. It typically grows in shaded, humid areas with well-drained soil.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
