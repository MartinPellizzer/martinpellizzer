Miconia annulata

Miconia annulata, commonly known as the annular miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its showy, often violet or red flowers. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a distinctive white or yellowish midrib, and it produces small, fleshy fruits that are dispersed by birds.

Miconia annulata is also notable for its ability to thrive in shaded understory environments within humid tropical forests.
