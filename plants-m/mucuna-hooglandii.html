Mucuna hooglandii

Mucuna hooglandii, commonly known as the Java velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and trifoliate leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces pods containing small, dark seeds that are rich in protein and have been utilized in various cultural practices. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in humid, shaded environments.

Botanically, it belongs to the Fabaceae family and is valued for its nitrogen-fixing properties.
