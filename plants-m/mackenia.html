Mackenia

Mackenia, commonly known as the frostweed, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Malvaceae.

It consists of several species, most notably Mackenia christiana, which is native to the southeastern United States. The plant is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs.

Mackenia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
