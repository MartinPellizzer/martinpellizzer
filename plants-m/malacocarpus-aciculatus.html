Malacocarpus aciculatus

Malacocarpus aciculatus, commonly known as the needleleaf cycad, is a unique cycad species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and a crown of stiff, needle-like leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces large, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs, typical of cycads. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine and as ornamental plants due to their distinctive appearance.

Malacocarpus aciculatus is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, requiring well-drained soil and ample sunlight for optimal growth.
