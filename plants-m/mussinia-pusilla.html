Mussinia pusilla

Mussinia pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf snowbell, is a small, alpine plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped, white or pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Mussinia pusilla is a perennial species that blooms in early summer, adding a delicate touch to its high-altitude habitat.
