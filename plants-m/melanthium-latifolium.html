Melanthium latifolium

Melanthium latifolium, commonly known as broadleaf meadow beauty, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, white or pale green flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a bluish-gray hue.

Melanthium latifolium is an important nectar source for pollinators and is often found in prairie and wetland ecosystems.
