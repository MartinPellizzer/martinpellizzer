Mendoncia vinciflora

Mendoncia vinciflora, commonly known as the golden vine, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its long, trailing stems and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot. Its leaves are broad and glossy, often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant's unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and hanging displays.
