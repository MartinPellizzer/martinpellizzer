Mangenotiella

Mangenotiella, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, delicate appearance with pale green or white flowers. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees without deriving nutrients from them. Mangenotiella has small, scale-like leaves and produces flowers that are often mistaken for leaves due to their shape.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is highly sought after in the horticultural trade.
