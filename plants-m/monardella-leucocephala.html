Monardella leucocephala

Monardella leucocephala, commonly known as whitehead monardella, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant features upright stems covered in soft, hairy foliage and produces showy, white to pale pink flowers clustered in whorls. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly fuzzy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for native gardens.
