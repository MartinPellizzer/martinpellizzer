Myrcia morroqueimadensis

Myrcia morroqueimadensis, commonly known as the Morroquimadense myrtle, is a species of flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. It typically grows in humid, shaded environments within the Atlantic Forest biome.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat fragmentation, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
