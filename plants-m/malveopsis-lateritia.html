Malveopsis lateritia

Malveopsis lateritia, commonly known as the alpine malveopsis, is a flowering plant species native to the high altitudes of the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton plants, and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves. The plant produces small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh alpine conditions with low temperatures and strong winds.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
