Myagrum sativum var. strictius

Myagrum sativum var. strictius, commonly known as the narrow-leafed myrtle, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, aromatic leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is often found in coastal and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their aromatic properties and are also valued in perfumery.

The species is notable for its resilience to salt spray and drought conditions.
