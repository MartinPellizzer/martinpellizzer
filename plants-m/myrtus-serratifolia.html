Myrtus serratifolia

Myrtus serratifolia, commonly known as the serrated myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, dark green, elliptical leaves with serrated edges, giving it its common name. The plant produces tiny, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by dark purple berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal areas.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental value make it a popular choice in horticulture.
