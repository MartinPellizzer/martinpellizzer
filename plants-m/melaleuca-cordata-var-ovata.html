Melaleuca cordata var. ovata

Melaleuca cordata var. ovata, commonly known as the broad-leaf paperbark, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark that reveals a fibrous inner layer, and its lance-shaped, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers clustered in spikes, which bloom in winter and attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and rainforests.

This variety is closely related to other melaleuca species and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
