Mutisia ilicifolia var. ilicifolia

Mutisia ilicifolia var. ilicifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf mutisia, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which results from a dense covering of fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, spherical fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in regions with a Mediterranean climate.
