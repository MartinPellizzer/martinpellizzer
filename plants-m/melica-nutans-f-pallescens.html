Melica nutans f. pallescens

Melica nutans f. pallescens, commonly known as the pale-leaf nodding grass, is a variant of the nodding grass species native to temperate regions.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its slender, often pale-colored leaves and drooping inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its flowers are small and greenish, arranged in spikelets, and it produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This form is distinguished by its reduced leaf coloration and subtle morphological differences from the typical species.
