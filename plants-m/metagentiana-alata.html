Metagentiana alata

Metagentiana alata, commonly known as the winged gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which are typically tubular in shape and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant's leaves are opposite and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its arid or semi-arid habitat.
