Melampodium sinuatum

Melampodium sinuatum, commonly known as the zigzag mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive zigzagging stems and showy yellow-orange flowers. The plant has palmate leaves with lobed margins, which contribute to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and prairies.

Melampodium sinuatum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
