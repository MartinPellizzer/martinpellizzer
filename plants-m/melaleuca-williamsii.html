Melaleuca williamsii

Melaleuca williamsii, commonly known as the Williams' tea tree, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the myrtle family (Myrtaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and showy clusters of pink or red flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal or sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and forest margins. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.

The species is also known for its ability to tolerate salt spray, making it well-suited to coastal environments.
