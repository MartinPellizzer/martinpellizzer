Mimosa pachycarpa

Mimosa pachycarpa, commonly known as the thick-leaved mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, fern-like leaves that are thick and velvety to the touch. The plant produces small, yellow, spherical flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Mimosa pachycarpa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and delicate floral display.
