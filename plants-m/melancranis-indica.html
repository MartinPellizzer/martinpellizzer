Melancranis indica

Melancranis indica, commonly known as the Indian melancranis, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Melancranidaceae.

It is characterized by its woody stems and lance-shaped leaves with a smooth, glossy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and ample sunlight.

Melancranis indica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some regions for medicinal purposes.
