Morus petiolaris

Morus petiolaris, commonly known as the Chinese mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, aggregate fruits. The plant is known for its rapid growth and is often cultivated for its fibrous bark, which is used in the production of paper and textiles. It thrives in temperate climates and is adaptable to various soil types.

The tree also plays a role in traditional medicine and as a source of food for wildlife.
