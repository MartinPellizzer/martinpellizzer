Miconia septuplinervis

Miconia septuplinervis, commonly known as the seven-veined miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive seven-veined pattern on the underside. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release numerous tiny seeds.

Miconia septuplinervis is often found in humid lowland forests and is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets.
