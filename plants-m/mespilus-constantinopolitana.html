Mespilus constantinopolitana

Mespilus constantinopolitana, commonly known as the Constantinople medlar, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Mespilus.

It is native to the region of Constantinople, now Istanbul, Turkey, and is characterized by its distinctive fruit, which is typically harvested when partially ripe. The plant produces small, round, and often spiny fruits that have a sweet, aromatic flavor when fully mature. It is a deciduous shrub or small tree with simple, alternate leaves and white or pinkish flowers that appear in early spring.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in horticulture.
