Mangifera applanata

Mangifera applanata, commonly known as the flat-seeded mango, is a species of flowering plant in the Anacardiaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its large, elongated fruits with a smooth, flat seed. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a glossy green appearance.

The fruit is valued for its sweet, aromatic pulp and is often used in both culinary and medicinal applications.
