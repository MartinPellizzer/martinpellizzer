Marrubium parviflorum var. oligodon

Marrubium parviflorum var. oligodon, commonly known as the small-flowered horehound, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. The plant features grey-green, hairy leaves and produces small, pale blue to violet flowers arranged in clusters. It is known for its medicinal properties, particularly its use in traditional herbal remedies.

The species is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive, aromatic foliage.
