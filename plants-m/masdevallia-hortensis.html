Masdevallia hortensis

Masdevallia hortensis, commonly known as the orchid of the Andes, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, often colorful flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees without parasitizing them. Its leaves are usually dark green and strap-like, while its inflorescence can produce multiple blooms in shades of white, pink, and violet.

Masdevallia hortensis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and unique floral structure.
