Monardella muriculata

Monardella muriculata, commonly known as the roughleaf monardella, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its hairy, serrated leaves and clusters of tubular, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Monardella muriculata is also used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
