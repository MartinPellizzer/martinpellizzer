Myrceugenia montevidensis

Myrceugenia montevidensis, commonly known as the Montevidensis myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to southern South America, particularly found in Uruguay and parts of Brazil. This evergreen shrub or small tree typically grows to a height of 3 to 5 meters, featuring glossy, elliptical leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by edible, spherical berries.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
