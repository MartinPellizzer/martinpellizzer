Malacantha

Malacantha, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a velvety texture. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from yellow to red, attracting various pollinators. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.

Malacantha is often found in disturbed habitats and can become invasive in some regions.
