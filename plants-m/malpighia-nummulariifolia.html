Malpighia nummulariifolia

Malpighia nummulariifolia, commonly known as the Mexican snowberry, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Malpighiaceae family and is characterized by its small, white, clustered flowers. The plant produces bright red, fleshy berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
