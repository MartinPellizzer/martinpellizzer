Melissa alba

Melissa alba, commonly known as lemon balm, is a perennial herb native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly fuzzy leaves that emit a citrusy scent when crushed. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Melissa alba thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a versatile plant for gardens and herbal cultivation.

It is widely used in traditional medicine and aromatherapy for its calming properties.
