Monechma bracteatum var. hirsutior

Monechma bracteatum var. hirsutior, commonly known as the hairy monechma, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in wetlands. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are densely covered with fine, hairy pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are small, white to pale pink, and clustered in loose inflorescences.

The species is notable for its ecological role in supporting local insect populations and its adaptation to wetland environments.
