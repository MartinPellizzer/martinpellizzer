Myrtillus sylvaticus

Myrtillus sylvaticus, commonly known as the wild myrtle, is a deciduous shrub native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in the spring, followed by dark blue berries that are edible for humans and birds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a glossy appearance with a strong, pleasant scent.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or shaded areas.
