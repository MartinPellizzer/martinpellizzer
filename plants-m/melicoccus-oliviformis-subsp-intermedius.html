Melicoccus oliviformis subsp. intermedius

Melicoccus oliviformis subsp.

intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate olive fruit, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its small, round, olive-green fruits that mature to a darker color.

The plant produces small, white flowers that are typically clustered together. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

This species is often cultivated for its edible fruits, which have a tart and slightly sweet flavor.
