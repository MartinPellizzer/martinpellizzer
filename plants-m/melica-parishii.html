Melica parishii

Melica parishii, commonly known as the Parish's meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks.

Melica parishii is often found in regions with temperate climates and is adapted to well-drained soils.
