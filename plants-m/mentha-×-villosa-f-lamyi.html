Mentha × villosa f. lamyi

Mentha × villosa f. lamyi, commonly known as woolly mint, is a hybrid species within the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

Mentha × villosa f. lamyi is often used in herbal remedies and is valued for its aromatic properties.
