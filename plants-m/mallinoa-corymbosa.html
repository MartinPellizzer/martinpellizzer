Mallinoa corymbosa

Mallinoa corymbosa, commonly known as the sweet potato vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, often variegated foliage. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its tuberous roots are edible and have been used in various cuisines.
