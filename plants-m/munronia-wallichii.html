Munronia wallichii

Munronia wallichii, commonly known as the Himalayan muhly, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear minute, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
