Melampodium copiosum

Melampodium copiosum, commonly known as the common ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its showy yellow to orange flowers bloom in late summer and attract various pollinators.

The plant has fibrous roots and can form dense colonies, contributing to soil stabilization in wetland environments.
