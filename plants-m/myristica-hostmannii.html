Myristica hostmannii

Myristica hostmannii, commonly known as the Hostmann's nutmeg, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is closely related to the nutmeg tree, Myristica fragrans. The plant produces aromatic seeds that are encased in a reddish, fleshy aril, which is often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a glossy surface, while its flowers are small and greenish-white in color.

The tree is valued for its unique fragrance and has been historically used in both culinary and medicinal applications.
