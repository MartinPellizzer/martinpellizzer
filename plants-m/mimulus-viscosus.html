Mimulus viscosus

Mimulus viscosus, commonly known as the sticky monkeyflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its glandular, sticky leaves and bright orange to yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as stream banks and forest edges. Its flowers are tubular with two lips, and it is pollinated primarily by bees and hummingbirds.

Mimulus viscosus is also known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.
