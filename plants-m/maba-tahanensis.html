Maba tahanensis

Maba tahanensis, commonly known as the Tahan mountain maba, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in cyathia, a unique floral structure typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Maba tahanensis is often found in humid, shaded environments within tropical forests.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various insect species.
