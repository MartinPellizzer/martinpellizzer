Matricaria pilosa

Matricaria pilosa, commonly known as the hairy chamomile, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like yellow flowers surrounded by white ray florets. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Matricaria pilosa is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its calming effects.
