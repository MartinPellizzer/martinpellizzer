Malus sylvestris

Malus sylvestris, commonly known as the wild apple or crabapple, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to Central Europe and western Asia, typically growing in woodland areas and along stream banks. The plant produces small, ornamental flowers in spring, followed by edible fruits that vary in size and color. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and the bark is smooth and grayish-brown.

Malus sylvestris is valued for its ecological role, supporting pollinators and providing food for wildlife.
