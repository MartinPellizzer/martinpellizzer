Malva parviflora proles microcarpa

Malva parviflora proles microcarpa, commonly known as the small-flowered mallows, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a soft, fuzzy texture and produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is characterized by its short, compact growth habit and is often found in rocky or dry soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.

The species is also notable for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it a resilient garden plant.
