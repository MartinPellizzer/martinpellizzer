Misopates orontium var. grandiflorum

Misopates orontium var. grandiflorum, commonly known as the giant marsh orchid, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its roots are adapted to thrive in waterlogged soils. It is a perennial species that plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.
