Malva sylvestris var. oxyloba

Malva sylvestris var. oxyloba, commonly known as the greater periwinkle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its upright stems and heart-shaped, velvety leaves with a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces showy, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and the presence of mucilage-producing cells in its tissues.
