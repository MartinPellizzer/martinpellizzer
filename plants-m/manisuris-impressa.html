Manisuris impressa

Manisuris impressa, commonly known as the African rock orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, often brightly colored flowers with intricate patterns that mimic the appearance of insects or other small creatures. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, while its roots are fibrous and well-developed.

Manisuris impressa plays an important ecological role in its habitat, supporting pollinators and contributing to local biodiversity.
