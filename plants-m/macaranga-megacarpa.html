Macaranga megacarpa

Macaranga megacarpa, commonly known as the giant maple, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 50 cm in diameter. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by distinctive, three-winged fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is notable for its rapid growth and is sometimes used in agroforestry systems.
