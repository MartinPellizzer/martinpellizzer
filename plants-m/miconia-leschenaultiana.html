Miconia leschenaultiana

Miconia leschenaultiana, commonly known as the giant miconia, is a striking tropical plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in diameter, making it one of the largest leaf plants in the world. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in the late afternoon. Its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets have made it an invasive species in some regions.

Botanically, it belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is known for its distinctive, showy inflorescences.
