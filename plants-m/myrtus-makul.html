Myrtus makul

Myrtus makul, commonly known as the Madagascar myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are small, dark berries that contain seeds, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

The leaves and flowers are used in traditional medicine for their antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects.
