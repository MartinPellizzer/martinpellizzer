Minuartia glomerata subsp. trichocalycina

Minuartia glomerata subsp.

trichocalycina, commonly known as the clustered rockcress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions. It typically forms dense mats of small, fleshy, often blue or purple leaves.

The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates.

Its botanical features make it a characteristic species of tundra and mountainous environments.
