Medinilla pinnatinervia

Medinilla pinnatinervia, commonly known as the velvet tangerine or velvet leaf, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are pinnately veined, giving it a distinctive foliage appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, often clustered in terminal panicles. Its ornamental value lies in its lush foliage and showy blooms, making it a popular choice for gardens and indoor cultivation.

Medinilla pinnatinervia thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-draining soil and indirect sunlight for optimal growth.
