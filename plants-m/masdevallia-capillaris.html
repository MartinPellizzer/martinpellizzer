Masdevallia capillaris

Masdevallia capillaris, commonly known as the feathered masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves and intricate, often colorful flower spikes that bear small, fragrant blooms. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments at high altitudes, often on tree trunks or among rocks. Its flowers are usually pink or purple with intricate patterns, and it is prized for its ornamental value in horticulture.

Masdevallia capillaris is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its unique, ephemeral blooming cycle.
