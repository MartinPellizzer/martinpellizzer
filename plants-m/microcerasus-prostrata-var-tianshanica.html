Microcerasus prostrata var. tianshanica

Microcerasus prostrata var. tianshanica, commonly known as the Tianshan hawthorn, is a shrub native to the Tianshan Mountains in China.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit, with multiple stems that lie close to the ground. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in the spring, followed by edible berries that are typically red when mature. Its leaves are simple, elliptical, and have a slightly serrated margin.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping due to its hardy nature and adaptability to various soil conditions.
