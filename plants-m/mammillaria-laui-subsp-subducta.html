Mammillaria laui subsp. subducta

Mammillaria laui subsp.

subducta, commonly known as the narrow-ribbed mammillaria, is a small cactus native to Mexico. It features a compact, globular stem with narrow, closely spaced ribs.

The plant produces pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its spines are short and often white or pale yellow, giving it a delicate appearance.

It is well-suited for rock gardens and requires bright light and minimal water to thrive.
