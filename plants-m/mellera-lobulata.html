Mellera lobulata

Mellera lobulata, commonly known as the lobed mallows, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are divided into several lobes, and its showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones.

Mellera lobulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential healing properties.
