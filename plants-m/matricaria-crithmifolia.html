Matricaria crithmifolia

Matricaria crithmifolia, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flower heads that resemble small daisies, surrounded by spiky, silver-green leaves. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and rocky soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Matricaria crithmifolia is often used in ornamental gardening for its unique appearance and hardy nature.
