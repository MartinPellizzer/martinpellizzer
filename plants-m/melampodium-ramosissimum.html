Melampodium ramosissimum

Melampodium ramosissimum, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb with a bushy growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Melampodium ramosissimum is often found in dry, disturbed soils and is adapted to arid environments.
