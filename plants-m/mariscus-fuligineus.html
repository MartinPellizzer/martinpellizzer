Mariscus fuligineus

Mariscus fuligineus, commonly known as the sooty mariscus, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, evergreen shrub that typically grows in coastal and sandy environments. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are thick and waxy, adapted to conserve water in arid conditions.

Mariscus fuligineus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
