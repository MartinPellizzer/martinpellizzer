Maharanga incanescens

Maharanga incanescens, commonly known as the white maharanga, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Araliaceae and is characterized by its striking white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and a woody, branched stem. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
