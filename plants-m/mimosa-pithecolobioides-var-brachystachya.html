Mimosa pithecolobioides var. brachystachya

Mimosa pithecolobioides var. brachystachya, commonly known as the dwarf mimosa, is a small shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant produces delicate, fern-like leaves that close when touched, a response triggered by mechanical stimulation. Its flowers are typically small and yellow, arranged in clusters, and it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
