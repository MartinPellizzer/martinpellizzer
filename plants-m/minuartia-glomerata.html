Minuartia glomerata

Minuartia glomerata, commonly known as clustered rockcress, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of white or pale yellow. The plant has fleshy, succulent leaves that help it conserve water in harsh, cold environments. It thrives in rocky soils and is often found in mountainous or tundra habitats.

Minuartia glomerata is a hardy species that plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native ecosystems.
