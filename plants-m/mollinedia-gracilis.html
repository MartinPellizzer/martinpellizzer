Mollinedia gracilis

Mollinedia gracilis, commonly known as the slender mollinedia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in wetlands. The plant features slender, upright stems with alternate, linear to lanceolate leaves. Its small, white to pale blue flowers are clustered in dense, terminal inflorescences.

Mollinedia gracilis is notable for its ecological importance as a food source for various pollinators and wildlife.
