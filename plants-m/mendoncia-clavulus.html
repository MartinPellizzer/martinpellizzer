Mendoncia clavulus

Mendoncia clavulus, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
