Micranthes laciniata

Micranthes laciniata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pearly everlasting, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with a delicate, almost powdery appearance. The plant has finely dissected, lance-shaped leaves that give it a feathery appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
