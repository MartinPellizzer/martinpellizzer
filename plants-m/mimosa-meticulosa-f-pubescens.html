Mimosa meticulosa f. pubescens

Mimosa meticulosa f. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features delicate, fern-like foliage with a soft, hairy texture on its stems and leaves. It produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun.
