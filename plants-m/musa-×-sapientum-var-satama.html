Musa × sapientum var. satama

Musa × sapientum var. satama, commonly known as the red banana, is a cultivar of the banana plant characterized by its striking red peel and sweet, creamy flesh.

It belongs to the Musaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia, where it has been cultivated for centuries. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, with tall, leafy pseudostems that support the inflorescence. Its fruit develops in clusters, each containing several elongated, fleshy segments.

This variety is prized not only for its unique appearance but also for its flavor and nutritional value.
