Microchloa thomaea

Microchloa thomaea, commonly known as the Thomas's microchloa, is a small, perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and fine, needle-like leaves. The plant typically forms dense mats or clumps, often growing in shaded, moist environments. Microchloa thomaea is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils, making it a resilient species in various ecological settings.

Its flowering is relatively rare, with small, inconspicuous inflorescences that produce tiny seeds.
