Mentha viretorum

Mentha viretorum, commonly known as green mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its fragrant, narrow leaves and small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its aromatic leaves are used in traditional herbal remedies and can be brewed into teas.

Mentha viretorum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
