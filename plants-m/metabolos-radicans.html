Metabolos radicans

Metabolos radicans, commonly known as the creeping rootstock, is a perennial plant characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit.

It features slender, horizontal stems that spread along the ground, often rooting at the nodes to form dense colonies. The plant has small, alternate leaves that are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually inconspicuous, appearing in clusters and producing small, greenish-white blooms.

Metabolos radicans is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
