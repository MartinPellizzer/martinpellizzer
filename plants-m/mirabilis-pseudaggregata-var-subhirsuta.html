Mirabilis pseudaggregata var. subhirsuta

Mirabilis pseudaggregata var. subhirsuta, commonly known as the hairy mistflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, showy flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The leaves are typically ovate with hairy surfaces, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

This variety is noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
