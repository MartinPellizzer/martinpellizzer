Myrcia longipes

Myrcia longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed myrtle, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers. Its fruits are small, capsule-like structures that release seeds when mature.

Myrcia longipes is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
