Melocactus longispinus

Melocactus longispinus, commonly known as the long-spined melon cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family native to South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem and dense covering of long, sharp spines. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its spiky appearance. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring well-draining soil and minimal water.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
