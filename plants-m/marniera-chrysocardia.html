Marniera chrysocardia

Marniera chrysocardia, commonly known as the golden star bush, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact shrub form and produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that resemble stars. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Marniera chrysocardia is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive floral display.
