Myrcia tessmannii

Myrcia tessmannii, commonly known as the Brazilian peppertree, is a shrub or small tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that are fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds.

Myrcia tessmannii is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
