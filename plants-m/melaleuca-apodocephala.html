Melaleuca apodocephala

Melaleuca apodocephala, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tea tree, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered white or pinkish flowers. The plant produces woody capsules that contain numerous small seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or sandy habitats.

Melaleuca apodocephala is valued for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in horticulture.
