Musa acuminata var. microcarpa

Musa acuminata var. microcarpa, commonly known as the dwarf banana plant, is a tropical species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Musaceae family and is characterized by its small, compact growth habit compared to other banana varieties. The plant produces small, clustered fruits that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. It thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil and requires ample sunlight for optimal growth.

Due to its unique size and fruit characteristics, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant or for experimental breeding purposes.
