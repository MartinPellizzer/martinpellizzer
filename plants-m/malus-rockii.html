Malus rockii

Malus rockii, commonly known as the Yunnan apple, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to China and is characterized by its ornamental value due to its showy white flowers and attractive red fruit. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, while its fruits are edible and often used in local cuisine.

Malus rockii is also valued for its ornamental use in gardens and for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
