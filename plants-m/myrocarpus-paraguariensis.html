Myrocarpus paraguariensis

Myrocarpus paraguariensis, commonly known as the Paraguayan mangrove, is a tropical tree native to South America, particularly found in the coastal regions of Brazil, Paraguay, and Argentina.

It is a member of the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its thick, woody trunk and dense, evergreen foliage. The plant thrives in saline and brackish water environments, making it an important component of mangrove ecosystems. Its bark and leaves have been traditionally used in local medicine for their purported healing properties.

The fruit, which is small and fleshy, contains seeds that are dispersed by water, aiding in the plant's propagation along coastal areas.
