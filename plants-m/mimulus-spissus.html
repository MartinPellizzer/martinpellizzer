Mimulus spissus

Mimulus spissus, commonly known as the dense yellow monkeyflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features showy, tubular flowers that are typically orange to yellow in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and stream banks.

Mimulus spissus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
