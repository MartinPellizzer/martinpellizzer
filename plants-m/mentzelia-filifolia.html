Mentzelia filifolia

Mentzelia filifolia, commonly known as silverleaf blazingstar, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, hair-like structures called trichomes. The plant produces showy, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Mentzelia filifolia is a perennial species that plays a role in supporting local pollinators and wildlife.
