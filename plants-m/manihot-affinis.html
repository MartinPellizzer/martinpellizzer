Manihot affinis

Manihot affinis, commonly known as the African arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a perennial herb that produces edible tubers, which are rich in starch and used as a food source. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. Its tubers are often used as a substitute for potatoes or cassava in cooking.

The botanical characteristics of Manihot affinis make it an important crop in regions with warm climates.
