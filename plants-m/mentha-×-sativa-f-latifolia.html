Mentha × sativa f. latifolia

Mentha × sativa f. latifolia, commonly known as broad-leaf mint, is a hybrid mint species characterized by its wide, lance-shaped leaves.

It is a perennial herb that thrives in moist, shady environments, often found in woodland areas or along stream banks. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale purple or white, arranged in whorled clusters. Its aromatic foliage contains high concentrations of menthol, contributing to its strong, cooling scent.

This variety is widely cultivated for use in culinary, medicinal, and aromatic applications.
