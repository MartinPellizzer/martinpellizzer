Melandrium gracile

Melandrium gracile, commonly known as the slender catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, woodland edges, and along roadsides. Melandrium gracile is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ease of care.
