Macrorhynchus glaucus var. laciniatus

Macrorhynchus glaucus var. laciniatus, commonly known as the lace-leaf glaucus, is a striking perennial plant characterized by its finely dissected, bluish-gray leaves.

Native to arid and semi-arid regions, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in shades of white to pale purple, which appear in late spring. Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in regions with Mediterranean climates.
