Mentha aquatica var. monocephala

Mentha aquatica var. monocephala, commonly known as the water mint, is a perennial herb native to wetland habitats in Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in whorled clusters, typical of the mint family. It thrives in moist soils and is often found near streams, ponds, and marshes.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been historically used in herbal remedies for digestive and respiratory ailments.
