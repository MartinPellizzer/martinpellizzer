Machairophyllum latifolium

Machairophyllum latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf spurge, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
