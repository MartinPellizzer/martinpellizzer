Monechma debile var. bracteosum

Monechma debile var. bracteosum, commonly known as the bracteose monechma, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features small, ovate to lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving them a silvery appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale pink flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a valuable addition to native plant landscapes.
