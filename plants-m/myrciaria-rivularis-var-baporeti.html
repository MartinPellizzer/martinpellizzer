Myrciaria rivularis var. baporeti

Myrciaria rivularis var. baporeti, commonly known as the Brazilian blackberry, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are rich in antioxidants and have a sweet-tart flavor. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves with a glossy surface and a fragrant aroma. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sun exposure.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its culinary uses.
