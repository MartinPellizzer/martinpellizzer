Matelea orthosioides

Matelea orthosioides, commonly known as the Mexican moonflower, is a striking plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is known for its large, fragrant white flowers that open at night. The plant is a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, tubular flowers that emit a strong, sweet scent. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical forests.

Due to its unique blooming pattern and ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
