Momordica subangulata

Momordica subangulata, commonly known as the angular bitter melon, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like cucumbers and pumpkins. The plant produces elongated, angular fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn orange when mature. Its leaves are heart-shaped with prominent veins, and the flowers are yellow with five petals.

The fruit is used in traditional medicine and cuisine, valued for its bitter taste and medicinal properties.
