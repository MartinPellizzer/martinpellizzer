Manulea namibensis

Manulea namibensis, commonly known as the Namib Desert lily, is a unique flowering plant native to the hyperarid regions of Namibia.

It is a perennial shrub that thrives in extreme arid conditions, often growing in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers during the brief rainy season, which is typically in late summer. Its leaves are adapted to minimize water loss, featuring a waxy coating and reduced surface area.

Manulea namibensis plays a significant role in the local ecosystem, providing nectar for pollinators and shelter for small desert animals.
