Mikania lancifolia

Mikania lancifolia, commonly known as the arrowleaf mikania, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by fluffy, white seeds that are easily dispersed by the wind. Mikania lancifolia is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges.

Its rapid growth and invasive tendencies make it a notable species in ecological studies.
