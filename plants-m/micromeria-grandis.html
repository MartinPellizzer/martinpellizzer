Micromeria grandis

Micromeria grandis, commonly known as the desert star, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, exhibiting adaptations to conserve water. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are narrow and covered in a fine layer of white hairs, reducing water loss.

Micromeria grandis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
