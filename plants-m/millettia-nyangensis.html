Millettia nyangensis

Millettia nyangensis, commonly known as the African ironwood tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its durability. The tree produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Due to its ecological and economic importance, it is also considered a species of conservation concern.
