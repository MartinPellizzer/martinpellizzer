Maxillaria spathacea

Maxillaria spathacea, commonly known as the Spathe Maxillaria, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its sympodial growth habit and produces pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and bears showy, fragrant flowers with a distinctive spathe-like structure. Its blooms typically appear in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with contrasting markings.

Maxillaria spathacea is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
