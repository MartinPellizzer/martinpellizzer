Macropiper reinwardtianum

Macropiper reinwardtianum, commonly known as the pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by elliptical, reddish berries. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
