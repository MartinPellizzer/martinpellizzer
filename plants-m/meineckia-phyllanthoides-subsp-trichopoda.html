Meineckia phyllanthoides subsp. trichopoda

Meineckia phyllanthoides subsp.

trichopoda, commonly known as the silverleaf sandmat, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It features a creeping, succulent stem system that allows it to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments.

The plant has small, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a whitish, powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss. Its flowers are typically small and yellow, arranged in clusters, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

This species is well adapted to sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland dune areas.
