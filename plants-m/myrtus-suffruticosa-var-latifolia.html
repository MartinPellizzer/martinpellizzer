Myrtus suffruticosa var. latifolia

Myrtus suffruticosa var. latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf myrtle, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces small white or pale pink flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Its ornamental value and evergreen nature make it a popular choice for landscaping.
