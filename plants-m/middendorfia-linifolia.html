Middendorfia linifolia

Middendorfia linifolia, commonly known as the threadleaf middendorfia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, elongated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces delicate, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially shaded woodland areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
