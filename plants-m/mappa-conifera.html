Mappa conifera

Mappa conifera, commonly known as the Christmas tree, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the genus *Pinus* and is characterized by its needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure. The tree typically grows in cold climates and has a conical shape with dense foliage. Its cones are usually woody and contain seeds that are dispersed by wind.

The plant is widely cultivated for its ornamental value during the holiday season.
