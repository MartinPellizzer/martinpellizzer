Medicago hispida

Medicago hispida, commonly known as goat's rue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces pods containing seeds with a distinctive hooked shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Medicago hispida is also known for its medicinal uses in traditional herbal remedies.
