Maxillaria longibracteata var. grandiflora

Maxillaria longibracteata var. grandiflora, commonly known as the grandiflora orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features a pseudobulb that supports its growth and produces a single, upright stem bearing multiple lance-shaped leaves. The plant is renowned for its large, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple with a distinctive white or yellow lip. Its flowers are fragrant and bloom during the warmer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
