Maxillaria superflua

Maxillaria superflua, commonly known as the showy maxillaria, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that often feature a combination of white, yellow, and green hues. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
