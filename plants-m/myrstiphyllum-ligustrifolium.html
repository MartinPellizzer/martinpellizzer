Myrstiphyllum ligustrifolium

Myrstiphyllum ligustrifolium, commonly known as the New Zealand whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and tea tree. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. It produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by woody, spherical fruits.

This species is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in coastal and subalpine regions.
