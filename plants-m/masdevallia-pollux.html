Masdevallia pollux

Masdevallia pollux, commonly known as the Pollux orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a compact, pseudobulbous structure and is known for its striking, colorful flowers. The plant typically produces a single, showy inflorescence with flowers that are often violet or purple in color. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store moisture in its humid, high-altitude habitat.

Masdevallia pollux is appreciated by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and unique floral morphology.
