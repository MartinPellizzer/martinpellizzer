Monotes dasyanthus var. dasyanthus

Monotes dasyanthus var. dasyanthus, commonly known as the woolly thornapple, is a species native to South America.

It is a shrub characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, hard seeds. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
