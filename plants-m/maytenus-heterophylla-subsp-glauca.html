Maytenus heterophylla subsp. glauca

Maytenus heterophylla subsp.

glauca, commonly known as the blue-leaf mayten, is a shrub native to parts of South America, particularly Brazil. It is characterized by its glaucous, waxy leaves that give it a distinctive bluish-gray appearance.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are simple and alternate, with a rough texture and prominent veins. Maytenus heterophylla subsp.

glauca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
