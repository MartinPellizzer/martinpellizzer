Myrtus romanifolia

Myrtus romanifolia, commonly known as Roman myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, dark green leaves that are aromatic and often used in culinary and traditional medicine. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. Myrtus romanifolia thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its historical significance dates back to ancient Roman times, where it was valued for its fragrance and symbolism.
