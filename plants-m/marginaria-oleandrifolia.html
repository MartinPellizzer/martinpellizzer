Marginaria oleandrifolia

Marginaria oleandrifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its flowers bloom throughout the growing season, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
