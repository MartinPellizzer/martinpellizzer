Marsana

Marsana, commonly known as the "wild orchid," is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant blossoms. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are usually pink or purple in color.

Marsana is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental beauty.
