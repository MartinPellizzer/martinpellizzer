Massonia longifolia var. candida

Massonia longifolia var. candida, commonly known as the white-flowered massonia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, cylindrical leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers, it is often cultivated as a decorative succulent in gardens and containers.
