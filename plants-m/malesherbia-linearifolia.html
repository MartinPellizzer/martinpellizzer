Malesherbia linearifolia

Malesherbia linearifolia, commonly known as the slenderleaf malesherbia, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in horticultural settings.
