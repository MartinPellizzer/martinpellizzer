Monnina cestrifolia

Monnina cestrifolia, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces delicate, blue-violet flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, branching stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Monnina cestrifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
