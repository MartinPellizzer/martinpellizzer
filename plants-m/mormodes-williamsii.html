Mormodes williamsii

Mormodes williamsii, commonly known as the Williams' orchid, is a species of orchid native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, often in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the forest floor.

Mormodes williamsii is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
