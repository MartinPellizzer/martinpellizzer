Mesembryanthemum coccineum

Mesembryanthemum coccineum, commonly known as the red cushion flower, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, fleshy, and often hairy foliage that helps it retain water in arid environments. The plant produces vibrant red or pink flowers that bloom in the morning and close by midday. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a valuable addition to Mediterranean and coastal climates.
