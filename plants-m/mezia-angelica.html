Mezia angelica

Mezia angelica, commonly known as angelica, is a biennial or perennial plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, hollow stems and large, umbrella-shaped compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into oval, dark purple berries. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It is valued for its aromatic leaves and roots, which have been used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
