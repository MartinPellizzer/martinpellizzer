Malaxis verticillata

Malaxis verticillata, commonly known as the spiral orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique spiral arrangement of leaves, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Malaxis verticillata is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
