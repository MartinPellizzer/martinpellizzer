Medicago afghanica

Medicago afghanica, commonly known as Afghan clover, is a perennial legume native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that bloom in shades of pink to purple. The plant has a deep root system, allowing it to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments. It is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Medicago afghanica is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
