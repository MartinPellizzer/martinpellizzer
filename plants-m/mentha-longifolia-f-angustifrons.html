Mentha longifolia f. angustifrons

Mentha longifolia f. angustifrons, commonly known as narrow-leaved mint, is a variant of the mint plant characterized by its slender, elongated leaves.

It is a perennial herb native to regions of Europe and Asia, often found in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or blue in color, arranged in spikes. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, giving it a distinctive minty scent.

This form is valued for its ornamental qualities and mild flavor, making it suitable for culinary and medicinal uses.
