Malva spicata var. ovata

Malva spicata var. ovata, commonly known as the broadleaf mallow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of the Mediterranean.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and prominent veins. The plant produces clusters of pink to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
