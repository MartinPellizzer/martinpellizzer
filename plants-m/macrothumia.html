Macrothumia

Macrothumia, commonly known as the giant flame violet, is a striking flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from deep purple to bright orange, often with intricate patterns. The plant has thick, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in its arid habitat. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, Macrothumia is admired by both botanists and horticulturists alike.
