Mammillaria nuda

Mammillaria nuda, commonly known as the naked mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are typically covered with white, woolly spines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is a low-maintenance plant that thrives in well-draining soil and full sunlight.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for succulent collectors and desert gardens.
