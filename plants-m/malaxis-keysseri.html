Malaxis keysseri

Malaxis keysseri, commonly known as the Keyser's lady's tresses, is an epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and a long, slender flowering spike that produces numerous small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. It has a complex root system adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from its surroundings.

Malaxis keysseri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
