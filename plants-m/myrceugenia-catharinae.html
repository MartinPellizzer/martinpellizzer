Myrceugenia catharinae

Myrceugenia catharinae, commonly known as the Santa Catarina myrtle, is a small evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. It produces small, round berries that are typically red when mature.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and potential ecological significance in its native habitat.
