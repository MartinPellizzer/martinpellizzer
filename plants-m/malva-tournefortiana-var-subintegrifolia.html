Malva tournefortiana var. subintegrifolia

Malva tournefortiana var. subintegrifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallows, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves with a smooth or slightly toothed margin. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its botanical features include a robust root system and a tendency to form clumps, which contribute to its resilience in various climates.
