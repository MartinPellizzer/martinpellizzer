Macaglia macrocarpa

Macaglia macrocarpa, commonly known as the giant macaranga, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a fast-growing shrub or small tree that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically white or yellow, and it produces berries that are eaten by various bird species.

The plant is adapted to thrive in humid, shaded environments within tropical rainforests.
