Marantochloa flexuosa

Marantochloa flexuosa, commonly known as the zigzag plant, is a member of the Marantaceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its distinctive zigzagging stems and striking, often colorful foliage. The leaves are typically arranged in a rosette pattern and can exhibit a variety of hues, including red, purple, and green. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually borne in clusters.
