Myrceugenia valientei

Myrceugenia valientei, commonly known as the Valiente's myrtle, is a species of flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in humid, subtropical climates and is often found in coastal forests and shrublands.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
