Magnolia kwangsiensis

Magnolia kwangsiensis, commonly known as the Guangxi magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to southern China.

It is characterized by its large, showy, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, with a fragrant scent.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
