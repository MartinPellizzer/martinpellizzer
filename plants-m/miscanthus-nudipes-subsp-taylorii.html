Miscanthus nudipes subsp. taylorii

Miscanthus nudipes subsp.

taylorii, commonly known as Taylor's rough grass, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of China. It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for erosion control, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and ecological restoration projects.
