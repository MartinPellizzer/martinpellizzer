Mikania violacea

Mikania violacea, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. Its showy blue-violet flowers bloom in late summer and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Mikania violacea is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
