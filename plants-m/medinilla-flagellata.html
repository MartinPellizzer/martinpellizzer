Medinilla flagellata

Medinilla flagellata, commonly known as the whip medinilla, is a tropical shrub native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and produces clusters of small, elliptical leaves. Its flowers are borne on long, slender pedicels, which give the plant its common name.

Medinilla flagellata thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
