Machilus curranii

Machilus curranii, commonly known as Curran's chinquapin, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by oval, woody fruits that contain a single seed.

Machilus curranii is often found in shaded, moist environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
