Moscharia arabica

Moscharia arabica, commonly known as the Arabian coffee plant, is a flowering shrub native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy fruits containing seeds. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid climates, requiring well-drained soil and full sun exposure. Its leaves are elliptical and have a rough texture, while its stems are woody and branched.

Moscharia arabica is historically significant for its role in the development of coffee cultivation in the region.
