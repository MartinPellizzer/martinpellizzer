Mercurialis annua var. serratifolia

Mercurialis annua var. serratifolia, commonly known as the serrated mercury plant, is a flowering plant in the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is characterized by its alternate, serrated leaves that are typically green with a slightly hairy texture. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats.

Due to its toxic properties, it is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
