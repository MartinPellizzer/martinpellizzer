Mangifera pajang

Mangifera pajang, commonly known as the Java mango, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its elongated, ovate leaves and fragrant white flowers. The fruit is typically small, with a thin, smooth skin and a sweet, fibrous pulp. It is valued for its unique flavor and is often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

The tree thrives in humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
