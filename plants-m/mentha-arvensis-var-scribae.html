Mentha arvensis var. scribae

Mentha arvensis var. scribae, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale purple or blue, arranged in loose spikes. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
