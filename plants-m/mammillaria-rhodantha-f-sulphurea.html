Mammillaria rhodantha f. sulphurea

Mammillaria rhodantha f. sulphurea, commonly known as the sulphur-yellow mammillaria, is a rare and striking cactus species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in dense, white to yellowish tubercles. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its unique coloration and compact growth make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in controlled environments to ensure its survival.
