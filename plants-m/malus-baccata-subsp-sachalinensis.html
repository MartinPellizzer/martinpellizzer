Malus baccata subsp. sachalinensis

Malus baccata subsp.

sachalinensis, commonly known as the Manchurian crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia. It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are typically gray-green in color.

The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers in spring, followed by edible, red berries in the fall. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.

The subspecies is notable for its adaptability to various soil types and its use in traditional medicine.
