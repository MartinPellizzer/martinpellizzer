Mariscus sieberianus var. evolutior

Mariscus sieberianus var. evolutior, commonly known as the Sieber's mariscus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, and are typically greenish-white in color.

This variety is notable for its hardy nature and adaptability to harsh, high-altitude conditions.
