Mormodes gurupiensis

Mormodes gurupiensis, commonly known as the Brazilian orchid, is a rare and endemic species native to the rainforests of Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in its humid, shaded habitat. Its leaves are usually narrow and strap-like, adapted to maximize light absorption in the dense forest understory.

Due to habitat destruction, Mormodes gurupiensis is considered endangered and is of great interest to botanists and conservationists.
