Mappia ovata var. championiana

Mappia ovata var. championiana, commonly known as the Champion's mappia, is a rare and distinctive species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its ovate, glossy leaves that are typically a deep green with a slight waxy coating. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, often white or pale yellow, which bloom during the rainy season. Its unique leaf shape and ornamental value make it a sought-after plant in botanical gardens and horticultural collections.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered endangered in the wild.
