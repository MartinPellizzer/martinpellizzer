Monodora microcarpa

Monodora microcarpa, commonly known as African nutmeg, is a small to medium-sized tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Anacardiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by oval, woody fruits containing a single seed. Its seeds are highly aromatic and have been traditionally used for their medicinal and culinary properties.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique fragrance.
