Melocactus rufispinus f. paucispinus

Melocactus rufispinus f. paucispinus, commonly known as the rare red-spined melocactus, is a rare and distinctive variant of the melocactus genus.

It is characterized by its short, thick stems and sparse, reddish spines, which set it apart from the more common forms of Melocactus rufispinus. This plant is native to a very limited range in northern Mexico, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid conditions. It has a slow growth rate and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native environment.
