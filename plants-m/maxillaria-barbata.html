Maxillaria barbata

Maxillaria barbata, commonly known as the hairy maxillaria, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure and produces clusters of fragrant, dark pink to violet flowers. The plant has long, slender leaves that are usually covered in a fine, hairy texture, contributing to its common name. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Maxillaria barbata is appreciated by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
