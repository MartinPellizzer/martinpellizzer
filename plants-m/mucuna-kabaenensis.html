Mucuna kabaenensis

Mucuna kabaenensis, commonly known as the Kabaenensis velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces elongated pods containing kidney-shaped seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in secondary forests or disturbed habitats.

Mucuna kabaenensis is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil.
