Miconia cucullata

Miconia cucullata, commonly known as the hooded miconia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that are followed by distinctive, round, red berries. Miconia cucullata is known for its rapid growth and can become invasive in certain environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and horticulture.
