Masdevallia ecallosa

Masdevallia ecallosa, commonly known as the Orchid of the Andes, is a rare and exotic orchid native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, often colorful flowers with specialized structures for pollination. The plant typically grows as a sympodial orchid, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, enabling it to thrive in its challenging mountainous habitat. Its flowers are often adorned with intricate patterns and varying hues, making it a prized specimen among orchid enthusiasts.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, it is considered a conservation concern in its native range.
