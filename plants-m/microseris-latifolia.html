Microseris latifolia

Microseris latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf scabiosa, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, showy flowers and pinnately lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are often arranged in a flat-topped cluster, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Microseris latifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
