Medicago sativa var. hybrida

Medicago sativa var. hybrida, commonly known as hybrid alfalfa, is a perennial legume characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers.

It is cultivated for its high protein content and nutritional value, making it a valuable forage crop. The plant has a deep root system that helps in soil stabilization and nitrogen fixation. It thrives in temperate climates and is often used in rotational farming systems.

Its hybrid nature allows for improved yield and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
