Mimulus × robertsii

Mimulus × robertsii, commonly known as the hybrid monkeyflower, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is a cross between Mimulus guttatus and Mimulus luteus, resulting in a plant with distinctive yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils along streambanks and in open woodlands. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves, and it produces showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Mimulus × robertsii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
