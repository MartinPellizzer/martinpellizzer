Microtoena subspicata var. intermedia

Microtoena subspicata var. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate microtoena, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale green to brown flowers. It is typically found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a component of diverse grassland ecosystems and its potential use in ecological restoration projects.
