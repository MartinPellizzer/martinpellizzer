Miconia paulina

Miconia paulina, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or red miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers, followed by bright red, fleshy berries that are attractive to birds. Miconia paulina is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in some regions, though it can become invasive if not managed properly.
