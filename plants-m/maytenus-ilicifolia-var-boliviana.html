Maytenus ilicifolia var. boliviana

Maytenus ilicifolia var. boliviana, commonly known as the Bolivian huito, is a shrub or small tree native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with small, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by elliptical, fleshy fruits that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid, temperate climates and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
