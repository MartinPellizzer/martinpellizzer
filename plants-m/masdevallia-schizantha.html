Masdevallia schizantha

Masdevallia schizantha, commonly known as the Split-leaf Masdevallia, is a tropical orchid native to the Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated pseudobulbs and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, white, and purple, often with a unique, spurred lip. It thrives in cool, high-altitude environments with high humidity and indirect light.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and rarity.
