Monochaetum cymosum

Monochaetum cymosum, commonly known as the Cape York spikeless grass, is a perennial grass species native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear tiny, spikelet-like flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is typically found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Monochaetum cymosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soils in its native habitat.
