Marsdenia officinalis

Marsdenia officinalis, commonly known as the Chinese moonseed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Apocynaceae family.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that contain seeds, and it is native to various regions in Asia, including China, India, and Southeast Asia.

Marsdenia officinalis has been used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties, though its botanical applications remain under scientific investigation.
