Marica bermudiana

Marica bermudiana, commonly known as the Bermuda buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Bermuda.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces bright yellow flowers with a distinctive cup-shaped structure. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in sandy, well-drained soils. It is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to the warm, humid climate of the Caribbean and the southeastern United States.

The Bermuda buttercup is also known for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
