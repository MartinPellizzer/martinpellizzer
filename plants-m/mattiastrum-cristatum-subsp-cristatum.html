Mattiastrum cristatum subsp. cristatum

Mattiastrum cristatum subsp.

cristatum, commonly known as the crested mat grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its dense, mat-like growth habit and distinctive, often tufted or crested inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local insect populations.
