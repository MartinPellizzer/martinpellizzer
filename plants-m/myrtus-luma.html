Myrtus luma

Myrtus luma, commonly known as the common myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant leaves with a glossy green surface and a distinctive white, waxy coating. The plant produces small, pale pink or white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by dark blue berries. Myrtus luma is valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in traditional medicine and as a decorative plant.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and versatile species suitable for various garden environments.
