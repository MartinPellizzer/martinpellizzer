Miconia subhirsuta

Miconia subhirsuta, commonly known as the velvetleaf miconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries.

Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine, and the plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments.
