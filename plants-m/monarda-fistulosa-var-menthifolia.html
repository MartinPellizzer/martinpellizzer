Monarda fistulosa var. menthifolia

Monarda fistulosa var. menthifolia, commonly known as lemon bee balm, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, tubular red or pink flowers arranged in whorled clusters atop sturdy stems. The plant has lance-shaped, aromatic leaves with a mint-like scent, which contribute to its distinctive fragrance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a hardy garden plant.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract bees, butterflies, and other pollinators, supporting biodiversity in native landscapes.
