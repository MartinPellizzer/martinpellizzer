Machaerium brasiliense var. erianthum

Machaerium brasiliense var. erianthum, commonly known as the Brazilian machaerium, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential ecological benefits in soil stabilization.
