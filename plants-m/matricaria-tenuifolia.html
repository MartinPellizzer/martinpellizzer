Matricaria tenuifolia

Matricaria tenuifolia, commonly known as yarrow, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features slender, upright stems and small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often used in herbal medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common garden plant.

Matricaria tenuifolia is also valued for its aromatic properties and historical use in traditional remedies.
