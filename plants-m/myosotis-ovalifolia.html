Myosotis ovalifolia

Myosotis ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leaved forget-me-not, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its small, delicate blue or purple flowers and oval-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its flowers bloom in spring and summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Myosotis ovalifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and conservation efforts.
