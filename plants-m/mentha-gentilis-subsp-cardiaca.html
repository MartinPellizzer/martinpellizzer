Mentha gentilis subsp. cardiaca

Mentha gentilis subsp.

cardiaca, commonly known as heart mint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly minty leaves that are often heart-shaped, giving it its common name.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are small, tubular, and usually pink or purple, appearing in clusters. Mentha gentilis subsp.

cardiaca is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
