Millettia solheidii

Millettia solheidii, commonly known as the African senna, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it valuable in soil enrichment. It produces flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Millettia solheidii is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of timber.
