Musa × sapientum var. pruinosa

Musa × sapientum var. pruinosa, commonly known as the pruinose banana, is a variant of the common banana plant.

It is characterized by its waxy, pruinose coating on the leaves, which gives it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces large, elongated fruits that are typically harvested when they are still green. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as for its edible fruit.
