Mentha reflexifolia

Mentha reflexifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaf mint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in whorls along the stems. Mentha reflexifolia is a member of the Lamiaceae family, known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.
