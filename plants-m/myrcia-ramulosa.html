Myrcia ramulosa

Myrcia ramulosa, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are alternate, oval-shaped, and have a glossy dark green surface.

The fruit is a small, red, fleshy drupe that ripens in late summer.
