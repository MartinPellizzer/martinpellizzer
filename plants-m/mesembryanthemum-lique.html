Mesembryanthemum lique

Mesembryanthemum lique, commonly known as the ice plant, is a member of the Aizoaceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of pink, red, and yellow. The plant is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick leaves and stems. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable in dry regions.
