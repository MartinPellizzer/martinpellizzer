Mentha longifolia var. favratii

Mentha longifolia var. favratii, commonly known as Corsican mint, is a variant of the Mentha longifolia species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly minty leaves and is often used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in temperate climates. It produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its unique fragrance and adaptability, it is valued in both traditional herbal medicine and modern aromatherapy.
