Mimosa widgrenii

Mimosa widgrenii, commonly known as the Narrowleaf Mimosa, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves that are typically narrow and bipinnate, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow, ball-shaped flowers that bloom in spring, adding a soft, luminous effect to its foliage. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Mimosa widgrenii is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in arid environments.
