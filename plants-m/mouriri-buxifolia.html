Mouriri buxifolia

Mouriri buxifolia, commonly known as the waxleaf mouriri, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, edible berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

Mouriri buxifolia is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
