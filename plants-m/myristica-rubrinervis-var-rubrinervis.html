Myristica rubrinervis var. rubrinervis

Myristica rubrinervis var. rubrinervis, commonly known as the red-veined myristica, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen tree that belongs to the Myristicaceae family, characterized by its aromatic and oily seeds. The plant features glossy, elliptical leaves with prominent red veins, which give it its common name. Its flowers are small and yellowish, typically clustered in panicles, and it produces drupes with a hard, woody outer layer.

The plant is valued for its seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.
