Myrcia mugiensis

Myrcia mugiensis, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, reddish-brown berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in humid, shaded environments.

Myrcia mugiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and aromatic properties.
