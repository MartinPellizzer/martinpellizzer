Malpighia mexicana

Malpighia mexicana, commonly known as Mexican acorn or red mombin, is a small evergreen shrub native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Malpighiaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces bright red, fleshy berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. Its fruit is rich in vitamin C and has a tart, acidic flavor.

Malpighia mexicana thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in shaded, moist environments.
