Medicago echinus var. tuberculata

Medicago echinus var. tuberculata, commonly known as the tuberculate bur clover, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its serrated, hairy leaves and distinctive spiny pods that contain small, yellow flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats. Its trifoliate leaves and compound pods are typical of the Fabaceae family.

It is valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a forage plant for livestock.
