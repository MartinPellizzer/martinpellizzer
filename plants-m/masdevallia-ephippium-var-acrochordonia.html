Masdevallia ephippium var. acrochordonia

Masdevallia ephippium var. acrochordonia, commonly known as the acrochordonic masdevallia, is a terrestrial orchid native to the cloud forests of Colombia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a pseudobulb and produces showy, lanceolate flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant thrives in cool, humid environments with high light exposure but requires protection from direct sunlight. Its unique floral structure and striking coloration make it a prized specimen among orchid enthusiasts.

It is typically cultivated in shaded greenhouses or under artificial lighting to mimic its natural habitat.
