Mitella × intermedia

Mitella × intermedia, commonly known as the hybrid evergreen mitella, is a perennial plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is a cross between Mitella nana and Mitella diphylla, resulting in a plant with distinctive foliage and floral characteristics. The leaves are typically oval to lance-shaped, with a bluish-green hue and a slightly hairy texture. It produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in late spring.

This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded forest environments.
