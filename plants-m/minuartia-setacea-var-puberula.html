Minuartia setacea var. puberula

Minuartia setacea var. puberula, commonly known as the silky sandwort, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It features small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are narrow, linear, and covered with fine, soft hairs, giving the plant a silvery appearance. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as tundra and mountain meadows.

This species is adapted to cold climates and is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family.
