Mimosa malacophylla

Mimosa malacophylla, commonly known as the velvetleaf mimosa, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which are soft and fuzzy to the touch, giving the plant its common name. The plant produces small, yellow, spherical flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Mimosa malacophylla is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and unique appearance.
