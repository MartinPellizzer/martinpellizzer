Melilotus striatus

Melilotus striatus, commonly known as yellow sweet clover, is a flowering plant in the legume family.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in dense clusters. The plant is native to Europe and Asia but has been introduced to various parts of the world. It has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets.

Melilotus striatus is often found in disturbed soils and is used in agriculture for forage and as a cover crop.
