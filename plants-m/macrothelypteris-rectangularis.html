Macrothelypteris rectangularis

Macrothelypteris rectangularis, commonly known as the broad-leaf huperzia, is a species of fern native to the southeastern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, broad, leathery leaves that are typically divided into segments. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, with creeping stems that form dense mats. Its fronds are usually evergreen, and they can reach lengths of up to 1 meter.

The plant prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
