Mentha wierzbickiana var. stenantha

Mentha wierzbickiana var. stenantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mint, is a perennial herb native to eastern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often slightly hairy. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in whorled clusters, typical of the Lamiaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in herbal teas or as a ornamental plant in gardens.
