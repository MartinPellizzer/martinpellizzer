Malva × zoernigii

Malva × zoernigii, commonly known as the velvetleaf mallow, is a hybrid perennial plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, velvety leaves and showy, pale pink to lavender flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its flowers are five-petaled and often have a distinctive central tuft of stamens, adding to its ornamental appeal.

It is native to the Mediterranean region but is cultivated in various parts of the world for its beauty and adaptability.
