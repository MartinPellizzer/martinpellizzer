Mucuna coriacea var. glabrialata

Mucuna coriacea var. glabrialata, commonly known as the glabrous cowpea, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and distinctive, twisted, woody pods. The plant produces small, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in clusters. Its seeds are typically round and have a hard, smooth coat.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its nitrogen-fixing properties in agricultural settings.
