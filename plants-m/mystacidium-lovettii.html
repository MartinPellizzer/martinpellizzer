Mystacidium lovettii

Mystacidium lovettii, commonly known as the desert lichen, is a symbiotic composite organism consisting of a green alga and a fungal partner.

It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, typically found on rock surfaces or soil in desert regions. The plant's structure is characterized by a thallus that is often crust-like or foliose in form. It plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and contributing to nutrient cycling in harsh ecosystems.

Due to its specialized adaptations, it is a key indicator species for arid habitats.
