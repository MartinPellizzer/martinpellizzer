Mahernia angustifolia

Mahernia angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mahernia, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its slender, angular stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of delicate, pale blue or purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to its harsh environment.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
