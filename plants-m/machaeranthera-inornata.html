Machaeranthera inornata

Machaeranthera inornata, commonly known as the plains coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the central and southern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in open, dry habitats such as prairies and woodland edges. Its yellow-orange flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
