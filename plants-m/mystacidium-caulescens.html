Mystacidium caulescens

Mystacidium caulescens, commonly known as the slender mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that primarily grows on the branches of eucalyptus trees in Australia.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along its stem. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its seeds are dispersed by birds that feed on the fleshy, red berries it produces.

The species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
