Mentha lavandulacea

Mentha lavandulacea, commonly known as lavender mint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or blue in color. Its aromatic leaves and flowers are often used in aromatherapy and as a culinary herb.

Mentha lavandulacea thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight.
