Mentha arvensis f. ramosissima

Mentha arvensis f. ramosissima, commonly known as field mint or creeping mint, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, serrated leaves and clusters of small, pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as meadows and woodlands. It has a spreading growth habit, often forming dense mats due to its stolons.

Its essential oils contain menthol and other compounds, making it valuable in both culinary and medicinal applications.
