Macrotyloma daltonii

Macrotyloma daltonii, commonly known as Dalton's cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are rich in protein and used in various cuisines.

It is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important crop in sustainable agriculture.
