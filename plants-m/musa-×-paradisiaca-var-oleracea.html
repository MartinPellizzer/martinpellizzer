Musa × paradisiaca var. oleracea

Musa × paradisiaca var. oleracea, commonly known as the banana plant, is a tropical herbaceous plant belonging to the Musaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, broad, sword-shaped leaves and a tall, segmented stem that bears clusters of fleshy, edible berries. The plant is native to Southeast Asia but is now cultivated worldwide for its fruit, which is rich in potassium and dietary fiber. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The plant's rapid growth and high yield make it a significant crop in many tropical regions.
