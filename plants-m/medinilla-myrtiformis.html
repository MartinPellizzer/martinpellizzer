Medinilla myrtiformis

Medinilla myrtiformis, commonly known as the velvet tangerine or velvetleaf, is a tropical flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of small, fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil.

Medinilla myrtiformis is also valued for its ability to produce edible fruits, which have a tangy, citrus-like flavor.
