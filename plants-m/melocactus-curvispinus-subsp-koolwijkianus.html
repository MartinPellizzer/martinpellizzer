Melocactus curvispinus subsp. koolwijkianus

Melocactus curvispinus subsp.

koolwijkianus, commonly known as the Koolwijk's hooked spine melocactus, is a cactus native to parts of South America, particularly Brazil. It is characterized by its columnar stem and dense, spiny areoles that give it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the dry season, which are typically borne at the top of the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its slow growth rate and unique morphology make it a prized specimen among cactus enthusiasts.
