Miconia pilifera

Miconia pilifera, commonly known as the silky miconia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by bright red berries. Miconia pilifera is known for its rapid growth and can become invasive in certain regions.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
