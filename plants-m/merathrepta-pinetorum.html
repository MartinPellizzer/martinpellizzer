Merathrepta pinetorum

Merathrepta pinetorum, commonly known as the pine forest fern, is a unique species native to the temperate forests of the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that emerge from a short, rhizomatous stem. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found growing along the bases of pine trees. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are typically located on the underside of the fronds.

Merathrepta pinetorum plays a vital role in forest ecosystems by contributing to soil stability and providing habitat for various microorganisms.
