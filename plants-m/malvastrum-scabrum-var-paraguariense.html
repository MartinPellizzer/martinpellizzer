Malvastrum scabrum var. paraguariense

Malvastrum scabrum var. paraguariense, commonly known as Paraguayan mallow, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus. The plant features hairy, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces showy, pink to purple flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking native flora.
