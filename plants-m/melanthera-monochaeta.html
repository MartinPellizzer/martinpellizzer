Melanthera monochaeta

Melanthera monochaeta, commonly known as the narrow-headed bluemink, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features slender, hairy stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Melanthera monochaeta is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.
