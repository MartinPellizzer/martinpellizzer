Microstachys virgata

Microstachys virgata, commonly known as the Virgin's tassel, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, branched inflorescences that resemble small tassels, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and shrublands. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture with a slightly glossy surface.

Microstachys virgata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
