Maieta rubrinervis

Maieta rubrinervis, commonly known as the red-veined mayweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves with prominent reddish veins. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in moist, disturbed soils along roadsides and in meadows.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
