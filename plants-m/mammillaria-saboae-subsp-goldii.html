Mammillaria saboae subsp. goldii

Mammillaria saboae subsp.

goldii, commonly known as the Goldii Pincushion Cactus, is a species native to Mexico. It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and is covered in white to light green tubercles.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its small size and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor collections.
