Mammillaria elongata f. rufocrocea

Mammillaria elongata f. rufocrocea, commonly known as the red-spined hedgehog cactus, is a fascinating succulent native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

This variety is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stem that can grow up to 30 centimeters in height. The plant features dense, spiny areoles covered with a distinctive reddish-brown coloration, giving it its common name. It produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by pale green fruits.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and collectors.
