Myristica guadalcanalensis

Myristica guadalcanalensis, commonly known as the Guadalcanal myristica, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the island of Guadalcanal in the Solomon Islands.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, oily seeds and dark, glossy leaves. The plant typically grows in lowland rainforests, thriving in humid and shaded environments. Its fruit is a drupe, containing a single seed that is rich in essential oils.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
