Mentha longifolia var. leioneura

Mentha longifolia var. leioneura, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are usually lighter in color compared to other mint varieties. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This variety is often used in herbal teas and traditional medicine for its aromatic and cooling properties.
