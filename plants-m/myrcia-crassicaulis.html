Myrcia crassicaulis

Myrcia crassicaulis, commonly known as the broad-leaved myrtle, is a shrub native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in coastal areas.

Myrcia crassicaulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and aromatic properties.
