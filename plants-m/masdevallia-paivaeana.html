Masdevallia paivaeana

Masdevallia paivaeana, commonly known as the Ecuadorian masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and strikingly colorful, often spotted or mottled flowers. The plant typically produces small, pendulous blooms in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which are highly ornamental. It thrives in cool, humid environments with high light exposure, making it a popular choice for greenhouse cultivation.

Due to its limited natural range, it is considered a rare and endangered species in the wild.
