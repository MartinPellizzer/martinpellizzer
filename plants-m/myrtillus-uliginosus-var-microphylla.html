Myrtillus uliginosus var. microphylla

Myrtillus uliginosus var. microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved bog bilberry, is a variant of the bog bilberry native to northern regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a low-growing shrub with small, dark blue berries that are typically edible. The plant thrives in acidic, wet soils, often found in bogs and heathlands. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, adapted to the moist, nutrient-poor environments it inhabits.

The species plays an important ecological role in its habitat, supporting various wildlife species.
