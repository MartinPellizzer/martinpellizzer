Mimosa pteridifolia var. nanophylla

Mimosa pteridifolia var. nanophylla, commonly known as the dwarf mimosa, is a low-growing shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, fern-like leaves that are typically less than an inch in length, giving it a delicate appearance. The plant produces fluffy, yellow or white flower clusters that bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its unique foliage and compact growth habit make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
