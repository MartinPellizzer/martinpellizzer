Malva hastata

Malva hastata, commonly known as the showy mallows, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features large, palmately lobed leaves with a hairy texture and a distinctive purple-tinged stem. The plant produces showy, pink to lavender flowers with five petals, which bloom in summer. It thrives in moist soils and is often found in wetlands, streambanks, and woodland edges.

Malva hastata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
