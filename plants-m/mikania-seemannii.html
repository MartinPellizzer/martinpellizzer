Mikania seemannii

Mikania seemannii, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rapid growth and trailing habit. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a distinctive tubular shape. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and often vine-like.

Mikania seemannii is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its use in traditional medicine in some cultures.
