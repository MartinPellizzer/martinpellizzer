Mimosa fera

Mimosa fera, commonly known as the sensitive plant, is a species native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves that fold shut when touched. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its small, clustered white flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The rapid leaf movement is a defensive mechanism against herbivores and environmental stress.
