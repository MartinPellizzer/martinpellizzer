Matricaria deflexa

Matricaria deflexa, commonly known as the common chamomile, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers with white petals. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are finely divided and aromatic, often used in herbal teas.

Matricaria deflexa is also known for its medicinal properties, particularly for its calming and anti-inflammatory effects.
