Mesua assamica

Mesua assamica, commonly known as the Assam flame of the forest, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers that bloom in dense clusters, creating a vibrant display during the spring season. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are elliptic in shape and have a smooth texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in moist, tropical climates.

Mesua assamica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine.
