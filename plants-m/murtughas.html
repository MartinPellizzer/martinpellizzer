Murtughas

Murtughas, commonly known as the Indian nettle or Urena lobata, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Murtughas is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional systems of medicine.
