Mouriri grandiflora subsp. grandiflora

Mouriri grandiflora subsp.

grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered mouriri, is a species native to the rainforests of South America. It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy, fragrant flowers.

The plant produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers that give way to small, round fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.

Botanically, it is notable for its unique floral structure and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
