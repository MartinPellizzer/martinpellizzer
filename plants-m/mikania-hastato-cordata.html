Mikania hastato-cordata

Mikania hastato-cordata, commonly known as the heart-shaped Mikania, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are usually hairy and have a rough texture. Its flowers are small and yellow, often arranged in clusters, and it produces seeds in a schizocarp that splits into two parts.

Mikania hastato-cordata is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.
