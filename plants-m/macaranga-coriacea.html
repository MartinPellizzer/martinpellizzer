Macaranga coriacea

Macaranga coriacea, commonly known as the rough-leafed macaranga, is a species of flowering plant in the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence. Macaranga coriacea is sometimes used in traditional medicine and is also a host plant for certain insect species.
