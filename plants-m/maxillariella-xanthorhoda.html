Maxillariella xanthorhoda

Maxillariella xanthorhoda, commonly known as the yellow-lipped orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow petals and a distinctive orange lip, which contribute to its striking appearance. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in the understory of tropical forests. Its flowers are relatively small but highly fragrant, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The botanical structure of Maxillariella xanthorhoda includes a sympodial growth habit and pseudobulbs, which help it store water and nutrients in its natural habitat.
