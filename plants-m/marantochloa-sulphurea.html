Marantochloa sulphurea

Marantochloa sulphurea, commonly known as the golden flame plant, is a striking member of the Marantaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its vibrant, golden-yellow foliage with intricate, veined patterns that resemble flames. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often displaying a striking contrast between the bright yellow center and the darker green edges.

Marantochloa sulphurea is prized as a decorative plant for its ornamental value and ability to thrive indoors with minimal care.
