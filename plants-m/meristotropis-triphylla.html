Meristotropis triphylla

Meristotropis triphylla, commonly known as the three-leaved meristotropis, is a unique plant belonging to the family Meristotropaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged symmetrically around a central axis. The plant exhibits a compact growth habit with small, clustered leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, often appearing in clusters at the base of the leaves.

This species is primarily found in specific microhabitats, such as rocky outcrops or shaded forest floors, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils.
