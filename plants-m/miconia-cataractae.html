Miconia cataractae

Miconia cataractae, commonly known as the "devil's ivy," is a member of the Melastomataceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

This plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in diameter. It produces showy, violet-blue flowers with five petals, which bloom in clusters. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments.

However, it is considered an invasive species in some areas due to its aggressive spreading habit.
