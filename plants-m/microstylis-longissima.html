Microstylis longissima

Microstylis longissima, commonly known as the long-styled microstylis, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family.

It is a small, perennial herb characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of sedges. Microstylis longissima is often found in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands or along the edges of water bodies.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, contributing to biodiversity and soil stabilization.
