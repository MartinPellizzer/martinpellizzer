Milium arundinaceum

Milium arundinaceum, commonly known as soft millet, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, cylindrical stems. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences, which are typically green or pale yellow in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Milium arundinaceum is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies.
