Miconia sandemanii

Miconia sandemanii, commonly known as the Sandeman's miconia, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves that are often heart-shaped. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, attracting various pollinators such as bees and birds. It is often found in moist, shaded areas of tropical forests, where it thrives in the understory.

The species is also known for its rapid growth and can become invasive in certain environments.
