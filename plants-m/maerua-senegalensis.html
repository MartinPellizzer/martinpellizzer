Maerua senegalensis

Maerua senegalensis, commonly known as the Senegalese thornbush, is a shrub or small tree native to the Sahel region of Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by round, fleshy fruits that contain several seeds. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.

Maerua senegalensis also plays a role in soil stabilization and is used in traditional medicine by local communities.
