Macrolobium brachystegioides var. sulfureum

Macrolobium brachystegioides var. sulfureum, commonly known as the sulfur yellow flame tree, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that are often a vibrant yellow or orange, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that range from yellow to orange-red, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
