Machaonia erythrocarpa subsp. parvifolia

Machaonia erythrocarpa subsp.

parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved red-fruited mahogany, is a species native to southern Africa. It is a deciduous tree that typically grows in forested areas with well-drained soils.

The plant features compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets and produces bright red, fleshy fruits that are an important food source for local wildlife. Its botanical characteristics include a smooth, dark bark and a preference for humid, subtropical climates.

The subspecies is distinguished by its smaller leaves compared to other members of the Machaonia genus.
