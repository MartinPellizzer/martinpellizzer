Malva olitoria

Malva olitoria, commonly known as edible mallows or common mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus, and is characterized by its soft, hairy stems and broad, lobed leaves. The plant produces showy, pink or purple flowers with five petals, often arranged in clusters at the ends of branches. Its leaves and young shoots are edible, with a mucilaginous texture that becomes gelatinous when soaked in water.

Malva olitoria is often cultivated for its nutritional value and used in traditional cuisines across various regions.
