Myrcia banilejoana

Myrcia banilejoana, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a shrub or small tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible red berries that are often used in traditional medicine and local cuisine. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

Myrcia banilejoana is also known for its aromatic properties and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
