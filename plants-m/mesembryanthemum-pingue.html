Mesembryanthemum pingue

Mesembryanthemum pingue, commonly known as the fat mesemb or the fat-leaf mesemb, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family, which is known for its diverse and often strikingly beautiful flora. The plant is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid environments. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in the early morning and close by midday, a trait common among many mesembs.

Its unique morphology and adaptation to harsh conditions make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
