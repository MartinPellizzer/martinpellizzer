Manihot nogueirae

Manihot nogueirae, commonly known as the Brazilian arrowroot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are rich in starch. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has lance-shaped leaves with a waxy surface. Its flowering stems bear small, yellowish-green flowers, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Due to its high nutritional value, it has been used traditionally for food and medicinal purposes.
