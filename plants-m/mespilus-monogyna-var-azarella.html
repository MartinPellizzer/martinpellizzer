Mespilus monogyna var. azarella

Mespilus monogyna var. azarella, commonly known as the Persian gooseberry, is a variant of the European gooseberry.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green fruits that ripen to a red-orange color, typically in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in temperate regions.

The variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its edible berries.
