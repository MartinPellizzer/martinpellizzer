Monoxalis robusta

Monoxalis robusta, commonly known as the giant yellow buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers with a distinctive cup-shaped structure. The plant has arrow-shaped leaves that are often hairy and can grow up to 15 cm in length. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Monoxalis robusta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
