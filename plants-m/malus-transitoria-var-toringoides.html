Malus transitoria var. toringoides

Malus transitoria var. toringoides, commonly known as the Toringo apple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental white flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, edible apples that are typically yellow or red in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value and fruit.

The leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
