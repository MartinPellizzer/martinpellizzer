Momordica calantha

Momordica calantha, commonly known as the horned melon, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiky, green fruit that turns yellow when ripe. The plant produces small, white flowers and has heart-shaped leaves with wavy edges. Its fruit contains a sweet, juicy pulp surrounded by a thick, fibrous rind.

The plant is often grown for its ornamental value and edible fruit, which is used in various culinary applications.
