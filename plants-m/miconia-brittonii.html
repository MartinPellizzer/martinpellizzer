Miconia brittonii

Miconia brittonii, commonly known as the red miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically green with a reddish underside. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Miconia brittonii is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, making it an invasive species in some regions.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and a potential threat to native ecosystems.
