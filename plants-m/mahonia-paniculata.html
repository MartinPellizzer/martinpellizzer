Mahonia paniculata

Mahonia paniculata, commonly known as the panicle mahonia, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, with leathery, dark green leaves that have a waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers in late spring, followed by blue-black berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its hardy nature and attractive appearance make it a valuable addition to native plant landscapes.
