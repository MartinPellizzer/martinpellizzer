Myrcia multiflora var. microsiphonata

Myrcia multiflora var. microsiphonata, commonly known as the small-siphonated myrcia, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white in color. These flowers are followed by small, round berries that ripen to a dark purple or black color.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
