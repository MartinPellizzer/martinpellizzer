Musa acuminata var. burmannicoides

Musa acuminata var. burmannicoides, commonly known as the Burmannicoides banana, is a wild banana species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, elongated leaves and clusters of small, greenish flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically not consumed by humans but are important for wildlife. It is closely related to the common banana and has been used in breeding programs to improve banana varieties.

This species is valued for its genetic diversity and potential contributions to banana crop resilience.
