Mitina sulphurea

Mitina sulphurea, commonly known as the sulfur yellow orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers with a sulfur-yellow hue, which give it its common name. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its delicate, pendulous blooms. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and moderate rainfall.

Its botanical features make it a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts and botanists.
