Microlicia avicularis

Microlicia avicularis, commonly known as the red-flowered orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy red flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and leathery, adapted to retain moisture in its native habitat.

Microlicia avicularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
