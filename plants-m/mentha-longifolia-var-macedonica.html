Mentha longifolia var. macedonica

Mentha longifolia var. macedonica, commonly known as Macedonian mint, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This variety is valued for its aromatic leaves, which are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
