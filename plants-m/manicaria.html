Manicaria

Manicaria, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that split open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Manicaria is often found in disturbed habitats and is considered an invasive species in some regions.
