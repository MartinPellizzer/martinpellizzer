Macrorhynchus aurantiacus

Macrorhynchus aurantiacus, commonly known as the orange bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange petals and tubular shape, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sunlight. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking color and unique form.
