Mikania hookeriana var. cardiophylla

Mikania hookeriana var. cardiophylla, commonly known as the heart-leaf Mikania, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically opposite and have a velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by fluffy, white seed heads. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is sometimes considered a problematic weed in certain areas.
