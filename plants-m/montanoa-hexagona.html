Montanoa hexagona

Montanoa hexagona, commonly known as the six-sided montanoa, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive hexagonal-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or open habitats.

Due to its unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
