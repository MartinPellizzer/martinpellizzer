Mentha requienii subsp. bistaminata

Mentha requienii subsp.

bistaminata, commonly known as the Kashmir mint, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayas. It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly fuzzy leaves and slender, creeping stems.

The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its essential oils are used in traditional medicine and aromatherapy for their calming properties.
