Maireana ciliata

Maireana ciliata, commonly known as the slender maireana, is a shrub native to Australia, particularly found in the arid regions of Western Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers, typically in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.

Maireana ciliata is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its potential use in horticulture.
