Myrcia acutiloba

Myrcia acutiloba, commonly known as the sharp-leaved myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds.

Myrcia acutiloba is often found in moist, shaded areas of tropical forests and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
