Maxillaria pallidiflava

Maxillaria pallidiflava, commonly known as the pale-flowered maxillaria, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that support broad, elliptical leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pale yellow to white in color. Its flowering period usually occurs during the warmer months, and it thrives in bright, indirect light.

Maxillaria pallidiflava is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
