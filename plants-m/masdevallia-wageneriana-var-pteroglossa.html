Masdevallia wageneriana var. pteroglossa

Masdevallia wageneriana var. pteroglossa, commonly known as the Wagenner's Masdevallia, is a striking orchid native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, pendulous inflorescence and vibrant, colorful flowers that often display a mix of red, orange, and yellow hues. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, exhibiting an epiphytic growth habit. Its leaves are long, narrow, and often have a waxy texture, adapted to the humid, shaded environments it inhabits.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is relatively rare in cultivation.
