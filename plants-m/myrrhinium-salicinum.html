Myrrhinium salicinum

Myrrhinium salicinum, commonly known as the silky sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, thriving in moist to saturated soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it its common name. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, greenish flowers that are wind-pollinated.

Myrrhinium salicinum plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and provides habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
