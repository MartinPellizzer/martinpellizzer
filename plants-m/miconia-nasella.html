Miconia nasella

Miconia nasella, commonly known as the dwarf miconia, is a small shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically orange or red in color, which are an important food source for various bird species. Miconia nasella is often found in shaded, moist environments such as rainforests and understories of dense vegetation.

Its rapid growth and ability to thrive in low-light conditions make it a common component of tropical flora.
