Myriocarpa colipensis

Myriocarpa colipensis, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a climbing vine native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its trumpet-shaped, violet to purple flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, smooth seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Myriocarpa colipensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
