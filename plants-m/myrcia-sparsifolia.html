Myrcia sparsifolia

Myrcia sparsifolia, commonly known as the sparsely-leaved myrtle, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are small, woody capsules that contain aromatic seeds.

Myrcia sparsifolia is often valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
