Miltoniopsis warszewiczii

Miltoniopsis warszewiczii, commonly known as the Warszewicz's Miltoniopsis, is a rare and delicate orchid native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, enabling it to thrive in its high-altitude habitat. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in a fine, waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Due to its rarity and specific ecological requirements, it is often cultivated in controlled environments by enthusiasts and botanists.
