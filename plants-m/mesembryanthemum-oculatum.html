Mesembryanthemum oculatum

Mesembryanthemum oculatum, commonly known as the eye-plant, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and small, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a powdery coating. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers that bloom in the morning and close by midday. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and ability to store water in its leaves make it well adapted to arid environments.
