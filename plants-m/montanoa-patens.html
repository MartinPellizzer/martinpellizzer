Montanoa patens

Montanoa patens, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces bright orange to red flowers that bloom from summer to fall. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Montanoa patens thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
