Masdevallia aphanes

Masdevallia aphanes, commonly known as the ghost masdevallia, is a terrestrial orchid native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces delicate, pendulous flowers in shades of white and pale pink, which are often obscured by the dense foliage, giving it its common name. It thrives in cool, humid environments with high air circulation and partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat requirements, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
