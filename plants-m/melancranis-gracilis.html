Melancranis gracilis

Melancranis gracilis, commonly known as the slender melancranis, is a rare flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, elongated leaves and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky crevices or shaded forest floors. Its unique morphology makes it a subject of interest for botanists studying alpine flora.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
