Miltitzia pusilla var. flagellaris

Miltitzia pusilla var. flagellaris, commonly known as the slender miltitzia, is a small, evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, adding ornamental value to the landscape. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens and rockeries.

Due to its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal, it is often used in horticultural settings for its visual interest and adaptability.
