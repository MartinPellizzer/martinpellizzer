Medea erecta

Medea erecta, commonly known as the upright medea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that can grow up to 60 cm in height, often branching towards the top. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Medea erecta thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
