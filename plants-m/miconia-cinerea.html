Miconia cinerea

Miconia cinerea, commonly known as the silver miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or pale yellow flowers, which are followed by large, round fruits containing numerous seeds. Miconia cinerea is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments.

However, it is also considered an invasive species in some regions due to its aggressive spread and ecological impact.
