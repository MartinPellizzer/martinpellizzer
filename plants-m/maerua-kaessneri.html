Maerua kaessneri

Maerua kaessneri, commonly known as the Desert Glory, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a shrub or small tree that thrives in hot, dry climates with well-drained soils. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are typically small and leathery, adapted to reduce water loss.

Maerua kaessneri is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental appeal in drought-tolerant landscaping.
