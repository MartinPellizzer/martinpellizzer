Micrococca scariosa

Micrococca scariosa, commonly known as the African velvet tamarind, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that turn from green to yellow as they mature. Micrococca scariosa is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

It is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
