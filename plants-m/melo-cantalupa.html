Melo cantalupa

Melo cantalupa, commonly known as the Persian melon, is a fruit-bearing plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes cucumbers and squash. The plant produces large, ribbed fruits with a pale green rind and a sweet, aromatic interior. Its leaves are broad and lobed, while its vines grow horizontally along the ground.

The fruit is typically harvested when fully mature, revealing a pale yellow or orange flesh with a high water content.
