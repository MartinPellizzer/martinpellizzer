Matthiola carnica

Matthiola carnica, commonly known as the Carnian stock, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other aromatic and ornamental plants. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, four-petaled flowers that are typically white or pale pink. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Matthiola carnica is also valued for its historical use in herbal medicine and as a source of essential oils.
