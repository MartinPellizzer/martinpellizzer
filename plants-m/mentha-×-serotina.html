Mentha × serotina

Mentha × serotina, commonly known as marsh mint, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Mentha aquatica and Mentha viridis.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and stream banks. The plant has square stems and opposite, serrated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, white or pale purple flowers appear in whorled clusters, and it produces a strong, minty aroma.

Mentha × serotina is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in herbal remedies.
