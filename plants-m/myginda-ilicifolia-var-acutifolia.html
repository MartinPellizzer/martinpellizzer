Myginda ilicifolia var. acutifolia

Myginda ilicifolia var. acutifolia, commonly known as the sharp-leaved myginda, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves that have a distinctive sharp tip, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in arid and semi-arid regions.
