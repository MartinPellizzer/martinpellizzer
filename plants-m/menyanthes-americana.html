Menyanthes americana

Menyanthes americana, commonly known as American bogbean, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as bogs, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. The plant features whorled leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their astringent properties.

The plant plays an important role in maintaining the ecological balance of wetland ecosystems.
