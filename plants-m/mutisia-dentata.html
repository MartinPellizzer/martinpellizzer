Mutisia dentata

Mutisia dentata, commonly known as the toothed mutisia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, giving it its common name. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Mutisia dentata is typically found in moist, tropical habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
