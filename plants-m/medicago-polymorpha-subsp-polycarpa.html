Medicago polymorpha subsp. polycarpa

Medicago polymorpha subsp.

polycarpa, commonly known as the common goat's rue, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions. It has a deep root system that helps in soil stabilization and nitrogen fixation.

Its seeds are small and often dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.
