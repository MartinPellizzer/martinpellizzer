Macadamia whelanii

Macadamia whelanii, commonly known as the silver nut, is a species native to Australia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that produces edible seeds enclosed in a hard, woody shell. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The seeds, often referred to as macadamia nuts, are rich in oil and have a high nutritional value.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and full sun.
