Miscanthidium erectum

Miscanthidium erectum, commonly known as the upright miscanthus, is a perennial grass species native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and feathery panicles that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and along waterways. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in wet conditions.
