Mimosa debilis var. aequatoriana

Mimosa debilis var. aequatoriana, commonly known as the Ecuadorian mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Ecuador and Peru.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that fold shut when touched, a response known as thigmonastic movement. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
