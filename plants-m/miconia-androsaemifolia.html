Miconia androsaemifolia

Miconia androsaemifolia, commonly known as the Andros leaf miconia, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean region.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in coastal areas or forest understories. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine, though its ecological impact as an invasive species in some regions is a concern.

The plant's ornamental value has led to its cultivation in certain horticultural settings.
