Marlierea involucrata

Marlierea involucrata, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant is typically herbaceous, with a sprawling growth habit, and often grows near water sources. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Marlierea involucrata is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils.
