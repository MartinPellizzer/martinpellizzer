Melanthera hastata subsp. lobata

Melanthera hastata subsp.

lobata, commonly known as the broad-leaved melanthera, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. It has hairy stems and leaves, which help it retain moisture in its preferred environment.

Its flowering period occurs in late summer, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
