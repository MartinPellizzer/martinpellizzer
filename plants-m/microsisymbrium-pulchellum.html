Microsisymbrium pulchellum

Microsisymbrium pulchellum, commonly known as the beautiful broomrape, is a parasitic plant belonging to the Orobanchaceae family.

It lacks chlorophyll and therefore cannot perform photosynthesis, relying entirely on its host plant for nutrients. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are often overlooked due to its inconspicuous appearance. It is typically found in association with grasses and other herbaceous plants in open habitats.

Its life cycle is closely tied to its host, making it an important study subject in plant parasitism.
