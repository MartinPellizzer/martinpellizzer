Mikania caldasana

Mikania caldasana, commonly known as the Caldasana morning glory, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rapid growth and distinctive blue to violet flowers. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that bloom in the morning. It is often found in moist, shaded areas in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in some environments.
