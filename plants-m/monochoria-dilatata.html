Monochoria dilatata

Monochoria dilatata, commonly known as the broadleaf water spinach, is a aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, arrow-shaped leaves that float on the water surface, with long, slender stems that trail beneath the water. The plant thrives in shallow, slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in marshes and wetlands. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in spike-like clusters, and it produces floating, bladder-like structures that aid in buoyancy.

Monochoria dilatata is often used in traditional medicine and as a vegetable in some Asian cuisines.
