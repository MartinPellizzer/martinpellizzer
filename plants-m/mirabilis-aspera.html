Mirabilis aspera

Mirabilis aspera, commonly known as the rough blushing morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red, often changing hue as they open during the day. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. Its leaves are typically hairy and have a rough texture, giving it its common name.

Mirabilis aspera is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in a variety of soil types.
