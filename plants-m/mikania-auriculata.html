Mikania auriculata

Mikania auriculata, commonly known as the golden vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its rapid growth and distinctive heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its aggressive growth, it can become invasive in certain areas.
