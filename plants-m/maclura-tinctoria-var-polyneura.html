Maclura tinctoria var. polyneura

Maclura tinctoria var. polyneura, commonly known as the yellow wood or American hogplum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and clusters of yellowish-green flowers that appear in the spring. The plant produces small, orange to red berries that are edible but have a strong, sour taste. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have serrated edges.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in dyeing and traditional medicine.
