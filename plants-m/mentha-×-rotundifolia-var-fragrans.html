Mentha × rotundifolia var. fragrans

Mentha × rotundifolia var. fragrans, commonly known as sweet mint, is a hybrid perennial herb native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its rounded, slightly toothed leaves and a distinctively sweet, aromatic scent. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its aromatic properties make it a popular choice for culinary and medicinal uses.
