Macropiper latifolium

Macropiper latifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaf pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants, and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, broad leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, fleshy fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and ethnobotanical contexts.
