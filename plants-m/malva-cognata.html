Malva cognata

Malva cognata, commonly known as the common mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a soft, velvety texture and produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus, and is characterized by its mucilaginous properties in its seeds and leaves. Malva cognata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, gardens, and along stream banks.

Its historical use in traditional medicine and culinary applications highlights its botanical and cultural significance.
