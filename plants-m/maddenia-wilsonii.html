Maddenia wilsonii

Maddenia wilsonii, commonly known as Wilson's maddenia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small, white or pale pink, and are clustered in cymes at the tips of the stems.

Maddenia wilsonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
