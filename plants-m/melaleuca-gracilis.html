Melaleuca gracilis

Melaleuca gracilis, commonly known as slender bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, cylindrical spikes of bright red or pink flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is often found in coastal or sandy soils. It is a member of the Myrtaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like eucalyptus and tea trees.

Melaleuca gracilis is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained conditions.
