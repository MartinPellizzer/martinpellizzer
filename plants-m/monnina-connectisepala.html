Monnina connectisepala

Monnina connectisepala, commonly known as the narrow-leaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual with slender, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves that are typically less than 5 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in the early morning, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in open woods and along stream banks.

The genus Monnina is characterized by its showy, often blue or violet flowers and is part of the family Polygalaceae.
