Mitostigma tubatum

Mitostigma tubatum, commonly known as the tubular mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces white, tubular flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant relies on its host for water and nutrients, while still performing photosynthesis. Its seeds are dispersed by birds that feed on its berries.

The species is native to parts of South America and is characterized by its unique morphology and ecological interactions.
