Meconopsis baileyi

Meconopsis baileyi, commonly known as the Himalayan blue poppy, is a striking alpine plant native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its large, showy blue or violet petals that are often speckled with darker markings. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments at high elevations, where it thrives in cool and humid conditions. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its rarity and beauty, Meconopsis baileyi is a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists alike.
