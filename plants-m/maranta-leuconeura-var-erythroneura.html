Maranta leuconeura var. erythroneura

Maranta leuconeura var. erythroneura, commonly known as the "Prayer Plant," is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful leaves that change hue in response to light exposure, a phenomenon known as nyctinasty. The leaves typically fold upward at night and unfold in the morning, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

This ornamental plant is popular for its aesthetic appeal and easy care requirements.
