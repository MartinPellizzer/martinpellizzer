Medinilla pycnantha

Medinilla pycnantha, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded inflorescences that resemble bells, which are typically pink or red in color. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as a decorative plant.

Medinilla pycnantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in floral arrangements.
