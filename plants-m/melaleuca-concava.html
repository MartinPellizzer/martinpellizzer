Melaleuca concava

Melaleuca concava, commonly known as the narrow-leaved paperbark, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia, particularly found in coastal areas and heathlands. The plant is characterized by its rough, flaky bark that peels away in strips, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a glossy upper surface and a pale underside.

The flowers are small, white or cream-colored, and are borne in dense clusters, attracting various pollinators.
