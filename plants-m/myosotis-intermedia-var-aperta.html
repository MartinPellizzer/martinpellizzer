Myosotis intermedia var. aperta

Myosotis intermedia var. aperta, commonly known as the open bluebell, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly in regions of Argentina and Chile.

It is characterized by its delicate, blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain the seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
