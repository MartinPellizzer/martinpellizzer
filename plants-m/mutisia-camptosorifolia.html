Mutisia camptosorifolia

Mutisia camptosorifolia, commonly known as the mountain mutisia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly in regions of Argentina, Chile, and Bolivia.

The plant typically grows in mountainous areas and has a woody stem with alternate, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, and it produces small, dry fruits called achenes.

Mutisia camptosorifolia is often found in shrublands and is adapted to high-altitude environments.
