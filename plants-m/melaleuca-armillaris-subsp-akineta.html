Melaleuca armillaris subsp. akineta

Melaleuca armillaris subsp.

akineta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bottlebrush, is a shrub native to eastern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and distinctive cylindrical flower spikes that range in color from pink to red.

The plant produces small, woody fruits called follicles, which contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting native wildlife.
