Mespilus lucida

Mespilus lucida, commonly known as the shining serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching structure and clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically yellow or red when mature. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a smooth texture with a slightly serrated edge.

Mespilus lucida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and fruit.
