Mentha canadensis f. pilosa

Mentha canadensis f. pilosa, commonly known as Canadian mint or hairy mint, is a flowering plant in the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that are typically oval to lance-shaped. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers arranged in whorls along the stems. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is native to eastern North America.

This variety is often used in herbal medicine and as a culinary herb due to its aromatic properties.
