Matthiola torulosa

Matthiola torulosa, commonly known as the woolly stock, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant features hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive, woolly appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Matthiola torulosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in perfumery.
