Medicago falcata var. aurea

Medicago falcata var. aurea, commonly known as the golden goat's rue, is a variant of the common goat's rue, characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers.

It is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Europe, often found in dry, rocky soils. The plant features slender, branching stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are typically small and borne in dense clusters, adding ornamental value to gardens.

It is adapted to full sun and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping in arid environments.
