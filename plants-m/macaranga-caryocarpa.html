Macaranga caryocarpa

Macaranga caryocarpa, commonly known as the red-flowered macaranga, is a tropical plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that are often surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, and it is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
