Metroxylon microcarpum

Metroxylon microcarpum, commonly known as the oil palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves and a tall, slender trunk that stores a significant amount of oil. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that develop into fruit, which contains a high-octane oil used in various industries. Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and economic contexts.

However, its cultivation has also raised concerns regarding environmental impact and sustainability.
