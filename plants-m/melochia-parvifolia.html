Melochia parvifolia

Melochia parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved melochia, is a tropical plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its palmate leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces fleshy, red berries that are edible and often consumed by birds and small mammals. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

Melochia parvifolia thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fallow fields.
