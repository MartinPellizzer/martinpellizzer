Mentha × schultziana f. malinvaldii

Mentha × schultziana f. malinvaldii, commonly known as the Malinvaldii mint, is a hybrid perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of the Middle East.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to violet flowers. This variety is often found in rocky or dry soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is used in herbal teas and traditional medicine for its cooling properties.

The plant is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
