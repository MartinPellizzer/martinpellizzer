Matthiola sinuata f. intermedia

Matthiola sinuata f. intermedia, commonly known as the Intermediate Wallflower, is a cultivar of the wallflower species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and profuse display of small, showy flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer and is well-suited for rock gardens or borders due to its drought tolerance. Its foliage is aromatic and slightly hairy, with serrated leaf margins that contribute to its ornamental appeal.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in full sun to partial shade conditions.
