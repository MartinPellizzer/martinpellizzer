Machaeranthera asteroides var. glandulosa

Machaeranthera asteroides var. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular aster, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered with glandular hairs, which give it its scientific variety name.

The glandular aster is often found in prairies and open woodlands, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
