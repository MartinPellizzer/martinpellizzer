Miconia pseudopinetorum

Miconia pseudopinetorum, commonly known as the false pineapple tree, is a member of the Melastomataceae family.

It is a large, fast-growing tree native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and showy, clustered white or pink flowers. It produces small, round, yellow fruits that are edible but not widely consumed by humans.

Due to its rapid growth and potential invasiveness, it is often considered a problematic species in certain ecosystems.
