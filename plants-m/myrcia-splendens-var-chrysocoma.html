Myrcia splendens var. chrysocoma

Myrcia splendens var. chrysocoma, commonly known as golden myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, fragrant yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are dark green and leathery, while its flowers are clustered in small umbels.

The fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.
