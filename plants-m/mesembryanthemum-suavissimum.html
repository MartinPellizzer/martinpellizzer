Mesembryanthemum suavissimum

Mesembryanthemum suavissimum, commonly known as the sweet mesem, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, often hairy leaves and vibrant, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid climates. Its flowers are typically short-lived, lasting only a day, which is a common trait among the Mesembryanthemum genus.

The plant is also known for its ability to store water in its leaves, aiding in its survival during dry periods.
