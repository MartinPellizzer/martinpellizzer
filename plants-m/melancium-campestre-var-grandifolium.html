Melancium campestre var. grandifolium

Melancium campestre var. grandifolium, commonly known as the large-leaved melancium, is a herbaceous plant characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green.

It produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is native to temperate regions of Europe and has been introduced to parts of North America.
