Moraea vallisbelli

Moraea vallisbelli, commonly known as the valley bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and landscaped areas.

Moraea vallisbelli is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
