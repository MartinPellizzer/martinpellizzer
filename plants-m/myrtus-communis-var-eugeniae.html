Myrtus communis var. eugeniae

Myrtus communis var. eugeniae, commonly known as the dwarf myrtle, is a compact evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, dark green leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, white flowers in spring. The plant is characterized by its dense growth habit and aromatic foliage, which emit a subtle, spicy scent. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and historical use in perfumery and traditional medicine.
