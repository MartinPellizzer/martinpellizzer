Microcodon glomeratus

Microcodon glomeratus, commonly known as the clustered microcodon, is a small, herbaceous plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth or along stream banks. The plant features tiny, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, with delicate, thread-like petals. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often arranged in a basal rosette.

Microcodon glomeratus is known for its unique reproductive structures and is of interest to botanists studying plant diversity in tropical ecosystems.
