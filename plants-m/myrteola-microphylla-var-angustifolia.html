Myrteola microphylla var. angustifolia

Myrteola microphylla var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved myrteola, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically less than 2 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, making it useful in traditional medicine.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has limited commercial use in the horticultural industry.
