Microlicia setifolia

Microlicia setifolia, commonly known as the silky microlicia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small shrub characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a silky appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is not widely distributed outside its native range.
