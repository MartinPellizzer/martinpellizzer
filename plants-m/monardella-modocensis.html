Monardella modocensis

Monardella modocensis, commonly known as Modoc penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as grasslands and open woodlands. Its flowers are usually pink to purple in color and attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Monardella modocensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native plant gardens.
