Malaxis purpureiflora

Malaxis purpureiflora, commonly known as the purple-flowered malaxis, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, pale green leaves. The plant produces showy, violet or purple flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its delicate appearance and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
