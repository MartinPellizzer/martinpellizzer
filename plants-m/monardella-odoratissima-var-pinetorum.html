Monardella odoratissima var. pinetorum

Monardella odoratissima var. pinetorum, commonly known as the pine mountain monardella, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and woodlands, often in association with pine trees. Its flowers are showy, tubular, and typically pink to purple in color, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
