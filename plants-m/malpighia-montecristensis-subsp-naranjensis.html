Malpighia montecristensis subsp. naranjensis

Malpighia montecristensis subsp.

naranjensis, commonly known as the orange mamey, is a species native to Central America and northern South America. It is a small, evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions.

The plant produces small, orange-red fruits that are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor. Its leaves are elliptical and dark green, with a smooth texture.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its fruit, which is used in both culinary and traditional medicinal applications.
