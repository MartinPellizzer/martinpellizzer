Magnolia fasciculata

Magnolia fasciculata, commonly known as the Japanese magnolia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate leaves that are usually green on top and hairy underneath. Its flowers are clustered in pendulous or upright racemes, adding to its ornamental value.

Magnolia fasciculata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens.
