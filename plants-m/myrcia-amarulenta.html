Myrcia amarulenta

Myrcia amarulenta, commonly known as the yellow myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant yellow flowers. It produces small, round berries that are typically orange to reddish in color.

Myrcia amarulenta is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses in indigenous communities.
