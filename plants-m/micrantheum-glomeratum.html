Micrantheum glomeratum

Micrantheum glomeratum, commonly known as the clustered micrantheum, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Apiaceae.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of white or pale yellow. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often aromatic, a common trait in the Apiaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shady areas.

Micrantheum glomeratum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
