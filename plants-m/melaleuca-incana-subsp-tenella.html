Melaleuca incana subsp. tenella

Melaleuca incana subsp.

tenella, commonly known as the slender bottlebrush, is a shrub native to eastern Australia. It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and small, pale blue to white flowers that appear in dense, brush-like clusters.

The leaves are narrow, greyish-green, and covered in fine, silky hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in horticulture for its unique foliage and flowering display.
