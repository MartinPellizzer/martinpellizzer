Maxillaria houtteana

Maxillaria houtteana, commonly known as the Houtte's orchid, is a tropical orchid native to South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are strap-like and arranged in a fan shape, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Maxillaria houtteana thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
