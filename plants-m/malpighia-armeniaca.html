Malpighia armeniaca

Malpighia armeniaca, commonly known as the Chinese hawthorn, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It produces small, bright red berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. The plant has simple, oval-shaped leaves and small white flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

The berries are rich in vitamin C and have a tart, slightly sweet flavor.
