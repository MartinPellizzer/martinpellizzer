Mussaenda wallichii

Mussaenda wallichii, commonly known as the golden flame or yellow glorybower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow bracts that resemble flowers, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are clustered in umbels. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Mussaenda wallichii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
