Malinvaudia

Malinvaudia, commonly known as the "butterfly flower," is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its showy, butterfly-like flowers that come in shades of pink, red, and white. The plants typically have palmate leaves and produce capsule fruits containing numerous small seeds. Malinvaudia species are often cultivated for their ornamental value in gardens due to their vibrant blooms and adaptability to various soil conditions.

They thrive in full sun and well-drained soil, making them popular choices for coastal and Mediterranean climates.
