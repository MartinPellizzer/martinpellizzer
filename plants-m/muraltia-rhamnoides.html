Muraltia rhamnoides

Muraltia rhamnoides, commonly known as the Indian date tree or wild date tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Rhamnaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by edible, fleshy fruits resembling dates in appearance and taste.

Muraltia rhamnoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the fruit, which can be used in culinary applications.
