Mitracarpus scabrellus

Mitracarpus scabrellus, commonly known as the roughleaf mitracarpus, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and often trifoliate leaves. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are hairy and rough to the touch, giving it its common name.

Mitracarpus scabrellus is often found in rocky soils and dry habitats, making it a resilient species adapted to arid environments.
