Morinda elliptifolia

Morinda elliptifolia, commonly known as the Indian madder or red sanders, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces bright red or orange berries that contain several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.

Morinda elliptifolia thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.
