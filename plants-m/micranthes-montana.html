Micranthes montana

Micranthes montana, commonly known as mountain micranthes, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in forested or rocky habitats. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are often white or pale purple, blooming in early to mid-summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy texture.

Micranthes montana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
