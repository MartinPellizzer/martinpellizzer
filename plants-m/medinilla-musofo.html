Medinilla musofo

Medinilla musofo, commonly known as the velvet tangerine or velvet apple, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and clusters of fragrant, waxy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisine and traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Medinilla musofo is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as birds and insects.
