Myrcia uberavensis var. ovata

Myrcia uberavensis var. ovata, commonly known as the ovate myrtle, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white to pinkish flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruits are small, woody capsules that contain numerous seeds, contributing to its ecological role in seed dispersal.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
