Myoxanthus sarcodactylae

Myoxanthus sarcodactylae, commonly known as the devil's claw, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, claw-like seed pods that contain numerous tiny seeds. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has a woody stem with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring.

The species is known for its hardy nature and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
