Myrcia lanceolata var. grandifolia

Myrcia lanceolata var. grandifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a species native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows up to 6 meters in height. The plant features elliptical, leathery leaves with a glossy dark green surface and a distinctive aromatic scent. Its flowers are small, clustered, and fragrant, producing red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for local wildlife.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been introduced to other regions, sometimes becoming invasive.
