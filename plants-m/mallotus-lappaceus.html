Mallotus lappaceus

Mallotus lappaceus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows in lowland rainforests. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptic to lanceolate in shape, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, and it produces woody capsules as fruit.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
