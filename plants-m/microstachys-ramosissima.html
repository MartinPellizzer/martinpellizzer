Microstachys ramosissima

Microstachys ramosissima, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, aromatic wood and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, with a smooth texture.

Microstachys ramosissima is valued for its timber and has been used in traditional medicine and as a source of incense.
