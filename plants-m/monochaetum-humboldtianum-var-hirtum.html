Monochaetum humboldtianum var. hirtum

Monochaetum humboldtianum var. hirtum, commonly known as the hairy mountain grass, is a perennial grass species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often bear a fine, hairy coating. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brownish flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous areas with well-drained soils.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
