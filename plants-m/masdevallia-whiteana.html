Masdevallia whiteana

Masdevallia whiteana, commonly known as the White's masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its intricate, often fragrant flowers that display a range of colors, including white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree bark without deriving nutrients from its host. Its leaves are usually elongated and arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps in capturing moisture from the air.

Masdevallia whiteana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
