Maxillaria strumata

Maxillaria strumata, commonly known as the fragrant orchid, is a species native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit. The plant features showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, with a unique, strap-like lip. Its leaves are usually dark green and arranged in a compact rosette.

Maxillaria strumata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and distinctive floral scent.
