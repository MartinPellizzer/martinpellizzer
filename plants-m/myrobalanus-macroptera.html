Myrobalanus macroptera

Myrobalanus macroptera, commonly known as the large-fruited chequersherry, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along the shoreline. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by orange to red berries that are an important food source for local wildlife. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a serrated margin, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in coastal ecosystems.
