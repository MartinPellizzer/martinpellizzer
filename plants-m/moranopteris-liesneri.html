Moranopteris liesneri

Moranopteris liesneri, commonly known as the Liesner's bracken fern, is a member of the fern family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its large, fertile fronds that produce spores on the underside, typical of ferns. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its rhizome is thick and creeping, allowing it to spread and form dense colonies.

Moranopteris liesneri plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various small organisms.
