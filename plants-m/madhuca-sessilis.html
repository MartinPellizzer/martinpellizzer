Madhuca sessilis

Madhuca sessilis, commonly known as the wax apple or kathal, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green on top and lighter underneath. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, round fruits with a yellowish skin and a sweet, juicy pulp. The fruit is highly valued for its flavor and is often consumed fresh or used in culinary preparations.

It also has cultural significance in various regions and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
