Miscanthus chrysander

Miscanthus chrysander, commonly known as the golden miscanthus, is a perennial grass native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, arching stems and feathery, golden-hued plumes that appear in late summer to autumn. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 3 meters and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly bluish-green coloration.

The botanical features of Miscanthus chrysander make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological landscaping.
