Macroditassa macrophylla

Macroditassa macrophylla, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter, providing a unique habitat for various aquatic life forms. The plant's flowers are typically white or pink, with a distinctive shape that resembles a bell, and they bloom at night. Its roots are submerged, anchoring it in the mud of slow-moving or still water bodies.

Macroditassa macrophylla plays an important ecological role in its environment by contributing to water filtration and offering shelter to aquatic organisms.
