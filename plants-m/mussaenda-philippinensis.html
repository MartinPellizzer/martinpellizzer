Mussaenda philippinensis

Mussaenda philippinensis, commonly known as the Philippine flame flower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its showy, clustered inflorescences. The flowers are typically bright yellow or orange, with a distinctive red or orange bract that resembles a flower. The leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a velvety texture.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
