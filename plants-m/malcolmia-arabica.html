Malcolmia arabica

Malcolmia arabica, commonly known as the white sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pale blue or white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Malcolmia arabica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
