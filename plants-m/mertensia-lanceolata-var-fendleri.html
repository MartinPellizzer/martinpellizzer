Mertensia lanceolata var. fendleri

Mertensia lanceolata var. fendleri, commonly known as the Fendler's bluebells, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, often found in rocky or sandy habitats. Its leaves are typically evergreen, providing year-round interest, while its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in native landscapes.
