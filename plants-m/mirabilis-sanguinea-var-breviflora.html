Mirabilis sanguinea var. breviflora

Mirabilis sanguinea var. breviflora, commonly known as the dwarf blood flower, is a compact, herbaceous annual plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its unique appearance and short blooming period make it a desirable addition to ornamental gardens.
