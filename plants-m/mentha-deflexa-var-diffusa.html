Mentha deflexa var. diffusa

Mentha deflexa var. diffusa, commonly known as creeping mint, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its low-growing, spreading habit and slender, hairy stems. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and have a strong minty aroma.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
