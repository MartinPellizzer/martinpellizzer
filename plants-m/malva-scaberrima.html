Malva scaberrima

Malva scaberrima, commonly known as the rough hollyhock, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a member of the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant is characterized by its hairy, rough stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. Its showy flowers, which range from pink to red, bloom in summer and attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as a ornamental plant due to its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal.
