Mitranthes eggersii

Mitranthes eggersii, commonly known as the Eggers' bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet-colored flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils, often in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Mitranthes eggersii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
