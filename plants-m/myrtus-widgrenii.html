Myrtus widgrenii

Myrtus widgrenii, commonly known as the Narrowleaf myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
