Myrtus casearioides

Myrtus casearioides, commonly known as the Christmas berry or white myrtle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red berries that are edible. Its leaves are lance-shaped, leathery, and have a strong aromatic scent.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
