Magnolia meleagrioides

Magnolia meleagrioides, commonly known as the spotted magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a smooth margin. The tree produces woody, cone-like fruits that contain seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
