Monnina tristaniana

Monnina tristaniana, commonly known as the Tristan mint or Tristan's bluemink, is a flowering plant native to the subantarctic island of Tristan da Cunha.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that thrives in rocky, coastal environments. The plant produces small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence.

Monnina tristaniana is adapted to harsh climatic conditions, including strong winds and cool temperatures.
