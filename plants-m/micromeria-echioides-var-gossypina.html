Micromeria echioides var. gossypina

Micromeria echioides var. gossypina, commonly known as the desert sand verbena, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is adapted to arid environments with its deep root system. It is often found in sandy or rocky soils along coastal dunes and inland deserts.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-suited for xeriscaping and ecological restoration projects.
