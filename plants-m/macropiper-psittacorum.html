Macropiper psittacorum

Macropiper psittacorum, commonly known as the golden gooseberry, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its small, yellowish-green berries that are often consumed by birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a mild, peppery aroma.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in coastal areas.
