Myristica lepidota

Myristica lepidota, commonly known as the hairy nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces an aromatic fruit that contains a valuable seed known as nutmeg, which is used in culinary and medicinal applications. Myristica lepidota is also noted for its ornamental value due to its fragrant foliage and unique fruit structure.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its economic and ecological significance.
