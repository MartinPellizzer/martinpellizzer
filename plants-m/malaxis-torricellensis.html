Malaxis torricellensis

Malaxis torricellensis, commonly known as the Torricelli's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom during the cooler months. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories where it can receive sufficient light for photosynthesis. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the soil, while its leaves are narrow and strap-like.

Malaxis torricellensis is relatively rare and is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations and limited distribution.
