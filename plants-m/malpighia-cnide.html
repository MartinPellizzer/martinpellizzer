Malpighia cnide

Malpighia cnide, commonly known as the coral tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and bees. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are often glossy and dark green. Its fruit is a small, bright red berry that contains several seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
