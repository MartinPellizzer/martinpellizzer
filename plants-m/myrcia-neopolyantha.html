Myrcia neopolyantha

Myrcia neopolyantha, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, narrow leaves and produces small, pinkish-white flowers. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Myrcia neopolyantha is also valued for its potential medicinal properties.
