Machilus thunbergii

Machilus thunbergii, commonly known as the Japanese boxwood, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant features small, oval-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green, often with a slight waxy coating. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically appearing in clusters, while the fruit is a dark blue drupe.

Machilus thunbergii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to form neat, formal shapes.
