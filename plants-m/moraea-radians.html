Moraea radians

Moraea radians, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a striking perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features tall, spiky flower stalks that bear bright orange-red blooms, which resemble poker chips, hence its common name. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Moraea radians is known for its long blooming period, typically from late spring to early summer.
