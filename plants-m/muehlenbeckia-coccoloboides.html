Muehlenbeckia coccoloboides

Muehlenbeckia coccoloboides, commonly known as the silverleaf gooseberry, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray, fuzzy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow-orange berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful. It thrives in coastal areas and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Muehlenbeckia coccoloboides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate salt spray and wind.
