Morella cerifera

Morella cerifera, commonly known as蜡烛果 or candleberry, is a deciduous shrub native to coastal areas of North America.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish-green leaves that turn red in the fall. The plant produces small, waxy berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in wet, sandy soils and is often found in estuaries and salt marshes.

Morella cerifera is also valued for its ability to stabilize shorelines and improve soil quality.
