Mammillaria pfeifferi var. flaviceps

Mammillaria pfeifferi var. flaviceps, commonly known as the yellow-capped hedgehog cactus, is a compact, spiny cactus native to Mexico.

It features a globular shape with short, cylindrical stems covered in white to pale green spines. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its varietal name, *flaviceps*, refers to the distinctive yellow coloration of its flowers.

It is well-suited for rock gardens and requires well-drained soil and minimal water.
