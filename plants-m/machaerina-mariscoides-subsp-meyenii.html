Machaerina mariscoides subsp. meyenii

Machaerina mariscoides subsp.

meyenii, commonly known as the New Zealand flax, is a member of the Asparagaceae family. It is a perennial shrub characterized by its fibrous, strap-like leaves that are often used for weaving and basket-making.

The plant is native to New Zealand and thrives in coastal and wetland environments. It produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are clustered in spikes.

The subspecies meyenii is distinguished by its robust growth and thick, fibrous stems.
