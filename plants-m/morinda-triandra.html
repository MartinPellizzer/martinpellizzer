Morinda triandra

Morinda triandra, commonly known as the African angelica, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, featuring smooth, often reddish bark. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in summer, followed by orange-red berries. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a slightly rough texture.

Morinda triandra is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
