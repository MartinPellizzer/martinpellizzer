Melocactus tenuissimus

Melocactus tenuissimus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved melon cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a white, powdery coating called farina. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a delicate touch to its otherwise spiky appearance. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to survive in dry conditions.

Melocactus tenuissimus is a slow-growing cactus that is often cultivated for its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
