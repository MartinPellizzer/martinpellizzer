Myrcia pachyclada var. spathulata

Myrcia pachyclada var. spathulata, commonly known as the broad-leaved myrcia, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in humid, shaded environments within the Atlantic Forest biome. The plant features elliptical to ovate leaves that are glossy and dark green, often with a slightly waxy texture. Its flowers are small, clustered, and usually pink or red, while the fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and floral display.
