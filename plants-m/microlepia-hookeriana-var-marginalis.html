Microlepia hookeriana var. marginalis

Microlepia hookeriana var. marginalis, commonly known as the marginal microlepia, is a fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its leaves are bipinnate, with finely divided segments that give it a feathery appearance.

The spores are borne on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy in the wild.
