Microepidendrum subulatifolium

Microepidendrum subulatifolium, commonly known as the slender-leaved epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, elongated leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale pink in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for both botanists and orchid enthusiasts.
