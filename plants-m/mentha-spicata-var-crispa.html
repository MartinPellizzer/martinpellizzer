Mentha spicata var. crispa

Mentha spicata var. crispa, commonly known as hedge mint, is a variant of the spearmint plant characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and serrated leaves.

It produces small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in whorls along the stems, typically in late summer. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its aromatic leaves contain high levels of menthol, making it useful in culinary and medicinal applications.

It is often grown as a decorative plant in gardens due to its attractive foliage and pleasant scent.
