Myrciaria glazioviana

Myrciaria glazioviana, commonly known as the Glaziovian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red when mature. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Myrciaria glazioviana is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
