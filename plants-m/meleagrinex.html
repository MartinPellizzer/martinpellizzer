Meleagrinex

Meleagrinex, commonly known as the sacred flame plant, is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant red and orange tubular flowers that resemble flames, which bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Native to tropical regions of South America, it thrives in warm, humid environments.

Due to its unique appearance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in botanical gardens and private collections.
