Myrcia isaiana

Myrcia isaiana, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of eastern Brazil, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are small, woody capsules that contain aromatic seeds.

Myrcia isaiana is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
