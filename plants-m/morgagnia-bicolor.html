Morgagnia bicolor

Morgagnia bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive bi-colored leaves, which typically display a mix of green and white or pale green hues. The plant produces showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Morgagnia bicolor is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in suitable climates.
