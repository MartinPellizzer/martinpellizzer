Malcolmia auranitica

Malcolmia auranitica, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while the stems are erect and branching.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
