Machilus thunbergii var. obovatus

Machilus thunbergii var. obovatus, commonly known as the broadleaf chinquapin, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its elliptical, obovate leaves that are glossy and dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by oval, woody fruits that contain one or two seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded forest environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native habitats.
