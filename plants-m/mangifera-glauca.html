Mangifera glauca

Mangifera glauca, commonly known as the white mango, is a tropical fruit tree native to Southeast Asia.

It produces small, pale yellow to white fruits with a thin, fibrous skin. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are elliptical and have a smooth, waxy surface. The flowers are small and fragrant, typically appearing in clusters.

It is valued for its unique flavor and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
