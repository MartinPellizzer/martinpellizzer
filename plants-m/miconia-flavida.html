Miconia flavida

Miconia flavida, commonly known as the golden miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are usually bright green with a glossy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit is a fleshy, orange-red berry that contains numerous small seeds.

Miconia flavida is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as rainforests and is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets.
