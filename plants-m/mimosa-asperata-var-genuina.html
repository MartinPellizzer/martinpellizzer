Mimosa asperata var. genuina

Mimosa asperata var. genuina, commonly known as the rough mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and yellow, spherical flower heads. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
