Millettia penicillata

Millettia penicillata, commonly known as the sacred lotus or sacred water lily, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its striking white or pink flowers that bloom above the water. The plant has floating leaves with a waxy surface and produces distinctive, spiral-shaped fruits. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

Its cultural and religious significance in various Asian traditions further highlights its importance beyond its botanical features.
