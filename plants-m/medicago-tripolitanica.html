Medicago tripolitanica

Medicago tripolitanica, commonly known as the Tripoli clover, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features trifoliate leaves with a hairy surface and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, exhibiting drought tolerance through deep root systems. It is often found in rocky soils and is used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing soil and providing forage for livestock.
