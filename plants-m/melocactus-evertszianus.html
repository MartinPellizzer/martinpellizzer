Melocactus evertszianus

Melocactus evertszianus, commonly known as the Evertszianus melon cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of northern Argentina.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem that is often covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair. The plant produces striking, elongated flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in full sun and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is a sought-after specimen among cactus enthusiasts.
