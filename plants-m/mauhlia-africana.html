Mauhlia africana

Mauhlia africana, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, legume-like pods that contain seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

The tree is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
