Morus deqinensis

Morus deqinensis, commonly known as the Deqin mulberry, is a species native to China, specifically found in the Yunnan Province.

It is a deciduous tree that belongs to the Moraceae family, which includes other well-known plants like figs and breadfruit. The tree is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically consumed by local wildlife.

Due to its limited distribution and unique ecological role, it is considered a valuable species for botanical research and conservation efforts.
