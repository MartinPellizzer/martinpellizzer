Moraea dracomontana

Moraea dracomontana, commonly known as the dragon's mountain glory, is a striking ornamental plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The blooms, which can be pink, purple, or white, appear in late winter to early spring, adding vibrant color to gardens. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its unique appearance and seasonal blooming make it a sought-after addition to botanical collections.
