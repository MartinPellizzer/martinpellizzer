Medicago pennata

Medicago pennata, commonly known as the slender medick, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its small, trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. It has a fibrous root system and can fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it a valuable plant for soil enrichment.

Medicago pennata is also used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
