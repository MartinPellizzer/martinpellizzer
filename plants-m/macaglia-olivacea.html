Macaglia olivacea

Macaglia olivacea, commonly known as the olive-leaved macaglia, is a species of flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub characterized by its small, olive-green leaves that are typically oval in shape. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
