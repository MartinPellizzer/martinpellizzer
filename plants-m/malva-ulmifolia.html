Malva ulmifolia

Malva ulmifolia, commonly known as the elm-leaved mallow, is a flowering plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in temperate regions, often found in moist soils along stream banks or in woodland areas. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture, and its showy pink or purple flowers bloom in summer. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dry seeds.

Malva ulmifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its soothing properties.
