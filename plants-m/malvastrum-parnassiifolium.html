Malvastrum parnassiifolium

Malvastrum parnassiifolium, commonly known as the Parnassian mallows, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a distinctive red or purple throat. The plant has palmately divided leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
