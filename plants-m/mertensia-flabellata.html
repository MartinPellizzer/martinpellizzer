Mertensia flabellata

Mertensia flabellata, commonly known as the bellflower mertensia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, woodland habitats. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, bell-shaped blue to purple flowers. It has a fibrous root system and is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments.

Mertensia flabellata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
