Medicago sativa var. turcomanica

Medicago sativa var. turcomanica, commonly known as Turcoman clover, is a perennial legume native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. This variety is well adapted to arid and semi-arid climates, making it a valuable forage crop in dry regions. It has deep roots that help prevent soil erosion and improve soil fertility through nitrogen fixation.

Its hardiness and resilience make it suitable for agricultural and ecological restoration purposes.
