Maxillaria sanderiana

Maxillaria sanderiana, commonly known as the Sander's orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers with white and yellow markings. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, exhibiting an epiphytic lifestyle. Its leaves are dark green, leathery, and arranged in a rosette pattern.

Maxillaria sanderiana is prized for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
