Malva latisecta

Malva latisecta, commonly known as broad-leaved mallow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features large, palmately lobed leaves that are typically covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Malva latisecta is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal remedies.
