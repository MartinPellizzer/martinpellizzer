Masdevallia harlequina

Masdevallia harlequina, commonly known as the Harlequin Masdevallia, is a tropical orchid native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that display a mix of red, orange, and yellow hues, resembling a harlequin's costume. The plant typically grows as a small, epiphytic orchid, attaching itself to trees in its native habitat. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern.

The species is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
