Mediocactus tricae

Mediocactus tricae, commonly known as the three-armed cactus, is a unique species native to the deserts of South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that typically branch into three distinct arms, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are pollinated by various insects. Mediocactus tricae is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
