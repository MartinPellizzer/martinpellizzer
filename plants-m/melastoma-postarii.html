Melastoma postarii

Melastoma postarii, commonly known as the mountain melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its showy, often pink or red flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are usually opposite and have a rough texture, while its fruits are capsules that release seeds upon maturity.

Melastoma postarii plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various small organisms.
