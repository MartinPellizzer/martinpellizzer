Medusantha crinita

Medusantha crinita, commonly known as the hairy ghost orchid, is a rare and ethereal orchid species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, hair-like roots and striking, translucent flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient absorption. Its unique morphology and elusive habitat make it a subject of fascination for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat destruction, Medusantha crinita is considered critically endangered, highlighting the need for its protection.
