Mentha spicata var. piperella

Mentha spicata var. piperella, commonly known as pepperwort or winter savory, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are lance-shaped and have a strong, pungent aroma due to the presence of essential oils. This variety is often used in cooking for its spicy flavor and is also valued in traditional herbal medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for various garden environments.
