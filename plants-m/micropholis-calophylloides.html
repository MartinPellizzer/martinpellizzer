Micropholis calophylloides

Micropholis calophylloides, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that appear in clusters, giving it a vibrant and eye-catching appearance. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a glossy surface. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture making.
