Michelia arfakiana

Michelia arfakiana, commonly known as the Arfak mountain magnolia, is a flowering plant native to the lowland forests of New Guinea.

It is characterized by its large, showy white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. It typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters.

The bark is smooth and greyish-brown, while the flowers emit a subtle fragrance, attracting pollinators like bees and moths.
