Mentha nigrescens

Mentha nigrescens, commonly known as black mint, is a perennial herb native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black foliage and aromatic, slightly minty scent. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small, tubular, and usually pink or purple in color.

Mentha nigrescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal remedies.
