Matthiola uruguensis

Matthiola uruguensis, commonly known as the Uruguayan stock, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, four-petaled flowers that are typically white or pale purple. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a mild, sweet scent.

Matthiola uruguensis prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for garden cultivation.
