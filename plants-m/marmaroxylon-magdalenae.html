Marmaroxylon magdalenae

Marmaroxylon magdalenae, commonly known as the Magdalena sandalwood, is a rare and ancient plant species native to the Magdalena River Basin in Colombia.

It is characterized by its unique, aromatic wood, which is highly valued for its dense texture and distinctive golden color. The plant thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks. Due to its limited distribution and habitat destruction, it is considered critically endangered.

Conservation efforts are ongoing to protect this rare botanical species from further decline.
