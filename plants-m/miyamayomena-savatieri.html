Miyamayomena savatieri

Miyamayomena savatieri, commonly known as the Japanese moonflower, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its large, white, night-blooming flowers that emit a sweet fragrance. The plant belongs to the Nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its showy, trumpet-shaped blooms. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

The flowers open at night and close during the day, making it a fascinating subject for nocturnal observation.
