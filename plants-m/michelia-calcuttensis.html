Michelia calcuttensis

Michelia calcuttensis, commonly known as the Calcutta michelia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas at elevations between 1,000 and 2,500 meters.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
