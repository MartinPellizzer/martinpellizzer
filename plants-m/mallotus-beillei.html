Mallotus beillei

Mallotus beillei, commonly known as the African snowball tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical clusters of white flowers that bloom in winter, giving it its common name. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, woody fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and riverine habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some African cultures.
