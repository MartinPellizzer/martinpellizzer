Mammillaria caput-medusae var. crassior

Mammillaria caput-medusae var. crassior, commonly known as the Medusa Cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems and prominent, white to pale yellow spines. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its growth habit is typically solitary or in small clusters, and it thrives in well-draining soil with minimal water.

This variety is prized for its unique appearance and is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
