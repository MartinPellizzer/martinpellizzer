Maxillaria quercicola

Maxillaria quercicola, commonly known as the oakleaf orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, oak-like leaves that are deeply lobed and have a leathery texture. The plant produces fragrant, orchid-like flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often in clusters. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Maxillaria quercicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
