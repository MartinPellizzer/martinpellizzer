Monardella nana subsp. tenuiflora

Monardella nana subsp.

tenuiflora, commonly known as narrowleaf wild bergamot, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States. It typically features aromatic, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often gray-green in color.

The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of pink to lavender, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as open woodlands and meadows.

Its foliage and flowers are often used in traditional herbal remedies and as a source of nectar for pollinators.
