Malache scabra

Malache scabra, commonly known as the rough malache, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured stems and leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Malache scabra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
