Myrcia umbellulifera

Myrcia umbellulifera, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are edible but often have a spicy, peppery taste. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in coastal regions and tropical rainforests.

Myrcia umbellulifera is also known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
