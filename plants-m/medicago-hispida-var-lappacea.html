Medicago hispida var. lappacea

Medicago hispida var. lappacea, commonly known as goat's horn vetch, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a legume, belonging to the Fabaceae family, and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and spiny stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, followed by small, hairy pods containing seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along riverbanks.

Its adaptability makes it a common weed in agricultural areas.
