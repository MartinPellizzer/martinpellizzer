Musa itinerans var. annamica

Musa itinerans var. annamica, commonly known as the Annamite banana, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Musaceae family and is characterized by its large, broad leaves and clustered, elongated fruit. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions. Its fruit, while edible, is less commonly cultivated compared to other banana varieties.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
