Malus domestica subsp. pumila

Malus domestica subsp.

pumila, commonly known as the dwarf apple, is a compact variant of the domestic apple tree. It is characterized by its small size, typically reaching only a few feet in height, making it suitable for small gardens or container growing.

The plant produces small, sweet apples that are often used for cooking or as ornamental features. Its foliage is dense and provides good shade, while its flowers contribute to its aesthetic appeal.

This subspecies is valued for its hardiness and adaptability to various climates.
