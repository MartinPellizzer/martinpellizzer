Mikania paceae

Mikania paceae, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a vigorous vine native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and runners. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and forest edges.

Due to its aggressive growth, it can become invasive in certain regions.
