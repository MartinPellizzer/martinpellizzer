Microchilus mosaicus

Microchilus mosaicus, commonly known as the mosaic fern, is a delicate, herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features intricate, lace-like fronds that are divided into small, overlapping segments, creating a mosaic appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests or along stream banks. Its leaves are usually dark green with a velvety texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often overlooked.

Microchilus mosaicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
