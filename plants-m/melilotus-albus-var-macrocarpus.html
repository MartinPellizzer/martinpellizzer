Melilotus albus var. macrocarpus

Melilotus albus var. macrocarpus, commonly known as large-seeded sweet clover, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and trifoliate leaves with a heart-shaped base. The plant produces small, yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its seeds are large and have a distinctive, sweet scent, which contributes to its use in various traditional and commercial applications.

It is often cultivated for forage, erosion control, and as a source of essential oils.
