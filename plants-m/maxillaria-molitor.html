Maxillaria molitor

Maxillaria molitor, commonly known as the Molitor orchid, is a species native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowering structures. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish-white with intricate markings, and they bloom during the rainy season.

Maxillaria molitor is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
