Mimosa glutinosa

Mimosa glutinosa, commonly known as sticky mimosa, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, sticky leaves that exude a milky sap when damaged. The plant produces small, yellow, spherical flower heads that bloom in spring. Its leaves are bipinnately compound, with numerous tiny leaflets arranged in a feathery pattern.

Mimosa glutinosa is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.
