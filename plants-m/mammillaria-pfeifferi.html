Mammillaria pfeifferi

Mammillaria pfeifferi, commonly known as the Pfeiffer's hedgehog cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, globular shape and dense covering of white spines. The plant typically grows to a height of about 5 to 10 centimeters and has a gray-green stem. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or orange, appearing in late spring or early summer.

It is a popular choice for rock gardens due to its compact size and ornamental appearance.
