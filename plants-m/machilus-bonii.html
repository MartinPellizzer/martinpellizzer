Machilus bonii

Machilus bonii, commonly known as the Guangdong lauraceous tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to China, particularly found in the Guangdong and Guangxi provinces. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are glossy and elliptical in shape. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by dark blue-black berries.

Machilus bonii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
