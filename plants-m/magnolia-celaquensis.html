Magnolia celaquensis

Magnolia celaquensis, commonly known as the dwarf magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the tropical regions of Central America and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its ornamental, fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate to elliptic leaves that are glossy and dark green. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, often peeling in thin layers.

Magnolia celaquensis is valued in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a street tree.
