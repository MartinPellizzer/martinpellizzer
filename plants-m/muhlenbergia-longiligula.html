Muhlenbergia longiligula

Muhlenbergia longiligula, commonly known as the slender muhlenbergia, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies, sandhills, and woodland edges. The plant features slender, tufted stems with fine, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of small, spike-like flowers that bloom in late summer.

Muhlenbergia longiligula is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local ecosystems.
