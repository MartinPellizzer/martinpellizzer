Moschoxylum emarginatum

Moschoxylum emarginatum, commonly known as the emarginate mossy grass, is a perennial grass species native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing minute, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices.

Botanically, it is notable for its ability to form dense mats, contributing to soil stabilization and habitat diversity in its native ecosystems.
