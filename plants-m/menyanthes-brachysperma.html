Menyanthes brachysperma

Menyanthes brachysperma, commonly known as the narrow-fruited wintergreen, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its small, dark green, oval-shaped leaves and produces clusters of tiny, white, fragrant flowers in early spring. The plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands, bogs, and along the edges of lakes and streams. Its most distinctive feature is its unique, boat-shaped fruit that contains multiple seeds.

Menyanthes brachysperma is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
