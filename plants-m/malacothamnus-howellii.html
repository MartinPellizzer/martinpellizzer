Malacothamnus howellii

Malacothamnus howellii, commonly known as Howell's thimbleweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, such as slopes and open woodlands. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in dense, spherical clusters, which give it its common name. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Malacothamnus howellii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in harsh, arid environments.
