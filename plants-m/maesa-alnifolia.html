Maesa alnifolia

Maesa alnifolia, commonly known as the Indian alder, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical in shape and have a glossy surface. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, and it produces small, berry-like fruits.

Maesa alnifolia is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
