Marsilea owambo

Marsilea owambo, commonly known as the Owambo water clover, is a small aquatic fern native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in shallow, still or slow-moving freshwater habitats such as ponds and wetlands. The plant has four small, heart-shaped leaves that are often submerged, with a distinct central vein. It produces tiny, yellowish flowers that are borne on short stalks above the water surface.

Marsilea owambo plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms and contributing to water filtration.
