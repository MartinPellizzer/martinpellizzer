Myrcia pubipetala

Myrcia pubipetala, commonly known as the hairy myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and glossy, dark green leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, giving it the common name "hairy myrtle.".

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
