Macroplacis cordata

Macroplacis cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf ivy, is a climbing plant native to South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green, with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often white or pale green, followed by spherical, berry-like fruits. It thrives in humid environments and is often used in hanging baskets or as a ground cover.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
