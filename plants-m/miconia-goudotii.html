Miconia goudotii

Miconia goudotii, commonly known as the velvetleaf miconia, is a member of the Melastomataceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs. It produces showy, pinkish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences.

Miconia goudotii is often found in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets.
