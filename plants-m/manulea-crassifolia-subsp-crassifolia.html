Manulea crassifolia subsp. crassifolia

Manulea crassifolia subsp.

crassifolia, commonly known as the thickleaf manulea, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and has a low, mat-like growth habit. Its flowers are small and usually pale green or yellow, appearing in clusters at the base of the leaves.

It is well adapted to withstand high temperatures and limited rainfall.
