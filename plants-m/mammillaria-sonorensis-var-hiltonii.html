Mammillaria sonorensis var. hiltonii

Mammillaria sonorensis var. hiltonii, commonly known as the Hilton's mammillaria, is a compact cactus species native to the Sonoran Desert.

It features a globular to cylindrical stem with numerous radial spines and a dense covering of white, woolly hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by small, red fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in rock gardens and as a houseplant.
