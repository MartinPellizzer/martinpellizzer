Melastoma sessiliflorum

Melastoma sessiliflorum, commonly known as the purple-flowered melastoma, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, clustered inflorescences and showy, violet or purple flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and mountainous regions. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its persistent floral display.
