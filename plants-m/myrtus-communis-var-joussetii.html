Myrtus communis var. joussetii

Myrtus communis var. joussetii, commonly known as Jousset's myrtle, is a compact evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, dark green, glossy leaves that are aromatic and have a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces tiny, fragrant white flowers in early spring, which are followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Its ornamental value and aromatic properties make it a popular choice for both ornamental and culinary use.
