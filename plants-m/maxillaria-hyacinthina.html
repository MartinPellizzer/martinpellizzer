Maxillaria hyacinthina

Maxillaria hyacinthina, commonly known as the hyacinth orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, hyacinth-like flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. Its pseudobulbs are cylindrical and serve as storage organs for water and nutrients.

Maxillaria hyacinthina is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
