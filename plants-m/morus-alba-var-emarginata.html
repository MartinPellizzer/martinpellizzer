Morus alba var. emarginata

Morus alba var. emarginata, commonly known as the emarginated mulberry, is a variant of the white mulberry native to China.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaf margins, which are notched or emarginated, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically white or pale pink in color. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of silk production.

This variety is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
