Melilotus graecus

Melilotus graecus, commonly known as Greek sweet clover, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant grows in a variety of habitats, including meadows, grasslands, and disturbed areas. It has a taproot system and produces small, spherical pods containing several seeds.

Melilotus graecus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
