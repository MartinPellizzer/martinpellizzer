Myrteola microphylla

Myrteola microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the rainforests of eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are small and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by aromatic, oval-shaped berries. Myrteola microphylla is often found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal uses.

Its foliage has a distinct aromatic scent, reminiscent of eucalyptus, which contributes to its ecological and horticultural significance.
