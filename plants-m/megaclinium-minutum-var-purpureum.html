Megaclinium minutum var. purpureum

Megaclinium minutum var. purpureum, commonly known as the purple dwarf sedge, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves that are typically a vibrant green with a purple tinge on the underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters during the summer months, which are pollinated by wind rather than insects. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other damp habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in native plant gardens.
