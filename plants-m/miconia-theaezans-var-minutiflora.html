Miconia theaezans var. minutiflora

Miconia theaezans var. minutiflora, commonly known as the small-flowered miconia, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that are typically followed by bright red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and tropical landscapes.
