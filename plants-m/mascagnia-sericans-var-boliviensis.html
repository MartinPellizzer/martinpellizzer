Mascagnia sericans var. boliviensis

Mascagnia sericans var. boliviensis, commonly known as the Bolivian trumpet flower, is a striking member of the Apocynaceae family native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from orange to red, often with contrasting white or yellow markings. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture, while its stems are woody and often branched.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
