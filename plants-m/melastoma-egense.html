Melastoma egense

Melastoma egense, commonly known as the red-flowered melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and shrublands. Its flowers are usually pink or red, with five petals and a distinctive tubular corolla.

Melastoma egense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
