Mentha arvensis f. pseudoagrestis

Mentha arvensis f. pseudoagrestis, commonly known as field mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a variant of the common mint species, characterized by its slender, often creeping stems and opposite, serrated leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in whorled clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as meadows and woodland edges.

Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, making it useful in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
