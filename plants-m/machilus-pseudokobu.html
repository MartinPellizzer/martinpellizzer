Machilus pseudokobu

Machilus pseudokobu, commonly known as the Japanese wax tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its fruit is a small, dark blue drupe that contains a single seed.

Machilus pseudokobu is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
