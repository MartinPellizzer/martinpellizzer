Melaleuca tomentosa

Melaleuca tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly honeymyrtle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly silver-gray foliage and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in winter. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and heathlands.

Melaleuca tomentosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional Aboriginal medicine.
