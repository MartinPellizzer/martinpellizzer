Maxillaria latifolia

Maxillaria latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, fleshy leaves and produces fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to thrive. Its flowers bloom in the spring and are often used in ornamental gardening due to their beauty and fragrance.

Maxillaria latifolia is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its aesthetic appeal and ease of cultivation.
