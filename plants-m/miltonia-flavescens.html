Miltonia flavescens

Miltonia flavescens, commonly known as the yellow lady's slipper orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow petals and a distinctive pouch-like lip that resembles a slipper. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air. Its flowers bloom in spring and summer, often displaying a mix of yellow and green hues.

Miltonia flavescens is prized for its ornamental value and is commonly cultivated in greenhouses and gardens.
