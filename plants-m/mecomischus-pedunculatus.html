Mecomischus pedunculatus

Mecomischus pedunculatus, commonly known as the slender-stemmed dodder, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies on host plants for nutrients.

It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. The plant's stems are slender and twining, allowing it to climb and attach to its host. Its reproductive strategy involves producing copious amounts of tiny seeds that can remain viable in the soil for years.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can negatively impact the growth of its host plants, making it a concern in agricultural settings.
