Mimusops browniana

Mimusops browniana, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and opposite, ovate leaves that are usually glossy green. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-red fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal forests and savannas.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape tree in warm climates.
