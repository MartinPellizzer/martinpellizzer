Monothrix stansburyi

Monothrix stansburyi, commonly known as the desert flax, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as deserts and rocky soils. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, blue or purple flowers in clusters. Its fibrous stems and leaves are adapted to conserve water, making it well-suited for harsh, dry climates.

Monothrix stansburyi is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and thrive in poor conditions.
