Malus × cerasifera f. microcarpa

Malus × cerasifera f. microcarpa, commonly known as the dwarf Chinese crabapple, is a compact, ornamental tree or shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, rounded leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers that appear in early spring. The fruit is a small, yellow or red apple-like berry that matures in late summer. This cultivar is valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

It is often used in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and seasonal interest.
