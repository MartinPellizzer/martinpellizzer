Monolena pygmaea

Monolena pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf whiteface, is a small, flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are small, white, and clustered in a rounded head.

Monolena pygmaea is a perennial species that thrives in partial shade and is relatively low maintenance once established.
