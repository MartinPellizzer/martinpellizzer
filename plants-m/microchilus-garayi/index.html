Microchilus Garayi

Microchilus garayi is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America. It belongs to the Campanulaceae family, known for its bell-shaped flowers. This species is characterized by its small, pale blue petals and short, hairy stems. Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered endangered. Its unique beauty and ecological significance make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
