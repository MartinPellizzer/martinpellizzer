Myosotis gethica

Myosotis gethica, commonly known as the Gethic forget-me-not, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, delicate blue or purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters and prefers well-drained, moist soils. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, sky-blue flowers.
