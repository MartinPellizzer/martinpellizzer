Micromyrtus striata

Micromyrtus striata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf paperbark, is a small evergreen shrub native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its narrow, strap-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish bloom. Its small, fragrant flowers are typically pale yellow or white, and it produces small, woody capsules as fruit.

Micromyrtus striata is well adapted to coastal environments and is often found in sandy soils.
