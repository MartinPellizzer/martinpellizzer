Malveopsis peruviana f. coerulea

Malveopsis peruviana f. coerulea, commonly known as the blue-flowered malveopsis, is a striking ornamental plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton, and is characterized by its showy, pentagonal flowers. The plant features palmate leaves with a velvety texture and produces vibrant blue to purple blooms that attract pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens in temperate climates.

Due to its unique coloration and ornamental value, it is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in botanical gardens and private collections.
