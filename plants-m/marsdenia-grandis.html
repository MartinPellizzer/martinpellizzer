Marsdenia grandis

Marsdenia grandis, commonly known as the large-flowered Marsdenia, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in the summer. Its fruit is a follicle that splits open to release seeds.

Marsdenia grandis is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its showy blooms and vigorous growth habit.
