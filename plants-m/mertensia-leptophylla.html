Mertensia leptophylla

Mertensia leptophylla, commonly known as the slenderleaf bluebell, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Mertensia leptophylla is a valuable addition to native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
