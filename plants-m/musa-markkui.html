Musa markkui

Musa markkui, commonly known as the "Bornean banana," is a rare and enigmatic species native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It belongs to the Musaceae family, which includes other well-known banana and plantain species. This plant is characterized by its large, elongated leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers that develop into edible fruits. Musa markkui is notable for its unique morphology and limited distribution, making it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss, it faces threats to its survival in the wild.
