Morus wittiorum

Morus wittiorum, commonly known as the black mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to southern China.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, black berries. The tree can grow up to 10 meters tall and is often found in moist, well-drained soils. Its bark is smooth and gray, with a distinctive flaky texture.

The plant is valued for its edible fruit and its role in traditional Chinese medicine.
