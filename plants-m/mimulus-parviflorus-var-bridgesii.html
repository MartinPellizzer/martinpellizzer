Mimulus parviflorus var. bridgesii

Mimulus parviflorus var. bridgesii, commonly known as the small-flowered monkeyflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features delicate, spurred flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in spring and early summer. The plant has hairy stems and heart-shaped, succulent leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in streambanks and woodland edges.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for wetland restoration and native plant gardens.
