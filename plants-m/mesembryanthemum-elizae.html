Mesembryanthemum elizae

Mesembryanthemum elizae, commonly known as the Eliza's ice plant, is a succulent shrub native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, ribbed leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in spring. It is a member of the Aizoaceae family, known for its adaptive features to dry climates.

Mesembryanthemum elizae is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and low maintenance requirements.
