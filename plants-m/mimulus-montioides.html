Mimulus montioides

Mimulus montioides, commonly known as the Sierra monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking orange to yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats, often in mountainous regions. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and is part of the Physaleae tribe within the family Phrymaceae.

Mimulus montioides is noted for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
