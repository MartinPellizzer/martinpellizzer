Mentha × skofitziana

Mentha × skofitziana, commonly known as the hybrid mint, is a cross between Mentha viridis and Mentha piperita.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its aromatic leaves and creeping growth habit. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Mentha × skofitziana is widely cultivated for its medicinal and culinary uses due to its high concentration of menthol and other essential oils.
