Macaranga quadriglandulosa var. digyna

Macaranga quadriglandulosa var. digyna, commonly known as the hairy macaranga, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a fast-growing shrub or small tree that typically reaches heights of up to 5 meters. The plant is characterized by its opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves, which have four glands on the petiole. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is notable for its role in ecological systems, particularly as a host plant for certain insect species.
