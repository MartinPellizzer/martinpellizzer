Magnolia peruviana

Magnolia peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate to elliptical leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. Its bark is smooth and grayish, with a subtle fragrance when crushed.

Magnolia peruviana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
