Malva fastigiata var. foliosa

Malva fastigiata var. foliosa, commonly known as the broadleaf mallow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branching stems that are covered in soft, fuzzy hairs. The leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and often have a waxy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal. Its flowers are typically pink or purple, with five petals and a distinctive central column.

This plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
