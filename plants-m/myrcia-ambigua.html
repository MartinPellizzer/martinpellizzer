Myrcia ambigua

Myrcia ambigua, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and dark, glossy leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Myrcia ambigua is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
