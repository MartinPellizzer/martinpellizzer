Marsdenia eriocarpa

Marsdenia eriocarpa, commonly known as the woolly Marsdenia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by follicles containing elongated seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical environments, often found in moist, shaded areas within forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
