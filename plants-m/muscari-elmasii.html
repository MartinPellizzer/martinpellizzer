Muscari elmasii

Muscari elmasii, commonly known as the Turkish grape hyacinth, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Turkey.

It produces dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, resembling tiny grapes. The plant typically blooms in early spring, adding a vibrant touch to gardens and natural landscapes. Muscari elmasii thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to naturalize over time, creating a striking display in the right conditions.
