Mecodium osmundoides

Mecodium osmundoides, commonly known as the water starwort, is a small, aquatic plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that are typically submerged or floating, with a slender, branching stem. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often forming dense mats on the water surface. Its reproductive structures are inconspicuous, relying on water for the dispersal of its spores.

Mecodium osmundoides plays a role in maintaining water quality by absorbing nutrients and providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
