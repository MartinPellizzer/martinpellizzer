Masdevallia virens

Masdevallia virens, commonly known as the green orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often pendulous stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces delicate, greenish-white flowers with a subtle fragrance, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts and botanists alike.
