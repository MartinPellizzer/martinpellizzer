Morinda citrifolia var. elliptica

Morinda citrifolia var. elliptica, commonly known as the noni tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves and small, white or yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces a fruit that is typically green when unripe and turns yellow or orange when mature. The fruit has a strong, fermented odor and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
