Mayepea ramiflora

Mayepea ramiflora, commonly known as the branched mayepea, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its legume fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features compound leaves with multiple leaflets and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of yellow and green. Its roots contain compounds that have been traditionally used in local medicine.

Mayepea ramiflora thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.
