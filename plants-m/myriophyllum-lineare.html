Myriophyllum lineare

Myriophyllum lineare, commonly known as watermilfoil, is a perennial aquatic plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feather-like leaves that are often mistaken for water lilies. The plant typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams. Its small, green flowers appear in late summer and are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences.

Myriophyllum lineare plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
