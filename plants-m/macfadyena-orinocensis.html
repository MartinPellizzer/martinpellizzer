Macfadyena orinocensis

Macfadyena orinocensis, commonly known as the Crown of Thorns, is a climbing plant native to South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is adapted to arid environments, with thick, fleshy stems that store water. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and resilience.

However, it can be invasive in some regions, posing a threat to local ecosystems.
