Mespilus amelanchier var. canadensis

Mespilus amelanchier var. canadensis, commonly known as the Canadian serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet-tart berries in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adaptable to a variety of soil types.

This species is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and wildlife benefits.
