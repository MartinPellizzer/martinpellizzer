Mentha × verticillata var. nitida

Mentha × verticillata var. nitida, commonly known as narrow-leaf water mint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as stream banks and wet meadows.

Its aromatic leaves and flowers make it a useful plant in both culinary and medicinal applications.
