Mentha × wagneriana

Mentha × wagneriana, commonly known as Wagner's mint, is a hybrid species of the mint genus Mentha.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and strong menthol scent, which are typical features of many mint species. This plant is native to Europe and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Mentha × wagneriana typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and has square stems and opposite leaves.

Its flowers are small, tubular, and usually violet or purple in color, appearing in whorls along the stem.
