Mespilus tanacetifolia var. typica

Mespilus tanacetifolia var. typica, commonly known as the meadow sweet, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are compound, with serrated edges, and it produces small, dark berries in the autumn.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically in herbal medicine.
