Mentha × carinthiaca var. diespasmena

Mentha × carinthiaca var. diespasmena, commonly known as the Mentha × carinthiaca var. diespasmena, is a hybrid mint species native to Europe.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, serrated leaves that are typically a deep green color. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom in late summer. It is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves used in culinary and medicinal applications.

This variety is known for its strong mint aroma and is relatively hardy in temperate climates.
