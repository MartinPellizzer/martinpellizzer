Manihot grandiflora

Manihot grandiflora, commonly known as the giant arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces edible tubers that are rich in starch and used in various culinary applications. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ornamental and agricultural purposes.
