Maxillaria egertoniana

Maxillaria egertoniana, commonly known as the Egerton's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, yellow, and pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments and has a pseudobulb structure that helps it store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually elongated and grass-like, and it produces racemose inflorescences with numerous small to medium-sized flowers.

Maxillaria egertoniana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
