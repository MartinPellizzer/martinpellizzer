Matricaria coronopifolia

Matricaria coronopifolia, commonly known as German chamomile, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers with white centers. The plant has finely divided, aromatic leaves that are often used in herbal teas. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, typically growing to a height of about 30 cm.

Matricaria coronopifolia is valued for its calming properties and is often used in traditional medicine.
