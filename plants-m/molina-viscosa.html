Molina viscosa

Molina viscosa, commonly known as velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Molina viscosa is often found in disturbed habitats and can become invasive in certain regions.
