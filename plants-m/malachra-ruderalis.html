Malachra ruderalis

Malachra ruderalis, commonly known as the rough leaved malachra, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in roadside verges and open grasslands.

Malachra ruderalis is adapted to arid conditions and is known for its resilience in harsh environments.
