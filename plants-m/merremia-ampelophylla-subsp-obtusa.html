Merremia ampelophylla subsp. obtusa

Merremia ampelophylla subsp.

obtusa, commonly known as the broad-leaved morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America. It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of blue or purple.

The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, round seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Due to its rapid growth and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a garden plant.
