Madagasikaria andersonii

Madagasikaria andersonii, commonly known as the Anderson's Madagascar orchid, is a rare and endemic orchid species found exclusively in the island nation of Madagascar.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique floral structure and specialized growth habits. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories where it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient absorption. Its flowers are small and delicate, often displaying a striking combination of colors that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species requiring conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
