Monticalia imbricatifolia

Monticalia imbricatifolia, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the humid forests of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, large, pentagonal leaves that are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom during the rainy season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded undergrowth.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
