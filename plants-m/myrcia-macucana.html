Myrcia macucana

Myrcia macucana, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of eastern Brazil, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruits are small, berry-like structures that contain several seeds.

Myrcia macucana is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
