Mussaenda aptera

Mussaenda aptera, commonly known as the smooth mussaenda, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that resemble clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant has smooth, glabrous stems and broad, ovate leaves with a glossy green surface. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers are typically white or pale yellow and are pollinated by insects.
