Marshallia graminifolia var. cyananthera

Marshallia graminifolia var. cyananthera, commonly known as the blueflower marshmallow, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands and marshy areas, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are usually erect and branching.

The showy inflorescence consists of both ray and disk flowers, making it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
