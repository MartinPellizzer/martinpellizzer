Megacarpaea gracilis

Megacarpaea gracilis, commonly known as the slender leafed cycad, is a unique plant species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Cycadaceae family and is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and feather-like leaves. The plant produces large, cone-like structures that contain its seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Megacarpaea gracilis is adapted to grow in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soils.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
