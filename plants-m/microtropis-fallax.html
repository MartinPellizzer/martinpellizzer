Microtropis fallax

Microtropis fallax, commonly known as the Malayan climbing shrub, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its twining stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that release tiny seeds when mature.

Microtropis fallax is often found in shaded, humid environments within forest understories.
