Mammillaria huitzilopochtli var. niduliformis

Mammillaria huitzilopochtli var. niduliformis, commonly known as the "Aztec Sun God Cactus," is a rare and distinctive species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, globular shape and numerous small, white to pale yellow spines. The plant features a central stem with radial ribs and a compact, rounded form that makes it visually striking. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect sunlight to flourish.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is highly sought after by collectors and cactus enthusiasts.
