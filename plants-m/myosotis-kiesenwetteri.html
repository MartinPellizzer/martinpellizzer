Myosotis kiesenwetteri

Myosotis kiesenwetteri, commonly known as the Kiesenwetter's forget-me-not, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It produces small, delicate blue or purple flowers with five petals, typical of the Myosotis genus. The plant has a slender, upright stem and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Myosotis kiesenwetteri is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
