Mirabilis prostrata

Mirabilis prostrata, commonly known as the prostrate four o'clock, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a low-growing annual herb that produces showy, tubular flowers that open in the late afternoon. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves and stems that help it retain moisture in arid environments. Its flowers come in a variety of colors, including red, orange, and yellow, and are pollinated by moths and other nocturnal insects.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens and is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.
