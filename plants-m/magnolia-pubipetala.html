Magnolia pubipetala

Magnolia pubipetala, commonly known as the pubescent petal magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with hairy petals that give it its common name. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape and often have a velvety texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

The flowers bloom in early spring, adding ornamental value to gardens and natural landscapes.
