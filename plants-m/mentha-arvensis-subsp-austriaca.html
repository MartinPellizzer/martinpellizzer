Mentha arvensis subsp. austriaca

Mentha arvensis subsp.

austriaca, commonly known as Austrian mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite, ovate leaves.

The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.

Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, making it useful in traditional herbal remedies and as a source of essential oils for fragrance and medicine.
