Melochia parvifolia f. albiflora

Melochia parvifolia f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered melochia, is a flowering plant in the Malvaceae family.

It is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia. The plant is characterized by its small, pale green leaves and clusters of white, five-petaled flowers. It produces small, round fruits that are often used in traditional medicine.

The white-flowered variant is distinguished by its reduced pigmentation compared to the typical form.
