Mimosa duckei

Mimosa duckei, commonly known as the golden mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that fold shut when touched. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Mimosa duckei is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Its growth habit is typically shrub-like, with a branching structure that adds visual interest to gardens.
