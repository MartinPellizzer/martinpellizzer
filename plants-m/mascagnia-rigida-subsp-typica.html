Mascagnia rigida subsp. typica

Mascagnia rigida subsp.

typica, commonly known as the rigid mascagnia, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It features woody stems with grayish bark and produces small, yellowish flowers in the spring.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its drought tolerance and seasonal blooming habit.
