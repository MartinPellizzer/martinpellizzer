Marsdenia hullsii

Marsdenia hullsii, commonly known as the Hualien Marsdenia, is a flowering plant native to southern China and Taiwan.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family, which includes milkweed species, and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often woody and can become semi-woody with age.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
