Malus dasyphylla var. gellidus

Malus dasyphylla var. gellidus, commonly known as the Gellidus apple, is a variant of the apple tree native to China.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish fruits that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. The plant has a deciduous habit, shedding its leaves in the autumn season. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its potential use in horticulture.
