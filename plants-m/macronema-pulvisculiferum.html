Macronema pulvisculiferum

Macronema pulvisculiferum, commonly known as the dust-encrusted liverwort, is a species of liverwort belonging to the family Pottiaceae.

It is a small, mat-forming plant that thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found on rocky surfaces or in forest floors. Its leaves are closely packed and covered in a layer of fine, powdery particles, which give it its common name. The plant lacks true roots and instead absorbs nutrients through its leaf-like structures.

It is well adapted to arid conditions, making it a resilient member of the liverwort family.
