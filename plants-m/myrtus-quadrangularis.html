Myrtus quadrangularis

Myrtus quadrangularis, commonly known as the African myrtle or red myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its quadrangular, often reddish-brown stems and small, fragrant leaves with a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are followed by dark purple, egg-shaped berries. Myrtus quadrangularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its aromatic properties.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in arid climates.
