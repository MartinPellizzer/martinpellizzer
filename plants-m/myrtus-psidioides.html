Myrtus psidioides

Myrtus psidioides, commonly known as the dwarf myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant features small, dark green leaves with a fragrant, resinous scent, and it produces small white or pinkish flowers in spring. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Myrtus psidioides thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.
