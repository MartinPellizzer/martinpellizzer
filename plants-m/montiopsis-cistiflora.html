Montiopsis cistiflora

Montiopsis cistiflora, commonly known as the flaxflower, is a flowering plant native to the high Andes of South America.

It is a member of the family Montiaceae and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers and succulent leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky, arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers are usually pale blue or white, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Montiopsis cistiflora is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
