Mariposa clavata var. recurvifolia

Mariposa clavata var. recurvifolia, commonly known as the recurved mariposa lily, is a striking member of the Liliaceae family native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, recurved leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or streamside habitats. Its foliage is often described as delicate and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
