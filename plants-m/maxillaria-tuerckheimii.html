Maxillaria tuerckheimii

Maxillaria tuerckheimii, commonly known as the Tuerckheim's orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant features long, slender petals and a distinctive, often recurved lip. It typically blooms during the summer months and prefers bright, indirect light with moderate humidity.

Maxillaria tuerckheimii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
