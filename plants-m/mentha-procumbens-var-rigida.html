Mentha procumbens var. rigida

Mentha procumbens var. rigida, commonly known as rigid mint, is a perennial herb native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its low-growing, spreading habit and hairy, square stems. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue to purple in color. Its leaves are oval-shaped, serrated, and have a strong, aromatic mint scent.

This variety is often used in traditional herbal medicine and as a culinary herb due to its refreshing flavor.
