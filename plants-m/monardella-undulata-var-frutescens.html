Monardella undulata var. frutescens

Monardella undulata var. frutescens, commonly known as fringed mountain mint, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and showy, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its foliage is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported calming effects.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
