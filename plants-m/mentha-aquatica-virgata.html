Mentha aquatica virgata

Mentha aquatica virgata, commonly known as water mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in whorls along the stem. The plant is typically found in moist environments such as wet meadows, stream banks, and marshes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a strong, minty aroma, while its roots are used in traditional medicine.

Mentha aquatica virgata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators and improving soil stability.
