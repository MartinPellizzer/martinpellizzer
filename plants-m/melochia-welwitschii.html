Melochia welwitschii

Melochia welwitschii, commonly known as the African glorybower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that split open to release small, hairy seeds.

Melochia welwitschii is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
