Mentha aquatica subsp. lanigera

Mentha aquatica subsp.

lanigera, commonly known as the woolly water mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance.

The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often along the edges of ponds, streams, and marshes. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in whorls, producing a mild minty scent.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting aquatic ecosystems.
