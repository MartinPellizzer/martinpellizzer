Mosenia

Mosenia, commonly known as the "wild orchid," is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

These plants are characterized by their intricate, often fragrant flowers and specialized root systems adapted for nutrient absorption in nutrient-poor soils. Mosenia species typically thrive in tropical and subtropical regions, often growing in shaded, moist environments such as rainforests. Their growth is supported by symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi, which help them access essential nutrients.

The botanical study of Mosenia provides insights into the evolutionary adaptations of orchids in diverse ecological niches.
