Maxillaria argyrophylla

Maxillaria argyrophylla, commonly known as the silver-leafed orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking silver-green foliage and fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows as a sympodial orchid, with pseudobulbs that support its growth and storage of nutrients. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to retain moisture in its natural habitat.

Maxillaria argyrophylla is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique appearance.
