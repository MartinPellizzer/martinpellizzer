Mussaenda forsteniana

Mussaenda forsteniana, commonly known as the golden trumpet or yellow flame, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange inflorescences that resemble trumpets, which are composed of numerous small flowers clustered together. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Mussaenda forsteniana is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
