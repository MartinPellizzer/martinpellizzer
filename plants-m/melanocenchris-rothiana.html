Melanocenchris rothiana

Melanocenchris rothiana, commonly known as the Roth's ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic orchid species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, pale greenish-white flowers and unique, elongated pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on tree trunks or branches in the canopy of rainforests. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, with a subtle fragrance that attracts specific pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Melanocenchris rothiana is considered a vulnerable species facing threats from deforestation and climate change.
