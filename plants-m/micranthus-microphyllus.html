Micranthus microphyllus

Micranthus microphyllus, commonly known as the dwarf dandelion, is a small flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant has a basal rosette of narrow, spoon-shaped leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that develop into fluffy white seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appeal, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
