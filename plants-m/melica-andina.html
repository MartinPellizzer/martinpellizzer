Melica andina

Melica andina, commonly known as Andean grass, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes Mountains.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant typically has narrow, elongated leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soils.

Melica andina is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
