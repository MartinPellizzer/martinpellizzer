Mentha × libertiana

Mentha × libertiana, commonly known as the hybrid mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family and is characterized by its square stems and opposite, serrated leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in whorls along the upper stems. Mentha × libertiana is known for its strong, aromatic scent and is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
