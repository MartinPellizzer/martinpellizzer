Mentha × incanescens

Mentha × incanescens, commonly known as the gray mint, is a hybrid mint species that combines traits from various Mentha species.

It is characterized by its grayish-green foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale purple, arranged in loose clusters. Mentha × incanescens is native to parts of Europe and Asia, where it thrives in moist, temperate climates.

It is often used in herbal teas and as a culinary herb due to its aromatic and slightly minty flavor.
