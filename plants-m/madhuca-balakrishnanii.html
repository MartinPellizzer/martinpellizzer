Madhuca balakrishnanii

Madhuca balakrishnanii, commonly known as the Kottayam tamarind tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The tree produces edible fruits that are used in traditional medicine and local cuisines. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in secondary forests and disturbed habitats.

Due to its ecological and cultural significance, it is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential for reforestation efforts.
