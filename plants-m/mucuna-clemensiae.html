Mucuna clemensiae

Mucuna clemensiae, commonly known as the velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and trifoliate leaves, which contribute to its ornamental appeal. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by pods containing edible seeds. It is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agricultural systems.

However, it can become invasive in certain environments if not managed properly.
