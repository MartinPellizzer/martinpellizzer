Misanteca capitata

Misanteca capitata, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant orange to red ray flowers and dark green, heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet and thrives in full sun with well-drained soil. Its botanical features include a stout, branching stem and showy composite flower heads that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Misanteca capitata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in warm climates.
