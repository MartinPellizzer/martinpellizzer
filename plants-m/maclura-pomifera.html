Maclura pomifera

Maclura pomifera, commonly known as the horse chestnut, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the United States.

It is characterized by its large, palmately compound leaves and produces distinctive, spiny, orange fruits known as "cherry bombs.". The plant has a deep root system and can thrive in a variety of soil types, though it prefers well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.

The fruit contains toxic compounds and should not be consumed by humans or animals.
