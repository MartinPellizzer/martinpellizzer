Matthiola ghorana

Matthiola ghorana, commonly known as the Ghorana stock, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of India.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters at the top of slender stems. It thrives in cool climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Matthiola ghorana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine in some regions.
