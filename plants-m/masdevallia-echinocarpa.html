Masdevallia echinocarpa

Masdevallia echinocarpa, commonly known as the spiny-pod masdevallia, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure and produces striking, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant's most distinctive feature is its intricate inflorescence, which bears small, fragrant flowers with intricate patterns of white and purple. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires high air humidity and indirect light for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a popular species among orchid enthusiasts and conservationists.
