Myrcia ochroides

Myrcia ochroides, commonly known as the red myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are edible but often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded areas of forests.

Myrcia ochroides is valued for its ornamental appeal and has a long history of use in folk remedies.
