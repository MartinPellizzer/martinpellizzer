Myrtus undulata

Myrtus undulata, commonly known as the undulating myrtle, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Myrtus undulata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.
