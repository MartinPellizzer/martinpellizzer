Melilotus cappadocius

Melilotus cappadocius, commonly known as Cappadocian sweet clover, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in temperate climates. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

Melilotus cappadocius is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop for livestock.
