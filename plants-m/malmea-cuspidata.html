Malmea cuspidata

Malmea cuspidata, commonly known as the Cape snowbush, is a South African shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, oval berries that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Malmea cuspidata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in coastal landscaping due to its salt tolerance.
