Myrcia longiramea

Myrcia longiramea, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible and contain numerous seeds. Myrcia longiramea is often found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional uses.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
