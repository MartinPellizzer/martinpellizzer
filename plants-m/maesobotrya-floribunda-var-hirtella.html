Maesobotrya floribunda var. hirtella

Maesobotrya floribunda var. hirtella, commonly known as the hairy-flowered maesobotrya, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub characterized by its hairy leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered with fine pubescence, while the flowers are clustered in inflorescences.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.
