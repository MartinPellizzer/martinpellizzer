Merremia sapinii

Merremia sapinii, commonly known as the velvetleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils.

Merremia sapinii is valued for its ornamental appeal but can be invasive in certain environments.
