Myrcia imbricata

Myrcia imbricata, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, red berries. The plant is evergreen, with a dense, rounded canopy that provides habitat for various wildlife species. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in dense inflorescences, often blooming in summer.

Myrcia imbricata is also known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
