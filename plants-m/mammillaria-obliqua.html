Mammillaria obliqua

Mammillaria obliqua, commonly known as the zigzag cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in white to pale green tubercles. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It prefers well-draining soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its compact size and unique appearance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cultivation.
