Marattia caudiformis

Marattia caudiformis, commonly known as the long-leaf marattia, is a species of fern belonging to the family Marattiaceae.

It is characterized by its large, feather-like fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant is native to tropical regions of Asia and is often found in humid, shaded environments. Its sporangia are clustered in sori on the underside of the fronds, a typical feature of ferns.

Marattia caudiformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
