Magnolia ferruginea

Magnolia ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The leaves are broad, ovate, and have a rough, hairy texture on the underside. The tree produces small, reddish-brown fruits that contain winged seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to moist, well-drained soils.
