Melicope polyadenia

Melicope polyadenia, commonly known as the Hawaiian pepper tree, is a member of the Rutaceae family and is native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, with smooth, grayish bark and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences, followed by small, fleshy berries that turn red when mature.

Its leaves and fruits contain compounds with potential medicinal properties, making it an important species in traditional Hawaiian medicine.
