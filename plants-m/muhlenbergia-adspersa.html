Muhlenbergia adspersa

Muhlenbergia adspersa, commonly known as the silverhair grass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray blades and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. The plant forms dense clumps and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value. It produces fluffy, pinkish-white seed heads that add visual interest in the fall.

Muhlenbergia adspersa is drought-tolerant and requires minimal maintenance once established.
