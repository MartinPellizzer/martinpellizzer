Matthiola viburnoides

Matthiola viburnoides, commonly known as the fragrant thyme.

This plant is a member of the Brassicaceae family and is native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in shades of white and pink. The leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, giving the plant a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
