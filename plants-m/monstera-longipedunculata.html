Monstera longipedunculata

Monstera longipedunculata, commonly known as the giant monstera, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 2 meters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, spiny fruits. Its aerial roots allow it to climb and spread along tree trunks in its natural habitat.

The leaves are known for their unique fenestrations, which are believed to aid in light penetration in dense forest environments.
