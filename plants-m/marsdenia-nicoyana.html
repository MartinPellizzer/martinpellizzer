Marsdenia nicoyana

Marsdenia nicoyana, commonly known as the Nicoyan Marsdenia, is a climbing plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruits are follicles that release seeds with tufts of hair for wind dispersal.

The species is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in shaded or semi-shaded environments.
