Myricaria rosea

Myricaria rosea, commonly known as the rose gooseberry, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellowish-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, red or orange berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. Myricaria rosea is adapted to harsh environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.

It plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
