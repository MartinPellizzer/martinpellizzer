Mikania amorimii

Mikania amorimii, commonly known as the Amazonian devil's vine, is a climbing plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rapid growth and distinctive, palmate leaves. The plant produces small yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, which are often hidden among its dense foliage. Mikania amorimii is known for its strong, woody stems that can entangle other vegetation, contributing to its role as an invasive species in some regions.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and challenging to manage in natural habitats.
