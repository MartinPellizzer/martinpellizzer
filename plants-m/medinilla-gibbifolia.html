Medinilla gibbifolia

Medinilla gibbifolia, commonly known as the velvet tangerine, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and fragrant, showy flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a glossy texture, and the plant produces small, orange-colored fruits that are edible. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

The species is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
