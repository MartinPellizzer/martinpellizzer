Matthiola longipetala

Matthiola longipetala, commonly known as the long-petalled stock, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its clustered, fragrant flowers that typically range in color from white to pink or purple. The plant has slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
