Maranta eburnea

Maranta eburnea, commonly known as the Ivory Plant, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that often feature intricate patterns and a waxy sheen. The plant produces clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in the evening, making it a nocturnal flowering species. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats, which is useful for ground cover in shaded environments.

Maranta eburnea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its aesthetic qualities.
