Mahonia napaulensis var. roxburghii

Mahonia napaulensis var. roxburghii, commonly known as the Himalayan mahonia, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas.

It features compound leaves with spiny margins and is characterized by its yellow, cone-shaped flowers that appear in late winter. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but have a tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and hardy nature in temperate climates.
