Milium phleoides

Milium phleoides, commonly known as the narrow-leafed millet, is a flowering plant species native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest floors or along streambanks. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a smooth, slightly hairy surface.

Milium phleoides is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
