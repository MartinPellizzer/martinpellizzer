Malope malacoides var. stipulacea

Malope malacoides var. stipulacea, commonly known as the stipule malope, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves with distinctive stipules. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its botanical characteristics include glandular hairs on the stems and leaves, which contribute to its unique appearance.

It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.
