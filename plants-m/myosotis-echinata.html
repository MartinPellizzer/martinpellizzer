Myosotis echinata

Myosotis echinata, commonly known as the spiked forget-me-not, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its small, blue or violet petals and a distinctive spiky center, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in damp, shaded areas such as forests and stream banks. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces flowers throughout the summer months.

Myosotis echinata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
