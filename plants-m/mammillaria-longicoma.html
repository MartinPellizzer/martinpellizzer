Mammillaria longicoma

Mammillaria longicoma, commonly known as the long-spined hedgehog cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem and spiny, ribbed structure. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its green foliage. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.
