Myrcia minutiflora

Myrcia minutiflora, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are edible but often have a sharp, acidic taste. It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, with lance-shaped leaves and a dense, evergreen canopy.

Myrcia minutiflora is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and is also known for its aromatic properties.
