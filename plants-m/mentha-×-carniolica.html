Mentha × carniolica

Mentha × carniolica, commonly known as the Hungarian mint, is a hybrid mint species native to the Carpathian region.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in whorls along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Mentha × carniolica is often used in herbal remedies and as an ornamental plant due to its aromatic properties.
