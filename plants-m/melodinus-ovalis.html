Melodinus ovalis

Melodinus ovalis, commonly known as the oval waxflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, waxy, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has narrow, strap-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Melodinus ovalis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable climates.
