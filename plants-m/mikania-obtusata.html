Mikania obtusata

Mikania obtusata, commonly known as Mexican morning glory, is a climbing vine native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Mikania obtusata is known for its rapid growth and is considered an invasive species in some regions.
