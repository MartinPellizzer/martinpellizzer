Melica pooides var. acuminata

Melica pooides var. acuminata, commonly known as the acuteleaf meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist meadows and along stream banks, preferring well-drained, loamy soils. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with acute tips, giving it its common name. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like arrangement of small, two-lobed spikelets.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
