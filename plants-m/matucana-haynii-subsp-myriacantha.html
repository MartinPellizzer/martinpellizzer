Matucana haynii subsp. myriacantha

Matucana haynii subsp.

myriacantha, commonly known as the Myriacantha torch cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America. It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that often branch into a crown-like structure.

The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are modified into spines, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

This subspecies is valued for its striking appearance and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
