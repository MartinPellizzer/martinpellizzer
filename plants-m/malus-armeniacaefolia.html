Malus armeniacaefolia

Malus armeniacaefolia, commonly known as the Armenian leaf apple, is a deciduous tree native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its ornamental foliage, with heart-shaped leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible, apple-like fruits. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Due to its aesthetic value and unique fruit, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
