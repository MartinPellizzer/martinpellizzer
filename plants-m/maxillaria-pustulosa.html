Maxillaria pustulosa

Maxillaria pustulosa, commonly known as the pustular maxillaria, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, often with intricate patterns on the petals. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern around the pseudobulbs.

Maxillaria pustulosa is appreciated in horticulture for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
