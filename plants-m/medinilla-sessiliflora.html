Medinilla sessiliflora

Medinilla sessiliflora, commonly known as the velvet tangerine or velvet flower, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and clusters of fragrant, waxy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are edible and have a sweet, citrus-like flavor. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and blooms.

Medinilla sessiliflora is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
