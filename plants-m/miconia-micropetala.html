Miconia micropetala

Miconia micropetala, commonly known as the small-leaved miconia, is a member of the Melastomataceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers with five petals, often in shades of red, pink, or white, which attract various pollinators. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in tropical gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
