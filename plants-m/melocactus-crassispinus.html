Melocactus crassispinus

Melocactus crassispinus, commonly known as the thick-spined melon cactus, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a solitary cactus with a short, thick stem that often remains close to the ground. The plant is characterized by its dense, spiny areoles and the presence of small, fleshy, spiny structures called tubercles. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, appearing at the base of the spines during the warmer months.

This species is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for desert gardens.
