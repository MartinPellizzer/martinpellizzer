Memora allamandiflora

Memora allamandiflora, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers that resemble small, drooping raindrops. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain seeds covered in a silky, white coating. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its rapid growth and showy blooms make it a striking addition to landscapes in warm climates.
