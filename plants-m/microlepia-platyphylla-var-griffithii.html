Microlepia platyphylla var. griffithii

Microlepia platyphylla var. griffithii, commonly known as the Griffith's broad-leafed chain fern, is a variant of the broad-leafed chain fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is a fern characterized by its long, arching fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant has a distinctive, segmented structure with a creeping rhizome that supports the upright fronds. Its leaves are typically broad and have a leathery texture, with a dark green coloration.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.
