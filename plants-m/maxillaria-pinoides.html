Maxillaria pinoides

Maxillaria pinoides, commonly known as the pin-like maxillaria, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces fragrant, showy flowers with a distinctive pink and white coloration. The plant typically features pseudobulbs that support its foliage, which consists of narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are borne on racemes and are known for their intricate patterns and strong scent, which attract pollinators.

Maxillaria pinoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements in suitable growing conditions.
