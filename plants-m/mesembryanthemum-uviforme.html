Mesembryanthemum uviforme

Mesembryanthemum uviforme, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in spring and summer. Its flowering period is often accompanied by a distinctive scent that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Mesembryanthemum uviforme is well adapted to hot, dry climates and is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
