Muhlenbergia schreberi subsp. curtisetosa

Muhlenbergia schreberi subsp.

curtisetosa, commonly known as the silver bluestem, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its fine, silver-blue foliage and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

This perennial grass is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance. Its inflorescence consists of slender, spike-like clusters that bloom in late summer.

The plant is also valued for its role in erosion control and as a wildlife habitat.
