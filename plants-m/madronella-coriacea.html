Madronella coriacea

Madronella coriacea, commonly known as the roughleaf madrona, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are grayish-green in color and have a distinctive rough texture. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters during the spring, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing a rugged appearance, and it is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

The plant is well adapted to dry, well-drained soils and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance.
