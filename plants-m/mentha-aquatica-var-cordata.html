Mentha aquatica var. cordata

Mentha aquatica var. cordata, commonly known as water mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and stream banks in temperate regions.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are often slightly serrated, with a distinctive minty aroma. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in riparian zones.

Its aromatic properties make it a popular choice for culinary and medicinal uses.
