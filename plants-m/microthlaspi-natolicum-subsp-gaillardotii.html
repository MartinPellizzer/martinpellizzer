Microthlaspi natolicum subsp. gaillardotii

Microthlaspi natolicum subsp.

gaillardotii, commonly known as the Gaillardot's rockcress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and segmented fruits.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its leaves are basal, pinnately divided, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

This subspecies is notable for its ability to thrive in harsh, high-altitude environments.
