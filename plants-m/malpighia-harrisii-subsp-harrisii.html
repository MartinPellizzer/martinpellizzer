Malpighia harrisii subsp. harrisii

Malpighia harrisii subsp.

harrisii, commonly known as the Harris's wax apple, is a species native to Central America. It is a small shrub or tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions.

The plant produces small, fleshy, red berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often covered in a waxy coating.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its fruit.
