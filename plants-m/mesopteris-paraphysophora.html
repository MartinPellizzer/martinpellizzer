Mesopteris paraphysophora

Mesopteris paraphysophora, commonly known as the giant tree fern, is a member of the Cyatheaceae family and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk covered in a thick layer of persistent, fibrous roots. The plant produces large, feather-like fronds that can grow up to several meters in length, giving it a distinctive and dramatic appearance. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are typically found on the undersides of the fronds.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
