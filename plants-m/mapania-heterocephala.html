Mapania heterocephala

Mapania heterocephala, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a striking member of the Malvaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its large, white, night-blooming flowers that emit a sweet fragrance. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its unique blooming pattern and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
