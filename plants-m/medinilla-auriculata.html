Medinilla auriculata

Medinilla auriculata, commonly known as the golden medinilla, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that resemble small ears, giving it its species name "auriculata.". The plant produces showy, bell-shaped blooms that are often used in floral arrangements due to their beauty and long-lasting nature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil and partial shade for optimal growth.

Medinilla auriculata is a popular ornamental plant in horticulture, valued for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various garden settings.
