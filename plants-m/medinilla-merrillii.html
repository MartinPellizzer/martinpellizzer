Medinilla merrillii

Medinilla merrillii, commonly known as the velvet tangerine, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, round, orange fruits that resemble tangerines, which are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor. Medinilla merrillii thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal.

It is also valued for its use in traditional medicine in some regions.
