Melilobus

Melilobus, commonly known as the California buckeye, is a deciduous tree native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, yellowish-white flowers. The tree produces pod-like fruits that contain toxic seeds, which are a key feature in its botanical classification. Its bark is smooth and grayish, and it often has a twisted growth habit.

Melilobus is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.
