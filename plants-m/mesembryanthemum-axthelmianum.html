Mesembryanthemum axthelmianum

Mesembryanthemum axthelmianum, commonly known as the desert star or rock rose, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in arid environments. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the warm season, typically in spring or summer. It belongs to the Aizoaceae family, known for its diverse array of succulent species.

Mesembryanthemum axthelmianum is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
