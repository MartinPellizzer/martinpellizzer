Miscanthus wardii

Miscanthus wardii, commonly known as the silver feather grass, is a perennial grass native to China.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and feathery, silver-gray plumes that give it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in late summer, which are clustered in panicles. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slight bluish-green hue.

Miscanthus wardii is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
