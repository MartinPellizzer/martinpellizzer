Maxillaria consanguinea

Maxillaria consanguinea, commonly known as the blood orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its intricate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are usually dark red or purple, with intricate patterns that resemble blood, hence its common name.

Maxillaria consanguinea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses or tropical gardens.
