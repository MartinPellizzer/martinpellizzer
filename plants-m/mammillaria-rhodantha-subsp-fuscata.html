Mammillaria rhodantha subsp. fuscata

Mammillaria rhodantha subsp.

fuscata, commonly known as the brown-tipped hedgehog cactus, is a species native to Mexico. It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and spiny, ribbed stems.

The plant features pale green to bluish-gray stems with prominent areoles and short, white to brown spines. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, emerging from the areoles during the warmer months.

This succulent is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.
