Myrcia pineticola

Myrcia pineticola, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that changes color from green to red as it matures.

Myrcia pineticola is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
