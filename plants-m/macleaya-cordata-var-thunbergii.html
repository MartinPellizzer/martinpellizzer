Macleaya cordata var. thunbergii

Macleaya cordata var. thunbergii, commonly known as Chinese bellflower, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to lilac, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has a rhizomatous root system and can grow up to 2 meters tall, with large, heart-shaped leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in some regions.
