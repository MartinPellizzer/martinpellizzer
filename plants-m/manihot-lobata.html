Manihot lobata

Manihot lobata, commonly known as the Brazilian arrowroot, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, simple, and often lobed leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous green flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its roots are the primary part used, valued for their starchy, edible qualities.

The plant is adapted to grow in wet, tropical environments and is often found in swampy or marshy areas.
