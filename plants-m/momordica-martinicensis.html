Momordica martinicensis

Momordica martinicensis, commonly known as the Martinique bitter melon, is a climbing vine native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like cucumbers and pumpkins. The plant produces distinctive, warty, green fruits that turn yellow when mature. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with five petals.

The plant is valued for its edible fruits, which have a bitter taste and are used in various culinary traditions.
