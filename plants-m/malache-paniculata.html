Malache paniculata

Malache paniculata, commonly known as the panicled malache, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae, which includes mahogany and other tropical hardwoods. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in panicles. It typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters.
