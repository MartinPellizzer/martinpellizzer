Myrcia maritima

Myrcia maritima, commonly known as coastal myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to coastal regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in sandy or rocky soils. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible berries. Its leaves are aromatic and often used in traditional medicine for their antiseptic properties.

Myrcia maritima is well adapted to coastal environments, showing resistance to salt spray and strong winds.
