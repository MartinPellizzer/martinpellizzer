Mirabilis jalapa var. planiflora

Mirabilis jalapa var. planiflora, commonly known as the angel's trumpet, is a striking ornamental plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep pink, often with a contrasting throat. The plant is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in warm climates and well-drained soil. Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy.

It produces its showy blooms in late spring to early summer, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.
