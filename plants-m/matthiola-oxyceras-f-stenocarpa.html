Matthiola oxyceras f. stenocarpa

Matthiola oxyceras f. stenocarpa, commonly known as the narrow-fruited wallflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of bright yellow or orange flowers. The leaves are linear to lanceolate, often with a slightly hairy surface, and the stems are usually hairy as well. The fruit is a short, cylindrical silique, distinguishing it from other forms of Matthiola.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often grown in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
