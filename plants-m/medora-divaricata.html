Medora divaricata

Medora divaricata, commonly known as the narrowleaf medora, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as a shrub with slender, often branched stems and small, narrow leaves. Its flowers are yellow and occur in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Medora divaricata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
