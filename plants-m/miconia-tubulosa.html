Miconia tubulosa

Miconia tubulosa, commonly known as the tropical beauty or false hibiscus, is a member of the Melastomataceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of the Americas, including parts of Central and South America. The plant is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Miconia tubulosa is also known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, making it an invasive species in some areas.
