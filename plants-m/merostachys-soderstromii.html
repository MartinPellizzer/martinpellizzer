Merostachys soderstromii

Merostachys soderstromii, commonly known as the Soderstrom's grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils and open habitats.

Due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements, it is sometimes used in xeriscaping and as a native plant in ecological restoration projects.
