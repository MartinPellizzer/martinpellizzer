Malpighia proctorii

Malpighia proctorii, commonly known as the Proctor's acorn vine, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Malpighiaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit, with slender stems that twine around supports. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, followed by bright red, fleshy fruits that are edible. Its botanical features include alternate leaves with stipules and a unique fruit structure that aids in seed dispersal.

Malpighia proctorii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also valued in traditional medicine.
