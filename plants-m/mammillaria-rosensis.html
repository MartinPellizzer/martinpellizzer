Mammillaria rosensis

Mammillaria rosensis, commonly known as the Rosens Mountain Cactus, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It is a small, globular cactus with a dense covering of white, woolly spines. The plant typically grows to a height of about 5 to 10 centimeters and has a compact, rounded shape. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or orange, emerging from the areoles in the spring.

Mammillaria rosensis is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil to thrive.
