Mimosa nanchititlana

Mimosa nanchititlana, commonly known as the Mexican mimosa, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fluffy flowers. The plant typically grows in shrubland and woodland habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually pink or pale yellow, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Mimosa nanchititlana is often used in traditional medicine and landscaping due to its ornamental value.
