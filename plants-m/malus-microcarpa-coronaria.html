Malus microcarpa coronaria

Malus microcarpa coronaria, commonly known as the Mexican apple or coral apple, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its small, red, apple-like fruits that are edible. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it produces white or pink flowers in the spring.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
