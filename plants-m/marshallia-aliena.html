Marshallia aliena

Marshallia aliena, commonly known as the white coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, white to pale pink petals and a distinctive, spiky yellow center. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as prairies and woodland edges. It produces numerous small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Marshallia aliena is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits in native gardens.
