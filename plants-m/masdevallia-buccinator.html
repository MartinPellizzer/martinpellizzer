Masdevallia buccinator

Masdevallia buccinator, commonly known as the "lip orchid," is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, showy flowers that resemble a lip, hence its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree trunks or branches in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are elongated and often covered in a dense layer of fine, hairy structures.

The flowers, which bloom in shades of purple and white, are highly fragrant and attract specific pollinators such as bees and moths.
