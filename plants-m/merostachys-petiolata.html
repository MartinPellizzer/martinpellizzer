Merostachys petiolata

Merostachys petiolata, commonly known as the petioled feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and delicate, feathery inflorescences that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its foliage is often used in floral arrangements due to its ornamental value.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small wildlife.
