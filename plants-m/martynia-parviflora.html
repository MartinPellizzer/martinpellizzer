Martynia parviflora

Martynia parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered morning glory, is a herbaceous annual plant native to South America.

It features slender, hairy stems that grow upright or prostrate, depending on the environmental conditions. The plant produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pale yellow to orange in color, blooming in the morning. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Martynia parviflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
