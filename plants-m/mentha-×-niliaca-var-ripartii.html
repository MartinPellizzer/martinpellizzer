Mentha × niliaca var. ripartii

Mentha × niliaca var. ripartii, commonly known as the broadleaf mint, is a hybrid species within the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and strong, aromatic fragrance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple in color.

This variety is often used in herbal teas and traditional medicine due to its therapeutic properties.
