Mentha longifolia f. subalpina

Mentha longifolia f. subalpina, commonly known as alpine mint, is a subspecies of the mint plant characterized by its aromatic, slightly hairy leaves and square stems.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Europe and is adapted to cool, moist environments. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are often used in herbal teas and traditional remedies due to their refreshing scent and mild medicinal properties.

This subspecies is particularly valued for its resilience in alpine and subalpine habitats.
