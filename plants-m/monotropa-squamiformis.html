Monotropa squamiformis

Monotropa squamiformis, commonly known as the squamose ghostplant, is a climbing vine native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its lack of chlorophyll, making it a mycoheterotroph that obtains nutrients through its mycorrhizal associations with fungi. It thrives in shaded, moist woodlands and is often found in association with decaying organic matter.
