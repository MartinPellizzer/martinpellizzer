Mercurialis reverchonii

Mercurialis reverchonii, commonly known as the creeping mercury.

It is a low-growing annual plant native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its slender stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant has hairy leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Due to its rapid growth and ability to spread, it is considered a weed in some regions.
