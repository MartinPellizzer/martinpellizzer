Maxillaria coniformis

Maxillaria coniformis, commonly known as the conical maxillaria, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, sympodial growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. The plant features elliptical, dark green leaves that are arranged in two ranks, and its pseudobulbs are short and thick. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil.

Maxillaria coniformis is prized for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate for orchid enthusiasts.
