Masdevallia trichaete

Masdevallia trichaete, commonly known as the hairy leek orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, strap-like leaves and produces striking, violet-blue flowers with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in cool, moist environments at high altitudes. Its unique hairy appearance on the leaves and stems gives it its common name.

Masdevallia trichaete is a prized species among orchid enthusiasts due to its ornamental value and rarity.
