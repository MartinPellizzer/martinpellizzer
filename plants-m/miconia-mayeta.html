Miconia mayeta

Miconia mayeta, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or red miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, bright red flowers that are followed by distinctive, winged fruits. Miconia mayeta is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

Its ornamental value has made it popular in horticulture, though it can become invasive in certain regions.
