Meistera botryoidea

Meistera botryoidea, commonly known as the clustered arrowroot, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features a rhizomatous root system and produces large, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that are followed by edible tubers resembling those of the arrowroot. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible roots.

Meistera botryoidea is a member of the Araceae family, which includes other plants like caladium and philodendron.
