Malvaviscus cuspidatus

Malvaviscus cuspidatus, commonly known as the scarlet bower vine, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant is characterized by its vibrant red or orange tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
