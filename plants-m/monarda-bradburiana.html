Monarda bradburiana

Monarda bradburiana, commonly known as the Bradburian beeplant, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant features showy, tubular red or orange flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly fuzzy texture, often with a strong mint-like scent.

Monarda bradburiana is typically found in moist woodlands and is valued for its ecological benefits and ornamental appeal.
