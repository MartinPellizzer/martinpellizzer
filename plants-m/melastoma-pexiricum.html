Melastoma pexiricum

Melastoma pexiricum, commonly known as the red-veined strobilanthes, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Melastomataceae and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories and mountainous regions. Its leaves are usually opposite and have a leathery texture, while its stems are often hairy.

Melastoma pexiricum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
