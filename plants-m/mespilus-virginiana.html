Mespilus virginiana

Mespilus virginiana, commonly known as the American persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, simple, and ovate leaves. The plant produces yellow-orange berries that ripen in the fall, containing a single seed surrounded by a sweet, edible pulp. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
