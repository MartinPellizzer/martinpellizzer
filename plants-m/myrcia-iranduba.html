Myrcia iranduba

Myrcia iranduba, commonly known as the iranduba myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Amazon rainforest of South America, particularly in regions of Brazil, Peru, and Colombia. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds.

The plant is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal uses.
