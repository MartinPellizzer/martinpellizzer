Melampodium panamense

Melampodium panamense, commonly known as Panama ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Melampodium panamense is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
