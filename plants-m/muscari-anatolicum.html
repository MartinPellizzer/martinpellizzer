Muscari anatolicum

Muscari anatolicum, commonly known as the Anatolian grape hyacinth, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue to violet in color. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders. Its bulbs are hardy and can be planted in the fall for spring blooming.

Muscari anatolicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to naturalize over time.
