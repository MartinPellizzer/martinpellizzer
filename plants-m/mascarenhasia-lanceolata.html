Mascarenhasia lanceolata

Mascarenhasia lanceolata, commonly known as the Mascarene fern, is a species of flowering plant native to the Mascarene Islands in the Indian Ocean.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds and small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in coastal areas or on limestone soils. Its botanical structure includes a rhizomatous root system and compound leaves with a pinnate arrangement.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
