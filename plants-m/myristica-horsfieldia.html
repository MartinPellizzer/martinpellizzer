Myristica horsfieldia

Myristica horsfieldia, commonly known as the Horsfield's nutmeg, is a flowering plant belonging to the Myristicaceae family.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that contains aromatic seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and economic contexts.
