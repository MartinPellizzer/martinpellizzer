Munroa squarrosa var. floccuosa

Munroa squarrosa var. floccuosa, commonly known as the woolly wallaby tree, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive, fluffy appearance. The leaves are typically grayish-green and covered in fine, soft hairs. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, spherical fruits.

This plant is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand drought and poor soil conditions.
