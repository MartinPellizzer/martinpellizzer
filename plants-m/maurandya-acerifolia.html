Maurandya acerifolia

Maurandya acerifolia, commonly known as the mapleleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its palmate, maple-like leaves that are deeply lobed and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink and purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
