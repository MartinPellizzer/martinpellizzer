Miconia trinitensis

Miconia trinitensis, commonly known as the Trinidad miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in coastal forests and wetlands.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in tropical gardens.
