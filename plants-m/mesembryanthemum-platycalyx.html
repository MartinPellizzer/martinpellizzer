Mesembryanthemum platycalyx

Mesembryanthemum platycalyx, commonly known as the rock rose, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family, known for its characteristic adaptations to arid environments. The plant features fleshy, ribbed leaves that store water, and it produces vibrant, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow and orange. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its ability to survive in harsh conditions makes it an excellent example of xerophytic adaptation.
