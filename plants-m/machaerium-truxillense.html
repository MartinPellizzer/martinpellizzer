Machaerium truxillense

Machaerium truxillense, commonly known as the Colombian machaerium, is a tropical perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming during the rainy season. Its root system is well-developed, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-poor soils, and it is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Machaerium truxillense is also valued for its potential in soil stabilization and as a forage plant for livestock.
