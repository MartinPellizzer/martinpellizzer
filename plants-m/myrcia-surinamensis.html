Myrcia surinamensis

Myrcia surinamensis, commonly known as Surinam myrtle, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruits are small, red berries that contain several seeds.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its aromatic properties.
