Micranthes subapetala

Micranthes subapetala, commonly known as the small-flowered waterleaf, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features small, rounded leaves that are typically heart-shaped with a smooth margin. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive addition to native plant gardens and wetland restoration projects.
