Myristica madagascariensis

Myristica madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar nutmeg tree, is a small evergreen tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic seeds, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant produces a distinctive reddish-brown fruit that contains a single seed, known for its high oil content and strong fragrance. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a leathery texture, while its flowers are small and inconspicuous.

This species is valued not only for its economic importance but also for its ecological role in its native habitat.
