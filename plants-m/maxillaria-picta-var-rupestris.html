Maxillaria picta var. rupestris

Maxillaria picta var. rupestris, commonly known as the Rock Orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a pseudobulb that supports a single, erect leaf. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with intricate patterns. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate in warm, humid environments.
