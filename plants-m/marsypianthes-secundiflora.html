Marsypianthes secundiflora

Marsypianthes secundiflora, commonly known as the slender-flowered marsh plant, is a herbaceous species native to wetland environments in Southeast Asia.

It features slender, erect stems that often grow up to 60 cm in height, adorned with small, linear leaves that are usually less than 2 cm in length. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in dense, terminal spikes, which bloom during the rainy season. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in waterlogged soils, making it an important component of aquatic ecosystems.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat preferences, it is often used in ecological restoration projects in its native range.
