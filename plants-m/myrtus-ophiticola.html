Myrtus ophiticola

Myrtus ophiticola, commonly known as the rock myrtle, is a flowering shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in rocky or coastal areas and is often used in hedges or as an ornamental plant.

Myrtus ophiticola is also valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
