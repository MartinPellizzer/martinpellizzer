Marica helenae

Marica helenae, commonly known as the Helen's marica, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
