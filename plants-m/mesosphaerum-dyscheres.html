Mesosphaerum dyscheres

The plant has a short flowering period, typically occurring at night, and is pollinated by specific species of moths. Its leaves are broad and waxy, adapted to retain moisture in its humid, shaded habitat.

Due to its rarity and ecological significance, Mesosphaerum dyscheres is considered a conservation priority.
