Marlea griffithii

Marlea griffithii, commonly known as Griffith's marigold, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming dense clumps.

Marlea griffithii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
