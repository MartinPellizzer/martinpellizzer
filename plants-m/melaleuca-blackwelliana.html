Melaleuca blackwelliana

Melaleuca blackwelliana, commonly known as the Blackwell's bottlebrush, is a small to medium-sized shrub native to the coastal regions of Queensland, Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bottlebrush-like flowers, which are typically pink to red in color and appear in dense clusters. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal and sandy environments.

Melaleuca blackwelliana is also known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
