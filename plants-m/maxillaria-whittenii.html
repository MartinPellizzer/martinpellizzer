Maxillaria whittenii

Maxillaria whittenii, commonly known as the Whitten's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and green, often with a distinctive red or orange lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Maxillaria whittenii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
