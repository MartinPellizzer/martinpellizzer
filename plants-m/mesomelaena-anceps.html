Mesomelaena anceps

Mesomelaena anceps, commonly known as the Cape goldenbush, is a shrub native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, yellowish-green foliage and clusters of small, golden-yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has small, leathery leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

Mesomelaena anceps is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and drought tolerance.
