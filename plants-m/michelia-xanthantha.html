Michelia xanthantha

Michelia xanthantha, commonly known as the golden michelia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has simple, opposite, ovate leaves that are dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

Michelia xanthantha is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional Chinese medicine.
