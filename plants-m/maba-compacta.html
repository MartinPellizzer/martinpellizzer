Maba compacta

Maba compacta, commonly known as the African mahogany, is a tropical tree native to regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood, which is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic appeal. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a glossy surface. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody fruits containing seeds.

The tree plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
