Meconopsis nyingchiensis

Meconopsis nyingchiensis, commonly known as the Nyingchi meconopsis, is a striking alpine plant native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its large, showy blue or violet flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in high-altitude meadows and rocky slopes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of white pubescence, which helps to reflect sunlight.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to its harsh environment.
