Mespilus myrtifolia

Mespilus myrtifolia, commonly known as the myrtleleaf medlar, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, dark green leaves and small, edible fruits. The plant produces white to pinkish flowers in early spring, which are followed by the distinctive, milky latex-filled fruits. Its botanical features include a rough, bark-like stem and a tendency to develop a bushy growth habit.

Mespilus myrtifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
