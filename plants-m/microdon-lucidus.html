Microdon lucidus

Microdon lucidus, commonly known as the glossy microdon, is a small flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy, glossy coating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Microdon lucidus thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in humid forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for studies on plant adaptation and biodiversity in tropical ecosystems.
