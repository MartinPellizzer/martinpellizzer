Mammillaria plumosa

Mammillaria plumosa, commonly known as the feathered hedgehog cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered with white, woolly spines that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a vibrant touch to its otherwise spiky form. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique morphology and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
