Mickelia pergamentacea

Mickelia pergamentacea, commonly known as the parchment leaf cycad, is a unique cycad species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, leathery, fan-shaped leaves that resemble parchment, hence its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically pollinated by insects. Its slow growth and longevity make it a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, Mickelia pergamentacea is classified as critically endangered.
