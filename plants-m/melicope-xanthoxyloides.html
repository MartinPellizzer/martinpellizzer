Melicope xanthoxyloides

Melicope xanthoxyloides, commonly known as the yellow sandalwood, is a member of the Rutaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its aromatic, smooth bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, followed by elliptical, woody fruits containing several seeds. Its wood is valued for its fine grain and fragrance, making it a sought-after material in traditional crafts and incense.

Melicope xanthoxyloides thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and hill forests.
