Medicago falcata subsp. erecta

Medicago falcata subsp.

erecta, commonly known as the erect sickle vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by elongated, bean-like pods. It has a deep root system that helps in soil stabilization and nitrogen fixation.

This subspecies is often found in disturbed soils and is used in forage and erosion control.
