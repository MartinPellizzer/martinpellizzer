Melaleuca interioris

Melaleuca interioris, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tea tree, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia, where it typically grows in coastal heathlands and sandy soils. The plant is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and small, fragrant white or pink flowers that appear in winter. It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and has some historical use in traditional medicine.

Melaleuca interioris is also valued for its ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
