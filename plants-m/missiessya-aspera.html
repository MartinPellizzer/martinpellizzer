Missiessya aspera

Missiessya aspera, commonly known as the roughleaf mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features hairy, ovate leaves that are often tinged with reddish or purple hues, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fibrous root system helps it thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

Missiessya aspera is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
