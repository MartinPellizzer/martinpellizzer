Mikania stipulacea

Mikania stipulacea, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a fast-growing vine native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant is known for its rapid spread and can become invasive in certain regions. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural and natural settings.
