Microchilus reddeniae

Microchilus reddeniae, commonly known as the Redden's orchid, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its small, intricate flowers that typically display vibrant hues of purple and white. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil to flourish. Its unique floral structure and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
