Micromystria nasturtium var. pinnatifida

Micromystria nasturtium var. pinnatifida, commonly known as the narrow-leaf watercress, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves with a delicate, peppery flavor, making it a popular culinary herb. The plant produces yellow flowers with four petals, typical of the Brassicaceae family. Its leaves are often used in salads and as a garnish due to their nutritional value and mild taste.

It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found near streams or in wetlands.
