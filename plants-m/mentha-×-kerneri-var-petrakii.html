Mentha × kerneri var. petrakii

Mentha × kerneri var. petrakii, commonly known as the Petrak's mint, is a hybrid mint species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly serrated leaves and clusters of small, pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported digestive and calming properties.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both horticultural and medicinal purposes.
