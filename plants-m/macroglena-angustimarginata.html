Macroglena angustimarginata

Macroglena angustimarginata, commonly known as the narrow-margin orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and linear, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically arranged in a raceme. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Botanically, it belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is noted for its unique floral structure and adaptability to moist, nutrient-rich soils.
