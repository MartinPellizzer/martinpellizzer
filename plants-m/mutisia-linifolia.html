Mutisia linifolia

Mutisia linifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf mutisia, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and its showy yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has a woody stem and is often found in dry, rocky habitats. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, white hairs that help reduce water loss in arid environments.
