Mormodes tigrina

Mormodes tigrina, commonly known as the tiger orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that resemble tiger stripes. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and succulent, adapted to store water in its native habitat.

The flowers bloom in clusters and are pollinated primarily by insects and small birds.
