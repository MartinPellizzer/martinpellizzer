Mallotus philippensis var. reticulatus

Mallotus philippensis var. reticulatus, commonly known as the net-veined mallot, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub with elliptical leaves that have a distinctive net-like venation pattern. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by yellowish or reddish drupes. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland forests and secondary growth areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
