Magnolia brasiliensis

Magnolia brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its large, showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring, often before the leaves emerge. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a smooth texture with a dark green upper surface. The tree can grow up to 25 meters tall, with a broad, spreading crown.

Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.
