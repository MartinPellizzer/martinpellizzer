Mespilus canadensis

Mespilus canadensis, commonly known as the American persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, simple, and ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in the spring, followed by fruit that ripens to a deep orange-red color in the fall. The fruit is edible when fully ripe and has a sweet, honey-like flavor.

It is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses.
