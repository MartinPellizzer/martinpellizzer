Malva stenopetala

Malva stenopetala, commonly known as the narrow-petaled mallow, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, lobed leaves and showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes other flowering plants such as cotton and hibiscus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Its flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological diversity.
