Mesembryanthemum patens

Mesembryanthemum patens, commonly known as the silver leaf mesembryanthemum, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, silver-green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white powder, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is a popular choice for low-maintenance landscaping.
