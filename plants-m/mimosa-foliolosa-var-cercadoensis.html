Mimosa foliolosa var. cercadoensis

Mimosa foliolosa var. cercadoensis, commonly known as the Cercado mimosa, is a perennial shrub native to South America, particularly found in regions of Brazil and Peru.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant has a woody stem with thorny branches, and its leaves are bipinnate, meaning they are divided into multiple small leaflets.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in dry, open habitats such as savannas and rocky areas.
