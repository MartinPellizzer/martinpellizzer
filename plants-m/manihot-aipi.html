Manihot aipi

Manihot aipi, commonly known as arrowleaf groundcherry, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in local cuisines. Its botanical features include hairy stems and a fibrous root system.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for both its nutritional value and ornamental appeal.
