Mimulus serotinus

Mimulus serotinus, commonly known as the late yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to western North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. The plant produces showy, orange-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. Its scientific name reflects its late blooming habit, as "serotinus" means "late-blooming" in Latin.

The flowers are tubular with two lips, a characteristic feature of the Mimulus genus.
