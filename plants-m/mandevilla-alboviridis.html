Mandevilla alboviridis

Mandevilla alboviridis, commonly known as the white-veined mandevilla, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, waxy leaves with white veins against a deep green background. The plant produces clusters of trumpet-shaped, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated as a decorative vine or shrub.

Mandevilla alboviridis is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for gardeners and landscapers.
