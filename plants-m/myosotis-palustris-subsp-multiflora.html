Myosotis palustris subsp. multiflora

Myosotis palustris subsp.

multiflora, commonly known as the marsh forget-me-not, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe. It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, blue to violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, such as along stream banks or in damp meadows. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators in wetland ecosystems.
