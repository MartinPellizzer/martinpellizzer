Melodinus guillauminii

Melodinus guillauminii, commonly known as the Guillaumin's melody vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible and have a sweet, tropical flavor. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant in tropical regions.
