Microtropis gagei

Microtropis gagei, commonly known as the Gage's microtropis, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
