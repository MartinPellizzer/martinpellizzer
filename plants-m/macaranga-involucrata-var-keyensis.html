Macaranga involucrata var. keyensis

Macaranga involucrata var. keyensis, commonly known as the Key Islands Macaranga, is a shrub or small tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its alternate, simple, and often elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges, thriving in moist, well-drained soils.

The species is notable for its rapid growth and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
