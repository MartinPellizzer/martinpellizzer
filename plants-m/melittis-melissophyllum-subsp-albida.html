Melittis melissophyllum subsp. albida

Melittis melissophyllum subsp.

albida, commonly known as the white-flowered melittis, is a perennial herb native to specific regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, clustered white flowers that bloom during the cooler months.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or woodland settings.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
