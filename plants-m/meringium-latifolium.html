Meringium latifolium

Meringium latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf meringium, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is a popular choice among gardeners.
