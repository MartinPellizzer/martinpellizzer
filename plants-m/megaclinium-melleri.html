Megaclinium melleri

Megaclinium melleri, commonly known as the Meller's giant grass, is a tall, perennial grass native to the high altitudes of South America.

It is characterized by its robust, erect growth and long, narrow leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces large, feathery inflorescences that are often used in floral arrangements due to their striking appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Due to its size and visual impact, it is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in landscaping.
