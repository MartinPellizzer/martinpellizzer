Mammillaria cordigera

Mammillaria cordigera, commonly known as the heart-shaped mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rounded, ribbed stems that are typically green to blue-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers during the spring and summer months. It has a compact growth habit and is often grown as a decorative houseplant.

Due to its slow growth and unique appearance, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
