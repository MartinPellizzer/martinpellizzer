Mespilus monogyna proles brevispina

Mespilus monogyna proles brevispina, commonly known as the European whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The tree has simple, alternate leaves that are oval-shaped with serrated edges. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that ripens to a yellowish color in late summer.

The bark is smooth and gray, becoming fissured with age, and the tree is known for its ecological importance in supporting various wildlife species.
