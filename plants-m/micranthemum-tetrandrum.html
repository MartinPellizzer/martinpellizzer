Micranthemum tetrandrum

Micranthemum tetrandrum, commonly known as water starwort, is a small aquatic plant native to the Americas.

It typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and ditches. The plant has tiny, opposite leaves that are usually less than 1 centimeter in length. Its flowers are small and clustered, with four stamens, which is reflected in its scientific name.

It plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates.
