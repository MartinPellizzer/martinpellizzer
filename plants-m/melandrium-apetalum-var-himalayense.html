Melandrium apetalum var. himalayense

Melandrium apetalum var. himalayense, commonly known as the Himalayan sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, and it produces capsules that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in rocky and sandy soils, often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
