Mikania papillosa

Mikania papillosa, commonly known as the hairy morning glory, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Due to its rapid growth, it can become invasive in certain regions.
