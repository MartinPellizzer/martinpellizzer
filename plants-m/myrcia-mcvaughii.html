Myrcia mcvaughii

Myrcia mcvaughii, commonly known as the McVaugh's myrtle, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruits are small, red berries that contain several seeds, and the plant is often used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.

Myrcia mcvaughii thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
