Malva hortensus

Malva hortensus, commonly known as the common mallow, is a flowering plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is native to Europe and has been naturalized in various parts of the world. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in gardens and along roadsides.

Malva hortensus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
