Malleola luongii

Malleola luongii, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight. Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Malleola luongii is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
