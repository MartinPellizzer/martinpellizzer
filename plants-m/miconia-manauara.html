Miconia manauara

Miconia manauara, commonly known as the Amazonian beautyberry, is a tropical shrub native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the wet season. Its fruit is a bright blue berry that contains several seeds, making it an important food source for various bird species.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
