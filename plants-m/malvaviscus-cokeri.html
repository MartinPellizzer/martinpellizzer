Malvaviscus cokeri

Malvaviscus cokeri, commonly known as the red mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant is characterized by its showy, red bracts that resemble flowers, though they are actually modified leaves. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall.
