Meconopsis aculeata var. typica

Meconopsis aculeata var. typica, commonly known as the Himalayan blue poppy, is a striking alpine plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue to violet petals, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, thriving in cool and moist conditions. Its leaves are palmately lobed and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Meconopsis aculeata var. typica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens that replicate its natural habitat.
