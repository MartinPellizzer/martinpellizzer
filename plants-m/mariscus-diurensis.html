Mariscus diurensis

Mariscus diurensis, commonly known as the diuretic marsh plant, is a species of flowering plant native to wetland environments in Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cucurbitaceae, which includes cucumbers and gourds, and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by edible, cucumber-like fruits. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a mild, slightly sweet aroma.

Mariscus diurensis is noted for its diuretic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
