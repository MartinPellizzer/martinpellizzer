Melaleuca phratra

Melaleuca phratra, commonly known as the narrow-leaf paperbark, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark that reveals a fibrous inner layer, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters, typically blooming in spring and summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine, silky hairs.

Melaleuca phratra is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in coastal and sandy areas.
