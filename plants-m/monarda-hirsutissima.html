Monarda hirsutissima

Monarda hirsutissima, commonly known as rough horsemint, is a perennial herb native to the eastern and central United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant features opposite, hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, and purple. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Monarda hirsutissima is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
