Melaleuca linearis var. pinifolia

Melaleuca linearis var. pinifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf melaleuca, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern Australia.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a whitish or greyish powdery coating, known as a bloom. The plant produces small, bottle-shaped flowers that are typically red or pink, which appear in clusters during the spring and summer months. It is adapted to coastal and sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal scrub.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
