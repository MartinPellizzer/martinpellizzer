Micromeria graeca var. latifolia

Micromeria graeca var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale blue or violet in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
