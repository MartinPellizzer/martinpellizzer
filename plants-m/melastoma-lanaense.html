Melastoma lanaense

Melastoma lanaense, commonly known as the hairy melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to red bracteate flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually opposite and have a rough texture, while its stems are often covered in fine hairs.

Melastoma lanaense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
