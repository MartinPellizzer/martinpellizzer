Matricaria albidiformis

Matricaria albidiformis, commonly known as white-flowered chamomile, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in temperate climates. The plant features daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center, and it has finely divided, feathery leaves. It is often cultivated for its aromatic properties and used in herbal remedies.

Matricaria albidiformis is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
