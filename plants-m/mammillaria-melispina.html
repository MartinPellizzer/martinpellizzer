Mammillaria melispina

Mammillaria melispina, commonly known as the Mexican milk barrel cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a small, globular cactus with a ribbed structure and is characterized by its white, woolly spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are often followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance, it is a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts.
