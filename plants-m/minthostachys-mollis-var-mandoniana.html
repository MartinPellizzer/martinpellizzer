Minthostachys mollis var. mandoniana

Minthostachys mollis var. mandoniana, commonly known as the Mandon's mint, is a perennial herb native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic and often medicinal properties. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture. Its small, tubular flowers are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorled clusters along the stems.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its fragrant foliage and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
