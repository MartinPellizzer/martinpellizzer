Mattia lateriflorum

Mattia lateriflorum, commonly known as the hanging flower or the cascading beauty, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous inflorescences that dangle from its stems, creating a dramatic visual effect. The plant produces small, brightly colored flowers, typically in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom sequentially along the stems. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence.

Mattia lateriflorum thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.
