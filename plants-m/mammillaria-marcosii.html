Mammillaria marcosii

Mammillaria marcosii, commonly known as the Marcosii Hedgehog Cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a small, globular cactus with a distinctive appearance, featuring short, spiny ribs and a dense covering of white to pale blue bristles. The plant typically grows to a height of about 5 to 10 centimeters and has a compact, rounded shape. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or orange, appearing in late spring or early summer.

It is well-suited for rock gardens and requires well-draining soil and full sun to thrive.
