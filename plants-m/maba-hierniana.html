Maba hierniana

Maba hierniana, commonly known as the African maba tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in catkins, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is often found in open woodlands and is valued for its timber and traditional uses.

The tree plays an important role in local ecosystems and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
