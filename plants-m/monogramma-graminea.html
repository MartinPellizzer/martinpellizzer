Monogramma graminea

Monogramma graminea, commonly known as the slender rock fern, is a member of the Blechnaceae family.

It is a small, terrestrial fern that typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as rocky crevices and forest floors. The plant has delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are often covered in a fine layer of silvery hairs. Its sporangia are clustered in sori along the margins of the fronds, a characteristic feature of many ferns.

Monogramma graminea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable humidity and light conditions.
