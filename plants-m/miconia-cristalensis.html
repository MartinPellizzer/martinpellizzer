Miconia cristalensis

Miconia cristalensis, commonly known as the crystalline miconia, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests, where it thrives in high humidity and warm temperatures. Miconia cristalensis is also noted for its rapid growth rate and ability to form dense thickets, which can impact local ecosystems.
