Macaranga gmelinifolia

Macaranga gmelinifolia, commonly known as the white-leaf ghost tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine white waxy coating. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed areas, it plays a significant role in ecological succession.
