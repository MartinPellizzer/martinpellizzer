Myrtus obovata

Myrtus obovata, commonly known as the evergreen myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, glossy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and slightly fragrant. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, which are followed by dark blue, edible berries. Myrtus obovata is valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in traditional medicine and as a decorative plant.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile addition to gardens.
