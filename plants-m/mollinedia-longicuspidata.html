Mollinedia longicuspidata

Mollinedia longicuspidata, commonly known as the long-pointed bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and tall, slender stems that bear showy, tubular flowers. The flowers are typically blue to violet in color and are arranged in clusters at the top of the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or swampy areas.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
