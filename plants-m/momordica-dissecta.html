Momordica dissecta

Momordica dissecta, commonly known as the dissected bitter gourd, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its deeply lobed, heart-shaped leaves and yellow-orange flowers. The fruit is typically elongated, with a ridged surface and a bitter taste, though it can be edible when mature. This plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like cucumbers and pumpkins.
