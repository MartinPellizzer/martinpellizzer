Medinilla venusta

Medinilla venusta, commonly known as the velvet tangerine or velvet flower, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, waxy flowers that range in color from pink to white. The plant produces small, orange fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed. Medinilla venusta thrives in warm, humid climates and is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance.

It is also valued for its ability to bloom year-round under proper care.
