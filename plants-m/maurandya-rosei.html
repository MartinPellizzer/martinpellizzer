Maurandya rosei

Maurandya rosei, commonly known as the rose mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 3 feet and prefers moist, well-drained soil. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Maurandya rosei is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
