Musa itinerans var. xishuangbannaensis

Musa itinerans var. xishuangbannaensis, commonly known as the Xishuangbanna banana, is a variant of the banana plant native to the Yunnan Province in China.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length, and it produces clusters of elongated, yellowish-green fruits. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. It is valued not only for its edible fruit but also for its ornamental qualities.

This variety is important for both local agriculture and botanical research due to its unique genetic traits.
