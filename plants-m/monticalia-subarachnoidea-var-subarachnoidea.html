Monticalia subarachnoidea var. subarachnoidea

Monticalia subarachnoidea var. subarachnoidea, commonly known as the spider plant or spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hanging clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pink in color. The plant has long, slender stems that produce aerial roots, which help it climb and attach to surfaces. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slightly hairy texture.

This species is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of growing conditions.
