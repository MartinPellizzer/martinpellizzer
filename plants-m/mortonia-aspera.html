Mortonia aspera

Mortonia aspera, commonly known as the rough-mortonia, is a perennial shrub native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It features dark green, elliptical leaves with a rough, textured surface that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers in clusters during the late spring to early summer. Its bark is thick and scaly, providing a distinctive appearance.

Mortonia aspera is known for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal remedies for respiratory ailments.
