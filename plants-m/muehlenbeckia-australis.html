Muehlenbeckia australis

Muehlenbeckia australis, commonly known as the Australian silverweed, is a low-growing, perennial shrub native to Australia.

It features small, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, oval-shaped fruits. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and full sun to partial shade.

Muehlenbeckia australis is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
