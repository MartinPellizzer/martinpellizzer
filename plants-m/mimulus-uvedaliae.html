Mimulus uvedaliae

Mimulus uvedaliae, commonly known as the Sierra primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow-orange flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. It has heart-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

Mimulus uvedaliae is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
