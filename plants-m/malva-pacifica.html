Malva pacifica

Malva pacifica, commonly known as the Pacific mallows, is a flowering plant native to western North America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks.

Malva pacifica is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
