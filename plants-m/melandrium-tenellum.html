Melandrium tenellum

Melandrium tenellum, commonly known as the common bladder campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

The fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, rounded seeds.
