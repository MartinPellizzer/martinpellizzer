Mecodium lineatum

Mecodium lineatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mecodium, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
