Magnolia rajaniana

Magnolia rajaniana, commonly known as the Rajaniana magnolia, is a rare and endemic species native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas, often at elevations between 1,000 and 2,000 meters. Its leaves are broad and elliptical, with a smooth surface and a glossy appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and is also of interest for botanical research due to its unique genetic makeup and ecological niche.
