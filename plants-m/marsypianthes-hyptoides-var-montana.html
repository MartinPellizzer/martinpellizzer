Marsypianthes hyptoides var. montana

Marsypianthes hyptoides var. montana, commonly known as the mountain Marsypianthes, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant produces delicate, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow, slightly hairy, and adapted to withstand cold and windy conditions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in alpine environments.
