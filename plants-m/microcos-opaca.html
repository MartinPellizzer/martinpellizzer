Microcos opaca

Microcos opaca, commonly known as the black microcos, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

The species is notable for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
