Miconia cancellata

Miconia cancellata, commonly known as the Brazilian starfruit or white miconia, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is a large shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its broad, palmate leaves that are often covered in a whitish, waxy coating. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in yellow or greenish hues.

The fruit is a fleshy, yellow-orange berry that contains numerous small seeds.
