Microstegium butuoense

Microstegium butuoense, commonly known as the Butuo grass, is a perennial grass species native to China.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its inflorescence consists of a spikelet cluster, and it produces small, two-lobed flowers.

Microstegium butuoense is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
