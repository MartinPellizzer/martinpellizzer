Malvinda cordifolia

Malvinda cordifolia, commonly known as heartleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, pink or purple flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or swampy areas.

Malvinda cordifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in some cultures.
