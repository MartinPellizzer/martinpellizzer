Melaleuca montis-zamia

Melaleuca montis-zamia, commonly known as the Zamia melaleuca, is a rare shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species.
