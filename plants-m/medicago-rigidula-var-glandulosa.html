Medicago rigidula var. glandulosa

Medicago rigidula var. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular medick, is a perennial legume native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and trifoliate leaves with glandular hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in dense, globular clusters. Its root system is capable of fixing atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.

It thrives in a variety of habitats, including grasslands, disturbed soils, and along roadsides.
