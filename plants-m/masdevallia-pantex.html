Masdevallia pantex

Masdevallia pantex, commonly known as the Pantex masdevallia, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and intricate floral structure. The plant typically produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom during the cooler months. Its pseudobulbs are short and stout, supporting the plant's foliage and floral display.

Masdevallia pantex thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil for optimal growth.
