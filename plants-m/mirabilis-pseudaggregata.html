Mirabilis pseudaggregata

Mirabilis pseudaggregata, commonly known as the redflower four o'clock, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that open in the late afternoon and close at dawn. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with slender, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are tubular and produce a distinctive, fragrant scent.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
