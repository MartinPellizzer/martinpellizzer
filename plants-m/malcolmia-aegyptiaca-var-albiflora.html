Malcolmia aegyptiaca var. albiflora

Malcolmia aegyptiaca var. albiflora, commonly known as white Egyptian malcolmia, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the Mediterranean and Middle East.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves and produces showy, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping. Its flowers are typically tubular with five petals, and it is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal.

The species is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
