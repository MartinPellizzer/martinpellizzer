Medicago romanica var. linifolia

Medicago romanica var. linifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf sainfoin, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers that bloom in shades of pink to purple. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a useful plant in agricultural settings.

Additionally, it is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and low maintenance requirements.
