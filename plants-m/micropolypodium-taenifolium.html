Micropolypodium taenifolium

Micropolypodium taenifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf polypody, is a small evergreen fern native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It typically grows as a lithophyte, meaning it attaches itself to rocks, often in shaded, moist environments. The plant has delicate, linear leaves that are usually less than 10 centimeters in length, with a distinct, serrated margin. Its rhizome is short and produces sporangia on the underside of the fronds, which release spores for reproduction.

Micropolypodium taenifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded garden settings.
