Myrcia killipii

Myrcia killipii, commonly known as the Killip's myrtle, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are glossy and leathery in texture. Its small, white flowers are fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.
