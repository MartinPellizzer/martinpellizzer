Melastoma haenkeanum

Melastoma haenkeanum, commonly known as the purple flame or blue mist shrub, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to blue. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with opposite, ovate leaves that have a glossy surface. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

The flowers attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, making it an important species in its ecosystem.
