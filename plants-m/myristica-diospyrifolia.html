Myristica diospyrifolia

Myristica diospyrifolia, commonly known as the Java nut or nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves and fragrant, yellowish flowers. The plant produces an edible seed, known as the nutmeg, which is enclosed in a hard, woody fruit. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its aromatic seeds and ornamental value.

Myristica diospyrifolia is also valued for its essential oils and has traditional medicinal uses in various cultures.
