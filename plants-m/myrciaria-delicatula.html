Myrciaria delicatula

Myrciaria delicatula, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or araçá-do-mato, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces bright red, fleshy berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping, while its ecological importance supports various wildlife species.
