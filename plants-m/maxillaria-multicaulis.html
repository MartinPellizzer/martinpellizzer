Maxillaria multicaulis

Maxillaria multicaulis, commonly known as the clustered maxillaria, is a tropical orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers. The plant typically features long, strap-like leaves and has a pseudobulb structure typical of many orchids. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive lip that is often darker in color.

Maxillaria multicaulis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
