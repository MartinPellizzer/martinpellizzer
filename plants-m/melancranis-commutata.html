Melancranis commutata

Melancranis commutata, commonly known as the commutated melancranis, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the family Melancranidaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically lance-shaped. The plant produces delicate, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded woodland areas.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native range.
