Myrcia silvatica

Myrcia silvatica, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a strong, aromatic scent.

The flowers are small, white or pale pink, and are borne in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bees and birds.
