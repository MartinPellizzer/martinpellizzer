Memecylon pervilleanum

Memecylon pervilleanum, commonly known as the white memecylon, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Melastomataceae and is characterized by its opposite, elliptical leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds.

It typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
