Masdevallia molossus

Masdevallia molossus, commonly known as the Andean orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments at high altitudes. The plant features a short, horizontal rhizome with a dense cluster of linear leaves. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, often displaying a range of colors including white, pink, and purple.

Masdevallia molossus is appreciated for its unique appearance and is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value.
