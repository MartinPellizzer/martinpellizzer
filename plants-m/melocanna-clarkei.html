Melocanna clarkei

Melocanna clarkei, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, narrow leaves. The plant can grow up to 20 meters in height, making it one of the tallest bamboo species. Its rhizomes are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines.
