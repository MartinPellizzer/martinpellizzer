Meum diffusum

Meum diffusum, commonly known as the common meadow vetcher, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its creeping growth habit and trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. Meum diffusum is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in grassy areas and along roadsides.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides nectar for pollinators.
