Microstegium vagans var. scandens

Microstegium vagans var. scandens, commonly known as the climbing false panicgrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems that twine around supports, often found in moist woodlands and along stream banks. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a grass-like appearance, and it typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters.

This species is often considered an invasive plant in some regions due to its rapid growth and ability to outcompete native vegetation.
