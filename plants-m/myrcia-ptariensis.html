Myrcia ptariensis

Myrcia ptariensis, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the Atlantic rainforests of Brazil and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and fragrant, white or pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are glossy and elliptical, while its fruit is a small, woody capsule containing aromatic seeds.

Myrcia ptariensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
