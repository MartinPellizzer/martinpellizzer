Macaranga calcicola var. calcifuga

Macaranga calcicola var. calcifuga, commonly known as the limestone macaranga, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, alternate leaves that are often gland-dotted and have a distinctive, rough texture. The plant thrives in rocky or limestone soils, showcasing its adaptability to nutrient-poor environments. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by oval, woody fruits containing numerous seeds.

This species is often found in disturbed habitats and is valued for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
