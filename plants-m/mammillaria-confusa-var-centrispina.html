Mammillaria confusa var. centrispina

Mammillaria confusa var. centrispina, commonly known as the Centrispina Mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and is covered in small, white to yellow spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a houseplant.
