Mentha sylvestris subsp. procurrens

Mentha sylvestris subsp.

procurrens, commonly known as wood mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a serrated margin and emits a strong, minty fragrance. Its flowering stems bear small, tubular flowers that are usually pale blue or purple.

This subspecies is valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in herbal remedies and culinary applications.
