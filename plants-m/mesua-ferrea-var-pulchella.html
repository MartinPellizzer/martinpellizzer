Mesua ferrea var. pulchella

Mesua ferrea var. pulchella, commonly known as the pink flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking pink to red floral bracts that resemble flames, which appear in clusters at the ends of branches. The tree has smooth, greyish-brown bark and elliptical, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and urban landscapes due to its vibrant floral display.
