Miconia poecilantha

Miconia poecilantha, commonly known as the variable miconia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Melastomataceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its large, broad leaves and showy, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
