Malaxis leucodon

Malaxis leucodon, commonly known as the white-lipped orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Southeast Asia, including China, India, and Indonesia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are linear and arranged in a basal rosette, and it produces a single, racemose inflorescence.

Malaxis leucodon is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its delicate floral structure and ecological significance in its native habitats.
