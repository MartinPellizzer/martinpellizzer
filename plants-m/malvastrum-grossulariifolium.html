Malvastrum grossulariifolium

Malvastrum grossulariifolium, commonly known as the common mallow, is a flowering plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and features showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has palmately lobed leaves with a velvety texture, and its stems are hairy and somewhat woody. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and waste areas.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
