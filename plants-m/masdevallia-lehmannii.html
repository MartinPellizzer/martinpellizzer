Masdevallia lehmannii

Masdevallia lehmannii, commonly known as the Lehmann's masdevallia, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments at high elevations. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces intricate, fragrant flowers with a distinctive coloration pattern. Its floral structure includes a spurred lip, a characteristic feature of many orchids.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its natural range.
