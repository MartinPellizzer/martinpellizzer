Marsdenia nana

Marsdenia nana, commonly known as the dwarf Marsdenia, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its compact growth habit and fleshy, aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, which bloom in the summer months. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.

Marsdenia nana prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a versatile addition to gardens and indoor spaces.
