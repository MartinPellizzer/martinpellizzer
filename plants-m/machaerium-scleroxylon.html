Machaerium scleroxylon

Machaerium scleroxylon, commonly known as the African leadwood, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its durability. The plant has bipinnate leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in compact clusters. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand arid conditions.
