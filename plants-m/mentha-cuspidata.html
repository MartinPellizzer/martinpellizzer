Mentha cuspidata

Mentha cuspidata, commonly known as Japanese mint, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, square stems and opposite, serrated leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming in late summer. Mentha cuspidata is often cultivated for its fragrant leaves used in culinary and medicinal applications.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.
