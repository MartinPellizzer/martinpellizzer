Menodora pulchella

Menodora pulchella, commonly known as the pretty false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Menodoreaceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, and the stems are hairy as well.

Menodora pulchella is typically found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks.
