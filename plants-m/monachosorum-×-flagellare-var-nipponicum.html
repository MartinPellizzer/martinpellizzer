Monachosorum × flagellare var. nipponicum

Monachosorum × flagellare var. nipponicum, commonly known as the Japanese flag iris, is a hybrid plant that exhibits characteristics of its parent species, Monachosorum flagellare and Monachosorum nipponicum.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan, typically found in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests. The plant features long, narrow leaves and striking, sword-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its botanical significance lies in its role as a hybrid in the Iridaceae family, showcasing genetic diversity and adaptability.

The varietal form, var. nipponicum, is distinguished by its specific floral and foliar traits that set it apart from other varieties within the species.
