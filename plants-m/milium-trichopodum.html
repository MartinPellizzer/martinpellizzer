Milium trichopodum

Milium trichopodum, commonly known as hairy millet, is a small annual grass that belongs to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas. Its leaves are narrow and covered with fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Milium trichopodum is adapted to a variety of climates and is sometimes used as a forage crop in certain regions.
