Meridiana oxyloba

Meridiana oxyloba, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep pink, often with a yellow throat. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are typically hairy and have a rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Meridiana oxyloba is often found in cloud forests and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance.
