Megalastrum galeottii

Megalastrum galeottii, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshade plants. The plant produces small, round, yellow-orange fruits that are edible and have a tangy flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its fruit.

The leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
