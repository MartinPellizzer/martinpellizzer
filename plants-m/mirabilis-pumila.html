Mirabilis pumila

Mirabilis pumila, commonly known as the dwarf four o'clock, is a low-growing, herbaceous plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Nyctaginaceae family and is characterized by its small, showy flowers that open in the late afternoon. The plant typically features slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs and produces clusters of pink, red, or yellow flowers. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with a waxy coating.

Mirabilis pumila thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.
