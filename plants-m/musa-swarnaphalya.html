Musa swarnaphalya

Musa swarnaphalya, commonly known as the golden banana, is a tropical plant belonging to the Musaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, elongated leaves and clusters of yellow-orange fruits that ripen to a deep red. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its flowers are typically hidden within the fruit clusters, and the plant is known for its high nutritional value.

Musa swarnaphalya is cultivated for both its edible fruit and ornamental appeal.
