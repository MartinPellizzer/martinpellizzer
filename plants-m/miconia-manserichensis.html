Miconia manserichensis

Miconia manserichensis, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and showy, pinkish-white bracts. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are followed by red, fleshy fruits.

It is often found in moist, shaded areas of tropical rainforests and is known for its rapid growth and invasive potential in some regions.
