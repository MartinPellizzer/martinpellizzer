Miconia quintuplinervia

Miconia quintuplinervia, commonly known as the five-veined miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves with five prominent veins, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that are often followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Miconia quintuplinervia is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

It is also noted for its allelopathic properties, which can inhibit the growth of nearby plants.
