Mariscus luzuliformis

Mariscus luzuliformis, commonly known as the slender bog-moss, is a small evergreen shrub native to the wetlands of northern Europe and parts of Asia.

It thrives in acidic, nutrient-poor soils and is often found in peat bogs and swampy areas. The plant has compact, dark green leaves that are arranged in a dense, cushion-like formation. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or reddish in color.

Mariscus luzuliformis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
