Maytenus vitis-idaea f. grandifolia

Maytenus vitis-idaea f. grandifolia, commonly known as the grandifolia maytenus, is a variant of the maytenus genus that exhibits distinct botanical features.

It is a shrub or small tree native to certain regions of South America, particularly in the Andean foothills. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptic to ovate in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants, indicating dioecy.

The fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that matures to a red or orange color, attracting various bird species.
