Meum graecum

Meum graecum, commonly known as Greek burnet, is a flowering plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round, edible fruits that are often used in traditional herbal remedies. Its leaves are also used in culinary applications, particularly in Mediterranean cuisine.

Meum graecum thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.
