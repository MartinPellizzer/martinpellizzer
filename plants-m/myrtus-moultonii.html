Myrtus moultonii

Myrtus moultonii, commonly known as the Moulton myrtle, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and leathery, dark green leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for coastal and rocky habitats.

Myrtus moultonii is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and its ability to attract pollinators.
