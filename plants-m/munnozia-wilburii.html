Munnozia wilburii

Munnozia wilburii, commonly known as the Wilbur's globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its glandular, hairy leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Munnozia wilburii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
