Mundia scoparia

Mundia scoparia, commonly known as African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. The plant has a bushy growth habit with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
