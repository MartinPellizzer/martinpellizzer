Mariscus cyperoides subsp. africanus

Mariscus cyperoides subsp.

africanus, commonly known as African marsh grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils.

The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers. It is characterized by its ability to form dense mats, which help stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

This subspecies is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and environmental benefits.
