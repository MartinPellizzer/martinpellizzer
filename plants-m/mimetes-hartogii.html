Mimetes hartogii

Mimetes hartogii, commonly known as the Hartog's mimic orchid, is a striking South African plant belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which resembles the appearance of other flowers, a trait that likely serves to attract specific pollinators. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is found in the Western Cape region of South Africa. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to orange, with intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry.

Mimetes hartogii is a perennial herb that thrives in full sun and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native habitat.
