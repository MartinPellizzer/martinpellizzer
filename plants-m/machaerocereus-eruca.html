Machaerocereus eruca

Machaerocereus eruca, commonly known as the long-spined cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant features prominent, sharp spines that provide protection from herbivores. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, appearing in clusters at the top of the stems.

Machaerocereus eruca is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils.
