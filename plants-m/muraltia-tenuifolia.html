Muraltia tenuifolia

Muraltia tenuifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved muraltia, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It features slender, upright stems and small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces fragrant, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that turns from green to red as it matures.

Muraltia tenuifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
