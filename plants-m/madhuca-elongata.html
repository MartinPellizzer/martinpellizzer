Madhuca elongata

Madhuca elongata, commonly known as the long-fruited oil palm, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its dense, glossy green leaves and clustered inflorescences. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into elongated, woody fruits containing edible seeds. Its latex, known as copal, is used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural resin.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in tropical forests.
