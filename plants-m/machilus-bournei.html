Machilus bournei

Machilus bournei, commonly known as the Chinese tallowtree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to southern China.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped, glossy dark green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange-red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been introduced to other parts of the world.

However, it can become invasive in some regions, posing a threat to native ecosystems.
