Mentha × carinthiaca var. brutteletii

Mentha × carinthiaca var. brutteletii, commonly known as the Alpine peppermint, is a hybrid mint species native to the Alps.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly mentholated leaves that are often used in herbal teas and culinary applications. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has a creeping growth habit with hairy stems. Its flowers are small and tubular, usually appearing in shades of purple or pink, and it is known for its strong, refreshing scent.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its medicinal properties.
