Megastachya bipinnata

Megastachya bipinnata, commonly known as the golden spike grass, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, pinnately compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces striking yellow to orange flower spikes that bloom in late summer, adding visual interest to gardens. Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.

It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
