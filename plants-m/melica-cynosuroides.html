Melica cynosuroides

Melica cynosuroides, commonly known as the narrowleaf brome, is a grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, exhibiting a clump-forming growth habit. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue. Its inflorescence consists of a spikelet cluster, bearing small, two-lobed florets.

Melica cynosuroides is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and ability to stabilize soil.
