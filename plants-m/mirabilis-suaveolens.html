Mirabilis suaveolens

Mirabilis suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-smelling four-o'clock, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that open in the late afternoon and close at dawn. The plant produces vibrant pink or red blossoms, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture, adding to its ornamental appeal.
