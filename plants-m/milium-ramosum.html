Milium ramosum

Milium ramosum, commonly known as the branched millet, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its compact, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spikelet-like inflorescences that are often used in traditional herbal medicine. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy texture.

Milium ramosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its historical use in herbal remedies.
