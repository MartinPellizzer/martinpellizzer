Merendera navis-noae

Merendera navis-noae, commonly known as the water-lily of the southern marshes, is a aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features broad, floating leaves that provide shade for aquatic life, while its roots anchor in the muddy substrate. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom above the water surface. Its leaves are often covered in a waxy layer to prevent water loss.

Merendera navis-noae plays a crucial role in maintaining water quality by absorbing nutrients and oxygenating the water.
