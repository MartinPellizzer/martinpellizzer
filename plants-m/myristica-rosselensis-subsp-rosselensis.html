Myristica rosselensis subsp. rosselensis

Myristica rosselensis subsp.

rosselensis, commonly known as the Bornean nutmeg, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the island of Borneo. It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, woody fruits that contain the highly valued nutmeg and mace spices.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by large, fleshy, orange-colored berries. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a smooth surface with a glossy appearance.

The tree thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.
