Malveopsis coromandeliana var. normalis

Malveopsis coromandeliana var. normalis, commonly known as the Coromandel mallow, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the rainy season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in warm climates.
