Melinis brevipila

Melinis brevipila, commonly known as littleleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It typically grows in open woodlands, prairies, and disturbed sites, reaching heights of 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant has narrow, bluish-green leaves that are short and blunt-tipped, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its inflorescence consists of compact, spike-like clusters of small, blue-green flowers that appear in late summer.

Melinis brevipila is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in native plant landscapes.
