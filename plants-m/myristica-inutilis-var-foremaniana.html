Myristica inutilis var. foremaniana

Myristica inutilis var. foremaniana, commonly known as the Foreman's nutmeg, is a species of flowering plant in the Myristicaceae family.

It is native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia, where it grows in humid, tropical rainforests. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers. Its fruit, which resembles a large nut, contains aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine.

The species is valued for its unique chemical compounds and ecological significance in its native habitat.
