Malus violacea eyresii

Malus violacea eyresii, commonly known as the pink-flowered apple tree, is a species native to South Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree that typically grows to a height of 5 to 10 meters. The plant is characterized by its attractive, fragrant pink or purple flowers that appear in clusters during the spring. Its leaves are ovate, with a smooth texture and a dark green upper surface.

The fruit is a small, edible apple-like berry that ripens in the summer, adding to its ornamental and potential culinary value.
