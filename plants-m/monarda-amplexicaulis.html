Monarda amplexicaulis

Monarda amplexicaulis, commonly known as narrowleaf horsemint, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of purple or pink, arranged in dense, terminal clusters. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a hardy addition to native plant gardens.

Its aromatic foliage and nectar-rich flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
