Myrceugenia × diemii

Myrceugenia × diemii, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a hybrid shrub or small tree native to the coastal regions of southern Brazil.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, dark red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas with sandy soils. Its botanical features include opposite leaf arrangement and showy, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters.

The species is also noted for its ability to tolerate salt spray, making it well adapted to coastal environments.
