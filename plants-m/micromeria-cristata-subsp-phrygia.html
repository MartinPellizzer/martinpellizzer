Micromeria cristata subsp. phrygia

Micromeria cristata subsp.

phrygia, commonly known as the Phrygian thyme, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for rock gardens and as a ground cover.

It is also valued in traditional herbal medicine for its essential oils and antiseptic properties.
