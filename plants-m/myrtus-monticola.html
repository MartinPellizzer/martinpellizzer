Myrtus monticola

Myrtus monticola, commonly known as the mountain myrtle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows in rocky or coastal areas. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, dark blue berries. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, with a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed.

Myrtus monticola is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
