Melissa taurica

Melissa taurica, commonly known as honeybee plant or lemon balm, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its aromatic leaves are often used in herbal teas and traditional medicine for their calming properties.

Melissa taurica thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight.
