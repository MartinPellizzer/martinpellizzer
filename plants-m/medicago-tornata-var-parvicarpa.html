Medicago tornata var. parvicarpa

Medicago tornata var. parvicarpa, commonly known as the small-fruited goat's rue, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pale yellow to greenish flowers. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that are typically less than 5 millimeters in diameter. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in dry, rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important plant in ecological studies and traditional herbal medicine.
