Maerua camporum

Maerua camporum, commonly known as the Natal glory bush, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spreading foliage and showy, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has small, elliptical leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its bark is smooth and often reddish in color. Maerua camporum is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for xeriscaping.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
