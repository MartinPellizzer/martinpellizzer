Marupa paraensis

Marupa paraensis, commonly known as the Brazilian sedge, is a perennial plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in marshy areas and along riverbanks.

Marupa paraensis plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
