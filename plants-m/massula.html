Massula

Massula, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Gesneriaceae and is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The leaves are typically velvety and heart-shaped, often with a waxy texture. Massula species are popular as ornamental plants due to their vibrant colors and ease of cultivation.

They thrive in well-drained soil and require bright, indirect light for optimal growth.
