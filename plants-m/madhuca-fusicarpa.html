Madhuca fusicarpa

Madhuca fusicarpa, commonly known as the black tamarind tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and produces clusters of small, yellow flowers. The tree bears fruit that resembles a small, dark brown drupe, which contains a single seed. It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its hard, durable wood.

The plant also plays an important role in local ecosystems and traditional medicine.
