Mahonia trifurca

Mahonia trifurca, commonly known as the thorned mahonia, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves that are divided into three to five segments, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in the spring, which are followed by blue-black berries in the fall. Mahonia trifurca thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its evergreen foliage and seasonal blooms.
