Microstylis triphylla

Microstylis triphylla, commonly known as the three-leaved microstylis, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and along stream banks. The plant features a basal rosette of three to five narrow, grass-like leaves. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are borne on short, erect stems and are often overlooked.

Microstylis triphylla is a member of the Cyperaceae family, commonly known as the sedge family.
