Myrcia alpina

Myrcia alpina, commonly known as the alpine myrtle, is a shrub native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Myrcia alpina is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
