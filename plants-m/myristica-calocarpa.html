Myristica calocarpa

Myristica calocarpa, commonly known as the fragrant nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen belonging to the Myristicaceae family.

It is native to the Indo-Pacific region, including parts of Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands. The plant produces distinctive yellowish-orange fruits that mature into bright red berries, each containing a single seed. The tree is valued for its aromatic seeds, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Its botanical features include compound leaves with elliptical leaflets and a woody, aromatic trunk.
