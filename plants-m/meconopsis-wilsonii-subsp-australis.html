Meconopsis wilsonii subsp. australis

Meconopsis wilsonii subsp.

australis, commonly known as the Southern Himalayan poppy, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the eastern Himalayas. It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers, which bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at elevations ranging from 2,500 to 4,500 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in alpine gardens for its vibrant color and unique appearance.
