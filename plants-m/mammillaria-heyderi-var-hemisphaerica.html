Mammillaria heyderi var. hemisphaerica

Mammillaria heyderi var. hemisphaerica, commonly known as the Hemispherical Mammillaria, is a compact cactus species native to Mexico.

It features a globular stem with a distinctive hemispherical shape, covered in dense, white to pale green tubercles. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking contrast to its textured surface. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for desert gardens.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated as a decorative and low-maintenance plant.
