Moehringia sedifolia

Moehringia sedifolia, commonly known as the silky sandwort, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features small, rounded leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale pink flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or arctic environments.
