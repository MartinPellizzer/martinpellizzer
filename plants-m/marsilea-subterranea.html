Marsilea subterranea

Marsilea subterranea, commonly known as the underground water clover, is a species of aquatic fern characterized by its unique subterranean growth habit.

It thrives in shallow, still waters and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. The plant's leaves are typically submerged, with small, floating leaves that resemble those of other water clovers. Its reproductive structures, including spore-producing sorus, are located on the underside of the leaves.

Due to its specialized habitat, Marsilea subterranea plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by contributing to nutrient cycling and providing habitat for microorganisms.
