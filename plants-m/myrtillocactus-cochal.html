Myrtillocactus cochal

Myrtillocactus cochal, commonly known as the cochineal cactus, is a species native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a columnar cactus with a distinctive dark green stem covered in spines. The plant produces small, violet flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking visual contrast to its foliage. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Myrtillocactus cochal is also known for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
