Malaxis aschista

Malaxis aschista, commonly known as the aschista orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, and it produces a single spike of flowers during the rainy season.

The orchid is noted for its delicate appearance and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
