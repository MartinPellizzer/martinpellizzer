Meconopsis latifolia

Meconopsis latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf meconopsis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are broad and ovate, often covered in a fine layer of white or silver hairs.

Meconopsis latifolia is a member of the Papaveraceae family, which includes other well-known genera such as Papaver and Eschscholzia.
