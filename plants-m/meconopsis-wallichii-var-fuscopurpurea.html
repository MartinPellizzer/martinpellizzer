Meconopsis wallichii var. fuscopurpurea

Meconopsis wallichii var. fuscopurpurea, commonly known as the purple-leaf meconopsis, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its distinctive purple-tinged leaves and bell-shaped flowers that range from purple to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments at high altitudes. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Meconopsis wallichii var. fuscopurpurea is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens with cool, humid conditions.
