Monanthes × anagiflora

Monanthes × anagiflora, commonly known as the hybrid lily, is a botanical hybrid resulting from the cross between Monanthes flaccida and Monanthes anagiffolia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically display a mix of white and pink hues. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
