Mila sublanata

Mila sublanata, commonly known as the silver-leafed mila, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, silver-green leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access groundwater in dry conditions.

Mila sublanata is often found in rocky outcrops and sandy soils, where it thrives with minimal water and nutrients.
