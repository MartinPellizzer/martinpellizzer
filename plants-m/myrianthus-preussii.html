Myrianthus preussii

Myrianthus preussii, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets, and it produces woody pods that contain seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental species.

Myrianthus preussii is also valued for its ecological benefits and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
