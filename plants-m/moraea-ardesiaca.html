Moraea ardesiaca

Moraea ardesiaca, commonly known as the Red-hot Poker, is a striking perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features tall, spiky inflorescences with bright orange-red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves that are evergreen and contribute to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Moraea ardesiaca is valued for its vibrant color and drought tolerance, though it requires careful watering to avoid root rot.
