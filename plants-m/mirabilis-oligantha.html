Mirabilis oligantha

Mirabilis oligantha, commonly known as the "Rainflower," is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, brightly colored flowers that bloom in the evening, a trait known as nyctinasty. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Mirabilis oligantha is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract nocturnal pollinators.
