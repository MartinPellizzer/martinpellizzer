Mariscus fulvus var. canescens

Mariscus fulvus var. canescens, commonly known as the silverleaf mariscus, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its narrow, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine, fuzzy hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice among gardeners in arid climates.
