Malva ciliata

Malva ciliata, commonly known as the soft-hairy mallow, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces showy pink or purple flowers with five petals, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Malva ciliata is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and flowers.
