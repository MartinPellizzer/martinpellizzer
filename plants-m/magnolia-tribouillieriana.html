Magnolia tribouillieriana

Magnolia tribouillieriana, commonly known as the Guianas magnolia, is a species native to South America, particularly found in the countries of Guyana, Suriname, and French Guiana.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 25 meters tall, characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The flowers, which are typically white or pale pink, bloom in the spring and are accompanied by reddish bracts that resemble petals.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ecological significance.
