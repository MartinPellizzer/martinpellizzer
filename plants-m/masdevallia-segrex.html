Masdevallia segrex

Masdevallia segrex, commonly known as the Segrex Masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its intricate, often colorful flowers that resemble miniature orchid blooms. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually narrow and elongated, adapted to capture moisture from the humid air.

The flowering period of Masdevallia segrex is typically during the cooler months, making it a sought-after plant among orchid enthusiasts.
