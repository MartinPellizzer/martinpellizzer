Malva floribunda

Malva floribunda, commonly known as the common mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes other familiar species like cotton and hibiscus. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to ecological gardens.
