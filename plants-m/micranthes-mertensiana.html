Micranthes mertensiana

Micranthes mertensiana, commonly known as the Mertens' rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many species with small, four-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to harsh, alpine environments. Its leaves are usually basal, rosette-like, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.
