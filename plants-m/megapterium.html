Megapterium

The plant's fronds are composed of numerous small, finger-like segments called pinnae, which increase its surface area for photosynthesis. Megapterium thrives in still or slow-moving water, often forming dense mats that can alter water flow and sediment dynamics.

Its reproductive structures, including spore-producing sorus, are located on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its propagation in wetland ecosystems.
