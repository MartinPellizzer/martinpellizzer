Monardella pringlei

Monardella pringlei, commonly known as Pringle's monardella, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its slender stems, narrow leaves, and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
