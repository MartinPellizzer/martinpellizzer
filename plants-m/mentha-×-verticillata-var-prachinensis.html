Mentha × verticillata var. prachinensis

Mentha × verticillata var. prachinensis, commonly known as Prachinburi mint, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its square stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale purple to white flowers in whorled clusters, typical of the Mentha genus. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.
