Molineria leptostachya

Molineria leptostachya, commonly known as the slender spike-rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded habitats, such as marshes and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth surface and a prominent midrib.

The flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in a panicle structure, and are adapted for wind pollination.
