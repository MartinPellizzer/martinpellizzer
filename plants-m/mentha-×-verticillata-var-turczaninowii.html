Mentha × verticillata var. turczaninowii

Mentha × verticillata var. turczaninowii, commonly known as the Turkish spearmint, is a hybrid perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers arranged in whorled clusters, which give it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along water edges.

This variety is valued for its aromatic leaves, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications.
