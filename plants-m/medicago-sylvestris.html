Medicago sylvestris

Medicago sylvestris, commonly known as wild vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces elongated pods containing several seeds, which are adapted for dispersal by wind and animals. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Medicago sylvestris is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
