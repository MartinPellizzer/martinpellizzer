Monotes africanus var. denudans

Monotes africanus var. denudans, commonly known as the African monotes, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often densely packed. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are typically deciduous, shedding during the dry season to conserve water.

This species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in harsh conditions.
