Millettia aromatica

Millettia aromatica, commonly known as the fragrant millettia, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its pinnate leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces pod-like fruits containing flat, winged seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its aromatic properties, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
