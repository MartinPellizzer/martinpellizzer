Mammillaria flaviceps

Mammillaria flaviceps, commonly known as the yellow-headed hedgehog cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and is covered in white to pale yellow spines. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its spiny structure. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ease of care, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.
