Marrubium plicatum

Marrubium plicatum, commonly known as the wrinkled horehound, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves that are often deeply wrinkled, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Marrubium plicatum has been used traditionally in herbal medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
