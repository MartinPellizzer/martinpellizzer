Myrtus suaveolens

Myrtus suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet myrtle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by dark blue berries. Its leaves are leathery, aromatic, and have a slightly oily texture.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
