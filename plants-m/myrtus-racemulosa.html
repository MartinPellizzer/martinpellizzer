Myrtus racemulosa

Myrtus racemulosa, commonly known as the common myrtle, is a flowering shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, evergreen leaves and small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible and often used in culinary and medicinal applications. Myrtus racemulosa thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
