Mentha × motolensis

Mentha × motolensis, commonly known as Hungarian mint, is a hybrid mint species native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly menthol-like fragrance and is often used in traditional herbal medicine. The plant typically grows as a perennial herb with square stems and opposite, serrated leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its essential oils contain compounds such as menthone and menthol, contributing to its medicinal and aromatic properties.
