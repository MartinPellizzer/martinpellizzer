Malva aegyptiaca

Malva aegyptiaca, commonly known as the Egyptian mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in full sun to partial shade. It has a hairy stem and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, brown seeds.

Malva aegyptiaca is also known for its mucilaginous properties, making it useful in traditional medicine and as a natural thickener.
