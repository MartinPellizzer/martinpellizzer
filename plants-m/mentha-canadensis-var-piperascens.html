Mentha canadensis var. piperascens

Mentha canadensis var. piperascens, commonly known as American peppermint, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale purple to greenish in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland areas and stream banks.

Its essential oils, rich in menthol, make it valuable for both culinary and medicinal uses.
