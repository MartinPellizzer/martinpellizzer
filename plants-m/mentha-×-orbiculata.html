Mentha × orbiculata

Mentha × orbiculata, commonly known as round-leaf mint, is a hybrid species within the Mentha genus.

It is characterized by its rounded, oval-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers arranged in whorls along the stem. It is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Mentha × orbiculata is valued for its aromatic properties and is used in both culinary and medicinal applications.
