Maranta borussica

Maranta borussica, commonly known as the arrowroot plant, is a tropical herbaceous plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Marantaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful leaves that often display vibrant red, purple, and green hues. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white, followed by the development of arrowhead-shaped tubers underground. These tubers are the primary edible part of the plant and are rich in starch, making it a valuable crop in some regions.

Maranta borussica is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and its nutritional benefits.
