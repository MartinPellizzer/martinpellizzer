Maxillaria pauciflora

Maxillaria pauciflora, commonly known as the few-flowered orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its sympodial growth habit and pseudo-bulbs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, typically during the summer months. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

Maxillaria pauciflora is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its elegant appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
