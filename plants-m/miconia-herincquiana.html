Miconia herincquiana

Miconia herincquiana, commonly known as the Brazilian starfruit tree, is a tropical plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a large evergreen tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish fruits that are edible but often have a mild, sweet flavor.

Its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
