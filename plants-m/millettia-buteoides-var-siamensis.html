Millettia buteoides var. siamensis

Millettia buteoides var. siamensis, commonly known as the Siam milk tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnate with 5 to 7 pairs of leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in panicle inflorescences, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing flat, seed-like structures. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
