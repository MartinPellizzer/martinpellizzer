Microbrochis apiifolia

Microbrochis apiifolia, commonly known as the false nettle, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Urticaceae family, which includes true nettles. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous annual or perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are heart-shaped with serrated edges and are often covered in fine hairs.

It produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, round fruits.
