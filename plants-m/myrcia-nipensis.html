Myrcia nipensis

Myrcia nipensis, commonly known as the Brazilian peppertree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces dark purple berries that are edible for some animals but toxic to others. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in a variety of soil types.

Myrcia nipensis is also notable for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
