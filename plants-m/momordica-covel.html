Momordica covel

Momordica covel, commonly known as bitter melon, is a tropical vine belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, warty, green fruit that turns orange when ripe. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and has tendrils that help it climb. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly prickly texture.

Bitter melon is native to Southeast Asia and is widely cultivated for its edible fruit, which is used in various cuisines and traditional medicine.
