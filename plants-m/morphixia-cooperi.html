Morphixia cooperi

Morphixia cooperi, commonly known as the Cooper's morphixia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often found in desert or semi-arid environments.

Morphixia cooperi is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance to local pollinators.
