Mespilus tanacetifolia proles orientalis

Mespilus tanacetifolia proles orientalis, commonly known as the Eastern Honeylocust, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Central Asia.

It features compound leaves with small, serrated leaflets that resemble those of the honeylocust genus. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in spring, followed by round, edible fruits that turn from green to red as they mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate climates.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate urban environments.
