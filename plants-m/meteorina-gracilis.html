Meteorina gracilis

Meteorina gracilis, commonly known as the slender moonflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its pale yellow to white, fragrant flowers that open at night. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and silvery-green, and it produces small, edible berries that turn red when mature.

Meteorina gracilis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and nocturnal blooming habit.
