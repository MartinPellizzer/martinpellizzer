Mentha × emarginata

Mentha × emarginata, commonly known as marsh mint, is a hybrid plant in the mint family (Lamiaceae).

It is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and aromatic, often hairy foliage. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as wet meadows, marshes, and stream banks. Its flowers are small, tubular, and usually violet or blue in color, arranged in whorls along the stem.

Mentha × emarginata is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
