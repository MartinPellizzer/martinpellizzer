Masdevallia lactea

Masdevallia lactea, commonly known as the milk orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking white to cream-colored petals and sepals, which often feature intricate patterns and a subtle sheen. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized epiphyte, attaching itself to tree trunks or branches in its natural habitat. Its flowers bloom in late winter to early spring, adding a unique ornamental value to gardens and collections.

Masdevallia lactea requires cool, humid conditions and indirect light to thrive, making it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
