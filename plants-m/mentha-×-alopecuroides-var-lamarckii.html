Mentha × alopecuroides var. lamarckii

Mentha × alopecuroides var. lamarckii, commonly known as French mint, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly serrated leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant is native to southern Europe and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. It has a strong, fresh mint scent and is used in both culinary and medicinal applications.

Its growth habit is typically perennial, with a spreading rhizomatous root system that allows it to form dense colonies.
