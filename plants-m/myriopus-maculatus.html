Myriopus maculatus

Myriopus maculatus, commonly known as the spotted flax, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale blue to purple flowers. Its leaves are narrow and often have a distinctive spotted or striped pattern, contributing to its ornamental value.
