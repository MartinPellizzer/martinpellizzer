Mentha longifolia var. tenella

Mentha longifolia var. tenella, commonly known as narrow-leaf mint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves that are typically less than 1 inch in length. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This variety is often used in culinary and medicinal applications due to its fragrant properties.
