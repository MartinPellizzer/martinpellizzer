Muscari vuralii

Muscari vuralii, commonly known as the Vural's grape hyacinth, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained soil in full sun to partial shade. Muscari vuralii is valued for its early spring bloom and is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant.

It is relatively low maintenance and resistant to pests and diseases.
