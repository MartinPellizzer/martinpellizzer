Magnolia eriostepta var. poilanei

Magnolia eriostepta var. poilanei, commonly known as the Poilane magnolia, is a rare and ornamental species native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its large, showy white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a glossy upper surface and a hairy underside. Its bark is smooth and grayish, often peeling in thin layers.

This variety is highly valued in horticulture for its striking floral display and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
