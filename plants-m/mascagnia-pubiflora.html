Mascagnia pubiflora

Mascagnia pubiflora, commonly known as the hairy mascagnia, is a flowering plant native to the dry, rocky regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and thick, fleshy leaves adapted to arid conditions. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. Its unique appearance and ability to thrive in harsh environments make it an interesting subject for botanical study.

Mascagnia pubiflora is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
