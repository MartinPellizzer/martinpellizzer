Melicope montana

Melicope montana, commonly known as the Hawaiian mahogany, is a small evergreen tree native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by dark blue to black berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
