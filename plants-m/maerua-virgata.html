Maerua virgata

Maerua virgata, commonly known as the white thorn or African white thorn, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, thorny branches and small, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged opposite each other. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody, three-lobed fruits. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.

Maerua virgata is often found in savannas and rocky areas, contributing to local biodiversity.
