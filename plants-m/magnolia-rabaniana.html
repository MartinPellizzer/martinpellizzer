Magnolia rabaniana

Magnolia rabaniana, commonly known as the Mexican magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The tree has simple, alternate, ovate to elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, often peeling in thin, papery layers.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
