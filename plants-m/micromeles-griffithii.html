Micromeles griffithii

Micromeles griffithii, commonly known as the Kashmir rose, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin.

The fruit is a small, red, fleshy drupe that contains a single seed.
