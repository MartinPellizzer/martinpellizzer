Miconia paleaciramis

Miconia paleaciramis, commonly known as the white-veined miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with prominent white veins, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Miconia paleaciramis is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
