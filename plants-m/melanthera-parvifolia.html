Melanthera parvifolia

Melanthera parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved blueweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. The plant features slender stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences and are usually blue or purple in color.

Melanthera parvifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
