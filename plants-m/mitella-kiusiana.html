Mitella kiusiana

Mitella kiusiana, commonly known as the alpine mitella, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, clustered flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its foliage consists of opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hair.

Mitella kiusiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
