Mammillaria marshalliana

Mammillaria marshalliana, commonly known as the Marshall's nipple cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a small, globular cactus characterized by its pale green to blue-gray color and prominent areoles. The plant features short, spiny stems with white or pale blue tubercles, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a vibrant touch to its otherwise muted foliage.

This species is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil to thrive.
