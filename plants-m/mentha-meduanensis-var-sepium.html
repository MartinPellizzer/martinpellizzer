Mentha meduanensis var. sepium

Mentha meduanensis var. sepium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence. The plant produces small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in terminal clusters during the summer months. It is valued for its aromatic foliage, which contains essential oils similar to those found in other mint species.

This variety is often used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent in various culinary applications.
