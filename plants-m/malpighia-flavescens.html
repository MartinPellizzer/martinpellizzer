Malpighia flavescens

Malpighia flavescens, commonly known as the yellow mombin, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Malpighiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant produces small, yellow-orange berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.

The species is also known for its ability to tolerate shade, making it suitable for understory planting in tropical forests.
