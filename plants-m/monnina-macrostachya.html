Monnina macrostachya

Monnina macrostachya, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, blue-violet flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and is often found in rocky soils and open woodlands. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.

Monnina macrostachya is also known for its drought tolerance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
