Medicago ciscaucasica

Medicago ciscaucasica, commonly known as Caucasian clover, is a perennial legume native to the Caucasus region.

It features trifoliate leaves with a hairy surface and produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant is adapted to temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its nitrogen-fixing abilities.

Its fibrous root system helps prevent soil erosion, making it valuable in agricultural and environmental contexts.
