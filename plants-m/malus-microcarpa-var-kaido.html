Malus microcarpa var. kaido

Malus microcarpa var. kaido, commonly known as the Kaido apple, is a rare and ornamental variant of the crabapple tree.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its small, ornamental fruits and showy flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a smooth texture and a vibrant green color.

The Kaido apple is valued for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and for its historical significance in traditional Japanese horticulture.
