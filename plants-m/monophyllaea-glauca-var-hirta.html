Monophyllaea glauca var. hirta

Monophyllaea glauca var. hirta, commonly known as the hairy bluebell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant features hairy stems and leaves, which give it its varietal name "hirta," meaning hairy in Latin. Its foliage is typically lance-shaped and grows in dense clusters.

This species thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland habitats.
