Magnolia eriostepta

Magnolia eriostepta, commonly known as the Mexican magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to central Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, showy white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has simple, ovate leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. Its bark is smooth and grayish in color, and it produces woody capsules containing numerous seeds.

Magnolia eriostepta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
