Miconia leblondii

Miconia leblondii, commonly known as the leblond's miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, showy, pink to purple bracts that resemble flowers. The plant has opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a glossy upper surface and a hairy underside. It produces small white or pale yellow flowers that are often clustered in umbels.

Miconia leblondii is typically found in moist, shaded understories of tropical forests and can grow up to 5 meters in height.
