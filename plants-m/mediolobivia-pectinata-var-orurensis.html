Mediolobivia pectinata var. orurensis

Mediolobivia pectinata var. orurensis, commonly known as the golden spire orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and clusters of bright yellow, spire-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs, which help to reduce water loss in its arid environment. It requires well-drained soil and full sun to thrive, making it a challenging but rewarding plant for experienced growers.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered endangered and is protected in several South American countries.
