Mentha × velutina var. genevensis

Mentha × velutina var. genevensis, commonly known as the Geneva peppermint, is a hybrid mint species originating from Switzerland.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and a strong, aromatic scent reminiscent of peppermint. The plant typically grows as a perennial herb with erect, branching stems and opposite leaves. Its flowers are small, tubular, and usually pink or purple, appearing in whorls along the stems.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its fragrance and visual interest.
