Metanarthecium yakumontanum

Metanarthecium yakumontanum, commonly known as the Yakutat sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the northwestern United States and southeastern Alaska.

It typically grows in wet, temperate environments such as bogs and marshes, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are typically pollinated by wind. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats, which help stabilize soil in its habitat.

The species is of particular interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to cold, wet climates.
