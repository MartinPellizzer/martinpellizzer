Melica uniflora var. autumnalis

Melica uniflora var. autumnalis, commonly known as autumn meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, tufted culms with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often slightly hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet per node, which is characteristic of the Poaceae family.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
