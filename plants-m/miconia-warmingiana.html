Miconia warmingiana

Miconia warmingiana, commonly known as the velvetleaf miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale green, followed by round, fleshy fruits that contain numerous seeds. Miconia warmingiana is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of tropical conditions.

However, it can become invasive in some regions, posing a threat to native ecosystems.
