Maytenus impressa

Maytenus impressa, commonly known as the impressa mayten, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish-brown bark and elliptical, leathery leaves that are often slightly indented. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by bright red, fleshy berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Maytenus impressa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
