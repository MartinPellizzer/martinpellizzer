Memora velutina

Memora velutina, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white in color. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Memora velutina is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its rapid growth rate, making it both a useful and sometimes invasive species.
