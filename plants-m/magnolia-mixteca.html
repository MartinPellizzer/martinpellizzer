Magnolia mixteca

Magnolia mixteca, commonly known as the Mexican magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of central Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. Its bark is smooth and grayish, becoming fissured with age.

The flowers are typically borne on short pedicels and emit a sweet, fragrant aroma.
