Monadelphanthus floridus

Monadelphanthus floridus, commonly known as the Florida honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, which bloom in the spring. These flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance.

Its adaptability to various soil types makes it a versatile plant for landscaping in warm climates.
