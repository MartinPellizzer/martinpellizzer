Mouroucoa scandens

Mouroucoa scandens, commonly known as the climbing mouroucoa, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant is characterized by its vigorous climbing habit and can reach lengths of several meters. Its flowers give way to elongated, cylindrical fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Mouroucoa scandens is often cultivated for its ornamental value in warm climates.
