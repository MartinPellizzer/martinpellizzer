Myrcia kylistophylla

Myrcia kylistophylla, commonly known as the red-leaf myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that turn reddish in color as they mature. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its woody structure and aromatic foliage make it a distinctive member of the myrtle family.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments within tropical and subtropical regions.
