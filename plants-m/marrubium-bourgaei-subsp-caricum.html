Marrubium bourgaei subsp. caricum

Marrubium bourgaei subsp.

caricum, commonly known as the Caricum horehound, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and is characterized by its erect stems and greyish, hairy foliage.

The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and often have a distinctive, slightly serrated edge.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
