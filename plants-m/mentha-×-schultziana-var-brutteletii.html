Mentha × schultziana var. brutteletii

Mentha × schultziana var. brutteletii, commonly known as the Brutteletii mint, is a hybrid species within the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its aromatic, square stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or blue, arranged in whorls along the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in herbal remedies.
