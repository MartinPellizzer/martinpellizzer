Macaranga laciniata

Macaranga laciniata, commonly known as the serrated macaranga, is a species of flowering plant in the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its palmate leaves with deeply lobed margins. The plant is often found in tropical rainforests, growing as an understory shrub or small tree. Its bark exudes a milky sap, which is a common trait among members of the Euphorbiaceae family.

The species is also noted for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed habitats.
