Malus domestica subsp. prunifolia

Malus domestica subsp.

prunifolia, commonly known as the common apple, is a deciduous tree native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its broad ovate leaves with serrated margins and clusters of white, fragrant flowers in spring.

The tree produces pomaceous fruits, typically ranging from 5 to 12 centimeters in diameter, with a smooth, thin skin and a crisp, juicy pulp. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This subspecies is widely cultivated for its edible fruit and ornamental value.
