Malcolmia patula var. longifolia

Malcolmia patula var. longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf malcolmia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture. This variety is often found in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
