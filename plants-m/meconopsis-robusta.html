Meconopsis robusta

Meconopsis robusta, commonly known as the Himalayan blue poppy, is a striking flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy blue or violet petals that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil. Its leaves are dark green, heart-shaped, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Meconopsis robusta is a perennial species that is highly valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological significance in its native range.
