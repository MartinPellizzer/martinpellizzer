Macrotyloma biflorum var. biflorum

Macrotyloma biflorum var. biflorum, commonly known as the two-flowered cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in clusters. The plant produces cylindrical pods containing several seeds, which are often used as a food source. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm climate for optimal growth.

This species is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
