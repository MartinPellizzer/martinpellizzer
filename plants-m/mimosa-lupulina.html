Mimosa lupulina

Mimosa lupulina, commonly known as the yellow mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant is known for its sensitive leaves that fold when touched, a trait called thigmonasty. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Mimosa lupulina is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
