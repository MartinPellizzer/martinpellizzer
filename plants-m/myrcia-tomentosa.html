Myrcia tomentosa

Myrcia tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage that gives it a soft, woolly appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom in clusters. Its leaves are broad and elliptical, with a leathery texture and a strong, aromatic scent.

Myrcia tomentosa is often found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental qualities and medicinal properties.
