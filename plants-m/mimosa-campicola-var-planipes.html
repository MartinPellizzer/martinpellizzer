Mimosa campicola var. planipes

Mimosa campicola var. planipes, commonly known as the flat-stemmed mimosa, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its slender, often prostrate stems and bipinnate leaves that are compound and delicate. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

The species is noted for its rapid growth and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
