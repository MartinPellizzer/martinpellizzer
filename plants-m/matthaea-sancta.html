Matthaea sancta

Matthaea sancta, commonly known as the sacred matthaea, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves with a smooth texture.

Matthaea sancta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
