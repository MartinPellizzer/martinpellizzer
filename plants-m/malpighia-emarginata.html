Malpighia emarginata

Malpighia emarginata, commonly known as the acerola cherry, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It belongs to the Malpighiaceae family and is characterized by its small, bright red fruits that are rich in vitamin C. The plant has simple, alternate leaves and produces small, white flowers that are often clustered together. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

The fruit's high acidity and unique flavor make it a popular ingredient in various culinary and medicinal applications.
