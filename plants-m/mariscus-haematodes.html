Mariscus haematodes

Mariscus haematodes, commonly known as the bloodroot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features a fleshy, reddish rhizome from which its common name is derived. The plant has a single, smooth, waxy stem that supports a cluster of bright green, heart-shaped leaves. Its most distinctive feature is the showy, red-orange flower that blooms in early spring.

The plant is often found in moist, shaded woodlands and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
