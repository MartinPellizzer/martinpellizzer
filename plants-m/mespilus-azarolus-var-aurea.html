Mespilus azarolus var. aurea

Mespilus azarolus var. aurea, commonly known as the golden medlar, is a rare and ornamental plant native to the eastern Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fragrant, and edible fruits that ripen in the autumn. The plant produces showy, pale yellow flowers in the spring, which are often followed by small, round fruits that turn golden as they mature. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture with serrated edges.

The golden medlar is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in traditional medicine and culinary practices.
