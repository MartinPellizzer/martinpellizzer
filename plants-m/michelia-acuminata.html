Michelia acuminata

Michelia acuminata, commonly known as the Chinese sweet olive, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has simple, opposite, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

Michelia acuminata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional Chinese medicine.
