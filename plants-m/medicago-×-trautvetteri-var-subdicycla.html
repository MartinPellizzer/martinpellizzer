Medicago × trautvetteri var. subdicycla

Medicago × trautvetteri var. subdicycla, commonly known as the narrow-leafed sainfoin, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is adapted to dry, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas. It has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion and improves soil fertility through nitrogen fixation.

Its ornamental value and ecological benefits make it a useful plant in both agricultural and landscaping settings.
