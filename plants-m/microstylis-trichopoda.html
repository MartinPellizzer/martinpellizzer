Microstylis trichopoda

Microstylis trichopoda, commonly known as the slender-styled feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, wiry stems and produces small, fluffy inflorescences that are often mistaken for seed heads. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous texture.

Microstylis trichopoda plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
