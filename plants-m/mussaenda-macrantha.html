Mussaenda macrantha

Mussaenda macrantha, commonly known as the yellow flame bush, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, featuring lance-shaped leaves with a smooth, waxy surface. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow, tubular flowers that are highly attractive to pollinators such as bees and birds. Its flowers are borne on long, slender pedicels, and the plant is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Mussaenda macrantha thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun.
