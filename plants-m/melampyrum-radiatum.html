Melampyrum radiatum

Melampyrum radiatum, commonly known as yellow brome or lesser burnet, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces trifoliate leaves, which are typically alternate and have a hairy texture. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Melampyrum radiatum is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a pioneer species in ecological succession.
