Millettia erythrocalyx var. fusca

Millettia erythrocalyx var. fusca, commonly known as the red calyx millettia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive red calyx that persists on the fruit. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind or animals.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
