Musa × calicutensis

Musa × calicutensis, commonly known as the Calicut banana, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Musaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its large, elongated, and often curved leaves. The plant produces clusters of fruit that are typically yellow when ripe, with a sweet and aromatic flavor. It is cultivated for both its edible fruit and ornamental value due to its striking foliage.

Musa × calicutensis is relatively drought-tolerant and thrives in warm, humid climates.
