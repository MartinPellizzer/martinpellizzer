Mitella stenopetala var. parryi

Mitella stenopetala var. parryi, commonly known as the Parry's twinflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features small, delicate white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has heart-shaped, toothed leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy layer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
