Mussaenda rotundifolia

Mussaenda rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaf Mussaenda, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, yellowish-white bracts that resemble flowers, which are actually modified leaves. The plant has smooth, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Mussaenda rotundifolia is also known for its ability to grow in a variety of soil types, making it a versatile choice for gardens.
