Memecylon semsei

Memecylon semsei, commonly known as the red-flowered memecylon, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to deep red, often with a distinctive purple or greenish tinge. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in secondary forests.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.
