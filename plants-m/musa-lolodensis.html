Musa lolodensis

Musa lolodensis, commonly known as the African banana, is a wild banana species native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its large, elongated leaves and clusters of yellowish-green bananas that mature in the shade. The plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and riverbanks. It has a fibrous pseudostem and produces berries that are edible but less commonly consumed than cultivated bananas.

This species is of interest to botanists and horticulturists for its genetic diversity and potential for breeding programs.
