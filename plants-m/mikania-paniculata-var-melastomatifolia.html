Mikania paniculata var. melastomatifolia

Mikania paniculata var. melastomatifolia, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a perennial vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces small, showy flowers that are typically white or pale blue. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to spread through both vegetative propagation and seed dispersal. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests and along stream banks.

Due to its invasive tendencies, it can outcompete native vegetation in certain ecosystems.
