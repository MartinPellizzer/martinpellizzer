Mesosphaerum paludosum

Mesosphaerum paludosum, commonly known as the swamp mesosphaerum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, creeping stems that root at the nodes, allowing it to spread vegetatively. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often floating on the water surface or partially submerged.

The small, white flowers appear in clusters and are adapted for wind pollination, contributing to its ecological role in aquatic ecosystems.
