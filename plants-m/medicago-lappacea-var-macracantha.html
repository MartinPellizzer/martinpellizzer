Medicago lappacea var. macracantha

Medicago lappacea var. macracantha, commonly known as the narrowleaf sainfoin, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, trifoliate leaves with a distinctive narrow shape. The plant produces yellow flowers in spikes, which are followed by small, elongated seed pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

This variety is valued for its forage quality and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
