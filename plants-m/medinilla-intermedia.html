Medinilla intermedia

Medinilla intermedia, commonly known as the velvet tangerine or velvet bell, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, pale pink to white flowers. The plant produces small, round, yellow-orange berries that are edible but not widely consumed. Medinilla intermedia thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and showy blooms.
