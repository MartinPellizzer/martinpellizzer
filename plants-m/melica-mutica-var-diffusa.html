Melica mutica var. diffusa

Melica mutica var. diffusa, commonly known as the slender meadowgrass, is a variant of the meadowgrass species native to temperate regions.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as meadows, marshes, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle made up of spikelets, which are small clusters of flowers.

This variant is often found in disturbed soils and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for insects.
