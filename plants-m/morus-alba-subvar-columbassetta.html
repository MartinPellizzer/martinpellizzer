Morus alba subvar. columbassetta

Morus alba subvar. columbassetta, commonly known as the white mulberry, is a variant of the white mulberry tree native to Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. It produces small, edible fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source for silkworms.

This subspecies is also valued for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to different climates.
