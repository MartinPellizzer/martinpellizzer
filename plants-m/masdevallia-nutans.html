Masdevallia nutans

Masdevallia nutans, commonly known as the drooping masdevallia, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous inflorescence, which features small, delicate flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows in cool, humid environments with high-altitude cloud forests. Its leaves are linear and often covered in a dense layer of fine, white pubescence.

Masdevallia nutans is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance.
