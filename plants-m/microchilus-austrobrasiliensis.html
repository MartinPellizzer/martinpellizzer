Microchilus austrobrasiliensis

Microchilus austrobrasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian microchilus, is a rare and specialized plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a small, herbaceous species with delicate, linear leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is adapted to the nutrient-poor soils of its native habitat.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is of particular interest to botanists studying plant adaptation and conservation.
