Malaxis ternatensis

Malaxis ternatensis, commonly known as the Ternate orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Indonesian island of Ternate.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the surrounding soil.

Malaxis ternatensis is noted for its unique floral structure and is of interest to botanists and orchid enthusiasts.
