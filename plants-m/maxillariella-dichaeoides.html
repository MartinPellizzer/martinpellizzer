Maxillariella dichaeoides

Maxillariella dichaeoides, commonly known as the Orchid of the Andes, is a terrestrial orchid native to South America.

It features a compact, rhizomatous growth habit with a single, upright stem that bears several lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and limited natural range, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
