Mariscus cornutus

Mariscus cornutus, commonly known as the horned spurge, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family, characterized by its milky sap and distinctive foliage. The plant features small, green, ovate leaves and produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers. It thrives in rocky or dry soils and is often found in coastal areas.
