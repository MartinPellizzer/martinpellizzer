Mimosa gracilis var. stipitata

Mimosa gracilis var. stipitata, commonly known as the slender mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. Its stems are slender and often hairy, and it produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Mimosa gracilis var. stipitata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
