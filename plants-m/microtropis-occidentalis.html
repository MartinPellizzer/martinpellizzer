Microtropis occidentalis

Microtropis occidentalis, commonly known as the western microtropis, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. The plant features simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. Its small, fragrant flowers are typically white or pale yellow and are clustered in inflorescences.

Microtropis occidentalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
