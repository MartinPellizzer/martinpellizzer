Madhuca thorelii

Madhuca thorelii, commonly known as the Malabar tallow tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain a single seed, which is encased in a hard, woody pulp. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in various parts of its native range.
