Mansoa erythraea

Mansoa erythraea, commonly known as the red mansoa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking red flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for ecological restoration.
