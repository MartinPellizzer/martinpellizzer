Maranta makoyana

Maranta makoyana, commonly known as the silver stripe arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a herbaceous perennial that belongs to the Marantaceae family, known for its striking foliage. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves with distinctive silver veins, which give it its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Maranta makoyana is also valued for its starchy roots, which can be used in culinary applications.
