Macleania salapa

Macleania salapa, commonly known as the Salapa orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant features a compact, pseudobulbous structure with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. Its flowers are small and clustered, displaying a range of colors from white to pale yellow, with intricate patterns on the petals.

Macleania salapa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant due to its specific care requirements.
