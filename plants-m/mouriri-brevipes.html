Mouriri brevipes

Mouriri brevipes, commonly known as the short-footed mouriri, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are small, woody capsules that release seeds when mature.

Mouriri brevipes is often found in moist, shaded understory habitats within tropical rainforests.
