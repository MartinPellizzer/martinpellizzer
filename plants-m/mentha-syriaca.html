Mentha syriaca

Mentha syriaca, commonly known as Syrian mint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, erect stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers in whorled clusters, typical of the Lamiaceae family. Its leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its characteristic minty fragrance.

Mentha syriaca is often cultivated for its aromatic properties and used in herbal remedies.
