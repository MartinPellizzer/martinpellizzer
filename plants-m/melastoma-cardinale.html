Melastoma cardinale

Melastoma cardinale, commonly known as the crimson flame bush, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. The plant has elliptical, glossy leaves that are often dark green in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Melastoma cardinale is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
