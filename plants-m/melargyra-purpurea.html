Melargyra purpurea

Melargyra purpurea, commonly known as the purple melargyra, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the southern hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Melargyraceae and is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are upright and branched.

Melargyra purpurea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
