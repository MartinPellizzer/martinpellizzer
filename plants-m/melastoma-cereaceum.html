Melastoma cereaceum

Melastoma cereaceum, commonly known as the white-flowered melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its showy, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas and has opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, dark seeds.

Melastoma cereaceum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
