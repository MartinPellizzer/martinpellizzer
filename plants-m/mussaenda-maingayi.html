Mussaenda maingayi

Mussaenda maingayi, commonly known as the flame flower or golden flame, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that resemble flames, with bright yellow bracts surrounding small white flowers. The plant is typically shrub-like, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Mussaenda maingayi is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
