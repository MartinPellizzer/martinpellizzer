Mesembryanthemum helianthoides

Mesembryanthemum helianthoides, commonly known as the sun rose, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed leaves and vibrant yellow flowers that resemble miniature sunflowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments. Its flowering period typically occurs during the warm seasons, and it is often used in rock gardens and as an ornamental plant.

The species is also valued for its resilience and ability to store water in its leaves, adapting to drought conditions.
