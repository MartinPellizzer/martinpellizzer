Melandrium apetalum f. okaidai

Melandrium apetalum f. okaidai, commonly known as the Okaidai bladder campion, is a rare and unusual variant of the bladder campion species.

It is characterized by its reduced floral structures, with the typical petals absent, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant is native to Japan and is found in specific, often moist and shaded habitats. Its foliage consists of rounded, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating.

Due to its limited distribution and unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
