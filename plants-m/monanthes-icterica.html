Monanthes icterica

Monanthes icterica, commonly known as the golden flame lily, is a striking perennial plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. The plant features strap-like leaves that are broad and lance-shaped, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a bold, colorful addition to their landscape.

Due to its unique appearance and seasonal bloom, it is often cultivated for both its aesthetic value and its ability to attract pollinators.
