Mentha × dalmatica var. juranyiana

Mentha × dalmatica var. juranyiana, commonly known as the Hungarian mint, is a hybrid species within the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly fuzzy leaves that are typically a deep green in color. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pale purple or blue, arranged in whorled clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This variety is valued for its use in herbal teas and as a ornamental plant in gardens.
