Myristica petiolata

Myristica petiolata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, evergreen leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces oval-shaped fruits that contain a single seed, which is the source of its essential oils and aromatic compounds. Its roots and leaves are also used in traditional medicine for their purported therapeutic properties.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its economic value.
