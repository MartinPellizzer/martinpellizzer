Masdevallia burzlaffiana

Masdevallia burzlaffiana, commonly known as the Burzlaff's masdevallia, is a terrestrial orchid native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, often colorful flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant typically features a short rhizome with a few fleshy roots and produces a single, erect scape bearing several showy, bilaterally symmetric flowers. Its leaves are linear and arranged in a rosette, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to cooler, humid environments.
