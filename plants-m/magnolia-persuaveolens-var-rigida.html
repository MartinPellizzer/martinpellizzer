Magnolia persuaveolens var. rigida

Magnolia persuaveolens var. rigida, commonly known as the stiff magnolia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a smooth texture and a dark green upper surface. The plant typically grows to a height of 6 to 15 feet and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively disease-resistant.
