Margaritaria indica f. indica

Margaritaria indica f. indica, commonly known as the Indian spider plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, white, spider-like flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for hanging baskets and indoor gardening.
