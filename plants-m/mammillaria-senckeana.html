Mammillaria senckeana

Mammillaria senckeana, commonly known as the Sencke's pincushion cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and dense covering of white to yellow spines. The plant produces small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its slow growth rate and unique appearance make it a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
