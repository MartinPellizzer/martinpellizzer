Myrcia neorubella

Myrcia neorubella, commonly known as the red-leaved myrtle, is a shrub or small tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green to reddish-brown leaves, which are often aromatic. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, dark red berries. Myrcia neorubella is adapted to humid, tropical climates and thrives in well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
