Melissa bicornis

Melissa bicornis, commonly known as lemon balm, is a perennial herb native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a serrated margin and a strong, pleasant lemony scent. Its small, pale yellow flowers are clustered in whorls along the stems and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Melissa bicornis is often cultivated for its calming properties and used in herbal teas and aromatherapy.
