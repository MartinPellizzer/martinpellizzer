Mimulus washoensis

Mimulus washoensis, commonly known as the Wasco monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange or yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and in open woodlands. It has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Mimulus washoensis is an important plant for pollinators such as bees and butterflies due to its nectar-rich flowers.
