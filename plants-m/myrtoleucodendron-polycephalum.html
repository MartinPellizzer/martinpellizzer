Myrtoleucodendron polycephalum

Myrtoleucodendron polycephalum, commonly known as the mountain myrtle, is a rare and endangered plant species native to the high-altitude regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests or on rocky slopes. Its flowers produce small, berry-like fruits that are an important food source for local wildlife.

Due to habitat loss and climate change, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
