Minuartia setacea subsp. setacea

Minuartia setacea subsp.

setacea, commonly known as the silky sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to rocky and coastal areas. It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and small, clustered flowers that range from white to pink.

The plant features narrow, linear leaves covered in fine, silky hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in maritime environments.

This subspecies is known for its resilience to harsh conditions, including salt spray and strong winds.
