Miscolobium glandulosum

Miscolobium glandulosum, commonly known as the stickyleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy seeds dispersed by the wind. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
