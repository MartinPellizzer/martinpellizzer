Memecylon ponmudianum

Memecylon ponmudianum, commonly known as the Malabar memecylon, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the family Melastomataceae and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth upper surface and a hairy underside. The plant produces small, clustered white or pink flowers that bloom during the rainy season. It is typically found in moist, shaded undergrowth of tropical forests.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, dry seeds.
