Melastoma aubletianum

Melastoma aubletianum, commonly known as the red-flowered melastoma, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its dense, glossy foliage and showy, tubular red flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its flowers are pollinated by birds and insects, and the fruit is a capsule that disperses seeds through wind.

Melastoma aubletianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
