Messerschmidia angustifolia

Messerschmidia angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to rocky and sandy soils in arid regions.

It features small, linear leaves that are typically less than 1 centimeter in width, arranged closely along its stems. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale pink flowers during the spring and early summer. It is adapted to thrive in harsh environments with minimal water availability.

Its botanical structure reflects its resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.
