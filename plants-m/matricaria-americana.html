Matricaria americana

Matricaria americana, commonly known as American chamomile, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers with white centers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas such as meadows and roadsides. Its leaves are finely divided and aromatic, often used in herbal teas.

The flowers are rich in essential oils and have been traditionally used for their calming and anti-inflammatory properties.
