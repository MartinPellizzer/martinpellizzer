Monolophus limianus

Monolophus limianus, commonly known as the lima bean vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy, butterfly-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several edible seeds, which are widely used in culinary applications. Its growth habit and morphology make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The species is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, contributing to soil fertility.
