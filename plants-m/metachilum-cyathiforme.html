Metachilum cyathiforme

Metachilum cyathiforme, commonly known as the cup-shaped orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, cup-like inflorescence that resembles a shallow bowl, which is a distinctive botanical feature. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in a dense cluster. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
