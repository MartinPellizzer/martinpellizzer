Myrcia coriacea var. swartziana

Myrcia coriacea var. swartziana, commonly known as the Brazilian peppercorn, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its small, dark red berries and aromatic leaves. The plant is evergreen, with glossy, thick foliage that provides dense shade. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
