Melaleuca lysicephala

Melaleuca lysicephala, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, red or pink flower spikes that resemble bottlebrushes, which are clustered at the ends of its branches. The leaves are narrow, linear, and often covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or sandy habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
