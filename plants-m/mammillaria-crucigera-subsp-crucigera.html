Mammillaria crucigera subsp. crucigera

Mammillaria crucigera subsp.

crucigera, commonly known as the cross-ribbed mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico. It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in white to pale green tubercles.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it thrives in well-drained soil with minimal water.

It is a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cultivation due to its compact size and ornamental appearance.
