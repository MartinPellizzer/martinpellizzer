Mapania ballehensis

Mapania ballehensis, commonly known as the Ballehensis maple, is a deciduous tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which typically have five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by wind. Its bark is smooth and grayish in color, and it thrives in humid, well-drained soils.

Mapania ballehensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often planted in gardens and parks for its aesthetic appeal.
