Mentha sylvestris subsp. nemorosa

Mentha sylvestris subsp.

nemorosa, commonly known as wood mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features oval, dark green leaves with a slightly serrated margin and produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in whorled clusters. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its characteristic minty scent.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
