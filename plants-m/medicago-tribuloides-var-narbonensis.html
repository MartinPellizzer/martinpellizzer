Medicago tribuloides var. narbonensis

Medicago tribuloides var. narbonensis, commonly known as the Narbonne sicklevetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and trifoliate leaves with narrow, pointed leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences, which are followed by elongated, twisted pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or semi-arid environments.

This variety is valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a forage plant for livestock.
