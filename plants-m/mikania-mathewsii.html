Mikania mathewsii

Mikania mathewsii, commonly known as the Mathews' Mikania, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine with slender, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. Its small, yellowish flowers appear in clusters and are followed by fluffy, white seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Mikania mathewsii is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges.
