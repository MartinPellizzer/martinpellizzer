Mentha aquatica var. limnobia

Mentha aquatica var. limnobia, commonly known as water mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist or aquatic environments such as marshes, ponds, and stream banks. The plant has square, hairy stems and opposite, serrated leaves that are often aromatic. Its small, pale blue or purple flowers appear in whorled clusters during the summer months.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in herbal teas and traditional remedies.
