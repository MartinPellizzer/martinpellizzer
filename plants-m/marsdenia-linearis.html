Marsdenia linearis

Marsdenia linearis, commonly known as the slender Marsdenia, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its long, slender stems and opposite, linear leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, and it is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its fibrous roots and woody stems make it adaptable to various soil types, and it is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
