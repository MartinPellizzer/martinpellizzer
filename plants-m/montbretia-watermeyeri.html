Montbretia watermeyeri

Montbretia watermeyeri, commonly known as the water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to southern Africa.

It features broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface, supported by long, flexible petioles. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white, with a distinctive yellow center. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in ponds and lakes.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and water features worldwide.
