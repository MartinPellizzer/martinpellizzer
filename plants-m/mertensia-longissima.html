Mertensia longissima

Mertensia longissima, commonly known as longleaf bluebells, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. Its leaves are long and narrow, often reaching up to 12 inches in length.

Mertensia longissima is a member of the nightshade family and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance.
