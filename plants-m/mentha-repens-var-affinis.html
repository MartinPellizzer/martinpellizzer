Mentha repens var. affinis

Mentha repens var. affinis, commonly known as creeping mint or ground mint, is a variant of the mint family (Lamiaceae) characterized by its low-growing, spreading habit.

It typically features aromatic, serrated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its small, tubular flowers are usually pale blue or purple. This plant is native to parts of Europe and Asia and thrives in moist, shady environments. It is often found in woodland areas, along stream banks, and in damp meadows.

Mentha repens var. affinis is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
