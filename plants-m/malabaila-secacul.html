Malabaila secacul

Malabaila secacul, commonly known as the red spider lily, is a striking ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features vibrant red flowers with a distinctive spider-like appearance, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has strap-like, dark green leaves that form a basal rosette, and it thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun. Malabaila secacul is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable climates.

It is also noted for its ability to naturalize and spread through underground bulbs.
