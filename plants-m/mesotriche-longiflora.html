Mesotriche longiflora

Mesotriche longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered mesotriche, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months. The plant's leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, arranged alternately along the stem. Mesotriche longiflora thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along streambanks.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
