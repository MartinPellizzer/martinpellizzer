Merremia tonkinensis

Merremia tonkinensis, commonly known as the Vietnamese morning glory, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces yellowish-orange flowers that open in the morning and close at dusk. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain numerous seeds.

Merremia tonkinensis is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in moist, shady environments.
