Myriopus paniculatus var. austrinus

Myriopus paniculatus var. austrinus, commonly known as the Australian sand couch, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained sandy soils and is often found in arid and semi-arid regions.

This species is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and is used in rehabilitation of degraded lands.
