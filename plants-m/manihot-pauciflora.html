Manihot pauciflora

Manihot pauciflora, commonly known as the sparse-flowered arrowroot, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, starchy rhizomes, which are used as a food source. The plant typically grows in wet, lowland areas and has simple, alternate leaves with a waxy surface. Its flowers are small and clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Due to its adaptability and nutritional value, it is sometimes cultivated for both culinary and medicinal purposes.
