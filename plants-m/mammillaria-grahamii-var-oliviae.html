Mammillaria grahamii var. oliviae

Mammillaria grahamii var. oliviae, commonly known as the Olivia's Hedgehog Cactus, is a compact, spineless cactus native to Mexico.

It features a globular or cylindrical stem with a dense covering of white, woolly hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by small, red fruits. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ease of care in both indoor and outdoor settings.
