Malus carolinensis

Malus carolinensis, commonly known as the Carolina crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its showy, single or clustered white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature, though they are not commonly consumed by humans. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and turn yellow in the fall, adding to its ornamental value.

The tree is often used in landscaping for its ornamental flowers and adaptability to various soil types.
