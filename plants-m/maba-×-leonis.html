Maba × leonis

Maba × leonis, commonly known as the leopard tree, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between *Maba dodecandra* and *Maba leonis*.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its large, leathery leaves and striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a decorative plant.
