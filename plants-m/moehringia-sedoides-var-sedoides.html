Moehringia sedoides var. sedoides

Moehringia sedoides var. sedoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically features small, hairy leaves that are lance-shaped and clustered at the base of the plant. The flowers are tiny, pink to purple, and clustered in dense, rounded inflorescences. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

This plant is adapted to cold climates and is often used in native plant gardens for its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
