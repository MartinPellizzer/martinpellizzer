Microlicia maximowicziana var. parvifolia

Microlicia maximowicziana var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved microlicia, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are often borne on short pedicels. Its flowering period usually occurs during the warmer months, and it is adapted to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and potential ecological role in its native habitat.
