Minthostachys acutifolia

Minthostachys acutifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mintgrass, is a perennial herb native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically ranging in color from white to pale purple. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.

Due to its aromatic properties, it is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
