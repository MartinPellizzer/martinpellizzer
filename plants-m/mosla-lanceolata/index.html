Mosla Lanceolata

Lanceleaf mosla, scientifically known as Mosla lanceolata, is a hardy perennial herb native to Asia. It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. Often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb, it has gained popularity in modern horticulture. This plant thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight. Its versatility and ornamental value make it a sought-after addition to gardens and landscapes.
