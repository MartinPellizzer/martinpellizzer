Morus alba var. indica

Morus alba var. indica, commonly known as Indian mulberry, is a variant of the white mulberry native to South Asia.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by heart-shaped leaves that are typically light green on top and pale underneath. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits called mulberries, which are edible and have a sweet, juicy flavor. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its fruit and as a source of leaves for silkworms.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various soil types.
