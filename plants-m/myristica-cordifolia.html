Myristica cordifolia

Myristica cordifolia, commonly known as the heart-shaped nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into woody capsules containing the aromatic seeds used for nutmeg and mace. Its bark and leaves are also used in traditional medicine for their medicinal properties.

The tree thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its valuable spices.
