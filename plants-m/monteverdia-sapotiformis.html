Monteverdia sapotiformis

Monteverdia sapotiformis, commonly known as the Mexican starfruit, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces bright orange, star-shaped fruits that are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

Monteverdia sapotiformis is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
