Monochilus

Monochilus, commonly known as the single-chambered orchid, is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

These plants are characterized by their unique floral structure, which features a single chambered lip that distinguishes them from other orchid genera. They are predominantly found in tropical and subtropical regions, often growing as epiphytes on trees or as terrestrial plants in moist environments. Monochilus species typically have slender, strap-like leaves and produce small, colorful flowers that bloom in clusters.

Their botanical significance lies in their specialized morphology and ecological adaptations, making them interesting subjects for botanical study.
