Mimulus × maculosus

Mimulus × maculosus, commonly known as the spotted monkeyflower, is a hybrid species within the genus Mimulus.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically range in color from yellow to orange, with distinctive red or purple spots. The plant is native to the western United States, where it thrives in moist, sunny habitats such as stream banks and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are erect and branching.

Mimulus × maculosus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
