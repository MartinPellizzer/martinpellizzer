Mariscus pungens

Mariscus pungens, commonly known as the spiked spurge, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny stems and alternate leaves that are typically lance-shaped. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in woodland edges or open habitats.

Mariscus pungens is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
