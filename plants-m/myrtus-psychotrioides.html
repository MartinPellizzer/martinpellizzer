Myrtus psychotrioides

Myrtus psychotrioides, commonly known as the Canary Islands myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and small white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically red when ripe. It thrives in well-drained soil and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its compact growth and fragrant foliage.

Myrtus psychotrioides is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
