Miconia pachydonta

Miconia pachydonta, commonly known as the thick-leaved miconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves that are often heart-shaped or ovate in form. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that are typically borne on long, slender peduncles. Its fruits are round, fleshy berries that turn red when mature, attracting various bird species.

Miconia pachydonta is often found in humid lowland forests and is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets.
