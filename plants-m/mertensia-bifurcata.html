Mertensia bifurcata

Mertensia bifurcata, commonly known as the twin-lobed bluebells, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features basal leaves that are heart-shaped and often have a bluish tinge, while its stems are hairy and upright. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in clusters, typically blooming in early spring. The flowers are usually pale blue to violet, with a distinctive two-lobed shape at the base.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland habitats.
