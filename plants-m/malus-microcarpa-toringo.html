Malus microcarpa toringo

Malus microcarpa toringo, commonly known as the Toringo apple, is a small, deciduous tree native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and oval-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, yellow-green fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value and historical significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
