Marsdenia angustata

Marsdenia angustata, commonly known as the narrowleaf Marsdenia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, often followed by follicles containing seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, typically in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Marsdenia angustata is valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
