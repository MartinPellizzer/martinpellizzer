Medinilla

Medinilla, commonly known as the "Christmas bell," is a genus of flowering plants native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Myrtle family (Myrtaceae) and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and white. The plants typically have glossy, elliptical leaves and are often grown as ornamental plants due to their attractive appearance.

Medinilla species are evergreen shrubs that thrive in tropical and subtropical climates, making them popular in horticulture.
