Myrsine asymmetrica subsp. parvifolia

Myrsine asymmetrica subsp.

parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved myrsine, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia. It features small, elliptical leaves that are usually glabrous and have a leathery texture.

The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by bright red berries that are an important food source for birds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its compact growth habit.
