Mentha microphylla var. glareosa

Mentha microphylla var. glareosa, commonly known as narrow-leaf peppermint, is a variety of the peppermint plant characterized by its slender, dark green leaves and strong menthol aroma.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves used in herbal teas, aromatherapy, and as a natural insect repellent.

The variety is noted for its high concentration of menthol and other essential oils, making it valuable in both traditional and modern applications.
