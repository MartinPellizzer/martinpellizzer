Marica candida

Marica candida, commonly known as the white bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. Its leaves are broad and oval, with a slightly hairy texture.

Marica candida is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
