Medicago orthoceras

Medicago orthoceras, commonly known as the narrow-leafed clover, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its small, trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is native to Europe and Asia but has been introduced to other regions, including North America. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed areas.

Medicago orthoceras is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
