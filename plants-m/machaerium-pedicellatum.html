Machaerium pedicellatum

Machaerium pedicellatum, commonly known as the red-stemmed grass, is a tropical perennial plant native to regions of South America.

Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Machaerium pedicellatum is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various climates.
