Macfadyena corymbosa

Macfadyena corymbosa, commonly known as the crown of thorns, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and the Americas.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is characterized by its woody stems and ability to climb by twining around supports. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, round seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to thrive in warm, sunny climates.
