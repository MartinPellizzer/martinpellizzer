Mimusops sulcata

Mimusops sulcata, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, fragrant yellow flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits that are encased in a thick, waxy coating. Its wood is highly valued for its durability and fine grain, making it important in local timber industries.

The plant is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
