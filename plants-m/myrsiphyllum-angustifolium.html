Myrsiphyllum angustifolium

Myrsiphyllum angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved myrsiphyllum, is a shrub native to the eastern regions of Australia.

It features small, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, often white or pale pink in color, which bloom during the spring and summer seasons. Its botanical structure includes a woody stem with dense, hairy foliage, and it is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understoreys.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticultural settings.
