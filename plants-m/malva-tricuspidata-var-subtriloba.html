Malva tricuspidata var. subtriloba

Malva tricuspidata var. subtriloba, commonly known as the three-lobed mallow, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features heart-shaped leaves with three distinct lobes, and its flowers are typically pink or purple, with five petals. The plant grows up to 1 meter tall and produces fluffy seed capsules that disperse seeds by wind. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
