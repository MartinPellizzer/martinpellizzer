Mentha × dumetorum var. pubescens

Mentha × dumetorum var. pubescens, commonly known as pine mint, is a hybrid plant that exhibits characteristics of both spearmint and water mint.

It is native to Europe and western Asia, often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. The plant features erect, hairy stems and oval-shaped leaves with a serrated margin. Its fragrant leaves emit a strong, minty aroma when crushed, making it a popular choice for culinary and medicinal uses.

The flowers are small, greenish-white, and typically appear in whorled clusters along the stem.
