Mikania campii

Mikania campii, commonly known as the Mexican vine or creeping Mikania, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rapid growth and trailing habit. The plant features small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are typically opposite, elliptical, and have a rough texture.

Mikania campii is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges, where it can spread aggressively.
