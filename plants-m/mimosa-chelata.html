Mimosa chelata

Mimosa chelata, commonly known as the velvet leaf mimosa, is a deciduous shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves that are typically pink to reddish in color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its growth habit is compact, making it suitable for ornamental use in gardens.

Mimosa chelata is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to drought conditions.
