Morina ludlowii

Morina ludlowii, commonly known as the Ludlow's moringa, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and elliptical, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds. It is adapted to grow in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates.

Due to its limited distribution and unique ecological role, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
