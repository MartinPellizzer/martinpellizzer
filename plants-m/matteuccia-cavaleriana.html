Matteuccia cavaleriana

Matteuccia cavaleriana, commonly known as the giant shield fern, is a striking perennial fern native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its large, shield-shaped fronds that can grow up to 1.5 meters in length, making it one of the largest ferns in the world. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas with rich, loamy soil. Its fronds are bipinnate, with a distinctive zigzag pattern along the rachis, and the spores are produced in clusters on the underside of the leaves.

Matteuccia cavaleriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with the right conditions.
