Mentha subtomentella

Mentha subtomentella, commonly known as the woolly mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring hairy, ovate leaves with a minty aroma. Its flowers are small, tubular, and usually pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem.

Mentha subtomentella is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks.
