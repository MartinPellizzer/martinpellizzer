Myroxylon balsamum var. pereirae

Myroxylon balsamum var. pereirae, commonly known as wild Copaiba, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and dense, pyramidal crown. The plant produces a valuable resin, known as copaiba balsam, which is harvested from its trunk. The leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, with a leathery texture.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is an important species for both ecological and commercial purposes.
