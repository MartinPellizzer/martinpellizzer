Microgramma persicariifolia

Microgramma persicariifolia, commonly known as the Persian feather fern, is a species of fern native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, lacy fronds that resemble the structure of a fern's traditional form. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are bipinnate, with small, finger-like segments that give it a feathery appearance.

The spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, which are clustered in sori, a typical feature of ferns.
