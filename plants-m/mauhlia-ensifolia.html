Mauhlia ensifolia

Mauhlia ensifolia, commonly known as the swordleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Mauhlia ensifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
