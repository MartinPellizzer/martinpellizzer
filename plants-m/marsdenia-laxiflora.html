Marsdenia laxiflora

Marsdenia laxiflora, commonly known as the slender-flowered Marsdenia, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, simple leaves. The flowers are small, tubular, and typically yellowish-green, with a prominent corona. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded areas.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
