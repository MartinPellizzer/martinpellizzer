Myrcia brevispicata

Myrcia brevispicata, commonly known as the short-spined myrtle, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in coastal areas.

Myrcia brevispicata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
