Mertensia bancroftii var. vitellina

Mertensia bancroftii var. vitellina, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine white down. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to woodland gardens and native plant landscapes.
