Myosotis petiolata var. pottsiana

Myosotis petiolata var. pottsiana, commonly known as the Potts' bluebell, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and streambanks. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a hairy texture, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting native insect populations.
