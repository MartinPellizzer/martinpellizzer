Myrciaria strigipes var. longifolia

Myrciaria strigipes var. longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf myrcia, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, rough-textured leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pink, followed by edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. Its growth habit includes dense foliage and adaptability to various soil types, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.

The varietal name "longifolia" refers to its long, slender leaves, which distinguish it from other varieties within the Myrciaria genus.
