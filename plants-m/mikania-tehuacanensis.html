Mikania tehuacanensis

Mikania tehuacanensis, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rapid growth and climbing habit. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, which bloom in the morning. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and often twine around supporting structures.

Mikania tehuacanensis is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges.
