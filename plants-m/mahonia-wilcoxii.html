Mahonia wilcoxii

Mahonia wilcoxii, commonly known as the Wilcox mahonia, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern California.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, with leathery, dark green leaves that have a distinctive serrated margin. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, followed by blue-black berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a hardy addition to coastal gardens.

Mahonia wilcoxii is valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to withstand coastal conditions.
