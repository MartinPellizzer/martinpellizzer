Melilotus psoraloides

Melilotus psoraloides, commonly known as yellow melilot, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod-like fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

Melilotus psoraloides is often found in meadows and along roadsides, and it has been used historically in traditional medicine.
