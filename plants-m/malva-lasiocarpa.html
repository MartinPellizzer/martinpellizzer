Malva lasiocarpa

Malva lasiocarpa, commonly known as the woolly mallow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus, and is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage. The plant produces showy pink to purple flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine, white pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Malva lasiocarpa is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.
