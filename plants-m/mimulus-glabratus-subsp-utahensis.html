Mimulus glabratus subsp. utahensis

Mimulus glabratus subsp.

utahensis, commonly known as the Utah monkeyflower, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which are typically tubular with a distinctive spur at the back.

The plant has smooth, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in stream banks and meadows.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
