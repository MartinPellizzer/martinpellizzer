Myzorrhiza multiflora

Myzorrhiza multiflora, commonly known as the Natal orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and rocky slopes. Its root system forms symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi, enhancing nutrient uptake.

The leaves are narrow and grass-like, while the inflorescence consists of clustered, showy blooms in shades of white, pink, and purple.
