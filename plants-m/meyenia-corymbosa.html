Meyenia corymbosa

Meyenia corymbosa, commonly known as the clustered meyenia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Meyenia corymbosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
