Mascagnia brevifolia

Mascagnia brevifolia, commonly known as the short-leaved mascagnia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in desert and semi-desert environments, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions.

Mascagnia brevifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
