Myonima latifolia

Myonima latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed myonima, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae, which includes eucalyptus and other aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Myonima latifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
