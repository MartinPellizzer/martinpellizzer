Myrciaria splendens

Myrciaria splendens, commonly known as the Brazilian orange or pitanga, is a small evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces small, orange-colored berries that are edible and have a tart, citrus-like flavor. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

Myrciaria splendens is also valued in traditional medicine for its various health benefits.
