Metrosideros laurifolia

Metrosideros laurifolia, commonly known as the coastal kohuhu, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive red or orange berries. It thrives in coastal environments and is often found in dunes and rocky shores.

The species is known for its hardy nature and ability to withstand salt spray and strong winds.
