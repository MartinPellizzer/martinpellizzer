Malvastrum depressum

Malvastrum depressum, commonly known as the Mexican mallow, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as prairies and wetlands. The plant features showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are palmately lobed and covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various soil types.
