Macaranga dioica

Macaranga dioica, commonly known as the two-leaf or split-leaf tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, simple, and often lobed leaves. The plant is typically found in lowland rainforests and can grow up to 20 meters tall. Its flowers are small and clustered in cupular inflorescences, and it produces latex that is used in traditional crafts.

The leaves are also used in local medicine for their purported healing properties.
