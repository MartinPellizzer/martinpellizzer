Macropeplus ligustrinus var. pyreneus

Macropeplus ligustrinus var. pyreneus, commonly known as the Pyrenean ivy.

This variant is a climbing plant native to the Pyrenees region, characterized by its vigorous growth and ability to climb walls and trees. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a glossy, dark green appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters, which are followed by orange-red berries.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to cover vertical surfaces.
