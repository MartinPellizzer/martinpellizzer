Myrcia communis var. latifolia

Myrcia communis var. latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf myrcia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. It produces edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine.

The plant's aromatic foliage and ornamental value make it a popular choice for horticultural use.
