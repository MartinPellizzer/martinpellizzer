Maclura mora

Maclura mora, commonly known as the yellow wood or devil's backbone, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the family Moraceae, which includes figs and mulberries. The plant is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant, and it produces small, round fruits that turn from green to yellow or orange as they ripen.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its hard, durable wood.
