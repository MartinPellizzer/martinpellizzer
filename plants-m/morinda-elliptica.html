Morinda elliptica

Morinda elliptica, commonly known as red sandalwood, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves and small, clustered flowers that range from yellow to red in color. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Morinda elliptica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in herbal remedies for its purported medicinal properties.
