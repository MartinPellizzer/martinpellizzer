Myrcia multiflora var. ramulosa

Myrcia multiflora var. ramulosa, commonly known as the red myrtle, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, which bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, red, fleshy drupe that contains several seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
