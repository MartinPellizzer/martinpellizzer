Mammillaria pomacea

Mammillaria pomacea, commonly known as the globular cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its spherical shape and dense covering of white to pale green spines. The plant typically grows to a height of about 5 to 10 centimeters, with a compact and rounded form. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, appearing in the axils of the areoles.

Mammillaria pomacea is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.
