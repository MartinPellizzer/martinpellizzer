Miconia summa

Miconia summa, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Miconia summa*, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruits are red, fleshy berries that contain numerous seeds.

Miconia summa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
