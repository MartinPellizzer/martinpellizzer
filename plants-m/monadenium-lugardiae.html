Monadenium lugardiae

Monadenium lugardiae, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that contains toxic compounds, which serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

Its ecological role includes providing nectar for pollinators and supporting local biodiversity.
