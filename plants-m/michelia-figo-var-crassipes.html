Michelia figo var. crassipes

Michelia figo var. crassipes, commonly known as the broad-leaved michelia, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in spring. The leaves are broad, ovate, and have a smooth, glossy surface. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
