Mauritiella disticha

Mauritiella disticha, commonly known as the double-leaved palm, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its distinctive two-rowed arrangement of leaves, giving it a unique appearance. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are broad, leathery, and often have a bluish-green hue.

The inflorescences are clustered and produce small, yellowish flowers that are followed by red, fleshy fruits.
