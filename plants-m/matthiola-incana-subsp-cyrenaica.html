Matthiola incana subsp. cyrenaica

Matthiola incana subsp.

cyrenaica, commonly known as the Cyprus wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its showy, brightly colored flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage is hairy, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
