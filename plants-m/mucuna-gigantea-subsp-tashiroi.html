Mucuna gigantea subsp. tashiroi

Mucuna gigantea subsp.

tashiroi, commonly known as the Japanese velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its large, trifoliate leaves and distinctive, hairy pods that contain edible seeds.

The plant produces showy, cream-colored flowers that are often clustered in racemes. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its use in traditional medicine and as a source of protein-rich seeds.
