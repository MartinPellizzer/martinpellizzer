Mascarenhasia phyllocalyx

Mascarenhasia phyllocalyx, commonly known as the Mascarene starfruit, is a flowering plant native to the Mascarene Islands in the Indian Ocean.

It is a small shrub characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers. The plant produces bright yellow, fleshy fruits that are edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
