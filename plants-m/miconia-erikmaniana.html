Miconia erikmaniana

Miconia erikmaniana, commonly known as the Brazilian starfruit, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy fruits resembling starfruits. Its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets make it an invasive species in some regions.

The plant's botanical features make it both ecologically significant and a challenge for land management.
