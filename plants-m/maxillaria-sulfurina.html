Maxillaria sulfurina

Maxillaria sulfurina, commonly known as the sulfur orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of yellow and green flowers with a distinctive sulfur-like scent, which is a key feature for attracting pollinators. Maxillaria sulfurina prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Its resilience and ornamental value contribute to its popularity among orchid enthusiasts.
