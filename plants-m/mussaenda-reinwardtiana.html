Mussaenda reinwardtiana

Mussaenda reinwardtiana, commonly known as the flame bush, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that can grow up to 3 meters tall, featuring dense, dark green leaves and showy, clustered flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The flowers are typically borne in terminal panicles and are surrounded by prominent bracts that enhance their visual appeal. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
