Menispermum canadense

Menispermum canadense, commonly known as Canadian arrowroot, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces bright red berries that contain toxic seeds, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found along stream banks and in woodland areas.

Its historical use by Indigenous peoples for medicinal purposes highlights its cultural significance.
