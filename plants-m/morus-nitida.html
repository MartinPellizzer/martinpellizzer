Morus nitida

Morus nitida, commonly known as the Chinese mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to China and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and smooth, dark bark. The plant produces small, pale green leaves that are opposite in arrangement, and it is known for its rapid growth rate. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Morus nitida is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical significance in traditional medicine.
