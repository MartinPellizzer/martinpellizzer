Melilotus bonplandii

Melilotus bonplandii, commonly known as the Bonpland's sweet clover, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is known for its strong, sweet fragrance, which attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open areas or along riverbanks.

Melilotus bonplandii is also valued for its potential in ecological restoration due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.
