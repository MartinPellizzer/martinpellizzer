Melanthesa ovalifolia

Melanthesa ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leaf melanthus, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its oval-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
