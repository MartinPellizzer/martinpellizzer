Malpighia punicifolia var. obovata

Malpighia punicifolia var. obovata, commonly known as the red mombin, is a species of flowering plant in the family Malpighiaceae.

It is native to Central and South America and is characterized by its showy, red-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that are edible and have a tart, citrus-like flavor. Its leaves are typically ovate to obovate in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the edible fruits it yields.
