Madhuca markleeana

Madhuca markleeana, commonly known as the Khasi madhuca, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the northeastern regions of India and Bangladesh.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by oval-shaped fruits containing a single seed.

It is often found in moist, shaded forest environments and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
