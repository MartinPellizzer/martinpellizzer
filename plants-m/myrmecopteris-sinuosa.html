Myrmecopteris sinuosa

Myrmecopteris sinuosa, commonly known as the velvet fern, is a species of cycad that is native to the subtropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, feathery fronds that give it a soft, velvety appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically pollinated by insects. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate.

Due to its slow growth and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
