Mentha × maximilianea var. schultzii

Mentha × maximilianea var. schultzii, commonly known as Schultze mint, is a hybrid perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, square stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, pale blue to violet flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Schultze mint is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
