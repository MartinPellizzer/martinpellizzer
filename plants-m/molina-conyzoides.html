Molina conyzoides

Molina conyzoides, commonly known as the common cudweed, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a low-growing annual herb that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant has small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in fluffy, spherical inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, with a serrated edge, and it often grows in disturbed soils.

Molina conyzoides is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environments, including roadsides and fields.
