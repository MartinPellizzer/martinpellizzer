Myrcia arenicola

Myrcia arenicola, commonly known as the coastal myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to coastal regions of Brazil.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red when mature. It thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal dunes and scrublands.

Myrcia arenicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
