Malacothamnus arcuatus var. arcuatus

Malacothamnus arcuatus var. arcuatus, commonly known as the bentleaf chenopod, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, scale-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hair. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to drought conditions and high sunlight exposure.
