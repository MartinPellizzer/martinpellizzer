Marsdenia callosa

Marsdenia callosa, commonly known as the woolly Marsdenia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. Its leaves are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs, giving it a woolly appearance.

Marsdenia callosa is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and showy blooms.
