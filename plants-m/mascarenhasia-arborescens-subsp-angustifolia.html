Mascarenhasia arborescens subsp. angustifolia

Mascarenhasia arborescens subsp.

angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf Mascarenhasia, is a shrub native to the Mascarene Islands. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers.

The plant thrives in coastal environments and is adapted to saline and well-drained soils. It is often found in dry, sunny habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Due to its restricted distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
