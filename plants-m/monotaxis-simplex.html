Monotaxis simplex

Monotaxis simplex, commonly known as the narrowleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2 to 5 meters. The plant features simple, opposite, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are showy, with five petals and a prominent central stamen, and they bloom in clusters.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, dry seeds.
