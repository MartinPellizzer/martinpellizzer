Melaleuca interstans

Melaleuca interstans, commonly known as the honeymyrtle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub that typically grows in coastal and sandy soils. The plant produces showy, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, often with a distinctive scent when crushed.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its aromatic properties.
