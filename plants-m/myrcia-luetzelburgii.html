Myrcia luetzelburgii

Myrcia luetzelburgii, commonly known as Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy red flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in coastal areas.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and potentially invasive in certain regions.
