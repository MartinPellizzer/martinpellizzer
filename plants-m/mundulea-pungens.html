Mundulea pungens

Mundulea pungens, commonly known as the African sandthorn, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that contain a single seed, which is often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its ecological importance includes providing food for wildlife and stabilizing sandy soils.
