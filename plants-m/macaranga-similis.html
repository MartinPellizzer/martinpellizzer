Macaranga similis

Macaranga similis, commonly known as the common macaranga, is a flowering plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by three-lobed, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Macaranga similis is also known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets.
