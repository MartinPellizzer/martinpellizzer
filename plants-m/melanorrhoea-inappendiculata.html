Melanorrhoea inappendiculata

Melanorrhoea inappendiculata, commonly known as the appendiculate melanic orchid, is a rare and striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black, velvety leaves and clusters of fragrant, pale yellow flowers. Native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, this plant thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its unique morphology includes a short, thick rhizome and specialized roots that aid in nutrient absorption.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern in its native range.
