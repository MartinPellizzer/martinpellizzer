Myrceugenia ovata var. gracilis

Myrceugenia ovata var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender myrtle, is a species native to the temperate rainforests of southern Brazil.

It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid coastal regions. Its foliage is used in traditional medicine, and it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat for various insects and birds.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological significance in its native habitat.
