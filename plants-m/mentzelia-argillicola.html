Mentzelia argillicola

Mentzelia argillicola, commonly known as the clay-plain bluemink, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, often bluish or purplish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy, or clay soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments.
