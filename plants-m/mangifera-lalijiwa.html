Mangifera lalijiwa

Mangifera lalijiwa, commonly known as the Lalijiwa mango, is a tropical fruit-bearing tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its large, elongated mango fruits with a smooth, thin skin. The fruit has a sweet and aromatic pulp, often with a distinct flavor profile compared to other mango varieties. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The tree is valued not only for its fruit but also for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and floral display.
