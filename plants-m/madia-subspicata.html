Madia subspicata

Madia subspicata, commonly known as the desert aster, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, displaying a low, sprawling growth habit with hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers bloom in shades of yellow to orange, often appearing in late summer to early fall.

Madia subspicata is adapted to withstand drought and heat, making it a resilient species in harsh desert conditions.
