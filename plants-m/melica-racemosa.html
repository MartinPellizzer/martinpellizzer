Melica racemosa

Melica racemosa, commonly known as slender false brome, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems and produces dense, spike-like clusters of small, greenish flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
