Muraltia micrantha

Muraltia micrantha, commonly known as the small-fruited muraltia, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to humid, warm climates.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal uses, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and research settings.
