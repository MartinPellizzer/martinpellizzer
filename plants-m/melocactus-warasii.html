Melocactus warasii

Melocactus warasii, commonly known as the Warasii melon cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of northern Argentina and southern Bolivia.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem and a single, large, globular fruit that resembles a melon, hence its common name. The plant produces a spiny, globular fruit that is typically yellow or orange when ripe. Its growth is slow, and it thrives in well-drained soils with minimal water.

Melocactus warasii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
