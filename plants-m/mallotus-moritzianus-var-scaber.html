Mallotus moritzianus var. scaber

Mallotus moritzianus var. scaber, commonly known as the rough-leaved holly, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage, with leathery, elliptical leaves that have a rough, textured surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by oval, red or black berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and semi-coastal regions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide habitat for native wildlife.
