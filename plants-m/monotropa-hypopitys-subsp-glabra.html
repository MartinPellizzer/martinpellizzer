Monotropa hypopitys subsp. glabra

Monotropa hypopitys subsp.

glabra, commonly known as ghost plant, is a parasitic plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies on fungi for nutrients. It is native to eastern North America and thrives in shaded, moist forest environments.

The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often remain evergreen throughout the year.

The species is notable for its unique ecological role in mycorrhizal networks.
