Meconopsis henrici var. psilonomma

Meconopsis henrici var. psilonomma, commonly known as the Tibetan blue poppy, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers, which bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

This variant is distinguished by its smaller, more compact flowers compared to other meconopsis species.
