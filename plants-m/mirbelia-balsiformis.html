Mirbelia balsiformis

Mirbelia balsiformis, commonly known as the balsam tree or false balsa, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, fibrous wood and distinctive, often twisted growth habit. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds.

Mirbelia balsiformis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
