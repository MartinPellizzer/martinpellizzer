Microcerasus glandulosa f. pogonostyla

Microcerasus glandulosa f. pogonostyla, commonly known as the hairy-stemmed Chinese mahonia, is a variant of the Chinese mahonia species.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, spiny stems and compound leaves with glandular dots. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by orange-red berries in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a decorative hedge.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
