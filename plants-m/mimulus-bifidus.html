Mimulus bifidus

Mimulus bifidus, commonly known as the twin-lobed monkeyflower, is a herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking orange to red flowers, which are typically spurred at the back, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often lobed, contributing to its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in streambanks and woodland areas.

Mimulus bifidus plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
