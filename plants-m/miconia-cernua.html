Miconia cernua

Miconia cernua, commonly known as the hanging miconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its broad, palmate leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, and it is known for its rapid growth and invasive potential in some regions. Its fruit is a fleshy, red berry that contains numerous seeds, aiding in its spread.

Miconia cernua is often found in moist, shaded areas of tropical forests.
