Mentha longifolia var. densicapilla

Mentha longifolia var. densicapilla, commonly known as the woolly mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage that gives it its common name, and it produces small, pale blue or purple flowers. The plant has square stems and opposite leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its aromatic leaves and is sometimes used in herbal teas or as a culinary herb.
