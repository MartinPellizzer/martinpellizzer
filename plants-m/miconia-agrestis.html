Miconia agrestis

Miconia agrestis, commonly known as the roughleaf miconia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves with a rough, hairy texture. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Miconia agrestis is also known for its ability to spread rapidly, making it an invasive species in some regions.
