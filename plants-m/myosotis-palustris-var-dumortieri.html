Myosotis palustris var. dumortieri

Myosotis palustris var. dumortieri, commonly known as the marsh forget-me-not, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and stream banks, where it forms dense colonies. The plant produces small, sky-blue flowers with five petals, typical of the Myosotis genus. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
