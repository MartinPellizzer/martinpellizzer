Miconia grandidentata

Miconia grandidentata, commonly known as the giant miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers, which are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Miconia grandidentata is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, making it a potential invasive species in certain regions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of tropical ecosystems, though its ecological impact remains a subject of study.
