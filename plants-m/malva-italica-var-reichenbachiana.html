Malva italica var. reichenbachiana

Malva italica var. reichenbachiana, commonly known as the Italian mallow or Reichenbach's mallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in meadows, along stream banks, and in disturbed habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its soothing properties.
