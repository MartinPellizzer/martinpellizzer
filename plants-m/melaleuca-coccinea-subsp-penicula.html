Melaleuca coccinea subsp. penicula

Melaleuca coccinea subsp.

penicula, commonly known as the red bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia. It is characterized by its distinctive red or orange cylindrical flower spikes that emerge from dense clusters of small, leaf-like structures.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, aromatic leaves that are often shed after flowering. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its flowers attract nectar-feeding birds and insects, contributing to its ecological significance.
