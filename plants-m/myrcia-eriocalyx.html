Myrcia eriocalyx

Myrcia eriocalyx, commonly known as the wax myrtle, is a shrub or small tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, waxy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, urn-shaped flowers that are typically white or pink, followed by red or purple berries. Its leaves contain essential oils and have been used for their purported medicinal properties, including antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects.

Myrcia eriocalyx thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in forest understories.
