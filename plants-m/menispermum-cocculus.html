Menispermum cocculus

Menispermum cocculus, commonly known as the devil's bit scabious, is a flowering plant belonging to the Menispermaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in moist woodlands and stream banks. Its fruit is a distinctive, spiny capsule that splits open to release its seeds.

The plant has been used historically in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
