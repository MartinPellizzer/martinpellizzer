Myrtiluma

Myrtiluma, commonly known as the blueberry shrub, is a genus of flowering plants in the Ericaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen or deciduous shrubs with small, often bilaterally symmetrical leaves. The flowers are typically bell-shaped and borne in clusters, while the fruit is a small, fleshy berry that ranges in color from blue to purple. Myrtiluma species are adapted to acidic soils and thrive in temperate climates.

These plants are valued for their ornamental qualities and edible fruits, which are rich in antioxidants.
