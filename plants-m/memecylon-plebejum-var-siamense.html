Memecylon plebejum var. siamense

Memecylon plebejum var. siamense, commonly known as the Siam memecylon, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with smooth or slightly hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by elliptical, woody fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the understory vegetation in its native habitat.
