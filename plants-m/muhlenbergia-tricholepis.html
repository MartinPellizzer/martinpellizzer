Muhlenbergia tricholepis

Muhlenbergia tricholepis, commonly known as the silverhair grass, is a native North American grass species characterized by its distinctive silver-gray foliage.

It is a perennial bunchgrass that typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and woodland edges. The plant features slender, wiry culms with narrow, grass-like leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle with small, spikelet-like flowers that are often tinged with purple or brown.

Muhlenbergia tricholepis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
