Microlaena tasmanica var. tasmanica

Microlaena tasmanica var. tasmanica, commonly known as Tasmanian microlaena, is a perennial grass native to Tasmania.

It typically grows in open habitats such as heathlands and grassy woodlands. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

This species is valued for its role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
