Myrcia crassinervia

Myrcia crassinervia, commonly known as the broad-veined myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pink in color. Its leaves are leathery and have a distinctive broad venation pattern, which gives it its scientific name.

Myrcia crassinervia is often found in humid, tropical environments and is valued for its ornamental qualities.
