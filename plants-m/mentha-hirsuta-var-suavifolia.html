Mentha hirsuta var. suavifolia

Mentha hirsuta var. suavifolia, commonly known as sweetleaf mint, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and a fragrant, minty aroma. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its flowering stems produce small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

This variety is valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
