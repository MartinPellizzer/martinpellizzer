Melochia venosa var. flava

Melochia venosa var. flava, commonly known as the golden melochia, is a tropical shrub native to regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that contrast with its dark green, velvety foliage. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are palmately lobed, and the plant is known for its ability to climb or trail when provided with support.
