Mahonia borealis var. perryi

Mahonia borealis var. perryi, commonly known as the Perry's Mahonia, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its spiny, compound leaves that are dark green and leathery in texture. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, which are clustered in upright panicles. These flowers are followed by blue-black berries that are edible but often considered bitter.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
