Macranthisiphon longiflorus

Macranthisiphon longiflorus, commonly known as the long-flowered macranthus, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens for its vibrant floral display.
