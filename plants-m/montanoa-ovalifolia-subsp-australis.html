Montanoa ovalifolia subsp. australis

Montanoa ovalifolia subsp.

australis, commonly known as the Mexican velvetleaf, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of Central America. It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically green with a slight pubescence.

The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive species within the Asteraceae family.
