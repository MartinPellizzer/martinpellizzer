Maxillaria chartacifolia

Maxillaria chartacifolia, commonly known as the chartaceous maxillaria, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves and produces fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are known for their intricate, three-dimensional structure.

Maxillaria chartacifolia prefers warm temperatures and high humidity, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
