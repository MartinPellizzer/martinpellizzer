Myrcia compta

Myrcia compta, commonly known as the Brazilian peppertree, is a small evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Myrcia compta is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been introduced to other regions, sometimes becoming an invasive species.

Its botanical features make it a notable plant in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
