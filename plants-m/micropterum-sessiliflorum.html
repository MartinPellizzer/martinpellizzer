Micropterum sessiliflorum

Micropterum sessiliflorum, commonly known as the common tamarisk, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the western United States.

It has small, scale-like leaves that reduce water loss, making it well adapted to drought conditions. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its root system is extensive and can tolerate saline soils, allowing it to thrive in harsh environments.

Micropterum sessiliflorum is often found along riverbanks and in desert landscapes, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
