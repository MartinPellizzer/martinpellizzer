Monteverdia communis

Monteverdia communis, commonly known as the common monteverdia, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Monteverdiaceae.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as along stream banks or in dense forests. Its leaves are broad and glossy, often with a slightly waxy texture.

Monteverdia communis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
