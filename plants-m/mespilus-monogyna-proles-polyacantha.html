Mespilus monogyna proles polyacantha

Mespilus monogyna proles polyacantha, commonly known as the common medlar, is a deciduous tree native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and alternate, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in spring, which are followed by fruiting bodies known as pomes. The fruit, which ripens in autumn, is typically a yellow or reddish-brown berry with a strong aroma and a gritty texture.

It is often used in culinary applications and has a long history in traditional medicine.
