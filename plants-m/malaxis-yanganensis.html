Malaxis yanganensis

Malaxis yanganensis, commonly known as the Yangan orchid, is a rare and endemic species native to China.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique floral structure and epiphytic growth habit. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments, showcasing its adaptation to subtropical climates. Its flowers are usually small and fragrant, with a distinctive coloration that aids in pollination by specific insect species.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species requiring conservation efforts.
