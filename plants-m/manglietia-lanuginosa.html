Manglietia lanuginosa

Manglietia lanuginosa, commonly known as the woolly manglietia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, woolly covering on its young stems and leaves, which gives it its common name. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture. It produces fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
