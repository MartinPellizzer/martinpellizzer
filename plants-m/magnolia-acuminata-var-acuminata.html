Magnolia acuminata var. acuminata

Magnolia acuminata var. acuminata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically 6 to 12 inches long. The tree produces large, showy white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

The plant is valued for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
