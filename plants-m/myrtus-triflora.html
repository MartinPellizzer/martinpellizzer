Myrtus triflora

Myrtus triflora, commonly known as the three-flowered myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. The plant produces tiny, urn-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in clusters of three, which gives it its scientific name. Myrtus triflora thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for coastal gardens.

Its compact size and fragrant foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
