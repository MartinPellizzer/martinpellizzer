Mouroucoa hirsutissima

Mouroucoa hirsutissima, commonly known as the hairy mouroucoa, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pale yellow. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of rainforests. Its leaves are broad and leathery, providing a textured appearance, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor soils.

Mouroucoa hirsutissima is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
