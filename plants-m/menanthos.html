Menanthos

Menanthos, commonly known as the pinkroot, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the genus Menanthus within the family Melanthiaceae, characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of pink or white, bell-shaped blooms in early spring. Its root system is notable for its aromatic, pinkish roots, which are used in traditional herbal medicine.

Menanthos thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
