Mentha laciniosa

Mentha laciniosa, commonly known as the woolly mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and square stems, typical of the mint family. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. Mentha laciniosa thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Its aromatic leaves are used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent.
