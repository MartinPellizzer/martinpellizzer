Moraea lineata

Moraea lineata, commonly known as the Zulu lily, is a striking ornamental plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer, producing clusters of vibrant purple or blue flowers that attract pollinators. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its striking appearance and relatively low maintenance, it is a popular choice for gardeners seeking a dramatic floral display.
