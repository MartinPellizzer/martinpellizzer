Matricaria parviflora

Matricaria parviflora, commonly known as the smallflower chamomile, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features finely divided leaves and yellow flowers with white ray florets, resembling a miniature dandelion. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Matricaria parviflora is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its calming effects.
