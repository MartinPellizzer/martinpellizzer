Melocalamus compactiflorus var. fimbriatus

Melocalamus compactiflorus var. fimbriatus, commonly known as the fimbriate bamboo, is a rare and unique variant of the compactiflorus bamboo species.

It is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit and slender, cylindrical stems that often exhibit a distinctive fluted or ribbed appearance. The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface depending on the environmental conditions. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded, forested areas.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a delicate and endangered species in its native range.
