Metastelma scoparium

Metastelma scoparium, commonly known as the rock fig, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. The plant produces small, pale green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. Its flowers are inconspicuous, and it is primarily known for its edible, fig-like fruits that are yellow when mature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
