Malus ioensis

Malus ioensis, commonly known as the Iowa serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its attractive white flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by edible berries that ripen in the summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters and has pinnately compound leaves. Its botanical features include a smooth, gray bark and a preference for well-drained soils.

It is often found in open woodlands and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
