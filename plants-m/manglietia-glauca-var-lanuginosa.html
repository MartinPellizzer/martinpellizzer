Manglietia glauca var. lanuginosa

Manglietia glauca var. lanuginosa, commonly known as the woolly manglietia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, woolly foliage and striking, fragrant white flowers. The plant belongs to the family Magnoliaceae and is known for its ornamental value due to its showy blooms. Its leaves are broad and glossy, and the tree can grow up to 20 meters in height.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.
