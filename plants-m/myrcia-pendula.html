Myrcia pendula

Myrcia pendula, commonly known as the hanging myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its drooping, fragrant leaves that emit a strong, spicy aroma. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that are often clustered together, followed by red or orange berries. Myrcia pendula thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its ornamental value and aromatic properties make it a popular plant in horticulture.
