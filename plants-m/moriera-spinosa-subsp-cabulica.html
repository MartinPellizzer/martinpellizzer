Moriera spinosa subsp. cabulica

Moriera spinosa subsp.

cabulica, commonly known as the Kabul moringa, is a shrub native to regions of Central Asia and parts of the Middle East. It is characterized by its spiny stems and pinnately compound leaves, which are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance and adaptability to poor soils.

The leaves and seeds are valued for their nutritional content and medicinal properties.
