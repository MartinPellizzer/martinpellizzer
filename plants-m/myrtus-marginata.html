Myrtus marginata

Myrtus marginata, commonly known as the narrowleaf myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, narrow leaves that are typically gray-green in color, with a fragrant, aromatic scent. The plant produces small, white, five-petaled flowers that bloom in spring, followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Myrtus marginata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in perfumery and traditional medicine.
