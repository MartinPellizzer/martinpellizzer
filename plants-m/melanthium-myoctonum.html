Melanthium myoctonum

Melanthium myoctonum, commonly known as the purple false nettle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants like buckwheat and sorrel. The plant features upright stems with alternate, ovate leaves that have a distinctive purple underside. Its flowers are small, clustered in spike-like inflorescences, and are typically greenish-white in color.

Melanthium myoctonum is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.
