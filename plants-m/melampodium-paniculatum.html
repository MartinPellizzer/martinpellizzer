Melampodium paniculatum

Melampodium paniculatum, commonly known as the golden crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as prairies and wetlands. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers with reddish bracts. Its inflorescence is a paniculate structure, giving it the common name "paniculatum.".

It is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive, long-lasting blooms.
