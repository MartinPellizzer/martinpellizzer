Magnolia × lenneana

Magnolia × lenneana, commonly known as the Saucer Magnolia, is a hybrid deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, bowl-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant typically grows to a height of 6 to 12 feet and has a rounded, compact form. Its leaves are broad, ovate, and have a matte green surface with a slightly serrated edge.

The Saucer Magnolia is prized for its ornamental value and is often planted in gardens for its early spring floral display.
