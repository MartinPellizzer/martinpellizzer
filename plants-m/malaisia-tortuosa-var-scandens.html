Malaisia tortuosa var. scandens

Malaisia tortuosa var. scandens, commonly known as the spiral fern, is a climbing fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, curling fronds that give it its common name. The plant has long, slender stems that twine around supports as it grows. Its leaves are pinnately divided, with a feathery appearance that adds to its ornamental value.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.
