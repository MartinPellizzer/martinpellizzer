Mesembryanthemum digitatum subsp. littlewoodii

Mesembryanthemum digitatum subsp.

littlewoodii, commonly known as Littlewood's ice plant, is a succulent plant native to South Africa. It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a fine, white powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the early morning and close by midday. It thrives in well-drained sandy soils and requires full sun exposure.

This species is drought-tolerant and is often used in rock gardens or as an ornamental plant.
