Miconia subandicola

Miconia subandicola, commonly known as the Andean miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that are typically followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. Miconia subandicola is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

It is also noted for its ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
