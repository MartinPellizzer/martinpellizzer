Mammillaria pleiocephala

Mammillaria pleiocephala, commonly known as the many-headed mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, globular shape and numerous small, white spines. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or orange, appearing in late spring or early summer.

This species is popular among cactus enthusiasts due to its compact size and attractive appearance.
