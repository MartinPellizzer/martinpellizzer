Momisia spinifera

Momisia spinifera, commonly known as the spiny momisia, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and often pod-like fruits. The plant is distinguished by its woody stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. It produces small, clustered white or yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
