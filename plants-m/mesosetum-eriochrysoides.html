Mesosetum eriochrysoides

Mesosetum eriochrysoides, commonly known as the silky panicgrass, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silky hairs that cover the stems and leaves, giving it a soft, feathery appearance. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist to saturated soil conditions. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical cluster of small, spikelet-like flowers.

Mesosetum eriochrysoides plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
