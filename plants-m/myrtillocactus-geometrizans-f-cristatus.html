Myrtillocactus geometrizans f. cristatus

Myrtillocactus geometrizans f. cristatus, commonly known as the cristate myrtillocactus, is a unique cactus species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive, flattened, and often branching stems that form a crown-like appearance. The plant produces small, dark purple to black fruits that contain edible seeds. Its leaves are modified into spines, providing protection from herbivores and reducing water loss.

This ornamental cactus is prized for its striking appearance and is often cultivated in desert gardens.
