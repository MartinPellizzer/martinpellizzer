Monotassa

Monotassa, commonly known as the giant water hyacinth, is a floating aquatic plant native to South America.

It has broad, dark green leaves that are often covered in a waxy layer, which helps it float on water. The plant produces striking purple-blue flowers that bloom in dense clusters, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Monotassa is known for its rapid growth rate, which can lead to overpopulation in water bodies if not controlled.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and potentially invasive in non-native environments.
