Melaleuca cajuputi subsp. cumingiana

Melaleuca cajuputi subsp.

cumingiana, commonly known as the Cuming's melaleuca, is a shrub or small tree native to northern Australia. It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and distinctive floral structures that produce abundant nectar.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, attracting a variety of pollinators. Its bark is often flaky and can be reddish or grey in color, while its wood is dense and durable.

This subspecies is adapted to coastal and sandy soils, often found in wetland or estuarine environments.
