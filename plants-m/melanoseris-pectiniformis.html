Melanoseris pectiniformis

Melanoseris pectiniformis, commonly known as the featherleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, feather-like leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces a single, tall flowering stem that bears a cluster of small, yellow flowers. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
