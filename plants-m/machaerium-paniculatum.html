Machaerium paniculatum

Machaerium paniculatum, commonly known as the panicled machaerium, is a tropical plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive panicles of small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands and along riverbanks, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.

Machaerium paniculatum is used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
