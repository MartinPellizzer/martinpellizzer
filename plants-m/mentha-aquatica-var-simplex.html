Mentha aquatica var. simplex

Mentha aquatica var. simplex, commonly known as water mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland areas of Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist soils near streams, ponds, and marshes, thriving in humid environments. The plant has square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often slightly serrated. Its fragrant, tubular flowers are blue to purple in color and bloom in late summer.

It is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.
