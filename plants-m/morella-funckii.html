Morella funckii

Morella funckii, commonly known as the Florida wax myrtle, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its narrow, waxy leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark blue berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in swamps and along waterways.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice for landscaping in suitable climates.
