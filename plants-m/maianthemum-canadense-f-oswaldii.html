Maianthemum canadense f. oswaldii

Maianthemum canadense f. oswaldii, commonly known as the Oswalt's false lily of the valley, is a botanical variant of the Canadian race of the false lily of the valley.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its dense, low-growing clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that form a rosette at the base. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Botanically, it is distinguished by its unique floral structure and genetic variation from the typical form.
