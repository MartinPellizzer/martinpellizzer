Miconia sieberi

Miconia sieberi, commonly known as the velvetleaf miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of velvety hair, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers, which are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Miconia sieberi is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, making it an invasive species in some regions.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and a challenge for land management.
