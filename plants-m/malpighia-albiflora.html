Malpighia albiflora

Malpighia albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered mallow, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Malpighiaceae family and is characterized by its showy, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub with simple, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its fruits are small, fleshy berries that contain several seeds, and the plant is adapted to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.

Malpighia albiflora is also noted for its potential medicinal uses in traditional herbal medicine.
