Myrcia saxicola var. grandifolia

Myrcia saxicola var. grandifolia, commonly known as the large-flowered myrcia, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in humid, shaded environments within the understory of tropical forests. The plant is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruits are woody capsules that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
