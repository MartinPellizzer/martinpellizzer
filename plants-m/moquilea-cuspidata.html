Moquilea cuspidata

Moquilea cuspidata, commonly known as the spiny moquilea, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant is known for its robust, thorny stems and narrow, grass-like leaves adapted to survive in dry conditions. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, appearing in clusters and attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Moquilea cuspidata is often used in traditional medicine and landscaping due to its hardy nature and ornamental value.
