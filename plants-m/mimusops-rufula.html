Mimusops rufula

Mimusops rufula, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Sapotaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, followed by spherical fruits containing a single seed.

It is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of tannins.
