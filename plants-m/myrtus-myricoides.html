Myrtus myricoides

Myrtus myricoides, commonly known as the common myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant features small, dark green leaves that are often aromatic when crushed. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink and are clustered in dense inflorescences.

The fruit is a small, fleshy berry that ripens to a dark purple color, containing several seeds.
