Monstera pertusa var. laniata

Monstera pertusa var. laniata, commonly known as the Variegated Monstera, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically split into lobes, with a distinctive variegated pattern of light and dark green. The plant thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light. Its rapid growth rate makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

The variegation in its leaves is due to a genetic mutation that affects chlorophyll production, resulting in unique leaf patterns.
