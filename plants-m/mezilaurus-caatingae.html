Mezilaurus caatingae

Mezilaurus caatingae, commonly known as the caatinga acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the caatinga biome of northeastern Brazil.

It is characterized by its spiny, branched stems and pinnate leaves composed of small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid conditions, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.

Mezilaurus caatingae plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.
