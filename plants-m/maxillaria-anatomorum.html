Maxillaria anatomorum

Maxillaria anatomorum, commonly known as the anatomous maxillaria, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and intricate floral structure. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers with a distinctive coloration pattern, typically featuring a mix of white, pink, and purple hues. Its pseudobulbs are cylindrical and covered in a dense layer of brownish bark, which helps in water retention.

The flowers are borne on terminal racemes and are known for their complex morphology, making it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
