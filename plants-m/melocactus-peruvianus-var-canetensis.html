Melocactus peruvianus var. canetensis

Melocactus peruvianus var. canetensis, commonly known as the Canet's melon cactus, is a rare and compact cactus species native to the arid regions of Peru.

It features a spherical or slightly elongated stem with a thick, ribbed structure that helps it store water. The plant produces vibrant orange to red flowers during the warmer months, which are typically short-lived but striking. Its spines are relatively short and often covered in a fine, white bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This variety is well adapted to harsh desert conditions, making it a hardy choice for collectors and desert gardeners.
