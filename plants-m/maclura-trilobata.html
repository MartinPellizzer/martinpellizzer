Maclura trilobata

Maclura trilobata, commonly known as the honeylocust, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves that are divided into three lobes, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, spiny pods. Its bark is thick and fissured, often appearing rough and scaly.

The honeylocust is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
