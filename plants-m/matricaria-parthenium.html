Matricaria parthenium

Matricaria parthenium, commonly known as the common chamomile, is an annual herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers with a white central disk. The plant is known for its aromatic properties, with the flowers containing essential oils that contribute to its distinctive scent. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in meadows and disturbed areas.

Matricaria parthenium is widely cultivated for its medicinal and aromatic uses.
