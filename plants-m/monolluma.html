Monolluma

Monolluma, commonly known as the sacred lotus of India, is a flowering plant belonging to the Nymphaeaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom on long, floating stems. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats, such as ponds and lakes. Its leaves are broad and rounded, often floating on the water's surface.

Monolluma is also valued for its symbolic and cultural significance in various traditions.
