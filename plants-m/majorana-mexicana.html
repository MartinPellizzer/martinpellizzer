Majorana mexicana

Majorana mexicana, commonly known as Mexican oregano, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant features square, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are often gray-green in color. Its small, clustered flowers are typically pink or lavender, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

It is valued for its strong aromatic leaves, which are used in cooking and traditional medicine.
