Miconia curvipetiolata

Miconia curvipetiolata, commonly known as the velvetleaf miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of soft, velvety hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that are typically followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. Its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed habitats make it an invasive species in several regions.

The botanical features of Miconia curvipetiolata make it both ecologically significant and a challenge for land managers.
