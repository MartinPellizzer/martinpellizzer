Micromeria thymoides var. citriodora

Micromeria thymoides var. citriodora, commonly known as lemon thyme, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves that emit a strong lemon scent when crushed. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its essential oils, rich in citral, are widely used in aromatherapy and as a natural insect repellent.
