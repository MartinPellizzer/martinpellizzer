Mangifera caesia var. kemanga

Mangifera caesia var. kemanga, commonly known as the Kemanga mango, is a tropical fruit tree native to Southeast Asia.

It produces large, elongated fruits with a thick, fibrous pulp and a sweet, aromatic flavor. The tree thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its flowers are small and fragrant, typically appearing in clusters, and it is primarily cultivated for its edible fruit.

The variety is valued for its unique taste and is often used in both fresh consumption and culinary applications.
