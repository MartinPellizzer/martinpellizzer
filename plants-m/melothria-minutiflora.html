Melothria minutiflora

Melothria minutiflora, commonly known as the dwarf passionfruit, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It produces small, yellow-orange fruits that are covered in a rough, hairy exterior. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a waxy surface and emits a strong, aromatic scent. Its flowers are typically white or pale green, with five petals and a distinctive crown of filaments.

The fruit is edible, though its flavor is often described as tart and acidic.
