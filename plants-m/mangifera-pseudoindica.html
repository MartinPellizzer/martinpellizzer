Mangifera pseudoindica

Mangifera pseudoindica, commonly known as the Indian mango or wild mango, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, with compound leaves and fragrant yellow flowers. The fruit is typically small, round, and has a thick, fibrous peel. It is botanically related to the common mango (Mangifera indica) but differs in size and fruit characteristics.

This species is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid climates with well-drained soil.
