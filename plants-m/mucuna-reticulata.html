Mucuna reticulata

Mucuna reticulata, commonly known as the netleaf morning glory, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive net-veined leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces elongated, spiral-shaped pods containing kidney-shaped seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.

Mucuna reticulata is also notable for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
