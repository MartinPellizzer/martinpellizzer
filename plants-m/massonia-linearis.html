Massonia linearis

Massonia linearis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved massonia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its linear, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered white or pink flowers. The plant is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is a member of the Aizoaceae family, also known as the ice plant family.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is popular in xeriscaping and rock gardens.
