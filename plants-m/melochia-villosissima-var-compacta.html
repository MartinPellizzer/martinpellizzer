Melochia villosissima var. compacta

Melochia villosissima var. compacta, commonly known as the velvetleaf or hairy melochia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine, white hairs, giving the plant its "velvet" appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

This plant is also known for its ability to spread rapidly, making it both a useful ornamental and a potential weed in certain environments.
