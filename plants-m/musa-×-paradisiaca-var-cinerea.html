Musa × paradisiaca var. cinerea

Musa × paradisiaca var. cinerea, commonly known as the gray banana, is a variant of the common banana plant characterized by its distinctive grayish-brown coloration on the outer layer of the pseudostem.

This variety is a hybrid species within the Musa genus, typically cultivated for its edible fruit, which is similar to the Cavendish banana but with a slightly different flavor profile. The leaves are large, broad, and glossy, contributing to the plant's ornamental appeal. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil, requiring consistent moisture for optimal growth.

Despite its ornamental value, it is also grown for its fruit, which is generally smaller and less sweet compared to other banana varieties.
