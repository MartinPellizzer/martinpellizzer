Mikania thyrsoidea

Mikania thyrsoidea, commonly known as the silky leaf vine, is a climbing herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by fluffy, white to pale green seed heads that aid in wind dispersal. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests or along stream banks.

Its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets make it both ecologically significant and potentially invasive in certain areas.
