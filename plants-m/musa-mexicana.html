Musa mexicana

Musa mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican banana, is a herbaceous plant native to Central America and Mexico.

It belongs to the Musaceae family and is characterized by its large, broad leaves and clustered inflorescences. The plant produces edible berries that are typically yellow or red when ripe. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
