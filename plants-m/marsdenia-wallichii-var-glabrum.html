Marsdenia wallichii var. glabrum

Marsdenia wallichii var. glabrum, commonly known as the smooth Marsdenia, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It is a member of the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are typically glabrous, giving it its varietal name, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some regions.
