Medicago asiatica subsp. turkestanica

Medicago asiatica subsp.

turkestanica, commonly known as the Turkestan clover, is a perennial legume native to Central Asia. It features trifoliate leaves with a hairy texture and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant is characterized by its erect growth habit and is often found in dry, rocky or sandy soils. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

This subspecies is valued for its forage quality and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
