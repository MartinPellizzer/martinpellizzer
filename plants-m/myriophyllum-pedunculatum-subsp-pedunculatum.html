Myriophyllum pedunculatum subsp. pedunculatum

Myriophyllum pedunculatum subsp.

pedunculatum, commonly known as watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America. It has slender, branching stems that are often green or reddish in color, and it produces small, white flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant is characterized by its opposite, narrow leaves and its ability to form dense underwater stands. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for fish and invertebrates.

However, it can also become invasive in some regions if not managed properly.
