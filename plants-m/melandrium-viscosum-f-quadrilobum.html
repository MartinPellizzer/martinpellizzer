Melandrium viscosum f. quadrilobum

Melandrium viscosum f. quadrilobum, commonly known as the sticky catchfly, is a variant of the common catchfly characterized by its distinctive quadrilobed leaves.

This plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, where it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. Its leaves are covered in fine, sticky hairs, which help to deter herbivores and retain moisture. The flowers are typically pink or purple, with five petals and numerous stamens, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It is an annual plant that produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.
