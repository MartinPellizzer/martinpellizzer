Medicago lappacea var. microdon

Medicago lappacea var. microdon, commonly known as the dwarf goat's rue, is a low-growing perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, trifoliate leaves and produces yellow flowers in early summer. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in rocky or disturbed soils. It has been used traditionally for its medicinal properties, particularly for treating ailments related to the urinary tract.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a forage plant and its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
