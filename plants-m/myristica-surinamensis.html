Myristica surinamensis

Myristica surinamensis, commonly known as the Surinam myrtle, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces bright red berries that contain a single seed, which is encased in a hard, woody shell. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
