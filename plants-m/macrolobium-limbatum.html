Macrolobium limbatum

Macrolobium limbatum, commonly known as the broad-leafed macrolobium, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Its root system has been studied for its potential nitrogen-fixing capabilities, which can benefit soil fertility.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
