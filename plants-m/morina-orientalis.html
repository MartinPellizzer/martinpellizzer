Morina orientalis

Morina orientalis, commonly known as the Chinese moringa or oriental moringa, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit, with woody stems and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruits are capsule-like, containing numerous small, flat seeds.

Morina orientalis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
