Medicago daghestanica var. coerulescens

Medicago daghestanica var. coerulescens, commonly known as the blue medic, is a perennial legume native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and nitrogen-fixing ability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and agricultural settings.
