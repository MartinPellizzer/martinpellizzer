Miconia smaragdina

Miconia smaragdina, commonly known as the emerald miconia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green color, often with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Miconia smaragdina is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

However, it is also considered an invasive species in some regions due to its aggressive spread and impact on native vegetation.
