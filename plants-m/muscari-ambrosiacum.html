Muscari ambrosiacum

Muscari ambrosiacum, commonly known as the Persian grape hyacinth, is a bulbous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It produces dense clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue and purple, resembling small grapes. The plant typically blooms in early spring and is well-suited for rock gardens or woodland settings. Its bulbs are perennial, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full to partial sun.

Muscari ambrosiacum is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in perfumery.
