Melaleuca huttensis

Melaleuca huttensis, commonly known as the Hutt's melaleuca, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and tea tree. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered white or pale pink flowers that appear in spring. It is typically found in coastal and lowland areas, often in sandy or gravelly soils.

Melaleuca huttensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and visual appeal.
