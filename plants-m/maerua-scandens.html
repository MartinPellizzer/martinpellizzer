Maerua scandens

Maerua scandens, commonly known as the African glorybower, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often climbing stems and its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savannas and rocky areas.

Maerua scandens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
