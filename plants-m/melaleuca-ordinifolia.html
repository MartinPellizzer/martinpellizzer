Melaleuca ordinifolia

Melaleuca ordinifolia, commonly known as the common tea tree, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia and is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and showy, pinkish-white flower clusters. The plant produces small, woody fruits called capsules, which contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal regions and heathlands.

Melaleuca ordinifolia is also valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional Australian remedies.
