Melica scabrosa var. limprichtii

Melica scabrosa var. limprichtii, commonly known as the limpricht's wood grass, is a perennial grass species native to certain regions of North America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a rough texture and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowers are usually greenish or pale blue, and it blooms during the summer months.

This variety is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in woodland or wetland environments.
