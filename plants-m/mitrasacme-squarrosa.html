Mitrasacme squarrosa

Mitrasacme squarrosa, commonly known as the woolly mitrasacme, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are small and clustered in terminal umbels, often with a pale yellow or greenish hue.

Mitrasacme squarrosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
