Malva biflora

Malva biflora, commonly known as the two-flowered mallows, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.

Malva biflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various purposes.
