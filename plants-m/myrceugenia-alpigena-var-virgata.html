Myrceugenia alpigena var. virgata

Myrceugenia alpigena var. virgata, commonly known as the alpine myrtle, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes species like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. It thrives in cool, humid climates and is often found in high-altitude environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species in the study of alpine flora and ecological adaptation.
