Malva peduncularis

Malva peduncularis, commonly known as the woolly mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, lobed leaves and showy pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous small, dry seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Malva peduncularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
