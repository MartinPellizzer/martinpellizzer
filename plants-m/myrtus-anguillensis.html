Myrtus anguillensis

Myrtus anguillensis, commonly known as the coastal myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its narrow, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, leathery berries that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and rocky habitats.

Myrtus anguillensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
