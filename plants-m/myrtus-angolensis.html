Myrtus angolensis

Myrtus angolensis, commonly known as African myrtle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by dark blue berries. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, with a strong, aromatic scent.

Myrtus angolensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional medicinal uses in some African cultures.
