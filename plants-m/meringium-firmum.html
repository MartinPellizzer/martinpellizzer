Meringium firmum

Meringium firmum, commonly known as the firm meringia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It features upright, slender stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, covered with fine, white hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale yellow to deep orange, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped, alternate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Meringium firmum is known for its adaptability to various soil types but thrives best in well-drained, loamy soils.
