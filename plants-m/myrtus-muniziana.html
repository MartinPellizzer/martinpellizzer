Myrtus muniziana

Myrtus muniziana, commonly known as the Canary Islands myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant features narrow, dark green leaves and produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. It is characterized by its aromatic foliage and is often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Myrtus muniziana thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to the unique climate of the Canary Islands.
