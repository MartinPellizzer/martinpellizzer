Mentha longifolia var. stenanthelmia

Mentha longifolia var. stenanthelmia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mint, is a variant of the hedge mint species.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and a strong, aromatic scent. This plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soil. It produces small, pale purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The leaves are often used in herbal teas and traditional medicine for their cooling properties.
