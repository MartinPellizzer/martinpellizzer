Messerschmidia gnaphalodes

Messerschmidia gnaphalodes, commonly known as the woolly sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that helps reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It typically grows on rocky soils or in sandy areas, demonstrating a high degree of drought tolerance.

Its structure and appearance make it well adapted to survive in nutrient-poor and water-scarce conditions.
