Myrobalanus brasiliensis

Myrobalanus brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and dense, elliptical leaves. The tree is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its unique fruit.
