Mespilus germanica var. diffusa

Mespilus germanica var. diffusa, commonly known as the Persian white mulberry, is a variant of the common mulberry tree native to parts of Asia and the Middle East.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, featuring a broad, rounded crown and smooth, gray bark. The leaves are ovate to elliptical, with a serrated margin and a distinctive white or pale green underside. It produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically white or pale yellow, ripening in late summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine and silk production.
