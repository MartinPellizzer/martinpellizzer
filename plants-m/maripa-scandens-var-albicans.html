Maripa scandens var. albicans

Maripa scandens var. albicans, commonly known as the white-flowered maripa, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its pale yellow to white flowers, which contrast with its green, often hairy leaves. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
