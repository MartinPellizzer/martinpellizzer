Magnolia cubensis

Magnolia cubensis, commonly known as the Cuban magnolia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly Cuba.

It is characterized by its large, showy white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces woody, fragrant flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its bark is smooth and greyish, and it typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in tropical and subtropical regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
