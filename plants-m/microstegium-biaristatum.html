Microstegium biaristatum

Microstegium biaristatum, commonly known as the two-stemmed wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial with a low-growing habit, featuring opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters during the spring and early summer. Its stems are typically slender and branched, with a distinctive two-stemmed structure that gives it its scientific name.

Microstegium biaristatum is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.
