Manihot auriculata

Manihot auriculata, commonly known as the ear-shaped cassava, is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces edible tuberous roots that are used in various culinary applications. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both agricultural and ornamental purposes.
