Mitella trifida var. violacea

Mitella trifida var. violacea, commonly known as violetleaf mitella, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting native wildlife.
