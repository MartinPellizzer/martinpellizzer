Manilkara longifolia

Manilkara longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaf manilkara, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its elongated, leathery leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by edible, sweet fruits that are often consumed by both humans and animals. Its bark contains latex, which has been used traditionally for various purposes, including as a natural adhesive.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and parks.
