Monoon sympetalum

Monoon sympetalum, commonly known as the white mangosteen, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces edible, fragrant fruits that are encased in a thick, purple rind. Its botanical features include opposite leaves and a woody structure typical of the Monoon genus.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.
