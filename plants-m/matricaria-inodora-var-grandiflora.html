Matricaria inodora var. grandiflora

Matricaria inodora var. grandiflora, commonly known as the greater chamomile, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers with white centers, which bloom in late summer. The plant has finely divided, aromatic leaves that emit a mild chamomile scent when crushed. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in dry, open habitats.

This variety is cultivated for its medicinal properties and use in herbal teas.
