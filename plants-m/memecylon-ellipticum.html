Memecylon ellipticum

Memecylon ellipticum, commonly known as the oval memecylon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Melastomataceae and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves that are often arranged in whorls. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pink, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
