Melicope crassifolia

Melicope crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved melicope, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by spherical, woody fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its aromatic wood and is also used in traditional medicine.
