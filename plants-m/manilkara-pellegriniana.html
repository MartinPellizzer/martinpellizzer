Manilkara pellegriniana

Manilkara pellegriniana, commonly known as the Pellegriniana chicle tree, is a tropical evergreen species native to Central America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and broad, elliptical leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by fleshy, orange-colored fruits that contain a sticky, latex-rich pulp. Its latex has historically been used to make chewing gum, earning it the common name "chicle.".

The plant is valued for both its ecological role and economic potential in traditional industries.
