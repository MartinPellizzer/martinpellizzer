Mentha serotina

Mentha serotina, commonly known as late mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils that give it a strong minty scent.

Mentha serotina is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks.
