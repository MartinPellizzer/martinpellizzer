Mentha hortensis var. citrata

Mentha hortensis var. citrata, commonly known as citron mint, is a variant of the peppermint plant characterized by its distinct lemony aroma.

This herb is native to Europe and is known for its bright green, serrated leaves and small, pale purple flowers. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil and partial shade. The plant is often used in culinary applications and herbal remedies due to its refreshing scent and medicinal properties.

Its essential oils contain high levels of limonene, contributing to its citrus fragrance.
