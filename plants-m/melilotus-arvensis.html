Melilotus arvensis

Melilotus arvensis, commonly known as field melilot, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender stems and trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its root system is fibrous, and it is often found in disturbed soils such as roadsides and fields.

Field melilot is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop.
