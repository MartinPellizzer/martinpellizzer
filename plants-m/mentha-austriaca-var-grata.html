Mentha austriaca var. grata

Mentha austriaca var. grata, commonly known as Austrian mint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features dark green, ovate leaves with a slightly serrated margin, and produces small, pale purple flowers in whorls along the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its strong mint aroma and is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
