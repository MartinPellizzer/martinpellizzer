Microspermum gracillimum

Microspermum gracillimum, commonly known as the slender seed orchid, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, grass-like foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers that typically bloom in late spring or early summer. The plant produces minute seeds enclosed in a fleshy, reddish capsule, which is a unique feature of its reproductive system. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate forests or along stream banks.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
