Macrozamia tenuifolia

Macrozamia tenuifolia, commonly known as the slender-leaf cycad, is a slow-growing, evergreen plant native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and a crown of feathery, pinnate leaves that are typically slender and elongated. The plant produces large, cone-like structures, with male and female cones occurring on separate plants. It is adapted to well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
