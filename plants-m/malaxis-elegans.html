Malaxis elegans

Malaxis elegans, commonly known as the elegant malaxis, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its leaves are elongated and grass-like, and it produces aerial roots that help it attach to trees or other substrates.

This orchid is valued for its delicate appearance and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
