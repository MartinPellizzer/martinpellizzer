Mucuna capitata

Mucuna capitata, commonly known as the cow pea or velvet bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive, dense, velvety pods that contain edible seeds. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for agricultural purposes. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet having a hairy surface.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for various health benefits.
