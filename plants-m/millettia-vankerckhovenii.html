Millettia vankerckhovenii

Millettia vankerckhovenii, commonly known as the African sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The tree produces long, flat pods that contain seeds dispersed by wind. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils and can grow up to 20 meters in height.

Due to its ornamental value and ability to fix nitrogen, it is sometimes cultivated in horticulture and reforestation projects.
