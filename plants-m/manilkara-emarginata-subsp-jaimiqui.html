Manilkara emarginata subsp. jaimiqui

Manilkara emarginata subsp.

jaimiqui, commonly known as the Jaimiqui sapodilla, is a species native to the Caribbean, particularly found in Cuba and the Isle of Youth. It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in moist, shaded environments within tropical forests.

The plant produces smooth, greyish bark and has large, palmately lobed leaves that are often used for shade. Its fruit is a yellowish, spiny berry that contains a sweet, starchy pulp surrounded by a fibrous layer.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local communities.
