Marlea tomentosa

Marlea tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly marula, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, grey-green foliage and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that are covered in a sticky, resinous substance. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along riverbanks.

The woolly marula is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local communities.
