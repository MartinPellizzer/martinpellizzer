Mammillaria dumetorum

Mammillaria dumetorum, commonly known as the "Cushion cactus," is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and small, spiny stems that form a compact cushion-like structure. The plant typically produces white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cultivation.
