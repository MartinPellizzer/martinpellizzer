Matucana weberbaueri var. flammea

Matucana weberbaueri var. flammea, commonly known as the Flame Ctenanthe, is a striking tropical plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage with deep green leaves featuring red and orange veins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers surrounded by a spathe, typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant in warm, humid environments.
