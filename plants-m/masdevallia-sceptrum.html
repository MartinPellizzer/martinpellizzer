Masdevallia sceptrum

Masdevallia sceptrum, commonly known as the royal masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated flower spikes that bear showy, often colorful blossoms. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually clustered at the base and are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white pubescence.

Masdevallia sceptrum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique and elegant floral display.
