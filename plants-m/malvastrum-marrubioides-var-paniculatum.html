Malvastrum marrubioides var. paniculatum

Malvastrum marrubioides var. paniculatum, commonly known as panicled mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It features upright stems that are hairy and often branched, with broad, heart-shaped leaves that have a rough texture. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers in paniculate inflorescences, which bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Malvaceae family.
