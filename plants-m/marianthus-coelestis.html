Marianthus coelestis

Marianthus coelestis, commonly known as the celestial marianthus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, sky-blue petals that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. Its flowers emit a subtle fragrance, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
