Mariscus asper

Mariscus asper, commonly known as the rough thornapple, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its spiny stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. Mariscus asper is often found in rocky or dry habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils.

Its toxic properties make it a plant to be handled with care.
