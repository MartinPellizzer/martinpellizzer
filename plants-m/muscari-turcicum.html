Muscari turcicum

Muscari turcicum, commonly known as the Turk's cap squill, is a bulbous plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in grassy areas and woodland edges. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, emerging from a basal rosette of bulbs.

Muscari turcicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
