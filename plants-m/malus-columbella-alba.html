Malus columbella alba

Malus columbella alba, commonly known as the white-flowered service tree, is a small deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental white flowers that bloom in early spring. The tree produces small, edible apples that are typically not cultivated for food but are valued for their ornamental appeal. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and turn yellow in the autumn, adding to its seasonal beauty.

The plant is often used in landscaping for its compact size and attractive spring blossoms.
