Mycaranthes merguensis

Mycaranthes merguensis, commonly known as the Merguensian ghost flower, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the cloud forests of Central America.

It is characterized by its unique, translucent, and often bioluminescent petals, which emit a faint glow under moonlight. The plant has a short blooming period, typically during the rainy season, and its flowers are pollinated by specific species of nocturnal moths. Its roots are known to form symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi, enhancing nutrient absorption in nutrient-poor soils.

Due to its elusive nature and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation priority.
