Melica ceretana

Melica ceretana, commonly known as the Italian meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist meadows, riverbanks, and other damp habitats. The plant has slender, erect stems that can reach up to 1 meter in height, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like. Its inflorescence consists of a spikelet-bearing panicle, which produces small, greenish flowers.

Melica ceretana is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
