Mosiera bullata subsp. bullata

Mosiera bullata subsp.

bullata, commonly known as the bullate mallow, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture.

The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is often cultivated in gardens and naturalized areas.
