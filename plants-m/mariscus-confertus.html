Mariscus confertus

Mariscus confertus, commonly known as the dense mariscus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Apiaceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in dry, rocky soils. The plant has small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, and its leaves are pinnately divided with a fern-like appearance. It is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities.

Mariscus confertus is also known for its aromatic foliage, which can be used in traditional herbal remedies.
