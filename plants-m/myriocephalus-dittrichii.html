Myriocephalus dittrichii

Myriocephalus dittrichii, commonly known as the Cape chestnut, is a member of the Proteaceae family and is native to South Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal fynbos vegetation. The plant is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and produces small, yellowish flowers clustered in spherical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to mild, temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
