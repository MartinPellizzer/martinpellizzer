Mammillaria albata

Mammillaria albata, commonly known as the white mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and dense covering of white, woolly spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for succulent collections.
