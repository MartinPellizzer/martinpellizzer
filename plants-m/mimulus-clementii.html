Mimulus clementii

Mimulus clementii, commonly known as the yellow sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs.

Mimulus clementii is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is known for its ecological importance to pollinators.
