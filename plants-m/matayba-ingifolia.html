Matayba ingifolia

Matayba ingifolia, commonly known as the yellow myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its bark and leaves contain essential oils, which have been used in traditional medicine for their antimicrobial properties.

Matayba ingifolia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in humid, tropical forests.
