Mutisia subulata var. cavanillesii

Mutisia subulata var. cavanillesii, commonly known as the Cavanilles' mutisia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in regions of Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, featuring narrow, needle-like leaves and showy yellow to orange flower heads. It thrives in dry, temperate climates and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is more commonly seen in its natural habitat.
