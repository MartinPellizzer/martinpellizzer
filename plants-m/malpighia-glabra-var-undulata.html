Malpighia glabra var. undulata

Malpighia glabra var. undulata, commonly known as the sour sop or custard apple, is a tropical shrub native to the Americas.

It produces large, showy flowers that are typically pink or white, followed by fruit that has a rough, spiny exterior. The fruit is characterized by its undulating, warty surface, which gives the variety its name. The leaves are ovate, glossy, and have a slightly serrated margin.

This plant is valued for its edible fruit, which has a creamy, tropical flavor and is used in both culinary and medicinal applications.
