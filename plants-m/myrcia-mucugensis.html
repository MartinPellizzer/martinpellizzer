Myrcia mucugensis

Myrcia mucugensis, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Amazon rainforest and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and emit a strong, aromatic scent when crushed.

Myrcia mucugensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
