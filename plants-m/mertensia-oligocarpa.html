Mertensia oligocarpa

Mertensia oligocarpa, commonly known as the blue cohosh, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has opposite, heart-shaped leaves. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release its seeds, and it is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.

Mertensia oligocarpa is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
