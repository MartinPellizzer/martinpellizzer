Montjolya integrifolia

Montjolya integrifolia, commonly known as the smoothleaf montjolya, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are typically smooth-edged. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Montjolya integrifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a ground cover.
