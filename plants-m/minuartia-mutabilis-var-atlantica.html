Minuartia mutabilis var. atlantica

Minuartia mutabilis var. atlantica, commonly known as the Atlantic rockcress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the coastal regions of the Atlantic coast.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh maritime climates with high salt exposure and strong winds. The plant features small, clustered leaves and produces delicate, white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many flowering plants with characteristic cruciform flowers.

This variety is notable for its ability to tolerate extreme environmental conditions, making it a resilient and hardy species.
