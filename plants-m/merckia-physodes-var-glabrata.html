Merckia physodes var. glabrata

Merckia physodes var. glabrata, commonly known as the smooth merckia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllis family (Amaryllidaceae) and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of flowers on short pedicels. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries due to its elegant appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.

The varietal form 'glabrata' is distinguished by its smooth, hairless leaves compared to the typical form.
