Machaonia peruviana

Machaonia peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet, often with a distinctive purple or brown throat. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, such as cloud forests and rocky slopes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in a fine, fuzzy layer, which helps to retain moisture.

Machaonia peruviana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
