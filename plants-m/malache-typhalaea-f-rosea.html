Malache typhalaea f. rosea

Malache typhalaea f. rosea, commonly known as the rose-flowered malache, is a rare and striking variant of the Malache typhalaea species.

It is characterized by its vibrant pink to rose-colored flowers, which contrast beautifully with its deep green foliage. The plant is a member of the Araceae family, known for its unique inflorescence structure called a spadix, enclosed by a modified leaf called a spathe. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistent moisture and well-draining soil.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in specialized botanical gardens and greenhouses.
