Medicago × hemicycla var. maxima

Medicago × hemicycla var. maxima, commonly known as the Greater Persian clover, is a perennial legume native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit, trifoliate leaves, and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. It has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in poor soil conditions and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.

Additionally, it is a valuable forage plant for livestock due to its high nutritional content.
