Maytenus hybanthifolia

Maytenus hybanthifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to South America.

It features opposite, simple, ovate to elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green on top with a lighter underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences, followed by small, red, fleshy berries. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in a variety of habitats including coastal areas and disturbed soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
