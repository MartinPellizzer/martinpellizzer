Medicago falcata var. viscosa

Medicago falcata var. viscosa, commonly known as sticky medick, is a perennial legume native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces elliptical, hairy pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along fences.

Its sticky seeds and hairy stems contribute to its ability to persist in various environments.
