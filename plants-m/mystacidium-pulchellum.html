Mystacidium pulchellum

Mystacidium pulchellum, commonly known as the beautiful mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees, primarily in the southern United States.

It possesses small, scale-like leaves and produces white, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various birds. The plant relies on its host for water and some nutrients, while it provides shelter and food for wildlife. Its life cycle involves both aerial and seed dispersal, with birds playing a crucial role in spreading its seeds.

Mystacidium pulchellum is adapted to thrive in shaded, humid environments, often found in forest canopies.
