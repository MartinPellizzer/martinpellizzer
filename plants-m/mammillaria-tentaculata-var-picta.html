Mammillaria tentaculata var. picta

Mammillaria tentaculata var. picta, commonly known as the Painted Mammillaria, is a compact, spineless cactus native to Mexico.

It features a globular to cylindrical stem with a distinctive pale green to bluish-gray coloration. The plant is characterized by its unique, irregularly shaped areoles that bear white to pale yellow flowers. Its foliage is adorned with small, white, tuberculate structures that give it a textured appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.
