Myrica pilulifera var. puberula

Myrica pilulifera var. puberula, commonly known as the smooth wax myrtle, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, wax-coated leaves that are typically smooth and glossy. The plant produces small, spherical berries that are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of aromatic oils. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and horticultural applications.
