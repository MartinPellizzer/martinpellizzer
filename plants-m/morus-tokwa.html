Morus tokwa

Morus tokwa, commonly known as the Japanese mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fuzzy flowers. The tree produces edible fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source for silkworms. Its bark is smooth and grayish, with a distinct, aromatic scent.

Morus tokwa typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates.
