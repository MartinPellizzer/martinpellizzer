Monarda punctata var. arkansana

Monarda punctata var. arkansana, commonly known as the Arkansas bee balm, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its showy, tubular red and pink flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 3 feet, featuring narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy addition to native gardens.

Its botanical features make it an important species for supporting local pollinator populations and ecological biodiversity.
