Muraltia saxicola

Muraltia saxicola, commonly known as the rock muraltia, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or black when mature. It is well adapted to dry environments, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in poor soil conditions.

Muraltia saxicola is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
