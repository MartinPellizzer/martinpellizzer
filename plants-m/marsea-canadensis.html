Marsea canadensis

Marsea canadensis, commonly known as the Canadian marsh grass, is a perennial herb native to wetland areas of North America.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets. Its fibrous roots help stabilize soil and prevent erosion in wetland ecosystems.

Marsea canadensis plays an important role in supporting local biodiversity by providing habitat and food for various insects and wildlife.
