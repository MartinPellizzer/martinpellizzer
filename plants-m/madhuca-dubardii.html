Madhuca dubardii

Madhuca dubardii, commonly known as the Indian shea tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and large, glossy leaves. The tree produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds surrounded by a fatty, edible kernel.

The plant is valued for its oil, which is used in traditional medicine and cosmetics.
