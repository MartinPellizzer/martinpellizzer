Megaclinium triste

Megaclinium triste, commonly known as the weeping gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a compact growth habit with slender, branching stems covered in fine, hairy foliage. Its botanical name reflects its melancholic appearance, with "triste" meaning sad in Latin.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
