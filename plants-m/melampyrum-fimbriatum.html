Melampyrum fimbriatum

Melampyrum fimbriatum, commonly known as the fringed cow-wheat, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its distinctive pod-like fruits and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers are usually reddish or purplish, with a spurred structure at the base of the petals.

Melampyrum fimbriatum is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
