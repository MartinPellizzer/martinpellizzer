Myrcia madida

Myrcia madida, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces bright red berries that are an important food source for various bird species. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

Myrcia madida is also valued for its aromatic leaves, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.
