Malpighia fuertesii subsp. minor

Malpighia fuertesii subsp.

minor, commonly known as the Mexican beautyberry, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America. It belongs to the Malpighiaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant red berries that persist through the winter.

The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking fruit and ecological benefits.
