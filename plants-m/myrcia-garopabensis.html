Myrcia garopabensis

Myrcia garopabensis, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a species native to Brazil, particularly found in the Atlantic Forest region.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows up to 6 meters in height. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a waxy texture. Its flowers are small, clustered, and usually appear in shades of white or pink, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for some animals.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has also been used in traditional medicine.
