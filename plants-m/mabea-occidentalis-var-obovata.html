Mabea occidentalis var. obovata

Mabea occidentalis var. obovata, commonly known as the broad-ovate mabea, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are typically hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

The botanical features of this variety make it well adapted to drought conditions and suitable for xeriscaping.
