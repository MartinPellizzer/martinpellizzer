Mischogyne michelioides

Mischogyne michelioides, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a large aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter, providing a unique habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant has a thick, spongy stem that supports its massive leaves and roots, which help stabilize it in water. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, with a distinctive scent that attracts pollinators like beetles.

Mischogyne michelioides plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by offering shelter and food for aquatic life.
