Miconia glaucescens

Miconia glaucescens, commonly known as the blue-green miconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that can grow up to 10 meters tall. The plant is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves that are often heart-shaped. Its flowers are small and white, clustered in inflorescences, and it produces berries that are yellow when mature.

Miconia glaucescens is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
