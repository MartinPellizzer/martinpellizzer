Matthiola fragrans subsp. daghestanica

Matthiola fragrans subsp.

daghestanica, commonly known as the Daghestan stock, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with showy blooms.

The plant produces clusters of fragrant, four-petaled flowers that are typically pink or white in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
