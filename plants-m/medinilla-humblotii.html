Medinilla humblotii

Medinilla humblotii, commonly known as the velvet tangerine.

This evergreen shrub is native to the Philippines and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, orange-red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Medinilla humblotii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and floral display.
