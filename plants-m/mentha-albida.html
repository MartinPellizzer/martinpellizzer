Mentha albida

Mentha albida, commonly known as white mint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves that are typically white or pale green in color. The plant produces small, pale blue or white flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Mentha albida is often used in herbal remedies and as a culinary herb due to its refreshing scent and mild flavor.
