Marsdenia arabica

Marsdenia arabica, commonly known as the Arabian milk vine, is a flowering plant native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a member of the Asclepiadaceae family, characterized by its milky sap and distinctive floral structures. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine with opposite, elliptic leaves. Its flowers are yellowish-green with a prominent corona, and it produces small, woody fruits.

Marsdenia arabica is often found in arid and semi-arid regions, adapting to harsh environmental conditions.
