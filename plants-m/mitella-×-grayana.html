Mitella × grayana

Mitella × grayana, commonly known as the gray's mitella, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Saxifragaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, clustered inflorescences of white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Mitella × grayana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
