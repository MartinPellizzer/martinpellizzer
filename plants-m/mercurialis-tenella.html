Mercurialis tenella

Mercurialis tenella, commonly known as the lesser mercury, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a low-growing annual herb that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, and its leaves are usually opposite, with a distinctive heart-shaped base. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

The plant is also known for its toxic properties, containing compounds that can be harmful to both animals and humans.
