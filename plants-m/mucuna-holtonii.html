Mucuna holtonii

Mucuna holtonii, commonly known as the velvet bean or cowpea, is a leguminous plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, dark purple to black pods that contain edible seeds. The plant is native to tropical regions of the Americas and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fallow fields. Mucuna holtonii is known for its rapid growth and ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

Its seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a source of protein in some cuisines.
