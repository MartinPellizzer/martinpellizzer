Miscanthus ridleyi

Miscanthus ridleyi, commonly known as the Ridley's reed, is a perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and feathery plumes that give it a graceful appearance. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as marshes and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and often have a silvery underside, adding to its ornamental value.

It is valued in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in humid climates.
