Marantochloa ramosissima

Marantochloa ramosissima, commonly known as the red bamboo or red bamboo grass, is a member of the Marantaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its slender, bamboo-like stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often reddish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Marantochloa ramosissima is often used in ornamental horticulture for its attractive foliage and ability to form dense clumps.
