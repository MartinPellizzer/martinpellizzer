Macrotyloma chrysanthum

Macrotyloma chrysanthum, commonly known as the golden cluster bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is known for its edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm climate for optimal growth.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a nitrogen-fixing plant, contributing to soil fertility.
