Melodinus axillaris

Melodinus axillaris, commonly known as the African bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and dense foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough, hairy texture.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in warm, arid climates.
