Muhlenbergia rigens

Muhlenbergia rigens, commonly known as the silver bluestem, is a warm-season grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and silvery-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fluffy seed heads that are often mistaken for cotton, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for native plant gardens.

Muhlenbergia rigens is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
