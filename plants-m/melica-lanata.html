Melica lanata

Melica lanata, commonly known as silky grass or woolly meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, soft, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks.

Melica lanata is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
