Miscanthus ecklonii

Miscanthus ecklonii, commonly known as the silver grass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and striking silver-gray foliage that turns coppery in the fall. The plant produces panicles of small, feathery flowers in late summer, which are often used in floral arrangements. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its drought tolerance and ability to form dense clumps make it a valuable plant for erosion control and wildlife habitat.
