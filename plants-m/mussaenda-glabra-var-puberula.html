Mussaenda glabra var. puberula

Mussaenda glabra var. puberula, commonly known as the soft-hairy mussaenda, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves and clusters of showy, tubular pink or orange flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and prefers well-drained soil.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to gardens.
