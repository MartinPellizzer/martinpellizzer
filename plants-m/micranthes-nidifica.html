Micranthes nidifica

Micranthes nidifica, commonly known as the nestleaf, is a small, herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, woodlands and streambanks, preferring partial to full shade. The plant features small, alternate, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are clustered in umbels and bloom in late spring, producing small, white or pale purple petals.

The plant is notable for its ability to thrive in shaded environments and its historical use in traditional medicine.
