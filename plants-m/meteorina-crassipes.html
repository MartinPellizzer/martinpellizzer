Meteorina crassipes

Meteorina crassipes, commonly known as the broad-leaved meteorina, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its robust, succulent stems and large, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to harsh, dry environments. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it a hardy species for xeriscaping.

Meteorina crassipes is often used in ornamental gardening for its striking appearance and resilience.
