Marilaunidium rupicola, commonly known as the rock lichen, is a unique lichen species that thrives in arid and rocky environments.

It is a symbiotic composite of a fungus and a cyanobacterium, which allows it to survive in nutrient-poor and harsh conditions. The plant's structure consists of a crust-like thallus that adheres tightly to rock surfaces, enabling it to retain moisture and withstand extreme temperatures.
