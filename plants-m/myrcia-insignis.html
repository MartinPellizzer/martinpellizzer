Myrcia insignis

Myrcia insignis, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces bright red, fleshy berries that are a food source for various birds and mammals. Myrcia insignis is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its dense foliage and fragrant aroma.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions, posing challenges to native ecosystems.
