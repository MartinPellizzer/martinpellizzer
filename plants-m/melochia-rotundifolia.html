Melochia rotundifolia

Melochia rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaf melochia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, featuring heart-shaped leaves with a smooth, waxy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by orange or red berries containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for ornamental gardens.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive addition to tropical flora collections.
