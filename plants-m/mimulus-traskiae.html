Mimulus traskiae

Mimulus traskiae, commonly known as the Trask's monkeyflower, is a species of flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as stream banks and meadows. It has heart-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

Mimulus traskiae is a perennial herb that plays a role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining ecological balance in its native environment.
