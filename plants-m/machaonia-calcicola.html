Machaonia calcicola

Machaonia calcicola, commonly known as the limestone machaon, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils, often found in high-altitude environments. Its leaves are typically narrow and evergreen, adapted to the harsh conditions of its habitat.

Machaonia calcicola plays an important role in its ecosystem, providing food and shelter for various insect species.
