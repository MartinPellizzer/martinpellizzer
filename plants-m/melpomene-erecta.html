Melpomene erecta

Melpomene erecta, commonly known as the scarlet glorybower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Melpomene erecta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
