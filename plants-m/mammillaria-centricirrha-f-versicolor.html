Mammillaria centricirrha f. versicolor

Mammillaria centricirrha f. versicolor, commonly known as the variegated mammillaria, is a succulent cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in white and yellow tubercles, which give it a distinctive, ornamental appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires ample sunlight to maintain its vibrant coloration.

Due to its unique appearance, it is a popular choice for collectors and enthusiasts of cacti.
