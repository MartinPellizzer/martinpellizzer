Mollugo crockeri

Mollugo crockeri, commonly known as Crocker's sandmat, is a low-growing annual plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the family Molluginaceae and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and small, fleshy leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with four petals, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
