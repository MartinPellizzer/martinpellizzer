Monochaetum uberrimum

Monochaetum uberrimum, commonly known as the broad-leafed grass tree, is a striking plant native to the rainforests of northern Queensland, Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, fan-shaped leaves that can reach up to 2 meters in length. The plant produces striking yellowish-white flower spikes that bloom in late summer, attracting a variety of pollinators. Its unique morphology and ecological significance make it a notable species in its native habitat.

However, it is also listed as vulnerable due to habitat loss and environmental changes.
