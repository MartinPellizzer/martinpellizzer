Melaleuca ringens

Melaleuca ringens, commonly known as the slender tea tree, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia, where it typically grows in heathlands and open forests. The plant is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in winter. Its bark is often flaky and reddish in color, and it is known for its aromatic properties.

Melaleuca ringens is also valued for its ecological role in supporting native wildlife and its use in traditional herbal medicine.
