Mentha × velutina

Mentha × velutina, commonly known as velvet mint, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Mentha viridis and Mentha spicata.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

Velvet mint is valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in herbal teas and traditional medicine.
