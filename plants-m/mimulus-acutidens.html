Mimulus acutidens

Mimulus acutidens, commonly known as the sharp-leaved monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats, including stream banks and woodland areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a serrated edge, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Mimulus acutidens is part of the Physaleae tribe within the Plantaginaceae family, showcasing its botanical significance in ecological and horticultural contexts.
