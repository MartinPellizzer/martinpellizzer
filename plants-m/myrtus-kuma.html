Myrtus kuma

Myrtus kuma, commonly known as the Kashmir myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It features small, dark green leaves that are glossy and aromatic, often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces tiny white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by small, dark blue berries. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in alpine meadows.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties make it a significant plant in its native region.
