Matricaria ledebourii

Matricaria ledebourii, commonly known as the desert chamomile, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, white to yellowish composite flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It has slender, branched stems and narrow, gray-green leaves.

Matricaria ledebourii is often used in traditional medicine for its purported calming and anti-inflammatory properties.
