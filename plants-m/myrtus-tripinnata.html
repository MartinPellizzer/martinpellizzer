Myrtus tripinnata

Myrtus tripinnata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved myrtle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring small, narrow leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces tiny, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, berry-like fruits. Myrtus tripinnata is well adapted to coastal and rocky environments, showing resistance to salt spray and drought.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
