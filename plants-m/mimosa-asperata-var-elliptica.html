Mimosa asperata var. elliptica

Mimosa asperata var. elliptica, commonly known as the rough mimosa, is a shrub native to South America.

It features compound leaves that are elliptical in shape and covered in fine, stiff hairs, giving it a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellow, globular flower heads that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to various garden conditions.

Its unique appearance and ability to tolerate drought make it a distinctive addition to native plant gardens.
