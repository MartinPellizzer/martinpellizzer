Macoubea paucifolia

Macoubea paucifolia, commonly known as the slender flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with numerous narrow leaflets, and it prefers well-drained, sandy soils.

Macoubea paucifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively low maintenance in suitable climates.
