Massowia commutata

Massowia commutata, commonly known as the common sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It typically features small, clustered leaves and produces clusters of tiny, white or pale pink flowers. The plant is often found in sandy or gravelly soils, thriving in well-drained environments. Its botanical structure includes a prostrate or ascending stem with opposite leaf arrangement.

Massowia commutata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in rock gardens or as a ground cover.
