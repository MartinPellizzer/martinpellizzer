Malaxis pyrulifera

Malaxis pyrulifera, commonly known as the pyramidal malaxis, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and unique, pyramidal-shaped flower spikes. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are often clustered in a dense inflorescence. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is typically found in forest understories.

Malaxis pyrulifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
