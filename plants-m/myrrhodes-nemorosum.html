Myrrhodes nemorosum

Myrrhodes nemorosum, commonly known as the forest myrrh, is a small evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in early summer. Its leaves are dark green, leathery, and arranged in whorls around the stems.

Myrrhodes nemorosum thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded woodland environments.
