Madronella odoratissima

Madronella odoratissima, commonly known as the sweet-scented madronella, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The fruit is a small, capsule-like structure that releases seeds when mature.
