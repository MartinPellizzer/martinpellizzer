Miconia longipetiolata

Miconia longipetiolata, commonly known as the long-petioled miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers, which are typically followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Miconia longipetiolata is known for its rapid growth and is often found in moist, shaded forest understories.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in some regions, though it can become invasive in certain environments.
