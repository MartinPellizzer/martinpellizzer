Mimosa asperata f. mollis

Mimosa asperata f. mollis, commonly known as the soft-stemmed mimosa, is a variant of the mimosa plant characterized by its softer, more delicate stems compared to the typical form.

It is a shrub native to South America, particularly found in regions of Argentina and Brazil. The plant features bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, yellow, spherical flower heads that bloom in late spring or early summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and adaptability to various soil types.
