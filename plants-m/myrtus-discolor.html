Myrtus discolor

Myrtus discolor, commonly known as the pink myrtle, is a flowering shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, evergreen leaves that are gray-green in color and have a slightly fuzzy texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by dark blue berries. Myrtus discolor thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it both visually appealing and ecologically beneficial to local wildlife.
