Morinda philippinensis

Morinda philippinensis, commonly known as the Philippine morinda, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, clustered white or pink flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, round seeds.

Morinda philippinensis is often found in forest understories and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
