Mesosphaerum alutaceum

Mesosphaerum alutaceum, commonly known as the slender sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth form and small, pale green to blue-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in spring. It is adapted to survive in nutrient-poor soils and can tolerate periods of drought.

Mesosphaerum alutaceum plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
