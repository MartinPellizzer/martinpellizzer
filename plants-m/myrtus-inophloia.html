Myrtus inophloia

Myrtus inophloia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved myrtle, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and tea tree. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in coastal environments.
