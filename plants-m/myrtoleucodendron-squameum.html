Myrtoleucodendron squameum

Myrtoleucodendron squameum, commonly known as the squamose myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, leathery leaves that are covered in a fine, whitish powder, giving them a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, with a strong aromatic scent.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
