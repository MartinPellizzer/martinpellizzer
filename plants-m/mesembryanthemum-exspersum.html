Mesembryanthemum exspersum

Mesembryanthemum exspersum, commonly known as the ice plant, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the morning and close by midday. It is often used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.

Mesembryanthemum exspersum is also valued for its ability to prevent soil erosion in sandy or coastal areas.
