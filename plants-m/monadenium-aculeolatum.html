Monadenium aculeolatum

Monadenium aculeolatum, commonly known as the spiny globemallow, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its globular shape and spiny stems, which provide protection against herbivores. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the dry season. Its leaves are often reduced to spines, minimizing water loss in harsh environments.

Monadenium aculeolatum is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
