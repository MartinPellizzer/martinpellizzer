Matucana oreodoxa subsp. rebutiiflora

Matucana oreodoxa subsp.

rebutiiflora, commonly known as the Rebutia-flowered matucana, is a species of cactus native to South America, specifically Bolivia. It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and is often covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair.

The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in rock gardens.
