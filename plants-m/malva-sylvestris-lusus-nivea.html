Malva sylvestris lusus nivea

Malva sylvestris lusus nivea, commonly known as the white marshmallow, is a variant of the common mallow.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant features large, velvety leaves and produces showy, pink or white flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.

Its fluffy seed heads are a distinctive feature that aid in wind pollination.
