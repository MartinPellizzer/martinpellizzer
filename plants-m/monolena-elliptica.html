Monolena elliptica

Monolena elliptica, commonly known as the broadleaf nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and peppers. The plant has simple, elliptic-shaped leaves that are usually arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are typically white or pale blue, with five petals and a distinctive yellow center.

Monolena elliptica produces small, round berries that contain seeds, and it often grows in disturbed soils or along roadsides.
