Meriania maxima

Meriania maxima, commonly known as the giant meriania, is a striking tropical shrub native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers with red stamens, which are often clustered in terminal inflorescences. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Meriania maxima is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
