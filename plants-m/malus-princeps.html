Malus princeps

Malus princeps, commonly known as the Chinese white apple, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The tree produces small, sweet, yellow or red fruits that are edible. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Malus princeps is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in traditional Chinese horticulture.
