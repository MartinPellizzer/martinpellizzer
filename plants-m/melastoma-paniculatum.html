Melastoma paniculatum

Melastoma paniculatum, commonly known as the purple flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences composed of clusters of small, often purple or red flowers. The plant has opposite, elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. It produces woody capsules as fruit, which contain numerous seeds.

Melastoma paniculatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
