Melandrium diurnum

Melandrium diurnum, commonly known as the morning catchfly, is a flowering plant in the family Caryophyllaceae.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant features slender stems and small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in the early morning. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.
