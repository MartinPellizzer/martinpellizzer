Mamanira

Mamanira, commonly known as the "tropical milkweed," is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its showy, orange-red flowers that attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are typically hairy and have a rough texture. Mamanira thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas or near water sources.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitat.
