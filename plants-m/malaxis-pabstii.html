Malaxis pabstii

Malaxis pabstii, commonly known as the Pabst's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the wetlands of southern Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and is often found in grasslands and forest understories. Its root system is fibrous, and it produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

Malaxis pabstii is a rare species, often requiring specific environmental conditions to thrive.
