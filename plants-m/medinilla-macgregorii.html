Medinilla macgregorii

Medinilla macgregorii, commonly known as the velvet tangerine, is a tropical shrub native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that resemble tangerines, hence its common name. Medinilla macgregorii thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
