Mikania pseudohoffmanniana var. pseudohoffmanniana

Mikania pseudohoffmanniana var. pseudohoffmanniana, commonly known as the creeping Mikania, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, daisy-like flowers. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often in late spring to early summer.

This species is often found in moist, shaded areas such as forest understories and along stream banks.
