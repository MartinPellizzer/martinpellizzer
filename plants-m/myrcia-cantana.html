Myrcia cantana

Myrcia cantana, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, featuring glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are edible for some animals but toxic to humans. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in moist, well-drained soils.

Myrcia cantana is also known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
