Mollia sphaerocarpa var. acrocarpa

Mollia sphaerocarpa var. acrocarpa, commonly known as the acrocarp spikemoss, is a type of moss belonging to the family Hypnaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, cushion-like growth form and dark green, wiry stems. The plant produces spore capsules that are borne on short, upright stalks, which are typically clustered at the tips of the stems. Its leaves are elongated and pointed, with a smooth, glossy surface.

This variety is often found in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices.
