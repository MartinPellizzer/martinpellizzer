Melocactus smithii

Melocactus smithii, commonly known as the Smith's melon cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its globular shape and smooth, dark green to blue-gray skin. The plant produces a single, fleshy fruit that resembles a melon, which is edible and has a sweet, mild flavor. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Due to its slow growth and unique appearance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
