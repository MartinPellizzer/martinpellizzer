Malveopsis setosa

Malveopsis setosa, commonly known as the silky mallow, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant is characterized by its soft, hairy stems and leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. Its flowers are typically pale pink or white, with five petals and numerous stamens.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
