Machaerium brasiliense var. densicomum

Machaerium brasiliense var. densicomum, commonly known as the dense-leaved machaerium, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense foliage and erect growth habit. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being ovate to lanceolate in shape, and it produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in spikes.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects due to its nitrogen-fixing capabilities.
