Microtoena urticifolia var. subdentata

Microtoena urticifolia var. subdentata, commonly known as the stinging nettle variant, is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its finely toothed leaves.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes various species of stinging nettles. The plant is notable for its hollow, slender stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs that release an irritating chemical, causing a skin rash upon contact.

This variant is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks.
