Madronella douglasii

Madronella douglasii, commonly known as the Douglas madrone, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish bark and clusters of white, five-petaled flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, orange-red berries that are edible for birds and mammals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Its unique appearance and ecological importance make it a notable species in its native habitat.
