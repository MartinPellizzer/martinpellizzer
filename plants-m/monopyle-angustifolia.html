Monopyle angustifolia

Monopyle angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf monopyle, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It features slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along its stem. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are often borne on short pedicels. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive species within the Monopyle genus.
