Monophyllaea pendula

Monophyllaea pendula, commonly known as the hanging leaf plant, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, pendulous inflorescences that bear small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Monophyllaea pendula is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in a garden setting.
