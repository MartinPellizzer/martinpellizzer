Matelea arizonica

Matelea arizonica, commonly known as Arizona milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, pale yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often branched and covered in a white, waxy coating.

Arizona milkweed plays an important role in supporting local pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
