Mentha laxiflora

Mentha laxiflora, commonly known as narrow-leaved mint, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia, including China, India, and Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves and small, pale purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream sides. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their cooling and digestive properties.

Mentha laxiflora is closely related to other members of the mint family, sharing similar aromatic compounds and growth habits.
