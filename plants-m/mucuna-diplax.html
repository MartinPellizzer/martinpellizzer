Mucuna diplax

Mucuna diplax, commonly known as velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety pubescence on its stems and leaves, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several elliptical seeds. Mucuna diplax is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in agricultural systems.

It also has medicinal uses, with its seeds containing compounds that have been studied for potential therapeutic properties.
