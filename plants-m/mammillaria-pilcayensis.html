Mammillaria pilcayensis

Mammillaria pilcayensis, commonly known as the Pilcay's hedgehog cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in white, woolly spines. The plant typically grows in rocky, arid environments and is adapted to thrive in low-water conditions. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, appearing in late spring or early summer.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a popular choice for collectors and enthusiasts of succulents.
