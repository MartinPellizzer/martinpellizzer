Microstylis fissa

Microstylis fissa, commonly known as the slender spike-rush, is a small, herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, often in temperate regions. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, with three stamens and a single pistil.

Microstylis fissa plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetlands and provides habitat for small invertebrates.
