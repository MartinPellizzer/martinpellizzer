Maesa pyrifolia

Maesa pyrifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf maesa, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage. The plant features small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries. Its leaves are leathery and oval-shaped, often with a waxy coating.

Maesa pyrifolia is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions.
