Mercurialis indica

Mercurialis indica, commonly known as the Indian mercury or devil's bit, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and it produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed soils, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
