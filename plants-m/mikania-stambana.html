Mikania stambana

Mikania stambana, commonly known as the tropical milkweed, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small clusters of flowers. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers with a distinctive tubular shape, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Mikania stambana is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

It is often used in ornamental gardening due to its vibrant blooms and medicinal properties.
