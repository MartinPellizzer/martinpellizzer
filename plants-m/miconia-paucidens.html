Miconia paucidens

Miconia paucidens, commonly known as the slender miconia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers with five petals, typically arranged in clusters. Its fruits are small, berry-like structures that contain numerous seeds.

Miconia paucidens is often found in moist, shaded understories of tropical forests.
