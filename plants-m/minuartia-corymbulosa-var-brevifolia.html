Minuartia corymbulosa var. brevifolia

Minuartia corymbulosa var. brevifolia, commonly known as the dwarf rockcress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to rocky and alpine environments.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically has a mat-forming growth habit with dense, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, sunny conditions.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appeal, it is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
