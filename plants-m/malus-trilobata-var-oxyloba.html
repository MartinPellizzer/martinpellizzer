Malus trilobata var. oxyloba

Malus trilobata var. oxyloba, commonly known as the Pacific crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by edible, red or yellow fruits. The leaves are typically ovate with three lobes, giving the plant its scientific name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for wildlife.
