Melaleuca exarata

Melaleuca exarata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf paperbark, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark that reveals a papery texture. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in spikes, which are typically arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a leathery texture and a distinct aromatic scent.

Melaleuca exarata is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils and salty sea breezes.
