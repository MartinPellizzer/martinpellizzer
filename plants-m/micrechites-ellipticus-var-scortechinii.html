Micrechites ellipticus var. scortechinii

Micrechites ellipticus var. scortechinii, commonly known as the narrow-leafed rock cress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to rocky and arid environments.

It typically grows in crevices and on rocky substrates, showcasing its adaptation to harsh conditions. The plant features slender, elliptical leaves that are usually less than 2 cm in length, giving it a compact appearance. Its small, white or pale yellow flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees.

This variety is noted for its resilience and is often found in mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.
