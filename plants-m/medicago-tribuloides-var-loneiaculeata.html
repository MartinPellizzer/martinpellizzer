Medicago tribuloides var. loneiaculeata

Medicago tribuloides var. loneiaculeata, commonly known as the narrowleaf sainfoin, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves with narrow, serrated leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by elongated, curved pods containing several seeds. It is adapted to dry, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed sites.

This variety is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability and historical use in livestock forage.
