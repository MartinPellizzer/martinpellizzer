Myrcia carangola

Myrcia carangola, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various bird species. Myrcia carangola is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and can thrive in a range of soil types.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions if not managed properly.
