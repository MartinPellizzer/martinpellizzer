Melia azedarach var. australasica

Melia azedarach var. australasica, commonly known as the Australian chinaberry tree, is a variant of the chinaberry tree native to Australia.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers that appear in spring. The tree produces small, berry-like fruits that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in warm climates and is often planted for its ornamental value.

However, it can become invasive in some regions, outcompeting native flora.
