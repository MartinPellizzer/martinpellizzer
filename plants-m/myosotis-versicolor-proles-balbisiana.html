Myosotis versicolor proles balbisiana

Myosotis versicolor proles balbisiana, commonly known as the Balbisian bluebell, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Myosotidaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The flowers bloom in early spring, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
