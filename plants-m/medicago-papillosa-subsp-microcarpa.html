Medicago papillosa subsp. microcarpa

Medicago papillosa subsp.

microcarpa, commonly known as the small-fruited goat's head, is a flowering plant in the legume family Fabaceae. It is a perennial herb characterized by its small, globular pods and trifoliate leaves.

The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia. It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, and its seeds are dispersed by wind.

The subspecies is distinguished by its smaller, more compact fruits compared to other members of the species.
