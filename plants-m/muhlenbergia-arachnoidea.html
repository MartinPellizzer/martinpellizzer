Muhlenbergia arachnoidea

Muhlenbergia arachnoidea, commonly known as the spidergrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-blue foliage and delicate, spider-like inflorescence that gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to form dense, low-growing mats.

Muhlenbergia arachnoidea is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
