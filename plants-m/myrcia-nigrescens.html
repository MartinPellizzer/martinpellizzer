Myrcia nigrescens

Myrcia nigrescens, commonly known as black myrtle, is a shrub or small tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and fragrant, reddish-brown flowers. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are rich in essential oils and have been used in traditional medicine. Myrcia nigrescens thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
