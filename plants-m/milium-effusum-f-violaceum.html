Milium effusum f. violaceum

Milium effusum f. violaceum, commonly known as violet moss, is a variant of the common millet grass.

It is characterized by its dense, carpet-like growth habit and its striking violet-colored inflorescences. The plant typically forms low, compact clumps with narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland habitats.
