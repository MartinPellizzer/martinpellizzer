Manihot esculenta var. flavicaulis

Manihot esculenta var. flavicaulis, commonly known as the yellow-stem cassava, is a variant of the cassava plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellowish stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces edible tuberous roots that are rich in starch and used as a food source. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its unique coloration and nutritional properties.
