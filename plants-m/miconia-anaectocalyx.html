Miconia anaectocalyx

Miconia anaectocalyx, commonly known as the red miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a bright red, fleshy capsule that contains numerous seeds.

Miconia anaectocalyx is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
