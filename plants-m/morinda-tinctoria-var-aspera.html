Morinda tinctoria var. aspera

Morinda tinctoria var. aspera, commonly known as the rough morinda, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by orange-red berries containing numerous seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and traditional medicinal uses.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to a variety of soil types and climates.
