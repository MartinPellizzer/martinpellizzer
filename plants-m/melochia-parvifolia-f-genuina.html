Melochia parvifolia f. genuina

Melochia parvifolia f. genuina, commonly known as the small-leaved melochia, is a compact shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves that are typically glabrous and arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers, which are followed by distinctive, inflated fruit capsules. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for ornamental gardening.

Its unique foliage and compact growth habit make it a desirable addition to tropical and subtropical landscapes.
