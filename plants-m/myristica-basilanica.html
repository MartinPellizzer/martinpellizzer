Myristica basilanica

Myristica basilanica, commonly known as the Basilan nutmeg.

It is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Philippines, particularly the island of Basilan. The plant belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its leaves are elliptical and glossy, while its flowers are small and white, often clustered in panicles.

The tree thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for both its ornamental and economic significance.
