Malveopsis coromandeliana var. parvifolia

Malveopsis coromandeliana var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-flowered malveopsis, is a flowering plant native to southern India.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and small, pale pink to white flowers. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along riverbanks.
