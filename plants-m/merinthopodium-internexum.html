Merinthopodium internexum

Merinthopodium internexum, commonly known as the tangled root vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. Its roots are often intertwined, giving it its common name.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
