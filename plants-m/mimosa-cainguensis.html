Mimosa cainguensis

Mimosa cainguensis, commonly known as the yellow mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils. It has a short lifespan, typically living for only a few years before dying.

Mimosa cainguensis is also known for its sensitivity to touch, as its leaves fold shut when disturbed.
