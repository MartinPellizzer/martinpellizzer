Marlea affinis

Marlea affinis, commonly known as the common marlhead, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in open grasslands or along coastal areas. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it is often used in gardens for its ornamental value.

Marlea affinis is adapted to arid conditions and can withstand periods of drought.
