Manettia cordifolia var. paranensis

Manettia cordifolia var. paranensis, commonly known as the Brazilian bellflower, is a species of flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in the summer months.

It is characterized by its aromatic foliage and is often found in moist, shaded environments within its native habitat.
