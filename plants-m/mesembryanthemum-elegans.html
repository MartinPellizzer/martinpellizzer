Mesembryanthemum elegans

Mesembryanthemum elegans, commonly known as the silver moonflower, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces showy, star-shaped flowers that open at night, typically in shades of pink or purple. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and nocturnal blooming habit make it a popular choice for rock gardens and drought-tolerant landscapes.
