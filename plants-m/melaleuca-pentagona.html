Melaleuca pentagona

Melaleuca pentagona, commonly known as the narrow-leaved paperbark, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia, where it typically grows in coastal and sandy soils. The plant is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It produces small, white or pink flowers that attract nectar-feeding birds and insects.

Melaleuca pentagona is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and ability to thrive in various environmental conditions.
