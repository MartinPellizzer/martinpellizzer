Miconia haemanthoides

Miconia haemanthoides, commonly known as the red miconia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a bright red, fleshy berry that contains numerous seeds.

Miconia haemanthoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage and unique floral display.
