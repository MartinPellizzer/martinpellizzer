Myriophyllum ternatum

Myriophyllum ternatum, commonly known as water milfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to North America.

It has slender, branching stems and small, opposite leaves that are often finely serrated. The plant produces tiny, floating flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical heads. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes.

Myriophyllum ternatum plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
