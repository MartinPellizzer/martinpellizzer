Mesua acuminatissima

Mesua acuminatissima, commonly known as the Malabar torchwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and distinctive, pendulous, red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has simple, alternate, elliptical leaves with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside. Its fruits are woody capsules that split open to release seeds.

Mesua acuminatissima is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in local medicine.
