Maranthes floribunda

Maranthes floribunda, commonly known as the clustered spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of showy, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Maranthes floribunda is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
