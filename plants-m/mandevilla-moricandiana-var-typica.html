Mandevilla moricandiana var. typica

Mandevilla moricandiana var. typica, commonly known as the Moricandia mandevilla, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant produces showy blooms throughout the growing season, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets and containers. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
