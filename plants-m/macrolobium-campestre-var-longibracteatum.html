Macrolobium campestre var. longibracteatum

Macrolobium campestre var. longibracteatum, commonly known as the long-bract wild bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features trifoliate leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets and produces yellow flowers clustered in racemes. Its distinctive feature is the long, bract-like structures that enclose the flower clusters, giving it its varietal name.

The plant is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas, where it thrives in full sun.
