Milla hyacinthina var. lactea

Milla hyacinthina var. lactea, commonly known as the milk hyacinth, is a striking plant characterized by its unique, milky-white flowers that resemble small bells.

Native to the mountainous regions of South America, it thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers well-drained, loamy soil. The plant features strap-like, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. Its blooming period typically occurs in late spring to early summer, adding a dramatic visual impact to gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and specific growing requirements, it is often cultivated in specialized gardens or greenhouses.
