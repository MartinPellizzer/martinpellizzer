Mesochlaena toppingii

Mesochlaena toppingii, commonly known as the slender swamp sedge, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets. It plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland flora in its native range.
