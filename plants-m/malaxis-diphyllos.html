Malaxis diphyllos

Malaxis diphyllos, commonly known as the two-leaf malaxis, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in two pairs, giving it its common name.

Malaxis diphyllos is noted for its delicate appearance and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
