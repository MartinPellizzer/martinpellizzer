Mentha × kerneri var. mucronulata

Mentha × kerneri var. mucronulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved peppermint, is a hybrid mint species native to Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and a strong, aromatic scent. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-drained soil. Its flowers are small and tubular, often pink or purple, and appear in clusters.

It is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, similar to other mint species.
