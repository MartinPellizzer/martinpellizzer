Megastachya gouinii

Megastachya gouinii, commonly known as the golden spike grass, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and distinctive golden spike-like inflorescence that emerges from the base of the plant. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, arranged in a dense, spike-like cluster. Megastachya gouinii thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or open habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
