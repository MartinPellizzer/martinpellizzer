Mentzelia crocea

Mentzelia crocea, commonly known as the yellow globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant features hairy, branched stems and heart-shaped, lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy, yellow flowers are clustered in dense, globular inflorescences, which give it its common name.

Mentzelia crocea is known for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
