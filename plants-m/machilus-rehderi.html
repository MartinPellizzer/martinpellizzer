Machilus rehderi

Machilus rehderi, commonly known as the Rehder's machilus, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite, and elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a drupe, containing a single seed, and it is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in horticultural settings.
