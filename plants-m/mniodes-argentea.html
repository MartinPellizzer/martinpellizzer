Mniodes argentea

Mniodes argentea, commonly known as silver mallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-green leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It has a creeping or upright growth habit and is often found in coastal or arid regions.

Mniodes argentea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
