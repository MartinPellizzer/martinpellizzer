Mimosa farinosa

Mimosa farinosa, commonly known as the silky mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellow, spherical flower heads that appear in clusters, often resembling miniature globes. Its leaves are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, velvety appearance.

Mimosa farinosa is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and delicate flowers.
