Monochaetum gleasonianum

Monochaetum gleasonianum, commonly known as the Gleason's spikeless grass, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its compact, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with inconspicuous flowers, typical of many grass species. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
