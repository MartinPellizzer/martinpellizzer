Myrcia tobagensis

Myrcia tobagensis, commonly known as Togaberry, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is a small, yellowish berry that ripens in the dry season.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
