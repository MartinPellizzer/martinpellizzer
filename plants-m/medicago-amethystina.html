Medicago amethystina

Medicago amethystina, commonly known as the amethyst burclover, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, violet-colored flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, three-lobed seed pods that contain numerous tiny, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Medicago amethystina is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
