Melinis leucantha

Melinis leucantha, commonly known as whiteleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, light green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue to white flowers in dense clusters during the summer months. It is adapted to a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained soils and full sun.

Melinis leucantha is often found in open woodlands, prairies, and along roadsides.
