Mantalania sambiranensis

Mantalania sambiranensis, commonly known as the Madagascar flame tree, is a striking tropical plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Anacardiaceae and is characterized by its vibrant, flame-like orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is also cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant in tropical regions.
