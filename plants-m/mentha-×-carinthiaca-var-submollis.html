Mentha × carinthiaca var. submollis

Mentha × carinthiaca var. submollis, commonly known as the woolly mint, is a hybrid perennial herb native to Europe.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This variety is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and mild minty fragrance.
