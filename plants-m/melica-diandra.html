Melica diandra

Melica diandra, commonly known as the slender brome, is a species of grass native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, slightly hairy stems. The plant typically produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brownish flowers. Melica diandra thrives in moist, temperate environments such as meadows and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of grassland ecosystems.
