Monelasmum molleri

Monelasmum molleri, commonly known as the slender mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees, primarily in the tropics.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers, followed by berries that are typically red or yellow. It is found in regions such as Southeast Asia, Australia, and parts of Africa.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting subject for studies on plant parasitism and ecological interactions.
