Medicago disciformis f. parvicarpa

Medicago disciformis f. parvicarpa, commonly known as the small-fruited goat's rue, is a botanical variant of the common goat's rue.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. Its fruits are distinctive for their small, spherical form, which sets it apart from other varieties.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry grasslands and disturbed areas.
