Mimulus nanus var. subuniflorus

Mimulus nanus var. subuniflorus, commonly known as the dwarf yellow monkeyflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a distinctive spurred shape. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a compact, ornamental plant.
