Mapania assimilis

Mapania assimilis, commonly known as the yellow star-of-Bethlehem, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow, star-shaped flowers in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

However, it is considered invasive in some areas due to its rapid spread and competitive nature.
