Mephitidia tomentosa

Mephitidia tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly mephit, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale green in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
