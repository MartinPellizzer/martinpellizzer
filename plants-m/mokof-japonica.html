Mokof japonica

Mokof japonica, commonly known as the Japanese bellflower, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and prefers well-drained soil in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

Mokof japonica is often cultivated for its attractive blooms and is relatively easy to care for in temperate climates.
