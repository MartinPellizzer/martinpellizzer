Mercurialis pumila

Mercurialis pumila, commonly known as the dwarf mercury plant, is a low-growing annual herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs and produces small, spherical fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Euphorbiaceae family.
