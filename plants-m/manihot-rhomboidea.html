Manihot rhomboidea

Manihot rhomboidea, commonly known as the arrowleaf manioc, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, arrow-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color. Its roots are used in various traditional practices, although it is less commonly cultivated for food compared to other manioc species.

The plant is also valued for its potential medicinal properties.
