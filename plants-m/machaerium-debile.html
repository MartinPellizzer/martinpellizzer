Machaerium debile

Machaerium debile, commonly known as slender machaerium, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and often nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant typically features slender, upright stems with small, trifoliate leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Machaerium debile is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local insect populations.
