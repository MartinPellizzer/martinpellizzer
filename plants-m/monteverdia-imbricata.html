Monteverdia imbricata

Monteverdia imbricata, commonly known as the Mexican orchid or the rock orchid, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pale yellow. The plant prefers shaded, humid environments and is often found growing on rocky substrates or in forest understories. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding environment, and it has a unique ability to form symbiotic relationships with certain fungi.
