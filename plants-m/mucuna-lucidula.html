Mucuna lucidula

Mucuna lucidula, commonly known as the shining cowpea, is a tropical leguminous plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are often glossy and dark green, and its distinctive yellow flowers. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain several elliptical, light green seeds. Mucuna lucidula is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a common ornamental and forage plant.

It also has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing soil fertility.
