Malva rotundifolia var. leiocarpa

Malva rotundifolia var. leiocarpa, commonly known as the round-leaved mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, rounded leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its stems are typically hairy and can grow up to 1 meter in height, with a tendency to spread through rhizomes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
