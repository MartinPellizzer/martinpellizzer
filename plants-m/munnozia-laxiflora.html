Munnozia laxiflora

Munnozia laxiflora, commonly known as the desert lily, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils and crevices. Its blooming period typically occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is well adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Munnozia laxiflora plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
