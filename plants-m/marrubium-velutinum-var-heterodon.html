Marrubium velutinum var. heterodon

Marrubium velutinum var. heterodon, commonly known as the hairy hartsage, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs.

Its medicinal properties have been historically recognized for their anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic effects.
