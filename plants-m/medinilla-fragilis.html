Medinilla fragilis

Medinilla fragilis, commonly known as the delicate medinilla, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with glossy, elliptical leaves. It produces small, clustered fruits that are edible but not widely consumed.

Medinilla fragilis thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
