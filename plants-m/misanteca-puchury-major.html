Misanteca puchury-major

Misanteca puchury-major, commonly known as the golden cup plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in dense, cup-shaped clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun to partial shade. It is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.

Misanteca puchury-major is also valued for its historical use in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.
