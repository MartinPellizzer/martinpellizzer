Mikania banisteriae

Mikania banisteriae, commonly known as the banana vine, is a vigorous climbing plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense foliage, often found in humid, shaded environments. Its stems are fibrous and can reach lengths of several meters, making it a common sight in tropical rainforests.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
