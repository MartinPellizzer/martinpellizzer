Malus coronaria var. angustifolia

Malus coronaria var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved apple or narrowleaf serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It features lance-shaped, dark green leaves that turn reddish in the fall, and it produces clusters of white flowers in early spring. The plant bears small, edible berries that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping and native plant gardens.
