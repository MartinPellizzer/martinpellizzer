Myrosmodes paludosa

Myrosmodes paludosa, commonly known as swamp myrtle, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, acidic soils, often found in swampy or marshy areas. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a whitish, waxy coating. Its fragrant white flowers bloom in the spring and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Myrosmodes paludosa plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and contributing to soil stabilization.
