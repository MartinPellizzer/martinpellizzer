Myrtus supraaxillaris

Myrtus supraaxillaris, commonly known as the African myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of tiny white flowers in the axillary buds, which are followed by small, red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
