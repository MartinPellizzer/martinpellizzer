Monoon fragrans

Monoon fragrans, commonly known as the sweet pepper tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are usually glossy green. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by bright red, fleshy berries that are edible. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
