Melocactus huallancensis

Melocactus huallancensis, commonly known as the Huallanca melon cactus, is a species native to the Andean region of South America.

It is a slow-growing cactus characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stem and a distinctive, rounded, fruit-like structure at the top. The plant produces a fleshy, spiky fruit that is typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring well-drained soil and minimal water.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
