Mitrasacme alsinoides var. indica

Mitrasacme alsinoides var. indica, commonly known as Indian mustards or Indian cress, is a flowering plant native to South Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other economically important plants like cabbage and broccoli. The plant is characterized by its small, white or yellow flowers and pinnately divided leaves. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Its seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some cuisines.
