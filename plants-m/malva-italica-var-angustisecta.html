Malva italica var. angustisecta

Malva italica var. angustisecta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallow, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry grasslands and rocky areas.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
