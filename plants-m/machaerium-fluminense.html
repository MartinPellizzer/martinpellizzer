Machaerium fluminense

Machaerium fluminense, commonly known as the Brazilian needlegrass, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It is typically found in moist, tropical environments and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its unique appearance.

Machaerium fluminense is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
