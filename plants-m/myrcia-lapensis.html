Myrcia lapensis

Myrcia lapensis, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various birds and mammals. Myrcia lapensis is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in moist, well-drained soils.

It is also valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
