Muehlenbeckia stuebelii

Muehlenbeckia stuebelii, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a low-growing shrub native to New Zealand.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has slender, wiry stems that are often covered in a silvery, fuzzy coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are narrow and linear, typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky habitats.
