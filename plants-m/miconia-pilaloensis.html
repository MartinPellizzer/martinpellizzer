Miconia pilaloensis

Miconia pilaloensis, commonly known as the Pacific Island miconia, is a tropical shrub native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers that are typically clustered in terminal panicles. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is sometimes considered a problematic species in certain regions.
