Mentha sylvestris var. petiolata

Mentha sylvestris var. petiolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, petiolate leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in whorled clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and woodland edges.

Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its use in traditional herbal remedies and culinary applications.
