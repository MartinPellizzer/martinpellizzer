Myrcia paniculata

Myrcia paniculata, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various birds and wildlife. It has a strong, aromatic scent and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Myrcia paniculata is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to different soil types.
