Monolena primuliflora

Monolena primuliflora, commonly known as the slender white violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, white, five-petaled flowers that bloom in early spring, often in clusters at the ends of stems. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces and a shallow root system. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and wetlands.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in ecological studies and native plant gardens.
