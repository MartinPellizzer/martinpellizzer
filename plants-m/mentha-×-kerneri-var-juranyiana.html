Mentha × kerneri var. juranyiana

Mentha × kerneri var. juranyiana, commonly known as the Hungarian germander, is a hybrid mint species native to Hungary and surrounding regions.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly hairy leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is often used in herbal teas and traditional medicine for its calming and digestive properties.
