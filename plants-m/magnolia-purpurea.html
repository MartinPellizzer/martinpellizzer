Magnolia purpurea

Magnolia purpurea, commonly known as the purple magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth, dark green upper surface. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
