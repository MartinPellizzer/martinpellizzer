Masdevallia mataxa

Masdevallia mataxa, commonly known as the Mataxa orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a compact growth habit and is characterized by its intricate, often colorful flower structure. The plant typically produces small, showy blooms in shades of pink, red, and purple, which are arranged in a spiral pattern along the inflorescence. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor soils, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Masdevallia mataxa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
