Myrcia involucrata

Myrcia involucrata, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its small, oval leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries. Myrcia involucrata is typically found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

It also has traditional uses in folk medicine.
