Melastoma humile

Melastoma humile, commonly known as the small melastoma, is a perennial shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, with five petals and prominent stamens. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that release small, winged seeds upon maturity.

Melastoma humile is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
