Macaranga trachyphylla

Macaranga trachyphylla, commonly known as the rough-leafed mallow, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The leaves are palmately lobed, with a rough, hairy texture on the underside. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This species is often found in moist, shaded forest environments and is adapted to thrive in high humidity and warm climates.
