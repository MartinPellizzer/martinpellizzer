Malva alcea subsp. ribifolia

Malva alcea subsp.

ribifolia, commonly known as the red campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its hairy stems and distinctive pink to red flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun. Its leaves are palmately lobed and covered in fine hairs, while its fruit is a dry capsule containing numerous small seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
