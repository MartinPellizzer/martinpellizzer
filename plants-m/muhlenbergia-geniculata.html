Muhlenbergia geniculata

Muhlenbergia geniculata, commonly known as the silver hairgrass, is a perennial grass native to the United States.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray blades and distinctive, curved inflorescences. This plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established. It is often used in xeriscaping and native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.

Muhlenbergia geniculata plays a role in supporting local wildlife, including birds and insects.
