Marrubium mollissimum

Marrubium mollissimum, commonly known as velvet horehound, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage that gives it a velvety texture, and its small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. It has a strong, aromatic scent and is known for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.

Marrubium mollissimum is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
