Myoxanthus lonchophyllus

Myoxanthus lonchophyllus, commonly known as the red-tipped hibiscus, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking red bracts that surround its small, yellow flowers. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Myoxanthus lonchophyllus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in warm climates.
