Monotagma tuberosum

Monotagma tuberosum, commonly known as the African violet, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette of velvety, dark green leaves and produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of purple, blue, and white. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and indirect sunlight, making it a popular houseplant. Its tubers store nutrients, allowing it to survive periods of drought.

The flowers are typically short-lived but are succeeded by small, round fruits containing seeds.
