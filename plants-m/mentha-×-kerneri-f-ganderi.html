Mentha × kerneri f. ganderi

Mentha × kerneri f. ganderi, commonly known as the Gander's mint, is a hybrid mint species native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its aromatic, serrated leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are often used in herbal teas and traditional remedies due to their menthol-like scent and cooling properties.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
