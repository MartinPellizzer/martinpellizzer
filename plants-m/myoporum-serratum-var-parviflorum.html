Myoporum serratum var. parviflorum

Myoporum serratum var. parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered myoporum, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in spring. It is adapted to coastal and sandy soils, showing some degree of salt tolerance.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and ornamental value.
