Malva altissima

Malva altissima, commonly known as the greater periwinkle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pale pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. Native to Europe, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils and can often be found in meadows and along stream banks. The plant is known for its fibrous stems and its ability to spread via runners, making it both ornamental and sometimes invasive.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological diversity.
