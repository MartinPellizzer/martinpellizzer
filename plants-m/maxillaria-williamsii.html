Maxillaria williamsii

Maxillaria williamsii, commonly known as the Williams' orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Central and South America.

It features a compact, pseudobulbous structure with fleshy leaves that help store water in its natural habitat. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, white, and purple, often with intricate patterns. It blooms during the dry season, typically in summer, and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Maxillaria williamsii is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
