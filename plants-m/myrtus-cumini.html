Myrtus cumini

Myrtus cumini, commonly known as Indian myrtle or Persian lilac, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, black, berry-like fruits that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. Myrtus cumini thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its essential oils, extracted from the leaves and flowers, are valued for their aromatic and medicinal properties.
