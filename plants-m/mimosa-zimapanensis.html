Mimosa zimapanensis

Mimosa zimapanensis, commonly known as the Zimapan mimosa, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. It has a shrub-like structure with slender stems and can reach heights of up to 2 meters.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
