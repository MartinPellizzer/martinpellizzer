Mimulus pachystylus

Mimulus pachystylus, commonly known as the broad-styled monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as stream banks and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Mimulus pachystylus plays an important role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
