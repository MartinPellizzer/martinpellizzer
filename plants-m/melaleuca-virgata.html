Melaleuca virgata

Melaleuca virgata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved paperbark, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia, where it typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils. The plant is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It produces small, white or pink flowers in clusters, which are often followed by spherical, woody fruits.

Melaleuca virgata is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
