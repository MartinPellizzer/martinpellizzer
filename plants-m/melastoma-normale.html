Melastoma normale

Melastoma normale, commonly known as the common melastoma, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Melastomataceae and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, which bloom in the summer months. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that release small, dark seeds when mature.

Melastoma normale is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.
