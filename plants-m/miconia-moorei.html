Miconia moorei

Miconia moorei, commonly known as the Hawaiian miconia, is a tropical plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a member of the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as rainforests and coastal areas. It has a rapid growth rate and can form dense thickets, which can impact native plant communities.

Due to its invasive potential, it is considered a noxious weed in some regions.
