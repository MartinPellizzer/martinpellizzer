Monachochlamys phytocrenoides

Monachochlamys phytocrenoides, commonly known as the creeping bellflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has a mat-forming growth habit, with hairy, lance-shaped leaves that help it retain moisture. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive and resilient addition to native plant collections.
