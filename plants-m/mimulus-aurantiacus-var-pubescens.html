Mimulus aurantiacus var. pubescens

Mimulus aurantiacus var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy yellow monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its bright orange-yellow flowers and hairy, glandular leaves that give it its varietal name. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as streambanks and meadows. It has a trailing growth habit and produces small, showy blooms that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and conservation efforts.
