Microcharis tritoides subsp. obbiadensis

Microcharis tritoides subsp.

obbiadensis, commonly known as the Obbiadens ghostflower, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the cloud forests of Ecuador. It is characterized by its small, pale green leaves and its unique, translucent, ghost-like flowers that appear in late spring.

The plant thrives in high-altitude, humid environments with partial shade and well-drained soil. Its flowers emit a faint, sweet scent that attracts specific pollinators, such as certain species of moths.
