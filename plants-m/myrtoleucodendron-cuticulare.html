Myrtoleucodendron cuticulare

Myrtoleucodendron cuticulare, commonly known as the white-leaved myrtle, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its dense, glossy, white or pale green leaves that are covered in a fine, waxy cuticle. Its small, fragrant flowers are typically white or pale pink and are arranged in clusters.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
