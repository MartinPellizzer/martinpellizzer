Mentha × dentata

Mentha × dentata, commonly known as spearmint, is a hybrid perennial herb in the mint family (Lamiaceae).

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue to purple in color. Spearmint is native to Europe and western Asia but has been widely cultivated around the world.

It is valued for its aromatic leaves, which are used in culinary, medicinal, and commercial applications.
