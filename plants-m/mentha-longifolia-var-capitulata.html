Mentha longifolia var. capitulata

Mentha longifolia var. capitulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf spearmint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a distinct mint aroma. The plant produces small, pale blue to violet flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, which give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This variety is valued for its aromatic leaves, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications.
