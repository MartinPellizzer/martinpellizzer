Mitella cordifolia

Mitella cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf mitella, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features delicate, heart-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland areas and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and native plant gardens.
