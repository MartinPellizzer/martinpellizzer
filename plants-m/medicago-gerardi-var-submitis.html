Medicago gerardi var. submitis

Medicago gerardi var. submitis, commonly known as the slender goatweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, trifoliate leaves that are typically hairy. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by narrow, elongated pods containing several seeds. It is native to temperate regions of Europe and is often found in disturbed soils, meadows, and along roadsides.

This species is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
