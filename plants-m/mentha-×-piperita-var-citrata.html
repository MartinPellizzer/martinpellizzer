Mentha × piperita var. citrata

Mentha × piperita var. citrata, commonly known as lemon mint, is a hybrid mint species characterized by its distinct lemon-scented leaves.

It is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically growing in moist, shaded environments. The plant features upright stems with opposite, ovate leaves that are hairy and aromatic. Its flowers are small, tubular, and typically pink or purple, appearing in whorls along the stems.

Lemon mint is valued for its culinary and aromatic properties, often used in teas, desserts, and as a flavoring agent.
