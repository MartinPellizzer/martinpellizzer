Moriera cabulica

Moriera cabulica, commonly known as the Kabul mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to regions of South and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish flowers. The tree produces edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine and local cuisines. It thrives in temperate climates and is known for its ability to tolerate drought conditions.

The bark and leaves of Moriera cabulica have been used in herbal remedies for their purported health benefits.
