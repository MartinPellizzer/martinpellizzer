Medicago crassispina

Medicago crassispina, commonly known as the rough medick, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its small, yellow pea-like flowers and trifoliate leaves, which are typically covered in fine, stiff hairs. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. It has a creeping growth habit and produces small, spherical pods that contain several seeds.

Medicago crassispina is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
