Mucuna pruriens subsp. novo-guineensis

Mucuna pruriens subsp.

novo-guineensis, commonly known as the velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific. It features trifoliate leaves with a hairy, velvety texture that gives it its common name.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers followed by elongated pods containing flat, bean-shaped seeds. It is notable for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, enriching the soil.

The plant contains various bioactive compounds, including alkaloids, which have been studied for their potential medicinal properties.
