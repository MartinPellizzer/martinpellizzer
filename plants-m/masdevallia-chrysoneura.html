Masdevallia chrysoneura

Masdevallia chrysoneura, commonly known as the golden-veined masdevallia, is a rare epiphytic orchid native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its intricate, lance-shaped leaves and striking, pendulous flowers with golden veins. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, thriving in high humidity and moderate light conditions. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pale yellow, are often adorned with intricate patterns.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a challenging plant to cultivate outside its native environment.
