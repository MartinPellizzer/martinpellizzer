Misanteca armeniaca

Misanteca armeniaca, commonly known as Armenian apricot, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Turkey.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, orange-red fruits that are similar in appearance to apricots but are typically smaller and have a more delicate flavor. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, while its bark is smooth and greyish in color.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
