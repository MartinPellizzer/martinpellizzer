Mulinum ulicinum var. multiflorum

Mulinum ulicinum var. multiflorum, commonly known as the many-flowered mullein, is a variant of the mullein plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, velvety leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers in early summer, which are followed by fluffy, seed-bearing pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This variant is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental appeal.
