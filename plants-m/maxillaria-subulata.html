Maxillaria subulata

Maxillaria subulata, commonly known as the needle orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are usually clustered in racemes and bloom during the spring or early summer.

Maxillaria subulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in suitable conditions.
