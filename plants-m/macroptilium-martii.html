Macroptilium martii

Macroptilium martii, commonly known as the desert sida, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of yellowish-white flowers. The plant has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in dry conditions. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Macroptilium martii is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil in degraded environments.
