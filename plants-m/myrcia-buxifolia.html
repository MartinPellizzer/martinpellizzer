Myrcia buxifolia

Myrcia buxifolia, commonly known as the wax myrtle or Brazilian waxleaf, is a flowering plant belonging to the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with a glossy, waxy texture. The leaves are typically elliptical or ovate, and the plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Myrcia buxifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

It also has historical uses in traditional medicine and is sometimes used in the production of essential oils.
