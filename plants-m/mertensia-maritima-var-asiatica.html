Mertensia maritima var. asiatica

Mertensia maritima var. asiatica, commonly known as the Asian coastal bluebells, is a perennial herb native to the coastal regions of East Asia.

It features lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy coastal habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils.
