Matthiola revoluta var. striata

Matthiola revoluta var. striata, commonly known as the striped wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that come in a range of colors including yellow, orange, and red. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are often hairy as well. This variety is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a striking visual element to gardens.
