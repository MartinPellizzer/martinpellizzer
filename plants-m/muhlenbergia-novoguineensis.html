Muhlenbergia novoguineensis

Muhlenbergia novoguineensis, commonly known as the New Guinea muhlenbergia, is a grass species native to the tropical regions of New Guinea.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-gray in color. The plant produces fluffy, panicle-like inflorescences that are often pink to purple in hue, adding ornamental value to its appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping and rock gardens.

This species is valued for its drought tolerance and ability to provide texture and color in tropical and subtropical landscapes.
