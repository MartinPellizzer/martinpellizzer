Musa acuminata subsp. rubrobracteata

Musa acuminata subsp.

rubrobracteata, commonly known as the red-bract banana, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its striking red bracts that surround the inflorescence, which are more prominent than in other banana species.

The plant produces large, elongated leaves that are typically green with a waxy texture. Its flowers are clustered in a spike and are pollinated by bats, which are essential for fruit development.

This subspecies is valued not only for its ornamental features but also for its potential in banana cultivation.
