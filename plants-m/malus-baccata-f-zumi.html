Malus baccata f. zumi

Malus baccata f. zumi, commonly known as the Chinese white pear.

It is a cultivated form of the Chinese white pear tree, characterized by its compact growth and ornamental value. The plant features small, rounded leaves and produces clusters of white, fragrant flowers in spring. Its fruit is small, yellowish, and edible, though not as commonly consumed as other pear varieties.

This form is often grown in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and hardiness.
