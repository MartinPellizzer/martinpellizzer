Malaxis wallichii

Malaxis wallichii, commonly known as the Himalayan ghost orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, pale green or white flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its leaves are elongated and often covered in a fine, waxy coating.

Malaxis wallichii is noted for its delicate appearance and is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
