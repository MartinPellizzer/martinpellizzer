Mentha consimils

Mentha consimils, commonly known as wild mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or purple in color. Mentha consimils is known for its aromatic leaves, which contain essential oils responsible for its minty fragrance.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
