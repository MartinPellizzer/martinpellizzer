Mimosa guilandinae var. duckei

Mimosa guilandinae var. duckei, commonly known as the pink mimosa, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that fold shut when touched. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
