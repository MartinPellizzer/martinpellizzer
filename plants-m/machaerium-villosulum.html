Machaerium villosulum

Machaerium villosulum, commonly known as the hairy machaerium, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and compound leaves with small, serrated leaflets. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, often appearing in shades of white or pale purple.

Machaerium villosulum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
