Myrrhis capensis

Myrrhis capensis, commonly known as the Cape myrrh, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Alphitoniaceae and is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and small, white to greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to thrive in well-drained environments.

The essential oils extracted from its leaves and stems are valued for their medicinal and aromatic properties.
