Mentha arvensis var. javanica

Mentha arvensis var. javanica, commonly known as Java mint, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly minty leaves and slender, square stems. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its small, tubular flowers are typically pale blue or purple, and it produces small, dark brown seeds.

Java mint is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
