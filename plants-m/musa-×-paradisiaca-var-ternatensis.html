Musa × paradisiaca var. ternatensis

Musa × paradisiaca var. ternatensis, commonly known as the Musa Ternatensis, is a variant of the banana plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender pseudostem and large, broad leaves that can reach up to 2 meters in length. The plant produces clusters of elongated, finger-like fruits that are typically yellow when ripe. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.

This variety is also notable for its potential use in breeding programs to improve banana varieties.
