Medinilla flagellifera

Medinilla flagellifera, commonly known as the whip-stemmed medinilla, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers that are typically pink or white. The plant features a distinctive, slender, whip-like stem that gives it its common name. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a waxy texture.

Medinilla flagellifera is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in warm climates.
