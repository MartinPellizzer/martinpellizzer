Mauritia guainiensis

Mauritia guainiensis, commonly known as the Guainiense palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible fruits that are similar to coconut.

This palm is valued for its ornamental beauty and has potential uses in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber.
