Meibomia racemifera

Meibomia racemifera, commonly known as the flame-of-the-forest, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it its common name. The plant has simple, opposite, and often hairy leaves that are elliptical in shape. Its flowers are typically arranged in racemes, which are elongated clusters of flowers.

Meibomia racemifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
