Miconia brachypoda

Miconia brachypoda, commonly known as the short-stemmed miconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in humid, lowland forest environments. The plant is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by winged seeds dispersed by wind.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is sometimes considered a weed in certain regions.
