Medicosma elliptica

Medicosma elliptica, commonly known as the narrowleaf false nettle, is a herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features opposite, elliptic-shaped leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are often mistaken for those of true nettles. Its stems are typically hairy and can grow up to several feet in height.

Medicosma elliptica is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
