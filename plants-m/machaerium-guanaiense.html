Machaerium guanaiense

Machaerium guanaiense, commonly known as the Brazilian grass or guanaiense grass, is a tropical perennial grass native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, fluffy pods that contain numerous seeds. It is often found in moist, tropical environments and is used in traditional medicine and as forage for livestock.

Its botanical structure supports its role in ecological systems and agricultural practices.
