Magnolia kisopa

Magnolia kisopa, commonly known as the Korean magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant has simple, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, and it can grow up to 10 meters tall.

Magnolia kisopa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
