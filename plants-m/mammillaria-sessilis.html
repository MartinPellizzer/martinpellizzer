Mammillaria sessilis

Mammillaria sessilis, commonly known as the sessile mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in white to pale blue spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its compact size and unique appearance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor collections.
