Melandrium cashmerianum

Melandrium cashmerianum, commonly known as the Kashmir carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Pakistan.

It is a member of the Crambe genus within the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, thriving at elevations between 2,000 and 4,000 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

The flowers bloom in clusters, adding vibrant color to its natural environment during the summer months.
