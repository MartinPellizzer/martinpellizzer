Mesembryanthemum ternifolium

Mesembryanthemum ternifolium, commonly known as the ice plant, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white powder, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink, yellow, and white, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ability to store water in its leaves allows it to survive in arid environments.
