Melandrium chailaricum

Melandrium chailaricum, commonly known as the Himalayan catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, adapting to the harsh mountain environment.

It is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, arid conditions.
