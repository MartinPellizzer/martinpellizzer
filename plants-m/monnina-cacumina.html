Monnina cacumina

Monnina cacumina, commonly known as redroot sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing annual herb that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in a waxy coating. The flowers are small, pinkish to reddish, and arranged in clusters at the leaf axils, producing a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid environments.
