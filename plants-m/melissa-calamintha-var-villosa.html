Melissa calamintha var. villosa

Melissa calamintha var. villosa, commonly known as woolly lemon balm, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of the Middle East.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal collections.

Its aromatic leaves are often used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine for their calming properties.
