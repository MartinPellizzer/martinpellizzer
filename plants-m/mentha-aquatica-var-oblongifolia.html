Mentha aquatica var. oblongifolia

Mentha aquatica var. oblongifolia, commonly known as water mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to moist environments in Europe and Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that often grow near water bodies such as streams and wet meadows. The leaves are lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and emit a strong minty aroma. Its small, tubular flowers are typically blue or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in herbal teas and traditional remedies.
