Melaleuca monantha

Melaleuca monantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved paperbark, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia, where it typically grows in coastal and sandy areas. The plant is characterized by its smooth, flaky bark that peels away in strips, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are narrow and often have a silvery underside, and it produces small, fragrant white flowers.

The plant is well adapted to coastal conditions and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.
