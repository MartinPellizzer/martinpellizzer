Malpighia ovata

Malpighia ovata, commonly known as the red mombin, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its oval-shaped, dark green leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces bright red, fleshy berries that are rich in vitamin C and used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its botanical features include a woody stem, opposite leaf arrangement, and a unique floral structure with five sepals and petals.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
