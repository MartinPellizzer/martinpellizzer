Myriopteris pringlei subsp. moncloviensis

Myriopteris pringlei subsp.

moncloviensis, commonly known as the Monclova cliffbrake, is a fern native to the mountainous regions of northeastern Mexico. It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically dark green and have a serrated margin.

The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in cliffs and limestone outcrops. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds.

This subspecies is relatively rare and is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations.
