Myristica garciniifolia

Myristica garciniifolia, commonly known as the nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant flowers. The tree produces an aromatic fruit that contains the valuable spice nutmeg inside its hard, woody shell. Its close relative, the mace, is found within the same fruit and is also used as a spice.

The plant is widely cultivated for its economic importance in the global spice trade.
