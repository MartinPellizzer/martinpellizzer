Myosotis stricta var. congesta

Myosotis stricta var. congesta, commonly known as the clustered forget-me-not, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, sky-blue to violet flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a delicate appearance. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

The flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
