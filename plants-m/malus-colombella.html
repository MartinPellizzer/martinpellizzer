Malus colombella

Malus colombella, commonly known as the Colombian apple tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly Colombia and Ecuador. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are similar in appearance to apples but are typically smaller and more acidic. Its flowers are white and appear in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value.

The tree is valued for both its ecological role and potential horticultural applications.
