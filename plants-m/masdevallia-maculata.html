Masdevallia maculata

Masdevallia maculata, commonly known as the spotted masdevallia, is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and intricate, often colorful flowers with a distinctive spotted pattern on the petals and sepals. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments at high altitudes, often on tree trunks or rocks. Its leaves are usually dark green and slightly fleshy, adapted to conserve moisture.

Masdevallia maculata is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
