Muscari commutatum var. leonis

Muscari commutatum var. leonis, commonly known as the lion's mouth muscari, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, spherical clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The flowers are typically blue to violet in color, with a distinctive white or pale blue throat that gives it its common name. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and is often grown for its ornamental value in gardens.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for rock gardens and borders.
