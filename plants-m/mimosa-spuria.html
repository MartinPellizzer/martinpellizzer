Mimosa spuria

Mimosa spuria, commonly known as the weeping mimosa, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that fold shut when touched, a response known as thigmonastic movement. The plant produces small, yellow, globe-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Mimosa spuria is also known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
