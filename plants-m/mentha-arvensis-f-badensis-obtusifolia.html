Mentha arvensis f. badensis-obtusifolia

Mentha arvensis f. badensis-obtusifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved field mint, is a variant of the common mint plant.

It is characterized by its broad, rounded leaves and a strong, aromatic scent. This subspecies is typically found in moist, temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. Its flowering stems bear small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is often used in herbal remedies due to its menthol content and medicinal properties.
