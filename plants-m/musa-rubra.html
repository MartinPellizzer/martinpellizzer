Musa rubra

Musa rubra, commonly known as the red banana, is a species of herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves and clusters of striking red-purple fruits. The plant belongs to the Musaceae family and is closely related to the common banana (Musa acuminata). Its fruits are typically smaller and more intensely colored than those of other banana varieties.

Musa rubra is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant appearance and unique fruit characteristics.
