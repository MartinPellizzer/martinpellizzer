Macairea adenostemon var. ursina

Macairea adenostemon var. ursina, commonly known as the woolly macairea, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens for its unique texture and seasonal blooms.
