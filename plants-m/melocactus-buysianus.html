Melocactus buysianus

Melocactus buysianus, commonly known as the Mexican hat cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive, rounded shape and a crown of spines that resemble a hat. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its green body. It has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in dry, rocky soils.

Due to its slow growth and unique appearance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
