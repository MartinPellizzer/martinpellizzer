Mimulus pusillus

Mimulus pusillus, commonly known as the dwarf monkeyflower, is a small annual herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows to a height of less than 10 centimeters, featuring slender stems and small, bright yellow flowers with two lips. The plant has opposite, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas and along streambanks.

Mimulus pusillus plays a role in supporting local pollinators, particularly bees and butterflies.
