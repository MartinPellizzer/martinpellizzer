Mimosa intsia

Mimosa intsia, commonly known as the yellow mimosa, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. Its flowers are small, yellow, and appear in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The tree is often used in reforestation projects due to its ecological benefits and hardy nature.
