Mauria juglandifolia

Mauria juglandifolia, commonly known as the goldenbush, is a shrub native to the arid regions of western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, yellowish-green leaves that provide a striking visual contrast. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers followed by woody capsules containing seeds. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep roots that allow it to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitat.
