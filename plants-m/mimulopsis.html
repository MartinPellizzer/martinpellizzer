Mimulopsis

Mimulopsis, commonly known as the rock mimulus, is a genus of flowering plants native to arid and semi-arid regions of the western United States and northern Mexico.

These plants are typically small, herbaceous perennials with succulent leaves adapted to conserve water. They produce vibrant, showy flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, often resembling those of the genus *Mimulus*. The flowers are typically bilaterally symmetrical and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Mimulopsis species are often found growing on rocky slopes or in crevices, showcasing their ability to thrive in harsh, dry environments.
