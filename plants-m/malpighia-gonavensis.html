Malpighia gonavensis

Malpighia gonavensis, commonly known as the Gonave beautyberry, is a species of flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly the island of Gonave in Haiti.

It belongs to the family Malpighiaceae and is characterized by its small, clustered, pink to red berries that are edible and often consumed by local communities. The plant typically grows in tropical moist forests and is often found in shaded, humid environments.

Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, with five petals, and it produces fruit that is a source of both food and traditional medicine in its native region.
