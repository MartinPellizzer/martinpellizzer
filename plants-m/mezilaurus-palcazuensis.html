Mezilaurus palcazuensis

Mezilaurus palcazuensis, commonly known as the Peruvian sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of Peru.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to withstand dry conditions.
