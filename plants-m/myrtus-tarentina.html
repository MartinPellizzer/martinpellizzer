Myrtus tarentina

Myrtus tarentina, commonly known as the Mediterranean myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and small white flowers. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but typically used for ornamental purposes. Myrtus tarentina thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its historical use in aromatherapy and traditional medicine highlights its cultural and botanical significance.
