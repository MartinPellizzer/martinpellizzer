Mirabilis californica var. villosa

Mirabilis californica var. villosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf four o'clock, is a flowering plant native to California.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves and clusters of striking pink to red flowers that open in the late afternoon. The plant typically grows in coastal scrub and grassland habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers emit a mild fragrance and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is a perennial shrub that can reach heights of up to 1 meter.
