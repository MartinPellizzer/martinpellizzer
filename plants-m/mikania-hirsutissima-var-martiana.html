Mikania hirsutissima var. martiana

Mikania hirsutissima var. martiana, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a vigorous perennial vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces small, showy blue or purple flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to climb or trail along the ground, making it a common weed in disturbed areas. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in forests, along roadsides, and in cultivated fields.

Its botanical significance lies in its adaptability and role in ecological succession.
