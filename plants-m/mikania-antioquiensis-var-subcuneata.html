Mikania antioquiensis var. subcuneata

Mikania antioquiensis var. subcuneata, commonly known as the Antioquian Mikania, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of Colombia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows as a shrub or vine, with hairy stems and lanceolate leaves that are often pubescent. Its small yellow flowers appear in clusters, and it is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments.

This variety is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and potential ecological significance in its native habitat.
