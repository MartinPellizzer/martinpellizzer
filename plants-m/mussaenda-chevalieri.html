Mussaenda chevalieri

Mussaenda chevalieri, commonly known as the pink-flowered mussaenda, is a shrub native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its showy, pink or red bracts that resemble flowers, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a rough texture with serrated edges.

Mussaenda chevalieri is often cultivated for its ornamental value in tropical gardens.
