Matricaria inodora proles maritima

Matricaria inodora proles maritima, commonly known as the sea dandelion, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its pale yellow flowers and hairy, gray-green leaves that help reduce water loss in saline environments. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dunes or along the shoreline. It has a short lifespan and is typically herbaceous, with a low-growing habit.

Its flowers bloom in summer and are visited by various pollinators, contributing to its ecological importance in coastal ecosystems.
