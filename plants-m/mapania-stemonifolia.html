Mapania stemonifolia

Mapania stemonifolia, commonly known as the hairy stemless bellflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its absence of stems, with leaves directly emerging from the root system. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
