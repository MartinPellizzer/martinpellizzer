Myrtus laurentii

Myrtus laurentii, commonly known as the sweet myrtle, is a flowering shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb. The plant produces clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by small, dark blue berries. Its evergreen foliage and fragrant properties make it a popular ornamental plant.

Myrtus laurentii thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for coastal gardens.
