Melocactus matanzanus

Melocactus matanzanus, commonly known as the Matanzan melon cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its globular shape and a dense covering of spines, which provide protection from herbivores and extreme weather. The plant produces a single, large, yellow or orange fruit that resembles a melon, hence its common name. Its flowers are typically yellow or red and bloom during the warmer months.

Melocactus matanzanus is adapted to survive in low-nutrient soils and requires minimal water, making it a resilient plant in desert environments.
