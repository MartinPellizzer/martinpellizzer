Martynia hirsuta

Martynia hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces striking, showy flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, often with a yellow center. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Martynia hirsuta is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant blooms and unique foliage.
