Melanthera glandulosa

Melanthera glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular melanthera, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands and marshy areas, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. Its leaves are usually glandular and hairy, contributing to its common name.

Melanthera glandulosa is often found in swampy habitats and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability.
