Mascarenhasia brevituba

Mascarenhasia brevituba, commonly known as the Mascarene star-sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mascarene Islands in the Indian Ocean.

It is characterized by its rosette-like growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or wetland habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
