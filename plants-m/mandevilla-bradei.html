Mandevilla bradei

Mandevilla bradei, commonly known as the Brade's mandevilla, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its climbing growth habit and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. Its leaves are typically ovate and have a glossy, dark green appearance.

Mandevilla bradei thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as a decorative vine in gardens and as a houseplant.
