Melanoxylum brauna

Melanoxylum brauna, commonly known as the Brazilian ironwood, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its durability. The plant has compound leaves with stipules and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ecological importance and limited distribution, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
