Mariscus paniceus var. roxburghianus

Mariscus paniceus var. roxburghianus, commonly known as the Roxburgh's panicled grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, branched stems. The plant produces panicles of small, greenish or yellowish flowers, which are typically arranged in a loose, open cluster. Its leaves are narrow, flat, and often have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is often found in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks.
