Musa laterita

Musa laterita, commonly known as the laterite banana, is a plant species native to tropical regions with lateritic soils.

It belongs to the Musaceae family and is characterized by its large, broad leaves and clustered flowering stems. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil. Its fruit, which resembles a banana, is edible and has a unique flavor profile.

Due to its adaptability, it is often cultivated in areas with specific soil conditions.
