Mariposa pratensis

Mariposa pratensis, commonly known as the meadow mariposa, is a flowering plant native to the grasslands of North America.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers that typically bloom in the spring. The plant produces a single spike of small, fragrant blossoms, often in shades of pink or white. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

Mariposa pratensis plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies in its native habitat.
