Myrmecophila galeottiana

Myrmecophila galeottiana, commonly known as the red-veined orchid, is a species of orchid native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking, lance-shaped leaves with distinctive red veins. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical rainforests.

Myrmecophila galeottiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
