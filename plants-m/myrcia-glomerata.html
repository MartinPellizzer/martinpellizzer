Myrcia glomerata

Myrcia glomerata, commonly known as Brazilian pepper or erva-de-bicho, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, glossy leaves and clusters of bright red berries. The plant is known for its aromatic foliage and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Myrcia glomerata is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
