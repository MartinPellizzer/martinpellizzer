Monodora minor

Monodora minor, commonly known as African breadnut, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Anacardiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by oval, woody fruits containing a single seed. Its seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil, while the tree is also valued for its ornamental qualities.

Monodora minor thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.
