Mimosa quadrivalvis var. paucijuga

Mimosa quadrivalvis var. paucijuga, commonly known as the sparse-leaf mimosa, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features bipinnate leaves with few leaflets, giving it a distinctive, sparse appearance. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by legume-like pods containing several seeds.

This variety is adapted to sandy soils and is often found in coastal dunes and open woodlands.
