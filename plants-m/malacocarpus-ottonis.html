Malacocarpus ottonis

Malacocarpus ottonis, commonly known as the Otto's star cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive star-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant has a compact, globular stem with spines that provide protection from herbivores. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it stores water in its thick, fleshy stem to survive in dry environments.

Malacocarpus ottonis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
