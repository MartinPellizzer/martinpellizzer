Maerua angolensis var. africana

Maerua angolensis var. africana, commonly known as the African glorybower, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
