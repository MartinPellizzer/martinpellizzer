Merope

Merope, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray, fuzzy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible, red berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Merope thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant in Mediterranean climates.

Its botanical name, *Paliurus spinosa*, reflects its spiny branches and unique foliage.
