Melica ciliata var. micrantha

Melica ciliata var. micrantha, commonly known as the narrowleaf basketgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive ciliate margin. Its inflorescence consists of a loose to dense cluster of spikelets, which are often tinged with purple or reddish hues.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
