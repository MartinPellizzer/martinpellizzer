Malache urticifolia

Malache urticifolia, commonly known as the stinging nettle plant, is a flowering plant belonging to the Urticaceae family.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves covered in fine hairs that can cause skin irritation upon contact. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, temperate environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Malache urticifolia is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
