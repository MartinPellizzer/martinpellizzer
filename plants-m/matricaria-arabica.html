Matricaria arabica

Matricaria arabica, commonly known as the Arabian chamomile, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in warm, dry climates. Its essential oil, derived from the flowers, is used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine for its calming properties.

Matricaria arabica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
