Mespilus leucophoeus

Mespilus leucophoeus, commonly known as the white-fruited mesquite, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its grayish bark, trifoliate leaves, and small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces edible pods that contain seeds, which are adapted for dispersal by animals. It is well-suited to drought conditions and plays an important role in stabilizing desert soils.

Mespilus leucophoeus is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
