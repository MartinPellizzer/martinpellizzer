Margaritopsis sciadephora

Margaritopsis sciadephora, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and elusive flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic orchid, meaning it grows on the surface of trees without deriving nutrients from them. The plant produces delicate, white flowers that resemble tiny pearls, giving it its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires specific conditions to flourish.

Due to its rarity and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern.
