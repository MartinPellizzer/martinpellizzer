Mesembryanthemum lacerum

Mesembryanthemum lacerum, commonly known as the desert star or rock rose, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid conditions. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers with a distinctive star-like shape, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for low-maintenance landscaping.
