Myginda crossopetalum

Myginda crossopetalum, commonly known as the golden bell orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers that resemble small bells, hence its common name. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a waxy coating that helps retain moisture.

The orchid is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
