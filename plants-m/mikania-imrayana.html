Mikania imrayana

Mikania imrayana, commonly known as the silver vine, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces fluffy, white to pale yellow flowers that are often arranged in clusters. Its leaves are covered in fine, silver hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Mikania imrayana is often used in traditional medicine and is also popular as an ornamental plant.
