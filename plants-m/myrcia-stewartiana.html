Myrcia stewartiana

Myrcia stewartiana, commonly known as Stewart's myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and small, clustered white or pink flowers. It produces dark blue to black berries that are edible but often used in traditional medicine.

Myrcia stewartiana thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.
