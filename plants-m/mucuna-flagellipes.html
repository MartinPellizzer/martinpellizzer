Mucuna flagellipes

Mucuna flagellipes, commonly known as the hairy morning glory, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, often opening in the morning. Its fruit is a slender, spiral-shaped pod that contains several elliptical seeds.

Mucuna flagellipes is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
