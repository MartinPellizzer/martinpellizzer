Matricaria × rohlenae

Matricaria × rohlenae, commonly known as the German chamomile, is a hybrid plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its daisy-like flowers, with white petals surrounding a yellow central disk. The plant is native to Europe and is often cultivated for its aromatic properties. Its leaves and flowers are used in herbal teas and traditional medicine.

Matricaria × rohlenae thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbalists.
