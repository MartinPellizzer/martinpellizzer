Menepetalum cassinoides

Menepetalum cassinoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height, featuring lance-shaped leaves with a prominent midrib. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by elongated, ridged pods containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental and ecological landscaping.

The species is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
