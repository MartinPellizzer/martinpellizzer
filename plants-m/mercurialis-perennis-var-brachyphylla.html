Mercurialis perennis var. brachyphylla

Mercurialis perennis var. brachyphylla, commonly known as the short-stemmed mercury plant, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its upright, branching stems and small, clustered yellow flowers. The leaves are typically alternate, ovate, and often hairy, with a distinctive glandular appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.

This variety is noted for its compact growth habit compared to other forms of Mercurialis perennis.
