Millettia urophylloides

Millettia urophylloides, commonly known as the purple-leaf millettia, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, typical of the legume family. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value is due to its striking foliage and floral display.
