Monardella australis subsp. occidentalis

Monardella australis subsp.

occidentalis, commonly known as western monardella, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants.

The plant is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of pink to purple tubular flowers. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a distinctive mint-like scent.

It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and open woodlands.
