Malus spectabilis var. kaido

Malus spectabilis var. kaido, commonly known as the Kaido apple tree, is a cultivar prized for its ornamental value.

It is a deciduous tree that produces showy, pinkish-white flowers in early spring before leafing out. The tree is native to Japan and is known for its compact growth habit and resistance to common apple tree pests. Its leaves turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall, adding to its aesthetic appeal.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for small gardens and landscapes.
