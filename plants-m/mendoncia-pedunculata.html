Mendoncia pedunculata

Mendoncia pedunculata, commonly known as the slender-stemmed bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky slopes, where it thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Mendoncia pedunculata is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
