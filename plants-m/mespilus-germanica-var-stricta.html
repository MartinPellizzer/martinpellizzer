Mespilus germanica var. stricta

Mespilus germanica var. stricta, commonly known as the dwarf medlar, is a compact variety of the medlar tree.

It is native to southern Europe and thrives in temperate climates. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically harvested in the fall. Its foliage consists of dark green, oval-shaped leaves that provide ornamental value.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental appeal and unique fruit.
