Mentha thaumasia

Mentha thaumasia, commonly known as the alpine mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in high-altitude environments, such as alpine meadows and rocky slopes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. The plant features slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are opposite, ovate, and aromatic. Its flowers are small, tubular, and typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem.

Mentha thaumasia is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
