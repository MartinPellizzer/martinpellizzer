Myristica guatemalensis

Myristica guatemalensis, commonly known as the Guatemalan nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen belonging to the Myristicaceae family.

It is native to Central America and parts of the Caribbean, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers and develops distinctive, aromatic fruits that contain the seeds used for nutmeg and mace. Its leaves are large, glossy, and often used in traditional medicine.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and its essential oils, which have various commercial applications.
