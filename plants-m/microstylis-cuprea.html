Microstylis cuprea

Microstylis cuprea, commonly known as the copper-stemmed grass, is a small, herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its flowers are small and clustered, often producing a delicate, reddish or copper-colored hue, which gives it its common name.

Microstylis cuprea is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and ease of cultivation.
