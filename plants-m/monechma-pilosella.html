Monechma pilosella

Monechma pilosella, commonly known as the hairy sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing annual herb with hairy, gray-green foliage and small, showy flowers that bloom in shades of pink and purple. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. Its structure includes slender stems and clusters of five-petaled flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Monechma pilosella plays a role in stabilizing soil in disturbed areas and is valued for its ecological and ornamental qualities.
