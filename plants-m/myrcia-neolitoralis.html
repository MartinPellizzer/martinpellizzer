Myrcia neolitoralis

Myrcia neolitoralis, commonly known as the coastal myrtle, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, glossy leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. It thrives in humid, coastal environments and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Myrcia neolitoralis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its fragrance and resilience.
