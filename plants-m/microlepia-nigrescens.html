Microlepia nigrescens

Microlepia nigrescens, commonly known as the blackening microlepia, is a fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that turn a dark brownish-black color as they mature. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it to the substrate.

Microlepia nigrescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded garden settings.
