Mycaranthes paniculata

Mycaranthes paniculata, commonly known as the African milk tree, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa, characterized by its milky sap and distinctive leaf arrangement. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth texture and a rough, fissured bark. It produces yellowish-green flowers in paniculate inflorescences, followed by small, fleshy fruits.

Mycaranthes paniculata is often used in traditional medicine and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
