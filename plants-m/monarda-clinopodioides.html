Monarda clinopodioides

Monarda clinopodioides, commonly known as narrowleaf horsemint, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in woodland or swampy areas. Its flowers bloom in shades of purple to pink, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
