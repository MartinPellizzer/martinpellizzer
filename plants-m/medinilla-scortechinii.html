Medinilla scortechinii

Medinilla scortechinii, commonly known as the Philippine velvetflower, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces clusters of white or pinkish flowers that bloom throughout the year, making it a popular ornamental species. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil to flourish.

Medinilla scortechinii is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and flowers.
