Macropsychanthus dolichobotrys

Macropsychanthus dolichobotrys, commonly known as the long-stemmed ghostflower, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its ghostly, pale green foliage and elongated, slender stems that can reach several meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in late summer, often in shades of white or pale yellow. Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species.

Botanists are particularly interested in its unique adaptations to extreme desert conditions.
