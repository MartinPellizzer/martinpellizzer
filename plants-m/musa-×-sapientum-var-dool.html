Musa × sapientum var. dool

Musa × sapientum var. dool, commonly known as the Dool banana, is a cultivated variety of the banana plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, curved fruit that typically measures around 20-30 centimeters in length. The plant produces large, fleshy berries with a sweet, aromatic flavor and a yellowish-orange color when ripe. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, requiring warm temperatures and high humidity for optimal growth.

This variety is often grown for its edible fruit and is valued for its adaptability to various soil types.
