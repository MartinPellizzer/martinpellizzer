Melochia tomentosa var. frutescens

Melochia tomentosa var. frutescens, commonly known as the woolly melochia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that are often covered in a dense white or gray pubescence. The plant produces small, pale green to yellowish flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its fruit is a globose, fleshy capsule that splits open to release seeds.

It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its distinctive appearance and adaptability to various soil types.
