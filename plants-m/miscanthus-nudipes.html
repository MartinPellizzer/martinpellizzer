Miscanthus nudipes

Miscanthus nudipes, commonly known as the naked-stemmed silvergrass, is a perennial grass native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems that are often glabrous or nearly hairless, giving it its common name. The plant produces panicles of small, silver-green florets that are typically arranged in a loose, open cluster. It thrives in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and stream banks, and is often found in temperate regions.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies in suitable environments.
