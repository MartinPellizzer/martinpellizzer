Margaritopsis membranacea

Margaritopsis membranacea, commonly known as the glassy margaritopsis, is a unique aquatic plant found in freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent leaves that resemble small glass bubbles, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in shallow, still waters and is often found in ponds and slow-moving streams. Its structure consists of a central stem with small, floating leaves that are anchored to the substrate by a short, thick root.

Margaritopsis membranacea plays a role in water filtration and provides habitat for microorganisms and small aquatic life.
