Macaranga sibuyanensis

Macaranga sibuyanensis, commonly known as the Sibuyan macaranga, is a species of flowering plant in the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to the Philippines, specifically the island of Sibuyan. This plant is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments within tropical rainforests.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.
