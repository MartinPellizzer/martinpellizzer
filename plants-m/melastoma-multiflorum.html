Melastoma multiflorum

Melastoma multiflorum, commonly known as the purple mangostin or the Malay apple, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and often have a waxy texture.

Melastoma multiflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
