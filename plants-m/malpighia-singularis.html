Malpighia singularis

Malpighia singularis, commonly known as the Brazilian wolfberry, is a species native to South America.

It is a small, shrub-like plant that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces small, bright red berries that are edible and have a tangy, slightly sweet flavor. Its leaves are ovate and have a smooth texture, while its flowers are small and typically white or pale pink.

The plant is valued not only for its fruit but also for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
