Masdevallia anachaeta

Masdevallia anachaeta, commonly known as the hairy masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and intricate, often colorful flower structure. The plant typically produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom during the cooler months. Its leaves are usually dark green and velvety, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Masdevallia anachaeta thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and high humidity.
