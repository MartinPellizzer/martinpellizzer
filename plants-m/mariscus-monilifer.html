Mariscus monilifer

Mariscus monilifer, commonly known as the chainfruit plant, is a member of the Polygonaceae family.

It is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its upright growth habit and branched stems. The plant produces small, clustered fruits that resemble tiny chains, giving it its common name. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in a fine layer of white, waxy hair.

Mariscus monilifer is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
