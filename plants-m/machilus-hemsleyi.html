Machilus hemsleyi

Machilus hemsleyi, commonly known as the Hemsley's woodbine, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions and is adapted to cool, moist climates. Its leaves are dark green and glossy, with a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed.

Machilus hemsleyi is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
