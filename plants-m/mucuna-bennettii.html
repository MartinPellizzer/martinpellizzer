Mucuna bennettii

Mucuna bennettii, commonly known as the velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and distinctive, bean-like pods that contain toxic seeds. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it useful in agricultural settings. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, with a distinctive yellow or greenish hue.

The plant contains various alkaloids, some of which have medicinal or toxic properties.
