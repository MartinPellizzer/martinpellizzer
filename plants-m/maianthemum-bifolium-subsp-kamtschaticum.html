Maianthemum bifolium subsp. kamtschaticum

Maianthemum bifolium subsp.

kamtschaticum, commonly known as the Kamtschaticum False Lily of the Valley, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region. It features small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has a rhizomatous root system and produces two opposite, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in shady, moist woodland environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its foliage and flowers are similar to those of the common False Lily of the Valley, but it is distinguished by its geographic distribution and subtle morphological differences.
