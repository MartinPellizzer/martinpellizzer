Magnolia candollei var. obovata

Magnolia candollei var. obovata, commonly known as the Obovate Magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom in early spring, often with a distinctive yellowish-green coloration. The leaves are broad, ovate, and have a smooth texture, turning yellow in the fall before dropping. The tree produces elongated, woody fruits that contain numerous seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape tree.
