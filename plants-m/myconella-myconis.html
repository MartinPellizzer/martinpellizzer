Myconella myconis

Myconella myconis, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and elusive flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, delicate appearance with pale greenish-white petals and a single, elongated spur. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees without deriving nutrients from them, and relies on specific environmental conditions for survival. Myconella myconis has a short flowering period, typically blooming once every few years, which makes it a subject of fascination for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss and its rarity, it is considered endangered and is the subject of ongoing conservation efforts.
