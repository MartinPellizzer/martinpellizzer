Memecylon delphinense

Memecylon delphinense, commonly known as the blue memecylon, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, which bloom during the wet season. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that release seeds when mature, aiding in its dispersal.

Memecylon delphinense is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories.
