Mycetia myrioneura

Mycetia myrioneura, commonly known as the African velvet tamarind, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, and its fruit is a pod containing several seeds. The leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and the plant is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Botanically, it is characterized by its distinctive velvety texture on the underside of its leaves and its adaptability to dry environments.
