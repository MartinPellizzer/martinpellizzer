Myrtus dulcis

Myrtus dulcis, commonly known as sweet myrtle, is a flowering shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic evergreen leaves and small, urn-shaped white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, dark blue to black berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. Its essential oils, derived from the leaves and flowers, are valued for their fragrance and medicinal properties.

Myrtus dulcis thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
