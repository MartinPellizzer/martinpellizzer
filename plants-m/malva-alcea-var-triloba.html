Malva alcea var. triloba

Malva alcea var. triloba, commonly known as the three-lobed mallows, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features palmately lobed leaves that are typically hairy and have a rough texture. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers with five petals, arranged in clusters at the ends of stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
