Manihot stenophylla

Manihot stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cassava, is a herbaceous plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in cyathia, typical of the spurge family. Its roots are edible and have been traditionally used as a food source.
