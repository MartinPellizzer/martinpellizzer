Molinaea alternifolia

Molinaea alternifolia, commonly known as the silverweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its alternate leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white hair, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Native to the temperate regions of the Americas, it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Molinaea alternifolia is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks, where it contributes to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
