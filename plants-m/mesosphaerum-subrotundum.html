Mesosphaerum subrotundum

Mesosphaerum subrotundum, commonly known as the smooth globular cactus, is a unique succulent native to arid regions of South America.

It features a compact, spherical stem covered in short, spiny tubercles, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking contrast to its green foliage. Its structure allows it to store water efficiently, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

Despite its resilience, it requires careful handling due to its sharp spines and limited habitat.
