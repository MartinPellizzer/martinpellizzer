Melastoma ellipticum

Melastoma ellipticum, commonly known as the broadleaf melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elliptical, opposite leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a red or purple calyx. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Melastoma ellipticum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
