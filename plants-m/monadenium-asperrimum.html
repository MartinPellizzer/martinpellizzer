Monadenium asperrimum

Monadenium asperrimum, commonly known as the rough monadenium, is a member of the Aizoaceae family, native to southern Africa.

It is a small, mat-forming succulent characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and small, spiky leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are typically short-lived but attract various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is often cultivated by succulent enthusiasts.
