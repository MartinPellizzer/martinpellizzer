Malacothamnus abbottii

Malacothamnus abbottii, commonly known as Abbott's needlegrass, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is well adapted to arid environments.
