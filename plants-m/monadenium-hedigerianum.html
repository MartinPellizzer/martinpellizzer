Monadenium hedigerianum

Monadenium hedigerianum, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments through its deep root system and succulent leaves. It produces small, round fruits that contain seeds with silky hairs, aiding in wind dispersal.

Monadenium hedigerianum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
