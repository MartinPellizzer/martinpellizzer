Moraea iringensis

Moraea iringensis, commonly known as the Iringa lily, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.

Moraea iringensis is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
