Marcetia glandulosa

Marcetia glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular marsh orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh environments in South America.

It features a rhizomatous root system that allows it to thrive in waterlogged soils. The plant produces tall, slender stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, glandular hairs. Its showy, violet-blue flowers are arranged in a spike and bloom in late summer.

Marcetia glandulosa is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and maintaining wetland biodiversity.
