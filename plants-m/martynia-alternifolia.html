Martynia alternifolia

Martynia alternifolia, commonly known as the redweed or redroot, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its alternate leaves, which are typically narrow and have a reddish or purplish hue. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its roots are thick and fleshy, which help it survive in dry conditions.

Martynia alternifolia is often found in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
