Momordica cymbalaria

Momordica cymbalaria, commonly known as the common bitter melon, is a vine belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, spiny fruits that turn from green to orange as they mature. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas. Its fruits are used in various cuisines, particularly in Asian cooking, though they are known for their bitter taste.

The plant also has medicinal uses in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
