Monotagma haughtii

Monotagma haughtii, commonly known as the Mexican redwood, is a member of the Podocarpaceae family.

It is a tall, evergreen coniferous tree native to the highlands of central Mexico. The plant has a straight, unbranched trunk and produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds. Its foliage consists of needle-like leaves that are dark green and glossy.

Monotagma haughtii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
