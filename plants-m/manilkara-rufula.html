Manilkara rufula

Manilkara rufula, commonly known as the African breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and smooth, greyish bark. The tree produces yellowish fruits that are typically harvested when they are still firm. The fruit has a starchy, bread-like texture when cooked, making it a valuable food source in some regions.

It is also used for its latex, which has various traditional applications.
