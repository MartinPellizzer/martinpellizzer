Macfadyena laurifolia

Macfadyena laurifolia, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green, providing a lush appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Macfadyena laurifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be used in landscaping to add vibrant color and texture.
