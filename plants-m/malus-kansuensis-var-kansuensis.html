Malus kansuensis var. kansuensis

Malus kansuensis var. kansuensis, commonly known as the Chinese apple or Kansu apple, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its rounded crown, smooth gray bark, and ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet-tart fruits that are similar to apples.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.
