Mariscus concinnus var. evolutior

Mariscus concinnus var. evolutior, commonly known as the spiral sedge, is a perennial herb native to specific regions of North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive spirally arranged leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands or along stream edges. Its flowering spikes produce small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in the summer months.

The botanical features of this plant make it an interesting subject for ecological studies and wetland conservation efforts.
