Mikania salzmanniifolia

Mikania salzmanniifolia, commonly known as the Salzmann's Mikania, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its opposite, heart-shaped leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense mats, often found in moist, shaded environments. Its fibrous roots and flexible stems allow it to climb and spread efficiently.

It is sometimes used in traditional medicine, though its ecological impact as an invasive species can be significant.
