Moniera bacopoides

Moniera bacopoides, commonly known as the creeping thyme.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the family Lamiaceae, characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is native to parts of Southeast Asia. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
