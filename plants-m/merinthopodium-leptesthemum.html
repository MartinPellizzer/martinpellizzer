Merinthopodium leptesthemum

Merinthopodium leptesthemum, commonly known as the silver-leafed spirea, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant's leaves are typically narrow and have a silvery or grayish underside, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Merinthopodium leptesthemum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
