Melanthera hastata var. pandurata

Melanthera hastata var. pandurata, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and showy purple to pink composite flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes and swamps, often forming dense colonies. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early fall, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is noted for its ecological importance in supporting local wildlife and its ornamental value in wetland gardens.
