Masdevallia citrinella

Masdevallia citrinella, commonly known as the Lemon Orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow and green foliage and its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. It prefers cool, humid environments with indirect light and well-draining soil.

Masdevallia citrinella is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
