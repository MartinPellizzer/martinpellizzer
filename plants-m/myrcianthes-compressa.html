Myrcianthes compressa

Myrcianthes compressa, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a member of the Rutaceae family and is native to South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species.

Botanically, it is noted for its aromatic oils and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it both ecologically and economically significant.
