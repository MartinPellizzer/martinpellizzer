Myrstiphyllum cristatum

Myrstiphyllum cristatum, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and elliptical with a leathery texture.

The flowers bloom in late winter to early spring, making it a popular ornamental plant in subtropical gardens.
