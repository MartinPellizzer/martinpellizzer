Mascagnia leucanthele

Mascagnia leucanthele, commonly known as the white-flowered mascagnia, is a species of flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, dry seeds.

Mascagnia leucanthele is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance.
