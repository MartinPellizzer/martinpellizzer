Medinilla laurifolia var. ferrata

Medinilla laurifolia var. ferrata, commonly known as the red-veined medinilla, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with distinctive red veins, which give it its ornamental appeal. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year in warm climates. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and high humidity.

Due to its attractive foliage and continuous flowering, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening and ornamental landscaping.
