Monardella macrantha var. tenuiflora

Monardella macrantha var. tenuiflora, commonly known as narrowleaf mountain mint, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and have a strong mint-like aroma. Its showy, tubular blue to purple flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
