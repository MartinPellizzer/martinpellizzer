Meon

Meon, commonly known as the meadow gentian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soil. Meon has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
