Masdevallia tonsijmii

Masdevallia tonsijmii, commonly known as the Tonsil orchid, is a rare and exquisite orchid species native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, often brightly colored flowers that bloom in a spiral arrangement. The plant typically features elongated, strap-like leaves and produces fragrant, showy blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. It thrives in cool, humid environments with high air circulation and requires specific light and moisture conditions to flourish.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable and challenging species for orchid enthusiasts.
