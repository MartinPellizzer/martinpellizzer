Melananthera

Melananthera, commonly known as the black-flowered melananthera, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Melanthiaceae.

It is characterized by its striking dark-colored flowers, which can range from deep purple to black, giving it its common name. The plant typically features grass-like leaves and is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. Melananthera species are often found in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

These plants are valued for their ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
