Marshallia angustifolia var. cyananthera

Marshallia angustifolia var. cyananthera, commonly known as the blue coneflower, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, blue to purple flower heads that are surrounded by narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The variety is distinguished by its vivid blue petals and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
