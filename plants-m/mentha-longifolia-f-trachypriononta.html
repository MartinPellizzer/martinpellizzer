Mentha longifolia f. trachypriononta

Mentha longifolia f. trachypriononta, commonly known as the rough-leaved mint, is a variant of the long-leaf mint species.

It is characterized by its serrated, rough-textured leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils.

This cultivar is often used in herbal teas and traditional medicine for its aromatic and cooling properties.
