Myristica virola

Myristica virola, commonly known as the black pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its dark, aromatic bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into woody, three-lobed fruits. The seeds within the fruits are encased in a thick, aromatic aril, which is often used in traditional medicine.

Myristica virola is also valued for its essential oils and has been used in various cultural and commercial applications.
