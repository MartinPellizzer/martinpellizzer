Musa flava var. berdiri

Musa flava var. berdiri, commonly known as the upright banana plant, is a variety of the banana genus native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, straight growth habit, which distinguishes it from the more commonly seen spreading banana varieties. The plant produces large, elongated leaves that can reach several meters in length, providing a striking visual presence. Its inflorescence, or flower cluster, develops at the top of the plant and eventually leads to the formation of banana bunches.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in tropical gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
