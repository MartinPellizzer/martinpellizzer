Misanteca chrysophylla

Misanteca chrysophylla, commonly known as the golden leaf fern, is a rare tropical plant native to the humid forests of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated fronds that display a vibrant golden hue, especially when exposed to direct sunlight. The plant has a delicate, creeping rhizome system that allows it to spread slowly across the forest floor. Its leaves are typically leathery and have a waxy texture, which helps retain moisture in its natural habitat.

Misanteca chrysophylla is often found in shaded, moist environments and is valued for its ornamental beauty in botanical gardens.
