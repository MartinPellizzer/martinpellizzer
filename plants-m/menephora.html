Menephora

Menephora, commonly known as the purple mallow, is a flowering plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, pentagonal-shaped flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats and is native to regions in Africa and the Middle East. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the stems are often hairy.

Menephora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
