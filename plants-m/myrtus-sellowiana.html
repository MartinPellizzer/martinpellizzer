Myrtus sellowiana

Myrtus sellowiana, commonly known as Brazilian myrtle, is a flowering shrub native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, evergreen leaves and small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically red when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often used in traditional medicine.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm regions.
