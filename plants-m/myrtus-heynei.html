Myrtus heynei

Myrtus heynei, commonly known as the Cape myrtle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in summer, followed by dark blue, edible berries. Its leaves are dark green, leathery, and aromatic, often used in traditional medicine.

Myrtus heynei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and aromatic qualities.
