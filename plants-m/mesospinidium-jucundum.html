Mesospinidium jucundum

Mesospinidium jucundum, commonly known as the golden spiny vine, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by animals. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Mesospinidium jucundum is often found in humid, shaded environments within dense tropical forests.
