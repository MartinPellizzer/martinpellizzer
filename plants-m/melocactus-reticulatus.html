Melocactus reticulatus

Melocactus reticulatus, commonly known as the netted melon cactus, is a species native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its globular shape and distinctive, net-like ridges that give it its common name. The plant produces a spiny, segmented stem and has a thick, fleshy body adapted for storing water. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange and bloom during the warmer months.

Melocactus reticulatus is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun.
