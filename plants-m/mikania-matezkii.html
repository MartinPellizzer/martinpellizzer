Mikania matezkii

Mikania matezkii, commonly known as the Mexican moonflower, is a climbing vine native to Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
