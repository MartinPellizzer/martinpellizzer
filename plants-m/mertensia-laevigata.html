Mertensia laevigata

Mertensia laevigata, commonly known as smooth bluebells, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Violaceae and is characterized by its clusters of bell-shaped, sky-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. Mertensia laevigata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
