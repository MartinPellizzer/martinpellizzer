Melilotus sulcatus proles elatus

Melilotus sulcatus proles elatus, commonly known as the hairy melilot, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is notable for its finely hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Melilotus sulcatus proles elatus is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of nectar for pollinators.
