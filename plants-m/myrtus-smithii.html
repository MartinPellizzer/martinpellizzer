Myrtus smithii

Myrtus smithii, commonly known as Smith's myrtle, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern California.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant features small, dark green leaves with a fragrant, slightly oily scent. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Myrtus smithii thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.
