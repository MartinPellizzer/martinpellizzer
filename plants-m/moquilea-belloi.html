Moquilea belloi

Moquilea belloi, commonly known as the Yucatán milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the Yucatán Peninsula in Mexico.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale pink flowers that bloom throughout the dry season. Moquilea belloi is adapted to arid environments and has a deep root system to access groundwater.

It also serves as a food source for certain native insects and is used in traditional medicine by local communities.
