Malcolmia strigosa var. macrantha

Malcolmia strigosa var. macrantha, commonly known as the large-flowered malcolmia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often grayish-green in color. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value and drought tolerance contribute to its popularity among gardeners.
