Mammillaria albicans f. dolorensis

Mammillaria albicans f. dolorensis, commonly known as the white-tipped hedgehog cactus, is a rare and striking cactus native to the dry regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its pale green to white cylindrical stems covered in small, white spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a vibrant contrast to its otherwise subdued appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is often sought after by cactus enthusiasts and collectors.
