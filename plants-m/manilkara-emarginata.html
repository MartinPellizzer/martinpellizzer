Manilkara emarginata

Manilkara emarginata, commonly known as the yellow mombin, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and large, lobed leaves that are alternate along the stems. The plant produces yellow-orange fruits that are edible and contain a single seed. It thrives in humid climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The tree is valued for its fruit, timber, and traditional medicinal uses.
