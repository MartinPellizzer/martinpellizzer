Mimosa rufescens

Mimosa rufescens, commonly known as the red mimosa, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves that fold shut when touched, a trait known as thigmonastic response. The plant produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to various climates.

Its ornamental value and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
