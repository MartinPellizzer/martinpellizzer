Masdevallia hypodiscus

Masdevallia hypodiscus, commonly known as the orchid with the disc-like lip, is a terrestrial orchid native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its intricate, showy flowers that typically feature a prominent, disc-shaped lip. The plant has a slender, fibrous root system and grows in shaded, humid environments. Its leaves are usually elongated and grass-like, contributing to its overall delicate appearance.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
