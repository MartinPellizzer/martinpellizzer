Merostachys scandens

Merostachys scandens, commonly known as the climbing grass or silver grass, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems that can reach up to several feet in length. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often silver-haired, giving it its common name. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly rough texture.

Merostachys scandens is often found in moist, disturbed soils along stream banks and in wetlands.
