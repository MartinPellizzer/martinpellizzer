Miconia tocoracemifera

Miconia tocoracemifera, commonly known as the coral tree, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets, and its bark is often smooth and reddish in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens, though it can become invasive in certain environments.
