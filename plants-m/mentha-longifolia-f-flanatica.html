Mentha longifolia f. flanatica

Mentha longifolia f. flanatica, commonly known as the sweet mint or flanatic mint, is a subspecies of the mint plant characterized by its aromatic, fragrant leaves.

It is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive, sweet scent. Its flowers are small, tubular, and typically pale blue or purple, appearing in clusters.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its culinary and medicinal uses, as well as for ornamental purposes in gardens.
