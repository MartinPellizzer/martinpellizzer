Melastoma trinerve

Melastoma trinerve, commonly known as the three-veined melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Melastomataceae and is characterized by its showy, urn-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and undergrowth. Its leaves are opposite, with three prominent veins, giving it its scientific name.

Melastoma trinerve is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some cultures.
