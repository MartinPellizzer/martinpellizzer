Millettia griffoniana

Millettia griffoniana, commonly known as the Griffon's millettia, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and somewhat elusive species in the wild.
