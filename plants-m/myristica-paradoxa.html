Myristica paradoxa

Myristica paradoxa, commonly known as the black pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, woody fruits that contain the spice known as long pepper. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark, spherical berries that enclose the aromatic seeds. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a glossy surface, while its bark is thick and deeply fissured.

The plant is valued not only for its medicinal properties but also for its role in traditional herbal remedies.
