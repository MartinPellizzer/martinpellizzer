Mallotus floribundus var. genuinus

Mallotus floribundus var. genuinus, commonly known as the hairy mangosteen, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and distinctive, clustered inflorescences. The leaves are typically ovate or elliptical, with a waxy texture and a prominent midrib.

Its flowers are small and fragrant, often arranged in panicles, and it produces edible, segmented fruits that are rich in vitamin C. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
