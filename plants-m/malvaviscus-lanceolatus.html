Malvaviscus lanceolatus

Malvaviscus lanceolatus, commonly known as the arrowleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton, and is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces vibrant pink or purple blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping in arid regions.

Its ornamental value and drought tolerance contribute to its appeal in both garden and ecological settings.
