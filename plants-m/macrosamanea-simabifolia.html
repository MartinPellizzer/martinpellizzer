Macrosamanea simabifolia

Macrosamanea simabifolia, commonly known as the giant fan palm, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves that can reach up to three meters in diameter, giving it a dramatic appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into clustered, fleshy fruits. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its size and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
