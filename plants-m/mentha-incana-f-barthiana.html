Mentha incana f. barthiana

Mentha incana f. barthiana, commonly known as the whitehound mint, is a subspecies of the mint genus Mentha.

It is characterized by its silver-white, hairy foliage and aromatic, slightly minty scent. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a soft, velvety texture.

Mentha incana f. barthiana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is also used in herbal remedies.
