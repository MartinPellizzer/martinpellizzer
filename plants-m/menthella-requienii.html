Menthella requienii

Menthella requienii, commonly known as the Requien's mint, is a species of flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its small, tubular flowers are typically pale blue to purple in color and are arranged in whorled clusters.

Menthella requienii is valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
