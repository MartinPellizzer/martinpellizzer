Monardella linoides subsp. erecta

Monardella linoides subsp.

erecta, commonly known as the erect monardella, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture.

The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry meadows and open woodlands.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
