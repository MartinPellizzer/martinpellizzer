Myrcia opaca var. latifolia

Myrcia opaca var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved myrcia, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom in clusters. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn red when mature.

This species is often found in moist, shaded forest environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal uses.
