Mesembryanthemum humile

Mesembryanthemum humile, commonly known as the dwarf ice plant, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, water-storing leaves and small, star-shaped flowers. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptation to drought conditions. It produces a profusion of bright yellow or white flowers during the cooler months, adding ornamental value to gardens.

Its compact growth habit makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
