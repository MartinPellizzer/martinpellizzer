Macropsychanthus scaber var. scaber

Macropsychanthus scaber var. scaber, commonly known as the rough-hairy mallow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It features broad, heart-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture that gives it its scientific name. The plant produces showy, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting addition to native plant gardens.
