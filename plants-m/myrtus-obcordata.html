Myrtus obcordata

Myrtus obcordata, commonly known as common myrtle, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant leaves that are often used in traditional medicine and perfumery. The plant produces small white flowers that give way to dark purple berries. Its leaves contain essential oils, which contribute to its aromatic properties.

Myrtus obcordata is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas.
