Macleania colorata

Macleania colorata, commonly known as the pink mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Macleania colorata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
