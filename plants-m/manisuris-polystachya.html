Manisuris polystachya

Manisuris polystachya, commonly known as the mountain bamboo, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is often found in mountainous regions with moist, well-drained soils. Its rhizomes are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
