Malpighia mutabilis

Malpighia mutabilis, commonly known as the variable passionflower, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that change hues as they mature, typically from yellow to red. The plant produces small, edible berries that are rich in vitamin C and have a tart flavor. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often covered in fine, velvety hairs.

Malpighia mutabilis is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its potential medicinal properties.
