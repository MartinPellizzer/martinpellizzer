Myrcia cancellata

Myrcia cancellata, commonly known as the red myrtle, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is a small, reddish drupe that contains a single seed.

Myrcia cancellata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
