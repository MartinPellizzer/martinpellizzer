Malaxis parviflora

Malaxis parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered malaxis, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its flowers are usually arranged in loose clusters and bloom during the spring or early summer.

Malaxis parviflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with high humidity and partial shade.
