Mimulus pissisi

Mimulus pissisi, commonly known as the yellow flag, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its showy, orange-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, fuzzy layer. It thrives in moist or aquatic environments, often found along the edges of ponds and streams.

Mimulus pissisi plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
