Macronema grindelioides

Macronema grindelioides, commonly known as the desert daisy, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, succulent-like growth habit and produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to survive in dry environments. Its flowers bloom during the rainy season, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Macronema grindelioides is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
