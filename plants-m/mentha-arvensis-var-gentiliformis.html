Mentha arvensis var. gentiliformis

Mentha arvensis var. gentiliformis, commonly known as field mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along stream banks. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a serrated edge and emit a strong minty fragrance when crushed.

This variety is notable for its medicinal uses and is sometimes cultivated for its essential oils.
