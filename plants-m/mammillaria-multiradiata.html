Mammillaria multiradiata

Mammillaria multiradiata, commonly known as the radiate mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and is covered in white, woolly spines that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in the spring, which are borne on short, erect stems. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique morphology and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and collectors.
