Malva martrinii

Malva martrinii, commonly known as the Mexican malva, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and in woodland areas.

Malva martrinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
