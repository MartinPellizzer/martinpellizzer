Mentha grisella

Mentha grisella, commonly known as gray mint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other mints, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, giving it a distinctive minty scent.

Gray mint is often used in traditional herbal medicine and as a culinary herb.
