Mnesithea rottboellioides

Mnesithea rottboellioides, commonly known as the rock dandelion, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, yellow, and arranged in a cluster, blooming in late spring to early summer.

Mnesithea rottboellioides is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
