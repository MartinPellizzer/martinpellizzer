Malpighia meyeriana

Malpighia meyeriana, commonly known as the Mexican wolfberry, is a small evergreen shrub native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Malpighiaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces bright red, edible berries that are rich in vitamin C and have a tangy flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and nutritional benefits, it is cultivated in gardens and for commercial use.
