Magnolia versicolor

Magnolia versicolor, commonly known as the western magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth, dark green upper surface. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
