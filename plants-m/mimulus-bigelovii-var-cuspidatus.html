Mimulus bigelovii var. cuspidatus

Mimulus bigelovii var. cuspidatus, commonly known as the bigelov's monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as stream banks and meadows. It has heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This variety is part of the Physaleae tribe within the Plantaginaceae family.
