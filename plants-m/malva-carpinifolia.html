Malva carpinifolia

Malva carpinifolia, commonly known as the wood spurge, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like hibiscus and cotton. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems, heart-shaped leaves, and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland edges and meadows.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
