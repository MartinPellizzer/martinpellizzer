Myrcianthes apiculata

Myrcianthes apiculata, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and small, clustered white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in swampy or wetland areas.

Myrcianthes apiculata is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
