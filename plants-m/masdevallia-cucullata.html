Masdevallia cucullata

Masdevallia cucullata, commonly known as the hooded masdevallia, is a striking orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, hood-like lip that gives it its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually violet to purple in color, with intricate patterns that enhance its ornamental appeal.

Masdevallia cucullata requires specific care, including high humidity and indirect light, to thrive in cultivation.
