Malephora crocea var. purpureocrocea

Malephora crocea var. purpureocrocea, commonly known as the purple-flowered orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions like Thailand and Indonesia. The plant features tall, upright stems with clusters of showy, violet-pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
