Myrcia hassleriana

Myrcia hassleriana, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are often consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Myrcia hassleriana is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine.
