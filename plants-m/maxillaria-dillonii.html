Maxillaria dillonii

Maxillaria dillonii, commonly known as the Dillon's orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the genus Maxillaria, which includes over 200 species of epiphytic and terrestrial orchids. The plant features showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

Maxillaria dillonii is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
