Momordica eberhardtii

Momordica eberhardtii, commonly known as the Eberhardt bitter gourd, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its elongated, warty fruit with a bitter taste. The plant produces yellow flowers and has trailing vines that can grow up to several meters in length. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

The fruit is often used in traditional medicine and cuisine, though its bitterness limits culinary use.
