Microcerasus japonica

Microcerasus japonica, commonly known as the Japanese cherry plum, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a smooth texture and turn yellow in autumn.

Microcerasus japonica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is commonly cultivated in gardens.
