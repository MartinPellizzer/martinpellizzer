Marsdenia cremea

Marsdenia cremea, commonly known as the Creamy Marsdenia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Marsdenia cremea is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
