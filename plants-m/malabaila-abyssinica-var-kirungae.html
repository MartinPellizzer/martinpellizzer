Malabaila abyssinica var. kirungae

Malabaila abyssinica var. kirungae, commonly known as the Ethiopian flame tree, is a tropical species native to East Africa.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its striking orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces woody pods containing flat, brown seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.

The botanical features of this plant make it a notable ornamental species in tropical and subtropical regions.
