Myrtus xylopioides

Myrtus xylopioides, commonly known as the Canary Island myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

Myrtus xylopioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and its historical use in traditional medicine.
