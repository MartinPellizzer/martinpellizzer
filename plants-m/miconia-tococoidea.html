Miconia tococoidea

Miconia tococoidea, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, bright red fruits that are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor. Miconia tococoidea is also notable for its showy, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain environments.
