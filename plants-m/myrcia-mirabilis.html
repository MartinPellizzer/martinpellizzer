Myrcia mirabilis

Myrcia mirabilis, commonly known as the Brazilian beauty or purple myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its aromatic, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a purple tinge. Myrcia mirabilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its leaves and flowers are also used in the production of essential oils and perfumes.
