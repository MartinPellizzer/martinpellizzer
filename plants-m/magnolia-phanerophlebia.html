Magnolia phanerophlebia

Magnolia phanerophlebia, commonly known as the Chinese magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, often peeling in thin layers.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and parks.
