Muhlenbergia pringlei

Muhlenbergia pringlei, commonly known as the Pringle's muhlenbergia, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, upright blades that can reach heights of up to 1 meter. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers in late summer, which are typically tan to brown in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for xeriscaping.

Muhlenbergia pringlei is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
