Microcerasus glandulosa f. consociiflora

Microcerasus glandulosa f. consociiflora, commonly known as the clustered-flowered Chinese hawthorn, is a compact shrub characterized by its small, glandular leaves and showy white to pink flowers that bloom in clusters.

It is native to China and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. The plant's floral structure is notable for its dense inflorescences, which enhance its ornamental value. It is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
