Masdevallia erinacea

Masdevallia erinacea, commonly known as the pink masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, pendulous flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant has a compact growth habit with a pseudobulb and is often grown as a terrestrial orchid in shaded, humid environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Masdevallia erinacea is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its unique floral display and ornamental value.
