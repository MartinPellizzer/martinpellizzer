Myagrum pumilum

Myagrum pumilum, commonly known as the dwarf pandanus, is a small shrub native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its dense, fibrous leaves that form a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, which are often used in traditional crafts and as a source of fiber. Myagrum pumilum thrives in tropical climates and is often found growing in coastal areas or on sandy soils.

Its unique appearance and practical uses make it an important plant in its native ecosystems.
