Madhuca tomentosa

Madhuca tomentosa, commonly known as the velvet tallow tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by edible, fleshy fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local cultures.
