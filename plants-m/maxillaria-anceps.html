Maxillaria anceps

Maxillaria anceps, commonly known as the showy maxillaria, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically blooms in late spring or early summer, with flowers that have a distinctive spurred lip. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, arranged in two ranks around the pseudobulbs.

Maxillaria anceps is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for orchid enthusiasts.
