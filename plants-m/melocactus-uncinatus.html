Melocactus uncinatus

Melocactus uncinatus, commonly known as the hooked melon cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stem and is typically covered in spines. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the morning, adding a striking visual contrast to its green body. It is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for desert gardens and rockeries.
