Musa × paradisiaca var. champa

Musa × paradisiaca var. champa, commonly known as the Chinese banana, is a cultivated variety of the banana plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, elongated leaves and clusters of elongated, pale yellow to orange fruits. The plant is typically grown for its edible fruit, which has a mild, sweet flavor and a texture similar to plantains. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

The variety is also valued for its ornamental qualities, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
