Mesembryanthemum verruculoides

Mesembryanthemum verruculoides, commonly known as the African snowball, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers during the cooler months. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ability to bloom in winter.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to dry climates and poor soil conditions.
