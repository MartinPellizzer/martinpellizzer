Manganaroa articulata

Manganaroa articulata, commonly known as the articulated mangrove, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline environments.

It is characterized by its segmented, jointed stems and roots that form a complex root system for stabilizing coastal sediments. The plant has small, alternate leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. It plays a crucial role in coastal ecosystems by preventing erosion and providing habitat for marine life.

Manganaroa articulata is typically found in mangrove forests along tropical and subtropical coastlines.
