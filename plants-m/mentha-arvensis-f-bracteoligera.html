Mentha arvensis f. bracteoligera

Mentha arvensis f. bracteoligera, commonly known as the narrow-leaved field mint, is a variant of the broad-leafed mint species native to temperate regions.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and a strong, minty aroma. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. It produces small, pale purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in herbal remedies and culinary applications.
