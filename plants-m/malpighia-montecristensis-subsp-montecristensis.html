Malpighia montecristensis subsp.

montecristensis, commonly known as the Montecristi acerola, is a species of flowering plant in the family Malpighiaceae. It is native to the humid lowland forests of Central America, particularly in countries like Costa Rica and Panama. The plant is characterized by its small, red, edible fruits that resemble cherries and are rich in vitamin C. It typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters.

The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture, while the flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences.
