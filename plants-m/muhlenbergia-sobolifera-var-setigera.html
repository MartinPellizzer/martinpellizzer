Muhlenbergia sobolifera var. setigera

Muhlenbergia sobolifera var. setigera, commonly known as the silver bluestem, is a warm-season grass native to the southern United States.

It features narrow, silver-gray blades that are often hairy, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces dense, tufted inflorescences with spikelets that are typically pink to purplish in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for erosion control and ornamental landscaping.

Its drought tolerance and ability to form dense clumps make it a hardy and low-maintenance plant.
