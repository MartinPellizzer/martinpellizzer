Mentha suaveolens var. reverchonii

Mentha suaveolens var. reverchonii, commonly known as the sweet germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and small, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of pink to purple. The plant is often cultivated for its aromatic foliage, which contains essential oils used in perfumery and traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile addition to gardens and herbal collections.
