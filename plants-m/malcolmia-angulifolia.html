Malcolmia angulifolia

Malcolmia angulifolia, commonly known as the angular mallows, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive angular stems. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly rough texture.

Malcolmia angulifolia is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
