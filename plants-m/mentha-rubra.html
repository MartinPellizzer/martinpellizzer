Mentha rubra

Mentha rubra, commonly known as wild mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often reddish underneath. The plant produces small, pale purple to white flowers arranged in whorls along the stem. Mentha rubra is known for its strong, aromatic menthol scent and is often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
