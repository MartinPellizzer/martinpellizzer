Malva angustifolia

Malva angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically narrow and have a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fibrous roots and stems are adapted to thrive in well-drained soils, often found in meadows and woodland edges.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential healing properties.
