Molinia caerulea subsp. altissima

Molinia caerulea subsp.

altissima, commonly known as the tall moor grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters.

The plant produces dense, tufted clusters of spikelets, which are typically blue-green in color. It thrives in wet, acidic soils and is often found in peat bogs and marshy areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland habitats.
