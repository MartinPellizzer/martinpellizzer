Marina orcuttii var. campanea

Marina orcuttii var. campanea, commonly known as the Sierra primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils in mountainous regions, thriving in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy texture.

The Sierra primrose is a short-lived perennial, often found in alpine meadows and open woodlands at high elevations.
