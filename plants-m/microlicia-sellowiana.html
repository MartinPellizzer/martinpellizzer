Microlicia sellowiana

Microlicia sellowiana, commonly known as the Brazilian nightshade, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and produces clusters of small, white flowers. It is also known for its edible berries, which are often used in local cuisines.

However, the plant contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
