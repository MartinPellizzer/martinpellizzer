Mariscus microcephalus

Mariscus microcephalus, commonly known as the dwarf moonflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, white, night-blooming flowers that emit a sweet fragrance. The plant has a compact growth habit with small, narrow leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique blooming pattern, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
