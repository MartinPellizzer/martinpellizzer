Myrcia hoffmannseggii

Myrcia hoffmannseggii, commonly known as Hoffmann's myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and guava. The plant features glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, black seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
