Mikania bergantinensis

Mikania bergantinensis, commonly known as the mountain morning glory, is a climbing vine native to the high Andes regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rapid growth and helical stem that wraps around supports. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, blooming throughout the warmer months. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Mikania bergantinensis is often found in moist, shaded environments at high elevations.
