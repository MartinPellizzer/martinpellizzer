Moraea ciliata var. barbigera

Moraea ciliata var. barbigera, commonly known as the feathered moraea, is a striking South African bulbous plant characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, funnel-shaped flowers.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is native to the Cape Province, where it thrives in sandy or rocky soils. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of vividly colored blooms, typically in shades of red, orange, or yellow. It is a perennial herb that flowers in late spring to early summer, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

The variety barbigera is distinguished by its hairy, filamentous bracts that give it its common name.
