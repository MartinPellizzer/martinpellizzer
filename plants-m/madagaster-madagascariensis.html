Madagaster madagascariensis

Madagaster madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar star vine, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its long, slender stems and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind and animals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
