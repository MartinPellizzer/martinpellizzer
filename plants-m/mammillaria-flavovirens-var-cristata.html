Mammillaria flavovirens var. cristata

Mammillaria flavovirens var. cristata, commonly known as the yellow-green hedgehog cactus, is a unique variant of the Mammillaria genus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, yellow-green spines and a compact, globular shape. The plant exhibits a distinctive "cristate" form, meaning it has a crest-like growth pattern, which is a rare and desirable trait in cacti. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its vibrant color and unusual form make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
