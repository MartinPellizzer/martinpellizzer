Microcerasus prostrata f. chorossanica

Microcerasus prostrata f. chorossanica, commonly known as the Shrub Cherry, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense, sprawling habit and dark green, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in spring, which are followed by edible, red berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and is often used in ornamental gardening for its compact form and seasonal interest.

This variety is also valued for its hardiness and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
