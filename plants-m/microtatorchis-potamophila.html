Microtatorchis potamophila

Microtatorchis potamophila, commonly known as the water lily of the genus Microtatorchis, is a rare aquatic plant native to specific freshwater habitats in South America.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves that provide shade and support for aquatic life. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the early morning, attracting pollinators such as beetles. Its rhizomes anchor it in the mud, allowing it to spread slowly through vegetative growth.

Due to habitat loss and pollution, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
