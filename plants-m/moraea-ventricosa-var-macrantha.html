Moraea ventricosa var. macrantha

Moraea ventricosa var. macrantha, commonly known as the Cape bellflower, is a South African plant belonging to the Iridaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy inflorescences during the summer months.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
