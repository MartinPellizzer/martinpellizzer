Mollinedia laurina var. friburgensis

Mollinedia laurina var. friburgensis, commonly known as the Friburgensis Mollinedia, is a variant of the evergreen shrub native to South America.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green, with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments.

This variety is well-suited for woodland gardens and requires well-drained soil and moderate moisture.
