Mazus stolonifer f. roseus

Mazus stolonifer f. roseus, commonly known as the pink mazus, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern China.

It features creeping stolons that root at the nodes, forming dense mats of foliage. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink to lavender, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a soft, velvety texture and a bluish-green hue.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for ground cover in woodland gardens.
