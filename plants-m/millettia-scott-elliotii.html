Millettia scott-elliotii

Millettia scott-elliotii, commonly known as the Elliot's millettia, is a species of legume native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves, which are typically palmately arranged. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and potential ecological roles, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
