Melilotus parviflorus var. rubrocalyx

Melilotus parviflorus var. rubrocalyx, commonly known as the red-stemmed sweet clover, is a herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems that often turn reddish in color, especially when mature. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, globular clusters, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being oval and slightly hairy.

This variety is often found in disturbed soils and is valued for its use in forage and as a cover crop.
