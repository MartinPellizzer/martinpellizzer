Mimosa acerba var. minor

Mimosa acerba var. minor, commonly known as the dwarf mimosa, is a low-growing shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, fern-like leaves that are sensitive to touch, curling when disturbed. The plant produces fluffy, pale yellow or pink flower heads that bloom in late spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to native gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive and resilient plant for ecological landscaping.
