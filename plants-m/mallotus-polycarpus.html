Mallotus polycarpus

Mallotus polycarpus, commonly known as the rough leaf mallot, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that are typically green when immature and turn red when mature. It is often found in moist, lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.

The species is also used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.
