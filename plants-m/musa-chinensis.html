Musa chinensis

Musa chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese banana, is a flowering plant belonging to the Musaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves and produces clusters of yellow, fleshy berries that are edible. The plant is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and warm temperatures. Its roots are fibrous, and it has a rhizomatous stem that supports its growth.

The fruit of Musa chinensis is often used in cooking and is valued for its nutritional content.
