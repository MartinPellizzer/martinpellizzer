Malaxis taurina

Malaxis taurina, commonly known as the Taurine Malaxis, is a terrestrial orchid native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and rocky areas. The plant features a slender, creeping rhizome with erect, linear leaves. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and often have a slight fragrance.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
