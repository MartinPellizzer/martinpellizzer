Menonvillea chilensis var. aptera

Menonvillea chilensis var. aptera, commonly known as the Chilean flatleaf cactus, is a succulent plant native to Chile.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water. The plant typically has a low-growing, sprawling habit with smooth, pale green to blue-gray foliage. Its leaves are usually reduced to spines, and it produces yellow flowers during the summer months.

This variant is notable for its unique morphology and adaptation to arid environments.
