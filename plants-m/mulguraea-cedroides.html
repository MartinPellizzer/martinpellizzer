Mulguraea cedroides

Mulguraea cedroides, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mulga, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in dry conditions. It plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.

Mulguraea cedroides is also valued for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in harsh climates.
