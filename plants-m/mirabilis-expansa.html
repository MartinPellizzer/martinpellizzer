Mirabilis expansa

Mirabilis expansa, commonly known as the broadleaf four-o'clock, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its broad, oval-shaped leaves and produces showy, tubular flowers that open in the late afternoon. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its flowers come in various colors, including pink, purple, and red, and are pollinated primarily by moths and bats.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
