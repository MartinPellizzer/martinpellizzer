Mimosa fascifolia

Mimosa fascifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf mimosa, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its fern-like, bipinnate leaves that are soft and velvety to the touch. The plant produces small, yellow, spherical flowers that appear in clusters, typically during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and gardens.

Mimosa fascifolia is also known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
