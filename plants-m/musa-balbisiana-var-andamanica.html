Musa balbisiana var. andamanica

Musa balbisiana var. andamanica, commonly known as the Andaman banana, is a tropical plant native to the Andaman Islands.

It belongs to the Musaceae family and is characterized by its large, broad leaves and clustered, fleshy berries. The plant is typically grown for its edible fruits, which have a sweet and aromatic flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.

This variety is also valued for its ornamental qualities and resilience to certain environmental conditions.
