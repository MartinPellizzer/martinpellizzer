Mucuna pachycarpa

Mucuna pachycarpa, commonly known as the giant urd or velvet bean, is a tropical legume native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad trifoliate leaves and distinctive, hairy, pod-like fruits. The plant produces yellow flowers and is known for its rapid growth and climbing habit. Its seeds contain high levels of protein and are used in traditional medicine and as a source of livestock feed.

The plant also has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing soil fertility.
