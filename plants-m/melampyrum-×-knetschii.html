Melampyrum × knetschii

Melampyrum × knetschii, commonly known as the hybrid burning bush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Orobanchaceae.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Melampyrum lathyroides and Melampyrum album, exhibiting characteristics of both parent species. The plant is characterized by its slender, branched stems and clusters of small, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Melampyrum × knetschii is typically found in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as woodland edges and meadows.
