Medicago cylindracea

Medicago cylindracea, commonly known as cylindrical medick, is a low-growing leguminous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features trifoliate leaves with oval, hairy stipules and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces cylindrical, hairy pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and agricultural fields.

Medicago cylindracea is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil enrichment and ecological restoration.
