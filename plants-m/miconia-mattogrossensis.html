Miconia mattogrossensis

Miconia mattogrossensis, commonly known as the Brazilian starfruit tree, is a tropical plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that are often visited by pollinators such as bees and wasps. Its fruit, which resembles a starfruit, is edible and has a sweet, acidic flavor.

Miconia mattogrossensis plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
