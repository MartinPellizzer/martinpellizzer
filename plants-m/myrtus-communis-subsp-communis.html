Myrtus communis subsp. communis

Myrtus communis subsp.

communis, commonly known as common myrtle, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region. It features small, fragrant leaves with a glossy dark green surface and a waxy underside.

The plant produces small white or pinkish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, edible berries. Its aromatic leaves and fruits have been used historically in perfumery, medicine, and culinary applications.

The plant is evergreen, with a compact growth habit and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
