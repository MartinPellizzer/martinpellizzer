Millettia boniana

Millettia boniana, commonly known as the Malacca acacia, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is known for its ornamental value, featuring delicate foliage and clusters of small, fragrant yellow flowers. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland forests.

Its seeds are encased in long, slender pods that split open to release the seeds when mature.
