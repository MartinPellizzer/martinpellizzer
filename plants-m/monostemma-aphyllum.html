Monostemma aphyllum

Monostemma aphyllum, commonly known as the leafless nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its lack of leaves, with photosynthesis occurring through its smooth, green stems. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a dry capsule that contains several seeds.

Monostemma aphyllum is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
