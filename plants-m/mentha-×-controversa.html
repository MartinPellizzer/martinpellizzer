Mentha × controversa

Mentha × controversa, commonly known as black mint, is a hybrid species within the mint family (Lamiaceae).

It is characterized by its dark purple to black foliage, which distinguishes it from other mint varieties. The plant typically grows as a perennial herb with square stems and opposite leaves. It produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in whorls along the stem.

Mentha × controversa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a more intense, spicy aroma compared to common mints.
