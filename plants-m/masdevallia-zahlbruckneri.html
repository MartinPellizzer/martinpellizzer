Masdevallia zahlbruckneri

Masdevallia zahlbruckneri, commonly known as the Zahler's masdevallia, is a rare and striking orchid native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its vibrant, spurred flowers that range in color from deep red to orange, often with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as a miniature epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are elongated and grass-like, adapted to capture moisture from the humid air.

Due to its rarity and specific ecological requirements, it is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
