Microstegium pleiostachyum

Microstegium pleiostachyum, commonly known as the broad-leaved wood sorrel, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of East Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive white veining pattern, and its flowers are typically small, white or pink, arranged in clusters. The plant grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. It is characterized by its fibrous root system and its ability to thrive in nutrient-rich soils.

Microstegium pleiostachyum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
