Meliocarpus microcarpus

Meliocarpus microcarpus, commonly known as the small-fruited ironwood, is a tropical tree native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, kidney-shaped fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is valued for its timber and ecological importance in its native habitat.
