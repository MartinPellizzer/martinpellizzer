Maihueniopsis subterranea subsp. pulcherrima

Maihueniopsis subterranea subsp.

pulcherrima, commonly known as the Andean groundsel, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains. It is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions. It has adapted to harsh climates with a deep root system that helps it survive in rocky soils.

Its botanical features make it a unique subject for ecological and horticultural study.
