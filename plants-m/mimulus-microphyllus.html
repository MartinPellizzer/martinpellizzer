Mimulus microphyllus

Mimulus microphyllus, commonly known as the small-leaved monkeyflower, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the western United States.

It features delicate, bright orange or yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Mimulus microphyllus plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
