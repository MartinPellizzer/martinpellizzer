Mahonia japonica var. trifurca

Mahonia japonica var. trifurca, commonly known as the Japanese mahonia, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It features compound leaves with three to five pairs of serrated leaflets, giving it a distinctive, spiky appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, which are followed by dark blue, egg-shaped berries. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is well-suited for moist, well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
