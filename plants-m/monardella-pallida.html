Monardella pallida

Monardella pallida, commonly known as pale monardella, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and woodlands. Its flowers are usually pale purple to white, and it produces small, hairy fruits called nutlets.

Monardella pallida is often used in native plant gardens due to its attractive appearance and ecological benefits.
