Melasanthus

Melasanthus, commonly known as the black beauty or melasanthus tree, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its woody structure and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing seeds. Melasanthus species are often cultivated for their ornamental value and are also used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
