Malpighia argentea

Malpighia argentea, commonly known as silver star or silver mallow, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring silver-gray foliage due to a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically pink or red, arranged in clusters at the ends of branches. Its fruit is a bright red, fleshy berry that contains several seeds.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
