Myrtus obtusissima

Myrtus obtusissima, commonly known as the evergreen myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are thick and leathery, with a slight aromatic scent. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, followed by edible, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it suitable for hedges, borders, or containers.
