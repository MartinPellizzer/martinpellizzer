Malus communis var. coronata

Malus communis var. coronata, commonly known as the cultivated apple, is a variety of the domestic apple tree.

It is characterized by its rounded, often bushy growth habit and is known for its ornamental value due to its showy flowers and colorful fruit. The plant typically produces small, edible fruits that are popular for both culinary and ornamental purposes. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is widely cultivated in gardens and orchards around the world.
