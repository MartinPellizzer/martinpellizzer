Medicago dzhawkhetica var. timofeewii

Medicago dzhawkhetica var. timofeewii, commonly known as the Timofeewii variant of the Russian clover, is a perennial legume native to Central Asia.

It features trifoliate leaves with a hairy texture and produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemes. The plant is characterized by its robust root system and ability to thrive in dry, sandy soils. It is often found in steppe and semi-desert environments.

Its botanical significance lies in its adaptability and potential use in ecological restoration projects.
