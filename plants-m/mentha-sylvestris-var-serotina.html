Mentha sylvestris var. serotina

Mentha sylvestris var. serotina, commonly known as wild mint or woodland mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped, serrated, and emit a strong, minty fragrance when crushed.

The flowers are small, tubular, and usually purple or pink, appearing in whorls along the stem.
