Mallotus ferrugineus

Mallotus ferrugineus, commonly known as the rusty-leaf mallot, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rough, reddish-brown bark and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by woody, spherical fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
