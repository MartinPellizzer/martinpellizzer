Musa cavendishii var. pumila

Musa cavendishii var. pumila, commonly known as the dwarf banana plant, is a variety of the banana genus native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, producing small, edible berries rather than the large, fleshy bananas typical of other Musa species. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, making it well-suited for container gardening or small spaces. Its leaves are broad and slightly lobed, with a smooth, waxy surface.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and unique fruit, which is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
