Miconia acalyphoides

Miconia acalyphoides, commonly known as the "false glorybower," is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that are typically arranged in panicles. Miconia acalyphoides is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, making it an invasive species in some regions.

Its botanical features make it an important subject in ecological and horticultural studies.
