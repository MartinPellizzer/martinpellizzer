Masdevallia inflata

Masdevallia inflata, commonly known as the inflated masdevallia, is a tropical orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, inflated pouch-like structure at the base of its flower, which resembles a small bladder. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with intricate patterns that enhance its ornamental value.

Masdevallia inflata requires high humidity and indirect light to thrive, making it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
