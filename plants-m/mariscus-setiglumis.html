Mariscus setiglumis

Mariscus setiglumis, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of blue or purple. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun to partial shade. Mariscus setiglumis is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

It is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
