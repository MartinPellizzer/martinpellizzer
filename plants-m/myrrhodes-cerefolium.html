Myrrhodes cerefolium

Myrrhodes cerefolium, commonly known as the white hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It features a rosette of large, waxy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, white, powdery coating. The plant produces umbel-like clusters of small, white to pale green flowers in the spring. Myrrhodes cerefolium is known for its medicinal properties, with historical use in treating various ailments.

However, it is toxic and should be handled with care.
