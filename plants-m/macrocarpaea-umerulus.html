Macrocarpaea umerulus

Macrocarpaea umerulus, commonly known as the golden star hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange petals and red stamens, which make it a visually appealing ornamental plant. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. Its leaves are ovate with a smooth surface and a glossy appearance, while its flowers bloom in clusters.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
