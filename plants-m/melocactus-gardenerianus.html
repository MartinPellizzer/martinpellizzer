Melocactus gardenerianus

Melocactus gardenerianus, commonly known as the Gardner's melon cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive, ribbed, spherical stems that are typically green with a yellowish or reddish tint. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, adding a striking visual contrast to its foliage. It has a slow growth rate and is well adapted to survive in hot, dry environments with minimal water.

Due to its unique appearance and hardiness, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and collectors.
