Melissa granatensis

Melissa granatensis, commonly known as granate lemon balm, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like mint and basil. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, lemon-scented leaves and small, pale yellow flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Melissa granatensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and mild, citrusy fragrance.
