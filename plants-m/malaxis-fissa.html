Malaxis fissa

Malaxis fissa, commonly known as the fissured malaxis, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. The plant features a slender, branched stem with elliptical, dark green leaves. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, often clustered in a spike, and are known for their subtle fragrance.

Malaxis fissa is valued in horticulture for its unique appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
