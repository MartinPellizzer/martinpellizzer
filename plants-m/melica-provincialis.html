Melica provincialis

Melica provincialis, commonly known as the provincial melic, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. The plant has slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle bearing small, spikelet-like flowers.

Melica provincialis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
