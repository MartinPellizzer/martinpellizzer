Mitrella aberrans

Mitrella aberrans, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night. The plant belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is known for its unique nocturnal blooming habit. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are woody and branched.

The fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, hard seeds.
