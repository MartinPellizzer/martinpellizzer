Momordica ecirrhata

Momordica ecirrhata, commonly known as the horned melon or African horned cucumber, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to tropical Africa and is characterized by its distinctive spiky, green, warty fruit. The plant produces large, elongated fruits that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The fruit's interior contains a sweet, white, fleshy pulp with black seeds.

It is often cultivated for its unique appearance and edible fruit, which is used in various culinary traditions.
