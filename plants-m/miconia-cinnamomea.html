Miconia cinnamomea

Miconia cinnamomea, commonly known as the cinnamon tree or Brazilian cinnamon, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is a large evergreen tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its fruits are woody capsules that contain several seeds, and the plant is known for its aromatic bark and leaves.

Miconia cinnamomea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.
