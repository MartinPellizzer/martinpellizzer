Mentha × schultziana var. isarensis

Mentha × schultziana var. isarensis, commonly known as the Isarensis mint, is a hybrid mint species native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly serrated leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers. This variety is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has a strong menthol scent and is often used in herbal teas and traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
