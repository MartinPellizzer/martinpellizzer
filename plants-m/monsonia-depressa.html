Monsonia depressa

Monsonia depressa, commonly known as the African violets or more specifically the "Cape violet," is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb with a compact, upright growth habit and features basal leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in clusters at the top of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate to bright indirect light.

Monsonia depressa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated as a houseplant.
