Magnolia figo var. skinneriana

Magnolia figo var. skinneriana, commonly known as the Skinner's magnolia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically eaten by birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a smooth texture and a glossy appearance.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
