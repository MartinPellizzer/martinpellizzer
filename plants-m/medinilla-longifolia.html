Medinilla longifolia

Medinilla longifolia, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a tropical flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and arranged in whorls around the stem.

Medinilla longifolia is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and as a potted plant.
