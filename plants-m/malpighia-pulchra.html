Malpighia pulchra

Malpighia pulchra, commonly known as the scarlet beauty, is a species of flowering plant in the Malpighiaceae family.

It is native to Central and South America, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its vibrant red flowers and succulent, fleshy fruits that are edible. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

The plant is well adapted to grow in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
