Machilus leptophyllus

Machilus leptophyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf machilus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are usually arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruits are drupes, typically containing a single seed, and it is often found in moist, shaded forest environments.

Machilus leptophyllus is valued for its ornamental potential and is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
