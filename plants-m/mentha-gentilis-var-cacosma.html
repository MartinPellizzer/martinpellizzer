Mentha gentilis var. cacosma

Mentha gentilis var. cacosma, commonly known as wild mint or field mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. The plant features square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a serrated edge. Its small, pale blue to purple flowers bloom in whorled clusters during the summer months.

Mentha gentilis var. cacosma is valued for its aromatic leaves and has been used historically in herbal medicine and culinary applications.
