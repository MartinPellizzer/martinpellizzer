Miconia lindenii

Miconia lindenii, commonly known as the white miconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, white flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers are bisexual and have five petals, with prominent stamens that contribute to its ornamental appeal.

Miconia lindenii is often cultivated in warm, humid climates for its aesthetic value.
