Matricaria italica

Matricaria italica, commonly known as the Italian chamomile, is an annual flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and prefers well-drained soils in full sun. Its leaves are finely divided and aromatic, often used in herbal teas and remedies.

The flowers are used in both culinary and medicinal applications due to their mild, chamomile-like scent and potential calming properties.
