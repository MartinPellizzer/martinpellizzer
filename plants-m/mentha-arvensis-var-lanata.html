Mentha arvensis var. lanata

Mentha arvensis var. lanata, commonly known as woolly mint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, woolly leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant has square stems and produces small, pale blue or purple flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in meadows and woodland areas.

Woolly mint is known for its strong, minty aroma and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
