Miconia ingens

Miconia ingens, commonly known as the giant miconia, is a large shrub or small tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, palmate leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are followed by distinctive, round, red berries. Miconia ingens is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, making it an invasive species in some areas.

Its botanical features make it an important component of tropical ecosystems, though its invasive potential requires careful management.
