Mallotus oppositifolius f. lindicus

Mallotus oppositifolius f. lindicus, commonly known as the Lind's mallow, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in secondary forests.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
