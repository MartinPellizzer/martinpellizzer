Myrcia adulterina

Myrcia adulterina, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall. The plant has simple, opposite, and ovate leaves with a glossy green surface. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in inflorescences, while the fruit is a red, fleshy drupe that attracts various birds and animals.

Myrcia adulterina is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils and saline environments.
