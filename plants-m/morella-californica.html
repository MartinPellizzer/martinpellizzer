Morella californica

Morella californica, commonly known as California wax myrtle, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, waxy, aromatic leaves that are often used in traditional herbal remedies. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by dark blue berries that are edible for birds and some mammals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and riparian zones.

Its adaptability and ecological importance make it a significant species in local ecosystems.
