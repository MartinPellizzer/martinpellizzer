Mammea novoguineensis

Mammea novoguineensis, commonly known as the Guinean mammea, is a tropical tree native to the rainforests of New Guinea.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds. It is often found in lowland and mountainous regions, thriving in humid, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
