Malvastrum capense var. virgatum

Malvastrum capense var. virgatum, commonly known as the silverleaf mallow, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features hairy, silver-gray leaves that are opposite and ovate in shape. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers with five petals, which bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its fibrous roots and mucilaginous sap have been used in traditional medicine.
