Mallotus tenuifolius var. subjaponicus

Mallotus tenuifolius var. subjaponicus, commonly known as the Japanese holly or Japanese evergreen, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy, dark green foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for hedging and landscape design.
