Masdevallia fissa

Masdevallia fissa, commonly known as the split-veined masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its intricate, often fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually dark green and strap-like, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Masdevallia fissa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and unique floral structure.
