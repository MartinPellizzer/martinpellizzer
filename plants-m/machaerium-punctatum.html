Machaerium punctatum

Machaerium punctatum, commonly known as the purple leaved grass or purple machaerium, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and striking purple foliage, which distinguishes it from other grass species. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles, which are typically green or pale brown. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or grassland habitats.

Machaerium punctatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique coloration.
