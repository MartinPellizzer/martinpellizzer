Malvastrum capense var. strictum

Malvastrum capense var. strictum, commonly known as the narrowleaf mallows, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features upright stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 1.5 meters tall. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers with five petals, which bloom in summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a distinctive serrated edge.

It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid conditions.
