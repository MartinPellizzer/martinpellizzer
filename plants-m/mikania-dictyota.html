Mikania dictyota

Mikania dictyota, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a tropical vine native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to climb or trail along the ground. Its stems are covered in fine, hairy hairs, and it produces small, round fruits that contain seeds.

Mikania dictyota is often found in moist, shaded environments and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
