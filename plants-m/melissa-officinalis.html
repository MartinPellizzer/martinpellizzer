Melissa officinalis

Melissa officinalis, commonly known as lemon balm, is a perennial herb native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, serrated leaves and yellowish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like mint and sage. Its leaves are often used in teas and culinary applications due to their calming properties.

Melissa officinalis thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight.
