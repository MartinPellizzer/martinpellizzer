Mespilus germanica pyriformis

Mespilus germanica pyriformis, commonly known as the Persian white mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to the Middle East and South Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple, ovate leaves that are slightly hairy underneath. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are followed by edible, yellow-orange berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its fruit and ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its historical use in silkworm farming.
