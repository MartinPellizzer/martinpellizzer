Mitracarpus linearis

Mitracarpus linearis, commonly known as the slender cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces cylindrical pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines.

Mitracarpus linearis is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for agricultural practices.
