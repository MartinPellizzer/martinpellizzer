Musa lawitiensis var. lawitiensis

Musa lawitiensis var. lawitiensis, commonly known as the Tongan banana, is a species native to the island of Tonga in the South Pacific.

It is a herbaceous plant with a pseudostem formed by the tightly packed leaf sheaths. The plant produces large, elongated, yellow-orange berries that are typically clustered in groups. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its unique flavor and is often cultivated for local consumption and traditional use.
