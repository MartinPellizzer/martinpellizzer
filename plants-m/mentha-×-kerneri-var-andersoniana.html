Mentha × kerneri var. andersoniana

Mentha × kerneri var. andersoniana, commonly known as the Anderson's mint, is a hybrid mint species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly fuzzy leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple, blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and mild mint-like fragrance.
