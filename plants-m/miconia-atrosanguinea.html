Miconia atrosanguinea

Miconia atrosanguinea, commonly known as the black blood miconia, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to several meters in height, characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. Its fruits are berries that turn from green to reddish when mature.

Miconia atrosanguinea is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain areas.
