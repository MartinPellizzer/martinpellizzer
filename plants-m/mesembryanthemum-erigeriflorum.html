Mesembryanthemum erigeriflorum

Mesembryanthemum erigeriflorum, commonly known as the woolly mesembryanthemum, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, often hairy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in response to sunlight. It is a member of the Aizoaceae family, known for its unique adaptations to desert conditions.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
