Mastixia pentandra subsp. philippinensis

Mastixia pentandra subsp.

philippinensis, commonly known as the Philippine mastix tree, is a species native to the Philippines. It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, grey bark and lanceolate leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by fleshy, orange-red berries. It is valued for its aromatic resin, which has been traditionally used in incense and medicinal applications.

The species is also noted for its ecological importance in tropical forests.
