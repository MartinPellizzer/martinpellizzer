Melaleuca protumida

Melaleuca protumida, commonly known as the broad-leaved paperbark, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to northern Australia, where it typically grows in coastal and wetland areas. The plant is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark that gives it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, and it produces small, fragrant white flowers during the spring and summer months.

The plant is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
