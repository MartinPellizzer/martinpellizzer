Monarda lilacina

Monarda lilacina, commonly known as purple blazing star, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its striking purple-pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet and has narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a hardy perennial suitable for native gardens.

Its showy inflorescences attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
