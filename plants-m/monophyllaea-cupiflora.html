Monophyllaea cupiflora

Monophyllaea cupiflora, commonly known as the cup-shaped leaf plant, is a unique species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive cup-like leaves that hold moisture, adapting to its arid environment. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
