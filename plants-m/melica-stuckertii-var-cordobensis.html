Melica stuckertii var. cordobensis

Melica stuckertii var. cordobensis, commonly known as the Cordoba panic grass, is a perennial grass native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and wetlands.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and ecological restoration projects.
