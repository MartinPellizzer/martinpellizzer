Myrtus arbutifolia

Myrtus arbutifolia, commonly known as sweet myrtle, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. Its foliage is evergreen, and it thrives in well-drained soils with full sun to partial shade.

Myrtus arbutifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and aromatic properties.
