Modecca populifolia

Modecca populifolia, commonly known as the willowleaf modecca, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive willow-like leaves, which are elongated and often have a silvery underside. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water sources.

Modecca populifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some African cultures.
