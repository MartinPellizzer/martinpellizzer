Mentha arvensis var. fontana

Mentha arvensis var. fontana, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces pale purple to pink flowers in whorls along the stem, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its aromatic leaves and is used in herbal teas and traditional medicine.
