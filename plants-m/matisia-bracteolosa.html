Matisia bracteolosa

Matisia bracteolosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf matisia, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of soft, velvety hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
