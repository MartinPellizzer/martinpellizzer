Morus rubra var. scabra

Morus rubra var. scabra, commonly known as the rough mulberry, is a variant of the red mulberry native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its rough, textured bark and small, inconspicuous leaves. The plant produces aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color, attracting various wildlife species. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is relatively drought-tolerant.

This variety is often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas, contributing to local biodiversity.
