Mallotus hirsutus

Mallotus hirsutus, commonly known as the hairy mallot, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive hairy leaves and clusters of small yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its bark is thick and rough, often exuding a milky sap when damaged.
