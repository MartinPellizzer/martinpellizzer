Meconopsis impedita

Meconopsis impedita, commonly known as the Himalayan blue poppy, is a striking alpine plant native to the high elevations of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue to violet petals, which are often speckled with contrasting colors. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its flowers bloom in early summer, adding a splash of color to the mountainous landscapes.

Meconopsis impedita is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological importance in alpine ecosystems.
