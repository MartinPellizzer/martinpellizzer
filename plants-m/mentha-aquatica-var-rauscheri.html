Mentha aquatica var. rauscheri

Mentha aquatica var. rauscheri, commonly known as Rauscher's water mint, is a variant of the water mint plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant that thrives in moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. The plant features square, hairy stems and opposite, serrated leaves that are often aromatic when crushed. Its flowers are small, tubular, and typically blue or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in herbal teas and traditional remedies.
