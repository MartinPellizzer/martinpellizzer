Melica gigantea

Melica gigantea, commonly known as giant brome, is a tall, grass-like plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces long, narrow leaves with a bluish-green color and panicles of small, spike-like flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is noted for its ecological importance in supporting various wildlife species.
