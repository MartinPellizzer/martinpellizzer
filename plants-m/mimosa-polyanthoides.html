Mimosa polyanthoides

Mimosa polyanthoides, commonly known as the weeping mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a deciduous shrub that features feathery, bipinnate leaves and produces small, yellowish-pink flowers in clusters. The plant is characterized by its delicate, drooping branches that give it a weeping appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Mimosa polyanthoides is also known for its sensitive leaves that fold shut when touched, a trait that adds to its unique botanical appeal.
