Mussaenda landolphioides

Mussaenda landolphioides, commonly known as the golden trumpet bush, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange inflorescences that resemble trumpets, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has simple, opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth surface and a slightly hairy underside. Its flowers are borne in terminal clusters and are accompanied by small, inconspicuous bracts.

Mussaenda landolphioides is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
