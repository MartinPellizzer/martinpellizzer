Masdevallia ignea var. tomasonii

Masdevallia ignea var. tomasonii, commonly known as the fiery torch orchid, is a striking epiphytic orchid native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red and orange petals, which are often adorned with intricate patterns and contrasting veins. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, enabling it to thrive in the humid, shaded environments of its natural habitat.

The species is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its unique coloration and delicate, orchid-like appearance.
