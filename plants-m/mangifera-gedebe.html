Mangifera gedebe

Mangifera gedebe, commonly known as the Ethiopian mango, is a tropical tree native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its elongated, oblong fruits with a thick, fibrous skin. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into the edible fruit, which has a sweet and aromatic flavor. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a rough texture with serrated edges.

The plant is adapted to high-altitude regions and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
