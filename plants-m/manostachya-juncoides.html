Manostachya juncoides

Manostachya juncoides, commonly known as the swamp rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in marshy areas, along streams, and in flooded soils, showcasing its adaptability to water-saturated conditions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often arranged in dense clusters at the base, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early spring. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense stands that help stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Manostachya juncoides plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
