Machaonia brasiliensis f. latifolia

Machaonia brasiliensis f. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved machaonia, is a variant of the Machaonia genus native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

This form is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
