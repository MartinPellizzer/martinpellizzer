Malveopsis acaulis

Malveopsis acaulis, commonly known as the slender mallow, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus, and is characterized by its hairy stems and showy flowers. The plant typically produces pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Malveopsis acaulis is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
