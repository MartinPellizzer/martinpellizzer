Mariscus ustulatus

Mariscus ustulatus, commonly known as the fireblow plant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that bear small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Mariscus ustulatus is notable for its ability to withstand drought and partial shade, making it a resilient addition to Mediterranean gardens.
