Macrostelia laurina

Macrostelia laurina, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, orange-yellow tubular flowers that bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Macrostelia laurina is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
