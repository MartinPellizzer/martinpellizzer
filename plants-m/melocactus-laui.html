Melocactus laui

Melocactus laui, commonly known as the Laui melon cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of central and southern Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem covered in dense, white spines that provide protection from herbivores and intense sunlight. The plant produces striking, bright orange flowers during the summer months, which are pollinated by nocturnal insects. Its succulent nature allows it to store water efficiently, making it well adapted to desert environments.

The species is relatively rare and is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique appearance.
