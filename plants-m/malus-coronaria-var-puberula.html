Malus coronaria var. puberula

Malus coronaria var. puberula, commonly known as the Downy Crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, pubescent leaves and clusters of white to pink flowers that bloom in spring. The fruit is a small, yellow or red apple-like berry that ripens in the fall. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

It is also important for supporting local wildlife, including birds and insects.
