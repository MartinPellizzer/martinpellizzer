Mikania argyreiae

Mikania argyreiae, commonly known as silverleaf mikania, is a vigorous climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by elongated, slender fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is considered a weed in some regions.
