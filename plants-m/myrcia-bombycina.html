Myrcia bombycina

Myrcia bombycina, commonly known as the silky myrtle, is a shrub or small tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale pink flowers and dark, glossy leaves. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded areas.

Myrcia bombycina is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine.
