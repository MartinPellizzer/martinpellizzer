Mycaranthes major

Mycaranthes major, commonly known as the giant mycaranthes, is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in vibrant shades of red, purple, and orange. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowering stems are often branched and can grow quite tall, making it a notable addition to gardens.

Native to tropical regions, it thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.
