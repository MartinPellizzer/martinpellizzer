Mandragora chinghaiensis

Mandragora chinghaiensis, commonly known as the Qinghai mandrake, is a rare and unique species native to the Qinghai region of China.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its distinctive, often bi-lobed leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. The plant produces small, dark berries that contain toxic alkaloids, similar to other members of its genus.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered an endangered species, making it of interest to botanists and conservationists.
