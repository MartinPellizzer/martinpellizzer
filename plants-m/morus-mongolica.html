Morus mongolica

Morus mongolica, commonly known as the Mongolian mulberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically eaten by birds and other animals. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forest edges and open woodlands.

Morus mongolica is valued for its ecological role in supporting wildlife and its historical use in traditional medicine.
