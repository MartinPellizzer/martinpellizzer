Moriera transhyrcana

Moriera transhyrcana, commonly known as the Central Asian moringa, is a deciduous shrub native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Moringaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and bipinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

Moriera transhyrcana is valued for its hardiness and potential use in traditional medicine and as a source of edible parts.
