Maytenus macrodonta

Maytenus macrodonta, commonly known as the large-fruited mayten, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically alternate and pinnate, with elliptical to ovate leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by distinctive, fleshy, orange-red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

Maytenus macrodonta is also valued for its hard, durable wood and has traditional uses in some indigenous cultures.
