Mentha aquatica var. latifolia

Mentha aquatica var. latifolia, commonly known as broad-leaf water mint, is a perennial herb native to moist environments in Europe and Asia.

It features opposite, broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers arranged in spikes, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in wet soils near streams, marshes, and pond edges.

Its aromatic leaves and flowers are used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.
