Mikania huitzensis

Mikania huitzensis, commonly known as Mexican wisteria, is a climbing vine native to Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its trailing stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed areas or along stream banks.

Due to its rapid growth, it can become invasive in certain regions.
