Myrcia pertusa

Myrcia pertusa, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are edible for some wildlife but toxic to humans. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in a variety of soil types.

Myrcia pertusa is also known for its aromatic oils, which have been used in traditional medicine.
