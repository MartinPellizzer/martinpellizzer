Muraltia inconstans

Muraltia inconstans, commonly known as the variable muraltia, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or pea family, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. Muraltia inconstans thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Its adaptability to various environmental conditions makes it a resilient and versatile plant in botanical gardens and natural habitats.
