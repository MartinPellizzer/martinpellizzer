Machaerina pulchra

Machaerina pulchra, commonly known as the velvetleaf tree, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its large, palm-like leaves that are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits. Machaerina pulchra thrives in coastal and forested areas, preferring well-drained soils and moderate to high levels of sunlight.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other parts of the world for horticultural purposes.
