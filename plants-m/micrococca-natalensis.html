Micrococca natalensis

Micrococca natalensis, commonly known as Natal yellowwood, is a small evergreen shrub native to the eastern regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles, followed by small, red drupes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is a valued plant in both horticulture and conservation efforts.
