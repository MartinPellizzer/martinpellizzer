Mirabilis multiflora

Mirabilis multiflora, commonly known as the evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that open in the evening and close by morning. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a hairy texture.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and its ecological importance to pollinators.
