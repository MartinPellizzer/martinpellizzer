Meehania urticifolia var. pedunculata

Meehania urticifolia var. pedunculata, commonly known as the stinging nettle vine, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Urticaceae, which includes other stinging plants like common nettle. The plant is characterized by its opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, which can cause a stinging sensation upon contact. It produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, and its stems are often hairy and can also cause irritation.

This species is often found in moist, shaded areas and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
