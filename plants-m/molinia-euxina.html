Molinia euxina

Molinia euxina, commonly known as the purple moor grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and produces purple to pinkish inflorescences that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wet heathland and bog environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue when young.

Molinia euxina plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
