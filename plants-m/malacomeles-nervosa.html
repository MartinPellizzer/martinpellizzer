Malacomeles nervosa

Malacomeles nervosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.
