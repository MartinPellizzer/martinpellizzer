Macaranga paxii

Macaranga paxii, commonly known as the giant macaranga, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a fast-growing tree or shrub that can reach heights of up to 15 meters. The plant has large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its rapid growth rate, making it a common species in disturbed habitats.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber.
