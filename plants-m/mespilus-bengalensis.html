Mespilus bengalensis

Mespilus bengalensis, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical leaves and yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are rich in vitamin C and other nutrients. Its bark is rough and fissured, often with a reddish-brown color.

Mespilus bengalensis is widely cultivated for its medicinal and nutritional value.
