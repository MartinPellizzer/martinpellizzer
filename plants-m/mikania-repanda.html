Mikania repanda

Mikania repanda, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It has heart-shaped leaves that are usually opposite and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in clusters. Its rapid growth habit and ability to thrive in various soil types make it both a useful ornamental plant and a potential weed.

Mikania repanda is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
