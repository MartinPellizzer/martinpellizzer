Mutellina adonidifolia var. mutellina

Mutellina adonidifolia var. mutellina, commonly known as the slender thornapple, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features opposite, lanceolate leaves that are often pubescent and have a distinct, serrated margin. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, wind-dispersed seeds.

This species is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
