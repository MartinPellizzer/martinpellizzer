Malaxis rheedeana

Malaxis rheedeana, commonly known as the red-tipped orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive red-brown tips on its leaves. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are small and clustered, with a unique coloration that ranges from green to reddish-brown.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
