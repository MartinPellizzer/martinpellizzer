Maytenus texana

Maytenus texana, commonly known as the Texas mayten, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by woody, elongated fruits containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
