Myrcia sellowiana var. costata

Myrcia sellowiana var. costata, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree or *Schinus terebinthifolius*, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often slightly serrated along the edges. The plant produces small, red berries that are clustered in dense, spherical formations. These berries contain seeds that are dispersed by birds and other animals.

It is widely cultivated for its ornamental value but can become invasive in certain regions.
