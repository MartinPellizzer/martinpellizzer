Myrtus exsucca

Myrtus exsucca, commonly known as the coastal myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in late spring, which are followed by dark purple berries. Myrtus exsucca thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it well-suited for coastal and rocky environments.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental berries make it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
