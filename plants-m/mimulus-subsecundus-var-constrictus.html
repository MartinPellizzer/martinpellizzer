Mimulus subsecundus var. constrictus

Mimulus subsecundus var. constrictus, commonly known as the narrowleaf monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically less than 2 inches in length. The flowers are usually bright orange or yellow, with five petals and a distinctive spurred lower petal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in streambanks and wet meadows.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
