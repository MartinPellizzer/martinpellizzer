Masdevallia spilantha

Masdevallia spilantha, commonly known as the spotted masdevallia, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated pseudobulbs and vibrant, often colorful flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often epiphytic on trees or rocks. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are adapted to conserve moisture.

The species is valued in horticulture for its unique appearance and relatively compact size, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.
