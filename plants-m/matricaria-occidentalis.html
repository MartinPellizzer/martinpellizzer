Matricaria occidentalis

Matricaria occidentalis, commonly known as western chamomile, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers with white centers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are finely divided and aromatic, often used in herbal teas.

The essential oil extracted from its flowers contains compounds like chamazulene, which contribute to its calming properties.
