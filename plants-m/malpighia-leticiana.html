Malpighia leticiana

Malpighia leticiana, commonly known as the Mexican beautyberry, is a species of flowering plant in the family Malpighiaceae.

It is native to Central America and parts of South America, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pink in color. Its fruit, which resembles small, round berries, is edible and has a tart, slightly sweet flavor.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
