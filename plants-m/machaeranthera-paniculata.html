Machaeranthera paniculata

Machaeranthera paniculata, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the central and eastern United States.

It features showy, purple-pink flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has a taproot system and thrives in well-drained soils, making it adaptable to various garden conditions. Its foliage is lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties.
