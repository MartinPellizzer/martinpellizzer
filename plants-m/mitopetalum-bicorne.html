Mitopetalum bicorne

Mitopetalum bicorne, commonly known as the horned milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, horn-shaped floral structures that give it its scientific name. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees. Its leaves are opposite and oval, with a slightly hairy texture.

Mitopetalum bicorne is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in full sun.
