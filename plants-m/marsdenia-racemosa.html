Marsdenia racemosa

Marsdenia racemosa, commonly known as the clustered Marsdenia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its fruits are follicles that release seeds with tufts of hair for wind dispersal.

Marsdenia racemosa is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in forests or along stream banks.
