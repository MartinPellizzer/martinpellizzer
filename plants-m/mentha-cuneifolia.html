Mentha cuneifolia

Mentha cuneifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf mint, is a perennial herb native to regions of Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and strong, aromatic scent. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are small, tubular, and usually pink or purple, appearing in late summer.

Mentha cuneifolia is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in herbal remedies.
