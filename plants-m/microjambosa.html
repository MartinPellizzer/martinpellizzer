Microjambosa

Microjambosa, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry, is a small evergreen tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The tree produces small, red, cherry-like fruits that are edible and often consumed fresh or used in jams. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is valued for its ornamental beauty and fruit yield.

Microjambosa is also appreciated for its aromatic foliage and potential medicinal properties.
