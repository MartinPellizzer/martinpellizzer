Maba geminata

Maba geminata, commonly known as the twin-leaf maba, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Malvaceae and is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that often split into two lobes. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often found in lowland rainforests.
