Myrcia brachylopadia

Myrcia brachylopadia, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of eastern Brazil, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of fragrant, white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds.

Myrcia brachylopadia is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
