Melaleuca fluviatilis

Melaleuca fluviatilis, commonly known as the river paperbark, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia and is characterized by its smooth, papery bark that peels in strips. The plant produces small, white or cream-colored flowers clustered in spikes, which bloom from late winter to early spring. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and have a distinctive aromatic scent.

The river paperbark is often found near watercourses and is adapted to wet, swampy environments.
