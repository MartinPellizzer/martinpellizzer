Moacroton lanceolatus var. ellipticus

Moacroton lanceolatus var. ellipticus, commonly known as the elliptic lanceleaf, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of South America.

It features lance-shaped, elliptic leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, often blooming in late spring to early summer. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and hairy stems, which help it adapt to various soil conditions.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
