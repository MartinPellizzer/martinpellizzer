Myrtus carnea

Myrtus carnea, commonly known as myrtle, is a flowering shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant white flowers and dark green, glossy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but typically consumed in moderation. Myrtle has a woody stem with a strong, aromatic scent, and it thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in various cultures.
