Megacarpaea laciniata

Megacarpaea laciniata, commonly known as the laceleaf cycad, is a unique cycad species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, finely divided, feathery leaves that give it a delicate appearance. The plant produces large, cone-like structures, with the female cones being particularly showy and colorful. It prefers warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
