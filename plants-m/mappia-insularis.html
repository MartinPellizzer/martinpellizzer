Mappia insularis

Mappia insularis, commonly known as the island mappia, is a rare flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a waxy surface. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in dense, lowland rainforests.

Due to habitat loss, Mappia insularis is considered endangered and is the subject of conservation efforts.
