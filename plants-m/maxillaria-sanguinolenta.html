Maxillaria sanguinolenta

Maxillaria sanguinolenta, commonly known as the blood-red orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a pseudobulb structure that supports its growth during periods of dormancy. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, often with a distinctive dark red or purple throat. Its leaves are typically elliptical and arranged in two rows along the stem.

This orchid thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
