Monotaxis linifolia var. occidentalis

Monotaxis linifolia var. occidentalis, commonly known as the western silkybark, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted stems and small, linear leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It is adapted to survive in low-nutrient soils and can endure prolonged droughts.

This variety is closely related to other species in the genus Monotaxis, sharing similar ecological and morphological traits.
