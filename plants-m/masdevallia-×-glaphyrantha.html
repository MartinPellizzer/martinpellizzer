Masdevallia × glaphyrantha

Masdevallia × glaphyrantha, commonly known as the hybrid masdevallia, is a hybrid orchid species resulting from the cross between Masdevallia tenuifolia and Masdevallia leucophaea.

It is native to the cloud forests of Colombia and Ecuador, where it thrives in cool, humid environments. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of pendulous, often fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. Its foliage consists of narrow, dark green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern.

This hybrid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
