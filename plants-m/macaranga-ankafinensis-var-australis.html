Macaranga ankafinensis var. australis

Macaranga ankafinensis var. australis, commonly known as the Southern Macaranga, is a species of flowering plant in the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is noted for its rapid growth and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
