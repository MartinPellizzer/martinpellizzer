Myriactis rupestris

Myriactis rupestris, commonly known as the rock myriactis, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and fleshy, ribbed stems that store water, enabling it to survive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Myriactis rupestris is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh environments.
