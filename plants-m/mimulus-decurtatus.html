Mimulus decurtatus

Mimulus decurtatus, commonly known as the short-stemmed monkeyflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as stream banks and meadows. The plant features slender, branching stems covered in hairy foliage. Its flowers are showy, with bright orange or yellow petals and a distinctive spur at the back of the bloom.

Mimulus decurtatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
