Malpighia polystachia

Malpighia polystachia, commonly known as the red mulberry, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically alternate and have a velvety texture. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe, containing numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
