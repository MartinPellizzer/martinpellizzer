Melocactus rufispinus f. pluriareolatus

Melocactus rufispinus f. pluriareolatus, commonly known as the multireolatus melocactus, is a rare and unique variant of the Rufispinus melocactus.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny growth and a distinctive pattern of areoles, which are the small, raised structures from which spines and flowers emerge. The plant typically forms a short, cylindrical stem with a thick, waxy coating that helps it retain moisture in its native arid environment. Its flowers, which are usually yellow or orange, bloom in the early morning and are pollinated by bees and other insects.

This cultivar is highly sought after by cactus enthusiasts due to its striking appearance and rarity in the wild.
