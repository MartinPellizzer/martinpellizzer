Myrtus coquimbensis var. rotundifolia

Myrtus coquimbensis var. rotundifolia, commonly known as the Chilean myrtle, is a shrub native to the arid regions of northern Chile.

It features small, dark green, rounded leaves that are densely packed along its stems. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by dark purple berries. It is well adapted to dry climates and can thrive in poor soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in the study of desert flora.
