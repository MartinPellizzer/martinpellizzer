Mussaenda obtusa

Mussaenda obtusa, commonly known as the woolly Mussaenda, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and showy, clustered yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological importance.

The plant is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
