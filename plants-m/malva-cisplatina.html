Malva cisplatina

Malva cisplatina, commonly known as the Paraguay marshmallow, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes plants like cotton and hibiscus. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a soft, velvety texture and produces showy, pink to purple flowers. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands and riverbanks.

Its fibrous stems and mucilaginous leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their soothing properties.
