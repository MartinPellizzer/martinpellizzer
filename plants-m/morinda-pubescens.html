Morinda pubescens

Morinda pubescens, commonly known as the Chinese wineberry, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, pubescent stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that give way to red, fleshy berries resembling small grapes. These berries are edible and have been used in traditional Chinese medicine for their purported health benefits.

Morinda pubescens is also cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
