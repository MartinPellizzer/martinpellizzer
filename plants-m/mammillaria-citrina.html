Mammillaria citrina

Mammillaria citrina, commonly known as the yellow globular cactus, is a small, spineless cactus native to Mexico.

It features a rounded, globular stem covered in white to yellow tubercles that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring or early summer, which are followed by pale pink or white fruits. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its compact size and ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in rock gardens.
