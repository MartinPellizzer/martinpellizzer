Muhlenbergia incumbens

Muhlenbergia incumbens, commonly known as the silver bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-blue foliage and compact growth habit, making it an attractive ornamental plant. The plant produces small, fluffy seed heads that add visual interest throughout the growing season. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

Muhlenbergia incumbens is often used in native plant gardens for its low maintenance and ecological benefits.
