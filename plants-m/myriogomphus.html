Myriogomphus

Myriogomphus, commonly known as the clubtail dragonfly, is a genus of dragonflies belonging to the family Gomphidae.

These insects are characterized by their elongated bodies and distinctive, club-like cerci at the end of their abdomens. Myriogomphus species are typically found near freshwater habitats such as ponds, streams, and marshes. They play an important role in aquatic ecosystems as both predators and prey.

The genus includes several species, each with unique variations in coloration and habitat preferences.
