Maxillaria pallidiflora

Maxillaria pallidiflora, commonly known as the pale-flowered maxillaria, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous growth habit and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink. The plant typically blooms in late winter to early spring, with flowers that have a distinctive, spurred lip. Its leaves are broad and elliptical, arranged in two rows along the stem.

Maxillaria pallidiflora is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for both novice and experienced orchid growers.
