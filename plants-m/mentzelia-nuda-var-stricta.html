Mentzelia nuda var. stricta

Mentzelia nuda var. stricta, commonly known as the naked mentzelia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features slender stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, with five petals and a distinctive, tubular corolla.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is adapted to thrive in poor, well-drained soils.
