Melica uniflora f. pilosa

Melica uniflora f. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy spikegrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and hairy leaves, which give it its common name. The inflorescence consists of a single spikelet, typically borne at the tip of the stem. The plant is native to regions of Europe and Asia and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

It is often found in meadows, along stream banks, and in other open, sunny habitats.
