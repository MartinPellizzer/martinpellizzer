Malveopsis spicata

Malveopsis spicata, commonly known as the spiky mallow, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant is characterized by its slender, branched stems and showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered with fine hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.
