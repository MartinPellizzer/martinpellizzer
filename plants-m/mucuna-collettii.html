Mucuna collettii

Mucuna collettii, commonly known as the velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety pubescence covering its stems and leaves, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, and it is often cultivated for its high-protein seeds. Mucuna collettii is also known for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of nitrogen-fixing benefits in agricultural systems.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it both a valuable and sometimes invasive species in various ecosystems.
