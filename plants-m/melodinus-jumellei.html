Melodinus jumellei

Melodinus jumellei, commonly known as the Jumelle's melodic vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its long, trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, often arranged in clusters. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns red when mature, containing several seeds.

Melodinus jumellei is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
