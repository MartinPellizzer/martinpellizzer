Monarda punctata subsp. coryi

Monarda punctata subsp.

coryi, commonly known as the scarlet beeplant, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, tubular red flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and emits a strong, mint-like fragrance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, often found in moist woodland areas.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
