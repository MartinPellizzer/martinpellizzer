Malache spicata

Malache spicata, commonly known as the spiky malache, is a flowering plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiky, bract-like structures that encase its small, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, well-drained soils. Its foliage is usually dark green and glossy, with lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems.

Malache spicata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
