Myrcia pungens

Myrcia pungens, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various birds and mammals. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Myrcia pungens has aromatic leaves and bark, which have been used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
