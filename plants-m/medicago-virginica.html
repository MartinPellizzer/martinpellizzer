Medicago virginica

Medicago virginica, commonly known as Virginia clover, is a perennial legume native to the eastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with a distinctive violet or blue hue, and small, pea-like flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is well adapted to a variety of soil types and can thrive in full sun to partial shade. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
