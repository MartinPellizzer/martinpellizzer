Mentha longifolia var. subobtusa

Mentha longifolia var. subobtusa, commonly known as narrow-leaved mint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are slightly hairy and have a distinctive minty aroma. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers arranged in whorled clusters along the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This variety is often used in culinary applications and traditional medicine due to its aromatic properties.
