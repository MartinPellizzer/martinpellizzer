Myrcia acevedoi

Myrcia acevedoi, commonly known as the Acevedo myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest biome in southeastern Brazil, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers. Its fruits are small, woody capsules that contain several seeds.

Due to habitat loss, Myrcia acevedoi is considered endangered and is of interest for conservation and botanical research.
