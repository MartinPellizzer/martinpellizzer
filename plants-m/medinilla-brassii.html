Medinilla brassii

Medinilla brassii, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and its distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. It thrives in warm, humid conditions and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Medinilla brassii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is popular in horticulture for its long-lasting blooms.
