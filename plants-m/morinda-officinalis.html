Morinda officinalis

Morinda officinalis, commonly known as Chinese wine berry or Indian madder, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces small, red berries that are rich in antioxidants. The plant belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is valued for its medicinal properties. Its roots and fruits are used in traditional medicine to treat various ailments, including inflammation and digestive issues.

The berries also contain a red pigment called morindin, which has been studied for its potential health benefits.
