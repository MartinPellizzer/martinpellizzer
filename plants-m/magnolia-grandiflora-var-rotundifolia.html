Magnolia grandiflora var. rotundifolia

Magnolia grandiflora var. rotundifolia, commonly known as the Southern Magnolia, is a deciduous species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are broad, ovate, and have a waxy texture, providing a lush green canopy throughout the growing season. This variety is known for its slow growth and long lifespan, often reaching heights of 40 to 50 feet.

It thrives in USDA hardiness zones 6 to 9, preferring full sun and well-drained soil.
