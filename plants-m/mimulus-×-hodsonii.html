Mimulus × hodsonii

Mimulus × hodsonii, commonly known as the hybrid yellow monkeyflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its bright orange to yellow flowers, which are typically spurred at the back, a feature typical of the Mimulus genus. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in streambanks and wet meadows.

Mimulus × hodsonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
