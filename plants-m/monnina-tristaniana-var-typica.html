Monnina tristaniana var. typica

Monnina tristaniana var. typica, commonly known as Tristan's bluemink, is a flowering plant native to the Tristan da Cunha archipelago in the South Atlantic Ocean.

It is a low-growing herbaceous perennial that produces small, delicate blue or violet flowers. The plant has slender, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in coastal environments with well-drained soil and is adapted to the island's maritime climate.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
