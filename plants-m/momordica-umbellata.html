Momordica umbellata

Momordica umbellata, commonly known as the tropical bitter gourd or horned melon, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It produces distinctive, spiky, green fruits that turn orange when ripe. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with prominent veins and bears yellow flowers that are often overlooked. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is cultivated for its edible fruits, which have a bitter taste.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
