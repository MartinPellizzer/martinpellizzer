Montia viridis

Montia viridis, commonly known as greenwater hillclover, is a small annual herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a fine layer of white hair. Its flowers are small, white or pale green, and arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Montia viridis is often found in rich, loamy soils and is adapted to thrive in cool, humid climates.
