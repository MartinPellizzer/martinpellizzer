Malpighia rzedowskii

Malpighia rzedowskii, commonly known as the Mexican wolfberry, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Malpighiaceae family and is characterized by its small, fleshy fruits that are typically red when ripe. The plant is shrub-like, with simple, opposite leaves and produces flowers that are often white or pink in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
