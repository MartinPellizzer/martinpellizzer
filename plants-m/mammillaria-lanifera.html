Mammillaria lanifera

Mammillaria lanifera, commonly known as the woolly mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, white, woolly coating that covers its stems, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, yellow flowers during the spring or early summer.

Mammillaria lanifera is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique texture and compact growth habit.
