Medinilla micrantha

Medinilla micrantha, commonly known as the velvet tangerine or golden torch, is a tropical shrub native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its dense, waxy, and often fragrant foliage, which gives it a velvety texture. The plant produces showy, red or orange flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, resembling torches. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Medinilla micrantha is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
