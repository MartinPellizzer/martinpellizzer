Macrotyloma densiflorum

Macrotyloma densiflorum, commonly known as the denseflower cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, branched inflorescences and small, often white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces trifoliate leaves and has a fibrous root system that helps in nitrogen fixation. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used as a forage crop or for soil improvement.

Its botanical features make it an important species in agricultural and ecological contexts.
