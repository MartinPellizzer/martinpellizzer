Mimosa amnis-atri

Mimosa amnis-atri, commonly known as the black water mimosa, is a rare flowering plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found near rivers and streams. Its name refers to the dark, almost black color of its seed pods, which develop after the flowers have faded.

Due to habitat loss, Mimosa amnis-atri is considered an endangered species, making it a focus of conservation efforts.
