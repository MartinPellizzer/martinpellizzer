Manihot quinquefolia

Manihot quinquefolia, commonly known as arrowleaf balsam, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, arrowhead-like leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive veining pattern. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in loose inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
