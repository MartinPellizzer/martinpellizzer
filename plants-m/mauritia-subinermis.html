Mauritia subinermis

Mauritia subinermis, commonly known as the yellow palm, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and northern South America.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces yellowish-white inflorescences that bear small, fragrant flowers. Its fruit is a fibrous, three-lobed capsule that contains several seeds.

This species is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and humid environments.
