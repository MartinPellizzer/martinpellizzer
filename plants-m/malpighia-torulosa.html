Malpighia torulosa

Malpighia torulosa, commonly known as the Brazilian wolfberry or pitaya roja, is a flowering plant in the family Malpighiaceae.

It is native to Central and South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant produces small, vibrant red berries that are rich in antioxidants and vitamins. Its leaves are ovate and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are typically yellow with five petals.

The plant is valued not only for its edible fruits but also for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
