Malpighia glabra var. typica

Malpighia glabra var. typica, commonly known as the pitahaya or passion fruit, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It produces small, colorful berries that are rich in vitamins and antioxidants. The plant has compound leaves with ovate leaflets and produces small, yellow flowers. Its fruit is typically harvested when it turns bright red or yellow, depending on the variety.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.
