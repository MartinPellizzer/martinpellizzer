Mattiastrum flaviflorum

Mattiastrum flaviflorum, commonly known as the yellow-flowered mat grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America and is characterized by its dense, mat-like growth habit. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in fine hairs.

Mattiastrum flaviflorum is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments.
