Monadenium dilunguense

Monadenium dilunguense, commonly known as the African dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. It produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into fluffy seed heads, aiding in wind dispersal.

The leaves are usually alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.
