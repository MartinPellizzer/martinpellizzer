Mutisia acuminata var. paucijuga

Mutisia acuminata var. paucijuga, commonly known as the slender mutisia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in regions of Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, featuring narrow, lance-shaped leaves and yellowish to orange-colored flowers. It thrives in dry to semi-arid environments and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
