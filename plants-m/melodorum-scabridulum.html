Melodorum scabridulum

Melodorum scabridulum, commonly known as the roughleaf melody plant, is a rare flowering plant native to the temperate regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Melodorum, characterized by its unique floral structures and leaf morphology. The plant features elongated, serrated leaves that are covered in a fine, rough texture, giving it its common name. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, typically displaying shades of white and pale pink.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
