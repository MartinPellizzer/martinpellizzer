Monechma bracteatum var. strictum

Monechma bracteatum var. strictum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf monechma, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, sandy soils along streambanks and wetlands. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, white to pale pink flowers bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months.

It is valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its use in native plant gardens.
