Majorana cretica var. hortorum

Majorana cretica var. hortorum, commonly known as sweet marjoram, is an aromatic herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial plant with small, oval leaves and clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces essential oils rich in compounds like carvacrol and thymol, contributing to its strong, spicy fragrance. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or dry environments.

This variety is valued both for its culinary uses and its role in traditional herbal medicine.
