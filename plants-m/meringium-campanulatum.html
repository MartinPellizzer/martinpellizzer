Meringium campanulatum

Meringium campanulatum, commonly known as the bell-shaped meringium, is a flowering plant characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers.

It belongs to the family Campanulaceae and is native to certain regions of Europe. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as woodland areas or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, hairy texture, while its flowers range in color from white to pale blue.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in cool climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
