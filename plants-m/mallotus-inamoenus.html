Mallotus inamoenus

Mallotus inamoenus, commonly known as the rough mallot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows in lowland rainforests. The plant is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and compound leaves with serrated margins. Its flowers are small and yellowish-green, arranged in panicles, while the fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional medicinal uses in local cultures.
