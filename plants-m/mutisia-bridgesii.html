Mutisia bridgesii

Mutisia bridgesii, commonly known as the Bridgman's mutisia, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its woody stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow or orange flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a dense, aromatic foliage that thrives in humid, tropical climates.

Mutisia bridgesii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
