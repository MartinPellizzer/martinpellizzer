Muscari praecox

Muscari praecox, commonly known as the early musk grape, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, bell-shaped blue-violet flowers. The plant produces strap-shaped, grass-like leaves that emerge from a bulbous base. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often blooming in early spring before the leaves fully develop.

Muscari praecox is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate.
