Mycetia bracteata

Mycetia bracteata, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces a distinctive fruit with a thick, fibrous outer layer and a sweet, sticky pulp. It is often found in dry, open habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid conditions.

Mycetia bracteata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
