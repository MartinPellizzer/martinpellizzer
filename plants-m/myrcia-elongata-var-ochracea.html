Myrcia elongata var. ochracea

Myrcia elongata var. ochracea, commonly known as the yellow myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the rainforests of eastern Australia, particularly in Queensland and New South Wales. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant yellow flowers. It produces woody capsules that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant foliage and unique floral display.
