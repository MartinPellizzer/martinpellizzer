Malaxis simillima

Malaxis simillima, commonly known as the similar orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its leaves are linear and often clustered at the base of the stem.

Malaxis simillima is part of the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
