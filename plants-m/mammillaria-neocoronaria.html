Mammillaria neocoronaria

Mammillaria neocoronaria, commonly known as the white-pearl cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its spherical to cylindrical shape and is covered in white, woolly spines that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.
