Melastoma lappaceum

Melastoma lappaceum, commonly known as the mountain melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its showy, often pink or red flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually opposite and oval-shaped, with a glossy surface.

The fruit is a capsule that contains small, dark seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.
