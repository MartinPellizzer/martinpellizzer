Micromelum coriaceum

Micromelum coriaceum, commonly known as the rough-leaved tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in panicles, followed by woody pods containing edible seeds. Its bark is rough and fissured, and the leaves have a distinctive, aromatic scent.

Micromelum coriaceum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some cultures.
