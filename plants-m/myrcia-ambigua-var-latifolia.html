Myrcia ambigua var. latifolia

Myrcia ambigua var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved myrcia, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, which bloom in clusters. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn red when mature and are edible.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
