Mikania tropaeolifolia

Mikania tropaeolifolia, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to twine around supports, making it a common ornamental species. Its seeds are enclosed in capsule-like fruits that split open when mature.

Mikania tropaeolifolia is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
