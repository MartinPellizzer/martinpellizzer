Mucizonia hispida subsp. abylaea

Mucizonia hispida subsp.

abylaea, commonly known as the hairy mucizonia, is a flowering plant native to parts of southern Africa. It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its scientific name.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
