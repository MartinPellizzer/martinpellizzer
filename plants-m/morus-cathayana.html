Morus cathayana

Morus cathayana, commonly known as the Chinese mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, non-showy flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces small, fuzzy fruits known as mulberries, which are edible and often consumed by both humans and animals. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

The tree has also been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
