Mallotus claoxyloides var. glabratus

Mallotus claoxyloides var. glabratus, commonly known as the smooth-leaved mallot, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy green surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by woody, spherical fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in secondary forests and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive species within the family Euphorbiaceae.
