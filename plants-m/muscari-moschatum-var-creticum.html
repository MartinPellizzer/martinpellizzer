Muscari moschatum var. creticum

Muscari moschatum var. creticum, commonly known as the Cretan musk grape, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, often with a distinctive musk-like fragrance. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or woodland settings. Its bulbs are perennial, allowing for reliable returns each growing season.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
