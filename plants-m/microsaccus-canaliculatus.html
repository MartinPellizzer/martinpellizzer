Microsaccus canaliculatus

Microsaccus canaliculatus, commonly known as the narrow-ribbed sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with tiny, three-lobed flowers. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands or along stream banks.

Microsaccus canaliculatus is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting aquatic life.
