Mentha × rhomboidea

Mentha × rhomboidea, commonly known as the round-leaf mint, is a hybrid perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its rounded, slightly toothed leaves and its ability to form dense mats through its creeping rhizomes. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or blue, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Mentha × rhomboidea is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in moist, shady environments.

It is often used in herbal teas and traditional medicine for its aromatic and cooling properties.
