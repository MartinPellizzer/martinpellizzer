Mangifera camptosperma

Mangifera camptosperma, commonly known as the wild mango or hill mango, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. The fruit is typically small, with a thick, fibrous peel and a sweet, edible pulp. It is often found in secondary forests and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.

The plant is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
