Mammea subsessilifolia

Mammea subsessilifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mammea, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows in lowland rainforests. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape with a smooth margin. Its flowers are small and clustered in axillary inflorescences, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in some cultures.
