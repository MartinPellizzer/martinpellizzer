Mandevilla denticulata

Mandevilla denticulata, commonly known as the toothed mandevilla, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Mandevilla denticulata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its showy blooms and vigorous growth.
