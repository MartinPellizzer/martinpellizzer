Marara caryotifolia

Marara caryotifolia, commonly known as the African pear, is a tropical tree native to western and central Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by edible fruits resembling pears. The fruit has a smooth, yellow skin and a sweet, juicy pulp with a mild flavor.

It is valued for its fruit, which is often consumed fresh or used in local cuisines.
