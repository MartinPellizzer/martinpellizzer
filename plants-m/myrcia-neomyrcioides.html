Myrcia neomyrcioides

Myrcia neomyrcioides, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of eastern Brazil, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its small, ovate leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds.

Myrcia neomyrcioides is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its aromatic properties.
