Mimulus cusickii

Mimulus cusickii, commonly known as the Cusick's monkeyflower, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, orange-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils along streambanks and in open woodlands. It has opposite, ovate leaves with a hairy texture, and its stems are often reddish in color.

Mimulus cusickii is a member of the Phrymaceae family and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
