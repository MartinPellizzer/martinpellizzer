Malaxis steyermarkii

Malaxis steyermarkii, commonly known as the Steyermark's lady's tresses, is a terrestrial orchid native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green leaves and its striking, fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that form a dense, mat-like inflorescence. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and is often found in association with other orchid species. It is a perennial herb that thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is known for its delicate, intricate floral structure.

Malaxis steyermarkii is an important component of the native flora in its habitat and is often considered a rare and endangered species.
