Monardella villosa var. leptosiphon

Monardella villosa var. leptosiphon, commonly known as slender false tickseed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features hairy, gray-green foliage and produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and woodlands. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.

The species is also an important nectar source for pollinators like bees and butterflies.
