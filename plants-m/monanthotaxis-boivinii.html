Monanthotaxis boivinii

Monanthotaxis boivinii, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a striking orchid species native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant produces a single, elongated flower spike with several clustered blooms, making it a visually distinctive species. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is a subject of interest for both botanists and orchid enthusiasts.
