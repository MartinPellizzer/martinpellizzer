Mimosa pallida

Mimosa pallida, commonly known as the white mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its soft, feathery leaves that fold when touched. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its delicate appearance and unique leaf response, it is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and landscapes.
