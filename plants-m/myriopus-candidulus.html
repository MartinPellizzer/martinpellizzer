Myriopus candidulus

Myriopus candidulus, commonly known as the white-flowered mat grass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to southern Australia.

It features dense, tufted clumps with narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically light green in color. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Myriopus candidulus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
