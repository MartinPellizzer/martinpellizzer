Medicago rigidula f. discoidea

Medicago rigidula f. discoidea, commonly known as the disked goat's rue, is a perennial legume characterized by its distinctive, flattened, disk-like leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in dense, globular clusters, which give it a unique appearance. It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in dry, rocky soils. The species is known for its hardy nature and ability to tolerate poor soil conditions.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
