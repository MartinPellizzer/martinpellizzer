Megaclinium lanuriense

Megaclinium lanuriense, commonly known as the Lanuriense grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to the high-altitude regions of South America, particularly in the Andes. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Due to its ecological importance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
