Medicago truncatula subsp. longeaculeata

Medicago truncatula subsp.

longeaculeata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed clover, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, trifoliate leaves and small, yellow pea-like flowers.

This subspecies is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. It has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion and improves soil fertility through nitrogen fixation.

Its adaptability makes it a valuable forage plant and a potential candidate for ecological restoration projects.
