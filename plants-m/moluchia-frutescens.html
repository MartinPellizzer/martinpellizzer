Moluchia frutescens

Moluchia frutescens, commonly known as the African glorybower, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in coastal and savanna regions.

Moluchia frutescens is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
