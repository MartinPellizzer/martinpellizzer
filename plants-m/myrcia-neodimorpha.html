Myrcia neodimorpha

Myrcia neodimorpha, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are usually elliptical, glossy, and have a distinctive citrus-like scent when crushed.

The fruit is a small, dark purple berry that contains several seeds.
