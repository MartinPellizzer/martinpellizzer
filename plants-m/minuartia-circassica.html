Minuartia circassica

Minuartia circassica, commonly known as the Circassian rockcress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a small, mat-forming perennial that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are usually narrow and fleshy, adapted to conserve water in arid environments.

Native to the Caucasus region, it is often found in mountainous areas with well-drained soil.
