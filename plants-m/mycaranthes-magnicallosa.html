Mycaranthes magnicallosa

Mycaranthes magnicallosa, commonly known as the giant callosa, is a rare and striking plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, waxy leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length, which help it retain moisture in its arid habitat. The plant produces striking yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom during the cooler months. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered vulnerable and requires protection.
