Mortoniopteris

Mortoniopteris, commonly known as the Morton's water fern, is a prehistoric plant that lived during the late Carboniferous period.

It is characterized by its large, delicate fronds that resemble those of modern ferns. The plant had a complex vascular system, which allowed it to thrive in swampy environments. Mortoniopteris was a significant component of the ancient forest ecosystems.

Fossil records indicate that it played a role in the formation of coal deposits.
