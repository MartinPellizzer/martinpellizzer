Mussaenda microdonta subsp. odorata

Mussaenda microdonta subsp.

odorata, commonly known as the sweet-scented Mussaenda, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its showy, clustered inflorescences that range in color from white to pink.

The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

The subspecies is noted for its fragrant flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
