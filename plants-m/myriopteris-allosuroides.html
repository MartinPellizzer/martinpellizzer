Myriopteris allosuroides

Myriopteris allosuroides, commonly known as the narrowleaf huperzia, is a species of fern that belongs to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a tight, compact rosette. The plant is native to the southeastern United States, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. Its sporangia are clustered in terminal clusters, a feature common among members of its family.

The plant's structure and growth habits make it an important component of understory vegetation in its native habitat.
