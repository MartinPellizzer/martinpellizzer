Momordica lanata

Momordica lanata, commonly known as hairy bitter gourd, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and fruits that are typically green to yellow when mature. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and has compound leaves with three to five leaflets. Its fruit is edible when young and is often used in various cuisines for its bitter taste.

Botanically, it belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other gourd and cucumber species.
