Mentha arvensis var. grossidentata

Mentha arvensis var. grossidentata, commonly known as broad-leaved mint, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, serrated leaves and strong, aromatic scent. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowering spikes produce small, pale blue to purple flowers that attract pollinators.

The essential oils of this variety are used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine for their cooling and calming properties.
