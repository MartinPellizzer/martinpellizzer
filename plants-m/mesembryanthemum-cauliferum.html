Mesembryanthemum cauliferum

Mesembryanthemum cauliferum, commonly known as the rock rose, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves and showy, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems and leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ability to survive in harsh conditions makes it a resilient and attractive addition to drought-tolerant landscapes.
