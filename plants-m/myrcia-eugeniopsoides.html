Myrcia eugeniopsoides

Myrcia eugeniopsoides, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Myrcia eugeniopsoides is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
