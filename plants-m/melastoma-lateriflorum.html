Melastoma lateriflorum

Melastoma lateriflorum, commonly known as the side-flowered melastoma, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple, with five petals and prominent stamens. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, dark seeds.

Melastoma lateriflorum is often found in moist, shaded habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
