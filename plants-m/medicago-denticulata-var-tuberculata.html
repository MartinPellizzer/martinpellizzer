Medicago denticulata var. tuberculata

Medicago denticulata var. tuberculata, commonly known as the tuberculate sainfoin, is a perennial legume native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves with serrated margins and small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant produces cylindrical, ribbed pods containing several small, kidney-shaped seeds. It is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and as a forage crop for livestock.
