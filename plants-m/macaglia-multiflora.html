Macaglia multiflora

Macaglia multiflora, commonly known as the many-flowered macaglia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Macaglia multiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in marginal environments.
