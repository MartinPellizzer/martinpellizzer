Malaxis rhabdophylla

Malaxis rhabdophylla, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests or along stream banks. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the soil.

Malaxis rhabdophylla is a member of the Orchidaceae family and exhibits typical orchid morphology with specialized roots and flowers.
