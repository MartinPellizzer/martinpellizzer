Morus alba var. nodosa

Morus alba var. nodosa, commonly known as the knotted mulberry, is a variant of the white mulberry native to China.

It is characterized by its unique, knotted growth pattern, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as figs, which are typically green when immature and turn red when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

This variety is also used in traditional Chinese medicine and as a source of silk production.
