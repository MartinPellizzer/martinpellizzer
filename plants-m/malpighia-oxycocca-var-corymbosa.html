Malpighia oxycocca var. corymbosa

Malpighia oxycocca var. corymbosa, commonly known as the Brazilian wolfberry or pitaya roxa, is a species native to South America.

It is a shrub that produces small, edible fruits with a vibrant purple or red color. The plant belongs to the Malpighiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The fruits are typically clustered in corymbs, which give the variety its name.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible produce.
