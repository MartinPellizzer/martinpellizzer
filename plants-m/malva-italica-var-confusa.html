Malva italica var. confusa

Malva italica var. confusa, commonly known as the Italian mallow or confusa mallow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features lobed, velvety leaves and produces showy, pink or mauve flowers with five petals. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.

Its growth habit and botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Malvaceae family.
