Monanthos roseoflavidus

Monanthos roseoflavidus, commonly known as the rose-flavoured single-flower plant, is a species of flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its single, showy flower that typically blooms in late spring or early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces a distinctive, fragrant aroma. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its unique appearance and scent, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its ornamental value.
