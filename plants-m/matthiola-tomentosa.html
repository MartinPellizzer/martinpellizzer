Matthiola tomentosa

Matthiola tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly stock, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and hairy, grayish foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Matthiola tomentosa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its visual appeal and ability to attract pollinators.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.
