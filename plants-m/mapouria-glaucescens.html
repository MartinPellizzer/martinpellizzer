Mapouria glaucescens

Mapouria glaucescens, commonly known as the blue-leaf mapouria, is a small shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage with a distinctive bluish sheen, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. Its leaves are opposite and oval-shaped, with a slightly waxy texture.

Mapouria glaucescens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
