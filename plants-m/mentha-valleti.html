Mentha valleti

Mentha valleti, commonly known as Vallet's mint, is a species of flowering plant in the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a serrated margin. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue to purple in color, arranged in whorled clusters. Mentha valleti is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils in full sun to partial shade.

It is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves and used in traditional herbal remedies.
