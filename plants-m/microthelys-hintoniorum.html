Microthelys hintoniorum

Microthelys hintoniorum, commonly known as the Hinton's ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic orchid species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent petals and a unique, elongated flower structure that sets it apart from other orchid species. The plant thrives in shaded, humid environments and relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake, making it highly sensitive to environmental changes. Its flowers emit a subtle fragrance, attracting specific pollinators such as certain species of beetles and moths.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Microthelys hintoniorum is considered a vulnerable species facing threats from deforestation and climate change.
