Micractis discoidea

Micractis discoidea, commonly known as the yellow starburst anemone, is a species of sea anemone found in tropical and subtropical marine environments.

It is characterized by its bright yellow coloration and a distinctive, disk-shaped body with numerous radial tentacles. The anemone's structure includes a gastrovascular cavity and a pedal disc for attachment to surfaces. It belongs to the family Actiniidae and is known for its ability to capture prey using its tentacles.

Micractis discoidea plays a role in marine ecosystems as both predator and prey.
