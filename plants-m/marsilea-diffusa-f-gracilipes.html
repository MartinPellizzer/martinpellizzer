Marsilea diffusa f. gracilipes

Marsilea diffusa f. gracilipes, commonly known as the slender water clover, is a aquatic fern belonging to the family Marsileaceae.

It is characterized by its small, floating leaves that are divided into four oval segments, giving it a clover-like appearance. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in ponds, ditches, and wetlands. It produces tiny, yellowish flowers that are typically submerged, followed by small, round seeds.

This species is notable for its ability to spread rapidly through vegetative reproduction, making it both a useful indicator of water quality and a potential invasive species in certain regions.
