Malus pumila var. domestica

Malus pumila var. domestica, commonly known as the common apple.

It is a deciduous tree native to central Asia and widely cultivated for its edible fruits. The plant belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, simple, and ovate leaves. Its flowers are typically white or pink, and it produces fleshy, crisp fruits with a wide range of colors and flavors.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in global agriculture.
