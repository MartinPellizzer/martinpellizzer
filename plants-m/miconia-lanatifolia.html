Miconia lanatifolia

Miconia lanatifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in soft, velvety hairs, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that are often fragrant. Its fruits are bright red berries that contain numerous seeds.

Miconia lanatifolia is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
