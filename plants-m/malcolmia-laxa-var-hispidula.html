Malcolmia laxa var. hispidula

Malcolmia laxa var. hispidula, commonly known as hairy malcolm, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It typically grows in dry, sunny habitats, including grasslands and rocky soils.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
