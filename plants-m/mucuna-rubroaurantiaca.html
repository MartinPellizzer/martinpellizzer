Mucuna rubroaurantiaca

Mucuna rubroaurantiaca, commonly known as the red-orange velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive bright red and orange flowers, which are often clustered in racemes. The plant produces large, flat, bean-like pods that contain several seeds. It is a member of the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing ability through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules.

The plant is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a forage crop.
