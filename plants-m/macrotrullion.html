Macrotrullion

Macrotrullion, commonly known as the giant trullion, is a rare and unique plant species characterized by its large, bulbous structure.

It belongs to the family Trullionaceae and is native to specific regions with high humidity and nutrient-rich soil. The plant features a single, thick stem that supports a cluster of broad, waxy leaves. Its blooming period occurs during the late spring, producing striking, bell-shaped flowers in shades of violet and white.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern in its natural environment.
