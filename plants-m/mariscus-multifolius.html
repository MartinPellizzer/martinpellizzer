Mariscus multifolius

Mariscus multifolius, commonly known as the many-leaved mariscus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves that are finely divided and arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade conditions.

Due to its ornamental foliage, it is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
