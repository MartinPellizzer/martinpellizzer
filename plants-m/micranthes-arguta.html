Micranthes arguta

Micranthes arguta, commonly known as the hairy mountain mint, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, white to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky soils in woodlands and streambanks. It produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Micranthes arguta is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
