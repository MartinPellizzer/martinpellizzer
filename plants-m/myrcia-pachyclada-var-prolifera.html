Myrcia pachyclada var. prolifera

Myrcia pachyclada var. prolifera, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It is a shrub or small tree with elliptical, glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles. Its fruits are red, fleshy drupes that contain a single seed.

It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in moist, well-drained soils.
