Malus × eleyi

Malus × eleyi, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Pyrus ussuriensis and Pyrus bretschneideri.

It is a deciduous tree that typically grows to a height of 5–10 meters, featuring a rounded crown and gray-brown bark. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in the spring, followed by edible, sweet fruit that ripens in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a serrated margin.

Malus × eleyi is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
