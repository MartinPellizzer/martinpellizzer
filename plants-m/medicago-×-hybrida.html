Medicago × hybrida

Medicago × hybrida, commonly known as the alfalfa clover, is a hybrid plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets, and its small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant is a perennial herb that thrives in temperate climates and is widely cultivated for its high nutritional value and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Medicago × hybrida is also valued for its role in forage production and soil conservation due to its extensive root system and dense growth habit.
