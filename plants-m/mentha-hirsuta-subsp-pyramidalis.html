Mentha hirsuta subsp. pyramidalis

Mentha hirsuta subsp.

pyramidalis, commonly known as the pyramidal hedge mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence.

The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers arranged in whorled clusters, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in damp meadows or along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its aromatic leaves and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes or as a source of essential oils.
