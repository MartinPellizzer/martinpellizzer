Mimosa acanthophora

Mimosa acanthophora, commonly known as the hairy mimosa, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by fluffy, spherical seed pods. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

The species is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed sites, making it both a useful pioneer plant and a potential invasive species in some regions.
