Malva tournefortiana proles colmeiroi

Malva tournefortiana proles colmeiroi, commonly known as the Colmeiro's mallows, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton, okra, and hibiscus. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes and produces showy, pink to purple flowers with five petals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
