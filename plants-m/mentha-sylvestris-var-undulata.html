Mentha sylvestris var. undulata

Mentha sylvestris var. undulata, commonly known as wild mint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features square, hollow stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a serrated edge. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in whorled clusters during the summer months. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its minty scent and medicinal properties.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
