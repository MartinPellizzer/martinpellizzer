Miscanthus condensatus var. shinanoensis

Miscanthus condensatus var. shinanoensis, commonly known as the Shinano bamboo, is a variant of the dense miscanthus species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 3 meters. The plant produces feathery panicles of small, fluffy flowers during the late summer and early autumn. Its foliage is often a striking silver-green, adding visual interest throughout the growing season.

This ornamental grass is well-suited for moist, partially shaded environments and is valued for its aesthetic appeal in garden landscapes.
