Mikania pachychaeta

Mikania pachychaeta, commonly known as the hairy Mikania, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in heads, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forests.

Due to its rapid growth, it can become invasive in certain areas.
