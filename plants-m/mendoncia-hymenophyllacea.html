Mendoncia hymenophyllacea

Mendoncia hymenophyllacea, commonly known as the velvet fern, is a unique epiphytic plant native to the humid forests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage that gives it its common name, with leaves that are often covered in a fine, moss-like coating. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks in tropical regions. It has small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white, and it produces capsule-like fruits that contain its seeds.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many parts of its native range.
