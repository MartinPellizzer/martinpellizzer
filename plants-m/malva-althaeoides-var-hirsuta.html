Malva althaeoides var. hirsuta

Malva althaeoides var. hirsuta, commonly known as hairy mallows, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features palmately lobed leaves that are covered in fine, hairy textures, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its botanical structure includes a robust root system and a branching stem that supports its vibrant floral display.
