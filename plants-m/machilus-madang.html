Machilus madang

Machilus madang, commonly known as the Madang-laurel, is a species of evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter on the underside. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark blue berries that are edible but not widely consumed.

Machilus madang is often found in lowland to montane forests, thriving in humid, shaded environments.
