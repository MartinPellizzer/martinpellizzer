Myrtus vaccinioides

Myrtus vaccinioides, commonly known as the dwarf myrtle, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and clusters of small white flowers that bloom in spring. Its fragrant foliage and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

The berries it produces are typically red or black and are often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
