Mimulus bigelovii var. panamintensis

Mimulus bigelovii var. panamintensis, commonly known as the Panamint monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mojave Desert in California and Nevada.

It is characterized by its bright orange or yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid environments. It has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds.

This variety is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and limited water availability.
