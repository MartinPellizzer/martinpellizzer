Miconia silvicola

Miconia silvicola, commonly known as the forest miconia, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, shaded environments within the understory of tropical forests. The plant is characterized by its large, glossy, ovate leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by dark purple, berry-like fruits.

Due to its ornamental value and potential ecological impact, it has been introduced to other regions, though its invasive potential remains a subject of study.
