Malleastrum obtusifolium

Malleastrum obtusifolium, commonly known as the blunt-leaf malleastrum, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its dense, woody stems and broad, ovate leaves with a smooth, often waxy surface. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by small, round berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid tropical forests.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
