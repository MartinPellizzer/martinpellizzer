Mormodes arachnopsis

Mormodes arachnopsis, commonly known as the spider orchid, is a flowering plant native to the wet tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, intricate flower structure that resembles a spider. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers with a distinctive, hairy appearance. It thrives in humid environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
