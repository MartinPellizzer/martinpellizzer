Mentha agrestis

Mentha agrestis, commonly known as field mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and riverbanks. Its fragrant leaves are used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.

Mentha agrestis produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pale blue or purple, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
