Maba bicolor

Maba bicolor, commonly known as the yellow maba tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in catkins, followed by orange or red fruits containing three winged seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood and has traditional uses in local medicine.
