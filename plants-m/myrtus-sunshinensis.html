Myrtus sunshinensis

Myrtus sunshinensis, commonly known as the sunshiny myrtle, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but often used more for ornamental purposes. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Myrtus sunshinensis is also valued for its fragrant oils, which are used in perfumes and traditional medicine.
