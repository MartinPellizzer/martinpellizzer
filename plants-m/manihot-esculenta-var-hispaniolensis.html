Manihot esculenta var. hispaniolensis

Manihot esculenta var. hispaniolensis, commonly known as the Hispaniola cassava, is a variant of the cassava plant native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its starchy, edible roots that are rich in carbohydrates and used as a staple food source. The plant has large, heart-shaped leaves and produces flowers that are typically green or purple in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, requiring well-drained soil and ample sunlight.

Due to its adaptability, it is cultivated in various regions for both food and industrial purposes.
