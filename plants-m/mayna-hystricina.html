Mayna hystricina

Mayna hystricina, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit, with tendrils that help it ascend supports. The plant produces vibrant orange to red flowers, which are followed by pods containing edible seeds. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three heart-shaped leaflets, and the plant is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.

Mayna hystricina thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
