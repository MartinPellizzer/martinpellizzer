Mesembryanthemum latens

Mesembryanthemum latens, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, water-storing leaves and vibrant, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of pink, yellow, and orange. The plant is often found in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its unique appearance and ability to survive in arid conditions make it a fascinating subject for botanical study.
