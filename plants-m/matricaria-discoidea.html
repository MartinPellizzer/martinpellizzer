Matricaria discoidea

Matricaria discoidea, commonly known as the common tansy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features bright yellow flowers surrounded by white ray florets, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are finely divided and aromatic, often used in traditional herbal remedies.

Matricaria discoidea is known for its medicinal properties, particularly in treating digestive issues and skin conditions.
