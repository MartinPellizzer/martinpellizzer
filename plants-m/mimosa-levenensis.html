Mimosa levenensis

Mimosa levenensis, commonly known as the desert mimosa, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves that fold shut when touched, a response known as thigmonasty. The plant produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in spring, followed by seed pods that split open to release its seeds. Mimosa levenensis is adapted to survive in harsh, drought-prone environments, with deep roots that allow it to access groundwater.

Its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules makes it an important plant in soil stabilization and ecological restoration.
