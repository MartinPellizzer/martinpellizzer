Machilus micranthus

Machilus micranthus, commonly known as the small-fruited laurel, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes other aromatic trees and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are elliptical and have a smooth, glossy surface. Its small, yellowish flowers are typically clustered in inflorescences, and it produces small, spherical berries that are edible.

Machilus micranthus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
