Manglietia

Manglietia, commonly known as the "Mexican beauty" or "flower of the gods," is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the family Magnoliaceae and is characterized by its showy, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has large, lance-shaped leaves and produces woody capsules as fruit, which contain numerous seeds. Manglietia is valued for its ornamental beauty and has cultural significance in some indigenous communities.

Its growth is typically slow, making it a prized specimen in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
