Mimosa niopo

Mimosa niopo, commonly known as the Hawaiian mimosa, is a shrub native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that fold shut when touched, a trait known as thigmonasty. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by elongated seed pods. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Mimosa niopo plays a role in stabilizing soils and provides habitat for native insects and birds.
