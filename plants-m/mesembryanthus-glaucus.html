Mesembryanthus glaucus

Mesembryanthus glaucus, commonly known as the ice plant, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, bluish-green leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in the early morning and close by midday. It is well adapted to hot, dry climates and requires minimal water once established.

Mesembryanthus glaucus is often used in xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and aesthetic appeal.
