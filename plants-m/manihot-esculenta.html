Manihot esculenta

Manihot esculenta, commonly known as cassava, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is cultivated for its starchy roots, which are a vital food source in many parts of the world. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its woody stem and large, heart-shaped leaves. Cassava is drought-resistant and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

Its versatility in culinary use and nutritional value make it an important crop globally.
