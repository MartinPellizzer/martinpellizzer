Myrcia longicaudata

Myrcia longicaudata, commonly known as the long-tailed myrtle, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. It produces berries that are typically red or black when mature, and the plant is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Myrcia longicaudata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
