Mentha viridis subsp. malinvaldii

Mentha viridis subsp.

malinvaldii, commonly known as the Malinvaldian green mint, is a subspecies of the green mint plant native to specific regions in Europe. It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its distinctive minty fragrance.
