Myroxylon conicarpum

Myroxylon conicarpum, commonly known as the Copaiba tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its large, lanceolate leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in panicles. The tree is renowned for its resinous sap, which is tapped for medicinal and aromatic purposes. Its bark and wood are also valued for their durability and aesthetic qualities.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitats and is often found in dense tropical forests.
