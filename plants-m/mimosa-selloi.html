Mimosa selloi

Mimosa selloi, commonly known as the velvet mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and it has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria. Its growth habit is typically shrub-like, with woody stems and a dense, bushy appearance.

Mimosa selloi is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
