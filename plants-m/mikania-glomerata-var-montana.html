Mikania glomerata var. montana

Mikania glomerata var. montana, commonly known as mountain Mikania, is a climbing vine native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by fluffy, white seed heads. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its rapid growth and ornamental appeal, it is sometimes cultivated as a garden plant.
