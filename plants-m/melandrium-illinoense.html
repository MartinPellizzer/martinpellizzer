Melandrium illinoense

Melandrium illinoense, commonly known as the Illinois catchfly, is a herbaceous plant native to the central United States.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its erect stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as prairies, meadows, and along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers have five petals and are surrounded by sepals.

Melandrium illinoense is often found in open, disturbed areas and is a common species in the region's flora.
