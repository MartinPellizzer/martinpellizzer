Mentha spicata var. rotundifolia

Mentha spicata var. rotundifolia, commonly known as round-leaf mint, is a variety of spearmint characterized by its rounded, slightly hairy leaves.

It is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in late summer, which are clustered in spikes. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils that give it a strong, refreshing scent.

This variety is often used in culinary and medicinal applications due to its flavorful and therapeutic properties.
