Mitella grandiflora

Mitella grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered mitella, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features upright stems with lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often slightly hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped white or pale pink flowers in the spring. It thrives in moist, shady woodland environments and is often found in rich, acidic soils.

Mitella grandiflora is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
