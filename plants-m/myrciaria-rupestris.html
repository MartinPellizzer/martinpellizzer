Myrciaria rupestris

Myrciaria rupestris, commonly known as the rock myrtle, is a shrub native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, small, white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, adapting well to poor soil conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its fruit is a small, yellowish drupe.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses.
