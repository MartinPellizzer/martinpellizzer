Malaxis calycina

Malaxis calycina, commonly known as the calycine orchid, is a species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant produces slender, erect stems with few leaves and bears small, greenish-white flowers. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the soil.

Malaxis calycina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
