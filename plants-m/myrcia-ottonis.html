Myrcia ottonis

Myrcia ottonis, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are edible for some animals but toxic to humans. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

Myrcia ottonis is also known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
