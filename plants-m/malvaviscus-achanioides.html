Malvaviscus achanioides

Malvaviscus achanioides, commonly known as the African mallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
