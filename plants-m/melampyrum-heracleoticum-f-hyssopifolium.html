Melampyrum heracleoticum f. hyssopifolium

Melampyrum heracleoticum f. hyssopifolium, commonly known as the hyssop-leaved yellow false foxglove, is a variant of the yellow false foxglove.

It is a herbaceous plant with distinctive, lance-shaped leaves that resemble those of hyssop. The flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in dense spikes, and bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

This fern-like plant is characterized by its unique foliage and is often found in temperate regions of Europe.
