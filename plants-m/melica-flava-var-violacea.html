Melica flava var. violacea

Melica flava var. violacea, commonly known as the violet false brome, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with a violet or bluish hue. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny florets, which are typically green or pale blue in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
