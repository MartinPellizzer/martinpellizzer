Mimulus pallidus

Mimulus pallidus, commonly known as the pale monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as stream banks and meadows. The plant features showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

Mimulus pallidus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
