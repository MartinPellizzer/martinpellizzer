Myopteron

Myopteron, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and elusive flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its delicate, translucent leaves and small, white or pale green flowers. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees or other plants without deriving nutrients from them. Myopteron thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires specific conditions to flourish.

Due to its rarity and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern in many areas.
