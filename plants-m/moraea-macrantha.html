Moraea macrantha

Moraea macrantha, commonly known as the giant moraea, is a striking perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, robust stems and large, showy flowers. The plant produces clusters of bright red or orange flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.

Moraea macrantha is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
