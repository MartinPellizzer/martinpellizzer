Melica ciliata var. brachyantha

Melica ciliata var. brachyantha, commonly known as the narrowleaf brome, is a perennial grass species native to parts of North America.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in open woodlands and meadows. The plant features slender, cylindrical stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that have ciliate margins. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of spikelets, each containing a single floret.

This variety is often noted for its ecological value, providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
