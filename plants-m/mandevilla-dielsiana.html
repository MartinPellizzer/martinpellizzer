Mandevilla dielsiana

Mandevilla dielsiana, commonly known as the yellow mandevilla, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth habit and its ability to climb or trail, making it suitable for trellises or hanging baskets. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often blooming throughout the growing season. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Mandevilla dielsiana thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
