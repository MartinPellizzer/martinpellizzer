Myrtus phillyraeoides

Myrtus phillyraeoides, commonly known as the common myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features fragrant, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional medicine and perfumery. The plant produces small, urn-shaped white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Myrtus phillyraeoides is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in ancient cultures.
