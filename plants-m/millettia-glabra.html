Millettia glabra

Millettia glabra, commonly known as the smooth millettia, is a woody legume native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often climbing stems and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in racemose inflorescences, followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in secondary forests or along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental potential and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
