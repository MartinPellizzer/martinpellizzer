Medinilla tenuipedicellata

Medinilla tenuipedicellata, commonly known as the narrow-stemmed Medinilla, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy green foliage and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are elliptical with a smooth texture, and the plant produces small, round berries that are edible but not commonly consumed.

Medinilla tenuipedicellata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
