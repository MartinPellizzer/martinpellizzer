Malacothamnus gracilis

Malacothamnus gracilis, commonly known as slender thimbleweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial with narrow, grass-like leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and slopes. Its botanical features include a basal rosette of leaves and a flowering stem that is typically less than 30 centimeters tall.

Malacothamnus gracilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
