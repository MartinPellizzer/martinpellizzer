Mikania decora var. typica

Mikania decora var. typica, commonly known as the showy blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and wetlands.

Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it a popular choice for native gardens and restoration projects.
