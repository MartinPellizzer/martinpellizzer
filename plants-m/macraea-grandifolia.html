Macraea grandifolia

Macraea grandifolia, commonly known as the large-flowered macraea, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain seeds surrounded by a fibrous layer. It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its timber and medicinal properties.

Its growth habit and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitats.
