Medicago × tianschanica var. obovata

Medicago × tianschanica var. obovata, commonly known as the Tibetan clover, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features trifoliate leaves with obovate leaflets that are typically hairy and have a dark green color. The plant produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in early summer. It is adapted to grow in rocky and arid environments, showcasing a deep root system for water absorption.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important forage plant for livestock in its native habitat.
