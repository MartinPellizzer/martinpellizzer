Mimosa santanderensis

Mimosa santanderensis, commonly known as the Santander mimosa, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of Colombia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features bipinnate leaves that fold shut when touched, a trait known as thigmonastic response. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
