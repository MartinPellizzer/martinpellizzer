Melilotoides ruthenica

Melilotoides ruthenica, commonly known as the Ruthenian melilot, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to Central and Eastern Europe, particularly found in grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in spike-like clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Melilotoides ruthenica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
