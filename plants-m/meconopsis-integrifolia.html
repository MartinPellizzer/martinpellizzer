Meconopsis integrifolia

Meconopsis integrifolia, commonly known as the Himalayan blue poppy, is a striking alpine plant native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue or violet petals and heart-shaped leaves, which are typically entire and smooth. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments at high altitudes, often growing in rocky or mountainous terrain. Its flowers bloom in spring, adding a splash of color to the alpine landscape.

Due to its beauty and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and protected species in its native range.
