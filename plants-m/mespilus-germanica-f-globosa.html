Mespilus germanica f. globosa

Mespilus germanica f. globosa, commonly known as the globe whitebeam, is a cultivar of the whitebeam tree characterized by its compact, rounded growth habit.

It features small, white to pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters, followed by small, edible berries. The leaves are typically oval with a smooth margin and a slightly serrated edge. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its unique shape and seasonal interest.

It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sun, making it a versatile addition to landscaped areas.
