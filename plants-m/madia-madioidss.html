Madia madioidss

Madia madioidss, commonly known as the desert aster, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and clusters of small, showy blue or purple flowers. The plant has narrow, hairy leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun exposure.
