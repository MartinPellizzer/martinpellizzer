Masakia × pseudoradicans

Masakia × pseudoradicans, commonly known as the hybrid milkweed, is a perennial herb native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its milky sap, which is toxic to many animals, is a distinguishing feature of the genus.

Masakia × pseudoradicans is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
