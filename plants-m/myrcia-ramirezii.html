Myrcia ramirezii

Myrcia ramirezii, commonly known as the Ramirez's myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the rainforests of Ecuador and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a strong aromatic scent when crushed.

Myrcia ramirezii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
