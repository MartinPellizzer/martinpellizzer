Machaerium bracteatum

Machaerium bracteatum, commonly known as the bracteate machaerium, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, opposite leaflets and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink or white.

Its ornamental value is often appreciated in horticulture due to its delicate appearance and adaptability to various soil types.
