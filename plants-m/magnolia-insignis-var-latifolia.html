Magnolia insignis var. latifolia

Magnolia insignis var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The leaves are broad and ovate, with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside. This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a versatile addition to landscapes.
