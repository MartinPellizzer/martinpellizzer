Myrtus cabanasensis

Myrtus cabanasensis, commonly known as the Canary Islands myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants such as eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a dark purple drupe, containing a single seed, and it is often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Myrtus cabanasensis thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to the humid subtropical climate of its native region.
