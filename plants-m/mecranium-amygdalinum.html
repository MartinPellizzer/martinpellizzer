Mecranium amygdalinum

Mecranium amygdalinum, commonly known as the almond orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, almond-shaped seeds that are enclosed in a hairy pod. Its botanical features include lance-shaped leaves and a sympodial growth pattern.

Mecranium amygdalinum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
