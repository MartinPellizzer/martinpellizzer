Mimetanthe pilosa

Mimetanthe pilosa, commonly known as the hairy mimic flower, is a unique plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and striking, showy flowers that resemble other plants, a trait known as mimicry. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are usually pink or purple and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Mimetanthe pilosa is not only ornamental but also has some medicinal uses in traditional herbal practices.
