Mimusops balata var. melinonis

Mimusops balata var. melinonis, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, light-gray bark and elongated, elliptical leaves that are glossy on the upper surface. The tree produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that are fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release smooth, rounded seeds.

The wood of this species is valued for its fine grain and durability, making it useful in furniture and carving.
