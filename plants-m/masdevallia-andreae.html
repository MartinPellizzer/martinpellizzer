Masdevallia andreae

Masdevallia andreae, commonly known as the Andean orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, often colorful flower structure. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees without deriving nutrients from them. Its leaves are usually elongated and fleshy, adapted to store water in its humid, high-altitude habitat.

Masdevallia andreae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
