Mimosa kitrokala

Mimosa kitrokala, commonly known as the silky mimosa, is a flowering plant native to the eastern and southern regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and often compound leaves. The plant is recognized for its delicate, fern-like leaves that fold shut when touched, a response triggered by the movement of water within its cells. Its small, yellow flowers bloom in clusters, adding a subtle ornamental value to the plant.

Mimosa kitrokala typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight.
