Marattia elegans

Marattia elegans, commonly known as the elegant marattia, is a fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, feather-like fronds that can reach lengths of up to two meters. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Marattiaceae and is noted for its complex and ornate frond structure.
