Myrcia bella

Myrcia bella, commonly known as the Brazilian beauty or white myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white or pale pink flowers. The plant has elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green, often with a waxy texture. It produces small, dark purple berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Myrcia bella is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its pleasant fragrance and attractive appearance.
