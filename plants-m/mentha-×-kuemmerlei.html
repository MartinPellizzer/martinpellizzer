Mentha × kuemmerlei

Mentha × kuemmerlei, commonly known as the German mint, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Mentha × piperita (peppermint) and Mentha spicata (spearmint).

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stems. Mentha × kuemmerlei is native to Europe and is widely cultivated for its aromatic leaves used in culinary and medicinal applications.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
