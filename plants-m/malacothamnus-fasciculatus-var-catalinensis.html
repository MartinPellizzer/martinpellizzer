Malacothamnus fasciculatus var. catalinensis

Malacothamnus fasciculatus var. catalinensis, commonly known as Catalina goldenbush, is a shrub native to the Channel Islands of California.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has small, narrow leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal scrub habitats.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and its role in supporting local pollinators.
