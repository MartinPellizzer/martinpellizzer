Machaerium pilosum

Machaerium pilosum, commonly known as hairy machaerium, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its feathery, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or grassland habitats.

Machaerium pilosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
