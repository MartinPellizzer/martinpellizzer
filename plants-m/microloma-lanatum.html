Microloma lanatum

Microloma lanatum, commonly known as the silky microloma, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate in shape and have a velvety texture due to the dense covering of fine hairs.

Microloma lanatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
