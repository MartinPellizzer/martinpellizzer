Malesherbia densiflora

Malesherbia densiflora, commonly known as the dense-flowered malesherbia, is a species native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It is a shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of pink to purple. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in a fine, silky hair, which helps reduce water loss.

Malesherbia densiflora is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
