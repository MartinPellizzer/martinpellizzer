Mendoncia tomentosa

Mendoncia tomentosa, commonly known as the wooly mendoncia, is a flowering plant native to the high Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, soft, white or grayish pubescence covering its stems and leaves, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
