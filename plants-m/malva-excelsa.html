Malva excelsa

Malva excelsa, commonly known as the tall mallows, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from pink to white, often with a distinctive red or purple throat. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are hairy and have a velvety texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or dry environments.

Malva excelsa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
