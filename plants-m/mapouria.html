Mapouria

Mapouria, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that come in various colors such as yellow, orange, and red. The plant typically grows in shrubland and grassland habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Mapouria is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant blooms and drought tolerance.

Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
