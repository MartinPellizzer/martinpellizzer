Macrosiphonia verticillata var. peduncularis

Macrosiphonia verticillata var. peduncularis, commonly known as the creeping bluemink, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has opposite, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland restoration efforts.
