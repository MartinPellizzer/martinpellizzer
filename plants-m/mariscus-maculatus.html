Mariscus maculatus

Mariscus maculatus, commonly known as the spotted mariscus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in umbels. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
