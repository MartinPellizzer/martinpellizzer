Mikania ruiziana var. lehmanniana

Mikania ruiziana var. lehmanniana, commonly known as the Lehmann's Mikania, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rapid growth and slender, hairy stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by fluffy, white seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its aggressive growth, it can become invasive in certain areas, making it both ecologically significant and a potential weed.
