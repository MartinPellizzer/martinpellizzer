Megalastrum microsorum

Megalastrum microsorum, commonly known as the dwarf water starwort, is a small aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shallow, slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and streams. The plant has finely divided, ribbon-like leaves that are often submerged, contributing to its delicate appearance. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually submerged or floating, followed by small, round fruits.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
