Masdevallia triquetra

Masdevallia triquetra, commonly known as the three-lobed orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean region of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, sympodial growth habit and its striking, three-lobed petals and sepals. The plant typically produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and delicate beauty, it is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
