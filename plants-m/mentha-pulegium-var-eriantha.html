Mentha pulegium var. eriantha

Mentha pulegium var. eriantha, commonly known as water mint, is a variant of the water mint species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in moist environments such as stream banks and wet meadows, often forming dense colonies.

Its aromatic leaves and flowers contain essential oils, making it a plant of interest in both traditional medicine and horticulture.
