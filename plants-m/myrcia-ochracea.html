Myrcia ochracea

Myrcia ochracea, commonly known as the yellow myrtle, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white or yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are usually elliptical and have a glossy surface, while the bark is often reddish or brownish in color.

Myrcia ochracea is often found in humid, tropical forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal uses.
