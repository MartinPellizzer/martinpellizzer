Mammillaria curvispina

Mammillaria curvispina, commonly known as the hooked spine mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its globular shape and dense covering of tubercles, which are small, rounded projections. The plant produces white flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its green foliage. Its spines are curved and often appear in clusters, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This species is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.
