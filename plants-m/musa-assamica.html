Musa assamica

Musa assamica, commonly known as the Assam banana, is a species of banana native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, elongated leaves and clusters of edible, yellow-orange fruit. The plant is a herbaceous perennial, typically growing up to 3 meters in height. Its flowers are clustered in a central stem and are typically white or pale green.

Musa assamica is valued for its high yield and is commonly cultivated for both commercial and subsistence purposes.
