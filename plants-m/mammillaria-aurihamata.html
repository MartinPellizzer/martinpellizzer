Mammillaria aurihamata

Mammillaria aurihamata, commonly known as the golden-hair mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are covered in dense, white radial spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice for collectors and succulent enthusiasts.
