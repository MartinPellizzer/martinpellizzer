Myrteola nannophylla

Myrteola nannophylla, commonly known as the small-leaf myrtle, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and dark green in color. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, which contribute to its use in traditional medicine.

The species is often grown for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
