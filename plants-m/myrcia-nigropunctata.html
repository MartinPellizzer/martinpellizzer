Myrcia nigropunctata

Myrcia nigropunctata, commonly known as the black-punctured myrtle, is a species of flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a distinctive dark green color with prominent veins.

The fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds, and the plant is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
