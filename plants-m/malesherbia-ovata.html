Malesherbia ovata

Malesherbia ovata, commonly known as the oval leaf malesherbia, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in humid, tropical environments. The plant features simple, ovate leaves that are often hairy on the underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually in terminal or axillary inflorescences, and are typically yellow or greenish in color.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage.
