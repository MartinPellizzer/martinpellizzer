Miconia tachirensis

Miconia tachirensis, commonly known as the Tachira miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America, particularly Venezuela and Colombia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that are often followed by bright red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it has been introduced to other regions, though it can become invasive in some areas.
