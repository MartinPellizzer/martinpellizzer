Mentzelia thompsonii

Mentzelia thompsonii, commonly known as Thompson's blazingstar, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open habitats. The plant features showy, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in dry conditions.

Mentzelia thompsonii is known for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
