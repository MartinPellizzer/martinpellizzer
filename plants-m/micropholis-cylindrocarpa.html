Micropholis cylindrocarpa

Micropholis cylindrocarpa, commonly known as the round-fruit micropholis, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurel trees and other aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its smooth, dark-barked trunk and lance-shaped, glossy leaves. Its fruit is a small, cylindrical drupe that matures to a reddish color, containing a single seed.

This species is often found in humid, lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
