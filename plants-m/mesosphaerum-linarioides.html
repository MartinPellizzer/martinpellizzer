Mesosphaerum linarioides

Mesosphaerum linarioides, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet. The plant features slender, branching stems covered in soft, silvery-blue hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small, clustered flowers are usually white or pale blue, blooming in late spring to early summer.

Mesosphaerum linarioides thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.
