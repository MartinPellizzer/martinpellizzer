Mesembryanthemum simile

Mesembryanthemum simile, commonly known as the ice plant, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, water-storing leaves and showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant thrives in hot, arid environments and is adapted to store water in its thick, fleshy stems and leaves. It produces a unique, powdery coating on its leaves that reflects sunlight, reducing water loss.

Mesembryanthemum simile is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
