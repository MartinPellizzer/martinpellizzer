Myrtopsis calophylla

Myrtopsis calophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved myrtle, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and flowers. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. It produces small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by woody capsules containing aromatic seeds.

Myrtopsis calophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional Aboriginal medicine.
