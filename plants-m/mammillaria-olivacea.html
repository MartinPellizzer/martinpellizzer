Mammillaria olivacea

Mammillaria olivacea, commonly known as the Olive Mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, globular shape and is covered in small, white spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appearance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and container cultivation.
