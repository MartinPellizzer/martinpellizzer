Malva creeana

Malva creeana, commonly known as the Canadian mallows, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Malva creeana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
