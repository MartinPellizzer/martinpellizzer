Musa × paradisiaca var. compressa

Musa × paradisiaca var. compressa, commonly known as the dwarf banana, is a cultivar of the banana plant characterized by its compact growth habit.

It features broad, dark green leaves that are shorter and more tightly packed compared to standard banana plants. The plant produces small, clustered fruit bunches, typically containing smaller bananas than typical cultivars. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained, fertile soil.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and as a tabletop plant due to its manageable size.
