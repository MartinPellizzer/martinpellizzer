Malus sieversii var. kudrjaschevii

Malus sieversii var. kudrjaschevii, commonly known as the Kudrjaschevii apple, is a wild species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, tart apples and robust growth habits, often found in mountainous regions. This variety exhibits a wide range of leaf shapes and sizes, contributing to its ornamental value. Its genetic diversity makes it an important ancestor in the domestication of modern apple varieties.

The plant is well adapted to harsh climates, thriving in poor soils and cold temperatures.
