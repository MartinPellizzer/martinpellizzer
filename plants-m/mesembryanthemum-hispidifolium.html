Mesembryanthemum hispidifolium

Mesembryanthemum hispidifolium, commonly known as the hairy leaf ice plant, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically during the warm seasons. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick leaves and stems.

Mesembryanthemum hispidifolium is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its drought tolerance and vibrant floral display.
