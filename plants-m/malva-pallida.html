Malva pallida

Malva pallida, commonly known as the pale mallows, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers, which bloom in late summer and early fall. Its leaves are velvety and heart-shaped, often with a distinctive silver or grayish underside.

Malva pallida thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in arid environments.
