Mitopetalum angustifolium

Mitopetalum angustifolium, commonly known as narrowleaf mitopetalum, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically less than 2 inches in width. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or white in color, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
