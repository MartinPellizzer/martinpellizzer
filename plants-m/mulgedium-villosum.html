Mulgedium villosum

Mulgedium villosum, commonly known as the hairy mulgedium, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Mulgedium villosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
