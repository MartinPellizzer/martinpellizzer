Molineria involucrata

Molineria involucrata, commonly known as the silver hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the northeastern United States and southeastern Canada.

It is characterized by its fine, silvery-haired leaves and produces fluffy, pale green to yellowish seed heads. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in wetland ecosystems.

Molineria involucrata plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
