Melica stuckertii

Melica stuckertii, commonly known as Stuckert's brome, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It is adapted to harsh environmental conditions, including low temperatures and poor soil nutrients.

Melica stuckertii is of interest to botanists and ecologists due to its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
