Miconia organensis

Miconia organensis, commonly known as the organensis miconia, is a species of flowering plant in the Melastomataceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central America, particularly in the Caribbean islands. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.

Miconia organensis is known for its rapid growth and can become invasive in certain habitats.
