Marica tinctoria

Marica tinctoria, commonly known as the Mexican marigold, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow and orange flowers, which are often used in traditional celebrations and as a natural dye source. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in warm, sunny climates. Its leaves are alternate and lance-shaped, while its stems are hairy and upright.

Marica tinctoria is also valued for its medicinal properties and its role in repelling pests in agricultural settings.
