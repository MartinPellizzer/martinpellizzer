Myriophyllum siamense

Myriophyllum siamense, commonly known as the Siam watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features finely divided, feather-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, followed by tiny, buoyant fruits that aid in its dispersal. It thrives in still to slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes.

Due to its rapid growth, it can become invasive in non-native regions, impacting local ecosystems.
