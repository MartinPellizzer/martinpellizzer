Myrcia variabilis

Myrcia variabilis, commonly known as the variable myrtle, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces small, fleshy berries that are edible.

The plant is valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
