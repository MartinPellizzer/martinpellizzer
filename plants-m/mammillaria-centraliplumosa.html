Mammillaria centraliplumosa

Mammillaria centraliplumosa, commonly known as the Central Plains Hedgehog Cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of central Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are covered in dense, white to pale green spines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its growth habit is typically solitary or in clusters, and it thrives in well-draining soil with minimal water.

Due to its compact size and unique appearance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and cactus collections.
