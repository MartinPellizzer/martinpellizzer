Myrcia aguitensis

Myrcia aguitensis, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, tropical environments and is often found in forest understories. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The fruit is small, dark, and contains a single seed, making it an important part of the local ecosystem.
