Myrcia phaeoclada var. guyanensis

Myrcia phaeoclada var. guyanensis, commonly known as Guyana myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Guyana and northern Brazil. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are glossy and elliptical in shape. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences.

The fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds.
