Mammillaria fera-rubra

Mammillaria fera-rubra, commonly known as the red-hair mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in white to grayish spines and small, rounded tubercles. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardiness, it is a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts.
