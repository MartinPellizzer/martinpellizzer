Meniscium acrostichoides

Meniscium acrostichoides, commonly known as the creeping water plantain, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland and marsh environments in North America.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are often submerged or floating, adapted to aquatic or semi-aquatic conditions. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats, helping to stabilize soil in wetland areas.

Meniscium acrostichoides plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
