Myristica tuberculata

Myristica tuberculata, commonly known as the nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, woody structure and dense, glossy foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by reddish, fleshy fruits containing the aromatic seeds used for nutmeg and mace. Its botanical significance lies in its essential oils and unique chemical compounds, which contribute to its commercial and medicinal value.

The tree thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
