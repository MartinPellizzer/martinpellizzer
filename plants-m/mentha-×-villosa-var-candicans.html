Mentha × villosa var. candicans

Mentha × villosa var. candicans, commonly known as the woolly mint or hairy mint, is a hybrid plant in the mint family (Lamiaceae).

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and aromatic, menthol-like scent. This perennial herb typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are small and pale blue to white, often clustered in whorls along the stems.

The plant is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, as well as its ornamental appeal in gardens.
