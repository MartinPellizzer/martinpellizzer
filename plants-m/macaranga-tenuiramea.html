Macaranga tenuiramea

Macaranga tenuiramea, commonly known as the narrow-leaf macaranga, is a species of flowering plant in the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. The species is notable for its ability to form symbiotic relationships with certain insect species, contributing to its ecological significance.
