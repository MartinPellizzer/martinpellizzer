Meniscium cuspidatum

Meniscium cuspidatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved meniscium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, erect stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that have a slightly serrated margin. Its small, white to pale blue flowers are clustered in dense umbels, blooming in early spring.

Meniscium cuspidatum is characterized by its delicate appearance and is often found in rich, organic soils.
