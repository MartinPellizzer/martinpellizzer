Melanthium

Melanthium, commonly known as the False Solomon's Seal, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Melanthiaceae.

It consists of several species, most of which are native to eastern North America. These plants typically have grass-like leaves and produce showy, white or pale pink flowers in late spring. The flowers are arranged in umbels and are often followed by small, red berries.

Melanthium species are often found in moist, shaded woodlands and are valued for their ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
