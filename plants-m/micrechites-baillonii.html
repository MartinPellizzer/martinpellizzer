Micrechites baillonii

Micrechites baillonii, commonly known as the Baillon's rockrose, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its small, spiny stems and dense clusters of yellow flowers. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in arid and rocky environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Micrechites baillonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
