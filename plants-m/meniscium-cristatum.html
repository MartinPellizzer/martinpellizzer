Meniscium cristatum

Meniscium cristatum, commonly known as the crown vetch, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it both a useful forage plant and an invasive species in some regions.
