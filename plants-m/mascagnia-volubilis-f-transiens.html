Mascagnia volubilis f. transiens

Mascagnia volubilis f. transiens, commonly known as the Transient Mascagnia, is a climbing plant native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pink to red blossoms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

This variety is noted for its ability to thrive in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for ornamental use in gardens.
