Mitranthes gemballae

Mitranthes gemballae, commonly known as the Malawi bellflower, is a striking perennial herb native to the highlands of Malawi.

It is characterized by its tubular, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale blue to violet, often with a contrasting darker throat. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Mitranthes gemballae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
