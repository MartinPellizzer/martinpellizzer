Mimosa radula var. calycina

Mimosa radula var. calycina, commonly known as the California mimosa, is a perennial shrub native to the coastal regions of California.

It is characterized by its compound leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in late spring to early summer. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to coastal climates with mild winters and warm, dry summers.

This variety is known for its ornamental value and is often used in native plant gardens.
