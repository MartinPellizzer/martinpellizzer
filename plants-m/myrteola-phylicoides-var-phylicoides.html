Myrteola phylicoides var. phylicoides

Myrteola phylicoides var. phylicoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved myrtle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small white or pale pink flowers. It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils in arid and semi-arid regions.

This species is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
