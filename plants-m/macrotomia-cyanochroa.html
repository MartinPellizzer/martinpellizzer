Macrotomia cyanochroa

Macrotomia cyanochroa, commonly known as the blue-veined orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue-green foliage and delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and blue. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

The botanical significance of Macrotomia cyanochroa lies in its unique floral structure and its role in the ecological dynamics of its native habitat.
