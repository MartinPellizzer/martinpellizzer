Madhuca esculenta

Madhuca esculenta, commonly known as the common tamanu tree, is a tropical evergreen species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The tree bears edible fruits that contain a single large seed, which is used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil. Its roots and leaves are also utilized in various herbal remedies.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.
