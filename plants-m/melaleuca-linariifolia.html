Melaleuca linariifolia

Melaleuca linariifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf paperbark, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia, where it typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils. The plant is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark that reveals a fibrous inner layer, and its small, fragrant white or pink flowers. It produces woody capsules that contain numerous seeds, and its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs.

Melaleuca linariifolia is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
