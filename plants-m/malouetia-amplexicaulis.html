Malouetia amplexicaulis

Malouetia amplexicaulis, commonly known as the clasping vine, is a climbing plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves that often wrap around supporting structures. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by round, fleshy fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its unique growth habit and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
