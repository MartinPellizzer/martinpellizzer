Macreightia ovalifolia

Macreightia ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leaf macreightia, is a flowering plant native to the humid forests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers during the summer months. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for shaded garden environments.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
