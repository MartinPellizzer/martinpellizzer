Mesembryanthemum crispum

Mesembryanthemum crispum, commonly known as the ice plant, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a fine, waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ability to store water in its leaves allows it to survive in drought conditions, showcasing its adaptation to harsh climates.
