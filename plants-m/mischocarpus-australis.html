Mischocarpus australis

Mischocarpus australis, commonly known as the Australian holly, is a deciduous shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green, with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and forested areas.

The berries are an important food source for various birds and small mammals.
