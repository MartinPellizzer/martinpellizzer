Mentha tomentella

Mentha tomentella, commonly known as woolly mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. The plant features hairy, grayish-green leaves that are lance-shaped and have a minty aroma. Its flowers are small, tubular, and usually pink or purple, appearing in clusters.

Mentha tomentella is valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in herbal remedies.
