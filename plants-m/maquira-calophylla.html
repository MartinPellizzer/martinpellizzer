Maquira calophylla

Maquira calophylla, commonly known as the Guiana apple or yellow cacao, is a tropical tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, smooth, dark green leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers. The tree's fruit is a hard, woody capsule that contains several seeds, which are used in traditional medicine. It plays a vital role in the ecological balance of its habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.

Due to habitat loss, it is also considered a vulnerable species in some regions.
