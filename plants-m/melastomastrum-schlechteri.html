Melastomastrum schlechteri

Melastomastrum schlechteri, commonly known as the Schlechter's melastoma, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows as an understory shrub, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually opposite and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The flowers are often clustered in inflorescences and are pollinated by insects, contributing to its ecological significance in forest ecosystems.
