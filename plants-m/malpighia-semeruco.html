Malpighia semeruco

Malpighia semeruco, commonly known as the yellow granadilla, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Malpighiaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pink to red flowers. The plant produces small, yellow-orange berries that are edible and have a tart, tangy flavor. These berries are rich in vitamin C and are often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Its ornamental value also makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
