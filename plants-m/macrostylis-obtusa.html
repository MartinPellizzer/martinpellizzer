Macrostylis obtusa

Macrostylis obtusa, commonly known as the broad-styled dandelion, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, particularly China and Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and other composite flowers. The plant features a basal rosette of leaves that are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a single head, with a distinctive, broad, and bluntly pointed style.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
