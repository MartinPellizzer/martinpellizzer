Mentha hybrida

Mentha hybrida, commonly known as hybrid mint, is a hybrid species resulting from the crossbreeding of different Mentha species.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its square stems and opposite, serrated leaves. The plant produces small, pale purple flowers that bloom in whorls along the stem. Mentha hybrida is widely cultivated for its aromatic leaves used in culinary and medicinal applications.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
