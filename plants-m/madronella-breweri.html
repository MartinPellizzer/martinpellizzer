Madronella breweri

Madronella breweri, commonly known as the Arizona madrona, is a shrub or small tree native to the Sonoran Desert.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible for some animals but toxic to humans. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Madronella breweri plays an important role in desert ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
