Matricaria

Matricaria, commonly known as chamomile, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers with white centers and a strong, aromatic scent. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are finely divided and serrated, while its stems are hairy and upright.

Matricaria is widely cultivated for its medicinal properties and is used in teas and herbal remedies.
