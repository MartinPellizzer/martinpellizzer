Merremia sibirica var. trichosperma

Merremia sibirica var. trichosperma, commonly known as the Siberian morning glory, is a climbing vine native to Central Asia.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pale purple or white. The plant is characterized by its fibrous, hairy stems and its ability to thrive in temperate climates. Its fruits are capsules that contain numerous small, hairy seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its historical use in traditional medicine.
