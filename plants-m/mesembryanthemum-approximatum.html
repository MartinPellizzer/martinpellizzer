Mesembryanthemum approximatum

Mesembryanthemum approximatum, commonly known as the African daisy, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid climates. It is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.

The species is also known for its ability to store water in its thick leaves, aiding in its survival in harsh environments.
