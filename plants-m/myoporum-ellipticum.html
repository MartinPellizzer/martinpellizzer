Myoporum ellipticum

Myoporum ellipticum, commonly known as silverleaf myoporum, is a shrub native to Australia and New Zealand.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically silver-gray in color, providing a striking visual contrast. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for low-maintenance gardening.
