Menispermum flavum

Menispermum flavum, commonly known as yellow false hellebore, is a member of the Menispermaceae family.

It is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States, typically found in moist, shaded woodlands. The plant features compound leaves with a distinctive yellowish-green color and emits a strong, unpleasant odor. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, followed by orange-red berries that mature in the fall.

The plant contains toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.
