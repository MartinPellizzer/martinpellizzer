Mutisia alata

Mutisia alata, commonly known as the silverleaf mutisia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features silver-gray, hairy leaves that give it its common name, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands.

Mutisia alata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive foliage.
