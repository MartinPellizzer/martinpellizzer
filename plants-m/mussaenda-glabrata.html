Mussaenda glabrata

Mussaenda glabrata, commonly known as the smooth mussaenda, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth texture. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that are often surrounded by bracts, giving it a showy appearance. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Mussaenda glabrata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and is often used in gardens and landscapes.
