Meconopsis auriculata

Meconopsis auriculata, commonly known as the auriculate meconopsis, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, though it requires specific growing conditions to flourish.
