Meconopsis aculeata

Meconopsis aculeata, commonly known as the prickly poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers and hairy, often prickly stems and leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Meconopsis aculeata is a perennial species that produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both ecological studies and horticultural cultivation.
