Mesicera repens

Mesicera repens, commonly known as the creeping mesicera, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to moist, shaded environments in tropical regions.

It typically features slender, prostrate stems that root at the nodes, allowing it to spread across the forest floor. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered near the base of the leaves. Its leaves are usually elliptical or ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface depending on the species.

Mesicera repens thrives in mesic habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
