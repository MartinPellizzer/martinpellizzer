Mogiphanes aquatica

Mogiphanes aquatica, commonly known as the water bladdernut, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its aquatic or semi-aquatic growth habit and features showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water surface, supported by long, flexible stems. Its fruit is a three-lobed capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Mogiphanes aquatica plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
