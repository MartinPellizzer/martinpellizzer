Maranta roseopicta

Maranta roseopicta, commonly known as the red-veined arrowroot, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with distinctive red and white veins that create a striking visual contrast. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers, followed by edible tubers that are often used in cooking. It thrives in humid, shady environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Maranta roseopicta is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
