Mistyllus mutabilis

Mistyllus mutabilis, commonly known as the shifting mistflower, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the misty highlands of the Andes.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent leaves that change color in response to humidity and light conditions. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in a sequence, appearing at different times throughout the year. Its root system is highly specialized, allowing it to absorb moisture from the damp, rocky soil.

Due to its unique adaptations, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical research.
