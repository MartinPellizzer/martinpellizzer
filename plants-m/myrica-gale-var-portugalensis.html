Myrica gale var. portugalensis

Myrica gale var. portugalensis, commonly known as the Portuguese wax myrtle, is a variant of the wax myrtle species native to parts of Europe and North Africa.

It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its narrow, dark green leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces waxy, berry-like fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is typically found in coastal and heathland environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
