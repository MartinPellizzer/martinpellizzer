Muehlenbeckia chilensis var. fascicularis

Muehlenbeckia chilensis var. fascicularis, commonly known as the Chilean muhly, is a perennial flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and arid environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
