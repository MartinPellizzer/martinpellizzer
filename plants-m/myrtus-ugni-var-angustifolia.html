Myrtus ugni var. angustifolia

Myrtus ugni var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf myrtle, is a shrub native to the Andes region of South America.

It features small, dark green, oval-shaped leaves that are typically narrow and have a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, berry-like fruits. It is evergreen, with a compact growth habit and a fragrance reminiscent of eucalyptus.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
