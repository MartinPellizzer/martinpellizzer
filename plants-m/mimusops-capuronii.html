Mimusops capuronii

Mimusops capuronii, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its dense, glossy green leaves and showy yellow flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits that contain a single seed enclosed in a hard, woody capsule. It is valued for its durable, aromatic wood and is often used in traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.
