Muehlenbeckia tamnifolia var. humboldtii

Muehlenbeckia tamnifolia var. humboldtii, commonly known as the Humboldt's muhly, is a flowering plant native to South America.

The plant produces tiny, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or as a ground cover in dry landscapes.
