Machaerophorus laticarpus

Machaerophorus laticarpus, commonly known as the broad-carpus grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its robust rhizomatous growth habit and erect, leafy stems that can reach heights of up to 1 meter. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are typically lanceolate and have a coarse texture, while the roots are fibrous and contribute to soil stabilization.

Machaerophorus laticarpus is often found in grasslands and open woodlands, where it plays a role in maintaining ecosystem biodiversity.
