Madronella subserrata

Madronella subserrata, commonly known as the narrow-lobed madrona, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its grayish-bark and lance-shaped leaves with finely serrated edges. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters during the spring, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
