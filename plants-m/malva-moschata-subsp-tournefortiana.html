Malva moschata subsp. tournefortiana

Malva moschata subsp.

tournefortiana, commonly known as the Turkish mallow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It features broad, velvety leaves with a distinctive waxy coating, and its flowers are large, showy, and typically pink or purple in color.

The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, flat seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry grasslands or rocky areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been cultivated for both garden use and traditional medicinal purposes.
