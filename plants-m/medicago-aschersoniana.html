Medicago aschersoniana

Medicago aschersoniana, commonly known as the Ascherson's clover, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that produces small, pea-like flowers in shades of pink to purple. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Medicago aschersoniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
