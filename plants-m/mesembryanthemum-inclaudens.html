Mesembryanthemum inclaudens

Mesembryanthemum inclaudens, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry or ozark star, is a member of the Aizoaceae family, known for its succulent leaves and flowers.

It is native to South Africa and thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features fleshy, often blue or purple-tinted leaves that store water, an adaptation to its drought-prone habitat. Its flowers are typically small, star-shaped, and open in the morning, attracting pollinators like bees.

Mesembryanthemum inclaudens is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
