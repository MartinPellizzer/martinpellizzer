Mertensia subflabellata

Mertensia subflabellata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bluebells, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with silver, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Mertensia subflabellata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
