Montia spathulata var. exigua

Montia spathulata var. exigua, commonly known as the narrow-leafed montia, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically features small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky stream banks. Its flowering period usually occurs in late spring to early summer.

The species is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in association with other shade-tolerant flora.
