Monnina glaberrima

Monnina glaberrima, commonly known as the smooth monnina, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae, which includes plants commonly known as buckwheats. The plant typically features slender, upright stems covered with small, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. Its flowers are small, clustered in spikes, and range in color from white to pink, adding ornamental value to gardens.

Monnina glaberrima is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for xeriscaping in arid regions.
