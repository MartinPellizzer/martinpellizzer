Mecodium crispatoalatum

Mecodium crispatoalatum, commonly known as the crinkled mecodium, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that are often crinkled or wavy in appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Its unique foliage and flowering make it a desirable addition to rock gardens and xeriscapes.
