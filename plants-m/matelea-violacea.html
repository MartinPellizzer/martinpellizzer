Matelea violacea

Matelea violacea, commonly known as the violet morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, violet-blue leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, black seeds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, often found in woodland or open areas.

Matelea violacea is a member of the Convolvulaceae family, known for its climbing or trailing growth habit.
