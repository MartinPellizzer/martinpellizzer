Malus mandshurica subsp. zhukovskyi

Malus mandshurica subsp.

zhukovskyi, commonly known as the Far Eastern apple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental white flowers and red or yellow berries.

The plant typically grows in forested areas and is adapted to temperate climates. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, while its bark is smooth and grayish-brown.

This subspecies is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its spring blossoms and autumn fruit.
