Mascagnia paraguayensis

Mascagnia paraguayensis, commonly known as the Paraguay nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America, particularly Paraguay and Brazil.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and tobacco. The plant features simple, ovate leaves with a hairy surface and produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple. Its berries, which are toxic, are orange-red when mature and contain the seeds.

The plant is often found in dry, open habitats and is adapted to withstand periods of drought.
