Michelia kisopa var. walkeri

Michelia kisopa var. walkeri, commonly known as the fragrant mountain magnolia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Magnoliaceae family and is characterized by its showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments and has glossy, dark green leaves. Its flowers emit a sweet, spicy fragrance, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
