Monadenium kundelunguense

Monadenium kundelunguense, commonly known as the Kundelungu cactus, is a rare and striking member of the Cactaceae family native to the Democratic Republic of the Congo.

This plant is characterized by its spiny, branching stems that store water, allowing it to survive in arid environments. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, emerging from areoles, and are often fragrant. The plant has a slow growth rate and is adapted to the unique ecological conditions of its habitat.

Due to its rarity and specific requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
