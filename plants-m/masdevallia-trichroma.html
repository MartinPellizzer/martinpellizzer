Masdevallia trichroma

Masdevallia trichroma, commonly known as the Three-Colored Masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its strikingly colorful flowers, which typically feature a combination of red, yellow, and green hues. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees without deriving nutrients from them. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that help it thrive in high-altitude, humid environments.

Masdevallia trichroma is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and adaptability to specific growing conditions.
