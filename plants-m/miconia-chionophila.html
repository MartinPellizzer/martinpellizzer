Miconia chionophila

Miconia chionophila, commonly known as the white-veined miconia, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean and northern South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that are followed by bright red berries, which are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Miconia chionophila is a member of the Melastomataceae family and is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets.

Due to its invasive potential, it is sometimes considered a weed in certain regions.
