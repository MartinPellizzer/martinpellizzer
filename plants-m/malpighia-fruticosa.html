Malpighia fruticosa

Malpighia fruticosa, commonly known as the West Indian cherry or acerola, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and small, bright red berries that are rich in vitamin C. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-white flowers that are pollinated by insects. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

The fruit is typically harvested when fully ripe, and it is valued for its nutritional content and use in various culinary and medicinal applications.
