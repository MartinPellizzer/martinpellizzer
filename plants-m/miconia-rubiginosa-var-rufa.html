Miconia rubiginosa var. rufa

Miconia rubiginosa var. rufa, commonly known as the red-stemmed miconia, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and northern South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, silver-white hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that are typically followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. Its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets make it an invasive species in many regions.

The plant's botanical features, including its hairy leaves and showy flowers, contribute to its ecological impact and ornamental appeal.
