Myrcia margarettae

Myrcia margarettae, commonly known as Margaret's myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants such as eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. It produces small, dark purple berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Myrcia margarettae is often found in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
