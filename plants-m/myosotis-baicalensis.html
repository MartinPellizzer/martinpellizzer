Myosotis baicalensis

Myosotis baicalensis, commonly known as the Baikal forget-me-not, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia, particularly in the Altai and Sayan ranges.

It is characterized by its delicate blue to purple flowers, which bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. Its flowers are arranged in clusters at the top of the stems, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Myosotis baicalensis is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance.
