Mesembryanthemum corniculatum

Mesembryanthemum corniculatum, commonly known as the "Crown of Thorns," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, ribbed stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces vibrant, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically blooming in spring and summer. It is well adapted to arid environments and thrives in full sun with minimal water.

Its unique appearance and resilience make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
