Miconia riedeliana

Miconia riedeliana, commonly known as the Riedel's miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers, which are often followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Miconia riedeliana is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

It is also notable for its potential ecological impact due to its invasive tendencies in certain regions.
