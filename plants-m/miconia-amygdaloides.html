Miconia amygdaloides

Miconia amygdaloides, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Miconia* tree, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves and showy, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain numerous seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage and floral display.

However, it can be invasive in certain regions, outcompeting native vegetation.
