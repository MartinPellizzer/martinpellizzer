Mandevilla antennacea

Mandevilla antennacea, commonly known as the spider vine, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth habit and cascading clusters of tubular, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces showy, often brightly colored blooms in shades of red, orange, and yellow. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a glossy surface.

Mandevilla antennacea is popular in ornamental horticulture for its ornate flowers and adaptability to various growing conditions.
