Malache cancellata

Malache cancellata, commonly known as the cancellated malache, is a striking member of the Malvaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its large, palmate leaves with a distinctive veining pattern. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters, adding to its ornamental appeal. Its fibrous roots and thick stems make it adaptable to various soil types, though it thrives in moist, well-drained environments.

Due to its unique appearance and growth habits, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
