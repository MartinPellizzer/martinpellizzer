Mimosa djiringa

Mimosa djiringa, commonly known as the desert mimosa, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, fern-like leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces fluffy, yellow to pale green flower heads that appear in clusters, typically during the warmer months. Mimosa djiringa is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, with deep root systems that allow it to access groundwater.

It plays an important ecological role by providing food and shelter for various desert wildlife.
