Muraltia uncinata

Muraltia uncinata, commonly known as the hooked muraltia, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy fruits resembling small berries. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Muraltia uncinata is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
