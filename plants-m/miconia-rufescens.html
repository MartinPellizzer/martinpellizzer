Miconia rufescens

Miconia rufescens, commonly known as the red-leaf miconia, is a species of flowering plant in the Melastomataceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its large, glossy leaves and showy, clustered inflorescences that range in color from white to pink. It produces small, fleshy fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is sometimes considered a weed in certain ecosystems.
