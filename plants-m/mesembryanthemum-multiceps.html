Mesembryanthemum multiceps

Mesembryanthemum multiceps, commonly known as the Cape fuchsia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family, which is characterized by its fleshy leaves and flowers that often open in response to sunlight. The plant features upright, branching stems covered with thick, woolly leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in arid conditions. Its flowers are typically pink or purple, with five petals and a distinctive central disk, blooming in spring and summer.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
