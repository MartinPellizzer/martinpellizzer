Myrcia melanosepala

Myrcia melanosepala, commonly known as the black-sepaled myrtle, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are glossy and dark green, and its small, fragrant white flowers. Myrcia melanosepala is often found in humid, tropical environments and thrives in shaded to partially shaded conditions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural use in warm climates.
