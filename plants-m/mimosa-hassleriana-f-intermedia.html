Mimosa hassleriana f. intermedia

Mimosa hassleriana f. intermedia, commonly known as the pink mimosa, is a botanical variant of the mimosa plant characterized by its distinctive pink flowers.

It is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant features bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, velvety appearance. Its flowers are small and grouped in spherical clusters, typically blooming in spring and early summer.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, fragrant blooms.
