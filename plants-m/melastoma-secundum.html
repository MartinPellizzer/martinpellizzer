Melastoma secundum

Melastoma secundum, commonly known as the red-flowered melastoma, is a species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub characterized by its small, opposite leaves and clusters of bright red or pink flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The botanical features of Melastoma secundum make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
