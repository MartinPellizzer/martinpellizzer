Metathelypteris laxa, commonly known as the loose fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Athyriaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are typically light green and have a soft texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate forests.

Metathelypteris laxa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its graceful appearance.
