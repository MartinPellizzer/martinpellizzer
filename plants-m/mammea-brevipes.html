Mammea brevipes

Mammea brevipes, commonly known as the short-stemmed mammea, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Mammea brevipes is often found in lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
