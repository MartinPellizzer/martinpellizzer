Mentha × villosa var. incanotomentosa

Mentha × villosa var. incanotomentosa, commonly known as the woolly mint, is a hybrid plant belonging to the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as woodlands or stream banks. Its flowers are small, pale blue to violet, and are arranged in whorls along the stems.

Mentha × villosa var. incanotomentosa is valued for its aromatic leaves and is sometimes used in herbal teas or as a decorative plant.
