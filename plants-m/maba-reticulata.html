Maba reticulata

Maba reticulata, commonly known as the net-veined maba, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its palmate leaves with a distinctive reticulate venation pattern. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, often surrounded by bracts. Its bark and leaves have been traditionally used in folk medicine for their purported therapeutic properties.

Maba reticulata is typically found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ecological and medicinal significance.
