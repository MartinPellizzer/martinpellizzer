Macaranga moluccana

Macaranga moluccana, commonly known as the Moluccan macaranga, is a species of flowering plant in the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia, Malaysia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

This species is often found in disturbed habitats and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
