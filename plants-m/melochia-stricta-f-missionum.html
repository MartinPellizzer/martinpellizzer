Melochia stricta f. missionum

Melochia stricta f. missionum, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a compact, trailing vine native to Mexico.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of violet and white. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as a decorative ground cover. It blooms from late spring to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its rapid growth and ornamental appeal make it a popular choice for tropical gardens and hanging baskets.
