Myrcia subrugosa

Myrcia subrugosa, commonly known as rough-leaved myrtle, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its dark green, rough-textured leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. It produces woody capsules that contain numerous small, yellowish seeds.

Myrcia subrugosa is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its aromatic properties.
