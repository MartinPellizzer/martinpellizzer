Mentha aquatica var. pseudopiperita

Mentha aquatica var. pseudopiperita, commonly known as water mint, is a variant of the water mint species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist environments such as stream banks and wet meadows. The plant has square, hairy stems and opposite, serrated leaves that are often aromatic. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pale blue or purple and bloom in late summer.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in herbal teas and as a natural remedy.
