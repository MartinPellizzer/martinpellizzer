Medicago intertexta var. ciliaris

Medicago intertexta var. ciliaris, commonly known as the narrow-leafed sainfoin, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has trifoliate leaves with narrow, elongated leaflets and is often found in dry, rocky soils. It is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability and use in forage and erosion control.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
