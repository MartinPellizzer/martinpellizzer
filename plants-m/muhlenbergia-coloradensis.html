Muhlenbergia coloradensis

Muhlenbergia coloradensis, commonly known as the Colorado muhlenbergia, is a grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, blue-green blades that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. The plant produces panicles of small, fluffy flowers that bloom in late summer, adding a delicate ornamental quality to its appearance. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

This native grass is valued for its ecological benefits, providing habitat and forage for local wildlife.
