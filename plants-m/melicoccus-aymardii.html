Melicoccus aymardii

Melicoccus aymardii, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to botanical gardens and ecological studies.
