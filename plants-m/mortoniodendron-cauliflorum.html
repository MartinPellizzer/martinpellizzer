Mortoniodendron cauliflorum

Mortoniodendron cauliflorum, commonly known as the giant water fern, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its large, floating leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter.

It is native to the Amazon basin and thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments. The plant's leaves are thick and waxy, which helps it retain moisture and float on the water's surface. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that emerge from the base of the leaves, a feature common in many caudex-bearing plants.

Mortoniodendron cauliflorum plays a vital role in its ecosystem by providing shelter for aquatic life and contributing to water filtration.
