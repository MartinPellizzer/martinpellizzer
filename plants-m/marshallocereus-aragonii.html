Marshallocereus aragonii

Marshallocereus aragonii, commonly known as the Aragon prickly pear, is a cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant produces edible fruits that are often used in local cuisines. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
