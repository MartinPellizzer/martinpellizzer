Myrsine weberbaueri

Myrsine weberbaueri, commonly known as the Weberbauer's myrsine, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrsinaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically glossy and dark green in color. It produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for some bird species.

Myrsine weberbaueri is often found in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
