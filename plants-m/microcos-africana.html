Microcos africana

Microcos africana, commonly known as the African tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy underside. The plant produces yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by elongated, woody pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savannas and woodland areas.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in medicine and cuisine.
