Myrtoleucodendron ciliosum

Myrtoleucodendron ciliosum, commonly known as the velvet-leaved myrtle, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and ability to thrive in shaded environments.

The species is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
