Maranta galanga

Maranta galanga, commonly known as the arrowroot plant, is a tropical perennial herb native to Central and South America.

It features rhizomatous growth and produces showy, colorful bracts that resemble flowers. The plant's leaves are typically large, heart-shaped, and have a velvety texture. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The underground stems, or rhizomes, are used in traditional medicine and as a starch source.
