Mutarda

Mutarda, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. Mutarda thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical name, Mutarda, reflects its historical use in herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
