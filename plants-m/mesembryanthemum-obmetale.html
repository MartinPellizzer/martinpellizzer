Mesembryanthemum obmetale

Mesembryanthemum obmetale, commonly known as the "white sand mat," is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family, commonly referred to as the ice plant family, and is characterized by its fleshy, water-storing leaves. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers that bloom in summer, often in clusters at the leaf axils. It thrives in well-drained sandy soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Mesembryanthemum obmetale is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
