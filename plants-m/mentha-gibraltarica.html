Mentha gibraltarica

Mentha gibraltarica, commonly known as Gibraltar mint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly minty scent and possesses slender, hairy stems. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly serrated edge.

Mentha gibraltarica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
