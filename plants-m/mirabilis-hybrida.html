Mirabilis hybrida

Mirabilis hybrida, commonly known as the four o'clock flower, is a tropical plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the late afternoon and close at dawn. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and produces vibrant pink, red, or white blooms. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
