Myrtus oleifolia

Myrtus oleifolia, commonly known as sweet myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, dark green leaves and produces clusters of tiny, fragrant white flowers in spring. The plant is known for its aromatic foliage, which contains essential oils with a pleasant, spicy scent. Its berries, which are typically red or black, are edible but not commonly consumed.

Myrtus oleifolia is often used in ornamental gardening and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
