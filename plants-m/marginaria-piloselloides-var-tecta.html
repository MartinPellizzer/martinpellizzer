Marginaria piloselloides var. tecta

Marginaria piloselloides var. tecta, commonly known as the hairy groundsel, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are usually hairy and alternate, with a distinctive silvery or grayish underside.

The flowers bloom in the summer, producing small yellow disc florets surrounded by pale green to white ray florets.
