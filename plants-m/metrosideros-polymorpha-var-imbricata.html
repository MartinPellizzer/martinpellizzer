Metrosideros polymorpha var. imbricata

Metrosideros polymorpha var. imbricata, commonly known as the 'Imbricata' or 'Hawaiian Christmas Bush,' is a variant of the native Hawaiian flowering plant.

It is characterized by its distinctive, overlapping leaves that give it its scientific name, "imbricata," meaning "overlapping like tiles.". The plant produces vibrant red flowers, which bloom in clusters and attract pollinators such as birds and insects. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in the understory of Hawaiian rainforests.

This variant is an important component of the island's unique flora and is often used in restoration projects due to its ecological significance.
