Mesembryanthemum aculeatum

Mesembryanthemum aculeatum, commonly known as the sharpleaf ice plant, is a succulent shrub native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, often hairy leaves that are adapted to store water, typical of plants in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange, which bloom in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its hardy nature and ability to withstand drought conditions make it a popular choice for low-maintenance landscaping.
