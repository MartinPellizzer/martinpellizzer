Medicago romanica var. sibirica

Medicago romanica var. sibirica, commonly known as Siberian clover, is a perennial leguminous plant native to temperate regions of Asia.

It features trifoliate leaves with a distinctive trifoliate shape and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, making it valuable in ecological restoration projects.

Its seeds are also used as forage for livestock.
