Mertensia stenoloba

Mertensia stenoloba, commonly known as narrowleaf bluebells, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Mertensia stenoloba is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
