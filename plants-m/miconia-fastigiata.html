Miconia fastigiata

Miconia fastigiata, commonly known as the velvetleaf miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by orange-red berries containing several seeds. Miconia fastigiata is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it an invasive species in some regions.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and a challenge for land management.
