Melandrium apricum var. oldhamianum

Melandrium apricum var. oldhamianum, commonly known as the Oldham's catchfly, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. This species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
