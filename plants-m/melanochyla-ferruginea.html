Melanochyla ferruginea

Melanochyla ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty leaf orchid, is a species of orchid native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, pale yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are fragrant and attract various pollinators, including bees and moths.

Melanochyla ferruginea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
