Manihot pentaphylla subsp. graminifolia

Manihot pentaphylla subsp.

graminifolia, commonly known as the grassleaf arrowleaf, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of South America. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and taxonomic studies.
