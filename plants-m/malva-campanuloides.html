Malva campanuloides

Malva campanuloides, commonly known as the bellflower mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, lobed leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
