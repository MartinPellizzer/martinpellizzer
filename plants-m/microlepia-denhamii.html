Microlepia denhamii

Microlepia denhamii, commonly known as the Denham's microlepia, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its small, delicate fronds that are typically less than 30 cm in length. The plant produces spores in clusters on the underside of its leaves, a feature typical of many ferns. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Microlepia denhamii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
