Millettia achtenii

Millettia achtenii, commonly known as the African senna, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces pods that contain lens-shaped seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to seasonal rainfall patterns.

Millettia achtenii is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
