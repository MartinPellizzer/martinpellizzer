Mimosa esmeraldae

Mimosa esmeraldae, commonly known as the Emerald Mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its growth habit is typically shrub-like, with multiple stems arising from the base.

The species is known for its rapid growth and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various soil types.
