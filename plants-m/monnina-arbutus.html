Monnina arbutus

Monnina arbutus, commonly known as the redroot sand verbena, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for arid landscapes.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
