Malpighia urens

Malpighia urens, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or sour sop, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible and have a tart, acidic flavor. The plant has simple, alternate leaves with a glossy surface and can grow up to 3 meters in height. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

The fruit is often used in culinary applications and has traditional medicinal uses in some cultures.
