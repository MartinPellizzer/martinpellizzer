Melia azedarach f. umbraculifera

Melia azedarach f. umbraculifera, commonly known as the Chinese sumac or umbrella tree, is a deciduous tree native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive umbrella-like arrangement of leaves, which gives it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in panicles, followed by orange-red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also noted for its ability to tolerate various soil types and environmental conditions.
