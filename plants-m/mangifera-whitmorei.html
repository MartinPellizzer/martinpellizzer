Mangifera whitmorei

Mangifera whitmorei, commonly known as the white mango, is a species of tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its large, elongated fruits with a smooth, pale yellow skin. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall, with broad, palmate leaves that are deeply lobed. Its flowers are small and clustered, and the fruit contains a single large seed surrounded by a fibrous pulp.

This species is valued for its edible fruit, which has a mild flavor and is often used in local cuisines.
