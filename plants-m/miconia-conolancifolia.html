Miconia conolancifolia

Miconia conolancifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or red miconia, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is a large shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its broad, glossy, dark green leaves and striking red or orange inflorescences. It produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy fruits.

The species is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
