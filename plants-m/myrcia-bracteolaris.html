Myrcia bracteolaris

Myrcia bracteolaris, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Due to its adaptability, it can become invasive in certain regions outside its native range.
