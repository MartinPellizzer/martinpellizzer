Macroplectrum cucullatum

Macroplectrum cucullatum, commonly known as the hooded orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, hood-like structure formed by the fused petals and sepals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, displaying a range of colors from white to pale pink.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.
