Mutisiopersea chrysophylla

Mutisiopersea chrysophylla, commonly known as the golden-leaf persimmon, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically a vibrant green, with a striking golden hue on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible fruits with a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruit, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and culinary purposes.
