Millettia maideniana

Millettia maideniana, commonly known as the Australian wattle or the yellow wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is typically shrub-like, with slender branches and small, pod-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and heathlands.

Millettia maideniana plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
