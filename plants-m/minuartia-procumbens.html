Minuartia procumbens

Minuartia procumbens, commonly known as the creeping rock-cress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of white or pink. Its ability to survive in extreme climates makes it an important component of alpine ecosystems.
