Malveopsis americana

Malveopsis americana, commonly known as the American mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers and palmately lobed leaves. The plant belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. It typically grows in wetland habitats and has a herbaceous growth habit.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland restoration efforts.
