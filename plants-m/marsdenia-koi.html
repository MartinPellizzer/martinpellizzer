Marsdenia koi

Marsdenia koi, commonly known as the Koi vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a velvety texture.

Marsdenia koi is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and as a houseplant.
