Medinilla diversifolia

Medinilla diversifolia, commonly known as the velvet tangerine.

This tropical plant is native to the Philippines and is characterized by its velvety, waxy leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The leaves are typically dark green and have a leathery texture, while the flowers are clustered in panicles and come in shades of pink to red. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil.

Medinilla diversifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and pleasant scent.
