Molina scelerata

Molina scelerata, commonly known as the silverleaf molina, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in dry, rocky environments and is often found in coastal regions.

Molina scelerata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses.
