Monocera palembanica

Monocera palembanica, commonly known as the Palembang orchid, is a rare and endemic orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pale pink. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with rhizomes that allow it to spread gradually over time. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern around the stem.

Due to habitat loss and overcollection, it is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
