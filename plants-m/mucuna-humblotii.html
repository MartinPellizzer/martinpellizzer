Mucuna humblotii

Mucuna humblotii, commonly known as the velvet bean, is a tropical legume native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves, which are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by elongated pods containing kidney-shaped seeds. Its rapid growth and nitrogen-fixing ability make it valuable in agricultural systems.

However, the seeds contain toxic alkaloids, requiring caution in cultivation and use.
