Mimulus cardinalis

Mimulus cardinalis, commonly known as the cardinal's mouth, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers with a distinctive yellow throat, which resemble a bird's beak. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and hummingbirds.

Mimulus cardinalis is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
