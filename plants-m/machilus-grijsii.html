Machilus grijsii

Machilus grijsii, commonly known as the Chinese laurustin, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and avocado trees. The plant is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves that are glossy and have a strong aromatic scent. It produces small, white, fragrant flowers followed by dark blue berries.

Machilus grijsii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional Chinese medicine.
