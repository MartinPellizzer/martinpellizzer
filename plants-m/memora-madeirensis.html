Memora madeirensis

Memora madeirensis, commonly known as the Madeiran bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Azores and Madeira islands.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant with attractive blue to violet flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves and produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are often clustered together. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Memora madeirensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its colorful display.
