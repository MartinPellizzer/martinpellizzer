Moquilea montana

Moquilea montana, commonly known as the mountain quillwort, is a small aquatic plant belonging to the plantain family (Plantaginaceae).

It is characterized by its delicate, grass-like foliage and its ability to thrive in freshwater environments. The plant typically grows in shallow, still waters such as ponds and marshes, where it can anchor itself to the substrate. Moquilea montana has a unique reproductive system, producing both male and female flowers on the same plant.

Its presence is often an indicator of clean, unpolluted water, making it an important species for ecological assessments.
