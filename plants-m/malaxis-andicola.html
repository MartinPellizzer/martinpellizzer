Malaxis andicola

Malaxis andicola, commonly known as the Andean Malaxis, is a terrestrial orchid native to the cloud forests of the Andes.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and intricate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces small, greenish-white or yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in cool, humid environments with well-drained, acidic soils.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
