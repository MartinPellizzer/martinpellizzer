Marsdenia paraguaiensis

Marsdenia paraguaiensis, commonly known as the Paraguay moonseed, is a climbing plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by orange to red berries. Due to its ornamental value and traditional uses, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and herbal practices.
