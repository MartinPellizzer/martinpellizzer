Melilotus uncinatus

Melilotus uncinatus, commonly known as goat's rue, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early summer, and it is often found in disturbed soils, meadows, and along roadsides. Its seeds are encased in a hairy, elongated pod, and the plant is known for its medicinal and forage uses.

Melilotus uncinatus is also valued in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
