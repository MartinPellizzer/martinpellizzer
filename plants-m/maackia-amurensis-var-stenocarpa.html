Maackia amurensis var. stenocarpa

Maackia amurensis var. stenocarpa, commonly known as the narrow-fruited maackia, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its flowers are small, clustered, and range in color from white to pale pink, attracting pollinators such as bees.

The fruit is a slender, dry pod that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its propagation in suitable habitats.
