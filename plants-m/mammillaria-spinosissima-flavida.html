Mammillaria spinosissima flavida

Mammillaria spinosissima flavida, commonly known as the yellow-spined hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the deserts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in white, woolly hair and bright yellow spines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.
