Mesembryanthemum spinosum

Mesembryanthemum spinosum, commonly known as the spiny carpetweed, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, spiny leaves that are adapted to store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are typically pink or yellow, blooming during the cooler morning hours. It is often found growing in rocky soils or along dry riverbeds.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens with well-draining soil.
