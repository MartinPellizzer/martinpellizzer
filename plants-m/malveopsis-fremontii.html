Malveopsis fremontii

Malveopsis fremontii, commonly known as the Fremont's mallow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is characterized by its showy, pink or purple flowers. It has heart-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.
