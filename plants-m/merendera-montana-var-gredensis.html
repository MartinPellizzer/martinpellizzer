Merendera montana var. gredensis

Merendera montana var. gredensis, commonly known as the Gredens Mountain Merendera, is a rare variant of the Merendera montana species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery pubescence.

Due to its limited distribution and unique appearance, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturalists alike.
