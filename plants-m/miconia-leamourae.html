Miconia leamourae

Miconia leamourae, commonly known as the Leamour's miconia, is a member of the Melastomataceae family, native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by birds and other animals.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens, though it can become invasive in certain environments.
