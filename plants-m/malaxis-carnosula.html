Malaxis carnosula

Malaxis carnosula, commonly known as the fat orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, succulent-like leaves and a single, showy spike of fragrant, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its flowers are often short-lived but attract various pollinators, including bees and flies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
