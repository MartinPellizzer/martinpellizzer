Marsypianthes chamaedrys

Marsypianthes chamaedrys, commonly known as the creeping rockrose, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has dark green, leathery leaves that are densely packed along its stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice for landscaping in mild climates.
