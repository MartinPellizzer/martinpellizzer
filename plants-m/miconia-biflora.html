Miconia biflora

Miconia biflora, commonly known as the two-flowered miconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are often eaten by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
