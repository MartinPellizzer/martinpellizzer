Micromeles alnifolia var. serrata

Micromeles alnifolia var. serrata, commonly known as the serrated alderleaf, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, simple leaves that are serrated along the edges, giving it its specific variety name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, winged fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
