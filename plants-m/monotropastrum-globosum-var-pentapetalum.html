Monotropastrum globosum var. pentapetalum

Monotropastrum globosum var. pentapetalum, commonly known as the five-petaled moonflower, is a rare and striking plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night, emitting a sweet scent to attract nocturnal pollinators. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in the understory of forests.
