Marsdenia Purpusiana

Marsdenia purpusiana is a unique and lesser-known flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It belongs to the Apocynaceae family, known for its diverse and often strikingly beautiful species. This plant is characterized by its woody stems, opposite leaves, and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. While not widely cultivated, it holds ecological importance in its native habitats. Its resilience and adaptability make it an interesting subject for botanists and plant enthusiasts alike.
