Myriopteris longipila

Myriopteris longipila, commonly known as the long-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically arching and gracefully curled. The plant produces small, clustered sporangia on the underside of its fronds, which are responsible for spore production. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Myriopteris longipila is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
