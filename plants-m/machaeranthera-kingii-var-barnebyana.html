Machaeranthera kingii var. barnebyana

Machaeranthera kingii var. barnebyana, commonly known as the Barneby's blazingstar, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
