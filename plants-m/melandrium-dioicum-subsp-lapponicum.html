Melandrium dioicum subsp. lapponicum

Melandrium dioicum subsp.

lapponicum, commonly known as the Lapland catchfly, is a herbaceous plant native to northern regions of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has alternate, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in open, disturbed habitats such as sandy soils and rocky areas.

The species is notable for its dioecious nature, with separate male and female plants.
