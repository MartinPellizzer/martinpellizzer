Morus italica

Morus italica, commonly known as the Italian mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fuzzy fruits. The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruits, which are consumed fresh or processed into jams and wines. It also serves as a host plant for the silkworm, making it significant in sericulture.

The tree's rapid growth and adaptability make it a valuable species for both ornamental and agricultural purposes.
