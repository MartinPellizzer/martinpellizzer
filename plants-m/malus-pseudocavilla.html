Malus pseudocavilla

Malus pseudocavilla, commonly known as the Chinese white apple, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. The tree produces small, yellowish fruits that are edible but not widely cultivated for commercial use. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, typical of many apple species.

The plant is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various soil conditions.
