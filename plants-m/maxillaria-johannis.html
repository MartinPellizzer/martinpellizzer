Maxillaria johannis

Maxillaria johannis, commonly known as the Johann's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the humid forests of Brazil.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, often with a distinctive yellow lip. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, and it prefers shady, moist environments.

Maxillaria johannis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or shade gardens.
