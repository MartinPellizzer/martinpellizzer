Mammillaria crassa

Mammillaria crassa, commonly known as the thick-ribbed hedgehog cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in prominent, rib-like structures. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It is a popular choice for rock gardens due to its compact size and ornamental appearance.

Mammillaria crassa requires well-draining soil and minimal water to thrive.
