Macodes petola

Macodes petola, commonly known as the Borneo orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia, particularly Borneo.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family, characterized by its striking, showy orchid-like flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, and it produces fragrant blooms in shades of white, pink, and purple.

Macodes petola is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
