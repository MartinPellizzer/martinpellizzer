Malus × sublobata

Malus × sublobata, commonly known as the Chinese crabapple, is a hybrid species within the rose family (Rosaceae).

It is native to China and is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring showy flowers and attractive foliage. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a serrated margin and a glossy dark green upper surface.

The fruit, a small apple-like pome, is often red or yellow and is used in traditional Chinese medicine and culinary applications.
