Matricaria australis

Matricaria australis, commonly known as the Australian chamomile, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers and finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant is often found in sandy or rocky soils, thriving in well-drained environments. It produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring and summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is sometimes used in herbal remedies due to its mild aromatic properties.
