Marrubium crassidens var. crassidens

Marrubium crassidens var. crassidens, commonly known as the broad-leaved horehound, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It features upright, branching stems that are often hairy, with lance-shaped leaves that are broad and slightly toothed. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Marrubium crassidens var. crassidens is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic effects.
