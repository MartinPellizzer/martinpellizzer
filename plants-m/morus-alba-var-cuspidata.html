Morus alba var. cuspidata

Morus alba var. cuspidata, commonly known as the Chinese mulberry, is a variant of the white mulberry native to Asia.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often lobed and have a distinctive pointed tip. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as mulberries, which are typically dark purple when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its leaves, which are used in silkworm farming.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to withstand urban environments.
