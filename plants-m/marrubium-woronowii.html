Marrubium woronowii

Marrubium woronowii, commonly known as the Crimean horehound, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in dry, rocky, or sandy soils, often in mountainous regions. The plant features upright stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture. It produces small, blue or purple flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months.

Marrubium woronowii is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly for its potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.
