Merremia geophiloides

Merremia geophiloides, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a vining herb that typically grows along the ground, with hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually blue or purple in color, blooming in the morning. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, dry seeds.

Merremia geophiloides is often found in disturbed soils and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
