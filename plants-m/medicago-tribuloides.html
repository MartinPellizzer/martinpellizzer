Medicago tribuloides

Medicago tribuloides, commonly known as goat's head, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a low-growing annual herb that typically reaches a height of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features trifoliate leaves with a hairy surface and produces small, yellow flowers that develop into distinctive, spiny pods. Its root system is capable of nitrogen fixation, making it a valuable plant in soil enrichment.

The species is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been introduced to other regions, sometimes becoming invasive.
