Mammillaria beneckei

Mammillaria beneckei, commonly known as the Mexican pincushion cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its globular shape and dense clusters of white to pale yellow spines. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10 centimeters and features a ribbed stem with areoles covered in fine, white hairs. It produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by red or orange fruits.

Mammillaria beneckei is well adapted to dry environments and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.
