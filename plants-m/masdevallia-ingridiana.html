Masdevallia ingridiana

Masdevallia ingridiana, commonly known as the Ingrid's masdevallia, is a rare and striking orchid native to the high-altitude cloud forests of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its intricate, often colorful inflorescence and unique, elongated flowers that resemble miniature orchids. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial orchid, with slender, fibrous roots and a compact, pseudobulbous stem. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that help it adapt to its humid, shaded environment.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is highly valued in botanical collections and conservation efforts.
