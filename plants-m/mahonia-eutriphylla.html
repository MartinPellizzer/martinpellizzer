Mahonia eutriphylla

Mahonia eutriphylla, commonly known as the golden mahonia, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, with leathery, dark green leaves that have a distinctive yellow or golden underside. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are followed by blue-black berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Mahonia eutriphylla is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract wildlife.
