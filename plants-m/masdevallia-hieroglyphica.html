Masdevallia hieroglyphica

Masdevallia hieroglyphica, commonly known as the Egyptian orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to the highlands of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its intricate, almost hieroglyphic-like patterns on its petals, which give it its distinctive name. The plant typically grows in cool, humid environments and requires specific conditions to thrive. Its flowers are usually pale green or white, with a contrasting red or purple lip.

It is a popular specimen among orchid enthusiasts due to its unique appearance and challenging cultivation requirements.
