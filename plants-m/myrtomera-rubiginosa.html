Myrtomera rubiginosa

Myrtomera rubiginosa, commonly known as the rusty myrtle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a characteristic reddish or rusty coloration on the underside.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
