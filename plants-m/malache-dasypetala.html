Malache dasypetala

Malache dasypetala, commonly known as the velvetleaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is often found in humid, shaded environments such as rainforests. Its leaves are typically thick and waxy, adapted to retain moisture in its native habitat.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, contributing to its ecological significance in its ecosystem.
