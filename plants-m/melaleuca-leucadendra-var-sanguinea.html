Melaleuca leucadendra var. sanguinea

Melaleuca leucadendra var. sanguinea, commonly known as the Crimson Myrtle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive crimson flowers that bloom in dense clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish bloom. It thrives in well-drained soils and is commonly found in coastal regions and sandy soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other parts of the world for horticultural purposes.
