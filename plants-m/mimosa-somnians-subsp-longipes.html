Mimosa somnians subsp. longipes

Mimosa somnians subsp.

longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed sensitive plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America. It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves that fold shut when touched, a trait known as thigmonastic response.

The plant produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and greenhouses.

Its unique morphology and sensitive behavior make it an intriguing subject for botanical study.
