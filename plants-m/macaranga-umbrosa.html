Macaranga umbrosa

Macaranga umbrosa, commonly known as the shadow fig, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by spherical, greenish fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is notable for its rapid growth and is sometimes used in reforestation efforts.
