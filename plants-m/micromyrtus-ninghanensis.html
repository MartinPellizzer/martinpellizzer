Micromyrtus ninghanensis

Micromyrtus ninghanensis, commonly known as the Ninghanensis Myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern China.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in coastal climates with high humidity.

Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a waxy coating, and it produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers during the spring and summer months.
