Musa peekelii subsp. peekelii

Musa peekelii subsp.

peekelii, commonly known as the African banana, is a tropical plant native to western and central Africa. It is a member of the Musaceae family and is characterized by its large, broad leaves and clustered inflorescences.

The plant produces edible fruits that are typically consumed raw or cooked. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruit, it is cultivated in various regions for both agricultural and horticultural purposes.
