Monotagma aurantiispathum

Monotagma aurantiispathum, commonly known as the orange-spurred orchid, is a striking tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange sepals and petals, which are adorned with distinctive red and yellow spurs. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded forest environments. Its flowers bloom in clusters, often displaying a complex and showy structure typical of the orchid family.

Botanically, it belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
