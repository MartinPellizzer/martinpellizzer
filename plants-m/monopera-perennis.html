Monopera perennis

Monopera perennis, commonly known as the everlasting flower, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant is known for its long-lasting blooms, which can persist for several months under favorable conditions. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
