Mentha hirsuta var. verticillata

Mentha hirsuta var. verticillata, commonly known as the upright hedge mint, is a variant of the hedge mint species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its aromatic leaves and is sometimes used in herbal remedies.
