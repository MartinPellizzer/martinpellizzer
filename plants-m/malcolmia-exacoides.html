Malcolmia exacoides

Malcolmia exacoides, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Malcolmia exacoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
