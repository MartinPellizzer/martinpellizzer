Macaranga quadriglandulosa var. genuina

Macaranga quadriglandulosa var. genuina, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Indian rubber plant, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and prominent veins. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are often covered in fine, sticky hairs, which contribute to its distinctive appearance.

This species is native to Southeast Asia and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
