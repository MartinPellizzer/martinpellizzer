Millina hispidula

Millina hispidula, commonly known as the hairy millina, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the wetlands and marshes of Southeast Asia.

It features delicate, grass-like leaves that are covered in fine, hairy textures, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in swampy or boggy habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological niche, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
