Marcania

Marcania, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Marcania is native to parts of Europe and Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
