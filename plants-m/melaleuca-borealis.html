Melaleuca borealis

Melaleuca borealis, commonly known as the northern tea tree, is a small evergreen shrub native to the northern regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish bloom. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically ranging in color from white to pale pink. Melaleuca borealis is adapted to dry, well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
