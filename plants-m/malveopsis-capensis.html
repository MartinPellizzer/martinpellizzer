Malveopsis capensis

Malveopsis capensis, commonly known as the Cape mallow, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant is characterized by its showy, pink or red flowers that bloom in summer. It has palmately lobed leaves and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Malveopsis capensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
