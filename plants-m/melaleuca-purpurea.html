Melaleuca purpurea

Melaleuca purpurea, commonly known as the purple myrtle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal and sandy soils. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in spring and summer. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, often with a slight powdery coating.

Melaleuca purpurea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often used in gardens for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in various conditions.
