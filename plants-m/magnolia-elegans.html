Magnolia elegans

Magnolia elegans, commonly known as the silver magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves appear. The leaves are broad, ovate, and have a silvery underside, giving the plant its common name. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall and prefers well-drained, acidic soil.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks.
