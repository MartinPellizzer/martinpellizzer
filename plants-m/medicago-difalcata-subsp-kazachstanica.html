Medicago difalcata subsp. kazachstanica

Medicago difalcata subsp.

kazachstanica, commonly known as Kazakh clover, is a perennial legume native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is well adapted to arid environments. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in nutrient-poor conditions.

This subspecies is valued for its forage quality and ecological role in stabilizing soil in semi-arid regions.
