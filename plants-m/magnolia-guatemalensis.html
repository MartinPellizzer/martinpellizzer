Magnolia guatemalensis

Magnolia guatemalensis, commonly known as the Guatemalan magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the highlands of Guatemala.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate leaves that are usually hairy on the underside. Its bark is smooth and grayish, and it can grow up to 15 meters tall.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beautiful floral display.
