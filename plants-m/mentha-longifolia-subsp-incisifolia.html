Mentha longifolia subsp. incisifolia

Mentha longifolia subsp.

incisifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved mint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves with serrated margins.

The plant produces small, pale blue to violet flowers arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is often used in culinary and medicinal applications due to its aromatic properties.
