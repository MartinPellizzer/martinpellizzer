Matthiola vulgaris

Matthiola vulgaris, commonly known as wallflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, yellow, and white. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and has linear, hairy leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Matthiola vulgaris is also valued for its fragrance and is often used in ornamental gardening.
