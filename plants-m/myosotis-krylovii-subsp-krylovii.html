Myosotis krylovii subsp. krylovii

Myosotis krylovii subsp.

krylovii, commonly known as the Krylov's forget-me-not, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, blue to violet flowers with five petals.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
