Mirabilis microchlamydea

Mirabilis microchlamydea, commonly known as the dwarf four o'clock, is a species of flowering plant in the family Nyctaginaceae.

It is characterized by its small, showy flowers that open in the late afternoon and close at night, a trait known as nyctinasty. The plant is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. It has hairy leaves and produces vibrant pink or red blooms.

Mirabilis microchlamydea is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique flowering behavior and compact growth habit.
