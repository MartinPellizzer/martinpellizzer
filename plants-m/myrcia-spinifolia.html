Myrcia spinifolia

Myrcia spinifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, red berries that are often mistaken for cherries. The plant belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is known for its aromatic leaves and flowers. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in coastal areas.

Myrcia spinifolia is also valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional remedies.
