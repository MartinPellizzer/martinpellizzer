Medinilla fenicis

Medinilla fenicis, commonly known as the fire tree, is a tropical shrub native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its large, waxy, leathery leaves that are typically dark green with a reddish or purple underside. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or pink flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Medinilla fenicis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has cultural significance in some island communities.
