Melastomastrum segregatum

Melastomastrum segregatum, commonly known as the small-flowered melastoma, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are usually opposite and have a rough texture, while its fruits are capsules that release small seeds.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
