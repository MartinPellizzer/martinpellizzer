Mentha firmicaulis

Mentha firmicaulis, commonly known as the rigid mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30-60 cm, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that have a serrated edge. Its small, tubular flowers are usually purple or blue, arranged in whorls along the stem.

Mentha firmicaulis is valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
