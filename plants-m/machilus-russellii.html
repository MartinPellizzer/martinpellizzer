Machilus russellii

Machilus russellii, commonly known as Russell's woodbine, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a glossy surface, while its fruit is a small, dark berry.

Machilus russellii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitats.
