Malva geranioides

Malva geranioides, commonly known as the common mallow, is a perennial herb native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a soft, velvety texture and produces showy pink or purple flowers with five petals. The plant belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like hibiscus and cotton. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed areas.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to gardens and natural habitats.
