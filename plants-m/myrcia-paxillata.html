Myrcia paxillata

Myrcia paxillata, commonly known as the red myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, red, berry-like fruits that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded areas of forests.

Myrcia paxillata is valued for its ornamental beauty and aromatic properties.
