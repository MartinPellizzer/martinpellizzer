Melandrium longicarpum

Melandrium longicarpum, commonly known as the long-seeded catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its upright, branched stems and small, white or pinkish flowers. Its leaves are opposite, linear, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Melandrium longicarpum is often found in dry, disturbed soils and thrives in sunny environments.
