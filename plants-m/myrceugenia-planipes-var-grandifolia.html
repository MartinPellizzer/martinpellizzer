Myrceugenia planipes var. grandifolia

Myrceugenia planipes var. grandifolia, commonly known as the large-fruited myrtle, is a species native to the temperate rainforests of southern South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers. Its fruits are berries that mature to a dark purple color, often containing several seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
