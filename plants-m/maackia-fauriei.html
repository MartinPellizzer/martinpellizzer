Maackia fauriei

Maackia fauriei, commonly known as the Chinese silk tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features pinnately compound leaves with ovate to lanceolate leaflets. It produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white or pale pink, which bloom in early spring.

The bark is smooth and grayish-brown, often exfoliating to reveal a reddish inner layer.
