Mahonia × convoluta

Mahonia × convoluta, commonly known as the creeping mahonia, is a hybrid evergreen shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features dense, leathery foliage with dark green, oval-shaped leaves that have a distinctive yellow or orange margin. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers in late spring, which are followed by orange-red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in partial to full shade and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness.

Mahonia × convoluta is also valued for its ability to tolerate moist, acidic soils and its low maintenance requirements.
