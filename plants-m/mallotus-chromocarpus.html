Mallotus chromocarpus

Mallotus chromocarpus, commonly known as the red-flowered hibiscus, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers, which are typically tubular and have five lobes. The plant has alternate, simple, and often hairy leaves that are elliptical in shape. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Mallotus chromocarpus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
