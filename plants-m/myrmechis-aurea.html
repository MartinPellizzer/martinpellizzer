Myrmechis aurea

Myrmechis aurea, commonly known as the golden myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Australia.

It features dense, leathery leaves that are typically a vibrant green, often with a slight reddish or bronze hue on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by orange or red berries. It is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.

Myrmechis aurea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and seasonal flowers.
