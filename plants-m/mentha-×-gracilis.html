Mentha × gracilis

Mentha × gracilis, commonly known as slender mint, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Mentha viridis and Mentha suaveolens.

It is characterized by its upright, often branched stems and narrow, serrated leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers arranged in whorls along the stem. Mentha × gracilis is native to Europe and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.

It is valued for its aromatic leaves and is used in culinary and medicinal applications.
