Microstylis schneideri

Microstylis schneideri, commonly known as the dwarf sedge, is a small, herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and compact, cylindrical flower clusters. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands or along stream banks. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated.

Due to its delicate appearance and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its ornamental and scientific value.
