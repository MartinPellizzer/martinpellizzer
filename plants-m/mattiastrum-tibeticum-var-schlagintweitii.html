Mattiastrum tibeticum var. schlagintweitii

Mattiastrum tibeticum var. schlagintweitii, commonly known as the Tibetan arrowhead, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its arrow-shaped leaves and striking inflorescence. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky soils at elevations above 3,000 meters. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies in its natural habitat.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
