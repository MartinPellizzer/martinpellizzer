Manihot caerulescens var. pubescens

Manihot caerulescens var. pubescens, commonly known as the blue arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue-green leaves and small, inconspicuous yellow flowers. The plant produces edible tubers that are similar in texture to traditional arrowroot but have a distinct blue hue. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible roots.

Due to its unique appearance and nutritional properties, it is gaining interest in both horticultural and culinary applications.
