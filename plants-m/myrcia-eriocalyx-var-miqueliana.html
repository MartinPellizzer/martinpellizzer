Myrcia eriocalyx var. miqueliana

Myrcia eriocalyx var. miqueliana, commonly known as the Miquel's myrtle, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its small, glossy leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers that bloom in the summer. Its fruits are small, reddish berries that contain several seeds.

This species is often found in moist, shaded forest environments and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
