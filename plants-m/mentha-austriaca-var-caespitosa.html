Mentha austriaca var. caespitosa

Mentha austriaca var. caespitosa, commonly known as the alpine mint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its compact, tufted growth habit and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers cool, moist environments.

Mentha austriaca var. caespitosa is valued for its aromatic leaves and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
