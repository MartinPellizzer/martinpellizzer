Meyera capitata

Meyera capitata, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical inflorescence composed of numerous small, brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its stems are erect and somewhat woody.

Meyera capitata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
