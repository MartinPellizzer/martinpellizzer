Melastoma subtriplinervium

Melastoma subtriplinervium, commonly known as the three-veined melastoma, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with three prominent veins. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
