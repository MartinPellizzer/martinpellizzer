Maxburretia gracilis

Maxburretia gracilis, commonly known as the slender tamarind tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by edible, sour fruits resembling small tamarinds. Its botanical features make it suitable for ornamental use and in traditional medicine.

The tree thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.
