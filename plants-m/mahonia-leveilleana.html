Mahonia leveilleana

Mahonia leveilleana, commonly known as the coastal mahonia, is a shrub native to the western coast of North America.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, with leathery, dark green leaves that have a distinctive herringbone pattern. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are followed by blue-black berries. It thrives in coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils.

Mahonia leveilleana is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness.
