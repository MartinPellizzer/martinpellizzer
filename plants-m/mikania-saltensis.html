Mikania saltensis

Mikania saltensis, commonly known as the saltbush mikania, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and hairy, succulent leaves adapted to conserve water. The plant typically grows as a shrub or subshrub, often forming dense colonies in disturbed soils. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in poor, saline soils.

Mikania saltensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
