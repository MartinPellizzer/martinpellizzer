Matayba steinbachii

Matayba steinbachii, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Erythroxylum steinbachii*, is a species of flowering plant in the family Erythroxylaceae.

It is native to the Amazon Basin, particularly in Brazil, Colombia, and Peru, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range from white to pink, and its elliptical, leathery leaves. It produces small, red fruits that contain a single seed, which is used in traditional medicine.

The plant is also known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
