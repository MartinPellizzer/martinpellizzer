Maxillaria viridis var. platysepala

Maxillaria viridis var. platysepala, commonly known as the flat-sepaled green orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact, greenish-white flowers with broad, flat sepals and petals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, tropical environments. Its leaves are fleshy and dark green, adapted to store water in its native habitat.

This variety is prized for its unique floral structure and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
