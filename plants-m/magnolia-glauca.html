Magnolia glauca

Magnolia glauca, commonly known as the western magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are simple, alternate, and elliptical with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter, sometimes hairy underside. The plant is noted for its ornamental value, with its fragrant blossoms and attractive foliage making it a popular choice in gardens.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, thriving in temperate climates.
