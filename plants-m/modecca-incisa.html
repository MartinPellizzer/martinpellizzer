Modecca incisa

Modecca incisa, commonly known as the narrow-leafed modecca, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring or early summer. Modecca incisa is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting subject for study in ecological and horticultural contexts.
