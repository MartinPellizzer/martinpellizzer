Mesembryanthemum mucosum

Mesembryanthemum mucosum, commonly known as the sticky ice plant, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, often hairy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. Its unique ability to exude a sticky substance from its leaves helps deter herbivores.

Mesembryanthemum mucosum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements.
