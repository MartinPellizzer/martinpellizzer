Mentha × piperita subsp. nepetoides

Mentha × piperita subsp.

nepetoides, commonly known as catnip, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the mint family, Lamiaceae. It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often fragrant.

The plant produces small, pale purple to white flowers in whorled clusters, which are typically borne in the axils of the upper leaves. It is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

The leaves and flowering tops are used in herbal remedies and as a natural insect repellent due to their high concentration of nepetalactone.
