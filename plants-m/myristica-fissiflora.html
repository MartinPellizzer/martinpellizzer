Myristica fissiflora

Myristica fissiflora, commonly known as the fragrant nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by oval, woody fruits containing a single seed. Its most notable botanical feature is the aromatic seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its unique fragrance and medicinal properties.
