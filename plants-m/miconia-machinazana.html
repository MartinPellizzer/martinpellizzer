Miconia machinazana

Miconia machinazana, commonly known as the Machinazana miconia, is a member of the Melastomataceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to several meters in height, characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that are often borne in dense inflorescences, attracting various pollinators such as bees and birds.

Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its spread in suitable habitats.
