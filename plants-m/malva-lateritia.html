Malva lateritia

Malva lateritia, commonly known as the rock mallows, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, oval-shaped leaves and produces showy pink or purple flowers with five petals. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats. It is part of the Malvaceae family, which includes other flowering plants such as hibiscus and cotton.

Malva lateritia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
