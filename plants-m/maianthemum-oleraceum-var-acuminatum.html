Maianthemum oleraceum var. acuminatum

Maianthemum oleraceum var. acuminatum, commonly known as the sharp-pointed false lily of the valley, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
