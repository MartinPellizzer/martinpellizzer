Monnina polygaloides

Monnina polygaloides, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a bluish-green hue.

The flowers are usually pink or purple and bloom in late spring to early summer.
