Mertensia tubiflora

Mertensia tubiflora, commonly known as the purple-flowered bluebells, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that range from deep violet to blue, blooming in early spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Mertensia tubiflora is often found in woodlands and along stream banks, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
