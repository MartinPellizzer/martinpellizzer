Micromeles rhamnoides

Micromeles rhamnoides, commonly known as the white-flowered rhamnus, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its small, simple leaves and clusters of white flowers. The plant produces small, oval berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Micromeles rhamnoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
