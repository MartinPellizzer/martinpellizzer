Magnolia moto

Magnolia moto, commonly known as the Southern magnolia, is a large evergreen tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, glossy, dark green leaves and showy, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant is known for its slow growth and long lifespan, often reaching heights of 40 to 60 feet. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing a rugged appearance.

The Southern magnolia is highly valued for its ornamental beauty and is commonly used in landscaping and as a specimen tree.
