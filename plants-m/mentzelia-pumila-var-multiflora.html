Mentzelia pumila var. multiflora

Mentzelia pumila var. multiflora, commonly known as the dwarf blazingstar, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southwestern United States.

It features slender, branching stems that often lie close to the ground, adorned with small, pale yellow to greenish-yellow flowers. The plant produces numerous small, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow, hairy, and arranged alternately along the stems.

It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in desert or semi-desert environments.
