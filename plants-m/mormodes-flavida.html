Mormodes flavida

Mormodes flavida, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with a sweet scent that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Mormodes flavida is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
