Miscanthus condensatus

Miscanthus condensatus, commonly known as narrowleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in wet to mesic habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often blue-green in color, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.

Miscanthus condensatus is also known for its adaptability to various soil conditions and its role in ecological restoration projects.
