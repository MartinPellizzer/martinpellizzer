Medicago sativa var. rosea

Medicago sativa var. rosea, commonly known as the pink alfalfa, is a variety of the common alfalfa plant characterized by its distinctive pink flower clusters.

It is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in temperate climates. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. It is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches soil fertility.

Its ornamental value also makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
