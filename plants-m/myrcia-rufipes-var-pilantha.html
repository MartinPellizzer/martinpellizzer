Myrcia rufipes var. pilantha

Myrcia rufipes var. pilantha, commonly known as the red-stemmed myrtle, is a shrub native to the humid forests of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its dark reddish-brown stems and opposite, elliptical leaves with a leathery texture. Its flowers are small, clustered, and typically pink or red, while the fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its aromatic properties.
