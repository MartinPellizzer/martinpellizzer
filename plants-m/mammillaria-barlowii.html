Mammillaria barlowii

Mammillaria barlowii, commonly known as the Barlow's hedgehog cactus, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in small, white spines and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments with minimal rainfall. It is a popular choice for rock gardens due to its compact size and ornamental appearance.

Mammillaria barlowii is also known for its ability to produce offsets, which can be propagated to create new plants.
