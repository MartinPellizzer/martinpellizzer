Myrcia guatemalensis

Myrcia guatemalensis, commonly known as Guatemalan myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Central America, particularly Guatemala, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are dark green, leathery, and have a strong, spicy scent, while its flowers are usually white or pale pink.

Myrcia guatemalensis is often found in humid, shaded environments within tropical and subtropical regions.
