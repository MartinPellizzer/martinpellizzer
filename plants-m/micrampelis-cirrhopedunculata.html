Micrampelis cirrhopedunculata

Micrampelis cirrhopedunculata, commonly known as the thread-stemmed orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, intricate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pale pink. The plant has a unique growth habit, often forming dense clusters along tree trunks or branches in its natural habitat. Its leaves are narrow and strap-like, adapted for efficient photosynthesis in low-light conditions.

Botanically, it is noted for its complex floral structure and specialized pollination mechanisms, making it a subject of interest in both horticulture and conservation efforts.
