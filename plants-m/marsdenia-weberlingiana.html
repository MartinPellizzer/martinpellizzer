Marsdenia weberlingiana

Marsdenia weberlingiana, commonly known as the Weber's Marsdenia, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by follicles containing seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
