Manilkara zenkeri

Manilkara zenkeri, commonly known as the wild sapodilla, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia and northern Australia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces a fruit with a thick, starchy pulp that is edible and has a sweet, creamy texture. Its latex, known as chicle, was historically used in the production of chewing gum.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitats.
