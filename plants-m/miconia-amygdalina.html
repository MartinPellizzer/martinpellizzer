Miconia amygdalina

Miconia amygdalina, commonly known as the black mangosteen, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces edible fruits that are typically purple or black when mature, with a sweet and tangy flavor. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and fruit, it is cultivated in various tropical regions for both commercial and horticultural purposes.
