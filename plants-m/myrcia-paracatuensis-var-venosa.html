Myrcia paracatuensis var. venosa

Myrcia paracatuensis var. venosa, commonly known as the velvet myrtle, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer seasons. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
