Myriantheia oppositifolia

Myriantheia oppositifolia, commonly known as the opposite-leaved myrianthea, is a flowering plant native to specific regions in Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite leaf arrangement, where leaves grow in pairs along the stem. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. Its botanical structure includes simple, alternate leaves and a branching inflorescence.

It is often found in moist, shaded habitats within tropical forests.
