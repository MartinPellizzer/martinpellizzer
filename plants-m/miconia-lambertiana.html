Miconia lambertiana

Miconia lambertiana, commonly known as the Lambert's miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
