Margaretta rosea subsp. occidentalis

Margaretta rosea subsp.

occidentalis, commonly known as the western margaret rose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its showy, semi-double pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
