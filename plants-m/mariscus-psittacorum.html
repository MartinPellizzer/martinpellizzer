Mariscus psittacorum

Mariscus psittacorum, commonly known as the parrot's beak, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
