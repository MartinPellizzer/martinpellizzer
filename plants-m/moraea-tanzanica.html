Moraea tanzanica

Moraea tanzanica, commonly known as the Tanzanian moraea, is a striking perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family, which includes many ornamental and medicinal plants. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to purple. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Moraea tanzanica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
