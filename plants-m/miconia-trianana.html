Miconia trianana

Miconia trianana, commonly known as the Brazilian starapple, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant has large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically green on top and lighter in color underneath. It produces small, white flowers that are clustered together, followed by edible, round fruits resembling small apples.

The botanical features of Miconia trianana make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
