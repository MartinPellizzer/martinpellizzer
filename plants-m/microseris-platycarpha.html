Microseris platycarpha

Microseris platycarpha, commonly known as the broad-fruited wintercress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is a biennial or short-lived perennial herb that typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as meadows, marshes, and streambanks. The plant has basal and cauline leaves that are pinnately lobed, and its flowers are small, yellow, and arranged in racemes. The fruit is a short, cylindrical silique containing several seeds.

It is native to North America and is often found in the eastern and central United States.
