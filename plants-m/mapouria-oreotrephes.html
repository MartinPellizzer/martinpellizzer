Mapouria oreotrephes

Mapouria oreotrephes, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are opposite and often trifoliate, and its showy, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically green when mature, turning red upon ripening. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
