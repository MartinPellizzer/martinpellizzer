Mammillaria rhodantha var. crassispina

Mammillaria rhodantha var. crassispina, commonly known as the thick-spined red globular cactus, is a compact, globular cactus native to Mexico.

It features a dense covering of white to pale pink spines, which are typically thick and robust, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fleshy, ribbed stems store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

It is a popular choice for rock gardens and container cultivation due to its attractive appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
