Mimulus floribundus var. geniculatus

Mimulus floribundus var. geniculatus, commonly known as the twisted yellow monkeyflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features slender, branching stems that often twist and curl, giving it a unique, ornamental appearance. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and slightly succulent.
