Malpighia roigiana

Malpighia roigiana, commonly known as the red mamey or red mamey sapote, is a tropical fruit-bearing shrub native to Central America.

It belongs to the Malpighiaceae family and is characterized by its small, bright red berries that resemble cherries. The plant produces small, lobed leaves and has a woody structure with thorny stems. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.

The fruit is prized for its sweet, tangy flavor and is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
