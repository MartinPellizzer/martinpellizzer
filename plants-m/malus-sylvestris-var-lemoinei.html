Malus sylvestris var. lemoinei

Malus sylvestris var. lemoinei, commonly known as the Lemoine crabapple, is a compact, ornamental tree native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink, which bloom in early spring. The tree produces small, tart fruits that are often used in jams and preserves. Its foliage turns a vibrant yellow in autumn, adding seasonal interest.

It is well-suited for small gardens and is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness.
