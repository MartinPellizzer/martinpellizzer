Morsacanthus nemoralis

Morsacanthus nemoralis, commonly known as the woodland morsacanthus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a smooth, waxy surface and small, clustered white flowers that bloom in early summer. Its root system is fibrous and adapted for nutrient absorption in poor soil conditions.

Morsacanthus nemoralis plays a role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining ecological balance in its habitat.
