Mertensia ciliata var. latiloba

Mertensia ciliata var. latiloba, commonly known as the broad-leaf bluebell, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features lance-shaped leaves with hairy margins and produces striking violet-blue flowers in early spring. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. Its foliage is often covered in fine, silky hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental value and ecological benefits in woodland gardens.
