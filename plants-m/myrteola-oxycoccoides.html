Myrteola oxycoccoides

Myrteola oxycoccoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved myrtle, is a shrub native to parts of Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes well-known plants like eucalyptus and tea tree. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, white or pinkish flowers. It is often found in coastal or sandy soils and is adapted to arid conditions.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental appearance make it a valued plant in horticulture.
