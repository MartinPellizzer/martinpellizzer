Melilotus sulcatus

Melilotus sulcatus, commonly known as the scabious melilot, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun. Its leaves are trifoliate, meaning they consist of three leaflets, and the stems are often hairy.

Melilotus sulcatus is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
