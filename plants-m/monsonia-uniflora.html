Monsonia uniflora

Monsonia uniflora, commonly known as the single-flower monsonia, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces a single, showy flower per stem, typically in shades of pink or purple, which blooms during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is popular among gardeners in arid regions.
