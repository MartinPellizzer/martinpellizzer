Mussaenda angustifolia

Mussaenda angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaf Mussaenda, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.

Mussaenda angustifolia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
