Maerua duchesnei

Maerua duchesnei, commonly known as the desert hibiscus, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its woody stems and opposite leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy yellow or orange flowers, which are typically visited by bees and other pollinators. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, smooth seeds.

Maerua duchesnei is adapted to survive in harsh environments with low rainfall and high temperatures.
