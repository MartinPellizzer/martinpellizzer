Myrciaria lanceolata var. laxa

Myrciaria lanceolata var. laxa, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *cereja-brasileira*, is a shrub or small tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible and often consumed by birds. The berries have a sweet, tart flavor and are used in local cuisine and traditional medicine.

It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.
