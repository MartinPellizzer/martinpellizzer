Monstera wilsoniensis

Monstera wilsoniensis, commonly known as the Wilson's monstera, is a tropical climbing plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that develop distinctive fenestrations as they mature. The plant thrives in humid, warm environments with indirect sunlight and well-draining soil. Its aerial roots allow it to climb and spread across trees, making it an important component of the canopy ecosystem.

Due to its unique foliage, it is a popular ornamental plant in indoor gardening.
