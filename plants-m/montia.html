Montia

Montia, commonly known as common watercress, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Montiaceae.

It consists of about 100 species, predominantly found in temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. The plants are typically herbaceous, with alternate leaves that are often succulent and fleshy. They produce small, white or greenish flowers that are clustered in racemes.

Montia species are often found growing in moist, rocky soils, particularly near streams and in mountainous areas.
