Mendoncia

Mendoncia, commonly known as the Mexican moonflower, is a tropical plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night and close by dawn. The plant belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its leaves are heart-shaped and glossy, while its stems are thick and woody.

The flower's unique nocturnal blooming pattern makes it a popular subject in botanical studies and horticulture.
