Malacocarpus langsdorfii

Malacocarpus langsdorfii, commonly known as the Langsdorf's cycad, is a unique member of the Cycadaceae family.

It is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of large, feathery leaves. The plant produces cones rather than flowers, with male and female cones typically occurring on separate plants. Native to the tropical regions of South America, it thrives in humid, warm climates.

Due to its slow growth and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
