Melaleuca ryeae

Melaleuca ryeae, commonly known as the West Cape Melaleuca, is a small shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, bottle-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland heathlands. Its growth habit is typically low and spreading, with multiple stems emerging from the base.

Melaleuca ryeae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
