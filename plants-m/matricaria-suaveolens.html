Matricaria suaveolens

Matricaria suaveolens, commonly known as sweet chamomile, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center. The plant is known for its aromatic foliage and is often used in herbal teas due to its calming properties. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal cultivation.

Matricaria suaveolens is also valued for its essential oils, which are used in aromatherapy and natural remedies.
