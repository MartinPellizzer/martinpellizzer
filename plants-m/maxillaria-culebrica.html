Maxillaria culebrica

Maxillaria culebrica, commonly known as the serpent orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, snake-like appearance, with long, narrow leaves and pendulous, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with intricate patterns that mimic the scales of a serpent.

Maxillaria culebrica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses or tropical gardens.
