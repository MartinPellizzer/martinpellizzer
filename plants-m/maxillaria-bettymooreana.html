Maxillaria bettymooreana

Maxillaria bettymooreana, commonly known as the Bettemore orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the humid lowland forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of pale yellow to greenish-white blooms with a distinctive red or purple lip. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Maxillaria bettymooreana is appreciated by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and unique floral structure.
