Matricaria chamomilla f. suaveolens

Matricaria chamomilla f. suaveolens, commonly known as sweet chamomile, is a cultivar of the chamomile plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feather-like leaves and small, daisy-like yellow flowers with white centers. The plant is often grown for its aromatic properties, with the flowers used in teas and herbal remedies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, typically reaching a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters.

Its essential oil, rich in chamazulene, is valued for its calming and anti-inflammatory effects.
