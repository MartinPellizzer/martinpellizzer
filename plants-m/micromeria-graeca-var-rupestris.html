Micromeria graeca var. rupestris

Micromeria graeca var. rupestris, commonly known as the Greek rock rose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has aromatic, glandular leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its botanical features make it a hardy and drought-tolerant species suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
