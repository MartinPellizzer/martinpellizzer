Myrceugenia cambessedesiana

Myrceugenia cambessedesiana, commonly known as the Magdalena myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the Andean region of Colombia and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in montane forests. It produces small, edible fruits that are an important food source for local wildlife.
