Macaranga bailloniana

Macaranga bailloniana, commonly known as the Baillon's makara, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, simple, and often hairy leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in cyathia, a feature common in the Euphorbiaceae family. Its latex is milky and can be used in traditional medicine.

The species is also valued for its rapid growth, making it useful in reforestation efforts.
