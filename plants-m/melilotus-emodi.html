Melilotus emodi

Melilotus emodi, commonly known as Himalayan sweet clover, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows and rocky slopes.

Melilotus emodi is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
