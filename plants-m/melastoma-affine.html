Melastoma affine

Melastoma affine, commonly known as the small-flowered melastoma, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Melastomataceae and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and it often grows in moist, shaded environments. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Melastoma affine is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
