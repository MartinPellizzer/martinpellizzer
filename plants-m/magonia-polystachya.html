Magonia polystachya

Magonia polystachya, commonly known as the red-flowered poinciana, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, feathery inflorescences that display bright red or orange hues. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or pea family. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Magonia polystachya is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in warm, sunny climates with well-drained soil.
