Merremia hastifolia

Merremia hastifolia, commonly known as the arrowleaf morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive arrowhead base, and its flowers are typically trumpet-shaped, ranging in color from white to purple. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along forest edges.

Botanically, it belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, characterized by its twining stems and showy flowers.
