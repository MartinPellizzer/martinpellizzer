Mystacidium phalaenophorum

Mystacidium phalaenophorum, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and elusive epiphytic plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, delicate appearance with pale green or white flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions. The plant lacks true leaves and instead relies on its roots for nutrient absorption from the air and surrounding substrate. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on tree trunks or branches.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a protected species in many regions.
