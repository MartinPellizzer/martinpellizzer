Mattia columnae

Mattia columnae, commonly known as the columnar cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant has spiny areoles and produces yellow flowers during the warmer months. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

Mattia columnae is adapted to survive in extreme conditions with minimal water availability.
