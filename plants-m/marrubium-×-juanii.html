Marrubium × juanii

Marrubium × juanii, commonly known as Juan's horehound, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Marrubium vulgare and Marrubium pulchellum.

It is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and spikes of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or limestone habitats. It is valued for its medicinal properties, with a history of use in traditional remedies for respiratory and digestive ailments.

The hybrid exhibits traits from both parent species, making it a unique example of natural genetic variation in the Marrubium genus.
