Manilkara dawei

Manilkara dawei, commonly known as the Chinese tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and large, leathery leaves. The tree produces clusters of yellowish flowers followed by fruits that are typically round or oval in shape. Its fruits, which contain a single seed surrounded by a fibrous pulp, are used in traditional medicine and as a source of tannins.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
