Medinilla suberosa

Medinilla suberosa, commonly known as the velvet tangerine.

It is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers. The leaves are typically dark green and leathery, while the flowers range in color from white to pink and are often described as resembling tangerines. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

The plant is also valued for its ability to produce small, edible fruits that have a citrus-like flavor.
