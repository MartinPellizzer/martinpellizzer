Mariscus dietrichiae subsp. brevibracteatus

Mariscus dietrichiae subsp.

brevibracteatus, commonly known as the short-bracted mariscus, is a rare and specialized plant species native to specific regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its compact, succulent growth form and thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or orange, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xerophytic gardens.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
