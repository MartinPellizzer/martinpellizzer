Mentha × galeopsifolia var. subalpina

Mentha × galeopsifolia var. subalpina, commonly known as alpine mint, is a hybrid mint species native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves and small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their cooling and digestive properties.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators in high-altitude environments.
