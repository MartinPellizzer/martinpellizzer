Macrotyloma sar-garhwalensis

Macrotyloma sar-garhwalensis, commonly known as the Garhwal clusterbean, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan region of India.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. The plant typically grows in rocky and mountainous areas, thriving in temperate climates with well-drained soil. Its leaves are trifoliate, and it produces small white or pale yellow flowers.

This species is valued for its nutritional seeds, which are rich in protein and used in local cuisines.
