Melica virgata

Melica virgata, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray blades and tufted inflorescences that resemble a small, rounded spike. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

Melica virgata is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
