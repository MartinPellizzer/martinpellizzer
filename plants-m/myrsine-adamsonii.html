Myrsine adamsonii

Myrsine adamsonii, commonly known as the African myrsine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Myrsinaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate or elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by small, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Myrsine adamsonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some local communities.
