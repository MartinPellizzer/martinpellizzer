Melicope vitiensis

Melicope vitiensis, commonly known as the Vitiensis Mahogany, is a small evergreen tree native to the Pacific Islands, particularly Vanuatu.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus and other aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and lance-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. It produces small, white flowers followed by orange-colored fruits that contain several seeds.

Melicope vitiensis is valued for its timber, which is durable and used in traditional crafts and construction.
