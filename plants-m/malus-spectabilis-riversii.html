Malus spectabilis riversii

Malus spectabilis riversii, commonly known as the River's Beauty apple tree, is a deciduous species native to China.

It is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring showy white to pink flowers that bloom in spring. The tree produces small, edible apples that are typically harvested in the fall. Its foliage turns vibrant shades of red and yellow in autumn, adding to its aesthetic appeal.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
