Millotia depauperata

Millotia depauperata, commonly known as the poor man's milkweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for various garden environments.

Millotia depauperata is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
