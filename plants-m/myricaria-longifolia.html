Myricaria longifolia

Myricaria longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf myricaria, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in panicle-like inflorescences, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its root system is extensive and deep, allowing it to access water from lower soil layers.

Myricaria longifolia is often found in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperature fluctuations and limited moisture.
