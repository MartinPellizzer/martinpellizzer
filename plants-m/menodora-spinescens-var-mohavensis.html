Menodora spinescens var. mohavensis

Menodora spinescens var. mohavensis, commonly known as the Mohave devil's backbone, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that store water, enabling it to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, which help reduce water loss and deter herbivores.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it well adapted to desert ecosystems.
