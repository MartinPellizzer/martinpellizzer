Mercurialis tomentosa var. pubescens

Mercurialis tomentosa var. pubescens, commonly known as the woolly mercury.

It is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia, characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

The species is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize open spaces.
