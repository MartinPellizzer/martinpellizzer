Marginaria arguta

Marginaria arguta, commonly known as the slender sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-like growth habit and small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in dry environments. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in desert scrub and rocky habitats.

Marginaria arguta plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
