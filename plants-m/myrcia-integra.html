Myrcia integra

Myrcia integra, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in a variety of soil types.

Myrcia integra is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other parts of the world.
