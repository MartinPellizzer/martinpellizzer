Madhuca ochracea

Madhuca ochracea, commonly known as the yellow buttercup tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish-brown bark and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody fruits containing single seeds. It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal uses.

The tree also plays a role in supporting local biodiversity due to its habitat and nectar-rich flowers.
