Machilus philippinensis

Machilus philippinensis, commonly known as the Philippine mahogany or Philippine lauan, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly the Philippines.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, dark blue to black berries that contain one or two seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland to mid-elevation forests.

Due to its valuable timber, it is also considered an important species in ecological and economic contexts.
