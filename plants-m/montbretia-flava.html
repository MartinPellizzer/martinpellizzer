Montbretia flava

Montbretia flava, commonly known as the golden ray, is a striking perennial plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the genus Crocosmia and is characterized by its vibrant orange-red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features strap-shaped, sword-like leaves that are dark green and glossy. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Montbretia flava is also known for its ability to naturalize and spread rapidly, often forming dense clumps.
