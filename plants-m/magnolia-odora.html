Magnolia odora

Magnolia odora, commonly known as the sweet-scented magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white to pale pink flowers that emit a fragrant, honey-like aroma. The plant's leaves are broad, ovate, and have a smooth, dark green surface. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

The tree is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and pleasant fragrance.
