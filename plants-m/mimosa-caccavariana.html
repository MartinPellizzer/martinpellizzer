Mimosa caccavariana

Mimosa caccavariana, commonly known as the pink mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy pink or red flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Mimosa caccavariana is also known for its sensitive leaves that fold shut when touched, a trait shared with other mimosa species.
