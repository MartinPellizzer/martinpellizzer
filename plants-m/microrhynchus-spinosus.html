Microrhynchus spinosus

Microrhynchus spinosus, commonly known as the spiny burrweed, is a small, annual plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and small, inconspicuous flowers that typically bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, spiny fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and other open areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is considered a weed in some agricultural regions.
