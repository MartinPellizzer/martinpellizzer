Marianthus ringens

Marianthus ringens, commonly known as the fragrant bellflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 3 feet. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, velvety texture.

Marianthus ringens thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
