Myrcia densiflora

Myrcia densiflora, commonly known as the dense-flowered myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically red when ripe, which are an important food source for various birds and mammals. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Myrcia densiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses among indigenous communities.
