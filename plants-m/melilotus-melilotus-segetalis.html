Melilotus melilotus-segetalis

Melilotus melilotus-segetalis, commonly known as yellow sweet clover, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in spherical heads, and it produces trifoliate leaves with three oval-shaped leaflets. This plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been introduced to other regions, including North America. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and wastelands.

Melilotus melilotus-segetalis is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil, and it has been used in agriculture and traditional medicine.
