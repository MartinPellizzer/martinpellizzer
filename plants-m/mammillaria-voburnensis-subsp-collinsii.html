Mammillaria voburnensis subsp. collinsii

Mammillaria voburnensis subsp.

collinsii, commonly known as the Collins' nipple cactus, is a small, spineless cactus native to southern Mexico. It features a globular or cylindrical stem with white to pale green areoles and short, white bristles.

The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, adding a striking contrast to its green body. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its compact size and unique appearance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor collections.
