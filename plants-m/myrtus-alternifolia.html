Myrtus alternifolia

Myrtus alternifolia, commonly known as the common myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, dark green leaves with a leathery texture and a distinctive, spicy scent. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits. Its leaves and berries are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

Myrtus alternifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitat.
