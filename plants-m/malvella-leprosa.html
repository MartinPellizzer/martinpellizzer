Malvella leprosa

Malvella leprosa, commonly known as the woolly mallow, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant is characterized by its hairy, grayish stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. It produces showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer.

Malvella leprosa is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and unique appearance.
