Melanolepis multiglandulosa var. pendula

Melanolepis multiglandulosa var. pendula, commonly known as the hanging mistflower, is a striking plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its drooping clusters of small, white, mist-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are covered in fine, glandular hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and wetland restoration projects.
