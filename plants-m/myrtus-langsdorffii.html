Myrtus langsdorffii

Myrtus langsdorffii, commonly known as the Cape myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It features narrow, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement and have a slightly aromatic scent. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers in clusters during the winter months. These flowers are followed by small, spherical berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

Myrtus langsdorffii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited to coastal and sandy soils.
