Myrtus formosa

Myrtus formosa, commonly known as the woolly myrtle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring dense, silver-gray foliage with a soft, woolly texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are evergreen and have a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed.

Myrtus formosa is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
