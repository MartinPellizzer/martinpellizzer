Memecylon capitellatum var. tinctorium

Memecylon capitellatum var. tinctorium, commonly known as the red-flowered memecylon, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant produces striking red or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, adding ornamental value to gardens. Its leaves are typically elliptical and have a smooth texture, while its stems are woody and often covered in fine hairs.

This species is also valued for its potential medicinal properties and use in traditional herbal remedies.
