Muscarella marginata

Muscarella marginata, commonly known as the marginated muscari, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its compact, bulbous form and dense clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces striking blue or violet blooms that appear in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and typically appear after the flowers have faded.

Muscarella marginata is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
