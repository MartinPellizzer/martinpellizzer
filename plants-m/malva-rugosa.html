Malva rugosa

Malva rugosa, commonly known as the woolly mallow, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, hairy leaves and showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its stems are often covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Malva rugosa is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its soothing properties.
