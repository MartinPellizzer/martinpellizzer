Melandrium hookeri

Melandrium hookeri, commonly known as the Hooker's catchfly, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features erect, branched stems covered in fine, white hairs, and produces small, clustered flowers with five petals. The plant's leaves are linear and alternate, often with a slightly hairy texture. It blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Melandrium hookeri is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in cooler, moist environments.
