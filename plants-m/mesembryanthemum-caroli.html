Mesembryanthemum caroli

Mesembryanthemum caroli, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family, known for its succulent characteristics and ability to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, round, yellow-orange fruits that resemble tiny tomatoes. Its leaves are fleshy and often covered in a fine, white powdery coating called farina.
