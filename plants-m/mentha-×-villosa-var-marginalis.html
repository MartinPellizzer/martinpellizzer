Mentha × villosa var. marginalis

Mentha × villosa var. marginalis, commonly known as the woolly mint or marginal mint, is a hybrid perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive texture and appearance. The plant produces small, pale purple to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its aromatic leaves are used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent in various culinary applications.
