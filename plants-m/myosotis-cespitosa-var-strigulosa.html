Myosotis cespitosa var. strigulosa

Myosotis cespitosa var. strigulosa, commonly known as the hairy forget-me-not, is a low-growing perennial herb native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features small, delicate blue or violet flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has a fibrous root system and forms dense, mat-like growths, often spreading through stolons. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and covered in fine, hairy texture, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along streambanks.
