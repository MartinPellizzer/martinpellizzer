Mertensia sibirica var. sibirica

Mertensia sibirica var. sibirica, commonly known as Siberian bluebells, is a perennial herb native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern Asia.

It features lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Mertensia sibirica var. sibirica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
