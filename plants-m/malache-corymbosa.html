Malache corymbosa

Malache corymbosa, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in dense, showy clusters. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its bark is smooth and often reddish in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds.
