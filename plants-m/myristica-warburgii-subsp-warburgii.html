Myristica warburgii subsp. warburgii

Myristica warburgii subsp.

warburgii, commonly known as the Nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia. It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, woody bark and broad, elliptical leaves.

The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by bright red, fleshy fruits that contain the valuable nutmeg seed. The seed, known as the nutmeg kernel, is encased in a toxic, aromatic aril called mace.

This species is economically significant for its spices and has been cultivated for centuries for its medicinal and culinary uses.
