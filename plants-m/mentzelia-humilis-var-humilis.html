Mentzelia humilis var. humilis

Mentzelia humilis var. humilis, commonly known as the low mentzelia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of less than 10 inches. The plant features slender stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-yellow, with five petals and a distinctive tubular shape.

It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.
