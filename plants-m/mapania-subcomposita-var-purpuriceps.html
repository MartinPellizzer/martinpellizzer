Mapania subcomposita var. purpuriceps

Mapania subcomposita var. purpuriceps, commonly known as the purple-headed mapania, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its most distinctive trait is the vivid purple coloration of its flower heads, which stand out against its green foliage.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
