Machaerium diadelphum

Machaerium diadelphum, commonly known as the purple machaerium, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, often purple or pink flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to several meters. Its flowers are arranged in dense, terminal clusters and are known for their ornamental value.

Machaerium diadelphum is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in soil enrichment.
