Myrcia nivea var. rosmarinifolia

Myrcia nivea var. rosmarinifolia, commonly known as the silver myrtle, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun exposure.

The leaves are used in traditional medicine and are also valued for their pleasant fragrance.
