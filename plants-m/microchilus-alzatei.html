Microchilus alzatei

Microchilus alzatei, commonly known as the Alzatea orchid, is a tropical orchid species native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and purple. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
