Monanthotaxis heterantha

Monanthotaxis heterantha, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a climbing shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has opposite, pinnately compound leaves with serrated margins, and its stems are often hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or forested areas.

The flowers emit a sweet fragrance, enhancing its role in the ecosystem and making it a popular ornamental plant.
