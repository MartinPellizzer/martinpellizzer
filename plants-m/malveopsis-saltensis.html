Malveopsis saltensis

Malveopsis saltensis, commonly known as the salt marsh mallow, is a flowering plant native to arid and saline environments.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus, and is characterized by its hairy stems and showy flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and saline soils, demonstrating a high tolerance for salt and drought conditions. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Malveopsis saltensis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils in harsh environments.
