Meum fatuum

Meum fatuum, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a rare and elusive plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a small annual herb with delicate, pale blue or white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain its seeds. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is considered a botanical curiosity.
