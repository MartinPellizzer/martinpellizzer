Morinda callicarpifolia

Morinda callicarpifolia, commonly known as the red-fruited morinda, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, often reddish stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels. Its fruit is a fleshy, bright red drupe that contains several seeds.
