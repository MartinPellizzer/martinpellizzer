Melica bulbosa var. caespitosa

Melica bulbosa var. caespitosa, commonly known as the tufted bulblet grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and the presence of bulbous structures at the base of the plant. The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, while the inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
