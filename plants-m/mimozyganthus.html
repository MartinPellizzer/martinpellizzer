Mimozyganthus

Mimozyganthus, commonly known as the African mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees in sub-Saharan Africa.

It possesses small, oval-shaped leaves and produces white or pink flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant's berries are often consumed by birds, which then disperse the seeds in new locations. Mimozyganthus has a symbiotic relationship with its host, drawing water and nutrients while providing shelter for certain insects.

Its life cycle is closely tied to the seasonal changes in its environment, making it an important component of the local ecosystem.
