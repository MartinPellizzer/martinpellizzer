Meliosma myriantha

Meliosma myriantha, commonly known as the broadleaf meliosma, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae, which includes mahogany and related trees. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white pubescence. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are typically greenish-yellow and appear in clusters, followed by small, winged fruits.

Meliosma myriantha thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland habitats.
