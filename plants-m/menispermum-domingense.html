Menispermum domingense

Menispermum domingense, commonly known as the Dominican arrowroot or Mexican arrowroot, is a climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are followed by fleshy, spiny fruits containing numerous seeds. Its roots are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
