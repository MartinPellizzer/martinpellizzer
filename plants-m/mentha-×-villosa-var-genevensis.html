Mentha × villosa var. genevensis

Mentha × villosa var. genevensis, commonly known as the Geneva mint, is a hybrid perennial herb native to Europe.

It is characterized by its fragrant, narrow leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its leaves are often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture.

It is valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in horticulture and traditional medicine.
