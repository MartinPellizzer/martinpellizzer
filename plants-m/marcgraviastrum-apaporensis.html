Marcgraviastrum apaporensis

Marcgraviastrum apaporensis, commonly known as the Apure River mistplant, is a climbing vine native to the humid lowland forests of Venezuela.

It belongs to the family Malvaceae and is characterized by its aerial roots and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale green flowers that are often hidden among its foliage. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is adapted to epiphytic growth on tree trunks and branches.
