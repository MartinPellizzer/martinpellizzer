Myrcia bipennis

Myrcia bipennis, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Myrcia bipennis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
