Miliusa sclerocarpa

Miliusa sclerocarpa, commonly known as the hard-shelled miliusa, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes cashew and poison ivy. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, red, fleshy fruits. The fruit contains a single, hard, black seed enclosed in a thick, woody sarcocarp.

It is often grown for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local cultures.
