Mimulus leibergii

Mimulus leibergii, commonly known as the yellow monkeyflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking yellow, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along streambanks and woodland edges. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Mimulus leibergii is valued in gardens for its vibrant color and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
