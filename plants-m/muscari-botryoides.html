Muscari botryoides

Muscari botryoides, commonly known as the grape hyacinth, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It produces dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, or white, resembling tiny grapes, hence its common name. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders. Its foliage consists of strap-like leaves that emerge in early spring, and it is relatively low maintenance once established.

Muscari botryoides is also valued for its ability to naturalize and spread over time, creating vibrant displays in gardens.
