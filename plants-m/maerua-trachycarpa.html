Maerua trachycarpa

Maerua trachycarpa, commonly known as the African sandthorn, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and woody, thorny stems. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in dry environments. Its fruit is a small, dry capsule that contains several seeds.

Maerua trachycarpa is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil in degraded areas.
