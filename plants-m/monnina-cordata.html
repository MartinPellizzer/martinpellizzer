Monnina cordata

Monnina cordata, commonly known as scarlet globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. The plant features palmate leaves with lobed edges, and it produces showy, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Monnina cordata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.

It is also used in traditional medicine by some indigenous communities.
