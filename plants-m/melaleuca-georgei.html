Melaleuca georgei

Melaleuca georgei, commonly known as George's tea-tree, is a small to medium-sized shrub native to the tropical regions of northern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, bottle-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces woody capsules as fruit, which contain numerous seeds dispersed by wind.

It is often found in coastal and sandy soils, and is adapted to withstand drought and salt spray.
