Milium triracemosum

Milium triracemosum, commonly known as the three-branched millet, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its compact, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, white to pale green flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth surface and a bluish-green color.

Milium triracemosum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its delicate appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
