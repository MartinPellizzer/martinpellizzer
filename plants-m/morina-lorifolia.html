Morina lorifolia

Morina lorifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf moringa, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are broadly ovate to elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry or semi-arid environments.

Morina lorifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
