Myrcia ascendens

Myrcia ascendens, commonly known as the ascending myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and fragrant, white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are glossy and elliptical, while the bark is smooth and reddish in color.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
