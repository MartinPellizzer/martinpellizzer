Mammillaria hamiltonhoytiae var. fulvaflora

Mammillaria hamiltonhoytiae var. fulvaflora, commonly known as the golden mammillaria, is a rare cactus species native to the deserts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in dense, white to yellow spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its foliage is typically pale green, and it thrives in well-drained soil with minimal water.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in controlled environments.
