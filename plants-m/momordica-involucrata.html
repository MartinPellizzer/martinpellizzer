Momordica involucrata

Momordica involucrata, commonly known as the hooded bitter gourd, is a vine species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like cucumbers and pumpkins. The plant produces distinctive fruit with a spiny, hooded shape and a bitter taste, which is often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while the stems are hairy and tend to climb.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is also used in culinary applications in some cultures.
