Macrocentrum neblinae

Macrocentrum neblinae, commonly known as the Neblinae water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, floating leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter, providing a unique habitat for aquatic life. The plant produces showy white flowers with a distinctive yellow center, which bloom during the warmer months. Its roots anchor it in shallow water, while its leaves float on the surface, facilitating photosynthesis.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
