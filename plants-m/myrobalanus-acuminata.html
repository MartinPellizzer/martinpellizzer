Myrobalanus acuminata

Myrobalanus acuminata, commonly known as the tamarind tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and compound leaves that are alternate and pinnate. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, pod-like fruits that contain sweet, sticky pulp and seeds. The botanical features of Myrobalanus acuminata make it well-suited for tropical and subtropical climates.

It is widely cultivated for its fruit, which is used in food, traditional medicine, and as a natural dye.
