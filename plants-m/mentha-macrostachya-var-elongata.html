Mentha macrostachya var. elongata

Mentha macrostachya var. elongata, commonly known as the long-stalked mint, is a perennial herb native to northern regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a serrated margin. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its aromatic leaves and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
