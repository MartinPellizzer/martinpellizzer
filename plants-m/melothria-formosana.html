Melothria formosana

Melothria formosana, commonly known as the Formosa passionfruit, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It produces small, orange-colored fruits that are typically 1 to 2 centimeters in diameter. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and emits a mild, aromatic scent. Its flowers are usually yellow with a red throat and are pollinated by insects.

The fruit is edible, with a tart flavor and a high vitamin C content.
