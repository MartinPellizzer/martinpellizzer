Myrcia rosulans

Myrcia rosulans, commonly known as the red myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. It produces red, fleshy berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine.

Myrcia rosulans is valued for its ornamental beauty and aromatic properties.
