Macrozamia concinna

Macrozamia concinna, commonly known as the slender cycad, is a unique member of the Cycadaceae family native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, unbranched trunk and a crown of feather-like leaves that can reach up to 2 meters in length. The plant produces large, cone-like structures, with the female cones typically appearing at the base of the plant. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal and inland regions with a temperate climate.

Due to its slow growth and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in some areas.
