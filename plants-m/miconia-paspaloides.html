Miconia paspaloides

Miconia paspaloides, commonly known as the false grass or silverleaf miconia, is a tropical plant belonging to the Melastomataceae family.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers that are typically clustered in terminal inflorescences. It is native to Central and South America and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
