Mirabilis austrotexana

Mirabilis austrotexana, commonly known as the Texas four o'clock, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of red, yellow, and orange, typically opening in the late afternoon. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice for gardens in arid regions.
