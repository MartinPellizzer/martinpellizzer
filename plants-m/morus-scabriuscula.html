Morus scabriuscula

Morus scabriuscula, commonly known as the rough mulberry, is a small deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its grayish-barked trunk and hairy leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine, and the tree is valued for its ornamental appeal.
