Myrcia paucantha

Myrcia paucantha, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are often red or orange in color, which are an important food source for various wildlife species. Myrcia paucantha is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.

However, it can become invasive in certain environments, posing challenges for local ecosystems.
