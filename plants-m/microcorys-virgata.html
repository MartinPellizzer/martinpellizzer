Microcorys virgata

Microcorys virgata, commonly known as the Virgin's crown, is a striking member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its unique, umbrella-like inflorescence.

Native to the eastern United States, it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its root system is fibrous and contributes to its ability to stabilize soil in wetland habitats.

Microcorys virgata is also noted for its aromatic properties, which are attributed to the presence of volatile oils in its foliage.
