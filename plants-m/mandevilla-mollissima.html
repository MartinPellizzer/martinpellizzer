Mandevilla mollissima

Mandevilla mollissima, commonly known as the velvet mandevilla, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant is a vigorous climber, often used in ornamental gardening for its showy blooms. It thrives in warm climates and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Its flowers are typically produced during the summer months, adding vibrant color to gardens and landscapes.
