Mentha × villosa var. salicetorum

Mentha × villosa var. salicetorum, commonly known as the woolly mint, is a hybrid plant that exhibits characteristics of both Mentha viridis and Mentha suaveolens.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in moist, calcareous soils. The plant features hairy, gray-green leaves and emits a strong, minty aroma. It produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in herbal remedies.
