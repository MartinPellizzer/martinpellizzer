Myriophyllum nudum

Myriophyllum nudum, commonly known as narrowleaf watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to North America.

It has slender, branching stems that are often reddish in color and bear small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces tiny, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in freshwater environments such as lakes and ponds, often forming dense mats near the water's surface.

Myriophyllum nudum plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
