Myrcia longipaniculata

Myrcia longipaniculata, commonly known as the longpanicle myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy inflorescences. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, arranged in long, branched clusters called panicles.

The leaves are glossy and evergreen, contributing to its ornamental value in tropical and subtropical gardens.
