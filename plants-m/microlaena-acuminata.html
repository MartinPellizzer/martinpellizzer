Microlaena acuminata

Microlaena acuminata, commonly known as the slender microlaena, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale blue or white flowers. It is often found in sandy or gravelly soils in open habitats such as grasslands and heathlands.

Microlaena acuminata is adapted to dry conditions and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
