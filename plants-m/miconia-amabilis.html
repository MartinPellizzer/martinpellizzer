Miconia amabilis

Miconia amabilis, commonly known as the pink miconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height, characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom throughout the year, attracting various pollinators. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns red when mature, containing numerous seeds.

Miconia amabilis is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along riverbanks.
