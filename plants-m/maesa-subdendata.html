Maesa subdendata

Maesa subdendata, commonly known as the tropical evergreen shrub, is a member of the Ericaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in humid, tropical environments. The plant features dense, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. Its flowers are small, white or pale pink, and it produces small, red berries that are edible.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
