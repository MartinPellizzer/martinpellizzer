Milleria quinqueflora var. maculata

Milleria quinqueflora var. maculata, commonly known as the spotted milleria, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with contrasting spots or streaks. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
