Mentha arvensiplicata

Mentha arvensiplicata, commonly known as field mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, giving it a fresh, minty scent.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.
