Muehlenbeckia trilobata

Muehlenbeckia trilobata, commonly known as the silverweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to South America.

It features wiry, trailing stems covered in small, silvery-white hairs that give it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are trifoliate, meaning they are divided into three leaflets, which are narrow and often slightly hairy. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color.

This plant is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
