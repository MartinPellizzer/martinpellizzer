Myrcia paracatuensis var. arenaria

Myrcia paracatuensis var. arenaria, commonly known as the arenaria myrcia, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest biome of Brazil.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, which are followed by orange to red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
