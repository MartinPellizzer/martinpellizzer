Macrosphyra longistyla

Macrosphyra longistyla, commonly known as the long-styled macrophyte, is a species of aquatic plant found in freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its long, slender leaves that are often submerged or floating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in clusters. Its roots help stabilize sediments and improve water quality by absorbing nutrients.

Macrosphyra longistyla plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
