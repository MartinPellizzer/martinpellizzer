Manisuris forficulata var. hirsuta

Manisuris forficulata var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy false dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It features basal leaves that are hairy and lanceolate, with a distinctive white underside. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

The hairy texture of its leaves and stems is a notable botanical characteristic that aids in identification.
