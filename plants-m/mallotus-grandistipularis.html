Mallotus grandistipularis

Mallotus grandistipularis, commonly known as the large-tipped mallot, is a flowering plant species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, large, acuminate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that are often surrounded by bracts. It is typically found in lowland tropical rainforests and thrives in humid, shady environments.
