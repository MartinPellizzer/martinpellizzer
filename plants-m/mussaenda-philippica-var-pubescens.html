Mussaenda philippica var. pubescens

Mussaenda philippica var. pubescens, commonly known as the velvet flower or hairy mussaenda, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that resemble clusters of small, lantern-like flowers. The leaves are typically opposite, ovate, and covered with fine, soft hairs, giving the plant a velvety texture. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a landscape plant.
