Muraltia diffusa

Muraltia diffusa, commonly known as the African olive, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant has compound leaves with stipules and produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white in color. Its fruit is a pod that contains several edible seeds, which are used in traditional cuisines.

Muraltia diffusa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
