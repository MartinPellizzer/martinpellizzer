Maclura fruticosa

Maclura fruticosa, commonly known as the yellow wood or devil's bush, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the United States.

It is characterized by its thorny branches and large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically lobed. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that give way to orange to red berries in the fall. Its bark is thick and deeply furrowed, often appearing rough and fissured.

The plant is known for its strong, aromatic scent and is sometimes used in herbal medicine.
