Morenoa grandiflora

Morenoa grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered morenoa, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, panicle-like inflorescences. The plant produces small, greenish to pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered in dense, branching clusters. It is often found in coastal and lowland areas, thriving in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
