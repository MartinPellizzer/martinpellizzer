Myrcia coumetoides

Myrcia coumetoides, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
