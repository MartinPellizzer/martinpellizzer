Miconia titanophylla

Miconia titanophylla, commonly known as the giant miconia, is a striking tropical plant native to the humid forests of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length, making it one of the largest leaf plants in the world. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that are often surrounded by showy bracts, enhancing its ornamental appeal. Miconia titanophylla thrives in warm, moist environments with high humidity and partial shade.

Due to its rapid growth and potential invasiveness, it is sometimes considered a problematic species in certain regions.
