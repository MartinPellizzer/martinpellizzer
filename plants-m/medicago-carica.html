Medicago carica

Medicago carica, commonly known as the burr medick, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces small, yellow flowers followed by distinctive, prickly seed pods. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along fences.

Medicago carica is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
