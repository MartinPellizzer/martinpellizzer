Malachium manticum

Malachium manticum, commonly known as the divination herb, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces showy blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Historically, it has been used in herbal medicine for its purported calming and healing properties.
