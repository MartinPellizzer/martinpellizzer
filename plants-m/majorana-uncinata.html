Majorana uncinata

Majorana uncinata, commonly known as wild marjoram, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and have a strong, spicy aroma.

Majorana uncinata is often found in dry, rocky soils and is valued for its medicinal and culinary uses.
