Mallotus nepalensis var. ochraceoalbidus

Mallotus nepalensis var. ochraceoalbidus, commonly known as the yellow-veined mallotus, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves with yellow veins against a green background. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are often surrounded by specialized structures called cyathia. Its bark and leaves have been traditionally used in Ayurvedic medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal due to its unique leaf patterns.
