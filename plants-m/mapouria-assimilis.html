Mapouria assimilis

Mapouria assimilis, commonly known as the African star apple, is a tropical tree native to Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with long, slender leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by yellow, star-shaped fruits. Its fruits are edible, with a sweet, juicy pulp that has a flavor similar to a mix of mango and pear.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine.
