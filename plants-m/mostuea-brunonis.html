Mostuea brunonis

Mostuea brunonis, commonly known as the black bamboo, is a striking ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black, bamboo-like stems that add a dramatic visual element to gardens. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers during the summer months, which are followed by bright red berries. Mostuea brunonis prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical climates.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for both landscape design and container gardening.
