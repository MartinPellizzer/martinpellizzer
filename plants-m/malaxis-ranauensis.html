Malaxis ranauensis

Malaxis ranauensis, commonly known as the Ranau orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. It has a sympodial growth habit, with each stem producing a single terminal inflorescence.

The species is notable for its unique floral structure and is often found in association with specific mycorrhizal fungi.
