Malus sylvestris subsp. orientalis

Malus sylvestris subsp.

orientalis, commonly known as the oriental crabapple, is a species native to parts of Asia, including China and the Himalayas. It is a deciduous tree characterized by its small, often fragrant flowers and ornamental, spiny fruits.

The leaves are alternate, ovate, and typically have serrated edges. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens.

This subspecies is closely related to the common crabapple and shares similar horticultural uses.
