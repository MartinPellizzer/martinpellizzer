Melochia venosa var. tunica

Melochia venosa var. tunica, commonly known as the velvetleaf or tunica melochia, is a tropical plant characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense, velvety pubescence.

The leaves are typically a deep green with a distinctive waxy texture, and they often have a slightly serrated margin. It produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are followed by round, fleshy fruits containing numerous seeds. This plant is native to regions of Africa and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and abandoned fields.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
