Malpighia atlantica

Malpighia atlantica, commonly known as the Brazilian wolfberry or Surinam cherries, is a small evergreen shrub native to South America.

It produces small, bright red berries that are rich in vitamin C and have a tart, tangy flavor. The plant has simple, alternate leaves with a leathery texture and dark green color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun.

The fruit is often used in culinary applications and is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
