Musa halabanensis

Musa halabanensis, commonly known as the Halaban banana, is a species native to the island of Socotra.

It is a member of the Musaceae family and is characterized by its large, elongated leaves and clustered inflorescences. The plant produces edible berries that are typically consumed raw or cooked. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting well to the unique climate of its native habitat.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
