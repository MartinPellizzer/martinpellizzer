Mertensia glaucescens var. cubense

Mertensia glaucescens var. cubense, commonly known as Cuban bluebells, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Cuba.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a distinctive blue or bluish-white waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
