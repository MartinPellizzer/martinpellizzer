Matricaria maritima subsp. borealis

Matricaria maritima subsp.

borealis, commonly known as the northern yarrow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along coastal areas and open fields.

The plant features finely divided, gray-green leaves and produces small, yellow flower heads that resemble daisies. It is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.

This subspecies is adapted to harsh, coastal environments and is often found in maritime climates.
