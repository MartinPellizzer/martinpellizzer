Mazus pulchellus var. primuliformis

Mazus pulchellus var. primuliformis, commonly known as the purple marshmallow, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features dense, mat-forming foliage with soft, velvety leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a purple tinge. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as wetlands and woodland edges.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
