Melilotus bungeanus

Melilotus bungeanus, commonly known as the Siberian sweet clover, is a herbaceous plant native to regions of Asia, including Siberia and China.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its typical legume structure and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in spherical clusters, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Melilotus bungeanus is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop due to its nutritional value.
