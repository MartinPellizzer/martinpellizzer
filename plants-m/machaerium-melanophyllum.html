Machaerium melanophyllum

Machaerium melanophyllum, commonly known as black-stemmed machaerium, is a tropical plant native to regions of South America.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories. Its dark-colored stems and small, clustered flowers make it distinctive within its habitat.

The leaves are often trifoliate, with serrated edges, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
