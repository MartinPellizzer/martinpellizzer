Mentha × cruciata

Mentha × cruciata, commonly known as catmint, is a hybrid perennial plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often gray-green in color. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers arranged in whorls along the stems. It is native to Europe and western Asia but has been naturalized in many other regions.

Catmint is valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
