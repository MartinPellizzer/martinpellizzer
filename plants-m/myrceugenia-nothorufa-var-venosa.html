Myrceugenia nothorufa var. venosa

Myrceugenia nothorufa var. venosa, commonly known as the red-veined myrtle, is a hybrid plant native to the temperate regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with prominent red veins. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible, aromatic berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its unique foliage and fragrant blooms make it a valuable addition to both horticultural and ecological landscapes.
