Malus communis var. vetan

Malus communis var. vetan, commonly known as the old apple tree, is a botanical variant of the domesticated apple.

It is characterized by its robust root system and thick, gnarled bark, which provide resilience against harsh environmental conditions. The tree produces small, tart apples that are often used for cider or cooking. Its leaves are broad and serrated, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside.

This variety is valued for its historical significance and adaptability to various soil types.
