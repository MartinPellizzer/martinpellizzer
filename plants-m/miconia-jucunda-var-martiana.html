Miconia jucunda var. martiana

Miconia jucunda var. martiana, commonly known as the red miconia, is a shrub or small tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often deeply lobed and covered in a dense layer of fine, white pubescence. The plant produces showy, pink to red bracts that surround small, inconspicuous flowers, which are typically pollinated by birds. Its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets make it an invasive species in some areas where it is introduced.

The plant's botanical features, including its unique leaf morphology and floral structure, contribute to its ecological impact and ornamental value.
