Melastoma malabatricum

Melastoma malabatricum, commonly known as the Malabar mallow, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are usually pink or red, clustered in inflorescences, and bloom during the rainy season.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, ellipsoid seeds.
