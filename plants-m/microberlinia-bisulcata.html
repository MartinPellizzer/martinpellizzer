Microberlinia bisulcata

Microberlinia bisulcata, commonly known as the African sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and broad, palmate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds.

It is often found in savanna and woodland habitats, and its wood is valued for its durability and workability.
