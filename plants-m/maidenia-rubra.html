Maidenia rubra

Maidenia rubra, commonly known as the red maidenhair vine, is a species of flowering plant in the family Violaceae.

It is native to Central and South America, where it typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, fragrant, violet flowers. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers well-drained, loamy soil.

Maidenia rubra is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be used in shaded garden settings.
