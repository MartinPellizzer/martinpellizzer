Mentha incana

Mentha incana, commonly known as woolly mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its grayish-white, fuzzy leaves and square, branched stems, typical of the mint family. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Mentha incana thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

It is often used in herbal teas and has a refreshing, aromatic scent.
