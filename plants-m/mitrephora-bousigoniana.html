Mitrephora bousigoniana

Mitrephora bousigoniana, commonly known as the Bousigon's mitrephora, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes cashew and mango. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves, which are alternate and pinnate, with elliptical to lance-shaped leaflets. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
