Moraea rivulicola

Moraea rivulicola, commonly known as the river moraea, is a member of the Iridaceae family, native to South Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and striking, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils near rivers and streams, thriving in humid environments. Its blooming period occurs during the summer months, adding ornamental value to its natural habitat.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
