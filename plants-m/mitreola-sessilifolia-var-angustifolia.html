Mitreola sessilifolia var. angustifolia

Mitreola sessilifolia var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mitreola, is a small, herbaceous plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its slender, angular stems and small, clustered leaves. The leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape, with a narrow, pointed tip, giving the plant its common name. It produces tiny, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in cyathia, a feature typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

This plant is often found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
