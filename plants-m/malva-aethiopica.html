Malva aethiopica

Malva aethiopica, commonly known as the Ethiopian mallow, is a flowering plant native to Africa and parts of the Middle East.

It is characterized by its broad, velvety leaves and showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes other familiar species like cotton and hibiscus. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and gardens.

Malva aethiopica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
