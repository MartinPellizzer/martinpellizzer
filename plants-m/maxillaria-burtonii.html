Maxillaria burtonii

Maxillaria burtonii, commonly known as the Burtons' orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit, producing new shoots from the base of the previous ones. The plant features elongated, strap-like leaves and produces fragrant, showy flowers with a distinct coloration pattern. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Maxillaria burtonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
