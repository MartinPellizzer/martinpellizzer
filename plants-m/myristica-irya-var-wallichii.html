Myristica irya var. wallichii

Myristica irya var. wallichii, commonly known as the white nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and cuisine. The plant produces large, fleshy berries that contain a single seed, which is the source of its valuable aromatic compounds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is notable for its unique chemical composition, including myristicin and elemicin, which contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.
