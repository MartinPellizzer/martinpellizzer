Macadamia verticillata

Macadamia verticillata, commonly known as the mountain macadamia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are often arranged in whorls of three to five at the nodes. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by woody capsules containing edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for seed production, it is cultivated in subtropical regions around the world.
