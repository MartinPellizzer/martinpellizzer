Myristica sinclairii

Myristica sinclairii, commonly known as the Malabar nut or Sincock's nut, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, woody fruits that contain a valuable nut inside. The plant produces small, white flowers and has compound leaves with long, narrow leaflets. Its seeds are encased in a hard, yellowish shell and are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

The tree thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in dense forest understories.
