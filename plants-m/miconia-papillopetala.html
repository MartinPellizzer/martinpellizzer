Miconia papillopetala

Miconia papillopetala, commonly known as the velvetleaf miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that are typically borne on short pedicels. Its fruits are berries that turn red when mature, containing numerous seeds.

Miconia papillopetala is often found in moist, shaded areas of tropical forests and is known for its rapid growth and invasive potential in some regions.
