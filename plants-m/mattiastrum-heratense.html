Mattiastrum heratense

Mattiastrum heratense, commonly known as the Herat mountain grass, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Afghanistan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its small, yellowish flowers bloom in clusters, and it has narrow, grass-like leaves.

Mattiastrum heratense is of interest to botanists due to its ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
