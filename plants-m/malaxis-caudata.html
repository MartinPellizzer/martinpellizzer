Malaxis caudata

Malaxis caudata, commonly known as the long-tailed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, pendulous flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Malaxis caudata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
