Myrmecophila tibicinis

Myrmecophila tibicinis, commonly known as the "Ant Plant," is a species of epiphytic plant native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its unique hollow stems that provide shelter for colonies of ants. The plant forms a symbiotic relationship with certain ant species, which in turn protect the plant from herbivores. Its leaves are typically elongated and arranged in a rosette pattern, facilitating the collection of rainwater.

The plant's ability to host ants makes it an important component of the rainforest ecosystem.
