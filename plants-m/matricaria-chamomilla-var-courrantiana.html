Matricaria chamomilla var. courrantiana

Matricaria chamomilla var. courrantiana, commonly known as the courrantian chamomile, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces small, yellow flower heads that resemble chamomile. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, slightly bitter scent and is often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

The flowers are commonly harvested for use in teas and herbal remedies.
