Milla ixioides

Milla ixioides, commonly known as the Mexican moonflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its large, fragrant white flowers that open at night. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically yellow or orange when mature. Its leaves are ovate with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

Milla ixioides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and nocturnal blooming habit.
