Morus alba var. lhou

Morus alba var. lhou, commonly known as the white mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits called mulberries, which are edible and used in various culinary applications. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its leaves, which feed silkworms.

This variety is also valued for its ornamental beauty and adaptability to different soil types.
