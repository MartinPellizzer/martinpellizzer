Mallotonia gnaphalodes

Mallotonia gnaphalodes, commonly known as the silky mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features silver-gray, fuzzy foliage that gives it a distinctive, soft appearance. It produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Mallotonia gnaphalodes is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and along streambanks.
