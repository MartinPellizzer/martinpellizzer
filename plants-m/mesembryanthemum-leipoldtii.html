Mesembryanthemum leipoldtii

Mesembryanthemum leipoldtii, commonly known as the Leipoldt's ice plant, is a succulent shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it an interesting addition to succulent collections.
