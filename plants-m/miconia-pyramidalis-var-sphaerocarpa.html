Miconia pyramidalis var. sphaerocarpa

Miconia pyramidalis var. sphaerocarpa, commonly known as the globe-fruit miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers, followed by distinctive spherical, fleshy fruits that are orange to red in color. These fruits are edible and have a sweet, juicy texture, often consumed raw or used in local cuisines.

The species is also noted for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
