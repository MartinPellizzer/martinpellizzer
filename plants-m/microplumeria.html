Microplumeria

Microplumeria, commonly known as the frangipani, is a tropical plant belonging to the Plumeriaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors, including yellow, orange, and pink. The plant typically has smooth, grey-green leaves and produces small, oval fruits that contain numerous seeds. Native to Central America and the Caribbean, it thrives in warm, sunny climates with well-drained soil.

Microplumeria is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking floral display and air-purifying properties.
