Machilus pachycladus

Machilus pachycladus, commonly known as the broad-leafed chinquapin, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The tree produces edible berries that are typically consumed by wildlife, contributing to its ecological significance. Its bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Machilus pachycladus is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays an important role in forest ecosystems.
