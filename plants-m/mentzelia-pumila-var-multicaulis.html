Mentzelia pumila var. multicaulis

Mentzelia pumila var. multicaulis, commonly known as the dwarf blazingstar, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial with slender, branching stems and small, pale yellow to greenish flowers. The plant typically thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The species is part of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its ecological importance to pollinators.
