Melia toosendan

Melia toosendan, commonly known as Chinese bitter ash, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Meliaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The tree produces woody fruits containing flat, winged seeds. Its bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antifungal properties.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in Chinese culture.
