Maxillaria powellii

Maxillaria powellii, commonly known as the Powell's maxillaria, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit and produces fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, exhibiting an epiphytic lifestyle. Its flowers are borne on terminal racemes and bloom during the warmer months.

The genus Maxillaria is known for its diverse floral forms and is part of the Orchidaceae family.
