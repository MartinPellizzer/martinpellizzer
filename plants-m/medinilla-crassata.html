Medinilla crassata

Medinilla crassata, commonly known as the Christmas Medinilla, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features dense, leathery foliage with elliptical leaves that are dark green and glossy. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of white, pink, and red, which bloom during the winter months. It thrives in humid, warm environments and requires well-draining soil.

Medinilla crassata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive appearance and seasonal blooms.
