Medicago × ochroleuca

Medicago × ochroleuca, commonly known as the yellow sweet clover, is a hybrid legume characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers.

It is native to Europe and has been introduced to various parts of the world, including North America. The plant is notable for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria in its root nodules. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained soils and full sun.

Medicago × ochroleuca is often used in agricultural settings for forage and soil improvement due to its hardy nature and ecological benefits.
