Maughania guineensis

Maughania guineensis, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its velvety, dark green leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often with a compact rosette structure. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers indirect sunlight, making it a popular houseplant.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by the variety of flower colors, including purple, pink, and white.
