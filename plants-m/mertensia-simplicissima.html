Mertensia simplicissima

Mertensia simplicissima, commonly known as the Carolina bluebell, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, woodlands and has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. Its root system consists of a rhizomatous structure that helps it spread and establish in woodland environments.

Mertensia simplicissima is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
