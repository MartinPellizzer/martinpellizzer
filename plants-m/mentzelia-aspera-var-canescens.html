Mentzelia aspera var. canescens

Mentzelia aspera var. canescens, commonly known as the hairy blazingstar, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It features upright stems covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flower clusters that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered with a dense layer of fine hairs.

This variety is adapted to arid environments and is often found in sandy or gravelly soils.
