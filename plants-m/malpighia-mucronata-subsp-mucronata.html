Malpighia mucronata subsp. mucronata

Malpighia mucronata subsp.

mucronata, commonly known as the acorn fruit or yellow mombin, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas. It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptical in shape.

The plant produces small, yellow-orange fruits that are clustered in panicles, each fruit resembling an acorn in appearance. The flowers are typically white or pale yellow, with five petals, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
