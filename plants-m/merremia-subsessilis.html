Merremia subsessilis

Merremia subsessilis, commonly known as the slender morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink to purple. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, smooth seeds. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often found in disturbed soils or along riverbanks.

Due to its rapid growth and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a garden plant.
