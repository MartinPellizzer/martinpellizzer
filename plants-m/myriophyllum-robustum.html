Myriophyllum robustum

Myriophyllum robustum, commonly known as water milfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features finely divided, feathery leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically borne in spikes, and it thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments. Myriophyllum robustum is often found in ponds, lakes, and along the edges of rivers.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
