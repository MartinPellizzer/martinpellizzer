Malanea citrifolia

Malanea citrifolia, commonly known as the custard apple, is a tropical plant native to the Americas.

It is a small tree or shrub that produces a fruit with a thick, waxy skin and a creamy, aromatic pulp. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit is often used in culinary applications and traditional medicine due to its nutritional and medicinal properties.
