Miconia quinquenervia

Miconia quinquenervia, commonly known as the "brazilian cherry" or "false hibiscus," is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that are typically clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its fruits are small, fleshy berries that turn red when mature.

Miconia quinquenervia is known for its rapid growth and can become invasive in certain regions, making it a notable species in ecological studies.
