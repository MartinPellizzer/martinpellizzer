Muscari orchioides

Muscari orchioides, commonly known as the Persian grape hyacinth, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It produces dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, resembling grapes. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is typically found in rocky or well-drained soils. It blooms in early spring and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Muscari orchioides is relatively drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun to partial shade.
