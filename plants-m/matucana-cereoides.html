Matucana cereoides

Matucana cereoides, commonly known as the yellow brittlewood, is a member of the Araliaceae family and is native to South America.

It is a shrub characterized by its slender, woody stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically green with a slight waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by spherical, berry-like fruits. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
