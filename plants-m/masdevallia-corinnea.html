Masdevallia corinnea

Masdevallia corinnea, commonly known as the Crimson Masdevallia, is a rare and striking orchid native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red and green foliage, with flowers that exhibit a unique combination of colors and intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as a miniature epiphyte, attaching itself to tree trunks or branches in its natural habitat. Its flowers are often fragrant and bloom during the cooler months, making it a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.

Due to its specific environmental requirements, it is challenging to cultivate outside its native range.
