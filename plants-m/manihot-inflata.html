Manihot inflata

Manihot inflata, commonly known as the giant arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces clusters of yellow flowers. The plant's tubers, which are its edible part, are starchy and have a mild flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its high starch content, it is cultivated for both culinary and industrial uses.
