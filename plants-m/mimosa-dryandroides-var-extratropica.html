Mimosa dryandroides var. extratropica

Mimosa dryandroides var. extratropica, commonly known as the tropical mimosa, is a shrub native to Central and South America.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves that fold shut when touched, a characteristic known as thigmonastic response. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical climates.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
