Muscari neglectum var. macranthum

Muscari neglectum var. macranthum, commonly known as the giant grape hyacinth, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features dense clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant produces long, grass-like leaves that emerge from a bulbous base. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

This variety is prized for its showy blooms and is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant.
