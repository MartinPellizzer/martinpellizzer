Melaleuca wimmerensis

Melaleuca wimmerensis, commonly known as Wimmera melaleuca, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea tree species. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils in open habitats.

Melaleuca wimmerensis is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
