Malpighia maxima

Malpighia maxima, commonly known as the Mexican beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is a shrub that typically grows up to 2 meters tall, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy green surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or white, followed by bright red berries that are edible and highly nutritious. These berries are rich in vitamin C and are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
