Mesosetum comatum

Mesosetum comatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, blue-green leaves and compact growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands and along stream banks. Its flowering stems are erect and bear small, spike-like inflorescences.

Mesosetum comatum plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
