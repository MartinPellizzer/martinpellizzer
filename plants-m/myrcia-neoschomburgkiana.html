Myrcia neoschomburgkiana

Myrcia neoschomburgkiana, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its small, fragrant leaves and showy, red or orange flowers. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine, and the plant is valued for its ornamental appeal.

The fruit is small and berry-like, containing several seeds.
