Moenchia mantica subsp. bulgarica

Moenchia mantica subsp.

bulgarica, commonly known as the Bulgarian moenchia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Eastern Europe. It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense spikes, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it a notable species for ecological studies and botanical gardens.
