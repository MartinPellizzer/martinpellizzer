Melaleuca hypericifolia

Melaleuca hypericifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tea tree, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in winter. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Melaleuca hypericifolia is valued for its aromatic foliage and historical use in traditional medicine.
