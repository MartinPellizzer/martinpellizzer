Malpighia mayana

Malpighia mayana, commonly known as the West Indian cherry, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It produces small, bright red berries that are rich in vitamin C and other nutrients. The plant has simple, alternate leaves with smooth, glossy surfaces. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in inflorescences.

The botanical characteristics of Malpighia mayana make it well adapted to warm, humid climates and poor soils.
