Melica setacea

Melica setacea, commonly known as slender false brome, is a perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in spikelets, which are typically arranged in clusters along the stem. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Melica setacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
