Melica diffusa var. nitens

Melica diffusa var. nitens, commonly known as the shining melica, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, greenish-white flowers.

This variety is noted for its distinctive shiny appearance and is often found in temperate regions with rich, well-drained soils.
