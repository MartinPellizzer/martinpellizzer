Mentha × rotundifolia var. lachnaiochroa

Mentha × rotundifolia var. lachnaiochroa, commonly known as the woolly mint, is a hybrid species within the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its rounded, velvety leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple in color.

This variety is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves and is used in herbal teas and culinary applications.
