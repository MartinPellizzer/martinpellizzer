Maerua cylindricarpa

Maerua cylindricarpa, commonly known as the round-fruited maerua, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and distinctive, cylindrical fruits that are typically yellow or reddish in color. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh environments, with deep roots that help it access water from deeper soil layers. It produces small, yellowish flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and birds.

Maerua cylindricarpa is often used in traditional medicine and has potential applications in ecological restoration projects.
