Morinda fasciculata

Morinda fasciculata, commonly known as the red sandalwood or Indian madder, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in whorls. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers followed by red or orange berries that contain several seeds. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The bark and roots contain compounds that have been studied for their potential pharmacological properties.
