Mokof brasiliensis

Mokof brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian starfruit, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, star-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellow fruits that are rich in nutrients and have a unique flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in various parts of the world.
