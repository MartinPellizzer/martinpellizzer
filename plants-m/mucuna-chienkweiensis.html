Mucuna chienkweiensis

Mucuna chienkweiensis, commonly known as the Chinese velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive, hairy pods that contain elongated seeds. The plant produces a significant amount of nitrogen through its symbiotic relationship with Rhizobium bacteria in its root nodules. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The species is valued both for its agricultural use as a green manure and for its ornamental appeal.
