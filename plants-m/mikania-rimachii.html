Mikania rimachii

Mikania rimachii, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a vigorous vine native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rapid growth and trailing habit. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.

Mikania rimachii is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and light conditions, making it both a useful ornamental plant and a potential weed.
