Massangea hieroglyphica

Massangea hieroglyphica, commonly known as the sacred script plant, is a rare flowering species native to the misty highlands of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, leaf-like structures that resemble ancient hieroglyphs, giving it its unique common name. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and moths. Its leaves are thick and waxy, adapted to retain moisture in its humid, shaded habitat.

Due to its rarity and distinctive appearance, it is highly valued in both botanical gardens and scientific research.
