Myrtus cuspidata var. tetramera

Myrtus cuspidata var. tetramera, commonly known as the four-lobed myrtle, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, aromatic leaves with a distinctive four-lobed shape, and its flowers are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its dense foliage and fragrant properties make it valuable in both horticultural and ecological contexts.
