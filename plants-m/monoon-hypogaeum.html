Monoon hypogaeum

Monoon hypogaeum, commonly known as the underground beauty or ground orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique growth habit, with its flowers emerging from beneath the soil surface. The plant has a short, erect stem and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to thrive in moist, shaded environments.

Monoon hypogaeum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a popular choice in tropical gardens.
