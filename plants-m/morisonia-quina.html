Morisonia quina

Morisonia quina, commonly known as the yellow sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its yellow, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It has slender, branching stems and small, opposite leaves.

Morisonia quina is often found in coastal dunes and other arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.
