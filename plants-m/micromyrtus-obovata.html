Micromyrtus obovata

Micromyrtus obovata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bottlebrush, is a small evergreen shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and distinctive red or orange bottlebrush-like flowers that appear in spring. The plant belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees. It typically grows in coastal heathlands and is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils.

Micromyrtus obovata is often used in ornamental gardening due to its vibrant floral displays and compact growth habit.
