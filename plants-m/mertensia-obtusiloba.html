Mertensia obtusiloba

Mertensia obtusiloba, commonly known as the blunt-leaf bluebells, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of bell-shaped, sky-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, often found in woodland habitats. Its flowers are pollinated by bees and other insects, and the plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits.

Mertensia obtusiloba is also noted for its ability to thrive in shaded environments, making it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
