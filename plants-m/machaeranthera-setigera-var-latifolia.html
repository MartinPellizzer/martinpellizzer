Machaeranthera setigera var. latifolia

Machaeranthera setigera var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf bluetail, is a perennial herb native to the central and eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has linear to lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as prairies and woodland edges, and it is known for its aromatic foliage.

The variety latifolia is distinguished by its broader leaves compared to the typical form of Machaeranthera setigera.
