Monnina laurifolia

Monnina laurifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf monnina, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing annual herb with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its stems are erect and branching, often reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters.

The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a common sight in disturbed soils and along roadsides.
