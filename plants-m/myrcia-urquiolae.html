Myrcia urquiolae

Myrcia urquiolae, commonly known as the Urquillo's myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Andes Mountains in South America, specifically in regions of Peru and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruits are small, woody capsules that contain several seeds.

Myrcia urquiolae is typically found in humid, shaded environments, such as cloud forests, where it thrives in the cooler, moist conditions.
