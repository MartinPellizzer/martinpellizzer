Maxillaria procurrens

Maxillaria procurrens, commonly known as the creeping maxillaria, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces flowers in shades of white and yellow. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its pseudobulbs are short and cylindrical, and it has linear, grass-like leaves.

The flowers are arranged in a raceme and are known for their subtle fragrance.
