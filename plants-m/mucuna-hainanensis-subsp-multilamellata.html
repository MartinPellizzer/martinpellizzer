Mucuna hainanensis subsp. multilamellata

Mucuna hainanensis subsp.

multilamellata, commonly known as the Hainan velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to southern China. It features trifoliate leaves with a velvety texture and produces distinctive, showy flowers in shades of pink to purple.

The plant is characterized by its compound pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and partial shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
