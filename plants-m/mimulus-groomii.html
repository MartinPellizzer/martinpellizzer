Mimulus groomii

Mimulus groomii, commonly known as the yellow monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, spurred flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as stream banks and meadows. It has heart-shaped leaves and a trailing growth habit, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Mimulus groomii is also valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for pollinators like bees and butterflies.
