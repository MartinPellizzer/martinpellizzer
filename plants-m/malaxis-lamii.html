Malaxis lamii

Malaxis lamii, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and grasslands. The plant has a slender, erect stem with several narrow, linear leaves. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and often clustered in a raceme, with a distinctive lip that is usually darker in color.

Malaxis lamii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
