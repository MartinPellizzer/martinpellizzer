Mila caespitosa var. pugionifera

Mila caespitosa var. pugionifera, commonly known as the needle grass, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale blue or white flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value comes from its compact form and subtle floral display.
