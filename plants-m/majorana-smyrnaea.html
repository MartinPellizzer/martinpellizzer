Majorana smyrnaea

Majorana smyrnaea, commonly known as sweet marjoram, is an aromatic herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, pale purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees. Its essential oils, derived from the leaves and flowers, are valued for their spicy, sweet scent and medicinal properties.

Majorana smyrnaea is often used in culinary applications and traditional herbal remedies.
