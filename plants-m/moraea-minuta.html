Moraea minuta

Moraea minuta, commonly known as the dwarf moraea, is a small, bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It produces delicate, tubular flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white, typically blooming in spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is a member of the Iridaceae family, which includes other ornamental plants like gladiolus and crocus.

Due to its compact size and attractive flowers, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.
