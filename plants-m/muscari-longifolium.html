Muscari longifolium

Muscari longifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved grape hyacinth, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces clusters of small, fragrant, blue-violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has strap-shaped, grass-like leaves that are long and narrow, giving it a distinctive appearance. It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate fragrance and early blooming habit.
