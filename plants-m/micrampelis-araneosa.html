Micrampelis araneosa

Micrampelis araneosa, commonly known as the spider vine, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems that allow it to ascend and entwine around supporting structures. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Micrampelis araneosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
