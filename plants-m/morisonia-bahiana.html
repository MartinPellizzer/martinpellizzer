Morisonia bahiana

Morisonia bahiana, commonly known as the Brazilian morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, violet to blue flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Morisonia bahiana is also used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
