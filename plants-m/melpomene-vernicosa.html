Melpomene vernicosa

Melpomene vernicosa, commonly known as the red lily or red lily of the valley, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking red or pink bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, giving it a glossy appearance. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Melpomene vernicosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
