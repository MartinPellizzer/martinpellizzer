Myagrum hispanicum

Myagrum hispanicum, commonly known as the Spanish myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant features small, glossy leaves and produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers that attract pollinators. Its fruits are small, woody capsules that contain aromatic seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
