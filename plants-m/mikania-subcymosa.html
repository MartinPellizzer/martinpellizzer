Mikania subcymosa

Mikania subcymosa, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rapid growth and climbing habit. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and gardens.

Due to its invasive nature in some regions, it can become a problematic weed.
