Mesua pustulata

Mesua pustulata, commonly known as the Indian rose or velvet tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling roses. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its bark is smooth and grayish, and it produces small, round berries that turn red when mature.

Mesua pustulata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
