Megalastrum pulverulentum

Megalastrum pulverulentum, commonly known as the dusty megalastrum, is a rare and enigmatic plant species native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth form and its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor, sandy soils. The plant produces small, pale green leaves that are covered in a fine, powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. Its flowers are inconspicuous, typically appearing as small, yellowish clusters during the spring.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native habitat.
