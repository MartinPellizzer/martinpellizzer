Monodora stocksii

Monodora stocksii, commonly known as African wild almond, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Anacardiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds. Its roots and leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties.

The tree is also valued for its timber, which is hard and durable.
