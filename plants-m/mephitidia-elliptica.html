Mephitidia elliptica

Mephitidia elliptica, commonly known as the elliptical mephit, is a rare flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the nutrient-poor soil of its habitat.

Due to habitat loss, Mephitidia elliptica is considered endangered and is protected in several conservation programs.
