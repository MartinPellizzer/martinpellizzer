Macaranga kingii var. platyphylla

Macaranga kingii var. platyphylla, commonly known as the broad-leaved macaranga, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a fast-growing shrub or small tree characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves. The leaves are typically smooth and have a glossy surface, with a distinct midrib. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by capsules containing numerous seeds.

This plant is often found in disturbed habitats and is adapted to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
