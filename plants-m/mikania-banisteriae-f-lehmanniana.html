Mikania banisteriae f. lehmanniana

Mikania banisteriae f. lehmanniana, commonly known as the creeping mikania, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, daisy-like flowers. The plant features opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces yellow flowers with pale green centers, which are typically arranged in clusters.

This species is often found in disturbed areas and can be invasive in certain regions.
