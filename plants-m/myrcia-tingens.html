Myrcia tingens

Myrcia tingens, commonly known as the pink myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy pink to red flowers. The plant produces small, dark blue to black berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Myrcia tingens thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
