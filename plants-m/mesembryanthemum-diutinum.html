Mesembryanthemum diutinum

Mesembryanthemum diutinum, commonly known as the everlasting ice plant, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to dry conditions. Its name "everlasting" refers to the durability of its flowers, which remain intact for extended periods.

Mesembryanthemum diutinum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and drought tolerance.
