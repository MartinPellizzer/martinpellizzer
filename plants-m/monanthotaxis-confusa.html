Monanthotaxis confusa

Monanthotaxis confusa, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a climbing shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are adapted to arid conditions. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Malvaceae, which includes hibiscus and cotton plants.
