Myginda disticha

Myginda disticha, commonly known as the two-branched myginda, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems that often grow in dense clusters. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface.

Myginda disticha is adapted to high-altitude environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.
