Morus insignis

Morus insignis, commonly known as the Mexican mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Moraceae family, which includes other well-known species like the common mulberry. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, aggregate fruits that turn from green to red when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in dry or semi-arid regions.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local cultures.
