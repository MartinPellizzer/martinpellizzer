Manihot japonica

Manihot japonica, commonly known as Japanese arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, tuberous roots that are used as a starch source. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has simple, alternate leaves. It produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in cyathia, a unique floral structure typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

The plant is valued both for its edible roots and its ornamental qualities.
