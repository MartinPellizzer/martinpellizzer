Merremia aturensis

Merremia aturensis, commonly known as the red-root morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges.

Due to its rapid growth, it can become invasive in certain areas.
