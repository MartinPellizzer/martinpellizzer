Mangifera siamensis

Mangifera siamensis, commonly known as the Siamese mango, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, yellow-orange fruits. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its fruit is valued for its sweet, aromatic pulp and is often used in local cuisines.

The tree also plays an important role in ecological systems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
