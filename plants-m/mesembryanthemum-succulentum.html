Mesembryanthemum succulentum

Mesembryanthemum succulentum, commonly known as the ice plant, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink, yellow, and white, typically during the cooler months. Its ability to form a dense mat makes it useful for erosion control and as a ground cover.

Mesembryanthemum succulentum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in drought conditions.
