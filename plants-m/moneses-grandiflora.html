Moneses grandiflora

Moneses grandiflora, commonly known as the white phlox, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces showy clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Moneses grandiflora is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
