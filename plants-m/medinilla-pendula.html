Medinilla pendula

Medinilla pendula, commonly known as the hanging Medinilla, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its pendulous clusters of showy, waxy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant has thick, leathery leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, warm environments and requires well-drained soil.

Medinilla pendula is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and long-lasting blooms.
