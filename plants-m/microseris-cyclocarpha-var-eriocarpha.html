Microseris cyclocarpha var. eriocarpha

Microseris cyclocarpha var. eriocarpha, commonly known as the woolly false prickly poppy, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features slender, upright stems covered in dense, white, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with long, narrow petals that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and also bear a fuzzy texture.

This variety is adapted to dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy addition to native plant gardens.
