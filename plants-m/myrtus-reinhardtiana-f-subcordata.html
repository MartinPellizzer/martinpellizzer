Myrtus reinhardtiana f. subcordata

Myrtus reinhardtiana f. subcordata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf myrtle, is a compact shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It features small, dark green, elliptical leaves that are often described as subcordate, or heart-shaped at the base. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers during the spring and summer months. Its aromatic foliage and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, thriving in Mediterranean climates.
