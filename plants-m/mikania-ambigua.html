Mikania ambigua

Mikania ambigua, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a vigorous climbing vine native to the Americas.

It features heart-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale purple. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Due to its invasive nature, it can outcompete native plants and is considered a weed in many regions.
