Muscari atlanticum var. valentinum

Muscari atlanticum var. valentinum, commonly known as the Valentine's musk grape.

This variety is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its clusters of small, bell-shaped blue-violet flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often forming dense foliage that resembles grass. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in rock gardens and borders.

Its flowers emit a subtle musky fragrance, contributing to its charm and appeal in horticultural settings.
