Matthiola tricuspidata subsp. pseudoxyceras

Matthiola tricuspidata subsp.

pseudoxyceras, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wallflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple.

The plant produces small, oval-shaped seeds enclosed in a capsule, which is a typical feature of the Brassicaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
