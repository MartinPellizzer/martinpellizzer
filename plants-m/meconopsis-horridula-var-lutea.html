Meconopsis horridula var. lutea

Meconopsis horridula var. lutea, commonly known as the yellow Himalayan gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in rocky, alpine environments, where it thrives in cool and moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which help to protect it from harsh weather.

Meconopsis horridula var. lutea is a perennial species that is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens with suitable conditions.
