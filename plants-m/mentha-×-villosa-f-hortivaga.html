Mentha × villosa f. hortivaga

Mentha × villosa f. hortivaga, commonly known as the woolly mint, is a hybrid plant that exhibits characteristics of both Mentha villosa and other mint species.

It is a perennial herb with a square stem and opposite leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or blue, arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in temperate regions.

Due to its aromatic properties, it is sometimes used in herbal teas and traditional medicine.
