Matthiola oxyceras var. forcipifera

Matthiola oxyceras var. forcipifera, commonly known as the swordleaf stock, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, sword-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. Native to southern Europe and western Asia, this plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.

The species is also valued for its historical use in traditional medicine.
