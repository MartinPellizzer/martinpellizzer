Macrozamia pulchra

Macrozamia pulchra, commonly known as the pretty cycad, is a striking evergreen plant native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the cycad family (Cycadaceae) and is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces distinctive cones, with male and female cones typically occurring on separate plants. Macrozamia pulchra is known for its slow growth and long lifespan, often living over 100 years.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
