Merremia sibirica var. sibirica

Merremia sibirica var. sibirica, commonly known as the Siberian morning glory, is a climbing vine native to temperate regions of Asia.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is typically found in moist, shady environments such as forests and stream banks. Its fruits are capsule-like and contain numerous small, dry seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
