Myrtus pubescens

Myrtus pubescens, commonly known as the common myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are followed by dark blue berries. Myrtus pubescens is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valued plant in both horticulture and herbal practices.
