Mussaenda parva

Mussaenda parva, commonly known as the small flame bush, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that are surrounded by bright red bracts, creating a striking visual display. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

The botanical features of Mussaenda parva make it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and landscapes.
