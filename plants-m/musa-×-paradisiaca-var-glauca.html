Musa × paradisiaca var. glauca

Musa × paradisiaca var. glauca, commonly known as the white banana, is a cultivar of the banana plant characterized by its striking white to pale yellow flesh.

It is a triploid hybrid, typically grown for its edible fruit, which has a sweet and creamy texture. The plant features large, broad leaves that are often a deep green, providing a lush appearance. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and warm temperatures.

This variety is also valued for its ornamental qualities, making it a popular choice for both cultivation and landscaping.
