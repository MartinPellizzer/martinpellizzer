Mimulus leptanthus

Mimulus leptanthus, commonly known as the slender monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, spurred petals and bright orange or yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as stream banks and woodland areas. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, seed-like fruits called capsules.

Mimulus leptanthus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
