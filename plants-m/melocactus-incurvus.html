Melocactus incurvus

Melocactus incurvus, commonly known as the hooked melon cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem that is often ribbed and covered in short, stiff spines. The plant produces striking yellow flowers during the rainy season, which are typically followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic environments.

The plant's unique morphology and adaptability to harsh conditions make it a notable subject in botanical studies.
