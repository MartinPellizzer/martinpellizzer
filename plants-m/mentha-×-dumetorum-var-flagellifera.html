Mentha × dumetorum var. flagellifera

Mentha × dumetorum var. flagellifera, commonly known as purple mint, is a hybrid plant in the mint family (Lamiaceae).

It is characterized by its slender, often hairless stems and small, opposite leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually purple or pink, which bloom in whorls along the stem. It is native to Europe and is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and waste areas.

This variety is valued for its aromatic leaves and is used in both culinary and medicinal applications.
