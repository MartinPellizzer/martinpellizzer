Montia exigua var. viridis

Montia exigua var. viridis, commonly known as green common watercress, is a low-growing, herbaceous annual plant native to North America.

It typically thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils along stream banks and wetlands. The plant features small, opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small, white or greenish-white, and arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

This variety is distinguished by its vibrant green foliage, which sets it apart from other varieties of Montia exigua.
