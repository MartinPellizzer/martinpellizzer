Monochaetum humboldtianum var. humboldtianum

Monochaetum humboldtianum var. humboldtianum, commonly known as the Humboldt's spikegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the coastal regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces inflorescences that consist of dense, spike-like clusters of tiny flowers. It thrives in sandy or coastal soils and is adapted to withstand salt spray and strong winds.

This variety is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations and limited distribution.
