Microcorys purpurea

Microcorys purpurea, commonly known as the purple microcorys, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spikelet-like inflorescences with purple to pink bracts, giving it its common name. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

Microcorys purpurea is often found in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and wetlands.
