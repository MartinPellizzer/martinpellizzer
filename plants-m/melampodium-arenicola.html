Melampodium arenicola

Melampodium arenicola, commonly known as sand everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial that thrives in sandy and rocky soils. The plant produces small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Melampodium arenicola is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
