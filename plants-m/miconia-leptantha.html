Miconia leptantha

Miconia leptantha, commonly known as the slender miconia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers, which are followed by round, orange-red fruits. Miconia leptantha is part of the Melastomataceae family, known for its ornamental value and ecological significance.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.
