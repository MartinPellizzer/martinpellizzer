Macaranga quinquelobata

Macaranga quinquelobata, commonly known as the five-lobed macaranga, is a species of flowering plant in the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which can make them appear fuzzy to the touch.

The species is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to thrive in disturbed soils.
