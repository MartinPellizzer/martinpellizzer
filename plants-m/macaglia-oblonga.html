Macaglia oblonga

Macaglia oblonga, commonly known as the oblong leaf fern, is a species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, narrow, leathery leaves that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires high humidity to flourish. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats over time.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is valued for its ornamental appeal in humid indoor settings.
