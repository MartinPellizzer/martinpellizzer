Mentha × verticillata var. concavidens

Mentha × verticillata var. concavidens, commonly known as narrow-leaf water mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, opposite leaves that are often slightly curled at the edges. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers arranged in whorls along the stems. It thrives in moist environments such as wet meadows, stream banks, and marshes.

Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, making it a valued species in traditional medicine and horticulture.
