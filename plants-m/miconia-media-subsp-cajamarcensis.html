Miconia media subsp. cajamarcensis

Miconia media subsp.

cajamarcensis, commonly known as the Peruvian miconia, is a member of the Melastomataceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America. It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to several meters in height, characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves.

The plant produces showy, clustered inflorescences with white to pinkish flowers, which are typically followed by bright red or orange berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests and lowland rainforests.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and has been introduced to other tropical regions, though it can become invasive in some areas.
