Macroditassa marianae

Macroditassa marianae, commonly known as the Marianas tree fern, is a species native to the tropical regions of the Mariana Islands.

It is a member of the Cyatheaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil. Its rhizome system is extensive, allowing it to anchor firmly in the substrate and absorb moisture efficiently.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
