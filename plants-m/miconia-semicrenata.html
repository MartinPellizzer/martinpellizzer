Miconia semicrenata

Miconia semicrenata, commonly known as the Brazilian starwood, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its opposite, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, spiny, berry-like fruits. Miconia semicrenata is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments.

However, it is also considered an invasive species in some regions due to its competitive nature.
