Myrtus munizii

Myrtus munizii, commonly known as the Socotra myrtle, is a unique evergreen shrub native to the Socotra archipelago.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically red or purple when mature. Myrtus munizii thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to the harsh climate of its native habitat.

Its distinctive appearance and ecological significance make it a notable species in the region's flora.
