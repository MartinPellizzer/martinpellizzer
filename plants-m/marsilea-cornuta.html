Marsilea cornuta

Marsilea cornuta, commonly known as the horned water clover, is a aquatic fern belonging to the family Marsileaceae.

It is characterized by its small, four-leaf clover-like fronds that float on the surface of water. The plant has distinctive horn-shaped structures called "cornuta," which are reproductive organs used for sexual reproduction. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in ponds and ditches.

Marsilea cornuta is an important component of aquatic ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
