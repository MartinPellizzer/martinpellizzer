Melicope kavaiensis

Melicope kavaiensis, commonly known as Kava kava, is a member of the Rutaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of the Pacific.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, fragrant flowers. It produces berries that are often used in traditional medicine and cultural practices.

Due to its ecological and cultural significance, it is also of interest for conservation efforts.
