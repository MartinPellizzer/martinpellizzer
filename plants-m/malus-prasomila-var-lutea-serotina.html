Malus prasomila var. lutea-serotina

Malus prasomila var. lutea-serotina, commonly known as the late-flowering yellow crabapple, is a variant of the crabapple species native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers that bloom later in the season compared to other crabapple varieties. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are ovate with a slightly serrated edge, and the fruit is a small, yellowish apple that ripens in late summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental flowers and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
