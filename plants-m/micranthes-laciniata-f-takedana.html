Micranthes laciniata f. takedana

Micranthes laciniata f. takedana, commonly known as the Takeda's finely-serrated bellflower, is a rare and unique botanical variant of the broader Micranthes laciniata species.

It is characterized by its finely lobed, serrated leaves and small, bell-shaped flowers that typically range in color from pale blue to white. This plant is native to specific regions in Japan, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its ornamental value lies in its delicate foliage and subtle floral display, making it a sought-after specimen among botanists and gardeners.
