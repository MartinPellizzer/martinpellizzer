Mimosa foliolosa var. grandibractea

Mimosa foliolosa var. grandibractea, commonly known as the giant mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, fern-like leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is known for its sensitive leaves that fold shut when touched, a trait called thigmonasty. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its unique appearance and adaptive features make it a notable species within the Mimosa genus.
