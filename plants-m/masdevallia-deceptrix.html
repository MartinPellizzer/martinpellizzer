Masdevallia deceptrix

Masdevallia deceptrix, commonly known as the deceptive masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often colorful flowers that resemble other orchid species, which may aid in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees without deriving nutrients from them. Its leaves are usually elongated and grass-like, adapted to capture moisture from the humid air.

The flowers bloom in a range of hues, including red, orange, and yellow, making it a visually appealing and ecologically significant plant in its habitat.
