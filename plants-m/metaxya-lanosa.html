Metaxya lanosa

Metaxya lanosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf or woolly metaxya, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, winged fruits. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
