Minuartia macrantha var. filiorum

Minuartia macrantha var. filiorum, commonly known as the filial rockcress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its compact rosette of fleshy, blue-green leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. It thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens and alpine plantings.
