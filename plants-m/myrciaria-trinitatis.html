Myrciaria trinitatis

Myrciaria trinitatis, commonly known as the Natal plum or yellow mombin, is a small evergreen tree native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, orange-colored fruits that are edible and have a tart, citrus-like flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas and humid lowlands.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
