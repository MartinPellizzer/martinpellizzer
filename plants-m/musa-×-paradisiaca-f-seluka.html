Musa × paradisiaca f. seluka

Musa × paradisiaca f. seluka, commonly known as the banana plant, is a cultivated variety of the banana genus.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves and clusters of fleshy, edible berries. The plant is a member of the Musaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia. It thrives in tropical climates and requires warm, humid conditions for optimal growth.

This variety is often grown for its fruit, which is rich in nutrients and widely consumed around the world.
