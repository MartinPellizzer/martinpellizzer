Maireana radiata

Maireana radiata, commonly known as the ghost gum, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale grey bark and small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during spring. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for dry landscapes.

Maireana radiata is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological benefits in native gardens.
