Magnolia

Magnolia, commonly known as the magnolia tree, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Magnoliaceae.

These plants are characterized by their large, showy flowers that often appear before the leaves in spring. The flowers are typically composed of numerous petals and are fragrant, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Magnolia trees can vary in size from small shrubs to large trees, with some species reaching heights of over 50 feet.

Their broad, glossy leaves and ornamental value make them popular in gardens and landscapes worldwide.
