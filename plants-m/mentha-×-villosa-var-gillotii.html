Mentha × villosa var. gillotii

Mentha × villosa var. gillotii, commonly known as the woolly mint, is a hybrid plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, hairy leaves and a strong, aromatic scent. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has a creeping growth habit. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported cooling and digestive properties.

The flowers are small and pale purple, appearing in clusters during the summer months.
