Magnolia wilsonii

Magnolia wilsonii, commonly known as Wilson's magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to eastern China.

It is characterized by its large, showy white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The leaves are broadly ovate with a smooth texture and a dark green upper surface. The tree produces elongated, woody fruits that contain winged seeds.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
