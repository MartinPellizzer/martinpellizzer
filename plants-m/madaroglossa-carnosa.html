Madaroglossa carnosa

Madaroglossa carnosa, commonly known as the fleshy-leaved orchid, is a unique species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces striking, colorful flowers that bloom in spring, often in shades of pink and purple. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
