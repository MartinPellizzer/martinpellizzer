Myosotis antonii f. major

Myosotis antonii f. major, commonly known as the giant forget-me-not, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its botanical features include a robust root system and a flowering stem that bears numerous clusters of small, five-petaled flowers.
