Morinda tinctoria

Morinda tinctoria, commonly known as the Indian madder, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. The plant produces red-orange berries that contain the seeds. Its roots and leaves are used in traditional medicine and as a natural dye source.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rubiaceae family, which includes over 13,000 species of flowering plants.
