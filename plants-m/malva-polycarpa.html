Malva polycarpa

Malva polycarpa, commonly known as the many-fruited mallows, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton, hibiscus, and okra. The plant is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release numerous small seeds.

It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of light conditions.
