Mallotus papuanus var. glabrescens

Mallotus papuanus var. glabrescens, commonly known as the Papuan mallot, is a shrub or small tree native to the tropical regions of New Guinea and nearby islands.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its hard, heavy wood used in traditional crafts and construction.
