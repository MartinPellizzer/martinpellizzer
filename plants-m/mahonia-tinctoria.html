Mahonia tinctoria

Mahonia tinctoria, commonly known as the golden barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves that are bright green in summer and turn yellow in autumn, giving it its common name. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Mahonia tinctoria is also valued for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.
