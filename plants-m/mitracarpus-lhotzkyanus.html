Mitracarpus lhotzkyanus

Mitracarpus lhotzkyanus, commonly known as the Lhotzkyan rock fig, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces edible fruits that are typically consumed fresh or used in traditional medicine. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in humid, tropical climates.
