Miersiophyton

Miersiophyton, commonly known as the "ancient fern," is a prehistoric plant that thrived during the Carboniferous period.

It is characterized by its simple, branching fronds and lack of true roots, stems, or leaves. The plant's structure suggests it was a primitive vascular plant, capable of transporting water and nutrients. Fossil evidence indicates it played a significant role in the early development of forest ecosystems.

Its study provides valuable insights into the evolution of plant life on Earth.
