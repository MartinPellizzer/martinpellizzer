Maytenus horrida

Maytenus horrida, commonly known as the rough mayten, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and dark green, leathery leaves that are often arranged in opposite pairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by elongated, woody fruits containing a single seed. It is often found in dry to moist forests and is valued for its hardy nature and ornamental features.

Maytenus horrida is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
