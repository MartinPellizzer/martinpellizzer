Melicope grisea var. crassifolia

Melicope grisea var. crassifolia, commonly known as the thick-leaved melicope, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green, thick, and leathery leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers followed by elliptical, woody fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
