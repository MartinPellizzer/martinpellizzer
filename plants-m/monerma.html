Monerma

Monerma, commonly known as the "ghost plant," is a unique aquatic plant that lacks true roots, stems, and leaves.

It belongs to the genus Monerma within the family Monermaceae and is characterized by its simple, thread-like structure. The plant is typically found in freshwater environments, such as ponds and slow-moving streams. Monerma reproduces through vegetative propagation, forming dense mats on the water surface.

Its botanical significance lies in its simplicity and its role in aquatic ecosystems.
