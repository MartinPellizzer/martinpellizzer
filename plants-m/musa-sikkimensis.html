Musa sikkimensis

Musa sikkimensis, commonly known as the Sikkim banana, is a species of flowering plant in the Musaceae family.

It is native to the eastern Himalayas, particularly in regions of India, Nepal, and Bhutan. The plant is characterized by its large, broad leaves and produces clusters of yellow, edible bananas. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Musa sikkimensis is valued not only for its fruit but also for its ornamental qualities and traditional uses in local communities.
