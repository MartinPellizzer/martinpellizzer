Mentzelia pumila var. rusbyi

Mentzelia pumila var. rusbyi, commonly known as the Rusby's plainscent, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of pale yellow to greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, typically found in sandy or gravelly soils.

Mentzelia pumila var. rusbyi is adapted to withstand drought and heat, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
