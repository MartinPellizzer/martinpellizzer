Myrceugenia latior

Myrceugenia latior, commonly known as the broad-leaved myrtle, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to southern Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule containing several seeds.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.
