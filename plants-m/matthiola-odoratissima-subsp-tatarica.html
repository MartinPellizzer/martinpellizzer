Matthiola odoratissima subsp. tatarica

Matthiola odoratissima subsp.

tatarica, commonly known as the Tatarian stock, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with mustard-like characteristics.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, often arranged in clusters. It is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in temperate climates.

The subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
