Mimosa orthocarpa

Mimosa orthocarpa, commonly known as the pink mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy pink flowers. The plant is typically shrub-like, with slender stems and can grow up to several feet tall. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are often visited by bees and other pollinators.

The plant is adapted to a variety of soil types and can thrive in both full sun and partial shade.
