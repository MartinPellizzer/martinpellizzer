Microchilus fendleri

Microchilus fendleri, commonly known as the Mexican orchid, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the highlands of Mexico.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its small, intricate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pale pink. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces elongated, pendulous flower spikes. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered endangered and requires specific environmental conditions to thrive.
