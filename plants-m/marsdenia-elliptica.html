Marsdenia elliptica

Marsdenia elliptica, commonly known as the elliptic Marsdenia, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, elliptical leaves. The flowers are typically small and clustered, with five petals that are often pink or white in color. It produces small, fleshy berries that contain seeds, and the plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and partial shade.
