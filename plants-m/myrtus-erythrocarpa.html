Myrtus erythrocarpa

Myrtus erythrocarpa, commonly known as the red-fruited myrtle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal and sandy soils. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are aromatic and have a mint-like scent, which is often used in traditional medicine.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
