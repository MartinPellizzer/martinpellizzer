Microchilus leucostictus

Microchilus leucostictus, commonly known as the white-streaked orchid, is a small, terrestrial orchid native to the southeastern United States.

It features a slender, erect stem with a single or few flowers at the top. The flowers are typically white or pale pink with distinctive yellow markings, and they emit a faint, sweet fragrance. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded, woodland environments.
