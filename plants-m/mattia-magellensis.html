Mattia magellensis

Mattia magellensis, commonly known as the Antarctic hairgrass, is a flowering plant native to the extreme environments of Antarctica and the Southern Ocean islands.

It is a low-growing perennial grass that thrives in cold, windy conditions with minimal soil. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are adapted to the harsh climate. It plays a crucial role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for microscopic organisms in its ecosystem.

Due to its unique adaptations, it is considered an important indicator of environmental change in polar regions.
