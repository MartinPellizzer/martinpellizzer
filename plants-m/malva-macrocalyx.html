Malva macrocalyx

Malva macrocalyx, commonly known as the large-flowered mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton, hibiscus, and okra. The plant is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowers have five petals and are surrounded by fuzzy, green sepals.

Malva macrocalyx is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.
