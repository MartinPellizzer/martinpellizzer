Metrosideros rigida

Metrosideros rigida, commonly known as the rough shieldvine, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal and lowland areas. The plant produces small, bright red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. Its leaves are leathery and have a rough texture, contributing to its common name.

The species is also known for its ability to thrive in sandy soils and tolerate salt spray.
