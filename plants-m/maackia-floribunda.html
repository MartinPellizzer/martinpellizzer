Maackia floribunda

Maackia floribunda, commonly known as the Japanese maackia, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It features compound leaves with ovate to elliptic leaflets that are often pubescent. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white to pale yellow flowers in early spring. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade.

The berries it produces are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.
