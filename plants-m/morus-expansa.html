Morus expansa

Morus expansa, commonly known as the broadleaf mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically lobed and have a smooth texture. The tree produces small, aggregate fruits called mulberries, which are edible and often used in traditional foods. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in silk production.
