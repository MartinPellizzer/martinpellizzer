Myoporum glandulosum

Myoporum glandulosum, commonly known as the wooly myoporum, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage that gives it a distinctive, woolly appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in spikes. It is well adapted to sandy soils and can tolerate salt spray, making it suitable for coastal landscapes.

Myoporum glandulosum is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and resilience.
