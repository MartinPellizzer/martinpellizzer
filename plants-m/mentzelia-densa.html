Mentzelia densa

Mentzelia densa, commonly known as dense mentzelia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its hairy stems and clustered, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Mentzelia densa is often found in rocky or sandy soils in arid environments.

It is part of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is noted for its ecological importance to pollinators.
