Microstylis brevicaulis

Microstylis brevicaulis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved microstylis, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and swampy areas. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are short and narrow, and it produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers in early spring. Its reproductive structures are adapted for wind pollination, which is common in its native habitat.

The species is often overlooked due to its delicate appearance and limited size.
