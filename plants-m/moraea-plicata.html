Moraea plicata

Moraea plicata, commonly known as the Zulu lily, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, funnel-shaped flowers that range from white to pink or purple. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and has strap-like, grassy leaves. It produces showy blooms during the summer months, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Moraea plicata is also valued for its cultural significance in some African communities.
