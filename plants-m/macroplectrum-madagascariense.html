Macroplectrum madagascariense

Macroplectrum madagascariense, commonly known as the Madagascar grass.

It is a perennial herb native to Madagascar, characterized by its tufted growth habit and fibrous root system. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
