Mentha × scotica

Mentha × scotica, commonly known as Scotch mint, is a hybrid perennial herb native to the British Isles.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly menthol-like leaves and flowers that range from white to pale purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a creeping growth habit with rhizomes that allow it to spread readily.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in ecological restoration and traditional herbal practices.
