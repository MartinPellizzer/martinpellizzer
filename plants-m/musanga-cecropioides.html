Musanga cecropioides

Musanga cecropioides, commonly known as the African elder or African sycamore, is a large, deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Moraceae, which includes figs and mulberries, and is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and smooth, grey bark. The tree produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by edible, round fruits that are often consumed by both humans and animals.
