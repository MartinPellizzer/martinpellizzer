Medinilla endertii

Medinilla endertii, commonly known as the velvet tangerine.

This evergreen shrub is native to the Philippines and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that resemble small, clustered tangerines. The plant produces bright orange-red fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil and partial shade.

Medinilla endertii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to bloom throughout the year.
