Morphixia juncifolia

Morphixia juncifolia, commonly known as the swamp willow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its fibrous root system helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion in aquatic environments.

Morphixia juncifolia is valued for its ecological role in supporting wetland biodiversity and its aesthetic appeal in water gardens.
