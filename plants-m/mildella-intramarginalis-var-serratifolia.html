Mildella intramarginalis var. serratifolia

Mildella intramarginalis var. serratifolia, commonly known as the serrated fringed gentian, is a perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and showy, blue to violet flowers with distinctive fringed petals. The leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have serrated edges, giving the plant its specific epithet. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
