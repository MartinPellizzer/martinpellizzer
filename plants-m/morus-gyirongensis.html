Morus gyirongensis

Morus gyirongensis, commonly known as the Gyirong mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to the Himalayan region of China and Nepal.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, aggregate fruits. The plant is adapted to high-altitude environments, often growing in rocky and mountainous terrains. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, while its leaves are typically pubescent on the underside.

The Gyirong mulberry plays a role in local ecosystems and has been used in traditional medicine.
