Macrocarphus

Macrocarphus, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant characterized by its large, floating leaves and showy white flowers.

It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in ponds and lakes. The leaves can reach up to 3 meters in diameter, providing shade and habitat for various aquatic organisms. Its flowers bloom in the early morning and close by midday, releasing a mild fragrance.

The plant plays an important role in maintaining water quality and supporting local biodiversity.
