Melothria pendula var. chlorocarpa

Melothria pendula var. chlorocarpa, commonly known as the green-fruit passion vine, is a tropical climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves with a glossy green surface. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by edible, green berries that turn red when mature. Its foliage and fruits are often used in traditional medicine and culinary practices.

The variety is distinguished by its consistently green fruit, setting it apart from other melothria species.
