Myrcia chilensis

Myrcia chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean myrtle, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal and mountainous regions. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are usually elliptical and have a leathery texture, while the fruit is a small, dark blue berry.

Myrcia chilensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine.
