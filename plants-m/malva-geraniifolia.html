Malva geraniifolia

Malva geraniifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
