Mnemion cornutum

Mnemion cornutum, commonly known as the horned milkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hairy stems and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces a milky sap, which is typical of the milkweed genus. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a waxy coating.

Mnemion cornutum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.
