Matelea linearis

Matelea linearis, commonly known as the slender milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species adapted to arid environments.

Matelea linearis is also known for its milky sap, which is a characteristic feature of the milkweed genus.
