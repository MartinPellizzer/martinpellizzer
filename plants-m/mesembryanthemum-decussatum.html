Mesembryanthemum decussatum

Mesembryanthemum decussatum, commonly known as the diamond flower or ice plant, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Mesembryanthemum decussatum is also known for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.
