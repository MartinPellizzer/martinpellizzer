Melandrium auriculatum

Melandrium auriculatum, commonly known as the yellow catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and can often be found in fields, roadsides, and along woodland edges.
