Miscanthus teretifolius

Miscanthus teretifolius, commonly known as narrow-leaved silvergrass, is a perennial grass native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves that are often silver-white underneath, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences that bloom in late summer, releasing light, feathery seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its ornamental value and potential as a bioenergy crop, it is widely cultivated in temperate regions.
