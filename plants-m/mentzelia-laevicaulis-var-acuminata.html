Mentzelia laevicaulis var. acuminata

Mentzelia laevicaulis var. acuminata, commonly known as the sharpleaf bluemink, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers arranged in a raceme, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in desert scrub and grassland environments.

Mentzelia laevicaulis var. acuminata is an important component of its native ecosystem, providing nectar for pollinators and contributing to local biodiversity.
