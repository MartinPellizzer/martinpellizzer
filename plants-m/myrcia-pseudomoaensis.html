Myrcia pseudomoaensis

Myrcia pseudomoaensis, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and small, showy white or pink flowers. It produces small, berry-like fruits that are often consumed by local wildlife.

Myrcia pseudomoaensis is valued for its ecological role and is also used in traditional medicine.
