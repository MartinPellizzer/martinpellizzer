Mappa tannensis

Mappa tannensis, commonly known as the Tannen tree, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters in height, featuring a broad, spreading canopy. The leaves are alternate, simple, and have a rough texture with a distinctive dark green upper surface. The flowers are small and greenish-white, appearing in clusters, while the fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds.

This plant is valued for its hard wood and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.
