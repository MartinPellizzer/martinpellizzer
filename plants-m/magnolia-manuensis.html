Magnolia manuensis

Magnolia manuensis, commonly known as the mountain magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. Its bark is smooth and grayish, and it often grows in moist, well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
