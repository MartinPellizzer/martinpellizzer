Melissa coccinea

Melissa coccinea, commonly known as red lemon balm, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and basil. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are fragrant and have a citrus-like scent, often used in herbal teas and traditional medicine.

Melissa coccinea thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
