Melicope pergamentacea

Melicope pergamentacea, commonly known as the paperwood or sandpaper tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows in coastal and lowland areas. The plant is characterized by its rough, sandpaper-like bark and simple, alternate leaves. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits.

Melicope pergamentacea is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
