Melodinus acutus

Melodinus acutus, commonly known as the sharp-leaved melodinus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and moths. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

The species is valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
