Melastoma ebracteatum

Melastoma ebracteatum, commonly known as the small-leaved melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are usually pink or white, arranged in clusters, and are followed by small, capsule-like fruits.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
