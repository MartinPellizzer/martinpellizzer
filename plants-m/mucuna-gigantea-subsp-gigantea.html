Mucuna gigantea subsp. gigantea

Mucuna gigantea subsp.

gigantea, commonly known as the giant velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of South America. It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, showy red or orange flowers.

The plant produces long, bean-like pods that contain numerous flat, dark seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and abandoned fields.

The species is notable for its rapid growth and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it ecologically significant in certain ecosystems.
