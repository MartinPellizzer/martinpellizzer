Myristica aruana

Myristica aruana, commonly known as the Aru nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Maluku Islands of Indonesia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is closely related to the nutmeg tree, Myristica fragrans. The tree produces a fruit that contains a single seed, which is encased in a reddish, fleshy aril. The seeds are valued for their aromatic oils and are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in both ecological and economic contexts.
