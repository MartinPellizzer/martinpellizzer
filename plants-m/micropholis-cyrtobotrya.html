Micropholis cyrtobotrya

Micropholis cyrtobotrya, commonly known as the Natal tamarind, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The tree has a dense canopy with lanceolate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in axillary or terminal inflorescences.

The fruit is a woody pod that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.
