Mucuna pulcherrima

Mucuna pulcherrima, commonly known as the velvet bean or cowpea vine, is a tropical legume native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety pubescence covering its stems and leaves, which gives it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that develop into elongated, cylindrical pods containing edible seeds. It is a climbing plant with trifoliate leaves and is often used in traditional medicine.

Mucuna pulcherrima is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it beneficial in agricultural settings.
