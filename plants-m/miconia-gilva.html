Miconia gilva

Miconia gilva, commonly known as the small miconia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its dense foliage and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments and can reach heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

Miconia gilva is also known for its ability to form dense thickets, which can impact local ecosystems.
