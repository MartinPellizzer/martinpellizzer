Mentha gentilis f. hackenbruckii

Mentha gentilis f. hackenbruckii, commonly known as the Hackenbruckii mint, is a subspecies of the peppermint plant.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly hairy leaves and a strong, menthol-like fragrance. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands or stream banks. It produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The subspecies is valued for its use in herbal teas and as a source of essential oils.
