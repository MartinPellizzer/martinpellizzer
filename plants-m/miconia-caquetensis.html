Miconia caquetensis

Miconia caquetensis, commonly known as the Amazonian miconia, is a member of the Melastomataceae family and is native to the rainforests of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height, characterized by its large, glossy, ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that are often surrounded by velvety bracts. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that release tiny seeds when mature.

Miconia caquetensis is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
