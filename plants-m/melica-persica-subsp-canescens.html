Melica persica subsp. canescens

Melica persica subsp.

canescens, commonly known as Persian milletgrass, is a perennial grass species native to regions of Central Asia and the Middle East. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered with a fine layer of silky hairs.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny florets, which are typically greenish or pale brown in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along riverbanks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
