Mespilus monogyna var. brevispina

Mespilus monogyna var. brevispina, commonly known as the European whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to Europe.

It is characterized by its small, rounded leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in spring. The fruit is a small, smooth, yellowish drupe that ripens in late summer. It prefers well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and hedgerows.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and historical significance in traditional landscapes.
