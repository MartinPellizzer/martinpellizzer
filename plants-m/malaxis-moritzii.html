Malaxis moritzii

Malaxis moritzii, commonly known as the Moritz's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, greenish or brownish flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and has a complex root system adapted to nutrient-poor soils. Its flowers are usually arranged in a raceme and are pollinated by specific insect species.

Malaxis moritzii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in specialized horticultural settings.
