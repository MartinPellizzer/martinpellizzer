Monocladus macrophyllus

Monocladus macrophyllus, commonly known as the giant monoclad, is a unique plant species characterized by its large, broad leaves.

It belongs to the family Monocladaceae and is native to certain regions of Southeast Asia. The plant exhibits a distinctive growth habit with a single stem and elongated, lance-shaped leaves. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and flowers that are typically small and clustered.

It is of interest to botanists due to its unusual morphology and limited distribution.
