Mucuna stanleyi

Mucuna stanleyi, commonly known as Stanley's velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers followed by elongated pods containing dark brown, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in secondary forests.

Mucuna stanleyi is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
