Mentha × kerneri var. castriferrensis

Mentha × kerneri var. castriferrensis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hybrid mint, is a cultivated variety resulting from the cross between Mentha × piperita and Mentha spicata.

It is characterized by its slender, serrated leaves and strong, menthol-like fragrance. This plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and prefers moist, well-drained soil in partial shade. It is often used in herbal teas and aromatherapy due to its aromatic properties.

The variety is notable for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of climates.
