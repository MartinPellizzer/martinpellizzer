Mimulus aurantiacus

Mimulus aurantiacus, commonly known as the yellow monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers that resemble small monkey faces, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as stream banks and meadows. It has heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Mimulus aurantiacus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local ecosystems.
