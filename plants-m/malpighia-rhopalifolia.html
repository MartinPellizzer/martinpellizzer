Malpighia rhopalifolia

Malpighia rhopalifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf granadilla, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of small, bright red berries that are edible. The plant belongs to the Malpighiaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and adaptability to various soil types. Its leaves are typically ovate with a smooth texture and a slightly pubescent surface.

The fruit, which ripens in late summer, is valued for its flavor and is sometimes used in culinary applications.
