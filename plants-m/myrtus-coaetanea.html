Myrtus coaetanea

Myrtus coaetanea, commonly known as the common myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are fragrant and often used in perfumery. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring, followed by bright red berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Myrtus coaetanea is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in religious and medicinal contexts.
