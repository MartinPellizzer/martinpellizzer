Machaerium discolor

Machaerium discolor, commonly known as the pink machaerium, is a tropical perennial plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and feathery, trifoliate leaves that are often tinged with pink or red hues. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
