Manilkara concolor

Manilkara concolor, commonly known as the green sapodilla, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen canopy and smooth, greyish bark. The tree produces large, heart-shaped leaves and yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit, which is typically round and smooth, contains a white, sweet pulp surrounded by a hard, inedible shell.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
