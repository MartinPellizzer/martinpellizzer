Myrcia neohotteana

Myrcia neohotteana, commonly known as the Hottentot myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains numerous small, dark seeds.

Myrcia neohotteana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has potential applications in traditional medicine.
