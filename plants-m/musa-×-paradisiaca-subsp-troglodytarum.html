Musa × paradisiaca subsp. troglodytarum

Musa × paradisiaca subsp.

troglodytarum, commonly known as the cave banana, is a subspecies of the banana plant characterized by its unique adaptation to subterranean environments. It possesses thick, fleshy rhizomes that store nutrients and water, enabling survival in dark, humid cave systems.

The leaves are broad and robust, with a waxy surface to reduce water loss in low-light conditions. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, with a structure that facilitates pollination by cave-dwelling insects.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its specialized morphology and ecological niche.
