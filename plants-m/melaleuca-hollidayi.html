Melaleuca hollidayi

Melaleuca hollidayi, commonly known as the Grey Melaleuca, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to the coastal regions of northern Queensland, Australia, where it thrives in sandy soils and humid climates. The plant is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in winter. It is often found in heathlands and coastal dunes, contributing to the biodiversity of these ecosystems.

Due to its ecological importance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
