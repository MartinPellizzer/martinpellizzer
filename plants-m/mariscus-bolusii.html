Mariscus bolusii

Mariscus bolusii, commonly known as the Bolus's ice plant, is a succulent native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that store water. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is popular among succulent enthusiasts.
