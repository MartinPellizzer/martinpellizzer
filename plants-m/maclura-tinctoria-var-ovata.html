Maclura tinctoria var. ovata

Maclura tinctoria var. ovata, commonly known as the yellow wood or golden vine, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, light-colored bark and heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers followed by orange to red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. Its wood is highly valued for its rich, golden color and is used in the production of dyes and furniture.

The plant is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used historically in traditional herbal remedies.
