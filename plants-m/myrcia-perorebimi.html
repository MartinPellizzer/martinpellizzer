Myrcia perorebimi

Myrcia perorebimi, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of eastern Brazil, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. Myrcia perorebimi produces small, red, berry-like fruits that are edible and often used in traditional medicine.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
