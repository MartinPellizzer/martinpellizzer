Microlicia graveolens var. seringeana

Microlicia graveolens var. seringeana, commonly known as the sweet-smelling evergreen shrub, is a botanical species native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters and thrives in humid, tropical climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive, pleasant scent when crushed.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.
