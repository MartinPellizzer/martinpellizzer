Myrcia laruotteana

Myrcia laruotteana, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are red when mature. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Myrcia laruotteana is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been introduced to other parts of the world, sometimes becoming invasive.
