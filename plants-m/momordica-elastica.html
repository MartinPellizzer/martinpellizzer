Momordica elastica

Momordica elastica, commonly known as the rubber vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, flexible stems that can elongate significantly, often reaching lengths of several meters. The plant produces pale yellow flowers followed by elongated, warty fruits that contain black, spherical seeds. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems possess a unique rubbery quality.

This species is notable for its potential use in natural rubber production and traditional medicinal practices.
