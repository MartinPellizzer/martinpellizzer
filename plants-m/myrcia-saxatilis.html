Myrcia saxatilis

Myrcia saxatilis, commonly known as the rock myrtle, is a species of flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows in rocky or coastal environments. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is a red, fleshy drupe that attracts birds and other wildlife.

Myrcia saxatilis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and visual appeal.
