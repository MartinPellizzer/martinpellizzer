Miconia serpens

Miconia serpens, commonly known as the snakeleaf miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Miconia serpens is often found in moist, shaded areas of rainforests and is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets.
