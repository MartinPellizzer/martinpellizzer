Masdevallia cuprea subsp. hepatica

Masdevallia cuprea subsp.

hepatica, commonly known as the coppery liverwort orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America. It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist forest understories.

The plant features a basal rosette of fleshy, greenish-brown leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, moss-like structures. Its flowers are usually small, with a distinctive coppery hue, and are borne on slender, erect stems.

This subspecies is noted for its unique morphology and is valued for its ornamental appeal in specialized horticulture.
