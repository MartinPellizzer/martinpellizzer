Mentha pseudhostii

Mentha pseudhostii, commonly known as the Siberian mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the colder regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas and has lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive minty aroma. Its flowers are small, tubular, and clustered in whorls, often producing a pale blue or purple hue.

Mentha pseudhostii is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
