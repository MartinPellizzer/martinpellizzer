Mnesithea striata

Mnesithea striata, commonly known as the stripe-leaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated leaves with distinct longitudinal stripes, which give it its common name. The plant produces showy, pale pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal and Mediterranean climates.

Mnesithea striata is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
