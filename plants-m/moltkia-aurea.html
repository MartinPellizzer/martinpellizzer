Moltkia aurea

Moltkia aurea, commonly known as golden mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that grows up to 2 feet tall, featuring hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and capsules that contain numerous small seeds.

Moltkia aurea is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert or arid environments.
