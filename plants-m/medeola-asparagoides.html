Medeola asparagoides

Medeola asparagoides, commonly known as the red root or red bladder, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the family Asparagaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are often reddish in color. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pale green flowers in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and is often found in rich, loamy soils.

Medeola asparagoides is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally by indigenous peoples.
