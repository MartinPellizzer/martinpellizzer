Marattia microphylla

Marattia microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaf marattia, is a species of fern belonging to the family Marattiaceae.

It is characterized by its large, leathery fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines. Its fronds are typically divided into narrow, feather-like segments called pinnae.

Marattia microphylla thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests or on rocky outcrops.
