Myrtus communis var. acuminata

Myrtus communis var. acuminata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved myrtle, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, dark green, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly aromatic scent. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible, dark purple berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice for landscaping and hedges.
