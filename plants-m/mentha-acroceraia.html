Mentha acroceraia

Mentha acroceraia, commonly known as the alpine mint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and cool climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and emit a strong minty scent when crushed.

Mentha acroceraia is often used in traditional medicine for its purported cooling and digestive properties.
