Mentha mollissima var. undulata

Mentha mollissima var. undulata, commonly known as the wavy-haired mint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves that have a wavy or undulating margin, giving it its specific epithet. The plant produces small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

This variety is valued for its aromatic leaves, which are used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.
