Melica taurica subsp. monticola

Melica taurica subsp.

monticola, commonly known as the mountain melica, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and southern Europe. It typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils, often in alpine or subalpine habitats.

The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves, and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowers are usually greenish or brownish, and it is wind-pollinated.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils in rugged terrain.
