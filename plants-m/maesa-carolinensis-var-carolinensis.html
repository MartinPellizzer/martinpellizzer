Maesa carolinensis var. carolinensis

Maesa carolinensis var. carolinensis, commonly known as Carolina maesa, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring lance-shaped leaves with a slightly rough texture. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
