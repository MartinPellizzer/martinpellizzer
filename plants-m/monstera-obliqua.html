Monstera obliqua

Monstera obliqua, commonly known as the Mexican giant leaf plant, is a tropical climbing plant native to southern Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, deeply lobed leaves that can grow up to two meters in length. The plant has a robust stem with aerial roots that allow it to climb and anchor itself to trees or structures. Its leaves are typically a deep green with a smooth texture and may have a slight waxy sheen.

Monstera obliqua is prized for its dramatic foliage and is a popular houseplant due to its ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
