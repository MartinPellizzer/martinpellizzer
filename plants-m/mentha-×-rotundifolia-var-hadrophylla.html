Mentha × rotundifolia var. hadrophylla

Mentha × rotundifolia var. hadrophylla, commonly known as round-leaf mint, is a hybrid plant that exhibits characteristics of both Mentha rotundifolia and Mentha × piperita.

It is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically growing in moist, shady environments. The plant features rounded, toothed leaves that are often a deep green color, and it produces small, pale blue to purple flowers. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, making it useful in culinary and medicinal applications.

The varietal form hadrophylla is distinguished by its more compact growth habit and enhanced floral display compared to the standard variety.
