Myristica pedicellata

Myristica pedicellata, commonly known as the nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, woody fruit that contains the aromatic seeds used for nutmeg and mace. The plant produces small, white flowers and has compound leaves with three to five leaflets. Its botanical significance lies in its role as a source of valuable spices and its ecological importance in tropical rainforests.

The tree thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
