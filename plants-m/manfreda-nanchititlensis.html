Manfreda nanchititlensis

Manfreda nanchititlensis, commonly known as the Nanchitlensis prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in spines and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to extreme drought conditions. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it stores water in its fleshy stems.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
