Mammillaria sempervivi

Mammillaria sempervivi, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and clusters of spines that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, colorful flowers in shades of red, pink, and white during the winter months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is popular as a houseplant and is relatively easy to care for.
