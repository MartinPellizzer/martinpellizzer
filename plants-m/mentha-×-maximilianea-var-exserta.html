Mentha × maximilianea var. exserta

Mentha × maximilianea var. exserta, commonly known as the Mexican mint or wild mint, is a hybrid perennial herb native to Central America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, serrated leaves and clusters of small, pale purple flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight. Its rapid growth and spreading habit make it an effective ground cover in shaded or damp environments.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and as a source of essential oils.
