Misopates chrysothales

Misopates chrysothales, commonly known as the golden star-of-Bethlehem, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, yellow-orange flowers with a distinctive star-shaped appearance. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
