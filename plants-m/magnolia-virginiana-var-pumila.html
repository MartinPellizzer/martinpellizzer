Magnolia virginiana var. pumila

Magnolia virginiana var. pumila, commonly known as the dwarf magnolia, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has smooth, dark green leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet, making it suitable for smaller garden spaces.

Its ornamental value comes from its showy flowers and compact growth habit.
