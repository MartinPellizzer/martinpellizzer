Moraea teretifolia

Moraea teretifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved moraea, is a striking perennial bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces tall, branched stems with showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and purple. The plant is adapted to rocky and arid environments, often growing in well-drained soils. Its flowering period typically occurs in late winter to early spring, making it a valuable addition to rock gardens.

Moraea teretifolia is also noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
