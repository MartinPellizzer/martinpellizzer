Malveopsis rotundifolia

Malveopsis rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its broad, rounded leaves and showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners interested in native plants.
