Muraltia obovata

Muraltia obovata, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or muraltia, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to South America.

It features opposite, obovate leaves that are glossy and dark green, often with a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, cherry-like fruits. It is well-suited for tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
