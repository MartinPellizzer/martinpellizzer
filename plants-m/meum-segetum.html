Meum segetum

Meum segetum, commonly known as field bindweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy and stems that are creeping or climbing. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers with five petals, typically blooming from June to September. Its roots are fibrous and can become invasive, making it a challenging weed in agricultural settings.

Meum segetum is adapted to a wide range of soil types and can thrive in both sunny and partially shaded environments.
