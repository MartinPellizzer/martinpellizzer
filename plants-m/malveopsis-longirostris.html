Malveopsis longirostris

Malveopsis longirostris, commonly known as the long-beaked mallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its fruit is a schizocarp consisting of three or four small, dry nuts. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
