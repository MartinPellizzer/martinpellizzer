Mentha longifolia var. ramosissima

Mentha longifolia var. ramosissima, commonly known as wild mint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a serrated edge. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in whorled clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its distinctive minty scent and various medicinal uses.
