Maxillaria serotina

Maxillaria serotina, commonly known as the late-blooming orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen pseudobulbs and linear, dark green leaves. The plant produces fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in late summer or early autumn. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Maxillaria serotina is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for orchid enthusiasts.
