Microlicia cinerea var. oblongifolia

Microlicia cinerea var. oblongifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf microlicia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves that are covered in fine, fuzzy hairs. The plant produces small, pale green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in tropical forests.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
