Miconia sepiaria

Miconia sepiaria, commonly known as the purple miconia, is a species of flowering plant in the Melastomataceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, purple-bracted inflorescences. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it has been introduced to other tropical regions, though it can become invasive in some areas.
