Micropiper pellucidum

Micropiper pellucidum, commonly known as the glassy pipevine, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its translucent, waxy leaves that give it a glass-like appearance, and its small, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. It has a long flowering period and produces distinctive, fragrant blossoms that attract specific pollinators.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
