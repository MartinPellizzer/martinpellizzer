Mesosetum sclerochloa

Mesosetum sclerochloa, commonly known as the rigid false beardgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. It thrives in sandy or well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along roadsides.

Mesosetum sclerochloa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in disturbed areas.
