Matayba falcata

Matayba falcata, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Erythroxylum falcatum*, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves that are often reddish underneath. The plant produces small, pale green flowers that give way to orange-red berries containing seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded areas of forests.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
