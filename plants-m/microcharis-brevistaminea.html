Microcharis brevistaminea

Microcharis brevistaminea, commonly known as the short-stemmed morning glory, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, blooming in the early morning. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its rapid growth and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a garden plant.
