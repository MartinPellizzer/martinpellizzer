Mesembryanthemum denticulatum

Mesembryanthemum denticulatum, commonly known as the toothed ice plant, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and yellow, typically during the cooler months. It thrives in well-draining soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ability to store water in its leaves allows it to survive in drought conditions, showcasing its adaptation to harsh climates.
