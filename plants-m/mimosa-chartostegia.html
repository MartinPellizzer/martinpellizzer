Mimosa chartostegia

Mimosa chartostegia, commonly known as the red mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, delicate flowers. The plant is known for its sensitive leaves that fold shut when touched, a response triggered by mechanical stimuli. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its ecological importance includes providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain insect species.
