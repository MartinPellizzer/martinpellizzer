Mitranthus

Mitranthus, commonly known as the "flower of the devil," is a genus of flowering plants in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

These plants are native to South America, particularly Brazil, and are characterized by their striking, often brightly colored flowers. The genus includes several species, such as Mitranthus coccineus, which is known for its vivid red blooms. The flowers are typically tubular and attract pollinators like hummingbirds and bees.

Mitranthus plants are often cultivated for their ornamental value due to their unique and eye-catching appearance.
