Malvinda alnifolia

Malvinda alnifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf mallows, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton, hibiscus, and okra. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant in arid and semi-arid regions.

Malvinda alnifolia is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
