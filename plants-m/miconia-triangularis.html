Miconia triangularis

Miconia triangularis, commonly known as the triangular miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that are followed by distinctive, three-lobed, red or orange fruits. Miconia triangularis is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments.

It is also noted for its ecological importance, providing food and habitat for various native wildlife species.
