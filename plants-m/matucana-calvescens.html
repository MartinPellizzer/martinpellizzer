Matucana calvescens

Matucana calvescens, commonly known as the Calvescens cycad, is a member of the Cycadaceae family and is native to Brazil.

It is a slow-growing, evergreen plant characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of large, feather-like leaves. The plant has a distinctive, rugged appearance with a thick, fibrous trunk that develops over time. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for warm, arid climates.

Due to its ornamental value and conservation status, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
