Medicago pourretii

Medicago pourretii, commonly known as the Pourret's clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow pea-like flowers. The plant is typically found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in nutrient-poor conditions.

Medicago pourretii is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
