Melaleuca angulata

Melaleuca angulata, commonly known as the angular melaleuca, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to northern Australia.

It typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, often found in heathlands and coastal dunes. The plant produces small, fragrant white or cream flowers that appear in clusters, usually during the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a leathery texture and a distinctive aromatic scent.

The species is notable for its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient component of its native ecosystem.
