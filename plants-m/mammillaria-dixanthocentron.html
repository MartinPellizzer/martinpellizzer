Mammillaria dixanthocentron

Mammillaria dixanthocentron, commonly known as the Mexican night bloomer, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in white, woolly spines and small, rounded tubercles. The plant produces pale yellow to orange flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and blooming habits, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
