Melocactus monoclonos

Melocactus monoclonos, commonly known as the single-stem melon cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its solitary, columnar stem that can grow up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces a spiny, ribbed structure with areoles that bear both spines and flowers. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, emerging from the areoles during the warmer months.

The species is well adapted to desert environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stem.
