Malaxis melanotoessa

Malaxis melanotoessa, commonly known as the black-tongued orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, elongated, and often dark-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its floral structure includes a single, showy flower with a distinctive, hood-like lip.

The species is noted for its rarity and is often found in specific microhabitats, making it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
