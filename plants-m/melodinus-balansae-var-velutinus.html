Melodinus balansae var. velutinus

Melodinus balansae var. velutinus, commonly known as the velvet sylph, is a unique variant of the Melodinus genus native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

This plant is characterized by its velvety, hairy foliage, which gives it its common name and distinguishes it from other varieties. It typically grows as an epiphytic or lithophytic plant, often found clinging to tree trunks or rock surfaces in humid tropical environments. The leaves are usually broad and lance-shaped, with a striking coloration that ranges from deep green to reddish tones.

Its flowering is rare and occurs in clusters, producing small, fragrant flowers that attract specific pollinators.
