Malva gymnocarpa

Malva gymnocarpa, commonly known as the smooth-seeded mallows, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton, hibiscus, and okra. The plant is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its fruit is a smooth, capsule-like structure that contains numerous small seeds.

Malva gymnocarpa is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and riverbanks.
