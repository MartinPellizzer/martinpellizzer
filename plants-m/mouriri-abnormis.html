Mouriri abnormis

Mouriri abnormis, commonly known as the Brazilian sour orange, is a small evergreen tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a glossy green surface. The plant produces fragrant, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, round fruits that are typically sour and orange in color.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts within its native habitat.
