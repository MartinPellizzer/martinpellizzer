Malaxis amplexicolumna

Malaxis amplexicolumna, commonly known as the columnar malaxis, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique morphology, including a slender, columnar structure. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically arranged in a raceme, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the forest floor, and it is often found growing on trees or rocks in its natural habitat.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
