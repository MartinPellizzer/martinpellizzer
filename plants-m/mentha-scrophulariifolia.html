Mentha scrophulariifolia

Mentha scrophulariifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mint, is a perennial herb native to regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Mentha scrophulariifolia is known for its aromatic, mint-like scent and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

It is a member of the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is valued for its potential medicinal properties.
