Manettia auratifolia

Manettia auratifolia, commonly known as the golden-leaved manettia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-green foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape and often have a velvety texture.

Manettia auratifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in humid, shady environments.
