Microbriza poimorpha

Microbriza poimorpha, commonly known as the pink sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual with slender, branching stems and small, showy pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in dry, open habitats such as deserts or rocky areas. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in arid gardens.
