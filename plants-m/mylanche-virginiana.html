Mylanche virginiana

Mylanche virginiana, commonly known as the Virginia mylanche, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. The plant features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a smooth texture. Its small, greenish-white flowers appear in clusters during the spring and summer months.

Mylanche virginiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
