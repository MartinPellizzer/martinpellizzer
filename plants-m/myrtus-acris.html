Myrtus acris

Myrtus acris, commonly known as sweet myrtle, is a flowering shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in spring, followed by dark blue berries. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or coastal gardens.

Myrtus acris is also valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in perfumery.
