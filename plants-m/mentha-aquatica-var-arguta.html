Mentha aquatica var. arguta

Mentha aquatica var. arguta, commonly known as narrow-leaved water mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist environments such as stream banks, wet meadows, and marshy areas. The plant features slender, aromatic leaves that are lance-shaped and often have a serrated edge. Its flowers are small, clustered in whorls, and typically bloom in shades of white or pale purple.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
