Melodorum clavipes

Melodorum clavipes, commonly known as the slender bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, woodland environments, preferring partial to full shade. The plant features slender, branching stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, which are usually pale blue or violet in color. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Melodorum clavipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
