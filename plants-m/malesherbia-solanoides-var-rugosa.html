Malesherbia solanoides var. rugosa

Malesherbia solanoides var. rugosa, commonly known as the roughleaf malesherbia, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It features woody stems with a rugged, textured appearance, and its leaves are typically ovate with a hairy or pubescent surface. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and delicate floral display.
