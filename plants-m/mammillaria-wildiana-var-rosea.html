Mammillaria wildiana var. rosea

Mammillaria wildiana var. rosea, commonly known as the roseate mammillaria, is a compact cactus native to Mexico.

It features a globular stem covered in white to pale pink tubercles, which are small, rounded growths. The plant produces pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is well-suited for rock gardens and requires well-draining soil and minimal water.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative succulent.
