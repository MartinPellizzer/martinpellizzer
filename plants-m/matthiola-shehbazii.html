Matthiola shehbazii

Matthiola shehbazii, commonly known as the Kashmiri stock, is a flowering plant native to the Kashmir region of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in early summer. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a long history of use in traditional medicine.

The species is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and climates.
