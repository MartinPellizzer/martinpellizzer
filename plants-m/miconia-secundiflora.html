Miconia secundiflora

Miconia secundiflora, commonly known as the Brazilian flame tree, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a bright red, fleshy berry that contains numerous seeds.

Miconia secundiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in landscaping in warm climates.
