Marginaria fimbriata

Marginaria fimbriata, commonly known as the fringed marginaria, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, hairy stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in coastal areas. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a distinctive fringed edge that gives it its common name.

Marginaria fimbriata is an important species for pollinators and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
