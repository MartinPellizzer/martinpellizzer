Myconia chrysanthemum

Myconia chrysanthemum, commonly known as the golden myconia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of soft, white hairs.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its vibrant color and unique appearance.
