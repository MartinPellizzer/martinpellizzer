Monnina salicifolia var. salicifolia

Monnina salicifolia var. salicifolia, commonly known as silky blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and its clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a silky texture due to the fine hairs covering them. The plant produces small, bead-like fruits that are dispersed by wind.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
