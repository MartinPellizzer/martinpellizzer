Malpighia baracoensis

Malpighia baracoensis, commonly known as the Brazilian wolfberry, is a shrub native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Malpighiaceae family and is characterized by its small, bright red berries that resemble tiny tomatoes. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that are typically pink or white in color. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The fruit is edible and has a tart, tangy flavor, making it a valued plant in traditional medicine and local cuisine.
