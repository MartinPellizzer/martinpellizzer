Micropus heterophyllus

Micropus heterophyllus, commonly known as the variable leaf cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its succulent, spiny stems that store water, allowing it to survive in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom during the warm season. Its leaves are often reduced to spines, while the stems are ribbed and can vary in shape depending on environmental conditions.

This plant is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a hardy species in desert and semi-arid regions.
