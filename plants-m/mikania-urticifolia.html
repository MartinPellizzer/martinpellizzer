Mikania urticifolia

Mikania urticifolia, commonly known as the stinging vine, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and opposite leaves. The leaves are often covered in fine hairs that can cause an irritating rash upon contact with skin. It produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are pollinated by insects.

This plant is often found in moist, disturbed habitats such as forests and along stream banks.
