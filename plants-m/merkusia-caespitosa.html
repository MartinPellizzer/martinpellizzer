Merkusia caespitosa

Merkusia caespitosa, commonly known as the tufted merkusia, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like growth habit and produces small, showy flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and grassy, with a slight bluish-green hue.

Merkusia caespitosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
