Maytenus buxifolia subsp. buxifolia

Maytenus buxifolia subsp.

buxifolia, commonly known as the waxleaf mayten, is a species native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Malaysia and Indonesia. It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub, typically reaching heights of up to 10 meters.

The plant is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage, with elliptical leaves that are glossy and often covered in a fine, waxy coating. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, fleshy berries that are yellow when ripe.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
