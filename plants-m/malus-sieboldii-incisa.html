Malus sieboldii incisa

Malus sieboldii incisa, commonly known as the Japanese flowering crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring clusters of white or pink flowers in spring. The plant has simple, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn, adding to its seasonal appeal. Its fruits are small, red drupes that ripen in late summer.

Malus sieboldii incisa is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty and adaptability to various soil types.
