Mentha hillebrandtii

Mentha hillebrandtii, commonly known as the Hillebrandt's mint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a serrated edge. The plant produces small, pale blue to violet flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. Mentha hillebrandtii is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is valued for its aromatic properties.

It is a member of the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
