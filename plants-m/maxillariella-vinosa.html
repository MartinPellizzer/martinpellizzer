Maxillariella vinosa

Maxillariella vinosa, commonly known as the pink lady's slipper, is a species of orchid native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking pink and white petals, which resemble a slipper, and its fragrant, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are elongated and grass-like, often forming a dense rosette.

Maxillariella vinosa is an epiphytic orchid, meaning it grows on the surface of trees or other plants without deriving nutrients from them.
