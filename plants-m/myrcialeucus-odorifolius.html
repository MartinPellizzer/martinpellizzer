Myrcialeucus odorifolius

Myrcialeucus odorifolius, commonly known as the fragrant myrtle, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, leathery leaves that emit a distinctive, pleasant scent. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their purported antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
