Macrolenes nemorosa var. bancana

Macrolenes nemorosa var. bancana, commonly known as the silver leaf tree, is a variant of the Macrolenes nemorosa species, characterized by its distinctive silvery foliage.

It is native to certain regions of Southeast Asia and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves with a prominent silver or grayish-white underside, giving it a striking appearance. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying properties.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.
