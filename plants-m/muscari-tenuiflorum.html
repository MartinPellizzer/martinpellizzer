Muscari tenuiflorum

Muscari tenuiflorum, commonly known as the narrowflower grape hyacinth, is a bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, resembling grapes, hence its common name. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and blooms in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Muscari tenuiflorum is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate.
