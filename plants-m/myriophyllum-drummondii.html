Myriophyllum drummondii

Myriophyllum drummondii, commonly known as water milfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to North America.

It has slender, branching stems and small, whorled leaves that are often mistaken for roots due to their appearance. The plant produces tiny, floating flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes.

Myriophyllum drummondii plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen for various aquatic organisms.
