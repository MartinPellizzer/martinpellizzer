Mariscus flavus var. humilis

Mariscus flavus var. humilis, commonly known as the yellow sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It features small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, blooming in early spring. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in dry environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical structure is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures.
