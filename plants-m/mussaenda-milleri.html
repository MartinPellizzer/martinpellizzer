Mussaenda milleri

Mussaenda milleri, commonly known as the golden trumpet bush, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange inflorescences that resemble trumpets, which are clustered at the ends of branches. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and it thrives in warm, humid climates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Mussaenda milleri is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
