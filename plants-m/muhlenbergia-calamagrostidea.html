Muhlenbergia calamagrostidea

Muhlenbergia calamagrostidea, commonly known as the California muhlenbergia, is a species of grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like blades that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer, adding ornamental value to gardens and natural landscapes. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

This plant is also valued for its ability to stabilize soils and prevent erosion in arid environments.
