Mucuna wertheimii

Mucuna wertheimii, commonly known as the velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and trifoliate leaves, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, hairy pods containing edible seeds that are rich in protein and nutrients. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its seeds and as a forage crop.

Mucuna wertheimii also has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing soil fertility.
