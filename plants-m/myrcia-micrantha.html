Myrcia micrantha

Myrcia micrantha, commonly known as the small-flowered myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule containing several small, dark seeds.

Myrcia micrantha is valued for its ornamental qualities and has also been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
