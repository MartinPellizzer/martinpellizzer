Melica argyrea

Melica argyrea, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray blades and tufted inflorescences that resemble a plume. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

Melica argyrea is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support wetland biodiversity.
