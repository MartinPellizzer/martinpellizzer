Malva cuneifolia

Malva cuneifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating, and its flowers are typically pink or purple, with five petals. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Malva cuneifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
