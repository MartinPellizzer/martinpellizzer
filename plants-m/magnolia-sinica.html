Magnolia sinica

Magnolia sinica, commonly known as the Chinese magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its large, showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves appear. The tree has simple, alternate, ovate to elliptical leaves that turn yellow in autumn. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

The plant is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
