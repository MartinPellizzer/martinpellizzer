Maranta pruinosa

Maranta pruinosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant produces small, white flowers that are typically clustered in spikes. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine and as a starch source.

Maranta pruinosa thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
