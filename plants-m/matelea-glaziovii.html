Matelea glaziovii

Matelea glaziovii, commonly known as the Glaziov's mescal bean, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous structure and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant produces striking, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of protein.
