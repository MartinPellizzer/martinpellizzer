Monardella australis

Monardella australis, commonly known as the California blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Monardella australis thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.
