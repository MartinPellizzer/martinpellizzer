Macradenia amazonica

Macradenia amazonica, commonly known as the Amazonian star orchid, is a tropical orchid native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, star-shaped flowers that come in shades of yellow and orange, often with contrasting purple or red markings. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and drawing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain. Its roots are specialized for absorbing nutrients from the surrounding environment, and it thrives in high humidity and indirect sunlight.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
