Microptelea parvifolia

Microptelea parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved microptelea, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically less than 2 cm in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Microptelea parvifolia is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
