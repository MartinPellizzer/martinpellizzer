Melocanna virgata

Melocanna virgata, commonly known as the giant bamboo or giant reed, is a tall, grass-like plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its hollow, jointed stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and construction. Melocanna virgata is adapted to wet, tropical environments and is often found in swamps or along riverbanks.

Its rapid growth and robust structure make it an important species in both ecological and economic contexts.
