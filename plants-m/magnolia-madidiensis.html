Magnolia madidiensis

Magnolia madidiensis, commonly known as the Madidi magnolia, is a species of flowering plant native to the Amazon rainforest in Bolivia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white to pink flowers that bloom in the early spring. The plant has broad, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and arranged alternately along the stem. Its bark is smooth and grayish in color, and it can grow up to 20 meters tall.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also of ecological importance in its native habitat.
