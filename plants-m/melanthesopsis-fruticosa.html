Melanthesopsis fruticosa

Melanthesopsis fruticosa, commonly known as the red sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a low-growing, shrub-like species with woody stems and small, fleshy leaves adapted to conserve water. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. Its root system is extensive and deep, allowing it to access groundwater in dry conditions.

Melanthesopsis fruticosa is often found in rocky soils and is well-suited to harsh, semi-desert environments.
