Malus sylvestris var. domestica

Malus sylvestris var. domestica, commonly known as the domestic apple, is a cultivar of the domesticated apple tree.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and showy, white or pink blossoms. The plant produces edible fruits that vary in size, color, and flavor depending on the specific cultivar. It is a deciduous tree that thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its fruit has been cultivated for thousands of years for both culinary and medicinal uses.
