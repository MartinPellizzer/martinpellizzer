Michelia champaca

Michelia champaca, commonly known as the night-blooming champaca, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, yellow to golden-orange flowers that bloom at night, emitting a strong, sweet scent. The plant has simple, opposite, ovate leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, often marked with vertical lenticels.

Michelia champaca is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine and as a source of perfume.
