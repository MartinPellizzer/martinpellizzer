Myrtus viridis

Myrtus viridis, commonly known as the green myrtle, is a flowering shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and rocky environments.

Myrtus viridis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
