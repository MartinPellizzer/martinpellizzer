Mimosa mascarenensis

Mimosa mascarenensis, commonly known as the Mascarene mimosa, is a shrub native to the Mascarene Islands in the Indian Ocean.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which are composed of numerous small leaflets arranged in a feathery pattern. The plant produces small, yellow, globular flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. It thrives in coastal and sandy soils, often found in dry, sunny environments.

Mimosa mascarenensis is adapted to withstand salt spray and is an important component of the local flora.
