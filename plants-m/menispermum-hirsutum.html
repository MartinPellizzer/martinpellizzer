Menispermum hirsutum

Menispermum hirsutum, commonly known as the hairy arrowhead, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, showy yellow flowers. The plant is often found in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that contains several seeds, and it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
