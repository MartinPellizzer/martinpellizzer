Monsonia grandiflora

Monsonia grandiflora, commonly known as the giant monsonia, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features a thick, fleshy stem and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powder. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is widely cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions.
