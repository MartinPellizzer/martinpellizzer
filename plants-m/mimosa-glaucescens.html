Mimosa glaucescens

Mimosa glaucescens, commonly known as the blue mimosa, is a shrub native to South America.

It features small, fern-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, giving it its common name. The plant produces fluffy, yellow or white flower clusters that bloom in spring. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

Mimosa glaucescens is also known for its sensitivity to touch, as its leaves fold when disturbed.
