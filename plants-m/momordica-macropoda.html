Momordica macropoda

Momordica macropoda, commonly known as the large-fruit bitter gourd, is a flowering plant in the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its distinctive, warty, green fruits that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces yellowish-green flowers. Its fruits are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant is also valued as a vegetable in various cuisines, though its bitter taste requires careful preparation.
