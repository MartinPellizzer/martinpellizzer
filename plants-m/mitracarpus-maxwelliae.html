Mitracarpus maxwelliae

Mitracarpus maxwelliae, commonly known as Maxwell's mitracarpus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain the seeds.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
