Messerschmidia milleri

Messerschmidia milleri, commonly known as the ghost plant, is a succulent species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces small, white flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring or early summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts.
