Melilotus rariflorus

Melilotus rariflorus, commonly known as slender melilot, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters along the stems, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

Melilotus rariflorus is often found in dry meadows and grasslands, and it has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
