Masdevallia polyphemus

Masdevallia polyphemus, commonly known as the Giant Masdevallia, is a rare and striking orchid native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 10 inches in diameter, making it one of the largest orchids in the Masdevallia genus. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its native habitat. Its flowers are usually a vibrant pink or red with intricate patterns, and it blooms during the wet season.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
