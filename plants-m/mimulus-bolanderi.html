Mimulus bolanderi

Mimulus bolanderi, commonly known as Bolander's monkeyflower, is a species of flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. It has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Mimulus bolanderi is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
