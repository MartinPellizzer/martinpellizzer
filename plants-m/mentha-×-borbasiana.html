Mentha × borbasiana

Mentha × borbasiana, commonly known as hybrid mint, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between spearmint (Mentha spicata) and watermint (Mentha aquatica).

It is a perennial herb characterized by its square, hollow stems and opposite, serrated leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in whorls along the stem. Mentha × borbasiana is known for its strong, aromatic scent and is widely used in culinary and medicinal applications.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often found in temperate regions.
