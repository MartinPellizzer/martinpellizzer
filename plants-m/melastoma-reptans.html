Melastoma reptans

Melastoma reptans, commonly known as the creeping melastoma, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that range from pink to red, often with a dark center, blooming in the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The fruit is a capsule that releases tiny seeds, aiding in its propagation in suitable habitats.
