Macrosamanea aquatica

Macrosamanea aquatica, commonly known as the water hibiscus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and thrives in moist, well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance.

Additionally, it plays a role in supporting local wildlife by providing nectar for pollinators.
