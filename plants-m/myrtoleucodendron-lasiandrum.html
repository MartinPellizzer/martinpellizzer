Myrtoleucodendron lasiandrum

Myrtoleucodendron lasiandrum, commonly known as the woolly-leaved myrtle, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees. It thrives in coastal and sandy soils, often found in heathland environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in suitable climates.
