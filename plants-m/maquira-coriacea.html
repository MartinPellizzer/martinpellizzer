Maquira coriacea

Maquira coriacea, commonly known as the rough maquira, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, bark-like trunk and large, palmate leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, lowland areas and is often found in rainforests.

The tree is valued for its durable wood and has ecological importance as a habitat for various wildlife species.
