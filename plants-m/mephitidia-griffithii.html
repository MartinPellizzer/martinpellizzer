Mephitidia griffithii

Mephitidia griffithii, commonly known as the Griffith's mephit, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its leaves are ovate with a smooth texture and a slightly waxy coating.

Mephitidia griffithii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
