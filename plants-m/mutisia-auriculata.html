Mutisia auriculata

Mutisia auriculata, commonly known as the golden cupleaf, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers and heart-shaped leaves that resemble ears, giving it its common name. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and it produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind. Mutisia auriculata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
