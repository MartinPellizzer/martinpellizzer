Mimosa biflora

Mimosa biflora, commonly known as the twin-flower mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers that open in response to touch. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments. It is often found in the wild along the coasts of Brazil and Argentina.

Mimosa biflora is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
