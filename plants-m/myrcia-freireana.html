Myrcia freireana

Myrcia freireana, commonly known as Brazilian pepper tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and clusters of small, red berries. The plant is evergreen, with a compact, rounded shape, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance.

However, it can be invasive in certain regions, posing challenges to native ecosystems.
