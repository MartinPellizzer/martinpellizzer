Melastoma maraiparaiense

Melastoma maraiparaiense, commonly known as the Maraipara melastoma, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring dense foliage and showy, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a reddish or violet coloration. Its leaves are usually opposite, elliptical in shape, and have a glossy texture.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.
