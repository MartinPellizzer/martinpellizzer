Microlicia linifolia var. naudiniana

Microlicia linifolia var. naudiniana, commonly known as the Naudin's silky mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the roots of host trees, primarily in tropical and subtropical regions.

It possesses a complex vascular system that allows it to extract water and nutrients from its host, while also performing photosynthesis through its leaves. The plant's foliage is typically dense and velvety, with small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers, followed by small, fleshy berries that are dispersed by birds.

Due to its parasitic nature and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native range.
