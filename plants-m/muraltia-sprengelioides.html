Muraltia sprengelioides

Muraltia sprengelioides, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or red muraltia, is a flowering plant species native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy, red or pink flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Muraltia sprengelioides is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
