Mimosa implexa

Mimosa implexa, commonly known as the woolly mimosa, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators like bees. Its growth habit is typically compact, making it suitable for ornamental use in gardens.

Mimosa implexa is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions, though it prefers well-drained soil.
