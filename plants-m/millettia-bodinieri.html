Millettia bodinieri

Millettia bodinieri, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of yellow flowers that resemble small showers, hence its common name. The plant has pinnately compound leaves and produces pods that contain flat, winged seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree also has medicinal uses in traditional herbal practices.
