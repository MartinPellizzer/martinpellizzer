Mitragyna ciliata

Mitragyna ciliata, commonly known as kratom, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, white flowers. The plant contains alkaloids, particularly mitragynine and 7-hydroxymitragynine, which contribute to its pharmacological properties. It thrives in humid, tropical climates with well-drained soil.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine and for their stimulant and analgesic effects.
