Mikania chimborazensis

Mikania chimborazensis, commonly known as the Ecuadorian milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by fluffy, white seed heads that aid in wind dispersal. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
