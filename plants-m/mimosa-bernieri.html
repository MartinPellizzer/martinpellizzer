Mimosa bernieri

Mimosa bernieri, commonly known as the golden mimosa, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that fold shut when touched, a trait known as thigmonastic response. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.

Its unique appearance and adaptive features make it a notable species in the Mimosa genus.
