Melastoma hirtellum

Melastoma hirtellum, commonly known as the hairy melastoma, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Melastomataceae and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit. The plant features opposite, hairy leaves and produces showy, clustered flowers with five petals. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Melastoma hirtellum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas.
