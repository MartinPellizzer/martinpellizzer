Microstylis diploceras

Microstylis diploceras, commonly known as the narrow-styled grass, is a member of the Poaceae family, characterized by its slender, grass-like structure.

It typically grows in moist, temperate environments, often found in meadows or along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. Its flowers are wind-pollinated, a common trait among grasses, and it plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.

Due to its delicate appearance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes.
