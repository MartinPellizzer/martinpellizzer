Mimosa subsericea f. acerosa

Mimosa subsericea f. acerosa, commonly known as the steel-leaf mimosa, is a variant of the mimosa plant characterized by its distinctive, stiff, and leathery leaves.

This plant is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. Its leaves are typically small, oval-shaped, and have a rough texture, which helps it retain moisture in arid conditions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a delicate touch to its overall appearance.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and adaptability to various soil types.
