Matthiola pseudoxyceras var. viridis

Matthiola pseudoxyceras var. viridis, commonly known as the green-flowered wallflower, is a hybrid species within the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically reaches a height of 20 to 40 centimeters and produces small, four-petaled flowers arranged in racemes. Its leaves are linear and slightly hairy, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its color and fragrance.
