Myrcia lindeniana

Myrcia lindeniana, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, red, berry-like fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Myrcia lindeniana thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some regions for medicinal purposes.
