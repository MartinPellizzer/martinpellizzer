Myriostachya wightiana

Myriostachya wightiana, commonly known as the Wight's panic grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and produces dense clusters of small, spikelet-like flowers. The plant typically grows in open, dry habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous texture, and the inflorescence is a panicle composed of numerous spikelets.

Myriostachya wightiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
