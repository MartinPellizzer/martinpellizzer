Mussaenda macrosepala

Mussaenda macrosepala, commonly known as the large-sepal mussaenda, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring attractive, showy yellow flowers clustered in terminal inflorescences. The leaves are opposite, simple, and usually elliptical or ovate in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, large, fleshy sepals that are often more prominent than the petals.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.
