Manihot pseudoheterophylla

Manihot pseudoheterophylla, commonly known as the variable arrowleaf, is a species of flowering plant in the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology. The plant exhibits heterophyllous traits, meaning it produces different types of leaves depending on environmental conditions. Its leaves are typically arrow-shaped with a waxy surface, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

The plant is often found in wetland or marshy habitats, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils.
