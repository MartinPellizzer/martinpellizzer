Melaleuca leucadendra var. angusta

Melaleuca leucadendra var. angusta, commonly known as narrow-leaved paperbark, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and smooth, white to pale grey bark that peels in thin layers. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, typically during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or sandy environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate salt spray, making it suitable for coastal gardens.
