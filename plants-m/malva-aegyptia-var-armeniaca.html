Malva aegyptia var. armeniaca

Malva aegyptia var. armeniaca, commonly known as Armenian mallow, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It features broad, velvety leaves with a distinctive heart shape and produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

The flowers are not only ornamental but also attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
