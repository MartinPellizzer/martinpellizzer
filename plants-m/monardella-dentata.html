Monardella dentata

Monardella dentata, commonly known as toothleaf monarda, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features upright, hairy stems that bear opposite, toothed, ovate leaves with a distinctive serrated margin. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, or purple, arranged in terminal clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Its foliage and flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
