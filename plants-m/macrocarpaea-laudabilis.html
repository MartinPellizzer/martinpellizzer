Macrocarpaea laudabilis

Macrocarpaea laudabilis, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has upright, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery down. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds.
