Mimulus langsdorffii var. grandis

Mimulus langsdorffii var. grandis, commonly known as the large yellow monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to California and Baja California.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in open woodland areas. It has heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its stems are erect and branched.

This variety is known for its robust growth and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
