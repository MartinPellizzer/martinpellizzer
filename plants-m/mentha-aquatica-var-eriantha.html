Mentha aquatica var. eriantha

Mentha aquatica var. eriantha, commonly known as the water mint, is a variant of the aquatic mint native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers arranged in whorls along the stem, typically blooming in late summer. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, giving it a minty scent and making it useful in herbal remedies.

This variety thrives in moist soils and is often found near streams, ponds, and marshes.
