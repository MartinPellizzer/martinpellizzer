Mentha aquatica var. pluridentata

Mentha aquatica var. pluridentata, commonly known as the water mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy and have a serrated margin. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist environments such as wet meadows, stream banks, and marshes.

Its aromatic leaves contain menthol and other volatile oils, making it a valued plant in both traditional medicine and culinary applications.
