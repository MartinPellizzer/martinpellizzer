Melica ramosa subsp. major

Melica ramosa subsp.

major, commonly known as the tall false brome, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist, grassy habitats such as meadows and riverbanks.

The plant is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and elongated, narrow leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets arranged in a panicle, which is often slightly drooping.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
