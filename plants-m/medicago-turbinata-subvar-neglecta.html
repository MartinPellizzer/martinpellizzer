Medicago turbinata subvar. neglecta

Medicago turbinata subvar. neglecta, commonly known as the lesser crown vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has a sprawling growth habit and produces small, elongated pods containing several seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it useful in soil restoration and erosion control.
