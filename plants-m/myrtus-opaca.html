Myrtus opaca

Myrtus opaca, commonly known as common myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, glossy, dark green leaves that are aromatic when crushed. The plant produces small, white, five-petaled flowers that bloom in spring, followed by dark blue, edible berries. Myrtus opaca has a compact growth habit and is often used in hedges or as a decorative plant.

Its leaves and berries contain essential oils and have been used in traditional medicine and perfumery.
