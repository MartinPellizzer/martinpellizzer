Mentha filina

Mentha filina, commonly known as slender mint, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a strong, menthol-like scent.

Mentha filina is often used in traditional medicine for its cooling properties and digestive benefits.
