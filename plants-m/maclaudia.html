Maclaudia

Maclaudia, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, silvery-green leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, often in the spring or early summer. Its bark is smooth and gray, and it can grow up to 20 meters tall in its natural habitat.

Maclaudia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
