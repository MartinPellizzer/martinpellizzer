Melica ciliata f. pillata

Melica ciliata f. pillata, commonly known as the pillared meadowgrass, is a botanical variant of the species Melica ciliata.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in moist, temperate environments, often found in meadows and along stream banks. The plant is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and its distinctive, narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like arrangement of small, spikelets, which are often adorned with silky hairs.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
