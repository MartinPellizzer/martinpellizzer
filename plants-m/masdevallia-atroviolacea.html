Masdevallia atroviolacea

Masdevallia atroviolacea, commonly known as the black-violet masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit and produces striking violet to purple flowers with dark markings. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments, thriving at high elevations. Its leaves are elliptical and arranged in a rosette, contributing to its unique appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its intricate floral displays.
