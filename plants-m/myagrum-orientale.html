Myagrum orientale

Myagrum orientale, commonly known as the native hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring thick, leathery leaves with a waxy coating. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which bloom from late winter to early spring. Its flowers are often visited by birds and insects, contributing to its ecological importance.

The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.
