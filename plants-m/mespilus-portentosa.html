Mespilus portentosa

Mespilus portentosa, commonly known as the Persian medlar, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, rough, and often cracked bark. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in spring, followed by edible fruits that ripen in autumn. The fruit has a unique texture, with a firm outer layer and a soft, sweet interior when fully mature.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
