Malus baccata var. cerasifera

Malus baccata var. cerasifera, commonly known as the common hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe, western Asia, and northern Africa.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval-shaped leaves, and clusters of small white flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces bright red berries in the fall, which are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often used in hedgerows and landscape design due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
