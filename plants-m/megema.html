Megema

Megema, commonly known as the white-flowered bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped white or pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, with lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowering period occurs in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Megema is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
