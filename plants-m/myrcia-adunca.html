Myrcia adunca

Myrcia adunca, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various birds and wildlife. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

Myrcia adunca is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
