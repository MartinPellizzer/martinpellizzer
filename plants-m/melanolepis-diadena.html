Melanolepis diadena

Melanolepis diadena, commonly known as the red-veined ivy, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy leaves with distinctive red veins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spadices, a common feature in the Araceae family. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly, making it both a popular ornamental plant and a potential weed.

Melanolepis diadena thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal.
