Mussaenda forbesii

Mussaenda forbesii, commonly known as the golden trumpet or flame bush, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that can grow up to 3 meters tall, featuring lance-shaped leaves with a smooth texture. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its floral structure is adapted to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.

Mussaenda forbesii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
