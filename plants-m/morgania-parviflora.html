Morgania parviflora

Morgania parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered morgania, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern United States.

It is a member of the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and thrives in acidic, well-drained soils. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped white or pink flowers in the spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are evergreen, leathery, and elliptical in shape, providing year-round interest in the landscape.

Morgania parviflora is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
