Mespilus monogyna var. hirsuta

Mespilus monogyna var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy whitebeam, is a variant of the common whitebeam, characterized by its hairy foliage and distinctive bark.

It is a deciduous tree native to parts of Europe and western Asia, often found in woodlands and rocky soils. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that ripen in late summer. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with serrated edges and a slightly hairy texture.

The hairy whitebeam is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
