Mandevilla pycnantha

Mandevilla pycnantha, commonly known as the clustered mandevilla, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into orange-red berries, which are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and hanging baskets.

Its foliage is dark green and glossy, adding visual appeal to its ornamental value.
