Matthiola pseudoxyceras var. incisa

Matthiola pseudoxyceras var. incisa, commonly known as the narrowleaf stock, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other economically important plants such as cabbages and mustard. The plant is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white. Its leaves are narrow and serrated, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and for its historical use in herbal medicine.
