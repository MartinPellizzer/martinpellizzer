Monnina pilosa

Monnina pilosa, commonly known as silky monnina, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing annual herb with slender, hairy stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pink to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Monnina pilosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant landscaping.
