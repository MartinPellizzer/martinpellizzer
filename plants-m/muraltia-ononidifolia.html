Muraltia ononidifolia

Muraltia ononidifolia, commonly known as the sour fig or Indian sour fig, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically sour and edible, often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forested areas or along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and medicinal purposes.
