Melissitus teheranicus

Melissitus teheranicus, commonly known as the Persian honeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant produces bright yellow to orange flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.

Melissitus teheranicus is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological role in supporting local pollinator populations.
