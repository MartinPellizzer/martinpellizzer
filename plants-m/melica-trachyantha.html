Melica trachyantha

Melica trachyantha, commonly known as the rough brome, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes, meadows, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, erect stems with rough, hairy leaves that are often bluish-green in color. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, each bearing two to three small, bristle-tipped flowers.

Melica trachyantha is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
