Mentha crinoides

Mentha crinoides, commonly known as the woolly mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and small, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Mentha crinoides is part of the mint family (Lamiaceae), which is known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses.

Its fragrance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardens and herbal collections.
