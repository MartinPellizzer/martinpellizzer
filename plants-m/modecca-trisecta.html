Modecca trisecta

Modecca trisecta, commonly known as the three-lobed bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of blue or purple. The leaves are palmately lobed, with three distinct segments, giving the plant its scientific name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or gravelly habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
