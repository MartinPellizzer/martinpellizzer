Mentha arvensis subsp. borealis

Mentha arvensis subsp.

borealis, commonly known as northern field mint, is a perennial herb native to northern regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in open areas such as meadows and wetlands.

The plant has slender, erect stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a distinct mint scent. Its flowers are small, tubular, and clustered in whorls, typically blooming in late summer.

This subspecies is valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
