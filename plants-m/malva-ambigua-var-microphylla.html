Malva ambigua var. microphylla

Malva ambigua var. microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved mallows, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant features palmately lobed leaves that are smaller than those of the typical Malva ambigua, giving it its varietal name. Its flowers are pink to purple, with five petals and a prominent central stamen, typical of the mallow family.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and wetlands.
