Mesembryanthemum dregeanum

Mesembryanthemum dregeanum, commonly known as the ice plant, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically blooming in spring and summer. It is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and aesthetic appeal.

Mesembryanthemum dregeanum is also valued for its ability to prevent soil erosion in dry landscapes.
