Miersiella kuhlmannii

Miersiella kuhlmannii, commonly known as the Kuhlmann's lichen, is a species of crustose lichen belonging to the family Teloschistaceae.

It is characterized by its pale to dark yellowish-green thallus, which is typically covered with a thin, continuous cortex. The lichen is composed of a symbiotic association between a photosynthetic alga and a fungal partner, primarily from the genus Xanthoria. It thrives in dry, sunny environments, often found on rock surfaces or tree bark in temperate regions.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and specialized species within the lichen community.
