Momordica sahyadrica

Momordica sahyadrica, commonly known as the Western bitter gourd, is a climbing vine native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like cucumbers and pumpkins. The plant produces elongated, green fruits that turn yellow when mature, often with a warty surface. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, and the flowers are typically yellow with prominent stamens.

The plant is valued for its edible fruits, which are used in various traditional cuisines and have medicinal properties.
