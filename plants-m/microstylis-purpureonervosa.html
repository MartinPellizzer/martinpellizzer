Microstylis purpureonervosa

Microstylis purpureonervosa, commonly known as the purple-veined microstylis, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family, commonly known as the sedge family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, clustered inflorescences. The leaves are typically narrow and have distinct purple veins, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

This species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
