Melinis tenuinervis f. mutica

Melinis tenuinervis f. mutica, commonly known as the hairy slenderleaf grass, is a variant of the slenderleaf grass species characterized by its distinctive hairy foliage.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields. The plant has narrow, linear leaves with a prominent vein structure, which is a key botanical feature. Its inflorescence consists of a spikelet-like cluster of small, two-lobed flowers.

This grass is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
