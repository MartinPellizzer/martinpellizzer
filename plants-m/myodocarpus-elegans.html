Myodocarpus elegans

Myodocarpus elegans, commonly known as the elegant myodocarp, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae, which includes other aromatic plants such as eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

The tree is often valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in horticulture for its aesthetic qualities.
