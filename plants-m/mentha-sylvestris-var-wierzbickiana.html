Mentha sylvestris var. wierzbickiana

Mentha sylvestris var. wierzbickiana, commonly known as Wierzbickiana mint, is a perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly hairy leaves that are typically lance-shaped and have a strong menthol scent. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pale purple to blue in color, arranged in whorls along the stems. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
