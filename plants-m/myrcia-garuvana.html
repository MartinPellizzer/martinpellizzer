Myrcia garuvana

Myrcia garuvana, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Myrcia garuvana is also noted for its aromatic properties and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
