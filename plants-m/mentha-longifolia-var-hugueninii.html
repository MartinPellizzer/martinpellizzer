Mentha longifolia var. hugueninii

Mentha longifolia var. hugueninii, commonly known as Huguenin's mint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves and small, pale purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade. It is often used in herbal medicine for its calming and digestive properties.

Its essential oil contains menthol, which contributes to its cooling and soothing effects.
