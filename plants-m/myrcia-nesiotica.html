Myrcia nesiotica

Myrcia nesiotica, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible but often have a spicy or peppery taste. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.

However, it can become invasive in some regions, posing a challenge to local ecosystems.
