Malvastrum scabrosum

Malvastrum scabrosum, commonly known as the rough mallow, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured stems and leaves, which are typically grayish-green in color. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its resilience and adaptability contribute to its presence in various habitats across its native range.
