Myosotis × cinerascens

Myosotis × cinerascens, commonly known as the sky blue forget-me-not, is a hybrid perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It produces small, delicate blue or sky-blue flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens or shady borders. Its leaves are heart-shaped and slightly hairy, while the stems are slender and often arching.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
