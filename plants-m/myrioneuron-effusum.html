Myrioneuron effusum

Myrioneuron effusum, commonly known as the creeping marsh fern, is a perennial herb native to wetlands and marshy areas in eastern North America.

It has a rhizomatous stem system that allows it to spread vegetatively and form dense colonies. The plant features pinnately compound leaves with slender, grass-like leaflets that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are borne in clusters and are pollinated by wind.

The plant thrives in moist, nutrient-poor soils and plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
