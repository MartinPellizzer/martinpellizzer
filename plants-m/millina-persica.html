Millina persica

Millina persica, commonly known as Persian apple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It produces small, apple-like fruits that are typically yellow or red in color, with a sweet and tangy flavor. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are opposite in arrangement and are covered in a fine, silvery down. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruits.

The flowers are white or pale pink and appear in clusters, adding to its aesthetic appeal.
