Marsdenia ridleyi

Marsdenia ridleyi, commonly known as the Ridley's Marsdenia, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, often with a distinctive corona. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a glossy surface, while its stems are woody and covered in fine hairs.

Marsdenia ridleyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
