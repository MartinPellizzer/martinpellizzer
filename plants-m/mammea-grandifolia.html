Mammea grandifolia

Mammea grandifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf mammea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves. The tree produces yellowish-orange flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody, spherical fruits containing numerous seeds. Its bark is thick and fissured, often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Mammea grandifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitats.
