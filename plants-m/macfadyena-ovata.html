Macfadyena ovata

Macfadyena ovata, commonly known as the Crown of Thorns, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, woody stems and pairs of opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces striking, showy flowers that are typically red or orange with a yellow center. It is adapted to arid environments and is drought-tolerant, making it a popular ornamental plant.

However, it can be invasive in some regions if not properly managed.
