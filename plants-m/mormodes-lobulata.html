Mormodes lobulata

Mormodes lobulata, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant features thick, waxy leaves that are typically velvety in texture. It produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters at the ends of its stems.

Mormodes lobulata is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
