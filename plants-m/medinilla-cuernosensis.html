Medinilla cuernosensis

Medinilla cuernosensis, commonly known as the horned medinilla, is a tropical flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences. The plant features elliptical, glossy leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Medinilla cuernosensis is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
