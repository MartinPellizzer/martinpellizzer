Moricandia ramburii var. microsperma

Moricandia ramburii var. microsperma, commonly known as the Kashmir spinach, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan region.

It is a herbaceous annual with slender, erect stems and small, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by small, spherical fruits. It is valued for its edible leaves, which have a slightly bitter taste similar to spinach.

The variety microsperma is distinguished by its smaller seeds compared to other varieties of Moricandia ramburii.
