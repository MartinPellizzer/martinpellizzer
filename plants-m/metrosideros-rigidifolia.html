Metrosideros rigidifolia

Metrosideros rigidifolia, commonly known as the rough-leaved pohutukawa, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its rough, textured leaves and clusters of bright red flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant thrives in coastal areas and is often found growing on rocky substrates. Its leaves are typically dark green and have a leathery texture, while its flowers are a striking contrast to the foliage.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in coastal ecosystems.
