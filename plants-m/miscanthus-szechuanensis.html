Miscanthus szechuanensis

Miscanthus szechuanensis, commonly known as the Sichuan silvergrass, is a perennial grass native to China.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and feathery plumes that appear in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in panicles. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and potential as a bioenergy crop, it has gained attention in horticulture and ecological restoration.
