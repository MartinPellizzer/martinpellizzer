Marrubium coerulescens

Marrubium coerulescens, commonly known as blue hawksbeard, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features erect, branched stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces bright blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture.

Marrubium coerulescens is typically found in rocky or dry soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical medicinal uses.
