Mucuna interrupta

Mucuna interrupta, commonly known as the velvet bean or cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several elliptical seeds. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial in agricultural systems.

Mucuna interrupta is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a forage crop.
