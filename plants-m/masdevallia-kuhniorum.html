Masdevallia kuhniorum

Masdevallia kuhniorum, commonly known as the Kuhn's masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, often brightly colored flowers that bloom in a spiral pattern along the stem. The plant typically features lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a rosette, and its roots are fibrous and adapted to its high-altitude environment. Masdevallia kuhniorum thrives in cool, humid conditions with high light exposure, making it a popular choice for specialized orchid cultivation.

Its unique floral structure and ornamental value have made it a sought-after specimen among orchid enthusiasts.
