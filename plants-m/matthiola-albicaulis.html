Matthiola albicaulis

Matthiola albicaulis, commonly known as the white-flowered stock, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbages and mustard. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, often with a peppery aroma.

Matthiola albicaulis is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
