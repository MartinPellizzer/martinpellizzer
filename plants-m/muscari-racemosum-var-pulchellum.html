Muscari racemosum var. pulchellum

Muscari racemosum var. pulchellum, commonly known as the Persian grape hyacinth, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It features dense clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring, resembling small grapes, hence its common name. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces underground bulbs that store nutrients for the next growing season. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its early blooming habit and delicate, clustered flowers.
