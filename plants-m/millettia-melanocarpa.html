Millettia melanocarpa

Millettia melanocarpa, commonly known as black-stemmed legume, is a woody climbing plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive black stems. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines.

It also has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, making it valuable for soil enrichment in agricultural settings.
