Merostachys claussenii

Merostachys claussenii, commonly known as the golden beardgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and produces dense, golden-yellow inflorescences that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color and is often found in rocky soils and open areas. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its botanical features make it suitable for xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
