Masdevallia orchipayanensium

Masdevallia orchipayanensium, commonly known as the Yungas orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Bolivia and Peru.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, often colorful floral structures. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree trunks or branches in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually small, with intricate patterns and a variety of hues, including white, pink, and purple.

Due to its habitat specificity and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
