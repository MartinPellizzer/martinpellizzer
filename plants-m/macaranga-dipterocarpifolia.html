Macaranga dipterocarpifolia

Macaranga dipterocarpifolia, commonly known as the tropical maple tree, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 30 meters tall, with smooth, often greyish bark. The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate to elliptic in shape, with a serrated margin. It produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits that release tiny seeds.

This plant is often found in lowland rainforests and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various species.
