Monotes hypoleucus var. discolor

Monotes hypoleucus var. discolor, commonly known as the white-veined orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, white-veined leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest undergrowth where it can receive indirect sunlight. Its unique coloration and morphology make it a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists.
