Melaleuca superba

Melaleuca superba, commonly known as the white bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical, white to pale pink flower spikes. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a glossy green surface.

Melaleuca superba is well adapted to coastal and sandy soils, making it a resilient and ornamental species in horticulture.
