Mentha aquatica subsp. ortmanniana

Mentha aquatica subsp.

ortmanniana, commonly known as water mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist or aquatic environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows.

The plant has slender, square stems and opposite, serrated leaves that are often aromatic when crushed. Its small, pale blue or purple flowers are clustered in whorls along the stem.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
