Melaleuca conothamnoides

Melaleuca conothamnoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved paperbark, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, flaky bark that peels in strips, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pink flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often covered in a fine, whitish bloom.

Melaleuca conothamnoides is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in coastal and forested areas.
