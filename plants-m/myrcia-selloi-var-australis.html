Myrcia selloi var. australis

Myrcia selloi var. australis, commonly known as the Australian myrtle, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or rainforest habitats.
