Malva spithamea

Malva spithamea, commonly known as the Persian mallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes and produces showy, pink or purple flowers with five petals. Its flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
