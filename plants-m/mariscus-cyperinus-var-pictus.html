Mariscus cyperinus var. pictus

Mariscus cyperinus var. pictus, commonly known as the painted sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant is characterized by its slender, triangular stems and small, greenish-white flowers that appear in dense, spike-like clusters. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slight bluish tint.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.
