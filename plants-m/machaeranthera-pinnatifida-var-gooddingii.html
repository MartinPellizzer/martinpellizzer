Machaeranthera pinnatifida var. gooddingii

Machaeranthera pinnatifida var. gooddingii, commonly known as the Goodding's tickseed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It features narrow, pinnately compound leaves and produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

This variety is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
