Mephitidia longifolia

Mephitidia longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf mephit, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom during the rainy season. Its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in dense forest understories.
