Maxillaria murilliana

Maxillaria murilliana, commonly known as the Murilliana orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its compact growth habit and the presence of pseudobulbs. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with intricate patterns. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern around the pseudobulbs.

Maxillaria murilliana thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
