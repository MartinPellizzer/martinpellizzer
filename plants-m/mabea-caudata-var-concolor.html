Mabea caudata var. concolor

Mabea caudata var. concolor, commonly known as the smoothleaf mabea, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features upright, branching stems that are typically covered in soft, hairy foliage. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale green, followed by capsule fruits containing several seeds. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a smooth or slightly hairy surface, contributing to its common name.

This species is often found in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and wet meadows.
