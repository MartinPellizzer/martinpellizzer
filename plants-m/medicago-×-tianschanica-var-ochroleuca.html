Medicago × tianschanica var. ochroleuca

Medicago × tianschanica var. ochroleuca, commonly known as the pale-leaved alfalfa, is a perennial legume characterized by its distinctive pale yellow foliage.

It is native to Central Asia and thrives in temperate climates, often found in grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in the summer months. Its trifoliate leaves and stipitate stipules are typical of the Fabaceae family.

This variety is valued for its forage quality and is sometimes cultivated for agricultural purposes.
