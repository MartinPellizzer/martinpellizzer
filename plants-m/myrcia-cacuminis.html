Myrcia cacuminis

Myrcia cacuminis, commonly known as the red myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its small, glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a small, red, fleshy drupe that contains several seeds.

Myrcia cacuminis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
