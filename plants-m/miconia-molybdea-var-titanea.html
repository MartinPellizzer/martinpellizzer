Miconia molybdea var. titanea

Miconia molybdea var. titanea, commonly known as the Titanea miconia, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often a deep green with a waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that are typically visited by various pollinators. Its fruit is a round, fleshy berry that contains several seeds, contributing to its dispersal in the wild.

The plant is notable for its ornamental value and has been studied for its potential ecological roles in its native habitats.
