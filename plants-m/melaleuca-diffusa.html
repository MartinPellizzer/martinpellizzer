Melaleuca diffusa

Melaleuca diffusa, commonly known as the slender bottlebrush, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, cylindrical flower spikes that are typically red or pink in color. The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves and produces aromatic, nectar-rich flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Melaleuca diffusa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
