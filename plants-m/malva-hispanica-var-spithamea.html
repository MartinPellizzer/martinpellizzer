Malva hispanica var. spithamea

Malva hispanica var. spithamea, commonly known as the Spanish mallow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a soft, velvety texture and produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for gardens and natural habitats. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
