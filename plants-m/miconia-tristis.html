Miconia tristis

Miconia tristis, commonly known as the weeping miconia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in dense, velvety hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Miconia tristis is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, making it an invasive species in some regions.

Its botanical features make it a notable plant in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
