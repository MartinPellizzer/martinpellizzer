Monachosorella nipponica

Monachosorella nipponica, commonly known as the Japanese flat moss, is a species of liverwort belonging to the family Porellaceae.

It is characterized by its closely appressed, flat thallus that forms dense, carpet-like growths on moist, shaded surfaces. The plant exhibits a simple morphology with a single layer of cells and lacks true roots, stems, and leaves. It is typically found in humid, temperate regions, often in forest floors or on rocks.

Monachosorella nipponica plays a role in soil stabilization and provides microhabitats for various invertebrates.
