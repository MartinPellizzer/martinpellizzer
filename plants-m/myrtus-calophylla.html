Myrtus calophylla

Myrtus calophylla, commonly known as the Australian tea tree, is a small evergreen shrub native to Australia.

It features lance-shaped, aromatic leaves that are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils. The plant produces small, white, five-petaled flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators like bees and birds. Its fruit is a small, black drupe that contains a single seed.

Myrtus calophylla is well adapted to coastal and sandy soils, making it a hardy plant in various environmental conditions.
