Miconia setinodis

Miconia setinodis, commonly known as the velvetleaf miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers, which are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Miconia setinodis is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it an invasive species in some regions.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and a challenge in certain ecosystems.
