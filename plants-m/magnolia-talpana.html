Magnolia talpana

Magnolia talpana, commonly known as the Mexican magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Magnoliaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. Its bark is smooth and grayish, and it can grow up to 15 meters tall in suitable conditions.

Magnolia talpana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beautiful blossoms.
