Mentha × brassaiana

Mentha × brassaiana, commonly known as hybrid mint, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Mentha spicata (spearmint) and Mentha × piperita (peppermint).

It is a perennial herb characterized by its square, erect stems and opposite, serrated leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue to purple in color, arranged in whorls along the stem. Mentha × brassaiana is widely cultivated for its essential oils, which are used in aromatherapy, food flavoring, and medicinal applications.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is commonly found in temperate regions around the world.
