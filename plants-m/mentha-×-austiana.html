Mentha × austiana

Mentha × austiana, commonly known as Australian spearmint, is a hybrid mint species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, serrated leaves and flowering spikes that produce small, pale blue to violet flowers. The plant is perennial, growing upright with a woody base and spreading horizontally through rhizomes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along waterways.

Mentha × austiana is valued for its essential oils, which are used in aromatherapy and natural remedies.
