Mimosa waterlotii

Mimosa waterlotii, commonly known as the water mimosa, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water sources such as rivers or ponds. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with bacteria.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it both a useful ornamental plant and a potential invasive species in some environments.
