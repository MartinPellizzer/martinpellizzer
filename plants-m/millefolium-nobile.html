Millefolium nobile

Millefolium nobile, commonly known as purple mountain geranium, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, or purple during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic foliage and long bloom period contribute to its ornamental value in gardens.
