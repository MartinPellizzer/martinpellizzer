Mesembryanthemum crassifolium

Mesembryanthemum crassifolium, commonly known as the thick-leaved ice plant, is a succulent species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically during the warmer months. It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its drought tolerance and vibrant blooms.

Mesembryanthemum crassifolium is also valued for its ability to prevent soil erosion in sandy or degraded landscapes.
