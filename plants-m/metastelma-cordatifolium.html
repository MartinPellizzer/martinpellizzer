Metastelma cordatifolium

Metastelma cordatifolium, commonly known as the heart-leaved metastelma, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Melastomataceae and is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
