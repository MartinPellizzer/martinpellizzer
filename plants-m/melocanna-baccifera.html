Melocanna baccifera

Melocanna baccifera, commonly known as the bamboo palm or Indian bamboo, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae).

It is a tall, clump-forming plant native to Southeast Asia, particularly India and Sri Lanka. The plant features long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber. Its rhizomes are edible and have a sweet, starchy flavor.

Melocanna baccifera is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
