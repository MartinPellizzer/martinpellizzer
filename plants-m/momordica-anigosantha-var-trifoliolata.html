Momordica anigosantha var. trifoliolata

Momordica anigosantha var. trifoliolata, commonly known as the three-leaf bitter gourd, is a variant of the bitter gourd plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets, and its elongated, spiny fruits. The plant is a climbing vine with hairy stems and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are typically used in traditional cuisines and have medicinal properties.

The botanical features of this variant make it distinct from other Momordica species.
