Mikania widgrenii

Mikania widgrenii, commonly known as the narrowleaf morning glory, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

However, it is considered an invasive species in some regions due to its aggressive spreading habit.
