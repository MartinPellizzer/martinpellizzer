Mentha sylvestris convar. cyrtophylla

Mentha sylvestris convar. cyrtophylla, commonly known as wild mint or field mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or blue in color, arranged in whorls along the stem. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, which contribute to its distinct menthol-like scent.

This variety is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses.
