Marlea virgata

Marlea virgata, commonly known as the silver leaf daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silvery-white leaves and yellow daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

Marlea virgata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
