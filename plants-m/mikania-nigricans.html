Mikania nigricans

Mikania nigricans, commonly known as the blackstem vine, is a fast-growing, woody vine native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by distinctive, spiny, three-lobed fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is considered a problematic species in many areas.
