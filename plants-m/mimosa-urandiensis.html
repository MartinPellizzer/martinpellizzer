Mimosa urandiensis

Mimosa urandiensis, commonly known as the yellow mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves that fold shut when touched, a response triggered by light or mechanical stimulation. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens in various regions.
