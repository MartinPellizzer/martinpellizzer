Mikania coriacea

Mikania coriacea, commonly known as roughleaf morning glory, is a climbing vine native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its rough, waxy leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and forests.

Mikania coriacea is a member of the Asteraceae family and is known for its rapid growth and invasive potential in some regions.
