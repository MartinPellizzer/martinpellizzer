Muricaria

Muricaria, commonly known as the rock fig or rock apple, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Moraceae.

These plants are native to various regions of the Americas, particularly in arid and semi-arid environments. Muricaria species are characterized by their shrub-like growth habit and the presence of milky sap. The leaves are typically alternate, simple, and often have a rough or hairy texture.

The fruit is a small, edible fig-like structure that is sometimes used in traditional medicine and local cuisines.
