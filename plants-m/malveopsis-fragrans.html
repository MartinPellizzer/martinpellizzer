Malveopsis fragrans

Malveopsis fragrans, commonly known as the sweet senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces bright yellow flowers with a sweet fragrance. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and pleasant scent.

The plant also has traditional medicinal uses in some cultures.
