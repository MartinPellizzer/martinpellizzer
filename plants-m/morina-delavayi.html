Morina delavayi

Morina delavayi, commonly known as Delavay's moringa, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of southwestern China.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers and elliptical leaves. The plant typically grows at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters, thriving in temperate and subtropical climates. Its bark and leaves contain various bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional medicine.

The plant's morphology and chemical composition reflect its adaptation to high-altitude environments.
