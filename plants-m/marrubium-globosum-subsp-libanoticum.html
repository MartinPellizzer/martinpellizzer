Marrubium globosum subsp. libanoticum

Marrubium globosum subsp.

libanoticum, commonly known as the Lebanon horehound, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and gray-green, hairy leaves that are often glandular.

The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous areas. Marrubium globosum subsp.

libanoticum is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
