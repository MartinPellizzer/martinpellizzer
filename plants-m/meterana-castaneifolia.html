Meterana castaneifolia

Meterana castaneifolia, commonly known as the Chinese chestnut tree, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple, ovate to elliptic leaves that are dark green on top and pale underneath. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by edible, spiny fruits called chestnuts. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in traditional medicine.

The species is also cultivated for its nuts, which are rich in nutrients and used in various culinary applications.
