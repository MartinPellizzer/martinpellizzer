Malva verticillata var. verticillata

Malva verticillata var. verticillata, commonly known as the common mallow, is a flowering plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, palmately lobed leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in fields, along roadsides, and in wetlands. It has a trailing or spreading growth habit and produces capsule fruits containing numerous small seeds.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its soothing properties.
