Miconia lasiostyla

Miconia lasiostyla, commonly known as the velvetleaf miconia, is a member of the Melastomataceae family.

It is a shrub or small tree native to the tropical regions of Central and South America. The plant is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine, velvety hairs. Its flowers are showy, with five petals that are typically pink or purple in color.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.
