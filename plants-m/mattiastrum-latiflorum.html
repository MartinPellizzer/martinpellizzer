Mattiastrum latiflorum

Mattiastrum latiflorum, commonly known as the broadflower matias, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Mattiastrum latiflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
