Mesembryanthemum tegens

Mesembryanthemum tegens, commonly known as the "Cape Hottentot" or "Ice Plant," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, often blue or purple, leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically during the summer months. Its leaves are covered in a waxy cuticle that reduces water loss and reflects sunlight.

Mesembryanthemum tegens is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
