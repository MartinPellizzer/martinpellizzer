Maba ferrea

Maba ferrea, commonly known as ironwood or iron tree, is a tropical tree native to parts of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly resistant to decay and insect damage. The plant has a deep root system and can thrive in poor soil conditions, making it adaptable to various environments. Its leaves are typically small and opposite, with a glossy surface.

The tree produces small, fragrant flowers that are often white or pale yellow, followed by fruit that contains a single seed.
