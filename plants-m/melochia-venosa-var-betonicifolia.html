Melochia venosa var. betonicifolia

Melochia venosa var. betonicifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf mallow, is a tropical shrub characterized by its striking, velvety foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is native to regions of Africa and parts of Asia. The plant features palmate leaves with a soft, fuzzy texture, and its flowers typically range in color from pink to purple. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

The plant is also noted for its ability to spread rapidly, making it both a ornamental and a potential weed.
