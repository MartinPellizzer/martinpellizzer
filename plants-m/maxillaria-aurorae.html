Maxillaria aurorae

Maxillaria aurorae, commonly known as the Aurora Maxillaria, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a rosette pattern, providing structural support.

Maxillaria aurorae is appreciated by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
