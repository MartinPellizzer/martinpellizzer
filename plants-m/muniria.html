Muniria

Muniria, commonly known as the Indian spinach or Muniria spinosa, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including India, Sri Lanka, and parts of Africa. Muniria is often cultivated for its edible leaves, which are rich in nutrients and used in traditional cuisines.

It also plays a role in soil stabilization due to its deep root system.
