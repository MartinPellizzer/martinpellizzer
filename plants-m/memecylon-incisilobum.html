Memecylon incisilobum

Memecylon incisilobum, commonly known as the sharp-toothed memecylon, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters, featuring opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by three-lobed, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Its foliage is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
