Maesa membranifolia

Maesa membranifolia, commonly known as the paperleaf maesa, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating, giving them a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, fleshy fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in warm climates.
