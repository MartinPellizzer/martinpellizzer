Muhlenbergia abata

Muhlenbergia abata, commonly known as the plains muhlenbergia, is a perennial grass native to the central and southern United States.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies, roadsides, and disturbed soils. The plant features slender, upright stems with fine, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a whitish bloom. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of small, spike-like flower clusters that are usually pink to purplish in color.

Muhlenbergia abata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in dry, well-drained conditions.
