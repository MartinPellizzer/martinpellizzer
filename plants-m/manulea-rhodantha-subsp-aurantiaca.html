Manulea rhodantha subsp. aurantiaca

Manulea rhodantha subsp.

aurantiaca, commonly known as the golden-flowered manulea, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Africa. It is characterized by its showy, orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, making it visually striking.

The plant typically grows in shrubland or rocky habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are simple and opposite, often covered in fine hairs.

The subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
