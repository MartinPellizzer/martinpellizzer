Mentha × maximilianea subsp. baileyi

Mentha × maximilianea subsp.

baileyi, commonly known as Bailey's mint, is a hybrid perennial herb native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly fuzzy leaves and square stems typical of the mint family.

The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a versatile addition to gardens.

Its strong menthol scent and culinary uses make it a valuable plant for both ornamental and practical purposes.
