Myrcia luschnathiana

Myrcia luschnathiana, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white or pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often have a strong, spicy flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Myrcia luschnathiana is also known for its potential medicinal uses in traditional medicine.
