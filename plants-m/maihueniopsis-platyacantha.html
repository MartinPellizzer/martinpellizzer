Maihueniopsis platyacantha

Maihueniopsis platyacantha, commonly known as the flat-stemmed burro's tail, is a succulent plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the family Aizoaceae, which is characterized by its fleshy leaves and flowers adapted to arid environments. The plant features prostrate stems that produce small, rounded leaves, and it produces yellow or orange flowers during the growing season. It is well-suited for rocky or sandy soils and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it an interesting addition to xeriscaping and succulent collections.
