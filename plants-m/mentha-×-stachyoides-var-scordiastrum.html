Mentha × stachyoides var. scordiastrum

Mentha × stachyoides var. scordiastrum, commonly known as garlic chives, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It is a hybrid species within the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, grass-like foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or violet in color. Its leaves are often used in culinary applications for their mild garlic-like flavor.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.
