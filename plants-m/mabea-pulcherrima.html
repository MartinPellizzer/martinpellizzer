Mabea pulcherrima

Mabea pulcherrima, commonly known as the red hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking red, pentagonal flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a contrasting yellow throat. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and have a rough texture.

Mabea pulcherrima is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
