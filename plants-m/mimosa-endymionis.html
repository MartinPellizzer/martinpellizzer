Mimosa endymionis

Mimosa endymionis, commonly known as the moonflower mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant is known for its nocturnal blooming habit, opening its flowers at night and closing them during the day. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its unique flowering pattern and graceful appearance make it a fascinating subject for botanists and gardeners alike.
