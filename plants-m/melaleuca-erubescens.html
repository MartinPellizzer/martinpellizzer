Melaleuca erubescens

Melaleuca erubescens, commonly known as the crimson myrtle, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces woody capsules that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and heathlands.

Melaleuca erubescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract native pollinators.
