Markhamia cauda-felina

Markhamia cauda-felina, commonly known as the cat-tail plant, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, spiky inflorescence that resembles a cat's tail. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils and saline conditions. Its yellow to orange flowers are arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters at the ends of the stems.

Markhamia cauda-felina is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in coastal gardens.
