Malpighia moureila

Malpighia moureila, commonly known as the Brazilian acerola, is a tropical shrub belonging to the Malpighiaceae family.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its small, bright red berries that are rich in vitamin C. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate or elliptical in shape. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural antioxidants.
