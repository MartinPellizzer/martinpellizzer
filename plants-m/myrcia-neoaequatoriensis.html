Myrcia neoaequatoriensis

Myrcia neoaequatoriensis, commonly known as the Ecuadorian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the humid forests of Ecuador and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy pink or red flowers. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and emit a fragrant scent when crushed.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has potential uses in traditional medicine.
