Meyna pubescens

Meyna pubescens, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and elliptical leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded areas of forests.
