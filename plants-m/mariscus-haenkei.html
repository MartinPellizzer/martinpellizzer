Mariscus haenkei

Mariscus haenkei, commonly known as the Mexican bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and can reach heights of up to 3 feet.

Mariscus haenkei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
