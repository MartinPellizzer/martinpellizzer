Manihot fabianae

Manihot fabianae, commonly known as the Fabian's arrowleaf, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique foliage.

The plant is also adapted to grow in a variety of soil types and thrives in warm, humid climates.
