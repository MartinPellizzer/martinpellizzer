Myristica incredibilis

Myristica incredibilis, commonly known as the miraculous nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen belonging to the Myristicaceae family.

It is native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces large, leathery leaves and fragrant, yellowish flowers, followed by fruits that contain the aromatic seeds used for nutmeg and mace. Its botanical significance lies in its unique chemical composition, which includes volatile oils and alkaloids.

Due to its rarity and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable species in both ecological and commercial contexts.
