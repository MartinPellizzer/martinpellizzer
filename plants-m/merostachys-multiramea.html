Merostachys multiramea

Merostachys multiramea, commonly known as the multibract grass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical features make it an important species for wetland ecosystems and ecological studies.
