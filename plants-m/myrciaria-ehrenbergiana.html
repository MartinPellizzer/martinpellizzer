Myrciaria ehrenbergiana

Myrciaria ehrenbergiana, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or araçá-do-brejo, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces bright red, fleshy berries that are edible and often consumed by both humans and animals. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is adapted to grow in wet, shaded environments.

Myrciaria ehrenbergiana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
