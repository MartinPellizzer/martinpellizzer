Muldera recurva

Muldera recurva, commonly known as the zigzag plant, is a succulent species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, twisting stems that form a cascading or shrub-like appearance. The plant features small, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a slight bluish tint. It produces clusters of yellow flowers during the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Muldera recurva is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
