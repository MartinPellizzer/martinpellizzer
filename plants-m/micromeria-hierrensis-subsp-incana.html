Micromeria hierrensis subsp. incana

Micromeria hierrensis subsp.

incana, commonly known as the woolly micromeria, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its glandular-hairy foliage, which gives it a distinctive silvery appearance.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
