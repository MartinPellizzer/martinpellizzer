Mentha × rotundifolia var. pedemontana

Mentha × rotundifolia var. pedemontana, commonly known as the Alpine peppermint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with hairy, oval-shaped leaves that are slightly rounded at the base. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers arranged in whorls along the stems. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky soils.

Its aromatic leaves contain menthol, making it a valued plant in both horticulture and traditional medicine.
