Mentha longifolia subsp. caucasica

Mentha longifolia subsp.

caucasica, commonly known as Caucasian mint, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region. It features slender, upright stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a serrated margin.

The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, contributing to its use in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.
