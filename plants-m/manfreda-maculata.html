Manfreda maculata

Manfreda maculata, commonly known as the spotted manfreda, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
