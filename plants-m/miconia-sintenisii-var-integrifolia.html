Miconia sintenisii var. integrifolia

Miconia sintenisii var. integrifolia, commonly known as the smooth-leaf miconia, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that are typically smooth and entire, giving it its varietal name "integrifolia.". The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that are often surrounded by showy bracts. It is a member of the Melastomataceae family and is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets.

Due to its invasive potential, it is considered a problematic species in certain ecosystems.
