Mascarenhasia arborescens var. comorensis

Mascarenhasia arborescens var. comorensis, commonly known as the Comoro starling tree, is a tropical plant native to the Comoros Islands.

It is a small to medium-sized tree characterized by its dense, rounded crown and smooth, gray bark. The leaves are simple, alternate, and elliptic in shape, with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside. Its flowers are small, clustered in panicles, and typically have five petals, displaying a range of colors from white to pink.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens for its visual beauty.
