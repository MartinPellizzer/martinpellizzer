Mesechites minimus

Mesechites minimus, commonly known as the small mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees, primarily in the southeastern United States.

It has small, scale-like leaves and produces small, white or pale green flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant relies on its host for water and some nutrients, while it provides nectar for pollinators like bees and wasps. Mesechites minimus is also known for its sticky, gelatinous berries that are an important food source for birds.
