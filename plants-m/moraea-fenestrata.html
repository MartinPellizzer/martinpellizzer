Moraea fenestrata

Moraea fenestrata, commonly known as the windowed moraea, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental, sword-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-like flowers. The plant produces clusters of vibrant, often purple or pink flowers that bloom in late summer. Moraea fenestrata thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its unique foliage and colorful blooms make it a valuable addition to both gardens and botanical collections.
