Mimusops cearensis

Mimusops cearensis, commonly known as the Cearense satinwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the family Sapotaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by spherical fruits that contain smooth, white seeds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
