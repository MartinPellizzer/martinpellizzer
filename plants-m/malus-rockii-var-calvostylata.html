Malus rockii var. calvostylata

Malus rockii var. calvostylata, commonly known as the hairless apple tree, is a variant of the rock apple species native to China.

It is characterized by its smooth, hairless bark and small, edible fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, while its flowers are white and appear in clusters.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional Chinese medicine.
