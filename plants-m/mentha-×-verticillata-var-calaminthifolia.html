Mentha × verticillata var. calaminthifolia

Mentha × verticillata var. calaminthifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaf water mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves and flowering spikes.

It typically grows in moist environments such as stream banks, wet meadows, and marshy areas, thriving in temperate climates. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are small, clustered in whorls, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is part of the mint family (Lamiaceae), known for its aromatic compounds and medicinal uses.

This variety is notable for its ability to tolerate waterlogged soils and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
