Moraea alticola

Moraea alticola, commonly known as the coastal moraea, is a South African plant species belonging to the Iridaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange. The plant is typically found in coastal areas, where it thrives in sandy or rocky soils. Moraea alticola is adapted to coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds.

Its blooming period occurs during the summer months, making it a notable ornamental plant in its native habitat.
