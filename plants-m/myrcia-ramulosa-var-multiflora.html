Myrcia ramulosa var. multiflora

Myrcia ramulosa var. multiflora, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering shrub or small tree native to Brazil.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are opposite and elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is considered a problematic species in some regions.
