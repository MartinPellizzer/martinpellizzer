Monechma troglodytica

Monechma troglodytica, commonly known as the cave bladderwort, is a rare aquatic plant found in subterranean water systems.

It is characterized by its small, bladder-like structures that trap air and aid in buoyancy. The plant typically grows in dark, nutrient-rich environments such as caves and underground springs. It lacks true leaves and instead has whorled, ribbon-like structures for photosynthesis.

Monechma troglodytica is a halophyte, meaning it can tolerate high salinity levels in its aquatic habitat.
