Mesophlebion falcatilobum var. apiculatum

Mesophlebion falcatilobum var. apiculatum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mistplant, is a unique variant of the mesophlebion genus.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. This plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing a subtle fragrance that attracts pollinators like moths and beetles.

The varietal name 'apiculatum' refers to the presence of a small, pointed tip on its leaf blades, distinguishing it from other variants within the species.
