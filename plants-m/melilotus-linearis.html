Melilotus linearis

Melilotus linearis, commonly known as slender melilot, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellow flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides.

Due to its ability to fix nitrogen, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
