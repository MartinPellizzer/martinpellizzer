Mogiphanes

Mogiphanes, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a striking plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, white, night-blooming flowers that emit a sweet fragrance, attracting nocturnal pollinators like moths. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in arid conditions. Its leaves are broad and often covered in a fine, silvery hair, which helps reduce water loss.

Mogiphanes is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique nighttime display.
