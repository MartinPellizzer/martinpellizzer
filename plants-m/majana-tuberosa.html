Majana tuberosa

Majana tuberosa, commonly known as the purple root or mountain potato, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its tuberous roots and showy violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky soils, and is adapted to cool, moist climates. Its tubers are edible and have been used traditionally in local cuisines, while the plant also serves as a source of medicinal compounds.

The species is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its ecological role in its native habitats.
