Mentha longifolia var. kozorensis

Mentha longifolia var. kozorensis, commonly known as Kozorensky mint, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan region.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and aromatic. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
