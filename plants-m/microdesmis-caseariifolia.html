Microdesmis caseariifolia

Microdesmis caseariifolia, commonly known as the flame tree or false flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of yellow to orange, which bloom during the warmer months. Its fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule that contains several seeds.

Microdesmis caseariifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adaptable to a variety of soil types.
