Miconia pisinniflora

Miconia pisinniflora, commonly known as the red miconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically bright green with a reddish underside. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. Miconia pisinniflora is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, making it an invasive species in some regions.

It plays a significant role in local ecosystems but can also disrupt native plant communities.
