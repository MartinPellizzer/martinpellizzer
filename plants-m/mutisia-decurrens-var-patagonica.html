Mutisia decurrens var. patagonica

Mutisia decurrens var. patagonica, commonly known as the Patagonian mutisia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats, such as grasslands and shrublands, and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and often have a strong aromatic scent, while the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

This variant is noted for its ecological importance in supporting local pollinators and its potential use in traditional medicine.
