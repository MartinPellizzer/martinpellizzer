Myrcia multipunctata

Myrcia multipunctata, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family, characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy red flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is also cultivated in some regions as a garden plant.
