Miconia madisonii

Miconia madisonii, commonly known as the Madison miconia, is a member of the Melastomataceae family and is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, featuring broad, glossy leaves with a distinctive reddish or purple underside. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, with five petals and numerous stamens. Its fruits are small, capsule-like structures that release tiny seeds when mature.

Miconia madisonii is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments.
