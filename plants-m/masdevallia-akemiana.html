Masdevallia akemiana

Masdevallia akemiana, commonly known as the Akemian orchid, is a species of orchid native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments at elevations between 2,000 and 3,500 meters. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces intricate, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. It is known for its unique morphology and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in the wild.
