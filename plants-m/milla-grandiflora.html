Milla grandiflora

Milla grandiflora, commonly known as the grandiflora milla, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a smooth texture and a woody stem that can grow up to several feet in height. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
