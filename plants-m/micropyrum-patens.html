Micropyrum patens

Micropyrum patens, commonly known as the broadleaf snowbell, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring, typically in shades of white or pale yellow. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, soft hair. Its roots are fibrous and can form dense colonies, making it a hardy plant adaptable to various soil types.

Micropyrum patens is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and is often cultivated in shaded garden environments.
