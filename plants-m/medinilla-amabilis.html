Medinilla amabilis

Medinilla amabilis, commonly known as the velvet tangerine, is a tropical flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, waxy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, round berries that are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor. Medinilla amabilis thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance.

It is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
