Myosotis × reyana

Myosotis × reyana, commonly known as the garden forget-me-not, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Myosotis sylvatica and Myosotis rhyzomata.

It is characterized by its delicate blue or purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 15–30 cm and prefers moist, well-drained soil in partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for.
