Mariscus concinnus var. khasianus

Mariscus concinnus var. khasianus, commonly known as the Khasi marigold, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern regions of India.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous areas. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
