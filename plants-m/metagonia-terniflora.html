Metagonia terniflora

Metagonia terniflora, commonly known as the three-flowered metagonia, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant has a low, spreading growth habit with succulent leaves that help it conserve water. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand periods of drought.

Metagonia terniflora is often found in rocky or sandy habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing the soil and supporting local biodiversity.
