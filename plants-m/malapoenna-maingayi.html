Malapoenna maingayi

Malapoenna maingayi, commonly known as the Malapoenna tree, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters in height, featuring a straight trunk and a broad, umbrella-like canopy. The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a velvety texture with a dark green upper surface. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, round fruits.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in local medicine.
