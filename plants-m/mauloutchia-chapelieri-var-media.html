Mauloutchia chapelieri var. media

Mauloutchia chapelieri var. media, commonly known as the dwarf mauloutchia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It features dense, glossy dark green foliage that remains attractive throughout the year. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white and pink, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or container planting.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is a popular choice for gardeners seeking low-maintenance, drought-tolerant plants.
