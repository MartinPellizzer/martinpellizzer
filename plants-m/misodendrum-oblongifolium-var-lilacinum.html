Misodendrum oblongifolium var. lilacinum

Misodendrum oblongifolium var. lilacinum, commonly known as the lilac-flowered mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that thrives on specific host trees.

It possesses small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in whorls along its stems. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale lilac flowers that bloom in late spring. Its fruits are small, berry-like structures that contain seeds dispersed by birds.

This species is native to parts of Southeast Asia and is often found in tropical and subtropical forest ecosystems.
