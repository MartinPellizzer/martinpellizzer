Medinilla botryocarpa

Medinilla botryocarpa, commonly known as the clustered medinilla, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form and its striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The flowers are typically pink or white, with a velvety texture and a strong, sweet scent. The plant produces small, round, green fruits that mature to a red or purple color.

It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its beauty and fragrance.
