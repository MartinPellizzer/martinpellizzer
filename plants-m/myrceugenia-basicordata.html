Myrceugenia basicordata

Myrceugenia basicordata, commonly known as the Chilean myrtle, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to southern Chile.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. It produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, fleshy berries.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine.
