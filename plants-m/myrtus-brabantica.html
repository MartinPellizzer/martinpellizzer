Myrtus brabantica

Myrtus brabantica, commonly known as Brabant myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its narrow, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

Myrtus brabantica is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
