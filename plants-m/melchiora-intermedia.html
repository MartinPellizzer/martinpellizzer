Melchiora intermedia

Melchiora intermedia, commonly known as the silverleaf melchior, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves that are covered in fine, dense hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, round, brown fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Melchiora intermedia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
