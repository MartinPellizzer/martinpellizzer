Mallotus moluccanus var. genuinus

Mallotus moluccanus var. genuinus, commonly known as the Moluccan hibiscus, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking floral display and adaptability to various soil types.
