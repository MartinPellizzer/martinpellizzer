Megalastrum villosum

Megalastrum villosum, commonly known as the hairy leaved forest tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, ovate leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within tropical rainforests. Its flowers are small and clustered, often white or pale yellow, and it produces small, round fruits.

Megalastrum villosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
