Mazus taihokuensis

Mazus taihokuensis, commonly known as the Taiwan mazus, is a low-growing perennial herb native to eastern Asia, particularly Taiwan.

It belongs to the family Primulaceae and is characterized by its creeping stems and small, hairy leaves. The plant produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers with five petals, which bloom in early spring. Mazus taihokuensis is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky slopes.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice for garden cultivation in temperate regions.
