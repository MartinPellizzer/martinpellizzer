Mespilus sorbifolia

Mespilus sorbifolia, commonly known as the common whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, white flowers in clusters during early spring, followed by small, edible berries that are typically dark blue to black in color.

It is often found in woodland areas and is known for its tolerance to a variety of soil types and environmental conditions.
