Miconia fosteri

Miconia fosteri, commonly known as the Hawaiian miconia, is a shrub or small tree native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, which are often mistaken for a single bloom. Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and a challenge for conservation efforts.
