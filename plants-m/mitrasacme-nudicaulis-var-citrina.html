Mitrasacme nudicaulis var. citrina

Mitrasacme nudicaulis var. citrina, commonly known as the yellow dwarf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual with slender, hairy stems and small, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The leaves are alternate, simple, and often hairy, with a distinctive heart-shaped base. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

The plant is valued for its ornamental flowers and is sometimes cultivated for its vibrant, early blooming display.
