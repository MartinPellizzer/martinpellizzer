Menispermum orbiculatum

Menispermum orbiculatum, commonly known as Indian snakeroot, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern North America.

It features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, often with a distinctive heart-shaped base. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into bright orange berries. Its roots contain alkaloids and have been used traditionally in herbal medicine.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
