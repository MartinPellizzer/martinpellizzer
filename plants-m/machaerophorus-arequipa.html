Machaerophorus arequipa

Machaerophorus arequipa, commonly known as the Arequipa thornapple, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky and arid environments. The plant features distinctive spiny stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance.

Machaerophorus arequipa is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and resilience.
