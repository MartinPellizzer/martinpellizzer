Melodorum chrysosericeum

Melodorum chrysosericeum, commonly known as the golden feather plant, is a strikingly ornamental species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, slender leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky white or golden fuzz, giving it a unique and delicate appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, which bloom throughout the warmer months. Its foliage is evergreen, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.

Due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements, it is widely cultivated as a decorative plant.
