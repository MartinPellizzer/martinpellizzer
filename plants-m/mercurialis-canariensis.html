Mercurialis canariensis

Mercurialis canariensis, commonly known as the Canary Islands mercury, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial plant that typically grows in rocky or coastal areas. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, and its leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped. It is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in arid conditions.

The species is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
