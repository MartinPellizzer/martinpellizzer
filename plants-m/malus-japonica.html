Malus japonica

Malus japonica, commonly known as the Japanese apple tree, is a deciduous tree native to Japan and Korea.

It is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring showy white flowers in early spring and vibrant red or yellow foliage in autumn. The plant produces small, edible apples that are typically used in culinary applications. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Malus japonica is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
