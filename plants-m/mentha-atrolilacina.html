Mentha atrolilacina

Mentha atrolilacina, commonly known as black-lilac mint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, two-lipped flowers that range in color from pale purple to deep violet, often appearing in whorls along the stems. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, which contribute to its minty scent and potential medicinal uses.

Mentha atrolilacina is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and in traditional herbal remedies.
