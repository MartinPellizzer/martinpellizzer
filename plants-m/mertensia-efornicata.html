Mertensia efornicata

Mertensia efornicata, commonly known as the silverleaf bluebell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale blue to violet, typically blooming in late spring. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often covered in a silvery-white coating, giving it its common name. It prefers well-drained, moist soils and thrives in partial to full shade.

Mertensia efornicata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
