Mertensia neglecta

Mertensia neglecta, commonly known as bluebells, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Mertensia neglecta is often found in woodlands and along stream banks, contributing to the ecological diversity of these habitats.
