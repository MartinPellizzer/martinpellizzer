Musa × paradisiaca var. glaberrima

Musa × paradisiaca var. glaberrima, commonly known as the African banana, is a variety of the banana plant native to Africa.

It is characterized by its large, elongated leaves and produces clusters of berries that are typically smaller than those of the common banana. The plant is a herbaceous perennial with a pseudostem formed by the tightly packed leaf sheaths. It thrives in tropical climates and requires warm temperatures and high humidity for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its edible fruit, which has a distinct flavor and texture compared to other banana cultivars.
