Malanea hypoleuca

Malanea hypoleuca, commonly known as the white-flowered malanea, is a member of the Malvaceae family.

It is a shrub native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida and the Caribbean. The plant is characterized by its showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.
