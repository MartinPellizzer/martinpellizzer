Myrcia lanceolata var. avenia

Myrcia lanceolata var. avenia, commonly known as the white myrcia, is a species of flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring lanceolate leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its fruit is a red, fleshy drupe that contains several seeds, and it is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The plant's botanical characteristics make it an important component of the local ecosystem and a subject of scientific study.
