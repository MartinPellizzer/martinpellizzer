Miconia renatoi

Miconia renatoi, commonly known as the Hawaiian miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, broad leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and is often found in the understory of native Hawaiian forests. It has become an invasive species in some areas outside its native range, outcompeting native vegetation.

Its rapid growth and ability to spread through seeds make it a significant ecological concern in certain regions.
