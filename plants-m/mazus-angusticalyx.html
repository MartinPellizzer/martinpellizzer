Mazus angusticalyx

Mazus angusticalyx, commonly known as narrow-leaf mazus, is a low-growing perennial herb native to China.

It features prostrate or ascending stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a hairy surface and a slightly serrated margin. It produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink or purple, typically blooming in early summer.

This plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
