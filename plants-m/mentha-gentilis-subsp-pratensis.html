Mentha gentilis subsp. pratensis

Mentha gentilis subsp.

pratensis, commonly known as field mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks.

The plant has square stems and opposite, serrated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, pale blue or purple flowers appear in whorled clusters during the summer months.

Field mint is valued for its aromatic leaves and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
