Mastichodendron williamsii

Mastichodendron williamsii, commonly known as the African mastichodendron, is a rare and unique plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, woody, and often twisted stems that resemble the limbs of a tree, giving it an unusual appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are followed by red, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are typically small and clustered at the base of the stems, and it is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered an endangered species.
