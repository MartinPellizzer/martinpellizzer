Mirabilis macfarlanei

Mirabilis macfarlanei, commonly known as the Macfarlane's four-o'clock, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Mirabilis genus, which includes several species known for their showy, often nocturnal blooms. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with hairy stems and lobed leaves. Its flowers, which open in the late afternoon, are usually pink or red, with four petals and a distinctive tubular shape.

It is cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
