Mapouria opaca

Mapouria opaca, commonly known as the African velvet tamarind, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant has compound leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture, giving it its common name. It produces yellowish-green flowers followed by elongated pods containing edible seeds.

Mapouria opaca is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine and cuisine.
