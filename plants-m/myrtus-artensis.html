Myrtus artensis

Myrtus artensis, commonly known as the Canary Islands myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its narrow, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule containing numerous seeds.

Myrtus artensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
