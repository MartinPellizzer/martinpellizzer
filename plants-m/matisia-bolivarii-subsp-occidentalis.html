Matisia bolivarii subsp. occidentalis

Matisia bolivarii subsp.

occidentalis, commonly known as the western matisia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Fabaceae. It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly in Bolivia and Peru.

The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
