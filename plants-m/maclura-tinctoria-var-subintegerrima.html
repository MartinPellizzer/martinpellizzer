Maclura tinctoria var. subintegerrima

Maclura tinctoria var. subintegerrima, commonly known as the yellow wood or golden root, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark that becomes deeply furrowed with age, and its alternate, compound leaves that are typically 5 to 9 leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in umbels, followed by orange to red berries that contain a single seed. It is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its purported health benefits.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
