Medicago × varia var. pseudoglomerata

Medicago × varia var. pseudoglomerata, commonly known as the Persian clover, is a hybrid perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a robust root system that helps prevent soil erosion and improves soil fertility through nitrogen fixation. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in forage and ecological restoration projects.

Its adaptability to various climates makes it a valuable species in agricultural and horticultural settings.
