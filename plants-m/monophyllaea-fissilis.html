Monophyllaea fissilis

Monophyllaea fissilis, commonly known as the fissured leaf bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant features palmate leaves that are deeply lobed, giving it a unique appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
