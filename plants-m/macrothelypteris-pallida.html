Macrothelypteris pallida

Macrothelypteris pallida, commonly known as the pale tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in lowland rainforests. Its fronds are typically a light green to pale yellow color, and it produces sporangia on the underside of its leaves.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
