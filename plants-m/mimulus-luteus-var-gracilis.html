Mimulus luteus var. gracilis

Mimulus luteus var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender yellow monkeyflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features slender, upright stems covered in small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom from spring to early summer. The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
