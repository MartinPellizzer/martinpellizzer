Manihot lagoensis

Manihot lagoensis, commonly known as the African arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces edible tubers, which are rich in starch and used as a food source. The plant has large, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers. Its tubers are often used as a substitute for potato or cornstarch in cooking.

The plant is also valued for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
