Mimosa laxa

Mimosa laxa, commonly known as the slender mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands. It is known for its sensitive leaves that fold when touched, a trait common in the Mimosa genus.

Mimosa laxa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.
