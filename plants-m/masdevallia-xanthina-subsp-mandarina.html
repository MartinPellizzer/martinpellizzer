Masdevallia xanthina subsp. mandarina

Masdevallia xanthina subsp.

mandarina, commonly known as the Yellow Mandarin orchid, is a striking orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America. It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers with intricate patterns and a delicate, strap-like appearance.

The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that help it conserve moisture in its natural habitat.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique coloration and form.
