Maxillaria fuscata

Maxillaria fuscata, commonly known as the brown maxillaria, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and hairy, dark green leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, often with a contrasting yellow lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Maxillaria fuscata is appreciated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate in suitable conditions.
