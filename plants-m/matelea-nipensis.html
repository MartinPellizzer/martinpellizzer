Matelea nipensis

Matelea nipensis, commonly known as the Mexican moonflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night. The plant is known for its fragrant blooms and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

Matelea nipensis is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique nighttime beauty.
