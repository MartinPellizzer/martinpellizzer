Mesembryanthemum splendens subsp. splendens

Mesembryanthemum splendens subsp.

splendens, commonly known as the Ice Plant, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a fine, powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss.

The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in spring and summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-draining soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ability to store water in its leaves allows it to survive in arid environments.
