Melicope pseudoanisata

Melicope pseudoanisata, commonly known as the Indian pepper tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant has opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a slightly rough texture. Its small, fragrant flowers are typically white or pale yellow and are followed by dark blue to black berries.

The plant is valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in traditional medicine.
