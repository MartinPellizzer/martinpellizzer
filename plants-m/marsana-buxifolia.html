Marsana buxifolia

Marsana buxifolia, commonly known as the waxleaf boxwood, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage that is typically glossy and dark green. The leaves are small, oval-shaped, and often have a waxy texture, which contributes to its ornamental appeal. It produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by red, fleshy fruits.

This plant is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
