Momordica pauciflora

Momordica pauciflora, commonly known as the few-flowered bitter gourd, is a climbing vine belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its pale green, elongated fruits that are often covered with small, spiny tubercles. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and has heart-shaped, lobed leaves with a rough texture. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in warm climates with well-drained soil.

It is cultivated for its edible fruits, which are used in various cuisines and traditional medicine.
