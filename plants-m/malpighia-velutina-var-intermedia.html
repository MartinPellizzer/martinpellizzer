Malpighia velutina var. intermedia

Malpighia velutina var. intermedia, commonly known as the velvet passionfruit, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a small, evergreen shrub that produces edible, orange-colored fruits with a sweet and tangy flavor. The plant features glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. Its fruit is often used in culinary applications and traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.
