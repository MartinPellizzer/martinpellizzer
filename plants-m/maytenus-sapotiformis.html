Maytenus sapotiformis

Maytenus sapotiformis, commonly known as the Mexican sapote, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Central America and northern South America.

It belongs to the family Sapotaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits with a thick, sweet pulp.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and ecological role in its native habitats.
