Mentha × rotundifolia var. variegata

Mentha × rotundifolia var. variegata, commonly known as variegated round-leaf mint, is a hybrid species of mint characterized by its unique foliage.

Its leaves are typically rounded and have a variegated pattern of green and white or yellow markings. This plant is native to Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its aromatic properties. It prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
