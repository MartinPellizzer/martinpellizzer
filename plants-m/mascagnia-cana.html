Mascagnia cana

Mascagnia cana, commonly known as the white-flowered trumpet vine, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features woody stems with opposite, ovate leaves that have a grayish-green color. Its showy, tubular flowers are pale yellow to white, often appearing in late spring to early summer.

Mascagnia cana is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to drought conditions.
