Mesembryanthemum frederici

Mesembryanthemum frederici, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, often hairy leaves and showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick leaves and stems. It produces small, orange to red berries that are edible and used in various culinary applications.

Mesembryanthemum frederici is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
