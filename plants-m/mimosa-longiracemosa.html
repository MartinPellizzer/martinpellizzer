Mimosa longiracemosa

Mimosa longiracemosa, commonly known as the long-branch mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive pod fruits. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling the shape of a fern. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
