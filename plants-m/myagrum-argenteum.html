Myagrum argenteum

Myagrum argenteum, commonly known as silver myrtle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage due to a dense covering of fine hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. Its leaves are aromatic and have been used traditionally by Indigenous Australians for various purposes.

Myagrum argenteum is typically found in coastal regions and is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.
