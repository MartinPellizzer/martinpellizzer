Malus dolichomorpha

Malus dolichomorpha, commonly known as the long-leaf apple, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its elongated leaves and small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes grown in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
