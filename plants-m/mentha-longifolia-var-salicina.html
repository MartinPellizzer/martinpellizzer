Mentha longifolia var. salicina

Mentha longifolia var. salicina, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mint, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and its ability to thrive in moist, shady environments. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, giving it a distinctive minty scent.

It is often used in culinary and medicinal applications due to its refreshing flavor and potential health benefits.
