Mahernia gracilis

Mahernia gracilis, commonly known as the slender mahernia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pale blue or violet, and they bloom in late spring to early summer.

Mahernia gracilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
