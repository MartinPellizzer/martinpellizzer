Malus americana

Malus americana, commonly known as the American crabapple, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to eastern North America and is characterized by its small, ornamental fruits and showy spring blossoms. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 12 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, while its flowers are usually white or pink.

The fruit, which is edible but often tart, is used in jams, jellies, and traditional remedies.
