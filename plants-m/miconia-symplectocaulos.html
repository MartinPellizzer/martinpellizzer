Miconia symplectocaulos

Miconia symplectocaulos, commonly known as the clustered miconia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with smooth or slightly hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, clustered white or pinkish flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds, aiding in its dispersal.

Miconia symplectocaulos is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories.
