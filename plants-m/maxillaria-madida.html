Maxillaria madida

Maxillaria madida, commonly known as the Madida orchid, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces dark green, strap-like leaves and clusters of pink to purple flowers with yellow markings. Maxillaria madida thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in both indoor and outdoor gardens.
