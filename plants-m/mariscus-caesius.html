Mariscus caesius

Mariscus caesius, commonly known as the bluebells of the Mediterranean, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically ranging in color from pale blue to deep violet. The flowers are bilaterally symmetrical, with three petals and three sepals, and are often fragrant. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone-rich habitats.
