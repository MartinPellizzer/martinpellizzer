Meconella oregana var. denticulata

Meconella oregana var. denticulata, commonly known as the Pacific silver fir, is a variant of the silver fir species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its conical shape, dense foliage, and dark green needles that are slightly serrated. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 100 meters in optimal conditions. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of high-elevation ecosystems.
