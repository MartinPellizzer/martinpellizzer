Montia mexicana

Montia mexicana, commonly known as Mexican watercress, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Montiaceae family and is characterized by its small, kidney-shaped leaves and delicate white flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments, often found along stream banks and in rocky crevices. Its leaves are edible and have a slightly peppery flavor, making it a traditional food in some indigenous communities.

Montia mexicana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
