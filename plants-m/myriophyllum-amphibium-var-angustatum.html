Myriophyllum amphibium var. angustatum

Myriophyllum amphibium var. angustatum, commonly known as narrowleaf watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to North America.

It has slender, branching stems that are often tinged with red, and its small, opposite leaves are narrow and strap-like. The plant produces tiny, yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically found in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for fish and invertebrates.

However, it can become invasive in some regions, outcompeting native vegetation.
