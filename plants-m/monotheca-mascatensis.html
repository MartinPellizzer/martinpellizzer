Monotheca mascatensis

Monotheca mascatensis, commonly known as the Mascat thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and high temperatures. Its leaves are spiny and often covered in fine hairs, providing protection against herbivores.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in clusters, contributing to its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
