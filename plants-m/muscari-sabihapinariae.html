Muscari sabihapinariae

Muscari sabihapinariae, commonly known as the Persian grape hyacinth, is a bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It features clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has strap-like, grassy foliage that emerges from a basal rosette of leaves. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Muscari sabihapinariae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, bell-shaped blooms.
