Masdevallia estradae var. xanthina

Masdevallia estradae var. xanthina, commonly known as the Yellow Masdevallia, is a rare and striking orchid native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its foliage consists of long, narrow leaves that help it capture moisture from the humid air.

Due to its limited distribution and specific environmental requirements, it is considered a challenging species for cultivation.
