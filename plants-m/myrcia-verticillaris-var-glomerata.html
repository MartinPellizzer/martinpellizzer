Myrcia verticillaris var. glomerata

Myrcia verticillaris var. glomerata, commonly known as the clustered myrtle, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage and small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and horticultural cultivation.
