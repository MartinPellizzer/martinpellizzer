Melastoma impetiginosum

Melastoma impetiginosum, commonly known as the spotted melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Melastomataceae and is characterized by its attractive, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural gardens.
