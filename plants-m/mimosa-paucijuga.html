Mimosa paucijuga

Mimosa paucijuga, commonly known as the small-leaved sensitive plant, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves that fold shut when touched. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its sensitive foliage, it is also referred to as the "shy plant" in some areas.
