Momordica balsamina

Momordica balsamina, commonly known as the common bitter melon, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, warty, green fruits that turn orange when mature. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruits.

The plant is also known for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for various health benefits.
