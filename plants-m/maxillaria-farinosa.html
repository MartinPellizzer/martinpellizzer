Maxillaria farinosa

Maxillaria farinosa, commonly known as the powdered maxillaria, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure and its showy, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant produces linear, grass-like leaves and has a unique, velvety texture on its petals. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Maxillaria farinosa is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
