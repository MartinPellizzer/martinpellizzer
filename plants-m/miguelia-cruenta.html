Miguelia cruenta

Miguelia cruenta, commonly known as the Mexican bristlecone, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its woody structure and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by pods containing edible seeds. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability.

Miguelia cruenta plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.
