Mucuna gigantea var. nigricans

Mucuna gigantea var. nigricans, commonly known as the black velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and distinctive black, velvety pods that contain numerous seeds. The plant is notable for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates. It produces a significant amount of nitrogen through its symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria in its root nodules.

This species is often cultivated for its forage value and as a cover crop due to its hardy nature and ecological benefits.
