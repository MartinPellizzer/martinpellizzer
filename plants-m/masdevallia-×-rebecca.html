Masdevallia × rebecca

Masdevallia × rebecca, commonly known as Rebecca's masdevallia, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Andes.

It is characterized by its striking, often colorful flowers that display a range of hues including red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that help it conserve moisture in its high-altitude environment.

Masdevallia × rebecca is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and unique floral structure.
