Miscolobium densiflorum

Miscolobium densiflorum, commonly known as the denseflower mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees in its native habitat.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces clusters of tiny, white to pinkish flowers. The plant relies on its host for water and nutrients, while still performing photosynthesis. Its seeds are dispersed by birds that eat its fleshy, red berries.

Miscolobium densiflorum plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and contributing to forest biodiversity.
