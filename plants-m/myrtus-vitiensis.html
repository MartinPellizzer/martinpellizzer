Myrtus vitiensis

Myrtus vitiensis, commonly known as the Vitiensis myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the island of Vanuatu.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves and flowers are used in traditional medicine and for their aromatic properties.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in coastal or forested areas.
