Myagrum bienne

Myagrum bienne, commonly known as the silverleaf myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to eastern Australia.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are silver-white on the underside, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Myagrum bienne is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional Indigenous practices.
