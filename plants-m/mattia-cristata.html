Mattia cristata

Mattia cristata, commonly known as the Crowned Viper's Bugloss, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a compact, upright growth habit with hairy, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Mattia cristata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
