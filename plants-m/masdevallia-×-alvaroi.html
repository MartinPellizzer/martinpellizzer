Masdevallia × alvaroi

Masdevallia × alvaroi, commonly known as the Alvaro's masdevallia, is a hybrid orchid species native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often brightly colored flowers that resemble miniature orchids, with intricate patterns and a unique shape. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, strap-like leaves that help it absorb moisture from the air.

Masdevallia × alvaroi is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
