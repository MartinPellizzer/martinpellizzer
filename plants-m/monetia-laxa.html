Monetia laxa

Monetia laxa, commonly known as the lax money plant, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and striking, spatulate-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow spathe, which encloses a spadix bearing numerous tiny, clustered flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive foliage.

Monetia laxa is also valued for its ability to climb and trail, making it suitable for hanging baskets or vertical gardens.
