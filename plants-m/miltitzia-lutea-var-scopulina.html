Miltitzia lutea var. scopulina

Miltitzia lutea var. scopulina, commonly known as the yellow miltitzia, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish-green leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and scrub habitats. Its botanical features include woody stems and a compact growth habit.

It is relatively drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun to partial shade.
