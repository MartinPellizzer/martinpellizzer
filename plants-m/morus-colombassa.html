Morus colombassa

Morus colombassa, commonly known as the Colombian mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Moraceae family, which includes other well-known species like the common mulberry. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
