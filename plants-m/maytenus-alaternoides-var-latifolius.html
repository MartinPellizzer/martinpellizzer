Maytenus alaternoides var. latifolius

Maytenus alaternoides var. latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf mayten, is a woody shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in dry forests and scrublands.

The species is valued for its hardy nature and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
