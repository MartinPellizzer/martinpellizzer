Malva hawaiensis

Malva hawaiensis, commonly known as the Hawaiian mallows, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from pink to white, often with a prominent yellow center. Its leaves are palmately lobed and covered in fine hairs, giving them a soft texture.

Malva hawaiensis typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as forest understories and stream banks.
