Matthiola fruticulosa var. major

Matthiola fruticulosa var. major, commonly known as the tall wallflower, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and clusters of bright yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its flowers bloom in spring and early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
