Melodorum cinerascens

Melodorum cinerascens, commonly known as the gray mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, grayish pubescence. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in loose, open inflorescences, blooming in the late spring to early summer.

It plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
