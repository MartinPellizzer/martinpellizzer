Monnina pycnophylla

Monnina pycnophylla, commonly known as the hairy monnina, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is a low-growing annual herb with small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Monnina pycnophylla is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops.
