Malus prunifolia f. edulis

Malus prunifolia f. edulis, commonly known as the edible crabapple, is a cultivated form of the crabapple tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, tart fruits that are often used in cooking and winemaking. The plant typically grows to a height of 6 to 10 feet, featuring showy white or pink flowers in spring. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it produces fruit in the fall.

The plant is valued for both its ornamental beauty and its culinary applications.
