Mentha × sativa subsp. humifusa

Mentha × sativa subsp.

humifusa, commonly known as purple mint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its low-growing, spreading habit and hairy, oval-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, pale purple flowers that bloom in summer, typically in whorled clusters. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, making it useful in culinary and medicinal applications.
