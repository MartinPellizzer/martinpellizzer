Morus alba var. multicaulis

Morus alba var. multicaulis, commonly known as the multi-stemmed mulberry, is a variant of the white mulberry native to Asia.

It is characterized by its multiple trunks that arise from a single root system, giving it a distinctive, bushy appearance. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits called figs, which are edible and attract various wildlife. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of food for silkworms.

Its leaves are used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some regions.
