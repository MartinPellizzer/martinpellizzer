Micropteryx laurifolia

Micropteryx laurifolia, commonly known as the laurel leaf moth, is a species of moth belonging to the family Eriocampsidae.

It is characterized by its small, delicate wings that are often adorned with intricate patterns resembling the leaves of its host plants. The larvae of this species feed on various species of laurel, particularly those in the genus *Persea*. The plant's botanical significance lies in its role as a host for this moth, contributing to the ecological diversity of forest ecosystems.

Its presence is often indicative of a healthy, undisturbed habitat.
