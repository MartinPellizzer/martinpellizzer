Montia major

Montia major, commonly known as the large marsh mallow, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, pink to purple flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows, streambanks, and marshy areas. Its flowers are typically clustered in branched inflorescences and bloom from late spring to early summer.

Montia major is a perennial herb that plays a role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining wetland ecosystems.
