Melica canescens

Melica canescens, commonly known as the gray beardgrass, is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as meadows, marshes, and stream banks. The plant has slender, hair-covered stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often grayish-green in color. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, two-lobed spikelets.

Melica canescens is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
