Mammillaria amoena

Mammillaria amoena, commonly known as the "Little White Flower" or "Papalina," is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in white to pale blue tubercles. The plant produces small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in spring. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appeal, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cultivation.
