Monnina elliptica

Monnina elliptica, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluestem, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are elliptical in shape, typically with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and disturbed areas.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
