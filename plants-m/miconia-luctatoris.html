Miconia luctatoris

Miconia luctatoris, commonly known as the mourning miconia, is a species of flowering plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows as an understory shrub, thriving in humid, shaded environments. It produces small, fleshy fruits that are dispersed by birds, contributing to its ecological role in the forest ecosystem.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in tropical gardens.
