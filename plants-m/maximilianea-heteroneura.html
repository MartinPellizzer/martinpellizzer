Maximilianea heteroneura

Maximilianea heteroneura, commonly known as the narrow-veined maximilian's grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and high temperatures. Its leaves are narrow and have distinct, parallel veins, which are diagnostic features for identification.

The inflorescence consists of spikelets, which are typical of grasses and contribute to its ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
