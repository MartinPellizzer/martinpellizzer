Mariscus thunbergii

Mariscus thunbergii, commonly known as the Japanese snowbell, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its slender, arching branches and clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers. The plant produces dark blue, waxy berries that are edible but often consumed by birds. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Mariscus thunbergii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract wildlife.
