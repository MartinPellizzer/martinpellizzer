Masdevallia harryana var. decora

Masdevallia harryana var. decora, commonly known as the Decorated Harry's Masdevallia, is a unique orchid species native to the high-altitude cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate floral structure with elongated petals and a prominent, spurred lip. The plant typically grows as a sympodial orchid, forming compact, pseudobulbous stems with fleshy leaves. Its flowers, which bloom in shades of purple and pink, are highly prized for their ornamental value and rarity.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in controlled environments to ensure its survival.
