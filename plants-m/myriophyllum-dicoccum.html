Myriophyllum dicoccum

Myriophyllum dicoccum, commonly known as water milfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to North America.

It has finely divided, feather-like leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, green flowers that bloom in late summer, and it is often found in still or slow-moving freshwater environments. Myriophyllum dicoccum plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

However, it can also become invasive in certain regions if not managed properly.
