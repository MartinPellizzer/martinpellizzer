Marrubium persicum

Marrubium persicum, commonly known as Persian horehound, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and gray-green, hairy leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Marrubium persicum is often found in rocky or arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

It has been used traditionally in herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
