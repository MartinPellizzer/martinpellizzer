Mirabilis diffusa

Mirabilis diffusa, commonly known as the "Flame Flower," is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from red to orange and yellow. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.
