Myrobalanus guianensis

Myrobalanus guianensis, commonly known as the Guianese persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, orange-red fruits that are similar in appearance to persimmons. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and its fruit, which is sometimes used in local cuisines.
