Metaplexis japonica var. platyloba

Metaplexis japonica var. platyloba, commonly known as the broadleaf metaplexis, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. It has a taproot system that allows it to survive in arid conditions.

The species is notable for its ability to produce both showy and less conspicuous flower types within the same plant.
