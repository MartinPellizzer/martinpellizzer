Magnolia amabilis

Magnolia amabilis, commonly known as the sweetbay magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The tree has simple, alternate, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. Its bark is smooth and gray, becoming fissured with age.

The plant is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal.
