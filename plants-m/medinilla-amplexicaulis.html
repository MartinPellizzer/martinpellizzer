Medinilla amplexicaulis

Medinilla amplexicaulis, commonly known as the clasping medinilla, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that often wrap around the stems, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in humid, warm environments and requires well-drained soil.

Medinilla amplexicaulis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and showy blooms.
