Mendoncia hirsuta

Mendoncia hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy mendoncia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
