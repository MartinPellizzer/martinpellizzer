Microlepia hirsutissima

Microlepia hirsutissima, commonly known as the hairy sword fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, slender fronds that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The fronds are also sometimes used as ornamental plants in horticulture due to their aesthetic appeal.
