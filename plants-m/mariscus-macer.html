Mariscus macer

Mariscus macer, commonly known as the common mariscus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features basal rosettes of lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the spring and early summer.

The plant is often found in association with other moisture-loving flora in its native habitat.
