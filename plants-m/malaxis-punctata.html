Malaxis punctata

Malaxis punctata, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers with distinctive dark spots. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the soil.

Malaxis punctata is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
