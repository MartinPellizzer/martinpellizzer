Meadia crenata

Meadia crenata, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink and white. Meadia crenata has a fibrous root system and is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value.

It plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for small wildlife.
