Myrcia aulomyrcioides

Myrcia aulomyrcioides, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its small, fragrant leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite and elliptical, with a glossy surface and a strong aromatic scent.

The fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds.
