Melocactus × albicephalus

Melocactus × albicephalus, commonly known as the white-headed melon cactus, is a hybrid species native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem that is often covered with a dense layer of white, woolly hair, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic environments.

Due to its unique morphology and ornamental value, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscape designs.
