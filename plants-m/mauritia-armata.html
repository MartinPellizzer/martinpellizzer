Mauritia armata

Mauritia armata, commonly known as the golden palm, is a striking tropical plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into round, edible fruits resembling small coconuts. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The golden palm is also valued for its ornamental appeal in landscaping and conservation efforts.
