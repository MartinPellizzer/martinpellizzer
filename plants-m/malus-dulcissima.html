Malus dulcissima

Malus dulcissima, commonly known as the sweet crabapple, is a deciduous tree native to central and eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to the apple genus, Malus. The plant is characterized by its small, ornamental flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible, tart fruits in the fall. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, and the tree typically grows to a height of 6 to 12 meters.

Malus dulcissima is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and for its historical significance in traditional medicine.
