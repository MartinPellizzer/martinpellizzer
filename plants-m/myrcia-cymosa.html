Myrcia cymosa

Myrcia cymosa, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its aromatic evergreen leaves and showy white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a fragrant, slightly peppery scent when crushed.

Myrcia cymosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
