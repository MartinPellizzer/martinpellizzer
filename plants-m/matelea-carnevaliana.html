Matelea carnevaliana

Matelea carnevaliana, commonly known as the "Mexican nightshade," is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are often branched and woody.

Matelea carnevaliana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
