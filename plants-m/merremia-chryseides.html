Merremia chryseides

Merremia chryseides, commonly known as the golden morning glory, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist woodland areas.

Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardens and green spaces.
