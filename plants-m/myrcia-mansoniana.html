Myrcia mansoniana

Myrcia mansoniana, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil, where it typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds.

Myrcia mansoniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
