Monteverdia subalata

Monteverdia subalata, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine pubescence.
