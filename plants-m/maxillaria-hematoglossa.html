Maxillaria hematoglossa

Maxillaria hematoglossa, commonly known as the blood-lip orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, velvety petals and sepals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are often pink or red, with a distinctive lip that is darker in color.

Maxillaria hematoglossa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique appearance.
