Myrcia ferruginea

Myrcia ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty myrtle, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are glossy and dark green, while the flowers are usually pink or red, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects.

The fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds.
