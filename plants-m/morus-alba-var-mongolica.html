Morus alba var. mongolica

Morus alba var. mongolica, commonly known as the Mongolian mulberry, is a variant of the white mulberry native to northern China and Mongolia.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple color. The plant is well adapted to harsh climates, thriving in arid and semi-arid environments. It plays a significant role in traditional agriculture, particularly as a food source for silkworms.

Its bark and leaves have also been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
