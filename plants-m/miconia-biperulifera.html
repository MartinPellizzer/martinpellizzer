Miconia biperulifera

Miconia biperulifera, commonly known as the two-bristled miconia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, hairy structures. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Miconia biperulifera is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments.

However, it is also considered an invasive species in some regions due to its aggressive spread and ecological impact.
