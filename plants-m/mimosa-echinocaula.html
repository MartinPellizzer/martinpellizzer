Mimosa echinocaula

Mimosa echinocaula, commonly known as the prickly mimosa, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, yellow, spherical flowers. The plant is a shrub or small tree, typically growing up to 6 feet tall, with thorny stems and branches. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Mimosa echinocaula is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils and open woodlands.
