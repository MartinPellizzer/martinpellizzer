Melastoma paulinum

Melastoma paulinum, commonly known as the purple-flowered melastoma, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple, attracting various pollinators. Its fruits are small, capsule-like structures that release seeds when mature.

Melastoma paulinum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.
