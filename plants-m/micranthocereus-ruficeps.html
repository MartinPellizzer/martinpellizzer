Micranthocereus ruficeps

Micranthocereus ruficeps, commonly known as the red-headed cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems and distinctive red or orange spines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in arid environments and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable specimen in botanical collections.
