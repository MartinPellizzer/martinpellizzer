Malpighia coriacea

Malpighia coriacea, commonly known as the rough granadilla, is a species of flowering plant in the family Malpighiaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of the Americas, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, red, edible fruits that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The rough granadilla is also valued for its ornamental appeal due to its vibrant flowers and unique fruit structure.
