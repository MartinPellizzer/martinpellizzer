Muscari racemosum var. motelayi

Muscari racemosum var. motelayi, commonly known as the Motelay bluebells, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale blue to deep violet. The plant produces a tall, leafless stem that bears numerous small, clustered flowers. Its bulbs are adapted to survive in well-drained soils and dry conditions.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and naturalized areas.
