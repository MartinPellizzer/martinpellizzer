Medinilla lorentziana

Medinilla lorentziana, commonly known as the velvet tangerine or velvet tangerine flower, is a tropical shrub native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that resemble tangerines. The plant produces small, yellow fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil and partial shade.

Medinilla lorentziana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and unique floral structure.
