Macrocnemum vestitum

Macrocnemum vestitum, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and abandoned fields.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive tendencies, it is considered a weed in many agricultural settings.
