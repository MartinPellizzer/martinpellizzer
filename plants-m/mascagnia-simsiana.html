Mascagnia simsiana

Mascagnia simsiana, commonly known as the yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant produces striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are usually narrow and evergreen, adapted to conserve water in harsh conditions.
