Mertensia revoluta

Mertensia revoluta, commonly known as purple jewelflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its attractive, waxy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in a fine, white pubescence. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Mertensia revoluta is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and naturalized areas.
