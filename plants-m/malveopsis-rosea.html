Malveopsis rosea

Malveopsis rosea, commonly known as the rose mallows, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant features showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture.

Malveopsis rosea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
