Myrcia venezuelensis

Myrcia venezuelensis, commonly known as the Venezuelan myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to South America, particularly Venezuela and neighboring regions.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the summer. Its fruits are small, red drupes that contain several seeds, and the plant is often found in moist, shaded areas of tropical forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
