Myrcia pinetorum

Myrcia pinetorum, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, reddish-brown fruits that are often consumed by birds and other wildlife. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and fragrant leaves.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions, posing challenges to native ecosystems.
