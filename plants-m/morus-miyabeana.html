Morus miyabeana

Morus miyabeana, commonly known as the Japanese mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish flowers. The plant produces edible fruits that are typically consumed by silkworms in sericulture. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ecological and economic benefits.

Its bark and leaves have also been used in traditional medicine.
