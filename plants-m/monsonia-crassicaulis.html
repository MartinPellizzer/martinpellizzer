Monsonia crassicaulis

Monsonia crassicaulis, commonly known as the broad-stemmed monsonia, is a striking perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features a thick, cylindrical stem that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, often covered in a dense layer of white or grayish hairs. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Monsonia crassicaulis is well adapted to arid conditions and is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
