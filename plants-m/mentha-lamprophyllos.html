Mentha lamprophyllos

Mentha lamprophyllos, commonly known as narrow-leaved mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern and eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in whorls along the stem. Mentha lamprophyllos is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

It is valued for its aromatic leaves and is sometimes used in culinary or medicinal applications.
