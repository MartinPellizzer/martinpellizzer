Musa chunii

Musa chunii, commonly known as the Chinese banana, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous plant that typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. The plant produces large, heart-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its flowers are clustered in a spike, and the fruit, while not commonly consumed, has some ornamental value.

Musa chunii is also valued for its ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
