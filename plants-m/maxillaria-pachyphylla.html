Maxillaria pachyphylla

Maxillaria pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-leaved orchid, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a terrestrial orchid that thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features thick, fleshy leaves that help it store water, adapting to its often dry habitat. Its flowers are typically yellow or green with red or purple markings, and they bloom during the summer months.

Maxillaria pachyphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
