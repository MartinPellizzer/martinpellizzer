Manihot gracilis var. tenerrima

Manihot gracilis var. tenerrima, commonly known as the slender arrowroot, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features slender, erect stems with alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is cultivated for its starchy, edible roots, which are similar in texture to yams and cassava.

Due to its ornamental value and nutritional benefits, it is sometimes grown in subtropical gardens.
