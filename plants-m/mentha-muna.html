Mentha muna

Mentha muna, commonly known as wild mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a strong menthol scent, while its flowers are small and tubular, usually purple or white.

Mentha muna is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional remedies.
