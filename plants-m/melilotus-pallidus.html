Melilotus pallidus

Melilotus pallidus, commonly known as pale melilot, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous structure and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in spikes, and it has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

Melilotus pallidus is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a forage crop for livestock.
