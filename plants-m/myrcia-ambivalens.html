Myrcia ambivalens

Myrcia ambivalens, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is native to South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub that can grow up to 10 meters tall. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves and produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

The plant is also known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
