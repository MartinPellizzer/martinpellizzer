Machaerium lanatum

Machaerium lanatum, commonly known as the hairy machaerium, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its feathery, hairy foliage and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to several meters. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with numerous small leaflets covered in fine, soft hairs.

The flowers are usually white or pale pink, and the plant produces small, flat pods that contain several seeds.
