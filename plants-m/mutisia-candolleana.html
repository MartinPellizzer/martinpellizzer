Mutisia candolleana

Mutisia candolleana, commonly known as the Candolleana mutisia, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy yellow to orange ray florets surrounded by tubular disc florets, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It typically grows in moist, shaded habitats at elevations ranging from 1,500 to 3,000 meters.

The leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
