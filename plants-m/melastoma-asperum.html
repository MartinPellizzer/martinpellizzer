Melastoma asperum

Melastoma asperum, commonly known as the rough melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its oval, leathery leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Melastoma asperum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
