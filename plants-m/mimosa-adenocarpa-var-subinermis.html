Mimosa adenocarpa var. subinermis

Mimosa adenocarpa var. subinermis, commonly known as the silky mimosa, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft appearance. The plant produces small, yellow, globular flower heads that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
