Merremia sibirica var. vesiculosa

Merremia sibirica var. vesiculosa, commonly known as the Siberian bubble gourd, is a climbing vine native to temperate regions of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and produces large, inflated fruits that can grow up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant is characterized by its yellow flowers and is often cultivated for its edible, tender fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun for optimal growth.

The varietal name 'vesiculosa' refers to the inflated, balloon-like structure of its fruit.
