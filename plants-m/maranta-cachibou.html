Maranta cachibou

Maranta cachibou, commonly known as the "princess flower," is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Marantaceae family, known for its ornamental foliage and unique growth habits. The plant features vibrant, heart-shaped leaves that often display intricate patterns and a striking red or purple underside. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Maranta cachibou is prized for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
