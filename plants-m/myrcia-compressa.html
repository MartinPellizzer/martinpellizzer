Myrcia compressa

Myrcia compressa, commonly known as the Brazilian peppercorn, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that can grow up to 6 meters tall. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, smooth, red drupe that contains a single seed.

The leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a glossy, dark green appearance with a slightly rough texture.
