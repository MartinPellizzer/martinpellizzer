Mammillaria barbata

Mammillaria barbata, commonly known as the barbed mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in white, woolly spines. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its spiny structure. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appearance, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses.
