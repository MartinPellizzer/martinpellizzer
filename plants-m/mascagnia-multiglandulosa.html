Mascagnia multiglandulosa

Mascagnia multiglandulosa, commonly known as the many-glandular mescal bean, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves with prominent glandular dots. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by elongated, bean-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

The species is notable for its chemical composition, which includes compounds of interest in pharmacological research.
