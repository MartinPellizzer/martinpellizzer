Myopsia

Myopsia, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the remote regions of South America.

It is characterized by its translucent, waxy leaves that appear almost ethereal under sunlight. The plant produces small, pale flowers that bloom only at night, emitting a faint bioluminescent glow. Myopsia thrives in humid, shaded environments and is adapted to absorb minimal light for photosynthesis.

Due to its elusive nature and unique botanical features, it is considered one of the most intriguing plants in the botanical world.
