Marsippospermum gracile

Marsippospermum gracile, commonly known as the slender Marsippospermum, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. The plant features simple, elliptical leaves with a smooth texture and a dark green color. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Marsippospermum gracile is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its compact growth and foliage.
