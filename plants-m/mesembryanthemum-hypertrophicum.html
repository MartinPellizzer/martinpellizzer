Mesembryanthemum hypertrophicum

Mesembryanthemum hypertrophicum, commonly known as the Cape marigold, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and thick, waxy leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in arid conditions. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in the early morning and close by midday. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions contributes to its adaptability in various environments.
