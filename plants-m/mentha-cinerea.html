Mentha cinerea

Mentha cinerea, commonly known as gray mint, is a perennial herb native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its grayish-green, hairy leaves and a strong, minty aroma. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas and has square stems with opposite leaves. Its flowers are small, tubular, and borne in whorls along the stem.

Mentha cinerea is often used in herbal medicine for its purported cooling and digestive properties.
