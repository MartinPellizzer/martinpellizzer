Milla magnifica

Milla magnifica, commonly known as the golden flame orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange and yellow flowers, which are adorned with intricate patterns resembling flames. The plant's leaves are broad and waxy, adapted to retain moisture in humid environments. It thrives in partial shade and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil.

Milla magnifica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a popular choice in botanical gardens and exotic plant collections.
