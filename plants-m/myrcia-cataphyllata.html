Myrcia cataphyllata

Myrcia cataphyllata, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are oval-shaped, glossy, and have a distinctive aromatic scent.

The flowers are small, white or pink, and are borne in clusters, attracting various pollinators.
