Meconopsis superba

Meconopsis superba, commonly known as the Himalayan blue poppy, is a striking alpine plant native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple petals and its ability to thrive in high-altitude environments. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and mountainous regions, often at elevations above 2,500 meters. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a splash of color to its harsh habitat.

Meconopsis superba is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
