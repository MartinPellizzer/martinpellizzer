Malva ludwigii

Malva ludwigii, commonly known as Ludwig's mallows, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant is characterized by its large, showy pink or purple flowers and heart-shaped leaves. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun.

Malva ludwigii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
