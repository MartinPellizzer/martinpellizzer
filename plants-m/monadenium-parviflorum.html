Monadenium parviflorum

Monadenium parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered monadenium, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters, characterized by its compact, bushy appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom during the rainy season. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, aiding in water conservation.

Monadenium parviflorum is adapted to survive in harsh environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
