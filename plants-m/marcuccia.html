Marcuccia

Marcuccia, commonly known as the "false dandelion," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It consists of several species, most notably Marcuccia arvensis, which is native to Europe and parts of Asia. Marcuccia is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it a common weed in agricultural settings.
