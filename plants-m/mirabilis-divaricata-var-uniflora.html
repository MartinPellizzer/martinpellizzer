Mirabilis divaricata var. uniflora

Mirabilis divaricata var. uniflora, commonly known as the single-flower four o'clock, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that open in the late afternoon and close at night. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with slender, branching stems. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and slightly hairy, while the flowers are usually pink or red.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique flowering behavior.
