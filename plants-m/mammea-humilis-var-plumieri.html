Mammea humilis var. plumieri

Mammea humilis var. plumieri, commonly known as the dwarf mammea, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features opposite, elliptical leaves that are dark green and glossy on top, with a lighter, sometimes hairy underside. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique addition to tropical and subtropical gardens.
