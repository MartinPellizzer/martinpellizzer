Matthiola tricuspidata var. arcuata

Matthiola tricuspidata var. arcuata, commonly known as the curled wallflower, is a perennial plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and slightly hairy, while the stems are often reddish in color. Native to southern Europe, this plant thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found growing along walls or in dry, sunny areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
