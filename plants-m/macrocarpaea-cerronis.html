Macrocarpaea cerronis

Macrocarpaea cerronis, commonly known as the common macrocarpa, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces distinctive, elongated fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky, resinous substance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a hardy species suitable for various garden environments.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.
