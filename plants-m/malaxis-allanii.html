Malaxis allanii

Malaxis allanii, commonly known as the Allan's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and forest floors. It has a rhizomatous root system and produces a single, slender stem with a few leaves.

Malaxis allanii is a rare and endangered species, requiring specific ecological conditions for successful growth and reproduction.
