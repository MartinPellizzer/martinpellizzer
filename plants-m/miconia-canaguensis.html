Miconia canaguensis

Miconia canaguensis, commonly known as the red miconia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers, which are followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. Miconia canaguensis is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

However, it is considered an invasive species in some regions due to its aggressive spreading habit.
