Mariscus subulatus

Mariscus subulatus, commonly known as the needleleaf mariscus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a small, perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils. The plant features narrow, needle-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are small and usually greenish-white, appearing in spring.

Mariscus subulatus is adapted to dry conditions and is often found in coastal or arid environments.
