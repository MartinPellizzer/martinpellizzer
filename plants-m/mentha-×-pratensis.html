Mentha × pratensis

Mentha × pratensis, commonly known as meadow mint, is a hybrid perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Meadow mint is valued for its aromatic leaves and has been used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.
