Muhlenbergia glomerata

Muhlenbergia glomerata, commonly known as silver bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and compact, fountain-shaped growth habit. The plant produces panicles of small, fluffy seeds that are often used in ornamental landscaping. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and ability to form dense clumps make it a valuable native plant for ecological restoration projects.
