Marsypopetalum

Marsypopetalum, commonly known as the silky mistflower, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy foliage and showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in clusters. Native to South America, particularly Brazil, these plants thrive in tropical and subtropical climates. The leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, giving them a silvery or grayish appearance.

Marsypopetalum species are often cultivated for their ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
