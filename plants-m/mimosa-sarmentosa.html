Mimosa sarmentosa

Mimosa sarmentosa, commonly known as the weeping mimosa, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, arching stems that hang downward, creating a cascading effect, and is characterized by its fern-like, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers in clusters during the spring, which are followed by fluffy, spherical seed pods. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Mimosa sarmentosa is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for beneficial insects.
