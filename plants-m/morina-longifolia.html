Morina longifolia

Morina longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved moringa, is a shrub native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and small white or yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous small, round seeds. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions.

Morina longifolia is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
