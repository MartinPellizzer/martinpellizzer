Myrtus baumanii

Myrtus baumanii, commonly known as Bauman's myrtle, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and clusters of white, fragrant flowers. It produces small, dark purple berries that are edible but not commonly consumed.

Myrtus baumanii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in coastal and rocky environments.
