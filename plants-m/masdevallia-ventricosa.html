Masdevallia ventricosa

Masdevallia ventricosa, commonly known as the "Bottle Orchid," is a terrestrial orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, bottle-shaped pseudobulbs and elongated, pendulous inflorescences. The plant produces striking, colorful flowers with intricate patterns, often in shades of red, orange, and yellow. It thrives in cool, humid environments with high light exposure and requires well-draining, acidic soil.

Masdevallia ventricosa is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and adaptability to cultivation.
