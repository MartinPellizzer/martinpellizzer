Masdevallia mentosa

Masdevallia mentosa, commonly known as the Mentosa orchid, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of South America, particularly in Peru and Ecuador.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a distinctive appearance, featuring elongated, strap-like leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically blooms in the spring, producing delicate, pinkish-white flowers with a subtle scent. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil.

Due to its unique beauty and limited natural range, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
