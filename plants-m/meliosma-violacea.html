Meliosma violacea

Meliosma violacea, commonly known as the violetleaf meliosma, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae, which includes mahogany and related trees. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are typically a deep violet or purple on the underside. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in panicles, and it produces small, round fruits that split open to release seeds.

Meliosma violacea is often found in moist, shaded woodlands and is valued for its ornamental foliage.
