Magnolia rubriflora

Magnolia rubriflora, commonly known as the red-flowered magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant has simple, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall, and its bark is smooth and grayish-brown. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular ornamental tree.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
