Magonia brachysepala

Magonia brachysepala, commonly known as the short-sepaled magnolia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has broad, glossy leaves and produces woody, cone-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

The species is relatively slow-growing and prefers well-drained soil and partial shade.
