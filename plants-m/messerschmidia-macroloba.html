Messerschmidia macroloba

Messerschmidia macroloba, commonly known as the large-leafed sandwort, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that help it store water in harsh environments. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is shallow and fibrous, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soils.

Due to its adaptability, it is often used in xeriscaping and as an ornamental plant in dry gardens.
