Maschalocorymbus villosus

Maschalocorymbus villosus, commonly known as the hairy false mallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous small, brown seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
