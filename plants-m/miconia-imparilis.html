Miconia imparilis

Miconia imparilis, commonly known as the velvetleaf miconia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of silky, velvety hair. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom during the warmer months. Miconia imparilis is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a common weed in disturbed areas.

It is also noted for its potential ecological impact due to its invasive nature in certain regions.
