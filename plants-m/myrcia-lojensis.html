Myrcia lojensis

Myrcia lojensis, commonly known as the Lojense myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. It produces small, fleshy berries that are typically reddish in color when mature.

Myrcia lojensis is often found in moist, shaded undergrowth and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
