Malvastrum corchorifolium

Malvastrum corchorifolium, commonly known as the yellow mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. The plant produces showy, five-petaled yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its fibrous stems and leaves have been historically used in traditional medicine and textile production.
