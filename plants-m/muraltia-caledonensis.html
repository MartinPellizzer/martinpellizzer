Muraltia caledonensis

Muraltia caledonensis, commonly known as the Cape York acacia, is a flowering plant native to northeastern Australia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows in coastal and sandy soils. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, bean-like pods. It is characterized by its compound leaves and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Muraltia caledonensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
