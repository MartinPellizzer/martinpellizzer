Muraltia tenuifolia var. intermedia

Muraltia tenuifolia var. intermedia, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood or pink mallow, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, often thorned stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces elliptical, leathery leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. Its flowers are borne in dense inflorescences and are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in warm climates for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
