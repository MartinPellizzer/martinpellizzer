Myrtus glazioviana

Myrtus glazioviana, commonly known as Glaziov's myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a fragrant, waxy texture. Its small white flowers are typically clustered in dense inflorescences and are followed by dark blue berries.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
