Mammillaria hirschtiana

Mammillaria hirschtiana, commonly known as the Hirschtianna mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and is covered in white to pale green spines. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its textured surface. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
