Mitrephora mollis

Mitrephora mollis, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish-brown bark and compound leaves with elliptical leaflets. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that turns from green to yellow or red when mature.

Mitrephora mollis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
