Macrotyloma axillare var. macranthum

Macrotyloma axillare var. macranthum, commonly known as the large-flowered cowpea, is a tropical legume native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves with small, hairy stipules. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a distinctive red or purple base. Its pods are typically slender and contain edible seeds that are used in various culinary applications.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and agricultural potential.
