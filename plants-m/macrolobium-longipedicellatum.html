Macrolobium longipedicellatum

Macrolobium longipedicellatum, commonly known as the long-stemmed macrolobium, is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its distinctive pod-like fruits.

The plant is native to tropical regions of South America and features compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, producing a unique, ornamental appearance. The species is notable for its long, slender pedicels that support its fruiting structures.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is relatively rare and often found in humid, shaded environments.
