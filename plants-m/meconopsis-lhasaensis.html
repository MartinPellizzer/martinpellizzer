Meconopsis lhasaensis

Meconopsis lhasaensis, commonly known as the Lhasa poppy, is a perennial herb native to the Tibetan Plateau.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky, alpine environments and prefers well-drained soils. Its leaves are palmately lobed and often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Meconopsis lhasaensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
