Monochoria dubia

Monochoria dubia, commonly known as the slender water hyacinth, is a semi-aquatic plant native to Asia.

It typically grows in shallow waters and wetlands, where it forms dense mats on the surface. The plant has elongated, narrow leaves that float on the water, supported by spongy, air-filled stems. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, round fruits that disperse seeds by water.

Monochoria dubia is often considered an invasive species in non-native regions due to its rapid growth and ability to alter aquatic ecosystems.
