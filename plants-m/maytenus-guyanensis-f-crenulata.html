Maytenus guyanensis f. crenulata

Maytenus guyanensis f. crenulata, commonly known as the Guianan mayten, is a tropical tree native to South America, particularly found in Guyana and adjacent regions.

It belongs to the family Celastraceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and opposite, elliptical leaves with crenulated margins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences, followed by woody, spherical fruits containing several seeds.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
