Margaretta rosea subsp. bidens

Margaretta rosea subsp.

bidens, commonly known as the Bidens rosea, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East. It is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early autumn.

The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or containers.

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
