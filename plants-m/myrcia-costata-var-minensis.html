Myrcia costata var. minensis

Myrcia costata var. minensis, commonly known as the Minas myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
