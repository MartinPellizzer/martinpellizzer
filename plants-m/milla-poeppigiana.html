Milla poeppigiana

Milla poeppigiana, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange inflorescences that resemble misty clouds, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as grasslands and riverbanks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Milla poeppigiana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
