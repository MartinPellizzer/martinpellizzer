Mimulus barbatus

Mimulus barbatus, commonly known as the bearded monkeyflower, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow-orange flowers, which are typically spurred at the back, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its textured appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in streambanks and woodland edges.

Mimulus barbatus plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
