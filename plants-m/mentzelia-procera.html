Mentzelia procera

Mentzelia procera, commonly known as desert smokeplant, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as deserts and rocky slopes. The plant features a tall, branched stem with narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, and are usually pale blue to purple in color.

Mentzelia procera is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
