Mentha × braousiana

Mentha × braousiana, commonly known as catnip, is a hybrid mint species that combines traits from various Mentha species.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, serrated leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom in late summer. Mentha × braousiana is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

It is widely cultivated for its aromatic properties and is known for its effects on cats and its use in herbal remedies.
