Melodinus polyadenus

Melodinus polyadenus, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical shrub native to regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, velvety hairs, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes, and it is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed soils. Melodinus polyadenus is often considered an invasive species in some areas due to its aggressive spreading habit.

Its botanical features make it an important plant in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
