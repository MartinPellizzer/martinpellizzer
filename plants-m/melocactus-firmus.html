Melocactus firmus

Melocactus firmus, commonly known as the rigid melon cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of northern South America.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height, and its spiny, cylindrical shape. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the rainy season, typically in the summer months. Its shallow root system allows it to absorb moisture efficiently from the surrounding soil.

Melocactus firmus is well adapted to harsh desert conditions, making it a hardy and resilient plant.
