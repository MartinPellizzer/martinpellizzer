Miconia laevipilis

Miconia laevipilis, commonly known as the velvetleaf miconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a large shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically yellow or white in color, and it produces edible fruits that are consumed by various wildlife species.

Miconia laevipilis is also known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, making it an invasive species in some regions.
