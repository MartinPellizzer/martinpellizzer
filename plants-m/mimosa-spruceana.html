Mimosa spruceana

Mimosa spruceana, commonly known as the spruce mimosa, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, yellowish-pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in woodland or chaparral habitats. It has a shallow root system and is adapted to arid environments, making it drought-tolerant.

The flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
