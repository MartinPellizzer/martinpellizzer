Michelia champaca var. pubinervia

Michelia champaca var. pubinervia, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a velvety texture with prominent veins. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its aromatic properties.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
