Melaleuca halmaturorum

Melaleuca halmaturorum, commonly known as the Halmaturong tea tree, is a small to medium-sized shrub native to the arid regions of South Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grey-green leaves and small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces woody capsules containing numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It is well adapted to dry conditions, with a deep root system that helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

This species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soils in harsh environments.
