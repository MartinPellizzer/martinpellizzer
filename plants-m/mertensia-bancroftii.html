Mertensia bancroftii

Mertensia bancroftii, commonly known as the pink mertensia, is a perennial flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens. Its botanical features include smooth, hairy stems and a distinctive, somewhat woolly texture on its foliage.

Mertensia bancroftii is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
