Mammillaria formosa subsp. formosa

Mammillaria formosa subsp.

formosa, commonly known as the Mexican night-blooming cactus, is a species native to Mexico. It is a small, spineless cactus with a globular shape and typically grows to about 10 centimeters in height.

The plant features white to pale blue flowers that bloom at night, attracting moths for pollination. Its fleshy, ribbed stems store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

It is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and ease of care.
