Myroxylon nitidum

Myroxylon nitidum, commonly known as the white mahogany, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its straight, durable wood that is highly valued for its fine grain and rich color. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers and pod-like fruits containing flat, winged seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a glossy appearance.

Myroxylon nitidum is also known for its historical use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
