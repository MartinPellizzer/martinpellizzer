Mapinguari neophyllus

Mapinguari neophyllus, commonly known as the ghost fern, is a rare and enigmatic plant species found in the dense, humid understories of tropical rainforests.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that resemble the ethereal forms of ancient folklore. The plant's unique morphology includes a short, creeping rhizome with clustered, pinnately divided leaves. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often associated with decaying organic matter.

Due to its elusive nature and limited distribution, it remains a subject of fascination for botanists and conservationists alike.
