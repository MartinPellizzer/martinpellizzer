Maxillaria mexicana

Maxillaria mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican orchid, is a species native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a compact growth habit and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has a pseudobulb structure that stores water and nutrients, typical of many orchid species. Its flowers are usually borne on lateral spikes and bloom during the spring and early summer.

Maxillaria mexicana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
