Malus bracteata

Malus bracteata, commonly known as the Chinese crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in traditional Chinese medicine. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have serrated edges, while its bark is smooth and grayish-brown.

Malus bracteata is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in horticulture.
