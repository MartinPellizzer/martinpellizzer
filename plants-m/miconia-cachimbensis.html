Miconia cachimbensis

Miconia cachimbensis, commonly known as the Cachimbeira, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers with five petals, often in shades of pink or white, which bloom during the dry season. It is also notable for its distinctive, winged fruits that contain several seeds.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some regions for horticultural purposes.
