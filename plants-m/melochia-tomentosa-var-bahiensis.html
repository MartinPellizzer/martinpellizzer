Melochia tomentosa var. bahiensis

Melochia tomentosa var. bahiensis, commonly known as the silky melochia, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically reddish when mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along riverbanks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens in tropical and subtropical regions.
