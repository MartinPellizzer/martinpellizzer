Malvastrum tomentosum subsp. pautense

Malvastrum tomentosum subsp.

pautense, commonly known as the woolly mallow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It features soft, fuzzy, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive, woolly appearance.

The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
