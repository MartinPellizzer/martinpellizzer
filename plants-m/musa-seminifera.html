Musa seminifera

Musa seminifera, commonly known as the giant banana plant, is a member of the Musaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, elongated leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces large, clustered inflorescences with both male and female flowers, which are essential for its reproductive process. Its fruit, often referred to as a banana, is typically yellow when ripe and contains numerous seeds.

This species is notable for its rapid growth and is cultivated for its edible fruit and ornamental value.
