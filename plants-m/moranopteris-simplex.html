Moranopteris simplex

Moranopteris simplex, commonly known as the simple fern, is a species of fern characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds.

It belongs to the family Pteridaceae and is native to tropical and subtropical regions. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures include sori that are clustered on the undersides of the fronds.

Moranopteris simplex is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
