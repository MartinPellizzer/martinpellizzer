Mimulus tilingii var. corallinus

Mimulus tilingii var. corallinus, commonly known as the coral trumpetflower, is a variant of the species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant coral-pink to red flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats, often in coastal areas or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and the stems are upright with a slightly woody base.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
