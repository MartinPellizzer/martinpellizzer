Mandevilla tomentosa var. hispida

Mandevilla tomentosa var. hispida, commonly known as the velvet mandevilla, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces clusters of trumpet-shaped, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated as a climbing plant or shrub.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and tropical landscapes.
