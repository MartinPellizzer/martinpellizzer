Manilkara lacera

Manilkara lacera, commonly known as the Brazilian lac tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves and smooth, grayish bark. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits containing a single seed. Its latex sap is historically used in the production of shellac, a natural resin.

The plant is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
