Muscadinia popenoei

Muscadinia popenoei, commonly known as the wild muscadine grape, is a species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous vine that produces small, dark purple berries that are rich in antioxidants. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and is typically found in woodland areas. It is closely related to other muscadine grape species and is valued for its hardy nature and unique flavor.

The berries are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
