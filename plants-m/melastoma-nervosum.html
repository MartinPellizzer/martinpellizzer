Melastoma nervosum

Melastoma nervosum, commonly known as the hairy mallow or red mallow, is a species of flowering plant in the family Melastomataceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Asia, including India, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with prominent veins, and its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to pink. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and shrublands.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.
