Machaerium oblongifolium var. subcordatum

Machaerium oblongifolium var. subcordatum, commonly known as the broadleaf machaerium, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. It typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine, and the plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and nitrogen-fixing capabilities.
