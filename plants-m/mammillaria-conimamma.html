Mammillaria conimamma

Mammillaria conimamma, commonly known as the Mexican fairy castle, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and is covered in white to pale green tubercles. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
