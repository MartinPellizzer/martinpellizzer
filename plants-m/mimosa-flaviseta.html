Mimosa flaviseta

Mimosa flaviseta, commonly known as the yellow mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shrubland and coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are often clustered in spherical inflorescences, and it produces small, legume-like pods that contain seeds.

Mimosa flaviseta is known for its rapid growth and ability to tolerate various environmental conditions.
