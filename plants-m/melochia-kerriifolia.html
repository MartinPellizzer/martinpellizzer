Melochia kerriifolia

Melochia kerriifolia, commonly known as the red bower vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves and striking red or purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when mature, containing a single seed. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Melochia kerriifolia is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
