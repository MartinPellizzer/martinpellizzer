Melo orientalis

Melo orientalis, commonly known as the Persian melon, is a flowering plant in the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to the Middle East and Central Asia and is characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit. The plant produces yellow flowers and oblong, sweet, aromatic fruits with a high water content. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and somewhat woody.

Melo orientalis is valued for its edible fruit, which is often used in fresh consumption or as a dessert ingredient.
