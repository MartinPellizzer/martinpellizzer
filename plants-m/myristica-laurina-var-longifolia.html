Myristica laurina var. longifolia

Myristica laurina var. longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, woody structure and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by distinctive, fleshy fruits containing the aromatic seeds used for nutmeg and mace. Its botanical features include a smooth, dark bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy surface.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in spice trade.
