Marsdenia pseudotinctoria

Marsdenia pseudotinctoria, commonly known as the red-flowered Marsdenia, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, red flowers that bloom in the summer. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous tiny seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.
