Moranopteris achilleifolia

Moranopteris achilleifolia, commonly known as the Achillea fern, is a fern species native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically bright green. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Moranopteris achilleifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
