Machaerina latifolia

Machaerina latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved machaerina, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in coastal and sandy soils, often found in the North Island of New Zealand.

Machaerina latifolia is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
