Mundulea pauciflora

Mundulea pauciflora, commonly known as the sparse-flowered acacia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is adapted to survive in drought conditions with its deep root system and waxy leaf surface. It produces small, yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and birds.

Due to its resilience, it is often used in ecological restoration projects in degraded landscapes.
