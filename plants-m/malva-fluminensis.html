Malva fluminensis

Malva fluminensis, commonly known as the Brazilian mallow, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist environments such as riverbanks and wetlands. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, pinkish-white flowers with five petals. Its hairy stems and fleshy leaves are characteristic of the Malvaceae family.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal.
