Mikania tripartita

Mikania tripartita, commonly known as the spreading glorybower, is a climbing vine native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are divided into three distinct leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Mikania tripartita is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
