Macodes javanica

Macodes javanica, commonly known as the Javanese macaranga, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits that release tiny seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various forest organisms.
