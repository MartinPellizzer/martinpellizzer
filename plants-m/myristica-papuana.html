Myristica papuana

Myristica papuana, commonly known as the Papuan nutmeg, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the island of New Guinea.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is closely related to the nutmeg tree (Myristica fragrans). The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and develops fruit that contains a dark, aromatic seed known as the nutmeg. Its botanical characteristics include a smooth, reddish-brown bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface.

The plant is valued for its aromatic seeds, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications.
