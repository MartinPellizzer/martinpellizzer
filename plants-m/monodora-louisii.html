Monodora louisii

Monodora louisii, commonly known as African tamarind, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Anacardiaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellowish flowers. The tree produces a heavy, aromatic fruit that contains hard, angular seeds. Its bark and leaves have been traditionally used in herbal medicine for their various therapeutic properties.

The plant is also valued for its timber, which is durable and suitable for construction.
