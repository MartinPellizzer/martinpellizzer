Machaerium orthocarpum

Machaerium orthocarpum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf machaerium, is a tropical shrub native to the Americas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically green or white, followed by pods containing several seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
