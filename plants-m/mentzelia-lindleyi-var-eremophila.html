Mentzelia lindleyi var. eremophila

Mentzelia lindleyi var. eremophila, commonly known as the desert blazingstar, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and showy, yellow-to-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils. It has adaptations such as deep roots and reduced leaf surface to conserve water.

The species is part of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and plays a role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
