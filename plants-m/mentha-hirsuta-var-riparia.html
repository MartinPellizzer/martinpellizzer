Mentha hirsuta var. riparia

Mentha hirsuta var. riparia, commonly known as river mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows near water bodies, such as rivers and streams, where it thrives in moist, sandy or gravelly soils. The plant has square, hairy stems and opposite, serrated leaves that are often covered with fine hairs. Its small, pale blue to purple flowers appear in whorled clusters during late spring to early summer.

It is valued for its aromatic leaves and is sometimes used in herbal teas or as a culinary herb.
