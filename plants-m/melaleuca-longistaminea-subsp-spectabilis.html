Melaleuca longistaminea subsp. spectabilis

Melaleuca longistaminea subsp.

spectabilis, commonly known as the Spectacular Honey Myrtle, is a shrub or small tree native to northern Australia. It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant produces woody capsules that contain numerous small, round seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy regions.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological significance in its native habitat.
