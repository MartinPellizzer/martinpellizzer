Melastoma vacillans

Melastoma vacillans, commonly known as the wavering melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its showy, bilaterally symmetrical flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with fine pubescence.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.
