Matsumurella

Matsumurella, commonly known as the Japanese lily, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in various colors, including white, pink, and red. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight. Matsumurella is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.

Its leaves are lance-shaped and emit a mild fragrance, adding to its appeal.
