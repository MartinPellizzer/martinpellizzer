Mammillaria aurorea

Mammillaria aurorea, commonly known as the golden mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and bright yellow to orange spines. The plant typically grows to a height of about 5-10 centimeters and features small, pale green to white flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its attractive appearance and relatively easy care, it is a popular choice for indoor and outdoor succulent gardens.
