Mentha laggeri

Mentha laggeri, commonly known as Lagger's mint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, giving it a minty fragrance.

Mentha laggeri is often used in traditional medicine for its purported cooling and digestive properties.
