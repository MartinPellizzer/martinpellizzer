Macadamia grandis

Macadamia grandis, commonly known as the smooth-shelled macadamia, is a species of evergreen tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces woody pods that contain one or two edible seeds, which are rich in oil and used in various food products. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its nut production.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native habitats.
