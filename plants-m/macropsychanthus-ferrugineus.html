Macropsychanthus ferrugineus

Macropsychanthus ferrugineus, commonly known as the rusty mallow, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features hairy, glandular stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped with a rough texture. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that release small seeds when mature.

Macropsychanthus ferrugineus is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
