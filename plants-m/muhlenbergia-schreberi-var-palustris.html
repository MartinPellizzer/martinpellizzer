Muhlenbergia schreberi var. palustris

Muhlenbergia schreberi var. palustris, commonly known as swamp muhlenbergia, is a perennial grass native to wetland habitats in the southeastern United States.

It features slender, arching stems that produce fluffy, silver-gray plumes during the summer months. The plant thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, pond edges, and along streams. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that adds visual interest to wetland landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
