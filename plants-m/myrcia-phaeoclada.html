Myrcia phaeoclada

Myrcia phaeoclada, commonly known as the dark-leaved myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some indigenous cultures.
